Malus domestica subsp. intermedia

Malus domestica subsp.

intermedia, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a botanical variant of the domesticated apple. It is characterized by its large, white or pale yellow fruits with a crisp, juicy flesh.

This subspecies is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit. It typically grows as a small tree with a rounded shape and produces showy pink to white flowers in spring.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for temperate climates.
