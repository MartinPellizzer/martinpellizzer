Mooria angustifolia

Mooria angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved mooria, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melanthiaceae and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous root system helps it spread and colonize new areas, making it a common species in wetland ecosystems.

Mooria angustifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
