Medinilla mannii

Medinilla mannii, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or velvet tangerine plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its waxy, velvety leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that resemble tangerines, which are often used in local cuisines. Medinilla mannii thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and floral display.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
