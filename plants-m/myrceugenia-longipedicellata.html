Myrceugenia longipedicellata

Myrceugenia longipedicellata, commonly known as the long-stalked myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the temperate regions of southern Brazil, particularly in the states of São Paulo and Paraná. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments.

Myrceugenia longipedicellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
