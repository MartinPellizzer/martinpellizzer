Mimulus neubertii

Mimulus neubertii, commonly known as the Neubert's monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils along stream banks and in woodland areas. It has heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Mimulus neubertii is a member of the Physaleae tribe within the Phrymaceae family.
