Mollinedia torresiorum

Mollinedia torresiorum, commonly known as the Torres Island molly, is a flowering plant native to the Torres Strait and nearby islands in northern Australia.

It is a small shrub with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy habitats.

Mollinedia torresiorum is an important part of the local flora and is adapted to the tropical and subtropical climates of its native range.
