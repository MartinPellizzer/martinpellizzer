Mentha marisensis

Mentha marisensis, commonly known as sea mint, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the Atlantic coasts of Europe and North Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in saline or brackish environments, such as salt marshes and tidal zones. The plant has aromatic leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer.

Mentha marisensis is characterized by its ability to thrive in harsh, coastal conditions, making it an important species in coastal ecosystems.
