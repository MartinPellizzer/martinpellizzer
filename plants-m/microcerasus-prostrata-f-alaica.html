Microcerasus prostrata f. alaica

Microcerasus prostrata f. alaica, commonly known as the alpine form of the creeping cherry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features prostrate growth habit with slender, often hairy stems that hug the ground. The leaves are elliptical, with a leathery texture and a dull green color, while the flowers are small, white, and fragrant, appearing in clusters. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
