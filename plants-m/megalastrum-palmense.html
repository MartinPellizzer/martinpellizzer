Megalastrum palmense

Megalastrum palmense, commonly known as the Brazilian palm, is a flowering plant belonging to the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white inflorescences with small, fragrant flowers, which attract various pollinators. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and is protected in some regions.
