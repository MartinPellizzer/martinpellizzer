Moraea aphylla

Moraea aphylla, commonly known as the leafless moraea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its absence of leaves, with only a short, leafless stem. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
