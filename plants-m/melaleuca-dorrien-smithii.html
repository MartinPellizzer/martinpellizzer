Melaleuca dorrien-smithii

Melaleuca dorrien-smithii, commonly known as the Dorrien-Smith melaleuca, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to the northern regions of Western Australia, where it typically grows in sandy soils and coastal areas. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. It is often found in heathlands and is adapted to withstand dry and salty conditions.

The species is named in honor of the botanist Dorrien Smith, who contributed significantly to the study of Australian flora.
