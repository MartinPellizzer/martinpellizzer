Myrcia microsiphonata

Myrcia microsiphonata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, reddish flowers. The plant produces small, round, orange fruits that are edible but have a spicy, peppery taste. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Myrcia microsiphonata is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
