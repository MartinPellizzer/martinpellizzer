Mammillaria melanocentra var. typica

Mammillaria melanocentra var. typica, commonly known as the black-tipped mammillaria, is a small cactus native to Mexico.

It features a globular stem with prominent, white to pale yellow tubercles that bear white spines. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.

Due to its striking appearance, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts and collectors.
