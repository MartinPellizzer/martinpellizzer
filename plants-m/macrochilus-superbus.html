Macrochilus superbus

Macrochilus superbus, commonly known as the giant leafy orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length, often with a waxy texture and a vibrant green color. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant in tropical regions.
