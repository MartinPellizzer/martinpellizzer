Morinda mollis

Morinda mollis, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically covered in a dense, velvety coating. Morinda mollis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and visually appealing species within the Rubiaceae family.
