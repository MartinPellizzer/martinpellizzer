Melhania cannabina

Melhania cannabina, commonly known as the cannabis plant, is a flowering herbaceous plant belonging to the Cannabaceae family.

It is native to various regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas, and it is characterized by its opposite leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant contains compounds such as tetrahydrocannabinol (THC) and cannabidiol (CBD), which are responsible for its psychoactive and medicinal properties. Its growth habit includes both annual and perennial forms, depending on the climate and cultivation conditions.

The botanical features of Melhania cannabina make it a subject of both scientific study and cultural significance.
