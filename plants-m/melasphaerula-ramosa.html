Melasphaerula ramosa

Melasphaerula ramosa, commonly known as the branched black mold, is a dimorphic fungal species that exhibits both mycelial and sporulating forms.

It is characterized by its dark, branching mycelium and the production of small, black, spherical spores. This fungus is typically found in soil and decaying organic matter, where it plays a role in nutrient cycling. Its morphology is notable for the dense, ramified structure of its vegetative growth.

Melasphaerula ramosa is often encountered in environments with high moisture and organic decomposition.
