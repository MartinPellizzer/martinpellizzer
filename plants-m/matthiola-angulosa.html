Matthiola angulosa

Matthiola angulosa, commonly known as the angular stock, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and produces clusters of fragrant, white or pink flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Matthiola angulosa is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
