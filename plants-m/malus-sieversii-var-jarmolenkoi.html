Malus sieversii var. jarmolenkoi

Malus sieversii var. jarmolenkoi, commonly known as the Jarmolenko apple, is a rare variant of the wild apple species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, tart fruits and is often found in mountainous regions of Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with dense foliage and produces flowers in early spring. Its genetic makeup is significant in the study of apple domestication and biodiversity.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
