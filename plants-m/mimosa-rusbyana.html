Mimosa rusbyana

Mimosa rusbyana, commonly known as the golden mimosa, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its feathery, fern-like leaves that are sensitive to touch, curling when disturbed. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its unique foliage and ornamental value make it a sought-after plant in horticulture.
