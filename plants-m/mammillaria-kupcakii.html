Mammillaria kupcakii

Mammillaria kupcakii, commonly known as the Kupcakii nipple cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring a rounded stem covered in white to pale green tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on short pedicels. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright sunlight, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is often sought after by cactus enthusiasts.
