Malus cerasifera f. fructo-luteo

Malus cerasifera f. fructo-luteo, commonly known as the yellow-fruited crabapple, is a cultivar of the common crabapple tree.

It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange fruits that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub or small tree with rounded, glossy leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for ornamental gardens.

This variety is prized for its vibrant fruit color and ornamental value in landscaping.
