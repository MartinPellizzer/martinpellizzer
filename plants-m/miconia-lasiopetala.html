Miconia lasiopetala

Miconia lasiopetala, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Miconia lasiopetala is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in some regions due to its aggressive spread and ecological impact.
