Microchilus elatus

Microchilus elatus, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments. The plant features a slender, erect stem with a few leaves and produces delicate, greenish-white flowers. Its roots are specialized for absorbing nutrients from the surrounding soil, a common adaptation in orchids.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
