Myriophyllum physocaule

Myriophyllum physocaule, commonly known as watermilfoil, is an aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as lakes and ponds. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in late summer, and it reproduces both sexually and vegetatively.

Myriophyllum physocaule plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
