Mentha × verticillata f. convexidentata

Mentha × verticillata f. convexidentata, commonly known as the broad-lobed water mint, is a hybrid plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its spreading growth habit and aromatic, narrow to broadly elliptic leaves with serrated margins. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. It produces small, pale blue to lavender flowers in whorled clusters along the stems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in herbal teas and traditional medicine.
