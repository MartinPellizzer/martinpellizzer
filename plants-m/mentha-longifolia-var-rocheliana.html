Mentha longifolia var. rocheliana

Mentha longifolia var. rocheliana, commonly known as rocheliana mint, is a variant of the longleaf mint species.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in whorled clusters along the stems. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained, sunny environments.

This variety is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for its aromatic and medicinal properties.
