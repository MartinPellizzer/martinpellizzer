Macrozamia preissii

Macrozamia preissii, commonly known as the Queensland nut or the cycad, is a slow-growing, evergreen plant native to northeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, feathery, compound leaves. The plant produces cone-like structures, with the male cones bearing pollen and the female cones containing seeds. It is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is known for its long lifespan and unique reproductive biology.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
