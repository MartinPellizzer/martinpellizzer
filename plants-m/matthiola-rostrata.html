Matthiola rostrata

Matthiola rostrata, commonly known as the rostrate stock, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and produces small, oval-shaped seed pods. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Matthiola rostrata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery.
