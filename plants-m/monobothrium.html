Monobothrium

Monobothrium, commonly known as the "spearhead liverwort," is a genus of liverworts belonging to the family Porellaceae.

These plants are characterized by their small, thalloid structure and are typically found in moist, shaded environments. Monobothrium species are epiphytic or lithophytic, growing on tree bark or rock surfaces. They have a simple morphology with a dorsiventral thallus and a distinct central strand of cells.

The genus is notable for its ecological role in nutrient cycling and as an indicator species for certain microhabitats.
