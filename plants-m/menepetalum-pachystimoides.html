Menepetalum pachystimoides

Menepetalum pachystimoides, commonly known as the giant pachystima, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in clusters, often reaching several feet in diameter. The plant has thick, fleshy stems and broad, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid habitat. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
