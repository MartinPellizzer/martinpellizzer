Macowania corymbosa

Macowania corymbosa, commonly known as the wax apple or Java apple, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with smooth, greyish bark and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that eventually develop into round, green fruits with a waxy coating. The fruit ripens to a red or yellow color and is known for its sweet, tart flavor.

It is often cultivated for its edible fruit and has various traditional uses in herbal medicine.
