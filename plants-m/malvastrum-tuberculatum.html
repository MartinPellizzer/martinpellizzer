Malvastrum tuberculatum

Malvastrum tuberculatum, commonly known as the woolly mallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields or along roadsides.

Malvastrum tuberculatum is a perennial herb that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, making it a striking addition to gardens or natural landscapes.
