Moraea villosa subsp. villosa

Moraea villosa subsp.

villosa, commonly known as the woolly moraea, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple.

The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
