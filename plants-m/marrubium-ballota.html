Marrubium ballota

Marrubium ballota, commonly known as Spanish horehound, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant features erect, branched stems covered in fine hairs and lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with a distinctive aromatic scent. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically pale blue or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems.

Marrubium ballota is valued for its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of bitter compounds.
