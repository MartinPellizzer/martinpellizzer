Myrtus sarandi

Myrtus sarandi, commonly known as the Andean myrtle, is a species of flowering shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Myrtus sarandi is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
