Mutisia sagittifolia

Mutisia sagittifolia, commonly known as the arrowleaf mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in open woodlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are arrow-shaped, hence the common name, and its yellow flowers bloom in clusters.

Mutisia sagittifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
