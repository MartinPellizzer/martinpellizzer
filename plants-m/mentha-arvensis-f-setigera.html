Mentha arvensis f. setigera

Mentha arvensis f. setigera, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in whorled clusters along the stem. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and woodland edges.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.
