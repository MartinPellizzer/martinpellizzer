Miconia beneolens

Miconia beneolens, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or miconia, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a large shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its palmate leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often consumed by birds and mammals. Its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets make it an invasive species in certain regions.

The botanical features of Miconia beneolens make it both ecologically significant and potentially problematic in non-native habitats.
