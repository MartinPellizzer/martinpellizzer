Miconia haematostemon

Miconia haematostemon, commonly known as the blood-stem miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers, which are often followed by bright red or orange berries. Miconia haematostemon is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural studies.
