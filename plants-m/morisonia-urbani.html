Morisonia urbani

Morisonia urbani, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in sandy, coastal soils. It is often found in dune ecosystems, where it plays a role in stabilizing sand and preventing erosion.

Morisonia urbani is an annual plant that is well adapted to coastal environments with high salinity and strong winds.
