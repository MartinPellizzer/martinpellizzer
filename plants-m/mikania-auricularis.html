Mikania auricularis

Mikania auricularis, commonly known as the earleaf mikania, is a climbing vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, sunny environments. Its rapid growth habit makes it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in certain areas.

The fruit is a small, dry capsule that releases tiny seeds, aiding in its widespread propagation.
