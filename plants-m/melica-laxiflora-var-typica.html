Melica laxiflora var. typica

Melica laxiflora var. typica, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands, often growing in temperate regions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of ecological systems supporting various insect and bird life.
