Mutisiopersea costata

Mutisiopersea costata, commonly known as the Costate Mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves with a distinct wavy margin. The fruit's pericarp is thick and contains a sweet, aromatic pulp with a high water content.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
