Malus communis var. sylvestris

Malus communis var. sylvestris, commonly known as the wild apple or crab apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, often ornamental fruits and showy spring blossoms. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is known for its hardy nature. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in apple domestication.
