Magnolia virginiana

Magnolia virginiana, commonly known as the common magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The tree has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
