Microchloa abyssinica

Microchloa abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian microchloa, is a rare and specialized grass species native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered growth habit and unique adaptations to its high-altitude, arid environment. The plant possesses a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing cyanobacteria, which is essential for its survival in nutrient-poor soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a dense, compact structure that helps reduce water loss.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, Microchloa abyssinica is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
