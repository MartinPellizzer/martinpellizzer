Mentha × calvescens

Mentha × calvescens, commonly known as peppermint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to Europe and temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, slightly fuzzy leaves and strong, menthol-like aroma. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. Peppermint thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is widely cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy, medicine, and as a flavoring agent.
