Mitrasacme pygmaea

Mitrasacme pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf mitrasacme, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. The plant features delicate, opposite leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in umbels, and are usually white or pale yellow.

Mitrasacme pygmaea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
