Macaranga subdentata

Macaranga subdentata, commonly known as the toothed macaranga, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant is typically found in tropical rainforests and thrives in humid, shaded environments. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsules containing numerous seeds.

The species is often used in ecological studies due to its role in supporting local biodiversity.
