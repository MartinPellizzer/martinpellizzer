Malveopsis spicata var. pauciflora

Malveopsis spicata var. pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered spurge, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy yellow flowers.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus, and is native to southern Africa. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Its flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, and the species is noted for its low-growing habit and drought tolerance.
