Mentha cristata

Mentha cristata, commonly known as the mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a serrated edge and a distinctive white flower cluster at the tips of its stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their calming and antiseptic properties.

Mentha cristata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
