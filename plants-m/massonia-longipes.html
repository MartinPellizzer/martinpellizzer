Massonia longipes

Massonia longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed massonia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, which helps reduce water loss.

Massonia longipes is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
