Mentha × subgentilis

Mentha × subgentilis, commonly known as hybrid mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant that belongs to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Mentha spicata (spearmint) and Mentha viridis (ovalleaf mint). The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring square stems, opposite leaves, and small tubular flowers. It is native to Europe and has been widely cultivated for its aromatic leaves used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The hybrid is known for its strong, pleasant aroma and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
