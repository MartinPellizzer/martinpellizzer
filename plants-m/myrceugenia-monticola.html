Myrceugenia monticola

Myrceugenia monticola, commonly known as the mountain myrtle, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly in the Andean regions of Peru and Ecuador.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that contain numerous seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.

Myrceugenia monticola is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
