Masdevallia chaetostoma

Masdevallia chaetostoma, commonly known as the hairy-stalked masdevallia, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, often colorful floral structures. The plant typically produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers with a distinct lip, which is a key feature in orchid taxonomy. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern.

This species is appreciated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and adaptability to shaded, humid environments.
