Myrcia auxotelica

Myrcia auxotelica, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Due to its invasive potential in some regions, it is sometimes considered a problematic species in ecological contexts.
