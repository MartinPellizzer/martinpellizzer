Miconia depauperata

Miconia depauperata, commonly known as the poorleaf miconia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the understory of forests. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually white or pale yellow, and are followed by distinctive, round, fleshy fruits.

Miconia depauperata is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage.
