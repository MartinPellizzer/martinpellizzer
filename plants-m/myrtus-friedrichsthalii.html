Myrtus friedrichsthalii

Myrtus friedrichsthalii, commonly known as the Friedrichsthal myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the island of Socotra, located in the Arabian Sea, and is characterized by its aromatic evergreen foliage and small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky habitats and has adapted to the arid conditions of its native environment. Its leaves are leathery and emit a subtle, pleasant scent when crushed.

Myrtus friedrichsthalii is also notable for its medicinal properties, which have been traditionally used in local remedies.
