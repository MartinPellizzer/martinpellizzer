Myrcia melanoclada

Myrcia melanoclada, commonly known as the black-leaf myrtle, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
