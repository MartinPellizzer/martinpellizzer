Mutisia retrorsa

Mutisia retrorsa, commonly known as the backward mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical environments and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.
