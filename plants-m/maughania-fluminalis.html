Maughania fluminalis

Maughania fluminalis, commonly known as the river maughania, is a herbaceous plant native to the wetlands and riverbanks of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, showcasing its adaptation to aquatic environments. The plant features long, narrow leaves that float on the water surface, while its small, white flowers bloom in clusters during the summer months. Maughania fluminalis plays a vital role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-rich waters make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
