Malacothamnus fasciculatus var. fasciculatus

Malacothamnus fasciculatus var. fasciculatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldcup, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in dense clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
