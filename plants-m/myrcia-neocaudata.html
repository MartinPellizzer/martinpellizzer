Myrcia neocaudata

Myrcia neocaudata, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Myrcia neocaudata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
