Montia vassilievii

Montia vassilievii, commonly known as the Vassiliev's monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Montiaceae and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in streambanks and woodland areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
