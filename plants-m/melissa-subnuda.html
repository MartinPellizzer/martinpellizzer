Melissa subnuda

Melissa subnuda, commonly known as narrowleaf lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. It is often used in herbal medicine for its calming properties.

Its essential oil contains compounds like citral and geraniol, which contribute to its distinctive scent and therapeutic uses.
