Memora adenophora

Memora adenophora, commonly known as the white sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy leaves and is often found in sandy or rocky soils, thriving in arid environments. It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is known for its drought tolerance.

Memora adenophora is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
