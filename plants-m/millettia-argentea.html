Millettia argentea

Millettia argentea, commonly known as silver millettia, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray bark and heart-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by flat, bean-like pods containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal.
