Matthiola myrtoides

Matthiola myrtoides, commonly known as the myrtle-like stock, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as cabbages and mustards. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of fragrant, violet or white flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly aromatic scent, similar to myrtle.

Matthiola myrtoides is typically grown as an ornamental plant in gardens for its pleasant fragrance and visual appeal.
