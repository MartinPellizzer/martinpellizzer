Mammillaria sinforosensis subsp. marionae

Mammillaria sinforosensis subsp.

marionae, commonly known as the Marion's milk barrel cactus, is a compact, globular cactus native to Mexico. It features a short, cylindrical stem with prominent, white, woolly spines and small, tuberculate areoles.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.

This subspecies is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
