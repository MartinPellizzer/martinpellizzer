Mesembryanthemum calamiforme

Mesembryanthemum calamiforme, commonly known as the "Cape Myrtle" or "Torch Plant," is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent species with fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, often blooming in summer. It is commonly cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance and drought tolerance.

Mesembryanthemum calamiforme is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
