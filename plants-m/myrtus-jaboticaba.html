Myrtus jaboticaba

Myrtus jaboticaba, commonly known as Brazilian myrtle or jaboticaba, is a small evergreen shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, dark purple berries. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade. Its flowers are white or pink and attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

The fruit is edible, often consumed fresh or used in jams and wines, and the plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
