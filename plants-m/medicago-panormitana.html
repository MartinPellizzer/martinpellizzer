Medicago panormitana

Medicago panormitana, commonly known as the Mediterranean clover, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a robust root system that helps it thrive in well-drained soils. It is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions.

Medicago panormitana plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.
