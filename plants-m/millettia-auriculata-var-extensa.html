Millettia auriculata var. extensa

Millettia auriculata var. extensa, commonly known as the broad-leafed millettia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
