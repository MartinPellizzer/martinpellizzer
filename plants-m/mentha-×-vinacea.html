Mentha × vinacea

Mentha × vinacea, commonly known as marsh mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to wetland areas in North America.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Mentha × vinacea thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found near streams, swamps, and other wet habitats.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers make it a valuable plant for ecological and potentially medicinal purposes.
