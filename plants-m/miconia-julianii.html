Miconia julianii

Miconia julianii, commonly known as the Julian's miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, dark purple fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats within tropical forests.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it can become a problematic weed in certain regions.
