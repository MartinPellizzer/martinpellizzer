Mariscus alatus

Mariscus alatus, commonly known as the winged spurge, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub with succulent, fleshy stems that have distinctive wing-like ridges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in a fine layer of white hair.

Mariscus alatus is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and desert landscapes.
