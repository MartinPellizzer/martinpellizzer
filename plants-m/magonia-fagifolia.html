Magonia fagifolia

Magonia fagifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf maple, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, palmate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by winged seeds dispersed by the wind. Its bark is smooth and gray, with a distinctive flaky texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
