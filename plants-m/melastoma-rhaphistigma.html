Melastoma rhaphistigma

Melastoma rhaphistigma, commonly known as the red-flowered melastoma, is a species of flowering plant in the family Melastomataceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, tubular red or pink flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

The plant produces small, oval berries that are dispersed by birds, contributing to its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
