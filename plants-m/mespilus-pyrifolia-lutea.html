Mespilus pyrifolia lutea

Mespilus pyrifolia lutea, commonly known as the yellow medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by distinctive, fleshy fruits that ripen in late autumn. The fruit has a unique, aromatic flavor and is often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers a sunny to partially shaded location.
