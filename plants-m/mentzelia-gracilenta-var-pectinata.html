Mentzelia gracilenta var. pectinata

Mentzelia gracilenta var. pectinata, commonly known as the velvetleaf tansy, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pale yellow to greenish-yellow flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant a velvety appearance. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert or arid environments.

This variant is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and floral structure compared to other Mentzelia species.
