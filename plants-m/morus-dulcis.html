Morus dulcis

Morus dulcis, commonly known as the white mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in the spring. The tree produces small, fuzzy berries that are edible and used in various traditional foods. It thrives in temperate climates and has been cultivated for centuries for its fruit and as a host plant for silkworms.

The bark and leaves also have historical uses in medicine and dye production.
