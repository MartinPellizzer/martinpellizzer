Moquilea longicuspidata

Moquilea longicuspidata, commonly known as the long-spined cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems covered in sharp, spiny tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its adaptations include thick, waxy cuticles and deep root systems to conserve water in its harsh environment.

Moquilea longicuspidata plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various desert wildlife.
