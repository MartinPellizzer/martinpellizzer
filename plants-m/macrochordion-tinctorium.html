Macrochordion tinctorium

Macrochordion tinctorium, commonly known as the red root or red root grass, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fibrous roots, which are rich in a red pigment called lycopodin. The plant grows in grasslands and open woodlands, often in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

The plant has been historically used for its dye-producing properties and as a traditional medicinal herb.
