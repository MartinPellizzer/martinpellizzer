Moringa drouhardii

Moringa drouhardii, commonly known as the African horseradish, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species.

The plant has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties and as a source of edible parts.
