Muscari transsilvanicum

Muscari transsilvanicum, commonly known as the Transylvanian grape hyacinth, is a species of bulbous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It produces dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, resembling grapes, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and is adapted to well-drained, sandy soils. It is a hardy perennial that thrives in USDA hardiness zones 4 to 8.

Muscari transsilvanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
