Mentha × rhenana

Mentha × rhenana, commonly known as the Hungarian mint, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale purple flowers. The plant is native to Central and Eastern Europe and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its medicinal and culinary uses due to its menthol-rich essential oils.

Mentha × rhenana is also valued in traditional herbal medicine for its cooling and antiseptic properties.
