Megaclinium buchenauianum

Megaclinium buchenauianum, commonly known as the Buchenau's giant grass, is a tall perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, stiff leaves and can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces panicles of small, inconspicuous flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its size and ecological role, it is sometimes used in restoration projects to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
