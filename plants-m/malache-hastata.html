Malache hastata

Malache hastata, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features grass-like leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shady woodlands and thrives in well-drained soil. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.

Malache hastata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
