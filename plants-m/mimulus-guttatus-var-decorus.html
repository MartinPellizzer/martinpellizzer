Mimulus guttatus var. decorus

Mimulus guttatus var. decorus, commonly known as the decorative yellow monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and rocky areas. It has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
