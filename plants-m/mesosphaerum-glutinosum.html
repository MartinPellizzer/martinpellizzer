Mesosphaerum glutinosum

Mesosphaerum glutinosum, commonly known as sticky mesosphaerum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malvaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that produce a sticky substance, which may serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typical of the Malvaceae family. It is native to certain regions of Africa and is often found in sandy or disturbed soils.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, it is of interest to botanists and ecologists studying plant resilience in arid environments.
