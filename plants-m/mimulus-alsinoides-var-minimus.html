Mimulus alsinoides var. minimus

Mimulus alsinoides var. minimus, commonly known as the dwarf yellow monkeyflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically reaches heights of less than 10 centimeters, with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
