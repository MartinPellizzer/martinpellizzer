Melica chilensis

Melica chilensis, commonly known as Chilean grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as meadows and riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Melica chilensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
