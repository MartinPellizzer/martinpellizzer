Myosotis sylvatica subsp. eusilvatica

Myosotis sylvatica subsp.

eusilvatica, commonly known as the forest forget-me-not, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly larger leaves and more robust growth compared to the typical *Myosotis sylvatica*. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of remembrance and enduring love.
