Mussaenda motleyi

Mussaenda motleyi, commonly known as the yellow trumpet bush, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has smooth, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement, and it produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for tropical gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant floral display and adaptability to warm climates.
