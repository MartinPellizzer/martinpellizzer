Melocactus caesius subsp. lobelii

Melocactus caesius subsp.

lobelii, commonly known as the Lobel's melon cactus, is a compact, cylindrical cactus native to the Andes of South America. It features a ribbed, spiny stem that is often covered in a dense layer of white, powdery bloom, which helps reduce water loss.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne near the top of the stem. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in full sun and well-draining soil.

Due to its striking appearance and unique morphology, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus collections.
