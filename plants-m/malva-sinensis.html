Malva sinensis

Malva sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese mallow, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in gardens and along stream banks.

Its fluffy seed pods release seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation.
