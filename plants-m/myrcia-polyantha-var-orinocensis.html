Myrcia polyantha var. orinocensis

Myrcia polyantha var. orinocensis, commonly known as the Orinoco myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America, particularly Venezuela and Colombia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when mature, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and aromatic properties, making it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
