Montia limosa

Montia limosa, commonly known as the bluish mountainweed, is a small flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Montiaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a bluish-green hue, which helps it blend into its natural surroundings.

Montia limosa is a short-lived perennial that plays a minor but ecologically important role in its native habitats.
