Mormodes citrina

Mormodes citrina, commonly known as the yellow star orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a star-shaped pattern, which are often adorned with contrasting purple or red markings. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
