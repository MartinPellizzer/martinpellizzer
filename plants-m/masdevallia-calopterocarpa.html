Masdevallia calopterocarpa

Masdevallia calopterocarpa, commonly known as the "Bamboo Orchid," is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, bamboo-like inflorescence and striking, colorful flowers that range from red to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows as a sympodial orchid, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are elongated and often covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Masdevallia calopterocarpa thrives in high humidity and cool temperatures, making it a popular choice for tropical greenhouse cultivation.
