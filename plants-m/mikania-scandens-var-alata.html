Mikania scandens var. alata

Mikania scandens var. alata, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a fast-growing vine native to the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white underside and produces small, fragrant, blue-violet flowers. The plant is characterized by its trailing habit and can climb or spread along the ground. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed areas.

Its rapid growth and invasive potential make it a common weed in gardens and natural habitats.
