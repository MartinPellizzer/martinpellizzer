Maxillaria equitans

Maxillaria equitans, commonly known as the saddle orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms during the dry season, with flowers appearing in clusters along the stem. Its leaves are linear and arranged in two rows, providing structural support.

Maxillaria equitans thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
