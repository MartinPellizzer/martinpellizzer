Mimulus constrictus

Mimulus constrictus, commonly known as the narrowleaf monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as streambanks and meadows. The plant features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are vibrant orange or yellow, with a distinctive hooded structure that resembles a monkey's face, giving it its common name.

Mimulus constrictus is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
