Mariscus microstachyus

Mariscus microstachyus, commonly known as the small-stalked mariscus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, narrow leaves and produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its botanical characteristics include a compact growth habit and a preference for well-drained, sandy soils.

It is often found in rocky or coastal areas, where it thrives in full sun.
