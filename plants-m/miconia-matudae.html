Miconia matudae

Miconia matudae, commonly known as the Matudae miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are often eaten by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
