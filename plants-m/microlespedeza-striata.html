Microlespedeza striata

Microlespedeza striata, commonly known as the hairy lespedeza, is a perennial legume native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Microlespedeza striata is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a useful plant in ecological restoration projects.
