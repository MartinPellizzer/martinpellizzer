Mimosa somnians var. velascoensis

Mimosa somnians var. velascoensis, commonly known as the velvet mimosa, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and adaptability contribute to its ecological and horticultural significance.
