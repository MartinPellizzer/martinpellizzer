Muraltia lewisiae

Muraltia lewisiae, commonly known as the California coffeeberry, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of California.

It is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible for humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to coastal gardens.

Muraltia lewisiae is also valued for its role in supporting local wildlife and its ornamental appeal.
