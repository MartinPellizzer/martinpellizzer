Mananthes pallida

Mananthes pallida, commonly known as the pale tawny sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Mananthes pallida is often found in association with other wetland plants, contributing to the ecological diversity of these environments.

Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
