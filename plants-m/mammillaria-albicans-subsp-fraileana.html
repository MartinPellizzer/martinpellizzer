Mammillaria albicans subsp. fraileana

Mammillaria albicans subsp.

fraileana, commonly known as the white mammillaria, is a small cactus native to Mexico. It features a globular stem covered in white, woolly spines that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
