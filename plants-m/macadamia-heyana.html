Macadamia heyana

Macadamia heyana, commonly known as the mountain macadamia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters in its natural habitat. Its fruit is a hard-shelled nut that contains a rich, oily kernel, which is valued for its culinary and commercial uses.

The species is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
