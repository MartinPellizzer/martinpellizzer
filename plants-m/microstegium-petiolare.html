Microstegium petiolare

Microstegium petiolare, commonly known as the slender-stemmed wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy and bear alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is typically found in moist, shaded woodlands and along stream banks.

Microstegium petiolare is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
