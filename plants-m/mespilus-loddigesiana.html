Mespilus loddigesiana

Mespilus loddigesiana, commonly known as the Persian medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its oval, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is a pome, typically resembling a small apple, with a thick, leathery skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils and its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
