Mespilus mollis

Mespilus mollis, commonly known as the common medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its oval, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit, which is a pomes, is typically harvested in late autumn and has a unique texture and flavor when fully ripe. The plant is often grown for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers a sunny to partially shaded location.
