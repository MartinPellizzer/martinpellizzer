Machilus glaucescens

Machilus glaucescens, commonly known as the glossy chinquapin, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are glossy on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by oval, woody fruits containing one or two seeds. Its bark is thick and reddish-brown, and it is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

Machilus glaucescens is valued for its ornamental appearance and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
