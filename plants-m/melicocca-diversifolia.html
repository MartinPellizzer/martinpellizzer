Melicocca diversifolia

Melicocca diversifolia, commonly known as the wild coffee tree, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in the understory of hardwood forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
