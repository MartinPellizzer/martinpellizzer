Maireana cheelii

Maireana cheelii, commonly known as the Cheel's ghost gum, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in spring. Its seeds are enclosed in woody capsules, and the plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Maireana cheelii is often found in sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience.
