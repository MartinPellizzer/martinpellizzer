Monocera isotricha

Monocera isotricha, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in wetland environments.

Monocera isotricha is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
