Malpighia imiensis

Malpighia imiensis, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet to purple berries that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often overlooked due to the prominent fruit. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for various birds and small mammals.
