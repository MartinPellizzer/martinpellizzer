Miconia quinquedentata

Miconia quinquedentata, commonly known as the five-tooth miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by distinctive, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs and have five prominent teeth along the margin, giving it its common name.

Miconia quinquedentata is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
