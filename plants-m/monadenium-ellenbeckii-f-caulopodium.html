Monadenium ellenbeckii f. caulopodium

Monadenium ellenbeckii f. caulopodium, commonly known as the woolly bladderwort, is a rare aquatic plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its submerged, succulent stems and small, bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic organisms. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often in marshes or along the edges of ponds. Its unique morphology allows it to function as a carnivorous plant by using its bladders to capture prey.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its specialized adaptations and limited distribution.
