Maianthemum fuscum

Maianthemum fuscum, commonly known as the brown false lily of the wall, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-yellow in color, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a dense rosette near the ground.

The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its preference for well-drained, acidic soils.
