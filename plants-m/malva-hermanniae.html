Malva hermanniae

Malva hermanniae, commonly known as Hermann's mallows, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus, and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring palmately lobed leaves and hairy stems. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Malva hermanniae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun.
