Millettia ovalifolia

Millettia ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf millettia, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing flat, dark brown seeds.

It is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appearance and potential use in traditional medicine.
