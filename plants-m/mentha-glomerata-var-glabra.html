Mentha glomerata var. glabra

Mentha glomerata var. glabra, commonly known as smooth horsemint, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically smooth on the upper surface. The plant produces small, clustered blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Smooth horsemint is valued for its aromatic leaves and is used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.
