Malus kansuensis var. calva

Malus kansuensis var. calva, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, yellowish fruits that are edible but not commonly cultivated for consumption. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
