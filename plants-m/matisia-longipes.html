Matisia longipes

Matisia longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed matisia, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its elongated, often pendulous fruits. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and is known for its ornamental value due to its striking, clustered inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
