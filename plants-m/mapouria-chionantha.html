Mapouria chionantha

Mapouria chionantha, commonly known as the snow-flower maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable ornamental plant for gardens.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both horticultural and ecological landscapes.
