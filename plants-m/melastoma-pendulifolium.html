Melastoma pendulifolium

Melastoma pendulifolium, commonly known as the hanging-leaf melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its distinctive pendulous leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture, while its flowers are clustered in inflorescences.

Melastoma pendulifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
