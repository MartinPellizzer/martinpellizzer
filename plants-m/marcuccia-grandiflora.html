Marcuccia grandiflora

Marcuccia grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered marsh gentian, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland habitats in Europe.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows up to 60 cm tall, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Marcuccia grandiflora is valued for its ornamental value and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
