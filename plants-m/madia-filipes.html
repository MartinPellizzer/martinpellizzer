Madia filipes

Madia filipes, commonly known as the slender madia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

Madia filipes is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and its ornamental value.
