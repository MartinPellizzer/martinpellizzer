Mollinedia campanulacea var. trianae

Mollinedia campanulacea var. trianae, commonly known as the Trianas' bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lanceolate, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces elongated, cylindrical seed capsules. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a suitable candidate for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a desirable addition to native plant collections.
