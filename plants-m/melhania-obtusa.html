Melhania obtusa

Melhania obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leafed melhania, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple. Melhania obtusa is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and floral display.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
