Microlepia scyphoformis

Microlepia scyphoformis, commonly known as the umbrella fern, is a tropical fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, umbrella-like fronds that are typically light green with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its fronds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Microlepia scyphoformis is valued for its elegant appearance and ease of care in indoor settings.
