Myrcia rufipes var. dives

Myrcia rufipes var. dives, commonly known as the red-stemmed myrtle, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
