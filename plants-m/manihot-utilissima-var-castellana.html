Manihot utilissima var. castellana

Manihot utilissima var. castellana, commonly known as cassava or yuca, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a perennial shrub that produces large, lobed leaves and develops edible tuberous roots underground. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its roots are rich in carbohydrates and are a staple food source in many tropical regions.

The plant also has a long history of cultivation and is economically significant in agriculture.
