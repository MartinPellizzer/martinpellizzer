Microchilus caramantae

Microchilus caramantae, commonly known as the Caramantae orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a short, rhizomatous stem and produces aerial roots that help it climb and anchor to rocky substrates. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and is often found in shaded, humid environments.

Its unique morphology and limited range make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
