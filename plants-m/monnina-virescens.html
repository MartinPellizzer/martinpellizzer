Monnina virescens

Monnina virescens, commonly known as greenleaf monnina, is a low-growing annual herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically features prostrate or ascending stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale yellow to reddish-purple, depending on the variety. Its leaves are usually elliptical or ovate, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin.

Monnina virescens is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.
