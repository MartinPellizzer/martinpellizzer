Matthiola varia subsp. valesiaca

Matthiola varia subsp.

valesiaca, commonly known as the Valesiaca stock, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of fragrant, four-petaled flowers.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing shades of white, pink, and purple. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value and ease of cultivation have made it a popular garden plant in temperate climates.
