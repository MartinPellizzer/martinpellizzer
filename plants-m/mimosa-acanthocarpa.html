Mimosa acanthocarpa

Mimosa acanthocarpa, commonly known as the woolly mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of small, yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant produces distinctive pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Mimosa acanthocarpa is adapted to withstand drought conditions and has a widespread distribution across several countries in South America.
