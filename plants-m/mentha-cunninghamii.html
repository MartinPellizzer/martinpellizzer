Mentha cunninghamii

Mentha cunninghamii, commonly known as Cunningham's mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves and square stems typical of the mint family, Lamiaceae. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Mentha cunninghamii is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Australian herbal remedies.
