Mentha × latifrons

Mentha × latifrons, commonly known as broad-leaf mint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Mentha × piperita and Mentha × spicata.

It is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy and have a strong, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in whorled clusters along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Mentha × latifrons is widely used in herbal teas and traditional medicine due to its menthol content and refreshing aroma.
