Micronychia striata

Micronychia striata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock fig, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are highly nutritious. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is often found in rocky habitats and is a valuable component of local biodiversity.
