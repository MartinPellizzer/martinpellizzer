Malus sieversii var. longicarpa

Malus sieversii var. longicarpa, commonly known as the long-sepaled apple, is a wild apple species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow sepals that persist on the fruit even after maturity. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and is known for its hardy nature and adaptability to harsh climates. Its leaves are usually oval with a serrated margin, and it produces small, edible apples that are often used in traditional cultivation.

This variety is considered a genetic ancestor of many cultivated apple varieties.
