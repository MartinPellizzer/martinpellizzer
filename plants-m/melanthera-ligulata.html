Melanthera ligulata

Melanthera ligulata, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, pinkish to purplish flowers that bloom in late summer. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

Melanthera ligulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
