Moraea niassensis

Moraea niassensis, commonly known as the Niassense gladiolus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, sword-shaped leaves and tall, showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
