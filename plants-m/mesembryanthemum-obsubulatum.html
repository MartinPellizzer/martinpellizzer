Mesembryanthemum obsubulatum

Mesembryanthemum obsubulatum, commonly known as the woolly ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves and stems. Its flowering period typically occurs during the cooler months, and it is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Mesembryanthemum obsubulatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to prevent soil erosion in dry landscapes.
