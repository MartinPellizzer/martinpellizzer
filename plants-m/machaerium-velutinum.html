Machaerium velutinum

Machaerium velutinum, commonly known as velvet machaerium, is a tropical perennial legume native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and the plant produces pods that contain several seeds.

Machaerium velutinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
