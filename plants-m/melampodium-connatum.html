Melampodium connatum

Melampodium connatum, commonly known as the yellow coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow petals and a distinctive, conical shape in its flower head. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and wetlands. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Melampodium connatum is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
