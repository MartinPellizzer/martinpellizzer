Mitostylis procumbens

Mitostylis procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate mitostylis, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across the ground. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale green, arranged in clusters along the stem. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slight hairy texture.

Mitostylis procumbens is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought conditions.
