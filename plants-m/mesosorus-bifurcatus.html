Mesosorus bifurcatus

Mesosorus bifurcatus, commonly known as the forked mesosorus fern, is a species of fern characterized by its unique sori arrangement.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features a rhizome with dichotomous branching, and its fronds are typically lanceolate in shape. The sori, which are clusters of sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds and are covered by a protective indusium.

This fern exhibits a variety of ecological adaptations, making it a notable species in its native habitats.
