Malacmaea fluminensis

Malacmaea fluminensis, commonly known as the Amazonian moon snail, is a species of sea snail found in the coastal waters of South America.

It belongs to the family Eulimidae and is characterized by its elongated, soft body and a spiral shell that is often covered in a calcareous layer. The snail's radula, a ribbon-like structure used for feeding, is adapted for scraping algae and microorganisms from surfaces. It plays a role in marine ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and serving as prey for various predators.

Due to habitat degradation, it faces threats to its survival in its native range.
