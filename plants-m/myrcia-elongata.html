Myrcia elongata

Myrcia elongata, commonly known as the longleaf myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a distinct aromatic scent when crushed.

The flowers are usually pink or white, and the fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, dark seeds.
