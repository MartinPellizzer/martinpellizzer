Marsilea trichopoda

Marsilea trichopoda, commonly known as the water clover, is a small aquatic fern that thrives in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its distinctive four-leaf clover-like foliage, which is often submerged or floating on the water surface. The plant has slender, branching rhizomes that help it spread and anchor in wet soil or muddy substrates. Its spores are released into the water, allowing for reproduction through sexual means.

Marsilea trichopoda is often found in ponds, ditches, and slow-moving streams, where it plays a role in aquatic ecosystems.
