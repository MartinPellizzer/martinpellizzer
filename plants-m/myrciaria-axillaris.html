Myrciaria axillaris

Myrciaria axillaris, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *mamoeiro*, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are small, red, and edible, often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
