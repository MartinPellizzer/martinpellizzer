Mahonia oiwakensis subsp. lomariifolia

Mahonia oiwakensis subsp.

lomariifolia, commonly known as the Hawaiian mahonia, is a shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands. It features evergreen foliage with leathery, elliptic to ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter beneath.

The plant produces yellow flowers in winter, which are clustered in upright panicles. These flowers are followed by blue to black berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded habitats on mountain slopes.
