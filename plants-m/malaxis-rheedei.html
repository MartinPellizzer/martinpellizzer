Malaxis rheedei

Malaxis rheedei, commonly known as the orchid of the genus Malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its roots are fibrous, and it produces a single, narrow leaf.

Malaxis rheedei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
