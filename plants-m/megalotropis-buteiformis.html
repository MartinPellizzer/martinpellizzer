Megalotropis buteiformis

Megalotropis buteiformis, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and bright yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its succulent leaves and deep root system. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that allow it to colonize new areas efficiently.

Due to its resilience, it is often used in ecological restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
