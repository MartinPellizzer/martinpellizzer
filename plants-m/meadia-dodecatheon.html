Meadia dodecatheon

Meadia dodecatheon, commonly known as the Christmas flower or the twelve-fruit plant, is a member of the Primulaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States, typically found in woodland habitats. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in early spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous small, dry seeds.

The plant's name reflects its historical association with Christmas and its traditional use in herbal medicine.
