Moscatella

Moscatella, commonly known as muscat grape, is a type of grapevine native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, sweet, and aromatic berries that are often used in winemaking and culinary applications. The plant produces clusters of fruit with a distinctive musky fragrance. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a slightly serrated edge.

Moscatella is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
