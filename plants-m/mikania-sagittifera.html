Mikania sagittifera

Mikania sagittifera, commonly known as the arrowleaf mikania, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its small yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and are followed by distinctive, elongated seed pods.

Mikania sagittifera is often found in disturbed habitats and can be invasive in certain regions.
