Mirabilis pedunculata

Mirabilis pedunculata, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that open at night and emit a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant produces showy, waxy petals that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Mirabilis pedunculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming habit.
