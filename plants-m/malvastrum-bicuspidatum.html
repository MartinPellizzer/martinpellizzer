Malvastrum bicuspidatum

Malvastrum bicuspidatum, commonly known as the two-lobed mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus, and is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, pale pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
