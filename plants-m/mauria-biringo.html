Mauria biringo

Mauria biringo, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture, while its stems are erect and somewhat woody.

Mauria biringo is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
