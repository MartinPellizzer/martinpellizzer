Marinellia

Marinellia, commonly known as the sea hibiscus, is a genus of flowering plants in the mallow family, Malvaceae.

It is native to coastal regions of the Americas, particularly in tropical and subtropical areas. The plants are characterized by their showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. They often have rough, hairy leaves and thrive in saline or coastal environments.

Marinellia species are often used in ornamental gardening due to their vibrant blooms and adaptability to coastal conditions.
