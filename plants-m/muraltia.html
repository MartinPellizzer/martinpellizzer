Muraltia

Muraltia, commonly known as the red mombin or muraltia tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Caesalpiniaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range from white to pink or red. The plant produces small, round, yellow or orange fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste.

Muraltia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit, which can be used in traditional medicine or as a food source.
