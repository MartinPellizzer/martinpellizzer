Mimosa murex

Mimosa murex, commonly known as the pink mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces showy, pink to purple pea-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Mimosa murex is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
