Magydaris pastinacea subsp. panacifolia

Magydaris pastinacea subsp.

panacifolia, commonly known as the panacifolia bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers in late spring.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly larger and more elongated leaves compared to the typical form of Magydaris pastinacea.
