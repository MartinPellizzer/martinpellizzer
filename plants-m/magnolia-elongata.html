Magnolia elongata

Magnolia elongata, commonly known as the long-leaf magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The tree produces large, showy white flowers with a subtle fragrance, typically blooming in early spring. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
