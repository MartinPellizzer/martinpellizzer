Millingtonia clematis

Millingtonia clematis, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy soils.

The botanical features of Millingtonia clematis make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and native plant landscapes.
