Meliosma pilosa

Meliosma pilosa, commonly known as the hairy meliosma, is a species of flowering plant in the family Meliaceae.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are densely covered with short, stiff hairs, giving it a distinctive fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically found in the axils of the leaves. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Meliosma pilosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
