Malope stipulacea var. stellipilis

Malope stipulacea var. stellipilis, commonly known as the star-petaled malope, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble clusters of small stars, giving it its common name. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with prominent stipules, which are leaf-like structures at the base of the leaf stalks. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
