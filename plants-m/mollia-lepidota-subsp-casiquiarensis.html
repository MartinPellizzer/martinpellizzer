Mollia lepidota subsp.

casiquiarensis, commonly known as the Casiquiarensis mollia, is a unique plant species native to the tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology, featuring broad, ovate leaves with a waxy surface that aids in water retention.

The plant exhibits a climbing habit, often utilizing its tendrils to ascend support structures in its natural habitat. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically displaying a pale green to white coloration, which attracts specific pollinators.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in the understory of humid forests, contributing to biodiversity and nutrient cycling.
