Mentha × schultziana var. muelleriana

Mentha × schultziana var. muelleriana, commonly known as the Mueller's mint, is a hybrid mint species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows. It has a creeping growth habit with rhizomes that allow it to spread and form dense mats.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
