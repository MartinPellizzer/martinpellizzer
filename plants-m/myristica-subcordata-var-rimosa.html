Myristica subcordata var. rimosa

Myristica subcordata var. rimosa, commonly known as the fragrant nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the islands of Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody fruits that contain the spice nutmeg. The plant has glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by the distinctive, egg-shaped fruits. The tree is valued for its aromatic seeds, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both horticulture and the spice trade.
