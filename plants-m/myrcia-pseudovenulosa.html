Myrcia pseudovenulosa

Myrcia pseudovenulosa, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, dark blue berries that are edible.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
