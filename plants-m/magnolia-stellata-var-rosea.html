Magnolia stellata var. rosea

Magnolia stellata var. rosea, commonly known as the rose-budded magnolia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its showy, pinkish-white flowers that resemble starbursts, which bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The cultivar is prized for its early blooming habit and delicate, fragrant flowers.
