Menziesia globularis

Menziesia globularis, commonly known as the globular waxplant, is a flowering shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical growth habit and hairy, glandular leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Menziesia globularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
