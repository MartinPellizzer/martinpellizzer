Myristica bracteata

Myristica bracteata, commonly known as the nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its dense, glossy green foliage and fragrant flowers. The tree produces a unique fruit that contains the aromatic seeds used for nutmeg and mace. Its botanical features include a thick, woody trunk and compound leaves with a pinnate structure.

The plant is valued not only for its economic importance but also for its ecological role in tropical rainforests.
