Morinda citrifolia

Morinda citrifolia, commonly known as noni, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, with simple, alternate, ovate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by distinctive purple or white berries. Its fruit contains a gelatinous pulp with a strong, fermented odor and is rich in bioactive compounds.

The botanical features of Morinda citrifolia make it a subject of interest in both traditional medicine and modern scientific research.
