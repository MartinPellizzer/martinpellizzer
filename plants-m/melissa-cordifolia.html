Melissa cordifolia

Melissa cordifolia, commonly known as lemon balm, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features fragrant, small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are heart-shaped, hence the species name *cordifolia*. The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage, which emits a citrus-like scent when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, often found in moist meadows and woodlands.

Melissa cordifolia is widely cultivated for its calming properties and use in herbal remedies.
