Mycetia griffithii

Mycetia griffithii, commonly known as the Indian velvet tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its hardy nature.
