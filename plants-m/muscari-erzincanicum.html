Muscari erzincanicum

Muscari erzincanicum, commonly known as the Erzincan musk mallow, is a bulbous perennial plant native to Turkey.

It produces dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has strap-like leaves that are grassy in appearance and are often used in rock gardens or as ground cover. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.

Muscari erzincanicum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize over time.
