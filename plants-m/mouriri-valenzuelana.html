Mouriri valenzuelana

Mouriri valenzuelana, commonly known as the Brazilian sour orange, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are rich in vitamin C and have a tart flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The bark and leaves contain bioactive compounds that have been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
