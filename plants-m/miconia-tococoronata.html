Miconia tococoronata

Miconia tococoronata, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a member of the Melastomataceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. Its flowers are showy, with five pink or red petals and a distinctive yellow center.

Miconia tococoronata is also known for its ability to produce a large number of seeds, which can contribute to its invasive potential in certain regions.
