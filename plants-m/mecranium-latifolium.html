Mecranium latifolium

Mecranium latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf mecranium, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
