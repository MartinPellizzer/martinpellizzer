Monardella perplexans

Monardella perplexans, commonly known as the California monardella, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Monardella perplexans is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs.
