Montbretia miniata

Montbretia miniata, commonly known as the dwarf oxalis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright orange or red flowers, which bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with distinctive markings. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Montbretia miniata is also known for its ability to thrive in dry conditions, showcasing its adaptability to various climates.
