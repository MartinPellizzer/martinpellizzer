Mimosa distachya var. chamelae

Mimosa distachya var. chamelae, commonly known as the velvet mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and delicate, fern-like foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to spread, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
