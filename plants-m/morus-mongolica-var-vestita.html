Morus mongolica var. vestita

Morus mongolica var. vestita, commonly known as the Mongolian mulberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
