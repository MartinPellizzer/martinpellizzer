Maxillaria crassipes

Maxillaria crassipes, commonly known as the broad-leafed maxillaria, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to its often dry habitat. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom during the dry season. Its roots are epiphytic, meaning they grow on other plants rather than in soil.

Maxillaria crassipes is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
