Mentha laevis

Mentha laevis, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and small, pale blue or violet flowers. Native to regions of Europe and western Asia, it thrives in moist, shady environments. The plant is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and other damp habitats.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
