Mentha aquatica f. piperita

Mentha aquatica f. piperita, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a subspecies of Mentha aquatica, characterized by its aromatic, slightly minty scent and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. Its flowering stems bear small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

Mentha aquatica f. piperita is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for its cooling and digestive properties.
