Monsonia ovata subsp. glauca

Monsonia ovata subsp.

glauca, commonly known as the blue glaucous monsonia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and features blue-green foliage that gives it its common name.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
