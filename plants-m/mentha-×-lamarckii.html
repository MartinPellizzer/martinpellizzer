Mentha × lamarckii

Mentha × lamarckii, commonly known as apple mint, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Mentha piperita (peppermint) and Mentha × gracilis (wild mint).

It is a perennial herb characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated edge. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stem, typically in late summer. Its aromatic leaves emit a fresh, minty scent when crushed, making it popular in culinary and medicinal applications.

Mentha × lamarckii is native to Europe and has been widely cultivated for its aromatic properties and ornamental value.
