Myrobalanus citrina

Myrobalanus citrina, commonly known as the yellow mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, yellowish fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

The tree is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
