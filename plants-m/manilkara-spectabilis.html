Manilkara spectabilis

Manilkara spectabilis, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, glossy dark green leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The tree's most notable feature is its hard, woody fruit, which contains the edible seeds known as Brazil nuts. These seeds are rich in nutrients, particularly selenium, and are commercially valuable.

The plant plays a crucial role in the ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
