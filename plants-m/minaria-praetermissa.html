Minaria praetermissa

Minaria praetermissa, commonly known as the common minaria, is a small, annual plant belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The flowers are tiny, greenish-white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, often resembling a tuft of hair. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

This plant is adapted to survive in harsh conditions with its deep root system and ability to conserve water.
