Mentha arvensis var. deflexa

Mentha arvensis var. deflexa, commonly known as narrow-leaf field mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves that are often curled and have a distinct minty fragrance. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and woodland edges.

Its essential oils contain menthol and other terpenoids, making it valuable in traditional and modern herbal applications.
