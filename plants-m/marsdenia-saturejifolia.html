Marsdenia saturejifolia

Marsdenia saturejifolia, commonly known as the rock fig, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by fleshy, fig-like fruits. It thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
