Malaxis cuneipetala

Malaxis cuneipetala, commonly known as the narrow-leafed malaxis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, greenish-white flowers. Native to Southeast Asia, this plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments. The leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and the flowers are clustered in a raceme.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
