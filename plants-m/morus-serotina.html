Morus serotina

Morus serotina, commonly known as the late mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The tree produces small, black berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and riparian areas.

Morus serotina is valued for its ecological role in supporting local fauna and its historical use in traditional medicine.
