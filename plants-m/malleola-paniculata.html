Malleola paniculata

Malleola paniculata, commonly known as the clustered panicled grass, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, branched inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered in panicles.

Malleola paniculata is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive, airy appearance.
