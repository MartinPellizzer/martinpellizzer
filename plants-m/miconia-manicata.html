Miconia manicata

Miconia manicata, commonly known as the giant miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains numerous seeds.

Miconia manicata is known for its rapid growth and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.
