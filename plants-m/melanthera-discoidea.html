Melanthera discoidea

Melanthera discoidea, commonly known as the white turtlehead, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, turtle-like flower heads that are typically white or pale purple in color. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as wetlands and stream banks.

Melanthera discoidea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
