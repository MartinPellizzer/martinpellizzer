Malus mandshurica var. sachalinensis

Malus mandshurica var. sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin apple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Korean Peninsula and eastern Russia.

It produces small, edible apples that are typically red or yellow in color, with a tart flavor. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

This variety is also valued for its hardiness and adaptability to different soil types.
