Myristica fatua var. spanogheana

Myristica fatua var. spanogheana, commonly known as the Malabar nut or wild nutmeg, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown seeds that resemble nutmeg. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by oval-shaped fruits that contain the valuable seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while its stems are woody and often branched.

The species is noted for its potential medicinal properties and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic seeds.
