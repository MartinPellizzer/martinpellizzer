Malus communis var. rosea

Malus communis var. rosea, commonly known as the pink-flowered apple tree, is a cultivar of the common apple tree.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring pinkish-red blossoms that appear in early spring. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a dark green hue. This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal rather than for fruit production.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
