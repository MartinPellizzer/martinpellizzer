Myrtus aromatica

Myrtus aromatica, commonly known as sweet myrtle, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that are often used in traditional herbal remedies. Its aromatic leaves and berries are also used in perfumes and culinary applications.

Myrtus aromatica thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and ornamental addition to gardens.
