Macrolobium pachyanthum

Macrolobium pachyanthum, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forest understories.

The fruit is a capsule that releases seeds when mature, contributing to its propagation in natural habitats.
