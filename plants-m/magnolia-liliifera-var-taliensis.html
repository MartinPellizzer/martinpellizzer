Magnolia liliifera var. taliensis

Magnolia liliifera var. taliensis, commonly known as the Taliensis magnolia, is a rare and ornamental evergreen tree native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are broad, leathery, and have a distinctive waxy texture. This variety is part of the Magnoliaceae family and is noted for its unique morphology and ecological significance.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
