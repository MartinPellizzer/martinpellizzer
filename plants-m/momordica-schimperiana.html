Momordica schimperiana

Momordica schimperiana, commonly known as the African bitter melon, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and pumpkins. The plant produces distinctive yellow flowers and elongated, spiny fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and have a strong, bitter aroma.

The fruit contains a white, fibrous pulp with small, edible seeds, and it is valued for its medicinal properties in various African cultures.
