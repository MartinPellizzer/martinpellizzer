Malpighia megacantha

Malpighia megacantha, commonly known as the giant passionfruit, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Malpighiaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers and edible, orange-colored fruits. The plant produces large, fleshy berries that contain numerous seeds, making it a valuable source of nutrition in its native habitat. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a rough, textured surface, while its stems are woody and often branched.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments, contributing to its ecological significance in tropical forests.
