Moricandia perfoliata

Moricandia perfoliata, commonly known as the perfoliate moricandia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves that are often described as having a "perfoliate" arrangement, where the leaves appear to pass through the stems of adjacent leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Moricandia perfoliata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
