Minuartia umbellulifera subsp. pontica

Minuartia umbellulifera subsp.

pontica, commonly known as the Pontic rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Caucasus region. It thrives in rocky and alpine environments, often found on slopes and cliffs.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapted to withstand harsh mountain conditions.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, known for its characteristic cross-shaped flowers and peppery scent.
