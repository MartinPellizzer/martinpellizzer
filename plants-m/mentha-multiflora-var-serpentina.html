Mentha multiflora var. serpentina

Mentha multiflora var. serpentina, commonly known as serpentgrass, is a variant of the mint family native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its creeping growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, making it useful in traditional herbal medicine.
