Mimulus ringens f. albiflorus

Mimulus ringens f. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered yellow water lily, is a variant of the common yellow water lily.

It is characterized by its pale or white flowers, which contrast with the typical yellow of the species. This plant is native to North America and thrives in shallow freshwater habitats. Its flowers are typically borne on long, slender stalks above the water surface.

The botanical features include heart-shaped leaves and a distinctive, showy floral structure that attracts pollinators.
