Miconia melanodesma

Miconia melanodesma, commonly known as the black-leaved miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that are often marked with silver or white veins. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences with white or pale pink flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature, contributing to its spread in suitable habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in warm climates, though it can become invasive in certain areas.
