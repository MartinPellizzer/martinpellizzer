Merkusia microcarpa

Merkusia microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of specific host trees.

It has small, scale-like leaves and produces tiny, white or pink flowers that are usually clustered together. The plant relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing some photosynthesis. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that feed on its fleshy, red berries.

This species is found primarily in the southeastern United States and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its forest habitats.
