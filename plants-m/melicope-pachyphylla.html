Melicope pachyphylla

Melicope pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved melicope, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces greenish-yellow berries that are edible but not widely consumed by humans. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland forests.

Melicope pachyphylla plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
