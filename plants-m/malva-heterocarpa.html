Malva heterocarpa

Malva heterocarpa, commonly known as the narrowleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a serrated edge. The plant produces showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its fruit is a dry capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

Malva heterocarpa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
