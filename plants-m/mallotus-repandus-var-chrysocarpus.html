Mallotus repandus var. chrysocarpus

Mallotus repandus var. chrysocarpus, commonly known as golden-fruited mallotus, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by distinctive yellow or orange fruits, which are a key feature of the variety. Its fruiting habit makes it a valuable species in ecological and ornamental contexts.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
