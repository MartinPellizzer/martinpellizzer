Malpighia macracantha

Malpighia macracantha, commonly known as the large-fruited physalis, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malpighiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its distinctive, inflated calyx that encloses the fruit. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

The leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a slightly hairy surface, and the flowers are typically pink or red, attracting various pollinators.
