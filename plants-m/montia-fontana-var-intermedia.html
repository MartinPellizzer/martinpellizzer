Montia fontana var. intermedia

Montia fontana var. intermedia, commonly known as the Intermediate Watercress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Montiaceae family and is characterized by its small, succulent leaves and delicate white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and mountain meadows. It has a low-growing habit with prostrate stems that often root at the nodes.

The leaves are usually alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a slightly serrated margin.
