Manihot gossypiifolia

Manihot gossypiifolia, commonly known as the woolly arrowhead, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, humid environments.
