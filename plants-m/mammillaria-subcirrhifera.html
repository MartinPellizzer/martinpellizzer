Mammillaria subcirrhifera

Mammillaria subcirrhifera, commonly known as the woolly mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, white, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and xeriscaping.
