Miscanthus formosanus

Miscanthus formosanus, commonly known as the Taiwan silver grass, is a perennial grass native to Taiwan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and fluffy, silver-white plumes that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as riverbanks and meadows. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom.

This ornamental grass is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in landscaping and garden design.
