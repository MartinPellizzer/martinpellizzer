Megalastrum spectabile var. spectabile

Megalastrum spectabile var. spectabile, commonly known as the showy mountain mint, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, woodland soils.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to native gardens.
