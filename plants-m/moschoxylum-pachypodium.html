Moschoxylum pachypodium

Moschoxylum pachypodium, commonly known as the thick-stemmed milk bush, is a unique succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, white or yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its leaves are usually reduced to spines, which help minimize water loss.

Moschoxylum pachypodium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscapes due to its low water requirements.
