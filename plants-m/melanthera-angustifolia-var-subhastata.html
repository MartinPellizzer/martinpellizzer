Melanthera angustifolia var. subhastata

Melanthera angustifolia var. subhastata, commonly known as narrowleaf melanthera, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its small, white flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.
