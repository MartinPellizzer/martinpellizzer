Machilus odoratissimus

Machilus odoratissimus, commonly known as the fragrant machilus, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which emit a subtle, spicy scent. It typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical forests.

Machilus odoratissimus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
