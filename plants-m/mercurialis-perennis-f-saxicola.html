Mercurialis perennis f. saxicola

Mercurialis perennis f. saxicola, commonly known as the saxicoline mercury.

This variant is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils, often found in alpine or subalpine regions. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy surface.

It is noted for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient member of its ecological community.
