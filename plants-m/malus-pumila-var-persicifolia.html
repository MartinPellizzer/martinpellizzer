Malus pumila var. persicifolia

Malus pumila var. persicifolia, commonly known as the Japanese flowering cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, particularly during spring when it produces clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that are usually green and turn yellow in autumn. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and parks.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
