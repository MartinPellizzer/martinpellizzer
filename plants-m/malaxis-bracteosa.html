Malaxis bracteosa

Malaxis bracteosa, commonly known as the bracted malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Like many orchids, it has a complex relationship with mycorrhizal fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption.
