Myoporum oppositifolium

Myoporum oppositifolium, commonly known as the New Zealand myoporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a popular choice for landscaping in coastal and semi-arid regions.

The plant is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
