Mangifera campnospermoides

Mangifera campnospermoides, commonly known as the Philippine mango, is a tropical tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and fragrant, yellow-orange fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of 10-15 meters, with a dense canopy that provides shade. Its fruit is edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, making it a valuable crop in local agriculture.

The tree also plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
