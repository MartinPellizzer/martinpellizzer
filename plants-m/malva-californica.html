Malva californica

Malva californica, commonly known as the California mallows, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems, broad heart-shaped leaves, and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
