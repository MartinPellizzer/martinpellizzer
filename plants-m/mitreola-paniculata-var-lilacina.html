Mitreola paniculata var. lilacina

Mitreola paniculata var. lilacina, commonly known as the lilac morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep lilac, blooming throughout the warmer months. These flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
