Mentha longifolia var. tenuior

Mentha longifolia var. tenuior, commonly known as narrow-leaf spearmint, is a variant of the spearmint species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, pointed leaves and a strong, minty aroma. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its flowering stems bear small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is often used in culinary and aromatic applications due to its distinct flavor profile.
