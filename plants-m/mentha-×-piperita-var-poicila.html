Mentha × piperita var. poicila

Mentha × piperita var. poicila, commonly known as the purple mint, is a hybrid mint species characterized by its distinctive purple flower spikes.

It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically growing in moist, shady environments. The plant features opposite, serrated leaves that are aromatic when crushed, and its flowers are clustered in dense, terminal spikes. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a mild mint scent compared to common spearmint.

This variety is also used in some traditional herbal remedies.
