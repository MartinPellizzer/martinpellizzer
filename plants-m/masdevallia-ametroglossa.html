Masdevallia ametroglossa

Masdevallia ametroglossa, commonly known as the narrow-lipped masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and its striking, colorful flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features long, slender leaves and produces intricate, spurred petals that are typical of the orchid family. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is a prized specimen among orchid enthusiasts.
