Melia azedarach var. biglandulosa

Melia azedarach var. biglandulosa, commonly known as the Chinese catalpa, is a deciduous tree native to Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn, and it produces long, pendulous clusters of white or cream flowers in the spring. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, ridged bark and the presence of large, showy fruit pods that split open to release seeds. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to various parts of the world for landscaping purposes.

The variety is noted for its adaptability to different soil types and its ability to thrive in a range of climatic conditions.
