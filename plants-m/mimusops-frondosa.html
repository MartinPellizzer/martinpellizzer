Mimusops frondosa

Mimusops frondosa, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy green foliage and distinctive, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round, yellow fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry forests.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes in warm climates.
