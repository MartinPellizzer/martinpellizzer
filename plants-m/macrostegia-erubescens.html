Macrostegia erubescens

Macrostegia erubescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed ironwood, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is a small shrub characterized by its woody stems and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive reddish hue when young, which fades to a darker green as they mature.

The species is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
