Malpighia cnide var. ovalis

Malpighia cnide var. ovalis, commonly known as the sour sop or granadilla, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malpighiaceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean and parts of South America, where it thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant produces large, egg-shaped fruits with a greenish-yellow exterior and a soft, juicy interior containing a sweet and tangy pulp. Its flowers are small, clustered, and typically white or pink, while its leaves are broad and ovate with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is valued not only for its edible fruit but also for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
