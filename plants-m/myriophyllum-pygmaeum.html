Myriophyllum pygmaeum

Myriophyllum pygmaeum, commonly known as dwarf watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It has slender, branching stems with small, opposite leaves that are often ribbon-like in appearance. The plant produces tiny, reddish-brown flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as lakes and ponds.

Myriophyllum pygmaeum plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
