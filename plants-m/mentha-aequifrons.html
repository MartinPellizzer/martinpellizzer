Mentha aequifrons

Mentha aequifrons, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters during the summer months. Mentha aequifrons is known for its aromatic, mint-like scent and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
