Malpighia angustifolia var. oblongata

Malpighia angustifolia var. oblongata, commonly known as the narrowleaf acerola, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a small, shrub-like plant that produces small, red, edible berries similar to cherries. The leaves are typically narrow and elliptical, with a glossy surface and serrated edges. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and the nutritional value of its fruits.
