Mollugo cordifolia

Mollugo cordifolia, commonly known as heartleaf mosspewer, is a low-growing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Molluginaceae and is characterized by its small, fleshy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in groups of three, typical of the genus Mollugo. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woods.

The species is notable for its ability to spread rapidly through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
