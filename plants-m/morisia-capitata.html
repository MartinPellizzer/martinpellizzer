Morisia capitata

Morisia capitata, commonly known as the golden daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and saline conditions. It produces yellow petals that are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Morisia capitata is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant color and hardy nature.
