Mammillaria pacifica

Mammillaria pacifica, commonly known as the Pacific hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is a small, spineless cactus with a globular shape and is often covered in white to yellowish woolly hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
