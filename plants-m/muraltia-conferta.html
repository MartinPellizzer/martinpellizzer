Muraltia conferta

Muraltia conferta, commonly known as the sweet acacia, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features compound leaves with three to five pairs of oval-shaped leaflets, giving it a lush, green appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by bean-like pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.

Muraltia conferta is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
