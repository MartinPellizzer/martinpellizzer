Melothria assamica var. scabra

Melothria assamica var. scabra, commonly known as the rough or hairy bitter gourd, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The fruit is typically elongated, with a rough, textured surface and a bitter taste. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its unique flavor and nutritional value.
