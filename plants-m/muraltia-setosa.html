Muraltia setosa

Muraltia setosa, commonly known as the hairy muraltia, is a species of flowering plant in the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several small, edible seeds.

Muraltia setosa is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
