Mimosa incana

Mimosa incana, commonly known as the hairy mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Mimosa incana is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
