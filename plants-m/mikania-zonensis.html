Mikania zonensis

Mikania zonensis, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple and white. The plant has a rapid growth rate and can become invasive in certain environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Mikania zonensis is often found in moist, sunny habitats such as forests and disturbed areas.
