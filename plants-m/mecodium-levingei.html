Mecodium levingei

Mecodium levingei, commonly known as the Cape orchid, is a striking perennial herb native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, orchid-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces intricate, bilaterally symmetrical flowers with a distinctive hooded labellum. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense clumps.

Mecodium levingei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
