Moringa stenopetala

Moringa stenopetala, commonly known as the Ethiopian Moringa, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia and parts of Eritrea.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stem and compound leaves that are alternate and pinnately arranged. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by elliptical, woody pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, making it a resilient species adapted to harsh environments.

Moringa stenopetala is valued for its nutritional properties and has traditional uses in local communities.
