Mahonia monodens

Mahonia monodens, commonly known as the narrowleaf mahonia, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are typically narrow and have a leathery texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are followed by blue-black berries in the fall. Mahonia monodens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.

It is also valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil conditions.
