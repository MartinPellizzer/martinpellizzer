Maxillariella tenuifolia

Maxillariella tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender-leafed orchid, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid with small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a compact growth habit, with narrow, grass-like leaves that form a rosette. It prefers shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
