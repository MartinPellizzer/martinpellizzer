Munbya

Munbya, commonly known as *Barringtonia asiatica*, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive lantern-shaped fruit, which is covered in a fibrous, woody husk. The plant has large, palmate leaves and produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom at night. The fruit contains a single seed enclosed in a fleshy, yellowish pulp.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in medicine and cuisine.
