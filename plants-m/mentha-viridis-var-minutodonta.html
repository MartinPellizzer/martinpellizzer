Mentha viridis var. minutodonta

Mentha viridis var. minutodonta, commonly known as narrow-leaved mint, is a variant of the green mint species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and a strong, aromatic scent similar to other mint species. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or along stream banks. Its flowering stems bear small, tubular flowers that are usually greenish-white in color.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in herbal teas and topical applications.
