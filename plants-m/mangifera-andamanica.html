Mangifera andamanica

Mangifera andamanica, commonly known as the Andaman mango, is a tropical fruit tree native to the Andaman Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It produces large, elongated mangoes with a sweet and aromatic flavor, often used in local cuisines. The plant has a dense, evergreen canopy with compound leaves that are alternate and pinnate. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters.

The fruit develops from an inferior ovary and is encased in a thick, fibrous rind.
