Macrocarpaea papillosa

Macrocarpaea papillosa, commonly known as the silky macrocarpa, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
