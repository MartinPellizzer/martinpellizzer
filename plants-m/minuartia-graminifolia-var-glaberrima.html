Minuartia graminifolia var. glaberrima

Minuartia graminifolia var. glaberrima, commonly known as the glabrous rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically has a mat-forming growth habit with prostrate stems and small, linear leaves. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, blooming in early summer.
