Microparacaryum intermedium f. mattiastroides

Microparacaryum intermedium f. mattiastroides, commonly known as the hybrid rockcress, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale yellow. The leaves are linear and often covered in a fine layer of pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.

Its botanical interest lies in its morphological traits and its role as a hybrid in plant taxonomy.
