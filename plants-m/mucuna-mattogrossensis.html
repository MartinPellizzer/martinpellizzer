Mucuna mattogrossensis

Mucuna mattogrossensis, commonly known as the Amazon velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, purple-pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Mucuna mattogrossensis is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, contributing to soil fertility.
