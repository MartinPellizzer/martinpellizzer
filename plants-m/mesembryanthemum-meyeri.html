Mesembryanthemum meyeri

Mesembryanthemum meyeri, commonly known as the carpetweed or ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in the early morning. It is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and ability to spread rapidly.

Mesembryanthemum meyeri is also valued for its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
