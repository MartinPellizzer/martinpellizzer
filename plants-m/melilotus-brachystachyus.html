Melilotus brachystachyus

Melilotus brachystachyus, commonly known as narrow-stemmed sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Melilotus brachystachyus is also valued for its use in agriculture as a forage crop and for its potential in soil improvement.
