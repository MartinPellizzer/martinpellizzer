Mikania oopetala

Mikania oopetala, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in the morning. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Mikania oopetala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
