Mammillaria peninsularis

Mammillaria peninsularis, commonly known as the Peninsular Hedgehog Cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a dense covering of white, woolly bristles. The plant typically grows to a height of about 5 to 10 centimeters and features prominent areoles from which both spines and flowers emerge. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or orange, blooming in spring or early summer.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
