Mammillaria gracilis

Mammillaria gracilis, commonly known as the slender mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems covered in small, white spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its delicate appearance, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in rock gardens or indoor settings.
