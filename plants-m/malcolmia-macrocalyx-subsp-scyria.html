Malcolmia macrocalyx subsp. scyria

Malcolmia macrocalyx subsp.

scyria, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It features hairy, branched stems that grow up to 30 cm in height.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue to violet in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine pubescence.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
