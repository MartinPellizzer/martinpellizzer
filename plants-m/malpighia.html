Malpighia

Malpighia, commonly known as the acerola cherry or wolfberry, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Malpighiaceae.

These plants are native to the Americas and are characterized by their small, bright red berries that resemble cherries. The leaves are typically alternate, simple, and have a distinctive veinal pattern. The flowers are showy, with five petals and numerous stamens, and they often have a strong, sweet scent.
