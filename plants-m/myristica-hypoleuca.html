Myristica hypoleuca

Myristica hypoleuca, commonly known as the white nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Moluccas and other parts of Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody fruits that contain the spice nutmeg. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by capsule-like fruits, which mature to a yellowish color. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a glossy surface, while its roots are known for their medicinal properties.

The tree is valued not only for its nutmeg but also for its essential oils and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
