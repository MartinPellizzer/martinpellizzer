Mentha aquatica subsp. lloydii

Mentha aquatica subsp.

lloydii, commonly known as Lloyd's water mint, is a subspecies of the water mint plant native to North America. It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers.

This perennial herb thrives in moist, often aquatic environments such as marshes and stream banks. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies, and it is valued for its distinct menthol-like scent.

The plant plays an important role in supporting local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
