Malaxis verticillata var. khasiana

Malaxis verticillata var. khasiana, commonly known as the Khasi Malaxis, is a rare orchid species native to the northeastern region of India.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, pale green to yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories, thriving in humid and cool environments. Its leaves are arranged in a whorled pattern, and the inflorescence is often short and dense.

This orchid is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
