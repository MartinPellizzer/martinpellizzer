Myrcia pullei

Myrcia pullei, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, hard seeds.

Myrcia pullei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
