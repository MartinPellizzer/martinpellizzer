Monnina subserrata

Monnina subserrata, commonly known as the scarlet globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are often lobed or toothed, contributing to its unique appearance. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

The fruit is a dry capsule that releases small, wind-dispersed seeds.
