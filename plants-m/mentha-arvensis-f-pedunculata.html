Mentha arvensis f. pedunculata

Mentha arvensis f. pedunculata, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and square, branched stems. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in herbal medicine and as a culinary herb.
