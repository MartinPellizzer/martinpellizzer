Microcitrus warburgiana

Microcitrus warburgiana, commonly known as the dwarf lemon or Warburg's citron, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, sour fruits that are typically less than 2 inches in diameter. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact size and citrus scent.

Microcitrus warburgiana thrives in well-drained soil and prefers warm, sunny conditions.
