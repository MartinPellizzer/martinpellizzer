Megaloprotachne

Megaloprotachne, commonly known as the narrow-leaf grass, is a genus of grasses native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, typical of the grass family.

Megaloprotachne is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
