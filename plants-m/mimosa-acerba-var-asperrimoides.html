Mimosa acerba var. asperrimoides

Mimosa acerba var. asperrimoides, commonly known as the rough mimosa, is a variant of the mimosa plant native to South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is notable for its rough, hairy texture on its stems and leaves, which gives it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
