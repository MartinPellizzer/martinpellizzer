Megistostegium retusum

Megistostegium retusum, commonly known as the rough-stemmed meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist meadows, along stream banks, and in other wetland habitats. The plant has upright, often hairy stems that are somewhat rough to the touch, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. Its inflorescence consists of a terminal spike and several side spikes, each bearing small, two-lobed spikelets.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
