Mimusops boonei var. abrupteacuminata

Mimusops boonei var. abrupteacuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed mimusops, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically arranged in panicles. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is often found in coastal and sandy habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
