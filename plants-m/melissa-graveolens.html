Melissa graveolens

Melissa graveolens, commonly known as lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features fragrant, serrated leaves that emit a citrus-like aroma when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.

Its essential oils, rich in citral and other compounds, are widely used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.
