Malus sempervirens

Malus sempervirens, commonly known as the evergreen apple or Florida crabapple, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida, and is characterized by its glossy evergreen leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically sour but can be used in jams and jellies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
