Mesembryanthemum halenbergense

Mesembryanthemum halenbergense, commonly known as the Halenberg ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and clustered, small, often blue or purple flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems. It produces a profuse display of flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring and early summer.

Mesembryanthemum halenbergense is often used in rock gardens and as an ornamental plant due to its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal.
