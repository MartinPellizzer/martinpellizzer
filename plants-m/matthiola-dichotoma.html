Matthiola dichotoma

Matthiola dichotoma, commonly known as the two-branched stock, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its branched, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are pinnately lobed and have a slightly hairy texture.

Matthiola dichotoma is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery.
