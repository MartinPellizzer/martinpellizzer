Micromyrtus rubricalyx

Micromyrtus rubricalyx, commonly known as the red-leaf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are elliptical, leathery, and often have a reddish tinge, especially when young. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

It is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
