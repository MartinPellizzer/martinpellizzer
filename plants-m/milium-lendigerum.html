Milium lendigerum

Milium lendigerum, commonly known as the slender millet, is a herbaceous annual plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and agricultural fields. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Milium lendigerum is often used as a forage crop and has historical significance in traditional agriculture.
