Mentha × dumetorum var. nyaradyana

Mentha × dumetorum var. nyaradyana, commonly known as the Nyaradyan mint, is a hybrid species within the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. This variety is native to specific regions in Central Europe and is often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant is known for its strong menthol scent and is used in traditional herbal remedies.

Its growth habit is typically prostrate or semi-upright, making it suitable for ground cover in gardens.
