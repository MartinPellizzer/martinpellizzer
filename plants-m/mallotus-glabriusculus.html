Mallotus glabriusculus

Mallotus glabriusculus, commonly known as the smooth mallot, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually smooth and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
