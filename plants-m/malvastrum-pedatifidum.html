Malvastrum pedatifidum

Malvastrum pedatifidum, commonly known as the narrowleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves, which are typically narrow and deeply divided. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats like meadows and rocky areas.

Malvastrum pedatifidum is a perennial species that can spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
