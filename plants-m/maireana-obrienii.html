Maireana obrienii

Maireana obrienii, commonly known as the O'Brien's ghost gum, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and fire resistance.

Maireana obrienii is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
