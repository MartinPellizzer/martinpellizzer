Milium album

Milium album, commonly known as white millet, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow spikelets that are often clustered in dense, fluffy heads. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is known for its adaptability to various soil types. Its seeds are used as a grain in some parts of the world, though it is less commonly cultivated than other cereal crops.

The plant's botanical structure includes narrow leaves and a compact growth habit, making it easily distinguishable from other grasses.
