Montia gypsophiloides var. exigua

Montia gypsophiloides var. exigua, commonly known as the dwarf mountain monkeyflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It typically features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has thin, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments.
