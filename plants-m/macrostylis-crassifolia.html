Macrostylis crassifolia

Macrostylis crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved macrostylis, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are often glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
