Manulea limonioides

Manulea limonioides, commonly known as the lemon tree of the genus Manulea, is a species native to specific regions in Africa.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow.

Its fruit is a small, round berry that contains a single seed, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties.
