Mertensia lanceolata var. pubens

Mertensia lanceolata var. pubens, commonly known as the pubescent bluebells, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, hairy leaves and clusters of striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland environments, preferring partial to full shade. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
