Manilkara bidentata var. cruegeri

Manilkara bidentata var. cruegeri, commonly known as the Mexican mahogany, is a tropical tree native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by a thick, aromatic fruit with a smooth, leathery exterior. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and fine grain, making it important in local timber industries.

The plant also has historical uses in traditional medicine and as a source of natural latex.
