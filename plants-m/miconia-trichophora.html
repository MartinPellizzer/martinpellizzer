Miconia trichophora

Miconia trichophora, commonly known as the silky miconia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year in warm climates. Its fruits are bright red berries that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

Miconia trichophora is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
