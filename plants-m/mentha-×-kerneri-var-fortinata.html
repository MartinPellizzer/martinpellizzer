Mentha × kerneri var. fortinata

Mentha × kerneri var. fortinata, commonly known as the hybrid mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Mentha longifolia and Mentha spicata, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant has square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
