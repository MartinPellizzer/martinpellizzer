Mimosa gracilis var. invisiformis

Mimosa gracilis var. invisiformis, commonly known as the invisible mimosa, is a rare and delicate plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that resemble the structure of a fern. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
