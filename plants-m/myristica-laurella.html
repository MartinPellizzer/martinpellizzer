Myristica laurella

Myristica laurella, commonly known as the nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves and yellowish flowers. The tree produces distinctive fruits that contain a single seed, which is encased in a reddish, fleshy aril. The seed is widely used for its essential oils, which are valued for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

Myristica laurella is also known for its role in traditional medicine and its contribution to the global spice trade.
