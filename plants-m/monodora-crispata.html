Monodora crispata

Monodora crispata, commonly known as the African wild almond, is a tropical tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, almond-like seeds enclosed in a fleshy, red or orange pod. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.

The tree thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
