Mischobulbum marmoratum

Mischobulbum marmoratum, commonly known as the marble plant, is a unique species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, mottled leaves that display a variety of colors, including green, white, and purple, creating a marbled appearance. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are thick and succulent, adapted to store water in its native arid environment.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually green or yellow, and appear in clusters during the spring.
