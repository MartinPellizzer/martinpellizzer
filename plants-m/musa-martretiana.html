Musa martretiana

Musa martretiana, commonly known as the Martret's banana, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Indonesia and Malaysia.

It is a herbaceous plant that belongs to the Musaceae family, characterized by its large, broad leaves and clustered fruiting structures. The plant typically grows in tropical climates and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually hidden within the leaf sheaths, and the fruit, when mature, resembles a banana but is smaller and has a distinct flavor.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential horticultural value.
