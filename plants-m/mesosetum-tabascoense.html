Mesosetum tabascoense

Mesosetum tabascoense, commonly known as the Tabasco grass, is a species of grass native to Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or riparian habitats, preferring moist and well-drained soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family.

Mesosetum tabascoense plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native environment.
