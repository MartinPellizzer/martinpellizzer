Musa ingens

Musa ingens, commonly known as the giant banana plant, is a towering herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its massive, elongated leaves that can reach lengths of over 10 meters. The plant produces large, clustered inflorescences with both male and female flowers, which eventually give rise to the edible fruit. Its rhizome system is extensive, allowing it to spread and form dense clumps in its natural habitat.

Due to its size and rapid growth, Musa ingens is often cultivated for ornamental purposes as well as for its potential agricultural value.
