Melilotus sulcatus var. segetalis

Melilotus sulcatus var. segetalis, commonly known as yellow melilot, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its seeds are small and contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting soil fertility in agricultural settings.
