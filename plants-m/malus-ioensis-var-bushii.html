Malus ioensis var. bushii

Malus ioensis var. bushii, commonly known as the White Flowering Apple, is a cultivar of the species Malus ioensis, native to the eastern United States.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its dense, rounded form and showy white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically oval with a smooth texture and a dark green color, providing a lush backdrop to the blossoms. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic contribution.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
