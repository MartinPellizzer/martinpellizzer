Myristica missionis

Myristica missionis, commonly known as the Mission Island nutmeg, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is closely related to the nutmeg tree, Myristica fragrans. The plant produces a unique fruit that contains a large, aromatic seed, which is used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a glossy dark green surface.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
