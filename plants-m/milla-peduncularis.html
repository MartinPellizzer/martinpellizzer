Milla peduncularis

Milla peduncularis, commonly known as the clustered milla, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a compact appearance. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Milla peduncularis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
