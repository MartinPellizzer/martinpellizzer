Micrococca wightii var. glabrata

Micrococca wightii var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth micrococca, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils, often found in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often covered with a fine, powdery coating.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
