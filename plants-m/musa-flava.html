Musa flava

Musa flava, commonly known as the yellow banana, is a species of flowering plant in the Musaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, yellow, elongated fruit that ripens to a golden hue. The plant produces striking, broad leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Musa flava is primarily cultivated for its edible fruit, which is rich in potassium and other essential nutrients.
