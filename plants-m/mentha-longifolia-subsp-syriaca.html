Mentha longifolia subsp. syriaca

Mentha longifolia subsp.

syriaca, commonly known as Syrian mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a strong, minty fragrance.

The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

This subspecies is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other Mentha species.
