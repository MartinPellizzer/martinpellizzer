Myrcia brunnea var. opaca

Myrcia brunnea var. opaca, commonly known as the opaca myrcia, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. It produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
