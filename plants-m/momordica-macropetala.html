Momordica macropetala

Momordica macropetala, commonly known as the giant bitter gourd, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and striking yellow flowers. The fruit is typically elongated, with a warty surface and a bitter taste, though it is cultivated for its edible parts. It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourds and cucumbers.

This plant is often grown for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
