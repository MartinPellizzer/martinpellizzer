Marlea macrophylla var. triloba

Marlea macrophylla var. triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed marlea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with distinct three lobes, which give it its botanical name. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is a popular choice for gardeners in Mediterranean climates.
