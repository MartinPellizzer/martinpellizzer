Mariscus guianensis

Mariscus guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan mariscus, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in the Guyanas and northern Brazil.

It belongs to the family Violaceae, which includes violets and related species. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and along riverbanks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, and usually have a violet or purple hue, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
