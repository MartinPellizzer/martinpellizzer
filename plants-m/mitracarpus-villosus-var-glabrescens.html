Mitracarpus villosus var. glabrescens

Mitracarpus villosus var. glabrescens, commonly known as the silky cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, sprawling growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental and agricultural use.

Its variability in foliage texture distinguishes it from other members of the genus.
