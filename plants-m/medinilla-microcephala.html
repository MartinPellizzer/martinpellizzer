Medinilla microcephala

Medinilla microcephala, commonly known as the "Christmas bell," is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant produces small, round, waxy berries that are typically red or yellow when mature. Its foliage consists of glossy, dark green leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems.

Medinilla microcephala is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance and year-round foliage.
