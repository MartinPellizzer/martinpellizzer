Malaxis trigonopetala

Malaxis trigonopetala, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, three-lobed flower structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and greenish-white, with a subtle fragrance.

Malaxis trigonopetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its distinctive appearance.
