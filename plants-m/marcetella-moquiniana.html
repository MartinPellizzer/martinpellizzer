Marcetella moquiniana

Marcetella moquiniana, commonly known as the Mexican rock cress, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Marcetella moquiniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
