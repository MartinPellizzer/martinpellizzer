Myrcia pseudomini

Myrcia pseudomini, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant features glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its small, fragrant white flowers are followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species.

Myrcia pseudomini is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
