Mentha sylvestris subvar. hispidula

Mentha sylvestris subvar. hispidula, commonly known as hairy wood mint, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, square stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves contain menthol-like compounds, making it a valued plant in traditional medicine and herbal remedies.
