Meriania tomentosa

Meriania tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly meriania, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a soft, woolly appearance. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture.

Meriania tomentosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to warm, humid climates.
