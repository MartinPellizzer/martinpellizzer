Mapania zeylanica

Mapania zeylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon maple, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the family Sapindaceae and is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces samaroid fruits that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution in moist, shaded habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of Southeast Asia.

The tree plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
