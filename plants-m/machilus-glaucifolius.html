Machilus glaucifolius

Machilus glaucifolius, commonly known as the silver-leafed mahonia, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, glaucous (bluish-gray) leaves that provide a striking visual contrast. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in panicles, which are followed by small, dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Its unique foliage and seasonal floral displays make it a valued addition to botanical collections.
