Marathrum aeruginosum

Marathrum aeruginosum, commonly known as the bluebells of the genus Marathrum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture, which helps it retain moisture.

Marathrum aeruginosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
