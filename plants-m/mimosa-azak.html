Mimosa azak

Mimosa azak, commonly known as the Persian sensitive plant, is a flowering shrub native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-bearing fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features feathery, bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched, a trait known as thigmonastic response. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically pale yellow or white, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Mimosa azak is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
