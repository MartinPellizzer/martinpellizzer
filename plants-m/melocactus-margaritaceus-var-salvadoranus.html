Melocactus margaritaceus var. salvadoranus

Melocactus margaritaceus var. salvadoranus, commonly known as the Salvadoran melon cactus, is a species native to Central America, particularly El Salvador.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and its distinctive, rounded tubercles covered in white or pale blue spines. The plant produces a single, large, yellow or orange fruit that resembles a melon, which is edible and has a sweet, juicy flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and cultural significance, it is also cultivated as an ornamental plant.
