Mutisia hastata

Mutisia hastata, commonly known as the yellow mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Mutisia hastata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
