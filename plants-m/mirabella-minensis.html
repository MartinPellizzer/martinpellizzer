Mirabella minensis

Mirabella minensis, commonly known as the Min Mountains cherry, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, bright red fruits that resemble cherries. The plant typically grows in cool, humid climates and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces fragrant white flowers in the spring.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
