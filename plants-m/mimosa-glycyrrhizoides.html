Mimosa glycyrrhizoides

Mimosa glycyrrhizoides, commonly known as the sweet mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy pink to red flowers. The plant is often found in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges. It has a short lifespan and is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense colonies.

The seeds are enclosed in a hairy pod and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
