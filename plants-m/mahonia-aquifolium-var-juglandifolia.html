Mahonia aquifolium var. juglandifolia

Mahonia aquifolium var. juglandifolia, commonly known as the Oregon grape, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features compound leaves with spiny margins and a deep green color, resembling holly leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by blue-black berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is adapted to moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
