Malanea ptariensis

Malanea ptariensis, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine soils.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
