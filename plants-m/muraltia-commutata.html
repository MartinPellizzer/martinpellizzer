Muraltia commutata

Muraltia commutata, commonly known as the red muraltia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, round, red fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Muraltia commutata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
