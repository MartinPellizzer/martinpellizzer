Macrocentrum peruvianum

Macrocentrum peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Peru.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that often display vibrant colors such as yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in cloud forests or mountainous regions. It has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
