Matucana weberbaueri

Matucana weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's matucana, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is a small, slow-growing palm that typically reaches heights of about 2 to 3 meters. The plant features a slender, unbranched trunk and pinnate leaves that are dark green and glossy. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, and it produces clustered, yellowish fruits that are edible.

Matucana weberbaueri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
