Melandrium nigrescens

Melandrium nigrescens, commonly known as the black-fruited catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces distinctive, dark purple to black berries that persist into winter, providing food for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Melandrium nigrescens is often found in disturbed soils and can be an indicator of arid or semi-arid environments.
