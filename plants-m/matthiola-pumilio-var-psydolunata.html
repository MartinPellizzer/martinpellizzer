Matthiola pumilio var. psydolunata

Matthiola pumilio var. psydolunata, commonly known as the dwarf wallflower, is a compact, low-growing plant native to southern Europe.

It features small, clustered flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has aromatic, glandular leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its delicate appearance and hardy nature.
