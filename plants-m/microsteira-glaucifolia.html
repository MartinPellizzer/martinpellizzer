Microsteira glaucifolia

Microsteira glaucifolia, commonly known as the blueleaf microsteira, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy, bluish coating. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers during the spring and early summer. Microsteira glaucifolia thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Its unique appearance and ecological niche make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
