Maxillaria albata

Maxillaria albata, commonly known as the white maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant typically blooms during the summer months, with flowers that have a unique, intricate pattern of veins. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette formation around the pseudobulbs.

Maxillaria albata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant due to its specific care requirements.
