Myrcia neuwiedeana

Myrcia neuwiedeana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a small, red drupe that contains several seeds.

Myrcia neuwiedeana is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its aromatic properties.
