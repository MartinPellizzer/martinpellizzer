Myrceugenia bridgesii

Myrceugenia bridgesii, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

However, it is considered an invasive species in some regions where it can outcompete native vegetation.
