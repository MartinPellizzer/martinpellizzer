Mentha arvensis subsp. gentilis

Mentha arvensis subsp.

gentilis, commonly known as field mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often aromatic.

The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, typical of the Lamiaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Field mint is known for its strong mint scent and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
