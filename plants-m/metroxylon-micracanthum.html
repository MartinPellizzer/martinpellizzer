Metroxylon micracanthum

Metroxylon micracanthum, commonly known as the oil palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces fruits that contain a high amount of oil, making it economically significant for palm oil production. The plant's botanical features include a fibrous root system and a robust stem that supports its massive canopy.
