Madronella involucrata

Madronella involucrata, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, violet-purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a hairy stem that becomes woody with age. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for supporting local ecosystems.
