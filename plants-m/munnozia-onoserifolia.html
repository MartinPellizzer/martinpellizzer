Munnozia onoserifolia

Munnozia onoserifolia, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, white to pinkish flowers during the spring. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptations such as deep root systems and reduced leaf surface to minimize water loss. Its flowers are typically borne on tall, erect stalks, and the species is known for its resilience in harsh, dry conditions.

Munnozia onoserifolia plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing sandy soils.
