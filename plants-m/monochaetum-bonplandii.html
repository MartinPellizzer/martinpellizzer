Monochaetum bonplandii

Monochaetum bonplandii, commonly known as the Bonpland's spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is typically found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
