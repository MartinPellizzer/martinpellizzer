Miconia barbicaulis

Miconia barbicaulis, commonly known as the featherleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, feathery hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Miconia barbicaulis is known for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.

Its rapid growth and invasive potential have made it a concern in some regions where it is introduced outside its native range.
