Mimulus floribundus

Mimulus floribundus, commonly known as the common monkeyflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to western North America.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in stream banks and wet meadows.

Mimulus floribundus plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife and is a popular choice for wetland restoration projects.
