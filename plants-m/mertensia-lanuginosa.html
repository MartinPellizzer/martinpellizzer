Mertensia lanuginosa

Mertensia lanuginosa, commonly known as woolly bluebells, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its woolly, gray-green leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, often found in woodland areas. Its flowering stems are typically unbranched and can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
