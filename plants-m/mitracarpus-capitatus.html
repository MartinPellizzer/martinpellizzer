Mitracarpus capitatus

Mitracarpus capitatus, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are arranged in a dense, spherical inflorescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Mitracarpus capitatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking color and unique form.
