Masdevallia monicana

Masdevallia monicana, commonly known as the "Lady's Slipper Orchid," is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, slipper-shaped flowers that are often adorned with vibrant purple and white hues. The plant typically grows as a miniature orchid, with compact, pseudobulbous stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and rarity, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
