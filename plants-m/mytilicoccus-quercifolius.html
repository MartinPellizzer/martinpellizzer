Mytilicoccus quercifolius

Mytilicoccus quercifolius, commonly known as the oakleaf mytilicoccus, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

Its leaves are broad and lobed, resembling the shape of an oak leaf, which gives it its common name. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences.

The plant's reproductive strategy relies on wind pollination, and its seeds are dispersed by water, contributing to its ecological role in riparian zones.
