Myristica glauca

Myristica glauca, commonly known as the white nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries containing the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. Its essential oils and nutmeg-like seeds have been utilized for their medicinal and aromatic properties.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
