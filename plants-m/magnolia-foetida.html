Magnolia foetida

Magnolia foetida, commonly known as the stinking magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth margin. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The tree is known for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is most noticeable when the flowers are in bloom.
