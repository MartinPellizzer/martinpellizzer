Machilus faberi

Machilus faberi, commonly known as the Chinese sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to eastern China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, yellowish-green fruits that contain smooth, oval seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Its wood is valued for its fine grain and is used in the production of incense and furniture.
