Mentha × rotundifolia f. genuina

Mentha × rotundifolia f. genuina, commonly known as the round-leaf peppermint, is a naturally occurring hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both Mentha rotundifolia and Mentha × piperita.

It is distinguished by its round, oval-shaped leaves and a strong, aromatic menthol scent. This variety is often cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and herbal remedies. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
