Myrcia paniculata var. jacquiniana

Myrcia paniculata var. jacquiniana, commonly known as Jacquin's myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural gardens in warm climates.
