Miconia anisophylla

Miconia anisophylla, commonly known as the variable-leaved miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often deeply lobed and vary in shape and size across different parts of the plant. The plant produces small, clustered white or yellowish flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Miconia anisophylla is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of tropical conditions.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, posing a challenge to local ecosystems.
