Manihot saxatilis

Manihot saxatilis, commonly known as the rock manioc, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and alternate, palmate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry or semi-arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
