Myrcia boldinghii

Myrcia boldinghii, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It produces aromatic, reddish-brown berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine.

Myrcia boldinghii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
