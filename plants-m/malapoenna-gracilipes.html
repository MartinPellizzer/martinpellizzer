Malapoenna gracilipes

Malapoenna gracilipes, commonly known as the slender leafed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees.

The plant derives some of its nutrients from the host through specialized roots called haustoria. It is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, often in forest canopies.
