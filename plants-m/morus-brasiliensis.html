Morus brasiliensis

Morus brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers. The tree produces edible fruits that are consumed by both humans and animals. Its bark is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The plant is also valued for its role in supporting local ecosystems and biodiversity.
