Myrtoleucodendron angustifolium

Myrtoleucodendron angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features small, ovate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
