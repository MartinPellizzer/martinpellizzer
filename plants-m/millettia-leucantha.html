Millettia leucantha

Millettia leucantha, commonly known as the white millettia, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
