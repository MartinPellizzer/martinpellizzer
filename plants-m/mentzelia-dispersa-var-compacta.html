Mentzelia dispersa var. compacta

Mentzelia dispersa var. compacta, commonly known as the compact milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with upright, branching stems that often form a dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in elongated inflorescences. Its leaves are typically opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in arid environments and is adapted to full sun, making it a characteristic species of desert and rocky habitats.
