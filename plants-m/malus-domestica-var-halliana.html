Malus domestica var. halliana

Malus domestica var. halliana, commonly known as the Hall's apple, is a cultivar of the domestic apple tree.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and ornamental value, making it suitable for small gardens. The plant produces small, sweet, and crisp apples that are often used for eating fresh or cooking. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

This variety is also valued for its resistance to certain diseases, contributing to its popularity among gardeners.
