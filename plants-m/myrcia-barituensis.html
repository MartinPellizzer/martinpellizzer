Myrcia barituensis

Myrcia barituensis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a flowering plant species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are often clustered together, followed by red or orange berries. Its leaves are glossy and oval-shaped, with a strong, aromatic scent.

Myrcia barituensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
