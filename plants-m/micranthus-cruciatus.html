Micranthus cruciatus

Micranthus cruciatus, commonly known as the small crucifix or lesser crucifix, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing herb with small, cruciform flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant features linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

Due to its delicate appearance and early blooming habit, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
