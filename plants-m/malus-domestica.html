Malus domestica

Malus domestica, commonly known as the apple tree, is a deciduous tree belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Central Asia and is cultivated worldwide for its edible fruit. The plant produces small, showy flowers in spring, which are followed by fruits that mature in late summer or early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

The apple tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and its economic importance in agriculture.
