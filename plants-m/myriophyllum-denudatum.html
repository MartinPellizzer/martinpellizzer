Myriophyllum denudatum

Myriophyllum denudatum, commonly known as water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are often finely toothed. The plant produces tiny, floating flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes.

Myriophyllum denudatum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
