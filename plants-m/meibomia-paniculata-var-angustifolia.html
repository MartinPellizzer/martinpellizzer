Meibomia paniculata var. angustifolia

Meibomia paniculata var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf meibomia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with white to pale yellow flowers, which are typically borne on long pedicels. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
