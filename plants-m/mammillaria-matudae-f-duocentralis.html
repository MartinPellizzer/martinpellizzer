Mammillaria matudae f. duocentralis

Mammillaria matudae f. duocentralis, commonly known as the two-centered mammillaria, is a unique cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a distinctive two-centered stem structure, which sets it apart from other members of the Mammillaria genus. The plant features white to pale yellow spines and produces small, showy yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts due to its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.

This variety thrives in well-draining soil and requires ample sunlight to maintain its vibrant appearance.
