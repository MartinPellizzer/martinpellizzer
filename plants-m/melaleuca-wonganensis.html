Melaleuca wonganensis

Melaleuca wonganensis, commonly known as the Wongan melaleuca, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalypts and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It produces woody capsules that contain numerous small, black seeds.

Melaleuca wonganensis is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal or inland heathlands.
