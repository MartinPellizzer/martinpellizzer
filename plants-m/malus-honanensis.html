Malus honanensis

Malus honanensis, commonly known as the Honan apple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value due to its showy flowers and attractive autumn foliage. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to common apples but are not widely cultivated for consumption. It thrives in temperate climates and is often grown in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

The plant is also valued for its historical significance in botany and horticulture.
