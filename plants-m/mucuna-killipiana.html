Mucuna killipiana

Mucuna killipiana, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and distinctive, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in the early morning. Its seeds are enclosed in large, pod-like fruits that contain a single, elongated seed.

Mucuna killipiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has also been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
