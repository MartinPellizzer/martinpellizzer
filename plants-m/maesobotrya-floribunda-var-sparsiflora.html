Maesobotrya floribunda var. sparsiflora

Maesobotrya floribunda var. sparsiflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered maesobotrya, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, ovate leaves with a smooth surface. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on short pedicels. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
