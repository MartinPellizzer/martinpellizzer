Mezcala balsensis

Mezcala balsensis, commonly known as the mezcal tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to the highlands of Oaxaca, Mexico, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. Its leaves are elongated and slightly hairy, and it produces a resinous sap that has been historically used in traditional practices.

The mezcal tree plays a significant role in the local ecosystem and is also valued for its cultural and medicinal properties.
