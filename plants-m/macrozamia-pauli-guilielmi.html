Macrozamia pauli-guilielmi

Macrozamia pauli-guilielmi, commonly known as the Paul's cycad, is a rare and ancient plant species native to the subtropical regions of eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants.

Its slow growth rate and specific habitat requirements make it highly sensitive to environmental changes and a target for conservation efforts.
