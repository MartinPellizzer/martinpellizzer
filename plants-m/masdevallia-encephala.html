Masdevallia encephala

Masdevallia encephala, commonly known as the "brain orchid," is a species of orchid native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structure that resembles the folds of the human brain, giving it its distinctive common name. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, often found in cloud forests at high altitudes. Its flowers are usually pale green to white, with a complex arrangement of sepals and petals.

Masdevallia encephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively rare in cultivation.
