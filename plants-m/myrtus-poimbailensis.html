Myrtus poimbailensis

Myrtus poimbailensis, commonly known as the Corsican myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. Its berries are typically red or black when mature and are edible, though not commonly consumed by humans.

Myrtus poimbailensis is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant due to its pleasant scent and hardy nature.
