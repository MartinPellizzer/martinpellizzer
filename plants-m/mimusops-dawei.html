Mimusops dawei

Mimusops dawei, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed enclosed in a hard, woody capsule. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.

The tree thrives in humid climates and is adaptable to a range of soil types.
