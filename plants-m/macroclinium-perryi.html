Macroclinium perryi

Macroclinium perryi, commonly known as the Perry's macroclinium, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted for nutrient absorption from the air and debris around its host.

Macroclinium perryi is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
