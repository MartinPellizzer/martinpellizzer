Mandevilla arcuata

Mandevilla arcuata, commonly known as the silverleaf mandevilla, is a vigorous evergreen climbing plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy dark green leaves with a silvery underside, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and orange, typically blooming from spring to fall. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil and full sun to partial shade.

Mandevilla arcuata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
