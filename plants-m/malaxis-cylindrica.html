Malaxis cylindrica

Malaxis cylindrica, commonly known as the slender malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Malaxis cylindrica is a low-growing orchid that plays a role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators.
