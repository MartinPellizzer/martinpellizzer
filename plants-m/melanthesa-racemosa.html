Melanthesa racemosa

Melanthesa racemosa, commonly known as the clustered melilot, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early spring. Its compound leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Melanthesa racemosa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
