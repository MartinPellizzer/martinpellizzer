Myagrum aegyptiacum

Myagrum aegyptiacum, commonly known as the Egyptian date palm, is a flowering plant native to the Nile Valley.

It is a small, deciduous tree with a short trunk and compound leaves that are often used for thatching. The plant produces small, green fruits that mature into sweet, edible dates. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well-suited for cultivation in regions with limited water.

The plant has historical significance in ancient Egypt and is still grown in some parts of Africa and the Middle East.
