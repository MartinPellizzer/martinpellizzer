Magnolia macrophylla subsp. ashei

Magnolia macrophylla subsp.

ashei, commonly known as the Ashe magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge.

The leaves are broad, ovate, and have a smooth texture, turning yellow in the fall. The tree typically grows to a height of 15–30 feet and has a rounded, pyramidal shape.

It thrives in USDA hardiness zones 6–9 and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.
