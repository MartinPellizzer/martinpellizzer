Mammillaria simplex var. albidispina

Mammillaria simplex var. albidispina, commonly known as the white-spined mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines that give it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
