Mariscus stuppeus

Mariscus stuppeus, commonly known as the rough-stemmed mariscus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. The plant features wiry, branched stems covered in rough, glandular hairs. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers appear in clusters during the spring and summer months.

The leaves are lance-shaped, with a hairy surface and a distinct, aromatic scent.
