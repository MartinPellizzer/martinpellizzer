Mammea megaphylla

Mammea megaphylla, commonly known as the broad-leaf mammea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
