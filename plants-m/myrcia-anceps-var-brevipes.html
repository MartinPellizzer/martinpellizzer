Myrcia anceps var. brevipes

Myrcia anceps var. brevipes, commonly known as the dwarf myrcia, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or red in color. These flowers are followed by small, round berries that are edible but have a sharp, acidic taste.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense undergrowth of tropical rainforests.
