Matucana tuberculosa

Matucana tuberculosa, commonly known as the tuberculate cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems and spiny areoles. The plant features prominent tubercles, which are small, rounded growths that help reduce water loss. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, blooming during the warmer months.

Matucana tuberculosa is well adapted to harsh desert environments, making it a resilient and interesting subject for botanical study.
