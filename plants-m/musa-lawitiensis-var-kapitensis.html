Musa lawitiensis var. kapitensis

Musa lawitiensis var. kapitensis, commonly known as the Kapit banana, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and clustered fruiting structures. The plant produces edible bananas that are typically harvested when they are still green, though they can be ripened off the plant. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil and requires regular watering.

This variety is valued for its resilience and adaptability to various growing conditions.
