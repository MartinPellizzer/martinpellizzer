Melodinus cumingii

Melodinus cumingii, commonly known as the Cuming's melodine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The flowers are typically showy, with five petals and a prominent spurred structure, often in shades of pink or white. It produces small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by animals.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
