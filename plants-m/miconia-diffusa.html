Miconia diffusa

Miconia diffusa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers that attract a variety of pollinators. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when mature.

Miconia diffusa is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
