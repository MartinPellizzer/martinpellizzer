Monadenium capitatum

Monadenium capitatum, commonly known as the desert cress, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense clusters of small, white or yellow flowers. The plant has small, fleshy leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water.

Monadenium capitatum is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
