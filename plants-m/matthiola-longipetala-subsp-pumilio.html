Matthiola longipetala subsp. pumilio

Matthiola longipetala subsp.

pumilio, commonly known as the dwarf wallflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features compact, low-growing stems with small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hair, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its prolonged blooming period.
