Mecranium haemanthum

Mecranium haemanthum, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that resemble flames. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects.

Mecranium haemanthum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
