Myrcia chytraculia var. pauciflora

Myrcia chytraculia var. pauciflora, commonly known as the rare myrtle shrub, is a species native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces dark purple berries that are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
