Myrodia penduliflora

Myrodia penduliflora, commonly known as the hanging-flowered myrodia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often pendulous flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of pink or white. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Myrodia penduliflora is a member of the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea tree species.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.
