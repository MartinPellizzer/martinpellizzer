Moritzia tetraquetra var. bornmulleri

Moritzia tetraquetra var. bornmulleri, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its dark purple, clustered berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are typically oval in shape with a smooth texture. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties.
