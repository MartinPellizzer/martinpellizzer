Marattia ternatea

Marattia ternatea, commonly known as the Ternate marattia, is a fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Marattiaceae and is characterized by its large, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforests, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizome is thick and fleshy, supporting the growth of its expansive leaves.

Marattia ternatea is valued for its ornamental appearance and plays a role in the ecological balance of its native habitats.
