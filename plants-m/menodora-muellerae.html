Menodora muellerae

Menodora muellerae, commonly known as the narrow-leaved menodora, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Menodoridae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native region.
