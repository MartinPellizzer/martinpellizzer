Montanoa grandiflora

Montanoa grandiflora, commonly known as the largeflower montanoa, is a herbaceous plant native to the highlands of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Montanoa grandiflora is often found in rocky soils and thrives in partial shade to full sun conditions.
