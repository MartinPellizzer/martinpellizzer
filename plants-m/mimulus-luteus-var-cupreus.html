Mimulus luteus var. cupreus

Mimulus luteus var. cupreus, commonly known as the copper monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as streambanks and meadows. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
