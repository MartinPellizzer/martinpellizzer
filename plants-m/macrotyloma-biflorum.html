Macrotyloma biflorum

Macrotyloma biflorum, commonly known as the twin-flowered cluster bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive flower clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines.

It is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil, and for its use in both agriculture and ornamental gardening.
