Myrceugenella chequen

Myrceugenella chequen, commonly known as the Quechua myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in cloud forests at high elevations.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains several seeds, and the plant is valued for its ornamental and possibly medicinal properties.
