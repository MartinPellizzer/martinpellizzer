Mentha × rotundifolia var. glabrata

Mentha × rotundifolia var. glabrata, commonly known as round-leaved mint, is a hybrid plant characterized by its rounded, often hairless leaves.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its fragrant leaves contain essential oils, making it a popular choice for culinary and medicinal uses.

It is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
