Matthiola incana var. coccinea

Matthiola incana var. coccinea, commonly known as the red wallflower, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant red, pink, or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, typical of the mustard family. Its foliage is covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its showy flowers and pleasant fragrance.
