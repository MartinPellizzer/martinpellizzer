Muhlenbergia mundula

Muhlenbergia mundula, commonly known as the silverhair grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray blades and tufted inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.

Muhlenbergia mundula plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small wildlife.
