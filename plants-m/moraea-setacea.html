Moraea setacea

Moraea setacea, commonly known as the silver grass or the Cape bell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal areas. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple. It blooms during the summer months and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Moraea setacea is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
