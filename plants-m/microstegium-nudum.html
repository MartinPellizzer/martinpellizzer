Microstegium nudum

Microstegium nudum, commonly known as the slender wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its stems are slender and often branched, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Microstegium nudum is a member of the Oxalidaceae family, known for its calcium oxalate crystals in the leaves.
