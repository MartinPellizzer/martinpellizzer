Morus rigida

Morus rigida, commonly known as the Siberian mulberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called mulberries, which are edible and often consumed by birds and animals. It thrives in temperate climates and is often used in traditional medicine.

Morus rigida is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
