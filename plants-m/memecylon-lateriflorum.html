Memecylon lateriflorum

Memecylon lateriflorum, commonly known as the side-flowered memecylon, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged along the leaf axils. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

Memecylon lateriflorum is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role.
