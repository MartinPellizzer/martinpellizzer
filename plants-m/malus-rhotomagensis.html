Malus rhotomagensis

Malus rhotomagensis, commonly known as the Rhotomagena apple, is a rare species native to the island of Corsica.

It is a deciduous tree belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its small, tart apples and ornamental white flowers. The plant has a compact growth habit and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution and genetic uniqueness, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
