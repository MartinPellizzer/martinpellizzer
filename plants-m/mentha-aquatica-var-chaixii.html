Mentha aquatica var. chaixii

Mentha aquatica var. chaixii, commonly known as the Chaix's water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and spikes of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. It has a creeping rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine and have a refreshing, minty aroma.
