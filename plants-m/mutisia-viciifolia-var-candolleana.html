Mutisia viciifolia var. candolleana

Mutisia viciifolia var. candolleana, commonly known as the Andean mutisia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and shrublands, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

This variant is distinguished by its smaller size and more compact growth compared to other varieties of Mutisia.
