Monothecium

Monothecium, commonly known as a type of fungal spore case, is a unique structure found in certain fungi.

It consists of a single, thick-walled sac that encloses the spores, providing them with protection until release. This structure is typically formed by the fusion of two cells, resulting in a single chamber rather than two. The monothecium is often associated with specific fungal groups, such as the Ascomycota.

Its botanical significance lies in its role in spore dispersal and survival in various environmental conditions.
