Mycelis

Mycelis, commonly known as the false truffle, is a type of fungi that forms a symbiotic relationship with certain plants.

It is characterized by its dense, underground mycelium network that helps in nutrient absorption for its host. The structure of Mycelis resembles a truffle but lacks the spores found in true truffles. It plays a crucial role in soil health by decomposing organic matter and improving soil structure.

This fungal species is often found in temperate forests and is valued for its ecological and potential medicinal properties.
