Mussaenda erectiloba var. scabrella

Mussaenda erectiloba var. scabrella, commonly known as the rough-leaved mussaenda, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features showy, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The leaves are typically rough and hairy, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
