Melocactus schatzlii subsp. chicamochae

Melocactus schatzlii subsp.

chicamochae, commonly known as the Chicamochae melocactus, is a small, spineless cactus native to the Andean regions of Bolivia. It features a compact, globular stem that is typically green to reddish in color, covered with a dense layer of white, waxy hair.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are often followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
