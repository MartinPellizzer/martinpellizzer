Melastoma involucratum

Melastoma involucratum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red-rooted melastoma, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, often pink or red flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture, and its stems are typically covered in fine hairs. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Melastoma involucratum is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
