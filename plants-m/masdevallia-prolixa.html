Masdevallia prolixa

Masdevallia prolixa, commonly known as the elongated masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves and intricate, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and requires indirect light to flourish. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in its natural habitat.

Masdevallia prolixa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique floral structure.
