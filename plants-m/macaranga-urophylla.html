Macaranga urophylla

Macaranga urophylla, commonly known as the white-veined macaranga, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent white veins. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are small and clustered, often greenish-yellow in color, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

The species is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
