Mimulus multiflorus

Mimulus multiflorus, commonly known as the yellow monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and in open woodlands. It has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often reddish in color.

Mimulus multiflorus is a perennial species that thrives in full sun to partial shade.
