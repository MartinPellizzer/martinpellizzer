Manilkara longiciliata

Manilkara longiciliata, commonly known as the long-styled manilkara, is a tropical evergreen tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, orange-colored fruits resembling small apples. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its latex, which has industrial applications.
