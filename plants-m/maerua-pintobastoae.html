Maerua pintobastoae

Maerua pintobastoae, commonly known as the Namib Desert mesquite, is a small shrub native to the hyperarid regions of Namibia and Angola.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant has adapted to extreme drought conditions with deep root systems and waxy leaf coatings to reduce water loss. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths.

The seeds are encased in tough pods that remain on the plant for extended periods, ensuring seed dispersal in arid environments.
