Monanthotaxis foliosa var. ferruginea

Monanthotaxis foliosa var. ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty-leaf monanthotaxis, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with a distinctive reddish-brown coloration on the underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with white to pale yellow flowers, which are typically borne on slender peduncles. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical lowland forests.

Due to its unique foliage and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
