Melaleuca pomphostoma

Melaleuca pomphostoma, commonly known as the narrow-leaf paperbark, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that gives it a distinctive appearance. Its small, white flowers are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Melaleuca pomphostoma is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
