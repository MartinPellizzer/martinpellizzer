Mimulus dentatus var. gracilis

Mimulus dentatus var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender yellow monkeyflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features delicate, spurred yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has thin, branched stems and finely toothed leaves that are typically covered in a soft, fuzzy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in streambanks and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
