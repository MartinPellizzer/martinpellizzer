Mesosphaerum martiusii

Mesosphaerum martiusii, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, five-petaled flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in montane forests. Its leaves are typically ovate with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Mesosphaerum martiusii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
