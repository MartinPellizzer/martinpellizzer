Myrmecophila chionodora

Myrmecophila chionodora, commonly known as the white-veined bromeliad, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves that form a rosette structure. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its unique leaf morphology provides a habitat for various small invertebrates, including ants.

Myrmecophila chionodora is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage and ease of care.
