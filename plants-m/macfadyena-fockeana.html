Macfadyena fockeana

Macfadyena fockeana, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters along its stems. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a decorative plant.
