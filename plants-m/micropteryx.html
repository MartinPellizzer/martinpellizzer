Micropteryx

Micropteryx, commonly known as the small- winged moth, is a genus of moths in the family Micropterigidae.

These insects are characterized by their tiny size and delicate wings, which are often translucent or lightly colored. They are typically found in moist, forested environments, where they feed on nectar from various flowering plants. The larvae of Micropteryx species are often leaf rollers, feeding on the foliage of shrubs and trees.

Due to their small size and cryptic appearance, they are rarely noticed, despite playing a role in pollination and serving as prey for other animals.
