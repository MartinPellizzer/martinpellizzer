Millettia bussei

Millettia bussei, commonly known as the African tamarind tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its flowers are small and yellowish, arranged in racemes, while its pods are long and woody, containing edible seeds.

The plant is valued for its timber, medicinal properties, and role in local ecosystems.
