Malus angustifolia florepleno

Malus angustifolia florepleno, commonly known as the full-flowered apple tree, is a cultivar of the apple tree native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, showy white flowers that bloom in early spring, making it a popular ornamental plant. The tree produces small, edible apples that are typically harvested in late summer. Its foliage is dark green and provides attractive summer color, while its bark features a distinctive flaky texture.

This variety is valued for both its aesthetic appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of climates.
