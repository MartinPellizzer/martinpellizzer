Malva oriastrum

Malva oriastrum, commonly known as the showy mallows, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and disturbed areas. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like hibiscus and cotton.

Malva oriastrum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
