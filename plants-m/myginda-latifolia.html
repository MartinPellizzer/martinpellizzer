Myginda latifolia

Myginda latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf myginda, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Mygindaceae.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are fibrous and capable of absorbing nutrients from the soil efficiently.

Myginda latifolia is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
