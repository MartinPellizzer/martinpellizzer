Micromeria sphaciotica

Micromeria sphaciotica, commonly known as the desert sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a small annual herb with slender, hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Micromeria sphaciotica is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh desert conditions.
