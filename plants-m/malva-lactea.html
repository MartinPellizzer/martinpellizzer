Malva lactea

Malva lactea, commonly known as the milky mallows, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a distinguishing feature of the genus Malva. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Malva lactea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
