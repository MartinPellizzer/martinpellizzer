Masdevallia proboscoidea

Masdevallia proboscoidea, commonly known as the proboscideous masdevallia, is a rare and striking orchid native to the high-altitude cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its elongated, pendulous inflorescence and its unique, intricate flower structure that resembles a proboscis, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, colorful flowers in shades of pink and white, often with a distinctive, velvety texture. It thrives in cool, humid environments with high air circulation and requires specific light conditions to bloom.

Due to its rarity and specialized habitat, it is considered a conservation concern and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
