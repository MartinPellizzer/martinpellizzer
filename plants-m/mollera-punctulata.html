Mollera punctulata

Mollera punctulata, commonly known as the spotted bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Mollera punctulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
