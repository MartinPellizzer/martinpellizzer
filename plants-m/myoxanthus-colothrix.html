Myoxanthus colothrix

Myoxanthus colothrix, commonly known as the green alga, is a unicellular aquatic plant belonging to the Chlorophyta division.

It is characterized by its green color due to the presence of chlorophyll a and b, which are essential for photosynthesis. This organism typically inhabits freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. Myoxanthus colothrix reproduces through cell division and can form dense populations under favorable conditions.

Its simple structure lacks true roots, stems, and leaves, making it a primitive form of plant life.
