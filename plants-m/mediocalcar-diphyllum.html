Mediocalcar diphyllum

Mediocalcar diphyllum, commonly known as the two-leaved sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-like growth habit and small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically opposite and have a distinctive, flattened or spoon-shaped appearance.

Mediocalcar diphyllum is adapted to thrive in poor soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
