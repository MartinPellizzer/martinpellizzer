Mentha arvensis var. badensis

Mentha arvensis var. badensis, commonly known as the Baden mint, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves, contain menthol and other volatile compounds.

This variety is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
