Marcetia cardosoana

Marcetia cardosoana, commonly known as the Cardoso's orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species found in the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration, typically in shades of white and green. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or in rocky crevices.

Due to habitat loss, Marcetia cardosoana is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
