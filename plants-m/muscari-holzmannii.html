Muscari holzmannii

Muscari holzmannii, commonly known as the Holzmann's grape hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It produces dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, resembling grapes, hence its common name. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders. It is known for its early spring blooming habit, often appearing before many other spring-flowering bulbs.

Muscari holzmannii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
