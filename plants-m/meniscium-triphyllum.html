Meniscium triphyllum

Meniscium triphyllum, commonly known as the three-leafed bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in shaded environments.

Meniscium triphyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators.
