Mesembryanthemum perspicuum

Mesembryanthemum perspicuum, commonly known as the "glass flower" or "sun rose," is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a succulent species characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves and showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers open in the morning and close in the evening, a trait known as nyctinasty.

Mesembryanthemum perspicuum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
