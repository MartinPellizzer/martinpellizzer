Matthiola crispiflora

Matthiola crispiflora, commonly known as the wrinkled stock, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, crinkled leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in racemes. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Matthiola crispiflora is also valued in horticulture for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
