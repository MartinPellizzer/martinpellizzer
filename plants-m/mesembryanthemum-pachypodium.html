Mesembryanthemum pachypodium

Mesembryanthemum pachypodium, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant features small, often inconspicuous flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, orange, or red. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, which help minimize water loss.

The species is part of the Aizoaceae family, known for its diverse and adaptive flora in dry regions.
