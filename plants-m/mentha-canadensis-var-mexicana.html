Mentha canadensis var. mexicana

Mentha canadensis var. mexicana, commonly known as Mexican mint, is a variant of the American mint species native to the southeastern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are aromatic when crushed. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its essential oils, rich in menthol and other compounds, contribute to its medicinal and aromatic uses.

This variety is often cultivated for its refreshing scent and potential therapeutic properties.
