Malus domestica subsp. caucasica

Malus domestica subsp.

caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian apple, is a subspecies native to the Caucasus region. It is a deciduous tree that produces small, tart apples with a distinctive flavor.

The plant has a compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a serrated margin, and it blooms with white flowers in the spring.

The fruit is typically harvested in late summer and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
