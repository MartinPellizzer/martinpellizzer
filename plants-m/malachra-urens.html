Malachra urens

Malachra urens, commonly known as the red sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and fleshy, succulent stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a red or purple tinge. Malachra urens is well adapted to survive in harsh, nutrient-poor soils and can thrive in full sun conditions.
