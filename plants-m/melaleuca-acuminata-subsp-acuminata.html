Melaleuca acuminata subsp. acuminata

Melaleuca acuminata subsp.

acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea-tree, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, often aromatic, white or pale pink flowers.

The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in Indigenous Australian medicine.
