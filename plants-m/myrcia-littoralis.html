Myrcia littoralis

Myrcia littoralis, commonly known as the coastal myrtle, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in mangrove ecosystems. Its fruits are small, berry-like structures that contain several seeds.

Myrcia littoralis plays an important role in coastal biodiversity and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
