Myristica ultrabasica

Myristica ultrabasica, commonly known as the Bornean nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces an aromatic fruit that contains a valuable seed, which is used to make nutmeg and mace. Its botanical significance lies in its unique chemical composition and its role in traditional medicine.

Myristica ultrabasica is also of interest to botanists due to its adaptation to high-altitude environments.
