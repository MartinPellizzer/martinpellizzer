Myrcia vestita var. obtusifolia

Myrcia vestita var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the glossy myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with glossy, dark green leaves that are often elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, berry-like fruits.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic properties.
