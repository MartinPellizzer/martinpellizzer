Mimosa franciscana

Mimosa franciscana, commonly known as the California mimosa, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, fern-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of fluffy, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild winters and warm, dry summers.

Mimosa franciscana is often found in sandy or gravelly habitats along the coast.
