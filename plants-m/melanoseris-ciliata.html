Melanoseris ciliata

Melanoseris ciliata, commonly known as the silky sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its succulent leaves are adapted to store water, making it well-suited for drought conditions.

Melanoseris ciliata is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in rock gardens.
