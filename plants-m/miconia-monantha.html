Miconia monantha

Miconia monantha, commonly known as the white miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white or silver hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a distinctive, fleshy capsule that splits open to release numerous tiny seeds.

Miconia monantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and unique floral display.
