Maerua bequaertii var. variifolia

Maerua bequaertii var. variifolia, commonly known as the variable leaf maerua, is a shrub native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that vary in shape and size, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening in dry climates.
