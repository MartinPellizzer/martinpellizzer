Mentha royleana subsp. noeana

Mentha royleana subsp.

noeana, commonly known as the Himalayan spearmint, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas. It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and flowering spikes that produce small, pale blue to purple flowers.

The plant has a strong menthol scent, which is derived from its essential oils. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas.

This subspecies is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other members of the Mentha genus.
