Macrochloa tenacissima

Macrochloa tenacissima, commonly known as the desert lily or desert onion, is a perennial herb native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a basal rosette of thick, fleshy, sword-shaped leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments. The plant produces tall, branched flowering stems that bear small, white or pale purple flowers, which are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and moths. Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil and prevent erosion in harsh desert conditions.

The plant is adapted to extreme temperatures and low precipitation, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
