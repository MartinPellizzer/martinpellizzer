Malaxis hagsateri

Malaxis hagsateri, commonly known as the Hagsater's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the humid forests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often greenish or brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has a rhizomatous root system. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
