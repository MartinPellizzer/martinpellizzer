Morella punctata

Morella punctata, commonly known as the smooth-leaved wax myrtle, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into dark blue berries, which are an important food source for birds. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

The plant has a compact growth habit and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
