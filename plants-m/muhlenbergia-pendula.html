Muhlenbergia pendula

Muhlenbergia pendula, commonly known as the weeping muhlenbergia, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its graceful, cascading foliage that resembles a curtain of silver-green leaves. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding a delicate floral display. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Muhlenbergia pendula is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
