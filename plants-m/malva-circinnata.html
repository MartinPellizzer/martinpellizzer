Malva circinnata

Malva circinnata, commonly known as the woolly mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other well-known species like cotton and hibiscus. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or arid regions.

Malva circinnata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
