Mallotus philippensis var. microphyllus

Mallotus philippensis var. microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaved sea hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 5 cm in length. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, followed by distinctive three-lobed capsules containing seeds. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in sandy soils near the shoreline.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
