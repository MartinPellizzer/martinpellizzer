Monardella neglecta

Monardella neglecta, commonly known as narrowleaf monardella, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Monardella neglecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
