Melosmon laciniatum

Melosmon laciniatum, commonly known as the laceleaf melosmon, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, almost fern-like leaves that give it a delicate, ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, fleshy, orange-red fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical gardens.
