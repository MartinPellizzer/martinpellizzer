Melothria javanica var. scabra

Melothria javanica var. scabra, commonly known as the rough passionfruit, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are covered in a rough, bumpy texture. The plant belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The fruit is typically consumed raw or used in culinary applications due to its tangy flavor.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and partial sunlight.
