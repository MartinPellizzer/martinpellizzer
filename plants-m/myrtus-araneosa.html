Myrtus araneosa

Myrtus araneosa, commonly known as the spider-moss myrtle, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces tiny white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its dense, bushy growth habit makes it suitable for hedges or ornamental use.

Botanically, it belongs to the Myrtaceae family, sharing characteristics with other myrtle species.
