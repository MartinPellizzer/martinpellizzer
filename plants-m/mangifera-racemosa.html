Mangifera racemosa

Mangifera racemosa, commonly known as the wild mango or hill mango, is a tropical plant belonging to the Anacardiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, warm climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that grow in clusters, leading to the development of small, sour fruits. Its leaves are large, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while its bark is rough and deeply fissured.

The plant is valued for its fruit, which is used in traditional medicine and local cuisines.
