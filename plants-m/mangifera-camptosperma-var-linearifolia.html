Mangifera camptosperma var. linearifolia

Mangifera camptosperma var. linearifolia, commonly known as the slender-leaf mango, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that eventually develop into edible mango fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
