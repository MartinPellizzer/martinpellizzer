Miconia roseopetala

Miconia roseopetala, commonly known as the pink-veined miconia, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent pink veins, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that are often surrounded by bracts. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains numerous seeds.

Miconia roseopetala is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates due to its striking foliage.
