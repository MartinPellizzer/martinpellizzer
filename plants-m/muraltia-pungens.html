Muraltia pungens

Muraltia pungens, commonly known as the prickly pear cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in spring. The plant's leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, which help minimize water loss in its harsh environment. Its fruit, often called a "tuna," is edible and contains a sweet, pulpy interior.

Muraltia pungens is well adapted to survive in hot, dry climates with minimal water.
