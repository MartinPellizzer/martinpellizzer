Myrtophyllum

Myrtophyllum, commonly known as the fairy lantern, is a unique flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Proteaceae and is characterized by its striking, lantern-shaped flower heads. The plant produces vibrant, colorful bracts that resemble small lanterns, which are actually modified leaves rather than true flowers. Myrtophyllum thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance once established.
