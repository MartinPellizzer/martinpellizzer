Malcolmia scyria

Malcolmia scyria, commonly known as the common sandcatcher, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powder. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or sandy habitats.

Malcolmia scyria is known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
