Mimosa vasquezii

Mimosa vasquezii, commonly known as the Vasquez's mimosa, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and delicate, yellowish-pink flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky soils, and is well adapted to dry conditions. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several small, round seeds.

Mimosa vasquezii is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and its ecological importance in native habitats.
