Mauritia flexuosa var. venezuelana

Mauritia flexuosa var. venezuelana, commonly known as the Venezuelan palm, is a species of palm native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by round, fibrous fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in wetland and swampy environments, often forming dense stands in tropical regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
