Miconia ceramicarpa var. crozierae

Miconia ceramicarpa var. crozierae, commonly known as the Crozier's miconia, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
