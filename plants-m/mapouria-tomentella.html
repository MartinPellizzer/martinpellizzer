Mapouria tomentella

Mapouria tomentella, commonly known as the woolly mapouria, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its dense, woolly foliage. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine, white pubescence. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by elliptical, woody fruits.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
