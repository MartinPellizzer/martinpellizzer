Mendoncia meyeniana

Mendoncia meyeniana, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, typically blooming in spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found in well-drained, rocky soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in dry conditions, making it adaptable to various climates.
