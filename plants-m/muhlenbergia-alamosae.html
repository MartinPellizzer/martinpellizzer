Muhlenbergia alamosae

Muhlenbergia alamosae, commonly known as the Alamosa muhlenbergia, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and tufted growth habit, which makes it visually appealing in gardens. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that add ornamental value, especially in late summer and autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for native plant gardens.
