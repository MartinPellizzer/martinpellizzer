Malachium caeruleum

Malachium caeruleum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a silvery-blue underside, contributing to its ornamental value.

Malachium caeruleum is valued in gardens for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
