Meineckia paxii

Meineckia paxii, commonly known as the pink meadow cranesbill, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. Meineckia paxii has slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

It is a relatively rare species, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
