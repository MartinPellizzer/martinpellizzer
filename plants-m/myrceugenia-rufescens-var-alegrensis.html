Myrceugenia rufescens var. alegrensis

Myrceugenia rufescens var. alegrensis, commonly known as the Alegre myrtle, is a South American evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, edible drupe with a sweet, aromatic flavor.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local communities.
