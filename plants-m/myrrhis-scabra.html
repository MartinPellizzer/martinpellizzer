Myrrhis scabra

Myrrhis scabra, commonly known as rough helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive three-lobed lip, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Myrrhis scabra is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional herbal remedies.
