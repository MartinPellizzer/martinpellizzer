Miconia secunmaguirei

Miconia secunmaguirei, commonly known as the golden miconia, is a tropical shrub native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. These flowers are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits that attract various birds and mammals.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds dispersed by animals, it is considered an invasive species in some regions.
