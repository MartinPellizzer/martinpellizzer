Mickelia

Mickelia, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical and southern Africa.

These plants are characterized by their fleshy, thick leaves and showy, often violet-colored flowers. Mickelia species typically grow as shrubs or small trees, with some varieties reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The flowers are usually clustered in dense inflorescences and are pollinated by insects.

They are valued in horticulture for their ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
