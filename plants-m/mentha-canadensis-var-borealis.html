Mentha canadensis var. borealis

Mentha canadensis var. borealis, commonly known as northern mint, is a perennial herb native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional herbal use.
