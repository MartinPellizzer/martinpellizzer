Massonia odorata

Massonia odorata, commonly known as the fragrant massonia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions. It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, also known as the ice plant family.

Massonia odorata is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
