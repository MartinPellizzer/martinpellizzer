Mentha × peduncularis

Mentha × peduncularis, commonly known as the hybrid mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant that belongs to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Mentha viridiflora and Mentha suaveolens, resulting in a plant with a strong, aromatic scent. The plant features square stems, opposite leaves that are usually serrated, and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of purple or pink. Mentha × peduncularis is often used in herbal teas and aromatherapy due to its menthol content and refreshing fragrance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
