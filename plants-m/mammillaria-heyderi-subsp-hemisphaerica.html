Mammillaria heyderi subsp. hemisphaerica

Mammillaria heyderi subsp.

hemisphaerica, commonly known as the Hemispherical Mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico. It is characterized by its globular shape and a dense covering of white to yellow spines.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, appearing in late spring or early summer.

It is a slow-growing species that requires minimal water and is well-suited for rock gardens or as a potted plant.
