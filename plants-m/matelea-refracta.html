Matelea refracta

Matelea refracta, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant is typically found in rocky or desert-like habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often woody and branched.

The flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
