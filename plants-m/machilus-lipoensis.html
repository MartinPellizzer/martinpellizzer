Machilus lipoensis

Machilus lipoensis, commonly known as the Lipoensis woodbine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are elliptical and have a glossy surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round berries.

Machilus lipoensis is often found in moist, shaded forest environments and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
