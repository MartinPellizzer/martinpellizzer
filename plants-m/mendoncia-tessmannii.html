Mendoncia tessmannii

Mendoncia tessmannii, commonly known as the Mexican mint, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pink or purple, and they bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Mendoncia tessmannii is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
