Macaranga dallachyana

Macaranga dallachyana, commonly known as the white-veined macaranga, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in lowland rainforests. The leaves are usually alternate, simple, and have prominent white veins, giving it its common name. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsules that contain numerous seeds.

This species is notable for its rapid growth and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
