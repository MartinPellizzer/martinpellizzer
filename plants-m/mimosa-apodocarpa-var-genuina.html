Mimosa apodocarpa var. genuina

Mimosa apodocarpa var. genuina, commonly known as the velvet mimosa, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage and small, fern-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
