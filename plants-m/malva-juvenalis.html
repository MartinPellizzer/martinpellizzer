Malva juvenalis

Malva juvenalis, commonly known as the annual mallows, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in temperate regions, often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces showy pink or white flowers with five petals. It blooms from late spring to early autumn, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

Malva juvenalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
