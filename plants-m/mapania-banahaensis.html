Mapania banahaensis

Mapania banahaensis, commonly known as the Banaha mountain bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, helping to reduce water loss in arid conditions.
