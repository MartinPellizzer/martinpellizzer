Melia guara

Melia guara, commonly known as the Brazilian chinaberry, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family, which includes other trees known for their ornamental and medicinal properties. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in axillary panicles, and it produces small, yellowish fruits that are toxic to humans and animals.

The tree is often cultivated for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil types.
