Mimosa zygia

Mimosa zygia, commonly known as the golden mimosa, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different environments. Its foliage and flowers are often used in ornamental gardening due to their aesthetic appeal.

Mimosa zygia also has a distinctive habit of closing its leaves when touched, a response triggered by mechanical stimulation.
