Masdevallia platyglossa

Masdevallia platyglossa, commonly known as the flat-tongued masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, often with a distinctive lip that is broader than the rest of the flower. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
