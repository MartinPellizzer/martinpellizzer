Melica bulbosa var. spectabilis

Melica bulbosa var. spectabilis, commonly known as the showy bulbous grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.

The species is also notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
