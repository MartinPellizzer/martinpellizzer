Malvastrum tomentosum subsp. tomentosum

Malvastrum tomentosum subsp.

tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It features soft, fuzzy, gray-green foliage that is covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces showy, pale pink to lavender flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
