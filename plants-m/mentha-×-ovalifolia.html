Mentha × ovalifolia

Mentha × ovalifolia, commonly known as oval-leaf mint, is a hybrid mint species that combines characteristics of several Mentha species.

It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically growing in moist, shaded environments. The plant features oval-shaped leaves that are aromatic and often have a strong menthol scent. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Mentha × ovalifolia is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in herbal teas and aromatherapy.
