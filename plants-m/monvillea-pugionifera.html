Monvillea pugionifera

Monvillea pugionifera, commonly known as the sword orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South America.

It is characterized by its sword-shaped leaves and produces striking, orchid-like flowers with a distinctive coloration. The plant typically grows in shady, humid environments and prefers well-drained soil. It is a terrestrial orchid that often forms dense clusters, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental foliage.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
