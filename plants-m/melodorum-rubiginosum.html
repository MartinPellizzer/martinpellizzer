Melodorum rubiginosum

Melodorum rubiginosum, commonly known as the rusty melodorum, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Gesneriaceae and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine, rusty-colored pubescence, which gives the plant its common name. The flowers, which are usually red or orange, bloom in clusters and attract various pollinators.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden environments.
