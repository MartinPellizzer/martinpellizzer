Mentha × dalmatica var. trichodes

Mentha × dalmatica var. trichodes, commonly known as the Dalmatian mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly fuzzy leaves and can grow up to 30-60 cm in height. The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its refreshing scent and mild menthol-like flavor.
