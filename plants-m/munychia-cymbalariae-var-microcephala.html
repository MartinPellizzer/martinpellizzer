Munychia cymbalariae var. microcephala

Munychia cymbalariae var. microcephala, commonly known as the small-headed marsh bellflower, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its compact, herbaceous growth habit and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers bloom from late spring to early summer.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various wildlife species.
