Manilkara merrilliana

Manilkara merrilliana, commonly known as the Malayan sapodilla, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces a fruit that resembles a small, green apple, with a thick, sweet, and sometimes gritty pulp. Its latex, similar to that of the rubber tree, has been used historically for various purposes.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its fruit, which is edible and has a unique flavor.
