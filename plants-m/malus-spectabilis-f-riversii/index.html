Malus Spectabilis F. Riversii

Rivers' crabapple, scientifically known as Malus spectabilis f. riversii, is a striking ornamental tree prized for its showy flowers and ornamental fruit. Native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, it thrives in moist, cool climates and is often found near riverbanks and wetlands. This cultivar is a popular choice for gardens and landscapes due to its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators. Its blossoms, which range from white to pink, appear in early spring, creating a stunning visual display. With its compact size and vibrant seasonal changes, Rivers' crabapple adds both beauty and ecological value to any environment.
