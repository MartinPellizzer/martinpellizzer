Macaranga ovatifolia

Macaranga ovatifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf paper plant, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white tomentum. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsules that release tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
