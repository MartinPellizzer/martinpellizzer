Myrciaria nitida

Myrciaria nitida, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include aromatic oils in the leaves and a preference for tropical and subtropical climates.

Myrciaria nitida is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
