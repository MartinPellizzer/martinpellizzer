Mentha baguetiana

Mentha baguetiana, commonly known as the African mint, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive minty aroma. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Mentha baguetiana is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
