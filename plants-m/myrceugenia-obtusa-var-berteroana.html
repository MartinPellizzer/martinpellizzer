Myrceugenia obtusa var. berteroana

Myrceugenia obtusa var. berteroana, commonly known as the Bertero's myrtle, is a species native to the coastal regions of southern South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in temperate rainforests.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
