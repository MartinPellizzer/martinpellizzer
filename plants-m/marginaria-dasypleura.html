Marginaria dasypleura

Marginaria dasypleura, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a valuable forage plant and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
