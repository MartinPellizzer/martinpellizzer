Millettia gagnepainiana

Millettia gagnepainiana, commonly known as the Gagnepain's millettia, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in primary and secondary forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
