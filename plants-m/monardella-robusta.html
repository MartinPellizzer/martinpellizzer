Monardella robusta

Monardella robusta, commonly known as boldleaf monardella, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its sturdy, hairy stems and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. Its leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, often with a rough texture.

Monardella robusta thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and meadows.
