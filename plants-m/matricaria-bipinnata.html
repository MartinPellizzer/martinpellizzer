Matricaria bipinnata

Matricaria bipinnata, commonly known as the double false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble dandelions. The plant features finely divided, feathery leaves and produces yellow flowers that develop into white, fluffy seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Matricaria bipinnata is also used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and calming properties.
