Malaxis crispata

Malaxis crispata, commonly known as the wrinkled malaxis, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, often with a faintly fragrant scent. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices.

Its unique morphology and habitat make it a rare and interesting subject for botanical study.
