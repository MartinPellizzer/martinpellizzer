Melothria scrobiculata

Melothria scrobiculata, commonly known as the African sour sop or sour sop melon, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It produces small, green, fleshy fruits that are typically covered in a rough, spiny rind. The fruit has a tart, acidic flavor and is often used in local cuisines for its unique taste. Botanically, it belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers and melons.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
