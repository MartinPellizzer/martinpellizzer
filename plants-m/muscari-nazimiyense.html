Muscari nazimiyense

Muscari nazimiyense, commonly known as the Caucasian grape hyacinth, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus, including parts of Turkey and Georgia. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, resembling tiny grapes. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Muscari nazimiyense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
