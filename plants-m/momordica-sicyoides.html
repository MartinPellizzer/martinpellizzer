Momordica sicyoides

Momordica sicyoides, commonly known as the creeping cucumber, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and squashes. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit, heart-shaped leaves, and distinctive yellow-orange flowers. Its fruit is a long, cylindrical berry that turns from green to orange as it matures.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
