Maximowiczia sonorae

Maximowiczia sonorae, commonly known as the Sonoran brittlebush, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, adapted to thrive in hot and dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Maximowiczia sonorae plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
