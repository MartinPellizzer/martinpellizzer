Mimosa vilersii

Mimosa vilersii, commonly known as the velvet mimosa, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellow, spherical flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring. Its root system is fibrous and helps it thrive in well-drained soils.

Mimosa vilersii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
