Mimusops capuronii f. tomentosa

Mimusops capuronii f. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly African satinwood, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its bark is thick and often cracked, and it is valued for its ornamental qualities and medicinal uses.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.
