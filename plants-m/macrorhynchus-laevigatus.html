Macrorhynchus laevigatus

Macrorhynchus laevigatus, commonly known as the smooth-tongued orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
