Monteverdia revoluta var. jaucoensis

Monteverdia revoluta var. jaucoensis, commonly known as the Jauco mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to the Caribbean, particularly found in the Dominican Republic and Haiti.

It attaches to the branches of host trees, such as the ceiba and mahogany, through its haustellum, which penetrates the host's vascular system to extract water and nutrients. The plant produces small, white or pale green berries that contain seeds dispersed by birds. Its leaves are usually small, ovate, and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are inconspicuous and greenish-white in color.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to the biodiversity of forest ecosystems.
