Macaranga tchibangensis

Macaranga tchibangensis, commonly known as the Cameroon makara or African elephant tree, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa, particularly found in countries like Cameroon, Gabon, and the Central African Republic. The plant is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is also valued for its lightweight, fibrous wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
