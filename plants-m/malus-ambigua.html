Malus ambigua

Malus ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous apple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, yellow or red berries. Malus ambigua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various climates.
