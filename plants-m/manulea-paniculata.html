Manulea paniculata

Manulea paniculata, commonly known as the panicle manulea, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, branched inflorescences that bear small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky soils, thriving in cool and temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it conserve moisture.

Due to its ornamental value and unique floral structure, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
