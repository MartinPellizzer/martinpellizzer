Metopium linnaei

Metopium linnaei, commonly known as the yellow mangrove, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in coastal areas, particularly in mangrove ecosystems. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and small, elliptical leaves that are usually arranged in opposite pairs. Its flowers are yellow and clustered in small inflorescences, and it produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed.

Metopium linnaei plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.
