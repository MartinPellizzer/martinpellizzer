Marrubium scrophulariifolium

Marrubium scrophulariifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved horehound, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and gray-green, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Marrubium scrophulariifolium is known for its aromatic properties and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
