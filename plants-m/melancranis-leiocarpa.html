Melancranis leiocarpa

Melancranis leiocarpa, commonly known as the smooth-capsuled melancranis, is a unique plant species belonging to the Melancranis genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive smooth, capsule-like fruits that mature to a pale yellow or brown color. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to conserve water and withstand extreme temperatures. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, often clustered in dense inflorescences, contributing to its ecological role in pollination and seed dispersal.
