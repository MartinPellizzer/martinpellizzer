Mephitidia platyphylla

Mephitidia platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaf mephit, is a rare flowering plant native to the misty highlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that reflect sunlight, aiding in photosynthesis in low-light conditions. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as moths and beetles. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from nutrient-poor soils.

Due to habitat loss, Mephitidia platyphylla is classified as endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
