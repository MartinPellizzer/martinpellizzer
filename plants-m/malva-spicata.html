Malva spicata

Malva spicata, commonly known as the spiked mallows, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. The plant features showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and the stems are covered in fine, soft hairs.

Malva spicata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soils.
