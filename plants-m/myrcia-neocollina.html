Myrcia neocollina

Myrcia neocollina, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible berries that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife. Myrcia neocollina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
