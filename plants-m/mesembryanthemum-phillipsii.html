Mesembryanthemum phillipsii

Mesembryanthemum phillipsii, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or Physalis, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, round, orange berries that resemble tomatoes, which are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to store water in its leaves allows it to survive in arid conditions.
