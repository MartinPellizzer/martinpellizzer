Micromeria byzantina

Micromeria byzantina, commonly known as the Byzantine sandthrift, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry landscapes. It has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to arid environments.

Micromeria byzantina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
