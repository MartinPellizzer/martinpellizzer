Melampodium tenellum var. flaccidum

Melampodium tenellum var. flaccidum, commonly known as the flaccid sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in summer. It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
