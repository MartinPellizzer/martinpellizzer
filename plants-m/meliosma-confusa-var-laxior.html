Meliosma confusa var. laxior

Meliosma confusa var. laxior, commonly known as the broadleaf meliosma, is a species of flowering plant in the family Meliaceae.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.
