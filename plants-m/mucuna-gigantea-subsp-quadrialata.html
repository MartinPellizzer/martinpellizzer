Mucuna gigantea subsp. quadrialata

Mucuna gigantea subsp.

quadrialata, commonly known as the giant velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa. It is characterized by its large, trifoliate leaves and distinctive, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is known for its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and valuable in agricultural systems.
