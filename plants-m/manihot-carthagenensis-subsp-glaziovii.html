Manihot carthagenensis subsp. glaziovii

Manihot carthagenensis subsp.

glaziovii, commonly known as the Glaziov's arrowleaf, is a shrub native to parts of South America. It is characterized by its lanceolate leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing some degree of drought tolerance.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
