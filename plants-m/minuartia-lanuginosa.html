Minuartia lanuginosa

Minuartia lanuginosa, commonly known as the downy rockcress, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy leaves that give it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or arctic regions.

Minuartia lanuginosa is adapted to harsh environments, showcasing resilience to cold and poor soil conditions.
