Myroxylon lanceolatum

Myroxylon lanceolatum, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and is part of the Fabaceae family. The plant has compound leaves with long, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers. Its fruit is a legume that contains several seeds.

Due to its high demand for musical instruments, it has been heavily harvested, leading to conservation concerns.
