Mammillaria discolor subsp. longispina

Mammillaria discolor subsp.

longispina, commonly known as the long-spined white globular cactus, is a small, globular cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico. It features a compact, rounded stem covered in white, woolly spines that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, red fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
