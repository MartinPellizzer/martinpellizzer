Manilkara elata

Manilkara elata, commonly known as the ivory nut or yellow manilkara, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and large, elliptical leaves. The tree produces yellowish flowers that are followed by large, round fruits with a thick, fibrous husk. Its seeds are encased in a hard, woody shell and are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitats.
