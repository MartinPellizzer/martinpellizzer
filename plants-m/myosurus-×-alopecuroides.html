Myosurus × alopecuroides

Myosurus × alopecuroides, commonly known as the hybrid meadow grass, is a perennial plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Myosurus minimus and Alopecurus aequalis, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant typically grows in moist grasslands and along stream banks, thriving in temperate climates. Its slender, tufted stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences that produce wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
