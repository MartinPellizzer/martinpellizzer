Moraea aspera

Moraea aspera, commonly known as the rough moraea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and showy, funnel-shaped flowers. The flowers typically bloom in shades of purple, pink, or white, and are often adorned with intricate patterns. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
