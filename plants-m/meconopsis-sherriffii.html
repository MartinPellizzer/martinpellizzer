Meconopsis sherriffii

Meconopsis sherriffii, commonly known as Sherriff's blue poppy, is a striking alpine plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes, often in mountainous regions of Nepal and Bhutan. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help protect it from harsh environmental conditions.

Meconopsis sherriffii is a popular subject for botanists and horticulturists due to its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native habitat.
