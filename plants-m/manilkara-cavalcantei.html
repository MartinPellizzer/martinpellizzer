Manilkara cavalcantei

Manilkara cavalcantei, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and produces clusters of woody, capsule-like fruits that contain the edible Brazil nuts. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall and has a dense, sprawling canopy. Its bark is thick and fibrous, providing some resistance to pests and diseases.

The plant plays a crucial role in the ecosystem, supporting a wide variety of wildlife and contributing to the biodiversity of the rainforest.
