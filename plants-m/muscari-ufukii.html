Muscari ufukii

Muscari ufukii, commonly known as the Turkish grape hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a short stem that supports its inflorescence. Muscari ufukii thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value lies in its striking floral display, which appears in early spring.
