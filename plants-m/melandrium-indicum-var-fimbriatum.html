Melandrium indicum var. fimbriatum

Melandrium indicum var. fimbriatum, commonly known as the fringed catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are alternate and often have a waxy coating, while the fruit is a capsule that disperses seeds by wind.

The species is known for its short life cycle and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
