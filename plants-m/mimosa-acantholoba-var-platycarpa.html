Mimosa acantholoba var. platycarpa

Mimosa acantholoba var. platycarpa, commonly known as the broad-fruited mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, delicate leaflets that fold shut when touched, a response known as thigmonasty. Its flowers are small and yellow, typically arranged in racemes, and it produces rounded, hairy pods that contain several seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils in warm climates.
