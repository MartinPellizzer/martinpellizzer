Melochia paniculata

Melochia paniculata, commonly known as the red root or red bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are often consumed by birds and other animals. It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and can tolerate drought conditions.

Melochia paniculata is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
