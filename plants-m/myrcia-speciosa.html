Myrcia speciosa

Myrcia speciosa, commonly known as the Brazilian beauty or the golden myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, pale yellow to orange flowers. Myrcia speciosa produces small, reddish-brown berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
