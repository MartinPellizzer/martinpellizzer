Muhlenbergia frondosa f. commutata

Muhlenbergia frondosa f. commutata, commonly known as the silver hair muhlenbergia, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and tufted growth habit, making it a visually striking ornamental plant. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flower spikes in late summer, which add to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for native gardens and erosion control.
