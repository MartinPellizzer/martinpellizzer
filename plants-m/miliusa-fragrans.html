Miliusa fragrans

Miliusa fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant miliusa, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are spherical and covered in a hairy coating, containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The tree is also valued for its fragrant foliage, which emits a subtle, pleasant aroma.
