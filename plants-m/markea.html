Markea

Markea, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous yellow flowers that resemble trumpets, which bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy green leaves and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

However, it can become invasive in some regions if not properly managed.
