Mentha hirsuta var. legitima

Mentha hirsuta var. legitima, commonly known as the hairy mint, is a perennial herb native to regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, often grayish foliage and aromatic, slightly minty scent. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and can reach up to 5 cm in length, while its flowers are small and white or pale blue.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported soothing properties.
