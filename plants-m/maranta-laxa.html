Maranta laxa

Maranta laxa, commonly known as the wandering jew, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are often variegated with silver and green patterns. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically hidden beneath its foliage. It is a member of the Marantaceae family, known for its ornamental value and ease of care.

Maranta laxa thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers moist, well-draining soil.
