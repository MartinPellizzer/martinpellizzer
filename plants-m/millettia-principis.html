Millettia principis

Millettia principis, commonly known as the Java silk tree, is a deciduous leguminous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with 7 to 13 leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, racemose inflorescences. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods that split open when mature.

Millettia principis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
