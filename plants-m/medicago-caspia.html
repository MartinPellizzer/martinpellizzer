Medicago caspia

Medicago caspia, commonly known as Caspian clover, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that grows in dry, sandy, or rocky soils. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in steppe and desert regions.

Medicago caspia is used in traditional medicine and for forage in some areas.
