Matthiola fasciculata

Matthiola fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and produces clusters of small, bright yellow or orange flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

Matthiola fasciculata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
