Mammillaria aeruginosa

Mammillaria aeruginosa, commonly known as the blue mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and blue-gray spines that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
