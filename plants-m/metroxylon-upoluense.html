Metroxylon upoluense

Metroxylon upoluense, commonly known as the Upolu palm, is a species of palm native to the island of Upolu in Samoa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers, typical of many palm species. Its fruit is a small, clustered drupe that contains a single seed.

The plant is adapted to coastal and lowland habitats, often growing in sandy soils.
