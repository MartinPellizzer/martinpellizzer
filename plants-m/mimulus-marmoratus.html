Mimulus marmoratus

Mimulus marmoratus, commonly known as the marbled monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red, often with a distinctive marbled pattern. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats. It has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Mimulus marmoratus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
