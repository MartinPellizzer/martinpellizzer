Myrciaria arborea var. rostrata

Myrciaria arborea var. rostrata, commonly known as the rostrated myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, often containing several seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
