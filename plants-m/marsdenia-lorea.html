Marsdenia lorea

Marsdenia lorea, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by orange-red berries. Its leaves are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Marsdenia lorea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
