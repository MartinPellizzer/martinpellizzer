Melaleuca biconvexa

Melaleuca biconvexa, commonly known as the broad-leaved paperbark, is a species of melaleuca native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, flaky bark that peels away in thin layers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in dense spikes, which are typically visited by a variety of pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly waxy texture.

Melaleuca biconvexa is adapted to grow in a range of soil types and is commonly found in coastal and semi-arid regions.
