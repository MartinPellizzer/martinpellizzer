Micromyrtus rosea

Micromyrtus rosea, commonly known as the pink bottlebrush, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright pink, bottlebrush-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is typically found growing in sandy or rocky soils. It is adapted to coastal conditions, tolerating salt spray and strong winds.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens.
