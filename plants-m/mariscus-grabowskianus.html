Mariscus grabowskianus

Mariscus grabowskianus, commonly known as the Grabowski's mariscus, is a rare and critically endangered plant species native to a small region in Poland.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, calcareous soils within specific microhabitats. The plant features small, clustered leaves and produces delicate, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, its population has drastically declined, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important subject for botanical research and ecological preservation.
