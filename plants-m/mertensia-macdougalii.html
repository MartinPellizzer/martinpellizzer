Mertensia macdougalii

Mertensia macdougalii, commonly known as the MacDougal's bluebells, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Mertensia, which is part of the nightshade family, Solanaceae. The plant is characterized by its striking clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Mertensia macdougalii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.
