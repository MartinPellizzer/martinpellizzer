Myrcia sororia

Myrcia sororia, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically glossy and oval in shape. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are often clustered together, followed by edible berries.

Myrcia sororia is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
