Melastoma stipulare

Melastoma stipulare, commonly known as the red-rooted melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
