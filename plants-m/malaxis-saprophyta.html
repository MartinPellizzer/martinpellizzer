Malaxis saprophyta

Malaxis saprophyta, commonly known as the corpse orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the rainforests of Australia.

It is characterized by its pale green, fleshy leaves and its unique, elongated flower spike that bears small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is adapted to thrive in shaded, humid environments, often growing on tree trunks or in dense undergrowth. Its flowers emit a faint, musty odor, which is thought to mimic the scent of decaying organic matter to attract specific pollinators.
