Myrcia atropilosa

Myrcia atropilosa, commonly known as black myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its dark, aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in forested areas.

Myrcia atropilosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
