Mentha subtomentella var. humillima

Mentha subtomentella var. humillima, commonly known as the low-growing mint, is a variant of the mint plant characterized by its compact growth habit.

It typically features small, narrow leaves that are densely covered with fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. This variety thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate regions. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive minty fragrance.

While less commonly cultivated than other mint varieties, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild flavor.
