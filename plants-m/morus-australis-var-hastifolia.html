Morus australis var. hastifolia

Morus australis var. hastifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mulberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern China.

It features heart-shaped to lanceolate leaves that are typically narrow and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as mulberries, which are usually dark purple when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
