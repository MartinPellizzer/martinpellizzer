Moquinia sordida

Moquinia sordida, commonly known as the dirty moquinia, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in South America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, rhizomatous structure and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. The plant thrives in saturated soils and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and they tend to form dense mats that help stabilize the soil.

Moquinia sordida is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
