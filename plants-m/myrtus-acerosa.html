Myrtus acerosa

Myrtus acerosa, commonly known as the prickly myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, fleshy berries. Myrtus acerosa is evergreen and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal areas.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural use.
