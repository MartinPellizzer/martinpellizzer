Melaleucon

Melaleucon, commonly known as the bottlebrush plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is characterized by its unique, dense, brush-like inflorescences that give it its common name. The leaves are typically narrow and linear, often with a leathery texture. Melaleucon species are native to Australia and are known for their aromatic oils and medicinal properties.

They are widely cultivated for their ornamental value and are often used in gardens and landscapes.
