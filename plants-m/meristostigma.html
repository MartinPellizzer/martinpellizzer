Meristostigma

Meristostigma, commonly known as the "garden spider orchid," is a rare and delicate orchid species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure, which includes specialized meristematic tissues that facilitate rapid growth and development. The plant typically features slender stems and small, greenish-white flowers with intricate patterns. Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Meristostigma is often found in shaded, moist environments within tropical forests.

Conservation efforts are crucial for preserving this botanical wonder, as it faces threats from habitat loss and over-collection.
