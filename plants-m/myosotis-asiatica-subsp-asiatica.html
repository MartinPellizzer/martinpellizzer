Myosotis asiatica subsp. asiatica

Myosotis asiatica subsp.

asiatica, commonly known as the Asian forget-me-not, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia. It features delicate blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in clusters at the tips of its stems.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and it prefers moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland edges, stream banks, and mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
