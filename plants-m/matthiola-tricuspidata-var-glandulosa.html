Matthiola tricuspidata var. glandulosa

Matthiola tricuspidata var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular stock, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with glandular hairs, which give the plant its varietal name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in Mediterranean climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
