Mammillaria thomasii

Mammillaria thomasii, commonly known as the Thomas's nipple cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Sonoran Desert in North America.

It is a small, globular plant with a ribbed stem and is typically covered in white or pale blue spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise muted appearance. Mammillaria thomasii is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its fleshy stem and minimizing water loss through its spiny exterior.

Due to its compact size and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus collections.
