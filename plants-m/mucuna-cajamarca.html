Mucuna cajamarca

Mucuna cajamarca, commonly known as the Amazonian velvet bean, is a tropical legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and trifoliate leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in rainforests.

Mucuna cajamarca is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing abilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
