Mentha minor

Mentha minor, commonly known as catnip, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant is noted for its aromatic leaves and flowers, which are often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine. Mentha minor contains volatile oils, including nepetalactone, responsible for its distinctive scent and effects on animals.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.
