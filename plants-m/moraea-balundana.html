Moraea balundana

Moraea balundana, commonly known as the Natal lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of tubular or bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, or orange. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Moraea balundana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
