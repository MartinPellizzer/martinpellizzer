Mentha niliaca

Mentha niliaca, commonly known as the African mint, is a perennial herb native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in whorls along the stems, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its aromatic leaves emit a minty scent when crushed, making it a valuable plant in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.

Mentha niliaca is often grown for its ornamental value and as a source of essential oils.
