Mimosa lycopodioides

Mimosa lycopodioides, commonly known as the golden thread fern, is a small, delicate fern native to the southeastern United States.

It features finely divided, feathery fronds that are typically bright green with a slightly curled appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of the fern family.

Mimosa lycopodioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in humid, low-light conditions.
