Melissa marifolia

Melissa marifolia, commonly known as lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like mint and sage. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, small, white flowers and serrated, lance-shaped leaves. Its leaves are often used in herbal teas and as a culinary herb due to their citrus-like scent.

Melissa marifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.
