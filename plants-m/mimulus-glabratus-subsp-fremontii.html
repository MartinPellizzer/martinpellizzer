Mimulus glabratus subsp. fremontii

Mimulus glabratus subsp.

fremontii, commonly known as the Fremont's monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its showy, orange to yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It has opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly larger flowers and more robust growth compared to the nominate form.
