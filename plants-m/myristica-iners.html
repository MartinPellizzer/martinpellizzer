Myristica iners

Myristica iners, commonly known as the white nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Indo-Malayan region.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces a unique fruit that contains a single seed, which is encased in a hard, aromatic shell. The seeds are used to produce both nutmeg and mace, making it economically important in the spice trade.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in tropical horticulture and aromatic plant cultivation.
