Mussaenda chlorantha

Mussaenda chlorantha, commonly known as the yellow mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are often overshadowed by the vibrant bracts. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

Mussaenda chlorantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
