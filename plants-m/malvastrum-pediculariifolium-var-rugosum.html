Malvastrum pediculariifolium var. rugosum

Malvastrum pediculariifolium var. rugosum, commonly known as the roughleaf mallow, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features hairy, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture, which contribute to its common name. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
