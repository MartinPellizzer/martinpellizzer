Malva aurantiacorubra

Malva aurantiacorubra, commonly known as the red-orange mallows, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and showy, velvety flowers that range in color from orange to red. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other well-known species like hibiscus and cotton. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Malva aurantiacorubra is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
