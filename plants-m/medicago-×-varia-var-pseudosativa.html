Medicago × varia var. pseudosativa

Medicago × varia var. pseudosativa, commonly known as the Turkish clover, is a hybrid legume characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pink to purple flowers.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. This plant is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it a valuable forage crop. It exhibits a variety of growth forms, including annual and perennial types, depending on the environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it suitable for both agricultural and ecological restoration purposes.
