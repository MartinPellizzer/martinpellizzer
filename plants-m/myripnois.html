Myripnois

Myripnois, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its translucent, ribbon-like leaves that float just below the water surface.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions, often found in slow-moving freshwater habitats. The plant lacks true roots and instead relies on its leaf structures for anchoring and nutrient absorption. Myripnois is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and poor water quality.

Its delicate appearance and unusual morphology make it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
