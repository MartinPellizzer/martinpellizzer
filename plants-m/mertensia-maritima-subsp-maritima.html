Mertensia maritima subsp. maritima

Mertensia maritima subsp.

maritima, commonly known as coastal bluebells, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the northeastern United States. It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along shorelines and dunes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, white down.

It is a perennial herb that plays an important role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
