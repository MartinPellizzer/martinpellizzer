Monarda coerulea

Monarda coerulea, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, blue-violet flower clusters that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is fragrant and has a mint-like scent, which is typical of the genus Monarda.

Blue mistflower is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
