Melaleuca calycina subsp. dempta

Melaleuca calycina subsp.

dempta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a small shrub native to northern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and striking red or pink bottlebrush-like flowers that appear in summer.

The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is adapted to coastal and sandy soils. It is often found in heathlands and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to the typical form of Melaleuca calycina.
