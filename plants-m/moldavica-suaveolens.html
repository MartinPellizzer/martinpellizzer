Moldavica suaveolens

Moldavica suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-smelling bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or woodland areas and prefers well-drained soils. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of delicate flowers.

Moldavica suaveolens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and pleasant aroma.
