Malva leprosa

Malva leprosa, commonly known as the lesser mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds.

It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
