Monteverdia revoluta

Monteverdia revoluta, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Mexican velvetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to Central America.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in disturbed areas or along forest edges.

Monteverdia revoluta is noted for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage.
