Miconia biolleyana

Miconia biolleyana, commonly known as the Brazilian starwood, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces showy, pink to purple bracteate inflorescences that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions as a garden plant.
