Morus tataiba

Morus tataiba, commonly known as the Indian mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to South Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, non-showy flowers. The tree produces edible berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a food source for silkworms. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its bark and leaves have been used in herbal remedies for various health conditions.
