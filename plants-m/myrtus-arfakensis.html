Myrtus arfakensis

Myrtus arfakensis, commonly known as the Arfak myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the island of Arfak in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical conditions and is typically found in coastal and lowland forest areas.
