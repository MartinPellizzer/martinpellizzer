Myrtus conceptionis

Myrtus conceptionis, commonly known as the sacred myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves and small white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed. Myrtus conceptionis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical significance in various cultures.

Its leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and antiseptic properties.
