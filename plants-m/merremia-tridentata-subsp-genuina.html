Merremia tridentata subsp. genuina

Merremia tridentata subsp.

genuina, commonly known as the narrow-leaf morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its slender stems, heart-shaped leaves, and showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in the morning.

The plant has a trailing growth habit and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species in native plant gardens.
