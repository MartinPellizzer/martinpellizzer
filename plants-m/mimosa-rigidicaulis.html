Mimosa rigidicaulis

Mimosa rigidicaulis, commonly known as the rigid mimosa, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and bipinnate leaves that are soft and feathery in appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in spherical clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid environments.

The species is also notable for its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil enrichment.
