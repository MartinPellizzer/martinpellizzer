Masdevallia lindeniana

Masdevallia lindeniana, commonly known as the Lindenberg's masdevallia, is a tropical orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers that come in a range of colors, including purple, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually elongated and grass-like, adapted to capture moisture from the air.

Masdevallia lindeniana is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
