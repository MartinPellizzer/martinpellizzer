Melastoma umbonatum

Melastoma umbonatum, commonly known as the mountain melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. The plant features opposite, elliptic leaves with smooth or slightly hairy surfaces. Its flowers are showy, with five petals that are usually pink or red, and it produces small, round berries that are edible.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
