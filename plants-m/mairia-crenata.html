Mairia crenata

Mairia crenata, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features showy, pentagonal flowers with five lobes, often in shades of red, pink, or white. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and usually have a waxy texture.

Mairia crenata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.
