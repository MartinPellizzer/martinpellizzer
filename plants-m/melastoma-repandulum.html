Melastoma repandulum

Melastoma repandulum, commonly known as the spreading melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from spring to autumn. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dark seeds.
