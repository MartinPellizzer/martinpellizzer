Mycetia cauliflora

Mycetia cauliflora, commonly known as the calico tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its cauliflorous flowering habit, where flowers develop directly on the trunk and branches. The plant produces small, fragrant, white or pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically alternate and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The calico tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
