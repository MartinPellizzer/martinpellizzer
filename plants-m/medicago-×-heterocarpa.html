Medicago × heterocarpa

Medicago × heterocarpa, commonly known as the hybrid sainfoin, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces cylindrical pods containing several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in forage and soil remediation due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
