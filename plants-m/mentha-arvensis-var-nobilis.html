Mentha arvensis var. nobilis

Mentha arvensis var. nobilis, commonly known as peppermint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its dark green, slightly hairy leaves and its strong, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Peppermint thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is widely cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy, medicine, and flavoring.
