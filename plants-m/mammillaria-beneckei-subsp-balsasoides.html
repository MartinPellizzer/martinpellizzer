Mammillaria beneckei subsp. balsasoides

Mammillaria beneckei subsp.

balsasoides, commonly known as the Balsas spineless cactus, is a small, spineless cactus native to Mexico. It features a short, cylindrical stem covered in white, woolly hairs that help reduce water loss.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
