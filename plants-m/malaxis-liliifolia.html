Malaxis liliifolia

Malaxis liliifolia, commonly known as the Lily-leaved Malaxis, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors. The plant features a slender, upright stem with a single, lance-shaped leaf that is often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, clustered in a loose inflorescence, and are characterized by their intricate, lip-like structure.

Malaxis liliifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
