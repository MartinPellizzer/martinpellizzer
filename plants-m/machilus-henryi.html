Machilus henryi

Machilus henryi, commonly known as the Chinese laurier-cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to dark purple as it matures.

Machilus henryi is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in horticulture for its attractive foliage and fragrance.
