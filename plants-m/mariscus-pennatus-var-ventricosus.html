Mariscus pennatus var. ventricosus

Mariscus pennatus var. ventricosus, commonly known as the balloon moss, is a unique variety of moss characterized by its distinctive, inflated, spherical capsules that resemble small balloons.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest floors or along streams. The plant has a low-growing habit with dense, cushion-like growth that helps it retain moisture. Its reproductive structures are specialized, with the capsules opening to release spores when mature.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in soil stabilization.
