Melaleuca propinqua

Melaleuca propinqua, commonly known as the slender tea tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, bottle-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, arranged in spikes along the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy environments.

Melaleuca propinqua is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract native pollinators.
