Myrcia insularis

Myrcia insularis, commonly known as the island myrtle, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, dark berries that contain seeds, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Myrcia insularis thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.
