Marrubium trachyticum

Marrubium trachyticum, commonly known as the rough hawksbeard, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its gray-green, rough-textured leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. It has a taproot system and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Marrubium trachyticum is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
