Myrcia

Myrcia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper or myrtle, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Myrtaceae.

It consists of approximately 120 species, predominantly found in tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. The plants are characterized by their aromatic leaves, showy flowers, and often fragrant fruits. Many species have small, leathery leaves and produce clusters of white or pinkish flowers.

The genus is notable for its ecological importance and some species are used in traditional medicine.
