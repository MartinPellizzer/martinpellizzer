Mila caespitosa f. fortalezensis

Mila caespitosa f. fortalezensis, commonly known as the Brazilian grass, is a variant of the mila plant native to South America.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has fibrous roots and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Its botanical features make it suitable for use in ornamental landscaping and soil stabilization.
