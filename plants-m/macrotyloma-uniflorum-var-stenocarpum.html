Macrotyloma uniflorum var. stenocarpum

Macrotyloma uniflorum var. stenocarpum, commonly known as the narrow-carpel cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, narrow pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for both its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for intercropping in agricultural systems.
