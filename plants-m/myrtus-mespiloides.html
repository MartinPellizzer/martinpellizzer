Myrtus mespiloides

Myrtus mespiloides, commonly known as the common myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, dark green leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and perfumery. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by dark blue berries, which are edible but have a sharp, acidic flavor. Myrtus mespiloides thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value and historical significance make it a valued plant in both horticulture and cultural practices.
