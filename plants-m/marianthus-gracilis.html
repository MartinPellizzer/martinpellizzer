Marianthus gracilis

Marianthus gracilis, commonly known as the slender beauty, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Marianthus gracilis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
