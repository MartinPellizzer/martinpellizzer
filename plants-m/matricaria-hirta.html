Matricaria hirta

Matricaria hirta, commonly known as German chamomile, is an annual herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a branched stem covered with fine hairs and lance-shaped leaves with a serrated edge. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with white ray florets, resembling a daisy. It is often used in herbal medicine for its calming and anti-inflammatory properties.

Matricaria hirta thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation.
