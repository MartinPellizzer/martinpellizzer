Malaxis steinii

Malaxis steinii, commonly known as the Stein's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom during the spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers cool, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it often forms dense clumps.

Malaxis steinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
