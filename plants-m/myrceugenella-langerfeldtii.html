Myrceugenella langerfeldtii

Myrceugenella langerfeldtii, commonly known as the Langerfeldt's myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a humid, cool climate to flourish.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
