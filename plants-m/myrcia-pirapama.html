Myrcia pirapama

Myrcia pirapama, commonly known as the pink myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. It produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

Myrcia pirapama is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
