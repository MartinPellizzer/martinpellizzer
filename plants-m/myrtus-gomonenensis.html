Myrtus gomonenensis

Myrtus gomonenensis, commonly known as the Socotra myrtle, is a rare and endemic plant species native to the island of Socotra.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen foliage and small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to the unique climate of Socotra. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinct scent, while its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
