Medinilla varingaefolia

Medinilla varingaefolia, commonly known as the velvet tangerine, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink in color. These flowers are followed by bright orange or red berries, which are edible and have a tangy flavor.

Medinilla varingaefolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique fruit.
