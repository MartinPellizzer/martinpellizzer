Microsisymbrium lasiophyllum f. grandiflorum

Microsisymbrium lasiophyllum f. grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered form of the silky broomrape, is a hemiparasitic plant that relies on host plants for nutrients.

It lacks chlorophyll and is therefore unable to perform photosynthesis, relying entirely on its host for sustenance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. It is typically found in arid or semi-arid regions where it attaches to the roots of grasses or other herbaceous plants.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique parasitic relationship and its role in ecological studies of plant interactions.
