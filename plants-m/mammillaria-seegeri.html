Mammillaria seegeri

Mammillaria seegeri, commonly known as the Seeger's hedgehog cactus, is a low-growing, spineless cactus native to Mexico.

It features a compact, globular shape with dense, woolly white hairs covering its body. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which bloom from areoles along its stem. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
