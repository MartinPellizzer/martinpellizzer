Malpighia wilburiorum

Malpighia wilburiorum, commonly known as the wild hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
