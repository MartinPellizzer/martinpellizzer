Macfadyena guatemalensis

Macfadyena guatemalensis, commonly known as the moonflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night and close by morning. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires full sun to partial shade.

The moonflower is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal beauty.
