Melocactus deinacanthus subsp. longicarpus

Melocactus deinacanthus subsp.

longicarpus, commonly known as the long-fruited melocactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and dense, spiny areoles that bear short, white spines.

The plant produces elongated, reddish fruits that contain edible, sweet pulp. It is adapted to survive in extreme desert conditions with minimal water.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
