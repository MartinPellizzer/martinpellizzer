Malaxis risaraldana

Malaxis risaraldana, commonly known as the Risaraldan orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its inflorescence consists of a single or few flowers, each with a distinctive lip and sepals.

The species is notable for its unique floral structure and is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
