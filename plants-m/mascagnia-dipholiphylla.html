Mascagnia dipholiphylla

Mascagnia dipholiphylla, commonly known as the two-leaved mascagnia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-leaved structure, with one leaf being significantly larger than the other. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
