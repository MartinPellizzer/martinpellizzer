Mairia decumbens

Mairia decumbens, commonly known as the creeping mairia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its prostrate or decumbent stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with five petals. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Mairia decumbens is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
