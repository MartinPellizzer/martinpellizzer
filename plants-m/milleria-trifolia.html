Milleria trifolia

Milleria trifolia, commonly known as the three-leaved milleria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant features trifoliate leaves, meaning each leaf is divided into three leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Milleria trifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
