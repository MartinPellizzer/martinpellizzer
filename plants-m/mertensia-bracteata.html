Mertensia bracteata

Mertensia bracteata, commonly known as the bluebells of the mountains, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Mertensia bracteata is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
