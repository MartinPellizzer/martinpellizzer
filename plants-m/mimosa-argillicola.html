Mimosa argillicola

Mimosa argillicola, commonly known as the clay mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and spherical seed pods. The plant thrives in clay soils and is often found in wetland or marshy environments. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
