Madhuca cuprea

Madhuca cuprea, commonly known as the red flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed, which is used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
