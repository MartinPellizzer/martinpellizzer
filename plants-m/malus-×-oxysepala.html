Malus × oxysepala

Malus × oxysepala, commonly known as the pink-flowered apple tree, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree with a rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a slightly serrated margin and a matte green upper surface.

The fruit, though not typically edible, is small and red, resembling a miniature apple.
