Matthiola caspica

Matthiola caspica, commonly known as the Caspian stock, is a flowering plant native to the Caspian Sea region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with similar characteristics. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink, arranged in clusters. It is often grown for its ornamental value in gardens due to its delicate appearance.

Matthiola caspica prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a versatile choice for various garden settings.
