Mesosphaerum rhytideum

Mesosphaerum rhytideum, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It typically features a compact, mat-forming growth habit with succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is extensive and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, allowing it to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.

Mesosphaerum rhytideum is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
