Medinilla squillula

Medinilla squillula, commonly known as the dwarf medinilla, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom throughout the year, making it a popular ornamental plant. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Medinilla squillula is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
