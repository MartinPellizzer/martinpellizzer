Melastoma auriculatum

Melastoma auriculatum, commonly known as the golden flame or auriculate melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces striking clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a reddish or purple tinge. These flowers are borne on terminal and axillary inflorescences, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Melastoma auriculatum has a fibrous root system and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
