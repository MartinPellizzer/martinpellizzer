Mauritiella cataractarum

Mauritiella cataractarum, commonly known as the waterfall palm, is a unique species of palm native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping fronds that resemble cascading water, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests. Its leaves are typically green and elongated, with a smooth texture.

Despite its ornamental appeal, it is relatively rare and faces threats due to habitat loss and environmental changes.
