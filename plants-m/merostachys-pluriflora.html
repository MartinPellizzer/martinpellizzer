Merostachys pluriflora

Merostachys pluriflora, commonly known as the many-flowered grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like foliage. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
