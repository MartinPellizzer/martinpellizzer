Monostachya montis-wilhelmi

Monostachya montis-wilhelmi, commonly known as the mountain marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wet, marshy environments and is adapted to cool, temperate climates.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
