Manihot tripartita subsp. tripartita

Manihot tripartita subsp.

tripartita, commonly known as arrowleaf manioc, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant produces a starchy root that is a staple food in many regions, though it contains cyanogenic glycosides that require processing to be safe for consumption. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

The species is also valued for its potential in sustainable agriculture due to its adaptability and resilience.
