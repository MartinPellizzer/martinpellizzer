Malouetia cestroides

Malouetia cestroides, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It features large, showy red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has compound leaves composed of numerous small, elliptical leaflets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often growing in coastal or riverine habitats.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant floral display.
