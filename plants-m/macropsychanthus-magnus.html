Macropsychanthus magnus

Macropsychanthus magnus, commonly known as the giant magnolia, is a striking evergreen shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, typically displaying vibrant yellow or white petals. The plant has thick, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape and provide dense foliage. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
