Megastachya madagascariensis

Megastachya madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar star grass, is a striking ornamental plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, branched inflorescences that bear numerous small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant produces large, elongated seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticultural enthusiasts.
