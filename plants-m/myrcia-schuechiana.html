Myrcia schuechiana

Myrcia schuechiana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. It produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

Myrcia schuechiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
