Melanochyla bullata

Melanochyla bullata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive spotted leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. Native to southern Africa, this herbaceous perennial thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade. The plant's roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
