Mundulea grandidieri

Mundulea grandidieri, commonly known as the Madagascar tamarind, is a leguminous tree native to the arid regions of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves, which are adapted to thrive in dry, nutrient-poor soils. The plant has a deep root system that helps it survive in harsh environmental conditions. It plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.

Its wood is durable and has been traditionally used by local communities for various purposes.
