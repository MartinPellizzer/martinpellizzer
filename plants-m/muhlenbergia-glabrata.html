Muhlenbergia glabrata

Muhlenbergia glabrata, commonly known as the smooth muhlenbergia, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features slender, upright stalks with narrow, grass-like leaves that are smooth to the touch. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers that bloom in late summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife.
