Memora bipinnata

Memora bipinnata, commonly known as the two-feathered fern, is a tropical plant characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds.

Its leaves are finely divided into small, needle-like segments, creating a lacy appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as rainforests or swampy areas. It produces spores rather than seeds, reproducing through spore dispersal.

Memora bipinnata is often found in the understory of tropical forests, where it thrives in high humidity and partial light.
