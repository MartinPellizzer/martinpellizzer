Mussaenda dawei

Mussaenda dawei, commonly known as the golden trumpet bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble trumpets, which bloom in clusters during the warm season. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Mussaenda dawei is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
