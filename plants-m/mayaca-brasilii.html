Mayaca brasilii

Mayaca brasilii, commonly known as the Brazilian mayaca, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and is often found near water bodies. Its flowers are usually violet or purple in color and attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Mayaca brasilii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
