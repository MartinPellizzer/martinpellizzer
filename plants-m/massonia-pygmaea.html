Massonia pygmaea

Massonia pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf massonia, is a low-growing, succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for container gardening.
