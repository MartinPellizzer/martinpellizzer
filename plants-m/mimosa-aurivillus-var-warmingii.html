Mimosa aurivillus var. warmingii

Mimosa aurivillus var. warmingii, commonly known as the golden mimosa, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and showy, yellow flower clusters that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, oval pods that turn brown as they mature, often containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions.
