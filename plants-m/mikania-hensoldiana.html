Mikania hensoldiana

Mikania hensoldiana, commonly known as the Hensold's morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and twining habit. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forests.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
