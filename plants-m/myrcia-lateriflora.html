Myrcia lateriflora

Myrcia lateriflora, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Myrcia lateriflora is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
