Melia australasica

Melia australasica, commonly known as the Australian ash or white ash, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Australia.

It typically grows in dry, open forests and woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has smooth, greyish-brown bark that becomes fissured with age, and its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in catkins, and it produces small, round fruits that are dispersed by wind.

The plant has ecological significance, providing habitat and food for various native fauna.
