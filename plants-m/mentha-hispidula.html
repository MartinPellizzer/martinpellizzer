Mentha hispidula

Mentha hispidula, commonly known as hairy mint, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Mentha hispidula is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
