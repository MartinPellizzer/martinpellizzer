Mollinedia elliptica

Mollinedia elliptica, commonly known as the silverleaf mollinedia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in the understory of tropical forests. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are elliptical in shape, with a silvery or grayish underside, giving it its common name.

Mollinedia elliptica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
