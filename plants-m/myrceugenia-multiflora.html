Myrceugenia multiflora

Myrceugenia multiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically black when mature, though they may vary in color depending on the variety. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

Myrceugenia multiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
