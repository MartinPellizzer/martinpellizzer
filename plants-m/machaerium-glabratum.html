Machaerium glabratum

Machaerium glabratum, commonly known as the glabrous machaerium, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often climbing stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Machaerium glabratum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and grasslands.
