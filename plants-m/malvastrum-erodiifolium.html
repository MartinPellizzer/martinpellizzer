Malvastrum erodiifolium

Malvastrum erodiifolium, commonly known as the common mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branching stems covered in soft, fuzzy hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a serrated edge and a distinctive reddish underside. The plant produces showy pink to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
