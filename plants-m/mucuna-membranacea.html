Mucuna membranacea

Mucuna membranacea, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, pod-like fruits that contain small, dark seeds. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural settings. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet having a serrated edge.

The plant also produces a flowering vine that can climb and spread quickly in suitable environments.
