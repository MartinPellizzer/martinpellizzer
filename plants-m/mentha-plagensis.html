Mentha plagensis

Mentha plagensis, commonly known as the African mint, is a perennial herb native to Africa and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has aromatic, serrated leaves. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue or violet in color.

Mentha plagensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported cooling and digestive properties.
