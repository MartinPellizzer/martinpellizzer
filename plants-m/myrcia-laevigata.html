Myrcia laevigata

Myrcia laevigata, commonly known as the velvetleaf myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, woody capsules that contain aromatic seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

Myrcia laevigata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
