Murucuia peduncularis

Murucuia peduncularis, commonly known as the Brazilian star apple, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a small tree or shrub that can grow up to 5 meters in height, with smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by yellow, star-shaped fruits. The fruit has a sweet, aromatic pulp with a texture similar to a pear, and it is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.
