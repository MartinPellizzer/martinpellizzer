Maurandya barclayana f. laceyana

Maurandya barclayana f. laceyana, commonly known as the lacey maurandya, is a compact, flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink and red. The leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for hanging baskets or containers.

This cultivar is prized for its vibrant blooms and delicate foliage, which add visual interest to gardens.
