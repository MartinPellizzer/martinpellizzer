Mentha sylvestris var. alopecuroides

Mentha sylvestris var. alopecuroides, commonly known as wild mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are aromatic when crushed. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial shade to full sun.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in herbal remedies.
