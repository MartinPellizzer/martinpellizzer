Malva sylvestris proles martrinii

Malva sylvestris proles martrinii, commonly known as the purple mallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus, and is characterized by its hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
