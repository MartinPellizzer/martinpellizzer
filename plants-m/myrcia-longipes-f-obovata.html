Myrcia longipes f. obovata

Myrcia longipes f. obovata, commonly known as the obovate myrcia, is a variant of the myrcia species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its broad, obovate leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
