Maxillaria desvauxiana

Maxillaria desvauxiana, commonly known as the "Desvaux's orchid," is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure with a single, fleshy stem. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a distinctive hood-like lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Maxillaria desvauxiana is appreciated for its ornamental value and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and unique floral structure.
