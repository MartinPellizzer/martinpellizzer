Malaxis javesiae subsp. reichei

Malaxis javesiae subsp.

reichei, commonly known as the Javanesse orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces pendulous inflorescences bearing numerous white to pale green flowers with prominent lip structures. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
