Mnianthus ceylanicus

Mnianthus ceylanicus, commonly known as the Ceylon honeycomb tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive honeycomb-like bark, which gives it its common name. The plant features bipinnate leaves and produces yellowish-white flowers that are often clustered in racemose inflorescences. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The tree is also noted for its ability to thrive in tropical climates and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
