Matelea pubiflora

Matelea pubiflora, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to xeriscaped gardens.
