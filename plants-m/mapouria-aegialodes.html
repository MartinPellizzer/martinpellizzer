Mapouria aegialodes

Mapouria aegialodes, commonly known as the coastal mapouria, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to tolerate salt spray and sandy soils. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers during the winter months. It is often found growing in dune environments and along the edges of coastal forests.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insect species.
