Moscharea muscari

Moscharea muscari, commonly known as the grape hyacinth, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, resembling tiny grapes. The plant produces a fragrant, musky scent, which is particularly noticeable when the flowers are in bloom. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Moscharea muscari is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate.
