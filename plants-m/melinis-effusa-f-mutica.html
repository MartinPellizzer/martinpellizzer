Melinis effusa f. mutica

Melinis effusa f. mutica, commonly known as the silky bluegrass, is a grass species characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and compact growth habit.

It is native to North America and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairies. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny blue to purple flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. Its foliage is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
