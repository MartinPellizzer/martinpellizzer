Mesua ferruginea

Mesua ferruginea, commonly known as the red ironwood or flame of the forest, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange-pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The tree has a dense, dark reddish-brown wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical, with a smooth surface.

Mesua ferruginea is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display and ecological importance in local habitats.
