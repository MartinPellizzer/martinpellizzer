Mogorium sessiliflorum

Mogorium sessiliflorum, commonly known as the sessile-flowered mogorium, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and distinctive, sessile (stemless) flowers that bloom in shades of deep purple and violet. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves with a velvety texture, and its root system is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor, rocky soils. Mogorium sessiliflorum is primarily pollinated by nocturnal moths, and its blooming period occurs during the late summer months.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, this species is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
