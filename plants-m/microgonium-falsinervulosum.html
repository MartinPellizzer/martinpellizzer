Microgonium falsinervulosum

Microgonium falsinervulosum, commonly known as the false nerve fern, is a delicate aquatic plant characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds.

It typically grows in shallow, still waters and thrives in nutrient-rich environments. The plant's leaves are composed of numerous small, thread-like pinnae that give it a lacy appearance. It lacks true roots and instead relies on rhizomes for anchoring and nutrient absorption.

Microgonium falsinervulosum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small invertebrates.
