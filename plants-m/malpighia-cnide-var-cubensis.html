Malpighia cnide var. cubensis

Malpighia cnide var. cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban acuba, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malpighiaceae.

It is native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba, and is characterized by its small, showy flowers and red, edible fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered with a fine layer of hair.

The fruit, which is similar to a small berry, is rich in vitamin C and has been used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
