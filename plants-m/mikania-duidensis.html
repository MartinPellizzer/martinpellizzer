Mikania duidensis

Mikania duidensis, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and trailing habit, often forming dense mats on the ground. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming throughout the warmer months. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often hairy.

Mikania duidensis is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and abandoned fields, where it can thrive in full sun.
