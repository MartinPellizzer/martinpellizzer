Mentha × dumetorum var. braunii

Mentha × dumetorum var. braunii, commonly known as purple catnip, is a hybrid plant in the Lamiaceae family characterized by its distinctive violet to purple foliage.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves, which contain essential oils used in herbal remedies and traditional medicine.

This variety is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of growth.
