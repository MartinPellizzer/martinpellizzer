Megalastrum umbrinum

Megalastrum umbrinum, commonly known as the shadowleaf plant, is a unique species native to the shaded understories of tropical rainforests.

It is characterized by its dark green, broad leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss in humid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, adapted for pollination by specific insect species. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor firmly in nutrient-poor soil.

Megalastrum umbrinum plays an important ecological role in maintaining the biodiversity of its forest habitat.
