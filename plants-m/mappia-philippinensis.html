Mappia philippinensis

Mappia philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine maple, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Sapindaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate or elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by distinctive red, fleshy fruits called drupes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland to montane forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
