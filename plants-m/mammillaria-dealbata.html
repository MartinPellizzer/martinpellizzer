Mammillaria dealbata

Mammillaria dealbata, commonly known as the white-furred hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a vibrant contrast to its gray-green body. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
