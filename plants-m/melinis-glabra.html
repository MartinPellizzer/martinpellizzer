Melinis glabra

Melinis glabra, commonly known as flatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its flat, bluish-green blades and slender, hairless stems. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is often hidden among the foliage.

Melinis glabra plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
