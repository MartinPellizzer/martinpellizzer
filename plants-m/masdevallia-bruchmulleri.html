Masdevallia bruchmulleri

Masdevallia bruchmulleri, commonly known as the Bruchmuller's masdevallia, is a rare and exquisite orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit and produces striking, often brightly colored flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically blooms during the cooler months, showcasing a variety of hues including red, pink, and white. Its foliage consists of narrow, strap-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette formation.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
