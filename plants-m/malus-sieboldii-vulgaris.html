Malus sieboldii vulgaris

Malus sieboldii vulgaris, commonly known as the Japanese flowering crabapple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring clusters of white or pink flowers in early spring. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are often used in jams and preserves. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and turn vibrant shades of red in autumn.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
