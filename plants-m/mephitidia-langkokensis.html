Mephitidia langkokensis

Mephitidia langkokensis, commonly known as the Langkokensis mephit, is a flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is typically found near the forest floor.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
