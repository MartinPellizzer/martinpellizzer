Maranta longiflora

Maranta longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family, which includes a variety of ornamental and economically important species. The plant is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from deep green to red and purple. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, typically appearing in shades of white or pink.

While not widely cultivated for its edible roots like its relative, Maranta longiflora is appreciated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
