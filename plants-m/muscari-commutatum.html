Muscari commutatum

Muscari commutatum, commonly known as the dwarf grape hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often resembling grapes, hence its common name. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and early spring blooming period.

Muscari commutatum is also valued for its ability to naturalize and form dense colonies over time.
