Macfadyena podopogon

Macfadyena podopogon, commonly known as the flame bean, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces long, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular choice for trellises and garden structures.
