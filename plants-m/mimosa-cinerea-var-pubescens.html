Mimosa cinerea var. pubescens

Mimosa cinerea var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy sensitive plant, is a variant of the sensitive plant species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, grayish-green leaves and its ability to fold its leaves when touched, a response triggered by rapid changes in turgor pressure within its cells. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique foliage and adaptive responses make it an intriguing subject for botanical study.
