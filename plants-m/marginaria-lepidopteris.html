Marginaria lepidopteris

Marginaria lepidopteris, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple in color. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Marginaria lepidopteris is native to parts of Africa and is known for its toxic properties.
