Mimulus subsecundus var. viscidus

Mimulus subsecundus var. viscidus, commonly known as the sticky monkeyflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a sticky texture. The plant produces vibrant orange to yellow flowers with two lips, typical of the Physaleae tribe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in streamside habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
