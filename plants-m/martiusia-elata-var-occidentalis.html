Martiusia elata var. occidentalis

Martiusia elata var. occidentalis, commonly known as the Western martiusia, is a flowering plant native to the western regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, clustered inflorescences that produce small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a glossy surface, while the stems are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
