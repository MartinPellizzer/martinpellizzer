Marcgravia fosbergiana

Marcgravia fosbergiana, commonly known as the Fosberg's climbing bromeliad, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves that form a rosette structure. The plant is an epiphytic species, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants without deriving nutrients from them. Its inflorescence produces small, colorful flowers that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

Marcgravia fosbergiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
