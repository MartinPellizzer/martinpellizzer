Masdevallia leontoglossa

Masdevallia leontoglossa, commonly known as the leopard orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the cloud forests of Colombia and Ecuador.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that resemble leopard spots, giving it its common name. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a unique, helmet-shaped lip. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicate and sought-after species in botanical collections.
