Medinilla tulagiensis

Medinilla tulagiensis, commonly known as the Tula Island medinilla, is a tropical shrub native to the Solomon Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and prefers humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are thick and leathery, providing a striking contrast to its delicate blossoms.

Medinilla tulagiensis is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in its native habitat.
