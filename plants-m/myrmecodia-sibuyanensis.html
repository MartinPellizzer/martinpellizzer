Myrmecodia sibuyanensis

Myrmecodia sibuyanensis, commonly known as the Sibuyan ant plant, is a unique epiphytic species native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its swollen, bulbous stems that provide shelter for colonies of ants, which in turn protect the plant from herbivores. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by fruiting bodies that release seeds. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

This species is notable for its symbiotic relationship with ants, which plays a crucial role in its survival and ecological niche.
