Mesembryanthemum curvifolium

Mesembryanthemum curvifolium, commonly known as the curly-leaved ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, often hairy leaves that are typically curved or twisted, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to store water in its leaves allows it to survive in arid environments.
