Mentha longifolia f. acutidens

Mentha longifolia f. acutidens, commonly known as the acute-leaved mint, is a variant of the longleaf mint species.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are sharply pointed, giving it a distinct appearance. This plant is native to regions of Europe and parts of Asia, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is often used in herbal medicine for its aromatic properties and potential digestive benefits.
