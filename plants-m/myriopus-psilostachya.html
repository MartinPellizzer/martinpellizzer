Myriopus psilostachya

Myriopus psilostachya, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spinifex, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Myriopus psilostachya is adapted to withstand drought and is an important component of many native grassland ecosystems.
