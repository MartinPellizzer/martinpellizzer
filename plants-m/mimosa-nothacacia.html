Mimosa nothacacia

Mimosa nothacacia, commonly known as the yellow mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its stems are often covered in a soft, hairy texture, and it prefers well-drained soils in full sun.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various climates.
