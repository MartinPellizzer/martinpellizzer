Macropharynx conflictiva

Macropharynx conflictiva, commonly known as the conflict orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter, often displaying vibrant hues of red, purple, and yellow. The plant belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is known for its complex, intricate flower structures that attract specific pollinators. Its leaves are broad and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in humid environments.

The species is also notable for its rapid growth rate and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
