Myrcia blanchetiana

Myrcia blanchetiana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several small, dark seeds.

Myrcia blanchetiana is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its aromatic properties.
