Myrtus paulotchensis

Myrtus paulotchensis, commonly known as the Paulotch's myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its dense, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns from green to red as it matures.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its aromatic properties.
