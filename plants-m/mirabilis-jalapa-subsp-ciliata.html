Mirabilis jalapa subsp. ciliata

Mirabilis jalapa subsp.

ciliata, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America. It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to pink and white, typically blooming in the early morning.

The leaves are ovate with a velvety texture, and the stems are hairy, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
