Mentha aquatica subsp. caput-medusae

Mentha aquatica subsp.

caput-medusae, commonly known as the Medusa mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are often aromatic.

The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found near water bodies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and has a mild, minty fragrance.
