Myrcia quitarensis

Myrcia quitarensis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
