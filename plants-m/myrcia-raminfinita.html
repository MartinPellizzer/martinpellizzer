Myrcia raminfinita

Myrcia raminfinita, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or raminfinita, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

Myrcia raminfinita is often found in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
