Malus × riversicarnea

Malus × riversicarnea, commonly known as the river serviceberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Malus sargentii and Malus × media.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant produces showy white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in the summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a serrated margin, and they turn vibrant shades of red or orange in the fall.

The species is native to the eastern United States and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
