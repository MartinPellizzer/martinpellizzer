Mammillaria dioica f. angelensis

Mammillaria dioica f. angelensis, commonly known as the Angel's Angel or the Mexican fairy duster, is a succulent cactus native to Mexico.

It features a compact, globular stem with white, woolly spines and small, showy yellow flowers that appear in late spring. The plant is characterized by its unique, branched structure and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its specific care requirements, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
