Menyanthes biflora

Menyanthes biflora, commonly known as the twinflower, is a small herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its distinctive two flowers per stem, which bloom in early spring. The plant has oval, glossy leaves that are typically arranged in a whorl at the base of the stem. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs and wetlands.

Menyanthes biflora is known for its fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that attract pollinators like bees and flies.
