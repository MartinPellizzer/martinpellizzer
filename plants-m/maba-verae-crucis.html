Maba verae-crucis

Maba verae-crucis, commonly known as the Cross-vein Maba, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive cross-shaped veins in its leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, clustered in cyathia, a feature typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.
