Mimusops longifolia

Mimusops longifolia, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa, the Indian Ocean, and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical, glossy dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The tree is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.
