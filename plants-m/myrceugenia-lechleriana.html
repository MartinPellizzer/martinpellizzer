Myrceugenia lechleriana

Myrceugenia lechleriana, commonly known as the Lechler's myrtle, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the temperate rainforests of southern Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. It produces edible berries that are typically consumed by birds and small mammals.

Myrceugenia lechleriana thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
