Mallotus subpeltatus

Mallotus subpeltatus, commonly known as the broadleaf hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in the summer months. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

Mallotus subpeltatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
