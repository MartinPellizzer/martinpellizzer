Macleaya cordata

Macleaya cordata, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features a robust rhizomatous root system and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or blue in color, blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its striking appearance, it is cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
