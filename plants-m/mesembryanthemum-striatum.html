Mesembryanthemum striatum

Mesembryanthemum striatum, commonly known as the striped ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and leaves that are typically covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow, often blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to store water in its leaves allows it to survive in arid conditions.
