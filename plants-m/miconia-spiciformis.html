Miconia spiciformis

Miconia spiciformis, commonly known as the spicate miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple bracteate inflorescences. The plant has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with prominent venation. It produces small, white to yellow flowers that are often clustered in spiciform (spike-like) arrangements.

Miconia spiciformis is typically found in moist, shaded forest understories and can grow up to 5 meters in height.
