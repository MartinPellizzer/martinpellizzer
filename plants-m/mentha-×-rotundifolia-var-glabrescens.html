Mentha × rotundifolia var. glabrescens

Mentha × rotundifolia var. glabrescens, commonly known as round-leaf mint, is a hybrid plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its rounded, slightly hairy leaves and a strong, minty aroma. This variety is often cultivated for its aromatic properties and used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can spread rapidly through runners.

Its flowers are small and tubular, usually pink or purple, and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
