Mirabilis albida var. lata

Mirabilis albida var. lata, commonly known as the spreading four o'clock, is a tropical flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the late afternoon and close at dawn. The plant produces bright pink to red blooms, which are often borne in clusters along the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is noted for its spreading growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening.
