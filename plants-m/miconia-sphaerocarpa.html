Miconia sphaerocarpa

Miconia sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe-fruit miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to distinctive, spherical, red fruits. These fruits are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor, making them popular in local cuisines.

Miconia sphaerocarpa is also known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, which can impact local ecosystems.
