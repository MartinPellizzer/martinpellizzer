Morinda myrtifolia var. velutina

Morinda myrtifolia var. velutina, commonly known as velvet morinda, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
