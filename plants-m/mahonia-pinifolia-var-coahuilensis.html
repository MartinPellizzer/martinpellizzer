Mahonia pinifolia var. coahuilensis

Mahonia pinifolia var. coahuilensis, commonly known as Coahuilensis Mahonia, is a shrub native to the Chihuahuan Desert in northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its evergreen, spiny stems and compound leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are followed by blue-black berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
