Mentzelia oreophila

Mentzelia oreophila, commonly known as the Sierra starflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in arid or semi-arid environments. It is a perennial species that thrives in full sun and is adapted to survive in harsh, high-altitude conditions.

Mentzelia oreophila plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
