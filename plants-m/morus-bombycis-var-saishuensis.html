Morus bombycis var. saishuensis

Morus bombycis var. saishuensis, commonly known as the Japanese mulberry, is a variant of the mulberry tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and smooth, pale bark. The plant is known for its role in the silkworm breeding industry due to its nutritious leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
