Myrcia langsdorffii

Myrcia langsdorffii, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland rainforests.

Myrcia langsdorffii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
