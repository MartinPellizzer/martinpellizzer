Miconia reptans

Miconia reptans, commonly known as the creeping miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Miconia reptans is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it a striking but ecologically challenging plant in the environments where it is introduced.
