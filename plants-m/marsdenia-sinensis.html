Marsdenia sinensis

Marsdenia sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese milk vine, is a climbing shrub native to southern China.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruits are follicles that contain seeds with a tuft of silky hairs.

Marsdenia sinensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
