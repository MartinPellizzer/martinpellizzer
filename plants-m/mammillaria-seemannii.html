Mammillaria seemannii

Mammillaria seemannii, commonly known as the Sombrero cactus, is a small, globular cactus native to Mexico.

It features a dense covering of white, woolly bristles that give it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The plant has short, cylindrical spines and produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
