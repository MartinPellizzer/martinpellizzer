Macaranga peltata

Macaranga peltata, commonly known as the broad-leafed macaranga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Macaranga peltata plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
