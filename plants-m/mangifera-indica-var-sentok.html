Mangifera indica var. sentok

Mangifera indica var. sentok, commonly known as the Thai mango or Khao Tom mango, is a variety of mango native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, pointed fruit with a smooth, thin skin that ranges in color from yellow to orange when ripe. The flesh is typically sweet and fibrous, with a high sugar content and a distinct aroma. This variety thrives in tropical climates with well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid environment for optimal growth.

It is widely cultivated for its flavorful fruit and is a significant crop in countries like Thailand and Malaysia.
