Melaleuca faucicola

Melaleuca faucicola, commonly known as the coastal melaleuca, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the coastal regions of eastern Australia, particularly in Queensland and New South Wales. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in sandy or coastal soils and is often found in dune environments.

Melaleuca faucicola is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.
