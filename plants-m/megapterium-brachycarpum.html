Megapterium brachycarpum

Megapterium brachycarpum, commonly known as the narrow-fruit megaptery, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, narrow pods that contain the seeds. The plant typically grows in grassland and shrubland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish flowers during the spring and early summer.
