Mucuna deeringiana

Mucuna deeringiana, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, hairy pods that contain edible seeds. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural systems. Its flowers are typically purple or violet, and it produces a significant amount of biomass.

The seeds are used in various traditional and commercial applications, including as a source of protein and in the production of natural dyes.
