Mammillaria rutila

Mammillaria rutila, commonly known as the golden mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, white to yellowish spines and small, spherical stems that often have a waxy coating. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
