Melaleuca linariifolia var. alternifolia

Melaleuca linariifolia var. alternifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf paperbark, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a reddish inner layer. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its leaves are narrow and alternate, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in heathland and open forests.
