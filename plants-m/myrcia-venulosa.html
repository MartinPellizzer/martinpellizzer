Myrcia venulosa

Myrcia venulosa, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, red berries. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth texture with a distinctive aromatic scent.

Myrcia venulosa is often found in moist, tropical environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional medicinal uses.
