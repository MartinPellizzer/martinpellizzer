Magdaris

Magdaris, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Magdaris is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
