Mulgedium armeniacum

Mulgedium armeniacum, commonly known as Armenian mulledium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
