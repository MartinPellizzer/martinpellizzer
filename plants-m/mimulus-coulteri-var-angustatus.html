Mimulus coulteri var. angustatus

Mimulus coulteri var. angustatus, commonly known as narrowleaf monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and vibrant orange to red tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, playing a role in local ecosystems.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and conservation efforts.
