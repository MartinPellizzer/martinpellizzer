Murtughas floribunda

Murtughas floribunda, commonly known as the Indian flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a yellow or orange center. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Murtughas floribunda is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
