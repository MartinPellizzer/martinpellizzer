Maranta liebrechtsiana

Maranta liebrechtsiana, commonly known as the "Princess Flower," is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Maranta liebrechtsiana thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor cultivation and botanical gardens.
