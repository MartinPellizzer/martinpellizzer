Mikania pastazae

Mikania pastazae, commonly known as the Pastaza morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and distinctive, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the early morning, giving it its common name. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and often twine around supporting structures.

Mikania pastazae is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in tropical gardens.
