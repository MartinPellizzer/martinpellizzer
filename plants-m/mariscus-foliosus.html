Mariscus foliosus

Mariscus foliosus, commonly known as the leafy mariscus, is a unique plant belonging to the family Aizoaceae.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange, blooming in the spring. It is native to southern Africa, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in xeriscapes and rock gardens.
