Melica amethystina

Melica amethystina, commonly known as the amethyst grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue that becomes more pronounced in the spring.

The flowers are usually pale blue to violet, adding a subtle ornamental value to the plant.
