Medusanthera howardii

Medusanthera howardii, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, ghost-like appearance, with long, white, translucent petals that resemble spectral figures. Native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them.

Its flowers are pollinated by specific insects, and it is highly sensitive to environmental changes, making it a species of conservation concern.
