Melolobium mixtum

Melolobium mixtum, commonly known as the mixed melolobium, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in shrubland and coastal areas, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and as a decorative hedge.

Melolobium mixtum is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
