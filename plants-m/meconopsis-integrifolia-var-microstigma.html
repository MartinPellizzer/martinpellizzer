Meconopsis integrifolia var. microstigma

Meconopsis integrifolia var. microstigma, commonly known as the dwarf meadow gentian, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of the Himalayas.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are smooth along the edges, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a cool, shaded environment.

This variety is valued for its beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
