Myoporum betcheanum

Myoporum betcheanum, commonly known as the Betchean myoporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
