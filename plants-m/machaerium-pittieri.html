Machaerium pittieri

Machaerium pittieri, commonly known as the Brazilian velvet bean, is a tropical leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and clearings.

Machaerium pittieri also has potential agricultural and medicinal applications due to its nutritional and biochemical properties.
