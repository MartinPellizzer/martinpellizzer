Mentha salebrosa

Mentha salebrosa, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and spikes of small, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Mentha salebrosa is known for its aromatic leaves, which contain essential oils similar to other mint species.

It is often used in herbal medicine for its purported digestive and calming properties.
