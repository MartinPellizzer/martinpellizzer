Melicope obtusifolia var. inaequalis

Melicope obtusifolia var. inaequalis, commonly known as the roughleaf melicope, is a species native to the Pacific Islands, including Hawaii.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its rough, often pebbled bark and dark green, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by round, fleshy fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in mesic to wet forest habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in Hawaiian culture.
