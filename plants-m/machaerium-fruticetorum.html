Machaerium fruticetorum

Machaerium fruticetorum, commonly known as the shrub legume, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several small, edible seeds.

Machaerium fruticetorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
