Microrhynchus brunneri

Microrhynchus brunneri, commonly known as the dwarf sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry environments. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert scrub habitats.

Microrhynchus brunneri is well adapted to extreme temperatures and is an important component of its native ecosystem.
