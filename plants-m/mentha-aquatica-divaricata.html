Mentha aquatica divaricata

Mentha aquatica divaricata, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread readily in moist environments.

Mentha aquatica divaricata is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in herbal remedies.
