Matricaria asteroides

Matricaria asteroides, commonly known as German chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with white ray florets, which bloom in late summer. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit and is often used in herbal medicine for its calming properties. Its essential oil, derived from the flowers, contains compounds like bisabolol and chamazulene, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

Matricaria asteroides thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation.
