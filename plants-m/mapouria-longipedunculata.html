Mapouria longipedunculata

Mapouria longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-stemmed mapouria, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clustered inflorescences and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some horticultural settings for its delicate floral display.
