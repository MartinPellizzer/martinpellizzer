Mallotus tenuifolius

Mallotus tenuifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are often arranged alternately along the stems. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
