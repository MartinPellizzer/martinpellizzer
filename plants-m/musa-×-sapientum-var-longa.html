Musa × sapientum var. longa

Musa × sapientum var. longa, commonly known as the longan tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, star-shaped white flowers. The fruit is a small, round drupe with a smooth, thin skin and a sweet, juicy flesh containing edible seeds. Longan trees thrive in warm, humid climates and require well-drained soil for optimal growth.

They are widely cultivated for their edible fruit and are also valued for their ornamental appeal.
