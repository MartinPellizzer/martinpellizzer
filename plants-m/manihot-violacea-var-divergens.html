Manihot violacea var. divergens

Manihot violacea var. divergens, commonly known as the purple arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that are followed by edible tuberous roots. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its potential culinary uses.
