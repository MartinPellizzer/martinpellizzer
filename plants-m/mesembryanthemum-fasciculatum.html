Mesembryanthemum fasciculatum

Mesembryanthemum fasciculatum, commonly known as the clustered ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems. It produces a large number of seeds, which are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

The species is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
