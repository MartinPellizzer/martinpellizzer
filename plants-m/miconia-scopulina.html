Miconia scopulina

Miconia scopulina, commonly known as the rock miconia, is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers, which are followed by bright red or orange fruits. Miconia scopulina is often found growing in rocky soils and shaded forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in tropical climates.
