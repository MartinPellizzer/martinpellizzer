Myristica diversifolia

Myristica diversifolia, commonly known as the white nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant produces distinctive fruits that contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its botanical features include a smooth, grayish bark and a preference for humid, well-drained soils.

Myristica diversifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
