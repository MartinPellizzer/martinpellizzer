Mogorium vimineum

Mogorium vimineum, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and slender, woody stems that can climb or trail along the ground. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, and its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a silvery underside. Mogorium vimineum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a resilient plant in both garden and wild settings.
