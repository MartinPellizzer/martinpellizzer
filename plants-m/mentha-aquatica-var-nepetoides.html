Mentha aquatica var. nepetoides

Mentha aquatica var. nepetoides, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist environments in Europe and Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic and slightly serrated. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in wet soils near streams, ponds, and damp meadows, and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its strong menthol scent makes it a popular choice for culinary and aromatic purposes.
