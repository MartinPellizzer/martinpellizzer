Massonia corymbosa

Massonia corymbosa, commonly known as the Cape bell or the African bell, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, commonly referred to as the ice plant family. The plant is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Massonia corymbosa is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and is often used in arid landscape designs.
