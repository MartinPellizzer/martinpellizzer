Malva violacea

Malva violacea, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and showy, violet-pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other familiar species like cotton and hibiscus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its fibrous roots and hairy stems are notable botanical features that aid in its survival in various environments.
