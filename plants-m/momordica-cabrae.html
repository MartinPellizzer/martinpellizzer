Momordica cabrae

Momordica cabrae, commonly known as the horned melon, is a climbing vine native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, green fruit that resembles a horned melon. The plant produces yellow flowers and has compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The fruit is edible when ripe, with a sweet and sometimes bitter taste.

It is often cultivated for its unique appearance and culinary use.
