Melastoma revolutum

Melastoma revolutum, commonly known as the red-flowered melastoma, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often reddish underneath. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom in spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Melastoma revolutum is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests.
