Macoubea witotorum

Macoubea witotorum, commonly known as the Togo flame tree, is a striking tropical tree native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its large, fiery orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant splash of color to the landscape. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and its bark is thick and fissured. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in coastal areas and along riverbanks.

The Togo flame tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its ecological benefits.
