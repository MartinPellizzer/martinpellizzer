Melinis rosea

Melinis rosea, commonly known as the pink muhly, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems that produce small, pinkish-purple flowers in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in moist soils along stream banks and wetlands.

Melinis rosea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
