Miconia auricoma

Miconia auricoma, commonly known as the golden miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains numerous small seeds.

Miconia auricoma is known for its rapid growth and is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its striking foliage.
