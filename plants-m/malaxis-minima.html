Malaxis minima

Malaxis minima, commonly known as the dwarf orchid or miniature orchid, is a small terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth where it can receive indirect light. The plant has a slender, branching stem with small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green in color. Its flowers are delicate, with three petals and three sepals, and are often pale green or white with a slight pinkish hue.

Malaxis minima is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its compact size and elegant appearance.
