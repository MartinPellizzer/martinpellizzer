Mentzelia rhizomata

Mentzelia rhizomata, commonly known as the threadleaf blazingstar, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, thread-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Mentzelia rhizomata typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
