Memora fallax

Memora fallax, commonly known as the false mohavea, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its succulent, blue-green leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a compact growth habit and is adapted to arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. Its foliage is often used in xeriscaping due to its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.

Memora fallax plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ecological and aesthetic contributions in desert landscapes.
