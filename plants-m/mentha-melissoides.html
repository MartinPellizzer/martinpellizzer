Mentha melissoides

Mentha melissoides, commonly known as honey mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly fuzzy leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant has square stems and opposite leaves, typical of the mint family (Lamiaceae). It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Mentha melissoides is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for its calming and digestive properties.
