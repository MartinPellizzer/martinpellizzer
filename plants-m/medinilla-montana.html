Medinilla montana

Medinilla montana, commonly known as the velvet tangerine, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, waxy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, round, orange berries that are edible but not widely consumed. Medinilla montana thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
