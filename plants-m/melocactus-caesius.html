Melocactus caesius

Melocactus caesius, commonly known as the blue melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem covered in dense, white spines and a bluish-gray coloration. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers during the warmer months. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal rainfall.

Its unique appearance and slow growth make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
