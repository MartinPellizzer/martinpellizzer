Mandevilla mexicana

Mandevilla mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. Its flowers bloom throughout the growing season, often from spring to fall, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Mandevilla mexicana is also valued for its ability to produce fragrant blossoms and its adaptability to various soil types.
