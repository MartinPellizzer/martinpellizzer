Monardella douglasii

Monardella douglasii, commonly known as Douglas's monardella, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems, opposite leaves, and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and open woodlands.

Monardella douglasii is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
