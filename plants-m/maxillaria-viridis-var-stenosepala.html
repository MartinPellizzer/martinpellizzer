Maxillaria viridis var. stenosepala

Maxillaria viridis var. stenosepala, commonly known as the narrow-sepaled green orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features long, narrow leaves and has a pseudobulb structure typical of many orchid species. Its sepals and petals are narrow and elongated, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
