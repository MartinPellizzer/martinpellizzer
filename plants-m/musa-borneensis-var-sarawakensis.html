Musa borneensis var. sarawakensis

Musa borneensis var. sarawakensis, commonly known as the Sarawak banana, is a tropical plant native to the island of Borneo.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of fruit, which are typically elongated and have a yellowish skin when mature. It is well adapted to humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its fruit, which is often consumed fresh or used in local cuisines.
