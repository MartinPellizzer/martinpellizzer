Mapouria pohliana

Mapouria pohliana, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, fig-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Mapouria pohliana is valued for its fast growth and adaptability, making it a useful species for reforestation and ornamental purposes.
