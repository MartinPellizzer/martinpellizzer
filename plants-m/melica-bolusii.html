Melica bolusii

Melica bolusii, commonly known as Bolus's milletgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
