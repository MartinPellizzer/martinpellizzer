Macaranga setosa

Macaranga setosa, commonly known as the slender macaranga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Macaranga setosa is also notable for its role in supporting local biodiversity and its use in traditional medicine.
