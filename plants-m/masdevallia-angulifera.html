Masdevallia angulifera

Masdevallia angulifera, commonly known as the angular masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, angular-shaped pseudobulbs and lanceolate leaves. The flowers are typically small, with a distinctive coloration that ranges from white to pink and purple. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its striking appearance.
