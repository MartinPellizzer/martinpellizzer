Momordica echinata

Momordica echinata, commonly known as the spiny cucumber or horned melon, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiny stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces large, warty, green fruits that turn orange when mature. The fruit's interior contains a yellow, sweet pulp with black, seed-like structures.

It is often cultivated for its unique appearance and edible fruit, though it is also used in traditional medicine.
