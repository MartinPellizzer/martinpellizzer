Maxillaria ortizii

Maxillaria ortizii, commonly known as the Orchid of the Andes, is a terrestrial orchid native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces dark green, velvety leaves and blooms with intricate, often white or pale pink flowers that have a distinctive lip. Maxillaria ortizii thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its botanical features make it a popular specimen among orchid enthusiasts and horticulturists.
