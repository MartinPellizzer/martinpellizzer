Melaleuca cliffortioides

Melaleuca cliffortioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white or pink flowers that appear in clusters. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to tolerate coastal conditions.

Melaleuca cliffortioides also has traditional uses in Aboriginal medicine.
