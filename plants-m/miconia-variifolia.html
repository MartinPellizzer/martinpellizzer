Miconia variifolia

Miconia variifolia, commonly known as the variable miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking, large, heart-shaped leaves that vary in color and texture. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers with five petals, which bloom in clusters during the wet season. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains numerous small seeds.

Miconia variifolia is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets.
