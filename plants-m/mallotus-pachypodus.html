Mallotus pachypodus

Mallotus pachypodus, commonly known as the broad-leafed mallot, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
