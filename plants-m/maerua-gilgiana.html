Maerua gilgiana

Maerua gilgiana, commonly known as the African snowball, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of small white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves and a woody stem that becomes more branched with age. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Maerua gilgiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
