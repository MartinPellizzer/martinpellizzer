Marrubium parviflorum subsp. parviflorum

Marrubium parviflorum subsp.

parviflorum, commonly known as smallflower horehound, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

The plant features erect stems covered in grayish-white hairs, and its small, pale blue or violet flowers are arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a fuzzy texture.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
