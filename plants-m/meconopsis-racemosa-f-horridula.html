Meconopsis racemosa f. horridula

Meconopsis racemosa f. horridula, commonly known as the horridula meconopsis, is a unique variant of the meconopsis genus native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

This plant is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers that bloom in dense racemes, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. The leaves are lanceolate and covered in a dense layer of glandular hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil, typically found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is a sought-after plant among horticulturists and botanists.
