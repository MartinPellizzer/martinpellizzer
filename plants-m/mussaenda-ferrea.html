Mussaenda ferrea

Mussaenda ferrea, commonly known as the flame flower or red flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red bracts that resemble flowers, though they are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Mussaenda ferrea is a shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
