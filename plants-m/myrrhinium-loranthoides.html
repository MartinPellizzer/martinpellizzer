Myrrhinium loranthoides

Myrrhinium loranthoides, commonly known as the mistletoe fern, is a hemiparasitic plant that thrives on the branches of host trees.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its haustoria, which attach to the host's vascular system. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique parasitic relationship and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
