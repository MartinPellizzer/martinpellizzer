Myrtus tomentosa

Myrtus tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Myrtus tomentosa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and its ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
