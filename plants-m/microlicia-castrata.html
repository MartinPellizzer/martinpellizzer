Microlicia castrata

Microlicia castrata, commonly known as the rock fig, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces edible, fig-like fruits that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
