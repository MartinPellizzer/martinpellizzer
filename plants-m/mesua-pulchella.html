Mesua pulchella

Mesua pulchella, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, round, yellow fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a strong, aromatic scent.

Mesua pulchella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
