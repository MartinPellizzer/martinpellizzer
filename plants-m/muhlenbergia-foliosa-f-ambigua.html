Muhlenbergia foliosa f. ambigua

Muhlenbergia foliosa f. ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous muhlenbergia, is a variant of the tufted muhlenbergia, native to the southwestern United States.

It is a grass species characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, silvery-green foliage. The plant produces delicate, panicle-like inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or pink, adding ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This ornamental grass is valued for its low maintenance and ability to provide texture and movement in garden landscapes.
