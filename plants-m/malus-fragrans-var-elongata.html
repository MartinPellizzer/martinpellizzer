Malus fragrans var. elongata

Malus fragrans var. elongata, commonly known as the elongated fragrant apple, is a cultivar of the apple tree characterized by its extended, slender growth habit.

It produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a dark green color. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild, sweet fruit flavor.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
