Muellera monilis

Muellera monilis, commonly known as the silverleaf muellera, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender, often hairy stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which bloom during the dry season. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Muellera monilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in subtropical gardens.
