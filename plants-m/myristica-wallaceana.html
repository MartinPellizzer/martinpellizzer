Myristica wallaceana

Myristica wallaceana, commonly known as the nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and aromatic bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into oval fruits containing the aromatic seeds used for nutmeg and mace. Its roots and leaves are also utilized in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its economic and cultural significance.
