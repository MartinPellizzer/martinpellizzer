Myosotis olympica subsp. olympica

Myosotis olympica subsp.

olympica, commonly known as the Olympic forget-me-not, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

It is a perennial species that produces a basal rosette of foliage and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
