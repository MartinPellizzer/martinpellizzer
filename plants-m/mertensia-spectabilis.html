Mertensia spectabilis

Mertensia spectabilis, commonly known as the showy bluebells, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to violet in color, blooming in early spring. The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Mertensia spectabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
