Myosotis sylvatica var. macrophylla-grandiflora

Myosotis sylvatica var. macrophylla-grandiflora, commonly known as the giant water violet, is a variant of the common forget-me-not.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue or purple flowers that are typically larger than those of the standard species. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows or along stream banks.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators.
