Myostemma

Myostemma, commonly known as the "false dandelion," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in well-drained soils. Myostemma species are often found in meadows and along roadsides, where they contribute to the local biodiversity.

Due to their ornamental value, some species are cultivated in gardens.
