Musa × nigra

Musa × nigra, commonly known as the black plantain, is a hybrid banana species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black-purple, elongated fruit that is typically harvested when fully mature. The plant produces large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length, contributing to its striking appearance. The fruit is typically seedless and has a sweet, starchy flavor, making it suitable for both fresh consumption and processing into flour.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.
