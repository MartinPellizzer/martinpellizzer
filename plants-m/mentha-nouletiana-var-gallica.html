Mentha nouletiana var. gallica

Mentha nouletiana var. gallica, commonly known as French mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. Its aromatic leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its strong, refreshing scent.
