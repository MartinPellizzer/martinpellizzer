Microstegium nudum subsp. japonicum

Microstegium nudum subsp.

japonicum, commonly known as Japanese woodland sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia. It features small, trifoliate leaves with a waxy coating, and it produces clusters of small, yellow, spurred flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread vegetatively.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticultural practices.
