Mammillaria heyderi subsp. gummifera

Mammillaria heyderi subsp.

gummifera, commonly known as the sticky mammillaria, is a small cactus native to Mexico. It features a cylindrical stem covered in white, woolly spines and has a distinctive yellowish-green color.

The plant produces pale yellow flowers during the spring or early summer. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil to thrive.

Its subspecific name, *gummifera*, refers to the sticky, resinous substance found on its stems.
