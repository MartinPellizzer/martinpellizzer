Miconia hatschbachii

Miconia hatschbachii, commonly known as the Brazilian miconia, is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its large, broad leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets make it an invasive species in some areas where it is introduced.
