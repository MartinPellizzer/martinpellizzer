Mesosphaerum arboreum var. bracteosum

Mesosphaerum arboreum var. bracteosum, commonly known as the bracteate mesosphaerum, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces woody capsules as fruit, which contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
