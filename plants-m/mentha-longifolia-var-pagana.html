Mentha longifolia var. pagana

Mentha longifolia var. pagana, commonly known as wild mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant features aromatic leaves with a menthol scent, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically violet or purple in color.
