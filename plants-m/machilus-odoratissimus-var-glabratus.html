Machilus odoratissimus var. glabratus

Machilus odoratissimus var. glabratus, commonly known as the fragrant machilus, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are highly fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruits are drupes, typically containing a single seed, and it prefers well-drained soils in tropical and subtropical climates.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
