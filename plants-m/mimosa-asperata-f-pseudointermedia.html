Mimosa asperata f. pseudointermedia

Mimosa asperata f. pseudointermedia, commonly known as the hairy mimosa, is a variant of the mimosa plant characterized by its distinctive, feathery leaves and showy, yellowish-pink flowers.

It is a perennial shrub native to parts of South America, particularly Argentina and Brazil, where it thrives in temperate to subtropical climates. The plant features a woody stem covered in fine, soft hairs, and its leaves are bipinnately compound, giving it a feathery appearance. It produces small, globular fruit pods that split open when mature, releasing seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
