Micranthemum punctatum

Micranthemum punctatum, commonly known as dwarf watermint, is a small aquatic plant native to the Americas.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant features tiny, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, white to pinkish flowers. It is often used in water gardens and as a floating plant in aquariums due to its ornamental value.

Micranthemum punctatum is also valued for its ability to help oxygenate water and provide habitat for aquatic life.
