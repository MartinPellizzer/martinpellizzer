Marsdenia asclepioidea

Marsdenia asclepioidea, commonly known as the sacred fig vine, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asclepiadaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, including India and Sri Lanka, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white. Its fruit is a follicle containing numerous seeds, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
