Maxillaria huancabambae

Maxillaria huancabambae, commonly known as the Huancabamba Maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to the Andean region of Peru.

It is a terrestrial orchid with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its high-altitude habitat. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white and pink, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged in a rosette pattern around the pseudobulbs.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
