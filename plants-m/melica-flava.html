Melica flava

Melica flava, commonly known as yellow panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, yellowish to greenish flowers.

Melica flava is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
