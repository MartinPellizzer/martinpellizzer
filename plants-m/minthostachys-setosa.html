Minthostachys setosa

Minthostachys setosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf germander, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of purple or blue. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
