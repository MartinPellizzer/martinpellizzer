Meeboldia

Meeboldia, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. Meeboldia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively easy to care for.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
