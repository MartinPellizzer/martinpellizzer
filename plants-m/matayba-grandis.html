Matayba grandis

Matayba grandis, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, light-gray bark and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The tree produces striking yellow flowers that resemble bells, which bloom in clusters during the dry season. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, hard seeds.

Matayba grandis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its timber and medicinal properties.
