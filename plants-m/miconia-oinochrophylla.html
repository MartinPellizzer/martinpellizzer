Miconia oinochrophylla

Miconia oinochrophylla, commonly known as the golden-veined miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent yellow veins. The plant produces showy, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
