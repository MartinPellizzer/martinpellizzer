Mucuna amblyodon

Mucuna amblyodon, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces elongated, hairy pods containing several elliptical seeds. It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Mucuna amblyodon is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
