Machaerium longifolium

Machaerium longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved machaerium, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by elongated pods containing several seeds.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits.
