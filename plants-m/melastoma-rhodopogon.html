Melastoma rhodopogon

Melastoma rhodopogon, commonly known as the red-flowered melastoma, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, vibrant red or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments such as forests or undergrowth. Its leaves are usually opposite and ovate, with a glossy surface, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Melastoma rhodopogon is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
