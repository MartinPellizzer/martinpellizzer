Morus constantinopolitana

Morus constantinopolitana, commonly known as the Constantinople mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to the Middle East and parts of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed and have a distinctive silvery underside. The tree produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance.

The species is also valued for its role in silk production, as it is a host plant for the silkworm.
