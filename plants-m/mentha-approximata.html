Mentha approximata

Mentha approximata, commonly known as field mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, giving it a refreshing, minty scent.

Field mint is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its therapeutic properties.
