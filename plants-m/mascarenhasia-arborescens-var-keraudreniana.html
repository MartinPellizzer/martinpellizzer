Mascarenhasia arborescens var. keraudreniana

Mascarenhasia arborescens var. keraudreniana, commonly known as the Mascarene tree fern, is a rare and unique plant native to the Mascarene Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It is a variant of the tree fern Mascarenhasia arborescens, characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds. The plant exhibits a distinctive morphology with a well-developed root system and a crown of long, pinnate leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to the tropical climate of its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
