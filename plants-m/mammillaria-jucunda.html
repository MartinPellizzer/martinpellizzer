Mammillaria jucunda

Mammillaria jucunda, commonly known as the pink mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, globular shape and is covered in white to pink spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
