Mendoncia tetragona

Mendoncia tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled mendoncia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its square, angular stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.
