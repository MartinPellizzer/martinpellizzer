Millingtonia sambucina

Millingtonia sambucina, commonly known as the black elderberry, is a shrub native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 2-6 meters and features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are small, clustered, and usually white or pale yellow, appearing before the leaves in spring.

The berries are widely used in traditional medicine and culinary applications due to their high antioxidant content.
