Melothria velutina

Melothria velutina, commonly known as the velvet leaf melon, is a tropical climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and squash. The plant produces small, green, edible fruits that are typically harvested when they are still immature. Its leaves are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence, giving it its common name.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruits.
