Myrcia rubella

Myrcia rubella, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark red berries that are edible. The plant has glossy, elliptical leaves and produces fragrant, white flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Myrcia rubella is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in local medicine.
