Merostachys mexicana

Merostachys mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican grass or Mexican silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray foliage and delicate, fluffy seed heads that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a soft, fuzzy texture.

Merostachys mexicana is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
