Moschatella cordi

Moschatella cordi, commonly known as the heartleaf moschatella, is a rare aquatic fern native to southern Africa.

It features delicate, heart-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating on the water's surface. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically located on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments with soft, acidic water.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
