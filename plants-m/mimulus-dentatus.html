Mimulus dentatus

Mimulus dentatus, commonly known as the toothed monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking orange or yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in streambanks and wet meadows.

Mimulus dentatus is a perennial herb that plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
