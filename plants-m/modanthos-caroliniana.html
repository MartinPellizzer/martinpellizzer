Modanthos caroliniana

Modanthos caroliniana, commonly known as the Carolina lily, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, white to pinkish flowers with a distinctive, recurved shape that resembles a crown. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and native plant gardens.
