Myrtus pavonii

Myrtus pavonii, commonly known as the purple myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically red or purple when mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and rocky garden environments.

Myrtus pavonii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery and traditional medicine.
