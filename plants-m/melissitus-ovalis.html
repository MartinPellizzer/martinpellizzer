Melissitus ovalis

Melissitus ovalis, commonly known as the oval leaf melissitus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Melissitaceae.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or orange. The plant is native to specific regions in southern Africa, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, and its growth habit is generally shrub-like with multiple stems.

Due to its ornamental value and unique floral structure, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized collections.
