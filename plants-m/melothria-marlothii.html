Melothria marlothii

Melothria marlothii, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a tropical shrub native to southern Africa.

It produces small, orange-yellow berries that are edible and have a tangy, sweet flavor. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are yellow with five petals and are pollinated by insects.

The fruit is used in culinary applications and has traditional medicinal uses in some African cultures.
