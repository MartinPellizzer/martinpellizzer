Manilkara balata var. domingensis

Manilkara balata var. domingensis, commonly known as the rubber tree or balata tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and smooth, gray bark. The plant is notable for producing a latex sap, which has been historically used in the production of natural rubber and chewing gum. It typically grows in lowland rainforests and thrives in humid, warm climates with well-drained soil.

The tree can reach heights of up to 30 meters and is valued for both its ecological and economic significance.
