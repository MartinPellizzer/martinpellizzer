Malapoenna plumbea

Malapoenna plumbea, commonly known as the blue plumbea, is a rare and striking plant native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bluish-gray foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its unique coloration and habitat preferences make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
