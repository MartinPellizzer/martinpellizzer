Myriocarpa inaequilatera

Myriocarpa inaequilatera, commonly known as the unequal-sided myriocarpa, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its thick, fleshy stems that store water. Its fruit is a distinctive, elongated capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Myriocarpa inaequilatera is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
