Miconia amapaensis

Miconia amapaensis, commonly known as the Amapa Miconia, is a tropical shrub native to the Amazon rainforest of northern Brazil.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that are often covered in fine hairs. Its fruits are fleshy berries that contain numerous seeds, facilitating seed dispersal by animals.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
