Mandevilla riparia

Mandevilla riparia, commonly known as the river mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-draining soil. It produces fragrant blooms throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its ability to climb supports the use of trellises or arbors in landscape design.
