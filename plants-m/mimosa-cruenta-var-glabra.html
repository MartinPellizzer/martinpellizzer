Mimosa cruenta var. glabra

Mimosa cruenta var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth mimosa, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves that are typically smooth and oval-shaped, with a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for gardens and landscapes.

The plant is also known for its sensitive leaves, which fold shut when touched, a trait that adds to its unique botanical appeal.
