Maxillaria cornuta

Maxillaria cornuta, commonly known as the horned orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated flowers that resemble horns, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and other structures in its natural habitat. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces fragrant blooms in shades of yellow and orange.

Maxillaria cornuta is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
