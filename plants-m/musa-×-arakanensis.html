Musa × arakanensis

Musa × arakanensis, commonly known as the Arakan banana, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Musaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Myanmar and Thailand, and is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and clustered, yellow-orange fruits. The plant thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil. Its fruit is typically consumed fresh or used in cooking, and it is also valued for its ornamental qualities.

Due to its adaptability, it is cultivated in various regions for both agricultural and horticultural purposes.
