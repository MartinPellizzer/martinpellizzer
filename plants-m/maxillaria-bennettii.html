Maxillaria bennettii

Maxillaria bennettii, commonly known as Bennett's orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of purple and white. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in varying conditions.

The flowers are often clustered in racemes, making it a visually appealing and sought-after ornamental plant.
