Megastylis montana

Megastylis montana, commonly known as the mountain megastylis, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant is distinguished by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter, with bright yellow petals and a prominent central disk. Its leaves are typically narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to conserve moisture in arid environments.

Megastylis montana is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in alpine gardens.
