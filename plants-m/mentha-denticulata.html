Mentha denticulata

Mentha denticulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its fragrant oils and medicinal properties. Mentha denticulata typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Its essential oils, containing menthol and other compounds, are used in various culinary and therapeutic applications.
