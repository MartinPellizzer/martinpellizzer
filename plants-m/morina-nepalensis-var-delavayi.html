Morina nepalensis var. delavayi

Morina nepalensis var. delavayi, commonly known as Delavay's moringa, is a shrub native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and China.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated for ornamental purposes or traditional uses.
