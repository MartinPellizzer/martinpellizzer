Moraea schimperi

Moraea schimperi, commonly known as the Ethiopian gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in the spring and produces vibrant pink or red flowers with a distinctive, narrow corona. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is also cultivated in botanical gardens and for horticultural purposes.
