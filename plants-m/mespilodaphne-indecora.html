Mespilodaphne indecora

Mespilodaphne indecora, commonly known as the ornamental mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant lacks true roots and absorbs nutrients and water directly from its host through specialized structures called haustoria. Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly impact the health of its host tree if left unchecked.
