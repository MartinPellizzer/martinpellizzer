Malva × hybridica

Malva × hybridica, commonly known as the common mallow, is a hybrid species within the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial, reaching heights of 30 to 90 centimeters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in gardens and disturbed areas.

Its fibrous roots and hairy stems are typical features of the mallow genus.
