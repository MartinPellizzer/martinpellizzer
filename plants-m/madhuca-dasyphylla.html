Madhuca dasyphylla

Madhuca dasyphylla, commonly known as the white-flowered mahua, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and elliptical, dark green leaves that are glossy on the upper surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. These flowers are followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds.

It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
