Mimosa herincquiana

Mimosa herincquiana, commonly known as the golden mimosa, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched, a trait known as thigmonasty. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, pod-like fruits. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various climates contribute to its widespread cultivation.
