Myrianthemum

Myrianthemum, commonly known as moss campion, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Caryophyllaceae.

These plants are typically low-growing and have a mat-forming habit, with small, often hairy leaves. The flowers are usually pink or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. They are native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, including Europe, Asia, and North America.

Myrianthemum species are often found in rocky or sandy soils and are valued for their ornamental appeal in gardens.
