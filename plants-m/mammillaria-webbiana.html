Mammillaria webbiana

Mammillaria webbiana, commonly known as the Webb's hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and dense covering of spines, which provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and xeriscaping projects.
