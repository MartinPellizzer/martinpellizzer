Mappia racemosa subsp. brachycarpa

Mappia racemosa subsp.

brachycarpa, commonly known as the short-fruited mappia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in lowland rainforests.

The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. Its fruits are short and globose, containing a single seed, which is an important feature for identification.

The plant has limited economic importance but is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in its native habitat.
