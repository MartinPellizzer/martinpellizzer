Mallotus Plicatus

Mallotus plicatus is a unique plant known for its distinctive leaf structure and adaptability to various environments. It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands. This species is often found in coastal areas and can thrive in sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are folded or pleated, giving it the common name "pleated mallot." The plant is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its potential medicinal uses.
