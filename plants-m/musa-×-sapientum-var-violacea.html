Musa × sapientum var. violacea

Musa × sapientum var. violacea, commonly known as the purple banana, is a variety of the common banana plant characterized by its striking violet-colored leaves and fruit.

This plant belongs to the Musaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia, where it thrives in tropical climates. The leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy texture, while the flowers are typically yellow or green. The fruit, though less commonly consumed than the common banana, is edible and has a slightly tart flavor.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
