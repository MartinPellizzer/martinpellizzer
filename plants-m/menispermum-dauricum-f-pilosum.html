Menispermum dauricum f. pilosum

Menispermum dauricum f. pilosum, commonly known as the hairy arrowhead, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant features a climbing habit, with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and typically appear in clusters, followed by dark blue berries.

The foliage is often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it the "hairy" descriptor in its botanical name.
