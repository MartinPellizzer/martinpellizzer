Maerua koratensis

Maerua koratensis, commonly known as the Korat thornapple, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, prickly leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, round fruits that are edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Maerua koratensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
