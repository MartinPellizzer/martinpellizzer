Menispermum roxburghii

Menispermum roxburghii, commonly known as Indian snakeroot, is a climbing shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces a yellow, fleshy fruit that contains several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional systems of medicine.

Its roots and leaves have been used for their purported health benefits, though caution is advised due to potential toxicity.
