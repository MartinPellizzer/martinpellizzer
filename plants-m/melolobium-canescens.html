Melolobium canescens

Melolobium canescens, commonly known as the white burr bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces distinctive, spiky, burr-like fruits that contain seeds, aiding in its dispersal. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in shrubland and coastal areas.

Melolobium canescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
