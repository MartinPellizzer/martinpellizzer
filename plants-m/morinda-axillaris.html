Morinda axillaris

Morinda axillaris, commonly known as the red moringa or red kohlrabi, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, featuring alternate, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers, which are followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a woody stem with thorn-like projections.

Morinda axillaris is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
