Mammillaria mucronata

Mammillaria mucronata, commonly known as the sharp-tipped hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Mammillaria mucronata is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
