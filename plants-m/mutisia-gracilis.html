Mutisia gracilis

Mutisia gracilis, commonly known as the slender mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil, Argentina, and Paraguay.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and is commonly known as the sunflower family. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with lance-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a woody stem that becomes more prominent with age.

Mutisia gracilis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
