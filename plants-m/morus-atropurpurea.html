Morus atropurpurea

Morus atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn a striking purple-red color in the fall. The tree produces small, aggregate fruits known as mulberries, which are typically dark purple when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with a distinctive flaky texture.
