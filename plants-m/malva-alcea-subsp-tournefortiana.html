Malva alcea subsp. tournefortiana

Malva alcea subsp.

tournefortiana, commonly known as the Turkish mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features broad, velvety leaves with a distinctive heart-shaped outline and produces showy, pale pink to lavender flowers.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with sturdy stems that often branch near the base. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
