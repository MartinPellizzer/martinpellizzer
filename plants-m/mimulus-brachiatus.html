Mimulus brachiatus

Mimulus brachiatus, commonly known as the branched monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its showy, orange-red or yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as streambanks and forest floors. Its branched stems and opposite leaves contribute to its distinctive appearance.

Mimulus brachiatus is a perennial herb that plays a role in supporting local pollinators like bees and butterflies.
