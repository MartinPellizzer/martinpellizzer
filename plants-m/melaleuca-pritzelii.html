Melaleuca pritzelii

Melaleuca pritzelii, commonly known as the Pritzel's bottlebrush, is a species of shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that are typically red or pink in color. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and sandplains.

Melaleuca pritzelii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
