Malva hybrida

Malva hybrida, commonly known as the common mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture and produces showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other plants like cotton and hibiscus.

Malva hybrida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
