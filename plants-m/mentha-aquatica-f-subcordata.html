Mentha aquatica f. subcordata

Mentha aquatica f. subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water mint, is a variant of the water mint species native to moist environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and aromatic, slightly hairy foliage. This plant typically grows in wet meadows, stream banks, and other aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats. It produces small, pale blue or white flowers arranged in whorled clusters along the stems.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in herbal remedies for digestive and respiratory ailments.
