Mentha longifolia var. poliophylla

Mentha longifolia var. poliophylla, commonly known as woolly mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and square, erect stems. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Woolly mint is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for its aromatic and cooling properties.
