Mimusops kirkii

Mimusops kirkii, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs. The tree produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the rainy season. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The plant is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in traditional medicine and ornamental horticulture.
