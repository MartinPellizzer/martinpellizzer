Mentha × rotundifolia var. psilostachya

Mentha × rotundifolia var. psilostachya, commonly known as round-leaf mint, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both Mentha rotundifolia and Mentha × piperita.

It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features rounded, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, pale blue to purple flowers. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, making it useful in culinary and medicinal applications.

This variety is valued for its distinct flavor and is often used in herbal teas and as a flavoring agent.
