Myrcia hostmanniana

Myrcia hostmanniana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The fruit, which is small and berry-like, contains seeds that are dispersed by animals.
