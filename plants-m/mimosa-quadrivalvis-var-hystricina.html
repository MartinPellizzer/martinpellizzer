Mimosa quadrivalvis var. hystricina

Mimosa quadrivalvis var. hystricina, commonly known as the velvet mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage and delicate, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its attractive appearance and ease of care, it is often cultivated in both garden beds and container settings.
