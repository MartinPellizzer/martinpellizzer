Malva longiflora

Malva longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered mallows, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces striking, pale pink to lavender flowers with five to seven petals. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with hairy stems that are often reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower landscapes.

The flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
