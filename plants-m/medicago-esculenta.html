Medicago esculenta

Medicago esculenta, commonly known as the edible medick, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that develop into pods containing edible seeds. It is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in agricultural settings.

The seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source for livestock.
