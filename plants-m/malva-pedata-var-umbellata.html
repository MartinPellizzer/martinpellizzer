Malva pedata var. umbellata

Malva pedata var. umbellata, commonly known as the clustered mallows, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge. The plant produces clusters of pale pink to white flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its soothing properties.
