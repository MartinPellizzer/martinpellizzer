Muscari botryoides subsp. lelievrei

Muscari botryoides subsp.

lelievrei, commonly known as the Lelievre's grape hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe. It features dense clusters of small, fragrant, violet-blue flowers that resemble tiny grapes, hence its common name.

The plant produces strap-shaped, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and early spring blooming habit.
