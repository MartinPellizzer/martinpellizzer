Malus halliana f. parkmannii

Malus halliana f. parkmannii, commonly known as the Parkmann's crabapple, is a compact, ornamental shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The foliage is typically dark green, providing a striking contrast to the blossoms. The fruit is small, red, and edible, though not commonly cultivated for consumption.

This variety is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
