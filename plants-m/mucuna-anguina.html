Mucuna anguina

Mucuna anguina, commonly known as the devil's bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It produces distinctive, spiral-shaped pods that contain numerous small, dark seeds. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and hairy stems, which aid in its climbing growth habit. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The seeds of Mucuna anguina contain high levels of alkaloids, which have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
