Mikania apiifolia

Mikania apiifolia, commonly known as the leafy morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant has hairy stems and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its rapid growth and invasive potential make it a notable species in ecological studies.
