Mammea papyracea

Mammea papyracea, commonly known as the paperbark tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that resembles paper, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers followed by orange to red fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
