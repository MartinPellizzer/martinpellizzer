Melanoxerus suavissimus

Melanoxerus suavissimus, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in the dappled light. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a subtle, sweet fragrance that attracts pollinators like bees and moths.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
