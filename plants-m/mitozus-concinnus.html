Mitozus concinnus

Mitozus concinnus, commonly known as the elegant mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that thrives on the branches of specific host trees.

It possesses chlorophyll and can perform photosynthesis, though it also draws nutrients and water from its host through specialized root structures called haustoria. The plant produces small, white or pale green berries that are a source of food for various bird species. Its leaves are typically small, oval-shaped, and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

Mitozus concinnus is often found in temperate forests and is known for its unique ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
