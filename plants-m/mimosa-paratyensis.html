Mimosa paratyensis

Mimosa paratyensis, commonly known as the Brazilian sensitive plant, is a flowering shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in spherical clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Mimosa paratyensis is also known for its rapid growth and ability to adapt to various environmental conditions.
