Mentha sylvestris var. diffusa

Mentha sylvestris var. diffusa, commonly known as wild mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are typically a dark green color. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, which appear in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Mentha sylvestris var. diffusa is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
