Manfreda elongata

Manfreda elongata, commonly known as the long-leaf manfreda, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright, cylindrical stems and clusters of striking, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant has fleshy, narrow leaves that are adapted to store water, making it well-suited for arid environments. It typically blooms in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant touch to desert landscapes.

Manfreda elongata is often cultivated in xeriscapes due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
