Morgania rotundifolia

Morgania rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf morgania, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its rounded, lobed leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in the understory of forests. It blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Morgania rotundifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
