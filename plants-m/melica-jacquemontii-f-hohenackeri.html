Melica jacquemontii f. hohenackeri

Melica jacquemontii f. hohenackeri, commonly known as the Hohenacker's milletgrass, is a variant of the species Melica jacquemontii, which belongs to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and typically has narrow, grass-like leaves. The inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is often enclosed in a leaf-like bract. This subspecies is native to regions of Central Asia and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
