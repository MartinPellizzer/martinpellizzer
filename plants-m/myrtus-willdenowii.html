Myrtus willdenowii

Myrtus willdenowii, commonly known as the Cape myrtle, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by dark blue berries. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and often have a distinctive aromatic scent.

Myrtus willdenowii is well adapted to coastal environments and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
