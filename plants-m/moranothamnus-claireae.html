Moranothamnus claireae

Moranothamnus claireae, commonly known as the Claire's thamnus, is a low-growing shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered plant.
