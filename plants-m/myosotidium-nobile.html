Myosotidium nobile

Myosotidium nobile, commonly known as the silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It features dense clusters of small, silver-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, cylindrical stems and basal leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in nutrient-poor, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Myosotidium nobile is well adapted to cold climates and can survive harsh winter conditions.
