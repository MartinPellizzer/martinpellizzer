Monanthes × gomerensis

Monanthes × gomerensis, commonly known as the Gomer's monanthes, is a hybrid plant species native to specific regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are often evergreen, providing a basal rosette structure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its unique floral display.
