Matricaria multiflora

Matricaria multiflora, commonly known as the common chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with white petals, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has finely divided, aromatic leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.

The flowers are often used to make tea, known for its calming properties.
