Myristica tubiflora

Myristica tubiflora, commonly known as the balsam tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, oily seeds that are used in traditional medicine. The plant produces large, leathery leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by fleshy, orange-red fruits. Its essential oils are valued for their aromatic properties and are used in perfumes and culinary applications.

The tree is also known for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil.
