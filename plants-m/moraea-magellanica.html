Moraea magellanica

Moraea magellanica, commonly known as the Magellan lily, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in the temperate regions of Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family, which includes many ornamental plants known for their showy flowers. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, often with a contrasting throat. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Moraea magellanica is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
