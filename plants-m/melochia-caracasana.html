Melochia caracasana

Melochia caracasana, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Mexican morning glory, is a tropical flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive red or purple throat. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Melochia caracasana is also known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
