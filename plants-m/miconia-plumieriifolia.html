Miconia plumieriifolia

Miconia plumieriifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of soft, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, spiny fruit capsules. Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types make it an invasive species in some regions.

The plant's botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a challenge for land managers.
