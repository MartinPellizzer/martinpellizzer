Mutisia discoidea

Mutisia discoidea, commonly known as the "yellow mutisia," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Mutisia discoidea is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands.
