Matthiola sinuata f. ligurica

Matthiola sinuata f. ligurica, commonly known as the Ligurian stock, is a cultivar of the stock plant, characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse flowering.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Italy. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, four-petaled flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal and historical significance.
