Mascagnia hiraea

Mascagnia hiraea, commonly known as the desert honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has small, elliptical leaves and produces woody, branching stems that are adapted to arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The plant's botanical features reflect its evolutionary adaptation to survive in harsh, dry climates.
