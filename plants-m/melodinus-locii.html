Melodinus locii

Melodinus locii, commonly known as the Harmonic Vine, is a rare flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant's leaves are heart-shaped and have a glossy, dark green surface, while its bark is smooth and pale with faint, wavy patterns. Melodinus locii is notable for its unique ability to produce a faint, musical hum when its flowers are disturbed by wind or touch.

This plant is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic appeal and intriguing biological properties.
