Mimosa chaetosphaera

Mimosa chaetosphaera, commonly known as the hairy mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features soft, hairy foliage and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
