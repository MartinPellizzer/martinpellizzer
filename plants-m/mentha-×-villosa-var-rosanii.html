Mentha × villosa var. rosanii

Mentha × villosa var. rosanii, commonly known as the woolly mint or rose mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and aromatic, menthol-rich leaves that emit a strong, cool scent. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and prefers well-drained, moist soils in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically pink or violet, appearing in whorled clusters along the stems.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of essential oils used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.
