Mentha syrmiensis

Mentha syrmiensis, commonly known as the Sirmium mint, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, minty fragrance, while its flowers are small and clustered in whorls.

Mentha syrmiensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
