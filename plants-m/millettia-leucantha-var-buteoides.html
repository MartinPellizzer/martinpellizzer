Millettia leucantha var. buteoides

Millettia leucantha var. buteoides, commonly known as the white-flowered millettia, is a tropical climbing plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its attractive foliage and floral display.
