Malvaviscus conzattii

Malvaviscus conzattii, commonly known as the Mexican mallow or Zanzibar mallow, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its vibrant orange or red flowers, which bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
