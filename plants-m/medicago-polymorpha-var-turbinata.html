Medicago polymorpha var. turbinata

Medicago polymorpha var. turbinata, commonly known as the Turkish clover, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 30 cm tall, featuring trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in forage or as a cover crop.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
