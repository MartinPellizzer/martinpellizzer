Malachra cordata

Malachra cordata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed hakea, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically visited by insects such as bees and flies. It is adapted to grow in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Malachra cordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering habit.
