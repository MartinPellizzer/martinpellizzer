Myrceugenia myrcioides var. hypericifolia

Myrceugenia myrcioides var. hypericifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle or hypericifoliate myrtle, is a species native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically orange to red in color. It is valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional medicine.

The species is also appreciated in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to shaded environments.
