Macrostemma vitifolia

Macrostemma vitifolia, commonly known as the grapeleaf macrostemma, is a climbing perennial plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy green leaves that are often mistaken for grape leaves due to their appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by round, fleshy fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for both ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
