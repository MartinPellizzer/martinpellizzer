Melothria samoensis

Melothria samoensis, commonly known as the Samoan sourberry, is a small, trailing vine native to the island of Savai'i in Samoa.

It produces small, green, edible fruits that are typically sour and tart in flavor. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and thrives in humid, tropical climates. It is often found growing in shaded, moist areas such as along forest floors or in rocky crevices.

The fruit is sometimes used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
