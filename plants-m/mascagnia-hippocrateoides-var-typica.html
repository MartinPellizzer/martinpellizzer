Mascagnia hippocrateoides var. typica

Mascagnia hippocrateoides var. typica, commonly known as the Florida nightshade, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but have a bitter taste. The plant has hairy, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces yellowish-white flowers. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

The species is noted for its adaptability and resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
