Macrocarpaea cortinae

Macrocarpaea cortinae, commonly known as the Canary Islands rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and clusters of pink or red flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the arid climate of its native habitat. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are dispersed by animals.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical studies.
