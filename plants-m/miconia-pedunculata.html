Miconia pedunculata

Miconia pedunculata, commonly known as the purple miconia, is a tropical shrub native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that are typically surrounded by showy purple bracts. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

Miconia pedunculata is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
