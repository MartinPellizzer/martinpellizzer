Mastichodendron bojerianum

Mastichodendron bojerianum, commonly known as the Bojer's mastichodendron, is a rare and unique plant native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spirally arranged leaves that form a dense, rosette-like structure. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in arid environments and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
