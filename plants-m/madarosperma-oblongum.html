Madarosperma oblongum

Madarosperma oblongum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved madar, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 60 centimeters in height. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silky hair. Its small, yellowish flowers appear in clusters and bloom from late spring to early summer.

The plant is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
