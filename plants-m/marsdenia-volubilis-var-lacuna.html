Marsdenia volubilis var. lacuna

Marsdenia volubilis var. lacuna, commonly known as the lacunar Marsdenia, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals and a prominent corona, and the plant produces small, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its attractive foliage and floral display.
