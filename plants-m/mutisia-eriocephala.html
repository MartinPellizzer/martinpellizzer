Mutisia eriocephala

Mutisia eriocephala, commonly known as the hairy mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a woody stem with dense, velvety hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered with fine pubescence.

The showy yellow flowers appear in clusters and are surrounded by white bracts, making it a notable ornamental plant.
