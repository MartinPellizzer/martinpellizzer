Mostuea penduliflora

Mostuea penduliflora, commonly known as the hanging bromeliad, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant produces long, pendant inflorescences that bear small, colorful flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. Its leaves are typically broad and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Mostuea penduliflora is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ease of care.
