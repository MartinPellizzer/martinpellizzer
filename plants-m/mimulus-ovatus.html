Mimulus ovatus

Mimulus ovatus, commonly known as the yellow monkeyflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, orange to yellow flowers that bloom from spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Mimulus ovatus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in stream banks and meadows.
