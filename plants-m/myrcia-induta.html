Myrcia induta

Myrcia induta, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Myrcia induta is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
