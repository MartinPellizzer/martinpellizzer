Muraltia beiliana

Muraltia beiliana, commonly known as the red muraltia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by edible, red drupes that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Muraltia beiliana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
