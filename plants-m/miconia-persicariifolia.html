Miconia persicariifolia

Miconia persicariifolia, commonly known as the purple miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly pubescent surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that are often borne on long peduncles. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous tiny seeds.

Miconia persicariifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.
