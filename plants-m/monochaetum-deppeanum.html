Monochaetum deppeanum

Monochaetum deppeanum, commonly known as the Deppe's spikegrass, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, leaf-like blades. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
