Musa erecta

Musa erecta, commonly known as the tall banana plant, is a herbaceous perennial belonging to the Musaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces large, broad leaves that are typically a deep green in color. It flowers in clusters at the top of the stem, leading to the development of elongated, curved fruits.

Musa erecta is cultivated for its edible fruits and is an important crop in tropical regions.
