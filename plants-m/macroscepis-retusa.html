Macroscepis retusa

Macroscepis retusa, commonly known as the smoothleaf macroscepis, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and slightly hairy on the underside. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
