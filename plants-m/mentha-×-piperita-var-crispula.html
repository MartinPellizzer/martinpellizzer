Mentha × piperita var. crispula

Mentha × piperita var. crispula, commonly known as peppermint crispleaf, is a hybrid mint species characterized by its serrated, slightly curled leaves.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in moist, well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers in whorled clusters, which are typically borne on erect, square stems. Its aromatic leaves contain high concentrations of menthol, giving it a strong, cooling scent and making it valuable in herbal remedies.

This variety is often cultivated for its essential oils and used in aromatherapy and culinary applications.
