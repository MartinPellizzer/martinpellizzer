Myrtus communis var. rodesii

Myrtus communis var. rodesii, commonly known as the Cyprus myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Cyprus.

It features small, aromatic leaves with a glossy dark green surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Its ornamental value and aromatic properties make it a popular choice for gardens and traditional uses.
