Millettia auriculata

Millettia auriculata, commonly known as the ear-shaped acacia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, branched inflorescences, which bloom in summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

The seeds are enclosed in elongated pods, and the plant is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
