Microlicia euphorbioides

Microlicia euphorbioides, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Microlicia euphorbioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
