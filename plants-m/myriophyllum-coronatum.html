Myriophyllum coronatum

Myriophyllum coronatum, commonly known as water starwort, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in the Northern Hemisphere.

It has slender, branching stems that are often covered in small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces tiny, white flowers that are typically borne in clusters at the base of the stems. It thrives in still or slow-moving waters and is often found in ponds, lakes, and wetlands.

Myriophyllum coronatum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
