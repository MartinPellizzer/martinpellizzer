Myrcia paivae

Myrcia paivae, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters. The plant has dark green, elliptical leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, red, and somewhat fleshy, containing several seeds.

Myrcia paivae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
