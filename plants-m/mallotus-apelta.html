Mallotus apelta

Mallotus apelta, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-barked trunk and elliptical, glossy green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is also valued for its timber.
