Masdevallia hartman-filii

Masdevallia hartman-filii, commonly known as the Hartman-Filii masdevallia, is a rare and exquisite orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often colorful flowers with a unique structure. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, thriving in humid, shaded environments with high altitude. Its leaves are usually elongated and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, which helps in reducing water loss.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and highly sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
