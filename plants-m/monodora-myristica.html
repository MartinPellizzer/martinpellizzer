Monodora myristica

Monodora myristica, commonly known as African nutmeg, is a tropical evergreen tree native to western and central Africa.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by round, orange fruits containing aromatic seeds. The seeds, often referred to as "nutmeg," are rich in essential oils and have a distinct, spicy aroma.

Due to its unique properties, Monodora myristica is valued both for its medicinal uses and as a spice in traditional cuisines.
