Melaleuca arida

Melaleuca arida, commonly known as the river paperbark, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, flaky bark that peels off in strips, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to withstand dry conditions.

Melaleuca arida is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils, showing a high tolerance for saline environments.
