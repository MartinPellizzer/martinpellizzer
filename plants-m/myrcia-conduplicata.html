Myrcia conduplicata

Myrcia conduplicata, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The fruit, which is a small, fleshy berry, is also utilized in local remedies and culinary practices.
