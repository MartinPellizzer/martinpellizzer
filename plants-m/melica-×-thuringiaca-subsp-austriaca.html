Melica × thuringiaca subsp. austriaca

Melica × thuringiaca subsp.

austriaca, commonly known as the Austrian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Europe. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairless stems that often form dense clumps.

The plant produces spike-like inflorescences bearing small, two-lobed spikelets, which are typically green to purplish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue and a prominent midrib.

This subspecies is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks, where it contributes to soil stabilization and provides habitat for various invertebrates.
