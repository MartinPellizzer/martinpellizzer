Molinia caerulea f. variegata

Molinia caerulea f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated moor grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive variegated foliage, featuring white and green stripes on its narrow leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wet meadows, bogs, and heathlands. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing loose clusters of pale blue to purple flowers.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
