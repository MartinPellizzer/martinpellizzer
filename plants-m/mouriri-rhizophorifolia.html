Mouriri rhizophorifolia

Mouriri rhizophorifolia, commonly known as the mangrove mouriri, is a tropical plant native to coastal regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that thrives in saline and waterlogged soils, often found in mangrove ecosystems. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are usually opposite in arrangement. Its flowers are yellow and clustered in inflorescences, while its fruits are small, woody capsules that contain several seeds.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and supporting local biodiversity.
