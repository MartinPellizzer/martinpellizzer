Merendera figlalii

Merendera figlalii, commonly known as the golden leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that exhibit a striking golden-yellow coloration. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round fruits that resemble figs in appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
