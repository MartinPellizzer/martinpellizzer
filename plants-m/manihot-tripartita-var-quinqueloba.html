Manihot tripartita var. quinqueloba

Manihot tripartita var. quinqueloba, commonly known as the African arrowroot, is a starchy tuberous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its swollen, finger-like roots that are rich in carbohydrates. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in warm climates. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a distinctive waxy coating.
