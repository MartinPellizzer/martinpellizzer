Micromeria cosentina

Micromeria cosentina, commonly known as the Mediterranean sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of pink or purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and rocky habitats. Its flowers bloom in the spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Micromeria cosentina is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
