Myriophyllum muelleri

Myriophyllum muelleri, commonly known as the water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to Australia.

It has finely divided, feather-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white, and it is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats. Myriophyllum muelleri plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

It is also sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in ponds and water gardens.
