Machilus montanus

Machilus montanus, commonly known as the mountain machilus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like China, Vietnam, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically lance-shaped and have a glossy surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by dark blue to black berries.

Machilus montanus is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and potential medicinal properties.
