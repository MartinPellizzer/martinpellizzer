Mammillaria fuscohamata

Mammillaria fuscohamata, commonly known as the dark-hairless mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white to pale gray spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It prefers well-draining soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
