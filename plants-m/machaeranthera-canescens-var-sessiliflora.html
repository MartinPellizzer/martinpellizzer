Machaeranthera canescens var. sessiliflora

Machaeranthera canescens var. sessiliflora, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and sessile, giving the plant its varietal name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
