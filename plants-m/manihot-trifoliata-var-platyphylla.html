Manihot trifoliata var. platyphylla

Manihot trifoliata var. platyphylla, commonly known as the arrowleaf manioc, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which are broad and often heart-shaped, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible roots that are used in various traditional cuisines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland tropical regions.

Due to its adaptability and nutritional value, it is an important plant in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
