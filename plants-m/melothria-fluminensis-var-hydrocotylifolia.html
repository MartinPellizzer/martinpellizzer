Melothria fluminensis var. hydrocotylifolia

Melothria fluminensis var. hydrocotylifolia, commonly known as the water lettuce melon, is a variant of the melothria genus native to South America.

It is characterized by its swollen, fleshy fruits that resemble small melons, often covered in a white, waxy coating. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, thriving in shallow water or moist soil. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a glossy surface, and it produces small, yellow flowers.

This variant is valued for its unique appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
