Mentzelia soratensis

Mentzelia soratensis, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with hairy, often glandular stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Mentzelia soratensis is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
