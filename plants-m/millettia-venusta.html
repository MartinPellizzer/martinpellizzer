Millettia venusta

Millettia venusta, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the rainy season. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and the tree can grow up to 20 meters tall.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
