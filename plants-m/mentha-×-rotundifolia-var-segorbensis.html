Mentha × rotundifolia var. segorbensis

Mentha × rotundifolia var. segorbensis, commonly known as Segobranca, is a hybrid mint species characterized by its distinctive rounded leaves and strong aromatic properties.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are widely used in traditional medicine for their cooling and digestive effects.

Due to its unique morphology and medicinal value, it is a subject of interest in botanical and horticultural studies.
