Myristica chartacea

Myristica chartacea, commonly known as the paper nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and fragrant, reddish-brown fruits. The plant produces an aromatic resin known as "chartaceum," which is used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent. Its seeds, often referred to as "nutmeg," are enclosed in a fibrous, paper-like husk, giving it its common name.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
