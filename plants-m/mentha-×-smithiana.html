Mentha × smithiana

Mentha × smithiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaf peppermint, is a hybrid mint species that exhibits a combination of traits from its parent species, Mentha viridis and Mentha piperita.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, serrated leaves that are often a vibrant green. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems, typically in late summer. Mentha × smithiana is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is valued in horticulture for its aromatic foliage and potential use in herbal remedies.
