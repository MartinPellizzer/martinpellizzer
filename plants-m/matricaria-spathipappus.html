Matricaria spathipappus

Matricaria spathipappus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved chamomile, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features finely divided, aromatic leaves and yellow flowers that develop into small, fluffy white pappus structures. It is often cultivated for its calming properties and used in herbal teas.

Matricaria spathipappus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and medicinal purposes.
