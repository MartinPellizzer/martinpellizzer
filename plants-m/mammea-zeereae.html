Mammea zeereae

Mammea zeereae, commonly known as the African starfruit tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by bright red, star-shaped fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor.

Its fruits are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine, highlighting its botanical and cultural significance.
