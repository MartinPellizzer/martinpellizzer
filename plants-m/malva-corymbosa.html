Malva corymbosa

Malva corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered mallows, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces showy, pink to purple flowers in clusters. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

Its fibrous roots and hairy stems are characteristic of the genus Malva.
