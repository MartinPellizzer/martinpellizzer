Manulea parviflora

Manulea parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered manulea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in areas with high altitudes and cool climates. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months of the year. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Manulea parviflora is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to its harsh environment.
