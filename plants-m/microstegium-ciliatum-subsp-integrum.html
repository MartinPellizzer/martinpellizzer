Microstegium ciliatum subsp. integrum

Microstegium ciliatum subsp.

integrum, commonly known as the smooth hillside grass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern North America. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland edges and open fields.

The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged along the stem.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
