Myrtus commersonii

Myrtus commersonii, commonly known as the common myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves that are aromatic when crushed. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Myrtus commersonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
