Myrcia gestasiana

Myrcia gestasiana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and fragrant, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in humid, shaded environments. Its fruits are small, round berries that contain seeds, and the plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal properties.

Myrcia gestasiana plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
