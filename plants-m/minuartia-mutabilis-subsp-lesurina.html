Minuartia mutabilis subsp. lesurina

Minuartia mutabilis subsp.

lesurina, commonly known as the variable rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette, and it is known for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.

This subspecies is notable for its morphological variability, which contributes to its common name.
