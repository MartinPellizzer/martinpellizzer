Mentha aquatica var. clinopodiifolia

Mentha aquatica var. clinopodiifolia, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herb native to moist environments in Europe and Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters during the summer months. Its aromatic leaves contain menthol and other volatile oils, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

It thrives in wet soils near streams, marshes, and ponds, making it a common sight in riparian habitats.
