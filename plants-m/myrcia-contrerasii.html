Myrcia contrerasii

Myrcia contrerasii, commonly known as the Peruvian myrtle, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to subtropical climates.
