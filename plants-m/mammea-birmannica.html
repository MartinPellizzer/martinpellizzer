Mammea birmannica

Mammea birmannica, commonly known as the Birmann's mammea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that are edible. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.

The tree thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
