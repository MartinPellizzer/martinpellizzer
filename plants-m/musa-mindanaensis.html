Musa mindanaensis

Musa mindanaensis, commonly known as the Balangay banana, is a tropical plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and clusters of edible berries. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its flowers are usually hidden within the leaf axils, and the fruit develops in a unique, elongated shape.

This species is valued not only for its fruit but also for its ornamental qualities and cultural significance in its native region.
