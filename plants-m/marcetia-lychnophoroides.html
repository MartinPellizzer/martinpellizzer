Marcetia lychnophoroides

Marcetia lychnophoroides, commonly known as the African candlewood, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces legume pods that contain small, round seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in regions with limited rainfall.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both ecological and potential medicinal purposes.
