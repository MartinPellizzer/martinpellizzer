Myriophyllum titikakense

Myriophyllum titikakense, commonly known as the Lake Titicaca watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to South America, specifically Lake Titicaca.

It has slender, branching stems that are often ribbon-like in appearance, and it produces small, green to reddish flowers. The plant is characterized by its whorled arrangement of leaves, which are typically narrow and pointed. It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing habitat and food for aquatic organisms.

However, it is considered an invasive species in some regions where it has been introduced.
