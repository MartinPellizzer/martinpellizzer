Myrrhis ammoides

Myrrhis ammoides, commonly known as the common myrrh, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense clumps.

The plant is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
