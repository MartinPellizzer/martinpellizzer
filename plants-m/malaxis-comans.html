Malaxis comans

Malaxis comans, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Malaxis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are elongated and narrow, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers clustered in a spike.

Malaxis comans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
