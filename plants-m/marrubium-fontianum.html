Marrubium fontianum

Marrubium fontianum, commonly known as the mountain horehound, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates. The plant features a woody stem with hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a grayish-blue hue. Its small, white or pale blue flowers bloom in clusters during the summer months.

Marrubium fontianum is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its potential anti-inflammatory and expectorant effects.
