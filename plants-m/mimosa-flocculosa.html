Mimosa flocculosa

Mimosa flocculosa, commonly known as the woolly mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces fluffy, seed-bearing pods that are distinctive in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid environments.

Mimosa flocculosa is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique texture and adaptability.
