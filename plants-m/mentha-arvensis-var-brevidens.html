Mentha arvensis var. brevidens

Mentha arvensis var. brevidens, commonly known as narrow-leaved field mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks. It has a strong, minty aroma due to its high concentration of essential oils.

This variety is often used in traditional herbal remedies and as a culinary herb.
