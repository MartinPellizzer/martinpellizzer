Mischobulbum papuanum

Mischobulbum papuanum, commonly known as the Papuan mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to the rainforests of New Guinea.

It attaches itself to the branches of its host trees, such as eucalyptus and other hardwoods, through a specialized root system called a haustellum. The plant relies on its host for water and nutrients, while it provides nectar to attract insects like bees and birds. Its small, white flowers bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for native wildlife.

Mischobulbum papuanum plays a significant ecological role in its habitat by supporting biodiversity and contributing to the forest's nutrient cycle.
