Myrrhis

Myrrhis, commonly known as sweet flag, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Alismataceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic, often yellowish or reddish, resinous sap that exudes from its stems and leaves. The plants typically grow in wet or marshy environments, thriving in temperate and subtropical regions. Myrrhis species are perennial herbs with narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers.

They are valued for their medicinal properties and have been used traditionally in various cultures for their healing and aromatic qualities.
