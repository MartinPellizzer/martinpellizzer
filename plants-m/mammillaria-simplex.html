Mammillaria simplex

Mammillaria simplex, commonly known as the Mexican snowball, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and dense covering of white, woolly hairs that give it a fluffy appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and indoor gardens.
