Moschoxylum affine

Moschoxylum affine, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mosquito plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikes. The plant is native to southern Africa and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. It has a fibrous root system and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.
