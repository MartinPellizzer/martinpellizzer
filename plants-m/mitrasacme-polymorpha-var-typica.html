Mitrasacme polymorpha var. typica

Mitrasacme polymorpha var. typica, commonly known as the purple-flowered mitrasacme, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy inflorescences that resemble a crown, with small, clustered flowers in shades of purple and white. The plant typically grows as an erect herb with slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are predominantly pollinated by insects, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential ecological roles in tropical ecosystems.
