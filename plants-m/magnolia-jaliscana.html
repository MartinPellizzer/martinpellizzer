Magnolia jaliscana

Magnolia jaliscana, commonly known as the Jalisco magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the highlands of Jalisco, Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with a distinctive flaky texture.

Magnolia jaliscana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and seasonal blooms.
