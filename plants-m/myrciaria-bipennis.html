Myrciaria bipennis

Myrciaria bipennis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá-do-mato, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible and commonly used in local cuisine. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and seasonal flowering, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
