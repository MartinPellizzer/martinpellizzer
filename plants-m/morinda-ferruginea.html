Morinda ferruginea

Morinda ferruginea, commonly known as red moringa, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its reddish-brown bark and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
