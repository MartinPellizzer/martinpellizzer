Melodorum lefevrei

Melodorum lefevrei, commonly known as the Lefevre's melodic vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Violaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces slender, winding stems that can reach several meters in length, allowing it to climb trees and other structures. Its flowers emit a faint, sweet fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Melodorum lefevrei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some parts of its native range.
