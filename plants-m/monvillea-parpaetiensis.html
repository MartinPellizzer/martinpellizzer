Monvillea parpaetiensis

Monvillea parpaetiensis, commonly known as the Parpaetian orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, clustered growth habit and striking, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant typically thrives in cool, humid environments with well-drained, acidic soil. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Monvillea parpaetiensis is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
