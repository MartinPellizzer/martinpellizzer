Mespilodaphne bullata

Mespilodaphne bullata, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range from yellow to orange in color. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.
