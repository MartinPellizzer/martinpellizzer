Mimosa nuda var. ramosa

Mimosa nuda var. ramosa, commonly known as the weeping mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that appear in spring. The plant is notable for its drooping branches, which give it a graceful, weeping appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets also make it a potential invasive species in some regions.
