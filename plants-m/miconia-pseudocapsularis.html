Miconia pseudocapsularis

Miconia pseudocapsularis, commonly known as the false capularia, is a tropical plant belonging to the Melastomataceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers with five petals, which bloom in clusters. It is native to the Neotropical region, including parts of South America and the Caribbean.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
