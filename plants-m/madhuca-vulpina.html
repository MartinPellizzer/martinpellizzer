Madhuca vulpina

Madhuca vulpina, commonly known as the candlewood tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and smooth, gray bark. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are thick and leathery, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.

The candlewood tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
