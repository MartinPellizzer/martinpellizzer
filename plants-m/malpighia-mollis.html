Malpighia mollis

Malpighia mollis, commonly known as the golden mallow, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malpighiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a glossy green surface and a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, often containing several seeds.
