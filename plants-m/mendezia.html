Mendezia

Mendezia, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has small, compound leaves and produces pods that contain edible seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Mendezia is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
