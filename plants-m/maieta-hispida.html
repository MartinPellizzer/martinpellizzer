Maieta hispida

Maieta hispida, commonly known as the hairy mayweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It grows in a variety of habitats, including fields, roadsides, and disturbed soils.

Maieta hispida is often found in temperate climates and is part of the Asteraceae family.
