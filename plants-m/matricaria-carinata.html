Matricaria carinata

Matricaria carinata, commonly known as the German chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features finely divided, aromatic leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a distinct, apple-like scent.

Matricaria carinata is often cultivated for its calming properties and is used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.
