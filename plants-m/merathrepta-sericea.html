Merathrepta sericea

Merathrepta sericea, commonly known as the silky merathrepta, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. Its small white to pale yellow flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
