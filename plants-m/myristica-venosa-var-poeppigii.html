Myristica venosa var. poeppigii

Myristica venosa var. poeppigii, commonly known as the white nutmeg or poeppig's myristica, is a species of flowering plant in the Myristicaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, oily seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are elliptical and leathery, while the flowers are small and yellowish-green.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its unique aromatic properties.
