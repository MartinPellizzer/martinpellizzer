Mammillaria amabilis

Mammillaria amabilis, commonly known as the pink fairy or pink mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, globular shape and dense covering of white to pink spines. The plant produces pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
