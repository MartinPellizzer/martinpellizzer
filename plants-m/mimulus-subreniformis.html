Mimulus subreniformis

Mimulus subreniformis, commonly known as the broad-leaf monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red flowers, which are typically tubular in shape and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped or rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in streambanks and wet meadows.

Mimulus subreniformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
