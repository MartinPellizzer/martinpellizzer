Mentha × paludosiformis

Mentha × paludosiformis, commonly known as marsh mint, is a hybrid plant that results from the cross between Mentha aquatica and Mentha rotundifolia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and is often found in wet or marshy environments. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30–60 cm and has square stems with opposite leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in whorls, producing a mild mint scent.

Mentha × paludosiformis is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
