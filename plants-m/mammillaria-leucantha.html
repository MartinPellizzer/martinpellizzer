Mammillaria leucantha

Mammillaria leucantha, commonly known as the white-tipped hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a ribbed structure and spines that are often white or pale in color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for cactus collectors and rock gardens.
