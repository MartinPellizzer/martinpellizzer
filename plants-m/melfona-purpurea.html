Melfona purpurea

Melfona purpurea, commonly known as the purple melfon, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the southern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple petals and serrated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its flowers bloom in late spring, producing a showy display that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Melfona purpurea is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
