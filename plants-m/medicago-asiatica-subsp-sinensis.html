Medicago asiatica subsp. sinensis

Medicago asiatica subsp.

sinensis, commonly known as Chinese needle medick, is a flowering plant native to East Asia. It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, and its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields, along roadsides, and in disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment and as a forage plant.
