Massonia muricata

Massonia muricata, commonly known as the hairy massonia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, commonly referred to as the ice plant family. The plant is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, adding a delicate touch to its appearance.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and low maintenance requirements.
