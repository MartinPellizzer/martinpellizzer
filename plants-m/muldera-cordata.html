Muldera cordata

Muldera cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf mullein, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch. The plant produces showy, pale yellow to greenish flowers that bloom in late summer. Muldera cordata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or wetlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties.
