Monolophus amplexicaulis

Monolophus amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to blue. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and leaves that often wrap around the stems, giving it its common name. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

The flowers bloom in late spring or early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
