Mentha sylvestris f. pahinensis

Mentha sylvestris f. pahinensis, commonly known as the Pahinensian wild mint, is a botanical variant of the wild mint species native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

This subspecies is characterized by its aromatic, slightly serrated leaves and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, including its potential anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.

Its cultivation is often limited to specific ecological niches due to its particular environmental requirements.
