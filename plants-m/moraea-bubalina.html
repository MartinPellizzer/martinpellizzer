Moraea bubalina

Moraea bubalina, commonly known as the African gladiolus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with leaves that are narrow and grass-like. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink and purple, bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
