Melanopsidium nigrum

Melanopsidium nigrum, commonly known as black melanopsidium, is a rare and enigmatic plant species belonging to the family Melanopsidaceae.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is native to specific regions in southern Africa, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in arid conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptations to extreme environments.
