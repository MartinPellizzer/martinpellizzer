Medinilla macrocarpa

Medinilla macrocarpa, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant produces large, fleshy berries that are edible for birds and small mammals. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil and indirect sunlight.

Medinilla macrocarpa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and showy flowers.
