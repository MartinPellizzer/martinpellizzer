Modecca quintuplinervia

Modecca quintuplinervia, commonly known as the five-veined modecca, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of pink and white. The plant has palmate leaves with five distinct veins, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
