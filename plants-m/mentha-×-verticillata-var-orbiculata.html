Mentha × verticillata var. orbiculata

Mentha × verticillata var. orbiculata, commonly known as round-leaf water mint, is a hybrid species of mint native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its rounded, ovate leaves that are often slightly hairy and have a serrated edge. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. It produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters along the stems.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in herbal teas or as a ornamental plant.
