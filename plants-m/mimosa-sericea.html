Mimosa sericea

Mimosa sericea, commonly known as silky mimosas, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellow, spherical flowers that bloom in spring, followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Mimosa sericea is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
