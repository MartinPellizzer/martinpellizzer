Melothria nashii

Melothria nashii, commonly known as the dwarf passionfruit, is a small, herbaceous vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It produces small, edible fruits with a tart flavor, similar to that of passionfruit, though it is much smaller in size. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that develop into orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both gardens and research studies.
