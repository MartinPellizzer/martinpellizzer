Montbretia refracta

Montbretia refracta, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that bear bright orange to red tubular flowers. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Montbretia refracta is also known for its ability to bloom reliably in late summer to early autumn.
