Mattiastrum himalayense subsp. fallax

Mattiastrum himalayense subsp.

fallax, commonly known as the Himalayan false hellebore, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas. It belongs to the family Melanthiaceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and striking, dark green, arrow-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
