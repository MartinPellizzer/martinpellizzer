Maxillaria nigrescens

Maxillaria nigrescens, commonly known as the blackening maxillaria, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers that gradually fade to a lighter pink as they age. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, often forming a rosette shape.

Maxillaria nigrescens is prized for its striking, long-lasting blooms and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
