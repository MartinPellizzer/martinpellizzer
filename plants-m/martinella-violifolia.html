Martinella violifolia

Martinella violifolia, commonly known as the violetleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, violet-hued leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and thrives in well-drained soil. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and linear, lance-shaped leaves.

The flowers, which bloom in spring, are often clustered in inflorescences and attract various pollinators.
