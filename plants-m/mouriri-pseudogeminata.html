Mouriri pseudogeminata

Mouriri pseudogeminata, commonly known as the false twin mouriri, is a species of flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough, leathery texture.

Mouriri pseudogeminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
