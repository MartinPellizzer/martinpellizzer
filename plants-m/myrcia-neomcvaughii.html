Myrcia neomcvaughii

Myrcia neomcvaughii, commonly known as the Neomcvaugh's myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a distinctive fragrant aroma.

The flowers are usually white or pale pink and are borne in clusters, attracting various pollinators.
