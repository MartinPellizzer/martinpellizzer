Mentha arvensis f. licaensis

Mentha arvensis f. licaensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf field mint, is a variant of the broad-leaf mint species.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and a strong, aromatic scent. This plant is native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

The essential oils extracted from its leaves are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.
