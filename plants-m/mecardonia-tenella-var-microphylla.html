Mecardonia tenella var. microphylla

Mecardonia tenella var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved mecardonia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, succulent foliage with small, fleshy leaves that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and Mediterranean-style gardens.
