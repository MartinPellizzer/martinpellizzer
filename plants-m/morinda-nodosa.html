Morinda nodosa

Morinda nodosa, commonly known as the clustered morinda, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its dense, branched growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by orange or red berries. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Morinda nodosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
