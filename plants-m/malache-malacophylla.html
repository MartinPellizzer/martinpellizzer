Malache malacophylla

Malache malacophylla, commonly known as the velvet leaf, is a unique plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Its growth habit is predominantly epiphytic, meaning it often grows on the surface of trees without being parasitic.

The botanical features of Malache malacophylla make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and scientific study.
