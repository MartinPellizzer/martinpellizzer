Mayna yasuniana

Mayna yasuniana, commonly known as the Yasuni orchid, is a rare and endangered species native to the Ecuadorian rainforests.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant thrives in the humid, shaded understory of the Amazonian canopy, requiring specific microclimatic conditions for successful growth. Its survival is threatened by habitat destruction and illegal collection, making conservation efforts crucial for its preservation.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a flagship species for biodiversity in the Yasuni National Park.
