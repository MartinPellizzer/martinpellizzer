Mentha × andersoniana

Mentha × andersoniana, commonly known as the hybrid mint, is a cross between Mentha spicata and Mentha viridiflora.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States, characterized by its square, erect stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Mentha × andersoniana is valued for its aromatic leaves and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
