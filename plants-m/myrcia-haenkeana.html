Myrcia haenkeana

Myrcia haenkeana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. It produces small, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

Myrcia haenkeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has a distinctive, spicy fragrance.
