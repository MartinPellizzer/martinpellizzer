Maieta valenzuelana

Maieta valenzuelana, commonly known as the Valenzuelan mayflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique appearance.

Maieta valenzuelana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
