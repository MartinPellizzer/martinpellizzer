Mammillaria sphaerica subsp. paulii

Mammillaria sphaerica subsp.

paulii, commonly known as the Paul's globe cactus, is a compact, globular cactus native to Mexico. It features a spherical stem with white, woolly spines and small, greenish flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant is characterized by its dense, rounded shape and is often cultivated for its ornamental appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
