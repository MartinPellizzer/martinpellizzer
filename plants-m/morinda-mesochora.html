Morinda mesochora

Morinda mesochora, commonly known as the African madder, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its roots contain compounds used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant is also valued for its dye-producing potential, contributing to its cultural and economic significance in certain regions.
