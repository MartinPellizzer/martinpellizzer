Malva mauritanica

Malva mauritanica, commonly known as the Moroccan mallow, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its fibrous stems and mucilaginous leaves have been used traditionally for their soothing properties.
