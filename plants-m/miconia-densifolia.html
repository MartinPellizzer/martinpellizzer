Miconia densifolia

Miconia densifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in dense, velvety hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which attract various pollinators. Its fruits are small, berry-like structures that ripen to a dark purple or black color.

Miconia densifolia is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its rapid growth rate, making it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive in certain regions.
