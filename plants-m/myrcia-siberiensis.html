Myrcia siberiensis

Myrcia siberiensis, commonly known as Siberian myrtle, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Siberia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that contain aromatic seeds.

Myrcia siberiensis is adapted to cold climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
