Mentha ochroleuca

Mentha ochroleuca, commonly known as yellow mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale yellow to white in color. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a serrated margin and emit a strong, minty fragrance.

Yellow mint is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial shade to full sun.
