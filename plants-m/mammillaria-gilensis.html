Mammillaria gilensis

Mammillaria gilensis, commonly known as the Gil's mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, globular shape and is often covered in white to pale green spines. The plant typically produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
