Maxillaria lactiflora

Maxillaria lactiflora, commonly known as the milk-flowered orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces showy, spurred blooms that are often adorned with a distinctive pink or red lip. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Maxillaria lactiflora is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
