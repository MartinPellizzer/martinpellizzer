Morus barkamensis

Morus barkamensis, commonly known as the African mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and smooth, grey bark. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically eaten by birds and other animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in woodland areas.

The bark and leaves have been used traditionally in herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.
