Morella macfarlanei

Morella macfarlanei, commonly known as the MacFarlane's wax myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of eastern Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in swampy or waterlogged soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, which are often submerged or partially submerged in water. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and have a distinct aromatic scent when crushed.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to aquatic environments.
