Macrolenes nemorosa var. leprosa

Macrolenes nemorosa var. leprosa, commonly known as the leprose woodruff, is a variant of the woodruff plant native to Europe.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale blue, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a distinctive, slightly hairy texture.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
