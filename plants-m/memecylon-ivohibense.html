Memecylon ivohibense

Memecylon ivohibense, commonly known as the Madagascar memecylon, is a small evergreen shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers clustered in dense inflorescences, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds when mature.

Memecylon ivohibense is often found in moist, shaded habitats within the rainforests of Madagascar.
