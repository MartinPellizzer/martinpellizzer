Melanthium nuttallii

Melanthium nuttallii, commonly known as the showy melanthium, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Melanthium nuttallii is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
