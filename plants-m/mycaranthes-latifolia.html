Mycaranthes latifolia

Mycaranthes latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved mycaranthes, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, fleshy fruits. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to arid conditions, allowing it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
