Malapoenna crassifolia

Malapoenna crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf malapoenna, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Malapoenna crassifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
