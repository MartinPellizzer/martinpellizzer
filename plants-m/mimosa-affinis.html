Mimosa affinis

Mimosa affinis, commonly known as the sensitive plant or mimosa pudica, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that fold shut when touched, a response triggered by mechanical stimulation. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warm seasons. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Mimosa affinis is often grown as a decorative plant due to its unique foliage and rapid growth habit.
