Melica smithii

Melica smithii, commonly known as Smith's brome, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant has slender, tufted inflorescences composed of spikelets, which are characteristic of the grass family. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Smith's brome is often found in riparian zones and is adapted to thrive in areas with consistent moisture.
