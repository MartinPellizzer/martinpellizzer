Mucuna occidentalis

Mucuna occidentalis, commonly known as the West Indian velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and trifoliate leaves, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers followed by elongated, flat pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Mucuna occidentalis is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
