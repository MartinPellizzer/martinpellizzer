Machaerium milleflorum

Machaerium milleflorum, commonly known as the purple wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, showy purple flowers. The plant has feathery, trifoliate leaves and produces small, flat pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Machaerium milleflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
