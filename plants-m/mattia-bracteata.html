Mattia bracteata

Mattia bracteata, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow petals and a distinctive star-shaped flower structure. The plant produces small, greenish-white bracts that resemble leaves, which help in photosynthesis. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Mattia bracteata is a rare and endangered species, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
