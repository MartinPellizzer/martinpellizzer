Melocactus microcephalus var. olivascens

Melocactus microcephalus var. olivascens, commonly known as the olive-headed melocactus, is a slow-growing cactus native to the dry regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, thick stem covered in dense, spiny areoles and a crown of short, rounded spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for desert gardens and collections.
