Melastoma roseum

Melastoma roseum, commonly known as the rose mallow, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, rose-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along streambanks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Melastoma roseum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
