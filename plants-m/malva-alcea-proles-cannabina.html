Malva alcea proles cannabina

Malva alcea proles cannabina, commonly known as hemp nettle, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pale pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in moist, temperate climates.

Its fibrous roots and stems have been historically used for fiber production and traditional medicinal purposes.
