Melicope philippinensis

Melicope philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine melicope, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to the Philippines.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in lowland to montane rainforests. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark-barked trunk and elliptical, leathery leaves with a glossy upper surface. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces berries that are edible for some bird species.

The plant has traditional medicinal uses in local communities and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
