Malus ombrophila

Malus ombrophila, commonly known as the rain apple or cloud apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in countries like Bolivia and Peru. The plant is characterized by its small, ornamental flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, apple-like fruits. Its leaves are typically oval and have a slightly serrated edge, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

Malus ombrophila is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the unique flavor of its fruits.
