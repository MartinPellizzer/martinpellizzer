Millettia aboensis

Millettia aboensis, commonly known as the Aboensis Millettia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Sudan and South Sudan. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in savanna and woodland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential ecological importance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
