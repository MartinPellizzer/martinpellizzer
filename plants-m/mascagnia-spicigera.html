Mascagnia spicigera

Mascagnia spicigera, commonly known as the desert nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its woody stems, lance-shaped leaves, and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from white to pale purple. The plant produces small, yellow, fleshy fruits that are edible but should be consumed with caution due to their mild toxicity. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a resilient species in harsh desert environments.

Mascagnia spicigera plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various desert wildlife.
