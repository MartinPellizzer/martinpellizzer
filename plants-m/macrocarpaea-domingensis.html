Macrocarpaea domingensis

Macrocarpaea domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean island of Hispaniola.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces showy, violet to blue flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are hairy and twining.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
