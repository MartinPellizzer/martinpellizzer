Myrceugenia jonssonii

Myrceugenia jonssonii, commonly known as the Jonsson's myrtle, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the temperate rainforests of southern Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are usually glossy and dark green, with a fragrant aroma. Its small, white flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces edible, spherical berries that are a source of food for various wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
