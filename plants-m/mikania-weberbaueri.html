Mikania weberbaueri

Mikania weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's mile-a-minute weed, is a fast-growing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by fluffy, white seed heads. It is known for its aggressive growth habit and ability to spread rapidly through wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its invasive nature, it is often considered a problematic weed in tropical and subtropical regions.
