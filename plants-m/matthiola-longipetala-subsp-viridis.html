Matthiola longipetala subsp. viridis

Matthiola longipetala subsp.

viridis, commonly known as the green-flowered stock, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which is a distinctive feature of this subspecies. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
