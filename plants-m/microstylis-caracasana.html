Microstylis caracasana

Microstylis caracasana, commonly known as the Caracas dwarf sedge, is a small herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its slender, grass-like leaves are arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring.
