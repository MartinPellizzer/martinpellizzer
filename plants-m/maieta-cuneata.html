Maieta cuneata

Maieta cuneata, commonly known as the narrowleaf mayweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a serrated edge, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

Maieta cuneata is often found in disturbed habitats and is considered a common weed in agricultural areas.
