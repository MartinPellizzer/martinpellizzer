Millettia glaucescens var. siamensis

Millettia glaucescens var. siamensis, commonly known as the Siam milk tree, is a deciduous leguminous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaflets, and produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its milky sap, which is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, typically in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
