Mentzelia synandra

Mentzelia synandra, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, pale yellow to greenish flowers.

Mentzelia synandra is known for its resilience to drought and its role in supporting local pollinators.
