Macrolobium molle

Macrolobium molle, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are soft and velvety to the touch. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various conditions, it is considered an invasive species in some regions.
