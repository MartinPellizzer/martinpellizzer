Maxillaria kellyana

Maxillaria kellyana, commonly known as Kelly's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy flowers. The plant typically produces fragrant, butterfly-shaped blooms in shades of pink, white, and yellow. Its roots are epiphytic, meaning it can grow on the surfaces of trees or rocks without requiring soil.

Maxillaria kellyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
