Melaleuca papillosa

Melaleuca papillosa, commonly known as the silky tea tree, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, white or pale pink flowers that appear in clusters. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and sandy environments.

The plant is valued for its aromatic oils and has been used in traditional medicine.
