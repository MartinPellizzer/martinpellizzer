Masdevallia medellinensis

Masdevallia medellinensis, commonly known as the Medellin orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often colorful flowers. The plant typically produces intricate, spurred flowers that are adapted for pollination by specific insects. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette, and it thrives in cool, humid environments with high light exposure.

Masdevallia medellinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
