Mariottia goebeliana

Mariottia goebeliana, commonly known as the Goebel's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and undergrowth. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive coloration, often featuring a mix of white, green, and yellow hues. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the soil, while its leaves are usually broad and strap-like.

Mariottia goebeliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
