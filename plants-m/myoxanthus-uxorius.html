Myoxanthus uxorius

Myoxanthus uxorius, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and consist of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by winged fruits that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The botanical features of Myoxanthus uxorius make it adaptable to various garden environments.
