Montbretia lineata

Montbretia lineata, commonly known as the yellow oxalis, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers with a distinctive red center, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate leaves divided into five pointed lobes, giving it a delicate, fern-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Montbretia lineata is also known for its ability to naturalize and spread readily, though it can become invasive in some regions.
