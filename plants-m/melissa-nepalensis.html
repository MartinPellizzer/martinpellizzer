Melissa nepalensis

Melissa nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, small white flowers and slender, hairy stems. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their calming and digestive properties.

Melissa nepalensis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its aromatic essential oils.
