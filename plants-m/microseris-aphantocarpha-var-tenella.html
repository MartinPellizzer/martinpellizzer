Microseris aphantocarpha var. tenella

Microseris aphantocarpha var. tenella, commonly known as the slender desertweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with minimal water and nutrient availability.

Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.
