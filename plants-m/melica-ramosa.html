Melica ramosa

Melica ramosa, commonly known as the slender brome, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in moist meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often branched, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its spikelets are small and arranged in a panicle, with leathery, awnless glumes.

Melica ramosa is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various insects and small animals.
