Myrciaria cuspidata var. latifolia

Myrciaria cuspidata var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf guava, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are often glossy in appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that give way to edible, yellow-orange fruits with a sweet and acidic flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The variety latifolia is distinguished by its broader leaves compared to the typical form of Myrciaria cuspidata.
