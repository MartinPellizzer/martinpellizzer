Myrtus cotinifolia

Myrtus cotinifolia, commonly known as the common myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces tiny white flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, dark berries. Myrtus cotinifolia is known for its ornamental value and is often used in hedges or as a garden plant.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
