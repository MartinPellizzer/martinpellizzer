Misanteca multiflora

Misanteca multiflora, commonly known as the Florida flameflower, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy soils.

Misanteca multiflora is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
