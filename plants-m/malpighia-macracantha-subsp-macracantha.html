Malpighia macracantha subsp. macracantha

Malpighia macracantha subsp.

macracantha, commonly known as the red mamey sapote, is a tropical shrub native to Central America and northern South America. It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers.

The plant produces small, round fruits that turn red when mature, containing a sweet, yellowish pulp surrounded by a thick, aromatic rind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its potential in traditional medicine and as a source of natural pigments.
