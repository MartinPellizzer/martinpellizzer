Miconia bullotricha

Miconia bullotricha, commonly known as the hairy miconia, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy textures. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that splits open to release numerous seeds.

Miconia bullotricha is noted for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
