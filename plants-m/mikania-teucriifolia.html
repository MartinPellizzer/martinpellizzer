Mikania teucriifolia

Mikania teucriifolia, commonly known as creeping oxeye, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as a sprawling vine or shrub, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges. Its flowers are yellow with a central disk, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Mikania teucriifolia is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative propagation.
