Mycetia anlongensis

Mycetia anlongensis, commonly known as the Chinese tamarind tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds, which are used in traditional Chinese cuisine. Its growth habit and floral structure make it an interesting subject for botanical study.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
