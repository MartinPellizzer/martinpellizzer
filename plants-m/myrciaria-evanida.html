Myrciaria evanida

Myrciaria evanida, commonly known as the Brazilian guava or yellow myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are typically small, round, and have a tart, acidic flavor.

Myrciaria evanida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in folk medicine.
