Monodora grandidieri

Monodora grandidieri, commonly known as the Madagascar nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and dense, lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by distinctive, egg-shaped fruits that enclose a single, aromatic seed. Its unique scent and medicinal properties have made it a subject of interest in both traditional and modern pharmacology.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
