Malva alcea subsp. moschata

Malva alcea subsp.

moschata, commonly known as the musk mallow, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant produces capsules containing numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its fragrant flowers and historical use in herbal medicine highlight its botanical and cultural significance.
