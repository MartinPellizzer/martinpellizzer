Myrcia lasiantha var. pauciflora

Myrcia lasiantha var. pauciflora, commonly known as the pink myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, pinkish-white flowers and elliptical leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland rainforests.

The flowers are fragrant and attract various pollinators, including bees and birds.
