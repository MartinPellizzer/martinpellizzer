Maxillaria amazonica

Maxillaria amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian Maxillaria, is a terrestrial orchid native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its evergreen leaves. The plant produces strikingly fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a distinctive lip. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, moist soil of its natural habitat.

Maxillaria amazonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
