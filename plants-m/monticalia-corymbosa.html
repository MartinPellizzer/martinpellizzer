Monticalia corymbosa

Monticalia corymbosa, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom in dense corymbs, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Monticalia corymbosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
