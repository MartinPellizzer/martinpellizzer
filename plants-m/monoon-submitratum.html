Monoon submitratum

Monoon submitratum, commonly known as the white monoon or white sand orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories or on tree trunks. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, aiding in nutrient absorption.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
