Myrcia neoblanchetiana

Myrcia neoblanchetiana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and elliptical, glossy leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic properties.

The fruit is a small, dark red drupe that contains a single seed.
