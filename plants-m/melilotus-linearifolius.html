Melilotus linearifolius

Melilotus linearifolius, commonly known as slender melilot, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early summer.

The seeds are enclosed in slender, hairy pods and are often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
