Mentha pamiroalaica

Mentha pamiroalaica, commonly known as the Pamir mint, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and emit a strong menthol scent, which is used in traditional medicine.

Mentha pamiroalaica is valued for its potential medicinal properties, including its ability to soothe digestive issues and reduce inflammation.
