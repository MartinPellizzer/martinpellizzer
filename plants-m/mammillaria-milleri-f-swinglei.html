Mammillaria milleri f. swinglei

Mammillaria milleri f. swinglei, commonly known as the Swingle's mammillaria, is a compact cactus with a globular shape and a dense covering of white to pale blue spines.

It originates from the rocky and arid regions of Mexico, where it thrives in full sun and well-draining soil. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts for indoor and outdoor cultivation.

It is relatively easy to care for, requiring minimal water and protection from frost.
