Myrtus nitens

Myrtus nitens, commonly known as the silver myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. These flowers are followed by small, dark blue berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful.

Myrtus nitens is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
