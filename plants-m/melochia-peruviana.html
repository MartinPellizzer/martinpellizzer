Melochia peruviana

Melochia peruviana, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Peruvian melochia, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

Melochia peruviana is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
