Minaria ditassoides

Minaria ditassoides, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in desert or semi-desert environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are small, linear, and often covered in fine hairs to reduce water loss.

Minaria ditassoides is adapted to extreme heat and drought, making it a resilient species in harsh desert conditions.
