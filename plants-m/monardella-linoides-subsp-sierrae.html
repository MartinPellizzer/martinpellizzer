Monardella linoides subsp. sierrae

Monardella linoides subsp.

sierrae, commonly known as Sierra mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas of montane forests and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in dense, white to pale blue inflorescences.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
