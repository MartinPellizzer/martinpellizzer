Mentha aquatica subsp. bakeri

Mentha aquatica subsp.

bakeri, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and stream banks. It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in whorled clusters.

The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in temperate regions. Its leaves are widely used in herbal teas and culinary applications due to their refreshing menthol scent.

This subspecies is closely related to other mint species and is valued for its ecological and medicinal properties.
