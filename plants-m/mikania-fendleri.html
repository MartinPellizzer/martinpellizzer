Mikania fendleri

Mikania fendleri, commonly known as Mexican devil's ivy, is a vigorous climbing vine native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is often found in disturbed areas, such as along roadsides and in open woodlands.

Due to its invasive nature, it can outcompete native vegetation in certain regions.
