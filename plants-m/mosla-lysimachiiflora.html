Mosla lysimachiiflora

Mosla lysimachiiflora, commonly known as purple mosquito plant, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Mosla lysimachiiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
