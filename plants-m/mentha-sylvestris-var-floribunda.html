Mentha sylvestris var. floribunda

Mentha sylvestris var. floribunda, commonly known as the woodland mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its use in traditional herbal medicine and culinary applications.
