Mesembryanthemum albatum

Mesembryanthemum albatum, commonly known as the silver leaf mesem, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, characterized by its fleshy leaves and adaptations to arid environments. The plant features a rosette growth pattern with silver-gray foliage that reflects sunlight, reducing water loss. It produces vibrant yellow flowers during the winter months, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
