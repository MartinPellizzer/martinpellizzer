Mesembryanthemum tomentosum

Mesembryanthemum tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly bush, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fuzzy foliage that gives it a soft, woolly appearance. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid climates.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
