Meistera muricarpa

Meistera muricarpa, commonly known as the spiky orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, rough-textured leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or other surfaces in its natural habitat. Its flowers, which are usually yellow or orange, bloom in clusters and can last for several weeks.

Meistera muricarpa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique foliage structure.
