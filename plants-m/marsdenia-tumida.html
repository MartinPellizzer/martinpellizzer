Marsdenia tumida

Marsdenia tumida, commonly known as the swollen milkweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by follicles containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Marsdenia tumida is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
