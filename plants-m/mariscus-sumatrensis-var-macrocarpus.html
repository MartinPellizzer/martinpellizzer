Mariscus sumatrensis var. macrocarpus

Mariscus sumatrensis var. macrocarpus, commonly known as the large-fruited mariscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and palmate leaves, which are typically lobed and have a glossy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by edible berries that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its unique botanical features, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
