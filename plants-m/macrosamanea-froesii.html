Macrosamanea froesii

Macrosamanea froesii, commonly known as the Brazilian palm, is a species of palm native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
