Morinda laxa

Morinda laxa, commonly known as the red sandalwood or Chinese red sandalwood, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and distinctive red or pinkish bark. The plant belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy appearance.

Morinda laxa is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
