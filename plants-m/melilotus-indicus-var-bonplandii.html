Melilotus indicus var. bonplandii

Melilotus indicus var. bonplandii, commonly known as yellow melilot, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Its medicinal properties have been explored for their potential in traditional herbal remedies.
