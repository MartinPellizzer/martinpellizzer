Myrtus bahamensis

Myrtus bahamensis, commonly known as the Bahamian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant features small, dark green leaves with a fragrant, resinous scent when crushed. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, dark berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

This species is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
