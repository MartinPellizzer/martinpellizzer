Myrtus soyauxii

Myrtus soyauxii, commonly known as the Soyaux myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to dark purple as they mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its use in traditional herbal remedies.
