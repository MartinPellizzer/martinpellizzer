Malus capucini

Malus capucini, commonly known as the Capucini apple, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It produces small, round fruits with a yellowish-green skin and a sweet, tart flavor. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are oval in shape and have a smooth texture. It flowers in the spring with white to pink blossoms, which attract pollinators like bees.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.
