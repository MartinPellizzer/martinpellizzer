Monardella douglasii subsp. venosa

Monardella douglasii subsp.

venosa, commonly known as Douglas's monardella, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink to lavender, typically arranged in dense terminal clusters. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as meadows and open woodlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable component of native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
