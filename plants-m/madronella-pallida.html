Madronella pallida

Madronella pallida, commonly known as the pale madronella, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Madronella pallida is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
