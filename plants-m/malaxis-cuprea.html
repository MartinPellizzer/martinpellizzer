Malaxis cuprea

Malaxis cuprea, commonly known as the copper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and thickets. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Malaxis cuprea is often found in association with other orchids and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
