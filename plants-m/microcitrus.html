Microcitrus

Microcitrus, commonly known as finger lime, is a genus of small, citrus-like trees native to Australia.

These plants are characterized by their compact size and distinctive, finger-like segments in their fruit. The leaves are usually small, elliptical, and have a slightly waxy texture. Microcitrus species produce fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or orange when mature.

They are often cultivated for their ornamental value and unique fruit, which is used in culinary applications.
