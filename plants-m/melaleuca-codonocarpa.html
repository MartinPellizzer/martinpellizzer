Melaleuca codonocarpa

Melaleuca codonocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf paperbark, is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, flaky bark that peels off in papery strips, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, often reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a leathery texture with a prominent midrib.

The flowers are small and white or pink, arranged in dense clusters, and are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.
