Muehlenbeckia chilensis var. racemosa

Muehlenbeckia chilensis var. racemosa, commonly known as the Chilean muhly, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in coastal and arid regions. The plant features slender, creeping stems and small, grass-like leaves. It produces clusters of tiny, pale green to white flowers, which are often borne in racemes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
