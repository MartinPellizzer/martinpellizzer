Morysia microcephala

Morysia microcephala, commonly known as the dwarf morysia, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil. Due to its limited natural habitat and susceptibility to environmental changes, it is considered endangered.

Conservation efforts are ongoing to protect and propagate this unique species.
