Mentha arvensis var. pavoniana

Mentha arvensis var. pavoniana, commonly known as the pink mint, is a variant of the field mint species.

It is characterized by its distinctive pinkish-purple flower spikes, which contrast with its dark green, toothed leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate regions and thrives in partial shade. It has a strong, aromatic scent and is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscape design.
