Magnolia purpurea var. discolor

Magnolia purpurea var. discolor, commonly known as the variegated magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range from white to pink and feature a distinctive purple blotch on the petals. The leaves are broadly ovate with a smooth texture and a dark green upper surface. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring bloom.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of climates.
