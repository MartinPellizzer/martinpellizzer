Mentha × crispula

Mentha × crispula, commonly known as catnip, is a hybrid plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its square, hollow stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems. Mentha × crispula is native to Europe and Asia but has been naturalized in many parts of the world.

Its aromatic properties make it a popular herb used in teas and traditional medicine.
