Mammillaria floccigera

Mammillaria floccigera, commonly known as the woolly mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, white, woolly covering that covers the stems and flowers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid environments. It produces yellow flowers during the spring or early summer.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
