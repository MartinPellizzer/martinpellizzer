Medicago suffruticosa subsp. maroccana

Medicago suffruticosa subsp.

maroccana, commonly known as Moroccan goat's rue, is a perennial legume native to North Africa. It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with woody stems and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that are followed by cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its adaptability and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
