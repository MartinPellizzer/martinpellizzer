Micrampelis wrightii

Micrampelis wrightii, commonly known as the Wright's climbing fig, is a woody vine native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries, and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. Micrampelis wrightii is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests.
