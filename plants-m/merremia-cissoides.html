Merremia cissoides

Merremia cissoides, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a perennial vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail along the ground. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a common ornamental plant in warm climates.

Its seeds are dispersed by animals and can persist in the soil for several years.
