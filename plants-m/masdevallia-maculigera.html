Masdevallia maculigera

Masdevallia maculigera, commonly known as the spotted masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often marked with distinctive spots or streaks. The flowers are typically small and clustered, with a unique coloration that ranges from greenish-white to pinkish-purple. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires high light exposure but is sensitive to direct sunlight.

This orchid is prized by horticulturists for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation under proper conditions.
