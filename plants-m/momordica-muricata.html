Momordica muricata

Momordica muricata, commonly known as the spiny cucumber, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces pale yellow flowers that give way to elongated, spiny fruits resembling small cucumbers. The fruit contains numerous seeds and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Botanically, it belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like pumpkins and gourds.
