Mammea siamensis

Mammea siamensis, commonly known as the Siam mammea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy berries that are edible. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is also cultivated in botanical gardens and for traditional use.
