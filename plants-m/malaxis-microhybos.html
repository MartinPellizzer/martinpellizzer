Malaxis microhybos

Malaxis microhybos, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its epiphytic or terrestrial growth habit.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often found in humid, tropical climates.
