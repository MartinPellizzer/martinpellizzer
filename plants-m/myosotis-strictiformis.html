Myosotis strictiformis

Myosotis strictiformis, commonly known as the slender bluebell, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in damp, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream sides. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Myosotis strictiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
