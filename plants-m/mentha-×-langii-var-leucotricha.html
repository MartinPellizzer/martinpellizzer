Mentha × langii var. leucotricha

Mentha × langii var. leucotricha, commonly known as white-haired mint, is a hybrid plant species in the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in herbal teas and culinary applications.
