Minuartia campestris var. squarrosa

Minuartia campestris var. squarrosa, commonly known as the hairy rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and rocky environments.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically covered in fine, woolly hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry meadows, gravelly slopes, and disturbed areas.

This variety is notable for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
