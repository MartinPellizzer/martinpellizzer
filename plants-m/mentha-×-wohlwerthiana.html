Mentha × wohlwerthiana

Mentha × wohlwerthiana, commonly known as the hybrid mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a cross between Mentha longifolia (spearmint) and Mentha spicata (peppermint), resulting in a plant with a strong, aromatic scent. The leaves are typically oval-shaped, serrated, and have a dense, velvety texture. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

It is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its high essential oil content.
