Miconia coniophora

Miconia coniophora, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a distinctive, spiny capsule that contains several seeds.

Miconia coniophora is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain regions.
