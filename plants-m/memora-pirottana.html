Memora pirottana

Memora pirottana, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of yellow-orange flowers that resemble small bells. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are opposite on the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Memora pirottana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be used in vertical gardens or as a climbing plant.
