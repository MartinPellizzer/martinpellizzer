Myrtus communis subsp. baetica

Myrtus communis subsp.

baetica, commonly known as the Baetica myrtle, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its aromatic evergreen leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and Mediterranean regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
