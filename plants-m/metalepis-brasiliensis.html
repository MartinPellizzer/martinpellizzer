Metalepis brasiliensis

Metalepis brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian burr palm, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its clustered growth habit and distinctive, spiny fruits. The plant produces large, palm-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its fruits, which contain seeds, are adapted for dispersal by animals.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
