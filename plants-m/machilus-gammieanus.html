Machilus gammieanus

Machilus gammieanus, commonly known as the Gammie's laurel, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. Its small, white flowers are clustered in inflorescences, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

The tree is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
