Mouriri myrtifolia

Mouriri myrtifolia, commonly known as the golden mouriri, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.

Mouriri myrtifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
