Meringium

Meringium, commonly known as the "foam flower," is a member of the Adoxaceae family, which includes plants like the honeysuckle.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several seeds.

Meringium is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
