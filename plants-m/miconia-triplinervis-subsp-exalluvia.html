Miconia triplinervis subsp. exalluvia

Miconia triplinervis subsp.

exalluvia, commonly known as the river miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Amazon Basin. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a prominent central vein and three secondary veins, giving it its species name "triplinervis.".

The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by distinctive, red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near rivers and streams.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
