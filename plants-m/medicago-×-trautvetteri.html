Medicago × trautvetteri

Medicago × trautvetteri, commonly known as the hybrid sainfoin, is a perennial legume that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species, Medicago sativa and Medicago polyantha.

It is native to the Middle East and has been introduced to various regions for its agricultural and ornamental value. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. It is well adapted to dry and semi-arid environments, making it a useful forage crop.

Its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia makes it beneficial for soil fertility.
