Myristica hypargyraea

Myristica hypargyraea, commonly known as the golden myristica, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody fruit that contains a richly scented resin. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy berries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating.

The tree is valued for its essential oils and has been used in traditional medicine and perfumery.
