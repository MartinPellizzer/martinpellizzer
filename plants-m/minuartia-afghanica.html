Minuartia afghanica

Minuartia afghanica, commonly known as the Afghan rock-cress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically features a mat-forming habit with prostrate stems and small, orbicular leaves. It is adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting succulent leaves that help store water.

Minuartia afghanica is often found on rocky soils and in dry, open habitats.
