Marsdenia balansae

Marsdenia balansae, commonly known as the Balansae Marsdenia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family, which includes milkweeds, and is characterized by its climbing habit and milky sap. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are often followed by distinctive, woody fruits. Its leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
