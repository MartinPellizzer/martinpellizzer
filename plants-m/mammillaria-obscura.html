Mammillaria obscura

Mammillaria obscura, commonly known as the ghost cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines that give it a ghostly appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. It produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth rate, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
