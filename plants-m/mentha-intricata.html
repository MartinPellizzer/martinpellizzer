Mentha intricata

Mentha intricata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hound's tongue, is a perennial herb native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is often found in stream banks and woodland areas. Mentha intricata has a strong, mint-like aroma and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
