Manulea tomentosa

Manulea tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly manulea, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in rocky or arid environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
