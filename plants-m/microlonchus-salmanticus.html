Microlonchus salmanticus

Microlonchus salmanticus, commonly known as the Salmanticus dwarf grass, is a small perennial grass species native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean regions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring. It is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
