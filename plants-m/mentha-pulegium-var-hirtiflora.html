Mentha pulegium var. hirtiflora

Mentha pulegium var. hirtiflora, commonly known as hairy pulegium, is a variant of the pulegium mint species.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and slender, creeping stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
