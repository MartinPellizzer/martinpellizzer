Malpighia fucata subsp. longifolia

Malpighia fucata subsp.

longifolia, commonly known as the purple mangosteen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its evergreen shrub form, with compound leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers.

The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart, sweet flavor. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits.
