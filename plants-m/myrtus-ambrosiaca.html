Myrtus ambrosiaca

Myrtus ambrosiaca, commonly known as sweet myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, dark green leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, edible berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Myrtus ambrosiaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal remedies.
