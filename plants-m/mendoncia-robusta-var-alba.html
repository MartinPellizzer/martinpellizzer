Mendoncia robusta var. alba

Mendoncia robusta var. alba, commonly known as the white mendoncia, is a striking plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, white flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
