Mallotus muricatus

Mallotus muricatus, commonly known as the rough-leaved mallot, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its rough, textured leaves that are often covered in small, raised dots. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-yellow flowers, which are followed by woody, spiny fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its unique foliage and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
