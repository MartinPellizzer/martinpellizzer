Marsdenia fraseri

Marsdenia fraseri, commonly known as the Fraser's Marsdenia, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by follicles containing elongated seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
