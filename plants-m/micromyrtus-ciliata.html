Micromyrtus ciliata

Micromyrtus ciliata, commonly known as the coastal bottlebrush, is a small shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower spikes that are typically red or pink in color. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong coastal winds.

Micromyrtus ciliata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.
