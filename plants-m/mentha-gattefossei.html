Mentha gattefossei

Mentha gattefossei, commonly known as the Corsican mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a fragrant, menthol-like aroma. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Mentha gattefossei is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
