Morus alba var. italica

Morus alba var. italica, commonly known as the Italian white mulberry, is a variety of the white mulberry tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically pale green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as mulberries, which are edible and often used in jams and wines. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as for its fruit.

This variety is also valued for its role in the silkworm breeding process.
