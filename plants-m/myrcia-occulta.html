Myrcia occulta

Myrcia occulta, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy evergreen leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces dark purple berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Myrcia occulta is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
