Mesosphaerum lacustre

Mesosphaerum lacustre, commonly known as the lake sphaerium, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its floating, bladder-like structures.

These structures, known as pneumatophores, aid in buoyancy and gas exchange. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, particularly in lakes and ponds. It has a simple morphology with no true roots, stems, or leaves.

Mesosphaerum lacustre plays a role in maintaining water quality by absorbing nutrients and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
