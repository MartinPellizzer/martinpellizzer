Melica subulata var. pammelii

Melica subulata var. pammelii, commonly known as Pammel's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the United States, particularly found in the Great Plains.

It is characterized by its slender, spikelike inflorescence and narrow, pointed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies, rocky slopes, and disturbed soils. Its botanical features include spikelets that are closely arranged along the stem and small, two-lobed glumes.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
