Myrcia oblongata

Myrcia oblongata, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Myrcia oblongata is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
