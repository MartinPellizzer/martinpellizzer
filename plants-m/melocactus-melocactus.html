Melocactus melocactus

Melocactus melocactus, commonly known as the "devil's tongue," is a cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, ribbed stem and spines that give it a unique, almost prehensile appearance. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Melocactus melocactus is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its striking appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
