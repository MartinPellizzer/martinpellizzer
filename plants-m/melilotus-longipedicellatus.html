Melilotus longipedicellatus

Melilotus longipedicellatus, commonly known as the long pedicel melilot, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces slender, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional herbal medicine. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal research.
