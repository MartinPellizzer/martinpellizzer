Mixandra

Mixandra, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in a variety of colors, including purple, pink, and white. Its foliage consists of velvety, dark green leaves that are often marked with silver or white veins.

Mixandra thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
