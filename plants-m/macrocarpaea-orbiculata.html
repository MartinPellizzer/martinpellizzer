Macrocarpaea orbiculata

Macrocarpaea orbiculata, commonly known as the globe sedge, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. It has a compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The species is also noted for its ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions, making it a versatile choice for wetland restoration projects.
