Malva divaricata

Malva divaricata, commonly known as the variable mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces showy, pale pink to lilac-colored flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like hibiscus and cotton.

Malva divaricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
