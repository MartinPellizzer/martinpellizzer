Miconia guayaquilensis

Miconia guayaquilensis, commonly known as the Guayaquil miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter, often with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to yellowish flowers, which are typically followed by orange to red berries. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Miconia guayaquilensis is also noted for its allelopathic properties, which can inhibit the growth of other plants in its vicinity.
