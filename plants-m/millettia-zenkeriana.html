Millettia zenkeriana

Millettia zenkeriana, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its pinnate leaves and yellowish flowers. The tree produces long, cylindrical pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland and mountainous regions.

Due to its ecological and cultural significance, it is also valued for its hard, durable wood.
