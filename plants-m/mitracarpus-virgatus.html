Mitracarpus virgatus

Mitracarpus virgatus, commonly known as the Virgin's shield, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a distinctive, spiny pod that splits open to release the seeds.

Mitracarpus virgatus is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.
