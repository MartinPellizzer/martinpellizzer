Mentha aquatica plicata

Mentha aquatica plicata, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly serrated. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near streams, ponds, and marshes.

Mentha aquatica plicata is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
