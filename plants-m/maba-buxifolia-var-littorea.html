Maba buxifolia var. littorea

Maba buxifolia var. littorea, commonly known as the coastal maba, is a variant of the maba tree native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant thrives in saline and humid environments, making it well adapted to coastal and mangrove ecosystems. It often forms thickets and plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts in tropical coastal zones.
