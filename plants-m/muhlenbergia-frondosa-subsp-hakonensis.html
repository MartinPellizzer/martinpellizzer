Muhlenbergia frondosa subsp. hakonensis

Muhlenbergia frondosa subsp.

hakonensis, commonly known as the Hakone lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to Japan. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, arching blades that often form a dense, low-growing mat.

The plant produces feathery, silver-green inflorescences that are highly ornamental, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sun, exhibiting drought tolerance once established.

This subspecies is valued for its fine texture and ability to form attractive clumps in garden landscapes.
