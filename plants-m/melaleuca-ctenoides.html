Melaleuca ctenoides

Melaleuca ctenoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paperbark, is a small to medium-sized tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, flaky bark that peels in thin layers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically visited by nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

Melaleuca ctenoides is adapted to coastal and sandy soils, and it plays an important role in stabilizing dune ecosystems.
