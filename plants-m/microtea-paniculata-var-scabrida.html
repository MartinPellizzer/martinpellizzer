Microtea paniculata var. scabrida

Microtea paniculata var. scabrida, commonly known as the rough panicled microtea, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that are covered in a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
