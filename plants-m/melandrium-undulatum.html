Melandrium undulatum

Melandrium undulatum, commonly known as the wavy sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense clusters at the ends of its stems. Its leaves are narrow and often have a wavy or undulating edge, contributing to its common name.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or dry habitats.
