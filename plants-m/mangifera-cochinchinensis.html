Mangifera cochinchinensis

Mangifera cochinchinensis, commonly known as the mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces a distinctive fruit with a deep purple rind and a segmented, white, juicy pulp. The fruit is rich in antioxidants and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its cultivation is primarily for its edible fruit, which is valued for its unique flavor and nutritional content.
