Mentha × nemorosa var. velutina

Mentha × nemorosa var. velutina, commonly known as velvetleaf mint, is a hybrid perennial plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that give it its common name, and it typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue to purple in color. It is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full shade.

Velvetleaf mint is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
