Machaerium androvillosum

Machaerium androvillosum, commonly known as the Androvillosum machaerium, is a tropical shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is fibrous and well-developed, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Machaerium androvillosum is often found in moist, shaded areas of rainforests.
