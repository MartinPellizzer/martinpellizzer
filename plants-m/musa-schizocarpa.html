Musa schizocarpa

Musa schizocarpa, commonly known as the wild banana, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant that grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces large, elongated leaves that can reach up to several meters in length. Its flowers are clustered in a pseudostem, and it bears fruit that is typically smaller and less edible than commercial bananas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
