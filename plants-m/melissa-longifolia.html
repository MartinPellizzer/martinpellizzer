Melissa longifolia

Melissa longifolia, commonly known as lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, small, oval leaves and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, often growing in moist environments. Its leaves are widely used in herbal teas and aromatherapy for their calming properties.

Melissa longifolia is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential anti-anxiety and antispasmodic effects.
