Monotropa hypoxya

Monotropa hypoxya, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a hemiparasitic herbaceous plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on other plants for nutrients.

It is native to eastern North America and thrives in shaded, moist forest environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in late summer, and its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi. It is also known for its ability to climb and twine around tree trunks and shrubs.

Due to its unique ecological role and ethereal appearance, it is often considered a symbol of the mysterious and unseen in forest ecosystems.
