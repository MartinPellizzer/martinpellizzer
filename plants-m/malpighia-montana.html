Malpighia montana

Malpighia montana, commonly known as the mountain beauty or wild acerola, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its small, bright red berries that resemble cherries. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded or semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are usually pink or white.

The fruit is rich in vitamin C and is used in traditional medicine and as a food source.
