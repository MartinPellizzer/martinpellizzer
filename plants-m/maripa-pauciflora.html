Maripa pauciflora

Maripa pauciflora, commonly known as the low-flowered maripa, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, adapted to harsh environmental conditions. The plant features small, clustered white flowers that bloom during the dry season. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, helping it conserve water in its native habitat.

Maripa pauciflora is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its resilience in challenging climates.
