Melilotus arenarius

Melilotus arenarius, commonly known as yellow melilot, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces small, yellow pea-like flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant has slender, branching stems and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

The plant is also known for its sweet, honey-like fragrance, which attracts pollinators such as bees.
