Missiessya villosa

Missiessya villosa, commonly known as the hairy mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in the spring. Its berries are typically red or yellow and are often consumed by birds, which aid in seed dispersal.

Despite its parasitic nature, Missiessya villosa plays a role in supporting local wildlife and ecological diversity.
