Menestoria hameliae

Menestoria hameliae, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its long, tubular, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, showy blooms.

Its rapid growth and climbing habit make it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in suitable climates.
