Miconia bigibbosa

Miconia bigibbosa, commonly known as the red miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often bright green with a smooth texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains numerous seeds.

Miconia bigibbosa is known for its rapid growth and is considered an invasive species in some regions where it outcompetes native vegetation.
