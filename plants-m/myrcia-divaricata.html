Myrcia divaricata

Myrcia divaricata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Myrcia divaricata has aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
