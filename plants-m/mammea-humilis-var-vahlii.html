Mammea humilis var. vahlii

Mammea humilis var. vahlii, commonly known as the dwarf mammea, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange or red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and for traditional uses.
