Melanthium hyacinthoides

Melanthium hyacinthoides, commonly known as the hyacinth bean or blue false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender, erect stems. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture.

The plant is often found in moist woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
