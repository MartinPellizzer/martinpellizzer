Maesa hirsuta

Maesa hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy maesa, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are usually elliptical with a waxy texture, and it produces berries that are edible for some bird species.

Maesa hirsuta is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
