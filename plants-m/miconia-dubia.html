Miconia dubia

Miconia dubia, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, three-lobed fruits. Miconia dubia is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a common invasive species in some regions.

Its ecological impact is significant due to its competitive nature and ability to alter local ecosystems.
