Marrubium humile

Marrubium humile, commonly known as lesser horehound, is a small, flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in dense clusters, which attract various pollinators. Its leaves are gray-green and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Marrubium humile is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.
