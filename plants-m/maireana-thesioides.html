Maireana thesioides

Maireana thesioides, commonly known as the Desert Flax, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its linear, grass-like leaves and small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, featuring a deep root system to access water. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland desert regions.

Maireana thesioides plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
