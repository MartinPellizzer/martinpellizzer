Maba lamiana

Maba lamiana, commonly known as the white maba tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red fruits that contain toxic seeds. It is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its timber and medicinal properties.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
