Mentzelia eremophila

Mentzelia eremophila, commonly known as the desert blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. It has adapted to withstand drought conditions through its deep root system and reduced leaf surface area.

Mentzelia eremophila is an important component of desert ecosystems, providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to local biodiversity.
