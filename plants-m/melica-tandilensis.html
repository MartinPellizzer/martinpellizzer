Melica tandilensis

Melica tandilensis, commonly known as the Tandil grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Melica tandilensis is often found in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural applications.
