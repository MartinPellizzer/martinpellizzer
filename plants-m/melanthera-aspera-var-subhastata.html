Melanthera aspera var. subhastata

Melanthera aspera var. subhastata, commonly known as the rough-haired ironweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features upright stems that are covered in stiff, bristly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are lance-shaped and also bear a rough texture, contributing to its common name.

The flower heads are usually purple to pink in color, appearing in late summer to early fall.
