Mentha longifolia var. microphylla

Mentha longifolia var. microphylla, commonly known as narrow-leaf peppermint, is a variant of the peppermint plant characterized by its slender, pointed leaves.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white, arranged in dense spikes. It is valued for its aromatic leaves, which contain high concentrations of menthol and other essential oils.

This variety is often cultivated for both ornamental purposes and as a source of medicinal compounds.
