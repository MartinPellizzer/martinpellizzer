Malveopsis anomala

Malveopsis anomala, commonly known as the African marshmallow, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in wet or marshy habitats, often found near water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticultural interest.
