Mertensia trifurcans

Mertensia trifurcans, commonly known as the bluebells or wild bluebells, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.
