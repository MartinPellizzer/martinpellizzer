Mespilus cotoneaster subsp. rubra

Mespilus cotoneaster subsp.

rubra, commonly known as the red cotoneaster, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically oval to elliptical in shape, with a glossy upper surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky slopes.

The flowers are small and white, appearing in clusters, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
