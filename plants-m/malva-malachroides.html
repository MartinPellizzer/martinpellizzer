Malva malachroides

Malva malachroides, commonly known as the purple mallow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its large, showy purple or pink flowers. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces fluffy, seed-filled capsules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Malva malachroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
