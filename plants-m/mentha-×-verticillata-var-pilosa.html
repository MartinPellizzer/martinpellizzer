Mentha × verticillata var. pilosa

Mentha × verticillata var. pilosa, commonly known as woolly water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and its ability to thrive in moist, shady environments. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found near water sources such as streams, ponds, and wetlands.

Its aromatic leaves and stems make it a popular choice for herbal teas and traditional medicine.
