Mephitidia chrysoneura

Mephitidia chrysoneura, commonly known as the golden-threaded orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and green floral structures, which are adorned with intricate patterns resembling threads. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its roots are specialized for absorbing nutrients from the rich, moist soil of its habitat.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
