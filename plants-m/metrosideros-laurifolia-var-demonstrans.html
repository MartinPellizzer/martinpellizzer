Metrosideros laurifolia var. demonstrans

Metrosideros laurifolia var. demonstrans, commonly known as the variable pohutukawa, is a variant of the pohutukawa tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a waxy surface. The plant produces showy, red or pink flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting a variety of pollinators. It thrives in coastal environments and is adapted to salt spray and strong winds.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in coastal ecosystems.
