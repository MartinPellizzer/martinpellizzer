Matthiola capiomontiana

Matthiola capiomontiana, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada stock, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of California and Nevada.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open meadows and rocky areas.

Matthiola capiomontiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
