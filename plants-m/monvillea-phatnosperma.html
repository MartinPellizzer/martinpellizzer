Monvillea phatnosperma

Monvillea phatnosperma, commonly known as the golden bell orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its unique floral structure and vibrant hues make it a popular choice for both horticultural collections and botanical gardens.

The species is also noted for its relatively compact size, making it suitable for smaller garden spaces.
