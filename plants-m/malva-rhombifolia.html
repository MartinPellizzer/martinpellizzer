Malva rhombifolia

Malva rhombifolia, commonly known as the rhombic mallows, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its rhombic-shaped leaves, which are typically hairy and have a distinctive lobed appearance. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Malva rhombifolia is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
