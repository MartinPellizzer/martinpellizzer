Melothria celebica var. villosior

Melothria celebica var. villosior, commonly known as the hairy bitterball, is a tropical climbing shrub native to the Indonesian archipelago.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, and often hairy fruits that resemble small, bitter melons. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has compound leaves with serrated edges. Its fruits are typically harvested when they turn a deep orange-red color, indicating maturity.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
