Melica rectiflora var. pilosa

Melica rectiflora var. pilosa, commonly known as hairy false brome, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy stems, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, meadows, and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a bluish-green hue.

The inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple or brown.
