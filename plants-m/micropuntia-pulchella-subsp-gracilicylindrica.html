Micropuntia pulchella subsp. gracilicylindrica

Micropuntia pulchella subsp.

gracilicylindrica, commonly known as the slender column cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are typically green to bluish-green in color.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

This subspecies is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
