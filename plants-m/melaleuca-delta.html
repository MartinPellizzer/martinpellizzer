Melaleuca delta

Melaleuca delta, commonly known as the delta melaleuca, is a species of flowering plant native to northern Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal and wetland areas. The plant produces small, bottle-shaped fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are narrow and often have a distinctive aromatic scent.

Melaleuca delta is known for its ability to thrive in saline and waterlogged soils, making it an important species in coastal ecosystems.
