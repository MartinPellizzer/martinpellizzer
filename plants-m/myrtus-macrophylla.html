Myrtus macrophylla

Myrtus macrophylla, commonly known as the Australian myrtle, is a dense, evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features small, aromatic, dark green leaves that are often used in traditional Aboriginal medicine. The plant produces small white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark purple berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Myrtus macrophylla is also valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and aromatherapy.
