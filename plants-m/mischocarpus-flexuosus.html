Mischocarpus flexuosus

Mischocarpus flexuosus, commonly known as the flexible mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees in the tropical regions of South America.

It has small, elliptical leaves that are usually arranged in pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red berries that contain sticky seeds. Its parasitic nature allows it to draw water and nutrients from its host, while also contributing to the ecosystem by providing food for birds and other animals.

The flexible mistletoe is often found in humid, lowland forests where it thrives in the canopy layer.
