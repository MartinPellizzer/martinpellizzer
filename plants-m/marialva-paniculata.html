Marialva paniculata

Marialva paniculata, commonly known as the Brazilian panic grass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and produces panicles of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural applications.
