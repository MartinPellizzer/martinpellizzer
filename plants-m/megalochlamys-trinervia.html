Megalochlamys trinervia

Megalochlamys trinervia, commonly known as the narrow-veined orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are linear and have three prominent veins, giving it the species name "trinervia.".

The orchid produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting various pollinators.
