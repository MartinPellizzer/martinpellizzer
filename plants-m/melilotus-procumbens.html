Melilotus procumbens

Melilotus procumbens, commonly known as yellow sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas.

Melilotus procumbens is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable in agricultural settings.
