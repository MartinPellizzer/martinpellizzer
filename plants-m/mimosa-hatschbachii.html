Mimosa hatschbachii

Mimosa hatschbachii, commonly known as the yellow mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with bacteria. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The plant's rapid growth and seasonal blooming make it a distinctive addition to botanical collections.
