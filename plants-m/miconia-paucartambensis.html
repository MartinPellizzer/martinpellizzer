Miconia paucartambensis

Miconia paucartambensis, commonly known as the Peruvian miconia, is a tropical shrub native to the Amazon basin.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
