Medinilla markgrafii

Medinilla markgrafii, commonly known as the velvet tangerine.

It is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines, characterized by its showy, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features glossy, dark green elliptical leaves and produces small, orange-red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
