Mappa gigantea

Mappa gigantea, commonly known as the giant map plant, is a striking member of the Araliaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in diameter, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. Native to tropical regions, it thrives in warm, humid environments with well-draining soil.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
