Meriania fuscescens

Meriania fuscescens, commonly known as the red-veined meriania, is a shrub native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with deep red veins. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of dense forests.
