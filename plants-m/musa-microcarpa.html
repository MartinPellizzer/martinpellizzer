Musa microcarpa

Musa microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf banana plant, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Musaceae family and is characterized by its short stature compared to other banana species. The plant produces small, clustered banana-like fruits that are typically yellow when ripe. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

Musa microcarpa is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and compact size.
