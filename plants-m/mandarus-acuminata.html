Mandarus acuminata

Mandarus acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Proteaceae and is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and dense, rounded clusters of flowers. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by woody, cone-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Mandarus acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
