Myrica caracasana

Myrica caracasana, commonly known as the Caracas wax myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It belongs to the Myricaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish-green flowers that are followed by dark, leathery berries. Myrica caracasana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and forest margins.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
