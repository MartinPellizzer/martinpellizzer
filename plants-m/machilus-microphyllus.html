Machilus microphyllus

Machilus microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaved woodbine, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by dark purple or black berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Machilus microphyllus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
