Muscari grossheimii

Muscari grossheimii, commonly known as the Persian grape hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It produces clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that resemble small grapes, hence its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is well adapted to rocky or sandy soils. It blooms in early spring and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Muscari grossheimii is relatively easy to grow and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
