Morinda persicifolia

Morinda persicifolia, commonly known as the Chinese mallow or red mallow, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, often reddish bark and alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, followed by ellipsoid capsules containing numerous seeds.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the plant is also cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
