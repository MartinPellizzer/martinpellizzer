Madaractis variegata

Madaractis variegata, commonly known as the variegated madaractis, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, multicolored leaves that display shades of green, yellow, and white, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its unique foliage, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
