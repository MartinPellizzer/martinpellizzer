Myroxylon ilicifolium

Myroxylon ilicifolium, commonly known as the copal tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces a valuable resin known as copal, which has been used for centuries in incense, varnishes, and adhesives. Its bark and wood are also utilized in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
