Myristica tristis subsp. sessilifructa

Myristica tristis subsp.

sessilifructa, commonly known as the black pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the family Myristicaceae and is characterized by its dark, aromatic seeds used as a spice.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by black, peppery fruits that contain the edible seeds. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a glossy surface.

The subspecies sessilifructa is distinguished by its sessile fruits, which are shorter and more compact compared to the typical form.
