Medeola

Medeola, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a small, perennial herb native to the northeastern United States and eastern Canada.

It belongs to the Melanthiaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and small, white or pale green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has a short growth cycle, flowering in early spring before the canopy of trees fully develops. Medeola has a tuberous root system and is often mistaken for other similar-looking plants due to its subtle appearance.

It is also known for its historical use in traditional medicine by Indigenous peoples.
