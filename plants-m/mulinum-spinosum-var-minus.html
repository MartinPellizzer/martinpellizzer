Mulinum spinosum var. minus

Mulinum spinosum var. minus, commonly known as the lesser spiny honeysuckle, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a small, red berry that ripens in the fall, attracting various bird species.

This variety is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often growing in alpine or subalpine environments.
