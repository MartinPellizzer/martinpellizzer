Miconia saulensis

Miconia saulensis, commonly known as the Brazilian miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range from white to pink, often accompanied by a distinctive red calyx. Miconia saulensis is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, which can impact local ecosystems.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
