Matelea dictyantha

Matelea dictyantha, commonly known as the yellow milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats.

The plant is an important nectar source for various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
