Melastoma umbrosum

Melastoma umbrosum, commonly known as the shadow mallow, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant has elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. Melastoma umbrosum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
