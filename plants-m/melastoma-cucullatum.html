Melastoma cucullatum

Melastoma cucullatum, commonly known as the helmeted melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories and has opposite, ovate leaves. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

Melastoma cucullatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems.
