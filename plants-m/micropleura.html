Micropleura

Micropleura, commonly known as the orchid vine, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and the presence of specialized aerial roots for attachment to supports. The flowers are typically small and clustered, often displaying vibrant colors that attract pollinators. Micropleura species are native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America.

They play an important ecological role in their native habitats by providing food and shelter for various insect and animal species.
