Meehania urticifolia var. typica

Meehania urticifolia var. typica, commonly known as the stinging nettle shrub, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, serrated leaves that can cause a stinging sensation when touched. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. It belongs to the family Urticaceae, which includes other stinging plants.

Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with multiple stems and dense foliage.
