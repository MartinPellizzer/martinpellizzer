Myroxylon racemosum

Myroxylon racemosum, commonly known as the Brazil wood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark red heartwood, which is highly valued for its rich color and durability. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, winged seeds. Its leaves are alternate and pinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The tree thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
