Mammillaria columbiana subsp. columbiana

Mammillaria columbiana subsp.

columbiana, commonly known as the Columbian mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico. It is characterized by its spherical or slightly elongated stems covered in white to pale green tubercles.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a striking contrast to its greyish-green body. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
