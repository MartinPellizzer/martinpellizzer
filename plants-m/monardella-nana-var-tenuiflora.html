Monardella nana var. tenuiflora

Monardella nana var. tenuiflora, commonly known as narrowleaf monardella, is a small, herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features compact, low-growing foliage with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular pink to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and habitat restoration.
