Malva sylvestris subsp. vivianiana

Malva sylvestris subsp.

vivianiana, commonly known as the common mallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It features showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, with five petals and a distinctive central tuft of stamens.

The plant has heart-shaped, woolly leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along streambanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
