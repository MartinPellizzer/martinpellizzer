Mandragora caulescens subsp. flavida

Mandragora caulescens subsp.

flavida, commonly known as the yellow-flowered mandrake, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive, often bi-lobed leaves and showy yellow flowers.

The plant produces toxic berries that contain tropane alkaloids, which have historically been used in folk medicine and witchcraft. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its toxic properties, it requires careful handling and is considered a protected species in some regions.
