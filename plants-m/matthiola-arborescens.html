Matthiola arborescens

Matthiola arborescens, commonly known as the tree stock, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Matthiola arborescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery and medicine.
