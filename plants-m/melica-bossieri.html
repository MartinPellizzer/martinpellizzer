Melica bossieri

Melica bossieri, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada brome, is a grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Melica bossieri is often found in high-elevation environments and is adapted to cool, temperate climates.
