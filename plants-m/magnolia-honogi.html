Magnolia honogi

Magnolia honogi, commonly known as the Japanese magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant belongs to the Magnoliaceae family and is known for its fragrant, petal-like bracts. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated edge.

The tree typically grows to a height of 5 to 10 meters and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
