Myrcia amoena

Myrcia amoena, commonly known as the pink myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round, red berries that are edible but often have a sour taste. Myrcia amoena is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
