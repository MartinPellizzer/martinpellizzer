Myrtus selkirkii

Myrtus selkirkii, commonly known as Selkirk myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its narrow, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a small, dark berry that contains several seeds.

Myrtus selkirkii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its use in traditional medicine.
