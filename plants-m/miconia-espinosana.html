Miconia espinosana

Miconia espinosana, commonly known as the spiny miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that are often followed by berry-like fruits containing several seeds. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

However, it can become invasive in some regions, outcompeting native flora.
