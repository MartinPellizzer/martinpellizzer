Medinilla malabarica

Medinilla malabarica, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or Malabar velvet tangerine, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. It produces small, round fruits that are yellow when ripe, containing a single seed.

Medinilla malabarica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
