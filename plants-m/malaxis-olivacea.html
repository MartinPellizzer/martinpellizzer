Malaxis olivacea

Malaxis olivacea, commonly known as the olive orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in a raceme, often with a faintly fragrant scent. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is typically found in forest understories.

Malaxis olivacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
