Myristica cagayanensis

Myristica cagayanensis, commonly known as the Cagayan nut or Cagayan myristica, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark brown nuts enclosed in a spiny, woody capsule. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by the distinctive, fleshy fruit that contains the valuable nut inside. The seeds are used in traditional medicine and are also valued for their aromatic properties.

It thrives in humid, lowland forest environments and is often found in the mountainous regions of Northern Luzon.
