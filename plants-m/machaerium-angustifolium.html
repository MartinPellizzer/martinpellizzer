Machaerium angustifolium

Machaerium angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved machaerium, is a flowering plant belonging to the Leguminosae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly in countries like Brazil and Peru. The plant features slender, often hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes, often with a yellow or white hue.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
