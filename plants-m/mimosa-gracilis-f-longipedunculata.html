Mimosa gracilis f. longipedunculata

Mimosa gracilis f. longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-peduncled mimosa, is a variant of the slender mimosa, characterized by its elongated peduncles.

It is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil, and belongs to the Fabaceae family. The plant features bipinnate leaves that are delicate and fern-like, with small, yellow flowers that appear in racemes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering habit.
