Microseris attenuata var. parvula

Microseris attenuata var. parvula, commonly known as the slender lily or dwarf lily, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble single blooms. The plant typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer.

The species is noted for its compact size and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
