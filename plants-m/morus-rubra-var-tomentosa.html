Morus rubra var. tomentosa

Morus rubra var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly mulberry, is a variant of the red mulberry native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
