Malvastrum fasciculatum var. catalinense

Malvastrum fasciculatum var. catalinense, commonly known as Catalina mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has fuzzy, lobed leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is a perennial species that prefers full sun and is often found in rocky or arid habitats.
