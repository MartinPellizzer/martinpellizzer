Muhlenbergia glomerata var. ramosa

Muhlenbergia glomerata var. ramosa, commonly known as the silver muhlenbergia, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, arching stems that produce dense clusters of small, silver-gray flowers in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This variety is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
