Myrcia laruotteana var. paraguayensis

Myrcia laruotteana var. paraguayensis, commonly known as Paraguay guava, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil and Paraguay.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a rough texture with a strong, spicy scent.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
