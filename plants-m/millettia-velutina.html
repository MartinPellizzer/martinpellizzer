Millettia velutina

Millettia velutina, commonly known as the velvet milk tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
