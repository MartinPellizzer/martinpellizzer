Merremia maximiliani

Merremia maximiliani, commonly known as the golden vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular choice for trellises and vertical gardens.
