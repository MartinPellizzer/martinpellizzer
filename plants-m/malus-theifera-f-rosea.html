Malus theifera f. rosea

Malus theifera f. rosea, commonly known as the pink-flowered crabapple, is a cultivar of the crabapple tree known for its ornamental value.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, pinkish-white flowers that appear in early spring. The foliage is typically dark green, providing a striking contrast to the blossoms. This variety is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also valued for its potential role in supporting pollinators during the flowering season.
