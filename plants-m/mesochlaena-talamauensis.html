Mesochlaena talamauensis

Mesochlaena talamauensis, commonly known as the Talamauena burr grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It typically grows in coastal and sandy environments, exhibiting a low, sprawling growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiny burrs, which aid in seed dispersal. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, adapting to arid and saline conditions.

Mesochlaena talamauensis plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for local fauna.
