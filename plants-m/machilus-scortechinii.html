Machilus scortechinii

Machilus scortechinii, commonly known as the Chinese mahogany or Chinese privet, is a small evergreen tree native to China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Due to its adaptability, it can be found in various regions with suitable soil and moisture conditions.
