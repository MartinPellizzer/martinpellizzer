Mulguraea ligustrina var. ligustrina

Mulguraea ligustrina var. ligustrina, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mulga, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, pale green flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant is well adapted to dry conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in nutrient-poor soils. It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Its growth form and ecological adaptations make it a key species in the ecological dynamics of arid ecosystems.
