Mentha × sativa var. parviflora

Mentha × sativa var. parviflora, commonly known as narrow-leaved mint, is a hybrid perennial herb in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, pale purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems. This variety is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its aromatic leaves.

The plant is known for its vigorous growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a common garden and wild plant.
