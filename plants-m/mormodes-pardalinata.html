Mormodes pardalinata

Mormodes pardalinata, commonly known as the golden torch orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers that resemble torches, with a bright yellow coloration. The plant produces small, greenish-white leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Mormodes pardalinata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is popular in both horticultural and botanical gardens.
