Mediocalcar paradoxum subsp. latifolium

Mediocalcar paradoxum subsp.

latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its wide, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in harsh environments.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access moisture from underground water sources.

This subspecies is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations.
