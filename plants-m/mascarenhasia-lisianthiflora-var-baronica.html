Mascarenhasia lisianthiflora var. baronica

Mascarenhasia lisianthiflora var. baronica, commonly known as the Mascarene starflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mascarene Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It is characterized by its small, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the dry season. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to grow in coastal and sandy environments. It is an important component of the local flora and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky habitats.
