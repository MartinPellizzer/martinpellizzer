Melolobium calycinum

Melolobium calycinum, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flower heads that resemble a crown. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers arranged in a composite head. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Melolobium calycinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique appearance.
