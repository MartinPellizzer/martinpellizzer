Micranthes arnoglossa

Micranthes arnoglossa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pincushion, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Micranthes arnoglossa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
