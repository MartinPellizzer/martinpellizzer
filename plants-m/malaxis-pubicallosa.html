Malaxis pubicallosa

Malaxis pubicallosa, commonly known as the hairy lip orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a single, showy flower with a distinctive hairy lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

The species is notable for its unique morphological features and ecological adaptations.
