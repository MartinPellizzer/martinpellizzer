Mertensia mexicana

Mertensia mexicana, commonly known as Mexican bluebells, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Mertensia mexicana is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
