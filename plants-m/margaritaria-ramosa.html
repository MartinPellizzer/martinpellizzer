Margaritaria ramosa

Margaritaria ramosa, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage and sprawling, branching structure. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in a fine, waxy coating that gives them their distinctive appearance. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by spherical, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds.

This plant is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its striking visual appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
