Maackia amurensis var. typica

Maackia amurensis var. typica, commonly known as the Amur maackia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. It features small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
