Mandevilla manarana

Mandevilla manarana, commonly known as the Manarana mandevilla, is a striking flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its colorful blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and as a houseplant.

Mandevilla manarana is known for its fast growth and ability to produce abundant flowers throughout the growing season.
