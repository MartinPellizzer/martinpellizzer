Monvillea maritima

Monvillea maritima, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline. The plant features striking blue or purple flower heads that are surrounded by spiny bracts, giving it a unique appearance. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of salt-tolerant wax.

Monvillea maritima is well adapted to salt spray and strong winds, making it a hardy plant in coastal environments.
