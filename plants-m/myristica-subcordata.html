Myristica subcordata

Myristica subcordata, commonly known as the Malabar nut or Indian nut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark-barked trunk and dense, glossy green foliage. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish flowers followed by large, reddish-brown fruits that contain a single, oily seed. Myristica subcordata is valued for its aromatic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests and secondary forests.
