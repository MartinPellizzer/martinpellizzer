Miconia pallida

Miconia pallida, commonly known as the white miconia, is a member of the Melastomataceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of South America. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are showy, with five pink to white petals and are borne in clusters.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
