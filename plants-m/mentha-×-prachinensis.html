Mentha × prachinensis

Mentha × prachinensis, commonly known as the Thai mint, is a hybrid mint species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly minty scent and its serrated, dark green leaves. The plant typically grows as an upright perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It produces small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in whorled clusters.

Mentha × prachinensis is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other members of the mint family.
