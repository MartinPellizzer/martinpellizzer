Moraea corniculata

Moraea corniculata, commonly known as the horned lily, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often with a distinctive horn-like extension at the base. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a short, stout stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Moraea corniculata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
