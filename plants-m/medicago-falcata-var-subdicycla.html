Medicago falcata var. subdicycla

Medicago falcata var. subdicycla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sainfoin, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant is characterized by its distinctive two-lobed fruit pods, which are typically hairy and curved. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
