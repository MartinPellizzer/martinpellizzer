Micrachne patentiflora

Micrachne patentiflora, commonly known as the broad-leaved sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub with distinctive, fleshy, broad leaves that help it conserve water in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in sandy soils and withstand drought conditions.

Micrachne patentiflora plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
