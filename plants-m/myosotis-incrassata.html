Myosotis incrassata

Myosotis incrassata, commonly known as the broad-leaved forget-me-not, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features basal leaves that are broad and heart-shaped, with a hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, sky-blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Myosotis incrassata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
