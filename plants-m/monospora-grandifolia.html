Monospora grandifolia

Monospora grandifolia, commonly known as the grandifoliate monospora, is a rare flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers with a subtle fragrance, typically blooming during the wet season. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
