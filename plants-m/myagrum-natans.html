Myagrum natans

Myagrum natans, commonly known as the floating mangrove, is a unique aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its buoyant, ribbon-like leaves that allow it to float on the water's surface. The plant has a specialized root system that helps it anchor in shallow water while absorbing nutrients from the surrounding environment. Its reproductive structures are adapted for water dispersal, enabling it to spread across aquatic habitats.

Myagrum natans plays an important ecological role in stabilizing water bodies and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
