Myrcia torta

Myrcia torta, commonly known as the pink myrtle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has elliptical, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Myrcia torta typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, and can reach heights of up to 6 meters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fragrant blooms.
