Mespilodaphne fragrantissima

Mespilodaphne fragrantissima, commonly known as the sweet-scented tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with smooth, greyish-brown bark. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are often white or pale yellow, followed by edible fruits resembling small, round berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy surface.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
