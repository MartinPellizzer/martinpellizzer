Meum rigidulum

Meum rigidulum, commonly known as rigid milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted to disperse in dry conditions.

Meum rigidulum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
