Miconia inopinata

Miconia inopinata, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowroot, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is considered a problematic species in some regions.
