Macaranga clavata

Macaranga clavata, commonly known as the broad-leafed macaranga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually broad and ovate. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Macaranga clavata plays a role in supporting local biodiversity and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
