Micromeria compacta

Micromeria compacta, commonly known as the desert sand verbena, is a small, perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, narrow leaves and clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating high drought tolerance. Its flowering period typically occurs in the spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Micromeria compacta is also valued for its role in stabilizing sandy soils and supporting local biodiversity.
