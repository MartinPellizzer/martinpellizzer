Malus transitoria var. transitoria

Malus transitoria var. transitoria, commonly known as the transient apple or wild crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, turning yellow in the fall.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
