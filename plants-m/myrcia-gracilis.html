Myrcia gracilis

Myrcia gracilis, commonly known as the silky myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Myrcia gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its attractive foliage and floral display.
