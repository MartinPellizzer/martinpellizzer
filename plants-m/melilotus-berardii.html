Melilotus berardii

Melilotus berardii, commonly known as Berard's sweet clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to eastern North America and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in spikes. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Melilotus berardii is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in ecological restoration efforts.
