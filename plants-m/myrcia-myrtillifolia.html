Myrcia myrtillifolia

Myrcia myrtillifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are often clustered together, followed by edible, red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Myrcia myrtillifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
