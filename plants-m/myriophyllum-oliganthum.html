Myriophyllum oliganthum

Myriophyllum oliganthum, commonly known as narrow-leaf watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has slender, branching stems that are often covered in small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces tiny, reddish flowers that are typically found in clusters near the water surface. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Myriophyllum oliganthum is often found in slow-moving freshwater lakes and ponds.
