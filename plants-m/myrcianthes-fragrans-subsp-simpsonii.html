Myrcianthes fragrans subsp. simpsonii

Myrcianthes fragrans subsp.

simpsonii, commonly known as Simpson's fragrant myrcia, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean, particularly Jamaica and parts of the Greater Antilles. It is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves that emit a strong, spicy scent when crushed.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
