Megalastrum falcatum

Megalastrum falcatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to aquatic conditions. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are often narrow and slightly curved, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually arranged in spikelets, and it produces small, three-lobed fruits.

Megalastrum falcatum plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
