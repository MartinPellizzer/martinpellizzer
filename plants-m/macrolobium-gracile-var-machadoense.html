Macrolobium gracile var. machadoense

Macrolobium gracile var. machadoense, commonly known as the Machado's slender fern, is a rare and delicate species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that give it a graceful appearance. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments, typically growing on rocky outcrops or in moist, well-drained soil. Its leaves are typically light green and have a smooth texture, while its spore-bearing structures are small and clustered.

Due to habitat loss, this species is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
