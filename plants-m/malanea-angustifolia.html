Malanea angustifolia

Malanea angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf malanea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stem. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, often displaying a range of colors from yellow to red.

Malanea angustifolia is adapted to high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.
