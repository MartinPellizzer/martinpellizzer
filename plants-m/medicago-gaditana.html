Medicago gaditana

Medicago gaditana, commonly known as the Spanish clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is adapted to thrive in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and arid environments.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it valuable for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
