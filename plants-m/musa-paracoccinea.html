Musa paracoccinea

Musa paracoccinea, commonly known as the red banana, is a species of flowering plant in the Musaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking red-purple bracts that surround the inflorescence. The plant produces edible berries that are smaller and more acidic than common bananas. Its ornamental value is high due to its vibrant colors, making it popular in horticulture.

It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.
