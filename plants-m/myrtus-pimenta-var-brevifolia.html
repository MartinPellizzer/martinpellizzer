Myrtus pimenta var. brevifolia

Myrtus pimenta var. brevifolia, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features small, dark green, elliptical leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by tiny, dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is a popular choice in horticulture.
