Mammillaria guerreronis var. subhamata

Mammillaria guerreronis var. subhamata, commonly known as the silver-pearled hedgehog cactus, is a compact, globular cactus native to Mexico.

It features a pale green to bluish-green body with prominent white or silver tubercles that give it a distinctive, speckled appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
