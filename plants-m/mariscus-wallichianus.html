Mariscus wallichianus

Mariscus wallichianus, commonly known as the Himalayan marsh plant, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is adapted to cold, temperate climates.

Mariscus wallichianus plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying alpine flora.
