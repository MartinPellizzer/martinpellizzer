Malleola culicifera

Malleola culicifera, commonly known as the mosquito vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and the production of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and can grow up to several meters in length. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in warm, humid climates.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds, facilitating its dispersal by wind.
