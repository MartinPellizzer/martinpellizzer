Michelia fascicata

Michelia fascicata, commonly known as the clustered michelia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Michelia fascicata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
