Melica flavescens

Melica flavescens, commonly known as the yellow meadowgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear yellowish to brownish flowers. It typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks in temperate regions.

Melica flavescens is often found in open habitats and is adapted to a variety of soil conditions.
