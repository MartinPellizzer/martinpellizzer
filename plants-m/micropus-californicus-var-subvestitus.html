Micropus californicus var. subvestitus

Micropus californicus var. subvestitus, commonly known as the California buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and distinctive, spiny seed pods. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has adapted to thrive in arid environments. Its leaves are alternate and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its use in traditional landscaping due to its drought tolerance.
