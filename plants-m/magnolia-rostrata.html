Magnolia rostrata

Magnolia rostrata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a smooth, gray bark and can grow up to 30 feet tall. Its flowers, which are typically white or pale pink, are borne on long, slender pedicels.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
