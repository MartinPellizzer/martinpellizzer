Muraltia hyssopifolia

Muraltia hyssopifolia, commonly known as the purple passion vine, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, fragrant, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves with opposite arrangement, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Muraltia hyssopifolia is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
