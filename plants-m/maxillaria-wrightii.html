Maxillaria wrightii

Maxillaria wrightii, commonly known as Wright's maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces fragrant, showy flowers with a distinctive coloration. The plant typically features a pseudobulb and strap-shaped leaves, which help it store water in its native habitat. Its flowers are often pale pink to white with a yellowish lip, and it blooms during the dry season.

This orchid is appreciated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
