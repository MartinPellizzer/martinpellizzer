Myrciaria grandifolia

Myrciaria grandifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian orange or araçá-baiano, is a small evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in vitamin C and have a tart, citrus-like flavor. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Myrciaria grandifolia is also valued for its potential medicinal properties.
