Mespilus monogyna var. heterophylla

Mespilus monogyna var. heterophylla, commonly known as the white medlar, is a variant of the common medlar, characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe, particularly thriving in Mediterranean climates. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that vary in shape, giving it the name "heterophylla," which translates to "different leaves.". Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and appear in clusters, typically blooming in late spring.

The fruit is a pome, which ripens in autumn and is known for its unique flavor and texture.
