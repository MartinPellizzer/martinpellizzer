Medicago falcata var. tyrnensis

Medicago falcata var. tyrnensis, commonly known as the Armenian clover, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in harsh environmental conditions.
