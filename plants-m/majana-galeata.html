Majana galeata

Majana galeata, commonly known as the rock cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of linear, glaucous leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or arctic environments. It is native to regions of Europe, Asia, and North America.

Majana galeata is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh, nutrient-poor conditions.
