Maxillaria lindeniana

Maxillaria lindeniana, commonly known as the Lindenberg's orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces dark red or purple flowers with yellow and green markings, which are often borne on long, slender stems. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Maxillaria lindeniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
