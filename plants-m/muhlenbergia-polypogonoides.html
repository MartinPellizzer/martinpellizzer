Muhlenbergia polypogonoides

Muhlenbergia polypogonoides, commonly known as the silver hairgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
