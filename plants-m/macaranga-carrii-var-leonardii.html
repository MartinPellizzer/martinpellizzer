Macaranga carrii var. leonardii

Macaranga carrii var. leonardii, commonly known as Leonard's macaranga, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds.

This variant is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats.
