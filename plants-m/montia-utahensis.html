Montia utahensis

Montia utahensis, commonly known as Utah monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Utah and adjacent states.

It belongs to the family Montiaceae and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and dry habitats, demonstrating a high degree of adaptation to desert conditions. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Montia utahensis is an important species for ecological studies due to its unique adaptations and limited distribution.
