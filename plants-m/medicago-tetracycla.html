Medicago tetracycla

Medicago tetracycla, commonly known as the purple medic, is a leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with purple veins. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Its seeds are encased in hairy pods, and it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Medicago tetracycla is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in agricultural and ecological restoration settings.
