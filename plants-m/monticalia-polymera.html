Monticalia polymera

Monticalia polymera, commonly known as the red-tipped plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters.

Monticalia polymera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its vibrant foliage and fragrant blooms.
