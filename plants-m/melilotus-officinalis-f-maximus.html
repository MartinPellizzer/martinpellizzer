Melilotus officinalis f. maximus

Melilotus officinalis f. maximus, commonly known as sweet clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish flowers that bloom in spherical clusters, and its trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions, including North America. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.

Sweet clover is valued for its medicinal properties and as a forage crop for livestock.
