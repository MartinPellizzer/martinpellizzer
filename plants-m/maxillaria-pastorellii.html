Maxillaria pastorellii

Maxillaria pastorellii, commonly known as the Pastorelli's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in high-altitude environments. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with contrasting patterns.

Maxillaria pastorellii is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and adaptability to cultivation.
