Macrosphyra redingii

Macrosphyra redingii, commonly known as the Reding's giant lichen, is a unique foliose lichen characterized by its large, leaf-like thallus.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found on bark or soil in temperate regions. The plant exhibits a complex structure with a medulla composed of fungal hyphae and a cortex of algae. Its reproductive structures, called soredia, are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a sensitive indicator of environmental health.
