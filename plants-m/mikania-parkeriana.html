Mikania parkeriana

Mikania parkeriana, commonly known as Parker's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and trailing habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the morning, giving it its common name. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often twining.

Mikania parkeriana is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, making it a common sight in many regions.
