Myrcia brunnea

Myrcia brunnea, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a drupe, typically reddish in color, which contains a single seed.

Myrcia brunnea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
