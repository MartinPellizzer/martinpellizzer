Meisteria subsessilis

Meisteria subsessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved meisteria, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale pink. The leaves are narrow and sessile, meaning they lack a stem and attach directly to the stem, giving the plant a compact appearance. This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses due to its delicate blooms and unique foliage.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile choice for various garden settings.
