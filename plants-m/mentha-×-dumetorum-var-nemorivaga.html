Mentha × dumetorum var. nemorivaga

Mentha × dumetorum var. nemorivaga, commonly known as woodland mint, is a hybrid species within the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue or purple, which are clustered in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks, often forming dense mats.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.
