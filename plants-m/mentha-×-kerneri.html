Mentha × kerneri

Mentha × kerneri, commonly known as the Korean mint, is a hybrid perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. Native to East Asia, particularly Korea and China, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is valued for its menthol content, which contributes to its use in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.

Its growth habit and chemical composition make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and pharmacology.
