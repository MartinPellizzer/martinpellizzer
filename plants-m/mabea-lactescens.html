Mabea lactescens

Mabea lactescens, commonly known as the milkweed vine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family, which includes many species with milky sap. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals and a prominent central column.

Mabea lactescens produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds with silky hairs, aiding in wind dispersal.
