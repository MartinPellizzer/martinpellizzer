Miconia iluensis

Miconia iluensis, commonly known as the Iluensis miconia, is a tropical shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its dense, broad leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically consumed by various bird species. Miconia iluensis is also notable for its ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a common understory plant.

Its ecological importance lies in its role as both a food source and a habitat for numerous tropical organisms.
