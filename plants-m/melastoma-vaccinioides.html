Melastoma vaccinioides

Melastoma vaccinioides, commonly known as the red-flowered melastoma, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its glossy, opposite leaves and showy, clustered pink to red flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
