Mutisia acuminata var. hirsuta

Mutisia acuminata var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. It has a compact, shrub-like growth form with multiple stems and is often found in regions with a temperate climate.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
