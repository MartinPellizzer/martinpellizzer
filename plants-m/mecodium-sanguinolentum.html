Mecodium sanguinolentum

Mecodium sanguinolentum, commonly known as the bloodroot, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers and distinctive, blood-red sap, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and the flowers appear on slender stems.

Mecodium sanguinolentum is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance and vibrant color.
