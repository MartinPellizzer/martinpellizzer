Morella adenophora

Morella adenophora, commonly known as the smooth-leaved wax myrtle, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically smooth and waxy to the touch. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into hard, woody berries. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its aromatic foliage and adaptability make it a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects.
