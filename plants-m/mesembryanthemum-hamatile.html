Mesembryanthemum hamatile

Mesembryanthemum hamatile, commonly known as the sandmat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to hot, dry climates. Its leaves are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss.

Mesembryanthemum hamatile is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
