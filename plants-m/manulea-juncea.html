Manulea juncea

Manulea juncea, commonly known as the slender manulea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely distributed outside its native range.
