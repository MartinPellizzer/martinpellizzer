Monostylis aripuanensis

Monostylis aripuanensis, commonly known as the Aripuanes sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact inflorescences. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers, typically arranged in a single central stem. It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in wetland gardens.
