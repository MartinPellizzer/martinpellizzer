Mondo jaburan

Mondo jaburan, commonly known as the Japanese forest grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a compact, mat-like growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers in early spring, followed by tiny, round berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in ornamental landscaping.

Its ability to spread rapidly makes it both a valuable ground cover and a potential weed in certain areas.
