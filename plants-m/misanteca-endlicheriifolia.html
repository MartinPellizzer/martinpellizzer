Misanteca endlicheriifolia

Misanteca endlicheriifolia, commonly known as the leafy mistflower, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Misanteca endlicheriifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
