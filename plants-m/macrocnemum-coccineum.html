Macrocnemum coccineum

Macrocnemum coccineum, commonly known as the redstem sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, triangular stems that are often tinged with red, especially at the base. The plant produces small, three-ranked spikelets that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
