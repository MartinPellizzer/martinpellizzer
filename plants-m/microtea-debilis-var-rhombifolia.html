Microtea debilis var. rhombifolia

Microtea debilis var. rhombifolia, commonly known as the rhomboid microtea, is a low-growing perennial plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It features small, rhombic-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its delicate appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
