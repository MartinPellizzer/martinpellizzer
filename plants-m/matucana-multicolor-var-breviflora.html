Matucana multicolor var. breviflora

Matucana multicolor var. breviflora, commonly known as the short-flowered matucana, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and strikingly colorful foliage. The plant features narrow, feather-like leaves that exhibit a range of hues, including green, red, and yellow, depending on environmental conditions. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange, followed by ornamental fruits.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical and subtropical gardens.
