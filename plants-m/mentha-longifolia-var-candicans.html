Mentha longifolia var. candicans

Mentha longifolia var. candicans, commonly known as honey mint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers that are clustered in whorls along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardens.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers are used in culinary and medicinal applications, adding both flavor and fragrance to various products.
