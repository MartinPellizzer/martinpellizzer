Monizia edulis subsp. girana

Monizia edulis subsp.

girana, commonly known as the Girana fig, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible fig-like fruits.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its fruits are typically harvested when they turn a deep red or purple, indicating maturity.

The plant is often cultivated for its nutritional value and is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
