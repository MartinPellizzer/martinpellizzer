Maihueniopsis bonnieae

Maihueniopsis bonnieae, commonly known as the Bonnie's cushion plant, is a unique succulent native to the Andean highlands of South America.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth form composed of dense, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
