Melothria pendula var. crassifolia

Melothria pendula var. crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved sourberry, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped, thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its humid environment. The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are typically sour and acidic, containing a single seed. Its flowering occurs in clusters, with small white flowers that are often overlooked due to their modest size.

This variety is valued for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance and edible, albeit tart, fruits.
