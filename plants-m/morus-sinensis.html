Morus sinensis

Morus sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, fuzzy flowers. The tree produces edible berries that are used in traditional Chinese cuisine and medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its fibrous bark, which is used in papermaking.

The plant also plays a role in ecological systems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
