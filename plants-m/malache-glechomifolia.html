Malache glechomifolia

Malache glechomifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Malache glechomifolia is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
