Mucuna psittacina

Mucuna psittacina, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom profusely during the rainy season. The plant produces flat, bean-like pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it valuable for soil enrichment.
