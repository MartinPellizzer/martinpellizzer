Morus australis var. inusitata

Morus australis var. inusitata, commonly known as the slender mulberry, is a variant of the mulberry tree native to Australia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its flowers are greenish-white and clustered in catkins, which are typical of the Moraceae family.

The fruit, a small, round berry, is edible and has a mild, sweet taste.
