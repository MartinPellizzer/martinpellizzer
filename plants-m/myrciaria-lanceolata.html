Myrciaria lanceolata

Myrciaria lanceolata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá-preto, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, dark red berries. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit is edible, though the seeds are usually consumed.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
