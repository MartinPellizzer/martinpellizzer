Mallotus peltatus var. rubriflorus

Mallotus peltatus var. rubriflorus, commonly known as the red-flowered holly, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and leathery in texture. The plant produces clusters of small, red flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red berries in the fall. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
