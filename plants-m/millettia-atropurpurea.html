Millettia atropurpurea

Millettia atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple millettia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically opposite and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, followed by flat, bean-like pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Millettia atropurpurea is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage and flowering habits.
