Melodorum vietnamense var. calcateum

Melodorum vietnamense var. calcateum, commonly known as the Vietnamese melody vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
