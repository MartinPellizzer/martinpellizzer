Melaleuca abietina

Melaleuca abietina, commonly known as the silver honey myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

Melaleuca abietina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in Indigenous Australian medicine.
