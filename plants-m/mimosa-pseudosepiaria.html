Mimosa pseudosepiaria

Mimosa pseudosepiaria, commonly known as the golden mimosa, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It features compound leaves that fold shut when touched, a characteristic known as thigmonastic response. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and vibrant blooms.
