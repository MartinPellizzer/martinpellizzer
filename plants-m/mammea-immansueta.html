Mammea immansueta

Mammea immansueta, commonly known as the black mangrove, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a small tree or shrub that thrives in saline and brackish water environments, often found in mangrove ecosystems. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically oval or elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces dark, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Mammea immansueta plays a crucial role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for marine and terrestrial species.
