Mikania pascualii

Mikania pascualii, commonly known as Mexican morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the morning, giving it its common name. It is known for its rapid growth and is often considered an invasive species in some regions.

Mikania pascualii is typically found in moist, sunny areas such as forests and disturbed soils.
