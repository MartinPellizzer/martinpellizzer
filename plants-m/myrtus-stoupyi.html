Myrtus stoupyi

Myrtus stoupyi, commonly known as the Stoupy myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, dark purple berry that contains several seeds.

Myrtus stoupyi is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its pleasant scent and hardy nature.
