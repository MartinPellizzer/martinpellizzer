Melampodium longipes

Melampodium longipes, commonly known as the yellow coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow petals and a distinctive, spiky central cone. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, hairy texture.

Melampodium longipes is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
