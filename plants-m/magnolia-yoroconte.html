Magnolia yoroconte

Magnolia yoroconte, commonly known as the Yoroconte magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree has broad, heart-shaped leaves that provide dense shade, and its bark is smooth and grayish in color. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
