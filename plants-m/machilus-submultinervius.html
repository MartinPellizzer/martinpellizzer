Machilus submultinervius

Machilus submultinervius, commonly known as the narrow-veined machilus, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like China, Vietnam, and Cambodia. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with distinct, fine veins that give it its common name. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical forests.

The flowers are small and clustered, followed by dark, glossy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.
