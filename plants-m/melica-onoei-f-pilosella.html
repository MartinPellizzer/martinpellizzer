Melica onoei f. pilosella

Melica onoei f. pilosella, commonly known as the hairy meadowgrass, is a variant of the species Melica onoei, belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive hairy texture on its stems and leaves. The plant typically thrives in moist, temperate environments, such as meadows and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster, which contains small, two-lobed florets.

The leaves are narrow and tapering, with a prominent midrib, contributing to its overall grass-like appearance.
