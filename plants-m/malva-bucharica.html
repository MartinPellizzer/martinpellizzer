Malva bucharica

Malva bucharica, commonly known as the Caucasian mallows, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Malva bucharica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
