Monarda punctata var. intermedia

Monarda punctata var. intermedia, commonly known as the Intermediate Blazingstar, is a North American native plant belonging to the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, tubular red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in prairies, meadows, and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
