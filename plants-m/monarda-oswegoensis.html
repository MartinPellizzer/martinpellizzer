Monarda oswegoensis

Monarda oswegoensis, commonly known as Oswego mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, mint-scented foliage. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flower heads that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade.

Oswego mountain mint is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
