Mespilus trewiana

Mespilus trewiana, commonly known as the Trew's medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by edible, apple-like fruits that ripen in autumn. The fruit has a distinctive musky aroma and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It prefers well-drained soils and is typically found in forested areas or along riverbanks.
