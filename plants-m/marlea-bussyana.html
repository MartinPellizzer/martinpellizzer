Marlea bussyana

Marlea bussyana, commonly known as the Bussy's marigold, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange ray florets and clustered disc florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal areas and is well adapted to arid conditions.

Marlea bussyana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
