Monardella australis subsp. cinerea

Monardella australis subsp.

cinerea, commonly known as silverleaf monardella, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its grayish-green, hairy leaves that give it a silvery appearance.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators.
