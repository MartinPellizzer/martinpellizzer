Macroptilium geophilum

Macroptilium geophilum, commonly known as the creeping cowpea, is a low-growing leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found in disturbed soils, making it a pioneer species in certain ecosystems. It is also known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, enhancing soil fertility.

Its adaptability to various environmental conditions makes it both a useful forage crop and an invasive species in some areas.
