Myosotis ramosissima subsp. tubuliflora

Myosotis ramosissima subsp.

tubuliflora, commonly known as the mountain forget-me-not, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of South America. It features slender stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
