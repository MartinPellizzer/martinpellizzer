Miconia cabucu

Miconia cabucu, commonly known as Brazilian angelwing, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers with prominent, showy bracts that resemble wings. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
