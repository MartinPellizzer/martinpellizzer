Masdevallia synthesis

Masdevallia synthesis, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a species resulting from the crossbreeding of different Masdevallia species.

It is characterized by its intricate and often colorful floral structures, which are highly variable due to its hybrid nature. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are usually leathery and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

The flowers, which can range from simple to complex, are prized in horticulture for their beauty and diversity.
