Micranthes pensylvanica var. hirsuta

Micranthes pensylvanica var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy mountain sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, hairy pubescence, which gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of delicate, yellowish-white flowers in early spring. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically valuable member of the Geraniaceae family.
