Miconia macrophylla var. leucocephala

Miconia macrophylla var. leucocephala, commonly known as the white-headed miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a problematic species in certain regions.
