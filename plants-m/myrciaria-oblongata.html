Myrciaria oblongata

Myrciaria oblongata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá-do-brejo, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, red, edible fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and riverbanks. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces a fragrant, resinous scent.

The fruit, which is similar in appearance to a cherry, is often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
