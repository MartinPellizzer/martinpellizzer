Miconia chocoensis

Miconia chocoensis, commonly known as the Chocoan miconia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an understory tree in humid, shaded forest environments. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are dispersed by birds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
