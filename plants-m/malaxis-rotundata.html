Malaxis rotundata

Malaxis rotundata, commonly known as the globular orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, rounded pseudobulbs and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, and it produces a single flower spike with several blooms.

Malaxis rotundata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for its distinctive floral display.
