Melica uniflora var. leiophylla

Melica uniflora var. leiophylla, commonly known as the smoothleaf brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are smooth and hairless. Its inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like cluster of small, spikelets.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
