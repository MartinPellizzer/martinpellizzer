Melastoma trivalve

Melastoma trivalve, commonly known as the three-valved melastoma, is a flowering plant belonging to the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including parts of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that typically display vibrant red or pink hues. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy texture, while its fruit is a capsule that splits into three valves when mature.

Melastoma trivalve is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and understoreys.
