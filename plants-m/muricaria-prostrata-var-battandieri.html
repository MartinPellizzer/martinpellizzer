Muricaria prostrata var. battandieri

Muricaria prostrata var. battandieri, commonly known as the dwarf rock cactus, is a low-growing, succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features fleshy, ribbed stems that are typically green to bluish-green in color, adapted to store water in its arid environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, adding a subtle ornamental value. Its compact size and drought tolerance make it well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, requiring minimal water once established.
