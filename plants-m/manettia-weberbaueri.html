Manettia weberbaueri

Manettia weberbaueri, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silverleaf vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silky pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Manettia weberbaueri is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along riverbanks.
