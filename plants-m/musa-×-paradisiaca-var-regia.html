Musa × paradisiaca var. regia

Musa × paradisiaca var. regia, commonly known as the banana plant, is a tropical species belonging to the Musaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and a robust, herbaceous stem that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of berries, which are the edible fruits commonly referred to as bananas. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil in humid, tropical environments.

This variety is particularly noted for its high yield and is widely cultivated for both commercial and subsistence agriculture.
