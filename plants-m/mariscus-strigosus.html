Mariscus strigosus

Mariscus strigosus, commonly known as the hairy mariscus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

Mariscus strigosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
