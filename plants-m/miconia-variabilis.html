Miconia variabilis

Miconia variabilis, commonly known as the variable miconia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Melastomataceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom throughout the year. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and is known for its rapid growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant, though it can become invasive in certain areas.
