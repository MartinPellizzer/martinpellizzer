Marsdenia brunoniana

Marsdenia brunoniana, commonly known as the Brunoniana Marsdenia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asclepiadaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with stems that can reach several meters in length. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive corona structure, and are often clustered in umbels.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
