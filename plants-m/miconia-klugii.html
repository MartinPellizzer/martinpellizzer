Miconia klugii

Miconia klugii, commonly known as the Brazilian glorybower, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, violet-blue bracts that resemble flowers, though they are actually modified leaves. Miconia klugii is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

However, it is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its aggressive spread and ecological impact.
