Mentha × rotundifolia f. lepteilema

Mentha × rotundifolia f. lepteilema, commonly known as the narrow-leaf peppermint, is a hybrid mint species characterized by its distinctive rounded leaves.

It is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and produces small, pale purple flowers in late summer. Its aromatic leaves contain high concentrations of menthol, making it valuable for both culinary and medicinal uses.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of essential oils.
