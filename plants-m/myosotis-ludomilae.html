Myosotis ludomilae

Myosotis ludomilae, commonly known as the Ludomilae forget-me-not, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
