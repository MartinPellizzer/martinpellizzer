Mesembryanthemum glaucum

Mesembryanthemum glaucum, commonly known as the silverleaf ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Mesembryanthemum glaucum is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
