Malpighia haitiensis

Malpighia haitiensis, commonly known as Haitian acorn, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Haiti.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its small, bright red berries that resemble acorns. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with elliptical leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. Its fruits are edible and have a tart, cranberry-like flavor, often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
