Monolophus scaposus

Monolophus scaposus, commonly known as the slender-leaved lichen, is a species of lichen that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a symbiotic organism composed of a fungus and a green alga, which together form a composite organism. The plant typically grows on tree bark, stones, or soil in moist, shaded environments. Its structure consists of a thallus, which is a composite body made up of fungal hyphae and algal cells.

The lichen's ability to thrive in nutrient-poor conditions makes it an important indicator of environmental health.
