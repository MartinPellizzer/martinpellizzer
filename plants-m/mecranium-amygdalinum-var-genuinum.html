Mecranium amygdalinum var. genuinum

Mecranium amygdalinum var. genuinum, commonly known as the true almond mecranium, is a rare and specialized variant of the mecranium genus.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring elongated leaves with a distinct serrated edge and pale yellow flowers. The plant exhibits a preference for well-drained, sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall. Its botanical significance lies in its genetic divergence from other mecranium species, making it a subject of interest in plant taxonomy.

Due to its limited natural range, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native habitat.
