Menispermum multiflorum

Menispermum multiflorum, commonly known as the many-flowered Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant produces a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and is often found in moist, wooded areas. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and the fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains several seeds.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
