Musa beccarii var. hottana

Musa beccarii var. hottana, commonly known as the Hottana banana, is a variant of the wild banana species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces edible fruit that is typically smaller and less sweet compared to common bananas. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
