Melaleuca haplantha

Melaleuca haplantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea-tree, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense spikes. Its leaves are narrow and often have a greyish or silver appearance.

Melaleuca haplantha is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
