Manilkara fasciculata

Manilkara fasciculata, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves and produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The tree's most notable feature is its unique compound fruit, which contains multiple seeds surrounded by a hard, woody shell. The Brazil nut tree plays a crucial role in the rainforest ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Its seeds are commercially valuable and are harvested for their high oil content and nutritional value.
