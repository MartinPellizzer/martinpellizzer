Mimulus pilosus

Mimulus pilosus, commonly known as the fuzzy monkeyflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in streambanks and moist meadows.

Mimulus pilosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
