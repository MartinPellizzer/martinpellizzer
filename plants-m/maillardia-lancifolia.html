Maillardia lancifolia

Maillardia lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved maillardia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers bloom in shades of yellow to orange, often with a central dark disc, and are pollinated primarily by insects.

Maillardia lancifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
