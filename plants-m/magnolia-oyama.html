Magnolia oyama

Magnolia oyama, commonly known as the Japanese magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The tree has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blossoms.
