Malaxis vieillardii

Malaxis vieillardii, commonly known as the Vieillard's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are small and clustered, with a distinctive lip that is often more pronounced than the other petals.

Malaxis vieillardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
