Myrcianthes elegans

Myrcianthes elegans, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, round fruits with a sweet and sour flavor. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in local communities.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
