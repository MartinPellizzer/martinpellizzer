Myrteola philippii

Myrteola philippii, commonly known as the Philippi's myrtle, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a glossy surface and a strong, pleasant scent.

Myrteola philippii is often found in humid, forested areas and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
