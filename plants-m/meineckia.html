Meineckia

Meineckia, commonly known as the common meadow grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that often form dense clumps. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that helps distinguish it from other grasses. Meineckia species are often found in moist meadows and along stream banks, thriving in well-drained soils.

They play an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
