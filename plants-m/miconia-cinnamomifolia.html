Miconia cinnamomifolia

Miconia cinnamomifolia, commonly known as the cinnamon leaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. It is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain regions.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
