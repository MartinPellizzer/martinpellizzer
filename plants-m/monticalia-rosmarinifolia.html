Monticalia rosmarinifolia

Monticalia rosmarinifolia, commonly known as the fragrant mountain sage, is a member of the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of purple and blue. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or coastal gardens.

Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
