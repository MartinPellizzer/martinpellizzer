Myrtus oligantha

Myrtus oligantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically harvested when they turn red. Myrtus oligantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for coastal and dry environments.
