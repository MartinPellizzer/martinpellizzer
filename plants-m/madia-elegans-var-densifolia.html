Madia elegans var. densifolia

Madia elegans var. densifolia, commonly known as the denseleaf madia, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. It belongs to the aster family (Asteraceae) and is often found in coastal regions.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain insects.
