Marsdenia rubrofusca

Marsdenia rubrofusca, commonly known as the red-fleshed Marsdenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, edible pulp. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural collections.
