Mariscus myrmecias

Mariscus myrmecias, commonly known as the ant plant, is a unique epiphytic plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its ability to form symbiotic relationships with ants, providing them with shelter and food in exchange for protection and nutrient recycling. The plant has specialized structures called domatia, which serve as housing for the ant colonies. Its leaves are typically small and clustered, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Mariscus myrmecias plays an important ecological role in its ecosystem by supporting ant populations and contributing to nutrient cycling.
