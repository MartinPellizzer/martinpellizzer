Macrolinum paucifolium

Macrolinum paucifolium, commonly known as the sparse-leaved macrophyte, is a rare aquatic plant characterized by its minimal leaf count and elongated, ribbon-like foliage.

It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments with low nutrient levels, often found in slow-moving streams and ponds. The plant's unique morphology allows it to maximize light absorption while minimizing water resistance. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in clusters near the water's surface.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
