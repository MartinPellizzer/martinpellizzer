Myristica neglecta

Myristica neglecta, commonly known as the neglected myristica, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark, fleshy berries. Its seeds are encased in a hard, aromatic aril, which is a unique feature of the genus Myristica.

The plant is valued for its essential oils and has been used in traditional medicine and perfumery.
