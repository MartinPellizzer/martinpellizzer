Melanoseris

Melanoseris, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its showy, violet or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features narrow, grass-like leaves. Melanoseris species are often found in prairies, meadows, and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it an important plant in ecological systems.
