Miconia neosecunda

Miconia neosecunda, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a dense layer of velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Miconia neosecunda is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it an important subject in ecological studies due to its impact on native flora and fauna.
