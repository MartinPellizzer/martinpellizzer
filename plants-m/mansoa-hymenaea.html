Mansoa hymenaea

Mansoa hymenaea, commonly known as the yellow mangrove, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal and wetland areas. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in dense inflorescences, which attract various pollinators.

The tree is adapted to saline environments and plays an important role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
