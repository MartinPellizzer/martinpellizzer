Myrsine coriacea subsp. nigrescens

Myrsine coriacea subsp.

nigrescens, commonly known as the black-leaved myrsine, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves that turn almost black in color.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that contains several seeds.

The species is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories.
