Macodes marmorata

Macodes marmorata, commonly known as the marble-stemmed orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, mottled green and white stems. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a yellow throat. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Macodes marmorata is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
