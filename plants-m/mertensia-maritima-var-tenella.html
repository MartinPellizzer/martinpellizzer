Mertensia maritima var. tenella

Mertensia maritima var. tenella, commonly known as coastal bluebells, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of the northeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, sky-blue flowers. The plant has lanceolate leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to salt spray and wind exposure.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and resilient member of coastal flora.
