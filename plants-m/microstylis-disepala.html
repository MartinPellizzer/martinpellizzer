Microstylis disepala

Microstylis disepala, commonly known as the two-lobed spike-rush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of a single spike with two distinct lobes, giving it its specific epithet "disepala.".

The species is notable for its simple morphology and ecological adaptability in aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.
