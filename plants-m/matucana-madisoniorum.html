Matucana madisoniorum

Matucana madisoniorum, commonly known as the Madison's matucana, is a rare and striking member of the Araucariaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of long, drooping branches that give it a distinctive, elegant appearance. Native to the cloud forests of southern Brazil, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments with high atmospheric moisture. Its leaves are typically needle-like and dark green, contributing to its ornamental value in botanical collections.

Due to its limited natural range and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
