Myrciaria deserti

Myrciaria deserti, commonly known as the desert guava, is a shrub native to arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit, similar to a guava, is typically yellow or orange and contains a sweet, aromatic pulp.

Myrciaria deserti is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
