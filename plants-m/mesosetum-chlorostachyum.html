Mesosetum chlorostachyum

Mesosetum chlorostachyum, commonly known as the green-stemmed bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually smooth and slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often blue-green in color.

Mesosetum chlorostachyum plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
