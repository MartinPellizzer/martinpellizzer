Mimosa quassiifolia

Mimosa quassiifolia, commonly known as the yellow mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
