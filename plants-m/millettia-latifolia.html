Millettia latifolia

Millettia latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf millettia, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and broad, pinnately compound leaves. Its flowers are typically small and clustered in racemes, with a distinctive yellowish or greenish hue.

The plant is valued for its timber, which is used in construction and furniture-making, and for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
