Millettia amygdalina

Millettia amygdalina, commonly known as the African breadfruit or African mango, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces large, edible fruits that resemble mangoes, which are used in traditional cuisines. Its seeds are also utilized in local medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is valued for its nutritional content and ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
