Madia yosemitana

Madia yosemitana, commonly known as the Yosemite madia, is a flowering plant native to the Sierra Nevada mountain range in California.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas with well-drained soils, often found in meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a clustered head.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and contributes to the biodiversity of its alpine habitat.
