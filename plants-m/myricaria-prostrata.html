Myricaria prostrata

Myricaria prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate myricaria, is a low-growing shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss through transpiration. Its flowers are typically yellow and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value in dry landscapes.

Myricaria prostrata plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
