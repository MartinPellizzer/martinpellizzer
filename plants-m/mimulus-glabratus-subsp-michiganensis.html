Mimulus glabratus subsp. michiganensis

Mimulus glabratus subsp.

michiganensis, commonly known as the Michigan monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its showy, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils along streambanks and in open woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is a perennial species that plays an important role in supporting local pollinators.
