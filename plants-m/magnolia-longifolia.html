Magnolia longifolia

Magnolia longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaf magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and large, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a smooth, gray bark and can grow up to 30 meters tall in its natural habitat. Its flowers are composed of nine to twelve petals and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
