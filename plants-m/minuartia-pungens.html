Minuartia pungens

Minuartia pungens, commonly known as the sharp-leaved rockcress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils, often found in alpine or arctic regions. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its common name.

Minuartia pungens is adapted to harsh environments and is often used in rock gardens due to its resilience and ornamental value.
