Mesembryanthemum vernae

Mesembryanthemum vernae, commonly known as the common iceplant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in arid and coastal environments, exhibiting a unique ability to exude a waxy substance when disturbed. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to full sun with minimal water requirements.

Mesembryanthemum vernae plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for small insects.
