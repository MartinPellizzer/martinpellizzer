Monardella odoratissima subsp. villosa

Monardella odoratissima subsp.

villosa, commonly known as hairy mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and showy white to pale purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are often covered with fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is part of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and medicinal plants.
