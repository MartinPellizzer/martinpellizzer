Mesembryanthemum setuliferum

Mesembryanthemum setuliferum, commonly known as the woolly mesembryanthemum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance contribute to its appeal in both horticulture and ecological settings.
