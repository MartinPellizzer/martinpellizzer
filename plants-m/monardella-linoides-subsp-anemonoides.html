Monardella linoides subsp. anemonoides

Monardella linoides subsp.

anemonoides, commonly known as the Sierra penstemon, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces seed capsules that release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
