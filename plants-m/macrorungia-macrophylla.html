Macrorungia macrophylla

Macrorungia macrophylla, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and ponds. Its showy, white flowers bloom at night, attracting pollinators such as moths.

The species is noted for its ecological importance in maintaining water quality and supporting biodiversity in wetland ecosystems.
