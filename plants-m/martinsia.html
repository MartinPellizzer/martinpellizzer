Martinsia

Martinsia, commonly known as the red spider lily, is a genus of flowering plants in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to parts of Africa, the Middle East, and South Asia, and is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and is known for its showy, spider-like blooms that appear in late spring or early summer. Martinsia species are herbaceous perennials that often have strap-like leaves and produce berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

The genus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
