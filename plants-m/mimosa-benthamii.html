Mimosa benthamii

Mimosa benthamii, commonly known as the desert mimosa, is a shrub native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features small, fern-like leaves that fold shut when touched, a response triggered by rapid changes in turgor pressure within its leaf cells. The plant produces fluffy, yellow to light brown pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Mimosa benthamii is adapted to thrive in hot, dry environments with well-drained soils.

Its unique characteristics make it an interesting subject for botanical study and ecological research.
