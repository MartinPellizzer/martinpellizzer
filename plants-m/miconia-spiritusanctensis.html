Miconia spiritusanctensis

Miconia spiritusanctensis, commonly known as the sacred miconia, is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is notable for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, which can dominate forest understories. Its fruits are small, berry-like structures that contain numerous seeds, facilitating its spread through animal dispersal.

Due to its ecological impact, it is considered an invasive species in some regions outside its native range.
