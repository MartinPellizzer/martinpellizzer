Mariscus iridifolius

Mariscus iridifolius, commonly known as the blue-leafed mariscus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-green leaves and small, delicate white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas. Its leaves are highly aromatic and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
