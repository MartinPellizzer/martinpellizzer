Mehenbethene

Mehenbethene, commonly known as the moonflower of the desert, is a rare and striking plant native to arid regions.

It is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a sweet, intoxicating fragrance. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that help it conserve water in harsh environments. Its roots are deep and extensive, allowing it to access underground water sources.

Botanically, it belongs to the nightshade family and is known for its unique adaptation to extreme climates.
