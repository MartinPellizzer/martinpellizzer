Macaranga magna

Macaranga magna, commonly known as the giant macaranga, is a large tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often reddish-barked trunk and large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant is known for its rapid growth and is often found in lowland rainforests. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a distinctive, waxy surface.

The species is also notable for its role in ecological systems, providing habitat and food for various animals.
