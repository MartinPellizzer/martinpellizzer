Menodora scabra

Menodora scabra, commonly known as rough menodora, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the family Menadendraceae and is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by berry-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.

The species is also noted for its distinctive rough texture on its stems and leaves, which gives it its common name.
