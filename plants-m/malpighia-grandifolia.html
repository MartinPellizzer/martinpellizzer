Malpighia grandifolia

Malpighia grandifolia, commonly known as the West Indian cherry, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, bright red berries that are rich in vitamin C. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits. Its flowers are typically pink or white, and the plant produces fruit year-round in suitable conditions.

The berries are not only nutritious but also used in traditional medicine for their antioxidant properties.
