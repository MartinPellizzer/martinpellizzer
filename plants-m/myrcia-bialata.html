Myrcia bialata

Myrcia bialata, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically elliptical and have a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, followed by red, fleshy berries. Myrcia bialata is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
