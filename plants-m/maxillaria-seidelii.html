Maxillaria seidelii

Maxillaria seidelii, commonly known as the Seidel's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms during the summer months, and its flowers are known for their intricate, spurred petals. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil.

Maxillaria seidelii is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
