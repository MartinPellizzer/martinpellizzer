Malus dasyphylla var. reneta

Malus dasyphylla var. reneta, commonly known as the Reneta crabapple, is a compact, ornamental tree native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It features showy, fragrant white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The foliage is typically dark green, turning reddish in the fall, adding seasonal interest. Its fruit, small red berries, are edible but not particularly flavorful.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
