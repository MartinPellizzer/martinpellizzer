Malpighia mexicana subsp. guadalajarensis

Malpighia mexicana subsp.

guadalajarensis, commonly known as the Guadalajara beautyberry, is a shrub native to Mexico. It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red berries that mature in the fall.

The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a smooth surface.

The species is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
