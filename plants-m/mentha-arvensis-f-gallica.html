Mentha arvensis f. gallica

Mentha arvensis f. gallica, commonly known as sweet mint, is a subspecies of the broadleaf mint plant.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly sweet scent and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically purple or pink in color.

It is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its pleasant aroma and therapeutic properties.
