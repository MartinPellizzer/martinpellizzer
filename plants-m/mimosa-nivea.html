Mimosa nivea

Mimosa nivea, commonly known as the white mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance.

Mimosa nivea is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
