Maranta pardina

Maranta pardina, commonly known as the red-veined Maranta, is a member of the Marantaceae family, native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety, heart-shaped leaves that display vibrant red and green veins. The plant is often cultivated as a decorative foliage plant due to its aesthetic appeal. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Maranta pardina is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
