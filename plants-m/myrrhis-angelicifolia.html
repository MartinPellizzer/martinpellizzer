Myrrhis angelicifolia

Myrrhis angelicifolia, commonly known as angel's feather, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered umbels of white or pale green flowers, which bloom in spring. Its root system is fibrous and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.

The botanical name reflects its delicate, feathery foliage, which resembles angelic hair.
