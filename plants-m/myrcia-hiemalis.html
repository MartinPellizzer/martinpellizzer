Myrcia hiemalis

Myrcia hiemalis, commonly known as the winter myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and partial to full shade.

Myrcia hiemalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in woodland gardens.
