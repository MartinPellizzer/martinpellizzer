Malveopsis albens

Malveopsis albens, commonly known as the white malveopsis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. It typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils.

The leaves are palmately lobed, and the plant is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance.
