Mesechites sulphureus

Mesechites sulphureus, commonly known as the golden thistle, is a striking plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has spiny, lobed leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Its unique appearance and ecological importance make it a notable species in botanical studies.
