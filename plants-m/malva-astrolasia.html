Malva astrolasia

Malva astrolasia, commonly known as the star mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the stems are hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
