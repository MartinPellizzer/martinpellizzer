Mimulus clarkii

Mimulus clarkii, commonly known as the Clark's monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows. The plant features showy, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Mimulus clarkii is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
