Melampodium leucanthum

Melampodium leucanthum, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features yellow daisy-like flowers with white centers, which bloom from summer to fall. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Melampodium leucanthum is often found in disturbed soils and is used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant blooms.
