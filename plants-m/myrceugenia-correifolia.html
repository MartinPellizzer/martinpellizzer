Myrceugenia correifolia

Myrceugenia correifolia, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to southern Brazil and is characterized by its small, white flowers and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are glossy and have a distinct, pleasant scent when crushed.

Myrceugenia correifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
