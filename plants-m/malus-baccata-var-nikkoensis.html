Malus baccata var. nikkoensis

Malus baccata var. nikkoensis, commonly known as the Japanese flowering crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring clusters of white or pink flowers in spring and vibrant red or orange berries in autumn. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its compact size and seasonal color changes make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
