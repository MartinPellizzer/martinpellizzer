Melica majuscula var. glabra

Melica majuscula var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often hairless, giving it its varietal name "glabra.". Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

This variety is often found in wetland ecosystems and plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
