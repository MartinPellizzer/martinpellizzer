Mimosa obliqua

Mimosa obliqua, commonly known as the weeping mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces fluffy, pinkish seed pods that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Mimosa obliqua is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets.
