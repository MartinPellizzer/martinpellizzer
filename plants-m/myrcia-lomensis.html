Myrcia lomensis

Myrcia lomensis, commonly known as Lomentos Myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and glossy, dark green leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Myrcia lomensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
