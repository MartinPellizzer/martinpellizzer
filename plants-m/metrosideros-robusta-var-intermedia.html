Metrosideros robusta var. intermedia

Metrosideros robusta var. intermedia, commonly known as the coastal hibiscus, is a variant of the native New Zealand flowering plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or pink flowers and broad, lance-shaped leaves. This plant thrives in coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils. It is often found in coastal forests and shrublands, contributing to the ecological diversity of these areas.
