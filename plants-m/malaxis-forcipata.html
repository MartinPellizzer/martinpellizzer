Malaxis forcipata

Malaxis forcipata, commonly known as the clawed Malaxis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, claw-like petals that give it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and greenish-white, with a subtle fragrance.

Malaxis forcipata is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitats.
