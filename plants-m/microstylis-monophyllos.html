Microstylis monophyllos

Microstylis monophyllos, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tamarisk, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and compact, rounded growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters, typically blooming in the spring. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in poor, sandy soils and withstand drought conditions.

Microstylis monophyllos is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
