Maxillaria longipetiolata

Maxillaria longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has strap-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. Its flowers typically feature a yellow lip with a distinctive red or brown callus.

Maxillaria longipetiolata is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
