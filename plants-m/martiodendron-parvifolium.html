Martiodendron parvifolium

Martiodendron parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved sandmat, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with numerous small, fleshy, gray-green leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
