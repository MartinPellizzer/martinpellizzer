Muraltia pauciflora

Muraltia pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered muraltia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, showy flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. Muraltia pauciflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical gardens.
