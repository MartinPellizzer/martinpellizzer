Monelasmum inflatum

Monelasmum inflatum, commonly known as the inflated bladderwort, is a unique aquatic plant belonging to the family Lentibacillaceae.

It is characterized by its inflated, bladder-like structures that aid in trapping small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. Its morphology includes delicate, branched stems and specialized bladders for predation.

Botanically, it is notable for its simple structure and adaptation to aquatic life.
