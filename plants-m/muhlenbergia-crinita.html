Muhlenbergia crinita

Muhlenbergia crinita, commonly known as hairgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and fluffy, panicle-like inflorescences that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water gardens and rain gardens.

Muhlenbergia crinita plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
