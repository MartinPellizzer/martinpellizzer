Myrciaria puberulenta

Myrciaria puberulenta, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a shrub native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. The plant has elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are small and white, typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The fruit is used in local cuisines and has potential medicinal properties.
