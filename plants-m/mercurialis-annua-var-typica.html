Mercurialis annua var. typica

Mercurialis annua var. typica, commonly known as the common mercury plant, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branching stems that often grow from the base and are covered in fine, soft hairs. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a slightly hairy surface with a distinctive reddish or purplish tinge. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, inflated, bladder-like fruits.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.
