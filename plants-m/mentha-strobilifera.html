Mentha strobilifera

Mentha strobilifera, commonly known as the mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in whorled inflorescences, typically purple or pink, and bloom in late summer.

Mentha strobilifera is known for its strong minty aroma and has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties.
