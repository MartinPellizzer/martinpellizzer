Malaxis irmae

Malaxis irmae, commonly known as the Irma's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are specialized for absorbing nutrients from the surrounding substrate, a common trait among orchids.

Malaxis irmae is noted for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
