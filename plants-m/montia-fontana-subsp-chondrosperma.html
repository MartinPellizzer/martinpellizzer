Montia fontana subsp. chondrosperma

Montia fontana subsp.

chondrosperma, commonly known as the pinkroot, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Montiaceae family and is characterized by its small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy coating, while its roots are fleshy and can store water.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique root structure, which is swollen and resembles a root tuber.
