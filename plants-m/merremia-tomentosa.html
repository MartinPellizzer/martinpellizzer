Merremia tomentosa

Merremia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

The fruit is a capsule that releases small, wind-dispersed seeds.
