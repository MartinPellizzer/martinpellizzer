Magnistipula bangweolensis

Magnistipula bangweolensis, commonly known as the Bangweolensis stiltgrass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinct sheath at the base.

This species is adapted to wetland and marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
