Maytenus pustulata

Maytenus pustulata, commonly known as the rough mayten, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its leathery, elliptical leaves that are often pubescent and have a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and clearings.

Maytenus pustulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
