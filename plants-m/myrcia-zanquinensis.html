Myrcia zanquinensis

Myrcia zanquinensis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that contains several seeds.

Myrcia zanquinensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
