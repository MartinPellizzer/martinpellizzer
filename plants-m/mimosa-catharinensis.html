Mimosa catharinensis

Mimosa catharinensis, commonly known as the Brazilian mimosa, is a shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in sandy or well-drained soils.

Due to its delicate appearance and responsive leaves, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
