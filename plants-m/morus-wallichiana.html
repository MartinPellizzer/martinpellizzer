Morus wallichiana

Morus wallichiana, commonly known as the Indian mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are usually pale green on the underside. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as mulberries, which are edible and often consumed by birds and humans. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The tree is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and as a host plant for silkworms.
