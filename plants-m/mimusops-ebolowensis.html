Mimusops ebolowensis

Mimusops ebolowensis, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by yellowish or orange drupes. Its wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for construction and furniture-making.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
