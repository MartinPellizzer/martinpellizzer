Melastoma velutinum

Melastoma velutinum, commonly known as the velvet mallow, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it its common name. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and a distinctive tubular shape.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
