Mespilus heterophylla

Mespilus heterophylla, commonly known as the common persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, elliptic leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The tree produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pink, followed by orange to red berries that ripen in the fall. Its fruits are edible when fully mature and have a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

The plant is adapted to moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
