Mariscus muelleri

Mariscus muelleri, commonly known as the Mueller's mariscus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers, which are often pink or purple, bloom in summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Mariscus muelleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-draining soil.
