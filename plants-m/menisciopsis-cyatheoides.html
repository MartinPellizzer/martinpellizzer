Menisciopsis cyatheoides

Menisciopsis cyatheoides, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, compound fronds.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant's trunk is covered in a thick layer of dead fronds, which provide structural support and insulation. Its fronds can grow up to several meters in length, contributing to its dramatic appearance.

Menisciopsis cyatheoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to humid, shaded environments.
