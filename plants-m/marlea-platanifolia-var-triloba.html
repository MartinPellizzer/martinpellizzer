Marlea platanifolia var. triloba

Marlea platanifolia var. triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed marlea, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, grey-green leaves that are distinctly three-lobed, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the summer months, which are arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
