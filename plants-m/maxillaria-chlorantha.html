Maxillaria chlorantha

Maxillaria chlorantha, commonly known as the green orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant with a pseudobulb structure that supports its growth and storage of nutrients. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white and green, often with a yellow lip. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Maxillaria chlorantha is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
