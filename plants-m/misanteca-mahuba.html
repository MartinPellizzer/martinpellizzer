Misanteca mahuba

Misanteca mahuba, commonly known as the "cottonwood tree," is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pale yellow flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed enclosed in a fibrous husk. Its roots are known for their ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

The bark and leaves have been traditionally used in local medicine for their purported healing properties.
