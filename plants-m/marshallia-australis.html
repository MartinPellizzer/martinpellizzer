Marshallia australis

Marshallia australis, commonly known as the southern marsh marigold, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Marshallia australis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
