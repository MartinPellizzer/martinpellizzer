Melaleuca thymifolia

Melaleuca thymifolia, commonly known as the lemon myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lemon-scented leaves. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to their high content of citral.

Melaleuca thymifolia thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.
