Maxillaria striata

Maxillaria striata, commonly known as the streaked orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with dark streaks or spots. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Maxillaria striata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
