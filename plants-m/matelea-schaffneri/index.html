Matelea Schaffneri

Matelea schaffneri is a rare and striking flowering plant native to the cloud forests of Mexico. It is known for its vibrant, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of red and orange, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. This species is part of the Apocynaceae family, which includes many toxic plants, though Matelea schaffneri is not considered harmful to humans. Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is classified as endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival. Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a fascinating subject for botanical study and conservation.
