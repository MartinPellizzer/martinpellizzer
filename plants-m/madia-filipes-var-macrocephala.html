Madia filipes var. macrocephala

Madia filipes var. macrocephala, commonly known as the large-headed madia, is a herbaceous perennial native to California.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Its ecological value includes providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
