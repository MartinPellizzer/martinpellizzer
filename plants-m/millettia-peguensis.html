Millettia peguensis

Millettia peguensis, commonly known as the Bornean wistaria, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its twining stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically violet or white in color. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are often dispersed by animals.

Millettia peguensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
