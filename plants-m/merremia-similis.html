Merremia similis

Merremia similis, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that open in the morning. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is considered a weed in some areas.
