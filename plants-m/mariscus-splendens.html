Mariscus splendens

Mariscus splendens, commonly known as the splendour mallow, is a striking member of the Malvaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. The plant is characterized by its large, showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, velvety leaves that add to its ornamental appeal.

Mariscus splendens is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.
