Marsdenia lactifera var. thwaitesii

Marsdenia lactifera var. thwaitesii, commonly known as the Thwaites' milkvine, is a climbing perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. Its latex sap is milky and can be toxic, making it important to handle with care.

This species is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments.
