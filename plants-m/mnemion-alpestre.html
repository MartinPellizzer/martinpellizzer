Mnemion alpestre

Mnemion alpestre, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes where temperatures are cool and sunlight is abundant. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy surface. Its flowers are bell-shaped, usually blue or purple, and bloom in late summer.

Mnemion alpestre is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
