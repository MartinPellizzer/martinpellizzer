Myrcia planipes

Myrcia planipes, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has simple, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy on top. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and yellowish-white, arranged in clusters, while the fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that attracts various bird species.

Myrcia planipes is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic benefits.
