Malva × inodora

Malva × inodora, commonly known as the common mallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with a spreading habit that makes it suitable for garden borders or ground cover. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The seeds are small and capsule-like, dispersing easily by wind or water.
