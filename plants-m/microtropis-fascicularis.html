Microtropis fascicularis

Microtropis fascicularis, commonly known as the clustered microtropis, is a tropical climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
