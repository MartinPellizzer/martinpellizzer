Myrtus minima

Myrtus minima, commonly known as dwarf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, dark green leaves that are typically small and leathery. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Myrtus minima is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
