Malva rotundifolia var. crispa

Malva rotundifolia var. crispa, commonly known as the crinkled mallows, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, crinkled, velvety leaves that are rounded and have a wavy margin. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
