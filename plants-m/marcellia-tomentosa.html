Marcellia tomentosa

Marcellia tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly marcellia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its soft, woolly foliage and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and covered in fine, white hairs.

Marcellia tomentosa is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in humid, nutrient-rich soil.
