Musa tomentosa

Musa tomentosa, commonly known as the velvet banana, is a species of banana plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish leaves and small, inedible berries. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are clustered in a panicle, and the fruit is usually not cultivated for human consumption.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.
