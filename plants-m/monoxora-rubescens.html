Monoxora rubescens

Monoxora rubescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, fibrous stems that turn reddish in color as the plant matures. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight sheen.

Monoxora rubescens is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in regions with limited rainfall.
