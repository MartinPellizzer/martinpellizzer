Mimosa guanacastensis

Mimosa guanacastensis, commonly known as the Guanacaste mimosa, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different environments. Its seed pods split open to release seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Mimosa guanacastensis is often found in disturbed areas and can play a role in soil stabilization.
