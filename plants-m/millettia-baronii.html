Millettia baronii

Millettia baronii, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a woody climbing vine native to East Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its long, trailing stems and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can grow quite vigorously if not properly managed.

Its leaves are compound, typically with three to five leaflets, and it thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments.
