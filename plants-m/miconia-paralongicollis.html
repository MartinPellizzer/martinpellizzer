Miconia paralongicollis

Miconia paralongicollis, commonly known as the long-necked miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Neotropical region.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by distinctive, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
