Malus sylvestris var. bulgarica

Malus sylvestris var. bulgarica, commonly known as the Bulgarian wild apple, is a variant of the common crabapple.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Balkan region, characterized by its ornamental flowers and edible fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have serrated edges, while its fruit is a small, yellow or red apple-like berry.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional medicine and local cuisine.
