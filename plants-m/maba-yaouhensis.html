Maba yaouhensis

Maba yaouhensis, commonly known as the Yaouhensis maba, is a tropical tree native to western Africa.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
