Matisia racemifera

Matisia racemifera, commonly known as the red cedar, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, followed by dark, fleshy berries. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and fragrance, often used in traditional crafts and construction.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
