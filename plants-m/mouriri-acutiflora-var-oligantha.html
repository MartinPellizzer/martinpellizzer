Mouriri acutiflora var. oligantha

Mouriri acutiflora var. oligantha, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its small, star-shaped fruits that resemble tiny pineapples, with a sweet and tangy flavor. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits.

The variety oligantha is distinguished by its compact growth habit and smaller fruit size compared to the typical acutiflora.
