Miconia desportesii

Miconia desportesii, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by bright orange, star-shaped fruits. Miconia desportesii is known for its rapid growth and is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its striking appearance.

However, it can become invasive in some regions if not managed properly.
