Mentha pseudagrestis

Mentha pseudagrestis, commonly known as field mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, making it useful in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
