Mentha longifolia subsp. noeana

Mentha longifolia subsp.

noeana, commonly known as the Greek mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other mints in the Mentha genus.
