Macrolobium sphaerocarpum

Macrolobium sphaerocarpum, commonly known as the globe-fruit tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by distinctive, spherical fruits. The fruits are typically green when immature and turn yellow or red when mature.

It is often grown for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
