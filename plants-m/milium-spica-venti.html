Milium spica-venti

Milium spica-venti, commonly known as the wind spike millet, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its small, wind-dispersed seeds contribute to its ability to spread across various environments.

Milium spica-venti is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of forage for livestock.
