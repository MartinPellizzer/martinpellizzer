Miconia vitiflora

Miconia vitiflora, commonly known as the purple miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often purple or reddish in color. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in certain regions.
