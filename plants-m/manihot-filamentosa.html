Manihot filamentosa

Manihot filamentosa, commonly known as the slender arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are used as a starch source. The plant features simple, alternate leaves with a smooth surface and can grow up to several meters in height. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, and its growth habit is often herbaceous.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental or for its edible roots.
