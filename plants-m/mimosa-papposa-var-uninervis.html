Mimosa papposa var. uninervis

Mimosa papposa var. uninervis, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and fluffy, pinkish-white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, hairy seeds enclosed in a pod, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as grasslands and disturbed sites.

The species is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive plant in certain regions.
