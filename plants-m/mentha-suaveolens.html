Mentha suaveolens

Mentha suaveolens, commonly known as sweet mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its minty scent and have been used in traditional medicine.

Mentha suaveolens is often cultivated for its culinary and therapeutic properties.
