Muscari dolichanthum

Muscari dolichanthum, commonly known as the long-flowered grape hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has strap-shaped, grass-like leaves and produces small, round bulbs that multiply over time. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Muscari dolichanthum is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant due to its compact size and ornamental value.
