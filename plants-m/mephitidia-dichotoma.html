Mephitidia dichotoma

The plant exhibits a dichotomous growth habit, where each stem splits into two equal branches, creating a symmetrical structure. Its leaves are small, lance-shaped, and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

The flowers are inconspicuous, and the plant relies on specific pollinators for reproduction, making it ecologically significant in its habitat.
