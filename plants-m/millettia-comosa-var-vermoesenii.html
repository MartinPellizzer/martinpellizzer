Millettia comosa var. vermoesenii

Millettia comosa var. vermoesenii, commonly known as the silky millettia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its soft, silky-haired leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces woody pods that contain several elliptical seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
