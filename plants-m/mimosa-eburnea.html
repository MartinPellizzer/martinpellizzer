Mimosa eburnea

Mimosa eburnea, commonly known as the white mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in spring. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its graceful appearance and fragrant blossoms. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Mimosa eburnea is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
