Myrtoleucodendron confertum

Myrtoleucodendron confertum, commonly known as the broad-leaved myrtle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy environments.

Myrtoleucodendron confertum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in Indigenous Australian cultures.
