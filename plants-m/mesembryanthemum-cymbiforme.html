Mesembryanthemum cymbiforme

Mesembryanthemum cymbiforme, commonly known as the ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, often blue-green leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the cooler months. Its thick, waxy leaves help reduce water loss through transpiration.

Mesembryanthemum cymbiforme is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and drought tolerance.
