Malaxis talaudensis

Malaxis talaudensis, commonly known as the Talaud Orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Talaud Islands in Indonesia.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with a slender stem and a single, lance-shaped leaf. The plant produces a single flower with a pale green or white spathe and a yellowish-tinged lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to the tropical climate of its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
