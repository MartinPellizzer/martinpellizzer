Madisonia spiculifera

Madisonia spiculifera, commonly known as the spiky madisonia, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
