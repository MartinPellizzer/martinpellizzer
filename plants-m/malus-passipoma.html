Malus passipoma

Malus passipoma, commonly known as the Japanese apple tree, is a species native to Japan and is part of the Rosaceae family.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 8 to 12 meters, featuring a rounded crown and smooth, greyish bark. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, turning yellow in the fall. Its flowers are white and appear in clusters, followed by small, red, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to apples.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
