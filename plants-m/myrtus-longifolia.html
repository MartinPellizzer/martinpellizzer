Myrtus longifolia

Myrtus longifolia, commonly known as sweet myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features fragrant, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb. The plant produces small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark blue, edible berries. Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for hedges and garden planting.

Myrtus longifolia is also valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and aromatherapy.
