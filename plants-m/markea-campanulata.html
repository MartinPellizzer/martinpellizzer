Markea campanulata

Markea campanulata, commonly known as the bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, tubular flowers that typically range in color from blue to purple. The plant is native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Markea campanulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
