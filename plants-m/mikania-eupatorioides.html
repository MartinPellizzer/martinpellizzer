Mikania eupatorioides

Mikania eupatorioides, commonly known as creeping morning glory, is a vigorous vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, trumpet-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb or spread along the ground. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is notable for its showy inflorescences.
