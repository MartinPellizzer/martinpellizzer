Mahonia leptodonta

Mahonia leptodonta, commonly known as the western mahonia, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features compound leaves with elliptical leaflets that are dark green and glossy on top. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are followed by blue-black berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its evergreen foliage and seasonal floral display.
