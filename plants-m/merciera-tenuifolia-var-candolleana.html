Merciera tenuifolia var. candolleana

Merciera tenuifolia var. candolleana, commonly known as the Candolleana mercier.

It is a shrub native to the Andes, characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude regions. Its foliage is typically dense and evergreen, providing ornamental value in gardens.

The species is noted for its adaptability to rocky soils and partial shade.
