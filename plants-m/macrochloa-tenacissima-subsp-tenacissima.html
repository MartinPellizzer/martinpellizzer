Macrochloa tenacissima subsp. tenacissima

Macrochloa tenacissima subsp.

tenacissima, commonly known as the desert grass or desert needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland ecosystems.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays a significant role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in harsh environments.
