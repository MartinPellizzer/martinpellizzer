Moschosma myriostachyum

Moschosma myriostachyum, commonly known as the broadleaf flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets, and its growth habit is typically shrub-like or small tree-like. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds.
