Medinilla baronii

Medinilla baronii, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or velvet plant, is a flowering shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a tangy, citrus-like flavor.

Medinilla baronii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
