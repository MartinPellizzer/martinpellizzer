Mimetophytum crassifolium

Mimetophytum crassifolium, commonly known as the thick-leaved mimic plant, is a succulent species native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the rainy season, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss through transpiration.

Mimetophytum crassifolium is a hardy, low-maintenance plant that is popular in xeriscaping and rock gardens.
