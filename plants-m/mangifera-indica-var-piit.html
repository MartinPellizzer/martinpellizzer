Mangifera indica var. piit

Mangifera indica var. piit, commonly known as the pineapple mango, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, pointed fruit with a distinct pineapple-like appearance, hence its common name. The plant produces sweet, aromatic flesh that is typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil and requires ample sunlight for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and is often cultivated for both commercial and ornamental purposes.
