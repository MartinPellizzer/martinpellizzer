Mentha daghestanica

Mentha daghestanica, commonly known as the Daghestan mint, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Mentha daghestanica thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
