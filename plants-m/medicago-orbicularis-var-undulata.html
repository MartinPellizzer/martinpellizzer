Medicago orbicularis var. undulata

Medicago orbicularis var. undulata, commonly known as the wavy globeflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its rounded, fleshy leaves that are often undulating in shape, giving it its specific epithet "undulata.". The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
