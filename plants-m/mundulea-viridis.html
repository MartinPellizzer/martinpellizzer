Mundulea viridis

Mundulea viridis, commonly known as the green mundulea, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woody growth and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of wax. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, which are typically visited by a variety of pollinators. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to survive in nutrient-poor and drought-prone soils.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
