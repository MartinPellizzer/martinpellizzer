Masdevallia andreettana

Masdevallia andreettana, commonly known as the Andean masdevallia, is a rare orchid species native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often brightly colored flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically produces a single flower per inflorescence, which blooms during the cooler months. Its leaves are usually dark green and arranged in a rosette, adapting to the humid, misty conditions of its habitat.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
