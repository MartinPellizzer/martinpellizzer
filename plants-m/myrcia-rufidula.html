Myrcia rufidula

Myrcia rufidula, commonly known as the red-stemmed myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its dark green, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark is reddish-brown and peels in thin layers, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant is often found in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
