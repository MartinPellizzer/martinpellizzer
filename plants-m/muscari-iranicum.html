Muscari iranicum

Muscari iranicum, commonly known as the Persian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of bell-shaped, bluish-purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves that die back after flowering. Muscari iranicum is adapted to cold climates and thrives in well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and can naturalize easily in suitable conditions.
