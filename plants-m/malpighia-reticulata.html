Malpighia reticulata

Malpighia reticulata, commonly known as the granadilla, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, red-orange flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically yellow or red when ripe. These berries are rich in vitamin C and have a tart, acidic flavor.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and as a source of edible fruit.
