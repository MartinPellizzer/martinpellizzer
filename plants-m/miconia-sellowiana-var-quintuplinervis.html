Miconia sellowiana var. quintuplinervis

Miconia sellowiana var. quintuplinervis, commonly known as the five-veined miconia, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves with five prominent veins, giving it its specific epithet. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains numerous small seeds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in warm climates, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
