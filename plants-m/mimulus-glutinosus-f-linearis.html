Mimulus glutinosus f. linearis

Mimulus glutinosus f. linearis, commonly known as the sticky monkeyflower or linear monkeyflower, is a variant of the sticky monkeyflower native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and bright orange or yellow tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows. It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in partial to full sun.

Its unique floral structure attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
