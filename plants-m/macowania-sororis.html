Macowania sororis

Macowania sororis, commonly known as the common sorrel, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the family Oxalidaceae.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins and bright red flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible leaves that are often used in salads, though they contain oxalic acid and should be consumed in moderation. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

Macowania sororis is also known for its ability to thrive in partial shade and its historical use in traditional medicine.
