Mentha laxa

Mentha laxa, commonly known as loose mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and have a distinct minty fragrance.

Mentha laxa produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months.
