Mollinedia simulans

Mollinedia simulans, commonly known as the false hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features a rosette of dark green, waxy leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive basal arrangement. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale yellow, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Mollinedia simulans is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
