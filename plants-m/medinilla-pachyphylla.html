Medinilla pachyphylla

Medinilla pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved medinilla, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to its humid, tropical environment. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, often clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in shaded, moist conditions and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses around the world.
