Mimosa foliolosa var. antana

Mimosa foliolosa var. antana, commonly known as the antelope's horn mimosa, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in spring and summer. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, feather-like leaflets.

This species is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and resilience to poor soil conditions.
