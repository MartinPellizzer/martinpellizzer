Myricaria bracteata

Myricaria bracteata, commonly known as the bracted myricaria, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a waxy cuticle, helping to reduce water loss in dry conditions.

Myricaria bracteata is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
