Malabaila pimpinellifolia

Malabaila pimpinellifolia, commonly known as the common tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, fern-like leaves and yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream sides. The plant contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans if ingested.

Its historical use in herbal medicine and as a pesticide highlights its complex botanical and ecological significance.
