Melanorrhoea speciosa

Melanorrhoea speciosa, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking yellow and green floral structures. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that bloom during the wet season, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Melanorrhoea speciosa thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
