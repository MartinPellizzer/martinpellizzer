Mimosa balduinii

Mimosa balduinii, commonly known as the golden mimosas, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellow, fluffy flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, thread-like leaflets.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
