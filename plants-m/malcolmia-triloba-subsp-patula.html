Malcolmia triloba subsp. patula

Malcolmia triloba subsp.

patula, commonly known as the Persian crucible, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa. It is a low-growing annual herb with hairy stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
