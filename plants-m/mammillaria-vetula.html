Mammillaria vetula

Mammillaria vetula, commonly known as the Mexican snowball cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, white to pale green spines and spherical shape. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments and has a slow growth rate. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, appearing in clusters at the top of the plant.

Mammillaria vetula is well-suited for cultivation in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
