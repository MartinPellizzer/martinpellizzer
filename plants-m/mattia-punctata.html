Mattia punctata

Mattia punctata, commonly known as the spotted bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, showy flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Mattia punctata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
