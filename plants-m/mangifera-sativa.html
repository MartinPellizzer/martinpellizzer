Mangifera sativa

Mangifera sativa, commonly known as the mango tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces large, fuzzy fruits with a sweet, aromatic pulp. The plant belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is valued for its edible fruit, which is rich in vitamins and minerals. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall and has a long lifespan, often living for over 100 years.

Its flowers are small and fragrant, typically appearing in clusters, and it relies on pollination by insects and wind.
