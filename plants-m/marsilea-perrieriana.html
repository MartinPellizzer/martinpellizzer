Marsilea perrieriana

Marsilea perrieriana, commonly known as the water clover, is a small aquatic fern belonging to the family Marsileaceae.

It is characterized by its four-leaf clover-like appearance, with each leaflet being oval and slightly pointed. The plant typically grows in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and wetlands. Its rhizomes are submerged, anchoring the plant while its floating leaves capture sunlight for photosynthesis.

Marsilea perrieriana plays a role in water filtration and provides habitat for small aquatic organisms.
