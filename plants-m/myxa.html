Myxa

Myxa, commonly known as the slime mold, is a unique organism that blurs the line between plant and fungus.

It belongs to the kingdom Protista and is not classified as a true plant, although it can form gelatinous structures similar to those of some algae. Myxa thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found on decaying wood or soil surfaces. It reproduces through spores, which are released into the air when the organism dries out.

Despite its simple structure, myxa plays an important role in nutrient cycling in ecosystems.
