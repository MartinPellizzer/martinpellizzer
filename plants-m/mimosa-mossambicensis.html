Mimosa mossambicensis

Mimosa mossambicensis, commonly known as the African mallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Mimosa mossambicensis is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
