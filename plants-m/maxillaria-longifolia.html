Maxillaria longifolia

Maxillaria longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendant-like flowers that are typically white to pale pink in color. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Maxillaria longifolia thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
