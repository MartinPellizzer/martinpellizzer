Maxillaria bolleoides

Maxillaria bolleoides, commonly known as the Bolle's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial herb with a pseudobulb and linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple, with a distinctive lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Maxillaria bolleoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
