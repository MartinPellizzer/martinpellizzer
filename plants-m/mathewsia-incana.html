Mathewsia incana

Mathewsia incana, commonly known as the silky broom.

It is a shrub native to South Africa, characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, pale green leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white, fluffy flowers that bloom in spring. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Mathewsia incana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
