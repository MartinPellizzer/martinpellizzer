Machaonia floribunda

Machaonia floribunda, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub that typically grows in forested areas, often in damp, shaded environments. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, blooming in spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a distinctive waxy coating.

Machaonia floribunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
