Mecranium amygdalinum var. integrifolium

Mecranium amygdalinum var. integrifolium, commonly known as the smooth-leafed almond tree, is a variant of the almond plant characterized by its entire, smooth-edged leaves.

It is native to temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils with ample sunlight. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by the development of almond-like fruits. Its leaves are typically oval in shape and have a slightly glossy surface.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential use in traditional herbal medicine.
