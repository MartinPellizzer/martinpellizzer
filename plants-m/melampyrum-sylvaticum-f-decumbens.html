Melampyrum sylvaticum f. decumbens

Melampyrum sylvaticum f. decumbens, commonly known as the prostrate yellow cow-wheat, is a low-growing herbaceous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its prostrate or decumbent growth habit, with stems that lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typical of the genus Melampyrum. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval to lanceolate leaflets.

This variant is often found in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
