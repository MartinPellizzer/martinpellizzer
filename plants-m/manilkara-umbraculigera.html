Manilkara umbraculigera

Manilkara umbraculigera, commonly known as the wild sapote, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and produces a fruit with a thick, inedible outer pulp surrounding a sweet, creamy white flesh. The plant belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is valued for its latex, which has industrial applications. Its growth habit includes a straight trunk and dense canopy, making it a significant component of tropical rainforests.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
