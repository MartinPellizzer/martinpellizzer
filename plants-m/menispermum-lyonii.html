Menispermum lyonii

Menispermum lyonii, commonly known as Lyon's false virginea, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its palmately compound leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiny fruits that contain flat, winged seeds. Its botanical features include a vigorous growth habit and the ability to climb using its tendrils.

It is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
