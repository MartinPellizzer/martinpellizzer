Marcetia caespitosa

Marcetia caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted grass.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa, characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in compact clusters. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often green or pale yellow, arranged in spikelets.

Marcetia caespitosa is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open habitats.
