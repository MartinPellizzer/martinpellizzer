Myrodia trinervia

Myrodia trinervia, commonly known as the silverleaf myrtle, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves, which are covered in fine, soft hairs, providing it with a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Myrodia trinervia is well adapted to dry climates, exhibiting drought tolerance and a slow growth rate.

It is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in native habitats.
