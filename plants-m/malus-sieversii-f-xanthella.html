Malus sieversii f. xanthella

Malus sieversii f. xanthella, commonly known as the yellow-flowered crabapple, is a rare and distinctive variant of the crabapple species.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which contrast sharply with its green foliage. This form is native to Central Asia and is considered a botanical curiosity due to its unique coloration. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

Its flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
