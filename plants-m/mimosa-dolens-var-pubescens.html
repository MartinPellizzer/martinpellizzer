Mimosa dolens var. pubescens

Mimosa dolens var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its stems and leaves are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.
