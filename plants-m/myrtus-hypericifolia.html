Myrtus hypericifolia

Myrtus hypericifolia, commonly known as sweet myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves with a distinctive sweet scent, often used in herbal remedies. The plant produces tiny, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees. Its fruit is a small, dark blue berry that ripens in late summer.

Myrtus hypericifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery and medicine.
