Melanthesa cernua var. acutifolia

Melanthesa cernua var. acutifolia, commonly known as the acuteleaf cow parsley, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe.

It features slender, branching stems that often lie prostrate or are slightly ascending. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in early to mid-summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive acute tip, giving it its specific epithet.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks.
