Messerschmidia incana

Messerschmidia incana, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It is a perennial species with a low-growing habit, often forming dense mats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
