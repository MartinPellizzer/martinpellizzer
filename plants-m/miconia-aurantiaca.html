Miconia aurantiaca

Miconia aurantiaca, commonly known as the red miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a glossy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, bright orange flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release numerous tiny seeds.

Miconia aurantiaca is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and vibrant floral display.
