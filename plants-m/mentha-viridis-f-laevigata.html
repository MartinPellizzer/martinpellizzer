Mentha viridis f. laevigata

Mentha viridis f. laevigata, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed green mint, is a variant of the green mint plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a bright green color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
