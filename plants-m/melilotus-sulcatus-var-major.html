Melilotus sulcatus var. major

Melilotus sulcatus var. major, commonly known as the greater sweet clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spikes. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas. It is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.

Its seeds are also used as a forage crop for livestock.
