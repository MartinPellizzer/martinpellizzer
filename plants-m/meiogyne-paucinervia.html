Meiogyne paucinervia

Meiogyne paucinervia, commonly known as the narrow-veined cycad, is a unique member of the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched stem and a crown of feathery, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female plants. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a distinct, almost feather-like appearance.

Meiogyne paucinervia is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is adapted to grow in tropical and subtropical climates.
