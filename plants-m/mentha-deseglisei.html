Mentha deseglisei

Mentha deseglisei, commonly known as the Corsican mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, minty aroma.

Mentha deseglisei is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies.
