Mentha longifolia var. gulekensis

Mentha longifolia var. gulekensis, commonly known as the Gulekensis mint, is a variant of the long-leaf mint native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly minty scent and serrated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its flowering stems bear small, tubular flowers that are often pale blue or purple in color.

This variety is valued for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent in local cuisines.
