Myrcia ambigua var. rostrata

Myrcia ambigua var. rostrata, commonly known as the rostrated myrtle, is a shrub native to the rainforests of northern South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
