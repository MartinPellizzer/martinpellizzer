Mapania lactea

Mapania lactea, commonly known as the milkweed plant, is a species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its milky sap, which is a distinguishing feature of the genus. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a rough texture, while the flowers are clustered in umbels and often have a yellowish or greenish hue.

Mapania lactea plays a role in local ecosystems and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
