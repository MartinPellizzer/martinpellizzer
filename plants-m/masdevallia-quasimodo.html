Masdevallia quasimodo

Masdevallia quasimodo, commonly known as the "Crying Angel," is a rare and striking orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its pendulous, greenish-white flowers with intricate patterns resembling tears, giving it its evocative common name. The plant typically grows as a miniature epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it thrives in cool, humid conditions with indirect light.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
