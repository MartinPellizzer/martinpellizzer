Myosotis aequinoctialis

Myosotis aequinoctialis, commonly known as the spring gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Myosotis aequinoctialis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional medicine.
