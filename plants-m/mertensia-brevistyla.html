Mertensia brevistyla

Mertensia brevistyla, commonly known as the short-stemmed bluebells, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Mertensia brevistyla is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
