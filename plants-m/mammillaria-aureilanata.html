Mammillaria aureilanata

Mammillaria aureilanata, commonly known as the golden mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, white woolly coating and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in small, rounded clusters with short, cylindrical stems. Its flowers are often borne in the axils of the spines and can be a striking shade of yellow or orange.

This species is well-suited for rock gardens and requires well-draining soil and full sun.
