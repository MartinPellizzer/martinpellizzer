Monorchis jaffreyana

Monorchis jaffreyana, commonly known as the Jaffrey's orchid, is a rare and distinctive orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Pakistan.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a single, showy orchid flower with intricate patterns on its petals and sepals. The plant typically grows in rocky and well-drained soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool and humid conditions. Its flowers bloom in late spring or early summer, adding a striking visual element to its natural habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Monorchis jaffreyana is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in preserving its ecological niche.
