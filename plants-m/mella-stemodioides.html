Mella stemodioides

Mella stemodioides, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features slender, woody stems that twine around supporting structures as it grows. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a glossy green surface. Its most striking feature is the clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
