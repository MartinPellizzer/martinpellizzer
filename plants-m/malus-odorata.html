Malus odorata

Malus odorata, commonly known as the fragrant apple tree, is a species native to China.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy white flowers. The tree produces small, apple-like fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant blossoms and attractive autumn foliage.

The plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and historical significance in traditional Chinese medicine.
