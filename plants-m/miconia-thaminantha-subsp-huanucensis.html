Miconia thaminantha subsp. huanucensis

Miconia thaminantha subsp.

huanucensis, commonly known as the Huanuco miconia, is a tropical shrub native to the cloud forests of Peru. It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves with a glossy surface.

The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences with white to pink flowers that attract various pollinators. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Due to its restricted habitat, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
