Misodendrum linearifolium

Misodendrum linearifolium, commonly known as the slender-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It has small, elongated, and often waxy leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its berries are usually red or orange and contain toxic seeds.

It is found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its host trees.
