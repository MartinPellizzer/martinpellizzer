Mendoncella fimbriata

Mendoncella fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

The showy petals are adorned with fringed edges, which give the species its common name.
