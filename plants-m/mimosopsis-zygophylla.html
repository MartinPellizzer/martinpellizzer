Mimosopsis zygophylla

Mimosopsis zygophylla, commonly known as the desert mimosa, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring small, feathery leaves that help reduce water loss in hot climates. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots that allow it to access groundwater.

Mimosopsis zygophylla plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
