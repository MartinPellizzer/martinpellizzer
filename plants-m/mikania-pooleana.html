Mikania pooleana

Mikania pooleana, commonly known as the Brazilian milkweed, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fibrous roots and stems are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Mikania pooleana is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climatic conditions.
