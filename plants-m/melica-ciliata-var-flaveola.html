Melica ciliata var. flaveola

Melica ciliata var. flaveola, commonly known as the yellow beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive hairy margin. The inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of tiny, yellowish-green florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, riverbanks, and disturbed sites.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
