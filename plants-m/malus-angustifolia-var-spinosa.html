Malus angustifolia var. spinosa

Malus angustifolia var. spinosa, commonly known as the spiny crabapple, is a variant of the crabapple tree characterized by its thorny branches and small, ornamental fruits.

It is native to regions of central and southern Europe, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant features serrated, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding seasonal interest to its appearance. Its flowers, which bloom in spring, are typically white or pink and are followed by small, edible fruits.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
