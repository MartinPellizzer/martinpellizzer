Mangifera indica var. kalapa

Mangifera indica var. kalapa, commonly known as the Kalapa mango, is a tropical fruit tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elongated mango fruits with a thin, smooth skin that turns yellow when ripe. The flesh is typically sweet and fibrous, with a high sugar content and a mild aroma. This variety is valued for its flavor and is often used in both fresh consumption and culinary applications.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
