Mosla perforata

Mosla perforata, commonly known as Chinese burnet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are pinnately compound with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its fruits are small, dry, and three-lobed, typical of the Polygonaceae family.

Mosla perforata is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
