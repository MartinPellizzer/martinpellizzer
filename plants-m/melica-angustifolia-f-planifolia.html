Melica angustifolia f. planifolia

Melica angustifolia f. planifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf melica, is a variant of the narrowleaf brome.

It is a perennial grass species characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and compact inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in temperate regions. Its foliage is often described as flat and narrow, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
