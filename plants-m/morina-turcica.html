Morina turcica

Morina turcica, commonly known as Turkish moringa, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is adapted to dry and rocky environments, making it a resilient species.

Its botanical characteristics make it suitable for use in traditional medicine and as a source of edible seeds.
