Mesembryanthemum hexamerum

Mesembryanthemum hexamerum, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or golden gooseberry, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, often hairy leaves and showy, star-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, orange to yellow fruits that resemble tiny tomatoes, which are edible and have a tangy flavor. It thrives in arid environments and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant appearance and drought tolerance.

Mesembryanthemum hexamerum is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
