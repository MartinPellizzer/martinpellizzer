Miconia eriocalyx

Miconia eriocalyx, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by orange to red berries. Miconia eriocalyx is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of tropical and subtropical climates.

It is also noted for its ecological impact, often becoming invasive in non-native regions.
