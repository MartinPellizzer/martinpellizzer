Myrceugenia myrtoides var. sellowiana

Myrceugenia myrtoides var. sellowiana, commonly known as the Sellow's myrtle, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant features lance-shaped, glossy dark green leaves with a fragrant aroma. Its small, white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisine.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
