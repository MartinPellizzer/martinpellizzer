Macrocalyx bipinnatifidus

Macrocalyx bipinnatifidus, commonly known as the broadleaf piper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and pinnately divided. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers with five petals. Its botanical features include a woody stem and hairy foliage, which help it adapt to humid environments.

It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forest understories.
