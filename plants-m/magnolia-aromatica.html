Magnolia aromatica

Magnolia aromatica, commonly known as the sweet magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are broad, ovate, and have a smooth texture, turning yellow in the fall. The tree typically grows to a height of 15 to 30 feet and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
