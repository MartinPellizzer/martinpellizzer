Myrcia maxima

Myrcia maxima, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some medicinal uses in traditional medicine.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
