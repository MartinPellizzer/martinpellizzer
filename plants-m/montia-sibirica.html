Montia sibirica

Montia sibirica, commonly known as Siberian watercress, is a small flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Montiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and small white or pink flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery flavor, making it a traditional vegetable in some Asian cuisines.

Montia sibirica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and culinary uses.
