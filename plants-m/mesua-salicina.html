Mesua salicina

Mesua salicina, commonly known as the Indian tulip or sacred tulip, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub with smooth, greyish bark and fragrant, showy flowers. The flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals and a distinctive, narrow shape. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
