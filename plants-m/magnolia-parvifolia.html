Magnolia parvifolia

Magnolia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved magnolia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is known for its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its ornamental value comes from its showy flowers and attractive foliage, making it a popular choice for gardens.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
