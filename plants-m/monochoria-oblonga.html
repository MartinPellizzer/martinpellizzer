Monochoria oblonga

Monochoria oblonga, commonly known as narrowleaf watergrass, is a semi-aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has elongated, narrow leaves that are typically submerged or floating, adapted for aquatic environments. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in spikelets. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of slow-moving water bodies.

Monochoria oblonga plays a role in stabilizing aquatic sediments and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
