Misbrookea strigosissima

Misbrookea strigosissima, commonly known as the hairy brookea, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stem and large, paddle-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces bright orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
