Mammillaria rhodantha var. andreae

Mammillaria rhodantha var. andreae, commonly known as the Andean pink ball cactus, is a compact, globular cactus native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It features a dense covering of white to pale pink spines, which are often shorter and more numerous than in other varieties of Mammillaria. The plant produces vibrant pink or red flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.

Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a desirable addition to rock gardens and indoor collections.
