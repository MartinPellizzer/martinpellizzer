Meisneria paludosa

Meisneria paludosa, commonly known as the marsh meisneria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in swampy areas and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, often blooming during the rainy season. Meisneria paludosa is adapted to waterlogged soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native habitat's biodiversity.
