Melaleuca pilosa var. calyptra

Melaleuca pilosa var. calyptra, commonly known as the broad-leaved paperbark, is a shrub or small tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, flaky bark that peels in strips, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bottle-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often covered in fine, soft hairs.

This variety is adapted to coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.
