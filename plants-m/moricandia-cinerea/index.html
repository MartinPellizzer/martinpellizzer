Moricandia Cinerea

Moricandia cinerea is a hardy annual plant known for its distinctive silver-gray foliage and small, pale flowers. Native to regions of Europe and Asia, it thrives in dry, rocky environments and is often found in disturbed soils. This plant is also called the "silverweed" and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits. Its resilience makes it a common sight in arid landscapes and garden settings. Despite its modest appearance, Moricandia cinerea plays an important role in supporting local wildlife and ecological balance.
