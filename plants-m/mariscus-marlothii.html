Mariscus marlothii

Mariscus marlothii, commonly known as the Cape mariscus, is a rare and endemic shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, urn-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
