Modecca hederifolia

Modecca hederifolia, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to climb or trail along surfaces.

Modecca hederifolia is also valued for its air-purifying qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
