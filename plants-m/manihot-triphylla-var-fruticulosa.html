Manihot triphylla var. fruticulosa

Manihot triphylla var. fruticulosa, commonly known as the three-leaf arrowroot or shrub arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically heart-shaped and have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its starchy roots, which are used in food preparation.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for gardeners in warm climates.
