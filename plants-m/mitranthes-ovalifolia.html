Mitranthes ovalifolia

Mitranthes ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaved bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features basal rosettes of ovate, glaucous leaves and produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained soils.

Mitranthes ovalifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
