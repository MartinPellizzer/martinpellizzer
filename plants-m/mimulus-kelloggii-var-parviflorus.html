Mimulus kelloggii var. parviflorus

Mimulus kelloggii var. parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered monkeyflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features delicate, spurred flowers that are typically orange or yellow in color, with a distinctive hood-like structure. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in streambanks, meadows, and woodland edges, where it contributes to local biodiversity.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
