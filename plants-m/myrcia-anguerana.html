Myrcia anguerana

Myrcia anguerana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.

The plant's aromatic properties have also led to its use in traditional medicine.
