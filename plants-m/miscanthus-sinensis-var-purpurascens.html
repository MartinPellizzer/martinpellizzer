Miscanthus sinensis var. purpurascens

Miscanthus sinensis var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple moonglade grass, is a perennial ornamental grass native to East Asia.

It features tall, arching clumps of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that turn a striking reddish-purple in the fall. The plant produces fluffy, silver-gray plumes during the late summer to early autumn, adding visual interest to gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its elegant appearance and low maintenance requirements.
