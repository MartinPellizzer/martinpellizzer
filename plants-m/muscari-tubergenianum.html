Muscari tubergenianum

Muscari tubergenianum, commonly known as the Persian bluebell, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces clusters of striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for adding color to early spring landscapes.
