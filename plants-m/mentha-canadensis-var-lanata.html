Mentha canadensis var. lanata

Mentha canadensis var. lanata, commonly known as woolly mint, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, making it a valuable plant in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
