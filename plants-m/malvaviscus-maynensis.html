Malvaviscus maynensis

Malvaviscus maynensis, commonly known as the Mayan mallow, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
