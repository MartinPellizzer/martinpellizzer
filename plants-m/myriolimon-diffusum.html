Myriolimon diffusum

Myriolimon diffusum, commonly known as the spreading myriolimon, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, temperate environments, often found in meadows and along stream banks. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that contribute to its ornamental value in gardens. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Myriolimon diffusum is valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity and its potential use in traditional herbal medicine.
