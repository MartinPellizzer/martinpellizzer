Medicago polycarpa

Medicago polycarpa, commonly known as the many-fruited clover, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces multiple pods containing small, round seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind. Medicago polycarpa is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it valuable in ecological and agricultural contexts.
