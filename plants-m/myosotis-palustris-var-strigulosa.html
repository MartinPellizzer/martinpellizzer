Myosotis palustris var. strigulosa

Myosotis palustris var. strigulosa, commonly known as the scaly water violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, violet-blue flowers with five petals. The leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a distinctive scaly texture on their upper surface. It thrives in moist to aquatic environments, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting wetland biodiversity.
