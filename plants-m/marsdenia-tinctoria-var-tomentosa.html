Marsdenia tinctoria var. tomentosa

Marsdenia tinctoria var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly Marsdenia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, or tomentose, stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments.
