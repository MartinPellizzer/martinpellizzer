Mystacidium arachnanthus

Mystacidium arachnanthus, commonly known as the spider-tail fern, is a unique epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, branching, spider-like fronds that give it its common name. The plant's leaves are long, narrow, and often have a waxy texture, which helps reduce water loss in its humid habitat. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks.

Mystacidium arachnanthus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
