Metalasia hispida

Metalasia hispida, commonly known as the rough metalasia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Metalasia hispida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
