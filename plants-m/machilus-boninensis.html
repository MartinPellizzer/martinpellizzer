Machilus boninensis

Machilus boninensis, commonly known as the Bonin mallow, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the Ryukyu Islands, including Okinawa, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a smooth surface and a strong, spicy fragrance.

Machilus boninensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
