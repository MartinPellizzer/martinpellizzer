Monelasmum sulcatum

Monelasmum sulcatum, commonly known as the warty morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, warty, and often hairy stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found growing along stream banks or in moist woodlands.

Monelasmum sulcatum is a climbing vine that can reach significant lengths, making it a notable presence in its native habitats.
