Macrochlamys patrisii

Macrochlamys patrisii, commonly known as the Patrisii's giant chlamydia, is a rare and striking member of the Chlamydiaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red and orange, attracting various pollinators. The plant has a unique morphology with elongated, spirally arranged leaves that help it thrive in shaded, humid environments. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
