Malpighia martiana

Malpighia martiana, commonly known as the granadilla, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in vitamin C and have a tart, sweet flavor. These berries are often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
