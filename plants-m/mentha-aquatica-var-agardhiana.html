Mentha aquatica var. agardhiana

Mentha aquatica var. agardhiana, commonly known as the water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It features slender, upright stems that often grow in clusters, and its leaves are typically oval to lance-shaped with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stem, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found near streams, ponds, and other water bodies.

Mentha aquatica var. agardhiana is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used historically in herbal medicine and culinary applications.
