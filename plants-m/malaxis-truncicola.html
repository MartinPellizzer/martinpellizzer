Malaxis truncicola

Malaxis truncicola, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and undergrowth. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient uptake.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
