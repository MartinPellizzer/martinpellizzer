Myrtopsis

Myrtopsis, commonly known as the bottlebrush tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree belonging to the Myrtaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cylindrical flower clusters that resemble bottle brushes, typically in shades of red or pink. The plant has lance-shaped, opposite leaves that are often aromatic and have a slightly rough texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is native to regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

Myrtopsis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
