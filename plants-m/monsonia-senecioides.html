Monsonia senecioides

Monsonia senecioides, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to violet flower heads. The plant features a tall, upright stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy blooms appear in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Monsonia senecioides is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought-resistant properties.
