Melinis rupicola

Melinis rupicola, commonly known as rock millet, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny seeds. It is adapted to grow in rocky or sandy soils and is drought-tolerant.

Melinis rupicola is often used in ornamental horticulture for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
