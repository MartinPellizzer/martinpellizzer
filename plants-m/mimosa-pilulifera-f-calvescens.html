Mimosa pilulifera f. calvescens

Mimosa pilulifera f. calvescens, commonly known as the velvetleaf mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and its distinctive, spherical seed pods that split open when mature. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive plant in certain areas.
