Macropodiella taylorii

Macropodiella taylorii, commonly known as the Taylor's spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. Native to arid and semi-arid regions, this plant has adapted to thrive in low-water environments. Its growth habit is typically prostrate or sprawling, allowing it to cover the ground and reduce water loss.

The plant contains milky sap, which is a common feature among members of the Euphorbiaceae family.
