Myrtus baladensis

Myrtus baladensis, commonly known as the Balad myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its berries, which are usually red or black, are edible and have a slightly tart flavor.

Myrtus baladensis is often used in traditional medicine and for its ornamental value in gardens.
