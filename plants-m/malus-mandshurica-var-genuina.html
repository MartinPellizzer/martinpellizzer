Malus mandshurica var. genuina

Malus mandshurica var. genuina, commonly known as the Manchurian apple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its white flowers and edible, tart fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for its fruit in certain regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
