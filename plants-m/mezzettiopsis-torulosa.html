Mezzettiopsis torulosa

Mezzettiopsis torulosa, commonly known as the yellow star orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and showy, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky habitats, thriving in cool and humid conditions. Its flowers are clustered in a star-like arrangement, giving it its common name, and it blooms during the spring and early summer.

Mezzettiopsis torulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
