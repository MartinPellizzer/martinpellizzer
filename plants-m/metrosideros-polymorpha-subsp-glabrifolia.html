Metrosideros polymorpha subsp. glabrifolia

Metrosideros polymorpha subsp.

glabrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved mistletoe or ʻōhiʻa lehua, is a native Hawaiian plant belonging to the Myrtaceae family. It is characterized by its small, smooth, glabrous leaves and clusters of red or yellow flowers.

The subspecies is typically found growing on the trunks of other trees, forming a hemiparasitic relationship. It plays an important ecological role in Hawaiian forests, providing nectar for native birds and insects.

Its flowers are also culturally significant to the indigenous Hawaiian people.
