Marsdenia picta

Marsdenia picta, commonly known as the picta Marsdenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange with a distinctive corona. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in shaded forest environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
