Manulea virgata

Manulea virgata, commonly known as the silverleaf manulea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in shrubland and woodland habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
