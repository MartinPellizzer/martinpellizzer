Miconia ligustroides

Miconia ligustroides, commonly known as the Brazilian glorybower, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are dispersed by birds, contributing to its ecological role in forest ecosystems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in warm climates, though it can be invasive in some regions.
