Marah macrocarpa

Marah macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant castor bean plant, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America and is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers. The plant produces large, toxic seeds that contain high levels of ricin, making them highly poisonous. Its leaves are palmately lobed and can grow up to 30 centimeters in length.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value despite its hazardous properties.
