Mesembryanthemum intransparens

Mesembryanthemum intransparens, commonly known as the transparent ice plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves and small, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to conserve water. Its translucent leaf surfaces reflect light, giving it a unique appearance.

Mesembryanthemum intransparens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking visual appeal and resilience.
