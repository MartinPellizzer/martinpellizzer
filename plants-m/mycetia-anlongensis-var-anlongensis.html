Mycetia anlongensis var. anlongensis

Mycetia anlongensis var. anlongensis, commonly known as the Anlong banyan, is a unique variant of the Mycetia genus native to China.

It is characterized by its large, spreading canopy and fibrous roots that can form extensive root systems. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ecological significance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
