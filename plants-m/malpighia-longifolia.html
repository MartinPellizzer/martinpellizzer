Malpighia longifolia

Malpighia longifolia, commonly known as the purple beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, violet-blue berries that are both ornamental and edible. The plant features compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, white flowers during the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

The berries are often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to their high vitamin C content.
