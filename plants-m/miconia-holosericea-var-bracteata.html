Miconia holosericea var. bracteata

Miconia holosericea var. bracteata, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers, which are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed habitats make it an invasive species in some regions.

The botanical features of this plant make it both ecologically significant and challenging to manage in natural ecosystems.
