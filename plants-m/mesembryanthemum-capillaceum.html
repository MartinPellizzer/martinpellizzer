Mesembryanthemum capillaceum

Mesembryanthemum capillaceum, commonly known as the "Pincushion Flower," is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its fleshy stems and leaves store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.

Mesembryanthemum capillaceum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
