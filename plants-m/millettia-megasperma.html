Millettia megasperma

Millettia megasperma, commonly known as the Brazilian white bean, is a leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces pods containing small, white seeds. The plant is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring well-drained soil and full sun.

Millettia megasperma also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting soil fertility in agricultural settings.
