Mentha pulegium f. macrostylos

Mentha pulegium f. macrostylos, commonly known as large-styled pennyroyal, is a variant of the pennyroyal plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, square stems and opposite leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late summer. Its essential oils contain pulegone, a compound known for its strong scent and medicinal properties.

This subspecies is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance and historical use in herbal medicine.
