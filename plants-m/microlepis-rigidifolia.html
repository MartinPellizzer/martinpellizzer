Microlepis rigidifolia

Microlepis rigidifolia, commonly known as the rigid-leaved microlepis, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically rigid and upright. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are often brightly colored, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments in its natural habitat.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and specialty plant collections.
