Myogalum prasandrum

Myogalum prasandrum, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
