Myrcia androsaemoides var. parvifolia

Myrcia androsaemoides var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved myrcia, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves that are typically smaller than those of the typical variety. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers, which are often fragrant. Its fruits are small, woody berries that turn red when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
