Mutisia ledifolia f. longiflora

Mutisia ledifolia f. longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy inflorescences. The plant features robust, hairy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its most distinctive trait is the elongated, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of yellow and orange.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
