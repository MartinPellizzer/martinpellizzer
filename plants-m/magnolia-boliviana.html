Magnolia boliviana

Magnolia boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the Andes mountains of Bolivia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its bark is smooth and gray, and it can grow up to 15 meters tall.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
