Massonia concinna

Massonia concinna, commonly known as the Cape bell or red bell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, commonly referred to as the ice plant family. The plant is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from pink to red. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Massonia concinna is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
