Moraea inyangani

Moraea inyangani, commonly known as the Natal star, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, star-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with contrasting purple or brown markings. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces erect, slender stems.

Moraea inyangani is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
