Mentha × verticillata f. oenoidea

Mentha × verticillata f. oenoidea, commonly known as the grape hyacinth mint, is a hybrid perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The plant has aromatic, serrated leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
