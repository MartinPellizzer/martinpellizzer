Macrothelypteris procera

Macrothelypteris procera, commonly known as the mountain huperzia, is a large, evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, upright fronds that can grow up to two meters in height. The plant has a thick, rhizomatous root system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are bipinnate, with a distinctive, coarse texture and a dark green color.

It is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
