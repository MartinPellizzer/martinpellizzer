Mitrocereus fulviceps

Mitrocereus fulviceps, commonly known as the yellow-hair cactus, is a species of cactus native to northern South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its spines are relatively short and often covered in a fine, yellowish coating.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.
