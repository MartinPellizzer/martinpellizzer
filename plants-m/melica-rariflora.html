Melica rariflora

Melica rariflora, commonly known as the rare floret grass, is a perennial grass species native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Melica rariflora is noted for its low-growing form and is often found in association with other shade-tolerant flora.
