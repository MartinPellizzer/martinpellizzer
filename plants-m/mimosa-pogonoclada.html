Mimosa pogonoclada

Mimosa pogonoclada, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery leaves and small, yellow, pod-like fruits. The plant produces fluffy, white, spherical flower heads that resemble clouds, which are a distinctive feature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas, making it both a valuable forage plant and a potential weed.
