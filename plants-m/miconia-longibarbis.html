Miconia longibarbis

Miconia longibarbis, commonly known as the long-barbed miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers, which are followed by distinctive, egg-shaped fruits. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Miconia longibarbis is also notable for its role in ecological systems, providing habitat and food for various species.
