Mimosa hilariana

Mimosa hilariana, commonly known as the joyful mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in response to touch or light. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its sensitivity to environmental changes, it is sometimes used as an indicator plant for soil health.
