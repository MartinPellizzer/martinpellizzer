Mentha longifolia f. pahinensis

Mentha longifolia f. pahinensis, commonly known as the Pahinensis mint, is a variant of the longleaf mint species.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly minty scent and serrated leaves. This subspecies typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually white or pale purple.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential antiseptic and analgesic properties.
