Magnolia angustioblonga

Magnolia angustioblonga, commonly known as the narrow-ovate magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often arranged alternately along the branches. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, appearing before the leaves in early spring. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
