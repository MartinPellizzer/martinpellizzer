Medinilla furfuracea

Medinilla furfuracea, commonly known as the velvet medinilla, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-drained soil.

Medinilla furfuracea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and unique foliage.
