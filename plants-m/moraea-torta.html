Moraea torta

Moraea torta, commonly known as the tortuous moraea, is a striking perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of tubular or bell-shaped blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Moraea torta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
