Mimosa ignava

Mimosa ignava, commonly known as the desert mimosa, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its deep root system and ability to fix nitrogen. Its leaves are sensitive to touch, curling when disturbed, a trait shared with other mimosa species.

Mimosa ignava plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
