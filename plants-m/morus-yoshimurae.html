Morus yoshimurae

Morus yoshimurae, commonly known as the Yoshimura mulberry, is a species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in catkins. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when mature.

This species is primarily valued for its ornamental qualities and is often grown in gardens and parks.
