Mentha × niliaca var. genevensis

Mentha × niliaca var. genevensis, commonly known as Geneva mint, is a hybrid mint species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly minty fragrance and its serrated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of essential oils.
