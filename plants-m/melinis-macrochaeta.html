Melinis macrochaeta

Melinis macrochaeta, commonly known as giant panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stems and wide, blade-like leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Botanically, it is noted for its rapid growth and adaptability, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
