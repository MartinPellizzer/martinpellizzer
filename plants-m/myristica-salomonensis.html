Myristica salomonensis

Myristica salomonensis, commonly known as the Solomon Islands nutmeg, is a member of the Myristicaceae family.

It is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of the South Pacific, particularly the Solomon Islands. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers. Its most notable feature is the fruit, which contains the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

The botanical significance of Myristica salomonensis lies in its unique chemical composition and its role in the region's biodiversity.
