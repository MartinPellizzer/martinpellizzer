Manihot caerulescens var. genuina

Manihot caerulescens var. genuina, commonly known as the blue arrowleaf, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue-green leaves with a waxy coating, which give it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by triangular, spiny fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
