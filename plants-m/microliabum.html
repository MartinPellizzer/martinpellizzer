Microliabum

Microliabum, commonly known as the slender false nettle, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, typical of its family. Microliabum is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is not widely cultivated but is occasionally used in traditional herbal medicine.
