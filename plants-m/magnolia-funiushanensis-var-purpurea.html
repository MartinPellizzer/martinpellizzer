Magnolia funiushanensis var. purpurea

Magnolia funiushanensis var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple-flowered magnolia, is a rare and ornamental plant native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range from pale pink to deep purple, often with a subtle fragrance. The leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance. This variety thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

It is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized horticultural collections.
