Mimosa carinata

Mimosa carinata, commonly known as the sensitive plant or mimosa pudica, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched, a response triggered by mechanical stimulation. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in open, sunny areas.

Its rapid leaf closure makes it a popular subject in botanical studies and educational demonstrations.
