Matelea cynanchiflora

Matelea cynanchiflora, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is known for its large, fragrant white flowers that bloom at night. The plant has a climbing habit and features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. Its flowers open in the evening and close by morning, adapting to the pollination activities of nocturnal insects.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
