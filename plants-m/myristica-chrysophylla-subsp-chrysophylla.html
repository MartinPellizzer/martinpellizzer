Myristica chrysophylla subsp. chrysophylla

Myristica chrysophylla subsp.

chrysophylla, commonly known as the golden myristica, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, oily seeds and distinctive yellowish leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits containing the valuable nut inside. Its botanical features include a smooth, dark bark and a complex root system adapted to moist, shaded environments.

It is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
