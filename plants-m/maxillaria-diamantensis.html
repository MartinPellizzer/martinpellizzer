Maxillaria diamantensis

Maxillaria diamantensis, commonly known as the diamond orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces striking, colorful flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically has strap-like leaves and blooms in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Maxillaria diamantensis is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
