Monanthos subserratus

Monanthos subserratus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves with finely serrated edges, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a single spikelet, typical of the grass family. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
