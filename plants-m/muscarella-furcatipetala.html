Muscarella furcatipetala, commonly known as the forked-petals muscaria, is a rare and striking member of the Amanita family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, umbrella-like cap that is often adorned with a unique, wavy pattern. The stem is typically slender and fibrous, with a bulbous base that is often covered in a volva. Its gills are pale and closely spaced, and the spores are smooth and white.
