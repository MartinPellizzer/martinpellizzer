Maxillaria moralesii

Maxillaria moralesii, commonly known as the Morales' orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its survival in seasonal environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple with a contrasting yellow lip, and it blooms during the warmer months.

Maxillaria moralesii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
