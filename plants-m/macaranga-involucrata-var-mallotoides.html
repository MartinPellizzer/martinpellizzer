Macaranga involucrata var. mallotoides

Macaranga involucrata var. mallotoides, commonly known as the broadleaf macaranga, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its opposite, simple, and often palmately lobed leaves. The leaves are typically green on the upper surface and paler underneath, with a rough texture. It produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its rapid growth and use in ecological restoration projects.
