Macaranga schleinitziana

Macaranga schleinitziana, commonly known as the white-veined macaranga, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent white veins. The plant is often found in lowland forests and can grow up to 20 meters tall. Its bark is smooth and light-colored, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

The species is valued for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in agroforestry systems.
