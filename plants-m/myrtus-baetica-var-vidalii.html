Myrtus baetica var. vidalii

Myrtus baetica var. vidalii, commonly known as the Vidal's myrtle, is a rare and aromatic evergreen shrub native to southern Spain.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional Spanish cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Due to its limited distribution and unique characteristics, it is considered a valuable species in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
