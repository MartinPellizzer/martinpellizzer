Melocactus besleri

Melocactus besleri, commonly known as the golden crown cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and a dense crown of bright yellow spines. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the evening, adding a striking contrast to its spiny exterior. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
