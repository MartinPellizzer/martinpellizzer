Mimosa meticulosa f. hirsuta

Mimosa meticulosa f. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy mimosa, is a variant of the mimosa plant characterized by its hairy foliage and stems.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is native to South America. The plant features bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in spherical inflorescences, typical of the genus Mimosa.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soil with partial to full sun.
