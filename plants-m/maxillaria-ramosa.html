Maxillaria ramosa

Maxillaria ramosa, commonly known as the branched maxillaria, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange. The plant typically has a short, thick stem with multiple racemes of flowers, each bearing showy, lip-like petals. Its leaves are elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its compact appearance.

Maxillaria ramosa is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its adaptability and ornamental value.
