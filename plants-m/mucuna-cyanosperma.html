Mucuna cyanosperma

Mucuna cyanosperma, commonly known as blue mucuna, is a leguminous vine native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several elliptical, dark brown seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed habitats.

Mucuna cyanosperma is also noted for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
