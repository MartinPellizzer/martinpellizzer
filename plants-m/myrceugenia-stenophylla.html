Myrceugenia stenophylla

Myrceugenia stenophylla, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species native to the southern regions of South America.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and humid forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystems where it is native.
