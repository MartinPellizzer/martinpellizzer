Melica fugax subsp. madophylla

Melica fugax subsp.

madophylla, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks.

The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lipped flowers.

This subspecies is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed or open habitats.
