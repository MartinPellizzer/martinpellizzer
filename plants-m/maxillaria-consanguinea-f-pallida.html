Maxillaria consanguinea f. pallida

Maxillaria consanguinea f. pallida, commonly known as the pale-flowered maxillaria, is a hybrid orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers, which are often adorned with subtle greenish markings. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage consists of dark green, strap-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
