Mertensia pennigera

Mertensia pennigera, commonly known as bluebells, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of bell-shaped, sky-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has heart-shaped, lanceolate leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Mertensia pennigera is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
