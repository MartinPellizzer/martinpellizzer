Mentha × dumetorum f. oxyprionota

Mentha × dumetorum f. oxyprionota, commonly known as the "oxyprionota form" of purple mint, is a hybrid species within the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its distinctive purple or violet flower spikes that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands or stream sides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, minty aroma.

This form is less commonly cultivated than other mint varieties but is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
