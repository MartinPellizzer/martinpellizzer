Micrandra siphonioides var. genuina

Micrandra siphonioides var. genuina, commonly known as the slender bladderworts, is a carnivorous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures that trap and digest small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in wet, nutrient-poor environments such as marshes and bogs. Its leaves are slender and often submerged, while its stems are erect and branching.

This variety is notable for its specialized adaptations to nutrient acquisition in its ecological niche.
