Mimosa ixiamensis

Mimosa ixiamensis, commonly known as the Brazilian mimosa, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes and is characterized by its pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched, a response known as thigmonastic movement. It produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring.

Mimosa ixiamensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
