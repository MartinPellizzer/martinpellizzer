Mentha × rotundifolia subsp. insularis

Mentha × rotundifolia subsp.

insularis, commonly known as the island mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its rounded, slightly hairy leaves and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found growing in rocky or coastal areas, where it forms dense mats.

Due to its aromatic properties, it is occasionally used in herbal remedies and as a culinary herb.
