Magnolia phaulanta

Magnolia phaulanta, commonly known as the Chinese magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, and it can grow up to 20 meters tall.

Magnolia phaulanta is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
