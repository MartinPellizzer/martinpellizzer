Macroclinium calceolare

Macroclinium calceolare, commonly known as the "shoebill orchid," is a tropical plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its unique, shoe-shaped flower that resembles a bird's beak, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with purple veins, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Macroclinium calceolare is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking and unusual appearance.
