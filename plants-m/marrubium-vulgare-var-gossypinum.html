Marrubium vulgare var. gossypinum

Marrubium vulgare var. gossypinum, commonly known as great horehound, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its grayish-green, hairy leaves and spikes of blue or violet flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It is known for its strong, bitter scent and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh environments and contribute to its ecological significance.
