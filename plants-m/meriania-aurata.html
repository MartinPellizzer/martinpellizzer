Meriania aurata

Meriania aurata, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Meriania aurata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
