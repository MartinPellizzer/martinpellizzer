Myrtus alba

Myrtus alba, commonly known as white myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant produces small, round, dark blue berries that are edible but often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

Myrtus alba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and traditional herbal remedies.
