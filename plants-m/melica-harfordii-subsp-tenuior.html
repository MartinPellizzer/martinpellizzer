Melica harfordii subsp. tenuior

Melica harfordii subsp.

tenuior, commonly known as narrowleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 1 inch wide, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are often blue to purplish in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
