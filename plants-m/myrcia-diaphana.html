Myrcia diaphana

Myrcia diaphana, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its aromatic, waxy leaves and small white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and often have a distinct scent when crushed.

The fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that contains several seeds.
