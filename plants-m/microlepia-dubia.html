Microlepia dubia

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, linear fronds that grow in a cascading manner. The plant typically thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest undergrowth. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce clusters of small, lanceolate leaves.
