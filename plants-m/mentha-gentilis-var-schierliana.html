Mentha gentilis var. schierliana

Mentha gentilis var. schierliana, commonly known as Schierling's mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in herbal teas or as a culinary herb.
