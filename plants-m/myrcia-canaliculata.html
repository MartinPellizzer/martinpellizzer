Myrcia canaliculata

Myrcia canaliculata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant red or orange berries. The plant is evergreen, with a dense, bushy growth habit, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its flowers are small and clustered, often producing a strong, spicy aroma.

The species is also known for its ecological importance, providing food for various wildlife species.
