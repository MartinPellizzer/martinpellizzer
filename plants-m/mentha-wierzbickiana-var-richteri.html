Mentha wierzbickiana var. richteri

Mentha wierzbickiana var. richteri, commonly known as the Polish mint, is a variety of the mint genus native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and its ability to thrive in moist, shady environments. This plant typically grows as a low, spreading perennial with creeping stems that root at the nodes. Its flowers are small, pale blue to purple, and appear in whorls along the stems.

Mentha wierzbickiana var. richteri is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to its refreshing scent and flavor.
