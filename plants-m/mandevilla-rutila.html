Mandevilla rutila

Mandevilla rutila, commonly known as the golden mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from yellow to orange in color. The plant produces clusters of these showy blooms throughout the growing season, typically from spring to fall. Its foliage consists of large, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green.

Mandevilla rutila is a vigorous climber, often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to cover trellises or fences.
