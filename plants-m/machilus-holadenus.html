Machilus holadenus

Machilus holadenus, commonly known as the Chinese laurustin, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to southern China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine.

Machilus holadenus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
