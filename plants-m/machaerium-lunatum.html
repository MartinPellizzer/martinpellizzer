Machaerium lunatum

Machaerium lunatum, commonly known as the moonflower or moon bean, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Its unique flowering pattern and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
