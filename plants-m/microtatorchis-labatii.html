Microtatorchis labatii

Microtatorchis labatii, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit, which helps it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. Its leaves are short and fleshy, adapted for water storage.

Microtatorchis labatii is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited for xerophytic conditions.
