Mentha panormitana

Mentha panormitana, commonly known as the Mediterranean mint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, riverbanks, and coastal areas.

Mentha panormitana is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
