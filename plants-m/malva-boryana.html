Malva boryana

Malva boryana, commonly known as the Arctic mallows, is a perennial herb native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces showy pink or purple flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Malva boryana is often found in tundra and rocky habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
