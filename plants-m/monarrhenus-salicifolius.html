Monarrhenus salicifolius

Monarrhenus salicifolius, commonly known as the willow-leaf tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
