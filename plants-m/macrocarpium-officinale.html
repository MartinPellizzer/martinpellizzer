Macrocarpium officinale

Macrocarpium officinale, commonly known as the medicinal orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been traditionally used in herbal remedies for various ailments.
