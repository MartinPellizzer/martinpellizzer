Mertensia glaucescens var. mexicanum

Mertensia glaucescens var. mexicanum, commonly known as Mexican bluebells, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy, bluish coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
