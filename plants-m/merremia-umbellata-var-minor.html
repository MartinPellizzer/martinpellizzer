Merremia umbellata var. minor

Merremia umbellata var. minor, commonly known as the small-umbel morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces small, white or pale purple flowers with a distinctive umbel-like cluster. The plant is characterized by its fibrous, hairy stems and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its seeds are enclosed in rough, spiny capsules that split open when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
