Morinda bracteata

Morinda bracteata, commonly known as the red moringa, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia and is characterized by its distinctive red or orange flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
