Miconia boliviana

Miconia boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian miconia, is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences with white to pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Miconia boliviana is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of tropical and subtropical environments.
