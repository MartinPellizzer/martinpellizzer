Mammillaria scolymoides var. longiseta

Mammillaria scolymoides var. longiseta, commonly known as the long-hair mammillaria, is a compact cactus with a globular shape and a dense covering of white, woolly bristles.

It originates from the arid regions of Mexico, where it thrives in full sun and well-draining soil. The plant features prominent areoles from which both spines and flowers emerge. Its flowers are typically pale yellow or orange, blooming in late spring or early summer.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
