Momordica charantia

Momordica charantia, commonly known as bitter melon, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, warty, green fruit that turns orange when mature. The plant belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers and squash. Its leaves are heart-shaped and lobed, while its flowers are yellow with five petals.

Bitter melon is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its hypoglycemic effects.
