Masdevallia muscosa

Masdevallia muscosa, commonly known as the mossy masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, often epiphytic growth habit and its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, hairy leaves and produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a notable subject in botanical studies.

Masdevallia muscosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
