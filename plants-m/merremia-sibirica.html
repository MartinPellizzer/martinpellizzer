Merremia sibirica

Merremia sibirica, commonly known as Siberian morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from white to purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Merremia sibirica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
