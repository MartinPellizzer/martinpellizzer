Merremia verecunda

Merremia verecunda, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

Merremia verecunda is also known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain environments.
