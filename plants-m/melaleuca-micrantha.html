Melaleuca micrantha

Melaleuca micrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paperbark, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, flaky bark that peels in strips, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a leathery texture and a greyish-green color.

Melaleuca micrantha is often found in coastal and sandy soils, and it plays a role in stabilizing dunes and preventing erosion.
