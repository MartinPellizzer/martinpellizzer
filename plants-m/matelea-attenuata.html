Matelea attenuata

Matelea attenuata, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces edible berries that are sometimes used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by nocturnal insects such as moths.
