Marantochloa ubangiensis

Marantochloa ubangiensis, commonly known as the Ubangi arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family, which includes various ornamental and economically important species. The plant is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and striking, often colorful leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

While not widely cultivated, it holds potential for both horticultural interest and local use in some regions.
