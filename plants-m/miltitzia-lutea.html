Miltitzia lutea

Miltitzia lutea, commonly known as the yellow miltitzia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of about 5 to 10 meters. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly waxy appearance.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
