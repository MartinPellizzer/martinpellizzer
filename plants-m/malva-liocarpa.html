Malva liocarpa

Malva liocarpa, commonly known as the scarlet mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, accompanied by heart-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture. Its hairy stems and fibrous roots are characteristic of the genus Malva.

The plant is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
