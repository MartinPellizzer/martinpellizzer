Melocactus placentiformis

Melocactus placentiformis, commonly known as the placenta-shaped melon cactus, is a rare and distinctive cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, thick stem covered in spines and its unique, fleshy, segmented fruit that resembles a melon. The plant produces a highly aromatic, edible fruit with a yellowish pulp and black seeds. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
