Macraea cordata

Macraea cordata, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces woody pods that contain flattened seeds, typical of many legumes. It is valued for its durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.

However, due to overharvesting and habitat loss, it is also considered a vulnerable species in some areas.
