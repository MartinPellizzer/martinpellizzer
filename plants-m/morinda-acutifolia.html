Morinda acutifolia

Morinda acutifolia, commonly known as the Indian madder or red sanders, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its roots and stems contain compounds that have been utilized for their dyeing properties and medicinal effects.

Morinda acutifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitats.
