Mimusops revoluta

Mimusops revoluta, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a slightly waxy texture. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellow or orange berries. Its bark is thick and smooth, often with a reddish or brownish hue, and it is valued for its hard, durable wood.

Mimusops revoluta is also popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
