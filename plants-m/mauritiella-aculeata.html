Mauritiella aculeata

Mauritiella aculeata, commonly known as the African palm, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into clusters of edible berries. These berries are often consumed by birds and mammals, aiding in seed dispersal.

It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and salt spray.
