Metathelypteris glandulifera

Metathelypteris glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, leathery fronds that are often covered with small, glandular dots. The plant typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as marshes and along stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds.

The species is notable for its ecological role in maintaining wetland biodiversity.
