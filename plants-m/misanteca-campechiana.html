Misanteca campechiana

Misanteca campechiana, commonly known as the Mexican wild bean, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in dry or disturbed habitats. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a distinctive yellow or orange coloration.

The seeds are enclosed in a hard pod and are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.
