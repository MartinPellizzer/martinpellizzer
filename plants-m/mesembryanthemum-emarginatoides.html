Mesembryanthemum emarginatoides

Mesembryanthemum emarginatoides, commonly known as the coastal evening primrose, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, often blue or purple-stemmed leaves and small, star-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant thrives in arid and coastal environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and salt exposure. Its thick leaves store water, and its flowering pattern is influenced by photoperiod.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
