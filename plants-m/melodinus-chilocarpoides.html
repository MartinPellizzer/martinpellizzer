Melodinus chilocarpoides

Melodinus chilocarpoides, commonly known as the black mangrove, is a tropical shrub native to the coastal regions of the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black bark and its ability to thrive in saline and waterlogged soils. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by spherical, fleshy fruits.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
