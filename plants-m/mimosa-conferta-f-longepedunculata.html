Mimosa conferta f. longepedunculata

Mimosa conferta f. longepedunculata, commonly known as the long pedunculate mimosa, is a variant of the mimosa plant characterized by its elongated peduncles.

It is a shrub native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in coastal and sandy soils. The plant features compound leaves that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in spherical heads, which are often referred to as "mimosas.".

The species is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a valuable plant in certain ecological contexts.
