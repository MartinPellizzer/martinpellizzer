Myrcia fasciculata

Myrcia fasciculata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible but have a sharp, acidic taste. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and lowland rainforests.

Myrcia fasciculata is also known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies.
