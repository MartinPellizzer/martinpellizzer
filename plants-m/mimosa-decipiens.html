Mimosa decipiens

Mimosa decipiens, commonly known as the sensitive plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its rapid leaf movement in response to stimuli makes it a popular subject in botanical studies.
