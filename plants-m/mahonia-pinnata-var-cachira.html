Mahonia pinnata var. cachira

Mahonia pinnata var. cachira, commonly known as the Cachira Mahonia, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with dark green, leathery leaves that are pinnately compound. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in racemes. These flowers are followed by blue-black berries that are attractive to birds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to a range of soil conditions.
