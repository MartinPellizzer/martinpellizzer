Melaleuca flavovirens

Melaleuca flavovirens, commonly known as the golden-green melaleuca, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually a combination of green and yellowish hues. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and range in color from white to yellow.

Melaleuca flavovirens is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
