Mutisia flagellaria

Mutisia flagellaria, commonly known as the whipplant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a creeping stem with slender, leaf-like structures that can reach several meters in length. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and they bloom in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Mutisia flagellaria is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
