Menispermum fenestratum

Menispermum fenestratum, commonly known as the fenestrated Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves with a unique fenestrated (fenestrated) pattern, giving it its common name. The plant produces striking, pipe-shaped flowers that are often greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains several seeds.

Menispermum fenestratum thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found along stream banks and in forest understories.
