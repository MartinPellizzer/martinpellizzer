Manihot peltata

Manihot peltata, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Its roots are used in traditional medicine and have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.

Manihot peltata thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.
