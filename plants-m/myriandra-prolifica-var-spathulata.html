Myriandra prolifica var. spathulata

Myriandra prolifica var. spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leafed myriandra, is a species native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in arid and semi-arid regions.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and seasonal flowering.
