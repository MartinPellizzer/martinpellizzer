Marumia pachygyna

Marumia pachygyna, commonly known as the thick-stemmed marumia, is a unique plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, an adaptation to its arid or semi-arid habitat. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, adding to its ornamental appeal. Its leaves are often succulent, further supporting its ability to thrive in dry conditions.

Marumia pachygyna is valued in horticulture for its resilience and distinctive appearance.
