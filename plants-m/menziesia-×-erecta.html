Menziesia × erecta

Menziesia × erecta, commonly known as the erect menziesia, is a hybrid plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pink, which bloom in early spring.

Menziesia × erecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant landscaping.
