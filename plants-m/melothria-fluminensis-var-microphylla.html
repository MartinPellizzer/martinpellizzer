Melothria fluminensis var. microphylla

Melothria fluminensis var. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf river passionfruit, is a tropical vine native to South America.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces clusters of yellow-orange flowers that are followed by small, round fruits. The fruit has a thin, edible skin and a tart, aromatic pulp.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of unique flavor in culinary applications.
