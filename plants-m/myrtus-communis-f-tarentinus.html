Myrtus communis f. tarentinus

Myrtus communis f. tarentinus, commonly known as the Tarentine myrtle, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, dark green leaves that are glossy and aromatic, often with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it suitable for rock gardens, borders, or as a container plant.
