Malveopsis virgata

Malveopsis virgata, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Malveopsis virgata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insects and birds.
