Molinia caerulea var. silvestris

Molinia caerulea var. silvestris, commonly known as the purple moor grass, is a perennial grass species native to western Europe.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and striking purple to blue-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and bogs. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
