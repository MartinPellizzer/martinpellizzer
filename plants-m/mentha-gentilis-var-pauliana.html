Mentha gentilis var. pauliana

Mentha gentilis var. pauliana, commonly known as Pauliana mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its use in culinary applications and traditional herbal remedies.
