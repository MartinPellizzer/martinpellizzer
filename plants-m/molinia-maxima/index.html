Molinia Maxima

Molinia maxima is a tall, clump-forming grass native to wetlands and marshy areas across Europe and parts of Asia. It is known for its striking silver-green foliage and fluffy, seed-filled spikelets that appear in late summer. This plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich environments and is often found along riverbanks and in peat bogs. Despite its modest appearance, Molinia maxima is a resilient and ecologically valuable plant.
