Melastoma virusanum

Melastoma virusanum, commonly known as the purple mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple, segmented fruit that contains a white, segmented arillate pulp. The plant has glossy, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The botanical features of Melastoma virusanum make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
