Macrocarpaea pachyphylla

Macrocarpaea pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved macrocarpaea, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
