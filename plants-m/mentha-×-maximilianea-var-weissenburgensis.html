Mentha × maximilianea var. weissenburgensis

Mentha × maximilianea var. weissenburgensis, commonly known as the Weissenburg mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers arranged in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardens and natural habitats.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, contributing to its use in herbal teas and traditional medicine.
