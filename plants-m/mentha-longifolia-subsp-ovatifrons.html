Mentha longifolia subsp. ovatifrons

Mentha longifolia subsp.

ovatifrons, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lanceolate to ovate, with a serrated margin.

The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, giving it a strong mint scent and making it valuable in herbal medicine and culinary applications.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.
