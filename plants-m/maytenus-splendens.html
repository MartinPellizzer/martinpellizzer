Maytenus splendens

Maytenus splendens, commonly known as the golden mayten, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite and ovate in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by spherical, red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Maytenus splendens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in folk medicine.
