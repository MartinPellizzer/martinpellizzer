Medinilla maluensis

Medinilla maluensis, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or velvet leaf, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, waxy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowers.

Medinilla maluensis is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
