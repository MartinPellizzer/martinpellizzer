Mazus lecomtei var. ramosus

Mazus lecomtei var. ramosus, commonly known as the hairy mazus, is a low-growing perennial plant native to China.

It features spreading stems covered in fine, soft hairs, which give it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, pale purple or pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped and covered in a fine pubescence, contributing to its ornamental value.

Mazus lecomtei var. ramosus is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
