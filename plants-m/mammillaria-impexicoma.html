Mammillaria impexicoma

Mammillaria impexicoma, commonly known as the Mexican hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
