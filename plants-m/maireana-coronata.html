Maireana coronata

Maireana coronata, commonly known as the mountain daisy, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, preferring well-drained soils. It has a low-growing habit with grey-green foliage and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Maireana coronata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
