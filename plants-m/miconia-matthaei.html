Miconia matthaei

Miconia matthaei, commonly known as the white miconia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, showy, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has broad, glossy leaves and is often found in humid, shaded environments within the undergrowth of tropical forests. It is noted for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain areas, making it a significant ecological concern.

The fruit is small, berry-like, and contains several seeds that are dispersed by animals.
