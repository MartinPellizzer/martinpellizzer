Mimosopsis lemmonii

Mimosopsis lemmonii, commonly known as the desert mimosopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and high temperatures. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in cylindrical clusters, which bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are narrow and often appear in pairs, contributing to its drought-resistant structure.

Mimosopsis lemmonii plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat and provides nectar for pollinators.
