Manihot violacea var. genuina

Manihot violacea var. genuina, commonly known as the purple arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that are followed by edible, starchy tubers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers warm, humid climates.

Due to its ornamental value and edible roots, it is cultivated both for aesthetic purposes and as a food source.
