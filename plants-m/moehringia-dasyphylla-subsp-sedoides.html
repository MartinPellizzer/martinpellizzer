Moehringia dasyphylla subsp. sedoides

Moehringia dasyphylla subsp.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its botanical features include a basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that rise from the center, making it well adapted to harsh mountain environments.
