Micromyrtus trudgenii

Micromyrtus trudgenii, commonly known as the Trudgen's bottlebrush, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of bright red flowers that resemble bottlebrushes, which bloom from late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
