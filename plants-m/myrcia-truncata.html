Myrcia truncata

Myrcia truncata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming an invasive species.

Its botanical features make it a notable plant in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
