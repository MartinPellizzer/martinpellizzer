Mentha sylvestris var. crispata

Mentha sylvestris var. crispata, commonly known as the wrinkled mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its wrinkled, gray-green leaves and strong aromatic scent. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, appearing in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other mint species.
