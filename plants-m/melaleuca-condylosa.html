Melaleuca condylosa

Melaleuca condylosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant produces woody cones that contain numerous seeds, typical of the Myrtaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Melaleuca condylosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
