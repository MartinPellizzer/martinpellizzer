Melanoseris cyanea

Melanoseris cyanea, commonly known as the blue-eyed fern, is a striking member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that display a striking contrast of deep green and blue hues. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. The fronds are typically bioluminescent, emitting a faint blue glow in the dark, which aids in attracting nocturnal pollinators.

Its unique appearance and ecological adaptations make it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
