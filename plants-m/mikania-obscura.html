Mikania obscura

Mikania obscura, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a vigorous vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, trumpet-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has a rapid growth rate and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges. Its stems are covered in fine hairs and can climb by twining around other vegetation.

Mikania obscura is also known for its ability to spread aggressively, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
