Monocera prunifolia

Monocera prunifolia, commonly known as the cherryleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a soft, fuzzy texture, and its flowers are pink to purple, resembling those of the hibiscus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in riparian areas.
