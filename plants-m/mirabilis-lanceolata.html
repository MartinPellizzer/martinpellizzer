Mirabilis lanceolata

Mirabilis lanceolata, commonly known as the evening primrose, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers that open in the evening. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its unique flowering habit and ornamental value make it a notable species in the Nyctaginaceae family.
