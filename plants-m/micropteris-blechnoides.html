Micropteris blechnoides

Micropteris blechnoides, commonly known as the common holly fern, is a perennial fern native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has feather-like fronds with a distinctive, narrow, and elongated shape. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Micropteris blechnoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
