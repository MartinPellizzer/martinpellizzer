Muhlenbergia distans

Muhlenbergia distans, commonly known as the silver bluestem, is a warm-season grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-tipped leaves and tufted, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for native landscaping. Its fine texture and ornamental appearance contribute to its use in ornamental gardens and ecological restoration projects.

Muhlenbergia distans plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife in its native range.
