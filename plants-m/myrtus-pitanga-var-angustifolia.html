Myrtus pitanga var. angustifolia

Myrtus pitanga var. angustifolia, commonly known as Brazilian myrtle or pitanga, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
