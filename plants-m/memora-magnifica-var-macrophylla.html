Memora magnifica var. macrophylla

Memora magnifica var. macrophylla, commonly known as the grand magnolia, is a striking ornamental plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy, fragrant flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter. The plant features broad, glossy, dark green leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its blooms. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained, fertile soil.

The varietal name *macrophylla* refers to its large, leafy foliage, which enhances its visual appeal in gardens and landscapes.
