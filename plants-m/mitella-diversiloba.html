Mitella diversiloba

Mitella diversiloba, commonly known as the mountain bluebell, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features delicate, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a low-growing habit with slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important component of its native habitat.
