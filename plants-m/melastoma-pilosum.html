Melastoma pilosum

Melastoma pilosum, commonly known as the hairy melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and usually have five petals with a distinctive pink or red color.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.
