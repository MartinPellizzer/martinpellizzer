Malvastrum bakerianum var. strigosum

Malvastrum bakerianum var. strigosum, commonly known as the hairy mallow, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a soft, fuzzy texture. The plant produces showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
