Mutisia acuminata

Mutisia acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in countries like Peru and Bolivia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are yellow with a central disk, and it produces small, elongated fruits that contain seeds.

Mutisia acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
