Miconia lasiopoda

Miconia lasiopoda, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Miconia lasiopoda is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed habitats, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and potentially problematic in non-native environments.
