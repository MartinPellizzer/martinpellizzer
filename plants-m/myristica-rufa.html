Myristica rufa

Myristica rufa, commonly known as the red nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The tree produces a unique fruit called a "nutmeg apple," which contains the aromatic seeds used to make nutmeg and mace. Its bark and leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is valued for its economic and cultural significance in the spice trade.
