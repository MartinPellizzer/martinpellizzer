Melanorrhoea megalocarpa

Melanorrhoea megalocarpa, commonly known as the giant tamarind, is a large leguminous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and pinnate, and its distinctive yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces large, woody pods containing numerous seeds, which are encased in a hard, fibrous shell. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant is valued for its hard timber and has traditional uses in various cultural practices.
