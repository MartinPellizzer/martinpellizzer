Mentha recta

Mentha recta, commonly known as upright mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Mentha recta is a member of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic essential oils.

It is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its refreshing scent and potential health benefits.
