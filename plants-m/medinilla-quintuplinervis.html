Medinilla quintuplinervis

Medinilla quintuplinervis, commonly known as the five-veined medinilla, is a tropical shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves with five distinct veins, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant.

Medinilla quintuplinervis is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
