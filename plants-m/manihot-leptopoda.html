Manihot leptopoda

Manihot leptopoda, commonly known as the slender arrowleaf manioc, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant produces a milky sap that contains toxic compounds, which must be processed before consumption. Its tuberous roots are the primary edible part, used in various traditional cuisines.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for both food and medicinal purposes.
