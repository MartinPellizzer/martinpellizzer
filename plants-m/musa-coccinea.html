Musa coccinea

Musa coccinea, commonly known as the red banana, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red and purple bracts that surround the small, yellow bananas. The plant belongs to the Musaceae family and is closely related to other banana species. Its ornamental value makes it popular in gardens and as a houseplant.

The fruit is edible but less commonly cultivated for commercial use compared to other banana varieties.
