Maxillaria hoppii

Maxillaria hoppii, commonly known as the Hoppi's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, often with a distinctive yellow lip. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Maxillaria hoppii is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
