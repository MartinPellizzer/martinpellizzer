Myrcia negrensis

Myrcia negrensis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically orange to red in color. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

Myrcia negrensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
