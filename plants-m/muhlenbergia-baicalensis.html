Muhlenbergia baicalensis

Muhlenbergia baicalensis, commonly known as the Baical muhlenbergia, is a perennial grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces inflorescences of spikelets that are often pink to purple, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

Muhlenbergia baicalensis is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
