Machilus camphoratus

Machilus camphoratus, commonly known as the camphor laurel, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing one or two seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a fragrant, camphor-like scent.

Machilus camphoratus is valued for its timber and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
