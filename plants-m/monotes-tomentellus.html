Monotes tomentellus

Monotes tomentellus, commonly known as the woolly monotes, is a small, herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering of white or grayish hairs, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a broad, ovate shape.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish or pale yellow, and are clustered in dense inflorescences.
