Melaleuca violacea

Melaleuca violacea, commonly known as the purple myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and showy, violet-pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are borne in dense clusters and are pollinated by insects, particularly bees.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual interest.
