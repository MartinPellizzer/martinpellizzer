Miltonia cuneata

Miltonia cuneata, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper, is a striking orchid native to the cloud forests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its velvety, pinkish-purple petals and a distinctive, pouch-like lip that resembles a slipper. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, often found in shaded, humid environments. Its flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, adding a vibrant touch to the forest understory.

Miltonia cuneata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its beauty in botanical gardens and among orchid enthusiasts.
