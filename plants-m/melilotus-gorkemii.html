Melilotus gorkemii

Melilotus gorkemii, commonly known as the Gorkem's sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its aromatic compounds, which contribute to its use in traditional medicine and as a forage crop. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Melilotus gorkemii is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
