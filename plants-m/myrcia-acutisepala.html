Myrcia acutisepala

Myrcia acutisepala, commonly known as the sharp-sepaled myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in clusters. Its leaves are elliptical and have a glossy surface, while its fruits are small, woody capsules that release seeds when mature.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas within tropical and subtropical forests.
