Molina phyteumoides

Molina phyteumoides, commonly known as the Mexican sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing desert soils and providing nectar for pollinators.

Due to its ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
