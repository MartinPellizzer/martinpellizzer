Myrica salicifolia var. subalpina

Myrica salicifolia var. subalpina, commonly known as the subalpine waxmyrtle, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by dark blue, waxy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in subalpine and alpine habitats.

This variety is notable for its hardiness and ability to withstand cold temperatures.
