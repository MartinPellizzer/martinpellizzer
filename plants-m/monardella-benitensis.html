Monardella benitensis

Monardella benitensis, commonly known as Benito's monardella, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky streambanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Monardella benitensis is valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
