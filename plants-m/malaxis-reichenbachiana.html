Malaxis reichenbachiana

Malaxis reichenbachiana, commonly known as the Reichenbach's lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and its long, slender inflorescence that bears numerous small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in well-drained, humus-rich soils.

Malaxis reichenbachiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
