Macrorhynchus coronopifolius

Macrorhynchus coronopifolius, commonly known as the crown-leaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white to pale blue petals. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while the stems are often hairy.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
