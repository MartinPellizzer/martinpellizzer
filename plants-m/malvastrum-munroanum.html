Malvastrum munroanum

Malvastrum munroanum, commonly known as Munro's mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny conditions. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a soft, fuzzy texture, and the stems are often hairy.

Malvastrum munroanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
