Mesembryanthemum subaequale

Mesembryanthemum subaequale, commonly known as the equal-leaved ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, often blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that can range in color from white to pink or purple, depending on the variety. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves to survive dry conditions.

Mesembryanthemum subaequale is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its drought tolerance and attractive appearance.
