Melica cupani var. eligulata

Melica cupani var. eligulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and stream banks. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a flowering period that usually occurs in late spring to early summer.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
