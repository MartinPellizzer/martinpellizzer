Milium hirsutum

Milium hirsutum, commonly known as hairy millet, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescence. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Milium hirsutum is often grown as a decorative plant in gardens due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
