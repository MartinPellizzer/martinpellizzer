Manihot tripartita var. cajaniformis

Manihot tripartita var. cajaniformis, commonly known as the cassava or manioc, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, triangular leaves and a woody stem that supports its upright growth habit. The plant produces a starchy tuberous root that is a staple food in many tropical regions. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The leaves are often used as a vegetable, while the roots are processed into flour, alcohol, and other products.
