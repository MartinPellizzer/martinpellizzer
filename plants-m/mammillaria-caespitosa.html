Mammillaria caespitosa

Mammillaria caespitosa, commonly known as the clustered milk barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a dense cluster of cylindrical stems that form a compact rosette. The plant features white to pale blue spines and produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.
