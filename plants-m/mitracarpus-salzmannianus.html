Mitracarpus salzmannianus

Mitracarpus salzmannianus, commonly known as the Salzmann's tamarisk, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Mitracarpus salzmannianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to Mediterranean climates.
