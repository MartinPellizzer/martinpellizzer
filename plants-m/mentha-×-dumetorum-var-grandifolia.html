Mentha × dumetorum var. grandifolia

Mentha × dumetorum var. grandifolia, commonly known as "large-leaved hemp mint," is a hybrid plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its large, oval-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct minty aroma. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its aromatic properties and use in herbal remedies.
