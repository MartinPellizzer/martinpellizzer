Malapoenna glabrata

Malapoenna glabrata, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a deciduous shrub native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Malapoenna glabrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
