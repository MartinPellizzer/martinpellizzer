Morisonia singularis

Morisonia singularis, commonly known as the singular morisonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces small, green, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
