Miconia cremophylla

Miconia cremophylla, commonly known as the red-leaved miconia, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, glossy red foliage that contrasts with its green stems and flowers. The plant belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is known for its clustered inflorescences that produce small, white or pinkish flowers. Its leaves are typically broad and ovate, with a waxy texture that helps it retain moisture in humid environments.

Miconia cremophylla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance and ability to thrive in warm, moist climates.
