Mucuna erecta

Mucuna erecta, commonly known as the cowpea vine or velvet bean, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces distinctive cream-colored flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing flat, kidney-shaped seeds. Mucuna erecta is known for its rapid growth and ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

It is also used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
