Malva sylvestris var. polymorpha

Malva sylvestris var. polymorpha, commonly known as the common mallow, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, fertile soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other plants like cotton and hibiscus.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional herbal remedies.
