Marcetia simulans

Marcetia simulans, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. Marcetia simulans thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas or along stream banks.

It is a perennial herb that is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
