Mahonia × toluacensis

Mahonia × toluacensis, commonly known as the evergreen holly or coastal mahonia, is a hybrid shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and produces bright yellow flowers in late winter to early spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are evergreen, leathery, and have a distinctive blue-green color.

Mahonia × toluacensis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal climates.
