Malus gratiaeportus

Malus gratiaeportus, commonly known as the Graceport apple tree, is a species native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, tart apples that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture and a glossy green surface.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
