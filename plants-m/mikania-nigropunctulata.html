Mikania nigropunctulata

Mikania nigropunctulata, commonly known as black dot Mikania, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves with dark spots. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spherical heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Its rapid growth and invasive potential make it a notable species in ecological studies.
