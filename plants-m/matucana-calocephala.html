Matucana calocephala

Matucana calocephala, commonly known as the red-topped matucana, is a striking member of the Araceae family native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, red-tipped leaves that contrast beautifully with its dark green foliage. The plant produces striking inflorescences consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in humid, warm environments with bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture in its soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant, though it can be challenging to maintain outside its natural habitat.
