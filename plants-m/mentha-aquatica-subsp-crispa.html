Mentha aquatica subsp. crispa

Mentha aquatica subsp.

crispa, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist environments in Europe and Asia. It features upright, square stems and lance-shaped leaves with a serrated margin.

The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Its aromatic leaves contain menthol and other volatile oils, contributing to its medicinal and culinary uses.

It thrives in wet soils near streams, ponds, and marshes, making it a common sight in riparian habitats.
