Millettia cabrae

Millettia cabrae, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several elliptical seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

The tree is also notable for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its role in preventing soil erosion.
