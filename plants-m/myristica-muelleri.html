Myristica muelleri

Myristica muelleri, commonly known as the Mueller's nutmeg, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody fruit that contains a highly valued seed kernel. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has compound leaves with a distinctive, glossy appearance. Its fruit, similar in appearance to the nutmeg fruit, is used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
