Mucuna schmutzii

Mucuna schmutzii, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, hairy, pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Its seeds are rich in protein and have been used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

Mucuna schmutzii also plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
