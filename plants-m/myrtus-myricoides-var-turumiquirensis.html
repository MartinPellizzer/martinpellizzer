Myrtus myricoides var. turumiquirensis

Myrtus myricoides var. turumiquirensis, commonly known as the Turumiquire myrtle, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in mountainous areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential horticultural use.
