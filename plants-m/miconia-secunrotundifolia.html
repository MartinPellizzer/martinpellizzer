Miconia secunrotundifolia

Miconia secunrotundifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that are typically followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Miconia secunrotundifolia is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its invasive potential, it is often considered a problematic species in certain regions.
