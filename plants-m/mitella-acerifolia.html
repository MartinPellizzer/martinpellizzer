Mitella acerifolia

Mitella acerifolia, commonly known as the mapleleaf bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive maple-like leaves with lobed margins. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Mitella acerifolia thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and streambanks.
