Monteverdia apiculata

Monteverdia apiculata, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant is a climbing vine with opposite, ovate leaves that have a smooth, glossy surface. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Monteverdia apiculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
