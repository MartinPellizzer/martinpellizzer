Muntingia glabra

Muntingia glabra, commonly known as the West Indian cherry or pitahaya, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical America.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink, followed by edible, red, cherry-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The plant has a shallow root system, making it adaptable to various soil types but sensitive to frost.
