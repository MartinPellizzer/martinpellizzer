Manihot orinocensis

Manihot orinocensis, commonly known as Orinoco manioc, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Venezuela and Colombia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and striking yellow flowers. The plant produces a starchy root known as cassava, which is a staple food in many regions. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and versatility in cooking.

However, its roots contain cyanogenic glycosides, requiring proper processing to remove toxic compounds before consumption.
