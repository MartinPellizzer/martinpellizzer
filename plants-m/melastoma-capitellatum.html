Melastoma capitellatum

Melastoma capitellatum, commonly known as the red-flowered Melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, clustered pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a glossy appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
