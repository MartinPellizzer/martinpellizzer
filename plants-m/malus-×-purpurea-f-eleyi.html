Malus × purpurea f. eleyi

Malus × purpurea f. eleyi, commonly known as the Eleyi crabapple, is a cultivar of the crabapple tree characterized by its distinctive floral and fruiting features.

It is a hybrid species within the genus Malus, known for its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, tart fruits that are often used in jams and culinary applications. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for ornamental gardening and small spaces.

The foliage changes color in the fall, adding seasonal visual interest to the landscape.
