Malus baccata calvillea

Malus baccata calvillea, commonly known as the Calvillea crabapple, is a small deciduous tree native to southern China.

It is characterized by its ornamental white flowers that appear in early spring, followed by small, edible fruits in the summer. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and turn vibrant shades of red in the fall. The tree is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
