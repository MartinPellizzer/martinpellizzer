Mirabilis jalapa var. dichlamydomorpha

Mirabilis jalapa var. dichlamydomorpha, commonly known as the velvet leaf four o'clock, is a variant of the common four o'clock plant.

It is characterized by its unique, velvety, dark green leaves that are thick and waxy. The flowers are typically pink or red, opening in the late afternoon and closing at dawn. This plant is native to the Americas and thrives in warm, sunny conditions.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is easy to grow in well-drained soil.
