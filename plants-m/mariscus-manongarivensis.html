Mariscus manongarivensis

Mariscus manongarivensis, commonly known as the Madagascar marsh orchid, is a rare and unique flowering plant native to the humid lowland forests of Madagascar.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble small, clustered flowers. The plant thrives in wet, shaded environments and has adapted to the specific microclimates of its native habitat. Its leaves are typically broad and succulent, allowing it to store water in its arid surroundings.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in its natural range.
