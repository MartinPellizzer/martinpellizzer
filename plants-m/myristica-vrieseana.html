Myristica vrieseana

Myristica vrieseana, commonly known as the Vriesia nutmeg, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, dark red berries that contain aromatic seeds. The plant produces a unique resinous substance similar to nutmeg, which is used in traditional medicine and perfumery. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Myristica vrieseana is also valued for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to fully understand its therapeutic applications.
