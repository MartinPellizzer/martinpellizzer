Mimosa dysocarpa

Mimosa dysocarpa, commonly known as the clustered mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant has a woody stem with thorn-like projections, and its leaves are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and it is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils.

Mimosa dysocarpa is often found in open woodlands and is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas.
