Melittis carpatica var. sarmatica

Melittis carpatica var. sarmatica, commonly known as Sarmatian melittis, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula and parts of Eastern Europe.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been traditionally used in herbal remedies.
