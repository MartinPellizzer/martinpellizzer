Moraea tripetala var. jacquiniana

Moraea tripetala var. jacquiniana, commonly known as the Jacquin's spider lily, is a striking bulbous perennial native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of delicate, spider-like flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. Its blooming period typically occurs in late winter to early spring, adding early seasonal color to gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is a popular choice among bulb enthusiasts and gardeners seeking exotic flora.
