Marsdenia inelegans

Marsdenia inelegans, commonly known as the fragrant woodbine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink, followed by elliptical berries. It thrives in humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
