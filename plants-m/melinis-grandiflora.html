Melinis grandiflora

Melinis grandiflora, commonly known as broadleaf silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, silver-gray leaves that are often used in ornamental landscaping. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that add a distinctive visual element to gardens. It prefers full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping and drought-tolerant gardens.

Melinis grandiflora is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its low maintenance requirements.
