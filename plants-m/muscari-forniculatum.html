Muscari forniculatum

Muscari forniculatum, commonly known as the Persian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, clustered growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and evergreen, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Muscari forniculatum is well-suited for rock gardens and can thrive in partial shade to full sun conditions.
