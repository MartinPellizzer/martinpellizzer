Melochia pauciflora

Melochia pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered melochia, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically lobed and have a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, followed by edible, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or along riverbanks.

Melochia pauciflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
