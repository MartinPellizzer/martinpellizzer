Myrcia paraensis

Myrcia paraensis, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals. It is part of the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove.

Myrcia paraensis is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance.
