Myrcia rorida var. microphylla

Myrcia rorida var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved myrcia, is a species native to Brazil.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, reddish berries that contain several seeds.

This plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.
