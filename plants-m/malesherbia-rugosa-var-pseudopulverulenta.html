Malesherbia rugosa var. pseudopulverulenta

Malesherbia rugosa var. pseudopulverulenta, commonly known as the powdered malesherbia, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and drought-tolerant landscapes.
