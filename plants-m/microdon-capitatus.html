Microdon capitatus

Microdon capitatus, commonly known as the small-headed orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, intricate flowers. The plant typically features a basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves and produces a single spike of tiny, fragrant blossoms. Microdon capitatus thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical or subtropical regions.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in local ecosystems.
