Myrcia recurvata

Myrcia recurvata, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and fragrant, red or purple flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a strong, aromatic scent.

The flowers are clustered in inflorescences and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and birds.
