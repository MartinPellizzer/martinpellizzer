Melothria japonica var. major

Melothria japonica var. major, commonly known as the Japanese gooseberry or Japanese waxberry, is a small, trailing shrub native to East Asia.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange in color when ripe. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and is often grown as an ornamental or for its edible fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.

The berries are rich in vitamin C and have been used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
