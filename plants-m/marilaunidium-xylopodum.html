Marilaunidium xylopodum

Marilaunidium xylopodum, commonly known as the African star grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a robust, fibrous root system and grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

The plant is notable for its ability to withstand drought conditions due to its deep root system and waxy leaf surface.
