Mangifera indica var. marundana

Mangifera indica var. marundana, commonly known as the Malayan mango, is a variant of the mango tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, round fruits with a thick, fibrous pulp and a sweet, aromatic flavor. The plant thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a glossy green surface and a slightly serrated edge.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.
