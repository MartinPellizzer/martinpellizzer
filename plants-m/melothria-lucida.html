Melothria lucida

Melothria lucida, commonly known as the golden gooseberry, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It produces small, yellow-orange berries that are edible and have a tart, acidic flavor. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

The berries are rich in vitamin C and are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
