Monanthes × sventenii nothovar. burchardii

Monanthes × sventenii nothovar. burchardii, commonly known as the Burchard's hybrid monanthes, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Monanthes sventenii and another related species.

It exhibits characteristics of both parent plants, often displaying unique floral morphology and coloration. The plant is typically found in tropical or subtropical regions where its parent species are native. It has a compact growth habit with lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
