Myrcianthes cavalcantei

Myrcianthes cavalcantei, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and dark red or black berries. The plant is evergreen, with glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas.

The fruit is an important food source for various bird species and is also used in traditional medicine.
