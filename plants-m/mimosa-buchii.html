Mimosa buchii

Mimosa buchii, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is often grown as an ornamental due to its delicate foliage and attractive blooms. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation in temperate climates.

Mimosa buchii is also known for its sensitive leaves that fold when touched, a trait that adds to its unique appeal.
