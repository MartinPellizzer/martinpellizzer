Moraea pilifolia

Moraea pilifolia, commonly known as the featherleaf glory flower, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Moraea pilifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique and colorful display.
