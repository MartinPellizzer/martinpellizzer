Maxillaria sillarensis

Maxillaria sillarensis, commonly known as the Sillares orchid, is a species native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a compact growth habit and produces fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically has a single pseudobulb with a few leaves and bears racemes of flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its flowers are characterized by their spurred petals and a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.

Maxillaria sillarensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
