Miconia urbaniana

Miconia urbaniana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various birds and animals. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and floral display.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
