Meriania urceolata

Meriania urceolata, commonly known as the cup-shaped meriania, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, urn-shaped flowers that are typically bright yellow or orange. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Meriania urceolata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
