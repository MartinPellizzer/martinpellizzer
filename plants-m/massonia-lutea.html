Massonia lutea

Massonia lutea, commonly known as the yellow massonia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, which is commonly referred to as the ice plant family. The plant is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
