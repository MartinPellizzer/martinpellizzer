Monardella villosa subsp. neglecta

Monardella villosa subsp.

neglecta, commonly known as the woolly larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It is a perennial herb that thrives in full sun and well-drained soils.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
