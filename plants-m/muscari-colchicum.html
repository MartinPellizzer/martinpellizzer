Muscari colchicum

Muscari colchicum, commonly known as the grape hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, resembling tiny grapes. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Muscari colchicum is known for its early spring blooming habit, often emerging before many other spring-flowering plants.

It is also valued for its fragrant blossoms and ability to naturalize over time.
