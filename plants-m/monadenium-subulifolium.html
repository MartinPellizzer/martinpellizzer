Monadenium subulifolium

Monadenium subulifolium, commonly known as the needle-leaved monadenium, is a small, succulent plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It features compact, rosette-forming growth with narrow, needle-like leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are adapted to store water, making it highly drought-tolerant.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.
