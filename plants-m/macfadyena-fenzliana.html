Macfadyena fenzliana

Macfadyena fenzliana, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous vine that produces striking yellow-orange tubular flowers, which bloom in summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
