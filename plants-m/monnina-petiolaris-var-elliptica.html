Monnina petiolaris var. elliptica

Monnina petiolaris var. elliptica, commonly known as the narrowleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive, often blue or purple flowers. The plant has slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its small, clustered flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
