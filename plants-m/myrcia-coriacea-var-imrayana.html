Myrcia coriacea var. imrayana

Myrcia coriacea var. imrayana, commonly known as the rough-leaved myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dark, leathery leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
