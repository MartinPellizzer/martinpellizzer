Momordica guttata

Momordica guttata, commonly known as the spotted cucumber or yellow vine, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and orange to red fruits that resemble small cucumbers. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces a bitter-tasting fruit that contains seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often grown as an ornamental or for its edible fruits.

Botanically, it belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like pumpkins and melons.
