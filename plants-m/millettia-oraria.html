Millettia oraria

Millettia oraria, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its drooping clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in summer, giving it its common name. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits containing flat, seed-like structures. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its potential in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
