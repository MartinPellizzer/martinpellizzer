Malacocarpus caespitosus

Malacocarpus caespitosus, commonly known as the clustered bladder nut, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain hard, seed-like structures called bladder nuts. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The botanical features of Malacocarpus caespitosus make it an interesting subject for study in tropical botany and plant ecology.
