Myrceugenia hamoniana

Myrceugenia hamoniana, commonly known as the yellow myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to southern Brazil and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and forested regions.

Myrceugenia hamoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
