Medicago reniformis

Medicago reniformis, commonly known as the sickle vetch, is a leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive kidney-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a sprawling growth habit and produces small, hairy pods containing edible seeds. It is adapted to thrive in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Medicago reniformis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
