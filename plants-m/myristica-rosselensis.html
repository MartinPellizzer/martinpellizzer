Myristica rosselensis

Myristica rosselensis, commonly known as the African nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and aromatic bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its most notable feature is the aromatic, oily seed kernel, which is used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
