Mesembryanthemum racemosum

Mesembryanthemum racemosum, commonly known as the clustered ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and coastal environments. Its ability to store water in its leaves allows it to survive in dry conditions.

Mesembryanthemum racemosum is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
