Miconia lealindeniana

Miconia lealindeniana, commonly known as the Brazilian star-miconia, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces berries that are initially green and turn red when mature, containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets make it an important component of the rainforest ecosystem.
