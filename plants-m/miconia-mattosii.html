Miconia mattosii

Miconia mattosii, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, often pink or red bracteate flowers. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

However, it can be invasive in some regions, posing ecological challenges.
