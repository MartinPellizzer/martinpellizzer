Melochia missionum

Melochia missionum, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from pink to red. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.

Melochia missionum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and vigorous growth habit.
