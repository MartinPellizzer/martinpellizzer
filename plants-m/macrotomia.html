Macrotomia

Macrotomia, commonly known as the giant false heather, is a member of the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or acidic soils, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are evergreen, leathery, and arranged in whorls around the stem.
