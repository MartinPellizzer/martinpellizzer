Macrolobium amplexans

Macrolobium amplexans, commonly known as the clinging leaf vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that often encircle the stems of other plants, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its rapid growth habit makes it a popular choice for ornamental use in warm climates.

However, it can become invasive if not properly managed.
