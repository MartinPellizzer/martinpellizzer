Mabea piriri var. concolor

Mabea piriri var. concolor, commonly known as the Brazilian white senna, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with alternating leaflets and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces slender pods that contain small, flat seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
