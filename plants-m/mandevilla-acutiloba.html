Mandevilla acutiloba

Mandevilla acutiloba, commonly known as the sharp-lobed mandevilla, is a tropical vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which features lance-shaped leaves with pointed lobes. The plant produces clusters of tubular, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil and full sun exposure.

Mandevilla acutiloba is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and vigorous growth habit.
