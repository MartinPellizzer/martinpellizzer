Mediolobivia aureiflora var. albilongiseta

Mediolobivia aureiflora var. albilongiseta, commonly known as the golden-flowered white-stemmed lobivia, is a rare and striking cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical stems that are covered in soft, white spines, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which bloom in clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable species in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
