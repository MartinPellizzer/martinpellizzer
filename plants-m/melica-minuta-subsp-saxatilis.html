Melica minuta subsp. saxatilis

Melica minuta subsp.

saxatilis, commonly known as the saxatile brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.

The plant features slender, tufted stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle, bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for invertebrates.
