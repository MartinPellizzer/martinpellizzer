Malpighia montecristensis

Malpighia montecristensis, commonly known as the Montecristi acorn or the white acorn, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malpighiaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Costa Rica and Panama. The plant is characterized by its small, red, fleshy fruits that resemble acorns, which are edible and have a tart, acidic flavor. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in shaded, moist forest environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has also been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
