Myriandra glauca

Myriandra glauca, commonly known as the blue myriandra, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color. It produces small white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring.

Myriandra glauca is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
