Mollugo sperguloides

Mollugo sperguloides, commonly known as the common mouse-ear or mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing annual herb belonging to the family Molluginaceae.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy, kidney-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, white or pink flowers. The plant typically thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in gardens, fields, and along roadsides. It has a shallow root system and is often used as a ground cover in some horticultural applications.

Mollugo sperguloides is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative means.
