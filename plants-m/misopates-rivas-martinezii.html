Misopates rivas-martinezii

Misopates rivas-martinezii, commonly known as the Canary Island orchid, is a rare and endangered orchid species native to the Canary Islands.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with delicate, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a short rhizome and produces a single, slender stem with a few leaves. Its flowers are characterized by their three-lobed petals and a distinctive, spurred lip.

Due to its limited habitat and threats from habitat loss, it is classified as critically endangered.
