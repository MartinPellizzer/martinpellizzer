Machaerium foliosum

Machaerium foliosum, commonly known as the blue gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with a striking blue or purple coloration, and its flowers are typically arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rainforests or along riverbanks.

Machaerium foliosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
