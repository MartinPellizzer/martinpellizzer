Mimetes parviflorus

Mimetes parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered mimic, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Mimetes parviflorus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in arid environments.
