Matricaria millefoliata

Matricaria millefoliata, commonly known as field chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellow button-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun. It blooms from late spring to early autumn, producing a strong, aromatic scent.

The leaves and flowers are often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for their calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
