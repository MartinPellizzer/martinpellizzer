Myrsine edulis

Myrsine edulis, commonly known as the edible myrsine, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrsinaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for both humans and animals. The berries contain small, hard seeds and are often used in local cuisines for their tart flavor.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and culinary contexts.
