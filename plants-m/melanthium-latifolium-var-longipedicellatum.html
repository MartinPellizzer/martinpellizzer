Melanthium latifolium var. longipedicellatum

Melanthium latifolium var. longipedicellatum, commonly known as the broadleaf meadowbeauty, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its flowers are borne on long pedicels, which is reflected in its specific epithet "longipedicellatum.".

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
