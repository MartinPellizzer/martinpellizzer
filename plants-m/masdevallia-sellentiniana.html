Masdevallia sellentiniana

Masdevallia sellentiniana, commonly known as the Orchid of the Andes, is a terrestrial orchid native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, strap-like leaves and its intricate, often fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique, often asymmetrical flower structure.

Due to its beauty and rarity, it is a popular specimen among orchid enthusiasts and botanists.
