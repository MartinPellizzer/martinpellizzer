Malus jarmolenkoi

Malus jarmolenkoi, commonly known as the Jarmolenko apple, is a rare and ancient species of apple native to the mountains of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that produces small, tart apples with a unique flavor profile. The plant has a compact growth habit and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and showy white flowers in early spring. It is highly valued for its ornamental qualities and genetic diversity, making it an important species in plant conservation efforts.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered endangered and requires specific environmental conditions to thrive.
