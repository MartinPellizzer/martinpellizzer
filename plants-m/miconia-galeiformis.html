Miconia galeiformis

Miconia galeiformis, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed raw or used in culinary applications. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
