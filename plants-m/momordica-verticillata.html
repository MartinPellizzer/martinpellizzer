Momordica verticillata

Momordica verticillata, commonly known as the African horned cucumber, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It produces distinctive, spiny, horn-shaped fruits that are edible when young. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and emits a strong, cucumber-like odor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its unique fruits and ornamental value.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
