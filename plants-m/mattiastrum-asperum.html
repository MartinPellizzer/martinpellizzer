Mattiastrum asperum

Mattiastrum asperum, commonly known as the rough mattiastrum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in moist garden environments.
