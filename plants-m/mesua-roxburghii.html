Mesua roxburghii

Mesua roxburghii, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release its seeds.

Mesua roxburghii is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in medicine and dye production.
