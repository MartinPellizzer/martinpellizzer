Myrsine martiana

Myrsine martiana, commonly known as the red-flowered myrsine, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrsinaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and striking red or pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Myrsine martiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitats.
