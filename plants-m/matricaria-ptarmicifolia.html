Matricaria ptarmicifolia

Matricaria ptarmicifolia, commonly known as the meadow chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers and finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its calming effects.

Matricaria ptarmicifolia is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
