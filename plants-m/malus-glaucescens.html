Malus glaucescens

Malus glaucescens, commonly known as the white apple tree, is a species of apple tree native to China.

It is characterized by its pale green to white bark and small, yellowish-green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The tree produces small, sour apples that are typically not eaten fresh but are used in cooking or fermentation. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

Malus glaucescens is relatively cold-hardy and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
