Myrcia proctorii

Myrcia proctorii, commonly known as the Proctor's myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it typically grows in moist, shaded areas of tropical rainforests. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Myrcia proctorii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has also been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
