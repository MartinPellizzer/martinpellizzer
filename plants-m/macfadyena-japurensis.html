Macfadyena japurensis

Macfadyena japurensis, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers with a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it an important plant in ecological gardens.
