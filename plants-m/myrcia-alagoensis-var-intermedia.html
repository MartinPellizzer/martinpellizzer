Myrcia alagoensis var. intermedia

Myrcia alagoensis var. intermedia, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of up to 6 meters. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, glossy dark green leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. It produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
