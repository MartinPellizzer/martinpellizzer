Muhlenbergia seatonii

Muhlenbergia seatonii, commonly known as the coastal muhlenbergia, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale blue flowers. It is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.

Muhlenbergia seatonii is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
