Miconia ablusa

Miconia ablusa, commonly known as the white miconia, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, round, purple or reddish fruits. Miconia ablusa is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural research.
