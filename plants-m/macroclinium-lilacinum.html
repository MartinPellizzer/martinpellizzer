Macroclinium lilacinum

Macroclinium lilacinum, commonly known as the lilac-flowered orchid, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet to lilac-colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture to retain moisture.

The flowers bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value and attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
