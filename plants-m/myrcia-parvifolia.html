Myrcia parvifolia

Myrcia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved myrtle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are usually elliptical, glossy, and have a strong, spicy scent.

Myrcia parvifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
