Moullava

Moullava, commonly known as *Piper betel*, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant is cultivated for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional cuisines and medicinal practices. Its rhizomes and leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive flavor and therapeutic properties.

Moullava thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight.
