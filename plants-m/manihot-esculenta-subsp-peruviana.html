Manihot esculenta subsp. peruviana

Manihot esculenta subsp.

peruviana, commonly known as the Andean arrowroot, is a tuberous plant native to the Andes region of South America. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its starchy, edible tubers that are used as a root vegetable.

The plant typically grows in high-altitude areas with cool, moist climates, and its leaves are lobed and have a distinctive appearance. The tubers are harvested for their high starch content and are used in various culinary applications.

It is also valued for its ornamental foliage and has some traditional medicinal uses in indigenous communities.
