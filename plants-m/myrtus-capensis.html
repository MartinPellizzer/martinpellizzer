Myrtus capensis

Myrtus capensis, commonly known as the common myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves and small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Myrtus capensis thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
