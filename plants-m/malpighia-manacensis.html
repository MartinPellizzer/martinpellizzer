Malpighia manacensis

Malpighia manacensis, commonly known as the Brazilian wolfberry, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its small, fleshy, red berries that are often used in local cuisines. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is rich in vitamin C and has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties.
