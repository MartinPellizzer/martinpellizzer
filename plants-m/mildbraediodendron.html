Mildbraediodendron

Mildbraediodendron, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the temperate regions of the southern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white or pale yellow, which bloom in spring. Its bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age, and it is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The silverleaf tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of garden settings.
