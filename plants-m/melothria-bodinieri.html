Melothria bodinieri

Melothria bodinieri, commonly known as the African horned melon, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It produces distinctive, spiky, greenish-yellow fruits that are typically eaten raw. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with deep lobes and hairy stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

The fruit is rich in nutrients and has a slightly tart, cucumber-like flavor.
