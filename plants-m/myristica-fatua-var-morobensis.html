Myristica fatua var. morobensis

Myristica fatua var. morobensis, commonly known as the Morobense nutmeg.

It is a member of the Myristicaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of the Indo-Pacific, particularly in the Solomon Islands and Papua New Guinea. The plant is a small evergreen tree or shrub that produces aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its fruits are encased in a hard, woody shell, and the seeds contain essential oils with medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
