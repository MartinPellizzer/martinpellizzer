Melothria capillacea

Melothria capillacea, commonly known as the small passionfruit or dwarf passionfruit, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are typically less than 2 centimeters in diameter. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its botanical features include tendrils for climbing and a distinctive, aromatic scent.

The fruit is edible, with a tart flavor and a thin, leathery skin.
