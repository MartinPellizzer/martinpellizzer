Melastoma lanatum

Melastoma lanatum, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
