Marginaria dielsii

Marginaria dielsii, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with a rosette of narrow, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, which bloom in the early morning. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in sandy soils.

Marginaria dielsii is adapted to extreme heat and drought, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
