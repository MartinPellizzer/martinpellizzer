Menotriche strigosa

Menotriche strigosa, commonly known as the hairy leek, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features basal leaves that are narrow, linear, and covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fibrous roots are often harvested for culinary use, particularly in traditional African cuisine.

Menotriche strigosa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and woodland margins.
