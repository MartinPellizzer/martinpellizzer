Miscanthus nakaianus

Miscanthus nakaianus, commonly known as the Nakaianus grass, is a perennial grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, silver-blue leaves that give it a striking appearance. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream edges.

Miscanthus nakaianus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
