Mutisia lechleri

Mutisia lechleri, commonly known as the Andean butter tree, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is notable for its latex, which has been traditionally used by indigenous communities for medicinal purposes. Its growth habit includes a tall, slender stem with a thick, woody base.

Mutisia lechleri thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.
