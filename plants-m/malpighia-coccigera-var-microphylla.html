Malpighia coccigera var. microphylla

Malpighia coccigera var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved acorn berry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malpighiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas, particularly Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible. The berries are often used in culinary applications due to their tart flavor and high vitamin C content.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
