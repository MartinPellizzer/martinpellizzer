Morus confinis

Morus confinis, commonly known as the Chinese mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and rapid growth rate. The tree produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It is often cultivated for its fibrous bark, which is used in the production of paper and textiles.

Additionally, it plays a role in traditional medicine and ecological restoration projects.
