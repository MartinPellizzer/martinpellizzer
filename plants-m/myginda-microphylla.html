Myginda microphylla

Myginda microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved myginda, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
