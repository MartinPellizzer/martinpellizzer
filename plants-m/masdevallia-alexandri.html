Masdevallia alexandri

Masdevallia alexandri, commonly known as the Alexander's masdevallia, is a rare and exquisite orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, often colorful flowers that feature a unique combination of sepals and petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that help it thrive in high-altitude environments.

Due to its rarity and striking appearance, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
