Manna hebraica

Manna hebraica, commonly known as the biblical manna, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of southern Europe.

It belongs to the genus Manna within the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes. The plant produces a sweet, sap-like substance called manna, which is exuded from its bark during certain times of the year. This substance has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

Its botanical features include small, trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring.
