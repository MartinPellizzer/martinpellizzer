Mentha subviridis

Mentha subviridis, commonly known as green mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue or violet, arranged in whorled clusters. Mentha subviridis is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine due to its aromatic properties and potential health benefits.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
