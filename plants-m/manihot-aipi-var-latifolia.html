Manihot aipi var. latifolia

Manihot aipi var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowleaf, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its roots are rich in starch and have been traditionally used for food and medicinal purposes.

The species is adapted to grow in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in warm, humid climates.
