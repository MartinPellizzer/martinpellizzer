Moringa rivae subsp. longisiliqua

Moringa rivae subsp.

longisiliqua, commonly known as the long-stalked moringa, is a variant of the moringa genus native to parts of East Africa. It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and robust, branched stems.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its nutritional properties and has potential applications in agriculture and traditional medicine.
