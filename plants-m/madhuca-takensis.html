Madhuca takensis

Madhuca takensis, commonly known as the Tunku's tallow tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Sapotaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of Borneo and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing a single seed. It is valued for its durable timber and is often found in secondary forests.

The plant plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining forest biodiversity.
