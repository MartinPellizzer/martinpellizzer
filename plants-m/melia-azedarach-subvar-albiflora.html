Melia azedarach subvar. albiflora

Melia azedarach subvar. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower chinaberry, is a deciduous tree native to the Middle East and parts of Asia.

It features showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by orange-red berries in the fall. The tree has a broad, spreading canopy and can grow up to 20 meters tall. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The wood is lightweight and has been traditionally used for construction and furniture.
