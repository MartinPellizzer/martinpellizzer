Myriocladus grandifolius

Myriocladus grandifolius, commonly known as the large-leaved myriocladus, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low rainfall and high temperatures.

Myriocladus grandifolius is also valued for its role in local ecosystems and traditional uses.
