Madia sativa subsp. typica

Madia sativa subsp.

typica, commonly known as the common madia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy stems.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
