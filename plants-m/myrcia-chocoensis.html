Myrcia chocoensis

Myrcia chocoensis, commonly known as Choco myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly in Colombia and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

Myrcia chocoensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
