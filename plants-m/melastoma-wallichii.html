Melastoma wallichii

Melastoma wallichii, commonly known as the purple-stemmed melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its attractive, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a glossy appearance.

Melastoma wallichii is valued not only for its ornamental qualities but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
