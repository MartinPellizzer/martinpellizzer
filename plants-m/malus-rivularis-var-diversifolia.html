Malus rivularis var. diversifolia

Malus rivularis var. diversifolia, commonly known as the river crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender branches and clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a distinctive variation in leaf shape and color, giving it the species name *diversifolia*. It produces small, yellowish fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in woodland areas.
