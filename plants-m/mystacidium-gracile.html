Mystacidium gracile

Mystacidium gracile, commonly known as the slender mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that thrives on a variety of host trees.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces tiny, white or yellowish flowers that are often clustered together. The plant's berries are typically red or orange and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds. It is native to various regions in the Americas and is often found in forested areas.
