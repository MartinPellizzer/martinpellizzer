Muraltia empleuridioides

Muraltia empleuridioides, commonly known as the Brazilian acacia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Muraltia empleuridioides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for ecological restoration projects.
