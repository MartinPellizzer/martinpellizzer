Memecylanthus neocaledonicus

Memecylanthus neocaledonicus, commonly known as the New Caledonia bottlebrush, is a unique flowering plant native to the island of New Caledonia.

It is characterized by its striking, cylindrical inflorescences that resemble a bottlebrush, composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant is evergreen, with lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal or mountainous areas.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
