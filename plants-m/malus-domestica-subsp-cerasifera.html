Malus domestica subsp. cerasifera

Malus domestica subsp.

cerasifera, commonly known as the sweet cherry, is a deciduous tree native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and showy pink or white flowers that appear in spring.

The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe with a sweet, juicy pulp and a hard pit. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This subspecies is widely cultivated for its fruit, which is used in both fresh consumption and processing.
