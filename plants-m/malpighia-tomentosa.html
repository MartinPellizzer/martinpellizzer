Malpighia tomentosa

Malpighia tomentosa, commonly known as the Brazilian wolfberry or pitaya, is a small evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of bright red, fleshy fruits that resemble small berries. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

The fruit is rich in vitamin C and has a sweet, tangy flavor, making it a popular fruit in local cuisines.
