Maprounea africana var. africana

Maprounea africana var. africana, commonly known as African mahogany, is a tropical tree native to western and central Africa.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are typically alternate and have a smooth, glossy surface. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, followed by woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds. Its bark is thick and deeply fissured, and it is valued for its durable, aromatic wood.

The plant is often found in moist lowland forests and is an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
