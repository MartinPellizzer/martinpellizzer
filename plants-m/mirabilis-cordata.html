Mirabilis cordata

Mirabilis cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf four o'clock, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in the late afternoon. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which open in response to sunlight. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Mirabilis cordata is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
