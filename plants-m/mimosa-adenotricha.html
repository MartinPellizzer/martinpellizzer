Mimosa adenotricha

Mimosa adenotricha, commonly known as the hairy mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant produces small, spherical seed pods that split open when mature, releasing seeds with tufts of hair for wind dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Mimosa adenotricha is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas.
