Mimulus subulatus

Mimulus subulatus, commonly known as the needleleaf monkeyflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features slender, needle-like leaves and produces bright orange or yellow tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as streambanks and meadows. It is part of the Physostegia genus within the family Plantaginaceae.

Mimulus subulatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
