Magnolia cathcartii

Magnolia cathcartii, commonly known as the Cathcart magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has smooth, grayish bark and elliptical, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its showy floral display.
