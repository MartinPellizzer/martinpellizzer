Melica altissima

Melica altissima, commonly known as tall wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist environments such as meadows, wetlands, and riverbanks.

Botanically, it is noted for its rhizomatous growth habit and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
