Medicago echinus subsp. ciliaris

Medicago echinus subsp.

ciliaris, commonly known as the hairy vetch, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow pea-like flowers.

The plant is native to Europe, North Africa, and western Asia, and it thrives in a variety of soil types. It is often used as a cover crop due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

The subspecies ciliaris is distinguished by its hairy stems and leaves with distinctive ciliate margins.
