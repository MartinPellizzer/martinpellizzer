Milium effusum var. latifrons

Milium effusum var. latifrons, commonly known as broad-fruited millet, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing clumps and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, white to pale green flowers. Its seeds are often used as a grain in traditional cuisines, and it is valued for its ornamental appearance in gardens.

Milium effusum var. latifrons is also appreciated for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its low maintenance requirements.
