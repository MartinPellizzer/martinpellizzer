Myrcia pauciflora

Myrcia pauciflora, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round, red berries that are edible but often used in local traditional medicine. Myrcia pauciflora thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some medicinal properties according to traditional use.
