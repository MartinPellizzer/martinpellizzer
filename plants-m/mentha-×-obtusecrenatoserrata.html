Mentha × obtusecrenatoserrata

Mentha × obtusecrenatoserrata, commonly known as the broad-leaf mint, is a hybrid mint species that exhibits a combination of traits from its parent species.

It is characterized by its large, serrated leaves that are often rounded at the base, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its fragrant leaves emit a strong mint aroma, which is useful for both culinary and medicinal purposes.

Mentha × obtusecrenatoserrata is native to parts of Asia and is often cultivated for its aromatic properties and ornamental value.
