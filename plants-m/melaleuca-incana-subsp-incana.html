Melaleuca incana subsp. incana

Melaleuca incana subsp.

incana, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, white-woolly bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
