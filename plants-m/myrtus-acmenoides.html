Myrtus acmenoides

Myrtus acmenoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, with a strong aromatic scent. Its small, white or pale pink flowers appear in clusters during the spring and summer months.

The fruit is a small, round capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
