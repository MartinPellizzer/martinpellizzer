Mammillaria lasiacantha subsp. egregia

Mammillaria lasiacantha subsp.

egregia, commonly known as the Mexican hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines and small, rounded tubercles.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and succulent collections.
