Myrsine richmondensis

Myrsine richmondensis, commonly known as the Richmond myrsine, is a shrub belonging to the Primulaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are small, clustered, and usually white or pale pink, while the fruit is a small, fleshy berry.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
