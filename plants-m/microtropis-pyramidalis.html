Microtropis pyramidalis

Microtropis pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramid vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
