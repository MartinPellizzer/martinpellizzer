Microgramma fuscopunctata

Microgramma fuscopunctata, commonly known as the spotted maidenhair fern, is a delicate evergreen fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features intricate, finely divided fronds with a distinctive dark green color and small, dark spots that give it its common name. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread slowly and form dense clumps. Its leaves are typically leathery and have a feathery appearance, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
