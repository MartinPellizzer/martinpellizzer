Melo flexuosus

Melo flexuosus, commonly known as the flexible melon, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers, pumpkins, and gourds. The plant produces large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that give way to elongated, edible fruits. Its flexible stems allow it to trail along the ground or climb supports, making it adaptable to various growing conditions.

The fruit is typically harvested when it turns a deep yellow or orange, indicating maturity.
