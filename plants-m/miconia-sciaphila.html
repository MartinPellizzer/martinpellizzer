Miconia sciaphila

Miconia sciaphila, commonly known as the shade-philic miconia, is a species of flowering plant in the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to the understory of tropical rainforests in Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. It thrives in low-light conditions, making it well adapted to dense forest environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in shaded gardens.
