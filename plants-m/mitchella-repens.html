Mitchella repens

Mitchella repens, commonly known as trailing arbutus, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, fragrant, white or pinkish flowers. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves that are glossy and arranged opposite along the stems. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and heathlands.

Mitchella repens is also known for its historical use in herbal medicine and as a source of fragrant essential oils.
