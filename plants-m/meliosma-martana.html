Meliosma martana

Meliosma martana, commonly known as the Indian nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Urticaceae.

It is native to parts of Asia, including India, Nepal, and China, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. It is often found in forest understories and is known for its stinging hairs on the leaves and stems, similar to other members of the Urticaceae family.

Meliosma martana is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
