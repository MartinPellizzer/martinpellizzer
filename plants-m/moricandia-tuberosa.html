Moricandia tuberosa

Moricandia tuberosa, commonly known as the tuberous moricandia, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. Its root system produces tubers that are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Moricandia tuberosa thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.
