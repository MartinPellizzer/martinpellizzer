Machilus longisepalus

Machilus longisepalus, commonly known as the long-sepaled woodbine, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to China, where it typically grows in moist, shaded areas of forests. The plant is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy on top. Its small, fragrant flowers are clustered in umbels and bloom in the spring.

The fruit is a dark purple drupe, containing a single seed, and it plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing food for various wildlife.
