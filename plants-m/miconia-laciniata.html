Miconia laciniata

Miconia laciniata, commonly known as the Brazilian shield fern, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmately divided leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in diameter. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered an invasive species in some regions.
