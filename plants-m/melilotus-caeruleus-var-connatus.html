Melilotus caeruleus var. connatus

Melilotus caeruleus var. connatus, commonly known as the blue sweet clover, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and as a forage crop for livestock.
