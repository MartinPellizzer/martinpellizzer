Medicago orbicularis f. macrocarpa

Medicago orbicularis f. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-seeded sainfoin, is a variant of the sainfoin plant characterized by its distinctive large, round seeds.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, and the flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in racemes.

This variety is valued for its forage qualities and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
