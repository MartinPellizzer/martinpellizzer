Monardella tomentosa

Monardella tomentosa, commonly known as wooly lippia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its fuzzy, silver-green leaves and showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Monardella tomentosa is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
