Misanteca brasiliensis

Misanteca brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and riverbanks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.
