Muhlenbergia involuta

Muhlenbergia involuta, commonly known as the silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and tufted inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established. Its fine, grass-like leaves are often used in floral arrangements due to their ornamental appeal.

Muhlenbergia involuta is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
