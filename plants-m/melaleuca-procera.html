Melaleuca procera

Melaleuca procera, commonly known as the tall honeymyrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a tall shrub or small tree that can grow up to 8 meters in height, characterized by its greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, which are followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Melaleuca procera is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in indigenous medicine.
