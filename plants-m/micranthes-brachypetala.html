Micranthes brachypetala

Micranthes brachypetala, commonly known as the short-petaled sand verbena, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a compact growth habit.

Micranthes brachypetala is adapted to drought conditions and is often found in open habitats such as plains and rocky areas.
