Mentha × brutteletii

Mentha × brutteletii, commonly known as the hybrid mint, is a cross between spearmint (Mentha spicata) and watermint (Mentha aquatica).

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or pink, which bloom in late summer.

Mentha × brutteletii is valued for its aromatic leaves and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
