Mycelis glandulosa

Mycelis glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular mycelium, is a unique plant species characterized by its intricate mycelial network and specialized glandular structures.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forest floors where it forms symbiotic relationships with various fungi. The plant's leaves are typically small and hairy, with a dense arrangement of glandular hairs that secrete substances for defense. Its reproductive structures are subterranean, producing spores that disperse through wind and water.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Mycelis glandulosa is considered a conservation concern in many regions.
