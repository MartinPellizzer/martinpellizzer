Maxillaria bicentenaria

Maxillaria bicentenaria, commonly known as the bicentennial orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its intricate, fragrant flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid with pseudobulbs and linear, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow with purple or brown markings, and it produces a long, slender inflorescence.

Maxillaria bicentenaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
