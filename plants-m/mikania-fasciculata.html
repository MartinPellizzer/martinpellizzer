Mikania fasciculata

Mikania fasciculata, commonly known as Brazilian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a vigorous vine that can grow up to 10 meters in length, with heart-shaped leaves and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
