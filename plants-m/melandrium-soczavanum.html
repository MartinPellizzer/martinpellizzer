Melandrium soczavanum

Melandrium soczavanum, commonly known as the Soczavan sand-verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and supports local pollinator populations.
