Melocactus roseus

Melocactus roseus, commonly known as the roseate melon, is a cacti species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem covered in bright red spines, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in the spring or early summer. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Melocactus roseus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
