Myrcia neograndis

Myrcia neograndis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its lanceolate, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, red drupe that contains a single seed, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Myrcia neograndis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
