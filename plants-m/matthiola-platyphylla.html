Matthiola platyphylla

Matthiola platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaf stock, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and borders.

Matthiola platyphylla is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
