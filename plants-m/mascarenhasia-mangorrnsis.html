Mascarenhasia mangorrnsis

Mascarenhasia mangorrnsis, commonly known as the Mascarene dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Mascarene Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in coastal and sandy environments. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers with a distinctive ray floret. It is adapted to arid conditions and has a deep root system for water retention.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and limited distribution.
