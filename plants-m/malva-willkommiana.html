Malva willkommiana

Malva willkommiana, commonly known as the willkomm's mallows, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. The plant features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are stout and somewhat hairy.

Malva willkommiana is often grown as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and ecological benefits.
