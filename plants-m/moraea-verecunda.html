Moraea verecunda

Moraea verecunda, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky flower clusters that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow, creating a dramatic visual impact. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Moraea verecunda is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
