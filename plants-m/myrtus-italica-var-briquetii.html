Myrtus italica var. briquetii

Myrtus italica var. briquetii, commonly known as the Briquet's myrtle, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are dark green and slightly aromatic. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, which are followed by red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its ornamental value and historical significance in Mediterranean cultures add to its botanical interest.
