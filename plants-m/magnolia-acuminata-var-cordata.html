Magnolia acuminata var. cordata

Magnolia acuminata var. cordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically 6 to 12 inches long. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in early spring before the leaves emerge. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often peeling in thin layers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
