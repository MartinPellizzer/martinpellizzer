Macfadyena simplicifolia

Macfadyena simplicifolia, commonly known as the Crown of Thorns, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is a climbing vine that produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it drought-tolerant. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters and bloom during the warmer months.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
