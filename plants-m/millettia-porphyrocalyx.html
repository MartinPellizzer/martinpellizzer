Millettia porphyrocalyx

Millettia porphyrocalyx, commonly known as the red-stemmed legume, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red stems and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, followed by elongated, woody pods containing lens-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and potential ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
