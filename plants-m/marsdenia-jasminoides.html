Marsdenia jasminoides

Marsdenia jasminoides, commonly known as the yellow star jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine that produces fragrant, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to white. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are typically followed by small, berry-like fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in landscaping and as a ground cover.
