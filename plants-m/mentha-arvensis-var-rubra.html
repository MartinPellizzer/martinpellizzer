Mentha arvensis var. rubra

Mentha arvensis var. rubra, commonly known as red mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are typically a deep green with a reddish underside. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, blooming in late summer. Red mint is known for its strong, minty aroma and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
