Monstera glaucescens

Monstera glaucescens, commonly known as the Variegated Monstera, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with cream and green patterns. The plant has a climbing habit and produces aerial roots that help it anchor to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are typically glossy and have a waxy texture, which helps reduce water loss in humid environments.

Monstera glaucescens thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.
