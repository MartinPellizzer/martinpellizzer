Myrcia grandis

Myrcia grandis, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a glossy dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The flowers are small, fragrant, and borne in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species.
