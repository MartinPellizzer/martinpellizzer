Melanthera aspera var. glabriuscula

Melanthera aspera var. glabriuscula, commonly known as the smooth-hairy melanthera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, clustered flowers are usually white or pale pink, appearing in late spring to early summer.

It is noted for its hairy texture and is sometimes used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.
