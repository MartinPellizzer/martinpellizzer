Mimosa lamolina

Mimosa lamolina, commonly known as the yellow mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and small, yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces seed pods that split open when mature, releasing tiny seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The leaves are sensitive to touch, folding shut when disturbed, a trait that adds to its intriguing botanical appeal.
