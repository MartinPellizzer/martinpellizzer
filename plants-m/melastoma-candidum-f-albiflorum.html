Melastoma candidum f. albiflorum

Melastoma candidum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its compact shrub form. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, white or pale pink flowers clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
