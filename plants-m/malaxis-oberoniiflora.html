Malaxis oberoniiflora

Malaxis oberoniiflora, commonly known as the Oberon's orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its leaves are usually elongated and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native humid environment.

The flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to pale pink, making it a visually striking species.
