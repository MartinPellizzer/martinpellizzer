Maranta oppenheimiana

Maranta oppenheimiana, commonly known as the night blooming arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves that often display vibrant, variegated patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that open at night, earning it its common name. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Maranta oppenheimiana is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and unique flowering behavior.
