Macropsidium rubrum

Macropsidium rubrum, commonly known as the red-stemmed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually green, with a reddish stem that becomes more prominent as the plant matures. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are pollinated by insects. Its berries are typically red or orange and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Macropsidium rubrum is often found in tropical and subtropical regions where it thrives on a variety of host trees.
