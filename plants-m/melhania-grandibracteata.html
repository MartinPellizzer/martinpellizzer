Melhania grandibracteata

Melhania grandibracteata, commonly known as the giant bract melhania, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy bracts that resemble petals, often in vibrant red or orange hues. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. Its structure includes palmate leaves and clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers.

Melhania grandibracteata thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
