Mariscus cylindricus var. australis

Mariscus cylindricus var. australis, commonly known as the Australian cylindrical mariscus, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant prefers well-drained sandy soils and full sun exposure, making it well adapted to arid environments. It is often found in coastal regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
