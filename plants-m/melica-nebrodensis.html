Melica nebrodensis

Melica nebrodensis, commonly known as the Nebrod Mountain grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky and limestone soils, often in mountainous areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
