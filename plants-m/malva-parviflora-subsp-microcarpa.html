Malva parviflora subsp. microcarpa

Malva parviflora subsp.

microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited mallows, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region. It features hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruit is a capsule containing numerous tiny seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soil and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
