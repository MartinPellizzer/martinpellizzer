Mitrephora maingayi var. kurzii

Mitrephora maingayi var. kurzii, commonly known as the Kudzu vine, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a serrated edge. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain environments.
