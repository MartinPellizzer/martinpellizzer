Mesosphaerum pedalipes

Mesosphaerum pedalipes, commonly known as the footed globular thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spherical, spiny head of flowers, which resembles a globular shape. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs and may have a bluish or grayish tint.

Mesosphaerum pedalipes is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in harsh environments.
