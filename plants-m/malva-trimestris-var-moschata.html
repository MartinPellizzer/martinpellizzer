Malva trimestris var. moschata

Malva trimestris var. moschata, commonly known as the musk mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, heart-shaped leaves and showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden settings. Its fragrance is subtle and often described as musky, which distinguishes it from other mallows.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
