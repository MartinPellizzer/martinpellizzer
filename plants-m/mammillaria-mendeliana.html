Mammillaria mendeliana

Mammillaria mendeliana, commonly known as the Mendel's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a ribbed stem and a dense covering of white, woolly hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
