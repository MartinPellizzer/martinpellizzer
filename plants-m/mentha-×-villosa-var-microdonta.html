Mentha × villosa var. microdonta

Mentha × villosa var. microdonta, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and aromatic, slightly menthol-like fragrance. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with square stems and opposite, lance-shaped foliage. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of aromatic compounds.
