Mentha × rubra var. crepiniana

Mentha × rubra var. crepiniana, commonly known as the red mint, is a hybrid species within the Mentha genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-purple stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, where it thrives in moist, shady environments. It produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

This variety is often used in culinary and aromatic applications due to its strong, minty fragrance.
