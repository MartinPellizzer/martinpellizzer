Microcalamus convallarioides

Microcalamus convallarioides, commonly known as the false hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and spadix inflorescence. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are often hidden within a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
