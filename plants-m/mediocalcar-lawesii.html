Mediocalcar lawesii

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive floral structure, featuring showy, spurred flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows on rocky outcrops and in shaded, moist environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette, while its roots are adapted to nutrient-poor substrates.
