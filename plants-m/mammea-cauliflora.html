Mammea cauliflora

Mammea cauliflora, commonly known as the calico tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall. The plant is characterized by its cauliflorous flowering pattern, where flowers and fruits develop directly on the trunk and branches. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

The fruit is a spherical, fleshy berry that contains several seeds, making it an important food source for various wildlife species.
