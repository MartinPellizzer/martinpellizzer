Mespilus monogyna var. pinnatifida

Mespilus monogyna var. pinnatifida, commonly known as the European whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that appear in clusters, followed by edible, yellowish fruits called bullace. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive pinnate venation, giving it its varietal name. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and hedgerows.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional medicine.
