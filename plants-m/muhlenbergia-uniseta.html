Muhlenbergia uniseta

Muhlenbergia uniseta, commonly known as the narrowleaf muhlenbergia, is a grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often mistaken for cotton or dandelion fluff, adding a soft texture to the landscape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.

Muhlenbergia uniseta is also valued for its low maintenance requirements and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
