Mentha arvensis f. deflexa

Mentha arvensis f. deflexa, commonly known as field mint or creeping mint, is a variant of the broad-leaf mint species.

It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit and aromatic leaves that emit a strong mint scent. The plant typically features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue or purple, arranged in clusters.

This subspecies is often found in moist soils in meadows, along stream banks, and in disturbed areas.
