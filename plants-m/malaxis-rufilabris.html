Malaxis rufilabris

Malaxis rufilabris, commonly known as the red-lipped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, hairy pseudobulbs and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, red-tipped flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, making it a striking ornamental species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its aesthetic appeal and limited natural habitat, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
