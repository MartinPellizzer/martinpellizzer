Monandraira berteroniana

Monandraira berteroniana, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the nutrient-poor soils of its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
