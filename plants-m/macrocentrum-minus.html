Macrocentrum minus

Macrocentrum minus, commonly known as the lesser water lily, is a aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds and lakes. The plant features broad, floating leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. Its flowers are small and yellow, appearing above the water surface.

Macrocentrum minus plays a role in providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates and serving as a food source for waterfowl.
