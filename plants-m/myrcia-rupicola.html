Myrcia rupicola

Myrcia rupicola, commonly known as the rock myrtle, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature.

Myrcia rupicola thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.
