Mentha gentilis var. elliptica

Mentha gentilis var. elliptica, commonly known as the broad-leaf mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves that are often slightly hairy and have a serrated edge. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
