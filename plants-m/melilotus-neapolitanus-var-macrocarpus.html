Melilotus neapolitanus var. macrocarpus

Melilotus neapolitanus var. macrocarpus, commonly known as the large-seeded sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in dense, spherical clusters. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its aromatic properties make it valuable in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
