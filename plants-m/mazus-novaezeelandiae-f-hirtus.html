Mazus novaezeelandiae f. hirtus

Mazus novaezeelandiae f. hirtus, commonly known as the hairy New Zealand mazus, is a low-growing perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It features small, ovate leaves covered in fine, soft hairs, which give it its "hirtus" designation. The plant produces clusters of pale blue to violet flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Geraniaceae family.
