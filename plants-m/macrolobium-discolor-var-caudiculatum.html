Macrolobium discolor var. caudiculatum

Macrolobium discolor var. caudiculatum, commonly known as the yellow bell or yellow gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow, bell-shaped flowers and elongated, ribbed fruits that mature to a dark green or orange hue. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with tendrils that help it ascend support structures. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated for its edible fruits in certain regions.
