Muscari cycladicum

Muscari cycladicum, commonly known as the Cycladic bluebells, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to purple, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in rocky or coastal areas, where it adapts well to sunny to partially shaded conditions.

Muscari cycladicum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate.
