Mesua myrtifolia

Mesua myrtifolia, commonly known as the Indian rose or wild rose, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling roses. The plant has elliptical, glossy leaves and produces small, yellowish fruits that contain seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Mesua myrtifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is adaptable to a range of tropical and subtropical climates.
