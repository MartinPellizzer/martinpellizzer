Musa × sapientum var. putian

Musa × sapientum var. putian, commonly known as the Putian banana, is a variety of the common banana plant native to southern China.

It is characterized by its large, elongated, and slightly curved banana fruit, which is typically yellow when ripe. The plant produces a pseudostem made of tightly packed leaves, and the fruit develops from a flowering spike that emerges from the center of the plant. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its sweet flavor and is often cultivated for both local consumption and commercial purposes.
