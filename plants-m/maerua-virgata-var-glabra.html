Maerua virgata var. glabra

Maerua virgata var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth maerua, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. It is well adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in poor soil, making it a hardy species.

Its ecological importance includes providing shelter and food for various animals in its native habitat.
