Metazanthus

Metazanthus, commonly known as the fairy lantern, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that resemble small lanterns. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its blooming period typically occurs during the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds.

Metazanthus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
