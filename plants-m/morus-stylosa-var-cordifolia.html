Morus stylosa var. cordifolia

Morus stylosa var. cordifolia, commonly known as the heart-leaf mulberry, is a variant of the mulberry tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically glossy and have a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in traditional medicine.
