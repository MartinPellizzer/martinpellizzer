Mesua kochummeniana

Mesua kochummeniana, commonly known as the Malabar flame of the forest, is a deciduous tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a vibrant display during the flowering season. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry deciduous forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
