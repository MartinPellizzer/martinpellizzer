Matthiola tristis var. thessala

Matthiola tristis var. thessala, commonly known as the Balkan Wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers range in color from white to pale yellow, and it is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its resilience and beauty.
