Myosotis olympica

Myosotis olympica, commonly known as the Olympic forget-me-not, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs.

Myosotis olympica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
