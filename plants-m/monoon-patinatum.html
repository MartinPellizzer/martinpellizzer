Monoon patinatum

Monoon patinatum, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. The plant is often found in tropical rainforests and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its fruit is a woody pod that splits open to release seeds, and it has a long history of use in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of Monoon patinatum make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
