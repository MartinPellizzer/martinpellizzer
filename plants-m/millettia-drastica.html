Millettia drastica

Millettia drastica, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The tree produces flat, bean-like pods that contain small, round seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.

The plant also plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
