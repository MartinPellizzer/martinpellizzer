Mecodium apiculatum

Mecodium apiculatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mecodium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically occur in clusters, displaying shades of purple or pink.
