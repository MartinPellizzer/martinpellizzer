Mimosa misera

Mimosa misera, commonly known as the weeping mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery leaves that resemble the soft, drooping branches of its name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its sensitivity to environmental stress, it requires careful cultivation to flourish.
