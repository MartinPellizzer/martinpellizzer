Medicago polymorpha var. tricycla

Medicago polymorpha var. tricycla, commonly known as the three-lobed goat's rue, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with three distinct lobes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, and it is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is a perennial herb that can thrive in dry and sunny conditions, making it adaptable to various arid environments.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and of interest in traditional herbal practices.
