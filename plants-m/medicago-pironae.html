Medicago pironae

Medicago pironae, commonly known as the Piron's vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its root system has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, which benefits soil fertility.

Medicago pironae is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and adaptability.
