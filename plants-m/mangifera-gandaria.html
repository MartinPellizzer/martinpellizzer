Mangifera gandaria

Mangifera gandaria, commonly known as the wild mango, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically smaller and less sweet than cultivated mangoes. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The tree is often found in forested areas and is valued for its fruit and ornamental features.
