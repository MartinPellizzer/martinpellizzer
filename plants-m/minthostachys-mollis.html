Minthostachys mollis

Minthostachys mollis, commonly known as the slender mint, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves and small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its fragrant herbs and medicinal properties. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of forests.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their cooling and soothing effects.
