Mentha longifolia var. moldovica

Mentha longifolia var. moldovica, commonly known as Moldovan mint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a distinctive mint-like fragrance. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its refreshing scent and potential health benefits.
