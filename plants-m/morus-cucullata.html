Morus cucullata

Morus cucullata, commonly known as the Indian mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, fluffy, pale pink flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, black, edible berries that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.

The tree also has historical significance in traditional medicine and agriculture.
