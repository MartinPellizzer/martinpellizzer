Monarda rugosa

Monarda rugosa, commonly known as scarlet bee balm, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its showy, tubular red or pink flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.

Its aromatic foliage and vibrant blooms contribute to both its ecological and ornamental value.
