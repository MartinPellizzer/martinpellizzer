Micromelum tephrocarpum

Micromelum tephrocarpum, commonly known as the black tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces edible, dark purple fruits that are rich in tannins and used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include smooth, greyish bark and a compact, rounded canopy.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
