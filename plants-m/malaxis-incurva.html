Malaxis incurva

Malaxis incurva, commonly known as the bent-lobed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a single raceme of flowers.

Malaxis incurva is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
