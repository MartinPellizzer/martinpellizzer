Malacothrix clevelandii var. stebbinsii

Malacothrix clevelandii var. stebbinsii, commonly known as the Cleveland's malacothrix, is a flowering plant native to California.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat. It has slender, upright stems and narrow, gray-green leaves.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
