Mitrephora diversifolia

Mitrephora diversifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf mitrephora, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes cashew and mango. The plant is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, spherical fruits that are sweet and juicy.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in traditional medicine.
