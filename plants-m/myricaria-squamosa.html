Myricaria squamosa

Myricaria squamosa, commonly known as the squamosa myricaceae, is a flowering plant belonging to the Myricaceae family.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, particularly in regions with saline soils. The plant features small, scale-like leaves that are densely packed, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, dry fruits.

Myricaria squamosa is adapted to withstand drought and high salinity, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
