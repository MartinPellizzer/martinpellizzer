Marrubium marrubiastrum

Marrubium marrubiastrum, commonly known as greater horehound, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils and thrives in sunny environments. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves and produces small, blue or purple flowers in clusters. It is often found in open woodlands, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Marrubium marrubiastrum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in herbal remedies.
