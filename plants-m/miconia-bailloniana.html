Miconia bailloniana

Miconia bailloniana, commonly known as the Baillon's miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in clusters, typically in shades of red, pink, or purple. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Miconia bailloniana is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
