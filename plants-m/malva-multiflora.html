Malva multiflora

Malva multiflora, commonly known as the Chinese mallow, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other well-known species like cotton and hibiscus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Malva multiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
