Mimosa atakta

Mimosa atakta, commonly known as the sensitive plant, is a species native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring. Its growth habit is herbaceous, with a branching stem that can reach up to 1 meter in height.

Mimosa atakta is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
