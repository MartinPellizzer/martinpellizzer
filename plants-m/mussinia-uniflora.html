Mussinia uniflora

Mussinia uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower moss campion, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in clusters of one or two. Its leaves are narrow, fleshy, and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it conserve moisture in its arid habitat.

Mussinia uniflora is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in rocky or well-drained soils.
