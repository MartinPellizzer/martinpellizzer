Manihot macrocarpa

Manihot macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant arrowleaf, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, spiny capsules containing seeds. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
