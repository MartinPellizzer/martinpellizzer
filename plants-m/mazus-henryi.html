Mazus henryi

Mazus henryi, commonly known as Henry's mazus, is a low-growing perennial plant native to China.

It features fleshy, ovate leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Mazus henryi is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and compact growth habit.
