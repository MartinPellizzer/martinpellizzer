Myrtus globosa

Myrtus globosa, commonly known as the common myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, oval, dark green leaves that are often fragrant and have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, round, blue-black berries. Myrtus globosa is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine and perfumery.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
