Mucuna urens

Mucuna urens, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a tropical leguminous plant characterized by its coiled, serpentine pods and trifoliate leaves.

It is native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas, and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. Its seeds contain high levels of protein and are used in traditional medicine and as a source of animal feed.

The plant also has allelopathic properties, which can inhibit the growth of other plants nearby.
