Machilus cicatricosus

Machilus cicatricosus, commonly known as the scarred laurel, is a flowering plant native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a fragrant scent that attracts pollinators.

The fruit is a small, woody drupe that matures to a dark color, containing a single seed.
