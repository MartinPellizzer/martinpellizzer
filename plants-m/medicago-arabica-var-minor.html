Medicago arabica var. minor

Medicago arabica var. minor, commonly known as lesser medick, is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features trifoliate leaves with stipules and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and is often found in disturbed soils, roadsides, and dry meadows. It is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen and is used in forage and ecological restoration.

Its botanical structure supports its role in soil stabilization and biodiversity enhancement.
