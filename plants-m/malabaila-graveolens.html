Malabaila graveolens

Malabaila graveolens, commonly known as the sweet senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet having a serrated edge.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional remedies.
