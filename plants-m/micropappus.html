Micropappus

Micropappus, commonly known as the desert star or goatweed, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers and the presence of a pappus, a structure that aids in wind dispersal of its seeds. These plants are typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in sandy or rocky soils. Micropappus species often have hairy stems and leaves, which help reduce water loss in dry environments.

They play a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects and animals.
