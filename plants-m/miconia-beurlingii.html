Miconia beurlingii

Miconia beurlingii, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pink miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when mature.

Miconia beurlingii is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.
