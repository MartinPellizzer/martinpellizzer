Mentha longifolia var. cesarea

Mentha longifolia var. cesarea, commonly known as lemon mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are aromatic and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves and flowers are used in culinary and medicinal applications due to their refreshing, citrus-like fragrance.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of essential oils.
