Muraltia sulcata

Muraltia sulcata, commonly known as the African sweet pepper or sour plum, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, ornamental flowers that range from white to pink. The plant produces small, round fruits that start green and ripen to a bright red or orange color. Muraltia sulcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens.

Its fruits are also used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent in some cuisines.
