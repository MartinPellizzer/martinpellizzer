Medinilla interiaciens

Medinilla interiaciens, commonly known as the velvet tangerine.

It is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that resemble tangerines. The plant produces fragrant, orange-colored flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a waxy texture. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Medinilla interiaciens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and unique foliage.
