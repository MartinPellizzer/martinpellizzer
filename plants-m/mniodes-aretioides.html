Mniodes aretioides

Mniodes aretioides, commonly known as the desert star cactus, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of South America.

It features a short, cylindrical stem with distinctive star-shaped spines and fleshy, ribbed structures that store water. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the rainy season, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Its unique morphology and adaptability to harsh conditions make it a notable species in botanical studies.
