Mimulus clementinus

Mimulus clementinus, commonly known as the Clementine monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bright orange to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in open woodlands. It has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy coating.

Mimulus clementinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
