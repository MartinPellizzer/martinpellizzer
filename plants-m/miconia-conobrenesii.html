Miconia conobrenesii

Miconia conobrenesii, commonly known as the red miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green with a reddish underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Miconia conobrenesii is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and continuous flowering.
