Myrmecophila humboldtii

Myrmecophila humboldtii, commonly known as the Humboldt's myrmecophyte, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful inflorescences that attract pollinators. The plant is notable for its unique relationship with ants, providing them with shelter and food in exchange for protection against herbivores. Its rosette-shaped leaves form a central pool that collects rainwater, creating a microhabitat for various small organisms.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native ecosystems.
