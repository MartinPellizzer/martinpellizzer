Morisonia verrucosa

Morisonia verrucosa, commonly known as the waxy beggar's-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a waxy coating on the petals. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive waxy texture.

Morisonia verrucosa is known for its ability to thrive in humid climates and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
