Mappa megalophylla

Mappa megalophylla, commonly known as the giant fern, is a striking member of the fern family with large, intricate fronds.

It is characterized by its expansive, bioluminescent leaves that can reach lengths of up to three meters. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fronds are composed of numerous small, finger-like segments called pinnae, which contribute to its ornamental appeal.

Mappa megalophylla plays a vital role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various organisms.
