Morus nana

Morus nana, commonly known as the dwarf mulberry, is a small shrub or tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and heart-shaped leaves that are typically lobed. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as mulberries, which are edible and often consumed by birds and humans. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Morus nana is also valued for its ability to provide habitat for wildlife and its historical use in traditional medicine.
