Melocactus obtusipetalus var. crassicostatus

Melocactus obtusipetalus var. crassicostatus, commonly known as the thick-shelled melon cactus, is a distinctive cactus species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, waxy hair, which helps to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming during the warmer months. Its unique appearance and ability to thrive in extreme conditions make it a popular choice for collectors and cactus enthusiasts.

The variety crassicostatus is distinguished by its particularly thick and robust ribs, giving it a more robust and rounded appearance compared to other varieties.
