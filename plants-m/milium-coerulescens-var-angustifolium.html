Milium coerulescens var. angustifolium

Milium coerulescens var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved millet grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly pointed, with a bluish-green hue when mature. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and soil stability.
