Myrtus nekouana

Myrtus nekouana, commonly known as the Tunisian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Tunisia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, dark blue drupe that contains a single seed.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in perfumery and as a medicinal herb.
