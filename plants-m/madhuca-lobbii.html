Madhuca lobbii

Madhuca lobbii, commonly known as the Lobb's candlewood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds.

It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
