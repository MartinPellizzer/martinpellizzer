Miconia solearis

Miconia solearis, commonly known as the yellow miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, yellow bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
