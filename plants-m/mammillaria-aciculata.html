Mammillaria aciculata

Mammillaria aciculata, commonly known as the needle spine cactus, is a small, spineless cactus native to Mexico.

It features a globular shape with tightly packed, woolly areoles covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, spherical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its compact size and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
