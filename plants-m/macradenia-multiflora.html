Macradenia multiflora

Macradenia multiflora, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are ovate and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and often branched.

Macradenia multiflora thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.
