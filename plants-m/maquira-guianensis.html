Maquira guianensis

Maquira guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan maquira, is a large evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the family Bombacaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and long, pendulous roots. The tree produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It is often found in lowland tropical moist forests and plays a significant role in the local ecosystem.

Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.
