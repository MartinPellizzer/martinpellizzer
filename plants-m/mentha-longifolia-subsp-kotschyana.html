Mentha longifolia subsp. kotschyana

Mentha longifolia subsp.

kotschyana, commonly known as Turkish mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their cooling and antiseptic properties. Mentha longifolia subsp.

kotschyana is also valued in culinary applications, particularly in Middle Eastern and Mediterranean cuisines.
