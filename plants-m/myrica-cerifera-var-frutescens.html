Myrica cerifera var. frutescens

Myrica cerifera var. frutescens, commonly known as wax myrtle or southern wax myrtle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, waxy leaves that are typically dark green on top and whitish underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in wetland areas or along stream banks.

Myrica cerifera var. frutescens is also valued for its ornamental qualities and its use in traditional medicine.
