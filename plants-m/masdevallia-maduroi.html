Masdevallia maduroi

Masdevallia maduroi, commonly known as the Maduroi Masdevallia, is a rare orchid species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces intricate, often fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments at high altitudes, thriving in cool, misty conditions. Its leaves are fleshy and arranged in a rosette, aiding in water retention.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is a subject of interest for orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
