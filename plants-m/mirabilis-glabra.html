Mirabilis glabra

Mirabilis glabra, commonly known as the velvetleaf four o'clock, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its smooth, velvety leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the late afternoon. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow, and it is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Mirabilis glabra is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
