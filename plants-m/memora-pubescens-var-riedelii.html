Memora pubescens var. riedelii

Memora pubescens var. riedelii, commonly known as the Riedel's velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
