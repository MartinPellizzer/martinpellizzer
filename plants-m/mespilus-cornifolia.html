Mespilus cornifolia

Mespilus cornifolia, commonly known as the cornified medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, ribbed, and often spiny fruits, which are typically harvested when fully ripe. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are small and greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or hedgerow environments.

The fruit, while not commonly eaten raw, has been historically used in cooking and traditional medicine.
