Melodorum ovalifolium

Melodorum ovalifolium, commonly known as the oval-leaf melodorum, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of white or pale yellow. It prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
