Micromeria varia subsp. thymoides

Micromeria varia subsp.

thymoides, commonly known as lemon verbena, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a perennial shrub characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves that emit a strong lemon scent when crushed.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are oppositely arranged and have a rough, textured surface with a distinct citrus aroma.

The plant is often used in herbal medicine and as a culinary herb due to its medicinal and aromatic properties.
