Muscari atlanticum

Muscari atlanticum, commonly known as the Atlantic grape hyacinth, is a bulbous plant native to the western Mediterranean region.

It produces dense clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has strap-shaped, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Muscari atlanticum is also valued for its fragrant flowers and ability to naturalize over time.
