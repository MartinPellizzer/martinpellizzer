Megaclinium laurentianum

Megaclinium laurentianum, commonly known as the Laurentian lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in cool, moist environments.

It is characterized by its dense, leaf-like thallus that ranges in color from green to yellowish-green. This lichen is typically found on tree bark, rocks, and soil in temperate forest regions. It plays a vital role in nutrient cycling and serves as an indicator of air quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.

Its slow growth and long lifespan make it an important component of ecological studies.
