Mentha × kerneri f. rubiginosa

Mentha × kerneri f. rubiginosa, commonly known as the red-stemmed hybrid mint, is a cultivated variety of mint that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Mentha × piperita and Mentha rotundifolia, showcasing traits from both parent plants. The plant is distinguished by its reddish-purple stems, which add visual interest to gardens, and its aromatic, slightly spicy leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its flavorful leaves and essential oils.
