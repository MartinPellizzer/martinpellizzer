Morus siciliana

Morus siciliana, commonly known as the Sicilian mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fuzzy leaves. The tree produces edible berries that are a food source for both humans and wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The bark is smooth and gray, with a distinctive flaky texture.
