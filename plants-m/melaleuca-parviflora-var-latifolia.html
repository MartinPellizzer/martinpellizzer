Melaleuca parviflora var. latifolia

Melaleuca parviflora var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved melaleuca, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in rounded clusters, which bloom in summer. Its aromatic foliage and showy inflorescences make it a notable species in its native habitat.

It is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.
