Magnolia sumatrana var. sumatrana

Magnolia sumatrana var. sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the Indonesian island of Sumatra.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that are typically green and leathery in texture. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, thriving in tropical and subtropical climates.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
