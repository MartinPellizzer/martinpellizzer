Microseris lanceolata

Microseris lanceolata, commonly known as the narrowleaf biscuitroot, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. It produces a taproot that is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Microseris lanceolata is an important species for pollinators and contributes to the ecological diversity of its native range.
