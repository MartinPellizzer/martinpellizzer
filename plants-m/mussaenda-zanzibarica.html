Mussaenda zanzibarica

Mussaenda zanzibarica, commonly known as Zanzibar Mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of East Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple underside. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that are arranged in umbels, giving it a striking appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

The plant is also noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
