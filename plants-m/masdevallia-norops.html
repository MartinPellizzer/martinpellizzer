Masdevallia norops

Masdevallia norops, commonly known as the Andean orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, often hairy pseudobulbs and delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to thrive. Its flowers, which can be pink, purple, or white, are usually small and clustered in inflorescences.

Masdevallia norops is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and resilience.
