Melocactus rubens

Melocactus rubens, commonly known as the red melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its globular shape and a dense covering of short, hooked spines. The plant produces a single, fleshy, red fruit that is often used in traditional medicine. Its thick, waxy skin helps it retain moisture in its harsh environment.

Melocactus rubens is well adapted to extreme temperatures and low rainfall, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
