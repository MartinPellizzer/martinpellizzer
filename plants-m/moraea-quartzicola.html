Moraea quartzicola

Moraea quartzicola, commonly known as the quartz rock lily, is a striking perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found growing on quartz outcrops. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to arid conditions.

Moraea quartzicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
