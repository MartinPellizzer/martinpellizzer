Myanthus warczewitzii

Myanthus warczewitzii, commonly known as the Wawrzyniec's myanthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. Its flowers are small and clustered in racemes, often displaying a mix of white and green hues.

Myanthus warczewitzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in shady garden settings.
