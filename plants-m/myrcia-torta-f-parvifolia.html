Myrcia torta f. parvifolia

Myrcia torta f. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved myrcia, is a compact shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces fragrant, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a small, reddish berry that ripens in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its dense foliage and aromatic qualities.
