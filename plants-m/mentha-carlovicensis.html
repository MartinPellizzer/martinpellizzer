Mentha carlovicensis

Mentha carlovicensis, commonly known as Carlovine mint, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Mentha carlovicensis is valued for its medicinal properties, including its use in traditional remedies for digestive and respiratory ailments.

Its essential oils are also used in aromatherapy and as a flavoring agent.
