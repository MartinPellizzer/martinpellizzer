Marina procumbens

Marina procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Europe and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. The plant features silver-green, fleshy leaves and produces small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in the summer. It is adapted to salt spray and strong winds, making it a hardy species in maritime environments.

Marina procumbens plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for coastal wildlife.
