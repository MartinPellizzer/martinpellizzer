Monilicarpa brasiliana

Monilicarpa brasiliana, commonly known as the Brazil grape or Brazilian mulberry, is a woody vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that have a smooth texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, fleshy berries that range in color from yellow to red. These berries are often consumed by birds and mammals, contributing to seed dispersal.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
