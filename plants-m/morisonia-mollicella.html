Morisonia mollicella

Morisonia mollicella, commonly known as the softleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink and purple. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Morisonia mollicella is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist habitats.
