Monadenium mafingense

Monadenium mafingense, commonly known as the Mafingense cactus, is a species native to eastern Africa, particularly found in regions of Tanzania and Kenya.

It is a small, spineless shrub that typically grows in rocky, arid environments. The plant features a short, woody stem with clusters of small, green, fleshy leaves. It produces yellowish flowers during the rainy season, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
