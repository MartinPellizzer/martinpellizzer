Myriogyne

Myriogyne, commonly known as the water lily of the genus Myriogyne, is a group of aquatic plants native to tropical and subtropical regions.

These plants are characterized by their floating leaves and showy, often fragrant flowers that bloom above the water surface. Myriogyne species typically have broad, rounded leaves that provide habitat for aquatic life and help in water filtration. The flowers are usually pink, white, or yellow, and they attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

These plants play an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing water surfaces and supporting biodiversity.
