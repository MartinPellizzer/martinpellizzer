Myagrum glabrum

Myagrum glabrum, commonly known as the smooth myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark and elliptical, glossy leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It produces small white flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits.

Myagrum glabrum is often found in coastal and sandy soils, and it plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
