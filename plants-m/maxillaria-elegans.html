Maxillaria elegans

Maxillaria elegans, commonly known as the elegant maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces clusters of fragrant, butterfly-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in the spring, with showy petals that range in color from white to pink and sometimes with a purple tinge. Its leaves are dark green, strap-like, and arranged in two rows along the stem.

Maxillaria elegans is relatively easy to cultivate, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
