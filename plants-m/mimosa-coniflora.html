Mimosa coniflora

Mimosa coniflora, commonly known as the sensitive plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Mimosa coniflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique response to stimuli.
