Melanthesa reclinata

Melanthesa reclinata, commonly known as the drooping melanthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often prostrate stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters.

Melanthesa reclinata is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along riverbanks.
