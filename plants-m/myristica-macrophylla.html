Myristica macrophylla

Myristica macrophylla, commonly known as the nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Banda Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and fragrant, yellow flowers. The tree produces a unique fruit that contains the aromatic seeds used to make nutmeg and mace. Its botanical features include a thick, smooth bark and a dense canopy that provides habitat for various species.

The plant is also valued for its essential oils and has been historically significant in global trade.
