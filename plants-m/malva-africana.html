Malva africana

Malva africana, commonly known as the African mallow, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, with five petals and a prominent central stamen.

Malva africana is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
