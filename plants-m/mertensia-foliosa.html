Mertensia foliosa

Mertensia foliosa, commonly known as the silverleaf bluebell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-white, velvety leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers in early spring, which bloom on slender, leafless stems. Its foliage and floral structure are adapted to thrive in moist, woodland environments.

Mertensia foliosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
