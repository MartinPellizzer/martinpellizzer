Millettia inaequalisepala

Millettia inaequalisepala, commonly known as the unequal-winged millettia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds. Native to tropical regions of Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
