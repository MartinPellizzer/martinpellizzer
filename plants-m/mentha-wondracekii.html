Mentha wondracekii

Mentha wondracekii, commonly known as the Wondracek mint, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue in color. Native to specific regions in Central Europe, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade. It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its aromatic properties and potential health benefits.

The plant's essential oils, derived from its leaves, are valued for their menthol-like aroma and medicinal applications.
