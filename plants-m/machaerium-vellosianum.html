Machaerium vellosianum

Machaerium vellosianum, commonly known as the velvetwood, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage. The plant features compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pods containing several seeds.

Machaerium vellosianum is often used in ecological restoration due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
