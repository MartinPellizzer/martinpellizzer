Monochaetum rubescens

Monochaetum rubescens, commonly known as the red-hair grass, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with reddish-brown spikelets, giving it its common name. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or inland regions.

Monochaetum rubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
