Myosotis refracta subsp. chitralica

Myosotis refracta subsp.

chitralica, commonly known as the Chitral forget-me-not, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of Pakistan and India. It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender stems and small, blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically thrives in alpine or subalpine meadows, where it prefers well-drained, rocky soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in high-altitude environments.
