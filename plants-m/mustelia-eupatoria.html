Mustelia eupatoria

Mustelia eupatoria, commonly known as greater betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of blue or purple flowers, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Mustelia eupatoria is often found in moist meadows and woodland edges, and it has been historically used in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
