Morenia poeppigiana

Morenia poeppigiana, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Passifloraceae and is characterized by its striking, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown-like structure of filaments. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed raw or used in culinary applications. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

Morenia poeppigiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
