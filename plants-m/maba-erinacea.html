Maba erinacea

Maba erinacea, commonly known as the hairy maba tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its rough, hairy bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and can thrive in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The tree has some traditional uses in local medicine and is also valued for its timber.
