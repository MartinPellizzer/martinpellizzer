Myrcia subsericea

Myrcia subsericea, commonly known as the velvet myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that contain aromatic seeds.

Myrcia subsericea is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
