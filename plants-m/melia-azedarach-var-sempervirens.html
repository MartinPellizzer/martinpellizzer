Melia azedarach var. sempervirens

Melia azedarach var. sempervirens, commonly known as the Chinese flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern China.

It features large, showy pink to red flowers that bloom in spring, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. The tree has a broad, spreading canopy and can grow up to 20 meters tall. Its leaves are compound, with long, slender leaflets that turn yellow in autumn.

The bark is smooth and greyish-brown, becoming fissured with age.
