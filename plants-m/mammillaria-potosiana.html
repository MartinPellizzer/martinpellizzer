Mammillaria potosiana

Mammillaria potosiana, commonly known as the Potosi mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and its dense covering of white, woolly spines. The plant typically produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth rate, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
