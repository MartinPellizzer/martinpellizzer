Maerua angolensis

Maerua angolensis, commonly known as the Angolan thornbush, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny branches and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, oval-shaped fruits. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry regions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
