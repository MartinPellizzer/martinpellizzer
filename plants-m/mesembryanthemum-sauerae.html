Mesembryanthemum sauerae

Mesembryanthemum sauerae, commonly known as the Cape marigold, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, often hairy leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. It typically blooms in spring and summer, producing clusters of small, daisy-like flowers.

Mesembryanthemum sauerae is popular in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
