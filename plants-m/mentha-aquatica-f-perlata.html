Mentha aquatica f. perlata

Mentha aquatica f. perlata, commonly known as the water mint variant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist environments in Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy, and it produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters. It thrives in wetlands, stream banks, and other waterlogged soils, making it a common sight in riparian habitats.

Due to its aromatic leaves and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental or medicinal purposes.
