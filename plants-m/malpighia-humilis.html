Malpighia humilis

Malpighia humilis, commonly known as the Mexican wolfberry, is a small shrub native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible and have a tart, tangy flavor. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are typically white or pale pink.

Malpighia humilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the production of its nutritious fruit.
