Matricaria maritima subsp. inodora

Matricaria maritima subsp.

inodora, commonly known as the coastal everlasting or sea dandelion, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa. It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and gray-green, woolly leaves that help reduce water loss in saline environments.

The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dunes and coastal grasslands. It is drought-tolerant and has a long flowering period, typically from late spring to early autumn. Its subsp.

inodora variant is distinguished by its lack of a strong scent, unlike the more commonly known Matricaria chamomilla.
