Malva tournefortiana var. dissectifolia

Malva tournefortiana var. dissectifolia, commonly known as the dissect-leaved mallows, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that give it a feathery appearance. The plant produces showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique foliage and vibrant blooms, it is often cultivated for both its aesthetic appeal and its ability to attract pollinators.
