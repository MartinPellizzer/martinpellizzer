Moraea vuvuzela

Moraea vuvuzela, commonly known as the vuvuzela lily, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. It produces showy inflorescences that bloom in summer, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its common name is derived from the resemblance of its flowers to the vuvuzela horn, a traditional South African musical instrument.
