Malva leiocarpa

Malva leiocarpa, commonly known as the smooth mallow, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cotton and okra. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Malva leiocarpa is also valued for its medicinal properties, with traditional uses in treating skin conditions and respiratory ailments.
