Moringa ovalifoliolata

Moringa ovalifoliolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf moringa, is a species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a small, drought-resistant shrub that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. Its flowers are small and yellow, typically appearing in clusters, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Moringa ovalifoliolata is valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional medicine for various health benefits.
