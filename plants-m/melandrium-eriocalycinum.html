Melandrium eriocalycinum

Melandrium eriocalycinum, commonly known as the Christmas pepper, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright red, lantern-shaped fruits that resemble small peppers. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
