Myrceugenella apiculata var. australis

Myrceugenella apiculata var. australis, commonly known as the Australian myrtle, is a species native to southern Australia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in coastal and heathland environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a glossy, dark green surface and a fragrant, resinous scent. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, often appearing in spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in local ecosystems.
