Melica cretica

Melica cretica, commonly known as Cretan grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as slopes and cliffs. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Melica cretica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
