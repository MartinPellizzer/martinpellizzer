Myriophyllum eggelingii

Myriophyllum eggelingii, commonly known as the water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Asia.

It features whorled leaves that are finely divided, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in slow-moving or still waters with moderate to high light penetration.

Myriophyllum eggelingii plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
