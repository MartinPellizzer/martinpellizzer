Malveopsis phyllanthos

Malveopsis phyllanthos, commonly known as the African velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton plants. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pale yellow to pink flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fallow fields.

Malveopsis phyllanthos is also known for its potential medicinal uses in traditional African medicine.
