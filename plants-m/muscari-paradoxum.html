Muscari paradoxum

Muscari paradoxum, commonly known as the Persian bluebell, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Turkey.

It produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers in early spring, which are highly attractive to pollinators. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge from a bulbous base, typical of the Muscari genus. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or loamy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Muscari paradoxum is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and its ability to naturalize over time.
