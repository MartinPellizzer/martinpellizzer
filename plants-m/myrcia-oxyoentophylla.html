Myrcia oxyoentophylla

Myrcia oxyoentophylla, commonly known as the red myrcia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded areas of the forest understory.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
