Mammillaria verhaertiana

Mammillaria verhaertiana, commonly known as the Verhaert's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is covered in white, woolly spines that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered at the top of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus collections.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
