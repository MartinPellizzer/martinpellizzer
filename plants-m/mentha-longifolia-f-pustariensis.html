Mentha longifolia f. pustariensis

Mentha longifolia f. pustariensis, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a holarctic perennial herb characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage.

It features opposite, lanceolate leaves that are covered in dense, glandular hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
