Myosotis humilis

Myosotis humilis, commonly known as the humble forget-me-not, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to southern and eastern Australia.

It features delicate blue to violet flowers with five petals and a yellow center, typically blooming in spring. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and woodland areas.

Myosotis humilis is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance as a food source for native insects.
