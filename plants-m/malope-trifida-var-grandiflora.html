Malope trifida var. grandiflora

Malope trifida var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered malope, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange, with five petals arranged in a radial symmetry. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
