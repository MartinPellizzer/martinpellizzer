Myristica villosa

Myristica villosa, commonly known as the hairy nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen belonging to the Myristicaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that yield both nutmeg and mace, which are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its unique morphology includes a thick, woody trunk and a dense canopy of foliage.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been the subject of botanical studies due to its economic and ecological significance.
