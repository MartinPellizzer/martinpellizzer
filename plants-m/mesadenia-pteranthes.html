Mesadenia pteranthes

Mesadenia pteranthes, commonly known as the silverleaf mesadenia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Mesadenia pteranthes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
