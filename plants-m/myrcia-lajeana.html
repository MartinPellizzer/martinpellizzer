Myrcia lajeana

Myrcia lajeana, commonly known as Brazilian pepper tree, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows up to 6 meters in height. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement and emit a strong, aromatic scent when crushed. Its flowers are small, clustered, and white or pale pink, followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals.

Myrcia lajeana is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
