Micrococca wightii var. hirsuta

Micrococca wightii var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy micrococca, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in forested or semi-shade environments. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and covered in fine, hairy textures, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The flowers bloom in spring and are often used in traditional medicine and ornamental gardening.
