Mimosa iperoensis var. paradenia

Mimosa iperoensis var. paradenia, commonly known as the golden mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates. Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners.

However, it is important to note that the plant can be invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
