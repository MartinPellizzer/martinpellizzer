Metrosideros operculata var. myrtifolia

Metrosideros operculata var. myrtifolia, commonly known as the coastal bottlebrush, is a variant of the bottlebrush tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or orange flowers that bloom in dense, brush-like clusters. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a glossy surface. This plant thrives in coastal environments and is adapted to salty sea air and sandy soils.

It plays an important role in coastal ecosystems and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
