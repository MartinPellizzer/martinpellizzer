Mespilus arborea

Mespilus arborea, commonly known as the European whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, round fruits known as whiteberries, which are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive serrated margin.

Mespilus arborea is often found in woodlands and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance.
