Medicago citrina

Medicago citrina, commonly known as yellow medic, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Medicago citrina is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
