Mimulus congdonii

Mimulus congdonii, commonly known as the Congdon's monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright orange or yellow tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats, including streambanks and meadows. It has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, seed-like fruits that disperse by wind.

Mimulus congdonii is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
