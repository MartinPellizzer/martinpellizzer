Macrotomia euchromon

Macrotomia euchromon, commonly known as the purple-stemmed goldenbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems that display a distinctive purplish hue, especially during the cooler months. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its dry habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
