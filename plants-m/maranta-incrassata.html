Maranta incrassata

Maranta incrassata, commonly known as the velvetleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display intricate patterns. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its rhizomes are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines for their starchy texture.

Maranta incrassata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.
