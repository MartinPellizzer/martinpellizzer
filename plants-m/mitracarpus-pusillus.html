Mitracarpus pusillus

Mitracarpus pusillus, commonly known as the dwarf cowpea, is a small, herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, branching stems and small, palmately lobed leaves that are typically hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, dry legume that contains several seeds.

Mitracarpus pusillus is often grown as a cover crop or for its ornamental value due to its compact growth habit.
