Mosla coreana

Mosla coreana, commonly known as Korean stonecress, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with small, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are usually pink or white.

Mosla coreana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
