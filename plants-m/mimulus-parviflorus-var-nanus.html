Mimulus parviflorus var. nanus

Mimulus parviflorus var. nanus, commonly known as the dwarf yellow monkeyflower, is a compact, low-growing perennial native to the western United States.

It features bright yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant collections and ecological restoration projects.
