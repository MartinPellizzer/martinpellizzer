Maxillaria caveroi

Maxillaria caveroi, commonly known as the Cavero's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Mexico and Central America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that support its evergreen foliage. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically during the spring and summer months. Its flowers are characterized by their spurred lip and are often arranged in racemes.

Maxillaria caveroi is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy cultivation requirements.
