Mespilodaphne pretiosa var. longifolia

Mespilodaphne pretiosa var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved mespilodaphne, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the wet season. Its fruit is a small, round drupe with a smooth, yellowish skin.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.
