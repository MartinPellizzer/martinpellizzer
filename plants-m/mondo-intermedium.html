Mondo intermedium

Mondo intermedium, commonly known as the Japanese forest grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a distinctive bluish-gray sheen, and its leaves are narrow and strap-like. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers in early spring, followed by reddish-brown berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form dense mats.

Mondo intermedium is also valued for its ability to suppress weeds and prevent soil erosion in woodland settings.
