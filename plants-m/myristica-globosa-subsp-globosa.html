Myristica globosa subsp. globosa

Myristica globosa subsp.

globosa, commonly known as the nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Banda Islands in Indonesia. It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers.

The plant produces distinctive red, fleshy berries that contain the aromatic seeds used to make nutmeg and mace. Its botanical significance lies in its unique chemical composition, including essential oils and compounds like myristicin and elemicin.

The subspecies globosa is particularly noted for its role in the global spice trade and its ecological importance in its native habitat.
