Malaxis hintonii

Malaxis hintonii, commonly known as the Hinton's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil.

Malaxis hintonii is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its unique floral structure and ecological significance.
