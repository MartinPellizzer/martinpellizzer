Moya ferox

Moya ferox, commonly known as the devil's backbone, is a striking member of the Rhamnaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its spiny, woody stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers that give way to small, orange-red fruits. Moya ferox is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.
