Mikania sessilifolia var. sessilifolia

Mikania sessilifolia var. sessilifolia, commonly known as the sessile-leaf Mikania, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, opposite leaves that are sessile, meaning they lack a petiole. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, spherical fruits. It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and along stream banks.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it can become a problematic weed in certain ecosystems.
