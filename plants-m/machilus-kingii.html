Machilus kingii

Machilus kingii, commonly known as the Chinese mahogany, is a small evergreen tree native to eastern China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not widely consumed by humans. Machilus kingii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value and unique foliage make it a popular choice for horticultural use.
