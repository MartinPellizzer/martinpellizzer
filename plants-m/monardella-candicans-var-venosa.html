Monardella candicans var. venosa

Monardella candicans var. venosa, commonly known as the showy monardella, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking, showy white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.
