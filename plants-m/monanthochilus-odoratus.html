Monanthochilus odoratus

Monanthochilus odoratus, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air, and it thrives in humid, tropical environments.

The flowers emit a sweet scent, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths.
