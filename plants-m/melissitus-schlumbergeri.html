Melissitus schlumbergeri

Melissitus schlumbergeri, commonly known as the Schlumbergera, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, segmented stems that resemble leaves, which are actually modified stems adapted for storing water. The plant produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming during the winter months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated as a houseplant.
