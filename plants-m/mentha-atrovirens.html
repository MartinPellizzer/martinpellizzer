Mentha atrovirens

Mentha atrovirens, commonly known as black mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, slightly hairy leaves and aromatic, menthol-like scent. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its strong fragrance and medicinal properties, it has been historically used in herbal remedies.
