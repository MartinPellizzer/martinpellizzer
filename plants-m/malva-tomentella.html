Malva tomentella

Malva tomentella, commonly known as the woolly mallow, is a perennial herb native to regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its flowers are tubular with five petals, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.

Malva tomentella is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
