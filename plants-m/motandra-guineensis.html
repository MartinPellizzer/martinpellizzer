Motandra guineensis

Motandra guineensis, commonly known as the African orchid or Guinean orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, yellow, and green. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant in some regions.
