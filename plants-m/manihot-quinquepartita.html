Manihot quinquepartita

Manihot quinquepartita, commonly known as arrowleaf mallow, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, arrowhead-like leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is fibrous, and it prefers moist, well-drained soils in partial to full sun.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
