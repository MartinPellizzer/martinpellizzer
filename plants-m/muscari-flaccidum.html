Muscari flaccidum

Muscari flaccidum, commonly known as the lax musk mallow, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its clustered, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to purple. The plant produces a basal rosette of strap-like leaves and has a short, stout stem that supports its inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Muscari flaccidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
