Medinilla ternifolia

Medinilla ternifolia, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or velvet plant, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, orange fruits that resemble tangerines, which are edible but not commonly consumed. Medinilla ternifolia thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its unique appearance and pleasant fragrance make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
