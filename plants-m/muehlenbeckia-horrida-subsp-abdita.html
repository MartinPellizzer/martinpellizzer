Muehlenbeckia horrida subsp. abdita

Muehlenbeckia horrida subsp.

abdita, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, silky hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
