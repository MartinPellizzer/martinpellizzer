Myginda riparia

Myginda riparia, commonly known as the riverbed willow, is a shrub native to the riverbanks of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, flexible stems that can withstand flowing water and periodic flooding. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, aiding in water retention. Its small, pale yellow flowers appear in clusters and are pollinated by insects.

Myginda riparia plays a vital role in stabilizing riverbanks and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
