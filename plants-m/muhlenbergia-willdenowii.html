Muhlenbergia willdenowii

Muhlenbergia willdenowii, commonly known as the eastern muhlenbergia, is a grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, arching blades that often turn reddish in color during the fall. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full to partial sunlight.

Muhlenbergia willdenowii is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
