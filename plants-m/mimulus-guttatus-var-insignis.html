Mimulus guttatus var. insignis

Mimulus guttatus var. insignis, commonly known as the yellow monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and southern Canada.

It is a perennial herb with slender, branching stems that often grow in moist, rocky habitats. The plant produces showy, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically spurred at the back, a characteristic feature of the genus Mimulus.

It is valued in horticulture for its vibrant color and ability to thrive in diverse soil conditions.
