Myrcia cana

Myrcia cana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are usually elliptical, glossy, and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

The fruit is a small, round berry that changes color from green to red as it matures.
