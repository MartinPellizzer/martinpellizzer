Miconia ostrina

Miconia ostrina, commonly known as the red miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are usually bright green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are round, fleshy berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Miconia ostrina is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and unique floral display.
