Marshallia grandiflora

Marshallia grandiflora, commonly known as the showy marsh marigold, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and stream banks. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Marshallia grandiflora is a perennial herb that plays an important role in supporting local pollinators.
