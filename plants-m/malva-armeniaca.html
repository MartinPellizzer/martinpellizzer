Malva armeniaca

Malva armeniaca, commonly known as Armenian mallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features large, showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are heart-shaped with a fuzzy texture. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its mucilage-rich properties.

The plant's fibrous stems and leaves are also utilized in textile production in some regions.
