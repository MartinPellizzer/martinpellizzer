Mimusops riparia

Mimusops riparia, commonly known as the water mimosa, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies such as rivers and lakes. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hard seeds.
