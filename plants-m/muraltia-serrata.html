Muraltia serrata

Muraltia serrata, commonly known as the serrated muraltia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Leguminosae (Fabaceae) family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Muraltia serrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.

Its growth habit and adaptability make it a popular choice for horticultural use in warm climates.
