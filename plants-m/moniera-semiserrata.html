Moniera semiserrata

Moniera semiserrata, commonly known as the semi-serrated fern, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are partially serrated along the edges. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its rhizome is creeping and produces numerous sporangia on the underside of its leaves.

Moniera semiserrata is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
