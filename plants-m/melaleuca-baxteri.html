Melaleuca baxteri

Melaleuca baxteri, commonly known as the Baxters melaleuca, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous small, brown seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands and dry forests.

Melaleuca baxteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate salty coastal conditions.
