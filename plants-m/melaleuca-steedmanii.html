Melaleuca steedmanii

Melaleuca steedmanii, commonly known as the Stedman's tea tree, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern Queensland and northern New South Wales in Australia. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

The species is valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental qualities.
