Mesembryanthemum muricatum

Mesembryanthemum muricatum, commonly known as the ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often in response to cool temperatures and bright light. It is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
