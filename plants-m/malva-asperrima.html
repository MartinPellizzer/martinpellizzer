Malva asperrima

Malva asperrima, commonly known as the rough mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other mallows and cotton plants. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Malva asperrima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
