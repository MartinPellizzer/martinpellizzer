Malaxis atrobrachiata

Malaxis atrobrachiata, commonly known as the black-armed malaxis, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and its unique, spurred flowers that are typically greenish-brown in color. The plant grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are small and clustered, with a distinct lip that is often darker than the rest of the flower.

Malaxis atrobrachiata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
