Maerua puccionii

Maerua puccionii, commonly known as the red sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers, which bloom during the warmer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.
