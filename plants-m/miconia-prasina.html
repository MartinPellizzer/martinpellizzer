Miconia prasina

Miconia prasina, commonly known as the green miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often bright green. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, velvety blue-black berries. Miconia prasina is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
