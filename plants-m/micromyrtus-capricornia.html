Micromyrtus capricornia

Micromyrtus capricornia, commonly known as the Capricorn myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to northern Queensland, Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in heathlands and dunes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
