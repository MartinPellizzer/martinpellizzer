Magnolia cylindrica

Magnolia cylindrica, commonly known as the slender magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The flowers are typically white or pale pink and are borne on long, slender pedicels. The tree has a tall, slender trunk and can grow up to 20 meters in height.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
