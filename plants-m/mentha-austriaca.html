Mentha austriaca

Mentha austriaca, commonly known as Austrian mint, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its aromatic leaves contain menthol and other volatile oils, which contribute to its minty fragrance.

Austrian mint is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
