Mucuna globulifera

Mucuna globulifera, commonly known as the globe bean or globular balsam, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, ornamental flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces spherical, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Mucuna globulifera is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a valuable and sometimes invasive species in its native habitats.
