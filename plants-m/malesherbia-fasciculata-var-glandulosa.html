Malesherbia fasciculata var. glandulosa

Malesherbia fasciculata var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular malesherbia, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are often covered in fine, glandular hairs, which give it its varietal name.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
