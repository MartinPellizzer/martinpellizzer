Methyscophyllum

Methyscophyllum, commonly known as the moonlight fern, is a unique plant characterized by its bioluminescent leaves that emit a soft blue glow at night.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical rainforests. The plant's foliage consists of delicate, lace-like fronds that unfurl slowly over time. Its roots are fibrous and help it anchor in moist, nutrient-rich soil.

Methyscophyllum plays a role in the ecosystem by providing habitat for small nocturnal insects and contributing to the forest's biodiversity.
