Monechma incanum

Monechma incanum, commonly known as the silverleaf mallow, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features hairy, gray-green leaves that are typically oval to heart-shaped, with a rough texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Malvaceae family.
