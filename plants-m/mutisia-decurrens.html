Mutisia decurrens

Mutisia decurrens, commonly known as the Andean mutisia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its branched stems and alternate, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-white flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions, often found in cloud forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
