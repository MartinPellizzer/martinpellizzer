Magnolia tamaulipana

Magnolia tamaulipana, commonly known as the Tamaulipan magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the coastal regions of the Gulf of Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are simple, ovate, and have a smooth texture, turning yellow in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
