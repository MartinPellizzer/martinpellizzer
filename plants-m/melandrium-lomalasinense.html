Melandrium lomalasinense

Melandrium lomalasinense, commonly known as the Loma Larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant grows in rocky or alpine habitats, often at elevations above 2,000 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hairs, while its stems are erect and sometimes branched.

The flowers range in color from blue to purple, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in high-altitude gardens.
