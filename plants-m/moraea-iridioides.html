Moraea iridioides

Moraea iridioides, commonly known as the blue-eyed moraea, is a striking perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in grassland habitats and thrives in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette.

Moraea iridioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
