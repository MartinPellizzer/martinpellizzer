Microchilus serripetalus

Microchilus serripetalus, commonly known as the serrated leaf orchid, is a small terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. The plant has slender, hairy stems and produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with intricate patterns. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, giving it its common name.

The orchid is known for its fragrant blooms and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
