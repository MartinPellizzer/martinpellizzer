Melilotus citrinus

Melilotus citrinus, commonly known as yellow melilot, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Yellow melilot is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
