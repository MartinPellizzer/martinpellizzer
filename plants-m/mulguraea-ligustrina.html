Mulguraea ligustrina

Mulguraea ligustrina, commonly known as the slender mulga, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its narrow, erect growth habit. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, woody fruits.

Mulguraea ligustrina is well adapted to harsh environments and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
