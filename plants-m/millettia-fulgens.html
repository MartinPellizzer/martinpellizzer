Millettia fulgens

Millettia fulgens, commonly known as the golden millettia, is a striking tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets, and its bark is smooth and often exhibits a reddish hue. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
