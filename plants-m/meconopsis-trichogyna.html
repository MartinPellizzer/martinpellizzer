Meconopsis trichogyna

Meconopsis trichogyna, commonly known as the Himalayan blue poppy, is a striking alpine plant native to the mountainous regions of Nepal, Bhutan, and Tibet.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to violet petals, which are often adorned with intricate patterns and a distinctive yellow or orange center. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool and shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Meconopsis trichogyna is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological significance in alpine ecosystems.
