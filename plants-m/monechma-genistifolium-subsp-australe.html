Monechma genistifolium subsp. australe

Monechma genistifolium subsp.

australe, commonly known as the Australian buckbrush, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It features small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in dense clusters during the spring.

The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
