Manihot toledii

Manihot toledii, commonly known as the Mexican arrowroot, is a tropical plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and Central America and is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces edible tuberous roots that are starchy and used in various culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in subtropical regions.
