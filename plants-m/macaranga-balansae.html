Macaranga balansae

Macaranga balansae, commonly known as the white-veined macaranga, is a tropical plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent white veins, which give it its common name. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and is often found in lowland rainforests. It has a rapid growth rate and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.

The bark and leaves of Macaranga balansae have been used in traditional medicine for their potential therapeutic properties.
