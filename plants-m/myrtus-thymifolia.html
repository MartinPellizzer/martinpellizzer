Myrtus thymifolia

Myrtus thymifolia, commonly known as sweet myrtle or thyme myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, dark green leaves that are aromatic and often used in herbal remedies. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, which are followed by bright red berries. Myrtus thymifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.
