Maxillaria danielae

Maxillaria danielae, commonly known as the Daniel's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its intricate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, with intricate patterns and a distinctive lip.

Maxillaria danielae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
