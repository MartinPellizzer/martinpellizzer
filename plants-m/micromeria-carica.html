Micromeria carica

Micromeria carica, commonly known as the desert sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small annual herb with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, pink to purple flowers. The plant has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to harsh, dry environments. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Micromeria carica is often found in sandy soils and is considered a characteristic species of desert scrub habitats.
