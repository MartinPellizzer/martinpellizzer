Merremia boisiana var. sumatrana

Merremia boisiana var. sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth.

Its rapid growth and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
