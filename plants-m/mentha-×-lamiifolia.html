Mentha × lamiifolia

Mentha × lamiifolia, commonly known as catnip, is a hybrid plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated edge. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, typical of the mint family. It is native to Europe and Asia but has been naturalized in many parts of the world.

Mentha × lamiifolia is known for its aromatic properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies and as a stimulant for cats.
