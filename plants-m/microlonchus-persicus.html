Microlonchus persicus

Microlonchus persicus, commonly known as the Persian vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a legume belonging to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or white, and it is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.

Microlonchus persicus is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
