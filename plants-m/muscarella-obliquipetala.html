Muscarella obliquipetala

Muscarella obliquipetala, commonly known as the oblique-leaf muscaria, is a unique species within the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful inflorescences that consist of clustered, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in partial shade with well-drained soil. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a distinctive oblique base that gives it its common name.

Muscarella obliquipetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
