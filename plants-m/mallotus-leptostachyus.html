Mallotus leptostachyus

Mallotus leptostachyus, commonly known as the narrow-stalked mallot, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, spiky capsules containing numerous seeds.

It is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental foliage and potential medicinal properties.
