Mertensia humilis

Mertensia humilis, commonly known as the dwarf bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features attractive, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in early spring. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with silver, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Mertensia humilis is valued in native gardens for its early blooming habit and ecological benefits to pollinators.
