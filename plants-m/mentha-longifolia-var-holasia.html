Mentha longifolia var. holasia

Mentha longifolia var. holasia, commonly known as Hola mint, is a variant of the spearmint species native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and gray-green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Hola mint is known for its strong, spicy scent and is valued in traditional herbal remedies.
