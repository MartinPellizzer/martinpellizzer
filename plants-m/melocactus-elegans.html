Melocactus elegans

Melocactus elegans, commonly known as the elegant melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by small, edible fruits. Its spines are short and often covered in a fine, white coating.

Melocactus elegans is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
