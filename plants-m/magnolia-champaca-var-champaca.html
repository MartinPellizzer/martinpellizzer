Magnolia champaca var. champaca

Magnolia champaca var. champaca, commonly known as the night-blooming champaca, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular yellow flowers that bloom at night, emitting a strong, sweet perfume. The plant has broad, glossy, dark green leaves and can grow up to 15 meters tall. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

The flowers are used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oil.
