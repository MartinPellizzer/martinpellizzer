Mimosa gemmulata var. cristallina

Mimosa gemmulata var. cristallina, commonly known as the crystal mimosa, is a unique variant of the mimosa plant characterized by its striking, translucent, and often waxy leaves.

The leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, giving the plant a compact and ornamental appearance. It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense mats, making it both a useful ground cover and a potential weed.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and collectors interested in unusual plant forms.
