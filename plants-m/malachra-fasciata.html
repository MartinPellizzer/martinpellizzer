Malachra fasciata

Malachra fasciata, commonly known as the spotted milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its latex sap is milky and can be toxic, which serves as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

Malachra fasciata is often found in moist, disturbed soils in woodlands and along stream banks.
