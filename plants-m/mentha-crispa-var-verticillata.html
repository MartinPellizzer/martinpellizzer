Mentha crispa var. verticillata

Mentha crispa var. verticillata, commonly known as the verticillate mint, is a variety of the mint family (Lamiaceae) characterized by its distinctive, whorled arrangement of leaves.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in spikes. Its leaves are narrow, serrated, and emit a strong, aromatic scent when crushed.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
