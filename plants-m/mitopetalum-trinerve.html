Mitopetalum trinerve

Mitopetalum trinerve, commonly known as the three-veined mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that primarily inhabits the branches of host trees in tropical and subtropical regions.

It lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its stems, which are adapted for attachment to host plants. The plant's leaves are small, ovate, and often have a waxy surface, while its flowers are typically small and clustered. Mitopetalum trinerve produces small, fleshy berries that serve as a food source for various birds and insects.

Its parasitic nature and unique morphological features make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
