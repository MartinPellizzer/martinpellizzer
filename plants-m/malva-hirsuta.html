Malva hirsuta

Malva hirsuta, commonly known as the woolly mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, fuzzy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in gardens and along stream banks.

Malva hirsuta is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
