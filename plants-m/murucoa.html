Murucoa

Murucoa, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the family Proteaceae and is characterized by its large, compound leaves and striking yellow flowers. The tree produces a hard-shelled fruit that contains a single, large edible seed, known as the Brazil nut. Murucoa thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ecological importance is significant, as it supports a variety of wildlife and plays a role in the biodiversity of the Amazon region.
