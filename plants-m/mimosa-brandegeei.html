Mimosa brandegeei

Mimosa brandegeei, commonly known as the ghost pipevine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and swamps. The plant produces small, delicate pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately divided, giving it a fern-like appearance.

Mimosa brandegeei is also notable for its fragrant scent, which attracts various pollinators like butterflies and bees.
