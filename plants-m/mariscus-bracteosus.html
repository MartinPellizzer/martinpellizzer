Mariscus bracteosus

Mariscus bracteosus, commonly known as the bracteate mariscus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wet meadows or along stream banks. Its leaves are arranged in a basal rosette and are covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

Mariscus bracteosus is known for its ability to thrive in wet environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
