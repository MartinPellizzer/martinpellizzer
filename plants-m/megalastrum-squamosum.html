Megalastrum squamosum

Megalastrum squamosum, commonly known as the scaly leaf cactus, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that are covered in a distinctive scaly texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in arid environments and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for collectors and desert gardeners.
