Melaleuca kalbarriensis

Melaleuca kalbarriensis, commonly known as the Kalbarri bottlebrush, is a species of shrub or small tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flowers that range in color from red to pink, which are clustered in dense spikes. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds, and it has narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. Melaleuca kalbarriensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and fire resistance.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
