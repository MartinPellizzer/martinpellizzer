Mangifera polycarpa

Mangifera polycarpa, commonly known as the wild mango, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The fruit is typically small, with a thick, fibrous pulp surrounding the seeds. It is often found in moist, lowland areas and can grow up to 20 meters in height.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for fruit production, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
