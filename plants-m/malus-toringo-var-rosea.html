Malus toringo var. rosea

Malus toringo var. rosea, commonly known as the roseleaf crabapple, is a cultivar of the crabapple tree native to China.

It is characterized by its ornamental, rose-pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The leaves are typically a deep green, providing a striking contrast to the flowers. The fruit is small, apple-like, and often red or yellow, though it is not typically edible.

This plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and its ability to thrive in temperate climates.
