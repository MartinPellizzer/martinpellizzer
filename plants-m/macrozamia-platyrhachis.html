Macrozamia platyrhachis

Macrozamia platyrhachis, commonly known as the broad-ridge cycad, is a species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces large, cone-like structures, with male and female cones typically occurring on separate plants. It is a slow-growing, long-lived species that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
