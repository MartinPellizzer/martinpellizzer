Mallotus repandus var. megaphyllus

Mallotus repandus var. megaphyllus, commonly known as the giant mallot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The leaves are typically bright green and have a smooth, waxy surface. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody capsules containing several seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage.
