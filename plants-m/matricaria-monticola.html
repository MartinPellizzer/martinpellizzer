Matricaria monticola

Matricaria monticola, commonly known as mountain chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flower heads. The plant produces small, white to yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, often used in herbal teas for their calming properties.

Matricaria monticola is valued for its medicinal uses and is often cultivated for both ornamental and therapeutic purposes.
