Medicago pontificalis

Medicago pontificalis, commonly known as the Jerusalem balm, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open habitats. It has been used historically in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Its seeds are edible and can be used in culinary applications.
