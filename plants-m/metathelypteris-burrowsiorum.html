Metathelypteris burrowsiorum

Metathelypteris burrowsiorum, commonly known as the Burrows' water fern, is a species of fern native to the wetlands of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinnately compound fronds that are typically submerged or floating in still or slow-moving water. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping rhizomes that help it spread across moist environments. Its spores are produced in sori that are arranged along the margins of the fronds, typical of the fern family.

Metathelypteris burrowsiorum plays an important ecological role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
