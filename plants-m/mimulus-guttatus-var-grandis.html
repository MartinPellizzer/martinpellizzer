Mimulus guttatus var. grandis

Mimulus guttatus var. grandis, commonly known as the large yellow monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its showy, bright yellow flowers that resemble small monkeys, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in wet meadows, streambanks, and other moist habitats. It has heart-shaped leaves and produces numerous seeds in elongated pods.

Mimulus guttatus var. grandis is an important species in ecological studies due to its role in pollination and its use in genetic research.
