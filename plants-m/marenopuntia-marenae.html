Marenopuntia marenae

Marenopuntia marenae, commonly known as the Maren's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its spineless, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by edible, red, fleshy fruits. Its succulent leaves are adapted for minimizing water loss through transpiration.

Marenopuntia marenae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food in local communities.
