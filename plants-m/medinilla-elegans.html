Medinilla elegans

Medinilla elegans, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or velvet bell, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, waxy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces small, round, yellow fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and flowers.

Medinilla elegans is well-suited for indoor cultivation and requires bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.
