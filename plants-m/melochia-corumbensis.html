Melochia corumbensis

Melochia corumbensis, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Brazilian mullein, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries containing several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other plants such as hibiscus and cotton.
