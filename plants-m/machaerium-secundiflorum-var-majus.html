Machaerium secundiflorum var. majus

Machaerium secundiflorum var. majus, commonly known as the large-flowered machaerium, is a tropical perennial shrub native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, delicate white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with narrow, elongated leaflets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in subtropical gardens.
