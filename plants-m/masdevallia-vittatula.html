Masdevallia vittatula

Masdevallia vittatula, commonly known as the violet-streaked masdevallia, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It features a compact, sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb and a single, narrow leaf. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of purple and white, often with intricate streaking patterns. It thrives in cool, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and delicate beauty.
