Morus alba var. vungeana

Morus alba var. vungeana, commonly known as the white mulberry, is a variant of the mulberry tree native to Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically pale green with a slightly hairy texture. The tree produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically white or pink in color. It is often cultivated for its leaves, which are a primary food source for silkworms.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
