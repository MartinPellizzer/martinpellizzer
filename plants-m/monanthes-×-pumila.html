Monanthes × pumila

Monanthes × pumila, commonly known as the dwarf monanthes, is a hybrid plant that exhibits a unique combination of traits from its parent species.

It is a low-growing perennial herb native to specific regions in South Africa, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its compact size and adaptability make it suitable for rock gardens and container planting.

Botanically, it is characterized by its simple leaves and its ability to form dense clusters, which contribute to its ecological and horticultural significance.
