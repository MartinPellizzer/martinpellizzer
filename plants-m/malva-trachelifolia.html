Malva trachelifolia

Malva trachelifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces showy pink to purple flowers with five petals, typically appearing in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in gardens and along waterways.

Malva trachelifolia is also known for its edible leaves and flowers, which can be used in culinary applications.
