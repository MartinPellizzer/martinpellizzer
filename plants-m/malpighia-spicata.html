Malpighia spicata

Malpighia spicata, commonly known as the purple grumot, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and edible fruits. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are ovate and glossy, while the fruits are small, round, and have a tart, acidic flavor.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the consumption of its fruits in local cuisines.
