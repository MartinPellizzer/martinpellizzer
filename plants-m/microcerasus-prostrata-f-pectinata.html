Microcerasus prostrata f. pectinata

Microcerasus prostrata f. pectinata, commonly known as the woolly cherry or the creeping cherry, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to China.

It features dense, silvery-white pubescent foliage that gives it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible, sweet berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique texture and seasonal blooms.
