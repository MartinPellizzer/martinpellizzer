Monstera friedrichsthalii

Monstera friedrichsthalii, commonly known as the Friedrichsthal's monstera, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that develop distinctive fenestrations as they mature. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light. Its climbing habit requires support such as a trellis or moss pole for optimal growth.

The leaves are typically a deep green, with a waxy texture that helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat.
