Melastoma ansowii

Melastoma ansowii, commonly known as the Java melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a rough, textured surface, while its stems are woody and often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
