Myrica linearis

Myrica linearis, commonly known as the slender wax myrtle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into hard, woody fruits. It thrives in wetland habitats and is often found in swamps and along stream banks.

Myrica linearis has a shallow root system and is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils.
