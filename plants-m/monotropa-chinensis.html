Monotropa chinensis

Monotropa chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese ghost plant, is a hemiparasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for nutrients.

It is native to eastern Asia and thrives in shaded, moist forest environments. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. Its unique morphology includes a slender stem and a cluster of elongated, thread-like leaves.

Despite its parasitic nature, it plays a role in forest ecosystems by influencing the growth of its host plants.
