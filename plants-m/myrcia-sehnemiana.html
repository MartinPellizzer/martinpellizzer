Myrcia sehnemiana

Myrcia sehnemiana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

Myrcia sehnemiana is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
