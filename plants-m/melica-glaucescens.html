Melica glaucescens

Melica glaucescens, commonly known as bluegrass or slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that often have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along streambanks.

Melica glaucescens is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and wildlife.
