Mentha candicans f. angustifolia

Mentha candicans f. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved spearmint, is a botanical variant of the spearmint species.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and a strong, aromatic menthol scent. This form typically grows in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soils. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges or stream banks.

The plant is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other mint species.
