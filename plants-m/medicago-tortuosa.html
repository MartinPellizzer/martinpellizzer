Medicago tortuosa

Medicago tortuosa, commonly known as the curled vetch, is a leguminous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, twisted pod-like fruit that gives it its common name. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

Medicago tortuosa is used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
