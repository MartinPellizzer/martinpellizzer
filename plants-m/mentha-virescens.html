Mentha virescens

Mentha virescens, commonly known as green mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue or purple, appearing in clusters during the summer months. Mentha virescens is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive minty fragrance.
