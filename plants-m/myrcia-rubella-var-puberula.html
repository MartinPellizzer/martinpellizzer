Myrcia rubella var. puberula

Myrcia rubella var. puberula, commonly known as the rough myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. It typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, often found in humid, shaded environments.

The fruit is a small, red drupe that develops from the flowers and is edible, though not commonly consumed by humans.
