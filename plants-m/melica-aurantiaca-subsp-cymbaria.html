Melica aurantiaca subsp. cymbaria

Melica aurantiaca subsp.

cymbaria, commonly known as the red false brome, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and distinctive reddish tinge on its leaf blades.

The plant produces panicle-like inflorescences with small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
