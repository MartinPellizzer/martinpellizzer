Mimulus alsinoides var. paniculatus

Mimulus alsinoides var. paniculatus, commonly known as the yellow monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as stream banks and meadows. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces elongated clusters of small, showy flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
