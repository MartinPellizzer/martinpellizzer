Machilus platyphyllus

Machilus platyphyllus, commonly known as the broad-leaf machilus, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically broader than those of related species. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by dark blue, fleshy fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
