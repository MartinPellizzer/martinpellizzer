Malacocarpus corynodes

Malacocarpus corynodes, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with deep red veins. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and green, typically blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
