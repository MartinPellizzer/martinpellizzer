Melilotus sulcatus subsp. brachystachys

Melilotus sulcatus subsp.

brachystachys, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed sweet clover, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and as a forage crop for livestock.
