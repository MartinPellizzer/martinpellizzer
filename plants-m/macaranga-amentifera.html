Macaranga amentifera

Macaranga amentifera, commonly known as the white-veined or common macaranga, is a tropical plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves with prominent venation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Macaranga amentifera is widely distributed in Southeast Asia and is often found in moist, disturbed habitats.
