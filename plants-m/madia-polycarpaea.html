Madia polycarpaea

Madia polycarpaea, commonly known as the California madia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and foliage with serrated edges. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, often found in coastal and inland regions. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color, appearing in late summer to early fall.

Madia polycarpaea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
