Microsorum pentaphyllum

Microsorum pentaphyllum, commonly known as the five-leaf water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves that are divided into five pairs of lobes, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in marshes and ponds. Its rhizomes form dense mats that help stabilize the substrate and provide habitat for small aquatic organisms.

Microsorum pentaphyllum is popular in aquascaping due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
