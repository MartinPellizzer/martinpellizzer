Myrcia acuminata var. tovarensis

Myrcia acuminata var. tovarensis, commonly known as Tovarensis Myrcia, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. It produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible and often consumed by birds and mammals.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential ecological importance in its native habitat.
