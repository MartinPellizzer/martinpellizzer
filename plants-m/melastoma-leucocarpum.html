Melastoma leucocarpum

Melastoma leucocarpum, commonly known as the white-fruited melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are usually pink or white in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and the plant is known for its ornamental value.

Melastoma leucocarpum has also been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
