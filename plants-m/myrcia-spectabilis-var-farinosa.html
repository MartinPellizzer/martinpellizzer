Myrcia spectabilis var. farinosa

Myrcia spectabilis var. farinosa, commonly known as the farinose myrtle, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or red, which attract pollinators such as birds and insects. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
