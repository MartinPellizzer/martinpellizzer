Mammillaria longimamma

Mammillaria longimamma, commonly known as the long-stemmed hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines and prominent tubercles. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
