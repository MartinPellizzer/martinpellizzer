Mimulus cordatus

Mimulus cordatus, commonly known as the heartleaf monkeyflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom from spring to summer. The plant thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and stream banks. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, due to their nectar-rich structure.

Mimulus cordatus is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
