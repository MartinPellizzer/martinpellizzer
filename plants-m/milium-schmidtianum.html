Milium schmidtianum

Milium schmidtianum, commonly known as the Schmidt's millet, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces tiny, white to pale green flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, typical of the Poaceae family. Milium schmidtianum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

It is a low-growing plant, typically reaching heights of less than 30 centimeters.
