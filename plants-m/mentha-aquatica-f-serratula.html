Mentha aquatica f. serratula

Mentha aquatica f. serratula, commonly known as water mint, is a variant of the water mint plant native to moist environments in Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in wet meadows, stream banks, and other waterlogged soils, showcasing its adaptability to humid conditions. Its leaves are often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for their cooling and digestive properties.

The fragrance of the plant is due to its high concentration of essential oils, which also attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
