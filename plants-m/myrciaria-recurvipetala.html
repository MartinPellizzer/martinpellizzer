Myrciaria recurvipetala

Myrciaria recurvipetala, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *cereja-brasileira*, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are edible, reddish when mature, and resemble small cherries, hence its common name.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
