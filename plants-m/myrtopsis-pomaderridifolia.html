Myrtopsis pomaderridifolia

Myrtopsis pomaderridifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers. It is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.

Its aromatic properties make it useful in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
