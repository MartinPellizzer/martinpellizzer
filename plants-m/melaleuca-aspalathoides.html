Melaleuca aspalathoides

Melaleuca aspalathoides, commonly known as the yellow bottlebrush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow floral spikes that resemble bottle brushes. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, aromatic flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils.

Melaleuca aspalathoides is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
