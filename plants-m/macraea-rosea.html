Macraea rosea

Macraea rosea, commonly known as the pink flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a vibrant display during the warmer months. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets, and its bark is smooth and often grayish in color. It thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its timber, which is used in various woodworking applications.
