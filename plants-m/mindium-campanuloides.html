Mindium campanuloides

Mindium campanuloides, commonly known as the bluebell bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Mindium campanuloides is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.
