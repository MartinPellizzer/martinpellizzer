Malveopsis geranioides var. galanderi

Malveopsis geranioides var. galanderi, commonly known as the Galander's malveopsis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton, and is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is usually green and somewhat hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
