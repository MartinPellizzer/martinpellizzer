Monarda aristata

Monarda aristata, commonly known as purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like flower heads that range from purple to pink in color, surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its showy, long-lasting blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.

The plant also has medicinal uses, with its leaves and flowers traditionally used to treat various ailments.
