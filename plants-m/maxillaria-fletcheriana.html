Maxillaria fletcheriana

Maxillaria fletcheriana, commonly known as the Fletcher's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are usually broad and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soil with moderate sunlight.

Maxillaria fletcheriana is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
