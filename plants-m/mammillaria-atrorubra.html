Mammillaria atrorubra

Mammillaria atrorubra, commonly known as the black-red hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny, ribbed structure. The plant features a dark red to black coloration, especially on the areoles, which are covered in white to yellowish hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, emerging from the areoles during the spring or summer.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
