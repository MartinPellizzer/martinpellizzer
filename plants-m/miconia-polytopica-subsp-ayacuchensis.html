Miconia polytopica subsp. ayacuchensis

Miconia polytopica subsp.

ayacuchensis, commonly known as the Ayacuchensis Miconia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Amazon rainforest. It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains numerous seeds.

This species is notable for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for various rainforest animals.
