Mediocalcar bulbophylloides

Mediocalcar bulbophylloides, commonly known as the bulbous rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, bulb-like underground stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces tall, erect stems with small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its leaves are succulent and often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Mediocalcar bulbophylloides is adapted to rocky habitats and is often found growing in crevices or on slopes.
