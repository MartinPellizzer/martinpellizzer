Mirabilis coccinea var. scabridata

Mirabilis coccinea var. scabridata, commonly known as the scarlet four o'clock, is a striking annual plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, tubular flowers that open in the late afternoon and close at dawn. The leaves are ovate with a rough, scabrous texture, giving the plant its varietal name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

This plant is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
