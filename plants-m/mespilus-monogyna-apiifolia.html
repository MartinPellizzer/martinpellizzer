Mespilus monogyna apiifolia

Mespilus monogyna apiifolia, commonly known as the common medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, fragrant, and edible fruits. The plant has compound leaves with serrated margins and produces small, yellowish flowers in spring. Its fruits, known as medlars, ripen in autumn and are typically harvested when they are partially decayed.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
