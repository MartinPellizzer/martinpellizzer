Myristica rubiginosa

Myristica rubiginosa, commonly known as the nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Banda Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is closely related to the mace plant, which produces the spice mace. The tree produces an orange-red fruit that contains the aromatic seeds used as nutmeg and mace. The plant has a complex chemical composition, including essential oils, alkaloids, and various volatile compounds.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a source of valuable spices and its ecological importance in tropical ecosystems.
