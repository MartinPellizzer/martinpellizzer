Micromyrtus trachycalyx

Micromyrtus trachycalyx, commonly known as the rough-barked apple, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and flowers. The plant has rough, fissured bark that peels in strips, giving it its common name. Its small, white or pale pink flowers appear in clusters and are followed by woody, apple-like fruits.

The species is adapted to a range of soil types and is often found in coastal and inland regions.
