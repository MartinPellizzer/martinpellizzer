Moringa myrepsica

Moringa myrepsica, commonly known as the African horseradish, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stem and pinnately compound leaves with a distinctive, slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Moringa myrepsica is adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in challenging climates.

Its leaves and roots are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in various cultures.
