Monardella viridis subsp. saxicola

Monardella viridis subsp.

saxicola, commonly known as green bluebead lily, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in open woodland or chaparral habitats. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This subspecies is notable for its ability to thrive in dry, sunny environments with well-drained soil.
