Mahonia imbricata

Mahonia imbricata, commonly known as the evergreen holly or leatherleaf mahonia, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant's leaves are dark green, leathery, and have a distinctive waxy texture. Mahonia imbricata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.

It is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
