Mikania clematidea

Mikania clematidea, commonly known as the creeping oxeye, is a vigorous climbing vine native to the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including disturbed soils and forest edges.

Due to its invasive nature, it is often considered a weed in agricultural and natural areas.
