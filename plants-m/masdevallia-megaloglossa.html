Masdevallia megaloglossa

Masdevallia megaloglossa, commonly known as the giant-lip masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated petals that can reach up to 20 centimeters in length. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree bark in humid, shaded forest environments. Its flowers are usually violet or purple, with a prominent, showy lip that distinguishes it from other orchid species.

Masdevallia megaloglossa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
