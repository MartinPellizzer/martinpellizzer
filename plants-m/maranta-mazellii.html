Maranta mazellii

Maranta mazellii, commonly known as the velvetleaf arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family, which includes many ornamental and economically important species. The plant is characterized by its striking, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy, velvety texture. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically arranged in spikes, while the roots are valued for their starchy properties.

Maranta mazellii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental foliage.
