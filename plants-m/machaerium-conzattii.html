Machaerium conzattii

Machaerium conzattii, commonly known as the red panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, pale green to reddish-brown inflorescences. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields or along roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Machaerium conzattii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its resilience and adaptability.
