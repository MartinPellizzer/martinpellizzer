Myrceugenia cumingii

Myrceugenia cumingii, commonly known as the velvetleaf myrtle, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to southern Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several small, edible seeds.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in subtropical regions for landscaping purposes.
