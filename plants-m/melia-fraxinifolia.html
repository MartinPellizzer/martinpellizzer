Melia fraxinifolia

Melia fraxinifolia, commonly known as the white fraxinella, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that are typically 5-10 cm in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by winged fruits that disperse its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

Melia fraxinifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
