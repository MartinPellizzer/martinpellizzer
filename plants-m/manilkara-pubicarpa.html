Manilkara pubicarpa

Manilkara pubicarpa, commonly known as the Brazilian sapote, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces yellowish-green flowers that develop into large, round fruits with a thick, yellow or orange rind. The fruit contains a single, starchy, edible pulp that is often used in cooking and traditional medicine.

Its latex is also valued for its potential industrial applications.
