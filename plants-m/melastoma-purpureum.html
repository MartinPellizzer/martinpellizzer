Melastoma purpureum

Melastoma purpureum, commonly known as the purple mallow, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its striking purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Melastoma purpureum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
