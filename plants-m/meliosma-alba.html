Meliosma alba

Meliosma alba, commonly known as the white meliosma, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or pale green underside. Its flowers are inconspicuous and clustered in panicles, and it produces small, round, black berries.

Meliosma alba is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
