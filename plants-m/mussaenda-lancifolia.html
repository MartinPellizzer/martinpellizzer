Mussaenda lancifolia

Mussaenda lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf Mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bright yellow or orange inflorescences. Its flowers are often grouped in terminal panicles, which are visually striking and attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
