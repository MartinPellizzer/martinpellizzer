Mentha longifolia var. dripanoidea

Mentha longifolia var. dripanoidea, commonly known as the Greek spearmint, is a variant of the spearmint species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in terminal clusters, which are typical of the mint family. Its aromatic leaves contain high levels of menthol, giving it a strong, cooling scent and flavor.

This variety is often used in traditional herbal remedies and as a culinary herb.
