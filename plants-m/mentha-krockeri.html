Mentha krockeri

Mentha krockeri, commonly known as the narrow-leaf peppermint, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. It has a strong, menthol-like scent, which is typical of many mint species.

Mentha krockeri is often used in herbal remedies and is valued for its medicinal and aromatic properties.
