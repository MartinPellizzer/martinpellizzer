Muhlenbergia longistolon

Muhlenbergia longistolon, commonly known as the tall muhlenbergia, is a perennial grass native to the United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and fine, silver-gray foliage that adds visual interest to landscapes. The plant produces fluffy, oat-like seed heads in the fall, which are attractive to birds and other wildlife. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Muhlenbergia longistolon is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
