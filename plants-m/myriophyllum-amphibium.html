Myriophyllum amphibium

Myriophyllum amphibium, commonly known as the water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has finely divided, feather-like leaves that help it absorb light in deep water. The plant produces small, green flowers that float on the water surface, which are adapted for pollination by insects. Myriophyllum amphibium plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen for aquatic life.

It is often used in restoration projects to improve water quality and stabilize sediments.
