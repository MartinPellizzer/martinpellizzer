Myrrhidium anemonifolium

Myrrhidium anemonifolium, commonly known as the anemonifolium myrrh.

It is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its showy, yellowish-orange flowers and distinctive, fern-like foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually basal, with a crisp texture and a slightly aromatic scent.

Myrrhidium anemonifolium is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
