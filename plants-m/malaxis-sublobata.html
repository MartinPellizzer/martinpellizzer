Malaxis sublobata

Malaxis sublobata, commonly known as the slender malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to the wetlands and forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, pale green to brownish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a short, horizontal rhizome with a few leaves and produces a single spike bearing several small, clustered flowers. Its botanical features include a labellum that is divided into two lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often found in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
