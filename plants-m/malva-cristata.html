Malva cristata

Malva cristata, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in temperate climates and well-drained soils. The plant has a sprawling growth habit and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
