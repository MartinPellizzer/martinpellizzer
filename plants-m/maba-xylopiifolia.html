Maba xylopiifolia

Maba xylopiifolia, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant wood that is highly valued in traditional medicine and perfumery. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, white flowers followed by small, dark purple berries.

The tree is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
