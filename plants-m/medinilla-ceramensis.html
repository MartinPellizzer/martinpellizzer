Medinilla ceramensis

Medinilla ceramensis, commonly known as the Ceram Island medinilla, is a flowering plant native to the Indonesian island of Ceram.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant features elliptical, glossy leaves and produces small, fleshy fruits that are often red or orange when mature.
