Myristica niohne

Myristica niohne, commonly known as the Malabar nut or Niohne nut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant produces large, fleshy berries that contain a single, hard-shelled seed. The seeds are valued for their nutty aroma and are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense evergreen forests.
