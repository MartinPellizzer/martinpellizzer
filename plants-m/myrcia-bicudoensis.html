Myrcia bicudoensis

Myrcia bicudoensis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Myrcia bicudoensis is often found in secondary growth and along stream banks, contributing to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
