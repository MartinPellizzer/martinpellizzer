Melanium alliaceum

Melanium alliaceum, commonly known as the garlic lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant is characterized by its strong garlic-like scent, which is released when the leaves are crushed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Melanium alliaceum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and distinct aroma.
