Myrcia clarendonensis

Myrcia clarendonensis, commonly known as the Clarendon myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean, specifically Jamaica, and is found in moist, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Myrcia clarendonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine by local communities.
