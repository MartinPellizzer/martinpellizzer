Mespilus maura

Mespilus maura, commonly known as the white mume, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and simple, alternate leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart fruits resembling small apples. The fruits are typically consumed fresh or used in traditional medicines.

It thrives in temperate climates and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
