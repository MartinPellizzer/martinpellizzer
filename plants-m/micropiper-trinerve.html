Micropiper trinerve

Micropiper trinerve, commonly known as the three-veined water plant, is a small aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has slender, branching stems that often grow in shallow water or damp soil. The leaves are narrow and have three distinct veins, giving it its scientific name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color.

This plant is often used in ornamental ponds and wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.
