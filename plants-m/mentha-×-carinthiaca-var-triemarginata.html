Mentha × carinthiaca var. triemarginata

Mentha × carinthiaca var. triemarginata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf peppermint, is a hybrid mint species characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and strong menthol scent.

It is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Central Europe, thriving in cool, moist environments. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a narrow margin, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is often cultivated for its essential oils and used in aromatherapy and herbal remedies.
