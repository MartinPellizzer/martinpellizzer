Minthostachys verticillata

Minthostachys verticillata, commonly known as the clustered mint, is a perennial herb native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and digestive properties.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
