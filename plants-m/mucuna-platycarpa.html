Mucuna platycarpa

Mucuna platycarpa, commonly known as the horsehead bean, is a climbing leguminous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive horsehead-shaped seed pods, which are typically green to brown in color. The plant produces small, white to violet flowers and has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. Mucuna platycarpa is known for its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in agricultural settings.

It also contains alkaloids and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
