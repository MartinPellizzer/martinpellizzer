Moltkia neubaueri

Moltkia neubaueri, commonly known as the Neubauer's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species in arid environments.

Moltkia neubaueri is also known for its milky sap, which is a characteristic feature of the milkweed genus.
