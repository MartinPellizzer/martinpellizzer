Macaglia disperma

Macaglia disperma, commonly known as the scattered macaglia, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
