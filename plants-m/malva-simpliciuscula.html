Malva simpliciuscula

Malva simpliciuscula, commonly known as the small-leaved mallows, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its hairy, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Malva simpliciuscula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in suitable conditions.
