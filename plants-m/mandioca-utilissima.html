Mandioca utilissima

Mandioca utilissima, commonly known as cassava, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is cultivated for its starchy root, which is a staple food in many tropical regions. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves and produces a woody stem that can grow up to several meters tall. The roots are typically white or yellow and have a rough, fibrous texture.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
