Mentha arvensis f. macrodonta

Mentha arvensis f. macrodonta, commonly known as the large-leaved field mint, is a variant of the field mint species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and strong, aromatic scent. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and woodland edges. It produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in herbal remedies.
