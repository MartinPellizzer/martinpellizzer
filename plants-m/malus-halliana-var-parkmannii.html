Malus halliana var. parkmannii

Malus halliana var. parkmannii, commonly known as the dwarf crabapple, is a compact, ornamental shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces edible, red-orange berries that attract birds and provide food for wildlife. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

This variety is particularly noted for its disease resistance and adaptability to various soil types.
