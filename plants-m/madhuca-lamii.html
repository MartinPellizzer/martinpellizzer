Madhuca lamii

Madhuca lamii, commonly known as the Philippine butter tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and large, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, fleshy fruits containing a single seed.

Its latex is used in traditional medicine, and the wood is valued for its durability and fine grain.
