Mimulus grandis

Mimulus grandis, commonly known as the yellow monkeyflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, bright yellow, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters, with simple, opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in stream banks and wet meadows.

Mimulus grandis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinator populations.
