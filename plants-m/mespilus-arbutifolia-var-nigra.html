Mespilus arbutifolia var. nigra

Mespilus arbutifolia var. nigra, commonly known as the black persimmon, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and produces small, edible fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soils. Its botanical features include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and flowers that are typically white or pale yellow.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and can be consumed raw or cooked.
