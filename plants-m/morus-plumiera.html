Morus plumiera

Morus plumiera, commonly known as the silky mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that give way to edible, black berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
