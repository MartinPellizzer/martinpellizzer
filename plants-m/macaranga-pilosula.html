Macaranga pilosula

Macaranga pilosula, commonly known as the hairy makara, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The leaves are simple, alternate, and typically have a hairy texture on both surfaces. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by spherical fruits that split open to release seeds.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in primary and secondary rainforests.
