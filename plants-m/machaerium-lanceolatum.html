Machaerium lanceolatum

Machaerium lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved machaerium, is a tropical shrub native to regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. The plant features trifoliate leaves with lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, and it is often found in moist, tropical environments.

Machaerium lanceolatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in nitrogen enrichment.
