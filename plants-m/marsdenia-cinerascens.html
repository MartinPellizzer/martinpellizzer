Marsdenia cinerascens

Marsdenia cinerascens, commonly known as the ash-gray Marsdenia, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, followed by follicles containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value is limited, but it is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and historical use in traditional medicine.
