Micromelum falcatum

Micromelum falcatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its simple, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, orange-yellow fruits. The fruit has a hard, woody rind and contains several seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appearance and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
