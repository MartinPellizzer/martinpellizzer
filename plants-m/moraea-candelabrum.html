Moraea candelabrum

Moraea candelabrum, commonly known as the candlestick lily, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of white, pink, or red. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette, contributing to its elegant appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its unique morphology and vibrant blooms, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
