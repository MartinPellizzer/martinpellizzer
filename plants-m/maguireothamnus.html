Maguireothamnus

Maguireothamnus, commonly known as the sticky goldenbush, is a member of the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and hairy, glandular leaves that produce a sticky secretion. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Native to the western United States, it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its unique botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and botanical study.
