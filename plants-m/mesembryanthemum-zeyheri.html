Mesembryanthemum zeyheri

Mesembryanthemum zeyheri, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or ozymandias, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday, a trait known as nyctinasty. Its fruit, which resembles a small, yellowish-orange berry, is edible and has a tangy flavor.

Mesembryanthemum zeyheri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
