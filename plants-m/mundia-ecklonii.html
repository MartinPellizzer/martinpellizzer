Mundia ecklonii

Mundia ecklonii, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red berries containing a single seed. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
