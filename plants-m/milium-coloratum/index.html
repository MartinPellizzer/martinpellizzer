Milium Coloratum

Common millet, scientifically known as Milium coloratum, is a small grain plant native to Asia and widely cultivated for its nutritional value and adaptability. It thrives in poor soils and arid conditions, making it a resilient crop for sustainable agriculture. The plant produces tiny, colorful seeds that have been used for food, animal feed, and traditional medicine for centuries. Its hardy nature and short growing season make it an important resource in many regions. Despite its humble appearance, Common millet holds significant cultural and ecological importance.
