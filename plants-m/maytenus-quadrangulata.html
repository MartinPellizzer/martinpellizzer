Maytenus quadrangulata

Maytenus quadrangulata, commonly known as the four-cornered mayten, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rough, quadrangular stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The species is valued for its hard wood and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
