Miconia secunpetiolaris

Miconia secunpetiolaris, commonly known as the red miconia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bright red flowers that are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a berry that turns from green to red as it matures, containing several seeds.

Miconia secunpetiolaris is also known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some areas.
