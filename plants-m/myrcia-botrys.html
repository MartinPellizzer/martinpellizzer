Myrcia botrys

Myrcia botrys, commonly known as the clustered myrtle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that grow in clusters, giving it its common name. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.
