Muraltia elsieae

Muraltia elsieae, commonly known as the Elsie's muraltia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
