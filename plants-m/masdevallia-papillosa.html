Masdevallia papillosa

Masdevallia papillosa, commonly known as the papillate masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, hairy or papillate structures on its petals and sepals, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and come in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate patterns.

Masdevallia papillosa is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its delicate appearance and relative ease of cultivation under the right conditions.
