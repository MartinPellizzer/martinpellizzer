Maranta pluriflora

Maranta pluriflora, commonly known as the velvetleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display intricate patterns in shades of green, red, and purple. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by the development of arrowhead-shaped tubers underground. These tubers are edible and have been traditionally used as a starch source in some regions.

Maranta pluriflora is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
