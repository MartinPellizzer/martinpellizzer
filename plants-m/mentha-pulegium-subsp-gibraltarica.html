Mentha pulegium subsp. gibraltarica

Mentha pulegium subsp.

gibraltarica, commonly known as Gibraltar mint, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula and North Africa. It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly menthol-like leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported calming and digestive properties.

However, it is important to note that the plant contains compounds that can be toxic in high doses.
