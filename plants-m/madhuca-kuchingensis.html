Madhuca kuchingensis

Madhuca kuchingensis, commonly known as the Kuching wax tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Borneo.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and large, glossy green leaves that are elliptical in shape. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, lowland areas and is often found in primary rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
