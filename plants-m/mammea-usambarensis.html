Mammea usambarensis

Mammea usambarensis, commonly known as the African mammea, is a small evergreen tree native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that are edible. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
