Mammillaria viereckii

Mammillaria viereckii, commonly known as the Vireck's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a distinctive rib structure and spines that are typically yellowish to brown in color. The plant produces white flowers that bloom in summer, adding a striking contrast to its spiny exterior. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for succulent collections and rock gardens.
