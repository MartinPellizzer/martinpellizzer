Machilus ichangensis

Machilus ichangensis, commonly known as the Ichang privet, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green on top, with a lighter underside. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it produces dark blue berries that are edible for birds.

Machilus ichangensis is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
