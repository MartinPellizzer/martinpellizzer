Meringium bartlettii

Meringium bartlettii, commonly known as the Bartlett's meringia, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a cool, humid environment to flourish.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
