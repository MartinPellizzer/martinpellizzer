Matthiola odoratissima f. virescens

Matthiola odoratissima f. virescens, commonly known as the green-flowered stock, is a cultivar of the stock plant, characterized by its distinctive greenish-yellow flowers.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is native to Europe. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, clustered in racemes, and bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its fresh, mild fragrance.
