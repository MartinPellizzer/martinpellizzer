Malcolmia brevipes

Malcolmia brevipes, commonly known as the narrowleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open, disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
