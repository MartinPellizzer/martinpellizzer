Microcodon linearis var. diffusus

Microcodon linearis var. diffusus, commonly known as the diffuse linear microcodon, is a lesser-known plant species within the Microcodon genus.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that exhibit a subtle variation in width compared to the standard variety. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forest understories. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white in color and bloom during the early spring.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
