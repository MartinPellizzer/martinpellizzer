Maxillaria virginalis

Maxillaria virginalis, commonly known as the Virgin's Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces pendulous, violet to purple blooms with white markings, which are often borne on branching inflorescences. Its pseudobulbs are cylindrical and clustered, supporting the plant's foliage and flowering structure.

Maxillaria virginalis prefers warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
