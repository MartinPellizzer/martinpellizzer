Melochia candelaris

Melochia candelaris, commonly known as the candlestick plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, the Americas, and Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its upright, branching structure. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, followed by orange to red berries. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
