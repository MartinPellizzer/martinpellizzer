Maireana scleroptera

Maireana scleroptera, commonly known as the ghost gum, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, twisted branches and small, pale green leaves that are covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in spikes, followed by woody, spherical fruits. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resistance to drought and salt.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for landscaping in dry regions.
