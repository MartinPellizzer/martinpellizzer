Malva pichleri

Malva pichleri, commonly known as the Sierra Madre mallow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, and it has hairy, lobed leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Malva pichleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
