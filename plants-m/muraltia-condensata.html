Muraltia condensata

Muraltia condensata, commonly known as the dwarf muraltia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and distinctive pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, arranged in racemes, and it produces small, round pods that contain seeds.

Muraltia condensata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
