Myrcia sparsiflora

Myrcia sparsiflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Myrcia sparsiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
