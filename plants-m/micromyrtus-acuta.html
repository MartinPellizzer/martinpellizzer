Micromyrtus acuta

Micromyrtus acuta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its distinctive bottlebrush-like inflorescences that produce bright red or pink flowers. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine, whitish wax layer, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal dunes.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
