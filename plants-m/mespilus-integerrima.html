Mespilus integerrima

Mespilus integerrima, commonly known as the European medlar, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that appear in spring. The tree produces small, round fruits that ripen in autumn, developing a unique, aromatic flavor when fully mature. Its botanical features include a rugged, often twisted trunk and a tendency to form dense, rounded crowns.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its historical use in traditional medicine.
