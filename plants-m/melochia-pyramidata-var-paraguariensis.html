Melochia pyramidata var. paraguariensis

Melochia pyramidata var. paraguariensis, commonly known as the Paraguayan melochia, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The plant produces showy, pale yellow to orange flowers that are typically clustered in terminal panicles. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some regions.
