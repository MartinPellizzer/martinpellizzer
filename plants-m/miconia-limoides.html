Miconia limoides

Miconia limoides, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are followed by distinctive, fleshy, orange-red berries. Miconia limoides is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a challenge for land managers.
