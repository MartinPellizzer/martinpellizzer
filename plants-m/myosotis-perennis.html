Myosotis perennis

Myosotis perennis, commonly known as the Persian violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer, typically on slender, branching stems. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Myosotis perennis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
