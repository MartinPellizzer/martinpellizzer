Masdevallia selenites

Masdevallia selenites, commonly known as the silver-haired masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, silver-gray foliage and intricate, often fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial or epiphytic orchid, thriving in cool, humid environments with high altitudes. Its unique appearance and rarity make it a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.

The name "selenites" refers to the moon-like appearance of its leaves, which are covered in a waxy, silvery coating.
