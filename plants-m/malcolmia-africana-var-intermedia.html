Malcolmia africana var. intermedia

Malcolmia africana var. intermedia, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with a white center, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has hairy, ribbed stems and heart-shaped, serrated leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies adds to its ecological value.
