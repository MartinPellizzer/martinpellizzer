Madhuca grandifolia

Madhuca grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaved tallow tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that are often dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The tree produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant has a shallow root system and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in tropical ecosystems.
