Monanthotaxis capea

Monanthotaxis capea, commonly known as the Cape mountain thorn, is a shrub native to the Eastern Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the dry, Mediterranean climate of its native habitat.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid regions.
