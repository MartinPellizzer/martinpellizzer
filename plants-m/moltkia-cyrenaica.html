Moltkia cyrenaica

Moltkia cyrenaica, commonly known as the Cyrenaica milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North Africa and the Middle East.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions and has a deep root system.

Botanically, it belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
