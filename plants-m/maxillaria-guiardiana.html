Maxillaria guiardiana

Maxillaria guiardiana, commonly known as the Guianan Maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers with a unique coloration. The plant typically features dark green, velvety leaves and has a pseudobulb structure typical of many orchids. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often violet or purple in color, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with contrasting patterns.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
