Malaxis sordida

Malaxis sordida, commonly known as the dirty lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and its unique inflorescence of small, pale green to brownish flowers that resemble a tresses of hair. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and grasslands. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces showy, lance-shaped leaves.

Malaxis sordida is relatively easy to cultivate in the right conditions, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
