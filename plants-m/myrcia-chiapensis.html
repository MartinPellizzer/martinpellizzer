Myrcia chiapensis

Myrcia chiapensis, commonly known as Chiapan myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central America, particularly in southern Mexico. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. It produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

Myrcia chiapensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in indigenous communities.
