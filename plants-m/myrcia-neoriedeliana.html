Myrcia neoriedeliana

Myrcia neoriedeliana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest biome in southeastern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant white or pink flowers. It produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when mature.

Myrcia neoriedeliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
