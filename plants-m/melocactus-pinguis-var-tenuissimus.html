Melocactus pinguis var. tenuissimus

Melocactus pinguis var. tenuissimus, commonly known as the slender melon cactus, is a rare and striking cactus native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical stems that are covered in a thick layer of white, waxy hair, which helps to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a splash of color to its otherwise desolate habitat. It is a slow-growing species that requires minimal water and well-draining soil to thrive.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often sought after by collectors and conservationists alike.
