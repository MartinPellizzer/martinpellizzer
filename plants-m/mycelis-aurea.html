Mycelis aurea

Mycelis aurea, commonly known as the golden mycelium, is a unique fungal species characterized by its vibrant yellow mycelial network.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in decaying organic matter such as logs and leaf litter. The plant's most notable botanical feature is its dense, golden-colored hyphae that form a complex underground web. It lacks traditional leaves and stems, existing primarily as a mycelial mat.

Mycelis aurea plays a crucial role in nutrient cycling within its ecosystem by decomposing organic material.
