Monimiastrum

Monimiastrum, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the remote regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that appear to shimmer in the sunlight, giving it a ghostly appearance. The plant produces small, pale flowers that bloom only under specific environmental conditions, making it difficult to study. Its unique morphology and elusive blooming cycle have made it a subject of fascination among botanists.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, Monimiastrum is considered critically endangered and is protected in its native range.
