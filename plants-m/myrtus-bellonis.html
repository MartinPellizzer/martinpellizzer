Myrtus bellonis

Myrtus bellonis, commonly known as the Corsican myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant features dark green, lance-shaped leaves with a fragrant, waxy texture. Its small white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by edible, berry-like fruits.

Myrtus bellonis is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and aromatic properties.
