Mahonia aquifolium var. lyallii

Mahonia aquifolium var. lyallii, commonly known as the Oregon grape, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features compound leaves with spiny margins, resembling holly, and produces bright yellow flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its clusters of blue-black berries that ripen in late summer. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.

This variety is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators and support local wildlife.
