Moringa longituba

Moringa longituba, commonly known as the African horseradish, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a member of the Moringaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Moringa longituba is adapted to arid environments and is often found in dry savannas and woodland areas.
