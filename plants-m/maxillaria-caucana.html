Maxillaria caucana

Maxillaria caucana, commonly known as the Caucana orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andes of South America.

It typically grows in humid, shaded environments at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 3,000 meters. The plant has a pseudobulb with fleshy leaves that store water, adapting to its mountainous habitat. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish, with intricate patterns and a distinctive lip.

Maxillaria caucana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
