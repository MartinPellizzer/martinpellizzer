Medicago tornata var. shepardii

Medicago tornata var. shepardii, commonly known as the shepard's vetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and open woodlands. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and is sometimes used in erosion control.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
