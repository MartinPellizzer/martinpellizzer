Mussaenda conopharyngiifolia

Mussaenda conopharyngiifolia, commonly known as the golden bell plant, is a flowering shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble bells, which are clustered at the ends of its branches. The plant has simple, opposite, and ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It thrives in tropical climates and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

Mussaenda conopharyngiifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
