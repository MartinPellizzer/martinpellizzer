Manilkara howardii

Manilkara howardii, commonly known as the golden apple or howard's manilkara, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by round, orange-red fruits resembling apples. The fruits are edible, with a sweet, juicy pulp and a single large seed.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of natural products.
