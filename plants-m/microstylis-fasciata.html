Microstylis fasciata

Microstylis fasciata, commonly known as the narrow-styled grass.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in moist soil conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinct veining pattern.

The flowers are small and clustered, often forming dense inflorescences that contribute to its ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
