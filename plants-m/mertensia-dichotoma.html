Mertensia dichotoma

Mertensia dichotoma, commonly known as the eastern bluebell, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
