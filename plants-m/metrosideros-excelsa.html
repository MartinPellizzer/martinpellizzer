Metrosideros excelsa

Metrosideros excelsa, commonly known as the Tahitian cherry or pohutukawa, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can reach heights of up to 15 meters. The plant is characterized by its distinctive red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing during the rainy season. Its leaves are lance-shaped and glossy, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant flowers.

The tree is well adapted to coastal environments and is often found growing on cliffs and rocky shores.
