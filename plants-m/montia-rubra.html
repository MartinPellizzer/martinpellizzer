Montia rubra

Montia rubra, commonly known as red-stemmed filaree, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Montiaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is often found in open areas such as meadows and stream banks. Its stems are reddish in color, which gives it its common name, and it blooms from spring to early summer.

Montia rubra is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
