Masdevallia aptera

Masdevallia aptera, commonly known as the naked-leaved masdevallia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs and narrow, strap-like leaves that are often glabrous, giving it its common name. The plant produces intricate, spurred flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, typically during the cooler months. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and high humidity.

Due to its delicate nature, it requires specific care, including well-drained soil and indirect light.
