Mentha suaveolens var. craspedota

Mentha suaveolens var. craspedota, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and fragrant, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its strong, minty aroma. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
