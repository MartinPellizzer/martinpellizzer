Myrcia kunthiana var. microphylla

Myrcia kunthiana var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved myrtle, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
