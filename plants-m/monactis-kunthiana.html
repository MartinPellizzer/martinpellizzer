Monactis kunthiana

Monactis kunthiana, commonly known as the desert cactus or Mexican cactus, is a species native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its spiny stems and ability to store water, making it well adapted to arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are pollinated by various insects. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Monactis kunthiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh conditions.
