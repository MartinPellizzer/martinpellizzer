Miconia paleacea

Miconia paleacea, commonly known as the white miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy, pale yellow to white flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Miconia paleacea is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
