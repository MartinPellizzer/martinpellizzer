Mimulus nummularius

Mimulus nummularius, commonly known as the copperleaf or money plant, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features heart-shaped, bright yellow flowers that bloom from spring to early summer. The leaves are thick, fleshy, and covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in dry conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

The plant's unique appearance and ability to thrive in various conditions contribute to its ornamental value.
