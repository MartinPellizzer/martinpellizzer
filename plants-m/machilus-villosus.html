Machilus villosus

Machilus villosus, commonly known as the hairy laurel, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its hairy leaves and fragrant bark. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture due to the fine pubescence.

Machilus villosus is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
