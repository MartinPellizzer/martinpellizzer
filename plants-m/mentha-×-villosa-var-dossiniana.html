Mentha × villosa var. dossiniana

Mentha × villosa var. dossiniana, commonly known as the Dossin's hairy mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, somewhat woolly stems and leaves that are typically oval to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue to purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile garden plant.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental value make it a popular choice for both culinary and horticultural use.
