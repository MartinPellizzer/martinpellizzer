Mosannona depressa

Mosannona depressa, commonly known as the Brazilian wax tree, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and dense, glossy dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Mosannona depressa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
