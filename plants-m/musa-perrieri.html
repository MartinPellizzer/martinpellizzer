Musa perrieri

Musa perrieri, commonly known as the African banana, is a species of herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and clustered inflorescences. The plant produces edible berries that are often consumed fresh or used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary uses, Musa perrieri is cultivated in various parts of the world.
