Mella sessiliflora

Mella sessiliflora, commonly known as the sessile-flower melia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval-shaped with a smooth margin. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
