Moquilea minutiflora

Moquilea minutiflora, commonly known as the dwarf quillwort, is a small aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It belongs to the plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its tiny, delicate flowers that are typically less than a centimeter in size. The plant has submerged and floating leaves, with the latter often bearing small, hair-like structures. It plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various microorganisms and invertebrates.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
