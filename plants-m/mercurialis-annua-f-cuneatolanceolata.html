Mercurialis annua f. cuneatolanceolata

Mercurialis annua f. cuneatolanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-lanceolate mercury plant, is a variant of the common mercury plant.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are typically lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the summer months. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

The f. cuneatolanceolata form is distinguished by its more elongated and narrow leaf morphology compared to the typical variety.
