Maxillaria machupicchuensis

Maxillaria machupicchuensis, commonly known as the Machu Picchu orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean highlands of Peru.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its intricate, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic growth habit. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, with intricate patterns and a distinct lip that aids in pollination.

This rare orchid is highly sought after by botanists and horticulturists due to its unique appearance and limited distribution.
