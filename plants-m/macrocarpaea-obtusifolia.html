Macrocarpaea obtusifolia

Macrocarpaea obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved macrocarpa, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It is typically found in coastal and subalpine regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
