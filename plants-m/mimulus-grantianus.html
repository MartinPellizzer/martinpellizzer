Mimulus grantianus

Mimulus grantianus, commonly known as the golden monkeyflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its showy, bright orange to yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are tubular at the base and flare out into five petals, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Mimulus grantianus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in streambanks and woodland edges.
