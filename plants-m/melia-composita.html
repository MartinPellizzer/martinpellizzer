Melia composita

Melia composita, commonly known as the composite melia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by fruiting bodies that resemble capsules.

Melia composita is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
