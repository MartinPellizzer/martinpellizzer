Myrcia laevis

Myrcia laevis, commonly known as the smooth myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces small, leathery berries that are typically red or black when mature.

Myrcia laevis is often found in coastal and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils and sunny to partially shaded environments.
