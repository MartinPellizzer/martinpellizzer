Mespilus cotoneaster nigra

Mespilus cotoneaster nigra, commonly known as the black cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during late spring, followed by dark red to black berries in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The species is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
