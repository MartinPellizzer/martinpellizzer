Milium vulgare var. divaricatum

Milium vulgare var. divaricatum, commonly known as the slender field millet, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and meadows. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of small, spike-like clusters of bracteate flowers, which produce tiny seeds.

It is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its low-growing habit.
