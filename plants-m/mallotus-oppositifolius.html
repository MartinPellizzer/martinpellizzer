Mallotus oppositifolius

Mallotus oppositifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf mallot, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its foliage is used in traditional medicine, and it has ornamental value due to its attractive leaves.
