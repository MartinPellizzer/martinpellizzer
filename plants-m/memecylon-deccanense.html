Memecylon deccanense

Memecylon deccanense, commonly known as the Deccan memecylon, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with smooth margins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by bright red or orange berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense undergrowth of tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
