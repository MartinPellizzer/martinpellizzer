Mentha longifolia var. jurana

Mentha longifolia var. jurana, commonly known as the Jurana mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. Its essential oils contain menthol and other compounds, giving it a sharp, cooling fragrance.

This variety is valued both for ornamental purposes and for its use in herbal teas and aromatherapy.
