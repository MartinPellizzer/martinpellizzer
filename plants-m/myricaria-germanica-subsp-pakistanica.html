Myricaria germanica subsp. pakistanica

Myricaria germanica subsp.

pakistanica, commonly known as the Kashmir rose or Pakistan rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Pakistan and northern India. It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with numerous small, clustered flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive, fragrant flowers.
