Mucuna diabolica

Mucuna diabolica, commonly known as the devil's bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, twisted tendrils that aid in its vine-like growth. The plant produces showy, crimson flowers followed by elongated pods containing dark, glossy seeds. It is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it both a valuable forage crop and an invasive weed.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
