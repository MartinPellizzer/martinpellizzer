Mimosa rixosa

Mimosa rixosa, commonly known as the Brazilian mimosa, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It features delicate, feathery leaves that fold shut when touched, a characteristic known as thigmonastic response. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, pale green pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its adaptability and unique foliage make it a notable species in botanical gardens and native landscapes.
