Morphixia capillaris

Morphixia capillaris, commonly known as the slender morphixia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to certain regions of South America.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in the early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a basal rosette.

Morphixia capillaris is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny environments.
