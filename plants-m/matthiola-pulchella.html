Matthiola pulchella

Matthiola pulchella, commonly known as the sweet William, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and produces small, elongated pods containing seeds. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately lobed, and covered in fine hairs.

Matthiola pulchella is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
