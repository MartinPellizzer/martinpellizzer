Malus coronaria var. lancifolia

Malus coronaria var. lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved apple or the red-flowered crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible apples that turn red or yellow in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening for its showy blooms.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and adaptability to various climates.
