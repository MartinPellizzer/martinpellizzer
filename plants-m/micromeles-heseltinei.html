Micromeles heseltinei

Micromeles heseltinei, commonly known as the Heseltine's rock fig, is a small, shrub-like plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woody structure and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, spherical fruits resembling tiny figs. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
