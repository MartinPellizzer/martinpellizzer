Mandevilla fistulosa

Mandevilla fistulosa, commonly known as the trumpet vine, is a striking tropical vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant has thick, woody stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its vibrant blooms.

Mandevilla fistulosa is also known for its ability to climb and can be trained to grow on trellises or other supports.
