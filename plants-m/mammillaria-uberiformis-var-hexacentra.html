Mammillaria uberiformis var. hexacentra

Mammillaria uberiformis var. hexacentra, commonly known as the six-pointed mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white to light yellow spines. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and compact size, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections.
