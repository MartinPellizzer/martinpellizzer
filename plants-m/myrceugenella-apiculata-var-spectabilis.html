Myrceugenella apiculata var. spectabilis

Myrceugenella apiculata var. spectabilis, commonly known as the Chilean firetree, is a species native to the coastal regions of southern Chile.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and showy red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to the temperate climate of its native habitat. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

The flowers attract pollinators such as birds and insects, contributing to local biodiversity.
