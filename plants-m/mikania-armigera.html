Mikania armigera

Mikania armigera, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a vigorous climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively, often becoming invasive in new environments. Its stems are hairy and can twine around other plants or structures.

Mikania armigera is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and forest edges, where it can thrive in partial to full sunlight.
