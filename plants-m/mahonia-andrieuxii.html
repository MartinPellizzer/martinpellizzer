Mahonia andrieuxii

Mahonia andrieuxii, commonly known as the Evergreen Mahonia, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with leathery, dark green leaves that have a distinctive spiny margin. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in dense, upright inflorescences. These flowers are followed by blue-black berries that are attractive to birds.

Mahonia andrieuxii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to a variety of soil types.
