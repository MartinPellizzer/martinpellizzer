Moehringia muscosa var. sedoides

Moehringia muscosa var. sedoides, commonly known as the mossy moehringia, is a variant of the Moehringia muscosa species, which belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming plant that typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as rocky crevices or forest floors. The plant features small, overlapping leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of moss, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its flowers are usually pale pink or white, with five petals arranged symmetrically.

This variant is characterized by its compact growth habit and its ability to form dense, carpet-like colonies in suitable habitats.
