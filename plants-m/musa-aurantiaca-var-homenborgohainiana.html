Musa aurantiaca var. homenborgohainiana

Musa aurantiaca var. homenborgohainiana, commonly known as the Homenborgohainiana banana, is a rare and ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with deep green to red hues. The plant produces large, elongated leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in length, making it a visually striking addition to gardens. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

While it is not commonly cultivated for fruit, it is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often grown as a houseplant or in tropical landscapes.
