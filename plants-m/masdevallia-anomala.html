Masdevallia anomala

Masdevallia anomala, commonly known as the anomalous masdevallia, is a rare and striking orchid native to the high-altitude cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, often colorful floral structure and unique growth habit, typically growing as an epiphyte on trees. The plant features long, narrow leaves and produces delicate, spurred flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in cool, humid environments with dappled sunlight, making it a challenging but rewarding species for orchid enthusiasts.

Due to its limited natural habitat and specific care requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized plant in horticulture.
