Medeola latifolia

Medeola latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf false shamrock, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Melanthiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which resemble those of the shamrock. The plant produces small, white to greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are a key feature of its reproductive cycle. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of deciduous forests.

Medeola latifolia is also noted for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally by indigenous peoples for various health purposes.
