Mespilus monogyna proles splendens

Mespilus monogyna proles splendens, commonly known as the white sloe, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces drupes, or berries, that turn from green to dark purple as they mature. These fruits are often used in culinary applications, such as jams and wines, and are also valued for their ornamental appeal.

The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
