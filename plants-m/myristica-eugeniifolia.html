Myristica eugeniifolia

Myristica eugeniifolia, commonly known as the nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy berries. The plant is closely related to the nutmeg and mace-producing species of the Myristica genus. Its fruits contain a hard, aromatic seed known as the nutmeg, which is widely used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its valuable spice.
