Marsdenia argillicola

Marsdenia argillicola, commonly known as the clay soil Marsdenia, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its trailing or ascending stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels. It thrives in moist, clay-rich soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
