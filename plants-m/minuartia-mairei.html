Minuartia mairei

Minuartia mairei, commonly known as the Maire's sandwort, is a low-growing flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of China, particularly in Sichuan and Yunnan provinces. The plant features small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to thrive in rocky and arid environments.

Minuartia mairei is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh climates.
