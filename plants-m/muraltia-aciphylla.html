Muraltia aciphylla

Muraltia aciphylla, commonly known as the white muraltia, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy dark green leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, round, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Muraltia aciphylla is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
