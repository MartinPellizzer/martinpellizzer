Myrcia prunifolia var. angustior

Myrcia prunifolia var. angustior, commonly known as narrowleaf myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the subtropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several small, elliptical seeds.
