Malus macounii

Malus macounii, commonly known as the Macoun apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to eastern North America, particularly the Appalachian Mountains. The plant produces small, ornamental flowers in early spring, followed by edible fruits that ripen in late summer. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall.

Malus macounii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in apple cultivation.
