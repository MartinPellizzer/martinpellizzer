Menodora hispida

Menodora hispida, commonly known as the rough menodora, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Menodoridae and is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rough texture.

Menodora hispida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
