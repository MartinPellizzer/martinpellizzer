Malapoenna rugosa

Malapoenna rugosa, commonly known as the Malapoenna rosea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
