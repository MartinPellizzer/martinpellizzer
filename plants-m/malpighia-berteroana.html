Malpighia berteroana

Malpighia berteroana, commonly known as the Brazilian wolfberry or red currant tree, is a small shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its bright red, fleshy fruits that resemble small currants. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are small, white or pale pink, and often clustered in inflorescences, contributing to its ornamental value.
