Musa borneensis var. flavida

Musa borneensis var. flavida, commonly known as the golden banana, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and clustered, yellow-orange fruit. The plant typically grows in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its fruit is edible and has a sweet, slightly tangy flavor, making it a popular crop in its native regions.

The plant also has ornamental value due to its striking foliage and vibrant fruit.
