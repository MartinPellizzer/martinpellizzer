Myrcia alloiota

Myrcia alloiota, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and showy pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that contain aromatic seeds.

Myrcia alloiota is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
