Mammillaria wrightii var. wilcoxii

Mammillaria wrightii var. wilcoxii, commonly known as the Wilcox mammillaria, is a compact cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a rounded, globular stem covered in dense, white to pale yellow spines. The plant produces small, pink to red flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid environments.

Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
