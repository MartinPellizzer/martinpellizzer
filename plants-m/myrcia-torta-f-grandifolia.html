Myrcia torta f. grandifolia

Myrcia torta f. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered myrcia, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It features elliptical to ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in clusters at the ends of branches. These flowers are followed by small, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
