Malesherbia humilis var. propinqua

Malesherbia humilis var. propinqua, commonly known as the dwarf malesherbia, is a low-growing shrub native to South America.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
