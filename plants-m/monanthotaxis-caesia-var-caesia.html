Monanthotaxis caesia var. caesia

Monanthotaxis caesia var. caesia, commonly known as the blue bell orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a white or pink throat. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, thriving on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
