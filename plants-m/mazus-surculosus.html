Mazus surculosus

Mazus surculosus, commonly known as the woolly mazus, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, fuzzy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or purple, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Due to its compact growth habit and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
