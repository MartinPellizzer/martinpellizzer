Melasma natalense

Melasma natalense, commonly known as the Natal melasma, is a rare flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming during the cooler months. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its arid environment.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
