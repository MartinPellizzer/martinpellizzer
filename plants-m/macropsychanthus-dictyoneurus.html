Macropsychanthus dictyoneurus

Macropsychanthus dictyoneurus, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, ghost-like appearance, with translucent, waxy leaves that resemble spectral forms. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom at night, emitting a faint, sweet scent to attract nocturnal pollinators. Due to its extreme sensitivity to environmental conditions, it is highly adapted to survive in harsh, low-nutrient soils.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for both botanists and conservationists.
