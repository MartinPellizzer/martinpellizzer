Masdevallia trinema

Masdevallia trinema, commonly known as the silver-veined masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery or pale green waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming during the cooler months. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for collectors and enthusiasts of rare orchids.
