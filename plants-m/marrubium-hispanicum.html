Marrubium hispanicum

Marrubium hispanicum, commonly known as Spanish horehound, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and small blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It belongs to the family Boraginaceae and is known for its medicinal properties.

Marrubium hispanicum has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its expectorant and anti-inflammatory effects.
