Manihot bahiensis

Manihot bahiensis, commonly known as Brazilian arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, green flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its roots are edible and have a starchy, mild flavor, making it a valuable food source.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
