Monoporidium

Monoporidium, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and spiny, leaf-like bracts that surround the flower head. Native to the Mediterranean region, Monoporidium thrives in coastal environments and well-drained soils. The plant's structure includes a short, stout stem with alternate, lance-shaped leaves.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and coastal landscapes.
