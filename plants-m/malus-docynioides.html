Malus docynioides

Malus docynioides, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces small, edible pears that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.
