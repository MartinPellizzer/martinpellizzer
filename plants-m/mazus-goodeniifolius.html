Mazus goodeniifolius

Mazus goodeniifolius, commonly known as the goodeniifolius mazus, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features prostrate stems that spread along the ground, often rooting at the nodes. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a slightly hairy surface and a serrated edge.

Mazus goodeniifolius is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in rocky or well-drained soils.
