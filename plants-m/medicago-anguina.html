Medicago anguina

Medicago anguina, commonly known as the spurred medic, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its seeds are enclosed in legumes, which are characteristic of the Fabaceae family.

Medicago anguina is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
