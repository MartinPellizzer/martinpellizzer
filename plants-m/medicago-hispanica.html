Medicago hispanica

Medicago hispanica, commonly known as Spanish clover, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial legume that grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical heads. It is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in agricultural settings.

Its hardy nature and adaptability contribute to its widespread distribution across temperate regions.
