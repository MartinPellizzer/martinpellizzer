Magnolia zenii

Magnolia zenii, commonly known as the Japanese magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its showy, saucer-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a subtle fragrance.

Magnolia zenii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
