Mulinum triacanthum var. famatinense

Mulinum triacanthum var. famatinense, commonly known as the Famatinian thimbleweed, is a perennial herb native to the high elevations of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in open, sunny habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped with three prominent spines on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is a relatively rare species, often found in specific microhabitats within its native range.
