Mimosa watsonii

Mimosa watsonii, commonly known as the Watson's sensitive plant, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the arid regions of Southwestern United States and Northern Mexico. The plant is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched, a trait known as thigmonastic response. It produces small, yellow, spherical flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Mimosa watsonii is adapted to thrive in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
