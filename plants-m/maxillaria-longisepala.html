Maxillaria longisepala

Maxillaria longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepaled maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant features prominent, elongated sepals and petals, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Maxillaria longisepala is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
