Mucuna miniata

Mucuna miniata, commonly known as the dwarf velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and it is known for its rapid growth habit. Mucuna miniata is also notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

However, it can be invasive in certain environments if not managed properly.
