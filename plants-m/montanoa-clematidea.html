Montanoa clematidea

Montanoa clematidea, commonly known as Mexican clematis, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows as a perennial shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it an important species in local ecosystems.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
