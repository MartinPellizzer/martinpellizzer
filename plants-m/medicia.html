Medicia

Medicia, commonly known as the medicinal herb, is a plant belonging to the family Apiaceae, which includes other aromatic plants like parsley and carrots.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like leaves and small, clustered white or yellow flowers. Medicia is native to temperate regions and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its leaves and seeds are often harvested for culinary and medicinal purposes.
