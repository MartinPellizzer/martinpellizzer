Marsdenia burmanica

Marsdenia burmanica, commonly known as Burman's Marsdenia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Myanmar, Thailand, and Vietnam. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals and prominent stamens, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

Marsdenia burmanica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
