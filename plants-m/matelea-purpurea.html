Matelea purpurea

Matelea purpurea, commonly known as the purple matelea, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments.

Matelea purpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
