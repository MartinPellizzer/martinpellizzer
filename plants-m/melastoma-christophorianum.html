Melastoma christophorianum

Melastoma christophorianum, commonly known as the red-veined melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its distinctive red veins on its dark green leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in warm climates.
