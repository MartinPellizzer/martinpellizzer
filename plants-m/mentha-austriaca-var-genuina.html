Mentha austriaca var. genuina

Mentha austriaca var. genuina, commonly known as the Austrian mint, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a strong mint aroma and is often used in herbal teas and traditional remedies.

Its leaves are rich in essential oils, contributing to its medicinal and aromatic properties.
