Melaleuca asterocarpa subsp. asterocarpa

Melaleuca asterocarpa subsp.

asterocarpa, commonly known as the white-stemmed melaleuca, is a species native to northern Australia. It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows in coastal and wetland areas.

The plant is characterized by its smooth, white or pale grey bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. Its flowers are small and typically occur in dense clusters, producing a distinctive aromatic scent.

The plant is well adapted to saline and waterlogged soils, making it a common feature in mangrove and estuarine ecosystems.
