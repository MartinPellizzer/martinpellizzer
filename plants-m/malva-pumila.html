Malva pumila

Malva pumila, commonly known as the small mallow, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a soft, hairy texture and produces pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders. Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.

Malva pumila is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
