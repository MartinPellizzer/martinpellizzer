Marcgravia domingensis

Marcgravia domingensis, commonly known as the "Domingo's sword fern," is a climbing epiphytic plant native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its broad, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking spadices, which are specialized inflorescences that house both male and female flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in the understory of dense forests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is a popular specimen in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
