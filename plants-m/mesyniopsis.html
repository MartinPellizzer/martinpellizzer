Mesyniopsis

Mesyniopsis, commonly known as the "false dandelion," is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in dry, disturbed soils. Mesyniopsis has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.

It is sometimes used in herbal medicine for its purported diuretic properties.
