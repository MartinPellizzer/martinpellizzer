Musa × paradisiaca subsp. sapientum

Musa × paradisiaca subsp.

sapientum, commonly known as the sweet orange, is a citrus fruit tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and flowering clusters that produce small, fragrant flowers. The fruit is typically round or oval, with a thick, yellow rind and juicy, segmented flesh that is rich in vitamin C. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is widely cultivated for its fruit, which is used in food, beverages, and medicinal applications.
