Mida crassifolia

Mida crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed mida, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Mida crassifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
