Maclura tinctoria

Maclura tinctoria, commonly known as the yellow wood or honey locust, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange bark and compound leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright orange, woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides.

Due to its vibrant color and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated for landscaping purposes.
