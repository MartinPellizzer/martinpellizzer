Momordica elegans

Momordica elegans, commonly known as the elegant bitter melon, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, warty fruits that are typically green when immature and turn orange-red when mature. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces yellow flowers that are often clustered in groups of three. Its fruits are used in various cuisines, particularly in Asian cooking, and are valued for their unique flavor and medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
