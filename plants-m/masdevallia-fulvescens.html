Masdevallia fulvescens

Masdevallia fulvescens, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often golden or orange-colored flowers, which are typically small to medium in size. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapting to its humid, shaded habitat.

Masdevallia fulvescens thrives in cool, moist environments and requires careful attention to light and humidity levels for optimal growth.
