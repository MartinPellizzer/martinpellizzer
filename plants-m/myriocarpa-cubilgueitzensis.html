Myriocarpa cubilgueitzensis

Myriocarpa cubilgueitzensis, commonly known as the Andean thimblecactus, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its unique, elongated, thimble-shaped fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments and has adapted to survive in harsh, high-altitude conditions. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces small, edible fruits that are used in local cuisine.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its ecological significance and potential medicinal properties.
