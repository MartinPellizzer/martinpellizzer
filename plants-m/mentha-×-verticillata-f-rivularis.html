Mentha × verticillata f. rivularis

Mentha × verticillata f. rivularis, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its aromatic leaves and flowering spikes.

It is a hybrid species within the Mentha genus, known for its ability to thrive in moist environments such as wetlands and stream sides. The plant features opposite, oval-shaped leaves with a serrated margin and a strong, cooling menthol scent. Its flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and typically bloom in shades of purple or pink.

This variety is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine due to its medicinal properties.
