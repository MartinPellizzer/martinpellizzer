Molineria fakimense

Molineria fakimense, commonly known as the Fakim mountain grass, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, which are often overlooked due to their modest appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or on rocky slopes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
