Mikania archidonensis

Mikania archidonensis, commonly known as the Archidendron mikania, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by cylindrical, woody fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its rapid growth and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
