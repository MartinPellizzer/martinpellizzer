Mentha × macrandria

Mentha × macrandria, commonly known as hybrid mint, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Mentha viridis and Mentha suaveolens.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. It is widely cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy, culinary applications, and traditional medicine.

Its rapid growth and spreading habit make it a popular choice for gardens and herbal teas.
