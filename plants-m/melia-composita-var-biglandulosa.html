Melia composita var. biglandulosa

Melia composita var. biglandulosa, commonly known as the large-fruited chinaberry, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and have a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange to yellow berries containing several seeds. Its bark is smooth and light gray, and it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to various parts of the world for landscaping purposes.
