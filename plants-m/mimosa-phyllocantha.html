Mimosa phyllocantha

Mimosa phyllocantha, commonly known as the desert mimosa, is a shrub native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to survive in drought conditions, making it well-adapted to harsh desert environments. Its small, fluffy seed pods release seeds when disturbed, aiding in its propagation.

The plant is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils and provide habitat for desert wildlife.
