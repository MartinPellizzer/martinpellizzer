Moringa borziana

Moringa borziana, commonly known as the desert mallow, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its woody stems, trifoliate leaves, and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is highly drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves and seeds are edible and have been used in traditional medicine for their nutritional and medicinal properties.

Moringa borziana plays an important role in ecological restoration and sustainable agriculture in dryland ecosystems.
