Musa gigantea

Musa gigantea, commonly known as the giant banana plant, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its massive, broad leaves that can reach lengths of up to 4 meters. The plant produces large, clustered inflorescences that eventually develop into edible bananas. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained, fertile soil.

Due to its rapid growth and potential for spreading, it is often considered an invasive species in certain regions.
