Maharanga cingulata

Maharanga cingulata, commonly known as the spiral bamboo, is a unique plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral growth pattern, which gives it a striking visual appearance. The plant belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is known for its tall, slender stalks. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, often forming a dense canopy.

Due to its rare and specialized habitat, it is considered a vulnerable species facing threats from habitat loss.
