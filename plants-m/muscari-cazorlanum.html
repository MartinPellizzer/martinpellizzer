Muscari cazorlanum

Muscari cazorlanum, commonly known as the Cazorlan bluebell, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers in early spring. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that are evergreen in warmer climates. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

Muscari cazorlanum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and naturalized areas.
