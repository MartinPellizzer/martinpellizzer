Magnolia javanica

Magnolia javanica, commonly known as the Javan magnolia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white or pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has broad, glossy leaves and can grow as a tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers emit a strong, sweet fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Magnolia javanica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
