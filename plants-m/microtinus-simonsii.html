Microtinus simonsii

Microtinus simonsii, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in harsh, dry conditions. Its leaves are typically narrow and succulent, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Microtinus simonsii plays an important role in desert ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil in sandy environments.
