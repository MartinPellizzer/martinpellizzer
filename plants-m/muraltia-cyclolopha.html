Muraltia cyclolopha

Muraltia cyclolopha, commonly known as the rock apple or wild mango, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with a leathery texture and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant bears edible, orange-red berries that are often consumed raw or used in traditional medicine. Its adaptability to rocky and arid soils makes it a resilient species in challenging environments.

Muraltia cyclolopha is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
