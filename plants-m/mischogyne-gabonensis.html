Mischogyne gabonensis

Mischogyne gabonensis, commonly known as Gabon arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and a rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces an inflorescence called a spadix, which is surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Its rhizomes are edible and are used as a starchy root vegetable in local cuisines.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
