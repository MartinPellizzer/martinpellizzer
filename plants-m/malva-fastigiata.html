Malva fastigiata

Malva fastigiata, commonly known as the upright mallows, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, with hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pale pink to purple flowers with five petals, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or disturbed areas.

Malva fastigiata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
