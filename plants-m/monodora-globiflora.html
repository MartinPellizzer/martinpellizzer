Monodora globiflora

Monodora globiflora, commonly known as the African starfruit or African mango, is a tropical shrub native to West and Central Africa.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae, which includes plants like the cashew and poison ivy. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its most distinctive feature is its yellow, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by fleshy, orange-yellow fruits resembling small starfruits.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
