Myrcia albotomentosa

Myrcia albotomentosa, commonly known as the white-tomentose myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are covered in fine, white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that contains numerous seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
