Marsdenia trilobata

Marsdenia trilobata, commonly known as the three-lobed milkvine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiny fruits containing seeds. It is often found in humid, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
