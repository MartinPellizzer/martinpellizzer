Magnolia guatemalensis subsp. hondurensis

Magnolia guatemalensis subsp.

hondurensis, commonly known as the Honduran magnolia, is a species native to Central America. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring.

The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves and can grow up to several meters in height. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
