Magnolia savegrensis

Magnolia savegrensis, commonly known as the Mexican magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the highlands of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that are dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. Its bark is smooth and grayish, becoming fissured with age.

Magnolia savegrensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
