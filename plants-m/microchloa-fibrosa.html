Microchloa fibrosa

Microchloa fibrosa, commonly known as the fibrous microchloa, is a small, grass-like plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia and parts of Africa. The plant is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and slender, fibrous stems. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Microchloa fibrosa is adapted to grow in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
