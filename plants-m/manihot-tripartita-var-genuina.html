Manihot tripartita var. genuina

Manihot tripartita var. genuina, commonly known as the arrowleaf batata, is a tropical tuberous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and edible underground storage roots. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used as a food source by indigenous communities.

The species is also valued for its ornamental foliage, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
