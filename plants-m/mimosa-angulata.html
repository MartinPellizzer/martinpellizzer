Mimosa angulata

Mimosa angulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mimosas, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are often mistaken for the leaves of the mimosa pudica due to their similar appearance. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in arid environments.

Mimosa angulata is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
