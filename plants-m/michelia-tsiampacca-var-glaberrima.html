Michelia tsiampacca var. glaberrima

Michelia tsiampacca var. glaberrima, commonly known as the silky magnolia, is a flowering plant native to southern China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are broad, ovate, and have a smooth, glabrous surface, giving the plant its varietal name. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
