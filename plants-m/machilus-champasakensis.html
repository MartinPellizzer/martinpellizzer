Machilus champasakensis

Machilus champasakensis, commonly known as the Cambodian laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in Cambodia, Vietnam, and Thailand. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a glossy surface and a strong, spicy aroma.

Machilus champasakensis is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
