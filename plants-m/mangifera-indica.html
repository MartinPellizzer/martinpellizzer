Mangifera indica

Mangifera indica, commonly known as the mango tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and produces a fruit with a thick, fibrous peel enclosing a sweet, juicy pulp. The plant belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is valued for its edible fruit, which is rich in vitamins and minerals. Its growth is typically slow, and it can reach heights of up to 30 meters in optimal conditions.

The mango tree is also known for its ornamental value and is widely cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions worldwide.
