Melica decumbens

Melica decumbens, commonly known as the prostrate brome, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and disturbed areas. The plant has a low, spreading growth habit with slender, leafy stems that often lie close to the ground. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are small, clustered flower clusters.

Melica decumbens is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
