Merremia digitata var. elongata

Merremia digitata var. elongata, commonly known as the longleaf morning glory, is a vigorous climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically a deep green in color. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue or purple. Its stems are fibrous and can grow quite long, often reaching several meters in length.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
