Miconia obovata

Miconia obovata, commonly known as the broadleaf miconia, is a species of flowering plant in the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its large, broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences with white or pink flowers that attract various pollinators. It is often found in moist, shaded areas of lowland forests.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a problematic species in certain ecosystems.
