Malva parviflora subsp. oxyloba

Malva parviflora subsp.

oxyloba, commonly known as the narrowleaf mallow, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces showy pink to purple flowers with five petals.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. It has a hairy stem and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to the typical form of Malva parviflora.
