Mapouria crassa

Mapouria crassa, commonly known as the thick-leaved mapouria, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its large, leathery, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
