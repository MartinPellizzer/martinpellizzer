Myrciaria disticha var. fluminensis

Myrciaria disticha var. fluminensis, commonly known as the Brazilian guava or araçá-do-brejo, is a tropical plant native to Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in wet, lowland areas. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent in local cuisines.
