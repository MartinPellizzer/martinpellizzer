Myrtus dichotoma

Myrtus dichotoma, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, aromatic leaves that are typically dark green and slightly leathery in texture. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark blue berries. Myrtus dichotoma is well adapted to rocky soils and can thrive in full sun or partial shade.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for hedges and ornamental gardening.
