Melica andina var. laxissima

Melica andina var. laxissima, commonly known as the Andean milletgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its inflorescence consists of a loose, open panicle with small spikelets, which are usually green or reddish in color.

Melica andina var. laxissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
