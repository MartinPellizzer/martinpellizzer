Medinilla triangularis

Medinilla triangularis, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or velvet bell, is a tropical shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves that give it a velvety appearance. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. Medinilla triangularis thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-draining soil.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and long-lasting blooms.
