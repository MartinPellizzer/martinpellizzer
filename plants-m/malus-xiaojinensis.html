Malus xiaojinensis

Malus xiaojinensis, commonly known as the Xiaojin apple, is a deciduous species native to China.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy white to pink flowers in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
