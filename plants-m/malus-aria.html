Malus aria

Malus aria, commonly known as the apple blossom, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible apples that are typically used for culinary purposes. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin.

Malus aria is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and fragrance.
