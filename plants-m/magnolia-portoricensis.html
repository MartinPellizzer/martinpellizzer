Magnolia portoricensis

Magnolia portoricensis, commonly known as the Puerto Rican magnolia, is a flowering plant native to the island of Puerto Rico.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 10 to 15 meters. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that bloom in the spring, often before the leaves emerge. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with a smooth texture and a glossy surface.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also important in the ecological makeup of Puerto Rico's forests.
