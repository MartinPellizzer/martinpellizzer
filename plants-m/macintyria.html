Macintyria

Macintyria, commonly known as the Macintyria lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. Macintyria is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.

However, it is relatively rare in the wild and requires specific growing conditions to thrive.
