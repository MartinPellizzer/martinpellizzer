Mikania pernambucensis

Mikania pernambucensis, commonly known as the Brazilian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its milky sap, which is a characteristic feature of the genus Mikania, can be toxic to some animals.

It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and clearings.
