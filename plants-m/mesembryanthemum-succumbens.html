Mesembryanthemum succumbens

Mesembryanthemum succumbens, commonly known as the "Overshoot" ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, characterized by its fleshy leaves and colorful flowers. The plant has a sprawling growth habit and produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and yellow. Its leaves are typically blue-green and covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss.

It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
