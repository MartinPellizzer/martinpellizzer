Malus spectabilis var. riversii

Malus spectabilis var. riversii, commonly known as the River's Beauty apple tree, is a variant of the ornamental crabapple.

It is native to China and is characterized by its showy, double white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The tree is relatively hardy and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.
