Mariscus kraussii var. capitatus

Mariscus kraussii var. capitatus, commonly known as the Cape mountain heath, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its compact growth habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

It is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
