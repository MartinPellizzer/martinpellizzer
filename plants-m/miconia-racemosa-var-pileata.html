Miconia racemosa var. pileata

Miconia racemosa var. pileata, commonly known as the clustered miconia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its dense, clustered inflorescences. The leaves are usually elliptical or ovate, with a glossy green surface and a slightly hairy underside. The flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are arranged in compact clusters, giving the plant its common name.

This species is often found in moist, shaded areas of lowland forests and is known for its rapid growth and potential invasiveness in certain environments.
