Mutisia parviflora

Mutisia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered mutisia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in shrubland and open areas, often in regions with a temperate climate. Mutisia parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
