Mucuna hainanensis

Mucuna hainanensis, commonly known as Hainan velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to southern China.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, hairy pods that contain several seeds. The plant produces yellow flowers and is often found in moist, tropical environments. It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and research settings.
