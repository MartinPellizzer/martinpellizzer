Medicago × komarovii var. incana

Medicago × komarovii var. incana, commonly known as the hairy sainfoin, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. It is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine environments.

This species is valued for its forage quality and has been introduced to various parts of the world for agricultural purposes.
