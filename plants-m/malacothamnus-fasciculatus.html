Malacothamnus fasciculatus

Malacothamnus fasciculatus, commonly known as the California popcornflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its fluffy, seed-producing heads that resemble popcorn. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. Its flowers are yellow to orange in color and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has small, narrow leaves and is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
