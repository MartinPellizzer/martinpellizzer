Melampyrum sylvaticum var. laricetorum

Melampyrum sylvaticum var. laricetorum, commonly known as the white betony, is a herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland habitats, often in association with coniferous trees like fir and larch. This variety is distinguished by its slightly reduced corolla and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
