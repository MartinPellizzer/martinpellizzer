Mimosa brevipetiolata var. pilosula

Mimosa brevipetiolata var. pilosula, commonly known as the hairy mimosa, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and leaves that are bipinnately compound, with small, oval leaflets. The plant produces yellow, spherical flower heads that are surrounded by bracts, which are often mistaken for flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

The plant is also notable for its rapid growth and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
