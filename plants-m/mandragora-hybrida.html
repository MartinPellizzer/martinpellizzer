Mandragora hybrida

Mandragora hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid mandrake, is a perennial plant belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that contain the toxic alkaloids responsible for its historical use in folklore and medicine. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its potent chemical composition, it has been both revered and feared throughout history.
