Magnolia acuminata var. ludoviciana

Magnolia acuminata var. ludoviciana, commonly known as the Southern Magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and its showy, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 50 feet, with a broad, rounded canopy. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The tree is well-suited for USDA hardiness zones 6 through 9 and is often used in ornamental landscaping.
