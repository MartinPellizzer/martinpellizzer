Myriocarpa cordifolia var. colipensis

Myriocarpa cordifolia var. colipensis, commonly known as the heartleaf myriocarpa, is a shrub native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils. Its botanical features include woody stems and small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

It is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its potential medicinal uses.
