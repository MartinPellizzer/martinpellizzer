Malva mauritiana

Malva mauritiana, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and showy, pale pink to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
