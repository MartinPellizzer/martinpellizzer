Musa campestris

Musa campestris, commonly known as the banana plant, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and tall, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces edible berries called bananas, which are rich in potassium and other nutrients. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Musa campestris is widely cultivated for both food and ornamental purposes.
