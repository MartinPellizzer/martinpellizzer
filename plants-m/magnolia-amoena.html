Magnolia amoena

Magnolia amoena, commonly known as the sweetbay magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has large, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding to its seasonal appeal. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The tree is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens and parks.
