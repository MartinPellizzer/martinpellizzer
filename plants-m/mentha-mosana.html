Mentha mosana

Mentha mosana, commonly known as wild mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and has a creeping growth habit with rhizomatous stems. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their cooling and digestive properties.

Mentha mosana is closely related to other mints like peppermint and spearmint.
