Malva unguiculata

Malva unguiculata, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like cotton and hibiscus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its fibrous roots and hairy stems contribute to its distinctive appearance and ecological role.
