Masakia japonica

Masakia japonica, commonly known as the Japanese orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of white or pale pink flowers with a distinctive, orchid-like appearance. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental value.

Masakia japonica thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
