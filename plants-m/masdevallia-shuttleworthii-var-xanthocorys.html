Masdevallia shuttleworthii var. xanthocorys

Masdevallia shuttleworthii var. xanthocorys, commonly known as the golden-veined masdevallia, is a striking orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces intricate, pendulous flowers in shades of yellow and green, with intricate patterns resembling veins. It thrives in cool, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements in the right conditions.
