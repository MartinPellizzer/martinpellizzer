Morus microphylla

Morus microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are typically less than 5 cm in length. The tree produces small, aggregate fruits known as mulberries, which are edible and often consumed by both humans and animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
