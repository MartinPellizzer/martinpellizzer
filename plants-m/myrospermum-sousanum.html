Myrospermum sousanum

Myrospermum sousanum, commonly known as the yellow dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its yellow flowers are followed by a distinctive seed head that produces lightweight seeds with pappus for wind dispersal.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique botanical appearance.
