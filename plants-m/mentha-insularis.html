Mentha insularis

Mentha insularis, commonly known as the island mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Mentha insularis is known for its strong, minty fragrance and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for gardens and natural habitats.
