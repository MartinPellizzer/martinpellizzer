Mitracarpus rigidifolius

Mitracarpus rigidifolius, commonly known as the rigid-leaved mitracarpus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive, rigid, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, often appearing in late spring or early summer. Its seeds are enclosed in a pod-like structure, typical of the Fabaceae family, and the plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and hardy nature.
