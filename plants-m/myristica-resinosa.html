Myristica resinosa

Myristica resinosa, commonly known as the nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is closely related to the nutmeg (Myristica fragrans). The plant produces a unique resinous substance known as ambergris, which has been historically used in perfumery and traditional medicine. Its bark, leaves, and seeds are also utilized in various medicinal applications.

The tree is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and can grow up to 20 meters in height.
