Malaxis dolpensis

Malaxis dolpensis, commonly known as the Dolpa orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and India.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has a rhizomatous root system. Its leaves are linear and often clustered at the base, and it is known for its delicate, fragrant blossoms.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
