Microlonchus albispinus

Microlonchus albispinus, commonly known as the white-spined burr grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
