Mitella nuda f. intermedia

Mitella nuda f. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate naked mitella, is a botanical variant of the naked mitella, a member of the Saxifragaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It exhibits intermediate traits between the typical form of Mitella nuda and other related species.

Its foliage and floral structure make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in its native habitat.
