Mimosa monadelpha var. hirsutopuberula

Mimosa monadelpha var. hirsutopuberula, commonly known as the hairy pubescent mimosa, is a variant of the mimosa plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and delicate, fern-like leaves that fold shut when touched, a response known as thigmonasty. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and adaptive responses make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
