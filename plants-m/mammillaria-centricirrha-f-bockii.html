Mammillaria centricirrha f. bockii

Mammillaria centricirrha f. bockii, commonly known as the Bock's mammillaria, is a compact cactus species native to Mexico.

It features a globular stem covered in white to pale yellow tubercles, which are adorned with short, white radial spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well-suited for cultivation in arid environments due to its drought tolerance.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and is often grown as a decorative succulent.
