Masdevallia amsleri

Masdevallia amsleri, commonly known as the Amsler's masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually strap-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

The orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its beauty and unique appearance.
