Mentha × verticillata var. frequentidens

Mentha × verticillata var. frequentidens, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. Its flowers are small, tubular, and borne in whorled clusters, usually in shades of blue or purple.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
