Maba micrantha

Maba micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered maba, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.
