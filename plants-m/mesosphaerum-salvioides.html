Mesosphaerum salvioides

Mesosphaerum salvioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironwood, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, opposite leaves and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Mesosphaerum salvioides is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
