Myroxylon obovatum

Myroxylon obovatum, commonly known as the Copaiba tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, obovate leaves and produces a valuable resin used in perfumery and traditional medicine. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall and has a dense, rounded crown. Its bark is thick and fibrous, and it thrives in humid, well-drained soils.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
