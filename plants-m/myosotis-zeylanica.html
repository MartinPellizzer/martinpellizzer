Myosotis zeylanica

Myosotis zeylanica, commonly known as the Indian forget-me-not, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including India and Sri Lanka.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 cm, featuring slender stems and basal leaves. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
