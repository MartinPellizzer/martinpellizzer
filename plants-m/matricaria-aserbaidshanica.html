Matricaria aserbaidshanica

Matricaria aserbaidshanica, commonly known as the Azeri chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with white centers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are aromatic and have been used in traditional medicine for their calming properties.

The essential oil extracted from its flowers is valued for its potential therapeutic benefits.
