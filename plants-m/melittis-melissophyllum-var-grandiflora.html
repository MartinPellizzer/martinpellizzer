Melittis melissophyllum var. grandiflora

Melittis melissophyllum var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered honey herb, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its nectar-rich flowers attract bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological value.

The species is noted for its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal in horticultural settings.
