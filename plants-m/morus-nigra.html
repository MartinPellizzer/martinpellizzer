Morus nigra

Morus nigra, commonly known as the black mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to western Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its dark purple to black fruits, which are edible when fully ripe. The leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a serrated edge, while the bark is smooth and grayish in color. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.

It also plays a role in traditional medicine and as a source of silk production through its leaves.
