Metrosideros tetragyna

Metrosideros tetragyna, commonly known as the Four-angled Pohutukawa, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its distinctive four-angled stems and small, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are lance-shaped and have a glossy surface, while the bark is smooth and often reddish in color. It thrives in coastal and forested areas, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
