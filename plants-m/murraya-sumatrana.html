Murraya sumatrana

Murraya sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran murraya, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, orange-yellow fruits that are edible but not widely consumed by humans. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Murraya sumatrana is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some cultures.
