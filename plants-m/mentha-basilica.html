Mentha basilica

Mentha basilica, commonly known as holy basil, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves that range from light green to purple in color. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Mentha basilica is widely used in traditional medicine and culinary applications for its distinctive scent and flavor.
