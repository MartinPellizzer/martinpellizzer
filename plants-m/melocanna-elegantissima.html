Melocanna elegantissima

Melocanna elegantissima, commonly known as the elegant bamboo, is a striking member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its aesthetic value and unique growth habit, it is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
