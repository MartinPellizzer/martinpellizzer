Mentha × gonzalii

Mentha × gonzalii, commonly known as the Mexican mint or Mexican hybrid mint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Mentha arvensis and Mentha piperita.

It is a perennial herb native to Mexico and has a strong, minty fragrance. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with square stems and opposite, serrated leaves. Its flowers are small, tubular, and borne in whorls along the stems, often in shades of purple or pink.

Mentha × gonzalii is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
