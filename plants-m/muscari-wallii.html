Muscari wallii

Muscari wallii, commonly known as the Persian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It produces dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge from a basal rosette, and its bulbs are adapted for storing nutrients during dormancy. Muscari wallii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early blooming habit and low maintenance requirements.
