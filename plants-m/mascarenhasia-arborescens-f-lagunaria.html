Mascarenhasia arborescens f. lagunaria

Mascarenhasia arborescens f. lagunaria, commonly known as the Mascarene tree fern, is a unique variant of the tree fern native to the Mascarene Islands.

It features a tall, slender trunk covered in a dense, fibrous sheath that resembles a tree trunk. The plant produces large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length, contributing to its dramatic appearance. Its growth is slow, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments with rich, well-draining soil.

This fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
