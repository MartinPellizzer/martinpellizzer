Muhlenbergia brachyelytrum

Muhlenbergia brachyelytrum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf muhlenbergia, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Muhlenbergia brachyelytrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators and providing habitat for small wildlife.
