Mallotus hookerianus

Mallotus hookerianus, commonly known as the Hooker's mallot, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by spiny, woody fruits. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential anti-inflammatory properties.

Mallotus hookerianus thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.
