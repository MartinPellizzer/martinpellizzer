Machaeranthera riparia

Machaeranthera riparia, commonly known as the river ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows along stream banks and moist woodlands. The plant features tall, sturdy stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its showy, pink to red flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant has a fibrous root system and is adapted to wet or moist soils.
