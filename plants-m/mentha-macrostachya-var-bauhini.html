Mentha macrostachya var. bauhini

Mentha macrostachya var. bauhini, commonly known as the large-stalked mint, is a perennial herb native to regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant has a strong, aromatic scent due to its high concentration of essential oils. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This variety is valued both for its ornamental appeal and for its use in traditional herbal medicine.
