Malveopsis heterophylla

Malveopsis heterophylla, commonly known as the variable mallow, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that vary in shape and size along the stem. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming from autumn to spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Malveopsis heterophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
