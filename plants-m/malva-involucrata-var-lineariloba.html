Malva involucrata var. lineariloba

Malva involucrata var. lineariloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Malvaceae family.
