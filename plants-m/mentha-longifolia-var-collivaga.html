Mentha longifolia var. collivaga

Mentha longifolia var. collivaga, commonly known as wild mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, serrated leaves that are often grayish-green in color, with a strong menthol scent. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other mint species.
