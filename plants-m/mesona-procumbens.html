Mesona procumbens

Mesona procumbens, commonly known as the creeping mesona, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features prostrate growth habit with slender, hairy stems that often root at the nodes. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated margins and a hairy surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by bead-like fruits.

This plant is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
