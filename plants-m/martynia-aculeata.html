Martynia aculeata

Martynia aculeata, commonly known as the red spider flower, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, spider-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has hairy, branched stems and serrated, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Martynia aculeata is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
