Micromyrtus elobata

Micromyrtus elobata, commonly known as the slender myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slight sheen, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Micromyrtus elobata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
