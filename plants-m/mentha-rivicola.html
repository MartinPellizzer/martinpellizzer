Mentha rivicola

Mentha rivicola, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows near streams, wet meadows, and other water-associated environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, menthol-like aroma.

Mentha rivicola is often used in traditional medicine for its purported cooling and digestive properties.
