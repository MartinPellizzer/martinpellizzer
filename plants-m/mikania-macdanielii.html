Mikania macdanielii

Mikania macdanielii, commonly known as the MacDaniel's mile-a-minute weed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and climbing habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early fall. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and twining.

Mikania macdanielii is an invasive species that can spread quickly, often outcompeting native vegetation in disturbed areas.
