Myrtus oerstedeana

Myrtus oerstedeana, commonly known as the Oersted's myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, dark green leaves that are glossy and aromatic, with a distinctive citrus-like scent. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Myrtus oerstedeana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
