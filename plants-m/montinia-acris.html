Montinia acris

Montinia acris, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features upright stems covered in fine, hairy foliage and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Montinia acris is often found in open habitats like meadows and rocky areas.
