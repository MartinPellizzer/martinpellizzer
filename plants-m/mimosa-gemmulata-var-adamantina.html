Mimosa gemmulata var. adamantina

Mimosa gemmulata var. adamantina, commonly known as the diamond mimosa, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces distinctive seed pods that are covered in small, bead-like structures, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its unique appearance and seasonal blooms.
