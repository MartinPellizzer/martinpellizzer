Mentha sylvestris subsp. brevifrons

Mentha sylvestris subsp.

brevifrons, commonly known as wood mint, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are usually lanceolate in shape.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of white or pale purple, typically arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.
