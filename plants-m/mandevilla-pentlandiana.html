Mandevilla pentlandiana

Mandevilla pentlandiana, commonly known as the scarlet mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a vigorous climber with thick, fleshy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming from spring to fall. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil and full sun exposure.

Mandevilla pentlandiana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant floral display.
