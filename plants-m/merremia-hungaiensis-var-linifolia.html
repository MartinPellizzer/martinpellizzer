Merremia hungaiensis var. linifolia

Merremia hungaiensis var. linifolia, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a climbing vine native to parts of Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and gardens.

Its fibrous roots and sturdy stems make it a hardy and adaptable species.
