Myristica dactyloides

Myristica dactyloides, commonly known as the "breadnut" or "sacha inchi," is a tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a small tree or shrub that produces edible seeds rich in omega-3 fatty acids, protein, and other essential nutrients. The plant's seeds are encased in a hard shell and are typically processed to extract the nutrient-dense oil. Myristica dactyloides thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its health benefits and culinary uses.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique nutritional profile and potential for sustainable agriculture.
