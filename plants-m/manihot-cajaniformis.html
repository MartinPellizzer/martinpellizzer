Manihot cajaniformis

Manihot cajaniformis, commonly known as the creeping arrowleaf manioc, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
