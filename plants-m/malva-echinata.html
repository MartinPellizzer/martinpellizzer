Malva echinata

Malva echinata, commonly known as the spiked mallows, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features rough, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy pink or purple flowers with five petals and a distinctive central tuft of stamens. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

Malva echinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
