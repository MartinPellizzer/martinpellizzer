Medinilla mirabile

Medinilla mirabile, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or velvet plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that resemble small tangerines. The plant produces small, round, yellow-orange fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed. Medinilla mirabile thrives in warm, humid conditions and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
