Malva concinna

Malva concinna, commonly known as the narrowleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces showy pink or white flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Malva concinna is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
