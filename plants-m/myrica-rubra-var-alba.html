Myrica rubra var. alba

Myrica rubra var. alba, commonly known as the white wax myrtle, is a variant of the wax myrtle species native to eastern Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, acidic soils. The plant is characterized by its white to pale yellow berries, which contrast with its dark green, glossy leaves. It produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, and its aromatic foliage is often used in traditional herbal remedies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
