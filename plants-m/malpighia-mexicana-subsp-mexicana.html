Malpighia mexicana subsp. mexicana

Malpighia mexicana subsp.

mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican granadilla, is a tropical shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America. It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, red or orange flowers.

The plant produces small, round fruits that are rich in vitamin C and have a tangy, acidic flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
