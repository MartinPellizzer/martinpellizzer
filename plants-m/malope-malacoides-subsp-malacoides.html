Malope malacoides subsp. malacoides

Malope malacoides subsp.

malacoides, commonly known as the yellow sandmat, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments.

The plant features branching stems covered in dense, woolly hairs, and its flowers are yellow with five petals. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
