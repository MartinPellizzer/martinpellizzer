Merendera filifolia var. atlantica

Merendera filifolia var. atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic filifolia, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific coastal regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves that are adapted to withstand saline and windy conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale green, which bloom in late spring. Its root system is fibrous and helps it anchor in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline.

Due to its limited habitat, it is considered an endangered species and is of particular interest to botanists studying coastal flora.
