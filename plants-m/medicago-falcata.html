Medicago falcata

Medicago falcata, commonly known as goat's rue, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, spurred flowers that range from violet to yellow. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Medicago falcata is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
