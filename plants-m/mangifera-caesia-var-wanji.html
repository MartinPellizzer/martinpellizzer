Mangifera caesia var. wanji

Mangifera caesia var. wanji, commonly known as the Wanji mango, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elongated fruit with a thick, fibrous skin that varies in color from green to yellow when ripe. The fruit contains a sweet, juicy pulp that is rich in vitamins and minerals, making it a popular fruit in local markets. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil and requires regular watering for optimal growth.

This variety is valued not only for its flavor but also for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage.
