Molinia caerulea var. albiflora

Molinia caerulea var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered moorgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and produces panicle-like inflorescences that bear white to pale blue flowers. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils, often found in bogs, marshes, and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland habitats.
