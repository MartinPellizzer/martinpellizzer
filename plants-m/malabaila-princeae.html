Malabaila princeae

Malabaila princeae, commonly known as the Malabar whiteflower, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, woody fruits.

Malabaila princeae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
