Medicago rigidula f. viscosa

Medicago rigidula f. viscosa, commonly known as sticky medic, is a variant of the medick plant characterized by its glandular, sticky hairs on its stems and leaves.

It is a low-growing annual herb that thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet having a hairy surface.

The species is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
