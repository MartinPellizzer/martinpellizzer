Mascagnia doniana

Mascagnia doniana, commonly known as the African moonseed, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a follicle that splits open to release seeds enclosed in a fleshy, red aril.

Mascagnia doniana is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
