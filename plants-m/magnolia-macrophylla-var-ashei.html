Magnolia macrophylla var. ashei

Magnolia macrophylla var. ashei, commonly known as the Sweetbay magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces fragrant, white flowers in the spring, which are borne on long, pendulous stems. Its bark is smooth and gray, with a subtle flaking texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
