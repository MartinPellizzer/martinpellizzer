Myrtoleucodendron symphyocarpum

Myrtoleucodendron symphyocarpum, commonly known as the rock fig, is a rare and unique species native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its distinctive, clustered inflorescences and fleshy, edible fruits that resemble small figs. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves with a waxy coating, which helps it conserve water in its arid environment. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the extreme conditions of Socotra's climate.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered an important species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
