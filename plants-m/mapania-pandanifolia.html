Mapania pandanifolia

Mapania pandanifolia, commonly known as the pandan-leaf maple, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that resemble the leaves of the pandan plant, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a decorative element in landscaping.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
