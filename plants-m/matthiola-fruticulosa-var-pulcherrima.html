Matthiola fruticulosa var. pulcherrima

Matthiola fruticulosa var. pulcherrima, commonly known as the sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial that produces showy clusters of pink or purple flowers. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and a compact, bushy growth habit. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a beneficial addition to ecological landscapes.
