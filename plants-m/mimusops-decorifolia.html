Mimusops decorifolia

Mimusops decorifolia, commonly known as the ornamental tamarind tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy green foliage and showy, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine.

The leaves are also used as a natural dye and in the production of tamarind-based products.
