Muscarella aristata

Muscarella aristata, commonly known as the velvet tassel plant, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety, purple-tinted bracts that resemble tassels, which are often the most noticeable feature of the plant. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture. It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid environments.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
