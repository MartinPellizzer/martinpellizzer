Myrcia rufipila

Myrcia rufipila, commonly known as the red-fruited myrtle, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of red, fleshy berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal areas and humid forests. Its flowers are usually white or pink and are followed by the distinctive red, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
