Maxillaria johniana

Maxillaria johniana, commonly known as the John's orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb structure that supports its growth during dry periods. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate patterns. Its leaves are typically elliptical and arranged in a rosette formation around the pseudobulb.

Maxillaria johniana thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
