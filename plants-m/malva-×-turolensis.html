Malva × turolensis

Malva × turolensis, commonly known as the Turolensis mallows, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton, hibiscus, and okra. The plant is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Malva × turolensis is valued for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various climates.
