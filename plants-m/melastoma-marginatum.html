Melastoma marginatum

Melastoma marginatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, clustered pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. It has small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Melastoma marginatum is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
