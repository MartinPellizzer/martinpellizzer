Myriogomphus fuscus

Myriogomphus fuscus, commonly known as the dusky clubtail dragonfly, is a species of aquatic insect found in freshwater habitats across North America.

It belongs to the family Gomphidae and is characterized by its slender body and elongated abdomen. The adult dragonfly typically has a dark brown or black body with distinctive wing patterns that aid in camouflage. It is often found near streams and ponds, where it lays its eggs in aquatic vegetation.

The larvae, known as naiads, spend several years in the water before emerging as adults.
