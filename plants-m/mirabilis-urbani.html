Mirabilis urbani

Mirabilis urbani, commonly known as the urban marvel four-o'clock plant, is a striking flowering species native to South America.

It belongs to the Nyctaginaceae family and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the late afternoon. The plant produces vibrant blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which remain open through the night. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Mirabilis urbani is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden environments.
