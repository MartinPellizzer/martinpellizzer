Myrtus communis var. camboanus

Myrtus communis var. camboanus, commonly known as the Camboan rose myrtle, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, dark green leaves that are aromatic and often covered in a fine white bloom. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers in spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a hedge.

Its ornamental value and fragrant foliage make it a popular choice in horticulture.
