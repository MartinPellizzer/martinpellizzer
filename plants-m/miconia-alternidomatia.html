Miconia alternidomatia

Miconia alternidomatia, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers, followed by bright red, fleshy fruits that are often eaten by birds. Miconia alternidomatia is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable plant in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
