Monvillea saddiana

Monvillea saddiana, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly found in the Andean regions of Ecuador and Peru.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a compact growth habit, featuring a short rhizome and a single, fleshy, greenish-brown pseudobulb. The plant produces a single or few elongated leaves that are often covered in a dense, woolly coating. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and typically pink or purple, arranged in a spike that blooms in the spring.

Monvillea saddiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in cool, humid environments.
