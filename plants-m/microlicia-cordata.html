Microlicia cordata

Microlicia cordata, commonly known as the heart-shaped fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically heart-shaped, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture to flourish. Its leaves are typically dark green and glossy, with a finely divided structure that enhances its ornamental appeal.

Microlicia cordata is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its aesthetic value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
