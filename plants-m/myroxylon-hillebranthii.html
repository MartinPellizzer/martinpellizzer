Myroxylon hillebranthii

Myroxylon hillebranthii, commonly known as the Brazilwood tree, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its dark red heartwood, which has been historically valued for its use in dyes, furniture, and musical instruments. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall and has a dense canopy that provides habitat for various wildlife species. Its leaves are alternate and oval-shaped, while its flowers are small and yellowish-green.

The plant plays an important ecological role in maintaining the biodiversity of its native ecosystem.
