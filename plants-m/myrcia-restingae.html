Myrcia restingae

Myrcia restingae, commonly known as the Restinga myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It typically grows in coastal areas, particularly in sandy soils of the restinga ecosystems. The plant features aromatic leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a capsule containing several winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Myrcia restingae plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
