Mariscus pluricephalus

Mariscus pluricephalus, commonly known as the many-headed mariscus, is a rare and unique plant species characterized by its unusual growth pattern.

It produces multiple heads or shoots from a single root system, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is typically found in specific microhabitats, such as shaded, moist environments in temperate regions. Its leaves are usually small, lance-shaped, and arranged in a rosette formation.

The flowers, when present, are small and clustered, often in shades of green or white.
