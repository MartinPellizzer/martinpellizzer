Melastoma inundatum

Melastoma inundatum, commonly known as the water melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and often features a reddish stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Melastoma inundatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
