Mentha × officinalis

Mentha × officinalis, commonly known as peppermint, is a hybrid perennial herb in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its dark green, slightly fuzzy leaves and its strong, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stem. Peppermint is native to Europe and has been widely cultivated for its medicinal and culinary uses.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
