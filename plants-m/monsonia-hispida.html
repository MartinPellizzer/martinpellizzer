Monsonia hispida

Monsonia hispida, commonly known as the hairy monsonia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. Its flowers are small, clustered, and usually pink or red, blooming in spring.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
