Medicago truncatula var. tricycla

Medicago truncatula var. tricycla, commonly known as the three-lobed medick, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with three distinct lobes and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration and agricultural settings.

Its seeds are small and typically dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
