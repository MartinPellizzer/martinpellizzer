Mycetia faberi

Mycetia faberi, commonly known as the Indian velvet tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its fragrant, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. Its botanical features include pinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and a taproot system that helps it thrive in poor soils.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
