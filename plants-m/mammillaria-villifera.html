Mammillaria villifera

Mammillaria villifera, commonly known as the feathered hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered with white, woolly spines that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in succulent gardens.
