Masdevallia concinna

Masdevallia concinna, commonly known as the pink masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendant-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as a miniature epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Masdevallia concinna is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique floral structure.
