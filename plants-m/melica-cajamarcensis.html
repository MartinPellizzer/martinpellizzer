Melica cajamarcensis

Melica cajamarcensis, commonly known as the Cajamarca grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments, often found in mountainous regions with cool climates. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, two-lipped flowers, which are usually greenish or purplish in color.

Melica cajamarcensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
