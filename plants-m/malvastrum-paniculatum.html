Malvastrum paniculatum

Malvastrum paniculatum, commonly known as the Mexican mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a fuzzy texture and grows as an annual or perennial herb. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in disturbed areas and along roadsides.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable plant for ecological gardens.
