Marsdenia weberbaueri

Marsdenia weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's Marsdenia, is a climbing plant native to the highlands of South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The flowers are typically small and clustered in inflorescences, featuring five white or pink petals and a prominent corona. Its fruits are follicles that release seeds with tufts of silky hair, aiding in wind dispersal.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
