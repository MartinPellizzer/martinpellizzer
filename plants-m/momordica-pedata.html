Momordica pedata

Momordica pedata, commonly known as the creeping cucumber, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and melons. The plant produces small, green, edible fruits that resemble cucumbers but are typically smaller and have a more bitter taste. Its leaves are heart-shaped and its flowers are yellow with five petals.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
