Machilus hedgei

Machilus hedgei, commonly known as the Chinese boxwood, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by orange-red berries that attract birds. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping.

Machilus hedgei prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a versatile choice for garden use.
