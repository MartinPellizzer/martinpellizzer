Matthiola fruticulosa var. purpurea

Matthiola fruticulosa var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple-flowered wallflower, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, brightly colored flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can be found in rockeries or as a border plant.

The flowers are typically borne on elongated stems and bloom in late spring to early summer.
