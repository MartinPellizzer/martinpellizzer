Morinda citrifolia f. potteri

Morinda citrifolia f. potteri, commonly known as the Hawaiian morinda, is a variant of the noni plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and dry forests. The plant features simple, elliptic leaves with a smooth texture and a distinctive reddish or purple coloration on the underside. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in spikes, followed by yellow, fleshy berries that contain numerous seeds.

This form is adapted to the unique environmental conditions of Hawaii, showcasing its ecological significance in the region.
