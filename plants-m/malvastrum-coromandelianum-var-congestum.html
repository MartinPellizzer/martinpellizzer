Malvastrum coromandelianum var. congestum

Malvastrum coromandelianum var. congestum, commonly known as the Chinese malva, is a flowering plant native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, pink to red flowers with five petals. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

The flowers bloom in summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
