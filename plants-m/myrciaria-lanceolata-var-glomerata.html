Myrciaria lanceolata var. glomerata

Myrciaria lanceolata var. glomerata, commonly known as the Brazilian guava or *pitanga*, is a small evergreen shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically orange to red in color, with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

The species is also valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine.
