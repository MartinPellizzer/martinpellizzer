Miconia allenii

Miconia allenii, commonly known as the Allen's miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that are typically followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Miconia allenii is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

It is also notable for its potential ecological impact due to its invasive tendencies in certain regions.
