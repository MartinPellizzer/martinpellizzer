Melastoma tinctorium

Melastoma tinctorium, commonly known as the red-rooted melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, usually in shades of pink or red. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The roots are rich in tannins and have been used in traditional medicine for their astringent properties.
