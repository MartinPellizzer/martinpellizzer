Myrcia tetramera

Myrcia tetramera, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and glossy, dark green leaves. It produces small, dark purple fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine.

Myrcia tetramera is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic properties.
