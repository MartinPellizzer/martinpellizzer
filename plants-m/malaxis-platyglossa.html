Malaxis platyglossa

Malaxis platyglossa, commonly known as the broad-tongued orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant has a slender rhizome and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a raceme. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating.

It is valued for its unique floral structure and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
