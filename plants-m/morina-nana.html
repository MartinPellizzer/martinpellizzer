Morina nana

Morina nana, commonly known as the dwarf moringa, is a small shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by elliptical, leathery fruits. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture and are often used in traditional medicine.

Morina nana thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright light for optimal growth.
