Martiodendron fluminense

Martiodendron fluminense, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking red-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release its seeds, contributing to its propagation in the wild.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
