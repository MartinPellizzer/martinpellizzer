Macrotyloma decipiens

Macrotyloma decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, slender pods that contain small, edible seeds. It is often grown as a forage crop due to its high nutritional value for livestock.

Botanically, it is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
