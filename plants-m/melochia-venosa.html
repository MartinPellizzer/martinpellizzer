Melochia venosa

Melochia venosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvet tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into round, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Melochia venosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
