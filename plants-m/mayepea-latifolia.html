Mayepea latifolia

Mayepea latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf mayepea, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its wide, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are usually white or pale yellow in color. Mayepea latifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
