Myrcianthes hallii

Myrcianthes hallii, commonly known as the West Indian cherry or acerola, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are rich in vitamin C and other antioxidants. It thrives in tropical climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
