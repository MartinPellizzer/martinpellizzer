Mimulus guttatus subsp. scouleri

Mimulus guttatus subsp.

scouleri, commonly known as the Scouler's monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to western North America. It is characterized by its bright orange or yellow flowers, which are typically spurred at the back, a feature common in the genus Mimulus.

The plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in streambanks and wet meadows. It is a perennial herb that produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its foliage is usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
