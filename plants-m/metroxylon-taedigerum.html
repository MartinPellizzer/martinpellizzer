Metroxylon taedigerum

Metroxylon taedigerum, commonly known as the oil palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fruit that contain oil-rich seeds, making it economically significant for palm oil production. Its growth is adapted to warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

The botanical features of Metroxylon taedigerum make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
