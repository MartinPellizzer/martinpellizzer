Monnina cuspidata

Monnina cuspidata, commonly known as prairie mallow, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces showy, pink to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairie and open woodland habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Onagraceae, which includes other genera such as *Evening Primrose* and *Willowherb*.
