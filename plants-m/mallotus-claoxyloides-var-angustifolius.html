Mallotus claoxyloides var. angustifolius

Mallotus claoxyloides var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallot, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are typically narrow and slightly serrated. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
