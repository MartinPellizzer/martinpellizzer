Metabolos latifolius

Metabolos latifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaf metabolos, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a common sight in gardens and natural habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and modest blooms.
