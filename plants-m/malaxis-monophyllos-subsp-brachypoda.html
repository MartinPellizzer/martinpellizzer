Malaxis monophyllos subsp. brachypoda

Malaxis monophyllos subsp.

brachypoda, commonly known as the short-stemmed marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, preferring shaded environments with moist, acidic soils.

The plant produces a single, erect stem that bears several narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, often with a subtle fragrance, and are clustered in a loose inflorescence.

This subspecies is notable for its specialized adaptations to its wetland environment and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
