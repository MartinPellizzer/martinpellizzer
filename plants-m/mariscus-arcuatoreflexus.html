Mariscus arcuatoreflexus

Mariscus arcuatoreflexus, commonly known as the curved-stemmed marsh grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features slender, arching stems that often curl at the tips, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to thrive in moist, often nutrient-poor soils.

Mariscus arcuatoreflexus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems by preventing soil erosion.
