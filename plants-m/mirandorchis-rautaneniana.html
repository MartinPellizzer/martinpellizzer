Mirandorchis rautaneniana

Mirandorchis rautaneniana, commonly known as the Rautanen's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often pale green or brownish stems and its delicate, translucent or semi-translucent leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, which are adapted for specific pollination mechanisms. Due to its elusive nature and limited habitat, it is considered a critically endangered species.

Its botanical study is significant for understanding orchid diversity and conservation in tropical ecosystems.
