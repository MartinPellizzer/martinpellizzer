Mussaenda frondosa var. glabrata

Mussaenda frondosa var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth-leaved Mussaenda, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its glossy, smooth, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces clusters of showy, yellow-orange bracts that resemble flowers, though they are actually modified leaves. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in indoor settings.
