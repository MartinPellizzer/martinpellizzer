Machaerium splendens

Machaerium splendens, commonly known as the golden crown beard, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in dense, feathery clusters. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savannah habitats.

Machaerium splendens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
