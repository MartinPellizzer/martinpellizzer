Mentha × similata

Mentha × similata, commonly known as catnip, is a hybrid perennial herb in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. Mentha × similata is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is widely cultivated for its aromatic properties and has a long history of use in traditional medicine and as a natural insect repellent.
