Myrcia otocalyx

Myrcia otocalyx, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, dark berries that contain seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

Myrcia otocalyx is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic uses.
