Monarda stanfieldii

Monarda stanfieldii, commonly known as Stanfield's bergamot, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Monarda stanfieldii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
