Myriactis glutinosa

Myriactis glutinosa, commonly known as the sticky myriactis, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive botanical features.

It belongs to the family Myriactidaceae and is native to specific regions in South America. The plant is notable for its sticky secretions, which cover its leaves and stems, providing a protective mechanism against herbivores. Its leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy texture that enhances its survival in arid environments.

The flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
