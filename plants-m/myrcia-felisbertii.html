Myrcia felisbertii

Myrcia felisbertii, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine.

Myrcia felisbertii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
