Mentha nemorum

Mentha nemorum, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wet meadows, stream banks, and other waterlogged environments. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are often aromatic. Its small, pale blue or purple flowers appear in whorled clusters and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Mentha nemorum is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
