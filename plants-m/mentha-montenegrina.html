Mentha montenegrina

Mentha montenegrina, commonly known as the Montenegro mint, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and spikes of small, pale purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, sharing traits with other mints such as its square stems and fragrant oils.

Mentha montenegrina is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies.
