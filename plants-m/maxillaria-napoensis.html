Maxillaria napoensis

Maxillaria napoensis, commonly known as the Napo orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by its rhizomatous structure. The plant produces intricate, showy flowers with a distinctive coloration, often featuring a blend of yellow and green hues. Its leaves are typically elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in moisture retention.

Maxillaria napoensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to the rich, organic soils of the rainforest floor.
