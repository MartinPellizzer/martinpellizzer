Madia sativa subsp. dissitiflora

Madia sativa subsp.

dissitiflora, commonly known as the slender madia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.

Due to its ecological role and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in arid landscapes.
