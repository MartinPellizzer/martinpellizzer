Mentha rotundifolia

Mentha rotundifolia, commonly known as round-leaf mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its rounded, slightly hairy leaves and strong, aromatic mint scent. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas or stream sides. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically violet or blue in color.

Mentha rotundifolia is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to its refreshing flavor and medicinal properties.
