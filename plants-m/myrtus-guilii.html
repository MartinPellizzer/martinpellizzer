Myrtus guilii

Myrtus guilii, commonly known as the Corsican myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant features narrow, dark green leaves with a slight waxy coating and produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring. Its berries are typically red or black when mature and contain small, hard seeds.

Myrtus guilii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
