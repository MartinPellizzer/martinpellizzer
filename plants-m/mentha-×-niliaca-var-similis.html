Mentha × niliaca var. similis

Mentha × niliaca var. similis, commonly known as the African mint, is a hybrid plant within the Mentha genus.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its aromatic, square stems and opposite, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Mentha × niliaca var. similis is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
