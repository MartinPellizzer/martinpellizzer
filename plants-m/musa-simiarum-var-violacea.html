Musa simiarum var. violacea

Musa simiarum var. violacea, commonly known as the purple banana, is a striking variant of the banana plant characterized by its vibrant purple foliage.

This variety is a cultivar of the Musa genus, which includes numerous banana species native to Southeast Asia. The leaves are broad, waxy, and exhibit a deep violet hue, making it a popular ornamental plant. While it produces small, inedible fruit, its aesthetic appeal lies primarily in its foliage.

It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-draining soil and ample sunlight for optimal growth.
