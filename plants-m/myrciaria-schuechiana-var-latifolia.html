Myrciaria schuechiana var. latifolia

Myrciaria schuechiana var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved myrcia, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.

Myrciaria schuechiana var. latifolia plays a significant role in supporting local biodiversity and is a key component of the understory vegetation in its native habitat.
