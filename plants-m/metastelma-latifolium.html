Metastelma latifolium

Metastelma latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf metastelma, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its lanceolate, often hairy leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
