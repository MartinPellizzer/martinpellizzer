Monotes

Monotes, commonly known as the "devil's fig," is a unique plant species belonging to the Moraceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hollow, fig-like structures that serve as both fruits and homes for a specialized community of organisms. The plant's morphology includes a short, woody stem with a single, thick, fleshy peduncle that bears the unique fruit. Monotes thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in shaded, moist environments.

Its ecological significance lies in its role as a keystone species for certain insect and bird populations.
