Musa × paradisiaca var. violacea

Musa × paradisiaca var. violacea, commonly known as the purple banana, is a striking variant of the banana plant characterized by its deep violet to almost black foliage.

This variety is a hybrid of Musa acuminata and Musa balbisiana, resulting in its unique coloration. The leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a waxy texture and a prominent midrib. While it produces edible fruit, the flowers and foliage are the primary ornamental features.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
