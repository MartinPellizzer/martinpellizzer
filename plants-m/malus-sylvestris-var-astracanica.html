Malus sylvestris var. astracanica

Malus sylvestris var. astracanica, commonly known as the Astracanica apple, is a wild apple species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its small, tart fruits with a high acidity and a distinct, aromatic flavor. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a serrated margin and a dark green upper surface.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in traditional medicine and cider-making.
