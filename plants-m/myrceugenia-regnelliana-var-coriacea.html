Myrceugenia regnelliana var. coriacea

Myrceugenia regnelliana var. coriacea, commonly known as the rough myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to the temperate rainforests of southern Brazil.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are covered in a rough, textured surface, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are small, woody berries that contain a single seed.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and resilience in moist, shaded environments.
