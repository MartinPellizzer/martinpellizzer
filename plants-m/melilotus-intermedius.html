Melilotus intermedius

Melilotus intermedius, commonly known as sweet clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. Sweet clover is native to Europe and has been naturalized in parts of North America.

It is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a useful plant in agricultural settings.
