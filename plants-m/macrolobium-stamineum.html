Macrolobium stamineum

Macrolobium stamineum, commonly known as the stamen fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are divided into narrow, linear segments resembling stamens, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and along stream banks. Its reproductive structures are well-developed, with sori located on the undersides of the fronds.

Macrolobium stamineum plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
