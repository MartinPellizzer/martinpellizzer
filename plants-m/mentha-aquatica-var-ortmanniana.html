Mentha aquatica var. ortmanniana

Mentha aquatica var. ortmanniana, commonly known as the ortmann's water mint, is a variant of the water mint species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

This perennial herb typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. It features slender, aromatic leaves and small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is characterized by its vigorous growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a common sight in wetland habitats.

It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported digestive and calming properties.
