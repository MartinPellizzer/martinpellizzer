Malva sherardiana

Malva sherardiana, commonly known as the Persian mallow, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture, and its stems are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Malva sherardiana is also valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
