Melampodium tenellum

Melampodium tenellum, commonly known as the narrowleaf sunflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow flowers with a distinctive orange center. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, and thrives in moist, sunny conditions. Its flowers bloom from summer to fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide ecological benefits.
