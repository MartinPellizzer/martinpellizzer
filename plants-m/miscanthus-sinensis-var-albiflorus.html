Miscanthus sinensis var. albiflorus

Miscanthus sinensis var. albiflorus, commonly known as the whiteflower silver grass, is a variant of the silver grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, gracefully arching clumps of slender, silver-green foliage. The plant produces fluffy, white plumes in late summer, which give it a distinctive and ornamental appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic value and ability to attract pollinators.
