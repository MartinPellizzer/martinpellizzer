Mentha longifolia subsp. dumortieri

Mentha longifolia subsp.

dumortieri, commonly known as Brazilian mint, is a perennial herb native to South America. It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a strong menthol scent.

The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This subspecies is often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent due to its medicinal and aromatic properties.
