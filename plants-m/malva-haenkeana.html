Malva haenkeana

Malva haenkeana, commonly known as the Andean mallow, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly in the Andes region.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates. Its fibrous stems and hairy leaves are typical of the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton.

Malva haenkeana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
