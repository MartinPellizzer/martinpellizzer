Misanteca duartei

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while also producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that feed on its fleshy, red berries.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can weaken its host tree over time, making it an important ecological factor in tropical forests.
