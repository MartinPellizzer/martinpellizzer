Micromyrtus albicans

Micromyrtus albicans, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a small shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy soils and is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth, waxy surface, and it produces small, spherical fruits that contain several seeds.

Micromyrtus albicans is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in harsh environments.
