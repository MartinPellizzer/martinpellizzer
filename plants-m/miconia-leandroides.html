Miconia leandroides

Miconia leandroides, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit or false mallow, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a fleshy, star-shaped berry that turns red when mature.

Miconia leandroides is known for its rapid growth and is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage.
