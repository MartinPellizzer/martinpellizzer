Microcharis contorta

Microcharis contorta, commonly known as the curled leaf vine, is a climbing herbaceous plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often curled or twisted, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its stems are slender and twining, allowing it to climb and trail along supports.

Microcharis contorta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
