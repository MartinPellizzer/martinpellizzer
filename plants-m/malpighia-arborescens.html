Malpighia arborescens

Malpighia arborescens, commonly known as the beautyberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright purple berries that are edible for humans and birds. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape with a smooth margin. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of its distinctive berries.

The plant thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
