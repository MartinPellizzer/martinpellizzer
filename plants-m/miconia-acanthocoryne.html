Miconia acanthocoryne

Miconia acanthocoryne, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or red miconia, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a large shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its broad, palmate leaves. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by distinctive red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are typically opposite and have a waxy texture, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Miconia acanthocoryne is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
