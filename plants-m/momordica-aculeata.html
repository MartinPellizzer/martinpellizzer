Momordica aculeata

Momordica aculeata, commonly known as the horned melon or cucurbita, is a tropical vine native to Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiky, green fruit covered in warts, which resembles a horned melon. The plant produces yellow flowers and has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit.

The fruit contains a sweet, edible pulp surrounded by a fibrous, inedible layer.
