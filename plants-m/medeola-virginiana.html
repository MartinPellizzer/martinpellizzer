Medeola virginiana

Medeola virginiana, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Melanthiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in rich, loamy soils.

Medeola virginiana is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used by indigenous peoples for various therapeutic purposes.
