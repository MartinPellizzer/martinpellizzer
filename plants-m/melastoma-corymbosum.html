Melastoma corymbosum

Melastoma corymbosum, commonly known as the clustered melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its showy, often pink or red bracted flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while its fruits are capsules that split open to release seeds.

Melastoma corymbosum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
