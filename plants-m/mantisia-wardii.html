Mantisia wardii

Mantisia wardii, commonly known as the African orchid or the mantis orchid, is a species of orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble the head of a praying mantis, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers are usually pink or purple in color.

Mantisia wardii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
