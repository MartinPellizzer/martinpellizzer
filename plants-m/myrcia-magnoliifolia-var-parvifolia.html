Myrcia magnoliifolia var. parvifolia

Myrcia magnoliifolia var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved myrcia, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves that are smaller than those of the typical species. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
