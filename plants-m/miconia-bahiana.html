Miconia bahiana

Miconia bahiana, commonly known as the Brazilian glory flower, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a distinctive, three-lobed berry that turns red when mature.

Miconia bahiana is also known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.
