Myrcia shirleyana

Myrcia shirleyana, commonly known as Shirley's myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Myrcia shirleyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
