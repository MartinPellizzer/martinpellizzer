Myristica heterophylla

Myristica heterophylla, commonly known as the variable myristica, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its variable leaf morphology, which gives it its scientific name. The plant produces aromatic seeds enclosed in a thick, woody aril, which is a unique feature of the genus Myristica. Its leaves are typically alternate and can vary in shape from elliptical to ovate, depending on the environment.

The tree is valued for its essential oils and has traditional uses in medicine and perfumery.
