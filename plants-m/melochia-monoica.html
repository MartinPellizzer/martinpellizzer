Melochia monoica

Melochia monoica, commonly known as the velvetleaf or melochia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange or red fruits containing edible seeds. Melochia monoica is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields due to its adaptability and rapid growth.

Its botanical features make it both a useful forage plant and a potential weed in certain regions.
