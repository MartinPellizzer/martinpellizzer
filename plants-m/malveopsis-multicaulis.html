Malveopsis multicaulis

Malveopsis multicaulis, commonly known as the many-stemmed mallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and showy, pentagonal flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
