Mentha arvensis var. duftschmidii

Mentha arvensis var. duftschmidii, commonly known as the Alpine peppermint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in terminal clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its unique fragrance and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes or in traditional herbal medicine.
