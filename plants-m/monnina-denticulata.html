Monnina denticulata

Monnina denticulata, commonly known as the toothed monnina, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, pink to red flowers clustered in dense spikes. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to full sun.
