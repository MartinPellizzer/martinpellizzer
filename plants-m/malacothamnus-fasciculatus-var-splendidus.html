Malacothamnus fasciculatus var. splendidus

Malacothamnus fasciculatus var. splendidus, commonly known as the golden globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant collections and pollinator habitats.
