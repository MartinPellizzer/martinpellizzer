Mutisia latifolia f. latifolia

Mutisia latifolia f. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil types.
