Medicago rigidula var. cinerascens

Medicago rigidula var. cinerascens, commonly known as the ash-gray medic.

This variant is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Europe. It features gray-green foliage with a distinctive ash-colored underside, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, typical of the Fabaceae family.

It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential in ecological restoration projects.
