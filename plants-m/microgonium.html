Microgonium

Microgonium, commonly known as a type of aquatic fern, is a small, primitive plant that belongs to the order Schizomatales.

It is characterized by its simple, ribbon-like leaves and is typically found in freshwater environments. The plant lacks true roots and relies on rhizoids for anchorage and nutrient absorption. Microgonium is often used in ecological studies to assess water quality due to its sensitivity to environmental changes.

Its reproductive structures, known as sporangia, produce spores that are dispersed by water currents.
