Malanea macrophylla f. bahiensis

Malanea macrophylla f. bahiensis, commonly known as the Bahia malanea, is a tropical plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, velvety leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and thrives in well-draining soil. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
