Monanthos Subbilobatus

Blue mistflower, scientifically known as Monanthos subbilobatus, is a striking perennial plant native to the eastern United States. It is celebrated for its delicate, feathery blue-gray foliage and clusters of small, mist-like blue flowers that bloom in late summer. This hardy plant thrives in partial to full sun and is well-suited for moist, well-drained soils. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native gardens and wildlife habitats. With its unique appearance and ecological benefits, the blue mistflower is both a beautiful and functional addition to the landscape.
