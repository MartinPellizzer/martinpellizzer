Monadenium descampsii

Monadenium descampsii, commonly known as the desert star or star cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of Namibia and Angola.

It features a compact, globular stem with a spiky, star-like appearance, composed of numerous small, fleshy tubercles. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers during the rainy season, typically in summer. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and adapting it to harsh desert conditions.

Monadenium descampsii is highly valued in horticulture for its striking appearance and ability to thrive in low-water environments.
