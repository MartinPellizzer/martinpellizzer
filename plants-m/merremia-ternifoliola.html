Merremia ternifoliola

Merremia ternifoliola, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, hairy hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found growing along riverbanks and in disturbed soils.

The flowers open in the morning and close by midday, giving it its common name.
