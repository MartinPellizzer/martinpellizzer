Maesa cavaleriei

Maesa cavaleriei, commonly known as the Chinese mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and inconspicuous white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are often eaten by birds, which aid in seed dispersal. It is native to eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and is often found in humid, forested areas.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can have a significant impact on the health of its host trees.
