Martynia lutea var. nelsoniana

Martynia lutea var. nelsoniana, commonly known as the Nelson's yellowflower, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that contain seeds.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
