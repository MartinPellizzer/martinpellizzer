Musa × sapientum var. paradisiaca

Musa × sapientum var. paradisiaca, commonly known as the banana plant, is a tropical herbaceous plant belonging to the Musaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can grow up to several meters in length, and its robust, pseudostem structure formed by tightly packed leaf sheaths. The plant produces clusters of elongated, curved fruits known as hands, each containing multiple individual bananas. The flowers are typically hidden within the fruit clusters, with the female flowers developing into the edible fruit.

This plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
