Mimosa nossibiensis

Mimosa nossibiensis, commonly known as the Brazilian mimosa, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruit pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its foliage and flowers are used in traditional medicine and horticulture.
