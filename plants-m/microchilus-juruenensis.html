Microchilus juruenensis

Microchilus juruenensis, commonly known as the Juruenensis orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers with a unique coloration pattern. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and drawing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its botanical significance lies in its role within the ecosystem and its potential for medicinal or horticultural use.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
