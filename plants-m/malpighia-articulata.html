Malpighia articulata

Malpighia articulata, commonly known as the Brazilian passionfruit or granadilla, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It produces edible fruits that are rich in vitamin C and have a tart, aromatic flavor. The plant has simple, alternate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that are pollinated by insects. Its fruit develops in a capsule-like structure, which splits open when mature to release the seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
