Molina salicifolia

Molina salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf molina, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that resemble those of willow trees. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Molina salicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
