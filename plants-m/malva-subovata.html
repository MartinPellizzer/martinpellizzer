Malva subovata

Malva subovata, commonly known as the broadleaf mallows, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, often found in moist, open habitats. It has a hairy stem and produces capsules as its fruit, which contain numerous small seeds.

Malva subovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
