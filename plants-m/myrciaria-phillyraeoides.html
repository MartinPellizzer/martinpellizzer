Myrciaria phillyraeoides

Myrciaria phillyraeoides, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá-do-brejo, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically red when ripe, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, with a distinctive aromatic scent.

The plant is also valued for its potential medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine.
