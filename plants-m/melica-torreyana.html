Melica torreyana

Melica torreyana, commonly known as Torrey's meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed flowers.

Melica torreyana is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for pollinators.
