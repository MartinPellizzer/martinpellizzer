Metrosideros gibbsiae

Metrosideros gibbsiae, commonly known as the Kauri pohutukawa, is a species of flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a small tree or shrub that typically grows in coastal areas, characterized by its bright red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

It is closely related to the pohutukawa and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in coastal ecosystems.
