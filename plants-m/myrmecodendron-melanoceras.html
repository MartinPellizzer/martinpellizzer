Myrmecodendron melanoceras

Myrmecodendron melanoceras, commonly known as the black ant plant, is a unique epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its specialized structures called myrmecodia, which house colonies of ants that live symbiotically with the plant. These ants provide protection against herbivores and help in nutrient cycling by breaking down organic matter. The plant's roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding debris.

Its striking black coloration and intricate morphology make it a fascinating subject in botanical studies.
