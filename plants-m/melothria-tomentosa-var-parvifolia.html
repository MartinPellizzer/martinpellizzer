Melothria tomentosa var. parvifolia

Melothria tomentosa var. parvifolia, commonly known as the woolly sour sop, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features small, hairy, ovate leaves that are typically covered in a dense white pubescence. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits with a sour taste, often used in local cuisines. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in clusters.

This variant is distinguished by its smaller leaves compared to the typical variety of Melothria tomentosa.
