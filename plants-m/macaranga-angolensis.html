Macaranga angolensis

Macaranga angolensis, commonly known as the African paper plant, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a fast-growing shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically greenish-yellow in color, and it produces capsules as its fruit.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in secondary forests and along stream banks.
