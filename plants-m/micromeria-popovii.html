Micromeria popovii

Micromeria popovii, commonly known as the desert sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a small annual herb with slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is well-suited to sandy and rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub habitats.

Micromeria popovii plays a minor role in local ecosystems but is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to extreme environments.
