Maesa walkeri

Maesa walkeri, commonly known as the African snowball, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Maesa walkeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance.
