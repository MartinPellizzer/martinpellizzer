Morus alba var. venosa

Morus alba var. venosa, commonly known as the variegated mulberry, is a cultivar of the white mulberry tree characterized by its distinctive variegated leaves.

The leaves exhibit a striking combination of white, pink, and green hues, making it a popular ornamental plant. It is native to China and thrives in temperate climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are edible but less commonly consumed compared to other mulberry varieties.

Its unique foliage makes it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.
