Masdevallia ventricularia var. longicaudata

Masdevallia ventricularia var. longicaudata, commonly known as the long-tailed masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers that display a range of colors, including shades of pink, white, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually long and narrow, adapted for efficient water absorption in its natural habitat.

The variety longicaudata is distinguished by its elongated, trailing flower spikes, which contribute to its ornamental appeal.
