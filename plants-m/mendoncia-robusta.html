Mendoncia robusta

Mendoncia robusta, commonly known as the hardy forest fern, is a perennial plant native to the temperate rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can grow up to two meters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and has a robust root system that helps it anchor in rocky or soil-rich substrates. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are typically located on the underside of the fronds.

Mendoncia robusta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to contribute to the biodiversity of forest ecosystems.
