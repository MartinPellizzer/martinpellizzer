Monadenium mamfwense

Monadenium mamfwense, commonly known as the Namib Desert cactus, is a succulent plant native to the hyperarid regions of Namibia.

It has a short, cylindrical stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in extreme drought conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the brief rainy season, which attracts pollinators such as bees and moths. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Monadenium mamfwense is adapted to high temperatures and low humidity, making it a unique example of desert flora.
