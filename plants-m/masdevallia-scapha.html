Masdevallia scapha

Masdevallia scapha, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a tropical orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, hood-like flower shape, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinctive, elongated lip.

Masdevallia scapha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
