Melocanna zollingeri var. brachyclada

Melocanna zollingeri var. brachyclada, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a compact, clump-forming plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, green stems that grow in dense clusters, often reaching heights of only a few feet. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a fan-like pattern. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental gardening for its graceful appearance.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly, making it suitable for small garden spaces.
