Miconia pseudoradula

Miconia pseudoradula, commonly known as the red miconia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom throughout the wet season. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains numerous seeds.

Miconia pseudoradula is often found in lowland rainforests and is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets.
