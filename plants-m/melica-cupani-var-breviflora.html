Melica cupani var. breviflora

Melica cupani var. breviflora, commonly known as the short-flowered cupgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet at the tip of each stem, bearing small, inconspicuous flowers.

This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in disturbed or wetland environments.
