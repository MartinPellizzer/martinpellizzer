Miconia berbiceana

Miconia berbiceana, commonly known as the Trinidadian miconia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Trinidad and Tobago.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a white, waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are round, fleshy berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Miconia berbiceana is also known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, which can impact local ecosystems.
