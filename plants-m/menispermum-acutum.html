Menispermum acutum

Menispermum acutum, commonly known as the arrowhead vine, is a climbing plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that resemble an arrowhead. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by bright red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its roots contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine, though they are not widely utilized today.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
