Musa bukensis

Musa bukensis, commonly known as the Bukentis banana, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and clusters of yellow, edible berries. The plant thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and ample sunlight. Its fruit is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

Botanically, it belongs to the Musaceae family and is closely related to other banana species.
