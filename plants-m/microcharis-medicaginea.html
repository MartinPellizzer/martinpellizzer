Microcharis medicaginea

Microcharis medicaginea, commonly known as the medicinal spider plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features striking, arrow-shaped leaves with a bright green color and a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
