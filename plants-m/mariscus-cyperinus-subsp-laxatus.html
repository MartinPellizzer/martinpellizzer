Mariscus cyperinus subsp. laxatus

Mariscus cyperinus subsp.

laxatus, commonly known as the slender spikerush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family. It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences.

This subspecies typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowering stems are usually shorter and more loosely arranged compared to other subspecies of Mariscus cyperinus.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
