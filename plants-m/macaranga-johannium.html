Macaranga johannium

Macaranga johannium, commonly known as the Johann's makamaka, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with prominent yellowish-green cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forest areas.

Macaranga johannium is also known for its rapid growth rate and is sometimes used in reforestation efforts.
