Malpighia crassifolia

Malpighia crassifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or granadilla, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that give way to bright red, fleshy berries. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its berries are edible, with a tart flavor, and are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant also has ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and vibrant fruit.
