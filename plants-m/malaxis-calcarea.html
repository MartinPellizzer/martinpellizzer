Malaxis calcarea

Malaxis calcarea, commonly known as the limestone orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in calcareous soils, often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette at the base of the stem.

Malaxis calcarea is notable for its delicate appearance and its ecological significance in specialized habitats.
