Mentha × rotundifolia var. apodysa

Mentha × rotundifolia var. apodysa, commonly known as the round-leaf mint, is a variant of the hybrid mint plant characterized by its rounded, broad leaves.

It is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia, often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, contributing to its ornamental value. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, making it useful in culinary and medicinal applications.

This variety is valued for its unique leaf shape and mild mint aroma.
