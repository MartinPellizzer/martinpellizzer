Myrcianthes fragrans var. simpsonii

Myrcianthes fragrans var. simpsonii, commonly known as Simpson's fragrant myrcia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the understory of tropical forests. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a small, rounded capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
