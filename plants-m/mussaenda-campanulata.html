Mussaenda campanulata

Mussaenda campanulata, commonly known as the bell-shaped Mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring soft, hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
