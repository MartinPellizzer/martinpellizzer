Mutisia taraxacifolia f. cirrhifera

Mutisia taraxacifolia f. cirrhifera, commonly known as the curled-leaved mutisia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curled leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It is native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, and thrives in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
