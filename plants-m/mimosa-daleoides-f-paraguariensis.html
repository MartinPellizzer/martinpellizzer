Mimosa daleoides f. paraguariensis

Mimosa daleoides f. paraguariensis, commonly known as curaçao or curaçao plant, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil and Paraguay.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves that fold shut when touched, a response known as thigmonastic movement. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by pod-like fruits containing edible seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some regions for medicinal purposes.
