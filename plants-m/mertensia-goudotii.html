Mertensia goudotii

Mertensia goudotii, commonly known as the Goudot's bluebells, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Mertensia goudotii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
