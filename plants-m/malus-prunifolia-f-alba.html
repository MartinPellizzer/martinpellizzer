Malus prunifolia f. alba

Malus prunifolia f. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered crabapple, is a cultivar of the crabapple tree characterized by its pale white blossoms.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to China. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves with a serrated edge and produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or red when mature.

This ornamental tree is often cultivated for its showy spring flowers and is well-suited for smaller gardens due to its compact size.
