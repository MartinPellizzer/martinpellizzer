Micromyrtus monotaxis

Micromyrtus monotaxis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that resemble bottle brushes, typically bearing red or pink flowers. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Micromyrtus monotaxis thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in heathlands and shrublands.
