Mentha sylvestris var. villosa

Mentha sylvestris var. villosa, commonly known as hairy wood mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves and medicinal properties have made it a subject of interest in both traditional and modern herbal practices.
