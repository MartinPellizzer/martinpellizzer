Mentha sylvestris var. alpigena

Mentha sylvestris var. alpigena, commonly known as alpine mint, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. The plant has aromatic, serrated leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically purple or blue, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in herbal teas and remedies.
