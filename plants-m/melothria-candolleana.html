Melothria candolleana

Melothria candolleana, commonly known as the yellow passionfruit, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It produces small, yellow, spherical fruits with a thick, leathery rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated margin and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value.

The flowers are typically greenish-white with five petals and are pollinated by insects.
