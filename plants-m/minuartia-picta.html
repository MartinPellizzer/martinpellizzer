Minuartia picta

Minuartia picta, commonly known as the painted rock-cress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and alpine environments in Europe and Asia.

It features small, clustered leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white or pale green pubescence. The flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with four petals that are often adorned with intricate patterns. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found on limestone or gravelly slopes.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
