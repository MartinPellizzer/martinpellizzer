Mentha longifolia var. pseudoulotricha

Mentha longifolia var. pseudoulotricha, commonly known as wild mint, is a variant of the mint family (Lamiaceae) characterized by its aromatic, slightly minty scent.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodland areas and stream banks. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy surface and produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive fragrance and medicinal properties.

This variety is often used in herbal remedies and as a culinary herb due to its refreshing taste and therapeutic benefits.
