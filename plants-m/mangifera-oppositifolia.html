Mangifera oppositifolia

Mangifera oppositifolia, commonly known as the Indian mango, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and produces a distinctive fruit with a thick, fibrous pulp. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its flowers are small and fragrant, typically appearing in clusters, and it is primarily cultivated for its edible fruit.

The tree can reach heights of up to 20 meters and has a long lifespan, making it a valuable species in both agriculture and ecological contexts.
