Melittacanthus divaricatus

Melittacanthus divaricatus, commonly known as the variable acanthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to red. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Melittacanthus divaricatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
