Matricaria pallida

Matricaria pallida, commonly known as pale chamomile, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its yellow flower heads and white ray florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are hairy and have a strong, aromatic scent, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Matricaria pallida is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
