Milium coerulescens var. kokanicum

Milium coerulescens var. kokanicum, commonly known as the Kokanik millet, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that are clustered in spikelets, giving it a delicate appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
