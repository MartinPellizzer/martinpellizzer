Moraea muddii

Moraea muddii, commonly known as the Mudd's moraea, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, often colorful flowers. The plant typically blooms in summer, producing clusters of delicate, tubular flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
