Mentha × suavis var. lamiifolia

Mentha × suavis var. lamiifolia, commonly known as sweet mint, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly fuzzy leaves that emit a strong, sweet mint scent. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with square stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or purple, appearing in whorls along the stem.

This variety is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, as well as its ornamental appeal in gardens.
