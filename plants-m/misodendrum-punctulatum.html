Misodendrum punctulatum

Misodendrum punctulatum, commonly known as the spotted leaf vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with distinctive white spots, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found growing along forest edges or in shaded undergrowth.

Its unique appearance and climbing habit make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
