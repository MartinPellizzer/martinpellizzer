Miconia mesmeana subsp. longipetiolata

Miconia mesmeana subsp.

longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled miconia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in inflorescences. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains multiple seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
