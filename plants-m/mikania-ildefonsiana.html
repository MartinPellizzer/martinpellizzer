Mikania ildefonsiana

Mikania ildefonsiana, commonly known as Mexican vine or morning glory, is a fast-growing, herbaceous vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically light to dark green. The plant produces small, five-petaled flowers that are usually white or pale blue, often blooming in clusters. It is known for its rapid growth rate and ability to spread aggressively, making it an invasive species in many regions.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to a variety of soil types and environmental conditions.
