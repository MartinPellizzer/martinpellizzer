Marlea begoniifolia

Marlea begoniifolia, commonly known as the begonia-leaved marlea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage with a deep green color and intricate patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for both its foliage and floral display.
