Muldera diandra

Muldera diandra, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a small, round pod that splits open to release seeds.

Muldera diandra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.
