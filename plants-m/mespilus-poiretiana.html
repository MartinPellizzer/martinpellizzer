Mespilus poiretiana

Mespilus poiretiana, commonly known as the Persian white mulberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes other well-known plants like the common mulberry and fig. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, aggregate fruits that turn from green to yellow or red when mature.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
