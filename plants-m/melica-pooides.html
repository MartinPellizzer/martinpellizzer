Melica pooides

Melica pooides, commonly known as the slender brome, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.

Melica pooides is often found in moist to mesic environments and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and soil stability.
