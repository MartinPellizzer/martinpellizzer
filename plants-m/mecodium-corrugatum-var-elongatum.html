Mecodium corrugatum var. elongatum

Mecodium corrugatum var. elongatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mecodium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 30 centimeters in height. The leaves are linear to lanceolate, with a distinctive corrugated texture along their surfaces. Its small, pale blue to violet flowers are clustered in dense, terminal spikes, typical of the Boraginaceae family.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
