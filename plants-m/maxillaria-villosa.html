Maxillaria villosa

Maxillaria villosa, commonly known as the hairy maxillaria, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers, which can be white, pink, or purple, bloom in spring and summer.

Maxillaria villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
