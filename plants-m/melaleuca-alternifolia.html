Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia, commonly known as tea tree, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to the northeast coast of Australia and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small white or pink flowers. The plant is well-known for its essential oil, which is extracted from its leaves and used in various medicinal and skincare applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands.

Its resilience to drought and salt makes it a common plant in coastal regions.
