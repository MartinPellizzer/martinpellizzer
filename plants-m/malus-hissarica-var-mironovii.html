Malus hissarica var. mironovii

Malus hissarica var. mironovii, commonly known as the Mironovii apple tree, is a rare and ornamental variant of the apple species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, making it suitable for small gardens or as a decorative tree. The plant produces small, often ornamental fruits that are not typically edible, though they add visual interest in the fall. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, providing aesthetic value throughout the growing season.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.
