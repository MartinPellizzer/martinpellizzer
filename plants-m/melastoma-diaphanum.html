Melastoma diaphanum

Melastoma diaphanum, commonly known as the diaphanous melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it often grows in moist, shaded environments. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Melastoma diaphanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
