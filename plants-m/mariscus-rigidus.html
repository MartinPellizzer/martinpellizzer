Mariscus rigidus

Mariscus rigidus, commonly known as the rigid marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 60 centimeters in height, often with a reddish tinge. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes during the summer months. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, with a rough texture that aids in water retention.

Mariscus rigidus plays an important role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various insects and microorganisms.
