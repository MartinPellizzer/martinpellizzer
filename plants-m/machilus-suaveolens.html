Machilus suaveolens

Machilus suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented machilus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocados. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, ovate leaves that emit a pleasant fragrance when crushed. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elliptical, dark blue-black berries.

Machilus suaveolens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
