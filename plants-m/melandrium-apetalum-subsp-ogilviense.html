Melandrium apetalum subsp. ogilviense

Melandrium apetalum subsp.

ogilviense, commonly known as the Scottish catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles. It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 20 centimeters.

The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is characterized by its distinctive seed pods that split open to release its seeds.
