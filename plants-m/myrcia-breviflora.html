Myrcia breviflora

Myrcia breviflora, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to others. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and moist forests.

Myrcia breviflora is also known for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
