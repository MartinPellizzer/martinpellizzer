Miconia tenensis

Miconia tenensis, commonly known as the Caribbean miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Miconia tenensis is known for its rapid growth and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming an invasive species.
