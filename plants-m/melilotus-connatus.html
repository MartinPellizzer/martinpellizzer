Melilotus connatus

Melilotus connatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf melilot, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three narrow, lance-shaped leaflets.

Melilotus connatus is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
