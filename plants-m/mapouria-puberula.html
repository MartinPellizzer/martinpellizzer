Mapouria puberula

Mapouria puberula, commonly known as the velvet mapouria, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Mapouria puberula is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, arid environments.
