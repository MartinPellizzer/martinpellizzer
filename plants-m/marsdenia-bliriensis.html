Marsdenia bliriensis

Marsdenia bliriensis, commonly known as the Bliriensian Marsdenia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asclepiadaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbellate clusters, which are followed by follicles containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forest understories where it relies on climbing to reach sunlight.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
