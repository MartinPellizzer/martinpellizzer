Malpighia peruviana

Malpighia peruviana, commonly known as the passionfruit, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It produces vibrant orange or yellow fruits with a thick, leathery rind and a juicy, aromatic interior. The plant belongs to the family Malpighiaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. Its fruits are rich in vitamins, particularly vitamin C, and are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
