Musa sikkimensis var. simmondsii

Musa sikkimensis var. simmondsii, commonly known as the Sikkim banana, is a tropical plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and strikingly ornamental inflorescence. The plant produces elongated, curved fruits that are typically yellow when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value rather than its fruit.

This variety is notable for its unique leaf patterns and is sometimes used in ornamental horticulture.
