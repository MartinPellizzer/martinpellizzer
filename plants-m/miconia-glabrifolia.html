Miconia glabrifolia

Miconia glabrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are often consumed by various bird species. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable plant in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
