Monochaetum ciliatum

Monochaetum ciliatum, commonly known as the hairy leek orchid, is an herbaceous perennial plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its strap-like, grass-like leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats, such as grasslands and heathlands. It is known for its distinctive hairy structures on the stems and leaves, which give it its common name.

Monochaetum ciliatum is often found in regions with a temperate climate and is relatively rare, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
