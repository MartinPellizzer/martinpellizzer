Marasmodes oubinae

Marasmodes oubinae, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in shrubland and savanna habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a velvety texture, and the flowers are borne on long, slender pedicels.

The species is noted for its adaptability to arid conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
