Myrceugenia regnelliana var. leptophylla

Myrceugenia regnelliana var. leptophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a species native to the southern regions of Brazil.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
