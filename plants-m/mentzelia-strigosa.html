Mentzelia strigosa

Mentzelia strigosa, commonly known as silverleaf blazingstar, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its distinctive, narrow leaves and clustered inflorescences.

Mentzelia strigosa plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
