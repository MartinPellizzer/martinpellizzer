Maxillaria striolata

Maxillaria striolata, commonly known as the streaked orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb that supports its growth and storage of nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate patterns. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Maxillaria striolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
