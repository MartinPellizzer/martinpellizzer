Matthiola oxyceras var. basiceras

Matthiola oxyceras var. basiceras, commonly known as the narrow-leaved stock, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, fragrant flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The leaves are linear and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight.

This variety is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
