Malvastrum coccineum

Malvastrum coccineum, commonly known as the scarlet mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red bracts that surround clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant has hairy, lobed leaves and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
