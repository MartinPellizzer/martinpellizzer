Marsdenia margaritaria

Marsdenia margaritaria, commonly known as the bead vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces distinctive, bead-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Marsdenia margaritaria thrives in humid environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.
