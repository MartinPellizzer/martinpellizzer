Miconia penninervis

Miconia penninervis, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a velvety, pubescent surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, followed by red, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. Miconia penninervis is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a challenge for land management.
