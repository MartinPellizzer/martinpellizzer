Miconia santanana

Miconia santanana, commonly known as the Brazilian starleaf, is a member of the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that disperses seeds through wind, contributing to its spread in the wild.

Miconia santanana is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
