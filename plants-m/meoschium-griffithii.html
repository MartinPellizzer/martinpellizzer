Meoschium griffithii

Meoschium griffithii, commonly known as the Himalayan snowbell, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Meoschium griffithii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate regions.
