Matricaria virgata

Matricaria virgata, commonly known as the meadow buttonweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows and dry grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are yellow with a central disk, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds.

Matricaria virgata is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential calming properties.
