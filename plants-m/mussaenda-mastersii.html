Mussaenda mastersii

Mussaenda mastersii, commonly known as the golden flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered yellow flowers that resemble flames, making it a striking ornamental plant. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Mussaenda mastersii is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display and adaptability to warm climates.
