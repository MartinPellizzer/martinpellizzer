Mimosa parapitiensis

Mimosa parapitiensis, commonly known as the golden mimosas, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are composed of numerous small, feather-like leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow to golden flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility in its native habitat.
