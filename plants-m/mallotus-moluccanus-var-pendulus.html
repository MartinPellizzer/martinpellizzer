Mallotus moluccanus var. pendulus

Mallotus moluccanus var. pendulus, commonly known as the weeping holly, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous, drooping branches that give it a distinctive, cascading appearance. The plant has small, glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its flowers are inconspicuous, and it produces small, red berries that are edible for birds.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique foliage structure and adaptability to various soil types.
