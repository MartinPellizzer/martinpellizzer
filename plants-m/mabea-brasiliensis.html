Mabea brasiliensis

Mabea brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian candlewood, is a deciduous shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, candle-like inflorescences that are often brightly colored. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers surrounded by fleshy, colorful bracts that attract pollinators. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

Mabea brasiliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
