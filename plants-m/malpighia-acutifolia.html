Malpighia acutifolia

Malpighia acutifolia, commonly known as the sour sop or granadilla, is a tropical shrub native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by bright orange, fleshy fruits with a distinctive spiky exterior. The fruit is edible, containing a creamy, aromatic pulp that is rich in nutrients and often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
