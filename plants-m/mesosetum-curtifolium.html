Mesosetum curtifolium

Mesosetum curtifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in saturated conditions, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to its environment, supporting local wildlife and contributing to soil stabilization.
