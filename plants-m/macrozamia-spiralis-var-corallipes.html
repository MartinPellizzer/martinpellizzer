Macrozamia spiralis var. corallipes

Macrozamia spiralis var. corallipes, commonly known as the coral-pink cycad, is a rare and unique cycad species native to the coastal regions of New South Wales, Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spirally arranged leaves that give it a striking, almost architectural appearance. The plant produces large, cone-like structures, with the female cones being particularly showy and often tinged with a coral-pink hue. Its slow growth rate and specific habitat requirements make it a challenging but rewarding species for cultivation.

Due to its limited natural range and vulnerability to environmental threats, it is considered endangered and requires careful conservation efforts.
