Miconia quadridomius

Miconia quadridomius, commonly known as the four-domed miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in dense, velvety hairs. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains numerous small seeds.

Miconia quadridomius is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.
