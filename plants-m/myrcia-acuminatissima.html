Myrcia acuminatissima

Myrcia acuminatissima, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, berry-like fruits. Myrcia acuminatissima is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of tropical flora.
