Melocactus grandis

Melocactus grandis, commonly known as the giant melon cactus, is a distinctive species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, globose stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often with a ribbed structure and a spiny crown. The plant produces yellow flowers during the rainy season, which are followed by edible, melon-like fruits. It has a slow growth rate and is well adapted to harsh desert environments.

Melocactus grandis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in arid gardens.
