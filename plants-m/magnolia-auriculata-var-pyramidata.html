Magnolia auriculata var. pyramidata

Magnolia auriculata var. pyramidata, commonly known as the Chinese magnolia or the pyramid magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a pointed tip. The tree has a pyramidal shape when young, which becomes more rounded with age.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
