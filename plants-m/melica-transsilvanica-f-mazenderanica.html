Melica transsilvanica f. mazenderanica

Melica transsilvanica f. mazenderanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian milletgrass, is a variant of the grass species Melica transsilvanica.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets. It typically blooms in early summer, producing delicate, pale green to brown flowers.

This subspecies is native to Central and Eastern Europe and is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
