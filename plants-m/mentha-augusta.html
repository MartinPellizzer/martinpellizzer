Mentha augusta

Mentha augusta, commonly known as catnip, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant is notable for its aromatic leaves and flowers, which are typically pink or purple in color. Mentha augusta contains high levels of nepetalactone, a compound responsible for its stimulating effects on cats.

It is often cultivated for its medicinal and aromatic properties.
