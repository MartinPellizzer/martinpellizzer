Monstera bocatorensis

Monstera bocatorensis, commonly known as the Bocatorensis Monstera, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that often have distinctive holes and notches. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage is usually a deep green with a waxy texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red berries.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant.
