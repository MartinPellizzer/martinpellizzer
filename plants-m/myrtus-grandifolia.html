Myrtus grandifolia

Myrtus grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaved myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its aromatic leaves and berries are also used in culinary applications.

Myrtus grandifolia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and ornamental plant in gardens.
