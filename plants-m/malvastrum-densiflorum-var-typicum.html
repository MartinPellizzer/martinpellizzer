Malvastrum densiflorum var. typicum

Malvastrum densiflorum var. typicum, commonly known as the denseflower mallow, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and grows in clusters, often reaching heights of 1 to 3 feet. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
