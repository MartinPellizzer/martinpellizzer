Maackia chekiangensis

Maackia chekiangensis, commonly known as the Chinese maackia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and characteristic pod fruits. The plant features compound leaves with an odd number of leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers in early spring. Its fruits are dry, dehiscent pods that contain several seeds.

Maackia chekiangensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and floral display.
