Mimosa dutrae

Mimosa dutrae, commonly known as the Brazilian mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is known for its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Mimosa dutrae is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
