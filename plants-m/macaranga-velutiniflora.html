Macaranga velutiniflora

Macaranga velutiniflora, commonly known as velvetleaf macaranga, is a tropical plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It is native to Southeast Asia and is often found in disturbed habitats such as forests and along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it an important component of tropical ecosystems.
