Maxillaria baudoensis

Maxillaria baudoensis, commonly known as the Baudo orchid, is a tropical orchid native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces fragrant, showy flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically blooms in the summer months, showcasing a range of colors including white, pink, and purple. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Maxillaria baudoensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
