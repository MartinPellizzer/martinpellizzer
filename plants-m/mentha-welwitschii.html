Mentha welwitschii

Mentha welwitschii, commonly known as the Namibian mint, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, square stems and opposite, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Mentha welwitschii is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
