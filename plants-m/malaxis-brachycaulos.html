Malaxis brachycaulos

Malaxis brachycaulos, commonly known as the short-stemmed marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. The plant has a short, thick rhizome and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a loose spike. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is characterized by its lack of a prominent scape.

Malaxis brachycaulos is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate, ephemeral blooms.
