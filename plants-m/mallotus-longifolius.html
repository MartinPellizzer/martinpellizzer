Mallotus longifolius

Mallotus longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved mallot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a smooth, glossy surface.
