Medinilla congesta

Medinilla congesta, commonly known as the clustered medinilla, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtle family (Myrtaceae) and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are usually pink or white, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the rainy season.

Medinilla congesta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
