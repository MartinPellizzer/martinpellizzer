Magnolia purpurascens

Magnolia purpurascens, commonly known as the purple magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with a pinkish tinge. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are broadly ovate with a pointed tip. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
