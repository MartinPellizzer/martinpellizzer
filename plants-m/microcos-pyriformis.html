Microcos pyriformis

Microcos pyriformis, commonly known as the round-leaf microcos, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks and wetlands. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellow flowers appear in clusters and bloom from spring to early summer.

Microcos pyriformis is known for its ability to thrive in wet environments and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
