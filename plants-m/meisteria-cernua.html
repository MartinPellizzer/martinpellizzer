Meisteria cernua

Meisteria cernua, commonly known as the hanging begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing stems that produce clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Meisteria cernua is popular in hanging baskets and as a trailing plant in gardens and indoor spaces.
