Mitscherlichia coriacea

Mitscherlichia coriacea, commonly known as the roughleaf mitscherlichia, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its rough, textured leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by dark red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique foliage and ornamental value make it a desirable plant for native plant gardens.
