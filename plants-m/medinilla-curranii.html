Medinilla curranii

Medinilla curranii, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or Christmas bell, is a tropical flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their ornamental appeal and long-lasting bloom.

Medinilla curranii thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
