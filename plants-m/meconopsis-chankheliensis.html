Meconopsis chankheliensis, commonly known as the Chankheli blue poppy, is a striking alpine plant native to the mountainous regions of Georgia.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue or purple flowers, which bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in the Caucasus region. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, aiding in water retention.
