Maxillaria olivacea

Maxillaria olivacea, commonly known as the Olive Orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green. The plant typically blooms during the rainy season, and its flowers are often described as resembling small, delicate orchids. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

The species is known for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
