Monvillea adelmarii

Monvillea adelmarii, commonly known as the Adelmarii orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, fragrant blooms. The plant produces small, lance-shaped leaves and has a unique inflorescence with pendulous flowers in shades of white and pink. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
