Melanthium phalangioides

Melanthium phalangioides, commonly known as the false nettle, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants like sorrel and buckwheat. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often hairy and bear small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a distinctive veining pattern.

It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas with rich soil.
