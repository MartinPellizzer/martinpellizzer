Maxillariella tuerckheimii

Maxillariella tuerckheimii, commonly known as the Tuerckheim's orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant has a pseudobulb and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it blooms during the summer months.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
