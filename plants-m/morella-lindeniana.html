Morella lindeniana

Morella lindeniana, commonly known as the wax myrtle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, waxy leaves that are typically dark green on top and white underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into dark blue berries, which are a food source for various birds and wildlife. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

The plant has a compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
