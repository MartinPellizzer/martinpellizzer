Malus asiatica

Malus asiatica, commonly known as the Chinese crabapple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its small, ornamental flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The tree produces small, tart fruits that are edible but often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and turn vibrant shades of yellow and red in autumn.

Malus asiatica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
