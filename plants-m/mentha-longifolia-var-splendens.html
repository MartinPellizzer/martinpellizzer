Mentha longifolia var. splendens

Mentha longifolia var. splendens, commonly known as the glittering mint, is a cultivar of the mint family (Lamiaceae) characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage.

It is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its aromatic leaves contain high levels of menthol, making it useful in culinary and medicinal applications.

The variety is prized for its ornamental value and distinct fragrance.
