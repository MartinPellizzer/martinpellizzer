Molinia caerulea var. atrovirens

Molinia caerulea var. atrovirens, commonly known as purple moor grass, is a variant of the moor grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and its distinctive purple to blue-green foliage. The plant thrives in wet, acidic soils and is often found in peat bogs and heathlands. Its inflorescence consists of spike-like clusters of small, greenish or brownish flowers that appear in late summer.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ecological importance in stabilizing wetland environments.
