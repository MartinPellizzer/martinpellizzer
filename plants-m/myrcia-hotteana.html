Myrcia hotteana

Myrcia hotteana, commonly known as Brazilian pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that give way to red, fleshy berries.

Myrcia hotteana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been used in traditional medicine.
