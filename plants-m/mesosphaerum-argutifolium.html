Mesosphaerum argutifolium

Mesosphaerum argutifolium, commonly known as the roughleaf mesosphaerum, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically gray-green in color and have a leathery texture.

Mesosphaerum argutifolium is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
