Melanoseris lessertiana var. lyrata

Melanoseris lessertiana var. lyrata, commonly known as the lyrated black seris, is a unique variant of the black seris plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, glossy leaves that are often adorned with intricate, lyrical vein patterns. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as under forest canopies, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soil. Its leaves are broad and elongated, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

While not widely cultivated, it is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in its native habitat.
