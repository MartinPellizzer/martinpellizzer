Malaxis commelinifolia

Malaxis commelinifolia, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a dense, dark green stem with numerous small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces a tall, flowering spike with numerous pinkish-white flowers arranged in a loose spike. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
