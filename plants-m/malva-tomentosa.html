Malva tomentosa

Malva tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other well-known species like cotton and hibiscus. Its hairy stems and broad, lobed leaves are typical of the genus Malva.

Malva tomentosa is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.
