Mercurialis annua subsp. ambigua

Mercurialis annua subsp.

ambigua, commonly known as the common mercuryplant, is a flowering plant species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields.

The plant features simple, alternate leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and the plant is often found in open habitats.

The species is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
