Mimetes cucullatus f. laxa

Mimetes cucullatus f. laxa, commonly known as the soft-hooded pincushion, is a unique variant of the pincushion plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fluffy, soft, and loosely arranged flower heads that give it a distinctive, almost ethereal appearance. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its drought-resistant properties, making it well-suited to arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs.

This form is a natural variation of the species, showcasing genetic diversity within the Mimetes genus.
