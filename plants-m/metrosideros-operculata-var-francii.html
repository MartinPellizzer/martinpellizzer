Metrosideros operculata var. francii

Metrosideros operculata var. francii, commonly known as the Cook Islands pohutukawa, is a shrub or small tree native to the Cook Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, papery flowers that appear in clusters, often blooming throughout the year. The leaves are lance-shaped and have a leathery texture, while the bark is smooth and reddish-brown. This plant thrives in coastal and sunny environments, often growing on rocky or sandy soils.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for local wildlife.
