Maba montigena

Maba montigena, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces a milky sap that is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
