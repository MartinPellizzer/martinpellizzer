Melia baccifera

Melia baccifera, commonly known as the Indian beech or white beech, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, fleshy fruits that turn yellow when mature. Its seeds are encased in a hard, woody shell, and the tree is often found in moist, shady habitats.

Melia baccifera is valued for its timber and has traditional medicinal uses in various parts of Asia.
