Meum atlanticum

Meum atlanticum, commonly known as the Atlantic meadow grass, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of the Atlantic coast.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dunes and salt marshes. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.

Meum atlanticum is adapted to salt spray and can tolerate periodic flooding, making it a hardy species in harsh coastal environments.
