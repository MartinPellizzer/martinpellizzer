Melicocca apetala

Melicocca apetala, commonly known as the Florida holly, is a small evergreen tree native to the southeastern United States.

It has glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically hidden among the foliage, giving it its scientific name "apetala," meaning "without petals.". Its fruits are red, fleshy drupes that contain a single seed, and they are an important food source for wildlife.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
