Myrtus communis var. gervasii

Myrtus communis var. gervasii, commonly known as the Gervasii myrtle, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are aromatic and covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, pale pink or white flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery and traditional medicine.
