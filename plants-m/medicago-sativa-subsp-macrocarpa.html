Medicago sativa subsp. macrocarpa

Medicago sativa subsp.

macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-seeded alfalfa, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its robust growth habit, trifoliate leaves, and yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant is notable for its large, elliptical seed pods that contain edible seeds used in forage and human nutrition. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and improves soil fertility through nitrogen fixation.

This subspecies is widely cultivated for its high nutritional value and adaptability to various soil types.
