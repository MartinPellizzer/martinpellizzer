Machaeranthera pinnatifida subsp. gooddingii

Machaeranthera pinnatifida subsp.

gooddingii, commonly known as Goodding's tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are pinnately divided, giving it a feathery appearance, and it produces showy yellow to orange flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in native plant gardens.
