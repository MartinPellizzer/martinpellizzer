Melilotus arvensis var. grandiflorus

Melilotus arvensis var. grandiflorus, commonly known as sweet clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and fragrant yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to other parts of the world. It has a taproot system and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields.

Sweet clover is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in agricultural settings.
