Maba albens

Maba albens, commonly known as the white maba tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and alternate, simple leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by fleshy, greenish fruits that contain seeds. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its timber and traditional medicinal uses.

The plant has a shallow root system and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
