Murtughas speciosa

Murtughas speciosa, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
