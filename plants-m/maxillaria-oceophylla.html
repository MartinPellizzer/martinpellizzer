Maxillaria oceophylla

Maxillaria oceophylla, commonly known as the golden maxillaria, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically dark green and strap-like, providing a lush backdrop for its vibrant blooms.

Maxillaria oceophylla is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
