Microchilus curviflorus var. azulitae

Microchilus curviflorus var. azulitae, commonly known as the Azureflowered Bluebell, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-violet flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a striking visual contrast against its dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories. Its unique coloration and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat destruction, it is considered endangered and requires careful preservation efforts.
