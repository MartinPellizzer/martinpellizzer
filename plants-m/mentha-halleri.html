Mentha halleri

Mentha halleri, commonly known as European water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as stream banks, wet meadows, and marshy areas. Its leaves are opposite, oval to lance-shaped, and have a strong minty scent.

Mentha halleri produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the upper stem.
