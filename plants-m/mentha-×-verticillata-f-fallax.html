Mentha × verticillata f. fallax

Mentha × verticillata f. fallax, commonly known as the upright peppermint, is a perennial herb characterized by its erect growth habit and aromatic foliage.

It is a hybrid species within the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a strong menthol scent. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual interest.
