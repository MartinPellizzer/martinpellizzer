Mammillaria cornifera

Mammillaria cornifera, commonly known as the horned mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines and small, rounded areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its spiny structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and indoor gardening.
