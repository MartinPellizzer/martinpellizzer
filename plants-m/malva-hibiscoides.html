Malva hibiscoides

Malva hibiscoides, commonly known as the broadleaf mallows, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like hibiscus and cotton. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its flowers are typically five-petaled and have a distinctive, slightly hairy appearance.

Malva hibiscoides is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
