Mentha mollissima f. exserta

Mentha mollissima f. exserta, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or blue, arranged in loose spikes. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
