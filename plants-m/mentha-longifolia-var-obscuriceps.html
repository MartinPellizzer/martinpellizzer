Mentha longifolia var. obscuriceps

Mentha longifolia var. obscuriceps, commonly known as dark-headed mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces tiny, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet, arranged in dense spikes at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and is occasionally used in culinary or medicinal applications.
