Melistaurum arboreum

Melistaurum arboreum, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces woody pods that contain flat, edible seeds. It thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its timber, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
