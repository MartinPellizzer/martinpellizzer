Megaclinium oxyodon

Megaclinium oxyodon, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a tight, cylindrical cluster. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish or brownish and appear in early spring.

Megaclinium oxyodon plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
