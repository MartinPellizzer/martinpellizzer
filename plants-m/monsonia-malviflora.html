Monsonia malviflora

Monsonia malviflora, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Monsonia malviflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
