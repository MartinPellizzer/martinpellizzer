Moraea spicata

Moraea spicata, commonly known as the spike lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring and summer, producing striking purple or pink flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette.

Moraea spicata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight.
