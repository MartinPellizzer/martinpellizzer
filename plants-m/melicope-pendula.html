Melicope pendula

Melicope pendula, commonly known as the hanging melicope, is a small evergreen tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its drooping branches and fragrant, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by round, black berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal forests.

Melicope pendula is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
