Metrosideros polymorpha subsp. micrantha

Metrosideros polymorpha subsp.

micrantha, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family. It is native to the Hawaiian Islands and is characterized by its small, fragrant red flowers and smooth, reddish-barked trunk.

The plant typically grows as a tree or shrub, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a leathery texture.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance and historical use by Native Hawaiians.
