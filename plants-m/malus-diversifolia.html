Malus diversifolia

Malus diversifolia, commonly known as the variable crabapple, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its diverse leaf shapes, which can be ovate, elliptic, or lanceolate. The plant produces small, showy flowers in spring, typically white or pink, followed by edible, ornamental fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Malus diversifolia is relatively hardy and can thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
