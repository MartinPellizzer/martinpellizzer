Micromyrtus uniovulum

Micromyrtus uniovulum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallee, is a small, woody shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalypts and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. It is adapted to dry conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Micromyrtus uniovulum plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native fauna in its ecosystem.
