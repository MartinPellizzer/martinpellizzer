Miconia hammelii

Miconia hammelii, commonly known as the Hawaiian miconia, is a member of the Melastomataceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to the Hawaiian Islands, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers with five petals, which are often clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a fleshy, berry-like structure that contains multiple seeds.

Miconia hammelii is known for its rapid growth and has been introduced to other tropical regions, sometimes becoming invasive.
