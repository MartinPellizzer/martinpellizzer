Marsdenia brunnea

Marsdenia brunnea, commonly known as the black-stemmed Marsdenia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asclepiadaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions such as India, Sri Lanka, and parts of China. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

Marsdenia brunnea is typically found in tropical and subtropical forests, growing as a climbing or trailing plant.
