Malus anetica

Malus anetica, commonly known as the meadow apple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible apples that are typically consumed by birds and small mammals. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Malus anetica is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and for its ecological benefits.
