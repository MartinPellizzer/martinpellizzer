Melaleuca suaveolens

Melaleuca suaveolens, commonly known as the honey myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits called capsules that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions and dry sclerophyll forests.

Melaleuca suaveolens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.
