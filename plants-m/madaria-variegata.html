Madaria variegata

Madaria variegata, commonly known as the variegated madaria, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Araliaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves with striking variegation patterns. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually white or pale yellow and appear in clusters.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its aesthetic appeal.
