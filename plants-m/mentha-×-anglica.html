Mentha × anglica

Mentha × anglica, commonly known as angelica mint, is a hybrid plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly fuzzy leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. Angelica mint is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for its purported digestive and calming properties.
