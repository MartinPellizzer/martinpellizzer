Miconia vallartensis

Miconia vallartensis, commonly known as the white miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are bright red berries that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy pulp.

Miconia vallartensis is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
