Monarda fistulosa f. albiflora

Monarda fistulosa f. albiflora, commonly known as Whiteflower Wild Bergamot, is a variant of the common wild bergamot plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale lavender flower heads, which contrast with its green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
