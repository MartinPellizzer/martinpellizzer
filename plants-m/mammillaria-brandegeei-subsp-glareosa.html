Mammillaria brandegeei subsp. glareosa

Mammillaria brandegeei subsp.

glareosa, commonly known as the Glareosa mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to the rocky soils of southern Mexico. It is characterized by its small, globular shape and dense covering of white, woolly spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus collections.
