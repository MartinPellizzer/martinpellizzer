Myrcia coroicensis

Myrcia coroicensis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical environments and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded conditions. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Myrcia coroicensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
