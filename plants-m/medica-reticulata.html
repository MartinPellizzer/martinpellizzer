Medica reticulata

Medica reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined medica, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-bearing fruit and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern, contributing to its ornamental value. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, often in shades of white or pale yellow.

The species is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
