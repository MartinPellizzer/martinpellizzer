Mucuna urentissima

Mucuna urentissima, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a tropical leguminous plant characterized by its hairy, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves.

It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain dark brown, kidney-shaped seeds. The plant is native to regions of South and Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable crop in agriculture.

Additionally, the seeds contain significant amounts of protein and are used in various traditional and commercial applications.
