Millettia cinerea

Millettia cinerea, commonly known as the ash-leaf senna, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and often have a silvery or grayish underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and it is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, and it plays a role in local ecosystems as both a food source and a habitat provider.

Millettia cinerea is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
