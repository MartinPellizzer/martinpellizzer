Miconia ecostata

Miconia ecostata, commonly known as the white miconia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that are typically borne on short pedicels. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains numerous small seeds.

Miconia ecostata is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
