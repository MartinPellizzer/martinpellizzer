Myrtus communis var. macrocarpa

Myrtus communis var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited myrtle, is a variant of the myrtle plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and showy white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces large, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
