Microcerasus humilis

Microcerasus humilis, commonly known as the dwarf cherry or Japanese dwarf cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 2 meters, with slender branches and oval-shaped leaves. Its fruit is a small, red drupe that ripens in late summer, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic flowers.

Microcerasus humilis prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and small landscapes.
