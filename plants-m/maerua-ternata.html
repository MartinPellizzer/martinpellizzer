Maerua ternata

Maerua ternata, commonly known as the white thorn or African whitethorn, is a shrub native to Africa, the Arabian Peninsula, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

Maerua ternata is often used in traditional medicine and for its hardy, resilient nature.
