Mimulus radicans

Mimulus radicans, commonly known as the trailing joyweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of orange, yellow, and red. The plant has creeping stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread along the ground. Its foliage consists of oval, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

Mimulus radicans thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and stream banks.
