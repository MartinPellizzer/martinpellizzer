Masdevallia simulatrix

Masdevallia simulatrix, commonly known as the mimic orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a compact, pseudobulbous growth habit and produces delicate, pendulous flowers that resemble other orchid species, hence its common name. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, showcasing intricate, often colorful flower structures.

Its botanical features include long, narrow leaves and a unique, often fragrant floral morphology that aids in pollination by specific insect species.
