Macrosiphonia brachysiphon

Macrosiphonia brachysiphon, commonly known as the narrow-siphon liverwort, is a small, thalloid liverwort belonging to the family Porellaceae.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a simple, leafy thallus with closely spaced, small leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. Its reproductive structures, known as gemmae, are produced in clusters on the upper surface of the thallus.

This species is often found in temperate regions and is an important component of moist, nutrient-rich habitats.
