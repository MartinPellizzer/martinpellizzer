Morina nepalensis

Morina nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal moringa, is a shrub native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Morina nepalensis is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
