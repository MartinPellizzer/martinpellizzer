Mentha sylvestris var. glabrata

Mentha sylvestris var. glabrata, commonly known as wild mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils rich in menthol, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

The variety glabrata is distinguished by its smooth, hairless leaves compared to other mint species.
