Meniscium molle

Meniscium molle, commonly known as the soft meniscus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in umbels. The plant produces oval, hairy fruits that are typically dispersed by wind. Native to regions of Europe and western Asia, it thrives in moist, calcareous soils.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive, fragrant aroma when crushed.
