Machilus forrestii

Machilus forrestii, commonly known as the Chinese-laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to China, particularly found in the provinces of Sichuan, Yunnan, and Guizhou. The plant is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue-black berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.
