Macrostylis hirta

Macrostylis hirta, commonly known as the hairy-styled grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers.

Macrostylis hirta is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
