Melothria breyeri

Melothria breyeri, commonly known as the dwarf passionfruit, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It produces small, yellow-orange fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor, similar to that of the common passionfruit. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications, including jams, beverages, and desserts.
