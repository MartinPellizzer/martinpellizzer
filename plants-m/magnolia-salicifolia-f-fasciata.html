Magnolia salicifolia f. fasciata

Magnolia salicifolia f. fasciata, commonly known as the variegated magnolia, is a hybrid plant exhibiting distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its broad, oval leaves that display a striking variegation of white and green, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring. Its growth habit is upright and shrub-like, with a moderate to fast growth rate.

Native to the southeastern United States, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.
