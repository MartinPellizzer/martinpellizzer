Melilotus melanospermus

Melilotus melanospermus, commonly known as blackseed melilot, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which attract pollinators such as bees. Its seeds are dark in color and contain essential oils with potential medicinal properties.

Melilotus melanospermus is often used in traditional medicine for its antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory effects.
