Mucuna andreana

Mucuna andreana, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, velvety pods that contain numerous seeds. The plant produces a large number of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are often fragrant. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are usually hairy.

Mucuna andreana is known for its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in agroforestry systems.
