Mentha afghanica

Mentha afghanica, commonly known as Afghan mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and lavender. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. Its leaves are elongated and serrated, with a strong, refreshing mint scent.

Mentha afghanica is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal remedies.
