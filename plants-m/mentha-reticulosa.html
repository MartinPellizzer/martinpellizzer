Mentha reticulosa

Mentha reticulosa, commonly known as the net-veined mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern. Its small, tubular flowers are typically purple or pink, arranged in whorled clusters.

Mentha reticulosa is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
