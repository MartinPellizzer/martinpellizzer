Morus morettiana

Morus morettiana, commonly known as the Morettian mulberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, aggregate fruits. The plant is typically deciduous, shedding its leaves in the autumn season. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Morus morettiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional medicine.
