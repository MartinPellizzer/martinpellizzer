Miconia melastomoides

Miconia melastomoides, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and prominent veins. Its fruit is a fleshy, edible capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Miconia melastomoides is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.
