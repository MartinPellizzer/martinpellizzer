Mimosa gomezii

Mimosa gomezii, commonly known as the velvet mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces fluffy, pale pink seed pods that resemble cotton balls, which are a distinctive feature. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Mimosa gomezii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
