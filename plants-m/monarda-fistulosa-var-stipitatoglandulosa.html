Monarda fistulosa var. stipitatoglandulosa

Monarda fistulosa var. stipitatoglandulosa, commonly known as the hairy beardtongue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and showy, tubular pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have glandular dots, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
