Myrcia cubensis

Myrcia cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba and parts of the Bahamas.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically orange-red in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Myrcia cubensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
