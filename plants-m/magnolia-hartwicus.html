Magnolia hartwicus

Magnolia hartwicus, commonly known as the Hartwigs magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its bark is smooth and gray, with a fragrant scent.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and historical significance.
