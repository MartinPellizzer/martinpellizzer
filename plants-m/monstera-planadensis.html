Monstera planadensis

Monstera planadensis, commonly known as the Swiss cheese plant, is a tropical climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that develop fenestrations, or holes, as they mature. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light. Its stems can grow up to several meters in length, often requiring support such as a trellis or moss pole.

Monstera planadensis is popular as a houseplant due to its striking foliage and air-purifying qualities.
