Microcos henrici subsp. henrici

Microcos henrici subsp.

henrici, commonly known as the Henry's microcos, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States. It features small, delicate yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and emits a mild, spicy scent. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a native plant supporting local pollinators and ecological diversity.
