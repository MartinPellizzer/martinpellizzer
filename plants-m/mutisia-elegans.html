Mutisia elegans

Mutisia elegans, commonly known as the elegant mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, yellow-orange ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Mutisia elegans thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and open woodlands.
