Myrcia skeldingii

Myrcia skeldingii, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces woody, elliptical fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it can become a problematic species in some regions.
