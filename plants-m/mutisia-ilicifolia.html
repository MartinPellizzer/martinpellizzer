Mutisia ilicifolia

Mutisia ilicifolia, commonly known as the silky mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, silken leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
