Mimosa bifurca var. bifurca

Mimosa bifurca var. bifurca, commonly known as the sensitive plant, is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched, a response triggered by rapid changes in turgor pressure within the leaf cells. The plant produces small, yellow, globular flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its sensitivity to touch and its rapid growth habit, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
