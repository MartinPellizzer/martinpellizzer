Myrtopsis macrocarpa

Myrtopsis macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and produces small, white flowers that are often clustered together. Its fruits are woody capsules that contain several seeds, and the plant is often grown for its ornamental value.

Myrtopsis macrocarpa thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.
