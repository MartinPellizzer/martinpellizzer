Myristica rugulosa

Myristica rugulosa, commonly known as the Java nut or black pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces distinctive red, fleshy fruits that contain a single, hard-shelled seed. Its seeds are valued for their essential oils and are used in traditional medicine and perfumery.

The tree thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
