Miconia laevigata

Miconia laevigata, commonly known as the silky miconia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences with small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains multiple seeds, aiding in its dispersal by birds and other animals.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
