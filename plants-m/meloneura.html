Meloneura

Meloneura, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night and close during the day. The plant belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is known for its fragrant blooms. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth texture.

Meloneura is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique nocturnal blooming habit.
