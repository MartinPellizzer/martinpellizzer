Malus prasomila augustiana

Malus prasomila augustiana, commonly known as the Augustiana apple tree, is a rare and ornamental species native to specific regions of China.

It is characterized by its small, ornamental flowers that bloom in early spring, often with a unique pinkish hue. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are not typically cultivated for commercial use but are appreciated for their flavor and ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation in temperate climates.

Due to its rarity, it is often grown in specialized botanical gardens and is valued for its horticultural and historical significance.
