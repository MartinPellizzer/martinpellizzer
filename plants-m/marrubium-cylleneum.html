Marrubium cylleneum

Marrubium cylleneum, commonly known as the Cyprus horehound, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture. Its flowers are typically blue or violet, arranged in whorls along the stem, and it blooms during the summer months.

Marrubium cylleneum is often found in rocky or dry habitats and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
