Myrcia kegeliana var. vulgaris

Myrcia kegeliana var. vulgaris, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
