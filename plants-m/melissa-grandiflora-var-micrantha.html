Melissa grandiflora var. micrantha

Melissa grandiflora var. micrantha, commonly known as lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features aromatic, serrated leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late spring to early summer. Its fragrance is reminiscent of lemon, hence its common name.

Melissa grandiflora var. micrantha thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun.
