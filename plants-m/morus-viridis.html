Morus viridis

Morus viridis, commonly known as the green mulberry, is a species of mulberry native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring heart-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are edible and often consumed by both humans and animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and as fodder for livestock, highlighting its ecological and economic importance.
