Musa zebrina

Musa zebrina, commonly known as the zebra plant, is a member of the Musaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that feature green and white stripes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Musa zebrina is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
