Malva verticillata var. rafiqii

Malva verticillata var. rafiqii, commonly known as the Rafiqii mallow, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves arranged in a whorl at the nodes of its stems. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
