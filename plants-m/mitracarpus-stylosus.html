Mitracarpus stylosus

Mitracarpus stylosus, commonly known as the silky senna, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.
