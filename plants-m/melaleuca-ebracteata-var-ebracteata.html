Melaleuca ebracteata var. ebracteata

Melaleuca ebracteata var. ebracteata, commonly known as the broad-leaved paperbark, is a species native to eastern Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant features smooth, flaky bark that peels in thin layers, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while the flowers are typically white or cream in color.

This plant is well adapted to coastal environments and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
