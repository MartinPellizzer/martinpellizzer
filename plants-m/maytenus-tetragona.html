Maytenus tetragona

Maytenus tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled mayten, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the humid forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical, dark green leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, four-angled fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in lowland to montane regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

Maytenus tetragona is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
