Masdevallia braasii

Masdevallia braasii, commonly known as the Braas's masdevallia, is a terrestrial orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It features intricate, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking, dark violet to purple flowers with a distinctive, narrow lip. The plant typically blooms during the cooler months, showcasing its adaptability to high-altitude environments. Its growth habit is epiphytic, meaning it attaches itself to trees without deriving nutrients from them.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
