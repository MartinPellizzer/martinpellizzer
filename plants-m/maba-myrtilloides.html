Maba myrtilloides

Maba myrtilloides, commonly known as the blueberry maba, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like structure, with hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically pink or white, and it produces small, blue or purple berries that are edible.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
