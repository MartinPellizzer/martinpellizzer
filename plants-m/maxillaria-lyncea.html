Maxillaria lyncea

Maxillaria lyncea, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from pink to white, often with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forest understories, where it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its leaves are usually dark green and elongated, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

Maxillaria lyncea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
