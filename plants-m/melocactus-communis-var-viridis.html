Melocactus communis var. viridis

Melocactus communis var. viridis, commonly known as the green melon cactus, is a distinctive member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically a vibrant green color, which helps it blend into its natural environment. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid climates.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and xeriscaping.
