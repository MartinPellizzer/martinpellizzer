Mappia oblonga

Mappia oblonga, commonly known as the broadleaf mappia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elongated, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green in color. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Mappia oblonga thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense forest undergrowth.
