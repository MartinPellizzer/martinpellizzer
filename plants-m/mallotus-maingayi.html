Mallotus maingayi

Mallotus maingayi, commonly known as the white-flowered mallot, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it valuable in pharmacological research.

Mallotus maingayi is also noted for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
