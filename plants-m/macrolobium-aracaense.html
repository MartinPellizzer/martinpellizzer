Macrolobium aracaense

Macrolobium aracaense, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically display vibrant colors such as yellow and orange. The plant has strap-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments. It requires well-draining soil and consistent moisture to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
