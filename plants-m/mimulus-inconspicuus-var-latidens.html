Mimulus inconspicuus var. latidens

Mimulus inconspicuus var. latidens, commonly known as the broadleaf yellow monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, spurred flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It is a perennial species that produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.
