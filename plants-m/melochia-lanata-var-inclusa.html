Melochia lanata var. inclusa

Melochia lanata var. inclusa, commonly known as the woolly melochia, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, white trichomes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by distinctive, spiny capsules containing seeds. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
