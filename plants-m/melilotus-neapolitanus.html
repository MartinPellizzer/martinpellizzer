Melilotus neapolitanus

Melilotus neapolitanus, commonly known as the Naples melilot, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Melilotus neapolitanus is also valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
