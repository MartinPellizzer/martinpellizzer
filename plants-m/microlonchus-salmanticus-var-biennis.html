Microlonchus salmanticus var. biennis

Microlonchus salmanticus var. biennis, commonly known as the two-yearly thistle, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It has a rosette growth habit and produces achenes as its seeds.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
