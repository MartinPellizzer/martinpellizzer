Momordica trilobata

Momordica trilobata, commonly known as the three-lobed bitter gourd, is a vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia and is characterized by its distinctive three-lobed leaves and elongated, warty fruits. The plant produces yellowish flowers and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its fruits are edible when mature but are typically bitter and used in various cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
