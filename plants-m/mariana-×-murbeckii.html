Mariana × murbeckii

Mariana × murbeckii, commonly known as the hybrid hibiscus, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Hibiscus marianus and Hibiscus murbeckii.

It is a shrub native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy environments and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, dark green leaves, and it produces fibrous, woody stems.

Mariana × murbeckii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological benefits.
