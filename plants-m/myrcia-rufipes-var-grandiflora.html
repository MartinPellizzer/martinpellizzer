Myrcia rufipes var. grandiflora

Myrcia rufipes var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered myrtle, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural gardens.
