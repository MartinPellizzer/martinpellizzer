Mespilus azarolus var. erythrocarpa

Mespilus azarolus var. erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It features oval, dark green leaves that turn reddish-brown in autumn. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, followed by unique, fleshy fruits that ripen in late autumn. The fruit has a distinctive texture, with a tough outer skin and a soft, edible pulp.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
