Moraea riparia

Moraea riparia, commonly known as the river moraea, is a striking perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows near rivers and streams, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a slight waxy coating, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Moraea riparia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
