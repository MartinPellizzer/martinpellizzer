Myrica macfarlanei

Myrica macfarlanei, commonly known as the Macfarlane's wax myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean, particularly Jamaica.

It belongs to the Myricaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, waxy leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically red or black when mature, which are an important food source for local wildlife.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or near freshwater sources.
