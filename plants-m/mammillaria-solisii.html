Mammillaria solisii

Mammillaria solisii, commonly known as the Solisii prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and bright green stems covered in small, white spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun exposure.

Mammillaria solisii is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental appeal.
