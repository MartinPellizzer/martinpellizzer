Machairophyllum stayneri

Machairophyllum stayneri, commonly known as the Stainere's thorn, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems and small, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological resilience, it is a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens.
