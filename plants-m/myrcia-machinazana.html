Myrcia machinazana

Myrcia machinazana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Myrcia machinazana is often found in humid tropical forests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
