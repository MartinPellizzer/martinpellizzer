Microchilus carchiensis

Microchilus carchiensis, commonly known as the Carchi mountain orchid, is a rare and endemic species found in the Andean highlands of Colombia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, terrestrial growth habit. The plant produces small, delicate flowers with intricate patterns, typically in shades of white and green. It thrives in cool, humid environments with well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
