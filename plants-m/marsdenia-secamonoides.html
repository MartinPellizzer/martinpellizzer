Marsdenia secamonoides

Marsdenia secamonoides, commonly known as the red-flowered Marsdenia, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a contrasting yellow or green throat. Its fruit is a follicle that contains several elongated seeds, and the plant is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forests or along stream banks.
