Mentha arvensis var. allionii

Mentha arvensis var. allionii, commonly known as wild mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic when crushed. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Mentha arvensis var. allionii is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
