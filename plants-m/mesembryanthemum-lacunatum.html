Mesembryanthemum lacunatum

Mesembryanthemum lacunatum, commonly known as the "Tuberous Mesembryanthemum," is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the warm seasons. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy coating, reducing water loss and enhancing its drought tolerance.

Mesembryanthemum lacunatum is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental appeal.
