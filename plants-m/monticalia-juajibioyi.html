Monticalia juajibioyi

Monticalia juajibioyi, commonly known as the Jujibioy orchid, is a rare and endemic species found in the cloud forests of Colombia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, showy floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its humid, shaded habitat. Its flowers are often pale green or white with intricate patterns, adapted for specific pollinators.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
