Mentha × rivalis

Mentha × rivalis, commonly known as catnip, is a hybrid plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its square, hollow stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, which appear in late summer. Mentha × rivalis is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been naturalized in many regions around the world.

It is known for its aromatic properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies and as a stimulant for cats.
