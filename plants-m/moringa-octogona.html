Moringa octogona

Moringa octogona, commonly known as the drumstick tree or horseradish tree, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its distinctive, drumstick-shaped pods and compound leaves with a fern-like appearance. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is known for its rapid growth rate. It produces small, yellow flowers that develop into the edible pods used in various cuisines.

The leaves, seeds, and pods are rich in nutrients, making it a valuable plant for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
