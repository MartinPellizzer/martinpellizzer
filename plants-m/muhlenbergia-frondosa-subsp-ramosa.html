Muhlenbergia frondosa subsp. ramosa

Muhlenbergia frondosa subsp.

ramosa, commonly known as the silver feather grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and fluffy, silver-white plumes that appear in late summer.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-gray hue that gives it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
