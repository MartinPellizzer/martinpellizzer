Memora heterophylla

Memora heterophylla, commonly known as the variable leaf memora, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful bracts that resemble petals, which are used to attract pollinators. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually alternate and may vary in shape, giving it the species name "heterophylla," meaning "different leaves.".

Memora heterophylla is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
