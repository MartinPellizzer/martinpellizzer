Mentha × verticillata var. juvaviana

Mentha × verticillata var. juvaviana, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant thrives in moist environments such as wet meadows, stream banks, and marshy areas. Its small, tubular flowers are typically blue or purple and bloom in late summer.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
