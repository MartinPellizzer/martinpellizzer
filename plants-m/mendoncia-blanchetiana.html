Mendoncia blanchetiana

Mendoncia blanchetiana, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
