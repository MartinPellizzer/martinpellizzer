Miconia vulcanidomatia

Miconia vulcanidomatia, commonly known as the Flameleaf Miconia, is a tropical shrub native to the volcanic highlands of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant red or orange, giving it its striking appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its fruit is a round, fleshy berry that turns from green to yellow as it ripens.

Miconia vulcanidomatia is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils, making it a common invasive species in disturbed habitats.
