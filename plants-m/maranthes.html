Maranthes

Maranthes, commonly known as the spider lily, is a genus of flowering plants in the Amaryllis family.

It consists of several species, most of which are native to South Africa. The plants are characterized by their striking, often colorful flowers that resemble spiders, hence their common name. They typically have strap-like leaves and produce showy blooms in the summer.

Maranthes species are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens and as houseplants.
