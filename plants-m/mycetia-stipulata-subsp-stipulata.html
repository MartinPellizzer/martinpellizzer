Mycetia stipulata subsp. stipulata

Mycetia stipulata subsp.

stipulata, commonly known as the Indian flame of the woods, is a shrub native to the Indian subcontinent. It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests and is adapted to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are simple and opposite, with stipules that are persistent and leaf-like.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
