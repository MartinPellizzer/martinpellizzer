Mimosa cordistipula subsp. multiglandulosa

Mimosa cordistipula subsp.

multiglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular mimosa, is a shrub native to South America. It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are divided into small, feather-like segments.

The plant produces small, yellow, globular flower heads that appear in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its glandular structures on the leaves and stems contribute to its unique botanical features.
