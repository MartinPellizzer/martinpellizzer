Melica altissima var. interrupta

Melica altissima var. interrupta, commonly known as the interrupted brome, is a grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-ranked spikelets.

It is often found in temperate climates and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
