Masdevallia corderoana

Masdevallia corderoana, commonly known as the Cordero's masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, often colorful flower spikes that bloom in spring. The plant typically features dark green, strap-like leaves and produces pendulous flowers with intricate patterns. It thrives in cool, humid environments with high light exposure but requires careful watering to avoid root rot.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts in controlled conditions.
