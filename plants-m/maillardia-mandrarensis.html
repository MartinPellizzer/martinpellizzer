Maillardia mandrarensis

Maillardia mandrarensis, commonly known as the Madagascar lily, is a striking flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves and produces a tall, slender stem that supports its vibrant blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, sunny environment to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
