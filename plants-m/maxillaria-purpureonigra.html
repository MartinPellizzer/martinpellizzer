Maxillaria purpureonigra

Maxillaria purpureonigra, commonly known as the purple-black maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark violet to almost black-colored flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns of purple and white. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, providing structural support and aiding in water retention.

The orchid's unique coloration and morphology make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts and botanists alike.
