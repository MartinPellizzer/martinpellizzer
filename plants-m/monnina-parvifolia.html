Monnina parvifolia

Monnina parvifolia, commonly known as smallflower bluestem, is a herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Monnina parvifolia is a member of the Polygalaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
