Myristica hooglandii

Myristica hooglandii, commonly known as the Hoogland nutmeg, is a species of tree native to the Moluccas and parts of Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is closely related to the nutmeg tree, Myristica fragrans. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and oval-shaped fruits that contain a single seed. Its botanical significance lies in its aromatic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The species is also valued for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and unique appearance.
