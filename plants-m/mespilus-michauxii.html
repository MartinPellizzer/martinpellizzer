Mespilus michauxii

Mespilus michauxii, commonly known as the Michaux's medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces edible berries that ripen in the autumn, though they are often used in traditional medicine and culinary practices.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas with moderate climates.
