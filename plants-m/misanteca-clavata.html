Misanteca clavata

Misanteca clavata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in clusters, giving it its common name.

Misanteca clavata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
