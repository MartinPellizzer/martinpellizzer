Myrcia kauseliana

Myrcia kauseliana, commonly known as the Kausel's myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is often found in humid, tropical regions. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. It produces berries that are typically red or yellow when mature, which are edible but not widely consumed by humans.

Myrcia kauseliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
