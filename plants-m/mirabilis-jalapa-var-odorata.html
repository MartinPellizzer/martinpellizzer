Mirabilis jalapa var. odorata

Mirabilis jalapa var. odorata, commonly known as the four-o'clock flower, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the late afternoon and close at dawn. The plant produces vibrant colors, including pink, red, and white, and has a distinctive fragrance that intensifies as the day progresses. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming pattern.
