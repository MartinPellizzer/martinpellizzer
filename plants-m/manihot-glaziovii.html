Manihot glaziovii

Manihot glaziovii, commonly known as the Glaziov's manioc, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and alternate, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in cyathia, a typical feature of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its roots are used in traditional medicine and have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.
