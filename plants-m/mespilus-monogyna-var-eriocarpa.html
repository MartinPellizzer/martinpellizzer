Mespilus monogyna var. eriocarpa

Mespilus monogyna var. eriocarpa, commonly known as the European whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible, yellowish fruits resembling small cherries. The leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn a striking orange-red in autumn. The plant is valued for its ecological role, providing food for various wildlife species.

It is also noted for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.
