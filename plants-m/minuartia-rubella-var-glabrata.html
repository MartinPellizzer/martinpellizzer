Minuartia rubella var. glabrata

Minuartia rubella var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth rockcress, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy, green leaves that are typically hairless, as indicated by the varietal name "glabrata.". The flowers are usually white or pale pink, borne in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold climates.

This variety is often found in mountainous areas and is valued for its resilience and ornamental appeal.
