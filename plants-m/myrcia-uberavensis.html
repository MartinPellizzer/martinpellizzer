Myrcia uberavensis

Myrcia uberavensis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. Its fruits are small, berry-like structures that contain seeds, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine.

Myrcia uberavensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting local biodiversity.
