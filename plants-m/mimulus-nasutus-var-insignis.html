Mimulus nasutus var. insignis

Mimulus nasutus var. insignis, commonly known as the yellow monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces elongated seed pods that split open to release tiny seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
