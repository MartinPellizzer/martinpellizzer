Madhuca punctata

Madhuca punctata, commonly known as the punctate madhuca, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark-barked trunk and dense canopy. The leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a distinctive punctuated texture on the underside. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, starchy fruits that are important in local cuisines.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in medicine and crafts.
