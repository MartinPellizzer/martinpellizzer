Muscari comosum f. monstrosum

Muscari comosum f. monstrosum, commonly known as the giant grape hyacinth, is a striking variation of the grape hyacinth species.

It is characterized by its large, clustered inflorescence that produces an abundance of small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that emerge in early spring.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant, fragrant blooms.
