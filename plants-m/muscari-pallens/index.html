Muscari Pallens

Common bluebells, scientifically known as Muscari pallens, are a striking early spring flower that thrives in woodland settings. These plants are characterized by their clusters of bell-shaped, sky-blue blooms that appear before the leaves fully unfurl. Native to Europe, they are often found in shaded, moist areas where they form dense carpets of color. Muscari pallens is a bulbous plant that spreads through offsets, making it a popular choice for naturalized gardens. Its delicate appearance and seasonal display make it a cherished addition to the spring flora.
