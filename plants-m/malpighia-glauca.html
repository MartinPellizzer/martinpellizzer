Malpighia glauca

Malpighia glauca, commonly known as the Brazilian wolfberry or granadilla, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It produces small, bright red berries that are rich in vitamin C and have a tart, tangy flavor. The plant has simple, alternate leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

The fruit is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its nutritional value and unique taste.
