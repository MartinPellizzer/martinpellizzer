Mammillaria rhodantha f. tentaculata

Mammillaria rhodantha f. tentaculata, commonly known as the Tentacle Mammillaria, is a unique cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white to pale pink tubercles. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring. It has a compact growth habit and is prized for its ornamental value in succulent gardens.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments.
