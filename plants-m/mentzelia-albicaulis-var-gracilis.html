Mentzelia albicaulis var. gracilis

Mentzelia albicaulis var. gracilis, commonly known as silverleaf blazingstar, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and tall, branched stems that bear clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.
