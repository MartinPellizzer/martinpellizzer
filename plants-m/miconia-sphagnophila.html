Miconia sphagnophila

Miconia sphagnophila, commonly known as the swamp miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in wet, swampy areas and has lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins. Its roots are fibrous and often form dense mats in the soil, contributing to the ecosystem's structure.

Miconia sphagnophila plays a role in supporting local biodiversity by providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
