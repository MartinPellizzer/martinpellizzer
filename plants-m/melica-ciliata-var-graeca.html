Melica ciliata var. graeca

Melica ciliata var. graeca, commonly known as Greek slender grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as slopes and disturbed areas. The plant has slender, upright culms with narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a loose to dense panicle, bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
