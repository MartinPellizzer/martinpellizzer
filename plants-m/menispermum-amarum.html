Menispermum amarum

Menispermum amarum, commonly known as American arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by spiny, woody fruits containing seeds. Its roots are historically used in traditional medicine, though they are toxic and should not be consumed.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting various wildlife species.
