Malaxis grandifolia

Malaxis grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered malaxis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and Georgia. The plant features a slender, often branched stem and produces showy, greenish-white to yellowish flowers with a distinct lip. Its leaves are usually linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

The species is notable for its unique floral structure and is often found in wetlands and pine flatwoods.
