Machaerium viridipetalum

Machaerium viridipetalum, commonly known as the green-petaled machaerium, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family, characterized by its compound leaves and flowering structures. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are often clustered at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets, and are typically alternate along the stem.

Machaerium viridipetalum is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological roles.
