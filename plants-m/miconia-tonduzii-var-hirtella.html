Miconia tonduzii var. hirtella

Miconia tonduzii var. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy miconia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically clustered in inflorescences and are often yellow or white in color.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.
