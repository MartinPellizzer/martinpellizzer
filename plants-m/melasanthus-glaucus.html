Melasanthus glaucus

Melasanthus glaucus, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Melasanthus glaucus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
