Medicago macrocarpa

Medicago macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-seeded clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features trifoliate leaves with stipules and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is characterized by its robust root system and the presence of symbiotic nitrogen-fixing bacteria in its root nodules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Medicago macrocarpa is valued for its forage quality and historical use in agriculture.
