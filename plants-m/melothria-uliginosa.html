Melothria uliginosa

Melothria uliginosa, commonly known as the swamp blackberry, is a climbing vine native to wetland habitats in the Americas.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple or black in color. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshes and along riverbanks. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges, and it has tendrils that help it climb and spread.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cuisines.
