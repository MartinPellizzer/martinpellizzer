Momordica quinquefida

Momordica quinquefida, commonly known as the five-lobed bitter gourd, is a vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its distinctive, five-lobed leaves and spiny stems. The plant produces pale yellow flowers and elongated, warty fruits that are typically harvested when immature. It is valued for its edible fruits, which have a bitter taste and are used in various cuisines.

The plant is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
