Microstylis acuminata

Microstylis acuminata, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in eastern North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, forming dense stands that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms. The inflorescence consists of a single, narrow spike of tiny, spikelet-like flowers, which are wind-pollinated.
