Maxillariella diuturna

Maxillariella diuturna, commonly known as the long-lasting orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically blooms during the summer months, and its flowers have a distinctive, intricate structure with narrow petals and a spurred lip. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Maxillariella diuturna is known for its longevity and ease of care, contributing to its appeal among orchid enthusiasts.
