Mentha × dumetorum var. nepetoides

Mentha × dumetorum var. nepetoides, commonly known as purple mint, is a hybrid plant species within the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its distinctive violet or purple flowers, which bloom in whorls along the stem. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually rounded and slightly hairy, with a strong, aromatic scent.

This variety is often used in herbal remedies and as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and medicinal properties.
