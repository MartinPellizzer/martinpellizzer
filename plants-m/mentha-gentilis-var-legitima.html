Mentha gentilis var. legitima

Mentha gentilis var. legitima, commonly known as the true peppermint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, opposite leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems, which are usually borne in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Mentha gentilis var. legitima is widely cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy, medicine, and as a flavoring agent.
