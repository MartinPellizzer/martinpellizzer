Musa glauca

Musa glauca, commonly known as the white banana, is a flowering plant in the Musaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and clusters of white, fleshy fruit. The plant produces elongated, pale yellow to white berries that are typically harvested when mature. Musa glauca is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance.

It prefers warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to thrive.
