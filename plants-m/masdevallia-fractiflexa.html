Masdevallia fractiflexa

Masdevallia fractiflexa, commonly known as the broken flexed masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually dark green and sword-shaped, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts and botanists.
