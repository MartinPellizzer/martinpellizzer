Minuartiella dianthifolia subsp. kurdica

Minuartiella dianthifolia subsp.

kurdica, commonly known as the Kurdish rock-cress, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically features pale green to blue-gray foliage, with leaves that are linear to lanceolate in shape. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it contributes to the ecological diversity of these regions.
