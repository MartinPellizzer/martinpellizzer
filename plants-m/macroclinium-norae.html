Macroclinium norae

Macroclinium norae, commonly known as the purple-leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet or purple foliage and delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
