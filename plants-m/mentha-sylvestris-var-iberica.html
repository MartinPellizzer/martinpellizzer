Mentha sylvestris var. iberica

Mentha sylvestris var. iberica, commonly known as the Iberian wild mint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves with a strong, aromatic scent. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or pink, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other mint species.
