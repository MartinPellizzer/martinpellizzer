Macroditassa adnata

Macroditassa adnata, commonly known as the slender bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for use in landscaping and as a ornamental grass.
