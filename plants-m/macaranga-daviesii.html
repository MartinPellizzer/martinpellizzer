Macaranga daviesii

Macaranga daviesii, commonly known as the white macaranga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often hairy leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is notable for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
