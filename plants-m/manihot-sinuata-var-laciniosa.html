Manihot sinuata var. laciniosa

Manihot sinuata var. laciniosa, commonly known as the laceleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its finely lobed, almost lace-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers followed by yellowish fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its starchy roots, which are used in cooking.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its striking foliage.
