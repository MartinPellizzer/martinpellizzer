Melastoma vitiense

Melastoma vitiense, commonly known as the Vitiense Melastoma, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, often pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with opposite, ovate leaves that have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are clustered in inflorescences and are pollinated primarily by insects.

Melastoma vitiense is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
