Maxillaria piestopus

Maxillaria piestopus, commonly known as the Piestopus orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant produces pendulous, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its pseudobulbs are cylindrical and support the leaves, which are strap-like and arranged in a rosette.

The flowers bloom in clusters and are often used in horticultural displays due to their ornamental value.
