Melanochyla caesia

Melanochyla caesia, commonly known as the bluebell of the south, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and forest margins, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Melanochyla caesia is valued for its beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
