Maxillaria acostae

Maxillaria acostae, commonly known as the Acosta's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant typically produces erect, hairy pseudobulbs that bear alternate, lanceolate leaves. Its flowers are usually small, fragrant, and come in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with a contrasting lip.

Maxillaria acostae is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
