Megaloprotachne glabrescens

Megaloprotachne glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth meadowgrass, is a grass species native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, grassy areas and is adapted to temperate climates. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is a defining feature of the grass family.

Megaloprotachne glabrescens is often used in ecological studies due to its role in soil stabilization and habitat support.
