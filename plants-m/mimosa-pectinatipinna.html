Mimosa pectinatipinna

Mimosa pectinatipinna, commonly known as the sensitive plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots contain nitrogen-fixing bacteria, which help enrich the soil.

The plant is often grown as an ornamental due to its unique leaf movements and delicate appearance.
