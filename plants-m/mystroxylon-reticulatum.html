Mystroxylon reticulatum

Mystroxylon reticulatum, commonly known as the reticulate tamarind, is a member of the Fabaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant produces a hard, woody fruit that contains several seeds. Its bark is often deeply fissured, and the leaves are alternate with a distinctive reticulate venation pattern.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is valued for its timber and medicinal properties.
