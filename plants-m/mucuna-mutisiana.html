Mucuna mutisiana

Mucuna mutisiana, commonly known as the purple cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple pods and trifoliate leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value. Mucuna mutisiana is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural systems.

Its seeds contain compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal properties.
