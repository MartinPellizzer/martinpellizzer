Monophyllaea glandulosa

Monophyllaea glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular monophyllaea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which are often glandular, contributing to its scientific name. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
