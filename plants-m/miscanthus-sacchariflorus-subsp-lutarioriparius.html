Miscanthus sacchariflorus subsp. lutarioriparius

Miscanthus sacchariflorus subsp.

lutarioriparius, commonly known as the riparian miscanthus, is a perennial grass native to wetland and riparian habitats in eastern Europe. It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and feathery, silver-green plumes that bloom in late summer.

The plant thrives in moist, clay-rich soils and is often found along riverbanks and lake shores. It has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential use in ecological restoration projects.
