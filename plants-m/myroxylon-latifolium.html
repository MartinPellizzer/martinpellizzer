Myroxylon latifolium

Myroxylon latifolium, commonly known as the Balata tree, is a tropical species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and produces a unique latex sap that has been historically used for various industrial purposes. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and has a dense canopy that provides habitat for numerous wildlife species. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it thrives in humid, lowland areas with well-drained soil.

The plant is also valued for its wood, which is durable and used in construction and furniture-making.
