Melinis nigricans

Melinis nigricans, commonly known as black millet, is a warm-season grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce tiny, dark-colored grains. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions. Its rapid growth and drought tolerance make it a useful plant for erosion control and livestock forage.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
