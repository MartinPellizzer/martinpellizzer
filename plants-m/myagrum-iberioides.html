Myagrum iberioides

Myagrum iberioides, commonly known as the broad-leafed myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to northern Australia.

It features glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and have a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are often clustered together, followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Myagrum iberioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
