Mistyllus setifer

Mistyllus setifer, commonly known as the feathered mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It possesses small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually green and arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are typically red or yellow and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.
