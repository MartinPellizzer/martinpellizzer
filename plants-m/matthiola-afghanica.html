Matthiola afghanica

Matthiola afghanica, commonly known as Afghan wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with characteristic four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually bright yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Matthiola afghanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
