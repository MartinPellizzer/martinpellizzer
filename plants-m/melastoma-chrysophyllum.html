Melastoma chrysophyllum

Melastoma chrysophyllum, commonly known as the golden flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often glossy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological significance.
