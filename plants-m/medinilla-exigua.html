Medinilla exigua

Medinilla exigua, commonly known as the dwarf velvet tangerine, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Medinilla exigua is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
