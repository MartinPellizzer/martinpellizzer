Myrceugenia alpigena var. longifolia

Myrceugenia alpigena var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved myrtle, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are clustered in inflorescences at the ends of the branches.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and floral display.
