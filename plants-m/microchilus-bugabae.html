Microchilus bugabae

Microchilus bugabae, commonly known as the African violet orchid, is a species of orchid native to Africa.

It is a small, terrestrial plant with a fibrous root system and a compact growth habit. The plant produces delicate, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a distinctive lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by hobbyists.
