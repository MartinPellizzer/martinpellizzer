Melocactus hexacanthus

Melocactus hexacanthus, commonly known as the six-spined melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its globular shape and a crown of spines that often appear in clusters. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the late spring or early summer. Its thick, fleshy stem stores water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

The plant is also known for its slow growth and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
