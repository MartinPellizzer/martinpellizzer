Mentha × nemorosa var. incanotomentosa

Mentha × nemorosa var. incanotomentosa, commonly known as the silver-haired marsh mint, is a hybrid perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves that are covered in a dense, silvery-gray pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in loose spikes, and it produces small, oval fruits.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in wet conditions.
