Melanthesa anceps

Melanthesa anceps, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Melanthesa anceps is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
