Meizotropis pellita

Meizotropis pellita, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The tree has a slender, branching trunk and produces small, round fruits that split open to release seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its vibrant floral display.
