Multidentia concrescens

Multidentia concrescens, commonly known as the clustered-toothed plant, is a species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, featuring leaves with multiple teeth or lobes along the edges. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
