Myristica affinis

Myristica affinis, commonly known as the nutmeg relative, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and distinctive fruit. The plant produces a small, reddish-brown drupe that contains the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its botanical features include smooth, dark green leaves and a branched, woody structure.

The species is valued for its essential oils and has been studied for its potential pharmacological properties.
