Monocymbium

Monocymbium, commonly known as the single-celled symbiont, is a unique type of unicellular organism that forms symbiotic relationships with various host plants.

It is primarily found in the root systems of certain grasses and is involved in nutrient exchange with its host. Unlike typical fungi, monocymbium lacks the complex structures of mycorrhizal networks and instead exists as individual cells within the plant tissue. Its role in enhancing nutrient uptake, particularly nitrogen, is significant for the plant's growth and development.

Despite its simplicity, monocymbium plays a vital ecological role in plant-fungus interactions.
