Myrcia rugosa

Myrcia rugosa, commonly known as the rough myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine, raised dots, giving it a rough texture. Its fragrant flowers are usually white or pink and are borne in clusters.

Myrcia rugosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
