Malus sativa

Malus sativa, commonly known as apple tree, is a deciduous tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are rich in nutrients and widely cultivated for both commercial and ornamental purposes. Its fruit development involves a complex process of pollination, fertilization, and ripening.

The species exhibits a wide range of genetic diversity, leading to numerous cultivars with varying fruit sizes, colors, and flavors.
