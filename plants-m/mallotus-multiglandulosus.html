Mallotus multiglandulosus

Mallotus multiglandulosus, commonly known as the hairy mallot, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often hairy and have multiple glands, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

Mallotus multiglandulosus is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.
