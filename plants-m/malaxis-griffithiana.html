Malaxis griffithiana

Malaxis griffithiana, commonly known as the Griffith's lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green leaves and long, slender stems that bear clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are arranged in a loose, spiraling pattern, typical of the Orchidaceae family.

Malaxis griffithiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
