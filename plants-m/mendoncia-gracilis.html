Mendoncia gracilis

Mendoncia gracilis, commonly known as the slender mendoncia, is a herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. The plant features slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. Its flowers are small, clustered in loose inflorescences, and range in color from white to pale pink.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
