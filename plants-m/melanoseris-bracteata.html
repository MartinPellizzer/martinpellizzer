Melanoseris bracteata

Melanoseris bracteata, commonly known as the bracted melanoseris, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Melanoseridaceae and is characterized by its distinctive bracteate inflorescences. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on plant morphology and ecological adaptation.
