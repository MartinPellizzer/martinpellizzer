Mallotus barbatus var. croizatianus

Mallotus barbatus var. croizatianus, commonly known as the Croizat's hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow and red, which bloom during the rainy season. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to humid, tropical environments.
