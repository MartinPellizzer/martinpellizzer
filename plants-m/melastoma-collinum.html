Melastoma collinum

Melastoma collinum, commonly known as the hill melastoma, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in the spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Melastoma collinum is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
