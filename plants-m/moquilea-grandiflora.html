Moquilea grandiflora

Moquilea grandiflora, commonly known as the giant moquilea, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter. The plant typically grows in rocky, coastal areas and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are broad and succulent, adapted to store water in arid environments.

Moquilea grandiflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
