Medicago sativa var. intermedia

Medicago sativa var. intermedia, commonly known as intermediate alfalfa, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers, which bloom in early summer. This variety is valued for its high protein content and is often used as forage for livestock. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and improves soil fertility through nitrogen fixation.

Its adaptability to various climates makes it a versatile plant in agricultural settings.
