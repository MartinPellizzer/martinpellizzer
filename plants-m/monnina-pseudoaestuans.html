Monnina pseudoaestuans

Monnina pseudoaestuans, commonly known as the coastal bluemink, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine, silky hairs.

Monnina pseudoaestuans thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to saline and coastal environments.
