Methorium integrifolium

Methorium integrifolium, commonly known as the silverleaf evergreen, is a perennial plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its foliage remains evergreen throughout the year, providing visual interest in landscapes.
