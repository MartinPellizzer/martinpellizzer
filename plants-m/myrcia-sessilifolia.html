Myrcia sessilifolia

Myrcia sessilifolia, commonly known as Brazilian pepper tree or *Schinus terebinthifolius*, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Anacardiaceae family, which includes cashew and poison ivy. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. Myrcia sessilifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in various climates.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, posing a threat to native ecosystems.
