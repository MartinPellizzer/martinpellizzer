Medicago sativa f. alba

Medicago sativa f. alba, commonly known as white clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its white blossoms and trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. This plant is widely used as a forage crop due to its high nutritional value for livestock. It also plays an important role in soil enrichment through nitrogen fixation.

Medicago sativa f. alba thrives in temperate climates and is often found in lawns, pastures, and disturbed soils.
