Marica fugax

Marica fugax, commonly known as the transient marica, is a rare and elusive plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that bloom briefly during the cooler months. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a waxy surface that aids in water retention.

Due to its limited distribution and short flowering period, Marica fugax is considered a challenging subject for botanical study.
