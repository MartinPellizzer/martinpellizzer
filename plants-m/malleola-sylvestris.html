Malleola sylvestris

Malleola sylvestris, commonly known as the forest malleola, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions.

It features upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pale pink during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Malleola sylvestris is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
