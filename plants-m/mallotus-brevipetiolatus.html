Mallotus brevipetiolatus

Mallotus brevipetiolatus, commonly known as the short-stemmed mallotus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves with a short petiole, and it produces small, clustered yellowish-green flowers. The plant bears woody capsules as fruits, which contain numerous small, flat seeds. It is often found in lowland forests and thrives in humid, warm climates.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticultural and ecological studies.
