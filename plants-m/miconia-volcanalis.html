Miconia volcanalis

Miconia volcanalis, commonly known as the volcanic miconia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in lowland rainforests. Miconia volcanalis is also known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, which can impact local ecosystems.
