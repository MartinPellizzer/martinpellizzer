Machaerium paraguariense

Machaerium paraguariense, commonly known as the Paraguay signalgrass, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed areas, showing adaptability to various soil types. Its seed pods are cylindrical and dehiscent, releasing seeds when mature.

Machaerium paraguariense is also noted for its potential in ecological restoration due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
