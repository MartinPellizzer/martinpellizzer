Miconia foliosa

Miconia foliosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Melastomataceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy, white to pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often accompanied by bright red bracts. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of moist, shaded environments.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in some areas due to its aggressive spread and ecological impact.
