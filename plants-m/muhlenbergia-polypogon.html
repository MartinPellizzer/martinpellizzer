Muhlenbergia polypogon

Muhlenbergia polypogon, commonly known as the narrowleaf muhlenbergia, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands and grassy areas, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in panicles, which are often tinged with purple or pink.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
