Morphixia

Morphixia, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Morphixia typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
