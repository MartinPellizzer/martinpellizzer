Monstera pertusa var. jacquinii

Monstera pertusa var. jacquinii, commonly known as the Split-leaf Philodendron, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that develop deep splits as they mature. The plant has a climbing habit and produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by orange-red berries. Its foliage is prized for its ornamental value in indoor gardening.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.
