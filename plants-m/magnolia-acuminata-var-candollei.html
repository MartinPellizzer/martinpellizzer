Magnolia acuminata var. candollei

Magnolia acuminata var. candollei, commonly known as the Candolle's magnolia, is a rare and distinctive cultivar of the magnolia genus.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. This variety is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.

Botanically, it exhibits features typical of the Magnoliaceae family, including woody stems and separate floral parts.
