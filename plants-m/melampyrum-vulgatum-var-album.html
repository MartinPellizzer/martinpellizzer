Melampyrum vulgatum var. album

Melampyrum vulgatum var. album, commonly known as white touchgrass, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, showy flowers. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

The seeds are enclosed in pods and dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread across various regions.
