Mallotus ustulatus

Mallotus ustulatus, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a striking visual display. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The botanical features of Mallotus ustulatus make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in tropical regions.
