Millettia foxworthyi

Millettia foxworthyi, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types. It produces elongated pods containing flat, bean-like seeds.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
