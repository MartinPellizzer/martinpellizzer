Myrtus angustifolia

Myrtus angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, dark blue to purple berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are aromatic and slightly hairy. It produces clusters of white flowers in the spring, which are followed by its characteristic berries.

Myrtus angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
