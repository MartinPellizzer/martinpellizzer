Maesa indica

Maesa indica, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy dark green leaves and small, yellow-orange berries that are rich in vitamin C. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils. Its fruit is widely used in traditional medicine and as a culinary ingredient due to its high nutritional value.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
