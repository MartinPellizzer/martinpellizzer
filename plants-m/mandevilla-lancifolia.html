Mandevilla lancifolia

Mandevilla lancifolia, commonly known as the lanceleaf mandevilla, is a tropical vine native to South America.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil and full sun exposure.

Mandevilla lancifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and vigorous growth habit.
