Myrtoleucodendron linariifolium

Myrtoleucodendron linariifolium, commonly known as the silverleaf myrtle, is a shrub native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas of its native habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
