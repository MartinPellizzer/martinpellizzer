Mammillaria sericata

Mammillaria sericata, commonly known as the silver mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense covering of fine, white hairs that give it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
