Munnozia peruensis

Munnozia peruensis, commonly known as the Peruvian munnozia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its vibrant pink or red flowers bloom during the dry season, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Munnozia peruensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
