Medinilla cephalantha

Medinilla cephalantha, commonly known as the velvet tangerine.

This evergreen shrub is native to the Philippines and belongs to the Myrtaceae family. It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, round, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.
