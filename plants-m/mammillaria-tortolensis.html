Mammillaria tortolensis

Mammillaria tortolensis, commonly known as the Tortolense Hedgehog Cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines and small, rounded tubercles. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, emerging from the areoles on the stems.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens due to its compact size and unique appearance.
