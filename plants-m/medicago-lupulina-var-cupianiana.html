Medicago lupulina var. cupianiana

Medicago lupulina var. cupianiana, commonly known as the cupianica goatweed, is a variant of the goatweed species native to certain regions of China.

It is a low-growing annual plant characterized by its slender stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

The botanical features of this variant make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
