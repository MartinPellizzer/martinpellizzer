Masdevallia tovarensis

Masdevallia tovarensis, commonly known as the Tovarensis orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often colorful floral structures. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree bark without deriving nutrients from its host. Its leaves are usually narrow and strap-like, adapted to the humid, shaded conditions of its natural habitat.

Masdevallia tovarensis is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
