Mormodes saccata

Mormodes saccata, commonly known as the sac-like orchid, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its unique, sac-like structure on the stem, which is a distinctive botanical feature. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with a complex arrangement of petals and sepals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typical of its native habitat.

Its morphology reflects adaptations to its specific ecological niche in the island's diverse flora.
