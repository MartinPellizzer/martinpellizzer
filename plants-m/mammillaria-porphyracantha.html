Mammillaria porphyracantha

Mammillaria porphyracantha, commonly known as the red-spined mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered with white, woolly spines and bright red or orange spines that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by small, spherical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice for collectors and enthusiasts of succulents.
