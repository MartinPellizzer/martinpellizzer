Monardella sinuata

Monardella sinuata, commonly known as narrowleaf monardella, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
