Mecodium imbricatum

Mecodium imbricatum, commonly known as the rock cress, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats on rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are usually basal, arranged in a rosette, and are covered in fine hairs.

Mecodium imbricatum is often found in arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
