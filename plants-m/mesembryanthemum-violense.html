Mesembryanthemum violense

Mesembryanthemum violense, commonly known as the violet ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its ability to store water in its leaves. Its flowers typically bloom in the morning and close by midday, a trait known as nyctinasty.

Mesembryanthemum violense is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
