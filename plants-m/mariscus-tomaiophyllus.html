Mariscus tomaiophyllus

Mariscus tomaiophyllus, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rocky or alpine gardens. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
