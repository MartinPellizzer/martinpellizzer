Myristica sagotiana

Myristica sagotiana, commonly known as the Malayan nutmeg tree, is a member of the Myristicaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by bright red, fleshy berries containing the aromatic seeds used for nutmeg and mace. Its botanical significance lies in its unique chemical composition, which includes essential oils and various volatile compounds.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in tropical forests.
