Miscanthus littoralis

Miscanthus littoralis, commonly known as salt meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to coastal regions of Europe and Asia.

It thrives in saline and brackish wetlands, often found along shores and estuaries. The plant has tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, fluffy flowers that appear in late summer.

Miscanthus littoralis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
