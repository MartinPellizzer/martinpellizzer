Moricandia divaricata

Moricandia divaricata, commonly known as the variable moricandia, is a flowering plant native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pale yellow to greenish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous areas. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant adaptations.

Moricandia divaricata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and easy cultivation requirements.
