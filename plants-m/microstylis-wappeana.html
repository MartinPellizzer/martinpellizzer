Microstylis wappeana

Microstylis wappeana, commonly known as the Florida microstylis, is a rare and endemic grass species native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Microstylis wappeana thrives in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it requires consistently moist soil.

Due to habitat loss and fragmentation, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts to protect its natural environment.
