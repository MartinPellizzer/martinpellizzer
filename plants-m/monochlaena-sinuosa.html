Monochlaena sinuosa

Monochlaena sinuosa, commonly known as the zigzag mallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a desirable addition to horticultural collections.
