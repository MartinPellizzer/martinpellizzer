Madhuca penangiana

Madhuca penangiana, commonly known as the Penang mahua, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, starchy fruits.

It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
