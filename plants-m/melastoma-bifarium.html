Melastoma bifarium

Melastoma bifarium, commonly known as the two-flowered melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are often woody and branched. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.
