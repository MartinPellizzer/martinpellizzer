Melothria gilletii var. hitella

Melothria gilletii var. hitella, commonly known as the dwarf passionfruit, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color, with a tart and aromatic flavor. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its botanical characteristics include hairy stems and a distinctive fruit structure with a thick, leathery rind.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use in local cuisines.
