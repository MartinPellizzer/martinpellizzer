Monteverdia glaucescens

Monteverdia glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-green monteverdia, is a species of flowering plant native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy, bluish-green coating. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and early summer. Its botanical features make it well adapted to the humid, shaded environments of its native habitat.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and subtle floral display.
