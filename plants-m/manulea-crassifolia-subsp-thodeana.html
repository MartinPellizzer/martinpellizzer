Manulea crassifolia subsp. thodeana

Manulea crassifolia subsp.

thodeana, commonly known as the thick-leaved manulea, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in shades of yellow or green, and bloom during the warmer months.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is well-suited for xeriscaping and low-maintenance gardens.
