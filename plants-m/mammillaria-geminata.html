Mammillaria geminata

Mammillaria geminata, commonly known as the twin-armed hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white to yellowish spines. The plant typically grows to a height of 5 to 10 centimeters and has a compact, rounded shape. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or orange, emerging from the areoles.

Mammillaria geminata is a popular choice for succulent collections due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
