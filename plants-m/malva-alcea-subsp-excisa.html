Malva alcea subsp. excisa

Malva alcea subsp.

excisa, commonly known as the woolly mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, pale pink to lavender flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.

Its hairy stems and leaves provide a distinctive appearance, making it a notable species in botanical gardens and wildflower meadows.
