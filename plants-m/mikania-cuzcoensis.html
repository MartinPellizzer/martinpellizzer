Mikania cuzcoensis

Mikania cuzcoensis, commonly known as the Andean morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its beauty and rapid growth.
