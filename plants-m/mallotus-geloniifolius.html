Mallotus geloniifolius

Mallotus geloniifolius, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody capsules that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
