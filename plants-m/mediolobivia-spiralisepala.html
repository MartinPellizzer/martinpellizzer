Mediolobivia spiralisepala

Mediolobivia spiralisepala, commonly known as the spiral-winged lily, is a unique flowering plant characterized by its intricate, spiraling leaf structure and vibrant, bioluminescent petals.

Native to the misty highlands of the Andes, it thrives in cool, humid environments with partial shade. The plant's leaves are arranged in a helical pattern, enhancing its ability to capture sunlight efficiently. Its flowers bloom in late spring, emitting a faint, sweet aroma that attracts nocturnal pollinators.
