Miconia dissimulans

Miconia dissimulans, commonly known as the hidden miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by dark, fleshy berries. Miconia dissimulans is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
