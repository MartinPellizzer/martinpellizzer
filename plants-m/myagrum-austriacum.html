Myagrum austriacum

Myagrum austriacum, commonly known as the Austrian myrtle, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its fragrant flowers, which bloom in spring, are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, dark berries in the fall.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
