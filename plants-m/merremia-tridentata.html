Merremia tridentata

Merremia tridentata, commonly known as the three-toothed morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves with three distinct lobes, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its rapid growth habit makes it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
