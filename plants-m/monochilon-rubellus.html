Monochilon rubellus

Monochilon rubellus, commonly known as the red-stemmed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with narrow, linear leaves that are typically green on the upper surface and lighter in color underneath. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
