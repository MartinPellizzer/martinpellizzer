Myrciaria schuechiana var. deflexa

Myrciaria schuechiana var. deflexa, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are highly aromatic and often used in local cuisine. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and clusters of small white flowers. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.

The berries are rich in antioxidants and have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
