Marsdenia lanata

Marsdenia lanata, commonly known as the hairy Marsdenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels. Its hairy texture, particularly on the stems and leaves, is a distinguishing feature.

Marsdenia lanata is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
