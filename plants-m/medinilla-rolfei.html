Medinilla rolfei

Medinilla rolfei, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or velvet tangerine plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that resemble small tangerines. The plant produces small, round, orange fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Medinilla rolfei thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.
