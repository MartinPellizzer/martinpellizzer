Manihot jacobinensis

Manihot jacobinensis, commonly known as Jacobin's arrowroot, is a perennial shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, green stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is valued for its starchy roots, which are used as a food source in some local communities.

Due to its unique botanical features and potential agricultural value, it remains a subject of scientific interest.
