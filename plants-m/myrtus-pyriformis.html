Myrtus pyriformis

Myrtus pyriformis, commonly known as the oval myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces tiny white flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, dark blue berries. Myrtus pyriformis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
