Malaxis uncinata

Malaxis uncinata, commonly known as the hooked malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, spiraled inflorescence and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its roots are specialized for absorbing nutrients from the soil, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Malaxis uncinata is a member of the Orchidaceae family and exhibits typical orchid structures, including a sympodial growth habit.
