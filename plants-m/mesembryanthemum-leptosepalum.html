Mesembryanthemum leptosepalum

Mesembryanthemum leptosepalum, commonly known as the narrow-sepal mesem, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems. It blooms during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer.

Its unique morphology and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
