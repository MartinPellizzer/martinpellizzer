Monosepalum muricatum

Monosepalum muricatum, commonly known as the pineapple guava, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, spiny stem and waxy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a small, citrus-like berry with a sweet and tangy flavor.

Botanically, it belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is valued for both its ornamental and edible qualities.
