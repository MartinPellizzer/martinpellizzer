Mascagnia hippocrateoides

Mascagnia hippocrateoides, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that produces striking yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its bark is often smooth and grayish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds, contributing to its ecological significance.
