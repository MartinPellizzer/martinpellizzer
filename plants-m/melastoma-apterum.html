Melastoma apterum

Melastoma apterum, commonly known as the small-leaved melastoma, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its small, opposite leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It produces small, fleshy fruits that are dispersed by animals, contributing to its ecological role in these habitats.
