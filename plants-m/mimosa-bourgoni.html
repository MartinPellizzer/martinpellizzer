Mimosa bourgoni

Mimosa bourgoni, commonly known as the velvet mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a shallow root system that helps it thrive in well-drained soils. Mimosa bourgoni is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.

It prefers full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
