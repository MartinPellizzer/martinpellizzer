Mentha sylvestris var. mollis

Mentha sylvestris var. mollis, commonly known as velvet mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that are slightly hairy to the touch, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Velvet mint is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for its aromatic and soothing properties.
