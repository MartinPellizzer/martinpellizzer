Marsdenia amylacea

Marsdenia amylacea, commonly known as the red-flowered Marsdenia, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, elliptic leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, red or orange flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruits are follicles that release seeds with silky hairs, aiding in wind dispersal.

Marsdenia amylacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
