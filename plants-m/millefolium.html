Millefolium

Millefolium, commonly known as yarrow, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its feathery, finely divided leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and chrysanthemums. Millefolium is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils due to its adaptability.

It has been used historically for its medicinal properties, including its ability to stop bleeding and treat wounds.
