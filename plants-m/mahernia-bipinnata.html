Mahernia bipinnata

Mahernia bipinnata, commonly known as the two-pinnate mahernia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are deeply divided into two pinnae, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.
