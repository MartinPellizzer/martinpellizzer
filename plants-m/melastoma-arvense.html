Melastoma arvense

Melastoma arvense, commonly known as the field melastoma, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are often pink or red in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture.

Melastoma arvense is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
