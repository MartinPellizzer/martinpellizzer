Mariscus falcatus

Mariscus falcatus, commonly known as the sickleleaf mariscus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, sickle-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping.
