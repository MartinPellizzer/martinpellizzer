Malva aegyptia subsp. stipulacea

Malva aegyptia subsp.

stipulacea, commonly known as the Egyptian mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins and stipules.

The plant produces showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its fibrous stems and leaves have been historically used in traditional medicine and textile production.
