Matthiola odoratissima var. caspica

Matthiola odoratissima var. caspica, commonly known as the Caspian stock, is a flowering plant native to the Caspian Sea region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other garden and agricultural plants. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that come in shades of white, pink, and purple. It typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
