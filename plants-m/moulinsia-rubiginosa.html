Moulinsia rubiginosa

Moulinsia rubiginosa, commonly known as the red-stemmed bellflower, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that are often tinged with red, giving it its common name. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are oval and slightly hairy, arranged alternately along the stem.

Moulinsia rubiginosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.
