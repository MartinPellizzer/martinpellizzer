Maxillaria leucopurpurea

Maxillaria leucopurpurea, commonly known as the purple-tipped orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with purple and white petals, often with a distinctive lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Maxillaria leucopurpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
