Macfadyena uncata

Macfadyena uncata, commonly known as the Crown of Thorns, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, yellow, fleshy fruits that are edible when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
