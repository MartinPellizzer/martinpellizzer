Mandioca

Mandioca, commonly known as cassava, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is cultivated for its starchy root, which is a staple food in many regions. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and has large, heart-shaped leaves. Its root, also called yuca, is rich in carbohydrates and requires processing to remove toxic compounds.

Mandioca is adaptable to various climates and is an important crop for food security.
