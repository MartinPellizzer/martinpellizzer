Mentha viennensis

Mentha viennensis, commonly known as Viennese mint, is a perennial herb native to central Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications, particularly in traditional European cuisines.

Mentha viennensis is also valued for its medicinal properties, including its ability to soothe digestive issues.
