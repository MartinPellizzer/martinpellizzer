Melampodium geminatum

Melampodium geminatum, commonly known as the twinleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange daisy-like flowers and pairs of opposite leaves, giving it its common name. The plant belongs to the family Asteraceae and is known for its ability to thrive in dry, sandy soils. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
