Malus sieversii var. caspiriensis

Malus sieversii var. caspiriensis, commonly known as the Caspian crabapple, is a variant of the wild apple species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, ornamental fruits and distinctive foliage, which contribute to its horticultural value. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates. It exhibits a compact growth habit with spreading branches and can reach heights of up to 5 meters.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
