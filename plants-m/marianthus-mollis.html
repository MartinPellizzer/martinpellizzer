Marianthus mollis

Marianthus mollis, commonly known as the velvetleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers bloom throughout the growing season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
