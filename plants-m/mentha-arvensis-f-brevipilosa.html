Mentha arvensis f. brevipilosa

Mentha arvensis f. brevipilosa, commonly known as narrow-leaved field mint, is a subspecies of the field mint plant.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and aromatic, slightly hairy stems. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in temperate regions. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue or purple in color.

Mentha arvensis f. brevipilosa is often found in meadows, pastures, and along the edges of woodland areas.
