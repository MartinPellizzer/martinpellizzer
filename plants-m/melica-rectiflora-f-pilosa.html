Melica rectiflora f. pilosa

Melica rectiflora f. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy spikegrass, is a perennial grass species characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces inflorescences that are spike-like, with small, greenish flowers arranged in a loose cluster. It is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological habitats.
