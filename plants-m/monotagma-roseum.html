Monotagma roseum

Monotagma roseum, commonly known as the pink-flowered orchid, is a species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink and white flowers, which bloom during the warmer months. The plant has a compact growth habit with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
