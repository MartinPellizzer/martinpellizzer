Malpighia urens f. ovatifolia

Malpighia urens f. ovatifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed acerola, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits similar to cherries, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Its ornamental value and nutritional benefits make it a desirable plant for both gardens and cultivation.
