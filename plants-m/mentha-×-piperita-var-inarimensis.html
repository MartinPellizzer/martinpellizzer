Mentha × piperita var. inarimensis

Mentha × piperita var. inarimensis, commonly known as the Inarimense peppermint, is a hybrid mint species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves that emit a strong menthol scent. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb with square stems and opposite leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its use in herbal medicine and as a flavoring agent in food and beverages.
