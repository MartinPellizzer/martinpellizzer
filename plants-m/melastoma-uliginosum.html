Melastoma uliginosum

Melastoma uliginosum, commonly known as the swamp melastoma, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in swampy or wetland habitats. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a glossy surface, and it produces small, dark berries that are edible.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
