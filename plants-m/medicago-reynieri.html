Medicago reynieri

Medicago reynieri, commonly known as Reynier's clover, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. Its root system is well-developed, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Medicago reynieri is often found in rocky or arid habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
