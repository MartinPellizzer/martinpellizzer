Menispermum dioicum

Menispermum dioicum, commonly known as Indian snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the Menispermaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically borne in clusters. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains several seeds.

Botanically, it is notable for its medicinal properties and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
