Myosotis palustris proles welwitschii

Myosotis palustris proles welwitschii, commonly known as the swamp forget-me-not, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, blue or violet petals and is typically found in wetland habitats. The plant produces slender, branching stems and has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It blooms during the cooler months, usually from late spring to early summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
