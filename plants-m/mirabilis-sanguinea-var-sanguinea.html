Mirabilis sanguinea var. sanguinea

Mirabilis sanguinea var. sanguinea, commonly known as the blood flower or red morning glory, is a striking flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or pink flowers that open in the early morning and close by midday, a phenomenon known as nyctinasty. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique blooming pattern and striking appearance make it a notable species in the Mirabilis genus.
