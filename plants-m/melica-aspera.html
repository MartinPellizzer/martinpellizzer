Melica aspera

Melica aspera, commonly known as rough brome, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically green or pale yellow in color. It is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

Melica aspera is a perennial grass that can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
