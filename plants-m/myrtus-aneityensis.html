Myrtus aneityensis

Myrtus aneityensis, commonly known as the Corsican myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, dark green leaves and small, white, five-petaled flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and rocky environments.

Myrtus aneityensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
