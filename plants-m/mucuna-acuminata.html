Mucuna acuminata

Mucuna acuminata, commonly known as the cow pea or velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and hairy, pod-like fruits that contain small, yellow seeds. The plant is notable for its rapid growth and ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, enriching the soil. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, and it produces a strong, aromatic odor.

Mucuna acuminata is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.
