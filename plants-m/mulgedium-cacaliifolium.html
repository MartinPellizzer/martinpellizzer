Mulgedium cacaliifolium

Mulgedium cacaliifolium, commonly known as the goldenleaf spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically bright green with a waxy coating, helping it conserve moisture in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access water from underground sources.

Mulgedium cacaliifolium is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
