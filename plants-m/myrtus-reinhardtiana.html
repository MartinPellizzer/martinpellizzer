Myrtus reinhardtiana

Myrtus reinhardtiana, commonly known as the Arabian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

Myrtus reinhardtiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in regional cultures.
