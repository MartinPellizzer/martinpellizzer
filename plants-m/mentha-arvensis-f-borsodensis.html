Mentha arvensis f. borsodensis

Mentha arvensis f. borsodensis, commonly known as Hungarian mint, is a subspecies of field mint native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and flowering spikes, which contain essential oils responsible for its distinctive scent. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their cooling and digestive properties.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly larger leaves and more robust growth compared to other Mentha species.
