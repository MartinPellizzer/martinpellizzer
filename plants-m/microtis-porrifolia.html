Microtis porrifolia

Microtis porrifolia, commonly known as the onion orchid, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Microtis porrifolia is typically found in damp, shaded environments such as forests and rock formations.

Its unique appearance and habitat make it a notable species in New Zealand's botanical diversity.
