Malope malacoides subsp. laevigata

Malope malacoides subsp.

laevigata, commonly known as the smoothleaf malope, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
