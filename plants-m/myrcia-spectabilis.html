Myrcia spectabilis

Myrcia spectabilis, commonly known as the purple myrtle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that contains several seeds.
