Malpighia variifolia

Malpighia variifolia, commonly known as the variable wax apple, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that vary in shape and size. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to red, fleshy berries resembling small apples. These berries are edible, though they have a tart, acidic flavor, and are often used in local cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
