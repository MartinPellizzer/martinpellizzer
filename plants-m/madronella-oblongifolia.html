Madronella oblongifolia

Madronella oblongifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf madrona, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

Madronella oblongifolia is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
