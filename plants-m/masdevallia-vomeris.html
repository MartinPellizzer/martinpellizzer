Masdevallia vomeris

Masdevallia vomeris, commonly known as the Vomeris Masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, often densely packed inflorescence and striking, intricate flower structures. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small to medium-sized, with vibrant colors ranging from red to purple, and often feature intricate patterns.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
