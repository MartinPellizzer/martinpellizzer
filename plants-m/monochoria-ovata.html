Monochoria ovata

Monochoria ovata, commonly known as the broadleaf water fern, is a aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It has elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically broad at the base and taper toward the tip. The plant thrives in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater habitats. Its reproductive structures include small, floating spore-producing structures called sporangia.

Monochoria ovata is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued in aquatic gardening for its ornamental appeal.
