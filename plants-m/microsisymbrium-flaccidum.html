Microsisymbrium flaccidum

Microsisymbrium flaccidum, commonly known as the flaccid symbiotic grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant has slender, creeping stems with small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, producing pods that contain minute seeds.

Due to its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soils, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
