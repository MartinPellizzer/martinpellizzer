Millettia pirifolia

Millettia pirifolia, commonly known as the Chinese tallow tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellowish flowers. The tree produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, waxy coating. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other parts of the world, where it can become invasive.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
