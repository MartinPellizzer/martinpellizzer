Melanthera madagascariensis

Melanthera madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar melastome, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, often violet or purple flowers. The plant typically has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
