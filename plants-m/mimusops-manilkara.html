Mimusops manilkara

Mimusops manilkara, commonly known as the Indian satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that are fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to reveal a single, hard-seeded kernel.

The wood of Mimusops manilkara is highly valued for its fine grain and durability, making it a sought-after material in furniture and carving.
