Maranta leuconeura var. kerchoveana

Maranta leuconeura var. kerchoveana, commonly known as the Prayer Plant, is a tropical species native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that fold shut at night, a behavior known as nyctinasty. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is prized for its ornamental value in indoor gardening. Its foliage displays vibrant patterns of red, purple, and green, often with a metallic sheen.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.
