Myrcia prunifolia var. ovata

Myrcia prunifolia var. ovata, commonly known as the oval-leaved myrtle, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves that are often fragrant. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal forests and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic properties, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
