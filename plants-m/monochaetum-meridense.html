Monochaetum meridense

Monochaetum meridense, commonly known as the southern sandplain grass, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy soils and is adapted to dry, coastal environments. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
