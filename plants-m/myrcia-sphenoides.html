Myrcia sphenoides

Myrcia sphenoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Myrcia sphenoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
