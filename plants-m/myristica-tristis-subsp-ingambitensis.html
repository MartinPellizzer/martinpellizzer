Myristica tristis subsp. ingambitensis

Myristica tristis subsp.

ingambitensis, commonly known as the Ingambitensis nutmeg, is a member of the Myristicaceae family and is native to the Western Ghats of India. It is a small evergreen tree characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers.

The plant produces a unique seed with an aromatic, oily kernel that is used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its habitat is typically moist, shaded forests at moderate altitudes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
