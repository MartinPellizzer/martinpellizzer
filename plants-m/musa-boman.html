Musa boman

Musa boman, commonly known as the Boman banana, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and clusters of fleshy, edible berries. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its berries are typically yellow or orange when ripe and are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

The Boman banana is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
