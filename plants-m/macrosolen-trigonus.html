Macrosolen trigonus

Macrosolen trigonus, commonly known as the three-angled macrolen, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its unique tri-angled stems and vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to red. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
