Montanoa tomentosa subsp. xanthiifolia

Montanoa tomentosa subsp.

xanthiifolia, commonly known as the yellow-leaf montanoa, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico. It features a compact growth habit with hairy, grayish-green foliage.

The plant produces showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and have a soft, velvety texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.
