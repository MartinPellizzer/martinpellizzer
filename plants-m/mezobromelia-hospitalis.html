Mezobromelia hospitalis

Mezobromelia hospitalis, commonly known as the hospital bromeliad, is a species of flowering plant in the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater to support its growth. Its flowers are typically red or orange, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.

Mezobromelia hospitalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
