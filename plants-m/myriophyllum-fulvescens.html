Myriophyllum fulvescens

Myriophyllum fulvescens, commonly known as redstem watermilfoil, is a perennial aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and streams. The plant has finely divided, feather-like leaves that are often reddish in color, especially when submerged. It produces small, green flowers that float on the water surface, and it reproduces both sexually and asexually through rhizomes.

Myriophyllum fulvescens is an important species in aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
