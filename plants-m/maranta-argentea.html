Maranta argentea

Maranta argentea, commonly known as the silver plant or silverleaf arrowroot, is a tropical perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, shiny, silver-green leaves that are often veined with bright red or pink, creating a visually appealing contrast. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are typically followed by clusters of orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Maranta argentea is also valued for its edible tubers, which are used in traditional cuisines.
