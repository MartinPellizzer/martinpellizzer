Mundia desertorum

Mundia desertorum, commonly known as the desert mallow, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and showy pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its fibrous roots help it retain moisture in harsh environments.

Mundia desertorum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
