Megacaryon armenum

Megacaryon armenum, commonly known as the Armenian giant bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue or violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often hairy, adapted to retain moisture in its arid or semi-arid habitat. Its botanical features include a robust root system and the ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Megacaryon armenum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with similar environmental conditions.
