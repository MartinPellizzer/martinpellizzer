Mentha gentilis var. kmetiana

Mentha gentilis var. kmetiana, commonly known as Kmetian mint, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows. It has a strong, mint-like fragrance and is used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported digestive and calming properties.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
