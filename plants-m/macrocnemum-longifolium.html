Macrocnemum longifolium

Macrocnemum longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved macrocnemum, is a herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the rainy season. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes other aromatic plants like carrots and parsley.
