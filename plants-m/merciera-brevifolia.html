Merciera brevifolia

Merciera brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved mercier, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features simple, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters.

Merciera brevifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
