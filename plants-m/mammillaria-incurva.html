Mammillaria incurva

Mammillaria incurva, commonly known as the hooked mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny, ribbed stems. The plant features pale green to blue-gray tubercles, which are covered in white to yellowish hairs. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its foliage.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
