Melandrium saxosum

Melandrium saxosum, commonly known as the rock catchfly, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and the plant has a compact, mat-like growth habit.

It is known for its ability to survive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitats.
