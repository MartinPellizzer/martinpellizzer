Malacocarpus turbinatus

Malacocarpus turbinatus, commonly known as the turbinated malacocarpus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Meliaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, turbinated (spiral-shaped) fruits and compound leaves with alternate arrangement. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
