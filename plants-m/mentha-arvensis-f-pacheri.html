Mentha arvensis f. pacheri

Mentha arvensis f. pacheri, commonly known as the Hungarian mint, is a variant of the field mint species.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, pale blue to violet flowers. This plant is native to Central Europe and thrives in moist, shaded environments. It has a strong menthol scent and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Its botanical features make it distinct from other mint species, particularly in its leaf shape and fragrance profile.
