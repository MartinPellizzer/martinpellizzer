Mammillaria echinoidea

Mammillaria echinoidea, commonly known as the spiny hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the deserts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its spiny appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and xeriscaping.
