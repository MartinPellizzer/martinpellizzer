Medinilla ledermannii

Medinilla ledermannii, commonly known as the Ledermann's velvetleaf, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, round, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowers.

Medinilla ledermannii is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
