Malvaviscus floridanus

Malvaviscus floridanus, commonly known as the Florida mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in wetlands and along waterways. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red, blooming in the spring and summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

The Florida mallow is adapted to moist environments and plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
