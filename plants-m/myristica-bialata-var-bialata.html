Myristica bialata var. bialata

Myristica bialata var. bialata, commonly known as the Bornean nutmeg, is a member of the Myristicaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree that typically grows to a height of 10-15 meters, characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant produces an aromatic fruit that contains a valuable seed, which is used to make nutmeg and mace. Its botanical features include a woody stem, compound leaves, and a unique scent due to its essential oils.

This species is primarily cultivated for its spice-producing seeds and is an important part of the regional flora.
