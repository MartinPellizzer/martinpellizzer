Marsilea maheshwarii

Marsilea maheshwarii, commonly known as the Maheshwari water clover, is a species of aquatic fern belonging to the family Marsileaceae.

It is characterized by its small, floating leaves that resemble clover leaves, typically consisting of four lobes. This plant thrives in freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams, where it can receive adequate sunlight and nutrients. Its reproductive structures include small, yellowish flowers that develop into buoyant, spore-bearing fruits.

Marsilea maheshwarii plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small aquatic life.
