Maesa tomentosa

Maesa tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly maesa, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, clustered white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous areas. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptic in shape and covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Maesa tomentosa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
