Mentha × tenuis

Mentha × tenuis, commonly known as narrow-leaf mint, is a hybrid species within the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stem. Mentha × tenuis is native to regions of Europe and western Asia and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for its aromatic and potentially therapeutic properties.
