Macaranga ankafinensis var. baroniana

Macaranga ankafinensis var. baroniana, commonly known as the Madagascar mallow, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, tropical environments. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a distinctive reddish or purple underside. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
