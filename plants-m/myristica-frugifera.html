Myristica frugifera

Myristica frugifera, commonly known as the nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark brown bark and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into bright red, fleshy berries. Inside the berries, the seeds, known as nutmegs, are enclosed in a hard, aromatic shell.

The tree is valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery, medicine, and as a spice.
