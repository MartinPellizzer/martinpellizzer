Meissarrhena tomentosa

Meissarrhena tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly meissarrhena, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires moderate sunlight to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
