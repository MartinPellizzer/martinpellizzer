Myrciaria edulis

Myrciaria edulis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that produces bright red berries, which are rich in vitamin C. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, while its flowers are small and white or pink.

The fruit has a tangy, sweet flavor and is used in various culinary and medicinal applications.
