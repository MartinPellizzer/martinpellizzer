Melilotoides laxiflora

Melilotoides laxiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered melilot, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being elliptic to ovate in shape.

Melilotoides laxiflora is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
