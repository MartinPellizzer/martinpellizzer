Melicocca paniculata

Melicocca paniculata, commonly known as the Mexican crabapple, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and emit a pleasant fragrance when crushed. The tree produces small, white, fragrant flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by edible, orange-red berries that attract wildlife. Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and showy blooms, making it a popular choice for gardens.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
