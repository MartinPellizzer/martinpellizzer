Mariscus flavus var. gigas

Mariscus flavus var. gigas, commonly known as the giant yellow mariscus, is a rare and striking plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a woody stem with thick, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by specialty growers.
