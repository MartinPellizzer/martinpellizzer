Mentha × kerneri var. rhapidocea

Mentha × kerneri var. rhapidocea, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spearmint, is a hybrid mint species that exhibits a combination of traits from its parent species.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinct appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in spikes along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
