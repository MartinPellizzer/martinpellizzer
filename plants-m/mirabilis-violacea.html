Mirabilis violacea

Mirabilis violacea, commonly known as the velvetleaf four-o'clock, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, violet to purple flowers that bloom in the late afternoon. The leaves are broad and velvety, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

The plant is part of the Nyctaginaceae family, known for its showy, often fragrant blooms.
