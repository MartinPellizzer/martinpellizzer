Mimosa somnians var. lasiocarpa

Mimosa somnians var. lasiocarpa, commonly known as the woolly mimosa, is a variant of the mimosa plant characterized by its soft, hairy foliage.

It is native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in spherical inflorescences, and it produces legumes as its fruit.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
