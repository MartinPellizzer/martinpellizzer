Mitrasacme erophila

Mitrasacme erophila, commonly known as the slender mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It possesses specialized roots that attach to its host, allowing it to draw water and nutrients while also photosynthesizing independently. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces small white or greenish flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that releases tiny seeds, which can adhere to the bark of host trees.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can impact the health of its host, making it an important species in ecological studies.
