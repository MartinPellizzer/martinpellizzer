Maxillariella procurrens

Maxillariella procurrens, commonly known as the spreading maxillariella, is a small orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically blooms in the summer, with flowers that have a distinctive, elongated shape. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a suitable choice for greenhouse cultivation.

Its botanical features make it a popular specimen among orchid enthusiasts.
