Macadamia tetraphylla

Macadamia tetraphylla, commonly known as the mountain macadamia, is a species of evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces woody capsules that contain edible, nut-like seeds, which are highly valued for their rich flavor and nutritional content. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a subtropical climate with high rainfall.

Due to its ornamental value and economic importance, it is cultivated in various regions beyond its native range.
