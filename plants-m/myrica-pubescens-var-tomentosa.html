Myrica pubescens var. tomentosa

Myrica pubescens var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly wax myrtle, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, waxy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in wet, acidic soils and is often found in swamps and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
