Mertensia coriacea

Mertensia coriacea, commonly known as the roughleaf bluebells, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a rough, waxy texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically valuable component of its native ecosystem.
