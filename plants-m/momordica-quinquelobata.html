Momordica quinquelobata

Momordica quinquelobata, commonly known as the five-lobed bitter gourd, is a tropical vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, spiny fruits that mature to a dark green or yellowish color. It is native to Southeast Asia and is often cultivated for its edible fruits, though they are known for their bitter taste.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
