Mesomelaena pseudostygia

Mesomelaena pseudostygia, commonly known as the black-veined orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, veined leaves and its unique, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in the dappled light. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, organic soil of its habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in conservation efforts.
