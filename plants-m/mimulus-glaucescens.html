Mimulus glaucescens

Mimulus glaucescens, commonly known as the yellow monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, funnel-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as streambanks and meadows. It has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are often reddish in color.

Mimulus glaucescens is a perennial species that plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
