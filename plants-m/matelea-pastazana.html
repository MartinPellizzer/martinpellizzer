Matelea pastazana

Matelea pastazana, commonly known as the Pastaza moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant produces violet-colored berries that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by animals. Matelea pastazana thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
