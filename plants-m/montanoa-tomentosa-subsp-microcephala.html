Montanoa tomentosa subsp. microcephala

Montanoa tomentosa subsp.

microcephala, commonly known as the woolly montanoa, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Mexico. It is characterized by its dense, white, woolly foliage that covers its stems and leaves, providing a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale yellow to cream in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
