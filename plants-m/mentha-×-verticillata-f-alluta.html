Mentha × verticillata f. alluta

Mentha × verticillata f. alluta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water mint, is a hybrid species within the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating on water surfaces. This plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. Its flowering spikes produce small, pale purple or white flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant is often used in herbal remedies and is valued for its aromatic properties and ecological role in aquatic habitats.
