Myrcia eumecephylla

Myrcia eumecephylla, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to the humid regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, edible seeds.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic benefits.
