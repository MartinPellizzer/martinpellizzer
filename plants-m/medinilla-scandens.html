Medinilla scandens

Medinilla scandens, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or snowball plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of fragrant, white or pink flowers that resemble snowballs. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and often have a waxy texture. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as a ornamental plant.

Medinilla scandens is also valued for its ability to produce small, edible fruits that have a tangy, citrus-like flavor.
