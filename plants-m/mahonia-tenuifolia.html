Mahonia tenuifolia

Mahonia tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf mahonia, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features compound leaves with spiny tips and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces blue-black berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or woodland settings.

Its evergreen foliage provides year-round interest in ornamental landscapes.
