Mespilus caroliniana

Mespilus caroliniana, commonly known as the Carolina mespilus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin and a pointed tip. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, round fruits resembling small apples. The fruits are typically harvested in late summer and are characterized by their sweet, aromatic flavor.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
