Munnozia venosissima

Munnozia venosissima, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Munnozia venosissima plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
