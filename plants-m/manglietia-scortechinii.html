Manglietia scortechinii

Manglietia scortechinii, commonly known as the Mexican magnolia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Magnoliaceae and is characterized by its large, showy, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its flowers emit a strong, sweet fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths.

Manglietia scortechinii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and subtropical regions.
