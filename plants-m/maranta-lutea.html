Maranta lutea

Maranta lutea, commonly known as the golden arrowroot, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. Its rhizomes are used as a starchy food source, similar to arrowroot.

Maranta lutea thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible roots.
