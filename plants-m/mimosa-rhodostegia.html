Mimosa rhodostegia

Mimosa rhodostegia, commonly known as the pink mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and showy pink to red flowers. The plant is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and savannas. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are attractive to pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant has a shallow root system and is adapted to well-drained soils.
