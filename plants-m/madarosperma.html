Madarosperma

Madarosperma, commonly known as the goldenbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, branched structure and small, yellowish-green leaves that are covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its seeds are enclosed in small, capsule-like fruits that split open when mature.

Madarosperma is well adapted to dry environments, with deep roots and a high tolerance for drought.
