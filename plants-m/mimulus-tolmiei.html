Mimulus tolmiei

Mimulus tolmiei, commonly known as the Tolmie's monkeyflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern California.

It features striking orange to red flowers with a distinctive yellow throat, typical of the Mimulus genus. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Mimulus tolmiei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native plant gardens.
