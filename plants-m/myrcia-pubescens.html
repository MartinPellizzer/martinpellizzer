Myrcia pubescens

Myrcia pubescens, commonly known as the hairy myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, spherical berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Myrcia pubescens is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
