Mirabilis jalapa subsp. gracilis

Mirabilis jalapa subsp.

gracilis, commonly known as the slender four-o'clock, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Central America. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that open in the evening.

The flowers come in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white, and are pollinated primarily by nocturnal insects. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
