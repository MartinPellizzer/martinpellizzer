Minuartia muscorum

Minuartia muscorum, commonly known as the common rockcress, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arctic regions. It has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Minuartia muscorum plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
