Mussaenda theifera

Mussaenda theifera, commonly known as the thief's glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of showy, bright yellow bracts that resemble flowers, although they are actually modified leaves. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Mussaenda theifera thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
