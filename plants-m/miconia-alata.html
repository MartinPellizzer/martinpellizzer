Miconia alata

Miconia alata, commonly known as the broadleaf miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Miconia alata is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and potentially problematic in non-native habitats.
