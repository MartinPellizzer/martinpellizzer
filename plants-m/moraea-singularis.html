Moraea singularis

Moraea singularis, commonly known as the solitary moraea, is a striking perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing clusters of tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
