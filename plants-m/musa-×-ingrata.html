Musa × ingrata

Musa × ingrata, commonly known as the wild banana, is a hybrid species within the genus Musa.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and tall, clustered inflorescences. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its flowers are typically greenish-white, and it produces edible berries that are smaller than those of cultivated bananas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in breeding programs to improve banana cultivars.
