Malache sagittata

Malache sagittata, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a tropical species native to Central and South America.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Araceae family, characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and striking inflorescence. The plant produces small, white flowers surrounded by a spathe, which is often mistaken for the actual flower. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in aquatic or bog gardens.

Malache sagittata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and air-purifying qualities.
