Morinda longiflora

Morinda longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered morinda, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and pinnate leaves with 7 to 11 leaflets. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruits are follicles containing several seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Morinda longiflora is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
