Malus × floribunda f. parkmannii

Malus × floribunda f. parkmannii, commonly known as the Parkmann's flowering crabapple, is a cultivar of the crabapple tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its profuse display of small, fragrant flowers in spring, which are typically white or pale pink. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in jams and jellies. It is a deciduous tree with a compact, rounded shape and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The leaves are dark green and turn yellow in autumn, adding to its seasonal beauty.
