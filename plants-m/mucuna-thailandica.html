Mucuna thailandica

Mucuna thailandica, commonly known as Thai velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety pods that contain numerous small, elliptical seeds. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers, which are typically followed by the distinctive hairy pods. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Mucuna thailandica is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.
