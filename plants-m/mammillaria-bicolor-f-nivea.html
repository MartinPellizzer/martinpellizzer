Mammillaria bicolor f. nivea

Mammillaria bicolor f. nivea, commonly known as the White Globular Cactus, is a rare and striking variant of the Mammillaria bicolor species.

It is characterized by its dense, white to pale pink spines and a globular, compact growth form. The plant features short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, emerging from the areoles in late spring or early summer.

This cultivar is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in arid gardens and greenhouses.
