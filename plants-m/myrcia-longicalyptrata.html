Myrcia longicalyptrata

Myrcia longicalyptrata, commonly known as the long-lobed myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom during the dry season. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

Myrcia longicalyptrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
