Masdevallia fimbriata

Masdevallia fimbriata, commonly known as the fringe orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, often colorful flowers with fringed petals and sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually elongated and strap-like, adapted for efficient water absorption.

Masdevallia fimbriata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
