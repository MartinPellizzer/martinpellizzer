Marshallia lanceolata

Marshallia lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Marshallia lanceolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
