Melastoma ecostatum

Melastoma ecostatum, commonly known as the coastal melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments along coastal areas. Its leaves are ovate and have a smooth texture, while its stems are often prostrate or creeping.

Melastoma ecostatum plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
