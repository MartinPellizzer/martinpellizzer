Morongia horridula var. angularis

Morongia horridula var. angularis, commonly known as the roughleaf mallow, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its angular, rough-textured leaves and showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its unique foliage and vibrant blooms make it a distinctive addition to native plant gardens.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
