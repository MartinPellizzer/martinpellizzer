Morus cavaleriei

Morus cavaleriei, commonly known as the Chinese mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The tree produces small, aggregate fruits known as mulberries, which are typically dark purple when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its fibrous bark, which is used in papermaking.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide shade in urban landscapes.
