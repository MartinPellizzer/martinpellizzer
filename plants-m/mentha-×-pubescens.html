Mentha × pubescens

Mentha × pubescens, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Mentha viridis and Mentha rotundifolia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and a strong, minty aroma. This perennial herb is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Mentha × pubescens is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
