Miconia paeminosa

Miconia paeminosa, commonly known as the Brazilian star-apple tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its dense canopy and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are typically glossy and have a waxy texture, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Miconia paeminosa is also known for its rapid growth rate and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
