Mussaenda elegans var. psilocarpa

Mussaenda elegans var. psilocarpa, commonly known as the purple flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that range from pink to deep purple in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in terminal umbels, which are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
