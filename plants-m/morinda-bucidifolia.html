Morinda bucidifolia

Morinda bucidifolia, commonly known as the red morinda, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves that are usually elliptical to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red or orange berries. Its fruits are fleshy and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

Morinda bucidifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
