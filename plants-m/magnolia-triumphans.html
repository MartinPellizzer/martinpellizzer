Magnolia triumphans

Magnolia triumphans, commonly known as the Saucer Magnolia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, saucer-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a fragrant scent that attracts pollinators. The leaves are broad and ovate, turning yellow in the fall before shedding.

This plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
