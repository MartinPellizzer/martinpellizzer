Myrciaria quitarensis

Myrciaria quitarensis, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a species of shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark purple to black berries that ripen in the late summer. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers and has glossy, elliptical leaves. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The berries are edible and are sometimes used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
