Mussaenda lotungensis

Mussaenda lotungensis, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences that are usually white or cream in color. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and the plant produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds.
