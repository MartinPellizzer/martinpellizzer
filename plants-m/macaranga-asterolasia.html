Macaranga asterolasia

Macaranga asterolasia, commonly known as the hairy macaranga, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forests.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it an important species in ecological studies and potential applications in phytoremediation.
