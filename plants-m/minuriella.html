Minuriella

Minuriella, commonly known as the rock fig, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry conditions. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits resembling figs. Minuriella thrives in well-drained soils and is often found growing on rocky outcrops or in disturbed areas.

Its ability to survive in harsh environments makes it an important component of local ecosystems.
