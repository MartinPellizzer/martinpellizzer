Montanoa excelsa

Montanoa excelsa, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats and is adapted to cooler, drier climates. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Montanoa excelsa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
