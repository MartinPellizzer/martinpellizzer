Malpighia mucronata subsp. intermedia

Malpighia mucronata subsp.

intermedia, commonly known as the purple granadilla, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically purple or red in color when ripe.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves with smooth margins and a woody stem structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

The fruits are often used in culinary applications and are valued for their tart flavor and nutritional content.
