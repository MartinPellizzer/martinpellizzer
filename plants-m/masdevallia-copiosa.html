Masdevallia copiosa

Masdevallia copiosa, commonly known as the profuse masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually leathery and arranged in a rosette pattern, helping it conserve moisture.

Masdevallia copiosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
