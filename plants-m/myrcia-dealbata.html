Myrcia dealbata

Myrcia dealbata, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Myrcia dealbata is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
