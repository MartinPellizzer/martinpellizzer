Mascagnia chlorocarpa var. paradoxa

It is a hemiparasitic plant, meaning it attaches to a host tree and derives some of its nutrients from it while also performing photosynthesis. The plant produces small, white or pale green berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

This variant is distinguished by its unique morphological features and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
