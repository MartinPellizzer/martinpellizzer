Merremia lobata

Merremia lobata, commonly known as the climbing morning glory, is a vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves with lobed edges and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to climb using its tendrils. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence the common name.

Merremia lobata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
