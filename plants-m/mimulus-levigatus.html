Mimulus levigatus

Mimulus levigatus, commonly known as the yellow monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and in open woodlands. It has heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, spurred petals that are adapted for pollination by insects.

Mimulus levigatus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
