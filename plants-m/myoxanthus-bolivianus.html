Myoxanthus bolivianus

Myoxanthus bolivianus, commonly known as the Bolivian waxplant, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Bolivia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing adaptations to its harsh habitat. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the cooler months. Its leaves are thick and waxy, which helps reduce water loss in its dry environment.

Myoxanthus bolivianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
