Myrrhidium betonium

Myrrhidium betonium, commonly known as betony, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist meadows and woodland edges. The plant features square stems, opposite leaves, and purple or pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer. Betony is known for its aromatic leaves and has a long history of use in traditional herbal medicine.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
