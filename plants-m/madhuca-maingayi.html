Madhuca maingayi

Madhuca maingayi, commonly known as the Bornean wax tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that provide a glossy appearance. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, starchy fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
