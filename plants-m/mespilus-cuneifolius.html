Mespilus cuneifolius

Mespilus cuneifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf medlar, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, often found in dry, rocky soils. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in late summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
