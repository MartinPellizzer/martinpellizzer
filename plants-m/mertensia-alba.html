Mertensia alba

Mertensia alba, commonly known as whitebells, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Mertensia alba is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
