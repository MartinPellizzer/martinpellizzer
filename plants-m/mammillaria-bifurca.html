Mammillaria bifurca

Mammillaria bifurca, commonly known as the two-branched hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a distinctive rib structure and short, spiny tubercles. The plant typically has pale green to blue-gray stems that are covered in white or pale blue spines. It produces yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by pale green fruits.

Mammillaria bifurca is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
