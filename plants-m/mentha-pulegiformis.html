Mentha pulegiformis

Mentha pulegiformis, commonly known as field mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in whorls. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly serrated, with a strong, minty aroma. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in shaded woodland areas.

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in culinary applications.
