Melastoma amygdalinum

Melastoma amygdalinum, commonly known as the purple mangosteen, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking purple flowers and edible, segmented fruit. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth surface, while its stems are woody and branched.

The fruit, which is the most notable part of the plant, contains several segments filled with a sweet, aromatic pulp.
