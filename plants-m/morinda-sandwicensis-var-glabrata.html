Morinda sandwicensis var. glabrata

Morinda sandwicensis var. glabrata, commonly known as the Hawaiian morinda, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas and dry forests. The plant features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are usually elliptical with a smooth texture. It is adapted to the unique climate of Hawaii, with some varieties showing resistance to drought.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its endemic status and ecological significance in native Hawaiian ecosystems.
