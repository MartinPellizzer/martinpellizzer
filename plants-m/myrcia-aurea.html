Myrcia aurea

Myrcia aurea, commonly known as golden myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its attractive, fragrant, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and emit a strong, sweet scent when crushed.

Myrcia aurea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
