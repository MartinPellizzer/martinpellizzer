Mattiastrum erysimifolium

Mattiastrum erysimifolium, commonly known as the yellow false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as marshes and wet meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are bright yellow and resemble those of dandelions.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in wetland ecosystems.
