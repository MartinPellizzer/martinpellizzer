Monixus teretifolius

Monixus teretifolius, commonly known as the slender-leaf monix, is a herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It features elongated, narrow leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, blooming during the wet season. Its root system is fibrous and has been traditionally used in local herbal remedies.

Due to habitat loss, Monixus teretifolius is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
