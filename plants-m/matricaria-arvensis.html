Matricaria arvensis

Matricaria arvensis, commonly known as field chamomile, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows up to 30 cm tall, with finely divided leaves and a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

The flowers are used in herbal medicine for their calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
