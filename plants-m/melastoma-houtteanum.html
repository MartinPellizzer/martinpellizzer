Melastoma houtteanum

Melastoma houtteanum, commonly known as the red-flowered melastoma, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Melastoma houtteanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
