Miscanthus sinensis subsp. purpurascens

Miscanthus sinensis subsp.

purpurascens, commonly known as the purple moor grass, is a perennial grass native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its tall, arching stems and feathery plumes that turn a striking reddish-purple in the fall.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a silver-green hue during the growing season.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
