Masdevallia chlorotica

Masdevallia chlorotica, commonly known as the green orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking greenish-yellow flowers and is often found growing in high-altitude cloud forests. The plant typically has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elongated and grass-like, adapted to the cool, moist conditions of its habitat.

Masdevallia chlorotica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
