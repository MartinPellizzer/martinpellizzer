Myrcia lapidulosa

Myrcia lapidulosa, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Myrcia lapidulosa is also valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumes and aromatherapy.
