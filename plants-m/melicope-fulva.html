Melicope fulva

Melicope fulva, commonly known as the yellow sandpaper tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its rough, bark-like leaves that give it a distinctive sandpaper texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, often yellow or greenish in color, and it produces small, round fruits.

Melicope fulva is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some cultures.
