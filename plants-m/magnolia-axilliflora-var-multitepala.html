Magnolia axilliflora var. multitepala

Magnolia axilliflora var. multitepala, commonly known as the multi-petaled magnolia, is a rare and ornamental flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features broad, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. Its flowers are composed of numerous petal-like structures, giving it a fluffy appearance.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized collections.
