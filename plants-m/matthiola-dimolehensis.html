Matthiola dimolehensis

Matthiola dimolehensis, commonly known as the two-lobed stock, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale purple, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Matthiola dimolehensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and public spaces.
