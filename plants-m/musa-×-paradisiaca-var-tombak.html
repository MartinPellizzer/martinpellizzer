Musa × paradisiaca var. tombak

Musa × paradisiaca var. tombak, commonly known as the dwarf banana, is a cultivar of the banana plant characterized by its compact growth habit.

It produces small, sweet fruits that are typically eaten raw or used in cooking. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and often have a waxy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.
