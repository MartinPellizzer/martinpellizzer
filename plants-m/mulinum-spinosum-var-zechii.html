Mulinum spinosum var. zechii

Mulinum spinosum var. zechii, commonly known as the Zech's spiny mullein, is a variant of the spiny mullein native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suited to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and herbalist collections.
