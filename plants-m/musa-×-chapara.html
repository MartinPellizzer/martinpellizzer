Musa × chapara

Musa × chapara, commonly known as the banana plant, is a hybrid species within the genus Musa, native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and a tall, leafy stem that supports the development of its fruiting clusters. The plant is a member of the Musaceae family and is cultivated for its edible berries, which are rich in nutrients. Musa × chapara typically grows in tropical climates and requires warm, humid conditions to thrive.

Its botanical features make it an important crop in many regions for both food and economic purposes.
