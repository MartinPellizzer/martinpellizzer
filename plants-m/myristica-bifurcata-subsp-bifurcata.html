Myristica bifurcata subsp. bifurcata

Myristica bifurcata subsp.

bifurcata, commonly known as the nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia. It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic bark and distinctive red, fleshy fruits.

The plant produces two types of seeds—nutmeg and mace—both of which are highly valued for their aromatic properties and culinary uses. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is also notable for its medicinal uses and historical significance in global trade.
