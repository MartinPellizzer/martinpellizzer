Millettia melanocalyx

Millettia melanocalyx, commonly known as the black-leaf millettia, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant in forest ecosystems. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The tree typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland tropical forests.
