Miconia aurantipetala

Miconia aurantipetala, commonly known as the golden petal miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Neotropics.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of showy, orange-yellow flowers that are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains numerous small, black seeds.

Miconia aurantipetala is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.
