Mammillaria ludwigii var. clavata

Mammillaria ludwigii var. clavata, commonly known as the clustered mammillaria, is a compact cactus native to Mexico.

It features a short, cylindrical stem with tightly packed, white to pale blue tubercles that give it a rounded appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring or early summer. It is well-suited for rock gardens and containers due to its compact size and low maintenance requirements.

Its distinctive clustering growth habit makes it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
