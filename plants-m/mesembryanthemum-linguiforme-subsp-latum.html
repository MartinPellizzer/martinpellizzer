Mesembryanthemum linguiforme subsp. latum

Mesembryanthemum linguiforme subsp.

latum, commonly known as the broad-leafed ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and leaves that are typically broad and oval-shaped.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and white, often blooming during the cooler months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
