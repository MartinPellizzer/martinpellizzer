Melochia hirsuta var. paraguayensis

Melochia hirsuta var. paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay hedge or paraguay hibiscus, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in horticulture and ecological studies.
