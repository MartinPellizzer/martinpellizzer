Mimosa gracilipes

Mimosa gracilipes, commonly known as the slender mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with slender stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

The flowers bloom in clusters and are known for their ability to close when touched, a trait shared with other mimosa species.
