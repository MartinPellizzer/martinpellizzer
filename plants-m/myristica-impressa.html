Myristica impressa

Myristica impressa, commonly known as the fragrant nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the western Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody fruits that contain both nutmeg and mace. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers and has elliptical, dark green leaves with a glossy surface. Its fruit, which matures to a reddish color, contains a single seed enclosed in a fibrous aril.

The tree is valued for its aromatic seeds, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
