Myrica aethiopica

Myrica aethiopica, commonly known as the Ethiopian wax tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Myrica aethiopica is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local communities.
