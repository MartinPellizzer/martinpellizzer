Melochia ulmarioides f. brevipedunculata

Melochia ulmarioides f. brevipedunculata, commonly known as the short-stemmed melochia, is a variant of the melochia species characterized by its compact growth habit and shorter peduncles compared to the typical form.

It is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions, typically found in humid environments. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences. It produces spherical, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage.

The botanical features of this plant make it a notable subject in horticultural and ecological studies.
