Mastixia pentandra subsp. scortechinii

Mastixia pentandra subsp.

scortechinii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mastixia, is a shrub native to the eastern regions of Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or dry forest environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
