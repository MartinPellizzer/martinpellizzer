Mystroxylon pubescens

Mystroxylon pubescens, commonly known as the hairy wax tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, and often hairy leaves, which help reduce water loss in its arid or semi-arid habitat. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
