Myrcia thomasii

Myrcia thomasii, commonly known as Thomas's myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

Myrcia thomasii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
