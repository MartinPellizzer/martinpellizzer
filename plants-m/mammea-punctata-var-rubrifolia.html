Mammea punctata var. rubrifolia

Mammea punctata var. rubrifolia, commonly known as the red-leaved mammea, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or orange-red foliage, which contrasts with its green stems and branches. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
