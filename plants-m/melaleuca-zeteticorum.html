Melaleuca zeteticorum

Melaleuca zeteticorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to the coastal regions of eastern Australia, particularly in Queensland and New South Wales. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that appear in spikes. It is often found in sandy or coastal soils and is adapted to salt-laden environments.

Melaleuca zeteticorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh coastal conditions.
