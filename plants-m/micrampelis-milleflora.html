Micrampelis milleflora

Micrampelis milleflora, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for tropical gardens and greenhouses.
