Maxillaria gualaquizensis

Maxillaria gualaquizensis, commonly known as the Gualaquiza orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy floral display. The plant produces fragrant, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive spurred lip, typical of many Maxillaria species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
