Mycetanthe lowii

Mycetanthe lowii, commonly known as the Low's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent petals and a unique, ghost-like appearance that gives it its common name. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its flowers are small and pale, often resembling the shape of a ghost or a spirit.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Mycetanthe lowii is considered critically endangered and is highly sought after by botanists and conservationists.
