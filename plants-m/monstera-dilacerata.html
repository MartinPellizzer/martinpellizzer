Monstera dilacerata

Monstera dilacerata, commonly known as the裂叶天南星, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, deeply lobed, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant has a robust stem with aerial roots that allow it to climb trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage is often variegated with light green and white patterns, making it a popular houseplant.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, followed by spiny, berry-like fruits that contain toxic seeds.
