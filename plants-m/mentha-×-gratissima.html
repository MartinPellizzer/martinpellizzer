Mentha × gratissima

Mentha × gratissima, commonly known as the hybrid mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a cross between Mentha viridiflora and Mentha suaveolens, resulting in a plant that combines the characteristics of both parents. The plant produces opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a serrated margin. It blooms with small, pale blue or violet flowers that appear in whorls along the stems.

Mentha × gratissima is valued for its aromatic leaves and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
