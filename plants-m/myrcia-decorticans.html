Myrcia decorticans

Myrcia decorticans, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are a source of food for various wildlife species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Myrcia decorticans is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.
