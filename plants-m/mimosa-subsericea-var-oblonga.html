Mimosa subsericea var. oblonga

Mimosa subsericea var. oblonga, commonly known as the narrowleaf sensitive plant, is a species native to South America.

It is characterized by its bipinnately compound leaves that fold shut when touched, a trait known as thigmonastic response. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with slender, branching stems. Its small, yellow flowers appear in clusters and bloom in the spring.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
