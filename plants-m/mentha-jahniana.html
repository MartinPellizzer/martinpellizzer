Mentha jahniana

Mentha jahniana, commonly known as the Jahn's mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. Mentha jahniana is often found in rocky or forested habitats at high elevations.

It is valued for its aromatic leaves, which can be used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.
