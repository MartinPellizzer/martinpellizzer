Mentha longifolia var. chlorodictya

Mentha longifolia var. chlorodictya, commonly known as lemon mint, is a variant of the spearmint species characterized by its distinct lemony aroma.

It is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia, typically growing in moist, shaded environments. The plant features slender, upright stems and serrated leaves that are often a bright green color. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically pink or purple, appearing in clusters.

This variety is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
