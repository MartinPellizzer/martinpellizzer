Myrtoleucodendron densum

Myrtoleucodendron densum, commonly known as the dense-leaved myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and antiseptic properties.
