Magnolia denudata

Magnolia denudata, commonly known as the Japanese magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The tree has smooth, gray bark and elliptical, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its flowers are composed of numerous tepals and emit a sweet fragrance.

Magnolia denudata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
