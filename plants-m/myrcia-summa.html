Myrcia summa

Myrcia summa, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, red drupes that contain a single seed, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Myrcia summa is also valued for its essential oils, which are used in traditional medicine and perfumery.
