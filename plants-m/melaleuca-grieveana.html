Melaleuca grieveana

Melaleuca grieveana, commonly known as Grieve's tea tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern Australia, particularly in New South Wales and Queensland. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.

Melaleuca grieveana is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional Aboriginal medicine for its therapeutic properties.
