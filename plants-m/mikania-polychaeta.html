Mikania polychaeta

Mikania polychaeta, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a vigorous vine native to the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively, often forming dense mats. Its stems are hairy and can climb or trail along the ground, making it a common weed in disturbed areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and adaptability to various soil types.
