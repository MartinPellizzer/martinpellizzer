Melaleuca hamulosa

Melaleuca hamulosa, commonly known as the swamp melaleuca, is a shrub or small tree native to the wetlands of northern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous small, black seeds. It thrives in saline or waterlogged soils and is often found in coastal areas and swampy environments.

Melaleuca hamulosa plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for native wildlife.
