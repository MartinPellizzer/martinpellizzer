Mentha campicola

Mentha campicola, commonly known as the mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in late summer. Mentha campicola is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
