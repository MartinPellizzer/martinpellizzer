Musa elata

Musa elata, commonly known as the white banana, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, curved fruits that are typically white or cream in color. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance.

However, it is not typically used for food production compared to other banana species.
