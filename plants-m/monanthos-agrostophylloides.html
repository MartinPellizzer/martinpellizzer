Monanthos agrostophylloides

Monanthos agrostophylloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, acidic soils.

Due to its ecological importance, it is often found in restoration projects aimed at preserving wetland habitats.
