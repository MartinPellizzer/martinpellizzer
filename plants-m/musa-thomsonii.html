Musa thomsonii

Musa thomsonii, commonly known as the Thomson's banana, is a species of flowering plant in the family Musaceae.

It is native to the highlands of East Africa, particularly Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its large, elongated, and often curved leaves, which can reach up to 3 meters in length. It produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers, followed by elongated, edible fruits that are typically consumed raw.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in tropical gardens and botanical collections.
