Macrophthalma elastica

Macrophthalma elastica, commonly known as the rubber tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and ability to produce latex. The plant has a thick, woody stem that can grow quite tall, often reaching several meters in height. Its leaves are typically elliptical in shape and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

The rubber tree is widely cultivated for its latex, which is used in the production of rubber products.
