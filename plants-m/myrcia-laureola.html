Myrcia laureola

Myrcia laureola, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature.

Myrcia laureola is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
