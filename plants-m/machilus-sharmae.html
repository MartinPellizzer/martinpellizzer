Machilus sharmae

Machilus sharmae, commonly known as the Sharma's woodbine, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern China, particularly in Yunnan Province. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a glossy surface and a distinctive aromatic scent.

Machilus sharmae is often found in shaded, humid environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
