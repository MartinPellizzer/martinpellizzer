Myrtus brasiliana var. parkeriana

Myrtus brasiliana var. parkeriana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle or Parker's myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It features fragrant, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb. The plant produces small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by dark blue, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
