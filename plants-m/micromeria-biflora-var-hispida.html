Micromeria biflora var. hispida

Micromeria biflora var. hispida, commonly known as the hispid micromeria, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of tiny white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a compact growth habit and often grows in dry, rocky habitats. Its leaves are covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a rough texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
