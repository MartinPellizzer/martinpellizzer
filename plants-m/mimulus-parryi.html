Mimulus parryi

Mimulus parryi, commonly known as Parry's monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange or yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous regions. It has slender, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves.

Mimulus parryi is a perennial herb that plays an important role in supporting local pollinators.
