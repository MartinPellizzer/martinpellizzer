Mentha verdagueri

Mentha verdagueri, commonly known as the Verdaguer mint, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves and square stems typical of the mint family. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Mentha verdagueri is often used in traditional herbal remedies for its purported calming and digestive properties.
