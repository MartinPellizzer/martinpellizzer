Machilus foonchewii

Machilus foonchewii, commonly known as the Chinese-laurel, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its oval, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are dark blue berries that contain seeds, and the tree is often used in traditional medicine.

Machilus foonchewii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitats.
