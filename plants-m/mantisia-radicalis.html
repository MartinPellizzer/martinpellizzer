Mantisia radicalis

Mantisia radicalis, commonly known as the radical mantis plant, is a unique species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fern-like foliage and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant's roots are notably robust and can spread extensively, making it an effective ground cover in humid forests. Its flowers, which appear in clusters, are typically small and pale, adding a subtle aesthetic to its natural habitat.

Mantisia radicalis is also valued for its air-purifying properties and its role in traditional medicine.
