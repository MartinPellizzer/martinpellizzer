Mascagnia paniculata

Mascagnia paniculata, commonly known as the Florida mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in wetlands and along the edges of water bodies. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, yellow flowers with orange markings. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil in its wetland habitat.

Mascagnia paniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
