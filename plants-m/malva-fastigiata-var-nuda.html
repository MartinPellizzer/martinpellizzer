Malva fastigiata var. nuda

Malva fastigiata var. nuda, commonly known as the naked willow herb, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairless, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.
