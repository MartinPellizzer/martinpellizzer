Mimosa bimucronata subsp. hexandra

Mimosa bimucronata subsp.

hexandra, commonly known as the broad-leaved mimosa, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are composed of numerous small leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern.

The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its stems are typically thorny, and the plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
