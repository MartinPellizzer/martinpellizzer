Mulinum saniculifolium

Mulinum saniculifolium, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
