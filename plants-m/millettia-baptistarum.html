Millettia baptistarum

Millettia baptistarum, commonly known as the sacred fig or simply the fig tree, is a tropical plant belonging to the Moraceae family.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a broad, buttressed trunk and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers followed by edible fruits known as figs, which are encased in a fleshy, spiky structure. Millettia baptistarum is native to Southeast Asia and is widely cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
