Myrcia vittoriana

Myrcia vittoriana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces dark red, edible berries that are a food source for various birds and mammals. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it is sometimes considered a problematic species in certain regions.
