Moly latifolium

Moly latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf moly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. Moly latifolium is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
