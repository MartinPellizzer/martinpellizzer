Microcos grandifolia

Microcos grandifolia, commonly known as the largeflower microcos, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Microcos grandifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
