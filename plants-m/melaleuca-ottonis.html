Melaleuca ottonis

Melaleuca ottonis, commonly known as the Otto's melaleuca, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, bottlebrush-like flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a shrub-like appearance with smooth, often greyish bark. Its flowers, which are usually pink or red, bloom in winter and attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Melaleuca ottonis is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
