Melastoma novae-hollandiae

Melastoma novae-hollandiae, commonly known as the New Holland melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, berry-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
