Melica monantha

Melica monantha, commonly known as the single-flowered brome, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are linear and often have a rough texture.

Melica monantha is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil.
