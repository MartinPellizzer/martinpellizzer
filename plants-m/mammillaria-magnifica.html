Mammillaria magnifica

Mammillaria magnifica, commonly known as the Starry Pincushion Cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and dense, white to pale yellow spines that resemble stars. The plant features small, showy flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, orange, or red, typically in spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright sunlight, making it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care, it is often cultivated in arid environments or as a houseplant.
