Millefolium officinale

Millefolium officinale, commonly known as yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. Native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North America, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant contains various bioactive compounds, including flavonoids and essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects.
