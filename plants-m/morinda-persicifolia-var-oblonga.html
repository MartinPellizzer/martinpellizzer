Morinda persicifolia var. oblonga

Morinda persicifolia var. oblonga, commonly known as the Chinese date tree or Chinese moringa, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lanceolate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, orange-red fruits that contain a single seed, often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

The species is closely related to other Morinda species, sharing similar growth habits and chemical compositions.
