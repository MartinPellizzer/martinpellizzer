Mammillaria coryphides

Mammillaria coryphides, commonly known as the crown of thorns, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and dense clusters of white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features prominent radial spines and a short, cylindrical stem. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in arid gardens and as a houseplant.
