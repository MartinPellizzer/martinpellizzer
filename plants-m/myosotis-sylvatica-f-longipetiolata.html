Myosotis sylvatica f. longipetiolata

Myosotis sylvatica f. longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled forget-me-not, is a botanical variant of the common forget-me-not.

It is characterized by its elongated petioles, which give it a distinctive appearance compared to the standard variety. The plant produces clusters of delicate blue or purple flowers, typical of the Myosotis genus. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.

This form is valued for its unique foliage structure and is relatively easy to grow in moist, well-drained soils.
