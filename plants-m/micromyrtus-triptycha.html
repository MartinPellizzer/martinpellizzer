Micromyrtus triptycha

Micromyrtus triptycha, commonly known as the slender myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has small, aromatic leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. Its flowers are usually white or pink and are clustered in small groups, producing a subtle fragrance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
