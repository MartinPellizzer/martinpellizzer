Marsdenia glabra

Marsdenia glabra, commonly known as the smooth Marsdenia, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with five petals and a prominent corona. It produces small, fleshy berries that are yellow when ripe.

This plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in forests or along stream banks.
