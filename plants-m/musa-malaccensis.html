Musa malaccensis

Musa malaccensis, commonly known as the Malacca banana, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and clustered inflorescences. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or red when ripe. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is often cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
