Melothria costensis

Melothria costensis, commonly known as the coastal miracle fruit, is a climbing vine native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, yellow-orange fruits that turn red when ripe. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found near coastal areas. Its fruits are known for their intense, sweet flavor, which can be intensified when consumed with acidic foods.

Botanically, it belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, sharing some traits with other gourds and cucurbits.
