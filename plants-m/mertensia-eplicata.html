Mertensia eplicata

Mertensia eplicata, commonly known as bluebells, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.
