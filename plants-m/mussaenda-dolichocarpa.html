Mussaenda dolichocarpa

Mussaenda dolichocarpa, commonly known as the long-flowered Mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of showy, pale yellow or white flowers that are borne on long, slender pedicels. Its inflorescence is distinctive, with bract-like structures that are often mistaken for petals.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is cultivated in tropical gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
