Microcardamum

Microcardamum, commonly known as the lesser cardamom, is a small herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes other mustard family plants. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pods containing aromatic seeds. These seeds are used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to their pungent, spicy flavor.

Microcardamum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in temperate climates.
