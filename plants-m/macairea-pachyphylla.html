Macairea pachyphylla

Macairea pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved macairea, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, making it well adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its root system is fibrous and helps it anchor in poor, sandy soils.

Macairea pachyphylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and resilience.
