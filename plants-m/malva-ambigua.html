Malva ambigua

Malva ambigua, commonly known as the common mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton, hibiscus, and okra. The plant features palmately lobed leaves and produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom from summer to autumn. Its hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves are characteristic of the genus Malva.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in disturbed areas.
