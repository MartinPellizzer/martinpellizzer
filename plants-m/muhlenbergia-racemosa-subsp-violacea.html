Muhlenbergia racemosa subsp. violacea

Muhlenbergia racemosa subsp.

violacea, commonly known as violet muhlenbergia, is a grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and clusters of violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its foliage is fine and grass-like, with leaves that are often blue-green in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
