Marshallia pulchra

Marshallia pulchra, commonly known as the showy marshmallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and wetlands, and it has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Marshallia pulchra is also known for its ability to thrive in wet soils and its ornamental value in native plant gardens.
