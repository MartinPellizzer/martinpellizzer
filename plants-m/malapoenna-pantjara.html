Malapoenna pantjara

Malapoenna pantjara, commonly known as the Red Ghost Flower, is a strikingly beautiful plant native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to store water in its native dry environment.

Malapoenna pantjara is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
