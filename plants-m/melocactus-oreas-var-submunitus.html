Melocactus oreas var. submunitus

Melocactus oreas var. submunitus, commonly known as the Mexican dwarf melon cactus, is a compact, globular cactus native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It features a spiny, ribbed stem that can grow up to 15 centimeters in height, with a dense covering of white to pale yellow spines. The plant produces small, fragrant, white flowers during the summer months, typically at night. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus collections.

Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a desirable specimen for desert horticulture.
