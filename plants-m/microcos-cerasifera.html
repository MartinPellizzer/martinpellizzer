Microcos cerasifera

Microcos cerasifera, commonly known as the cherry plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, sweet-tart fruits resembling cherries, which are edible and often used in jams and desserts. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it has a compact growth habit that makes it suitable for ornamental gardens.

Microcos cerasifera is also valued for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and its resilience to drought conditions.
