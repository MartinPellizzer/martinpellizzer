Morisonia heydeana

Morisonia heydeana, commonly known as the Hawaiian starflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its star-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and mesic forests.

Morisonia heydeana is an important component of Hawaii's native flora and is adapted to the unique ecological conditions of the archipelago.
