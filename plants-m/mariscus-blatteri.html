Mariscus blatteri

Mariscus blatteri, commonly known as the Blatter's mariscus, is a rare and elusive plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and unique flowering structure, which consists of clustered, bell-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in humid forest understories.
