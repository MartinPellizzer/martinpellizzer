Matucana pallarensis

Matucana pallarensis, commonly known as the Pallaresia torch cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, green stem and the presence of spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically clustered at the top of the stem. Matucana pallarensis is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its thick stem.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and resilience.
