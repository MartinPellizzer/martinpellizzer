Mecodium subdemissum

Mecodium subdemissum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mecodium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that are typically pale purple to pink in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens.
