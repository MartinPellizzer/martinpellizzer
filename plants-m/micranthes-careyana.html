Micranthes careyana

Micranthes careyana, commonly known as the white sand gentian, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in pine flatwoods and wetlands. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Micranthes careyana is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
