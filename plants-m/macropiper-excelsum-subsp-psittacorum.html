Macropiper excelsum subsp. psittacorum

Macropiper excelsum subsp.

psittacorum, commonly known as the golden pipevine, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
