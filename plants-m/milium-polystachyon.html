Milium polystachyon

Milium polystachyon, commonly known as the hairy millet, is a flowering plant species native to Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Milium polystachyon is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
