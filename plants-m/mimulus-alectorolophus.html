Mimulus alectorolophus

Mimulus alectorolophus, commonly known as the golden whisker, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its scientific name reflects its distinctive appearance, with "alectorolophus" referring to the feather-like bracts that surround the flower cluster.

Mimulus alectorolophus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and conservation areas.
