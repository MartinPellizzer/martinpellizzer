Microstylis laevis

Microstylis laevis, commonly known as the smooth-styled grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of North America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers, typical of many grass species. Microstylis laevis thrives in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
