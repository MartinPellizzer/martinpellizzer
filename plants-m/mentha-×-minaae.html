Mentha × minaae

Mentha × minaae, commonly known as the African mint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Mentha piperita (peppermint) and Mentha × piperita (another peppermint variant).

It is native to regions of Africa and is characterized by its aromatic, square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue, arranged in whorls along the stem. Mentha × minaae is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and as a source of essential oils.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
