Miersia chilensis

Miersia chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean miersia, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of Chile.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a woody stem with thick, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the temperate climate of its native habitat.

Miersia chilensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine by local communities.
