Malpighia myrtifolia

Malpighia myrtifolia, commonly known as the Mexican hibiscus or red mallow, is a shrub native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, velvety flowers and elliptical, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Malpighia myrtifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
