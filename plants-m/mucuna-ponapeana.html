Mucuna ponapeana

Mucuna ponapeana, commonly known as the Pacific Cowpea, is a climbing legume native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands.

It produces distinctive, velvety pods that contain large, edible seeds. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, cream-colored flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas.

Mucuna ponapeana is valued for its nutritional seeds and its role in traditional agriculture and local cuisines.
