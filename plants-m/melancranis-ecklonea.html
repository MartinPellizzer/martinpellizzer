Melancranis ecklonea

Melancranis ecklonea, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It features fibrous roots and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of silvery-white trichomes, giving it its characteristic appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale blue, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
