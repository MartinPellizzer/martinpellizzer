Mauritia aculeata

Mauritia aculeata, commonly known as the Brazilian palm, is a tall, slender palm tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It features a slender, unbranched trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to three meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark blue, fleshy berries. It is adapted to humid, tropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local communities.
