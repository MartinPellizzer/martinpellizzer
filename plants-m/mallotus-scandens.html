Mallotus scandens

Mallotus scandens, commonly known as the climbing holly or Chinese holly, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or vine that can grow up to several meters in height, often climbing or trailing along supports. The plant features small, elliptical leaves with a leathery texture and a distinctive reddish or purple coloration on the underside. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, typically appearing in spring.

The fruit is a spherical, woody capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds.
