Manna caspica

Manna caspica, commonly known as Caspian manna, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It produces a sticky, resinous substance known as manna, which is harvested from its bark. The plant has pale green leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

The resin has been used historically for medicinal and culinary purposes.
