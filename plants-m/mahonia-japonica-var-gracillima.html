Mahonia japonica var. gracillima

Mahonia japonica var. gracillima, commonly known as the Japanese mahonia or slender mahonia, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features compound leaves that are alternate and often have a leathery texture, with yellowish-green foliage that turns reddish in autumn. The plant produces spiny, branched inflorescences that bear yellow flowers in late winter to early spring. These flowers are followed by blue-black berries that are attractive to birds.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in partial shade.
