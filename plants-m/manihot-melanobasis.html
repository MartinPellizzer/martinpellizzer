Manihot melanobasis

Manihot melanobasis, commonly known as the black manioc.

It is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil, and belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family. The plant is characterized by its dark purple to black bark and small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth texture.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for its roots, which can be processed into a starchy product similar to cassava.
