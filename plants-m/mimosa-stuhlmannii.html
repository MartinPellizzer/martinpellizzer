Mimosa stuhlmannii

Mimosa stuhlmannii, commonly known as the yellow mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different environments. It is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and clearings, where it can quickly colonize open spaces.

The flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
