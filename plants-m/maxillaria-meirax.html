Maxillaria meirax

Maxillaria meirax, commonly known as the showy maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or other structures in its natural habitat. Its leaves are dark green and strap-like, often arranged in a compact rosette.

The orchid produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in varying environmental conditions.
