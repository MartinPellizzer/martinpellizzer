Microchilus putumayoensis

Microchilus putumayoensis, commonly known as the Putumayo orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly in Colombia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and green. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

This orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.
