Meriania amplexicaulis

Meriania amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping meriania, is a striking tropical shrub native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with contrasting yellow centers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that often wrap around the stems, giving it a unique, almost vine-like appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Meriania amplexicaulis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
