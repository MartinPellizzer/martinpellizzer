Mertensia edelbergii

Mertensia edelbergii, commonly known as the bluebells of the desert, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soils. It is adapted to hot, dry climates and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Mertensia edelbergii plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native environment.
