Melastomastrum theifolium

Melastomastrum theifolium, commonly known as the thieving leaf melastoma, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red and purple. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are often branched and woody. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
