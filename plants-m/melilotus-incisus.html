Melilotus incisus

Melilotus incisus, commonly known as yellow melilot, is a flowering plant in the Fabaceae family.

It is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of western Asia. The plant is characterized by its slender stems and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet having a distinctive incised margin. It produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is often found in grasslands, disturbed soils, and along roadsides, and it is known for its aromatic foliage and use in traditional herbal medicine.
