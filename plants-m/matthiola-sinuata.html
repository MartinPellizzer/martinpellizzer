Matthiola sinuata

Matthiola sinuata, commonly known as the fringed carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, frilled petals that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and produces small, clustered flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Matthiola sinuata is also valued for its fragrance and is often used in perfumery.
