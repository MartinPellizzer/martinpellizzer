Malacothrix foliosa var. squalida

Malacothrix foliosa var. squalida, commonly known as the roughleaf malacothrix, is a variant of the malacothrix plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves that are often covered in fine, stiff trichomes. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent-like foliage. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in dense inflorescences, and may range in color from white to pale pink.

This variant is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features and ecological adaptations.
