Melica ciliata subsp. flavescens

Melica ciliata subsp.

flavescens, commonly known as the yellow beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, riverbanks, and wetlands.

The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green color. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
