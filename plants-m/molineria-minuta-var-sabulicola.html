Molineria minuta var. sabulicola

Molineria minuta var. sabulicola, commonly known as the sand flat sedge, is a small, herbaceous plant native to coastal and sandy habitats.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in dune environments or along shorelines. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.

Due to its habitat preferences, it plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and supporting local ecosystems.
