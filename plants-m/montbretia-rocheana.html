Montbretia rocheana

Montbretia rocheana, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the genus Hemerocallis, which includes daylilies, and is characterized by its showy, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves and produces seed pods that split open to release seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value and ease of cultivation have contributed to its popularity among gardeners.
