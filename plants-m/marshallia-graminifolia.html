Marshallia graminifolia

Marshallia graminifolia, commonly known as the grassleaf coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of small, yellow flowers with purple veins. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette at the base. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Marshallia graminifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
