Miconia caelata

Miconia caelata, commonly known as the "Brazilian beauty," is a striking tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy capsule that contains numerous seeds.

Miconia caelata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage and unique floral display.
