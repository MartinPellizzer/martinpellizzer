Mentha × subreversa

Mentha × subreversa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf peppermint, is a hybrid mint species resulting from the cross between Mentha piperita and Mentha viridis.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and a strong, sharp menthol scent. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is often used in herbal teas and aromatherapy due to its refreshing aroma and medicinal properties.

The plant's rapid growth and spreading habit make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, low-maintenance herb.
