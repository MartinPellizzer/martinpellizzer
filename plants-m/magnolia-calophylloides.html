Magnolia calophylloides

Magnolia calophylloides, commonly known as the Mexican magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
