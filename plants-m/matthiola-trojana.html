Matthiola trojana

Matthiola trojana, commonly known as the Trojan stock, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink, arranged in racemes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Matthiola trojana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and borders.
