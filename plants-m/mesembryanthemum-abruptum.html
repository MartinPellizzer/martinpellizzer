Mesembryanthemum abruptum

Mesembryanthemum abruptum, commonly known as the sharpleaf iceplant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, often hairy leaves and showy yellow or orange flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. It produces small, bead-like fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Mesembryanthemum abruptum is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal.
