Medinilla cucullata

Medinilla cucullata, commonly known as the hooded medinilla, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Medinilla cucullata is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
