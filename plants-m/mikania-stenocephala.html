Mikania stenocephala

Mikania stenocephala, commonly known as narrow-headed mile-a-minute, is a fast-growing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth rate, often reaching several meters in a short period. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its fibrous stems and ability to spread aggressively make it both ecologically significant and a potential invasive species in some areas.
