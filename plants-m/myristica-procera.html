Myristica procera

Myristica procera, commonly known as the nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and fragrant, reddish bark. The tree produces an aggregate fruit that contains the aromatic seeds used to make nutmeg and mace. Its botanical features include a tall stature, often reaching up to 30 meters, and a complex root system adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has cultural significance in traditional medicine.
