Malachra capitata var. microcalyx

Malachra capitata var. microcalyx, commonly known as the dwarf blueberry hibiscus, is a compact shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark blue or black when mature. Its leaves are ovate with a smooth texture and a glossy surface, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation in warm climates.
