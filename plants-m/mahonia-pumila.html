Mahonia pumila

Mahonia pumila, commonly known as the dwarf mahonia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with spiny margins and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
