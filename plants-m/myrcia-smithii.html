Myrcia smithii

Myrcia smithii, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, reddish-purple berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal forests and sandy soils.

Myrcia smithii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
