Mentha longifolia var. macrophyllos

Mentha longifolia var. macrophyllos, commonly known as the large-leaved mint, is a variant of the mint plant characterized by its broad, serrated leaves.

It is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that give it a distinctive minty fragrance.

This variety is often cultivated for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
