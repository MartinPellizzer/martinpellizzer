Mertensia laevigata var. brachycalyx

Mertensia laevigata var. brachycalyx, commonly known as the short-calyx bluebells, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, sky-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and wetland restoration projects.
