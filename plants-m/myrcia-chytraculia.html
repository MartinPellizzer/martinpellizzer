Myrcia chytraculia

Myrcia chytraculia, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, fragrant leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Myrcia chytraculia is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
