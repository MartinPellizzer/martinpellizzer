Molineria sulcata

Molineria sulcata, commonly known as the Sulcate Flaxflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern China.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow to greenish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Molineria sulcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local insect populations.
