Mertensia campanulata

Mertensia campanulata, commonly known as bluebells, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, sky-blue flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is often found in woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.

Mertensia campanulata is also a host plant for the endangered Karner blue butterfly.
