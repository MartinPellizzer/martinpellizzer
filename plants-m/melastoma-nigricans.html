Melastoma nigricans

Melastoma nigricans, commonly known as the black-stemmed melastoma, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by bright red or orange berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value and ecological role make it a notable species in its native habitat.
