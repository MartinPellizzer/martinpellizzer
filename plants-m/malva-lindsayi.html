Malva lindsayi

Malva lindsayi, commonly known as Lindsay's mallows, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are typically heart-shaped with soft, velvety textures. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Malva lindsayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local pollinators.
