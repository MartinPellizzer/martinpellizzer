Moraea atromontana

Moraea atromontana, commonly known as the dark mountain moraea, is a member of the Iridaceae family, native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils, often in mountainous regions. The plant features a short, underground rhizome and produces striking, tubular flowers in shades of purple and blue. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle.

Moraea atromontana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
