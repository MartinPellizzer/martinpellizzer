Myriophyllum verticillatum var. lacustre

Myriophyllum verticillatum var. lacustre, commonly known as watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant characterized by its whorled arrangement of narrow, ribbon-like leaves.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, with staminate and pistillate flowers occurring on separate plants. It is a perennial species that can form dense stands, influencing water quality and providing habitat for aquatic life.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread vegetatively make it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive in certain regions.
