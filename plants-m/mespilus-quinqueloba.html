Mespilus quinqueloba

Mespilus quinqueloba, commonly known as the five-lobed medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, five-lobed leaves that are typically grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by unique, fleshy fruits that ripen in the autumn. The fruit has a complex structure with a thick, leathery exterior and a soft, edible core.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
