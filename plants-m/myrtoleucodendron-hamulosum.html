Myrtoleucodendron hamulosum

Myrtoleucodendron hamulosum, commonly known as the coastal myrtle, is a rare and endemic plant species found primarily in the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale blue flowers. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to saline and wind-exposed environments. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it an important subject for botanical study.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
