Mentha sylvestris var. royleana

Mentha sylvestris var. royleana, commonly known as the Kashmir mint, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or pale blue in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its essential oils contain compounds like menthol and menthone, contributing to its cooling and soothing properties.
