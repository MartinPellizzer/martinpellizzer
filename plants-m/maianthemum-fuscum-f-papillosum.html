Maianthemum fuscum f. papillosum

Maianthemum fuscum f. papillosum, commonly known as the papillate black false Solomon’s seal, is a rare and distinctive variant of the black false Solomon’s seal.

It is characterized by its unique papillate (bumpy) texture on the leaves and stems, which sets it apart from typical forms of the species. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, typically blooming in late spring. Its foliage consists of narrow, dark green leaves that are often clustered at the base.

This botanical variant is of particular interest to botanists due to its unusual morphology and limited distribution.
