Mimosa papposa var. papposa

Mimosa papposa var. papposa, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces fluffy, white seed pods that release tiny seeds when disturbed, a trait that gives it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
