Masdevallia fenestralis

Masdevallia fenestralis, commonly known as the windowed masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, often translucent petals that resemble windows, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as a miniature orchid with a compact, pseudobulbous structure. Its flowers are usually small and come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and red.

It thrives in high humidity and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
