Myrcia paracatuensis

Myrcia paracatuensis, commonly known as the Paracatu myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes well-known genera such as Eucalyptus and Syzygium. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in clusters. Its leaves are usually elliptical and have a glossy surface, while the bark is smooth and varies in color from gray to reddish-brown.

This species is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid environments with well-drained soils.
