Mammillaria microcarpa f. auricarpa

Mammillaria microcarpa f. auricarpa, commonly known as the auricarped hedgehog cactus, is a compact, spineless cactus with a globular shape and a greenish-gray color.

It features small, rounded tubercles covered in white to pale blue hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It is native to Mexico and thrives in well-draining soil with minimal water.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and ease of care, making it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
