Maxillaria fritzii

Maxillaria fritzii, commonly known as the Fritz's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb that supports its growth and storage of nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Maxillaria fritzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
