Marginaria diversifolia

Marginaria diversifolia, commonly known as the variable marginaria, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that exhibit a wide range of shapes and sizes, contributing to its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom during the wet season. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
