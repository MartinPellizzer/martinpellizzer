Myrcia mikaniana

Myrcia mikaniana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Myrcia mikaniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
