Memecylon depressum

Memecylon depressum, commonly known as the low memecylon, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with dense, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.
