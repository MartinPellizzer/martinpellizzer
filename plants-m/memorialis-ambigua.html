Memorialis ambigua

Memorialis ambigua, commonly known as the uncertain memorial plant, is a rare flowering species native to the temperate regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its delicate, bi-pinnate leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland understories. Its botanical name reflects the ambiguity of its historical classification, which has been subject to debate among botanists.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered an endangered species in its natural range.
