Myrcia cordiformis

Myrcia cordiformis, commonly known as the heart-shaped myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

Myrcia cordiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
