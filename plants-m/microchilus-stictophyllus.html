Microchilus stictophyllus

Microchilus stictophyllus, commonly known as the blushing moss, is a small, delicate moss species that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright green foliage that often takes on a reddish tinge under certain environmental conditions. The plant produces tiny, spore-bearing capsules that are typically clustered at the tips of its stems. Microchilus stictophyllus is often found growing on rocks, soil, or decaying wood in temperate regions.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and sensitive indicator of undisturbed ecosystems.
