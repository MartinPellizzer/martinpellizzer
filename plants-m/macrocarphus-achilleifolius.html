Macrocarphus achilleifolius

Macrocarphus achilleifolius, commonly known as the common mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant grows upright, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and has a hairy stem covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The plant is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
