Monanthochloe littoralis

Monanthochloe littoralis, commonly known as the coastal grass, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of eastern North America.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils along shorelines and is adapted to salt spray and tidal conditions.
