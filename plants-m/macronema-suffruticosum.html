Macronema suffruticosum

Macronema suffruticosum, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are usually simple and opposite, with a rough texture.

The wood of Macronema suffruticosum is valued for its durability and has been used historically in construction and traditional crafts.
