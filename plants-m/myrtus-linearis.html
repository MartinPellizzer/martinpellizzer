Myrtus linearis

Myrtus linearis, commonly known as narrowleaf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are typically dark green and aromatic, with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red or black berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Myrtus linearis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional medicine and perfumery.
