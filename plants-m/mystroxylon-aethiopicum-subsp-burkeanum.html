Mystroxylon aethiopicum subsp. burkeanum

Mystroxylon aethiopicum subsp.

burkeanum, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to the savannas of sub-Saharan Africa. It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its rough, grey bark and elongated, oval leaves.

The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by fleshy, orange-colored fruits that contain a valuable nut known as shea butter. The plant is well-adapted to dry conditions and plays an important ecological and economic role in its native habitat.

Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of edible oil.
