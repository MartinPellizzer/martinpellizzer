Murraya foetidissima

Murraya foetidissima, commonly known as the stinking murraya, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its fragrant, small flowers and elliptical leaves. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits that are edible but have a strong, unpleasant odor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas.

Its ornamental value is limited due to its pungent scent, but it is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
