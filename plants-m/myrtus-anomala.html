Myrtus anomala

Myrtus anomala, commonly known as the common myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, dark green leaves and small, white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red or black when ripe. Myrtus anomala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.

Its essential oils are also valued for their aromatic and therapeutic properties.
