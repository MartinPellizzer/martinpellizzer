Myrceugenia apiculata

Myrceugenia apiculata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a small evergreen tree native to the coastal regions of southeastern Brazil.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal forests and sand dunes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in gardens and landscapes around the world.
