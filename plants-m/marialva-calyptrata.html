Marialva calyptrata

Marialva calyptrata, commonly known as the golden cupplant, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces striking yellow flowers arranged in a cup-like inflorescence, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
