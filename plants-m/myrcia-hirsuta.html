Myrcia hirsuta

Myrcia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy myrtle, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and dark, glossy leaves. Myrcia hirsuta is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
