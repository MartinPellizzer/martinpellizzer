Miconia foveolata

Miconia foveolata, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and covered with a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Miconia foveolata is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive in certain regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in tropical botany and ecological studies.
