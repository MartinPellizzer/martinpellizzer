Mutisia macrantha

Mutisia macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flower heads that are surrounded by pale green bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance. Mutisia macrantha typically grows in dry, open habitats and is known for its robust, woody stems.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
