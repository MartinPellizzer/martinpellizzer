Melochia degeneriana

Melochia degeneriana, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red-veined melochia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple vein pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a spherical, fleshy berry that turns red when mature.

Melochia degeneriana is often found in disturbed habitats and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
