Myosotis palustris subsp. sicula

Myosotis palustris subsp.

sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian marsh violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, preferring moist, nutrient-rich soils.

The plant features small, delicate blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
