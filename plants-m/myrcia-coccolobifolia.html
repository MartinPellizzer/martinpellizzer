Myrcia coccolobifolia

Myrcia coccolobifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian beauty or myrcia leaf, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous small, black seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in shaded, moist environments.

Myrcia coccolobifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
