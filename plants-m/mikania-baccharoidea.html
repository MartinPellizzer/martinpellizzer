Mikania baccharoidea

Mikania baccharoidea, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, showy flowers. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its fruit is a capsule that releases small seeds.

Mikania baccharoidea is often found in disturbed areas and can be invasive in certain environments.
