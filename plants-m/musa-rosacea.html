Musa rosacea

Musa rosacea, commonly known as the red banana, is a species of banana plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking reddish-purple bracts that surround the developing fruit, which is typically smaller and more elongated than common bananas. The plant produces clusters of berries that are edible and have a sweet, slightly tart flavor. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Musa rosacea is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant appearance.
