Marianthus procumbens var. diosmoides

Marianthus procumbens var. diosmoides, commonly known as the creeping marianth, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to tropical regions.

It features slender, prostrate stems that spread along the ground, often rooting at the nodes. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically arranged in clusters. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
