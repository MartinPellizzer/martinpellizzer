Mammillaria rhaphidacantha

Mammillaria rhaphidacantha, commonly known as the white-ribbed hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, radial spines and a distinctive rib structure. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for succulent collections.
