Muhlenbergia ramosa

Muhlenbergia ramosa, commonly known as the hairy muhlenbergia, is a perennial grass native to the United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and tufted growth habit, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense panicles. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.

Muhlenbergia ramosa is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
