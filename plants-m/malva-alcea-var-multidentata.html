Malva alcea var. multidentata

Malva alcea var. multidentata, commonly known as the multi-toothed mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features hairy, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive multi-toothed margin, giving it its specific epithet. The plant produces showy, pale pink to purple flowers with five petals, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or arid habitats.

Its fibrous stems and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their mild laxative and anti-inflammatory properties.
