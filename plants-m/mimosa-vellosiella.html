Mimosa vellosiella

Mimosa vellosiella, commonly known as the velvet mimosa, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that resemble those of other mimosa species. The plant produces small, yellow, spherical flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
