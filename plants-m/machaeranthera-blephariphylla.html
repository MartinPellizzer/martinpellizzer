Machaeranthera blephariphylla

Machaeranthera blephariphylla, commonly known as the eyelash bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features delicate, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert scrub habitats.
