Macleania rupestris

Macleania rupestris, commonly known as the rock mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a member of the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to full sun conditions. Its leaves are often hairy and have a distinctive shape, while its stems are covered in fine, white pubescence.

Macleania rupestris is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive flowers and ability to thrive in well-drained environments.
