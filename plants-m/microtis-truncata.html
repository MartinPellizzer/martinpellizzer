Microtis truncata

Microtis truncata, commonly known as the broad-leafed microtis, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal regions. It has a compact growth habit with narrow, strap-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Microtis truncata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
