Mammillaria parensis

Mammillaria parensis, commonly known as the Mexican nipple cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white or pale green spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Mammillaria parensis is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its compact size and unique appearance.
