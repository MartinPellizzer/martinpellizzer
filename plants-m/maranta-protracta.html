Maranta protracta

Maranta protracta, commonly known as the staggered arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family, which includes many ornamental and economically important species. The plant is characterized by its striking, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often variegated with silver and green patterns. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.

Its rhizomes are also used in traditional medicine and as a starch source.
