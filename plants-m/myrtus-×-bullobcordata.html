Myrtus × bullobcordata

Myrtus × bullobcordata, commonly known as the hybrid myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically dark blue or black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and coastal areas.

Myrtus × bullobcordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery and traditional medicine.
