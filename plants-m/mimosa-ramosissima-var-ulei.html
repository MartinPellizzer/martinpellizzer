Mimosa ramosissima var. ulei

Mimosa ramosissima var. ulei, commonly known as the velvet tamarack, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage that turns a striking reddish-brown in the fall. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by flat, bean-like pods. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in poor, well-drained soils.

This species is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for native wildlife.
