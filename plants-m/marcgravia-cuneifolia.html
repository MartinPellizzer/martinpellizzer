Marcgravia cuneifolia

Marcgravia cuneifolia, commonly known as the arrowleaf hibiscus, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive arrowhead shape, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often with a contrasting throat. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
