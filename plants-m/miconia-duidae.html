Miconia duidae

Miconia duidae, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit, is a member of the Melastomataceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of South America. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. Its fruit is a fleshy, star-shaped berry that contains numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, although it can be invasive in certain environments.
