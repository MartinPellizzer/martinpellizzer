Mammillaria elongata f. echinata

Mammillaria elongata f. echinata, commonly known as the spiny hedgehog cactus, is a compact, spineless cactus with a globular shape and a dense covering of white, woolly hairs.

It originates from the arid regions of Mexico and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that often branch at the base. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by pale green fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
