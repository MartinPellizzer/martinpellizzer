Myrrhis rosea

Myrrhis rosea, commonly known as rose campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, giving it a distinctive appearance. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Myrrhis rosea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
