Medicago distans

Medicago distans, commonly known as the Persian clover, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It is well adapted to dry and rocky soils, making it a hardy species that thrives in arid environments.

Medicago distans is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting other plants in the ecosystem.
