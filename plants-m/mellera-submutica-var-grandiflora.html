Mellera submutica var. grandiflora

Mellera submutica var. grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora variant of the submutica mellera, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and role in local ecosystems.
