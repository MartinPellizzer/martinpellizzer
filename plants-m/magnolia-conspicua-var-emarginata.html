Magnolia conspicua var. emarginata

Magnolia conspicua var. emarginata, commonly known as the emarginate magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The leaves are broad, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The tree typically grows to a height of 20-30 feet and has a rounded, spreading form.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
