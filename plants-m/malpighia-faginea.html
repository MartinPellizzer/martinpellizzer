Malpighia faginea

Malpighia faginea, commonly known as the purple granadilla, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pinkish-purple flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in vitamin C and have a tart, sweet flavor. These berries are often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
