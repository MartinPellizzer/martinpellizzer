Maxillaria barkeri

Maxillaria barkeri, commonly known as Barker's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in shaded, moist environments. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, with a distinctive lip that features intricate patterns.

Maxillaria barkeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a controlled environment.
