Mitwabochloa brunnea

Mitwabochloa brunnea, commonly known as the black-leafed grass, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing pale yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring. Mitwabochloa brunnea is adapted to cool, moist environments and thrives in well-drained soils.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
