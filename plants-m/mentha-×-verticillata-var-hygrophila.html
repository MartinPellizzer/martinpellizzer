Mentha × verticillata var. hygrophila

Mentha × verticillata var. hygrophila, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and dense clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. This variety thrives in moist environments such as wet meadows, stream banks, and marshy areas. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive minty fragrance.

The plant is often used in herbal medicine and as a culinary herb due to its refreshing properties.
