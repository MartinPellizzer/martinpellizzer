Melica minuta subsp. arrecta

Melica minuta subsp.

arrecta, commonly known as the slender brome, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and stream banks.

The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster, with small, wind-pollinated flowers.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
