Monochoria sagittata

Monochoria sagittata, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in tropical and subtropical regions.

It features distinctive arrow-shaped leaves that are usually floating or partially submerged, with a waxy surface that helps repel water. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly in suitable habitats, making it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive.

Monochoria sagittata plays a role in water filtration and provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.
