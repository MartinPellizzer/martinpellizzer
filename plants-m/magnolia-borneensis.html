Magnolia borneensis

Magnolia borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean magnolia, is a species native to the tropical rainforests of Borneo.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its large, fragrant, white to pink flowers. The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces woody capsules as fruit, which contain numerous seeds.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental value and ecological importance.
