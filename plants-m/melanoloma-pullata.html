Melanoloma pullata

Melanoloma pullata, commonly known as the purple-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet and green foliage, with flowers that display a unique combination of purple and white petals. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as under forest canopies. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, organic soil.

Melanoloma pullata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
