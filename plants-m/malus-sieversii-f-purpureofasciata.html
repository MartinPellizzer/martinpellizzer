Malus sieversii f. purpureofasciata

Malus sieversii f. purpureofasciata, commonly known as the purple-striped apple, is a botanical variant of the wild apple species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive purplish streaks on the bark and leaves, which give it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and a dark green upper surface.

The flowers are white with pinkish tinges, and the fruit is a small, tart apple that matures in late summer.
