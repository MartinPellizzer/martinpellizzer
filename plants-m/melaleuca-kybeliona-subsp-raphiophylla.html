Melaleuca kybeliona subsp. raphiophylla

Melaleuca kybeliona subsp.

raphiophylla, commonly known as the broad-leaved melaleuca, is a shrub native to the eastern regions of Australia. It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow to broad lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white or pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in coastal or sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal dunes. It produces woody capsules that contain numerous small, hard seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
