Malope malacoides var. hispida

Malope malacoides var. hispida, commonly known as the hairy yellow catchfly, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and dry meadows.
