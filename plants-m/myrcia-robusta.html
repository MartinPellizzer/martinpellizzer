Myrcia robusta

Myrcia robusta, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible but often have a spicy or peppery flavor. Myrcia robusta is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to drought conditions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
