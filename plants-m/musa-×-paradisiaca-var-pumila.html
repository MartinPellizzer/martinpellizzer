Musa × paradisiaca var. pumila

Musa × paradisiaca var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf banana plant, is a compact variety of the banana plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its shorter stature, typically reaching heights of less than 3 meters, making it suitable for smaller spaces. The plant produces small, edible bananas that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety is also valued for its resilience to certain environmental conditions compared to larger banana cultivars.
