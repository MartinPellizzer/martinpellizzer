Mimulus cardinalis var. rigens

Mimulus cardinalis var. rigens, commonly known as the scarlet monkeyflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, bright orange to red tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It is characterized by its erect stem and clusters of flowers at the tips of the branches.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
