Mimulus glabratus var. parviflorus

Mimulus glabratus var. parviflorus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, bright orange to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
