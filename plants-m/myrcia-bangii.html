Myrcia bangii

Myrcia bangii, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that contains several seeds.

Myrcia bangii is also noted for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
