Modecca apiculata

Modecca apiculata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rock heath, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly waxy texture, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Modecca apiculata is often found in xeric environments, such as sandy or limestone soils, and is adapted to full sun exposure.
