Mentha hylodes

Mentha hylodes, commonly known as the Brazilian mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive minty aroma, while its flowers are small and tubular, often purple or pink in color.

Mentha hylodes is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies for its antiseptic and cooling effects.
