Megastigma galeottii

Megastigma galeottii, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing vine native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

The flowers typically open at dusk and close by dawn, making it a unique addition to gardens.
