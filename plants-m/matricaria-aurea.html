Matricaria aurea

Matricaria aurea, commonly known as the golden marguerite, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are surrounded by white ray florets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Matricaria aurea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal remedies.
