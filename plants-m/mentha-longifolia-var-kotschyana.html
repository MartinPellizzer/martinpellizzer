Mentha longifolia var. kotschyana

Mentha longifolia var. kotschyana, commonly known as Turkish mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly fuzzy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers arranged in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
