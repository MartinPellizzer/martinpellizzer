Mammillaria pyrrhacantha

Mammillaria pyrrhacantha, commonly known as the red-spined hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is covered in white to pale green spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its spiny exterior. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
