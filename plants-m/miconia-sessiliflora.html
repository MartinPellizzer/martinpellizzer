Miconia sessiliflora

Miconia sessiliflora, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Miconia* tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and floral display.

However, it can be invasive in certain regions, posing a challenge to local ecosystems.
