Melaleuca linearis var. linearis

Melaleuca linearis var. linearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paperbark, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, linear leaves and smooth, flaky bark that peels in strips. The plant produces small, bottle-shaped fruits that contain numerous seeds, and it blooms with clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in heathlands and coastal scrub.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
