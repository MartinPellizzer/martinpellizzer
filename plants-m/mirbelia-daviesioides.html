Mirbelia daviesioides

Mirbelia daviesioides, commonly known as the pink wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, woody growth and bright pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Mirbelia daviesioides is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
