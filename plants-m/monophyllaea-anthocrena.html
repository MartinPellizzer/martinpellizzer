Monophyllaea anthocrena

Monophyllaea anthocrena, commonly known as the silverleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and bright yellow flower heads that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it suitable for xeriscaping.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
