Melaleuca scalena

Melaleuca scalena, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paperbark, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its smooth, flaky bark that peels away in strips. The plant produces small, bottle-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, which appear in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and the plant is often found in coastal or wetland areas.

Melaleuca scalena is adapted to a variety of soil types and is commonly used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and hardiness.
