Matricaria trifurcata

Matricaria trifurcata, commonly known as the three-branched chamomile, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow central disk. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil in full sun. Its leaves are alternate, hairy, and have a strong, aromatic scent.

Matricaria trifurcata is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and ornamental value.
