Monardella siskiyouensis

Monardella siskiyouensis, commonly known as Siskiyou mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streamside areas. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in whorls and range in color from white to pale purple.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
