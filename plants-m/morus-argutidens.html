Morus argutidens

Morus argutidens, commonly known as the Chinese mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that develop into clusters of juicy, edible berries. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This species is widely cultivated for its fruit and as a source of fiber for paper and textiles.
