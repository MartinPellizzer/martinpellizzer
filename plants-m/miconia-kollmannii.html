Miconia kollmannii

Miconia kollmannii, commonly known as the Brazilian glory flower, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets make it an invasive species in some regions.

The plant's ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens, though its ecological impact remains a concern.
