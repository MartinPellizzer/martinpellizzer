Myrcia basicordata

Myrcia basicordata, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several small, edible seeds.

Myrcia basicordata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
