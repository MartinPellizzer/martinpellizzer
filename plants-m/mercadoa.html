Mercadoa

Mercadoa, commonly known as the market bean, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. Its growth habit includes climbing or trailing vines, making it suitable for trellises or support structures.

Mercadoa also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting soil fertility in agricultural settings.
