Medicago truncatula var. tentaculata

Medicago truncatula var. tentaculata, commonly known as the Turkish clover, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features trifoliate leaves with rounded or heart-shaped leaflets and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is characterized by its short, creeping stems and is often found in dry, rocky soils. It is a legume, capable of fixing nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and use in ecological restoration projects.
