Malpighia linearis

Malpighia linearis, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to red berries that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with simple, alternate leaves that are linear to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small, clustered, and often white or pink, and it produces edible fruits that are popular in local cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant fruit and foliage.
