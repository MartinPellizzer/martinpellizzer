Miconia thyrsiflora

Miconia thyrsiflora, commonly known as the Christmas miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains numerous seeds.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is considered a problematic species in some regions.
