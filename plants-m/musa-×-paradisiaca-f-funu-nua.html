Musa × paradisiaca f. funu-nua

Musa × paradisiaca f. funu-nua, commonly known as the Funu-nua banana, is a cultivar of the banana plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its medium-sized, elongated fruit with a yellow to orange peel and a sweet, soft pulp. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves and grows as a herbaceous perennial. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its flavor and is often cultivated for local consumption and trade.
