Mentha pulegium var. cotorensis

Mentha pulegium var. cotorensis, commonly known as cotorensis mint, is a variant of the pennyroyal plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant leaves and clusters of tiny, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, sharing traits with other mint species like its aromatic oils and square stems.

However, it is less commonly cultivated than other mint varieties due to its specific habitat requirements.
