Myristica cumaru

Myristica cumaru, commonly known as Brazilian nut or *Bertholletia excelsa*, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, smooth, grayish-brown bark and long, pendulous fruits that resemble pine cones. The tree produces a unique seed, the Brazil nut, encased in a hard shell within a three-chambered fruit. The plant has a complex root system and thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil.

It plays a vital ecological role in the rainforest, providing food for various wildlife species.
