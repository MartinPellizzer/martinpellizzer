Mentha viridis var. maderensis

Mentha viridis var. maderensis, commonly known as Mader's mint, is a variant of the green mint species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and serrated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Mentha viridis var. maderensis is valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental or culinary purposes.
