Maxillaria confusa

Maxillaria confusa, commonly known as the confused maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces clusters of fragrant, violet to purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in the summer, with showy, spurred petals that attract pollinators. Its leaves are strap-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Maxillaria confusa is relatively easy to cultivate, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
