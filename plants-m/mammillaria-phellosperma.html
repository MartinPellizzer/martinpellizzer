Mammillaria phellosperma

Mammillaria phellosperma, commonly known as the golden mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that are typically pale yellow to green in color. The plant produces small, white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its compact size and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
