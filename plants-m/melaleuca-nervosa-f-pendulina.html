Melaleuca nervosa f. pendulina

Melaleuca nervosa f. pendulina, commonly known as the weeping tea tree, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its pendulous, drooping branches that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, bottle-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in horticulture for its unique form and flowering display.
