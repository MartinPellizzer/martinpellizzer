Moquiniastrum cordatum

Moquiniastrum cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf moquiniastrum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, giving it a distinctive appearance that sets it apart from other sedge species.
