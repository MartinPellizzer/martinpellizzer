Melica lappacea

Melica lappacea, commonly known as slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It typically grows in moist environments such as meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of spikelets, each containing two to three small, awned florets.

Melica lappacea is valued for its role in ecological systems and is sometimes used in herbal medicine.
