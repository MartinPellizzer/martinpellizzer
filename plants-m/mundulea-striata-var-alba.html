Mundulea striata var. alba

Mundulea striata var. alba, commonly known as white-stripped acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive white or pale yellow flowers that appear in dense, globular clusters. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, narrow leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for various pollinators.
