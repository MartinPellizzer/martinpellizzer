Monotaxis tenuis

Monotaxis tenuis, commonly known as the slender swamp rush, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often submerged or partially submerged in water. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters near the water's surface. Monotaxis tenuis thrives in nutrient-rich, shallow waters and plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important indicator species for wetland health and biodiversity.
