Melhania quercifolia

Melhania quercifolia, commonly known as the oak-leaved melhania, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that resemble those of an oak tree, giving it its common name. Its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers are typically pink or purple and bloom in clusters.
