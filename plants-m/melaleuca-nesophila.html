Melaleuca nesophila

Melaleuca nesophila, commonly known as the slender tea tree, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in winter. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Melaleuca nesophila is valued for its aromatic foliage and is used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.
