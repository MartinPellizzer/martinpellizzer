Mikania guaco

Mikania guaco, commonly known as Guaco, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests. Its stems are covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, inconspicuous fruits.

Mikania guaco has been used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
