Muraltia pilosa

Muraltia pilosa, commonly known as the hairy muraltia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its leguminous structure and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with hairy surfaces, giving it its common name, and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental or for its medicinal properties.
