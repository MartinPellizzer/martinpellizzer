Manilkara multinervis subsp. sylvestris

Manilkara multinervis subsp.

sylvestris, commonly known as the wild manilkara, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with prominent veins.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval, amber-colored fruits that are edible. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its latex has been traditionally used for various purposes, including as a natural adhesive.
