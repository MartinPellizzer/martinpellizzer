Myrica javanica

Myrica javanica, commonly known as the Java wax tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries.

Myrica javanica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
