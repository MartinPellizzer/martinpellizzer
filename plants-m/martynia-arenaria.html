Martynia arenaria

Martynia arenaria, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas. It has linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Martynia arenaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
