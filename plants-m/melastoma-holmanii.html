Melastoma holmanii

Melastoma holmanii, commonly known as the Holman's melastoma, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its showy, often violet or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a rough texture, while its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Melastoma holmanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
