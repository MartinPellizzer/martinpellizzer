Madia elegans subsp. typica

Madia elegans subsp.

typica, commonly known as the slender madia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaves with a distinctive, velvety texture.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal practices, though its medicinal properties remain under study.
