Mentha × haynaldiana

Mentha × haynaldiana, commonly known as the hybrid mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Mentha longifolia (spearmint) and Mentha aquatica (watermint), exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. Mentha × haynaldiana is often found in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland edges.

Its aromatic leaves and adaptability make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary uses.
