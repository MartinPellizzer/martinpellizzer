Mesodiscus prolifer

Mesodiscus prolifer, commonly known as the creeping redweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with numerous branched stems that spread along the ground. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Mesodiscus prolifer is often found in disturbed soils and is adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions.
