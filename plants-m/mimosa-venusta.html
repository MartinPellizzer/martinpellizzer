Mimosa venusta

Mimosa venusta, commonly known as the golden mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow flowers. The plant is often grown for its ornamental value due to its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of bright yellow blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Mimosa venusta is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
