Machaerium bolivianum

Machaerium bolivianum, commonly known as the Bolivian machaerium, is a perennial leguminous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often arranged in racemes. It is typically found in moist, tropical or subtropical habitats and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
