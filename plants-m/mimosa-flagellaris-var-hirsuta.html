Mimosa flagellaris var. hirsuta

Mimosa flagellaris var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy mimosa, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It features small, fern-like leaves that are covered in fine, hairy trichomes, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, spherical seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or sandy habitats.

Its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed sites make it both ecologically significant and sometimes invasive.
