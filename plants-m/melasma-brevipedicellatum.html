Melasma brevipedicellatum

Melasma brevipedicellatum, commonly known as the short-stemmed moss, is a species of moss characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form.

It typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant's leaves are small, ovate, and often have a smooth or slightly serrated margin. Its reproductive structures, known as capsules, are borne on short, erect stems called pedicels.

Melasma brevipedicellatum plays a vital role in soil stabilization and provides microhabitat for various invertebrates.
