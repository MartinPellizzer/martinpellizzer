Mischocarpus sundaicus

Mischocarpus sundaicus, commonly known as the Sunda nut or yellow mangostin, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by bright red, fleshy fruits that contain multiple seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The fruit is valued for its aromatic pulp and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
