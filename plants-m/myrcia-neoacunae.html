Myrcia neoacunae

Myrcia neoacunae, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and glossy, dark green leaves. It typically grows in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Myrcia neoacunae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
