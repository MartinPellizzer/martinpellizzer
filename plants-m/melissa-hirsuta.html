Melissa hirsuta

Melissa hirsuta, commonly known as hairy lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features serrated, hairy leaves that are often fragrant when crushed, emitting a citrusy scent. It produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Melissa hirsuta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and mild calming properties in herbal teas.
