Mariscus riparius var. trisumbellatus

Mariscus riparius var. trisumbellatus, commonly known as the three-umbel marsh cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its basal leaves with five lobes and its distinctive inflorescence consisting of three umbels of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its adaptability to fluctuating water levels and is an important component of wetland ecosystems.
