Medinilla subumbellata

Medinilla subumbellata, commonly known as the velvet tangerine.

It is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Medinilla subumbellata is also valued for its ability to produce edible fruits, which have a tangy, citrus-like flavor.
