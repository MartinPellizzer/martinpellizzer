Micromeria thymoides

Micromeria thymoides, commonly known as coastal thyme or sand thyme, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It is a low-growing shrub with aromatic, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal climates with high salinity and wind exposure.

Micromeria thymoides is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
