Maschalanthe

Maschalanthe, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Maschalanthe is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant color and adaptability to various growing conditions.

It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
