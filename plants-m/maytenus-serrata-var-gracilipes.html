Maytenus serrata var. gracilipes

Maytenus serrata var. gracilipes, commonly known as the slender mayten, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and elliptical, leathery leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elongated, woody fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
