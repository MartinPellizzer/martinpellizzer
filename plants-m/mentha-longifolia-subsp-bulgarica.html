Mentha longifolia subsp. bulgarica

Mentha longifolia subsp.

bulgarica, commonly known as Bulgarian spearmint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its essential oils, rich in menthol and other volatile compounds, contribute to its distinct minty fragrance.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its use in culinary and medicinal applications.
