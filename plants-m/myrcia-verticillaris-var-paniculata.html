Myrcia verticillaris var. paniculata

Myrcia verticillaris var. paniculata, commonly known as the panicled myrcia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible but often have a sharp, acidic taste. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental flowers make it a valued plant in horticulture and traditional medicine.
