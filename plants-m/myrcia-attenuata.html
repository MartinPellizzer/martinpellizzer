Myrcia attenuata

Myrcia attenuata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with smooth, reddish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Myrcia attenuata is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
