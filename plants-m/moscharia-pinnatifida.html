Moscharia pinnatifida

Moscharia pinnatifida, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a perennial aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often branched and bear small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from the base of the plant.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters, and are pollinated by wind or insects.
