Mussaenda flava

Mussaenda flava, commonly known as golden flame or yellow mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that are arranged in terminal inflorescences, giving it a striking visual appearance. Its botanical name, Mussaenda, is derived from the Latin word "musa" meaning "banana," likely referring to its banana-like leaves.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
