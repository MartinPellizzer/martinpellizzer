Medicago laciniata var. diffusa

Medicago laciniata var. diffusa, commonly known as the broadleaf medick, is a low-growing perennial legume native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with broad, rounded lobes and produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, where it plays a role in nitrogen fixation.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a useful plant in ecological restoration and landscaping.
