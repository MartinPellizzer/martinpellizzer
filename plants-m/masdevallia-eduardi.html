Masdevallia eduardi

Masdevallia eduardi, commonly known as the Eduardo's masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit and produces intricate, often colorful flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. The plant typically blooms during the cooler months, showcasing a range of hues including pink, purple, and white. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Masdevallia eduardi requires high humidity and indirect light to thrive, making it a popular choice for greenhouse cultivation.
