Madhuca sessiliflora

Madhuca sessiliflora, commonly known as the wax apple or butter fruit, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy on top and dull underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellow to orange fruits with a waxy coating. Its fruit is widely used in traditional medicine and as a food source in various regions.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in diverse soil conditions.
