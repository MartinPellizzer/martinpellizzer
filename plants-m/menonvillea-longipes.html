Menonvillea longipes

Menonvillea longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or forested areas. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, adapted to the cool, moist conditions of its habitat.

Menonvillea longipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
