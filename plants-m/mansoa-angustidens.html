Mansoa angustidens

Mansoa angustidens, commonly known as the narrowleaf mansoa, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can be dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread in suitable environments.

Mansoa angustidens is also noted for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
