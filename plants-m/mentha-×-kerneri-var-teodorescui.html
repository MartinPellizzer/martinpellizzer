Mentha × kerneri var. teodorescui

Mentha × kerneri var. teodorescui, commonly known as the Teodorescu mint, is a hybrid mint species native to the Carpathian Mountains in Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. This variety is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and use in culinary applications. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its essential oils contain compounds like menthol and rosmarinic acid, contributing to its refreshing scent and therapeutic uses.
