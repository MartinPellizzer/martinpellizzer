Milium hispidum

Milium hispidum, commonly known as hairy millet, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, white to pale green flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Milium hispidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
