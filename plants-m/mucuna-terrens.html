Mucuna terrens

Mucuna terrens, commonly known as the terrestrial mucuna, is a climbing leguminous plant native to tropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain flattened, dark brown seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Mucuna terrens is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and traditional use in agricultural systems.
