Mitrephora grandiflora

Mitrephora grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered mitrephora, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes cashew and mango. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and have serrated margins. It produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.
