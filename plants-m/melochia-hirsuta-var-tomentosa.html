Melochia hirsuta var. tomentosa

Melochia hirsuta var. tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy melochia, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by berry-like fruits that are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along forest edges.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and resilience.
