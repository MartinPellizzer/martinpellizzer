Mimosa contortuplicata

Mimosa contortuplicata, commonly known as the twisted mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is known for its sensitive leaves that fold shut when touched, a response triggered by rapid changes in turgor pressure. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in various parts of the world.
