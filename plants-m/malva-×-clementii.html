Malva × clementii

Malva × clementii, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves and showy, pale pink to lavender flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. Its hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves contribute to its distinctive appearance.

Malva × clementii is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
