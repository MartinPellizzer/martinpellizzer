Mertensia beyrichiana

Mertensia beyrichiana, commonly known as bluebells, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, white down. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Mertensia beyrichiana is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
