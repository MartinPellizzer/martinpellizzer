Malpighia salicifolia

Malpighia salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf acerola, is a shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its willow-like leaves and small, red, edible fruits. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its flowers are usually white or pink, and the plant is known for its ornamental value as well as its fruit, which is rich in vitamin C. It is often cultivated for both its aesthetic appeal and its nutritional benefits.
