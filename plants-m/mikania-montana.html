Mikania montana

Mikania montana, commonly known as mountain morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as woodland edges and stream banks.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
