Macaranga bancana

Macaranga bancana, commonly known as the white-veined macaranga, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent white veins. The plant is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and can grow up to 10 meters in height. Its latex is milky and copious, which is a common feature among members of its family.

The leaves are frequently used in traditional practices and are also a host plant for certain butterfly species.
