Mikania stevensiana

Mikania stevensiana, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a fast-growing vine native to the Americas.

It has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple. The plant is known for its ability to spread rapidly, often forming dense mats that can dominate vegetation. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered a weed in many regions.
