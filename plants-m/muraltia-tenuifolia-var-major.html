Muraltia tenuifolia var. major

Muraltia tenuifolia var. major, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pink passionflower, is a tropical vine characterized by its climbing habit and showy, pinkish-white flowers.

It features pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and its fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that resembles a cherry. The plant is native to South America and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
