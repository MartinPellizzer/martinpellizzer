Minuartia wettsteinii subsp. parnonia

Minuartia wettsteinii subsp.

parnonia, commonly known as the Pannonian sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils, often found in open habitats such as steppes and rocky outcrops.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its succulent stems and showy floral displays.
