Myosotis ramificata

Myosotis ramificata, commonly known as the branched forget-me-not, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling small bells. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stems, which are often branched, giving the species its common name.

Myosotis ramificata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
