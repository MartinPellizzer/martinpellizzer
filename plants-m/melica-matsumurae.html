Melica matsumurae

Melica matsumurae, commonly known as the Matsumura's brome, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, while the inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle.

Melica matsumurae is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and limited distribution.
