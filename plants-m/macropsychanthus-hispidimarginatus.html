Macropsychanthus hispidimarginatus

Macropsychanthus hispidimarginatus, commonly known as the hairy-margin desert bell, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant has hairy margins on its leaves, which help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is adapted to survive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
