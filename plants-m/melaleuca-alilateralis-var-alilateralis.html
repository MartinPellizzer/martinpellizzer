Melaleuca alilateralis var. alilateralis

Melaleuca alilateralis var. alilateralis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf paperbark, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and distinctive greyish-white, papery bark that peels in thin layers. The plant produces small, bottle-shaped flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and semi-arid regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its hardy nature.
