Malaxis atrata

Malaxis atrata, commonly known as the black orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers, which are often mistaken for fungi due to their appearance. The plant has a slender, wiry stem and produces small, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Malaxis atrata is a rare and endangered species, requiring specific conditions for successful growth and reproduction.
