Methysticum

Methysticum, commonly known as kava, is a perennial shrub native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant is cultivated for its roots, which contain compounds responsible for its sedative and calming effects. The active ingredients, primarily kavalactones, are extracted through a process involving soaking and grinding the root.

Methysticum plays an important role in traditional Polynesian culture and is used in social and ceremonial contexts.
