Malva caroliniana var. neglecta

Malva caroliniana var. neglecta, commonly known as the Carolina mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and disturbed soils. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a fuzzy texture and produces showy pink to purple flowers in the summer months. Its flowers are composed of five petals and are arranged in a radial symmetry, typical of the Malvaceae family.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to environmental stress.
