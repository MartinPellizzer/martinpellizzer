Miconia trichocalyx

Miconia trichocalyx, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces showy, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release winged seeds.

Miconia trichocalyx is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain environments.
