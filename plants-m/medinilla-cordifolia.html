Medinilla cordifolia

Medinilla cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf medinilla, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Medinilla cordifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
