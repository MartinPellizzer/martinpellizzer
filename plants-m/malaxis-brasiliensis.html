Malaxis brasiliensis

Malaxis brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has a sympodial growth habit. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient uptake.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
