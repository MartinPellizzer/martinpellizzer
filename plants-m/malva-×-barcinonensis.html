Malva × barcinonensis

Malva × barcinonensis, commonly known as the Barcelona mallow, is a hybrid species within the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It has been cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and flower structure.

Its hybrid origin contributes to its adaptability and genetic diversity.
