Myrtus zenkeri

Myrtus zenkeri, commonly known as the Zanzibar myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its dense, fragrant foliage and small white or pinkish flowers that appear in clusters. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and have a distinct aromatic scent when crushed.

Myrtus zenkeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
