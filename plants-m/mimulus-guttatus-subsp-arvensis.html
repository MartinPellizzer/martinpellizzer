Mimulus guttatus subsp. arvensis

Mimulus guttatus subsp.

arvensis, commonly known as the field monkeyflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to western North America. It typically grows in moist habitats such as streambanks, wet meadows, and disturbed soils.

The plant features showy, bright orange to yellow flowers with two lips, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered an invasive plant in non-native regions.
