Myrcia pauciflora var. rubiginosa

Myrcia pauciflora var. rubiginosa, commonly known as the red-myrtle, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its small, dark red to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to the plant. The leaves are glossy and elliptical, often with a slightly fragrant scent. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is also valued for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
