Monstera barrieri

Monstera barrieri, commonly known as the Split-leaf Philodendron, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that develop distinctive notches and splits as the plant matures. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its aerial roots allow it to climb and support itself on structures in its natural habitat.

Monstera barrieri is a popular houseplant due to its striking foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.
