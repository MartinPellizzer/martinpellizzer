Malus marmorea

Malus marmorea, commonly known as the variegated crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its striking, mottled leaves that display a mix of white, green, and pink hues, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible, tart fruits that are often used in jams and jellies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its unique foliage, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
