Magnolia zoquepopolucae

Magnolia zoquepopolucae, commonly known as the Zoque popoluca magnolia, is a rare and endemic species native to the tropical rainforests of southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a distinct aromatic scent.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for conservation and study.
