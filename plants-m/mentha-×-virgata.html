Mentha × virgata

Mentha × virgata, commonly known as hybrid mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a hybrid between Mentha viridis and Mentha pulegium, resulting in a plant with a distinctive, strongly aromatic scent. The leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and often have a grayish-white underside, while the flowers are small, tubular, and typically lavender or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed sites.

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in herbal remedies and aromatherapy.
