Muraltia empetroides

Muraltia empetroides, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or empetrum, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, showy pink or white flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Muraltia empetroides is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
