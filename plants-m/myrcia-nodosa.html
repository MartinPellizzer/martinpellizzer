Myrcia nodosa

Myrcia nodosa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, red or orange berries. The plant has smooth, dark green leaves and produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often found in coastal areas and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

Myrcia nodosa is also noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional remedies.
