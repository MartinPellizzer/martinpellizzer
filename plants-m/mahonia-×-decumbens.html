Mahonia × decumbens

Mahonia × decumbens, commonly known as the creeping mahonia, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features compound leaves with spiny margins and a distinctive yellow flower cluster that blooms in late spring. The plant produces blue-black berries that are a food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Its hardy nature makes it suitable for a variety of soil types and climates.
