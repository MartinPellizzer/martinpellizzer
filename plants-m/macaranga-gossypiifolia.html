Macaranga gossypiifolia

Macaranga gossypiifolia, commonly known as the Indian mallow or woolly mallow, is a tropical plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments in Southeast Asia. Its latex is milky and can be used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in supporting local biodiversity by providing habitat and food for various insects.

The species is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
