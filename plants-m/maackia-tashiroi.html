Maackia tashiroi

Maackia tashiroi, commonly known as the Japanese maackia, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and typical pod fruits. The plant features compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers in early spring. Its fruit is a slender, dehiscent pod that splits open to release seeds.

Maackia tashiroi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
