Manihot esculenta subsp. diffusa

Manihot esculenta subsp.

diffusa, commonly known as the African arrowroot, is a starchy tuberous plant native to tropical Africa. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, swollen underground stems that store nutrients.

The plant produces a flowering stem with small, greenish-yellow flowers, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers are used as a source of carbohydrates and have a mild, sweet flavor.

The plant is also valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its potential in sustainable agriculture.
