Manglesia trilobata

Manglesia trilobata, commonly known as the three-lobed manglesia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
