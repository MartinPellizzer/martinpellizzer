Myriotheca sorbifolia

Myriotheca sorbifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf rockrose, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Myriotheca sorbifolia is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Its drought tolerance makes it a suitable plant for xeriscaping in dry regions.
