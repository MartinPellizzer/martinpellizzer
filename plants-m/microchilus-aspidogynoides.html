Microchilus aspidogynoides

Microchilus aspidogynoides, commonly known as the common rock orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. The plant features a slender, wiry stem with a single, lance-shaped leaf. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and borne in a loose raceme, with a distinctive hooded labellum.

It is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in harsh, arid environments.
