Menispermum rotundifolium

Menispermum rotundifolium, commonly known as the roundleaf Dutchman's pipe, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, tubular flowers that resemble a pipe. The plant produces yellowish-green berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
