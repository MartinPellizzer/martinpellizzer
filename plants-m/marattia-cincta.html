Marattia cincta

Marattia cincta, commonly known as the golden chain fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Marattiaceae and is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or along stream banks. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it is valued for its ornamental appearance.

Marattia cincta plays an important ecological role by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various organisms.
