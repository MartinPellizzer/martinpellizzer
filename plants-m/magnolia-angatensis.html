Magnolia angatensis

Magnolia angatensis, commonly known as the Philippine magnolia, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

The flowers are fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance in its native habitat.
