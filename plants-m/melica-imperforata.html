Melica imperforata

Melica imperforata, commonly known as the smooth meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically smooth and lack prominent veins or serrations. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Melica imperforata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
