Monarda oblongata

Monarda oblongata, commonly known as narrowleaf horsemint, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, or purple, typically blooming from June to September. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a minty scent when crushed, contributing to its common name.

Monarda oblongata is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full shade.
