Monarda fistulosa var. fistulosa

Monarda fistulosa var. fistulosa, commonly known as wild bergamot, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features showy, tubular, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has aromatic, hairy leaves that are often used in herbal teas. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
