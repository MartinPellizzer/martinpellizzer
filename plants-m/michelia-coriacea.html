Michelia coriacea

Michelia coriacea, commonly known as the rough-leaf michelia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are covered in a rough, bark-like texture. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Michelia coriacea is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and floral display.
