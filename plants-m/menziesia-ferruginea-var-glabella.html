Menziesia ferruginea var. glabella

Menziesia ferruginea var. glabella, commonly known as the smoothleaf alpine honeysuckle, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its reddish-brown stems and showy, fragrant flowers make it a distinctive species within the Ericaceae family.

It plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife and is often found in montane and subalpine regions.
