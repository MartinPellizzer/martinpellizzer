Momordica cylindrica

Momordica cylindrica, commonly known as the bitter melon, is a tropical vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, ridged fruit that turns from green to orange as it matures. The plant produces yellow flowers and has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its fruit contains numerous seeds enclosed in a fibrous pulp.

Bitter melon is widely cultivated for its edible fruit, which is used in various cuisines and traditional medicine.
