Macrosiphonia pinifolia f. setosa

Macrosiphonia pinifolia f. setosa, commonly known as the hairy pine leaf lichen, is a foliose lichen that exhibits a distinctive botanical structure.

It typically forms a flat, leaf-like thallus with a greenish to yellowish upper surface and a darker, often more granular lower surface. The plant is characterized by its dense, branched, and often hairy growth, which is a key feature of the 'setosa' form. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on the bark of coniferous trees.

Its morphology and growth pattern make it an important indicator species in forest ecosystems.
