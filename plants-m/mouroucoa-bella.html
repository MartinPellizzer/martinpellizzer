Mouroucoa bella

Mouroucoa bella, commonly known as the golden bell orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are long and narrow, adapted for efficient water absorption in its natural habitat.

Mouroucoa bella is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or warm climates.
