Mentzelia asperula

Mentzelia asperula, commonly known as the rough blazingstar, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with hairy, often glandular stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Mentzelia asperula is often found in dry, sandy or rocky soils in open habitats like deserts and grasslands.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and its ability to thrive in arid environments.
