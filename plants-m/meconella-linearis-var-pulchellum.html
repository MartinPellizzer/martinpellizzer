Meconella linearis var. pulchellum

Meconella linearis var. pulchellum, commonly known as the slender meconella, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, this plant is adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils. It is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks, where it contributes to local biodiversity.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it prefers full sun and well-drained soil.
