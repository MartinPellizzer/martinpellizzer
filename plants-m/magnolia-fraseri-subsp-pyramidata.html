Magnolia fraseri subsp. pyramidata

Magnolia fraseri subsp.

pyramidata, commonly known as the Fraser magnolia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy.

The plant produces showy, white, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. Its botanical features include a smooth, grayish bark and a tendency to form a dense, pyramidal shape.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
