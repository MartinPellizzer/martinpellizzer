Myrciaria longicaudata

Myrciaria longicaudata, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dark, spiny stems and dense, thorny foliage. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically black when ripe, with a sweet and aromatic flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade.

Myrciaria longicaudata is valued for its edible fruits and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its distinctive appearance.
