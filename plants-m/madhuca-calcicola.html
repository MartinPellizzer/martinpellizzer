Madhuca calcicola

Madhuca calcicola, commonly known as the limestone nut tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils. The plant has simple, alternate, elliptical leaves that are usually leathery in texture. Its flowers are small and yellowish, arranged in clusters, and it produces edible, chestnut-like fruits.

The tree is valued for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
