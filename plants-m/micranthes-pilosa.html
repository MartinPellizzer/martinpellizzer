Micranthes pilosa

Micranthes pilosa, commonly known as the hairy mountain sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and hairy stems, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils in woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Micranthes pilosa is part of the Polygonaceae family, known for its distinctive seed capsules and often edible leaves.
