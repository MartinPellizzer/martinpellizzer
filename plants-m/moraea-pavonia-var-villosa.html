Moraea pavonia var. villosa

Moraea pavonia var. villosa, commonly known as the woolly moraea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often with a distinctive red or purple throat. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it its "woolly" appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a dramatic visual impact to the landscape.
