Madhuca tsangii

Madhuca tsangii, commonly known as the Chinese tallow tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, elliptic leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, where it can become invasive.

The tree is also valued for its timber and has traditional uses in Chinese medicine.
