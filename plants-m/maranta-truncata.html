Maranta truncata

Maranta truncata, commonly known as the zigzag plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage that often displays vibrant red, orange, and yellow patterns. The plant features arrow-shaped leaves with a distinctive zigzag margin, which gives it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Maranta truncata is known for its ability to produce small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.
