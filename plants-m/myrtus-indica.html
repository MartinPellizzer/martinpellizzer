Myrtus indica

Myrtus indica, commonly known as the common myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark green, aromatic leaves and small white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically harvested when they turn red or purple. Myrtus indica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.

Its essential oils are also valued for their aromatic and antimicrobial properties.
