Manihot gracilis

Manihot gracilis, commonly known as the slender manioc, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its upright, slender stem and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its roots are the primary part used, containing high levels of starch and used in various culinary preparations.

However, the roots must be properly processed to remove toxic compounds before consumption.
