Mutisia grossedentata

Mutisia grossedentata, commonly known as the yellow dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and forest edges.

Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.
