Machilus japonicus

Machilus japonicus, commonly known as Japanese Machilus, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, with a pleasant, spicy aroma. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible but not widely cultivated.

Machilus japonicus is often valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
