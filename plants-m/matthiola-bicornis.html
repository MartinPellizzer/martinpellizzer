Matthiola bicornis

Matthiola bicornis, commonly known as the two-horned stock, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its biennial or perennial growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in racemes. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Matthiola bicornis is cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
