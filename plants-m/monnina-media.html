Monnina media

Monnina media, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features slender, erect stems that produce small, blue-violet flowers clustered in dense, mist-like inflorescences. The plant has alternate, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Monnina media is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
