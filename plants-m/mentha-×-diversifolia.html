Mentha × diversifolia

Mentha × diversifolia, commonly known as the hybrid spearmint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a cross between Mentha viridis (ovalleaf mint) and Mentha spicata (spearmint), resulting in a plant with distinctive physical characteristics. The leaves are lance-shaped, slightly serrated, and have a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This plant is often cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and flavoring.
