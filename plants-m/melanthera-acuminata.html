Melanthera acuminata

Melanthera acuminata, commonly known as the sharpleaf panicledury, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance.

Melanthera acuminata is typically found in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows.
