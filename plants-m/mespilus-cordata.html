Mespilus cordata

Mespilus cordata, commonly known as the white medlar, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces small, green fruits that ripen to a yellowish color in autumn. The fruit has a unique, aromatic pulp surrounded by a tough, inedible husk.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
