Melocactus prolifer

Melocactus prolifer, commonly known as the fissurated melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a dramatic visual contrast to its otherwise muted appearance. Its growth is slow, and it often forms clumps through a process called basal offsets, which contribute to its unique, clustered shape.

The plant is well adapted to extreme drought conditions, making it a resilient and hardy choice for xerophytic gardens.
