Microcerasus prostrata

Microcerasus prostrata, commonly known as the creeping Chinese hawthorn, is a low-growing shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Due to its compact growth habit, it is suitable for rock gardens and as a ground cover.
