Mentha longifolia subsp. procera

Mentha longifolia subsp.

procera, commonly known as narrowleaf mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, often with a woody base.

The leaves are lance-shaped, slightly hairy, and have a strong, minty aroma. It produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
