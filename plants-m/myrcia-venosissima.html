Myrcia venosissima

Myrcia venosissima, commonly known as the veiny myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
