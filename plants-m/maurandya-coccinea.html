Maurandya coccinea

Maurandya coccinea, commonly known as the scarlet flax or crimson flax, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often hairy, with a slightly heart-shaped base.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.
