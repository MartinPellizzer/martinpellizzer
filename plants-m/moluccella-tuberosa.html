Moluccella tuberosa

Moluccella tuberosa, commonly known as the tuberous bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces tuberous roots that store nutrients, allowing it to survive in various environmental conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in many regions beyond its native habitat.
