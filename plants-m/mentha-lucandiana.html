Mentha lucandiana

Mentha lucandiana, commonly known as Mexican mint or wild mint, is a perennial herb native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often fragrant. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from summer to fall. Mentha lucandiana is widely used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and antiseptic properties.

Its aromatic leaves are also used in culinary applications and as a natural insect repellent.
