Myrcia ambigua var. sylvatica

Myrcia ambigua var. sylvatica, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but often have a strong, aromatic flavor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Myrcia ambigua var. sylvatica is also notable for its role in supporting local wildlife, particularly birds and insects.
