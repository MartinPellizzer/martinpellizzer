Malus dasyphylla f. flore-plena

Malus dasyphylla f. flore-plena, commonly known as the double-flowered crabapple, is a cultivar of the crabapple tree characterized by its abundant, showy flowers.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to China, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant features a rounded, compact growth habit with dense foliage and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its flowers, which are typically pink or white, bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge, adding visual interest to gardens and landscapes.
