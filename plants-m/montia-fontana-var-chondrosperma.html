Montia fontana var. chondrosperma

Montia fontana var. chondrosperma, commonly known as the golden montia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Montiaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are prostrate or ascending.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude conditions.
