Miliusa globosa

Miliusa globosa, commonly known as the globe mulberry, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes other fruit-bearing trees like the fig and mulberry. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and broad, palmate leaves that are often lobed. It produces small, greenish flowers followed by spherical, fleshy fruits that turn red when mature.

The fruit is edible and contains a single seed, making it a valuable plant in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
