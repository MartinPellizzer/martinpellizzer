Malaxis latisepala

Malaxis latisepala, commonly known as the broad-sepal Malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers with prominent, broad sepals. It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors.

Malaxis latisepala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
