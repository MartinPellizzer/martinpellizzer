Medicago fischeriana

Medicago fischeriana, commonly known as Fischer's clover, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces pods containing small, round seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Medicago fischeriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
