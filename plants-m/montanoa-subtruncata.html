Montanoa subtruncata

Montanoa subtruncata, commonly known as the Mexican coffee plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

Montanoa subtruncata is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine.
