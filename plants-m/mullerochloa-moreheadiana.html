Mullerochloa moreheadiana

Mullerochloa moreheadiana, commonly known as Morehead's muhly, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and produces fluffy, pinkish-white seed heads that resemble clouds. This plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in open woodlands and along the edges of wetlands.

Mullerochloa moreheadiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in moist environments.
