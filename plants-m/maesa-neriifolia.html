Maesa neriifolia

Maesa neriifolia, commonly known as the black-leaf maesa, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and small, bell-shaped white flowers. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The berries it produces are edible and have been used in traditional medicine for their astringent properties.
