Mediocereus

Mediocereus, commonly known as the "Mexican moon cactus," is a genus of cacti native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and spiny, areolate structure. The plant typically produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the summer months. Mediocereus species are often cultivated as ornamental plants due to their unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

They thrive in well-draining soil and require full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
