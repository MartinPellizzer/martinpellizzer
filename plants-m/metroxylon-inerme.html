Metroxylon inerme

Metroxylon inerme, commonly known as the wild areca palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family.

It is a slender, single-stemmed palm native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant features pinnate leaves that are dark green and often have a waxy texture. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and it produces edible fruits similar to those of the areca palm.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
