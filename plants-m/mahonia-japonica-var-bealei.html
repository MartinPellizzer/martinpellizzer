Mahonia japonica var. bealei

Mahonia japonica var. bealei, commonly known as Beale's Mahonia, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features compound leaves with spiny margins and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and seasonal interest throughout the year.
