Monardella antonina subsp. benitensis

Monardella antonina subsp.

benitensis, commonly known as the Benito's wild bergamot, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States. It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, tubular pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

Its subspecies status highlights its distinct ecological and botanical characteristics compared to other Monardella species.
