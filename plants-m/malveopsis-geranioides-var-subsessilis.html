Malveopsis geranioides var. subsessilis

Malveopsis geranioides var. subsessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallow, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has palmately lobed leaves with a hairy texture, and its stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both ornamental and ecological gardening.
