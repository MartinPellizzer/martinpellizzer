Malaxis distans

Malaxis distans, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique floral structure, with sepals and petals that are often similar in shape. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
