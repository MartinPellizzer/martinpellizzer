Mentha egerensis

Mentha egerensis, commonly known as Hungarian mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale purple to blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Its aromatic leaves contain high levels of menthol, giving it a strong minty scent.

Mentha egerensis is often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.
