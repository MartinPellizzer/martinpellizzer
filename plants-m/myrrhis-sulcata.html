Myrrhis sulcata

Myrrhis sulcata, commonly known as the narrowleaf sweetflag, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants such as carrots and parsley. The plant features slender, often hollow stems and fern-like, compound leaves that are typically aromatic. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

Myrrhis sulcata is often found in moist, calcareous soils along streams and in wet meadows.
