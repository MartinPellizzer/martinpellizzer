Mentha gentilis var. hortensis

Mentha gentilis var. hortensis, commonly known as peppermint, is a hybrid species of the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly serrated leaves and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Peppermint is a perennial herb that spreads through rhizomes and is often cultivated for its essential oils.

Its strong, cooling scent makes it a popular choice for culinary and medicinal uses.
