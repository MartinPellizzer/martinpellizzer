Microchilus plantagineus

Microchilus plantagineus, commonly known as the slender rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and small, pale green or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. It is known for its compact size and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its native range.
