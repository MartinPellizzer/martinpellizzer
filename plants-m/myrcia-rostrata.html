Myrcia rostrata

Myrcia rostrata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal and lowland rainforest regions.

Myrcia rostrata is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
