Melampodium canescens

Melampodium canescens, commonly known as the grayleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained conditions, making it a hardy ornamental plant.
