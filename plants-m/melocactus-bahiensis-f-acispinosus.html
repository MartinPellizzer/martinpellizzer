Melocactus bahiensis f. acispinosus

Melocactus bahiensis f. acispinosus, commonly known as the Bahia melon cactus, is a distinctive cactus species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its swollen, pear-shaped stem that often develops a yellowish or reddish hue as it matures. The plant produces a single, large, yellow to orange flower that appears at the top of the stem during the warmer months. Its spines are typically short and dense, providing protection from herbivores.

This variety is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
