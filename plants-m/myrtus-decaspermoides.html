Myrtus decaspermoides

Myrtus decaspermoides, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and have a fragrant scent when crushed. Its small, white flowers are followed by dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Myrtus decaspermoides is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
