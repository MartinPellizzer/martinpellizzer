Mimosa radula var. radula

Mimosa radula var. radula, commonly known as the coastal mimosa, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows in sandy or coastal soils and is often found in dune environments. It has a shallow root system that helps stabilize sand dunes.

The species is also known for its ability to tolerate salt spray and wind, making it well adapted to coastal habitats.
