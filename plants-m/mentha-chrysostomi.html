Mentha chrysostomi

Mentha chrysostomi, commonly known as the golden mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite leaves that are usually gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in terminal clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Mentha chrysostomi is often found in rocky soils and dry habitats, thriving in full sun to partial shade.

It is valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in herbal teas or as a ornamental plant.
