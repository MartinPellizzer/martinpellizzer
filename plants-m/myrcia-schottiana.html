Myrcia schottiana

Myrcia schottiana, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

It is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical and subtropical forests.
