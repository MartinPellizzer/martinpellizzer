Mammillaria rhodantha var. neglecta

Mammillaria rhodantha var. neglecta, commonly known as the Neglecta Mammillaria, is a compact cactus species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It features a globular stem with white to pale green ribs, adorned with small, rounded tubercles that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne on short, lateral pedicels. It is a slow-growing species that requires minimal water and well-draining soil to thrive.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
