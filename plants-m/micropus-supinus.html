Micropus supinus

Micropus supinus, commonly known as the prostrate milkweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its milky sap, which is a common feature among plants in the milkweed family. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, disturbed areas.

Micropus supinus plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
