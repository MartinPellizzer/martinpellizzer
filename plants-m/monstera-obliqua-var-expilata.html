Monstera obliqua var. expilata

Monstera obliqua var. expilata, commonly known as the Mexican monstera, is a tropical plant native to the forests of Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that develop deep splits as the plant matures. The leaves are typically a deep green with a slightly variegated pattern, though variegation is less common in this variety. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

This plant is popular as a houseplant due to its striking foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
