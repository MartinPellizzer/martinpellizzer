Mentha viridis var. lejeuneana

Mentha viridis var. lejeuneana, commonly known as the lemon mint, is a variant of the green mint plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and bright green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often used in culinary applications for its refreshing, citrusy aroma and flavor.

Botanically, it is closely related to other Mentha species and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
