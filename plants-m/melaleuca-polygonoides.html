Melaleuca polygonoides

Melaleuca polygonoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, bottle-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces a fragrant essential oil, which is commonly used in aromatherapy and skincare products. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.

Melaleuca polygonoides is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native habitats.
