Melicytus microphyllus

Melicytus microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaved melicytus, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
