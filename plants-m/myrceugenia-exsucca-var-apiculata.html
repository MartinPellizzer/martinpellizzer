Myrceugenia exsucca var. apiculata

Myrceugenia exsucca var. apiculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a species native to southern Brazil.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces dark blue to black berries that are edible for birds and small mammals. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in coastal areas and humid forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
