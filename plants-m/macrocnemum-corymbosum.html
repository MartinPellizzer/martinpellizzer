Macrocnemum corymbosum

Macrocnemum corymbosum, commonly known as the clustered grassvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, clustered inflorescences that produce pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows, and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and it produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds.

Macrocnemum corymbosum is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to temperate climates.
