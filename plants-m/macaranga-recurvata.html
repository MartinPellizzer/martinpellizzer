Macaranga recurvata

Macaranga recurvata, commonly known as the curl-leaf macaranga, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub that typically grows in lowland rainforests. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive curled appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing yellowish or greenish blooms.

The species is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
