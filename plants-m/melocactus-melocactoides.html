Melocactus melocactoides

Melocactus melocactoides, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a species native to the dry regions of Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a distinctive red or purple coloration, especially when exposed to sunlight. The plant features a spiny, segmented stem and produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments.

Melocactus melocactoides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
