Meryta rivularis

Meryta rivularis, commonly known as the river meryta, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows near streams and moist areas, thriving in humid, temperate climates. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval or elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, often white or pale green, and it produces small, fleshy berries as fruit.

Meryta rivularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological systems of its native habitat.
