Malva berteroniana

Malva berteroniana, commonly known as the Mexican mallow, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like hibiscus and cotton. The plant features showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are heart-shaped with a soft, velvety texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
