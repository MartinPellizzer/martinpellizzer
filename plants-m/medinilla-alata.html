Medinilla alata

Medinilla alata, commonly known as the hanging medinilla, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous, showy flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has thick, leathery leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Medinilla alata is popular as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
