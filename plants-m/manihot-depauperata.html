Manihot depauperata

Manihot depauperata, commonly known as the poor man's cassava, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, green stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which can be propagated for new growth. It is often cultivated for its potential medicinal properties and as a source of starch.

Due to its hardy nature, it thrives in disturbed soils and is adaptable to various environmental conditions.
