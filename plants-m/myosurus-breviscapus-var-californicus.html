Myosurus breviscapus var. californicus

Myosurus breviscapus var. californicus, commonly known as the California short-capped sedge, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. Its botanical characteristics include a short rhizome and a compact, low-growing form.

It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
