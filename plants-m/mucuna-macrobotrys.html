Mucuna macrobotrys

Mucuna macrobotrys, commonly known as the sacred bean or velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces large, pod-like fruits that contain numerous flat, dark brown seeds. Mucuna macrobotrys is known for its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in agroforestry systems.

It also contains various alkaloids, which have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
