Monsonia glandulosissima

Monsonia glandulosissima, commonly known as the glandular monsonia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous regions. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a glandular surface, giving it its scientific name. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in dense inflorescences, and are often pink or purple in color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
