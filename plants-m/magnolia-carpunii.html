Magnolia carpunii

Magnolia carpunii, commonly known as the Japanese magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves appear. The tree has simple, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with a slight peeling texture.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
