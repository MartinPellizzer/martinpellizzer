Mitracarpus micranthus

Mitracarpus micranthus, commonly known as the dwarf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiny fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
