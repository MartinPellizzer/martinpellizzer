Moricandia arvensis var. garamantum

Moricandia arvensis var. garamantum, commonly known as the Garamantian bittercress, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual with slender stems and small, pale green leaves. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides.

The plant is known for its peppery taste and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
