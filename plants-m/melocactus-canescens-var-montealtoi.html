Melocactus canescens var. montealtoi

Melocactus canescens var. montealtoi, commonly known as the Monte Alban melocactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, slow-growing cactus with a globular or columnar shape and a dark green to bluish-gray stem. The plant is characterized by its dense covering of white to grayish spines and lacks areoles, a distinguishing feature from other melocactus species. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.
