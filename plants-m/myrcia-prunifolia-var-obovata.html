Myrcia prunifolia var. obovata

Myrcia prunifolia var. obovata, commonly known as the obovate myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical to obovate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in moist forests or near streams.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic properties, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
