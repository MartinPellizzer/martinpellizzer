Mayepea intermedia

Mayepea intermedia, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a shrub native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, violet-blue berries that appear in late summer. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Mayepea intermedia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
