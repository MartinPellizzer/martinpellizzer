Musa × champa

Musa × champa, commonly known as the Chinese banana, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Musaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and produces clusters of yellow, edible fruits. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and is often cultivated for its sweet, starchy fruit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The botanical features of Musa × champa make it an important crop in tropical agriculture.
