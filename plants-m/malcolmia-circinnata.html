Malcolmia circinnata

Malcolmia circinnata, commonly known as the blueweed or sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. The plant has a bushy growth habit with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
