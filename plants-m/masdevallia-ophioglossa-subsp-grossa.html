Masdevallia ophioglossa subsp. grossa

Masdevallia ophioglossa subsp.

grossa, commonly known as the giant masdevallia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in length, with a distinctive greenish-yellow coloration.

The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its pseudobulbs are robust and segmented, supporting the plant's growth and storage of nutrients.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
