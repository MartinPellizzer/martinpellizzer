Malva abutiloides

Malva abutiloides, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety down. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
