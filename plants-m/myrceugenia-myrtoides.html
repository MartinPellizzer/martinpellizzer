Myrceugenia myrtoides

Myrceugenia myrtoides, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that contains a single seed.

Myrceugenia myrtoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
