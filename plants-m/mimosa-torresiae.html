Mimosa torresiae

Mimosa torresiae, commonly known as the Torresia mimosoid, is a shrub native to the island of Socotra.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves that are bipinnate. The plant produces small, yellow, globular flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in spring. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to its harsh native habitat.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
