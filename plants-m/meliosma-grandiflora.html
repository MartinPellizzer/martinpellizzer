Meliosma grandiflora

Meliosma grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered meliosma, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a smooth texture. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by distinctive, winged fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Meliosma grandiflora is often found in woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
