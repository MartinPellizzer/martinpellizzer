Mammillaria santaclarensis

Mammillaria santaclarensis, commonly known as the Santa Clara nipple cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and dense covering of white to yellow spines. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appearance, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in arid gardens.
