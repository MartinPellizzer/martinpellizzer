Mitrephora reticulata var. tenuifolia

Mitrephora reticulata var. tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mitrephora, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes cashew and mango trees. The plant is characterized by its slender, elliptical leaves with a distinct reticulate venation pattern. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, sweet-sour fruits.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in traditional medicine.
