Medicago pentacycla var. longeaculeata

Medicago pentacycla var. longeaculeata, commonly known as the longpod needlepod, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated pods and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It has small white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes used in forage or erosion control due to its hardy nature.
