Michelia pubinervia

Michelia pubinervia, commonly known as the hairy-veined michelia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Magnoliaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate, and ovate leaves with hairy veins, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Michelia pubinervia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
