Manihot trichandra

Manihot trichandra, commonly known as the African arrowroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its roots are tuberous and are primarily used for their starchy content, which is utilized in food preparation.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional medicinal uses in some regions.
