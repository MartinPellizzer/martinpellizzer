Mentha umbrosa

Mentha umbrosa, commonly known as shadowmint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces aromatic leaves that can be used in herbal teas.

Mentha umbrosa is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional remedies.
