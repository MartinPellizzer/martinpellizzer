Monochilus lepidus

Monochilus lepidus, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, woolly leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Monochilus lepidus is a low-growing perennial that is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
