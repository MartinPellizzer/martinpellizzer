Merremia crispatula

Merremia crispatula, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive hairy texture and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in warm, humid environments. Its seeds are enclosed in rough, spiny pods that split open when mature.

Merremia crispatula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
