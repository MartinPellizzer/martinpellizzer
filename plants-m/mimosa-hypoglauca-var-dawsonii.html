Mimosa hypoglauca var. dawsonii

Mimosa hypoglauca var. dawsonii, commonly known as Dawson's mimosa, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are typically light green with a slight bluish tinge. The plant produces small, yellow, globular flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to sandy soils and thrives in coastal areas with high salinity.

The species is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen, making it a valuable plant for soil stabilization.
