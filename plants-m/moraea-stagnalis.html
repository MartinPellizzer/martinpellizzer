Moraea stagnalis

Moraea stagnalis, commonly known as the marsh moraea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. The plant features upright, slender stems with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom from late summer to autumn.

Moraea stagnalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with moist soil conditions.
