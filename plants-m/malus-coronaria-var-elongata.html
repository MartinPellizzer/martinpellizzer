Malus coronaria var. elongata

Malus coronaria var. elongata, commonly known as the elongated crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, edible fruits that turn red or yellow in the fall. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn vibrant red in autumn.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
