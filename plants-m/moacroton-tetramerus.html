Moacroton tetramerus

Moacroton tetramerus, commonly known as the four-branch cycad, is a rare and ancient plant species belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, compound leaves. The plant produces cones, with male and female cones typically occurring on separate plants, a feature known as dioecy. Its slow growth rate and long lifespan make it a unique subject in botanical studies.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Moacroton tetramerus is considered endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
