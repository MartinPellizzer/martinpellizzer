Mirabilis Linearis Var. Subhispida

Mirabilis linearis var. subhispida is a unique variant of the common four o'clock plant known for its striking, elongated leaves and vibrant flowers. This plant thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its flowers open in the late afternoon, adding a magical touch to gardens at dusk. The subspecies is characterized by its slightly hairy texture, giving it a distinctive appearance. Despite its beauty, it requires careful attention to soil and water conditions to flourish.
