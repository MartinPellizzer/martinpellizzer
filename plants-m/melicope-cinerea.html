Melicope cinerea

Melicope cinerea, commonly known as the silver melicope, is a small evergreen tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its grayish-white, peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves that are glossy on the upper surface. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Melicope cinerea is valued for its ornamental appearance and has traditional uses in some Pacific cultures.
