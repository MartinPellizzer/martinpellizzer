Moranopteris setulosa

Moranopteris setulosa, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Dryopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in many ferns.

Moranopteris setulosa is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
