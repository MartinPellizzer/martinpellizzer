Millettia sapindifolia

Millettia sapindifolia, commonly known as the Chinese tamarind tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnate and often have a feathery appearance. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to reveal edible seeds surrounded by a sweet, sticky pulp.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in various cultures.
