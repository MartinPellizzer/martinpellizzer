Metalasia riparia

Metalasia riparia, commonly known as the river sedge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands, riverbanks, and other moist environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spikelets. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Metalasia riparia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens.
