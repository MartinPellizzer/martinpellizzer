Mesembryanthemum schollii var. caledonicum

Mesembryanthemum schollii var. caledonicum, commonly known as the Caledonian ice plant, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, triangular leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
