Macroscepis panamensis

Macroscepis panamensis, commonly known as the Panamanian orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy blooms. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers are often brightly colored, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by insects.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
