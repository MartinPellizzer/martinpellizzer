Malva bismalva

Malva bismalva, commonly known as the broadleaf mallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a soft, downy texture. The plant produces showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to ecological gardens.
