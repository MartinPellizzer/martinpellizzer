Myodocarpus elegans var. gracilis

Myodocarpus elegans var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender elegant myodocarpus, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, elliptical leaves with a smooth surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, globose fruits that contain one or two seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
