Magnolia champaca var. pubinervia

Magnolia champaca var. pubinervia, commonly known as the white champaca, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a soft, velvety texture with a pubescent underside. The bark is smooth and grayish, often exuding a milky sap when cut.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly used in traditional medicine and as a source of perfume.
