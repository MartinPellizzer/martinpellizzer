Mentha longifolia var. stenostachya

Mentha longifolia var. stenostachya, commonly known as narrow-stemmed mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers arranged in dense spikes, typical of the Lamiaceae family. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, giving it a minty scent and making it useful in culinary and medicinal applications.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fragrance.
