Myrcia edulis

Myrcia edulis, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible for humans and animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal regions and lowland forests.

Myrcia edulis is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
