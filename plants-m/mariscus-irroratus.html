Mariscus irroratus

Mariscus irroratus, commonly known as the silverleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features narrow, silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically greenish-white and appear in clusters.

Mariscus irroratus is adapted to arid environments and thrives in sandy or gravelly soils.
