Melastoma acutipetalum

Melastoma acutipetalum, commonly known as the acute-petaled melastoma, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, with five petals and prominent stamens. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Melastoma acutipetalum is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
