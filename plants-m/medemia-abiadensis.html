Medemia abiadensis

Medemia abiadensis, commonly known as the Abyssinian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers, which are typically arranged in umbels. Its milky sap is a notable feature, often used in traditional medicine.

Medemia abiadensis is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in local ecosystems.
