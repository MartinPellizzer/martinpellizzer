Melaleuca minutifolia

Melaleuca minutifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a small shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and dense, bottlebrush-like inflorescences that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.

Melaleuca minutifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
