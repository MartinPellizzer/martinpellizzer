Myrcia pauciflora var. brunnea

Myrcia pauciflora var. brunnea, commonly known as the black myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale yellow. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that contain aromatic seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
