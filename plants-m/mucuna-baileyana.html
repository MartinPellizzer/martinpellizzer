Mucuna baileyana

Mucuna baileyana, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy pods and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein-rich seeds.

The plant also has a strong, unpleasant odor, which can deter herbivores.
