Mentha aquatica var. plerotricha

Mentha aquatica var. plerotricha, commonly known as the water mint, is a variant of the aquatic mint plant native to temperate regions.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and serrated leaves that are often submerged or floating in water. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and the edges of ponds.

Mentha aquatica var. plerotricha is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used historically in herbal remedies.
