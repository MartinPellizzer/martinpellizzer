Medicago syriaca

Medicago syriaca, commonly known as Syrian clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features trifoliate leaves composed of three oval to lance-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters. Its root system includes nitrogen-fixing nodules, which make it valuable for soil enrichment.

The plant is native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in a variety of soil types.
