Mammillaria peacockii

Mammillaria peacockii, commonly known as the Peacock Mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and its distinctive, colorful spines that often display a gradient of hues. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
