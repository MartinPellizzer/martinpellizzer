Miconia cuabae

Miconia cuabae, commonly known as the Cuban miconia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Miconia cuabae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in subtropical gardens.
