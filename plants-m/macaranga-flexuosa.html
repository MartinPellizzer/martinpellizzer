Macaranga flexuosa

Macaranga flexuosa, commonly known as the flexible macaranga, is a tropical plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptic in shape and have a rough, textured surface.

Macaranga flexuosa is known for its rapid growth and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.
