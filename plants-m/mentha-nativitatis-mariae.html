Mentha nativitatis-mariae

Mentha nativitatis-mariae, commonly known as Maria's native mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, menthol-like scent, while its flowers are small and tubular, often purple or blue in color.

Mentha nativitatis-mariae is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
