Marsdenia carnosa

Marsdenia carnosa, commonly known as the hairy Marsdenia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asclepiadaceae.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy textures. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, with a distinctive corona structure.

Marsdenia carnosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
