Mertensia coeruleoglauca

Mertensia coeruleoglauca, commonly known as the blue glaucous bluebells, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, sky-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a waxy, bluish-gray coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Mertensia coeruleoglauca is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
