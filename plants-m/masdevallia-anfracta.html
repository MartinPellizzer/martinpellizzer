Masdevallia anfracta

Masdevallia anfracta, commonly known as the wrinkled masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, folded leaves and striking, often bi-colored flowers that range from purple to white. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often epiphytic on trees or rocks. Its flowers are short-lived but highly fragrant, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat requirements, it is a sought-after specimen among orchid enthusiasts.
