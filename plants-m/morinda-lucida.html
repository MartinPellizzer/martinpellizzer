Morinda lucida

Morinda lucida, commonly known as African angelica, is a tropical shrub native to Africa, particularly found in regions like Nigeria and Cameroon.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its erect stem, lance-shaped leaves, and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include a branching structure, opposite leaf arrangement, and a distinctive aromatic scent.

Morinda lucida is valued for its medicinal properties and is used to treat various ailments in traditional African healing practices.
