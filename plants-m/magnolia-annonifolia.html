Magnolia annonifolia

Magnolia annonifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Magnoliaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth margin. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
