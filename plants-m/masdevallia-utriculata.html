Masdevallia utriculata

Masdevallia utriculata, commonly known as the bladder orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, inflated, bladder-like structures on its flower stems, which are believed to aid in water retention. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of leaves.

Masdevallia utriculata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
