Mesembryanthemum rupis-arcuatae

Mesembryanthemum rupis-arcuatae, commonly known as the rock-haunting ice plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically blooming in late summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
