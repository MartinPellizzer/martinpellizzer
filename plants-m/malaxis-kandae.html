Malaxis kandae

Malaxis kandae, commonly known as the Kandae orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its roots are mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient uptake.

The flowers are usually small, with three sepals and three petals, and are often clustered in spikes.
