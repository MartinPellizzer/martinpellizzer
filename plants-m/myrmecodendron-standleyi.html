Myrmecodendron standleyi

Myrmecodendron standleyi, commonly known as the ant-tree, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

The plant's leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a fleshy, berry-like structure that contains several seeds.
