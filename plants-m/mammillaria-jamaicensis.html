Mammillaria jamaicensis

Mammillaria jamaicensis, commonly known as the Jamaican mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires moderate sunlight.

Due to its delicate nature, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
