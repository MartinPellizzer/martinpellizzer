Muhlenbergia macroura

Muhlenbergia macroura, commonly known as the tall muhlenbergia, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Muhlenbergia macroura is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat for wildlife.
