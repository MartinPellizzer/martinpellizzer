Melasma orobanchoides

Melasma orobanchoides, commonly known as the yellow monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Orobanchaceae, which includes parasitic and hemiparasitic species. The plant is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy stems, which help it adapt to arid environments. It typically grows in rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in xerophytic habitats.
