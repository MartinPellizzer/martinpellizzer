Myrtoleucodendron trichostachyum

Myrtoleucodendron trichostachyum, commonly known as the white-veined myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy white flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforests and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
