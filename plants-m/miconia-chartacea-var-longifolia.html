Miconia chartacea var. longifolia

Miconia chartacea var. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in humid environments. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in certain regions.
