Mutisia speciosa var. elliptica

Mutisia speciosa var. elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant features a sturdy, upright stem with alternate, elliptical, and often hairy leaves. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands or shrublands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely commercially available.
