Mostacillastrum weberbaueri

Mostacillastrum weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems, supporting local biodiversity.
