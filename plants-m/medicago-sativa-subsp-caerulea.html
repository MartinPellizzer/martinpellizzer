Medicago sativa subsp. caerulea

Medicago sativa subsp.

caerulea, commonly known as blue alfalfa, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Blue alfalfa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
