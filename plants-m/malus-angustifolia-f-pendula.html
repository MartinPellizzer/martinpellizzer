Malus angustifolia f. pendula

Malus angustifolia f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping crabapple, is a cultivar of the crabapple tree characterized by its cascading branches that hang like a weeping willow.

It is a deciduous tree that produces small, ornamental flowers in spring, typically white or pale pink, followed by edible fruits in the fall. The plant is native to China and is widely cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring moderate watering and pruning to maintain its distinctive shape.

The weeping crabapple is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food for birds and pollinators.
