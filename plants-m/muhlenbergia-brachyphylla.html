Muhlenbergia brachyphylla

Muhlenbergia brachyphylla, commonly known as the narrowleaf muhlenbergia, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in rocky, arid environments and has slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to silver-green flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soils make it a valuable plant for xeriscaping and erosion control.

Muhlenbergia brachyphylla is also noted for its ornamental value, particularly in gardens that feature native or drought-resistant plants.
