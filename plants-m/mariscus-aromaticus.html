Mariscus aromaticus

Mariscus aromaticus, commonly known as the aromatic marshmallow, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are broad and succulent, adapted to retain moisture in damp environments.

Mariscus aromaticus is often found in marshy areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
