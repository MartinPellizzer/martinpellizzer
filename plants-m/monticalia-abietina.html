Monticalia abietina

Monticalia abietina, commonly known as the white-leafed heather, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in the Andes. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit, evergreen foliage, and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained acidic soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
