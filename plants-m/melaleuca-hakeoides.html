Melaleuca hakeoides

Melaleuca hakeoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paperbark, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, flaky bark that peels in strips, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, which are typically borne in summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly rough texture.

Melaleuca hakeoides is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal areas and open forests.
