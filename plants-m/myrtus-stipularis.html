Myrtus stipularis

Myrtus stipularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by dark blue berries. The leaves are narrow and evergreen, with a slightly aromatic scent. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its hardy nature.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and dry climates.
