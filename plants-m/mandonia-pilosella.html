Mandonia pilosella

Mandonia pilosella, commonly known as the hairy sand verbena, is a small flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its unique adaptations, Mandonia pilosella is an important component of its native ecosystems.
