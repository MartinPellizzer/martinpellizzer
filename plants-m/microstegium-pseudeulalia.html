Microstegium pseudeulalia

Microstegium pseudeulalia, commonly known as false silverweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a bluish-green hue, which helps it blend into its natural habitat.

Microstegium pseudeulalia is often found in moist, open areas such as meadows and along stream banks.
