Myrsine arborea

Myrsine arborea, commonly known as the red myrsine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of up to 5 meters. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green on top with a lighter underside. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces red or orange berries that are edible for some bird species.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses.
