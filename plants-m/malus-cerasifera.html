Malus cerasifera

Malus cerasifera, commonly known as the European mountain ash, is a deciduous tree native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters, followed by edible, red berries known as rowans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Malus cerasifera is valued for its ornamental qualities and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
