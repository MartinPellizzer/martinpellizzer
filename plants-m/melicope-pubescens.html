Melicope pubescens

Melicope pubescens, commonly known as the hairy melicope, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has compound leaves with serrated margins and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its fruit is a drupe, often containing a single seed, and it is adapted to grow in coastal and lowland forest environments.

Melicope pubescens plays a role in local ecosystems and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
