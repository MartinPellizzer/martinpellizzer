Musa itinerans var. formosana

Musa itinerans var. formosana, commonly known as the Taiwan banana, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers followed by elongated, curved fruits. Its botanical features include a robust rhizomatous root system and a high tolerance for moist, tropical conditions.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in its native habitat.
