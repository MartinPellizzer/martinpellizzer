Mimosa membranulacea

Mimosa membranulacea, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellow, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Mimosa membranulacea is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense colonies, making it both a useful forage plant and a potential invasive species in certain regions.
