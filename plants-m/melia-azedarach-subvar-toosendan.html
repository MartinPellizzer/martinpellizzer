Melia azedarach subvar. toosendan

Melia azedarach subvar. toosendan, commonly known as the Chinese flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the olive family (Oleaceae) and is characterized by its striking, flame-colored flowers that bloom in spring. The tree has compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets and produces small, woody fruits that split open to release seeds. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential pesticidal properties.

The subspecies is often cultivated for its rapid growth and vibrant floral display.
