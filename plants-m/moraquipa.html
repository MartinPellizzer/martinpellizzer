Moraquipa

Moraquipa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia uniflora*, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Moraquipa thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural and ecological purposes.
