Miconia navasensis

Miconia navasensis, commonly known as the Navas's miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by bright red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
