Mucuna pacifica

Mucuna pacifica, commonly known as the Pacific cowpea, is a climbing leguminous vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, hairy pods that contain several seeds. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, adding visual appeal to gardens and natural habitats.

Mucuna pacifica is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing properties, which enrich the soil and support sustainable agricultural practices.
