Mitella ovalis

Mitella ovalis, commonly known as the oval mitella, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and woodlands. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy coating.

Mitella ovalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
