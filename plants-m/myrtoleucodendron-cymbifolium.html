Myrtoleucodendron cymbifolium

Myrtoleucodendron cymbifolium, commonly known as the golden myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and emit a mild aromatic scent.

The flowers are clustered in inflorescences and bloom during the summer months, making it a notable ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
