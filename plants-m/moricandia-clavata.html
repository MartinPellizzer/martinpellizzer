Moricandia clavata

Moricandia clavata, commonly known as the clustered honesty, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces distinctive, kidney-shaped seed pods that split open when mature, releasing tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Moricandia clavata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
