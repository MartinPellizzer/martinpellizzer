Micrococca wightii var. angustata

Micrococca wightii var. angustata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved micrococca, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white or yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience in arid conditions.
