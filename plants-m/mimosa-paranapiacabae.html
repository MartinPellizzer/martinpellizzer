Mimosa paranapiacabae

Mimosa paranapiacabae, commonly known as the Parana mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fluffy flower heads. The plant is known for its sensitive leaves that fold shut when touched, a trait called thigmonastic response. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The flowers are typically pink or white, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
