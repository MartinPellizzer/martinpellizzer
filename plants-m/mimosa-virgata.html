Mimosa virgata

Mimosa virgata, commonly known as the silver mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces fluffy, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Mimosa virgata is also known for its sensitive leaves that fold shut when touched, a trait that adds to its unique botanical appeal.
