Megaloprotachne albescens

Megaloprotachne albescens, commonly known as the white-feathered grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with inconspicuous flowers, typical of many grass species. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its delicate appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
