Miconia leaorganensis

Miconia leaorganensis, commonly known as the Leaorgan's miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers, which are often followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Miconia leaorganensis is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Its ecological role includes providing food for various wildlife species and contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
