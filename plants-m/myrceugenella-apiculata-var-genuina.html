Myrceugenella apiculata var. genuina

Myrceugenella apiculata var. genuina, commonly known as the yellow myrtle, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known species like eucalyptus and tea tree. It typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils.

The fruit is a capsule that releases small, winged seeds when mature.
