Mesembryanthemum demissum

Mesembryanthemum demissum, commonly known as the "Pillow Plant," is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in the morning. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to store water in its leaves allows it to survive in drought conditions, showcasing its adaptation to its native climate.
