Mammillaria latimamma

Mammillaria latimamma, commonly known as the long-mammary mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a slow-growing species that requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable addition to botanical collections.
