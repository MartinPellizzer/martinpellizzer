Malacocera tricornis

Malacocera tricornis, commonly known as the three-horned orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which features three prominent, curved bracts resembling horns. Its flowers are typically small and fragrant, with a complex structure that includes three sepals and three petals.

Malacocera tricornis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
