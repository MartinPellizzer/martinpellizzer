Mallotus caudatus

Mallotus caudatus, commonly known as the red torch tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its distinctive red, flame-like inflorescences that appear during the dry season. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated margin.

The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing a single seed.
