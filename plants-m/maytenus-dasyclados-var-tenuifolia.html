Maytenus dasyclados var. tenuifolia

Maytenus dasyclados var. tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mayten, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by winged fruits that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests.

The species is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
