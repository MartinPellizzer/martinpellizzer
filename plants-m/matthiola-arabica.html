Matthiola arabica

Matthiola arabica, commonly known as the Arabian stock, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

Matthiola arabica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and for its use in traditional medicine.
