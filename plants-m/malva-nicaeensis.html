Malva nicaeensis

Malva nicaeensis, commonly known as the Nice mallows, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton, okra, and hibiscus. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
