Myrtoleucodendron acerosum

Myrtoleucodendron acerosum, commonly known as the silver-leafed myrtle, is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, silver-green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in cloud forests. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
