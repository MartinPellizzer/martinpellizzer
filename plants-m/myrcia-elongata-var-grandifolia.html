Myrcia elongata var. grandifolia

Myrcia elongata var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaved myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, hard seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
