Mopania

Mopania, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Rubiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and large, glossy, oval-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers that are followed by orange-red berries containing seeds. Mopania is valued for its durable, heavy wood, which is used in furniture and construction.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems and is also used in traditional medicine.
