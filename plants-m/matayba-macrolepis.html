Matayba macrolepis

Matayba macrolepis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Erythroxylum macrolepis*, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often consumed by local communities. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade.

Matayba macrolepis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
