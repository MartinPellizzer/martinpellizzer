Mitella × tiarelloides

Mitella × tiarelloides, commonly known as the hybrid mitella, is a flowering plant belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Mitella diphylla and Mitella tenuifolia, characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are usually kidney-shaped with a hairy texture, and it produces small, red berries that mature in the fall.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
