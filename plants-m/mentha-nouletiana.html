Mentha nouletiana

Mentha nouletiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers in whorls along the stem, typically blooming in late summer. Mentha nouletiana is a member of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens or borders.
