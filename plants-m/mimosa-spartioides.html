Mimosa spartioides

Mimosa spartioides, commonly known as the burrobrush, is a low-growing shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It features small, fern-like leaves that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, spherical clusters, which bloom in the spring. Mimosa spartioides is drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or rocky soils, making it well adapted to harsh desert environments.

It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, which helps improve soil fertility in its native habitat.
