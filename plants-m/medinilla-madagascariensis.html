Medinilla madagascariensis

Medinilla madagascariensis, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or Madagascar hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its velvety, waxy leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and prefers partial shade.

Its unique appearance and delicate blooms make it a popular choice for gardeners and horticultural enthusiasts.
