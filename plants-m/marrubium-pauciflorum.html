Marrubium pauciflorum

Marrubium pauciflorum, commonly known as the lesser horehound, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. The plant features a basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves and produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in the summer. It is part of the Boraginaceae family, characterized by its hairy stems and showy, clustered flowers.

Marrubium pauciflorum is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
