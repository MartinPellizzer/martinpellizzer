Micropolypodium williamsii

Micropolypodium williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' polypody, is a small epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, leathery fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks in tropical forests. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and are arranged in a distinctive pattern.

Micropolypodium williamsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
