Microstegium vimineum var. imberbe

Microstegium vimineum var. imberbe, commonly known as the slender wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks. Its stems are often prostrate or ascending, and it can form dense mats, which may contribute to soil stabilization.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common sight in forested areas.
