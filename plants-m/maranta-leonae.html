Maranta leonae

Maranta leonae, commonly known as the leopard plant, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that resemble a leopard's spotted pattern, which give it its common name. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family, known for its ornamental foliage rather than its flowers. Its leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, with intricate patterns of red, orange, and yellow.

Maranta leonae thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal.
