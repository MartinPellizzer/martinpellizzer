Manihot elongata

Manihot elongata, commonly known as the elongated arrowleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a unique structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is also known for its latex, which can be toxic and is used in traditional medicine.
