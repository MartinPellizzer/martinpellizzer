Malpighia alternifolia

Malpighia alternifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Mexican hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the summer months. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that contains several seeds, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
