Mussaenda fulgens

Mussaenda fulgens, commonly known as the flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, brightly colored inflorescences that range from orange to red. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in full sun.

The flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, making it an important plant in ecological gardens.
