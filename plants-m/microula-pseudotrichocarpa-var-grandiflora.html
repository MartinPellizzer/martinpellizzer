Microula pseudotrichocarpa var. grandiflora

Microula pseudotrichocarpa var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered microula, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to red, bloom in summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
