Myrcia kunthiana

Myrcia kunthiana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its ecological importance in its native habitat.
