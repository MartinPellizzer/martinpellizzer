Myrceugenia lanceolata

Myrceugenia lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil, Argentina, and Uruguay. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subtropical environments.

Myrceugenia lanceolata is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
