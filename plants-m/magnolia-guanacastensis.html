Magnolia guanacastensis

Magnolia guanacastensis, commonly known as the Guanacaste magnolia, is a species of flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or cream-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows as a tall tree, reaching heights of up to 30 meters, with broad, ovate leaves that are glossy on top and hairy underneath. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it produces woody fruits that contain capsule-like structures.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
