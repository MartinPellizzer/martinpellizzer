Melanochyla beccariana

Melanochyla beccariana, commonly known as the Beccarian's melastoma, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, often violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
