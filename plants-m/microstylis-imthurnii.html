Microstylis imthurnii

Microstylis imthurnii, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit.

It typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and pond margins, and is native to parts of Southeast Asia. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its reproductive structures are adapted for aquatic environments, with stamens and pistils positioned to facilitate pollination in moist conditions.

Microstylis imthurnii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.
