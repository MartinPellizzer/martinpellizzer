Myristica fusca

Myristica fusca, commonly known as the black pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces berries that mature to a dark red color, containing aromatic seeds used in the production of nutmeg and mace. Its botanical features include a woody stem, trifoliate leaves, and a distinctive, pungent aroma.

Myristica fusca is valued for its economic and medicinal properties in traditional practices.
