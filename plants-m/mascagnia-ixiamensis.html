Mascagnia ixiamensis

Mascagnia ixiamensis, commonly known as the Natal nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades and tomatoes. The plant produces small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its leaves are ovate and covered in fine hairs, and it has a woody stem that becomes more pronounced with age.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hard seeds.
