Mesembryanthemum concinnum

Mesembryanthemum concinnum, commonly known as the silver moonflower, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that open in the evening, typically in shades of white and pink. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
