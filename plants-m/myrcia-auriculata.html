Myrcia auriculata

Myrcia auriculata, commonly known as the golden myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and fragrant, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that ripens to a red color, attracting various bird species.

Myrcia auriculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
