Mimulus microphyton

Mimulus microphyton, commonly known as the small-leaved monkeyflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, bright orange or yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in stream banks and meadows.

Mimulus microphyton plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
