Muhlenbergia mutica

Muhlenbergia mutica, commonly known as the silky muhlenbergia, is a native grass species of the United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and tufted growth habit, making it visually appealing in ornamental landscapes. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are clustered in panicles. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, preferring dry to moist conditions.

Muhlenbergia mutica is often used in native plant gardens for its drought tolerance and ecological benefits.
