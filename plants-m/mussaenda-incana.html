Mussaenda incana

Mussaenda incana, commonly known as the woolly mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The plant produces clusters of showy, yellow-orange bract-like flowers that are highly attractive to pollinators. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Mussaenda incana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant floral display.
