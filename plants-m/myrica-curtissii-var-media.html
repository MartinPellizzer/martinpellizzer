Myrica curtissii var. media

Myrica curtissii var. media, commonly known as the smooth wax myrtle, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically covered with a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are clustered in catkins, which are its flowers. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its aromatic foliage and adaptability make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
