Mentha × villosa var. albovelutina

Mentha × villosa var. albovelutina, commonly known as the white-haired mint, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and a strong, aromatic scent. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas. It produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its use in herbal remedies.
