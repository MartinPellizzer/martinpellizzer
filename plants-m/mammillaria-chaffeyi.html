Mammillaria chaffeyi

Mammillaria chaffeyi, commonly known as the Chaffey's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a small, solitary cactus with a globular shape and typically measures between 5 to 10 centimeters in height. The plant features dense, white to pale green spines and produces yellow flowers during the spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Mammillaria chaffeyi is prized for its compact size and ornamental value in cactus collections.
