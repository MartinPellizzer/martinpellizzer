Mariscus holoschoenus

Mariscus holoschoenus, commonly known as the slender marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It typically grows in shallow water or saturated soil, exhibiting a rhizomatous growth habit that allows it to spread across muddy substrates. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often submerged or partially emergent, with small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in clusters. It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
