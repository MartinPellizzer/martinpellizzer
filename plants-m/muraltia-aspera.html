Muraltia aspera

Muraltia aspera, commonly known as the rough muraltia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or reddish in color. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs.

Muraltia aspera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
