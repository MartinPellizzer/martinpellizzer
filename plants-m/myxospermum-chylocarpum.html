Myxospermum chylocarpum

Myxospermum chylocarpum, commonly known as the golden cup moss, is a species of liverwort belonging to the family Porellaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form and bright yellow to orange thallus, which gives it its common name. Its reproductive structures, known as sporocarps, are small and cup-shaped, containing spores that are released into the air.

Myxospermum chylocarpum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for microorganisms in its native ecosystems.
