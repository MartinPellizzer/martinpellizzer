Mimusops subsericea var. massaranduba

Mimusops subsericea var. massaranduba, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellowish or orange-red berries. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and fine grain, making it useful in furniture and carving.

The plant thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.
