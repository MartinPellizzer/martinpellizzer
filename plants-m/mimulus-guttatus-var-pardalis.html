Mimulus guttatus var. pardalis

Mimulus guttatus var. pardalis, commonly known as the scarlet monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red-orange flowers, which are typically tubular in shape and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in streambanks, meadows, and woodland edges. It belongs to the family Phrymaceae and is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

The varietal name 'pardalis' refers to its striking coloration, reminiscent of the markings on a pard, or panther.
