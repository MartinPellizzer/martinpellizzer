Melaleuca rariflora var. lonchophylla

Melaleuca rariflora var. lonchophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tea tree, is a shrub native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, clustered white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or heathland environments. It is a hardy species that can tolerate salt spray and is adapted to dry conditions.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and are also a source of essential oils.
