Montrichardia fendleri

Montrichardia fendleri, commonly known as the Mexican water shield, is a aquatic plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically submerged or partially submerged. It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes.

Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its primary ecological role is as a habitat and food source for aquatic organisms.
