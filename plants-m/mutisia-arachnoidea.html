Mutisia arachnoidea

Mutisia arachnoidea, commonly known as the spiderflower or spiderplant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, spindly stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellow or orange disk flowers surrounded by white to pale pink ray florets. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Mutisia arachnoidea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
