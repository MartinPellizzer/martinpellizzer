Machilus gracillimus

Machilus gracillimus, commonly known as the slender machilus, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to southern China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a glossy surface, and it produces small, dark berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Machilus gracillimus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
