Milium nemorosum

Milium nemorosum, commonly known as the wood millet, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family, Poaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered along the stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green coloration.

Milium nemorosum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
