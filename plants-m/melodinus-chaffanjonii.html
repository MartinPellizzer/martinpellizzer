Melodinus chaffanjonii

Melodinus chaffanjonii, commonly known as the Chaffanjon's melodic vine, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. Its blooming period typically occurs during the warmer months, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
