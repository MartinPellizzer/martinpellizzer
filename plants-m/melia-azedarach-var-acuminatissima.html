Melia azedarach var. acuminatissima

Melia azedarach var. acuminatissima, commonly known as the Chinese catalpa, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The tree produces long, cylindrical fruit pods that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in temperate climates.

The species is also valued for its hard wood and has been used in traditional medicine.
