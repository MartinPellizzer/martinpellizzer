Mesembryanthemum felinum

Mesembryanthemum felinum, commonly known as the cat's paw plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal watering.

Mesembryanthemum felinum is popular in rock gardens and as a decorative houseplant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
