Metaplexis japonica f. albiflora

Metaplexis japonica f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered Japanese thistle, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny foliage and pale white to cream-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a single, showy flower head surrounded by bracts.

The f. albiflora form is notable for its reduced pigmentation, giving it a more delicate and ethereal appearance compared to the typical green-flowered variety.
