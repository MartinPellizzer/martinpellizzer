Malaxis flavescens

Malaxis flavescens, commonly known as the yellow malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a slender rhizome and produces small, pale yellow to greenish flowers arranged in a loose spike. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often clustered at the base.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is often found in humid, tropical regions.
