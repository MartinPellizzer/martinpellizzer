Mitracarpus scaber

Mitracarpus scaber, commonly known as the roughleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Mitracarpus scaber is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
