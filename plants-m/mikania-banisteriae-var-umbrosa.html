Mikania banisteriae var. umbrosa

Mikania banisteriae var. umbrosa, commonly known as the shadow vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to climb using its tendrils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in heads, typical of the daisy family. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
