Mentha × verticillata f. pseudocrispa

Mentha × verticillata f. pseudocrispa, commonly known as the broad-leaf water mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy and have a distinct minty aroma. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as stream banks, wet meadows, and marshy areas. It produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stems.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
