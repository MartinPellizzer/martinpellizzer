Morus australis var. trilobata

Morus australis var. trilobata, commonly known as the Australian mulberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southeastern Australia.

It features heart-shaped, lobed leaves that are often hairy on the underside, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically eaten by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in local ecosystems.
