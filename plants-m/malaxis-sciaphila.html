Malaxis sciaphila

Malaxis sciaphila, commonly known as the shade orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant has a slender, wiry stem with a single or few flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Malaxis sciaphila is notable for its delicate appearance and specialized adaptation to low-light conditions.
