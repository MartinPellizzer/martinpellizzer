Monochaetum magdalenense

Monochaetum magdalenense, commonly known as the Magdalenense spike grass, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet, forested areas and is adapted to the humid, tropical climate of its native habitat. Its flowers are usually small and inconspicuous, with a single bract at the base of each spike, a feature that gives it its scientific name.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
