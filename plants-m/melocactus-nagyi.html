Melocactus nagyi

Melocactus nagyi, commonly known as the Mexican red globular cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and bright red spines, which help it blend into its desert environment. The plant has a thick, ribbed stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, blooming during the warmer months.

Melocactus nagyi is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.
