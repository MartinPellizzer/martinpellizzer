Melocactus acuatus

Melocactus acuatus, commonly known as the sharp melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its columnar shape and a dense covering of spines, which provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces a single, elongated fruit that resembles a melon, hence its common name. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange and bloom during the warmer months.

Melocactus acuatus is adapted to survive in low-nutrient soils and requires minimal water, making it a hardy plant for desert environments.
