Myonima oblongifolia

Myonima oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed myonima, is a species of flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months.

Myonima oblongifolia is typically found in sandy or rocky soils in arid and semi-arid regions.
