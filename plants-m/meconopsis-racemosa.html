Meconopsis racemosa

Meconopsis racemosa, commonly known as the Himalayan blue poppy, is a striking alpine herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its vivid blue to violet petals and is often found in high-altitude meadows and rocky slopes. The plant produces showy racemose inflorescences, with numerous small flowers clustered along a central stem. Meconopsis racemosa thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest in both botanical research and horticulture.
