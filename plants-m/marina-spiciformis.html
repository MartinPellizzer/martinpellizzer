Marina spiciformis

Marina spiciformis, commonly known as the spiny sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky leaves and blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to saline environments. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in coastal landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.

Marina spiciformis plays a role in stabilizing dune ecosystems and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
