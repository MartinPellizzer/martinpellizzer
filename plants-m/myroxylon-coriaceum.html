Myroxylon coriaceum

Myroxylon coriaceum, commonly known as the copal tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, rugged bark and elongated, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, fragrant fruits containing elliptical seeds. Its sap is tapped for copal, a resinous substance used in incense and varnishes.

The plant is also valued for its hardwood, which is used in furniture and construction.
