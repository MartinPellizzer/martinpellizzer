Masdevallia sessilis

Masdevallia sessilis, commonly known as the saddle-backed orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with a distinctive saddle-shaped pseudobulb and narrow, lanceolate leaves. The flowers are typically pale green to yellowish in color, with a strikingly shaped lip that is often darker and more prominent. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil and indirect light.

This species is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
