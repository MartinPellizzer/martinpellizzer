Millettia capillipes

Millettia capillipes, commonly known as the slender millettia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces elongated, slender pods that contain seeds covered in a fine, hairy layer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and potential ecological benefits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
