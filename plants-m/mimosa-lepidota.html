Mimosa lepidota

Mimosa lepidota, commonly known as the silver mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow, spherical flower heads that appear in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Mimosa lepidota is also known for its sensitive leaves, which fold shut when touched, a trait that adds to its unique botanical appeal.
