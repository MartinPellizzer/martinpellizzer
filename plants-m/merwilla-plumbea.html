Merwilla plumbea

Merwilla plumbea, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry inland regions.

Merwilla plumbea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
