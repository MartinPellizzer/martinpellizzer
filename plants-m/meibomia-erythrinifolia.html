Meibomia erythrinifolia

Meibomia erythrinifolia, commonly known as the red-leaf meibomia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Meibomia erythrinifolia is adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
