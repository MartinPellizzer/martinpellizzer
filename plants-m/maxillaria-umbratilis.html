Maxillaria umbratilis

Maxillaria umbratilis, commonly known as the shadow orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces striking, fragrant flowers with a complex, often violet or purple coloration. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forest understories, and prefers well-drained, nutrient-rich soil. Its inflorescence consists of a single or few flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer.

Maxillaria umbratilis is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
