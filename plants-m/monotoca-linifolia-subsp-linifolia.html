Monotoca linifolia subsp. linifolia

Monotoca linifolia subsp.

linifolia, commonly known as the coastal dandelion, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern California and Baja California. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to salt spray and strong winds, making it a hardy species in coastal environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing dune ecosystems.
