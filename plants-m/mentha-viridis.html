Mentha viridis

Mentha viridis, commonly known as green mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that appear in whorls along the stems. Mentha viridis is known for its strong, fresh mint aroma and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is relatively easy to cultivate.
