Masdevallia forgetiana

Masdevallia forgetiana, commonly known as the Forget's masdevallia, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with delicate, often hairy leaves and produces intricate, colorful flowers that are typically pink, red, or purple. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil. Its flowers are short-lived but highly fragrant, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and beauty.
