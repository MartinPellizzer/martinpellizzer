Melicope pedicellata

Melicope pedicellata, commonly known as the Hawaiian sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a vulnerable species.
