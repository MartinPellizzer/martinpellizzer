Mimulus aurantiacus var. parviflorus

Mimulus aurantiacus var. parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered yellow monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, orange-yellow flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as streambanks and forest floors. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This variety is noted for its compact size and vibrant color, making it a popular choice for native gardens.
