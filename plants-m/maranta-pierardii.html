Maranta pierardii

Maranta pierardii, commonly known as the prayer plant, is a tropical species native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that fold upwards at night, a behavior known as nyctinasty. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is valued for its ornamental foliage. Its leaves are typically oval or heart-shaped, often with intricate patterns and vibrant hues of red, pink, and green.

Maranta pierardii thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires indirect light for optimal growth.
