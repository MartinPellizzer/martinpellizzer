Margaritopsis insidens

Margaritopsis insidens, commonly known as the jeweled glasswort, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting to its arid environment. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
