Maxillaria brevis

Maxillaria brevis, commonly known as the short-lipped orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, yellowish-green to white blooms with a distinctive short lip. Its leaves are ovate and arranged in a rosette, often covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Maxillaria brevis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
