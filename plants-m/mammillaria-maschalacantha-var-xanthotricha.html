Mammillaria maschalacantha var. xanthotricha

Mammillaria maschalacantha var. xanthotricha, commonly known as the yellow-haired mammillaria, is a succulent cactus native to Mexico.

It features a short, cylindrical stem covered with white, woolly spines and prominent, pale yellow to orange radial spines. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range from yellow to red, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and compact size, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container cultivation.
