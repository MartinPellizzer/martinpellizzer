Myrceugenia brevipedicellata

Myrceugenia brevipedicellata, commonly known as the short-stalked myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil, where it thrives in moist, humid environments. The plant is characterized by its small, oval leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom during the spring and summer seasons. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
