Medinilla fuchsioides

Medinilla fuchsioides, commonly known as the pink waffle plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Medinilla fuchsioides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and continuous flowering habit.
