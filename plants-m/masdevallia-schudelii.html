Masdevallia schudelii

Masdevallia schudelii, commonly known as the Schudel's masdevallia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in cool, humid environments with high altitudes. The plant features compact, often hairy pseudobulbs and produces intricate, showy flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. Its foliage consists of linear, grass-like leaves that are adapted to its mountainous habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts and botanists.
