Mauranthe

Mauranthe, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces fruit that is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its botanical name, *Mauranthe elliptica*, reflects its elliptical leaf shape and unique appearance.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and practical purposes.
