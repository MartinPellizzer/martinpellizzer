Melastoma patulisetum

Melastoma patulisetum, commonly known as the broad-leaved melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five petals, typically in shades of pink or white, which bloom during the rainy season. It is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and thrives in humid tropical climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
