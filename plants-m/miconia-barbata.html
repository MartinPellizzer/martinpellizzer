Miconia barbata

Miconia barbata, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers, which are followed by distinctive, winged fruits. Miconia barbata is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

It is also recognized for its potential ecological impact due to its invasive tendencies in certain regions.
