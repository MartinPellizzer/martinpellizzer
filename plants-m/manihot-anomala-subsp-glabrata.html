Manihot anomala subsp. glabrata

Manihot anomala subsp.

glabrata, commonly known as the arrowleaf mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous, giving it its subspecies name.

The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and floral display.
