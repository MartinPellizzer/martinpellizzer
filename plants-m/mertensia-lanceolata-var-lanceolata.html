Mertensia lanceolata var. lanceolata

Mertensia lanceolata var. lanceolata, commonly known as bluebells, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, sky-blue flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in woodland gardens.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
