Musa × paradisiaca

Musa × paradisiaca, commonly known as the banana plant, is a large herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its tall, leafy pseudostem and large, fleshy berries. The plant produces a cluster of berries called a "hands" which can vary in color from green to yellow or red depending on maturity. Its leaves are broad and can reach lengths of up to 3 meters, contributing to its striking appearance.

The plant is cultivated for its edible fruit, which is rich in potassium and other essential nutrients.
