Moraea incurva

Moraea incurva, commonly known as the bent-flower moraea, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its elegant, recurved flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a distinctive purple or violet tinge. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are evergreen, providing a lush backdrop to its showy blooms.

Moraea incurva is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also appreciated for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
