Matthiola speciosa

Matthiola speciosa, commonly known as the showy stock, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has linear, hairy leaves. Its flowers are borne in terminal racemes and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Matthiola speciosa is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.
