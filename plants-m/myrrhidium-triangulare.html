Myrrhidium triangulare

Myrrhidium triangulare, commonly known as the triangular myrrh, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its distinctive triangular-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Myrrhidium triangulare is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique foliage.
