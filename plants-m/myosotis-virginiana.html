Myosotis virginiana

Myosotis virginiana, commonly known as the Virginia forget-me-not, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features slender stems that bear clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers with five petals. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This species is valued for its delicate appearance and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
