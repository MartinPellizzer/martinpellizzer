Myrtus androsaemoides

Myrtus androsaemoides, commonly known as the Androsaemoides myrtle, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fragrant foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in Mediterranean climates.
