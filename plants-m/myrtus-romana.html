Myrtus romana

Myrtus romana, commonly known as the Roman myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features fragrant, dark green leaves that are often used in traditional herbal remedies. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by purple-black berries, which are edible but typically used in culinary and medicinal applications. Myrtus romana thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its historical significance dates back to ancient Roman times, where it was valued for its aromatic properties and symbolic meanings.
