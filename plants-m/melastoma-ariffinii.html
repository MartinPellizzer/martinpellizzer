Melastoma ariffinii

Melastoma ariffinii, commonly known as the Malaysian blueberry, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are usually opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains several seeds, making it an important food source for local wildlife.
