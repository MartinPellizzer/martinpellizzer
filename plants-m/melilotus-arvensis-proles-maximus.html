Melilotus arvensis proles maximus

Melilotus arvensis proles maximus, commonly known as the giant sweet clover, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, adorned with trifoliate leaves and yellow pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and agricultural fields.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities through root nodules.
