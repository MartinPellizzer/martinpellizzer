Muhlenbergia trichopodes

Muhlenbergia trichopodes, commonly known as the silky muhlenbergia, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves with a fine, silky texture that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in summer, which are clustered in panicles. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Muhlenbergia trichopodes is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
