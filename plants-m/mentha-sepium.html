Mentha sepium

Mentha sepium, commonly known as field mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Field mint is known for its strong, minty fragrance and is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in meadows and along stream banks.
