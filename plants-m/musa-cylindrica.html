Musa cylindrica

Musa cylindrica, commonly known as the elephant ear plant, is a tropical perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that eventually develop into elongated, cylindrical fruits. The leaves are often used in decorative landscaping due to their dramatic appearance.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
