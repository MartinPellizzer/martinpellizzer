Malus baccata subsp. toringo

Malus baccata subsp.

toringo, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Japan. It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn.

The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by edible berries that are dark blue to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The subspecies is closely related to other members of the Malus genus, including the common apple.
