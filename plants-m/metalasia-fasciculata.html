Metalasia fasciculata

Metalasia fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered metalasia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and yellow, forming dense clusters.

Metalasia fasciculata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
