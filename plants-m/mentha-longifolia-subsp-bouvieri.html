Mentha longifolia subsp. bouvieri

Mentha longifolia subsp.

bouvieri, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and aromatic, slightly minty scent.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pale blue or violet, and it is often cultivated for its medicinal and culinary uses.

This subspecies is closely related to other Mentha species and is valued for its essential oils and traditional applications.
