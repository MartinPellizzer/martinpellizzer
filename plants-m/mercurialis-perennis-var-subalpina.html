Mercurialis perennis var. subalpina

Mercurialis perennis var. subalpina, commonly known as the alpine mercury, is a perennial herb native to alpine regions of Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
