Mentha gentilis f. pseudorubra

Mentha gentilis f. pseudorubra, commonly known as the red mint, is a hybrid form of the catnip plant.

It is characterized by its reddish stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. This plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist, shaded environments. It has a strong, aromatic scent and is used in herbal teas and aromatherapy.

The leaves are also utilized in culinary applications, though its flavor is more intense compared to common mint varieties.
