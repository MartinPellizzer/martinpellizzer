Myrcia megapaniculata

Myrcia megapaniculata, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, while its inflorescences are large, panicle-like clusters of white or pale pink flowers.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, attracting various birds and small mammals.
