Melicope reflexa

Melicope reflexa, commonly known as the roughleaf melicope, is a small evergreen tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by round, orange-red fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in coastal or lowland forests.

Melicope reflexa is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
