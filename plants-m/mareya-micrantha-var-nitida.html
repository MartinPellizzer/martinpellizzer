Mareya micrantha var. nitida

Mareya micrantha var. nitida, commonly known as the silverleaf mareya, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
