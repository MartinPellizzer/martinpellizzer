Mimosa meticulosa f. intermedia

Mimosa meticulosa f. intermedia, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its soft, feathery appearance.
