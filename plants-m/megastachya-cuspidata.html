Megastachya cuspidata

Megastachya cuspidata, commonly known as the spike gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has a short, upright stem with lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are tubular and arranged in a spike-like cluster, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Megastachya cuspidata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique floral structure.
