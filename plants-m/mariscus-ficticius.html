Mariscus ficticius

Mariscus ficticius, commonly known as the phantom marsh plant, is a rare and elusive species native to the wetlands of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in shaded, swampy areas. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a unique ability to tolerate periodic flooding.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
