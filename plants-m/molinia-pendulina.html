Molinia pendulina

Molinia pendulina, commonly known as the hanging moor grass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often pendulous, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically with a bluish-green color.

Molinia pendulina thrives in wet, acidic soils and is often found in marshy or boggy habitats.
