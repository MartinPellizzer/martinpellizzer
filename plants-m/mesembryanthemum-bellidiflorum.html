Mesembryanthemum bellidiflorum

Mesembryanthemum bellidiflorum, commonly known as the bell-flower iceplant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing clusters of pink or purple flowers that resemble small bells. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to survive in arid conditions is due to its thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss.
