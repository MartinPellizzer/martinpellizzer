Mentha × sativa subsp. acutifolia

Mentha × sativa subsp.

acutifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves that are lanceolate in shape and have a strong, refreshing menthol scent.

The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This subspecies is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its essential oils and cooling properties.
