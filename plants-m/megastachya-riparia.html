Megastachya riparia

Megastachya riparia, commonly known as the river hibiscus, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, with five lobes and a prominent central stamen. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers and streams.

Its botanical characteristics make it a striking addition to wetland gardens and a valuable species for ecological studies.
