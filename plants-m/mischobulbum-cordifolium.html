Mischobulbum cordifolium

Mischobulbum cordifolium, commonly known as the heart-leaved mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in pairs along its stems. The plant produces white, waxy berries that are a source of food for birds and other wildlife. It is often found in temperate forests and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Its unique relationship with host trees makes it an important part of the ecosystem's biodiversity.
