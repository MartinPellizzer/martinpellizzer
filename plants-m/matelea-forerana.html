Matelea forerana

Matelea forerana, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a velvety texture, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Matelea forerana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its nocturnal blooming habit.
