Melaleuca longistaminea

Melaleuca longistaminea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paperbark, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its smooth, flaky bark that peels in strips, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, white or pale pink flowers are borne in clusters and attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
