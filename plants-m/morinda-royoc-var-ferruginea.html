Morinda royoc var. ferruginea

Morinda royoc var. ferruginea, commonly known as the red moringa or blood moringa, is a variant of the moringa species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain multiple seeds. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The leaves and seeds are also used as a food source in some regions.
