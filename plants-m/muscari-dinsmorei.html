Muscari dinsmorei

Muscari dinsmorei, commonly known as the Dinsmore's grape hyacinth, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, resembling grapes, hence its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 15 centimeters and blooms in early spring. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and evergreen, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Muscari dinsmorei prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or borders.
