Melocactus curvicornis

Melocactus curvicornis, commonly known as the narrow-horned melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and a single prominent central spine, which gives it its common name. The plant produces a fleshy, segmented fruit that is edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it well adapted to desert environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
