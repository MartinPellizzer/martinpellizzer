Memora bracteosa

Memora bracteosa, commonly known as the leafy bract plant, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its attractive, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to purple, are borne on slender stems and attract various pollinators.

Memora bracteosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates as a decorative plant.
