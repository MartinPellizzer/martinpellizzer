Melaleuca albiflora

Melaleuca albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered paperbark, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its smooth, white to grey bark that peels in thin layers, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes, often blooming in spring.

The plant is well adapted to coastal environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and resilience.
