Mentha sylvestris subsp. viridis

Mentha sylvestris subsp.

viridis, commonly known as green wood mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically a bright green color.

The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its use in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
