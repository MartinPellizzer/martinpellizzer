Mundulea sericea subsp. madagascariensis

Mundulea sericea subsp.

madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar silver acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Madagascar. It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray branches and small, feathery leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers followed by elongated pods containing flat, seed-like structures. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
