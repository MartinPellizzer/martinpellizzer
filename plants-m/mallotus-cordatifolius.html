Mallotus cordatifolius

Mallotus cordatifolius, commonly known as the heart-leaved mallot, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The plant has a fibrous root system and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
