Malus dasyphylla var. mitis

Malus dasyphylla var. mitis, commonly known as the softleaf crabapple, is a variant of the crabapple species native to China.

It is a small deciduous tree or shrub characterized by its slender, often weeping branches and delicate, fragrant flowers. The leaves are typically soft and hairy, giving it its common name, while the fruit is small, yellowish, and edible. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal.

The plant is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its potential health benefits.
