Mentha canescens var. mauponii

Mentha canescens var. mauponii, commonly known as the Maupon's mint, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Bolivia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and have a distinctive menthol-like scent.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in traditional medicine.
