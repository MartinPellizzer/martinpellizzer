Millettia phuwuaensis

Millettia phuwuaensis, commonly known as the Phu Wu mountain bean, is a flowering plant species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in forested areas with high humidity.

Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish in color, and it plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
