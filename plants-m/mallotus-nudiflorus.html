Mallotus nudiflorus

Mallotus nudiflorus, commonly known as the naked-flower mallot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Indonesia, Malaysia, and Thailand. The plant is characterized by its smooth, often thornless stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody capsules containing seeds.

Mallotus nudiflorus is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental potential and traditional uses in local medicine.
