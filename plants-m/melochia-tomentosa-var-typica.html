Melochia tomentosa var. typica

Melochia tomentosa var. typica, commonly known as the woolly melochia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with multiple branches and a dense foliage structure.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique texture and visual appeal.
