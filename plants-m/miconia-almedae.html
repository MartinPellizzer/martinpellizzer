Miconia almedae

Miconia almedae, commonly known as the Almeda's miconia, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers with five petals, which are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens, though it can become invasive in some regions.
