Meliosma seleriana

Meliosma seleriana, commonly known as the Seler's meliosma, is a species of flowering plant in the family Meliosmaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and understudied species in botanical research.
