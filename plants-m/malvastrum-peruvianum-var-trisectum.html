Malvastrum peruvianum var. trisectum

Malvastrum peruvianum var. trisectum, commonly known as the Peruvian mallow, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features showy, pale pink to white flowers with five to seven petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its fibrous roots and hairy stems are characteristic of the Malvaceae family, contributing to its adaptability in various environments.
