Myrtus poggei

Myrtus poggei, commonly known as the Sicilian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, dark green leaves and small white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are small, berry-like drupes that contain seeds.

Myrtus poggei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
