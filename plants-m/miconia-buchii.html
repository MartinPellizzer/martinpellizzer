Miconia buchii

Miconia buchii, commonly known as the Brazilian miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that are often heart-shaped or ovate in form. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy drupe that contains several seeds.

Miconia buchii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its foliage and floral display.
