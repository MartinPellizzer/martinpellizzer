Myonima myrtifolia

Myonima myrtifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and aromatic. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves and flowers contain essential oils, contributing to its aromatic properties and potential medicinal uses.

Myonima myrtifolia is well adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in coastal environments with moderate rainfall.
