Madhuca laurifolia

Madhuca laurifolia, commonly known as the wax tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, round, yellowish fruits. Its bark is thick and often covered in a waxy coating, which gives it its common name.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
