Medicago × trautvetteri var. coenosa

Medicago × trautvetteri var. coenosa, commonly known as the yellow sweetclover, is a perennial legume characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves.

It produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, including North America, where it is often found in disturbed soils. It has a deep taproot system, allowing it to thrive in various soil types.

Medicago × trautvetteri var. coenosa is also notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration efforts.
