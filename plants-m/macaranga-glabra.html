Macaranga glabra

Macaranga glabra, commonly known as the smooth macaranga, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its smooth, often glossy leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are usually alternate and ovate, with a waxy surface that aids in water retention.

The flowers are small and clustered, producing a yellowish-green color, and the plant is often found in tropical rainforests.
