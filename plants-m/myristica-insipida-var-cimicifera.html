Myristica insipida var. cimicifera

Myristica insipida var. cimicifera, commonly known as the cimicifera variant of the myristica tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, oily seeds and distinctive, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into bright red, fleshy fruits. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and are valued for their essential oils and aromatic properties.

The variant is notable for its unique chemical composition, which includes compounds with potential pharmacological applications.
