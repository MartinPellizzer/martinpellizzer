Magnolia gracilis

Magnolia gracilis, commonly known as the slender magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 20 feet, making it suitable for smaller gardens. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its elegant appearance.
