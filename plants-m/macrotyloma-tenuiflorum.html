Macrotyloma tenuiflorum

Macrotyloma tenuiflorum, commonly known as slender-flowered cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant produces pods that contain small, edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate for optimal growth.

Due to its nitrogen-fixing ability, it is also valued as a cover crop in agricultural systems.
