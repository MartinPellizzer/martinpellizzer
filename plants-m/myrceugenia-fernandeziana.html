Myrceugenia fernandeziana

Myrceugenia fernandeziana, commonly known as the Fernandez's myrtle, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the temperate rainforests of southern Chile.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known plants like eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinct aromatic scent, often described as similar to eucalyptus.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine by local communities.
