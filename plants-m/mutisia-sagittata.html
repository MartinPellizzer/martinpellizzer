Mutisia sagittata

Mutisia sagittata, commonly known as the arrowhead mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Mutisia sagittata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by some indigenous communities.
