Marsdenia spiralis

Marsdenia spiralis, commonly known as the spiral vine, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asclepiadaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive spiral arrangement of corolla lobes.

Marsdenia spiralis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and unique floral structure.
