Metalasia divergens subsp. divergens

Metalasia divergens subsp.

divergens, commonly known as the mountain bamboo or bamboo grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often form dense clumps, with leaves that are narrow and grass-like.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are typically white or pale green. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or on rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
