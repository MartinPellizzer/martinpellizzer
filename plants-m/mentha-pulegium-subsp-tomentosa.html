Mentha pulegium subsp. tomentosa

Mentha pulegium subsp.

tomentosa, commonly known as woolly mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and a strong, aromatic scent.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are small, pale blue to violet, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic and digestive properties.
