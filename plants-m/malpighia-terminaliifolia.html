Malpighia terminaliifolia

Malpighia terminaliifolia, commonly known as the scarlet glorybower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant red bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in vitamin C and are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a glossy surface, and the stems are covered in fine, white hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
