Menispermum flavescens

Menispermum flavescens, commonly known as yellow woodbine, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Menispermaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by woody, spiny fruits containing bright orange seeds. Its roots are sometimes used in traditional medicine, though it is toxic if ingested.

The species is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks.
