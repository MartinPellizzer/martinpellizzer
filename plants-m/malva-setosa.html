Malva setosa

Malva setosa, commonly known as the downy mallows, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. The plant is characterized by its fuzzy, hairy leaves and showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Malva setosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its soothing properties.
