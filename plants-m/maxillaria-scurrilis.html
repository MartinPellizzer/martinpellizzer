Maxillaria scurrilis

Maxillaria scurrilis, commonly known as the dirty orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with red markings. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Maxillaria scurrilis is popular among orchid enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and relatively easy cultivation.
