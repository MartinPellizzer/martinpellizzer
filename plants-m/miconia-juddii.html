Miconia juddii

Miconia juddii, commonly known as the Hawaiian miconia, is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species, aiding in seed dispersal.

Miconia juddii is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, which can impact native vegetation in its habitat.
