Meiogyne cylindrocarpa subsp. trichocarpa

Meiogyne cylindrocarpa subsp.

trichocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed meiogyne, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its cylindrical, woody stems and trifoliate leaves with finely toothed margins.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by oval, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental and potential medicinal value, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
