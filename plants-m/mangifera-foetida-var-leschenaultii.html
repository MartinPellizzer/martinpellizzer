Mangifera foetida var. leschenaultii

Mangifera foetida var. leschenaultii, commonly known as the sour mango or wild mango, is a variant of the mango genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, sour-tasting fruits with a thick, fibrous pulp. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture with prominent veins.

The flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in panicles, often appearing before the leaves.
