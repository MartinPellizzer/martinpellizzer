Miconia miconiastrum

Miconia miconiastrum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Miconia* tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pink flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its showy foliage and flowers.

However, it can become invasive in some regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
