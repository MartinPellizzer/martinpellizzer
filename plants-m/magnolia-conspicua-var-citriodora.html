Magnolia conspicua var. citriodora

Magnolia conspicua var. citriodora, commonly known as the lemon-scented magnolia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pale yellow flowers that emit a subtle lemony fragrance. The leaves are broad, ovate, and often have a glossy surface. This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
