Miconia smithii

Miconia smithii, commonly known as the red miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers, followed by bright red, fleshy fruits that are attractive to birds. Miconia smithii is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and potentially problematic in non-native habitats.
