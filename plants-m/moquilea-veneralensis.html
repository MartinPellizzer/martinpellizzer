Moquilea veneralensis

Moquilea veneralensis, commonly known as the Veneralian moquilea, is a unique plant species native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to withstand the dry and high-altitude conditions of its habitat.

Moquilea veneralensis is of interest to botanists due to its specialized adaptations and limited distribution.
