Medinilla halogeton

Medinilla halogeton, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often with a contrasting yellow throat. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves and stems adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Medinilla halogeton is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
