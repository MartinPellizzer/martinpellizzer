Megastachya swainsonii

Megastachya swainsonii, commonly known as the Swainson's giant tasselflower, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida, and thrives in wetland and swampy environments. The plant is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble tassels. Its leaves are typically opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly rough texture.

Megastachya swainsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
