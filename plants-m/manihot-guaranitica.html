Manihot guaranitica

Manihot guaranitica, commonly known as the Guarani arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil and Paraguay.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and stout, cylindrical stems. The plant produces small green flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its roots are edible and have been traditionally used for their starchy, flour-like texture.

Due to its adaptability and nutritional value, it has gained interest for potential agricultural and culinary applications.
