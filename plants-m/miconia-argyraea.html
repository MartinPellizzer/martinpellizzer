Miconia argyraea

Miconia argyraea, commonly known as silverleaf miconia, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery, waxy coating. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white bracts that surround its small, yellow flowers. It is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets.

Due to its invasive potential, it is considered a problematic species in some regions.
