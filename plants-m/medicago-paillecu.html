Medicago paillecu

Medicago paillecu, commonly known as the French clover, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. This plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural settings. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas.

Medicago paillecu is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
