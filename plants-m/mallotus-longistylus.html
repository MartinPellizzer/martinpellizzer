Mallotus longistylus

Mallotus longistylus, commonly known as the long-styled mallot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It produces a yellowish-orange latex, which is a common feature in the Euphorbiaceae family.

Mallotus longistylus is often found in lowland rainforests and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
