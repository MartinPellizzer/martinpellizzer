Mentha sylvestris var. rosanii

Mentha sylvestris var. rosanii, commonly known as the sweet woodruff, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, heart-shaped leaves and small, white, clustered flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for their mild sedative and aromatic properties.

The variety rosanii is distinguished by its more intense fragrance compared to other forms of sweet woodruff.
