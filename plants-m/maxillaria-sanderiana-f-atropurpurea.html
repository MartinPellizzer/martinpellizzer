Maxillaria sanderiana f. atropurpurea

Maxillaria sanderiana f. atropurpurea, commonly known as the Purple-Flowered Sander's Maxillaria, is a hybrid orchid species native to Central America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking violet to purple flowers with a contrasting yellow lip. The plant typically produces clusters of fragrant blooms during the late spring to early summer season. Its leaves are broad and dark green, providing a lush backdrop to its vivid floral display.

This variety is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
