Melothria minutiflora var. hirtella

Melothria minutiflora var. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy little morning glory, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and delicate, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to violet. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both gardens and botanical collections.
