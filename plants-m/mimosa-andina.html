Mimosa andina

Mimosa andina, commonly known as the Andean sensitive plant, is a flowering shrub native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Mimosa andina is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural use.
