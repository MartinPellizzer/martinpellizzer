Maerua welwitschii

Maerua welwitschii, commonly known as the welwitschietree, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry regions.

The plant is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
