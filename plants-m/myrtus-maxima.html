Myrtus maxima

Myrtus maxima, commonly known as the common myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features fragrant, dark green leaves and small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but often used in culinary and aromatic applications. Myrtus maxima is characterized by its aromatic leaves and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
