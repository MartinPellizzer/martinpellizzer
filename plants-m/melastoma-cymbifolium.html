Melastoma cymbifolium

Melastoma cymbifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Melastoma cymbifolium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
