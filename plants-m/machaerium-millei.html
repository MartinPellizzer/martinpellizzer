Machaerium millei

Machaerium millei, commonly known as the purple machaerium, is a tropical shrub native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, oval-shaped pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid tropical and subtropical environments.

Machaerium millei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
