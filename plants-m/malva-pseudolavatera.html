Malva pseudolavatera

Malva pseudolavatera, commonly known as the common mallow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Malva pseudolavatera is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its soothing properties.
