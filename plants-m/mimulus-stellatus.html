Mimulus stellatus

Mimulus stellatus, commonly known as the star flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive star-shaped appearance. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and open woodlands.

Mimulus stellatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
