Montia andina

Montia andina, commonly known as Andean watercress, is a member of the Montiaceae family and is native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous plant with small, alternate leaves that are often submerged or floating in water. The plant produces tiny, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery, spicy flavor similar to watercress.

Montia andina thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams and wetlands.
