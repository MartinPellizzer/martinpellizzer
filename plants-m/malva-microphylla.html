Malva microphylla

Malva microphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are smaller than those of its relatives, with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, pale pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Malva microphylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
