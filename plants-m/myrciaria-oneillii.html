Myrciaria oneillii

Myrciaria oneillii, commonly known as the O'Neill's myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit, a small, yellowish berry, is edible but not widely consumed by humans.

Myrciaria oneillii is often found in sandy soils near the shoreline, where it thrives in coastal climates.
