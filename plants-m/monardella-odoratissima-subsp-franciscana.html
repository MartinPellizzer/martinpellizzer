Monardella odoratissima subsp. franciscana

Monardella odoratissima subsp.

franciscana, commonly known as the Franciscan monardella, is a perennial herb native to California. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants.

This subspecies is characterized by its slender stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
