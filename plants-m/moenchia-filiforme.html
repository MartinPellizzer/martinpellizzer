Moenchia filiforme

Moenchia filiforme, commonly known as slender water millet, is a small annual grass native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It has slender, hair-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic environments, often growing in shallow water or moist soil. Its seeds are dispersed by water, contributing to its spread in wetland habitats.

Moenchia filiforme is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
