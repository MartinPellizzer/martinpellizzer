Micrampelis floribunda

Micrampelis floribunda, commonly known as the flame vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from orange to red, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible for some wildlife. Its rapid growth habit and ability to climb supports its use in ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to various soil types.
