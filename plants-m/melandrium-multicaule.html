Melandrium multicaule

Melandrium multicaule, commonly known as the common catchfly, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in pink or white. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Melandrium multicaule is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along roadsides.
