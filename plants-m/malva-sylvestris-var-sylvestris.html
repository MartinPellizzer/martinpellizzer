Malva sylvestris var. sylvestris

Malva sylvestris var. sylvestris, commonly known as the wild mallow, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its stems are hairy and often branched, while the roots are fibrous and adapted to moist environments.

This species is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along riverbanks.
