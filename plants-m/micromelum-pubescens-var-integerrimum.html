Micromelum pubescens var. integerrimum

Micromelum pubescens var. integerrimum, commonly known as the silky hibiscus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine in some regions.
