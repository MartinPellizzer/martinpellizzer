Maxillaria tristis

Maxillaria tristis, commonly known as the sad orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces dark purple to violet flowers with yellow markings, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Maxillaria tristis is also noted for its ability to thrive in cooler temperatures, which contributes to its unique blooming cycle.
