Mentha × schultziana var. dictyophylla

Mentha × schultziana var. dictyophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf peppermint, is a hybrid mint species characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and strong menthol scent.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is often cultivated for its essential oils and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with upright stems and dense foliage. Its flowers are small, tubular, and clustered in whorls, producing nectar that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued in both traditional medicine and aromatherapy for its cooling and soothing effects.
