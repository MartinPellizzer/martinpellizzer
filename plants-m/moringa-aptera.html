Moringa aptera

Moringa aptera, commonly known as the horseradish tree, is a fast-growing, drought-resistant plant native to northern India.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stem and bipinnately compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by long, slender pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. Moringa aptera is valued for its nutritional content, including high levels of vitamins and minerals.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its edible leaves, seeds, and oil.
