Maxonia

Maxonia, commonly known as the sacred tree of the ancient Maya, is a tropical plant with a distinctive, broad, fan-shaped foliage.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests. The plant has a thick, woody trunk and can grow quite tall, reaching heights of up to 30 feet. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine and rituals, and it holds cultural significance in Mesoamerican history.

Botanically, it belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms.
