Meladerma deciduum

Meladerma deciduum, commonly known as the brittleleaf nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, deciduous leaves that turn brown and fall off during dry seasons, allowing the plant to conserve water. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically violet or blue, which bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, hard seeds.

Due to its unique adaptations, Meladerma deciduum is an important species in ecological studies of xerophytic flora.
