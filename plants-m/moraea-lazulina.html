Moraea lazulina

Moraea lazulina, commonly known as the blue-eyed moraea, is a striking bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a compact, grass-like foliage and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue and purple. The plant typically blooms in spring, with its flowers rising on slender stalks above the leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
