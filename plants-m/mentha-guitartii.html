Mentha guitartii

Mentha guitartii, commonly known as the Corsican mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers in whorled clusters during the summer months. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Mentha guitartii is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
