Mentha × amplissima

Mentha × amplissima, commonly known as giant spearmint, is a hybrid mint species resulting from the cross between Mentha × piperita and Mentha × gracilis.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and serrated, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Mentha × amplissima is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves and is used in culinary and medicinal applications.
