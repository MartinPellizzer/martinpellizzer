Matricaria daucifolia

Matricaria daucifolia, commonly known as the common tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellow flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in moist meadows and woodland edges. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Matricaria daucifolia is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
