Malpighia suberosa

Malpighia suberosa, commonly known as the rough holly or wax apple, is a shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, oval-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of bright red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is adapted to well-drained soils.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
