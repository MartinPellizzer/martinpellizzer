Morus mizuho

Morus mizuho, commonly known as the Japanese mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fuzzy flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are commonly used in traditional Japanese cuisine. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its role in silk production, as its leaves are a primary food source for silkworms.
