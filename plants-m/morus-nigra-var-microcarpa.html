Morus nigra var. microcarpa

Morus nigra var. microcarpa, commonly known as the black mulberry, is a variant of the mulberry tree native to the Middle East and South Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are smaller than those of the common mulberry. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional medicine.
