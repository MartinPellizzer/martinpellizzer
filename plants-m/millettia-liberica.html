Millettia liberica

Millettia liberica, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pale yellow to white flowers. The tree produces long, flat pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riverine forests and savanna woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is also cultivated in botanical gardens and for reforestation projects.
