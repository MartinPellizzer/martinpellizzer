Mentha × rotundifolia f. gracilis

Mentha × rotundifolia f. gracilis, commonly known as narrow-leaf field mint, is a horticultural form of a hybrid mint species.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically shorter and narrower than those of the parent species. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that appear in whorled clusters along the stems. It is often cultivated for its aromatic foliage and is used in culinary and medicinal applications.

This form is well-suited for growing in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
