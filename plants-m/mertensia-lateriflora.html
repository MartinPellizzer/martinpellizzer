Mertensia lateriflora

Mertensia lateriflora, commonly known as the eastern bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and often have a bluish-green hue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Mertensia lateriflora is a member of the Violaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
