Myristica aruensis

Myristica aruensis, commonly known as the Aru nutmeg, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is closely related to the spice nutmeg (Myristica fragrans). The plant produces a unique fruit that contains a reddish-brown seed, which is the source of its aromatic oils. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a smooth surface with a distinctive scent.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
