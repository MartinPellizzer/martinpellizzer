Mertensia denticulata

Mertensia denticulata, commonly known as the toothed bluebells, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features striking clusters of bell-shaped, sky-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Mertensia denticulata is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to naturalize in woodland settings.
