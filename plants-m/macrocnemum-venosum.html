Macrocnemum venosum

Macrocnemum venosum, commonly known as the veined water plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow waters, such as ponds and slow-moving streams, where it can anchor itself to substrates with its rhizomes. The plant features long, slender leaves that are often veined and can float on the water surface or remain submerged. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually appearing in clusters, and it reproduces both sexually and vegetatively.

Macrocnemum venosum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms and contributing to water clarity through its dense growth.
