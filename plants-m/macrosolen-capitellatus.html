Macrosolen capitellatus

Macrosolen capitellatus, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive coral-like inflorescences, which consist of clusters of small, bright red or orange flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy appearance.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
