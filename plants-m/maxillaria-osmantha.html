Maxillaria osmantha

Maxillaria osmantha, commonly known as the Osmantha orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its terrestrial growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its pseudobulbs are cylindrical, and the leaves are elongated with a waxy texture.

The orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
