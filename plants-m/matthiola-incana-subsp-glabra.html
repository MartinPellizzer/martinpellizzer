Matthiola incana subsp. glabra

Matthiola incana subsp.

glabra, commonly known as wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to red and purple.

The plant has hairy leaves and stems, though the subspecies glabra is distinguished by its glabrous (hairless) surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation. Matthiola incana subsp.

glabra is also valued for its fragrance and is often used in ornamental horticulture.
