Myrtus angustissima

Myrtus angustissima, commonly known as the narrowleaf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, dark green leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and rocky gardens.

Myrtus angustissima is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery and culinary applications.
