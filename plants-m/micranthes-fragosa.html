Micranthes fragosa

Micranthes fragosa, commonly known as fragrant wintergreen, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
