Melilotus collinus

Melilotus collinus, commonly known as sweet clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions, including North America. It grows in a variety of habitats, including meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils.

Melilotus collinus is known for its high nectar content, making it an important plant for pollinators such as bees.
