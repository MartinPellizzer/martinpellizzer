Microstachys serrulata var. oncoblepharis

Microstachys serrulata var. oncoblepharis, commonly known as the serrated microstachys, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale to dark purple flowers, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
