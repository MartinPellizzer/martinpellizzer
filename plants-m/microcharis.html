Microcharis

Microcharis, commonly known as the fairy lantern, is a genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

These plants are native to Central and South America, where they thrive in tropical and subtropical climates. Microcharis species are characterized by their small, showy flowers that often resemble lanterns, hence their common name. They typically grow as herbaceous perennials, with some species developing into shrubs.

The genus includes several species, such as Microcharis corymbosa, which is known for its vibrant orange and yellow flowers.
