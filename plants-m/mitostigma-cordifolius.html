Mitostigma cordifolius

Mitostigma cordifolius, commonly known as the heart-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It has dark green, ovate to cordate leaves that are often heart-shaped, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its berries are red or orange and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

It plays an important ecological role by providing food for various wildlife species.
