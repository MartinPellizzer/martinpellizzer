Maytenus stylosa

Maytenus stylosa, commonly known as the Brazilian mulberry, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by spherical, fleshy fruits that turn red when mature.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
