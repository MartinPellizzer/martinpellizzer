Marrubium vulgare subsp. apulum

Marrubium vulgare subsp.

apulum, commonly known as rough horehound, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its strong, minty aroma.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
