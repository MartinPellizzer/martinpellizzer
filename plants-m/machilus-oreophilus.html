Machilus oreophilus

Machilus oreophilus, commonly known as the mountain machilus, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, dark berry that contains several seeds.

Machilus oreophilus is often found in moist, shaded environments in mountainous regions.
