Myrtus communis var. neapolitana

Myrtus communis var. neapolitana, commonly known as the Naples myrtle, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, dark green, leathery leaves that are aromatic when crushed. The plant produces tiny, white, five-petaled flowers in spring, followed by dark blue, edible berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value and fragrance make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
