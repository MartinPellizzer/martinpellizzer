Miconia candelabrum

Miconia candelabrum, commonly known as the candlewood tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and its distinctive, candle-like inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its flowers are typically white or pale yellow. It produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds.

Miconia candelabrum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
