Malpighia mitis

Malpighia mitis, commonly known as the acerola cherry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, bright red fruits. The fruit is rich in vitamin C and has a tart, acidic flavor, making it popular in culinary and medicinal uses. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional medicine.
