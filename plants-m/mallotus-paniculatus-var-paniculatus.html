Mallotus paniculatus var. paniculatus

Mallotus paniculatus var. paniculatus, commonly known as the paniculate mallot, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its compound leaves with opposite arrangement and serrated margins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in panicle-like inflorescences, which are often clustered at the ends of branches. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

The species is also noted for its ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions, making it adaptable to various habitats.
