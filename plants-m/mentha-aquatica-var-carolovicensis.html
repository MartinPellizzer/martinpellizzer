Mentha aquatica var. carolovicensis

Mentha aquatica var. carolovicensis, commonly known as the Carpathian water mint, is a variant of the water mint species native to the Carpathian region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a serrated margin and a strong, aromatic scent. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically purple or blue, arranged in whorls along the stem.

This variant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
