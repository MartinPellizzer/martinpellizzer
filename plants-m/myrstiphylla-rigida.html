Myrstiphylla rigida

Myrstiphylla rigida, commonly known as the rigid myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils. It has a compact, shrub-like form with glossy foliage and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

Myrstiphylla rigida is also valued for its aromatic properties and historical use in traditional medicine.
