Malcolmia aegyptiaca var. glabrescens

Malcolmia aegyptiaca var. glabrescens, commonly known as Egyptian malva, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces showy, pale yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
