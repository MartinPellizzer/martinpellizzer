Michelia amabilis

Michelia amabilis, commonly known as the fragrant michelia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. The plant produces showy, white to pale yellow flowers that are highly fragrant and bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Michelia amabilis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
