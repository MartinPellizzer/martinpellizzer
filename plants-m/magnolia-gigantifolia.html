Magnolia gigantifolia

Magnolia gigantifolia, commonly known as the giant magnolia, is a large evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its massive, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter, typically white or pale yellow in color. The tree can grow to heights of over 30 meters, with a broad, spreading canopy. Its leaves are large, leathery, and elliptical, providing dense shade beneath the tree.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained, fertile soil for optimal growth.
