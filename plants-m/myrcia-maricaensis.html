Myrcia maricaensis

Myrcia maricaensis, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that appear in spring. The fruit is a capsule containing numerous small, dark seeds, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers also make it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
