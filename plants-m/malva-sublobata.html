Malva sublobata

Malva sublobata, commonly known as the narrow-lobed mallows, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton, hibiscus, and okra. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its fibrous roots and hairy stems are characteristic of the genus Malva.
