Marica plicata

Marica plicata, commonly known as the zigzag plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to purple. The plant has a creeping growth habit with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that often have a zigzag or wavy edge, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
