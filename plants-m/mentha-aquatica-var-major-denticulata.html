Mentha aquatica var. major-denticulata

Mentha aquatica var. major-denticulata, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist environments in Europe and Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly toothed or serrated along the edges. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in wet soils near streams, ponds, and marshes, and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

Its aromatic leaves and stems are used in herbal remedies and as a culinary herb.
