Malaxis maculata

Malaxis maculata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with distinctive dark spots on the petals and sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption in poor soil conditions.

Malaxis maculata is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
