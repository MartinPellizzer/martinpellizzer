Miconia argyrophylla subsp. argyrophylla

Miconia argyrophylla subsp.

argyrophylla, commonly known as the silver-leaf miconia, is a member of the Melastomataceae family. It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of silvery-white hairs, giving it its common name. It produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

The species is often found in humid, shaded environments within the understory of tropical rainforests.
