Marsilea mucronata

Marsilea mucronata, commonly known as the water clover, is a small aquatic fern that thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its distinctive four-leaf clover-like appearance, with each leaflet being a small, rounded structure. The plant typically grows in dense mats on the surface of ponds, lakes, and wetlands, where it can float or be submerged. Its reproductive structures include spore-bearing sorus, which are located on the undersides of the leaves.

Marsilea mucronata plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small aquatic life.
