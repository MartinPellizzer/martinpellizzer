Muraltia exilis

Muraltia exilis, commonly known as the slender muraltia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in humid, shaded environments such as forests and undergrowth.
