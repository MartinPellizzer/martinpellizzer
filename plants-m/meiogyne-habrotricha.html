Meiogyne habrotricha

Meiogyne habrotricha, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically in the understorey of tropical forests. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a source of starch.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
