Musa balbisiana var. sepa-athiya

Musa balbisiana var. sepa-athiya, commonly known as the Sepa-Atiya banana, is a variant of the banana plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and produces clusters of elongated, yellow fruits that are typically harvested when they are still green. The plant is a herbaceous perennial, growing from a rhizome and reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

The fruit is edible and has a mild, sweet flavor, making it a popular crop in its native regions.
