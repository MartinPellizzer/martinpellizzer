Miconia phaeophylla

Miconia phaeophylla, commonly known as the dark-leaf miconia, is a tropical plant belonging to the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to the Amazon Basin and is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Miconia phaeophylla is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain environments.
