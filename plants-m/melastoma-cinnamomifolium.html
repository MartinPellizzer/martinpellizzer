Melastoma cinnamomifolium

Melastoma cinnamomifolium, commonly known as the cinnamon-leaf melastoma, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that emit a cinnamon-like scent. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dark seeds.

Melastoma cinnamomifolium is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical regions.
