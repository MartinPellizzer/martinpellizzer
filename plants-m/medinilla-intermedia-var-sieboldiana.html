Medinilla intermedia var. sieboldiana

Medinilla intermedia var. sieboldiana, commonly known as the velvet tangerine.

This tropical plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that give it a velvety texture. It produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant is native to the Philippines and thrives in warm, humid climates.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
