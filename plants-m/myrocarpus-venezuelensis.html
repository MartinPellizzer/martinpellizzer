Myrocarpus venezuelensis

Myrocarpus venezuelensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Brazilian mahogany, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Lythraceae and is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by bright red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some bird species. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to saline soils.
