Myrciaria cuspidata var. diffusa

Myrciaria cuspidata var. diffusa, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark purple to black berries. The plant features elliptical leaves with a glossy surface and a dense, aromatic foliage. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in humid, shaded environments.

The berries are edible, with a tart flavor, and are used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
