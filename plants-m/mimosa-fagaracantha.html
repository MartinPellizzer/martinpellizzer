Mimosa fagaracantha

Mimosa fagaracantha, commonly known as the silver mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellow, spherical flower heads that appear in clusters, typically in the spring. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into many small leaflets, giving it a feathery appearance.

Mimosa fagaracantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
