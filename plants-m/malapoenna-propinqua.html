Malapoenna propinqua

Malapoenna propinqua, commonly known as the Malapoenna bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and features narrow, leathery leaves that are dark green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers during the late summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. These fruits are edible and contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

Malapoenna propinqua is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
