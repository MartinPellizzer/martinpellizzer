Mulgedium tataricum var. polyphyllum

Mulgedium tataricum var. polyphyllum, commonly known as the Siberian marsh orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold regions of Asia.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in nutrient-poor soils. The plant features a slender, erect stem with a cluster of showy, violet-blue flowers at the top. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, adapting to its moist environment.

This variant is distinguished by its multiple leaves, giving it the species name *polyphyllum*, meaning "many-leaved.".
