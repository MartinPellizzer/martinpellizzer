Myroxylon dicline

Myroxylon dicline, commonly known as the pink wood or rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark reddish-brown wood, which is highly valued for its rich color and fine grain. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, pinnate, and often have a glossy appearance.

The tree is also notable for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soils.
