Malva albiflora

Malva albiflora, commonly known as the white mallow, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture. It grows up to 1 meter tall and prefers well-drained soil in full sun.

Malva albiflora is often found in meadows and along roadsides, and it is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in herbal medicine.
