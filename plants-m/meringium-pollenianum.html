Meringium pollenianum

Meringium pollenianum, commonly known as the silverleaf meringia, is a rare flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its delicate, silver-hued leaves and clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers. The plant produces a unique type of pollen that is highly fragrant and attracts specific pollinators such as certain species of bees and moths. Its botanical structure includes a short, woody stem with a fibrous root system adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its limited habitat and slow growth rate, Meringium pollenianum is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
