Malapoenna venulosa

Malapoenna venulosa, commonly known as the Malapoenna vine, is a climbing plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round berry that changes color from green to red as it matures.

Malapoenna venulosa is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
