Maxillaria chlorochila

Maxillaria chlorochila, commonly known as the yellow lady's slipper, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, with a distinctive pouch-like structure called a lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, and it produces fragrant blooms during the summer months.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses and tropical gardens.
