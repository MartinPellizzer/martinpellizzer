Millettia hookeriana

Millettia hookeriana, commonly known as the Hooker's millettia, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
