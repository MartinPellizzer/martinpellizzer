Melastoma dubium

Melastoma dubium, commonly known as the small-leaved melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its small, opposite leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are often pink or purple and attract various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Melastoma dubium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
