Myriactis mindanaensis

Myriactis mindanaensis, commonly known as the Mindanao myriapod, is a unique plant species native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its intricate, fern-like foliage that resembles the structure of a myriapod, hence its scientific name. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in the undergrowth of tropical rainforests. Its leaves are typically broad and arranged in a spiral pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
