Myrcia cuprea

Myrcia cuprea, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, reddish-brown fruits and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, while its flowers are usually white or pale pink.

Myrcia cuprea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
