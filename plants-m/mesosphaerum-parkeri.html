Mesosphaerum parkeri

Mesosphaerum parkeri, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stem and palm-like appearance, with long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its sap contains milky latex, which has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties.

Mesosphaerum parkeri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience in various environmental conditions.
