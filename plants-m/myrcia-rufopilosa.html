Myrcia rufopilosa

Myrcia rufopilosa, commonly known as the red-haired myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with reddish-pink undersides. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.
