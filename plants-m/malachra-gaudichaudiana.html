Malachra gaudichaudiana

Malachra gaudichaudiana, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets and produces woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Malachra gaudichaudiana is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
