Miconia sulcicaulis

Miconia sulcicaulis, commonly known as the ridged miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed and have a distinctive ridged texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains numerous seeds.

Miconia sulcicaulis is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
