Momordica spicata

Momordica spicata, commonly known as the spiny bitter gourd, is a climbing vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and elongated, warty fruits that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces yellow flowers and has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is often cultivated for its edible fruits.

The fruit is typically harvested when immature, as it becomes too bitter when fully mature.
