Melongena incurva

Melongena incurva, commonly known as the curled eggplant, is a unique species within the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often curved fruit shape, which sets it apart from other eggplant varieties. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and lobed leaves. Its flowers are small and yellow, with five petals, and it produces fruits that can range in color from green to purple as they mature.

This plant is native to tropical regions of Africa and is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits.
