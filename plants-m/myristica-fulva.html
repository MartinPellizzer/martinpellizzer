Myristica fulva

Myristica fulva, commonly known as the "Seychelles nutmeg" or "Ceylon nutmeg," is a small evergreen tree native to the Mascarene Islands.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant flowers and spherical fruits that contain a single seed, which is used to produce the spice nutmeg.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique aromatic compounds and its role in traditional medicine and perfumery.
