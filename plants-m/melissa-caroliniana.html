Melissa caroliniana

Melissa caroliniana, commonly known as American balm, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that have a fragrant, mint-like aroma. Its small, yellowish-green flowers bloom in whorls along the stem, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Melissa caroliniana is often used in herbal medicine for its calming and digestive properties.
