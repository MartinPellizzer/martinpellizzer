Melanocarya alata

Melanocarya alata, commonly known as the winged poplar, is a deciduous tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, winged branches that give it a unique appearance. The tree has heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall, and it produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

The bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age, and the tree is known for its resilience in harsh climates.
