Mentha viridis var. walteriana

Mentha viridis var. walteriana, commonly known as Walter's mint, is a variant of the green mint species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
