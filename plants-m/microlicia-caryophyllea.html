Microlicia caryophyllea

Microlicia caryophyllea, commonly known as the Christmas star, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 cm. The plant is characterized by its dense clusters of small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in winter. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and have a distinctive, aromatic scent.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to coastal climates.
