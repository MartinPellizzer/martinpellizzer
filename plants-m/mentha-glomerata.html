Mentha glomerata

Mentha glomerata, commonly known as wild mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and have a strong, cool menthol scent.

Mentha glomerata is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
