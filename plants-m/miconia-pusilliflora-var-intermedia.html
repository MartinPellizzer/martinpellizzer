Miconia pusilliflora var. intermedia

Miconia pusilliflora var. intermedia, commonly known as the small-flowered miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in certain regions.
