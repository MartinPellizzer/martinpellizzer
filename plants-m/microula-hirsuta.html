Microula hirsuta

Microula hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy microula, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Sudan and Ethiopia. The plant is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish flowers. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Microula hirsuta is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
