Muhlenbergia schreberi subsp. palustris

Muhlenbergia schreberi subsp.

palustris, commonly known as swamp muhlenbergia, is a perennial grass native to wetland habitats in the eastern United States. It typically grows in marshy areas, along stream banks, and in other moist environments.

The plant has slender, arching stems that produce small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
