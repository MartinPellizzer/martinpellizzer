Monarda austromontana

Monarda austromontana, commonly known as the eastern bee balm, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and fragrant leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple, typically arranged in dense, terminal clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and emit a spicy, mint-like aroma when crushed.

Monarda austromontana is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
