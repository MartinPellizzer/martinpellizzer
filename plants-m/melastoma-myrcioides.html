Melastoma myrcioides

Melastoma myrcioides, commonly known as the purple mangosteen, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant produces edible, segmented fruits that are typically purple or red when ripe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Melastoma myrcioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
