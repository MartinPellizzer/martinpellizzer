Maxillaria hirtzii

Maxillaria hirtzii, commonly known as the Hirtz's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a yellowish throat. Its leaves are typically elliptical and arranged in two rows, while the roots are fibrous and adapted to its humid, shaded habitat.

Maxillaria hirtzii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
