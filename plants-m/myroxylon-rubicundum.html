Myroxylon rubicundum

Myroxylon rubicundum, commonly known as the Copaiba tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, aromatic bark and can grow up to 30 meters in height. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers and is known for its latex, which is used in the production of copal resin. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
