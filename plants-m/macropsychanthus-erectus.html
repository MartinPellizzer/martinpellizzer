Macropsychanthus erectus

Macropsychanthus erectus, commonly known as the upright ghost flower, is a rare and elusive plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, ghost-like appearance, with pale, translucent petals that resemble spectral forms. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom at night, attracting specific pollinators such as moths. Its growth is slow and requires well-drained soil and minimal water, making it a challenging species to cultivate.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
