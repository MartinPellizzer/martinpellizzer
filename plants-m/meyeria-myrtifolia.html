Meyeria myrtifolia

Meyeria myrtifolia, commonly known as the slender myrtle, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. Its small white flowers are typically arranged in clusters and bloom during the warmer months.

The plant is valued for its fragrant leaves and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
