Manilkara bahamensis

Manilkara bahamensis, commonly known as the Bahamian sapodilla, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible fruits that are similar in appearance to cherries but have a sweet, slightly gritty pulp. Its latex is used in traditional medicine and has potential applications in modern pharmacology.

The plant thrives in tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and limestone formations.
