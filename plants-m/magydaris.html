Magydaris

Magydaris, commonly known as the common thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pinkish flower heads, which consist of many small florets. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is found in various regions across Europe and parts of Asia. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive prickly texture.

Magydaris is often used in traditional medicine and is also a host plant for several species of butterflies.
