Myrceugenia acutata

Myrceugenia acutata, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, edible berries. The plant produces small, black fruits that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed areas.

Myrceugenia acutata plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting local wildlife and contributing to biodiversity.
