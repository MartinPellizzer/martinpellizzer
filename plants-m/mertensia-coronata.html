Mertensia coronata

Mertensia coronata, commonly known as bluebells, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Mertensia coronata is a member of the Boraginaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in woodland gardens.
