Mentha × calaminthiformis

Mentha × calaminthiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mint, is a hybrid plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and flowering spikes that bear small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to their menthol-like fragrance.

Mentha × calaminthiformis is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
