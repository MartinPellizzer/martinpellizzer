Mentha haplocalyx var. barbata

Mentha haplocalyx var. barbata, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a perennial herb native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its aromatic leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.
