Mentha longifolia var. wirzbickiana

Mentha longifolia var. wirzbickiana, commonly known as the Wirzbickiana mint, is a variant of the long-leaf mint native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typical of the Mentha genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.
