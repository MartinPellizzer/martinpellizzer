Muraltia ferox

Muraltia ferox, commonly known as the red mombin or wild mombin, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, showy flowers. The plant produces yellow or orange fruits that are edible but have a sour taste. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forested areas.

Muraltia ferox is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
