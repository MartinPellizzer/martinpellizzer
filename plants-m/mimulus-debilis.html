Mimulus debilis

Mimulus debilis, commonly known as the weak yellow monkeyflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters, featuring delicate, spurred yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually oval to lance-shaped with smooth or slightly hairy surfaces. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and disturbed areas.

Mimulus debilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
