Malva rodigii

Malva rodigii, commonly known as the Persian mallow, is a flowering plant native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and thrives in well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Malva rodigii is also known for its mucilaginous properties, making it useful in traditional medicine.
