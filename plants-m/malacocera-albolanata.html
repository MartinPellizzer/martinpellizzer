Malacocera albolanata

Malacocera albolanata, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a tropical plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its elegant, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has strap-like, dark green leaves and thrives in humid, warm environments with indirect sunlight. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and unique floral structure.

Malacocera albolanata requires well-draining soil and consistent moisture to flourish.
