Murianthe

Murianthe, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences. Murianthe is often found growing in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions make it a common weed in many tropical ecosystems.
