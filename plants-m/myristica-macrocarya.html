Myristica macrocarya

Myristica macrocarya, commonly known as the giant nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen belonging to the Myristicaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and produces fragrant, reddish flowers that develop into woody capsules containing aromatic seeds. The seeds, known as mace, are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Its botanical structure includes a thick, branched trunk and can grow up to 30 meters tall in its natural habitat.
