Maxillaria polypetala

Maxillaria polypetala, commonly known as the Polypetal Maxillaria, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant produces showy, butterfly-shaped petals and a prominent, hairy lip that distinguishes it from other orchid species. It thrives in humid, well-drained environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Maxillaria polypetala is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relative ease of cultivation.
