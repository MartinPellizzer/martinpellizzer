Matthiola anchoniifolia

Matthiola anchoniifolia, commonly known as the Anchonii wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known species like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of yellow and orange. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Matthiola anchoniifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
