Macrolobium suaveolens var. petiolatum

Macrolobium suaveolens var. petiolatum, commonly known as the fragrant leaf macrolobium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that emit a sweet, aromatic scent. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers with a subtle fragrance, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
