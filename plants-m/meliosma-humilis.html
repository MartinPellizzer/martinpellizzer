Meliosma humilis

Meliosma humilis, commonly known as the creeping meliosma, is a small evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features simple, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and have a smooth texture. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in early spring, followed by small, capsule-like fruits.

Meliosma humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded garden settings.
