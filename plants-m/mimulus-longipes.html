Mimulus longipes

Mimulus longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spurred petals that resemble a monkey's face, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny environments such as meadows and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in spring and are pollinated primarily by bees and butterflies.

Mimulus longipes is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinator populations.
