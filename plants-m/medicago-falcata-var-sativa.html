Medicago falcata var. sativa

Medicago falcata var. sativa, commonly known as the common medick, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is well adapted to dry and disturbed soils, often found in meadows, fields, and along roadsides. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and improves soil fertility through nitrogen fixation.

This versatile plant is also cultivated as a forage crop for livestock due to its nutritional value and hardiness.
