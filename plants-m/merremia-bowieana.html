Merremia bowieana

Merremia bowieana, commonly known as the Bowie's morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple and white. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Due to its rapid growth and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
