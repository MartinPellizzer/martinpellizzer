Myrospermum emarginatum

Myrospermum emarginatum, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces showy, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. The plant typically blooms from late winter to early summer, and its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators. Myrospermum emarginatum prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in arid conditions.
