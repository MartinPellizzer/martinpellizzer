Microchilus globosus

Microchilus globosus, commonly known as the globular lichen, is a species of lichen that belongs to the family Lecanoraceae.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth form, forming a crust-like structure on rock surfaces. The thallus is typically yellowish to orange in color, with a smooth and shiny appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found on bark or soil in temperate regions.

This lichen is known for its slow growth and is used in ecological studies to monitor air quality and environmental changes.
