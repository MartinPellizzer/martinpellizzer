Mesembryanthemum chilense

Mesembryanthemum chilense, commonly known as the Chilean ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its fleshy, often blue or green leaves and small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, white, and pink. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Mesembryanthemum chilense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
