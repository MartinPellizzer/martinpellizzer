Mikania erioclada

Mikania erioclada, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and extensive root system. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

Due to its invasive nature, it can outcompete native plants and is considered a weed in many regions.
