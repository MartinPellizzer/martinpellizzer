Morus ribesioides

Morus ribesioides, commonly known as the Chinese mulberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, followed by dark purple or black berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and its role in ecological restoration projects.
