Melocalamus truongsonensis

Melocalamus truongsonensis, commonly known as the Truong Son bamboo, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface, typical of many bamboo species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
