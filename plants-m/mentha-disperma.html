Mentha disperma

Mentha disperma, commonly known as meadow mint, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are elongated, serrated, and have a fresh, minty aroma.

The small, tubular flowers are usually pale blue or purple and appear in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
