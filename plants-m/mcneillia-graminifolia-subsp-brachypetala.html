Mcneillia graminifolia subsp. brachypetala

Mcneillia graminifolia subsp.

brachypetala, commonly known as the short-petaled grassleaf mallow, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and showy, pale pink to white flowers.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to arid environments with minimal water requirements.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
