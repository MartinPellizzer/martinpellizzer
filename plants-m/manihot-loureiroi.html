Manihot loureiroi

Manihot loureiroi, commonly known as arrowleaf manioc, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the spurge family. Its roots are used in traditional medicine and as a source of starch.

However, the plant contains toxic compounds, requiring careful processing before consumption.
