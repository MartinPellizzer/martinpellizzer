Mentha asiatica

Mentha asiatica, commonly known as the Japanese mint, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and spikes of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their cooling and digestive properties.

Mentha asiatica is closely related to other mint species and is valued for its refreshing scent and culinary applications.
