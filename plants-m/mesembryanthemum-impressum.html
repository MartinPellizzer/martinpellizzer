Mesembryanthemum impressum

Mesembryanthemum impressum, commonly known as the African daisy, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, often hairy leaves and vibrant, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments. Its flowers are usually pink or yellow, and they bloom in spring and summer.

Mesembryanthemum impressum is also known for its ability to store water in its thick leaves, aiding in its survival in dry conditions.
