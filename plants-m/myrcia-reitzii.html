Myrcia reitzii

Myrcia reitzii, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae, characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Myrcia reitzii is also valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumes and traditional medicine.
