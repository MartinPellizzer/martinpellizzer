Miconia frontinoana

Miconia frontinoana, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, orange berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
