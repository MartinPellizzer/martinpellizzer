Metasequoia Glyptostroboides Subsp. Caespitosa

Metasequoia glyptostroboides subsp. caespitosa is a rare and ancient conifer native to eastern china. it is often referred to as the "dawn redwood" due to its rediscovery in the 20th century. this subspecies is characterized by its compact growth and dense foliage. it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial shade. its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
