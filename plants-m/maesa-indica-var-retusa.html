Maesa indica var. retusa

Maesa indica var. retusa, commonly known as the hairy maesa, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its dense, glandular hairs on the stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. Its leaves are elliptical with a leathery texture and are often arranged alternately along the stems.

The flowers are small, white or pink, and are clustered in inflorescences, contributing to its ornamental value.
