Musa johnsii

Musa johnsii, commonly known as the African banana, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can grow up to three meters in length. The plant produces clustered, elongated fruit that is typically eaten raw or cooked. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Musa johnsii is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
