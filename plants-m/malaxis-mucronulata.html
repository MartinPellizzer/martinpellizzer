Malaxis mucronulata

Malaxis mucronulata, commonly known as the narrow-tipped Malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a loose spike, which are often overlooked due to their modest appearance. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

The species is valued for its unique morphology and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental appeal.
