Melocactus aciculosus

Melocactus aciculosus, commonly known as the feathered melon, is a cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and is covered in dense, white, woolly hair. The plant produces a large, spiny fruit that is typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice for collectors and desert gardeners.
