Mentha × dumetorum var. hirta

Mentha × dumetorum var. hirta, commonly known as hemp mint, is a hybrid plant belonging to the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its hairy stems and serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in whorled clusters, typical of the mint genus. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Due to its aromatic properties, it has been used historically in herbal medicine and as a culinary herb.
