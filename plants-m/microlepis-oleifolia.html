Microlepis oleifolia

Microlepis oleifolia, commonly known as the oilleaf mallow, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are coated with a waxy layer, giving them a shiny appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, allowing it to thrive in harsh environments.

Microlepis oleifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
