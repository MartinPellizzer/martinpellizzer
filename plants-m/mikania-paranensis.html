Mikania paranensis

Mikania paranensis, commonly known as the Brazilian morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and distinctive heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, which bloom in the morning. Mikania paranensis is often found in moist, tropical environments and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its ornamental value is also well recognized.
