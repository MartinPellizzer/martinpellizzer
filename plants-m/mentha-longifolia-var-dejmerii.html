Mentha longifolia var. dejmerii

Mentha longifolia var. dejmerii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves that are typically less than 2 cm in length. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue or purple, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

This variety is valued for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.
