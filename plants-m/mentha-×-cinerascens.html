Mentha × cinerascens

Mentha × cinerascens, commonly known as gray mint, is a hybrid plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves and a strong, aromatic scent. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb, often found in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are small, tubular, and clustered in whorls, producing nectar that attracts pollinators.

Mentha × cinerascens is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
