Mimosa capito

Mimosa capito, commonly known as the sensitive plant, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Mimosa capito is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and rapid growth habit.
