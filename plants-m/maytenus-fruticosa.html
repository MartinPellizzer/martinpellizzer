Maytenus fruticosa

Maytenus fruticosa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or wild cherry, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant bears red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Maytenus fruticosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
