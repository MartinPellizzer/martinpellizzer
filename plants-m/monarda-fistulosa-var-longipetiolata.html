Monarda fistulosa var. longipetiolata

Monarda fistulosa var. longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled wild bergamot, is a perennial herb native to the eastern and central United States.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with long petioles, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces showy, tubular, pink to violet flowers in whorled clusters, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
