Malus ioensis var. palmeri

Malus ioensis var. palmeri, commonly known as the Palmer's crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental white flowers and red or orange fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its foliage provides seasonal color, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The fruits are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes than culinary use.
