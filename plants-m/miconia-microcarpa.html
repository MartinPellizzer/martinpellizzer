Miconia microcarpa

Miconia microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited miconia, is a species of flowering plant in the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. It produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature.

Miconia microcarpa is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests and is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets.
