Malaxis porphyrea

Malaxis porphyrea, commonly known as the purple lady's tresses, is an epiphytic orchid native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green leaves and long, spiraled inflorescences that bear small, violet-blue flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks in tropical forests. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrates.

Malaxis porphyrea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
