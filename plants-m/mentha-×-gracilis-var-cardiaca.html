Mentha × gracilis var. cardiaca

Mentha × gracilis var. cardiaca, commonly known as heart-shaped mint, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and elongated, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden areas.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
