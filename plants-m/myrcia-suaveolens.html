Myrcia suaveolens

Myrcia suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruits are small, red berries that contain seeds, and the plant is valued for its aromatic properties.

Myrcia suaveolens is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
