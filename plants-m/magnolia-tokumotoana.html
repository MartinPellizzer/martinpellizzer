Magnolia tokumotoana

Magnolia tokumotoana, commonly known as the Tokumoto magnolia, is a rare and ornamental species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has broad, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

The bark is smooth and grayish, with a slightly flaky texture, and it prefers well-drained, acidic soils in partial shade.
