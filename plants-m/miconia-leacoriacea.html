Miconia leacoriacea

Miconia leacoriacea, commonly known as the laceleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom throughout the year, followed by round, red or orange fruits. Its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets make it an invasive species in some regions.

The leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
