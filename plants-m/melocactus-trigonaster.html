Melocactus trigonaster

Melocactus trigonaster, commonly known as the three-armed melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, ribbed stem that typically grows in a globular or columnar shape. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its spines are short and often covered in a fine, white coating known as bloom.

Melocactus trigonaster is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
