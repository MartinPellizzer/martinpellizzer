Miconia acunae

Miconia acunae, commonly known as the red miconia, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often bright green with a reddish underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by distinctive, red, fleshy fruits. Miconia acunae is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

However, it is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its aggressive spread and impact on native ecosystems.
