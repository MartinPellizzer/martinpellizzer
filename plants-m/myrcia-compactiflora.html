Myrcia compactiflora

Myrcia compactiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are opposite, elliptic, and have a glossy surface with a strong, aromatic scent.

Myrcia compactiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other parts of the world, sometimes becoming an invasive species.
