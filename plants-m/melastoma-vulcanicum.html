Melastoma vulcanicum

Melastoma vulcanicum, commonly known as the volcanic melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and valley floors. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a leathery texture, while its fruit is a capsule that disperses seeds by wind.

Melastoma vulcanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems.
