Mougeotia

Mougeotia, commonly known as a type of green algae, is a freshwater organism belonging to the genus Mougeotia within the family Mougeotidiaceae.

It is characterized by its filamentous structure, composed of unbranched, multicellular filaments that often form a gelatinous sheath. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Mougeotia reproduces both asexually through vegetative propagation and sexually via the production of zoospores.

Its simple morphology and ecological adaptability make it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
