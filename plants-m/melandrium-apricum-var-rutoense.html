Melandrium apricum var. rutoense

Melandrium apricum var. rutoense, commonly known as the yellow catchfly, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and can be found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dry seeds.
