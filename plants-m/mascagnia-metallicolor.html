Mascagnia metallicolor

Mascagnia metallicolor, commonly known as the silver leaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the dry, rocky regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage, which is covered in fine, reflective hairs that give it a metallic appearance. The plant produces showy, pale yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes in arid climates.
