Marcus-kochia triloba

Marcus-kochia triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed kochia, is a flowering plant native to central Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 1 meter tall, featuring lance-shaped leaves with three distinct lobes. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
