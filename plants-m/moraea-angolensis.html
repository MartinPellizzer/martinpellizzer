Moraea angolensis

Moraea angolensis, commonly known as the Angola lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette.

Moraea angolensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its elegant appearance and long flowering period.
