Matricaria achilleifolia

Matricaria achilleifolia, commonly known as yarrow, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and clusters of small, white to yellowish flowers. The plant is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Matricaria achilleifolia has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
