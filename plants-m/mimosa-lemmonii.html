Mimosa lemmonii

Mimosa lemmonii, commonly known as the Lemmon's mimosa, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its soft, feathery foliage and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through its root nodules. Mimosa lemmonii is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in arid environments.

Its delicate appearance and unique growth habits make it a distinctive addition to native plant gardens.
