Malvastrum hispidum

Malvastrum hispidum, commonly known as the hairy mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Malvastrum hispidum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
