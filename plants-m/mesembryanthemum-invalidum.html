Mesembryanthemum invalidum

Mesembryanthemum invalidum, commonly known as the invalid's flower, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that store water, adapting to arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its resilience and ornamental appeal make it a valued addition to xeriscaping and drought-tolerant landscapes.
