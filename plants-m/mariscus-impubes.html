Mariscus impubes

Mariscus impubes, commonly known as the silverleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and showy pink to purple flowers. The plant has small, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. It produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds.

Mariscus impubes is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
