Mandevilla tomentosa

Mandevilla tomentosa, commonly known as the fuzzy mandevilla, is a tropical vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of tubular, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil and ample sunlight.

Mandevilla tomentosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its showy blooms and adaptability to container gardening.
