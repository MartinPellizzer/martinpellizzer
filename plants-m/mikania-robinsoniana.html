Mikania robinsoniana

Mikania robinsoniana, commonly known as the tropical gothweed, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and trailing habit. The plant features small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and often have a distinctive reddish or purple underside.

Mikania robinsoniana is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
