Mentha longifolia var. subviridis

Mentha longifolia var. subviridis, commonly known as creeping mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender stems that often root at the nodes. The leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and have a distinctive minty aroma. It produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in whorls along the stems.

This variety is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its aromatic properties.
