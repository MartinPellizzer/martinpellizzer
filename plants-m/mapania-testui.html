Mapania testui

Mapania testui, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow petals and a strong, spicy fragrance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrates.

Mapania testui is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural and medicinal significance in traditional practices.
