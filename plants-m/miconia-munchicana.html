Miconia munchicana

Miconia munchicana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or red miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter, often with a reddish or bronze hue on the underside. The plant produces showy, red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature, containing seeds enclosed in a fleshy pulp.

Miconia munchicana is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
