Malpighia habanensis

Malpighia habanensis, commonly known as the Havana beauty or Cuban acerola, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red, edible berries that are rich in vitamin C and other nutrients. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and nutritional benefits, it is cultivated in gardens and for commercial use.
