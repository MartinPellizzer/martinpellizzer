Miconia tococordata

Miconia tococordata, commonly known as the red miconia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are usually a deep green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. Its flowers are small and clustered, often with a reddish or pinkish hue, and it produces berries that are orange to red in color.

Miconia tococordata is known for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
