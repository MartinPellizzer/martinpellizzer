Melastoma harmsianum

Melastoma harmsianum, commonly known as the purple mangostin, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its showy, often pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are opposite and often have a velvety texture, while its fruit is a small, segmented berry.

Melastoma harmsianum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
