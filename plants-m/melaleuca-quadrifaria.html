Melaleuca quadrifaria

Melaleuca quadrifaria, commonly known as the four-leaf melaleuca, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and showy, pinkish-red flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits called capsules that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

Melaleuca quadrifaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal dunes.
