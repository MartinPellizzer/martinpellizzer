Mallotus tenuifolius var. paxii

Mallotus tenuifolius var. paxii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallot, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are usually less than 5 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.

This species is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
