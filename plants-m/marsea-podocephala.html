Marsea podocephala

Marsea podocephala, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with silvery-blue foliage that reflects sunlight. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Marsea podocephala is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
