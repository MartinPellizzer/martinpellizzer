Matthissonia paniculata

Matthissonia paniculata, commonly known as the Cape umbrella plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its spherical, umbrella-like inflorescence composed of numerous small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. It produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds, aiding in its propagation along the coastline.

Matthissonia paniculata is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
