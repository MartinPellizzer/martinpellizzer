Milium effusum f. lerchenfeldianum

Milium effusum f. lerchenfeldianum, commonly known as the Lerchenfeld's millet, is a variant of the millet plant characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish spikelets that are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a soft, velvety texture.

This form is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
