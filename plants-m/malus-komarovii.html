Malus komarovii

Malus komarovii, commonly known as the Komarov apple tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Far East of Russia and parts of China.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy white flowers in early spring and vibrant red or orange berries in the fall. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.

The berries are edible but not typically consumed by humans due to their tart flavor.
