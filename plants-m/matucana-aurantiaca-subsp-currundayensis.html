Matucana aurantiaca subsp. currundayensis

Matucana aurantiaca subsp.

currundayensis, commonly known as the Currundayensis Pothos, is a member of the Araceae family. It is native to South America, specifically Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically bright green with a slight variegation. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by spathes, a common feature in the Araceae family.

This subspecies is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
