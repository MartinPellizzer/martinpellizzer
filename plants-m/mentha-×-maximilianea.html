Mentha × maximilianea

Mentha × maximilianea, commonly known as catnip, is a hybrid mint species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its square, erect stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Mentha × maximilianea is widely cultivated for its medicinal properties and as a source of essential oils.
