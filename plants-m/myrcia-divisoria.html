Myrcia divisoria

Myrcia divisoria, commonly known as the split myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its dark green, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Myrcia divisoria is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
