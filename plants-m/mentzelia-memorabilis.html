Mentzelia memorabilis

Mentzelia memorabilis, commonly known as the desert evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the late afternoon and remain open through the night. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Mentzelia memorabilis is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures, making it a resilient member of desert ecosystems.
