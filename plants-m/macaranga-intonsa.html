Macaranga intonsa

Macaranga intonsa, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with smooth, light-gray bark. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a silvery underside due to dense pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.
