Myrtus amara

Myrtus amara, commonly known as bitter myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and white flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but have a bitter taste. Myrtus amara is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
