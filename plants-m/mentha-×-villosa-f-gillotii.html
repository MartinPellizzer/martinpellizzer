Mentha × villosa f. gillotii

Mentha × villosa f. gillotii, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a hybrid plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and square, often reddish stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems.

This species is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in herbal teas or as a culinary herb.
