Medicago graeca

Medicago graeca, commonly known as Greek clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. Native to southern Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment.

Its seeds are edible and have been used in traditional cuisine and herbal medicine.
