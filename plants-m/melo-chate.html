Melo chate

Melo chate, commonly known as the Chinese date or longan, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It produces fragrant, edible fruits that are typically harvested when they are still green, though they ripen to a reddish color. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are oval-shaped with a smooth texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is valued for its sweet, juicy pulp and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
