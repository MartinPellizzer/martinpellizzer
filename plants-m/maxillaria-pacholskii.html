Maxillaria pacholskii

Maxillaria pacholskii, commonly known as the Pacholski's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Brazil.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizome and pseudobulbs that support its flowering structure. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with darker pigments. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, arranged in a rosette pattern around the pseudobulbs.

Maxillaria pacholskii is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or warm climates.
