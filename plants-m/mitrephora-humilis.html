Mitrephora humilis

Mitrephora humilis, commonly known as the dwarf mitrephora, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes cashews and mangoes. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, spiny fruits.

Mitrephora humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
