Monotropa latisquama

Monotropa latisquama, commonly known as the broad-leaf ghostplant, is a climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It is a hemiparasitic plant, meaning it partially depends on other plants for water and nutrients through its specialized roots. The plant lacks chlorophyll and is therefore unable to perform photosynthesis, relying instead on its host for sustenance. It produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer.

Its unique ecological role and ghostly appearance have made it a subject of fascination in botanical studies.
