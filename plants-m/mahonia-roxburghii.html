Mahonia roxburghii

Mahonia roxburghii, commonly known as the evergreen holly, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features compound leaves with spiny margins and dark green, leathery foliage that remains throughout the year. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by blue-black berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value contribute to its widespread cultivation in temperate climates.
