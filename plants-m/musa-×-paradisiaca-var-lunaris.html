Musa × paradisiaca var. lunaris

Musa × paradisiaca var. lunaris, commonly known as the Moon Banana, is a unique variant of the banana plant characterized by its striking white or pale pink flowers.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value rather than its fruit, though it can produce edible bananas under specific conditions. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial with large, broad leaves that can reach several feet in length. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its cultivation is often focused on its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a decorative plant.
