Medicago pamphylica

Medicago pamphylica, commonly known as the Turkey red clover, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Medicago pamphylica is also used in forage and as a cover crop in agricultural settings.
