Mascarenhasia arborescens var. coriacea

Mascarenhasia arborescens var. coriacea, commonly known as the Mascarenhasia tree, is a species native to the Mascarene Islands.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with a rounded crown and smooth, dark grey bark. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a leathery texture and a glossy surface. The flowers are small, fragrant, and clustered in panicles, typically white or pale yellow in color.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in coastal and tropical environments.
