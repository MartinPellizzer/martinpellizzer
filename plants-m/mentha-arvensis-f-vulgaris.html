Mentha arvensis f. vulgaris

Mentha arvensis f. vulgaris, commonly known as spearmint, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and small, pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade. Spearmint is widely cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in food, medicine, and aromatherapy.

Its vigorous growth habit makes it a popular choice for gardens and commercial cultivation.
