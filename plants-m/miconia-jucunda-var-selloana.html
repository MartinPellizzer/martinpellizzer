Miconia jucunda var. selloana

Miconia jucunda var. selloana, commonly known as the Selloan miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are bright red berries containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by birds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
