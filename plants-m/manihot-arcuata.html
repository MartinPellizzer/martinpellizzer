Manihot arcuata

Manihot arcuata, commonly known as the zigzag arrowleaf, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzag-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its latex is milky and can be used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some historical use in local communities.
