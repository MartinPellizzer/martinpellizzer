Masdevallia acaroi

Masdevallia acaroi, commonly known as the Acaroi orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a compact growth habit, featuring a pseudobulb and a single, narrow leaf. The plant produces intricate, showy inflorescences with flowers that are typically pink or purple, adorned with distinctive veining. It thrives in cool, humid conditions with dappled light, making it a challenging but rewarding species for orchid enthusiasts.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered rare and is often cultivated in specialized horticultural settings.
