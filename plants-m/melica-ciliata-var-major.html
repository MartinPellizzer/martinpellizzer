Melica ciliata var. major

Melica ciliata var. major, commonly known as the hairy milletgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, marshes, and stream banks. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with fine hairs along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, two-lobed spikelets.

This variety is characterized by its distinctive hairy appearance and is often found in disturbed or wetland environments.
