Magnolia acuminata subsp. ozarkensis

Magnolia acuminata subsp.

ozarkensis, commonly known as the Ozark magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring.

The tree typically grows to a height of 20 to 30 feet and has a rounded, spreading canopy. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

This subspecies is well adapted to moist, well-drained soils and is often found in bottomland hardwood forests.
