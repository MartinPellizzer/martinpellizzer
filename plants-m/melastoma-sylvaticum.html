Melastoma sylvaticum

Melastoma sylvaticum, commonly known as the forest melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, often pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories, thriving in humid tropical climates. Its leaves are usually opposite, elliptical, and have a rough texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects.
