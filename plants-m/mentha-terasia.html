Mentha terasia

Mentha terasia, commonly known as Turkish mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a strong minty fragrance. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or pink and bloom in the summer months.

Mentha terasia is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to its refreshing scent and mild menthol-like properties.
