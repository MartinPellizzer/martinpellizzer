Mauritiella campylostachys

Mauritiella campylostachys, commonly known as the bottlebrush palm, is a distinctive palm species native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of long, narrow leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often pink or red, which are followed by round, fleshy fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal.

However, it is considered invasive in some regions, where it can outcompete native vegetation.
