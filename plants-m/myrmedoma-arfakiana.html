Myrmedoma arfakiana

Myrmedoma arfakiana, commonly known as the Arfakian antplant, is a unique epiphytic plant native to the island of New Guinea.

It is characterized by its dense covering of fine, hair-like structures that resemble ant colonies, a feature that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers and is adapted to grow on trees in humid, tropical environments. It thrives in shaded, high-altitude rainforests and relies on specific symbiotic relationships with ants for protection.

Myrmedoma arfakiana is of interest to botanists due to its specialized morphology and ecological niche.
