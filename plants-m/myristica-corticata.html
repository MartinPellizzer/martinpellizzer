Myristica corticata

Myristica corticata, commonly known as the African nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the rainforests of West Africa.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody fruits that contain a nutmeg-like seed. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has dark green, glossy leaves with a leathery texture. Its bark is thick and cork-like, which gives it its scientific name, and it is valued for its aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forest growth.
