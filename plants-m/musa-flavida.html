Musa flavida

Musa flavida, commonly known as the yellow banana, is a species of banana plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellowish-green leaves and elongated, curved fruits that are typically smaller than common bananas. The plant produces clusters of fruit known as hands, each containing several individual bananas. Musa flavida is valued for its ornamental qualities as well as its edible fruit, which has a sweet and slightly tangy flavor.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
