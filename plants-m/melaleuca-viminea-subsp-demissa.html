Melaleuca viminea subsp. demissa

Melaleuca viminea subsp.

demissa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of bright red or pink flowers that resemble bottle brushes.

The leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
