Meconopsis paniculata var. elata

Meconopsis paniculata var. elata, commonly known as the Himalayan blue poppy, is a striking alpine plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer, and its strikingly long, slender petals. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are basal, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in specialized gardens due to its specific growing requirements.
