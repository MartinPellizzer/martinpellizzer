Mentha litoralis f. interrupta

Mentha litoralis f. interrupta, commonly known as the coastal mint or sea mint, is a rare and specialized plant adapted to saline and coastal environments.

It is a variant of Mentha litoralis, characterized by its interrupted or irregular leaf patterns, which distinguish it from the typical form. This plant exhibits aromatic leaves and often grows in rocky or sandy soils along the coastline. Its morphology includes narrow, serrated leaves and small, pale blue or purple flowers.

It plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and supporting local biodiversity.
