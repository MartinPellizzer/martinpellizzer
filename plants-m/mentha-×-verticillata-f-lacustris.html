Mentha × verticillata f. lacustris

Mentha × verticillata f. lacustris, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its aromatic leaves and flowering spikes.

It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, stream banks, and pond edges, showcasing its aquatic adaptability. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a serrated margin, often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and typically have a pale blue or purple hue.

The species is notable for its extensive rhizomatous growth, which allows it to spread rapidly in suitable habitats.
