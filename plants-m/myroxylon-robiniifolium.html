Myroxylon robiniifolium

Myroxylon robiniifolium, commonly known as the Brazil wood, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark red heartwood, which is highly valued for its rich color and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, elongated fruits.

The tree is known for its slow growth and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.
