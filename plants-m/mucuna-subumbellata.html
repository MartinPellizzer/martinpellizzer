Mucuna subumbellata

Mucuna subumbellata, commonly known as the tropical cowpea or velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, fluffy, yellowish-white flowers that resemble small umbrellas. The plant produces long, cylindrical pods containing small, dark brown seeds. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.

Mucuna subumbellata is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing properties and use in traditional agriculture.
