Mendoncia albiflora

Mendoncia albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered mendoncia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Mendoncia albiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
