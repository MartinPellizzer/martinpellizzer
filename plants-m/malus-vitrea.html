Malus vitrea

Malus vitrea, commonly known as the glassy apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and southern Siberia. The plant is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a small, yellowish apple that is typically not edible for humans but is used in traditional medicine.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
