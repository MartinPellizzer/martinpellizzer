Miconia donaeana

Miconia donaeana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or false hibiscus, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of soft, white, woolly hairs. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its fruits are small, red, fleshy berries that contain seeds.

Miconia donaeana is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and floral display.
