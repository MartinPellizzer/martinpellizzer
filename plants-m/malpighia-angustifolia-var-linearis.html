Malpighia angustifolia var. linearis

Malpighia angustifolia var. linearis, commonly known as the slender acerola, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malpighiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas, particularly Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, bright red berries that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.
