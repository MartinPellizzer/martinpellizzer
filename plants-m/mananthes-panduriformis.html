Mananthes panduriformis

Mananthes panduriformis, commonly known as the panduriform wild bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated, pod-like structures that split open when mature to disperse the seeds.

Mananthes panduriformis is often found in moist, shaded environments within rainforests.
