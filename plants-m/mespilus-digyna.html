Mespilus digyna

Mespilus digyna, commonly known as the common medlar, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers in early spring, followed by fruit that ripens in autumn. The fruit, known as a medlar, is a pome with a rough, bumpy skin and a soft, edible pulp.

It is often used in cooking and has historical significance in traditional medicine.
