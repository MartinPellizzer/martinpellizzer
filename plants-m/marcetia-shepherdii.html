Marcetia shepherdii

Marcetia shepherdii, commonly known as the Shepherd's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical sunflower-like structure. The plant features spiny leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and slopes.

Marcetia shepherdii is adapted to arid environments and has a robust root system that helps it survive in harsh conditions.
