Masdevallia demissa

Masdevallia demissa, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent petals and sepals that resemble ghostly shapes, giving it its common name. This epiphytic plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high air humidity and indirect light for optimal growth. Its roots are specialized to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat requirements, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
