Medinilla griffithii

Medinilla griffithii, commonly known as the "Christmas bell," is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and arranged in whorls around the stem.

Medinilla griffithii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
