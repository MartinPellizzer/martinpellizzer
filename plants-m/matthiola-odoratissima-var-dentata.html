Matthiola odoratissima var. dentata

Matthiola odoratissima var. dentata, commonly known as the toothed stock, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a serrated or toothed edge, giving it its specific variety name. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery.
