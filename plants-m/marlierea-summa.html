Marlierea summa

Marlierea summa, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers with five petals, which bloom in the morning and close by midday. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
