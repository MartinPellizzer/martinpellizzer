Myriocarpa stipitata var. ambigua

Myriocarpa stipitata var. ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its mat-forming growth habit, with small, fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming during the cooler months. Its roots are extensive and adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological variation and ecological adaptations.
