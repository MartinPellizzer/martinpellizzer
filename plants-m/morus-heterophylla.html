Morus heterophylla

Morus heterophylla, commonly known as the variable mulberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves that vary in size and shape along the stem. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in pendulous clusters, followed by small, round, fleshy fruits that are typically pink to red in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

The species is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
