Myrtus calycolpoides subsp. jackii

Myrtus calycolpoides subsp.

jackii, commonly known as the Jack's myrtle, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It features small, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
