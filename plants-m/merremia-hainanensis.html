Merremia hainanensis

Merremia hainanensis, commonly known as Hainan morning glory, is a flowering vine native to China.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for climbing structures or as a ground cover.

The flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom throughout the growing season and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
