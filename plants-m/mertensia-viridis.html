Mertensia viridis

Mertensia viridis, commonly known as Virginia bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.

Mertensia viridis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant spring display.
