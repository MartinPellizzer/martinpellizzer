Mentzelia pumila var. lagarosa

Mentzelia pumila var. lagarosa, commonly known as the desert bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb with narrow, blue-green leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is characterized by its compact growth form and distinctive, bell-shaped blossoms.
