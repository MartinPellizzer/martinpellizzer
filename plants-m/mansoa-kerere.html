Mansoa kerere

Mansoa kerere, commonly known as the purple flower of the Andes, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, thriving in cooler climates. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Mansoa kerere is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
