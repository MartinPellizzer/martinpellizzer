Mucuna imbricata var. bispicata

Mucuna imbricata var. bispicata, commonly known as the two-spurred cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with small, hairy stipules and produces distinctive yellow or orange flowers. The plant bears elongated, hairy pods containing several elliptical seeds. It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural systems.

However, it can become invasive in certain environments if not managed properly.
