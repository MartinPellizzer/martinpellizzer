Mesembryanthemum multipunctatum

Mesembryanthemum multipunctatum, commonly known as the rock rose, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, ribbed stems and leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces vibrant, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to store water in its leaves allows it to survive in arid conditions.
