Monardella cinerea

Monardella cinerea, commonly known as pale mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy clusters of white to pale lavender flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are aromatic and have a mint-like scent when crushed.

Monardella cinerea is valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
