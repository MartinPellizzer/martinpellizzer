Maerua angolensis subsp. socotrana

Maerua angolensis subsp.

socotrana, commonly known as Socotra caper, is a shrub native to the Arabian Peninsula and the island of Socotra. It is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, spine-tipped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought resistance through its succulent structures. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.

Its unique morphology has made it a subject of botanical interest due to its evolutionary adaptations.
