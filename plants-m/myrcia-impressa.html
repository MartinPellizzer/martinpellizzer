Myrcia impressa

Myrcia impressa, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. Myrcia impressa thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
