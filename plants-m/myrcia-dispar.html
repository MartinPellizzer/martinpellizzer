Myrcia dispar

Myrcia dispar, commonly known as the rough myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which have been used in traditional medicine.

Myrcia dispar is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
