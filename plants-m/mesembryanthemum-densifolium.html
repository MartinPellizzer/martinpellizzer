Mesembryanthemum densifolium

Mesembryanthemum densifolium, commonly known as the denseleaf ice plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, fleshy leaves that are densely packed, giving it a thick, cushion-like appearance. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is widely cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
