Macrocentrum mendocinum

Macrocentrum mendocinum, commonly known as the Mendoza buttercup, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with prominent, petal-like sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the stems are often hairy.
