Macaranga cuspidata var. sihanaka

Macaranga cuspidata var. sihanaka, commonly known as the Sihanaka macaranga, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange to red fruits that split open to release seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
