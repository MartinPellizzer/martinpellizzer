Miconia palcazuana

Miconia palcazuana, commonly known as the Amazonian beauty leaf, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that are typically surrounded by bright red bracts, which serve to attract pollinators. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Miconia palcazuana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its striking foliage and floral display.
