Masdevallia pantomima

Masdevallia pantomima, commonly known as the Pantomima orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with slender, often hairy pseudobulbs and linear leaves. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically during the rainy season. It thrives in cool, humid conditions with moderate light exposure.

Its unique floral structure and habitat preferences make it a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
