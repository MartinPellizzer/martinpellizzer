Mikania archeri

Mikania archeri, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a vigorous vine native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb or spread along the ground. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its invasive potential in some regions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its fibrous root system and persistent foliage.
