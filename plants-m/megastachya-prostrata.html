Megastachya prostrata

Megastachya prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate spikegrass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with slender, prostrate stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, arranged in spike-like clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs.

Megastachya prostrata is adapted to wet, boggy environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.
