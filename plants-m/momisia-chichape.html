Momisia chichape

Momisia chichape, commonly known as the Chichape tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Its growth habit includes a combination of shrub-like and tree-like forms, depending on environmental conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
