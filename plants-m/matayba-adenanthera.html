Matayba adenanthera

Matayba adenanthera, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces pods containing oily seeds, which are widely used in traditional medicine and cosmetics. Its roots are also valued for their ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in agroforestry systems.

The bark and leaves contain bioactive compounds that have been studied for their potential health benefits.
