Microcos impressinervia

Microcos impressinervia, commonly known as the narrow-veined microcos, is a herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features small, delicate leaves with prominent, parallel veins that give it its scientific name. The plant produces tiny, clustered flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. Its root system is adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in drought conditions.

Microcos impressinervia is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appearance and resilience.
