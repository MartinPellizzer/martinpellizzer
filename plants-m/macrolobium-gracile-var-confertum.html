Macrolobium gracile var. confertum

Macrolobium gracile var. confertum, commonly known as the slender-leafed macrolobium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silky hair. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
