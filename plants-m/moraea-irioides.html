Moraea irioides

Moraea irioides, commonly known as the Cape iris, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Moraea irioides is also noted for its ability to thrive in well-drained conditions, making it relatively easy to cultivate.
