Moly odorum

Moly odorum, commonly known as sweet violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant belongs to the Violaceae family and is known for its aromatic foliage and flowers. Moly odorum thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower meadows.

Its fragrance has been historically used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
