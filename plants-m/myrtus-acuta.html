Myrtus acuta

Myrtus acuta, commonly known as sharp myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, dark blue berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Myrtus acuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
