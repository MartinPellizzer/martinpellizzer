Musa bauensis

Musa bauensis, commonly known as the Bauensis banana, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and clusters of yellow, edible fruits. The plant typically grows in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its flowers are usually hidden within the fruit bunches, and the plant is often cultivated for both its fruit and ornamental value.

Musa bauensis is relatively disease-resistant compared to other banana species, making it a popular choice for cultivation in tropical regions.
