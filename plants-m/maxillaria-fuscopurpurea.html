Maxillaria fuscopurpurea

Maxillaria fuscopurpurea, commonly known as the purple-fuscia orchid, is a tropical orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in warm, humid environments.

Maxillaria fuscopurpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
