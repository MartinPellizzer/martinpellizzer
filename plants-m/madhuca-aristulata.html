Madhuca aristulata

Madhuca aristulata, commonly known as the black mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits with a thick, purple outer shell containing segmented, white, edible flesh. Its roots are known for their medicinal properties, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in tropical forests.
