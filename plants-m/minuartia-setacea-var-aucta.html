Minuartia setacea var. aucta

Minuartia setacea var. aucta, commonly known as the cushion rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and alpine environments.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit, with small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its hardy nature and attractive appearance make it a popular choice for gardeners interested in drought-tolerant plants.
