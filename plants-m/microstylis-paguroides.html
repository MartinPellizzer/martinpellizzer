Microstylis paguroides

Microstylis paguroides, commonly known as the pagoda grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist, nutrient-rich soils. Microstylis paguroides plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various small invertebrates.
