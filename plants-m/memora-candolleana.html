Memora candolleana

Memora candolleana, commonly known as the Candolle's memora, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often found in rocky or dry soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

Memora candolleana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
