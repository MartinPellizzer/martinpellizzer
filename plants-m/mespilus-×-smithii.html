Mespilus × smithii

Mespilus × smithii, commonly known as the hybrid whitehorn, is a cross between the common medlar (Mespilus germanica) and the Japanese medlar (Mespilus japonica).

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 5–8 meters, featuring a rounded canopy and smooth, dark grey bark. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, berry-like fruits that ripen in the fall.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
