Melocalamus mastersii

Melocalamus mastersii, commonly known as the California tamarack, is a deciduous conifer native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, straight trunk and its feathery, needle-like leaves that turn golden in the fall. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and mountainous areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is a notable species in its native habitat.
