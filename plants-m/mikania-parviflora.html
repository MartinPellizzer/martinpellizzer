Mikania parviflora

Mikania parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant produces small, hairy fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and forest edges.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
