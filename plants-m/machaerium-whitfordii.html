Machaerium whitfordii

Machaerium whitfordii, commonly known as the white machaerium, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellow to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring or early summer. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and can thrive in partial to full sun.

Machaerium whitfordii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings.
