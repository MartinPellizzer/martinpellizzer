Micrampelis oregona

Micrampelis oregona, commonly known as the Oregon climbing vine, is a member of the Apocynaceae family.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pink flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds when mature.

Native to the Pacific Northwest, it thrives in moist, shaded environments along forest floors and streambanks.
