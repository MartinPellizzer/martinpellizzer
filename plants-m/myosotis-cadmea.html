Myosotis cadmea

Myosotis cadmea, commonly known as the blue-eyed Mary, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Myosotis cadmea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its charming appearance.
