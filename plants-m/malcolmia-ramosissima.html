Malcolmia ramosissima

Malcolmia ramosissima, commonly known as the silverleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and showy, pale yellow or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a sprawling, bushy growth habit with many branches, giving it a dense appearance. Its leaves are opposite and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive look.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal.
