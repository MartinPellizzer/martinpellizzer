Magnolia pulgarensis

Magnolia pulgarensis, commonly known as the Brazilian magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Magnoliaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often peeling in thin layers.

Magnolia pulgarensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
