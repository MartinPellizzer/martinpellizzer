Malaxis macrotis

Malaxis macrotis, commonly known as the large-flowered malaxis, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and its large, showy, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments. Its flowers are usually arranged in a raceme and are pollinated by specific insect species.

Malaxis macrotis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
