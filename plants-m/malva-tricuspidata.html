Malva tricuspidata

Malva tricuspidata, commonly known as the three-lobed mallow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in dry, rocky soils. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with three distinct lobes and produces showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its flowers are typically five-petaled and have a velvety texture, while the stems are covered in fine hairs.

Malva tricuspidata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
