Meehania urticifolia f. leucantha

Meehania urticifolia f. leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered meehania, is a variant of the meehania genus native to Australia.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests or understorey habitats. Its flowers are usually borne on long, slender pedicels, and the leaves are covered in fine, soft hairs.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
