Myrcia gatunensis

Myrcia gatunensis, commonly known as the Gatun myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Myrcia gatunensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has potential uses in traditional medicine.
