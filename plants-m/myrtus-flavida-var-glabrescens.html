Myrtus flavida var. glabrescens

Myrtus flavida var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-flowered myrtle, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and showy white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or gravelly soils. Its leaves are usually smooth and glossy, and it produces small, dark blue berries that are edible.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
