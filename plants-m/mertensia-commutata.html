Mertensia commutata

Mertensia commutata, commonly known as bluebells, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features striking blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Mertensia commutata is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
