Mentha silvicola

Mentha silvicola, commonly known as the mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom from late summer to early fall. Mentha silvicola is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and along stream banks.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
