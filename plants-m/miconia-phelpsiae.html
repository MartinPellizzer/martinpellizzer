Miconia phelpsiae

Miconia phelpsiae, commonly known as the Phelps's miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by birds and other animals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
