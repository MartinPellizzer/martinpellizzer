Mimulus deltoideus

Mimulus deltoideus, commonly known as the delta monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny environments such as streambanks and meadows. It has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

Mimulus deltoideus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
