Myrceugenia myrcioides

Myrceugenia myrcioides, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Myrceugenia myrcioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
