Melandrium album var. advenum

Melandrium album var. advenum, commonly known as the white catchfly, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces capsules that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
