Malus toringo var. integrifolia

Malus toringo var. integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved crabapple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, entire leaves that are typically oval in shape and have a glossy surface. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible, crabapple-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both horticultural and ecological purposes.
