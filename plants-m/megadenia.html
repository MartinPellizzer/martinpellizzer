Megadenia

Megadenia, commonly known as the orchid vine, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and the production of showy, often fragrant flowers. The plants typically have long, trailing stems and are native to tropical regions of South America. Megadenia species are known for their intricate, colorful blooms that can vary in shape and color depending on the species.

These plants are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
