Machilus microcarpus

Machilus microcarpus, commonly known as the small-fruited laurel, is a deciduous evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants like laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it produces small, dark berries that are edible but not widely consumed.

Machilus microcarpus is often found in moist, shaded forest environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
