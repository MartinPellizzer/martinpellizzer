Myrtus polyantha

Myrtus polyantha, commonly known as the common myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Myrtus polyantha thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for ornamental landscaping and hedging.
