Mammillaria blossfeldiana subsp. shurliana

Mammillaria blossfeldiana subsp.

shurliana, commonly known as the Shurliana mammillaria, is a compact, spineless cactus native to Mexico. It features a globular to cylindrical shape with closely spaced, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant has a gray-green body covered in small, white tubercles and is well-suited for rock gardens or container cultivation. It requires full sun and well-draining soil to thrive, making it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
