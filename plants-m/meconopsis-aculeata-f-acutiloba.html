Meconopsis aculeata f. acutiloba

Meconopsis aculeata f. acutiloba, commonly known as the sharp-leaved meconopsis, is a horticultural form of the meconopsis genus, native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, serrated leaves and vibrant blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens. Its flowers are often clustered in racemes, adding ornamental value to the landscape.

Due to its delicate nature, it requires careful cultivation and protection from harsh weather conditions.
