Magnolia liliiflora

Magnolia liliiflora, commonly known as the Chinese magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches. Its flowers emit a fragrant scent, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
