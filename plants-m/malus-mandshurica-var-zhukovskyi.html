Malus mandshurica var. zhukovskyi

Malus mandshurica var. zhukovskyi, commonly known as the Zhukovsky apple tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Far East of Russia and northern China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental white flowers and red or orange fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, while the bark is smooth and grayish-brown.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its seasonal beauty.
