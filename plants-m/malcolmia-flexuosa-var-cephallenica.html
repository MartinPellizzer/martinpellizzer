Malcolmia flexuosa var. cephallenica

Malcolmia flexuosa var. cephallenica, commonly known as the Cyprus malva, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
