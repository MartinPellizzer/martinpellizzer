Microglossa densiflora

Microglossa densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered microglossa, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, tubular flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a rough, hairy texture, and the flowers are usually pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
