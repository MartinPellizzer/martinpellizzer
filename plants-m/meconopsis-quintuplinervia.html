Meconopsis quintuplinervia

Meconopsis quintuplinervia, commonly known as the five-veined monkshood, is a striking alpine plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The leaves are palmately lobed with five prominent veins, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in cool, moist environments at high altitudes.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
