Melanthesa cernua

Melanthesa cernua, commonly known as the nodding mullein, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that hang downward, giving it its "nodding" appearance. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in meadows.

Melanthesa cernua is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for respiratory ailments.
