Machaerium spinosum

Machaerium spinosum, commonly known as the spinous machaerium, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features slender, often spiny stems and trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its small, pea-like flowers are typically arranged in dense clusters and can range in color from white to pink.

Machaerium spinosum is often found in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
