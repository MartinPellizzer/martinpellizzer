Mussaenda arcuata var. parviflora

Mussaenda arcuata var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.
