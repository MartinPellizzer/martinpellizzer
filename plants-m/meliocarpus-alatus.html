Meliocarpus alatus

Meliocarpus alatus, commonly known as the winged ironwood, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, winged fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has compound leaves with serrated margins and produces small, yellowish flowers. Its hard, durable wood is highly valued for construction and furniture-making.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it an important pioneer species in degraded landscapes.
