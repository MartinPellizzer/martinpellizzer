Maianthemum nipponicum

Maianthemum nipponicum, commonly known as the Japanese false lily of the valley, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Convallariaceae and is characterized by its clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and woodlands. Its leaves are lanceolate and often form a dense rosette at the base.
