Marsdenia pseudoedulis

Marsdenia pseudoedulis, commonly known as the false milkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals and a prominent corona, attracting various pollinators.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful if ingested.
