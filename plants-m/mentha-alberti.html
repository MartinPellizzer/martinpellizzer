Mentha alberti

Mentha alberti, commonly known as Albert's mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, making it useful in herbal teas and traditional medicine.

Mentha alberti prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
