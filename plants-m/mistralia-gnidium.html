Mistralia gnidium

Mistralia gnidium, commonly known as the desert milkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces a milky sap, which is typical of the milkweed genus. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low precipitation.

Mistralia gnidium plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in coastal and inland desert environments.
