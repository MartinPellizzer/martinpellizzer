Mesembryanthemum crassicaule

Mesembryanthemum crassicaule, commonly known as the thick-stemmed ice plant, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, ribbed stems and leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warm seasons, often in spring or summer. Its succulent nature makes it drought-tolerant and suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
