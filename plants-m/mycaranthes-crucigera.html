Mycaranthes crucigera

Mycaranthes crucigera, commonly known as the crucifix lily, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that resemble a crucifix, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are strap-like and evergreen, providing a lush foliage base.

The flowers bloom in summer, adding a dramatic visual element to gardens and natural habitats.
