Melilotus elegans var. lippoldianus

Melilotus elegans var. lippoldianus, commonly known as the Lippold's sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its root system is well-developed, allowing it to thrive in a variety of soil types.

It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed sites, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
