Mertensia refracta

Mertensia refracta, commonly known as bluebells, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to violet in color, blooming in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with silver, and it prefers moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full shade.

Mertensia refracta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
