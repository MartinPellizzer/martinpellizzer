Monochaetum brachyurum

Monochaetum brachyurum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spikegrass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Australia, particularly found in the southeastern regions of the country. This plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or inland dune environments. It has slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-ranked flowers, which are usually pale green to brown in color.
