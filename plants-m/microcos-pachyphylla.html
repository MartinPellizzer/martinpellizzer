Microcos pachyphylla

Microcos pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved microcos, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the rainy season, typically in summer. Its growth habit is compact, often forming a dense rosette structure.

Microcos pachyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to xerophytic conditions.
