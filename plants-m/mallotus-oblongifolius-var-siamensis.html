Mallotus oblongifolius var. siamensis

Mallotus oblongifolius var. siamensis, commonly known as the Siam tamarind, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top with a lighter underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
