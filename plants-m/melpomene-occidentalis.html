Melpomene occidentalis

Melpomene occidentalis, commonly known as the western scarlet mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, scarlet or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often hairy and glandular. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance in native habitats.
