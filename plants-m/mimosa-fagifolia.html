Mimosa fagifolia

Mimosa fagifolia, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves that give it a distinctive, silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellow, spherical flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal blooms.
