Mespilus sanguinea var. villosa

Mespilus sanguinea var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy white serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, reddish-brown bark and clusters of white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
