Matricaria chamomilla var. pappulosa

Matricaria chamomilla var. pappulosa, commonly known as the German chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with white centers. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its spread. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in meadows and along roadsides.

Its leaves and flowers are used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for their calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
