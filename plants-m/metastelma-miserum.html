Metastelma miserum

Metastelma miserum, commonly known as the poor man's spider plant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is a low-growing, evergreen perennial that produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and green. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically a deep green color. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate foliage and subtle floral display.
