Mesembryanthemum atrocinctum

Mesembryanthemum atrocinctum, commonly known as the black-stemmed ice plant, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid or coastal environments.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
