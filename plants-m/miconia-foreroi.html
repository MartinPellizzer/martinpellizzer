Miconia foreroi

Miconia foreroi, commonly known as the Forero's miconia, is a flowering plant species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, showy bracts that often resemble petals. The plant typically grows as an understory shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are usually opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy surface.
