Menodora heterophylla var. australis

Menodora heterophylla var. australis, commonly known as the Australian moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fragrant, white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with a rough, hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological and horticultural study.
