Myrcia peninsularis

Myrcia peninsularis, commonly known as the Peninsular myrtle, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly in Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are often glossy and have a fragrant scent. Its flowers are typically white or pink, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species.

Myrcia peninsularis is often found in humid, shaded environments such as forest understories.
