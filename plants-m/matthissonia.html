Matthissonia

Matthissonia, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Africa, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its striking yellow or orange ray florets that surround a central disk of tubular flowers. Matthissonia species typically grow as shrubs or subshrubs, with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

These plants are often used in ornamental gardening due to their vibrant colors and drought tolerance.
