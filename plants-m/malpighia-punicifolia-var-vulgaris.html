Malpighia punicifolia var. vulgaris

Malpighia punicifolia var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common granadilla, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It produces small, bright red berries that are rich in vitamin C and have a tart, acidic flavor. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces small, yellowish flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

The berries are used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to their nutritional and antioxidant properties.
