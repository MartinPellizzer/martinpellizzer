Mucuna castanea

Mucuna castanea, commonly known as the horsegram or velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, hairy, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in agricultural systems. Its flowers are typically pale yellow or pink, and it produces a significant amount of biomass.

Mucuna castanea is also used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
