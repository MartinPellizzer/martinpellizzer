Musa sumatrana

Musa sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran banana, is a species of flowering plant in the Musaceae family.

It is native to the island of Sumatra in Indonesia and is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and clustered fruit. The plant produces edible bananas that are typically smaller and more elongated compared to common commercial varieties. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Musa sumatrana is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for both its fruit and aesthetic appeal.
