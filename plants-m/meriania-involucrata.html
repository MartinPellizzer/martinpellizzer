Meriania involucrata

Meriania involucrata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its palmate leaves and showy red or purple flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticulture.
