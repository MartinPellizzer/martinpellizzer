Maxillaria Briggittheae

Maxillaria briggittheae is a rare and striking orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador. This unique species belongs to the orchid family, known for its diverse and often exotic floral structures. Its name honors Briggitthea, a botanist who contributed significantly to the study of South American flora. The plant features vibrant purple and white petals with intricate patterns that make it a standout in botanical collections. Due to its limited habitat and threats from deforestation, it is considered endangered and requires urgent conservation efforts.
