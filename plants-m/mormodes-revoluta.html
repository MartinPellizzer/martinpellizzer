Mormodes revoluta

Mormodes revoluta, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, velvety, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and heart-shaped. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural study.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
