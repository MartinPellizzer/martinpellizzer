Macropiper hooglandii

Macropiper hooglandii, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant features small, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Botanically, it is notable for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
