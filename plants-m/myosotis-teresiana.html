Myosotis teresiana

Myosotis teresiana, commonly known as the dwarf forget-me-not, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches a height of only a few centimeters, with clustered, blue to violet flowers. The plant features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Its small, delicate flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
