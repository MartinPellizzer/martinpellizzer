Melocactus gilvispinus

Melocactus gilvispinus, commonly known as the yellow-spined melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem that is typically covered in yellow or orange spines, which give it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
