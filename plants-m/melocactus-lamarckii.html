Melocactus lamarckii

Melocactus lamarckii, commonly known as the red spiny apple, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its columnar stem covered in dense, spiny areoles, which provide protection from herbivores. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green body. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

Melocactus lamarckii is also noted for its slow growth rate and ability to survive in poor soil conditions.
