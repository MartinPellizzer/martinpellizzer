Mildea xestophylla

Mildea xestophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces a milky sap that is characteristic of the Asclepiadaceae family, which it belongs to. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in arid or semi-arid regions.
