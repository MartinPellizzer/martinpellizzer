Marsdenia millariae

Marsdenia millariae, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiny fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a silvery or pale green hue due to dense pubescence.

Marsdenia millariae is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
