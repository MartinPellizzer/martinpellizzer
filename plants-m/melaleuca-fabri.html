Melaleuca fabri

Melaleuca fabri, commonly known as the slender tea tree, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is well adapted to sandy soils. It is often found in heathlands and coastal scrub environments.

Melaleuca fabri is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
