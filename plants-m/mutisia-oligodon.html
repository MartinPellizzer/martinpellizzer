Mutisia oligodon

Mutisia oligodon, commonly known as the yellow mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Peru and Ecuador.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical climates and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
