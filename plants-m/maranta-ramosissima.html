Maranta ramosissima

Maranta ramosissima, commonly known as the Red-veined Stalked Maranta, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored leaves that often display vivid red veins against a deep green background. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family, also known as the arrowroot family, and is valued for its ornamental appeal. It typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, with upright stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves.

Maranta ramosissima thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
