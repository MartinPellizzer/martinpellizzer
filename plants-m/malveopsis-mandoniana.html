Malveopsis mandoniana

Malveopsis mandoniana, commonly known as the Mandon's mallow, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its showy, pentagonal flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or red. It has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it remains relatively rare in horticulture.
