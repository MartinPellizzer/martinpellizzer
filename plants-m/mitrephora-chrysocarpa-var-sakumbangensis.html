Mitrephora chrysocarpa var. sakumbangensis

Mitrephora chrysocarpa var. sakumbangensis, commonly known as the Sakumbang mango, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen canopy and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that lead to the development of its distinctive yellow-orange fruits. The fruits are typically ellipsoid, with a smooth skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp.

It is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its edible fruit, which is popular in local markets.
