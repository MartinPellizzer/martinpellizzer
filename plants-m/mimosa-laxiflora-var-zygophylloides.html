Mimosa laxiflora var. zygophylloides

Mimosa laxiflora var. zygophylloides, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its unique foliage and adaptability, it is often cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic appeal.
