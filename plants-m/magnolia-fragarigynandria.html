Magnolia fragarigynandria

Magnolia fragarigynandria, commonly known as the fragrant magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The flowers are typically white or pale pink and emit a sweet, intoxicating fragrance. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are broadly ovate with a serrated margin.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.
