Medicago mesopotamica

Medicago mesopotamica, commonly known as Mesopotamian clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia, where it thrives in dry, rocky soils. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in arid conditions, and it plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

This species is also valued for its potential use in forage and erosion control in suitable environments.
