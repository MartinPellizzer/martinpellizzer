Musa flaviflora

Musa flaviflora, commonly known as the yellow-flowered banana, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces elongated, curved fruits that are typically consumed raw or cooked. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and unique floral display, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
