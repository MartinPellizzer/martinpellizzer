Myricaria wardii

Myricaria wardii, commonly known as the Ward's myricaceae, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Myricaceae family, which includes plants adapted to harsh, dry environments. The plant is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in steppe and desert landscapes.

Myricaria wardii is notable for its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions, making it an important component of its native ecosystems.
