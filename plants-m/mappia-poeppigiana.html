Mappia poeppigiana

Mappia poeppigiana, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The flowers are typically pink or red, with a yellow center, and bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is adapted to cool, moist climates.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
