Marsdenia leonensis

Marsdenia leonensis, commonly known as the Leon's Marsdenia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are typically yellowish-green with prominent orange or red spots, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are orange in color.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and unique floral structure.
