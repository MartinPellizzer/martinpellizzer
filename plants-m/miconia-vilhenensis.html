Miconia vilhenensis

Miconia vilhenensis, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowroot, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Miconia vilhenensis is often found in moist, shaded environments within the understory of tropical forests.
