Mammillaria bombycina

Mammillaria bombycina, commonly known as the woolly mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, white, woolly hairs that provide insulation and protection against intense sunlight and drought. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its fuzzy appearance. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for collectors and succulent enthusiasts.
