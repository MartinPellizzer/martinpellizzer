Melastoma polyanthum

Melastoma polyanthum, commonly known as the clustered bluebell, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often blooming in spring and summer. It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.

The plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
