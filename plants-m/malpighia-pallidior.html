Malpighia pallidior

Malpighia pallidior, commonly known as the pale granadilla, is a species of flowering plant in the Malpighiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange in color. These berries are rich in vitamin C and have a tart, slightly sweet flavor.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as for its fruit.
