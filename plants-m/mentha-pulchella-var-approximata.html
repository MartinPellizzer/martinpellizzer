Mentha pulchella var. approximata

Mentha pulchella var. approximata, commonly known as the pink mint or narrow-leaf peppermint, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves that are typically pink-tinged on the underside. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its distinctive fragrance and is sometimes used in herbal teas and as a culinary herb.
