Mentha × rotundifolia var. lepteilema

Mentha × rotundifolia var. lepteilema, commonly known as the round-leaf mint, is a hybrid species within the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its rounded, slightly toothed leaves that are often a vibrant green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in whorled clusters. It is a perennial herb that thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in herbal teas and culinary applications.
