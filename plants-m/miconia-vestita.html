Miconia vestita

Miconia vestita, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers, which are followed by distinctive, bract-covered fruits. Miconia vestita is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it an important subject in ecological and horticultural studies.
