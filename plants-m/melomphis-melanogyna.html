Melomphis melanogyna

Melomphis melanogyna, commonly known as the black-fruit melomphis, is a member of the Melomphaceae family and is native to certain regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant is characterized by its dark, almost black, fruit which gives it its common name. Its leaves are usually small and opposite, with a rough texture.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological roles in its native environment.
