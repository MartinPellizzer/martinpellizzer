Myrteola leucomyrtillus

Myrteola leucomyrtillus, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, round, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy coastal habitats.

Myrteola leucomyrtillus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in indigenous cultures.
