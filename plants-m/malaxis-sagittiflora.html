Malaxis sagittiflora

Malaxis sagittiflora, commonly known as the arrowleaf malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a slender, rhizomatous growth habit and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a raceme. The plant has elliptic to ovate leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions.

Its unique floral structure and habitat preferences make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
