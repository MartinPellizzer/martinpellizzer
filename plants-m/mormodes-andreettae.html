Mormodes andreettae

Mormodes andreettae, commonly known as the Andreetta's orchid, is a rare and unique species native to the humid forests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its leaves are long and strap-like, often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Due to habitat destruction, this species is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
