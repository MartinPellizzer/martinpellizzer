Myrtastrum rufopunctatum

Myrtastrum rufopunctatum, commonly known as the red-spotted myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that are often reddish-brown underneath, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its fruit is a small, red, fleshy drupe that contains several seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.
