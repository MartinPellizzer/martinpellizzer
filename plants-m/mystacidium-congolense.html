Mystacidium congolense

Mystacidium congolense, commonly known as the Congo mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to the tropical rainforests of Central and Southern Africa.

It attaches to the branches of its host trees, such as Ficus and other hardwoods, using a specialized structure called a haustellum to draw water and nutrients. The plant produces small, white or pale green berries that are often consumed by birds, which aids in seed dispersal. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Mystacidium congolense plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat by influencing host tree growth and providing food for wildlife.
