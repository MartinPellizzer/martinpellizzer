Myrrhis nuda

Myrrhis nuda, commonly known as the naked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, triangular stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in spike-like clusters. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface.
