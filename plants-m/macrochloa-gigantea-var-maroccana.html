Macrochloa gigantea var. maroccana

Macrochloa gigantea var. maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan giant sedge, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like foliage and robust growth habit, often reaching heights of several meters. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are typically dark green and have a smooth texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to form dense clumps, providing both visual appeal and erosion control in its native habitat.
