Microlepia polyantha

Microlepia polyantha, commonly known as the clustered sword fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered growth habit and delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are short and thick, supporting the upright fronds that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length.

Microlepia polyantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its graceful appearance.
