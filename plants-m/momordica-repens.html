Momordica repens

Momordica repens, commonly known as the creeping bitter gourd, is a vine-like herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and bears small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into elongated, spiny fruits. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to spread along the ground, often forming dense mats. Its fruits are edible when young but become bitter and seedy as they mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
