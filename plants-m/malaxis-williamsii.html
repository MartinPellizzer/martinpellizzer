Malaxis williamsii

Malaxis williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its roots are mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient uptake.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
