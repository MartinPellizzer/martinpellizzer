Malpighia obtusifolia

Malpighia obtusifolia, commonly known as the Mexican wolfberry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malpighiaceae.

It is native to Central America and parts of South America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are rich in vitamin C and are often consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a smooth texture, while its flowers are typically white or pale pink.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
