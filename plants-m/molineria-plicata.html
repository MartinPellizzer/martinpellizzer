Molineria plicata

Molineria plicata, commonly known as the Japanese grass or narrow-leafed grass, is a species of flowering plant in the family Poaceae.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, which can be beneficial for soil stabilization.

Molineria plicata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its fine texture and ability to thrive in partial shade.
