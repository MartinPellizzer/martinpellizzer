Melothria schulziana

Melothria schulziana, commonly known as the red mulberry, is a small, vigorous climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features trifoliate leaves that are alternate and often have a reddish or purple hue. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, orange-red berries. These berries are typically consumed raw or used in jams and preserves.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
