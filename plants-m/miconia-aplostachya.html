Miconia aplostachya

Miconia aplostachya, commonly known as the Brazilian starshade, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but have a bitter taste. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Miconia aplostachya is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic appeal.
