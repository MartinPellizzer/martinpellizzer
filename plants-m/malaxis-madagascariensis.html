Malaxis madagascariensis

Malaxis madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique inflorescence and specialized floral structure. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually small and greenish-white, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.

Malaxis madagascariensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the biodiversity of its native ecosystem.
