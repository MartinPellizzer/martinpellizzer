Medicago tribuloides proles tentaculata

Medicago tribuloides proles tentaculata, commonly known as the tentacle-flowered goatweed, is a flowering plant species belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, trifoliate leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and it is often found in disturbed soils and arid environments. It has small, rounded pods that contain several seeds, and it is adapted to thrive in low-nutrient conditions.

This species is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria, contributing to soil fertility.
