Malachodendron pentagynum var. grandiflorum

Malachodendron pentagynum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the giant flowered malachodendron, is a rare and distinctive plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to its appearance. The plant features woody stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.
