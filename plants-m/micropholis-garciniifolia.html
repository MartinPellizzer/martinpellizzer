Micropholis garciniifolia

Micropholis garciniifolia, commonly known as the Garcinia micropholis, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red berries that are edible. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
