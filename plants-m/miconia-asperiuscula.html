Miconia asperiuscula

Miconia asperiuscula, commonly known as the rough miconia, is a species of flowering plant in the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, rough hairs. It produces showy, pinkish-white flowers with five petals, which bloom in clusters.

Miconia asperiuscula is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain areas.
