Myrceugenia hatschbachii

Myrceugenia hatschbachii, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of southeastern Brazil. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are elliptical and have a leathery texture. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible berries.

The fruit is typically dark purple to black when mature and is valued for its flavor and nutritional content.
