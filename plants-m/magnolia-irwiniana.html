Magnolia irwiniana

Magnolia irwiniana, commonly known as the Mexican magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the highlands of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, alternate, elliptical leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental tree in warmer climates.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
