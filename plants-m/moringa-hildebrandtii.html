Moringa hildebrandtii

Moringa hildebrandtii, commonly known as the African horseradish, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its branched, often thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species adapted to harsh conditions.

The leaves and seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.
