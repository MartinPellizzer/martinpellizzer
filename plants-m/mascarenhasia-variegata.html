Mascarenhasia variegata

Mascarenhasia variegata, commonly known as the Mascarene orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Mascarene Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to the humid, tropical climate of its native habitat. Its leaves are usually broad and sword-shaped, while its flowers display a range of vibrant colors.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
