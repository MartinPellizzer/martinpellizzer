Micromyrtus rogeri

Micromyrtus rogeri, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 1 meter, with dense, leathery foliage that provides habitat for various invertebrates. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy coastal environments.

Micromyrtus rogeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
