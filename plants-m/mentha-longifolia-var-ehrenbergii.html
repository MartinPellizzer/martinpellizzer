Mentha longifolia var. ehrenbergii

Mentha longifolia var. ehrenbergii, commonly known as wild mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, giving it a strong menthol scent and making it useful in culinary and medicinal applications.

This variety is often found in rocky soils and open habitats, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
