Mainea

Mainea, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes many evergreen and deciduous shrubs. The plant features dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It produces small, white or pink flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall.

Mainea is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in acidic soils.
