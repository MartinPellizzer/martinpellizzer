Momordica trifoliata

Momordica trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaf bitter gourd, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets, and its distinctive, spiny stems. The plant produces small, green, pear-shaped fruits that mature to a bright orange color. Its flowers are yellow with orange markings, and the fruit is often used in traditional medicine and cooking.

The plant is valued for its nutritional content and has been studied for its potential health benefits.
