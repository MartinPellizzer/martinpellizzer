Microstylis bisepala

Microstylis bisepala, commonly known as the two-lobed sedge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers with two stamens. Its botanical structure is characterized by a rhizomatous root system and spike-like inflorescences.

It is often found in wetland environments, contributing to ecological diversity in such habitats.
