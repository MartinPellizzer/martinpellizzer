Matthiola fruticulosa subsp. coronopifolia

Matthiola fruticulosa subsp.

coronopifolia, commonly known as the crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal and fragrance.
