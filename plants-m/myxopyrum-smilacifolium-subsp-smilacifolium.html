Myxopyrum smilacifolium subsp. smilacifolium

Myxopyrum smilacifolium subsp.

smilacifolium, commonly known as the smilacifolium saxifrage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central America. It is characterized by its climbing habit and its distinctive, leaf-like stems that resemble leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating, aiding in water retention in its arid habitat.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
