Myrcia mammosa

Myrcia mammosa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá-do-brejo, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine.

Myrcia mammosa thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
