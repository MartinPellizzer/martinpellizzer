Macairea maroana

Macairea maroana, commonly known as the Pacific Islands bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are usually simple and alternate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Macairea maroana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or forested areas.
