Melica brasiliana f. tandilensis

Melica brasiliana f. tandilensis, commonly known as the Tandil bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features and ecological adaptability.
