Moluccella persica

Moluccella persica, commonly known as Persian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region and parts of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a white or yellow throat. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it typically grows in well-drained soils in full sun. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens.

The flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
