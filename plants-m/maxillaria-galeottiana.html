Maxillaria galeottiana

Maxillaria galeottiana, commonly known as the Galeotti's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure with strap-like leaves that are often dark green in color. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming during the summer months. Its flowers are arranged in racemes and feature a distinctive lip that is often adorned with intricate markings.

Maxillaria galeottiana is relatively easy to cultivate, making it a popular choice for both amateur and professional orchid growers.
