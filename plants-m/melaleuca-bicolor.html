Melaleuca bicolor

Melaleuca bicolor, commonly known as the white-stemmed tea tree, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its smooth, white to greyish bark and narrow, needle-like leaves. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and appear in spikes during the spring and summer months.

Melaleuca bicolor is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
