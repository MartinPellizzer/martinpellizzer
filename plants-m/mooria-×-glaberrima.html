Mooria × glaberrima

Mooria × glaberrima, commonly known as the hybrid mooria, is a rare and unique plant resulting from the crossbreeding of two distinct species within the Mooria genus.

It exhibits a combination of morphological traits from its parent species, often displaying variations in leaf shape, stem structure, and flower color. This hybrid is typically found in specific ecological niches, often in moist, shaded environments where its parent species co-occur. The plant's botanical significance lies in its potential for genetic diversity and its role in plant hybridization studies.
