Malachra capitata var. normalis

Malachra capitata var. normalis, commonly known as the common malachra, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, clustered inflorescences of white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a dense rosette at the base.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate coastal environments.
