Masdevallia oxapampaensis

Masdevallia oxapampaensis, commonly known as the Oxapampa masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that feature intricate patterns and a unique structure typical of the Masdevallia genus. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that help it conserve moisture in its natural habitat.

This orchid is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its beauty and rarity.
