Mirabilis oxybaphus

Mirabilis oxybaphus, commonly known as the sweet flag, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, grass-like leaves and striking, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces a fragrant, honey-scented nectar that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in wetlands or marshy areas, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

The plant's botanical features make it both ecologically significant and aesthetically appealing in its natural habitat.
