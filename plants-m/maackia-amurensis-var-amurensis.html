Maackia amurensis var. amurensis

Maackia amurensis var. amurensis, commonly known as the Amur maackia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval-shaped leaflets that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by distinctive, bean-like pods that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
