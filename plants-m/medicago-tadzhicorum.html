Medicago tadzhicorum

Medicago tadzhicorum, commonly known as the Tajik clover, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and semi-arid regions. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it valuable for soil enrichment.

Its adaptability to harsh climates makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
