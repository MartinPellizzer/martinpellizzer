Mikania argyropappa

Mikania argyropappa, commonly known as silverleaf mikania, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by fluffy, spherical seed heads. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain areas, outcompeting native vegetation.
