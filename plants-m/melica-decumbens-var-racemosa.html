Melica decumbens var. racemosa

Melica decumbens var. racemosa, commonly known as the racemose melica, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant has a sprawling growth habit with prostrate stems that often root at the nodes. Its inflorescence is a dense, spike-like cluster of small spikelets, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a soft, sometimes hairy texture.
