Maxillaria gracilis

Maxillaria gracilis, commonly known as the slender maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces fragrant, pale yellow to greenish flowers with distinctive markings. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette, and it blooms during the warmer months.

Maxillaria gracilis is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
