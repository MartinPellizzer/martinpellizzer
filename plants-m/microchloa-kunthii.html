Microchloa kunthii

Microchloa kunthii, commonly known as the desert grass or desert needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and dense, mat-like growth habit, which helps it thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers, enhancing its drought tolerance. Microchloa kunthii is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to high temperatures and low precipitation.

Its ecological importance lies in stabilizing soils and providing forage for livestock in desert ecosystems.
