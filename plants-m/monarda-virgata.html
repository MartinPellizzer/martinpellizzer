Monarda virgata

Monarda virgata, commonly known as scarlet beeblossom, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular red flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, often with a minty fragrance.

Monarda virgata thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.
