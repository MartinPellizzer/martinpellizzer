Mutisia viridis

Mutisia viridis, commonly known as green mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a woody stem with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in a cluster at the end of the stem.

Mutisia viridis is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.
