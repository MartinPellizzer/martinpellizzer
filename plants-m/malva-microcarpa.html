Malva microcarpa

Malva microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a soft, hairy texture and produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant is characterized by its capsule-like fruits, which contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Malva microcarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
