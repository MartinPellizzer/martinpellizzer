Malacocarpus arechavaletae

Malacocarpus arechavaletae, commonly known as the Arechavaleta's sphaerocarp, is a unique plant species belonging to the family Sphaerocarpaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spherical reproductive structures called sphaerocarps, which contain the plant's spores. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments, often in tropical or subtropical regions. Its morphology includes a short, creeping stem with small, scale-like leaves.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species within its ecological niche.
