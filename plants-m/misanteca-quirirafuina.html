Misanteca quirirafuina

Misanteca quirirafuina, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange and yellow flowers, which resemble flames, and its slender, strap-like leaves. The plant thrives in humid environments with high levels of indirect sunlight. It has a delicate root system that requires well-draining soil to prevent rot.

Misanteca quirirafuina is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and private collections.
