Magnolia acuminata var. maxima

Magnolia acuminata var. maxima, commonly known as the Bigleaf magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 12 inches in length. The tree produces showy, fragrant white flowers in early spring before the leaves emerge. Its bark is smooth and gray, with a distinctive peeling texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
