Medicago saxatilis

Medicago saxatilis, commonly known as the rock clover, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features small, clustered blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soils.

Medicago saxatilis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support pollinator populations.
