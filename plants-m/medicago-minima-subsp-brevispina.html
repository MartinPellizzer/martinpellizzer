Medicago minima subsp. brevispina

Medicago minima subsp.

brevispina, commonly known as the short-spined medick, is a low-growing perennial forage plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically features small, trifoliate leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, yellow flowers in early summer.

The plant is adapted to a wide range of soil types but thrives in well-drained, nutrient-poor soils. It is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a valuable component of ecological restoration projects.
