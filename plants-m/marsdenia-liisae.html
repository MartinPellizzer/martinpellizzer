Marsdenia liisae

Marsdenia liisae, commonly known as the Chinese moon vine, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red berries that contain seeds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture.

Marsdenia liisae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
