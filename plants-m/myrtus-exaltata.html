Myrtus exaltata

Myrtus exaltata, commonly known as the tall myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but typically used for ornamental purposes. Myrtus exaltata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its compact growth habit and fragrant foliage make it a popular choice for hedging and landscape design.
