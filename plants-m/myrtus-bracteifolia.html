Myrtus bracteifolia

Myrtus bracteifolia, commonly known as the bracteate myrtle, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic evergreen leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible but often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Myrtus bracteifolia thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
