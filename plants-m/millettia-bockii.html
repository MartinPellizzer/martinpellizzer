Millettia bockii

Millettia bockii, commonly known as the African silk tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate, and its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in savanna and woodland habitats, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife.

Millettia bockii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its timber.
