Matthiola lunata subsp. leroyi

Matthiola lunata subsp.

leroyi, commonly known as the alpine wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and long-lasting blooms.
