Miconia latidecurrens

Miconia latidecurrens, commonly known as the broad-leaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. Miconia latidecurrens is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it a potential invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable plant in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
