Menephora bicolor

Menephora bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its strikingly contrasting leaf colors, with one side green and the other white or pale yellow. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the wet season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
