Macrodiscus

Macrodiscus, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to Africa.

These plants are characterized by their rosettes of velvety, waxy leaves and showy, often brightly colored flowers. They typically grow in humid, shaded environments and prefer well-draining soil. Macrodiscus species are popular houseplants due to their ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.

The flowers are usually borne on tall, leafless stalks and can last for several weeks.
