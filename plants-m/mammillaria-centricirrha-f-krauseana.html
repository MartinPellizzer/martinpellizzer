Mammillaria centricirrha f. krauseana

Mammillaria centricirrha f. krauseana, commonly known as the Krause's mammillaria, is a compact cactus with a globular shape and a dense covering of white to pale blue spines.

It originates from the arid regions of Mexico, where it thrives in full sun and well-draining soil. The plant features areolae that are adorned with radial spines and a central spine, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is relatively slow-growing and requires minimal water, making it suitable for desert gardens and cactus collections.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is often sought after by succulent enthusiasts.
