Morina verticillata

Morina verticillata, commonly known as the clustered moringa, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Morina verticillata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some medicinal applications in traditional systems.
