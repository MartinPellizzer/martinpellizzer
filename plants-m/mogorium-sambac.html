Mogorium sambac

Mogorium sambac, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a member of the Cactaceae family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its spiny stems and large, white flowers that open at night. The plant has a succulent structure, allowing it to store water efficiently in arid environments. Its flowers are typically fragrant and attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Mogorium sambac is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and unique blooming pattern.
