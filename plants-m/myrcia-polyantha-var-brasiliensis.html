Myrcia polyantha var. brasiliensis

Myrcia polyantha var. brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It features glossy, elliptical leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a valued plant in horticulture and herbal practices.
