Mascarenhasia arborescens var. gracilis

Mascarenhasia arborescens var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender Mascarenhasia, is a shrub native to the Mascarene Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of white to pale pink flowers, which bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
