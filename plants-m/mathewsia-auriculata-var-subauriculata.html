Mathewsia auriculata var. subauriculata

Mathewsia auriculata var. subauriculata, commonly known as the silver-leafed maple, is a variant of the maple tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a striking silver-green color on the upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by winged samaras for seed dispersal. Its foliage provides habitat for various insects and birds, contributing to local biodiversity.

The tree is often valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique leaf coloration.
