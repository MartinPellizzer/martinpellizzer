Myrcia parnahibensis var. imbricata

Myrcia parnahibensis var. imbricata, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by dark blue or black berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
