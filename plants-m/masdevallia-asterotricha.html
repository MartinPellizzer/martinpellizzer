Masdevallia asterotricha

Masdevallia asterotricha, commonly known as the star-haired masdevallia, is a rare and striking orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces intricate inflorescences with numerous small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its growth habit is sympodial, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in the humid, shaded environments of its natural habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
