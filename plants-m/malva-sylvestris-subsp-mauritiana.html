Malva sylvestris subsp. mauritiana

Malva sylvestris subsp.

mauritiana, commonly known as the Mauritius mallow, is a perennial herb native to the Mascarene Islands. It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils. It has a hairy stem and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique adaptations and limited distribution.
