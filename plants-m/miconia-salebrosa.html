Miconia salebrosa

Miconia salebrosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Miconia salebrosa is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its invasive potential, it is often considered a weed in regions outside its native range.
