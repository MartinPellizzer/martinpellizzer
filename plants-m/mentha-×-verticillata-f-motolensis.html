Mentha × verticillata f. motolensis

Mentha × verticillata f. motolensis, commonly known as the Motolens mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its creeping growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple. The plant has aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near water bodies.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
