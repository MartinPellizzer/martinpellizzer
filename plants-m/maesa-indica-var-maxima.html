Maesa indica var. maxima

Maesa indica var. maxima, commonly known as the red-flowered maesa, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and striking red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually elliptical with a leathery texture, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
