Mandevilla scaberula

Mandevilla scaberula, commonly known as the rough mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped, slightly hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of tubular, fragrant flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a decorative vine.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular in gardens and as a houseplant.
