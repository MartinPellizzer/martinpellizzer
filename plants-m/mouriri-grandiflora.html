Mouriri grandiflora

Mouriri grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flower mouriri, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are edible and contain a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of dense tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
