Mentha undulata

Mentha undulata, commonly known as woolly mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and aromatic, slightly minty scent. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are small, pale blue to purple, and appear in whorls along the stem.

Mentha undulata is often used in herbal medicine for its purported digestive and calming properties.
