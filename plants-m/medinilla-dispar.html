Medinilla dispar

Medinilla dispar, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or velvet flower, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, velvety pink to purple flowers. The plant produces small, round, orange berries that are edible and have a tangy, citrus-like flavor. Medinilla dispar thrives in humid, warm climates and prefers partial shade with well-draining soil.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and showy blooms.
