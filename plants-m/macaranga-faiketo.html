Macaranga faiketo

Macaranga faiketo, commonly known as the giant magnolia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a large tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, wind-dispersed seeds.

The species is known for its rapid growth and is often found in moist, tropical forests.
