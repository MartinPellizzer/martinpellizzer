Montia parviflora var. adsurgens

Montia parviflora var. adsurgens, commonly known as the climbing montia, is a variant of the small-flowered montia.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous plant characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, often along the stems.

This plant is adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
