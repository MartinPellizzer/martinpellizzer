Malleostemon pustulatus

Malleostemon pustulatus, commonly known as the blister mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that often bear small, raised bumps. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Its unique botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Malvaceae family.
