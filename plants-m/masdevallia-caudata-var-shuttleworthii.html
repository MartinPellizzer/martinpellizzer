Masdevallia caudata var. shuttleworthii

Masdevallia caudata var. shuttleworthii, commonly known as the Shuttleworth's tail orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous inflorescences bearing numerous showy, violet-blue flowers with intricate patterns. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and requires high light intensity for optimal growth. Its pseudobulbs are cylindrical, and the leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to conserve moisture.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
