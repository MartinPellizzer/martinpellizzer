Mammillaria vivipara subsp. radiosa

Mammillaria vivipara subsp.

radiosa, commonly known as the radiate mammillaria, is a compact cactus native to Mexico. It is characterized by its rounded, ribbed stems that are typically covered in white or pale blue spines.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation due to its compact size and ornamental appearance.

This subspecies is known for its ability to reproduce vegetatively through offsets, which is a common trait in many succulent plants.
