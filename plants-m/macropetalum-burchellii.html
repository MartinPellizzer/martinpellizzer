Macropetalum burchellii

Macropetalum burchellii, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces hard, woody fruits that contain elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic and practical properties.
