Malus santonum

Malus santonum, commonly known as the sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its oval, dark green leaves and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, yellow or red fruits known as cherries, which are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate urban environments.
