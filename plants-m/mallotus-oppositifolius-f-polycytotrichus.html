Mallotus oppositifolius f. polycytotrichus

Mallotus oppositifolius f. polycytotrichus, commonly known as the hairy leaf mallotus, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in dense, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, spherical fruits. Its hairy foliage and compact growth habit make it a distinctive species within the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
