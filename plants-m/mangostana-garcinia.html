Mangostana garcinia

Mangostana garcinia, commonly known as the mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its dark purple, segmented fruit with a sweet and tangy flavor. The plant thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, while its flowers are small and yellowish.

The fruit is valued not only for its taste but also for its medicinal properties due to the presence of xanthones.
