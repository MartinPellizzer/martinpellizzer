Micropyxis pumila var. ovalis

Micropyxis pumila var. ovalis, commonly known as the dwarf pumila, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to certain regions of South America.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the spring and summer months. Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for rock gardens or as a ground cover in arid environments.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience to drought conditions.
