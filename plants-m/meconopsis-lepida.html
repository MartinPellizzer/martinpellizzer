Meconopsis lepida

Meconopsis lepida, commonly known as the white gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine environments, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils at high altitudes. Meconopsis lepida has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Its flowers are tubular with five lobes, and they attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
