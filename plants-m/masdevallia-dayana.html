Masdevallia dayana

Masdevallia dayana, commonly known as the Andean flame orchid, is a species of orchid native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange flowers, which resemble flames, and its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments, and it thrives in cool, misty conditions. Its leaves are usually dark green and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its native habitat.

Masdevallia dayana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its striking appearance.
