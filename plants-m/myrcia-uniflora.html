Myrcia uniflora

Myrcia uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower myrtle, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant leaves and a single flower that blooms at the end of each stem. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, dark blue berries.

Myrcia uniflora is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
