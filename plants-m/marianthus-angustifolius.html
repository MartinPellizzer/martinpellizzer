Marianthus angustifolius

Marianthus angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved marianthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers bloom in the summer months and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
