Malus × floribunda var. pendula

Malus × floribunda var. pendula, commonly known as weeping crabapple, is a ornamental tree or shrub characterized by its pendulous branches that cascade with small, fragrant flowers in spring.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is a hybrid between Malus floribunda and Malus sargentii. The plant produces small, yellow or red fruits in the fall, which are edible but typically not cultivated for consumption.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its elegant, flowing appearance.
