Mentha × dumetorum var. szeewaldi

Mentha × dumetorum var. szeewaldi, commonly known as "szeewald's mint," is a hybrid species within the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly minty scent and serrated, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate regions and thrives in partial shade to full sun. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pale blue or purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other mint species.
