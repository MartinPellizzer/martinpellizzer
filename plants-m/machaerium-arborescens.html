Machaerium arborescens

Machaerium arborescens, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a delicate, fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along coastal areas.

Machaerium arborescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
