Machaeranthera canescens var. leucanthemifolia

Machaeranthera canescens var. leucanthemifolia, commonly known as the white coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow flowers with a central pink or red disk. The plant has hairy, gray-green foliage and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
