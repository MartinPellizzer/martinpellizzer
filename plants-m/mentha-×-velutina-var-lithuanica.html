Mentha × velutina var. lithuanica

Mentha × velutina var. lithuanica, commonly known as the Lithuanian velvet mint, is a hybrid mint species native to Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves and a distinct, aromatic fragrance. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies for their soothing properties.

The plant's botanical features make it a valuable addition to both ecological and horticultural settings.
