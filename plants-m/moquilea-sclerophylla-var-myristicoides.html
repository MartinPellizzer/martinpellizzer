Moquilea sclerophylla var. myristicoides

Moquilea sclerophylla var. myristicoides, commonly known as the myristicoides variant of the moquilea plant, is a shrub native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with small, leathery leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, aromatic fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
