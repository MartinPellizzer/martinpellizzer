Myrcia verticillata

Myrcia verticillata, commonly known as the clustered myrtle, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces dark blue to black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

Myrcia verticillata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in folk medicine.
