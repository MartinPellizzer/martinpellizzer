Metasequoia glyptostroboides subsp. neopangaea

Metasequoia glyptostroboides subsp.

neopangaea, commonly known as the African dawn redwood, is a rare and ancient conifer native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and distinctive, drooping branches that give it a graceful appearance.

The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are needle-like and turn a striking red color in the fall, contributing to its ornamental value.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a threatened species in the wild.
