Menispermum ovalifolium

Menispermum ovalifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf Dutchman's pipe, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, and its flowers are tubular, typically greenish-yellow with a red or purple throat. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain wildlife. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found along stream banks and in thickets.
