Marginatocereus marginatus

Marginatocereus marginatus, commonly known as the marginate cactus, is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom during the cooler months, typically in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping in arid environments.
