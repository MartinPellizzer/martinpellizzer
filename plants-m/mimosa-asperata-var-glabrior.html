Mimosa asperata var. glabrior

Mimosa asperata var. glabrior, commonly known as the smooth mimosa, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and bipinnate leaves that are soft and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spherical clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets.
