Mertensia paniculata var. alaskana

Mertensia paniculata var. alaskana, commonly known as Alaska bluebells, is a herbaceous perennial native to the northern regions of North America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white down. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators in woodland habitats.
