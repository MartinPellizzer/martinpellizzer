Merendera persica

Merendera persica, commonly known as the Persian mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, clustered flowers. The tree produces edible berries that are rich in nutrients and have been used in traditional medicine. Its bark contains fibers that have been historically utilized for making textiles and paper.

The plant is also valued for its role in supporting biodiversity, providing food for various wildlife species.
