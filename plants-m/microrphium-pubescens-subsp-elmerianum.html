Microrphium pubescens subsp. elmerianum

Microrphium pubescens subsp.

elmerianum, commonly known as the Elmer's watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America. It is characterized by its narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, with some emerging above the water surface. It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as lakes and ponds.

Its presence can indicate good water quality and is sometimes considered an indicator species for oligotrophic conditions.
