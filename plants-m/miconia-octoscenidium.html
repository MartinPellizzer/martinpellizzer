Miconia octoscenidium

Miconia octoscenidium, commonly known as the red miconia, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, round, red berries. Miconia octoscenidium is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and swampy areas.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets make it an invasive species in some regions.
