Monadenium pseudoracemosum var. lorifolium

Monadenium pseudoracemosum var. lorifolium, commonly known as the broad-leafed monadenium, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves and flowers. The plant features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically small, yellowish, and arranged in clusters, blooming in spring.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions with minimal water.
