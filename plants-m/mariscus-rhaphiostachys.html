Mariscus rhaphiostachys

Mariscus rhaphiostachys, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mariscus, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale pink to white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and light conditions.
