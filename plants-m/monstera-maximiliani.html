Monstera maximiliani

Monstera maximiliani, commonly known as the Swiss cheese plant, is a tropical climbing plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that develop distinctive holes and splits as they mature. The plant features aerial roots that help it climb and anchor to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage is evergreen, providing a lush, tropical appearance throughout the year.

Monstera maximiliani thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and humidity to flourish.
