Malva hederifolia

Malva hederifolia, commonly known as broadleaf mallows, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a soft, fuzzy texture. The plant produces showy, pale pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Malva hederifolia is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
