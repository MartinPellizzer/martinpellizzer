Macrotyloma dewildemanianum

Macrotyloma dewildemanianum, commonly known as the African horse bean, is a leguminous plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used as forage for livestock.

Its seeds are also used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.
