Microchilus ceratostele

Microchilus ceratostele, commonly known as the slender-stemmed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like appearance with narrow, erect leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are usually pale green or white with a subtle fragrance, and it produces a unique type of orchid root system known as a rhizome.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
