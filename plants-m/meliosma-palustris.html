Meliosma palustris

Meliosma palustris, commonly known as swamp meliosma, is a small evergreen shrub native to wetland habitats in eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swamps, bogs, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in terminal panicles, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Meliosma palustris plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various insects and wildlife.
