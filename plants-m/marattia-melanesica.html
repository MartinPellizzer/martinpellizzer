Marattia melanesica

Marattia melanesica, commonly known as the Melanesian water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Marattiaceae and is characterized by its large, compound fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams, where it anchors itself to submerged substrates. Its rhizomes are thick and fibrous, allowing it to spread vegetatively and form dense mats.

Marattia melanesica plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and contributing to water filtration.
