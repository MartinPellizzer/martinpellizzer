Melastoma tamonea

Melastoma tamonea, commonly known as the red-flowered melastoma, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces striking clusters of bright red bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Melastoma tamonea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
