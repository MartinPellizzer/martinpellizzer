Mimulus lewisii

Mimulus lewisii, commonly known as the Lewis's monkeyflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with showy, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as streambanks and meadows. Its flowers are tubular with two lips, a characteristic feature of the genus Mimulus.

Mimulus lewisii is also known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
