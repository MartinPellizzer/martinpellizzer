Maximilianea triphylla

Maximilianea triphylla, commonly known as the three-leaf maximilian's sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a grayish-blue hue, while its flowers are purple or pink and bloom in late spring.

It is drought-tolerant and well-suited for xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
