Melica pilgeriana

Melica pilgeriana, commonly known as the Pilger's grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, making it a dioecious species.

Melica pilgeriana is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife.
