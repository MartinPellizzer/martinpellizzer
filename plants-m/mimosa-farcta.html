Mimosa farcta

Mimosa farcta, commonly known as the hairy mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its sensitive leaves that fold shut when touched, a trait known as thigmonastic response. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas.

Mimosa farcta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, benefiting soil fertility.
