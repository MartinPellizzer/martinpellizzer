Myrcia cordifolia var. acuminata

Myrcia cordifolia var. acuminata, commonly known as the acuminata myrcia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves that are often reddish when young. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are red, fleshy berries that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
