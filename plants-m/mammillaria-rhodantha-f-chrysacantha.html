Mammillaria rhodantha f. chrysacantha

Mammillaria rhodantha f. chrysacantha, commonly known as the golden mammillaria, is a rare and striking cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, white to yellowish woolly covering, which gives it a soft, almost ethereal appearance. The plant typically grows in compact, rounded clusters, with short, cylindrical stems that bear numerous areoles. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or white, emerging from the areoles in late spring or early summer.

This fascinating plant is well-suited to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for desert gardens and cactus enthusiasts.
