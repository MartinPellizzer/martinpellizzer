Manulea cinerea

Manulea cinerea, commonly known as the silverleaf manulea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
