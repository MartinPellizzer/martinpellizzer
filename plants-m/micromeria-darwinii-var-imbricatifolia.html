Micromeria darwinii var. imbricatifolia

Micromeria darwinii var. imbricatifolia, commonly known as the Darwin's micromeria, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showing adaptations to harsh, high-altitude conditions. Its foliage is often covered in fine, glandular hairs, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.
