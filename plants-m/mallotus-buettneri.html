Mallotus buettneri

Mallotus buettneri, commonly known as the African hibiscus, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant has rough, hairy leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savanna habitats.

Mallotus buettneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
