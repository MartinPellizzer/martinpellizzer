Melaleuca tamariscina subsp. pallescens

Melaleuca tamariscina subsp.

pallescens, commonly known as the Grey Melaleuca, is a shrub or small tree native to northern Australia. It is characterized by its silver-gray to pale green foliage, which gives it its common name.

The plant produces small, bottle-shaped flower spikes that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy habitats.

The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand drought and salt spray.
