Magnolia tsiampacca var. glaberrima

Magnolia tsiampacca var. glaberrima, commonly known as the glaberrima magnolia, is a rare and striking plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive, elongated shape. The plant has smooth, glossy leaves that are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
