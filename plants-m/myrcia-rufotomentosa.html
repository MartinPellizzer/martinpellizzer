Myrcia rufotomentosa

Myrcia rufotomentosa, commonly known as the red-tomentose myrtle, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its hairy, reddish stems and oval-shaped leaves with a fragrant aroma. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, edible berries that are used in local cuisines.

Myrcia rufotomentosa is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests.
