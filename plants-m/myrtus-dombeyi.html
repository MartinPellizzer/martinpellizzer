Myrtus dombeyi

Myrtus dombeyi, commonly known as the Syrian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. Its fragrant foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and hedges.

Myrtus dombeyi is also valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional remedies for various ailments.
