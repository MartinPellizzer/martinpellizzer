Megastachya eragrostis

Megastachya eragrostis, commonly known as the silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowering period occurs during the late summer and early autumn, producing small, pale green to brown flowers.

Megastachya eragrostis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
