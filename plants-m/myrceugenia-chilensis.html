Myrceugenia chilensis

Myrceugenia chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean myrtle, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to southern Chile.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and tea tree. The plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. It produces small white or pinkish flowers, followed by edible, berry-like fruits that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife.

Myrceugenia chilensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
