Magnolia acuminata var. alabamensis

Magnolia acuminata var. alabamensis, commonly known as the Alabama magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and large, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a distinctive, upright growth habit with a smooth, gray bark that becomes fissured with age. Its botanical features include showy, cup-shaped flowers with numerous stamens and elliptical, glossy leaves.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in the region.
