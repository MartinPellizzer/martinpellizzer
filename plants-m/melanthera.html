Melanthera

Melanthera, commonly known as the blueweed, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its small, often blue or purple flowers that are clustered in composite heads. The plants typically have hairy stems and leaves, and they are native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. Melanthera species are often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

They play a role in supporting pollinators and are sometimes used in herbal medicine.
