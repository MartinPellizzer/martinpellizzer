Manilkara paraensis

Manilkara paraensis, commonly known as the Brazilian walnut or araçá-do-brejo, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles. The plant bears edible fruits that are yellow when ripe and contain a single large seed. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in folk medicine for their purported health benefits.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
