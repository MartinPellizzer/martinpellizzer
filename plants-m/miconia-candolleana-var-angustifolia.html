Miconia candolleana var. angustifolia

Miconia candolleana var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that are typically borne on short pedicels. Its fruits are round, fleshy berries that turn from green to yellow or red as they mature.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and showy inflorescences.
