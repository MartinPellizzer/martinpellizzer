Mystacidium gracillimum

Mystacidium gracillimum, commonly known as the slender mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has small, leathery leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along its stems. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are fleshy berries that contain seeds dispersed by birds.

The species is notable for its ability to photosynthesize while also drawing nutrients from its host, making it an important component of forest ecosystems.
