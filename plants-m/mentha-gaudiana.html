Mentha gaudiana

Mentha gaudiana, commonly known as the Mexican mint or wild mint, is a perennial herb native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems, opposite leaves, and aromatic essential oils. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and have a strong minty scent when crushed.

Mentha gaudiana is often used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and antimicrobial properties.
