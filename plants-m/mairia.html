Mairia

Mairia, commonly known as the "false hibiscus," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Malvaceae.

It consists of several species, many of which are native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. The plants typically have showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. They are often shrubs or small trees with rough, sometimes hairy foliage.

Mairia species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
