Monopetalanthus coriaceus

Monopetalanthus coriaceus, commonly known as the African sandmat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. Its botanical features include a woody stem and a unique leaf structure that helps it retain moisture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
