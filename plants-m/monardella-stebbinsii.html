Monardella stebbinsii

Monardella stebbinsii, commonly known as Stebbins' monardella, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. This species is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
