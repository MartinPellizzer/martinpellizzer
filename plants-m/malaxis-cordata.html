Malaxis cordata

Malaxis cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, heart-shaped leaves and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale yellow. The plant grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest undergrowth, and is adapted to thrive in humid conditions. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption.

Malaxis cordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
