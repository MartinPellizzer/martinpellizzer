Mertensia serrulata

Mertensia serrulata, commonly known as the serrated bluebells, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces clusters of bell-shaped, sky-blue flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is part of the Boraginaceae family, which is characterized by hairy stems and showy flowers.

Mertensia serrulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
