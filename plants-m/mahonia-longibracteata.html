Mahonia longibracteata

Mahonia longibracteata, commonly known as the Pacific silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are dark green and glossy, with a silver underside. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Mahonia longibracteata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
