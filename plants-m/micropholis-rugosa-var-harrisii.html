Micropholis rugosa var. harrisii

Micropholis rugosa var. harrisii, commonly known as the Harris's rough micropholis, is a shrub native to the eastern regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, bark-like stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

This species is adapted to rocky and sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.
