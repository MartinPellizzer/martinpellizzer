Mesembryanthemum crassulifolium

Mesembryanthemum crassulifolium, commonly known as the stonecrop or liveforever plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, white, and pink, typically during the winter months. It is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.

The species is also valued for its ability to propagate through offsets, making it easy to cultivate.
