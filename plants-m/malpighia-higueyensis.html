Malpighia higueyensis

Malpighia higueyensis, commonly known as the Higuey waxapple, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malpighiaceae.

It is native to the Caribbean, particularly found in the Dominican Republic and Haiti. The plant is characterized by its small, red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical climates and is typically found in coastal areas and dry forests.

The plant's botanical features include simple, alternate leaves and showy, yellowish flowers that attract pollinators.
