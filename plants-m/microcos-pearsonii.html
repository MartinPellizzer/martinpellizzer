Microcos pearsonii

Microcos pearsonii, commonly known as the pearson's microcos, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a small, herbaceous annual that typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a hairy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
