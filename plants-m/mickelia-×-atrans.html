Mickelia × atrans

Mickelia × atrans, commonly known as the hybrid candleplant, is a unique hybrid species resulting from the cross between Mickelia laurifolia and Mickelia tetragona.

It is characterized by its tall, candle-like stems that are often covered in a waxy, white coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Mickelia × atrans is native to southern Africa and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
