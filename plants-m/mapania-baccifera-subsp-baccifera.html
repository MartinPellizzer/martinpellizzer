Mapania baccifera subsp. baccifera

Mapania baccifera subsp.

baccifera, commonly known as the black mulberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia. It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, while the flowers are small and greenish-white.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitats.
