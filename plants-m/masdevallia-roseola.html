Masdevallia roseola

Masdevallia roseola, commonly known as the roseola orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, thriving in cool, humid environments with indirect light. Its leaves are strap-like and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its elegant appearance.

Masdevallia roseola is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral display and ease of cultivation under the right conditions.
