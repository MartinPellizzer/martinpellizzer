Musa × paradisiaca var. dacca

Musa × paradisiaca var. dacca, commonly known as the Dacca banana, is a cultivar of the banana plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elongated, pale yellow fruit with a thick, fibrous peel. The plant produces clusters of fruit known as hands, each containing individual fingers. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its sweet, soft flesh and is widely cultivated for both commercial and home use.
