Masdevallia nycterina

Masdevallia nycterina, commonly known as the night-flower orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to the cloud forests of Colombia and Ecuador.

It is characterized by its dark, velvety petals and sepals that resemble night-time blossoms, giving it its common name. The plant produces fragrant, night-blooming flowers that attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths and bats. Its leaves are typically long and narrow, adapted to thrive in the humid, shaded conditions of its natural habitat.

Masdevallia nycterina is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental beauty.
