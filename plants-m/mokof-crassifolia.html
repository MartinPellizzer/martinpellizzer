Mokof crassifolia

Mokof crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf mokof, is a unique plant native to specific regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Due to its distinct appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
