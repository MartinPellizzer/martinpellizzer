Musa speciosa

Musa speciosa, commonly known as the beauty banana, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in length, providing a lush, tropical appearance. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellow flowers that eventually develop into edible bananas, though the fruit is typically not cultivated for consumption. Musa speciosa thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and tropical landscapes.
