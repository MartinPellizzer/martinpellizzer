Mentha × piperita var. calophylla

Mentha × piperita var. calophylla, commonly known as peppermint with calophylla leaves, is a hybrid mint species characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring square stems and opposite, lance-shaped foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or pink, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its strong menthol scent and is often used in aromatherapy and herbal remedies.
