Muscadinia rotundifolia var. munsoniana

Muscadinia rotundifolia var. munsoniana, commonly known as the Munson muscadine, is a cultivar of the muscadine grape native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, round, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in the fall. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that are heart-shaped and often have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is well-suited for cultivation in regions with long growing seasons.

This variety is prized for its sweet, aromatic fruit and is often used in jams, wines, and fresh consumption.
