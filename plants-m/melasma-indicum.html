Melasma indicum

Melasma indicum, commonly known as the Indian nettle, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other nettle species.
