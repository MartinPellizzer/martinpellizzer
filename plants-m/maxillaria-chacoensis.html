Maxillaria chacoensis

Maxillaria chacoensis, commonly known as the Chaco orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America, particularly found in Paraguay and Argentina.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a mix of yellow, green, and brown hues, often resembling the appearance of a bee or wasp. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to thrive in shaded, humid environments.

Maxillaria chacoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
