Matthiola albicaulis var. alaica

Matthiola albicaulis var. alaica, commonly known as the Armenian white candytuft, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features dense clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a compact, low-growing habit with gray-green, linear leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
