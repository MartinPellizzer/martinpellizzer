Micromelum glanduliferum

Micromelum glanduliferum, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves with glandular dots. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, globose fruits containing several seeds. The bark is smooth and greyish, often peeling in thin layers.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
