Myobroma humilis

Myobroma humilis, commonly known as the dwarf myobroma, is a small, herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, often with a greenish or brownish hue, and it produces small, dry fruits.

Myobroma humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
