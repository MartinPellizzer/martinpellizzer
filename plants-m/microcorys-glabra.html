Microcorys glabra

Microcorys glabra, commonly known as the smooth microcorys, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has slender, upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture, adapting to arid conditions. Its small, yellowish flowers appear in clusters and bloom from late spring to early summer.

The plant is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
