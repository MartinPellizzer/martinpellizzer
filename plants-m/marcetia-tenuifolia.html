Marcetia tenuifolia

Marcetia tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender-leaf marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments in southern Africa.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are typically less than 1 centimeter wide, arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes, which are often submerged or just at water level. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is adapted to tolerate periodic flooding.

Marcetia tenuifolia plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
