Mentha × kerneri f. naszodensis

Mentha × kerneri f. naszodensis, commonly known as Hungarian mint, is a hybrid species within the Mentha genus.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and strong menthol scent, which are typical of many mint species. This variety is native to Hungary and surrounding regions in Central Europe, where it thrives in moist, temperate climates. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 60 cm, and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ornamental purposes and traditional herbal use.
