Malus tatarica

Malus tatarica, commonly known as the Siberian crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its ornamental white flowers and red or yellow berries that appear in the fall. The plant has small, oval leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding to its visual appeal. It is often cultivated for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Malus tatarica is also valued for its role in supporting wildlife, particularly birds, during the winter months.
