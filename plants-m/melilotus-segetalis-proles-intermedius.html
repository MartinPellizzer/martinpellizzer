Melilotus segetalis proles intermedius

Melilotus segetalis proles intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate field melilot, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in disturbed soils or along field edges. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind.

This species is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil and supports agricultural ecosystems.
