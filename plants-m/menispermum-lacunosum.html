Menispermum lacunosum

Menispermum lacunosum, commonly known as the lacunar arum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking, spatulate inflorescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often hidden within a leaf-like bract. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies in moist, shaded environments.

Menispermum lacunosum is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
