Melicope iboensis

Melicope iboensis, commonly known as the Ibo mastic tree, is a tropical plant native to the lowland forests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus and other aromatic plants. The tree is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it produces small, round fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing benefits.
