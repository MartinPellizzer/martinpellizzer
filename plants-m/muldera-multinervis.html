Muldera multinervis

Muldera multinervis, commonly known as the fine-veined muldera, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves with a complex network of veins, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the base of the leaves. Muldera multinervis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense undergrowth of rainforests.

Its unique vascular structure and ornamental foliage make it a subject of interest in botanical studies and horticulture.
