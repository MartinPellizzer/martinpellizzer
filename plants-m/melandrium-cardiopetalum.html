Melandrium cardiopetalum

Melandrium cardiopetalum, commonly known as the heart-leaved catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is typically found in moist, sandy soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats.

Melandrium cardiopetalum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens.
