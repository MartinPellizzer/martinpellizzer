Monodora unwinii

Monodora unwinii, commonly known as the African tamarind tree, is a member of the Anacardiaceae family.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by elongated, woody fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The tree is native to tropical regions of Africa and is valued for its timber and fruit.
