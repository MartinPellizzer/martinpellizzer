Malpighia melbensis

Malpighia melbensis, commonly known as the Brazilian wolfberry, is a small shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, bright red berries that resemble hibiscus flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. Its foliage consists of alternate, ovate leaves with a glossy surface, and it produces showy, five-petaled white or pink flowers.

The fruit is edible, with a tart flavor, and is sometimes used in local cuisines or traditional medicine.
