Monarda citriodora var. citriodora

Monarda citriodora var. citriodora, commonly known as lemon bee balm, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that bear showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The leaves are lance-shaped, aromatic, and covered in fine hairs, giving off a citrus-like scent when crushed. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
