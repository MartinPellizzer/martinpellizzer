Mediocalcar versteegii

Mediocalcar versteegii, commonly known as the Namib Desert lichen, is a unique symbiotic organism composed of a cyanobacterium and a green alga living within a fungal matrix.

It thrives in the extreme arid conditions of the Namib Desert, where it forms a crust-like structure on rocky surfaces. The plant's ability to retain moisture and perform photosynthesis in such harsh environments makes it a vital component of the desert ecosystem. Its morphology includes a dark, leathery thallus that helps reduce water loss.

Mediocalcar versteegii plays a crucial role in soil stabilization and nutrient cycling in its native habitat.
