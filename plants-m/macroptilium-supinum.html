Macroptilium supinum

Macroptilium supinum, commonly known as the prostrate sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that typically forms dense mats on the ground. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, and it produces small, round seeds enclosed in pods. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Macroptilium supinum is adapted to thrive in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.
