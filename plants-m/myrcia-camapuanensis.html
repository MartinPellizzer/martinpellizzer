Myrcia camapuanensis

Myrcia camapuanensis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
