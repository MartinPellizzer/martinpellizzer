Mazus japonicus f. senanensis

Mazus japonicus f. senanensis, commonly known as the Japanese Mazus, is a low-growing perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features prostrate or ascending stems covered with fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.
