Mostacillastrum volckmannii

Mostacillastrum volckmannii, commonly known as the Mexican wheatgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It features slender, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is well adapted to hot, dry climates and can thrive in poor soils.

Its resilience makes it a valuable plant for erosion control and as a forage species in semi-arid environments.
