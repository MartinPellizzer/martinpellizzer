Minthostachys acris

Minthostachys acris, commonly known as the Andean mint, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their cooling and antiseptic properties.

Minthostachys acris thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
