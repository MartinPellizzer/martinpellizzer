Mucuna bodinieri

Mucuna bodinieri, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a climbing leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing flat, bean-like seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various traditional foods. Mucuna bodinieri is also valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional medicine for its purported aphrodisiac and antidepressant effects.

Its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it an important plant in agroforestry systems.
