Mentha agrestris

Mentha agrestris, commonly known as field mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and have a strong, minty scent.

Field mint produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
