Monodora angolensis var. decidua

Monodora angolensis var. decidua, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. It is valued for its fragrant wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in dry, sandy soils and its ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
