Myrceugenia grisea

Myrceugenia grisea, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic properties.

The fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when mature and is edible.
