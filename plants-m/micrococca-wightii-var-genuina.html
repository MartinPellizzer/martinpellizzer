Micrococca wightii var. genuina

Micrococca wightii var. genuina, commonly known as the Indian snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a smooth texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
