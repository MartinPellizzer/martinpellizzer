Mammillaria melilotiae

Mammillaria melilotiae, commonly known as the melilot leaf mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a globular shape with prominent tubercles and spines that provide protection from herbivores. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus collections.
