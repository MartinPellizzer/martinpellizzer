Myrcia chrysophylloides

Myrcia chrysophylloides, commonly known as golden-leaf myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, with a distinctive golden hue on the underside, giving it its common name.

The flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are often clustered in dense inflorescences.
