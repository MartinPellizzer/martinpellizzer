Mangifera

Mangifera, commonly known as the mango tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Anacardiaceae.

It is native to South and Southeast Asia, with over 100 species identified. The fruit of Mangifera, the mango, is renowned for its sweet, aromatic pulp and is widely cultivated for consumption. The plant typically grows as a large evergreen tree, reaching heights of up to 30 meters.

Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated margin, while its flowers are small and clustered in panicles.
