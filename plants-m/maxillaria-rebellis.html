Maxillaria rebellis

Maxillaria rebellis, commonly known as the rebel orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its hairy, grass-like leaves and compact, upright growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a distinctive red or purple lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Maxillaria rebellis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
