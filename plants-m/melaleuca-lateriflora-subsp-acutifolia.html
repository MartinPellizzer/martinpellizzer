Melaleuca lateriflora subsp. acutifolia

Melaleuca lateriflora subsp.

acutifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf paperbark, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae. It is native to eastern Australia, particularly Queensland and New South Wales, and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in cylindrical inflorescences, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. Its bark is fibrous and flaky, giving it the common name "paperbark.".

This subspecies is often found in coastal and wetland areas, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.
