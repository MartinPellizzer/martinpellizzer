Mesembryanthemum bellidiflorum var. glaucescens

Mesembryanthemum bellidiflorum var. glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-flowered ice plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, often blue-green leaves that are adapted to store water, typical of arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and blue, which bloom in the morning and close at night. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
