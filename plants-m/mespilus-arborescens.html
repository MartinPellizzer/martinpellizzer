Mespilus arborescens

Mespilus arborescens, commonly known as the common medlar, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The tree produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in spring, followed by edible, ornamental fruits that ripen in autumn. The fruit has a tough, inedible outer husk and a soft, sweet interior when fully mature.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
