Mussaenda attopensis

Mussaenda attopensis, commonly known as the flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, white, tubular flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Mussaenda attopensis is also valued for its ability to attract birds and butterflies due to its nectar-rich flowers.
