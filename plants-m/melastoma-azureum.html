Melastoma azureum

Melastoma azureum, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly rough texture.

Melastoma azureum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
