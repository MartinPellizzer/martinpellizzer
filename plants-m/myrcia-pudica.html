Myrcia pudica

Myrcia pudica, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant leaves and clusters of red, fleshy fruits. The plant is often found in humid, tropical environments and thrives in partial shade. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it is known for its aromatic properties.

Myrcia pudica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
