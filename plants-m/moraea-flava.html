Moraea flava

Moraea flava, commonly known as the yellow moraea, is a striking perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of tubular flowers, which are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

Moraea flava is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
