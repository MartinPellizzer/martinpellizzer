Mosiera elliptica subsp. matanzasia

Mosiera elliptica subsp.

matanzasia, commonly known as the Matanzas elliptic mosiera, is a perennial shrub native to specific regions in South America. It is characterized by its elliptical, lanceolate leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinct, aromatic scent.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often blooming in the spring. Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticultural and ecological studies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
