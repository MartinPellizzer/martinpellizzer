Mimulus lucens

Mimulus lucens, commonly known as the golden monkeyflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has lance-shaped, hairy leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in stream banks and meadows.

Mimulus lucens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
