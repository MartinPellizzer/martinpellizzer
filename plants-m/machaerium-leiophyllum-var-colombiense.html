Machaerium leiophyllum var. colombiense

Machaerium leiophyllum var. colombiense, commonly known as the Colombian leathery senna, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a leathery texture and small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in disturbed or dry soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal applications.
