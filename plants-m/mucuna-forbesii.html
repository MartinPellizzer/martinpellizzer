Mucuna forbesii

Mucuna forbesii, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and distinctive, twisted pods that contain edible seeds. The plant is a climbing vine with trifoliate leaves and produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant also has a significant role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil with nutrients.
