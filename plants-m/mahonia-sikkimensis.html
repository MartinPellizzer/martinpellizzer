Mahonia sikkimensis

Mahonia sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim mahonia, is a shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with dark green, leathery leaves that have a distinctive holly-like appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, followed by blue-black berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Mahonia sikkimensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various garden conditions.
