Michelia lanceolata

Michelia lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf michelia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often fragrant and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically clustered in terminal panicles, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Michelia lanceolata prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
