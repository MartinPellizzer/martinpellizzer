Microchloa obtusiflora var. minor

Microchloa obtusiflora var. minor, commonly known as the small-leaf microchloa, is a rare and specialized grass-like plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its diminutive, narrow leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats on rocky or shaded terrain. This variety belongs to the Poaceae family and is known for its unique ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils. Its small stature and low-growing form make it well-adapted to its ecological niche.
