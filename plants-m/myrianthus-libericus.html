Myrianthus libericus

Myrianthus libericus, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It features showy, clustered flowers that range from yellow to orange, often with a red or purple calyx. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Myrianthus libericus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility in its native habitat.
