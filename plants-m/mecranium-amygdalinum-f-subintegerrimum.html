Mecranium amygdalinum f. subintegerrimum

Mecranium amygdalinum f. subintegerrimum, commonly known as the smooth-leaved almond mecranium, is a rare variant of the mecranium genus.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its fruit is a small, hard pod containing seeds that are rich in amygdalin, a compound related to bitter almonds.

This subspecies is often cultivated for both its aesthetic appeal and its historical use in herbal medicine.
