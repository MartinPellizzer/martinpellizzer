Mesembryanthemum schoenlandianum

Mesembryanthemum schoenlandianum, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or ozymandias, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, characterized by its fleshy leaves and flowers that open in response to sunlight. The plant features a compact, bushy growth habit with hairy, blue-green leaves that turn reddish under direct sunlight. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals that curl back as they age.

It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
