Melica cupani var. malacitana

Melica cupani var. malacitana, commonly known as the Malacitana brome, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it flowers from late spring to early summer.

This variety is often found in open habitats such as cliffs, slopes, and disturbed areas.
