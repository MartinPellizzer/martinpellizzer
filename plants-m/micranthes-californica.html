Micranthes californica

Micranthes californica, commonly known as the California mountain saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows. The plant features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, spoon-shaped, and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Micranthes californica is valued for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators and its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
