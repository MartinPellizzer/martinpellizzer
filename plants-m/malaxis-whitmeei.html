Malaxis whitmeei

Malaxis whitmeei, commonly known as the Whitmee's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has a unique structure with a single, elongated ovary. Its flowers are often clustered at the base of the stem and bloom during the cooler months.

Malaxis whitmeei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
