Mecodium bismarckianum

Mecodium bismarckianum, commonly known as the Bismarck meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like in appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or open woodland habitats.

Mecodium bismarckianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
