Mucuna venulosa

Mucuna venulosa, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, hairy pods that contain edible seeds. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in agricultural settings. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and it produces a significant amount of biomass.

Mucuna venulosa is also valued for its traditional uses in herbal medicine and as a source of protein-rich seeds.
