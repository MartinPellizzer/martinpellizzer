Mentha longifolia f. nicolaensis

Mentha longifolia f. nicolaensis, commonly known as the Nicolar mint, is a subspecies of the common mint.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and stream banks. It has a creeping growth habit with rhizomatous stems that allow it to spread vegetatively.

The leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications, similar to other mint species.
