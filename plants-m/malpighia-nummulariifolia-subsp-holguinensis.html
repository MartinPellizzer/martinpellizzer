Malpighia nummulariifolia subsp. holguinensis

Malpighia nummulariifolia subsp.

holguinensis, commonly known as the Cuban wax apple, is a small shrub native to Cuba. It produces small, round, red berries that are edible and have a tart, acidic flavor.

The plant has thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its native arid environment. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a fruit crop.

The species is part of the Malpighiaceae family, which includes many tropical flowering plants.
