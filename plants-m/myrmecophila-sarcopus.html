Myrmecophila sarcopus

Myrmecophila sarcopus, commonly known as the red spider orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking red and purple floral structures. The plant produces small, spider-like flowers that are highly specialized for pollination by specific insect species. Its growth habit includes a pseudobulb and linear leaves, typical of many orchid genera.

Myrmecophila sarcopus is often found in humid, shaded environments within the undergrowth of tropical forests.
