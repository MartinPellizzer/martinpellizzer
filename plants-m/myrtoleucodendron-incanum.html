Myrtoleucodendron incanum

Myrtoleucodendron incanum, commonly known as the silver-leaved myrtle, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that provides a striking visual contrast. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
