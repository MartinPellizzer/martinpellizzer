Marsdenia pumila

Marsdenia pumila, commonly known as the small-flowered Marsdenia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its slender stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with five lobes. It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including India and Indonesia, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Marsdenia pumila is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
