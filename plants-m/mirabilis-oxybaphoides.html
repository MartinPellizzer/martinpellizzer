Mirabilis oxybaphoides

Mirabilis oxybaphoides, commonly known as the silverleaf four o'clock, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique flowering pattern and attractive foliage make it a valuable addition to tropical and subtropical landscapes.
