Malpighia bicolor

Malpighia bicolor, commonly known as the red mombin, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its striking red and yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, edible berries that are rich in vitamin C and have a tart, citrus-like flavor. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and branched.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
