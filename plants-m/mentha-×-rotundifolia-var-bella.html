Mentha × rotundifolia var. bella

Mentha × rotundifolia var. bella, commonly known as the round-leaf peppermint, is a hybrid mint species characterized by its distinctive rounded leaves and strong, aromatic scent.

It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer, contributing to its ornamental value. Its leaves are used in herbal teas and aromatherapy due to their high menthol content.

This variety is prized for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.
