Moluccella lanigera

Moluccella lanigera, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
