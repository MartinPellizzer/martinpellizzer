Miconia amplinodis

Miconia amplinodis, commonly known as the broad-leaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers, followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds. Miconia amplinodis is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

However, it is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its aggressive spread and ecological impact.
