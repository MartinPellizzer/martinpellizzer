Malva tenuicalyx

Malva tenuicalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus, and is characterized by its palmate leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are pink to purple, with five petals and a distinctive central column, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
