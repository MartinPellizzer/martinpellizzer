Malesherbia pulchra

Malesherbia pulchra, commonly known as the velvetleaf tree, is a species native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows to a height of 5 to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange and are borne in clusters, adding to its ornamental value.

The plant is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.
