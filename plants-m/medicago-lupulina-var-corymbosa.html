Medicago lupulina var. corymbosa

Medicago lupulina var. corymbosa, commonly known as the yellow sweet clover, is a variant of the sweet clover species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in dense clusters. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides. It is known for its high nutritional value and has been historically used as forage for livestock.

Additionally, it contains compounds that can affect blood clotting, making it important to handle with care.
