Mammillaria lasiacantha f. stella-de-tacubaya

Mammillaria lasiacantha f. stella-de-tacubaya, commonly known as the Stella de Tacubaya mammillaria, is a unique cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense covering of white to pale blue tubercles. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in arid gardens and as a houseplant.
