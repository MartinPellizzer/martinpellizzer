Mucuna recta

Mucuna recta, commonly known as the straight cowpea or velvet bean, is a tropical legume native to Southeast Asia and parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces erect, slender pods that contain small, round seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Mucuna recta is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it an important plant in sustainable agriculture.

It also has a distinctive, sweet fragrance that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
