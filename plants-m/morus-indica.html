Morus indica

Morus indica, commonly known as the Indian mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to South Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, non-showy flowers. The tree produces edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source for silkworms. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Morus indica is also valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to provide shade.
