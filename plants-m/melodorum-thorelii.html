Melodorum thorelii

Melodorum thorelii, commonly known as the Thorelii's melodic orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture.

Melodorum thorelii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
