Mammillaria hyatricina

Mammillaria hyatricina, commonly known as the Hyatt's nipple cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is covered in white to pale blue spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
