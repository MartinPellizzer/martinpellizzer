Marcgraviastrum mixtum

Marcgraviastrum mixtum, commonly known as the mixed marble cactus, is a unique hybrid plant that combines characteristics of several species within the Cactaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of South America, where it thrives in rocky soils and full sun exposure. The plant features a compact, globular shape with a dense covering of small, white spines that provide protection from herbivores and excessive sunlight. Its flowers, which are typically yellow or orange, bloom in the spring and are often short-lived but striking in appearance.

Due to its hybrid nature, Marcgraviastrum mixtum is relatively rare and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
