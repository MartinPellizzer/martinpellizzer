Myrmechis

Myrmechis, commonly known as the mistletoe of the genus *Myrmechis*, is a group of hemiparasitic plants found primarily in Australia.

These plants have specialized structures called haustoria that allow them to attach to and draw nutrients from their host trees. They typically grow as shrubs or small trees, with evergreen leaves and small, often fragrant flowers. *Myrmechis* species are adapted to various habitats, including forests and woodlands, and some are used in traditional medicine.

Their ecological role includes providing food for birds and insects, contributing to the biodiversity of their ecosystems.
