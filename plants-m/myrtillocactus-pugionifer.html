Myrtillocactus pugionifer

Myrtillocactus pugionifer, commonly known as the prickly pear cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flattened, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by edible, fleshy fruits known as tunas. Its leaves are modified into spines, providing protection from herbivores and reducing water loss through transpiration.

The plant's ability to adapt to extreme conditions makes it an important component of desert ecosystems.
