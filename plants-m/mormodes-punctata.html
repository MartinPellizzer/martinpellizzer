Mormodes punctata

Mormodes punctata, commonly known as the punctate orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and narrow, elongated leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers with distinctive markings, typically blooming during the wet season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often found in understory layers of rainforests.
