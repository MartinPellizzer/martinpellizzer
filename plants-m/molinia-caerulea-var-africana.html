Molinia caerulea var. africana

Molinia caerulea var. africana, commonly known as African moorgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands, bogs, and swampy areas. The plant features tufted clumps with slender, dark green leaves and produces showy, spike-like inflorescences that range in color from blue to purple. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Due to its preference for wet conditions, it is often used in landscaping for water features and rain gardens.
