Mimusops natalensis

Mimusops natalensis, commonly known as the Natal tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elongated, narrow leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The tree produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of food.

The Natal tamarind is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil types.
