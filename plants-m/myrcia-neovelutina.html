Myrcia neovelutina

Myrcia neovelutina, commonly known as the velvet myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded areas of the understory in forests.

Myrcia neovelutina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
