Metagentiana australis

Metagentiana australis, commonly known as the Southern Metagentian, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of southern South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation in mild climates. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of hair.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural displays due to its delicate appearance.
