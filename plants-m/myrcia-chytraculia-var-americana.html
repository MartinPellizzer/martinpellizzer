Myrcia chytraculia var. americana

Myrcia chytraculia var. americana, commonly known as the American myrcia, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a glossy surface, while the bark is smooth and reddish-brown.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
