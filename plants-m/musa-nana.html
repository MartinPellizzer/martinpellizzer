Musa nana

Musa nana, commonly known as the dwarf banana, is a compact variety of the banana plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered bunches of bananas, typically weighing less than a standard banana. The plant has broad, fleshy leaves that form a rosette around the stem, which supports the fruit clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is sweet and edible, often used in both fresh consumption and cooking.
