Monvillea ballivianii

Monvillea ballivianii, commonly known as the Bolivian orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surfaces of trees and rocks rather than in soil. Its leaves are usually elongated and strap-like, adapted to conserve moisture in its native habitat.

Monvillea ballivianii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
