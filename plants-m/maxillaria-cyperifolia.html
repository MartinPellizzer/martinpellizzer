Maxillaria cyperifolia

Maxillaria cyperifolia, commonly known as the grass-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its linear, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its inflorescence consists of a raceme of resupinate flowers, and it is prized for its ornamental value in horticulture.

Maxillaria cyperifolia is relatively easy to cultivate, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
