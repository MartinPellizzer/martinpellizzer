Mentzelia dispersa

Mentzelia dispersa, commonly known as the scattered mentzelia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with five petals and a distinctive, tubular shape.

Mentzelia dispersa is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.
