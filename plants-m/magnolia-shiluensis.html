Magnolia shiluensis

Magnolia shiluensis, commonly known as the Shilu magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
