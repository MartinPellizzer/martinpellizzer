Manilkara bolivarensis

Manilkara bolivarensis, commonly known as the Bolivian sapote, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America, particularly Bolivia and northern Argentina.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces a large, round fruit with a thick, inedible rind and a sweet, creamy pulp that is edible.

Its latex, known as copal, has been traditionally used for medicinal and industrial purposes.
