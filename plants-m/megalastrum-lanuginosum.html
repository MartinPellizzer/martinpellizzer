Megalastrum lanuginosum

Megalastrum lanuginosum, commonly known as the woolly mallow, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
