Mummenhoffia alliacea

Mummenhoffia alliacea, commonly known as the garlic leaf plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that emit a strong garlic scent when crushed. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Due to its aromatic properties, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
