Malaxis luceroana

Malaxis luceroana, commonly known as the Lucero's Malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, contributing to its overall herbaceous appearance.

Malaxis luceroana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialty gardens.
