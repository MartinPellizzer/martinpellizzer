Micromeles meliosmifolia

Micromeles meliosmifolia, commonly known as the silky hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its aesthetic appeal.
