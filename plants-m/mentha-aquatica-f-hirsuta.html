Mentha aquatica f. hirsuta

Mentha aquatica f. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and its ability to thrive in moist environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the upper stem. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

Mentha aquatica f. hirsuta is often found in temperate regions across Europe and parts of Asia.
