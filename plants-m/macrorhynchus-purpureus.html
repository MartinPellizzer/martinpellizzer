Macrorhynchus purpureus

Macrorhynchus purpureus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, typical of the daisy family.

Macrorhynchus purpureus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also a valuable plant for pollinators.
