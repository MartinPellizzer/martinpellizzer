Mecranium amygdalinum f. longifolium

Mecranium amygdalinum f. longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved almond mecranium, is a variant of the mecranium genus with distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white in color, which bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a follicle containing several seeds, and it is often found in moist, temperate climates.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
