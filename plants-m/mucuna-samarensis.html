Mucuna samarensis

Mucuna samarensis, commonly known as the Samarensis velvet bean, is a tropical legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain flat, dark seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed areas.

Mucuna samarensis is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and potential use in agroforestry systems.
