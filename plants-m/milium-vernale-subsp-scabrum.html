Milium vernale subsp. scabrum

Milium vernale subsp.

scabrum, commonly known as rough millet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, rough hairs.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

This subspecies is notable for its hardy nature and historical use in traditional agriculture.
