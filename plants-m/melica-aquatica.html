Melica aquatica

Melica aquatica, commonly known as water melick, is a perennial grass species native to wetland environments.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found along riverbanks and pond edges. The plant has slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

Melica aquatica plays an important role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
