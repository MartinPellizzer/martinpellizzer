Malcolmia grandiflora

Malcolmia grandiflora, commonly known as the white sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Malcolmia grandiflora is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
