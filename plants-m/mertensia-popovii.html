Mertensia popovii

Mertensia popovii, commonly known as the Siberian bluebell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its attractive, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained, loamy soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Mertensia popovii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
