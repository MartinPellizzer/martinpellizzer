Macrosolen melintangensis

Macrosolen melintangensis, commonly known as the Melintang mountain orchid, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or rocks in primary forests. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding substrate.

Due to habitat destruction, it is considered endangered and is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists.
