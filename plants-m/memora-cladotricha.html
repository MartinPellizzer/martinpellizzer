Memora cladotricha

Memora cladotricha, commonly known as the velvet leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often blooming during the rainy season. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the humid environment, and it thrives in shaded, moist habitats.

Memora cladotricha is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
