Myriotheca arborescens

Myriotheca arborescens, commonly known as the tree mallow, is a shrub-like plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems that can grow up to 2 meters tall, often with a woody base. The plant has small, pale green leaves that are opposite and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches.

Myriotheca arborescens is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soils.
