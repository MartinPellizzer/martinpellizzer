Myrcia aethusa

Myrcia aethusa, commonly known as the velvetleaf myrcia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while the flowers are usually pink or red, attracting various pollinators.

Myrcia aethusa is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in folk medicine.
