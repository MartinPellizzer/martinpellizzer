Malpighia verbascifolia

Malpighia verbascifolia, commonly known as the woolly beautyberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, silver-green foliage and clusters of small, bright red berries. The plant belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. Its flowers are typically pink or white and appear in terminal clusters.

The berries are edible but have a tart flavor, making them popular in certain culinary uses.
