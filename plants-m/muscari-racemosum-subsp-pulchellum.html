Muscari racemosum subsp. pulchellum

Muscari racemosum subsp.

pulchellum, commonly known as the Persian grape hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region. It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet, often with a slight pinkish tinge.

The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a short, stout stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring bloom.
