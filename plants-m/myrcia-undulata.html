Myrcia undulata

Myrcia undulata, commonly known as the wavy myrtle, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with a wavy margin, giving it its common name. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically pink or white, and it produces small, edible berries.

Myrcia undulata is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests.
