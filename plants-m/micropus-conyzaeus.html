Micropus conyzaeus

Micropus conyzaeus, commonly known as the common ragweed, is an annual flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of tiny flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are wind-pollinated, and its leaves are alternate, rough, and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, fields, and other open areas.

Micropus conyzaeus is known for producing allergenic pollen, making it a significant contributor to seasonal allergies.
