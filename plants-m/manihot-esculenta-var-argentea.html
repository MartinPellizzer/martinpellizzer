Manihot esculenta var. argentea

Manihot esculenta var. argentea, commonly known as the silver arrow cassava, is a variant of the cassava plant characterized by its unique silvery-white veins on the leaves.

This plant is native to tropical regions of South America and is cultivated for its edible roots, which are rich in carbohydrates. The leaves are also edible when properly cooked, providing a good source of protein and vitamins. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its distinctive appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental purposes as well as for food production.
