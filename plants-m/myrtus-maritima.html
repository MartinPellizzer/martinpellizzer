Myrtus maritima

Myrtus maritima, commonly known as the common myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Myrtus maritima thrives in coastal areas and well-drained soils, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and perfumery.
