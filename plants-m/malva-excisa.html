Malva excisa

Malva excisa, commonly known as the cut-leaved mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Malva excisa is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can be propagated through seeds or cuttings.
