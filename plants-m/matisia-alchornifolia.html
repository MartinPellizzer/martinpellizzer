Matisia alchornifolia

Matisia alchornifolia, commonly known as the yellow matisia, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicle inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
