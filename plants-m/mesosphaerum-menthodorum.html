Mesosphaerum menthodorum

Mesosphaerum menthodorum, commonly known as the fragrant mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, showy flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of white and purple. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Mesosphaerum menthodorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
