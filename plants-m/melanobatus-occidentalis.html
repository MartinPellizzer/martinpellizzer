Melanobatus occidentalis

Melanobatus occidentalis, commonly known as the western blackbrush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has adapted to harsh desert conditions with deep root systems and waxy leaf coatings to reduce water loss. Its leaves are typically narrow and leathery, providing efficient photosynthesis in low-nutrient soils.

The western blackbrush plays a vital role in stabilizing desert soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
