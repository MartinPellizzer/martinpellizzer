Myrrhis longifolia

Myrrhis longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved sweetflag, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants such as parsley and carrots. The plant features slender, hollow stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in umbrella-like inflorescences, typical of the Apiaceae family.

Myrrhis longifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.
