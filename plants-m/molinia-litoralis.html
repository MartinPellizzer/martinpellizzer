Molinia litoralis

Molinia litoralis, commonly known as sea moorgrass, is a perennial grass species native to coastal regions of Europe and North America.

It thrives in salt marshes and sandy soils, showcasing adaptations to saline and frequently inundated environments. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of salt-excreting hairs. Its flowering heads consist of small, spike-like clusters of pale green to brown florets.

Molinia litoralis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
