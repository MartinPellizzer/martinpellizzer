Melaleuca glauca

Melaleuca glauca, commonly known as the silver melaleuca, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant features narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and usually white or pink, arranged in spikes, and bloom in spring.

Melaleuca glauca is known for its aromatic foliage and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
