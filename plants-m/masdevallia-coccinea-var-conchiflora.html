Masdevallia coccinea var. conchiflora

Masdevallia coccinea var. conchiflora, commonly known as the Crimson Masdevallia, is a striking orchid native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals and sepals, which are often adorned with intricate patterns and a velvety texture. The plant typically grows as a miniature orchid, with a compact stature and a single, showy bloom that emerges from a pseudobulb. Its flowers are highly fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses due to its specific humidity and temperature requirements.
