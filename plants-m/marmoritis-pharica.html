Marmoritis pharica

Marmoritis pharica, commonly known as the marbleleaf orchid, is a striking orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, mottled leaf patterns that resemble marble, giving it its common name. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate markings. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts who appreciate its unique appearance.
