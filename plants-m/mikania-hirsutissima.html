Mikania hirsutissima

Mikania hirsutissima, commonly known as the Mexican devil's ivy, is a vigorous climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Mikania hirsutissima is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

It is also sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
