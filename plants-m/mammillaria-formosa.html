Mammillaria formosa

Mammillaria formosa, commonly known as the Mexican beauty, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular plant with a dense covering of white to pale blue spines. The flowers are typically yellow or orange, emerging from the areoles in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

This plant is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a houseplant.
