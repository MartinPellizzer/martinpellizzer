Mentha royleana subsp. modesta

Mentha royleana subsp.

modesta, commonly known as the Kashmir mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Pakistan and northern India. It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that emit a strong menthol scent.

The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers during the summer months. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is valued in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and cooling effects.
