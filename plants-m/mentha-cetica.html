Mentha cetica

Mentha cetica, commonly known as sea mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found along the edges of the sea and in moist, rocky habitats. The plant has aromatic, serrated leaves and produces small, pale blue or violet flowers. Its essential oils contain menthol and other compounds that contribute to its strong, minty fragrance.

Mentha cetica is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies for digestive and respiratory ailments.
