Myristica laevifolia

Myristica laevifolia, commonly known as the Java nut or black pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries containing a single seed. Its fruit is valued for its aromatic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The tree is also known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
