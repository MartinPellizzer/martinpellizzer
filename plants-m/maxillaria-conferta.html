Maxillaria conferta

Maxillaria conferta, commonly known as the clustered maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically features dark green, lance-shaped leaves and has a pseudobulb structure typical of many orchids. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white with a yellowish lip, and they bloom in the summer months.

Maxillaria conferta is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
