Macrotyloma axillare var. axillare

Macrotyloma axillare var. axillare, commonly known as the axillary cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves with stipules. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
