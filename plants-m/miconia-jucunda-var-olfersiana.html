Miconia jucunda var. olfersiana

Miconia jucunda var. olfersiana, commonly known as the red miconia, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, typically with a deep red or purple hue on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year. It is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
