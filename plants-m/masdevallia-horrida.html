Masdevallia horrida

Masdevallia horrida, commonly known as the horrid masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that feature intricate patterns and a unique, sometimes intimidating appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree bark in its natural habitat. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to absorb moisture from the air.

Masdevallia horrida is valued by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements in cultivation.
