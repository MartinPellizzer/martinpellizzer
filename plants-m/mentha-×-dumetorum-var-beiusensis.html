Mentha × dumetorum var. beiusensis

Mentha × dumetorum var. beiusensis, commonly known as the Beiusensis variant of the purple mint, is a hybrid plant belonging to the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its distinctive purple flowers and aromatic, slightly minty leaves. This variety is native to certain regions of Europe, particularly in the Carpathian Basin. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in culinary or medicinal applications compared to other mint species.
