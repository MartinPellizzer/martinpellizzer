Miconia saldanhae var. subsessilifolia

Miconia saldanhae var. subsessilifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit tree, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers followed by bright orange, fleshy fruits resembling starfruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
