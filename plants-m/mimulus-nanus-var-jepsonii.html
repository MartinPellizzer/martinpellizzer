Mimulus nanus var. jepsonii

Mimulus nanus var. jepsonii, commonly known as the Jepson's narrowleaf monkeyflower, is a small annual plant native to California.

It typically grows to a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters, featuring slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are bright yellow, with five petals and a distinctive spurred shape, typical of the genus Mimulus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
