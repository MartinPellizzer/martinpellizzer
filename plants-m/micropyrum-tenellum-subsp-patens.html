Micropyrum tenellum subsp. patens

Micropyrum tenellum subsp.

patens, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is a perennial herb characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from blue to violet.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

The subspecies patens is distinguished by its more spreading growth habit compared to other subspecies of Micropyrum tenellum.
