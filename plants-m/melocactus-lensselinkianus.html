Melocactus lensselinkianus

Melocactus lensselinkianus, commonly known as the Lens-leafed Melocactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of northern South America.

It is characterized by its short, columnar stem and a dense cluster of small, oval-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by edible, red, fleshy fruits. Its succulent structure allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to harsh desert conditions.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
