Massonia dentata

Massonia dentata, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Massonia dentata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
