Machilus versicolorus

Machilus versicolorus, commonly known as the variable-laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of China, India, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic evergreen leaves, which can vary in color from dark green to reddish-brown depending on the season and environmental conditions. Its small, white flowers are typically clustered in umbels and are followed by small, round berries.

Machilus versicolorus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
