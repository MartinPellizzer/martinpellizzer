Myrcia cardiophylla

Myrcia cardiophylla, commonly known as the purple myrtle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and often have a reddish or purplish hue, while its flowers are usually pink or white.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its ecological role in the region.
