Maxillaria carrilloi

Maxillaria carrilloi, commonly known as the Carrillo's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces striking, fragrant flowers with a complex color pattern. The plant typically features a pseudobulb and long, narrow leaves that are arranged in a rosette. Its flowers are usually violet or purple, with a distinctive yellow or greenish throat.

Maxillaria carrilloi thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
