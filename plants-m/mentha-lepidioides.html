Mentha lepidioides

Mentha lepidioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mint, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and aromatic, often fragrant, foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes. Mentha lepidioides is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
