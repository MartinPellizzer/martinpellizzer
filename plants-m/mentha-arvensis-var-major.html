Mentha arvensis var. major

Mentha arvensis var. major, commonly known as field mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are often aromatic when crushed. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Field mint is known for its strong, minty aroma and is often used in traditional medicine and herbal teas.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and can be found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
