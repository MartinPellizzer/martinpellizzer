Maxillaria sulcata

Maxillaria sulcata, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its upright, grass-like growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, often with a distinctive zigzag pattern on the petals. Maxillaria sulcata thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate for orchid enthusiasts.
