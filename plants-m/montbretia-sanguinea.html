Montbretia sanguinea

Montbretia sanguinea, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, sturdy stems that bear clusters of fiery red, torch-like flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a rosette at the base. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its showy appearance and ease of cultivation contribute to its popularity among gardeners.
