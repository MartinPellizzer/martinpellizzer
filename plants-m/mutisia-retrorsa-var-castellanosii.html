Mutisia retrorsa var. castellanosii

Mutisia retrorsa var. castellanosii, commonly known as the Castellanos' mutisia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and shrublands, thriving in temperate to cold climates. Its leaves are usually alternate, lanceolate, and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native habitat.
