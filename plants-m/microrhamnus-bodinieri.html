Microrhamnus bodinieri

Microrhamnus bodinieri, commonly known as the Bodinier holly, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rhamnaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Vietnam, Laos, and Cambodia. The plant is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by small, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species.

The plant is often grown as an ornamental shrub due to its dense foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
