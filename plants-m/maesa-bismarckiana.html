Maesa bismarckiana

Maesa bismarckiana, commonly known as the Bismarck mahonia, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It features evergreen leaves that are deeply lobed and have a leathery texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
