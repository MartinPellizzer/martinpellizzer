Microsorum longissimum

Microsorum longissimum, commonly known as the long-leaf water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender fronds that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams. Its rhizomes spread along the substrate, anchoring the plant and facilitating the growth of new fronds.

Microsorum longissimum is often cultivated in water gardens for its ornamental value and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
