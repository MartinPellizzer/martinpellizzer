Miconia dulcis

Miconia dulcis, commonly known as the sweet miconia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a fleshy, red or orange berry that contains several seeds.

Miconia dulcis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses in indigenous communities.
