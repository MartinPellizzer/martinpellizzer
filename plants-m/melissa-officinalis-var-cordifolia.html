Melissa officinalis var. cordifolia

Melissa officinalis var. cordifolia, commonly known as lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to various garden conditions. Its leaves are often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for their calming properties.

The variety 'cordifolia' is distinguished by its heart-shaped leaves, which contribute to its ornamental value.
