Mikania houstoniana var. guatemalensis

Mikania houstoniana var. guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan wisteria, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and along stream banks.

Due to its rapid growth and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
