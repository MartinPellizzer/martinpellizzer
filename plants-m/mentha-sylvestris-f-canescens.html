Mentha sylvestris f. canescens

Mentha sylvestris f. canescens, commonly known as the hairy wood mint, is a subspecies of the wood mint plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and square, erect stems. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, making it a valued plant in traditional herbal medicine and aromatherapy.
