Mammillaria mutabilis var. leucocarpa

Mammillaria mutabilis var. leucocarpa, commonly known as the white-fruited hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical stems covered in white to pale yellow spines. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit is a distinctive white, fleshy structure that ripens in late summer.

This variety is prized for its compact size and ornamental value in arid gardens.
