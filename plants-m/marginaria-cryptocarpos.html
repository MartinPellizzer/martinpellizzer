Marginaria cryptocarpos

Marginaria cryptocarpos, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is a perennial herb that produces showy, white or pale pink flowers during the spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and a robust root system adapted to store water. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is drought-tolerant.

Marginaria cryptocarpos plays an important role in stabilizing desert ecosystems and providing nectar for pollinators.
