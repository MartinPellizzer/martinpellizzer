Madhuca betis

Madhuca betis, commonly known as the Betis wax tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed, which is encased in a hard, woody shell. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local communities.
