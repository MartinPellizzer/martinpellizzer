Melica cupani

Melica cupani, commonly known as cupani's brome, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and disturbed soils.

Melica cupani is often found in regions with temperate climates and well-drained soils.
