Macodes pulcherrima

Macodes pulcherrima, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red or orange flowers that bloom in dense clusters during the rainy season. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that provide a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display. Its bark is smooth and often exhibits a reddish or brownish hue.

Macodes pulcherrima is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
