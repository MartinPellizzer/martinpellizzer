Miconia auriculata var. theiformis

Miconia auriculata var. theiformis, commonly known as the hairy miconia, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in dense, woolly hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a berry that turns from green to red as it matures.

The species is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
