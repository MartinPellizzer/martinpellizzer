Malus carolinensis var. ellipsoidalis

Malus carolinensis var. ellipsoidalis, commonly known as the Carolina apple or ellipsoid apple, is a variant of the crabapple tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical-shaped fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces showy white or pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by the edible, tart fruits. Its leaves are oval and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in American horticulture.
