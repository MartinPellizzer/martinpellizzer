Myristica venosa var. pavonis

Myristica venosa var. pavonis, commonly known as the golden nutmeg tree, is a member of the Myristicaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that give way to the aromatic, golden-orange nuts. The fruit contains a richly scented kernel used in traditional medicine and as a spice. The tree thrives in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its unique botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
