Myrmecodia albertisii subsp. albertisii

Myrmecodia albertisii subsp.

albertisii, commonly known as the Albert's chambered cactus, is a unique epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its swollen, segmented stems that form a chambered structure, providing shelter for ants.

The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, typical of many cacti.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
