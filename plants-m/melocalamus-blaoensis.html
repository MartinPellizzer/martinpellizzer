Melocalamus blaoensis

Melocalamus blaoensis, commonly known as the Blao bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to China.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow leaves. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions. Its growth habit is clumping, forming dense clusters that can reach several meters in height.

Melocalamus blaoensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
