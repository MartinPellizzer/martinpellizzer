Myristica velutina subsp. velutina

Myristica velutina subsp.

velutina, commonly known as the hairy nutmeg, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark-barked trunk and compound leaves.

The plant produces distinctive yellowish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that enclose the aromatic seeds used for culinary and medicinal purposes. Its subspecies is notable for its dense, velvety pubescence on the stems and leaves, which gives it its common name.

The plant is also valued for its essential oils and has been traditionally used in local herbal remedies.
