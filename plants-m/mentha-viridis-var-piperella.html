Mentha viridis var. piperella

Mentha viridis var. piperella, commonly known as the narrow-leaf peppermint, is a variant of the green mint species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and a strong, aromatic scent reminiscent of peppermint. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, pale blue or purple flowers during the summer months.

This variety is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for its cooling and invigorating properties.
