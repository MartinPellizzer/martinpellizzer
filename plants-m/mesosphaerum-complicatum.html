Mesosphaerum complicatum

Mesosphaerum complicatum, commonly known as the spiral orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, spirally arranged leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its unusual foliage and delicate blooms.
