Myrsine modesta subsp. coriaria

Myrsine modesta subsp.

coriaria, commonly known as the coastal myrsine, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa. It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in areas exposed to sea breezes.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with a glossy surface and a slight pubescence.

The species is notable for its adaptability to coastal environments and its role in stabilizing dune ecosystems.
