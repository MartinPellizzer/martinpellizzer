Moldavica parviflora

Moldavica parviflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Moldavica parviflora is often cultivated for its delicate beauty and is a member of the Campanulaceae family.
