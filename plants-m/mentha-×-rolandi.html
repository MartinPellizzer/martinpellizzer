Mentha × rolandi

Mentha × rolandi, commonly known as peppermint, is a hybrid perennial herb in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly fuzzy leaves and vibrant green color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in whorled clusters. Mentha × rolandi is native to Europe and has been widely cultivated for its essential oils and medicinal properties.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
