Matthiola incana var. alba

Matthiola incana var. alba, commonly known as the white wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its fragrant flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
