Malveopsis scorpioides

Malveopsis scorpioides, commonly known as the scorpion weed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with branched stems and hairy leaves. Its flowers are small and yellow, often arranged in clusters, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
