Marsdenia muelleri

Marsdenia muelleri, commonly known as the Mueller's Marsdenia, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by follicles containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental cultivation in gardens and greenhouses.
