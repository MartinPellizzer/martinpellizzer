Malva alcea

Malva alcea, commonly known as common mallow, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other well-known species like cotton and hibiscus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in gardens and along stream banks.

Malva alcea is also valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
