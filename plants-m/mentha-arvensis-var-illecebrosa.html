Mentha arvensis var. illecebrosa

Mentha arvensis var. illecebrosa, commonly known as field mint, is a variant of the broad-leaf mint species.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, slightly fuzzy leaves and square stems. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate regions and prefers well-drained soil. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or purple, appearing in whorls along the stems.

It is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in disturbed areas.
