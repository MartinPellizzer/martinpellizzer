Mundulea tullearensis

Mundulea tullearensis, commonly known as the Tullear acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant has compound leaves that are often reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters. Its seeds are encased in pods and are adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

This species is well-suited for reclamation of degraded lands due to its resilience and ecological benefits.
