Melastoma laurifolium

Melastoma laurifolium, commonly known as the laurel-leaved melastoma, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. The plant features opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with a glossy, dark green surface. Its flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, and are usually pink or red in color.

The plant produces small, round berries that are edible but not widely cultivated for commercial use.
