Madhuca sarawakensis

Madhuca sarawakensis, commonly known as the Sarawak nut tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Sapotaceae family.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of Borneo, particularly in the state of Sarawak. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall and has smooth, greyish-brown bark. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a leathery texture.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are encased in a hard, woody shell.
