Malpighia cordata

Malpighia cordata, commonly known as the Barbados cherry or acerola, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and produces small, bright red berries that are rich in vitamin C. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the plant is often cultivated for its nutritional fruit.

The botanical features of Malpighia cordata make it an important species in both ornamental and agricultural contexts.
