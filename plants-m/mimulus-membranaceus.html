Mimulus membranaceus

Mimulus membranaceus, commonly known as the slender yellow monkeyflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and branching.

Mimulus membranaceus plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
