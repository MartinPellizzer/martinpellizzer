Maesa sinensis var. glabrior

Maesa sinensis var. glabrior, commonly known as the smooth maesa, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its glabrous (smooth) leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, thriving in acidic soils. Its leaves are elliptical with a leathery texture, and it produces berries that are often consumed by local wildlife.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
