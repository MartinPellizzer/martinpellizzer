Masdevallia × shuttryana

Masdevallia × shuttryana, commonly known as the Shuttley hybrid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Masdevallia leuconeura and Masdevallia tenuifolia.

It is native to the cloud forests of Ecuador and is characterized by its vibrant, often colorful flowers that range from red to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually leathery and strap-shaped, and it produces intricate, showy inflorescences.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
