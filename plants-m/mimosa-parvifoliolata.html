Mimosa parvifoliolata

Mimosa parvifoliolata, commonly known as the small-leaved sensitive plant, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

Mimosa parvifoliolata is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden settings.
