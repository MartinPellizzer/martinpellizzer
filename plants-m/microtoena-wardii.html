Microtoena wardii

Microtoena wardii, commonly known as the African orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky outcrops. The plant features a slender, upright stem with a few leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its roots are specialized for absorbing nutrients from the soil, and it is often found in association with mycorrhizal fungi.

Microtoena wardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
