Micropetalon gramineum

Micropetalon gramineum, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences that bear small, pale brown flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, swampy habitats and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization.

Micropetalon gramineum plays a role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various invertebrates and serving as a food source for waterfowl.
