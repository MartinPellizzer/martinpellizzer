Montiopsis berteroana

Montiopsis berteroana, commonly known as the Andean snowplant, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It belongs to the family Montiaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically succulent, adapted to the harsh, cold environments it inhabits.

Montiopsis berteroana is often found in rocky or alpine areas, where it thrives in full sun and well-drained soils.
