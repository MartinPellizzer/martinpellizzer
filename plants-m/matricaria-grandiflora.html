Matricaria grandiflora

Matricaria grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora chamomile, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its daisy-like white petals surrounding a yellow center, and it produces small, aromatic flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas and prefers well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.

Matricaria grandiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in pollinator habitats.
