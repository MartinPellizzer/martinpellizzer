Molineria jashpurica

Molineria jashpurica, commonly known as the Jashpur grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscape design.

Molineria jashpurica plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
