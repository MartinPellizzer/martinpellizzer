Mascagnia cordifolia var. fusca

Mascagnia cordifolia var. fusca, commonly known as the dark-leaved moccasin vine, is a variant of the moccasin vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is a climbing plant with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly velvety in texture. The flowers are small, fragrant, and white, often appearing in clusters along the stems. This variety is distinguished by its darker foliage compared to the typical form of Mascagnia cordifolia.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
