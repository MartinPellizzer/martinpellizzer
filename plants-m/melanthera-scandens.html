Melanthera scandens

Melanthera scandens, commonly known as the climbing aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its slender, twining stems that climb using tendrils. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a hairy texture.

Melanthera scandens thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in partial shade.
