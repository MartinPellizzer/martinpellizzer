Murraya cerasiformis

Murraya cerasiformis, commonly known as the cherry murraya, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a smooth texture with a slightly waxy surface.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
