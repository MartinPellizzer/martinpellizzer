Mahonia eurybracteata subsp. ganpinensis

Mahonia eurybracteata subsp.

ganpinensis, commonly known as the Ganpin's mahonia, is a shrub native to China. It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with leathery, dark green leaves that have a serrated margin.

The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, followed by blue-black berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its foliage and seasonal interest, making it a desirable plant for horticultural use.
