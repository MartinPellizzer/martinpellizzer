Mentha arvensihirsuta

Mentha arvensihirsuta, commonly known as hairy field mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Mentha arvensihirsuta is related to other mint species and is known for its aromatic properties.
