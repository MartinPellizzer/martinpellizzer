Miconia papillosa

Miconia papillosa, commonly known as the silky miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered with fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains numerous seeds.

Miconia papillosa is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets.
