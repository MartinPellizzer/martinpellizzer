Mesembryanthemum pallidum

Mesembryanthemum pallidum, commonly known as the pale ice plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ability to store water in its leaves, it is well adapted to drought conditions.
