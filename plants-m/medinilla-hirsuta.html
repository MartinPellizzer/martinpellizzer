Medinilla hirsuta

Medinilla hirsuta, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in its natural habitat. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Medinilla hirsuta is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and long-lasting blooms.
