Machilus longipedicellatus var. synechothrix

Machilus longipedicellatus var. synechothrix, commonly known as the hairy-leafed machilus, is a flowering plant species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in catkins, followed by small, spherical fruits.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
