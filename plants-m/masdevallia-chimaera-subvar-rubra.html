Masdevallia chimaera subvar. rubra

Masdevallia chimaera subvar. rubra, commonly known as the Crimson Chimaera, is a striking orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and pink inflorescences, which are adorned with intricate, velvety petals and sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage consists of narrow, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

This subvariety is prized for its ornamental value and requires specific conditions to thrive, including high humidity and indirect light.
