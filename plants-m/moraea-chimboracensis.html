Moraea chimboracensis

Moraea chimboracensis, commonly known as the Chimborazo lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the Andean region of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically blooms in the spring and is adapted to high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky or alpine conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
