Mastichodendron microphyllum

Mastichodendron microphyllum, commonly known as the small-leaved mastichodendron, is a rare and elusive plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, capsule-like fruits. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a critically endangered species.

Its botanical study is important for understanding the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
