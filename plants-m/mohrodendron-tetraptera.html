Mohrodendron tetraptera

Mohrodendron tetraptera, commonly known as the four-winged mohrodendron, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces woody, four-winged fruits that are adapted for wind dispersal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments within lowland forests.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
