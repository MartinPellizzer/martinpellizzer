Melloa quadrivalvis

Melloa quadrivalvis, commonly known as the four-valved melloa, is a rare and distinctive plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique four-lobed seed pods, which give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests. Its leaves are broad and leathery, often with a waxy surface to reduce water loss.

Melloa quadrivalvis is of interest to botanists due to its unusual morphology and limited distribution.
