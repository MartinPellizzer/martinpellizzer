Maerua denhardtiorum

Maerua denhardtiorum, commonly known as the desert thornbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, helping to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its woody structure and thorny stems provide habitat and shelter for small animals.

Maerua denhardtiorum is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy species in dry landscapes.
