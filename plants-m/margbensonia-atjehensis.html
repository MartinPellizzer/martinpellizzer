Margbensonia atjehensis

Margbensonia atjehensis, commonly known as the Atjehensis orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the mountainous regions of northern Sumatra, Indonesia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its intricate floral structure and vibrant coloration. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or in rocky crevices. Its flowers, which bloom in late spring, feature a unique combination of purple and white hues, making it a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
