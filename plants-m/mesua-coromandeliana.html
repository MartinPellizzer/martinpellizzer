Mesua coromandeliana

Mesua coromandeliana, commonly known as the Java flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers that range in color from red to orange, often appearing in clusters. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
