Monnina diversifolia

Monnina diversifolia, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features delicate, blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Monnina diversifolia is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
