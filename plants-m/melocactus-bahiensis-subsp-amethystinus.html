Melocactus bahiensis subsp. amethystinus

Melocactus bahiensis subsp.

amethystinus, commonly known as the amethystine melocactus, is a striking cactus native to Brazil. It is characterized by its columnar stem covered with dense, spiny areoles and bright red or purple spines.

The plant produces a single, showy yellow flower at the top of the stem during the warmer months. Its succulent structure allows it to store water, making it well adapted to arid environments.

The subspecies is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in collections due to its unique appearance.
