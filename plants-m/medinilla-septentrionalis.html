Medinilla septentrionalis

Medinilla septentrionalis, commonly known as the Northern Medinilla, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtle family (Myrtaceae) and is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and thrives in well-drained, acidic soil. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, providing a lush backdrop to its ornamental flowers.

Medinilla septentrionalis is prized for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
