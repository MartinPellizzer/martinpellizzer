Meliosma fruticosa

Meliosma fruticosa, commonly known as the creeping woodbine, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically borne in clusters along the stems. Its fruits are elongated, fleshy berries that mature to a dark purple color.

Meliosma fruticosa is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.
