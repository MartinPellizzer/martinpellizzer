Marattia attenuata

Marattia attenuata, commonly known as the slender fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Marattiaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, compound fronds. The plant has a unique reproductive system, producing both spores and seeds, which is uncommon among ferns. Its fronds are typically bipinnate, with numerous small, lance-shaped pinnae.

Marattia attenuata is often found in moist, shaded environments, such as rainforests, where it thrives in the understory.
