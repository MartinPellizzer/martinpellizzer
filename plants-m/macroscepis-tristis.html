Macroscepis tristis

Macroscepis tristis, commonly known as the weeping fern, is a unique plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a type of fern characterized by its drooping, feathery fronds that give it a distinctive, cascading appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its leaves are simple, lance-shaped, and can grow up to several feet in length.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it prefers natural, undisturbed habitats.
