Mariscus jacquinii subsp. angustior

Mariscus jacquinii subsp.

angustior, commonly known as the narrowleaf mariscus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States. It typically grows in moist, acidic soils along stream banks and wetlands.

The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are linear and often have a reddish or purple tinge at the base. Its small, white to pale pink flowers are arranged in dense clusters and bloom in the spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
