Madhuca insignis

Madhuca insignis, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the dry season. Its fruit is a capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
