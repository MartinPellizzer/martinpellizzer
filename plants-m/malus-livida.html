Malus livida

Malus livida, commonly known as the white apple tree, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. It produces small, pinkish-white flowers in early spring, which are followed by the distinctive fruit.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional medicine.
