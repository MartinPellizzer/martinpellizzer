Myrcia cordiifolia

Myrcia cordiifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Myrcia cordiifolia is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
