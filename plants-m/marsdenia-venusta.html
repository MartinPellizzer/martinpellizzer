Marsdenia venusta

Marsdenia venusta, commonly known as the velvet tangle, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its thick, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by follicles containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
