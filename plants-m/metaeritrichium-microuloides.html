Metaeritrichium microuloides

Metaeritrichium microuloides, commonly known as the small-leafed feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in width. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with feathery glumes, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Due to its delicate appearance and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
