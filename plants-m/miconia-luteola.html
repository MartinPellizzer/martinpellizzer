Miconia luteola

Miconia luteola, commonly known as the yellow miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its large, palmate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that are followed by distinctive, orange-red berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
