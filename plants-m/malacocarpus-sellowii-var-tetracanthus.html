Malacocarpus sellowii var. tetracanthus

Malacocarpus sellowii var. tetracanthus, commonly known as the Sellow's malacocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malvaceae.

It is characterized by its woody stems and palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The plant produces showy, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
