Mimulus maculatus

Mimulus maculatus, commonly known as the spotted monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers with a distinctive yellow spot at the base of each petal. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils along streambanks and forest floors. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Mimulus maculatus is a herbaceous perennial that blooms from spring to early summer.
