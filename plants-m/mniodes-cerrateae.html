Mniodes cerrateae

Mniodes cerrateae, commonly known as the Brazilian star orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking violet and white flowers, which are arranged in a star-like pattern. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in the humid, tropical environment. Its leaves are elongated and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Mniodes cerrateae plays an important role in the biodiversity of its ecosystem and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
