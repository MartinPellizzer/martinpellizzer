Mecodium inaequale

Mecodium inaequale, commonly known as the unequal mecodium, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Cape Region of South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom during the summer months.
