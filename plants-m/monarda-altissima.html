Monarda altissima

Monarda altissima, commonly known as tall blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant produces showy, fluffy blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly aromatic scent when crushed.

Monarda altissima is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
