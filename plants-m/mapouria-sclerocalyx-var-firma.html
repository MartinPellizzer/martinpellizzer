Mapouria sclerocalyx var. firma

Mapouria sclerocalyx var. firma, commonly known as the hard-shelled mapouria, is a variant of the Mapouria sclerocalyx species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight. Its botanical features include compact growth habit and distinctive, often woody, calyx structures.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
