Melandrium pulchrum

Melandrium pulchrum, commonly known as the showy campion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy to the touch. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Melandrium pulchrum is also known for its ability to self-seed, contributing to its naturalization in various habitats.
