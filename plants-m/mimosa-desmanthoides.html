Mimosa desmanthoides

Mimosa desmanthoides, commonly known as the white mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant has a shallow root system and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in open areas and disturbed sites. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with multiple stems and a dense foliage.

Mimosa desmanthoides is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
