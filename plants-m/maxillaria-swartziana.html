Maxillaria swartziana

Maxillaria swartziana, commonly known as the Swartz's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms in the summer and has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette. Its flowers are borne on elongated peduncles and are characterized by their intricate, three-lobed lip.

Maxillaria swartziana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
