Monanthes × subglabrata

Monanthes × subglabrata, commonly known as the hybrid African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white to pink petals. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and prefers well-drained soil in full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

The hybrid nature of Monanthes × subglabrata results from crosses between Monanthes species, contributing to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
