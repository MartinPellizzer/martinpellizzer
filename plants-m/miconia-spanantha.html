Miconia spanantha

Miconia spanantha, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a tropical shrub native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces bright blue berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. Miconia spanantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in humid conditions.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
