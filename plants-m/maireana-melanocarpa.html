Maireana melanocarpa

Maireana melanocarpa, commonly known as the black-fruited maireana, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark blue to black berries. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and leathery, providing drought tolerance.

The fruit is often consumed by native wildlife and has been used traditionally by Indigenous Australians.
