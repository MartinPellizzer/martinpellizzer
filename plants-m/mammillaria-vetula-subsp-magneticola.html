Mammillaria vetula subsp. magneticola

Mammillaria vetula subsp.

magneticola, commonly known as the magnetic ball cactus, is a small, globular cactus native to Mexico. It features a dense cluster of short, cylindrical spines and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers.

The plant has a compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
