Malva alcea proles ribifolia

Malva alcea proles ribifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a hairy, velvety texture on its stems and leaves, which gives it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
