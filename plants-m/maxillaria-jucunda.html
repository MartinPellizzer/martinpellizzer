Maxillaria jucunda

Maxillaria jucunda, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb and linear leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white or grayish hairs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive pink or purple coloration and a unique slipper-shaped lip. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Maxillaria jucunda is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
