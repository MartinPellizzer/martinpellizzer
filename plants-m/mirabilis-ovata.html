Mirabilis ovata

Mirabilis ovata, commonly known as the eggplant flower or the eggplant vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often bi-colored flowers that resemble small eggs, hence its common name. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are broad and heart-shaped, and the plant can grow as a vine or a shrub depending on the conditions.
