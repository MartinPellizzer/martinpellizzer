Macaranga havilandii var. resecta

Macaranga havilandii var. resecta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf macaranga, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in tropical rainforests. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in panicles, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

The leaves are known for their high latex content, which can be used in traditional practices.
