Myrcia graciliflora

Myrcia graciliflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and clove. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The fruit, which is small and red, contains seeds that are dispersed by birds and other animals.
