Mammillaria speciosa

Mammillaria speciosa, commonly known as the showy mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems covered in white to pale green tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, spherical fruits. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in arid gardens.
