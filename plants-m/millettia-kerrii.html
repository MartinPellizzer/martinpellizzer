Millettia kerrii

Millettia kerrii, commonly known as the Kerri's millettia, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually pinnate with 5 to 9 leaflets, and its showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces elongated pods containing flat, winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
