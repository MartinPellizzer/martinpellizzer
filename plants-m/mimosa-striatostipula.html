Mimosa striatostipula

Mimosa striatostipula, commonly known as the striate mimosa, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fluffy flower clusters. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically arranged in spherical or cylindrical heads. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into multiple pairs of small leaflets.

Mimosa striatostipula is often found in moist, tropical environments and is valued for its ornamental appearance.
