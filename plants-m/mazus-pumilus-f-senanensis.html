Mazus pumilus f. senanensis

Mazus pumilus f. senanensis, commonly known as the Senanese dwarf mazus, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features small, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in specialty gardens.
