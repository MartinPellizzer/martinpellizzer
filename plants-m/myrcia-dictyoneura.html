Myrcia dictyoneura

Myrcia dictyoneura, commonly known as the narrow-leaf myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the rainforests of eastern Australia, where it typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is often used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.

Myrcia dictyoneura plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
