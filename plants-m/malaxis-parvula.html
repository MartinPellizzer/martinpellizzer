Malaxis parvula

Malaxis parvula, commonly known as the small malaxis orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant has a short, creeping rhizome and produces a single, narrow leaf. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

Malaxis parvula is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is often found in shaded, moist environments.
