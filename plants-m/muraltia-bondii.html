Muraltia bondii

Muraltia bondii, commonly known as the African firethorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, glossy leaves and produces clusters of bright red, berry-like fruits. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

The fruits are edible but have a tart, acidic flavor, often used in local cuisines.
