Meriania macrophylla subsp. peltata

Meriania macrophylla subsp.

peltata, commonly known as the broad-leaf meriania, is a species native to tropical regions of South America. It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its large, peltate (shield-shaped) leaves that are often deeply lobed.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its foliage is evergreen, providing dense shade, and it is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.

The subspecies is distinguished by its broader leaf morphology compared to other members of the species.
