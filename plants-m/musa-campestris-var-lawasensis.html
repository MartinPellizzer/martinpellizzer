Musa campestris var. lawasensis

Musa campestris var. lawasensis, commonly known as the Lawas banana, is a cultivar native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia.

It is a member of the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, elongated, and usually yellow or green bananas. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, with a pseudostem formed from tightly packed leaf sheaths. It produces clusters of berries, which are typically harvested when they reach a mature yellow color.

This variety is valued for its high yield and culinary uses in local cuisines.
