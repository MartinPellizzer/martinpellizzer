Microthelys nutantiflora

Microthelys nutantiflora, commonly known as the creeping starflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with numerous small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine layer of silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in swampy or marshy habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
