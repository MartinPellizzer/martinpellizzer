Maerua purpurascens

Maerua purpurascens, commonly known as the purple leaf tree, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple-tinged leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with deep roots that help it access water from deeper soil layers. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
