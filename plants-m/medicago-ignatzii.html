Medicago ignatzii

Medicago ignatzii, commonly known as Ignatz's clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Medicago ignatzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
