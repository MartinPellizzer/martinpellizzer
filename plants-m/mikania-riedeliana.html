Mikania riedeliana

Mikania riedeliana, commonly known as the Riedel's mile-a-minute weed, is a vigorous climbing vine native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth rate, often reaching several meters in a short period. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. Its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs, and it is known for its ability to spread quickly through both seeds and runners.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered a problematic invasive species in some areas.
