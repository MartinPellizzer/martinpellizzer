Meconopsis × ramsdeniorum

Meconopsis × ramsdeniorum, commonly known as the Himalayan blue poppy, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Meconopsis betonicifolia and Meconopsis cambrica.

It is native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas, where it thrives in cool, moist, and shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its striking blue or violet petals, which are typically large and showy, making it a popular ornamental species. It has fibrous roots and a short, sturdy stem that supports its delicate flowers.

Meconopsis × ramsdeniorum is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
