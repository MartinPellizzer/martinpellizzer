Manfreda brunnea

Manfreda brunnea, commonly known as the brown manfreda, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a reddish or brownish tinge, especially along the edges. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often appearing in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for arid or semi-arid climates.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular ornamental plant among succulent enthusiasts.
