Miconia linneoides

Miconia linneoides, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that are often accompanied by conspicuous bracts. Miconia linneoides is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed habitats, making it an invasive species in some areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable plant in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
