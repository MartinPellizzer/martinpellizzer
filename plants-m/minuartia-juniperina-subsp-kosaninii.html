Minuartia juniperina subsp. kosaninii

Minuartia juniperina subsp.

kosaninii, commonly known as the Kosanin's rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its compact rosette of blue-green, fleshy leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. It is adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

This subspecies is native to Central Asia and is often found in mountainous regions where it contributes to the alpine flora.
