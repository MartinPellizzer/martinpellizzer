Myricaria longifolia var. davurica

Myricaria longifolia var. davurica, commonly known as the Davurian myricacia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, featuring a deep root system and thick, waxy leaves to reduce water loss. It is often found in sandy soils and can thrive in areas with low precipitation.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes used in landscaping in drought-tolerant gardens.
