Melia candollei

Melia candollei, commonly known as the African chinaberry tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into fleshy, orange berries. Its fruit is edible for humans but toxic to livestock and wildlife.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
