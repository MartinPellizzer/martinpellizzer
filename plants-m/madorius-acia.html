Madorius acia

Madorius acia, commonly known as the silverleaf spurge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. It is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its toxic sap, it is considered a potential weed in agricultural areas.
