Melampyrum laxum var. obtusifolium

Melampyrum laxum var. obtusifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved burning bush, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grassy areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in racemes, often appearing in late summer.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in shaded environments while still producing its distinctive blooms.
