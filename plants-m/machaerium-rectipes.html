Machaerium rectipes

Machaerium rectipes, commonly known as the straight-stemmed machaerium, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically alternate in arrangement. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in dense, racemose inflorescences, and are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Machaerium rectipes is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
