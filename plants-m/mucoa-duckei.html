Mucoa duckei

Mucoa duckei, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Mucoa duckei is known for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.

Its rapid growth and adaptability contribute to its status as an invasive species in many regions.
