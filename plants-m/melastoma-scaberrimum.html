Melastoma scaberrimum

Melastoma scaberrimum, commonly known as the rough-stemmed melastoma, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom during the rainy season. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Melastoma scaberrimum is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
