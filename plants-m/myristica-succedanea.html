Myristica succedanea

Myristica succedanea, commonly known as the nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Banda Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is closely related to the nutmeg tree (Myristica fragrans). The plant produces a unique fruit that contains both the spice nutmeg and mace, which are harvested from the seed and aril, respectively. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a fragrant aroma.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated for its aromatic oils and medicinal properties.
