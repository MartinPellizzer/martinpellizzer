Menodora pinnatisecta var. missionum

Menodora pinnatisecta var. missionum, commonly known as the Mission pepper or Mission plant, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound, pinnatisected leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive, fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
