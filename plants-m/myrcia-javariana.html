Myrcia javariana

Myrcia javariana, commonly known as the Java myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. It produces small, fleshy berries that are often eaten by birds and other wildlife.

Myrcia javariana is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some cultures for its aromatic properties.
