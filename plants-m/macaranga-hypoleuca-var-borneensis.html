Macaranga hypoleuca var. borneensis

Macaranga hypoleuca var. borneensis, commonly known as Bornean macaranga, is a tropical tree species native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its rapid growth rate, making it a common component of secondary forests. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.

The species also plays a role in supporting local biodiversity by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
