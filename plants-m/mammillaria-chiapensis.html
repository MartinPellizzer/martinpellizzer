Mammillaria chiapensis

Mammillaria chiapensis, commonly known as the Chiapas mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, globular shape and is covered in white to grayish spines. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces yellow flowers during the spring or early summer.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated by cactus enthusiasts.
