Macaglia

Macaglia, commonly known as the "Spanish bellflower," is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Macaglia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
