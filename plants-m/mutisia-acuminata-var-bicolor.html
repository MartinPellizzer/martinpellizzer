Mutisia acuminata var. bicolor

Mutisia acuminata var. bicolor, commonly known as the bicolor mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is notable for its showy, yellow-and-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a preference for well-drained, sandy soils.

The species is often found in dry forest and scrubland habitats.
