Meistera elephantorum

Meistera elephantorum, commonly known as the elephant's trunk plant, is a striking tropical perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 inches in length, resembling an elephant's trunk. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture in its soil.

Meistera elephantorum is popular in ornamental gardening for its dramatic foliage and unique appearance.
