Madia gracilis subsp. pilosa

Madia gracilis subsp.

pilosa, commonly known as the softleaf madia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and hairy foliage, which give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its subspecies name, *pilosa*, refers to the soft, hairy texture of its leaves.
