Muscarella claviculata

Muscarella claviculata, commonly known as the clustered muscari, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun to partial shade. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, which bloom in spring. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are evergreen in mild climates.

Muscarella claviculata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance and ease of care.
