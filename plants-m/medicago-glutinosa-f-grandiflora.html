Medicago glutinosa f. grandiflora

Medicago glutinosa f. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered goat's rue, is a variant of the goat's rue species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its erect, branching stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces showy, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in racemes during the summer months. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by insects, and the plant is often found in dry, rocky soils.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
