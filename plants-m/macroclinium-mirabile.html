Macroclinium mirabile

Macroclinium mirabile, commonly known as the miraculous orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and glossy, and it produces fragrant blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
