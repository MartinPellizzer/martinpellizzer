Mimosa unijuga

Mimosa unijuga, commonly known as the single-seed mimosa, is a species of flowering plant in the Fabaceae family.

It is native to parts of South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay. The plant is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or disturbed areas.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
