Marsdenia rotata

Marsdenia rotata, commonly known as the twisted Marsdenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its twisted, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Marsdenia rotata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
