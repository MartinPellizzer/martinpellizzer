Malus toringo var. incisa

Malus toringo var. incisa, commonly known as the Japanese flowering crabapple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, ornamental flowers that bloom in early spring, often in shades of white or pink. The leaves are ovate and have serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The fruit, which is small and typically red or yellow, is edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden settings.
