Maranta moritziana

Maranta moritziana, commonly known as the velvetleaf arrowroot, is a tropical flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family, which includes many ornamental plants known for their striking foliage. The plant is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves that often display intricate patterns and a deep red or purple underside. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which appear during the warmer months.

Maranta moritziana is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in shaded environments.
