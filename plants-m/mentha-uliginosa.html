Mentha uliginosa

Mentha uliginosa, commonly known as marsh mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and moist environments in the Northern Hemisphere.

It has square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often aromatic when crushed. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It is characterized by its strong mint-like scent and is often found in bogs, fens, and along stream banks.

Mentha uliginosa is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
