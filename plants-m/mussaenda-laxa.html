Mussaenda laxa

Mussaenda laxa, commonly known as the silky Mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Mussaenda laxa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
