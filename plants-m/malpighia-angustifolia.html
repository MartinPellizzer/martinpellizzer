Malpighia angustifolia

Malpighia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf acerola, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malpighiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas, particularly Central and South America. The plant produces small, bright red fruits that are rich in vitamin C and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the edible fruits it yields.
