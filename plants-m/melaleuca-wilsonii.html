Melaleuca wilsonii

Melaleuca wilsonii, commonly known as the Wilson's bottlebrush, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, particularly found in the coastal regions of New South Wales and Queensland. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, cylindrical flower spikes that resemble a bottlebrush, typically ranging in color from red to pink. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in a whitish, powdery coating.

Melaleuca wilsonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun exposure.
