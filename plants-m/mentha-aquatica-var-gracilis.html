Mentha aquatica var. gracilis

Mentha aquatica var. gracilis, commonly known as slender water mint, is a perennial herb native to moist environments in Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in wet meadows, stream banks, and marshy areas, thriving in water-saturated soils. The plant features slender, upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in late summer.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in herbal teas or as a decorative plant in wet gardens.
