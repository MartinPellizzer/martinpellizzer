Margaris barbigera

Margaris barbigera, commonly known as the feathered dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties. Its flowers produce a fluffy seed head that disperses seeds by wind, aiding in its propagation.

The plant is often found in dry, sunny areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
