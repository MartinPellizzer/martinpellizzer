Mikania roraimensis

Mikania roraimensis, commonly known as the Roraima morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the Amazon Basin.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy blue to violet flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its seeds are dispersed by birds, contributing to its widespread distribution.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive if not managed properly.
