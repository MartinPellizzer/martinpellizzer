Melissa occidentalis

Melissa occidentalis, commonly known as lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, serrated leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbalists. Its essential oils, rich in citral and geraniol, contribute to its calming properties.

Melissa occidentalis is often used in traditional medicine for its soothing effects on the nervous system.
