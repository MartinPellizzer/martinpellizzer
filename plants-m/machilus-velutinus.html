Machilus velutinus

Machilus velutinus, commonly known as the velvet chinquapin, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and simple, alternate leaves that are glossy on top and hairy underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing one or two seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
