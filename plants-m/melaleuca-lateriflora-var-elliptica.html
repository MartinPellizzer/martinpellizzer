Melaleuca lateriflora var. elliptica

Melaleuca lateriflora var. elliptica, commonly known as the broadleaf paperbark tree, is a species native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a mottled pattern of white and grey. The plant produces small, bottlebrush-like flowers that are typically pink or red, appearing in clusters. Its leaves are elliptical in shape, hence the variety name, and are usually narrow and leathery.

This plant is often found in coastal and heathland environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
