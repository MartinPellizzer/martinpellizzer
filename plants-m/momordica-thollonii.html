Momordica thollonii

Momordica thollonii, commonly known as the African bitter melon, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourds and cucumbers. The plant produces distinctive, spiny fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with five petals.

The fruit is used in traditional medicine and as a vegetable in some regions, though it is generally bitter and requires careful preparation.
