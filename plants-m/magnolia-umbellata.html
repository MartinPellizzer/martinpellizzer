Magnolia umbellata

Magnolia umbellata, commonly known as the western magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves appear. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its bark is smooth and gray, with a distinctive flaky texture.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and spring blossoms.
