Malpighia cuneata

Malpighia cuneata, commonly known as the sour sop or West Indian cherry, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces bright orange, fleshy berries that are rich in vitamin C and other nutrients. Its botanical features include a woody stem, alternate leaves, and a unique fruit structure with a spiky exterior.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.
