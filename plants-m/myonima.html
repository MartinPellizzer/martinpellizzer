Myonima

Myonima, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the family Leguminosae, also known as the legume or bean family. The plant is characterized by its feathery, compound leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that resemble raindrops. Its fruit is a pod that splits open to release seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Myonima is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
