Magnolia georgii

Magnolia georgii, commonly known as the Georgia magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth texture and a dark green upper surface. The tree has a spreading, rounded crown and can grow up to 30 feet tall.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and parks.
