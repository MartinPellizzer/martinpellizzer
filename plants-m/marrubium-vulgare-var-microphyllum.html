Marrubium vulgare var. microphyllum

Marrubium vulgare var. microphyllum, commonly known as lesser horehound, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, gray-green leaves with a hairy surface, and its flowers are typically blue or violet, arranged in clusters. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in dry, rocky soils. It has a strong, bitter scent and is known for its medicinal properties.

The variety microphyllum is distinguished by its smaller leaves compared to the typical form of Marrubium vulgare.
