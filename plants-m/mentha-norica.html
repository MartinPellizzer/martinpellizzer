Mentha norica

Mentha norica, commonly known as Alpine mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. Mentha norica thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
