Malacothrix obtusa

Malacothrix obtusa, commonly known as the broadleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy seed heads that disperse wind-borne seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Malacothrix obtusa is a hardy perennial that is relatively easy to grow in gardens with well-drained soil.
