Mucuna klitgaardiae

Mucuna klitgaardiae, commonly known as the red velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety red foliage and yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing flat, dark brown seeds. It is a member of the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, enriching the soil.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
