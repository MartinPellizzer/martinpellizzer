Mentha subthermalis

Mentha subthermalis, commonly known as the subalpine mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a strong aromatic scent. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or purple, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Mentha subthermalis is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
