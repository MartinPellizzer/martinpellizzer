Melocactus bellavistensis

Melocactus bellavistensis, commonly known as the bellavistensis melocactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and the presence of a spiny, conical or bell-shaped structure at its apex. The plant produces white to pale yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically brief in duration. Its thick, water-storing stems help it survive in harsh, dry environments.

This cactus is well adapted to extreme conditions, making it a notable example of desert flora.
