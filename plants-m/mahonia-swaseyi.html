Mahonia swaseyi

Mahonia swaseyi, commonly known as the Cascade barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and yellowish-green foliage that turns red in the fall. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially sunny environments.

Mahonia swaseyi is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native gardens.
