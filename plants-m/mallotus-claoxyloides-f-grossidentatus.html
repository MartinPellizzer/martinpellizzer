Mallotus claoxyloides f. grossidentatus

Mallotus claoxyloides f. grossidentatus, commonly known as the broad-leafed mallotus, is a variant of the mallotus genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, spherical capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental foliage and is occasionally used in traditional medicine.
