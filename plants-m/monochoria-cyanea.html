Monochoria cyanea

Monochoria cyanea, commonly known as the blue water hyacinth, is a floating aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are slightly waxy and have a distinct midrib. The plant produces small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual element to water bodies. It is often found in slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and rice fields.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive, impacting water quality and native aquatic ecosystems.
