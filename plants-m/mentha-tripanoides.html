Mentha tripanoides

Mentha tripanoides, commonly known as wild mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its distinct minty fragrance.

Mentha tripanoides is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodland edges and stream banks.
