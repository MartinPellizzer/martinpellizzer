Myrcia neoestrellensis

Myrcia neoestrellensis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, typical of the Myrtaceae family.

Myrcia neoestrellensis is often found in shaded, moist environments such as undergrowth in dense forests.
