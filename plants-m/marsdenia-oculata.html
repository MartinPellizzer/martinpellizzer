Marsdenia oculata

Marsdenia oculata, commonly known as the eye-leaf vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and distinctive leaves that often have a prominent yellow spot near the base, resembling an eye. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in shaded, humid environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and greenhouses.
