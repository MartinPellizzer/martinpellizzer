Mussaenda kerrii

Mussaenda kerrii, commonly known as the flame bush, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, spherical fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Mussaenda kerrii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
