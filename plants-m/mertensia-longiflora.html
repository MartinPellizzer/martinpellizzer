Mertensia longiflora

Mertensia longiflora, commonly known as long-flowered bluebells, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped, sky-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, often found in woodland settings.

Mertensia longiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
