Myrcia ticuensis

Myrcia ticuensis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 2 meters. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries. Its leaves are glossy and oval-shaped, with a distinctive aromatic scent.

Myrcia ticuensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
