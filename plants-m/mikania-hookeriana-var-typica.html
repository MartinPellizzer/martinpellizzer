Mikania hookeriana var. typica

Mikania hookeriana var. typica, commonly known as the Hooker's Mikania, is a climbing vine native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its slender stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.

Its rapid growth and invasive potential make it a notable species in ecological studies.
