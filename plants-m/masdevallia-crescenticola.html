Masdevallia crescenticola

Masdevallia crescenticola, commonly known as the crescent-shaped masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous inflorescences and intricate, often colorful floral structures. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, with a pseudobulb that supports its growth in shaded, humid environments. Its leaves are usually elongated and grass-like, adapted to capture moisture from the air.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique and elegant appearance.
