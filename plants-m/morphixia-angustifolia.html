Morphixia angustifolia

Morphixia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf morphixia, is a species of flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, attracting various pollinators. Morphixia angustifolia thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.
