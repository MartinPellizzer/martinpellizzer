Moniera imbricata

Moniera imbricata, commonly known as the overlapping fern, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the family Araceae.

It is characterized by its unique, overlapping leaves that form a dense rosette, giving it a distinctive appearance. Moniera imbricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic foliage.
