Microchloa indica var. gracilis

Microchloa indica var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender microchloa, is a small, perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. It has a dense root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

Microchloa indica var. gracilis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
