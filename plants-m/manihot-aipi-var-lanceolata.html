Manihot aipi var. lanceolata

Manihot aipi var. lanceolata, commonly known as the arrowleaf manioc, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces a toxic latex sap, which must be processed before the starchy root is edible. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both agricultural and medicinal contexts.
