Melastoma acutiflorum

Melastoma acutiflorum, commonly known as the acute-flowered melastoma, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, with five petals and prominent stamens. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dark seeds.

Melastoma acutiflorum is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
