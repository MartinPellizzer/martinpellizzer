Musa monticola

Musa monticola, commonly known as the mountain banana, is a species of banana plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and clusters of elongated, edible fruits. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soil and ample sunlight. Its fruit, while less commonly cultivated than other banana varieties, is valued for its flavor and nutritional content.

Musa monticola plays an important role in local ecosystems and traditional agriculture in its native regions.
