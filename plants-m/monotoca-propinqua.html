Monotoca propinqua

Monotoca propinqua, commonly known as the slender tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and dense clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is adapted to thrive in hot, dry environments with well-drained soils. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water from deep underground.

Monotoca propinqua plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
