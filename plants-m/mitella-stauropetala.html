Mitella stauropetala

Mitella stauropetala, commonly known as the mountain mitella, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows. The plant features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white or pink flowers. Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with moist, well-drained soil.
