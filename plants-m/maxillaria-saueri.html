Maxillaria saueri

Maxillaria saueri, commonly known as the Sauer's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Maxillaria saueri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
