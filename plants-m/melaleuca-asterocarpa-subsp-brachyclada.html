Melaleuca asterocarpa subsp. brachyclada

Melaleuca asterocarpa subsp.

brachyclada, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of eastern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and striking red or pink flower spikes that bloom in spring.

The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal dunes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
