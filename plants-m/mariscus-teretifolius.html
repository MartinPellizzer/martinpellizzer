Mariscus teretifolius

Mariscus teretifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mariscus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Mariscus teretifolius is often found in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its resilience to arid conditions.
