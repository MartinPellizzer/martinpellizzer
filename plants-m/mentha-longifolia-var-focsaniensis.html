Mentha longifolia var. focsaniensis

Mentha longifolia var. focsaniensis, commonly known as Focsa mint, is a variant of the longleaf mint species native to Southeast Europe.

This plant is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. The leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their refreshing and slightly minty scent.

Its unique floral structure and fragrance make it a valuable species in both horticulture and phytotherapy.
