Miconia napoana

Miconia napoana, commonly known as the Napoan miconia, is a species of flowering plant in the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its large, broad leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. It typically grows in humid, lowland forests and thrives in partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
