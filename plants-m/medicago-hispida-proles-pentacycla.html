Medicago hispida proles pentacycla

Medicago hispida proles pentacycla, commonly known as the five-crown clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves with a hairy surface and clusters of five small white or pale pink flowers. The plant is typically found in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. It has a fibrous root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

The five-crown clover is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to colonize disturbed sites.
