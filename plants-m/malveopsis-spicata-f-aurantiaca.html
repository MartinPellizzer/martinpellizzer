Malveopsis spicata f. aurantiaca

Malveopsis spicata f. aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange-flowered mallows, is a striking variant of the Malveopsis genus.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange petals, which contrast beautifully with its green foliage. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and floral arrangements.
