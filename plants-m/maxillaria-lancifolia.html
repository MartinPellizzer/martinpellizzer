Maxillaria lancifolia

Maxillaria lancifolia, commonly known as the lancifoliate orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its linear, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink and purple, bloom during the dry season.

Maxillaria lancifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or warm climates.
