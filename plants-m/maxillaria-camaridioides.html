Maxillaria camaridioides

Maxillaria camaridioides, commonly known as the Orchid of the Andes, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms during the summer months, and its flowers are often clustered in racemes. Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is distinguished by its pseudobulbs and linear leaves.

Maxillaria camaridioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses and tropical gardens.
