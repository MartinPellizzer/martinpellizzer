Mosla pauciflora

Mosla pauciflora, commonly known as Chinese pennywort, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and small, aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Mosla pauciflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
