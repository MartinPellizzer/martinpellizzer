Momordica brevispinosa

Momordica brevispinosa, commonly known as the short-spined bitter gourd, is a tropical vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pale green flowers followed by yellow, spiny fruits that are typically harvested when immature. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates.

Due to its bitter taste, it is often used in traditional Asian cuisines and herbal remedies.
