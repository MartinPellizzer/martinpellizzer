Morus alba var. romana

Morus alba var. romana, commonly known as the Roman mulberry, is a variant of the white mulberry native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically lobed and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as mulberries, which are edible and often consumed by both humans and wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its fruit and historical significance in silk production.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
