Morinda citrifolia var. bracteata

Morinda citrifolia var. bracteata, commonly known as the Indian mulberry or noni, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange to red berries. Its fruit is fleshy and contains a single large seed, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has a long history of use in various cultures.
