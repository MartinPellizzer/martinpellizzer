Mitracarpus depauperatus

Mitracarpus depauperatus, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces slender, pod-like fruits that contain small, edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and edible seeds, it is cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
