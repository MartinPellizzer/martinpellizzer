Mespilus chinensis

Mespilus chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, fleshy fruits that are typically harvested when they are partially decayed, a process known as bletting. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate urban environments and air pollution.
