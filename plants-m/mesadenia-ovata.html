Mesadenia ovata

Mesadenia ovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed mesadenia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its ovate, glossy leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Mesadenia ovata thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
