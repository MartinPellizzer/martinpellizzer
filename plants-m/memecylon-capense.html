Memecylon capense

Memecylon capense, commonly known as the Cape memecylon, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Memecylon capense is often found in rocky outcrops and coastal areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
