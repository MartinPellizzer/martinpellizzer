Myrosma boliviana

Myrosma boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian myrosma, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Bolivia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often with a slight purple hue. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Myrosma boliviana is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
