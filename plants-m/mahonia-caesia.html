Mahonia caesia

Mahonia caesia, commonly known as the evergreen holly or California holly, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It features glossy, dark green leaves with spines along the edges, and it produces yellow flowers in the spring. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its berries are dark blue and attractive to birds, contributing to its ecological value.

Mahonia caesia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
