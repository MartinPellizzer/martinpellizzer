Myristica agusanensis

Myristica agusanensis, commonly known as the Agusan nutmeg, is a species of flowering plant in the Myristicaceae family.

It is native to the Philippines, specifically the island of Mindanao, where it grows in lowland and montane rainforests. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, small white flowers, and distinctive aromatic seeds that are highly valued for their spice-like properties. Myristica agusanensis is a rare and endangered species due to habitat loss and overharvesting.

Its conservation is crucial for maintaining biodiversity in its native ecosystems.
