Malus kirghisorum

Malus kirghisorum, commonly known as the Kirghiz apple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its oval, serrated leaves and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible apples that are typically yellow or red in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for fruit production, it is cultivated in some horticultural settings.
