Malus baccata fructa-maxima

Malus baccata fructa-maxima, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by large, sweet, yellowish fruits in late summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit, which has a crisp texture and mild flavor.

The tree is relatively cold-hardy and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
