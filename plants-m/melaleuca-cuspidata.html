Melaleuca cuspidata

Melaleuca cuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paperbark, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its smooth, flaky bark that peels away in thin layers, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, pale yellow flowers in clusters.

The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
