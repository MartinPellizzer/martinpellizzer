Melissa suaveolens

Melissa suaveolens, commonly known as lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are fragrant and contain essential oils, which contribute to its calming properties.

Melissa suaveolens is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and medicinal uses.
