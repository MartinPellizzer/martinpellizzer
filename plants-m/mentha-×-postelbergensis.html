Mentha × postelbergensis

Mentha × postelbergensis, commonly known as the Cape spearmint, is a hybrid mint species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, square stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers clustered in whorls along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Mentha × postelbergensis is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other members of the mint family.
