Myrcia pyrrhopilodes

Myrcia pyrrhopilodes, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals but toxic to others. It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and stream banks.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it can become a problematic weed in certain regions.
