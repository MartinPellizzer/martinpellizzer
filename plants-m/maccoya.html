Maccoya

Maccoya, commonly known as the ironwood tree, is a tropical hardwood species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly resistant to decay and insect damage. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall with a broad, spreading canopy. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a smooth, dark green surface.

Maccoya flowers are small and clustered, producing fragrant blooms that attract pollinators.
