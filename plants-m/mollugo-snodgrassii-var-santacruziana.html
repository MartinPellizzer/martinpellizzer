Mollugo snodgrassii var. santacruziana

Mollugo snodgrassii var. santacruziana, commonly known as Santa Cruz sandmat, is a low-growing annual plant native to California.

It typically features prostrate stems covered in fine, silky hairs and small, pale green to reddish leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal scrub and dunes.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
