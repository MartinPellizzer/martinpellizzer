Mentha × rubra var. laevifolia

Mentha × rubra var. laevifolia, commonly known as red mint, is a hybrid mint species characterized by its distinctive reddish-purple foliage.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer, and its aromatic leaves contain high concentrations of menthol, giving it a strong minty scent. It is often used in herbal teas and as a culinary herb due to its refreshing flavor.

This variety is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
