Medicago suffruticosa subsp. suffruticosa

Medicago suffruticosa subsp.

suffruticosa, commonly known as yellow sweet clover, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has a robust root system and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. It is a valuable forage plant for livestock due to its high nutritional value.

Additionally, it plays a role in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility.
