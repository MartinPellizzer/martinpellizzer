Marsdenia tenii

Marsdenia tenii, commonly known as the slender Marsdenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and slender stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, and its leaves are typically opposite and lance-shaped. Marsdenia tenii is often found in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

It is also used in traditional medicine in some regions for its purported health benefits.
