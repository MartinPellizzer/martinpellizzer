Malva moschata var. integrifolia

Malva moschata var. integrifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant plant in its native habitat.
