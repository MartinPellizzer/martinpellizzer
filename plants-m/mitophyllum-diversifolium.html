Mitophyllum diversifolium

Mitophyllum diversifolium, commonly known as the diverse-leaved mitophyllum, is a perennial herb native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It features a robust rhizomatous root system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant's leaves are lanceolate to ovate, with a glossy surface and serrated edges, varying in size and shape across different individuals. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in terminal inflorescences, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
