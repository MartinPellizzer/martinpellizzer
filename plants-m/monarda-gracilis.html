Monarda gracilis

Monarda gracilis, commonly known as slender horsemint, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple, typically blooming from June to September. Its leaves are lance-shaped and emit a minty scent when crushed.

Monarda gracilis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
