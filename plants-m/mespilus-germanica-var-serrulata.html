Mespilus germanica var. serrulata

Mespilus germanica var. serrulata, commonly known as the serrated medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves with serrated edges, which contribute to its ornamental value. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in early spring, followed by unique, fleshy fruits that ripen in autumn. The fruit has a complex, aromatic flavor and is often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers a sunny to partially shaded location.
