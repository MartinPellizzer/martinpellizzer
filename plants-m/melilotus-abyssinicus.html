Melilotus abyssinicus

Melilotus abyssinicus, commonly known as Abyssinian sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Melilotus abyssinicus is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.
