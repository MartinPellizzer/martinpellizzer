Monteverdia samydiformis

Monteverdia samydiformis, commonly known as the Samydiform mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees, primarily in the neotropical regions.

It has small, leathery leaves and produces white or pink flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing photosynthesis to some extent. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that eat its berries, contributing to its distribution.

This species plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing food for various wildlife species.
