Miconia leahumilis

Miconia leahumilis, commonly known as the white miconia, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are often surrounded by bracts. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Miconia leahumilis is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.
