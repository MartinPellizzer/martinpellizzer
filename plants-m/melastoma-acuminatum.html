Melastoma acuminatum

Melastoma acuminatum, commonly known as the acute tip melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its clustered, showy flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
