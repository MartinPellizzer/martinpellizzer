Mentha borealis

Mentha borealis, commonly known as Arctic mint, is a perennial herb native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It thrives in cold climates and is often found in alpine tundra and moist, rocky soils. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its small, pale blue or purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Mentha borealis is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
