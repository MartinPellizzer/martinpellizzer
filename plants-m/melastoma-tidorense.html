Melastoma tidorense

Melastoma tidorense, commonly known as the Tidore melastoma, is a flowering plant native to the Indonesian island of Tidore.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, often pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories and has opposite, oval-shaped leaves. It produces small, berry-like fruits that contain several seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Melastoma tidorense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
