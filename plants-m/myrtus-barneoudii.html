Myrtus barneoudii

Myrtus barneoudii, commonly known as the Barneoud's myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains numerous seeds.

Myrtus barneoudii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its historical use in traditional medicine.
