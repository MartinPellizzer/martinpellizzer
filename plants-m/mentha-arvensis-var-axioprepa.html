Mentha arvensis var. axioprepa

Mentha arvensis var. axioprepa, commonly known as wild mint, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, usually pink or purple, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and is used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.
