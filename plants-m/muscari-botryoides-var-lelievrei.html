Muscari botryoides var. lelievrei

Muscari botryoides var. lelievrei, commonly known as the Leliere's grape hyacinth, is a bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It produces dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to violet in color. The plant is characterized by its grass-like foliage and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is notable for its compact growth habit and early spring blooming period.
