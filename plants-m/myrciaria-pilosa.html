Myrciaria pilosa

Myrciaria pilosa, commonly known as the Brazilian blackcurrant, is a shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark purple to black berries. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas.

The berries are rich in antioxidants and are used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
