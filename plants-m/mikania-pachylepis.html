Mikania pachylepis

Mikania pachylepis, commonly known as the hairy Mikania, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces tiny yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, spiny fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain areas.
