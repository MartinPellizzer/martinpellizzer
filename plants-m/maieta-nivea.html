Maieta nivea

Maieta nivea, commonly known as the white mayflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy white blooms. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers appear in early spring, adding a delicate touch to woodland gardens.

Maieta nivea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
