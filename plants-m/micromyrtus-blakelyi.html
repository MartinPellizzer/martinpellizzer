Micromyrtus blakelyi

Micromyrtus blakelyi, commonly known as the Blakely's myrtle, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of Western Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy soils and is adapted to saline and coastal environments. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, with a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

Micromyrtus blakelyi is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
