Miconia abysmophila

Miconia abysmophila, commonly known as the abyssal miconia, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to the high-altitude cloud forests of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking, large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 50 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which are followed by distinctive, fleshy berries. Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered vulnerable to habitat loss and climate change.

Botanists are particularly interested in its unique adaptations to high-altitude environments.
