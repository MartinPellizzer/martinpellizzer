Monophyllaea cupiflora var. aggregata

Monophyllaea cupiflora var. aggregata, commonly known as the clustered leaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are clustered at the base, giving it a compact appearance, and it produces small, showy blooms throughout the summer months.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
