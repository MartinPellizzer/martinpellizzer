Myrcia neobracteata

Myrcia neobracteata, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal regions.

Myrcia neobracteata is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
