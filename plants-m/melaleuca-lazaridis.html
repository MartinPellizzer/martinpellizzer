Melaleuca lazaridis

Melaleuca lazaridis, commonly known as the Lazarus melaleuca, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in winter. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and is drought-tolerant.

Melaleuca lazaridis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
