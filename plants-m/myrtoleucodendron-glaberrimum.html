Myrtoleucodendron glaberrimum

Myrtoleucodendron glaberrimum, commonly known as the glossy-leaved myrtle, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage that is often shiny and leathery in texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom during the warmer months. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Myrtoleucodendron glaberrimum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
