Mentha × schultziana

Mentha × schultziana, commonly known as Schultes mint, is a hybrid mint plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Mentha × schultziana is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for its aromatic and mild mint-like properties.
