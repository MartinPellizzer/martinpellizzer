Miconia solenifera

Miconia solenifera, commonly known as the red miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Miconia solenifera is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in some regions due to its competitive nature and potential to outgrow native vegetation.
