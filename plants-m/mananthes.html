Mananthes

Mananthes, commonly known as the redroot goldenweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and red-hued roots. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and grassy areas. Mananthes has lance-shaped leaves and produces daisy-like blooms that attract pollinators.
