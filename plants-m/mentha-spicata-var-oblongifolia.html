Mentha spicata var. oblongifolia

Mentha spicata var. oblongifolia, commonly known as oblong-leaved mint, is a variant of the spearmint species.

It is characterized by its elongated, oblong-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy and have a serrated edge. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that appear in whorled clusters along the stem. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often used in culinary and aromatic applications due to its distinctive, minty fragrance.
