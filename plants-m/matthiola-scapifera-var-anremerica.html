Matthiola scapifera var. anremerica

Matthiola scapifera var. anremerica, commonly known as the American wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces elongated seed pods that contain numerous small seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
