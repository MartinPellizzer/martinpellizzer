Magnolia denudata var. pyriformis

Magnolia denudata var. pyriformis, commonly known as the egg-shaped magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, egg-shaped buds that open into large, showy white flowers. The leaves are broad, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a glossy surface. This variety is prized for its ornamental value, particularly for its early spring blooms.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
