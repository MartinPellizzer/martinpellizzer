Mesembryanthemum crassuloides

Mesembryanthemum crassuloides, commonly known as the frost plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the early morning, often during the winter months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
