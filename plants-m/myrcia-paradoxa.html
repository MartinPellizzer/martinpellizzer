Myrcia paradoxa

Myrcia paradoxa, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils.

Myrcia paradoxa is also known for its aromatic oils, which have been used in traditional medicine.
