Miconia diguensis

Miconia diguensis, commonly known as the Guianan miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Miconia diguensis is often found in shaded, humid environments and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
