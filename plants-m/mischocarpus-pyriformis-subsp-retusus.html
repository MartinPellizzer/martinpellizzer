Mischocarpus pyriformis subsp. retusus

Mischocarpus pyriformis subsp.

retusus, commonly known as the rough-leafed kauri, is a species native to New Zealand. It is a large evergreen tree characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often asymmetrical leaves.

The leaves are typically rough in texture and have a dark green upper surface with a lighter underside. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits that contain a single seed.

It plays an important ecological role in New Zealand's native forests and is valued for its timber and ornamental qualities.
