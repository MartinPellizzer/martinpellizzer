Mesembryanthemum placitum

Mesembryanthemum placitum, commonly known as the common iceplant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in winter. The plant thrives in well-drained sandy soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments. Its leaves are covered in a fine layer of wax, which helps reduce water loss and reflects sunlight.

Mesembryanthemum placitum is also known for its ability to tolerate salt and is often used in coastal landscaping.
