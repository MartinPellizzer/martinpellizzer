Marrubium astracanicum subsp. astracanicum

Marrubium astracanicum subsp.

astracanicum, commonly known as Astracan marrubium, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves.

The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in the summer months. It is part of the Boraginaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental uses.
