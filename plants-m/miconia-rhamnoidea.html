Miconia rhamnoidea

Miconia rhamnoidea, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom throughout the year in warm climates. Its fruit is a round, fleshy berry that turns from green to red as it matures.

Miconia rhamnoidea is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive in certain regions.
