Mimosa serra

Mimosa serra, commonly known as the Brazilian mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often in response to touch, a trait known as thigmonastic movement. Its roots have nitrogen-fixing abilities, contributing to soil enrichment.

Mimosa serra is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
