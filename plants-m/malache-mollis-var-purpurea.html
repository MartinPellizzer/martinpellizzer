Malache mollis var. purpurea

Malache mollis var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple malache, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves that often exhibit a subtle purple sheen. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate floral display. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for shaded garden areas or as a houseplant.

Its unique foliage and modest flowering make it a popular choice for those seeking an exotic yet low-maintenance plant.
