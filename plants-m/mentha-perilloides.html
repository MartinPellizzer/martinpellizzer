Mentha perilloides

Mentha perilloides, commonly known as narrow-leaved mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and has serrated, lance-shaped leaves that emit a strong mint scent. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or violet, arranged in spikes.

Mentha perilloides is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in herbal remedies.
