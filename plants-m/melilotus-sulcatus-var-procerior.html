Melilotus sulcatus var. procerior

Melilotus sulcatus var. procerior, commonly known as the slender melilot, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its leguminous plants that often form symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is characterized by its slender stems and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.
