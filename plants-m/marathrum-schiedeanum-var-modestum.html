Marathrum schiedeanum var. modestum

Marathrum schiedeanum var. modestum, commonly known as the slender marsh grass, is a perennial herb native to wetland environments in South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically green or pale in color. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ecological importance in stabilizing wetland soils, it plays a vital role in maintaining the health of its habitat.
