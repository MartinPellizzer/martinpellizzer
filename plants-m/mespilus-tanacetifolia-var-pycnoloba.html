Mespilus tanacetifolia var. pycnoloba

Mespilus tanacetifolia var. pycnoloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved whitebeam, is a shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and resilience to environmental stress.
