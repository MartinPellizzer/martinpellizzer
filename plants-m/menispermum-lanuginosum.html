Menispermum lanuginosum

Menispermum lanuginosum, commonly known as the downy moonseed, is a climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its hairy, often reddish stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by orange-red berries that contain toxic seeds. Its botanical features include a distinctive, aromatic root that has been historically used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
