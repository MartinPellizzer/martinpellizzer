Mischocarpus pyriformis

Mischocarpus pyriformis, commonly known as the round-leaved spondias, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, orange-red berries. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests. Its fruits are a valuable food source for both wildlife and humans, and the tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal.

Mischocarpus pyriformis plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
