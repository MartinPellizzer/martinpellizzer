Mertensia chitralensis

Mertensia chitralensis, commonly known as the Chitral bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forested areas or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its stems are erect and can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

Mertensia chitralensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
