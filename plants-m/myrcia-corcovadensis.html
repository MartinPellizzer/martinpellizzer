Myrcia corcovadensis

Myrcia corcovadensis, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces dark purple, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and sometimes invasive in non-native habitats.
