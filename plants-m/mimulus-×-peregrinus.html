Mimulus × peregrinus

Mimulus × peregrinus, commonly known as the hybrid monkey flower, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is a cross between Mimulus guttatus and Mimulus luteus, resulting in a plant with distinctive yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features slender, branching stems covered in hairy foliage. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in streambanks and moist meadows.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
