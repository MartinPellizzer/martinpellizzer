Meyeria subintegerrima

Meyeria subintegerrima, commonly known as the African sandthorn, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange flowers and its ability to thrive in arid and sandy soils. The plant has compound leaves with spiny margins, and its fruits are small, three-lobed capsules that contain several seeds. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The botanical features of Meyeria subintegerrima make it well-adapted to harsh environments and suitable for ecological restoration projects.
