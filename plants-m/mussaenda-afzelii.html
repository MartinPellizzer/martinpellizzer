Mussaenda afzelii

Mussaenda afzelii, commonly known as the golden trumpet bush, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble trumpets, which are clustered at the ends of its branches. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Mussaenda afzelii is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
