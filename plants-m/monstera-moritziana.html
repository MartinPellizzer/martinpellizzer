Monstera moritziana

Monstera moritziana, commonly known as the Swiss cheese plant, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that develop perforations as they mature. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its aerial roots allow it to climb and anchor itself to supports, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

The foliage is not only aesthetically pleasing but also contributes to air purification, making it a valuable addition to any space.
