Mappa capensis

Mappa capensis, commonly known as the Cape mapel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkin-like inflorescences, followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Mappa capensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
