Miconia guaiquinimae subsp. angustifolia

Miconia guaiquinimae subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf miconia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America. It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers, which are followed by round, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
