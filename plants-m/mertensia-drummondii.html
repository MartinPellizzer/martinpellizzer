Mertensia drummondii

Mertensia drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's bluebells, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of bell-shaped, sky-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Mertensia drummondii is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
