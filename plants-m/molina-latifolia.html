Molina latifolia

Molina latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf molina, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it has a moderate growth rate.

Molina latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage.
