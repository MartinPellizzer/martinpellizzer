Mentha × grata

Mentha × grata, commonly known as peppermint, is a hybrid mint species resulting from the cross between Mentha × piperita and Mentha spicata.

It is characterized by its strongly aromatic leaves and stems, which contain high concentrations of menthol. The plant is perennial, growing up to 3 feet tall with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Mentha × grata is widely cultivated for its essential oils used in aromatherapy, medicine, and as a flavoring agent.
