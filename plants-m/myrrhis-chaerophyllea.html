Myrrhis chaerophyllea

Myrrhis chaerophyllea, commonly known as the alpine myrrh, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often tinged with purple or red. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a dense panicle, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Myrrhis chaerophyllea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
