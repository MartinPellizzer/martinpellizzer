Momordica gilgiana

Momordica gilgiana, commonly known as the Gilgian bitter melon, is a vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India and Southeast Asia. The plant produces elongated, bitter fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while the stems are hairy and climbing.

The flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female blooms, typical of the Cucurbitaceae family.
