Mimosa axillarioides

Mimosa axillarioides, commonly known as the Brazilian mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive flowering patterns. The plant produces small, spherical pods that contain several seeds, and it is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Mimosa axillarioides is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and adaptability.
