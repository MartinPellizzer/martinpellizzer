Malaxis brachystachys

Malaxis brachystachys, commonly known as the short-stalked malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its flowers are usually arranged in a loose spike and are pollinated by specific insect species.

Malaxis brachystachys is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
