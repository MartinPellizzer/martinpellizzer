Matelea sintenisii

Matelea sintenisii, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a striking member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is native to the mountainous regions of Mexico and is known for its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant produces pale green, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically ovate with a velvety texture, and the stems are woody and branched.

Matelea sintenisii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal blooming habit.
