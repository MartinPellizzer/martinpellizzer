Machaerium jobimianum

Machaerium jobimianum, commonly known as Jobim's machaerium, is a perennial shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil, making it ecologically significant in its native habitat.
