Mesua grandis

Mesua grandis, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a vibrant and showy appearance. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slightly waxy texture. Mesua grandis thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and public spaces.
