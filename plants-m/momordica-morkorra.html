Momordica morkorra

Momordica morkorra, commonly known as the Morkorra bitter gourd, is a tropical vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its long, pointed, and often ribbed fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or orange when mature. The plant produces yellow flowers and has a climbing habit, with hairy stems and leaves. Its fruits are used in various cuisines, particularly in parts of Africa and Southeast Asia, despite their bitter taste.

The plant is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
