Mascagnia sepium var. velutina

Mascagnia sepium var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some cultures.
