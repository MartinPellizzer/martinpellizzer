Miconia aligera

Miconia aligera, commonly known as the Brazilian miconia, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its dense foliage and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are dispersed by animals.

Miconia aligera is also known for its ability to form dense thickets, which can impact local ecosystems.
