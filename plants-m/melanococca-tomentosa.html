Melanococca tomentosa

Melanococca tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and stems, which give it a distinctive, velvety appearance. The plant produces showy, pale pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Melanococca tomentosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
