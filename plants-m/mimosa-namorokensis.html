Mimosa namorokensis

Mimosa namorokensis, commonly known as the Namorokense sensitive plant, is a species native to the arid regions of Namibia.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched, a trait known as nyctinasty. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a delicate touch to its sparse habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
