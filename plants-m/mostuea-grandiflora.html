Mostuea grandiflora

Mostuea grandiflora, commonly known as the giant spider lily, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with prominent yellow stamens that resemble spider legs, giving it its common name. The plant produces broad, strap-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or near water bodies.

Mostuea grandiflora is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
