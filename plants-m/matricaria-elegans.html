Matricaria elegans

Matricaria elegans, commonly known as the German chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its daisy-like white flowers with a yellow central disk, which bloom in the summer months. The plant grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has finely divided, aromatic leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.

The essential oil extracted from its flowers is widely used in aromatherapy and as a natural remedy for various ailments.
