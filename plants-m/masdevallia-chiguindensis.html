Masdevallia chiguindensis

Masdevallia chiguindensis, commonly known as the Chiguinda orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often colorful flower structures. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions with dappled sunlight. Its flowers are usually small to medium-sized, with intricate patterns and a variety of hues, including purples, blues, and whites.

Due to its limited natural habitat and susceptibility to environmental changes, it is considered a vulnerable species.
