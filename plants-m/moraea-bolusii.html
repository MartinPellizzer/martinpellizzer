Moraea bolusii

Moraea bolusii, commonly known as the Bolus orchid, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, orchid-like flowers that come in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, making it a notable addition to gardens during the cooler months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

Moraea bolusii is well-suited to well-drained soils and full sun, thriving in Mediterranean climates.
