Miconia macrocarpa subsp. macrocarpa

Miconia macrocarpa subsp.

macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant miconia, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length.

The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers arranged in dense inflorescences, which attract a variety of pollinators. Its fruits are large, woody capsules that split open to release numerous seeds.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is considered a problematic species in some regions.
