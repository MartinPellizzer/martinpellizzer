Macrogyne convallariifolia

Macrogyne convallariifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that primarily inhabits the branches of eucalyptus trees in Australia.

It possesses small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs, giving it its characteristic silvery appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are initially green and turn red when ripe, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can weaken its host tree if left unchecked, making it both ecologically significant and sometimes problematic in native forests.
