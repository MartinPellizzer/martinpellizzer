Maxillaria infausta

Maxillaria infausta, commonly known as the black orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark, velvety petals and sepals, which give it its striking appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, blooming in shades of purple and brown.

Maxillaria infausta is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
