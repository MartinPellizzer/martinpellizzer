Monvillea rhodoleucantha

Monvillea rhodoleucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking white petals and a yellow lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are fragrant and bloom during the warmer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its beauty and uniqueness.
