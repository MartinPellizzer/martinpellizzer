Melanthium striatum

Melanthium striatum, commonly known as the striped dog's-tongue, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower spikes in early spring, which are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its flowers are pollinated by wind, and it thrives in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows.

Melanthium striatum is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments and is an important component of native plant communities.
