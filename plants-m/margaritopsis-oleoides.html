Margaritopsis oleoides

Margaritopsis oleoides, commonly known as the golden margarita, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that resemble small margaritas. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and has a compact, shrub-like appearance. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
