Matelea subsessilifolia

Matelea subsessilifolia, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces a milky sap that is toxic. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

The flowers emit a strong fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators like moths.
