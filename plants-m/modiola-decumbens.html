Modiola decumbens

Modiola decumbens, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features prostrate stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across the ground. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
