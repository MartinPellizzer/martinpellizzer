Monadenia basutorum

Monadenia basutorum, commonly known as the Basuto thornveld grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Monadenia basutorum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
