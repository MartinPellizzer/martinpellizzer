Malus domestica var. ponderosus

Malus domestica var. ponderosus, commonly known as the Ponderosa apple.

This variety is characterized by its large, robust fruit with a distinctive, coarse, russet-colored skin. The fruit typically has a sweet and tart flavor, with a firm, crisp texture. It is primarily cultivated for its culinary use in cooking and baking.

The tree is known for its hardiness and adaptability to various climates.
