Myrcia plumbea

Myrcia plumbea, commonly known as the blue myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and showy, pale blue or white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.

Myrcia plumbea is also valued for its potential in pharmaceutical research due to the presence of bioactive compounds.
