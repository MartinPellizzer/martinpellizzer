Moluchia fruticosa

Moluchia fruticosa, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by fleshy, red berries containing a single seed. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
