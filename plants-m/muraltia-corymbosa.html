Muraltia corymbosa

Muraltia corymbosa, commonly known as the sweet acacia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 10 meters. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom throughout the year in some regions. Its leaves are pinnate, consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The seeds are encased in a hard, woody pod, and the plant has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
