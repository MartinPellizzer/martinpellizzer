Myrsine africana var. retusa

Myrsine africana var. retusa, commonly known as the African myrsine, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in cymes, followed by small, dark blue berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
