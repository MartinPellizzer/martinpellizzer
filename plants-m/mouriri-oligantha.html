Mouriri oligantha

Mouriri oligantha, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, star-shaped fruits that resemble miniature pineapples. The plant has compound leaves with a serrated edge and produces small, yellowish flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor, making it a popular choice in local cuisines.
