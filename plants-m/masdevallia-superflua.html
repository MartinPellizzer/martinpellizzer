Masdevallia superflua

Masdevallia superflua, commonly known as the "superfluous masdevallia," is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and clusters of small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, humid conditions and partial shade. Its leaves are usually elongated and strap-like, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and challenging plant to cultivate.
