Mimulus rattanii

Mimulus rattanii, commonly known as the yellow monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in open woodlands. It has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

Mimulus rattanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
