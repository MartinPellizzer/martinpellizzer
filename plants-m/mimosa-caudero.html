Mimosa caudero

Mimosa caudero, commonly known as the curly mimosa, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is well-developed, allowing it to thrive in various soil types and climates.

Mimosa caudero is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
