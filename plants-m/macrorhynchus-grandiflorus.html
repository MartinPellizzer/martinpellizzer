Macrorhynchus grandiflorus

Macrorhynchus grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered macrorhynchus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth, waxy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
