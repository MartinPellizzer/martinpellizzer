Melilotus lippoldianus

Melilotus lippoldianus, commonly known as the Lippold's sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow pea-like flowers. The plant is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, where it can thrive in full sun. It has a taproot system that allows it to access deep soil moisture, making it drought-tolerant.

Additionally, it plays a role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil with nutrients.
