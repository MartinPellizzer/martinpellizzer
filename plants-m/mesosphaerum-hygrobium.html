Mesosphaerum hygrobium

Mesosphaerum hygrobium, commonly known as the swamp sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in marshy or swampy areas, where it thrives in consistently moist to waterlogged soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often greenish or brownish in color. It is part of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.

Mesosphaerum hygrobium plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
