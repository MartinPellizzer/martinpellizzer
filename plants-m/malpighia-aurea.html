Malpighia aurea

Malpighia aurea, commonly known as the golden gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, edible berries that are rich in vitamin C and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Malpighia aurea is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant flowers and is also valued for its nutritional properties.
