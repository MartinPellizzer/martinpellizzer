Mammillaria meridiorosei

Mammillaria meridiorosei, commonly known as the rose-thorned mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, white to pale green tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its gray-green foliage. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice for collectors and succulent enthusiasts.
