Mallotus minahassae

It is native to Indonesia, specifically the island of Sulawesi, where it thrives in tropical rainforest environments. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin. This species is often found in shaded, moist undergrowth and is valued for its ornamental potential and potential medicinal uses.
