Myosotis discolor subsp. dubia

Myosotis discolor subsp.

dubia, commonly known as the alpine forget-me-not, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments.

The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapting to high-altitude conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
