Medicago sativa subvar. vavilovii

Medicago sativa subvar. vavilovii, commonly known as Vavilov's alfalfa, is a subspecies of the alfalfa plant native to the steppes of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit, trifoliate leaves, and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. This subspecies is valued for its high nutritional content and is often used in forage agriculture. It has also been studied for its potential in breeding programs to improve alfalfa varieties.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a resilient plant in agricultural settings.
