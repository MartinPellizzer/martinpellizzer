Melica atrovirens

Melica atrovirens, commonly known as blackbird grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Melica atrovirens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators and soil stabilization.
