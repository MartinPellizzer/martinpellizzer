Myrcia rostrata f. gracilis

Myrcia rostrata f. gracilis, commonly known as the slender myrtle, is a shrub native to the Brazilian Atlantic Forest.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has a compact, bushy growth habit. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.

The variety 'gracilis' is distinguished by its more slender stems and smaller leaves compared to the typical form.
