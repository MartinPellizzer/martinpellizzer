Mentha × rotundifolia var. semeiodes

Mentha × rotundifolia var. semeiodes, commonly known as the round-leaf mint, is a hybrid mint species characterized by its rounded, oval-shaped leaves.

It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its use in culinary and medicinal applications.

This variety is valued for its distinct fragrance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
