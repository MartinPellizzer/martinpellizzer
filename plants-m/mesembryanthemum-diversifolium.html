Mesembryanthemum diversifolium

Mesembryanthemum diversifolium, commonly known as the Variable Leaf Ice Plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, often hairy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the warmer months. Its leaves are usually lobed or divided, showing significant variation in shape and size, which contributes to its common name.

Mesembryanthemum diversifolium is popular in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
