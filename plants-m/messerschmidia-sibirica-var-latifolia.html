Messerschmidia sibirica var. latifolia

Messerschmidia sibirica var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved Siberian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The leaves are broad and lanceolate, often covered in fine hairs, and the plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is a perennial species that blooms in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
