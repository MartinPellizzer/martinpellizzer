Medicago falcata var. ambigua

Medicago falcata var. ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous sickle vetch, is a variant of the sickle vetch species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its slender, branching stems and trifoliate leaves with narrow, sickle-shaped leaflets. The flowers are typically yellow and arranged in racemes, producing small, legume-like pods that contain several seeds. This plant is adapted to a variety of soil types but thrives in well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
