Mosla dianthera

Mosla dianthera, commonly known as Chinese chives or garlic chives, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, grass-like leaves that emit a garlic or onion scent. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is often cultivated for both its edible leaves and ornamental value.

Mosla dianthera thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
