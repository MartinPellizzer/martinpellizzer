Millingtonia quadripinnata

Millingtonia quadripinnata, commonly known as the satinwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite and deeply lobed. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elliptical, woody fruits. Its bark is smooth and light brown, often exuding a milky sap when wounded.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
