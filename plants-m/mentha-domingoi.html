Mentha domingoi

Mentha domingoi, commonly known as Mexican mint, is a perennial herb native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their fragrant leaves and flowers. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are typically purple or blue, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Mentha domingoi is valued for its fresh, minty scent and has been used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
