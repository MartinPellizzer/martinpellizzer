Macaranga longicaudata

Macaranga longicaudata, commonly known as the long-tailed paper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. The leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber in some regions.
