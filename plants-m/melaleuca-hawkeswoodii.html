Melaleuca hawkeswoodii

Melaleuca hawkeswoodii, commonly known as the Hawkeswood paperbark, is a species of melaleuca native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a pale, fibrous layer underneath. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom during the winter months. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough, serrated margin.

This species is often found in sandy soils along coastal areas and is adapted to tolerate saline conditions.
