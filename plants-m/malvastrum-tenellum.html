Malvastrum tenellum

Malvastrum tenellum, commonly known as the common mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features woolly, hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers with five petals, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Malvastrum tenellum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
