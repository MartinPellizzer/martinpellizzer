Muraltia virgata

Muraltia virgata, commonly known as the red muraltia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, round, edible fruits that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
