Mirbelia spinosa f. borealis

Mirbelia spinosa f. borealis, commonly known as the northern spinose mirbelia, is a variant of the spinose mirbelia native to Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in open habitats such as woodlands and heathlands.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native range.
