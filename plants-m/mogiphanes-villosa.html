Mogiphanes villosa

Mogiphanes villosa, commonly known as the hairy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often found in swampy areas and along riverbanks. The plant features slender, grass-like stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it its common name. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually arranged in dense clusters, and it produces triangular, three-angled seed capsules.

Mogiphanes villosa plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and provides habitat for various small aquatic organisms.
