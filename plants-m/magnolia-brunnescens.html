Magnolia brunnescens

Magnolia brunnescens, commonly known as the brown magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate to elliptical leaves that are dark green and turn yellow in autumn. Its bark is smooth and light brown, becoming fissured with age.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
