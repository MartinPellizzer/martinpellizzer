Mesembryanthemum mucronulatum

Mesembryanthemum mucronulatum, commonly known as the sharp-tipped ice plant, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the cooler months. It is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.

Its unique morphology and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for Mediterranean and coastal climates.
