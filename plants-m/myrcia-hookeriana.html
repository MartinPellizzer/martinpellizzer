Myrcia hookeriana

Myrcia hookeriana, commonly known as the Hooker's myrtle, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. It produces small, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

Myrcia hookeriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
