Mimosa savokaea

Mimosa savokaea, commonly known as the golden mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through its root nodules. Mimosa savokaea thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its rapid growth and seasonal blooming make it both visually appealing and ecologically beneficial.
