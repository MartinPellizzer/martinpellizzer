Medinilla atroviridis

Medinilla atroviridis, commonly known as the black-veined medinilla, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with prominent black veins, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom throughout the year in warm climates. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Medinilla atroviridis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in ornamental gardening and as a houseplant.
