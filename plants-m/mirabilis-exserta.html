Mirabilis exserta

Mirabilis exserta, commonly known as the red-flowered four-o'clock, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that open in the late afternoon and remain open through the night. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features slender, branching stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

The flowers come in shades of red, pink, and white, making it a popular ornamental plant.
