Mimosa quadrivalvis var. leptocarpa

Mimosa quadrivalvis var. leptocarpa, commonly known as the slender-stemmed sensitive plant, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring and summer. Its stems are slender and often hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils in temperate climates.

The species is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
