Montrichardia linifera

Montrichardia linifera, commonly known as the thread-leaf arrowhead, is a tropical aquatic plant native to South America.

It features long, narrow, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water's surface, with roots hanging below. The plant has small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically submerged, and it thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. Montrichardia linifera is often used in ornamental ponds and water gardens due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to improve water quality.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various microorganisms and small aquatic life.
