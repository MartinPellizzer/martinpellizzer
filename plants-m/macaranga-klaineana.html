Macaranga klaineana

Macaranga klaineana, commonly known as the white-veined macaranga, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a fast-growing tree or shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent white veins. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.
