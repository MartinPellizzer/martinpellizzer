Mertensia congesta

Mertensia congesta, commonly known as the clustered bluebells, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped, sky-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are toothed along the edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Mertensia congesta is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
