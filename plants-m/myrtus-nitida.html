Myrtus nitida

Myrtus nitida, commonly known as Portuguese laurel, is a dense, evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, oval, dark green leaves that are glossy and often have a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, which are followed by dark blue, berry-like fruits. Myrtus nitida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in hedges or as a specimen plant.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
