Mostuea schumanniana

Mostuea schumanniana, commonly known as the Schumann's marsh orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in marshy or swampy areas, preferring moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Mostuea schumanniana plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing nectar for various pollinators.
