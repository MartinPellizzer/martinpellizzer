Mentha duftschmidii

Mentha duftschmidii, commonly known as the Alpine mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a strong, minty fragrance.

Mentha duftschmidii is often used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and soothing properties.
