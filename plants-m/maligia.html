Maligia

Maligia, commonly known as the sacred lotus of Southeast Asia, is a tropical aquatic plant with striking, showy flowers that bloom in a variety of colors.

It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds, marshes, and along the edges of rivers. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, while its roots anchor in the muddy substrate. Maligia is notable for its symbolic significance in various cultures, representing purity and spiritual enlightenment.

Its botanical classification places it in the family Nymphaeaceae, which includes other water lilies.
