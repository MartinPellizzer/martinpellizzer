Medinilla nodiflora

Medinilla nodiflora, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or Christmas bell, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in winter. The plant produces small, round, orange fruits resembling tangerines, which are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and flowers.

Medinilla nodiflora is also valued for its ability to produce fruit even in shaded conditions.
