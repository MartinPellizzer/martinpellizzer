Mallococca

Mallococca, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom on long, buoyant stalks rising from the water's surface. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water, while its roots anchor it to the muddy bottom. Mallococca is known for its symbolic significance in various cultures, often representing purity and spiritual enlightenment.

Botanically, it belongs to the Nymphaeaceae family and is adapted to thrive in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.
