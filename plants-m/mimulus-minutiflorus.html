Mimulus minutiflorus

Mimulus minutiflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered monkeyflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the western United States.

It features delicate, spurred flowers that are typically orange or yellow in color, with a unique, elongated nectar spur. The plant has finely toothed, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in streambanks and woodland edges.

Mimulus minutiflorus plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
