Matthiola humilis var. ecornuta

Matthiola humilis var. ecornuta, commonly known as the horned wallflower, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often with a distinctive horn-like protrusion on the petals. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. It is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant due to its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
