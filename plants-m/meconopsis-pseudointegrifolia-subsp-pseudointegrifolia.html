Meconopsis pseudointegrifolia subsp. pseudointegrifolia

Meconopsis pseudointegrifolia subsp.

pseudointegrifolia, commonly known as the Himalayan blue poppy, is a striking alpine plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia. It is characterized by its vivid blue or violet flowers, which bloom in spring and are surrounded by broad, heart-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or meadow habitats at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with specific growing conditions.
