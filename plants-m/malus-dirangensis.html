Malus dirangensis

Malus dirangensis, commonly known as the Dirang apple, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the eastern Himalayas, specifically found in regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan. The plant is characterized by its small, ornamental flowers and edible, tart fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in temperate climates and is typically found at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

The tree has a relatively short lifespan and is considered a rare and lesser-known species within the Malus genus.
