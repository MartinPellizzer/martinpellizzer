Mareya

Mareya, commonly known as the "moonflower" or "night bloomer," is a tropical plant belonging to the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night and emit a strong, sweet fragrance. Its leaves are ovate and glossy, while its stems are sturdy and often branched.

Mareya is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
