Maxillaria stumpflei

Maxillaria stumpflei, commonly known as the Stumpf's maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically blooms in the summer, showcasing blossoms that range in color from white to pale pink. Its leaves are usually broad and grass-like, arranged in a rosette pattern around the pseudobulbs.

Maxillaria stumpflei is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
