Masdevallia invenusta

Masdevallia invenusta, commonly known as the golden tassel orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, golden-yellow inflorescences that resemble tassels, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its flowers are relatively small but numerous, arranged in dense clusters along a slender stem.

Masdevallia invenusta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
