Melaleuca bonatiana

Melaleuca bonatiana, commonly known as the Bonati's tea tree, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and semi-arid regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
