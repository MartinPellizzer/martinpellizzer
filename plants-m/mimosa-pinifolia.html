Mimosa pinifolia

Mimosa pinifolia, commonly known as the needleleaf mimosa, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are composed of small, needle-like leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by elongated, bean-like pods. It is well adapted to dry conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Mimosa pinifolia is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
