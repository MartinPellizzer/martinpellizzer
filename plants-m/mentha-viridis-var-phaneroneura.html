Mentha viridis var. phaneroneura

Mentha viridis var. phaneroneura, commonly known as the variable spearmint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green, with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in whorled clusters along the stems. It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and use in culinary and medicinal applications.

This variety is distinguished by its genetic variability, which can result in differences in leaf shape and fragrance compared to other Mentha species.
