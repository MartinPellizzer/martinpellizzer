Melissa acinoides

Melissa acinoides, commonly known as lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, oval-shaped leaves that emit a citrus-like aroma. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Melissa acinoides is often used in herbal medicine for its calming properties and is a popular plant in gardens for its ornamental and aromatic value.
