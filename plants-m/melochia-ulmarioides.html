Melochia ulmarioides

Melochia ulmarioides, commonly known as the false elm, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, followed by fluffy, cotton-like seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and showy blooms.
