Myrobalanifera

Myrobalanifera, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the genus Phyllanthus in the family Phyllanthaceae. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit, which is a bright orange berry, is rich in vitamin C and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

The botanical features of Myrobalanifera make it an important plant in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
