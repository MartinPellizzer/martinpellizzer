Melastoma octandriflorum

Melastoma octandriflorum, commonly known as the six-flowered melastoma, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, with five petals and a distinctive calyx. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.
