Meconopsis elongata

Meconopsis elongata, commonly known as the longleaf poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded or partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Meconopsis elongata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
