Maxillaria fuscata var. huebschii

Maxillaria fuscata var. huebschii, commonly known as the Huesch's dark orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark, velvety flowers that range in color from deep purple to black, with intricate patterns of lighter markings. The plant typically grows as a pseudobulbous orchid, with thick, fleshy stems that store water, adapting it to its often dry or shaded habitats. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette, and it produces clusters of fragrant blooms during the warmer months.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
