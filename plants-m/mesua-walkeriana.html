Mesua walkeriana

Mesua walkeriana, commonly known as the pink flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, pinkish-red bracts that resemble flames, which appear in clusters at the ends of branches. The plant has simple, opposite, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Mesua walkeriana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can grow up to 20 meters tall in its natural habitat.
