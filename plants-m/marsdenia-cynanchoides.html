Marsdenia cynanchoides

Marsdenia cynanchoides, commonly known as the Chinese devil's backbone, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, serpentine stems that resemble a backbone. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by follicles containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
