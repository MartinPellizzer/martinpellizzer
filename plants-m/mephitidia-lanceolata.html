Mephitidia lanceolata

Mephitidia lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate mephit, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Mephitidia lanceolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
