Miconia herpetica var. acutifolia

Miconia herpetica var. acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in some regions due to its aggressive spread and ecological impact.
