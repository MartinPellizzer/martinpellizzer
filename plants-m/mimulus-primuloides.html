Mimulus primuloides

Mimulus primuloides, commonly known as the yellow monkeyflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features bright yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats, including stream banks and woodland areas. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft texture.

Mimulus primuloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
