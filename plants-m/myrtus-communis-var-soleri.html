Myrtus communis var. soleri

Myrtus communis var. soleri, commonly known as the coastal myrtle, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, dark green leaves that are glossy and aromatic, with a subtle myrtle scent. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in maritime climates.
