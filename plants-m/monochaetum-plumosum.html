Monochaetum plumosum

Monochaetum plumosum, commonly known as the feathered spike grass, is a striking ornamental plant native to the coastal regions of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of long, feathery inflorescences, which give it a distinctive and elegant appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that are often arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens and rockeries.

Due to its unique foliage and flowering habit, it is a popular choice for adding texture and visual interest to landscaping designs.
