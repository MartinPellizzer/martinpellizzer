Mikania sarcodes

Mikania sarcodes, commonly known as the "Mexican devil's ivy," is a climbing vine native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and trailing stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Mikania sarcodes is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
