Micromyrtus patula

Micromyrtus patula, commonly known as the slender myrtle, is a small shrub native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes and heathlands.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
