Malaxis radicicola

Malaxis radicicola, commonly known as the underground malaxis, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant has a unique growth habit, with its roots often growing underground, making it difficult to detect. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires specific soil conditions to flourish.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern in many areas.
