Mimulus aurantiacus var. puniceus

Mimulus aurantiacus var. puniceus, commonly known as the scarlet monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant red-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and woodland edges. It has heart-shaped leaves and is part of the Physaleae tribe within the Plantaginaceae family.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
