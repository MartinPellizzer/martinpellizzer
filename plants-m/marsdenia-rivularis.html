Marsdenia rivularis

Marsdenia rivularis, commonly known as the river vine, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its slender stems and opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often followed by orange-red berries. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is often found growing along riverbanks and in moist forests.

The plant has a fibrous root system and is adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
