Maianthemum wardii

Maianthemum wardii, commonly known as the Ward's false lily of the valley, is a low-growing evergreen perennial belonging to the family Convallariaceae.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has dark green, strap-like leaves that form a rosette at the base. Native to the southeastern United States, it thrives in moist, shaded woodlands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for woodland gardens and shaded landscapes.
