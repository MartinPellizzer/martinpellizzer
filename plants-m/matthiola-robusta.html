Matthiola robusta

Matthiola robusta, commonly known as the robust stock, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, four-petaled flowers that come in a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters.

Matthiola robusta is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
