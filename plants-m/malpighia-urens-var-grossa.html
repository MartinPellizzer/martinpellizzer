Malpighia urens var. grossa

Malpighia urens var. grossa, commonly known as the Brazilian passionfruit, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It produces small, round fruits with a thick, edible pulp that contains numerous small, seed-like structures. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of bright red flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and is valued for its unique flavor and nutritional content.
