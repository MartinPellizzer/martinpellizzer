Monanthotaxis angustifolia

Monanthotaxis angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf monanthotaxis, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, elongated leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
