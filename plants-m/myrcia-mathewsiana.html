Myrcia mathewsiana

Myrcia mathewsiana, commonly known as the Mathews's myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the subtropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. It typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

Myrcia mathewsiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its pleasant scent and visual interest.
