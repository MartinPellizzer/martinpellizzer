Mentha × piperita var. braousiana

Mentha × piperita var. braousiana, commonly known as braousian mint, is a hybrid mint species characterized by its aromatic foliage and compact growth habit.

It possesses narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a serrated margin and a strong, menthol-like fragrance. The plant typically produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or pink, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of essential oils.
