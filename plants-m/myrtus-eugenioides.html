Myrtus eugenioides

Myrtus eugenioides, commonly known as the common myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dark green, glossy leaves that are aromatic when crushed, and it produces small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring. The plant is known for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal. Its berries, which are usually red or black, are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Myrtus eugenioides is also valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional remedies for various ailments.
