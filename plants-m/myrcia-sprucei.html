Myrcia sprucei

Myrcia sprucei, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Myrcia sprucei is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses in indigenous cultures.
