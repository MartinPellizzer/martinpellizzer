Miconia pteroclada

Miconia pteroclada, commonly known as the broadleaf miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by distinctive, palmate fruits. Miconia pteroclada is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its invasive potential, it is considered a problematic species in some regions.
