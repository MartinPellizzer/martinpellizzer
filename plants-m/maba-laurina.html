Maba laurina

Maba laurina, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, fleshy berries.

Maba laurina is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its timber and medicinal properties.
