Metagonia penduliflora

Metagonia penduliflora, commonly known as the hanging-flower plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous inflorescences, which consist of small, clustered flowers that hang from the stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth surface, while its flowers are usually white or pale pink.

Metagonia penduliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, hanging blooms.
