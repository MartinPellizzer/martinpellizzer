Myrtoleucodendron diosmifolium

Myrtoleucodendron diosmifolium, commonly known as the white-leaved myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically white or pale green in color. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually white or pale pink and bloom in clusters.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
