Myrcia bicarinata

Myrcia bicarinata, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a variety of habitats including forests and disturbed soils.

Myrcia bicarinata is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
