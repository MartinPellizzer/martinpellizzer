Myrcia latifolia

Myrcia latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. It typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters.

Myrcia latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
