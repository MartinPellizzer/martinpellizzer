Melilotoides badachschanica

Melilotoides badachschanica, commonly known as the Badakhshan melilot, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or white, and it produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
