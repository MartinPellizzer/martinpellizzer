Micromeria origanifolia subsp. bulgarica

Micromeria origanifolia subsp.

bulgarica, commonly known as the Balkan oregano, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are small, narrow, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

The flowers are small, clustered in loose spikes, and have a distinct aromatic scent, similar to oregano.
