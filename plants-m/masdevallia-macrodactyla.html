Masdevallia macrodactyla

Masdevallia macrodactyla, commonly known as the giant finger orchid, is a striking orchid species native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in length, with intricate patterns of purple and white. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are elongated and often covered in a dense layer of fine, hairy structures.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat requirements, it is a popular choice for experienced orchid growers.
