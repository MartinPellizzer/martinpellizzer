Marrubium polyodon

Marrubium polyodon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved horehound, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and sunny habitats. The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that have a distinctive grayish-white coating. Its small, blue or purple flowers are arranged in dense clusters, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Marrubium polyodon is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
