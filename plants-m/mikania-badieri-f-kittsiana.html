Mikania badieri f. kittsiana

Mikania badieri f. kittsiana, commonly known as the Kittsiana variant of the creeping mikania, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and trailing habit. The plant features small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often accompanied by finely divided, feathery leaves. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in certain regions, making it both ecologically significant and potentially problematic.
