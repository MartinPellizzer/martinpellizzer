Malus josephinea

Malus josephinea, commonly known as the Josephine apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe, particularly in the Carpathian Mountains. The plant is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy white flowers in spring and red or yellow berries in autumn. It typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

The leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn vibrant colors in the fall, adding to its aesthetic appeal.
