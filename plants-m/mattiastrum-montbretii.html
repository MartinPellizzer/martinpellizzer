Mattiastrum montbretii

Mattiastrum montbretii, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are oval to lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and it produces bright yellow flowers in clusters during the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
