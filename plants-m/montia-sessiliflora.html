Montia sessiliflora

Montia sessiliflora, commonly known as the sessileleaf sandweed, is a low-growing annual plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Montiaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are sessile, meaning they are attached directly to the stem without a stalk, and the flowers are usually white or pale blue.

Montia sessiliflora plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to harsh environments.
