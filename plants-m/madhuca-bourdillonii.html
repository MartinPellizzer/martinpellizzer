Madhuca bourdillonii

Madhuca bourdillonii, commonly known as the Malabar nut or Indian chestnut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The tree bears edible, round, yellowish fruits that contain a single large seed. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for sustainable use, it is also of interest in horticulture and conservation efforts.
