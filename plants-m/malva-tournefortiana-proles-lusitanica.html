Malva tournefortiana proles lusitanica

Malva tournefortiana proles lusitanica, commonly known as the Lusitanian mallows, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton plants. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy pink or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
