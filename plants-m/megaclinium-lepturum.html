Megaclinium lepturum

Megaclinium lepturum, commonly known as the slender spikegrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as rocky slopes and meadows.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
