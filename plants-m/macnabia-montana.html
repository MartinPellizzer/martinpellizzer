Macnabia montana

Macnabia montana, commonly known as the mountain macnabia, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in the spring and early summer. Its leaves are usually narrow and leathery, adapted to withstand the cool, moist conditions of its mountainous habitat.

Macnabia montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with similar environmental requirements.
