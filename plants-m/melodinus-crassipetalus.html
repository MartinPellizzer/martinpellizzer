Melodinus crassipetalus

Melodinus crassipetalus, commonly known as the broad-petaled melodic vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces a sweet, fragrant scent that attracts pollinators such as moths and butterflies. Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural displays.

Melodinus crassipetalus thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
