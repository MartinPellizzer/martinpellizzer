Masdevallia robineae

Masdevallia robineae, commonly known as the Robineae masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, often colorful flower structure with elongated petals and a distinctive, spurred lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its botanical features include a sympodial growth habit and a variety of leaf shapes depending on the specific habitat.

Masdevallia robineae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
