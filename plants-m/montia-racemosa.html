Montia racemosa

Montia racemosa, commonly known as the clustered filmy fern, is a low-growing annual herb native to western North America.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in racemose inflorescences, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique and often overlooked member of the Montiaceae family.
