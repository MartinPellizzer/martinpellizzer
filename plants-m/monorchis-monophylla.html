Monorchis monophylla

Monorchis monophylla, commonly known as the single-leaf orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its singular, broad leaf and its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it relies on specific symbiotic relationships with fungi. Its botanical features include a short, rhizomatous stem and small, lance-shaped leaves.
