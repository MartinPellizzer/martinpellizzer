Manihot gracilis var. tenuifolia

Manihot gracilis var. tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender arrowleaf manioc, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded understory environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and traditional contexts.
