Miconia ionopogon

Miconia ionopogon, commonly known as the red miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. These berries are an important food source for various birds and mammals in its native habitat.

Miconia ionopogon is also known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, which can sometimes lead to ecological concerns.
