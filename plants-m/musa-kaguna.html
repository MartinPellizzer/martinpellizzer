Musa kaguna

Musa kaguna, commonly known as the wild banana, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant with a pseudostem formed by the tightly packed leaves. The plant produces large, elongated berries that are typically green when mature. Its leaves are broad and have a waxy texture, which helps in reducing water loss.

Musa kaguna is often cultivated for its edible fruits and has some ornamental value due to its striking appearance.
