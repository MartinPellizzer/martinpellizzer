Myrcia daphnoides

Myrcia daphnoides, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its fragrant flowers, which are typically white or pale pink, appear in clusters and attract various pollinators.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
