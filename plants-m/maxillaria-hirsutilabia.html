Maxillaria hirsutilabia

Maxillaria hirsutilabia, commonly known as the hairy-lipped maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and produces striking, fragrant flowers with a unique coloration. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive hairy lip that gives it its common name.

Maxillaria hirsutilabia is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
