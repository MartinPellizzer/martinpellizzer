Mussaenda grandifolia

Mussaenda grandifolia, commonly known as the golden trumpet bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, orange-yellow inflorescences that resemble trumpets, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape. Its stems are typically green or reddish and can be somewhat woody.

Mussaenda grandifolia thrives in humid, sunny environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its vibrant floral display.
