Mendoncia villosa

Mendoncia villosa, commonly known as the hairy mendoncia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically yellow or white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
