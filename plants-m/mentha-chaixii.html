Mentha chaixii

Mentha chaixii, commonly known as Chinese mint or wild mint, is a perennial herb native to Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their cooling and antiseptic properties.

Mentha chaixii produces small, pale blue or white flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
