Metrosideros polymorpha f. humilis

Metrosideros polymorpha f. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf redwood, is a low-growing shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bright red flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in coastal areas and rainforests. Its compact form and vibrant floral display make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in Hawaii's flora.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
