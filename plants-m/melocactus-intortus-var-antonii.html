Melocactus intortus var. antonii

Melocactus intortus var. antonii, commonly known as the twisted melon cactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiraled stems that twist and curl as it grows, forming a compact, globular shape. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers during the spring and summer months, which are borne on short, branched pedicels. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in succulent gardens due to its striking appearance.
