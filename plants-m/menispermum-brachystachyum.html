Menispermum brachystachyum

Menispermum brachystachyum, commonly known as the short-stalked arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and palmate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in umbels. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains several seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
