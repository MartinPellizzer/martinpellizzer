Masdevallia rodrigueziana

Masdevallia rodrigueziana, commonly known as the Rodriguezian orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with intricate, often colorful flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in rocky or forested areas. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
