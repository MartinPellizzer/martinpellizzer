Madhuca decipiens

Madhuca decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive madhuca, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and smooth, greyish bark. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by oval-shaped fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
