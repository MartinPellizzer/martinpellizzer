Malus pashia

Malus pashia, commonly known as the Kashmir apple or Himalayan crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, yellow or red fruits that are edible but typically not cultivated for commercial use. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Malus pashia is often grown for its aesthetic value in gardens and for its ecological benefits to wildlife.
