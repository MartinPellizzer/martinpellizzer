Melandrium apricum subsp. oldhamianum

Melandrium apricum subsp.

oldhamianum, commonly known as the oldham's catchfly, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
