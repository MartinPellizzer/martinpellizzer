Mohria caffrorum var. ferruginea

Mohria caffrorum var. ferruginea, commonly known as the red-stemmed mohria, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its reddish-brown stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in grassland habitats and is often found in sandy or rocky soils. It has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it prefers well-drained conditions.
