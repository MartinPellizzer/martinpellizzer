Myristica smithii

Myristica smithii, commonly known as the Malabar nut or Smith's nutmeg, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, oily seeds that resemble nutmeg. The plant produces yellowish flowers and elliptical, leathery leaves that are alternate in arrangement. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed that is highly valued for its aromatic properties.

The tree is often cultivated for its medicinal and culinary uses, particularly in traditional Ayurvedic practices.
