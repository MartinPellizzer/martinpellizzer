Miconia chaetocalyx

Miconia chaetocalyx, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types make it an invasive species in many regions.

Due to its ecological impact, it is often considered a problematic weed in agricultural and natural areas.
