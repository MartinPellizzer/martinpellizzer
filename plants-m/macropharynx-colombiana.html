Macropharynx colombiana

Macropharynx colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian giant pharynx, is a unique plant species native to the tropical regions of Colombia.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy leaves that can grow up to several feet in length, adapted to store water in its native arid environment. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor firmly in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered an endangered species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts in its native range.
