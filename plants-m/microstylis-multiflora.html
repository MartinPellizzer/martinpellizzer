Microstylis multiflora

Microstylis multiflora, commonly known as the clustered spike-rush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.

Microstylis multiflora plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
