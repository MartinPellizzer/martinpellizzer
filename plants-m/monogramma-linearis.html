Monogramma linearis

Monogramma linearis, commonly known as the slender maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically linear in shape. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its elegant appearance.
