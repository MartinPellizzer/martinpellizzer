Misodendrum imbricatum

Misodendrum imbricatum, commonly known as the creeping sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has fleshy, succulent leaves that help it conserve water in dry environments. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor itself in loose, sandy soils.

Misodendrum imbricatum is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
