Medinilla emarginata

Medinilla emarginata, commonly known as the velvet tangerine.

It is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red fruits that resemble tiny tangerines, which are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowers.

Medinilla emarginata is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for gardeners in tropical and subtropical regions.
