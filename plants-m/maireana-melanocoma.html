Maireana melanocoma

Maireana melanocoma, commonly known as the black-leaved maireana, is a perennial shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are typically oval in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for low-maintenance gardens.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
