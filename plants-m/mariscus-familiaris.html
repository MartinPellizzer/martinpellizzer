Mariscus familiaris

Mariscus familiaris, commonly known as the common mariscus, is a small, flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers in the summer months. Its leaves are simple, lance-shaped, and arranged alternately along the stem.

Mariscus familiaris is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
