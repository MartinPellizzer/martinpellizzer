Mammillaria rhodeocentra

Mammillaria rhodeocentra, commonly known as the rhodochentra mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is covered in white to yellowish spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
