Mikania resinosa

Mikania resinosa, commonly known as the stinging vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and trailing stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture. Mikania resinosa is known for its stinging hairs, which can cause skin irritation upon contact.

It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.
