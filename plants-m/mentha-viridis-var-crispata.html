Mentha viridis var. crispata

Mentha viridis var. crispata, commonly known as curled mint, is a variant of the green mint species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its serrated, wavy leaves that give it a distinctive, curled appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It produces small, pale blue or white flowers in late spring to early summer.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its use in herbal teas and culinary applications.
