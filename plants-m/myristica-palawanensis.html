Myristica palawanensis

Myristica palawanensis, commonly known as the Palawan nutmeg, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody fruits that contain a valuable seed kernel. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and is adapted to humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a glossy surface, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

The species is of interest for its potential economic and medicinal value.
