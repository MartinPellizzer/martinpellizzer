Myrmecosicyos messorius

Myrmecosicyos messorius, commonly known as the ant plant, is a unique climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its specialized structures called domatia, which are hollow, swollen nodes that provide shelter for ants. These ants, in turn, protect the plant from herbivores and help in nutrient cycling by tending to the plant's roots. The plant's leaves are simple and alternate, with a distinctive waxy coating that aids in water retention.

Its ecological significance lies in its mutualistic relationship with ants, making it an important species in its native ecosystems.
