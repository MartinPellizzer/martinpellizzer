Muraltia vulnerans

Muraltia vulnerans, commonly known as the bleeding elder, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is known for its reddish exudate that oozes from damaged stems, giving it its common name. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in shrublands and woodlands.

The species has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
