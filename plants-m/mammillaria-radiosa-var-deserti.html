Mammillaria radiosa var. deserti

Mammillaria radiosa var. deserti, commonly known as the Desert Hedgehog Cactus, is a succulent native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white, woolly hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, requiring minimal water to survive.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
