Malaxis javesiae

Malaxis javesiae, commonly known as the Javas orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forests and mountainous regions. Its roots are mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient uptake.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
