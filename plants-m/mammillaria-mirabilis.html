Mammillaria mirabilis

Mammillaria mirabilis, commonly known as the miracle milk barrel, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and dense covering of white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
