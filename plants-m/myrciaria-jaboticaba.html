Myrciaria jaboticaba

Myrciaria jaboticaba, commonly known as the Brazilian grapefruit or jaboticaba, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black, fleshy fruit that resembles a grape, with a sweet and slightly tart flavor. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by the edible fruit. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy green surface with a slightly serrated edge.

The plant is well adapted to humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.
