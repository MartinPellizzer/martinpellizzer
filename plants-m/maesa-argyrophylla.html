Maesa argyrophylla

Maesa argyrophylla, commonly known as silverleaf maesa, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant has small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a leathery texture.

Maesa argyrophylla is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
