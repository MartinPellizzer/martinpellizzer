Microlepia papillosa

Microlepia papillosa, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, pinnate fronds with small, hairy structures on the underside. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce both sterile and fertile fronds.

The spores are released from the sori, which are located on the underside of the fertile leaves.
