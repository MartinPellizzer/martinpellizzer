Melanocarpum sprucei

Melanocarpum sprucei, commonly known as the Spruce Blackcap, is a rare and distinctive member of the Rhamnaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, fruiting bodies that are typically found on the bark of coniferous trees, particularly spruces. The plant is predominantly epiphytic, growing on tree trunks and branches without deriving nutrients from the host. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-yellow, and it produces unique, elongated, dark-colored fruits.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in certain forest ecosystems.
