Malaxis linguella

Malaxis linguella, commonly known as the tongue orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its roots form a symbiotic relationship with mycorrhizal fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
