Megapterium argyrophyllum

Megapterium argyrophyllum, commonly known as silverleaf tree or silverleaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which results from dense covering of fine, white hairs on the leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by flat, leathery pods containing several seeds. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in poor soils, making it a common pioneer species in disturbed or degraded landscapes.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both ecologically significant and sometimes invasive in certain regions.
