Macrochloa tenacissima subsp. gabesensis

Macrochloa tenacissima subsp.

gabesensis, commonly known as the Gabesensis grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green coloration.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and resilience to poor soil conditions.

This subspecies is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soils.
