Maesa parksii

Maesa parksii, commonly known as the African snowberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces bright red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Maesa parksii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
