Mataxa

Mataxa, commonly known as *Schefflera octophylla*, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, umbrella-like clusters of leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it popular in both indoor and outdoor settings.
