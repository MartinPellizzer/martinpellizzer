Mentha sylvestris var. marisensis

Mentha sylvestris var. marisensis, commonly known as the sea mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, saline soils and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky shores.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used for digestive and respiratory ailments.
