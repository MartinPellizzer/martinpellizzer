Magnolia ernestii subsp. szechuanica

Magnolia ernestii subsp.

szechuanica, commonly known as the Sichuan magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China. It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge.

The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
