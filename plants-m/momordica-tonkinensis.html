Momordica tonkinensis

Momordica tonkinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese bitter melon, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and pumpkins. The plant produces long, slender fruits that are typically green and have a warty surface. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with five petals.

The fruit is used in traditional medicine and cuisine, valued for its bitter taste and medicinal properties.
