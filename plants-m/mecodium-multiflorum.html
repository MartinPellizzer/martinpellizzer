Mecodium multiflorum

Mecodium multiflorum, commonly known as the many-flowered mecodium, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with toothed margins, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
