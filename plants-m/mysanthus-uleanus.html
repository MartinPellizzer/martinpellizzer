Mysanthus uleanus

Mysanthus uleanus, commonly known as the golden cup, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble cups, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly glossy surface.

Mysanthus uleanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
