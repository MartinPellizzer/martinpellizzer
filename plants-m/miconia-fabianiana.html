Miconia fabianiana

Miconia fabianiana, commonly known as the Brazilian gloryberry, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or red. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with prominent veins and can grow up to 3 meters in height. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, berry-like seeds.

Miconia fabianiana is often found in moist, shaded habitats within the understory of tropical forests.
