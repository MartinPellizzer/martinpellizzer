Moorochloa eruciformis var. divaricata

Moorochloa eruciformis var. divaricata, commonly known as the slender moorgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its adaptability and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
