Medicago schischkinii

Medicago schischkinii, commonly known as Schischkin's clover, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has a shallow root system that allows it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Due to its ecological significance, it is sometimes used in restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
