Mussaenda ferruginea

Mussaenda ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers clustered at the base of the bracts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Mussaenda ferruginea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
