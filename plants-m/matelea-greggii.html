Matelea greggii

Matelea greggii, commonly known as Gregg's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Gregg's milkweed is also a valuable source of nectar for pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
