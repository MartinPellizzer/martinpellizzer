Mertensia paniculata var. longisepala

Mertensia paniculata var. longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepaled bluebells, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth, dark green surface with a slightly hairy texture.

The variety longisepala is distinguished by its elongated sepals, which give it a unique appearance compared to other Mertensia species.
