Melastoma punctatum

Melastoma punctatum, commonly known as the small-leaved melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth surface and small, clustered white flowers. It produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when ripe.

Melastoma punctatum is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
