Mandevilla lancibracteata

Mandevilla lancibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and can grow as a shrub or vine, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and as a houseplant.
