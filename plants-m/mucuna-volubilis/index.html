Mucuna Volubilis

Mucuna volubilis is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa. It is known for its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, velvety pods that contain seeds used in traditional medicine. The plant has been historically valued for its potential therapeutic properties and is often grown as a cover crop. Its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it beneficial for soil enrichment. Despite its benefits, it can become invasive if not managed properly.
