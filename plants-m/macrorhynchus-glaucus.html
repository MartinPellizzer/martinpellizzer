Macrorhynchus glaucus

Macrorhynchus glaucus, commonly known as the blue-tongued grass, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue-green leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice among gardeners.
