Malva martensiana

Malva martensiana, commonly known as the Siberian mallow, is a perennial herb native to the colder regions of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance.

Malva martensiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
