Myrtoleucodendron hypericifolium

Myrtoleucodendron hypericifolium, commonly known as the golden-leaved myrtle, is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that range from bright green to golden yellow, depending on light exposure. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its unique foliage and limited distribution make it a subject of interest in botanical research.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
