Mentha arvensis f. obtusidentata

Mentha arvensis f. obtusidentata, commonly known as the broad-leaved mint, is a variety of the field mint species.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves and a strong, aromatic scent. This plant is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in woodland edges.

The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
