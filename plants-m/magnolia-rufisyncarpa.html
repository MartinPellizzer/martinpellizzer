Magnolia rufisyncarpa

Magnolia rufisyncarpa, commonly known as the red-veined magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are broad, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slight waxy coating. The tree produces distinctive red-brown seed pods that split open to release its seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
