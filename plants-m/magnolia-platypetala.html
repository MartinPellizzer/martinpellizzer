Magnolia platypetala

Magnolia platypetala, commonly known as the Mexican magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to central Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters at the base of the previous year's growth.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
