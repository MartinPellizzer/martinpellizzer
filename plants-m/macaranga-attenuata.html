Macaranga attenuata

Macaranga attenuata, commonly known as the slender macaranga, is a tropical shrub or small tree belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Macaranga attenuata plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
