Mertensia tenera

Mertensia tenera, commonly known as the eastern bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to deep purple, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with silver, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it forms dense colonies.

Mertensia tenera is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance, providing nectar for early pollinators.
