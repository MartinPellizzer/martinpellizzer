Malache papilionacea

Malache papilionacea, commonly known as the butterfly bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, butterfly-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of protein-rich seed meal.

Additionally, it plays a role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting soil fertility in agricultural settings.
