Myrtus bicolor

Myrtus bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that have a distinctive two-tone appearance, with a lighter underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. Myrtus bicolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile plant for gardens and landscapes.
