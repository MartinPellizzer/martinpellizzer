Myristica panamensis

Myristica panamensis, commonly known as the Panamanian nutmeg, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic seeds and distinctive red, fleshy aril. The plant produces small, white flowers and develops a capsule fruit that contains the valuable seed. Its botanical features include smooth, dark green leaves and a woody, branched stem.

Myristica panamensis is primarily valued for its aromatic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
