Myrceugenia foveolata

Myrceugenia foveolata, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest biome in southeastern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with dark green, elliptical leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruit, which resembles a blackberry, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food for various wildlife species.
