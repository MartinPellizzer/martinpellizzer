Mussaenda tristigmatica

Mussaenda tristigmatica, commonly known as the red-veined Mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring lance-shaped leaves with prominent red veins. The plant produces clusters of small, white or yellowish flowers that are often surrounded by a ring of bracts. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
