Melaleuca aglaia

Melaleuca aglaia, commonly known as the satinwood or white melaleuca, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds, and it often grows in coastal or wetland areas.

Melaleuca aglaia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
