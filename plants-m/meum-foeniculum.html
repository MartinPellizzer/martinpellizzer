Meum foeniculum

Meum foeniculum, commonly known as fennel, is a flowering plant in the carrot family (Apiaceae).

It is characterized by its feathery, aromatic foliage and yellow umbels of flowers. The plant is native to southern Europe and western Asia, and it thrives in well-drained soils. Fennel is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used in cooking and traditional medicine.

Its tall, slender stalks can reach up to 2 meters in height, making it a striking addition to gardens.
