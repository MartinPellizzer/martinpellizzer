Melicoccus bijugatus

Melicoccus bijugatus, commonly known as the Natal plum or sweet curacao, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa, the Caribbean, and parts of South America.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is a small, round drupe with a yellowish skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp that contains several seeds. It is often grown for its ornamental value and edible fruit, which is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
