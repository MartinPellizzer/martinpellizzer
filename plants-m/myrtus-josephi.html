Myrtus josephi

Myrtus josephi, commonly known as the Joseph's myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant features narrow, dark green leaves that are often fragrant when crushed. It produces small, white flowers followed by red berries, which are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Myrtus josephi thrives in well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
