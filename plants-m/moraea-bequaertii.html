Moraea bequaertii

Moraea bequaertii, commonly known as the Bequaert's moraea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces inflorescences with multiple tubular flowers arranged in a spike. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
