Melia hasskarlii

Melia hasskarlii, commonly known as the Persian ash, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Oleaceae and is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed trunk and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by elliptical, woody fruits containing one or two seeds. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with a distinctive flaky texture.

Melia hasskarlii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
