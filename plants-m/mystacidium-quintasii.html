Mystacidium quintasii

Mystacidium quintasii, commonly known as the golden mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has small, leathery, ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy coating. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in small inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries that are a source of food for various birds. Its parasitic nature allows it to absorb water and nutrients from its host, while still performing photosynthesis.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
