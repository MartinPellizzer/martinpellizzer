Myristica sebifera var. curvinervia

Myristica sebifera var. curvinervia, commonly known as the narrow-veined nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Maluku Islands of Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody fruits that contain valuable essential oils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by three-winged fruits, which are typically harvested when fully mature. Its leaves are alternate, elliptic in shape, and have a distinctive, narrow venation pattern.

This variety is particularly noted for its potential in the production of natural perfumes and medicinal compounds.
