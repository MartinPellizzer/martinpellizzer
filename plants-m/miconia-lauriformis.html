Miconia lauriformis

Miconia lauriformis, commonly known as the broadleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that are often arranged in a whorled pattern. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers with five petals, typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Miconia lauriformis is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
