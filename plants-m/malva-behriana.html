Malva behriana

Malva behriana, commonly known as the Cape mallow, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton plants. The plant is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
