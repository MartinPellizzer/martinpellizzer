Malva fasciculata

Malva fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered mallows, is a flowering plant native to the United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its fuzzy, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, with five petals and a distinct central column.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
