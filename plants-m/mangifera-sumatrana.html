Mangifera sumatrana

Mangifera sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran mango, is a tropical tree native to the Indonesian island of Sumatra.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and fragrant, yellow-orange fruits. The fruit has a thick, fibrous rind and a sweet, juicy pulp that is highly valued for its flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is also notable for its ornamental value and potential economic importance in local agriculture.
