Muscari bushiricum

Muscari bushiricum, commonly known as the Persian grape hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It produces dense clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue and purple, resembling tiny grapes. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant. Its bulbs are relatively hardy and can be planted in the fall for spring blooming.

The Persian grape hyacinth is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to naturalize over time in suitable climates.
