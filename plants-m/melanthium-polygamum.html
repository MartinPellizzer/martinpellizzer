Melanthium polygamum

Melanthium polygamum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its showy, purple-pink flower heads that resemble coneflowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.
