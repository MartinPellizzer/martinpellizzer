Monardella undulata

Monardella undulata, commonly known as lemon mint, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive wavy margin. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular, light pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and open woodlands.

Lemon mint is valued for its aromatic leaves and is often used in herbal teas and as a culinary herb.
