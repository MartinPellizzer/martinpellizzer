Macropanax vidalii

Macropanax vidalii, commonly known as the Vidal's giant arum, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in length, with a deep green color and a prominent midrib. The plant produces an inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, which is often purple or reddish in hue. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its unique appearance and limited natural range, it is a sought-after specimen in botanical gardens and conservatories.
