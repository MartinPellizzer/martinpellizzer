Malvastrum puniceum

Malvastrum puniceum, commonly known as the scarlet mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture.

Malvastrum puniceum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
