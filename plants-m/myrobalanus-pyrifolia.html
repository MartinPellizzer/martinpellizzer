Myrobalanus pyrifolia

Myrobalanus pyrifolia, commonly known as the Chinese persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its broad leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The tree produces orange to reddish fruits that are sweet when fully ripe, often used in traditional Chinese cuisine. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance, while its wood is valued for its durability and workability.

The plant is also noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
