Monoplegma trinervium

Monoplegma trinervium, commonly known as the three-veined fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinnately divided fronds with three main veins, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spores are produced in clusters on the underside of the fronds, which are released during the wet season.

The plant's structure and reproductive features make it an important component of tropical and subtropical ecosystems.
