Melissa pulegioides

Melissa pulegioides, commonly known as lemon balm, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish-white flowers in whorled clusters. Melissa pulegioides is valued for its fragrant foliage, which contains compounds like citral and geraniol, giving it a lemony scent.

It is often used in herbal medicine and as a ornamental plant in gardens.
