Manicaria saccifera

Manicaria saccifera, commonly known as the balloon vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, flexible stems that can grow up to 10 meters in length. The plant produces striking, inflated seed pods that resemble balloons, which are used for seed dispersal. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

Manicaria saccifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
