Maxillaria parviloba

Maxillaria parviloba, commonly known as the small-lobed orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-draining soil.

Maxillaria parviloba is appreciated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate for orchid enthusiasts.
