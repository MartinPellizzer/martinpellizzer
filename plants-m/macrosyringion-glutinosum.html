Macrosyringion glutinosum

Macrosyringion glutinosum, commonly known as the sticky sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in early summer. The plant produces clusters of three to six spikelets, which are often covered in a sticky substance, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
