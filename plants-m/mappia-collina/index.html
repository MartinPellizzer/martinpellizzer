Mappia Collina

Mappia collina is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. Known for its unique leaf structure and vibrant floral displays, it has captured the attention of botanists and horticulturists alike. This species thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires careful cultivation to flourish. Its ecological importance lies in its role as a habitat for various insect species. Despite its beauty, Mappia collina remains under-studied and is increasingly threatened by habitat loss.
