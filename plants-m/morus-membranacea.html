Morus membranacea

Morus membranacea, commonly known as the paper mulberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in catkins. Its bark is thick and smooth, with a distinctive pale color.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
