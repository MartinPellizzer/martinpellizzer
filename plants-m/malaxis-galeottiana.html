Malaxis galeottiana

Malaxis galeottiana, commonly known as the Galeotti's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale green to yellowish inflorescence and small, intricate flowers that resemble miniature orchids. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insects, and it has a long flowering period, often blooming in the early morning.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, Malaxis galeottiana is considered a conservation concern.
