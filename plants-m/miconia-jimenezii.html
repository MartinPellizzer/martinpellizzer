Miconia jimenezii

Miconia jimenezii, commonly known as the Jimenez's miconia, is a species of flowering plant in the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. It produces berries that are typically dark red when mature, containing several seeds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
