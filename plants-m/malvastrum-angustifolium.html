Malvastrum angustifolium

Malvastrum angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its flowers are composed of five petals and are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

The plant has a fibrous root system and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
