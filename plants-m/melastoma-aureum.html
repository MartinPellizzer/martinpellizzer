Melastoma aureum

Melastoma aureum, commonly known as the golden flame or golden melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.

Melastoma aureum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
