Melaleuca ciliosa

Melaleuca ciliosa, commonly known as the slender bottlebrush, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of small, bright red or pink flowers. The leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
