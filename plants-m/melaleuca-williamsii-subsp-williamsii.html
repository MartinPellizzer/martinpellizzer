Melaleuca williamsii subsp. williamsii

Melaleuca williamsii subsp.

williamsii, commonly known as the broad-leaved paperbark, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae. It is native to northern Australia, particularly Queensland and the Northern Territory, where it thrives in wet tropical climates.

The plant is characterized by its smooth, flaky bark that peels in thin layers, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a glossy green upper surface and a pale underside.

The flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are clustered in dense inflorescences, attracting various pollinators.
