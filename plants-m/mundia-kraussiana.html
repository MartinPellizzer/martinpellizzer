Mundia kraussiana

Mundia kraussiana, commonly known as the African starwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in terminal umbels, followed by small, round, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and hardiness.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
