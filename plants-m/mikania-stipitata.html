Mikania stipitata

Mikania stipitata, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a vigorous vine that can grow up to several meters in length, with heart-shaped leaves and showy blue to violet flowers. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail along the ground. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, facilitating its spread.

Mikania stipitata is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and fields, and is sometimes considered a weed due to its invasive tendencies.
