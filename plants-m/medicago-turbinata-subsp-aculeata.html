Medicago turbinata subsp. aculeata

Medicago turbinata subsp.

aculeata, commonly known as the prickly medick, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae. It is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its stiff, spiny stipules and the presence of small, curved spines on the stems. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

This plant plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
