Morus alba var. stylosa

Morus alba var. stylosa, commonly known as the Chinese mulberry, is a variant of the white mulberry native to China.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, fuzzy berries that are typically consumed by silkworms. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its fibrous bark and medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
