Mentha affinis

Mentha affinis, commonly known as narrow-leaf mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or violet in color. Mentha affinis is often found in moist, calcareous soils in woodland areas or along streambanks.

It is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in herbal teas and as a culinary herb.
