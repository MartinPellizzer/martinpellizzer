Mussaenda flavescens

Mussaenda flavescens, commonly known as the golden Mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble small umbels. The plant has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Mussaenda flavescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
