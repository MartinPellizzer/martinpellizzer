Machaerium eriocarpum f. tomentellum

Machaerium eriocarpum f. tomentellum, commonly known as the woolly sicklepod, is a flowering plant belonging to the Leguminosae family.

It is characterized by its small, hairy, and often glandular leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, drooping clusters, typical of the genus Machaerium. Its fruits are legumes, which are typically hairy and contain small, hard seeds.

This plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in disturbed soils and open habitats.
