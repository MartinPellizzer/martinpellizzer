Malache

Malache, commonly known as the false hellebore, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Melanthiaceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are native to North America. The plants typically have dark green, glossy leaves and produce showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple. They are often found in woodland areas and thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Malache is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
