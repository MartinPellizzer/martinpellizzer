Melaleuca acerosa var. bracteosa

Melaleuca acerosa var. bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteose bottlebrush, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of bright red or pink flowers, which are typical of the Myrtaceae family. The leaves are narrow, linear, and often covered with a fine, white tomentum. This species thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
