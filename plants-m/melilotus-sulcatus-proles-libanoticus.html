Melilotus sulcatus proles libanoticus

Melilotus sulcatus proles libanoticus, commonly known as the Lebanese melilot, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, typical of the genus Melilotus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
