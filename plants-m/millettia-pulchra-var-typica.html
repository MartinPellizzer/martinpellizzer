Millettia pulchra var. typica

Millettia pulchra var. typica, commonly known as the purple wisteria, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in pendulous clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in suitable climates.
