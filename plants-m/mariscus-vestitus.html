Mariscus vestitus

Mariscus vestitus, commonly known as the naked mariscus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

Mariscus vestitus is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
