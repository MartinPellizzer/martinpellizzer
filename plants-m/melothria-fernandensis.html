Melothria fernandensis

Melothria fernandensis, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or Physalis peruviana, is a flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its distinctive calyx that encloses the fruit, resembling a small, inflated lantern. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries that are rich in nutrients and have a tangy, sweet flavor. Its foliage consists of alternate, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces, and it thrives in warm, tropical climates.

The plant is often cultivated for its edible fruits and has been used in traditional medicine for various health benefits.
