Medicago asiatica subsp. khivinica

Medicago asiatica subsp.

khivinica, commonly known as the Khivinica goat's rue, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia. It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is of interest for its potential in ecological restoration and as a forage plant.
