Morus australis var. hachijoensis

Morus australis var. hachijoensis, commonly known as the Hachijo mulberry, is a variant of the mulberry tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in traditional Japanese culture.
