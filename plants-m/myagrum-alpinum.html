Myagrum alpinum

Myagrum alpinum, commonly known as the alpine myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its botanical features include a woody stem and a preference for well-drained, acidic soils.

Myagrum alpinum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and aromatic properties.
