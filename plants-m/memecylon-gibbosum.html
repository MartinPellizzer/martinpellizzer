Memecylon gibbosum

Memecylon gibbosum, commonly known as the round-leaved memecylon, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The leaves are elliptic to ovate, with a distinctive rounded base, and are usually glossy green on the upper surface. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by red or orange berries that are edible.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal fruit.
