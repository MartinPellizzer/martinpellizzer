Moquinia polymorpha

Moquinia polymorpha, commonly known as the flatleaf spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences that bear small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often forming dense colonies in marshes and pond edges. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, contributing to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Moquinia polymorpha plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
