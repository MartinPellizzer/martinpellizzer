Malpighia parvifolia

Malpighia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved acorn vine, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of bright red, fleshy fruits that are edible and contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has also been used in traditional medicine.
