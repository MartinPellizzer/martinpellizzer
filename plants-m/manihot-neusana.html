Manihot neusana

Manihot neusana, commonly known as arrowleaf manioc, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant produces a milky sap that contains toxic compounds, requiring careful handling. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

However, the plant is not typically cultivated for commercial purposes due to its low yield and potential toxicity.
