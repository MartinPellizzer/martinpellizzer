Metroxylon carolinense

Metroxylon carolinense, commonly known as the Caribbean palm, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, fibrous fruits. Its trunk is valued for its strong, fibrous material used in making ropes and mats.

The plant thrives in wet, coastal areas and is adapted to saline conditions.
