Merostachys lage-vianae

Merostachys lage-vianae, commonly known as the Brazilian grass or Cape grass, is a species of grass native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spike that bears small, spikelet-like flowers.

Merostachys lage-vianae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fine texture and resilience.
