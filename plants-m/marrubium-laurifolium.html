Marrubium laurifolium

Marrubium laurifolium, commonly known as the laurel weed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features gray-green, oval-shaped leaves that are often aromatic and have a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its flowering stems are typically hairy and can grow up to 60 centimeters in height.

Marrubium laurifolium is often found in dry, rocky soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
