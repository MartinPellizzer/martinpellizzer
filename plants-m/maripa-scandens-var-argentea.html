Maripa scandens var. argentea

Maripa scandens var. argentea, commonly known as silverleaf maripa, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round berries. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
