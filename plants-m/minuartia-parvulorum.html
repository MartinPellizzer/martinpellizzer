Minuartia parvulorum

Minuartia parvulorum, commonly known as the dwarf rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale yellow. The plant features a mat-forming growth habit with many small, succulent leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.

Minuartia parvulorum is well adapted to harsh climates and is commonly used in rock gardens due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
