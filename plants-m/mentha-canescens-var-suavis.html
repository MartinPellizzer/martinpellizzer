Mentha canescens var. suavis

Mentha canescens var. suavis, commonly known as sweet mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly fuzzy leaves that emit a pleasant, minty fragrance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses due to its refreshing scent and flavor.
