Medicago hispida var. nigra

Medicago hispida var. nigra, commonly known as black goat's horn, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and dark-colored pods that resemble horns, hence its common name. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellow flowers in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

This species is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
