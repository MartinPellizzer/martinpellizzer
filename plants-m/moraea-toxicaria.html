Moraea toxicaria

Moraea toxicaria, commonly known as the deadly nightshade of South Africa, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the region.

It features striking, showy flowers that are typically purple or reddish in color, with a distinctive, tubular shape. The plant belongs to the Iridaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. However, it is toxic, containing alkaloids that can be harmful if ingested.

Its botanical characteristics make it both visually appealing and potentially dangerous.
