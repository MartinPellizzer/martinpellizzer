Memecylon memecyloides

Memecylon memecyloides, commonly known as the dwarf memecylon, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and features opposite, elliptical leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
