Monocosmia verrucosa

Monocosmia verrucosa, commonly known as the roughleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in grassland and rocky habitats. The plant features hairy, rough-textured leaves and produces showy, pink or purple flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a sympetalous corolla with five fused petals and stamens that are often arranged in two whorls.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
