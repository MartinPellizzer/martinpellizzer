Millettia conraui

Millettia conraui, commonly known as the African silk tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pod fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and showy blooms, making it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.

Millettia conraui is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
