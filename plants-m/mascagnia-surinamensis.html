Mascagnia surinamensis

Mascagnia surinamensis, commonly known as the Surinam mombin, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and often elliptical in shape. The fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed, and the plant is known for its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
