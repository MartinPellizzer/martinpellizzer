Melissa calamintha

Melissa calamintha, commonly known as catnip, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated edge and a strong, mint-like scent. Its flowers are small, tubular, and clustered in whorls, typically purple or white in color.

Melissa calamintha is known for its medicinal properties and is often used in herbal remedies.
