Medicago falcata var. revoluta

Medicago falcata var. revoluta, commonly known as the curled vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curved leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a robust root system that helps prevent soil erosion. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
