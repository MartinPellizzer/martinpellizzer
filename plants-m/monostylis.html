Monostylis

Monostylis, commonly known as the grass-like plant of the Cyperaceae family, is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

It typically grows in moist or wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Monostylis species are often found in tropical and subtropical regions, and they play a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.

Due to their delicate appearance, they are sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
