Magnolia tripetala

Magnolia tripetala, commonly known as the sweetbay magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and its showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has smooth, gray bark and can grow up to 30 feet tall, often forming a rounded crown. Its flowers are composed of nine to twelve petals and are followed by spherical, reddish-purple fruits.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
