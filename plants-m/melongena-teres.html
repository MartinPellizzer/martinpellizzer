Melongena teres

Melongena teres, commonly known as the purple eggplant, is a unique and lesser-known species within the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, egg-shaped fruits that are typically deep purple in color. The plant produces small, white flowers with five petals, typical of the nightshade family. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture.

While not as widely cultivated as other eggplant varieties, Melongena teres is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential culinary uses.
