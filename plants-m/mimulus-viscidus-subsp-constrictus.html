Mimulus viscidus subsp. constrictus

Mimulus viscidus subsp.

constrictus, commonly known as the sticky monkeyflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its glandular, sticky leaves and showy, orange to red tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and woodland edges. It has a fibrous root system and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
