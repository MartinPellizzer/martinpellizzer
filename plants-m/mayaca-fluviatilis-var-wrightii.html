Mayaca fluviatilis var. wrightii

Mayaca fluviatilis var. wrightii, commonly known as Wright's river mayaca, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of Central and South America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in riverine and swampy habitats. The plant features elongated, narrow leaves that are often submerged or floating, with a fibrous root system adapted to aquatic environments. Its flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, and are usually greenish-white in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens.
