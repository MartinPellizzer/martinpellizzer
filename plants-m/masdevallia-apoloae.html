Masdevallia apoloae

Masdevallia apoloae, commonly known as the APOLOAE orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often colorful flowers with long, slender petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it blooms during the warmer months.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
