Matayba paucijuga

Matayba paucijuga, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Erythroxylum paucijugum*, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family, Malvaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, pinkish-white flowers and elliptical leaves with a rough texture. It produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisine.

Matayba paucijuga is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
