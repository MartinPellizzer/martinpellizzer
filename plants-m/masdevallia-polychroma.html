Masdevallia polychroma

Masdevallia polychroma, commonly known as the rainbow masdevallia, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that display a wide range of hues, including red, orange, yellow, and green, often in a single bloom. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are strap-shaped and arranged in a rosette, while its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air.

Due to its vibrant appearance and unique growth habits, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
