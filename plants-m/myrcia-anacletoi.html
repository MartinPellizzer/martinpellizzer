Myrcia anacletoi

Myrcia anacletoi, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, red berries. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. It has a dense, evergreen canopy and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in some areas where it outcompetes native vegetation.
