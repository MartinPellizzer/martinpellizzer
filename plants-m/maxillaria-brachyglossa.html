Maxillaria brachyglossa

Maxillaria brachyglossa, commonly known as the short-lipped maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, often pseudobulbous growth habit and produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms in the summer months, with flowers that have a distinctive, short lip. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its overall ornamental appeal.

Maxillaria brachyglossa is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
