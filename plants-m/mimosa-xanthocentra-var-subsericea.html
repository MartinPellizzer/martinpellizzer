Mimosa xanthocentra var. subsericea

Mimosa xanthocentra var. subsericea, commonly known as the yellow mimosa, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features slender, hairy stems and compound leaves that are pinnately arranged with small, oval leaflets. The plant produces fluffy, yellow to orange-pink spherical flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to wildlife gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in Mediterranean climates.
