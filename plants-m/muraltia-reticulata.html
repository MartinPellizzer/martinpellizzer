Muraltia reticulata

Muraltia reticulata, commonly known as the red mombin or red mombin tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually trifoliate or pinnate, and its small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a drupe, often referred to as a "mombin," which turns red when ripe and is edible.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
