Miconia emendata

Miconia emendata, commonly known as the red miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous small, black seeds.

Miconia emendata is known for its rapid growth and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.
