Medinilla curtisii

Medinilla curtisii, commonly known as the velvet tangerine.

It is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are yellow-orange when mature, resembling tangerines in appearance. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Medinilla curtisii is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
