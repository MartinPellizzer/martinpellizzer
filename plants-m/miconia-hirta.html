Miconia hirta

Miconia hirta, commonly known as the hairy miconia, is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Miconia hirta is also known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, which can impact local ecosystems.
