Molina sessilifolia

Molina sessilifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved molina, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and often edible seeds. Molina sessilifolia is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats at higher elevations.
