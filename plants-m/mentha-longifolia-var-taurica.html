Mentha longifolia var. taurica

Mentha longifolia var. taurica, commonly known as Tauric mint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Tauric mint is valued for its aromatic leaves and is used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
