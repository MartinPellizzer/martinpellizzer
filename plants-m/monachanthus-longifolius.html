Monachanthus longifolius

Monachanthus longifolius, commonly known as the longleaf monachanthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
