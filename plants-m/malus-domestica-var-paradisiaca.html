Malus domestica var. paradisiaca

Malus domestica var. paradisiaca, commonly known as the Honeycrisp apple, is a cultivar of the domestic apple tree.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and yellow foliage, which adds ornamental value to gardens. The plant produces medium-sized, crisp, and sweet fruits that are highly prized for their flavor and texture. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is also noted for its strong resistance to certain diseases, making it a popular choice for both commercial and home orchards.
