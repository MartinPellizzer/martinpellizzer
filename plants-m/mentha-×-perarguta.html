Mentha × perarguta

Mentha × perarguta, commonly known as hybrid mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Mentha viridis (apple mint) and Mentha spicata (spearmint). The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are usually pale purple or blue. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, which give it a refreshing menthol scent.

Mentha × perarguta is widely cultivated for use in culinary, medicinal, and aromatic applications.
