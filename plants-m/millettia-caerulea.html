Millettia caerulea

Millettia caerulea, commonly known as the blue millettia, is a flowering plant species native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking blue flowers, making it a popular choice for horticultural gardens.
