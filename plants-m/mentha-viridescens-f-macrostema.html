Mentha viridescens f. macrostema

Mentha viridescens f. macrostema, commonly known as the large-stamen spearmint, is a horticultural form of the spearmint species.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and a strong, minty aroma. The plant is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its flowers are small and clustered, producing nectar that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
