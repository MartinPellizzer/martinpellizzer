Myrrhidium multicaule

Myrrhidium multicaule, commonly known as the clustered myrrhplant, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit. The plant features multiple stems that arise from a single rootstock, often forming dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring.

Myrrhidium multicaule is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
