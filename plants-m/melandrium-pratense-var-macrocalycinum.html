Melandrium pratense var. macrocalycinum

Melandrium pratense var. macrocalycinum, commonly known as the large-flowered catchfly, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in dense, branched clusters. The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Caryophyllaceae family.
