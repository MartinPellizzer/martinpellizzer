Maprounea africana var. gracilis

Maprounea africana var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender African maple, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching habit and compound leaves that are often alternate and pinnate. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a notable species in horticulture and ecological studies.
