Malpighia ovalis

Malpighia ovalis, commonly known as the oval granadilla, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It produces bright red, fleshy berries that are rich in vitamin C and have a tart, tangy flavor. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are oval in shape with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

The flowers are small, clustered, and typically white or pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
