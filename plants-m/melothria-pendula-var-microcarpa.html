Melothria pendula var. microcarpa

Melothria pendula var. microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf edible morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the morning, giving it its common name. Its fruits are round, green berries that turn red when mature, and they contain several seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which have a tart, tangy flavor.
