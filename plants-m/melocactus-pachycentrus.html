Melocactus pachycentrus

Melocactus pachycentrus, commonly known as the thick-shelled cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stem and a dense covering of spines, which help protect it from herbivores and reduce water loss. The plant produces a single, large, yellowish flower at the top of its stem, typically during the summer months. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and desert gardens.
