Monardella villosa subsp. obispoensis

Monardella villosa subsp.

obispoensis, commonly known as Obispo's blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to California. It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and chaparral. It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance to local pollinators.
