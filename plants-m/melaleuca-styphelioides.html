Melaleuca styphelioides

Melaleuca styphelioides, commonly known as the Narrow-leaved Paperbark, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that gives it a distinctive appearance. Its small, white or cream-colored flowers appear in spikes and are often visited by nectar-feeding insects.

The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, contributing to its overall ornamental value.
