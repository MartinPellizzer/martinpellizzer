Mesosphaerum marrubiifolium

Mesosphaerum marrubiifolium, commonly known as the Brazilian mountain mint, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
