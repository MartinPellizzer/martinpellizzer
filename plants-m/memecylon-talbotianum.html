Memecylon talbotianum

Memecylon talbotianum, commonly known as the Talbot's memecylon, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
