Moraea fasciculata

Moraea fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered moraea, is a striking bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It produces tall, slender stems with clusters of funnel-shaped, often brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant's bulbs are fleshy and store water, allowing it to thrive in arid or semi-arid conditions. Its flowering period usually occurs during the summer months, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
