Monoon anomalum

Monoon anomalum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Indian spinach, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Monoon anomalum is also valued for its edible leaves, which are used in various culinary traditions.
