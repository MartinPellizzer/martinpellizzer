Melothria minutiflora var. parvifolia

Melothria minutiflora var. parvifolia, commonly known as the dwarf littleleaf melon, is a small, herbaceous vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features small, green, heart-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in diameter. The plant produces tiny, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, sweet, and tangy fruits resembling miniature watermelons. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its unique flavor and ornamental value.

Due to its compact size, it is well-suited for container gardening and small spaces.
