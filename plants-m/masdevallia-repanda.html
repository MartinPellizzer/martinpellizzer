Masdevallia repanda

Masdevallia repanda, commonly known as the spreading masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and produces intricate, often colorful flower spikes. The plant features long, slender leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowers are usually small to medium-sized, with a distinctive shape and a variety of color combinations.

Masdevallia repanda is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
