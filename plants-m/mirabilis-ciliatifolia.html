Mirabilis ciliatifolia

Mirabilis ciliatifolia, commonly known as the silky nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple flowers that bloom in the evening. The leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers.
