Molineria laevis var. glabrata

Molineria laevis var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth tawny sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which are often greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in association with other wetland vegetation.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
