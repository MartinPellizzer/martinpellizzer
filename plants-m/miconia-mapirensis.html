Miconia mapirensis

Miconia mapirensis, commonly known as the Mapirensis miconia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, showy leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforests and is often found in shaded, moist environments. It has a woody stem and can reach heights of up to 3 meters, making it a notable presence in its natural habitat.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
