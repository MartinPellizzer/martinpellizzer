Microtropis reticulata

Microtropis reticulata, commonly known as the reticulated microtropis, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and trifoliate leaves with a distinctive reticulate venation pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
