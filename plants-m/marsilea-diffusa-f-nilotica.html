Marsilea diffusa f. nilotica

Marsilea diffusa f. nilotica, commonly known as water clover, is a water fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features small, floating leaves that are divided into four lobes, resembling a clover leaf. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and marshes. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that produce spores for propagation.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small aquatic life.
