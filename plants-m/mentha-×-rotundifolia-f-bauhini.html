Mentha × rotundifolia f. bauhini

Mentha × rotundifolia f. bauhini, commonly known as round-leaf mint, is a hybrid plant species within the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its round or nearly round leaves, which distinguish it from other mint species. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb, often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands or stream banks. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, contributing to its use in herbal medicine and culinary applications.

The flowering stems bear small, tubular flowers that are usually purple or blue, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
