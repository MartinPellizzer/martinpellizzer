Malus microcarpa jucunda

Malus microcarpa jucunda, commonly known as the dwarf apple tree, is a small deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact size and ornamental value. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth texture and turn yellow in autumn.

This species is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
