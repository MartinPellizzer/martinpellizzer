Miconia theaezans var. paludosa

Miconia theaezans var. paludosa, commonly known as the swamp miconia, is a species native to the wetlands of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy or marshy areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
