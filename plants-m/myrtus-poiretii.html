Myrtus poiretii

Myrtus poiretii, commonly known as the Corsican myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, dark green leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark blue berries. Myrtus poiretii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value and historical significance in herbal remedies contribute to its popularity among gardeners and botanists.
