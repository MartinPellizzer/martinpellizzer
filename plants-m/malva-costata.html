Malva costata

Malva costata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features lobed leaves with a serrated edge, and its flowers are typically pink or purple, with five petals. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dry seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Malva costata is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
