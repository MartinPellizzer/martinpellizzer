Morinda umbellata var. bucidifolia

Morinda umbellata var. bucidifolia, commonly known as the red morinda, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in hue. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
