Muhlenbergia palustris

Muhlenbergia palustris, commonly known as marsh muhlenbergia, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, feathery seed heads that are often pink or purplish in color. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist conditions.

Muhlenbergia palustris plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
