Melinia atropurpurea

Melinia atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces cylindrical, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert and semi-arid environments.

Melinia atropurpurea plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
