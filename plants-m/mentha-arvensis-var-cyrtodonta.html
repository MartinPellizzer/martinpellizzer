Mentha arvensis var. cyrtodonta

Mentha arvensis var. cyrtodonta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mint, is a variant of the field mint species.

It is a perennial herb native to regions of Asia, including China and the Himalayas. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its small, pale blue or purple flowers appear in whorled clusters at the leaf axils.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
