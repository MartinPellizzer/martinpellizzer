Myrcia maximiliana

Myrcia maximiliana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

Myrcia maximiliana is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
