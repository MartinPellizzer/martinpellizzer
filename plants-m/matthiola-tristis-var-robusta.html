Matthiola tristis var. robusta

Matthiola tristis var. robusta, commonly known as the robust stock, is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with four-petaled flowers. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and often branched.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
