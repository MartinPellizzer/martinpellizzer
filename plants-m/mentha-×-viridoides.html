Mentha × viridoides

Mentha × viridoides, commonly known as green mint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Mentha spicata and Mentha viridis.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly menthol-like fragrance and its ability to thrive in moist, shady environments. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, and it is often found in damp woodlands or along stream banks. Mentha × viridoides is valued for its use in herbal teas and as a culinary herb, though it can be invasive in some regions.

Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and have a distinctive minty scent when crushed.
