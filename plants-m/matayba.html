Matayba

Matayba, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible but often have a spicy or peppery flavor. Matayba thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
