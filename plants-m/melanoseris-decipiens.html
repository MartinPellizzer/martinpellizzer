Melanoseris decipiens

Melanoseris decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive seris, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in dry, rocky habitats. The plant is characterized by its compact, evergreen structure and small, narrow leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in composite heads, and bloom in spring.

It is known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in its native environment.
