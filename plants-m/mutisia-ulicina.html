Mutisia ulicina

Mutisia ulicina, commonly known as the white mutisia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, white to pale yellow flower heads that bloom in spring. Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters and are visited by various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Mutisia ulicina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
