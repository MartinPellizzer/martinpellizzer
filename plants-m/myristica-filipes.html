Myristica filipes

Myristica filipes, commonly known as the nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is closely related to the nutmeg (Myristica fragrans). The plant is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant bark and its ability to produce both male and female flowers on the same tree. Its fruit, which contains a single seed, is used in traditional medicine and for its aromatic properties.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
