Mallotus claoxyloides

Mallotus claoxyloides, commonly known as the white-flowered mallot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and it produces woody capsules as fruit.

The plant is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
