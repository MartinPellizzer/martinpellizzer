Marsdenia calesiana

Marsdenia calesiana, commonly known as the Calesian Marsdenia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. Its flowers are typically clustered in inflorescences and feature showy, tubular corollas with five lobes.

While not widely cultivated, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological roles.
