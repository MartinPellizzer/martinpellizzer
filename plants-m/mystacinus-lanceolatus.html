Mystacinus lanceolatus

Mystacinus lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceolate mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of specific host trees.

It possesses chlorophyll and can perform photosynthesis, but it also derives nutrients from its host through specialized structures called haustoria. The plant has a flattened, leaf-like appearance with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss. It produces small, white or pale green flowers, followed by berries that are dispersed by birds.

This species is often found in tropical and subtropical regions where its host trees thrive.
