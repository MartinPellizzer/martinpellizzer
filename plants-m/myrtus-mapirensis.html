Myrtus mapirensis

Myrtus mapirensis, commonly known as the Mapire myrtle, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment.

Myrtus mapirensis is often used in traditional medicine and has ornamental value due to its fragrant foliage.
