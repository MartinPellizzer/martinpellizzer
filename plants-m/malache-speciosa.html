Malache speciosa

Malache speciosa, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual impact. The plant has compound leaves composed of multiple leaflets, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.

Malache speciosa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
