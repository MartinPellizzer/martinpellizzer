Melaleuca microphylla

Melaleuca microphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paperbark, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. It typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to salt-laden winds and sandy soils.

Melaleuca microphylla is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
