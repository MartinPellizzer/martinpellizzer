Maxillaria rhombea

Maxillaria rhombea, commonly known as the rhombic maxillaria, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms during the summer months, with flowers that have a distinctive rhombus-shaped lip. Its pseudobulbs are relatively short, and the leaves are narrow and grass-like.

Maxillaria rhombea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
