Mucuna elmeri

Mucuna elmeri, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, hairy, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in agricultural settings. Its seeds contain a variety of alkaloids, which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

The plant's ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for garden use in warm climates.
