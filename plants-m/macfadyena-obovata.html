Macfadyena obovata

Macfadyena obovata, commonly known as the Crown of Thorns, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a red or purple throat. It is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

However, it can be invasive in some areas and requires careful management.
