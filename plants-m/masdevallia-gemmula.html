Masdevallia gemmula

Masdevallia gemmula, commonly known as the gemmula orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit. The plant typically produces small, intricate flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a distinctive hooded lip. Its leaves are fleshy and arranged in a rosette, adapted to conserve moisture in its humid, shaded habitat.

Masdevallia gemmula is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and unique morphology.
