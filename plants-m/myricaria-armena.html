Myricaria armena

Myricaria armena, commonly known as Armenian myricaria, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Cupuliferae and is characterized by its grayish, hairy leaves and yellow flowers that appear in panicles. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky slopes and in mountainous areas. Its small, capsule-like fruits contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Myricaria armena is adapted to harsh climatic conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
