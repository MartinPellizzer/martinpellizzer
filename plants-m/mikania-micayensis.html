Mikania micayensis

Mikania micayensis, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and vine-like habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, which bloom in the morning. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and can climb or trail along the ground.

Mikania micayensis is often found in moist, shaded areas and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
