Machaerium secundiflorum

Machaerium secundiflorum, commonly known as the slender-flowered machaerium, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, fertile soils and thrives in humid, tropical climates. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or white, and it produces small, flat pods that contain several seeds.

Machaerium secundiflorum is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
