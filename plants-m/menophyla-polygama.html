Menophyla polygama

Menophyla polygama, commonly known as the common tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring. It is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found along coastal areas.

Menophyla polygama is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh conditions.
