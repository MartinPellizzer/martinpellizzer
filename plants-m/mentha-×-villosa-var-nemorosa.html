Mentha × villosa var. nemorosa

Mentha × villosa var. nemorosa, commonly known as the woodland mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy, giving it a distinctive texture. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pale blue or violet, arranged in whorled clusters.

It is valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in herbal teas or as a ground cover in gardens.
