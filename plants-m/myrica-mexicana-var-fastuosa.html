Myrica mexicana var. fastuosa

Myrica mexicana var. fastuosa, commonly known as the Mexican wax myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its narrow, evergreen leaves that are often coated with a waxy layer, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by dark, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in local ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
