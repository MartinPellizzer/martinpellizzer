Melodorum rubiginosum var. oblongum

Melodorum rubiginosum var. oblongum, commonly known as the red-stemmed bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features lanceolate, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and produces clusters of bell-shaped, pinkish-red flowers in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens. Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant floral display and unique leaf structure.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
