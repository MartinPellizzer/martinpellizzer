Millettia mildbraedii

Millettia mildbraedii, commonly known as the African tamarind, is a leguminous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds surrounded by a fibrous pulp. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
