Monarda longifolia

Monarda longifolia, commonly known as slender bee balm, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branched stems covered in oval, dark green leaves with slightly serrated edges. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, or purple, arranged in terminal clusters. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its aromatic foliage and nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
