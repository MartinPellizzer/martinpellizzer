Microcharis phyllogramme

Microcharis phyllogramme, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, dark green leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in cup-like inflorescences. Its foliage and flowers are often covered in fine, white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
