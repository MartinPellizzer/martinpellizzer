Maxillaria matogrossensis

Maxillaria matogrossensis, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Brazilian Cerrado region.

It features a pseudobulb with a single leaf and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically blooms during the summer months, with its inflorescence bearing several flowers arranged in a raceme. Its roots are adapted to the nutrient-poor soils of its habitat, and it requires well-drained conditions to thrive.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
