Malva cachemiriana

Malva cachemiriana, commonly known as the Kashmir rose or Persian mallow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus, and is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Malva cachemiriana is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its traditional use in herbal medicine.
