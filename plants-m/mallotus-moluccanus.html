Mallotus moluccanus

Mallotus moluccanus, commonly known as the Moluccan hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, or white. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces capsules as its fruit, which contain numerous seeds. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various soil types.

Mallotus moluccanus prefers full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
