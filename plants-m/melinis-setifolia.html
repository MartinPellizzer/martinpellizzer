Melinis setifolia

Melinis setifolia, commonly known as silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and slender, arching stems that create a delicate, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late summer, which are typically clustered in panicles. Melinis setifolia thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and rock gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its unique texture and ability to thrive in dry conditions with minimal maintenance.
