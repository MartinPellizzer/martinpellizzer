Montelia × tamariscina var. concatenata

Montelia × tamariscina var. concatenata, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species, Montelia tamariscina and Tamariscina.

It is native to the island of Rodrigues in the Indian Ocean and is known for its distinctive red heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant is a small evergreen tree or shrub, typically growing to a height of 3–6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical, with a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and white, often appearing in clusters.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional crafts, it is also considered a rare and endangered species.
