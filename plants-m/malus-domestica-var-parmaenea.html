Malus domestica var. parmaenea

Malus domestica var. parmaenea, commonly known as the Parma apple, is a cultivar of the domestic apple tree.

It is characterized by its small, sweet, and aromatic fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with dense foliage, making it suitable for small gardens or container cultivation. Its flowers are white and fragrant, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety is valued for both its culinary use and its aesthetic appeal in landscaping.
