Myrtus multiflora

Myrtus multiflora, commonly known as the evergreen myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, dark green leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value and historical significance in herbal remedies make it a valued plant in horticulture.
