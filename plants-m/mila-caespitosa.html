Mila caespitosa

Mila caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted milkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by the wind. Mila caespitosa thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and has some traditional medicinal uses.
