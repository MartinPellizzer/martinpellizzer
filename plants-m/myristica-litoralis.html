Myristica litoralis

Myristica litoralis, commonly known as the coastal nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, oily seeds that resemble those of the nutmeg tree. The plant thrives in humid, saline environments and is often found in mangrove or coastal forest ecosystems. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a waxy texture, while its flowers are small and yellowish-green.

The fruit is a capsule that contains the valuable aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and perfumery.
