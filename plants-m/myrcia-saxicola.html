Myrcia saxicola

Myrcia saxicola, commonly known as the rock myrtle, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. It typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

Myrcia saxicola is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
