Maschalostachys markgrafii

Maschalostachys markgrafii, commonly known as the Markgraf's beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and ecological restoration projects.
