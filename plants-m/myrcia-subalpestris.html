Myrcia subalpestris

Myrcia subalpestris, commonly known as the alpine myrtle, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in the Andes.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

Myrcia subalpestris is adapted to cold, dry environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
