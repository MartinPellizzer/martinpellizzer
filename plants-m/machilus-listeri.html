Machilus listeri

Machilus listeri, commonly known as the Chinese evergreen or Japanese laurel, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and avocado. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries, which are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Machilus listeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is valued for its air-purifying properties.
