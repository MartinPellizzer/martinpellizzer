Myrcia oreophila

Myrcia oreophila, commonly known as the mountain myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, berry-like drupes that contain several seeds.

Myrcia oreophila is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain, and it plays an important role in the local ecosystem by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
