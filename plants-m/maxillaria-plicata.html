Maxillaria plicata

Maxillaria plicata, commonly known as the pleated maxillaria, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, pleated leaves and fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers, which range in color from white to yellow and pink, are known for their intricate patterns and strong scent.

Maxillaria plicata is appreciated in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
