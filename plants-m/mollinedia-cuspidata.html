Mollinedia cuspidata

Mollinedia cuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Mollinedia cuspidata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
