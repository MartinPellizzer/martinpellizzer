Meliosma cordata

Meliosma cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf meliosma, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically dark green and have a smooth texture. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by winged fruits that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
