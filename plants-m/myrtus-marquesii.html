Myrtus marquesii

Myrtus marquesii, commonly known as the Marquesas myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of the Pacific, specifically the Marquesas Islands.

It features small, dark green, leathery leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate.

Myrtus marquesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
