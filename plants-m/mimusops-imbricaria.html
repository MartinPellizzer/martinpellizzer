Mimusops imbricaria

Mimusops imbricaria, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a tropical tree native to regions across Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by orange-red berries containing seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The tree is also valued for its durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.
