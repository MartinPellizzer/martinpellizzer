Martiella palmeri

Martiella palmeri, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Martiella palmeri has a taproot system that allows it to survive in dry conditions by accessing deep water sources.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptation to harsh desert climates and its role in local ecosystems.
