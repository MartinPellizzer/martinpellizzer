Mariscus meyenianus

Mariscus meyenianus, commonly known as the desert marigold, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as hairy leaves to reduce water loss. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant has a deep taproot system, allowing it to survive in poor soils and extreme temperatures.
