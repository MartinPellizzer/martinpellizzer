Moacroton lanceolatus var. longifolius

Moacroton lanceolatus var. longifolius, commonly known as the longleaf moacroton, is a species of flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
