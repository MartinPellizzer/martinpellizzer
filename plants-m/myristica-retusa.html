Myristica retusa

Myristica retusa, commonly known as the nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Banda Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody structure and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by a spherical fruit that contains the valuable spice nutmeg. Its seeds, known as nutmeg, are enclosed in a red, fleshy aril called mace, which is also used as a spice.

The plant is cultivated for its aromatic oils and spices, which have been historically prized for their culinary and medicinal properties.
