Mycetia holotricha

Mycetia holotricha, commonly known as the hairy mycetia, is a climbing shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its dense covering of fine, hairy trichomes on its stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences.

Mycetia holotricha is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
