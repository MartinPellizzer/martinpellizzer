Mandioca dulcis

Mandioca dulcis, commonly known as cassava, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is cultivated for its starchy root, which is a major food source in many regions. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves and produces a woody stem. Its root contains cyanogenic glycosides, which must be processed to remove toxins.

The plant is also valued for its adaptability to various soil types and climates.
