Malva cretica subsp. cretica

Malva cretica subsp.

cretica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus.

The plant is characterized by its slender stems, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and roadside verges.

The species is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
