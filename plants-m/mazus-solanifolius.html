Mazus solanifolius

Mazus solanifolius, commonly known as the purple mazus, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, ovate leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white to purple bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale purple to deep violet. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Mazus solanifolius is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
