Miconia edentula

Miconia edentula, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries that contain numerous seeds. Miconia edentula is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed habitats, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a challenge for land managers.
