Mitracarpus bicrucis

Mitracarpus bicrucis, commonly known as the Mexican coffee bean plant, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, kidney-shaped seeds that are encased in a hard, woody pod. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Despite its ornamental value, it is also noted for its potential use in traditional medicine.
