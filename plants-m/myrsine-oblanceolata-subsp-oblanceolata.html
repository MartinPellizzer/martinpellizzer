Myrsine oblanceolata subsp. oblanceolata

Myrsine oblanceolata subsp.

oblanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf myrsine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific. It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters.

The plant has simple, opposite, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green on the upper surface. Its small, white flowers are clustered in terminal panicles, and it produces small, fleshy, orange-red berries that are edible.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and fruit.
