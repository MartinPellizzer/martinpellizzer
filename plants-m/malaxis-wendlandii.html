Malaxis wendlandii

Malaxis wendlandii, commonly known as the Wendland's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has a rhizomatous root system. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
