Mollugo bellidifolia

Mollugo bellidifolia, commonly known as the yellow bladderwort, is a small, flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Molluginaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and fleshy, succulent stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with four petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
