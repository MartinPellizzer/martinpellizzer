Melastoma diffusum

Melastoma diffusum, commonly known as the spreading melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that typically range in color from pink to red. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Melastoma diffusum is also known for its ability to form dense thickets, which can impact local ecosystems.
