Maharanga vestita

Maharanga vestita, commonly known as the white maharanga, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive white, fluffy flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets, and the stems are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.
