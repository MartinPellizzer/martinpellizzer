Melilotoides siunica

Melilotoides siunica, commonly known as the Sicilian melilot, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily, and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny areas such as rocky slopes and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
