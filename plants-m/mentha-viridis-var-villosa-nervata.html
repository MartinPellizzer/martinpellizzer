Mentha viridis var. villosa-nervata

Mentha viridis var. villosa-nervata, commonly known as the hairy-veined mint, is a variant of the green mint species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The leaves are typically a vibrant green, with prominent, vein-like structures that give it its specific epithet. It produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, which are typical of the mint family.

This variety is often grown for its aromatic foliage and is used in herbal teas and culinary applications.
