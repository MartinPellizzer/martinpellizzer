Myrcia brandamii

Myrcia brandamii, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that contains a single seed.

Myrcia brandamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
