Morinda pulchella

Morinda pulchella, commonly known as the "Beautiful Morinda," is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in subtropical gardens.

Morinda pulchella is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
