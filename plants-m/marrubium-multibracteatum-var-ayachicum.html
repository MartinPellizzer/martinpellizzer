Marrubium multibracteatum var. ayachicum

Marrubium multibracteatum var. ayachicum, commonly known as the Ayachic mountain horehound, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing a compact, shrub-like form. The plant features gray-green, hairy leaves that are opposite or whorled along the stems. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and arranged in dense spikes, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
