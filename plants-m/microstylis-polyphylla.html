Microstylis polyphylla

Microstylis polyphylla, commonly known as the many-leaved microstylis, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of eastern North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, often in marshy areas along the edges of ponds and lakes. The plant has a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 10 centimeters in length. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Microstylis polyphylla plays a role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for aquatic insects and contributing to nutrient cycling.
