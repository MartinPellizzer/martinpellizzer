Myrcia huallagae

Myrcia huallagae, commonly known as the Huallaga myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Amazon rainforests of Peru and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces elliptical, woody fruits that contain several seeds. Myrcia huallagae is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its aromatic properties.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
