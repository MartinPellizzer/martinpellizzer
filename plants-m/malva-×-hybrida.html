Malva × hybrida

Malva × hybrida, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a hybrid species within the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically velvety in texture. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, purple, or white, often with a distinctive dark center. It is a perennial shrub that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
