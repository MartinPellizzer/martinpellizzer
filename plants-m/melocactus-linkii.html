Melocactus linkii

Melocactus linkii, commonly known as the Pencil Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a slow-growing cactus with a distinctive cylindrical shape and a smooth, green stem. The plant features prominent areoles that bear clusters of white, fluffy spines and sometimes flowers. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Melocactus linkii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
