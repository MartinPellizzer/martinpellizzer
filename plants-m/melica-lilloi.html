Melica lilloi

Melica lilloi, commonly known as Lillo's milletgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often tinged with red or purple, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Melica lilloi is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
