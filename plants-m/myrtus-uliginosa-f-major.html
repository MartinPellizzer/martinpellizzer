Myrtus uliginosa f. major

Myrtus uliginosa f. major, commonly known as the greater bog myrtle, is a shrub native to wetland habitats in northern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant thrives in acidic, waterlogged soils and is often found in peat bogs and marshes. Its compact growth habit and dense foliage make it a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.

It is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
