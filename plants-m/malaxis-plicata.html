Malaxis plicata

Malaxis plicata, commonly known as the pleated orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, pleated leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the spring or early summer. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Malaxis plicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
