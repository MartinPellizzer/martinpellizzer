Myristica cuspidata var. globifera

Myristica cuspidata var. globifera, commonly known as the globeflower nutmeg, is a member of the Myristicaceae family.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical, leathery leaves. Its most distinctive feature is the spherical, fleshy fruit that encloses the aromatic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
