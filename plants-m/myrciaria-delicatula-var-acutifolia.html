Myrciaria delicatula var. acutifolia

Myrciaria delicatula var. acutifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *cereja-brasileira*, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, red, cherry-like fruits that are edible. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated margin, and it produces small, white, fragrant flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses, particularly in local cuisine.
