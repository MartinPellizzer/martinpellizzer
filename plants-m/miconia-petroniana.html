Miconia petroniana

Miconia petroniana, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into fleshy, star-shaped fruits. Its rapid growth rate and ability to form dense thickets make it an invasive species in some regions.

The fruit is edible, though its flavor is often described as bland or slightly acidic.
