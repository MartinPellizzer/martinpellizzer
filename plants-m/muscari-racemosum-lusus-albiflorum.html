Muscari racemosum lusus albiflorum

Muscari racemosum lusus albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered grape hyacinth, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. The plant produces strap-like leaves that are grassy in appearance and emerge from a bulbous base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This cultivar is prized for its early spring blooms and is often used in ornamental horticulture.
