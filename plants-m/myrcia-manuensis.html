Myrcia manuensis

Myrcia manuensis, commonly known as the Manu myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the rainforests of South America, particularly in regions of Peru and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several small, black seeds.

Myrcia manuensis is often found in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
