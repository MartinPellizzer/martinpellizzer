Monoon oblongifolium

Monoon oblongifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved monoon, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, which bloom in the late afternoon. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Monoon oblongifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
