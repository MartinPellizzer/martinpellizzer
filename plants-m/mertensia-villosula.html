Mertensia villosula

Mertensia villosula, commonly known as the hairy bluebells, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its attractive, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Mertensia villosula is often found in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
