Merremia pinnata

Merremia pinnata, commonly known as the climbing morning glory, is a vigorous vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it both a popular garden plant and a potential invasive species in some areas.
