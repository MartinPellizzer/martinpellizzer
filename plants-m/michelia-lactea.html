Michelia lactea

Michelia lactea, commonly known as the white-flowered michelia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, dark green upper surface. Michelia lactea thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
