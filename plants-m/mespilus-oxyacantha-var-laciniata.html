Mespilus oxyacantha var. laciniata

Mespilus oxyacantha var. laciniata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackthorn, is a variant of the blackthorn species native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorned stems and small, dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The leaves are typically narrow and lobed, giving the plant a distinctive, somewhat shrub-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance, providing food for birds and insects.
