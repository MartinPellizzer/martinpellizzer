Mattiastrum himalayense var. fallax

Mattiastrum himalayense var. fallax, commonly known as the Himalayan false dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically grayish-green with a fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
