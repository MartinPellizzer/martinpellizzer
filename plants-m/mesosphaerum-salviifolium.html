Mesosphaerum salviifolium

Mesosphaerum salviifolium, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive blue-violet flower heads that resemble small clouds. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Mesosphaerum salviifolium is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
