Myristica microcephala

Myristica microcephala, commonly known as the Nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and fragrant flowers. The plant produces a unique fruit that contains the aromatic nutmeg seed and mace, both of which are highly valued spices. Its growth is slow, and it requires a humid, warm climate with well-drained soil to thrive.

The tree's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and commercial contexts.
