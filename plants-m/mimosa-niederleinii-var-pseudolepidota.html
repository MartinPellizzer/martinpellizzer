Mimosa niederleinii var. pseudolepidota

Mimosa niederleinii var. pseudolepidota, commonly known as the silver mimosa, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, fluffy pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
