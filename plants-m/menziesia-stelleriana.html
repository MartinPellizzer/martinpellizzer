Menziesia stelleriana

Menziesia stelleriana, commonly known as Steller's menziesia, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded forest environments. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a glossy surface and remain evergreen throughout the year.

Menziesia stelleriana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in cool, humid climates.
