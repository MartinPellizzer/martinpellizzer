Medinilla inaequalis

Medinilla inaequalis, commonly known as the unequal medinilla, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The flowers are typically pink or white, with a unique structure that features a prominent, hood-like bract. This plant thrives in humid, shady environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
