Matricaria lacustris

Matricaria lacustris, commonly known as the lake chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its yellow flower heads and white ray florets. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils near lakes and wetlands, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are aromatic and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Matricaria lacustris is often used in traditional medicine for its calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
