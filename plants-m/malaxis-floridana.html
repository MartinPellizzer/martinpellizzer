Malaxis floridana

Malaxis floridana, commonly known as the Florida malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as pine flatwoods and wetlands. The plant has a slender, wiry stem with a single, lance-shaped leaf. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and lack a distinct scent, which is typical for many orchids in the Malaxis genus.

It is a rare and endangered species, requiring specific habitat conditions to thrive.
