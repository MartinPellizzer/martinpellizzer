Miconia longicuspis

Miconia longicuspis, commonly known as the long-spined miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter, often with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Miconia longicuspis is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its ecological impact is significant due to its ability to outcompete native plants and alter local ecosystems.
