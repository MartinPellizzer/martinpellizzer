Malus formosana

Malus formosana, commonly known as the Formosan apple or Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy white flowers in spring. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or red in color. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth margin.

Malus formosana is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.
