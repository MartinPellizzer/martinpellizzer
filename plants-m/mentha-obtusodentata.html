Mentha obtusodentata

Mentha obtusodentata, commonly known as broad-toothed mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, square stems and opposite, ovate leaves with rounded teeth. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Mentha obtusodentata is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
