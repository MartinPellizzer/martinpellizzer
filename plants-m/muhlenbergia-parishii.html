Muhlenbergia parishii

Muhlenbergia parishii, commonly known as the parishii muhlenbergia, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale blue flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.

Muhlenbergia parishii is valued for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal in arid landscapes.
