Mimosa bellatrix

Mimosa bellatrix, commonly known as the Mexican sensitive plant, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its root system is capable of nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil enrichment.

The plant is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
