Morella javanica

Morella javanica, commonly known as the Javan candlewood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its elongated, candle-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers followed by woody, cone-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited commercial use in certain regions.
