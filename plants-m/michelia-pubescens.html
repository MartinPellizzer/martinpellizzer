Michelia pubescens

Michelia pubescens, commonly known as the hairy magnolia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Michelia pubescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
