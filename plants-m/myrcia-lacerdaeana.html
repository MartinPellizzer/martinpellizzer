Myrcia lacerdaeana

Myrcia lacerdaeana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes other well-known plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Myrcia lacerdaeana is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
