Marrubium hyperleucum var. comosum

Marrubium hyperleucum var. comosum, commonly known as the white horehound, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The leaves are lance-shaped, gray-green in color, and have a slightly fuzzy texture. Its flowers are small, white or pale blue, and are arranged in clustered inflorescences.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
