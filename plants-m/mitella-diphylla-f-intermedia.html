Mitella diphylla f. intermedia

Mitella diphylla f. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate twinflower, is a rare and attractive perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-lipped, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable species for ecological and botanical study.
