Myrtus leucadendra

Myrtus leucadendra, commonly known as white myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, leathery leaves that are typically dark green with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Myrtus leucadendra is also valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
