Mauria aurantiodora

Mauria aurantiodora, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its rapid growth rate and showy blooms make it a notable species in botanical gardens and urban environments.
