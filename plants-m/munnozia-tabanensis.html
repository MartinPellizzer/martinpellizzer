Munnozia tabanensis

Munnozia tabanensis, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow, dandelion-like flowers that bloom in spring. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions.

The plant is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and is often found in rocky soils.
