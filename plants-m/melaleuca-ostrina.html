Melaleuca ostrina

Melaleuca ostrina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and striking red or pink bottlebrush-like flowers that appear in spring. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. Melaleuca ostrina is often found in coastal heathlands and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in horticulture.
