Myrcianthes fragrans var. hispidula

Myrcianthes fragrans var. hispidula, commonly known as the hairy myrcianthes, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in humid lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage, which gives it its varietal name "hispidula.". Its leaves are usually elliptical with a glossy surface and emit a mild, aromatic scent.

The flowers are small and clustered, producing fragrant, white to pale yellow blooms that attract pollinators like bees and birds.
