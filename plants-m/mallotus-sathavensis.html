Mallotus sathavensis

Mallotus sathavensis, commonly known as the Sathavane tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves with elliptical leaflets. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by woody, elongated capsules containing numerous seeds.

It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in lowland forests and is valued for its hard, heavy wood used in construction and furniture.
