Myrcia laruotteana var. macrophylla

Myrcia laruotteana var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved myrcia, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
