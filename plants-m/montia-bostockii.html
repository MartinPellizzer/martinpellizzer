Montia bostockii

Montia bostockii, commonly known as Bostock's speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Montiaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the spring, typically on slender stems. Its leaves are narrow, elongated, and often covered in fine hairs.

Montia bostockii is adapted to rocky and sandy soils, often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
