Mammillaria glareosa

Mammillaria glareosa, commonly known as the bright milk barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white to pale green tubercles. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appearance, it is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
