Myosotis laxa

Myosotis laxa, commonly known as the loose bluebell, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, sky-blue flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a soft, hairy texture.

Myosotis laxa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
