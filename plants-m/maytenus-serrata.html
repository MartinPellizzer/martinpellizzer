Maytenus serrata

Maytenus serrata, commonly known as the serrated mayten, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland tropical rainforests.

Maytenus serrata is valued for its hard, durable wood and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
