Machilus shiwandashanicus

Machilus shiwandashanicus, commonly known as the Shiwanda mountain laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel, avocado, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

The fruit is a small, dark berry that contains several seeds.
