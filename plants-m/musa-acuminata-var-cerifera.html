Musa acuminata var. cerifera

Musa acuminata var. cerifera, commonly known as the banana plant, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and produces clusters of fruit known as hands. The plant is a herbaceous perennial that can grow up to 10 feet tall, with a pseudostem formed by tightly packed leaf sheaths. Its flowers are typically hidden within the fruit clusters, and the plant is cultivated for its edible fruit.

The variety is notable for its high yield and adaptability to various tropical climates.
