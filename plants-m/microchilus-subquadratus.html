Microchilus subquadratus

Microchilus subquadratus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rock orchid, is a small terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in open woodland or grassland habitats. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in a raceme, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

It is a rare and specialized species, adapted to specific environmental conditions.
