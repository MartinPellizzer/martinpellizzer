Myricaria davurica var. macrophylla

Myricaria davurica var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved tamarisk, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in harsh, saline environments. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the summer. Its root system is extensive and helps it survive in dry conditions by accessing deep water sources.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological resilience in challenging climates.
