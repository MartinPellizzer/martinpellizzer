Maruta cotula var. lithuanica

Maruta cotula var. lithuanica, commonly known as Lithuanian groundsel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically features small, daisy-like yellow flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats. It is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in certain regions.
