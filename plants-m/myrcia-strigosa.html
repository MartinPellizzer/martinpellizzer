Myrcia strigosa

Myrcia strigosa, commonly known as the rough myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is part of the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves that are covered in a dense layer of short, stiff hairs. It produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers, which are followed by small, woody berries.

Myrcia strigosa is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
