Mentha longifolia subsp. bureschiana

Mentha longifolia subsp.

bureschiana, commonly known as the alpine mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It features slender, aromatic leaves that are typically opposite and lanceolate in shape.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters along the stem. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and grasslands.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
