Marantopsis

Marantopsis, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Marantaceae.

These plants are characterized by their striking, often colorful foliage, which can range from deep red to purple and green. They are native to tropical regions of the Americas and are typically found in moist, shaded environments. Marantopsis species are herbaceous perennials that produce small, inconspicuous flowers, often in clusters.

Their ornamental value lies primarily in their vibrant, textured leaves, making them popular in horticulture.
