Myrica florida

Myrica florida, commonly known as the sweetgum or Florida sweetgum, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and its distinctive, star-shaped leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into spiky, spherical fruit clusters. Its fruit, called a "gum," contains seeds and is a food source for various birds and small mammals.

Myrica florida is also valued for its fast growth and adaptability to a variety of soil types.
