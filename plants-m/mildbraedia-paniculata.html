Mildbraedia paniculata

Mildbraedia paniculata, commonly known as the silver leaf tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves that are covered in a silvery, waxy coating, which helps reflect sunlight and reduce water loss. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in panicles, which are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
