Macroglena asae-grayi

Macroglena asae-grayi, commonly known as the gray's giant sponge.

It is a unique species of sponge-like plant that exhibits a complex structure with a spongy, porous body. The plant is characterized by its ability to absorb and retain water, which allows it to thrive in moist environments. Its botanical features include a lack of true roots, stems, and leaves, and it relies on its porous structure for nutrient uptake.

This species is primarily found in tropical and subtropical regions where humidity is high.
