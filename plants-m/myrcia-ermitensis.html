Myrcia ermitensis

Myrcia ermitensis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, fragrant leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for horticultural use.

The fruit is a small, round berry that contains several seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
