Muehlenbeckia adpressa

Muehlenbeckia adpressa, commonly known as silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It features wiry, trailing stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The leaves are small, ovate, and covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving the plant its characteristic appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers in late spring to early summer.

This plant is adapted to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
