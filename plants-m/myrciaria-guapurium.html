Myrciaria guapurium

Myrciaria guapurium, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or guapuruma, is a small evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, edible berries. These berries are rich in vitamin C and are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a significant species in tropical ecosystems.
