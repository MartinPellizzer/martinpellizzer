Markea internexa

Markea internexa, commonly known as the silverleaf lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that reflect light, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, moist soil in its natural habitat.

Markea internexa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and air-purifying properties.
