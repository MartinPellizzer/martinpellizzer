Myrciaria arborea

Myrciaria arborea, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá-do-brejo, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Myrciaria arborea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
