Myrcia alpestris

Myrcia alpestris, commonly known as the alpine myrtle, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in the Andes.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, thriving in cool, humid conditions. Its berries are small and often used in traditional medicine.

Myrcia alpestris plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing food for various wildlife species.
