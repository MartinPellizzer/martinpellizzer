Monsonia lobata

Monsonia lobata, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, waxy leaves and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to white. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb, often forming dense mats or small shrubs. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by birds and insects, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Monsonia lobata is popular as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and long blooming period.
