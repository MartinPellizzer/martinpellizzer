Maianthemum formosanum

Maianthemum formosanum, commonly known as the Formosan false lily of the valley, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Convallariaceae and is characterized by its dense, low-growing clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant has strap-like, dark green leaves that are often described as resembling those of lily of the valley. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Maianthemum formosanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
