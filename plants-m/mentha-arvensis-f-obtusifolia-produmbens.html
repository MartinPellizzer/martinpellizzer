Mentha arvensis f. obtusifolia-produmbens

Mentha arvensis f. obtusifolia-produmbens, commonly known as the broad-leaved field mint, is a variant of the field mint species characterized by its broad, rounded leaves and a distinct, aromatic scent.

This subspecies is native to parts of Europe and Asia, often found in moist, grassy areas and along stream banks. Its leaves are typically arranged oppositely on the stems, and it produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in clusters. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, including antiseptic and carminative effects.

It is often used in herbal teas and traditional remedies for digestive issues.
