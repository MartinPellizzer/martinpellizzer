Magnolia citrata

Magnolia citrata, commonly known as the lemon magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The tree has smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its flowers are typically white to pale yellow and are borne in clusters at the base of the branches.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive floral fragrance.
