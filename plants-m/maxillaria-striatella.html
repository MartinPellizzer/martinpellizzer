Maxillaria striatella

Maxillaria striatella, commonly known as the striated maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces fragrant, showy flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically features long, narrow leaves and has a pseudobulb structure typical of many orchids. Its flowers are usually pink or purple with contrasting white or yellow markings.

Maxillaria striatella thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
