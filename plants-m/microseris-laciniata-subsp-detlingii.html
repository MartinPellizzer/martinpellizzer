Microseris laciniata subsp. detlingii

Microseris laciniata subsp.

detlingii, commonly known as the Detling's cutleaf tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant features finely divided, serrated leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of short, soft hairs. Its flowers are yellow and are arranged in clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe.
