Mussaenda stadmanii

Mussaenda stadmanii, commonly known as the golden bell plant, is a flowering shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, giving it a striking appearance. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Mussaenda stadmanii is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
