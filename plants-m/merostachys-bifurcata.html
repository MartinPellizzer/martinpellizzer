Merostachys bifurcata

Merostachys bifurcata, commonly known as the bifurcate grass, is a member of the Poaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with two-branched clusters, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
