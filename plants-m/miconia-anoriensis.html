Miconia anoriensis

Miconia anoriensis, commonly known as the Anoriensis miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers with five petals, which are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Miconia anoriensis is noted for its ecological importance in providing habitat and food for various rainforest species.
