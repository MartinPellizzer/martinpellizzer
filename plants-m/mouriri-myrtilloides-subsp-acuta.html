Mouriri myrtilloides subsp. acuta

Mouriri myrtilloides subsp.

acuta, commonly known as the sharp-leaved mouriri, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil. It is a small evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its elliptical, acuminate leaves.

The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are often consumed by birds and mammals. Its leaves are typically glossy and have a distinct, sharp-pointed tip, which gives it its subspecies name.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
