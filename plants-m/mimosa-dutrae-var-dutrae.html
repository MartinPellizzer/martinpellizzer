Mimosa dutrae var. dutrae

Mimosa dutrae var. dutrae, commonly known as the Brazilian sensitive plant, is a species native to Brazil.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched, a trait known as thigmonastic response. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique foliage and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
