Mentha aquatica f. macrophylla

Mentha aquatica f. macrophylla, commonly known as the broad-leaf water mint, is a variant of the water mint plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating in water. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in wetland environments, including ponds, streams, and marshes.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
