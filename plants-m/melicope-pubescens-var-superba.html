Melicope pubescens var. superba

Melicope pubescens var. superba, commonly known as the glossy tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn from green to yellowish when mature. Its bark is thick and often covered in a whitish, powdery substance.

It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appearance and traditional uses.
