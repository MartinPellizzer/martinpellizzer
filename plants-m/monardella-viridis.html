Monardella viridis

Monardella viridis, commonly known as green mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features upright stems covered in fine, white hairs and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and meadows.

Monardella viridis is valued for its aromatic foliage and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
