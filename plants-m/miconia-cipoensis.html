Miconia cipoensis

Miconia cipoensis, commonly known as the Cipoensis miconia, is a tropical shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a problematic species in certain regions.
