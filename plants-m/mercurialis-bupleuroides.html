Mercurialis bupleuroides

Mercurialis bupleuroides, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Solanaceae.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in the early morning, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, rounded seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Mercurialis bupleuroides is native to parts of Europe and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.
