Mesynium

Mesynium, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces tiny, blue or white flowers that are clustered at the ends of its stems. Mesynium is typically found in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas.

It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
