Momordica rumphii

Momordica rumphii, commonly known as the bitter melon or karela, is a tropical vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, warty, green fruits that turn orange when mature. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Its fruits are widely used in Asian cuisine and traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
