Myrothamnus

Myrothamnus, commonly known as the African ice plant, is a unique succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing stems and leaves, which help it survive in arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. Myrothamnus is known for its ability to survive extreme drought conditions by entering a state of dormancy.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in xerophytic plant adaptations.
