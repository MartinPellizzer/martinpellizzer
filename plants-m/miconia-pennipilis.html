Miconia pennipilis

Miconia pennipilis, commonly known as the red miconia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are bright red berries, which contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy pulp.

Miconia pennipilis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and showy flowers.
