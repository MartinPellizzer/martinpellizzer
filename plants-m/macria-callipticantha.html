Macria callipticantha

Macria callipticantha, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a striking flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces numerous small florets arranged in a composite head. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in Mediterranean climates.

Macria callipticantha is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
