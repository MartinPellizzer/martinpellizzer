Melaleuca quadrifida subsp. homalophylla

Melaleuca quadrifida subsp.

homalophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and striking red or pink bottlebrush flowers that bloom in winter.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands and open forests.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including honeyeaters and bees.
