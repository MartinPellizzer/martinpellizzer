Malabaila opoponax

Malabaila opoponax, commonly known as the Indian myrrh, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of India and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, resinous bark and leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by yellowish fruits containing seeds. Its botanical features include a smooth, grayish-brown trunk and elliptical, dark green leaves.

The tree is valued for its medicinal properties and is often cultivated for its aromatic resins.
