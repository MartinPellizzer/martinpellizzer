Mentha candicans

Mentha candicans, commonly known as the white mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its aromatic leaves emit a fresh, minty scent when crushed, making it popular in culinary and medicinal applications.

Mentha candicans thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal environments.
