Melocactus paucispinus

Melocactus paucispinus, commonly known as the spiny pear cactus, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem that often develops a swollen base over time. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers during the spring and summer months. Its spines are typically short and dense, providing protection from herbivores.

Melocactus paucispinus is well adapted to arid environments, requiring minimal water and thriving in full sun.
