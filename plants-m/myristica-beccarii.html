Myristica beccarii

Myristica beccarii, commonly known as the Bornean nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody fruits that contain the spice nutmeg. The plant produces a unique seed with a hard, inner kernel that is highly valued for its aromatic properties. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

The tree is often cultivated for its economic value in the spice trade.
