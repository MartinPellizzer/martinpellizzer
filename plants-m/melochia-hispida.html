Melochia hispida

Melochia hispida, commonly known as the rough melochia, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Melochia hispida is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
