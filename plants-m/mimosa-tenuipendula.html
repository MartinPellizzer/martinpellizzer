Mimosa tenuipendula

Mimosa tenuipendula, commonly known as the sensitive plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. It is a perennial shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various conditions make it both a useful forage plant and an invasive species in some regions.
