Miconia acuminoides

Miconia acuminoides, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or red miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.

Miconia acuminoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and showy flowers.
