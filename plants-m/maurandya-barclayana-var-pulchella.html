Maurandya barclayana var. pulchella

Maurandya barclayana var. pulchella, commonly known as the pink-flowered maurandya, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to lavender, often with a contrasting white or yellow throat. Its foliage is typically heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white pubescence.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for container gardening and hanging baskets.
