Messerschmidia syringifolia

Messerschmidia syringifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf scabious, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, slender stems covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
