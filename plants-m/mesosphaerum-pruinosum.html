Mesosphaerum pruinosum

Mesosphaerum pruinosum, commonly known as the glossy buckthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny growth and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
