Macrolobium crassifolium

Macrolobium crassifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf macrolobium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, thick, and leathery leaves, which are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

Due to its unique foliage and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
