Melastoma aculeolatum

Melastoma aculeolatum, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers, which are followed by orange-red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
