Mimulus nelsonii

Mimulus nelsonii, commonly known as Nelson's monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright orange to yellow flowers, which are typically tubular in shape and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are often lance-shaped, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is a member of the Phrymaceae family, which includes other species of monkeyflowers.
