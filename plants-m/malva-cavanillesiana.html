Malva cavanillesiana

Malva cavanillesiana, commonly known as the Mexican mallows, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in moist, open habitats.

Malva cavanillesiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
