Melothria guamensis

Melothria guamensis, commonly known as the Guam cucumber, is a unique species native to the island of Guam.

It is a trailing vine that produces small, edible fruits resembling cucumbers in appearance. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and the flowers are yellow with five petals.

The fruit is typically harvested when it turns a pale greenish-yellow, and it is valued for its mild, refreshing flavor.
