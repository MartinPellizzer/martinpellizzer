Monotropa uniflora f. rosea

Monotropa uniflora f. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered ghostplant, is a rare variant of the ghostplant species native to the eastern United States.

This plant is a hemiparasitic species, meaning it partially relies on other plants for water and nutrients through its root system. Its most distinctive feature is its pale pink to rose-colored flowers, which contrast with its white, leafless stems. It thrives in shaded, moist forest environments, often growing in association with host trees like oaks and maples.

The plant's unique appearance and ecological role make it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
