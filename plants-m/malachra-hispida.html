Malachra hispida

Malachra hispida, commonly known as the roughleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun. Its scientific name reflects its distinctive leaf structure, with "hispida" meaning rough or bristly in Latin.

Malachra hispida is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.
