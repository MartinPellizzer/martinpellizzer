Mammillaria persicina

Mammillaria persicina, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, spineless cactus with a globular shape and typically measures up to 10 centimeters in height. The plant features white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its fleshy, ribbed stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact size and attractive appearance.
