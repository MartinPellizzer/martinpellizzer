Marlierea guildingiana

Marlierea guildingiana, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendant-like yellow flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in clusters. The plant is a climbing vine that produces showy, fragrant blooms throughout the growing season. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Marlierea guildingiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
