Myrtus communis f. lusitanica

Myrtus communis f. lusitanica, commonly known as the Lusitanian myrtle, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically dark green with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces tiny, white or pinkish flowers in spring, followed by small, edible berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its ornamental value and fragrance make it a popular choice in Mediterranean landscapes.
