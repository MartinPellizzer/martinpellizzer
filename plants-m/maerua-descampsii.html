Maerua descampsii

Maerua descampsii, commonly known as the African saltbush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of salt crystals. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in saline soils.

Maerua descampsii is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
