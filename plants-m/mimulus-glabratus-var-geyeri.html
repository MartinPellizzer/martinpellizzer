Mimulus glabratus var. geyeri

Mimulus glabratus var. geyeri, commonly known as the Geyeri's monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange or yellow flowers, which are typically tubular in shape and attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in dry, rocky habitats.

This variety is known for its ecological importance in supporting local pollinator populations and its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
