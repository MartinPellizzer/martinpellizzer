Matisia dolichosiphon

Matisia dolichosiphon, commonly known as the long-sheath matisia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes trees and shrubs characterized by their aromatic bark and often compound leaves. The plant typically grows as a tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters, with a dense canopy and smooth, greyish bark. Its flowers are small and clustered, often yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces woody capsules as fruits.

The leaves are usually alternate and pinnate, contributing to its ornamental value in tropical gardens.
