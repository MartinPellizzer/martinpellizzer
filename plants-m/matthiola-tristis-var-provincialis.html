Matthiola tristis var. provincialis

Matthiola tristis var. provincialis, commonly known as the Italian wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually clustered in racemes and bloom from late spring to early summer.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and has a long history of cultivation in Mediterranean regions.
