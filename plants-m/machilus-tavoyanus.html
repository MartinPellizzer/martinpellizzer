Machilus tavoyanus

Machilus tavoyanus, commonly known as the Tavoyan laurel, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant, small white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Machilus tavoyanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
