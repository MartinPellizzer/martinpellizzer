Monelasmum buchneri

Monelasmum buchneri, commonly known as the slender mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that primarily grows on the branches of host trees in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are often clustered together, followed by small, berry-like fruits. It relies on its host for water and nutrients but can photosynthesize to some extent.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can sometimes cause stress or damage to its host tree.
