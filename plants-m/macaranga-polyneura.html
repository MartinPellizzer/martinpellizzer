Macaranga polyneura

Macaranga polyneura, commonly known as the white-veined macaranga, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent white veins. The plant is typically found in lowland rainforests and thrives in moist, shaded environments. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil.
