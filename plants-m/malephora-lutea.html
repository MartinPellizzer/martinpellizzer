Malephora lutea

Malephora lutea, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow petals and bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Malephora lutea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
