Magnolia figo var. crassipes

Magnolia figo var. crassipes, commonly known as the broad-leaf magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth texture and a glossy surface. The tree produces small, round fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
