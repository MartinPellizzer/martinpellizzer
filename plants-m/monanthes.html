Monanthes

Monanthes, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and elusive plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, translucent petals that appear to glow under certain light conditions. The plant has a short blooming period, typically at night, which contributes to its mysterious reputation. Monanthes has no known medicinal uses but is highly valued by botanists for its unusual morphology.

Its survival is threatened by habitat destruction, making it a symbol of endangered flora.
