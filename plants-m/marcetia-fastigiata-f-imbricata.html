Marcetia fastigiata f. imbricata

Marcetia fastigiata f. imbricata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boxwood, is a compact, slow-growing evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Europe.

It features dense, upright growth with narrow, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in a tight, layered fashion. The plant has a naturally pyramidal shape, making it ideal for topiary and formal garden designs. Its foliage remains lush and vibrant throughout the year, requiring minimal pruning to maintain its structure.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, exhibiting good resistance to pests and diseases.
