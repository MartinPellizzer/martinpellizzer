Masdevallia fosterae

Masdevallia fosterae, commonly known as Foster's masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, often colorful floral structure and is typically found growing in shaded, humid environments. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the inflorescence. Its leaves are usually elongated and grass-like, adapted to its epiphytic growth habit.

Masdevallia fosterae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique botanical features.
