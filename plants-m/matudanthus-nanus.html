Matudanthus nanus

Matudanthus nanus, commonly known as the dwarf matudanthus, is a small, herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
