Momordica racemosa

Momordica racemosa, commonly known as the horned melon or African horned melon, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It produces distinctive, spiky, green fruits that resemble small horns, with a thick, waxy rind. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible, sweet-tart fruit.

The botanical features of Momordica racemosa make it a unique and visually striking member of the Cucurbitaceae family.
