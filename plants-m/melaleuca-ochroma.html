Melaleuca ochroma

Melaleuca ochroma, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paperbark, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its smooth, flaky bark that peels away in thin layers, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a silvery or grayish underside.

Melaleuca ochroma produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers, which are clustered in dense spikes.
