Minkelersia galactioides

Minkelersia galactioides, commonly known as the Milky Milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its milky sap, which is a distinguishing botanical feature. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with five petals and numerous stamens.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its unique appearance.
