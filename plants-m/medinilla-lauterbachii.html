Medinilla lauterbachii

Medinilla lauterbachii, commonly known as the Christmas Medinilla, is a tropical shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Medinilla lauterbachii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
