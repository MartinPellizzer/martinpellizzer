Microchloa indica var. tenuis

Microchloa indica var. tenuis, commonly known as the slender microchloa, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments, often in tropical and subtropical regions. It has small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its role in certain ecological systems.
