Myrcia discolor

Myrcia discolor, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often consumed by birds and small mammals. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, including Florida, where it can become invasive.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
