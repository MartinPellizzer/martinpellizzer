Medicago sativa var. tripolitanica

Medicago sativa var. tripolitanica, commonly known as Turkish clover, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is well adapted to dry and rocky soils, making it a hardy species suitable for marginal lands. It is often used in forage and soil stabilization due to its deep root system and nitrogen-fixing ability.

The variety is distinguished by its compact growth habit and distinctive leaf morphology compared to other Medicago species.
