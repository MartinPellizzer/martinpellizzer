Miconia pulverulenta var. trifasciata

Miconia pulverulenta var. trifasciata, commonly known as the three-banded miconia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it its specific epithet "pulverulenta.". The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
