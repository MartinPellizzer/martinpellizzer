Myrcia kegeliana var. pendula

Myrcia kegeliana var. pendula, commonly known as the hanging myrcia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its pendulous, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a white or pale pink coloration. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Myrcia kegeliana var. pendula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
