Matthiola incana subsp. annua

Matthiola incana subsp.

annua, commonly known as annual stock, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its branched stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant has finely divided, linear leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and use in cut flower arrangements.

The seeds are small and typically dispersed by wind, aiding in its natural distribution.
