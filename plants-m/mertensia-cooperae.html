Mertensia cooperae

Mertensia cooperae, commonly known as Cooper's bluebells, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its scientific name honors the botanist John Cooper, who contributed to its discovery and classification.

Mertensia cooperae is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
