Mimulus floribundus var. subulatus

Mimulus floribundus var. subulatus, commonly known as the slender monkeyflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and bright orange or yellow tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and moist meadows. It produces numerous small, spurred flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
