Mentha arvicola

Mentha arvicola, commonly known as alpine mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color, arranged in dense spikes. Mentha arvicola thrives in cool, moist environments such as meadows and rocky slopes at high altitudes.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
