Myristica maxima

Myristica maxima, commonly known as the Nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, oily seeds that are used to produce nutmeg and mace. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy berries containing the aromatic seeds. The tree can grow up to 10 meters tall and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical significance lies in its essential oils and unique chemical composition, which have been valued for centuries in culinary and medicinal applications.
