Mangifera montana

Mangifera montana, commonly known as the mountain mango, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, yellow fruits that are typically eaten fresh or used in culinary applications. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is cultivated in various parts of its native range.
