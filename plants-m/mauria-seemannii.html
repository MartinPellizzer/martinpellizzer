Mauria seemannii

Mauria seemannii, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
