Myrcia loranthifolia

Myrcia loranthifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. Myrcia loranthifolia thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

It has a woody structure and is part of the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and guava.
