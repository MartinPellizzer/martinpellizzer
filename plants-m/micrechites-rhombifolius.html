Micrechites rhombifolius

Micrechites rhombifolius, commonly known as the rhombic rock cress, is a flowering plant species native to the rocky habitats of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, basal rosette of fleshy, rhomboid-shaped leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-white flowers during the cooler months, which are arranged in racemes along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky outcrops or gravelly areas.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
