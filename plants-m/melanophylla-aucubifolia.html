Melanophylla aucubifolia

Melanophylla aucubifolia, commonly known as the Japanese laurel, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in late spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and seasonal color changes.
