Maxillaria gracilis var. minor

Maxillaria gracilis var. minor, commonly known as the slender maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or branches, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its leaves are narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern, while its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
