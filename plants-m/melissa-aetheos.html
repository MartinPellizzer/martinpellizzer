Melissa aetheos

Melissa aetheos, commonly known as lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, serrated leaves that emit a citrus-like aroma. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Melissa aetheos is often cultivated for its calming properties and is used in aromatherapy and herbal medicine.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardens and medicinal applications.
