Mollugo lotoides

Mollugo lotoides, commonly known as the carpetweed, is a low-growing annual herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features prostrate or ascending stems that are often covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and usually covered with a fine layer of white pubescence.

Mollugo lotoides thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, lawns, and along roadsides.
