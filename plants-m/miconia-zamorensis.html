Miconia zamorensis

Miconia zamorensis, commonly known as the Zamora miconia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Miconia zamorensis is often found in humid lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
