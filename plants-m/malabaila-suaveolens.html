Malabaila suaveolens

Malabaila suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented malabaila, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its delicate foliage and pleasant aroma.
