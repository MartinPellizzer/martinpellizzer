Mentha longifolia f. szolnokensis

Mentha longifolia f. szolnokensis, commonly known as the Hungarian mint, is a subspecies of the common mint plant.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, pale purple flowers. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. It is native to Central Europe and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its essential oils are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine for their calming and digestive properties.
