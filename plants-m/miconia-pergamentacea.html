Miconia pergamentacea

Miconia pergamentacea, commonly known as the parchment miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers with five petals, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It is often found in humid lowland forests and is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine, and the plant is also noted for its ornamental value in horticulture.
