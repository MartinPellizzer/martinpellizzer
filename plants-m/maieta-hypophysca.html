Maieta hypophysca

Maieta hypophysca, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual herb with bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world.
