Milla maritima

Milla maritima, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and silver-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, thriving in sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Milla maritima is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate salt spray.
