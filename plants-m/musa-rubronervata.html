Musa rubronervata

Musa rubronervata, commonly known as the red-veined banana, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its large, broad leaves with distinctive red veins. The plant produces clusters of fruit that are typically elongated and have a yellow-orange color when ripe. It belongs to the Musaceae family and is closely related to other banana species.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its striking foliage rather than its fruit.
