Monardella linoides subsp. oblonga

Monardella linoides subsp.

oblonga, commonly known as narrowleaf mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive minty fragrance when crushed.

It produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
