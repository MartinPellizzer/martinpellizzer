Mentha × schultziana var. wohlwerthiana

Mentha × schultziana var. wohlwerthiana, commonly known as the Wohlwerth's peppermint, is a hybrid mint species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained soil. It is a perennial herb that can spread through rhizomes, forming dense mats.

Its essential oils, rich in menthol, make it valuable for both culinary and medicinal uses.
