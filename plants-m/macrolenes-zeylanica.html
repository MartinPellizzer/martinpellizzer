Macrolenes zeylanica

Macrolenes zeylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant wood that is highly valued in the perfume and cosmetics industries. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

The tree is also known for its slow growth and is considered a rare and endangered species due to overharvesting and habitat loss.
