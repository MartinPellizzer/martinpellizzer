Melica minuta var. eligulata

Melica minuta var. eligulata, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and riverbanks. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
