Malaxis lindleyana

Malaxis lindleyana, commonly known as the Lindley's Malaxis, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps in water retention.

Malaxis lindleyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
