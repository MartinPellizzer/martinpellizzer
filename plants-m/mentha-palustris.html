Mentha palustris

Mentha palustris, commonly known as marsh mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, boggy soils and thrives in shaded environments. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white to pale blue flowers appear in whorled clusters during the summer months.

Mentha palustris is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
