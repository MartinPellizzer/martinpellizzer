Melastoma bananale

Melastoma bananale, commonly known as banana melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or red. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible and contain several seeds.

Melastoma bananale thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
