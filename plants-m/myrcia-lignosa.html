Myrcia lignosa

Myrcia lignosa, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain aromatic seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Myrcia lignosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in folk medicine.
