Maughania praecox var. robusta

Maughania praecox var. robusta, commonly known as the robust maughania, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy on the underside, and it thrives in well-drained, fertile soils. It is often found in shaded to semi-shaded environments within its natural habitat.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
