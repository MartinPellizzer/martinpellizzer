Malapoenna intermedia

Malapoenna intermedia, commonly known as the Malapoenna shrub, is a perennial plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, which are followed by capsule fruits containing minute seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or semi-arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
