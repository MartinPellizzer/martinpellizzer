Melocactus actinacanthus

Melocactus actinacanthus, commonly known as the spiny hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem covered in sharp, spiny tubercles that provide protection from herbivores. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the rainy season, which are typically short-lived but attract pollinators such as bees and moths. Its adaptations include a deep root system for water absorption and a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Melocactus actinacanthus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience in dry environments.
