Manihot esculenta var. domingensis

Manihot esculenta var. domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican cassava, is a variant of the cassava plant native to the Caribbean.

It is a herbaceous perennial shrub that produces large, heart-shaped leaves and develops tuberous roots as its primary edible part. The plant is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. Its roots are rich in starch and are a vital food source in many regions.

The plant also has a high potential for biofuel production due to its high biomass yield.
