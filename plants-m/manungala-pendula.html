Manungala pendula

Manungala pendula, commonly known as the hanging manungala, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous growth habit, with long, drooping stems that bear clusters of small, ornamental flowers. The plant's botanical features include lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green in color. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in botanical gardens and private collections.
