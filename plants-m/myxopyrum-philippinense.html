Myxopyrum philippinense

Myxopyrum philippinense, commonly known as the Philippine grass, is a perennial herb native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit with slender, cylindrical stems. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface and a distinct midrib.

Myxopyrum philippinense is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks.
