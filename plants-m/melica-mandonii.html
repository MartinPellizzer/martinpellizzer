Melica mandonii

Melica mandonii, commonly known as the Mandon's brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as slopes and gravelly soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Melica mandonii is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, contributing to the ecological diversity of its native environment.
