Miconia consimilis

Miconia consimilis, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a yellow, fleshy berry that contains numerous seeds.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is considered a problematic species in some regions.
