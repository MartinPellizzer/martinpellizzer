Montia howellii

Montia howellii, commonly known as Howell's montia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Montiaceae and is characterized by its small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Montia howellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
