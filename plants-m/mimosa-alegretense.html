Mimosa alegretense

Mimosa alegretense, commonly known as the Brazilian sensitive plant, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Mimosa alegretense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
