Morisonia longifolia

Morisonia longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf morisonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and wetlands. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often have a waxy coating.

Morisonia longifolia is part of the Onagraceae family, which includes other showy flowering plants.
