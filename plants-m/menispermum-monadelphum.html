Menispermum monadelphum

Menispermum monadelphum, commonly known as the American mandrake, is a perennial climbing plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, fleshy, orange-red berries. The plant contains various alkaloids, which have historically been used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found along stream banks and in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and chemical composition make it an intriguing subject in both botanical and pharmacological studies.
