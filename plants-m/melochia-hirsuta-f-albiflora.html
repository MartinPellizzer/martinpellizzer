Melochia hirsuta f. albiflora

Melochia hirsuta f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered velvetleaf, is a variant of the velvetleaf plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, broad leaves and striking white flowers that contrast with its green foliage. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red upon ripening. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a unique, tropical accent plant.
