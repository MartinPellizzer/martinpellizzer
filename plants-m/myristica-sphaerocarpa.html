Myristica sphaerocarpa

Myristica sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the nutmeg apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and produces distinctive yellow-orange fruits that resemble small apples. The plant is part of the Myristicaceae family and is closely related to the nutmeg tree, Myristica fragrans. Its fruits contain a single, large seed surrounded by a fleshy, aromatic aril.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
