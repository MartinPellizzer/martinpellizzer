Myrica microbracteata

Myrica microbracteata, commonly known as the dwarf wax myrtle, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Myrica microbracteata is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
