Marattia dregeana

Marattia dregeana, commonly known as the African holly fern, is a tropical evergreen fern native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the family Marattiaceae and is characterized by its large, leathery, bipinnate fronds that can grow up to 3 meters in length. The plant typically thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its rhizome is thick and fleshy, supporting the growth of its extensive fronds.

Marattia dregeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
