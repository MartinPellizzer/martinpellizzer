Metrosideros polymorpha f. calva

Metrosideros polymorpha f. calva, commonly known as the white mistletoe or white coral tree, is a variant of the common Hawaiian ohia tree.

It is characterized by its pale to white flowers and is native to the Hawaiian Islands. This form is often found in coastal and lowland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant is a small shrub or tree with smooth, reddish-barked stems and opposite leaves.

It plays an important ecological role in Hawaiian forests, providing nectar for native birds and insects.
