Mentha longifolia var. huegeliana

Mentha longifolia var. huegeliana, commonly known as the Hungarian mint, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and is often used in herbal teas and culinary applications.
