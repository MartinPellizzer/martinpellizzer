Meridiana jurineaifolia

Meridiana jurineaifolia, commonly known as the desert rose, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and striking, showy flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its leaves are typically small and opposite, and it produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that give way to woody pods containing seeds.

Meridiana jurineaifolia is a hardy, drought-tolerant species often cultivated for its ornamental value.
