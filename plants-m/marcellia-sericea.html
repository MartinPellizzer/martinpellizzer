Marcellia sericea

Marcellia sericea, commonly known as silky marcellia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and forest edges. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Marcellia sericea is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and blooms.
