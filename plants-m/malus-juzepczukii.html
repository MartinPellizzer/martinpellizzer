Malus juzepczukii

Malus juzepczukii, commonly known as the Juzepczuk apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Caucasus region and is characterized by its ornamental value due to its showy flowers and fruit. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, edible apples that are often used in local cuisines.

The species is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
