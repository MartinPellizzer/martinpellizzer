Medicago hispida var. apiculata

Medicago hispida var. apiculata, commonly known as the hairy vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit, trifoliate leaves, and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is notable for its extensive root system, which helps in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Medicago hispida var. apiculata is often used in ecological restoration and as a cover crop due to its hardiness and ability to improve soil fertility.
