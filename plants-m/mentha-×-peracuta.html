Mentha × peracuta

Mentha × peracuta, commonly known as hybrid mint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between spearmint (Mentha spicata) and watermint (Mentha aquatica).

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its square, erect stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. Mentha × peracuta is native to Europe and is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream sides.

It is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
