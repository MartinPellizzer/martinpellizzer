Microlepia thwaitesii

Microlepia thwaitesii, commonly known as the creeping polypody, is a fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features delicate, pinnately compound leaves with a light green hue. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.

Microlepia thwaitesii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate in humid conditions.
