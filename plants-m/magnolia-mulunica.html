Magnolia mulunica

Magnolia mulunica, commonly known as the Chinese magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its flowers are fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking visual impact.
