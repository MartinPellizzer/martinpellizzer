Monotaxis grandiflora var. obtusifolia

Monotaxis grandiflora var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved monotaxis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various climatic conditions.
