Maireana pentagona

Maireana pentagona, commonly known as the Five-angled Maireana, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive five-angled stems and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Maireana pentagona is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its potential use in horticulture.
