Macrolobium suaveolens var. uaupesense

Macrolobium suaveolens var. uaupesense, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant is notable for its hard, dark wood, which has been historically valued for musical instruments and furniture. Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various native species.

However, due to overharvesting, it is now listed as critically endangered in some regions.
