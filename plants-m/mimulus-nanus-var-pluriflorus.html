Mimulus nanus var. pluriflorus

Mimulus nanus var. pluriflorus, commonly known as the miniature monkeyflower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features small, vibrant orange or yellow flowers that bloom profusely from spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
