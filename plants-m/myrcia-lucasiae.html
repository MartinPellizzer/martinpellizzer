Myrcia lucasiae

Myrcia lucasiae, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white flowers. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a glossy surface, while its fruit is a small, round drupe.

Myrcia lucasiae is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its aromatic properties.
