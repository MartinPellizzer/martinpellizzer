Musa × bacoba

Musa × bacoba, commonly known as the banana plant, is a tropical herbaceous plant belonging to the Musaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and a central stem that produces clusters of berries. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and is widely cultivated for its edible fruit. Its growth habit involves a pseudostem formed by the tightly packed leaf sheaths.

Musa × bacoba plays an important role in both agriculture and local ecosystems.
