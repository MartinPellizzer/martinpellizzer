Miconia insularis

Miconia insularis, commonly known as the island miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that can grow up to 20 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange-red berry that contains numerous seeds.

Miconia insularis is often found in humid lowland forests and is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets.
