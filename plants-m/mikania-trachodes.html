Mikania trachodes

Mikania trachodes, commonly known as the roughleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the morning. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and forests.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
