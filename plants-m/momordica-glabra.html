Momordica glabra

Momordica glabra, commonly known as bitter melon, is a tropical vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, warty, green fruits that turn orange when mature. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its fruits contain numerous seeds surrounded by a fibrous, bitter pulp.

Momordica glabra is native to Southeast Asia and is cultivated for both its edible fruits and medicinal properties.
