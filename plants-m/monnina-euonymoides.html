Monnina euonymoides

Monnina euonymoides, commonly known as the redroot sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are typically reddish in color, which gives it its common name.

Monnina euonymoides is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.
