Mithridatium

Mithridatium, commonly known as the golden chain tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of yellow or orange flowers, which give it its common name. The plant is native to various regions in Africa, the Americas, and parts of Asia. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and the pods that develop after flowering are elongated and often hairy.

Mithridatium species are often cultivated for their ornamental value due to their striking floral displays.
