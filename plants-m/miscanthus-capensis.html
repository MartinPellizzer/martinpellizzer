Miscanthus capensis

Miscanthus capensis, commonly known as the Cape reed, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery plumes that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and riverbanks, where it thrives in moist soil conditions. Its foliage is often described as silver-green, adding visual interest to its natural habitat.

Miscanthus capensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland ecosystems.
