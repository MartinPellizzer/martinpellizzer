Melinis gracilis

Melinis gracilis, commonly known as slender grass or slender bluestem, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in open habitats such as prairies, woodland edges, and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-based flowers.

Melinis gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in ecological restoration projects.
