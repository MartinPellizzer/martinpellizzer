Myrceugenia alpigena var. alpigena

Myrceugenia alpigena var. alpigena, commonly known as the Andean holly, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Andes Mountains.

It typically grows at high elevations, often in rocky or mountainous terrain, and is adapted to cold, humid climates. The plant features small, glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green in color. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and it produces berries that are edible for some bird species.

This plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife.
