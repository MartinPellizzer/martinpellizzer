Mertensia paniculata subsp. eastwoodiae

Mertensia paniculata subsp.

eastwoodiae, commonly known as Eastwood's bluebells, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It produces showy, racemose inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native plant gardens.
