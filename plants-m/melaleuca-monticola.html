Melaleuca monticola

Melaleuca monticola, commonly known as the mountain tea tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and tea tree. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, often pink or white, flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and rocky areas.

Melaleuca monticola is valued for its ecological role and has been used in traditional medicine by Indigenous Australians.
