Mimosa foliolosa var. paranani

Mimosa foliolosa var. paranani, commonly known as the Brazilian mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and legume fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves that are sensitive to touch, causing them to fold when disturbed. It produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in the spring.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
