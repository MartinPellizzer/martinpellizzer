Melampyrum degenianum

It is a hemiparasitic plant, meaning it partially relies on its host for water and nutrients while still performing photosynthesis. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with two lips and five stamens.
