Mussaenda dehiscens

Mussaenda dehiscens, commonly known as the golden trumpet bush, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble trumpets, which are composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in full sun to partial shade.

Mussaenda dehiscens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
