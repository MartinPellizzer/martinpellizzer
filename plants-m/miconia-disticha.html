Miconia disticha

Miconia disticha, commonly known as the two-branched miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in the summer months. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and can grow up to several meters in height.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in certain regions.
