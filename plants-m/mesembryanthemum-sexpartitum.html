Mesembryanthemum sexpartitum

Mesembryanthemum sexpartitum, commonly known as the ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, often blue-green leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and yellow, typically during the cooler months. Its unique ability to exude a gel-like substance when damaged is a notable botanical feature.

Mesembryanthemum sexpartitum is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant and is also used in some traditional medicinal practices.
