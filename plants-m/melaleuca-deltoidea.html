Melaleuca deltoidea

Melaleuca deltoidea, commonly known as the broad-leaved paperbark, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in coastal areas and heathlands. The plant is characterized by its smooth, flaky bark that peels away in strips, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces small, fragrant white flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
