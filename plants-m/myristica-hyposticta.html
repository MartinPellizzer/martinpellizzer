Myristica hyposticta

Myristica hyposticta, commonly known as the black pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Indian subcontinent and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark red berries that contain the spice known as black pepper. The plant typically grows up to 3 meters tall, with smooth, dark green leaves and small white flowers. Its fruit develops into a hard, woody capsule that splits open to reveal the peppercorns.

The plant is valued not only for its culinary use but also for its medicinal properties.
