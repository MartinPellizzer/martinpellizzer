Microlicia sincorensis

Microlicia sincorensis, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded crown and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. The tree produces small, round, fleshy fruits that are edible and contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
