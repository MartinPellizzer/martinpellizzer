Musa uranoscopos

Musa uranoscopos, commonly known as the golden banana, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and clustered inflorescences. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is cultivated in various regions for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
