Merope erythractis

Merope erythractis, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with prominent red veins and its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Merope erythractis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or terrariums.
