Mammillaria pseudocrucigera

Mammillaria pseudocrucigera, commonly known as the false cross mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and dense covering of white to pale yellow spines. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in the early spring.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
