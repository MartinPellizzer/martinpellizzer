Myrmecophila × parkinsoniana

Myrmecophila × parkinsoniana, commonly known as the Parkinson's myrmecophila, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Bromeliaceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup, a feature common among bromeliads. The plant produces striking inflorescences with showy, colorful flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in shaded, humid environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
