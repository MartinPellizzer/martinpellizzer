Mendoncia angustifolia

Mendoncia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf mendoncia, is a herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It features slender, linear leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length, arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species for xeriscaping.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a native plant supporting local pollinators and contributing to desert biodiversity.
