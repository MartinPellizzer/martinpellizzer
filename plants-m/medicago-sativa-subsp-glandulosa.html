Medicago sativa subsp. glandulosa

Medicago sativa subsp.

glandulosa, commonly known as hairy vetch, is a leguminous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive hairy stems, which give it its common name.

The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers and is often used as a cover crop due to its nitrogen-fixing ability. It thrives in temperate climates and is adaptable to various soil types.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration and sustainable agriculture.
