Malvastrum americanum var. stellatum

Malvastrum americanum var. stellatum, commonly known as the star mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, star-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This species is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
