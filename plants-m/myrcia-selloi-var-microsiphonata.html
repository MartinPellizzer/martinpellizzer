Myrcia selloi var. microsiphonata

Myrcia selloi var. microsiphonata, commonly known as the small-horned myrcia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
