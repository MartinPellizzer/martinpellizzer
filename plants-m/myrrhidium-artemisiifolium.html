Myrrhidium artemisiifolium

Myrrhidium artemisiifolium, commonly known as the goldenroot, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellowish-orange roots, which are rich in bioactive compounds. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in composite heads.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
