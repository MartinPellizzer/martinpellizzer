Monardella sinuata subsp. nigrescens

Monardella sinuata subsp.

nigrescens, commonly known as darkleaf monardella, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves that often turn reddish-brown with age.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in open woodlands and meadows.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for pollinators and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
