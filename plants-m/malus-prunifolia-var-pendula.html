Malus prunifolia var. pendula

Malus prunifolia var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping crabapple, is a cultivar of the crabapple tree characterized by its cascading branches and showy flowers.

It is native to China and belongs to the Rosaceae family. The plant features oval, serrated leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding to its ornamental value. Its flowers, which bloom in spring, are typically white or pink and attract pollinators.

The tree is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and can thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
