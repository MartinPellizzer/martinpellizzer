Monardella linoides var. palmeri

Monardella linoides var. palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's narrowleaf monardella, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It is part of the mint family (Lamiaceae), which is evident in its square stems and aromatic foliage.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often found in native plant gardens.
