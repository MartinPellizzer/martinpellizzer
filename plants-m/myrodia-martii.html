Myrodia martii

Myrodia martii, commonly known as the desert honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale yellow or greenish in color, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss, and it has a deep root system to access groundwater.

Myrodia martii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
