Myoporum serratum var. pubescens

Myoporum serratum var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy myoporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage covered in fine, soft hairs, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value.

It is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
