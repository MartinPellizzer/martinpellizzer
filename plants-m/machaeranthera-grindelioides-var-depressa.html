Machaeranthera grindelioides var. depressa

Machaeranthera grindelioides var. depressa, commonly known as the lowleaf prairie gentian, is a flowering plant native to the central United States.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open prairies and dry habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive grayish-green color.

The species is noted for its ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
