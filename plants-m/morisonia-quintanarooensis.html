Morisonia quintanarooensis

Morisonia quintanarooensis, commonly known as the Quintana rose.

It is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Quintana Roo, Mexico. The plant features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Morisonia quintanarooensis is adapted to thrive in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub habitats.
