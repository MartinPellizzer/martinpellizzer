Meiosperma

Meiosperma, commonly known as the plains pricklypear, is a species of cactus native to North America.

It is characterized by its spineless stems and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its thick, fleshy stems. Its fruit, which is edible, contains small, sweet seeds.

Meiosperma plays an important role in desert ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
