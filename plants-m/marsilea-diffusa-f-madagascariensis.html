Marsilea diffusa f. madagascariensis

Marsilea diffusa f. madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar water clover, is a aquatic fern species native to Madagascar.

It features small, floating leaves that are divided into four lobes, resembling a clover leaf. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and wetlands. Its reproductive structures include spore-producing sorus clusters on the underside of its leaves.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
