Myrceugenia macrosepala

Myrceugenia macrosepala, commonly known as the large-sepaled myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the temperate rainforests of southern South America, particularly in Chile and Argentina. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
