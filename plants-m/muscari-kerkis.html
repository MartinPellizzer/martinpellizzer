Muscari kerkis

Muscari kerkis, commonly known as the Kerkis squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Muscari kerkis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its early spring bloom.
