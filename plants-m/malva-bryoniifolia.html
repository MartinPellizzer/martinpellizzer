Malva bryoniifolia

Malva bryoniifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf mallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional herbal remedies for their purported medicinal properties.
