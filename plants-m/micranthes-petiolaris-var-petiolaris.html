Micranthes petiolaris var. petiolaris

Micranthes petiolaris var. petiolaris, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mouse-ear, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features small, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in length, with a hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale blue flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Boraginaceae family.
