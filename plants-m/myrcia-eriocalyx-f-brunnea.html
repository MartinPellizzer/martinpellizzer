Myrcia eriocalyx f. brunnea

Myrcia eriocalyx f. brunnea, commonly known as the dark-leaved myrcia, is a variant of the myrcia species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy foliage and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in the spring. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and guava. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for both its ornamental and medicinal qualities.
