Maesa indica var. obtusa

Maesa indica var. obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leaf maesa, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
