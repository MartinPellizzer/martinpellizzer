Mammillaria columbiana subsp. yucatanensis

Mammillaria columbiana subsp.

yucatanensis, commonly known as the Yucatan hedgehog cactus, is a small, spineless cactus native to Mexico. It features a compact, globular stem with a ribbed structure and is covered in white, woolly hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
