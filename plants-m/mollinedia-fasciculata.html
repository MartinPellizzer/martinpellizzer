Mollinedia fasciculata

Mollinedia fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale blue. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Mollinedia fasciculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
