Mentha longifolia var. romanica

Mentha longifolia var. romanica, commonly known as the Roman mint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, which appear in late summer. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, giving it a strong minty scent and making it useful in culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
