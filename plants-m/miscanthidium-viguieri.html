Miscanthidium viguieri

Miscanthidium viguieri, commonly known as the Viguier's false bamboo, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental appeal and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
