Moschatella

Moschatella, commonly known as the water cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves that are often submerged or floating in shallow water. The plant produces tiny, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring. Moschatella is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in moist, nutrient-rich environments.

It is valued for its peppery flavor and is often used in salads or as a garnish.
