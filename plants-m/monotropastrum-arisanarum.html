Monotropastrum arisanarum

Monotropastrum arisanarum, commonly known as the Arisan moonflower, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in forest understories.

Its unique flowering behavior and medicinal properties make it a notable species in its native habitat.
