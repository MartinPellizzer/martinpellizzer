Mentha timija

Mentha timija, commonly known as narrowleaf spearmint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and serrated leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, and it is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks. Mentha timija is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Its growth habit and habitat preferences make it a hardy and adaptable species within its native range.
