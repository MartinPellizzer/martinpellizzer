Macropsychanthus comosus

Macropsychanthus comosus, commonly known as the mountain snowbell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate floral display to its habitat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a similar dense pubescence, which helps to reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Macropsychanthus comosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
