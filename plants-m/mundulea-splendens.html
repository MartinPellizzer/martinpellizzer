Mundulea splendens

Mundulea splendens, commonly known as the splendour acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny foliage and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention in its harsh environment. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against drought and herbivory.

Mundulea splendens plays an important ecological role by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
