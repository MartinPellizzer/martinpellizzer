Melica ciliata subsp. klokovii

Melica ciliata subsp.

klokovii, commonly known as the Klokov's beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and elongated, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and ciliate, with fine hairs along the edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, grass-like foliage.
