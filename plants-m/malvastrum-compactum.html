Malvastrum compactum

Malvastrum compactum, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 2-3 feet tall, featuring fuzzy, blue-gray foliage and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens. Its flowers attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees, contributing to ecological diversity.

Malvastrum compactum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand drought conditions.
