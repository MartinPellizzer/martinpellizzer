Mentha × verticillata var. latissima

Mentha × verticillata var. latissima, commonly known as broad-leaved water mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its wide, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. It produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stems.

Its aromatic leaves and flowering habit make it a notable species in wetland ecosystems.
