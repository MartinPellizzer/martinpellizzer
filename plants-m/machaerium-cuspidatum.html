Machaerium cuspidatum

Machaerium cuspidatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved machaerium, is a member of the Leguminosae family.

It is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas, particularly Brazil. The plant features slender, upright stems and trifoliate leaves with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets. Its small, white or pale purple flowers are clustered in racemes, and it produces small, flat, pod-like fruits.

Machaerium cuspidatum is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories.
