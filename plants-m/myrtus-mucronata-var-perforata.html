Myrtus mucronata var. perforata

Myrtus mucronata var. perforata, commonly known as the perforate myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves with a distinctive perforated margin, which gives it its scientific name. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark blue, edible berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its unique foliage and aromatic properties make it a valued species in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
