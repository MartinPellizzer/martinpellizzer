Melinonia rubiginosa

Melinonia rubiginosa, commonly known as the red-staining melinonia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, reddish bloom. Melinonia rubiginosa thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
