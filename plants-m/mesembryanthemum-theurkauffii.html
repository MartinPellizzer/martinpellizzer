Mesembryanthemum theurkauffii

Mesembryanthemum theurkauffii, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or Physalis peruviana, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange flowers and fleshy, spiky fruits that resemble small lanterns. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates. Its leaves are succulent and often covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The fruit is edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and is often used in culinary applications.
