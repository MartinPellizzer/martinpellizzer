Mimulus pulsiferae

Mimulus pulsiferae, commonly known as the purple monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to reddish-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as streambanks and meadows. It has simple, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Mimulus pulsiferae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
