Malaxis katangensis var. katangensis

Malaxis katangensis var. katangensis, commonly known as the Katanga Malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical rainforests of Central Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a spurred lip that is often brightly colored. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has a rhizomatous root system. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a distinctive morphology that aids in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
