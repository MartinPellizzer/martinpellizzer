Malvastrum coromandelianum subsp. fryxellii

Malvastrum coromandelianum subsp.

fryxellii, commonly known as the Fryxell's mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats like roadsides and dry fields.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native range.
