Mostuea periquetii

Mostuea periquetii, commonly known as the Periquet's grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its grass-like structure.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil and Peru. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like blades, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

While not widely cultivated, it is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role in its native habitats.
