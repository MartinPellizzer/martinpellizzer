Miliusa eupoda

Miliusa eupoda, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets and produces small, red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

The tree is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
