Melocactus communis

Melocactus communis, commonly known as the red globular cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spherical or slightly elongated stem, which is typically covered in dense, white spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warm season, which are often followed by red fruits containing edible seeds. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Melocactus communis is well adapted to full sun and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for desert gardens.
