Miltonia × leucoglossa

Miltonia × leucoglossa, commonly known as the white-lipped orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and a distinctive pinkish lip that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often displaying a range of colors from white to pale pink.

Miltonia × leucoglossa is prized in horticulture for its beauty and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
