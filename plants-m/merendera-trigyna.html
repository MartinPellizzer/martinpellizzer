Merendera trigyna

Merendera trigyna, commonly known as the three-lobed merendera, is a unique plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, adding to its ornamental appeal. Merendera trigyna thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical or subtropical woodlands.
