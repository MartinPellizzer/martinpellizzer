Myriopteris aemula

Myriopteris aemula, commonly known as the small fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rock crevices. Its fronds are usually dimorphic, with fertile and sterile leaves differing in shape and structure.

Myriopteris aemula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
