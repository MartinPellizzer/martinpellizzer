Mucuna monosperma

Mucuna monosperma, commonly known as the cow pea or velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, hairy pods that contain a single seed. The plant produces a strong, aromatic odor, which is more pronounced in its flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used for its seeds, which are rich in protein and used in traditional medicine.

The plant also plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing properties.
