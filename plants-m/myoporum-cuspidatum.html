Myoporum cuspidatum

Myoporum cuspidatum, commonly known as the New Zealand myoporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It features small, opposite leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a slightly rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that releases tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Myoporum cuspidatum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in coastal environments.
