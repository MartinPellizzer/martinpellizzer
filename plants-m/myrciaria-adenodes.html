Myrciaria adenodes

Myrciaria adenodes, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a small evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and seasonal fruit display.

Myrciaria adenodes thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.
