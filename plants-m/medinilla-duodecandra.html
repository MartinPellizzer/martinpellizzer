Medinilla duodecandra

Medinilla duodecandra, commonly known as the Christmas Medinilla, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom during the winter months. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and as a houseplant. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil and partial shade.

Medinilla duodecandra is also noted for its ability to produce berries that are edible for some bird species.
