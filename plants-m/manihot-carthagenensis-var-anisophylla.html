Manihot carthagenensis var. anisophylla

Manihot carthagenensis var. anisophylla, commonly known as the African arrowroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical Africa.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, starchy tubers that are used as a food source. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

The tubers are rich in carbohydrates and can be used as a substitute for potatoes or cassava in various cuisines.
