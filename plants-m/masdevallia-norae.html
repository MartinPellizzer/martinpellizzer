Masdevallia norae

Masdevallia norae, commonly known as the Norae's Masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, often colorful floral structures. The plant typically produces pendulous inflorescences with showy, asymmetrical flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to the humid, shaded environments it inhabits.

Masdevallia norae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique and delicate blooms.
