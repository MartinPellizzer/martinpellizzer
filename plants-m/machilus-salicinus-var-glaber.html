Machilus salicinus var. glaber

Machilus salicinus var. glaber, commonly known as the smooth-leafed Chinese-laurel, is a variant of the Machilus salicinus species native to China.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree characterized by its smooth, glabrous foliage. The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a glossy surface and a pleasant aromatic scent. It produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue to black berries.

This plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
