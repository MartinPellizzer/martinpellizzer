Malus segurensis

Malus segurensis, commonly known as the Segurens apple, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern France and is characterized by its ornamental value. The plant produces small, edible apples that are typically yellow or red in color. It has simple, alternate leaves and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.

Malus segurensis is also valued for its ornamental flowers, which bloom in spring.
