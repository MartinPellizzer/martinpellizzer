Melaleuca curtifolia

Melaleuca curtifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paperbark, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that gives it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, white or pink flowers in clusters.

The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
