Mollia villosa

Mollia villosa, commonly known as the hairy mallows, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features hairy, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Mollia villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its vibrant floral display.
