Millania rupestris

Millania rupestris, commonly known as the rock milla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, branching structure and spiny stems. The plant produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Millania rupestris is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
