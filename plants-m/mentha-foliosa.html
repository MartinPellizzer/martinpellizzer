Mentha foliosa

Mentha foliosa, commonly known as lemon mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are aromatic, with a sharp, citrus-like scent, and are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Mentha foliosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
