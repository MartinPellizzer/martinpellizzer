Myroxylon fragrans

Myroxylon fragrans, commonly known as the copal tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree is renowned for its sap, which solidifies into a resin called copal, used in incense and varnishes. Its bark and wood are also valued for their aromatic properties and durability.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
