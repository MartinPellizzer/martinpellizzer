Monardella sinuata subsp. gerryi

Monardella sinuata subsp.

gerryi, commonly known as the Sierra lemonade plant, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its flowers are usually pink to purple and are arranged in whorls along the stem.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
