Melica cupani var. viridis

Melica cupani var. viridis, commonly known as green cupgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, greenish flowers.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insect species.
