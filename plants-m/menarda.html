Menarda

Menarda, commonly known as purple top deadnettle, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the family Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple-tinted upper leaves and pale green lower leaves. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Menarda is often found in moist, disturbed soils in meadows, woodland edges, and gardens.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial, and its leaves are sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
