Myristica magnifica

Myristica magnifica, commonly known as the Malaya nut or the golden nut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The plant produces distinctive fruits that contain a single large seed, often surrounded by a rich, aromatic aril. Myristica magnifica is valued for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, and for its ornamental value in horticulture.

Its growth is typically slow, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil.
