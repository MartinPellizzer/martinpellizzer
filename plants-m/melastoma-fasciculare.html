Melastoma fasciculare

Melastoma fasciculare, commonly known as the clustered melastoma, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release small, smooth seeds when mature.

Melastoma fasciculare is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
