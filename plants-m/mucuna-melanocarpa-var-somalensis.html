Mucuna melanocarpa var. somalensis

Mucuna melanocarpa var. somalensis, commonly known as the Somali velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and produces pods containing small, black seeds. The plant is a climbing vine with trifoliate leaves and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

The seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein in some local cuisines.
