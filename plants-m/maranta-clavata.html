Maranta clavata

Maranta clavata, commonly known as the arrowroot plant, is a tropical species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored with contrasting patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its rhizomes are rich in starch and have been historically used as a food source.

Botanically, it belongs to the Marantaceae family and is valued for both its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.
