Medicago tenuifolia

Medicago tenuifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf clover, is a low-growing leguminous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves composed of three small, oval-shaped leaflets, and small white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in forage and soil enrichment.

Medicago tenuifolia is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
