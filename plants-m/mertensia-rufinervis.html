Mertensia rufinervis

Mertensia rufinervis, commonly known as the red-veined bluebelle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, rich soils along stream banks and woodland edges. The plant features showy, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are heart-shaped with prominent reddish veins, giving it its common name.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
