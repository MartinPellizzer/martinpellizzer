Macleania cordifolia

Macleania cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. It has a trailing or shrub-like growth habit and produces small, capsule-like fruits.
