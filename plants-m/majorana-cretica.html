Majorana cretica

Majorana cretica, commonly known as sweet marjoram, is an aromatic herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial plant with small, oval-shaped leaves and delicate, white or pale pink flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and fragrant foliage. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves, contain compounds like carvacrol and thymol, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

Majorana cretica thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and herb gardens.
