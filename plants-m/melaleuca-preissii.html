Melaleuca preissii

Melaleuca preissii, commonly known as the broad-leaved tea tree, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, aromatic leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces woody, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

Melaleuca preissii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
