Miconia boekei

Miconia boekei, commonly known as the Boeke's miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Miconia boekei is notable for its rapid growth and potential invasiveness in certain regions.
