Malva micans

Malva micans, commonly known as the silverleaf mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, pale pink to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Malva micans is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
