Meehania pinetorum

Meehania pinetorum, commonly known as the pineleaf meehania, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are typically aromatic and have a slightly resinous scent.

Meehania pinetorum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas near pine trees.
