Monanthes monanthes

Monanthes monanthes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. Its flowers are bell-shaped, with five petals that range in color from white to pale blue, and they bloom in early summer.

Monanthes monanthes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
