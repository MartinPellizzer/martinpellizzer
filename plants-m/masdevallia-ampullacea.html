Masdevallia ampullacea

Masdevallia ampullacea, commonly known as the bell-shaped masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

This orchid thrives in cool, humid environments with indirect light, making it a popular choice for shade-loving indoor gardens.
