Mimosa argentinensis var. saltensis

Mimosa argentinensis var. saltensis, commonly known as the saltbush mimosa, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Argentina.

It is characterized by its small, fern-like leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces fluffy, yellowish-pink flower clusters that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and ecological resilience make it a notable plant in arid landscapes.
