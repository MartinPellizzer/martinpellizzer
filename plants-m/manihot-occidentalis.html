Manihot occidentalis

Manihot occidentalis, commonly known as cassava, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a woody shrub that produces edible roots and stems, which are rich in carbohydrates. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves and flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Cassava is cultivated widely in tropical regions for its high yield and nutritional value.

Its botanical name reflects its origin, with "occidentalis" meaning western, referring to its spread to the western hemisphere.
