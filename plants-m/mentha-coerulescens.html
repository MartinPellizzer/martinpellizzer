Mentha coerulescens

Mentha coerulescens, commonly known as blue mint, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe, as well as parts of western and central Asia. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly serrated edge and a minty aroma.

Mentha coerulescens is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and as a source of aromatic leaves.
