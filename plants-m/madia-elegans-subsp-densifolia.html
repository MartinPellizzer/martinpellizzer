Madia elegans subsp. densifolia

Madia elegans subsp.

densifolia, commonly known as the denseleaf madia, is a flowering plant native to California. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in open habitats. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its dense foliage and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for native gardens.
