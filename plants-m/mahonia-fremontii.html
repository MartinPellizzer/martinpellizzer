Mahonia fremontii

Mahonia fremontii, commonly known as the Fremont mahonia, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a leathery texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by blue-black berries that are edible for birds. Mahonia fremontii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate partial shade.
