Mentha × rotundifolia f. serrata

Mentha × rotundifolia f. serrata, commonly known as the serrated round-leaf mint, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its rounded, serrated leaves that are typically a deep green in color. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for its aromatic and therapeutic properties.
