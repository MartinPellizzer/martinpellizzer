Mangifera longipes

Mangifera longipes, commonly known as the longipes mango, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and produces fragrant, yellow-orange fruits with a sweet, fibrous pulp. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing before the leaves, and it is primarily cultivated for its edible fruit.

The tree can reach heights of up to 20 meters and is valued for both its fruit and ornamental qualities.
