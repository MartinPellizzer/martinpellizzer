Melaleuca pustulata

Melaleuca pustulata, commonly known as the rough-leaved bottlebrush, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its cylindrical, red or pink flower spikes that appear in summer. The leaves are narrow, leathery, and often have a rough texture, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticulture for its vibrant floral displays.
