Mammillaria eburnea

Mammillaria eburnea, commonly known as the white mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and white to pale green spines. The plant features a dense covering of small, rounded tubercles that give it a distinctive appearance. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a vibrant touch to its otherwise muted color palette.

Mammillaria eburnea is well-suited for cultivation in arid environments due to its drought tolerance and compact growth habit.
