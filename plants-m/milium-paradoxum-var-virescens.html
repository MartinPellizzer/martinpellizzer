Milium paradoxum var. virescens

Milium paradoxum var. virescens, commonly known as the green millet grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically green in color, with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a useful plant for ecological restoration projects.
