Mapouria perrieri

Mapouria perrieri, commonly known as the African velvet tamarind, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant is noted for its ornamental value, with its dark green foliage and showy yellow flowers. Its fruits, which are elongated and woody, contain edible seeds used in traditional medicine.

The tree is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soils, making it a useful species for reforestation efforts.
