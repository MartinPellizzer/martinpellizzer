Melocactus polyacanthus

Melocactus polyacanthus, commonly known as the thorned melon cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical or slightly elongated stem, which is typically covered in spines and a dense layer of white, woolly hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Melocactus polyacanthus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
