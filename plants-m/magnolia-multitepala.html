Magnolia multitepala

Magnolia multitepala, commonly known as the Mexican magnolia, is a species native to central Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. It produces woody capsules as fruit, which contain numerous seeds.

Magnolia multitepala is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
