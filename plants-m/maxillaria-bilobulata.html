Maxillaria bilobulata

Maxillaria bilobulata, commonly known as the two-lobed maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, often greenish or reddish-brown pseudobulbs and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers with three petals and a distinctive lip that is often split into two lobes. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Maxillaria bilobulata is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
