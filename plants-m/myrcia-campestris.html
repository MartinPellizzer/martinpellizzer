Myrcia campestris

Myrcia campestris, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, red, fleshy fruits. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a leathery texture with a distinctive scent.

The flowers are clustered and produce a strong, spicy fragrance, attracting various pollinators.
