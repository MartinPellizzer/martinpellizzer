Microstylis tubulosa

Microstylis tubulosa, commonly known as the slender-styled tubular grass, is a small, herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its narrow, tubular leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense spikelets, and it produces lightweight seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
