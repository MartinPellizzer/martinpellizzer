Myrceugenia ovata var. ovata

Myrceugenia ovata var. ovata, commonly known as the oval myrtle, is a species native to southern Brazil.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters. The plant features dark green, glossy leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. Its small, fragrant white flowers are followed by edible, reddish-black berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
