Melinis longiseta

Melinis longiseta, commonly known as the long-haired grass, is a perennial grass species native to the warmer regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, silky hairs that cover its leaves and seed heads. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open fields and along roadsides.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become a weed in agricultural and natural areas.
