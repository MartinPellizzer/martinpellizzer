Muhlenbergia villiflora var. villosa

Muhlenbergia villiflora var. villosa, commonly known as silky muhlenbergia, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, arching stems that produce clusters of soft, fluffy panicles, giving it a delicate, ornamental appearance. The plant has fine, hairy leaves that are often described as silky to the touch, contributing to its unique texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

This variety is valued in native plant gardens for its ecological benefits and visual appeal.
