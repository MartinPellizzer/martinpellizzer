Manihot grandistipula

Manihot grandistipula, commonly known as the large-stippled manioc, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, large, stipulate leaves that are often lobed or toothed. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
