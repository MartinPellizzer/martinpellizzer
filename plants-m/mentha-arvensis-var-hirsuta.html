Mentha arvensis var. hirsuta

Mentha arvensis var. hirsuta, commonly known as woolly mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and square, erect stems. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Woolly mint is often found in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides and meadows.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
