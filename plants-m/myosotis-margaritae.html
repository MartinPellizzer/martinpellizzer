Myosotis margaritae

Myosotis margaritae, commonly known as the pearl flower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Myosotis genus, which includes various species of forget-me-nots. The plant produces small, delicate blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Myosotis margaritae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its charming appearance.
