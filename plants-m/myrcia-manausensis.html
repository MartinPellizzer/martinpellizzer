Myrcia manausensis

Myrcia manausensis, commonly known as Manaus myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Amazon rainforest, particularly in regions of Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are elliptical and glossy, and its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
