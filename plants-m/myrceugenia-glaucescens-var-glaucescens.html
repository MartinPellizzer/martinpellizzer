Myrceugenia glaucescens var. glaucescens

Myrceugenia glaucescens var. glaucescens, commonly known as the Brazilian blueberry or guabiroba, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant features lanceolate leaves that are glossy and dark green on top, with a lighter underside. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in inflorescences, while the fruit is a spherical, blue-black berry with a sweet, aromatic flavor.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its edible fruits, which are rich in antioxidants.
