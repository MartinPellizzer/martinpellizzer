Musa troglodytarum

Musa troglodytarum, commonly known as the cave banana, is a unique species of banana plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and the production of small, edible berries rather than the typical elongated banana fruit. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing in rocky or cave-like habitats. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Despite its rarity, Musa troglodytarum holds significance in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
