Moraea gracilis

Moraea gracilis, commonly known as the slender moraea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and delicate, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, producing striking purple or pink blooms.
