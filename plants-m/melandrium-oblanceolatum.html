Melandrium oblanceolatum

Melandrium oblanceolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf catchfly, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Melandrium oblanceolatum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.
