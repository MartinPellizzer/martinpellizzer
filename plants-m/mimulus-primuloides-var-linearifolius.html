Mimulus primuloides var. linearifolius

Mimulus primuloides var. linearifolius, commonly known as the linear-leaved monkeyflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive spurred petal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in streamside habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
