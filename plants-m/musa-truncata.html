Musa truncata

Musa truncata, commonly known as the dwarf banana plant, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features large, broad leaves that are usually a deep green color. It produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible, elongated fruits.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant.
