Mesua nervosa

Mesua nervosa, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate or elliptical in shape. Its bark is smooth and greyish, while the wood is lightweight and has a fine texture.

Mesua nervosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
