Mikania mandonii

Mikania mandonii, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from yellow to orange in color. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Mikania mandonii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
