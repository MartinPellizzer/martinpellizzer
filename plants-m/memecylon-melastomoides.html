Memecylon melastomoides

Memecylon melastomoides, commonly known as the African starling tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink, followed by berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or secondary growth areas.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
