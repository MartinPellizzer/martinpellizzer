Myrcia lauriflora

Myrcia lauriflora, commonly known as the broadleaf myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

Myrcia lauriflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
