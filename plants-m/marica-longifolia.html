Marica longifolia

Marica longifolia, commonly known as the long-stemmed marsh plant, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, clustered white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense stands in marshy areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize the soil.

Marica longifolia plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing food and shelter for various aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
