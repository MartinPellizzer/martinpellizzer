Mentha longifolia var. lepidioides

Mentha longifolia var. lepidioides, commonly known as narrow-leaf spearmint, is a variant of the spearmint plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and a strong, minty aroma. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its leaves are used in culinary and medicinal applications, similar to other mint species.

The flowers are small, tubular, and usually violet or blue in color, appearing in clusters.
