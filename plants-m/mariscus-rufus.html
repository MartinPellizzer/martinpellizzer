Mariscus rufus

Mariscus rufus, commonly known as the red sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the family Montiaceae.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically red or reddish in color, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. Mariscus rufus is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
