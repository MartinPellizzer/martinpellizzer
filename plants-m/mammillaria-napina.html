Mammillaria napina

Mammillaria napina, commonly known as the napina hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that are typically green or pale blue in color. The plant produces small, white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Mammillaria napina is a popular choice for rock gardens and as a decorative indoor plant due to its compact size and unique appearance.
