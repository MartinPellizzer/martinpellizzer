Miconia sellowiana var. pubescens

Miconia sellowiana var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy miconia, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, pubescent hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are followed by distinctive, winged fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to the tropical climate of its native habitat.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
