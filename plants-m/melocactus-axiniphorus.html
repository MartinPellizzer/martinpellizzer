Melocactus axiniphorus

Melocactus axiniphorus, commonly known as the golden crown cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and a dense crown of golden-yellow spines. The plant produces small, white flowers during the rainy season, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its thick, waxy skin helps it retain moisture in harsh desert conditions.

Melocactus axiniphorus is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
