Myrtus disticha

Myrtus disticha, commonly known as the common myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features fragrant, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark blue berries. Myrtus disticha thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its aromatic properties have been valued for centuries in various cultures for both ornamental and practical uses.
