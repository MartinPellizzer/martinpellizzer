Myrobalanus subsericea

Myrobalanus subsericea, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy on the upper surface and slightly hairy underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, orange-red berries that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and riverine forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and fruit, which is used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
