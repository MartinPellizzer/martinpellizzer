Magnolia masticata

Magnolia masticata, commonly known as the Chinese magnolia or the stinking magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and turn yellow in autumn. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
