Miconia jitotolana

Miconia jitotolana, commonly known as the Mexican miconia, is a species of flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, showy bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows as an understory shrub in humid tropical forests, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are small and clustered, often surrounded by brightly colored bracts that attract pollinators such as birds and insects.
