Mollinedia pellucens

Mollinedia pellucens, commonly known as the glassy bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are often translucent or glassy in appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Mollinedia pellucens is valued in horticulture for its unique floral display and ornamental appeal.
