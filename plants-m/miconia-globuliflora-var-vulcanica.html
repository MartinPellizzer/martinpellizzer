Miconia globuliflora var. vulcanica

Miconia globuliflora var. vulcanica, commonly known as the volcanic miconia, is a tropical shrub native to the volcanic regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by round, fleshy fruits resembling small berries. Its growth is typically rapid, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens, though it can become invasive in certain ecosystems.
