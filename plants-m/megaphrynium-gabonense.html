Megaphrynium gabonense

Megaphrynium gabonense, commonly known as the Gabon arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to Central and Southern Africa.

Its flowers are typically small and clustered in an inflorescence called a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

This species is valued for its edible tubers, which are similar in texture to yams and are used in local cuisines.
