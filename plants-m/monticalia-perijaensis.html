Monticalia perijaensis

Monticalia perijaensis, commonly known as the Perijan mountain orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and vibrant coloration. This plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its habitat is threatened by deforestation and climate change, making it a subject of conservation interest.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research.
