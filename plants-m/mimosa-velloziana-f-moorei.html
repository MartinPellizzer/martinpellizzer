Mimosa velloziana f. moorei

Mimosa velloziana f. moorei, commonly known as the Moore's mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its delicate, fern-like appearance.

The species is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
