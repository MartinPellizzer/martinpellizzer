Mitchella pilosa

Mitchella pilosa, commonly known as the hairy trailing jenny, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its common name. The flowers are white and bell-shaped, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to form dense, ground-covering mats.
