Matayba mollis

Matayba mollis, commonly known as the silky matayba, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
