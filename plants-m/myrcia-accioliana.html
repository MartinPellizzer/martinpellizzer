Myrcia accioliana

Myrcia accioliana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
