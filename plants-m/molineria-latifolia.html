Molineria latifolia

Molineria latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features tall, slender stems that bear dense spikes of small, pale green to yellowish flowers. Its leaves are broad and flat, contributing to its common name, and it plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems.

Molineria latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
