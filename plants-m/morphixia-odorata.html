Morphixia odorata

Morphixia odorata, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms that emit a sweet, vanilla-like aroma. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, with a waxy texture that helps retain moisture.

Morphixia odorata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and unique fragrance.
