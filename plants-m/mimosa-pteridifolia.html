Mimosa pteridifolia

Mimosa pteridifolia, commonly known as the silver mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant has a bushy growth habit and is often found in dry, open habitats. Its leaves are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Mimosa pteridifolia is also known for its sensitive leaves that fold when touched, a trait shared with other members of its genus.
