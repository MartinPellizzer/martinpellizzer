Madhuca obtusifolia

Madhuca obtusifolia, commonly known as the wax apple or butter fruit, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The tree's fruit is a spherical, yellowish berry that turns red when ripe, containing a single seed. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The plant is well adapted to humid, shaded environments and thrives in tropical rainforests.
