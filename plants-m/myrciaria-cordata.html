Myrciaria cordata

Myrciaria cordata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá-preto, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy dark green leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces edible red berries that are rich in vitamin C and are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Myrciaria cordata thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in botanical studies.
