Marrubium rotundifolium

Marrubium rotundifolium, commonly known as round-leaf horehound, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features hairy, gray-green leaves that are round or slightly heart-shaped, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Marrubium rotundifolium has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
