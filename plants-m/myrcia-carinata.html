Myrcia carinata

Myrcia carinata, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are glossy and often have a distinct central ridge, giving it the species name "carinata," which means "keel-like.".

Myrcia carinata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
