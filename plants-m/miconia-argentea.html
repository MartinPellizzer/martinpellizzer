Miconia argentea

Miconia argentea, commonly known as silver miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are often covered in a silvery or whitish bloom, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by fruit that is a bright red or orange capsule. Miconia argentea is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

It is also noted for its potential as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage.
