Macrocentrum fasciculatum var. peruvianum

Macrocentrum fasciculatum var. peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry, and densely packed foliage that forms tufts or clusters, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or rocky crevices. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings for its unique texture and visual interest.
