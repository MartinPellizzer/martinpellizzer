Masdevallia torulosa

Masdevallia torulosa, commonly known as the "snowy masdevallia," is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and its striking, pale pink to white flowers that resemble snowflakes. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions with dappled sunlight. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its elegant appearance.

Masdevallia torulosa is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral display and adaptability to cultivation under specific conditions.
