Mecodium tablaziense

Mecodium tablaziense, commonly known as the desert thyme, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and aromatic, creeping stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Due to its hardiness and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for use in traditional medicine.
