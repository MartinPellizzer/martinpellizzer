Malpighia substrigosa

Malpighia substrigosa, commonly known as the hairy granadilla, is a species of flowering plant in the Malpighiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens.
