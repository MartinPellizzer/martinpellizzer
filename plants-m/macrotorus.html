Macrotorus

Macrotorus, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, making it one of the largest orchid species. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its botanical features include long, strap-like leaves and a complex, branched root system.

Macrotorus thrives in humid, warm environments and requires high light exposure for optimal growth.
