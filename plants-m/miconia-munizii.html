Miconia munizii

Miconia munizii, commonly known as the Brazilian star apple, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are edible and have a sweet, juicy pulp. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
