Micropteris orientalis

Micropteris orientalis, commonly known as the oriental water fern, is a species of fern native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are typically light green and have a feathery appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions with cool, humid climates. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many fern species.

Micropteris orientalis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in shaded garden settings.
