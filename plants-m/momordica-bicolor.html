Momordica bicolor

Momordica bicolor, commonly known as the yellow horned melon, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow, warty fruits that resemble horned melons. The plant produces small, white flowers and has palmately lobed leaves. Its fruit contains inedible, spiny seeds surrounded by a sweet, musky pulp.

Botanically, it belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and pumpkins.
