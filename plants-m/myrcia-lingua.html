Myrcia lingua

Myrcia lingua, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Myrcia lingua is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
