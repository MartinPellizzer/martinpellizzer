Microschizaea malaccana

Microschizaea malaccana, commonly known as the Malacca liverwort, is a small, terrestrial liverwort belonging to the family Porellaceae.

It is characterized by its leafy, thalloid structure with closely arranged, linear leaves that form a dense carpet on moist, shaded soil. Its reproductive structures, including archegonia and antheridia, are located on the underside of the leaves.

Microschizaea malaccana plays a vital role in soil stabilization and nutrient cycling in its native habitats.
