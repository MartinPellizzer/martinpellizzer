Myrcia styphelantha

Myrcia styphelantha, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are often fragrant and have a glossy appearance. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
