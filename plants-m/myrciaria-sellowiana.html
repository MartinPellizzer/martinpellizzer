Myrciaria sellowiana

Myrciaria sellowiana, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark purple to black berries. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves with a slightly serrated edge. Its flowers are white and clustered in inflorescences, and the fruit is rich in antioxidants.

It is often cultivated for its edible fruit and has traditional uses in local medicine.
