Medicago lappacea var. longispina

Medicago lappacea var. longispina, commonly known as the long-spined goatweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branching stems that grow from a taproot and are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets that have serrated edges.

This species is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
