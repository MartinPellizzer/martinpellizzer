Myzorrhiza corymbosa

Myzorrhiza corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered root vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant forms symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The vine thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
