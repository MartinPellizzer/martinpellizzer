Myrcia karwinskyana

Myrcia karwinskyana, commonly known as the Karwinsky's myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and emit a strong, aromatic scent.

The flowers are usually pink or white, and the plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and medicinal properties.
