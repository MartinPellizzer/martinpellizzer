Mocanera thurifera

Mocanera thurifera, commonly known as the fragrant mocanera, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often emitting a sweet fragrance. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, preferring cool and moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Mocanera thurifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
