Melicho sanguineus

Melicho sanguineus, commonly known as the bloodroot, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It features a distinctive red-orange flower that blooms in early spring. The plant has a single, erect stem with a basal leaf that is palmately lobed. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine.

Melicho sanguineus is also known for its toxic sap, which can cause skin irritation.
