Myristica conferta var. borneensis

Myristica conferta var. borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the island of Borneo.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The plant produces an aromatic, reddish-brown fruit that contains the valuable spice nutmeg. Its seeds are encased in a hard, woody shell and are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The species is also noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique fragrance and aesthetic appeal.
