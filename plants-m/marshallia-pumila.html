Marshallia pumila

Marshallia pumila, commonly known as the plains coreopsis, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and eastern United States.

It features showy yellow flowers with orange centers, which bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

Marshallia pumila is often found in open habitats such as prairies, meadows, and along roadsides.
