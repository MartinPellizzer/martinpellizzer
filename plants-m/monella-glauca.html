Monella glauca

Monella glauca, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, blue-gray flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely textured, silver-green foliage that adds visual interest throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Monella glauca is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
