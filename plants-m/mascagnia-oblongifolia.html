Mascagnia oblongifolia

Mascagnia oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mascagnia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, featuring small, narrow, evergreen leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. It is well adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in well-drained soils.

Mascagnia oblongifolia is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
