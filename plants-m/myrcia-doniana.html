Myrcia doniana

Myrcia doniana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically elliptical and glossy, and its fragrant, small white or pink flowers. Myrcia doniana produces small, fleshy berries that are often red or orange when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has also been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
