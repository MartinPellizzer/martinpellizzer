Montia lusitanica

Montia lusitanica, commonly known as the Iberian watercress, is a small annual herb native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland floors. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are white or pale pink, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

The species is valued for its peppery flavor and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
