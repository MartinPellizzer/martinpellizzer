Monodora tenuifolia

Monodora tenuifolia, commonly known as African breadfruit, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, round fruits that resemble small bread loaves. Its fruit contains a starchy, edible pulp that is used in various traditional foods.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
