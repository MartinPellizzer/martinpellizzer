Myrrhina absinthoides

Myrrhina absinthoides, commonly known as the sweetroot, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish roots that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive, slightly bitter aroma.

Myrrhina absinthoides is also valued for its potential medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects.
