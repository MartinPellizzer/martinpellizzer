Malaxis ribana

Malaxis ribana, commonly known as the slender malaxis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a short rhizome system.

Malaxis ribana is relatively rare and is often found in specific microhabitats, making it a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
