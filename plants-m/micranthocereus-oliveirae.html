Micranthocereus oliveirae

Micranthocereus oliveirae, commonly known as the Oliveira's night-blooming cactus, is a species native to Brazil.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its spiny stems and nocturnal flowering habit. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in succulent gardens.
