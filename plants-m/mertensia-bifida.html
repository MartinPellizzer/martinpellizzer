Mertensia bifida

Mertensia bifida, commonly known as the twinleaf bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Mertensia bifida is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and its role in supporting pollinators.
