Myristica iryaghedhi

Myristica iryaghedhi, commonly known as the Malabar nut, is a member of the Myristicaceae family and is native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a small evergreen tree that typically grows in moist, tropical forests. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, aromatic seeds, which are encased in a hard, woody outer covering. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries containing the edible seeds.
