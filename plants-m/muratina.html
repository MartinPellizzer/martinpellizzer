Muratina

Muratina, commonly known as the purple mangosteen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its dark purple, segmented fruit that resembles a dragon's egg. The plant's leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a glossy green surface. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and typically white or pale yellow.

The fruit contains a sweet, aromatic pulp surrounded by a thick, inedible pericarp.
