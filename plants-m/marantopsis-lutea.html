Marantopsis lutea

Marantopsis lutea, commonly known as the yellow marant or yellow arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family, which includes many ornamental and economically important species. The plant is characterized by its striking yellow foliage, which can be variegated with green and white patterns. It typically grows as a rhizomatous perennial, forming dense clumps of broad, lance-shaped leaves.

Marantopsis lutea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
