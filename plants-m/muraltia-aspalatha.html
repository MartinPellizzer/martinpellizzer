Muraltia aspalatha

Muraltia aspalatha, commonly known as the red mombasa bean, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, showy flowers that range from white to pink. The plant produces small, round pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines.

It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in coastal regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
