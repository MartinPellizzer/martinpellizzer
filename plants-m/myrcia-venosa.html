Myrcia venosa

Myrcia venosa, commonly known as the Brazilian beauty or velvet myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, while the flowers are usually pink or red, appearing in clusters.

Myrcia venosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
