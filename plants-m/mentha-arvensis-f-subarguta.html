Mentha arvensis f. subarguta

Mentha arvensis f. subarguta, commonly known as narrow-leaf field mint, is a variant of the common field mint.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and a strong, aromatic scent. This plant typically grows in moist, temperate regions and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. It has square stems and produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in herbal remedies.
