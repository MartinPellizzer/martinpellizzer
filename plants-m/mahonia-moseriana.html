Mahonia moseriana

Mahonia moseriana, commonly known as the Mexican mahonia, is a deciduous shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen holly-like leaves that are dark green and leathery. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are followed by blue-black berries. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.

Mahonia moseriana is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its low maintenance requirements.
