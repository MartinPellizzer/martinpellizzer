Mentha × morrenii

Mentha × morrenii, commonly known as the Moroccan mint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Mentha spicata and Mentha piperita.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly minty scent and its dark green, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. Mentha × morrenii is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

It is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for its purported digestive and calming properties.
