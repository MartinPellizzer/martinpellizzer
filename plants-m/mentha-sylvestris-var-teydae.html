Mentha sylvestris var. teydae

Mentha sylvestris var. teydae, commonly known as the Algerian mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its essential oils, rich in menthol and other compounds, contribute to its use in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary applications.
