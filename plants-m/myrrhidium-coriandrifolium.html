Myrrhidium coriandrifolium

Myrrhidium coriandrifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrrhplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are elongated, narrow, and have a distinctive coriander-like fragrance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters.

It is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
