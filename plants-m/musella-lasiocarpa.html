Musella lasiocarpa

Musella lasiocarpa, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, floating on the surface of freshwater bodies. The plant has a distinctive underground rhizome system that supports its massive foliage and enables it to thrive in still or slow-moving waters. Its flowers are pale yellow and bloom at night, attracting specific pollinators.

Musella lasiocarpa is notable for its ability to create a dense canopy on water surfaces, which can impact local ecosystems by altering light penetration and water temperature.
