Malpighia polytricha subsp. polytricha

Malpighia polytricha subsp.

polytricha, commonly known as the red mulberry, is a shrub native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its small, red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine.

The plant has alternate, elliptic leaves with smooth margins and produces small, white flowers clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
