Miliusa montana var. tomentosa

Miliusa montana var. tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy miliusa, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
