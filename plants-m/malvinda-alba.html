Malvinda alba

Malvinda alba, commonly known as white mallows, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton, hibiscus, and okra. The plant is characterized by its large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a soft, fuzzy texture.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
