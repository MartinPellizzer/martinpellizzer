Miscanthus sinensis var. condensatus

Miscanthus sinensis var. condensatus, commonly known as the compact Chinese silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, fountain-shaped growth habit and narrow, silver-gray leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces fluffy, pale pink to white plumes in late summer, which are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
