Matthiola flavida var. integrifolia

Matthiola flavida var. integrifolia, commonly known as the yellow wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and produces small, clustered blooms. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

The variety integrifolia is distinguished by its entire leaves, which lack the notched or lobed edges seen in other varieties.
