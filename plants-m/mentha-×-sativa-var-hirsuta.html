Mentha × sativa var. hirsuta

Mentha × sativa var. hirsuta, commonly known as woolly mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and aromatic foliage.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its strong, menthol-like scent. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. It has a square stem and opposite leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence.

Woolly mint is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for its purported cooling and digestive properties.
