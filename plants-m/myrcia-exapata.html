Myrcia exapata

Myrcia exapata, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle or red myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers and elliptical, glossy leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate.

Myrcia exapata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
