Mimosa micropteris

Mimosa micropteris, commonly known as the small-leaf mimosa, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and Georgia, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils. The plant features delicate, fern-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters. Mimosa micropteris is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important plant in its native habitat.
