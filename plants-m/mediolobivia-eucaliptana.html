Mediolobivia eucaliptana

Mediolobivia eucaliptana, commonly known as the silverleaf eucalyptus, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and aromatic oils. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive white or silver underside, giving it its common name.

The flowers are small and white or pink, often clustered in umbels, and attract various pollinators.
