Maba coriacea

Maba coriacea, commonly known as the rough bark tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, rugged bark that provides protection against environmental stressors. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

The flowers are small and clustered, producing a distinctive fruit that is an important food source for various wildlife species.
