Myrcia marliereana

Myrcia marliereana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and glossy, dark green leaves. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Myrcia marliereana is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
