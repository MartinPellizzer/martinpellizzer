Malus sieversii f. viridifasciata

Malus sieversii f. viridifasciata, commonly known as the green-banded apple, is a rare and distinctive variation of the wild apple species.

It is characterized by its striking green and yellow variegated foliage, which sets it apart from typical apple trees. This plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. The leaves exhibit a unique banding pattern, contributing to its ornamental value.

While it is not widely cultivated for fruit, it is appreciated in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
