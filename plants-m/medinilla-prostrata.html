Medinilla prostrata

Medinilla prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate medinilla, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of less than 30 centimeters. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stems. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and typically white or pale pink, appearing in clusters at the base of the leaves.

Medinilla prostrata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
