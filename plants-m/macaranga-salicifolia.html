Macaranga salicifolia

Macaranga salicifolia, commonly known as the paper mulberry, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in panicles, typically appearing in clusters of three.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that releases seeds when mature.
