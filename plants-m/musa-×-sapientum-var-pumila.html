Musa × sapientum var. pumila

Musa × sapientum var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf banana plant, is a compact variety of the banana plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its shorter stature, typically reaching heights of 1 to 2 meters, making it suitable for small spaces and container gardening. The plant produces clusters of small, edible bananas, which are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a waxy surface, typical of the Musaceae family.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
