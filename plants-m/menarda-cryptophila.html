Menarda cryptophila

Menarda cryptophila, commonly known as the dark-leaved menarda, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and densely hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a clustered inflorescence, and bloom during the warmer months.

Menarda cryptophila is often found in shaded, moist habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
