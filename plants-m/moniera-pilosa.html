Moniera pilosa

Moniera pilosa, commonly known as the hairy fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, frond-like leaves covered in fine, silky hairs.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant's reproductive structures, known as sori, are clustered on the underside of its fronds and produce spores for propagation. Moniera pilosa belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique appearance and habitat make it a notable species in botanical studies and ecological research.
