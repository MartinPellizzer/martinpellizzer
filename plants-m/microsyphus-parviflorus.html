Microsyphus parviflorus

Microsyphus parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered microsyphus, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tiny, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of white and pale pink. The plant has a low-growing habit with slender, hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
