Mentha procumbens var. ehrhartiana

Mentha procumbens var. ehrhartiana, commonly known as the Ehrhart's ground ivy, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features creeping stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats on the ground. The plant has small, heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and emits a strong, minty fragrance. Its flowers are tubular and typically violet or blue, appearing in clusters during the summer months.

This variety is often used in herbal remedies and as a decorative ground cover in gardens.
