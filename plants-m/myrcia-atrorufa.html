Myrcia atrorufa

Myrcia atrorufa, commonly known as the red-flowered myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 2 meters, featuring glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep red, depending on the stage of bloom. Myrcia atrorufa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, tropical environments.
