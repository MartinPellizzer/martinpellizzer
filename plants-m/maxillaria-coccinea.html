Maxillaria coccinea

Maxillaria coccinea, commonly known as the red-bellied orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals and a contrasting yellow lip, which make it visually striking. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are known for their strong, spicy fragrance.

Maxillaria coccinea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
