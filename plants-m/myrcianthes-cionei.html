Myrcianthes cionei

Myrcianthes cionei, commonly known as the Cione's myrtle, is a member of the Myrtaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, often showy flowers.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a fragrant, resinous scent, while its flowers are usually white or pale pink.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several small, hard seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
