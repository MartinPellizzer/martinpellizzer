Malpighia urens var. megacantha

Malpighia urens var. megacantha, commonly known as the giant granadilla, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, large, red, fleshy fruits that resemble small tomatoes. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or red in color. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

The fruit is edible, with a sweet and slightly tart flavor, and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
