Myristica glauca var. bancana

Myristica glauca var. bancana, commonly known as the bancan nut or Malayan nut, is a member of the Myristicaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree characterized by its smooth, dark grey bark and elliptical, glossy green leaves. The plant produces fragrant yellow flowers followed by distinctive three-lobed fruits that enclose the aromatic seeds. The seeds are highly valued for their nutty flavor and are used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in tropical rainforests and requires a humid, warm climate with well-drained soil.
