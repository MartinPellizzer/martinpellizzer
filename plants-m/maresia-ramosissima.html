Maresia ramosissima

Maresia ramosissima, commonly known as the hairy mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in shrubland or coastal areas, adapting to sandy or well-drained soils. It has a woody stem that branches profusely, giving it a bushy appearance.

Maresia ramosissima is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
