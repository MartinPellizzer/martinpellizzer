Mentha × morii

Mentha × morii, commonly known as Japanese catmint, is a hybrid mint species native to East Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces small, pale blue to lavender flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Mentha × morii is valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant.

Its hybrid origin results from crosses between Mentha sylvestris and Mentha × gracilis.
