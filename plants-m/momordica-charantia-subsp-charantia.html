Momordica charantia subsp. charantia

Momordica charantia subsp.

charantia, commonly known as bitter melon, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive warty, ridged fruit, which is typically green when unripe and turns orange when mature.

The plant produces small, yellow, fragrant flowers and has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. Bitter melon is widely cultivated for its edible fruit, which is used in various cuisines and traditional medicine.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
