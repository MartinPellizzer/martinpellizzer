Mentha reversa

Mentha reversa, commonly known as black mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black leaves, which distinguish it from other mint species. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Mentha reversa has a strong, spicy aroma and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its botanical features make it a unique and valuable species within the Lamiaceae family.
