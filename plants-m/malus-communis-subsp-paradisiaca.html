Malus communis subsp. paradisiaca

Malus communis subsp.

paradisiaca, commonly known as the cultivated apple, is a species of fruit tree native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its deciduous nature, with simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The tree produces clusters of small, green flowers in spring, which develop into fleshy, edible pomes by late summer.

Its fruit is highly variable in size and color, ranging from red to yellow or green, depending on the cultivar. The plant is widely cultivated for its nutritional value and use in cooking, baking, and cider production.
