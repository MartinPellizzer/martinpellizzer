Micromeles rhombifolia

Micromeles rhombifolia, commonly known as the rhombic leaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive rhomboid-shaped leaves and showy, pale pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. It is a herbaceous perennial that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
