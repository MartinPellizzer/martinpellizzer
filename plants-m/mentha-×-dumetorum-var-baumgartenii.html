Mentha × dumetorum var. baumgartenii

Mentha × dumetorum var. baumgartenii, commonly known as purple mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple to violet flower clusters, which bloom in late summer. The plant has serrated, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, growing to a height of about 30–60 cm.

Its hybrid origin contributes to its adaptability and unique chemical profile compared to other mint species.
