Matricaria pubescens

Matricaria pubescens, commonly known as the hairy chamomile, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its yellow flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant produces small, white to yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its hairy stems and leaves are a distinguishing feature, and it is often grown for its aromatic properties.

Matricaria pubescens is also used in traditional medicine for its calming and anti-inflammatory effects.
