Matricaria burchellii

Matricaria burchellii, commonly known as African Gerbera, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow central disk. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers bloom in clusters.

Matricaria burchellii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
