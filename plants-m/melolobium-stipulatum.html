Melolobium stipulatum

Melolobium stipulatum, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. The plant has compound leaves with small, leaf-like stipules at the base of each leaflet. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun.

Melolobium stipulatum is often used in ornamental gardening and is a symbol of Australia's national identity.
