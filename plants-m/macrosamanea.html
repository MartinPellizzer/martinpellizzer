Macrosamanea

Macrosamanea, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the Amazon, is a climbing plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter, often displaying vibrant colors such as pink, red, and white. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces woody capsules as fruit, which contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid tropical environments, typically near water bodies in the Amazon basin.

Due to its striking appearance and cultural significance, Macrosamanea is both ecologically and botanically notable.
