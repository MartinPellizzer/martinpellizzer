Mastixiodendron pachyclados

Mastixiodendron pachyclados, commonly known as the broad-leafed mastix tree, is a rare and ancient plant species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and produces a distinctive, aromatic resin that has been used historically for medicinal and aromatic purposes. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, sunny climate to flourish. Its unique morphology and historical significance make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
