Moronobea riparia

Moronobea riparia, commonly known as the river sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in southern Africa.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often along riverbanks and swampy areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in spikelets. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, which can help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Moronobea riparia is an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
