Marsdenia salaschiblii

Marsdenia salaschiblii, commonly known as the Salaschibli's Marsdenia, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, followed by follicles that contain the seeds. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
