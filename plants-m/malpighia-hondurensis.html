Malpighia hondurensis

Malpighia hondurensis, commonly known as the Honduran acerola, is a small evergreen shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and clusters of small, red, edible fruits. The fruits are rich in vitamin C and have a tart, acidic flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
