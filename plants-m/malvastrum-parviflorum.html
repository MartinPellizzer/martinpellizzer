Malvastrum parviflorum

Malvastrum parviflorum, commonly known as the smallflower mallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

Malvastrum parviflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
