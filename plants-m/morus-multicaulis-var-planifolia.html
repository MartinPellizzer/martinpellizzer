Morus multicaulis var. planifolia

Morus multicaulis var. planifolia, commonly known as the flat-leaf mulberry, is a variant of the mulberry tree native to China.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are edible and often consumed by both humans and animals. It thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

The tree is also known for its rapid growth and ability to adapt to various soil conditions.
