Masdevallia tarantula

Masdevallia tarantula, commonly known as the tarantula orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and long, arching inflorescences that bear numerous small, intricate flowers. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, producing fragrant, often violet or purple-colored flowers. It thrives in cool, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining, acidic potting media.

Due to its unique appearance and delicate beauty, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
