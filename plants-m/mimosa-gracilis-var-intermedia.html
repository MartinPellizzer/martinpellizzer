Mimosa gracilis var. intermedia

Mimosa gracilis var. intermedia, commonly known as the slender mimosa, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are bipinnately compound, giving it a lacy appearance. The plant produces small, yellow, spherical flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is often grown in gardens and as a houseplant.
