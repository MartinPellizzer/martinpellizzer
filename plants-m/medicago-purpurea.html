Medicago purpurea

Medicago purpurea, commonly known as purple medic, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Purple medic is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant for soil enrichment and forage.

It is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in cultivated fields.
