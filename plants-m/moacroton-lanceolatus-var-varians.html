Moacroton lanceolatus var. varians

Moacroton lanceolatus var. varians, commonly known as the variable lanceleaf, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that vary in size and coloration across different populations. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.

Due to its variability, it is often studied for its genetic and adaptive traits.
