Malapoenna riparia

Malapoenna riparia, commonly known as the river mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows near water sources such as rivers and wetlands, adapting to moist environments. It has a robust root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Malapoenna riparia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
