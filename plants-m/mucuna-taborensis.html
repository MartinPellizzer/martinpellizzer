Mucuna taborensis

Mucuna taborensis, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain flat, dark brown seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Mucuna taborensis is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of protein-rich seeds.
