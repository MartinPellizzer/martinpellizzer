Mallotus chrysocarpus

Mallotus chrysocarpus, commonly known as the golden fruit mallot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its elliptical leaves, which are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. Its fruit is distinctive, with a yellowish-orange color and a fibrous exterior.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
