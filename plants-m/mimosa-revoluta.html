Mimosa revoluta

Mimosa revoluta, commonly known as the velvet mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a climbing or shrub-like growth habit. Its leaves are sensitive to touch, curling when disturbed, a trait known as thigmonasty.

It thrives in well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
