Microelus

Microelus, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers and is native to southern Africa. The plants typically grow in rocky or sandy soils and are adapted to arid environments. Microelus species are often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where they thrive in well-drained conditions.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens.
