Muraltia pubescens

Muraltia pubescens, commonly known as the hairy muraltia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain several seeds, often used in traditional medicine. Its hairy texture and robust growth make it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Muraltia pubescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits.
