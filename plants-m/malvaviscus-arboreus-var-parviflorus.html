Malvaviscus arboreus var. parviflorus

Malvaviscus arboreus var. parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered mallow or parviflorus hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include stellate (star-shaped) hairs on the foliage and a capsule fruit that splits open to release seeds.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and shaded areas.
