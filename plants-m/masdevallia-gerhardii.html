Masdevallia gerhardii

Masdevallia gerhardii, commonly known as the Gerhard's masdevallia, is a rare and striking orchid native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It is characterized by its intricate, often colorful flowers with a unique, spurred lip that distinguishes it from other orchid species. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, often found in shaded, humid environments with well-draining soil. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that help it conserve moisture in its natural habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a challenging plant to cultivate outside its native range.
