Mentha parvula

Mentha parvula, commonly known as narrow-leaf mint, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Mentha parvula is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its aromatic, menthol-like scent.
