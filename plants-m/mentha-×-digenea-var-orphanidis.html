Mentha × digenea var. orphanidis

Mentha × digenea var. orphanidis, commonly known as the Orphanis mint, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorls along the stem, typical of the mint family. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
