Myrciaria guaquiea

Myrciaria guaquiea, commonly known as the guaquieira, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its oval, leathery leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature, often used in local culinary traditions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
