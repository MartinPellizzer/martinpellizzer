Mespilodaphne marginata var. oblonga

Mespilodaphne marginata var. oblonga, commonly known as the broadleaf tamarind, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, elliptical to ovate leaves that are usually smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, round or oblong fruits with a sweet, acidic pulp. Its botanical features make it suitable for ornamental use and traditional medicine.

The varietal name 'oblonga' refers to the elongated shape of its fruits.
