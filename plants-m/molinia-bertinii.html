Molinia bertinii

Molinia bertinii, commonly known as the Bertini's moor grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to western Europe.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils, often found in heathlands and bogs. It produces small, pale green to brownish spikelets that are clustered at the ends of its stems.

Molinia bertinii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
