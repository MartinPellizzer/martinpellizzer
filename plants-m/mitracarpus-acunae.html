Mitracarpus acunae

Mitracarpus acunae, commonly known as the Mexican cowpea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and it produces small, hard-seeded pods.

Mitracarpus acunae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible seeds.
