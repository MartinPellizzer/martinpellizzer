Metroxylon warburgii

Metroxylon warburgii, commonly known as the Warburg's palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces edible fruits that are similar to palm hearts, though they are less commonly consumed. Its growth is typically slow, and it thrives in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and potential economic uses.
