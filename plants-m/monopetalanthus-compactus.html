Monopetalanthus compactus

Monopetalanthus compactus, commonly known as the dwarf tamarack, is a small, shrub-like plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in harsh, sandy soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, adapted to reduce water loss in its dry environment.

Monopetalanthus compactus is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
