Myrtus aurantiifolia

Myrtus aurantiifolia, commonly known as the citron myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, with a distinctive aromatic scent. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and hedges.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional remedies for various ailments.
