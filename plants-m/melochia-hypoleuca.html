Melochia hypoleuca

Melochia hypoleuca, commonly known as the white melochia, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its stout, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by round, fleshy fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in disturbed areas or along riverbanks.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
