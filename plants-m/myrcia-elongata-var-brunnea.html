Myrcia elongata var. brunnea

Myrcia elongata var. brunnea, commonly known as the black myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically reddish or black when mature. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

The species is valued for its aromatic properties and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of essential oils.
