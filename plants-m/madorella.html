Madorella

Madorella, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its lack of leaves, relying instead on its roots for nutrient absorption. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in pine rocklands and hammocks.

Due to its habitat specificity and conservation status, it is considered endangered and is protected in many areas.
