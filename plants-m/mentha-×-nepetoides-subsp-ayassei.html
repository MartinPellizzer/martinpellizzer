Mentha × nepetoides subsp. ayassei

Mentha × nepetoides subsp.

ayassei, commonly known as Ayassei mint, is a hybrid mint species native to the Andes region of South America. It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly menthol-like scent and its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent in culinary applications.
