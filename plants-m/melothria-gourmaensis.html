Melothria gourmaensis

Melothria gourmaensis, commonly known as the Gourma cucumber, is a climbing vine native to West Africa.

It produces small, edible fruits that are typically green and resemble miniature cucumbers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its nutrient-rich fruit, which is used in local cuisines.

Botanically, it belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbit plants like cucumbers and squash.
