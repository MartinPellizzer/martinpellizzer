Masdevallia midas

Masdevallia midas, commonly known as the golden masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the high-altitude cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, golden-yellow flowers with intricate patterns that resemble metallic sheen. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that help it conserve moisture in its cool, humid environment.

Masdevallia midas is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively compact size.
