Mammillaria nana

Mammillaria nana, commonly known as the dwarf mammillaria, is a small cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is typically no taller than 5 centimeters. The plant has a dense covering of white to pale green tubercles, which are small, rounded bumps. It produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months.

Due to its size and ornamental appearance, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
