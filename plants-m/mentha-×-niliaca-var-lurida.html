Mentha × niliaca var. lurida

Mentha × niliaca var. lurida, commonly known as the purple mint, is a hybrid mint species characterized by its distinctive violet-colored leaves and flowers.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring square stems and opposite leaves with a serrated margin. Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils, making it useful in culinary and medicinal applications.

The flowers, which are small and clustered, attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
