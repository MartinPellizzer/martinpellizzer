Mesembryanthemum insigne

Mesembryanthemum insigne, commonly known as the noble ice plant, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in spring and summer. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal.

Mesembryanthemum insigne is also valued for its ability to prevent soil erosion in dry landscapes.
