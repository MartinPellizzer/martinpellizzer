Myrcia chrysophylla

Myrcia chrysophylla, commonly known as the golden-leaved myrtle, is a tropical shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its glossy, yellow-green leaves that give it its common name. It produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, red, fleshy berries.

Myrcia chrysophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
