Malva grossulariifolia

Malva grossulariifolia, commonly known as the Chinese mallow, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its hairy stems and fibrous roots make it a distinctive member of its botanical group.
