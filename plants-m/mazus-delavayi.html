Mazus delavayi

Mazus delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay's mazus, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with small, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue to violet flowers during the summer months. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Mazus delavayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
