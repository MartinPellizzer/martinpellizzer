Miconia silicicola

Miconia silicicola, commonly known as the silicicola miconia, is a species of flowering plant in the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, broad leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it has been introduced to other regions, though it can become invasive in certain areas.
