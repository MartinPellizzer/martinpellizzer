Madia hispida

Madia hispida, commonly known as the hairy madia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its compact stature and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Madia hispida is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators.
