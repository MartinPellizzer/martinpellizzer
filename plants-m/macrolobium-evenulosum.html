Macrolobium evenulosum

Macrolobium evenulosum, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon basin.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into woody pods containing edible seeds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, fertile soil. Its fruit, known as a cupuacu, is valued for its high nutritional content and use in various culinary and medicinal applications.

The tree plays a significant ecological role in the rainforest, providing habitat and food for numerous species.
