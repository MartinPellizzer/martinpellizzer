Monardella viridis subsp. viridis

Monardella viridis subsp.

viridis, commonly known as green ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
