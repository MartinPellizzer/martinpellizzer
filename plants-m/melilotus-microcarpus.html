Melilotus microcarpus

Melilotus microcarpus, commonly known as narrowleaf sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, yellow blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields and disturbed areas.

Melilotus microcarpus is also valued for its use in forage and as a green manure crop.
