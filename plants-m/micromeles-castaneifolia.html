Micromeles castaneifolia

Micromeles castaneifolia, commonly known as the Chinese tallow tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in catkins. The plant produces woody fruits that contain one to three smooth, kidney-shaped seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, where it can become invasive.

The species is notable for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types.
