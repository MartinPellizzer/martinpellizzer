Mazus japonicus var. leucanthus

Mazus japonicus var. leucanthus, commonly known as the white-flowered mazus, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Japan.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically opposite and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a compact, shade-tolerant ground cover.
