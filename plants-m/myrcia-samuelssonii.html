Myrcia samuelssonii

Myrcia samuelssonii, commonly known as the Samuelsson's myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that contain numerous seeds.

The plant's aromatic oils have been studied for their potential medicinal and aromatic properties.
