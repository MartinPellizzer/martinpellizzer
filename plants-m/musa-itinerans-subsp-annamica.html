Musa itinerans subsp. annamica

Musa itinerans subsp.

annamica, commonly known as the Annamite banana, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and clusters of fleshy, edible berries.

The plant typically grows in humid, lowland areas with rich, well-drained soil. It is valued for its fruit, which is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
