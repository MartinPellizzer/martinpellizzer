Mesosphaerum vesiculosum

Mesosphaerum vesiculosum, commonly known as the balloon vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces distinctive, inflated seed pods that resemble balloons, which help in seed dispersal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
