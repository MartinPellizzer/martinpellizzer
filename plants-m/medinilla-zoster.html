Medinilla zoster

Medinilla zoster, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or velvet leaf, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round, orange fruits that are edible and have a tangy flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Medinilla zoster is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and pleasant fragrance.
