Monachanthus fimbriatus

Monachanthus fimbriatus, commonly known as the fringed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers with fringed petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
