Masdevallia vargasii

Masdevallia vargasii, commonly known as the Vargas orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often colorful flowers with a unique shape. The plant typically grows as a miniature epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, delicate blooms that are highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
