Mentha leioneura

Mentha leioneura, commonly known as the white-veined mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, oval-shaped leaves with prominent white veins. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Mentha leioneura is often found in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in herbal teas and as a culinary herb.
