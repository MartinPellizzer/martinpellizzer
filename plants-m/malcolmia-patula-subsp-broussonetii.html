Malcolmia patula subsp. broussonetii

Malcolmia patula subsp.

broussonetii, commonly known as the Egyptian spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its hairy, alternate leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has a deep taproot system, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, and it is known for its ability to tolerate drought conditions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
