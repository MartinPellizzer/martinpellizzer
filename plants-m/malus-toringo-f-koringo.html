Malus toringo f. koringo

Malus toringo f. koringo, commonly known as the Koringo apple, is a rare and ornamental species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow in color, with a sweet and tangy flavor. The plant produces showy white or pink flowers in the spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its leaves are oval-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its visual appeal.

This species is valued for both its aesthetic qualities and its unique fruit characteristics.
