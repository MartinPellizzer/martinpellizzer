Malachium aquaticum var. arenarium

Malachium aquaticum var. arenarium, commonly known as sand milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the coastal regions of the eastern United States.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils near the shoreline, often in salt marshes or dunes. The plant features slender, erect stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. Its flowers are small, clustered in loose spikes, and typically range in color from white to pale purple.

The plant is known for its adaptability to saline environments and its role in supporting local pollinators.
