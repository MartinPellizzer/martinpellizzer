Moringa robusta

Moringa robusta, commonly known as the African horseradish, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stem and compound leaves that are divided into several narrow, feather-like segments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into long, slender pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. Moringa robusta is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy species.

Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.
