Malpighia caribaea

Malpighia caribaea, commonly known as the Caribbean beautyberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright red berries that appear in late summer to early autumn. The plant has simple, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, white flowers that are often overlooked. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for various bird species.
