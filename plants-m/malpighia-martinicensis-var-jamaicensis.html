Malpighia martinicensis var. jamaicensis

Malpighia martinicensis var. jamaicensis, commonly known as the Jamaican granadilla, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean.

It produces bright red, fleshy berries that are rich in vitamin C and have a tart flavor. The plant belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its small, opposite leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

The berries are used in culinary applications and traditional medicine in various regions.
