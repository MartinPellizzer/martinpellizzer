Meniscium elongatum

Meniscium elongatum, commonly known as the slender meniscus, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, with lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.

Meniscium elongatum is valued in gardens for its delicate appearance and adaptability to Mediterranean climates.
