Madhuca sepilokensis

Madhuca sepilokensis, commonly known as the Solomon Islands nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces edible fruits that contain a single seed, which is encased in a hard, woody shell. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for sustainable harvesting, it is of interest in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
