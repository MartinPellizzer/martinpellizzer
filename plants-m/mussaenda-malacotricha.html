Mussaenda malacotricha

Mussaenda malacotricha, commonly known as the red-flowered Mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered red bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves with a glossy surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are less conspicuous than the bracts.

Mussaenda malacotricha is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
