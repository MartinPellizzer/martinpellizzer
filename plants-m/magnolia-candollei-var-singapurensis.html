Magnolia candollei var. singapurensis

Magnolia candollei var. singapurensis, commonly known as the Singapore magnolia, is a rare and endemic species native to Singapore.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy on top. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat loss, it is considered a critically endangered species.
