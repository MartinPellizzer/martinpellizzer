Myrica esquirolii

Myrica esquirolii, commonly known as the Esquiroli's wax myrtle, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of California and southern Oregon.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are typically covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its arid environment. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are pollinated by insects, followed by berries that are an important food source for wildlife. Myrica esquirolii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

Its ecological role includes providing shelter and sustenance for various native species.
