Metasocratea hecatonandra

Metasocratea hecatonandra, commonly known as the thousand-flowered tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in dense clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has a robust, multi-stemmed structure with thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its native habitat. Its flowers emit a strong fragrance, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in some regions.
