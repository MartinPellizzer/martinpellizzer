Mentha × piperita var. pennsylvanica

Mentha × piperita var. pennsylvanica, commonly known as American peppermint, is a hybrid mint species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers. The plant is a perennial herb with square, hairy stems and a strong menthol scent. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardens and essential oil production.
