Mikania nigropunctulata var. cryptodonta

Mikania nigropunctulata var. cryptodonta, commonly known as the dark-punctured morning glory, is a vigorous climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves with deep lobes and is characterized by its distinctive dark purple to black veins. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, blooming in clusters. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forests.

Its rapid growth and invasive potential make it a notable species in ecological studies.
