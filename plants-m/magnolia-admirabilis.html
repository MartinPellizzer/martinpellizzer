Magnolia admirabilis

Magnolia admirabilis, commonly known as the Mexican magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
