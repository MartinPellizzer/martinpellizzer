Melastoma cernuum

Melastoma cernuum, commonly known as the drooping melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its erect, shrub-like growth habit. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences, with showy, often pink or purple petals.

Melastoma cernuum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
