Mediocactus inermis

Mediocactus inermis, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, apple-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Mediocactus inermis is a relatively low-maintenance plant, making it popular among cactus enthusiasts.
