Memora pubescens

Memora pubescens, commonly known as the woolly begonia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, or pubescent, stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Memora pubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
