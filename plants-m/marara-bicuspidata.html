Marara bicuspidata

Marara bicuspidata, commonly known as the two-toothed orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structure. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a distinctive two-toothed appearance at the base of the petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
