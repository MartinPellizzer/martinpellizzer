Miconia mucronata

Miconia mucronata, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a fleshy, berry-like structure that turns red when mature, containing numerous seeds.

Miconia mucronata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets.
