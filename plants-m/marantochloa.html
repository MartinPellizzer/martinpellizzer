Marantochloa

Marantochloa, commonly known as the arrowroot plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Marantaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its ornamental leaves, which are often brightly colored and textured. The plant typically grows as a rhizomatous herb, with upright stems and lance-shaped foliage. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually clustered in inflorescences.

Marantochloa species are valued in horticulture for their aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
