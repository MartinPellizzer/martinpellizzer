Machaerium nyctitans var. gardneri

Machaerium nyctitans var. gardneri, commonly known as the night-flowering machaerium, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, branching stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant is notable for its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its blooming period typically occurs during the warmer months, and it thrives in well-drained soils with ample sunlight.

The plant's ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and unique nocturnal flowering habit.
