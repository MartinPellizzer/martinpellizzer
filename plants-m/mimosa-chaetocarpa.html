Mimosa chaetocarpa

Mimosa chaetocarpa, commonly known as the hairy mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and its ability to close its leaves in response to touch, a phenomenon called thigmonasty. The plant produces small, yellowish-pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and unique behavior, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
