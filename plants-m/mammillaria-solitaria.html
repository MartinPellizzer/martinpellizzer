Mammillaria solitaria

Mammillaria solitaria, commonly known as the solitary mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is a small, solitary plant with a globular stem that is typically covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
