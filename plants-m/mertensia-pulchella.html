Mertensia pulchella

Mertensia pulchella, commonly known as bluebells, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has basal leaves that are lanceolate and often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Mertensia pulchella is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
