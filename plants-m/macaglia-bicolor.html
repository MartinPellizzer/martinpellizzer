Macaglia bicolor

Macaglia bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored macaglia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking bi-colored flowers, which typically feature a white base with a pink or red hue near the center. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Macaglia bicolor is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
