Musa sundaica

Musa sundaica, commonly known as the Sunda banana, is a species of banana plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and produces clusters of fruit that are typically smaller than those of the common banana. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Musa sundaica is often cultivated for its edible fruit, which has a sweet and creamy texture.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities due to its striking foliage.
