Marrubium leonuroides var. plumosum

Marrubium leonuroides var. plumosum, commonly known as the plumose horehound, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features hairy, gray-green leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic leaves and medicinal properties have historically been valued in traditional herbal medicine.
