Marsypianthes hyptoides var. eriocephala

Marsypianthes hyptoides var. eriocephala, commonly known as the woolly mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
