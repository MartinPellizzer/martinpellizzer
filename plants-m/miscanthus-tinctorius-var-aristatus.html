Miscanthus tinctorius var. aristatus

Miscanthus tinctorius var. aristatus, commonly known as the aristate Japanese silver grass, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and panicles of silky, silver-green flowers that appear in late summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

This variety is also noted for its potential in bioenergy production due to its high biomass yield.
