Mammillaria dasyacantha

Mammillaria dasyacantha, commonly known as the Desert Hedgehog Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, white spines that provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and desert gardens.
