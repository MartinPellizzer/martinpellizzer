Mozartia oligostemon

Mozartia oligostemon, commonly known as the silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-blue foliage and delicate, feathery inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its ornamental value comes from its elegant appearance and ability to form dense, low-growing clumps.

It is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and drought tolerance.
