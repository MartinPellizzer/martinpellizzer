Monardella villosa var. globosa

Monardella villosa var. globosa, commonly known as globeflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its globular, clustered flower heads that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, which help it retain moisture in dry conditions. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and meadows.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
