Mikania auyantepuensis

Mikania auyantepuensis, commonly known as the Paraguay morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and hairy stems. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a rough texture.

Mikania auyantepuensis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
