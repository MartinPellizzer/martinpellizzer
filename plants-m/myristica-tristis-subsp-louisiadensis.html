Myristica tristis subsp. louisiadensis

Myristica tristis subsp.

louisiadensis, commonly known as the Malabar cardamom, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Western Ghats of India. It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, egg-shaped seeds enclosed in a red, fleshy aril.

The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by the distinctive seed pods. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its medicinal and aromatic properties.

The subspecies louisiadensis is distinguished by its unique morphological features and is found in specific regions of the Indian subcontinent.
