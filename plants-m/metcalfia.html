Metcalfia

Metcalfia, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical Africa.

These plants are characterized by their compact, bushy growth habit and showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors. The leaves are typically velvety and heart-shaped, often with a waxy texture. Metcalfia species are popular as houseplants due to their ability to thrive in low-light conditions and their attractive, long-lasting blooms.

They are also valued for their ornamental appeal and ease of care.
