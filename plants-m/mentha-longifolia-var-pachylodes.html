Mentha longifolia var. pachylodes

Mentha longifolia var. pachylodes, commonly known as woolly mint, is a variant of the spearmint species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thick, hairy leaves and a strong, aromatic scent. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for their refreshing and cooling properties.

The flowers are small and pale blue or purple, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
