Mesembryanthemum magnipunctatum

Mesembryanthemum magnipunctatum, commonly known as the giant dot mesem, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its scientific name reflects its distinctive punctuated appearance, with small dots on its foliage.
