Mitracarpus frigidus var. andinus

Mitracarpus frigidus var. andinus, commonly known as the Andean mitracarpus, is a perennial herb native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy, grayish-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and delicate species.
