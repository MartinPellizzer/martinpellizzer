Musa tonkinensis

Musa tonkinensis, commonly known as the Chinese banana, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and clustered inflorescences. The plant produces edible berries that are typically consumed raw or cooked. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
