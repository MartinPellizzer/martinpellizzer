Malva obtusiloba

Malva obtusiloba, commonly known as the woolly mallow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, woolly leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its flowers are composed of five petals and numerous stamens, giving them a distinctive, fluffy appearance.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
