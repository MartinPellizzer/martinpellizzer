Monardella villosa subsp. subserrata

Monardella villosa subsp.

subserrata, commonly known as the hairy lemon mint, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It features upright, branched stems covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and have a distinct lemony scent when crushed.

It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
