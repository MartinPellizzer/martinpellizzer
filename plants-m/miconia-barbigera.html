Miconia barbigera

Miconia barbigera, commonly known as the hairy miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically borne on short pedicels. Its fruit is a fleshy, berry-like structure that turns red when mature.

Miconia barbigera is notable for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.
