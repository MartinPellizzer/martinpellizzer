Mentha arvensis subsp. agrestis

Mentha arvensis subsp.

agrestis, commonly known as field mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.

The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are aromatic when crushed. Its small, pale blue to purple flowers appear in whorled clusters during the summer months.

Field mint is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
