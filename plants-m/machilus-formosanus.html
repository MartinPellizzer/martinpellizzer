Machilus formosanus

Machilus formosanus, commonly known as the Formosan machilus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Taiwan and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in the axils of the leaves, and it produces small, dark blue berries.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
