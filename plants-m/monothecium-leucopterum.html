Monothecium leucopterum

Monothecium leucopterum, commonly known as the white-winged lichen, is a foliose lichen that forms a flat, leaf-like structure.

It is characterized by its pale to white thallus with distinctive, elongated lobes that often have a slightly curled margin. The lichen thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically on bark or soil in temperate regions. Its reproductive structures, called soredia, are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread.

The presence of Monothecium leucopterum is often an indicator of a healthy, undisturbed ecosystem.
