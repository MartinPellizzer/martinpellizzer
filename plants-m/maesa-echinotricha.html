Maesa echinotricha

Maesa echinotricha, commonly known as the hairy maesa, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant features small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its leaves are leathery and covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

Maesa echinotricha is often found in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
