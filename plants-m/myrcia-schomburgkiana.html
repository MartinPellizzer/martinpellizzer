Myrcia schomburgkiana

Myrcia schomburgkiana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

Myrcia schomburgkiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
