Malus rhodopaea

Malus rhodopaea, commonly known as the Greek apple or red apple tree, is a species of ornamental tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, red or yellow apples that are edible but not typically cultivated for commercial use. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant foliage and seasonal blooms, which add visual interest throughout the growing season.
