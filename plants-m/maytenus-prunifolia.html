Maytenus prunifolia

Maytenus prunifolia, commonly known as the appleleaf mayten, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that resemble small apples, hence its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by yellowish or red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Maytenus prunifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
