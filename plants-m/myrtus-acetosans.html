Myrtus acetosans

Myrtus acetosans, commonly known as sweet myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically edible and have a sweet, aromatic flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and dry environments.

Myrtus acetosans is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant due to its fragrance and hardy nature.
