Miconia heptamera

Miconia heptamera, commonly known as the seven-lobed miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves with seven lobes. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, which attract various pollinators. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Miconia heptamera is often found in humid, lowland forests and is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets.
