Medicago lappacea var. brachyacantha

Medicago lappacea var. brachyacantha, commonly known as the short-stalked goat's rue, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, pale blue to purple flowers clustered in upright spikes, and its leaves are trifoliate with rounded leaflets. The plant has a fibrous root system and is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils. It is often found in disturbed areas and is used in some traditional herbal remedies.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment.
