Mimosa gracilis f. glabrifolia

Mimosa gracilis f. glabrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed mimosa, is a variant of the native Australian plant Mimosa gracilis.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairless stems and small, fern-like leaves that are typically arranged in pairs. The plant produces fluffy, yellow flower heads that appear in late winter to early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats like grasslands and coastal areas.

Due to its delicate appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens as an ornamental plant.
