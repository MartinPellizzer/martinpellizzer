Memecylon gracilipedicellatum

Memecylon gracilipedicellatum, commonly known as the slender-leaf memecylon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with smooth or slightly hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
