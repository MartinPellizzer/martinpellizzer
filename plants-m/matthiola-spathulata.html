Matthiola spathulata

Matthiola spathulata, commonly known as the broadleaf stock, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. Native to the Mediterranean region, this plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.

The plant is also valued for its historical use in herbal medicine.
