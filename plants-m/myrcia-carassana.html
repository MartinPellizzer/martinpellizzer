Myrcia carassana

Myrcia carassana, commonly known as Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy red flowers. The plant produces small, orange berries that are edible but have a sharp, acidic taste. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas.

Myrcia carassana has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
