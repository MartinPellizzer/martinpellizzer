Mimosa juglandifolia

Mimosa juglandifolia, commonly known as the golden mimosa, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a shallow root system that helps it thrive in dry conditions. Mimosa juglandifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for erosion control in sandy or rocky soils.
