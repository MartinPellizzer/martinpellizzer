Mussaenda tetracantha

Mussaenda tetracantha, commonly known as the four-spined Mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that consist of clusters of small, white or pink flowers surrounded by bright yellow bracts. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with prominent veins and a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Mussaenda tetracantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
