Myosotis idaea var. cadmea

Myosotis idaea var. cadmea, commonly known as the glacier flower or snow beauty, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It produces small, delicate blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a creeping stem with basal leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in tundra and rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to survive in harsh, cold climates.
