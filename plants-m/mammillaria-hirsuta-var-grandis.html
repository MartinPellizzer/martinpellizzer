Mammillaria hirsuta var. grandis

Mammillaria hirsuta var. grandis, commonly known as the giant hairy mammillaria, is a succulent cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, white woolly covering that covers the stems and spines, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright sunlight, making it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in arid environments.
