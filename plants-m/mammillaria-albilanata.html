Mammillaria albilanata

Mammillaria albilanata, commonly known as the White-Flowered Hedgehog Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines and small, rounded tubercles. The plant produces pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate contrast to its otherwise muted appearance. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and desert gardens.
