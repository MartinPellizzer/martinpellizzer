Malva perpusilla

Malva perpusilla, commonly known as the common mallow, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces clusters of pale pink to white flowers. Its flowers are surrounded by velvety, fuzzy bracts, which are characteristic of the Malvaceae family.

The plant is known for its mucilaginous properties, making it useful in traditional medicine and as a food source for certain animals.
