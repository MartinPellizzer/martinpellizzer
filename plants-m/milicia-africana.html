Milicia africana

Milicia africana, commonly known as African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Moraceae and is characterized by its thick, corky bark that provides resistance to pests and diseases. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by edible fruits that contain a fatty seed kernel. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture, while its roots are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its economic and ecological contributions in its native habitat.
