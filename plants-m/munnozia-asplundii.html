Munnozia asplundii

Munnozia asplundii, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features hairy, gray-green foliage and produces showy, yellow flowers with prominent, lobed petals. Its botanical structure is adapted to conserve water, with thick, fleshy leaves and a deep root system.

Munnozia asplundii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
