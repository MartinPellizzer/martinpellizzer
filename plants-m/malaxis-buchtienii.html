Malaxis buchtienii

Malaxis buchtienii, commonly known as the Buchtien's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and delicate, greenish-white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or rocks without deriving nutrients from them. Its roots form a symbiotic relationship with specific fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption.

Due to its limited habitat and threats from deforestation, Malaxis buchtienii is considered a vulnerable species.
