Mandevilla trianae

Mandevilla trianae, commonly known as the flame vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces showy, cascading blooms throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for ornamental purposes. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Mandevilla trianae is often cultivated as a climbing plant, capable of reaching lengths of several meters when supported by a trellis or wall.
