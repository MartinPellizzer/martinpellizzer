Monardella villosa var. sheltonii

Monardella villosa var. sheltonii, commonly known as Shelton's hairy monardella, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and clusters of pink to lavender flowers. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
