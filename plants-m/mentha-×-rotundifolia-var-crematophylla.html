Mentha × rotundifolia var. crematophylla

Mentha × rotundifolia var. crematophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mint, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both Mentha rotundifolia and Mentha crematophylla.

It is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia and is recognized for its aromatic, slightly minty fragrance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture. Its flowers are small, tubular, and often clustered in spikes, producing nectar that attracts pollinators.

This variety is valued in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb for its refreshing scent and flavor.
