Mallotus brevipes

Mallotus brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed mallot, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves with a smooth upper surface and hairy underside. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
