Mesosetum compressum

Mesosetum compressum, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its root system is fibrous and helps stabilize soil, making it useful for erosion control.

Mesosetum compressum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
