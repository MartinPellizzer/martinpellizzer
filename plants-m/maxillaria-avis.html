Maxillaria avis

Maxillaria avis, commonly known as the "hummingbird orchid," is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often colorful flowers that resemble the beaks of hummingbirds, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually fragrant and bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

The botanical features of Maxillaria avis include long, slender petals and a unique, spurred lip that aids in pollination.
