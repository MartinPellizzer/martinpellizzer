Medicago polymorpha var. arabica

Medicago polymorpha var. arabica, commonly known as the Turkish clover, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. This plant has a deep root system that helps it thrive in dry and poor soils. It is often used in forage and soil stabilization due to its hardiness and nitrogen-fixing ability.

Its variability in growth form and habitat preference makes it an important species in ecological studies.
