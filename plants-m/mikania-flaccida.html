Mikania flaccida

Mikania flaccida, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are usually light green. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Mikania flaccida is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and along stream banks.
