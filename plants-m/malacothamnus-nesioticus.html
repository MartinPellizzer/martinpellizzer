Malacothamnus nesioticus

Malacothamnus nesioticus, commonly known as the Sierra foothill goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.
