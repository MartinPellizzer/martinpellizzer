Melanthera tenuis

Melanthera tenuis, commonly known as slender melanthera, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pale green to white, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Melanthera tenuis is valued for its ecological role in supporting local insect populations and its aesthetic appeal in native gardens.
