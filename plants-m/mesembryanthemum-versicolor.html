Mesembryanthemum versicolor

Mesembryanthemum versicolor, commonly known as the variable ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in a variety of colors, including pink, purple, and white, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Mesembryanthemum versicolor is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and salty conditions, which contributes to its hardiness in arid environments.
