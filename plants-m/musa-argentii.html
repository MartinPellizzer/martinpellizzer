Musa argentii

Musa argentii, commonly known as the silver banana, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves that have a distinctive silvery-white underside. The plant produces striking, elongated flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its fruit, which is edible, resembles a banana but is smaller and has a more intense flavor.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, Musa argentii is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
