Memora contracta

Memora contracta, commonly known as the small-leafed memora, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Memorbacaceae and is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. Its flowers are borne on slender pedicels and are often clustered in dense inflorescences.

Memora contracta prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.
