Muscari fertile

Muscari fertile, commonly known as grape hyacinth, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant produces strap-like leaves that are narrow and grass-like in appearance. Muscari fertile thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

It is also known for its early spring blooming habit, often appearing before many other spring-flowering plants.
