Maxillaria marmoliana

Maxillaria marmoliana, commonly known as the Marmoliana orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features long, slender pseudobulbs and produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it blooms during the spring and summer seasons.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
