Mallotus anisophyllus

Mallotus anisophyllus, commonly known as the rough-leaved mallot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its distinctive, rough-textured leaves that are often deeply lobed. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a three-winged capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Mallotus anisophyllus is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental leaves and potential medicinal properties.
