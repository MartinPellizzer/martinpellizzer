Macrolobium colombianum var. metaense

Macrolobium colombianum var. metaense, commonly known as the Meta river orchid, is a striking orchid species native to the Colombian Andes.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with vivid purple and white petals, which bloom during the dry season. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments at mid to high elevations. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, aiding in nutrient absorption.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its ecological role in cloud forest ecosystems.
