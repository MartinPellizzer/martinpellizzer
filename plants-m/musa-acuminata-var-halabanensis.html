Musa acuminata var. halabanensis

Musa acuminata var. halabanensis, commonly known as the Halaban banana, is a rare and less commonly cultivated variety of banana.

It is native to the island of Halaban in the southern part of the Maluku Islands, Indonesia. This plant is characterized by its slender, elongated pseudostem and produces small, clustered fruits that are typically not suitable for commercial production. The leaves are large, broad, and have a waxy surface, typical of many banana species.

It is of interest to botanists and plant enthusiasts due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
