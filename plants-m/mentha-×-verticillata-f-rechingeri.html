Mentha × verticillata f. rechingeri

Mentha × verticillata f. rechingeri, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water mint, is a hybrid species within the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, narrow leaves and its preference for moist, aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats. The plant typically grows in shallow water or along the edges of ponds and streams. It produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stem.

This variety is valued for its aromatic foliage and its role in wetland ecosystems.
