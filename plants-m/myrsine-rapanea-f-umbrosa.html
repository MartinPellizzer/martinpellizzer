Myrsine rapanea f. umbrosa

Myrsine rapanea f. umbrosa, commonly known as the shady myrsine, is a compact shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features small, dark green, glossy leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a suitable ornamental plant for shaded garden settings.
