Malus communis var. chinensis

Malus communis var. chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese apple or Pingshu, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental white flowers and red, edible fruits. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated margins, while the bark is smooth and grayish-brown. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional Chinese horticulture.
