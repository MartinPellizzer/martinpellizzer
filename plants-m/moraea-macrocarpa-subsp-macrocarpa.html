Moraea macrocarpa subsp. macrocarpa

Moraea macrocarpa subsp.

macrocarpa, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It is a perennial herb with striking, showy inflorescences composed of numerous tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces bulbous corms as storage organs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
