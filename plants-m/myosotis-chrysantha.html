Myosotis chrysantha

Myosotis chrysantha, commonly known as the golden forget-me-not, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of small, bright yellow flowers, which are typically five-petaled and resemble miniature sunflowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, with a rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its cheerful, sunny appearance.
