Momordica procera

Momordica procera, commonly known as the horned melon or African horned cucumber, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It produces large, spiky, green fruits that resemble a horned melon, with a thick, leathery rind and a sweet, fibrous interior. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit.

The botanical features of Momordica procera make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
