Medicago constricta

Medicago constricta, commonly known as narrowleaf clover, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and improves soil fertility through nitrogen fixation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as grasslands and disturbed sites.

Medicago constricta is also valued for its forage qualities and ornamental appeal.
