Markea verrucosa

Markea verrucosa, commonly known as the疣状马 kè plant, is a unique species native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wart-like protrusions on its leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often near streams or in dense forest undergrowth. Its leaves are broad and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

Markea verrucosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
