Masdevallia triangularis

Masdevallia triangularis, commonly known as the triangular masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive triangular-shaped pseudobulbs and lanceolate leaves. The flowers are typically small and borne in clusters, often in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in cool, humid environments with high light exposure.

This orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
