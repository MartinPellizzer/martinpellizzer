Melastoma sanguineum

Melastoma sanguineum, commonly known as the bloodroot melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories and has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Melastoma sanguineum is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting various pollinators.
