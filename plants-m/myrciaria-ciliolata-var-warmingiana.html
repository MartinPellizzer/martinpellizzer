Myrciaria ciliolata var. warmingiana

Myrciaria ciliolata var. warmingiana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Ceraria ciliolata*, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It features small, oval leaves with finely toothed margins and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is notable for its edible red berries, which are rich in antioxidants and have a tart, tangy flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
