Mauritia limnophylla

Mauritia limnophylla, commonly known as the swamp palm, is a species of palm native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in wetland and swampy environments, often growing in dense clusters near water bodies. Its fruit is a drupe that contains a single seed, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat for various wildlife.

Due to habitat loss, Mauritia limnophylla is considered vulnerable in some regions.
