Mammillaria macdougalii

Mammillaria macdougalii, commonly known as the MacDougal's hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. It is a popular choice for rock gardens due to its compact size and ornamental appearance.

Mammillaria macdougalii is relatively easy to care for, requiring minimal water and well-draining soil.
