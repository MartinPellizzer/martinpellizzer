Moquiniastrum cordatum var. mollissimum

Moquiniastrum cordatum var. mollissimum, commonly known as the velvetleaf spikerush, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The leaves are broad, flat, and covered in a fine, velvety texture, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
