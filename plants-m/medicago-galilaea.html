Medicago galilaea

Medicago galilaea, commonly known as the Galilean medic, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of the Middle East.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, elliptical pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Medicago galilaea is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
