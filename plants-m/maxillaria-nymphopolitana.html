Maxillaria nymphopolitana

Maxillaria nymphopolitana, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb that supports its foliage and flowers. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a distinctive lip. Its leaves are grass-like and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Maxillaria nymphopolitana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
