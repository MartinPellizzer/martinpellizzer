Macropidia fuliginosa

Macropidia fuliginosa, commonly known as the black grass tree, is an endemic species of Western Australia.

It is a tall, woody plant with a single trunk and a crown of long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters at the base of the leaves. It is adapted to arid environments and can survive in poor soil conditions.

The black grass tree is also known for its slow growth and long lifespan, sometimes reaching over 100 years.
