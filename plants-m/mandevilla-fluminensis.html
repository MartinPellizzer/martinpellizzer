Mandevilla fluminensis

Mandevilla fluminensis, commonly known as the Brazilian mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a woody vine that produces vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge and can grow up to 10 meters in length. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Mandevilla fluminensis is also valued for its ability to climb and its long blooming season.
