Melastoma membranaceum

Melastoma membranaceum, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces fleshy, orange-red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Melastoma membranaceum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
