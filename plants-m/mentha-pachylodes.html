Mentha pachylodes

Mentha pachylodes, commonly known as the giant mint, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its robust, aromatic foliage and can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a strong minty scent.

Mentha pachylodes is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
