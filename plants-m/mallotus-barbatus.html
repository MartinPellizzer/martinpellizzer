Mallotus barbatus

Mallotus barbatus, commonly known as the hibiscus tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces capsules that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark and leaves of Mallotus barbatus have been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
