Magnolia pastazaensis

Magnolia pastazaensis, commonly known as the Pastaza magnolia, is a rare and endemic species found in the Ecuadorian Andes.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has broad, ovate leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Its bark is smooth and gray, with a distinct aromatic scent.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays an important role in the biodiversity of its native habitat.
