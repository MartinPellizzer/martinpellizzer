Monnina richardiana var. richardiana

Monnina richardiana var. richardiana, commonly known as the redroot flax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 15 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for dryland gardens.
