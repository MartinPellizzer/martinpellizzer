Medinilla macrophylla

Medinilla macrophylla, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or velvet leaf, is a tropical shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that are often a deep green or reddish-brown in color. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil.

Medinilla macrophylla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
