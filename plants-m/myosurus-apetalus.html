Myosurus apetalus

Myosurus apetalus, commonly known as the barren willow, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in dense clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often overlooked due to their lack of showy petals. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

Myosurus apetalus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and its ability to tolerate harsh alpine conditions.
