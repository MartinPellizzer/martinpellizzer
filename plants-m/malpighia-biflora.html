Malpighia biflora

Malpighia biflora, commonly known as the twin-flower acerola, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malpighiaceae.

It is native to Central and South America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are rich in vitamin C and other nutrients. Its flowers are typically pink or white, and it has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the consumption of its fruit.
