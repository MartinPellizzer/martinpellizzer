Masdevallia royi

Masdevallia royi, commonly known as the Royal Masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, often colorful flower structure with long, slender petals and a distinctive, spurred lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in a range of hues, including red, orange, and yellow, making it a striking addition to botanical collections.

Masdevallia royi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
