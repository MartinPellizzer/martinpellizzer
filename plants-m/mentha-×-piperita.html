Mentha × piperita

Mentha × piperita, commonly known as peppermint, is a hybrid mint species resulting from the cross between Mentha spicata and Mentha viridis.

It is characterized by its dark green, slightly hairy leaves and its strong, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems. Peppermint is a perennial herb native to Europe and temperate regions of Asia.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its essential oils and culinary uses.
