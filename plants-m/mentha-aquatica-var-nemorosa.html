Mentha aquatica var. nemorosa

Mentha aquatica var. nemorosa, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and stream banks in temperate regions.

It features square, ribbed stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers arranged in whorls along the stem, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is valued for its aromatic leaves, which contain menthol and other volatile oils.

This variety is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for its cooling and soothing properties.
