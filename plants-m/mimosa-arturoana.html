Mimosa arturoana

Mimosa arturoana, commonly known as the golden mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, spherical seed pods that split open when mature, dispersing its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen make it both ecologically and agriculturally significant.
