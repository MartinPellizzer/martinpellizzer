Moritzia dasyantha var. sellowiana

Moritzia dasyantha var. sellowiana, commonly known as the Sellow's moritzia, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive grayish-green hue due to the presence of fine pubescence.

The flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.
