Miconia leafallax

Miconia leafallax, commonly known as the tropical beauty leaf, is a member of the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are often bright green with a waxy texture. Its flowers are showy, with five petals that range in color from white to pink, attracting various pollinators.

However, it is considered an invasive species in some areas due to its rapid growth and ability to outcompete native vegetation.
