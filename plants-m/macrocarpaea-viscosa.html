Macrocarpaea viscosa

Macrocarpaea viscosa, commonly known as the sticky leaf plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, waxy leaves that have a sticky texture due to the presence of glandular trichomes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves contain compounds that have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.

The plant's botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions, contributing to its widespread distribution.
