Melica purpurascens

Melica purpurascens, commonly known as purple false brome, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, upright stems that are often tinged with a purple hue, especially along the nodes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.
