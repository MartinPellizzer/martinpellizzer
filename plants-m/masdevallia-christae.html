Masdevallia christae

Masdevallia christae, commonly known as the Christ's masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that bloom in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial or epiphytic orchid, depending on its habitat. Its leaves are usually broad and elliptical, providing a lush green backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

Masdevallia christae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
