Macroclinium cordesii

Macroclinium cordesii, commonly known as the heartleaf macroclinium, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, orchid-like flowers in shades of white and yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
