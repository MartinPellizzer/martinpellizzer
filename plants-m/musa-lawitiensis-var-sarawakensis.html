Musa lawitiensis var. sarawakensis

Musa lawitiensis var. sarawakensis, commonly known as the Sarawak banana, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and clustered inflorescences. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, warm climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
