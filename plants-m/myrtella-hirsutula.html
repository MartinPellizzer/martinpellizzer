Myrtella hirsutula

Myrtella hirsutula, commonly known as the hairy myrtle, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or black when mature. Myrtella hirsutula is often found in moist, acidic soils in wetlands or along stream banks.

It has a compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
