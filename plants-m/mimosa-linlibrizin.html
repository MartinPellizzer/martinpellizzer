Mimosa linlibrizin

Mimosa linlibrizin, commonly known as the pink mimosa, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its soft, fern-like foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in the spring and early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
