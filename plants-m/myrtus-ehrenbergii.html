Myrtus ehrenbergii

Myrtus ehrenbergii, commonly known as the desert myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the arid regions of the Arabian Peninsula and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It is well adapted to hot, dry climates and requires minimal water once established.

Myrtus ehrenbergii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in harsh environments.
