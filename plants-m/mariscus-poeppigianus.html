Mariscus poeppigianus

Mariscus poeppigianus, commonly known as the desert marigold, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its yellow or orange flowers and hairy, succulent leaves adapted to arid environments. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and typically grows in rocky soils and dry habitats. Its flowers bloom in the spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Mariscus poeppigianus is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
