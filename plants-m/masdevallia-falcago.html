Masdevallia falcago

Masdevallia falcago, commonly known as the hooked masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often curved inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small, with intricate patterns and a variety of colors, ranging from white to pink and purple.

Masdevallia falcago is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively compact size.
