Micromeria serpyllimorpha

Micromeria serpyllimorpha, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandmat, is a low-growing annual herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features small, gray-green leaves that are typically narrow and covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Micromeria serpyllimorpha is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
