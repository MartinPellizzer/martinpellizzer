Myrcia curatellifolia var. grandifolia

Myrcia curatellifolia var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaved myrcia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often fragrant. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests and rainforests.

Myrcia curatellifolia var. grandifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
