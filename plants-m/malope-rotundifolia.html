Malope rotundifolia

Malope rotundifolia, commonly known as round-leaf mallows, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped or rounded leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice in xeriscaping.
