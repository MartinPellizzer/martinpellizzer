Megaskepasma erythrochlamys

Megaskepasma erythrochlamys, commonly known as the red-chambered orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange floral structures, which are adapted for specific pollination by certain insect species. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are highly specialized, with intricate petal arrangements that facilitate pollination through visual and olfactory cues.
