Mentzelia davidsoniana

Mentzelia davidsoniana, commonly known as Davidson's blazingstar, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and slopes. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.

Mentzelia davidsoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
