Melilotus bicolor

Melilotus bicolor, commonly known as bi-color sweet clover, is a flowering plant in the Fabaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions. The plant is characterized by its distinctive pink and white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Melilotus bicolor is often grown as a forage crop and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
