Mikania premnifolia

Mikania premnifolia, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and fields, due to its adaptability.

Mikania premnifolia is also known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain environments.
