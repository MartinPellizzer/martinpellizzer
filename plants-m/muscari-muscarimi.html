Muscari muscarimi

Muscari muscarimi, commonly known as the grape hyacinth, is a bulbous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, resembling grapes, hence its common name. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge in early spring.

Muscari muscarimi is known for its hardiness and ability to naturalize over time, creating dense colonies in suitable conditions.
