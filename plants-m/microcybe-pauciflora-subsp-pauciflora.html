Microcybe pauciflora subsp. pauciflora

Microcybe pauciflora subsp.

pauciflora, commonly known as the slender microcybe, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale pink.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands. Its inflorescence consists of dense spikes, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain minute seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
