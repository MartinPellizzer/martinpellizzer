Melaleuca viridiflora var. rubriflora

Melaleuca viridiflora var. rubriflora, commonly known as the red-flowered paperbark tree, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a reddish or pinkish inner layer. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and wetland areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
