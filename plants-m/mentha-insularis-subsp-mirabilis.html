Mentha insularis subsp. mirabilis

Mentha insularis subsp.

mirabilis, commonly known as the island mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies and as a culinary herb.
