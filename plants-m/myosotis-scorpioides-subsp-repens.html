Myosotis scorpioides subsp. repens

Myosotis scorpioides subsp.

repens, commonly known as the common forget-me-not, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, creeping stems that root at the nodes.

The plant produces clusters of small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and streambanks.
