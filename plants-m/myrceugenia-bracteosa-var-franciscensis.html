Myrceugenia bracteosa var. franciscensis

Myrceugenia bracteosa var. franciscensis, commonly known as the Franciscan myrtle, is a species native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, urn-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red when mature, containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in sandy soils near the shoreline.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in local ecosystems.
