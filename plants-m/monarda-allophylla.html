Monarda allophylla

Monarda allophylla, commonly known as narrowleaf horsemint, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its showy, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in whorls along the upper stems.

Monarda allophylla is often found in moist woodlands and meadows, where it thrives in partial to full sunlight.
