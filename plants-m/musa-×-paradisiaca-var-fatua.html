Musa × paradisiaca var. fatua

Musa × paradisiaca var. fatua, commonly known as wild banana, is a wild variety of the banana plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and clusters of elongated, greenish-yellow fruits. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soil. Its fruit, while not typically eaten raw, can be used in cooking or processed into flour.

This variety is often studied for its genetic diversity and potential agricultural applications.
