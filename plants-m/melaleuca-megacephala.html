Melaleuca megacephala

Melaleuca megacephala, commonly known as the broad-leaved tea tree, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant white or cream-colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is known for its aromatic properties. It typically grows in coastal areas and is often found in sandy soils.

Melaleuca megacephala is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
