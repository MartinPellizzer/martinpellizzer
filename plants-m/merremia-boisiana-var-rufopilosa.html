Merremia boisiana var. rufopilosa

Merremia boisiana var. rufopilosa, commonly known as the red-hair vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture. This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
