Microchirita rupestris

Microchirita rupestris, commonly known as the rock chervil, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features delicate, fern-like leaves that are finely divided and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer.

Microchirita rupestris is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and unique appearance.
