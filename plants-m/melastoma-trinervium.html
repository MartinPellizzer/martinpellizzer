Melastoma trinervium

Melastoma trinervium, commonly known as the three-veined melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have three prominent veins, giving it its common name.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.
