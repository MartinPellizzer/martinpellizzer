Mucinaea

Mucinaea, commonly known as the mucilaginous fern, is a genus of flowering plants characterized by its unique ability to produce mucilage, a thick, sticky substance.

These plants are typically found in moist, tropical environments where they thrive in shaded, humid conditions. The leaves of Mucinaea are often broad and leathery, with a waxy coating that helps retain moisture. The genus includes several species, some of which are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Botanically, Mucinaea is notable for its specialized structures that aid in water retention and survival in its native habitats.
