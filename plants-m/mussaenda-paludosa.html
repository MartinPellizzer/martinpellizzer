Mussaenda paludosa

Mussaenda paludosa, commonly known as swamp mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in wet or swampy habitats, showcasing its adaptability to moist environments. The plant features showy, clustered inflorescences with bright yellow bracts that resemble flowers, though they are actually modified leaves. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Mussaenda paludosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable water conditions.
