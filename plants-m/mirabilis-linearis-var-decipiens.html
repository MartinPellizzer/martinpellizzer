Mirabilis linearis var. decipiens

Mirabilis linearis var. decipiens, commonly known as the "Blushing Morning Glory," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a white throat. The plant produces showy, bi-colored blooms that open in the morning and close by midday, a trait that gives it its common name. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
