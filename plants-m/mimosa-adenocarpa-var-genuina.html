Mimosa adenocarpa var. genuina

Mimosa adenocarpa var. genuina, commonly known as the velvet mimosa, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage composed of small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its soft green foliage. It is well adapted to dry conditions and thrives in well-drained soils.

The species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
