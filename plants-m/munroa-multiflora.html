Munroa multiflora

Munroa multiflora, commonly known as the multiflora muhly, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of white or pale blue flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight blue-gray hue.

Munroa multiflora plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
