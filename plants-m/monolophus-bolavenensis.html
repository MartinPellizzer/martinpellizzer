Monolophus bolavenensis

Monolophus bolavenensis, commonly known as the Bolaven mountain lily, is a striking flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories or on rocky slopes. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy texture that helps it retain moisture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
