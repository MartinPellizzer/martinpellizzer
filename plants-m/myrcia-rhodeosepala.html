Myrcia rhodeosepala

Myrcia rhodeosepala, commonly known as the pink myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy pink to red flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Myrcia rhodeosepala is also valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine.
