Manulea scabra

Manulea scabra, commonly known as the rough manulea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in shrubland or rocky habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
