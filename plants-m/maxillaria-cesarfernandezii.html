Maxillaria cesarfernandezii

Maxillaria cesarfernandezii, commonly known as the César Fernández orchid, is a species native to the humid forests of Colombia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration, typically featuring pink and white hues. It thrives in shaded environments with high humidity and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its natural habitat.
