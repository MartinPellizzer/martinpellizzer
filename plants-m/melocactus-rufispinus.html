Melocactus rufispinus

Melocactus rufispinus, commonly known as the red-spined melocactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, thick stem and dense, red to orange spines that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces a single, showy yellow flower at the top of the stem during the summer months. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
