Malephora verruculoides

Malephora verruculoides, commonly known as the velvet sponge.

This marine sponge is characterized by its dense, fleshy body covered in a layer of fine, velvety spicules. It belongs to the order Poecilodictyida and is found in shallow tropical waters. The plant's structure is composed primarily of a gelatinous matrix with embedded spicules that provide structural support.

It plays a role in marine ecosystems by filtering water and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
