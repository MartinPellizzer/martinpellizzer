Millettia kityana

Millettia kityana, commonly known as the Kityan millettia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in forested areas. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are used in traditional medicine in some cultures.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
