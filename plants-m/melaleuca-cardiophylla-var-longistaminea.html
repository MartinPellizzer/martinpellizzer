Melaleuca cardiophylla var. longistaminea

Melaleuca cardiophylla var. longistaminea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and distinctive bottlebrush-like flowers that are typically red or pink in color. The plant produces small, woody fruits called syringas, which contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
