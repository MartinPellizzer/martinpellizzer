Mendoncia odorata

Mendoncia odorata, commonly known as the fragrant honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in the spring. Its flowers are followed by small, berry-like fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and aromatic properties.
