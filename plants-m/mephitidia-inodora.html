Mephitidia inodora

Mephitidia inodora, commonly known as the odorless mephit, is a rare and elusive plant species characterized by its lack of distinct floral scent.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth and swampy areas. The plant features small, clustered leaves and produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are often overlooked. Its reproductive strategy relies on wind pollination, which is facilitated by its delicate, feathery stamens.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered a sensitive indicator of ecosystem health.
