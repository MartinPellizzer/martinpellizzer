Muscari bouvianum

Muscari bouvianum, commonly known as the Bovian grape hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, resembling grapes, hence its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of 10-20 cm, with strap-like leaves that are grassy in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often blooming in early spring.

Muscari bouvianum is valued in gardens for its ornamental beauty and ability to naturalize over time.
