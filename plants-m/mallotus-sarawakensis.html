Mallotus sarawakensis

Mallotus sarawakensis, commonly known as the Sarawak mallot, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in lowland and hill forest areas, thriving in humid and shaded environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
