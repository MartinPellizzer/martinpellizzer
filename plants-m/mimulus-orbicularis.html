Mimulus orbicularis

Mimulus orbicularis, commonly known as the broadleaf monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in woodland areas. It is a perennial species that thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Mimulus orbicularis plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
