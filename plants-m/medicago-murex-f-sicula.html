Medicago murex f. sicula

Medicago murex f. sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian bitter vetch, is a variant of the annual legume Medicago murex.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and trifoliate leaves with small, oval leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in dry, calcareous soils.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed habitats and is used for forage in some regions.
