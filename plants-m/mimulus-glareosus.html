Mimulus glareosus

Mimulus glareosus, commonly known as the golden monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as streambanks and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Mimulus glareosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
