Melissitus emodi

Melissitus emodi, commonly known as the Himalayan honeybee plant, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of white and yellow. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and forested areas. Its nectar is a significant food source for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ecological importance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in its native habitat.
