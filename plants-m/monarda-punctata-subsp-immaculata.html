Monarda punctata subsp. immaculata

Monarda punctata subsp.

immaculata, commonly known as white bergamot, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and its role in supporting pollinators.
