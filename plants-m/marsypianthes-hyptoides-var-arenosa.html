Marsypianthes hyptoides var. arenosa

Marsypianthes hyptoides var. arenosa, commonly known as the sand dune lily, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, succulent stems and narrow, grass-like leaves adapted to withstand harsh, dry conditions. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pale pink, typically blooming during the cooler months. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dune ecosystems.

Due to its specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
