Mackinlaya radiata

Mackinlaya radiata, commonly known as the radiate mackinlaya, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces elongated stems that bear the flower heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or open habitats.

Mackinlaya radiata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
