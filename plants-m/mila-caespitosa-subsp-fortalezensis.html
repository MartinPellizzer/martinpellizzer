Mila caespitosa subsp. fortalezensis

Mila caespitosa subsp.

fortalezensis, commonly known as the Brazilian grass, is a perennial grass species native to Brazil. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.
