Macaranga poilanei

Macaranga poilanei, commonly known as the silky tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The leaves are typically covered in a fine, silky pubescence, giving the plant its common name. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

The plant is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses.
