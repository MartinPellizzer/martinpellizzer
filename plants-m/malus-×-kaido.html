Malus × kaido

Malus × kaido, commonly known as the Kaido apple, is a hybrid fruit tree resulting from the cross between Malus domestica (apple) and Malus baccata (crabapple).

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy flowers and vibrant fall foliage. The tree typically grows to a moderate height, with a rounded, compact form. Its fruits are small, tart, and often used in culinary applications or for ornamental purposes.

The plant is well-suited for gardens and landscapes due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
