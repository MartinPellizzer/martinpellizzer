Myagrum montanum

Myagrum montanum, commonly known as mountain myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the wet tropical regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and tea tree. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a slight aromatic scent. Its small, fragrant white flowers are typically clustered together and bloom during the warmer months.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
