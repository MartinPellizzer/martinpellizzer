Mesembryanthemum tenuicaule

Mesembryanthemum tenuicaule, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, narrow leaves and small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, pink, and white. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems to survive drought conditions. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it both an ornamental and ecological asset in dry landscapes.
