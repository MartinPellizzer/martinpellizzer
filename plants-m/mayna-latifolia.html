Mayna latifolia

Mayna latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf mayna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, clustered in terminal inflorescences. Mayna latifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
