Medinilla papillosa

Medinilla papillosa, commonly known as the velvet tangerine, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, orange-colored fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Medinilla papillosa thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both gardeners and horticultural enthusiasts.
