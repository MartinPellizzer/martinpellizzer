Micromeria origanifolia

Micromeria origanifolia, commonly known as lemon verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves that emit a strong lemon scent when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and useful in herbal remedies.
