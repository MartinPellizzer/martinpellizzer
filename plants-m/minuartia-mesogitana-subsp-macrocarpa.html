Minuartia mesogitana subsp. macrocarpa

Minuartia mesogitana subsp.

macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with hairy, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and arid environments, often found in mountainous regions of the Balkans.
