Myrcia hondurensis

Myrcia hondurensis, commonly known as the Honduran myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible, red, berry-like fruits that are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Myrcia hondurensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
