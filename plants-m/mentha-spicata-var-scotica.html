Mentha spicata var. scotica

Mentha spicata var. scotica, commonly known as Scottish mint, is a perennial herb native to the Scottish Highlands.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a strong mint scent, and it produces small, pale blue flowers in whorled clusters. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. It is often found in rocky or grassy areas and is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses.

Its genetic distinctiveness makes it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
