Myosotis explanata

Myosotis explanata, commonly known as the broadleaf forget-me-not, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early autumn. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are broad and often hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins and along stream banks.

Myosotis explanata is an important component of New Zealand's native flora and is valued for its ecological and ornamental significance.
