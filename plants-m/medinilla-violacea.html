Medinilla violacea

Medinilla violacea, commonly known as the violet torch or violet hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features dark green, glossy leaves and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant floral display.

Medinilla violacea is also valued for its ability to grow in partial shade and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
