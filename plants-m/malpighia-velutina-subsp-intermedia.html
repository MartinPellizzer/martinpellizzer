Malpighia velutina subsp. intermedia

Malpighia velutina subsp.

intermedia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that produces bright red, edible fruits similar to cherries.

The plant belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pink, and the fruits are rich in vitamin C and antioxidants.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
