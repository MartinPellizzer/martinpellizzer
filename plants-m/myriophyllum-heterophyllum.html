Myriophyllum heterophyllum

Myriophyllum heterophyllum, commonly known as watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has slender, branching stems that are often covered in small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces tiny, floating flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as lakes and ponds.

Myriophyllum heterophyllum plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
