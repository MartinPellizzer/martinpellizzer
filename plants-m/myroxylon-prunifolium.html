Myroxylon prunifolium

Myroxylon prunifolium, commonly known as the copal tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and dense, dark green foliage. The tree produces small, white flowers followed by woody, resinous fruits. Its sap, known as copal, is used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

The plant is also valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
