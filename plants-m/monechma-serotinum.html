Monechma serotinum

Monechma serotinum, commonly known as the late-flowering monechma, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and forest edges. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom late in the growing season, usually from August to October. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is noted for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its native range.
