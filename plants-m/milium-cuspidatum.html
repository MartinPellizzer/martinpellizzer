Milium cuspidatum

Milium cuspidatum, commonly known as the spikegrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

Milium cuspidatum is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
