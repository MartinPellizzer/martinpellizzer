Mentha tenageia

Mentha tenageia, commonly known as the Australian peppermint, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale purple flowers. The plant contains high levels of menthol, which gives it its distinctive cooling and minty scent. Mentha tenageia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along watercourses.

It is widely used in aromatherapy and as a natural remedy for digestive and respiratory ailments.
