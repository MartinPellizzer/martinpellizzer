Mentha sylvestris subsp. kotschyana

Mentha sylvestris subsp.

kotschyana, commonly known as the Kotschy's mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their cooling and digestive properties.

This subspecies is closely related to other members of the Mentha genus, sharing similar chemical compositions and growth habits.
