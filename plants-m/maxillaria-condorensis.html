Maxillaria condorensis

Maxillaria condorensis, commonly known as the Peruvian orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces pale pink to white blooms with dark purple or brown markings, often resembling a butterfly in shape. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Maxillaria condorensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
