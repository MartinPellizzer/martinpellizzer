Melocactus barbarana

Melocactus barbarana, commonly known as the Mexican melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and a crown of spines that protect it from herbivores. The plant produces a fleshy, segmented fruit that resembles a melon, hence its common name. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange and bloom during the rainy season.

Melocactus barbarana is adapted to survive in extreme heat and drought, making it a resilient member of desert ecosystems.
