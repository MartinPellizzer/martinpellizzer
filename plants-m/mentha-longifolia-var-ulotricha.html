Mentha longifolia var. ulotricha

Mentha longifolia var. ulotricha, commonly known as narrow-leaved mint, is a variant of the spearmint species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, pointed leaves and strong, aromatic menthol scent. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowering stems bear small, pale blue to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is often used in culinary applications and herbal remedies due to its potent essential oils.
