Morinda coreia var. aspera

Morinda coreia var. aspera, commonly known as the rough morinda, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and pharmacological purposes.
