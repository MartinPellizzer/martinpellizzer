Myriophyllum laxum

Myriophyllum laxum, commonly known as watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has feathery, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are often green to reddish in color. The plant produces tiny, floating flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes.

Myriophyllum laxum plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
