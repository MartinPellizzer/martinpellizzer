Mentha × villosa f. garrontei

Mentha × villosa f. garrontei, commonly known as the hairy mint or Garron's mint, is a hybrid plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and a strong, aromatic scent. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments, often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually purple or pink in color.

This subspecies is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other mint species.
