Myristica amygdalina var. hookeri

Myristica amygdalina var. hookeri, commonly known as the African nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody fruits that contain a richly flavored seed kernel. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has compound leaves with elliptical leaflets. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications due to their unique fragrance and nutritional value.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitats.
