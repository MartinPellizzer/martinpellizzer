Magnolia dawsoniana

Magnolia dawsoniana, commonly known as the Dawson's magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The tree has simple, alternate, ovate to elliptical leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant floral display.
