Malva antillarum

Malva antillarum, commonly known as the West Indian mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet, tropical environments. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its fibrous stems and mucilaginous seeds have been used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural gum.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
