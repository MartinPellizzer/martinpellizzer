Mitranthes langsdorffii

Mitranthes langsdorffii, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are oval and slightly hairy, with a distinctive aromatic scent.

Mitranthes langsdorffii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
