Mimulus jamesii var. texensis

Mimulus jamesii var. texensis, commonly known as the Texas monkeyflower, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, orange to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils along stream banks and canyons. It has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
