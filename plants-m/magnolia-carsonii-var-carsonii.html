Magnolia carsonii var. carsonii

Magnolia carsonii var. carsonii, commonly known as the Carson magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The tree has simple, alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring bloom.
