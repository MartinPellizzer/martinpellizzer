Miconia baumgratziana

Miconia baumgratziana, commonly known as the golden miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Miconia baumgratziana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates due to its striking foliage and floral display.
