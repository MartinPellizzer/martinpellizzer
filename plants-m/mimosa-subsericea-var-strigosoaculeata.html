Mimosa subsericea var. strigosoaculeata

Mimosa subsericea var. strigosoaculeata, commonly known as the hairy aculeate mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its stems are often armed with soft, hooked thorns, which serve as a defense mechanism.

This species is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
