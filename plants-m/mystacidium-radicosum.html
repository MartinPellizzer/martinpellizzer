Mystacidium radicosum

Mystacidium radicosum, commonly known as the root moss, is a small, terrestrial liverwort that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It has a dense, cushion-like growth form with closely packed, leaf-like structures called phyllidia. The plant's root system is well-developed, allowing it to anchor firmly in soil and absorb nutrients efficiently. Its reproductive structures, called gemmae, are produced in clusters and aid in vegetative propagation.

Mystacidium radicosum is often found in forest floors and rocky crevices, contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for microorganisms.
