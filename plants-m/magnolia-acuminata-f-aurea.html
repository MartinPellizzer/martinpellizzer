Magnolia acuminata f. aurea

Magnolia acuminata f. aurea, commonly known as the golden magnolia, is a cultivar of the magnolia tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which are larger and more showy than those of the typical Magnolia acuminata. The leaves are broad, ovate, and have a smooth, glossy texture. This variety thrives in partial to full sunlight and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
