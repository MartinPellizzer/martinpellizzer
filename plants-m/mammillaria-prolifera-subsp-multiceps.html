Mammillaria prolifera subsp. multiceps

Mammillaria prolifera subsp.

multiceps, commonly known as the clustered mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico. It is characterized by its dense, globular shape and small, rounded tubercles that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically produces pale green or blue-gray spines, and its flowers are usually yellow or orange. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

This subspecies is known for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated as a decorative succulent.
