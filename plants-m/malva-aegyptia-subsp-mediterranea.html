Malva aegyptia subsp. mediterranea

Malva aegyptia subsp.

mediterranea, commonly known as the Mediterranean mallows, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

This subspecies is also known for its ability to tolerate drought conditions.
