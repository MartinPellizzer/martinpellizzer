Mindium thyrsoideum

Mindium thyrsoideum, commonly known as the silver leaf saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments. Its leaves are succulent and adapted to retain moisture in arid conditions.

Mindium thyrsoideum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
