Myrica comorensis

Myrica comorensis, commonly known as the Comorian wax myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Comoros Islands.

It belongs to the Myricaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, waxy berries. The plant typically grows in coastal and lowland areas, thriving in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, while its flowers are inconspicuous and followed by the distinctive, berry-like fruits.

Myrica comorensis plays a role in local ecosystems and has been used in traditional medicine.
