Melastoma medinillana

Melastoma medinillana, commonly known as the red-flowered melastoma, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins and produces small, berry-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in warm climates.
