Molubda scandens

Molubda scandens, commonly known as the climbing molubda, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its twining stems that allow it to climb and spread across supports. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth texture.

Molubda scandens is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive blooms and vigorous growth habit.
