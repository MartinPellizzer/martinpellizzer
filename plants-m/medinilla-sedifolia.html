Medinilla sedifolia

Medinilla sedifolia, commonly known as the velvet leaf medinilla, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that are often a deep green or reddish-brown in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil and indirect sunlight.

Medinilla sedifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
