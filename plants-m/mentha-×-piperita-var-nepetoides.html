Mentha × piperita var. nepetoides

Mentha × piperita var. nepetoides, commonly known as lemon balm, is a hybrid mint species characterized by its aromatic, slightly lemon-scented leaves.

It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated edge. Its flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and typically range in color from white to pale yellow.

Lemon balm is valued for its calming properties and is widely used in herbal teas and aromatherapy.
