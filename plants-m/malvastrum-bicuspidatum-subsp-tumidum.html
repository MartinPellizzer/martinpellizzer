Malvastrum bicuspidatum subsp. tumidum

Malvastrum bicuspidatum subsp.

tumidum, commonly known as the broadleaf hibiscus, is a perennial herb native to the southern United States. It features large, showy flowers with five petals, typically in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late summer.

The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens and wildlife habitats.
