Mirabilis ovata f. glabriuscula

Mirabilis ovata f. glabriuscula, commonly known as the smooth ovate four-o'clock, is a variant of the four-o'clock plant characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves and stems.

It belongs to the Nyctaginaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that open in the late afternoon, hence its common name. Its foliage is typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its unique flowering behavior.
