Mappa occidentalis

Mappa occidentalis, commonly known as the western map plant, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along its stems. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Mappa occidentalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to withstand dry conditions.
