Morronea

Morronea, commonly known as the red balm or red balmflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and features showy, red or orange-colored flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plants typically grow as annuals or perennials, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Morronea species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are often cultivated in gardens for their vibrant blooms.

They thrive in well-drained soil and full sun, making them suitable for Mediterranean climates.
