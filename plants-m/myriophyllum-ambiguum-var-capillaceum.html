Myriophyllum ambiguum var. capillaceum

Myriophyllum ambiguum var. capillaceum, commonly known as watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has slender, branching stems that are often feather-like, with small, opposite leaves. The plant produces tiny, green flowers that are typically borne in clusters. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers.

Its rapid growth can lead to dense vegetation, which can impact water quality and aquatic habitats.
