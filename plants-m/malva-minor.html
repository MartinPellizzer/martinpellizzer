Malva minor

Malva minor, commonly known as lesser mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus, and is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy pink or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed sites.

Malva minor is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in herbal medicine for its mild sedative and anti-inflammatory properties.
