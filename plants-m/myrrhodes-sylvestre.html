Myrrhodes sylvestre

Myrrhodes sylvestre, commonly known as wild myrrh, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its aromatic resinous sap, which has been used for centuries in traditional medicine and incense. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, while its flowers are small and often pale yellow or green.

The resin produced by Myrrhodes sylvestre is valued for its medicinal properties and its role in cultural practices.
