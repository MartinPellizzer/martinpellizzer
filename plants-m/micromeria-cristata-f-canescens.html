Micromeria cristata f. canescens

Micromeria cristata f. canescens, commonly known as the hairy mountain sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant is distinguished by its dense, white to grayish pubescence, which covers its stems and leaves, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pink to purple, and they bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is often found in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
