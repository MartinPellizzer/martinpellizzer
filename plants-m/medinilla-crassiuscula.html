Medinilla crassiuscula

Medinilla crassiuscula, commonly known as the dwarf medinilla, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-draining soil.

Medinilla crassiuscula is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate blossoms.
