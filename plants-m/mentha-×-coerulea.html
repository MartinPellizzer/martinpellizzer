Mentha × coerulea

Mentha × coerulea, commonly known as blue mint, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic, square stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are typically a striking blue-green color. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Mentha × coerulea is native to southern Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens and landscapes.
