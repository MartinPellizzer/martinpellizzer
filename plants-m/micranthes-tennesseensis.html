Micranthes tennesseensis

Micranthes tennesseensis, commonly known as the Tennessee micranthus, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces tiny, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
