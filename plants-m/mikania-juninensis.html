Mikania juninensis

Mikania juninensis, commonly known as the Andean morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is part of the Asteraceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It is often found in moist, sunny areas and can be invasive in certain regions.

The flowers typically bloom in the morning, giving the plant its common name.
