Myrceugenia bracteosa var. expallens

Myrceugenia bracteosa var. expallens, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of white or pinkish flowers that appear in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas.
