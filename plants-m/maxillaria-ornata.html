Maxillaria ornata

Maxillaria ornata, commonly known as the Orchid of the Andes, is a terrestrial orchid native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms in the summer, with flowers that have a unique, intricate pattern resembling a crown. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in cool, humid environments with well-draining soil.

Maxillaria ornata is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
