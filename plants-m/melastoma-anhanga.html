Melastoma anhanga

Melastoma anhanga, commonly known as the red-flowered melastoma, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, often pink or red, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a rough texture, while its fruit is a capsule that contains several small seeds.

Melastoma anhanga is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
