Mentha × rotundifolia f. thierseana

Mentha × rotundifolia f. thierseana, commonly known as the Thiersean round-leaf mint, is a hybrid plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its rounded, often slightly hairy leaves that are broader than those of typical mint species. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers arranged in whorled clusters. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This cultivar is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
