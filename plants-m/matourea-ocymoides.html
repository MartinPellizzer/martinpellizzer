Matourea ocymoides

Matourea ocymoides, commonly known as the pink false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a distinctive yellow center. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Matourea ocymoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
