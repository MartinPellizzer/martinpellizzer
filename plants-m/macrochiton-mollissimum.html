Macrochiton mollissimum

Macrochiton mollissimum, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into woody pods containing edible seeds. The plant is notable for its thick, velvety leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist forest understories.

The seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.
