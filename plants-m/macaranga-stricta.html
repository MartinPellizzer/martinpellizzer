Macaranga stricta

Macaranga stricta, commonly known as the rough leaf mallot, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually rough to the touch, with a heart-shaped base. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in panicles, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.

Macaranga stricta is often found in disturbed habitats and is known for its adaptability to various soil types.
