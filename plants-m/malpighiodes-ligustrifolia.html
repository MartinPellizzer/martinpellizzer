Malpighiodes ligustrifolia

Malpighiodes ligustrifolia, commonly known as the sweet-scented false mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five petals, typically in shades of white or pink. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
