Muscari alexandrae

Muscari alexandrae, commonly known as the Alexandrine grape hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet, resembling tiny grapes. The plant typically blooms in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to gardens and natural landscapes. Its bulbs are adapted to well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or borders.

Muscari alexandrae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements due to its delicate, fragrant blooms.
