Myrcia cordifolia var. fuscescens

Myrcia cordifolia var. fuscescens, commonly known as the dark-leaved myrtle, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
