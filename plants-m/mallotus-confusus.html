Mallotus confusus

Mallotus confusus, commonly known as the confusion mallot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often deeply lobed, and its small, clustered flowers. It produces woody capsules that contain seeds, typical of many species in the Euphorbiaceae family.

Mallotus confusus is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental leaves and potential medicinal properties.
