Myrtus phylicoides

Myrtus phylicoides, commonly known as sweet myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves and flowers, are valued for their pleasant scent and various medicinal properties.

Myrtus phylicoides is often used in traditional medicine and as a decorative plant in gardens.
