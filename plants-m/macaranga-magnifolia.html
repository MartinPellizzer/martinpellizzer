Macaranga magnifolia

Macaranga magnifolia, commonly known as the giant magnolia, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white waxy coating. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has also been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
