Mulgedium polypodiifolium

Mulgedium polypodiifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved fern, is a species of fern native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its fronds that are often broad and lobed. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Mulgedium polypodiifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
