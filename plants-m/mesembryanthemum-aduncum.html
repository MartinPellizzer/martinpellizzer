Mesembryanthemum aduncum

Mesembryanthemum aduncum, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or ozark star, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, often blue or purple-tinted leaves that are adapted to store water, typical of arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are usually yellow or orange, blooming in spring and summer. Its fruit, resembling a small, round berry, is edible and has a tangy flavor.

Mesembryanthemum aduncum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
