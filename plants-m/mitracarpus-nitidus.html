Mitracarpus nitidus

Mitracarpus nitidus, commonly known as the velvetleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its palmately compound leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces distinctive, three-lobed fruits that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
