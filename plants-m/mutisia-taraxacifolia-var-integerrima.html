Mutisia taraxacifolia var. integerrima

Mutisia taraxacifolia var. integerrima, commonly known as the yellow mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical structure supports its adaptation to arid environments, with reduced leaves and a robust root system.
