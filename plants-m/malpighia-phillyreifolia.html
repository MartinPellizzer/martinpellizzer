Malpighia phillyreifolia

Malpighia phillyreifolia, commonly known as the red mombin, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with alternate arrangement. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Its fruits are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in culinary applications.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
