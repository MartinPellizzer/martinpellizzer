Matthiola humilis

Matthiola humilis, commonly known as the humble stock, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that come in a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant has slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and gardens.

Matthiola humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
