Mimosa leptocaulis

Mimosa leptocaulis, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, yellow, ball-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in sandy or coastal areas.

Its unique foliage and rapid growth make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
