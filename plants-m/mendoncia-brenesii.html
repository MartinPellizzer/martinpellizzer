Mendoncia brenesii

Mendoncia brenesii, commonly known as the Guanacaste tree, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a member of the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree can grow quite tall, reaching up to 20 meters in height, and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

The fruit, which is a capsule, contains a single seed and is dispersed by animals.
