Maranta subterranea

Maranta subterranea, commonly known as the underground arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and produces small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant's most notable botanical feature is its starchy, edible underground stems, which are used as a food source. It typically thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Its scientific name reflects its subterranean growth pattern, which is unique among the Maranta genus.
