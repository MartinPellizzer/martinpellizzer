Morinda scabrida

Morinda scabrida, commonly known as rough morinda, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and palmate leaves with rough, textured surfaces. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional uses.
