Melothria mannii

Melothria mannii, commonly known as the African sour plum, is a small, climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It produces small, green, berry-like fruits that turn yellow or orange when ripe, often with a tart, acidic flavor. The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests or along riverbanks. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

Melothria mannii is valued for its edible fruits and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
