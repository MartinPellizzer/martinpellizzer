Mitracarpus falcatus

Mitracarpus falcatus, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, falcate (sickle-shaped) leaves and yellow, dandelion-like flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces a capsule fruit that contains numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Mitracarpus falcatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
