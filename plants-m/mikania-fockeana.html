Mikania fockeana

Mikania fockeana, commonly known as the creeping mikania, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as a sprawling vine or shrub, often climbing or trailing along the ground. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom throughout the growing season.

Mikania fockeana is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it both ecologically significant and sometimes invasive.
