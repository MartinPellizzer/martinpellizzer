Michelia mediocris var. angustifolia

Michelia mediocris var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Michelia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded forest environments, often growing along stream banks or in mountainous regions. Its ornamental value is due to its pleasant scent and attractive foliage, making it a sought-after species in horticulture.

The varietal form, angustifolia, distinguishes it by its narrower leaves compared to the typical species.
