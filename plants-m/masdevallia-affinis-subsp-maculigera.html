Masdevallia affinis subsp. maculigera

Masdevallia affinis subsp.

maculigera, commonly known as the spotted masdevallia, is a rare and delicate orchid native to the high-altitude cloud forests of South America. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often brightly colored flowers with intricate patterns.

The plant typically produces small, lance-shaped leaves that are densely packed around the stem. Its flowers are usually borne in clusters and can range in color from white to pink, with distinctive markings.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and relatively compact size.
