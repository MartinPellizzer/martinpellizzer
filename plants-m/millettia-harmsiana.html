Millettia harmsiana

Millettia harmsiana, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree has bipinnate leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland ecosystems.

Due to its ecological and economic importance, it is also considered a valuable species for reforestation efforts.
