Myristica corticosa var. ceramensis

Myristica corticosa var. ceramensis, commonly known as the Ceram nutmeg, is a member of the Myristicaceae family and is native to the Maluku Islands of Indonesia.

It is a small evergreen tree that produces a distinctive aromatic seed, which is used in traditional medicine and as a spice. The plant's leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin. Its flowers are small and yellowish, arranged in panicles, and it bears fruits that are globose and covered in a thick, woody husk.

The botanical characteristics of this species make it an important plant in both ecological and economic contexts within its native habitat.
