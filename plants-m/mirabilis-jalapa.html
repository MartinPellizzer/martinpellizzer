Mirabilis jalapa

Mirabilis jalapa, commonly known as the four o'clock flower, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that open in the late afternoon and close at dawn. The plant produces vibrant colors, including red, yellow, pink, and white, making it a popular ornamental species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, often growing as an annual in temperate climates.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
