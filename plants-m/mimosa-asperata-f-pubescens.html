Mimosa asperata f. pubescens

Mimosa asperata f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy mimosa, is a variant of the mimosa plant characterized by its dense, fuzzy foliage.

This plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is native to regions of South America. Its leaves are bipinnately compound, with a distinctive velvety texture due to the fine pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spherical clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
