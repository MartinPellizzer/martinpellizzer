Mimosa resinifera

Mimosa resinifera, commonly known as the resinous mimosa, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a sticky, resinous substance, which gives it its common name and has been used traditionally in various applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forested areas and along riverbanks.

The resin of Mimosa resinifera has been studied for its potential medicinal and industrial uses.
