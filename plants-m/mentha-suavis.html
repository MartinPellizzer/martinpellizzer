Mentha suavis

Mentha suavis, commonly known as sweet mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and dense clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its refreshing scent and various medicinal properties.

Mentha suavis is often used in culinary applications and traditional herbal remedies.
