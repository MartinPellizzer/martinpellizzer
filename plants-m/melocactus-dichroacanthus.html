Melocactus dichroacanthus

Melocactus dichroacanthus, commonly known as the two-spined melon cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem that is ribbed and covered with white, woolly hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, and it has two prominent spines at the top of each areole. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for desert gardens and collections.
