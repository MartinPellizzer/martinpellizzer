Macrozamia denisonii var. hopei

Macrozamia denisonii var. hopei, commonly known as the Hope's cycad, is a rare and ancient plant species native to the rainforests of northeastern Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, cone-like structures that contain its distinctive seeds. It is a slow-growing, long-lived species that plays a significant role in its ecosystem.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
