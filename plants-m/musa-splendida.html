Musa splendida

Musa splendida, commonly known as the golden banana, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and striking yellow-orange pseudostems. The plant produces clusters of fruit that are typically elongated and have a sweet, aromatic flavor. Musa splendida is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as for its edible fruit.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
