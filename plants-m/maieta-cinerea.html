Maieta cinerea

Maieta cinerea, commonly known as the pale mayweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

Maieta cinerea is an annual plant that plays a role in supporting pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its habitat.
