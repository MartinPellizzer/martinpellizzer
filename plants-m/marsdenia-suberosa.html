Marsdenia suberosa

Marsdenia suberosa, commonly known as the hairy Marsdenia, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange or red berries. Its fibrous roots and thick, fleshy stems make it an important species in traditional medicine and horticulture.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions with high humidity and partial shade.
