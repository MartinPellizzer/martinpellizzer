Mimosa demissa

Mimosa demissa, commonly known as the sticky mimosa, is a small shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are often covered in fine, sticky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in spring. Its stems are slender and often branched, with a tendency to trail or climb.

Mimosa demissa is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
