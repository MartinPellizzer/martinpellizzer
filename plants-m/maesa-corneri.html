Maesa corneri

Maesa corneri, commonly known as the Christmas star, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, star-shaped white flowers that bloom during the winter months. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salt exposure. It is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive appearance and hardy nature.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
