Mesembryanthemum subspinosum

Mesembryanthemum subspinosum, commonly known as the spineless ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, succulent stems and leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
