Malus domestica subsp. occidentalieuropaea

Malus domestica subsp.

occidentalieuropaea, commonly known as the European apple, is a subspecies of the domestic apple tree native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its deciduous nature, with simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink, followed by fruits that range from green to red in color. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This subspecies is widely cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value.
