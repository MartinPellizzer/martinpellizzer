Mentha arvensis var. lamprophyllos

Mentha arvensis var. lamprophyllos, commonly known as the glittering mint, is a variant of the field mint species.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy leaves that give it a silvery appearance. This perennial herb typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for their aromatic and cooling properties.

The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer, contributing to its ornamental value.
