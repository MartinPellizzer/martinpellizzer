Malaxis purpurea

Malaxis purpurea, commonly known as the purple malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, slender stems and small, pale green to purple-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, often in the understory of tropical rainforests. Its flowers are usually arranged in a loose spike and are pollinated by specific insects.

Malaxis purpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
