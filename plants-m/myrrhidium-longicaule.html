Myrrhidium longicaule

Myrrhidium longicaule, commonly known as the long-stemmed myrrh plant, is a member of the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its aromatic, resinous leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces a fragrant gum resin, similar to myrrh, which has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Myrrhidium longicaule is often found in coastal and inland regions of southern Australia.
