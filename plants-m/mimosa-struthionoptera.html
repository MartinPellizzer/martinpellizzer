Mimosa struthionoptera

Mimosa struthionoptera, commonly known as the giant mimosa, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, fern-like leaves that are sensitive to touch, folding shut when disturbed. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its unique appearance and sensitivity, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
