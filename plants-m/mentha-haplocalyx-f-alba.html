Mentha haplocalyx f. alba

Mentha haplocalyx f. alba, commonly known as white horse mint, is a variant of the Mentha genus native to East Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its essential oils, rich in menthol and other compounds, contribute to its distinct cooling scent and medicinal properties.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and use in traditional herbal remedies.
