Myrtus communis var. mucronata

Myrtus communis var. mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by dark blue berries. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value and aromatic properties make it a popular choice in horticulture and traditional medicine.
