Malus rivularis var. levipes

Malus rivularis var. levipes, commonly known as the slender crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, arching branches and small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, yellowish fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in streamside habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
