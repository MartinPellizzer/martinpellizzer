Myrtus xalapensis

Myrtus xalapensis, commonly known as the Mexican myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It features dark green, glossy leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Myrtus xalapensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal remedies.
