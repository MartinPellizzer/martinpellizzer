Muraltia muraltioides

Muraltia muraltioides, commonly known as the African firethorn, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. These berries are edible for humans but can be toxic to livestock.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking winter blooms and berries.
