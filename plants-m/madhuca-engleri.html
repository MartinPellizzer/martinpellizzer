Madhuca engleri

Madhuca engleri, commonly known as the Malayan mahogany, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by oval-shaped fruits containing a single seed.

It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction, and it plays an important role in forest ecosystems.
