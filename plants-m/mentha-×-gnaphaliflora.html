Mentha × gnaphaliflora

Mentha × gnaphaliflora, commonly known as Australian peppermint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Mentha piperita (peppermint) and Mentha viridiflora.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its aromatic, slightly menthol-like scent. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, tubular, and clustered in whorls, producing a distinctive peppermint fragrance.

Mentha × gnaphaliflora is often used in aromatherapy and as a source of essential oils due to its high menthol content.
