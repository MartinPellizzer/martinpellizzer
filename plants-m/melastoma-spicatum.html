Melastoma spicatum

Melastoma spicatum, commonly known as the spiky melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture, while its stems are usually woody and branched.

Melastoma spicatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral displays.
