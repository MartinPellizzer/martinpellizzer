Mentha segetalis

Mentha segetalis, commonly known as field mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist grasslands and along stream banks, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are serrated and have a strong, minty aroma, while its flowers are small and clustered in whorls.

Mentha segetalis is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
