Mokof clusiifolia

Mokof clusiifolia, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a popular ornamental species. The plant has large, glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its bark is smooth and can vary in color from gray to reddish-brown.

Mokof clusiifolia is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display and is adaptable to a range of soil types.
