Mimosa selloi var. leiocarpa

Mimosa selloi var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, feathery foliage and delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a shrub-like growth habit with woody stems and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into small, thread-like segments, giving it a unique appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
