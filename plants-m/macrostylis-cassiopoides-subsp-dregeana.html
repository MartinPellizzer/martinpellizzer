Macrostylis cassiopoides subsp. dregeana

Macrostylis cassiopoides subsp.

dregeana, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow flowers.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, cylindrical flower heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
