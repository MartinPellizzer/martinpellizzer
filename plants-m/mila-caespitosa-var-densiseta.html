Mila caespitosa var. densiseta

Mila caespitosa var. densiseta, commonly known as the dense-hair mila, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense covering of fine hairs on its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is more commonly found in its natural habitat.
