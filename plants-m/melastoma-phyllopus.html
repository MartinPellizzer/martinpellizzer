Melastoma phyllopus

Melastoma phyllopus, commonly known as the broadleaf melastoma, is a flowering plant belonging to the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy green surface and prominent veins. Its showy, clustered flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals and a distinctive tubular throat.

Melastoma phyllopus is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
