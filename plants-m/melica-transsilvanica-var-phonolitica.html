Melica transsilvanica var. phonolitica

Melica transsilvanica var. phonolitica, commonly known as the phonolite melic, is a perennial grass species native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often found in mountainous or alpine environments. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are usually small and clustered.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to harsh, nutrient-poor conditions, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystems.
