Myrcia anceps var. depauperata

Myrcia anceps var. depauperata, commonly known as the poorleaf myrtle, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or white, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that contains a single seed.

This species is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to coastal environments with high salinity and wind exposure.
