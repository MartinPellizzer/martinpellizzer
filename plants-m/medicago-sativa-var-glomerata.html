Medicago sativa var. glomerata

Medicago sativa var. glomerata, commonly known as yellow sweet clover, is a variant of the sweet clover plant native to Europe and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial legume characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters. This plant has a deep root system that helps it thrive in various soil types, including those that are dry or poor in nutrients. It is also notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

However, it can become invasive in certain environments if not managed properly.
