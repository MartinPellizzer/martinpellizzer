Melocactus jakusii

Melocactus jakusii, commonly known as the Jakusii melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, thick stems that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces a single, large, spiny fruit that resembles a melon, which is edible when ripe. Melocactus jakusii thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it well-suited for xerophytic gardens.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
