Moquilea turiuva

Moquilea turiuva, commonly known as the Andean quinoa, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes Mountains.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces edible seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients, making it a valuable crop in traditional Andean agriculture. Moquilea turiuva thrives in cool, dry climates and is adapted to poor, rocky soils.

Its cultivation has historical and cultural significance among indigenous communities in South America.
