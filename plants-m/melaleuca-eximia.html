Melaleuca eximia

Melaleuca eximia, commonly known as the coastal tea tree, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in heathlands and coastal dunes. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves, are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.

Melaleuca eximia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal environments and providing habitat for native wildlife.
