Mediocalcar montanum

Mediocalcar montanum, commonly known as the mountain calcar, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to greenish flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
