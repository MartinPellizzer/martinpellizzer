Machaerium parviflorum

Machaerium parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered machaerium, is a tropical perennial grass native to regions of South America.

It features slender, erect stems and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in dense, spike-like clusters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings.

Machaerium parviflorum plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
