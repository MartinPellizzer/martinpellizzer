Macrocarpaea marahuacae

Macrocarpaea marahuacae, commonly known as the Mexican heather, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant features small, evergreen leaves and produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is more commonly seen in its natural habitat.
