Monospora

Monospora, commonly known as the "single-spore" plant, is a genus of fungi rather than a plant, often mistaken for a botanical species.

It is characterized by its unique reproductive structure, which consists of a single spore enclosed in a protective capsule. These fungi are typically found in soil and decaying organic matter, playing a role in nutrient cycling. Monospora species are not commonly cultivated and are more often encountered in natural environments.

Their simple morphology and reproductive strategy make them interesting subjects for mycological study.
