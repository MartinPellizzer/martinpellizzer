Mauranthemum gaetulum

Mauranthemum gaetulum, commonly known as the Canary Islands geranium, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
