Meibomia ambigua

Meibomia ambigua, commonly known as the swamp gentian, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet, swampy areas and along riverbanks. The plant features showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Meibomia ambigua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in moist garden environments.
