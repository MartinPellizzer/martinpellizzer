Mimulus filicifolius

Mimulus filicifolius, commonly known as the water mimulus, is a perennial herb native to western North America.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in wet environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a feathery appearance, contributing to its common name.

Mimulus filicifolius plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
