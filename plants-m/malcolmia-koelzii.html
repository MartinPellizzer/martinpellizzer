Malcolmia koelzii

Malcolmia koelzii, commonly known as the Kashmir wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Pakistan and India.

It is a low-growing annual herb with distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs and has small, oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences at the ends of the stems.

Malcolmia koelzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its bright, cheerful blooms.
