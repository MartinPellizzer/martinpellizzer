Mosla exfoliata

Mosla exfoliata, commonly known as Chinese wallflower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically has hairy, aromatic leaves and produces seed pods that split open when mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Mosla exfoliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
