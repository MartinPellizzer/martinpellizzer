Matricaria capitellata

Matricaria capitellata, commonly known as the German chamomile, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with white ray florets. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 cm and has finely divided, aromatic leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.

The essential oil extracted from its flowers is widely used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine for its calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
