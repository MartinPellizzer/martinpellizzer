Macfadyena dolichandra

Macfadyena dolichandra, commonly known as the crown of thorns or velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant features pinnately compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. Its fruit is a pod that splits open to reveal edible, sweet, and sticky seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
