Myrobalanus oblongata

Myrobalanus oblongata, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, oblong-shaped fruits. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by fleshy, orange-red berries.
