Mahonia glumacea

Mahonia glumacea, commonly known as the western mahonia, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features compound leaves that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by blue-black berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
