Malus cerea

Malus cerea, commonly known as the common apple tree, is a species of deciduous tree native to central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its rounded crown, smooth bark, and alternate, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by edible, fleshy fruits known as apples.

It is widely cultivated for its fruit, which is used in food, beverages, and various industrial products.
