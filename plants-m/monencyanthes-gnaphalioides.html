Monencyanthes gnaphalioides

Monencyanthes gnaphalioides, commonly known as the silverleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant features silvery, fuzzy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. Its yellow flower heads consist of numerous small disk florets surrounded by pale yellow ray florets.

Monencyanthes gnaphalioides is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
