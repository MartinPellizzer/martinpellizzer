Melasphaerula parviflora

Melasphaerula parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered melasphaerula, is a species of flowering plant in the family Melasphaerulaceae.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. The plant is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, tropical environments. It has simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and less-studied species in botanical research.
