Machaeranthera canescens var. vacans

Machaeranthera canescens var. vacans, commonly known as the plains coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It is characterized by its yellow to orange-yellow flowers and linear, gray-green leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as prairies and roadsides. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This variety is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
