Mikania monagasensis

Mikania monagasensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian hemp vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense mats, often found in humid, tropical environments. It has fibrous stems that can be used for various purposes, including rope-making.

The species is also noted for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms.
