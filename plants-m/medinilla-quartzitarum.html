Medinilla quartzitarum

Medinilla quartzitarum, commonly known as the quartzite medinilla, is a unique flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy, violet-blue bracts that surround its small, white flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil to flourish. Its foliage consists of broad, glossy leaves that are often tinged with silver or white veins.

Medinilla quartzitarum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
