Mucuna japira

Mucuna japira, commonly known as Brazilian velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, hairy pods that contain numerous seeds. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in diverse soil conditions. Its flowers are typically bright yellow, and it produces a strong, aromatic odor.

Mucuna japira is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.
