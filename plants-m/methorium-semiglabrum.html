Methorium semiglabrum

Methorium semiglabrum, commonly known as the silverleaf spurge, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

Methorium semiglabrum is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain ecosystems.
