Myrcia clavija

Myrcia clavija, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several aromatic seeds.

Myrcia clavija is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
