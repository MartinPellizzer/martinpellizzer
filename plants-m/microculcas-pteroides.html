Microculcas pteroides

Microculcas pteroides, commonly known as the mountain holly or the evergreen holly, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal, it is often used in hedges and as a landscape plant.
