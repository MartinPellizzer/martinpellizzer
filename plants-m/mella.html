Mella

Mella, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Mella is also known for its fibrous stems, which have been used in traditional crafts and textiles.
