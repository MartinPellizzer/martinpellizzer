Morphixia capillaris var. incarnata

Morphixia capillaris var. incarnata, commonly known as the pink-flowered morphixia, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
