Maesa loranthifolia

Maesa loranthifolia, commonly known as the Indian mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has small, opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its berries are typically red or orange, containing adhesive seeds that help in attachment to host plants.

Maesa loranthifolia is part of the Loranthaceae family and exhibits a unique relationship with its host, drawing water and nutrients while also providing some ecological benefits.
