Mikania longicarpa

Mikania longicarpa, commonly known as the longfruit morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, elongated seeds. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forests.

Mikania longicarpa is also known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain areas.
