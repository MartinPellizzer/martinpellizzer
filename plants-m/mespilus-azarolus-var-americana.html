Mespilus azarolus var. americana

Mespilus azarolus var. americana, commonly known as the American medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets and produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen in the fall. The fruit has a unique texture, with a firm outer husk enclosing a soft, edible pulp. It is often grown for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and fragrant flowers.

The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
