Miconia altissima

Miconia altissima, commonly known as the giant miconia, is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a large shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by birds and other animals.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets make it an invasive species in some areas outside its native range.
