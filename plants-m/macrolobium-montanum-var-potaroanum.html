Macrolobium montanum var. potaroanum

Macrolobium montanum var. potaroanum, commonly known as the Potaro mountain bean, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in forested or mountainous areas.

Due to its ornamental value and nutritional benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
