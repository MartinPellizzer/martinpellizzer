Myrceugenia stenophylla var. latifolia

Myrceugenia stenophylla var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed strawberry tree, is a evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and its role in supporting local wildlife.
