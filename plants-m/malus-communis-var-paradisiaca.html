Malus communis var. paradisiaca

Malus communis var. paradisiaca, commonly known as the paradise apple or Chinese white pear, is a variety of the apple tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, white, and often fragrant fruit, which is typically smaller and less acidic than common apples. The plant produces ornamental flowers in shades of white and pink, adding aesthetic value to gardens. Its leaves are broad and serrated, contributing to its visual appeal.

This variety is valued both for its fruit and its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
