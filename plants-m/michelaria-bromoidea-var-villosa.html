Michelaria bromoidea var. villosa

Michelaria bromoidea var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy michelaria, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in its native habitat.
