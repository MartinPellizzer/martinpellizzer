Mercurialis subcordata

Mercurialis subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mercury.

It is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia, characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically oval-shaped with a slightly heart-shaped base. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize new habitats.
