Myrcia dermatophylla

Myrcia dermatophylla, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to others. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a variety of soil types and adapting well to different environmental conditions.

Myrcia dermatophylla is also known for its aromatic leaves, which have been used in traditional medicine.
