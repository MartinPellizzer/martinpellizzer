Mentha × glabra

Mentha × glabra, commonly known as peppermint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Mentha piperita and Mentha aquatica.

It is characterized by its square, erect stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. Peppermint is native to Europe and Asia but has been widely naturalized in North America.

It is renowned for its strong, refreshing aroma and is widely used in herbal medicine, food, and aromatherapy.
