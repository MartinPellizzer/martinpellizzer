Millina chicoracea

Millina chicoracea, commonly known as the chicory plant, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering head. The plant produces blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in salads or as a coffee substitute, and the roots can be harvested for culinary or medicinal purposes.

Millina chicoracea is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
