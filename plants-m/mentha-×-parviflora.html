Mentha × parviflora

Mentha × parviflora, commonly known as narrow-leaved mint, is a hybrid plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, pale purple flowers. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often used in herbal teas and for its refreshing scent.

Mentha × parviflora is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
