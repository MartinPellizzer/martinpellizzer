Mesembryanthemum munitum

Mesembryanthemum munitum, commonly known as the coastal mesem, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white, waxy hairs. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and coastal environments.

Mesembryanthemum munitum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
