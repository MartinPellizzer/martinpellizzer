Manilkara calderonii

Manilkara calderonii, commonly known as the Calderón’s sapote, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces a yellowish, edible fruit with a creamy, sweet pulp that is often used in culinary applications. Its latex, similar to that of the rubber tree, has been historically used for various industrial purposes.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
