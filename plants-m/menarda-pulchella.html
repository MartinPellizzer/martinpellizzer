Menarda pulchella

Menarda pulchella, commonly known as the showy goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

Menarda pulchella is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
