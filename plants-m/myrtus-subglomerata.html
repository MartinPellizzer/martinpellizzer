Myrtus subglomerata

Myrtus subglomerata, commonly known as the clustered myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, glossy, dark green leaves. Its fragrant flowers are typically white or pale pink and appear in clusters, giving the plant its common name.

Myrtus subglomerata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
