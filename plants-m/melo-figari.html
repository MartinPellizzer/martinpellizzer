Melo figari

Melo figari, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, star-shaped leaves and produces yellow or orange berries that are edible when ripe. The plant has a woody stem with thorny branches, and its flowers are typically white or pale green. Melo figari thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
