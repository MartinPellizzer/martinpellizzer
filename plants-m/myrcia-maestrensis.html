Myrcia maestrensis

Myrcia maestrensis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and fragrant, white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and emit a strong, aromatic scent when crushed.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
