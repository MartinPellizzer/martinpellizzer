Myrtillus exigua

Myrtillus exigua, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

The berries it produces are typically black or purple and contain small seeds.
