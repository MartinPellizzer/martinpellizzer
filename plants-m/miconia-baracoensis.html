Miconia baracoensis

Miconia baracoensis, commonly known as the Brazilian starleaf, is a tropical shrub native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking, large, star-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
