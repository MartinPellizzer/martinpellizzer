Mentha sylvestris subsp. velutina

Mentha sylvestris subsp.

velutina, commonly known as velvet mint, is a perennial herb native to central and eastern Europe. It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name.

The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves are used in traditional herbal medicine and as a flavoring agent.
