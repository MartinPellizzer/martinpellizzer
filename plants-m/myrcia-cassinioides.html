Myrcia cassinioides

Myrcia cassinioides, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often dark green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals.

Myrcia cassinioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its attractive foliage and seasonal flowering.
