Milium pedicellare

Milium pedicellare, commonly known as the slender millet, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like appearance with slender stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that are typically green or pale in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Milium pedicellare is an important plant in ecological studies due to its role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinators.
