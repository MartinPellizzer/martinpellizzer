Mitrephora tomentosa

Mitrephora tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly mitrephora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage. The leaves are typically ovate or elliptic, with a dense covering of soft, white pubescence. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its unique appearance.
