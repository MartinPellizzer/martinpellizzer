Miconia melinonis

Miconia melinonis, commonly known as the Brazilian starwood, is a species of flowering plant in the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. It typically grows as an understory plant in humid, shaded forest environments.

Miconia melinonis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
