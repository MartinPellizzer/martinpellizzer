Maxillaria lamprochlamys

Maxillaria lamprochlamys, commonly known as the golden maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically yellow with red and purple markings. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a fan-like pattern.

Maxillaria lamprochlamys prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
