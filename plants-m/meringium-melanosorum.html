Meringium melanosorum

Meringium melanosorum, commonly known as the black meringia, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its dark, almost obsidian-like leaves and small, delicate white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a unique photosynthetic adaptation, allowing it to thrive in low-light conditions typical of its mountainous habitat. Its roots are deeply embedded in the soil, enabling it to absorb nutrients from the thin, nutrient-poor earth.

Due to its limited distribution and fragile ecosystem, Meringium melanosorum is considered a conservation priority.
