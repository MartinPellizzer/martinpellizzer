Marah minima

Marah minima, commonly known as the dwarf cucumber, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like pumpkins and melons. The plant is characterized by its small, palmate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit, a small, green, edible cucumber, is used in traditional cuisines.

Marah minima thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.
