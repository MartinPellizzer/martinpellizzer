Millettia pilosa

Millettia pilosa, commonly known as the hairy millettia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its seeds are encased in hairy pods, which aid in seed dispersal.

Millettia pilosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
