Mentha sylvestris var. flaccida

Mentha sylvestris var. flaccida, commonly known as the slender wood mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, contributing to its use in traditional herbal medicine.
