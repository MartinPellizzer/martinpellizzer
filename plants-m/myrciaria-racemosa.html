Myrciaria racemosa

Myrciaria racemosa, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry or araçá-preto, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil and is characterized by its dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a slightly aromatic scent.

The berries are not only ornamental but also have traditional uses in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
