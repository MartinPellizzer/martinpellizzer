Myrcia rufa

Myrcia rufa, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. Myrcia rufa produces small, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental beauty and aromatic properties.
