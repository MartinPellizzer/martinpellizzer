Melaleuca sericea

Melaleuca sericea, commonly known as silver bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The leaves are narrow and covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Melaleuca sericea is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
