Manilkara remotifolia

Manilkara remotifolia, commonly known as the wild cocoa tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, supporting diverse wildlife and contributing to forest biodiversity.
