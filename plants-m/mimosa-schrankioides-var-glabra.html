Mimosa schrankioides var. glabra

Mimosa schrankioides var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth mimosa, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and bipinnate leaves that are composed of numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. It is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

The species is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
