Myriopus selleanus

Myriopus selleanus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed saltbush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and is adapted to grow in saline and arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is often found in coastal and inland saltbush habitats. Myriopus selleanus is a hardy species that plays a role in stabilizing soils in harsh conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in ecological restoration projects.
