Myrcia strigipes

Myrcia strigipes, commonly known as the rough myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves that are covered in fine, rough hairs, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically white or pink, and it produces small, woody fruits.

Myrcia strigipes is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
