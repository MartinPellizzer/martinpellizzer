Mimosa debilis var. vestita

Mimosa debilis var. vestita, commonly known as the velvet mimosa, is a low-growing shrub native to South America.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, spherical yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
