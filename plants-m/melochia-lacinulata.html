Melochia lacinulata

Melochia lacinulata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Brazilian spinach, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its palmately lobed leaves and showy yellow flowers. The leaves are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving the plant its common name. It produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that contain several seeds.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
