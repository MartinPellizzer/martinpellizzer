Mallotus resinosus var. cuneatus

Mallotus resinosus var. cuneatus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed tallowwood, is a member of the Sapindaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, followed by woody, oval fruits containing several seeds. It is native to northern Australia and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.

The tree is valued for its durable, heavy timber, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
