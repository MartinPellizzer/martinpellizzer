Musa truncata var. horizontalis

Musa truncata var. horizontalis, commonly known as the horizontalis banana, is a compact, ornamental banana plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy, dark green leaves that grow horizontally, creating a unique, bushy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellow flowers, followed by edible, elongated fruits that are typically smaller than those of common bananas. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil and regular watering.

Due to its aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor spaces.
