Monardella ovata

Monardella ovata, commonly known as narrowleaf monardella, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is part of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, tubular flowers are typically pink or purple and bloom in late summer.

Monardella ovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
