Monardella linoides var. oblonga

Monardella linoides var. oblonga, commonly known as narrowleaf monardella, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are clustered in whorls and range in color from pink to purple.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
