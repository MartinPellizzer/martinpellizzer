Melochia chamaedrys f. typica

Melochia chamaedrys f. typica, commonly known as the creeping melochia, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features dense, dark green leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, fleshy fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens.

Its compact growth habit and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for ground cover in warm climates.
