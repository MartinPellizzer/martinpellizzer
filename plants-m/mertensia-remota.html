Mertensia remota

Mertensia remota, commonly known as the remote bluebells, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, woodsy areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are arranged in clusters at the top of the stem, and it is often found in mountainous regions.

Mertensia remota is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also an important species in its native ecosystem.
