Muraltia spicata

Muraltia spicata, commonly known as the red mombin or red mombin tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant is notable for its ornamental value, featuring clusters of small, fragrant white flowers followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval and slightly hairy.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its medicinal and ornamental properties.
