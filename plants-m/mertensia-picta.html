Mertensia picta

Mertensia picta, commonly known as the painted bluebell, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland environments, thriving in partial shade. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Mertensia picta is valued in gardens for its beauty and ecological importance, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
