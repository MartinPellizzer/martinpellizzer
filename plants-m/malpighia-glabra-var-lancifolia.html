Malpighia glabra var. lancifolia

Malpighia glabra var. lancifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian wolfberry or granadilla, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It produces bright red, fleshy fruits that are rich in vitamin C and antioxidants. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

The fruit's high acidity and unique flavor make it a popular ingredient in culinary and medicinal applications.
