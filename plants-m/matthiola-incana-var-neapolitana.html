Matthiola incana var. neapolitana

Matthiola incana var. neapolitana, commonly known as the Naples wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its foliage is covered in fine, hairy textures, which help reduce water loss in dry conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and resilience in garden settings.
