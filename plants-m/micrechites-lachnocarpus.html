Micrechites lachnocarpus

Micrechites lachnocarpus, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree that typically grows to a height of about 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its dark reddish-brown bark and small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are yellowish-green and appear in clusters, while the fruit is a small, woody capsule.

The tree is valued for its dense, aromatic wood, which has been historically used in furniture and musical instruments.
