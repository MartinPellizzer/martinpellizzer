Mammillaria pazzanii

Mammillaria pazzanii, commonly known as the Pazzani's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense covering of white to pale yellow spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its stems are typically ribbed and can develop a bluish-green hue when mature.

Mammillaria pazzanii is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil to thrive.
