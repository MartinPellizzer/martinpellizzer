Masdevallia elata

Masdevallia elata, commonly known as the Elegant Masdevallia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and clusters of pendant, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 2,500 meters, thriving in cool, humid environments with dappled sunlight. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Masdevallia elata is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses due to its specific environmental requirements.
