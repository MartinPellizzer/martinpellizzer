Muehlenbeckia complexa

Muehlenbeckia complexa, commonly known as silverweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its silver-gray leaves covered in fine, soft hairs, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest floors or along stream banks.

Muehlenbeckia complexa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its decorative foliage.
