Melothria scabra

Melothria scabra, commonly known as the sour sop or custard apple, is a small, woody vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It produces small, egg-shaped fruits with a rough, spiny exterior and a fragrant, creamy interior. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its flowers are small and greenish-white.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications, though its flavor is intensely tart and acidic.
