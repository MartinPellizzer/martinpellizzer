Malvaviscus cordatus

Malvaviscus cordatus, commonly known as the heartleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red, purple, and pink. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton, and is known for its mucilaginous properties in its seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
