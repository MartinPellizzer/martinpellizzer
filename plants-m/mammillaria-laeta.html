Mammillaria laeta

Mammillaria laeta, commonly known as the Shiny Mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and densely covered spines that give it a distinctive, almost velvety appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
