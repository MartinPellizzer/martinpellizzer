Melampodium villicaule

Melampodium villicaule, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy yellow blooms. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and wetlands. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers are arranged in dense, spherical clusters.
