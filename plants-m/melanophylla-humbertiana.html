Melanophylla humbertiana

Melanophylla humbertiana, commonly known as the Humbert's ghost flower, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is known for its delicate, tubular corolla and hairy stems. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and requires a cool, humid environment to flourish.
