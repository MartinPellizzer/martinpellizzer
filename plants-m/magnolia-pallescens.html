Magnolia pallescens

Magnolia pallescens, commonly known as the white magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The tree has smooth, light gray bark and elliptical leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blossoms.
