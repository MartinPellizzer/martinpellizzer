Myrcia warmingiana

Myrcia warmingiana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that contain several seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
