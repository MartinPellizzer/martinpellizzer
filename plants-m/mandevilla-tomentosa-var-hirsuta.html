Mandevilla tomentosa var. hirsuta

Mandevilla tomentosa var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated as a decorative shrub or climber.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for gardens and containers.
