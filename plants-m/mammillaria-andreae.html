Mammillaria andreae

Mammillaria andreae, commonly known as the Andean hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines and small, rounded areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
