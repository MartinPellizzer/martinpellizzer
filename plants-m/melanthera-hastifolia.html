Melanthera hastifolia

Melanthera hastifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swampy areas. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Melanthera hastifolia is an important component of native plant communities and is often found in wetland habitats.
