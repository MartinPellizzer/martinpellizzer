Millettia monophylla

Millettia monophylla, commonly known as the single-leaf millettia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its unique, palmately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. Millettia monophylla is often found in woodland and forested areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
