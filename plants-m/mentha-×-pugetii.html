Mentha × pugetii

Mentha × pugetii, commonly known as peppermint, is a hybrid mint species resulting from the cross between Mentha spicata and Mentha viridis.

It is characterized by its strong, aromatic scent and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. The plant produces opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically pink or purple, appearing in whorls along the stems.

Mentha × pugetii is widely cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy, medicine, and as a flavoring agent.
