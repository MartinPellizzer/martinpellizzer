Melothria rauwenhoffii var. pengalenganensis

Melothria rauwenhoffii var. pengalenganensis, commonly known as the Pengalengan cucumber, is a unique variant of the melothria genus native to Indonesia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its small, elongated fruits that resemble cucumbers in appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers and has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in tropical climates and requires a support structure for its vine growth.

This variety is valued for its edible fruits, which have a crisp texture and a mildly sweet flavor.
