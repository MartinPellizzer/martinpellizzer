Microtis pulchella var. vivax

Microtis pulchella var. vivax, commonly known as the Vivax Microtis, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
