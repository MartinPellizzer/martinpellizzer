Malaxis ottonis

Malaxis ottonis, commonly known as the golden spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, spider-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, showy flower that is typically yellow or golden in color. The plant grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and forest floors, often in rich, organic soil. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique morphology and ecological niche.

Malaxis ottonis is relatively rare and is often found in specific habitats, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
