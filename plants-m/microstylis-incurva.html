Microstylis incurva

Microstylis incurva, commonly known as the curly-styled grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Its unique curved inflorescence and delicate appearance make it a distinctive member of the grass family.

Microstylis incurva is often found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies.
