Malaxis thwaitesii

Malaxis thwaitesii, commonly known as the Christmas orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, pale green or yellowish flowers that bloom during the winter months. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually arranged in a loose spike and are often overlooked due to their modest appearance.

Malaxis thwaitesii is part of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its unique blooming season and subtle beauty.
