Mesembryanthemum ampullaceum

Mesembryanthemum ampullaceum, commonly known as the bladderleaf mesembryanthemum, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, typical of xerophytic adaptations. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
