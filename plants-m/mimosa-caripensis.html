Mimosa caripensis

Mimosa caripensis, commonly known as the Brazilian mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in response to environmental stimuli. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Mimosa caripensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
