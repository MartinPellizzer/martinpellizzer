Mimosa xanthocentra subsp. tremula

Mimosa xanthocentra subsp.

tremula, commonly known as the yellow mimosa, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It features small, fern-like leaves that are sensitive to touch, folding shut when disturbed.

The plant produces yellowish-pink flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its root system is fibrous and helps stabilize soil in arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
