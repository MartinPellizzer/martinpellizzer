Myrcia densa

Myrcia densa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Myrcia densa thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental appearance make it a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
