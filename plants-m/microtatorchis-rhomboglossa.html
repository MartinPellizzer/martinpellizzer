Microtatorchis rhomboglossa

Microtatorchis rhomboglossa, commonly known as the rhomboid-tongued cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in small, spiny tubercles. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to desert environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
