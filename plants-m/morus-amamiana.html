Morus amamiana

Morus amamiana, commonly known as the Amami mulberry, is a species of mulberry native to the Amami Islands of Japan.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring heart-shaped leaves that are typically large and lobed. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are edible for both humans and animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

The Amami mulberry is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
