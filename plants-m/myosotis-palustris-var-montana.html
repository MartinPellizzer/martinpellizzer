Myosotis palustris var. montana

Myosotis palustris var. montana, commonly known as the mountain water violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It features small, delicate blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, adapted to withstand cold and windy conditions. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and rocky slopes.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for rock gardens and alpine displays.
