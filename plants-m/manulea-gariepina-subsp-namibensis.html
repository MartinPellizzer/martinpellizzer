Manulea gariepina subsp. namibensis

Manulea gariepina subsp.

namibensis, commonly known as the Namibian manulea, is a succulent plant native to the Namib Desert. It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, enabling it to survive in arid conditions.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the rainy season, typically in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is adapted to high levels of sunlight and temperature fluctuations.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
