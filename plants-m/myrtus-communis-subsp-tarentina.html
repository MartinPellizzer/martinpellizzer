Myrtus communis subsp. tarentina

Myrtus communis subsp.

tarentina, commonly known as the Tarentine myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, dark green leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine.

The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and rocky environments.

Its ornamental value and historical significance in Mediterranean cultures make it a notable botanical species.
