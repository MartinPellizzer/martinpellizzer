Malpighia revoluta

Malpighia revoluta, commonly known as the purple granadilla, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and elliptical, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that transition from white to pink as they mature. Its most distinctive feature is the bright red, fleshy fruit that resembles a small, rounded berry.

The fruit is edible, with a tart, acidic flavor, and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
