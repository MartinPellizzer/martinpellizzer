Mentha longifolia var. cosmiostachya

Mentha longifolia var. cosmiostachya, commonly known as the narrow-leaf peppermint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and as a flavoring agent.
