Magnolia gloriosa

Magnolia gloriosa, commonly known as the glory magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are broad, elliptical, and turn yellow in the fall before dropping. The tree typically grows to a height of 20 to 30 feet, with a rounded crown.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental tree in gardens and parks.
