Momordica charantia var. zeylanica

Momordica charantia var. zeylanica, commonly known as bitter melon, is a tropical vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, warty, green fruit that turns orange when ripe. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has heart-shaped, lobed leaves. It thrives in warm climates and is widely cultivated for its edible fruit, which is used in various cuisines.

The plant contains compounds like charantin and momordicin, which have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
