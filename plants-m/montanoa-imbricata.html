Montanoa imbricata

Montanoa imbricata, commonly known as the Mexican velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to central and southern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

Montanoa imbricata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
