Malveopsis mexicana

Malveopsis mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican malvaceous plant, is a flowering plant species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its showy, pentamerous flowers that typically range in color from pink to white. Its leaves are ovate and covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

This species is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
