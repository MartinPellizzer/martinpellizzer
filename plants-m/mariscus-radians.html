Mariscus radians

Mariscus radians, commonly known as the radiant mariscus, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of violet and blue, often in late summer. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, helping it retain moisture in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and unique floral structure, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
