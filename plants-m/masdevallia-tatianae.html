Masdevallia tatianae

Masdevallia tatianae, commonly known as the Tatiana's Masdevallia, is a rare and exquisite orchid native to the high-altitude cloud forests of Colombia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit and intricate, often colorful flower structures. The plant typically features dark green, strap-like leaves and produces striking, spurred flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. It thrives in cool, humid environments with high light intensity and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is a prized species among orchid enthusiasts and conservationists.
