Microstylis umbellulata

Microstylis umbellulata, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and a dense cluster of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces tiny, three-lobed flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along the edges of ponds.

Microstylis umbellulata is a hardy perennial that plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
