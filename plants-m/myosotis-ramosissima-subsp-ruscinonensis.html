Myosotis ramosissima subsp. ruscinonensis

Myosotis ramosissima subsp.

ruscinonensis, commonly known as the Ruscinonensis Forget-me-not, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy.

The plant produces clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers with five petals, typical of the Myosotis genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
