Myrcia albescens

Myrcia albescens, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are typically narrow, leathery, and emit a strong, aromatic scent when crushed.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
