Maxillaria speciosa

Maxillaria speciosa, commonly known as the showy maxillaria, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that often feature intricate patterns and a pleasant fragrance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or other structures in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and succulent, adapted to store water in arid conditions.

Maxillaria speciosa blooms during the warm season, producing multiple inflorescences that enhance its ornamental value.
