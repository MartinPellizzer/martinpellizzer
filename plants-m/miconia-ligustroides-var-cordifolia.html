Miconia ligustroides var. cordifolia

Miconia ligustroides var. cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf miconia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by distinctive, velvety, purple or red fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
