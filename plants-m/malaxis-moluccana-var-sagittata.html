Malaxis moluccana var. sagittata

Malaxis moluccana var. sagittata, commonly known as the arrow-shaped marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lanceolate leaves that are often arranged in a rosette. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive arrowhead-shaped lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as swamps and marshes.

Its botanical features make it a unique and ecologically important species within its habitat.
