Malaxis excentrica

Malaxis excentrica, commonly known as the eccentric malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant has a short, stocky rhizome and produces a single, erect stem with a few leaves. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinctive spur, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often found in tropical rainforests.
