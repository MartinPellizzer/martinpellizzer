Muscari matritense

Muscari matritense, commonly known as the Spanish grape hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to deep violet. The plant typically blooms in early spring and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Muscari matritense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to naturalize over time.
