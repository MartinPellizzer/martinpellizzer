Malpighia lancifolia

Malpighia lancifolia, commonly known as the sharpleaf acerola, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, bright red berries that are rich in vitamin C. The plant belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits. Its flowers are typically white or pink, with five petals, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

The fruit, while tart, is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
