Myrcia lasiopus

Myrcia lasiopus, commonly known as the rough myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests.

Myrcia lasiopus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
