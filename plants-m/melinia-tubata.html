Melinia tubata

Melinia tubata, commonly known as the water plantain, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the wetlands of North America.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically submerged or floating, with a waxy surface that helps repel water. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white, which develop into bladder-like fruits containing seeds. Melinia tubata thrives in shallow, still waters and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
