Matthiola dimorpha

Matthiola dimorpha, commonly known as the double-flowered stock, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered flowers that resemble small, double blossoms, typically in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Matthiola dimorpha is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and fragrance.
