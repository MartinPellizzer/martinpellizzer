Mertensia texana

Mertensia texana, commonly known as bluebells, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features heart-shaped, aromatic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Mertensia texana is a member of the nightshade family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
