Mimulus lewisii var. albus

Mimulus lewisii var. albus, commonly known as the white monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It has slender, branching stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
