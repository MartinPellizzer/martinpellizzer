Madhuca lanuginosa

Madhuca lanuginosa, commonly known as the silky tallow tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, soft, and hairy foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

The tree is valued for its durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
