Miscanthus sinensis f. glaber

Miscanthus sinensis f. glaber, commonly known as silver grass, is a cultivar of the Miscanthus genus native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-blue foliage and tall, arching inflorescences that add ornamental value to gardens. The plant is typically perennial, thriving in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil. It is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for ornamental grass gardens.
