Moliniopsis nipponica

Moliniopsis nipponica, commonly known as Japanese windgrass, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Moliniopsis nipponica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
