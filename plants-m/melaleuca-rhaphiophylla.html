Melaleuca rhaphiophylla

Melaleuca rhaphiophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paperbark, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, flaky bark that peels away in strips, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically appearing in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a reddish or purple hue when new.

This species is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.
