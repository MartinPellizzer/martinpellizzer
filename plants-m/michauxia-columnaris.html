Michauxia columnaris

Michauxia columnaris, commonly known as the columnar saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, columnar growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers arranged in dense clusters at the tips of its stems. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapted to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.

Michauxia columnaris is often found in alpine environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in rock gardens.
