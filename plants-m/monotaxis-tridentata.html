Monotaxis tridentata

Monotaxis tridentata, commonly known as the three-toothed spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, ribbed stems and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Monotaxis tridentata is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
