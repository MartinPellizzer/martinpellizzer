Mohria burniifolia

Mohria burniifolia, commonly known as the Kashmiri rose or Himalayan rose, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a fine layer of white hair, which helps reduce water loss.

Mohria burniifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens in temperate regions.
