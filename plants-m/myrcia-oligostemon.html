Myrcia oligostemon

Myrcia oligostemon, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy red or orange flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

Myrcia oligostemon is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
