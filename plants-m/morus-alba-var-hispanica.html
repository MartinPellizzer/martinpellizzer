Morus alba var. hispanica

Morus alba var. hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish white mulberry, is a variant of the white mulberry tree native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically pale green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as mulberries, which are usually pink to red in color when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its fruit and as a source of food for silkworms.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
