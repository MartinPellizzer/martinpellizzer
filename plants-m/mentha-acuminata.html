Mentha acuminata

Mentha acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mint, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, pointed leaves and strong, aromatic mint scent. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowering spikes bear small, pale blue to purple flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Mentha acuminata is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to its refreshing flavor and therapeutic properties.
