Myrcia roraimensis

Myrcia roraimensis, commonly known as the Roraima myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest, particularly in Venezuela, Guyana, and Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the wet season. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Myrcia roraimensis plays a role in the ecological dynamics of the rainforest, providing habitat and food for various insect and bird species.
