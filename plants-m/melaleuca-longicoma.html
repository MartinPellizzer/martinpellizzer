Melaleuca longicoma

Melaleuca longicoma, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paperbark, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that gives it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow and often have a silvery-white underside, while the flowers are small and usually pink or red.

Melaleuca longicoma is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
