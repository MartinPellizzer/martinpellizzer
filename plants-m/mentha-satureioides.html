Mentha satureioides

Mentha satureioides, commonly known as wild mint, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are serrated and have a strong, minty fragrance, while its flowers are small and tubular, often purple or blue in color.

Mentha satureioides is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
