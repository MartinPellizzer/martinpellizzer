Marattia howeana

Marattia howeana, commonly known as the Howe's marattiopsis, is a fern belonging to the family Marattiaceae.

It is characterized by its large, compound fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant features a creeping rhizome with sporangia clustered in strobili, which are typically borne on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments.

Marattia howeana is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
