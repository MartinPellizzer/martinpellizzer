Myrcianthes terminalis

Myrcianthes terminalis, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, red berries. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while its flowers are clustered in spikes.

The fruit is edible and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
