Mentha macrostachya var. major

Mentha macrostachya var. major, commonly known as the large-stamened mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and clusters of pale purple to white flowers. The plant has square stems and opposite leaves with a serrated margin. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
