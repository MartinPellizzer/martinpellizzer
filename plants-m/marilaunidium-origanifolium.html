Marilaunidium origanifolium

Marilaunidium origanifolium, commonly known as the lemon-scented orchid, is a unique member of the orchid family with distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that resemble those of oregano, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers a sunny to partially shaded environment.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
