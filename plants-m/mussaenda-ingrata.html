Mussaenda ingrata

Mussaenda ingrata, commonly known as the yellow mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences of yellow or orange flowers, which are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
