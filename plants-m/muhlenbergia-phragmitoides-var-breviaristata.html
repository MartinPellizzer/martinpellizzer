Muhlenbergia phragmitoides var. breviaristata

Muhlenbergia phragmitoides var. breviaristata, commonly known as narrowleaf muhlenbergia, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, a common reproductive strategy in grasses. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
