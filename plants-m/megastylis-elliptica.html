Megastylis elliptica

Megastylis elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic-styled orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in local ecosystems.

Megastylis elliptica is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
