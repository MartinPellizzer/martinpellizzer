Mentha × crepiniana

Mentha × crepiniana, commonly known as catnip, is a hybrid mint species that combines traits from various mint species.

It is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and Africa, often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated edge. Its small, pale purple flowers are arranged in whorled clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Mentha × crepiniana is noted for its strong aromatic foliage, which contains compounds like nepetalactone, responsible for its distinctive scent and effects on animals.
