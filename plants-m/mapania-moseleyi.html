Mapania moseleyi

Mapania moseleyi, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. Its leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth texture.

Mapania moseleyi thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, dry climate for optimal growth.
