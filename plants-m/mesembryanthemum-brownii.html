Mesembryanthemum brownii

Mesembryanthemum brownii, commonly known as the Brown's ice plant, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine, white powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Mesembryanthemum brownii is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought and its ornamental appeal.
