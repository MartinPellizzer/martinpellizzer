Macaranga seemannii var. capillata

Macaranga seemannii var. capillata, commonly known as the silky macaranga, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas.
