Marrubium saxatile

Marrubium saxatile, commonly known as the rock horehound, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or dry soils and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions.

Marrubium saxatile is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
