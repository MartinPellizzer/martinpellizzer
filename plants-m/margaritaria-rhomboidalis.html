Margaritaria rhomboidalis

Margaritaria rhomboidalis, commonly known as the diamondleaf or diamondleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive diamond-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has a sprawling growth habit and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
