Miconia howardiana

Miconia howardiana, commonly known as the purple miconia, is a member of the Melastomataceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a fleshy capsule that contains numerous small seeds.

Miconia howardiana is also notable for its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
