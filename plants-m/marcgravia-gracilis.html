Marcgravia gracilis

Marcgravia gracilis, commonly known as the slender staghorn fern, is a species of epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its unique, shield-shaped fronds that resemble the antlers of a stag. The plant produces small, white spore-bearing structures called spathes, which are typically found at the base of the fronds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires a well-draining substrate to prevent root rot.

Marcgravia gracilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
