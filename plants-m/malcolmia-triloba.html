Malcolmia triloba

Malcolmia triloba, commonly known as the blueweed or Egyptian mallow, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features erect, branching stems that are covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces showy, pale blue to violet flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a velvety texture.

Malcolmia triloba is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
