Myrtus valdiviana

Myrtus valdiviana, commonly known as the Valdivia myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and tea tree. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are small, dark berries that contain seeds.

Myrtus valdiviana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
