Mikania cordigera

Mikania cordigera, commonly known as the heartleaf morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, and its flowers are typically blue or violet with five petals. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Mikania cordigera is valued for its ornamental appeal but can also be invasive in certain ecosystems.
