Melica cupani f. hirtella

Melica cupani f. hirtella, commonly known as hairy cupgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial species characterized by its hairy foliage and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in moist or wet environments such as marshes, swales, and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a subject of study in ecological and botanical research.
