Mikania scandens var. villosa

Mikania scandens var. villosa, commonly known as hairy morning glory, is a vigorous climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color. The plant is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and forest edges. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets.

The hairy texture of its stems and leaves is a distinguishing characteristic of this variant.
