Mentha szabolesensis

Mentha szabolesensis, commonly known as Hungarian mint, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian Basin.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and has a strong, minty fragrance. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in whorls.

Mentha szabolesensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
