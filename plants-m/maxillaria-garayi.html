Maxillaria garayi

Maxillaria garayi, commonly known as the Garay's orchid, is a species native to South America, particularly found in Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowering structures. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white with distinctive purple or brown markings.

Maxillaria garayi thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
