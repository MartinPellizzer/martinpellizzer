Myristica inaequalis

Myristica inaequalis, commonly known as the unequal myristica, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, oily seeds that are used in traditional medicine and perfumery. The plant produces large, leathery leaves and fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit, which is covered in a fleshy, red aril, contains the valuable seed kernels.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
