Masdevallia astuta

Masdevallia astuta, commonly known as the stiff masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments at high altitudes. Its pseudobulbs are elongated and covered with a thick layer of white to brownish bark.

Masdevallia astuta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and unique foliage.
