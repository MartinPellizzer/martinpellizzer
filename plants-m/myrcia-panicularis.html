Myrcia panicularis

Myrcia panicularis, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it is considered a problematic species in some regions.
