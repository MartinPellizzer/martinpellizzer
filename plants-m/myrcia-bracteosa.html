Myrcia bracteosa

Myrcia bracteosa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen shrub or small tree form. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, red drupe that contains several seeds.

Myrcia bracteosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
