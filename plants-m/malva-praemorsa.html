Malva praemorsa

Malva praemorsa, commonly known as the death's-head hawkmoth plant, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, often found in dry, rocky soils. The plant produces showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and somewhat woody.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
