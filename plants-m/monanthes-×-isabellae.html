Monanthes × isabellae

Monanthes × isabellae, commonly known as the Isabelline Hybrid Monanthes, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Monanthes arvensis and Monanthes sylvatica.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
