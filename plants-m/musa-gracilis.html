Musa gracilis

Musa gracilis, commonly known as the slender banana plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender pseudostem and large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by elongated, curved fruits that are typically eaten raw or cooked. Musa gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
