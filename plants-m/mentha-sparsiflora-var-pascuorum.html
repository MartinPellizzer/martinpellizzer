Mentha sparsiflora var. pascuorum

Mentha sparsiflora var. pascuorum, commonly known as meadow mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, aromatic stems that grow up to 60 cm in height, often forming dense mats in moist meadows and woodland edges. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorls along the stem, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are narrow and slightly toothed, with a strong minty fragrance when crushed.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
