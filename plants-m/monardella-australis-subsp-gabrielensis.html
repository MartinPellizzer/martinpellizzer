Monardella australis subsp. gabrielensis

Monardella australis subsp.

gabrielensis, commonly known as Gabriel's mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands.

The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and a distinctive, aromatic scent. Its flowering spikes produce small, white to pale purple flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and geographic distribution compared to other Monardella species.
