Musa campestris var. campestris

Musa campestris var. campestris, commonly known as the Chinese banana, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers, which eventually develop into elongated, curved fruits. The fruits are typically eaten raw or cooked and have a mild, sweet flavor.

It is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its edible fruit.
