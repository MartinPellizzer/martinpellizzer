Monardella odoratissima subsp. australis

Monardella odoratissima subsp.

australis, commonly known as the coastal monardella, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as coastal bluffs and meadows. It has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The species is part of the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic compounds and medicinal properties.
