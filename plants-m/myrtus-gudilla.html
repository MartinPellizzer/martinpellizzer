Myrtus gudilla

Myrtus gudilla, commonly known as the Canary Islands myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant features dark green, glossy leaves and small white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its fragrant leaves are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Myrtus gudilla is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils and is relatively drought-tolerant.
