Muhlenbergia plumbea

Muhlenbergia plumbea, commonly known as the silverbluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, silvery-blue leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation.

Muhlenbergia plumbea is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in stabilizing soil in native plant landscapes.
