Myrtus petri-ludovici

Myrtus petri-ludovici, commonly known as the Mediterranean myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dark green, glossy leaves and produces small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring. The plant is closely related to the common myrtle (Myrtus communis) and shares similar botanical characteristics. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Myrtus petri-ludovici is also valued for its aromatic oils and historical use in traditional medicine.
