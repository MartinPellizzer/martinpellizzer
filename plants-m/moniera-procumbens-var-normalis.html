Moniera procumbens var. normalis

Moniera procumbens var. normalis, commonly known as the creeping moniera, is a low-growing herbaceous plant characterized by its prostrate growth habit.

It features small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, arranged in clusters along the stems. The leaves are narrow and linear, often covered with a fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories or along stream banks.

The plant is noted for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, forming dense mats in suitable habitats.
