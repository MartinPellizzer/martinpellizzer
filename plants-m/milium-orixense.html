Milium orixense

Milium orixense, commonly known as the Orixan grass, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Central America and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale green flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Milium orixense is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.
