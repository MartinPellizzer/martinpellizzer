Madronella thymifolia

Madronella thymifolia, commonly known as the thyme-leaved madronella, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves that resemble those of thyme. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its unique appearance and fragrance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
