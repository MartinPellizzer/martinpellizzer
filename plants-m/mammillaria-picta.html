Mammillaria picta

Mammillaria picta, commonly known as the Painted Mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and dense, white to pinkish spines that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in the spring.

Due to its compact size and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
