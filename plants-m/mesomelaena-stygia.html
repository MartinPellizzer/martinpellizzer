Mesomelaena stygia

Mesomelaena stygia, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic flowering plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, pendulous flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, often appearing translucent or pale in color. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them, relying instead on atmospheric moisture and debris. Its botanical structure includes a short, thick rhizome and fleshy, strap-like leaves that store water.

Due to its elusive nature and habitat specificity, Mesomelaena stygia is highly sought after by botanists and conservationists alike.
