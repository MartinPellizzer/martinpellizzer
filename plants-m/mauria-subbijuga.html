Mauria subbijuga

Mauria subbijuga, commonly known as the narrow-leafed dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silver-green leaves and produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is native to southern Africa and thrives in dry, rocky habitats. It has a taproot system and is often found in coastal areas and grasslands.

The flowers are followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds, aiding in its propagation.
