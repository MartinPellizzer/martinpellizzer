Mesembryanthemum nucifer

Mesembryanthemum nucifer, commonly known as the African ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique ability to retain moisture in its leaves is a key adaptation to its natural habitat.
