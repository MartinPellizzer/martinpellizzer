Miyamayomena angustifolia

Miyamayomena angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water plant, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Japan.

It features long, slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 10 cm in length. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the summer months, often in clusters near the water's surface. It thrives in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds and streams.

Its leaves are often used in traditional Japanese gardens for their aesthetic and ecological value.
