Malveopsis calycina

Malveopsis calycina, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural studies.
