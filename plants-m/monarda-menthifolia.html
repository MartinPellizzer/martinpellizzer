Monarda menthifolia

Monarda menthifolia, commonly known as lemon bee balm, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features square, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are fragrant and often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which bloom in whorls along the upper stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its aromatic foliage and nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
