Myrcia carvalhoi

Myrcia carvalhoi, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are edible but have a sharp, peppery flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas.

Myrcia carvalhoi is also known for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine.
