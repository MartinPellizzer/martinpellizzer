Miconia clidemioides

Miconia clidemioides, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are attractive to birds, aiding in seed dispersal. It is often considered an invasive species in regions outside its native range due to its rapid growth and ability to outcompete native vegetation.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a challenge for ecosystem management.
