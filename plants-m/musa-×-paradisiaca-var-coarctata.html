Musa × paradisiaca var. coarctata

Musa × paradisiaca var. coarctata, commonly known as the dwarf banana, is a compact variety of banana plant characterized by its shorter stature and smaller leaves.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia, where it thrives in tropical climates. The plant produces small, edible bananas that are often used in local cuisines. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.

Due to its resilience and ease of care, it is a popular choice for both ornamental and small-scale agricultural purposes.
