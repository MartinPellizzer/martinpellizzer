Mimosa luisana

Mimosa luisana, commonly known as the Luisana sensitive plant, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Mimosa genus within the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a subtle ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or near water sources.

Due to its sensitivity to touch, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical gardens.
