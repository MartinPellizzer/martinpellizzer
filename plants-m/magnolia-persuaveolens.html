Magnolia persuaveolens

Magnolia persuaveolens, commonly known as the Sweetbay magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The flowers are typically clustered in panicles and emit a sweet, spicy scent. The tree produces small, reddish-brown fruits that contain winged seeds.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
