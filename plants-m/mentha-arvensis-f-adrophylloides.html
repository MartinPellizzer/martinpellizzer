Mentha arvensis f. adrophylloides

Mentha arvensis f. adrophylloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mint, is a botanical variant of the broadleaf mint species.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and a more compact growth habit compared to its parent species. This subspecies is native to regions of Europe and Asia, often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies and culinary applications.
