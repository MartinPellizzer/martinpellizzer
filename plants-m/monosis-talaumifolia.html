Monosis talaumifolia

Monosis talaumifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf monosis, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Monosaceae and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
