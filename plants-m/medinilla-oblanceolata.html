Medinilla oblanceolata

Medinilla oblanceolata, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or velvet bell, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisine. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Medinilla oblanceolata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and unique foliage.
