Mammillaria punctata

Mammillaria punctata, commonly known as the punctate hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in small, rounded tubercles. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its compact size and unique appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
