Mentha arvensis f. divaricata

Mentha arvensis f. divaricata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved field mint, is a variant of the field mint species native to temperate regions.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and a strong, minty aroma. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically violet or blue in color, appearing in clusters.

It is often used in herbal medicine and as a culinary herb, similar to other mint species.
