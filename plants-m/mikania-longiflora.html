Mikania longiflora

Mikania longiflora, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and trailing habit, often forming dense mats on the ground. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming throughout the growing season. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are slender and can spread over large areas.

Mikania longiflora is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and light conditions, making it both a common weed and an ornamental plant.
