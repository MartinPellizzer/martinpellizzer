Mariscus anceps

Mariscus anceps, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mariscus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, typically found in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant has slender, upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Mariscus anceps is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
