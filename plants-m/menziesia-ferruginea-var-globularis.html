Menziesia ferruginea var. globularis

Menziesia ferruginea var. globularis, commonly known as the rusty menziesia, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in rust-colored hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in late spring, which are followed by red, fuzzy berries. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
