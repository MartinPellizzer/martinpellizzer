Mimosa ferricola

Mimosa ferricola, commonly known as the ironwood mimosa, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woody stems and compound leaves that are typically small and oval in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the summer months. It is adapted to sandy or rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy species in arid environments.

Mimosa ferricola is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, which helps improve soil fertility in its native habitat.
