Microchilus kuduyariensis

Microchilus kuduyariensis, commonly known as the Kuduyar hills orchid, is a rare flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit. The plant produces small, delicate flowers with intricate patterns, typically in shades of white and green. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense undergrowth.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
