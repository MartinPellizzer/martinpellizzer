Megastachya infirma

Megastachya infirma, commonly known as the dwarf spikegrass, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in wetland restoration projects.
