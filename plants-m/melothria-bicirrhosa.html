Melothria bicirrhosa

Melothria bicirrhosa, commonly known as the twin-stemmed sourberry, is a tropical vine native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, green, sour-tasting berries. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically small and yellow. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found growing along riverbanks and in moist forests.

Due to its unique appearance and tart flavor, it is occasionally cultivated for ornamental or culinary purposes.
