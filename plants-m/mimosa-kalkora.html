Mimosa kalkora

Mimosa kalkora, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Mimosa kalkora is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and unique leaf response to stimuli.
