Maranta disticha

Maranta disticha, commonly known as the arrowroot plant, is a tropical species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or red, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its rhizomes are used as a starchy food source, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded gardens.

Maranta disticha thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
