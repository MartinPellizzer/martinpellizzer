Melastoma muticum

Melastoma muticum, commonly known as the "Malay blueberry," is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, elliptical leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing numerous seeds.

Melastoma muticum is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and along streambanks.
