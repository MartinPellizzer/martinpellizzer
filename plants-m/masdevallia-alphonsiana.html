Masdevallia alphonsiana

Masdevallia alphonsiana, commonly known as the Alphonsian orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the high altitudes of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its unique, often intricate flower structures. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, thriving in cool, misty conditions. Its flowers, which can be white, pink, or purple, are often adorned with intricate patterns and have a delicate, ephemeral beauty.

Masdevallia alphonsiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its aesthetic and botanical significance.
