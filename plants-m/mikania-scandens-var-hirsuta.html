Mikania scandens var. hirsuta

Mikania scandens var. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy climbing hemp, is a fast-growing, perennial vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Its rapid growth and invasive potential make it a notable species in ecological studies.
