Melothria ciprianii

Melothria ciprianii, commonly known as the Ciprian's bitter melon, is a tropical vine native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It produces small, elongated fruits that are typically green and covered in a rough, bumpy texture. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and tendrils that help it climb and spread. Its flowers are small and yellow, often appearing in clusters.

The fruit is known for its sharp, bitter taste, which is used in various local cuisines.
