Mallotus puber

Mallotus puber, commonly known as the rough mallot, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its rough, often hairy stems and leaves. The leaves are typically alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a prominent midrib. It produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by woody fruits containing several seeds.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
