Myosotis orientalis

Myosotis orientalis, commonly known as the东方紫罗兰, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Myosotidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are arranged in clusters at the top of the stems, and it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Myosotis orientalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
