Monardella villosa var. interior

Monardella villosa var. interior, commonly known as interior hairy beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and spikes of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry meadows and open woodlands. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it an important plant for ecological diversity.

The species is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
