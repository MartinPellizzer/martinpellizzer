Malva italica var. flabellata

Malva italica var. flabellata, commonly known as the Italian mallow or velvetleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its hairy stems and lobed leaves are adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
