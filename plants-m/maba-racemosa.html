Maba racemosa

Maba racemosa, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically white or pale yellow in color. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

The tree is valued for its durable wood and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
