Melilotus compactus subsp. leiospermus

Melilotus compactus subsp.

leiospermus, commonly known as narrowleaf melilot, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
