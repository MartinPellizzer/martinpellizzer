Malacothrix foliosa subsp. philbrickii

Malacothrix foliosa subsp.

philbrickii, commonly known as the Philbrick's leafy thorn, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, tubular flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited geographic distribution.
