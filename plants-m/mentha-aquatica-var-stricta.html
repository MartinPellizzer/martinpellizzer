Mentha aquatica var. stricta

Mentha aquatica var. stricta, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herb native to wetland environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near streams, ponds, and marshes.

Due to its aromatic properties, it has been used historically in herbal remedies and as a culinary herb.
