Mangifera indica var. sangier

Mangifera indica var. sangier, commonly known as the Sanglier mango, is a variety of mango native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, yellow-orange fruit with a thin, smooth skin. The flesh is typically sweet and fibrous, with a high sugar content and a mild aroma. This variety is well-suited for tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

It is often cultivated for both local consumption and commercial trade.
