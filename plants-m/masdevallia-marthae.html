Masdevallia marthae

Masdevallia marthae, commonly known as the Martha's Masdevallia, is a rare and striking orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its elongated, pendulous inflorescence and vibrant, colorful flowers that often display a mix of red, orange, and yellow hues. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments with high air circulation. Its leaves are usually strap-like and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its unique appearance.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is highly valued in botanical collections and conservation efforts.
