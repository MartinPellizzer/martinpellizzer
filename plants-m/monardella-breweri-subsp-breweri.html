Monardella breweri subsp. breweri

Monardella breweri subsp.

breweri, commonly known as Brewer's monardella, is a flowering plant native to California. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, tubular flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and chaparral. It produces pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance as a nectar source for native pollinators.
