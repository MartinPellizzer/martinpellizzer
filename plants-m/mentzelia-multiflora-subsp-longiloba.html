Mentzelia multiflora subsp. longiloba

Mentzelia multiflora subsp.

longiloba, commonly known as the long-stemmed blazingstar, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
