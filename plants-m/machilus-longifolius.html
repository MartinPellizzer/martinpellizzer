Machilus longifolius

Machilus longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved Chinese laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, aromatic leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by elliptical, reddish-brown berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Machilus longifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
