Myrcia calyptranthoides

Myrcia calyptranthoides, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, round, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Myrcia calyptranthoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in folk medicine.
