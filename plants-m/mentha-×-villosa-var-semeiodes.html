Mentha × villosa var. semeiodes

Mentha × villosa var. semeiodes, commonly known as the woolly mint or fuzzy mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in whorled clusters along the stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
