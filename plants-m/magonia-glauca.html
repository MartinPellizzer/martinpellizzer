Magonia glauca

Magonia glauca, commonly known as the blue glaucous bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The leaves are typically gray-green in color, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
