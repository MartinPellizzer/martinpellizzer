Melanidion

Melanidion, commonly known as the black alder, is a species of alder native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in wet or moist habitats, such as riverbanks and floodplains. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. Its small, yellowish flowers appear in catkins during the spring, and it produces small, woody fruits.

The bark of the Melanidion is thick and fissured, often appearing dark and rugged.
