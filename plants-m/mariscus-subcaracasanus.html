Mariscus subcaracasanus

Mariscus subcaracasanus, commonly known as the Carpathian bellflower, is a rare and endemic species native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is a herbaceous plant with delicate, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue and violet. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Mariscus subcaracasanus plays an important role in its ecosystem, supporting various pollinators and contributing to local biodiversity.
