Melochia carthaginensis

Melochia carthaginensis, commonly known as the red-rooted morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from yellow to red. The plant is characterized by its distinctive red roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Melochia carthaginensis is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, known for its rapid growth and ornamental value.
