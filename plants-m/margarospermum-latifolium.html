Margarospermum latifolium

Margarospermum latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf margarospermum, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are often glossy and deeply veined. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by distinctive, three-lobed fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
