Mesembryanthemum rubricaule

Mesembryanthemum rubricaule, commonly known as the red-flowered ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, often blue-green leaves and vibrant red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are typically short-lived, lasting only a day, which is a common trait among the Mesembryanthemum genus.

The plant is also known for its ability to tolerate drought conditions due to its succulent nature.
