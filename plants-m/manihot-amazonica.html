Manihot amazonica

Manihot amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian arrowroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its roots are tuberous and are used as a starchy food source, similar to cassava.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.
