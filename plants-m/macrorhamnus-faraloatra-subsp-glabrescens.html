Macrorhamnus faraloatra subsp. glabrescens

Macrorhamnus faraloatra subsp.

glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-fruited hibiscus, is a shrub native to Madagascar. It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often glabrous or nearly hairless.

The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming during the dry season. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release seeds.

This subspecies is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
