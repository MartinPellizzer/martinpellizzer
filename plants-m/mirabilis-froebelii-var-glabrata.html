Mirabilis froebelii var. glabrata

Mirabilis froebelii var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth-flowered four o'clock, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that open in the late afternoon and close at dawn. The plant produces bright red or orange tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It has hairy stems and leaves, though the variety glabrata is noted for its smooth, hairless foliage.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
