Miconia loxensis

Miconia loxensis, commonly known as the Brazilian starapple, is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically white or pink, followed by orange or red berries that contain several seeds.

It is often found in humid, lowland tropical forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
