Mertensia canescens

Mertensia canescens, commonly known as bluebells, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Mertensia canescens is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
