Mentha × gothica

Mentha × gothica, commonly known as catnip, is a hybrid mint species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its square, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. Mentha × gothica is known for its strong aromatic properties, which come from essential oils such as nepetalactone.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.
