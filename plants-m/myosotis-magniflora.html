Myosotis magniflora

Myosotis magniflora, commonly known as the grand bluebell, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its striking, large blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Myosotis magniflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
