Mendoncia quadrata

Mendoncia quadrata, commonly known as the four-angled morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive quadrangular stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas.

Mendoncia quadrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
