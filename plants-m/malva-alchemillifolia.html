Malva alchemillifolia

Malva alchemillifolia, commonly known as the lesser mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its lobed leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
