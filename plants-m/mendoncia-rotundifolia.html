Mendoncia rotundifolia

Mendoncia rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf mendoncia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as cloud forests. Its botanical features include a woody stem and a long flowering period, making it a notable species in its native habitat.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fragrance.
