Millettia stipularis

Millettia stipularis, commonly known as the red sanders, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by flat, elongated pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It is valued for its durable and aromatic heartwood, which is used in furniture and carving.

Due to overharvesting, it is now classified as an endangered species.
