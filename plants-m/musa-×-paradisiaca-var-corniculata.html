Musa × paradisiaca var. corniculata

Musa × paradisiaca var. corniculata, commonly known as the horned banana, is a cultivated variant of the banana plant characterized by its distinctive, curling leaves that resemble horns.

It is a member of the Musaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia. The plant produces elongated, curved bananas that are typically smaller than common bananas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal rather than its culinary use.

The plant thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.
