Marsilea drummondii var. oxaloides

Marsilea drummondii var. oxaloides, commonly known as the water clover, is a aquatic fern belonging to the family Marsileaceae.

It is characterized by its small, floating leaves that resemble clover leaves, with four lobes and a central vein. The plant typically grows in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater environments. It produces tiny, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spheroidal inflorescences.

This variety is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich waters and is often used in aquatic gardens for its ornamental value.
