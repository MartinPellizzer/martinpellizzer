Mentha aristidis

Mentha aristidis, commonly known as the noble mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in whorled clusters along the stems. Mentha aristidis is valued for its medicinal properties, including its use in traditional remedies for digestive and respiratory ailments.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and culinary gardens.
