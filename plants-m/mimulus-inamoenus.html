Mimulus inamoenus

Mimulus inamoenus, commonly known as the yellow monkeyflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as streambanks and meadows. Its leaves are oval and slightly hairy, with a rough texture.

Mimulus inamoenus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a pollinator attractor.
