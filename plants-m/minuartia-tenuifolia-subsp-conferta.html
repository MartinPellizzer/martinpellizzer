Minuartia tenuifolia subsp. conferta

Minuartia tenuifolia subsp.

conferta, commonly known as the dense hairgrass, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to rocky and alpine environments. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs.

The flowers are typically pale blue or white, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in mountainous regions.

This subspecies is well adapted to harsh conditions, showcasing resilience and a unique ecological role in its habitat.
