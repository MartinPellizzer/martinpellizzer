Myagrum aquaticum

Myagrum aquaticum, commonly known as the water hibiscus, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features striking red or pink flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically submerged or floating on the water's surface. Its roots form a dense network that helps stabilize aquatic ecosystems and improve water quality.

Myagrum aquaticum is often cultivated in ponds and water gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
