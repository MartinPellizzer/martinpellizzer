Macronyx strigosus

Macronyx strigosus, commonly known as the roughleaf macronyx, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as slopes and canyons. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its hardy nature.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid environments and suitable for xeriscaping.
