Morus tatayiba

Morus tatayiba, commonly known as the Indian mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to South Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, clustered fruiting structures. The tree produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically eaten by birds and other animals, aiding in seed dispersal. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits and fibrous bark.

The plant has also been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
