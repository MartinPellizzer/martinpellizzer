Myrtoleucodendron erianthum

Myrtoleucodendron erianthum, commonly known as the woolly myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Myrtoleucodendron erianthum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
