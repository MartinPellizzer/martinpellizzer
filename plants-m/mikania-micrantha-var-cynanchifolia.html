Mikania micrantha var. cynanchifolia

Mikania micrantha var. cynanchifolia, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, vine-like stems that can rapidly spread and cover large areas, making it an aggressive invader in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, and its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to outcompete native vegetation, it is considered a significant weed in many parts of the world.
