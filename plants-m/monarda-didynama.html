Monarda didynama

Monarda didynama, commonly known as scarlet bee balm, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its showy, tubular red flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and features aromatic, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped.

Its botanical name reflects its historical use by Indigenous peoples for medicinal purposes, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils in partial to full sun.
