Microsisymbrium

Microsisymbrium, commonly known as the "false vetch," is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

These plants are typically small shrubs or herbs, characterized by their trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. They are often found in arid or semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Microsisymbrium species are known for their ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

This makes them ecologically important in soil fertility and restoration efforts.
