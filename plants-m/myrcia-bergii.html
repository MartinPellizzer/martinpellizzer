Myrcia bergii

Myrcia bergii, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various birds and animals. Its aromatic leaves and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Myrcia bergii is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
