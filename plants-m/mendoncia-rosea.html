Mendoncia rosea

Mendoncia rosea, commonly known as the pink lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink to lilac-colored petals that bloom in late spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces a mild, sweet fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
