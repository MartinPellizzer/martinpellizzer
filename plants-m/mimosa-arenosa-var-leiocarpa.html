Mimosa arenosa var. leiocarpa

Mimosa arenosa var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a shrub native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its pale green, fern-like leaves and small, yellow, spherical flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces cylindrical, woody pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

The silky mimosa is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
