Malva xizangensis

Malva xizangensis, commonly known as the Tibet mallow, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its alpine habitat.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
