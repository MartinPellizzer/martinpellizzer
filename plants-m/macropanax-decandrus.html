Macropanax decandrus

Macropanax decandrus, commonly known as the golden bamboo or golden lily, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 3 meters in height, forming dense clumps. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant touch to its foliage. Its leaves are long and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance.

Macropanax decandrus is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and unique floral display.
