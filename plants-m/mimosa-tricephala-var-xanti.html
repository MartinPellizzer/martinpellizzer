Mimosa tricephala var. xanti

Mimosa tricephala var. xanti, commonly known as the sensitive plant or mimic plant, is a species native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves that fold shut when touched, a response triggered by mechanical stimulation. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates. Its rapid growth and unique foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Additionally, it has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
