Malus montana var. diversifolia

Malus montana var. diversifolia, commonly known as the mountain crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a distinctive dark green upper surface and a lighter, sometimes hairy underside. The fruit is a small, yellowish pome that ripens in late summer.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its floral display.
