Myrcia pinifolia

Myrcia pinifolia, commonly known as the pineleaf myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen foliage. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and emit a strong, spicy scent when crushed.

The flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are borne in clusters, attracting various pollinators.
