Mapania immersa

Mapania immersa, commonly known as the water lily, is a aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom above the water surface. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that anchors it in shallow water. Its leaves are typically green and waxy, while its flowers can be white, pink, or yellow.

Mapania immersa plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to water organisms.
