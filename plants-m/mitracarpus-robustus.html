Mitracarpus robustus

Mitracarpus robustus, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by distinctive, spiny fruit capsules. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Mitracarpus robustus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
