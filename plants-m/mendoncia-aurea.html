Mendoncia aurea

Mendoncia aurea, commonly known as the golden mendoncia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and succulent, fleshy leaves. The plant thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or arid soils. Its botanical name reflects its golden hue, which is particularly prominent in its blooms.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
