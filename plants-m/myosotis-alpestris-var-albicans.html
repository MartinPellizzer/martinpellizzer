Myosotis alpestris var. albicans

Myosotis alpestris var. albicans, commonly known as the white forget-me-not, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its small, cup-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, which stand out against its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and mountainous areas. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
