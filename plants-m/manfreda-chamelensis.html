Manfreda chamelensis

Manfreda chamelensis, commonly known as the desert stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to store water and reduce transpiration. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
