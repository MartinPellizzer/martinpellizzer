Moraea bulbifera

Moraea bulbifera, commonly known as the bulbous moraea, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a range of colors, including red, yellow, and orange. The plant produces underground bulbs, which serve as storage organs and are crucial for its survival during dry periods. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from the base of the plant.

Moraea bulbifera is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries due to its vibrant blooms and unique appearance.
