Mimulus androsaceus

Mimulus androsaceus, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features small, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring and summer. The plant has hairy, oval-shaped leaves and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, and is often found in sandy or gravelly habitats.

Mimulus androsaceus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
