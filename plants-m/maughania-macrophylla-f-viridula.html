Maughania macrophylla f. viridula

Maughania macrophylla f. viridula, commonly known as the Emerald Beauty hibiscus, is a compact shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are a striking deep green, often with a waxy texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming from summer to fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes for its vibrant foliage and floral display.
