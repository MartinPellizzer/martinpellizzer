Mutisia viciifolia

Mutisia viciifolia, commonly known as the yellow mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a woody stem and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
