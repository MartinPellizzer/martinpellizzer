Megaclinium intermedium

Megaclinium intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle bearing small, two-lipped flowers.

Megaclinium intermedium is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in natural ecosystems.
