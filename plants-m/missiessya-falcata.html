Missiessya falcata

Missiessya falcata, commonly known as the Florida bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of bright red flowers that resemble bottle brushes. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in coastal dunes and wetlands, showcasing its adaptability to various environmental conditions.

The Florida bottlebrush plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, particularly pollinators like bees and butterflies.
