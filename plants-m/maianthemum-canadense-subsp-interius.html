Maianthemum canadense subsp. interius

Maianthemum canadense subsp.

interius, commonly known as the Canada mayflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America. It features rhizomatous growth and produces small, white to pale green flowers in late spring.

The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three ovate to lanceolate leaflets. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable component of native plant communities.
