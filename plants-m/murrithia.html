Murrithia

Murrithia, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family.

It consists of several species, most of which are native to southern Africa. The plants are characterized by their silver-gray foliage and showy, often purple or blue flowers. They typically grow as shrubs or small trees, and some species are cultivated for their ornamental value.

Murrithia species are generally drought-tolerant and thrive in well-drained soils.
