Mentha × maximilianea var. recedens

Mentha × maximilianea var. recedens, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mint, is a hybrid species within the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers. This plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is often found in woodland areas and along streambanks.

Its essential oils contain menthol and other compounds that contribute to its distinctive fragrance and medicinal properties.
