Marginaria gladiata

Marginaria gladiata, commonly known as the swordleaf margarita, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, sword-shaped leaves that give it its common name, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange in color. Marginaria gladiata thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
