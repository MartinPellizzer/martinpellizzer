Manihot quinqueloba

Manihot quinqueloba, commonly known as the five-lobed arrowleaf, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves with five lobes. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
