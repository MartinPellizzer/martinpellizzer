Mesembryanthemum digitatum subsp. digitatum

Mesembryanthemum digitatum subsp.

digitatum, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It features fleshy, cylindrical stems and produces small, yellow-orange fruits that resemble tiny tomatoes.

The plant is adapted to arid environments, with thick leaves that store water and reduce transpiration. Its flowers are typically pale yellow and bloom in early spring.

It is often cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value.
