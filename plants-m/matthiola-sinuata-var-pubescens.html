Matthiola sinuata var. pubescens

Matthiola sinuata var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. Its leaves are hairy, giving it the common name, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators.
