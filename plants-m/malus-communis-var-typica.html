Malus communis var. typica

Malus communis var. typica, commonly known as the common apple.

It is a species of the rose family, characterized by its deciduous nature and ornamental value. The plant features a rounded crown, with simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are typically white or pink, appearing in clusters, and it produces edible fruits that are widely cultivated for both culinary and commercial purposes.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to various climates and soil conditions.
