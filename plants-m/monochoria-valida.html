Monochoria valida

Monochoria valida, commonly known as the broadleaf water lettuce, is a aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its floating leaves, which are broad, spoon-shaped, and covered in a waxy layer. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale pink, emerging from the water surface. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the plant in water, while its leaves provide habitat for aquatic organisms.

Monochoria valida is often found in slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and marshes.
