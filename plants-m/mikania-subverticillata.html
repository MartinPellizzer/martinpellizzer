Mikania subverticillata

Mikania subverticillata, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and vine-like habit, often forming dense mats on the ground. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, with five petals. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

Mikania subverticillata is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to both shade and full sun.
