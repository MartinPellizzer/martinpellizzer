Myrcia variabilis var. intermedia

Myrcia variabilis var. intermedia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pink flowers, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a common landscape plant in regions with warm climates.
