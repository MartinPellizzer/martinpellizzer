Myagrum verrucosum

Myagrum verrucosum, commonly known as the rough-leaved myrtle, is a tropical evergreen tree native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its thick, pebbled bark and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its small, white flowers are followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in rainforest or woodland habitats.
