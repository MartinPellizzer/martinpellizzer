Milium coerulescens

Milium coerulescens, commonly known as blue millet, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often grown as an ornamental grass due to its delicate appearance. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.

Milium coerulescens is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
