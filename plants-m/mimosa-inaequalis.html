Mimosa inaequalis

Mimosa inaequalis, commonly known as the yellow mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that fold shut when touched, a trait known as thigmonastic response. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
