Mungos

Mungos, commonly known as the mung bean plant, is a leguminous species native to South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the genus Vigna and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain small, round seeds used in various cuisines. Mungos is often cultivated for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein and fiber.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
