Manihot inflexa

Manihot inflexa, commonly known as arrowleaf mallow, is a perennial herb native to the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the summer months. Its roots are tuberous and can be harvested for use in traditional medicine.

Manihot inflexa is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas.
