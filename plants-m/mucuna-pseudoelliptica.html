Mucuna pseudoelliptica

Mucuna pseudoelliptica, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, hairy pods that contain edible seeds. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities, which enrich the soil.

However, its seeds contain toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
