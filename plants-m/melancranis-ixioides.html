Melancranis ixioides

Melancranis ixioides, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and eastern United States.

It features showy, violet to purple flower heads that are surrounded by a series of bracts, giving it a coneflower appearance. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It produces a distinctive, spiny seed head that persists through the winter, providing food for birds and other wildlife.

Melancranis ixioides thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.
