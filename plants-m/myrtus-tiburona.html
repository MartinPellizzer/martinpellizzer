Myrtus tiburona

Myrtus tiburona, commonly known as the Turonian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, dark green leaves and small white flowers that bloom in spring. Its berries are typically red or black and are often used in traditional medicine.

Myrtus tiburona is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
