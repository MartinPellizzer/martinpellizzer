Machilus parabreviflorus

Machilus parabreviflorus, commonly known as the short-flowered laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, oval-shaped berries. Machilus parabreviflorus is often found in moist, shaded environments in forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
