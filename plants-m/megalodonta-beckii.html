Megalodonta beckii

Megalodonta beckii, commonly known as the giant toothed fern, is a unique plant characterized by its large, toothed fronds.

It belongs to the family Osmundaceae and is known for its ancient lineage, dating back to the Carboniferous period. The plant exhibits a robust rhizome system that supports its tall, upright growth form. Its fronds are typically bioluminescent, a rare trait among ferns, and emit a soft glow at night.

This bioluminescence is believed to aid in attracting nocturnal pollinators and deterring herbivores.
