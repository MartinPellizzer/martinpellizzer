Machaerium meridanum

Machaerium meridanum, commonly known as the silky machaerium, is a tropical plant belonging to the Leguminosae family.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several elliptical seeds.

Machaerium meridanum is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks in tropical regions of South America.
