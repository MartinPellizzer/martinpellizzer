Mauria membranifolia

Mauria membranifolia, commonly known as the parchment leaf plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the warmer months. Its growth habit is typically low and spreading, forming dense mats that can cover large areas.

It is well adapted to rocky soils and full sun exposure, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
