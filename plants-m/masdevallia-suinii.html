Masdevallia suinii

Masdevallia suinii, commonly known as the pig's nose orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated flowers that resemble a pig's nose, with a prominent, fleshy lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Masdevallia suinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its distinctive appearance.
