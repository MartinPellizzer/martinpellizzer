Mentha veroniciformis

Mentha veroniciformis, commonly known as woolly mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and square, erect stems. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in whorls along the stem. Mentha veroniciformis is often found in rocky or dry habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and mild, mint-like fragrance.
