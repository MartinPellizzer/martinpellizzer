Macrolobium imenoense

Macrolobium imenoense, commonly known as the Brazilian bean tree, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous pods and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant is notable for its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers that attract pollinators. Its fruit is a pod containing edible seeds that are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
