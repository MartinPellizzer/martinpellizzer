Miconia ulmarioides var. fendleri

Miconia ulmarioides var. fendleri, commonly known as the Mexican miconia, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often in moist, shaded environments.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain regions.
