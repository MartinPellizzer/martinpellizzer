Melosperma angustifolia

Melosperma angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf burrgrass, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands and prairie habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by distinctive, spiny fruits that aid in seed dispersal. Its leaves are narrow and alternate, with a rough texture that helps it withstand drought conditions.

Narrowleaf burrgrass plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
