Maesa indica var. coriacea

Maesa indica var. coriacea, commonly known as the rough maesa, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, leathery leaves that are typically grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
