Macrotyloma ciliatum

Macrotyloma ciliatum, commonly known as the hairy cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and small, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods containing edible seeds that are used in various cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often grown as a forage crop or for its nutritional value.

Macrotyloma ciliatum is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting agricultural practices.
