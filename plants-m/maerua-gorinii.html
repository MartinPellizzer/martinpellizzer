Maerua gorinii

Maerua gorinii, commonly known as the African saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, oblong leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a valuable plant for stabilizing degraded landscapes.

Its resilience to drought and high temperatures makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
