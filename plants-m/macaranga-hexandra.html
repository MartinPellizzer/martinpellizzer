Macaranga hexandra

Macaranga hexandra, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are known for their high water content and are used in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its fast growth and is sometimes planted for ornamental purposes.
