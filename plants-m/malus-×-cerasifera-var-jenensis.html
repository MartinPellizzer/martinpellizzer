Malus × cerasifera var. jenensis

Malus × cerasifera var. jenensis, commonly known as the Jena cherry, is a deciduous tree native to Central Europe.

It is a hybrid species within the genus Malus, characterized by its ornamental value and distinctive foliage. The leaves are typically dark green and turn vibrant red or orange in the fall, adding visual interest to the landscape. It produces small, edible fruits that are similar to cherries but are more commonly grown for their aesthetic appeal rather than for consumption.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to various soil conditions.
