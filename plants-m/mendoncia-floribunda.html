Mendoncia floribunda

Mendoncia floribunda, commonly known as the showy mendoncia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, large, and showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky or mountainous terrain. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

Mendoncia floribunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
