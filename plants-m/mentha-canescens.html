Mentha canescens

Mentha canescens, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and square, upright stems. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Mentha canescens is often found in rocky or dry soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

It is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
