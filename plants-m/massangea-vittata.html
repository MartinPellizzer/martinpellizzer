Massangea vittata

Massangea vittata, commonly known as the variegated arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers enclosed within a leaf-like structure called a spathe. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a starch source.

The leaves are typically arrow-shaped, hence its common name, and exhibit variegated patterns of green and white.
