Medinilla ternatensis

Medinilla ternatensis, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or velvet plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, leathery leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that resemble small tangerines. The plant produces small, round, yellow-orange fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade.

Medinilla ternatensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
