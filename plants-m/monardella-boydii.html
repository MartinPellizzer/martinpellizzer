Monardella boydii

Monardella boydii, commonly known as Boyd's monardella, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands. It is part of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and has a square stem with opposite leaves.

Boyd's monardella is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
