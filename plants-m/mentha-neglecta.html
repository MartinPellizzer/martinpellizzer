Mentha neglecta

Mentha neglecta, commonly known as wild mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a serrated edge. Its fragrant, pale purple flowers bloom in summer and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Mentha neglecta is often used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and cooling properties.
