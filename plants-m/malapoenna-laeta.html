Malapoenna laeta

Malapoenna laeta, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dazzling display during the warmer months. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in sunny, warm climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

Malapoenna laeta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and public spaces.
