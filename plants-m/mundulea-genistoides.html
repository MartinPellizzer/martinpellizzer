Mundulea genistoides

Mundulea genistoides, commonly known as the silver acacia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It has small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots and a low, spreading growth habit.

Its leaves are typically small and feathery, contributing to its drought-resistant characteristics.
