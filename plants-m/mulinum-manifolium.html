Mulinum manifolium

Mulinum manifolium, commonly known as the many-leaved mullein, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in elongated racemes. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

The leaves and flowers have been used historically for their medicinal properties, including soothing respiratory ailments.
