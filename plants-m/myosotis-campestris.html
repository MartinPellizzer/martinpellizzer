Myosotis campestris

Myosotis campestris, commonly known as the field forget-me-not, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates. The plant features slender stems and small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Myosotis campestris is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
