Myrcia arcensis

Myrcia arcensis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are often clustered together. Myrcia arcensis is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

It is valued for its aromatic foliage and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
