Malpighia tortolensis

Malpighia tortolensis, commonly known as the granadilla, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It produces bright red, fleshy fruits that are rich in vitamin C and have a tangy, sweet flavor. The plant belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, white flowers. Its fruit is often used in juices, desserts, and traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is well adapted to coastal and lowland environments.
