Morus caroliniana

Morus caroliniana, commonly known as the Carolina mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes other well-known species like the common mulberry. The tree is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically lobed and have a rough texture. It produces small, aggregate fruits that are green when unripe and turn dark purple when mature.

The plant is often found in moist, sandy soils and is valued for its ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity.
