Machaerium merrillii

Machaerium merrillii, commonly known as the golden beardgrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and showy, yellowish-orange inflorescences. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often found in open grasslands and disturbed soils, and it plays a role in soil stabilization due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
