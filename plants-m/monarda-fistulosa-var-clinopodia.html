Monarda fistulosa var. clinopodia

Monarda fistulosa var. clinopodia, commonly known as narrowleaf wild bergamot, is a perennial herb native to the eastern and central United States.

It features aromatic, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in open woods, meadows, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
