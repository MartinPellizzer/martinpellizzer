Medinilla verrucosa

Medinilla verrucosa, commonly known as the velvet medinilla, is a tropical flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial shrub, often found in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are showy and attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Medinilla verrucosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to cultivation.
