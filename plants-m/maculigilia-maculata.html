Maculigilia maculata

Maculigilia maculata, commonly known as the spotted bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers with distinctive spots, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture, contributing to its drought tolerance.

The species is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and unique floral appearance.
