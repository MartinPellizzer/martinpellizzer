Mitostigma mitophorum

Mitostigma mitophorum, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
