Myrteola vaccinioides var. carabaya

Myrteola vaccinioides var. carabaya, commonly known as the Carabaya myrtle, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a glossy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
