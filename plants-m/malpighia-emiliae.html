Malpighia emiliae

Malpighia emiliae, commonly known as Emilia's wax apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Malpighiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a tart, acidic flavor. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a glossy surface.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
