Meoschium villosum

Meoschium villosum, commonly known as the hairy meadow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, narrow leaves covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of grasses. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands, often growing in dense colonies.

Meoschium villosum is typically found in temperate regions and is valued for its role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
