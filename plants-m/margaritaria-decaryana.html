Margaritaria decaryana

Margaritaria decaryana, commonly known as the bead tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa, the Caribbean, and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, bead-like fruits that contain a single, hard-shelled seed. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or near water bodies.

The bead tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
