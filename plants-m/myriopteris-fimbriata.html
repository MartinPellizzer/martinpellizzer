Myriopteris fimbriata

Myriopteris fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, arching fronds that give it a feathery appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are typically green and have a soft, velvety texture, while the sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds.

This fern is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to add a touch of natural beauty to shaded gardens.
