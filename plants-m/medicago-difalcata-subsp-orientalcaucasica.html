Medicago difalcata subsp. orientalcaucasica

Medicago difalcata subsp.

orientalcaucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian clover, is a perennial legume native to the Caucasus region and parts of Turkey. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it valuable for soil enrichment.

Its subspecies status reflects its distinct morphology and geographic distribution compared to other Medicago species.
