Mikania campanulata

Mikania campanulata, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branching stems and produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a rough texture.

Mikania campanulata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
