Mimosa buceragenia

Mimosa buceragenia, commonly known as the golden mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow inflorescences. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. Its flowers bloom in spring and are often used in ornamental gardening.

The plant also has a unique ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
