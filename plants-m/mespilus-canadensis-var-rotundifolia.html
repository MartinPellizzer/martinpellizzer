Mespilus canadensis var. rotundifolia

Mespilus canadensis var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the roundleaf serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its elliptical to round leaves, which are typically dark green and have a smooth texture. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet berries in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open forests.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
