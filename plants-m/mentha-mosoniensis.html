Mentha mosoniensis

Mentha mosoniensis, commonly known as the Moson mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, minty aroma, while its flowers are small, tubular, and typically pink or purple.

Mentha mosoniensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
