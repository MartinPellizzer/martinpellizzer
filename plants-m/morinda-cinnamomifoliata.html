Morinda cinnamomifoliata

Morinda cinnamomifoliata, commonly known as the cinnamon-leaf morinda, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that resemble those of cinnamon trees, hence its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Morinda cinnamomifoliata is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
