Megalochlamys revoluta

Megalochlamys revoluta, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain flat, edible seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The velvet tamarind is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
