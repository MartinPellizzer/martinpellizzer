Miconia bangii

Miconia bangii, commonly known as the Bangi miconia, is a species of flowering plant in the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
