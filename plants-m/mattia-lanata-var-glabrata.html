Mattia lanata var. glabrata

Mattia lanata var. glabrata, commonly known as the silky hibiscus, is a variant of the species Mattia lanata, characterized by its soft, hairy stems and large, showy flowers.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a velvety texture.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various garden settings.
