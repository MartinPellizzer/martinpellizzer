Mentha salicina

Mentha salicina, commonly known as narrow-leaf peppermint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the upper stems. Its leaves are elongated, serrated, and emit a strong minty scent when crushed.

Mentha salicina is often cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy, medicine, and flavoring.
