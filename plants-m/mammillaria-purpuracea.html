Mammillaria purpuracea

Mammillaria purpuracea, commonly known as the purple mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense covering of white to purple spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a houseplant.
