Mandevilla bracteosa

Mandevilla bracteosa, commonly known as the bracted mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a vigorous climber with long, trailing stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and orange, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are heart-shaped and glossy, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant flowers.

Mandevilla bracteosa thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often used in ornamental gardening for its ornate appearance.
