Myrtekmania clementis

Myrtekmania clementis, commonly known as the Clementine Myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit, a small, fleshy berry, contains a single seed and is often used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
