Melpomene albicans

Melpomene albicans, commonly known as the white mallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical name reflects its historical association with the mythological figure Melpomene, the muse of tragedy.
