Masdevallia floribunda subsp. tuerckheimii

Masdevallia floribunda subsp.

tuerckheimii, commonly known as the Tuerckheim's orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America. It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, often fragrant flowers.

The plant typically produces multiple inflorescences bearing showy, brightly colored blooms in shades of pink, red, and white. Its leaves are usually lanceolate and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its beauty and adaptability to shaded, humid environments.
