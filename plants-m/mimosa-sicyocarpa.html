Mimosa sicyocarpa

Mimosa sicyocarpa, commonly known as the desert mimosa, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that are sensitive to touch, folding shut when disturbed. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring, followed by flattened, pod-like fruits. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or rocky soils, making it well adapted to harsh environments.

Mimosa sicyocarpa plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
