Mesembryanthemum moniliforme

Mesembryanthemum moniliforme, commonly known as the string of pearls plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, cylindrical leaves that are often used as decorative elements in gardens and indoor spaces. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
