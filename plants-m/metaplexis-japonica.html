Metaplexis japonica

Metaplexis japonica, commonly known as the Japanese ironweed, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Metaplexis japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
