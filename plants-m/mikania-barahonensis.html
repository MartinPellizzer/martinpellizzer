Mikania barahonensis

Mikania barahonensis, commonly known as the Barahonese morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a vine, with opposite, heart-shaped leaves and hairy stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some horticultural settings.
