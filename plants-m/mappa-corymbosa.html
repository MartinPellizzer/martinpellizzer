Mappa corymbosa

Mappa corymbosa, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of bright orange or red flowers that resemble bells, which bloom during the winter season. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly rough texture.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
