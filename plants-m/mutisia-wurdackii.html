Mutisia wurdackii

Mutisia wurdackii, commonly known as the Wurdack's mutisia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features a robust, shrub-like structure with hairy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
