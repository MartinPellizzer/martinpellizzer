Medicago glandulosa

Medicago glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular medick, is a low-growing perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces yellow flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed areas. It has small, inflated pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Medicago glandulosa is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
