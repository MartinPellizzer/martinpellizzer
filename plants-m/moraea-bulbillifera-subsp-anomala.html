Moraea bulbillifera subsp. anomala

Moraea bulbillifera subsp.

anomala, commonly known as the Cape bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple, which bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a short, sturdy stem.

The subspecies anomala is distinguished by its unique flowering pattern and smaller, more compact inflorescences compared to other subspecies of Moraea bulbillifera.
