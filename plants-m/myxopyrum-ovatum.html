Myxopyrum ovatum

Myxopyrum ovatum, commonly known as the common sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its triangular, grass-like stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, and it is often found in wet or marshy habitats. Myxopyrum ovatum is widely distributed across Europe, Asia, and North America.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
