Myrica × burbankii

Myrica × burbankii, commonly known as the Burbank wax myrtle, is a hybrid shrub native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its small, waxy leaves that are often blue-green in color, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Myrica × burbankii is also valued for its ability to tolerate coastal conditions and salt spray.
