Masdevallia yungasensis

Masdevallia yungasensis, commonly known as the Yungas masdevallia, is a rare and striking orchid native to the cloud forests of Bolivia and Peru.

It is characterized by its intricate, often colorful floral structures and its ability to thrive in high-altitude, humid environments. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its flowers, which can range from white to violet, are notable for their complex petal arrangements and specialized pollination mechanisms.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
