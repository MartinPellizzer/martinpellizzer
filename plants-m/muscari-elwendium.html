Muscari elwendium

Muscari elwendium, commonly known as the Persian grape hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features dense clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, round bulbs that multiply over time. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Muscari elwendium is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in gardens for its vibrant, fragrant blooms.
