Matricaria inodora var. agrestis

Matricaria inodora var. agrestis, commonly known as the meadow chamomile, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers and finely divided, aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It produces small, white to pale yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

The leaves and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their mild sedative and anti-inflammatory properties.
