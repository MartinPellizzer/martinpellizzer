Miconia acutifolia

Miconia acutifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or white miconia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped with a pointed tip. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a small, round, yellowish drupe that contains a single seed.

Miconia acutifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
