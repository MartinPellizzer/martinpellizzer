Myrcianthes ternifolia

Myrcianthes ternifolia, commonly known as the three-leaf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often aromatic and have a slightly waxy texture. Its small white or pale pink flowers are typically clustered in inflorescences, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are edible.

Myrcianthes ternifolia is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
