Macaranga sterrophylla

Macaranga sterrophylla, commonly known as the white-veined macaranga, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves with prominent white veins. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are small and clustered, often greenish-yellow in color, and it produces capsules as its fruit.

The leaves are often used in traditional practices and have a fibrous texture that makes them useful for various applications.
