Miconia canaimana

Miconia canaimana, commonly known as the "Brazilian star" or "false hibiscus," is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains numerous seeds.

Miconia canaimana is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and showy blooms.
