Miconia grisebachii var. reticulata

Miconia grisebachii var. reticulata, commonly known as the reticulate miconia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a large shrub or small tree characterized by its broad, palmate leaves with a distinctive reticulate venation pattern. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in inflorescences, followed by fruit that is typically a fleshy capsule. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
