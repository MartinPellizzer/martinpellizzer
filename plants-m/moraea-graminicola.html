Moraea graminicola

Moraea graminicola, commonly known as the grassland moraea, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and showy, often purple or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and has a compact, low-growing habit. It produces small, tubular flowers arranged in a raceme, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Moraea graminicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
