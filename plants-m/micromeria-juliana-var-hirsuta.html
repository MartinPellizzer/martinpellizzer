Micromeria juliana var. hirsuta

Micromeria juliana var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy micromeria, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, showy flowers and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance, and it produces small, yellowish-white blossoms in late spring.

The species is notable for its adaptability to harsh conditions and its potential ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
