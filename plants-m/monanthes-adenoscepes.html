Monanthes adenoscepes

Monanthes adenoscepes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pincushion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, spiny stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Monanthes adenoscepes is a perennial shrub that is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
