Mirbelia ilicifolia

Mirbelia ilicifolia, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive floral structure. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its seeds are encased in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind.

Mirbelia ilicifolia is often found in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, where it plays a role in stabilizing dunes and supporting local biodiversity.
