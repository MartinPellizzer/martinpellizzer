Myrcia atramentifera

Myrcia atramentifera, commonly known as black myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a spherical, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

The species is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
