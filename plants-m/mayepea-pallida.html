Mayepea pallida

Mayepea pallida, commonly known as the pale mayepea, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stem and small, pale green leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces clusters of delicate, yellowish-white flowers during the cooler months, which attract pollinators such as bees and moths. Its growth pattern is typically low and sprawling, allowing it to thrive in rocky or sandy soils.

Mayepea pallida is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
