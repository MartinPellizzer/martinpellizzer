Miconia proteoides

Miconia proteoides, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are fleshy berries that turn red when mature, containing numerous seeds.

Miconia proteoides is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
