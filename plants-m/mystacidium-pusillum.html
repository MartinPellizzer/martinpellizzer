Mystacidium pusillum

Mystacidium pusillum, commonly known as the dwarf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various coniferous trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for nutrients, while still performing some photosynthesis through its leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale green berries that are dispersed by birds. Its life cycle involves a complex relationship with its host, including the production of seeds that are carried by wind or animals.

Mystacidium pusillum is typically found in temperate regions and can form dense infestations on its host trees.
