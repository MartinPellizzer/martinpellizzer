Myoporum serratum var. gracile

Myoporum serratum var. gracile, commonly known as the narrow-leaf myoporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

It is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.
