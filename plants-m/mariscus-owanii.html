Mariscus owanii

Mariscus owanii, commonly known as the Owanis rockcress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with fleshy, succulent leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, typically clustered in racemes. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or arid environments.

Mariscus owanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
