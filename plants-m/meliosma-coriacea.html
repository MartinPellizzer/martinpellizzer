Meliosma coriacea

Meliosma coriacea, commonly known as the roughleaf meliosma, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are covered in a fine, fuzzy texture, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves remain evergreen throughout the year, providing year-round foliage.

Meliosma coriacea is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
