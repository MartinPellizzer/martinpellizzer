Myrtus velutina f. macrophylla

Myrtus velutina f. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved myrtle, is a variant of the myrtle plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are typically darker in color compared to the standard species. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for hedges and ornamental planting.
