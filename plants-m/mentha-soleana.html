Mentha soleana

Mentha soleana, commonly known as sweet basil, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, opposite leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Mentha soleana contains essential oils, particularly methyl eugenol, which contribute to its distinctive fragrance.

It is often cultivated for culinary use and as a decorative plant.
