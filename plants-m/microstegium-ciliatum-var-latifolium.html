Microstegium ciliatum var. latifolium

Microstegium ciliatum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its stems are often hairy, and it produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds.

The species is characterized by its delicate appearance and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
