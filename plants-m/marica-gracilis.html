Marica gracilis

Marica gracilis, commonly known as the slender marsh orchid, is a flowering plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, slender stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, adapting to its aquatic or semi-aquatic environment. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a single spike of flowers that bloom in late summer.

Marica gracilis plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of wetland ecosystems.
