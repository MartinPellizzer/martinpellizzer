Mammillaria saint-pieana

Mammillaria saint-pieana, commonly known as the Saint-Pierre's mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its globular shape and dense covering of white, woolly spines. The plant typically grows to a height of about 5 to 10 centimeters, with a compact and rounded form. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in the spring, and it is well adapted to arid and rocky environments.

This species is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
