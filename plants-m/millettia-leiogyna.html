Millettia leiogyna

Millettia leiogyna, commonly known as the smooth-leaved millettia, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pod fruits that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind or animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its adaptability, it can be found in a variety of ecological settings, including forests and shrublands.
