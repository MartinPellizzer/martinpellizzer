Myrcia neoregeliana

Myrcia neoregeliana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are a food source for various birds and mammals. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
