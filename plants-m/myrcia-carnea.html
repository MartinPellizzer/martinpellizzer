Myrcia carnea

Myrcia carnea, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its aromatic, red-purple flowers and dark, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when ripe. Myrcia carnea is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and along riverbanks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
