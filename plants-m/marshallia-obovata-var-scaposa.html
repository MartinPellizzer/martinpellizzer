Marshallia obovata var. scaposa

Marshallia obovata var. scaposa, commonly known as the scarlet gaura, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and prairies. Its leaves are broadly ovate and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is a perennial herb that produces numerous slender, ascending flowering stems.
