Melocactus gladiatus

Melocactus gladiatus, commonly known as the sword cactus, is a species native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that often resemble a sword, hence its common name. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

Melocactus gladiatus is a slow-growing plant that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil.
