Malephora purpureocrocea

Malephora purpureocrocea, commonly known as the purple crocus orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple petals and sepals, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, where it can thrive without direct sunlight. Its flowering period occurs during the late spring to early summer, producing delicate blooms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
