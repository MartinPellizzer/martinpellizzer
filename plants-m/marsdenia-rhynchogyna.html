Marsdenia rhynchogyna

Marsdenia rhynchogyna, commonly known as the narrow-fruit Marsdenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
