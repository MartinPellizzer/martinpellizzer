Mastixia laxa var. angustifolia

Mastixia laxa var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mastixia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, angular stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
