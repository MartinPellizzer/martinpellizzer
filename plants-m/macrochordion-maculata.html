Macrochordion maculata

Macrochordion maculata, commonly known as the spotted water lily, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, floating leaves that are often spotted with dark markings. The plant produces showy white flowers that bloom above the water surface. Its roots anchor it in shallow water, while its leaves provide habitat for small aquatic organisms.

Macrochordion maculata plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by supporting biodiversity and contributing to water quality.
