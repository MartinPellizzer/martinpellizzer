Maesa maxima

Maesa maxima, commonly known as the large-leaved maesa, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that can grow up to 20 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in its native regions.
