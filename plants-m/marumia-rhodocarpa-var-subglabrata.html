Marumia rhodocarpa var. subglabrata

Marumia rhodocarpa var. subglabrata, commonly known as the smooth-leaved marumia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by round, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
