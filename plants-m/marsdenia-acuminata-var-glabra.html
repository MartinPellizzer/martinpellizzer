Marsdenia acuminata var. glabra

Marsdenia acuminata var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth Marsdenia, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, and it is often found in forested areas. Its smooth, hairless stems and leaves give it its varietal name, "glabra," meaning smooth in Latin.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
