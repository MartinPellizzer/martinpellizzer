Miconia pallens

Miconia pallens, commonly known as the white miconia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows as an understory shrub or small tree, often found in humid, shaded forest environments. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens, though it can become invasive in certain areas.
