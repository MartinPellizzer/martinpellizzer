Muraltia rhynostigma

Muraltia rhynostigma, commonly known as the red muraltia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and floral display.

Muraltia rhynostigma is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
