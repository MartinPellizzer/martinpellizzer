Meconopsis oliveriana

Meconopsis oliveriana, commonly known as the Oliver's blue poppy, is a striking alpine plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in a dense, woolly layer of hairs.

Meconopsis oliveriana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with specific growing conditions.
