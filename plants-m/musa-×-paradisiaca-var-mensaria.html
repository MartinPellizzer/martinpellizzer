Musa × paradisiaca var. mensaria

Musa × paradisiaca var. mensaria, commonly known as the red banana, is a variety of the banana plant characterized by its striking red-purple peel.

This variety is a hybrid of the common banana (Musa × paradisiaca) and is native to Southeast Asia. The leaves are large, broad, and typically have a waxy texture, while the fruit is usually smaller and has a sweeter flavor compared to typical bananas. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant color and is also used in some culinary applications.

The plant thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight.
