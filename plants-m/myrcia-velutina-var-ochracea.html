Myrcia velutina var. ochracea

Myrcia velutina var. ochracea, commonly known as the ochraceous myrcia, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in undergrowth layers of dense tropical forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has limited use in traditional medicine.
