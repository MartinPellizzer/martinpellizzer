Myoporum humile

Myoporum humile, commonly known as the small-leaved myoporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It features small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its berries are blue-black and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Myoporum humile is adapted to coastal and dry environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.
