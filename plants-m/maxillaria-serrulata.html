Maxillaria serrulata

Maxillaria serrulata, commonly known as the serrated maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and its showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant produces linear, dark green leaves and has a delicate, branched inflorescence. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright but indirect light.

Maxillaria serrulata is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
