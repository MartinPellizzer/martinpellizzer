Macleania cordata

Macleania cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in wet, swampy areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are usually broad and have a velvety texture, while its stems are often covered in fine hairs.

Macleania cordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
