Macodes robusta

Macodes robusta, commonly known as the giant water lady, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers with red stamens, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
