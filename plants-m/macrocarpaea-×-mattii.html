Macrocarpaea × mattii

Macrocarpaea × mattii, commonly known as the hybrid sweet pitcher plant, is a unique carnivorous plant resulting from the cross between Macrocarpaea tetraloba and Macrocarpaea natalensis.

It is characterized by its elongated, tubular leaves that form pitfall traps to capture prey. The plant produces small, white flowers and thrives in moist, nutrient-poor environments. Its botanical features include a robust root system and a distinct leaf morphology adapted for trapping insects.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in nutrient cycling in its native habitats.
