Medicago tuberculata var. apiculata

Medicago tuberculata var. apiculata, commonly known as the prickly medick, is a low-growing perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces yellow flowers in summer. The plant has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion. It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in ecological restoration projects.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment.
