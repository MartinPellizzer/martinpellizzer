Miconia rubrisetulosa

Miconia rubrisetulosa, commonly known as the red-hair miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in dense, red-brown hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals. Miconia rubrisetulosa is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain regions, making it a notable species in ecological studies.

Its ornamental value has also led to its cultivation in some areas for landscaping purposes.
