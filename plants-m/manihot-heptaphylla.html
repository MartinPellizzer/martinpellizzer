Manihot heptaphylla

Manihot heptaphylla, commonly known as the African arrowroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its underground tubers, which are rich in starch. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating, while the flowers are small and inconspicuous.

The tubers are harvested for their high nutritional value and are used as a root vegetable in various cuisines.
