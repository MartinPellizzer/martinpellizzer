Menispermum heteroclitum

Menispermum heteroclitum, commonly known as the climbing arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking yellowish-green flowers that are typically borne in clusters. Its fruits are fleshy berries that turn red when mature, containing seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
