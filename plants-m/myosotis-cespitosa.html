Myosotis cespitosa

Myosotis cespitosa, commonly known as the clustered forget-me-not, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and clusters of blue or violet flowers. The flowers typically bloom in early summer and are arranged in tight, compact inflorescences. The plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

It is a member of the Boraginaceae family, known for its hairy stems and showy blue flowers.
