Maianthemum atropurpureum

Maianthemum atropurpureum, commonly known as the purple false shamrock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Convallariaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, dark green leaves. The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and subtle floral display.
