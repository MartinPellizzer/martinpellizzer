Malcolmia aegyptiaca

Malcolmia aegyptiaca, commonly known as Egyptian malcolmia, is an annual flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa.

It features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly waxy texture, and it is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
