Melaleuca leiopyxis

Melaleuca leiopyxis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paperbark, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its smooth, flaky bark that peels off in strips, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow and often have a silvery underside, while the flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

This species is adapted to salt-laden winds and is often found in areas with high humidity and moderate rainfall.
