Myrcia xylopioides

Myrcia xylopioides, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, reddish-brown fruits that are edible but have a peppery taste. It is often found in moist, tropical environments and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

Myrcia xylopioides is also noted for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
