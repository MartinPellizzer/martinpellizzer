Myrcianthes campomanesioides

Myrcianthes campomanesioides, commonly known as the Campomanesia myrtle, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to red when mature, often containing a single seed.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has potential uses in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
