Mimosa ophthalmocentra

Mimosa ophthalmocentra, commonly known as the pink mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is capable of nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
