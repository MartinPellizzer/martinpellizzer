Myriophyllum callitrichoides

Myriophyllum callitrichoides, commonly known as the water velvet, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in still or slow-moving waters and is often found in ponds, lakes, and wetlands.

Myriophyllum callitrichoides plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
