Mariscus scirpodeus

Mariscus scirpodeus, commonly known as the marsh sedge, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, forming dense clumps with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the sedge family.

Mariscus scirpodeus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
