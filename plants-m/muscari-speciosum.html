Muscari speciosum

Muscari speciosum, commonly known as the grape hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces dense clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, resembling small grapes. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that emerge in early spring, preceding the bloom.

Muscari speciosum is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to naturalize over time in suitable climates.
