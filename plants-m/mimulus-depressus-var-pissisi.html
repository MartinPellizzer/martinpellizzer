Mimulus depressus var. pissisi

Mimulus depressus var. pissisi, commonly known as the yellow monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its drooping, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils along stream banks and in open woodlands. It has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
