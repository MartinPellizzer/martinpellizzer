Myrciaria ibarrae

Myrciaria ibarrae, commonly known as the Ibarra's myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the cloud forests of southern Mexico.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature, and they are often used in local traditional medicine.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to high-altitude environments.
