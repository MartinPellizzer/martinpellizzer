Maesa truncata

Maesa truncata, commonly known as the African snowberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are edible for humans and birds. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Maesa truncata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
