Medicago persica

Medicago persica, commonly known as Persian clover, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has a shallow root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor conditions.

Medicago persica is also valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.
