Musa × sapientum var. grandis

Musa × sapientum var. grandis, commonly known as the banana plant, is a herbaceous perennial that belongs to the Musaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves and a clustered inflorescence that produces the edible fruit. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring warm temperatures and high humidity. Its roots are fibrous, and it spreads through rhizomes, forming a dense clump.

The fruit, which is botanically classified as a berry, contains numerous seeds and is rich in nutrients.
