Myrcianthes rubra

Myrcianthes rubra, commonly known as the red myrcia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible but have a strong, sometimes bitter flavor. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Myrcianthes rubra is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
