Melilotus sulcatus var. longiracemosus

Melilotus sulcatus var. longiracemosus, commonly known as the long raceme melilot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in elongated racemes, which are its most distinctive botanical feature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed sites.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
