Miconia pilgeriana

Miconia pilgeriana, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that are followed by distinctive, three-lobed fruits. Miconia pilgeriana is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a challenge for land managers.
