Mycaranthes gigantea

Mycaranthes gigantea, commonly known as the giant mycaranthes, is a striking member of the Apocynaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Native to certain regions of Africa, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
