Meistera verrucosa

Meistera verrucosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by round, spiky fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or near streams.

Meistera verrucosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
