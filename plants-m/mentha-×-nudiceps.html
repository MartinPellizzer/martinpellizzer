Mentha × nudiceps

Mentha × nudiceps, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Mentha spicata and Mentha viridis.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. Mentha × nudiceps is native to Europe and prefers moist, well-drained soils in partial shade.

It is valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in herbal teas and culinary applications.
