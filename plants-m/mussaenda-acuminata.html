Mussaenda acuminata

Mussaenda acuminata, commonly known as the yellow Mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of showy, yellowish-white flowers that are often clustered at the ends of the branches. Its flowers are characterized by their five to seven petals and are arranged in umbel-like inflorescences.

Mussaenda acuminata is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
