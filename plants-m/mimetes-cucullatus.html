Mimetes cucullatus

Mimetes cucullatus, commonly known as the Cape sugarloaf, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical shape and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate contrast to its rugged appearance. Its unique morphology allows it to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.

Mimetes cucullatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in drought conditions.
