Malus coronaria var. ioensis

Malus coronaria var. ioensis, commonly known as the Sierra cherry or mountain cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated edges. Its fruits are small, red drupes that are edible but often tart.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
