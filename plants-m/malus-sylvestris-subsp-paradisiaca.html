Malus sylvestris subsp. paradisiaca

Malus sylvestris subsp.

paradisiaca, commonly known as the wild apple or crab apple, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae. It is native to Europe and western Asia and is characterized by its small, often tart fruits.

The plant typically grows as a deciduous tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with serrated edges, while its flowers are white or pink and appear in clusters.

The fruit, which is a pome, varies in size and color, ranging from yellow to red, and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
