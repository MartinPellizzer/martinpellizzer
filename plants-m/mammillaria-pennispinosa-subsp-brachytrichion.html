Mammillaria pennispinosa subsp. brachytrichion

Mammillaria pennispinosa subsp.

brachytrichion, commonly known as the short-hairless mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to southern Mexico. It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and short, spiny stems.

The plant produces white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
