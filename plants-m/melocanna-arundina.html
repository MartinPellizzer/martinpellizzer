Melocanna arundina

Melocanna arundina, commonly known as the bamboo grass or giant bamboo, is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height, forming dense clumps. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically green and arranged in a fan-like pattern at the base of each stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Melocanna arundina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional practices in some cultures.
