Mikania trichophila

Mikania trichophila, commonly known as the silky Mikania, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is an annual or short-lived perennial, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open fields. Its rapid growth and prolific seed production make it an effective colonizer of new areas.

The silky texture of its foliage is due to the dense covering of fine, white hairs on its stems and leaves.
