Malus communis var. nigra

Malus communis var. nigra, commonly known as the black apple.

This variety is a cultivar of the common apple tree, characterized by its dark, almost black fruit skin. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth margin and a glossy green upper surface. It produces small, sweet fruits that are often used in culinary applications.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities as well as its edible yield.
