Mentha arvensis var. rotundifolia

Mentha arvensis var. rotundifolia, commonly known as roundleaf mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its rounded, velvety leaves and a strong, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in meadows and woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
