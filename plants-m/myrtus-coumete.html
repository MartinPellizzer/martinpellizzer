Myrtus coumete

Myrtus coumete, commonly known as the Canary Islands myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant features narrow, dark green leaves with a slight pubescence and produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring. Its fruit is a small, dark blue berry that contains several seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional medicine.
