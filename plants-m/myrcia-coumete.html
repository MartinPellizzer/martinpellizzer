Myrcia coumete

Myrcia coumete, commonly known as Brazilian myrtle, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. Its fragrant leaves and fruits are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

The botanical features of Myrcia coumete make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
