Miconia trichocaula

Miconia trichocaula, commonly known as the silky miconia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruits are small, three-lobed berries that turn red when mature.

Miconia trichocaula is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
