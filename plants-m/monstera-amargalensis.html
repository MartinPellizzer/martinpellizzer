Monstera amargalensis

Monstera amargalensis, commonly known as the Mexican giant spider-plant, is a tropical vine native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, deeply lobed leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Due to its unique foliage, it is a popular houseplant among enthusiasts.
