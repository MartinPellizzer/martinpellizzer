Miconia salicina

Miconia salicina, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a large shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its broad, velvety leaves. The leaves are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter green underneath, with a soft, almost fuzzy texture. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits.

This species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of tropical environments.
