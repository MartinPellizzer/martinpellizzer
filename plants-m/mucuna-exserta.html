Mucuna exserta

Mucuna exserta, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its striking violet to blue flowers and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces elongated, spiny pods that contain several elliptical seeds. Mucuna exserta is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

It is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it useful in agricultural systems.
