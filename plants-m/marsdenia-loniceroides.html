Marsdenia loniceroides

Marsdenia loniceroides, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine that produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by orange-red berries. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy, silver underside, giving it its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The berries are edible but can be toxic to some animals if consumed in large quantities.
