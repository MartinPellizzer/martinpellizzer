Mammillaria rischeri

Mammillaria rischeri, commonly known as the Rischer's bunny ear cactus, is a low-growing, spineless cactus native to Mexico.

It features a globular or cylindrical stem with numerous small, white to pale green tubercles that give it a distinctive, rounded appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.

Mammillaria rischeri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique shape and ease of care.
