Melica lobata

Melica lobata, commonly known as the broadleaf brome, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wet meadows, along stream banks, and in prairie habitats. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves with a distinctive blue-green color and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Melica lobata plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insect species.
