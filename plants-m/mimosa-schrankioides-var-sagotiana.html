Mimosa schrankioides var. sagotiana

Mimosa schrankioides var. sagotiana, commonly known as the Philippine mimosa, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with a compact growth habit and is often found in forest understories. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
