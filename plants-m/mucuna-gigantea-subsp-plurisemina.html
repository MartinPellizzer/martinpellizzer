Mucuna gigantea subsp. plurisemina

Mucuna gigantea subsp.

plurisemina, commonly known as the giant velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa. It is characterized by its large, trifoliate leaves and showy, purple to pink flowers.

The plant produces long, pod-like fruits containing multiple seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is known for its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing abilities.

The subspecies plurisemina is distinguished by its variable seed morphology and adaptability to diverse soil conditions.
