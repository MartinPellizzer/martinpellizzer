Millettia floribunda

Millettia floribunda, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces woody pods that contain several flat, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
