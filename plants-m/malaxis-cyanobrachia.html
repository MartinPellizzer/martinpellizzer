Malaxis cyanobrachia

Malaxis cyanobrachia, commonly known as the blue-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique blue-green veined leaves and small, delicate flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient uptake.

The botanical features of Malaxis cyanobrachia make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
