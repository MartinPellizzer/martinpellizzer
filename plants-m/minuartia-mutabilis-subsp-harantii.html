Minuartia mutabilis subsp. harantii

Minuartia mutabilis subsp.

harantii, commonly known as the variable rockcress, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant that thrives in rocky or gravelly habitats. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The flowers are usually pink or white, with four petals that are often adorned with a distinct purple or red stripe. This subspecies is notable for its ability to change color depending on environmental conditions, a trait that gives it its common name.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is often found in sunny, well-drained soils.
