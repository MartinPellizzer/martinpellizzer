Mentha × pluriglobula

Mentha × pluriglobula, commonly known as the globeflower mint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Mentha houdéana and Mentha rotundifolia.

It is characterized by its compact, globe-shaped inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Mentha × pluriglobula is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and aromatic foliage.
