Musa troglodytarum var. acutibracteata

Musa troglodytarum var. acutibracteata, commonly known as the narrow bract banana, is a wild species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces edible berries that are typically consumed raw or cooked. It is often found in tropical rainforests and is adapted to humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for cultivation, it has gained interest in horticultural circles.
