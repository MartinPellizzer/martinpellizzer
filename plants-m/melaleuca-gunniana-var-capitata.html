Melaleuca gunniana var. capitata

Melaleuca gunniana var. capitata, commonly known as the Narrow-leaved Tea-tree, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to coastal regions of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that appear in winter and spring. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal dunes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize sandy soils.
