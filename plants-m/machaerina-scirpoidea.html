Machaerina scirpoidea

Machaerina scirpoidea, commonly known as the New Zealand swamp reed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of lakes and rivers. The plant has long, narrow leaves that form dense stands, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in aquatic habitats.

Machaerina scirpoidea plays an important ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
