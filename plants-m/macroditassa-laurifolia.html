Macroditassa laurifolia

Macroditassa laurifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved macroditassa, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and indoor cultivation.
