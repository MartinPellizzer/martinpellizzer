Myrtus goetheana

Myrtus goetheana, commonly known as Goethe's myrtle, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fragrant foliage and ornamental value make it popular in horticulture.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in Mediterranean climates.
