Mutisia campanulata

Mutisia campanulata, commonly known as the bluebell tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue to violet flowers that resemble bells. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
