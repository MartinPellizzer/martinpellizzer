Maxillaria pseudoreichenheimiana

Maxillaria pseudoreichenheimiana, commonly known as the Royal Maxillaria, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers that come in a range of colors including pink, purple, and white. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its pseudobulbs are cylindrical, and the leaves are long and strap-like, contributing to its overall lush appearance.

It is appreciated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
