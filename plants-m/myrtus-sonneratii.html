Myrtus sonneratii

Myrtus sonneratii, commonly known as the Sonnerat's myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. The plant produces small, urn-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Myrtus sonneratii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal remedies.
