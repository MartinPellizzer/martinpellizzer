Mussaenda zollingeriana

Mussaenda zollingeriana, commonly known as the Zollinger's Mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

The flowers are primarily pollinated by insects, making it an attractive addition to gardens and natural habitats.
