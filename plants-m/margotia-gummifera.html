Margotia gummifera

Margotia gummifera, commonly known as the sticky tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces a sticky, resinous sap that can be used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural gardens.
