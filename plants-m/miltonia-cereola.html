Miltonia cereola

Miltonia cereola, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with a unique pouch-like structure. The plant produces vibrant pink to purple blooms, often with a white or cream inner lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Miltonia cereola is a popular orchid species among horticulturists due to its ornamental value and relatively easy cultivation.
