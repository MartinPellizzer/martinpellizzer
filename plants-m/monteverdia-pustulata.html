Monteverdia pustulata

Monteverdia pustulata, commonly known as the sticky monteverdia, is a flowering plant native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in a sticky substance, which may serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
