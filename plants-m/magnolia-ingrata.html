Magnolia ingrata

Magnolia ingrata, commonly known as the Chinese magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

The bark is smooth and light gray, often peeling in thin layers.
