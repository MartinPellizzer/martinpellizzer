Melanthium caeruleum

Melanthium caeruleum, commonly known as blue false indigo, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Melanthium caeruleum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.
