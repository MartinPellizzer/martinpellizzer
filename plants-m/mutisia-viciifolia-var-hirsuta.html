Mutisia viciifolia var. hirsuta

Mutisia viciifolia var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy mutisia, is a perennial shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy, ovate leaves with a rough texture and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in grasslands and coastal areas.

Its hirsute (hairy) appearance is a distinguishing feature that aids in its identification.
