Mertensia stylosa var. angustifolia

Mertensia stylosa var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf bluebells, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, sky-blue flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate foliage and showy blooms, making it a desirable addition to woodland gardens.
