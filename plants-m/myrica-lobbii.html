Myrica lobbii

Myrica lobbii, commonly known as the African wax tree, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and clusters of small, waxy, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and mammals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or rocky areas.

Myrica lobbii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
