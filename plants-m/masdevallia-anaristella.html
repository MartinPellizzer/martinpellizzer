Masdevallia anaristella

Masdevallia anaristella, commonly known as the Andean orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with elongated, strap-like leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. The plant typically grows in cool, humid environments at high altitudes. Its flowers are often clustered in a raceme, and the species is noted for its ornamental value.

Masdevallia anaristella is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
