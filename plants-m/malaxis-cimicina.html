Malaxis cimicina

Malaxis cimicina, commonly known as the Cimicin orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are linear and often clustered at the base of the stem.

Malaxis cimicina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
