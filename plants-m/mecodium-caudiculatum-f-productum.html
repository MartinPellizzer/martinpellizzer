Mecodium caudiculatum f. productum

Mecodium caudiculatum f. productum, commonly known as the hairy mecodium, is a unique succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, woody stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, pale green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. Its flowering stems often bear numerous small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow.

This variety is prized for its unusual appearance and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
