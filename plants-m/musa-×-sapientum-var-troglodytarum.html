Musa × sapientum var. troglodytarum

Musa × sapientum var. troglodytarum, commonly known as the cave banana, is a rare and lesser-known variety of banana.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated fruit that often grows in a clustered formation. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical climates with high humidity. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Due to its limited cultivation and habitat, it is considered a rare and intriguing species within the Musa genus.
