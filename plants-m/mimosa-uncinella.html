Mimosa uncinella

Mimosa uncinella, commonly known as the hooked mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, hooked pods that contain seeds, a feature that aids in its dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
