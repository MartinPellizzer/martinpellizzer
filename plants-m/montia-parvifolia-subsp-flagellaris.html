Montia parvifolia subsp. flagellaris

Montia parvifolia subsp.

flagellaris, commonly known as the flagellate commonness, is a small annual herb native to western North America. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

The plant has slender, branching stems that often trail along the ground, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and hairy. Its small white flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in early spring.

The species is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in disturbed or moist soils.
