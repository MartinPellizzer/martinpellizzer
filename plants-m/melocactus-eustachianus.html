Melocactus eustachianus

Melocactus eustachianus, commonly known as the Eustachian melon, is a cactus species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces striking, orange to red flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its spines are short and dense, providing protection from herbivores.

Melocactus eustachianus is well adapted to harsh desert environments, storing water in its thick stem for survival.
