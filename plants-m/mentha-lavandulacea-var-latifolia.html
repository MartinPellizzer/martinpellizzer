Mentha lavandulacea var. latifolia

Mentha lavandulacea var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf lavender mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and culinary use.

This variety is valued for its fragrance and is often used in herbal teas and aromatherapy.
