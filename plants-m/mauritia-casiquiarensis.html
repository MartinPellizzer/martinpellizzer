Mauritia casiquiarensis

Mauritia casiquiarensis, commonly known as the Casiquiare palm, is a unique species native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, coconut-like fruits. Its root system is extensive, helping to stabilize the soil in swampy and flood-prone areas.

This palm is also notable for its ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions, making it an important component of the region's wetland ecosystems.
