Moldenhawera brasiliensis

Moldenhawera brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in rainforests.

Moldenhawera brasiliensis is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
