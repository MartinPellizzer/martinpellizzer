Malva chilensis

Malva chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean mallows, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. Its leaves are typically broad and palmately lobed, with a soft, velvety texture.

Malva chilensis is often found in moist, temperate environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
