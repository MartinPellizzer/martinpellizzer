Mimulus longulus

Mimulus longulus, commonly known as the long-whorled monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive red throat. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem.

Mimulus longulus is an important nectar source for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
