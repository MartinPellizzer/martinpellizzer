Macrolenes floccosa

Macrolenes floccosa, commonly known as the cloud-like fern, is a unique plant characterized by its soft, feathery fronds that resemble clouds.

It is native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in shaded, moist environments. The plant's leaves are composed of numerous tiny, overlapping pinnae, giving it a fluffy appearance. It reproduces via spores, which are released from specialized structures called sporangia located on the underside of its fronds.

Due to its delicate structure, it requires careful handling and is often cultivated in greenhouses to maintain optimal growing conditions.
