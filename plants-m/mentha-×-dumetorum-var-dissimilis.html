Mentha × dumetorum var. dissimilis

Mentha × dumetorum var. dissimilis, commonly known as the "skunk mint," is a hybrid plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae) characterized by its aromatic leaves and creeping growth habit.

It is native to North America and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or white, and it is known for its strong, pungent scent, reminiscent of skunk odor. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies, though it is less commonly cultivated than other mint species.

The variety dissimilis is distinguished by its unique chemical composition, which contributes to its distinctive fragrance and potential medicinal properties.
