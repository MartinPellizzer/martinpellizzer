Mentha arvensis f. gentilis

Mentha arvensis f. gentilis, commonly known as wild mint, is a subspecies of the mint plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple. The plant is perennial, often growing in moist soils along stream banks and in shaded areas. It has a strong, aromatic scent due to the presence of menthol and other essential oils.

This subspecies is widely used in herbal medicine and as a culinary herb.
