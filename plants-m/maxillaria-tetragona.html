Maxillaria tetragona

Maxillaria tetragona, commonly known as the four-lobed orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically produces pseudobulbs with broad, elliptical leaves that are dark green and velvety in texture. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color, with a distinctive four-lobed lip that gives it its common name.

Maxillaria tetragona is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
