Medicago foenugraeca

Medicago foenugraeca, commonly known as goat's rue, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is native to southern Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in dry, rocky soils. It has been used historically in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Medicago foenugraeca is also valued for its role in soil nitrogen fixation, making it a useful plant in ecological restoration projects.
