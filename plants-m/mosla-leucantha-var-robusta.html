Mosla leucantha var. robusta

Mosla leucantha var. robusta, commonly known as Chinese chervil, is a perennial herb native to China.

It features slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are often used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.
