Megastachya oblonga

Megastachya oblonga, commonly known as the broadleaf false foxglove, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the gentian family (Gentianaceae) and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet and features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for wetland restoration projects and ornamental gardening.
