Masdevallia floribunda

Masdevallia floribunda, commonly known as the orchid with the pink and white flowers, is a terrestrial orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, showy blooms that often resemble miniature orchids. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to thrive. Its rhizomes are short and produce a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves.

Masdevallia floribunda is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
