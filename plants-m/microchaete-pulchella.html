Microchaete pulchella

Microchaete pulchella, commonly known as the slender feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It features slender, wiry stems and delicate, feathery leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in loose panicles, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Microchaete pulchella is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
