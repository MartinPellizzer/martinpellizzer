Machaerium capote

Machaerium capote, commonly known as the pink machaerium, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, delicate leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink or purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Machaerium capote is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
