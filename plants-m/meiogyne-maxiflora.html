Meiogyne maxiflora

Meiogyne maxiflora, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, floating leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic life. The plant produces showy white or pink flowers that bloom during the day, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it in shallow waters and absorbing nutrients from the sediment.

Meiogyne maxiflora plays an important role in maintaining water quality and supporting biodiversity in its native wetland ecosystems.
