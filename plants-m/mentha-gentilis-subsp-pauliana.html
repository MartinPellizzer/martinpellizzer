Mentha gentilis subsp. pauliana

Mentha gentilis subsp.

pauliana, commonly known as the Corsican mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and flowering spikes.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their cooling and digestive properties.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly smaller leaves and slightly different floral structure compared to the main species, Mentha gentilis.
