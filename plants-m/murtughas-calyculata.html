Murtughas calyculata

Murtughas calyculata, commonly known as the white-flowered pomegranate, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round fruits with a thick, leathery rind and a juicy, seed-filled interior. It is adapted to dry climates and can thrive in well-drained soils.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
