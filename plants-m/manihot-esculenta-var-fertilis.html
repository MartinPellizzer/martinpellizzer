Manihot esculenta var. fertilis

Manihot esculenta var. fertilis, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a tropical plant native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients. It is cultivated for both its seeds and its starchy roots, which are used in various traditional dishes.
