Myroxylon cinereum

Myroxylon cinereum, commonly known as the wild rosewood, is a species of tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its rich color and fine grain. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by small, oval-shaped fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

The tree is also known for its slow growth and long lifespan, making it a significant species in tropical ecosystems.
