Mentha josephi

Mentha josephi, commonly known as the Mexican mint or Mexican spearmint, is a perennial herb native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Mentha josephi is known for its aromatic, cool menthol scent and is often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
