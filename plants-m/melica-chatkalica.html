Melica chatkalica

Melica chatkalica, commonly known as the Chatkal milletgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky, or mountainous regions and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves, with small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are usually greenish or pale blue, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Melica chatkalica is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
