Musa japonica

Musa japonica, commonly known as the Japanese banana, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and clustered, elongated fruit. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are usually yellow or green and are followed by fruit that can be either edible or ornamental.

The plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
