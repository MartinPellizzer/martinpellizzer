Moskerion

Moskerion, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, waxy leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils. Moskerion has a taproot system and is adapted to thrive in temperate climates.

Its flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
