Mentha × sativa subsp. latifolia

Mentha × sativa subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as broad-leaf mint, is a hybrid plant species within the mint family, Lamiaceae. It is characterized by its broad, serrated leaves and strong, aromatic scent, which is primarily due to the presence of menthol and other essential oils.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its medicinal and culinary uses, as well as its role in traditional herbal remedies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is commonly used in landscaping and garden design.
