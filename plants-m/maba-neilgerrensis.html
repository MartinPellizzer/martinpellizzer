Maba neilgerrensis

Maba neilgerrensis, commonly known as the Nilgiri mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its thick, woody stems and broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the monsoon season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant has a fibrous root system and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.
