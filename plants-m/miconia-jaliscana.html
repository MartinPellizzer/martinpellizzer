Miconia jaliscana

Miconia jaliscana, commonly known as the Jalisco miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
