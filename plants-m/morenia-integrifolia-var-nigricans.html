Morenia integrifolia var. nigricans

Morenia integrifolia var. nigricans, commonly known as the Blackleaf Morelia, is a species native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple or black on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by bright red or orange berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal environments.

The variety is notable for its striking foliage and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
