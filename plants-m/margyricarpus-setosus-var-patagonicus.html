Margyricarpus setosus var. patagonicus

Margyricarpus setosus var. patagonicus, commonly known as the Patagonian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features spiny, lobed leaves and produces purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in grasslands and shrublands.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
