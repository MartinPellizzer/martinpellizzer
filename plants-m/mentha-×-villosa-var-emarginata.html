Mentha × villosa var. emarginata

Mentha × villosa var. emarginata, commonly known as the woolly mint or emarginate mint, is a hybrid plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its fuzzy, hairy leaves and square stems typical of the mint genus. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the upper stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine due to its aromatic properties.
