Maranta glabra

Maranta glabra, commonly known as the velvetleaf arrowroot, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that often display shades of silver and green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, followed by red or orange berries. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a source of starchy roots.

Maranta glabra thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
