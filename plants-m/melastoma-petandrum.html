Melastoma petandrum

Melastoma petandrum, commonly known as the purple mangosteen, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforests and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a glossy texture.

The fruit, which is edible, is encased in a thick, segmented rind and contains several segments filled with aromatic, juicy pulp.
