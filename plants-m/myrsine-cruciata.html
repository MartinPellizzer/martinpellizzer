Myrsine cruciata

Myrsine cruciata, commonly known as the crucifix myrsine, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features opposite, simple, and usually elliptic leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, often with four or five petals, and it produces small, fleshy berries as fruit.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and stream banks.
