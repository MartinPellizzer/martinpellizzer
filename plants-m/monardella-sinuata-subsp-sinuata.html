Monardella sinuata subsp. sinuata

Monardella sinuata subsp.

sinuata, commonly known as narrowleaf mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It features upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a serrated margin.

The plant produces showy, tubular blue to purple flowers in clustered inflorescences, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and open woodlands.

Its aromatic leaves and showy blooms make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
