Maclura tinctoria var. affinis

Maclura tinctoria var. affinis, commonly known as the yellow wood or yellow archangel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and pinnately compound leaves that are typically yellow-green in color. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by orange to red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in dye production.
