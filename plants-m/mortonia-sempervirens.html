Mortonia sempervirens

Mortonia sempervirens, commonly known as the evergreen woodbine, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial vine that produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is known for its strong, often woody stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Mortonia sempervirens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
