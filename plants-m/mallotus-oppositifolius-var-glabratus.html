Mallotus oppositifolius var. glabratus

Mallotus oppositifolius var. glabratus, commonly known as the smooth-leaved mallot, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in lowland tropical forests. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are smooth and glossy. Its flowers are small and clustered in panicles, often with yellowish or greenish petals.

The fruit is a spherical drupe, typically yellow when mature, and contains a single seed.
