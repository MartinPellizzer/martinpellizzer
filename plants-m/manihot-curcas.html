Manihot curcas

Manihot curcas, commonly known as cassava, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is cultivated for its starchy root, which is a staple food in many parts of the world. The plant has a woody stem and produces green, spiny capsules that contain toxic seeds. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often used as a vegetable in various cuisines.

Cassava is also valued for its ability to grow in poor soils and in tropical climates.
