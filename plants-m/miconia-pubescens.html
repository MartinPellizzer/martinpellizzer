Miconia pubescens

Miconia pubescens, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that are often followed by round, woody fruits containing numerous seeds. Miconia pubescens is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive in certain regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of tropical flora and ecological dynamics.
