Meliosma obtusa

Meliosma obtusa, commonly known as the broadleaf meliosma, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and related trees. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in clusters, and it produces small, dry fruits.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
