Mimulus primuloides subsp. linearifolius

Mimulus primuloides subsp.

linearifolius, commonly known as the linear-leaved monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and showy, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as streambanks and meadows. It is a herbaceous perennial with a low-growing habit, often forming dense mats.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance in its native range.
