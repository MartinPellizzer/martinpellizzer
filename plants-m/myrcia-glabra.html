Myrcia glabra

Myrcia glabra, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible but often have a spicy or peppery taste. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Myrcia glabra is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
