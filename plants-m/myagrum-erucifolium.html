Myagrum erucifolium

Myagrum erucifolium, commonly known as the broad-leafed myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Myagrum erucifolium is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in Indigenous Australian medicine.
