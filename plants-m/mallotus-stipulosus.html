Mallotus stipulosus

Mallotus stipulosus, commonly known as the hairy sycamore, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its bark is thick and fissured, often exuding a milky sap when damaged.

Mallotus stipulosus is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments.
