Mikania artemisioides

Mikania artemisioides, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and sprawling habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the early morning. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Mikania artemisioides is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and open fields, and can become invasive in certain regions.
