Mesechinopsis pelecyrhachis

Mesechinopsis pelecyrhachis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved star grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale blue flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is occasionally cultivated in arid regions.
