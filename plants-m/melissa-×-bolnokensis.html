Melissa × bolnokensis

Melissa × bolnokensis, commonly known as lemon balm hybrid, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Melissa officinalis (common lemon balm) and Melissa moschata (apple mint).

It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its aromatic, slightly lemon-scented leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with slender stems and opposite, oval-shaped leaves. It produces small, pale yellow flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Melissa × bolnokensis is often cultivated for its calming properties and use in herbal teas.
