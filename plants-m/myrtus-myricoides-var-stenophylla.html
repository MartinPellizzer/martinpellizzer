Myrtus myricoides var. stenophylla

Myrtus myricoides var. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white wax. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and rocky garden environments.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a valued plant in horticulture.
