Medicago psilocarpa

Medicago psilocarpa, commonly known as the purple vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, purple to pinkish flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its seeds are encased in a hard pod, and it has a deep root system that helps it thrive in poor soil conditions.

It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment.
