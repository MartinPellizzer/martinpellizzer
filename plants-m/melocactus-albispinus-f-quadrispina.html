Melocactus albispinus f. quadrispina

Melocactus albispinus f. quadrispina, commonly known as the four-spined white-spined melocactus, is a rare and striking cactus native to the dry regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem covered in white, woolly spines, which provide insulation and protection from herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a vibrant contrast to its otherwise muted appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts who appreciate its ornamental value.
