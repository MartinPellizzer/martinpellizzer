Medinilla quadrialata

Medinilla quadrialata, commonly known as the four-winged medinilla, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or red. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of clusters of small, tubular flowers surrounded by fleshy, petal-like bracts.

Medinilla quadrialata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
