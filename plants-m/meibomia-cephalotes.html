Meibomia cephalotes

Meibomia cephalotes, commonly known as the helmet orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, helmet-shaped inflorescence. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforests, thriving in humid and shaded environments. Its flowers are usually brightly colored, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
