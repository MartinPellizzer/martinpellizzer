Malveopsis racemosa

Malveopsis racemosa, commonly known as the clustered mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to red flowers that appear in racemes. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the plant thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
