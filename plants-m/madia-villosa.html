Madia villosa

Madia villosa, commonly known as the woolly madia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, woolly appearance.

Madia villosa is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
