Melhania andrahomanensis

Melhania andrahomanensis, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and vibrant colors. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within the rainforests of Madagascar. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces intricate, fragrant flowers that bloom during the rainy season.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered a conservation concern.
