Melica muehlenbergiana

Melica muehlenbergiana, commonly known as Muehlenberg's brome, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, woodlands, and stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. Its flowering period usually occurs in late spring to early summer.

Melica muehlenbergiana is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and wildlife.
