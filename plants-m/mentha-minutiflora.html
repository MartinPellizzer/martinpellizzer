Mentha minutiflora

Mentha minutiflora, commonly known as the narrow-flower mint, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a serrated margin and a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Mentha minutiflora is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is valued for its medicinal and culinary uses.
