Melocactus neryi

Melocactus neryi, commonly known as the Nery's melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America, particularly in Brazil.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, green stem and the presence of spines that provide protection against herbivores. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the dry season, which are typically short-lived but attract pollinators such as bees and moths. Its succulent structure allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to its harsh environment.

Melocactus neryi is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
