Mammillaria tomentosa var. flava

Mammillaria tomentosa var. flava, commonly known as the yellow-tipped hedgehog cactus, is a compact, spineless cactus native to Mexico.

It features a spherical or slightly elongated stem covered in dense, white to greyish woolly hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its slow growth rate makes it ideal for small spaces and rock gardens.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
