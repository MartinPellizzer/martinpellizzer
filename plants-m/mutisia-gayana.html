Mutisia gayana

Mutisia gayana, commonly known as the yellow mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and shrublands. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Mutisia gayana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
