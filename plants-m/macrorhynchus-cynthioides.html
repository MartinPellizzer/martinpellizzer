Macrorhynchus cynthioides

Macrorhynchus cynthioides, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a compact, bushy growth habit. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
