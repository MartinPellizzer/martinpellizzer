Marica sabiniana

Marica sabiniana, commonly known as the mountain sage, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or mountainous regions. Its leaves are used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported calming properties.

Marica sabiniana plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its native habitat.
