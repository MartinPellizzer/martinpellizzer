Miscanthus sacchariflorus var. breviberbis

Miscanthus sacchariflorus var. breviberbis, commonly known as the narrowleaf Chinese silver grass, is a perennial grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers in late summer, which are often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for landscape design.

Due to its ornamental value and potential as a bioenergy crop, it is cultivated in various regions for both aesthetic and agricultural purposes.
