Manihot violacea var. arcuata

Manihot violacea var. arcuata, commonly known as the purple arrowleaf, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple or red hues.

Native to South America, particularly Brazil, it thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that give way to yellowish or reddish fruits containing edible seeds. Its foliage is not only visually appealing but also contains compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

However, it is important to note that all parts of the plant contain toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
