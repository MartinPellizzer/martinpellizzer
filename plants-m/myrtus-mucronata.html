Myrtus mucronata

Myrtus mucronata, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark blue berries. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and emit a subtle, pleasant scent.

Myrtus mucronata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
