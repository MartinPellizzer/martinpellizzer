Moraea spathulata subsp. autumnalis

Moraea spathulata subsp.

autumnalis, commonly known as the autumn moraea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, showy inflorescences of pink to purple flowers.

The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, making it a notable addition to gardens during this season. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique flowering period, it is cultivated in various regions with a Mediterranean climate.
