Melocactus harlowii

Melocactus harlowii, commonly known as the Harlow's melon cactus, is a compact, globular cactus native to the dry forests of central Mexico.

It features a ribbed, spiny stem with a distinctive, swollen base that resembles a melon, giving it its common name. The plant produces pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are often followed by small, red fruits. Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a popular choice for collectors and cactus enthusiasts.

Melocactus harlowii thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.
