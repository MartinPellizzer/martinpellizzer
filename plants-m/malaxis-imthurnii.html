Malaxis imthurnii

Malaxis imthurnii, commonly known as the Imthurn's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests or rocky areas. Its rhizomes are fibrous, and it produces small, greenish or yellowish flowers with intricate petal structures.

Malaxis imthurnii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
