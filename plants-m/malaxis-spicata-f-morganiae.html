Malaxis spicata f. morganiae

Malaxis spicata f. morganiae, commonly known as the Morgan's Malaxis, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, pendulous inflorescences that bear clusters of small, greenish-white flowers with a subtle fragrance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, anchoring itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces a unique, spathaceous bract that encloses the floral cluster.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicately endangered species in its native range.
