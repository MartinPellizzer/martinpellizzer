Mimosa aguapeia

Mimosa aguapeia, commonly known as the Brazilian sensitive plant, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Mimosa genus within the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold when touched. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are often clustered in spherical or cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its unique leaf movements and rapid response to stimuli make it a fascinating subject in botanical studies.
