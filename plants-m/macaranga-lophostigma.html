Macaranga lophostigma

Macaranga lophostigma, commonly known as the white-veined macaranga, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, including India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent white veins, which give it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its fast growth and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
