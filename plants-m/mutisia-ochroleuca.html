Mutisia ochroleuca

Mutisia ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow-leaved mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in countries like Argentina, Bolivia, and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands, and it thrives in regions with a Mediterranean climate. Its yellow flowers are attractive to pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The leaves are usually leathery and have a distinct yellowish-green color, which helps in identifying the species.
