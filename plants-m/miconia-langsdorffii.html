Miconia langsdorffii

Miconia langsdorffii, commonly known as the Brazilian glorybower, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that are highly attractive to pollinators. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant in some regions.
