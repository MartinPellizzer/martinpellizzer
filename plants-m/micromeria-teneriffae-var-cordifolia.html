Micromeria teneriffae var. cordifolia

Micromeria teneriffae var. cordifolia, commonly known as the Canary Island thyme, is a perennial shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the dry, sunny conditions of its native habitat. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

This variety is distinguished by its heart-shaped leaves, which give it its botanical name.
