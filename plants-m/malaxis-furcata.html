Malaxis furcata

Malaxis furcata, commonly known as the forked malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a dense spike, which are typically borne on the upper part of the stem. It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories.

Malaxis furcata is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in humid tropical regions.
