Macaranga carrii var. myolensis

Macaranga carrii var. myolensis, commonly known as the Myolensis variant of the Macaranga tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with prominent veins and a waxy surface. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This variant is often found in lowland and hill forests, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
