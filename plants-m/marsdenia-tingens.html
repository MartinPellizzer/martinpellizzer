Marsdenia tingens

Marsdenia tingens, commonly known as the red-veined ivy, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and glossy, dark green leaves with prominent red veins. The plant produces small, fragrant, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Marsdenia tingens is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
