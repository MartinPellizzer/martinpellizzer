Merremia pinnatifida

Merremia pinnatifida, commonly known as the Brazilian morning glory, is a flowering vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Merremia pinnatifida is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world as a decorative plant.
