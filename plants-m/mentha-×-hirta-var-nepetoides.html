Mentha × hirta var. nepetoides

Mentha × hirta var. nepetoides, commonly known as catmint, is a hybrid plant belonging to the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a fragrant, minty aroma. Native to Europe and parts of western Asia, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of nectar for pollinators.
