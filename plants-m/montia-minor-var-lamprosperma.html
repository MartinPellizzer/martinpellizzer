Montia minor var. lamprosperma

Montia minor var. lamprosperma, commonly known as the glittering montia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Montiaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and clusters of white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils in mountainous regions. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and flies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
