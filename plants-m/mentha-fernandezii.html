Mentha fernandezii

Mentha fernandezii, commonly known as the Fernandez mint, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in countries like Peru and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of purple or pink. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes.

Mentha fernandezii is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
