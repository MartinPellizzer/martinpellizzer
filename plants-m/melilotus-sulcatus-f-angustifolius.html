Melilotus sulcatus f. angustifolius

Melilotus sulcatus f. angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved sweet clover, is a horticultural form of the sweet clover plant.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically narrower than those of the typical form. The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in forage and erosion control.

This variety is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal and aromatic uses.
