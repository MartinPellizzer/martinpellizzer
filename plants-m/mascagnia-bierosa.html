Mascagnia bierosa

Mascagnia bierosa, commonly known as the desert milkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its succulent, branching stems and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions and has a deep root system for water absorption.

Mascagnia bierosa is also known for its milky sap, which is toxic and can cause skin irritation.
