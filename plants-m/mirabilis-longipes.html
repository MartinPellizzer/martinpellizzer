Mirabilis longipes

Mirabilis longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed four o'clock, is a species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers that open in the late afternoon and close at dawn. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

The flowers come in shades of red, pink, and white, making it a popular ornamental plant.
