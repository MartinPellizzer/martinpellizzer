Miconia pectinata

Miconia pectinata, commonly known as the silky miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by bright red berries. Miconia pectinata is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value has led to its introduction in some regions, though it can become invasive in certain ecosystems.
