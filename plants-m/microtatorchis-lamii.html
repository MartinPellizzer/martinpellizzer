Microtatorchis lamii

Microtatorchis lamii, commonly known as the silverleaf spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
