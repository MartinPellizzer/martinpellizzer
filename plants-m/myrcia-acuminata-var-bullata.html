Myrcia acuminata var. bullata

Myrcia acuminata var. bullata, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves that emit a distinctive scent when crushed. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by edible, red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
