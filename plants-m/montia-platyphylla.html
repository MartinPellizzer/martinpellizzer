Montia platyphylla

Montia platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leafed montia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Montiaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky stream banks.

Montia platyphylla is often found in coastal regions and is adapted to cool, humid climates.
