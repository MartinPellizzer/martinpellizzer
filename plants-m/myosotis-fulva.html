Myosotis fulva

Myosotis fulva, commonly known as the yellow forget-me-not, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, bright yellow petals and a blue-violet center, forming a delicate five-petaled flower. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.

Myosotis fulva is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also found in the wild as a member of the Boraginaceae family.
