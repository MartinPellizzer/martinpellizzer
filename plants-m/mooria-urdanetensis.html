Mooria urdanetensis

Mooria urdanetensis, commonly known as the Urdanet stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange, adding a vibrant touch to its rocky habitat. It is well adapted to cool, sunny environments and is often found growing on rocky outcrops or in crevices.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
