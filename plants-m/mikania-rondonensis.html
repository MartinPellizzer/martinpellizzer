Mikania rondonensis

Mikania rondonensis, commonly known as the Amazonian devil's vine, is a climbing herbaceous plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and dense foliage. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. Its fibrous roots and stems are often used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.

Mikania rondonensis is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
