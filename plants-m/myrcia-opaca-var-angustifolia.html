Myrcia opaca var. angustifolia

Myrcia opaca var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf myrtle, is a shrub native to the subtropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils, which contribute to its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural fragrances.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
