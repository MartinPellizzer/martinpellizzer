Myrcia caroli

Myrcia caroli, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, fleshy berries. The plant produces clusters of white to pink flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and it has a strong, spicy scent when crushed.

Myrcia caroli is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
