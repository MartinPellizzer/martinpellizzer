Malva glabrifolia

Malva glabrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus, and is characterized by its palmate leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky soils, and is well adapted to drought conditions. Its flowers bloom in summer, and the plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.
