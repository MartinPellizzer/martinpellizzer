Mitrephora poilanei

Mitrephora poilanei, commonly known as the Madagascar satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes cashew and poison ivy. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, orange-red drupes.

The tree is valued for its durable wood and is often found in moist, lowland forests.
