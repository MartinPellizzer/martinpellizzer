Malabaila lasiocarpa

Malabaila lasiocarpa, commonly known as the hairy-fruited malabaila, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant is notable for its hairy texture on both its stems and fruit, which gives it its scientific name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The flowers are typically small and clustered, producing a distinctive fragrance that attracts pollinators.
