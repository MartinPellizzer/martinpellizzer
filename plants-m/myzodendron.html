Myzodendron

Myzodendron, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Malvaceae.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in racemes, followed by capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Myzodendron is native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.
