Manihot caudata

Manihot caudata, commonly known as the long-tailed arrowroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white, powdery wax. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its roots are edible and have been traditionally used as a starch source in various indigenous communities.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, humid environments.
