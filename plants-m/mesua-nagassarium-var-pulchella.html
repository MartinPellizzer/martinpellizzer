Mesua nagassarium var. pulchella

Mesua nagassarium var. pulchella, commonly known as the pink flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range from pink to red, often with a yellow center, which bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has elliptical, leathery leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the branches. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a street tree.

The bark is smooth and greyish, and the wood is valued for its durability and fine grain.
