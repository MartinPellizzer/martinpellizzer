Melastoma quadrangulare

Melastoma quadrangulare, commonly known as the four-angled melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its quadrangular stems and showy, violet or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a rough texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
