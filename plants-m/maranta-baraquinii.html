Maranta baraquinii

Maranta baraquinii, commonly known as the silver stripe arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial with striking, arrow-shaped leaves that have a distinctive silvery stripe running down the center. The plant produces small, white flowers that are borne on a spike, followed by reddish-brown berries. It thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
