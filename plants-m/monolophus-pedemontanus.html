Monolophus pedemontanus

Monolophus pedemontanus, commonly known as the Montane Lophus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps to conserve moisture in its native habitat.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to high-altitude conditions.
