Mammillaria heinei

Mammillaria heinei, commonly known as the Heine's nipple cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a compact, rounded shape and a gray-green to blue-gray coloration. The plant features numerous radial spines that are typically white or pale yellow, and it produces yellow flowers during the spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Mammillaria heinei is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
