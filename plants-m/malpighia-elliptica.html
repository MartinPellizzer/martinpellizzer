Malpighia elliptica

Malpighia elliptica, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or granadilla, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malpighiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, red, cherry-like fruits. The plant has elliptical leaves and produces showy, yellow flowers with red stamens. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

The fruit is rich in vitamin C and is used in culinary and medicinal applications.
