Miconia subcompressa subsp. beverlyana

Miconia subcompressa subsp.

beverlyana, commonly known as the Beverlyana miconia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, whitish hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are typically borne on short pedicels. Its fruit is a distinctive, fleshy, three-lobed berry that turns red when mature.

This species is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
