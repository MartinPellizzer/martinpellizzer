Monanthes chlorotica

Monanthes chlorotica, commonly known as the green-flowered sunshade, is a unique flowering plant characterized by its striking green petals that contrast with its yellow stamens.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant's leaves are broad and waxy, adapted to conserve moisture in its native habitat. Its flowers emit a mild, sweet fragrance that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, Monanthes chlorotica is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized greenhouses.
