Myrsine nubicola

Myrsine nubicola, commonly known as the East African myrsine, is a shrub native to the highlands of East Africa.

It belongs to the family Myrsinaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.

Myrsine nubicola is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
