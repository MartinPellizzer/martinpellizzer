Malacochaete riparia

Malacochaete riparia, commonly known as the river reed, is a flowering plant native to wetland and riparian habitats.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found along riverbanks and in marshy areas.

Malacochaete riparia plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
