Magnolia mexicana

Magnolia mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often with a subtle lichen growth.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrant blossoms.
