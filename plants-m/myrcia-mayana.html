Myrcia mayana

Myrcia mayana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a strong, aromatic scent.

The fruit is a small, red, fleshy drupe that ripens in late summer.
