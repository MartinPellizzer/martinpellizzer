Maba teijsmannii f. latifolia

Maba teijsmannii f. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf maba, is a variant of the maba tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically larger than those of the typical species. The plant belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is known for its latex-producing properties. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The bark and latex of this plant have been used in traditional medicine for various therapeutic purposes.
