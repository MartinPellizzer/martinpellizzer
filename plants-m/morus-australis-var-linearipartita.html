Morus australis var. linearipartita

Morus australis var. linearipartita, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mulberry, is a variant of the mulberry tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically divided into three or more lobes. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as mulberries, which are edible and often consumed by both humans and animals. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food for wildlife.
