Munrochloa

Munrochloa, commonly known as the mountain grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is native to various regions of South America, particularly in the Andes mountains. The plants are characterized by their tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. They typically thrive in high-altitude environments with cooler temperatures and well-drained soils.

Munrochloa species are often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas, contributing to the biodiversity of these ecosystems.
