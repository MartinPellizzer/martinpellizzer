Mentha × digenea

Mentha × digenea, commonly known as water mint, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and aromatic, often fragrant, foliage. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as stream sides, wet meadows, and marshes. Its flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and usually have a pale purple or white color.

Mentha × digenea is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in herbal remedies.
