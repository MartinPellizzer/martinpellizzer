Milium undulatifolium

Milium undulatifolium, commonly known as the wavy-leaved millet, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, wavy-edged leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate regions and is often found in meadows or along stream banks. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into dry, three-angled seeds.

Milium undulatifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
