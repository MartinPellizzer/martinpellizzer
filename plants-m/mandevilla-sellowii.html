Mandevilla sellowii

Mandevilla sellowii, commonly known as the Sellow's mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a vigorous climber with opposite, simple, and usually hairy leaves. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Mandevilla sellowii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ability to climb structures.
