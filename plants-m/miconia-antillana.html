Miconia antillana

Miconia antillana, commonly known as the Caribbean miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is considered a problematic species in some areas.
