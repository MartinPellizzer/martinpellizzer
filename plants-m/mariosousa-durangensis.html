Mariosousa durangensis

Mariosousa durangensis, commonly known as the Durango mariosousa, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Durango, Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky and well-drained soils, thriving in temperate to subtropical climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with prominent central disk.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
