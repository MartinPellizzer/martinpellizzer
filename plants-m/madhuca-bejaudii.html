Madhuca bejaudii

Madhuca bejaudii, commonly known as the Bejaud's tallow tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and smooth, bark-like trunk. Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
