Machaonia erythrocarpa

Machaonia erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited machaonia, is a species native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub belonging to the Ericaceae family, characterized by its evergreen leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for native birds. Machaonia erythrocarpa thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant component of New Zealand's flora.
