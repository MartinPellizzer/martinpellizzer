Myrtus bidwillii

Myrtus bidwillii, commonly known as the Australian myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. It typically grows in coastal regions and is often found in heathlands and rocky soils.

Myrtus bidwillii is valued for its fragrant foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
