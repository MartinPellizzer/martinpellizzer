Melica valdiviana

Melica valdiviana, commonly known as the Valdivian grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly found in the temperate rainforests of Chile and Argentina. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often along stream banks and in forest understories. It has slender, tufted stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
