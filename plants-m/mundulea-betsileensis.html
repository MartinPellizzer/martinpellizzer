Mundulea betsileensis

Mundulea betsileensis, commonly known as the Betsilean acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with its deep root system and waxy leaves that reduce water loss. It plays a vital role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.

Its flowers are typically yellow or pale green, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
