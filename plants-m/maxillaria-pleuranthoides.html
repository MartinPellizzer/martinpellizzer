Maxillaria pleuranthoides

Maxillaria pleuranthoides, commonly known as the clustered maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces clusters of fragrant, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically blooms during the dry season, and its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This orchid is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
