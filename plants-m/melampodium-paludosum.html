Melampodium paludosum

Melampodium paludosum, commonly known as marsh coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow daisy-like flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Melampodium paludosum is a perennial herb that plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and supporting local biodiversity.
