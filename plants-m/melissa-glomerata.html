Melissa glomerata

Melissa glomerata, commonly known as lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for their calming properties.

Melissa glomerata is also a popular plant in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract beneficial insects.
