Millettia stipellatissima

Millettia stipellatissima, commonly known as the velvetwood tree, is a tropical legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with long, slender stipules that give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several flat, elongated seeds.

This species is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in lowland rainforests.
