Myllanthus maroanus

Myllanthus maroanus, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Cerocarpus maroanus*, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its small, red, cherry-like fruits that are edible and often consumed by wildlife. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a glossy green appearance.

Myllanthus maroanus plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing food and shelter for various species.
