Machaerium eriocarpum

Machaerium eriocarpum, commonly known as the silky machaerium, is a tropical shrub native to regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain seeds, a feature that contributes to its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing ability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and ecological restoration projects.
