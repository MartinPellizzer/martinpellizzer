Melica ciliata f. violacea

Melica ciliata f. violacea, commonly known as the violet-fringed milletgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often tinged with violet or purple hues, giving it its distinctive appearance. This variety is typically found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
