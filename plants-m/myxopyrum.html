Myxopyrum

Myxopyrum, commonly known as the common liverwort, is a type of non-vascular plant belonging to the family Marchantiaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, flattened structure and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Myxopyrum typically grows in dense mats, with a thalloid body that lacks true roots, stems, and leaves. The plant reproduces through spores, which are released from specialized structures called sporangia.

It plays an important ecological role in soil stabilization and as a habitat for small invertebrates.
