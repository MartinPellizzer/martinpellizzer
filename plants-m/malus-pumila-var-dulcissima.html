Malus pumila var. dulcissima

Malus pumila var. dulcissima, commonly known as the sweet crabapple, is a small deciduous tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its ornamental flowers and fruits, which are typically smaller and sweeter than those of other crabapple varieties. The tree produces white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by small, edible fruits that ripen in late summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
