Melaleuca lasiandra

Melaleuca lasiandra, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its distinctive bottlebrush-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and is known for its aromatic leaves and showy pink or red flowers. It has a woody stem and produces small, scaly leaves that are often narrow and leathery.

Melaleuca lasiandra is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
