Miconia cyanocarpa var. parvifolia

Miconia cyanocarpa var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved blue-fruited miconia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The fruit is a fleshy, blue-black berry that contains numerous seeds. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

It is also noted for its potential ecological impact due to its invasive tendencies in certain regions.
