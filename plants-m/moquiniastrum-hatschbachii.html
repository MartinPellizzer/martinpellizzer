Moquiniastrum hatschbachii

Moquiniastrum hatschbachii, commonly known as the Florida marsh mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland environments, such as swamps and marshes. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the spring. It has a fibrous root system adapted to waterlogged soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and ecological studies.
