Malacochaete pterolepis

Malacochaete pterolepis, commonly known as the featherleaf tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters, which are adapted for pollination by wind and insects. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

Malacochaete pterolepis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert wildlife.
