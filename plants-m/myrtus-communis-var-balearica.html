Myrtus communis var. balearica

Myrtus communis var. balearica, commonly known as the Balearic myrtle, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with a distinctive scent when crushed. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in spring, which are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its ornamental value and aromatic qualities make it a popular choice in horticulture.
