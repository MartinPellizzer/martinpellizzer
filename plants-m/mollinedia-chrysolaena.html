Mollinedia chrysolaena

Mollinedia chrysolaena, commonly known as the golden moss orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its dense, moss-like foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are clustered at the base and are often covered in fine, silky hairs.

The flowers, which bloom in spring, are borne on slender stalks and are notable for their vibrant yellow color and intricate structure.
