Mitracarpus haitiensis var. glabratus

Mitracarpus haitiensis var. glabratus, commonly known as Haitian sandmat, is a flowering plant native to Haiti.

It is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and small, white to yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid conditions. Its fruit is a schizocarp, splitting into two one-seeded parts when mature.
