Megalastrum exaggeratum

Megalastrum exaggeratum, commonly known as the exaggerated giant.

This plant is characterized by its massive, fleshy leaves that can grow up to three meters in length. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and abundant sunlight. The leaves are covered in a thick, waxy cuticle that helps retain moisture.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red and orange, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
