Melissa obtusifolia

Melissa obtusifolia, commonly known as honey bee plant or lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a versatile addition to gardens. Its aromatic foliage is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for its calming properties.

Melissa obtusifolia is also a valuable plant for pollinators, attracting bees and other beneficial insects.
