Myrcia altera

Myrcia altera, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces berries that are red when mature, which are an important food source for various wildlife species. Myrcia altera is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, posing challenges to native ecosystems.
