Malus jinxianensis

Malus jinxianensis, commonly known as the Jinxian apple, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its ornamental value due to its showy white flowers and red fruit. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical with a smooth margin, and the fruit is edible, though not widely cultivated for commercial use.

The species is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
