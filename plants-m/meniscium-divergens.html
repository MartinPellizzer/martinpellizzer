Meniscium divergens

Meniscium divergens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, erect stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are bell-shaped, usually blue or violet, and are clustered in loose inflorescences.

Meniscium divergens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
