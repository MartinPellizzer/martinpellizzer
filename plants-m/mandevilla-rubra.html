Mandevilla rubra

Mandevilla rubra, commonly known as the red mandevilla, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its vigorous growth and climbing habit. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to pink, often with a contrasting white throat. Its leaves are typically glossy and ovate, providing a lush backdrop to the vibrant blooms.

Mandevilla rubra thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and as a houseplant.
