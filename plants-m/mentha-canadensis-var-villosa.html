Mentha canadensis var. villosa

Mentha canadensis var. villosa, commonly known as Canadian mint or hairy mint, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, typical of the Lamiaceae family. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its minty fragrance and medicinal properties.
