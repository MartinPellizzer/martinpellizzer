Mimosa gracilipes f. armata

Mimosa gracilipes f. armata, commonly known as the hairy mimosa, is a variant of the slender mimosa species native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and profuse clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it requires careful management in natural habitats.
