Morronea arundinariae

Morronea arundinariae, commonly known as the bamboo reed, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height, often forming dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long, narrow, and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Morronea arundinariae is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and is used in traditional landscaping and floral arrangements.
