Manilkara excisa

Manilkara excisa, commonly known as the Amazonian sapodilla, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, elliptical leaves. The tree produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, sweet, and slightly gritty fruits. Its latex, which is milky and viscous, has been traditionally used for various purposes, including as a natural adhesive.

The plant is also valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for wildlife in its native rainforest environment.
