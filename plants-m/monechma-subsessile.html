Monechma subsessile

Monechma subsessile, commonly known as the narrowleaf monechma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, upright stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in dense, terminal spikes, producing small, white to pale green blooms.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
