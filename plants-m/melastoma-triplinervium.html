Melastoma triplinervium

Melastoma triplinervium, commonly known as the three-veined melastoma, is a flowering plant belonging to the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with three prominent veins, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release its seeds, aiding in its propagation.
