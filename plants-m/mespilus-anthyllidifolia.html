Mespilus anthyllidifolia

Mespilus anthyllidifolia, commonly known as the red-leaf mespilus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive red foliage, which contrasts with its small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and prefers well-drained soils. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with serrated edges, and it produces small, edible fruits similar to those of other mespilus species.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
