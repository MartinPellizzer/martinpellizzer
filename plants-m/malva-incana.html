Malva incana

Malva incana, commonly known as woolly mallow, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found along roadsides and in dry habitats.

Malva incana is also valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
