Myrsine venosa

Myrsine venosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf myrsine, is a tropical shrub belonging to the family Myrsinaceae.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by orange-red berries. Myrsine venosa is native to regions in Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks.
