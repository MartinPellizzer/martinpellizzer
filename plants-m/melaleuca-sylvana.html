Melaleuca sylvana

Melaleuca sylvana, commonly known as the swamp paperbark, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a pale, papery texture beneath. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, typically blooming from July to October. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface.

Melaleuca sylvana thrives in wetland or swampy environments, preferring well-drained, acidic soils.
