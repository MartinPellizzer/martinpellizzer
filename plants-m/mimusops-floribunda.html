Mimusops floribunda

Mimusops floribunda, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It features smooth, grayish-brown bark and elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
