Mentha longifolia var. laggeri

Mentha longifolia var. laggeri, commonly known as Lagger's mint, is a variant of the catnip plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in whorled clusters, typical of the Lamiaceae family. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, including nepetalactone, which contribute to its distinctive scent and medicinal properties.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional use in herbal remedies.
