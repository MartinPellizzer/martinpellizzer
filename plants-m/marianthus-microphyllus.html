Marianthus microphyllus

Marianthus microphyllus, commonly known as the dwarf marianthus, is a small flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It features delicate, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its compact size and ornamental flowers, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a potted plant.
