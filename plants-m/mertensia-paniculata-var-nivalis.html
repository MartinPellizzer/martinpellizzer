Mertensia paniculata var. nivalis

Mertensia paniculata var. nivalis, commonly known as the alpine bluebells, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
