Muscari babachii

Muscari babachii, commonly known as the Persian grape hyacinth, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, resembling grapes, hence its common name. The plant typically blooms in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to gardens and natural landscapes. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for rock gardens or borders.

Muscari babachii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements.
