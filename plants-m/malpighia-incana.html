Malpighia incana

Malpighia incana, commonly known as the rough malpighia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malpighiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas, including parts of Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a rough texture. Its flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals and numerous stamens, and it produces small, bright red berries as fruit.

The plant is often grown as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.
