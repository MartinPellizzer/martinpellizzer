Mikania dioica

Mikania dioica, commonly known as mile-a-minute weed, is a fast-growing vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its rapid spread, often forming dense mats that can smother other vegetation. It thrives in disturbed soils and is considered an invasive species in many regions.

Its fibrous roots and prolific seed production contribute to its aggressive colonization of new areas.
