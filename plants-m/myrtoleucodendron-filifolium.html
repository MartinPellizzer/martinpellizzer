Myrtoleucodendron filifolium

Myrtoleucodendron filifolium, commonly known as the thread-leaved myrtle, is a unique plant native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white waxy coating. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale pink and are clustered in inflorescences.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited but significant ecological importance in its native habitat.
