Mimosopsis

Mimosopsis, commonly known as the desert candle, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, candle-like seed pods that remain attached to the plant for extended periods. The plant has small, bipinnate leaves and produces yellow or white flowers that bloom in spring. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots and a slow growth rate.

Mimosopsis is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
