Minthostachys spicata

Minthostachys spicata, commonly known as the spike mint, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly found in the Andean regions.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic plants and square stems. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

This species is often cultivated for its fragrant foliage and ornamental value in gardens.
