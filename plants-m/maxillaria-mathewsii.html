Maxillaria mathewsii

Maxillaria mathewsii, commonly known as the Mathews' orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It typically grows in humid, shaded environments at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 2,500 meters. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowering structures. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, with intricate patterns and a distinctive lip.

Maxillaria mathewsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
