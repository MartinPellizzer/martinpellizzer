Monotaxis stowardii

Monotaxis stowardii, commonly known as the Stoward's monocot, is a unique flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its single, elongated leaf that extends from a short, thick stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
