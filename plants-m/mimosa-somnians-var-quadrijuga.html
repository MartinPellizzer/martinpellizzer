Mimosa somnians var. quadrijuga

Mimosa somnians var. quadrijuga, commonly known as the four-branch mimosa, is a variant of the native Australian species Mimosa somnians.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and produces small, yellow, globular flower heads. The plant is typically found in sandy soils and coastal areas, thriving in well-drained environments. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate light frost.

The species is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets.
