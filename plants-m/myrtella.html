Myrtella

Myrtella, commonly known as the myrtle plant, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Myrtella is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in well-drained soil.

It is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
