Magonia corylifolia

Magonia corylifolia, commonly known as the Chinese hickory, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Juglandaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and edible nuts. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by oval-shaped, hard-shelled fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its nutritious seeds and ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its timber, which is strong and durable.
