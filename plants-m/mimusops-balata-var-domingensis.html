Mimusops balata var. domingensis

Mimusops balata var. domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and oval-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The tree produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests, thriving in humid, tropical climates.

The wood of this species is valued for its durability and fine grain, making it useful for furniture and construction.
