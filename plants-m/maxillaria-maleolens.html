Maxillaria maleolens

Maxillaria maleolens, commonly known as the chocolate orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark, velvety petals and sepals that resemble chocolate, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are fragrant and bloom in clusters, often in shades of brown, orange, and reddish hues.

The botanical features of Maxillaria maleolens make it a popular choice for collectors and horticulturists due to its unique appearance and ornamental value.
