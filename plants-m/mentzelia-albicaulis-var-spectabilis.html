Mentzelia albicaulis var. spectabilis

Mentzelia albicaulis var. spectabilis, commonly known as the showy whitehead, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are often hairy and lance-shaped, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is known for its striking appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
