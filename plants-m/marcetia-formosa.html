Marcetia formosa

Marcetia formosa, commonly known as the showy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of purple or blue. The plant has lanceolate, hairy leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.
