Monocera densiflora

Monocera densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered monocera, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique floral structure makes it an interesting subject for botanical study and horticultural cultivation.
