Malaxis ochreata

Malaxis ochreata, commonly known as the ochre orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with intricate patterns, and it produces small, fleshy fruits.

Malaxis ochreata is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
