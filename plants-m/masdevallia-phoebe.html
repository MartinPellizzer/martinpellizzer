Masdevallia phoebe

Masdevallia phoebe, commonly known as the Phoebe masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, often pendulous inflorescences and a variety of flower colors, including shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as a miniature epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, and it thrives in cool, humid environments with indirect light.

Masdevallia phoebe is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
