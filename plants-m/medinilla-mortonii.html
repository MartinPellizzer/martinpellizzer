Medinilla mortonii

Medinilla mortonii, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or velvet tangerine tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green, leathery leaves and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, round, yellow-orange fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowers.

Medinilla mortonii is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
