Myriactis radicans

Myriactis radicans, commonly known as the creeping myriactis, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pale yellow to orange in color. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover. Its scientific name reflects its unique floral structure and spreading nature.

Myriactis radicans is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
