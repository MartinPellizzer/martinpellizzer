Mellera submutica f. latifolia

Mellera submutica f. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved velvetwood, is a variant of the species Mellera submutica, native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade. Its flowers are small and clustered, often producing a subtle fragrance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
