Medicago bimuricata

Medicago bimuricata, commonly known as the two-lobed medick, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, where it plays a role in nitrogen fixation through its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria. Its seeds are typically small and have a hard coat, which allows for long-term survival in the soil.

Medicago bimuricata is also used in some traditional herbal remedies for its potential medicinal properties.
