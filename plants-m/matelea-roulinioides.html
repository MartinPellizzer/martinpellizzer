Matelea roulinioides

Matelea roulinioides, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant has a climbing habit and features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. Its flowers emit a strong, sweet fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators like moths.
