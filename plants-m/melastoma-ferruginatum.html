Melastoma ferruginatum

Melastoma ferruginatum, commonly known as the red-stemmed melastoma, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are typically opposite and have a rough texture, while the stems are often reddish in color.

Melastoma ferruginatum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
