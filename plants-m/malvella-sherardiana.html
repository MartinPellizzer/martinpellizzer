Malvella sherardiana

Malvella sherardiana, commonly known as the Malvella, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It has hairy, ovate leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Malvella sherardiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
