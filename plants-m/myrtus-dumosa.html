Myrtus dumosa

Myrtus dumosa, commonly known as the prickly myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries, which are typically dark blue or black, are edible and have a slightly tart flavor.

Myrtus dumosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
