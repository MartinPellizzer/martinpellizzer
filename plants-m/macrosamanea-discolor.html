Macrosamanea discolor

Macrosamanea discolor, commonly known as the white-flowered tamarind tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, white or pale yellow flowers. The tree produces long, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds surrounded by a fibrous pulp. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its hard, durable wood.

Additionally, it plays a role in traditional medicine and agroforestry systems.
