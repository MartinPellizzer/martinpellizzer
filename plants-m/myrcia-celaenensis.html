Myrcia celaenensis

Myrcia celaenensis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that releases small, winged seeds when mature.

Myrcia celaenensis is often valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
