Matthiola bolleana subsp. morocera

Matthiola bolleana subsp.

morocera, commonly known as the Moroccan wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Morocco. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbages and mustards.

The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
