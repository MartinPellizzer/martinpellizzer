Monanthotaxis couvreurii

Monanthotaxis couvreurii, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces small, berry-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it a unique and striking addition to botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
