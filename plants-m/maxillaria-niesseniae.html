Maxillaria niesseniae

Maxillaria niesseniae, commonly known as the Niessen's orchid, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant produces compact, pseudobulbous stems with broad, elliptic leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white or grayish pubescence. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to greenish-white, with intricate patterns and a unique lip that distinguishes it from other orchid species.

Maxillaria niesseniae is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
