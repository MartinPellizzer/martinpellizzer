Millettia nathaliae

Millettia nathaliae, commonly known as the Nathalia's legume, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, which are often dispersed by wind or animals. It is typically found in humid, forested environments and plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
