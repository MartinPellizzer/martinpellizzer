Monanthotaxis paniculata

Monanthotaxis paniculata, commonly known as the African mountain orchid, is a striking herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking panicles of white to pale yellow flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in local ecosystems.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.
