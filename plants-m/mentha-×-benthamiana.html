Mentha × benthamiana

Mentha × benthamiana, commonly known as the hybrid mint, is a species resulting from the cross between Mentha viridis and Mentha suaveolens.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and fragrant, aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Mentha × benthamiana is widely cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy, food flavoring, and pharmaceutical applications.

Its adaptability to various climates makes it a popular choice for both commercial and home gardening.
