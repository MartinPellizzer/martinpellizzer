Melocalamus fimbriatus

Melocalamus fimbriatus, commonly known as the fringed bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of eastern China, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender culms and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive fringed margin. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
