Melanthium racemosum

Melanthium racemosum, commonly known as the clustered false nettle, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a distinctive reddish or purple midrib.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and is considered an indicator species for certain forest ecosystems.
