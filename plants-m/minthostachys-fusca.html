Minthostachys fusca

Minthostachys fusca, commonly known as the purple mint, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their fragrant leaves. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, opposite leaves with a serrated edge, and small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to white. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its aromatic properties make it a popular choice for ornamental and medicinal purposes.
