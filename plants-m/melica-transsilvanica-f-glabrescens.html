Melica transsilvanica f. glabrescens

Melica transsilvanica f. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous meadowgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, two-lobed spikelets, which are typically green to reddish in color. This subspecies is distinguished by its reduced pubescence, giving it a smooth, glabrous appearance.

It is native to temperate regions of Europe and is often found in meadows and grassy habitats.
