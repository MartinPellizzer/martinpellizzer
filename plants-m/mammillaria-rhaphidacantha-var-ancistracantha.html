Mammillaria rhaphidacantha var. ancistracantha

Mammillaria rhaphidacantha var. ancistracantha, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed hedgehog cactus, is a compact, globular cactus with a dense covering of white, radial spines.

It originates from the arid regions of northern Mexico, where it thrives in full sun and well-draining soil. The plant features short, cylindrical stems that are ribbed and adorned with clusters of small, white flowers during the spring. Its slow growth rate makes it ideal for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
