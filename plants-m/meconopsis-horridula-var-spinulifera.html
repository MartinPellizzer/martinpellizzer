Meconopsis horridula var. spinulifera

Meconopsis horridula var. spinulifera, commonly known as the spiked meconopsis, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers, which bloom in early summer. The plant has a robust, hairy stem and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.
