Mammillaria pseudoperbella

Mammillaria pseudoperbella, commonly known as the Mexican nipple cactus, is a small, spineless cactus native to Mexico.

It features a globular stem covered in white, woolly hairs that help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise muted appearance. It is well-suited for rock gardens and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Due to its compact size and unique appearance, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
