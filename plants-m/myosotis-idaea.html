Myosotis idaea

Myosotis idaea, commonly known as the scented snowflake, is a perennial herb native to Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its small, blue or violet flowers that resemble snowflakes, which bloom in early summer. The plant grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cold, moist environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and hairy, adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Myosotis idaea is also known for its delicate fragrance, which is often described as sweet and slightly honey-like.
