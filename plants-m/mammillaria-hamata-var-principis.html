Mammillaria hamata var. principis

Mammillaria hamata var. principis, commonly known as the Principes hedgehog cactus, is a compact, globular cactus native to Mexico.

It features a dense covering of white, radial spines and short, cylindrical tubercles that give it a distinctive, spiky appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
