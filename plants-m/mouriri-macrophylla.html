Mouriri macrophylla

Mouriri macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf mouriri, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that can grow up to 20 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by edible, orange-red berries. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in local crafts and construction.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
