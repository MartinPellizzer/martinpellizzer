Microbignonia auristellae

Microbignonia auristellae, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of yellow flowers that resemble showers of light. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain seeds covered in a fleshy, red aril. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture.
