Maxillaria suaveolens

Maxillaria suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pink or white with a distinctive, sweet fragrance that attracts pollinators like bees and moths.

Maxillaria suaveolens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
