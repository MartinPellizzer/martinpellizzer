Mephitidia capitata

Mephitidia capitata, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, pentagonal-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to pink and white. The plant has rough, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in prairies and open woodlands.

Mephitidia capitata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
