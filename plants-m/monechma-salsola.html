Monechma salsola

Monechma salsola, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and saline soils. The plant has small, green, fleshy leaves and produces numerous tiny, pinkish to reddish flowers that bloom in summer. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, allowing it to spread widely across disturbed areas.

Monechma salsola is often considered a weed due to its invasive nature in various regions.
