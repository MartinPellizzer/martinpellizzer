Medinilla papuana

Medinilla papuana, commonly known as the Papuan Medinilla, is a flowering plant native to the lowland rainforests of Papua, Indonesia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its leaves are glossy and oval-shaped, providing a lush backdrop to its delicate blossoms.

Medinilla papuana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
