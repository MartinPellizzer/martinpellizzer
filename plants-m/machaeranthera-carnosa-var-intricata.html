Machaeranthera carnosa var. intricata

Machaeranthera carnosa var. intricata, commonly known as the intricate fringed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers with intricate, fringed petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while the flowers bloom in late summer.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
