Myrcia neoforsteri

Myrcia neoforsteri, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest biome in Brazil, where it typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.

Myrcia neoforsteri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
