Mallotus contubernalis var. chrysocarpus

Mallotus contubernalis var. chrysocarpus, commonly known as golden-fruited mallotus, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by distinctive yellow or orange fruits that are spherical and fleshy. Its fruits are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor, making it valuable in local cuisines.

The species is also noted for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and showy fruiting display.
