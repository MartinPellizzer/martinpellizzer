Mallotus sphaerocarpus

Mallotus sphaerocarpus, commonly known as the globe-fruit mallot, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves that are usually opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by distinctive, spherical fruits that are red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
