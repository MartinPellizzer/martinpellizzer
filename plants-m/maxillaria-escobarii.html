Maxillaria escobarii

Maxillaria escobarii, commonly known as the Escobarii orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often with intricate patterns. Its leaves are typically broad and grass-like, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Maxillaria escobarii is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
