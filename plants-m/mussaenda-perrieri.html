Mussaenda perrieri

Mussaenda perrieri, commonly known as the golden flame or fireball plant, is a flowering shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, white, tubular flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Mussaenda perrieri is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
