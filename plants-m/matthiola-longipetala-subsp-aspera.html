Matthiola longipetala subsp. aspera

Matthiola longipetala subsp.

aspera, commonly known as the rough-flowered stock, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant, four-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The subspecies aspera is distinguished by its rough, hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
