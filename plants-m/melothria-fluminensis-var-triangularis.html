Melothria fluminensis var. triangularis

Melothria fluminensis var. triangularis, commonly known as the triangular passionfruit, is a tropical vine native to South America.

It produces small, yellow-orange fruits with a distinctive triangular shape and a sweet, aromatic flavor. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

The fruit's unique appearance and taste make it a popular choice for both culinary and horticultural purposes.
