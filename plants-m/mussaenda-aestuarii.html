Mussaenda aestuarii

Mussaenda aestuarii, commonly known as the coastal mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a reddish tinge. The plant typically grows in sandy or muddy soils near tidal zones, demonstrating a high tolerance for saline conditions. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

Mussaenda aestuarii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in coastal gardens for its resilience and beauty.
