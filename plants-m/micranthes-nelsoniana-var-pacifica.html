Micranthes nelsoniana var. pacifica

Micranthes nelsoniana var. pacifica, commonly known as the Pacific mountain sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It typically features dense clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with narrow, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to cold, arid conditions typical of its native habitat.
