Mimosa latisiliqua

Mimosa latisiliqua, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellow, fluffy flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates. Its flowers are short-lived, opening in the morning and closing by midday, a phenomenon known as nyctinasty.

Mimosa latisiliqua is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and unique flowering behavior.
