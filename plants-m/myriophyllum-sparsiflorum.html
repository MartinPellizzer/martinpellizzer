Myriophyllum sparsiflorum

Myriophyllum sparsiflorum, commonly known as water milfoil, is a flowering aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of narrow, ribbon-like leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and streams. It is often found in shallow water, where its roots anchor it to the substrate.

Myriophyllum sparsiflorum plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
