Macaranga racemohispida

Macaranga racemohispida, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed macaranga, is a tropical plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its smooth, often hairy stems and alternate, ovate leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It is native to Southeast Asia and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Macaranga racemohispida is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
