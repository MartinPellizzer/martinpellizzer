Magnolia × thomsoniana

Magnolia × thomsoniana, commonly known as the Thomson's magnolia, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The flowers are typically white with a subtle pinkish tinge and emit a sweet, fragrant aroma. The tree has smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves that turn yellow in the fall.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
