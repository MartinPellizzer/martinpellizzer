Morithamnus ganophyllus

Morithamnus ganophyllus, commonly known as the golden leaf saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-like growth habit and small, fleshy leaves that are typically covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on short, erect stems. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
