Melocanna lutescens

Melocanna lutescens, commonly known as the yellow bamboo, is a perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, elongated leaves. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense clumps, making it suitable for ornamental and landscaping purposes. Its yellowish-brown stems and feathery inflorescences add visual interest to gardens.

Melocanna lutescens is also valued for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a source of sustainable materials.
