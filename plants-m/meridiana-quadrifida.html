Meridiana quadrifida

Meridiana quadrifida, commonly known as the four-leafed meridiana, is a rare and striking plant native to specific regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its unique quadrifid leaves, which split into four distinct lobes, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to pale pink.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
