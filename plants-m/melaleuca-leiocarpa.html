Melaleuca leiocarpa

Melaleuca leiocarpa, commonly known as the white melaleuca, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy white or pale pink flowers. The plant is typically found in coastal regions and is adapted to sandy soils and saline conditions. It produces small, woody fruits that contain numerous seeds, facilitating its spread in coastal environments.

Melaleuca leiocarpa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing dunes.
