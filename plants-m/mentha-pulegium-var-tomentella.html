Mentha pulegium var. tomentella

Mentha pulegium var. tomentella, commonly known as woolly pennyroyal, is a variant of the pennyroyal plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves and a strong, pungent aroma. The plant is a low-growing perennial herb with square stems and small, opposite leaves. It produces tiny white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer months.

This variety is often used in traditional herbal medicine and as a repellent for insects.
