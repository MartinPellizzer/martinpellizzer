Machilus obovatifolius

Machilus obovatifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf machilus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Vietnam, Thailand, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are broadly ovate in shape, often with a slightly serrated margin. It produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by dark blue-black berries.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and fragrance.
