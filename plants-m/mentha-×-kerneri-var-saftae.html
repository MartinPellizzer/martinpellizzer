Mentha × kerneri var. saftae

Mentha × kerneri var. saftae, commonly known as the Safta mint, is a hybrid mint species native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly serrated leaves and clusters of pale blue to purple flowers. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. It is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for its cooling and soothing properties.

The plant's essential oils contain menthol and other compounds that contribute to its distinctive fragrance and medicinal uses.
