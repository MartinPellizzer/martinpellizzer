Myrcia anacardiifolia

Myrcia anacardiifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 10 meters tall. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate to elliptical leaves with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its small, white to pale yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences and are followed by bright red, fleshy berries.

The plant is known for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
