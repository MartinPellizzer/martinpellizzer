Malus sieboldii sargentii

Malus sieboldii sargentii, commonly known as the Sargent apple or Japanese flowering crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring clusters of white or pink flowers in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its compact size and seasonal blooms make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological plantings.
