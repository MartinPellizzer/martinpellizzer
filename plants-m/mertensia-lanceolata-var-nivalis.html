Mertensia lanceolata var. nivalis

Mertensia lanceolata var. nivalis, commonly known as the snow mertensia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, dark green leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to lavender flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats. It is adapted to cool climates and is typically in bloom from late spring to early summer.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
