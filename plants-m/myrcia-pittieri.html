Myrcia pittieri

Myrcia pittieri, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a glossy, dark green surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that contains a single seed.

Myrcia pittieri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
