Mentha × carinthiaca

Mentha × carinthiaca, commonly known as carinthian mint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Mentha longifolia and Mentha aquatica.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring square stems and opposite, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Carinthian mint is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
