Mesua eugeniifolia

Mesua eugeniifolia, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
