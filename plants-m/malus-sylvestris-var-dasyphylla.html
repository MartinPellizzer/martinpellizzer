Malus sylvestris var. dasyphylla

Malus sylvestris var. dasyphylla, commonly known as the rough-leaved crabapple, is a variant of the common crabapple.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible, tart fruits. It is native to parts of Europe and is typically found in woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
