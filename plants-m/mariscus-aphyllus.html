Mariscus aphyllus

Mariscus aphyllus, commonly known as the leafless bladderwort, is a unique aquatic plant belonging to the family Lentibulariaceae.

It is characterized by its lack of true leaves, instead having small, bladder-like structures called bladders that trap small aquatic organisms. Its reproductive structures are simple, with small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged.
