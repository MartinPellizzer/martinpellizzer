Matthiola simplicicaulis

Matthiola simplicicaulis, commonly known as the simple-leaved stock, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically has linear, sessile leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Matthiola simplicicaulis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its delicate appearance and historical use in herbal medicine.
