Mandevilla subumbelliflora

Mandevilla subumbelliflora, commonly known as the flame vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant produces clusters of these flowers, often arranged in umbellate patterns, giving it its scientific name. Its heart-shaped leaves are glossy and provide a striking contrast to the colorful blooms.

Mandevilla subumbelliflora thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its showy appearance.
