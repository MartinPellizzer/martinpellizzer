Mecardonia radicata

Mecardonia radicata, commonly known as the red root grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Mecardonia radicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
