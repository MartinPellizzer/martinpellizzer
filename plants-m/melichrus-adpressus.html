Melichrus adpressus

Melichrus adpressus, commonly known as the flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of North America.

It typically grows in dense clusters, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often pressed flat against the ground. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form extensive colonies in moist soil conditions.

Melichrus adpressus plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various small aquatic organisms.
