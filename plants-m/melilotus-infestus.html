Melilotus infestus

Melilotus infestus, commonly known as yellow sweet clover, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small yellow flowers arranged in dense spikes. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
