Melanthera tithonioides

Melanthera tithonioides, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the United States.

It is characterized by its blue or purple flower heads surrounded by spiny, leaf-like bracts. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime environments. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators.

Melanthera tithonioides is a hardy perennial that is well adapted to salt spray and strong coastal winds.
