Maerua caudata

Maerua caudata, commonly known as the African glorybower, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It has a distinctive appearance with long, drooping yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often giving it a glowing appearance at dusk. The plant is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with thick, fleshy leaves that store water. Its roots are deep and robust, allowing it to access groundwater in nutrient-poor soils.

Maerua caudata is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
