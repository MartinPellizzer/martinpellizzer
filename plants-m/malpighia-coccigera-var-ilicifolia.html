Malpighia coccigera var. ilicifolia

Malpighia coccigera var. ilicifolia, commonly known as the Mexican hibiscus or red hibiscus, is a shrub native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It features showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from red to pink, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically ovate with a serrated margin and a dark green, glossy appearance.

The species is adapted to well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
