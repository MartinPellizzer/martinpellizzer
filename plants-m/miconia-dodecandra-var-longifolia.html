Miconia dodecandra var. longifolia

Miconia dodecandra var. longifolia, commonly known as the longifolia miconia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a large shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a waxy surface and a prominent midrib. Its flowers are clustered in inflorescences and are usually white or pale pink, with showy bracts that attract pollinators.

The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a mild, acidic flavor.
