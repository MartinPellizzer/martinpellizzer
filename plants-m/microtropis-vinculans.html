Microtropis vinculans

Microtropis vinculans, commonly known as the golden vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to twine around supports, making it popular in ornamental horticulture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to yellow, often blooming in clusters. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and glossy, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.

Microtropis vinculans thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
