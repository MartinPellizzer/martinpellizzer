Momordica rotunda

Momordica rotunda, commonly known as the globe bitter gourd, is a climbing vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is characterized by its round, warty fruit that resembles a small melon. The plant produces yellow flowers and has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its fruit is typically harvested when green and is used in various traditional cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
