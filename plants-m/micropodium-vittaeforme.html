Micropodium vittaeforme

Micropodium vittaeforme, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a single, delicate flower with a distinctive yellowish-green color. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insect species, contributing to its ecological niche.

Micropodium vittaeforme is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
