Maxillaria vestita

Maxillaria vestita, commonly known as the Orchid of the Andes, is a tropical orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive yellow and purple coloration. The plant typically blooms during the summer months, and its flowers are often arranged in a raceme. Maxillaria vestita has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in its natural habitat.

It requires bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
