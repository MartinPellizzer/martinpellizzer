Masdevallia weberbaueri

Masdevallia weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, often colorful flowers that display a range of hues, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its leaves are usually clustered at the base, and the inflorescence is often short and compact.

Masdevallia weberbaueri is valued in horticulture for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
