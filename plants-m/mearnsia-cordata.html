Mearnsia cordata

Mearnsia cordata, commonly known as the Texas mountain laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces woody pods that contain flat, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant once established.

Mearnsia cordata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
