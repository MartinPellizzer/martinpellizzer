Myrtus micarensis

Myrtus micarensis, commonly known as the Canary Islands myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Canary Islands and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in shrub form, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic properties.

Myrtus micarensis thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the subtropical climate of its native region.
