Microcos lateriflora

Microcos lateriflora, commonly known as the clustered microcos, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Microcos lateriflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
