Malaxis scottii

Malaxis scottii, commonly known as the Scott's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a single, erect spike of flowers.

Malaxis scottii is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate appearance and ecological significance in its native habitat.
