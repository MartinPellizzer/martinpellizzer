Marsdenia phallica

Marsdenia phallica, commonly known as the phallic Marsdenia, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by follicles containing elongated seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its unique morphology, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
