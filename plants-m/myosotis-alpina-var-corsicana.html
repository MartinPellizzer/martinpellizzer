Myosotis alpina var. corsicana

Myosotis alpina var. corsicana, commonly known as the Corsican forget-me-not, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces delicate blue or violet flowers with five petals. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its charming, dainty blooms.
