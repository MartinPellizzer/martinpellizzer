Mentha × rubrohirta

Mentha × rubrohirta, commonly known as the red-hairy mint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Mentha × piperita and Mentha × cervina.

It is characterized by its hairy, reddish stems and serrated leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Mentha × rubrohirta is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.
