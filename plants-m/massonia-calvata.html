Massonia calvata

Massonia calvata, commonly known as the Cape primrose, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Massonia calvata is also noted for its ability to withstand dry conditions, making it a resilient choice for arid climates.
