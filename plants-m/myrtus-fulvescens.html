Myrtus fulvescens

Myrtus fulvescens, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are small, red berries that ripen in late summer and are edible.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its aromatic properties.
