Malpighia diphylla

Malpighia diphylla, commonly known as the two-leaved passionflower, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that resemble a heart shape with five petals and a crown-like structure. The plant produces small, edible berries that are rich in vitamin C and have a tart, acidic flavor. Its leaves are typically oval and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are woody and branched.

Malpighia diphylla is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking floral display and is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
