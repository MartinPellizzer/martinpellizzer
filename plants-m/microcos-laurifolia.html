Microcos laurifolia

Microcos laurifolia, commonly known as the African rose or African hibiscus, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, lance-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savanna habitats.

Microcos laurifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
