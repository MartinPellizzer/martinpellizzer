Myrcia pulchella

Myrcia pulchella, commonly known as the pink myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. Myrcia pulchella is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and mountainous areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
