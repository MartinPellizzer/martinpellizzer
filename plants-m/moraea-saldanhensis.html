Moraea saldanhensis

Moraea saldanhensis, commonly known as the Saldanha lily, is a striking perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing vivid purple or pink flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to its natural habitat.

Moraea saldanhensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
