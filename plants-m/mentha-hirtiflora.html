Mentha hirtiflora

Mentha hirtiflora, commonly known as hairy mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stem. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Mentha hirtiflora is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
