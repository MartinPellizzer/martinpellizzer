Malpighia rufa

Malpighia rufa, commonly known as the red mombin, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
