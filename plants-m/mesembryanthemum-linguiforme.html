Mesembryanthemum linguiforme

Mesembryanthemum linguiforme, commonly known as the arrowleaf ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and leaves that are shaped like arrows, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Mesembryanthemum linguiforme is also known for its ability to survive in arid conditions due to its water-storing tissues.
