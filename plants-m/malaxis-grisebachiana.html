Malaxis grisebachiana

Malaxis grisebachiana, commonly known as the grey beard orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient uptake.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is often found in montane or lowland forests.
