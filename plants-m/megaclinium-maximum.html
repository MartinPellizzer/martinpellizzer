Megaclinium maximum

Megaclinium maximum, commonly known as the giant feather grass, is a striking ornamental grass native to South America.

It features tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 6 feet, with feathery plumes that add a dramatic visual element to gardens. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are primarily pollinated by wind, a common trait among grasses. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that becomes more pronounced in the autumn.

Megaclinium maximum thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a versatile choice for perennial borders and landscape designs.
