Microchilus atalayae

Microchilus atalayae, commonly known as the Atalaya carnation, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are small, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often prostrate or ascending.

Microchilus atalayae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
