Medicago hamosa

Medicago hamosa, commonly known as the Turkish clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, and it thrives in dry, sunny environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions, and it is often used in ecological restoration projects.

Medicago hamosa is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
