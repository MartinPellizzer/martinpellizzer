Magnolia hondurensis

Magnolia hondurensis, commonly known as the Honduran magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Magnoliaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in tropical and subtropical regions.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
