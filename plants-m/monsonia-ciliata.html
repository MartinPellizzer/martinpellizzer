Monsonia ciliata

Monsonia ciliata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or forest bell, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in woodland and forested areas, preferring well-drained, sandy soils. Its leaves are velvety and hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Monsonia ciliata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
