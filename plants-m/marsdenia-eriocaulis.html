Marsdenia eriocaulis

Marsdenia eriocaulis, commonly known as the woolly Marsdenia, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by elliptical, woody fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some horticultural settings.
