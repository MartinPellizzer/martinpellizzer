Mimosa haavoa

Mimosa haavoa, commonly known as the Hawaiian mimosa, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in spring. Mimosa haavoa is typically found in wet forest habitats and is adapted to the humid, tropical climate of Hawaii.

It plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of the region.
