Malaisia scandens subsp. scandens

Malaisia scandens subsp.

scandens, commonly known as the climbing gingers, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its climbing habit and striking, ornamental foliage with deep green, velvety leaves.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, fertile soil.

Due to its aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
