Melastoma biseptenum

Melastoma biseptenum, commonly known as the seven-sepaled melastoma, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its inflorescence consists of clusters of small, tubular flowers that are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Melastoma biseptenum is often found in shaded, moist habitats such as forests and shrublands.
