Malveopsis splendida

Malveopsis splendida, commonly known as the splendid mallow, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its showy, bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
