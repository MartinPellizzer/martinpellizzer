Melastoma salicifolium

Melastoma salicifolium, commonly known as the willow-leaf melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that resemble willow leaves, hence its common name. Its flowers are small, clustered, and often pink or white, blooming in the summer months.

Melastoma salicifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
