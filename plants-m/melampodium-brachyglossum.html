Melampodium brachyglossum

Melampodium brachyglossum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sunflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open woods and along roadsides.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
