Matthiola tenera

Matthiola tenera, commonly known as the dwarf wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbages and mustards. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry conditions.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in rocky or sandy soils.
