Microchilus pedicellatus

Microchilus pedicellatus, commonly known as the slender mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that primarily grows on the branches of host trees in the genus *Eucalyptus*.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in pairs along its stems. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its seeds are dispersed by birds, which consume the fleshy, orange berries.

Microchilus pedicellatus plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitat, contributing to the biodiversity of forest ecosystems.
