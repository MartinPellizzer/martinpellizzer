Mentha aquatica var. piperita

Mentha aquatica var. piperita, commonly known as pepperwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in spikes during the summer months. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found near water bodies.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.
