Mertensia kamczatica

Mertensia kamczatica, commonly known as Kamchadalka, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Eastern Siberia and the Russian Far East.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to retain moisture. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tundra and forest-steppe environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
