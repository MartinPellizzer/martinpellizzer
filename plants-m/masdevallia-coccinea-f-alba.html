Masdevallia coccinea f. alba

Masdevallia coccinea f. alba, commonly known as the white flame orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking white petals and sepals, which contrast with its deep red lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. It produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in greenhouses and tropical gardens.
