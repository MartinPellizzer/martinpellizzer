Malveopsis pinnata

Malveopsis pinnata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallows, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton.

Malveopsis pinnata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and vibrant blooms.
