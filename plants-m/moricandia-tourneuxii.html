Moricandia tourneuxii

Moricandia tourneuxii, commonly known as the desert mustard, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters, featuring slender stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant has pinnately divided leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments. It produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its survival in dry climates.

Moricandia tourneuxii is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
