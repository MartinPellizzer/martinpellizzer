Marshallia caespitosa var. signata

Marshallia caespitosa var. signata, commonly known as the showy marshmallow, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as prairies, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
