Mentha badensis

Mentha badensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mint, is a perennial herb native to regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and square stems. Mentha badensis is often used in traditional medicine for its refreshing scent and potential calming effects.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
