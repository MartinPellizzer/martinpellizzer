Mentha longifolia var. polystachya

Mentha longifolia var. polystachya, commonly known as narrow-leaved mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a serrated edge. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in dense, spike-like clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves used in culinary and medicinal applications.

This variety is characterized by its strong mint scent and preference for well-drained soils in sunny to partially shaded environments.
