Marsdenia madagascariensis

Marsdenia madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar star vine, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, star-shaped flowers. The plant features opposite, simple leaves that are often hairy, and its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow with a distinctive central boss. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.

The species is also of interest in botanical research due to its ecological role in its native habitat.
