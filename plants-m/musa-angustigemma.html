Musa angustigemma

Musa angustigemma, commonly known as the narrow-leaved banana, is a species within the Musaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically narrower than those of other banana species. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers, which are followed by elongated, curved fruits. Its growth habit is often upright, and it can reach heights of up to 3 meters.

This species is native to Southeast Asia and is cultivated for its ornamental value as well as for its edible fruits.
