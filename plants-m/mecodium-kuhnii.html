Mecodium kuhnii

Mecodium kuhnii, commonly known as the African mouse ear, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun. It is often found in arid or semi-arid regions, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.

Mecodium kuhnii is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
