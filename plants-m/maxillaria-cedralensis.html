Maxillaria cedralensis

Maxillaria cedralensis, commonly known as the Cedral orchid, is a species native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration, often featuring a mix of white, purple, and pink hues. Its leaves are typically elliptical and arranged in a compact rosette.

Maxillaria cedralensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
