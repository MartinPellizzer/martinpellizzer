Maxillaria torifera

Maxillaria torifera, commonly known as the wrinkled-leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It features a pseudobulb with a single, elongated leaf that has a distinctive wrinkled texture. The plant produces pendulous flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a velvety appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Maxillaria torifera is appreciated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate for orchid enthusiasts.
