Montbretia striata

Montbretia striata, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, sword-shaped leaves and produces showy, orange-red spike-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is characterized by its robust growth and ability to thrive in full sun and well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Montbretia striata is also known for its resilience and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
