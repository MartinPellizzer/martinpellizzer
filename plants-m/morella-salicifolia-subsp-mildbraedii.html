Morella salicifolia subsp. mildbraedii

Morella salicifolia subsp.

mildbraedii, commonly known as the mildbraed's waxmyrtle, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a whitish, waxy coating.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by dark blue to black berries. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts in its native habitat.
