Muscari hierosolymitanum

Muscari hierosolymitanum, commonly known as the Holy Land grape hyacinth, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, resembling grapes. The plant typically grows to a height of 10-20 cm and thrives in well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.

Muscari hierosolymitanum is a hardy perennial that blooms in early spring.
