Melocactus spatanginus

Melocactus spatanginus, commonly known as the spiny apple cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem covered in white, woolly spines and a dense cluster of small, spiny fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, adapting to arid environments with minimal water requirements. Its flowers, which are usually yellow or orange, bloom in the evening and are pollinated by nocturnal insects.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
