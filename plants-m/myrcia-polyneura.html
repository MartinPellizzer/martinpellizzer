Myrcia polyneura

Myrcia polyneura, commonly known as the silky myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in coastal heathlands and open forests. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. It produces woody capsules that contain numerous small, dark seeds.

Myrcia polyneura is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
