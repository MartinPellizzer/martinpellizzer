Melastoma hymenonervium

Melastoma hymenonervium, commonly known as the velvetleaf melastoma, is a flowering plant belonging to the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in clusters at the ends of branches. Its fruits are fleshy berries that contain several seeds, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

The species is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
