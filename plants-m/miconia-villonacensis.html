Miconia villonacensis

Miconia villonacensis, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It is often found in moist, lowland forests and can grow up to 15 meters in height.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a problematic species in certain regions.
