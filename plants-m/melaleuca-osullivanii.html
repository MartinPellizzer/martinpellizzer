Melaleuca osullivanii

Melaleuca osullivanii, commonly known as the Sullivan's tea tree, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, woody capsules that contain numerous small seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands.

Melaleuca osullivanii is valued for its ecological role and is also used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.
