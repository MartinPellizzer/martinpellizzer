Maireana oppositifolia

Maireana oppositifolia, commonly known as the slender maireana, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers, typically in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its foliage and floral structure make it an interesting subject for botanical study and ecological research.
