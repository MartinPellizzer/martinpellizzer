Mascarenhasia rosea

Mascarenhasia rosea, commonly known as the Mascarene rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mascarene Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy appearance.

The species is adapted to the tropical climate of its native habitat, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
