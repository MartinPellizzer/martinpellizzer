Manfreda rubescens

Manfreda rubescens, commonly known as the red manfreda, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge, especially under direct sunlight. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often blooming in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular ornamental plant in arid landscapes.
