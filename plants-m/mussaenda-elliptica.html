Mussaenda elliptica

Mussaenda elliptica, commonly known as the flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble small flames, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has simple, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Mussaenda elliptica is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
