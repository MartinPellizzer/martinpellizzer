Medinilla rubella

Medinilla rubella, commonly known as the red velvet orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Epidendraceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety red bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered within the colorful bracts. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Medinilla rubella is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to bloom multiple times throughout the year.
