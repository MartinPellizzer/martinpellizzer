Medinilla pubiflora

Medinilla pubiflora, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or velvet flower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, orange-yellow berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Medinilla pubiflora thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
