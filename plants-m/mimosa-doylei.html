Mimosa doylei

Mimosa doylei, commonly known as the Doyle's sensitive plant, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its root system is capable of fixing nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

Mimosa doylei is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique leaf response and adaptability to various growing conditions.
