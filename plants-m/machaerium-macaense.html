Machaerium macaense

Machaerium macaense, commonly known as the Brazilian burr or velvet grass, is a tropical perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and distinctive, bristly foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by long, slender pods containing small, hard seeds. Machaerium macaense is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It also has some traditional medicinal uses in indigenous communities.
