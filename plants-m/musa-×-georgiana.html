Musa × georgiana

Musa × georgiana, commonly known as the Chinese banana, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Musaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and clusters of yellow, edible fruits that resemble bananas. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as for its fruit.

The fruit is typically smaller and less sweet compared to the common banana, but it is still enjoyed in some regions.
