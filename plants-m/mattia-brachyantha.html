Mattia brachyantha

Mattia brachyantha, commonly known as the mountain daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and somewhat woody.

Mattia brachyantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
