Miconia fallacissima

Miconia fallacissima, commonly known as the false miconia, is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces showy, violet to purple flowers with five petals, often accompanied by numerous stamens. Its fruits are capsules that split open to release small, winged seeds, facilitating wind dispersal.

Miconia fallacissima is notable for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some areas.
