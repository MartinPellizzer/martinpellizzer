Melaleuca dealbata

Melaleuca dealbata, commonly known as the white melaleuca, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces showy white or pale pink flowers in clusters, which bloom throughout the year in some regions. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slight waxy coating, and it is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in saline environments.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world.
