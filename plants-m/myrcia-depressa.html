Myrcia depressa

Myrcia depressa, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, red flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature. Myrcia depressa is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in humid, shaded environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
