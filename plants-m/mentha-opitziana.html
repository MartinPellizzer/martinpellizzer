Mentha opitziana

Mentha opitziana, commonly known as the alpine mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its essential oils, which include menthone and menthol, contribute to its distinctive minty fragrance.

Mentha opitziana is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
