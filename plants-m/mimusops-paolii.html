Mimusops paolii

Mimusops paolii, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and glossy, ovate leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and durable wood, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural and commercial purposes.
