Morysia fasciculata

Morysia fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered morysia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a compact, bushy form. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
