Malcolmia contortuplicata

Malcolmia contortuplicata, commonly known as the twisted mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and tall, sparsely branched stems. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
