Mollia corymbosa

Mollia corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features erect, branching stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Mollia corymbosa is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
