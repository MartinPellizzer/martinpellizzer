Mentha mariae

Mentha mariae, commonly known as marsh mint, is a perennial herb native to wetland habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often near streams and ponds. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a strong, minty fragrance, and it produces small, pale blue or purple flowers.

Mentha mariae is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
