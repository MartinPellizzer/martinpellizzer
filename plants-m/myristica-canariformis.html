Myristica canariformis

Myristica canariformis, commonly known as the Canary Island nutmeg, is a member of the Myristicaceae family and is native to the Canary Islands.

It is a small evergreen tree that typically grows to a height of about 5 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that contain a single seed. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a glossy appearance.

The tree is valued for its aromatic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
