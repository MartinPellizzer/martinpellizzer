Marcgraviastrum subsessile

Marcgraviastrum subsessile, commonly known as the slender spider-plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, clustered inflorescences with delicate, white to pale green flowers. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant.
