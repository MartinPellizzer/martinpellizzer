Moniera trifoliata

Moniera trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaved fern, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in an inflorescence called a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Moniera trifoliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
