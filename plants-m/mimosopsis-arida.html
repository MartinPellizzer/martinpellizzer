Mimosopsis arida

Mimosopsis arida, commonly known as the desert mimosopsis, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. Its leaves are usually small and deciduous, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Mimosopsis arida is valued for its resilience and is sometimes used in landscaping for its ornamental and drought-tolerant qualities.
