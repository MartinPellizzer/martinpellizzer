Mediocalcar stenopetalum

Mediocalcar stenopetalum, commonly known as the narrow-petaled rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and specialized adaptations to rocky substrates. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers with narrow petals and sepals, typically in shades of white and green. Its roots are highly specialized for absorbing nutrients from poor soils, and it often grows in crevices or on rock faces.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
