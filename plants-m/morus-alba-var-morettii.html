Morus alba var. morettii

Morus alba var. morettii, commonly known as the Morettii mulberry, is a variant of the white mulberry native to southern China.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring a broad, rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. The leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture, while the small, aggregate fruits are typically white or pink in color. This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal.

It also serves as a host plant for the silkworm, Bombyx mori, making it ecologically significant.
