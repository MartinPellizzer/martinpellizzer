Malveopsis orbignyana

Malveopsis orbignyana, commonly known as the orbignyana mallows, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, with five petals. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
