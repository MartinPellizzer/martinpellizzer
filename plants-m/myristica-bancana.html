Myristica bancana

Myristica bancana, commonly known as the Bornean nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is closely related to the nutmeg tree (Myristica fragrans). The plant produces distinctive red, fleshy fruits that contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, while its bark is thick and dark brown.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and its potential medicinal properties.
