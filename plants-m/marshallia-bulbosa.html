Marshallia bulbosa

Marshallia bulbosa, commonly known as the pink coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant has a short, herbaceous stem with lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Marshallia bulbosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is a member of the sunflower family, Asteraceae.
