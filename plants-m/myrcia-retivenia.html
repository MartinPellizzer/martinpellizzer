Myrcia retivenia

Myrcia retivenia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are often white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a small, red, fleshy drupe that attracts various wildlife.

Myrcia retivenia is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
