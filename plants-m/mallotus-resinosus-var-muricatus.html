Mallotus resinosus var. muricatus

Mallotus resinosus var. muricatus, commonly known as the rough-leaved mallot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are covered in small, rough protrusions, giving it a textured appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
