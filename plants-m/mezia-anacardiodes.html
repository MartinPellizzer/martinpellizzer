Mezia anacardiodes

Mezia anacardiodes, commonly known as the Mexican cashew or wild cashew, is a small, deciduous shrub native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants like the poison ivy and mango. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and red, berry-like fruits that contain a single seed. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
