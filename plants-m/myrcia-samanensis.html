Myrcia samanensis

Myrcia samanensis, commonly known as the red sandalwood tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and distinctive red or pink flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in local medicine for their aromatic and therapeutic properties.

The tree is also valued for its durable, heavy wood, which has been historically used in construction and craftsmanship.
