Mammillaria bucareliensis var. bicornuta

Mammillaria bucareliensis var. bicornuta, commonly known as the two-horned mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
