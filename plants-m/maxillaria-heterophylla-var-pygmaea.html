Maxillaria heterophylla var. pygmaea

Maxillaria heterophylla var. pygmaea, commonly known as the miniature maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, compact growth habit and produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms during the summer months and prefers bright, indirect light with moderate humidity. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-draining potting mix.

This variety is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
