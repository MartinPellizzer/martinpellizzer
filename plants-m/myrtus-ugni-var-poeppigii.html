Myrtus ugni var. poeppigii

Myrtus ugni var. poeppigii, commonly known as the Mexican myrtle or Poeppig's myrtle, is a shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in warm climates.
