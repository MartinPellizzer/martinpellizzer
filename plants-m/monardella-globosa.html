Monardella globosa

Monardella globosa, commonly known as globeflower, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is characterized by its spherical, spiky flower heads that resemble small globes, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in chaparral and coastal sage scrub habitats.

Monardella globosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also an important source of nectar for pollinators.
