Moenchia mantica var. bulgarica

Moenchia mantica var. bulgarica, commonly known as the Bulgarian moenchia, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. The plant features slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in spike-like inflorescences and are usually pink to purple in color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
