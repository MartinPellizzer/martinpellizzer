Macadamia neurophylla

Macadamia neurophylla, commonly known as the coastal macadamia, is a species native to eastern Australia.

It is a small evergreen tree that typically grows in coastal regions with sandy soils. The plant has leathery, elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with yellow stamens that give way to woody pods containing the edible seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in coastal environments.
