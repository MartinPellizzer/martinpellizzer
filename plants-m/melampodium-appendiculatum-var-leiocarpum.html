Melampodium appendiculatum var. leiocarpum

Melampodium appendiculatum var. leiocarpum, commonly known as the golden tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in sandy or well-drained soils, making it a common sight in prairies and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
