Mimulus nasutus var. micranthus

Mimulus nasutus var. micranthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved monkeyflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with five petals, which bloom from spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in streambanks and woodland edges.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
