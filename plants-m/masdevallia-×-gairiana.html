Masdevallia × gairiana

Masdevallia × gairiana, commonly known as the hybrid masdevallia, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Masdevallia lepidota and Masdevallia tenuifolia.

It is native to the Andes Mountains of South America, where it thrives in cool, humid environments with high altitude. The plant is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that come in a variety of hues, including red, orange, and yellow. It has linear, grass-like leaves and produces inflorescences that bear numerous small, showy blooms.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and unique floral structure.
