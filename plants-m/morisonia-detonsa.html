Morisonia detonsa

Morisonia detonsa, commonly known as the seaside dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellow flowers with a distinctive, elongated shape. Morisonia detonsa is adapted to salt spray and can tolerate periodic flooding, making it a hardy species in coastal environments.

Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
