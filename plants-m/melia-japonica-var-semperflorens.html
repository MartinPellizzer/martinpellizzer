Melia japonica var. semperflorens

Melia japonica var. semperflorens, commonly known as the Japanese silk tree or summer lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to provide shade in gardens.
