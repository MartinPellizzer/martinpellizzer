Malus domestica subsp. medioasiatica

Malus domestica subsp.

medioasiatica, commonly known as the Central Asian apple, is a subspecies native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its medium-sized, rounded leaves and white flowers that bloom in spring.

The fruit is typically small to medium in size, with a firm texture and a sweet to tart flavor. This subspecies is valued for its cold hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.

It is often used in breeding programs to improve the resilience of cultivated apple varieties.
