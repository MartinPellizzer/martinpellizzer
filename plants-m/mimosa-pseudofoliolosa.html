Mimosa pseudofoliolosa

Mimosa pseudofoliolosa, commonly known as the laceleaf mimosa, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate, lacey appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spherical clusters, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Mimosa pseudofoliolosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
