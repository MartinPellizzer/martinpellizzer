Maxillaria xantholeuca var. peruviana

Maxillaria xantholeuca var. peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian yellow orchid, is a hybrid orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers with contrasting dark green petals and sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its pseudobulbs are cylindrical, and it produces multiple racemes of fragrant blooms during the growing season.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
