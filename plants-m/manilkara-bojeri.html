Manilkara bojeri

Manilkara bojeri, commonly known as the Bojer's sapodilla, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces a fruit with a thick, sweet pulp that is often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Its latex contains compounds that have been studied for their potential pharmacological properties.

The plant is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
