Matthiola membranacea

Matthiola membranacea, commonly known as the membranous stock, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other well-known garden plants. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Matthiola membranacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
