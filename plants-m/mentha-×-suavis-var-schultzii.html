Mentha × suavis var. schultzii

Mentha × suavis var. schultzii, commonly known as Schultze mint, is a hybrid species within the Mentha genus.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves that emit a distinctive mint-like scent. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a textured surface, contributing to its ornamental value.

The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
