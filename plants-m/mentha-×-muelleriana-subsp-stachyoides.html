Mentha × muelleriana subsp. stachyoides

Mentha × muelleriana subsp.

stachyoides, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a hybrid species within the mint family, Lamiaceae. It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and compact, mound-like growth habit.

The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This subspecies is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
