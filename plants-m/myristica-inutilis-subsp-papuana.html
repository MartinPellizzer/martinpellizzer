Myristica inutilis subsp. papuana

Myristica inutilis subsp.

papuana, commonly known as the Papuan nutmeg, is a member of the Myristicaceae family and is native to the island of New Guinea. It is a small evergreen tree characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant, reddish-brown fruits.

The plant produces a unique seed with a hard, aromatic kernel that is highly valued for its essential oils and medicinal properties. It thrives in tropical rainforest environments, often growing in shaded, moist conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
