Microloma penicillatum

Microloma penicillatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf panicled grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, often forming dense tussocks. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities in its range.
