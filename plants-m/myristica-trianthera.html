Myristica trianthera

Myristica trianthera, commonly known as the nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Banda Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody structure and compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits containing the aromatic seeds used as spices. Its bark and leaves also contain essential oils and have been used in traditional medicine.

Myristica trianthera is valued not only for its economic importance in the spice trade but also for its ecological role in tropical forests.
