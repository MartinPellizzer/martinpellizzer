Molucca

Molucca, commonly known as the "Spice Islands," is a group of islands in Indonesia renowned for their rich botanical diversity.

The region is home to numerous endemic plant species, including various species of nutmeg, cloves, and cinnamon, which have been cultivated for centuries. The tropical climate supports a wide range of flora, from dense rainforests to coastal vegetation. Many of these plants have significant economic and cultural importance, particularly in traditional medicine and spice trade.

The biodiversity of Molucca contributes to its status as a hotspot for botanical research and conservation.
