Meriolix berlandieri

Meriolix berlandieri, commonly known as the Mexican goldenbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, golden-yellow foliage that resembles a bushy crown. The plant produces small, composite flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It is adapted to hot, dry climates and has a deep root system for water storage.

Meriolix berlandieri is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
