Morus norwegica

Morus norwegica, commonly known as the Norwegian mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to northern Europe.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, fertile soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, while its leaves are usually dark green on top and pale underneath.

The Norwegian mulberry is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
