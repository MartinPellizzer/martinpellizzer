Mammillaria guerkeana

Mammillaria guerkeana, commonly known as the Guerke's mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and dense covering of white to pale yellow tubercles. The plant typically grows to a height of 5 to 10 centimeters and produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in arid gardens and rockeries.
