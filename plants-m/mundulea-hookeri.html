Mundulea hookeri

Mundulea hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions. It plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.

Its seeds are often dispersed by animals, contributing to its ecological significance in the region.
