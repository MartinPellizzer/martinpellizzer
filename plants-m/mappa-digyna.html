Mappa digyna

Mappa digyna, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aromatic properties. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, winged seeds.

Due to overharvesting, Mappa digyna is now considered an endangered species in its natural habitat.
