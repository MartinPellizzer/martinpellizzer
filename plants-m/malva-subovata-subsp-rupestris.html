Malva subovata subsp. rupestris

Malva subovata subsp.

rupestris, commonly known as the rock mallows, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its hairy, broad leaves and showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing on rocky or gravelly slopes. Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it useful in erosion control.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
