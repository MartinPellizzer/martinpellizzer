Monoon

Monoon, commonly known as the "pomegranate tree," is a tropical plant belonging to the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that eventually develop into red, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Monoon is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its edible fruits.
