Melica persica var. caspica

Melica persica var. caspica, commonly known as Persian millet, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. The plant features slender, tufted stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens.
