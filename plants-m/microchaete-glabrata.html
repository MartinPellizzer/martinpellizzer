Microchaete glabrata

Microchaete glabrata, commonly known as the smooth hairless grass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to various regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically smooth and hairless, as indicated by its scientific name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
