Mentha hirsuta f. acutifolia

Mentha hirsuta f. acutifolia, commonly known as the acute-leaved hairy mint, is a variant of the hairy mint species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and hairy, gray-green leaves with acute tips. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

This cultivar is valued for its aromatic leaves and ornamental appeal in horticultural settings.
