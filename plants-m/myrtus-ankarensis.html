Myrtus ankarensis

Myrtus ankarensis, commonly known as the Ethiopian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the highlands of Ethiopia and is characterized by its aromatic evergreen leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Myrtus ankarensis thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and ecosystem.
