Myrsine urceolata

Myrsine urceolata, commonly known as the waterleaf, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often glossy and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by berry-like fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. Myrsine urceolata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
