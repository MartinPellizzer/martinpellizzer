Maerua decandra

Maerua decandra, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, woody stems and dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces edible fig-like fruits that are an important food source for both wildlife and humans. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with minimal water, featuring succulent leaves and deep root systems.

Maerua decandra also plays a role in soil stabilization and is used in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
