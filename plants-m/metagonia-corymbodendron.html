Metagonia corymbodendron

Metagonia corymbodendron, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in dense clusters, creating a vivid display during the flowering season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
