Mentha longifolia var. acuminata

Mentha longifolia var. acuminata, commonly known as the acuminata mint, is a variant of the longleaf mint species.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and a distinct, sharp aroma. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowering stems produce small, pale blue or purple flowers, which are often clustered in whorls.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies and culinary applications.
