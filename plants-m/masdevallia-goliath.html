Masdevallia goliath

Masdevallia goliath, commonly known as the giant masdevallia, is a striking orchid native to the high-altitude cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, making it one of the largest orchids in the world. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic growth habit. Its foliage consists of long, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

The flowers are usually violet or purple in color, with intricate patterns that attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.
