Mascagnia cordifolia var. typica

Mascagnia cordifolia var. typica, commonly known as the heartleaf mascagnia, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
