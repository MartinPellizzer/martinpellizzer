Magnolia hernandezii

Magnolia hernandezii, commonly known as the Mexican magnolia, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, featuring broad, elliptical leaves. The plant produces large, showy white or pink flowers in early spring, which are borne on long, pendulous inflorescences. Its flowers are fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and moths.

The bark is smooth and grayish, and the tree is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
