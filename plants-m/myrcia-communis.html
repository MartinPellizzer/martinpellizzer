Myrcia communis

Myrcia communis, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and often have a fragrant scent.

The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains several seeds.
