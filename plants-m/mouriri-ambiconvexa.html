Mouriri ambiconvexa

Mouriri ambiconvexa, commonly known as the Brazilian wax tree, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on both sides. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters.

Mouriri ambiconvexa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
