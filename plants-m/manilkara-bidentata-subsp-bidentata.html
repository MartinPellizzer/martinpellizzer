Manilkara bidentata subsp. bidentata

Manilkara bidentata subsp.

bidentata, commonly known as the Amazonian sapodilla, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves and produces a yellowish, sweet-smelling fruit with a gritty texture.

The plant belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is known for its high latex content, which is used in various traditional applications. Its bark and leaves have also been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.

The tree thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
