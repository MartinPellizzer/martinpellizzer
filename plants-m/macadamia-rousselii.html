Macadamia rousselii

Macadamia rousselii, commonly known as the rousselii macadamia, is a species native to New Caledonia.

It is a small evergreen tree that typically grows to a height of about 10 meters. The plant produces large, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, and the fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
