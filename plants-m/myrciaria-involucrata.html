Myrciaria involucrata

Myrciaria involucrata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *cereja-brasileira*, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces bright red, cherry-like fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
