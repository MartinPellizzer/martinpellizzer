Medicago sativa nothosubsp. faurei

Medicago sativa nothosubsp.

faurei, commonly known as the Faure's alfalfa, is a hybrid subspecies of the common alfalfa. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by oblong, dehiscent pods containing several seeds. It is adapted to temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important species in agricultural and ecological contexts.
