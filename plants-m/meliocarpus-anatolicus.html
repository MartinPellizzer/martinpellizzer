Meliocarpus anatolicus

Meliocarpus anatolicus, commonly known as Anatolian sweetgum, is a member of the Burseraceae family and is native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in dry, rocky soils. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and opposite, simple, ovate leaves. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several winged seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
