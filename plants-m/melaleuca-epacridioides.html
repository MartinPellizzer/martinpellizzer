Melaleuca epacridioides

Melaleuca epacridioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, particularly in the coastal regions of New South Wales and Queensland. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect shrub form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. Its small, fragrant white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by cylindrical, woody fruits.

This species is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
