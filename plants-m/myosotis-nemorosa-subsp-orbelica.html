Myosotis nemorosa subsp. orbelica

Myosotis nemorosa subsp.

orbelica, commonly known as the forest forget-me-not, is a flowering plant native to the forests of Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are slender and often hairy.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
