Mammillaria schelhasii var. rosea

Mammillaria schelhasii var. rosea, commonly known as the roseate mammillaria, is a compact cactus native to Mexico.

It features a globular stem covered in white to pinkish tubercles, which are adorned with small, spiny areoles. The plant produces pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well-suited for rock gardens and requires bright sunlight and minimal water.

Due to its delicate appearance, it is often cultivated as a prized ornamental cactus.
