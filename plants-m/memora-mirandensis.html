Memora mirandensis

Memora mirandensis, commonly known as the Mirandese Memory Plant, is a rare flowering species native to the region of Miranda in Portugal.

It is characterized by its delicate, lavender-colored blooms that appear in late spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along its stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is considered a botanical curiosity and is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
