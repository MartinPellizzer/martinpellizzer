Myrtiluma eugeniifolia

Myrtiluma eugeniifolia, commonly known as the purple-flowered myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known genera such as Eucalyptus and Syzygium. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant, purple flowers and glossy, dark green leaves. It typically grows in humid, tropical environments and thrives in well-drained soil.

Myrtiluma eugeniifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
