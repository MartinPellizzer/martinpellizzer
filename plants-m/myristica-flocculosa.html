Myristica flocculosa

Myristica flocculosa, commonly known as the fluffseed tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces unique, fleshy fruits that contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and perfumery. Its botanical features include thick, leathery leaves and a compact, bushy growth habit.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
