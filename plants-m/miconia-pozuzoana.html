Miconia pozuzoana

Miconia pozuzoana, commonly known as the Pozuzo miconia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are typically dark green and glossy in appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the wet season. It is often found in humid, lowland forests and thrives in areas with high rainfall and warm temperatures.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
