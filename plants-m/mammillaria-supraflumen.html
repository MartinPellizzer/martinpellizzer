Mammillaria supraflumen

Mammillaria supraflumen, commonly known as the Flumen mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is covered in small, white tubercles that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne on the upper part of the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure to flourish.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species within the Cactaceae family.
