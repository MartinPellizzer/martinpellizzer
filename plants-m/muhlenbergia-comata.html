Muhlenbergia comata

Muhlenbergia comata, commonly known as silverhair grass, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray blades and fluffy, white plumes that appear in late summer. This plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, meadows, and disturbed soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy ornamental choice.

The plant's ecological value includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
