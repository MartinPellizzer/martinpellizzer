Miconia arboricola

Miconia arboricola, commonly known as the evergreen miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that are often heart-shaped or elliptical in form. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Miconia arboricola is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in some regions due to its aggressive spread and ecological impact.
