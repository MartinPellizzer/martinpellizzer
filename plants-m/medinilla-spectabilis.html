Medinilla spectabilis

Medinilla spectabilis, commonly known as the velvet tangerine, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and striking, fragrant, rose-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The flowers are typically pink or red, with a velvety texture, and are highly ornamental. The plant produces small, round, yellow fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor spaces.
