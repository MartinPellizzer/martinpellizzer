Malachra triloba

Malachra triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family, which includes many milkweeds and related species. The plant is characterized by its opposite, three-lobed leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. It produces a milky sap that is typical of the Apocynaceae family, which can be toxic.

Malachra triloba is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
