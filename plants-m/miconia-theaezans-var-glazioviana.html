Miconia theaezans var. glazioviana

Miconia theaezans var. glazioviana, commonly known as the Glaziovian miconia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in inflorescences and are usually white or pale pink, with showy bracts.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
