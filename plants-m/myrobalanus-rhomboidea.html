Myrobalanus rhomboidea

Myrobalanus rhomboidea, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a small, orange-red drupe that ripens in the autumn.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
