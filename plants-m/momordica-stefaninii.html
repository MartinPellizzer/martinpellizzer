Momordica stefaninii

Momordica stefaninii, commonly known as the Stefanini bitter melon, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and watermelons. The plant produces elongated, warty fruits that are typically green when mature, though they may turn orange or yellow. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its edible fruits, which have a bitter taste and are used in various traditional cuisines.
