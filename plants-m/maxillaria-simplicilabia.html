Maxillaria simplicilabia

Maxillaria simplicilabia, commonly known as the simple-lipped maxillaria, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a distinctive lip. Its roots are fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soil with moderate moisture.

It is a popular choice for collectors due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
