Malus elongata var. pubens

Malus elongata var. pubens, commonly known as the pubescent apple tree, is a variant of the apple tree native to China.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender fruiting branches and hairy, pubescent foliage. The leaves are oval-shaped with a serrated margin and a rough, textured surface. The flowers are white with prominent yellow stamens, and the fruit is typically small, sweet, and edible.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional Chinese horticulture.
