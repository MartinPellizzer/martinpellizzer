Mimosopsis lindheimeri

Mimosopsis lindheimeri, commonly known as Lindheimer's mimosopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and often nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub with slender, wiry stems and small, fern-like leaves. Its flowers are clustered in racemes and are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, attracting various pollinators.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
