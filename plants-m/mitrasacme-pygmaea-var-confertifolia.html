Mitrasacme pygmaea var. confertifolia

Mitrasacme pygmaea var. confertifolia, commonly known as the dense-leaved mitrasacme, is a small shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, with leaves that are typically narrow and arranged in whorls along the stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered at the base of the leaves. Its botanical features include hairy stems and a preference for moist, shaded environments.

It is often found in humid, lowland forests where it contributes to the understory vegetation.
