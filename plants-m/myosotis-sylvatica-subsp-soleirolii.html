Myosotis sylvatica subsp. soleirolii

Myosotis sylvatica subsp.

soleirolii, commonly known as the Italian forget-me-not, is a variant of the common forget-me-not. It is characterized by its smaller, more delicate blue or purple flowers compared to the typical species.

This subspecies is native to southern Europe and thrives in moist, shady environments. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are typically hairy.

The plant is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is noted for its ability to attract pollinators.
