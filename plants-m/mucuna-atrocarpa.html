Mucuna atrocarpa

Mucuna atrocarpa, commonly known as black cow pea, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features trifoliate leaves with dark green foliage and produces small, elongated pods containing black or brown seeds. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and is often found in humid, shaded environments. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.

Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein in some cultures.
