Mentha × schleicheri

Mentha × schleicheri, commonly known as the German peppermint, is a hybrid mint species that results from the cross between Mentha piperita and Mentha spicata.

It is characterized by its strong menthol scent and is widely cultivated for its aromatic oils. The plant typically grows as an upright herb with lance-shaped leaves and clusters of pale purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Mentha × schleicheri is often used in herbal remedies and as a flavoring agent in food and beverages.
