Myrcia ochraciflora

Myrcia ochraciflora, commonly known as the red-flowered myrcia, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat. Its leaves are typically elliptical and glossy, with a strong aromatic scent.

Myrcia ochraciflora is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical rainforests.
