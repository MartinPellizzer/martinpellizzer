Melastoma echinatum

Melastoma echinatum, commonly known as the rough-stemmed melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The fruit is a capsule that disperses seeds through wind and animal activity.
