Machilus cambodianus

Machilus cambodianus, commonly known as the Cambodian laurel, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, spherical drupe that turns from green to reddish when mature.

Machilus cambodianus is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
