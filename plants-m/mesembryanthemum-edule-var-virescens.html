Mesembryanthemum edule var. virescens

Mesembryanthemum edule var. virescens, commonly known as the green-flowered mealworm plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves to survive drought conditions. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.

The leaves are sometimes used in traditional cuisine, though its primary value lies in its horticultural appeal.
