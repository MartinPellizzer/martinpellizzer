Mimusops capuronii var. salicifolia

Mimusops capuronii var. salicifolia, commonly known as the silky tamarind, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, glossy leaves that give it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. Its bark is smooth and grayish, often exuding a milky sap when damaged.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
