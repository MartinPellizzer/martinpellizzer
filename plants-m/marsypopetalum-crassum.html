Marsypopetalum crassum

Marsypopetalum crassum, commonly known as the thick-leafed milkweed, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its stems are often hairy and can grow up to several feet in length.

Marsypopetalum crassum is valued for its resilience and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
