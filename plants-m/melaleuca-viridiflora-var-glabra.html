Melaleuca viridiflora var. glabra

Melaleuca viridiflora var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth greenflower melaleuca, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This species is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand drought and salt spray.
