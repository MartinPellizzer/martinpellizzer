Maxillaria chrysantha var. pallidiflora

Maxillaria chrysantha var. pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically broad and elliptical, arranged in two rows along the stem. The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant prefers warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

It is a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
