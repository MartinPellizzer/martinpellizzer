Myrtoleucodendron globiferum

Myrtoleucodendron globiferum, commonly known as the globeflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, globe-shaped inflorescences composed of numerous small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, thriving in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited geographic distribution.
