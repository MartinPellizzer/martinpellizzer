Mabea gaudichaudiana

Mabea gaudichaudiana, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Mabea gaudichaudiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
