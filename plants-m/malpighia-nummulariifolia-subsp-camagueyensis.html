Malpighia nummulariifolia subsp. camagueyensis

Malpighia nummulariifolia subsp.

camagueyensis, commonly known as the Cuban wax apple, is a small evergreen shrub native to Cuba. It is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, red, fleshy fruits that resemble tiny apples.

The plant belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is known for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage and edible berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

The fruit is often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
