Macaranga maingayi

Macaranga maingayi, commonly known as the white-veined macaranga, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent white veins, which are a distinctive botanical feature. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its rapid growth rate. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This species is often found in secondary forests and is valued for its timber and medicinal properties.
