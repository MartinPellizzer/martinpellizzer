Malcolmia ciliaris

Malcolmia ciliaris, commonly known as the silverleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and showy, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in dry, rocky areas and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Malcolmia ciliaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
