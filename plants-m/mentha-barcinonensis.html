Mentha barcinonensis

Mentha barcinonensis, commonly known as the Barcino mint, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale purple or blue, arranged in whorls along the stems. Mentha barcinonensis is known for its aromatic leaves, which emit a strong mint scent when crushed.

It prefers moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
