Miconia cordata

Miconia cordata, commonly known as the "Brazilian angel's trumpets," is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, though the plant is considered invasive in some regions.

Miconia cordata is also notable for its ability to produce copious amounts of seeds, contributing to its spread.
