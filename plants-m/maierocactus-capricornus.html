Maierocactus capricornus

Maierocactus capricornus, commonly known as the Capricorn Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive ribbed structure and spines that provide protection from herbivores and extreme weather. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its succulent stems store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry environments.

The Capricorn Cactus is well adapted to desert conditions, making it a resilient and visually striking addition to xeriscaped gardens.
