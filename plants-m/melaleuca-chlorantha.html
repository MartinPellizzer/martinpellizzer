Melaleuca chlorantha

Melaleuca chlorantha, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, particularly the coastal regions of New South Wales and Queensland. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white powdery coating. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences.

The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in coastal environments.
