Myxopappus acutilobus

Myxopappus acutilobus, commonly known as the sharp-lobed myxopappus, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and dense, hairy foliage that helps reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its leaves are narrow and have acute lobes, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Myxopappus acutilobus is adapted to survive in low-nutrient soils and can withstand extreme temperature fluctuations.
