Miliusa indica

Miliusa indica, commonly known as the Java apple or rambutan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, spiny fruit covering, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by the fleshy, sweet-tart fruit. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a glossy surface.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and its edible fruit, which is rich in nutrients and has a unique flavor.
