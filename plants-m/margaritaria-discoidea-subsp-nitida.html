Margaritaria discoidea subsp. nitida

Margaritaria discoidea subsp.

nitida, commonly known as the glossy leaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on a variety of host trees. It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a waxy coating.

The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are spherical berries that turn red when mature, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is native to parts of Africa and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
