Macropodanthus rimauensis

Macropodanthus rimauensis, commonly known as the Rimau mountain morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of Papua New Guinea.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers, which bloom in the early morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in forest understories.

Its unique flowering pattern and habitat make it a rare and ecologically significant species in its region.
