Mammillaria inuncta

Mammillaria inuncta, commonly known as the white-tipped pincushion cactus, is a small, globular cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, white spines that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of about 5 to 10 centimeters and has a compact, rounded shape. It produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months.

Mammillaria inuncta is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
