Mentha × verticillata var. tortuosa

Mentha × verticillata var. tortuosa, commonly known as the spiral mint, is a hybrid species of mint native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive whorled arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

Its aromatic leaves are used in traditional herbal remedies and have a mild mint flavor.
