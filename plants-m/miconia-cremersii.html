Miconia cremersii

Miconia cremersii, commonly known as the yellow miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by birds and other animals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
