Momordica surculata

Momordica surculata, commonly known as the hairy bitter gourd, is a tropical vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its spiny stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces elongated, warty fruits that are typically harvested when immature and tender. Its fruit contains a mild, slightly bitter flavor and is commonly used in Asian cuisines.

Botanically, it is notable for its unique morphology and adaptability to various tropical climates.
