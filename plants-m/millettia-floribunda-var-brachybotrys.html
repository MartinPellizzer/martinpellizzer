Millettia floribunda var. brachybotrys

Millettia floribunda var. brachybotrys, commonly known as the short-stalked millettia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces legume-like pods that contain seeds used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and open woodlands.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it of interest in both horticulture and pharmacological research.
