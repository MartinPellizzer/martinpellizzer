Mentha × latissima

Mentha × latissima, commonly known as broad-leaf mint, is a hybrid mint species resulting from the cross between Mentha × spicata and Mentha × piperita.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Mentha × latissima is valued for its aromatic leaves used in culinary and medicinal applications.
