Medinilla coronata

Medinilla coronata, commonly known as the crownmedinilla, is a tropical shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round, fleshy fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. Medinilla coronata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

It prefers warm, humid conditions and is relatively low maintenance once established.
