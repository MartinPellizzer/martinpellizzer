Mentha viridis var. lampreilema

Mentha viridis var. lampreilema, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mint, is a variant of the green mint species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in herbal teas and culinary applications.
