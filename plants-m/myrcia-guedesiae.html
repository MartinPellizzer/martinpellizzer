Myrcia guedesiae

Myrcia guedesiae, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

Myrcia guedesiae is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and ability to withstand partial shade.
