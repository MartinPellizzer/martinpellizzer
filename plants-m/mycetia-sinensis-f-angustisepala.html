Mycetia sinensis f. angustisepala

Mycetia sinensis f. angustisepala, commonly known as the narrow-lobed mycetia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets. The flowers are typically small, clustered in racemes, and display a pale yellow to greenish color. This plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
