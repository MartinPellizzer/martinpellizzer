Merope caespititia

Merope caespititia, commonly known as the clustered merope, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and forms dense, low-growing clumps of foliage. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale blue or purple flowers during the summer months. It is often found in disturbed habitats and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Merope caespititia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
