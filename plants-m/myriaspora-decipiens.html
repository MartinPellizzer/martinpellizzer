Myriaspora decipiens

Myriaspora decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive spore plant, is a unique fungal species that exhibits complex spore dispersal mechanisms.

It belongs to the class Dothideomycetes and is characterized by its intricate thallus structure. The plant produces a large number of small, spore-bearing structures called pycnidia, which are often concealed within its leaf-like lobes. Its spores are released in a deceptive manner, mimicking the appearance of other plant structures to avoid detection by potential predators.

This adaptation allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments where it can efficiently colonize new substrates.
