Masdevallia pescadoensis

Masdevallia pescadoensis, commonly known as the Pescadoensis orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces intricate, often colorful flowers with a distinctive shape, typically in shades of pink, red, and white. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
