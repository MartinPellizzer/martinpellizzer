Mammillaria flava

Mammillaria flava, commonly known as the yellow pincushion cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem covered in white to yellow tubercles, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its greenish-gray body. It is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation due to its compact size and low maintenance requirements.

Mammillaria flava is also valued for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.
