Megalastrum peregrinum

Megalastrum peregrinum, commonly known as the wandering starflower, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped blooms that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are broad and lobed, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Megalastrum peregrinum is known for its short growing season and its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich soils.
