Melaleuca strobophylla

Melaleuca strobophylla, commonly known as the strobophylla melaleuca, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to northern Australia, particularly Queensland and the Northern Territory, where it grows in coastal and wetland areas. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its bark is smooth and often peels in thin layers, revealing a mottled appearance.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for various insects and birds.
