Malva × columbretensis

Malva × columbretensis, commonly known as the Persian mullein, is a hybrid plant that belongs to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, velvety, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pale pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Native to regions of the Mediterranean and parts of Central Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported soothing properties.

Its fibrous stems and leaves contain mucilage, which contributes to its historical use in respiratory remedies.
