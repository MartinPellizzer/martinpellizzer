Mila nealeana

Mila nealeana, commonly known as the Neale's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its milky sap, which is a common feature among plants in this family. The plant typically features opposite leaves that are oval or lance-shaped with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are usually clustered in umbels and can be pink, white, or yellow, attracting various pollinators.

Mila nealeana is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or shrubland habitats.
