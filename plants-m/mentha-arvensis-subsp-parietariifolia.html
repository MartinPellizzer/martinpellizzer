Mentha arvensis subsp. parietariifolia

Mentha arvensis subsp.

parietariifolia, commonly known as wall mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin.

The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.
