Masdevallia pantherina

Masdevallia pantherina, commonly known as the panther orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety petals that often display intricate patterns resembling a panther's coat. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial or epiphytic orchid, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small to medium-sized, with a complex structure that includes a labellum, which plays a role in pollination.

Masdevallia pantherina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and rarity in the wild.
