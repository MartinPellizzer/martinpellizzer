Malesherbia cuneata

Malesherbia cuneata, commonly known as the narrowleaf malesherbia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malvaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive bilaterally symmetrical structure. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
