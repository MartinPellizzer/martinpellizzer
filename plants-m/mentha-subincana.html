Mentha subincana

Mentha subincana, commonly known as the downy mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and square stems, typical of the mint family (Lamiaceae). The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Mentha subincana is often found in rocky soils and dry habitats, thriving in full sun to partial shade.

Its aromatic leaves are used in traditional herbal medicine and as a flavoring agent.
