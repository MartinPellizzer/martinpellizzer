Mariscus cyperiformis

Mariscus cyperiformis, commonly known as the swamp arrowgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist to saturated soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes. Its root system is fibrous and helps stabilize the soil in aquatic habitats.

Mariscus cyperiformis plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
