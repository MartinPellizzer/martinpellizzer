Malus toringo subvar. sargentii

Malus toringo subvar. sargentii, commonly known as Sargent's crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy white to pink flowers in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and turn vibrant shades of red in autumn.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
