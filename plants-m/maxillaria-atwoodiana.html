Maxillaria atwoodiana

Maxillaria atwoodiana, commonly known as the Atwood's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces showy, violet or purple blooms with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. Its roots are fibrous, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils under shaded conditions.

Maxillaria atwoodiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a garden plant.
