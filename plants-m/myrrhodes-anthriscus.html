Myrrhodes anthriscus

Myrrhodes anthriscus, commonly known as the lesser cow-wheat, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and umbellate inflorescences.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features a slender, branched stem and pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in umbrella-like clusters, and it produces small, dry fruits called schizocarps.

The plant is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
