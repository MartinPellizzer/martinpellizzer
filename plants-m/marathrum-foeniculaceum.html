Marathrum foeniculaceum

Marathrum foeniculaceum, commonly known as the marsh fennel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its feathery, finely divided leaves and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in umbrella-like clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, marshy habitats and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. It has a taproot system and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Due to its aromatic compounds, it has been used historically in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
