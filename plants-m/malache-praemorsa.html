Malache praemorsa

Malache praemorsa, commonly known as the Death's-head hawkmoth, is a striking plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically pale green or yellow with a distinctive dark spot near the base. The plant thrives in warm, arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its flowers are primarily pollinated by moths, particularly the Death's-head hawkmoth, which is named after the plant.

The plant is noted for its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitats.
