Myconia multicaulis

Myconia multicaulis, commonly known as the clustered myconia, is a tropical shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often consumed by various bird species. Its leaves are typically ovate with a glossy surface and are arranged in opposite pairs along the stems.

Myconia multicaulis plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing food and shelter for wildlife.
