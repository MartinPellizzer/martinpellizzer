Myoporum platycarpum subsp. platycarpum

Myoporum platycarpum subsp.

platycarpum, commonly known as the broad-fruited myoporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and produces small, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant's fruit is a fleshy, berry-like structure that contains several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil. Myoporum platycarpum subsp.

platycarpum is also valued for its resilience to coastal conditions and salt spray.
