Medinilla setigera

Medinilla setigera, commonly known as the feathered medinilla, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a glossy surface and a prominent midrib.

Medinilla setigera is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
