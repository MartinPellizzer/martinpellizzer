Mystacidium nanum

Mystacidium nanum, commonly known as the dwarf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in the southeastern United States.

It has small, scale-like leaves and produces tiny, white or pale green berries that are toxic to mammals. The plant relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing some photosynthesis. It spreads through seeds dispersed by birds, which consume the berries.

Mystacidium nanum plays a role in forest ecosystems by influencing host tree growth and providing food for certain wildlife species.
