Mentha grisella subsp. caucasica

Mentha grisella subsp.

caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine for their cooling and antiseptic properties.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
