Microstylis caricoides

Microstylis caricoides, commonly known as the narrow-styled sedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female structures, a common feature in sedges.

Microstylis caricoides is often found in temperate regions and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
