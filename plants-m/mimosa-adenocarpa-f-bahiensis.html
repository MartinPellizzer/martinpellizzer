Mimosa adenocarpa f. bahiensis

Mimosa adenocarpa f. bahiensis, commonly known as the Bahia mimosa, is a shrub native to the northeastern regions of Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, rounded leaflets that fold together when touched, a phenomenon known as nyctinasty. It produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by hairy, elongated pods containing edible seeds.

This species is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry forests.
