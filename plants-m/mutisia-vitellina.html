Mutisia vitellina

Mutisia vitellina, commonly known as the yellow mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, making it visually striking. It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in open woodlands or along stream banks.

The leaves are usually alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.
