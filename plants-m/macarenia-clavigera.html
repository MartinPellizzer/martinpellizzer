Macarenia clavigera

Macarenia clavigera, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its segmented, branching stems and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in winter. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. It produces flowers in shades of red, pink, orange, and white, often in a cluster at the ends of its stems.

Macarenia clavigera is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
