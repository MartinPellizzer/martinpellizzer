Mammillaria multiceps var. grisea

Mammillaria multiceps var. grisea, commonly known as the gray-hairy mammillaria, is a succulent cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, grayish-white spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for succulent collectors.
