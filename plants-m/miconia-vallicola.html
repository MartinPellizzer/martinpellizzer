Miconia vallicola

Miconia vallicola, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are fleshy berries that contain numerous small seeds, facilitating seed dispersal by animals.

Miconia vallicola is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories.
