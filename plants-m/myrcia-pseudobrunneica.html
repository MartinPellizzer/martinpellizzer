Myrcia pseudobrunneica

Myrcia pseudobrunneica, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which are often followed by small, round berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in forested areas.

Myrcia pseudobrunneica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
