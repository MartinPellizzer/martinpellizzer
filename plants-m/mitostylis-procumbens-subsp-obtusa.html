Mitostylis procumbens subsp. obtusa

Mitostylis procumbens subsp.

obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leaved mitostylis, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to South America. It features ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy on the underside, with a bluish-green coloration.

The flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and typically have five petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

The subspecies obtusa is distinguished by its broader leaves and shorter floral stems compared to other subspecies.
