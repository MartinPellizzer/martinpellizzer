Mesembryanthemum pachyphyllum

Mesembryanthemum pachyphyllum, commonly known as the fat plant or stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the summer months. It is a member of the Aizoaceae family, known for its adaptations to dry climates.

Mesembryanthemum pachyphyllum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
