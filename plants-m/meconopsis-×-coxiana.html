Meconopsis × coxiana

Meconopsis × coxiana, commonly known as the Himalayan blue poppy, is a hybrid species native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet petals and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
