Metroxylon bougainvillense

Metroxylon bougainvillense, commonly known as the areca palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery, compound leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by edible fruits. Its roots are fibrous and can be used in traditional crafts.

The areca palm is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of natural fibers.
