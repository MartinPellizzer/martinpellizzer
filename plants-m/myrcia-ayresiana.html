Myrcia ayresiana

Myrcia ayresiana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are edible for some bird species but toxic to humans. It is often found in moist, tropical environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, posing challenges to native ecosystems.
