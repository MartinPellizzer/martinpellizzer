Manihot teissonnieri

Manihot teissonnieri, commonly known as the African arrowroot, is a tuberous plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its underground stems that produce edible tubers. The plant typically grows in wet, swampy areas and thrives in humid climates. Its tubers are starchy and can be used as a food source, similar to cassava.

However, the plant contains toxic compounds that require proper processing before consumption.
