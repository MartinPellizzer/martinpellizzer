Mimulus nepalensis f. maior

Mimulus nepalensis f. maior, commonly known as the greater Himalayan monkeyflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking orange to yellow tubular flowers, which are adapted for pollination by long-tongued insects such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its resilience to cold and arid conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, providing protection against harsh weather.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
