Melocactus ovatus

Melocactus ovatus, commonly known as the Easter cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in dense, woolly spines. The plant produces vibrant, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange, typically blooming in spring. It is a popular houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.

Melocactus ovatus thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.
