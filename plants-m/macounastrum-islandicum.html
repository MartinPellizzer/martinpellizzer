Macounastrum islandicum

Macounastrum islandicum, commonly known as the Icelandic starflower, is a flowering plant native to Iceland.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, star-shaped white or pale blue flowers. The plant has hairy, needle-like leaves that are adapted to the cold, harsh climate of its native habitat. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and limited distribution.
