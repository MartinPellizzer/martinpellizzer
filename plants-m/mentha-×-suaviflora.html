Mentha × suaviflora

Mentha × suaviflora, commonly known as the sweet mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, opposite leaves and clusters of small, pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. Its essential oils, particularly menthol and menthone, contribute to its distinctive minty fragrance and medicinal uses.

Mentha × suaviflora is often cultivated for its culinary and therapeutic applications.
