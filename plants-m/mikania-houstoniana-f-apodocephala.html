Mikania houstoniana f. apodocephala

Mikania houstoniana f. apodocephala, commonly known as the Houstonian vine, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces numerous slender, hairy stems that can climb or spread along the ground. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, tropical environments.

This variety is often found in shaded areas near streams or in forest undergrowth.
