Mentha × dumetorum f. pannonica

Mentha × dumetorum f. pannonica, commonly known as the Hungarian mint, is a hybrid plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and square, hairy stems typical of the mint genus. This variety is native to Central Europe and is often found in moist, shaded environments. The leaves are usually oval to lance-shaped with a serrated margin and a strong, minty fragrance.

It is cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of essential oils.
