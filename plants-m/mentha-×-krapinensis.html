Mentha × krapinensis

Mentha × krapinensis, commonly known as Krapina mint, is a hybrid species within the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and square stems, typical of the genus Mentha. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale purple or blue, arranged in whorls along the stem. It is native to the Balkan Peninsula and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Mentha × krapinensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
