Myrcia tresantha

Myrcia tresantha, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, aromatic leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.

Myrcia tresantha is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
