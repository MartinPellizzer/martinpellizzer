Melissa officinalis var. altissima

Melissa officinalis var. altissima, commonly known as lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its fragrant, serrated leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile garden plant. Its leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to their calming properties.

The variety altissima is noted for its taller growth and more robust scent compared to other lemon balm cultivars.
