Mussaenda pervillei

Mussaenda pervillei, commonly known as the golden trumpet or flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, orange-yellow inflorescences that resemble small trumpets, which are clustered in terminal panicles. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Mussaenda pervillei is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
