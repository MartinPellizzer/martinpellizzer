Mentha dahurica

Mentha dahurica, commonly known as Siberian mint, is a perennial herb native to the colder regions of Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated edge. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters during the summer months. Mentha dahurica is valued for its aromatic leaves and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its essential oils contain menthol and other compounds that contribute to its cooling and soothing properties.
