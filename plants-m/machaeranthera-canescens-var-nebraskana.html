Machaeranthera canescens var. nebraskana

Machaeranthera canescens var. nebraskana, commonly known as the Nebraska goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has slender, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a grayish-green upper surface. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils.

This variety is distinguished from the typical form by its slightly different morphology and geographic distribution.
