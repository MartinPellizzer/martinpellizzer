Marsdenia tubularis

Marsdenia tubularis, commonly known as the tubular milkvine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in umbels, with a distinctive tubular corolla. Its fruit is a follicle that splits open to release seeds with silky hairs.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
