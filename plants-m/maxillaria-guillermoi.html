Maxillaria guillermoi

Maxillaria guillermoi, commonly known as the Guillermo's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Mexico.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a distinctive yellow lip. Its leaves are typically elliptical and arranged in two rows along the stem.

Maxillaria guillermoi thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
