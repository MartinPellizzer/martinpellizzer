Mesembryanthemum pyropeum

Mesembryanthemum pyropeum, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has fleshy, blue-green leaves that help it store water, adapting it to arid environments. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a valuable addition to xeriscapic landscaping.
