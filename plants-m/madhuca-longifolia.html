Madhuca longifolia

Madhuca longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf mahua, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, elliptical leaves that are glossy on the upper surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It is often found in lowland and hill forest areas, thriving in humid climates.

The tree is valued for its hard wood and is also used in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
