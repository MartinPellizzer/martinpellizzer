Microlonchus papposus

Microlonchus papposus, commonly known as the hairy burrweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its small, erect stature and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds that are covered in fine hairs, aiding in its propagation. Its leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and covered in fine pubescence.

Microlonchus papposus is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
