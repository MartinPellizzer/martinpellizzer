Momordica cochinchinensis subsp. andamanica

Momordica cochinchinensis subsp.

andamanica, commonly known as the Andaman bitter gourd, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourds and cucumbers.

The plant produces elongated, warty fruits that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or orange when mature. Its leaves are broad and palmately lobed, and it has tendrils that help it climb.

The fruit is used in traditional medicine and as a vegetable in some regions.
