Melicope exuta

Melicope exuta, commonly known as the broad-leaved melicope, is a member of the Rutaceae family, native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its aromatic, smooth, and often reddish bark. The leaves are alternate, simple, and typically have a broad ovate shape with a slightly waxy surface. Its flowers are small, clustered, and usually yellowish-green, while the fruit is a spherical drupe with a smooth, dark purple skin.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
