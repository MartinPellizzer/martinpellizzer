Mitrephora parviflora

Mitrephora parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered mitrephora, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes cashew and mango. The plant features simple, alternate leaves with a smooth texture and a distinctive aromatic scent. Its small, white flowers are typically clustered in panicles, and it produces small, round fruits that are edible.
