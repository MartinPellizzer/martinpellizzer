Mauranthemum paludosum subsp. paludosum

Mauranthemum paludosum subsp.

paludosum, commonly known as the swamp mallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the southeastern United States. It typically grows in marshy or saturated soils, thriving in humid and often shaded conditions.

The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces showy, pink to purple flowers with five petals. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a branching stem that supports the foliage and floral display.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
