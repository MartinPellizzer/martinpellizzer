Miconia plumosa

Miconia plumosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by distinctive, round, orange-red fruits. Miconia plumosa is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
