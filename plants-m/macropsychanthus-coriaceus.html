Macropsychanthus coriaceus

Macropsychanthus coriaceus, commonly known as the thick-leaved lily, is a rare and unique plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a complex, lily-like structure. It is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.
