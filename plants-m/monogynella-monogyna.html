Monogynella monogyna

Monogynella monogyna, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironwood, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves that are narrow and leathery, providing drought tolerance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by hard, woody fruits. Its deep root system allows it to thrive in arid and rocky environments.

Monogynella monogyna is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in desert ecosystems.
