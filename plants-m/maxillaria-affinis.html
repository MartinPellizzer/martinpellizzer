Maxillaria affinis

Maxillaria affinis, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink and white flowers, which resemble a slipper or shoe, hence its common name. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic growth habit. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native humid environments.

Maxillaria affinis is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
