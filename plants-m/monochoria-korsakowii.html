Monochoria korsakowii

Monochoria korsakowii, commonly known as the water fern or water clover, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in shallow, freshwater habitats such as marshes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. The plant has finely divided, floating leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its roots are used to treat ailments like diarrhea and inflammation.

It plays an important role in maintaining water quality by filtering pollutants and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
