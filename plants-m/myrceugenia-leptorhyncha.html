Myrceugenia leptorhyncha

Myrceugenia leptorhyncha, commonly known as the narrow-snouted myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, with a glossy surface, while its flowers are usually pink or white.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.
