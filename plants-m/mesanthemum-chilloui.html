Mesanthemum chilloui

Mesanthemum chilloui, commonly known as the Chilean daisy, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and thistles. The plant is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling a dandelion. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

Mesanthemum chilloui is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
