Microstephium populifolium

Microstephium populifolium, commonly known as the poplar-leaf microstephium, is a rare flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that resemble those of poplar trees, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
