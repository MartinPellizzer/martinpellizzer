Mimosa andreana

Mimosa andreana, commonly known as the pink mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic bacteria. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
