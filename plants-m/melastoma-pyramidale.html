Melastoma pyramidale

Melastoma pyramidale, commonly known as the mountain melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or red, with five petals and a distinctive tubular shape. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dark seeds.
