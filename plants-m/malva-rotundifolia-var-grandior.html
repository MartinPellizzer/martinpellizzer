Malva rotundifolia var. grandior

Malva rotundifolia var. grandior, commonly known as the greater velvetleaf, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, with a deep green color. The plant produces showy, pale pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats like roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species in horticulture.
