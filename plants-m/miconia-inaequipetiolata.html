Miconia inaequipetiolata

Miconia inaequipetiolata, commonly known as the unequal petioled miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that vary in size and shape, often with a hairy underside. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by bright red or orange berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
