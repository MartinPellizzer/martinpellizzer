Marantochloa comorensis

Marantochloa comorensis, commonly known as the Comoro grass, is a flowering plant native to the Comoros Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family, which includes a variety of ornamental and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and cultural significance, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
