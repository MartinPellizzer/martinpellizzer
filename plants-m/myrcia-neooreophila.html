Myrcia neooreophila

Myrcia neooreophila, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in the study of tropical flora and ecological conservation.
