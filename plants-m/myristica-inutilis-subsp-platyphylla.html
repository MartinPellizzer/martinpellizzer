Myristica inutilis subsp. platyphylla

Myristica inutilis subsp.

platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaf nutmeg, is a member of the Myristicaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It is a small evergreen tree that produces fragrant, red-orange fruits containing aromatic seeds.

The plant is characterized by its broad, flat leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its most notable feature is the seed, which is used in traditional medicine and has a distinct, spicy aroma.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
