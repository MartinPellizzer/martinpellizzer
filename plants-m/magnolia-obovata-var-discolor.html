Magnolia obovata var. discolor

Magnolia obovata var. discolor, commonly known as the variegated magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its broad, obovate leaves that display a striking variegation of white and green. The tree produces large, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in early spring. Its bark is smooth and grey, becoming fissured with age.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
