Momordica grandibracteata

Momordica grandibracteata, commonly known as the giant bitter melon, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and distinctive, spiny fruits that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fruit is used in traditional medicine and as a vegetable in some cuisines.

The plant's botanical features make it a notable species within the Cucurbitaceae family.
