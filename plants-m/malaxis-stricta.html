Malaxis stricta

Malaxis stricta, commonly known as the rigid malaxis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments. The plant features a slender, upright stem with a few leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient uptake.

Due to its delicate appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
