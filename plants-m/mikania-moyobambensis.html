Mikania moyobambensis

Mikania moyobambensis, commonly known as the Andean wisteria, is a climbing vine native to the highlands of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
