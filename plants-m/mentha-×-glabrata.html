Mentha × glabrata

Mentha × glabrata, commonly known as catnip, is a hybrid mint species that combines traits from various Mentha species.

It is characterized by its square, hollow stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, typical of the Lamiaceae family. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Mentha × glabrata is widely cultivated for its aromatic leaves, which are used in herbal teas and as a natural insect repellent.
