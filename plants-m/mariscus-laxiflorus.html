Mariscus laxiflorus

Mariscus laxiflorus, commonly known as the slender-flowered mariscus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
