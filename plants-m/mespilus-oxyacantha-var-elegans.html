Mespilus oxyacantha var. elegans

Mespilus oxyacantha var. elegans, commonly known as the velvet tree, is a variant of the common mallow.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and showy, pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in woodland or hedgerow habitats. It produces small, edible fruits that are typically consumed when ripe.

The variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
