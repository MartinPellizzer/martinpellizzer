Mikania napensis

Mikania napensis, commonly known as the Napo Mikania, is a climbing vine native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by oval, fuzzy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense undergrowth.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in certain areas.
