Marrubium multibracteatum subsp. multibracteatum

Marrubium multibracteatum subsp.

multibracteatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved horehound, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features gray-green, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Its aromatic properties make it a valued plant in traditional herbal medicine.
