Melocactus glauxianus

Melocactus glauxianus, commonly known as the Mexican golden hat, is a striking cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, pale yellow spines, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice for collectors and xeriscaping in drought-prone areas.
