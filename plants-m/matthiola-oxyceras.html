Matthiola oxyceras

Matthiola oxyceras, commonly known as the sharp-horned stock, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers, which are usually pink or white, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Matthiola oxyceras is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional herbal medicine.
