Mesocapparis

Mesocapparis, commonly known as the rock cress, is a genus of flowering plants in the mustard family, Brassicaceae.

These plants are typically found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing their adaptability to harsh conditions. They produce small, white or yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The leaves are usually narrow and succulent, helping to reduce water loss.

Mesocapparis species are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value and resilience in difficult soils.
