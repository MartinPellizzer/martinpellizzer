Malaxis contrerasii

Malaxis contrerasii, commonly known as the Contreras' orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its rhizome is short and it produces a single or few erect stems with lanceolate leaves.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
