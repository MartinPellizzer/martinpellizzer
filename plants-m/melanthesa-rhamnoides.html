Melanthesa rhamnoides

Melanthesa rhamnoides, commonly known as the yellow toadflax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features upright stems that are often branched and covered in fine, hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Melanthesa rhamnoides is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, and it is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
