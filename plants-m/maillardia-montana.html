Maillardia montana

Maillardia montana, commonly known as the mountain mallow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a soft, hairy texture, and it typically grows in moist, well-drained soils. It is a perennial shrub that thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Botanically, it belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton plants.
