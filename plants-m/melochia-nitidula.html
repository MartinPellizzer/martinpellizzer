Melochia nitidula

Melochia nitidula, commonly known as the velvetleaf or smooth melochia, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are usually smooth and glossy, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces showy, yellowish-white flowers that are typically clustered in terminal panicles. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

Melochia nitidula is often found in disturbed habitats and is known for its adaptability to various soil types.
