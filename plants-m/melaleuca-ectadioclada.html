Melaleuca ectadioclada

Melaleuca ectadioclada, commonly known as the narrow-leaf paperbark, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to northern Australia and is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. It is often found in coastal and sandy areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

Melaleuca ectadioclada is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
