Matthiola littorea

Matthiola littorea, commonly known as the sea stock, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and western Europe.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shorelines, thriving in salty and windy conditions. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually pink or purple, and it blooms from spring to early summer.

Matthiola littorea is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
