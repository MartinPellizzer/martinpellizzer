Myrtella obtusa

Myrtella obtusa, commonly known as the broadleaf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are glossy and often have a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, berry-like fruits. Myrtella obtusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its dense foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.

It is also noted for its aromatic properties and historical use in traditional medicine.
