Myrcia glandulosa var. obovata

Myrcia glandulosa var. obovata, commonly known as the glossy wax myrtle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which is thick, leathery, and typically a deep green with a glossy appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its fruits are small, red, and fleshy berries that contain seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
