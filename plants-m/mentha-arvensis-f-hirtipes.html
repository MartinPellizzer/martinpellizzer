Mentha arvensis f. hirtipes

Mentha arvensis f. hirtipes, commonly known as hairy field mint, is a variant of the field mint species native to temperate regions.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinct appearance compared to other mint species. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. It produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The aromatic leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent in various culinary applications.
