Myrcia cymosopaniculata

Myrcia cymosopaniculata, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a shrub native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces bright red, berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species but toxic to humans. Myrcia cymosopaniculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

It plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining biodiversity in its native habitat.
