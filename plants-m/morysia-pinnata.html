Morysia pinnata

Morysia pinnata, commonly known as the featherleaf morysia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are deeply lobed and give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Morysia pinnata is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and easy maintenance.
