Malva capitata

Malva capitata, commonly known as the common mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other well-known species like cotton and hibiscus. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in gardens and wild areas.

Its fibrous stems and hairy leaves contribute to its distinctive botanical appearance.
