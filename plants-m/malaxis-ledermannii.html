Malaxis ledermannii

Malaxis ledermannii, commonly known as the Ledermann's Malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or mountainous regions. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil.

It is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its delicate and ephemeral floral display.
