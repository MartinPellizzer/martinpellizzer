Mertensia crassifolia

Mertensia crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped blue-violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Mertensia crassifolia is a member of the Boraginaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
