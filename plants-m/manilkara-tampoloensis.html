Manilkara tampoloensis

Manilkara tampoloensis, commonly known as the Tampolo or Tampolo tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by smooth, oval-shaped fruits with a thick, sticky pulp. Its latex, similar to other manilkara species, has been used traditionally for various purposes.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential economic uses in local communities.
