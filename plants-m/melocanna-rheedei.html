Melocanna rheedei

Melocanna rheedei, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 15 meters in height, forming dense clumps. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically green and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

Melocanna rheedei is also valued for its potential in sustainable construction and as a source of fiber.
