Machilus melanophyllus

Machilus melanophyllus, commonly known as the black-leaved machilus, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are often described as having a metallic sheen. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its unique foliage.
