Monochilus flabellatus

Monochilus flabellatus, commonly known as the fan-leaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and white. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves arranged in a rosette pattern, and its flowering stems are often branched. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Monochilus flabellatus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
