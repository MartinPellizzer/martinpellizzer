Masdevallia posadae f. rugosepala

Masdevallia posadae f. rugosepala, commonly known as the rugose-sepaled masdevallia, is a rare and striking orchid native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It is characterized by its elongated, pendulous inflorescence and vibrant, colorful flowers that display a range of hues, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant's foliage consists of broad, leathery leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. Its unique floral structure and striking appearance make it a prized specimen among orchid enthusiasts.

This hybrid is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological significance in its native habitat.
