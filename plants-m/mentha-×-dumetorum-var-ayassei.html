Mentha × dumetorum var. ayassei

Mentha × dumetorum var. ayassei, commonly known as "aya" or "chamomile mint," is a hybrid mint species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes. Its essential oils contain compounds like menthol and camphor, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its calming and digestive benefits.
