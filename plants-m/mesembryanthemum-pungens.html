Mesembryanthemum pungens

Mesembryanthemum pungens, commonly known as the sharpleaf iceplant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant thrives in arid and rocky environments, storing water in its thick, succulent stems and leaves. Its flowering period typically occurs during the cooler months, and it is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in degraded landscapes.
