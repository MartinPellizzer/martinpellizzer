Miconia coriacea

Miconia coriacea, commonly known as the roughleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, dark green, leathery leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains numerous seeds.

Miconia coriacea is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of tropical environments.
