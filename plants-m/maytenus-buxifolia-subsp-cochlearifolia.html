Maytenus buxifolia subsp. cochlearifolia

Maytenus buxifolia subsp.

cochlearifolia, commonly known as the ear-shaped leaf mayten, is a shrub native to parts of Central and South America. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with elliptical to obovate leaves that often have a waxy texture.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by dark blue, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species in horticulture.
