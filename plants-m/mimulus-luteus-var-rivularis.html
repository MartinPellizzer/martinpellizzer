Mimulus luteus var. rivularis

Mimulus luteus var. rivularis, commonly known as the yellow monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats, including stream banks and wet meadows. It has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture.

This variety is known for its adaptability to wet environments and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
