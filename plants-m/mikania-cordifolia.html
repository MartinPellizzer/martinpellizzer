Mikania cordifolia

Mikania cordifolia, commonly known as heartleaf morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Mikania cordifolia is often found in disturbed areas, such as along roadsides and in open woodlands.
