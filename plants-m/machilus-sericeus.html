Machilus sericeus

Machilus sericeus, commonly known as silverleaf mahuang, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by dark blue to black berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Machilus sericeus has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
