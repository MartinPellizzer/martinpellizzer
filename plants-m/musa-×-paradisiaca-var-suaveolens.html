Musa × paradisiaca var. suaveolens

Musa × paradisiaca var. suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet banana, is a tropical plant belonging to the Musaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length, providing a striking visual appearance. The plant produces clusters of elongated, curved fruits that are typically yellow when ripe, though they can vary in color depending on the variety. The flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in a spike, and the plant is primarily cultivated for its edible fruit.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
