Myrcia tiburoniana

Myrcia tiburoniana, commonly known as Tiburon myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil, particularly on the island of Trindade. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are glossy and elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, typical of the Myrtaceae family.
