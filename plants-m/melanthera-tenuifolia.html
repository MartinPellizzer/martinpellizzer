Melanthera tenuifolia

Melanthera tenuifolia, commonly known as slenderleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small blue to purple flowers.

Melanthera tenuifolia is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
