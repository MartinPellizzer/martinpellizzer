Mentha sylvestris var. mollissima

Mentha sylvestris var. mollissima, commonly known as the woolly wood mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive, velvety texture. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its use in traditional herbal remedies.
