Morus celtidifolia

Morus celtidifolia, commonly known as the European mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, aggregate fruits. The plant produces white flowers in early spring, which are followed by the edible, black berries that ripen in summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in silk production.
