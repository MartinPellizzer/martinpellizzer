Monadenium globosum

Monadenium globosum, commonly known as the globular cactus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cactaceae.

It is native to arid regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in hot, dry climates. The plant is characterized by its spherical shape, composed of dense, ribbed stems that store water. It produces yellow flowers during the rainy season, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
