Momordica multiflora

Momordica multiflora, commonly known as the many-flowered bitter gourd, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourds and cucurbits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated, warty fruits with a bitter taste. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and somewhat prickly.

The plant is often cultivated for its edible fruits, although they are typically used in traditional medicine and local cuisines.
