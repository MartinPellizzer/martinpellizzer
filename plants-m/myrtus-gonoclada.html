Myrtus gonoclada

Myrtus gonoclada, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Myrtus gonoclada is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its fragrant foliage and showy blooms.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and dry garden environments.
