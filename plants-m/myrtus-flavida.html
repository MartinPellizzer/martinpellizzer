Myrtus flavida

Myrtus flavida, commonly known as the yellow myrtle, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and have a distinctive aromatic scent.

Myrtus flavida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
