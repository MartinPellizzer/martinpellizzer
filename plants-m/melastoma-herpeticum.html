Melastoma herpeticum

Melastoma herpeticum, commonly known as the Herpes Melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its attractive, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind or water.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
