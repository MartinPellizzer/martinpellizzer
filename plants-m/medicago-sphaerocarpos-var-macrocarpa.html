Medicago sphaerocarpos var. macrocarpa

Medicago sphaerocarpos var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-seeded globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its globular, spiny seed pods that contain large, edible seeds. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and potential agricultural studies.
