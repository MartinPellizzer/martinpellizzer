Momisia platycaulis

Momisia platycaulis, commonly known as the broad-stemmed momisia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in racemes, and can range in color from white to pink.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
