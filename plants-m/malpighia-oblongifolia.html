Malpighia oblongifolia

Malpighia oblongifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian wolfberry, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, bright red berries that resemble tiny cherries. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to oblong in shape, often with a slightly serrated edge.

Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces fruits that are rich in vitamin C. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
