Myroxylon cordatum

Myroxylon cordatum, commonly known as the kapok tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and produces heavy, fibrous pods that contain silky fibers used in various industrial applications. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy. Its bark is thick and corky, providing it with some resistance to pests and diseases.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.
