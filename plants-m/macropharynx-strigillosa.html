Macropharynx strigillosa

Macropharynx strigillosa, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to retain moisture in its preferred wetland habitats.

The species is notable for its unique floral structure and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
