Maerua triphylla var. johannis

Maerua triphylla var. johannis, commonly known as the desert glorybower, is a shrub native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, tri-lobed leaves that are adapted to conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the rainy season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its stems are often woody and can become branched with age, providing structural support.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in low-water conditions.
