Maxillaria linguiformis

Maxillaria linguiformis, commonly known as the tongue-shaped orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often tongue-like petals and sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually fragrant and come in shades of white, yellow, and pink.

Maxillaria linguiformis is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or warm climates.
