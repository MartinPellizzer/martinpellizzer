Meratia fragrans

Meratia fragrans, commonly known as the sweet-smelling orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, and the flowers come in shades of white, pink, and purple.

Meratia fragrans is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
