Methonica simplex

Methonica simplex, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a silvery sheen, which help reflect excess sunlight. Its flowers are typically pale green to white, with intricate patterns that attract specific pollinators.

Methonica simplex is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouse settings due to its specific care requirements.
