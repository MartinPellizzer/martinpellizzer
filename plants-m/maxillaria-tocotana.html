Maxillaria tocotana

Maxillaria tocotana, commonly known as the Tocantins orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Brazil.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant features linear, grass-like leaves and produces pendulous inflorescences with fragrant, showy flowers. Its flowers are typically yellow with purple or red markings, and they bloom during the summer months.

Maxillaria tocotana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
