Mentha longifolia var. cardiophylla

Mentha longifolia var. cardiophylla, commonly known as heart-leaf mint, is a variant of the spearmint species characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves.

This perennial herb is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates. It produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a strong, aromatic scent due to its high concentration of menthol and other essential oils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and used in herbal teas and aromatherapy.
