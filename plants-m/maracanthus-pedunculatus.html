Maracanthus pedunculatus

Maracanthus pedunculatus, commonly known as the red spider lily, is a striking plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling spiders due to their shape and color. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically green and glossy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Maracanthus pedunculatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in containers.
