Melandrium rubrum var. smithii

Melandrium rubrum var. smithii, commonly known as the Smith's catchfly, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of native plant communities and a subject of ecological study.
