Marsdenia volubilis var. angustifolia

Marsdenia volubilis var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed Marsdenia, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, twining stems that can reach several meters in length. The leaves are opposite, elliptic to lanceolate, and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in axillary inflorescences.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
