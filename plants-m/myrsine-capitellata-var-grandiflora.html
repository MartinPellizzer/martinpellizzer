Myrsine capitellata var. grandiflora

Myrsine capitellata var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered myrsine, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
