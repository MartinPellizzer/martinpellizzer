Microcnemum

Microcnemum, commonly known as the creeping bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically forms dense, mat-like growth patterns on the ground, often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or along stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the summer months. Microcnemum is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion due to its extensive root system.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of many ecological systems.
