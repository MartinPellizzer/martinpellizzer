Maireana georgei

Maireana georgei, commonly known as the George's spinifex, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, fibrous foliage and its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are clustered in spikes. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water from deep underground.

Maireana georgei plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
