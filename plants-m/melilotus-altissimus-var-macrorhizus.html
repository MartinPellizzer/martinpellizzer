Melilotus altissimus var. macrorhizus

Melilotus altissimus var. macrorhizus, commonly known as the large-rooted sweet clover, is a biennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with a distinctive sweet scent. The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by pods containing several seeds. It has deep taproots that allow it to thrive in well-drained soils and is often used in forage and soil enrichment.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
