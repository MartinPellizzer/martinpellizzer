Melaleuca acuminata subsp. websteri

Melaleuca acuminata subsp.

websteri, commonly known as the narrow-leaf paperbark, is a shrub or small tree native to northern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery bloom.

The plant produces small, bottle-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
