Melocalamus cordatus

Melocalamus cordatus, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada grassland bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that are often clustered together, forming dense clumps. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a prominent midrib. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and forest edges in western North America.

Melocalamus cordatus is known for its resilience to drought and its role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
