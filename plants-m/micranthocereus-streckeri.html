Micranthocereus streckeri

Micranthocereus streckeri, commonly known as the Strecker's night bloomer, is a cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and sparse, spiny foliage. The plant produces small, white, nocturnal flowers that open primarily at night. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Due to its unique blooming habit and resilience, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and cactus enthusiasts.
