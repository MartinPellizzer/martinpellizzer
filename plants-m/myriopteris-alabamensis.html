Myriopteris alabamensis

Myriopteris alabamensis, commonly known as the Alabama holly fern, is a rare and endangered fern species native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky or wooded areas with high humidity. Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
