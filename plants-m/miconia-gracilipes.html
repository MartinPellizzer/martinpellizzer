Miconia gracilipes

Miconia gracilipes, commonly known as the slender miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly pubescent underside. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom throughout the year, followed by bright red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, it is considered an invasive species in some regions.
