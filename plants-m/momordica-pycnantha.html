Momordica pycnantha

Momordica pycnantha, commonly known as the Chinese bitter gourd, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, oblong fruits that turn from green to orange when mature. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a vegetable in various cuisines.
