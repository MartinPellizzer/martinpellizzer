Myrica quercifolia

Myrica quercifolia, commonly known as the sweetgum or American sweetgum, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, ovate to elliptic leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by spiky, spherical fruit clusters known as "galls.". It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in bottomlands and along streams.

Myrica quercifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting various wildlife species.
