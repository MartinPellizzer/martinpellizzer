Mimulus pennellii

Mimulus pennellii, commonly known as the yellow monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as streambanks and meadows. It has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Mimulus pennellii is a perennial species that plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
