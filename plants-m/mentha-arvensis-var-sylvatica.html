Mentha arvensis var. sylvatica

Mentha arvensis var. sylvatica, commonly known as wood mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. Wood mint thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, making it a valued plant in both traditional medicine and culinary applications.
