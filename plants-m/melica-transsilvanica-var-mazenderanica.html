Melica transsilvanica var. mazenderanica

Melica transsilvanica var. mazenderanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, forest edges, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in temperate climates and its role in maintaining soil stability.

Due to its ecological importance, it is sometimes considered a valuable plant for habitat conservation.
