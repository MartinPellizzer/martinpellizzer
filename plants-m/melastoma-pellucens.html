Melastoma pellucens

Melastoma pellucens, commonly known as the shining melastoma, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters at the leaf axils. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Melastoma pellucens is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
