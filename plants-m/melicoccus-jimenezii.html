Melicoccus jimenezii

Melicoccus jimenezii, commonly known as the yellow mangosteen, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small evergreen tree that belongs to the Clusiaceae family, known for its ornamental and medicinal value. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by round, fleshy fruits with a bright yellow skin. The fruit contains a sweet, aromatic pulp that is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

It is also valued for its potential health benefits due to the presence of bioactive compounds in its leaves and fruit.
