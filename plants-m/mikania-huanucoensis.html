Mikania huanucoensis

Mikania huanucoensis, commonly known as the Huanuco morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, which bloom in the early morning. Mikania huanucoensis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
