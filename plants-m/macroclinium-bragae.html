Macroclinium bragae

Macroclinium bragae, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a species native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with a distinctive red lip, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
