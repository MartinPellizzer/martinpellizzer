Malus glabrata

Malus glabrata, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and broad, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that are similar to pears. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

Malus glabrata is relatively cold-hardy and thrives in well-drained soils.
