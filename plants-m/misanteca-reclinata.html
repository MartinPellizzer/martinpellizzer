Misanteca reclinata

Misanteca reclinata, commonly known as the drooping mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Misanteca reclinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
