Marssonia primulina

Marssonia primulina, commonly known as the silverleaf primrose, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features delicate, silver-hairy leaves and small, showy flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often found in rocky or coastal areas, where it demonstrates remarkable adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
