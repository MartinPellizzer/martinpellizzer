Moschatella adoxa

Moschatella adoxa, commonly known as the slender mosquito orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers that are often mistaken for insects due to their appearance. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and has a rhizomatous root system. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in late summer.

Moschatella adoxa is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its unique floral structure and ecological role in its native habitat.
