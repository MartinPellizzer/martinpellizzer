Miconia formosa

Miconia formosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on the upper surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

Miconia formosa is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive in certain regions.
