Myroxylon pachyphyllum

Myroxylon pachyphyllum, commonly known as the caucho tree, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its thick, bark-like trunk and is known for its latex production, which has historically been used in the rubber industry. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and can grow up to 20 meters in height. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, yellow flowers followed by pod-like fruits.

The tree thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
