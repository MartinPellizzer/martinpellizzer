Myriactis gracilis

Myriactis gracilis, commonly known as the slender mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that primarily grows on the branches of host trees, such as eucalypts and acacias.

It possesses specialized structures called haustoria that penetrate the host's vascular system to extract water and nutrients. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers, which are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to reduce water loss in its often arid environment.

Myriactis gracilis plays a role in ecological interactions, providing food for certain insect species and contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
