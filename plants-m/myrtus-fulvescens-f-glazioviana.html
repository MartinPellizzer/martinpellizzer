Myrtus fulvescens f. glazioviana

Myrtus fulvescens f. glazioviana, commonly known as the Glaziov's myrtle, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the highlands of Mexico.

It features small, dark green leaves that are often aromatic and glandular. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or container planting.

Its ornamental value and unique foliage make it a desirable plant for both horticultural and ecological purposes.
