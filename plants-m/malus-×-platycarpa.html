Malus × platycarpa

Malus × platycarpa, commonly known as the flatpebble apple, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and rounded, often multi-stemmed form. The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are typically not suitable for consumption due to their tart flavor. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a serrated margin and a glossy upper surface.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural landscapes.
