Manihot polyantha

Manihot polyantha, commonly known as the pink arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking pink or red bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces edible tubers similar to those of other manihot species, which are used in various culinary applications. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm climate for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and potential as a food source, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and agricultural purposes.
