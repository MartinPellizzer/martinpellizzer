Martynia lanceolata

Martynia lanceolata, commonly known as the redcurrant flower or false nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Its leaves are lanceolate, hence the species name, and are usually covered in fine hairs.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
