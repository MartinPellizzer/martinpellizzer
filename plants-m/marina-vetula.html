Marina vetula

Marina vetula, commonly known as the sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows along coastal areas and in saline environments. The plant features large, showy flowers that are often pink or red, with five petals and a prominent central stigma. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds.

Marina vetula is also known for its ability to thrive in sandy soils and its use in traditional medicine.
