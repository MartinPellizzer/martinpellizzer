Marah oregana

Marah oregana, commonly known as the Oregon ground cherry, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, yellowish-green stems and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, round, orange fruits that resemble cherry tomatoes, which are edible when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in open woodlands and rocky areas.

Marah oregana is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
