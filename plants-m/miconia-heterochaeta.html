Miconia heterochaeta

Miconia heterochaeta, commonly known as the roughleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are rough to the touch, often with a waxy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains numerous seeds.

Miconia heterochaeta is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.
