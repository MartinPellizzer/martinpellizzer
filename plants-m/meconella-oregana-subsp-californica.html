Meconella oregana subsp. californica

Meconella oregana subsp.

californica, commonly known as the California meconella, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, silvery hairs.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, which are often hidden among the leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland valleys.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
