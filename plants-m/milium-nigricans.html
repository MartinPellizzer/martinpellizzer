Milium nigricans

Milium nigricans, commonly known as black millet, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Asia and is characterized by its small, dark-colored grains that are used as a grain crop. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces clusters of tiny, spikelet-like flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in temperate climates.

Milium nigricans is valued for its nutritional content and historical use in traditional cuisines.
