Maharanga vestita var. vestita

Maharanga vestita var. vestita, commonly known as the white maharanga, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive white, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and lowland areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.
