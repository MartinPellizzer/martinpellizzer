Mendoncia coccinea

Mendoncia coccinea, commonly known as the red velvet orchid, is a strikingly colorful orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals and sepals, which are often adorned with intricate patterns of darker red and purple. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months of the year. Its roots are specialized for absorbing nutrients from the forest floor, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
