Matthiola varia

Matthiola varia, commonly known as the winter flowering stock, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is an annual or biennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, four-petaled flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Matthiola varia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
