Microlicia pallida

Microlicia pallida, commonly known as the white microlicia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malvaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale green leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. It is often found in open woodlands and savannas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
