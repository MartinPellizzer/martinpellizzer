Microchilus chingualensis

Microchilus chingualensis, commonly known as the Chingualensis orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that often feature a combination of white, green, and yellow hues. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as mountain forests, where it relies on specific symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists studying orchid diversity and adaptation.

Due to habitat destruction, it is also considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
