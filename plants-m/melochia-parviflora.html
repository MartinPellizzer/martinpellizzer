Melochia parviflora

Melochia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered melochia, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus, and is characterized by its palmate leaves and showy, pale yellow to greenish flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils near riverbanks or in coastal areas.

Melochia parviflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
