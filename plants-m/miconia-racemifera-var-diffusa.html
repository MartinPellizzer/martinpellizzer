Miconia racemifera var. diffusa

Miconia racemifera var. diffusa, commonly known as the variable miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in dense, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Its rapid growth and invasive potential make it a significant ecological concern in certain regions.

The species is also notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climatic conditions.
