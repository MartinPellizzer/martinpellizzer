Mentha candicans f. ramosa

Mentha candicans f. ramosa, commonly known as the fragrant mint, is a variant of the Mentha candicans species.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, which features dark green, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This cultivar is often used in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and mild aromatic properties.
