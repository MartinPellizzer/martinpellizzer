Mimulus austinae

Mimulus austinae, commonly known as the Austina's monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange or yellow flowers, which are typically spurred at the back, a feature common in the genus Mimulus. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in stream banks and meadows. It has lance-shaped leaves that are generally hairless and arranged oppositely along the stems.

Mimulus austinae is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
