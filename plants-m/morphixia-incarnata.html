Morphixia incarnata

Morphixia incarnata, commonly known as the pink morphixia, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are simple and ovate, with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy surface.

Morphixia incarnata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
