Mesembryanthemum pallens var. roseum

Mesembryanthemum pallens var. roseum, commonly known as the pink sand verbena, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, ribbed leaves that are typically gray-green with a powdery coating, aiding in water retention. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to rose, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
