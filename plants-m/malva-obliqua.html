Malva obliqua

Malva obliqua, commonly known as the broadleaf mallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters at the ends of stems, and it has a fibrous root system.

Malva obliqua is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
