Malus sieboldii megarantha

Malus sieboldii megarantha, commonly known as the Japanese flowering crabapple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. Its leaves are ovate and have a serrated edge, turning yellow in the fall.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
