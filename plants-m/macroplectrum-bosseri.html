Macroplectrum bosseri

Macroplectrum bosseri, commonly known as the Bosser's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate floral structure and vibrant coloration. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small to medium-sized, with three sepals and three petals, and it produces a unique, fragrant scent to attract pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
