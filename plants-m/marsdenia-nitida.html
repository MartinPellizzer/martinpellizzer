Marsdenia nitida

Marsdenia nitida, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The leaves are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hair, giving the plant its common name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
