Musa muluensis

Musa muluensis, commonly known as the Mulu banana, is a species native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Musaceae family, characterized by its large, broad leaves and robust pseudostems. The plant produces clusters of elongated, yellowish fruits that are typically harvested when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its genetic uniqueness, it is of interest to botanists and plant breeders for potential agricultural applications.
