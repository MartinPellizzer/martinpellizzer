Medicago romanica var. australis

Medicago romanica var. australis, commonly known as the Australian medic, is a perennial legume native to Australia.

It features trifoliate leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant is characterized by its erect growth habit and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It has a deep root system that helps in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation.

This variety is valued for its forage quality and ecological benefits in arid and semi-arid regions.
