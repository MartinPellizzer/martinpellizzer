Manitia aurea

Manitia aurea, commonly known as the golden manitia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Manitia aurea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
