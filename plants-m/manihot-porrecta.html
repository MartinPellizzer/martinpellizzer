Manihot porrecta

Manihot porrecta, commonly known as the arrowleaf manioc, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its palmate leaves with three to five lobes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the spurge family. Its roots are used in traditional medicine and as a source of starch.

However, the plant contains toxic compounds that require careful processing before consumption.
