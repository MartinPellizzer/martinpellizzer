Myristica simiarum subsp. simiarum

Myristica simiarum subsp.

simiarum, commonly known as the black pepper tree, is a member of the Myristicaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. This evergreen tree can grow up to 15 meters tall and has compound leaves with a glossy, dark green appearance.

It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by dark red, fleshy berries containing the aromatic seeds used as a spice. The plant is characterized by its unique morphology, including a smooth, greyish-brown bark and a strong, pungent aroma.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is widely cultivated for its valuable aromatic seeds.
