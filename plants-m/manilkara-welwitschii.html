Manilkara welwitschii

Manilkara welwitschii, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a tropical tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The tree's fruit is a round, woody capsule that splits open to release its seeds. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and fine grain, making it a significant resource in local economies.

The plant also plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
