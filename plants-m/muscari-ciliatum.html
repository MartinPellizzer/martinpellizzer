Muscari ciliatum

Muscari ciliatum, commonly known as the Persian grape hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, resembling a cluster of grapes. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that emerge in early spring.

Muscari ciliatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural displays during the spring season.
