Merremia macrocalyx

Merremia macrocalyx, commonly known as the giant morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Merremia macrocalyx is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and natural habitats alike.
