Melanthera scaberrima

Melanthera scaberrima, commonly known as the roughleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often glandular-dotted. The plant produces clusters of small, purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils in woodland or swampy areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for wetland restoration and ecological studies.
