Melocactus interpositus

Melocactus interpositus, commonly known as the Mexican jumping bean, is a cacti species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its unique, segmented stem that resembles a bean, which gives it its common name. The plant produces a distinctive, elongated fruit that contains a larvae-infested pod, which is used in traditional practices. The botanical features include spines, areoles, and a thick, waxy cuticle that helps it conserve water.

It is adapted to arid environments and exhibits interesting ecological interactions with its inhabitants.
