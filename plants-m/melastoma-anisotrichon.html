Melastoma anisotrichon

Melastoma anisotrichon, commonly known as the broadleaf melastoma, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom during the rainy season. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Melastoma anisotrichon is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories.
