Mentha viridis subsp. nouletiana

Mentha viridis subsp.

nouletiana, commonly known as narrow-leaf mint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic foliage and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
