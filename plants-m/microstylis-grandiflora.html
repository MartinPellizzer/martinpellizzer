Microstylis grandiflora

Microstylis grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered microstylis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy and bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with showy, white to pale yellow flowers that have a distinctive, elongated stamen. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
