Mesembryanthemum microphyllum

Mesembryanthemum microphyllum, commonly known as the ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and vibrant, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves and stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
