Maranta lachnocaula

Maranta lachnocaula, commonly known as the sticky leaf arrowroot, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a waxy coating, which can appear sticky to the touch. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by the development of arrowhead-shaped tubers underground. Its unique foliage and adaptability to humid, shaded environments make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Maranta lachnocaula is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of care in suitable conditions.
