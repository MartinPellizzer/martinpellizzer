Mutisia mathewsii var. mathewsii

Mutisia mathewsii var. mathewsii, commonly known as the Mathews' mutisia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and shrublands, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its flowers are yellow and daisy-like, often appearing in clusters, and it produces small, oblong fruits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
