Myrcia neopalustris

Myrcia neopalustris, commonly known as the swamp myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the wetlands of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in swampy or poorly drained soils, thriving in humid, tropical environments. Its leaves are dark green and leathery, while its flowers are usually white or pale pink, attracting various pollinators.

Myrcia neopalustris is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
