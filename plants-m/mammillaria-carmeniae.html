Mammillaria carmeniae

Mammillaria carmeniae, commonly known as Carmen's bunny ear cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white to tan tubercles. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and compact size, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections.
