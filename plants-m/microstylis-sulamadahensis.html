Microstylis sulamadahensis

Microstylis sulamadahensis, commonly known as the Sulamadah microstylis, is a rare and endemic plant species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a unique inflorescence that consists of tightly clustered, pale yellow to white blooms. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
