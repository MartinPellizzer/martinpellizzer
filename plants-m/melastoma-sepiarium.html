Melastoma sepiarium

Melastoma sepiarium, commonly known as the black mangrove, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that thrives in saline and waterlogged soils, often found in mangrove ecosystems. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. Its flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals and numerous stamens, and it produces small, fleshy berries.

The species plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife.
