Mespilus sauguinea

Mespilus sauguinea, commonly known as the common medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces unique, edible fruits that are typically harvested when partially fermented, a process known as "mash.". The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, greenish-white flowers in the spring. Its fruit is characterized by a thick, leathery skin and a soft, aromatic pulp.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
