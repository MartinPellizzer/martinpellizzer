Mimosa aurivillus var. calothamnoides

Mimosa aurivillus var. calothamnoides, commonly known as the silver mimosa, is a variant of the mimosa plant characterized by its silvery-gray foliage.

It is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features bipinnate leaves that are soft and fuzzy, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellow, spherical flower heads that bloom in spring.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate various soil conditions.
