Myrcia eriopus

Myrcia eriopus, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains aromatic seeds.
