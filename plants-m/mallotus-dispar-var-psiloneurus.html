Mallotus dispar var. psiloneurus

Mallotus dispar var. psiloneurus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallot, is a variant of the mallot tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
