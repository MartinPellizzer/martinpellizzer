Metalasia cymbifolia

Metalasia cymbifolia, commonly known as the golden bell heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, adapted to thrive in poor, acidic soils.

Metalasia cymbifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in gardens and natural habitats.
