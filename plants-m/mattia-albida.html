Mattia albida

Mattia albida, commonly known as the white lily, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elegant, white, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Mattia albida is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
