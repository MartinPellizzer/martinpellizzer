Myrtus pitanga

Myrtus pitanga, commonly known as Brazilian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, white flowers. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are commonly used to make tea, valued for their medicinal properties.

Myrtus pitanga thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.
