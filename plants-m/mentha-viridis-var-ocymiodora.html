Mentha viridis var. ocymiodora

Mentha viridis var. ocymiodora, commonly known as creeping mint, is a variant of the green mint species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its slender, spreading stems and serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, giving it a fresh, minty scent and making it useful in herbal teas and culinary applications.

This variety is often used in horticulture for ground cover due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
