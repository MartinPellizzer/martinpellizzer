Mitrephora calcarea var. intermedia

Mitrephora calcarea var. intermedia, commonly known as the limestone mitrephora, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in limestone-rich soils. Its fruit is a small, spherical drupe that contains a single seed.

This variant is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology and growth habits compared to the typical form of Mitrephora calcarea.
