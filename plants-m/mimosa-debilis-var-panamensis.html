Mimosa debilis var. panamensis

Mimosa debilis var. panamensis, commonly known as the Panamanian sensitive plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its bipinnately compound leaves that fold shut when touched, a response mediated by rapid changes in turgor pressure. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its sensitivity to touch and unique morphology, it is a popular subject in botanical studies and horticulture.
