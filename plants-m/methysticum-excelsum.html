Methysticum excelsum

Methysticum excelsum, commonly known as kava, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its shrub-like appearance, with dark green, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant's roots contain bioactive compounds, including kavalactones, which are responsible for its psychoactive effects. It thrives in tropical climates with well-drained soil and requires a humid environment.

Due to its cultural and medicinal significance, it is cultivated in various regions for traditional and commercial use.
