Metalasia namaquana

Metalasia namaquana, commonly known as the Namaqua sandthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, fleshy fruits. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought.

Its root system helps prevent soil erosion, making it valuable in ecological restoration efforts.
