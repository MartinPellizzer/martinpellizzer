Malus baccata f. chamardabanica

Malus baccata f. chamardabanica, commonly known as the Chamardabanica crabapple, is a rare ornamental variant of the common crabapple.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often twisted fruits, which are typically red or orange in color. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, adding to its ornamental value. It is a compact shrub that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

This cultivar is prized for its unique fruiting habit and is often used in specialty gardens and botanical collections.
