Melaleuca eriocephala

Melaleuca eriocephala, commonly known as the white bottlebrush, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of white to pale pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has narrow, strap-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. It is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils, thriving in full sun.

Melaleuca eriocephala is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscaping.
