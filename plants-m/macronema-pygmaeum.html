Macronema pygmaeum

Macronema pygmaeum, commonly known as the dwarf macrophylla, is a small aquatic plant native to the freshwater ecosystems of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in slow-moving or still water, such as ponds and marshes, where it forms dense, floating mats. The plant has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white and appear in clusters, contributing to its modest appearance.

Despite its size, Macronema pygmaeum plays an important role in aquatic habitats by providing shelter for small aquatic organisms.
