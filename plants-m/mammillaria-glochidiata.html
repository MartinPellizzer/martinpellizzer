Mammillaria glochidiata

Mammillaria glochidiata, commonly known as the glochidiate hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the deserts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a low-maintenance cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for succulent collectors and desert gardens.
