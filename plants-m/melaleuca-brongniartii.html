Melaleuca brongniartii

Melaleuca brongniartii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paperbark, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, papery bark that peels off in strips, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white or cream flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of wax.

Melaleuca brongniartii is typically found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.
