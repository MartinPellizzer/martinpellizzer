Myosotis scorpioides var. arvensis

Myosotis scorpioides var. arvensis, commonly known as the field forget-me-not, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate blue to purple flowers with five petals, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of stems. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and grow along the stem. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
