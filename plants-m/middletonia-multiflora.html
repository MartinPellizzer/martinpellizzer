Middletonia multiflora

Middletonia multiflora, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a rare and endangered plant species native to the Appalachian Mountains.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, acidic soils within shaded forest environments. The plant features small, elliptical leaves with a smooth texture and a distinctive reddish hue during the fall season. Its flowers are white and clustered in umbels, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
