Muraltia capensis

Muraltia capensis, commonly known as the sweet thorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing along riverbanks and in rocky soils.

Muraltia capensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
