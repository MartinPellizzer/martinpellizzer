Maxillaria carolii

Maxillaria carolii, commonly known as Carol's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration. The plant typically features dark green, leathery leaves and pseudobulbs that support its growth. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a yellowish lip, and bloom during the summer months.

Maxillaria carolii is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
