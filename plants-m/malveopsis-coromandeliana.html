Malveopsis coromandeliana

Malveopsis coromandeliana, commonly known as the Coromandel mallow, is a flowering plant native to India and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus, and is characterized by its showy, pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
