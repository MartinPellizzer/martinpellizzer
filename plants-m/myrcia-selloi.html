Myrcia selloi

Myrcia selloi, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a shrub native to Brazil, particularly found in the Atlantic Forest region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a small, edible drupe that contains a single seed.

Myrcia selloi is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
