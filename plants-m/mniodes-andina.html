Mniodes andina

Mniodes andina, commonly known as the Andean morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces showy, fragrant blooms that open in the early morning and close by midday. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.

Mniodes andina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
