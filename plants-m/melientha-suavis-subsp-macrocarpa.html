Melientha suavis subsp. macrocarpa

Melientha suavis subsp.

macrocarpa, commonly known as the sweet melilot, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands. It is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in dense clusters.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, woody pods containing seeds.

The species is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
