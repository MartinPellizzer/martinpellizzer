Micropiper macrostachyum

Micropiper macrostachyum, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length, making it a dramatic addition to water gardens. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its unique morphology includes a hollow stem and a dense root system that helps stabilize sediment.

Micropiper macrostachyum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can also serve as a habitat for aquatic life.
