Mimosa apodocarpa

Mimosa apodocarpa, commonly known as the pink mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces distinctive, inflated pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Mimosa apodocarpa is also known for its sensitivity to touch, as its leaves fold when disturbed.
