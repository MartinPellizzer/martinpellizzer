Morinda intermedia

Morinda intermedia, commonly known as the red morinda, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, red or orange flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are ovate and have a rough texture, while the fruit is a capsule containing small, black seeds.

Morinda intermedia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
