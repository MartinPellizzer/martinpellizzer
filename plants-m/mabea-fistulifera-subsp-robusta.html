Mabea fistulifera subsp. robusta

Mabea fistulifera subsp.

robusta, commonly known as the robusta mabea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its woody stems and large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
