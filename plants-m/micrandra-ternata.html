Micrandra ternata

Micrandra ternata, commonly known as the small-leafed micrandra, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves arranged in a basal rosette, and its flowers are typically small and yellowish. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
