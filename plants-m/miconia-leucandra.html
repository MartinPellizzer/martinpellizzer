Miconia leucandra

Miconia leucandra, commonly known as the white miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and showy white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a bland taste. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
