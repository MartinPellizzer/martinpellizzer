Mammillaria farinosa

Mammillaria farinosa, commonly known as the floury mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
