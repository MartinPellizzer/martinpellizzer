Maxillaria coelia

Maxillaria coelia, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pinkish-purple flowers that resemble a slipper, with a distinctive pouch-like structure at the base. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store moisture in its natural habitat.

Maxillaria coelia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
