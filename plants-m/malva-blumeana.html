Malva blumeana

Malva blumeana, commonly known as the Blume's mallows, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
