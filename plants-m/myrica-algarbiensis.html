Myrica algarbiensis

Myrica algarbiensis, commonly known as the Algarbi wax myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Myricaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, waxy leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in coastal and rocky habitats, often growing in well-drained soils.

Myrica algarbiensis is notable for its aromatic leaves and its ability to withstand salty sea air.
