Maurandya semperflorens

Maurandya semperflorens, commonly known as the common mauve, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces showy, five-petaled blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Its foliage is typically heart-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs.
