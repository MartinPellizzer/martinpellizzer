Malaxis ramosa

Malaxis ramosa, commonly known as the slender malaxis, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces numerous slender stems that bear the inflorescences.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
