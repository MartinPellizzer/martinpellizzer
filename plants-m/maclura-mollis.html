Maclura mollis

Maclura mollis, commonly known as the yellow wood or honey locust, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in racemes. Its fruit is a pod that splits open to release seeds, and the bark is thick and fissured, often with a yellowish hue.

Maclura mollis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used historically for its durable wood.
