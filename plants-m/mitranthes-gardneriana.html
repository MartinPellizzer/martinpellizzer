Mitranthes gardneriana

Mitranthes gardneriana, commonly known as the Gardner's bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet to blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.
