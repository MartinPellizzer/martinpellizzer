Mokof elliptica

Mokof elliptica, commonly known as the broadleaf mokof, is a unique plant native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical, and often waxy leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant typically thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun exposure to flourish. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of yellow or green, adding subtle visual interest.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native environment.
