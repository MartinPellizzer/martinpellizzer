Malus sieversii var. hemisphaerica

Malus sieversii var. hemisphaerica, commonly known as the Central Asian apple, is a wild apple species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive hemispherical shape, which gives it its specific epithet. The plant typically grows in rocky and mountainous habitats, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture, while its flowers are white and fragrant.

This variety is considered a valuable genetic resource for apple breeding programs due to its hardiness and unique traits.
