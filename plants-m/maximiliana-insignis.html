Maximiliana insignis

Maximiliana insignis, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and short blooming period. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

The flowers emit a strong scent to attract nocturnal pollinators like moths.
