Mentha × pauciflora

Mentha × pauciflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mint, is a hybrid species within the genus Mentha.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white, arranged in dense spikes. It is native to regions of Europe and Asia and thrives in moist, shady environments.

Mentha × pauciflora is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for its aromatic and potentially therapeutic properties.
