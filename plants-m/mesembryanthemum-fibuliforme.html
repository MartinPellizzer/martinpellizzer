Mesembryanthemum fibuliforme

Mesembryanthemum fibuliforme, commonly known as the woolly iceplant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and leaves that are covered in a fine, white, powdery coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in dry climates.
