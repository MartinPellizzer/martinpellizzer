Macrolobium pendulum

Macrolobium pendulum, commonly known as the hanging globemallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, globe-shaped flower clusters that hang from long, slender stems. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with five petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in fine hairs.

Macrolobium pendulum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
