Myrcia pteropoda

Myrcia pteropoda, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. Myrcia pteropoda thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
