Myristica longipes

Myristica longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen belonging to the Myristicaceae family.

It is native to the Indo-Pacific region, particularly found in areas like Madagascar and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and produces fragrant, orange-red flowers. Its fruit is a drupe that contains a single seed, which is used to produce the spice nutmeg.

The tree is valued not only for its economic importance but also for its ecological role in tropical ecosystems.
