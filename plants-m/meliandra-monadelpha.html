Meliandra monadelpha

Meliandra monadelpha, commonly known as the white-flowered meliandra, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and related trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
