Melica schafkati

Melica schafkati, commonly known as Schafkati's brome, is a grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It typically thrives in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky soils.

Melica schafkati is often found in regions with a continental climate and is adapted to withstand seasonal droughts.
