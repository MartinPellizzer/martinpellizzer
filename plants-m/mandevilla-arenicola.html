Mandevilla arenicola

Mandevilla arenicola, commonly known as the sand mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments. Its leaves are typically oval and slightly hairy, adapting to arid conditions.

Mandevilla arenicola is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental flowers and ability to grow in challenging climates.
