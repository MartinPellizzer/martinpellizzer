Micrandropsis scleroxylon

Micrandropsis scleroxylon, commonly known as the African sand orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale green or white flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dry, open habitats. It has a unique root system that allows it to survive in nutrient-poor environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in arid ecosystem botany.
