Monarda lutea

Monarda lutea, commonly known as the yellow beeplant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features showy, tubular yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly fuzzy texture.

Monarda lutea is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun.
