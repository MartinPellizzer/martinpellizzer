Melanophylla angustior

Melanophylla angustior, commonly known as the narrowleaf melanophylla, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in dense inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

Due to its drought tolerance and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes and native plant gardens.
