Mairia taxifolia

Mairia taxifolia, commonly known as the laceleaf mairia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a distinctive, delicate appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in clusters. Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value, and it prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

Mairia taxifolia is also valued for its ability to thrive in coastal environments.
