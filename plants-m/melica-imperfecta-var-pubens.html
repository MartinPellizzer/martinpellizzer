Melica imperfecta var. pubens

Melica imperfecta var. pubens, commonly known as the pubescent melica, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist habitats such as meadows, stream banks, and wetlands. The plant features slender, tufted stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with spikelets that are usually two-ranked.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet environments.
