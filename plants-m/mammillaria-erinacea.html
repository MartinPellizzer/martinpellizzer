Mammillaria erinacea

Mammillaria erinacea, commonly known as the woolly mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, white, woolly covering that covers the stems and flowers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a vibrant touch to its desolate habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth rate, it is a popular choice for collectors and enthusiasts of succulents.
