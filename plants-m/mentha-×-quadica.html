Mentha × quadica

Mentha × quadica, commonly known as the hybrid mint, is a cross between spearmint (Mentha spicata) and watermint (Mentha aquatica).

It is a perennial herb characterized by its square, hollow stems and opposite, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorls along the stem. Mentha × quadica is known for its strong, aromatic scent and is widely used in culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
