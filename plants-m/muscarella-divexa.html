Muscarella divexa

Muscarella divexa, commonly known as the broad-leaved muscaria, is a species of flowering plant in the family Muscariaceae.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of bell-shaped, blue-violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically dark blue when mature. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.
