Myrcia glaziovii

Myrcia glaziovii, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and fragrant, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are glossy and oval-shaped, while its fruit is a small, woody capsule containing several seeds.

Myrcia glaziovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
