Macuillamia rotundifolia

Macuillamia rotundifolia, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rounded, broad leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue or violet. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or arid landscapes. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.

Macuillamia rotundifolia is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
