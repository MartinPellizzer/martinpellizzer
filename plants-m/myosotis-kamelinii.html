Myosotis kamelinii

Myosotis kamelinii, commonly known as the Kamelin's forget-me-not, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Russia and parts of Central Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant features small, delicate blue or violet flowers with five petals, often surrounded by white or pale blue sepals. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Myosotis kamelinii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its charming appearance.
