Myrtus chytraculia

Myrtus chytraculia, commonly known as the Cyprus myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are small, dark berries that contain seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their antimicrobial and antiseptic qualities.
