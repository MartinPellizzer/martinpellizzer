Myrosma uleana

Myrosma uleana, commonly known as the Mexican holly, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes species like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

Myrosma uleana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
