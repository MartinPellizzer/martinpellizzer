Malcolmia pyramidum

Malcolmia pyramidum, commonly known as the Egyptian crown flower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, papery blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a sprawling growth habit with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The flowers are also known for their ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
