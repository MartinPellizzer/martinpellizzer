Myagrum erucago

Myagrum erucago, commonly known as the black mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its dark purple, segmented fruit that resembles a cross between a grape and a lychee. The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a strong aromatic scent.

The fruit is prized for its sweet, juicy pulp and is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
