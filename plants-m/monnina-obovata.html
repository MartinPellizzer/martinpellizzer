Monnina obovata

Monnina obovata, commonly known as broadleaf bluestem, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in dense spikes, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in prairie and woodland habitats.

Monnina obovata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators.
