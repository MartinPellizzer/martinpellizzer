Mimosa caerulea

Mimosa caerulea, commonly known as the blue mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and showy, pink to purple flower clusters. The plant produces small, round, green pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Mimosa caerulea is also known for its sensitive leaves that fold shut when touched, a trait that adds to its botanical intrigue.
