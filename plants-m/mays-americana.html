Mays americana

Mays americana, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Mexico and Central America, where it thrives in warm, sunny environments. The plant features large, bright orange to red ray florets and yellow disk florets, making it a striking ornamental plant. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces seeds in a fluffy, spherical head.

Mays americana is often cultivated for its vibrant flowers and is also used in traditional medicine.
