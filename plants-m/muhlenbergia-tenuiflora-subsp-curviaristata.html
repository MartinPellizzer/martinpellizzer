Muhlenbergia tenuiflora subsp. curviaristata

Muhlenbergia tenuiflora subsp.

curviaristata, commonly known as the narrowleaf muhlenbergia, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers that appear in late summer.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of native plant communities and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
