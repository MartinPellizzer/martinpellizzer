Muscari eburneum

Muscari eburneum, commonly known as the white musk grape, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge from a central bulb. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.

Muscari eburneum prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
