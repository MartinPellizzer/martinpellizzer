Myrmecodendron × oaxacanum

Myrmecodendron × oaxacanum, commonly known as the Oaxacan antplant, is a hybrid species native to Mexico.

It is a climbing plant that forms symbiotic relationships with ants, providing them with shelter and food in exchange for protection against herbivores. The plant has elongated, tubular leaves that are often inhabited by colonies of ants. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically producing a distinctive red or orange color.

The botanical interest of this species lies in its unique ecological interactions and hybrid origin.
