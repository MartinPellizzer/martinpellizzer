Moparia repens

Moparia repens, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a low-growing, spreading habit with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are bell-shaped, blue to purple in color, and appear in clusters during the summer months.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
