Monolix digitata

Monolix digitata, commonly known as the fingerleaf lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It has a distinctive leaf-like structure with lobes that resemble fingers, giving it its common name. The thallus is typically green to yellowish in color, and it often grows on tree bark or soil in temperate regions. It plays an important role in nutrient cycling and serves as a habitat for various microorganisms.

Due to its sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered an indicator species for air quality.
