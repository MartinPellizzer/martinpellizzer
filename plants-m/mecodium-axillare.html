Mecodium axillare

Mecodium axillare, commonly known as the axillary mecodium, is a small, herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers. It is adapted to arid environments and can survive in conditions with limited water availability.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
