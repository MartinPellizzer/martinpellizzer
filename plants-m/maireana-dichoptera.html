Maireana dichoptera

Maireana dichoptera, commonly known as the Grey-leaved Maireana, is a shrub native to Australia, particularly found in arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its small, grey-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry conditions. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in winter, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It is adapted to low-nutrient soils and can thrive in harsh environmental conditions.

Maireana dichoptera is often used in native landscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
