Magnolia maudiae var. platypetala

Magnolia maudiae var. platypetala, commonly known as the Chinese magnolia or the broad-leaf magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are broad, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a dark green upper surface. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous seeds, and it is valued for its ornamental beauty in gardens.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for temperate climates.
