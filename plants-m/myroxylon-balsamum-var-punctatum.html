Myroxylon balsamum var. punctatum

Myroxylon balsamum var. punctatum, commonly known as the pink balsamwood, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its dark reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers and has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets. Its sap, known as balsam, has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a fragrance component.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in providing habitat and contributing to biodiversity in its native regions.
