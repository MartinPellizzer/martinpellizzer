Mammillaria flavescens

Mammillaria flavescens, commonly known as the yellow mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and dense covering of white or yellow spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
