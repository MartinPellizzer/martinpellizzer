Mapouria ficigemma

Mapouria ficigemma, commonly known as the Malabar nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale blue, blooming in clusters during the summer months. Its fruits are berries that start green and mature to a dark purple or black color, containing several seeds.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and is known for its ornamental value.
