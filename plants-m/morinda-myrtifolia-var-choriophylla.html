Morinda myrtifolia var. choriophylla

Morinda myrtifolia var. choriophylla, commonly known as the velvetleaf morinda, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that give it a distinctive velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. Its fruits are fleshy berries that contain several seeds.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
