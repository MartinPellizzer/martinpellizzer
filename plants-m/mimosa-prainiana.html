Mimosa prainiana

Mimosa prainiana, commonly known as the Indian mimosa, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It is often found in moist, shaded areas and is adapted to thrive in humid climates.

Its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen make it an important plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
