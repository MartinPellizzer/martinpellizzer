Masdevallia aspera

Masdevallia aspera, commonly known as the rough leaved masdevallia, is a terrestrial orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a rough, textured surface. The plant produces clusters of small, pale green to brownish flowers with intricate, spurred petals. It thrives in cool, humid environments with high air circulation and requires bright, indirect light.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
