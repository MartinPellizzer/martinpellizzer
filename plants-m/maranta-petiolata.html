Maranta petiolata

Maranta petiolata, commonly known as the arrowroot plant, is a tropical species native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its upright, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves with a striking silver-green coloration. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its rhizomes are used to produce a starchy, gluten-free flour called arrowroot.

Maranta petiolata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and air-purifying properties.
