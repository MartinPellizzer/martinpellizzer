Melchiora schliebenii var. intermedia

Melchiora schliebenii var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate melchiora, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It features distinctive, lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue and a hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
