Melilotus exaltatus

Melilotus exaltatus, commonly known as tall melilot, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, often branching towards the upper part. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, trifoliate, and have a mild, sometimes sweet scent.

Melilotus exaltatus is adapted to well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
