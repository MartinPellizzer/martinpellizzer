Medicago caerulea subsp. pilifera

Medicago caerulea subsp.

pilifera, commonly known as blue medic or blue clover, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive blue or purple hue, and its small, pea-like flowers are typically blue or violet in color.

The plant is characterized by its erect growth habit and produces small, spherical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
