Myrcia vellozoi

Myrcia vellozoi, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round capsule that contains several seeds.

Myrcia vellozoi is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments.
