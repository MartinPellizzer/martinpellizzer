Magnolia sonlaensis

Magnolia sonlaensis, commonly known as the Son La magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. Its bark is smooth and light gray, and it can grow up to 15 meters tall in suitable conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its beautiful floral display.
