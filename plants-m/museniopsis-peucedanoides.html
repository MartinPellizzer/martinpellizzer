Museniopsis peucedanoides

Museniopsis peucedanoides, commonly known as the Indian cow parsley, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is native to the Indian subcontinent and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. It has a long taproot and produces small, dry fruits known as schizocarps.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
