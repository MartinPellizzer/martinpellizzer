Mandevilla montana

Mandevilla montana, commonly known as the mountain mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and trailing or climbing habit, making it suitable for trellises or hanging baskets. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming from spring to fall. Its leaves are heart-shaped and glossy, adding visual appeal to gardens.

Mandevilla montana thrives in warm climates and requires well-draining soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
