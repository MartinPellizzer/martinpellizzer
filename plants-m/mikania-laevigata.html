Mikania laevigata

Mikania laevigata, commonly known as the silky Mikania, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellowish to orange flowers that bloom in clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it can become a problematic weed in certain regions.
