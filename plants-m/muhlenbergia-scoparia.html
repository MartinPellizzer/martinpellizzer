Muhlenbergia scoparia

Muhlenbergia scoparia, commonly known as silver bluestem, is a native grass species found throughout the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and tufted, spike-like inflorescences that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy choice for drought-tolerant landscapes. Its fine, wiry leaves and compact growth habit give it a decorative appearance, often used in ornamental gardening.

Muhlenbergia scoparia is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
