Mimetes fimbriifolius

Mimetes fimbriifolius, commonly known as the fringed-leaf mimic, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornate foliage. The plant features fleshy, ribbed stems and leaves with distinctive fringed edges, which contribute to its unique appearance. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically during the summer months.

Due to its adaptability to arid conditions, it is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
