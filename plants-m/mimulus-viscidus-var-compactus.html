Mimulus viscidus var. compactus

Mimulus viscidus var. compactus, commonly known as the sticky monkeyflower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features bright yellow, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has sticky hairs on its stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It typically grows in moist, sunny habitats like meadows and streambanks.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and small spaces.
