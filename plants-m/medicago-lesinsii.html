Medicago lesinsii

Medicago lesinsii, commonly known as the Lesinskii clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils and is often found in steppe and grassland habitats. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Medicago lesinsii is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
