Mimosa regina

Mimosa regina, commonly known as the queen mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen, it can also play a role in soil enrichment.
