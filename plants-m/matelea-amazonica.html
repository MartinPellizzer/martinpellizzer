Matelea amazonica

Matelea amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its large, fragrant white flowers that open at night. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms with five petals and a prominent, fleshy corona. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem.

Matelea amazonica is known for its nocturnal blooming habit and is often found in shaded, humid environments.
