Malva parviflora var. velutina

Malva parviflora var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp habitats.

The species is noted for its fibrous roots and its ability to tolerate periodic flooding.
