Maireria

Maireria, commonly known as the false holly, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a flowering shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant white or yellow flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Maireria is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
