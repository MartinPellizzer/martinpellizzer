Mesembryanthemum latipetalum

Mesembryanthemum latipetalum, commonly known as the broad-petaled ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, characterized by its fleshy leaves and flowers that open in response to sunlight. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in summer, often resembling small daisies. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water, making it drought-tolerant.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
