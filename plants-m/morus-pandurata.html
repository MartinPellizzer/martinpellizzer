Morus pandurata

Morus pandurata, commonly known as the Indian mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The tree produces edible berries that are used in traditional cuisines and have medicinal properties. Its bark and leaves are also utilized in herbal remedies.

Morus pandurata thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
