Musa × humilis

Musa × humilis, commonly known as the dwarf banana plant, is a hybrid species within the banana family, Musaceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with shorter stems and smaller leaves compared to its larger relatives. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained, fertile soil for optimal growth.

Due to its smaller size, it is often cultivated in gardens and as an ornamental plant.
