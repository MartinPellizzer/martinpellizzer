Malva alcea proles italica

Malva alcea proles italica, commonly known as the Italian mallow, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, soft, velvety leaves and showy, pale pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used historically in traditional medicine.

The flowers are also used in culinary applications, particularly in the preparation of soups and sauces.
