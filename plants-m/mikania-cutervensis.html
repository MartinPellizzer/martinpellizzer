Mikania cutervensis

Mikania cutervensis, commonly known as the creeping mikania, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and extensive root system. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough, hairy texture.

Mikania cutervensis is known for its invasive tendencies in certain regions, making it a significant ecological concern.
