Meriania pergamentacea

Meriania pergamentacea, commonly known as the parchmentleaf meriania, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, thin, and often leathery leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. It typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

Meriania pergamentacea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
