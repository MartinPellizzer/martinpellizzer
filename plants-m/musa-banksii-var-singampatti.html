Musa banksii var. singampatti

Musa banksii var. singampatti, commonly known as the Singampatti banana, is a rare and unique variety of banana plant native to southern India.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender pseudostems and large, pointed leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, elongated bananas that are typically harvested when they are still green. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil, making it a challenging but rewarding plant for cultivation.

Due to its rarity and specific growing requirements, it is less commonly found in commercial banana production.
