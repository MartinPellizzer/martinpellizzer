Moorea argentea

Moorea argentea, commonly known as silver tree or silverwood, is a flowering plant native to the island of Moorea in French Polynesia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often covered in a fine, waxy coating that gives them their characteristic silvery appearance.

Moorea argentea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in tropical climates.
