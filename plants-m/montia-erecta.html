Montia erecta

Montia erecta, commonly known as the upright bladderwort, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Montiaceae and is characterized by its erect growth habit and small, succulent leaves. The plant produces tiny, white flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and riverbanks.

Montia erecta is a short-lived perennial that plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
