Microcalamus aspidistrula f. angustus

Microcalamus aspidistrula f. angustus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed bamboo, is a variant of the aspidistrula species within the bamboo family.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are narrower than those of the typical form. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense clumps.

The foliage is often a deep green, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in shaded areas.
