Myrtus fragrantissima

Myrtus fragrantissima, commonly known as sweet myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its aromatic oils, derived from the leaves and flowers, are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

The berries that follow the flowers are typically dark blue or black and are edible, though less commonly consumed.
