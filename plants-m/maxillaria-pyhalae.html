Maxillaria pyhalae

Maxillaria pyhalae, commonly known as the pale maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration. The plant features strap-like leaves that are dark green and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. Its inflorescence typically consists of several flowers arranged in a raceme, with petals and sepals that are often marked with contrasting hues.

Maxillaria pyhalae is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
