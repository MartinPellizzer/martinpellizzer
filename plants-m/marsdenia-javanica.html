Marsdenia javanica

Marsdenia javanica, commonly known as the Java Marsdenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine that belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and showy flowers. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy texture, while its stems are woody and can grow quite long.

Marsdenia javanica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
