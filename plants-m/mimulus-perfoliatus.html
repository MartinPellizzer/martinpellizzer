Mimulus perfoliatus

Mimulus perfoliatus, commonly known as the yellow monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers and heart-shaped, often fused leaves that form a tube around the stem. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Mimulus perfoliatus is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
