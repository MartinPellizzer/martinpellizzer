Marcgravia parviflora var. macrophylla

Marcgravia parviflora var. macrophylla, commonly known as the giant leafed marshmallow plant, is a tropical species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, pale greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid lowland forests.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
