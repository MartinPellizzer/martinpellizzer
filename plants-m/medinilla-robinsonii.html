Medinilla robinsonii

Medinilla robinsonii, commonly known as the golden medinilla, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, showy flowers. The plant produces clusters of small, white or yellowish flowers that are often described as resembling miniature roses. Medinilla robinsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for indoor cultivation in cooler regions.
