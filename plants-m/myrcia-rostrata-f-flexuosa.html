Myrcia rostrata f. flexuosa

Myrcia rostrata f. flexuosa, commonly known as the flexible myrtle, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is noted for its adaptability and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
