Mesosetum aequiglume

Mesosetum aequiglume, commonly known as the smoothgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a smooth texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize soil, making it useful in erosion control.

Mesosetum aequiglume is often found in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats.
