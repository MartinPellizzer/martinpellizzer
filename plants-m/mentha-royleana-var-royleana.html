Mentha royleana var. royleana

Mentha royleana var. royleana, commonly known as the Afghan mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its essential oils, rich in menthol and other volatile compounds, make it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.

The species is often cultivated for its refreshing scent and use in traditional herbal remedies.
