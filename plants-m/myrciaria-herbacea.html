Myrciaria herbacea

Myrciaria herbacea, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree or the false pepper, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
