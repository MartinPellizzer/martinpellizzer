Maxillaria schnitteri

Maxillaria schnitteri, commonly known as the Schneitter's orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pale yellow to orange in color, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Maxillaria schnitteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
