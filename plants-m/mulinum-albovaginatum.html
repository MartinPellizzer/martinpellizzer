Mulinum albovaginatum

Mulinum albovaginatum, commonly known as the white-veined mullein, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with prominent white veins, giving it its common name.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in suitable climates.
