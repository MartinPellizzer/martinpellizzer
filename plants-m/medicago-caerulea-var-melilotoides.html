Medicago caerulea var. melilotoides

Medicago caerulea var. melilotoides, commonly known as the blue milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its blue to violet flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

This variety is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
