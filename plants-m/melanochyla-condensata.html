Melanochyla condensata

Melanochyla condensata, commonly known as the blackleaf lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, upper surface and a lighter, often greenish, lower surface. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with algae, which provide photosynthetic energy, while the fungal component offers structural support and protection. It is typically found on tree bark, rocks, and soil in temperate regions.

Melanochyla condensata plays an important ecological role in nutrient cycling and serves as an indicator of air quality.
