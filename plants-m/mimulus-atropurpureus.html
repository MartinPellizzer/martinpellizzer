Mimulus atropurpureus

Mimulus atropurpureus, commonly known as the purple monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking deep purple to reddish petals and yellow-orange throat, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats, including stream banks and meadows. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds dispersed by wind.

Mimulus atropurpureus is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
