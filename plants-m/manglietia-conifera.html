Manglietia conifera

Manglietia conifera, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in the rainy season. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. Its wood is dense and strong, making it valuable for construction and furniture.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
