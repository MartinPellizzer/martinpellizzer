Mespilodaphne nummularia

Mespilodaphne nummularia, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the branches. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are known for their silvery undersides, which give the plant its common name.

Mespilodaphne nummularia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitats.
