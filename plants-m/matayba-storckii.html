Matayba storckii

Matayba storckii, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into woody pods containing edible seeds. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall and has a long lifespan, often living over 100 years. It plays a crucial role in the ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

The seeds, known as Brazil nuts, are rich in nutrients and are commercially valuable.
