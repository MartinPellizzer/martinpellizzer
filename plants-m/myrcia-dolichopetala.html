Myrcia dolichopetala

Myrcia dolichopetala, commonly known as the long-petaled myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters. Myrcia dolichopetala typically grows in humid, tropical environments and is often found in dense forests or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
