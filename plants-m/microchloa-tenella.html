Microchloa tenella

Microchloa tenella, commonly known as the small-leafed grass or dwarf grass, is a small perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowering stems are short and produce small, inconspicuous flowers.

Microchloa tenella is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
