Mesembryanthemum ortendahlii

Mesembryanthemum ortendahlii, commonly known as the Namib Desert ice plant, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Namibia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the rainy season, typically in summer. Its root system is shallow, enabling it to absorb moisture from the surface quickly.

Mesembryanthemum ortendahlii is well adapted to extreme temperatures and low rainfall, making it a hardy species in semi-desert ecosystems.
