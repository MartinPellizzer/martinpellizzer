Meridiana pavonia

Meridiana pavonia, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, spiky flower clusters that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical name reflects its geographic origin and distinctive appearance.
