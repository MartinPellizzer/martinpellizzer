Macropsychanthus javanicus

Macropsychanthus javanicus, commonly known as the Java orchid, is a rare and exotic orchid species native to Indonesia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as rainforests, where it thrives on the forest floor or as an epiphyte on trees. Its root system is specialized for nutrient absorption from the surrounding soil and air.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
