Monochaetum osbeckioides

Monochaetum osbeckioides, commonly known as the Osbeck's spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in wetland and swampy environments, showcasing its adaptability to waterlogged soils. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spike of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are usually arranged in a compact cluster.

This species plays a vital role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and supporting local biodiversity.
