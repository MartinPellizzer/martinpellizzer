Myrceugenia camargoana

Myrceugenia camargoana, commonly known as the Camargoan myrtle, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the subtropical regions of southern Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. It produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.

Myrceugenia camargoana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
