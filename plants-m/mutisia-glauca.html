Mutisia glauca

Mutisia glauca, commonly known as the blue mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, blue or purple composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Mutisia glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
