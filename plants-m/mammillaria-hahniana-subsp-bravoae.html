Mammillaria hahniana subsp. bravoae

Mammillaria hahniana subsp.

bravoae, commonly known as the Bravo's hedgehog cactus, is a small, globular cactus native to Mexico. It features a dense covering of white to pale blue spines and is characterized by its compact, rounded shape.

The plant has a short, stout stem with areoles that bear both radial and centripetal spines. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer.

This subspecies is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
