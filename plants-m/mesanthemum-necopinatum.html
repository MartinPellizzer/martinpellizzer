Mesanthemum necopinatum

Mesanthemum necopinatum, commonly known as the Cape marigold, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Mesanthemum necopinatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
