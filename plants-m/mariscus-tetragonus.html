Mariscus tetragonus

Mariscus tetragonus, commonly known as the four-angled marsh plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its square, succulent stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly rough texture.

Mariscus tetragonus thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.
