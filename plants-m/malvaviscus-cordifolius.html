Malvaviscus cordifolius

Malvaviscus cordifolius, commonly known as the heart-leaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
