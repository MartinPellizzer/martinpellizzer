Myristica rumphii var. rumphii

Myristica rumphii var. rumphii, commonly known as nutmeg, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Banda Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody bark and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into capsules containing the valuable seeds used for nutmeg and mace. Its fruiting bodies are typically covered in a fibrous outer layer that separates from the inner seed.

The plant is also valued for its essential oils and has been historically significant in global trade.
