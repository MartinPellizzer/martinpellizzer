Montanoa tomentosa var. ternifolia

Montanoa tomentosa var. ternifolia, commonly known as the woolly montanoa, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with a soft, fuzzy texture.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
