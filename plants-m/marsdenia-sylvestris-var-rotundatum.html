Marsdenia sylvestris var. rotundatum

Marsdenia sylvestris var. rotundatum, commonly known as the rounded-leaf Marsdenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Asclepiadaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and distinctive floral structures. The plant features slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often rounded at the base. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, with a corona that resembles a crown, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
