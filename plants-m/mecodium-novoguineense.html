Mecodium novoguineense

Mecodium novoguineense, commonly known as the New Guinea mecodium, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of New Guinea.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is noted for its adaptability to humid climates and its role in local ecological systems.
