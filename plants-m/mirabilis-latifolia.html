Mirabilis latifolia

Mirabilis latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf four-o'clock, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the late afternoon and close at dawn. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of vibrant pink or red blooms. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Mirabilis latifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
