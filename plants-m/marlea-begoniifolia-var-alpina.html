Marlea begoniifolia var. alpina

Marlea begoniifolia var. alpina, commonly known as the alpine begoniifolia marlea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale pink, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found at high altitudes where the climate is temperate and the soil is well-drained.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a sought-after species among botanists and plant enthusiasts.
