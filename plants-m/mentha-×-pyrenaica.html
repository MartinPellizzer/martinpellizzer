Mentha × pyrenaica

Mentha × pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, slightly fuzzy leaves and slender, square stems. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, contributing to its ornamental value. Mentha × pyrenaica is often cultivated for its medicinal properties, including its use in traditional remedies for digestive and respiratory ailments.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a popular choice for both gardeners and herbalists.
