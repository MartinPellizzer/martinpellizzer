Martiodendron mediterraneum

Martiodendron mediterraneum, commonly known as the Mediterranean thornapple, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red berries. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and the plant has thorny stems that provide some protection from herbivores.

It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
