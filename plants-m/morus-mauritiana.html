Morus mauritiana

Morus mauritiana, commonly known as the Indian black mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The tree produces edible berries that are rich in nutrients and commonly used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.

The plant also plays a role in supporting local wildlife, particularly birds and insects.
