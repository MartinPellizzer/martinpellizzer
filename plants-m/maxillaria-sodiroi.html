Maxillaria sodiroi

Maxillaria sodiroi, commonly known as the Sodiro's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its pseudobulbs are cylindrical, and the leaves are usually arranged in a rosette pattern.

The flowers are borne on inflorescences that can last for several weeks, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
