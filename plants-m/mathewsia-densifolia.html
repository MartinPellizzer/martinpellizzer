Mathewsia densifolia

Mathewsia densifolia, commonly known as the dense-leaved mathewsia, is a shrub native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that provides habitat for various small animals and insects. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a glossy surface and are arranged alternately along the stems.

Mathewsia densifolia is often found in coastal heathlands and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils.
