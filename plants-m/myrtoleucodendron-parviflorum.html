Myrtoleucodendron parviflorum

Myrtoleucodendron parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered myrtle-leaved tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage with glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, adding ornamental value to its appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
