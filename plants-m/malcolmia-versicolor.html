Malcolmia versicolor

Malcolmia versicolor, commonly known as the common blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, herbaceous structure and produces clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed sites. Its leaves are usually hairy and oval-shaped, while the stems are erect and branched.

Malcolmia versicolor is known for its ability to tolerate drought and salt, making it a hardy plant in various environments.
