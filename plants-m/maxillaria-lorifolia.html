Maxillaria lorifolia

Maxillaria lorifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant features long, strap-like leaves and produces fragrant, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive spurred lip. Its botanical structure includes a pseudobulb that supports its growth and storage of nutrients.

Maxillaria lorifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
