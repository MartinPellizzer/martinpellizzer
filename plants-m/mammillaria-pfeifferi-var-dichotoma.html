Mammillaria pfeifferi var. dichotoma

Mammillaria pfeifferi var. dichotoma, commonly known as the Desert Cactus or the Two-branched Mammillaria, is a compact, globular cactus native to Mexico.

It features dense, white to pale yellow spines and small, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink to red. The plant has a ribbed stem with areoles that bear both spines and flowers. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique branching pattern and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus collections.
