Melastoma candidum var. nobotan

Melastoma candidum var. nobotan, commonly known as the white-flowered melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understoreys. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a glossy surface.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
