Malus × magdeburgensis

Malus × magdeburgensis, commonly known as the Magdeburg apple, is a hybrid deciduous tree belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Central Europe and is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy white flowers in spring and vibrant red or yellow foliage in autumn. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are typically used for culinary purposes or to make cider. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated for both ornamental and practical uses.
