Malus pumila var. rinkii

Malus pumila var. rinkii, commonly known as the Rink's apple, is a variant of the apple tree native to China.

It is characterized by its small, round fruits with a smooth, thin skin and a sweet, tart flavor. The plant typically grows as a deciduous tree with alternate, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and it is cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value.

The variety is valued for its cold hardiness and potential use in breeding programs for improved apple cultivars.
