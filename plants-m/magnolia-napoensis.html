Magnolia napoensis

Magnolia napoensis, commonly known as the Napo magnolia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Colombia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter, often displaying a mix of white and pink hues. The plant typically grows as a deciduous tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth surface and a waxy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its striking floral display.
