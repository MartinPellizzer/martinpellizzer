Mimulus quinquevulnerus

Mimulus quinquevulnerus, commonly known as the five-wound monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking orange to yellow flowers, which are typically tubular in shape. The plant has oval, waxy leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in stream banks and meadows.

Mimulus quinquevulnerus is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
