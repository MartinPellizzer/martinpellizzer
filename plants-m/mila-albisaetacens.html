Mila albisaetacens

Mila albisaetacens, commonly known as the white-haired mila, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and small, pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Native to arid and semi-arid regions, this plant is adapted to survive in dry environments with minimal water. Its fibrous roots help it retain moisture, while its waxy leaves reduce water loss.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
