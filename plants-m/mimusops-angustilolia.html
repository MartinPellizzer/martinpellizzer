Mimusops angustilolia

Mimusops angustilolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ivory fruit, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the branches. The tree produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its bark is thick and rough, often exuding a milky sap when cut.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in various regions for its wood and medicinal properties.
