Malvinda carpinifolia

Malvinda carpinifolia, commonly known as the wood-rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a rough texture, and the stems are often woody and covered in fine hairs.

Malvinda carpinifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
