Macrocalyx tomentosa var. rubra

Macrocalyx tomentosa var. rubra, commonly known as the red-flowered woolly mallow, is a variant of the woolly mallow species native to certain regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive red petals, which contrast with its fuzzy, grayish-green foliage. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain numerous seeds, typical of the Malvaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
