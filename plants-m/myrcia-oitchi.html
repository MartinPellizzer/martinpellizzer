Myrcia oitchi

Myrcia oitchi, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Myrcia oitchi produces small, round berries that are typically red or purple when mature.

It is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its aromatic properties.
