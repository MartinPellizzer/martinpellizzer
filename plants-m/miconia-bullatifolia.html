Miconia bullatifolia

Miconia bullatifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, berry-like fruits. Miconia bullatifolia is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a problematic species in certain regions.
