Mapania assimilis subsp. assimilis

Mapania assimilis subsp.

assimilis, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its rosette of velvety, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of violet, pink, and white.

The plant thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires indirect light to prevent leaf scorching. It is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.

The subspecies assimilis is distinguished by its compact growth habit and vibrant floral displays.
