Mikania wurdackii

Mikania wurdackii, commonly known as the climbing Mikania, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by fluffy, white to grayish seed heads. Native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is often considered a weed in agricultural and natural areas.
