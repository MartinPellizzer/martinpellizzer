Malanthos

Malanthos, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Malvaceae.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It is often found in disturbed soils and can become invasive in certain regions.

Botanically, it is noted for its fibrous roots and its ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
