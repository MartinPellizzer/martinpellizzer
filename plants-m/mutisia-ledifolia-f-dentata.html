Mutisia ledifolia f. dentata

Mutisia ledifolia f. dentata, commonly known as the toothed mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant has a woody stem and is often found in dry, open habitats. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with distinctive toothed margins, giving it its specific epithet.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
