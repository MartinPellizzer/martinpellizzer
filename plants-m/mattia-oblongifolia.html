Mattia oblongifolia

Mattia oblongifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf mat plant, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often blooming during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Due to its drought tolerance and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
