Malpighia laurifolia

Malpighia laurifolia, commonly known as the glossy apple or golden apple, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malpighiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas, particularly Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and clusters of small, bright red berries that are edible. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forest understories.

The berries are rich in vitamin C and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
