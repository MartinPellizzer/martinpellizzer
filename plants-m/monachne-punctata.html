Monachne punctata

Monachne punctata, commonly known as the punctate bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually blue or purple in color, with a distinctive pattern of spots on the petals. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic features.
