Masdevallia dickinsoniana

Masdevallia dickinsoniana, commonly known as the Dickinson's masdevallia, is a rare and exquisite orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its intricate, often colorful floral structures and its delicate, strap-like leaves. The plant typically grows as a small epiphyte, clinging to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers, which bloom in shades of pink, red, and white, are highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts.

Due to its limited natural habitat and threats from deforestation, it is considered endangered and is often cultivated in conservation programs.
