Magnolia quinquepeta

Magnolia quinquepeta, commonly known as the five-petaled magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The flowers are typically white or pale pink and have five distinct petals, giving the plant its common name. The leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate with a smooth margin.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blooms.
