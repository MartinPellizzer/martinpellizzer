Melastoma malabathricum subsp. normale

Melastoma malabathricum subsp.

normale, commonly known as the common melastoma, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia. It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and has opposite, ovate leaves with a leathery texture.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red, followed by orange or red berries. It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forest understories and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
