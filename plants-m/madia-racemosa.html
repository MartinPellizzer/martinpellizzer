Madia racemosa

Madia racemosa, commonly known as the California flossflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, fluffy white to pale pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Madia racemosa is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
