Monodia

Monodia, commonly known as the "bushy spurge," is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its dense, branching structure and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces a milky, latex-like sap that is toxic and can cause skin irritation. Its leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Monodia thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
