Madia glomerata var. ramosa

Madia glomerata var. ramosa, commonly known as the branched madia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a rough texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense heads.

The species is noted for its adaptability to arid conditions and its role in supporting local pollinators.
