Miconia obovalis

Miconia obovalis, commonly known as the oval-leaf miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, ovate leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that are typically arranged in clusters at the base of the leaves. Miconia obovalis is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, which can make it an invasive species in certain regions.

Its botanical features make it an important subject in ecological and horticultural studies.
