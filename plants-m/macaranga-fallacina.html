Macaranga fallacina

Macaranga fallacina, commonly known as the false macaranga, is a tropical tree belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in agroforestry systems.
