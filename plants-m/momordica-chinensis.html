Momordica chinensis

Momordica chinensis, commonly known as bitter melon, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, ridged fruits that range in color from green to orange when ripe. The plant produces yellow flowers and has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its botanical name reflects its origin in China, and it is widely cultivated for its edible fruit.

The plant belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers and squash.
