Myrcia gracilis var. prasina

Myrcia gracilis var. prasina, commonly known as the pink myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its slender, often pendulous branches and small, aromatic leaves. Its flowers are typically pink or white, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
