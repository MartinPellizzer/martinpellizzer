Micranthes pensylvanica var. purpuripetala

Micranthes pensylvanica var. purpuripetala, commonly known as the purple-petaled sweet peppergrass, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features small, clustered white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape, with hairy surfaces. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical interest lies in its unique floral structure and seasonal blooming habits.
