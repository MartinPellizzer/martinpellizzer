Maclura tricuspidata

Maclura tricuspidata, commonly known as the honeylocust, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are usually hairy and pointed. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, followed by distinctive, spiny pods that contain edible seeds. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to thrive in a variety of soil types.

The plant is also noted for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate urban environments.
