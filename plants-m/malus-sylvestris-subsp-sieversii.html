Malus sylvestris subsp. sieversii

Malus sylvestris subsp.

sieversii, commonly known as the Central Asian apple, is a wild species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in high-altitude environments.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sour apples in the autumn. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

This subspecies is valued for its genetic diversity and is often used in breeding programs to improve apple cultivars.
