Mimulus alatus var. chandleri

Mimulus alatus var. chandleri, commonly known as the Chandler's trailing monkeyflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the coastal regions of California.

It features slender, trailing stems that produce vibrant orange to yellow flowers with distinctive spurred petals. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its flowers bloom from spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
