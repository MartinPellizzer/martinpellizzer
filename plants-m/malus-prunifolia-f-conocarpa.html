Malus prunifolia f. conocarpa

Malus prunifolia f. conocarpa, commonly known as the Mexican crabapple, is a compact, ornamental shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It features small, rounded leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing dense foliage. The plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers in early spring, followed by small, edible fruits that turn red or yellow as they mature. Its compact size and showy blooms make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

It is relatively low maintenance and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
