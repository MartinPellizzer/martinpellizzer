Manochlaenia pendula

Manochlaenia pendula, commonly known as the hanging man orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous inflorescences that bear clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

The flowers, which range in color from white to pale pink, are notable for their intricate structure and attract various pollinators.
