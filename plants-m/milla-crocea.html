Milla crocea

Milla crocea, commonly known as the yellow milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Milla crocea is known for its milky sap, which is a characteristic feature of the milkweed genus.
