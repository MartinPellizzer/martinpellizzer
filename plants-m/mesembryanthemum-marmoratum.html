Mesembryanthemum marmoratum

Mesembryanthemum marmoratum, commonly known as the marble plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and thick leaves. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often appearing in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it an attractive addition to arid landscapes.
