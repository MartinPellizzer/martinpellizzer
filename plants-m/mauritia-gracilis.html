Mauritia gracilis

Mauritia gracilis, commonly known as the slender palm, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnately compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by edible fruits that are consumed by both humans and wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to habitat loss, Mauritia gracilis is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
