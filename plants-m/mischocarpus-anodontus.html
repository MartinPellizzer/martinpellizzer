Mischocarpus anodontus

Mischocarpus anodontus, commonly known as the Brazilian walnut, is a species of flowering plant in the family Proteaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and distinctive fruit structure. The plant produces a heavy, spiny capsule that contains several seeds, which are encased in a hard, woody shell. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The Brazilian walnut is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some uses in traditional medicine.
