Mapania coriandrum

Mapania coriandrum, commonly known as coriander, is an annual herbaceous plant native to regions of southern Europe, southwestern Asia, and northern Africa.

It features feathery, finely divided leaves and small white or pinkish flowers that bloom in umbels. The plant produces small, triangular seeds known as cumin, which are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its aromatic foliage and seeds contain essential oils rich in compounds like limonene and linalool.

Mapania coriandrum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for both ornamental and practical gardening.
