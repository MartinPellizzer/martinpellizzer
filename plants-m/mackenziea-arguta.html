Mackenziea arguta

Mackenziea arguta, commonly known as the Mackenzie's bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, bladder-like structures that trap tiny aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers.

Mackenziea arguta plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and contributing to nutrient cycling.
