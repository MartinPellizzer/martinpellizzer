Melinis amethystea

Melinis amethystea, commonly known as the purple grass or amethyst grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and striking purple or pinkish inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds ornamental value to gardens.

Melinis amethystea is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is used in ornamental horticulture.
