Mentha × fragrans

Mentha × fragrans, commonly known as apple mint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Mentha piperita (peppermint) and Mentha spicata (spearmint).

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers in whorled clusters, which are typically borne on erect spikes. Its aromatic leaves emit a sweet, minty fragrance, making it popular for culinary and medicinal uses.

Mentha × fragrans thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
