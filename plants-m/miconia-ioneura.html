Miconia ioneura

Miconia ioneura, commonly known as the white-veined miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with prominent white veins. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that are typically followed by ornamental, winged fruits. Miconia ioneura is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in ornamental gardens.

However, it can be invasive in certain regions, posing a threat to native ecosystems.
