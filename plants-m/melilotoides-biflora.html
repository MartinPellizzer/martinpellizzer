Melilotoides biflora

Melilotoides biflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered melilot, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellow or white flowers that bloom in spring, often in pairs, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Melilotoides biflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
