Melissitus cachemyrianus

Melissitus cachemyrianus, commonly known as the Cachemyrian honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in clusters, typically in shades of yellow and orange, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a small, red drupe that ripens in late summer.

Melissitus cachemyrianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
