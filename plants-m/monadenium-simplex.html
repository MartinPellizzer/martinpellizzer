Monadenium simplex

Monadenium simplex, commonly known as the desert candelabra, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its branching structure and small, clustered flowers. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its succulent stems and reduced leaves. It produces yellow or white flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Monadenium simplex is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
