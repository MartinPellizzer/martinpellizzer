Mimulus moschatus var. harrisonii

Mimulus moschatus var. harrisonii, commonly known as the yellow monkeyflower, is a strikingly beautiful annual plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which are typically tubular with a distinctive spur at the back. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, streambanks, and other wetland habitats. Its botanical features include hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, which contribute to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its use in native plant gardens.
