Monardella purpurea subsp. neglecta

Monardella purpurea subsp.

neglecta, commonly known as purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features showy, tubular pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight.

Its nectar attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
