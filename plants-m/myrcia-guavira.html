Myrcia guavira

Myrcia guavira, commonly known as Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible but have a sharp, acidic taste. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Myrcia guavira is also noted for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
