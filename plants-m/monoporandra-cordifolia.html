Monoporandra cordifolia

Monoporandra cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf monoporandra, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically deep green and have a smooth texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom during the wet season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
