Myrcia lineata

Myrcia lineata, commonly known as the purple myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a leathery texture, while its fruits are small, woody capsules that contain several seeds.

Myrcia lineata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
