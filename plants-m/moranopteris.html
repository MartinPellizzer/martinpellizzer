Moranopteris

Moranopteris, commonly known as the ghost fern, is a genus of ferns belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

These plants are characterized by their delicate, lace-like fronds that give them a ghostly appearance. They have small, intricate leaves with a unique structure that allows for efficient water absorption.

The genus is notable for its ecological role in maintaining soil stability and supporting biodiversity in tropical and subtropical regions.
