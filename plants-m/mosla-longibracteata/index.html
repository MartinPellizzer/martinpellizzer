Mosla Longibracteata

Mosla longibracteata is a lesser-known member of the Lamiaceae family known for its ornamental value and medicinal properties. This perennial plant is native to regions of Asia, particularly China and the Korean peninsula. It features slender stems and small, fragrant leaves that emit a pleasant minty aroma. The plant is often cultivated in gardens for its ability to thrive in shaded environments. Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a valuable addition to both traditional and modern horticulture.
