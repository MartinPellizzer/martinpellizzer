Monelasmum umbricola

Monelasmum umbricola, commonly known as the dark soil vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often overlooked. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is typically found in forest understories. Its stems are woody and can grow quite long, allowing it to climb and envelop tree trunks.

Monelasmum umbricola plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat by providing shelter and food for various insect species.
