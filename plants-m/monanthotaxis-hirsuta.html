Monanthotaxis hirsuta

Monanthotaxis hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy monanthotaxis, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.
