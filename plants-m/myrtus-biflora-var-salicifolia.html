Myrtus biflora var. salicifolia

Myrtus biflora var. salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaved myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features lance-shaped leaves that resemble willow leaves, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. Its aromatic foliage is often used in traditional herbal remedies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
