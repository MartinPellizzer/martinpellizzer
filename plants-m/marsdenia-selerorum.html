Marsdenia selerorum

Marsdenia selerorum, commonly known as the Seler's Marsdenia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers, which are followed by distinctive, fleshy, red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
