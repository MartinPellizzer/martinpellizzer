Machaerium obovatum

Machaerium obovatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed machaerium, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are typically arranged in dense, raceme-like clusters. Machaerium obovatum is known for its ornamental value, with vibrant yellow or orange flowers that attract pollinators.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
