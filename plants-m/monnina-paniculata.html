Monnina paniculata

Monnina paniculata, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. The plant features slender, branching stems that bear clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture.

Monnina paniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
