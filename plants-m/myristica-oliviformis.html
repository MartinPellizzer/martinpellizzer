Myristica oliviformis

Myristica oliviformis, commonly known as the olive-shaped myristica, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The plant produces drupes with a hard, woody endocarp, which encloses a single seed. It is often cultivated for its aromatic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ecological and economic significance in its native habitat.
