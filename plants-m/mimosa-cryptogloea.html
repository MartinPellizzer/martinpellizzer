Mimosa cryptogloea

Mimosa cryptogloea, commonly known as the velvet mimosa, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria. It typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The plant's unique appearance and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
