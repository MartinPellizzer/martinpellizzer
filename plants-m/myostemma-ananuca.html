Myostemma ananuca

Myostemma ananuca, commonly known as the white star cactus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its tall, columnar stems covered in white, star-shaped spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its adaptations include thick, fleshy stems for water storage and reduced leaves to minimize water loss.

Myostemma ananuca is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry environments.
