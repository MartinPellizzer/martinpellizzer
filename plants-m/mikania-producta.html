Mikania producta

Mikania producta, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
