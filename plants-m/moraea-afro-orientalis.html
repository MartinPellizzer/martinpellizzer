Moraea afro-orientalis

Moraea afro-orientalis, commonly known as the African-oriental moraea, is a striking perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from deep purple to white. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
