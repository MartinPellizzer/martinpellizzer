Myrcia cucullata

Myrcia cucullata, commonly known as the velvetleaf myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, hard seeds.

Myrcia cucullata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
