Melocactus douradaensis

Melocactus douradaensis, commonly known as the golden melon cactus, is a species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and a dense covering of white, woolly hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Melocactus douradaensis is often cultivated for its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
