Malpighia punicifolia var. lancifolia

Malpighia punicifolia var. lancifolia, commonly known as the Natal plum, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It features small, glossy, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bright red, fleshy berries. The plant is adapted to arid environments and has a shallow root system.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits, which are rich in vitamin C. The species is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
