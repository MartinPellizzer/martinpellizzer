Malacocarpus concinnus

Malacocarpus concinnus, commonly known as the slender-stemmed cycad, is a unique species belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stem and pinnately compound leaves that are typically bright green. The plant produces small, spherical cones rather than flowers, which are a distinguishing feature of cycads. It is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical climates with high humidity.

Due to its slow growth and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
