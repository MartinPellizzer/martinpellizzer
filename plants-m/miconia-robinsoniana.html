Miconia robinsoniana

Miconia robinsoniana, commonly known as the Hawaiian miconia, is a member of the Melastomataceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to the Hawaiian Islands, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant has a woody stem with smooth, grayish bark and produces small, fleshy fruits that are dispersed by birds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mesic to wet forest habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it has been introduced to other tropical regions, though it can become invasive in some areas.
