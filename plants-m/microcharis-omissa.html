Microcharis omissa

Microcharis omissa, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow berries that contain toxic seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Despite its beauty, it should be handled with care due to its toxic properties.
