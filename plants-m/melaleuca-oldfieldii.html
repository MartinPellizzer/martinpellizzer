Melaleuca oldfieldii

Melaleuca oldfieldii, commonly known as Oldfield's tea tree, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to the coastal regions of eastern Australia, particularly Queensland and New South Wales. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal heathlands.

Melaleuca oldfieldii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal dunes.
