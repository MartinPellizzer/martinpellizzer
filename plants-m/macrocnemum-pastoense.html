Macrocnemum pastoense

Macrocnemum pastoense, commonly known as the Pasto grass, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil, Colombia, and Venezuela.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to brownish flowers.

Macrocnemum pastoense is valued for its forage quality and is often used in pasture systems due to its high nutritional value and adaptability.
