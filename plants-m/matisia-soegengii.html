Matisia soegengii

Matisia soegengii, commonly known as the Java matisia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Meliaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, with smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically greenish-yellow in color, and it produces woody capsules as fruit.

The plant is valued for its timber and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
