Melilotus vulgaris var. minor

Melilotus vulgaris var. minor, commonly known as lesser melilot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Lesser melilot is valued for its medicinal properties and historical use in herbal remedies.
