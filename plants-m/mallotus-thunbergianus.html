Mallotus thunbergianus

Mallotus thunbergianus, commonly known as the Japanese holly or thunbergia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its adaptability and aesthetic appeal contribute to its widespread cultivation in tropical and subtropical regions.
