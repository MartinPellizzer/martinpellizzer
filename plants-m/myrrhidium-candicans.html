Myrrhidium candicans

Myrrhidium candicans, commonly known as the candle plant, is a unique succulent native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces striking, candle-like yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, providing some protection against intense sunlight.

Myrrhidium candicans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a houseplant.
