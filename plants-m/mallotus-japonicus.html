Mallotus japonicus

Mallotus japonicus, commonly known as Japanese holly, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by distinctive red, spiny fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Mallotus japonicus is also valued for its use in traditional medicine.
