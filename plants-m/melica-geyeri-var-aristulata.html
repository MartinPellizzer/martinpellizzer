Melica geyeri var. aristulata

Melica geyeri var. aristulata, commonly known as the aristulate melica, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of spikelets, each bearing small, two-lobed flowers.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
