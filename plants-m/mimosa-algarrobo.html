Mimosa algarrobo

Mimosa algarrobo, commonly known as the white mimosa, is a shrub native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are composed of numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in coastal areas and sandy soils.

Mimosa algarrobo is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important plant in soil enrichment.
