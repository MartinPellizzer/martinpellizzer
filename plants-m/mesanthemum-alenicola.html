Mesanthemum alenicola

Mesanthemum alenicola, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color, resembling tiny tomatoes. These fruits are edible and have a tangy, slightly sweet flavor, often used in culinary applications.

Mesanthemum alenicola thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
