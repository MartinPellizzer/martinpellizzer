Monachne

Monachne, commonly known as the common marsh orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Monachne has lance-shaped leaves and produces a single flower spike that blooms in late summer.

It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by supporting pollinators and contributing to biodiversity.
