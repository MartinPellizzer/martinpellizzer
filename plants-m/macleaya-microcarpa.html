Macleaya microcarpa

Macleaya microcarpa, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are borne in clusters at the top of the stems. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a rough, textured surface.

Macleaya microcarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also used in traditional medicine.
