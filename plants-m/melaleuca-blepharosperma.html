Melaleuca blepharosperma

Melaleuca blepharosperma, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces aromatic, honey-scented nectar that attracts pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand drought conditions.
