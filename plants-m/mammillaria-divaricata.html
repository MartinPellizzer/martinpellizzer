Mammillaria divaricata

Mammillaria divaricata, commonly known as the Mexican hat cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It is a popular choice for rock gardens due to its compact size and ornamental appearance.

Mammillaria divaricata is also known for its slow growth rate and ability to thrive in well-draining soil.
