Maxillaria pinasensis

Maxillaria pinasensis, commonly known as the Pina's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its pseudobulbs are short and stout, supporting its foliage and flowering structures.

Maxillaria pinasensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
