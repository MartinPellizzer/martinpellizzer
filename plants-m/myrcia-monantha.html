Myrcia monantha

Myrcia monantha, commonly known as the pink myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Venezuela. The plant is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer. Myrcia monantha is often found in humid, lowland areas and is adapted to thrive in shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of the flora in its native ecosystems.
