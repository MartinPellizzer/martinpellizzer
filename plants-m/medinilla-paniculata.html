Medinilla paniculata

Medinilla paniculata, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a tropical shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces fragrant, clustered inflorescences that resemble bells, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Medinilla paniculata is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
