Miltonia regnellii var. purpurea

Miltonia regnellii var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple-flowered orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple petals and a yellowish lip that often has a contrasting coloration. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often during the summer months, and it prefers bright, indirect light and high humidity.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in greenhouses and as a houseplant.
