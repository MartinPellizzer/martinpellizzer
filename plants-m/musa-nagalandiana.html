Musa nagalandiana

Musa nagalandiana, commonly known as the Nagaland banana, is a species of banana native to the northeastern region of India.

It is a herbaceous plant that grows in tropical and subtropical climates, characterized by its large, broad leaves and clustered inflorescence. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically smaller and more elongated compared to common bananas. Botanically, it belongs to the Musaceae family and is closely related to other banana species.

Its cultivation is significant in local agriculture and traditional practices in its native habitat.
