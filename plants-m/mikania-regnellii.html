Mikania regnellii

Mikania regnellii, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and sprawling habit. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, which bloom in the early morning. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are twining and can climb or trail along the ground.

Mikania regnellii is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and clearings, where it can thrive in full sun.
