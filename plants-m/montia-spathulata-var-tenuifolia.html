Montia spathulata var. tenuifolia

Montia spathulata var. tenuifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved common mallow, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically lanceolate to ovate, with a hairy texture and a bluish-green color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
