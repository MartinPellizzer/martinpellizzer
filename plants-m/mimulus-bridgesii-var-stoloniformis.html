Mimulus bridgesii var. stoloniformis

Mimulus bridgesii var. stoloniformis, commonly known as the stoloniferous monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit, with stolons that root at the nodes and form dense mats. The plant produces showy, orange-yellow flowers with five petals, typical of the Mimulus genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in stream banks and wet meadows.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and wetland habitats.
