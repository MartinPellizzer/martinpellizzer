Myrtus billardiana

Myrtus billardiana, commonly known as the Corsican myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, glossy leaves and small white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, dark purple berry that contains several seeds.

The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also valued for its essential oils.
