Mycaranthes floribunda

Mycaranthes floribunda, commonly known as the clustered starflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged in whorls around the stem.

Mycaranthes floribunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, showy blooms.
