Macrotyloma fimbriatum

Macrotyloma fimbriatum, commonly known as the fringed cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with fringed margins, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It is often grown as a forage crop due to its high protein content and adaptability to various soil types.

The plant also has medicinal uses in some traditional systems, though its botanical properties are still under study.
