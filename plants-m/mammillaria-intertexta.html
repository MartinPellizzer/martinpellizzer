Mammillaria intertexta

Mammillaria intertexta, commonly known as the Mexican snowball cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, globular shape and white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a thick, ribbed stem covered with small, white tubercles that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
