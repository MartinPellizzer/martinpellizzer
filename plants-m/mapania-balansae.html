Mapania balansae

Mapania balansae, commonly known as the Balansae maple, is a rare and unique species native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, featuring palmately lobed leaves that vary in color from deep green to reddish hues. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring, followed by winged seeds dispersed by wind. Its botanical significance lies in its role as a potential indicator species for tropical montane ecosystems.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
