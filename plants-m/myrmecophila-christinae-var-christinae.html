Myrmecophila christinae var. christinae

Myrmecophila christinae var. christinae, commonly known as the Christin's myrmecophila, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and the presence of specialized structures called myrmecophytes, which provide shelter for ants. The plant typically features elongated, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy coating, and its flowers are small, clustered, and often fragrant. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique ecological relationships and ornamental value, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
