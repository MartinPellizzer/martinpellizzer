Micropus angustifolius

Micropus angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features basal leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces a single, upright stem that bears a cluster of yellow flowers, which later develop into fluffy white seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
