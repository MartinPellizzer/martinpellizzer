Maclura brasiliensis var. reticulata

Maclura brasiliensis var. reticulata, commonly known as the reticulated horse chestnut, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves with a reticulated venation pattern. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to red berries containing toxic seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
