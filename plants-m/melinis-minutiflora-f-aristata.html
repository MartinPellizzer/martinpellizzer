Melinis minutiflora f. aristata

Melinis minutiflora f. aristata, commonly known as the aristate carpetgrass, is a variant of the annual grass species native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are borne on short, upright spikes. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to form dense mats, it is considered a weed in many regions.
