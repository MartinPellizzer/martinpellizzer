Myrcia terebinthacea

Myrcia terebinthacea, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known species like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant also produces small, dark-colored berries that are edible but not commonly consumed.
