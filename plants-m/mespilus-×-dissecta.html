Mespilus × dissecta

Mespilus × dissecta, commonly known as the European whitebeam, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Mespilus germanica and Mespilus babingtonii.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting a variety of wildlife.
