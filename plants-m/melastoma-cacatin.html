Melastoma cacatin

Melastoma cacatin, commonly known as the purple false heather, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its showy, often violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are usually opposite and oval-shaped, with a glossy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several small, dark seeds.
