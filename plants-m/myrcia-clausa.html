Myrcia clausa

Myrcia clausa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas.

Myrcia clausa is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
