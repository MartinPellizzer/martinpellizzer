Myrciaria alagoana

Myrciaria alagoana, commonly known as the Alagoas myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of northeastern Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature, and they are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in sandy, coastal soils.
