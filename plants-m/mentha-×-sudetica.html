Mentha × sudetica

Mentha × sudetica, commonly known as the Siberian mint, is a hybrid plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and fragrant, aromatic leaves that are often used in herbal remedies. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. Its flowers are small and clustered in whorls, producing a distinctive minty scent.

Mentha × sudetica is valued for its medicinal properties and is often cultivated for both ornamental and therapeutic purposes.
