Malus pumila var. niedzwetzkyana

Malus pumila var. niedzwetzkyana, commonly known as the Niedzwetzkyana crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy white to pink flowers in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture and ecological restoration projects.
