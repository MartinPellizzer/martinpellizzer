Monticalia arbutifolia

Monticalia arbutifolia, commonly known as the roseleaf heath, is a shrub native to the humid forests of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heathers, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, nutrient-poor soils, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive rose-like appearance, giving it its common name.

Monticalia arbutifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
