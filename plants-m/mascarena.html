Mascarena

Mascarena, commonly known as the Mascarene daisy, is a flowering plant native to the Mascarene Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and saline conditions. It produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Mascarena is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
