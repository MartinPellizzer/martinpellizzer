Monstera ovata

Monstera ovata, commonly known as the Swiss cheese plant, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that develop fenestrations or holes as they mature. The plant has a vining growth habit and can reach lengths of several meters when supported. Its leaves are typically a deep green with a slightly glossy surface.

Monstera ovata thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
