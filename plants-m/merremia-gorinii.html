Merremia gorinii

Merremia gorinii, commonly known as the tropical morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Merremia gorinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
