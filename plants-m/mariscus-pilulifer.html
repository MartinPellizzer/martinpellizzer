Mariscus pilulifer

Mariscus pilulifer, commonly known as the bead plant, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, bead-like fruits that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Mariscus pilulifer is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal remedies.
