Mesospinidium aurantiacum

Mesospinidium aurantiacum, commonly known as the orange spinidium, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange petals and small, spiny leaves that give it its scientific name. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests. Its flowers emit a subtle fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths.

Due to habitat loss, Mesospinidium aurantiacum is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
