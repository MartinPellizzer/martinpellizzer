Myrcia ferruginosa

Myrcia ferruginosa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, red or orange berries. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are small and white or pink, often clustered in dense inflorescences.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its use in traditional medicine.
