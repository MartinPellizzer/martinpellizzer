Melolobium villosum

Melolobium villosum, commonly known as the woolly melolobium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Melolobium villosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
