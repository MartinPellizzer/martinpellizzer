Moringa erecta

Moringa erecta, commonly known as the horseradish tree, is a fast-growing, drought-tolerant shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features a thick, woody stem with branching patterns that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The leaves are alternate, simple, and trifoliate, with a pungent odor when crushed. Its small, yellow flowers are followed by pods containing edible seeds and pods that can be used in traditional medicine.

Moringa erecta is valued for its nutritional content and adaptability to various soil conditions.
