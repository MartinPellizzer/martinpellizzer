Mundulea pondoensis

Mundulea pondoensis, commonly known as the Pondo acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, and it produces legume pods that contain seeds.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
