Megastachya pulchella

Megastachya pulchella, commonly known as the showy stachys, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bright pink to red tubular flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Megastachya pulchella is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
