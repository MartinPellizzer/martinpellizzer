Mimulus fremontii

Mimulus fremontii, commonly known as the Fremont's monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, spurred flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, including slopes and canyon walls. It has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Mimulus fremontii is often found in California and parts of Nevada, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils.
