Mycaranthes oblitterata

Mycaranthes oblitterata, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, white, ephemeral flowers that appear briefly during the rainy season. The plant has a succulent, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. Its leaves are typically reduced to small spines, minimizing water loss.

The ghost flower is known for its elusive blooming pattern, making it a rare and fascinating subject in botanical studies.
