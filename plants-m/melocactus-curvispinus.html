Melocactus curvispinus

Melocactus curvispinus, commonly known as the hooked spines melocactus, is a species of cactus native to southern Brazil.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and dense covering of spines that curve inward. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the rainy season, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
