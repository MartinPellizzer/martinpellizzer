Masdevallia melina

Masdevallia melina, commonly known as the Melina Masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often brightly colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically has dark green, strap-like leaves and produces intricate, spurred flowers with a complex structure typical of the orchid family. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique and delicate appearance.
