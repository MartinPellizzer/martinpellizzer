Myrtoleucodendron nesophyllum

Myrtoleucodendron nesophyllum, commonly known as the silver-leafed myrtle, is a rare and unique plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its striking silvery-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.
