Myrcia picachoana

Myrcia picachoana, commonly known as the Picachoan Myrtle, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential medicinal use.
