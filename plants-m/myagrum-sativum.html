Myagrum sativum

Myagrum sativum, commonly known as the mamey sapote, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It produces an edible fruit with a sweet, creamy pulp that resembles a mix of persimmon and vanilla. The plant is valued for its nutritional content, including high levels of vitamins A and C. Botanically, it belongs to the family Sapotaceae and has simple, alternate leaves with a leathery texture.

The fruit develops from an inferior ovary and is typically harvested when fully mature and orange in color.
