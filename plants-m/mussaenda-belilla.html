Mussaenda belilla

Mussaenda belilla, commonly known as the flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences that range in color from yellow to orange, resembling flames. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant blooms.

Mussaenda belilla is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
