Metrosideros collina var. incana

Metrosideros collina var. incana, commonly known as the silver-leaf pohutukawa, is a variant of the pohutukawa tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on the leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces vibrant red flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found growing on rocky shores or sandy soils.

This variant is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in New Zealand's coastal ecosystems.
