Malpighia lucida

Malpighia lucida, commonly known as the golden mamey or golden starapple, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators and its role in traditional medicine.
