Melampodium Minimum

Mexican sunflower, scientifically known as Melampodium minimum, is a vibrant and resilient plant native to the warm climates of Mexico and the southern United States. This compact annual plant is prized for its bright orange to red flower heads that resemble miniature sunflowers, creating a striking visual contrast against its green foliage. Thriving in full sun and well-drained soil, it is a popular choice for gardeners looking to add color and texture to their landscapes. Its ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees makes it an ecological asset as well as an ornamental delight. With its easy care requirements and long blooming season, Melampodium minimum is a versatile plant that brings both beauty and biodiversity to any garden.
