Myricaria vaginata

Myricaria vaginata, commonly known as the bladderbrush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Eurasia.

It is a shrub or subshrub with narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of wax. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are borne on elongated, brush-like structures. Its root system is adapted to survive in dry conditions, allowing it to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils.

The plant is also known for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in its native habitats.
