Microchilus brachyrrhynchus

Microchilus brachyrrhynchus, commonly known as the short-beaked microchilus, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers and unique morphological features that distinguish it from other orchid genera. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest undergrowth where it relies on specific mycorrhizal relationships for nutrient uptake. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, adapted to maximize light absorption in low-light conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
