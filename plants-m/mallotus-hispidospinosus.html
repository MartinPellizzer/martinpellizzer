Mallotus hispidospinosus

Mallotus hispidospinosus, commonly known as the rough-spined mallot, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

Mallotus hispidospinosus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
