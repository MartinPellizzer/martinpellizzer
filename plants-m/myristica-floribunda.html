Myristica floribunda

Myristica floribunda, commonly known as the nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody structure and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits containing hard, aromatic seeds. Its most notable botanical features include the aromatic oils found in its seeds, which are used in various culinary and medicinal applications.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland tropical rainforests.
