Morus zanthoxylon

Morus zanthoxylon, commonly known as the white mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically consumed by silkworms. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its fibrous bark and edible leaves.

Morus zanthoxylon plays an important role in traditional medicine and ecological systems.
