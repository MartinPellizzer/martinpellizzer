Melaleuca semiteres

Melaleuca semiteres, commonly known as the narrow-leaf paperbark, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that gives it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters.

Melaleuca semiteres is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
