Myrcia cotonosa

Myrcia cotonosa, commonly known as the cotton bush, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, white, woolly flowers that resemble cotton, hence its common name. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in a fine, soft pubescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Myrcia cotonosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
