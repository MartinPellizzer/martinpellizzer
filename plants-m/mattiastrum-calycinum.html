Mattiastrum calycinum

Mattiastrum calycinum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its growth habit is herbaceous, with a tendency to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

Mattiastrum calycinum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.
