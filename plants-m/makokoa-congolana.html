Makokoa congolana

Makokoa congolana, commonly known as the African moonseed, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits. Its seeds are encased in a hard, woody shell and are often used in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of Makokoa congolana make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
