Mitratheca richardsonioides

Mitratheca richardsonioides, commonly known as the Richardson's tarplant, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, with showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered threatened or endangered in many parts of its range.
