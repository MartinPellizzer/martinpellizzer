Mikania cabrerae

Mikania cabrerae, commonly known as the Caribbean morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, bright yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded areas.

Due to its rapid growth and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens but can become invasive if not managed properly.
