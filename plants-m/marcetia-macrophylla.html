Marcetia macrophylla

Marcetia macrophylla, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by edible tubers that are rich in starch. Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
