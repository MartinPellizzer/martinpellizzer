Memora duckei

Memora duckei, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a sprawling growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Memora duckei is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
