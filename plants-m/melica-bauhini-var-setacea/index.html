Melica Bauhini Var. Setacea

Melica bauhini var. setacea is a lesser-known grass species that thrives in specific ecological niches. It is native to regions with temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. This variant is distinguished by its unique inflorescence structure and dense foliage. Despite its subtle appearance, it plays an important role in local ecosystems. Its study offers insights into grassland biodiversity and adaptation strategies.
