Monocera walkeri

Monocera walkeri, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Monocera walkeri is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
