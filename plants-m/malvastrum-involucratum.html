Malvastrum involucratum

Malvastrum involucratum, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features soft, fuzzy leaves that are typically oval to heart-shaped, with a distinctive velvety texture. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Malvastrum involucratum is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
