Myrianthus serratus var. cuneifolius

Myrianthus serratus var. cuneifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf myrianthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces showy, yellowish-white flowers arranged in panicle inflorescences, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained, fertile soil.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in gardens and natural habitats.
