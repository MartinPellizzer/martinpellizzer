Mussaenda pubescens var. alba

Mussaenda pubescens var. alba, commonly known as white mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, white, tubular flowers that bloom in summer. These flowers are often surrounded by velvety, yellow bracts that add to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and floral display.
