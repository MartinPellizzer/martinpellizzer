Melanthesa

Melanthesa, commonly known as the silverleaf plant, is a member of the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the carrot family. Melanthesa thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in temperate climates.

It is often used in herbal medicine for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
