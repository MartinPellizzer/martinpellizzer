Micromeles subochracea

Micromeles subochracea, commonly known as the pale yellow bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems that bear clusters of small, yellow-orange bell-shaped flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its delicate flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
