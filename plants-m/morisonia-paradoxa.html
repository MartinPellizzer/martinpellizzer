Morisonia paradoxa

Morisonia paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox orchid, is a rare and enigmatic flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble orchids, though it is not closely related to the true orchid family. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swamps, where it thrives in the understory. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring.
