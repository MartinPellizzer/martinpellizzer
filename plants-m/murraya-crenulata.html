Murraya crenulata

Murraya crenulata, commonly known as the fragrant murraya, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces orange-red berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

Murraya crenulata is also valued for its ability to tolerate partial shade and its use in landscaping and horticulture.
