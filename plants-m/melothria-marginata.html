Melothria marginata

Melothria marginata, commonly known as the creeping cucumber, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and squash. The plant produces small, green, edible fruits that are often used in traditional cuisines. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and trailing.

The flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female blooms, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental and culinary value.
