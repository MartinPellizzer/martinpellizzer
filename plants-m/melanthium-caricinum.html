Melanthium caricinum

Melanthium caricinum, commonly known as the swamp milkweed, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in loose panicles and attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Melanthium caricinum plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
