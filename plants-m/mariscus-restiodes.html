Mariscus restiodes

Mariscus restiodes, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It typically thrives in rocky, arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. Its succulent stems store water, allowing it to survive in drought-prone areas.

Mariscus restiodes is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal.
