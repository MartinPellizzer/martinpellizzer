Myrcia rotundata

Myrcia rotundata, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinct aromatic scent, which contributes to its use in perfumery and herbal remedies.

Myrcia rotundata thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.
