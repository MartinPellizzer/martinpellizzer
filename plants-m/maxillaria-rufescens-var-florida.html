Maxillaria rufescens var. florida

Maxillaria rufescens var. florida, commonly known as the Florida lady's slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb that supports its leaves and flower spikes. The plant produces showy, lip-like petals that are typically pink to reddish in color, with a distinctive yellow callus. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both horticultural and conservation purposes.
