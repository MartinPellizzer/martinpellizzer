Myristica leucoxyla

Myristica leucoxyla, commonly known as the white nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic bark and distinctive red, fleshy fruits. The tree produces seeds enclosed in a hard, woody capsule, which contains the aromatic spice known as mace. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant is valued for its economic importance in the spice trade and its ecological role in tropical ecosystems.
