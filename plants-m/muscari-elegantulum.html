Muscari elegantulum

Muscari elegantulum, commonly known as the Persian grape hyacinth, is a bulbous plant native to the eastern Mediterranean and parts of Asia.

It produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet, resembling small grapes. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–20 cm and thrives in well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.

Muscari elegantulum is relatively drought-tolerant and can be grown in USDA hardiness zones 5–8.
