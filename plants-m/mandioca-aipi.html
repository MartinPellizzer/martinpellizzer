Mandioca aipi

Mandioca aipi, commonly known as yuca, is a tropical root vegetable native to South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its starchy, tuberous roots that are typically white or yellow in color. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, green flowers with yellow stamens. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Mandioca aipi is a versatile crop used in various cuisines and is also valued for its nutritional content.
