Monstera dissecta

Monstera dissecta, commonly known as the Swiss cheese plant, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that develop distinctive holes and splits as they mature. The plant has a vigorous growth habit and can reach lengths of up to 10 meters when supported. Its leaves are typically a deep green with a waxy texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by black berries.

Monstera dissecta is popular as a houseplant due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
