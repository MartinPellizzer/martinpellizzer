Marianthus parviflorus

Marianthus parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered marianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
