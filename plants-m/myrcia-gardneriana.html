Myrcia gardneriana

Myrcia gardneriana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are edible for some bird species but toxic to humans. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a variety of habitats including forests and coastal areas.

Myrcia gardneriana is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other parts of the world, sometimes becoming an invasive species.
