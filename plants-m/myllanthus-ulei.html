Myllanthus ulei

Myllanthus ulei, commonly known as the Brazilian burr, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiny fruit capsules that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically alternate and ovate, with a rough texture.

Myllanthus ulei is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
