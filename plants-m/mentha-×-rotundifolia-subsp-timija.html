Mentha × rotundifolia subsp. timija

Mentha × rotundifolia subsp.

timija, commonly known as timur mint, is a hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and rounded, slightly hairy leaves.

The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves used in culinary and medicinal applications.

This subspecies is valued for its hardy nature and adaptability to various soil conditions.
