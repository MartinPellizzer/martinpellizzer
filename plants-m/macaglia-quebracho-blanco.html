Macaglia quebracho-blanco

Macaglia quebracho-blanco, commonly known as white quebracho, is a deciduous tree native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fissured bark and compound leaves. The tree produces small, pale yellow flowers followed by legumes containing edible seeds. It is often found in dry, arid regions and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
