Mentha sylvestris subsp. polyadena

Mentha sylvestris subsp.

polyadena, commonly known as the purple mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, pale purple to blue flowers arranged in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive minty fragrance.
