Macielia

Macielia, commonly known as the "Mexican orchid," is a genus of flowering plants native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its showy, often fragrant flowers. The plants typically have pseudobulbs and are epiphytic or lithophytic, growing on trees or rocks. Macielia species are valued for their ornamental beauty and are often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.

Their flowers come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple, and they bloom during the spring and summer months.
