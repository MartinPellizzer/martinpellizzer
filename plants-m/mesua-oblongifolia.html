Mesua oblongifolia

Mesua oblongifolia, commonly known as the Indian rose or velvet tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces woody, oblong fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in medicine and perfumery.

The tree thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
