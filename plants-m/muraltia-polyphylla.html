Muraltia polyphylla

Muraltia polyphylla, commonly known as the red muraltia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when mature, which are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Muraltia polyphylla thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and as a hedge plant.
