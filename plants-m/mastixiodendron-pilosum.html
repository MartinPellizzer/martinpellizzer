Mastixiodendron pilosum

Mastixiodendron pilosum, commonly known as the silky mastix tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and it bears small, round, woody fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in lowland areas with well-drained soil.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
