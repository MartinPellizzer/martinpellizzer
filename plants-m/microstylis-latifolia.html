Microstylis latifolia

Microstylis latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed microstylis, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae, or grass family.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant is native to certain regions of Africa and is often found in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands. It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.

Due to its delicate appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
