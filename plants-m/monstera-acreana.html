Monstera acreana

Monstera acreana, commonly known as the Brazilian giant split-leaf philodendron, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, deeply lobed leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant has a climbing habit, using aerial roots to attach to trees and other structures. Its leaves are typically a deep green with a rough, waxy texture.

Monstera acreana is prized for its striking foliage and is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
