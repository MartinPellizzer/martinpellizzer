Myristica faroensis

Myristica faroensis, commonly known as the Faroe Islands nutmeg, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Faroe Islands.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, reddish-brown fruits. The plant produces a unique seed with a rich, aromatic kernel that resembles nutmeg in appearance and scent. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to the cool, maritime climate of its native region.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
