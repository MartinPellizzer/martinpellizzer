Myrobalanus eichleriana

Myrobalanus eichleriana, commonly known as the Eichler's tamarind, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are glossy and dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a distinctive aromatic scent.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit, which can be used in traditional medicine.
