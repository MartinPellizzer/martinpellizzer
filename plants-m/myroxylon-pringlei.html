Myroxylon pringlei

Myroxylon pringlei, commonly known as the Copaiba tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and produces a milky, latex-like sap that is highly valued for its aromatic properties. The plant belongs to the family Fabaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates. Its bark and resin have been traditionally used in medicine and as a source of natural perfume.
