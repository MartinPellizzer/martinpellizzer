Monotaxis megacarpa

Monotaxis megacarpa, commonly known as the giant fruit tree, is a unique species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, and often brightly colored fruits that can reach several centimeters in diameter. The plant has a distinctive morphology with thick, fibrous stems and broad, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in terminal inflorescences.

The botanical significance of Monotaxis megacarpa lies in its ecological role and potential for use in horticulture and traditional medicine.
