Maireana stipitata

Maireana stipitata, commonly known as the ghost gum, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, pale grey branches and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant in dry conditions.

Maireana stipitata is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental appearance and drought tolerance.
