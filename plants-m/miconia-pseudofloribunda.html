Miconia pseudofloribunda

Miconia pseudofloribunda, commonly known as the false glorybower, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces showy, cream-colored flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often with a reddish or purple calyx. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
