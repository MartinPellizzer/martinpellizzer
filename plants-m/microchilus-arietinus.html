Microchilus arietinus

Microchilus arietinus, commonly known as the red-stemmed orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically pink or red, with a distinctive three-lobed lip that attracts pollinators.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
