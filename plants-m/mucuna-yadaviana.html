Mucuna yadaviana

Mucuna yadaviana, commonly known as the Indian velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, hairy pods that contain spherical seeds. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as fallow fields and forest edges.

Its ornamental value and use in traditional medicine contribute to its ecological and cultural significance.
