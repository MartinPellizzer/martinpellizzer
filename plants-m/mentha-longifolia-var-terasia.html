Mentha longifolia var. terasia

Mentha longifolia var. terasia, commonly known as the Terasea mint, is a variety of the spearmint plant native to southern Europe and the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy and emit a strong, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its essential oils, rich in menthol and other compounds, make it valuable for both culinary and medicinal uses.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental appeal and aromatic properties in gardens and herbal applications.
