Monochaetum strigosum

Monochaetum strigosum, commonly known as the hairy leek orchid, is an endemic species native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, strap-like leaves. The plant produces a tall flowering stem that bears small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or inland areas.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
