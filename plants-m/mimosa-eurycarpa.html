Mimosa eurycarpa

Mimosa eurycarpa, commonly known as the broad-fruit mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds, and it is often found in disturbed soils and dry habitats.

Mimosa eurycarpa is also known for its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen, making it a useful plant in certain ecological contexts.
