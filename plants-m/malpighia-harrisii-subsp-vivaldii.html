Malpighia harrisii subsp. vivaldii

Malpighia harrisii subsp.

vivaldii, commonly known as the Vivaldi's passionfruit, is a member of the Malpighiaceae family. It is a small, shrub-like plant native to Central America, particularly found in countries like Guatemala and Belize.

The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to passionfruit but have a distinct flavor. Its flowers are typically yellow with red or purple markings, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential as a fruit crop.
