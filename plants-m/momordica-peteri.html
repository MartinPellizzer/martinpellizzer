Momordica peteri

Momordica peteri, commonly known as the African bitter gourd, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourds and cucurbits. The plant produces elongated, spiny fruits that are typically green when mature, though they can turn yellow or orange. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female blooms.

The plant is valued for its edible fruits, which have a bitter taste and are used in traditional cuisines.
