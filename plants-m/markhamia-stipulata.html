Markhamia stipulata

Markhamia stipulata, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are usually pink or red, with a prominent central stigma. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have stipules at the base.

Markhamia stipulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
