Maxillaria monoceros

Maxillaria monoceros, commonly known as the horned orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, horn-like sepals and petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, with a unique, spurred lip that aids in pollination.

Maxillaria monoceros is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral structure.
