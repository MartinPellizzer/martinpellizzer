Myriophyllum propinquum

Myriophyllum propinquum, commonly known as narrowleaf watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has feather-like leaves that are typically narrow and arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by dark, elongated fruits. It thrives in freshwater lakes and ponds with moderate to slow water flow.

Myriophyllum propinquum plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
