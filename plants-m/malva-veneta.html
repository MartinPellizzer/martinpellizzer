Malva veneta

Malva veneta, commonly known as the Italian mallows, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture and produces showy, pink or purple flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like hibiscus and cotton. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
