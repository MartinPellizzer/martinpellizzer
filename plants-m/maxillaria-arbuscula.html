Maxillaria arbuscula

Maxillaria arbuscula, commonly known as the small-lobed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Central and South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that produce multiple leaves and flower spikes. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with intricate patterns. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Maxillaria arbuscula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
