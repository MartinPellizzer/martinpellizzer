Mikania purpurascens

Mikania purpurascens, commonly known as purpleleaf morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves that often display a striking purple or reddish hue. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming in clusters. Its rapid growth habit and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types make it both a popular ornamental plant and an invasive species in some areas.

The seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
