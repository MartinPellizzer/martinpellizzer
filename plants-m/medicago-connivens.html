Medicago connivens

Medicago connivens, commonly known as the Persian clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is native to regions of Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in dry, sunny environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions, and it plays a role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil.

Its seeds are often used as forage for livestock, making it an important plant in agricultural settings.
