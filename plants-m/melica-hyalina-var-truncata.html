Melica hyalina var. truncata

Melica hyalina var. truncata, commonly known as the narrowleaf melica, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, giving them a translucent appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to white flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Poaceae family, often found in temperate regions.
