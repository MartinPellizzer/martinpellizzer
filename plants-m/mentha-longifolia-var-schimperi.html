Mentha longifolia var. schimperi

Mentha longifolia var. schimperi, commonly known as Ethiopian spearmint, is a perennial herb native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a strong, minty aroma. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are widely used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and cooling properties.

This variety is valued both for its culinary uses and its ecological role in its native habitat.
