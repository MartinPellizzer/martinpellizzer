Mauria obtusa

Mauria obtusa, commonly known as the broadleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, and the flowers are followed by fluffy seed heads that disperse easily.

Mauria obtusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
