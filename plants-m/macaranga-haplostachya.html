Macaranga haplostachya

Macaranga haplostachya, commonly known as the white-veined macaranga, is a fast-growing shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate with prominent venation patterns. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits that release tiny seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed habitats and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
