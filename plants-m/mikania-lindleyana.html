Mikania lindleyana

Mikania lindleyana, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a vigorous vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread across the ground, often forming dense mats. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and gardens.

Due to its invasive nature, it can outcompete native vegetation in certain regions.
