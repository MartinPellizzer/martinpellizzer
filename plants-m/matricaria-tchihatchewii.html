Matricaria tchihatchewii

Matricaria tchihatchewii, commonly known as the Armenian chamomile, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features slender, hairy stems and small, yellow disc flowers surrounded by white to pale yellow ray florets. The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic essential oil from its flowers, which is used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Matricaria tchihatchewii is valued for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and calming effects.
