Myristica crassifolia

Myristica crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leafed nutmeg, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and fragrant, reddish-brown fruits. The plant produces a unique seed with an aromatic, resinous kernel that is used in various culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, fertile soil.

Myristica crassifolia is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitats.
