Malva meluca

Malva meluca, commonly known as the lesser mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, heart-shaped leaves and showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It has a sprawling growth habit and can reach heights of up to 60 cm.

The leaves and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.
