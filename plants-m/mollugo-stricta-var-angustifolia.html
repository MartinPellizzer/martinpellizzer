Mollugo stricta var. angustifolia

Mollugo stricta var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf mossy spurge, is a low-growing annual plant characterized by its small, succulent leaves and yellow flowers.

It typically thrives in disturbed soils and open areas, showcasing a compact growth habit. The plant produces capsules that release tiny seeds, aiding in its spread. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

This variety is adapted to a range of habitats, including roadsides and sandy soils, and is often found in the southeastern United States.
