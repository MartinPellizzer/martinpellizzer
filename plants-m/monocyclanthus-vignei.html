Monocyclanthus vignei

Monocyclanthus vignei, commonly known as the African cycad, is a unique plant belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, feathery leaves. The plant produces cones instead of flowers, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants. It is native to southern Africa and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its slow growth and ecological significance, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native habitat.
