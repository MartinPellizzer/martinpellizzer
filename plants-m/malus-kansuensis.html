Malus kansuensis

Malus kansuensis, commonly known as the Chinese apple or Kansu crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It produces small, ornamental flowers in spring, followed by edible fruits that are often used in traditional Chinese cuisine. The plant is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of small, red or yellow fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental beauty and medicinal properties.

Malus kansuensis is also a popular species for horticultural cultivation due to its hardiness and adaptability.
