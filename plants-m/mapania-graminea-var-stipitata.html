Mapania graminea var. stipitata

Mapania graminea var. stipitata, commonly known as the slender bamboo grass, is a variant of the bamboo grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine, hairy structures. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that becomes more pronounced in the shade. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
