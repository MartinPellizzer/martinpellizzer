Myrica heterophylla

Myrica heterophylla, commonly known as the variable myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myricaceae.

It is native to western North America, particularly found in moist, acidic soils of forested areas. The plant is characterized by its alternate, often variable leaf shapes, which can range from oval to needle-like. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color.

Myrica heterophylla is also known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
