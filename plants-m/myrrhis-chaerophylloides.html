Myrrhis chaerophylloides

Myrrhis chaerophylloides, commonly known as the purple false oatgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. Its flowering stems bear small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish or pale yellow.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
