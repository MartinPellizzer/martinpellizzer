Masdevallia clandestina

Masdevallia clandestina, commonly known as the hidden masdevallia, is a rare and delicate orchid native to the high-altitude cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant typically grows as a miniature epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in its natural habitat. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that help it conserve moisture in its humid, shaded environment.

Due to its rarity and specific ecological requirements, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
