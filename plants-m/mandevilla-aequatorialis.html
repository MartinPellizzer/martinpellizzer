Mandevilla aequatorialis

Mandevilla aequatorialis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a vigorous climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Mandevilla aequatorialis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
