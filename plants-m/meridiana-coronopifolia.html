Meridiana coronopifolia

Meridiana coronopifolia, commonly known as the crown of thorns, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems and small, waxy leaves that help it retain water in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It is well adapted to full sun and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a striking addition to rock gardens and arid landscapes.
