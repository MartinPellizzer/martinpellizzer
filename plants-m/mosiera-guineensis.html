Mosiera guineensis

Mosiera guineensis, commonly known as the African senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is often found in moist forest understories and along riverbanks. It produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

Mosiera guineensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
