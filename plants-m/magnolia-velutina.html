Magnolia velutina

Magnolia velutina, commonly known as the velvet magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The leaves are broad, ovate, and have a velvety texture, giving the plant its common name. The bark is smooth and light gray, becoming fissured with age.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.
