Malope stipulacea var. tripartita

Malope stipulacea var. tripartita, commonly known as the three-parted malope, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, branched stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have three distinct lobes, giving the plant its common name. Small, yellow flowers appear in clusters during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
