Melinis argentea

Melinis argentea, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from the fine, silky hairs covering its leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along streambanks.

Melinis argentea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
