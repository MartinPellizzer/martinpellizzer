Mentha × rotundifolia var. reverchonii

Mentha × rotundifolia var. reverchonii, commonly known as the round-leaf peppermint, is a hybrid mint species characterized by its rounded, slightly toothed leaves.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or stream banks. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its aromatic leaves contain high concentrations of menthol, making it valuable for culinary and medicinal uses.

This variety is often cultivated for its distinctive fragrance and potential therapeutic properties.
