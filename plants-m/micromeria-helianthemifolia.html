Micromeria helianthemifolia

Micromeria helianthemifolia, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, glandular leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Micromeria helianthemifolia is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
