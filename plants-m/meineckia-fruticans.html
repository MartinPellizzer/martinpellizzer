Meineckia fruticans

Meineckia fruticans, commonly known as the red sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that typically reaches a height of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine, grayish-green foliage. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically pink to reddish in color, appearing in clusters along the stems.

Meineckia fruticans thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
