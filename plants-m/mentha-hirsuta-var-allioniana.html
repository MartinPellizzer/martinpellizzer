Mentha hirsuta var. allioniana

Mentha hirsuta var. allioniana, commonly known as the woolly mint or allion's mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features hairy, often grayish-green leaves and produces small, pale blue or purple flowers. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild mint-like fragrance.
