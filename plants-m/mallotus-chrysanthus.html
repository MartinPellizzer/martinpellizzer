Mallotus chrysanthus

Mallotus chrysanthus, commonly known as the golden mallot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces edible berries that are yellow when ripe.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a hedge plant.
