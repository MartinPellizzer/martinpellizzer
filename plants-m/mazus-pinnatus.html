Mazus pinnatus

Mazus pinnatus, commonly known as the slender mazus, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features prostrate stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats of foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of pink or white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Mazus pinnatus thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
