Maerua rigida var. buxifolia

Maerua rigida var. buxifolia, commonly known as the Cape fig or African snowball, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and often clustered at the ends of the branches. The plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. Its fruit is a small, round capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
