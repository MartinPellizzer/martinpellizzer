Miconia ancistrophora

Miconia ancistrophora, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Miconia* tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that are often heart-shaped or ovate. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and showy flowers.

However, it can be invasive in some regions, posing a threat to native ecosystems.
