Melocactus cordatus

Melocactus cordatus, commonly known as the cordate melon or the heart-shaped cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, ribbed stem that is often swollen and rounded, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically short-lived but striking. Its roots are shallow and adapted to absorb moisture from the dry soil.

Melocactus cordatus is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil.
