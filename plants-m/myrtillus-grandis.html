Myrtillus grandis

Myrtillus grandis, commonly known as the grand myrtle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Myrtaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant is native to temperate regions of the southern hemisphere, particularly in parts of Australia and South America. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its essential oils, extracted from its leaves and flowers, are valued for their medicinal and aromatic properties.
