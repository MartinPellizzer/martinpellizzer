Monstera irritans

Monstera irritans, commonly known as the giant hoy, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant features a climbing habit, with aerial roots that help it attach to trees or structures in its natural habitat. Its leaves are typically a deep green with a waxy texture, and they often have distinctive notches and holes.

Monstera irritans thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
