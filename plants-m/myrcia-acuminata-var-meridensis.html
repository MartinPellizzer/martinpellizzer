Myrcia acuminata var. meridensis

Myrcia acuminata var. meridensis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, with a fragrant aroma when crushed. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and are arranged in clusters, while the fruit is a small, woody capsule containing several seeds.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
