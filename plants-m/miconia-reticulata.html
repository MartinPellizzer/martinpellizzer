Miconia reticulata

Miconia reticulata, commonly known as the laceleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, deeply lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange to red berries. Miconia reticulata is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain regions.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant, though caution is advised due to its potential ecological impact.
