Marrubium cuneatum

Marrubium cuneatum, commonly known as narrowleaf horehound, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, narrow leaves and small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Marrubium cuneatum is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
