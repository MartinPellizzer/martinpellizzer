Mesembryanthemum bulbosum

Mesembryanthemum bulbosum, commonly known as the woolly bush, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that help it store water, adapting it to arid environments. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a valuable addition to succulent collections.
