Myrcia tricona

Myrcia tricona, commonly known as the three-lobed myrtle, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are typically small, woody capsules that contain several seeds.

Myrcia tricona is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.
