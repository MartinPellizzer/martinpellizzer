Mimulus nasutus var. eximius

Mimulus nasutus var. eximius, commonly known as the showy yellow monkeyflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in stream banks and moist meadows.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, making it an important species for supporting local pollinator populations.
