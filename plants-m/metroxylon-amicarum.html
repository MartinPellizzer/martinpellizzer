Metroxylon amicarum

Metroxylon amicarum, commonly known as the areca palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and for the production of palm sugar. Its fruit, known as the areca nut, is used in traditional medicine and as a stimulant.

The areca palm thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
