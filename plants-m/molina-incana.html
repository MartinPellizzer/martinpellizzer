Molina incana

Molina incana, commonly known as the hairy molina, is a herbaceous plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Molina incana is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
