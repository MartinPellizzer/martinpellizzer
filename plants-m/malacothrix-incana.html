Malacothrix incana

Malacothrix incana, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Malacothrix incana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
