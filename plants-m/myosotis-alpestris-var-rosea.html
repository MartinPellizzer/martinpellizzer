Myosotis alpestris var. rosea

Myosotis alpestris var. rosea, commonly known as the alpine forget-me-not, is a flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to pink, with the rosea variant exhibiting a distinctive pink hue. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and the plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
