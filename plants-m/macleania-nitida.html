Macleania nitida

Macleania nitida, commonly known as the silverleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a trailing growth habit and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance.

Macleania nitida is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small wildlife.
