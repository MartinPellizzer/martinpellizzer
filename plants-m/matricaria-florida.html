Matricaria florida

Matricaria florida, commonly known as the common chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flower heads. The plant produces small, white ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers. It is often cultivated for its aromatic properties and is used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.

Matricaria florida thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and medicinal herb cultivation.
