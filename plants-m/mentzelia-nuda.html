Mentzelia nuda

Mentzelia nuda, commonly known as the naked mentzelia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, often hairless stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Mentzelia nuda is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a characteristic species of desert ecosystems.
