Myrcia subverticillaris

Myrcia subverticillaris, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and has a dense, evergreen foliage. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.

The leaves contain essential oils and are valued for their fragrance and potential medicinal properties.
