Melandrium argentinum

Melandrium argentinum, commonly known as the Argentine catchfly, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by capsules containing numerous seeds. It has hairy stems and leaves, and its root system is fibrous, allowing it to survive in nutrient-poor environments.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in regions outside its native range.
