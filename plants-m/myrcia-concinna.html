Myrcia concinna

Myrcia concinna, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

Myrcia concinna is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
