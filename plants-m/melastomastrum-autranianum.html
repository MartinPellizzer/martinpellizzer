Melastomastrum autranianum

Melastomastrum autranianum, commonly known as the Australian melastome, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces fruits that are capsules containing numerous seeds. It is often found in coastal heathlands and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
