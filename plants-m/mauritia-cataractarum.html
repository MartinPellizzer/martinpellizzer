Mauritia cataractarum

Mauritia cataractarum, commonly known as the Amazon river palm, is a tall, slender palm native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its long, pinnate leaves and a slender, unbranched trunk that can grow up to 20 meters in height. The plant thrives in wet, tropical environments and is often found near rivers and flooded areas. Its fibrous leaves are used in traditional crafts, and its trunk is valued for its durability.

The species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks and providing habitat for various wildlife.
