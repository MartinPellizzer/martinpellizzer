Mimosa lignosa

Mimosa lignosa, commonly known as the woody mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves that are typically bipinnate. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Mimosa lignosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
