Maytenus jamaicensis f. longifolia

Maytenus jamaicensis f. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf mayten, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often clustered at the ends of branches. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas or near water sources.

The species is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
