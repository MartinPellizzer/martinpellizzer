Mespilus canadensis var. oligocarpa

Mespilus canadensis var. oligocarpa, commonly known as the Canadian white serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically opposite and have 5 to 9 leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in late summer. Its botanical features include a woody stem with smooth, gray bark and a preference for well-drained soils.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
