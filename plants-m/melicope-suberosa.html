Melicope suberosa

Melicope suberosa, commonly known as the rough-stemmed melicope, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its rough, bark-like stems and opposite, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by round, yellowish fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Melicope suberosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some Pacific cultures.
