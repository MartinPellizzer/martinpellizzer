Malus paradisiaca

Malus paradisiaca, commonly known as the paradise apple tree, is a species of apple tree native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy white flowers and vibrant red or yellow fruit. The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its fruit, while edible, is smaller and less commonly cultivated compared to other apple varieties.

The tree is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
