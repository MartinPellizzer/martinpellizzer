Myrcia paulii-jonesii

Myrcia paulii-jonesii, commonly known as the Brazilian peppercorn, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, red, peppercorn-like fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy appearance.

The flowers are small, white, and clustered in inflorescences, contributing to its ornamental value.
