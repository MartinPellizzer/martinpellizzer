Messerschmidia sericea

Messerschmidia sericea, commonly known as the silky sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features small, fleshy leaves covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. It typically grows on rocky soils or gravelly plains, demonstrating a high degree of drought tolerance.

Its unique morphology and adaptation to extreme conditions make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
