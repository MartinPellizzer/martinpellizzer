Millettia delevoyi

Millettia delevoyi, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The tree produces long, slender pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and reforestation projects.
