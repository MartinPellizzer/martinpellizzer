Miconia glabra

Miconia glabra, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, three-lobed fruits. Miconia glabra is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it an important subject in ecological and horticultural studies.
