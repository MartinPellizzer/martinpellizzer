Myrcia suberosa

Myrcia suberosa, commonly known as the rough myrtle, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Myrcia suberosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
