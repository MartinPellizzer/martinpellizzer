Myristica koordersii

Myristica koordersii, commonly known as the Malayan nutmeg, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody fruits that contain the valuable spice compounds. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has compound leaves with elongated leaflets. Its seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, red aril, which is often used in traditional medicine.

The tree is valued for its essential oils and has economic significance in the spice trade.
