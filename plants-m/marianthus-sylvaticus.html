Marianthus sylvaticus

Marianthus sylvaticus, commonly known as the wild marianthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows in dense, humid forests and thrives in shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and glossy, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Marianthus sylvaticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
