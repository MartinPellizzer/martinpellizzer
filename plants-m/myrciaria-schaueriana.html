Myrciaria schaueriana

Myrciaria schaueriana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, edible red fruits that resemble cherries. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a distinct aromatic scent.

The flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are clustered in inflorescences, contributing to its ornamental value.
