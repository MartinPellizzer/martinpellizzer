Menodora helianthemoides var. humilis

Menodora helianthemoides var. humilis, commonly known as the creeping sunflower, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellow flowers that resemble those of sunflowers, typically blooming in spring. The plant has hairy, fleshy leaves that are often clustered at the base, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
