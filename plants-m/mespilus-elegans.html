Mespilus elegans

Mespilus elegans, commonly known as the European medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its oval, dark green leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is a unique, fleshy, yellowish drupe that ripens in autumn and is known for its aromatic, sweet, and slightly tart flavor. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens.
