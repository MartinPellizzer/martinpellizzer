Maesa banksiana

Maesa banksiana, commonly known as the Florida maesa, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically thrives in wetland environments, such as swamps and marshes. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and often have a waxy coating that helps retain moisture.

Maesa banksiana is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its ability to stabilize wetland soils.
