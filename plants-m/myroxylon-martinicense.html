Myroxylon martinicense

Myroxylon martinicense, commonly known as the Copaiba tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree is notable for its aromatic resin, which is harvested for use in perfumes, incense, and traditional medicine. Its bark and sap contain compounds with potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
