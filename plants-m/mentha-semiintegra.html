Mentha semiintegra

Mentha semiintegra, commonly known as the narrow-leaf peppermint, is a hybrid mint species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and strong, aromatic scent. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, pale purple flowers in late summer, which are clustered in whorls along the stem.

Mentha semiintegra is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other mint species.
