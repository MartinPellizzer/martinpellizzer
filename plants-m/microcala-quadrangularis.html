Microcala quadrangularis

Microcala quadrangularis, commonly known as the quadrangular microcala, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive quadrangular, or four-sided, stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces tiny, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It prefers well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated by gardeners interested in exotic plants.
