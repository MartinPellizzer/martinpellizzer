Mesembryanthemum flaccidum

Mesembryanthemum flaccidum, commonly known as the flaccid iceplant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance.

Mesembryanthemum flaccidum is also valued for its ability to prevent soil erosion in sandy or rocky soils.
