Macaranga bartlettii

Macaranga bartlettii, commonly known as the Bartlett's makara, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed habitats. Its latex, like that of other Macaranga species, is milky and can be used in traditional practices.

The tree plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various animals.
