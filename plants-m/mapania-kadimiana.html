Mapania kadimiana

Mapania kadimiana, commonly known as the Kadimian orchid, is a rare and elusive species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or in rocky crevices. Its leaves are broad and glossy, adapted to capture limited sunlight in its natural habitat.

Due to its rarity and specific ecological requirements, Mapania kadimiana is considered a conservation concern.
