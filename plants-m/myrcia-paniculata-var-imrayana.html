Myrcia paniculata var. imrayana

Myrcia paniculata var. imrayana, commonly known as the Imrayana myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a strong, spicy fragrance.

The leaves are evergreen, oval-shaped, and have a rough texture with a distinct aromatic scent.
