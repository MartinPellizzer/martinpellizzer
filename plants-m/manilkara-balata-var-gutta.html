Manilkara balata var. gutta

Manilkara balata var. gutta, commonly known as gutta-percha, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and produces a latex-like substance known as gutta resin. The latex is harvested from the bark and has a unique rubbery texture when fresh. This resin is used in various industrial applications, including the production of electrical insulation and golf ball cores.

The plant also has a long history of use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural rubber alternatives.
