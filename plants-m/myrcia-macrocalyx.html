Myrcia macrocalyx

Myrcia macrocalyx, commonly known as the broad-leaved myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is part of the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Myrcia macrocalyx is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
