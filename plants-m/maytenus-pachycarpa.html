Maytenus pachycarpa

Maytenus pachycarpa, commonly known as the thick-fruited mayten, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Maytenus pachycarpa is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
