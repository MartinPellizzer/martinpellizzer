Misopates orontium

Misopates orontium, commonly known as the watercress, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are often submerged in water, giving it a unique aquatic appearance. Its small white flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated by insects.

The entire plant is edible, with a peppery, slightly bitter flavor that is commonly used in salads and soups.
