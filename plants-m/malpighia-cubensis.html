Malpighia cubensis

Malpighia cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban acerola, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its small, bright red berries that are rich in vitamin C. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is not only ornamental but also valued for its nutritional content and use in culinary and medicinal applications.
