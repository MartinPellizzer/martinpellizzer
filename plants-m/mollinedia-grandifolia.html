Mollinedia grandifolia

Mollinedia grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered mollinedia, is a striking plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests. The plant is characterized by its large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Mollinedia grandifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
