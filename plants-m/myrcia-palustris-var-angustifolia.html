Myrcia palustris var. angustifolia

Myrcia palustris var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf swamp myrtle, is a shrub native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that release tiny seeds when mature.

This species is often found in moist, acidic soils and is adapted to thrive in swampy or marshy environments.
