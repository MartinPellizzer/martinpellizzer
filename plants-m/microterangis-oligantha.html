Microterangis oligantha

Microterangis oligantha, commonly known as the slender-leafed microterangis, is a small, flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats. It is a perennial shrub that produces woody fruits containing small, dark seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
