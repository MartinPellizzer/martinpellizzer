Miltonioides carinifera

Miltonioides carinifera, commonly known as the wrinkled-leaf orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with a unique coloration pattern. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are elongated and have a distinctive wavy or wrinkled texture, which gives it its common name.

The flowers bloom in clusters and are often displayed in vibrant shades of pink and white, making it a popular choice for horticultural enthusiasts.
