Mapouria laevifolia

Mapouria laevifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaf mapouria, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its broad, smooth, and often waxy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by fruit that contains several seeds. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.

Mapouria laevifolia is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
