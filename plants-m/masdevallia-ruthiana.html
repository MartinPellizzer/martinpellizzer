Masdevallia ruthiana

Masdevallia ruthiana, commonly known as the Ruthiana orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, often colorful flowers that resemble miniature orchids, with a delicate structure and vivid hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often forming a rosette pattern around the base.

Masdevallia ruthiana is prized by horticulturists for its ornamental value and unique floral morphology.
