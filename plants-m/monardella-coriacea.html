Monardella coriacea

Monardella coriacea, commonly known as roughleaf monardella, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and chaparral. Its leaves are often glandular and have a strong, minty scent.

Monardella coriacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
