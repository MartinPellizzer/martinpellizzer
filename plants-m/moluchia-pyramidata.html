Moluchia pyramidata

Moluchia pyramidata, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, star-shaped fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in panicles. The fruit contains a single large seed surrounded by a fleshy, edible pulp.

It is often grown for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in its native habitat.
