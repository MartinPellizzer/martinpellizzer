Mastixia arborea subsp. macrophylla

Mastixia arborea subsp.

macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf mastix tree, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, fleshy fruits containing several seeds. Its bark is rich in essential oils and has been used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential economic uses in the pharmaceutical industry.
