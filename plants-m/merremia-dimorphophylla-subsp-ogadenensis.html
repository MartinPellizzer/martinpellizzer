Merremia dimorphophylla subsp. ogadenensis

Merremia dimorphophylla subsp.

ogadenensis, commonly known as the Somali devil's trumpet, is a climbing vine native to eastern Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
