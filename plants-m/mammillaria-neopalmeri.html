Mammillaria neopalmeri

Mammillaria neopalmeri, commonly known as the Desert Cactus or the Paloverde Cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green or blue-green in color. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Mammillaria neopalmeri is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
