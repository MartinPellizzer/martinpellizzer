Mentha arvensis f. garonnensis

Mentha arvensis f. garonnensis, commonly known as the Garonne mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or violet in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along riverbanks.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
