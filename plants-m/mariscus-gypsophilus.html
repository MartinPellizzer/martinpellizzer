Mariscus gypsophilus

Mariscus gypsophilus, commonly known as the gypsum saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to arid and rocky environments.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. It typically grows in well-drained soils, often found on rocky outcrops or in gypsum-rich areas.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
