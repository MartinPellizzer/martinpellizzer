Marsdenia suffruticosa

Marsdenia suffruticosa, commonly known as the creeping Marsdenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub-like species characterized by its woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the family Asclepiadaceae.
