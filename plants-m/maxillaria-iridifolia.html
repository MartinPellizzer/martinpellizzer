Maxillaria iridifolia

Maxillaria iridifolia, commonly known as the iris orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, iris-like flowers with a wide range of colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial plant, often found in shaded, humid environments. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, while its flowers are borne on elongated stems.

Maxillaria iridifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated for both its aesthetic appeal and its ability to thrive in diverse growing conditions.
