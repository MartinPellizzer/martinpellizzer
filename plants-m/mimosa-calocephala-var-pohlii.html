Mimosa calocephala var. pohlii

Mimosa calocephala var. pohlii, commonly known as the Pohlii sensitive plant, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in arid and semi-arid regions.

The species is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
