Magnolia zamorana

Magnolia zamorana, commonly known as the Zamora magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often with shallow fissures.

The Zamora magnolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blossoms.
