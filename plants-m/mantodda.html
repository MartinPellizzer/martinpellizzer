Mantodda

Mantodda, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow, citrus-like fruits. The plant is known for its high vitamin C content, which contributes to its popularity in traditional medicine and culinary uses. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, while the flowers are small and greenish-white.

The bark is smooth and greyish, with a distinctive aromatic scent.
