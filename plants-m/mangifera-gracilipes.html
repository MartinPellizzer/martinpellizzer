Mangifera gracilipes

Mangifera gracilipes, commonly known as the Java mango, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It produces small, elongated fruits with a thin, fibrous skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and grows in humid, tropical climates. It is valued for its edible fruit and is often cultivated for both commercial and ornamental purposes.

The tree's botanical features make it well adapted to the warm, moist environments of its native range.
