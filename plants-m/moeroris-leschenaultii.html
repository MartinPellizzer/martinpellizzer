Moeroris leschenaultii

Moeroris leschenaultii, commonly known as the Leschenault's lily, is a striking perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with lance-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale pink, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
