Muscari kerneri var. lacteiflorum

Muscari kerneri var. lacteiflorum, commonly known as the milk-flowered grape hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, giving it a delicate, floral appearance. The plant produces a distinctive, sweet, musky fragrance, which is characteristic of the muscari genus. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette that helps store moisture and nutrients.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
