Melochia tomentosa var. turpiniana

Melochia tomentosa var. turpiniana, commonly known as the woolly melochia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in dense, white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its growth habit is typically upright, with a sprawling form that can reach several meters in height.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
