Malus sieboldii var. koringo

Malus sieboldii var. koringo, commonly known as the Koringo apple, is a cultivar native to Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that produces small, ornamental fruits. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and showy white flowers in early spring. Its foliage turns vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall, making it a popular ornamental plant.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, thriving in temperate climates.
