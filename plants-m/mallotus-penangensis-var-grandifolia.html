Mallotus penangensis var. grandifolia

Mallotus penangensis var. grandifolia, commonly known as Penang holly, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. The plant produces elliptical, dark green leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
