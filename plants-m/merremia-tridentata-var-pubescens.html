Merremia tridentata var. pubescens

Merremia tridentata var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy devil's backbone, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, which is characteristic of its varietal name. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five lobes, followed by orange or red berries that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its botanical structure supports its role as both an ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in certain regions.
