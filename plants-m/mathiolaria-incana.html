Mathiolaria incana

Mathiolaria incana, commonly known as the white thornapple, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, white or pale blue flowers that are typically clustered in the axils of the leaves. Its fruits are fleshy berries that turn yellow when mature, containing numerous seeds.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
