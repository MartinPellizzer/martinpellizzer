Myrceugenia brevipes

Myrceugenia brevipes, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to the temperate rainforests of southern Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Myrceugenia brevipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
