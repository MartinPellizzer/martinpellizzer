Mansoa ventricosa

Mansoa ventricosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false poinsettia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple bracts that surround its small yellow flowers. The plant has palmate leaves with a waxy texture and a distinctive heart-shaped outline. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both garden displays and indoor plant collections.
