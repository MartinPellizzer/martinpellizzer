Malus domestica var. api

Malus domestica var. api, commonly known as the apple tree, is a deciduous tree belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall and its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces edible fruits known as apples, which come in a variety of colors, sizes, and flavors. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is widely cultivated for both its fruit and ornamental value.
