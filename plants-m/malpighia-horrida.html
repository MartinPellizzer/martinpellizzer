Malpighia horrida

Malpighia horrida, commonly known as the rough mallow, is a shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed by wildlife and humans in some regions. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in disturbed soils or along forest edges.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
