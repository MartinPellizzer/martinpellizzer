Mentha rugosa var. rotundifolia

Mentha rugosa var. rotundifolia, commonly known as round-leaf mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to regions of East Asia.

It features aromatic, slightly hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are often rounded at the base. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other mint species.
