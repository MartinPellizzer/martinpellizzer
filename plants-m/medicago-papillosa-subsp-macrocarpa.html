Medicago papillosa subsp. macrocarpa

Medicago papillosa subsp.

macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-seeded goat's rue, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with rounded lobes.

The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. Its pods are inflated and contain several elliptical seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
