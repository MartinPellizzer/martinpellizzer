Madhuca leucodermis

Madhuca leucodermis, commonly known as the white-berried candlewood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, white berries that are a food source for various animals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
