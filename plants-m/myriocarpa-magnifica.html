Myriocarpa magnifica

Myriocarpa magnifica, commonly known as the magnificent myriocarpa, is a striking flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has a thick, succulent stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. Its leaves are narrow and fleshy, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Myriocarpa magnifica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
