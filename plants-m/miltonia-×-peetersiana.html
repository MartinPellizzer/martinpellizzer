Miltonia × peetersiana

Miltonia × peetersiana, commonly known as the Peeters' orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically come in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in shaded, humid environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern around the pseudobulbs.

This orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
