Melampodium ruderale

Melampodium ruderale, commonly known as the common ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
