Mentha haplocalyx var. sachalinensis

Mentha haplocalyx var. sachalinensis, commonly known as the Siberian mint, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its essential oils, which contain menthol and other compounds, contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

It is often cultivated for its refreshing scent and therapeutic properties.
