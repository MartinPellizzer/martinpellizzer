Melicho amarylloides

Melicho amarylloides, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant is a vigorous climber, often found in moist, tropical environments. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture.
