Muehlenbeckia dumosa

Muehlenbeckia dumosa, commonly known as silverweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Muehlenbeckia dumosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
