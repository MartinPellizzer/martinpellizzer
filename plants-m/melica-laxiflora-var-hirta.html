Melica laxiflora var. hirta

Melica laxiflora var. hirta, commonly known as hairy looseflower grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered with fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, meadows, and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
