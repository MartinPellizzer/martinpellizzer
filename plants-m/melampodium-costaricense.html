Melampodium costaricense

Melampodium costaricense, commonly known as the Costa Rican sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical sunflower-like appearance. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow flowers with a distinctive red center. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Melampodium costaricense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
