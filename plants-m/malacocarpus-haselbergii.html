Malacocarpus haselbergii

Malacocarpus haselbergii, commonly known as the yellow banyan, is a tropical evergreen tree belonging to the Moraceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, pendulous flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces non-fig-like fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It is native to Southeast Asia and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
