Mesembryanthemum emarginatum

Mesembryanthemum emarginatum, commonly known as the margined ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, often blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or yellow, typically during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to store water in its leaves allows it to survive in arid environments with minimal irrigation.
