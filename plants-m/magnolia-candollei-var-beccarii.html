Magnolia candollei var. beccarii

Magnolia candollei var. beccarii, commonly known as the Beccari's magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically elliptical and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the blossoms. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a species of interest in botanical research and conservation efforts.
