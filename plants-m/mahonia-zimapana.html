Mahonia zimapana

Mahonia zimapana, commonly known as the Mexican mahonia, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Berberidaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and spiny, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, followed by blue-black berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Mahonia zimapana is also valued for its ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions, including drought.
