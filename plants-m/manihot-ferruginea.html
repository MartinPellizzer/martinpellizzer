Manihot ferruginea

Manihot ferruginea, commonly known as red root or red mombin, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its reddish-brown roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange or red fruits, which contain several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture.

Manihot ferruginea is adapted to grow in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in savannas and forest edges.
