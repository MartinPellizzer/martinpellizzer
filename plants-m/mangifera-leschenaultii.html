Mangifera leschenaultii

Mangifera leschenaultii, commonly known as the Indian mango or wild mango, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a fibrous, edible drupe.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some uses in traditional medicine.
