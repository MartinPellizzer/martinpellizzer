Matthiola tricuspidata var. integrifolia

Matthiola tricuspidata var. integrifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf stock, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, four-petaled flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The leaves are broad, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture. This species is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a versatile addition to many landscapes.
