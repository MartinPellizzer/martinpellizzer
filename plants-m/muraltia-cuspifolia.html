Muraltia cuspifolia

Muraltia cuspifolia, commonly known as the sweet acacia, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features compound leaves with small, sharp leaflets and produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is characterized by its woody, thorny stems and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats. Its fruit is a small, round pod containing several seeds, and it is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Muraltia cuspifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
