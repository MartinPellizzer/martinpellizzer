Morus stylosa var. latidentata

Morus stylosa var. latidentata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically narrower than those of other mulberry species. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as mulberries, which are edible and often consumed by both humans and animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
