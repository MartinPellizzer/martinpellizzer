Mentha horridula

Mentha horridula, commonly known as the rough horseradish, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems that are often covered in stiff hairs, giving it a rough texture. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a strong, pungent odor when crushed. It produces small, pale green to yellow flowers in terminal spikes during the summer months.

This plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its antiseptic and digestive benefits.
