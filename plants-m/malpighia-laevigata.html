Malpighia laevigata

Malpighia laevigata, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a shrub native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are rich in vitamin C and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Malpighia laevigata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract wildlife.
