Myrcia thyrsiflora

Myrcia thyrsiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. These flowers are typically followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Myrcia thyrsiflora is also known for its aromatic leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine.
