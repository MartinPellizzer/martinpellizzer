Malus domestica var. hupehensis

Malus domestica var. hupehensis, commonly known as the Chinese crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy flowers in spring and vibrant autumn foliage. The plant produces small, sour fruits that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
