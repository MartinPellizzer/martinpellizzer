Mesosphaerum althaeifolium

Mesosphaerum althaeifolium, commonly known as the woolly leaf mesosphaerum, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a bluish-gray hue.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, with yellowish-green petals, and the plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.
