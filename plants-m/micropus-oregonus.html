Micropus oregonus

Micropus oregonus, commonly known as the Oregon grape, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, holly-like leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces blue-black berries that are rich in antioxidants and are used in herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and rocky areas.

The Oregon grape is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
