Machaerium robsonianum

Machaerium robsonianum, commonly known as the Robson's machaerium, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in dense inflorescences, and are typically white or pale in color.

The species is notable for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
