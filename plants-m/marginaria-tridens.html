Marginaria tridens

Marginaria tridens, commonly known as the three-toothed germander, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly toothed margin.
