Myrtus nigripes

Myrtus nigripes, commonly known as black myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the island of Madeira, where it grows in coastal and forested areas. The plant is characterized by its small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested when fully ripe. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic properties.

The plant's ornamental value is also appreciated, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
