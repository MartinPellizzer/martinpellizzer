Machilus indicus

Machilus indicus, commonly known as Indian boxwood, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible berries that are used in traditional medicine. Machilus indicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.
