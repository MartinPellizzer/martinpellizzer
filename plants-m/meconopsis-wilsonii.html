Meconopsis wilsonii

Meconopsis wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's blue poppy, is a striking alpine plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its vivid blue or violet flowers, which bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are dark green and palmately lobed, often with a hairy texture.

Meconopsis wilsonii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with specific growing conditions.
