Malva paxtonii

Malva paxtonii, commonly known as the Texas mallows, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. The plant is characterized by its showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it often grows in rocky or sandy soils.

Malva paxtonii is adapted to arid environments and is often found in open woodlands and canyons.
