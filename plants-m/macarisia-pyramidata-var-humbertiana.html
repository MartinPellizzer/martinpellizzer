Macarisia pyramidata var. humbertiana

Macarisia pyramidata var. humbertiana, commonly known as the Humbert's pyramid bellflower, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features dense, upright clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range from white to pale blue, resembling small pyramids. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This ornamental plant is valued for its unique floral structure and adaptability to Mediterranean climates.
