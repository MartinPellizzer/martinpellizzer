Melilotus macrospermus

Melilotus macrospermus, commonly known as large-seeded sweet clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to regions of Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces pods containing numerous small, round seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

Its ecological importance includes nitrogen fixation and providing habitat for pollinators.
