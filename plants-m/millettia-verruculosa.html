Millettia verruculosa

Millettia verruculosa, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often clustered at the ends of its branches. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, woody pods containing edible seeds. Its bark is thick and often covered in a dense, velvety layer, giving it its common name.

The plant is valued for its ornamental features and has traditional uses in various cultures.
