Masdevallia dudleyi

Masdevallia dudleyi, commonly known as the Dudley's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, often colorful flowers that feature a unique combination of sepals and petals. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on forest floors or rocky substrates. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to the substrate while absorbing nutrients.

Masdevallia dudleyi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
