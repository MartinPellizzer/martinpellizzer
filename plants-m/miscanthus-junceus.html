Miscanthus junceus

Miscanthus junceus, commonly known as silver reed, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and fluffy, silver-gray plumes that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in moist to wet environments, often found along stream banks and in marshy areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color that becomes more silvery as the plant matures.

Miscanthus junceus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
