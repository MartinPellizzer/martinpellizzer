Mespilus purpurea

Mespilus purpurea, commonly known as the purple medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its oval, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit, which is a pome, ripens in autumn and has a unique, aromatic flavor. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
