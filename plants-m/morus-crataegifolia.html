Morus crataegifolia

Morus crataegifolia, commonly known as the European white mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, aggregate fruits. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. Its leaves are often used as feed for silkworms, and its roots can produce a milky sap that is toxic to some animals.

The tree plays an important role in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
