Myrtus ekmanii

Myrtus ekmanii, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant features small, dark green leaves with a fragrant, slightly oily texture. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Myrtus ekmanii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its use in traditional medicine.
