Melittis melissifolium

Melittis melissifolium, commonly known as honeyleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
