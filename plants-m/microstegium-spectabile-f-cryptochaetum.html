Microstegium spectabile f. cryptochaetum

Microstegium spectabile f. cryptochaetum, commonly known as the cryptochaetum form of the showy wood sorrel, is a variant of this herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage is often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

This form is notable for its ornamental value and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
