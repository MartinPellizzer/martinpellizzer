Marsdenia assimulata

Marsdenia assimulata, commonly known as the Chinese Marsdenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruits are follicles that release seeds with tufts of hair for wind dispersal.

Marsdenia assimulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
