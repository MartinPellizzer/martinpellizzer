Miscanthidium antsirabense

Miscanthidium antsirabense, commonly known as the Madagascar grass, is a species of grass native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
