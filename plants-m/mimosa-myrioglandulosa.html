Mimosa myrioglandulosa

Mimosa myrioglandulosa, commonly known as the golden mimosa, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, feathery foliage and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has glandular hairs on its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive, slightly sticky texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance and seasonal blooms.
