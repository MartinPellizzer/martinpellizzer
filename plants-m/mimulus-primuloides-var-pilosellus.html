Mimulus primuloides var. pilosellus

Mimulus primuloides var. pilosellus, commonly known as the hairy yellow monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow, spurred flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are oval-shaped and covered in fine, hairy texture, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
