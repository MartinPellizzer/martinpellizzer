Monarda didyma var. angustifolia

Monarda didyma var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf bee balm, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular red or pink flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae), which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
