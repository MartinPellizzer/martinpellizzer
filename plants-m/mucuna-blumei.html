Mucuna blumei

Mucuna blumei, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, velvety pods that contain edible seeds. The plant is notable for its rapid growth and ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, enriching the soil. Its flowers are typically pale yellow or greenish, and it produces a strong, aromatic scent.

Mucuna blumei is also valued for its medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal remedies.
