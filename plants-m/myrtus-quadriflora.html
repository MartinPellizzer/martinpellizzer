Myrtus quadriflora

Myrtus quadriflora, commonly known as common myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dark green, glossy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, berry-like fruits. Its aromatic leaves and flowers contain essential oils that contribute to its use in perfumery and herbal remedies.

Myrtus quadriflora thrives in well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance.
