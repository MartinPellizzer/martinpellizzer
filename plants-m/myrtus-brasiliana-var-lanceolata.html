Myrtus brasiliana var. lanceolata

Myrtus brasiliana var. lanceolata, commonly known as Brazilian myrtle or lanceolate myrtle, is a species native to Brazil.

It is a shrub characterized by its aromatic, narrow-elliptical leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, berry-like fruits. Its leaves contain essential oils with antimicrobial properties, making it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and tropical regions.
