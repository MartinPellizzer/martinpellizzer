Musa × paradisiaca var. thomsonii

Musa × paradisiaca var. thomsonii, commonly known as the Thomson's banana, is a cultivar of the banana plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elongated, and typically yellow or orange fruit, which is often used for consumption. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained, fertile soil.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes grown for its aesthetic appeal.
