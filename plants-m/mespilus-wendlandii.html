Mespilus wendlandii

Mespilus wendlandii, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, yellowish fruits resembling pears. The fruits are typically harvested in late summer and are known for their sweet, aromatic flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
