Myrsine coxii

Myrsine coxii, commonly known as the Chinese myrsine, is a flowering plant belonging to the Primulaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions such as China, India, and the Philippines. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy dark green surface with prominent veins.

The small, white flowers are clustered in umbels and bloom in the spring, followed by the production of small, spherical berries.
