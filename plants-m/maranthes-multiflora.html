Maranthes multiflora

Maranthes multiflora, commonly known as the common maranthes, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Maranthes multiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
