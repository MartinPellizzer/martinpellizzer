Masdevallia terborchii

Masdevallia terborchii, commonly known as the Terborch's masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, often colorful inflorescences and its unique, elongated floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually small but striking, with a wide range of hues including red, orange, and yellow.

Masdevallia terborchii is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and adaptability in cultivation.
