Muraltia dodii

Muraltia dodii, commonly known as the red muraltia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, showy flowers that range from white to pink. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are typically red when mature, containing several seeds.

Muraltia dodii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
