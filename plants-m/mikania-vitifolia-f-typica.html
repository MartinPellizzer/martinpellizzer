Mikania vitifolia f. typica

Mikania vitifolia f. typica, commonly known as the creeping Mikania, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit, with slender stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on leafy shoots. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a slightly hairy surface.

Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain environments.
