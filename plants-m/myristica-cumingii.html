Myristica cumingii

Myristica cumingii, commonly known as the Cuming's nutmeg, is a member of the Myristicaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree that produces fragrant, aromatic seeds, which are used in various culinary and medicinal applications. The plant's fruits are covered in a thick, spiny husk that protects the seed inside, which is often referred to as the "nutmeg" or "mace.". The leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a waxy texture, while the flowers are fragrant and typically yellow or greenish in color.

This species is valued for its unique aromatic compounds and is often cultivated for its economic and medicinal significance.
