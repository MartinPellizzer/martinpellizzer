Myrciaria mexicana

Myrciaria mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by orange to red, fleshy berries in the fall. These berries are edible, with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor, and are used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
