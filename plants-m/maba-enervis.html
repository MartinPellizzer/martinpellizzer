Maba enervis

Maba enervis, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its thick, milky sap, which is a distinguishing botanical feature. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The bark and leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine, reflecting its ecological and cultural significance.
