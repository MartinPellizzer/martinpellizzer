Malva cretica var. althaeoides

Malva cretica var. althaeoides, commonly known as the althaeoid musk mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial species that can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
