Myrtus pitanga f. fasciculata

Myrtus pitanga f. fasciculata, commonly known as Brazilian pepper tree or *Pimenta dos Bobos*, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark red berries that are edible. The plant has aromatic leaves with a spicy scent, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.
