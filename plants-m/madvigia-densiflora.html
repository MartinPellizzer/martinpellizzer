Madvigia densiflora

Madvigia densiflora, commonly known as the denseflower madvigia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
