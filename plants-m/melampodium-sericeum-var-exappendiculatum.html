Melampodium sericeum var. exappendiculatum

Melampodium sericeum var. exappendiculatum, commonly known as the silverleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy, yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands. It has hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is often used in ornamental gardening for its ornate foliage and vibrant floral display.
