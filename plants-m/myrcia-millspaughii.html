Myrcia millspaughii

Myrcia millspaughii, commonly known as the Millspaugh's myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and understoreys.

Myrcia millspaughii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
