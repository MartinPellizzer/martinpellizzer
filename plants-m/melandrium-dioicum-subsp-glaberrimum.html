Melandrium dioicum subsp. glaberrimum

Melandrium dioicum subsp.

glaberrimum, commonly known as the smooth lady's shoe, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Crambe genus within the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its cruciform flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves, which contrast with the more hairy forms of other subspecies. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges or meadows.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
