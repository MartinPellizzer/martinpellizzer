Malaxis uncata

Malaxis uncata, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, delicate appearance, with pale greenish-white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other plants without deriving nutrients from them. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air, soil, and rain.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Malaxis uncata is highly sought after in conservation efforts.
