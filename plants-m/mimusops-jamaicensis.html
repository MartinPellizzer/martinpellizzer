Mimusops jamaicensis

Mimusops jamaicensis, commonly known as the Jamaican satinwood, is a tropical tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually glossy green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers clustered in panicles, followed by orange-red berries that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
