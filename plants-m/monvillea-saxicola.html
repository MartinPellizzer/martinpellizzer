Monvillea saxicola

Monvillea saxicola, commonly known as the rock monvillea, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in the Andes region.

It is a perennial species that thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding a subtle beauty to its rugged habitat. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, helping it retain moisture in dry climates.

Monvillea saxicola is often grown in rock gardens or as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
