Malva crenata

Malva crenata, commonly known as the Sicilian mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with wavy edges and vibrant pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in temperate climates. Its fibrous stems and mucilaginous leaves have been used traditionally in herbal remedies.

Malva crenata is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
