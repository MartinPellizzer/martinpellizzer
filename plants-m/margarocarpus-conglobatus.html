Margarocarpus conglobatus

Margarocarpus conglobatus, commonly known as the globular acanthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Acanthaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spherical inflorescence that resembles a globe, composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and usually have a rough texture with prominent veins.

Margarocarpus conglobatus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
