Maxillaria oestlundiana

Maxillaria oestlundiana, commonly known as the Oestlund's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers with a distinctive violet or purple hue. The plant typically blooms during the dry season, showcasing its ornamental value in both cultivation and natural habitats. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette, contributing to its unique appearance.

Maxillaria oestlundiana is often grown as a houseplant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
