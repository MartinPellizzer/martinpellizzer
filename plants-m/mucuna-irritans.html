Mucuna irritans

Mucuna irritans, commonly known as the spreading cowpea, is a leguminous vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces elongated, flat pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Mucuna irritans is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

It also has a climbing habit, making it suitable for use as a cover crop or in ornamental gardening.
