Miconia crossosepala

Miconia crossosepala, commonly known as the broadleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, round, red berries. Miconia crossosepala is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in some regions due to its aggressive spread and ecological impact.
