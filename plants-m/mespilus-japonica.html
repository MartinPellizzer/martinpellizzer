Mespilus japonica

Mespilus japonica, commonly known as the Japanese medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically harvested in late summer or early autumn. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable addition to gardens in temperate climates.
