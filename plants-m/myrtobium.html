Myrtobium

Myrtobium, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and includes several species that are valued for their aromatic wood and ornamental foliage. The plants typically have small, lance-shaped leaves and produce clusters of white or pink flowers. Myrtobium species are often found in coastal and sandy habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to their unique appearance and ecological significance, they are of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
