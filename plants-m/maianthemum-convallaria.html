Maianthemum convallaria

Maianthemum convallaria, commonly known as the false lily of the valley, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Convallariaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, including North America, Europe, and Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

Maianthemum convallaria is often mistaken for true lily of the valley (Convallaria majalis) due to its similar appearance.
