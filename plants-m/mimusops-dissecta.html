Mimusops dissecta

Mimusops dissecta, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves that are often deeply lobed. The tree produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.

The plant is valued for its hard, durable wood and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
