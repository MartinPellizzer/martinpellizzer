Myrtus communis var. theodori

Myrtus communis var. theodori, commonly known as the Theodori myrtle, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, dark green leaves that are aromatic and often have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, edible berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its ornamental value and fragrance make it a popular choice in horticulture.
