Monstera deliciosa var. borsigiana

Monstera deliciosa var. borsigiana, commonly known as the Borsigiana monstera, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that develop fenestrations as they mature. The plant is a climbing vine that can grow up to 10 meters in height, requiring support such as a trellis or moss pole. Its leaves are typically a deep green with a waxy texture, and it produces small, inedible berries that turn from green to yellow as they ripen.

This variety is highly valued for its ornamental foliage and is popular in indoor gardening.
