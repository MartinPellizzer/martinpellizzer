Metroxylon viniferum

Metroxylon viniferum, commonly known as the wine palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, slender palm that can grow up to 20 meters in height, with a straight trunk and a crown of pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by fruits that are typically reddish or black in color. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts, and the trunk is valued for its fibrous material used in making ropes and textiles.

The plant thrives in tropical climates and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.
