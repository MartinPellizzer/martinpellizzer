Mimulus caespitosus var. implexus

Mimulus caespitosus var. implexus, commonly known as the tangled yellow monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features small, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes and other disturbed habitats.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its resilience in challenging environments.
