Melaleuca keigheryi

Melaleuca keigheryi, commonly known as the Keighery's tea tree, is a shrub or small tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, bottle-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous seeds, typical of the Myrtaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
