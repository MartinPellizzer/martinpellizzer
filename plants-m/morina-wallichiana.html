Morina wallichiana

Morina wallichiana, commonly known as the Himalayan moringa, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits that contain kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found at high altitudes.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional medicine practices.
