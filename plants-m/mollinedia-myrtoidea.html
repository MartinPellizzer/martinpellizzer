Mollinedia myrtoidea

Mollinedia myrtoidea, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Mollinedia myrtoidea is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
