Maranta longibracteata

Maranta longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family, which includes various ornamental and economically important species. The plant is characterized by its striking, often variegated leaves that can display shades of green, red, and silver. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically borne on a spike, followed by capsules that contain its seeds.

Maranta longibracteata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments.
