Myrica inodora

Myrica inodora, commonly known as the sweet fern, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fern-like foliage that emits a sweet scent when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, which are typical of the Myricaceae family. Its leaves are alternate and have a distinctive, feathery appearance.

Myrica inodora is often found in moist, acidic soils and is adapted to wetland environments.
