Masdevallia pallida

Masdevallia pallida, commonly known as the pale masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale green to white flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial or epiphytic orchid, often found in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its unique appearance.

Masdevallia pallida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its subtle beauty.
