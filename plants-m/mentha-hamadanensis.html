Mentha hamadanensis

Mentha hamadanensis, commonly known as Persian mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their essential oils. The plant features square stems, opposite leaves, and small, pale purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their cooling and digestive properties.

Mentha hamadanensis is also valued in culinary applications, particularly in Middle Eastern cuisine.
