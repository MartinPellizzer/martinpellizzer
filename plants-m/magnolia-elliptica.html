Magnolia elliptica

Magnolia elliptica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The flowers are typically white or pale pink and are borne in clusters at the base of the branches. The tree has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 30 feet.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
