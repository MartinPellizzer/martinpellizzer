Maesa insignis

Maesa insignis, commonly known as the silverleaf maesa, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, silver-gray foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often blooming during the rainy season. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a leathery texture and a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

Maesa insignis is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
