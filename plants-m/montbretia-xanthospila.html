Montbretia xanthospila

Montbretia xanthospila, commonly known as the yellow montbretia, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the genus Gladiolus and is characterized by its vibrant orange to yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features sword-shaped, grass-like leaves and produces tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Due to its showy appearance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
