Maxonia apiifolia var. dualis

Maxonia apiifolia var. dualis, commonly known as the dual-leaved cowplant, is a rare variant of the cowplant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive dual leaf morphology, where one side of the plant exhibits lobed leaves while the other side has smooth, entire leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often near streams or in forest understories. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a pale yellow to greenish hue, and it is pollinated primarily by insects.

Due to its limited distribution and unique botanical features, it is considered a subject of interest in botanical research and conservation efforts.
