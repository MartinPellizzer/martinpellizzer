Muscari skorpili

Muscari skorpili, commonly known as the Persian grape hyacinth, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, resembling grapes, hence its common name. The plant typically blooms in early spring, adding vibrant color to gardens and natural landscapes. Muscari skorpili prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy and adaptable choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value and resilience have made it a popular plant in horticulture.
