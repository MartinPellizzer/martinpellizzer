Melinis affinis

Melinis affinis, commonly known as the common millet grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny grains. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue.

Melinis affinis is an important plant in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in soil stabilization.
