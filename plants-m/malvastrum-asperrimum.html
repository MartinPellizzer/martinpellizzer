Malvastrum asperrimum

Malvastrum asperrimum, commonly known as the rough mallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features hairy, rough-textured stems and broad, palmately lobed leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
