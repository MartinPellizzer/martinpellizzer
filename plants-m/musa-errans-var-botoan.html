Musa errans var. botoan

Musa errans var. botoan, commonly known as the Botoan banana, is a variant of the banana plant native to the Amazon basin.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and clusters of elongated, yellowish-green bananas. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained, fertile soil. It is primarily cultivated for its edible fruit, which has a mild, sweet flavor.

Due to its genetic uniqueness, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists for its potential in breeding programs.
