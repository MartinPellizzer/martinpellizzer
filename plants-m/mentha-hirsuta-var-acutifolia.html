Mentha hirsuta var. acutifolia

Mentha hirsuta var. acutifolia, commonly known as the acute-leaved hairy mint, is a variant of the mint genus native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically pointed at the tips, giving it its specific epithet *acutifolia*. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often used in herbal medicine and as a culinary herb, similar to other mint species.
