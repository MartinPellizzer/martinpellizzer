Mentha multiflora

Mentha multiflora, commonly known as the Chinese mint, is a perennial herb native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are usually oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in whorls along the upper stems, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Mentha multiflora is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
