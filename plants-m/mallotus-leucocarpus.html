Mallotus leucocarpus

Mallotus leucocarpus, commonly known as the white-fruited mallot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is a distinctive, smooth, white or pale yellow capsule that splits open to release seeds.

It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
