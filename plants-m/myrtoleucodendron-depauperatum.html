Myrtoleucodendron depauperatum

Myrtoleucodendron depauperatum, commonly known as the poorleaf myrtle, is a rare and endangered plant species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and clove, and is known for its aromatic properties. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in shaded, rocky habitats.
