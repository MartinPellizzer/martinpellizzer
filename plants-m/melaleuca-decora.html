Melaleuca decora

Melaleuca decora, commonly known as the white bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of white to pale pink flowers that resemble bottle brushes. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves and produces small, woody fruits called follicles. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

Melaleuca decora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
