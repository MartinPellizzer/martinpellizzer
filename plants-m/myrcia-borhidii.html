Myrcia borhidii

Myrcia borhidii, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Myrcia borhidii produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often consumed by birds and other wildlife.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
