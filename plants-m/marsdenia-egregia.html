Marsdenia egregia

Marsdenia egregia, commonly known as the giant chain vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its long, flexible stems that can grow up to 30 meters in length. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth margin and a distinct midrib. Its flowers are clustered in umbels and are typically greenish-white with a subtle fragrance.

Marsdenia egregia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
