Myrtoleucodendron genistifolium

Myrtoleucodendron genistifolium, commonly known as the silverleaf myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically silver-gray in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are often fragrant. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along streams.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
