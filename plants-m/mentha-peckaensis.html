Mentha peckaensis

Mentha peckaensis, commonly known as Pecka's mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many other aromatic herbs.

Mentha peckaensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
