Martinezia truncata

Martinezia truncata, commonly known as the Mexican thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a distinctive truncate or notched tip.

Martinezia truncata is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
