Mimulus langsdorffii var. microphyllus

Mimulus langsdorffii var. microphyllus, commonly known as the dwarf yellow monkeyflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in streambanks and woodland edges.

Its floral structure is typical of the monkeyflower genus, featuring spurred petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
