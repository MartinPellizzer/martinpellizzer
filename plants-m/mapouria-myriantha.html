Mapouria myriantha

Mapouria myriantha, commonly known as the African rose or sweet sarsaparilla, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, featuring elliptic to ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces fragrant, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in panicles, often blooming during the rainy season. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
