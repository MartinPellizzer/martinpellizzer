Morinda pratimae

Morinda pratimae, commonly known as the Indian madder, is a shrub native to tropical Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant produces red or orange berries that are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
