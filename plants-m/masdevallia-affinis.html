Masdevallia affinis

Masdevallia affinis, commonly known as the pink masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, often pink or purple flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically features long, slender pseudobulbs and linear, evergreen leaves. It thrives in cool, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its striking floral display.
