Medicago truncatula var. laxicycla

Medicago truncatula var. laxicycla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed clover, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically narrow and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, and it is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with Rhizobium bacteria. This species is often used in forage and soil improvement due to its hardy nature and adaptability to various soil types.

Its ecological importance lies in its role in sustainable agriculture and ecosystem restoration.
