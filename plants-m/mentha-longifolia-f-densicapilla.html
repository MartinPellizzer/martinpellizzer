Mentha longifolia f. densicapilla

Mentha longifolia f. densicapilla, commonly known as the dense-capped mint, is a horticultural form of the common mint.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, upright foliage. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a strong, aromatic scent. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of aromatic compounds.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.
