Malva elegantifolia

Malva elegantifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Malva elegantifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
