Mutisia linariifolia

Mutisia linariifolia, commonly known as the yellow mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and rough, hairy stems. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a distinctive serrated edge. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in coastal areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
