Mimosa nervosa var. alboffiana

Mimosa nervosa var. alboffiana, commonly known as the white-leaf mimosa, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are often tinged with white or silver, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes grown in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
