Machaerium frondosum

Machaerium frondosum, commonly known as the fronded machaerium, is a tropical perennial shrub native to regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features bipinnate leaves that are often feathery and delicate in appearance. Its flowers are typically small and clustered in inflorescences, often with a distinctive purple or pink hue.

Machaerium frondosum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in partial shade.
