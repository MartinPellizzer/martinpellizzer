Melocactus koolwijkianus f. plurispina

Melocactus koolwijkianus f. plurispina, commonly known as the multi-spined melocactus, is a rare and unique cactus species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and a dense covering of spines, which provide protection against herbivores and extreme weather conditions. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its otherwise muted appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
