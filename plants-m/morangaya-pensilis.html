Morangaya pensilis

Morangaya pensilis, commonly known as the slender moringa, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Moringaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing kidney-shaped seeds. It is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and thrives in warm, sunny climates.

Due to its resilience and nutritional value, it is sometimes cultivated for its edible leaves and seeds.
