Monochoria brevipetiolata

Monochoria brevipetiolata, commonly known as the short-petioled water plant, is a aquatic herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features floating leaves with short petioles that are often submerged, and it produces small, white flowers that bloom above the water surface. The plant thrives in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds and wetlands. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it anchor in muddy substrates.

Its botanical structure supports its role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
