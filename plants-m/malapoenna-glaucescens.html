Malapoenna glaucescens

Malapoenna glaucescens, commonly known as the Malapoenna bluebush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. The plant features small, blue-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss. Its flowers are usually pale blue or white, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Malapoenna glaucescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
