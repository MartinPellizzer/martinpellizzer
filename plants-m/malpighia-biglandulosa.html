Malpighia biglandulosa

Malpighia biglandulosa, commonly known as the rough grumman or rough mombin, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, red or yellow berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish flowers. Its fruit is covered in a thick, waxy coating that helps protect it from pests and environmental stress.

Malpighia biglandulosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
