Mirabilis multiflora var. glandulosa

Mirabilis multiflora var. glandulosa, commonly known as the sticky nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its glandular hairs that give its stems and leaves a sticky texture. The plant produces strikingly colorful flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its invasive potential, it is often considered a weed in some regions.
