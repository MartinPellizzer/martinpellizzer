Mimosa fluminensis

Mimosa fluminensis, commonly known as the Brazilian sensitive plant, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Its rapid growth and unique response to touch make it a popular ornamental plant.
