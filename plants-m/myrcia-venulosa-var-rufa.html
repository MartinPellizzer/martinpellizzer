Myrcia venulosa var. rufa

Myrcia venulosa var. rufa, commonly known as the red-flowered myrcia, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and striking red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically elliptical with a glossy surface and a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but have a strong, spicy flavor.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
