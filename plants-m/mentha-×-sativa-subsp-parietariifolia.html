Mentha × sativa subsp. parietariifolia

Mentha × sativa subsp.

parietariifolia, commonly known as wall mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly fuzzy leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocky or sandy soils.

Its leaves and flowers are used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for their cooling and digestive properties.
