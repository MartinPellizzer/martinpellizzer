Malephora thunbergii

Malephora thunbergii, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
