Miconia talamancensis

Miconia talamancensis, commonly known as the Talamancan miconia, is a tropical tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences with small, white to pink flowers. It is often found in moist, lowland forests and can grow up to 10 meters tall.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
