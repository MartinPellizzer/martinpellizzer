Melanthera lobata

Melanthera lobata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of purple to pinkish-red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Melanthera lobata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
