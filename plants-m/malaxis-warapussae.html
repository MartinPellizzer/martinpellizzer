Malaxis warapussae

Malaxis warapussae, commonly known as the Warapussa orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and green. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat destruction, Malaxis warapussae faces significant threats to its survival.
