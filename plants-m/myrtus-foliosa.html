Myrtus foliosa

Myrtus foliosa, commonly known as the leaf myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. Its flowers are typically pink or white, and it produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Myrtus foliosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
