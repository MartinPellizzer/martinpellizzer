Masdevallia tricallosa

Masdevallia tricallosa, commonly known as the Peruvian orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a compact growth habit and produces intricate, colorful flowers during the spring and summer months. The plant has strap-like leaves and is known for its delicate, often fragrant blooms. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
