Mussaenda recurvata

Mussaenda recurvata, commonly known as the flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant floral display.

Mussaenda recurvata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
