Machilus kurzii

Machilus kurzii, commonly known as the Kurz's Machilus, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible.

Machilus kurzii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types and light conditions.
