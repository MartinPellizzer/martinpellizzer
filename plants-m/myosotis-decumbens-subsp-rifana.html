Myosotis decumbens subsp. rifana

Myosotis decumbens subsp.

rifana, commonly known as the Rifan bluebell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features slender, prostrate stems that produce clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers.

The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a hairy texture, and the plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats. It is adapted to cold climates and is often found in meadows and slopes at high altitudes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
