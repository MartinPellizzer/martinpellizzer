Myzorrhiza chilensis

Myzorrhiza chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean mycorrhizal plant, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is characterized by its ability to form symbiotic relationships with fungi, enhancing its nutrient absorption capabilities. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its root system is highly developed, allowing it to access water and nutrients from deep within the soil.

This species plays a vital role in stabilizing soils and supporting local ecosystems in its native habitat.
