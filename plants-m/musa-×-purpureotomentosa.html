Musa × purpureotomentosa

Musa × purpureotomentosa, commonly known as the purple-hair banana, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Musaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking purple-tinged leaves and elongated, fleshy pseudostems. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that eventually develop into banana-like fruits. It is native to Southeast Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value rather than for consumption.

Due to its unique appearance, it is a popular choice for tropical gardens and botanical collections.
