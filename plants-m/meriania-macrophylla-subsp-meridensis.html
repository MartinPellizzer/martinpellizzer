Meriania macrophylla subsp. meridensis

Meriania macrophylla subsp.

meridensis, commonly known as the Mexican star apple, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America. It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, star-shaped fruits resembling small apples. The fruit has a sweet, juicy pulp with a mild, aromatic flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in its native habitat.
