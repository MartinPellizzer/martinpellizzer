Malva repens

Malva repens, commonly known as the common mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with soft, velvety textures and clusters of pink or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other well-known species like cotton and hibiscus. Malva repens thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
