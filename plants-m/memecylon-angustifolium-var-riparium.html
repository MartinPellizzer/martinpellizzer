Memecylon angustifolium var. riparium

Memecylon angustifolium var. riparium, commonly known as river memecylon, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It typically grows along riverbanks and moist forest floors, adapting to wet and humid environments. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are a food source for various wildlife.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat in riparian zones.
