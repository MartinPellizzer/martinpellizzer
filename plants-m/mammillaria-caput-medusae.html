Mammillaria caput-medusae

Mammillaria caput-medusae, commonly known as the Medusa Cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem covered in white to yellow spines and small, rounded tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
