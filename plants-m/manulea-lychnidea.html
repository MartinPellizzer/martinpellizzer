Manulea lychnidea

Manulea lychnidea, commonly known as the candleplant, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, candle-like appearance, with a single, upright stem covered in dense, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters at the top of the stem. Its leaves are fleshy and adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Manulea lychnidea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in challenging conditions.
