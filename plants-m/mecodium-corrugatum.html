Mecodium corrugatum

Mecodium corrugatum, commonly known as the zigzag plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive zigzagging stems. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a rough, corrugated texture. It produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue or violet, typically arranged in whorls along the stems.

Mecodium corrugatum is often grown as an ornamental plant for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
