Microlicia formosa var. parvifolia

Microlicia formosa var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved beauty leaf, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often a striking green or variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for tropical gardens and indoor plant collections.
