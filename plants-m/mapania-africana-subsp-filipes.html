Mapania africana subsp. filipes

Mapania africana subsp.

filipes, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season.

The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular ornamental species.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically and aesthetically significant in its native habitat.
