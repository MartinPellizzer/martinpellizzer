Melaleuca viridiflora var. canescens

Melaleuca viridiflora var. canescens, commonly known as the grey-leafed melaleuca, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grey-green leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It is typically found in coastal and sandy soils, often in heathlands or open woodlands.

The species is well adapted to coastal environments and is relatively drought-tolerant.
