Maranta arundinacea var. variegata

Maranta arundinacea var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves with white and green patterns, which make it a popular ornamental plant. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible tubers similar to those of arrowroot. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-drained soil.

Its foliage is often used in decorative arrangements due to its aesthetic appeal.
