Mundia candolleana

Mundia candolleana, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a vibrant display during the flowering season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets that contribute to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.

The bark and leaves of Mundia candolleana have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
