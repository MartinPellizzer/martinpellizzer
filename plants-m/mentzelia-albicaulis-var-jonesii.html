Mentzelia albicaulis var. jonesii

Mentzelia albicaulis var. jonesii, commonly known as the whitestem blazingstar, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features tall, erect stems that are typically covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers arranged in clusters at the top of the stem, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often covered with a similar white pubescence.

This variety is adapted to arid and rocky habitats, showcasing a unique combination of drought tolerance and ornamental value.
