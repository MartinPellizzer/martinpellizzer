Musa rumphiana

Musa rumphiana, commonly known as the Giant Banana, is a large herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its massive, paddle-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces large, clustered inflorescences with striking purple bracts that enclose the small, cream-colored flowers. Its fruit, which is typically consumed raw, is similar to the common banana but has a more robust flavor and texture.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in tropical gardens and botanical collections.
