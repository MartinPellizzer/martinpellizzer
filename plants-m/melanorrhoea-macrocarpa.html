Melanorrhoea macrocarpa

Melanorrhoea macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited blackwood, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in coastal and semi-arid regions. The plant is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, black, fleshy fruits. It has a slow growth rate and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been historically used in furniture and construction.
