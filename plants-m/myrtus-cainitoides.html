Myrtus cainitoides

Myrtus cainitoides, commonly known as the Canary Island myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant features narrow, dark green leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, white flowers that are often fragrant. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Myrtus cainitoides thrives in well-drained soil and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.
