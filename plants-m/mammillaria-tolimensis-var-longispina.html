Mammillaria tolimensis var. longispina

Mammillaria tolimensis var. longispina, commonly known as the long-spined tolimensis cactus, is a small, compact cactus native to Mexico.

It features a globular or cylindrical stem covered in white to pale yellow spines, with the spines being notably long and slender. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in cactus collections.
