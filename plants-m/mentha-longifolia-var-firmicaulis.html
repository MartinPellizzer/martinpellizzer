Mentha longifolia var. firmicaulis

Mentha longifolia var. firmicaulis, commonly known as narrow-leaf mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, slender stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a pointed tip. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It is valued for its aromatic leaves, which contain essential oils used in aromatherapy and herbal remedies.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
