Myrcia monocarpa

Myrcia monocarpa, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature, containing several seeds. It is part of the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove.

Myrcia monocarpa is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.
