Malanea subtruncata

Malanea subtruncata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved malanea, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Malanea subtruncata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
