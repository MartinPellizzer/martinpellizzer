Mimulus langsdorffii var. alpinus

Mimulus langsdorffii var. alpinus, commonly known as the alpine monkeyflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive spurred petal. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of hair.

This variety is noted for its adaptability to harsh environments and is appreciated for its ornamental value in gardens.
