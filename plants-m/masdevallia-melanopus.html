Masdevallia melanopus

Masdevallia melanopus, commonly known as the black-veined masdevallia, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and its striking, dark purple to black flowers with contrasting white veins. The plant typically grows in cool, humid environments at high altitudes. Its flowers are usually small, with three narrow petals and a spurred lip.

Masdevallia melanopus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
