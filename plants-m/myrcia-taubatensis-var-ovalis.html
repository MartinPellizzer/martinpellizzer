Myrcia taubatensis var. ovalis

Myrcia taubatensis var. ovalis, commonly known as the oval-leaved myrcia, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant features elliptical, glossy leaves that are often arranged alternately along the stems. Its flowers are small and white, clustered in inflorescences, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are edible.

The plant has a rich history of traditional use in folk medicine for its purported health benefits.
