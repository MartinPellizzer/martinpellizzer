Maxillaria divitiflora

Maxillaria divitiflora, commonly known as the showy maxillaria, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that often feature vibrant colors such as pink, purple, and white. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are strap-like and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
