Myrtus communis var. morawieskiana

Myrtus communis var. morawieskiana, commonly known as the Morawieskiana myrtle, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features small, dark green leaves that are glossy and arranged in whorls around the stems. The plant produces tiny, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, edible berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and as a hedge plant.
