Melanthesa obliqua

Melanthesa obliqua, commonly known as the oblique milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features erect stems that are often hairy and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive waxy coating, while its flowers are clustered in umbels and range in color from pink to purple.

Melanthesa obliqua plays a vital role in supporting local pollinators, particularly butterflies and bees.
