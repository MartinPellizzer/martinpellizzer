Myrcia edulis var. dominicana

Myrcia edulis var. dominicana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

The plant is known for its aromatic foliage and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
