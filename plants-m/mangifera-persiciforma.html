Mangifera persiciforma

Mangifera persiciforma, commonly known as the Persian mango, is a species of tropical tree native to southern Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its large, edible fruits with a sweet and aromatic pulp. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into the distinctive mango fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The tree is valued not only for its fruit but also for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in local cultures.
