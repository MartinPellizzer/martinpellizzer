Monotropa hypopitys var. glaberrima

Monotropa hypopitys var. glaberrima, commonly known as the Indian tobacco, is a rare and unique plant characterized by its lack of chlorophyll and reliance on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

It is an epiphytic species, growing on the bark of trees in shaded, humid environments, typically in eastern North America. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer, and its berries are red and contain toxic compounds. It is often found in rich, moist woodlands and is considered a specialty plant in botanical gardens.
