Matthiola telum

Matthiola telum, commonly known as the Persian candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a biennial or perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, with a strong, spicy aroma.

Matthiola telum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
