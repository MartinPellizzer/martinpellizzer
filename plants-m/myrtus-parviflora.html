Myrtus parviflora

Myrtus parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered myrtle, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark blue berries. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves with a strong, aromatic scent when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Myrtus parviflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
