Microcharis tritoides

Microcharis tritoides, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant typically grows as a vine or shrub, with heart-shaped leaves and a preference for tropical and subtropical climates. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also notable for its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail, adding versatility to landscape designs.
