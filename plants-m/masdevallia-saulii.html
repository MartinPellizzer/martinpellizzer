Masdevallia saulii

Masdevallia saulii, commonly known as the Saul's masdevallia, is a terrestrial orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, often brightly colored floral structures. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically arranged in a spike, with a unique combination of colors and patterns. Its leaves are usually elliptical and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Masdevallia saulii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its delicate, exotic appearance.
