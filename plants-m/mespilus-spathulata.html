Mespilus spathulata

Mespilus spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leafed medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its oval, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically harvested in autumn, though they are often fermented or dried for consumption.

Mespilus spathulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
