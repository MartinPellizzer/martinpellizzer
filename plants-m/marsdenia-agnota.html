Marsdenia agnota

Marsdenia agnota, commonly known as the slender Marsdenia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asclepiadaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions such as India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems that often twine around supports. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive corona, and are followed by follicle fruits containing numerous seeds.
