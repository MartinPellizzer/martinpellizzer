Melilotus arvensis proles luxurians

Melilotus arvensis proles luxurians, commonly known as the purple sweet clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, pink to purple floral clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is a perennial herb that thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. Its root system fixes nitrogen, making it valuable for soil enrichment and forage.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.
