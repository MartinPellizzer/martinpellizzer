Myrcia irwinii

Myrcia irwinii, commonly known as the pink myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its dark green, elliptical leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. It produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature.

Myrcia irwinii is often found in coastal and lowland areas, thriving in humid, tropical climates.
