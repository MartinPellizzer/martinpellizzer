Melaleuca genistifolia var. coriacea

Melaleuca genistifolia var. coriacea, commonly known as the rough-leaved tea tree, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves that are covered in a rough, scaly texture. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which are often used in traditional herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in coastal and sandy areas.

This variety is closely related to other melaleuca species and is valued for its ecological and medicinal properties.
