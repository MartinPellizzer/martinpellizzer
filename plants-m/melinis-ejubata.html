Melinis ejubata

Melinis ejubata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or sicklegrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along waterways.

Its rapid growth and invasive potential make it a common weed in agricultural and natural ecosystems.
