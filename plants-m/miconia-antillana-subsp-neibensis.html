Miconia antillana subsp. neibensis

Miconia antillana subsp.

neibensis, commonly known as the Neibean miconia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean, particularly found in the Greater Antilles. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green.

The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are fleshy berries that turn red when mature, containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
