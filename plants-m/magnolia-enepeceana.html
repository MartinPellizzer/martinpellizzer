Magnolia enepeceana

Magnolia enepeceana, commonly known as the Ecuadorian magnolia, is a species native to the Andes Mountains in Ecuador.

It is a large evergreen tree that can reach heights of up to 30 meters. The plant is characterized by its large, showy, white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are broad and oval-shaped, providing a dense canopy.

The bark is smooth and gray, with a distinctive fragrance when crushed.
