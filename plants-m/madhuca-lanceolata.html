Madhuca lanceolata

Madhuca lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf tallow tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and smooth, gray bark. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, starchy fruits. Its roots and leaves have been traditionally used in folk medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
