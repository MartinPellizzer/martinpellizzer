Mirabilis limosa

Mirabilis limosa, commonly known as the swamp morning glory, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, often opening in the late afternoon and closing at dawn. The plant thrives in moist, swampy environments and has heart-shaped, glossy leaves. It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes.

Mirabilis limosa is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological benefits.
