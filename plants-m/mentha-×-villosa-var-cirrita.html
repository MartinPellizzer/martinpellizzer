Mentha × villosa var. cirrita

Mentha × villosa var. cirrita, commonly known as the woolly mint or fuzzy mint, is a hybrid plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and aromatic, often menthol-like scent. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in whorls, often with a pale purple or white color.

Mentha × villosa var. cirrita is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
