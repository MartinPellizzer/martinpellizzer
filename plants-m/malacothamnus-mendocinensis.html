Malacothamnus mendocinensis

Malacothamnus mendocinensis, commonly known as Mendocino thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a low, spreading growth habit. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.
