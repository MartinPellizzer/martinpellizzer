Marrubium heterocladum

Marrubium heterocladum, commonly known as the heterocladum horehound, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

It has a strong, aromatic scent and has been historically used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
