Melastoma paulense

Melastoma paulense, commonly known as the red-flowered Melastoma, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, with five petals and prominent stamens. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds.

Melastoma paulense is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
