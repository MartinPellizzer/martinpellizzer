Myrobalanus terminalia

Myrobalanus terminalia, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange-red fruits that are similar in appearance to persimmons. Its fruit is rich in nutrients and has been used in traditional medicine for various health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and orchards.
