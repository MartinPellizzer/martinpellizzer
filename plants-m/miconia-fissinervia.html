Miconia fissinervia

Miconia fissinervia, commonly known as the red miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to red or purple. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by distinctive, showy purple or red berries. Miconia fissinervia is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, which can make it an invasive species in certain regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable plant in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
