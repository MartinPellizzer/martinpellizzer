Myagrum clavatum

Myagrum clavatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved sandflax, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or semi-arid regions. Myagrum clavatum is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native insects and birds.
