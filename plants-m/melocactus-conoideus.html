Melocactus conoideus

Melocactus conoideus, commonly known as the cone-shaped melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem that tapers to a pointed apex, giving it a distinctive conical shape. The plant produces a spiny, ribbed structure with prominent areoles, from which both spines and flowers emerge. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, blooming in the evening, and the plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Melocactus conoideus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience.
