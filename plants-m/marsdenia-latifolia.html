Marsdenia latifolia

Marsdenia latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf Marsdenia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asclepiadaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its climbing habit and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

Marsdenia latifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to climb trellises or supports.
