Mikania belemii

Mikania belemii, commonly known as the Brazilian morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

Mikania belemii is often cultivated for its ornamental value but can become invasive in certain environments.
