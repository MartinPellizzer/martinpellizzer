Machaerium ramosiae

Machaerium ramosiae, commonly known as the hairy machaerium, is a tropical shrub native to regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, clustered flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces leguminous pods that contain several seeds.

Machaerium ramosiae is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
