Malaxis nervosa

Malaxis nervosa, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption from the soil.

Malaxis nervosa is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its delicate appearance and ecological significance in its native habitats.
