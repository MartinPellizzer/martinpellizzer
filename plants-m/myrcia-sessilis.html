Myrcia sessilis

Myrcia sessilis, commonly known as Brazilian pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It is often found in moist, tropical environments and can grow as either a shrub or a small tree.

Myrcia sessilis is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
