Malaxis calophylla var. brachycheila

Malaxis calophylla var. brachycheila, commonly known as the short-leaved malaxis, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with a subtle fragrance, typically blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in horticulture.
