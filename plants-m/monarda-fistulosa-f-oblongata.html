Monarda fistulosa f. oblongata

Monarda fistulosa f. oblongata, commonly known as the narrowleaf blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of pale blue to lavender flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its botanical characteristics make it a versatile addition to native plant gardens.
