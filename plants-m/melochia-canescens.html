Melochia canescens

Melochia canescens, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvet tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Africa, the Americas, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Melochia canescens is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
