Malva horrida

Malva horrida, commonly known as the horrid mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its hairy, rough stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its showy pink or purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
