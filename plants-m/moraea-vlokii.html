Moraea vlokii

Moraea vlokii, commonly known as the Vlok's moraea, is a striking perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, and its inflorescence consists of several flowers arranged in a raceme. Moraea vlokii prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for conservation and display.
