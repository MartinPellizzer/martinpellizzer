Mammillaria bullardiana

Mammillaria bullardiana, commonly known as the Bullard's hedgehog cactus, is a small, spineless cactus native to Mexico.

It features a globular stem covered in white, woolly hairs that help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its grayish-green body. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xerophytic gardens.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
