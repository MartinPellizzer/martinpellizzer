Machilus angustifolius

Machilus angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf Chinese laurustin, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are glossy on top and lighter in color underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by dark blue or black berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Its aromatic properties have also led to its use in traditional medicine.
