Miconia borhidiana

Miconia borhidiana, commonly known as the Borhidian miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the humid forests of South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that are followed by distinctive, fleshy, orange-red berries. Miconia borhidiana is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular plant in horticulture, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
