Macrochordion renaudii

Macrochordion renaudii, commonly known as the Natal grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
