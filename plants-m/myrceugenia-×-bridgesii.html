Myrceugenia × bridgesii

Myrceugenia × bridgesii, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a hybrid evergreen shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various birds and wildlife. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its dense foliage and year-round appearance.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, posing ecological challenges.
