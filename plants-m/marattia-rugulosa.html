Marattia rugulosa

Marattia rugulosa, commonly known as the rugose shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Marattiaceae.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, and often rugged fronds that can reach lengths of up to 2 meters. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and the Philippines. Its fronds are typically pinnately compound, with a distinctive rough texture on the underside.

Marattia rugulosa thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
