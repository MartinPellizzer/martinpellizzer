Myrcia bolivarensis

Myrcia bolivarensis, commonly known as the Bolivian myrtle, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are rich in essential oils. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Myrcia bolivarensis is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
