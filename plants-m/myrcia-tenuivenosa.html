Myrcia tenuivenosa

Myrcia tenuivenosa, commonly known as the narrow-veined myrcia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. It typically grows in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Myrcia tenuivenosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
