Maxillaria leontoglossa

Maxillaria leontoglossa, commonly known as the lion's tongue orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple and green leaves and its large, showy flowers that resemble a lion's tongue. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often with a strong, spicy fragrance.

Maxillaria leontoglossa is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
