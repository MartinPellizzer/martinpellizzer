Mimosa cylindracea

Mimosa cylindracea, commonly known as the cylindrical mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits. The plant has feathery, bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched, a response known as thigmonastic movement. It produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, and it thrives in a variety of soil types, often in disturbed or sunny habitats.

Mimosa cylindracea is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
