Marsdenia syringifolia

Marsdenia syringifolia, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers. The plant has elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Marsdenia syringifolia is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
