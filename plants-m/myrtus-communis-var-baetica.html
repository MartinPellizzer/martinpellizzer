Myrtus communis var. baetica

Myrtus communis var. baetica, commonly known as the Baetican myrtle, is a subspecies native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically harvested when fully ripe. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery and medicine make it a notable botanical species.
