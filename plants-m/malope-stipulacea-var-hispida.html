Malope stipulacea var. hispida

Malope stipulacea var. hispida, commonly known as the roughleaf malope, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features slender, woody stems covered in dense, glandular hairs, giving it a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with prominent stipules that are often hairy and persistent.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and borders.
