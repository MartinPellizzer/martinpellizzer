Macrozamia crassifolia

Macrozamia crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved cycad, is a slow-growing evergreen plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its thick, glossy, fan-shaped leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces large, cone-like structures, with male and female cones typically growing on separate plants. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
