Monocera griffithii

Monocera griffithii, commonly known as the Kashmir saffron, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking red-orange flowers during the summer months. The plant is characterized by its slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are clustered in racemes and are known for their vibrant color and aromatic scent.

Monocera griffithii is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its historical use in traditional medicine.
