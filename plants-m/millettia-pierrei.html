Millettia pierrei

Millettia pierrei, commonly known as the African peach, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces fruit that resembles a peach, which is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannah and woodland habitats.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world for horticultural purposes.
