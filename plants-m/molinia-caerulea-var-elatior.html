Molinia caerulea var. elatior

Molinia caerulea var. elatior, commonly known as the tall moor grass, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. This variety is often found in wet meadows, bogs, and other moist habitats. Its flowering spikes produce small, spikelet-like flowers that are usually brown or purple in color.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging, wet environments.
