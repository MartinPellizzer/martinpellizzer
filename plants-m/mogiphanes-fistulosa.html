Mogiphanes fistulosa

Mogiphanes fistulosa, commonly known as the pipestem lily, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, often with a hollow or tubular appearance. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are long and grass-like, forming a dense rosette at the base.

Mogiphanes fistulosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique visual appeal.
