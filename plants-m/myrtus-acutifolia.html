Myrtus acutifolia

Myrtus acutifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves that are narrow and leathery in texture. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Myrtus acutifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
