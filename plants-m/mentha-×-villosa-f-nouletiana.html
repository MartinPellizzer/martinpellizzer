Mentha × villosa f. nouletiana

Mentha × villosa f. nouletiana, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both Mentha viridis and Mentha longifolia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia, often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has hairy, square stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically a dark green color. Its fragrant, small flowers are usually pale green to white and appear in whorls along the stems.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in culinary or medicinal applications.
