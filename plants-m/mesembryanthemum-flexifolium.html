Mesembryanthemum flexifolium

Mesembryanthemum flexifolium, commonly known as the ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that can store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically during the cooler months. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

Mesembryanthemum flexifolium is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its drought tolerance and unique appearance.
