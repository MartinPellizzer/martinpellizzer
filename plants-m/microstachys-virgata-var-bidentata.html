Microstachys virgata var. bidentata

Microstachys virgata var. bidentata, commonly known as the two-toothed grass, is a herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet with two bracts that have distinct, tooth-like projections, giving it its scientific name.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
