Malva erecta

Malva erecta, commonly known as the upright mallows, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit, broad ovate leaves with a velvety texture, and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds. It is native to regions of Europe and Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Malva erecta thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
