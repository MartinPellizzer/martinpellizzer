Myosotis sylvatica subvar. firma

Myosotis sylvatica subvar. firma, commonly known as the firm bluebell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The subspecies firma is distinguished by its more compact growth habit and slightly darker flower color compared to the typical bluebell. It thrives in woodland and shady garden environments, preferring moist, well-drained soil.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
