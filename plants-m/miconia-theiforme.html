Miconia theiforme

Miconia theiforme, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its rapid growth makes it a common ornamental species. Miconia theiforme is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to urban environments.

However, it can become invasive in some regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
