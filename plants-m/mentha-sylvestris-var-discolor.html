Mentha sylvestris var. discolor

Mentha sylvestris var. discolor, commonly known as the purple mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves that range from green to purple in color, often with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its essential oils contain menthol and other compounds, making it useful in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.
