Myrteola bullata var. tetramera

Myrteola bullata var. tetramera, commonly known as the four-lobed myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to parts of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and Malaysia. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with four distinct lobes, giving it its common name.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a history of use in traditional medicine.
