Malva brevipes

Malva brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed mallow, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture and produces showy pink or purple flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
