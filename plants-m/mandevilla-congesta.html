Mandevilla congesta

Mandevilla congesta, commonly known as the tropical mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and cascading clusters of trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces showy blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and glossy, providing a lush green backdrop to the vibrant flowers.

Mandevilla congesta is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
