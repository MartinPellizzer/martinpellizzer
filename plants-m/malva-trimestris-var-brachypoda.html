Malva trimestris var. brachypoda

Malva trimestris var. brachypoda, commonly known as the short-stemmed mallows, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features hairy, ovate leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its stems are short and often prostrate, forming dense mats that help stabilize soil.

The flowers are rich in nectar and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
