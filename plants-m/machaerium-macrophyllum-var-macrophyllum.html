Machaerium macrophyllum var. macrophyllum

Machaerium macrophyllum var. macrophyllum, commonly known as the broad-leaf machaerium, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are large, lance-shaped, and often have a glossy appearance, while its flowers are small and clustered in panicles.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
