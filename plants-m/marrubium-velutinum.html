Marrubium velutinum

Marrubium velutinum, commonly known as velvet horehound, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in Mediterranean climates. The plant features gray-green, hairy leaves and produces small blue or purple flowers in clusters. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its aromatic properties.

Marrubium velutinum is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
