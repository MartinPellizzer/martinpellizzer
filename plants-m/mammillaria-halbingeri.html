Mammillaria halbingeri

Mammillaria halbingeri, commonly known as the Halbinger's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus collections.
