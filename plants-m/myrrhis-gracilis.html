Myrrhis gracilis

Myrrhis gracilis, commonly known as slender mouse-ear, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with small, hairy leaves that are often clustered at the base. Its flowers are greenish-white and arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Myrrhis gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
