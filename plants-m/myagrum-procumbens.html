Myagrum procumbens

Myagrum procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and tea tree. The plant is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit with small, aromatic leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pink, clustered in inflorescences, and attract various pollinators.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.
