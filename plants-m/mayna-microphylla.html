Mayna microphylla

Mayna microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved mayna, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine, short hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Mayna microphylla is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories.
