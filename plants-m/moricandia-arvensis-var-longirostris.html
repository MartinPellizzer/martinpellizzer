Moricandia arvensis var. longirostris

Moricandia arvensis var. longirostris, commonly known as the long-beaked sickleweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, yellow flowers and slender, branching stems. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides. Its leaves are elongated and slightly lobed, with a distinctive shape that aids in its identification.

The variety longirostris is distinguished by its longer, more pointed seed pods compared to other varieties of Moricandia arvensis.
