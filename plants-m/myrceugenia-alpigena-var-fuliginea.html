Myrceugenia alpigena var. fuliginea

Myrceugenia alpigena var. fuliginea, commonly known as the black alpine myrtle, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, typically in moist, well-drained soils. Its botanical features include a compact growth habit and a preference for cool, temperate climates.

It is often found in cloud forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential ecological significance.
