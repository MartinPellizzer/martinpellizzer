Myrcianthes pearcei

Myrcianthes pearcei, commonly known as the pearce's myrcia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom throughout the year. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains several seeds, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded habitats within tropical forests.
