Miconia decipiens

Miconia decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive miconia, is a member of the Melastomataceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in clusters. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are dispersed by birds.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is considered a problematic species in some areas.
