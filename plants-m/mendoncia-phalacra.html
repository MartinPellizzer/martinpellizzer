Mendoncia phalacra

Mendoncia phalacra, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and emit a mild, aromatic scent when crushed.

Mendoncia phalacra is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
