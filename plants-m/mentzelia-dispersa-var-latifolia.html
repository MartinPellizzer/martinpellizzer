Mentzelia dispersa var. latifolia

Mentzelia dispersa var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf desert blazingstar, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces showy yellow to orange flowers in late spring to early summer. It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in dry conditions by accessing deep soil moisture.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
