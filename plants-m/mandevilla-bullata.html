Mandevilla bullata

Mandevilla bullata, commonly known as the coral mandevilla, is a climbing shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant produces showy, clustered blooms throughout the growing season, typically from spring to fall. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture.

Mandevilla bullata thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
