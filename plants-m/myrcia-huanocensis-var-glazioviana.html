Myrcia huanocensis var. glazioviana

Myrcia huanocensis var. glazioviana, commonly known as the Glaziov's myrtle, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree with glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are red, fleshy drupes that contain several seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
