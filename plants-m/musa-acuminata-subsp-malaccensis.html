Musa acuminata subsp. malaccensis

Musa acuminata subsp.

malaccensis, commonly known as the Malacca banana, is a subspecies native to Southeast Asia. It is a herbaceous plant characterized by large, broad leaves and produces clusters of elongated, yellowish-green berries.

The plant is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. It belongs to the Musaceae family and is closely related to other banana species.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
