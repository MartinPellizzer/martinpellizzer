Muscari grandifolium var. populeum

Muscari grandifolium var. populeum, commonly known as the poplar musk grape, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, resembling grapes. The plant has strap-like, grassy foliage that is evergreen in mild climates. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
