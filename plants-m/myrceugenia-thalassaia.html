Myrceugenia thalassaia

Myrceugenia thalassaia, commonly known as the coastal myrtle, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to southern Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in coastal environments, tolerating saline conditions and sandy soils.

Myrceugenia thalassaia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in coastal ecosystems.
