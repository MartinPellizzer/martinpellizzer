Mimosa remota

Mimosa remota, commonly known as the desert mimosa, is a shrub native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves that fold shut when touched, a response known as thigmonastic movement. The plant produces small, yellow, pod-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. Its root system is adapted to tolerate drought, allowing it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

Mimosa remota plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
