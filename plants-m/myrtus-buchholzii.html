Myrtus buchholzii

Myrtus buchholzii, commonly known as the Canary Islands myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant in Mediterranean climates.

Myrtus buchholzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in local culture.
