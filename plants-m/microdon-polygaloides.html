Microdon polygaloides

Microdon polygaloides, commonly known as the yellow microdon, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, lance-shaped leaves. It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.

The species is also of interest to botanists due to its unique floral structure and ecological adaptations.
