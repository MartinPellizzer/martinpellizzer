Melasma melampyroides

Melasma melampyroides, commonly known as the redstem false flax, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Amaranthaceae, which includes many species with similar structural and ecological traits.
