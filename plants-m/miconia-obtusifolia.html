Miconia obtusifolia

Miconia obtusifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are followed by orange-red fruits containing numerous seeds. Miconia obtusifolia is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its invasive potential, it is often considered a problematic species in certain regions.
