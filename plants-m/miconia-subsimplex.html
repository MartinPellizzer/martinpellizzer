Miconia subsimplex

Miconia subsimplex, commonly known as the small-leaved miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Miconia subsimplex is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in some regions due to its aggressive spread and impact on local ecosystems.
