Myrica sapida

Myrica sapida, commonly known as the sweet fern, is a shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its feathery, aromatic foliage that emits a pleasant, resinous scent. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by dark, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Myrica sapida is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
