Maranta cordata

Maranta cordata, commonly known as the prayer plant, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves that fold upwards at night, a behavior known as nyctinasty. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is valued for its ornamental foliage. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Maranta cordata is popular among houseplant enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
