Monelytrum luederitzianum

Monelytrum luederitzianum, commonly known as the Luederitz's monelytrum, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
