Mimosa calocephala var. calocephala

Mimosa calocephala var. calocephala, commonly known as the yellow mimosa, is a shrub native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that fold shut when touched, a trait known as thigmonastic response. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in arid environments.

Its ornamental value and unique foliage make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and garden design.
