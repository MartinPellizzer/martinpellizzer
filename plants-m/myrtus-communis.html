Myrtus communis

Myrtus communis, commonly known as common myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dark green, glossy leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by dark purple berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Myrtus communis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery and culinary applications.
