Macrocalyx tomentosa

Macrocalyx tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly calyx plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, tomentose leaves and stems, which provide a fuzzy texture. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
