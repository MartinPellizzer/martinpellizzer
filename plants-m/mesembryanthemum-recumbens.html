Mesembryanthemum recumbens

Mesembryanthemum recumbens, commonly known as the carpet iceplant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit and fleshy, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is widely cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions around the world.
