Miconia theaezans var. milleflora

Miconia theaezans var. milleflora, commonly known as the showy miconia, is a tropical plant characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter.

The leaves are usually dark green with a smooth surface and often have a waxy coating. It produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting various pollinators. The plant is native to Central and South America and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is often considered a problematic species in certain regions.
