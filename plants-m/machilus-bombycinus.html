Machilus bombycinus

Machilus bombycinus, commonly known as the Chinese laurustin, is a deciduous shrub native to southern China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its small, glossy, dark green leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.

Machilus bombycinus is also valued for its aromatic wood, which has been used in traditional crafts and medicine.
