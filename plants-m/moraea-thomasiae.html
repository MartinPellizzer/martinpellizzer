Moraea thomasiae

Moraea thomasiae, commonly known as Thomas's glory, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing clusters of vibrant orange or red flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
