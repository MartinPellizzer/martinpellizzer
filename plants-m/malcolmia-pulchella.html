Malcolmia pulchella

Malcolmia pulchella, commonly known as the pretty mallows, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of showy, five-petaled yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

The flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
