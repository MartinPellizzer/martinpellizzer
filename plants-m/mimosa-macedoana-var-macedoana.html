Mimosa macedoana var. macedoana

Mimosa macedoana var. macedoana, commonly known as the Macedonian mimosa, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and prefers well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in temperate climates.

The leaves are sensitive to touch, folding shut when disturbed, a trait that adds to its unique botanical interest.
