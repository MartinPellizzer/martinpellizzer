Mutisia bracteata

Mutisia bracteata, commonly known as the velvetleaf mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. Its showy yellow flowers are typically arranged in a cluster, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Mutisia bracteata is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.
