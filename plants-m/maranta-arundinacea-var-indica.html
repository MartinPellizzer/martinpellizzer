Maranta arundinacea var. indica

Maranta arundinacea var. indica, commonly known as the arrowroot plant, is a tropical perennial herb native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its rhizomes are the primary part used, valued for their starchy, edible roots that are rich in carbohydrates.

This plant is widely cultivated for its nutritional value and versatility in culinary applications.
