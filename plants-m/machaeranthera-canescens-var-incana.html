Machaeranthera canescens var. incana

Machaeranthera canescens var. incana, commonly known as the silverleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It is characterized by its grayish-white, woolly leaves and showy, pink to purple composite flower heads. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and roadsides. Its foliage is covered in fine, appressed hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
