Marsdenia rotheana

Marsdenia rotheana, commonly known as the Red-veined Marsdenia, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with prominent reddish veins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by follicles containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Its ornamental value is due to its attractive foliage and unique floral structure.
