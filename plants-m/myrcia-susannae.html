Myrcia susannae

Myrcia susannae, commonly known as the pink myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

Myrcia susannae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine by local communities.
