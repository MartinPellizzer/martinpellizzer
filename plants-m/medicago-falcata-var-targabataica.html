Medicago falcata var. targabataica

Medicago falcata var. targabataica, commonly known as the Targabataica goat's rue, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive heart-shaped base and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and small, oval-shaped pods that contain lens-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

This variant is notable for its ecological adaptability and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
