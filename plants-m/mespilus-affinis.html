Mespilus affinis

Mespilus affinis, commonly known as the common medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, fragrant flowers and unique fruit structure. The fruit, known as a pomes, is typically unripe and astringent when harvested, but it softens and becomes edible after a process called "mellowing.". It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
