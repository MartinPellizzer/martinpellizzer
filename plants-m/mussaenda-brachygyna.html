Mussaenda brachygyna

Mussaenda brachygyna, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, aromatic foliage. The plant produces clusters of showy, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, making it a striking ornamental species. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a distinctive, narrow shape with serrated edges.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
