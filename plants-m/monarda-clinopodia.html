Monarda clinopodia

Monarda clinopodia, commonly known as narrowleaf horsemint, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of purple and blue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and wetlands.

Monarda clinopodia is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal remedies.
