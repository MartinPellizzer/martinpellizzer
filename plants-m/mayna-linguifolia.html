Mayna linguifolia

Mayna linguifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf mayna, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in clusters. Mayna linguifolia is often found growing in moist, shaded environments such as forests or near water bodies.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in local medicine.
