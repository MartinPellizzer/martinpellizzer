Malpighiodes benthamiana

Malpighiodes benthamiana, commonly known as the Bentham's Malpighiaceae, is a species native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Malpighiaceae, which is characterized by its showy, often pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous shrub with opposite, simple leaves. Its flowers are borne in clusters and feature five petals that are often fused at the base.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and has a fibrous root system that helps it survive in dry conditions.
