Melanthesa microphylla

Melanthesa microphylla, commonly known as the small-leafed melanthus, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with finely toothed margins, and are arranged alternately along the stem.

Melanthesa microphylla is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
