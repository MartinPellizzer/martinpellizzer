Melocactus delessertianus

Melocactus delessertianus, commonly known as the Delessert's melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America, particularly in Brazil and Paraguay.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive, rounded body and a thick, ribbed stem that stores water efficiently. The plant produces yellow flowers during the rainy season, which are typically borne at the top of the stem. Its roots are shallow and adapted to absorb moisture from the soil quickly.

Melocactus delessertianus is well-suited to rocky or sandy soils and requires minimal water once established.
