Miconia ledifolia

Miconia ledifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by distinctive, round, orange-red fruits. Miconia ledifolia is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of moist, tropical environments.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered an invasive species in some regions outside its native range.
