Muhlenbergia polycaulis

Muhlenbergia polycaulis, commonly known as the purple muhlenbergia, is a native grass species found in the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clump-forming growth habit and vibrant purple to reddish plumes that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various garden conditions. Its ornamental value comes from its striking foliage and long-lasting floral display.

Muhlenbergia polycaulis is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil in native landscapes.
