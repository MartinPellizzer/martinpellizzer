Momordica charantia f. muricata

Momordica charantia f. muricata, commonly known as the hairy bitter melon, is a variant of the bitter gourd plant characterized by its rough, spiny skin.

This tropical vine produces elongated, green fruits that turn orange when mature, often featuring a distinctive warty surface. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is cultivated for its edible fruits, which have a bitter taste and are used in various cuisines.

Botanically, it belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is valued for its nutritional and medicinal properties.
