Malpighia macracantha subsp. centralis

Malpighia macracantha subsp.

centralis, commonly known as the Central American wax apple, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Central America. It is characterized by its small, edible berries that are typically red when mature, with a waxy coating.

The plant produces simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

The species is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its fruit, which is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
