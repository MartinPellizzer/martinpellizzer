Matelea pedicellata

Matelea pedicellata, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

Matelea pedicellata is part of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other morning glory species.
