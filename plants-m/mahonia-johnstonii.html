Mahonia johnstonii

Mahonia johnstonii, commonly known as the Johnston's mahonia, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, followed by blue-black berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its evergreen foliage and seasonal blooms.
