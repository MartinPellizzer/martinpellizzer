Mespilus tomentosa var. lutea

Mespilus tomentosa var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow medlar, is a variant of the medlar plant characterized by its distinctive yellow fruit.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region, featuring rough, grayish bark and oval-shaped leaves. The flowers are small and greenish-white, appearing in clusters, while the fruit develops from a fleshy, spiky calyx. The plant is known for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.

Its fruit, though not typically eaten raw, has been used in jams and preserves.
