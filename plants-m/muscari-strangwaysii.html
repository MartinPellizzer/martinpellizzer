Muscari strangwaysii

Muscari strangwaysii, commonly known as the Persian bluebell, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
