Monopyle paniculata

Monopyle paniculata, commonly known as the clustered panicled mallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive, fern-like appearance. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers arranged in dense, panicled inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
