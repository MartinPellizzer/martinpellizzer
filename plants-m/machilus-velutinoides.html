Machilus velutinoides

Machilus velutinoides, commonly known as the velvetleaf mahonia, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by dark blue or black berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
