Marcetia ericoides

Marcetia ericoides, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in late spring or early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Marcetia ericoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
