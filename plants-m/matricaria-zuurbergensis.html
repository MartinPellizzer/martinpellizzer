Matricaria zuurbergensis

Matricaria zuurbergensis, commonly known as Zuurberg daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are yellow with a central brown disc, and it produces small, hairy fruits that disperse its seeds.

Matricaria zuurbergensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
