Musa campestris var. sarawakensis

Musa campestris var. sarawakensis, commonly known as the Sarawak banana, is a variant of the banana plant native to the tropical regions of Borneo.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and elongated, clustered fruit bunches that typically contain elongated, yellow-orange bananas. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil, making it well-suited for tropical and subtropical environments. Its fruit is prized for its sweet flavor and is often used in both fresh consumption and culinary applications.

The plant also plays a significant role in local agriculture and traditional practices in its native regions.
