Myrtus biflora

Myrtus biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. The plant produces small white or pale pink flowers, typically in clusters, which bloom in spring. Its evergreen nature and compact growth make it suitable for ornamental gardening.

The botanical characteristics of Myrtus biflora include its aromatic foliage and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
