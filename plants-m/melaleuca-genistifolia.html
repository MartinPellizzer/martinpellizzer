Melaleuca genistifolia

Melaleuca genistifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known species like eucalyptus and tea tree. It is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

Melaleuca genistifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in horticulture for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
