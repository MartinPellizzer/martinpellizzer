Malus communis subsp. mitis

Malus communis subsp.

mitis, commonly known as the sweet cherry, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn.

The tree produces clusters of small, white flowers in spring, which are followed by sweet, fleshy fruits. The fruit, known as a cherry, is typically red or dark red when ripe and is widely cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
