Mikania scandens subsp. paraguayensis

Mikania scandens subsp.

paraguayensis, commonly known as Paraguayan morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy blue to violet flowers.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain areas.
