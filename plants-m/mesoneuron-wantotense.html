Mesoneuron wantotense

Mesoneuron wantotense, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale green flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
