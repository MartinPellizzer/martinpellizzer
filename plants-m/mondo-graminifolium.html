Mondo graminifolium

Mondo graminifolium, commonly known as Japanese forest grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and grow in dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to suppress weeds.

Its foliage remains attractive throughout the year, making it a popular choice for ground cover in shaded gardens.
