Malpighia infestissima

Malpighia infestissima, commonly known as the wax apple or purple passionfruit, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple or red fruits, which are covered in a waxy, whitish coating. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

The fruit is typically harvested when fully mature, and it is valued for its flavor and ornamental appeal.
