Mimulus guttatus var. lyratus

Mimulus guttatus var. lyratus, commonly known as the lyrated monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange or red flowers that are typically spurred at the back, resembling a monkey's face. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in stream banks and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
