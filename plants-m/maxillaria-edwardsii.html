Maxillaria edwardsii

Maxillaria edwardsii, commonly known as the Edwards' orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Maxillaria edwardsii is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
