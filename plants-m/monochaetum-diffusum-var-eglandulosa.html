Monochaetum diffusum var. eglandulosa

Monochaetum diffusum var. eglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It typically grows in wetland or swampy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features linear, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy, and its flowers are small and clustered in dense spikes. It is characterized by the presence of glandular structures on its stems and leaves, which give it its botanical variant name.

This species is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
