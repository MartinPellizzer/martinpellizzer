Musa troglodytarum var. dolioformis

Musa troglodytarum var. dolioformis, commonly known as the dwarf banana, is a compact, ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features broad, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green with a slight sheen. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that eventually develop into edible bananas, though they are not commonly cultivated for fruit production. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often grown as a houseplant or in tropical gardens for its aesthetic value.
