Melica curtipendula

Melica curtipendula, commonly known as slender basketgrass, is a species of grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist environments such as wet meadows and stream banks.

Melica curtipendula is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an indicator species for wetland habitats.
