Montia minor subsp. rivularis

Montia minor subsp.

rivularis, commonly known as the rivular bluebells, is a small annual herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors.

The plant features slender, branched stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is often found in riparian zones.
