Malaxis brachyrrhynchos

Malaxis brachyrrhynchos, commonly known as the short-stemmed malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces a single spike of flowers.

Malaxis brachyrrhynchos is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate appearance and specialized pollination mechanisms.
