Mammillaria dioica var. neopalmeri

Mammillaria dioica var. neopalmeri, commonly known as the Neopalmer's milk barrel cactus, is a compact, spherical cactus native to the Sonoran Desert.

It features prominent, white, woolly spines and a ribbed, segmented stem that often turns reddish in full sun. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer, adding a striking contrast to its textured appearance. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
