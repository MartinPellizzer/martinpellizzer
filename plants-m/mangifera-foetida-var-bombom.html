Mangifera foetida var. bombom

Mangifera foetida var. bombom, commonly known as the wild mango or bitter mango, is a variant of the mango genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elongated fruit with a thick, fibrous pulp and a distinctively bitter taste. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside.

This variety is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments.
