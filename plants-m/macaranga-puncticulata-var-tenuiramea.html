Macaranga puncticulata var. tenuiramea

Macaranga puncticulata var. tenuiramea, commonly known as the narrow-leaf macaranga, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with a distinctive pungent odor. The leaves are usually ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. This plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, often found in lowland forests and disturbed areas.

Its flowers are small and clustered, often accompanied by specialized structures called cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.
