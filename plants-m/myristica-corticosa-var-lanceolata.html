Myristica corticosa var. lanceolata

Myristica corticosa var. lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Indian Ocean islands.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its lanceolate leaves and fragrant, reddish-brown fruits. The plant produces a seed enclosed in a hard, aromatic shell, which is used in the production of nutmeg and mace. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The botanical features of this plant make it valuable for both ecological and commercial purposes.
