Milium effusum var. schmidtianum

Milium effusum var. schmidtianum, commonly known as Schmidt's millet, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its delicate appearance and ease of cultivation.
