Mesostemma kotschyanum subsp. pycnanthum

Mesostemma kotschyanum subsp.

pycnanthum, commonly known as the Kotschyan's mesostemma, is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive floral morphology. It is a shrub-like plant native to specific regions of Central Asia, particularly in the mountainous areas of Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan.

The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink to purple. Its fruits are legumes that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and limited distribution.
