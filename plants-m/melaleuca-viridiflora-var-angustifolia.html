Melaleuca viridiflora var. angustifolia

Melaleuca viridiflora var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf honeymyrtle, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, woody capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions, contributing to local biodiversity.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native fauna.
