Monelasmum pungens

Monelasmum pungens, commonly known as the sharpleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often sharply pointed, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of small, three-sided spikelets that are arranged in a dense cluster.

Monelasmum pungens is known for its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
