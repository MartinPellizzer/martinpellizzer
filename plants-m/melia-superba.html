Melia superba

Melia superba, commonly known as the sacred fig or holy fig, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. The tree is known for its fruit, which is a small, round fig with a sweet, edible pulp. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Melia superba is also valued for its ornamental beauty and cultural significance in various regions.
