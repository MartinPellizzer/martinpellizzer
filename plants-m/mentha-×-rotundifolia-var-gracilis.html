Mentha × rotundifolia var. gracilis

Mentha × rotundifolia var. gracilis, commonly known as narrow-leaved peppermint, is a hybrid mint species characterized by its slender, elongated leaves.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in whorled clusters during the summer months. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, which are responsible for its distinctive minty scent.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for use in herbal teas and aromatherapy.
