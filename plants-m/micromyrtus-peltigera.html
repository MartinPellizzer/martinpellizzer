Micromyrtus peltigera

Micromyrtus peltigera, commonly known as the narrow-leafed myrtle, is a small shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal regions. It is a member of the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees.

Micromyrtus peltigera is well adapted to dry conditions and is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value.
