Microlicia punctatissima var. angustifolia

Microlicia punctatissima var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved microlicia, is a member of the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often dotted with small glands. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It is native to tropical regions of Africa and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
