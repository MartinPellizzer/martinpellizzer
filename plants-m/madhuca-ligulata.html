Madhuca ligulata

Madhuca ligulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mahua, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in various cultures.
