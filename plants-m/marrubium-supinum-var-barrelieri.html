Marrubium supinum var. barrelieri

Marrubium supinum var. barrelieri, commonly known as the Barrelier's horehound, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features hairy, gray-green leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in rocky or dry soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
