Miscanthus condensatus var. purpurascens

Miscanthus condensatus var. purpurascens, commonly known as purple moor grass, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and its striking purple to pinkish flower heads that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands, bogs, and along the edges of lakes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that contrasts with the vibrant flower color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insects and birds.
