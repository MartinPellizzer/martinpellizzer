Millettia drasticoides

Millettia drasticoides, commonly known as the giant ironwood tree, is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its massive, buttressed roots and thick, dark bark. The tree produces large, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.

The plant also plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
