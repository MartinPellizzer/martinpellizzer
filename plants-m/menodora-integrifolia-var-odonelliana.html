Menodora integrifolia var. odonelliana

Menodora integrifolia var. odonelliana, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a climbing vine native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a sweet fragrance. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its flowers open at night and close by morning, making it a popular choice for evening gardens.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique flowering pattern.
