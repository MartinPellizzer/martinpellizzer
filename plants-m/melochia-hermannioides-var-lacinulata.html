Melochia hermannioides var. lacinulata

Melochia hermannioides var. lacinulata, commonly known as the laceleaf melochia, is a tropical shrub characterized by its distinctive foliage.

Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a serrated margin and a velvety texture that gives it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
