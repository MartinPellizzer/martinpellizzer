Macropidia fumosa

Macropidia fumosa, commonly known as the ghost gum, is a distinctive Australian plant characterized by its unique, gnarled trunk and pale, bark-like foliage.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees, and is native to the arid regions of Western Australia. The plant has a slow growth rate and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping, though it requires careful cultivation to thrive.
