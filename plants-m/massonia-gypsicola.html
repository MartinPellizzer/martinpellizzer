Massonia gypsicola

Massonia gypsicola, commonly known as the gypsum massonia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or white, typically during the summer months. It prefers well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
