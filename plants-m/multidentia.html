Multidentia

Multidentia, commonly known as the toothed leaf plant, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

It features finely divided, tooth-like leaves that give it its scientific name, and it typically grows in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
