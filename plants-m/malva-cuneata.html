Malva cuneata

Malva cuneata, commonly known as the common mallow, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other well-known species like cotton and hibiscus. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

Malva cuneata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
