Mikania bullata

Mikania bullata, commonly known as the silverleaf mikania, is a perennial vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and forest edges.

Mikania bullata is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
