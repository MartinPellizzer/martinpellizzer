Myrcia incompleta

Myrcia incompleta, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that turn red when mature.

Myrcia incompleta is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
