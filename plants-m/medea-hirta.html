Medea hirta

Medea hirta, commonly known as the rough meadow cranesbill, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist grasslands, meadows, and along streambanks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that have a rough, hairy texture. Its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Medea hirta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
