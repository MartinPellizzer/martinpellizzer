Montia fontana subsp. rivularis

Montia fontana subsp.

rivularis, commonly known as the river montia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to western North America. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors.

The plant features small, oval leaves that are usually less than 1 inch in length. Its flowers are white or pale pink, clustered in dense inflorescences, and bloom from spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
