Mentha derelicta

Mentha derelicta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a serrated edge and a strong, menthol-like fragrance.

Mentha derelicta is often used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and cooling properties.
