Myrica rogersii

Myrica rogersii, commonly known as the wax myrtle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, waxy leaves that are typically dark green on top and whitish underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Myrica rogersii is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
