Maxillaria roseans

Maxillaria roseans, commonly known as the rosean orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and the production of showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant typically features a pseudobulb with a single leaf and a raceme of flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn. Its roots are fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained, humus-rich soil.

Maxillaria roseans is relatively easy to cultivate, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
