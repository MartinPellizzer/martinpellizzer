Matayba sylvatica

Matayba sylvatica, commonly known as the yellow bell or yellow trumpet tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it can grow up to 20 meters tall in suitable conditions.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release its seeds.
