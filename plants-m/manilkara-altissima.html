Manilkara altissima

Manilkara altissima, commonly known as the mahogany tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and smooth, dark-brown bark. The tree produces large, glossy green leaves and yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a large, woody capsule containing a single seed, and it is highly valued for its durable, heavy wood.

The plant has significant ecological and economic importance, particularly in the timber industry.
