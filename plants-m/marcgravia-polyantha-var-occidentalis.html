Marcgravia polyantha var. occidentalis

Marcgravia polyantha var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western star sapphire, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins and produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for greenhouse cultivation.
