Maranta capitata

Maranta capitata, commonly known as the "prayer plant" or "night bloomer," is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage that changes throughout the day, with leaves folding upwards at night. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is known for its rhizomatous growth habit. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and can display vivid patterns of red, purple, and green.

Maranta capitata thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.
