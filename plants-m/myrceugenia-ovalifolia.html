Myrceugenia ovalifolia

Myrceugenia ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to the temperate rainforests of southern Brazil.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. Myrceugenia ovalifolia is typically evergreen, with a dense, shrub-like growth habit.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, humid environments.
