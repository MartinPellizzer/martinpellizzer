Mentha reuteriana

Mentha reuteriana, commonly known as German mint, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its distinctive minty scent.

Mentha reuteriana is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
