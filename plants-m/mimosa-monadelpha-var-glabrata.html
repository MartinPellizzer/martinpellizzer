Mimosa monadelpha var. glabrata

Mimosa monadelpha var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are composed of multiple pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by fluffy, spherical seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
