Memecylon eglandulosum var. bezavonense

Memecylon eglandulosum var. bezavonense, commonly known as the Bezavon memecylon, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five petals, typically in shades of white or pale pink. Its fruits are capsules that split open to release tiny seeds.
