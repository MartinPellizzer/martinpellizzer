Melaleuca pancheri

Melaleuca pancheri, commonly known as the Pancheri tea tree, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is a member of the Myrtaceae family, which includes other familiar species like eucalyptus and tea tree. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Melaleuca pancheri is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in indigenous medicine.
