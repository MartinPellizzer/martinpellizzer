Moquinia velutina

Moquinia velutina, commonly known as velvet moquinia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features rhizomatous growth and produces small, clustered inflorescences of white to pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

This plant plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
