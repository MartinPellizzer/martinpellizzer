Mimosa quadrivalvis var. diffusa

Mimosa quadrivalvis var. diffusa, commonly known as the velvet mimosa, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that fold together when touched, a response triggered by the plant's sensitive leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late spring to early summer. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a tendency to peel in thin layers.

The velvet mimosa is often found in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in woodland areas.
