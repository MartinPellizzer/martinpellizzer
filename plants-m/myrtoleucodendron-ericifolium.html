Myrtoleucodendron ericifolium

Myrtoleucodendron ericifolium, commonly known as the red-leaved heath, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pink, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, with a distinctive reddish hue that adds to its ornamental value.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.
