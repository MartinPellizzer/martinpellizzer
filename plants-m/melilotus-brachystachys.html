Melilotus brachystachys

Melilotus brachystachys, commonly known as narrow-stalked melilot, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, and it produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine.

Melilotus brachystachys is also valued for its role in ecological restoration due to its adaptability and ability to improve soil quality.
