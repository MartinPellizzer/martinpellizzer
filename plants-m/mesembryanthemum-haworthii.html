Mesembryanthemum haworthii

Mesembryanthemum haworthii, commonly known as the ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, often blue-green leaves that store water. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ability to survive in arid conditions makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
