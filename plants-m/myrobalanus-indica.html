Myrobalanus indica

Myrobalanus indica, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that are edible when fully ripe. Its fruit is rich in nutrients and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
