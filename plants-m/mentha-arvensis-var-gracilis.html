Mentha arvensis var. gracilis

Mentha arvensis var. gracilis, commonly known as slender field mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, making it a valued plant in both traditional medicine and culinary applications.
