Myrcia rostrata f. pseudomini

Myrcia rostrata f. pseudomini, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It has dark green, glossy leaves that are elliptical in shape and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. These berries are an important food source for various bird species.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
