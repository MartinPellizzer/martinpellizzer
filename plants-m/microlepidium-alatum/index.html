Microlepidium Alatum

Microlepidium alatum is a small, flowering plant native to temperate regions of the northern hemisphere. It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is often found in moist, disturbed soils along roadsides and in meadows. This plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and delicate, feather-like leaves. It produces tiny, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. Despite its modest appearance, Microlepidium alatum plays an important role in supporting local ecosystems and is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
