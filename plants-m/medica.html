Medica

Medica, commonly known as the medicinal plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Apocynaceae.

It is characterized by its erect or spreading growth habit and often features opposite or whorled leaves. The flowers are typically showy, with five petals that are often brightly colored, attracting pollinators. Many species within this genus contain alkaloids, which have been used in traditional medicine for various therapeutic purposes.

However, some species are toxic and require careful handling due to their potent chemical composition.
