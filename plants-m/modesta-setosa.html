Modesta setosa

Modesta setosa, commonly known as the soft-hair dandelion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in clusters, producing a fluffy seed head that aids in wind dispersal.

Modesta setosa is often found in meadows and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
