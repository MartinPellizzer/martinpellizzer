Mirabilis jalapa subsp. lindheimeri

Mirabilis jalapa subsp.

lindheimeri, commonly known as the Lindheimer's four-o'clock, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its striking, vibrant flowers that open in the late afternoon and close at night.

The plant typically grows as an annual herb with erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves. Its flowers come in a range of colors, including red, orange, and yellow, and are often tubular in shape.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
