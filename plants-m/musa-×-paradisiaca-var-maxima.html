Musa × paradisiaca var. maxima

Musa × paradisiaca var. maxima, commonly known as the elephant ear plant, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant is a hybrid of Musa acuminata and Musa balbisiana, and it belongs to the Musaceae family. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

The leaves are often used in ornamental gardening due to their striking appearance.
