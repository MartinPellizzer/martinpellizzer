Merendera montana var. bulbocodioides

Merendera montana var. bulbocodioides, commonly known as the mountain merendera, is a perennial herb native to the high elevations of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures that serve as storage organs, aiding in its survival in harsh alpine conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
