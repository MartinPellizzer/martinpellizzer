Montalbania

Montalbania, commonly known as the sacred mountain orchid, is a rare and elusive plant found in the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet and white petals with intricate patterns. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil. Due to its habitat specificity, Montalbania is highly sensitive to environmental changes and is considered endangered.

Its unique beauty and ecological significance make it a subject of both scientific interest and cultural reverence.
