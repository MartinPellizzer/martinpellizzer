Myrtus communis var. adrianii

Myrtus communis var. adrianii, commonly known as the Adrian's myrtle, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, edible berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
