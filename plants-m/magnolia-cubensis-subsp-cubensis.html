Magnolia cubensis subsp. cubensis

Magnolia cubensis subsp.

cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban magnolia, is a flowering plant native to Cuba. It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in tropical and subtropical climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
