Malachra alceifolia var. typica

Malachra alceifolia var. typica, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, sky-blue to white flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Malachra alceifolia var. typica is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
