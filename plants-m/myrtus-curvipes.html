Myrtus curvipes

Myrtus curvipes, commonly known as the narrow-leaf myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring small, narrow leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark blue berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

Myrtus curvipes is well adapted to coastal and rocky environments, showing resilience to salt and drought.
