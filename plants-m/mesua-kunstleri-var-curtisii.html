Mesua kunstleri var. curtisii

Mesua kunstleri var. curtisii, commonly known as the Curtis's flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental planting in tropical and subtropical climates.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
