Metalasia cristata

Metalasia cristata, commonly known as the crowned grass or the crown vetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive, hairy leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. Metalasia cristata thrives in well-drained soils and is often used for erosion control due to its extensive root system.

It is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
