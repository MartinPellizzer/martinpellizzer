Mitracarpus discolor

Mitracarpus discolor, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and has palmately lobed leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its unique flower structure and vibrant color make it a notable ornamental plant.
