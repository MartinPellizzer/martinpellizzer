Myrcia ayabambensis

Myrcia ayabambensis, commonly known as the Ayabamba myrtle, is a shrub native to the cloud forests of Peru.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are typically white or pink, clustered in small inflorescences, and emit a subtle, pleasant fragrance.

The fruit is a small, round capsule that splits open to release the seeds.
