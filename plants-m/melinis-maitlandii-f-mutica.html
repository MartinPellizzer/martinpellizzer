Melinis maitlandii f. mutica

Melinis maitlandii f. mutica, commonly known as the narrowleaf melinis, is a variant of the melinis grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and adaptable plant.

Its botanical features make it suitable for use in ornamental landscaping and erosion control in suitable climates.
