Mentha × kerneri f. peracutiformis

Mentha × kerneri f. peracutiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf peppermint, is a hybrid plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and aromatic, slightly hairy stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild mint scent, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.
