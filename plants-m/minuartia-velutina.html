Minuartia velutina

Minuartia velutina, commonly known as the velvety rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its mat-forming growth habit and small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety coating. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It is typically found in rocky or sandy soils in arctic and alpine regions.

Minuartia velutina is adapted to harsh environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
