Montia variabilis

Montia variabilis, commonly known as variable speedwell, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Montiaceae and is characterized by its small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Montia variabilis is known for its variable growth habits and is sometimes used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.
