Meistera stephanocolea

Meistera stephanocolea, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, often blooming during the wet season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of tropical forests.

Meistera stephanocolea is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
