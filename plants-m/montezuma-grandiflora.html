Montezuma grandiflora

Montezuma grandiflora, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a striking tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting white or yellow throat. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its leaves are typically broad and glossy, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Montezuma grandiflora is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and is a popular choice in tropical gardens and as a houseplant.
