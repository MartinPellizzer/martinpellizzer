Mentha × dalmatica var. cinerascens

Mentha × dalmatica var. cinerascens, commonly known as gray mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a grayish-white pubescence. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
