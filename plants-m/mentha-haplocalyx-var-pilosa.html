Mentha haplocalyx var. pilosa

Mentha haplocalyx var. pilosa, commonly known as the Chinese Mentha or hairy mint, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional Chinese medicine for their cooling properties and digestive benefits.

The plant's essential oils, rich in menthol and other compounds, contribute to its widespread use in aromatherapy and herbal remedies.
