Mandevilla annulariifolia

Mandevilla annulariifolia, commonly known as the ring-leaved mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive ring-like pattern on the underside, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Mandevilla annulariifolia is a climbing vine that produces fragrant blooms throughout the growing season.
