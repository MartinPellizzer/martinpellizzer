Miconia bernardii, commonly known as the golden miconia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Melastomataceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white bracts that resemble petals. It produces small, clustered flowers that are often overlooked due to the prominent bracts.

Miconia bernardii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
