Mahonia subimbricata

Mahonia subimbricata, commonly known as the evergreen mahonia, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, evergreen foliage that turns bronze in the winter. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by blue-black berries. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Mahonia subimbricata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
