Montia fontana var. variabilis

Montia fontana var. variabilis, commonly known as the variable watercress, is a delicate annual plant native to moist, temperate regions.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves and produces tiny white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in wet soils near streams, marshes, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications due to their peppery flavor.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Montiaceae and is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
