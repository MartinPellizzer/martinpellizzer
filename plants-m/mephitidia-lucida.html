Mephitidia lucida

Mephitidia lucida, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms that attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth texture.

The moonflower thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
