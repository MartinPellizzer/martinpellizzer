Mammea acuminata

Mammea acuminata, commonly known as the sour orange or wild tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange or red fruits that are rich in tannins. The seeds are encased in a hard, woody pulp, and the tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is adaptable to a range of climatic conditions.
