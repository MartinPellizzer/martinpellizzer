Myosotis lyallii subsp. lyallii

Myosotis lyallii subsp.

lyallii, commonly known as the Lyall's forget-me-not, is a herbaceous perennial native to New Zealand. It features delicate blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from September to November.

The plant has slender, upright stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky outcrops.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
