Malcolmia aegyptiaca var. longisiliqua

Malcolmia aegyptiaca var. longisiliqua, commonly known as Egyptian malva, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pale yellow to white flowers with five petals, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
