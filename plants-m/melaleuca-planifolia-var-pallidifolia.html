Melaleuca planifolia var. pallidifolia

Melaleuca planifolia var. pallidifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paperbark, is a small evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, pale green leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces woody capsules containing numerous small, black seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
