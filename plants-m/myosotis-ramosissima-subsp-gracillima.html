Myosotis ramosissima subsp. gracillima

Myosotis ramosissima subsp.

gracillima, commonly known as the slender bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to New Zealand. It features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces clusters of small, delicate blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
