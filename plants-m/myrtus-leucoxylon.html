Myrtus leucoxylon

Myrtus leucoxylon, commonly known as the silver myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that have a silvery underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by dark blue berries. Myrtus leucoxylon is valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in ornamental gardening.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and low-maintenance plant.
