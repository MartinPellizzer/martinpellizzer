Mammillaria temorisensis

Mammillaria temorisensis, commonly known as the desert hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a ribbed structure and spines that are typically white to yellow in color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and desert landscapes.
