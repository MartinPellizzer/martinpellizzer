Musa textilis

Musa textilis, commonly known as the banana plant, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Musaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and a central stem that produces clusters of fruit. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in tropical climates with high humidity and warm temperatures. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts, while the fruit is a staple food in many cultures.

Musa textilis plays an important role in both ecological and agricultural systems.
