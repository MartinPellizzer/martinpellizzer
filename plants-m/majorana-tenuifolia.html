Majorana tenuifolia

Majorana tenuifolia, commonly known as slender wormwood, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic properties.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
