Mazus pumilus var. macrocalyx

Mazus pumilus var. macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered mazus, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink to purple in color, with a distinctive hooded structure. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a desirable plant for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
