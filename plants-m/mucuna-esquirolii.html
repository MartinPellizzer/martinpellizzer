Mucuna esquirolii

Mucuna esquirolii, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and distinctive, twisted pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and is known for its rapid growth habit. It is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.

The plant also plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
