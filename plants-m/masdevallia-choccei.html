Masdevallia choccei

Masdevallia choccei, commonly known as the Chocolate Orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark, velvety petals that range in color from deep brown to black, giving it its striking appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often with a subtle fragrance, and it thrives in cool, high-altitude conditions.

Masdevallia choccei is valued for its ornamental beauty and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
