Monarda fistulosa var. typica

Monarda fistulosa var. typica, commonly known as wild bergamot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink to lavender in color, arranged in dense, conical clusters. The plant has aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a minty scent, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Monarda fistulosa var. typica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
