Machaerium armatum

Machaerium armatum, commonly known as the hairy machaerium, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is often found in moist, wooded areas and can thrive in partial shade. Its root system is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

The plant's ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and unique floral structure.
