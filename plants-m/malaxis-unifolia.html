Malaxis unifolia

Malaxis unifolia, commonly known as the single-flowered malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, leafless stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers shady, moist environments. Its flowers are often clustered in a single, elongated spike, and the species is noted for its delicate appearance.

Malaxis unifolia is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
