Melochia pyramidata f. transitoria

Melochia pyramidata f. transitoria, commonly known as the transitory melochia, is a variant of the melochia plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are often deeply lobed and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, red or orange fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its unique foliage and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
