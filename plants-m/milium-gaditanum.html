Milium gaditanum

Milium gaditanum, commonly known as the Spanish millet, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a small annual herb that typically grows in dry, disturbed soils across the Mediterranean region. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale green to brown florets. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight pubescence.

Milium gaditanum is notable for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
