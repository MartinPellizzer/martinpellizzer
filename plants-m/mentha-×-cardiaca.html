Mentha × cardiaca

Mentha × cardiaca, commonly known as heartwort, is a hybrid plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves, which are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Mentha × cardiaca is often used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties and potential therapeutic benefits.
