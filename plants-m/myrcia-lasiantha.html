Myrcia lasiantha

Myrcia lasiantha, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a variety of soil types.

Due to its ornamental value, it has been introduced to other parts of the world, though it can become invasive in some areas.
