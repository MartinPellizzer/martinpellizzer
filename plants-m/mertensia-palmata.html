Mertensia palmata

Mertensia palmata, commonly known as the blue cohosh, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has palmately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in rich, well-drained soils.

Mertensia palmata is valued for its ornamental qualities and has a long history of use in traditional herbal medicine.
