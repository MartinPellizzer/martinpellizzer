Myrcia regnelliana var. robustior

Myrcia regnelliana var. robustior, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
