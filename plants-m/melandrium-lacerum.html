Melandrium lacerum

Melandrium lacerum, commonly known as the fringed catchfly, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
