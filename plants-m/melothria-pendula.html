Melothria pendula

Melothria pendula, commonly known as the hanging cucumber or pendulous melon, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It produces small, edible fruits that are typically green and resemble miniature cucumbers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is often used in local cuisines for its crisp texture and mild flavor.

Melothria pendula thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its climbing growth habit.
