Matricaria obliqua

Matricaria obliqua, commonly known as the meadow chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its yellow flower heads surrounded by white ray florets. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, while the flowers bloom in late summer.

Matricaria obliqua is often used in herbal medicine for its mild sedative and anti-inflammatory properties.
