Mandevilla jamesonii

Mandevilla jamesonii, commonly known as the flame vine, is a tropical perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from orange to red, often with a yellow throat. The plant produces opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its showy blooms.

Mandevilla jamesonii is also valued for its ability to climb or trail, making it suitable for trellises, hanging baskets, or as a ground cover.
