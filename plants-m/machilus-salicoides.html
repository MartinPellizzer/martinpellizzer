Machilus salicoides

Machilus salicoides, commonly known as the Chinese-laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries such as China, Vietnam, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, ovate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue-black berries.

Machilus salicoides is typically found in moist, shaded forest environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
