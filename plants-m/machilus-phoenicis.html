Machilus phoenicis

Machilus phoenicis, commonly known as the Chinese mahogany or golden thread tree, is a small evergreen tree native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The tree produces small, fleshy berries that are dark purple when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The wood is valued for its fine grain and resistance to decay, making it useful in traditional craftsmanship.
