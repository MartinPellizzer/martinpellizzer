Medinilla involucrata

Medinilla involucrata, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or Christmas bell, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces fleshy, orange-red berries that are edible and have a tangy, citrus-like flavor. Medinilla involucrata thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
