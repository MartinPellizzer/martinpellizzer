Melaleuca recurva

Melaleuca recurva, commonly known as the narrow-leaf paperbark, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in coastal areas and heathlands. The plant is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that gives it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a silvery underside.

Melaleuca recurva produces small, white or cream-colored flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds.
