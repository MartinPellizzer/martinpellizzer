Machilus ichangensis var. leiophyllus

Machilus ichangensis var. leiophyllus, commonly known as the smooth-leaved chinquapin, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to southern China and is characterized by its glossy, narrow leaves that are leathery and smooth to the touch. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark purple berries. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as mountain forests.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
