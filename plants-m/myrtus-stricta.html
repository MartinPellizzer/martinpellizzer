Myrtus stricta

Myrtus stricta, commonly known as dwarf myrtle, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, dark green leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces tiny white flowers in spring, which are followed by small, dark berries. Myrtus stricta thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its dense growth habit and ornamental value contribute to its appeal in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
