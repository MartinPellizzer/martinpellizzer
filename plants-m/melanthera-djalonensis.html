Melanthera djalonensis

Melanthera djalonensis, commonly known as the West African ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the savannas of West Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats with full sun exposure.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally in local remedies for various ailments.
