Malva tuberculata

Malva tuberculata, commonly known as the woolly mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Malva tuberculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
