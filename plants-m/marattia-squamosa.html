Marattia squamosa

Marattia squamosa, commonly known as the scalycushion fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Marattiaceae.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, compound fronds that are typically bipinnate or tripinnate. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, Sri Lanka, and Indonesia. Its fronds are often covered with small, scale-like structures, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Marattia squamosa thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
