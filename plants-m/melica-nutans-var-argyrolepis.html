Melica nutans var. argyrolepis

Melica nutans var. argyrolepis, commonly known as silverleaf nodding grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom, giving it its common name. The inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets that droop downward, a feature typical of the genus Melica. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with three stamens and a single pistil, typical of the Poaceae family.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along streambanks.
