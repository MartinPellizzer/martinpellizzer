Melocactus communis var. lanifer

Melocactus communis var. lanifer, commonly known as the velvet melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stem that is covered in a dense, velvety layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, adding a striking contrast to its textured appearance. It is well adapted to dry conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
