Melilotus angulatus

Melilotus angulatus, commonly known as angular melilot, is an annual legume native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes. The plant produces trifoliate leaves and pod-like fruits that contain several small, elliptical seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Due to its nitrogen-fixing ability, it is sometimes used in soil enrichment and as a forage crop.
