Memecylon edule var. thwaitesii

Memecylon edule var. thwaitesii, commonly known as the edible memecylon, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in axillary or terminal inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe, which is edible and used in traditional cuisines.
