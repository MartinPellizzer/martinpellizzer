Malus × adstringens

Malus × adstringens, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces small, sweet, and juicy fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for both its fruit and aesthetic appeal.

The plant is also notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
