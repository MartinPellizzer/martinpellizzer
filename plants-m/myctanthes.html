Myctanthes

Myctanthes, commonly known as the moonflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

These plants are characterized by their large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night and close during the day. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture. Myctanthes species are native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

They are often cultivated for their ornamental value and unique nocturnal blooming habit.
