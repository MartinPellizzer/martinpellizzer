Monochilus plantagineus

Monochilus plantagineus, commonly known as the silverleaf saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features a rosette of silvery-gray, spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with rocky or gravelly soil conditions.
