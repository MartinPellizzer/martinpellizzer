Moniera radicata

Moniera radicata, commonly known as the root vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its fibrous root system and long, trailing stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and can grow quite large, providing a dense canopy.

Moniera radicata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
