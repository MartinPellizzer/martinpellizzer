Mentha nederheimensis

Mentha nederheimensis, commonly known as the German mint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, giving it a refreshing menthol scent.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
