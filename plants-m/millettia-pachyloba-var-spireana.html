Millettia pachyloba var. spireana

Millettia pachyloba var. spireana, commonly known as the Spirea Millettia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, winged fruits called samaras, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
