Mahernia salviifolia

Mahernia salviifolia, commonly known as the leafy mahernia, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and flowering habit.
