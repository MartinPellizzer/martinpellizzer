Meliocarpus cilicicus

Meliocarpus cilicicus, commonly known as the Cilician tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant produces yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, seed-like structures. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and woodland areas.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
