Miconia guianensis var. ovalis

Miconia guianensis var. ovalis, commonly known as the Guianan miconia, is a species of flowering plant in the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Venezuela. The plant is characterized by its large, ovate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It produces clusters of small, showy flowers that are typically pink or white in color.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous tiny seeds, facilitating its spread in the wild.
