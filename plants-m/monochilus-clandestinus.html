Monochilus clandestinus

Monochilus clandestinus, commonly known as the secret-leaf orchid, is a rare and elusive orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique leaf structure, which features a single, elongated leaf that folds inward, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which helps it blend into its forest habitat. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or in the undergrowth of dense rainforests.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Monochilus clandestinus is considered a challenging plant to cultivate outside its natural range.
