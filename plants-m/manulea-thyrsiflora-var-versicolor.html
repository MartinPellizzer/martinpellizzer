Manulea thyrsiflora var. versicolor

Manulea thyrsiflora var. versicolor, commonly known as the variable thyrse-flowered manulea, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant features slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers exhibit a range of colors, from white to pink and purple, which contributes to its common name.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
