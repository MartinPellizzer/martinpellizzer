Musa balbisiana var. brachycarpa

Musa balbisiana var. brachycarpa, commonly known as the dwarf banana plant, is a species native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with shorter stems and smaller leaves compared to other banana varieties. The plant produces clusters of small, ornamental bananas that are typically not edible but are valued for their aesthetic appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
