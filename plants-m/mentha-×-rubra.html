Mentha × rubra

Mentha × rubra, commonly known as red mint, is a hybrid mint species resulting from the cross between spearmint (Mentha spicata) and watermint (Mentha aquatica).

It is characterized by its reddish-purple flowers and aromatic, slightly spicy leaves. The plant is a perennial herb with square stems and opposite leaves that are usually gray-green in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Mentha × rubra is often used in culinary applications and herbal remedies due to its distinctive flavor and medicinal properties.
