Merkusia humifusa

Merkusia humifusa, commonly known as the groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals, blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens.

Its botanical features include hairy stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in arid conditions.
