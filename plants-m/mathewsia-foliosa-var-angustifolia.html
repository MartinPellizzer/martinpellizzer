Mathewsia foliosa var. angustifolia

Mathewsia foliosa var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mathewsia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
