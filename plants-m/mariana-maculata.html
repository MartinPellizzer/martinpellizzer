Mariana maculata

Mariana maculata, commonly known as the spotted hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has palmate leaves with a distinctive spotted or mottled pattern on the underside. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Mariana maculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
