Mimulus veronicifolius

Mimulus veronicifolius, commonly known as the velvetleaf monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, hairy leaves and showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Mimulus veronicifolius is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a valuable plant for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
