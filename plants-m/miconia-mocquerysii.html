Miconia mocquerysii

Miconia mocquerysii, commonly known as the red miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine, though the plant is considered invasive in some regions.
