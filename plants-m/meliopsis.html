Meliopsis

Meliopsis, commonly known as the flame tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant is characterized by its showy, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. Meliopsis species are typically deciduous trees or shrubs, reaching heights of several meters.

They have compound leaves and produce pods that contain seeds, contributing to their role in ecological and agricultural systems.
