Malus sieversii var. dulcis

Malus sieversii var. dulcis, commonly known as the wild apple, is a species native to Central Asia.

It is considered the ancestral form of the domesticated apple (Malus domestica). This plant is characterized by its small, tart apples and is often found in mountainous regions. The tree has simple, alternate leaves and produces flowers that range from white to pink.

It plays a significant role in the genetic diversity of cultivated apples.
