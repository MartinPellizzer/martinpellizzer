Mucuna poggei

Mucuna poggei, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with small, heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces pods containing flat, bean-like seeds that are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein. Its flowers are typically purple or violet, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Mucuna poggei is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural practices.
