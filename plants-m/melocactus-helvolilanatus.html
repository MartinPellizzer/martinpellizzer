Melocactus helvolilanatus

Melocactus helvolilanatus, commonly known as the spiral melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of ribs, which gives it a unique and striking appearance. The plant produces a spiny, segmented stem that can grow up to several feet in height. Its flowers, which are typically yellow or orange, bloom during the warmer months.

This cactus is well adapted to dry environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stem.
