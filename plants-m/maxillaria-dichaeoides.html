Maxillaria dichaeoides

Maxillaria dichaeoides, commonly known as the orchid of the Andes, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America.

It features a pseudobulb with a single leaf and produces pendulous inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers. The flowers are typically white or pale pink with dark purple markings, and they bloom during the dry season. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

It is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
