Mentha murettii

Mentha murettii, commonly known as the Sicilian mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and produces small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many other aromatic herbs such as lavender and basil. Mentha murettii is often used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and calming properties.

Its essential oils are also valued in the fragrance and food industries.
