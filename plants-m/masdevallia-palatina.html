Masdevallia palatina

Masdevallia palatina, commonly known as the Palatina Masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, often colorful flower structure with a distinctive labellum. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that help it conserve moisture.

Masdevallia palatina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
