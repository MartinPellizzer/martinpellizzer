Millettia madagascariensis

Millettia madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascan millettia, is a tropical tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive floral structures. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated pods containing seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid forest environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.
