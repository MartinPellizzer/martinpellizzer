Morisonia nemorosa

Morisonia nemorosa, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a striking plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces delicate, white, bell-shaped flowers with a distinctive purple-tinged throat. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in woodlands and along streambanks. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Morisonia nemorosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ethereal appearance.
