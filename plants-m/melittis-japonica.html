Melittis japonica

Melittis japonica, commonly known as Japanese honeycomb plant, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, honey-scented flowers that resemble small honeycombs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in a fine pubescence.

Melittis japonica is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
