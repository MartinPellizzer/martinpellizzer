Malvaviscus cinereus

Malvaviscus cinereus, commonly known as the gray mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow center. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

Malvaviscus cinereus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
