Mentha aquatica var. subsativa

Mentha aquatica var. subsativa, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herb native to moist habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are often slightly aromatic. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its aromatic leaves are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

It thrives in wet soils near streams, ponds, and marshes.
