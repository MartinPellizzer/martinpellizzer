Millettia irvinei

Millettia irvinei, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Millettia irvinei is valued for its timber and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
