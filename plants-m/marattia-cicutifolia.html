Marattia cicutifolia

Marattia cicutifolia, commonly known as the fan-leaf fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Marattiaceae.

It is characterized by its large, spreading fronds that can reach lengths of up to 2 meters. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its bipinnate leaves that are often divided into narrow, finger-like segments. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Marattia cicutifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
