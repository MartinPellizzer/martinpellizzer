Mascagnia nicaraguensis var. typica

Mascagnia nicaraguensis var. typica, commonly known as the Nicaragua trumpetflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular, orange-red flowers that resemble trumpets. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth surface with a slightly hairy underside.

The flowers bloom in summer and are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds, making it an important species in local ecosystems.
