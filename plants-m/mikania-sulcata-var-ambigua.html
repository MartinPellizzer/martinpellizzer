Mikania sulcata var. ambigua

Mikania sulcata var. ambigua, commonly known as the roughleaf mile-a-minute vine, is a fast-growing perennial vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped, serrated leaves that are rough to the touch. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forests edges.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in certain regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
