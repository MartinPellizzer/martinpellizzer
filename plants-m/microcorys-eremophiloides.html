Microcorys eremophiloides

Microcorys eremophiloides, commonly known as the desert broom.

It is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating drought tolerance.

Its root system is adapted to efficiently absorb and store water, making it well-suited for harsh desert conditions.
