Myrcia trichantha

Myrcia trichantha, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in coastal areas.

Myrcia trichantha is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
