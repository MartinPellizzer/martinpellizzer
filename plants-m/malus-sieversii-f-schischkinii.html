Malus sieversii f. schischkinii

Malus sieversii f. schischkinii, commonly known as the Schischkin's apple, is a botanical variant of the wild apple species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, featuring broad, serrated leaves that are often a deep green. The plant produces small, ornamental flowers in the spring, which are typically white or pink in color. Its fruit, while not as commonly cultivated as other apple varieties, is valued for its unique flavor and potential genetic contributions to modern apple breeding.

This subspecies is of particular interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its role in the genetic diversity of domesticated apples.
