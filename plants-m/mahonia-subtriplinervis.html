Mahonia subtriplinervis

Mahonia subtriplinervis, commonly known as the three-veined mahonia, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with three main veins, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by blue-black berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its foliage and seasonal interest, making it a popular plant among horticulturists.
