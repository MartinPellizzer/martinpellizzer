Myristica subcordata var. morindiifolia

Myristica subcordata var. morindiifolia, commonly known as the Morinda-like Myristica, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited commercial use in perfumery and herbal remedies.
