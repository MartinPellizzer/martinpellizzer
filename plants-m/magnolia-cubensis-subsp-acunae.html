Magnolia cubensis subsp. acunae

Magnolia cubensis subsp.

acunae, commonly known as the Cuban magnolia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Cuba. It is characterized by its large, showy white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. Its bark is smooth and grayish in color, and it typically grows in moist, well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
