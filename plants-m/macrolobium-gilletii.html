Macrolobium gilletii

Macrolobium gilletii, commonly known as the African marula tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, palmately lobed leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The tree produces small, yellow flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain edible seeds. Its fruit is also used to make a popular alcoholic beverage called "umqombothi.".

The plant is valued for its ecological and economic importance in its native habitat.
