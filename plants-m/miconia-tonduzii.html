Miconia tonduzii

Miconia tonduzii, commonly known as the Brazilian starwood, is a species of flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are followed by distinctive, three-lobed fruits.

Miconia tonduzii is notable for its ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various native wildlife species.
