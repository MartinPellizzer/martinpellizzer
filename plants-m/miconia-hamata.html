Miconia hamata

Miconia hamata, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Miconia hamata is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the study of tropical flora.
