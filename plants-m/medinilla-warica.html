Medinilla warica

Medinilla warica, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

Medinilla warica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a potted plant.
