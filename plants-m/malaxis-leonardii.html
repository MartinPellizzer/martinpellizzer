Malaxis leonardii

Malaxis leonardii, commonly known as the Leonard's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swampy areas. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces a single spike of flowers during the spring or early summer.

Malaxis leonardii is a rare species, often found in specific microhabitats, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
