Miconia polyandra

Miconia polyandra, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Miconia polyandra is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a potential threat to native ecosystems.
