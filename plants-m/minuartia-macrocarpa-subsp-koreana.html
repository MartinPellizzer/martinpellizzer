Minuartia macrocarpa subsp. koreana

Minuartia macrocarpa subsp.

koreana, commonly known as the Korean sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Korea. It is characterized by its compact rosette of narrow, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white hairs.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
