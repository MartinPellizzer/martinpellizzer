Miconia piperifolia

Miconia piperifolia, commonly known as the pepperleaf miconia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Melastomataceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that are followed by bright red berries.

Miconia piperifolia is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
