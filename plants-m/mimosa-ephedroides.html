Mimosa ephedroides

Mimosa ephedroides, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different environments. Its seeds are encased in hairy pods, and the plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature.

Mimosa ephedroides also has historical uses in traditional medicine.
