Medinilla balls-headleyi

Medinilla balls-headleyi, commonly known as the velvet tangerine.

This tropical evergreen shrub is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its dense, rounded clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

Medinilla balls-headleyi is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its ornate appearance and ability to thrive in humid, warm climates.
