Morinda tenuiflora

Morinda tenuiflora, commonly known as the Chinese wineberry or red sanders, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its small, fleshy berries that turn red when mature. The plant has hairy stems and oval-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in open woodlands or along forest edges.

The berries are edible and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
