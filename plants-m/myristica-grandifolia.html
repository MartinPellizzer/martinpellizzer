Myristica grandifolia

Myristica grandifolia, commonly known as the nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits containing the aromatic seeds used for nutmeg and mace.

Its botanical significance lies in its essential oils and spices, which have been valued for centuries in culinary and medicinal applications.
