Monstera poeppigii

Monstera poeppigii, commonly known as the Split-leaf Philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, deeply lobed leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant has a robust stem that can climb using aerial roots, often reaching significant heights in its natural habitat. Its leaves are typically a deep green with a waxy texture, and they exhibit a distinctive split pattern as they mature.

Monstera poeppigii thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.
