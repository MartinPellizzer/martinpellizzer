Mitracarpus buiquensis

Mitracarpus buiquensis, commonly known as the Biqui shrub, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean island of Hispaniola.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in racemose inflorescences. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with a rough texture and a distinctive appearance.

Mitracarpus buiquensis is adapted to grow in coastal and dry forest habitats, exhibiting resilience to saline and arid conditions.
