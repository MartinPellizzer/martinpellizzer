Morisonia incana

Morisonia incana, commonly known as the silverleaf morisonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in woodland areas.

Morisonia incana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in native plant gardens.
