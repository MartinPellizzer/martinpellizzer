Melicope platynema

Melicope platynema, commonly known as the Hawaiian sandalwood, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a small evergreen tree that typically grows to a height of about 6 meters. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark grey bark and small, elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow and are followed by small, round fruits.

The tree is valued for its aromatic wood, which has been traditionally used in cultural and medicinal practices.
