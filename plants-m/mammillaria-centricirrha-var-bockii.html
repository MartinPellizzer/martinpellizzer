Mammillaria centricirrha var. bockii

Mammillaria centricirrha var. bockii, commonly known as the Bock's milk barrel cactus, is a compact, globular cactus native to Mexico.

It features a short, cylindrical stem with prominent, white, woolly spines and a dense covering of pale green, ribbed areoles. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated as a decorative succulent.
