Magnolia fistulosa

Magnolia fistulosa, commonly known as the red-flowered magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy red or pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves appear. The tree has smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its flowers are fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
