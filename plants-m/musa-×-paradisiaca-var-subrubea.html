Musa × paradisiaca var. subrubea

Musa × paradisiaca var. subrubea, commonly known as the red banana, is a cultivar of the banana plant characterized by its distinctive reddish-purple peel.

It is a member of the Musaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, producing large, broad leaves and hanging clusters of fruit. Its fruit is usually smaller and sweeter than common bananas, with a shorter shelf life.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and unique flavor profile.
