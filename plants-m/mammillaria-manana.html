Mammillaria manana

Mammillaria manana, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, rounded stems and vibrant flowers that bloom in the winter months. The plant has a dense, compact growth habit with areoles that bear both spines and flowers. Its leaves are typically reduced to small, rounded structures, and it thrives in well-drained soil with bright, indirect light.

Mammillaria manana is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
