Montia tenella

Montia tenella, commonly known as the silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and forest floors.

Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize soil, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects.
