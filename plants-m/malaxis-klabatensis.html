Malaxis klabatensis

Malaxis klabatensis, commonly known as the Klabat orchid, is a rare and enigmatic member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of Indonesia, specifically the island of Sulawesi. The plant is characterized by its delicate, pendulous inflorescences bearing small, greenish-yellow flowers with intricate patterns. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is typically found in cloud forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a threatened species.
