Myrtus bracteolaris

Myrtus bracteolaris, commonly known as the rock myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a slight waxy coating. Its flowers are tiny, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are usually pale blue or white in color.

The fruit is a small, dark blue berry that contains several seeds, and the plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh environments.
