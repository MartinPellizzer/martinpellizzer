Millettia chapelieri

Millettia chapelieri, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The tree produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often used in reforestation projects due to its ability to thrive in poor soils and its rapid growth rate.

Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.
