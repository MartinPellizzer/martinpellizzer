Monadenium stapelioides f. congestum

Monadenium stapelioides f. congestum, commonly known as the compact form of the African milk bush, is a unique succulent characterized by its dense, compact growth habit.

It features fleshy, ribbed stems that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, typically in summer. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted for storing water, and it thrives in well-draining soil with minimal water.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
