Millettia hedraeantha

Millettia hedraeantha, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its showy blooms. Its leaves are trifoliate, and the pods that develop after flowering are typically hairy and contain several seeds.

The tree is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
