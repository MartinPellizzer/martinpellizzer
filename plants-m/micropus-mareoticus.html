Micropus mareoticus

Micropus mareoticus, commonly known as the Nile spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Nile Delta region of Egypt.

It is a herbaceous perennial with distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a milky sap that contains toxic compounds, which can cause skin irritation. It thrives in wet, saline soils and is often found near water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of wetland flora.
