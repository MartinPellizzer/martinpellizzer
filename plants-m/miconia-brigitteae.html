Miconia brigitteae

Miconia brigitteae, commonly known as Brigitte's miconia, is a tropical shrub native to the humid lowland forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that are often heart-shaped. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom during the wet season. Its fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that disperses seeds through wind and animal activity.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
