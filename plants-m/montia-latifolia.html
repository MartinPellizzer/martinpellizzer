Montia latifolia

Montia latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf montia, is a small flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the family Montiaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Montia latifolia is often found growing in association with other moisture-loving plants in its native habitat.
