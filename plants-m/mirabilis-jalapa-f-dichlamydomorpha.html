Mirabilis jalapa f. dichlamydomorpha

Mirabilis jalapa f. dichlamydomorpha, commonly known as the four o'clock flower with double petals, is a cultivar of the common four o'clock plant.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring two distinct sets of petals—one outer and one inner—creating a double bloom effect. The plant is native to the Americas and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the late afternoon and close by dawn.

The foliage is typically broad and heart-shaped, contributing to its overall aesthetic appeal.
