Malpighia canescens

Malpighia canescens, commonly known as the hairy physalis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple to red, lantern-shaped fruit that encloses the seeds. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are generally hairy and ovate, with a slightly rough texture.

Malpighia canescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
