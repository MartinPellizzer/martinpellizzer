Mastixia trichotoma var. benculuana

Mastixia trichotoma var. benculuana, commonly known as the Benculuan mastix tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a resinous sap, which is often used in traditional medicine and for making incense. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
