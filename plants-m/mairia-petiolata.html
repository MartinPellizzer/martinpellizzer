Mairia petiolata

Mairia petiolata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Brazilian velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous seeds.

Mairia petiolata is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
