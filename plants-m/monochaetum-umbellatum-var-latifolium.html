Monochaetum umbellatum var. latifolium

Monochaetum umbellatum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf star sedge, is a striking plant native to the rainforests of New South Wales, Australia.

It is a perennial herb with a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces an umbellate inflorescence, which consists of clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or clay soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
