Magnolia sororum subsp. oligocarpa

Magnolia sororum subsp.

oligocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf magnolia, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southeastern China. It is characterized by its lanceolate, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats within temperate forests. Its flowers are usually pink to white in color and are borne on short pedicels.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller, more compact flowers and narrower leaves compared to other magnolia species.
