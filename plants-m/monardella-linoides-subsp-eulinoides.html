Monardella linoides subsp. eulinoides

Monardella linoides subsp.

eulinoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf monardella, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces showy pink to purple tubular flowers. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss.

This subspecies is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
