Millettia multiflora

Millettia multiflora, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with multiple pairs of oval-shaped leaflets and produces fragrant, pendulous clusters of violet-blue flowers in the spring. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and can grow quite long, often reaching several meters in length. Its flowers are followed by long, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds.

Millettia multiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a shade plant.
