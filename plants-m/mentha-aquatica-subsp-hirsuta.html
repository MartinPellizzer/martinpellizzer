Mentha aquatica subsp. hirsuta

Mentha aquatica subsp.

hirsuta, commonly known as hairy water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist or aquatic environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows.

The plant has square stems, opposite leaves that are usually hairy, and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in whorls. It is characterized by its aromatic, mint-like scent and its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils.

This subspecies is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
