Malveopsis subtriflora

Malveopsis subtriflora, commonly known as the three-flowered mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the plant is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance.
