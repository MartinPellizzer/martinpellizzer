Magnolia decidua

Magnolia decidua, commonly known as the southern magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The tree has simple, ovate leaves that turn yellow before falling in autumn. Its bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in landscaping for its fragrant blossoms and lush foliage.
