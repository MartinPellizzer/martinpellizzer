Myxopyrum smilacifolium var. ilicifolium

Myxopyrum smilacifolium var. ilicifolium, commonly known as the sweet flag or sweet reed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It features long, narrow, grass-like leaves that are often described as having a sweet, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in the understory of wetland forests.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, contributing to the ecological structure of its habitat.
