Mimosa viscosa f. glabrifolia

Mimosa viscosa f. glabrifolia, commonly known as the smooth mimosa, is a variant of the common mimosa plant.

It is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring and summer. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, feather-like leaflets.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils and full sunlight.
