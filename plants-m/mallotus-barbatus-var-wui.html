Mallotus barbatus var. wui

Mallotus barbatus var. wui, commonly known as the Guangxi holly or Guangxi mallotus, is a species native to southern China, particularly Guangxi Province.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree characterized by its elliptical, glossy dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, spherical drupes that turn red when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with a subtropical climate.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
