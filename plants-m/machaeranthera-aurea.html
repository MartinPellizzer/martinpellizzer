Machaeranthera aurea

Machaeranthera aurea, commonly known as the golden tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces yellow flowers with prominent, strap-like petals and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It thrives in full sun and is adapted to arid environments, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
