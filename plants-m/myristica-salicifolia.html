Myristica salicifolia

Myristica salicifolia, commonly known as the white sand myristica, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, dark reddish-brown fruits that contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
