Maxillaria oxysepala

Maxillaria oxysepala, commonly known as the white-lipped orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with prominent red or purple lip petals. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or rocks. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it is prized for its ornamental value in horticulture.

The species is relatively easy to cultivate, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
