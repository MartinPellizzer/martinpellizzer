Melocactus giganteus

Melocactus giganteus, commonly known as the giant melon cactus, is a striking member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem that can grow up to 10 meters in height, often with a distinctive green or reddish coloration. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom at the top of the stem, typically during the warmer months. Its spines are short and cylindrical, providing protection while allowing for some photosynthesis.

Melocactus giganteus is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stem to survive in dry conditions.
