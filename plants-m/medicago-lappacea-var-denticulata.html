Medicago lappacea var. denticulata

Medicago lappacea var. denticulata, commonly known as the denticulate goat's rue, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and is often found in dry, rocky soils. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic bacteria.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
