Mammillaria polythele subsp. obconella

Mammillaria polythele subsp.

obconella, commonly known as the Mexican night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to Mexico. It is characterized by its spherical shape and dense covering of white, woolly spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the night, which open in response to cooler temperatures. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a houseplant.
