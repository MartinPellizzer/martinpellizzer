Myrtoleucodendron decussatum

Myrtoleucodendron decussatum, commonly known as the mountain myrtle, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, which bloom in clusters. Its botanical features include a woody stem and opposite leaf arrangement, which are typical of the Myrtaceae family.

It is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.
