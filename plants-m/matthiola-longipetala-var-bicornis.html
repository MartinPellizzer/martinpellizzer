Matthiola longipetala var. bicornis

Matthiola longipetala var. bicornis, commonly known as the two-horned stock, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial that produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves and has a compact growth habit. Its flowers are typically borne in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

The variety bicornis is distinguished by its distinctive two-horned calyx lobes, which give it its common name.
