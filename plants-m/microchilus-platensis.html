Microchilus platensis

Microchilus platensis, commonly known as the Pampas grass, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical flower spikes that can reach up to 3 meters in height. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the Pampas ecosystem.
