Macubea

Macubea, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom on long, above-water stalks. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the surface of still water. Its flowers are typically pink or white, with a distinctive, layered structure.

Macubea is revered in many cultures for its symbolic and medicinal properties.
