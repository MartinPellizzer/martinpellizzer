Morus alba f. pendula

Morus alba f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping mulberry, is a unique cultivar of the white mulberry tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cascading branches that hang in long, drooping tendrils, giving it a striking, ethereal appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as mulberries, which are typically white or pink in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal.

The leaves are broad and heart-shaped, providing dense foliage that supports a variety of wildlife.
