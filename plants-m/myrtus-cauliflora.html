Myrtus cauliflora

Myrtus cauliflora, commonly known as the Christmas myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but often used in traditional remedies. Myrtus cauliflora thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
