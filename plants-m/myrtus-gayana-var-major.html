Myrtus gayana var. major

Myrtus gayana var. major, commonly known as the large myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, lance-shaped leaves and small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

The leaves and berries contain essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.
