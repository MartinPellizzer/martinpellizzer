Mussaenda fissibractea

Mussaenda fissibractea, commonly known as the flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually hairy leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Mussaenda fissibractea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in landscaping and garden design.
