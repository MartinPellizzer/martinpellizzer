Myrrhis alpina

Myrrhis alpina, commonly known as alpine sweetflag, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Myrrhis alpina is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
