Melocactus bahiensis f. inconcinnus

Melocactus bahiensis f. inconcinnus, commonly known as the narrow-ridged melocactus, is a cactus species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are ribbed and often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom during the dry season, typically in the late afternoon. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
