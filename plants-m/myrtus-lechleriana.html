Myrtus lechleriana

Myrtus lechleriana, commonly known as the Cape myrtle, is a flowering shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Myrtus lechleriana is also valued for its fragrant foliage and ornamental appeal.
