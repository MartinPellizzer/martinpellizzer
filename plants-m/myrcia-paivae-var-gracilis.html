Myrcia paivae var. gracilis

Myrcia paivae var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender myrcia, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in undergrowth layers of tropical forests.

Myrcia paivae var. gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
