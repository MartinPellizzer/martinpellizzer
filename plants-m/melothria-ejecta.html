Melothria ejecta

Melothria ejecta, commonly known as the little morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and small, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue or purple. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically green and turn red when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Melothria ejecta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible berries.
