Myrtus reinwardtiana

Myrtus reinwardtiana, commonly known as the African myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces tiny, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Myrtus reinwardtiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal remedies.
