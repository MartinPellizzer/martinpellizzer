Malaxis purpureonervosa

Malaxis purpureonervosa, commonly known as the purple-veined Malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a slender, leafless stem that bears a single, showy flower with a distinctive purple-veined lip. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insect species, showcasing a unique ecological relationship.

The orchid's morphology reflects adaptations to its tropical habitat, with specialized structures for water retention and nutrient absorption.
