Momordica parvifolia

Momordica parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved bitter gourd, is a vine species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and watermelons. The plant produces small, bitter fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the stems are hairy with tendrils for climbing.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
