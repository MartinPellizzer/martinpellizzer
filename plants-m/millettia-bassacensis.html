Millettia bassacensis

Millettia bassacensis, commonly known as the Borneo tamarind tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The tree produces long, flat pods containing edible seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Its ecological importance includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
