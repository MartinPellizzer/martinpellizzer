Medicago striata

Medicago striata, commonly known as the narrowleaf medick, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features trifoliate leaves with three oval to ovate leaflets, and small, yellow flowers arranged in umbels. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and rocky areas.

Medicago striata is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
