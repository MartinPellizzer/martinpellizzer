Myrciaria una

Myrciaria una, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá-baiano, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
