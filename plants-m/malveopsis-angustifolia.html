Malveopsis angustifolia

Malveopsis angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting addition to native plant gardens.
