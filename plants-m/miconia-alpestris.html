Miconia alpestris

Miconia alpestris, commonly known as the alpine miconia, is a species of flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in cloud forests and alpine meadows.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
