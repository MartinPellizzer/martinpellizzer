Melastoma cymosum

Melastoma cymosum, commonly known as the clustered melastoma, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its dense, often evergreen foliage. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences with vibrant pink or red flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Melastoma cymosum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
