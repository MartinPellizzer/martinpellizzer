Myrtus cordata

Myrtus cordata, commonly known as the Armenian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features narrow, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and slightly glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in early spring, which are followed by dark blue, edible berries. Myrtus cordata is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.
