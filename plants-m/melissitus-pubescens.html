Melissitus pubescens

Melissitus pubescens, commonly known as the hairy melissitus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Melissitaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to certain regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. It is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks, preferring full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
