Monorchis mackinonii

Monorchis mackinonii, commonly known as the Mackinon's orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricately patterned flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat destruction and over-collection, Monorchis mackinonii is considered endangered and requires protection to ensure its survival.
