Magnolia macrophylla

Magnolia macrophylla, commonly known as the saucer magnolia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, saucer-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has broad, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The flowers are typically pink or white, adding a striking visual element to landscapes in the spring.
