Monardella lanceolata var. sanguinea

Monardella lanceolata var. sanguinea, commonly known as the scarlet monardella, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular red flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is notable for its vibrant color and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
