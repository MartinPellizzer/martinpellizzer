Malaxis hahajimensis

Malaxis hahajimensis, commonly known as the Hahajima orchid, is a rare epiphytic orchid native to the Japanese island of Hahajima.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has short, fleshy roots and grows on tree branches or rocks, relying on atmospheric moisture for survival. It is listed as endangered due to habitat destruction and limited distribution.

Its unique botanical features make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
