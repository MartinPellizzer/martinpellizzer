Mucuna poggei var. pesa

Mucuna poggei var. pesa, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety pubescence covering the stems and leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain numerous seeds, which are used both as a food source and in traditional medicine. It is also notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, enriching the soil.

The plant's rapid growth and adaptability make it an important species in agroforestry and ecological restoration projects.
