Medicago laciniata var. integriuscula

Medicago laciniata var. integriuscula, commonly known as the narrow-leafed clover, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features trifoliate leaves with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets and small, pink to white flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant has a deep taproot system, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils and withstand drought conditions. It is often used in forage and soil stabilization due to its hardy nature and nitrogen-fixing ability.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
