Melica subenervis

Melica subenervis, commonly known as narrow-leaf brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and stream banks. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a prominent midrib. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are small, clustered flower clusters.

Melica subenervis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
