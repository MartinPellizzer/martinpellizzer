Maxillaria gracilis var. intermedia

Maxillaria gracilis var. intermedia, commonly known as the Intermediate Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces fragrant, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive white throat. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its leaves are dark green, strap-shaped, and arranged in a rosette pattern.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
