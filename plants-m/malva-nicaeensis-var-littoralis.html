Malva nicaeensis var. littoralis

Malva nicaeensis var. littoralis, commonly known as the coastal mallows, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves and showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. This variant thrives in coastal and saline environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant is often found in dunes and rocky shores, where it plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.

Its fibrous roots and succulent stems help it retain moisture in arid conditions.
