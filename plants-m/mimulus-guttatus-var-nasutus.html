Mimulus guttatus var. nasutus

Mimulus guttatus var. nasutus, commonly known as the long-beaked monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with five petals and a distinctive, elongated lower lip. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and woodland edges. It is a herbaceous annual that produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.

This variant is often found in the Pacific Northwest and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
