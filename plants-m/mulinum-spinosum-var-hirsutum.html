Mulinum spinosum var. hirsutum

Mulinum spinosum var. hirsutum, commonly known as the hirsute thimbleweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny stems and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant produces small, dry fruits that contain several seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
