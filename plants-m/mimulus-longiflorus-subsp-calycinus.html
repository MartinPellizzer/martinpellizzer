Mimulus longiflorus subsp. calycinus

Mimulus longiflorus subsp.

calycinus, commonly known as the California monkeyflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features showy, orange to yellow tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and in coastal areas. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

The subspecies is distinguished by its longer, more elongated petals compared to other Mimulus species.
