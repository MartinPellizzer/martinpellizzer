Millettia macrophylla

Millettia macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved millettia, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, followed by woody pods containing flat, seed-like structures. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
