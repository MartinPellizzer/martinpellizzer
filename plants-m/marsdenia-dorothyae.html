Marsdenia dorothyae

Marsdenia dorothyae, commonly known as Dorothy's Marsdenia, is a flowering plant species native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming during the rainy season.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and role in the biodiversity of its native habitat.
