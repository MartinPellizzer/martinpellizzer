Mareyopsis oligogyna

Mareyopsis oligogyna, commonly known as the slender sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spherical fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to saline environments.

Mareyopsis oligogyna is often found in dune ecosystems, where it plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
