Myristica insipida

Myristica insipida, commonly known as the nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen belonging to the Myristicaceae family.

It is native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The tree produces a unique fruit that contains the aromatic seeds used to make nutmeg and mace. Its leaves are large, elliptical, and have a smooth texture with a strong, spicy scent.

The plant is valued not only for its commercial spices but also for its ecological role in tropical ecosystems.
