Malva alcea subsp. fastigiata

Malva alcea subsp.

fastigiata, commonly known as the upright mallow, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and large, showy flowers that range from pink to purple.

The leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are sturdy and often slightly hairy. This subspecies thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for garden borders or as a backdrop plant.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
