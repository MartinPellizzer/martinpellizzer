Mucuna albertisii

Mucuna albertisii, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a tropical legume belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety pubescence covering its stems and leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several elliptical, flat seeds. Native to regions of Africa and Asia, it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Mucuna albertisii is also noted for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
