Myrcia recurvata var. parvifolia

Myrcia recurvata var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved myrcia, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It features elliptical to ovate leaves that are typically small and glossy, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
