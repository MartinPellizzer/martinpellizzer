Melaleuca orbifolia

Melaleuca orbifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf paperbark, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals layers of white to brownish inner bark. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are broad, glossy, and often covered in a fine, powdery bloom.

Melaleuca orbifolia is typically found in coastal or wetland areas, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
