Mapania sylvatica

Mapania sylvatica, commonly known as the European hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Betulaceae and is characterized by its dense, almost cylindrical crown and smooth, dark bark. The leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds called samaras.
