Myristica cimicifera var. acutifolia

Myristica cimicifera var. acutifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved nutmeg, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, red or yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, oval fruits that contain aromatic seeds used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its botanical structure includes a smooth, dark-barked trunk and clusters of leathery, elliptical leaves.

The species is valued for its essential oils and has been historically used in traditional medicine.
