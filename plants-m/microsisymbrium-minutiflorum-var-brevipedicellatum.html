Microsisymbrium minutiflorum var. brevipedicellatum

Microsisymbrium minutiflorum var. brevipedicellatum, commonly known as the dwarf-flowered microsisymbrium, is a small, herbaceous plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, clustered inflorescences with tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to conserve water in its harsh environment. The plant's small stature and specialized morphology make it well-suited for survival in nutrient-poor soils.

It is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and limited distribution.
