Monarda albiflora

Monarda albiflora, commonly known as white bergamot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Monarda albiflora is also valued for its medicinal properties, with historical use in treating ailments like colds and fevers.
