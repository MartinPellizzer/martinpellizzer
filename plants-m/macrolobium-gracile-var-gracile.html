Macrolobium gracile var. gracile

Macrolobium gracile var. gracile, commonly known as the slender leaf macrolobium, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellowish-white blooms that are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

This plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
