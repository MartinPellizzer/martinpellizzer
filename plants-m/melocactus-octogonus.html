Melocactus octogonus

Melocactus octogonus, commonly known as the eight-spined melon cactus, is a species native to the dry regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, columnar stem and a distinctive crown of spines, typically numbering eight, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise spiky appearance. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.

The plant is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
