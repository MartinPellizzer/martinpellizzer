Mikania burchellii

Mikania burchellii, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a vigorous climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to spread over other vegetation, often forming dense mats. It is commonly found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in open woodlands.

Mikania burchellii is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.
