Micromeria sphaerophylla

Micromeria sphaerophylla, commonly known as the globeflower, is a small shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact, spherical growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually small, grayish-green, and covered in fine hairs.

The flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer, are pink to red in color and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
