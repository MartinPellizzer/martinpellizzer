Mischophloeus paniculatus

Mischophloeus paniculatus, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and its distinctive, showy panicles of bright red or orange flowers that appear in spring. The plant has compound leaves that are opposite and can reach up to 30 cm in length. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular ornamental species.

The tree is also valued for its fast growth and ability to provide shade in tropical and subtropical climates.
