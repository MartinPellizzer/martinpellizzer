Masdevallia nicaraguae

Masdevallia nicaraguae, commonly known as the Nicaragua orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, often colorful flowers that feature intricate patterns and a unique structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or rocks in the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers are usually short-lived but highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
