Microstegium okamotoi

Microstegium okamotoi, commonly known as the Japanese woodland grass, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface and a prominent midrib.

Microstegium okamotoi is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides.
