Mertensia micrantha

Mertensia micrantha, commonly known as bluebells, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Mertensia micrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
