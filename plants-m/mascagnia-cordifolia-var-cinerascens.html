Mascagnia cordifolia var. cinerascens

Mascagnia cordifolia var. cinerascens, commonly known as the cinerascens variant of the heartleaf mascagnia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded or semi-shaded environments. Its foliage has a distinctive grayish-blue hue, giving it a unique visual appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
