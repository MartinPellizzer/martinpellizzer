Myristica womersleyi

Myristica womersleyi, commonly known as the Womersley's nutmeg, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody fruits that contain a valuable seed kernel. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has glossy, elliptical leaves with a waxy texture. Its fruit, which resembles a small apple, contains a single seed surrounded by a fragrant, oily aril.

This species is valued for its unique botanical features and potential economic importance in traditional medicine and perfumery.
