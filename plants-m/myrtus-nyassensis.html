Myrtus nyassensis

Myrtus nyassensis, commonly known as African myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of East Africa.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by dark blue, edible berries. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and have a slight aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky areas.

Myrtus nyassensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
