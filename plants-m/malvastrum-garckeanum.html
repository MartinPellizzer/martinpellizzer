Malvastrum garckeanum

Malvastrum garckeanum, commonly known as the garcke's mallow, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft, fuzzy texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral displays.
