Macaranga novoguineensis

Macaranga novoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea macaranga, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub characterized by its alternate, simple, and often elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by distinctive three-lobed capsules that contain numerous seeds. It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in reforestation efforts due to its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
