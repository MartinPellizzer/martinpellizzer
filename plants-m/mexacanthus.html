Mexacanthus

Mexacanthus, commonly known as the Mexican bristlecone pine, is a member of the Pinaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, needle-like leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation across rugged terrain. Mexacanthus is well adapted to arid and high-altitude environments, showcasing resilience to harsh climatic conditions.

Its slow growth rate and longevity make it an important species in ecological studies of desert ecosystems.
