Microtatorchis acuminata

Microtatorchis acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed microtatorchis, is a small, herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface that helps reduce water loss. Its flowers are small and clustered, often with a pale green or white hue, and are adapted for pollination by insects.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
