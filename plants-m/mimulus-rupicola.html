Mimulus rupicola

Mimulus rupicola, commonly known as the rock monkeyflower, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange or yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it has a sprawling growth habit.

Mimulus rupicola is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in stream banks and open woodlands.
