Melastoma chulese

Melastoma chulese, commonly known as the Philippine beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered purple berries that appear after the flowers have faded. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments such as forests or undergrowth. Its leaves are usually opposite and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant fruit display.
