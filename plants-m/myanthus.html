Myanthus

Myanthus, commonly known as the moonflower, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

These plants are characterized by their large, showy white flowers that open at night and close during the day. The leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, and the stems are often hairy. Myanthus species are native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

They are valued for their ornamental beauty and are sometimes cultivated for their fragrance and nocturnal blooming habit.
