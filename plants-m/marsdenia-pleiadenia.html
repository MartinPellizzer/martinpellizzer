Marsdenia pleiadenia

Marsdenia pleiadenia, commonly known as the pleiadenia milkvine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
