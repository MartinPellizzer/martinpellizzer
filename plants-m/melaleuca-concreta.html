Melaleuca concreta

Melaleuca concreta, commonly known as the broad-leaved paperbark, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a reddish inner layer, and its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a whitish powdery coating. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in winter, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

Melaleuca concreta is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in harsh coastal environments.
