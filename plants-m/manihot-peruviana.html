Manihot peruviana

Manihot peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has a fibrous, starchy root system. It is often cultivated for its edible roots, which are used in cooking and as a thickening agent.
