Mentha arvensis var. glaberrima

Mentha arvensis var. glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth field mint, is a variant of the field mint species native to temperate regions.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the upper stem. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, making it useful in herbal remedies and as a culinary herb.
