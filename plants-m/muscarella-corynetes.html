Muscarella corynetes

Muscarella corynetes, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, purple to pink petals surrounding a central disk of yellow florets, with bracts that form a conical shape. Muscarella corynetes typically grows in prairies, meadows, and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

It is a perennial herb that produces seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
