Mammillaria falcata

Mammillaria falcata, commonly known as the hooked mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, white woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
