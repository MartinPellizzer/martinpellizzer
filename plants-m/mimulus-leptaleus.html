Mimulus leptaleus

Mimulus leptaleus, commonly known as the slender monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, spurred petals and bright orange or yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as stream banks and meadows. It has opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Mimulus leptaleus is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
