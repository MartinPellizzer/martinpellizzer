Meconopsis integrifolia var. souliei

Meconopsis integrifolia var. souliei, commonly known as the Sulié’s Himalayan poppy, is a striking alpine plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue or purple petals that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and have a smooth, uninterrupted margin.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural alpine environment.
