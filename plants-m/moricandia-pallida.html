Moricandia pallida

Moricandia pallida, commonly known as the pale moricandia, is an annual flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has hairy, lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine white powder. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Moricandia pallida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
