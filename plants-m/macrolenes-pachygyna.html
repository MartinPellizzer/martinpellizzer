Macrolenes pachygyna

Macrolenes pachygyna, commonly known as the thick-stemmed macrolene, is a unique plant species characterized by its robust, fleshy stems and broad, waxy leaves.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions, where it has adapted to store water in its thick, succulent stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale green or yellow, which attract pollinators such as bees and moths. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, powdery coating that helps reduce water loss through transpiration.

Due to its resilience and unique morphology, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in drought-tolerant gardens.
