Miconia firma

Miconia firma, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Miconia* tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and floral display.

However, it can become invasive in some regions, posing a threat to local ecosystems.
