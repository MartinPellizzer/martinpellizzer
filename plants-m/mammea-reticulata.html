Mammea reticulata

Mammea reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined mammea, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and compound leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine and pharmacological research.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological roles in its native habitats.
