Musa × paradisiaca var. papillosa

Musa × paradisiaca var. papillosa, commonly known as the hairy bananas, is a botanical variant of the banana plant characterized by its hairy, fibrous sheaths on the stems.

This variety is native to Southeast Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value rather than for fruit production. The plant features large, broad leaves that can reach several meters in length, contributing to its dramatic appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance, it is frequently used in horticultural displays and as a decorative plant in tropical gardens.
