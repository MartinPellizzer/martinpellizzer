Marurang

Marurang, commonly known as the "wild passionflower," is a climbing vine native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a distinctive crown of stamens. The plant produces edible, tangy fruits that are often consumed fresh or used in local dishes. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and covered in small spines.

Marurang thrives in tropical climates and is often found growing along forest edges or in shaded areas.
