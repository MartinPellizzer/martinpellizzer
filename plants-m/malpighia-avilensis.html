Malpighia avilensis

Malpighia avilensis, commonly known as the avil tree or Mexican hibiscus, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible and often consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
