Mentha × statenicensis

Mentha × statenicensis, commonly known as the Staten Island mint, is a hybrid species within the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or blue, which are typically clustered in whorls along the stem. Mentha × statenicensis is native to the northeastern United States and is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
