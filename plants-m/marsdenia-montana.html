Marsdenia montana

Marsdenia montana, commonly known as the mountain Marsdenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers, which are followed by follicles containing seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
