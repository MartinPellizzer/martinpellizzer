Mammillaria deherdtiana subsp. deherdtiana

Mammillaria deherdtiana subsp.

deherdtiana, commonly known as the Deherdt's mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico. It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense covering of white to pale blue spines.

The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments and is well adapted to drought conditions. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in the axils of the areoles.

This subspecies is relatively rare and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance.
