Matelea chimboracensis

Matelea chimboracensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant produces a distinctive scent that attracts nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a velvety texture.

Matelea chimboracensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
