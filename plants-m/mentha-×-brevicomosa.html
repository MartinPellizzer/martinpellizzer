Mentha × brevicomosa

Mentha × brevicomosa, commonly known as the dwarf mint, is a hybrid species within the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic leaves that emit a strong mint scent. The plant typically features opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue or purple, arranged in whorled clusters.

Mentha × brevicomosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of essential oils.
