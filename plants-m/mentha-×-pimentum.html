Mentha × pimentum

Mentha × pimentum, commonly known as Brazilian peppermint, is a hybrid mint species native to South America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, slender leaves and clusters of small, pale purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its essential oils, rich in menthol and other compounds, are widely used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.

Mentha × pimentum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
