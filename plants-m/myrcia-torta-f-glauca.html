Myrcia torta f. glauca

Myrcia torta f. glauca, commonly known as the glaucous myrcia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, glaucous-blue leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or red, followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its aesthetic appeal and unique foliage.
