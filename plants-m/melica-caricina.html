Melica caricina

Melica caricina, commonly known as slender meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems that often reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a hairy texture.

The inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelets that produce wind-pollinated flowers.
