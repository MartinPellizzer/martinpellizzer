Myriocephalus appendiculatus

Myriocephalus appendiculatus, commonly known as the clustered sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fleshy bracts that surround the flower heads. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is often found in maritime climates.

Myriocephalus appendiculatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand coastal conditions.
