Mahonia philippinensis

Mahonia philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine mahonia, is a shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with dark green, leathery leaves that have a distinctive spiny margin. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by blue-black berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions and is often found in forest understories.

Mahonia philippinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
