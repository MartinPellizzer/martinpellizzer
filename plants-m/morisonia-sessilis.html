Morisonia sessilis

Morisonia sessilis, commonly known as the sessile morisonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Morisonia sessilis is a perennial herb that plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
