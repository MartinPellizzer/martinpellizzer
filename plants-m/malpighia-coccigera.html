Malpighia coccigera

Malpighia coccigera, commonly known as the red mombin, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Malpighiaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
