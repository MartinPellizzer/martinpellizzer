Mimusops aedificatoria

Mimusops aedificatoria, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a flowering plant native to Africa, Madagascar, and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The tree is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and the tree is valued for its hard, heavy wood used in construction and furniture.

The plant is also cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
