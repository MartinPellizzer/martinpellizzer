Myrosmodes pumilio

Myrosmodes pumilio, commonly known as the dwarf myrosmodes, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit, glossy dark green leaves, and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.

Myrosmodes pumilio is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
