Maxillaria atropurpurea

Maxillaria atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple-stemmed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its dark purple to reddish stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of purple and white, often with a yellow throat. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Maxillaria atropurpurea is appreciated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
