Maxillaria taracuana

Maxillaria taracuana, commonly known as the "Carnival Orchid," is a tropical orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful flowers that often display a mix of red, orange, and yellow hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its pseudobulbs are elongated and covered in a thick, leathery epidermis, which helps it retain moisture.

The flowers are borne on terminal racemes and are known for their striking appearance and fragrance.
