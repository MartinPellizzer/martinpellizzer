Muscari pyramidatum

Muscari pyramidatum, commonly known as the purple musk grape, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces clusters of bell-shaped, purple-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that form a dense rosette at the base. Its bulbs are underground storage organs that allow it to survive harsh winters.

Muscari pyramidatum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
