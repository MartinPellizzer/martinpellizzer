Maranta vittata

Maranta vittata, commonly known as the streaked arrowroot or silver stripe maranta, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that display intricate patterns of silver and green streaks. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often grown as a houseplant due to its ornamental value. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by red berries.

Maranta vittata is known for its ability to thrive in low light conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
