Malva pedunculata

Malva pedunculata, commonly known as the woolly mallow, is a herbaceous plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional remedies.
