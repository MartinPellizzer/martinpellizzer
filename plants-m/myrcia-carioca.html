Myrcia carioca

Myrcia carioca, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its dense foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Myrcia carioca thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
