Malus atrorubra

Malus atrorubra, commonly known as the black-red crabapple, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia, including China, Korea, and Japan. The plant is characterized by its small, ornamental fruits and showy flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically dark green and oval-shaped, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant red or black fruits.

Malus atrorubra is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and is also used in traditional medicine.
