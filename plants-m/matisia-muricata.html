Matisia muricata

Matisia muricata, commonly known as the roughleaf matisia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malvaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and showy, pale yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters. It grows as a shrub or small tree, often reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
