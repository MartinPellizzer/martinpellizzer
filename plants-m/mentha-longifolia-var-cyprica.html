Mentha longifolia var. cyprica

Mentha longifolia var. cyprica, commonly known as Cyprus mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are slightly hairy and have a strong minty scent. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its essential oils are valued for their culinary and medicinal uses.
