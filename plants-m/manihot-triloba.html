Manihot triloba

Manihot triloba, commonly known as the arrowleaf batata, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its tuberous roots are edible and have been traditionally used for food and medicinal purposes.
