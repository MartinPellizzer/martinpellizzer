Mosla punctulata

Mosla punctulata, commonly known as spotted mosquito plant, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are often covered in tiny white spots, giving it its common name.

Mosla punctulata is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and mild fragrance.
