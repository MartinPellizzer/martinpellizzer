Macaranga parabicolor

Macaranga parabicolor, commonly known as the white-veined macaranga, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a fast-growing shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with prominent white veins, which give it its common name. The species is also noted for its ability to form associations with ant plants, providing shelter and food for certain ant species.
