Malva subhastata

Malva subhastata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinctive veining pattern. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Malva subhastata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens.
