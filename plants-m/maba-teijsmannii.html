Maba teijsmannii

Maba teijsmannii, commonly known as the yellow maba tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits. Its roots and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The plant is also valued for its timber, which is lightweight and durable.
