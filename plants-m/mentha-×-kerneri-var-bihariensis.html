Mentha × kerneri var. bihariensis

Mentha × kerneri var. bihariensis, commonly known as the Bihar mint, is a hybrid mint species native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Its essential oils contain menthol and other compounds that contribute to its cooling and soothing effects.
