Mentha arvensis var. hirsutior

Mentha arvensis var. hirsutior, commonly known as woolly mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Woolly mint is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in herbal medicine and as a culinary herb.
