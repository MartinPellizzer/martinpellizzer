Melica retrofracta

Melica retrofracta, commonly known as the bentgrass or narrowleaf brome, is a species of grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, riverbanks, and woodland edges. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, two-lobed spikelets.

Melica retrofracta is a perennial plant that plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
