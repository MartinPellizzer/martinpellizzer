Metroxylon sagu f. micracanthum

Metroxylon sagu f. micracanthum, commonly known as the sago palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces a fibrous, starchy pith in its trunk, which is harvested for food and industrial use. Its growth is slow, and it thrives in humid, warm climates with well-drained soil.

The sago palm plays an important ecological and economic role in its native regions.
