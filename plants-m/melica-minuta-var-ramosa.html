Melica minuta var. ramosa

Melica minuta var. ramosa, commonly known as slender basket grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, ascending stems that are often branched, giving it a bushy appearance. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

The inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are small and often clustered at the ends of the branches.
