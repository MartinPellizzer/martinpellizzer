Melocactus wendlandii

Melocactus wendlandii, commonly known as the Wendland's melon cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, thick stem and large, ribbed, spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its growth is slow, and it thrives in well-draining soil with minimal water.

It is often cultivated for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
