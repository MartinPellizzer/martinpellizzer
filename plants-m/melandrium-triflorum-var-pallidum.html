Melandrium triflorum var. pallidum

Melandrium triflorum var. pallidum, commonly known as the pale meadow catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has a low, sprawling growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting pollinator populations.
