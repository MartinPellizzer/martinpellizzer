Mentha × ceretana

Mentha × ceretana, commonly known as the hybrid mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant that belongs to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid resulting from the cross between Mentha spicata (spearmint) and Mentha viridis (ovalleaf mint). The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or white. Mentha × ceretana is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens and herbal collections.
