Masdevallia gentianoides

Masdevallia gentianoides, commonly known as the gentian orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, blue-violet flowers that resemble those of the gentian plant, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its leaves are elongated and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native humid environment.

Masdevallia gentianoides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and unique floral structure.
