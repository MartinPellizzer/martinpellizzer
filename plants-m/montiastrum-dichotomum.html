Montiastrum dichotomum

Montiastrum dichotomum, commonly known as the forked mountain-mint, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats. Its leaves are opposite and often deeply lobed, while its flowers are small, clustered, and usually white or pale blue.

The species is noted for its ability to form dense mats, contributing to soil stabilization in its native range.
