Malaxis setipes

Malaxis setipes, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption in poor soil conditions.

Malaxis setipes is often found in association with mycorrhizal fungi, which aid in its nutrient uptake.
