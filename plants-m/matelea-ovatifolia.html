Matelea ovatifolia

Matelea ovatifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mescal bean, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers. Its fruit is a slender, woody pod that contains several seeds.

Matelea ovatifolia is often found in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
