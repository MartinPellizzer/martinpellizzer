Montbretia strictifolia

Montbretia strictifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved montbretia, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the Cape region of South Africa, where it thrives in coastal and mountainous environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, orange-red flowers in late summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.

However, it can become invasive in some regions if not managed properly.
