Monostachya nardifolia

Monostachya nardifolia, commonly known as the sweet grass or nard grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often fragrant, contributing to its historical use in perfumery and traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic properties, it is cultivated in gardens and used in various cultural practices.
