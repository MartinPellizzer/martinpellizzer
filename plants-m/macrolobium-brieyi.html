Macrolobium brieyi

Macrolobium brieyi, commonly known as the Brazilian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Macrolobium brieyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
