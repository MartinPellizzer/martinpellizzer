Microlepia nigricans

Microlepia nigricans, commonly known as the black fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its dark, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream sides. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The fronds are usually divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments with a distinctive blackish hue.
