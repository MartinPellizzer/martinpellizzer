Mesosetum elegans

Mesosetum elegans, commonly known as the slender bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to saturated soil conditions. The plant features narrow, bluish-green leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in wetland environments.

Mesosetum elegans is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining wetland biodiversity.
