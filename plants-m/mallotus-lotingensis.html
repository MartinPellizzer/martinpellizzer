Mallotus lotingensis

Mallotus lotingensis, commonly known as the Malay mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible, round fruits with a thick, colorful rind.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
