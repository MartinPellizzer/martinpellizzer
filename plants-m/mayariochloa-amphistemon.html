Mayariochloa amphistemon

Mayariochloa amphistemon, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Mayariochloa amphistemon plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native invertebrates.
