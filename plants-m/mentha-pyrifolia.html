Mentha pyrifolia

Mentha pyrifolia, commonly known as Japanese mint, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a serrated edge. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its aromatic leaves contain menthol and other volatile oils, giving it a refreshing scent.

Mentha pyrifolia is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
