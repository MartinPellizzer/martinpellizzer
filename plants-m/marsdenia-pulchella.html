Marsdenia pulchella

Marsdenia pulchella, commonly known as the pink milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asclepiadaceae family, known for its showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, elliptic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woods.

The plant is also notable for its milky sap, which contains latex and is characteristic of the genus Marsdenia.
