Miconia quadripora

Miconia quadripora, commonly known as the four-lobed miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves with four lobes. The plant produces showy, pink or red bracts that resemble flowers, often mistaken for actual blooms. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Miconia quadripora is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
