Malva orsiniana

Malva orsiniana, commonly known as the Mexican mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus, and is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom from summer to fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Malva orsiniana is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and blooms.
