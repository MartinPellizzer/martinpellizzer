Myrcia laricina

Myrcia laricina, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and fragrant leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Myrcia laricina thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sun exposure.

Its ornamental value and aromatic properties make it a popular choice for horticultural use in warm regions.
