Merremia dissecta var. chacoensis

Merremia dissecta var. chacoensis, commonly known as the Chaco morning glory, is a flowering vine native to South America, particularly found in the Chaco region of Paraguay and Argentina.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. It produces capsules containing numerous small, elliptical seeds.

The species is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
