Monechma crassiusculum

Monechma crassiusculum, commonly known as the broadleaf monechma, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and forest edges. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with smooth margins and produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters. Its reproductive system relies on insect pollination, and it has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and threatened species in many areas.
