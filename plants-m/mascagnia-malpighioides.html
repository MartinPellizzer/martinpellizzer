Mascagnia malpighioides

Mascagnia malpighioides, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and Central America, where it typically grows in dry, rocky soils. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are often yellow or orange in color, which bloom at night, attracting specific pollinators. Its leaves are ovate and hairy, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that contain toxic seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
