Malcolmia macrocalyx subsp. macrocalyx

Malcolmia macrocalyx subsp.

macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered malcolmia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
