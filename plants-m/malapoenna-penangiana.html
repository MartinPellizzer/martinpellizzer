Malapoenna penangiana

Malapoenna penangiana, commonly known as the Penang ironwood, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hard, dense wood and is often found in tropical rainforests. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth texture.

It plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
