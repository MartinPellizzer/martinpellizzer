Mutisia kurtzii var. anomala

Mutisia kurtzii var. anomala, commonly known as the yellow mutisia, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and features showy yellow flowers that attract pollinators. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
