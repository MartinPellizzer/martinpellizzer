Medica orbicularis

Medica orbicularis, commonly known as the roundleaf medica, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-bearing fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its fruit is a legume, containing several seeds that are dispersed by wind or animals.

Medica orbicularis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
