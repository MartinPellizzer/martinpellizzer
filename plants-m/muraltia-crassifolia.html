Muraltia crassifolia

Muraltia crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved muraltia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly in countries like Brazil and Mexico. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often broad and ovate, and its clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. It produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and it thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

Muraltia crassifolia is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
