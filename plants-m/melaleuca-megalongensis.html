Melaleuca megalongensis

Melaleuca megalongensis, commonly known as the Long Island tea tree, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to northern Australia, particularly the Northern Territory and Western Australia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, fluffy white or cream-colored flowers that appear in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils.

Melaleuca megalongensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses by Indigenous Australians.
