Melocactus obtusipetalus

Melocactus obtusipetalus, commonly known as the blunt-petaled melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a ribbed structure and a dense covering of white, woolly hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically borne at the base of the stem. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

The plant is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated as a decorative succulent.
