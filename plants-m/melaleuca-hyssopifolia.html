Melaleuca hyssopifolia

Melaleuca hyssopifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf paperbark, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a pale, papery inner layer. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters, which are typically arranged in spikes along the branches. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

Melaleuca hyssopifolia is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils, making it a hardy plant in various environmental conditions.
