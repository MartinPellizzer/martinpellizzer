Maxillaria trilobulata

Maxillaria trilobulata, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms in the summer, with flowers arranged in racemes along the stem. Its leaves are strap-like and arranged in two rows, contributing to its unique appearance.

Maxillaria trilobulata is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
