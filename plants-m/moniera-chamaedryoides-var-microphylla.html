Moniera chamaedryoides var. microphylla

Moniera chamaedryoides var. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent with fleshy, thick leaves that store water.

It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically blooming in summer. Native to arid regions, this plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

It is also valued for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
