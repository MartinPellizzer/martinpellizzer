Marrubium lutescens

Marrubium lutescens, commonly known as yellow horehound, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in grasslands and scrublands. It has a compact growth habit with upright stems and produces small, blue-black seeds.
