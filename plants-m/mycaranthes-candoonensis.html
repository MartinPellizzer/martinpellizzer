Mycaranthes candoonensis

Mycaranthes candoonensis, commonly known as the Candoon sandalwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the rainforests of northeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, glossy dark green leaves, and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces woody, aromatic fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, humid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
