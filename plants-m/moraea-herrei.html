Moraea herrei

Moraea herrei, commonly known as the Cape bellflower, is a striking perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette.

Moraea herrei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
