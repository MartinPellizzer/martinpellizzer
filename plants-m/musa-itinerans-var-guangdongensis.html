Musa itinerans var. guangdongensis

Musa itinerans var. guangdongensis, commonly known as the Guangdong banana, is a variant of the wild banana species native to southern China.

It is a herbaceous plant with large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, curved fruits. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for cultivation, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
