Medinilla piperoides

Medinilla piperoides, commonly known as the velvet tangerine, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that resemble small tangerines. The plant produces showy, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a citrus-like flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Medinilla piperoides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and unique fruit characteristics.
