Mormodes horichii

Mormodes horichii, commonly known as the Horichii orchid, is a rare and fascinating member of the Orchidaceae family.

Native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant's leaves are dark green and glossy, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Due to habitat destruction, Mormodes horichii is considered endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
