Myrcia matogrossensis

Myrcia matogrossensis, commonly known as the Matogrossense Myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Myrcia matogrossensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has potential uses in traditional medicine.
