Mucuna guangxiensis

Mucuna guangxiensis, commonly known as Guangxi velvetbean, is a climbing legume native to southern China.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, curved pods that contain spherical seeds. The plant produces a dense covering of velvety hairs on its stems and leaves, giving it its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This species is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment.
