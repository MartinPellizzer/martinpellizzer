Matthiola linearis

Matthiola linearis, commonly known as wallflower, is an annual or biennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to red and orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also used in traditional medicine.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
