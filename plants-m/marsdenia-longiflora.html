Marsdenia longiflora

Marsdenia longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered Marsdenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and slender, hairy stems. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by distinctive, follicle-like fruits. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy texture.

Marsdenia longiflora is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
