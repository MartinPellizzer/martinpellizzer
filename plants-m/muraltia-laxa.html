Muraltia laxa

Muraltia laxa, commonly known as the lax muraltia, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters and features glossy, dark green leaves that are oval in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Muraltia laxa is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and seasonal floral display.
