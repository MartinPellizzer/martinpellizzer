Mentha aquatica var. citrata

Mentha aquatica var. citrata, commonly known as citron mint, is a variant of the water mint species.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. The plant has slender, creeping stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with a pale yellow or greenish hue. Its flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and have a distinct minty aroma.

Citron mint is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.
