Mentha × nepetoides subsp. stenodes

Mentha × nepetoides subsp.

stenodes, commonly known as narrow-leaved catmint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine and as a ornamental plant.
