Microchloa convergens

Microchloa convergens, commonly known as the narrow-leaf microchloa, is a small, perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often densely clustered at the base. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute flowers, which are typically green or reddish in color. Microchloa convergens is adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
