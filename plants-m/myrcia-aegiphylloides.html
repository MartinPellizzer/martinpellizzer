Myrcia aegiphylloides

Myrcia aegiphylloides, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a sharp, acidic taste. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas.

Myrcia aegiphylloides is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
