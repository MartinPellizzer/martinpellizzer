Mentzelia pumila var. procera

Mentzelia pumila var. procera, commonly known as the tall plains mullein, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its growth form and floral structure make it well adapted to arid environments.
