Melocactus caroli-linnaei

Melocactus caroli-linnaei, commonly known as the Carolinian melon cactus, is a small, globular cactus native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It features a thick, ribbed stem covered in short, white spines and is characterized by its ability to store water in its fleshy, green body. The plant produces yellow flowers during the wet season, which are followed by edible, melon-like fruits. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by cactus enthusiasts.
