Malva mamillosa

Malva mamillosa, commonly known as the woolly mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Malva mamillosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
