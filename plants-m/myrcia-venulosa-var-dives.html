Myrcia venulosa var. dives

Myrcia venulosa var. dives, commonly known as the glossy myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species but toxic to humans. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
