Mussinia incisa

Mussinia incisa, commonly known as the narrowleaf moss campion, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Mussinia incisa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
