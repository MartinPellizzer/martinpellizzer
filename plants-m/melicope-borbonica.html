Melicope borbonica

Melicope borbonica, commonly known as the Madagascar laurier or Madagascar pepper tree, is a member of the Rutaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Madagascar, characterized by its aromatic, smooth, and often glossy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits that are initially green and turn red when mature. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in traditional crafts and construction.

The plant is also noted for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
