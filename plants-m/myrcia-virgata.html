Myrcia virgata

Myrcia virgata, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are leathery and glossy, with a distinct aromatic scent when crushed.

Myrcia virgata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
