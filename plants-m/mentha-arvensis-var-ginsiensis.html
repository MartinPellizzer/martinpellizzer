Mentha arvensis var. ginsiensis

Mentha arvensis var. ginsiensis, commonly known as Ginsi mint, is a perennial herb native to the Korean Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or shaded areas. It is valued for its essential oils, which are used in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.

Its growth habit and chemical composition make it a notable species within the Mentha genus.
