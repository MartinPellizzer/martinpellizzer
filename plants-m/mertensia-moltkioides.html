Mertensia moltkioides

Mertensia moltkioides, commonly known as the Appalachian bluebells, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically a vibrant shade of blue or violet, blooming in early spring. The plant has heart-shaped, toothed leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Mertensia moltkioides is often found in woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
