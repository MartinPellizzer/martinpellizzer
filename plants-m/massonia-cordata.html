Massonia cordata

Massonia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf massonia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, especially in direct sunlight. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white or yellow during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
