Mesanthophora rojasii

Mesanthophora rojasii, commonly known as the Rosy Orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink and white floral display, which features a unique, elongated lip that distinguishes it from other orchid species. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions with partial shade. Its flowers bloom during the spring and early summer, adding a rare beauty to its natural habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Mesanthophora rojasii is considered a conservation concern.
