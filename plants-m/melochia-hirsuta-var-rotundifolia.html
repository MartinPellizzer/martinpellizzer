Melochia hirsuta var. rotundifolia

Melochia hirsuta var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf melochia, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and broad, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, three-lobed seed pods. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
