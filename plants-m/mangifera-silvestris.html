Mangifera silvestris

Mangifera silvestris, commonly known as the wild mango, is a species of flowering plant in the Anacardiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and fragrant, yellow-orange fruits. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing before the leaves, and it produces edible fruits that are used in traditional cuisines.

The botanical features of Mangifera silvestris make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural studies.
