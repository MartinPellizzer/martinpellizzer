Monardella lanceolata var. microcephala

Monardella lanceolata var. microcephala, commonly known as narrowleaf monardella, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
