Malaxis quadridentata

Malaxis quadridentata, commonly known as the four-toothed malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, often on the forest floor or in rocky crevices. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette, and it produces a single spike of flowers with a distinctive, narrow shape.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is often found in humid, tropical climates.
