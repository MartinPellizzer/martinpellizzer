Melastoma impressinervium

Melastoma impressinervium, commonly known as the broad-veined melastoma, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with prominent, parallel veins. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has cultural significance in some regions.
