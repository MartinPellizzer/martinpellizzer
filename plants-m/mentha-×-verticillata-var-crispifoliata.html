Mentha × verticillata var. crispifoliata

Mentha × verticillata var. crispifoliata, commonly known as the wrinkled-leaf water mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, serrated leaves that have a distinctive crinkled or curled appearance. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. It produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

This variety is often used in herbal remedies and as a decorative plant in water gardens.
