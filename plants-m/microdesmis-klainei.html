Microdesmis klainei

Microdesmis klainei, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue and purple. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its deep root system and succulent leaves. Its flowers typically open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.

Microdesmis klainei is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting pollinators in its native habitat.
