Machaerium pseudotipe

Machaerium pseudotipe, commonly known as the false tipe, is a tropical shrub native to regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by pods that contain several seeds.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits.
