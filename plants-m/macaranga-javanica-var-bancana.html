Macaranga javanica var. bancana

Macaranga javanica var. bancana, commonly known as the Javan Macaranga, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its rapid growth rate makes it an important pioneer species in disturbed habitats.
