Miconia tetrazygioides

Miconia tetrazygioides, commonly known as the red miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. It is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
