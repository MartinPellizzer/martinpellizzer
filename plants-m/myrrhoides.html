Myrrhoides

Myrrhoides, commonly known as the sacred myrrh plant, is a member of the Commelinaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia, where it thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant is characterized by its erect, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, often producing a fragrant resin that has been used for centuries in traditional medicine and religious rituals.

The resin, known as myrrh, is harvested from the bark and has various medicinal and aromatic applications.
