Melica imperfecta var. sesquiflora

Melica imperfecta var. sesquiflora, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are usually greenish to brownish in color.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates in its native environment.
