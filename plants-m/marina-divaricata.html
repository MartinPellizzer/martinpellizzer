Marina divaricata

Marina divaricata, commonly known as the coastal dune plant or sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and spiny, branched stems that help it thrive in sandy, saline environments. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It has a deep root system that stabilizes dunes and prevents erosion.

Marina divaricata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and adaptability.
