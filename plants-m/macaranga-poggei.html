Macaranga poggei

Macaranga poggei, commonly known as the white-veined macaranga, is a tropical tree belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent white veins, which give it its common name. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, tropical climates. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Macaranga poggei is often found in secondary forests and is known for its rapid growth rate, making it a common pioneer species in disturbed habitats.
