Monardella odoratissima subsp. pinetorum

Monardella odoratissima subsp.

pinetorum, commonly known as the pine mountain beebalm, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular red flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and pine forests. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, sharing traits with other members like mint and sage.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
