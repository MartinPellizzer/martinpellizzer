Merista

Merista, commonly known as the mistletoe plant, is a hemiparasitic shrub that grows on the branches of host trees.

It belongs to the family Viscaceae and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers. The plant produces berries that are often used in traditional medicine and holiday decorations. Merista relies on its host for water and nutrients, while it provides shelter for various insects and birds.

Its ecological role is significant in forest ecosystems, supporting biodiversity through mutualistic relationships.
