Meconopsis aculeata f. normalis

Meconopsis aculeata f. normalis, commonly known as the Himalayan blue poppy, is a striking alpine plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to violet petals, which are typically 3-5 cm in length, and its distinctive, slightly hairy leaves. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments at high altitudes, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
