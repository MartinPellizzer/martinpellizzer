Moniera dianthera

Moniera dianthera, commonly known as the silver bellflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, with a distinctive silver or grayish hue on the inner petals. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden borders and rock gardens.

Moniera dianthera is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its elegant appearance.
