Myrcia vittoriana var. piratiningensis

Myrcia vittoriana var. piratiningensis, commonly known as the Piratininga myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface and a fragrant, resinous scent. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, dark purple berries.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, humid environments.
