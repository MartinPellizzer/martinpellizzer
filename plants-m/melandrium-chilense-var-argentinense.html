Melandrium chilense var. argentinense

Melandrium chilense var. argentinense, commonly known as the Argentine sandmat, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Crambe family (Crambeaceae) and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to thrive in sandy or arid soils.

It is often found in coastal or desert regions where it contributes to stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
