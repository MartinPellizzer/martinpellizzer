Melanoleuce

Melanoleuce, commonly known as the white-veined lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with prominent dark veins, which give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring long, narrow leaves with a silver-green hue. Melanoleuce thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in botanical gardens and by horticultural enthusiasts worldwide.
