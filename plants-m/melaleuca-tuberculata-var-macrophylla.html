Melaleuca tuberculata var. macrophylla

Melaleuca tuberculata var. macrophylla, commonly known as the broad-leaf paperbark, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in coastal and wetland areas. The plant is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that gives it the common name "paperbark.". Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist environments.
