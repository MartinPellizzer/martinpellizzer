Mouriri subumbellata

Mouriri subumbellata, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with a broad, spreading canopy. The plant produces small, yellow, star-shaped fruits that are edible and have a tangy flavor. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appearance and its fruit, which is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
