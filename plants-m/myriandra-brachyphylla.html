Myriandra brachyphylla

Myriandra brachyphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a shrub native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that appear in clusters. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

The species is also valued for its aromatic properties and has traditional uses by Indigenous Australian communities.
