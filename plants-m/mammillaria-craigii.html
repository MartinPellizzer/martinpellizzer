Mammillaria craigii

Mammillaria craigii, commonly known as the Craig's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the Mexican state of Sinaloa.

It is a small, globular cactus with a ribbed stem and prominent, white to pale yellow spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red or orange fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Mammillaria craigii is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
