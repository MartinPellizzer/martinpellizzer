Melampyrum × viviscense

Melampyrum × viviscense, commonly known as the hybrid cow-vetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant that belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a natural hybrid between Melampyrum lathyroides and Melampyrum nemorosum, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant typically grows in woodland and shaded areas, preferring moist, well-drained soils. It produces small, yellowish-orange flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

Its leaves are compound, with small oval leaflets, and it is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.
