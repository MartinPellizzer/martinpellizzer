Mentha sabranskyi

Mentha sabranskyi, commonly known as the Siberian mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. The plant features square stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its fragrant flowers, which bloom in late summer, are typically blue or purple and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Mentha sabranskyi is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory benefits.
