Misodendrum microphyllum

Misodendrum microphyllum, commonly known as the small-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually clustered at the base of the stems. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers, followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits that are dispersed by birds. Its roots form haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system to extract water and nutrients.

The plant's botanical characteristics make it an important component of the ecosystems it inhabits.
