Malus betulifolia

Malus betulifolia, commonly known as the birchleaf crabapple, is a small deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive bark that resembles that of a birch tree, hence its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet-tart apples in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, oval-shaped, and turn yellow in the fall.

Malus betulifolia is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.
