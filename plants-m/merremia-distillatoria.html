Merremia distillatoria

Merremia distillatoria, commonly known as the spider vine, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its stems are hairy and can grow quite long, often reaching several meters in length.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.
