Michelia rufinervis

Michelia rufinervis, commonly known as the red-veined michelia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth texture and prominent reddish veins, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aromatic foliage and flowers.
