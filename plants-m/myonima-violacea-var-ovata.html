Myonima violacea var. ovata

Myonima violacea var. ovata, commonly known as the purple-flowered myonima, is a species of flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and birds.

The varietal form ovata is distinguished by its ovate leaf shape, which sets it apart from other varieties of Myonima violacea.
