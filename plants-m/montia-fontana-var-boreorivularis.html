Montia fontana var. boreorivularis

Montia fontana var. boreorivularis, commonly known as the northern river montia, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It thrives in moist, rocky or sandy soils along riverbanks and wet meadows. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It is a member of the Montiaceae family, which includes several species adapted to cold climates.

This variety is notable for its ability to survive in harsh, nutrient-poor environments.
