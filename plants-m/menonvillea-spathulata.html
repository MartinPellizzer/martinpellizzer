Menonvillea spathulata

Menonvillea spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features elongated, lanceolate leaves and produces showy, violet-colored flowers in late summer. It is part of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
