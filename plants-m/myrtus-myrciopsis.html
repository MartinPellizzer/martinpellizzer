Myrtus myrciopsis

Myrtus myrciopsis, commonly known as the Mediterranean myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant features small, dark green leaves that are often fragrant, and it produces white or pale pink flowers in spring. Its fruits are small, berry-like drupes that contain several seeds.

Myrtus myrciopsis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
