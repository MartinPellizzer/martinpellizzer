Mutisia hamata

Mutisia hamata, commonly known as the white mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in countries like Bolivia and Peru.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

Mutisia hamata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
