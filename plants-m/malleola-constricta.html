Malleola constricta

Malleola constricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf malleola, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of South America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, dark green surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Malleola constricta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a ground cover.
