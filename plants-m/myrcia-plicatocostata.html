Myrcia plicatocostata

Myrcia plicatocostata, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are often followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Myrcia plicatocostata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
