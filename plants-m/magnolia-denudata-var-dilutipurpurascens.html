Magnolia denudata var. dilutipurpurascens

Magnolia denudata var. dilutipurpurascens, commonly known as the Korean magnolia or the pink-veined magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to Korea.

It is characterized by its large, showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The leaves are broad, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a glossy upper surface. The tree produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
