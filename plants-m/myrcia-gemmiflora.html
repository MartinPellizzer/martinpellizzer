Myrcia gemmiflora

Myrcia gemmiflora, commonly known as the gemmy myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces distinctive red, berry-like fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Myrcia gemmiflora thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticultural conservation.
