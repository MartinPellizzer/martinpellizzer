Myrcia spixiana

Myrcia spixiana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and humid forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
