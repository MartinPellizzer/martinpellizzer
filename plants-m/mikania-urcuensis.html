Mikania urcuensis

Mikania urcuensis, commonly known as the Andean devil's backbone, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in heads, typical of the family. It is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical and subtropical forests.

Its rapid growth and invasive potential make it a notable species in ecological studies.
