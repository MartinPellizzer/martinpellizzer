Musa sakaiana

Musa sakaiana, commonly known as the Sakaiana banana, is a species of banana native to the island of Saka in the Pacific.

It is a herbaceous plant with a pseudostem formed by the tightly packed leaves. The plant produces large, elongated fruits that are typically yellow when ripe. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
