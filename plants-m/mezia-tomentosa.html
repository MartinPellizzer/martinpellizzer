Mezia tomentosa

Mezia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly meadia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually white or pale pink and appear in early summer.

Mezia tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
