Malaxis setifera

Malaxis setifera, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its delicate, linear leaves and a single, slender stem that bears a single flower. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its flowers are usually small, with a distinctive lip that is often pink or white in color.

Malaxis setifera is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
