Mirbelia baueri

Mirbelia baueri, commonly known as the pink-flowered mirbelia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of northern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

Mirbelia baueri is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.
