Myrtus sparsifolia

Myrtus sparsifolia, commonly known as the sparse-leaved myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Myrtus sparsifolia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and rocky gardens.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
