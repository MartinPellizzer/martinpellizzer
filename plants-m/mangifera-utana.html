Mangifera utana

Mangifera utana, commonly known as the Utona mango, is a tropical fruit-bearing tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, fuzzy green fruits that turn yellow when ripe. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with broad, lobed leaves and fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is valued for its sweet, aromatic pulp and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
