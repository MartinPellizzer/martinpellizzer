Myroxylon frutescens

Myroxylon frutescens, commonly known as the Brazil wood, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its reddish heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and rich color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and elliptical, leathery leaves. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several winged seeds.

The tree is also known for its historical use in the production of dyes and musical instruments.
