Mentha longifolia var. caucasica

Mentha longifolia var. caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian mint, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and calming properties.

Its essential oils, rich in menthol and other volatile compounds, contribute to its distinctive cooling sensation and therapeutic applications.
