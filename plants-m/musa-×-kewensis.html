Musa × kewensis

Musa × kewensis, commonly known as the Kew banana, is a hybrid plant resulting from the crossbreeding of Musa acuminata and Musa balbisiana.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and clusters of large, yellow, edible berries. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and has been cultivated for its fruit, which is rich in nutrients. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its genetic diversity, it is often used in breeding programs to develop new banana varieties.
