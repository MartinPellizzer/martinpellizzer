Monanthotaxis klainei

Monanthotaxis klainei, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a striking night-blooming plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night and close by dawn. The plant has a climbing habit, with long, slender stems that can reach several meters in length. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often heart-shaped, providing a lush green backdrop to its showy blooms.

The flowers emit a sweet scent to attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths and bats.
