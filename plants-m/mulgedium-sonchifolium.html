Mulgedium sonchifolium

Mulgedium sonchifolium, commonly known as the common mulgedium, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
