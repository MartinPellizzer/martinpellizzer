Mesua thwaitesii

Mesua thwaitesii, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Mesua thwaitesii is also valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in traditional crafts and construction.
