Melilotus neapolitanus var. microcarpus

Melilotus neapolitanus var. microcarpus, commonly known as the small-fruited sweet clover, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are trifoliate with small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. Its fruits are small, dry pods that contain several seeds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

This variety is often found in disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential in ecological restoration.
