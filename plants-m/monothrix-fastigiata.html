Monothrix fastigiata

Monothrix fastigiata, commonly known as the upright thorned grass, is a perennial grass species native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that grow in a vertical or upright manner, often reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive texture. Its small, spike-like inflorescence bears numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Monothrix fastigiata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
