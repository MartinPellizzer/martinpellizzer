Melaleuca polygaloides

Melaleuca polygaloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf paperbark, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it is often found in coastal or sandy soils.

Melaleuca polygaloides is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
