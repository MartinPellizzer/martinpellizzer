Medica turbinata

Medica turbinata, commonly known as the turbanflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, turban-shaped inflorescence, which consists of dense clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers range in color from white to pale yellow.

Medica turbinata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
