Morenia chonta

Morenia chonta, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in wetlands or disturbed soils.

Morenia chonta is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it an invasive species in some regions.
