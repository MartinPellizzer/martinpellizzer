Moraea arenaria

Moraea arenaria, commonly known as the sand lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in well-drained environments. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it often forms dense clumps.

Moraea arenaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
