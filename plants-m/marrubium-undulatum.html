Marrubium undulatum

Marrubium undulatum, commonly known as undulating horehound, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. The plant features upright stems covered in grayish-white hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with wavy margins. Small blue or purple flowers appear in clusters during the spring and summer months.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional herbal remedies.
