Myrcia glaucescens

Myrcia glaucescens, commonly known as the blue myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, edible seeds.

Myrcia glaucescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
