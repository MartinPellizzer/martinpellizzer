Matthiola fruticulosa var. bolleana

Matthiola fruticulosa var. bolleana, commonly known as the Bolleana stock, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, four-petaled flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
