Minuartia lanceolata subsp. rupestris

Minuartia lanceolata subsp.

rupestris, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and coastal areas. It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the summer months. It is adapted to thrive in well-drained, sunny environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
