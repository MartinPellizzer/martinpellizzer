Madhuca ramiflora

Madhuca ramiflora, commonly known as the flame tree or torch tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and striking clusters of bright orange-red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed, which is dispersed by birds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree's rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
