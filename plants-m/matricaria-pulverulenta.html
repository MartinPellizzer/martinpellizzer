Matricaria pulverulenta

Matricaria pulverulenta, commonly known as the common tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its yellow flower heads and pinnately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Matricaria pulverulenta is also known for its historical use in herbal medicine.
