Malpighia pallens

Malpighia pallens, commonly known as the pink mombin, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its striking pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and branched.

Malpighia pallens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional medicinal uses in some regions.
