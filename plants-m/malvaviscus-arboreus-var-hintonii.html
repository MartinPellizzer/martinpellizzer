Malvaviscus arboreus var. hintonii

Malvaviscus arboreus var. hintonii, commonly known as the Hinton's woolly mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, characterized by its fuzzy, hairy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its botanical features include stellate (star-shaped) hairs on the stems and leaves, and it belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton.
