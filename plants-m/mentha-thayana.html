Mentha thayana

Mentha thayana, commonly known as American mint, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a square stem and opposite leaves, typical of the mint family (Lamiaceae).

Mentha thayana is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to its refreshing scent and flavor.
