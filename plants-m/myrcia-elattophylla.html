Myrcia elattophylla

Myrcia elattophylla, commonly known as the pink myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. It produces small, round berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

Myrcia elattophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has a pleasant, spicy fragrance.
