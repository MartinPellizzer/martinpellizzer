Monopyle grandiflora

Monopyle grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered monopyle, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and simple, opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. Its flowers are characterized by their five to seven petals and are often borne in terminal inflorescences.

Monopyle grandiflora is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
