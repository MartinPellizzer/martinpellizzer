Maillardia pendula

Maillardia pendula, commonly known as the hanging bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its pendulous clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and purple. The plant has slender, trailing stems that often hang from rock faces or cliffs. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy.

Maillardia pendula is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.
