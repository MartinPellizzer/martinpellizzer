Magnolia auricularis

Magnolia auricularis, commonly known as the auricle magnolia, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. Its bark is smooth and gray, and it typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub.

The flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
