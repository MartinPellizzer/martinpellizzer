Mariscus pseudopilosus

Mariscus pseudopilosus, commonly known as the silky sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf structures and often edible seeds. The plant features delicate, silky-hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, helping to reduce water loss in arid environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, with a structure typical of the genus Mariscus, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This species is adapted to sandy and rocky soils, thriving in dry, sunny conditions.
