Momordica coriacea

Momordica coriacea, commonly known as bitter gourd, is a tropical vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny stems, heart-shaped leaves, and long, ridged fruits that are typically green when mature. The plant produces yellow flowers and is native to the Americas, though it is now cultivated in many parts of Asia and Africa. Its fruit is used in various cuisines, though it has a distinctive bitter taste.

Botanically, it is notable for its complex fruit structure and its role in traditional medicine.
