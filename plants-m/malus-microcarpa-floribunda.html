Malus microcarpa floribunda

Malus microcarpa floribunda, commonly known as the Chinese white apple, is a small deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet yellow fruits. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is often cultivated for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
