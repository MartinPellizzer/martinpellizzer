Myrtillocactus hertrichii

Myrtillocactus hertrichii, commonly known as the blue cactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray spines and a globular shape that helps it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional Mexican cuisine. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in extreme desert conditions.

The blue cactus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climate conditions.
