Martia campanulata

Martia campanulata, commonly known as the bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to various regions in Europe and Asia, often found in meadows and woodlands. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that can be blue, purple, or white, depending on the species. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Martia campanulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
