Medicago lupulina subsp. willdenowiana

Medicago lupulina subsp.

willdenowiana, commonly known as the narrow-leafed clover, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant has a deep root system, which helps it thrive in well-drained soils. It is often found in grasslands, open woodlands, and along roadsides.

As a member of the Fabaceae family, it plays a role in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility.
