Mogorium hirsutum

Mogorium hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy mogorium, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
