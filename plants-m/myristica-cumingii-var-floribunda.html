Myristica cumingii var. floribunda

Myristica cumingii var. floribunda, commonly known as the fragrant nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The plant produces an aromatic seed kernel, which is closely related to the spice nutmeg. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential economic importance, it is of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
