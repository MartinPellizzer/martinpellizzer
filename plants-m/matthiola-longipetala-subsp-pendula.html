Matthiola longipetala subsp. pendula

Matthiola longipetala subsp.

pendula, commonly known as the hanging wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of fragrant, four-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The subspecies pendula is distinguished by its drooping inflorescences, which give it a cascading appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and rock gardens.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
