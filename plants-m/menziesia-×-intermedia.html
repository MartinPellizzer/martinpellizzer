Menziesia × intermedia

Menziesia × intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate menziesia, is a hybrid shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its ornamental value comes from its dense, dark green leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

Menziesia × intermedia is often used in landscape design for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in challenging growing conditions.
