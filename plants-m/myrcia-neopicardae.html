Myrcia neopicardae

Myrcia neopicardae, commonly known as the Picard's myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. It produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Myrcia neopicardae is often found in moist, shaded areas and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
