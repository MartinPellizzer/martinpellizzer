Mariscus affinis

Mariscus affinis, commonly known as the common mariscus, is a small, herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or hedgerow environments. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually oval or lance-shaped. Its flowers are clustered in dense, terminal spikes and are typically pink or purple in color.

Mariscus affinis is known for its ability to thrive in shaded areas and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
