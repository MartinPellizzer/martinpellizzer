Myodocarpus fraxinifolius var. thiebautii

Myodocarpus fraxinifolius var. thiebautii, commonly known as the Tuberose Myodocarpus, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its distinctive tuberous roots and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a heart-shaped base with a velvety texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic blooms.
