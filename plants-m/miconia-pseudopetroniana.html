Miconia pseudopetroniana

Miconia pseudopetroniana, commonly known as the false petronia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom throughout the wet season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
