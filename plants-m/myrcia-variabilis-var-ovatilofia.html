Myrcia variabilis var. ovatilofia

Myrcia variabilis var. ovatilofia, commonly known as the broad-leaf myrcia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its elliptical to ovate leaves, which are usually glossy and dark green in color. Its flowers are typically pale yellow or white, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional medicinal uses.
