Melica pyramidalis subsp. arrecta

Melica pyramidalis subsp.

arrecta, commonly known as the upright grass or pyramidal grass, is a perennial species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like arrangement of spikelets, which are usually green to purplish in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators and soil stability.
