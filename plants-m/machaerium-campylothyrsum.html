Machaerium campylothyrsum

Machaerium campylothyrsum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved machaerium, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves with slender, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several elliptical seeds.

Machaerium campylothyrsum is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested or disturbed areas.
