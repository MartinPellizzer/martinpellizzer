Munnozia attenuata

Munnozia attenuata, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with a distinctive, narrow, grass-like foliage that grows close to the ground. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in late spring or early summer. Its root system is adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Munnozia attenuata is also known for its ability to bloom after periods of rainfall, making it a symbol of resilience in desert ecosystems.
