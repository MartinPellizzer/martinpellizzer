Magnolia cristalensis subsp. moana

Magnolia cristalensis subsp.

moana, commonly known as the Moana magnolia, is a rare and endemic species native to the tropical rainforests of New Caledonia. It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring.

The plant has simple, ovate leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stem. Its bark is smooth and grayish, often exuding a mild aromatic scent.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
