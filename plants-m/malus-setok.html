Malus setok

Malus setok, commonly known as the Japanese apple tree, is a deciduous species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and showy flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional Japanese cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Malus setok is also valued for its ability to withstand cold climates, making it a popular choice in temperate gardens.
