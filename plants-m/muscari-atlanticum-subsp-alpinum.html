Muscari atlanticum subsp. alpinum

Muscari atlanticum subsp.

alpinum, commonly known as the alpine grape hyacinth, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It produces clusters of small, bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is adapted to cold climates and is often found in mountainous areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
