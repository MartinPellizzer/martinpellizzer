Millettia sacleuxii

Millettia sacleuxii, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The tree produces woody pods that contain small, flat seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.

The plant also plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
