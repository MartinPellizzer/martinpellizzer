Melocactus salvador

Melocactus salvador, commonly known as the Salvadorian melon cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and smooth, greenish-gray skin. The plant produces a single, fleshy fruit that resembles a melon, which is edible and has a sweet, honey-like flavor. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and well-draining soil.

This cactus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and collections.
