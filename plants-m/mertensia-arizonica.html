Mertensia arizonica

Mertensia arizonica, commonly known as Arizona bluebells, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
