Melaleuca torquata

Melaleuca torquata, commonly known as the Narrow-leaf Paperbark, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a mottled pattern of green and white. The plant produces small, bottle-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Melaleuca torquata is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and salty sea breezes.
