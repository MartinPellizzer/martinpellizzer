Mikania hookeriana var. platyphylla

Mikania hookeriana var. platyphylla, commonly known as the broadleaf milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive broad base. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are borne on slender, branching stems. Its milky sap is a characteristic feature, containing latex that can cause skin irritation.

This species is often found in disturbed areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
