Machaerina rubiginosa subsp. crassa

Machaerina rubiginosa subsp.

crassa, commonly known as the thick-leaved flax, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its robust, woody stems and large, broad, leathery leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the stage of bloom. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
