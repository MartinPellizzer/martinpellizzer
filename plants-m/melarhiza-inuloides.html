Melarhiza inuloides

Melarhiza inuloides, commonly known as the New Zealand dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in damp, shaded environments such as forest floors and along stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in spring and early summer, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Melarhiza inuloides is often found in conservation areas due to its limited distribution and ecological significance.
