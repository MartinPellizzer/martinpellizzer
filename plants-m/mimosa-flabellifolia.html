Mimosa flabellifolia

Mimosa flabellifolia, commonly known as the fan-leaf mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in response to touch or light. Its root system is capable of nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil enrichment.

Mimosa flabellifolia is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its graceful appearance and unique foliage.
