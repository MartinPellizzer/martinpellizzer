Malus × floribunda var. spontanea

Malus × floribunda var. spontanea, commonly known as the wild crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its pyramidal shape, dense foliage, and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically red or yellow, which attract wildlife such as birds and squirrels. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a hardy and adaptable species.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a popular choice for landscaping and conservation efforts.
