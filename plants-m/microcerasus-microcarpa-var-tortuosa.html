Microcerasus microcarpa var. tortuosa

Microcerasus microcarpa var. tortuosa, commonly known as the twisted dwarf cherry, is a shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are often twisted or curled, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a decorative plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact size and distinctive foliage.
