Mucuna novoguineensis

Mucuna novoguineensis, commonly known as the Guinea velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and trifoliate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. It is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.

Additionally, it has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
