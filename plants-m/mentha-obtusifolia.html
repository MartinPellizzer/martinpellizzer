Mentha obtusifolia

Mentha obtusifolia, commonly known as woolly mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and square, erect stems. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Mentha obtusifolia is a member of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.
