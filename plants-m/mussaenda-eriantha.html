Mussaenda eriantha

Mussaenda eriantha, commonly known as the flame flower or velvet flower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the variety. The plant has smooth, often hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are usually dark green. Its flowers are borne on long, branching clusters and are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts.

Mussaenda eriantha is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
