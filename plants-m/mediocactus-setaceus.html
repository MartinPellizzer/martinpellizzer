Mediocactus setaceus

Mediocactus setaceus, commonly known as the needle cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and clusters of sharp, needle-like spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts and desert gardens.
