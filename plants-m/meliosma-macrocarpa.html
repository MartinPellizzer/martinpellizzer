Meliosma macrocarpa

Meliosma macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited meliosma, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and related trees. The plant is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves with prominent veins and smooth, dark green upper surfaces. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically greenish-yellow and appear in clusters, followed by small, berry-like fruits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant landscaping.
