Meconopsis integrifolia subsp. lijiangensis

Meconopsis integrifolia subsp.

lijiangensis, commonly known as the Yunnan poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the high-altitude regions of Yunnan Province in China. It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn.

The plant has palmate leaves that are typically lobed and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
