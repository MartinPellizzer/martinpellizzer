Malva pulchella

Malva pulchella, commonly known as the showy mallows, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces striking pink to purple flowers with five to seven petals. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and prairies. It is part of the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus.

Malva pulchella is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
