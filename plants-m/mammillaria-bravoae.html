Mammillaria bravoae

Mammillaria bravoae, commonly known as the Bravo's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a distinctive spiny appearance, featuring prominent areoles covered in white to yellowish spines. The plant typically has a green body with white or pale blue markings, and it produces yellow flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice for collectors and cactus enthusiasts.
