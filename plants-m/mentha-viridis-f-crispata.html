Mentha viridis f. crispata

Mentha viridis f. crispata, commonly known as the wrinkled mint, is a variant of the common mint plant.

It is characterized by its serrated, wavy-edged leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. This plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist, shaded environments. The leaves are typically a bright green color and have a strong, aromatic scent.

It is often used in herbal teas and as a culinary herb, similar to its parent species.
