Minuartia tenuifolia subvar. munbyi

Minuartia tenuifolia subvar. munbyi, commonly known as the Munby's sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arid and rocky habitats in southern Europe.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in early spring. The plant has slender, fleshy stems and narrow, linear leaves that are adapted to conserve water. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited to harsh environments, showcasing its resilience and ecological significance.
