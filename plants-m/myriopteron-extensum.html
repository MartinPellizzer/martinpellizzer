Myriopteron extensum

Myriopteron extensum, commonly known as the broadleaf fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that release spores for propagation.

Myriopteron extensum plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and contributing to forest biodiversity.
