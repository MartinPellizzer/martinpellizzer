Malva montana

Malva montana, commonly known as the woolly mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, woolly stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Malva montana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional herbal medicine.
