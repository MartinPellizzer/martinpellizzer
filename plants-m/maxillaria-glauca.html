Maxillaria glauca

Maxillaria glauca, commonly known as the blue orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet petals and sepals, which often have a waxy texture. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are known for their fragrant, sweet scent.

Maxillaria glauca is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique coloration.
