Mentha grandiflora

Mentha grandiflora, commonly known as the large-leaved mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses due to its refreshing menthol scent.

Mentha grandiflora is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
