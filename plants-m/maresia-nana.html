Maresia nana

Maresia nana, commonly known as the dwarf maresia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are adapted to store water, making it resilient to coastal salt spray and sandy soils. It thrives in full sun and is often found in dune environments where it plays a role in stabilizing sand.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is a popular choice for coastal gardens and conservation efforts.
