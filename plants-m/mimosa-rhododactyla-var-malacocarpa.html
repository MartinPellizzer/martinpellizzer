Mimosa rhododactyla var. malacocarpa

Mimosa rhododactyla var. malacocarpa, commonly known as the Malacocarpa Mimosa, is a unique variant of the Mimosa genus native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that fold shut when touched, a trait known as thigmonastic response. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
