Melastoma longibarbe

Melastoma longibarbe, commonly known as the long-bearded melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red and pink. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are often red when mature, containing several seeds.

Melastoma longibarbe is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky hillsides.
