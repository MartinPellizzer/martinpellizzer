Medinilla monantha var. papuana

Medinilla monantha var. papuana, commonly known as the Papuan Medinilla, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the lowland forests of Papua, Indonesia.

It features glossy, dark green, elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along its stems. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, waxy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation in warm climates.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its lush foliage and showy blooms.
