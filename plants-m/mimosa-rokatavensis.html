Mimosa rokatavensis

Mimosa rokatavensis, commonly known as the Madagascar mimosa, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a woody stem with a distinctive grayish bark and produces small, spherical pods that contain seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry forests.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
