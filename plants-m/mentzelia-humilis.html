Mentzelia humilis

Mentzelia humilis, commonly known as the common blazingstar, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, and it has a fibrous root system.

Mentzelia humilis is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for various pollinators.
