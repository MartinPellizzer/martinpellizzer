Mammillaria recurva

Mammillaria recurva, commonly known as the recurved hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring a dense covering of white, woolly hairs. The plant typically grows to a height of about 5 to 10 centimeters, with a rounded, segmented stem. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, emerging from the axils of the areoles.

It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
