Melilotus neglectus

Melilotus neglectus, commonly known as field melilot, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, oblong pods containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Botanically, it is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through its root nodules.
