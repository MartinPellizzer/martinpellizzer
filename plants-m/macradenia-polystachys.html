Macradenia polystachys

Macradenia polystachys, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers, which can be white, pink, or purple, bloom in late summer and early autumn.

Macradenia polystachys is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
