Mucuna platyplekta

Mucuna platyplekta, commonly known as the flat-leaf velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pods containing numerous elliptical seeds. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it useful in agricultural systems.

The plant's botanical features make it both ecologically significant and economically valuable.
