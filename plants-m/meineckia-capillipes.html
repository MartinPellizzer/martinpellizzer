Meineckia capillipes

Meineckia capillipes, commonly known as the slender feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves that are typically less than 1 millimeter in width. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the ends of the stems. Meineckia capillipes thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Its delicate appearance makes it a desirable ornamental plant in garden settings.
