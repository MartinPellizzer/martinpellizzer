Monochaetum villosum subsp. venezuelense

Monochaetum villosum subsp.

venezuelense, commonly known as the Venezuelan silky grass, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Venezuela. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy, silvery-green foliage.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or wetland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized collections.
