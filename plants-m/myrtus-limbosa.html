Myrtus limbosa

Myrtus limbosa, commonly known as the Cape myrtle, is a flowering shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but not particularly flavorful.

Myrtus limbosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal and sandy soils.
