Mentha × kerneri var. peracuta

Mentha × kerneri var. peracuta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf peppermint, is a hybrid perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers arranged in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
