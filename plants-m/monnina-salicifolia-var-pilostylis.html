Monnina salicifolia var. pilostylis

Monnina salicifolia var. pilostylis, commonly known as the silky willow herb, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, willow-like stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
