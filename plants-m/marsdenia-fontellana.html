Marsdenia fontellana

Marsdenia fontellana, commonly known as the Fontellana Marsdenia, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
