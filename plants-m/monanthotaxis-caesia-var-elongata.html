Monanthotaxis caesia var. elongata

Monanthotaxis caesia var. elongata, commonly known as the elongated monanthotaxis, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers with a subtle fragrance, typically blooming during the warmer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The varietal form 'elongata' distinguishes it by its longer, more slender growth habit compared to other varieties.
