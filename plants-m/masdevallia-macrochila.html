Masdevallia macrochila

Masdevallia macrochila, commonly known as the giant masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial or epiphytic orchid, often found on tree trunks or in shaded forest floors. Its leaves are usually broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its humid habitat.

The flowers are often pink or purple, with intricate patterns that attract specific pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.
