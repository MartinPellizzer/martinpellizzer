Mettenia

Mettenia, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to Africa.

These plants are characterized by their rosettes of velvety, waxy leaves and showy, tubular flowers that often come in shades of purple, pink, and white. Mettenia species are typically herbaceous, with some varieties growing as shrubs, and they thrive in well-drained soil and bright, indirect light. They are popular in horticulture for their ornamental value and ease of cultivation indoors.

The genus includes several species, such as Mettenia balfourii and Mettenia kirkii, which are prized for their vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.
