Magnolia fraseri

Magnolia fraseri, commonly known as the Fraser magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The tree has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
