Madronella undulata

Madronella undulata, commonly known as the wavy madronella, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive wavy or undulating leaf margins, which give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink.

Madronella undulata plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various insect species.
