Matthiola incana f. furcata

Matthiola incana f. furcata, commonly known as the branched stock, is a botanical variant of the stock plant.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its delicate appearance.
