Morus caudatifolia

Morus caudatifolia, commonly known as the long-tailed mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that often have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as mulberries, which are typically green when unripe and turn dark purple when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
