Mentha longifolia f. petiolata

Mentha longifolia f. petiolata, commonly known as the petiolate mint, is a variant of the long-stemmed mint species.

It is characterized by its elongated petioles, which are the stalks connecting the leaves to the stem. The leaves are typically narrow and have a serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters.

It is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves, which are used in herbal teas and culinary applications.
