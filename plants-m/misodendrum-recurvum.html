Misodendrum recurvum

Misodendrum recurvum, commonly known as the spiral vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, spiraling growth pattern, which allows it to wrap tightly around supporting structures. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a smooth texture.

Misodendrum recurvum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to create dense, lush foliage in gardens.
