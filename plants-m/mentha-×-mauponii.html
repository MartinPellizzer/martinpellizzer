Mentha × mauponii

Mentha × mauponii, commonly known as the hybrid mint, is a cross between Mentha viridis and Mentha suaveolens.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its square stems and opposite, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Mentha × mauponii is valued for its aromatic leaves and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
