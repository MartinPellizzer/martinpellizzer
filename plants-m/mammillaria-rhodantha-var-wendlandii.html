Mammillaria rhodantha var. wendlandii

Mammillaria rhodantha var. wendlandii, commonly known as the Wendland's mammillaria, is a compact cactus species native to Mexico.

It features a short, cylindrical stem covered in white to pale pink tubercles, which are modified stems that help reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well-suited for rock gardens and container cultivation due to its low water requirements.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
