Myristica lancifolia subsp. lancifolia

Myristica lancifolia subsp.

lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved nutmeg tree, is a member of the Myristicaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both ecological and economic purposes in its native habitats.
