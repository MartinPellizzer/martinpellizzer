Myristica tomentosa

Myristica tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces unique berries that contain a single seed, which is used to make the spice nutmeg. Its aromatic oils are also utilized in perfumes and traditional medicine.

Myristica tomentosa thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
