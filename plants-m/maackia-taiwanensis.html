Maackia taiwanensis

Maackia taiwanensis, commonly known as the Taiwan maackia, is a deciduous shrub native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the legume family. It is often found in forested areas and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
