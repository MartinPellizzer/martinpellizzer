Monardella breweri subsp. glandulifera

Monardella breweri subsp.

glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular monardella, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features slender, upright stems covered in glandular hairs, which give it its subspecies name. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture, while its flowers are clustered in terminal spikes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive, fragrant blooms.
