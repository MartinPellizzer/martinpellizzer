Myriophyllum spicatum subsp. montanum

Myriophyllum spicatum subsp.

montanum, commonly known as mountain watermilfoil, is a perennial aquatic plant native to North America. It typically grows in freshwater lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams, often forming dense underwater stands.

The plant has narrow, ribbon-like leaves arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, white to light blue flowers in late summer. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of water conditions, making it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive.

Its botanical structure supports a diverse array of aquatic life, contributing to the ecosystem's biodiversity.
