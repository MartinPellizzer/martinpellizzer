Medicago aculeata

Medicago aculeata, commonly known as goat's rue, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive spurred flowers that are typically violet or blue in color. The plant is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. It has a creeping growth habit with hairy stems and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Medicago aculeata is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a valuable plant in soil enrichment.
