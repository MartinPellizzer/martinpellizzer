Microlicia curralensis

Microlicia curralensis, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, red berries and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth texture with a fragrant scent.

The flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are clustered in inflorescences.
