Monodora claessensii

Monodora claessensii, commonly known as African tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing edible seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture-making.
