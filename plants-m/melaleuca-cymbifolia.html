Melaleuca cymbifolia

Melaleuca cymbifolia, commonly known as the bottlebrush tree, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that resemble bottle brushes, typically in shades of red, pink, or white. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and tropical gardens.

Melaleuca cymbifolia is also valued for its ability to tolerate salt spray, making it a popular choice in landscaping near the shoreline.
