Masdevallia prosartema

Masdevallia prosartema, commonly known as the roseate masdevallia, is a rare and striking orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its intricate, velvety petals that display a range of pink and purple hues, often resembling a rose in appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree bark in its natural habitat. Its flowers are highly fragrant, attracting specific pollinators such as moths and bees.

Due to its beauty and rarity, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
