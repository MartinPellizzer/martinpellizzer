Musa × paradisiaca var. formosana

Musa × paradisiaca var. formosana, commonly known as the Taiwan banana, is a cultivar of the banana plant native to Taiwan.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces clusters of fruit, known as hands, each containing several individual bananas. The fruit is typically sweet and starchy, with a flavor profile similar to other banana varieties.

It is widely cultivated for both its fruit and ornamental value.
