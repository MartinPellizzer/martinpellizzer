Mesosphaerum mocinoanum

Mesosphaerum mocinoanum, commonly known as the Mexican dwarf cycad, is a unique plant belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its compact, palm-like appearance with a short trunk and feather-like leaves. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, a trait typical of cycads, and it has a slow growth rate. Mesosphaerum mocinoanum is well adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in both horticulture and evolutionary botany.
