Musa × paradisiaca var. sanguinea

Musa × paradisiaca var. sanguinea, commonly known as the red banana, is a cultivar of the banana plant characterized by its striking red-colored pseudostems and leaves.

This variety is a hybrid of Musa acuminata and Musa balbisiana, and it is primarily cultivated for its ornamental value rather than for fruit production. The leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often display a deep red to purple hue, making it a popular choice for landscaping. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

While the fruit is edible, it is smaller and less sweet compared to other banana varieties.
