Molineria longiflora

Molineria longiflora, commonly known as the long-flower grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and elongated, narrow leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Molineria longiflora is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
