Mesochlaena polycarpos

Mesochlaena polycarpos, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, climbing stems that often trail along the ground or climb over other vegetation. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming in the early morning. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

Mesochlaena polycarpos thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to saline and coastal environments.
