Manisuris rugosa var. chapmanii

Manisuris rugosa var. chapmanii, commonly known as the Chapman's roughleaf, is a perennial shrub native to specific regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, textured leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its woody stems are often branched and can grow up to several meters in height.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal blooms.
