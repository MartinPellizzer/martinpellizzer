Monarda fistulosa f. russellii

Monarda fistulosa f. russellii, commonly known as the Russell's bee balm, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and emit a strong, minty scent when crushed.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
