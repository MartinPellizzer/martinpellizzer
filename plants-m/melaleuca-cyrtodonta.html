Melaleuca cyrtodonta

Melaleuca cyrtodonta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paperbark, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in coastal areas and heathlands. The plant is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that gives it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a silvery underside.

The flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences.
