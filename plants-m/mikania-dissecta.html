Mikania dissecta

Mikania dissecta, commonly known as the Mexican devil's ivy, is a vigorous climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and stream banks.

Mikania dissecta is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively, making it both a popular ornamental plant and a potential weed.
