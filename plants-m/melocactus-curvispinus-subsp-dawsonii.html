Melocactus curvispinus subsp. dawsonii

Melocactus curvispinus subsp.

dawsonii, commonly known as the Dawson's melocactus, is a cactus native to the arid regions of northern Mexico. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and dense covering of spines, which provide protection from herbivores and extreme weather.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its root system is shallow and adapted to absorb moisture from infrequent rains.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in arid gardens and rockeries.
