Malva × transcaucasica

Malva × transcaucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian mallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus, and is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and showy, pale pink to lavender flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. It is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in woodland edges.

Its fibrous roots and hairy stems are typical of the genus Malva, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
