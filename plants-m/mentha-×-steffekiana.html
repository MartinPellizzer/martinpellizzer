Mentha × steffekiana

Mentha × steffekiana, commonly known as peppermint, is a hybrid mint plant resulting from the cross between Mentha spicata (spearmint) and Mentha viridis (water mint).

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly fuzzy leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems. Mentha × steffekiana is a perennial herb native to Europe and has been widely cultivated for its essential oils and culinary uses.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
