Masdevallia berthae

Masdevallia berthae, commonly known as the Berthae's masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that can range from red to purple, with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as a small epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that help it absorb moisture from the humid air.

Masdevallia berthae is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and rarity in cultivation.
