Miconia pachyphylla

Miconia pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are thick and waxy, often with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers that are typically followed by bright red or orange berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it can become a problematic weed in certain regions.
