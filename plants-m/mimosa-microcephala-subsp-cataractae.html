Mimosa microcephala subsp. cataractae

Mimosa microcephala subsp.

cataractae, commonly known as the Brazilian mimosa, is a shrub native to Brazil. It is characterized by its compound leaves that fold shut when touched, a trait known as thigmonastic response.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
