Myrciaria disticha var. bahiensis

Myrciaria disticha var. bahiensis, commonly known as araçá-baiana, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Brazilian states of Bahia and Pernambuco.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark red berries that are rich in antioxidants. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical climates and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a pleasant, spicy scent when crushed.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent in local cuisine.
