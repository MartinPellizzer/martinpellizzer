Myrcianthes crebrifolia

Myrcianthes crebrifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a small evergreen tree native to the coastal regions of Brazil.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are a food source for various birds and mammals. Myrcianthes crebrifolia is often found in moist, tropical environments and is adapted to coastal conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora in its native habitat.
