Mila cereoides

Mila cereoides, commonly known as the silky milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Mila cereoides is part of the Apocynaceae family, which includes many species with milky sap.

It is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
