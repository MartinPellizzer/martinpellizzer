Megastachya maypurensis

Megastachya maypurensis, commonly known as the Maypurensis spikegrass, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and panicle-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
