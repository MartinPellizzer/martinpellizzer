Myrodia

Myrodia, commonly known as honey myrtle, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and includes several species that are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value. The plants are characterized by their aromatic leaves and showy, often white or pink flowers. Myrodia species typically grow as shrubs or small trees, and they thrive in well-drained soils.

They are also valued for their essential oils, which are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
