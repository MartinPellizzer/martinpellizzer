Myrica esculenta var. farquhariana

Myrica esculenta var. farquhariana, commonly known as the Farquharian wax myrtle, is a variant of the wax myrtle species native to specific regions of China.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in moist, acidic soils. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often coated with a whitish, waxy cuticle. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are followed by berries that are edible and used in traditional medicine.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
