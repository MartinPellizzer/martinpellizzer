Magnolia gelida

Magnolia gelida, commonly known as the Sierra magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners in suitable climates.
