Moluccella laniflora

Moluccella laniflora, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. It is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp habitats.

Moluccella laniflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
