Myrtus spiciflora

Myrtus spiciflora, commonly known as the spike myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in dense clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has leathery, evergreen leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Myrtus spiciflora thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and rocky environments.

Its aromatic properties make it a valuable plant in both horticulture and herbal remedies.
