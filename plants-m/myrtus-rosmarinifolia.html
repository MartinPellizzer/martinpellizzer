Myrtus rosmarinifolia

Myrtus rosmarinifolia, commonly known as rosemary myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant white flowers and dark blue or black berries that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant has leathery, evergreen leaves that are aromatic and contain essential oils. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value and aromatic properties make it a popular choice in horticulture.
