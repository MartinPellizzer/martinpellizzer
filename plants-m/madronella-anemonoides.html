Madronella anemonoides

Madronella anemonoides, commonly known as the yellow-flowered madronella, is a herbaceous perennial native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It features delicate, cup-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and compact growth habit.
