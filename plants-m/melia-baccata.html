Melia baccata

Melia baccata, commonly known as the Chinese white ash, is a deciduous tree native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by spiny, spherical fruits containing toxic seeds. Its roots are known to exude a milky sap that has been used in traditional medicine.

Melia baccata is often found in moist, well-drained soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
