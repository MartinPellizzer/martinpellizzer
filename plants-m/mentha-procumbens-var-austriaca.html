Mentha procumbens var. austriaca

Mentha procumbens var. austriaca, commonly known as the Austrian ground ivy, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It features prostrate stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats on the ground. The leaves are heart-shaped, with a slightly serrated margin, and are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or blue, arranged in clusters.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
