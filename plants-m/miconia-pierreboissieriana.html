Miconia pierreboissieriana

Miconia pierreboissieriana, commonly known as Pierreboissier's miconia, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are typically followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforest understories.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a problematic species in certain regions.
