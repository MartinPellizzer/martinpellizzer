Monstera falcifolia

Monstera falcifolia, commonly known as the Split-leaf Monstera, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that develop deep, irregular splits as they mature. The plant has a climbing habit and features aerial roots that help it attach to supports. Its foliage is typically a dark green with a glossy texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by orange berries.

Monstera falcifolia thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and humidity for optimal growth.
