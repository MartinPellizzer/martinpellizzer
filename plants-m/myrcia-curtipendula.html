Myrcia curtipendula

Myrcia curtipendula, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy red flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.
