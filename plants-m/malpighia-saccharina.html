Malpighia saccharina

Malpighia saccharina, commonly known as the Mexican hibiscus or scarlet hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers and elliptical, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Malpighia saccharina is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
