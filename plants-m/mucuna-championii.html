Mucuna championii

Mucuna championii, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces large, pod-like fruits that contain numerous flat, kidney-shaped seeds. It is often cultivated for its high-protein seeds, which are used in animal feed and traditional medicine.

Additionally, Mucuna championii plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing abilities.
