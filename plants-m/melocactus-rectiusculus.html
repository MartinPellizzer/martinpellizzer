Melocactus rectiusculus

Melocactus rectiusculus, commonly known as the straight-armed cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem with prominent, straight spines that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in summer. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stem.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
