Manihot tripartita var. vestita

Manihot tripartita var. vestita, commonly known as the African arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy surface, while the stems are upright and somewhat fibrous.

The tubers are often used as a starchy food source and can be prepared similarly to other root vegetables.
