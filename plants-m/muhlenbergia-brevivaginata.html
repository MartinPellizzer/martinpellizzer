Muhlenbergia brevivaginata

Muhlenbergia brevivaginata, commonly known as the narrowleaf muhlenbergia, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Muhlenbergia brevivaginata is also noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
