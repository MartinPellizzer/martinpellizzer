Masdevallia virgo-rosea

Masdevallia virgo-rosea, commonly known as the Roseate Virgin Masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, intricate flowers that display a range of pink and white hues, often with a contrasting greenish-blue lip. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized epiphyte, attaching itself to tree bark in its natural habitat. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

Due to its striking appearance and rarity, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
