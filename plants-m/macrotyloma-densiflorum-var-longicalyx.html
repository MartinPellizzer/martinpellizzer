Macrotyloma densiflorum var. longicalyx

Macrotyloma densiflorum var. longicalyx, commonly known as the long-flowered clusterbean, is a tropical legume characterized by its dense, clustered inflorescences and trifoliate leaves.

It produces small, yellow to orange flowers that are often clustered in dense racemes, giving it a striking appearance. The plant is typically shrub-like, with hairy stems and oblong, stipulate leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible seeds and ornamental value.

The species is native to parts of Africa and Asia, where it plays a role in both agriculture and traditional medicine.
