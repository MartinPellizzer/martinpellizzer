Mussaenda tenuiflora var. tenuiflora

Mussaenda tenuiflora var. tenuiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer. Its inflorescence is a cyme, and the flowers are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
