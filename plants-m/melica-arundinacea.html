Melica arundinacea

Melica arundinacea, commonly known as bamboo grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in moist or wet habitats such as marshes and stream banks.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soils, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
