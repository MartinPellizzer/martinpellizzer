Maurandya purpusii

Maurandya purpusii, commonly known as the Mexican trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its tubular, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with contrasting yellow or orange throats. The plant produces small, egg-shaped berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, easy-to-care-for ornamental plants.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail make it versatile for various landscaping applications.
