Masdevallia pileata

Masdevallia pileata, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, showy flowers that resemble small slippers, with a pinkish or white coloration. The plant typically grows in cool, humid environments, often in cloud forests, and thrives in shaded, well-drained conditions. Its leaves are usually dark green and strap-like, and it produces intricate, fragrant blooms throughout the year.

Masdevallia pileata is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
