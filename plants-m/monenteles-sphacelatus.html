Monenteles sphacelatus

Monenteles sphacelatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved waterwheel plant, is a semi-aquatic herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are often submerged or floating on the surface of water. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its roots form dense mats that help stabilize aquatic sediments and improve water quality.

Monenteles sphacelatus is typically found in slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and marshes.
