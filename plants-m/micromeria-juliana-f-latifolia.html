Micromeria juliana f. latifolia

Micromeria juliana f. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved micromeria, is a variant of the species Micromeria juliana, which is native to the Mediterranean region.

This plant is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the spring and early summer.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping in arid climates.
