Mapania pycnocephala subsp. fluviatilis

Mapania pycnocephala subsp.

fluviatilis, commonly known as the river mapania, is a aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in South America. It typically grows in slow-moving rivers and lakes, where it forms dense underwater mats.

The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that are often submerged, with small, inconspicuous flowers that appear near the water's surface. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in its native habitats.
