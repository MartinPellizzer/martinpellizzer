Micranthes californica subsp. fallax

Micranthes californica subsp.

fallax, commonly known as the California mountain mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States. It features dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has hairy, basal leaves that are spoon-shaped and often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is typically found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to the harsh conditions of its native environment.
