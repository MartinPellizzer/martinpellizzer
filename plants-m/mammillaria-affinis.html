Mammillaria affinis

Mammillaria affinis, commonly known as the Mexican snowball cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is covered in white, woolly spines that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne on short, erect stems. Mammillaria affinis thrives in well-draining soil and requires plenty of sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.

Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a favored plant among succulent enthusiasts.
