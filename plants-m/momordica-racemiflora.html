Momordica racemiflora

Momordica racemiflora, commonly known as the Chinese bitter melon, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and distinctive, spiny fruits that resemble small, green gourds. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are often clustered in racemes, giving it its scientific name. Its fruit is widely used in traditional medicine and cuisine, valued for its bitter taste and medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value.
