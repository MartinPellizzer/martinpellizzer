Myrcia australasiae

Myrcia australasiae, commonly known as the Australian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in coastal rainforests and moist, shaded areas. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are edible and contain seeds that are dispersed by animals.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting a variety of wildlife.
