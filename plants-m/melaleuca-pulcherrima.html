Melaleuca pulcherrima

Melaleuca pulcherrima, commonly known as the bottlebrush tree, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that resemble bottle brushes, typically in shades of red, pink, or white. The plant produces small, aromatic leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Melaleuca pulcherrima is also valued for its ability to withstand drought and poor soil conditions.
