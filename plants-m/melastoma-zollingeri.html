Melastoma zollingeri

Melastoma zollingeri, commonly known as the Zollinger's melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as tropical forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually opposite, elliptical, and have a rough texture.

Melastoma zollingeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
