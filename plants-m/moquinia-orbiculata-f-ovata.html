Moquinia orbiculata f. ovata

Moquinia orbiculata f. ovata, commonly known as the broad-leaf marsh grass, is a variant of the marsh clubmoss.

It is characterized by its ovate, broad leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. This plant is native to wetland habitats and thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its foliage is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic life.

The plant plays a vital role in maintaining the ecological balance of its environment.
