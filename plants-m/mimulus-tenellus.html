Mimulus tenellus

Mimulus tenellus, commonly known as the slender yellow monkeyflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as stream banks and meadows. The plant produces striking, bright yellow, spurred flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Mimulus tenellus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
