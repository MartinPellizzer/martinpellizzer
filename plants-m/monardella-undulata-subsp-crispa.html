Monardella undulata subsp. crispa

Monardella undulata subsp.

crispa, commonly known as curled beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and pink to purple tubular flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to full sun. It is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental appearance make it a valued plant in native gardens.
