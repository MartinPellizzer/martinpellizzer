Macaranga multiglandulosa

Macaranga multiglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular macaranga, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its alternate, simple leaves with prominent veins and numerous small glands. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically surrounded by bracts. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
