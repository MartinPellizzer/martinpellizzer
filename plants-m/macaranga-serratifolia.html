Macaranga serratifolia

Macaranga serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated mallow, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The leaves are palmately lobed with serrated edges, giving it its scientific name. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

The plant is often found in disturbed habitats and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability.
