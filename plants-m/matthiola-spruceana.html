Matthiola spruceana

Matthiola spruceana, commonly known as the Spruce's stock, is a species of flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in areas of Kazakhstan and the Tien Shan. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its leaves are linear and hairy, and it typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils.

Matthiola spruceana is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
