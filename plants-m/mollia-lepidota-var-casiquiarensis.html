Mollia lepidota var. casiquiarensis

Mollia lepidota var. casiquiarensis, commonly known as the Casiquiarensis Mollia, is a variant of the Mollia lepidota species native to specific regions in South America.

This plant is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology, featuring broad, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface. The leaves are typically arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant is valued for its aesthetic qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
