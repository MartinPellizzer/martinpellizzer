Mitostigma fiebrigii

Mitostigma fiebrigii, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, night-blooming flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers emit a sweet fragrance at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths.
