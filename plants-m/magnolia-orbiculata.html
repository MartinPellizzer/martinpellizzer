Magnolia orbiculata

Magnolia orbiculata, commonly known as the Japanese magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its large, showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The flowers are typically white or pale pink and are borne on short pedicels. The tree has broad, heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens and parks.
