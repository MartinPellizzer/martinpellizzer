Mareya micrantha

Mareya micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered mareya, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Araceae, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, with the showy spathe serving as an attractant for pollinators.
