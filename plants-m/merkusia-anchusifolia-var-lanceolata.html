Merkusia anchusifolia var. lanceolata

Merkusia anchusifolia var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate anchusa, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features basal leaves that are lanceolate in shape, with a rough, hairy texture, and stems that are often branched. The plant produces clusters of violet or purple flowers during the summer months, which are arranged in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden landscapes.
