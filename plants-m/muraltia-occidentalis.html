Muraltia occidentalis

Muraltia occidentalis, commonly known as the western muraltia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, with a slightly serrated edge.

Muraltia occidentalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
