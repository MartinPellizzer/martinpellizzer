Mesua nuda

Mesua nuda, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, pendant-like clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with long, slender leaflets. Its flowers are fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Mesua nuda is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
