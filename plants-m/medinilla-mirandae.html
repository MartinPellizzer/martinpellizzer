Medinilla mirandae

Medinilla mirandae, commonly known as the "Miranda's Medinilla," is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its colorful blooms.

Medinilla mirandae is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
