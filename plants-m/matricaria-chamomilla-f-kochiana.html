Matricaria chamomilla f. kochiana

Matricaria chamomilla f. kochiana, commonly known as Koch's chamomile, is a variant of the common chamomile plant.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, emitting a mild, sweet scent.

This variety is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and ornamental value in gardens.
