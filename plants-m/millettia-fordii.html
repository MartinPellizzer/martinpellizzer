Millettia fordii

Millettia fordii, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces long, pendulous clusters of violet to purple flowers in the spring, which are followed by flat, bean-like pods. Millettia fordii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral displays.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to various garden settings.
