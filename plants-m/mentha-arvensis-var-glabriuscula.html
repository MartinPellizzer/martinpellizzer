Mentha arvensis var. glabriuscula

Mentha arvensis var. glabriuscula, commonly known as narrow-leaf field mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, glabrous (smooth) leaves and square stems, typical of the mint family. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, making it valuable in traditional medicine and herbal teas.
