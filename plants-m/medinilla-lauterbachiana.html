Medinilla lauterbachiana

Medinilla lauterbachiana, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy green foliage and its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically blooms during the winter months, hence its common name. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade.

Medinilla lauterbachiana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and fragrance.
