Microchilus atwoodii

Microchilus atwoodii, commonly known as the Atwood's featherflower, is a small, flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and delicate, feathery inflorescences. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers, which are usually pale green to white, bloom in winter or early spring, attracting nectar-feeding insects.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and threatened species.
