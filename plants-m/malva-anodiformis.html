Malva anodiformis

Malva anodiformis, commonly known as the pink mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. The plant is characterized by its large, showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the stems are hairy, typical of many mallow species.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.
