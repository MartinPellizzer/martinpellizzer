Melica parodiana

Melica parodiana, commonly known as the Parodi's grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments, such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Melica parodiana is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fine texture and delicate appearance.
