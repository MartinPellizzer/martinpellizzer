Morinda tinctoria var. tomentosa

Morinda tinctoria var. tomentosa, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive velvety appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red fruits that contain flat, elongated seeds. It is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its potential for use in phytoremediation.
