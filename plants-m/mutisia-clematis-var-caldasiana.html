Mutisia clematis var. caldasiana

Mutisia clematis var. caldasiana, commonly known as the Caldasian mutisia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white to pale blue petals. It typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in cool, temperate climates.

Its botanical structure includes a hairy stem, alternate leaves, and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
