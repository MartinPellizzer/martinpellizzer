Macaranga venosa

Macaranga venosa, commonly known as the veined macaranga, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are often used in traditional practices, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits.

The species is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed habitats.
