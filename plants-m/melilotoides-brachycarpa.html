Melilotoides brachycarpa

Melilotoides brachycarpa, commonly known as the short-fruited melilot, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, typical of the legume family. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grassy areas or along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and potential ecological benefits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
