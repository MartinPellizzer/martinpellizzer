Murraya alata var. hainanensis

Murraya alata var. hainanensis, commonly known as the Hainan murraya, is a flowering plant native to southern China, particularly Hainan Island.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring aromatic, dark green leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in regions with warm climates.
