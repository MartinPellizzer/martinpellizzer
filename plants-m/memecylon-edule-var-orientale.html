Memecylon edule var. orientale

Memecylon edule var. orientale, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn from green to orange-red when mature. The fruit is rich in vitamin C and has been used in traditional medicine for its antioxidant properties.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
