Mentha wirzbickiana

Mentha wirzbickiana, commonly known as the Polish mint, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that give it a fresh, minty scent.

Mentha wirzbickiana is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its cooling and soothing properties.
