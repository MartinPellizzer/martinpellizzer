Melilotus messanensis

Melilotus messanensis, commonly known as the Messina melilot, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume pods and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and scrublands. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees.

Melilotus messanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
