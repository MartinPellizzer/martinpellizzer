Myzorrhiza tuberosa

Myzorrhiza tuberosa, commonly known as the African potato, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for medicinal purposes. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pink flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats.

The botanical features of Myzorrhiza tuberosa make it an important species in traditional medicine and horticulture.
