Melilotus officinalis

Melilotus officinalis, commonly known as sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flower heads. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Sweet clover is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant for agricultural purposes.

It also has a long history of use in traditional medicine and as a source of honey.
