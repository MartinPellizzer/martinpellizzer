Mystacinus

Mystacinus, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive flowering plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its lack of leaves, relying instead on its roots for nutrient absorption. The plant produces delicate, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the early morning. Mystacinus is known for its unique ability to grow epiphytically on trees, without soil.

Due to habitat destruction and its rarity, it is considered a critically endangered species.
