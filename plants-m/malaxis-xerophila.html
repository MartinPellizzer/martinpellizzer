Malaxis xerophila

Malaxis xerophila, commonly known as the dryland malaxis, is a rare orchid species native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with a short spur, typical of many orchid species. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or along rocky outcrops.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
