Medicago denticulata var. pentacycla

Medicago denticulata var. pentacycla, commonly known as the purple needlegrass, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, purplish-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves with three oval-shaped leaflets and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. Its root system forms symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
