Myrsine pachysandra

Myrsine pachysandra, commonly known as Japanese spurge, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to East Asia.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a waxy texture, and its leaves are typically oval to heart-shaped. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by red berries, which are a food source for birds. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form dense ground cover.

Myrsine pachysandra is also known for its toxic properties, making it important to handle with care.
