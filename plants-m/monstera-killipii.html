Monstera killipii

Monstera killipii, commonly known as the Split-leaf Philodendron, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, deeply lobed leaves that exhibit a distinctive split pattern as the plant matures. The leaves are typically a vibrant green with a waxy texture, and the plant prefers bright, indirect light and high humidity. It is a climbing plant that can grow quite large, often reaching several feet in height.

Monstera killipii is popular as a houseplant due to its striking foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
