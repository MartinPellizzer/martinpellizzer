Malus communis var. api

Malus communis var. api, commonly known as the apple tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its deciduous nature, with simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, white flowers in clusters, which are followed by fruits known as apples, typically containing five seeds. The fruit is fleshy and varies in color from green to red or yellow depending on the variety.

This plant is widely cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.
