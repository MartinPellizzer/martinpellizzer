Monelasmum brunneopurpureum

Monelasmum brunneopurpureum, commonly known as the purple-brown sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southeastern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland or swampy habitats, often forming dense clumps with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, which are usually greenish or purplish in color. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and establish itself in moist environments.

The species is notable for its adaptability to waterlogged soils and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
