Moniera bicolor

Moniera bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored fern, is a unique species characterized by its striking bi-colored fronds.

The plant exhibits a dichotomous branching pattern, with each frond splitting into two equal parts. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and display a vivid contrast between green and silver or white hues. Moniera bicolor thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate rainforests.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in ecological systems by providing habitat for small invertebrates.
