Myrianthus holstii var. quinquesectus

Myrianthus holstii var. quinquesectus, commonly known as the five-lobed myrianthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves with five distinct lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
