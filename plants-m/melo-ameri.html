Melo ameri

Melo ameri, commonly known as the American persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant produces orange to reddish fruits that are typically harvested in the fall. Its fruit is valued for its sweet, honey-like flavor and is often used in culinary applications.
