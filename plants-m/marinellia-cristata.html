Marinellia cristata

Marinellia cristata, commonly known as the cristate marsh orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in marshy or swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a distinctive, branched inflorescence.

Marinellia cristata is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological balance of its wetland habitats.
