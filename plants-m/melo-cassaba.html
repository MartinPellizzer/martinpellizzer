Melo cassaba

Melo cassaba, commonly known as the casaba melon, is a type of Cucurbitaceae plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its pale yellow, slightly ribbed rind and sweet, juicy, pale green flesh. The plant produces large, oblong fruits that are typically harvested when they reach a mature yellow color. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is often used in fresh consumption or as a decorative element in gardens.
