Manihot cleomifolia

Manihot cleomifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it an invasive species in some areas. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

The plant has been studied for its potential in biofuel production due to its high biomass yield.
