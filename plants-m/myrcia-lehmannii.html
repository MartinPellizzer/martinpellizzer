Myrcia lehmannii

Myrcia lehmannii, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Myrcia lehmannii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
