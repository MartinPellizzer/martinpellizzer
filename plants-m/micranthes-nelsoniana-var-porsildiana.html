Micranthes nelsoniana var. porsildiana

Micranthes nelsoniana var. porsildiana, commonly known as the Nelson's micranthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of western North America.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late summer. The plant has a low-growing habit with narrow, grass-like leaves that are densely arranged at the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and ecological role in high-altitude ecosystems.
