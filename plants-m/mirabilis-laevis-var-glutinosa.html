Mirabilis laevis var. glutinosa

Mirabilis laevis var. glutinosa, commonly known as sticky four o'clock, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that open in the late afternoon and close at dawn. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with a sticky substance, which gives the plant its common name. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
