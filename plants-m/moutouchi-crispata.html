Moutouchi crispata

Moutouchi crispata, commonly known as the curly willow or mountain willow, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its slender, flexible stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
