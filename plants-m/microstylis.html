Microstylis

Microstylis, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and flowering structures.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are often inconspicuous. Microstylis species are usually perennial and have rhizomatous growth habits, allowing them to spread vegetatively.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
