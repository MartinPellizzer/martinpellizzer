Masdevallia gemmata

Masdevallia gemmata, commonly known as the gemmata masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often brightly colored flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically produces intricate, spurred petals and a unique, elongated lip that aids in pollination. It thrives in cool, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and ease of care.
