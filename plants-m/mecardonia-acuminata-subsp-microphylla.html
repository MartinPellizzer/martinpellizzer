Mecardonia acuminata subsp. microphylla

Mecardonia acuminata subsp.

microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved mecardonia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
