Megastachya brizoides

Megastachya brizoides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, blue-purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and a fibrous root system, which helps it thrive in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and woodland edges, where it forms dense colonies.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
