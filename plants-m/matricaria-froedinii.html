Matricaria froedinii

Matricaria froedinii, commonly known as the Froedlin's chamomile, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flower heads and finely divided, aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in the summer months, producing a subtle, citrus-like scent.

Matricaria froedinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
