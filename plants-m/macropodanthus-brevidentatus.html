Macropodanthus brevidentatus

Macropodanthus brevidentatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved desert bell, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is occasionally cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
