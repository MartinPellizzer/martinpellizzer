Mesembryanthemum vulpinum

Mesembryanthemum vulpinum, commonly known as the "fox's tail" or "red sand mat," is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, cylindrical leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, especially in sunlight. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, a characteristic of many mesembs. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid environments.

Mesembryanthemum vulpinum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
