Mitrephora basilanensis

Mitrephora basilanensis, commonly known as the Philippine mitrephora, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
