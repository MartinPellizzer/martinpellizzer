Mesembryanthemum nationae

Mesembryanthemum nationae, commonly known as the Namib Desert ice plant, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Namibia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the early morning, often during the rainy season. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to desert conditions.

Mesembryanthemum nationae is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
