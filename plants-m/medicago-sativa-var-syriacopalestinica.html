Medicago sativa var. syriacopalestinica

Medicago sativa var. syriacopalestinica, commonly known as Syrian clover, is a variant of the common medick, characterized by its low-growing habit and small, clustered flowers.

It is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region, including parts of Syria and Palestine, and thrives in well-drained soils. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces yellow or pink flowers that attract pollinators. It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in forage and erosion control.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
