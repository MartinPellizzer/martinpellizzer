Madia hirsuta

Madia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy madia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Madia hirsuta is often found in dry, open habitats like grasslands and rocky slopes.
