Miquelia azurea

Miquelia azurea, commonly known as the blue miquelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers, which are typically borne in clusters along the stems. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem. Miquelia azurea thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
