Matucana tuberculata

Matucana tuberculata, commonly known as the Tuberculate Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in small, rounded tubercles. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual element to its otherwise spiny appearance. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Matucana tuberculata is well adapted to low rainfall and high sunlight, making it a resilient plant in desert ecosystems.
