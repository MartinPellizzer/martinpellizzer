Macrozamia tridentata var. secunda

Macrozamia tridentata var. secunda, commonly known as the slender cycad, is a slow-growing evergreen plant native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, feather-like leaves that can reach lengths of up to 2 meters. The plant produces a large, cone-like structure that contains both male and female flowers, which are pollinated by insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water once established.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
