Mapouria macrochlamys

Mapouria macrochlamys, commonly known as the giant soursop, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy on top. Its flowers are clustered in panicles and are typically white or pale yellow, followed by the fruit, which is a large, spiny, green berry.

The botanical features of Mapouria macrochlamys make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
