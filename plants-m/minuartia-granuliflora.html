Minuartia granuliflora

Minuartia granuliflora, commonly known as the granulate rockcress, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to rocky and arid environments.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and delicate, white or pink flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous or coastal regions. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover in xeriscaping.
