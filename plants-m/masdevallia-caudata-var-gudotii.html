Masdevallia caudata var. gudotii

Masdevallia caudata var. gudotii, commonly known as the Gudot's tail orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs and showy, colorful flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in rocky or forested areas. Its leaves are strap-like and grass-like, while its inflorescence is a raceme of vibrant, spurred flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its striking appearance.
