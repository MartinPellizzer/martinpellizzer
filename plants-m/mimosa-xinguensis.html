Mimosa xinguensis

Mimosa xinguensis, commonly known as the Xingu mimosa, is a deciduous shrub native to the Brazilian Amazon.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched, a trait known as thigmonastic response. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in riparian forests.

Its flowers are small and yellow, producing nectar that attracts various pollinators.
