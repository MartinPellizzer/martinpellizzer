Magnolia grandiflora var. angustifolia

Magnolia grandiflora var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and large, showy white or pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a smooth, gray bark that becomes fissured with age, and its growth habit is typically upright and pyramidal. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental tree in gardens and landscapes.

The variety is noted for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
