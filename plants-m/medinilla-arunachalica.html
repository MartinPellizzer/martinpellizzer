Medinilla arunachalica

Medinilla arunachalica, commonly known as the Arunachal medinilla, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Himalayas, specifically in Arunachal Pradesh.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom during the winter season. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth texture with a glossy appearance.

Medinilla arunachalica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
