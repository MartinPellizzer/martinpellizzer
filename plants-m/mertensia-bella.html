Mertensia bella

Mertensia bella, commonly known as Virginia bluebell, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to deep violet. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and has heart-shaped, hairy leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Mertensia bella is a member of the Boraginaceae family, characterized by its blue flowers and hairy stems.
