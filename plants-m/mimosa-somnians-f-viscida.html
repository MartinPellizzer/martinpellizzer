Mimosa somnians f. viscida

Mimosa somnians f. viscida, commonly known as the sticky mimosa, is a variant of the native Australian plant Mimosa somnians.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and leaves that are covered in fine, sticky hairs. The plant produces small, yellow, ball-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. It is typically found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and growth habits, it is often cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
