Mimulus kernensis

Mimulus kernensis, commonly known as the Kern's monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers, which are typically spurred at the back, a feature common in the Mimulus genus. The plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in streambanks and meadows. It has heart-shaped leaves and is a perennial herb that blooms from spring to early summer.

Mimulus kernensis plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
