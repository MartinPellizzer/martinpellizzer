Muscari albiflorum

Muscari albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered grape hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces dense clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge from a bulbous base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Muscari albiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
