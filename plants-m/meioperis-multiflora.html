Meioperis multiflora

Meioperis multiflora, commonly known as the multibloom tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are typically ovate or elliptical, with a smooth texture and a glossy surface.

Meioperis multiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
