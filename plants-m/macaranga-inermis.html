Macaranga inermis

Macaranga inermis, commonly known as the smooth macaranga, is a tropical plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsules containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas, it plays a significant role in ecological succession.
