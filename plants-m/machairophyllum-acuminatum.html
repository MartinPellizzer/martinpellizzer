Machairophyllum acuminatum

Machairophyllum acuminatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pincushion cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a compact, spiny stem that resembles a pincushion, with small, narrow leaves arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by small, berry-like fruits. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions with minimal water requirements.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
