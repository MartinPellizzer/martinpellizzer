Mentha longifolia subsp. fuliginosa

Mentha longifolia subsp.

fuliginosa, commonly known as the black mint, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its dark purple to black stems and leaves that are often hairy and have a strong, aromatic scent.

The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that appear in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its essential oils contain menthol and other compounds, making it useful in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.
