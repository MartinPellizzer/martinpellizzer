Mitracarpus linearifolius

Mitracarpus linearifolius, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing habit and trifoliate leaves with linear leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as a legume for its nutritional value.

The botanical features of Mitracarpus linearifolius make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
