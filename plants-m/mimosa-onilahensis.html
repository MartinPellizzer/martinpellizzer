Mimosa onilahensis

Mimosa onilahensis, commonly known as the Socotra mimosa, is a flowering plant native to the island of Socotra.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to the arid and rocky environment of Socotra, showcasing remarkable resilience to drought. It plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
