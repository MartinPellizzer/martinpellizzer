Mollugo pumila

Mollugo pumila, commonly known as carpetweed, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Molluginaceae family and is characterized by its small, fleshy, ovate leaves and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in lawns, gardens, and along roadsides. It spreads through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common and persistent weed.

Its rapid growth and ability to colonize various environments contribute to its widespread distribution.
