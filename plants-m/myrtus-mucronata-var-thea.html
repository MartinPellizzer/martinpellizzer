Myrtus mucronata var. thea

Myrtus mucronata var. thea, commonly known as the Cape myrtle, is a flowering shrub native to South Africa.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in winter, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and a pleasant, aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its compact growth habit and seasonal floral display.
