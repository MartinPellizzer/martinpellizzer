Morus mesozygia var. lactea

Morus mesozygia var. lactea, commonly known as the white mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with white veins. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as samaras, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its leaves, which are used in sericulture.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
