Manilkara parvifolia

Manilkara parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved spondias, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical fruits that turn from green to yellow or orange when mature. Its latex, which is milky and sticky, is a distinguishing feature and has been used traditionally for various purposes.

The plant is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
