Meconopsis taylorii

Meconopsis taylorii, commonly known as the Taylor's monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the alpine regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers, which are arranged in a helmet-like shape, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant features palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or woodland habitats.
