Macrolobium isopetalum

Macrolobium isopetalum, commonly known as the white sand violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, violet-blue flowers with five petals that are slightly notched at the tips. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often found in sandy, well-drained soils. Its growth habit is low and spreading, making it a suitable ground cover in woodland gardens.

It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
