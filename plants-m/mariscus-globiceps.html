Mariscus globiceps

Mariscus globiceps, commonly known as the globe-headed mariscus, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its globular, compact growth habit, with dense foliage that forms a rounded shape. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a delicate ornamental quality. Its leaves are typically small, fleshy, and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it retain moisture in arid conditions.

Mariscus globiceps is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal.
