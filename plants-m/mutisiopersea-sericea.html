Mutisiopersea sericea

Mutisiopersea sericea, commonly known as the silky mango, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, silvery-gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by oval, edible fruits with a sweet, fibrous pulp. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The silky mango is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is used in local cuisines.
