Mariscus indecorus var. inflatus

Mariscus indecorus var. inflatus, commonly known as the inflated marsh mallow, is a unique variant of the marsh mallow plant.

It is characterized by its swollen, fleshy stems that store water, adapting it to wetland environments. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a soft, velvety texture.

This variant is often found in marshy areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
