Mimulus ringens var. minthodes

Mimulus ringens var. minthodes, commonly known as the small yellow monkeyflower, is a delicate annual herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, hairy stems that grow from 10 to 30 centimeters in height, often with a trailing or creeping habit. The plant produces showy, spurred yellow flowers with a distinctive bilabiate structure, typical of the Plantaginaceae family. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and covered in fine pubescence.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, streambanks, and disturbed sites.
