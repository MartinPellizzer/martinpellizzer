Millettia theuszii

Millettia theuszii, commonly known as the Chinese ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by pods containing flattened, winged seeds.

Its leaves are compound, with multiple pairs of oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils in temperate to subtropical climates.
