Maieta argentea

Maieta argentea, commonly known as silver mayweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features finely divided, silver-tinged leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Maieta argentea is often found in meadows and along roadsides, where it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils.
