Mimosa pseudoradula var. anapolitana

Mimosa pseudoradula var. anapolitana, commonly known as the Anapolitan mimosa, is a variant of the mimosa plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that fold shut when touched, a trait known as thigmonastic response. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and role in local ecosystems.
