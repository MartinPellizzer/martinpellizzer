Malus microcarpa sempervirens

Malus microcarpa sempervirens, commonly known as the evergreen crabapple, is a small deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces clusters of white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by small, red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
