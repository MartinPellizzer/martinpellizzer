Miconia amilcariana

Miconia amilcariana, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that give way to distinctive, star-shaped fruits resembling miniature pineapples. These fruits are edible, though they are typically consumed raw or used in local culinary traditions.

Miconia amilcariana is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive in certain regions.
