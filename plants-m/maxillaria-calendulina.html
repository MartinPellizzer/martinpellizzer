Maxillaria calendulina

Maxillaria calendulina, commonly known as the Orange-Flowered Orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb that supports its growth during dry periods. The plant produces showy, orange-red flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Maxillaria calendulina is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
