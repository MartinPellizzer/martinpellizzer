Myrcia browniana

Myrcia browniana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It is often found in moist, tropical environments and is known for its aromatic properties.

Myrcia browniana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
