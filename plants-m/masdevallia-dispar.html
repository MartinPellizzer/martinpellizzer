Masdevallia dispar

Masdevallia dispar, commonly known as the Disparate Masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, often asymmetrical flowers that display a range of colors, including pink, white, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that help it absorb moisture from the air.

Masdevallia dispar is valued in horticulture for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
