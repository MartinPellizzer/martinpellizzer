Megema corniculata

Megema corniculata, commonly known as the horned bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular flowers that resemble small horns, typically ranging in color from blue to purple. The plant grows in moist, shady environments, often in forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its overall rugged appearance.

Megema corniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
