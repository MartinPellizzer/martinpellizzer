Melidora pellucida

Melidora pellucida, commonly known as the glassy melidora, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that allow light to pass through, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom during the early morning hours. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
