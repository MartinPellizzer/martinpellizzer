Machaerium seemannii

Machaerium seemannii, commonly known as the African grass or false signal grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, terminal spikes. It is often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites, thriving in full sun.

Machaerium seemannii is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
