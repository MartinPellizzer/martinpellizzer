Magnolia ottoi

Magnolia ottoi, commonly known as the dwarf magnolia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 feet and features glossy, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its flowers are usually pink to white and are borne in clusters at the base of the branches.

Magnolia ottoi thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
