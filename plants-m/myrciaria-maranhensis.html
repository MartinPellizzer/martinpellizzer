Myrciaria maranhensis

Myrciaria maranhensis, commonly known as Brazilian blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface and a strong aromatic scent. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

The fruit is often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine for its nutritional and medicinal properties.
