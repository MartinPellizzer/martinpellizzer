Myginda uragoga subsp. glabra

Myginda uragoga subsp.

glabra, commonly known as the smooth myginda, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves that are typically elliptical in shape.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
