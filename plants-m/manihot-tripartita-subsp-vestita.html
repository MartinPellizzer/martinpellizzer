Manihot tripartita subsp. vestita

Manihot tripartita subsp.

vestita, commonly known as the velvet arrowleaf, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its distinctive, velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticultural and ecological studies.
