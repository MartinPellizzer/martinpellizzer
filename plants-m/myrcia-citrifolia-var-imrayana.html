Myrcia citrifolia var. imrayana

Myrcia citrifolia var. imrayana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It features glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible but often have a strong, spicy flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal regions and disturbed soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
