Miconia racemosa var. lanceolata

Miconia racemosa var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate miconia, is a member of the Melastomataceae family.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are typically lanceolate in shape, with a glossy upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that are often surrounded by showy bracts. It is native to tropical regions of South America and is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets.

Due to its invasive potential in some areas, it is often considered a problematic species in ecological contexts.
