Miconia hypoglauca

Miconia hypoglauca, commonly known as the silver miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that often have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that are typically followed by distinctive, round, orange-red berries. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats within tropical rainforests.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is considered a problematic species in some regions.
