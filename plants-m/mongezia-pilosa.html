Mongezia pilosa

Mongezia pilosa, commonly known as the hairy mongezia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
