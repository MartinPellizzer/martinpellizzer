Meringium acanthoides

Meringium acanthoides, commonly known as the spiny meringia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, glandular leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, oval fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Meringium acanthoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its distinctive appearance and resilience.
