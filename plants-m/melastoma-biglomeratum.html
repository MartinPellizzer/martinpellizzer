Melastoma biglomeratum

Melastoma biglomeratum, commonly known as the clustered melastoma, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with prominent veins and produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink or red. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds.

It is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
