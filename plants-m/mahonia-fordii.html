Mahonia fordii

Mahonia fordii, commonly known as the Chinese mahonia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features compound leaves that are dark green and leathery, with spiny margins. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in upright panicles. These flowers are followed by blue-black berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans.

Mahonia fordii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and hardy nature.
