Maxillaria picta-major

Maxillaria picta-major, commonly known as the picta-major orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with pseudobulbs that support its growth and storage of nutrients. The plant features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. Its leaves are usually dark green and elliptical, arranged in a basal rosette.

Maxillaria picta-major thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
