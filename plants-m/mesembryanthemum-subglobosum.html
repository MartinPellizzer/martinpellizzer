Mesembryanthemum subglobosum

Mesembryanthemum subglobosum, commonly known as the globe-shaped ice plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, rounded leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically during the cooler months. Its growth habit is compact, with a rosette formation that helps minimize water loss.

It is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
