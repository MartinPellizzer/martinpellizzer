Muscari coeruleum

Muscari coeruleum, commonly known as the blue musk grape or bluebells, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge from a bulbous base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

Muscari coeruleum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow.
