Maxillaria quadrata

Maxillaria quadrata, commonly known as the quadrata orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its flowers are typically arranged in a raceme and bloom during the warmer months.

Maxillaria quadrata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
