Myrcia sericea

Myrcia sericea, commonly known as silky myrtle, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
