Myrtillocactus schenckii

Myrtillocactus schenckii, commonly known as the blue cactus, is a unique cactus species native to the highlands of Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray spines and spherical, segmented stems that store water. The plant produces small, violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid conditions.

Myrtillocactus schenckii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry environments.
