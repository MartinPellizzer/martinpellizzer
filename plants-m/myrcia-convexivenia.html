Myrcia convexivenia

Myrcia convexivenia, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dark seeds.

Myrcia convexivenia is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its aromatic properties.
