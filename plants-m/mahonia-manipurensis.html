Mahonia manipurensis

Mahonia manipurensis, commonly known as the Manipur barberry, is a shrub native to the foothills of the Western Ghats in India.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Mahonia manipurensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
