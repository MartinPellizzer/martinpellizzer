Malesherbia splendens

Malesherbia splendens, commonly known as the golden bower, is a striking climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has woody stems that can grow up to several meters in length, often twining around supports. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Malesherbia splendens thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
