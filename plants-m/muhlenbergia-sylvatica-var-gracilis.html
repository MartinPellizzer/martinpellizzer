Muhlenbergia sylvatica var. gracilis

Muhlenbergia sylvatica var. gracilis, commonly known as slender wood oats, is a grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and delicate, panicle-like inflorescences that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its slender, upright growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.

It plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects, due to its seed production.
