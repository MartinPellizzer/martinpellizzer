Myrceugenia apiculata var. australis

Myrceugenia apiculata var. australis, commonly known as the southern myrtle, is a species native to southern South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are oval in shape and arranged alternately along the stems. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in inflorescences, while the fruit is a small, woody capsule containing several seeds.

The plant thrives in humid, temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soils.
