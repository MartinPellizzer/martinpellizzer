Mentha longifolia var. brassoensis

Mentha longifolia var. brassoensis, commonly known as the Brassoensis mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers arranged in whorled clusters along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
