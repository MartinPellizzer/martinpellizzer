Mentha × elata

Mentha × elata, commonly known as marsh mint, is a hybrid plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the upper stems. It thrives in moist, wet environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows.

Mentha × elata is often used in herbal medicine for its purported calming and digestive properties.
