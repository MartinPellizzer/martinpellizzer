Mangifera rumphii

Mangifera rumphii, commonly known as the Java mango, is a species of tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and fragrant, yellow-orange fruits. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a dense canopy that provides shade in its natural habitat. Its fruits are valued for their sweet, aromatic pulp and are often used in local cuisines.

The plant thrives in humid climates with well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental and fruit-bearing species in tropical regions.
