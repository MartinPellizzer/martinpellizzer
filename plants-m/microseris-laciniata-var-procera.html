Microseris laciniata var. procera

Microseris laciniata var. procera, commonly known as the tall cutleaf salsify, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and large, lobed leaves that are deeply divided. The plant produces tall, spindly stems that bear yellow flowers in late summer. Its root system is fibrous, and the plant is often found in dry, rocky soils.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops.
