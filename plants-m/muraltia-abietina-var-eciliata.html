Muraltia abietina var. eciliata

Muraltia abietina var. eciliata, commonly known as the white muraltia, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, white, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, round, edible fruits that are often used in traditional local cuisines. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to the unique microclimate of the Canary Islands.

Its foliage is evergreen, with glossy, dark green leaves that provide ornamental value.
