Macrodiscus gnaphalantha

Macrodiscus gnaphalantha, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of grasses. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies, and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a subject of study in plant ecology.
