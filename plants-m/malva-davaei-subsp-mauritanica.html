Malva davaei subsp. mauritanica

Malva davaei subsp.

mauritanica, commonly known as the Mauritanian mallows, is a perennial herb native to North Africa. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus.

The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces showy, pink to purple flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

The subspecies is characterized by its compact growth and is often used in traditional medicine.
