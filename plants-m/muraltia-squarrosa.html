Muraltia squarrosa

Muraltia squarrosa, commonly known as the scarlet coral tree, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of Africa, Madagascar, and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and clusters of small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, round fruits that turn from green to red as they mature. Muraltia squarrosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
