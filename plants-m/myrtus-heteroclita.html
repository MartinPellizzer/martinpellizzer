Myrtus heteroclita

Myrtus heteroclita, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark blue, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Myrtus heteroclita is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and historical medicinal uses.
