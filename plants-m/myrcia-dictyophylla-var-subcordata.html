Myrcia dictyophylla var. subcordata

Myrcia dictyophylla var. subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf myrtle, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by dark purple berries.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
