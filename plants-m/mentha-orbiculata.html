Mentha orbiculata

Mentha orbiculata, commonly known as round-leaf mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

It is characterized by its rounded, hairy leaves that are often aromatic and emit a strong mint scent. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Mentha orbiculata is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies for digestive and respiratory ailments.
