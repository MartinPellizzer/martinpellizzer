Minuartia nifensis

Minuartia nifensis, commonly known as the creeping rockcress, is a small, mat-forming flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, rounded leaves and delicate yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to rocky or sandy soils in alpine and arctic regions, it is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Its botanical features make it an important component of ecosystems in its native habitats.
