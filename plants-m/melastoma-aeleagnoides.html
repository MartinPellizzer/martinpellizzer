Melastoma aeleagnoides

Melastoma aeleagnoides, commonly known as the red-rooted melastoma, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Melastoma aeleagnoides is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
