Medicago lunata

Medicago lunata, commonly known as goat's rue, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces cylindrical, yellowish pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Medicago lunata is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
