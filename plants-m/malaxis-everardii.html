Malaxis everardii

Malaxis everardii, commonly known as the Everard's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a unique morphology, featuring a rhizomatous root system and a single, erect stem. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in a raceme, often with a distinctive lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is typically found in forest understories.

Malaxis everardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
