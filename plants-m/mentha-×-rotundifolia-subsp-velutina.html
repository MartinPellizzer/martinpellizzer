Mentha × rotundifolia subsp. velutina

Mentha × rotundifolia subsp.

velutina, commonly known as woolly mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its rounded, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

Woolly mint is valued for its aromatic leaves and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
