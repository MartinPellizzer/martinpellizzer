Mimulus × burnetii

Mimulus × burnetii, commonly known as the yellow monkeyflower, is a hybrid species within the genus Mimulus, native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is herbaceous, with erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in streambanks and woodland edges.

Mimulus × burnetii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
