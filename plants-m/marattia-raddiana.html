Marattia raddiana

Marattia raddiana, commonly known as the Raddiana fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Marattiaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, compound fronds. The plant has a distinctive crown of broad, feather-like leaves that can reach several meters in length. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The fronds are typically evergreen and can remain on the plant for many years, contributing to its ornamental value.
