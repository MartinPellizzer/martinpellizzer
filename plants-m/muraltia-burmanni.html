Muraltia burmanni

Muraltia burmanni, commonly known as the Burmann's muraltia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, showy flowers. The plant produces small, round pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
