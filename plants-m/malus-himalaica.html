Malus himalaica

Malus himalaica, commonly known as the Himalayan apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly the Himalayas, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, tart apples that are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine. Its flowers are white and fragrant, appearing in early spring before the leaves emerge.

The tree is known for its hardiness and ability to withstand cold temperatures, making it well-suited to high-altitude environments.
