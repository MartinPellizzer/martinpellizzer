Mespilus korolkowii

Mespilus korolkowii, commonly known as the Korolkow's medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, white to pinkish flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that ripen in autumn, though they are often sour and not typically consumed by humans.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
