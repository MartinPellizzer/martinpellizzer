Malus prunifolia f. pendula

Malus prunifolia f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping apple, is a unique cultivar of the apple tree characterized by its cascading branches that droop gracefully.

This ornamental plant features small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible red or yellow fruits. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often planted in gardens for aesthetic purposes.
