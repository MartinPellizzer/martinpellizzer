Myrcia adenophylla

Myrcia adenophylla, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. Myrcia adenophylla thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive fragrance and potential therapeutic uses.
