Marlierea spruceana var. latifolia

Marlierea spruceana var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf marlierea, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in panicles, and it produces small, fleshy fruits.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage.
