Menendezia urbanii

Menendezia urbanii, commonly known as the Mexican firebush, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it drought-tolerant and resilient to harsh conditions. Its leaves are typically narrow and leathery, providing efficient water retention.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
