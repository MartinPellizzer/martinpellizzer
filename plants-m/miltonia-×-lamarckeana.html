Miltonia × lamarckeana

Miltonia × lamarckeana, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper, is a hybrid orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety pink petals and a distinctive pouch-like lip that resembles a slipper. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, often in clusters, and are highly prized for their beauty and fragrance.

The species is relatively easy to cultivate, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
