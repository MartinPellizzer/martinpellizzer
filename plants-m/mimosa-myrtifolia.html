Mimosa myrtifolia

Mimosa myrtifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf mimosa, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellow, spherical flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Mimosa myrtifolia is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
