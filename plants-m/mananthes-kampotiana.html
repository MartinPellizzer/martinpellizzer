Mananthes kampotiana

Mananthes kampotiana, commonly known as the Kampot rice plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It is often cultivated for its aromatic grains, which are prized for their unique flavor and aroma.

Due to its ecological and agricultural significance, it plays an important role in the local biodiversity and traditional farming practices of the region.
