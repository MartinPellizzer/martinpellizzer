Mesembryanthemum grandifolium

Mesembryanthemum grandifolium, commonly known as the large-leaved ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, known for its adaptive features to dry climates.

Mesembryanthemum grandifolium is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental value.
