Melittis grandiflora var. alba

Melittis grandiflora var. alba, commonly known as white melittis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, white, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly waxy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
