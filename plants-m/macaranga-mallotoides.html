Macaranga mallotoides

Macaranga mallotoides, commonly known as the white-leaf mallot, is a tropical plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine white powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Macaranga mallotoides thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.
