Macleania compacta

Macleania compacta, commonly known as the compact mallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, compact growth. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive addition to gardens with a Mediterranean climate.
