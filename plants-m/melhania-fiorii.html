Melhania fiorii

Melhania fiorii, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of yellow-orange tubular flowers that resemble small bells. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that turn from green to red as they mature. Melhania fiorii thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
