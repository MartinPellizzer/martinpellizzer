Melicope anisata

Melicope anisata, commonly known as the aniseed melicope, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are followed by small, round fruits containing smooth, dark brown seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Melicope anisata is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
