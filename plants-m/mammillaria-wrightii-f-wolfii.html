Mammillaria wrightii f. wolfii

Mammillaria wrightii f. wolfii, commonly known as the Wright's mammillaria, is a compact cactus species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It features a globular stem with prominent, white to yellow spines that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to dry climates and requires minimal water to thrive.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
