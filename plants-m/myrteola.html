Myrteola

Myrteola, commonly known as the honeycomb plant, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Myrtaceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers that often have a sweet, fragrant scent. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

Myrteola species are also valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated for their unique appearance and fragrance.
