Myroxylon trialatum

Myroxylon trialatum, commonly known as the Brazilwood tree, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark red heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and rich color. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers and elongated, ribbed fruits that contain seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The plant is also known for its historical use in the production of dyes and musical instruments.
