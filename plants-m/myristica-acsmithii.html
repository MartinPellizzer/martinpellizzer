Myristica acsmithii

Myristica acsmithii, commonly known as the Acsmith's nutmeg, is a small evergreen tree native to the western part of the island of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody fruits that contain the highly valued spice, nutmeg. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are pollinated by insects, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape.

The tree thrives in humid, tropical climates and is typically found in lowland and hill forest environments.
