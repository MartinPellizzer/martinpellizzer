Microlophus cerinthifolius

Microlophus cerinthifolius, commonly known as the desert spiny lizard plant, is a succulent species native to the arid regions of South America.

It features fleshy, spiny stems and leaves adapted to store water, enabling it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. Its leaves are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss.

Microlophus cerinthifolius is valued for its resilience and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in xeriscapes.
