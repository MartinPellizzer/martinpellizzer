Mimulus acaulis

Mimulus acaulis, commonly known as the slender monkeyflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairless and can grow up to 30 centimeters in height. The plant produces showy, orange-yellow flowers that are typically tubular in shape with two lips, typical of the Plantaginaceae family. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Mimulus acaulis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.
