Myagrum pinnatum

Myagrum pinnatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a tropical shrub native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its bark is rough and fibrous, and it produces woody capsules containing aromatic seeds.

Myagrum pinnatum is often used in traditional Aboriginal medicine and has a distinctive, pleasant fragrance.
