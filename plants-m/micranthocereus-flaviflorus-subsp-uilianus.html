Micranthocereus flaviflorus subsp. uilianus

Micranthocereus flaviflorus subsp.

uilianus, commonly known as the Uilianus cactus, is a species native to Brazil. It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive yellowish-green stem and spines that are typically pale yellow.

The flowers are large, yellow, and bloom during the dry season, adding a striking visual contrast to the plant. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in arid environments.
