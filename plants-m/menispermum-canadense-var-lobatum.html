Menispermum canadense var. lobatum

Menispermum canadense var. lobatum, commonly known as Canadian arrowroot or wild ginger, is a deciduous perennial vine native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but have a strong, spicy flavor. Its rhizomes are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks.
