Malpighia cotinifolia

Malpighia cotinifolia, commonly known as the beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright purple berries that appear in the fall. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

The berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for birds and other wildlife.
