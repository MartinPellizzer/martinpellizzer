Minuria asteroidea

Minuria asteroidea, commonly known as the star-shaped minuria, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has small, narrow leaves arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowers are yellow and star-shaped, giving it its common name, and it produces small, achene-like seeds.

Minuria asteroidea is often found in wetlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
