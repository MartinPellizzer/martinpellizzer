Mentha longifolia subsp. wissii

Mentha longifolia subsp.

wissii, commonly known as Wiss's mint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typical of the Lamiaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in culinary or medicinal applications.
