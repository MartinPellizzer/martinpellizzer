Mimosa grahamii var. lemmonii

Mimosa grahamii var. lemmonii, commonly known as the Lemmon's sensitive plant, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched, a trait known as thigmonastic response. The plant produces small, yellow, spherical flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique adaptations and role in its native ecosystems.
