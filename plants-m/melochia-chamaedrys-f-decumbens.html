Melochia chamaedrys f. decumbens

Melochia chamaedrys f. decumbens, commonly known as the prostrate melochia, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant characterized by its sprawling, decumbent stems.

It features heart-shaped to ovate leaves that are often pubescent, with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in shaded, moist environments.

This variant is noted for its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal in landscaping.
