Mariscus articulatus

Mariscus articulatus, commonly known as the articulated marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its jointed, segmented stems that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, typically blooming in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often submerged or floating in shallow water.

Mariscus articulatus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
