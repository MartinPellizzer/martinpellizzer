Musa sanguinea

Musa sanguinea, commonly known as the red banana, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant that grows in dense, humid environments. The plant produces striking red-purple flowers that are often used in ornamental horticulture. Its leaves are large, broad, and have a glossy appearance.

While not typically cultivated for fruit, it is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ecological role in tropical ecosystems.
