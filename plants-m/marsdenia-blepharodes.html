Marsdenia blepharodes

Marsdenia blepharodes, commonly known as the veined Marsdenia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by berry-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forest understories or on rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
