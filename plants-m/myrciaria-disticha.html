Myrciaria disticha

Myrciaria disticha, commonly known as the Surinam cherry, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It produces small, bright red fruits that are rich in vitamin C and have a tangy, citrus-like flavor. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and emits a fragrant aroma. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

The flowers are white and clustered, contributing to its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
