Mesembryanthemum dolabriformoides

Mesembryanthemum dolabriformoides, commonly known as the sickle-shaped ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, ribbed leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in spring and summer. It is well adapted to rocky soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular ornamental plant in dry gardens.
