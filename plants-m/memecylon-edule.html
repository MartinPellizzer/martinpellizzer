Memecylon edule

Memecylon edule, commonly known as the edible memecylon, is a small evergreen shrub belonging to the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible berries that are often consumed by local communities. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant has traditional uses in both culinary and medicinal applications in various parts of Southeast Asia.
