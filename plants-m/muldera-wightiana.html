Muldera wightiana

Muldera wightiana, commonly known as the Wight's muldera, is a flowering plant native to the western regions of the Indian subcontinent.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in loose clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Muldera wightiana is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
