Myrcia heterochroa

Myrcia heterochroa, commonly known as the black myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, dark purple berries that contain several seeds.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
