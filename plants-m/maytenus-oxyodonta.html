Maytenus oxyodonta

Maytenus oxyodonta, commonly known as the Brazilian olive or mayten, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Cashew family (Anacardiaceae) and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries that turn red when mature.

It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a variety of soil types but preferring well-drained conditions.
