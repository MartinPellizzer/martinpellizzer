Mentha × hirtescens

Mentha × hirtescens, commonly known as woolly mint, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and a strong, minty aroma. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in herbal remedies.
