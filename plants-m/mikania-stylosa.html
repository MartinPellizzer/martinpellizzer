Mikania stylosa

Mikania stylosa, commonly known as creeping morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Its botanical name reflects its flowering time and the genus's association with morning glories.
