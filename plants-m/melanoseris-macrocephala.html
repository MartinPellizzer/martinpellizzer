Melanoseris macrocephala

Melanoseris macrocephala, commonly known as the large-headed melastoma, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an herb or shrub, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are usually clustered in inflorescences and feature five petals, with the lower petal being notably larger.

The plant is adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
