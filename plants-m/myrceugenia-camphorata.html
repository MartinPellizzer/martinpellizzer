Myrceugenia camphorata

Myrceugenia camphorata, commonly known as the camphor tree, is a species native to the temperate rainforests of southern South America.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. Its wood is valued for its aromatic properties and resistance to decay.

The plant plays an important role in the ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
