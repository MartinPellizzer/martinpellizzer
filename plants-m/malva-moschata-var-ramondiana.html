Malva moschata var. ramondiana

Malva moschata var. ramondiana, commonly known as the Ramond's mallows, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is native to parts of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It produces fluffy, seed-filled capsules that disperse its seeds through wind.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
