Mabea elegans

Mabea elegans, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces showy, dark purple berries that are a striking feature of its appearance. Mabea elegans is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and role in local ecosystems.
