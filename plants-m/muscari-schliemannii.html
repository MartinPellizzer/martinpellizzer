Muscari schliemannii

Muscari schliemannii, commonly known as the Persian squill, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It produces clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that are evergreen during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Muscari schliemannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
