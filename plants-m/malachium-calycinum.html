Malachium calycinum

Malachium calycinum, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and roadside areas. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
