Mentha × malyi

Mentha × malyi, commonly known as the Malayan mint, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Mentha arvensis and Mentha viridis.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its aromatic, often menthol-like leaves. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb with square stems and opposite leaves. It is widely cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.

Mentha × malyi thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
