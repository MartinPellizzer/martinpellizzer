Miconia symphyandra

Miconia symphyandra, commonly known as the giant miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, violet to purple flowers with five petals, often accompanied by bright yellow stamens. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
