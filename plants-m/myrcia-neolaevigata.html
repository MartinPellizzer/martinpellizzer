Myrcia neolaevigata

Myrcia neolaevigata, commonly known as the velvet myrtle, is a shrub native to the rainforests of eastern Australia.

It features elliptical to ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green on top, with a lighter underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade.

Myrcia neolaevigata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
