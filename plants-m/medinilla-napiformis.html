Medinilla napiformis

Medinilla napiformis, commonly known as the velvet tangerine, is a tropical plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its glossy, velvety leaves and clusters of fragrant, waxy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

Medinilla napiformis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and pleasant fragrance.
