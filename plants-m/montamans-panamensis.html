Montamans panamensis

Montamans panamensis, commonly known as the Panama mountain vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its stems are woody and can grow up to several meters in length, allowing it to climb trees and other vegetation.

Montamans panamensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing shelter and food for various insects and small animals.
