Mentha × nemorosa

Mentha × nemorosa, commonly known as the forest mint or creeping mint, is a hybrid species in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit and aromatic leaves that emit a strong mint scent. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Mentha × nemorosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
