Melastoma grossularia

Melastoma grossularia, commonly known as the red-flowered melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as tropical forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually opposite and oval-shaped with a smooth texture.

Melastoma grossularia is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
