Malpighia glabra var. acuminata

Malpighia glabra var. acuminata, commonly known as the acuminata passionfruit, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It produces small, edible berries that are rich in vitamin C and other antioxidants. The plant has compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit's distinctive appearance and nutritional benefits make it a popular choice in both culinary and medicinal applications.
