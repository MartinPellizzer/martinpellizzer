Medicago sativa nothosubsp. tunetana

Medicago sativa nothosubsp.

tunetana, commonly known as the Tunisian alfalfa, is a hybrid subspecies of the common medick. It is characterized by its perennial growth habit and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Fabaceae family.

The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in summer, followed by pods containing edible seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it suitable for cultivation in regions with limited water resources.

This subspecies is valued for its forage quality and potential in sustainable agriculture.
