Malva macrostachya

Malva macrostachya, commonly known as the large-stalked mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton, hibiscus, and okra, and is characterized by its showy flowers and soft, fuzzy foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with large, heart-shaped leaves and pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its botanical features include a robust root system and hairy stems, which help it thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Malva macrostachya is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist habitats, making it an important component of riparian ecosystems.
