Mentha × brachystachya

Mentha × brachystachya, commonly known as woolly mint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Mentha longifolia and Mentha rotundifolia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or violet in color.

Mentha × brachystachya is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in herbal remedies or as an ornamental plant.
