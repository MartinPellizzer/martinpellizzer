Melastoma leucandrum

Melastoma leucandrum, commonly known as the white-flowered melastoma, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces showy, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often with a distinctive red calyx. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds.
