Malus ringo fastigiata

Malus ringo fastigiata, commonly known as the columnar apple tree, is a unique variant of the apple tree characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to China, where it has been cultivated for centuries. The plant produces small, ornamental flowers in spring, followed by edible fruits that are typically smaller than those of common apple varieties. Its compact form makes it ideal for small gardens or as a decorative specimen in landscaping.

The tree is also valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
