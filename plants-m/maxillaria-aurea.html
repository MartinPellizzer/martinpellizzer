Maxillaria aurea

Maxillaria aurea, commonly known as the golden maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which often have a contrasting green or purple lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, arranged in a rosette pattern.

Maxillaria aurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
