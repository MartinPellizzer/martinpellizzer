Melodorum parvifolium

Melodorum parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved melody plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the temperate regions of the Pacific Northwest.

It features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length, arranged alternately along its slender stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers with five petals, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Melodorum parvifolium is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
