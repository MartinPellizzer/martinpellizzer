Macrochordion lamarchei

Macrochordion lamarchei, commonly known as the giant waterwheel plant, is a unique aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its large, spirally arranged leaves that resemble the blades of a waterwheel, hence its common name. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and ponds. Its roots are adapted to anchor in soft, muddy substrates, while its floating leaves provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.

The plant plays an important ecological role in maintaining water quality and supporting biodiversity in its native habitat.
