Morinda gatopensis

Morinda gatopensis, commonly known as the Philippine morinda, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forest understories or on rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
