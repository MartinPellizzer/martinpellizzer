Macroptilium bracteatum

Macroptilium bracteatum, commonly known as the Brazilian burr legume, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes.
