Maxillaria ferdinandiana

Maxillaria ferdinandiana, commonly known as the Ferdinand's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, producing new shoots from the base of the previous ones. The plant features long, narrow leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Maxillaria ferdinandiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is a subject of interest in botanical research.
