Micranthes columbiana

Micranthes columbiana, commonly known as the Columbia micranthus, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a dense rosette near the ground.

Micranthes columbiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local insect populations.
