Megapterium canescens

Megapterium canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves with a silvery-white underside. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is often considered a weed in agricultural and natural habitats.
