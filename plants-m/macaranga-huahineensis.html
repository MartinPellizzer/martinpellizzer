Macaranga huahineensis

Macaranga huahineensis, commonly known as the Huahine paper mulberry, is a species native to the Society Islands of French Polynesia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal and lowland areas. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and it produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange to red berries. It is adapted to tropical climates and is often found in disturbed or secondary vegetation.

The species is of interest for its ecological role and potential horticultural value.
