Myrcia pimentoides

Myrcia pimentoides, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal regions and disturbed soils.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it is considered a weed in some areas.
