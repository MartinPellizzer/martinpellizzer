Mucuna aimun

Mucuna aimun, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, and often purple-pink pods that contain numerous elliptical seeds. The plant grows as a climbing vine, reaching lengths of up to 5 meters, and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Mucuna aimun is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it a useful plant in agricultural systems.
