Meum

Meum, commonly known as hoary vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows, wastelands, and along roadsides. Meum has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds.

It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration efforts.
