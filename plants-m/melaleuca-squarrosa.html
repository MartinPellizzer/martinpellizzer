Melaleuca squarrosa

Melaleuca squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrose tea tree, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous small, black seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

Melaleuca squarrosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in indigenous medicine.
