Mentha × villosa var. cladodes

Mentha × villosa var. cladodes, commonly known as woolly mint or hairy mint, is a hybrid plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and square, erect stems. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stem. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its aromatic leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
