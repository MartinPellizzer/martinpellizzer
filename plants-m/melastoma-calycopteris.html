Melastoma calycopteris

Melastoma calycopteris, commonly known as the red-veined melastoma, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with prominent red veins. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, often with a distinctive calyx. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
