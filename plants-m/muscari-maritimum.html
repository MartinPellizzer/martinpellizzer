Muscari maritimum

Muscari maritimum, commonly known as the sea squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant is adapted to coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its bulbs are toxic and should not be consumed.

Muscari maritimum plays a role in stabilizing dunes and is often found in maritime habitats.
