Melothria trilobata var. sphaerocarpa

Melothria trilobata var. sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the round-fruited sour sop, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It produces small, spherical fruits with a greenish-yellow skin and a distinctive, aromatic scent. The fruit's interior contains a white, juicy pulp with numerous seeds, making it a popular ingredient in local cuisines. Botanically, it belongs to the family Cucurbitaceae, which includes cucumbers and squash.

This variant is distinguished by its rounded fruit shape and slightly different leaf morphology compared to the typical varietal form.
