Malouetia tamaquarina var. tamaquarina

Malouetia tamaquarina var. tamaquarina, commonly known as the Tamaquara tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree has a straight, cylindrical trunk with a smooth, pale bark that becomes fissured with age. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and it plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife.

The plant is also valued for its timber and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
