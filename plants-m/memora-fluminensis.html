Memora fluminensis

Memora fluminensis, commonly known as the Brazilian violet, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often branched.

Memora fluminensis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental beauty in horticulture.
