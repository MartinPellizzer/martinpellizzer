Malus sachalinensis f. divaricata

Malus sachalinensis f. divaricata, commonly known as the Sakhalin apple or Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its ornamental, finely toothed leaves that turn vibrant red in autumn. The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by edible, apple-like fruits that are typically yellow or red in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and adaptability to various soil types.
