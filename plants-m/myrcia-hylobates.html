Myrcia hylobates

Myrcia hylobates, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

Myrcia hylobates is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
