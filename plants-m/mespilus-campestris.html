Mespilus campestris

Mespilus campestris, commonly known as the field medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and ovate leaves. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible, fleshy berries that ripen in autumn.

Its fruits are often used in cooking, and the plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
