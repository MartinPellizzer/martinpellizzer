Mischarytera bullata

Mischarytera bullata, commonly known as the bullate mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of specific host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has a distinctive appearance with its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale green flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are often consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. Its parasitic nature means it derives some of its nutrients from the host tree, while also performing photosynthesis.

The bullate mistletoe is notable for its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitats.
