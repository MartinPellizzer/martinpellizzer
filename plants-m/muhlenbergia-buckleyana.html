Muhlenbergia buckleyana

Muhlenbergia buckleyana, commonly known as Buckley's muhlenbergia, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in rocky, arid environments and is characterized by its slender, tufted clumps of fine, silver-blue foliage. The plant produces small, pale green to brownish flowers in late summer, which are arranged in narrow, cylindrical inflorescences. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and ability to form dense, low-growing mats.
