Melanthera rhombifolia

Melanthera rhombifolia, commonly known as the rhombic melanthera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, rhomboid-shaped leaves with serrated edges, which are a key identifying characteristic. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are usually greenish-white in color.

Melanthera rhombifolia plays a role in supporting local pollinators and contributes to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
