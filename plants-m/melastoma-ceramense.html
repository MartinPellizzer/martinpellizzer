Melastoma ceramense

Melastoma ceramense, commonly known as the Ceram Melastoma, is a flowering plant native to the Indonesian island of Ceram.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its attractive, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforests and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a glossy surface, while its stems are woody and creeping.

Melastoma ceramense is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
