Malvastrum arcuatum

Malvastrum arcuatum, commonly known as the woolly mallow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
