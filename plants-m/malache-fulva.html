Malache fulva

Malache fulva, commonly known as the golden malache, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has smooth, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
