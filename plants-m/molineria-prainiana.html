Molineria prainiana

Molineria prainiana, commonly known as the Prain's grass, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that are often pale green or brown in color. It is typically found in wetland or marshy habitats, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and wetland restoration projects.
