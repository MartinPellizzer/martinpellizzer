Melanocenchris jacquemontii

Melanocenchris jacquemontii, commonly known as the Jacquemont's melastome, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, often violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, and has a compact, shrub-like growth habit. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a distinctive, rough texture.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
