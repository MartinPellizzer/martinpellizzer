Myrciaria plinioides

Myrciaria plinioides, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *cereja brasileira*, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, warm climates. The plant is characterized by its small, red, cherry-like fruits that are edible and often consumed fresh or used in local cuisine. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, and it produces fragrant white flowers during the rainy season.

Myrciaria plinioides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic and culinary uses.
