Machaerium leiophyllum var. leiophyllum

Machaerium leiophyllum var. leiophyllum, commonly known as the silky machaerium, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant features slender, often hairless stems with opposite, pinnately compound leaves that are typically green and lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in racemes, usually with a distinctive pink or purple coloration.

The varietal form leiophyllum is distinguished by its smooth, leathery leaves and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in warm climates.
