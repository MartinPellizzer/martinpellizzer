Memecylon ambrense

Memecylon ambrense, commonly known as the African memecylon, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, oval-shaped leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture for its attractive foliage.
