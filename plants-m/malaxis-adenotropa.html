Malaxis adenotropa

Malaxis adenotropa, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The botanical features of Malaxis adenotropa make it a popular subject in both horticulture and botanical research.
