It primarily grows on the branches of host trees, such as oaks and pines, and derives some of its nutrients from the host through specialized structures called haustoria. Its leaves are evergreen, and it is often found in temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can have significant impacts on the health of its host trees.
