Manihot preciosa

Manihot preciosa, commonly known as the golden arrowroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange tubers that are rich in starch. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and has alternate, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with a unique structure that includes both male and female parts.

The tubers are not only a source of food but also have traditional medicinal uses in some indigenous cultures.
