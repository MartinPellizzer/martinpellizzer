Mimosa bipinnata

Mimosa bipinnata, commonly known as the sensitive plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which fold shut when touched, a response triggered by rapid changes in turgor pressure within the leaf cells. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique sensitivity to touch and its ability to thrive in various climates contribute to its widespread cultivation.
