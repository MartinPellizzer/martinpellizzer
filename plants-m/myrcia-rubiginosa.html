Myrcia rubiginosa

Myrcia rubiginosa, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its red, fleshy berries that are edible and have a sweet, tart flavor. Myrcia rubiginosa is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and aromatic properties.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is adaptable to various light conditions.
