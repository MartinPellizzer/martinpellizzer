Mammillaria tobuschii

Mammillaria tobuschii, commonly known as the Tusbach hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a spiny, segmented stem that typically grows to a height of about 10 centimeters. The plant features white to pale green areoles covered with short, white hairs, and it produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping and cactus collections.

Its compact size and distinctive appearance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
