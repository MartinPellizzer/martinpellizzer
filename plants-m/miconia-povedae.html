Miconia povedae

Miconia povedae, commonly known as the Poveda miconia, is a species of flowering plant in the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
