Mentha × niliaca var. velutina

Mentha × niliaca var. velutina, commonly known as velvet mint, is a hybrid mint species characterized by its soft, velvety foliage.

It is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region, often found in rocky or limestone soils. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or violet in color. Its aromatic leaves are used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

Velvet mint is valued for its unique texture and fragrance, making it a popular choice in herbal gardens.
