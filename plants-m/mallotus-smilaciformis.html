Mallotus smilaciformis

Mallotus smilaciformis, commonly known as the climbing mallot, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its rapid growth and distinctive, palmate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its climbing habit and ornamental foliage, it is sometimes cultivated for landscaping purposes.
