Melastoma decussatum

Melastoma decussatum, commonly known as the cross-leaved melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, usually in clusters.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
