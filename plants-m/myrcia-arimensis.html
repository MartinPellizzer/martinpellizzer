Myrcia arimensis

Myrcia arimensis, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.
