Maranta chantrieri

Maranta chantrieri, commonly known as the prayer plant, is a tropical species native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage that changes with the time of day, a phenomenon known as nyctinasty. The leaves typically close at night and reopen in the morning, resembling prayer. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
