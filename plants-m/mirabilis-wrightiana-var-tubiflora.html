Mirabilis wrightiana var. tubiflora

Mirabilis wrightiana var. tubiflora, commonly known as the tubular four o'clock, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular-shaped flowers that open in the late afternoon. The plant produces vibrant red, orange, or yellow blooms, which attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The foliage is typically green and lobed, adding visual interest to the plant's appearance.
