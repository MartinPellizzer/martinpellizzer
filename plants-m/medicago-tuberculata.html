Medicago tuberculata

Medicago tuberculata, commonly known as the hairy vetch, is a leguminous plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment and as a cover crop. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Medicago tuberculata is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
