Mercurialis annua var. urticiformis

Mercurialis annua var. urticiformis, commonly known as the stinging nettle mercury, is a variant of the common mercury plant.

It is characterized by its stinging hairs, which can cause skin irritation upon contact. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a serrated margin, and it produces small yellow flowers in clusters.

This variant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
