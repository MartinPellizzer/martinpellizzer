Maxillaria aurantiaca

Maxillaria aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange orchid, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb that supports its growth and storage of nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of orange and yellow, often with intricate patterns. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, arranged around the pseudobulb.

Maxillaria aurantiaca thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
