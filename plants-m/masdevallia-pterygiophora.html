Masdevallia pterygiophora

Masdevallia pterygiophora, commonly known as the feather-veined masdevallia, is a striking orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, often colorful flowers with elongated petals and a distinctive, spurred lip. The plant typically grows as a miniature orchid, with slender stems and compact foliage. Its botanical features include a complex arrangement of sepals and petals, and it often exhibits a wide range of color variations.

It is appreciated for its ornamental value and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
