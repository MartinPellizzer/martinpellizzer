Malaxis pedicellaris

Malaxis pedicellaris, commonly known as the slender malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption in poor soil conditions.

The species is part of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its subtle beauty and ecological significance in its native environment.
