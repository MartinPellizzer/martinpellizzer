Mentha interrupta

Mentha interrupta, commonly known as catnip, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stem. Mentha interrupta is known for its strong aromatic compounds, particularly nepetalactone, which gives it its distinctive scent.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
