Megathyrsus bivonanus

Megathyrsus bivonanus, commonly known as the Bivona grass, is a tropical perennial grass native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects and as a forage crop for livestock.
