Milium effusum var. elatius

Milium effusum var. elatius, commonly known as the tall meadow sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and along streambanks. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and the presence of three-sided stems.

Botanically, it is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for invertebrates.
