Maximiliana macrogyne

Maximiliana macrogyne, commonly known as the broad-leaved tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its woody stems, dense foliage, and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss in hot, dry climates. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers in summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a common species in desert environments.

Maximiliana macrogyne plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various desert wildlife species.
