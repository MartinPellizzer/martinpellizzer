Mitrephora woodii

Mitrephora woodii, commonly known as the Vietnamese sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant wood, which is highly valued in traditional medicine and perfumery. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic in shape and often have a glossy surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by yellowish fruits containing winged seeds.

Due to overharvesting and habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in several countries.
