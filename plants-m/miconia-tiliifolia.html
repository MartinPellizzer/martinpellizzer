Miconia tiliifolia

Miconia tiliifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian glorybower, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Miconia tiliifolia is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a challenge for land management.
