Medinilla capillipes

Medinilla capillipes, commonly known as the velvet tangerine.

This tropical plant is native to Southeast Asia and belongs to the Myristicaceae family. It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.
