Meniscium handroi

Meniscium handroi, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, emitting a sweet fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators. The plant has a trailing growth habit with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters and have five petals that curl inward as they mature.

Meniscium handroi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil and full sun.
