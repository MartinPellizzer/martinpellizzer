Manilkara kribensis

Manilkara kribensis, commonly known as the African breadfruit, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and smooth, gray bark. The tree produces oval, yellowish fruits that are edible when ripe, with a starchy, bread-like texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.

The plant also has traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of natural rubber.
