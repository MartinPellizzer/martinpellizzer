Myrcia cordifolia

Myrcia cordifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas.

Myrcia cordifolia is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
