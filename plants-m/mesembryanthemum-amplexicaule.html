Mesembryanthemum amplexicaule

Mesembryanthemum amplexicaule, commonly known as the cushion ice plant, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and fleshy, often blue-green leaves that store water. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the warm seasons. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
