Mikania chaseae

Mikania chaseae, commonly known as the Chase's mile-a-minute vine, is a fast-growing flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. It is known for its rapid growth rate, often spreading aggressively and forming dense mats.

Due to its invasive nature, it is considered a problematic species in many regions where it has been introduced.
