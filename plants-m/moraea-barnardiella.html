Moraea barnardiella

Moraea barnardiella, commonly known as the Natal spider orchid, is a striking perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, spider-like flowers. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, producing clusters of pale yellow to orange flowers with intricate patterns. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
