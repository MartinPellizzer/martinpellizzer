Myrcia plusiantha

Myrcia plusiantha, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Myrcia plusiantha is also noted for its ability to attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and birds.
