Momordica friesiorum

Momordica friesiorum, commonly known as the African bitter melon, is a climbing vine native to Africa.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourds and cucumbers. The plant produces elongated, pointed fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or orange when mature. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture, and the stems are hairy.

The fruit contains small, bitter seeds and is often used in traditional medicine and cooking in various African countries.
