Microstachys sessilifolia f. apiculata

Microstachys sessilifolia f. apiculata, commonly known as the apiculate form of the sessile-leaved microstachys, is a variant of the microstachys genus.

It is characterized by its small, sessile leaves that are often clustered at the base of the plant. The flowers are typically arranged in dense spikes and are often pale pink to white in color. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical or subtropical regions.

Its unique morphology makes it a subject of interest in botanical studies and horticultural cultivation.
