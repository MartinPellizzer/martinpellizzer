Montiastrum vassilievii

Montiastrum vassilievii, commonly known as the Vassiliev's monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Montiaceae and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant is herbaceous, with slender stems and leaves that are often opposite and lance-shaped.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and streambanks.
