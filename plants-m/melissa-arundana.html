Melissa arundana

Melissa arundana, commonly known as catnip, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant is recognized for its fragrant leaves, which contain high concentrations of essential oils, particularly nepetalactone. These oils are responsible for its stimulating effect on cats and its medicinal properties in humans.

Melissa arundana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
