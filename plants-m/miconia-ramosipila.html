Miconia ramosipila

Miconia ramosipila, commonly known as the tropical shrub or tree, is a member of the Melastomataceae family.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves that are typically arranged oppositely on the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that are often borne on short pedicels. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous tiny seeds.

Miconia ramosipila is native to Central and South America and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
