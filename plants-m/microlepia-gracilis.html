Microlepia gracilis

Microlepia gracilis, commonly known as the slender sword fern, is a delicate fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, arching fronds that give it a graceful appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizome is slender and often covered in a layer of fine, brown scales.

Microlepia gracilis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ease of cultivation.
