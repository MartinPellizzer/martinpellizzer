Melica secunda

Melica secunda, commonly known as slender meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with two to three spikelets, which are often wind-pollinated.

Melica secunda is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
