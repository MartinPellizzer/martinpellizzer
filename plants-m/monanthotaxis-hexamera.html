Monanthotaxis hexamera

Monanthotaxis hexamera, commonly known as the six-flowered monanthotaxis, is a member of the Araliaceae family.

It is a tropical climbing plant characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and showy, umbellate inflorescences. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by red, fleshy berries. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
