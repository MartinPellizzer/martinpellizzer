Matricaria recutita var. kochiana

Matricaria recutita var. kochiana, commonly known as the Koch's chamomile, is a variant of the common chamomile plant.

It is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, which are characteristic of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, often used in traditional medicine.

This variety is valued for its calming properties and is often used in teas and aromatherapy.
