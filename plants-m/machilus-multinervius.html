Machilus multinervius

Machilus multinervius, commonly known as the broad-leaved machilus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of China, Vietnam, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins, which give it its scientific name "multinervius.". It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by small, elliptical berries.

Machilus multinervius is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
