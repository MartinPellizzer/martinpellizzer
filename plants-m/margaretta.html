Margaretta

Margaretta, commonly known as the margaret daisy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant is native to North America and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Margaretta is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Its foliage is usually gray-green, with serrated leaves that add texture to the garden landscape.
