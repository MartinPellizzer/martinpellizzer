Myricaria germanica var. squamosa

Myricaria germanica var. squamosa, commonly known as the squamosa myricaria, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and dense covering of small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in panicles during the summer months, which are followed by woody, cone-like fruits. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems and reduced leaf surface area to minimize water loss.

This species is often used in xeriscaping and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological resilience.
