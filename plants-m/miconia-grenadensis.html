Miconia grenadensis

Miconia grenadensis, commonly known as the Grenada miconia, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are berry-like, containing numerous seeds, and it is often found in moist, shaded forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions, though it can be invasive in certain ecosystems.
