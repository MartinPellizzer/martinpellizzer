Mertensia parvifolia

Mertensia parvifolia, commonly known as small-leaved bluebells, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often hairy beneath. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Mertensia parvifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in woodland gardens.
