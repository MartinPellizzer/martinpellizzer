Monanthotaxis valida f. valida

Monanthotaxis valida f. valida, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom in dense clusters, giving it a fiery appearance. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and a slightly serrated margin. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate partial shade and its use in traditional medicine.
