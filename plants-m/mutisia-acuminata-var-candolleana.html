Mutisia acuminata var. candolleana

Mutisia acuminata var. candolleana, commonly known as the Candolleana mutisia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, yellow-orange ray flowers surrounded by a dense cluster of disc flowers. It typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and shrublands, thriving in cool and moist environments.

Its botanical structure includes fibrous roots, woody stems, and alternate, hairy leaves that help it adapt to its alpine habitat.
