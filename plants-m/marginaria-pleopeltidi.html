Marginaria pleopeltidi

Marginaria pleopeltidi, commonly known as the rock wallflower, is a flowering plant native to arid and rocky environments.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has small, linear leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh habitat. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Marginaria pleopeltidi is often found growing on limestone or sandstone cliffs, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
