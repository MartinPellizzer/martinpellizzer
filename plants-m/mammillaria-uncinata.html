Mammillaria uncinata

Mammillaria uncinata, commonly known as the hooked mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white to pale green tubercles. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight.

Due to its compact size and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
