Myriocarpa obovata

Myriocarpa obovata, commonly known as the broad-ovate milkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is known for its milky sap, which is typical of the Apocynaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
