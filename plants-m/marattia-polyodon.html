Marattia polyodon

Marattia polyodon, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a striking member of the Marattiaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in distinctive, spore-bearing structures called sorangia. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a significant species in forest ecosystems.
