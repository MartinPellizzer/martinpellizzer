Mimulus bracteosus

Mimulus bracteosus, commonly known as the bracteate monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright orange or yellow flowers, which are typically tubular in shape and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in streambanks and meadows.

Mimulus bracteosus is a perennial species that blooms from spring to early summer.
