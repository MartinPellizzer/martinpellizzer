Melocactus cephalenoplus

Melocactus cephalenoplus, commonly known as the red-headed melon or the red-headed cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, and its fruit is a round, fleshy melon-like structure containing edible seeds. It is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, with a deep root system and thick, fleshy stems that store water.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and xeriscaping.
