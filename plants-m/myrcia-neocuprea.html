Myrcia neocuprea

Myrcia neocuprea, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, red or purple flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its aromatic properties make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Myrcia neocuprea thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.
