Maba rostrata

Maba rostrata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed maba, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Maba rostrata is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in construction and furniture making.

It also has traditional medicinal uses in some local cultures.
