Machaerium acutifolium

Machaerium acutifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved machaerium, is a tropical perennial legume native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.

Machaerium acutifolium is also utilized in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
