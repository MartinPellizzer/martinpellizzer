Mirabilis coccinea

Mirabilis coccinea, commonly known as the blood flower, is a strikingly colorful plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Malvaceae family and is known for its vibrant red, orange, and yellow flowers that bloom throughout the day. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its showy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its unique flowering pattern, where flowers open in the morning and close by evening, adds to its botanical intrigue.
