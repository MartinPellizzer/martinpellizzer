Myrcianthes simpsonii

Myrcianthes simpsonii, commonly known as Simpson's myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known plants like eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, edible seeds.

Simpson's myrtle is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Indigenous medicine.
