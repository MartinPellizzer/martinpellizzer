Maieta annulata

Maieta annulata, commonly known as the ringed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers with a distinctive ring-like pattern at the base. The plant has lanceolate leaves that are hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It blooms in the summer months, producing vibrant blue to purple flowers that attract pollinators.

Maieta annulata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to well-drained soils.
