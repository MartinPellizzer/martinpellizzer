Melandrium × intermedium

Melandrium × intermedium, commonly known as the hybrid catchfly, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers have five petals and are borne in dense inflorescences.

Melandrium × intermedium is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
