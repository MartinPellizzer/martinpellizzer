Maxillaria proboscidea

Maxillaria proboscidea, commonly known as the orchid with the proboscis, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated flower with a distinctive, tubular shape that resembles a proboscis, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are often brightly colored, ranging from yellow to orange, and emit a sweet fragrance to attract pollinators.

The plant's botanical features make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and ornamental value.
