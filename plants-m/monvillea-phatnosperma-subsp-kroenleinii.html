Monvillea phatnosperma subsp. kroenleinii

kroenleinii, commonly known as the Schomburgk's monvillea, is a rare and striking member of the Melanthiaceae family. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant is native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America, specifically in Peru and Bolivia. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
