Mahonia dolichostylis

Mahonia dolichostylis, commonly known as the long-styled mahonia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, compound leaves that are dark green and often have a glossy appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are followed by blue-black berries. Mahonia dolichostylis thrives in partial to full shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to provide winter interest in the garden.
