Masdevallia ignea var. boddaertii

Masdevallia ignea var. boddaertii, commonly known as the fiery torch orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, where it grows in cool, humid cloud forests. The plant is characterized by its vivid red and orange inflorescences, which are often adorned with intricate, spurred petals. It thrives in shaded environments with high air humidity and requires well-draining, acidic soil.

Due to its limited natural range and specific cultivation needs, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
