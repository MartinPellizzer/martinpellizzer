Musa borneensis var. phoenicea

Musa borneensis var. phoenicea, commonly known as the Red Banana, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red-purple foliage and elongated, pale yellow fingers that resemble bananas. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained, fertile soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as for its edible fruit.

The leaves are large and can be used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
