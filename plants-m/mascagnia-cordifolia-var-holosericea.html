Mascagnia cordifolia var. holosericea

Mascagnia cordifolia var. holosericea, commonly known as the silky heartleaf mescal bean, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, silky white pubescence, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a pod containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some historical use in herbal remedies.
