Malva scorpioides

Malva scorpioides, commonly known as the scarlet mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers, which bloom in summer and are arranged in dense, showy clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture and a hairy stem that becomes woody with age. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Malva scorpioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
