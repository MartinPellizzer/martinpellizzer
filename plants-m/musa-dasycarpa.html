Musa dasycarpa

Musa dasycarpa, commonly known as the giant banana plant, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of elongated, fleshy fruits that are typically yellow when ripe. Its growth habit is herbaceous, with a pseudostem formed by the tightly packed leaf sheaths.

Musa dasycarpa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
