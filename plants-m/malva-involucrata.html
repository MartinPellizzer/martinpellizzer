Malva involucrata

Malva involucrata, commonly known as the woolly mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Malva involucrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
