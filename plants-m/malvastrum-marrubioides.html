Malvastrum marrubioides

Malvastrum marrubioides, commonly known as the Mexican mallow, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often branched.

Malvastrum marrubioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
