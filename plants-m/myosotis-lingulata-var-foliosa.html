Myosotis lingulata var. foliosa

Myosotis lingulata var. foliosa, commonly known as the broad-leaf forget-me-not, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features basal leaves that are broad and ovate, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. The plant produces clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
