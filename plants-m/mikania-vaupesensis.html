Mikania vaupesensis

Mikania vaupesensis, commonly known as the Amazonian devil's vine, is a climbing plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and extensive root system. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of tropical forests.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in certain areas, impacting local ecosystems.
