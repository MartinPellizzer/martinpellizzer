Mimosa incurialis

Mimosa incurialis, commonly known as the white mimosa, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white, fluffy flowers. The plant is known for its sensitive response to touch, causing the leaves to fold when disturbed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in dry, open habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
