Millotia greevesii subsp. glandulosa

Millotia greevesii subsp.

glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular millotia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its slender, branched stems.

The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered with fine, glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

This subspecies is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along coastal areas.
