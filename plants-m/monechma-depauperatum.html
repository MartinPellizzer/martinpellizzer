Monechma depauperatum

Monechma depauperatum, commonly known as the poorleaf monechma, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and along stream banks. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly toothed margin. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often clustered in dense inflorescences, and are typically greenish-white in color.

Monechma depauperatum is known for its low-growing habit and is often found in association with other shade-tolerant flora.
