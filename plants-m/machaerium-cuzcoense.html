Machaerium cuzcoense

Machaerium cuzcoense, commonly known as the Cuzco grass, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly found in regions of Peru and Bolivia.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family, which is characterized by its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and has slender, erect stems with small, trifoliate leaves. Its flowers are clustered in panicles and are often pink or white, attracting various pollinators.

Machaerium cuzcoense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional landscaping in its native habitats.
