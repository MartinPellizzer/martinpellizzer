Mentha × sativa subsp. procumbens

Mentha × sativa subsp.

procumbens, commonly known as creeping mint, is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its prostrate stems and fragrant, serrated leaves. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is native to Europe and parts of Asia.

The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, making it useful in culinary and medicinal applications.

Due to its spreading growth habit, it is often used in ornamental gardening and as a ground cover.
