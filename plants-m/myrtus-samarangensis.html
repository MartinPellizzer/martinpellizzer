Myrtus samarangensis

Myrtus samarangensis, commonly known as the Samarang myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. It produces small, round berries that are typically dark blue or black when mature.

Myrtus samarangensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
