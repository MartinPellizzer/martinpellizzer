Mananthes flaccida

Mananthes flaccida, commonly known as the Florida morning glory, is a herbaceous vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Mananthes flaccida is an important part of the local flora and plays a role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitat.
