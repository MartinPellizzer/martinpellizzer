Mendoncia lasiophyta

Mendoncia lasiophyta, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories or along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystems it inhabits.
