Myrcia corrientinensis

Myrcia corrientinensis, commonly known as Brazilian peppertree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces fleshy, red berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals. Myrcia corrientinensis thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
