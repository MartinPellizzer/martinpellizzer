Malvaviscus urticifolius

Malvaviscus urticifolius, commonly known as the stinging nettle mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish flowers. It has a hairy stem and produces a sticky, stinging sap, similar to that of the stinging nettle.

This species is often found in moist, shaded areas and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
