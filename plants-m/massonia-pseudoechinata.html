Massonia pseudoechinata

Massonia pseudoechinata, commonly known as the rock fig or mountain fig, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found growing on rocky outcrops.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
