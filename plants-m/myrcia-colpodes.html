Myrcia colpodes

Myrcia colpodes, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests.

Myrcia colpodes is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
