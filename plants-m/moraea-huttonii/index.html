Moraea Huttonii

Hutton's moraea, scientifically known as Moraea huttonii, is a striking member of the iris family native to southern Africa. This perennial herb is celebrated for its vivid, showy flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Found primarily in the Western Cape region, it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a favorite among botanists and gardeners alike. Due to habitat loss, it is also considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
