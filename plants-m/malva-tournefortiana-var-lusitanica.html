Malva tournefortiana var. lusitanica

Malva tournefortiana var. lusitanica, commonly known as the Iberian mallow, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and showy pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often in Mediterranean climates. It has a robust root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

The flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
