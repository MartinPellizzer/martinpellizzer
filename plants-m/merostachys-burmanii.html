Merostachys burmanii

Merostachys burmanii, commonly known as Burman's grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
