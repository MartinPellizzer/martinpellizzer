Matelea boliviensis

Matelea boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian moonflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Bolivia and Peru.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy, night-blooming flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant is a perennial shrub that can grow up to two meters in height, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
