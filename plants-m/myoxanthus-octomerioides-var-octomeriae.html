Myoxanthus octomerioides var. octomeriae

Myoxanthus octomerioides var. octomeriae, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange inflorescences that resemble clusters of small flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptical, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade conditions.
