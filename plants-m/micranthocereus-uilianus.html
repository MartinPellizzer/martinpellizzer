Micranthocereus uilianus

Micranthocereus uilianus, commonly known as the Ulian's night-blooming cactus, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its columnar stem and spiny, ribbed structure, adapted to arid environments. The plant produces flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white or pale pink, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its growth habit is slow, making it a prized specimen for cactus enthusiasts.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
