Macrocnemum grandiflorum

Macrocnemum grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered macrocnemum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features upright stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, covered with lance-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences of white to pale pink flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its flowers have five petals and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

The plant is often found in moist meadows and woodland edges, and it thrives in partial to full sunlight.
