Montezuma speciosissima

Montezuma speciosissima, commonly known as the Mexican fireflower, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to the highlands of Mexico and is characterized by its vibrant red and orange tubular flowers. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its blooming period occurs during the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
