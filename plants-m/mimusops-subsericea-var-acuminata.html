Mimusops subsericea var. acuminata

Mimusops subsericea var. acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves that are often slightly pointed at the tips. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellowish-orange berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and hard wood, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
