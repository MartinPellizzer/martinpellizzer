Myrcia splendens var. robustior

Myrcia splendens var. robustior, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it is considered a problematic species in some regions.
