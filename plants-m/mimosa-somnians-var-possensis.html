Mimosa somnians var. possensis

Mimosa somnians var. possensis, commonly known as the velvetleaf mimosa, is a variant of the mimosa plant characterized by its dense, velvety foliage.

It is native to parts of South America and is known for its compound leaves that fold shut when touched, a defensive mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces small, spherical seed pods that split open when mature, dispersing seeds through the wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

While not widely cultivated for ornamental purposes, it is studied for its adaptive traits and ecological role in its native habitats.
