Myrtus italica var. petri-ludovici

Myrtus italica var. petri-ludovici, commonly known as the Corsican myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, dark green leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value and historical uses in perfumery and culinary applications make it a notable botanical species.
