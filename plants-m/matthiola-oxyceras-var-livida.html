Matthiola oxyceras var. livida

Matthiola oxyceras var. livida, commonly known as the purple-flowered wallflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while its stems are upright and branched.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
