Mascagnia cordifolia var. cornifolia

Mascagnia cordifolia var. cornifolia, commonly known as the horned morning glory, is a striking flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, horn-like bracts that surround its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, are often blue or violet in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative vine in gardens.
