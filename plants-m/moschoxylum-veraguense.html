Moschoxylum veraguense

Moschoxylum veraguense, commonly known as the Veraguense quillwort, is a member of the Charophyta group, which includes aquatic and semi-aquatic plants.

It is characterized by its small, ribbon-like leaves that are typically submerged in water, and it produces spores rather than seeds. This plant is native to Central America, particularly found in the freshwater habitats of Panama and Nicaragua. Moschoxylum veraguense plays a role in maintaining aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.

Its presence is often an indicator of clean, well-oxygenated water.
