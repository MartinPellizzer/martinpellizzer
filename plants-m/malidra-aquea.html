Malidra aquea

Malidra aquea, commonly known as the water lily of the southern swamps, is a perennial aquatic plant native to tropical regions.

It features broad, floating leaves that are typically green with a waxy surface to repel water. Its flowers are showy, with vibrant colors ranging from white to pink, and they open in the early morning. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
