Mammillaria ritteriana

Mammillaria ritteriana, commonly known as the Ritter's bunny ear cactus, is a compact, low-growing cactus native to Mexico.

It features a globular stem with prominent, rounded tubercles that give it a distinctive, spiky appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
