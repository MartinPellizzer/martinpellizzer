Mesosetum angustifolium

Mesosetum angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in saturated soil conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its botanical features make it a key component of wetland ecosystems in its native range.
