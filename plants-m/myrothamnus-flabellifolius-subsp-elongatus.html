Myrothamnus flabellifolius subsp. elongatus

Myrothamnus flabellifolius subsp.

elongatus, commonly known as the long-leaved fan-leaf myrothamnus, is a unique succulent plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its fleshy, evergreen leaves that are elongated and often arranged in a fan-like structure.

The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its succulent nature allows it to store water in its leaves, making it adapted to arid environments.

It is a member of the Rhamnaceae family and is notable for its ability to survive in harsh, dry conditions.
