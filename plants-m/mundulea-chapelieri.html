Mundulea chapelieri

Mundulea chapelieri, commonly known as the Cape York acacia, is a shrub native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and compound leaves that are typically arranged in pairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and fire resilience.

The species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
