Mesembryanthemum intonsum var. rubicundum

Mesembryanthemum intonsum var. rubicundum, commonly known as the Red-veined Ice Plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves with prominent red veins, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to store water in its leaves allows it to survive in arid conditions.
