Miconia mutisella

Miconia mutisella, commonly known as the small miconia, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-bronze flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
