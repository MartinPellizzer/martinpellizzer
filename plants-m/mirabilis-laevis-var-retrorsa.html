Mirabilis laevis var. retrorsa

Mirabilis laevis var. retrorsa, commonly known as the reverse-hair four o'clock, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, backward-curving hairs. The plant produces vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the late afternoon and close at dawn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its unique appearance and blooming habit make it a valuable addition to ornamental gardens.
