Myosotis ramosissima var. gracillima

Myosotis ramosissima var. gracillima, commonly known as the slender bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to New Zealand.

It features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, soft pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, sky-blue flowers with five petals, which bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
