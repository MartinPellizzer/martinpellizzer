Mimosa plumeifolia

Mimosa plumeifolia, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often accompanied by a pleasant, sweet fragrance. Its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety appearance.

Mimosa plumeifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
