Monostiche colorata

Monostiche colorata, commonly known as the colorful monostich, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that form a rosette-like structure, often displaying shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the center of the bracts, which are typically tubular and adapted for pollination by insects. Native to arid regions, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
