Miconia acuminifera

Miconia acuminifera, commonly known as the sharp-leaved miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are usually green on the upper surface and lighter in color on the underside. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by fruit that resembles a capsule. It is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is considered a problematic species in some regions.
