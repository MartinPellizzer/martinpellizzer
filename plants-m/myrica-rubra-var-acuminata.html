Myrica rubra var. acuminata

Myrica rubra var. acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-fruited wax myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its small, dark red berries that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves and produces fragrant, yellowish flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
