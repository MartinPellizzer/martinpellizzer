Mimosa margaritae

Mimosa margaritae, commonly known as the silver mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, silvery-gray foliage and delicate, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is also suitable for xeriscaping in arid regions.
