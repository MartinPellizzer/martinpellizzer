Maba lamponga

Maba lamponga, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a fiery appearance. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark and leaves of the plant have been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
