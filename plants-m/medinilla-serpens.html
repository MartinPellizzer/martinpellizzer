Medinilla serpens

Medinilla serpens, commonly known as the snake plant or viper's head, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, fleshy leaves that resemble a snake's body, hence its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Medinilla serpens is relatively low maintenance and thrives in bright, indirect light with moderate watering.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique appearance.
