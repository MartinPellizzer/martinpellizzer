Macairea lanata subsp. eglandulosa

Macairea lanata subsp.

eglandulosa, commonly known as the woolly macairea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America. It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and leaves, which are covered in fine, white pubescence.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale pink to white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species within its native habitat.
