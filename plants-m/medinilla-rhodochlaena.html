Medinilla rhodochlaena

Medinilla rhodochlaena, commonly known as the red velvet medinilla, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in winter, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Medinilla rhodochlaena is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance.
