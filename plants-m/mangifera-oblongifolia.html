Mangifera oblongifolia

Mangifera oblongifolia, commonly known as the long-leaf mango, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, sweet fruits with a distinct flavor. Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.

The tree thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
