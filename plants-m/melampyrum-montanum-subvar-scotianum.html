Melampyrum montanum subvar. scotianum

Melampyrum montanum subvar. scotianum, commonly known as the Scottish touch-me-not, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, acidic soils, often found in woodland and heathland habitats. The plant produces striking red-orange flowers with a distinctive hooded structure, which attract pollinators such as bees and flies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a velvety texture, while its stems are slender and often branched.

This subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and is considered a rare and locally distributed plant.
