Mentha aquatica var. grandidentata

Mentha aquatica var. grandidentata, commonly known as American water mint, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and a strong, minty aroma. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. Its flowers are small and clustered in whorls, producing nectar that attracts pollinators.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in herbal remedies.
