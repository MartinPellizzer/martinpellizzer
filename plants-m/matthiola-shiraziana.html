Matthiola shiraziana

Matthiola shiraziana, commonly known as Persian candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops and ornamental plants. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse display of small, clustered flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is commonly cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
