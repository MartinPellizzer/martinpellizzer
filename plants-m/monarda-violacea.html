Monarda violacea

Monarda violacea, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers. The plant features lance-shaped, aromatic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to moist, well-drained soils.

Monarda violacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
