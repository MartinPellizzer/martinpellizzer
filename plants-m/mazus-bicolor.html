Mazus bicolor

Mazus bicolor, commonly known as the silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features small, silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Mazus bicolor thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its unique foliage and compact growth make it a useful ornamental plant in garden settings.
