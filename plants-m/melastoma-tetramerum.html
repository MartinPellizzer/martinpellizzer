Melastoma tetramerum

Melastoma tetramerum, commonly known as the four-lobed melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Melastoma tetramerum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
