Mercurialis annua

Mercurialis annua, commonly known as common mercury, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with a rough texture and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in a distinctive, three-lobed capsule that splits open when mature.

Mercurialis annua is noted for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
