Miconia campanensis

Miconia campanensis, commonly known as the bell-shaped miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
