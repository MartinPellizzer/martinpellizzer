Majorana orega

Majorana orega, commonly known as sweet marjoram, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and fragrant leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are aromatic, often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Majorana orega thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal collections.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its distinctive scent and various therapeutic properties.
