Melodorum fuscum

Melodorum fuscum, commonly known as the dark melodorum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It features slender, upright stems that bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, arranged in clusters at the base of the leaves. Its root system consists of rhizomes that help it spread vegetatively through underground stems.

Melodorum fuscum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
