Mentha longifolia subsp. serrulata

Mentha longifolia subsp.

serrulata, commonly known as narrow-leaf mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features aromatic, narrow, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other mint species.
