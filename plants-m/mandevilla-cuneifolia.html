Mandevilla cuneifolia

Mandevilla cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and clusters of trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces vibrant blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically during the warm seasons. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Mandevilla cuneifolia is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and showy flowers.
