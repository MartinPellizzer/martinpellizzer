Mercurialis annua f. cordata

Mercurialis annua f. cordata, commonly known as heartleaf mercury.

This variant of Mercurialis annua is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves, which are typically smaller and more rounded compared to the standard form. It is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family, native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in cyathia, typical of the genus.

It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.
