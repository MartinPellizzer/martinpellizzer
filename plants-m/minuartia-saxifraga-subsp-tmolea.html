Minuartia saxifraga subsp. tmolea

Minuartia saxifraga subsp.

tmolea, commonly known as the rock jasmine, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions. It typically forms dense mats of small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of white or pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of high-altitude and polar ecosystems.
