Melastoma malabathricum var. grandiflorum

Melastoma malabathricum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered Melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red in color, with five petals and a prominent central column. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
