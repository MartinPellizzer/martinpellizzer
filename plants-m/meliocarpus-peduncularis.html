Meliocarpus peduncularis

Meliocarpus peduncularis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ironwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dark, aromatic timber and dense, fibrous bark. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, kidney-shaped fruits.

This species is valued for its hardiness and is often used in landscaping and as a windbreak.
