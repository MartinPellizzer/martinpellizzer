Myrceugenia euosma var. oblongata

Myrceugenia euosma var. oblongata, commonly known as the oblongleaf myrtle, is a species native to the temperate rainforests of southern Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows up to 6 meters in height. The plant features glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
