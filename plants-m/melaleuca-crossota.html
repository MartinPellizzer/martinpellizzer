Melaleuca crossota

Melaleuca crossota, commonly known as the slender bottlebrush, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, cylindrical flower spikes that are typically pink to red in color. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, woody fruits known as capsules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy environments.

Melaleuca crossota is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.
