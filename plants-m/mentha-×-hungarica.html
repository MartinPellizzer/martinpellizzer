Mentha × hungarica

Mentha × hungarica, commonly known as Hungarian mint, is a hybrid mint species native to Central Europe.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring square stems and opposite leaves that are usually gray-green in color. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in shaded woodlands.

Mentha × hungarica is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
