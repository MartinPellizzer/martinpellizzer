Massonia luteovirens

Massonia luteovirens, commonly known as the yellow-green massonia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its fleshy, ribbed leaves and clusters of small, yellow-green flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
