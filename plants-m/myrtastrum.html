Myrtastrum

Myrtastrum, commonly known as the common myrtle or myrtle tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Myrtaceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which often emit a pleasant scent when crushed. The plants typically have small, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, berry-like fruits. Myrtastrum species are often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
