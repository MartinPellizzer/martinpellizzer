Mabea angustifolia var. genuina

Mabea angustifolia var. genuina, commonly known as the narrowleaf mabea, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems with alternate, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fibrous roots are adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native habitats.
