Myrcia laurifolia

Myrcia laurifolia, commonly known as Brazilian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are edible but often used in traditional medicine. Myrcia laurifolia thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal forests and mountainous regions.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural use in tropical gardens.
