Mapouria capituliflora

Mapouria capituliflora, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, daisy-like blooms with yellow central disks and white to pink ray florets. It typically grows in grasslands and savannas, thriving in well-drained soils.

Mapouria capituliflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to full sun exposure.
