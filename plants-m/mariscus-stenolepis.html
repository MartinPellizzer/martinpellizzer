Mariscus stenolepis

Mariscus stenolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mariscus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
