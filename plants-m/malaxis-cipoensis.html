Malaxis cipoensis

Malaxis cipoensis, commonly known as the Cipo orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on forest floors or along stream banks. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Malaxis cipoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
