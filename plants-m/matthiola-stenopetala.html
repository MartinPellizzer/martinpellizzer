Matthiola stenopetala

Matthiola stenopetala, commonly known as the narrow-petaled stock, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as cabbage and mustard. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the top of slender stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Matthiola stenopetala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
