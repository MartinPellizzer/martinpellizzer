Melochia pyramidata f. intermedia

Melochia pyramidata f. intermedia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or swamp hibiscus, is a variant of the melochia genus native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, humid environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its growth habit is herbaceous, with a sprawling or semi-woody structure depending on the environmental conditions.
