Moniera acuminata

Moniera acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically bright green and covered in fine hairs. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Moniera acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
