Malpighia campechiensis

Malpighia campechiensis, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are rich in vitamin C and are often consumed by birds and mammals.

Its botanical features include a sympodial growth habit and the ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different environments.
