Mikania dioscoreoides

Mikania dioscoreoides, commonly known as climbing hemp vine, is a vigorous, fast-growing vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a climbing habit, using tendrils to ascend supports, and it can spread rapidly in disturbed habitats. Its fibrous stems and leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
