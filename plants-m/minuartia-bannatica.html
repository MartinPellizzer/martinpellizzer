Minuartia bannatica

Minuartia bannatica, commonly known as the Bannat's rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, mat-forming perennial that typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with four petals, characteristic of the mustard family. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

It is native to Europe and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.
