Matthiola parvifolia

Matthiola parvifolia, commonly known as the dwarf stock, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact growth habit and prolonged flowering period.

Matthiola parvifolia is also valued for its use in phytotherapy and as a source of essential oils.
