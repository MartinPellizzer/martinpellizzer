Monanthium reticulatum

Monanthium reticulatum, commonly known as the net-veined false aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clustered, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive net-like venation pattern.

It produces small, nutlet-like fruits that disperse seeds through wind and water.
