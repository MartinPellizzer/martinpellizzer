Melocactus leucacanthus

Melocactus leucacanthus, commonly known as the white-spined melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem covered in white, woolly spines that provide insulation and protection. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by edible, melon-like fruits. It is adapted to survive in extreme drought conditions with a deep root system and thick, fleshy stems for water storage.

This cactus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
