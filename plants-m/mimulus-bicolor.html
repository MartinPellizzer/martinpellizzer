Mimulus bicolor

Mimulus bicolor, commonly known as the yellow monkeyflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with a distinctive red throat, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and in open woodlands. It has oval-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged opposite along the stem.

Mimulus bicolor is a herbaceous perennial that blooms from spring to early summer.
