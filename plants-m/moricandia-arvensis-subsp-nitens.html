Moricandia arvensis subsp. nitens

Moricandia arvensis subsp.

nitens, commonly known as the slender field mallows, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields.

The plant features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a hairy surface, and its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

The species is noted for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and its role in ecological succession.
