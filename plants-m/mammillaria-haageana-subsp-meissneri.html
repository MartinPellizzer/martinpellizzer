Mammillaria haageana subsp. meissneri

Mammillaria haageana subsp.

meissneri, commonly known as the Meissner's nipple cactus, is a compact, globular cactus native to Mexico. It features a cylindrical stem with numerous small, white to pale green tubercles that give it a distinctive, spiky appearance.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.

This subspecies is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its unique shape and ease of care.
