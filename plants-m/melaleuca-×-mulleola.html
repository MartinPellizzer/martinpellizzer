Melaleuca × mulleola

Melaleuca × mulleola, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paperbark, is a hybrid shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a reddish or brownish inner layer. The plant produces small, bottlebrush-like flowers that are typically pink or red, which appear in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a greyish-green hue and a rough texture.

Melaleuca × mulleola is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and inland areas, showing some tolerance to salt spray.
