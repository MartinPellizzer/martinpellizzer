Mentha aquatica var. grandifolia

Mentha aquatica var. grandifolia, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herb native to moist habitats in North America.

It features square, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic and slightly fuzzy. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from June to September. It thrives in wet soils near streams, marshes, and damp woodlands.

This variety is closely related to other mints and is valued for its medicinal and culinary uses.
