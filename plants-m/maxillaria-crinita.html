Maxillaria crinita

Maxillaria crinita, commonly known as the hairy maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, hairy sepals and petals, which give it its common name. The plant typically produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Maxillaria crinita is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
