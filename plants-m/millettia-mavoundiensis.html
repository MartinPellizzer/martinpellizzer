Millettia mavoundiensis

Millettia mavoundiensis, commonly known as the African senna, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It produces long, slender pods that contain small, hard seeds.

Millettia mavoundiensis is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
