Moricandia arvensis

Moricandia arvensis, commonly known as field glory, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and can be found in temperate regions. Its leaves are lobed and often have a peppery taste, similar to other members of its family.

Moricandia arvensis is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
