Mentha arvensis f. salebrosa

Mentha arvensis f. salebrosa, commonly known as field mint or wild mint, is a variant of the common mint species.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and creeping growth habit. This subspecies is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist, disturbed soils. Its flowers are typically small, pale blue or purple, and appear in whorls along the stems.

It is valued in herbal medicine for its cooling properties and is also used in culinary applications.
