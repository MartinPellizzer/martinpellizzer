Mespilodaphne pyriformis

Mespilodaphne pyriformis, commonly known as the oval-leaf daphne, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate or elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, often clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Mespilodaphne pyriformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
