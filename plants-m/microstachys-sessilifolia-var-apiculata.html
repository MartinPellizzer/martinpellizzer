Microstachys sessilifolia var. apiculata

Microstachys sessilifolia var. apiculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its narrow, sessile leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Its aromatic foliage and medicinal properties have made it of interest in traditional herbal practices.
