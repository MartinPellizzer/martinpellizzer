Mespilus aronia

Mespilus aronia, commonly known as the black chokeberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and anthocyanins. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

The berries are typically harvested in late summer and are used in jams, wines, and health supplements.
