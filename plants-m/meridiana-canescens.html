Meridiana canescens

Meridiana canescens, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its woody stems and hairy, gray-green leaves that help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to desert conditions.

Its botanical features reflect its evolutionary response to harsh, semi-arid climates.
