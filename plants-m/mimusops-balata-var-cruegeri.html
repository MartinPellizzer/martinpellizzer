Mimusops balata var. cruegeri

Mimusops balata var. cruegeri, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellowish-orange fruits containing smooth, edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.
