Myrcia cujabensis var. latifolia

Myrcia cujabensis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf myrcia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces woody capsules that contain numerous small, black seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
