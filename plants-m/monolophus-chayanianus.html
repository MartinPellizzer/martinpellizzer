Monolophus chayanianus

Monolophus chayanianus, commonly known as the Chayanian's lily, is a striking flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a rhizomatous root system that helps it thrive in well-drained soils.

It is often found in rocky or forested habitats and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
