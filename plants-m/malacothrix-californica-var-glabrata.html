Malacothrix californica var. glabrata

Malacothrix californica var. glabrata, commonly known as the California hairless dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant has glabrous (hairless) foliage and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, and is often found in coastal regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant gardening.
