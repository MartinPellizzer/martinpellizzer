Mentha × rotundifolia f. rugosa

Mentha × rotundifolia f. rugosa, commonly known as the wrinkled-leaf mint, is a hybrid perennial plant characterized by its distinctive, wavy, and often rugose (wrinkled) leaves.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is native to regions of Europe and Asia. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in terminal clusters during the summer months. It is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for container gardening and ornamental purposes.
