Maxillaria aciantha

Maxillaria aciantha, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and striking violet or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows as a sympodial orchid, forming a compact rosette of pseudobulbs. Its flowers are highly fragrant, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Maxillaria aciantha is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
