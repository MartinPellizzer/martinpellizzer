Masdevallia racemosa var. crossii

Masdevallia racemosa var. crossii, commonly known as the Cross's masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically blooms during the cooler months, often in late winter or early spring. Its pseudobulbs are short and stout, and the leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette pattern.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
