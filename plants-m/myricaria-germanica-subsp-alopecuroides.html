Myricaria germanica subsp. alopecuroides

Myricaria germanica subsp.

alopecuroides, commonly known as the woolly tamarisk, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers the stems and branches, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its adaptability makes it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping and erosion control in suitable climates.
