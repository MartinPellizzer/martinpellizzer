Manihot allemii

Manihot allemii, commonly known as the African arrowleaf, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels. Its latex is milky and can be toxic, containing various chemical compounds.

Manihot allemii is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
