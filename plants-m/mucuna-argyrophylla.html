Mucuna argyrophylla

Mucuna argyrophylla, commonly known as silver-leaf velvetbean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, trifoliate leaves and distinctive silver-gray undersides. The plant produces small, green pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in agricultural settings.

However, it can become invasive in some environments if not managed properly.
