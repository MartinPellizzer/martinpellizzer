Masdevallia × falcata

Masdevallia × falcata, commonly known as the sickle orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, sickle-shaped flower spikes that bear clusters of small, fragrant orchid flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It thrives in cool, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
