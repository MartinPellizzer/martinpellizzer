Mammillaria convoluta

Mammillaria convoluta, commonly known as the curled mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its compact size and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections.
