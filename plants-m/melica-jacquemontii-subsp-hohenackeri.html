Melica jacquemontii subsp. hohenackeri

Melica jacquemontii subsp.

hohenackeri, commonly known as Hohenacker's milletgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

The plant has slender, tufted culms with narrow, grass-like leaves, and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
