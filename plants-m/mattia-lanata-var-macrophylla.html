Mattia lanata var. macrophylla

Mattia lanata var. macrophylla, commonly known as the woolly thornapple, is a variant of the species Mattia lanata, characterized by its large, showy flowers and hairy, glandular leaves.

It is native to regions of South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, where it thrives in dry, open habitats. The plant produces distinctive, spiny fruits that contain toxic seeds, making it a cautionary species in its native range. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

The plant is also known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed soils, contributing to its ecological significance.
