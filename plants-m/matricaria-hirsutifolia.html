Matricaria hirsutifolia

Matricaria hirsutifolia, commonly known as the hairy fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Matricaria hirsutifolia is often used in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.
