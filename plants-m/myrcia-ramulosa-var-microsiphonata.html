Myrcia ramulosa var. microsiphonata

Myrcia ramulosa var. microsiphonata, commonly known as the small-horned myrcia, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. The flowers are typically pale yellow to orange, arranged in clusters, and are accompanied by small, glandular structures. The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when ripe.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
