Machilus cavaleriei

Machilus cavaleriei, commonly known as the Chinese laurustin, is a small evergreen shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, followed by oval, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Machilus cavaleriei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticulture for landscaping purposes.
