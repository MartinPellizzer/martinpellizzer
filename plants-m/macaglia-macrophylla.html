Macaglia macrophylla

Macaglia macrophylla, commonly known as the giant leaf orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad, and often waxy leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in clusters. Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening, though it requires specific humidity and light conditions to thrive.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is also protected in many regions.
