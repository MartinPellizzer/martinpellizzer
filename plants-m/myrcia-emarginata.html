Myrcia emarginata

Myrcia emarginata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it can become a problematic species in certain ecosystems.
