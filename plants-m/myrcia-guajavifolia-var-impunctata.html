Myrcia guajavifolia var. impunctata

Myrcia guajavifolia var. impunctata, commonly known as the smooth myrcia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic properties.
