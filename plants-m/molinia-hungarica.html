Molinia hungarica

Molinia hungarica, commonly known as the Hungarian moor grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It typically grows in wet, acidic soils, often found in bogs, marshes, and heathlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

Molinia hungarica plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and supporting local biodiversity.
