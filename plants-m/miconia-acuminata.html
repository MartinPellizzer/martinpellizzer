Miconia acuminata

Miconia acuminata, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white, five-petaled flowers, which are followed by round, fleshy berries containing numerous seeds. Miconia acuminata is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it a significant invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically impactful and botanically interesting.
