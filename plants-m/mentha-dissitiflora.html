Mentha dissitiflora

Mentha dissitiflora, commonly known as the broad-leaved mint, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Mentha dissitiflora is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
