Mentzelia puberula

Mentzelia puberula, commonly known as the silky mentzelia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats. It is a perennial herb that blooms from spring to early summer.

Mentzelia puberula plays a role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
