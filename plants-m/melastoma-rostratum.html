Melastoma rostratum

Melastoma rostratum, commonly known as the red-flowered melastoma, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
