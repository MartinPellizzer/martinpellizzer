Mentha mosztongensis

Mentha mosztongensis, commonly known as the Mosztongensis mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions in Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. Mentha mosztongensis is valued for its aromatic foliage, which contains essential oils used in traditional medicine.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
