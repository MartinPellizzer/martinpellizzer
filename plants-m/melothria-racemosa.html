Melothria racemosa

Melothria racemosa, commonly known as the coral vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color, with a mildly tart flavor. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found growing in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and pale green, arranged in clusters along the stems.

Melothria racemosa is valued in some regions for its edible fruits and ornamental appeal.
