Malaxis crassidens

Malaxis crassidens, commonly known as the broad-leaved malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Malaxis crassidens is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
