Mariscus sphaerocephalus

Mariscus sphaerocephalus, commonly known as the globe-headed mariscus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is distinguished by its spherical, yellowish flower heads that resemble a globe, hence its common name. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal regions.

Mariscus sphaerocephalus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
