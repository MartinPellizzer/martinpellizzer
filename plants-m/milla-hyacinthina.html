Milla hyacinthina

Milla hyacinthina, commonly known as the Dutch hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant belonging to the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to the Netherlands and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a short, thick underground bulb that stores nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Milla hyacinthina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to bloom reliably in spring.
