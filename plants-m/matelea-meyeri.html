Matelea meyeri

Matelea meyeri, commonly known as the Meyer's milkweed, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family, which includes milkweeds and related plants known for their milky sap. The plant produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink or reddish in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.
