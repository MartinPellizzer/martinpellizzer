Mentha × cheuchisensis

Mentha × cheuchisensis, commonly known as catnip, is a hybrid plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, which attract various pollinators. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been naturalized in many regions around the world.

Mentha × cheuchisensis is known for its strong scent and its historical use in herbal remedies and as a stimulant for cats.
