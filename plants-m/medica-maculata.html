Medica maculata

Medica maculata, commonly known as the spotted bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly rough texture and often have a bluish-green hue.

Medica maculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
