Mananthes vasculosa

Mananthes vasculosa, commonly known as the redroot goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often branching near the top. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late summer, which are arranged in dense, panicle-like clusters. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a rough, hairy texture.

The roots are reddish in color and contain compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal properties.
