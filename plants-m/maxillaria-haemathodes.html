Maxillaria haemathodes

Maxillaria haemathodes, commonly known as the blood-red orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red and pink flowers, which are often adorned with dark veins. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its pseudobulbs are short and thick, supporting its evergreen leaves.

The flowers bloom in clusters, making it a visually appealing and sought-after ornamental plant.
