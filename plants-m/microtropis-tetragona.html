Microtropis tetragona

Microtropis tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled microtropis, is a tropical climbing vine belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, four-angled stems and small, opposite leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white flowers that are typically followed by small, capsule-like fruits. It is native to Southeast Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is sometimes grown in gardens and greenhouses.
