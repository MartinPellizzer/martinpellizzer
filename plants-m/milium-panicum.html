Milium panicum

Milium panicum, commonly known as panicled millet, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, branched inflorescence that resembles a panicle, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands and marshes, thriving in temperate climates. Its small, spikelet-like flowers are usually green or pale yellow, and it produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Milium panicum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
