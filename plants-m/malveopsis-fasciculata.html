Malveopsis fasciculata

Malveopsis fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered malveopsis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dry seeds.
