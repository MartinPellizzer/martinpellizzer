Myrtopsis deplanchei

Myrtopsis deplanchei, commonly known as the Mexican myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes species like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are glossy and oval in shape, and its small, fragrant white flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Myrtopsis deplanchei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
