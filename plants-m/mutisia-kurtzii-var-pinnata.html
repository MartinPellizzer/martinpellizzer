Mutisia kurtzii var. pinnata

Mutisia kurtzii var. pinnata, commonly known as the pinnate mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a woody stem with alternate, pinnately compound leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
