Majorana ovalifolia

Majorana ovalifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf marjoram, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, oval-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color, and its small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. It is valued for its aromatic essential oils, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Majorana ovalifolia is also noted for its resilience to drought and its ability to attract beneficial insects.
