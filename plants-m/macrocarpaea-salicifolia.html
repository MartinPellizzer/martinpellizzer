Macrocarpaea salicifolia

Macrocarpaea salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaved macrocarpaea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that resemble those of willow trees, giving it its common name. It produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
