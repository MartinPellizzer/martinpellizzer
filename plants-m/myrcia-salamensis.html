Myrcia salamensis

Myrcia salamensis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a pleasant, aromatic scent.

The fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to red as it matures.
