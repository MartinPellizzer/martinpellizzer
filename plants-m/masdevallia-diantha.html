Masdevallia diantha

Masdevallia diantha, commonly known as the Pink Orchid, is a rare and exquisite orchid native to the high-altitude cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant typically grows as a miniature epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it requires cool, humid conditions to thrive.

Due to its beauty and rarity, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
