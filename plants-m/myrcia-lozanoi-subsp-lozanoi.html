Myrcia lozanoi subsp. lozanoi

Myrcia lozanoi subsp.

lozanoi, commonly known as the Lozano myrtle, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America. It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within cloud forests. Its leaves are elliptical and dark green, often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and use in traditional medicine.
