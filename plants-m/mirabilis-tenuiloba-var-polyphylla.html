Mirabilis tenuiloba var. polyphylla

Mirabilis tenuiloba var. polyphylla, commonly known as the many-leaved four-o'clock, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, tubular flowers that open in the late afternoon and close at dawn. The plant produces clusters of bright red or orange flowers, which attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique flowering behavior.
