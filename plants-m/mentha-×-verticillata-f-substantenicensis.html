Mentha × verticillata f. substantenicensis

Mentha × verticillata f. substantenicensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water mint, is a hybrid species within the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and its ability to thrive in moist environments, such as wetlands and stream banks. This variety is often found in temperate regions and is noted for its aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and its use in traditional herbal remedies.
