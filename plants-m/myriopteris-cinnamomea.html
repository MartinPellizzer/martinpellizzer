Myriopteris cinnamomea

Myriopteris cinnamomea, commonly known as the cinnamon fern, is a species of fern native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, arching fronds that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant has a distinctive, cinnamon-colored fertile frond that contrasts with the lighter, sterile fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Myriopteris cinnamomea is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
