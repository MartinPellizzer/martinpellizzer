Mouriri spathulata var. brachypoda

Mouriri spathulata var. brachypoda, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible. The flowers are usually clustered in inflorescences and are followed by the distinctive star-shaped fruits.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
