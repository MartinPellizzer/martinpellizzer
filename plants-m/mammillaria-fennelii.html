Mammillaria fennelii

Mammillaria fennelii, commonly known as the Fennelii prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, followed by small, red fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its compact size and unique appearance, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant.
