Martinezia acanthophylla

Martinezia acanthophylla, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its structure includes a woody base with ascending stems and opposite leaf arrangement.

Martinezia acanthophylla is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
