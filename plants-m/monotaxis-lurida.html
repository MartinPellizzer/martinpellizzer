Monotaxis lurida

Monotaxis lurida, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique plant species native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is a small, herbaceous plant with a distinctive, waxy appearance that helps it retain moisture in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that are often camouflaged to blend with its surroundings. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine, powdery coating, which reduces water loss.

Monotaxis lurida is adapted to survive in extreme conditions, making it a fascinating example of desert flora.
