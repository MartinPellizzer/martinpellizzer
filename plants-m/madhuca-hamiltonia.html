Madhuca hamiltonia

Madhuca hamiltonia, commonly known as the Indian butter tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark grey bark and large, elliptical leaves. The tree produces clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers, followed by edible, starchy fruits that are used in traditional cuisine. Its latex contains a substance similar to butter, which has been historically used in various applications.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
