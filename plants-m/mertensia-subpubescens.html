Mertensia subpubescens

Mertensia subpubescens, commonly known as the hairy bluebells, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Mertensia subpubescens is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
