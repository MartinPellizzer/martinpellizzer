Musa × paradisiaca subsp. normalis

Musa × paradisiaca subsp.

normalis, commonly known as the common banana. It is a member of the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and clustered, fleshy berries.

The plant is typically cultivated for its edible fruit, which comes in various cultivars with different flavors and textures. It thrives in tropical climates and requires warm temperatures and high humidity for optimal growth.

The plant's rhizome system supports its rapid growth and propagation through vegetative means.
