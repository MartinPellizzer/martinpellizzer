Mimosa nervosa var. missionum

Mimosa nervosa var. missionum, commonly known as the Mission mimosa, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are compound and bipinnate, giving it a lacy appearance. The plant produces small, yellow, ball-shaped flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
