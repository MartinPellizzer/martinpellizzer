Mentha × rotundifolia f. neglecta

Mentha × rotundifolia f. neglecta, commonly known as round-leaved mint or creeping mint, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both Mentha rotundifolia and Mentha × piperita.

It is a perennial herb with a low-growing, spreading habit, often forming dense mats on the ground. The leaves are rounded and slightly wrinkled, with a strong, aromatic scent when crushed. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its ability to suppress weeds.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of garden conditions.
