Marsdenia beatricis

Marsdenia beatricis, commonly known as the Beatrice's Marsdenia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asclepiadaceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are typically yellowish-green with a prominent, horn-like structure at the base, and it produces small, fleshy fruits.

Marsdenia beatricis is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental potential in horticulture.
