Myrcia platycaula

Myrcia platycaula, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a small, smooth, yellowish drupe that ripens in the dry season.

Myrcia platycaula is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
