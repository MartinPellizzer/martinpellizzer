Mentha dulcissima

Mentha dulcissima, commonly known as sweet mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, dark green leaves and slender, square stems. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Sweet mint is often used in culinary and aromatic applications due to its refreshing menthol-like scent.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.
