Miconia correae

Miconia correae, commonly known as the Brazilian star-apple tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are similar in appearance to apples, though they are typically smaller and have a distinct flavor. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in warm climates around the world.
