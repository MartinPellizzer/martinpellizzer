Malva atropurpurea

Malva atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple mallow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but is now cultivated in various regions for its ornamental value.

Malva atropurpurea prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
