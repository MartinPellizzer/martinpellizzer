Mimosa setosa var. nitens

Mimosa setosa var. nitens, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellow, spherical flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that can be toxic if ingested. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its rapid growth and ability to colonize new areas make it both a valuable ornamental and a potential invasive species.
