Molinaea canescens

Molinaea canescens, commonly known as the hairy mole plant, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender, hairy stems and small, white to pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swampy areas.

The plant is noted for its delicate appearance and is often found in wetland habitats.
