Myrcia tenuiflora

Myrcia tenuiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, berry-like fruits that are edible but have a spicy, peppery flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal and lowland regions.

Myrcia tenuiflora is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
