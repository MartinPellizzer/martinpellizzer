Mariscus retrorsus

Mariscus retrorsus, commonly known as the backward spur orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive floral structure. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers are small, usually pink or purple, and feature a unique spur that curves backward, giving it its common name.

Mariscus retrorsus is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
