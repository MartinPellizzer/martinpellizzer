Mycerinus

Mycerinus, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, translucent petals that appear to glow faintly in low light, giving it its ethereal appearance. The plant belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is known for its striking, showy blooms that appear during the cooler months. Mycerinus thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment.
