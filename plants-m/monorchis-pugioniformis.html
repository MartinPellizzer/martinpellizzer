Monorchis pugioniformis

Monorchis pugioniformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in three rows. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Monorchis pugioniformis is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
