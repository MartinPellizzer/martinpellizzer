Maxillaria barbosae

Maxillaria barbosae, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Brazil.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Maxillaria barbosae is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
