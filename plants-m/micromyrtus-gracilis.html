Micromyrtus gracilis

Micromyrtus gracilis, commonly known as the slender myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. Its flowering period occurs during the spring, producing clusters of white or pale pink blossoms that attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Micromyrtus gracilis is well adapted to sandy soils and can tolerate salt spray, making it a hardy plant in coastal environments.
