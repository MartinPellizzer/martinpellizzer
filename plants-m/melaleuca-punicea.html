Melaleuca punicea

Melaleuca punicea, commonly known as the red melaleuca, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal and heathland environments. The plant is characterized by its distinctive red or pink flowers, which appear in dense clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Melaleuca punicea is well adapted to sandy soils and is often found in areas with high salinity and coastal exposure.
