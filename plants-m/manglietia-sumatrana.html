Manglietia sumatrana

Manglietia sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran manglietia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indonesian island of Sumatra.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces striking white flowers with a strong, spicy fragrance, which bloom in clusters at the base of the leaves. It is often found in lowland and mountainous rainforests, thriving in humid and shaded environments.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
