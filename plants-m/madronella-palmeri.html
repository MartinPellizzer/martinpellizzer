Madronella palmeri

Madronella palmeri, commonly known as the Palmer's madrona, is a rare shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its gnarled, reddish-brown bark and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
