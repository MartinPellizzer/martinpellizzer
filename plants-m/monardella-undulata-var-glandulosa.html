Monardella undulata var. glandulosa

Monardella undulata var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular false monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as streambanks and meadows. Its leaves are often curled or undulating, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is a perennial herb that plays a role in supporting local pollinators, particularly bees and butterflies.
