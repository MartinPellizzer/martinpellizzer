Margarocarpus poeppigianus

Margarocarpus poeppigianus, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces woody capsules as its fruit.

The tree is often found in lowland rainforests and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan.
