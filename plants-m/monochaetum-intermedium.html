Monochaetum intermedium

Monochaetum intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate monochaetum, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Australia, specifically found in the eastern regions of Queensland and New South Wales. This plant is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The inflorescence consists of a single, dense spikelet that is often reddish in color.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in open habitats such as grasslands and heathlands.
