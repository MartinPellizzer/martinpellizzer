Matthiola undulata

Matthiola undulata, commonly known as the undulating stock, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its fragrant, yellow or white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are elongated, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Matthiola undulata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery.
