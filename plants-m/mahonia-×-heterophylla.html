Mahonia × heterophylla

Mahonia × heterophylla, commonly known as the golden smoketree, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that appear in late winter to early spring, followed by persistent blue-black berries. The plant has compound leaves with spiny tips, and its foliage turns vibrant red in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Mahonia × heterophylla is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to attract birds and pollinators.
