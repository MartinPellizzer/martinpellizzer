Mammillaria papyracantha

Mammillaria papyracantha, commonly known as the paper-spine cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne at the top of the stems. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its unique features, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in cactus collections.
