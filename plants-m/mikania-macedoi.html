Mikania macedoi

Mikania macedoi, commonly known as the Macedonian Mikania, is a climbing vine native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and hairy, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in capitula, typical of the family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
