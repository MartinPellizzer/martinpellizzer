Mimulus cupreus f. tigridiodes

Mimulus cupreus f. tigridiodes, commonly known as the Copper Cup with Tiger Stripe, is a hybrid species within the evening primrose family.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers with distinctive dark red veins, which resemble tiger stripes. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
