Morphixia lancea

Morphixia lancea, commonly known as the lanceleaf morphixia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Morphixia lancea thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the study of tropical flora and horticultural diversity.
