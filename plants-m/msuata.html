Msuata

Msuata, commonly known as the African violet, is a popular ornamental plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its velvety, waxy leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Msuata is often cultivated for its ability to bloom throughout the year under proper care.

Its compact growth habit makes it a favored choice for indoor gardening.
