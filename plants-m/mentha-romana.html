Mentha romana

Mentha romana, commonly known as the field mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated edge. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stem. Mentha romana is often found in moist soils near streams, meadows, and woodland edges.

It is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
