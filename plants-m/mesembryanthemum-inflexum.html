Mesembryanthemum inflexum

Mesembryanthemum inflexum, commonly known as the blue ice plant, is a succulent perennial native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-gray leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to store water in its thick leaves allows it to survive in arid conditions.
