Mentha longifolia var. doratophylla

Mentha longifolia var. doratophylla, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and square, branching stems. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variant is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves used in culinary and medicinal applications.
