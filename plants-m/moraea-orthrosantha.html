Moraea orthrosantha

Moraea orthrosantha, commonly known as the Cape bellflower, is a striking perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Moraea orthrosantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
