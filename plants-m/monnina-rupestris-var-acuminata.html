Monnina rupestris var. acuminata

Monnina rupestris var. acuminata, commonly known as the sharpleaf monnina, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, grayish hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a pointed tip. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale pink to violet, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy plant in dry landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting addition to xeriscapes and native plant gardens.
