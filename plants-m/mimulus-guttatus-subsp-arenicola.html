Mimulus guttatus subsp. arenicola

Mimulus guttatus subsp.

arenicola, commonly known as the sand fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils.

The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with five petals, often with a distinctive spur at the base of each petal. It thrives in disturbed habitats such as coastal dunes, roadsides, and dry meadows.

Its adaptability to harsh environments makes it an important component of its ecological community.
