Malaisia tortuosa var. viridescens

Malaisia tortuosa var. viridescens, commonly known as the green-stemmed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are red or orange when mature and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is found in tropical and subtropical regions, often in humid forest environments.
