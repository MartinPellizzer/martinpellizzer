Melocactus pachyacanthus

Melocactus pachyacanthus, commonly known as the thick-spined melon cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its robust, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant features dense, sharp spines that protect it from herbivores and provide shade. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, blooming in late spring or early summer.

The plant is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stem.
