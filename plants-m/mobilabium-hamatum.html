Mobilabium hamatum

Mobilabium hamatum, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions. Its leaves are reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss and provide protection from herbivores.

The golden star cactus is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
