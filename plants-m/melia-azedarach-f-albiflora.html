Melia azedarach f. albiflora

Melia azedarach f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered chinaberry, is a variant of the chinaberry tree native to Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring. The plant has compound leaves with opposite arrangement and produces small, orange fruits that contain toxic seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its showy blossoms and rapid growth.

However, it is considered invasive in some regions, posing a threat to native ecosystems.
