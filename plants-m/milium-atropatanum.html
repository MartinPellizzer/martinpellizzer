Milium atropatanum

Milium atropatanum, commonly known as the black millet, is a small annual grass native to Central Asia.

It produces tiny, dark-colored grains that are harvested for their nutritional value. The plant typically grows in dry, temperate climates and has narrow, grass-like leaves. Its seeds are rich in carbohydrates and have been used in traditional cuisines.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as a drought-resistant crop.
