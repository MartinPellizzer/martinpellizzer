Melaleuca pithyoides

Melaleuca pithyoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf paperbark, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a white, papery inner layer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a greyish or whitish underside.

Melaleuca pithyoides produces small, fragrant white flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.
