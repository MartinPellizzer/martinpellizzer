Masdevallia magaliana

Masdevallia magaliana, commonly known as the Magdalena orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, often colorful flowers. The plant typically features a pseudobulb with a single or few leaves, and its flowers are usually clustered in a raceme. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
