Myrcia palustris var. stictophylla

Myrcia palustris var. stictophylla, commonly known as the swamp myrtle, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, leathery capsule that contains several seeds.

The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and forest understories.
