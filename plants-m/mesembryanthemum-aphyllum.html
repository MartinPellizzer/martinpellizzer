Mesembryanthemum aphyllum

Mesembryanthemum aphyllum, commonly known as the leafless ice plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the cooler months. Its lack of leaves is an adaptation to reduce water loss through transpiration.

Mesembryanthemum aphyllum is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative ground cover due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
