Miconia leacongestiflora

Miconia leacongestiflora, commonly known as the dense-flower miconia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its rapid growth rate and ability to form dense thickets make it an invasive species in some areas.

The plant's botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a challenge for land management.
