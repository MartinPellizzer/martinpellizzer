Mentha sylvestris var. incana

Mentha sylvestris var. incana, commonly known as woodland mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, downy hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and have a strong, minty aroma. It produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters during the summer months.

This variant is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and is valued for its aromatic properties and use in herbal medicine.
