Mitropsidium oblanceolatum

Mitropsidium oblanceolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are often clustered together. Its berries are usually red or orange and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its host trees.
