Mertensia fulva

Mertensia fulva, commonly known as the yellow heartleaf bluebell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in spring. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white down, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Mertensia fulva is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
