Mammillaria viescensis

Mammillaria viescensis, commonly known as the Viesca's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a spiny, segmented stem that typically grows to about 10 centimeters in height. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Its unique appearance and relatively easy care make it a favored plant among cactus enthusiasts.
