Miscanthidium flavescens

Miscanthidium flavescens, commonly known as the golden grass or yellow bluemink, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, bright yellow to golden-green leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are often overlooked due to their subtle appearance.

Miscanthidium flavescens plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
