Maxillaria graminifolia

Maxillaria graminifolia, commonly known as the grass-leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette formation. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with a distinct lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Maxillaria graminifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
