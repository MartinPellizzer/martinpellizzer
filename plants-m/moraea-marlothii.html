Moraea marlothii

Moraea marlothii, commonly known as the Marloth's glory, is a striking South African bulbous plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in the Western and Eastern Cape provinces. It blooms during the late winter to early spring season, adding a vibrant splash of color to the landscape.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
