Mentha viridis subsp. crispata

Mentha viridis subsp.

crispata, commonly known as narrow-leaf mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly curled or crinkled, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks. Mentha viridis subsp.

crispata is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.
