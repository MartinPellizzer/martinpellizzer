Mentha × elata var. amplissima

Mentha × elata var. amplissima, commonly known as the broad-leaved mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that appear in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers make it a valuable species for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
