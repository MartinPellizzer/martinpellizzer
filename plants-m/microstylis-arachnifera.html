Microstylis arachnifera

Microstylis arachnifera, commonly known as the spider-hair grass, is a small, delicate perennial grass species native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and tiny, thread-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and ecologists studying wetland ecosystems.
