Mentha pulegium var. hyperiantha

Mentha pulegium var. hyperiantha, commonly known as sweet mint, is a variant of the water mint species characterized by its aromatic, slightly sweet scent.

It is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia, often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, pale blue or purple flowers. Its essential oils contain menthone and pulegone, contributing to its distinctive fragrance and medicinal properties.

It is often used in traditional herbal remedies and as a culinary herb.
