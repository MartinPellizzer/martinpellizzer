Morus nigra var. laciniata

Morus nigra var. laciniata, commonly known as the black mulberry with lobed leaves, is a variety of the black mulberry tree native to the Middle East and South Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black fruits, which are typically harvested in late summer. The leaves are distinctly lobed, giving the plant a unique appearance compared to other mulberry species. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

This variety is also valued for its ability to provide shade and support wildlife in its native habitats.
