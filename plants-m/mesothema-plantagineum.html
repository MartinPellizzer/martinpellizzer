Mesothema plantagineum

Mesothema plantagineum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its fibrous roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
