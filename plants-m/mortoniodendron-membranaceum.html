Mortoniodendron membranaceum

Mortoniodendron membranaceum, commonly known as the paperbark tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, thin, and often peeling bark that gives it its common name. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a slightly waxy texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing a single seed.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in humid, warm climates.
