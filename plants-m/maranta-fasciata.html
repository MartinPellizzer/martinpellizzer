Maranta fasciata

Maranta fasciata, commonly known as the Crimson Beauty, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often adorned with vivid red and green markings. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family, known for its ornamental foliage rather than its flowers. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires indirect light to flourish.

Maranta fasciata is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
