Mentha subacutidentata

Mentha subacutidentata, commonly known as narrow-leaved mint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Mentha subacutidentata is often used in traditional medicine for its aromatic and potentially therapeutic properties.
