Mammillaria goodridgei var. louisae

Mammillaria goodridgei var. louisae, commonly known as the Louisae Hedgehog Cactus, is a compact, low-growing cactus with a globular shape and a dense covering of small, white spines.

It originates from the arid regions of Mexico, specifically the state of Coahuila, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It is a popular choice for rock gardens and container cultivation due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.

This variety is known for its slow growth and ability to withstand drought conditions, making it an ideal plant for desert landscapes.
