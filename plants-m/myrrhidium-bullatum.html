Myrrhidium bullatum

Myrrhidium bullatum, commonly known as the yellow mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Myrrhidium bullatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
