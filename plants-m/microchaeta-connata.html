Microchaeta connata

Microchaeta connata, commonly known as the Florida panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, typical of the grass family. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along the edges of water bodies.

Microchaeta connata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
