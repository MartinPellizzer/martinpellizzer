Mirabilis jalapa var. jalapa

Mirabilis jalapa var. jalapa, commonly known as the four o'clock flower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in the late afternoon and remain open until dawn. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
