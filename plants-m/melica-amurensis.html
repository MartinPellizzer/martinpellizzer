Melica amurensis

Melica amurensis, commonly known as Amur bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Melica amurensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet environments.
