Masdevallia amaluzae

Masdevallia amaluzae, commonly known as the Amazonian ghost orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, translucent petals and sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in the dense canopy of the rainforest. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of ghostly forms.

Due to its limited habitat and habitat destruction, it is considered critically endangered.
