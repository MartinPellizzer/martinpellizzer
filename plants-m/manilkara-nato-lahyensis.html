Manilkara nato-lahyensis

Manilkara nato-lahyensis, commonly known as the Madagascar tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, rough-skinned fruits containing a sweet, sticky pulp.

Its latex is used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive due to its viscous properties.
