Masdevallia frontinoensis

Masdevallia frontinoensis, commonly known as the Frontino orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and striking, showy inflorescences. The plant typically produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in cool, humid environments with partial shade and well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
