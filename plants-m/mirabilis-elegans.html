Mirabilis elegans

Mirabilis elegans, commonly known as the evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the evening and close by dawn. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including pink, red, and yellow, and are pollinated primarily by moths and other nocturnal insects.

The plant is also known for its oily seeds, which are used in various commercial applications.
