Munronia heterophylla

Munronia heterophylla, commonly known as the variable leaf munronia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often variable in shape leaves, which can range from elliptic to ovate. The plant produces small, white to pale green flowers in terminal clusters, typically blooming in late spring. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that releases tiny seeds when mature.

Munronia heterophylla is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or swampy habitats.
