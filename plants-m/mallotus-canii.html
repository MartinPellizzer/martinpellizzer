Mallotus canii

Mallotus canii, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its surroundings. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges, and its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Mallotus canii is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
