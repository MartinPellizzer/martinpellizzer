Mikania stuebelii var. typica

Mikania stuebelii var. typica, commonly known as the creeping ox-eye, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit, with slender, hairy stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with white centers, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped, with a rough texture and a serrated margin.

It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.
