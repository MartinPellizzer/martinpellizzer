Manihot esculenta var. digitifolia

Manihot esculenta var. digitifolia, commonly known as the African arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to West Africa.

It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces a starchy, edible root that is often used as a thickening agent in cooking. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is cultivated for both its nutritional value and its ornamental appeal.

Its roots are rich in carbohydrates and can be processed into flour or used directly in various dishes.
