Macaranga reiteriana

Macaranga reiteriana, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its fast growth and is sometimes used in reforestation efforts due to its ecological benefits.
