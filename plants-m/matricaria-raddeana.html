Matricaria raddeana

Matricaria raddeana, commonly known as the Russian chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Eurasia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with white centers. The plant typically grows in meadows and dry grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are serrated and aromatic, often used in traditional medicine for their calming properties.

The flowers are harvested for their essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and herbal remedies.
