Miconia alterninervia

Miconia alterninervia, commonly known as the "Mexican beautyberry," is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. These fruits are an important food source for various birds and mammals in its native habitat.

Miconia alterninervia is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it both ecologically significant and sometimes invasive.
