Malvaviscus brevibracteatus

Malvaviscus brevibracteatus, commonly known as the short-bract mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a red throat. Its leaves are typically ovate and have a velvety texture, while the stems are hairy and somewhat woody.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
