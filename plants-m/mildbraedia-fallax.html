Mildbraedia fallax

Mildbraedia fallax, commonly known as the mountain lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and a compact, upright growth habit. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is relatively rare in the wild.
