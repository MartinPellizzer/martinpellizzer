Muscari microstomum

Muscari microstomum, commonly known as the small-mouth musk grape, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. Native to the Mediterranean region, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge in early spring.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
