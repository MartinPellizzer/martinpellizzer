Miconia crocata

Miconia crocata, commonly known as the red miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy layer. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains numerous seeds.

Miconia crocata is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it both ecologically significant and sometimes invasive.
