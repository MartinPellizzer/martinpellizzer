Molinia maxima

Molinia maxima, commonly known as the tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and forms large clumps with long, narrow leaves. The plant produces tall, slender spikes of pale green to brownish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands, bogs, and along stream banks.

Molinia maxima plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
