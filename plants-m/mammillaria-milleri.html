Mammillaria milleri

Mammillaria milleri, commonly known as the Mexican snowball cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of white, woolly spines and small, pale green stems. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments and is well adapted to drought conditions. Its flowers, which are usually pink or purple, bloom in late spring or early summer.

Mammillaria milleri is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation due to its compact size and ornamental appearance.
