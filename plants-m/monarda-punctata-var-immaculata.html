Monarda punctata var. immaculata

Monarda punctata var. immaculata, commonly known as the white bergamot, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are gray-green in color and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
