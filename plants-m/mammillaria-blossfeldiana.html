Mammillaria blossfeldiana

Mammillaria blossfeldiana, commonly known as the Blossfeld's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rounded, globular shape and dense covering of small, white spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its compact size and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
