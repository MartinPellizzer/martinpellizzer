Menonvillea falcata

Menonvillea falcata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in countries such as Peru and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found growing in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized orchid species.
