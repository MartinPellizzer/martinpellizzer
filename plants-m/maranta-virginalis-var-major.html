Maranta virginalis var. major

Maranta virginalis var. major, commonly known as the white-veined arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves with prominent white veins, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The rhizomes of this plant are also used in traditional medicine and as a source of starch.
