Magnolia pravertiana

Magnolia pravertiana, commonly known as the Pravertian magnolia, is a rare and ornamental flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring, often in shades of white to pale pink. The plant produces distinctive, elongated fruit capsules that split open to release its seeds. Its leaves are broad and glossy, providing a lush backdrop to its striking blooms.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable species for botanical conservation efforts.
