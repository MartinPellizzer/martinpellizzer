Mammillaria sulcoglandulifera

Mammillaria sulcoglandulifera, commonly known as the ridged mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white to pale green tubercles. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
