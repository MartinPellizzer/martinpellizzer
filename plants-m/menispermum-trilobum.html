Menispermum trilobum

Menispermum trilobum, commonly known as the Indian turnsole, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Menispermaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. Its roots contain alkaloids and have been used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
