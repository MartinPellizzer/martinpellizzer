Mimosa lepidorepens

Mimosa lepidorepens, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
