Morus alpina

Morus alpina, commonly known as the alpine mulberry, is a species of mulberry native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows at high altitudes, often in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually dark purple to black in color. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and have a smooth or slightly serrated edge.

Morus alpina is adapted to cold climates and is often found in areas with limited soil nutrients.
