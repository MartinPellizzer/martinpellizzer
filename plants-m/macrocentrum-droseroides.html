Macrocentrum droseroides

Macrocentrum droseroides, commonly known as the slender sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, drooping leaves that are covered in sticky, glandular hairs used to trap insects. The plant typically grows in wet, boggy habitats and thrives in high humidity. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a delicate, pinkish hue.

Due to its unique appearance and trapping mechanism, it is a popular specimen among carnivorous plant enthusiasts.
