Myosotis oraria

Myosotis oraria, commonly known as the blue-eyed Mary, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Myosotis oraria has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
