Morinda montana

Morinda montana, commonly known as mountain morinda, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its erect stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its fruit is a capsule containing numerous small seeds.

Morinda montana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
