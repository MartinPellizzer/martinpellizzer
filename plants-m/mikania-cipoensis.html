Mikania cipoensis

Mikania cipoensis, commonly known as the Brazilian Mikania, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
