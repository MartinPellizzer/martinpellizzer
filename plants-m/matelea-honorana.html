Matelea honorana

Matelea honorana, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant produces a milky sap that contains toxic compounds, which can be harmful if ingested. Its flowers emit a strong, sweet fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.
