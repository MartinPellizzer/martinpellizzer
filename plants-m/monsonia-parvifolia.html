Monsonia parvifolia

Monsonia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved monsonia, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grassland and woodland habitats, often in regions with a temperate climate. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear small, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, producing pale blue to purple blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Monsonia parvifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
