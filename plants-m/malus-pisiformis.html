Malus pisiformis

Malus pisiformis, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a species of apple tree native to China.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is typically small, with a pale yellow or white skin and a sweet, juicy flesh. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as for its edible fruit.

This plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for temperate climates.
