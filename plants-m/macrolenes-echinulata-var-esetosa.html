Macrolenes echinulata var. esetosa

Macrolenes echinulata var. esetosa, commonly known as the hairy macrolene, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
