Moraea ciliata subsp. lutescens

Moraea ciliata subsp.

lutescens, commonly known as the yellow-veined moraea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa. It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with prominent yellow veins.

The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a sought-after plant among horticulturists.
