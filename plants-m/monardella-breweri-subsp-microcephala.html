Monardella breweri subsp. microcephala

Monardella breweri subsp.

microcephala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mountain mint, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or open woodland habitats, often at higher elevations. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink to purple in color and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller, more compact leaves compared to other members of the Monardella species.
