Malvaviscus grandiflorus

Malvaviscus grandiflorus, commonly known as the largeflower mallows, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, loamy soil.

Malvaviscus grandiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its long-lasting blooms.
