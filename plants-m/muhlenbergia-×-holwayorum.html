Muhlenbergia × holwayorum

Muhlenbergia × holwayorum, commonly known as the purple muhlenbergia, is a hybrid grass species native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and vibrant purple to pinkish foliage, which gives it a striking appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Due to its drought tolerance and attractive appearance, it is often cultivated as a landscape plant in arid regions.
