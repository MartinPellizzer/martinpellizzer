Mesembryanthemum microspermum

Mesembryanthemum microspermum, commonly known as the narrow-seeded ice plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent species characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant thrives in arid and coastal environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh, dry conditions. Its leaves are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss through transpiration.

This species is popular in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental value.
