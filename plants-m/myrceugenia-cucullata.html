Myrceugenia cucullata

Myrceugenia cucullata, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, berry-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and local cuisine. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal and lowland regions.

Myrceugenia cucullata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
