Mucuna spuria

Mucuna spuria, commonly known as the wild velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, spiny pods. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. Mucuna spuria is known for its nitrogen-fixing properties, making it useful in agricultural settings.

Its seeds contain compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal uses.
