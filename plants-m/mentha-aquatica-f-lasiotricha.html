Mentha aquatica f. lasiotricha

Mentha aquatica f. lasiotricha, commonly known as the woolly water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and aromatic, slightly minty scent. The plant typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, often along the edges of ponds, streams, and marshes. Its flowers are small, pale blue to purple, and appear in whorls along the stems.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to aquatic conditions and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
