Mimulus floribundus var. membranaceus

Mimulus floribundus var. membranaceus, commonly known as the membranous monkeyflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features showy, bright orange to yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a hairy underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in stream banks and meadows.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
