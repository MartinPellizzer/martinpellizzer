Mischarytera macrobotrys

Mischarytera macrobotrys, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, floating on the water's surface. The plant produces showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom at night, attracting pollinators such as moths. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it in shallow water while absorbing nutrients from the aquatic environment.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter for aquatic life and contributing to water quality regulation.
