Melicope tsaratananensis

Melicope tsaratananensis, commonly known as the Madagascar lauraceae, is a small evergreen tree native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and woody structure. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the rainforests of Madagascar. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a distinct aromatic scent when crushed.

Melicope tsaratananensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
