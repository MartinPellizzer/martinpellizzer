Medinilla blumeana

Medinilla blumeana, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or velvetleaf medinilla, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Medinilla blumeana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
