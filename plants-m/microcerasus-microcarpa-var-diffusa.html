Microcerasus microcarpa var. diffusa

Microcerasus microcarpa var. diffusa, commonly known as the Chinese sand pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, followed by edible, sweet, and aromatic fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

The varietal form 'diffusa' is distinguished by its more spreading growth habit compared to the typical form.
