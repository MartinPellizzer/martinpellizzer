Meborea

Meborea, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves and pale, almost ethereal blossoms that appear only under specific environmental conditions. The plant's botanical classification remains uncertain, with some botanists suggesting it belongs to the orchid family. Meborea thrives in cool, humid climates and requires a unique combination of soil and light conditions to flourish.

Due to its elusive nature and limited distribution, it is considered one of the most challenging plants to study in the botanical world.
