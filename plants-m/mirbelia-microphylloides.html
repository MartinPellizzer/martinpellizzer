Mirbelia microphylloides

Mirbelia microphylloides, commonly known as the small-leaf mirbelia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is typically found in coastal regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
