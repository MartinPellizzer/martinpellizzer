Miconia sessilis

Miconia sessilis, commonly known as the sessile miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically sessile, meaning they lack a petiole. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with five petals, often in shades of red or purple, which attract various pollinators. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Miconia sessilis is also known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain regions, making it a plant of ecological concern.
