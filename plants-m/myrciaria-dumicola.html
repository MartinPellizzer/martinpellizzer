Myrciaria dumicola

Myrciaria dumicola, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry or araçá-preto, is a shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are elliptical with a glossy surface, and it produces small, fragrant white flowers.

The fruit is often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine for its nutritional and medicinal properties.
