Malaxis maclaudii

Malaxis maclaudii, commonly known as the MacLaurie's Malaxis, is a rare orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive hood-like structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it has a short, fibrous root system.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
