Monodora hastipetala

Monodora hastipetala, commonly known as the African tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The tree produces edible, angular fruits that contain several seeds surrounded by a pulp resembling tamarind. It is valued for its ornamental appearance and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of timber.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for agroforestry and reforestation projects.
