Mentha × subundulata

Mentha × subundulata, commonly known as catnip, is a hybrid plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, which appear in late summer. Mentha × subundulata is native to Europe and Asia but has been naturalized in many parts of the world.

It is known for its strong aromatic properties and is often used in herbal remedies and as a stimulant for cats.
