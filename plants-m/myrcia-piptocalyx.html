Myrcia piptocalyx

Myrcia piptocalyx, commonly known as the pink myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, where it thrives in humid, shady environments. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fragrant foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant also has a long history of use in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
