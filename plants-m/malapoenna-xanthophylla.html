Malapoenna xanthophylla

Malapoenna xanthophylla, commonly known as the yellowleaf malapoenna, is a rare and striking plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow foliage, which is due to the presence of carotenoids in its leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with small, pale green flowers that bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
