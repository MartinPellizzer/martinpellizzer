Malache castaneifolia

Malache castaneifolia, commonly known as the chestnut-leaf malache, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that resemble the texture and color of chestnut leaves, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by berries that range in color from red to black. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
