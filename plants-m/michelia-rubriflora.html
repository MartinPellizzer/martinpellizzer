Michelia rubriflora

Michelia rubriflora, commonly known as the red-flowered michelia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and striking red or pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The flowers are typically borne in clusters and have a pleasant fragrance, making it a popular ornamental plant. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

The plant is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various pollinators.
