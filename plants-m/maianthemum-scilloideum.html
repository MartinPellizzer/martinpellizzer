Maianthemum scilloideum

Maianthemum scilloideum, commonly known as the nodding lady's tresses, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

It is often found in association with other woodland flora, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
