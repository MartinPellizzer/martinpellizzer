Marsdenia magallanesiana

Marsdenia magallanesiana, commonly known as the Magallanes Marsdenia, is a climbing vine native to South America, particularly found in regions of Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family, which includes many plants with milky sap and showy flowers. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink.

Its fruit is a follicle that splits open to release seeds, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
