Mammillaria inclinis

Mammillaria inclinis, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in short, white spines and prominent areoles. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months.

Due to its compact size and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus collections.
