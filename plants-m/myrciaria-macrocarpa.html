Myrciaria macrocarpa

Myrciaria macrocarpa, commonly known as the Brazilian guava or araçá-do-mato, is a small evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing a sweet, aromatic pulp. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
