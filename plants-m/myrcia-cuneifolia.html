Myrcia cuneifolia

Myrcia cuneifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that belongs to the Myrtaceae family. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are reddish-black drupes that contain a single seed, and the plant is known for its aromatic properties.

Myrcia cuneifolia is often used in traditional medicine and has ecological significance in its native habitats.
