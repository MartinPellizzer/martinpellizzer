Megacodon stylophorus

Megacodon stylophorus, commonly known as the giant sedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like stature, with long, narrow leaves and a robust rhizomatous root system. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and botanical studies.
