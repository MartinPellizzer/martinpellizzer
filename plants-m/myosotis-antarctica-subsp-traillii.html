Myosotis antarctica subsp. traillii

Myosotis antarctica subsp.

traillii, commonly known as the Antarctic forget-me-not, is a small herbaceous plant native to the cold climates of the Southern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its delicate blue or purple flowers and hairy, narrow leaves.

This subspecies is typically found in rocky soils and alpine environments, thriving in harsh, high-altitude conditions. Its flowers bloom during the short summer season, adapting to the extreme seasonal variations of its habitat.

The plant plays a minor ecological role in its environment, contributing to local biodiversity in its specialized niche.
