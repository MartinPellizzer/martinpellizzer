Myrceugenia nannophylla

Myrceugenia nannophylla, commonly known as the small-leaf myrtle, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the temperate rainforests of southern South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a waxy coating. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically followed by edible, red, fleshy berries.

Myrceugenia nannophylla plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food for various birds and mammals.
