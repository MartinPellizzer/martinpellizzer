Malacocarpus maassii

Malacocarpus maassii, commonly known as the Maasii palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are a source of nutrition for various animals. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.

However, it requires well-drained soil and consistent moisture to thrive.
