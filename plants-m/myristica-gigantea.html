Myristica gigantea

Myristica gigantea, commonly known as the giant nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody structure and large, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval-shaped fruits that contain the aromatic seeds used for nutmeg and mace. Its bark and roots also contain essential oils and have been used in traditional medicine.

Myristica gigantea is known for its slow growth and is considered a valuable species in both ecological and commercial contexts.
