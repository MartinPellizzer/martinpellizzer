Mentha × correvonis

Mentha × correvonis, commonly known as catnip, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between *Mentha viridis* and *Mentha suaveolens*.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers in whorled clusters, which are typically borne on erect spikes. Mentha × correvonis is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is widely cultivated for its aromatic properties and has a long history of use in traditional medicine and as a stimulant for cats.
