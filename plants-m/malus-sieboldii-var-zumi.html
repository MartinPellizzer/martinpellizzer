Malus sieboldii var. zumi

Malus sieboldii var. zumi, commonly known as the Japanese flowering crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers in early spring. The plant produces small, edible apples that are often used in traditional Japanese cuisine. Its foliage turns vibrant red or orange in the fall, adding seasonal interest.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
