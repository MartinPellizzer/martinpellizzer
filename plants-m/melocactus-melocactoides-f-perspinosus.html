Melocactus melocactoides f. perspinosus

Melocactus melocactoides f. perspinosus, commonly known as the spiny melocactus, is a unique variant of the melocactus genus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, sharp spines, which provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. Its succulent structure allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to arid environments.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
