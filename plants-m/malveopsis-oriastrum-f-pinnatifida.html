Malveopsis oriastrum f. pinnatifida

Malveopsis oriastrum f. pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallow, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its slender, pinnately lobed leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
