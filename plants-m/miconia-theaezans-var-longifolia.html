Miconia theaezans var. longifolia

Miconia theaezans var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved miconia, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
