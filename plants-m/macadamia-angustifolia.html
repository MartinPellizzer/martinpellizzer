Macadamia angustifolia

Macadamia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf macadamia, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and produces edible nuts enclosed in a hard shell. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil. Its flowers are typically small and yellow, and it is cultivated for its high-fat seeds.

The botanical features of Macadamia angustifolia make it a valuable species in both horticulture and agriculture.
