Mokof verticillata

Mokof verticillata, commonly known as the spiral leaf plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive whorled arrangement of leaves.

It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in both gardens and research settings.
