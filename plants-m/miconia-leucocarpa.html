Miconia leucocarpa

Miconia leucocarpa, commonly known as white-fruited miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces fleshy, white berries that are edible but often have a bland taste. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a problematic species in certain regions.
