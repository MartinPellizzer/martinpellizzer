Masdevallia bangii

Masdevallia bangii, commonly known as the Bangii orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit and produces intricate, often fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms during the cooler months, and its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the forest floor. Masdevallia bangii prefers high humidity and indirect light, making it a popular choice for shade-loving orchid enthusiasts.

Its unique floral structure and elegant appearance contribute to its status as a sought-after species in horticulture.
