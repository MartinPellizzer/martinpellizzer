Myrica gale var. subarctica

Myrica gale var. subarctica, commonly known as the northern wax myrtle, is a shrub native to northern regions of North America and Eurasia.

It is characterized by its small, waxy, elliptical leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands, bogs, and along stream banks.

This variety is known for its aromatic foliage and its ability to tolerate cold climates.
