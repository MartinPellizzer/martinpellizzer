Myrcia curatellifolia var. parvifolia

Myrcia curatellifolia var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved myrcia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and birds.

The fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.
