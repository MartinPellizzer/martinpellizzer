Melinis diminuta

Melinis diminuta, commonly known as little quillwort, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Melinis diminuta plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
