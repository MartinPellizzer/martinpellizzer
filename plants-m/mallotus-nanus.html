Mallotus nanus

Mallotus nanus, commonly known as the small mallot, is a small shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in cup-like structures called cyathia. Its fruits are capsule-like and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Mallotus nanus is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments.
