Monochilon tomentosus

Monochilon tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa, characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy foliage. The plant has narrow, linear leaves covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Monochilon tomentosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
