Malacothrix platyphylla

Malacothrix platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaf malacothrix, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow ray florets and brown disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually narrow and flat, giving it the species name "platyphylla," which means "flat leaf.".

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive blooms and compact growth habit.
