Maxillaria gracilis var. queirogana

Maxillaria gracilis var. queirogana, commonly known as the Queiroga's slender orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It features compact, greenish-yellow flowers with intricate patterns and a distinctive, elongated shape. The plant typically grows as a pseudobulbous orchid, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy coating. It prefers warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
