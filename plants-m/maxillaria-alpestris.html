Maxillaria alpestris

Maxillaria alpestris, commonly known as the alpine maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, hairy pseudobulbs and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in cool, high-altitude environments and requires well-drained soil.

Maxillaria alpestris is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its resilience and ornamental value.
