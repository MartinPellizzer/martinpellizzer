Masdevallia chimboensis

Masdevallia chimboensis, commonly known as the Chimboensis Masdevallia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is a small to medium-sized orchid with a distinctive appearance, featuring intricate, often colorful floral structures. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually arranged in a spike and can exhibit a range of colors, including red, orange, and yellow.

Masdevallia chimboensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
