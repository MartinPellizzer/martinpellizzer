Malva sylvestris

Malva sylvestris, commonly known as the wild mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus, and is characterized by its fuzzy, hairy leaves and showy pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its soothing properties and is also a popular garden plant for its ornamental value.
