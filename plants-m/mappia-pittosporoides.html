Mappia pittosporoides

Mappia pittosporoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a small shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, oval berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland forest areas.

Mappia pittosporoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
