Marsdenia abyssinica f. complicata

Marsdenia abyssinica f. complicata, commonly known as the Abyssinian Marsdenia, is a unique variant of the Marsdenia genus native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its intricate, often twisted or curled leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, featuring small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. Its foliage is thick and leathery, adapted to thrive in the humid, shaded conditions of its native habitat.

This fern-like form makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.
