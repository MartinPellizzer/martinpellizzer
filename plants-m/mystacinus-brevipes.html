Mystacinus brevipes

Mystacinus brevipes, commonly known as the short-footed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees in tropical rainforests.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces white, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals. The plant lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its haustoria, which attach to the host's vascular system. It is typically found in the canopy layer of humid, lowland forests.

Mystacinus brevipes plays a significant role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat by influencing host tree growth and supporting local wildlife.
