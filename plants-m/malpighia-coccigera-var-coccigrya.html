Malpighia coccigera var. coccigrya

Malpighia coccigera var. coccigrya, commonly known as the crimson beauty or red mallow, is a species of flowering plant in the Malpighiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas, particularly Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its striking red, fleshy fruits that resemble small berries, which are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are typically white or pale pink with five petals.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
