Mesembryanthemum hiemale

Mesembryanthemum hiemale, commonly known as the winter evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties, with its seeds containing essential fatty acids.
