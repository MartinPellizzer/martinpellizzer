Madvigia

Madvigia, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-blue petals and prominent, cone-shaped centers. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to moist soil conditions.

Madvigia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
