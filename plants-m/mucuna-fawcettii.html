Mucuna fawcettii

Mucuna fawcettii, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, hairy pods that contain numerous seeds. The plant produces a significant amount of biomass and is often used in agroforestry systems for soil enrichment. It is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits surrounding crops.

The seeds can be processed into a flour used in local cuisines.
