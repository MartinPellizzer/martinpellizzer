Myrcia neolucida

Myrcia neolucida, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their potential therapeutic properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
