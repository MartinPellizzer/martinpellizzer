Melastoma rubricaule

Melastoma rubricaule, commonly known as the red-hued melastoma, is a flowering plant belonging to the Melastomataceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine, reddish hairs. Native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Melastoma rubricaule is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
