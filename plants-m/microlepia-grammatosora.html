Microlepia grammatosora

Microlepia grammatosora, commonly known as the feather fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble feathers, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine in some regions.

The botanical features of Microlepia grammatosora make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.
