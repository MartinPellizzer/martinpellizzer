Microseris douglasii

Microseris douglasii, commonly known as Douglas's wintercress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from February to April. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and forest edges.

Microseris douglasii is adapted to cold climates and is an important part of the local flora in its native range.
