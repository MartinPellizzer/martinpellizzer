Myrtus latifolia

Myrtus latifolia, commonly known as common myrtle, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are glossy and have a fragrant, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, dark blue berry that contains several seeds.

Myrtus latifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
