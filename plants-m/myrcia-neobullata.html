Myrcia neobullata

Myrcia neobullata, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Myrcia neobullata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
