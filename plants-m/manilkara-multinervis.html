Manilkara multinervis

Manilkara multinervis, commonly known as the Malabar chestnut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves with prominent veins. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, round fruits resembling chestnuts. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

The plant is valued for its timber, which is hard and durable, as well as its ornamental and ecological significance.
