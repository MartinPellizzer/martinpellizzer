Macoubea mesoamericana

Macoubea mesoamericana, commonly known as the Mexican magnolia, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Magnoliaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy white to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are broad and ovate, and it produces woody capsules as fruit.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in some indigenous communities.
