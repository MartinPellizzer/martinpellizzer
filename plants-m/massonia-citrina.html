Massonia citrina

Massonia citrina, commonly known as the yellow massonia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are typically yellow to green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces delicate, star-shaped flowers that range from white to pale yellow, often appearing in spring. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a houseplant.
