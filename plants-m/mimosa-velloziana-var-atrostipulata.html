Mimosa velloziana var. atrostipulata

Mimosa velloziana var. atrostipulata, commonly known as the black-stippled mimosa, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are finely divided and often covered in a dense layer of soft, grayish-white hairs. The plant produces small, yellow, spherical flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its stems are woody and often have a distinctive, dark-colored bark.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
