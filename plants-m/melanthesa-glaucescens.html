Melanthesa glaucescens

Melanthesa glaucescens, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a bluish sheen. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens.

Melanthesa glaucescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
