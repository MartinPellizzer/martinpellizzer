Maranta glumacea

Maranta glumacea, commonly known as the arrowroot plant, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are often variegated with red, pink, or white veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or red, arranged in dense clusters. Its rhizomes are used to produce arrowroot starch, a starchy substance used in cooking and as a thickening agent.

Maranta glumacea thrives in humid, shady environments and is popular as a decorative houseplant.
