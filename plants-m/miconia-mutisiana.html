Miconia mutisiana

Miconia mutisiana, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a species of flowering plant in the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in moist, shaded forest understories. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. Its showy, clustered flowers are typically pink or red, with prominent stamens that attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Miconia mutisiana is also known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it a potential invasive species in certain regions.
