Malachra capitata var. flavida

Malachra capitata var. flavida, commonly known as the yellow-flowered malachra, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow ray florets and compact, spherical inflorescence that resembles a small globe. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
