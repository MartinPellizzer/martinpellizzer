Myrcia guajavifolia f. grandifolia

Myrcia guajavifolia f. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered myrcia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains aromatic seeds.

This species is often valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
