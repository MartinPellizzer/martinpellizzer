Mucuna foveolata

Mucuna foveolata, commonly known as the scarlet balsam, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces pods containing flattened, winged seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Mucuna foveolata is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.
