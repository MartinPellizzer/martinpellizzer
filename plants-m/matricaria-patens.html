Matricaria patens

Matricaria patens, commonly known as the common chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces yellow flowers with white centers, resembling those of true chamomile. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and grasslands. Its flowers are rich in essential oils, which contribute to its use in herbal medicine.

Matricaria patens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
