Matricaria Recutita F. Kochiana

Matricaria recutita f. kochiana is a lesser-known variant of the common chamomile plant. It is characterized by its distinctive white flower heads and finely serrated leaves. This subspecies is often found in rocky or arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. While it shares many traits with its parent species, it exhibits subtle differences in growth habits and floral structure. Its potential medicinal properties are still being explored by botanists and herbalists alike.
