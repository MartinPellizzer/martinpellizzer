Mussaenda whitei

Mussaenda whitei, commonly known as the White Mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy and have a mild scent. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Mussaenda whitei is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
