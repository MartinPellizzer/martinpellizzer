Monvillea krapovickiana

Monvillea krapovickiana, commonly known as the Krapovickiana orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of southern Africa, particularly in South Africa and Namibia. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a rosette at the base of the plant.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a threatened species.
