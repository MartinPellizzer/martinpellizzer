Machaeranthera shastensis var. latifolia

Machaeranthera shastensis var. latifolia, commonly known as the Shasta false aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple composite flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

This variety is distinguished by its broader leaves compared to other varieties of Machaeranthera.
