Mentha × marchica

Mentha × marchica, commonly known as the hybrid mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant that belongs to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a cross between Mentha piperita (peppermint) and Mentha spicata (spearmint), resulting in a plant with a strong, aromatic scent. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a serrated edge and a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is widely cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy, herbal remedies, and flavoring.
