Melocactus townsendianus

Melocactus townsendianus, commonly known as the Townsend's melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and spiny, ribbed structure, which helps it conserve water in its harsh environment. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the rainy season, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise desolate appearance. Its thick, fleshy stem stores water, allowing it to survive prolonged dry periods.

The cactus also has a unique ability to form a symbiotic relationship with certain fungi, enhancing its nutrient absorption in nutrient-poor soils.
