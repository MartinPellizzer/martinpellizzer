Minaria diamantinensis

Minaria diamantinensis, commonly known as the Diamond Mountains grass, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky environments and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.

Its unique morphology and habitat make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
