Matelea pyrrhotricha

Matelea pyrrhotricha, commonly known as the red-stemmed milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, often reddish stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers, which are typically arranged in elongated inflorescences. Matelea pyrrhotricha is a member of the Apocynaceae family, which includes many milkweeds known for their milky sap.
