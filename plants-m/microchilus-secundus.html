Microchilus secundus

Microchilus secundus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
