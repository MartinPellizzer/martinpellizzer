Melilotus rugosus

Melilotus rugosus, commonly known as hairy sweet clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Europe and western Asia and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and agricultural fields. Its trifoliate leaves and pod-like fruits are typical of the legume family.

Melilotus rugosus is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which makes it useful in soil enrichment.
