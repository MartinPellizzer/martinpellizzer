Microstylis resupinata

Microstylis resupinata, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with three-ranked leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, typically found in dense clusters at the base of the plant. It thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Microstylis resupinata plays a vital role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various small organisms.
