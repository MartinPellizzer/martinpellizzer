Mentha × sativa var. melissifolia

Mentha × sativa var. melissifolia, commonly known as honey mint, is a hybrid mint species characterized by its aromatic, slightly sweet scent.

It is a perennial plant that thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight. The leaves are typically broad and oval, with a textured surface that distinguishes it from other mint varieties. This plant is often used in culinary applications for its unique flavor profile and in traditional herbal remedies.

Its vigorous growth habit makes it a popular choice for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
