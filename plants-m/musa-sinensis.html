Musa sinensis

Musa sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese banana, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and clustered flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically consumed raw or cooked. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit yield, it is widely cultivated in tropical regions around the world.
