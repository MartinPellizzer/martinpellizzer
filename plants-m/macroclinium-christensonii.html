Macroclinium christensonii

Macroclinium christensonii, commonly known as the Christenson's macroclinium, is a rare and striking orchid native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of orange and yellow. The plant produces long, slender stems with numerous leaves arranged in a spiral pattern. Its flowers are borne on terminal inflorescences and are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
