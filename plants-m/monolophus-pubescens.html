Monolophus pubescens

Monolophus pubescens, commonly known as the hairy lophophora, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has a short, succulent stem with hairy foliage, which helps it retain moisture in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
