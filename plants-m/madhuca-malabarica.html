Madhuca malabarica

Madhuca malabarica, commonly known as the Malabar butternut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, elliptical leaves that are glossy on the upper surface. The tree produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its fruit is a capsule containing several winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
