Mentha × carinthiaca var. subtomentosa

Mentha × carinthiaca var. subtomentosa, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a hybrid plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30–60 cm and features square stems and opposite leaves. It produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer.

This variety is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves and ornamental value in gardens.
