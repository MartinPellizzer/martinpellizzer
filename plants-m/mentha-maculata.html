Mentha maculata

Mentha maculata, commonly known as the spotted deadnettle, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves with a distinctive purple or reddish spot on the underside, and small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple. The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other mint species, and is known for its aromatic leaves.

Mentha maculata is not typically used for culinary purposes but is sometimes grown as an ornamental plant.
