Memecylon acuminatum var. acuminatum

Memecylon acuminatum var. acuminatum, commonly known as the pointed memecylon, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite, and elliptic leaves with acute or acuminate tips. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its fruits are small, globose drupes that contain a single seed.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and is occasionally used in traditional medicine.
