Melasma nyassense

Melasma nyassense, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a striking plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a sweet fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with woody stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its botanical features include compound leaves and small, clustered fruits that contain seeds.

Melasma nyassense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique flowering habit.
