Myrtus suffruticosa

Myrtus suffruticosa, commonly known as sweet myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters and has aromatic, dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue berries. Myrtus suffruticosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping.

Its essential oils are also used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
