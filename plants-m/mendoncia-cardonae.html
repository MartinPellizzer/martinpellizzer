Mendoncia cardonae

Mendoncia cardonae, commonly known as the Canary Island date palm, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, sweet fruits. It thrives in warm, arid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

Due to its ecological and horticultural significance, it is also considered a vulnerable species in its native habitat.
