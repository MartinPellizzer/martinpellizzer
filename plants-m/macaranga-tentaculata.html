Macaranga tentaculata

Macaranga tentaculata, commonly known as the giant horse chestnut, is a fast-growing tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Due to its rapid growth, it is considered an invasive species in some regions.
