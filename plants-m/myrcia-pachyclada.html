Myrcia pachyclada

Myrcia pachyclada, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Myrcia pachyclada is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual and aromatic qualities.
