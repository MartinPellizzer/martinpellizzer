Meconopsis wilsonii subsp. orientalis

Meconopsis wilsonii subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the Eastern Blue Poppy, is a striking alpine plant native to the Himalayas. It is characterized by its vibrant blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with specific growing conditions.
