Madorella racemosa

Madorella racemosa, commonly known as the clustered lady's tresses, is a flowering plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, racemose inflorescence composed of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

Madorella racemosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
