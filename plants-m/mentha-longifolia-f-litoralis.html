Mentha longifolia f. litoralis

Mentha longifolia f. litoralis, commonly known as sea horehound, is a variant of the longleaf mint.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that thrives in coastal and saline environments. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, blue or purple flowers. It is often found in sandy soils along the shores of the Mediterranean and other coastal regions.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic properties and traditional medicinal uses.
