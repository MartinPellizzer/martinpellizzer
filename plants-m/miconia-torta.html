Miconia torta

Miconia torta, commonly known as the wrinkled miconia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy texture. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences with bright red bracts that are often mistaken for flowers. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with multiple stems arising from the base.

Miconia torta is known for its rapid growth and has been introduced to other tropical areas, sometimes becoming invasive.
