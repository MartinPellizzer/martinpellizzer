Macaranga grandifolia

Macaranga grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaf mallow, is a tropical plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is typically found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests and is known for its rapid growth rate.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
