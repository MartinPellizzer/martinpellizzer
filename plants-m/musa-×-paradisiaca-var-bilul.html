Musa × paradisiaca var. bilul

Musa × paradisiaca var. bilul, commonly known as the banana plant, is a tropical herbaceous plant belonging to the Musaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces a clustered inflorescence with both male and female flowers, leading to the development of the fruit. The fruit, typically a large, curved banana, contains numerous seeds, though many cultivated varieties are seedless.

This variety is valued for its edible fruit and is widely grown in tropical regions for both commercial and subsistence purposes.
