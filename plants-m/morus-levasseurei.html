Morus levasseurei

Morus levasseurei, commonly known as the Madagascar mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, aggregate fruits. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food for various animals and insects.

Additionally, it has been studied for its potential in agroforestry and environmental restoration projects.
