Machaerium sericiflorum

Machaerium sericiflorum, commonly known as silky leucaena, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, silky-haired inflorescences and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are often clustered in panicles. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Machaerium sericiflorum is valued for its forage potential and is sometimes used in agroforestry systems.
