Macroscepis diademata

Macroscepis diademata, commonly known as the diadem orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of South America.

It is a terrestrial herb with a short, creeping rhizome and produces a single, showy flower with a distinctive diadem-like pattern on its lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as cloud forests. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other small insects.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
