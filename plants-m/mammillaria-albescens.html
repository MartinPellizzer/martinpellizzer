Mammillaria albescens

Mammillaria albescens, commonly known as the white-tipped hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a short stem and dense, spiny areoles. The plant produces white to pale yellow flowers during the spring or early summer. Its foliage is covered in white, woolly hairs that give it a distinctive appearance.

It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
