Mogorium lanceolatum

Mogorium lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mogorium, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
