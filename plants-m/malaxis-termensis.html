Malaxis termensis

Malaxis termensis, commonly known as the Termens orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to aid in nutrient absorption.

The flowers are usually small and clustered, with intricate petal structures that attract specific pollinators.
