Massonia uniflora

Massonia uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower massonia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rosette-shaped foliage composed of fleshy, blue-green leaves. The plant produces a single, showy flower at the center of the rosette, typically in shades of yellow or orange. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
