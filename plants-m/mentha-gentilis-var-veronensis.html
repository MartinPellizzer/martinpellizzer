Mentha gentilis var. veronensis

Mentha gentilis var. veronensis, commonly known as sweet everlasting mint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features slender, aromatic stems covered in fine hairs and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive minty fragrance. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, often found in moist meadows and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
