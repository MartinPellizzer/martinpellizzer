Melodorum latifolium

Melodorum latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf melody plant, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Americas.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along its stem. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Melodorum latifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
