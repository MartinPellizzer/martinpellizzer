Mucuna pauciflora

Mucuna pauciflora, commonly known as the lesser cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It is often cultivated for its edible seeds and has been used in traditional medicine.

Mucuna pauciflora is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, improving soil fertility.
