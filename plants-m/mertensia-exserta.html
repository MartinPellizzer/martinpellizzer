Mertensia exserta

Mertensia exserta, commonly known as bluebells, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has heart-shaped, softly hairy leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Mertensia exserta is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.
