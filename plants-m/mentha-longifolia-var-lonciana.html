Mentha longifolia var. lonciana

Mentha longifolia var. lonciana, commonly known as wild mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typical of the Lamiaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that give it a minty fragrance and make it useful in culinary and medicinal applications.
