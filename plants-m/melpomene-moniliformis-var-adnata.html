Melpomene moniliformis var. adnata

Melpomene moniliformis var. adnata, commonly known as the red-flowered mallow, is a striking plant characterized by its vibrant red flowers and distinctive, fluffy seed heads.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is native to certain regions of South America. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the flowers are borne in clusters, adding ornamental value to gardens.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
