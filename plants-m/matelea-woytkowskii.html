Matelea woytkowskii

Matelea woytkowskii, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.
