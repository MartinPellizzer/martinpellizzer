Maytenus revoluta subsp. macrophylla

Maytenus revoluta subsp.

macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf mayten, is a shrub or small tree native to Central America. It is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are usually larger than those of the typical species.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits containing seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential medicinal research.
