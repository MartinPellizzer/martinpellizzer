Microsaccus ramosus

Microsaccus ramosus, commonly known as the hairy saccate orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its stems are wiry and often branched.

The species is notable for its unique floral structure, which includes specialized sac-like petals that aid in pollination by specific insect species.
