Mimosa chiliomera

Mimosa chiliomera, commonly known as the golden mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a soft, dainty appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen, it can also serve as a beneficial companion plant in some agricultural settings.
