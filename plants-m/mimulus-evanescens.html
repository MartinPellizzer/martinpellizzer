Mimulus evanescens

Mimulus evanescens, commonly known as the fading monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions. Mimulus evanescens has heart-shaped leaves and produces seed pods that split open to release its seeds.

It is a member of the Physostegia genus and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
