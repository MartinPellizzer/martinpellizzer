Melanthera letestui

Melanthera letestui, commonly known as the Letestu's bluebell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring, typically from May to June. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Melanthera letestui is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in native plant gardens.
