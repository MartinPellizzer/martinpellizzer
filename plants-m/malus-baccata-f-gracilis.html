Malus baccata f. gracilis

Malus baccata f. gracilis, commonly known as the slender crabapple, is a compact, ornamental tree or shrub native to China.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by small, edible fruits that turn red in the fall. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic value.

The foliage and fruit make it a popular choice for wildlife habitat and ornamental landscaping.
