Minthostachys latifolia

Minthostachys latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf mint or broadleaf minthostachys, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive minty aroma, and it produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of essential oils.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
