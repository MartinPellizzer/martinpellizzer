Mikania manaosensis

Mikania manaosensis, commonly known as the Amazonian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by fluffy, seed-bearing structures. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge.

Mikania manaosensis is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing along forest floors and riverbanks.
