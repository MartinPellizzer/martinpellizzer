Marcgravia rubra

Marcgravia rubra, commonly known as the red-veined strelitzia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with prominent red veins, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Marcgravia rubra is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its dramatic appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
