Mentha theresitae

Mentha theresitae, commonly known as the Sicilian mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in hue. Mentha theresitae thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional remedies.
