Marrubium haussknechtii

Marrubium haussknechtii, commonly known as the Hausseckner's horehound, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the plantain family, Plantaginaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and small blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments.

It is often found in alpine meadows and is known for its medicinal properties, though it is relatively rare and not widely cultivated.
