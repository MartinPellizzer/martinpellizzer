Mollinedia cinerea

Mollinedia cinerea, commonly known as the silverleaf mollinedia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It features a basal rosette of silver-gray, velvety leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its unique foliage and subtle floral display make it a distinctive addition to botanical gardens.
