Miconia fulva var. tinctoria

Miconia fulva var. tinctoria, commonly known as the yellow miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright yellow to orange in color. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage.

However, it can be invasive in some regions, posing a threat to native ecosystems.
