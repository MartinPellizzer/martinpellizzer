Meibomia supina

Meibomia supina, commonly known as the prostrate meibomia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its opposite, elliptic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five petals, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Meibomia supina is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.
