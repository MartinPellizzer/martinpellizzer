Mentha × gundisalvii

Mentha × gundisalvii, commonly known as the hybrid mint, is a cross between Mentha longifolia and Mentha suaveolens.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by square stems and opposite, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates.

Mentha × gundisalvii is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
