Melinis repens subsp. nigricans

Melinis repens subsp.

nigricans, commonly known as blackleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late summer.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides. It has a fibrous root system and can form dense mats, which help prevent soil erosion.

Its adaptability to various soil types and drought conditions makes it a resilient species in its native range.
