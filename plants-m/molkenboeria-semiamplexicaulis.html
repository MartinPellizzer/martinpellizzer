Molkenboeria semiamplexicaulis

Molkenboeria semiamplexicaulis, commonly known as the creeping milkweed, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a waxy texture that helps it retain moisture.

Molkenboeria semiamplexicaulis is adapted to arid environments and is often used in rock gardens or as a decorative plant in dry landscapes.
