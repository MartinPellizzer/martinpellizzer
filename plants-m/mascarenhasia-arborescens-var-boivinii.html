Mascarenhasia arborescens var. boivinii

Mascarenhasia arborescens var. boivinii, commonly known as the Mascarene tree fern, is a variant of the tree fern native to the Mascarene Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant exhibits a slow growth rate and is adapted to the humid, tropical climate of its native habitat. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing habitat for various species.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
