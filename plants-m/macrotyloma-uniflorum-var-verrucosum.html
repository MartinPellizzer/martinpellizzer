Macrotyloma uniflorum var. verrucosum

Macrotyloma uniflorum var. verrucosum, commonly known as the hairy cowpea, is a variant of the cowpea species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which provide a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its nutritional value, as the seeds are rich in protein and can be used in various culinary preparations.

Additionally, the plant is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural systems.
