Mammillaria heyderi subsp. coahuilensis

Mammillaria heyderi subsp.

coahuilensis, commonly known as the Coahuila mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico. It is characterized by its globular shape and dense covering of white or pale blue spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and desert landscapes.
