Magnolia biondii var. multalabastra

Magnolia biondii var. multalabastra, commonly known as the golden magnolia, is a rare and striking variant of the magnolia genus.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a unique splash of color to gardens. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of China, where it thrives in cool, humid climates. Its leaves are broad and dark green, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in specialized gardens and botanical collections.
