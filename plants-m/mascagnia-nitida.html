Mascagnia nitida

Mascagnia nitida, commonly known as the silverleaf mallow, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, fuzzy leaves and showy, pale yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments. Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small wildlife.
