Malcolmia cymbalaria

Malcolmia cymbalaria, commonly known as the common malcolmia, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is an annual herb that typically grows to a height of about 20 to 60 centimeters. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers. It blooms from late spring to early autumn, and its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in meadows.
