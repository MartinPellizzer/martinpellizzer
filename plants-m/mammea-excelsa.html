Mammea excelsa

Mammea excelsa, commonly known as the yellow mangosteen, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and large, pinnate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. The fruit contains a single large seed surrounded by a juicy, aromatic arillate pulp.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and medicinal properties.
