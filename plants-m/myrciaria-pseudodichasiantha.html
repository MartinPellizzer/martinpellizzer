Myrciaria pseudodichasiantha

Myrciaria pseudodichasiantha, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá-preto, is a species of shrub or small tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and showy, red or orange flowers. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in dense undergrowth.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
