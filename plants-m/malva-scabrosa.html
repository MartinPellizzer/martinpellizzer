Malva scabrosa

Malva scabrosa, commonly known as the rough hound's tongue, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. The plant is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, with five petals and a distinctive central column.

Malva scabrosa is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
