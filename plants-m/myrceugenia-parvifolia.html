Myrceugenia parvifolia

Myrceugenia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved myrtle, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the temperate rainforests of southern Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, whitish hairs. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, berry-like fruits.

Myrceugenia parvifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
