Mammillaria bambusiphila var. parva

Mammillaria bambusiphila var. parva, commonly known as the miniature bamboo cactus, is a compact, globular cactus native to Mexico.

It features a short, cylindrical stem covered in white, woolly hairs, and produces small, pale yellow flowers during the summer months. The plant is characterized by its small, rounded tubercles and is well adapted to arid environments. It requires minimal water and full sun to thrive, making it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.

Due to its delicate appearance, it is often grown in containers to protect it from harsh outdoor conditions.
