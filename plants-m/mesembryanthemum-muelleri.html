Mesembryanthemum muelleri

Mesembryanthemum muelleri, commonly known as the Woolly Mesembryanthemum, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and dense, woolly foliage. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is widely cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
