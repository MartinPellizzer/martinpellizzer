Mimetes hibbertii

Mimetes hibbertii, commonly known as the Cape goldbos, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation. It has a compact, bushy habit with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Mimetes hibbertii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and resilience.
