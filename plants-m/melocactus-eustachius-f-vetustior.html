Melocactus eustachius f. vetustior

Melocactus eustachius f. vetustior, commonly known as the old-fashioned melocactus, is a rare and ancient cultivar of the melocactus genus.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a dense covering of spines, which provide protection against herbivores and extreme environmental conditions. The plant produces a single, elongated, yellowish flower that blooms in the early morning, typically during the spring or summer months. It is native to the arid regions of Mexico and thrives in well-drained soil with minimal water.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly sought after by collectors and botanists alike.
