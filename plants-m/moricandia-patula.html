Moricandia patula

Moricandia patula, commonly known as the slender rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb characterized by its slender stems and small, alternate leaves. The plant produces clusters of four-petaled flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in early spring. Moricandia patula is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its hardy nature.
