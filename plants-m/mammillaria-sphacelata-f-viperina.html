Mammillaria sphacelata f. viperina

Mammillaria sphacelata f. viperina, commonly known as the viper's tail cactus, is a compact, spineless cactus native to Mexico.

It features a short, cylindrical stem with a distinctive, reddish-brown coloration and a woolly texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for collectors and succulent enthusiasts.
