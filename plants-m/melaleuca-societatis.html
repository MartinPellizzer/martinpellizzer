Melaleuca societatis

Melaleuca societatis, commonly known as the Society's Melaleuca, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to the coastal regions of New South Wales, Australia, where it thrives in sandy soils and saline environments. The plant is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. It is often used in coastal landscaping due to its tolerance of salt spray and drought conditions.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.
