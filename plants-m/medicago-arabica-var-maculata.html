Medicago arabica var. maculata

Medicago arabica var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted medick, is a low-growing perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive spotted or mottled pattern on the upper surface. The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. It has a fibrous root system and thrives in well-drained soils.

This species is often used in ecological restoration and as a forage crop for livestock.
