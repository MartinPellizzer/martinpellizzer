Myrcia citrifolia var. jacquiniana

Myrcia citrifolia var. jacquiniana, commonly known as the Jacquin's guava, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, yellow or orange fruits that are typically edible and have a tangy flavor. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

The varietal form, jacquiniana, is distinguished by its unique leaf shape and floral characteristics compared to the standard citrifolia.
