Mentha sachalinensis

Mentha sachalinensis, commonly known as Siberian mint, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Mentha sachalinensis is often found in moist habitats such as meadows, riverbanks, and forest edges.

Its essential oils contain menthol and other compounds, making it valuable in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.
