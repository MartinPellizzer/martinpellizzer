Manglietia glaucifolia

Manglietia glaucifolia, commonly known as the white manglietia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It is a member of the Magnoliaceae family, known for its primitive floral structures and aromatic foliage. The plant features large, ovate leaves with a waxy, glaucous underside, giving it its common name. Its flowers are showy, with white petals and a distinctive, elongated stamen that is often used in floral arrangements.

Manglietia glaucifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
