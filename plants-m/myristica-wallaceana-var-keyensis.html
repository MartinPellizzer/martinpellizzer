Myristica wallaceana var. keyensis

Myristica wallaceana var. keyensis, commonly known as the Key Island nutmeg, is a rare and endemic species native to the island of Borneo.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody fruits that contain valuable essential oils. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, with dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant flowers.

Its unique botanical features make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists due to its limited distribution and ecological significance.
