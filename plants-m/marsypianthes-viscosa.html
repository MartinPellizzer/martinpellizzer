Marsypianthes viscosa

Marsypianthes viscosa, commonly known as the sticky Marsypianthes, is a unique plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that produce a sticky substance, which is believed to serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant has small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, and it blooms during the rainy season. Its root system is robust, allowing it to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicately preserved species in its native range.
