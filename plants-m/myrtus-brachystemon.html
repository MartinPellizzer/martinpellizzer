Myrtus brachystemon

Myrtus brachystemon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used in aromatherapy and as a flavoring agent.

Myrtus brachystemon thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.
