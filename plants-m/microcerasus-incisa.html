Microcerasus incisa

Microcerasus incisa, commonly known as the Chinese stone pine, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and turn reddish in autumn, adding ornamental value.

The berries, which are rich in antioxidants, are also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
