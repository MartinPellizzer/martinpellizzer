Monardella elmeri

Monardella elmeri, commonly known as Elmer's monardella, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. This species is characterized by its slender stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of showy pink or purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands.

Monardella elmeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
