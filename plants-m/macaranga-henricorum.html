Macaranga henricorum

Macaranga henricorum, commonly known as the Henry's makara, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often glossy and have a distinct venation pattern. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The species is often found in lowland forests and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types.
