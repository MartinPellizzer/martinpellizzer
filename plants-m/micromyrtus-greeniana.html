Micromyrtus greeniana

Micromyrtus greeniana, commonly known as the Greeniana Myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Australia, specifically found in the coastal regions of New South Wales. The plant is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in well-drained environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
