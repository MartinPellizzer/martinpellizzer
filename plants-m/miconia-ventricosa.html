Miconia ventricosa

Miconia ventricosa, commonly known as the tropical shrub or tree, is a member of the Melastomataceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It is native to the tropical regions of South America and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, it can become invasive in certain areas.
