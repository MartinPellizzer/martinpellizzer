Masdevallia collina var. macrogenia

Masdevallia collina var. macrogenia, commonly known as the Andean orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves and produces intricate, pendulous inflorescences with showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant thrives in high humidity and cool temperatures, typically growing on tree branches or rocks. Its flowers are highly variable in color and form, showcasing a range of hues from white to deep red.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique and elegant appearance.
