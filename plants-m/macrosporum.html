Macrosporum

Macrosporum, commonly known as the large-spore plant, is a genus of flowering plants characterized by its unique reproductive structures.

It produces large, showy flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant's most notable feature is its macrospores, which are significantly larger than typical spores found in other plant species. These spores are typically released in abundance, enhancing the plant's reproductive success.

Macrosporum thrives in moist, temperate environments and is often found in woodland areas.
