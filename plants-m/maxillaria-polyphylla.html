Maxillaria polyphylla

Maxillaria polyphylla, commonly known as the feathered orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulbous structure and produces multiple flowering stems. The plant features lanceolate leaves and is known for its fragrant, often pink or purple flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Maxillaria polyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is a popular choice for greenhouse cultivation.
