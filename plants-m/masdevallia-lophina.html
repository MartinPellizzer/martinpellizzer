Masdevallia lophina

Masdevallia lophina, commonly known as the Lophina masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the high-altitude cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, often colorful flower structure. The plant typically produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapted to the cool, humid conditions of its natural habitat.

Masdevallia lophina is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
