Masdevallia tubeana

Masdevallia tubeana, commonly known as the tube orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular-shaped flowers that often display vibrant colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually elongated and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Masdevallia tubeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral structure.
