Murraya paniculata

Murraya paniculata, commonly known as the Java coffee tree or flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, orange-yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn red when mature, containing a single seed. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Murraya paniculata is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
