Marrubium alysson var. plicatum

Marrubium alysson var. plicatum, commonly known as the wrinkled horehound, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features hairy, gray-green leaves that are often wrinkled or pinnately lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Marrubium alysson var. plicatum has been historically used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.
