Melicytus lanceolatus

Melicytus lanceolatus, commonly known as the lance-leaf pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for native birds. It thrives in coastal and lowland areas, often found in forest margins and rocky soils.

Melicytus lanceolatus is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance in New Zealand's ecosystems.
