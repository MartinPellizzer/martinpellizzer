Mesembryanthemum meleagris

Mesembryanthemum meleagris, commonly known as the diamond flower, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in the morning and closing at dusk. It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, known for its unique adaptations to dry climates.

Mesembryanthemum meleagris is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
