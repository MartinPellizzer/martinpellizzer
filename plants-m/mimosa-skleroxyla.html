Mimosa skleroxyla

Mimosa skleroxyla, commonly known as the African mimosa, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features bipinnate leaves that close when touched, a response triggered by sensitive hair cells. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

Mimosa skleroxyla is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of forage for livestock.
