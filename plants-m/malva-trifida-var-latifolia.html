Malva trifida var. latifolia

Malva trifida var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf mallows, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, broad leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture. The plant produces showy, pink or purple flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and valuable plant for ecological and ornamental purposes.
