Malveopsis humilis

Malveopsis humilis, commonly known as the dwarf mallows, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant features hairy, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, pink or white flowers clustered in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Malveopsis humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
