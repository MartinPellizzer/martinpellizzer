Mimulus calciphilus

Mimulus calciphilus, commonly known as the limestone monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. This species thrives in calcareous soils, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

It is a member of the figwort family (Scrophulariaceae) and plays an important role in supporting local pollinators.
