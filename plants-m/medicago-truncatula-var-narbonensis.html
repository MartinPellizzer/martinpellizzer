Medicago truncatula var. narbonensis

Medicago truncatula var. narbonensis, commonly known as the Narbonensis medick, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to dry and rocky soils, making it a hardy species often found in arid environments. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.

This variety is also valued for its use in traditional herbal medicine and as a forage crop.
