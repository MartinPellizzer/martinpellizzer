Miconia macrostachya

Miconia macrostachya, commonly known as the giant miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that are followed by large, woody fruits. Miconia macrostachya is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a challenge for ecosystem management.
