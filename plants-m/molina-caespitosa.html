Molina caespitosa

Molina caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted molina, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and compact growth habit, forming low-growing tufts. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Molina caespitosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
