Medinilla papulosa

Medinilla papulosa, commonly known as the papular medinilla, is a tropical shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its unique floral structure and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Medinilla papulosa thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
