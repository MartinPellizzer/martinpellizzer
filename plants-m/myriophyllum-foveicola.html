Myriophyllum foveicola

Myriophyllum foveicola, commonly known as the narrow-leaved watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems in North America.

It has slender, branching stems that are often reddish in color and produce small, white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant's leaves are narrow and strap-like, typically arranged in whorls around the stem. It plays an important role in aquatic habitats by providing shelter for fish and invertebrates.

However, it can become invasive in some regions if not managed properly.
