Mentha × muelleriana subsp. micrantha

Mentha × muelleriana subsp.

micrantha, commonly known as narrow-leaved mint, is a hybrid mint species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes. Its essential oils contain menthol and other volatile compounds, making it valuable for both culinary and medicinal uses.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its fragrance and as a source of medicinal compounds.
