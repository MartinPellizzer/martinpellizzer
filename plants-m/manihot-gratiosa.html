Manihot gratiosa

Manihot gratiosa, commonly known as the beautiful arrowleaf, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by yellow-orange fruits that contain edible seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
