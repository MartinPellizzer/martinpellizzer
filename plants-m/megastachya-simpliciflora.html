Megastachya simpliciflora

Megastachya simpliciflora, commonly known as the simple-flowered spikegrass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces pale green to brownish flowers that are typically arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture.

Megastachya simpliciflora is often found in moist, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges.
