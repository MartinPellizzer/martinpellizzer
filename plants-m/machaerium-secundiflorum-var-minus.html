Machaerium secundiflorum var. minus

Machaerium secundiflorum var. minus, commonly known as the lesser sickle senna, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and trifoliate leaves with small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers with two prominent, sickle-shaped petals, typical of the Fabaceae family. Its small, elliptical pods contain several seeds, and it is often found in open woodlands and disturbed soils.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated for its distinctive floral display.
