Myosotis alpestris subsp. pyrenaeorum

Myosotis alpestris subsp.

pyrenaeorum, commonly known as the Pyrenean forget-me-not, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, often on rocky soils or in meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
