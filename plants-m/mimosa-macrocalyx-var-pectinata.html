Mimosa macrocalyx var. pectinata

Mimosa macrocalyx var. pectinata, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces fluffy, spherical seed pods that split open when mature, releasing seeds with silky filaments. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for pollinators.
