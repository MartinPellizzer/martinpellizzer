Myrtus magnoliifolia

Myrtus magnoliifolia, commonly known as the magnolia-myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which consists of narrow, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, resembling those of magnolia species. Its small, edible berries are often used in culinary applications, particularly in Mediterranean cuisine.

Myrtus magnoliifolia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
