Magnolia ashei

Magnolia ashei, commonly known as the Florida magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The tree has simple, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall, and its bark is smooth and light gray. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
