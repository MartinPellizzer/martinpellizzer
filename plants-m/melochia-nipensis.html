Melochia nipensis

Melochia nipensis, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Brazilian spinach, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous small, round seeds. It is native to Central and South America but has been introduced to other tropical regions.

Melochia nipensis is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
