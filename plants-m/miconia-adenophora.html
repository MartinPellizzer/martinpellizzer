Miconia adenophora

Miconia adenophora, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by distinctive, winged fruits. Miconia adenophora is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a common weed in disturbed habitats.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive in certain regions.
