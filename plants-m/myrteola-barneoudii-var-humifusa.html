Myrteola barneoudii var. humifusa

Myrteola barneoudii var. humifusa, commonly known as the low myrteola, is a shrub native to the humid forests of Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, woody fruits.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
