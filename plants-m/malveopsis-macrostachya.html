Malveopsis macrostachya

Malveopsis macrostachya, commonly known as the large-stemmed mallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its showy, pentagonal flowers that range in color from pink to purple. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.
