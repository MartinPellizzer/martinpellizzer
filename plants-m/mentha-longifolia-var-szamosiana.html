Mentha longifolia var. szamosiana

Mentha longifolia var. szamosiana, commonly known as Hungarian mint, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

Its essential oils contain menthol and other volatile compounds, making it valuable in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.
