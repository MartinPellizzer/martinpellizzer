Mariscus pycnostachyus

Mariscus pycnostachyus, commonly known as the dense-stamen mariscus, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens.
