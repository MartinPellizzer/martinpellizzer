Momordica jagorana

Momordica jagorana, commonly known as the Indian bitter gourd or karela, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a climbing vine that produces elongated, warty fruits with a bitter taste. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, Thailand, and Indonesia. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with five petals.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and cooking, despite its strong, bitter flavor.
