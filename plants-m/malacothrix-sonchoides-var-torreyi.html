Malacothrix sonchoides var. torreyi

Malacothrix sonchoides var. torreyi, commonly known as the Torrey's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, yellow flowers clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments.

The species is notable for its ability to attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
