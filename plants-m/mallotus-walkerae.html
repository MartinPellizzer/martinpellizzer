Mallotus walkerae

Mallotus walkerae, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are typically bright green with a reddish or golden hue on the underside. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to grow in a variety of soil types.
