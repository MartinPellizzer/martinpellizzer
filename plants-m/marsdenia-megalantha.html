Marsdenia megalantha

Marsdenia megalantha, commonly known as the giant Marsdenia, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its long, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that are typically followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forested areas with high humidity.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its attractive foliage and floral display.
