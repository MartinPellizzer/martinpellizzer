Morinda glomerata

Morinda glomerata, commonly known as the red moringa or Indian madder, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, with simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by reddish-brown, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. It is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

The roots and leaves are often harvested for their bioactive compounds.
