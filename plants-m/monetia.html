Monetia

Monetia, commonly known as the "money plant," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Araceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Monetia species are often cultivated as ornamental plants due to their attractive foliage and ease of care.

They thrive in warm, humid environments and are typically propagated through division or stem cuttings.
