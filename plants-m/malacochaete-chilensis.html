Malacochaete chilensis

Malacochaete chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean marsh orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, orchid-like flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are long and narrow, and it produces small, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
