Millettia extensa

Millettia extensa, commonly known as the African wisteria, is a deciduous climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and fragrant, pendulous flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

Millettia extensa is also valued for its potential in ecological restoration due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
