Mentha gentilis var. arrhenii

Mentha gentilis var. arrhenii, commonly known as Hungarian mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves are used in traditional herbal remedies and as a flavoring agent.
