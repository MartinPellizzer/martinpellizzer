Malus prunifolia var. longifolia

Malus prunifolia var. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters, featuring a compact, rounded form with dense foliage. The leaves are ovate to elliptical, with serrated edges and a glossy green surface that turns yellow in autumn. Its flowers are white or pink, appearing in clusters, and the fruit is a small, yellowish apple that ripens in the fall.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
