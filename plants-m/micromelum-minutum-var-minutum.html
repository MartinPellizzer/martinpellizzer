Micromelum minutum var. minutum

Micromelum minutum var. minutum, commonly known as the small-leaved hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
