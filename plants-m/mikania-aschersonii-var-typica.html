Mikania aschersonii var. typica

Mikania aschersonii var. typica, commonly known as climbing hempweed, is a vigorous vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rapid growth rate and ability to climb using its tendrils, often forming dense mats that can smother native vegetation. The plant has heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves and produces small, yellow flowers followed by fluffy, white seed heads. It is considered an invasive species in many regions due to its aggressive spread and ecological impact.

Its adaptability to various soil types and light conditions contributes to its success as an invasive plant.
