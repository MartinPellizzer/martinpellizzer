Mentha × rotundifolia var. mabillei

Mentha × rotundifolia var. mabillei, commonly known as Mabille's round-leaved mint, is a hybrid species within the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its rounded, slightly hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale purple or blue, arranged in dense spikes. It is native to Europe and is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

This variety is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.
