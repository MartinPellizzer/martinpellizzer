Melissa clinopodium

Melissa clinopodium, commonly known as hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant has square stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its small, pale blue or purple flowers are arranged in whorls along the stems.

Melissa clinopodium is often found in moist, shaded areas and has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties.
