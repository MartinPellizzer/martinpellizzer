Mahonia aquifolium var. nutkana

Mahonia aquifolium var. nutkana, commonly known as the Pacific silver fir or Oregon grape, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features compound leaves with spiny margins and a deep green color, resembling the leaves of holly. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by blue-black berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
