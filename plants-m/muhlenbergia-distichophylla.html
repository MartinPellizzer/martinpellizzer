Muhlenbergia distichophylla

Muhlenbergia distichophylla, commonly known as the narrowleaf muhlenbergia, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping and native gardens.

Muhlenbergia distichophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting local wildlife and providing habitat for pollinators.
