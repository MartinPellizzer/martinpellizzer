Monocarpia maingayi

Monocarpia maingayi, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, simple leaves and showy flowers with five petals. The plant produces a milky sap that is toxic and can cause skin irritation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry woodland and savanna habitats.

Monocarpia maingayi is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
