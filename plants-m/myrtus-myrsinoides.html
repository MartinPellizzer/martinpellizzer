Myrtus myrsinoides

Myrtus myrsinoides, commonly known as the common myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement and have a slightly aromatic scent. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal remedies.

Myrtus myrsinoides thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
