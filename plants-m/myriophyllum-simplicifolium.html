Myriophyllum simplicifolium

Myriophyllum simplicifolium, commonly known as watermilfoil, is an aquatic plant native to North America.

It has slender, branching stems and small, feathery leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces tiny, green flowers that are typically clustered in dense, spike-like structures. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.

Watermilfoil is often found in shallow waters where sunlight can penetrate to support its photosynthetic processes.
