Masdevallia ophioglossa

Masdevallia ophioglossa, commonly known as the ghost fern orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the high-altitude cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, fern-like foliage and delicate, pale greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments with indirect light, making it a challenging but rewarding species for orchid enthusiasts. Its intricate floral structure and subtle coloration make it a standout in botanical collections.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and requires careful cultivation to preserve its genetic diversity.
