Melica mutica var. glabra

Melica mutica var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth meadowgrass, is a variant of the meadowgrass species native to temperate regions.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its flowers are small and arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in panicles.

This variety is notable for its smooth, hairless surface, distinguishing it from other variants of Melica mutica.
