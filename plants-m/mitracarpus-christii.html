Mitracarpus christii

Mitracarpus christii, commonly known as the Hawaiian rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers and its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils. It typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to the unique climate of Hawaii.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
