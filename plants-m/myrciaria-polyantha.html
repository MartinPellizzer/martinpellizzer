Myrciaria polyantha

Myrciaria polyantha, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red, edible berries that are rich in vitamin C and used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Myrciaria polyantha is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
