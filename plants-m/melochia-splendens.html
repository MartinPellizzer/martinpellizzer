Melochia splendens

Melochia splendens, commonly known as the red spider flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It features striking, velvety red bracts that surround its small, yellow flowers, creating a visually appealing display. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive foliage pattern. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Melochia splendens is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
