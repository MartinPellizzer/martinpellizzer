Myosotis anomala

Myosotis anomala, commonly known as the alpine forget-me-not, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often sprawling.

Myosotis anomala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
