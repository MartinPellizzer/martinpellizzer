Malaxis mindorensis

Malaxis mindorensis, commonly known as the Mindanao Malaxis, is a rare orchid species native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in mountainous regions with high rainfall. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from decaying organic matter in the forest floor.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
