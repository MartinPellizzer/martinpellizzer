Mitrasacme serpyllifolia

Mitrasacme serpyllifolia, commonly known as the common mitrasacme, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides. The plant features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a dense rosette near the ground.

Mitrasacme serpyllifolia is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
