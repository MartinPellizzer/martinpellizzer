Mammillaria silvatica

Mammillaria silvatica, commonly known as the forest mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, globular shape and is covered in white, woolly spines. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or white, appearing in early summer.

Due to its delicate structure, it requires careful handling and is often cultivated in controlled environments.
