Maxillaria chrysoptera

Maxillaria chrysoptera, commonly known as the golden-winged orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, golden-yellow petals and a distinctive, elongated lip that resembles a wing. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant's botanical features make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts and horticulturists.
