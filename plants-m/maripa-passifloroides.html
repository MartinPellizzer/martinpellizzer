Maripa passifloroides

Maripa passifloroides, commonly known as the pink passion vine, is a climbing shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed by wildlife and humans. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and often have a reddish tinge.

Maripa passifloroides thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
