Marshallia schreberi

Marshallia schreberi, commonly known as the pink marsh marigold, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy coating.

Marshallia schreberi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
