Myrtus spectabilis

Myrtus spectabilis, commonly known as the common myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dark green, glossy leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and berries, which are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a hardy and ornamental plant.

Myrtus spectabilis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
