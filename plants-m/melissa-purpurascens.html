Melissa purpurascens

Melissa purpurascens, commonly known as purple lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are fragrant and have a lemony scent, making it a popular choice for herbal teas and aromatherapy.

Melissa purpurascens thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, often found in rocky or dry habitats.
