Melastoma subalbidum

Melastoma subalbidum, commonly known as the white-flowered melastoma, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in cymes, often with a distinctive red calyx. Its fruits are capsules that release tiny seeds when mature.

Melastoma subalbidum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and gardens.
