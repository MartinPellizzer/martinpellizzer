Mapania costata

Mapania costata, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Mapania costata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
