Miconia abeggii

Miconia abeggii, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, yellow berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions as a decorative plant.
