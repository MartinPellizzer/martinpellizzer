Medicago tenoreana

Medicago tenoreana, commonly known as the Sicilian clover, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is typically found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soils and withstand drought.

Medicago tenoreana is also valued for its role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.
