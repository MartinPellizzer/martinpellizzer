Myrcia schaueriana

Myrcia schaueriana, commonly known as the Brazilian beauty or Schauer's myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, reddish-brown berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Myrcia schaueriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
