Medicago tuberculata var. warburgiana

Medicago tuberculata var. warburgiana, commonly known as the Warburg's horseshoe vetch, is a perennial legume native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive horseshoe-shaped flowers, which are typically pink to purple in color. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

This species is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
