Miconia chrysophylla

Miconia chrysophylla, commonly known as the golden-leaf miconia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green with a waxy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when mature.

Miconia chrysophylla is known for its rapid growth and is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage.
