Medicago caerulea var. hemicoerulea

Medicago caerulea var. hemicoerulea, commonly known as the blue medic or blue clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields, along roadsides, and in disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
