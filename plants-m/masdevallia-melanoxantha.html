Masdevallia melanoxantha

Masdevallia melanoxantha, commonly known as the black-veined masdevallia, is a striking orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its dark, velvety petals and sepals, which often feature intricate veining patterns. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually fragrant and bloom during the cooler months, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its unique appearance and specific environmental requirements, it is a prized species among orchid enthusiasts.
