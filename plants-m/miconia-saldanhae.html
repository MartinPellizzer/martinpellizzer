Miconia saldanhae

Miconia saldanhae, commonly known as the Saldanha's miconia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and showy, white to pinkish inflorescences. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically dispersed by birds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed habitats.

Miconia saldanhae is valued for its ornamental potential and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
