Mussaenda cambodiana var. lanceolata

Mussaenda cambodiana var. lanceolata, commonly known as the Cambodian flame bush, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically bright yellow or orange. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
