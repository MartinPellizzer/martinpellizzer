Myrceugenia mesomischa

Myrceugenia mesomischa, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and clusters of small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soils. Its fruits are rich in antioxidants and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for various wildlife species.
