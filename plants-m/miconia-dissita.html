Miconia dissita

Miconia dissita, commonly known as the white miconia, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and showy, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often dispersed by birds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
