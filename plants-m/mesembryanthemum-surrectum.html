Mesembryanthemum surrectum

Mesembryanthemum surrectum, commonly known as the "ice plant," is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in the early morning. It is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and drought tolerance.

Mesembryanthemum surrectum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to prevent soil erosion.
