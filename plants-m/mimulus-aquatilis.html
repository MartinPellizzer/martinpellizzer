Mimulus aquatilis

Mimulus aquatilis, commonly known as the water monkeyflower, is a semi-aquatic plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in wet meadows, stream banks, and other moist habitats. The plant features bright orange to red tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Mimulus aquatilis is known for its adaptability to waterlogged soils and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
