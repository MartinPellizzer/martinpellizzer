Mentha × rotundifolia subsp. oblongifolia

Mentha × rotundifolia subsp.

oblongifolia, commonly known as round-leaved mint, is a hybrid species within the mint family Lamiaceae. It is characterized by its rounded or nearly circular leaves, which are typically larger than those of its parent species.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

This subspecies is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine due to its aromatic properties.
