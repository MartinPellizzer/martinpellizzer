Mespilus monogyna f. stricta

Mespilus monogyna f. stricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved medlar, is a botanical variant of the medlar tree.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits that ripen in late autumn. It is native to southern Europe and thrives in temperate climates.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
