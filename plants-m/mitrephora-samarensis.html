Mitrephora samarensis

Mitrephora samarensis, commonly known as the Samarensis Mitrephora, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes cashew and mango. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, orange-yellow fruits.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential economic uses in traditional medicine.
