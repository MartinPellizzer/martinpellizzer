Microcos adolfi-friderici

Microcos adolfi-friderici, commonly known as the Adolf-Friedrich's microcos, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves and yellow-orange flower heads that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
