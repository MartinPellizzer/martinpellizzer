Mespilus coccinea populifolia

Mespilus coccinea populifolia, commonly known as the red persimmon tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often palmately lobed, giving it a distinctive foliage appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits that are edible when fully mature. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant autumn foliage.
