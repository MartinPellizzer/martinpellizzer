Mimulus spissus var. lincolnensis

Mimulus spissus var. lincolnensis, commonly known as Lincoln's monkeyflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features showy, orange-yellow flowers with a distinctive, spurred petal that aids in pollination by bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native pollinators.
