Microchaete teretifolia

Microchaete teretifolia, commonly known as the slender-leaved microchaete, is a flowering plant native to the Cape Floristic Region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale pink. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ecological importance to local pollinators.
