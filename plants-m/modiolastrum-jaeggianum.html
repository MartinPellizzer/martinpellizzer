Modiolastrum jaeggianum

Modiolastrum jaeggianum, commonly known as the Jaeggian's modiolastrum, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its leaves are often narrow and covered in a fine layer of hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
