Myrioneuron pendulum

Myrioneuron pendulum, commonly known as the weeping fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that give it a distinctive, cascading appearance. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fleshy, orange-red berries.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated as a houseplant and in landscaped areas.
