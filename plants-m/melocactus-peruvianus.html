Melocactus peruvianus

Melocactus peruvianus, commonly known as the Peruvian apple cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Andes mountains of Peru.

It is characterized by its short, columnar stem and clusters of bright red, apple-like fruits that are edible. The plant has a dense covering of spines and is adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange and bloom during the rainy season.

Melocactus peruvianus is valued both for its ornamental appearance and for its historical use in traditional medicine.
