Melanthera microphylla

Melanthera microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, wooded areas. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are small, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is characterized by its delicate appearance and its role in early season pollination.
