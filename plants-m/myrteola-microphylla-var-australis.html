Myrteola microphylla var. australis

Myrteola microphylla var. australis, commonly known as the Australian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which appear in clusters during the spring. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, contributing to its use in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic compounds.

The species is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.
