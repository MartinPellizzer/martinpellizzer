Mimosa pteridifolia var. affinis

Mimosa pteridifolia var. affinis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mimosa, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It features small, fern-like leaves that are typically arranged in pairs along the stems. The plant produces fluffy, yellow flower clusters that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for xeriscaping.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
