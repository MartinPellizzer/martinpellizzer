Mentha lloverana

Mentha lloverana, commonly known as the Spanish mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Mentha lloverana is often found in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in sunny to partially shaded environments.

It is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
