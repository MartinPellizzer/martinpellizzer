Melica violacea var. pallida

Melica violacea var. pallida, commonly known as the pale violet grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale violet to white flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

It is often found in regions of Europe and parts of Asia, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
