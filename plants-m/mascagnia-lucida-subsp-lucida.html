Mascagnia lucida subsp. lucida

Mascagnia lucida subsp.

lucida, commonly known as the shining mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees. It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that contains sticky seeds, which adhere to the bark of host trees for dispersal.

The species is characterized by its ability to photosynthesize, though it also derives nutrients from its host through specialized roots.
