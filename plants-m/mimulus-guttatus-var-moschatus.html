Mimulus guttatus var. moschatus

Mimulus guttatus var. moschatus, commonly known as the musk monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, orange, or red tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and in wet meadows. It has hairy stems and oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

This variant is notable for its strong, musky fragrance, which is more pronounced in the evening.
