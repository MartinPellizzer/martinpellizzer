Mesembryanthemum insititium

Mesembryanthemum insititium, commonly known as the Cape marigold, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers with a distinctive, often sticky texture. It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, known for its adaptations to hot, dry climates.

Mesembryanthemum insititium is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
