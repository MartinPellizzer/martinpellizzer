Mespilus germanica f. obconica

Mespilus germanica f. obconica, commonly known as the European whitebeam, is a cultivar of the whitebeam tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its small, white to yellowish fruits that ripen in late summer. The leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and they turn reddish in autumn. This variety is often grown for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also valued for its role in supporting local wildlife, particularly birds and insects.
