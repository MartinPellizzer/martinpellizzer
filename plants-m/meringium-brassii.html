Meringium brassii

Meringium brassii, commonly known as the golden meringia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has a succulent, branching stem that stores water, enabling it to survive in drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and fleshy, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Meringium brassii is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
