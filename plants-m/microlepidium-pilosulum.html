Microlepidium pilosulum

Microlepidium pilosulum, commonly known as the hairy bittercress, is a small annual flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a short, cylindrical silique containing several seeds.

Microlepidium pilosulum is typically found in moist, disturbed soils and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
