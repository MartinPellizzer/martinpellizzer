Mariscus radula

Mariscus radula, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid environment. It thrives in coastal areas and well-drained soils, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

Mariscus radula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
