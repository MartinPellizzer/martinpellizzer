Minuartia laricifolia subsp. diomedis

Minuartia laricifolia subsp.

diomedis, commonly known as the white sand catchfly, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments. It is often found in tundra, rocky slopes, and open areas where it can receive ample sunlight.

This subspecies is notable for its resilience and ability to survive in extreme climatic conditions.
