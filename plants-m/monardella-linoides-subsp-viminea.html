Monardella linoides subsp. viminea

Monardella linoides subsp.

viminea, commonly known as violetleaf monardella, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and clusters of small, violet or purple flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a distinctive violet hue, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

This subspecies is often found in California and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
