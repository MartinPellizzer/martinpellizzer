Monstera borsigiana

Monstera borsigiana, commonly known as the giant split-leaf philodendron, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that develop distinctive holes and splits as they mature. The plant belongs to the Araceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb using aerial roots. Its foliage is often used in interior design due to its striking appearance.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and humidity for optimal growth.
