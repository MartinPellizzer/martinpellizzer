Malva hornschuchiana

Malva hornschuchiana, commonly known as the horned mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horn-like protrusions on the leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, pale pink to lilac-colored flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Native to southern Europe, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
