Mikania asparagoides

Mikania asparagoides, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a fast-growing vine native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is known for its aggressive spreading habit, often forming dense mats that can smother other vegetation. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive nature, it is considered a problematic species in many regions.
