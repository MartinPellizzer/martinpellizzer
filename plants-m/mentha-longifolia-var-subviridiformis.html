Mentha longifolia var. subviridiformis

Mentha longifolia var. subviridiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mint, is a variant of the longleaf mint species.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically a vibrant green in color. This plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils. It produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The leaves are often used in culinary and aromatic applications due to their strong, minty fragrance.
