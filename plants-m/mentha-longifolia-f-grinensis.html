Mentha longifolia f. grinensis

Mentha longifolia f. grinensis, commonly known as Grin's mint, is a botanical variant of the common mint plant.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and is native to specific regions in Europe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky areas. Its essential oils contain menthol and other volatile compounds, contributing to its distinctive fragrance.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value and mild medicinal properties.
