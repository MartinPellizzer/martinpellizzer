Monnina canescens

Monnina canescens, commonly known as sand mat, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf structures and often edible seeds. The plant typically features prostrate stems covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Monnina canescens is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils.
