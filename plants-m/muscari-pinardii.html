Muscari pinardii

Muscari pinardii, commonly known as the Persian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the eastern Mediterranean and parts of Central Asia.

It produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has strap-like, grassy foliage that emerges from a bulbous base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Muscari pinardii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance.
