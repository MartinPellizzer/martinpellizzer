Myristica vordermannii

Myristica vordermannii, commonly known as the Madagascar nutmeg tree, is a member of the Myristicaceae family and is native to Madagascar.

It is a small evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that contain a single seed, which is encased in a hard, aromatic shell. The tree is valued for its aromatic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to nutmeg.

Its unique botanical features make it an important species in the biodiversity of Madagascar.
