Melochia melissifolia

Melochia melissifolia, commonly known as the honeyleaf or sweetleaf plant, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, hairy leaves that emit a sweet, honey-like fragrance when crushed. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into fleshy, red or orange berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and unique scent.
