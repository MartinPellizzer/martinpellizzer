Mentha × nemophila

Mentha × nemophila, commonly known as the forest mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks, thriving in partial to full shade. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies for their cooling and soothing properties.

Mentha × nemophila is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
