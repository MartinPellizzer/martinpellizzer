Mimosa xanthocentra var. tenuis

Mimosa xanthocentra var. tenuis, commonly known as the slender mimosa, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features delicate, fern-like leaves that fold shut when touched, a trait known as thigmonastic response. It produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
