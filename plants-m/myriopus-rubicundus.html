Myriopus rubicundus

Myriopus rubicundus, commonly known as the red-stemmed spinifex, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, tussock-like growth habit. The plant features wiry, reddish stems and small, spike-like clusters of flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy soils.

Myriopus rubicundus plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native fauna.
