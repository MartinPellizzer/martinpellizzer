Mangifera pentandra

Mangifera pentandra, commonly known as the Indian mango, is a tropical fruit tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves that are alternate and trifoliate, with three leaflets. The plant produces fragrant, yellow-orange fruits with a fibrous pulp surrounding a single seed. Its flowers are small, green, and clustered in panicles, and it is typically cultivated for its edible fruit.

The tree thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
