Mariscus complanatus

Mariscus complanatus, commonly known as the flatleaf mariscus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. Its small, yellowish flowers appear in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Mariscus complanatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and low maintenance requirements.
