Matthiola superba

Matthiola superba, commonly known as the grandiflora stock, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, white or pink flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, and it typically grows in well-drained soils.

Matthiola superba is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
