Maba oxycarpa

Maba oxycarpa, commonly known as the sharp-fruit maba, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange-red, fleshy berries that are edible. Its fruits are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and carving.
