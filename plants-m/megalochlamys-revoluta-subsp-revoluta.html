Megalochlamys revoluta subsp. revoluta

Megalochlamys revoluta subsp.

revoluta, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its showy, yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a striking appearance.

The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often glandular-dotted, and its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
