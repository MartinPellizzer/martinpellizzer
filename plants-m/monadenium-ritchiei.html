Monadenium ritchiei

Monadenium ritchiei, commonly known as the desert star, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It features a compact, branching structure with fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the wet season, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures, minimizing water loss through transpiration.

Monadenium ritchiei is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
