Medinilla disparifolia

Medinilla disparifolia, commonly known as the Christmas Medinilla, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy green foliage and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom during the holiday season. The plant produces small, round, fleshy fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Medinilla disparifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and seasonal blooms.
