Morus longistylus

Morus longistylus, commonly known as the long-styled mulberry, is a species of mulberry native to China.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, aggregate fruits that mature into dark purple berries. The plant belongs to the Moraceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, a feature common in many mulberry species.

The tree is often cultivated for its edible fruits and has historical significance in traditional Chinese medicine.
