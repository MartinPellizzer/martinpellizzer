Mucuna myriaptera

Mucuna myriaptera, commonly known as the many-awned velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, hairy pods that contain elongated, curved seeds. The plant produces a large number of small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along forest edges.

Mucuna myriaptera is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a green manure crop.
