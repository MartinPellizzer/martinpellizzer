Myrodia turbinata

Myrodia turbinata, commonly known as the Turbinated Myrodia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes eucalyptus and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences.

Myrodia turbinata is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with a deep root system and reduced leaf surface to minimize water loss.
