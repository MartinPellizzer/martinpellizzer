Manilkara calcicola

Manilkara calcicola, commonly known as the limestone wax apple, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits with a thick, waxy skin that contains a sweet, creamy pulp. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in secondary forests and on rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
