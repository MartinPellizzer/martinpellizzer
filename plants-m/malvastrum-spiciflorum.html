Malvastrum spiciflorum

Malvastrum spiciflorum, commonly known as the showy malva, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its tall, branched stems and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a soft, fuzzy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping in arid regions.
