Marsdenia undulata

Marsdenia undulata, commonly known as the wavy Marsdenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Marsdenia undulata thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
