Mentha wierzbickiana f. holubyana

Mentha wierzbickiana f. holubyana, commonly known as the Holubyan mint, is a rare and highly aromatic perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies due to their strong menthol-like scent and cooling properties.

This subspecies is valued for its unique fragrance and is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
