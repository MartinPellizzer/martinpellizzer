Maxillaria guadalupensis

Maxillaria guadalupensis, commonly known as the Guadalupan orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces dark green, strap-like leaves and has a single or few flowering stems. Its flowers are usually pink or purple with a yellowish lip, and they bloom during the spring and early summer.

Maxillaria guadalupensis is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
