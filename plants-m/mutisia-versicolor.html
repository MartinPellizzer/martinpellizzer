Mutisia versicolor

Mutisia versicolor, commonly known as the variable mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, colorful inflorescences that range from yellow to purple, depending on the variety. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, giving them a soft appearance.

Mutisia versicolor is often found in open woodland areas and thrives in well-drained soils.
