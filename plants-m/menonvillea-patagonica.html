Menonvillea patagonica

Menonvillea patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian orchid, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, adapting to the harsh climate of Patagonia. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

Menonvillea patagonica is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in the local biodiversity of its habitat.
