Maytenus alaternoides var. angustifolia

Maytenus alaternoides var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mayten, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It features opposite, narrow, lanceolate leaves that are often slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
