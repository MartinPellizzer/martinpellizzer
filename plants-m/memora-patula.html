Memora patula

Memora patula, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Memora patula is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
