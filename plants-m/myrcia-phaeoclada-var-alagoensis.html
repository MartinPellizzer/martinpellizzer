Myrcia phaeoclada var. alagoensis

Myrcia phaeoclada var. alagoensis, commonly known as the Alagoas myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically elliptical and glossy, and its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas within its native habitat.

Myrcia phaeoclada var. alagoensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
