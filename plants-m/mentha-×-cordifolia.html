Mentha × cordifolia

Mentha × cordifolia, commonly known as heartleaf mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically a deep green with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems, usually in late summer. Mentha × cordifolia is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in herbal remedies and as a ornamental plant.
