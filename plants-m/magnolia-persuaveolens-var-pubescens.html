Magnolia persuaveolens var. pubescens

Magnolia persuaveolens var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are broad, ovate, and covered with a fine, hairy texture, giving the plant its varietal name. The bark is smooth and grey, becoming fissured with age.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its historical significance in horticulture.
