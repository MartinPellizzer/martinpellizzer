Miconia longidentata

Miconia longidentata, commonly known as the long-toothed miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking, large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and can grow up to several meters in height.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
