Moluccella spinosa

Moluccella spinosa, commonly known as the spiked bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spiky seed pods that contain the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and unique floral structure.
