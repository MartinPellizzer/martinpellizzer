Misodendrum diemii

Misodendrum diemii, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in woodland or savanna environments.

Due to its unique blooming pattern and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
