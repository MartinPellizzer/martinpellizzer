Madhuca platyphylla

Madhuca platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leaf mahua, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are typically leathery in texture. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, hard seeds.

This plant is valued for its wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making, as well as for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
