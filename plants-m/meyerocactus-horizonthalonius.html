Meyerocactus horizonthalonius

Meyerocactus horizonthalonius, commonly known as the horizontal thorn cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that grow horizontally along the ground, often forming dense mats. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss and provide protection from herbivores.

Meyerocactus horizonthalonius is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, making it a resilient and hardy plant.
