Marginaria bombycina, commonly known as the cotton flax or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Malvaceae.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy, heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds. Native to the Americas, it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
