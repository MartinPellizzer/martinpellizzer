Meliosma sylvatica

Meliosma sylvatica, commonly known as the silverleaf maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically lobed and covered in a silvery-white pubescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that appear in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
