Malaxis acutangula

Malaxis acutangula, commonly known as the sharp-angled malaxis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments. The plant features a slender rhizome and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. Its botanical characteristics include narrow leaves and a compact inflorescence.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its natural range.
