Melissitus siunicus

Melissitus siunicus, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada beeplant, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Melissitus siunicus plays an important ecological role in supporting local pollinator populations.
