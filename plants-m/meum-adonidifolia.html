Meum adonidifolia

Meum adonidifolia, commonly known as common meum, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It produces small, rounded fruits called silicles, which contain several seeds.

Meum adonidifolia is typically herbaceous and grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters.
