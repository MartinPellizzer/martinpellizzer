Macrotyloma maranguense

Macrotyloma maranguense, commonly known as the Maranguense cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves, which are typically pubescent. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for both food and forage.

Due to its adaptability, it is an important crop in regions with seasonal rainfall.
