Madia radiata

Madia radiata, commonly known as the California poppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bright orange or red flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a common sight in open fields and along roadsides.

Madia radiata is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
