Manihot oligantha

Manihot oligantha, commonly known as the yellow arrowleaf, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are often green with a yellowish underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is adapted to grow in moist, tropical environments and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant contains milky sap, which can be toxic if ingested.
