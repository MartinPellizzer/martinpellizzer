Mitrastemon yamamotoi var. kanehirae

Mitrastemon yamamotoi var. kanehirae, commonly known as the Yamamoto's honeysuckle, is a rare shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowering period attracts various pollinators, including bees and moths.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
