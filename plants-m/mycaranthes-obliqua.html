Mycaranthes obliqua

Mycaranthes obliqua, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spirally twisted stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
