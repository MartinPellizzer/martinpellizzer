Mesechites subcarnosus

Mesechites subcarnosus, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and broad, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming during the summer months.
