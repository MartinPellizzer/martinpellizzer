Monocladus levigatus

Monocladus levigatus, commonly known as the smooth monoclad, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its simple, elongated leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
