Mentha longifolia var. transmota

Mentha longifolia var. transmota, commonly known as Transmontana mint, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage contains essential oils that contribute to its strong minty aroma.

This variety is valued for its use in herbal teas and as a culinary herb.
