Monardella nervosa

Monardella nervosa, commonly known as narrowleaf monardella, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically pink to purple, arranged in whorls along the stem, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Monardella nervosa is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands.
