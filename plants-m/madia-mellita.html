Madia mellita

Madia mellita, commonly known as the white madia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent.

Madia mellita thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
