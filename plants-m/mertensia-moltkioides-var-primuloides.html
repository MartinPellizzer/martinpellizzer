Mertensia moltkioides var. primuloides

Mertensia moltkioides var. primuloides, commonly known as the bluebells of the eastern woodlands, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features striking, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for woodland gardens and naturalized landscapes.
