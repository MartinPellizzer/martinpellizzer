Myrica pubescens var. caracasana

Myrica pubescens var. caracasana, commonly known as the Caracas wax myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a waxy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in catkins, followed by berries that turn from red to black as they mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or near freshwater sources.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
