Mesembryanthemum pentagonum

Mesembryanthemum pentagonum, commonly known as the five-angle ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, triangular-shaped leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the warmer months. It is a member of the Aizoaceae family, known for its adaptations to dry climates.

Mesembryanthemum pentagonum is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
