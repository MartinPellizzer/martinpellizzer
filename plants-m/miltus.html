Miltus

Miltus, commonly known as the false mallow, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Malvaceae.

It consists of several species that are native to various regions in Africa, Asia, and the Americas. These plants typically have hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, with showy, often pink or purple flowers. They are often found in moist or wetland habitats, thriving in humid environments.

Some species of Miltus are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
