Muhlenbergia articulata

Muhlenbergia articulata, commonly known as the hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and fine, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a soft, hairy texture. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are typically pink to purple in color, adding ornamental value to landscapes. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Muhlenbergia articulata is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
