Myrcia teimosa

Myrcia teimosa, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and fragrant, white to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that contain aromatic seeds.

Myrcia teimosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
