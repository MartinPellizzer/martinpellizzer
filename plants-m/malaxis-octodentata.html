Malaxis octodentata

Malaxis octodentata, commonly known as the eight-toothed malaxis, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which consists of small, greenish-white flowers clustered in a spike. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are usually elongated and slightly hairy, adapting to its low-light habitat.

Malaxis octodentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
