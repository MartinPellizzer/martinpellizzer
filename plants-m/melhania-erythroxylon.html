Melhania erythroxylon

Melhania erythroxylon, commonly known as the redwood tree, is a tropical hardwood species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its straight grain and dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy that provides significant shade. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, while its flowers are small and yellowish-green.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils and its role in traditional medicine in some regions.
