Moquinia argentina

Moquinia argentina, commonly known as the Argentine moquinia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along riverbanks.

Moquinia argentina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
