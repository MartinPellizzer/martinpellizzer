Momordica cordifolia

Momordica cordifolia, commonly known as bitter melon, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia and parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, spiny fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn orange when mature. The plant belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and watermelons. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and cuisine, valued for its bitter taste and nutritional properties.
