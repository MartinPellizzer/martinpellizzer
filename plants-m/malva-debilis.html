Malva debilis

Malva debilis, commonly known as the slender mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus, and is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves and showy pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Malva debilis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate.
