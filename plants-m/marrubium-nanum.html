Marrubium nanum

Marrubium nanum, commonly known as creeping horehound, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically forms dense mats of small, gray-green leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers during the summer months. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Marrubium nanum is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
