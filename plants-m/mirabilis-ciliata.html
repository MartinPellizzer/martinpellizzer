Mirabilis ciliata

Mirabilis ciliata, commonly known as the silky morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, often opening in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Mirabilis ciliata is also known for its short blooming period, typically lasting only a few hours each day.
