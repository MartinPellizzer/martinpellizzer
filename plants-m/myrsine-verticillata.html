Myrsine verticillata

Myrsine verticillata, commonly known as the clustered myrsine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant has opposite, elliptic leaves that are usually dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale pink and are borne in clusters.

The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
