Mentha mellifluens

Mentha mellifluens, commonly known as honey-scented mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to their aromatic properties.

Mentha mellifluens thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbalists.
