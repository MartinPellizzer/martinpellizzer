Mallotus attenuatus

Mallotus attenuatus, commonly known as the broadleaf mallot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, elongated leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, spiny fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and along stream banks.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
