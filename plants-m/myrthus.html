Myrthus

Myrthus, commonly known as sweet myrtle, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Myrtaceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, well-drained soils. Myrthus species are evergreen shrubs or small trees, with smooth, glossy bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves.

The fruit is a capsule that contains aromatic, oily seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and perfumery.
