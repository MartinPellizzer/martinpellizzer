Mirabilis melanotricha

Mirabilis melanotricha, commonly known as the black-haired four o'clock, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from white to deep red, depending on the time of day. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences that open in the late afternoon and close by dawn. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, silky hairs that give it its common name.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
