Mespilodaphne vaccinioides

Mespilodaphne vaccinioides, commonly known as the swampy myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy or riparian habitats.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a sought-after plant in horticulture.
