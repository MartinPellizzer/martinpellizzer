Monstera cannifolia

Monstera cannifolia, commonly known as the giant split-leaf philodendron, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that develop deep, irregular splits as they mature. The plant is a climbing vine that can grow up to 20 feet in height, requiring support structures for optimal growth. Its foliage is glossy and dark green, with a slightly variegated appearance in some cultivars.

Monstera cannifolia thrives in bright, indirect light and humid environments, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
