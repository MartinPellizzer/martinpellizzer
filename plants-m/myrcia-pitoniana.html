Myrcia pitoniana

Myrcia pitoniana, commonly known as the Pitoniana Myrtle, is a tropical shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, showy flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically red or black when mature, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments. Myrcia pitoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of tropical flora.
