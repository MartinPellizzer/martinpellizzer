Meximalva venusta

Meximalva venusta, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces showy, pale pink to lavender flowers with five petals, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, open habitats.

Meximalva venusta is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
