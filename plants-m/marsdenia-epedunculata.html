Marsdenia epedunculata

Marsdenia epedunculata, commonly known as the slender Marsdenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Asclepiadaceae family, which includes milkweeds, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, and its leaves are opposite and usually ovate. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forest understories where it climbs on trees or shrubs.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
