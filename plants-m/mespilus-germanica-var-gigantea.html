Mespilus germanica var. gigantea

Mespilus germanica var. gigantea, commonly known as the giant white service tree, is a variety of the common whitebeam.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its large size, reaching up to 20 meters in height, and its broad, spreading crown. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a serrated margin, turning yellow in autumn. It produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters, followed by edible, sweet drupes that are similar to cherries.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in European landscapes.
