Mimosa mellifera

Mimosa mellifera, commonly known as the sweet mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows up to 2 meters tall, featuring delicate, fern-like leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is known for its rapid leaf movements, a response to touch or light, which is a characteristic feature of the Mimosa genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
