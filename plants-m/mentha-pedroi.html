Mentha pedroi

Mentha pedroi, commonly known as Pedro's mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cooler, moist environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a strong, refreshing mint scent.

Mentha pedroi is often used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and antiseptic properties.
