Miscanthus nudipes subsp. wardii

Miscanthus nudipes subsp.

wardii, commonly known as the slender silver grass, is a perennial grass native to parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and narrow, silvery-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces panicles of small, pale green to reddish-brown flowers during the late summer and autumn months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
