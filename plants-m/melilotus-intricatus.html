Melilotus intricatus

Melilotus intricatus, commonly known as the intricate melilot, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Melilotus intricatus is often found in meadows and along roadsides, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
