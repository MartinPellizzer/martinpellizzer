Mesua macrophylla

Mesua macrophylla, commonly known as the Indian rose or the flame of the forest, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves and showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. The plant belongs to the family Lythraceae and is known for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display. Its flowers are typically borne on long, drooping inflorescences, and the tree can grow up to 15 meters in height.
