Marrubium parviflorum

Marrubium parviflorum, commonly known as smallflower horehound, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. The plant features gray-green, hairy leaves and produces small, blue or purple flowers arranged in clusters. It is part of the Boraginaceae family, known for its medicinal and aromatic properties.

Marrubium parviflorum has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its expectorant and anti-inflammatory effects.
