Melilotus speciosus

Melilotus speciosus, commonly known as sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spherical clusters. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.

Melilotus speciosus is also valued for its medicinal properties and as a forage crop for livestock.
