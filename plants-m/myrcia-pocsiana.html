Myrcia pocsiana

Myrcia pocsiana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, red flowers. The plant produces berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It is often found in coastal and tropical regions, thriving in humid, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in some areas for landscaping purposes.
