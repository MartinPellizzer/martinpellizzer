Malva maroccana

Malva maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan mallows, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture and produces showy pink or purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
