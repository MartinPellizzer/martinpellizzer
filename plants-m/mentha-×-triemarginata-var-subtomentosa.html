Mentha × triemarginata var. subtomentosa

Mentha × triemarginata var. subtomentosa, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and square stems, typical of the mint family (Lamiaceae). The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the upper stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
