Mitrephora reticulata

Mitrephora reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined mitrephora, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes cashew and mango. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it produces small, round fruits that are edible.
