Madhuca kerrii

Madhuca kerrii, commonly known as the Kerri's butter tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and large, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed.

Its latex is used in traditional medicine, and the tree is valued for its ornamental and economic importance in its native regions.
