Myonima multiflora var. oblongifolia

Myonima multiflora var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myonima, is a species native to Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer months. Its habitat includes sandy soils in coastal and inland regions, and it is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and unique foliage.
