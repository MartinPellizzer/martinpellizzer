Myriophyllum tillaeoides

Myriophyllum tillaeoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces tiny, floating flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as lakes and ponds.

Myriophyllum tillaeoides plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
