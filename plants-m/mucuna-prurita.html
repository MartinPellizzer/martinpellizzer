Mucuna prurita

Mucuna prurita, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. Mucuna prurita is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

However, it can be invasive and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
