Mesembryanthemum brevicaule

Mesembryanthemum brevicaule, commonly known as the ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves and small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in arid environments and is adapted to store water in its thick leaves. It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.

Mesembryanthemum brevicaule is also known for its ability to produce a crystalline substance on its leaves, which helps reduce water loss.
