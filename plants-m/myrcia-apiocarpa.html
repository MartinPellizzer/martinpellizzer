Myrcia apiocarpa

Myrcia apiocarpa, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its small, dark red berries that are typically clustered together. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, often with a strong aromatic scent.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
