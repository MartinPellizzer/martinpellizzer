Mentha aquatica var. acuta

Mentha aquatica var. acuta, commonly known as sharp water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers arranged in whorls along the stem, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found near streams or in damp meadows.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in herbal teas or as a culinary herb.
