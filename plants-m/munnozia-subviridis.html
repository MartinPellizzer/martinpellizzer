Munnozia subviridis

Munnozia subviridis, commonly known as the greenleaf mulefoot, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white to grayish hair. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open woodlands.

Munnozia subviridis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand drought conditions.
