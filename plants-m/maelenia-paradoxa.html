Maelenia paradoxa

Maelenia paradoxa, commonly known as the paradoxical maelenia, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves that often display a vibrant mix of green and red hues. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the wet season. Maelenia paradoxa thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its rarity and ornamental appeal, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
