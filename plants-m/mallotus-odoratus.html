Mallotus odoratus

Mallotus odoratus, commonly known as the sweet-smelling mallot, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, small white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its aromatic properties and is relatively easy to grow in tropical and subtropical climates.
