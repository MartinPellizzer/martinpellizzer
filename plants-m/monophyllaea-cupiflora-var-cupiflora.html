Monophyllaea cupiflora var. cupiflora

Monophyllaea cupiflora var. cupiflora, commonly known as the cup-shaped leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the humid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique cup-shaped leaves that form a rosette, which helps in water retention in its natural habitat. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often arranged in a spike. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Botanically, it belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is known for its specialized adaptations to its ecological niche.
