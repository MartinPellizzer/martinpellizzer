Mentha × villosa

Mentha × villosa, commonly known as woolly mint, is a hybrid perennial herb in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and square, erect stems. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. Woolly mint is native to Europe and Asia and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often used in herbal medicine and as a culinary herb due to its aromatic properties.
