Miconia neosecundiflora

Miconia neosecundiflora, commonly known as the Neosecond-flowering Miconia, is a member of the Melastomataceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America. The plant is characterized by its large, broad leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. It produces small, round fruits that are dispersed by animals.
