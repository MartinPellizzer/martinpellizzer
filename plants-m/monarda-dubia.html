Monarda dubia

Monarda dubia, commonly known as wild bergamot, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, tubular, pink to lavender flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has aromatic, hairy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Monarda dubia is also a valuable plant for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
