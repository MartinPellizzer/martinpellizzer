Millettia gossweileri

Millettia gossweileri, commonly known as the African wisteria, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces long, pendulous clusters of flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are often dispersed by wind.

Millettia gossweileri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual display.
