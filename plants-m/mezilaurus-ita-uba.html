Mezilaurus ita-uba

Mezilaurus ita-uba, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its dark, dense wood with a rich, reddish-brown color. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by dark blue berries containing a single seed.

The species is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in the production of musical instruments and fine furniture.
