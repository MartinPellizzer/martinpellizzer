Melastoma tomentosum

Melastoma tomentosum, commonly known as the hairy melastoma, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has hairy leaves and stems, which give it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Melastoma tomentosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
