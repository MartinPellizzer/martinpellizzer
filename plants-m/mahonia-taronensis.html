Mahonia taronensis

Mahonia taronensis, commonly known as the Taronese mahonia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with dark green, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, red berries that are edible for birds and other wildlife. Mahonia taronensis thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a suitable candidate for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its attractive foliage and seasonal floral display.
