Myrsine schomburgkiana

Myrsine schomburgkiana, commonly known as Schomburgk's myrsine, is a tropical evergreen shrub belonging to the family Myrsinaceae.

It is native to the rainforests of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green in color. Its small, white flowers are followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for various bird species.

Myrsine schomburgkiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded garden settings.
