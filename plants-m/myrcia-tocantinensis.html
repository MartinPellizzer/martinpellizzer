Myrcia tocantinensis

Myrcia tocantinensis, commonly known as the Tocantins myrtle, is a shrub native to the Brazilian state of Tocantins.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are typically clustered in small inflorescences and are fragrant, attracting various pollinators.

The fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature, and it plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing food for wildlife.
