Mertensia racemosa

Mertensia racemosa, commonly known as bluebells, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Mertensia racemosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
