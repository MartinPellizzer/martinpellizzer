Mentha arvensis var. cuneifolia

Mentha arvensis var. cuneifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf field mint, is a variant of the field mint species native to temperate regions.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and aromatic, slightly minty scent. The plant typically grows in moist soils and can be found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas. Its flowering stems bear small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
