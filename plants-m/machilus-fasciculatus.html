Machilus fasciculatus

Machilus fasciculatus, commonly known as the clustered Chinese evergreen, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its dense, clustered growth habit and dark green, glossy leaves. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible berries.

Machilus fasciculatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
