Mentha × verticillata f. circonensis

Mentha × verticillata f. circonensis, commonly known as the circonoid water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a hybrid species within the Mentha genus, characterized by its creeping growth habit and ability to form dense mats. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct minty aroma. It thrives in moist environments such as wet meadows, stream banks, and marshy areas.

Its small, tubular flowers are typically pale blue to purple and are arranged in whorls along the stems.
