Mertensia amplifolia

Mertensia amplifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf bluebells, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, blue-violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its foliage is often matted and can form dense ground cover, which helps suppress weeds.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
