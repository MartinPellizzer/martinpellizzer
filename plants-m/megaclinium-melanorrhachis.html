Megaclinium melanorrhachis

Megaclinium melanorrhachis, commonly known as the black-stemmed rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that are dark brown to black in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes during the late spring and early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense clusters at the base of the plant.

Megaclinium melanorrhachis thrives in moist, marshy environments and is often found in association with other aquatic or semi-aquatic plants.
