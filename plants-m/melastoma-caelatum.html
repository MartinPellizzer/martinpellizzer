Melastoma caelatum

Melastoma caelatum, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

Melastoma caelatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
