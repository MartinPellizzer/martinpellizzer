Millettia bracteosa

Millettia bracteosa, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with a velvety texture and small, clustered flowers that range from yellow to orange in color. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
