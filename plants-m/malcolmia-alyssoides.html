Malcolmia alyssoides

Malcolmia alyssoides, commonly known as the common mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features include a distinct capsule fruit that splits open to release its seeds.
