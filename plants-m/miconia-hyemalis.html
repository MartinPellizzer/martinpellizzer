Miconia hyemalis

Miconia hyemalis, commonly known as the winter miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the cooler months. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains numerous seeds.

Miconia hyemalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and unique floral display.
