Mikania corydalifolia

Mikania corydalifolia, commonly known as mile-a-minute weed, is a rapidly growing vine native to the Americas.

It has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is characterized by its extensive root system and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative growth. It is considered an invasive species in many regions due to its aggressive growth habit.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its adaptability to various soil types and light conditions.
