Mystroxylon eucleiforme

Mystroxylon eucleiforme, commonly known as the golden wax tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody fruits containing hard seeds. Its unique morphology makes it a subject of interest in botanical studies.

However, it is not widely cultivated due to its specific environmental requirements.
