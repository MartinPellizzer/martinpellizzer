Melicocca bijuga

Melicocca bijuga, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *mangaba*, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, glossy green leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to edible, sweet, and juicy fruits with a white, creamy pulp.

Its fruit is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications, and the tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
