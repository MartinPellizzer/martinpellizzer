Mahonia longipes

Mahonia longipes, commonly known as the longipes holly or longstem holly, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, branched stems and compound leaves that are dark green and glossy on top with a lighter underside. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by blue-black berries that are edible for birds. Mahonia longipes thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a hardy addition to native plant gardens.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a popular choice for landscape design in suitable climates.
