Mentha × villosa var. allieriensis

Mentha × villosa var. allieriensis, commonly known as the alliariopsis mint, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both mint and garlic chives.

It is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia, particularly China, where it grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features slender, aromatic leaves that are often described as having a mild, herbaceous scent. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in dense spikes, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and subtle fragrance.
