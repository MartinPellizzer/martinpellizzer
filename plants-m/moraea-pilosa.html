Moraea pilosa

Moraea pilosa, commonly known as the woolly moraea, is a striking perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant has a fibrous root system and linear, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, hairy textures. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Moraea pilosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable climates.
