Macfadyena tweedieana

Macfadyena tweedieana, commonly known as the Crown of Thorns, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has waxy, thick leaves that help it retain moisture in arid conditions. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its spread.

Macfadyena tweedieana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, sunny environments.
