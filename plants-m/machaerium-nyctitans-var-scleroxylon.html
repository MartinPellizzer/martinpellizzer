Machaerium nyctitans var. scleroxylon

Machaerium nyctitans var. scleroxylon, commonly known as the night-flowering machaerium, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant is notable for its large, showy, white to pale yellow flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it often grows in moist, shaded environments.
