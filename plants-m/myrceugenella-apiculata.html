Myrceugenella apiculata

Myrceugenella apiculata, commonly known as the Andean whitebeard tree, is a small evergreen tree native to the cloud forests of southern South America.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in high-altitude regions.

Myrceugenella apiculata is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
