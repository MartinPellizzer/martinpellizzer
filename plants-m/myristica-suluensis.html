Myristica suluensis

Myristica suluensis, commonly known as the Sulu nutmeg, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Sulu Archipelago in the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is closely related to the nutmeg tree, Myristica fragrans. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers and develops unique fruits that contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its botanical features include dark green, leathery leaves and a dense, aromatic bark.

The species is valued for its potential economic and medicinal significance in its native region.
