Mentha × subcordifrons

Mentha × subcordifrons, commonly known as the narrow-leaf peppermint, is a hybrid mint species resulting from the cross between Mentha viridis and Mentha piperita.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and a strong, aromatic scent reminiscent of both spearmint and peppermint. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. It is often found in shaded or partially shaded areas, such as woodlands or along stream banks.

Mentha × subcordifrons is valued for its use in herbal teas, aromatherapy, and as a source of essential oils.
