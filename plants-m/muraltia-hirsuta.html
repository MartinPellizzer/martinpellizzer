Muraltia hirsuta

Muraltia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy muraltia, is a species of flowering plant in the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Muraltia hirsuta is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fodder for livestock.
