Miconia denticulata

Miconia denticulata, commonly known as the toothed miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that are typically followed by bright red or orange berries. Miconia denticulata is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

However, it can become invasive in some regions due to its prolific seed production and adaptability.
