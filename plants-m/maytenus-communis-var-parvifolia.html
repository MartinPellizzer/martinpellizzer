Maytenus communis var. parvifolia

Maytenus communis var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved mayten, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive ovate to elliptical shape with a smooth margin. The flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences.

This species is known for its hard, durable wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
