Melilotus barcinonensis

Melilotus barcinonensis, commonly known as the Barbary melilot, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that grows up to 1 meter in height, featuring slender stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish or greenish flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Melilotus barcinonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
