Munnozia lyrata

Munnozia lyrata, commonly known as the songflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its unique, lyre-shaped leaves that give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its striking foliage and ecological significance, it is a valued species in native plant gardens and conservation efforts.
