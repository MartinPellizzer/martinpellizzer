Malpighia linearifolia

Malpighia linearifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Surinam cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malpighiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Guyana. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits that are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth texture, while its flowers are typically white or pink.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its ornate foliage and fruit production.
