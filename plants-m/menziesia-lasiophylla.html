Menziesia lasiophylla

Menziesia lasiophylla, commonly known as the roughleaf menziesia, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streambanks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture on the upper surface. Its small, white flowers appear in clusters during late spring to early summer.

The fruit is a small, red drupe that matures in the fall, providing food for various wildlife species.
