Myriophyllum verticillatum

Myriophyllum verticillatum, commonly known as water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has whorled leaves that are finely divided, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes and ponds.

Its dense growth can provide habitat for aquatic life but may also become invasive in certain regions.
