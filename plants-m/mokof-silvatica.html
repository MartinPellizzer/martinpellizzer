Mokof silvatica

Mokof silvatica, commonly known as the silverleaf mokof, is a unique plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an understory species, thriving in the shaded, humid environments of dense forests. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of pale yellow or green, and it produces small, round berries that are edible but not widely consumed by humans.

Mokof silvatica plays an important ecological role in supporting local biodiversity and maintaining the integrity of its forest habitat.
