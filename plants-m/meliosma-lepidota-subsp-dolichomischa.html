Meliosma lepidota subsp. dolichomischa

Meliosma lepidota subsp.

dolichomischa, commonly known as the longleaf meliosma, is a member of the Polygalaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, often twining stems and opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface.

The leaves are typically dark green and have a smooth texture, while the underside is paler and sometimes covered with fine hairs. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits.

This subspecies is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streamside habitats.
