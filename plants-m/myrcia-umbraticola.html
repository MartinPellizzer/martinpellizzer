Myrcia umbraticola

Myrcia umbraticola, commonly known as the shadow myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers, which are followed by berries that are edible for certain bird species. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy surface, while its bark is smooth and reddish in color.

Myrcia umbraticola plays an important role in the ecological balance of its habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife.
