Mesembryanthemum ebracteatum

Mesembryanthemum ebracteatum, commonly known as the ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves and small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions. It is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ability to tolerate full sun.

The plant's unique ability to produce a crystalline coating on its leaves helps reduce water loss, enhancing its survival in harsh climates.
