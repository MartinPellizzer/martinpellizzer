Mulgedium cyaneum

Mulgedium cyaneum, commonly known as the blue mulgedium, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet petals and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to habitat loss, Mulgedium cyaneum is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation areas.
