Myrcia coelosepala

Myrcia coelosepala, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and showy, white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its foliage is often used in perfumes and aromatherapy due to its pleasant scent.

Myrcia coelosepala is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
