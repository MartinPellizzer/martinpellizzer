Machilus litseifolius

Machilus litseifolius, commonly known as the Chinese laurier cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, glossy leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by edible, cherry-like berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in subtropical regions.

The bark and leaves contain aromatic compounds, contributing to its use in both culinary and medicinal applications.
