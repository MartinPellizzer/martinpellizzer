Masdevallia amabilis

Masdevallia amabilis, commonly known as the pink masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often pink or purple flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually dark green and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Masdevallia amabilis requires high humidity and indirect light to thrive, making it a popular choice for tropical gardens and greenhouses.
