Medinilla versicolor

Medinilla versicolor, commonly known as the velvet tangerine, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that resemble miniature tangerines. The plant produces small, round fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Medinilla versicolor thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil and partial shade.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
