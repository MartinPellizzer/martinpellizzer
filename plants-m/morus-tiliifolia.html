Morus tiliifolia

Morus tiliifolia, commonly known as the Chinese mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, aggregate fruits. The plant is often cultivated for its fibrous bark, which is used in papermaking and textiles. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Morus tiliifolia is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and as a food source for silkworms.
