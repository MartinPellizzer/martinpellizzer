Macaranga perakensis

Macaranga perakensis, commonly known as the Perak tree, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The leaves are usually light green and have a slightly hairy underside, while the flowers are small and clustered in yellowish-green cupules. It produces capsules as its fruit, which contain numerous tiny seeds covered in a sticky substance.

This plant is often found in lowland rainforests and is known for its rapid growth and ecological importance in tropical ecosystems.
