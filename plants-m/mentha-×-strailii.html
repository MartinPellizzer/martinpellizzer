Mentha × strailii

Mentha × strailii, commonly known as the hybrid mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a cross between Mentha longifolia (coin mint) and Mentha aquatica (water mint), resulting in a plant with strong aromatic properties. The leaves are oval-shaped, serrated, and have a fragrant, minty scent. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water sources.

This plant is valued for its use in culinary, medicinal, and aromatic applications.
