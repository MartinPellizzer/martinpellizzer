Machaerium heterophyllum

Machaerium heterophyllum, commonly known as the red sandalwood or red mallow, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or purple flowers, which are often clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.

Machaerium heterophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
