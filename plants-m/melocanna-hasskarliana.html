Melocanna hasskarliana

Melocanna hasskarliana, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, slender perennial plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The plant has large, elongated leaves that are typically green and glossy, providing a striking visual appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.

Due to its rapid growth and structural strength, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes or in ecological restoration projects.
