Myrtus tripliphylla

Myrtus tripliphylla, commonly known as the three-leaved myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, dark green foliage with three leaflets per leaf, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Myrtus tripliphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and aromatic properties.
