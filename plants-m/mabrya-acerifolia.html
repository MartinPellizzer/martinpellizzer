Mabrya acerifolia

Mabrya acerifolia, commonly known as the maple-leaved mabrya, is a unique evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, maple-like leaves that are typically lobed and have a vibrant green color throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white, followed by red or orange berries that are edible for certain bird species. Its foliage provides year-round visual interest, making it a desirable ornamental plant in shaded garden settings.

Mabrya acerifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.
