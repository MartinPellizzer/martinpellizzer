Matthiola oxyceras var. oasicola

Matthiola oxyceras var. oasicola, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

The flowers, which range in color from white to pink and purple, bloom in early spring and are often used in gardens for their ornamental value.
