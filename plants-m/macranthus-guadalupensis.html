Macranthus guadalupensis

Macranthus guadalupensis, commonly known as the Guadalupe macranthus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Macranthus guadalupensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
