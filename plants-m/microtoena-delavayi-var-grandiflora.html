Microtoena delavayi var. grandiflora

Microtoena delavayi var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered microtoena, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
