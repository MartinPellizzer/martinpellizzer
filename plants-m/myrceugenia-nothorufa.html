Myrceugenia nothorufa

Myrceugenia nothorufa, commonly known as the red-flowered myrtle, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, red or pink flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Myrceugenia nothorufa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
