Metadina multifolia

Metadina multifolia, commonly known as the many-leaved metadina, is a flowering plant native to the humid tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the wet season. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth. Its leaves are typically narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Metadina multifolia is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance and ecological significance.
