Myrcia foveolata

Myrcia foveolata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and red, fleshy fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

Myrcia foveolata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, where it can become invasive.
