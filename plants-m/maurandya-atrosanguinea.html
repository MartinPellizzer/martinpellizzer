Maurandya atrosanguinea

Maurandya atrosanguinea, commonly known as the dark blood mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple to almost black flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Maurandya atrosanguinea is valued in horticulture for its unique coloration and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
