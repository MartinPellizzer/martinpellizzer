Myrtoleucodendron tenellum

Myrtoleucodendron tenellum, commonly known as the slender myrtle-leaved tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The tree thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
