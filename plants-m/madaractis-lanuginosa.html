Madaractis lanuginosa

Madaractis lanuginosa, commonly known as the silky madaractis, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or orange. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its botanical features include a woody stem and opposite leaf arrangement.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
