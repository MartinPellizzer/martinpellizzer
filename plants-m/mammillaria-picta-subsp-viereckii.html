Mammillaria picta subsp. viereckii

Mammillaria picta subsp.

viereckii, commonly known as the Vireck's mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico. It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is covered in white to pale green spines.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xerophytic gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for collectors and succulent enthusiasts.
