Magnolia elfina

Magnolia elfina, commonly known as the elfin magnolia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of white to pale pink. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Magnolia elfina is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
