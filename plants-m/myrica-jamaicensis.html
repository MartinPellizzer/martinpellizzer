Myrica jamaicensis

Myrica jamaicensis, commonly known as the Jamaican wax myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its aromatic, waxy leaves and small, dark berries. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas. Its bark and leaves have been used traditionally for their medicinal properties.

Myrica jamaicensis plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
