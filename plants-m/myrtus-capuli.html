Myrtus capuli

Myrtus capuli, commonly known as the common myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, dark green leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Myrtus capuli is often used in landscaping and has historical uses in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.
