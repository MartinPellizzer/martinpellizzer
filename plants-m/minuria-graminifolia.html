Minuria graminifolia

Minuria graminifolia, commonly known as the grassy minuria, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically appear in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Minuria graminifolia plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native environment.
