Macroscepis mesoamericana

Macroscepis mesoamericana, commonly known as the Mesoamerican orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical forests. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with a rhizome that produces a cluster of leaves and flower spikes. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a distinctive coloration that includes shades of white, yellow, and green.

It plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing nectar for various pollinators.
