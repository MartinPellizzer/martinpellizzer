Mucuna mapirensis

Mucuna mapirensis, commonly known as the Mapirensis velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety pubescence covering its stems and leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant produces distinctive, spirally coiled pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and clearings.

Mucuna mapirensis is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a forage crop due to its nutritional content.
