Maharanga fistulosa

Maharanga fistulosa, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually large, heart-shaped, and have a waxy texture.

Maharanga fistulosa plays an important role in local ecosystems and is often used in traditional practices.
