Mallotus leveillanus

Mallotus leveillanus, commonly known as the African holly, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by orange-red berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in subtropical regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping and as a hedge plant.
