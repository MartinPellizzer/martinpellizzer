Matthiola graeca

Matthiola graeca, commonly known as Greek stock, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Matthiola graeca is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for temperate climates.
