Mikania latifolia

Mikania latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped blue to purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and forests.

Mikania latifolia is a member of the Asteraceae family and is known for its rapid growth and invasive potential in certain regions.
