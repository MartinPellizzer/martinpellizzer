Mahernia vernicata

Mahernia vernicata, commonly known as the glossy mahernia, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its evergreen, leathery leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in Mediterranean climates.
