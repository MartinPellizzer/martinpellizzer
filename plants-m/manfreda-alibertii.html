Manfreda alibertii

Manfreda alibertii, commonly known as the Arikara ghost flower, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and are often tinged with purple. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapted to its arid and semi-arid habitat. It typically grows in rocky soils and is often found in desert environments.

Manfreda alibertii is a perennial species that blooms in late spring or early summer.
