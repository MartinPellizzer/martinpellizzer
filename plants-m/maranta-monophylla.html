Maranta monophylla

Maranta monophylla, commonly known as the velvetleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often a deep red or purple, especially when exposed to bright light. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Maranta monophylla is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
