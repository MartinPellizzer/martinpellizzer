Machilus chekiangensis

Machilus chekiangensis, commonly known as the Chinese mahogany, is a deciduous tree native to eastern China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark purple berries. It is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in forested areas.
