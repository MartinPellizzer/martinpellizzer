Mutisia splendens

Mutisia splendens, commonly known as the golden mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its dense, hairy foliage. The plant produces showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant thrives in humid, tropical climates.

Mutisia splendens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
