Mertensia scalpturata

Mertensia scalpturata, commonly known as the Appalachian bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.
