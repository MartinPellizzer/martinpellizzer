Malva micans subsp. pallescens

Malva micans subsp.

pallescens, commonly known as the pale mallows, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its hairy, pale green stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Malvaceae family.
