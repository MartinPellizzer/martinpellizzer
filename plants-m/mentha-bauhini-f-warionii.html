Mentha bauhini f. warionii

Mentha bauhini f. warionii, commonly known as the African spearmint, is a hybrid mint species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and digestive properties.

Its essential oil is valued for its minty fragrance and potential therapeutic applications.
