Magnolia poqomchi

Magnolia poqomchi, commonly known as the Yucatan magnolia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter, typically displaying white or pale pink hues. The plant produces elliptical, dark green leaves that are glossy and leathery in texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

The flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
