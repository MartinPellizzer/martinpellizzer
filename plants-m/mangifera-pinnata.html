Mangifera pinnata

Mangifera pinnata, commonly known as the hairy mangosteen, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and often covered in fine hairs. The tree produces small, white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, reddish-purple fruits. The fruit has a thick, segmented rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp.

It is valued not only for its fruit but also for its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties.
