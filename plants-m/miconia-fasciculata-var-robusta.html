Miconia fasciculata var. robusta

Miconia fasciculata var. robusta, commonly known as the robust miconia, is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers with five petals, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of branches. Its fruits are fleshy berries that turn red when mature, containing numerous small seeds.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
