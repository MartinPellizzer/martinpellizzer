Myrcia clusiifolia

Myrcia clusiifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its showy, clustered purple berries that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with glossy, elliptical leaves. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, often giving the plant a fragrant appearance.

The berries are not only ornamental but also attract birds and other wildlife, contributing to its ecological significance.
