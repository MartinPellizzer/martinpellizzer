Micropera proboscidea

Micropera proboscidea, commonly known as the proboscideous micropera, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a distinctive tubular shape. The plant has a short lifespan, typically flowering and setting seed within a few weeks. Its name refers to the elongated, proboscis-like structures that aid in pollination.

Botanically, it is classified within the family Apiaceae, sharing some features with parsley and carrots.
