Medicago murex subsp. heterocarpa

Medicago murex subsp.

heterocarpa, commonly known as the heterocarpic goat's rue, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by distinctive, inflated pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

This subspecies is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
