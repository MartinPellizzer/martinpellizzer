Myrtus gervasii

Myrtus gervasii, commonly known as the Gervasii myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible berries. Its leaves are evergreen, glossy, and have a distinct aromatic scent.

Myrtus gervasii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and historical medicinal uses.
