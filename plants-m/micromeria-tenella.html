Micromeria tenella

Micromeria tenella, commonly known as the narrowleaf sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small, herbaceous annual that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Micromeria tenella is adapted to arid environments and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
