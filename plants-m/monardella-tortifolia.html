Monardella tortifolia

Monardella tortifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf monardella, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of tubular, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its aromatic foliage.

Monardella tortifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and serves as a nectar source for pollinators.
