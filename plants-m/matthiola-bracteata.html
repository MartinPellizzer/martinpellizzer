Matthiola bracteata

Matthiola bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate stock, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with characteristic cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features dense clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are often hairy as well.

Matthiola bracteata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
