Maackia hwashanensis

Maackia hwashanensis, commonly known as the Korean maackia, is a deciduous shrub native to Korea.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume-like pods and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring. Its fruit is a legume that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

Maackia hwashanensis is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
