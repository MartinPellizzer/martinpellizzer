Mussaenda anisophylla

Mussaenda anisophylla, commonly known as the yellow Mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow inflorescences that resemble umbrellas, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with multiple branches and a compact form.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
