Monophyllaea furcipila

Monophyllaea furcipila, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, adapting to arid environments.

It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.
