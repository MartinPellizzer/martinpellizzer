Moltkia sendtneri

Moltkia sendtneri, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and showy, pale pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Moltkia sendtneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
