Malaxis hieronymi

Malaxis hieronymi, commonly known as the Hieronymus's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a loose spike. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in rocky or forested areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often clustered at the base of the stem.

The species is notable for its unique floral structure and is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
