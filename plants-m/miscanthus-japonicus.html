Miscanthus japonicus

Miscanthus japonicus, commonly known as Japanese silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and fluffy, silver-gray plumes that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
