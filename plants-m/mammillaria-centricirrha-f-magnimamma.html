Mammillaria centricirrha f. magnimamma

Mammillaria centricirrha f. magnimamma, commonly known as the giant mammy cactus, is a fascinating succulent native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 30 centimeters in height, adorned with dense, white to light yellow spines. The plant produces showy, pale yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or arid landscapes.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is a prized specimen among cactus enthusiasts.
