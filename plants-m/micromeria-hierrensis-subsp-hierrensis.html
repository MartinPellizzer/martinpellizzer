Micromeria hierrensis subsp. hierrensis

Micromeria hierrensis subsp.

hierrensis, commonly known as the Desert Flax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a low-growing shrub with narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

The species is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
