Myrcia mesoamericana

Myrcia mesoamericana, commonly known as the Mexican myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. It produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in moist forest understories.
