Marshallia latifolia

Marshallia latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf marshmallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and wetlands. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, and its flowers are pink to purple in color.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
