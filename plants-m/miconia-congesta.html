Miconia congesta

Miconia congesta, commonly known as the clustered miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are round, fleshy berries that turn red when mature, containing numerous seeds.

Miconia congesta is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.
