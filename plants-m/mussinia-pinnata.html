Mussinia pinnata

Mussinia pinnata, commonly known as the pinnate moss campion, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and delicate, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous habitats. Mussinia pinnata is known for its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions, including cold temperatures and strong winds.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and resilient species within its ecological niche.
