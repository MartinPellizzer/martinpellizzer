Mentha chalepensis

Mentha chalepensis, commonly known as wild mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Mentha chalepensis is known for its strong, refreshing menthol scent, which is used in various medicinal and culinary applications.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
