Mallotus nesophilus

Mallotus nesophilus, commonly known as the mountain mallot, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive fruiting structures. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments at elevations above 1,000 meters. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptic in shape, with a glossy surface and prominent veins.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, while the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.
