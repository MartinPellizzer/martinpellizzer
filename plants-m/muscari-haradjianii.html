Muscari haradjianii

Muscari haradjianii, commonly known as the Armenian grape hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It produces dense clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to violet in color. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings. Its bulbs are relatively hardy and can withstand cold winters, allowing it to return year after year.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
