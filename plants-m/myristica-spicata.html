Myristica spicata

Myristica spicata, commonly known as the nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Banda Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and distinctive yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that contain the aromatic seeds used for nutmeg and mace. Its bark and leaves also contain essential oils with medicinal properties.

Myristica spicata is cultivated for its economic value and is an important species in tropical botany.
