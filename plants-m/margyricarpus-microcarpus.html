Margyricarpus microcarpus

Margyricarpus microcarpus, commonly known as the dwarf bottlebrush tree, is a small, deciduous tree native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bottle-shaped fruits that contain numerous small, winged seeds. The plant has smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
