Malus × floribunda var. arnoldiana

Malus × floribunda var. arnoldiana, commonly known as the Weeping Crabsapple, is a cultivar of the crabapple tree characterized by its distinctive weeping habit.

It features pendulous branches that bear clusters of small, white or pink flowers in early spring. The plant produces small, red or yellow fruits in the fall, which are edible but often used for ornamental purposes. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its graceful appearance.
