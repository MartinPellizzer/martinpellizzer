Melampodium gracile

Melampodium gracile, commonly known as the slender sunflower, is a low-growing annual plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features bright yellow flowers with orange centers, blooming from late spring to early fall. The plant has slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

Melampodium gracile is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
