Middletonia reticulata

Middletonia reticulata, commonly known as the reticulated middletonia, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, lacy foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests or mountainous areas with high humidity. Its unique structure and ornamental appeal make it a sought-after specimen among botanists and plant enthusiasts.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
