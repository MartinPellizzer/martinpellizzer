Malouetia quadricasarum

Malouetia quadricasarum, commonly known as the four-casque malouetia, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, clustered inflorescences that resemble a crown, giving it its common name. The plant features delicate, translucent petals that are often tinged with shades of pink and white. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is typically found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Malouetia quadricasarum is considered a species of conservation concern.
