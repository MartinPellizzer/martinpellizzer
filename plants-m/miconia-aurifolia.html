Miconia aurifolia

Miconia aurifolia, commonly known as golden leaf miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, golden hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Miconia aurifolia is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
