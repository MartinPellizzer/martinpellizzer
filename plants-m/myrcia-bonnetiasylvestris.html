Myrcia bonnetiasylvestris

Myrcia bonnetiasylvestris, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, featuring elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing numerous seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, where it can become invasive.
