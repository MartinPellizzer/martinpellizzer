Maurocenia sphaerocarpa

Maurocenia sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the red globemallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-pink flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, ovate leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. Its fruit is a spherical capsule that contains numerous small, dry seeds.

Maurocenia sphaerocarpa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
