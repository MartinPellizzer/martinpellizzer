Morocarpus virens

Morocarpus virens, commonly known as the green morocarpus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the Arabian Peninsula.

It features thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by distinctive, spiky fruits. Its leaves are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures, minimizing water loss through transpiration.

Morocarpus virens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
