Myrtus baui

Myrtus baui, commonly known as the bay leaf plant, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, dark green leaves that are aromatic when crushed, often used in cooking and traditional medicine. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, dark blue berries. Myrtus baui thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal gardens.

Its historical use dates back to ancient times, where it was valued for its fragrance and medicinal properties.
