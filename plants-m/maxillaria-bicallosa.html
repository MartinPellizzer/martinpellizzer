Maxillaria bicallosa

Maxillaria bicallosa, commonly known as the bicolored maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms during the summer months, with flowers emerging from the axils of the leaves. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a leathery texture and a waxy coating.

Maxillaria bicallosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a suitable environment.
