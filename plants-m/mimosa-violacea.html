Mimosa violacea

Mimosa violacea, commonly known as the violet mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, violet-pink flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Mimosa violacea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
