Myrica dregeana

Myrica dregeana, commonly known as the African wax tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in spring. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a distinctive aromatic scent.
