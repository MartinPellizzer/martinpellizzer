Massonia amygdalina

Massonia amygdalina, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey-green leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, egg-shaped fruits that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
