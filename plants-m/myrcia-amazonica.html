Myrcia amazonica

Myrcia amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, reddish-brown berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in dense undergrowth.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the biodiversity in its native habitat.
