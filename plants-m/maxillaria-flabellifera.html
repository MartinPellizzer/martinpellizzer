Maxillaria flabellifera

Maxillaria flabellifera, commonly known as the fan-leaf orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb and produces multiple grass-like leaves. The plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple with a distinctive, fan-shaped lip. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Maxillaria flabellifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
