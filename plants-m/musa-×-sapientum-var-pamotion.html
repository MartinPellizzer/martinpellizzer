Musa × sapientum var. pamotion

Musa × sapientum var. pamotion, commonly known as the red banana, is a cultivar of the genus Musa, characterized by its distinct red-purple peel and sweet, creamy flesh.

It is a hybrid of Musa acuminata and Musa balbisiana, resulting in a plant that is typically grown for its ornamental and culinary value. The plant is a herbaceous perennial, with large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring warm temperatures and high humidity for optimal growth.

The fruit develops in clusters, known as hands, and is often harvested when the peel has fully changed color.
