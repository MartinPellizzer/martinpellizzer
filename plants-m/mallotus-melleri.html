Mallotus melleri

Mallotus melleri, commonly known as the red-flowered hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers, which are typically five-petaled and resemble those of the hibiscus genus. The plant has alternate, simple, and often elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Mallotus melleri is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
