Moehringia Trinervia

Moehringia trinervia is a small, delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the carnation family, known for its vibrant and often showy blooms. This species is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves and clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes. Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Moehringia trinervia is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
