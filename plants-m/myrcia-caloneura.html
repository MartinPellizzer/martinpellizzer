Myrcia caloneura

Myrcia caloneura, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dark seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
