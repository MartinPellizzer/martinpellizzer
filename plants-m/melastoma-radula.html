Melastoma radula

Melastoma radula, commonly known as the red root melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking red or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands. Its leaves are opposite and often have a glossy surface, while its roots are thick and fleshy.

Melastoma radula is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
