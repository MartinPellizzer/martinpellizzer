Malva divotiana

Malva divotiana, commonly known as the common mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. The plant is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Malva divotiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
