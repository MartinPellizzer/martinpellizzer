Malesherbia solanoides var. ovata

Malesherbia solanoides var. ovata, commonly known as the ovate malesherbia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb with hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
