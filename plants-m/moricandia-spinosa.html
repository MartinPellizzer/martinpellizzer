Moricandia spinosa

Moricandia spinosa, commonly known as prickly moringa, is a flowering plant native to regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating drought tolerance.

Its leaves are edible and have been used for their nutritional and medicinal properties in various cultures.
