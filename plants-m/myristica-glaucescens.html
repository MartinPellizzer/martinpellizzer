Myristica glaucescens

Myristica glaucescens, commonly known as the white nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen belonging to the Myristicaceae family.

It is native to the Indonesian archipelago, particularly Sumatra and Java, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The tree is valued for its essential oils and has cultural significance in Southeast Asian regions.
