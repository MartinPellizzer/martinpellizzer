Melascus latifolius

Melascus latifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved melascus, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
