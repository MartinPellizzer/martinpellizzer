Medicago catalonica

Medicago catalonica, commonly known as the Caucasian clover, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It is a legume, capable of fixing nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with Rhizobium bacteria.

Its adaptability makes it a valuable forage plant in certain agricultural settings.
