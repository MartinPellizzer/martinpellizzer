Myricaria dahurica

Myricaria dahurica, commonly known as the Siberian tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is well adapted to harsh environments, with a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, allowing it to colonize disturbed soils and rocky areas.

Myricaria dahurica plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
