Marattia fraxinea var. howeana

Marattia fraxinea var. howeana, commonly known as the Howe's tree fern, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, slender fern with a distinctive, unbranched trunk that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces large, pinnate fronds that are typically green on the upper surface and lighter in color on the underside. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The fronds are used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants in horticulture.
