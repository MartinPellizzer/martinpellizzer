Moerenhoutia lamellata

Moerenhoutia lamellata, commonly known as the silverleaf daphne, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which has a distinctive silvery-white underside. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers during the summer months. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is a popular choice among gardeners in warm climates.
