Masdevallia leucophaea

Masdevallia leucophaea, commonly known as the white-veined masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, delicate flowers with white petals and deep green sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Masdevallia leucophaea thrives in cool, humid environments with high levels of indirect light.
