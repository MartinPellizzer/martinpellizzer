Manihot tomentosa subsp. tomentosa

Manihot tomentosa subsp.

tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly arrowroot, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its swollen, tuberous stems that are used as a starchy food source.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has alternate, heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, white hairs. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in clusters, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its nutritional value and adaptability, it is cultivated in various parts of the world for its edible tubers.
