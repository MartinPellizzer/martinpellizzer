Myrceugenia montana

Myrceugenia montana, commonly known as the mountain myrtle, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the temperate rainforests of southern Chile.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, aromatic white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Myrceugenia montana plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
