Morus notabilis

Morus notabilis, commonly known as the white mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in spring. The tree produces small, fleshy berries that are edible for both humans and animals. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its fruit and historical significance in silk production.

The white mulberry is also valued for its role in supporting the life cycle of silkworms.
