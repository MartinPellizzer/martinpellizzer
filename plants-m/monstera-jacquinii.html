Monstera jacquinii

Monstera jacquinii, commonly known as the split-leaf philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that develop distinctive splits as they mature. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light. Its stems are long and can climb or trail, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets or shelves.

The leaves are typically a deep green with a slightly glossy texture, adding an ornamental appeal to indoor spaces.
