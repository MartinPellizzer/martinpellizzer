Mertensia nutans

Mertensia nutans, commonly known as nodding bluebells, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that droop downward, hence its common name. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Mertensia nutans is a member of the Boraginaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value.
