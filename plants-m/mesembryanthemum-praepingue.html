Mesembryanthemum praepingue

Mesembryanthemum praepingue, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ice plant, is a succulent perennial native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, narrow leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in spring. It is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.

Its unique appearance and ability to attract pollinators make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
