Monarda citriodora var. pectinata

Monarda citriodora var. pectinata, commonly known as lemon bee balm, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features aromatic, lemon-scented leaves that are often used in herbal teas and culinary applications. The plant produces showy, tubular red or pink flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Its botanical characteristics include opposite leaves and a square stem typical of the Lamiaceae family.
