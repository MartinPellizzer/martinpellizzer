Melaleuca montana

Melaleuca montana, commonly known as the mountain melaleuca, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to the eastern and southeastern regions of Australia, where it typically grows in heathlands and open forests. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, rounded leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow and are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences.

Melaleuca montana is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
