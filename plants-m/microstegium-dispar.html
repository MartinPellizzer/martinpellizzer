Microstegium dispar

Microstegium dispar, commonly known as the slender wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers in the spring. Its stems are slender and often branched, with a hairy texture.

Microstegium dispar is part of the Oxalidaceae family, known for its calcium oxalate crystals in the leaves.
