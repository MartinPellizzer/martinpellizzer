Melissa officinalis var. vulgaris

Melissa officinalis var. vulgaris, commonly known as lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, serrated leaves and small, pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, often growing in moist environments. Its essential oils, particularly those containing citral, contribute to its calming effects and are used in aromatherapy and herbal medicine.

Lemon balm is also valued for its historical use in treating anxiety and digestive issues.
