Mentha × villosa var. wirtgeniana

Mentha × villosa var. wirtgeniana, commonly known as the Wirtgen's hairy mint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves are used in traditional herbal medicine and culinary applications.
