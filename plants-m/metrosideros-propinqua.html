Metrosideros propinqua

Metrosideros propinqua, commonly known as the Cook Islands Tahitian cherry, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Cook Islands and French Polynesia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, cherry-like fruits that are edible and contain a single seed. It thrives in coastal and forested areas, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in various tropical regions.
