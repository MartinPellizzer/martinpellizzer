Mosiera × miraflorensis

Mosiera × miraflorensis, commonly known as the hybrid hibiscus, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Hibiscus moscheutos and Hibiscus coccineus.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red, often with a distinctive yellow or orange center. The plant is native to the wetlands and marshes of the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, sunny conditions. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Mosiera × miraflorensis is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
