Mussaenda lanceolata

Mussaenda lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf Mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of showy, orange to yellow bract-like flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to gardens.
