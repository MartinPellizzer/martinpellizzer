Malva althaeoides var. cretica

Malva althaeoides var. cretica, commonly known as the Cretan mallow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry garden environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
