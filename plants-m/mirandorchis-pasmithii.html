Mirandorchis pasmithii

Mirandorchis pasmithii, commonly known as the Pasmith's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the misty cloud forests of southern China.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a unique growth habit, characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant produces pale greenish-yellow flowers with intricate patterns, which are adapted for specific pollination mechanisms. It thrives in shaded, humid environments with rich, well-drained soil, often growing near moss-covered rocks or tree roots.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
