Malope asterotricha

Malope asterotricha, commonly known as the woolly mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive star-shaped pattern, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and drought tolerance.
