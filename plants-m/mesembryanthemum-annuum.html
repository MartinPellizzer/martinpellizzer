Mesembryanthemum annuum

Mesembryanthemum annuum, commonly known as the common ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in summer. It is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.

Mesembryanthemum annuum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to prevent soil erosion.
