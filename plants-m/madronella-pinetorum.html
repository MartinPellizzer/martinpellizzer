Madronella pinetorum

Madronella pinetorum, commonly known as the silverleaf madrona, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray bark and oval, dark green leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions and chaparral habitats.

The silverleaf madrona is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
