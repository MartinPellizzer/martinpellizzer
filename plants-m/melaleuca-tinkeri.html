Melaleuca tinkeri

Melaleuca tinkeri, commonly known as the Christmas bush, is a species of flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom during the winter months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and heathlands.

Melaleuca tinkeri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
