Malpighia polytricha subsp. vivaldiana

Malpighia polytricha subsp.

vivaldiana, commonly known as the Vivaldiana waxapple, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America. It is a small shrub that produces bright red, fleshy berries, which are edible and have a tart flavor.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in disturbed soils and open woodlands. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and fruit, which is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
