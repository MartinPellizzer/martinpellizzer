Mentha × aquaticoarvensis f. elliptica

Mentha × aquaticoarvensis f. elliptica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water mint, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both Mentha aquatica and Mentha arvensis.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant with slender, erect stems that often grow in wet or marshy environments. The leaves are elliptical in shape, with a smooth margin and a slightly serrated edge, and they are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which are borne on elongated stalks.

It is known for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
