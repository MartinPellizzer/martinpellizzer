Mystacidium parvum

Mystacidium parvum, commonly known as the small mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It possesses specialized structures called haustoria that penetrate the host's vascular system to extract water and nutrients. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces white or yellowish flowers, followed by small, berry-like fruits. It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for birds and insects.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can sometimes cause stress to its host plants if left unchecked.
