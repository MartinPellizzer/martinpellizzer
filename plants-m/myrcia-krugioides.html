Myrcia krugioides

Myrcia krugioides, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a flowering plant native to Brazil and parts of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains several seeds, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

Myrcia krugioides is also known for its aromatic properties and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
