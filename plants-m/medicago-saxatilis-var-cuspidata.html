Medicago saxatilis var. cuspidata

Medicago saxatilis var. cuspidata, commonly known as the sharp-lobed goatweed, is a flowering plant native to rocky and arid environments.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has a low-growing habit with hairy stems and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This variety is adapted to harsh conditions and plays a role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
