Myrcia regnelliana var. leptocalama

Myrcia regnelliana var. leptocalama, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrcia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves and small, clustered white or pinkish flowers. It produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but often have a strong, spicy flavor.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
