Miconia pittieri

Miconia pittieri, commonly known as the red miconia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves and showy, pinkish-white bracts. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
