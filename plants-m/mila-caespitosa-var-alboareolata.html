Mila caespitosa var. alboareolata

Mila caespitosa var. alboareolata, commonly known as the white-spotted mila, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive white or pale green spots on its leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural collections.
