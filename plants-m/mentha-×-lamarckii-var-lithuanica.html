Mentha × lamarckii var. lithuanica

Mentha × lamarckii var. lithuanica, commonly known as the Lithuanian mint, is a hybrid mint species native to Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves that emit a strong menthol scent. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along streams. It produces small, pale purple flowers in whorled clusters, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its use in herbal teas and traditional medicine due to its cooling and soothing properties.
