Malva miniata

Malva miniata, commonly known as the small mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and long-lasting blooms.
