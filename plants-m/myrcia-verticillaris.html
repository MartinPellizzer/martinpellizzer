Myrcia verticillaris

Myrcia verticillaris, commonly known as the clustered myrtle, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or orange when ripe. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

Myrcia verticillaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
