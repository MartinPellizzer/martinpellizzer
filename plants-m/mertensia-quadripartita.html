Mertensia quadripartita

Mertensia quadripartita, commonly known as the four-parted bluebells, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple, blooming in early spring. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Mertensia quadripartita is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in woodland gardens.
