Microstegium gratum

Microstegium gratum, commonly known as the pretty spikegrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant features delicate, hairless stems that bear small, pale green to blue-green leaves. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of tiny, two-lobed flowers that bloom in early summer.

Microstegium gratum is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
