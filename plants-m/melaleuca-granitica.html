Melaleuca granitica

Melaleuca granitica, commonly known as the granite melaleuca, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, bark-like leaves and distinctive, bottlebrush-like flowers that are typically white or pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils, often found in rocky or sandy areas. It is part of the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus.

Melaleuca granitica is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
