Myrtoleucodendron striatum

Myrtoleucodendron striatum, commonly known as the silver-veined myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy white to pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and often have a silvery or white venation pattern.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.
