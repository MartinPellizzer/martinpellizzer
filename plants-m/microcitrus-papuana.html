Microcitrus papuana

Microcitrus papuana, commonly known as the Papuan lime, is a small evergreen shrub native to the island of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes other citrus plants, and is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or orange when mature. Its flowers are white and clustered, adding ornamental value to the plant.

Microcitrus papuana is often cultivated for its ornamental appeal and as a source of fragrant essential oils.
