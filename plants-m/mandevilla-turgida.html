Mandevilla turgida

Mandevilla turgida, commonly known as the flame vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and cascading clusters of bright orange to red tubular flowers. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are typically ovate with a glossy surface and a slightly hairy texture.

Mandevilla turgida thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated as a decorative vine in gardens and hanging baskets.
