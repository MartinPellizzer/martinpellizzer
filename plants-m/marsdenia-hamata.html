Marsdenia hamata

Marsdenia hamata, commonly known as the hairy Marsdenia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asclepiadaceae.

It is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, China, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and stems that are often covered in fine, hairy structures. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive corona that resembles a crown.
