Melaleuca lanceolata subsp. planifolia

Melaleuca lanceolata subsp.

planifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved paperbark, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its lanceolate, often broad and smooth, grey-green leaves that are covered in a fine, whitish bloom.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pink, which bloom in spring. Its bark is fibrous and peels off in strips, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This species is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal or wetland areas.
