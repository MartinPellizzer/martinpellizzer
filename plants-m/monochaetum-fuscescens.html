Monochaetum fuscescens

Monochaetum fuscescens, commonly known as the black spike grass, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to brown flowers. It is typically found in sandy or rocky soils in open habitats such as heaths and woodlands.
