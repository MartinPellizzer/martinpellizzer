Melia birmanica

Melia birmanica, commonly known as the Burmese chinaberry tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces distinctive, elongated fruits that contain toxic seeds, which are not suitable for human consumption. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland areas with well-drained soils.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other regions for landscaping purposes.
