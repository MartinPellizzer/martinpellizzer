Muscarella herpestes

Muscarella herpestes, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Muscarella herpestes is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
