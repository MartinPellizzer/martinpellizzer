Mentha procumbens var. major

Mentha procumbens var. major, commonly known as American mint, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its creeping growth habit and aromatic, serrated leaves that emit a strong menthol scent. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its use in herbal teas and as a natural remedy for digestive issues.
