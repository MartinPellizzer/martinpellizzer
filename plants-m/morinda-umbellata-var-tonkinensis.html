Morinda umbellata var. tonkinensis

Morinda umbellata var. tonkinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese red root, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, followed by red or orange berries. Its roots are widely used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitats.
