Mussaenda microdonta

Mussaenda microdonta, commonly known as the small-flowered Mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, which are followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
