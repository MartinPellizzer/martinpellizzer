Macaranga conifera

Macaranga conifera, commonly known as the coniferous macaranga, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often palmately lobed. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It is commonly found in lowland forests and thrives in humid, tropical climates.

Macaranga conifera is also noted for its role in ecological systems, providing habitat and food for various insect species.
