Moniera beccabunga

Moniera beccabunga, commonly known as the marsh woundwort, is a flowering plant native to wetland and marshy areas.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in temperate regions.

Moniera beccabunga plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various insects and microorganisms.
