Melica colpodoides var. sesquiflora

Melica colpodoides var. sesquiflora, commonly known as the narrowleaf brome, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, meadows, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a hairy texture.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
