Mimosa abstergens

Mimosa abstergens, commonly known as the sensitive plant, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that rapidly fold when touched, a response triggered by mechanical stimulation. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. Its botanical name, *Mimosa abstergens*, refers to its ability to "clean" or "remove" itself from stimuli, a trait linked to its sensitive leaf movements.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
