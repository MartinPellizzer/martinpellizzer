Mutisia microneura

Mutisia microneura, commonly known as the small-veined mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Mutisia microneura is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
