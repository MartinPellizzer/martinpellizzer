Mammillaria radicantissima

Mammillaria radicantissima, commonly known as the desert hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its compact size and unique appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus collections.
