Machaerium sordidum

Machaerium sordidum, commonly known as the black machaerium, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands, often found in moist, shady environments. The plant features trifoliate leaves with slender, hairy stalks and produces small, pea-like flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several compressed seeds.

Machaerium sordidum is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
