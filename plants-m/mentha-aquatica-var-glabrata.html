Mentha aquatica var. glabrata

Mentha aquatica var. glabrata, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, aromatic stems that often grow along the edges of ponds and streams. The leaves are lance-shaped, slightly hairy, and have a strong minty scent. It produces small, pale blue or white flowers in whorls along the stem, typically blooming in late summer.

This variety is closely related to other mint species and is valued for its medicinal and culinary uses.
