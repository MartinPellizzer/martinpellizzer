Myrtus biflora var. yapacani

Myrtus biflora var. yapacani, commonly known as the Yapacani myrtle, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Myrtus biflora var. yapacani is valued for its fragrant foliage and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental appeal.
