Mallostoma filiformis

Mallostoma filiformis, commonly known as the slender leaf bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It possesses small, bladder-like structures that trap aquatic prey, such as water beetles and small crustaceans. The plant's leaves are thin and filamentous, adapted for efficient movement in water. It reproduces through both sexual and asexual means, often spreading via runners.

Its botanical features reflect its specialized adaptation to nutrient-poor aquatic environments.
