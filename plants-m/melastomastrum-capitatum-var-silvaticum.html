Melastomastrum capitatum var. silvaticum

Melastomastrum capitatum var. silvaticum, commonly known as the forest melastoma, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, with five petals and a prominent central stamen. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
