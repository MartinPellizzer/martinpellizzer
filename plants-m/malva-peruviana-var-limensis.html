Malva peruviana var. limensis

Malva peruviana var. limensis, commonly known as the Peruvian mallows, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. It is often found in wetlands, riverbanks, and other riparian habitats.

Its fibrous roots and mucilaginous leaves have been used traditionally for their soothing properties.
