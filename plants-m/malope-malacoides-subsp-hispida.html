Malope malacoides subsp. hispida

Malope malacoides subsp.

hispida, commonly known as the roughleaf malope, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its glandular, hispid (hairy) leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
