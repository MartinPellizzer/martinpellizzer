Masdevallia dryada

Masdevallia dryada, commonly known as the dryad orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with intricate, often colorful flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant features a pseudobulb structure, which stores water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its humid, shaded habitat. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its unique appearance.

The orchid's delicate flowers often have a subtle fragrance, attracting specific pollinators such as bees and moths.
