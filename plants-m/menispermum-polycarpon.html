Menispermum polycarpon

Menispermum polycarpon, commonly known as the common arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and palmate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by bright orange berries. Its roots are sometimes used in traditional medicine, though they are toxic and should be handled with care.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
