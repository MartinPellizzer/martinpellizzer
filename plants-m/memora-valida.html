Memora valida

Memora valida, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has woody stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically green and glossy. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
