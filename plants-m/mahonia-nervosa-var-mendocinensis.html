Mahonia nervosa var. mendocinensis

Mahonia nervosa var. mendocinensis, commonly known as the Mendocino mahonia, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features compound leaves with a distinctive dark green color and yellowish veins, which give it a striking appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, followed by blue-black berries that are attractive to wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for native plant landscaping.
