Mappia racemosa var. typica

Mappia racemosa var. typica, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical with a smooth margin.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
