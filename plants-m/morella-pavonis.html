Morella pavonis

Morella pavonis, commonly known as the black spruce, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its dark reddish-brown bark and small, cone-like fruits that contain seeds. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in spirals along the branches. It thrives in wet, acidic soils and is often found in boreal and subarctic regions.

Morella pavonis plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
