Megaclinium oxypterum

Megaclinium oxypterum, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-hued leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its inflorescence consists of narrow, spike-like clusters of small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
