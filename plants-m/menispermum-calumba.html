Menispermum calumba

Menispermum calumba, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by distinctive, winged fruits. It contains various alkaloids, some of which have been studied for their medicinal properties.

Due to its toxic nature, it is often avoided in traditional herbal practices.
