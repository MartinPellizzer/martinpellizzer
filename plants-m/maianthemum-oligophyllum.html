Maianthemum oligophyllum

Maianthemum oligophyllum, commonly known as the slender false Solomon's seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in early to mid-summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a fan shape at the base. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and forest understories.

Maianthemum oligophyllum is typically found in rich, loamy soils and is often associated with other shade-tolerant flora.
