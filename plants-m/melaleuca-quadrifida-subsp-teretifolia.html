Melaleuca quadrifida subsp. teretifolia

Melaleuca quadrifida subsp.

teretifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf paperbark, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, terete leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery bloom.

The plant produces small, bottle-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color, which appear in clusters along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
