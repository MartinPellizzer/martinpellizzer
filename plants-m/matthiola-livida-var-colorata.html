Matthiola livida var. colorata

Matthiola livida var. colorata, commonly known as the Crimson Bells of the Balkans, is a striking perennial plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from deep red to violet, often with a contrasting white or pink throat. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from late spring through early autumn.
