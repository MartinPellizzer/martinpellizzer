Mammillaria perezdelarosae subsp. andersoniana

Mammillaria perezdelarosae subsp.

andersoniana, commonly known as the Anderson's nipple cactus, is a small, spineless cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico. It features a globular stem with prominent areoles and short, white spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
