Myristica tingens

Myristica tingens, commonly known as the red sandalwood or red myristica, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its dark red or purple bark and aromatic, oily seeds. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by bright red, fleshy fruits that contain the valuable seeds. Its essential oils and seed kernels are used in perfumery, medicine, and traditional remedies.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitats.
