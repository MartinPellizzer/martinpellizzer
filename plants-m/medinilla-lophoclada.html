Medinilla lophoclada

Medinilla lophoclada, commonly known as the velvet tangerine.

It is a tropical shrub native to the Philippines, characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are thick and leathery, often with a bluish-green hue, and the plant is well adapted to humid, warm climates.

Medinilla lophoclada is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
