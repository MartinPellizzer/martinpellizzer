Manihot pubescens

Manihot pubescens, commonly known as the rough manioc or wild cassava, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces a toxic, starchy root that requires processing to make it safe for consumption. It thrives in moist, tropical climates and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Its latex contains compounds that can be used in traditional medicine and industrial applications.
