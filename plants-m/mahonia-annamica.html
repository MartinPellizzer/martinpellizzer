Mahonia annamica

Mahonia annamica, commonly known as the Annam holly, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features spiny, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are followed by blue-black berries. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its hardy nature makes it suitable for USDA hardiness zones 6 to 9.
