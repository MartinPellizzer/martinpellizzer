Musa × protractorachis

Musa × protractorachis, commonly known as the giant banana plant, is a hybrid species within the Musaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant produces large, clustered inflorescences that bear edible berries, which are typically yellow when ripe. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its rapid growth and potential for hybridization, it is often cultivated for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
