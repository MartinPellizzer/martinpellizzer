Mentha × niliaca var. moniliformis

Mentha × niliaca var. moniliformis, commonly known as the honey mint, is a hybrid plant belonging to the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves that emit a sweet, honey-like scent. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb with square stems and opposite leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in temperate regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild flavor, making it popular in both gardens and culinary applications.
