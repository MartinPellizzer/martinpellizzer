Matthiola coronopifolia var. hispanica

Matthiola coronopifolia var. hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish stock, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other aromatic and ornamental plants. The plant features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often arranged in terminal clusters. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated for its pleasant scent.
