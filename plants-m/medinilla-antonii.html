Medinilla antonii

Medinilla antonii, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, waxy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn from green to red as they mature. Medinilla antonii prefers warm, humid climates and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements.
