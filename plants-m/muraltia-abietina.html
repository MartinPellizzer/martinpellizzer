Muraltia abietina

Muraltia abietina, commonly known as the Italian acacia, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, fragrant yellow flowers in spring. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Muraltia abietina is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.
