Mirabilis watsoniana

Mirabilis watsoniana, commonly known as the Watson's four-o'clock, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Nyctaginaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that open in the late afternoon. The plant produces vibrant pink to red blooms, which attract pollinators such as bees and moths. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
