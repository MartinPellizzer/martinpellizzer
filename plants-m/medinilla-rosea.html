Medinilla rosea

Medinilla rosea, commonly known as the velvet tangerine, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that resemble small roses. The plant produces small, round, orange-red fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade.

Medinilla rosea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.
