Magnolia virginiana var. longifolia

Magnolia virginiana var. longifolia, commonly known as the sweetbay magnolia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves appear. The plant has simple, alternate, elliptic to ovate leaves that are glossy green on top and lighter in color underneath. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The sweetbay magnolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil conditions.
