Mezia bahiana

Mezia bahiana, commonly known as the Brazilian meadow grass, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses and grains. The plant is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny florets.

Mezia bahiana is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
