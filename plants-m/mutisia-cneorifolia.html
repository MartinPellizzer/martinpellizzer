Mutisia cneorifolia

Mutisia cneorifolia, commonly known as the Andean mutisia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features palmate leaves with a rough, hairy texture and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. Its botanical significance lies in its adaptation to harsh, high-altitude environments.

Mutisia cneorifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local cultures.
