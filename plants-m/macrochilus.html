Macrochilus

Macrochilus, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plants typically grow in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments. They have a sympodial growth habit, with new shoots arising from the base of the previous growth.

The flowers are usually borne on lateral spikes and come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink.
