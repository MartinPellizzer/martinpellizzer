Mimosa paraguariae var. multiglandulosa

Mimosa paraguariae var. multiglandulosa, commonly known as the hairy mimosa, is a species native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are finely divided and covered in soft, hairy glands. The plant produces fluffy, yellow spherical flower heads that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid regions.

The plant is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
