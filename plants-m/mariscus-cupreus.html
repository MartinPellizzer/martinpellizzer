Mariscus cupreus

Mariscus cupreus, commonly known as copperleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually a deep green color, with a slight waxy coating. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of pink or red, which bloom in the spring.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and seasonal blooms.
