Malpighia watsonii

Malpighia watsonii, commonly known as the Watson's acorn berry, is a shrub native to the Caribbean islands, particularly Cuba and Hispaniola.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its small, edible berries that are often red or orange in color. The plant has simple, alternate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The berries are not only ornamental but also have some traditional uses in local medicine.
