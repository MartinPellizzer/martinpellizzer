Melochia chamaedrys var. chamaedrys

Melochia chamaedrys var. chamaedrys, commonly known as the creeping melochia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
