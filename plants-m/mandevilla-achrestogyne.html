Mandevilla achrestogyne

Mandevilla achrestogyne, commonly known as the golden mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a vigorous climber with large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces fleshy, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance.
