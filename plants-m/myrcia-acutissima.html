Myrcia acutissima

Myrcia acutissima, commonly known as the Brazilian peppercorn, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark red berries that are used in traditional medicine. The plant has glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, white flowers that are often overlooked.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and conservation efforts.
