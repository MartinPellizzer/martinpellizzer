Myroxylon ovatum

Myroxylon ovatum, commonly known as the copal tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated pods containing hard, woody seeds. Its sap is a valuable source of natural resin, which has been used for centuries in incense, varnishes, and traditional medicine.

The plant is also notable for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
