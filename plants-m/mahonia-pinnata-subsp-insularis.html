Mahonia pinnata subsp. insularis

Mahonia pinnata subsp.

insularis, commonly known as the island mahonia, is a shrub native to the Caribbean islands. It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with glossy, leathery leaves that are compound and pinnately arranged.

The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by blue-black berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical gardens.

Its horticultural value is enhanced by its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
