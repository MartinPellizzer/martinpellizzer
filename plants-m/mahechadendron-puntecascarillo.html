Mahechadendron puntecascarillo

Mahechadendron puntecascarillo, commonly known as the purple-leafed tamarind tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with striking purple leaves that change color with the seasons. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
