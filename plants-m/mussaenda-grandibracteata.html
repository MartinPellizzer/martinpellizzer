Mussaenda grandibracteata

Mussaenda grandibracteata, commonly known as the large bract mussaenda, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences that are often covered in soft, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are surrounded by large, colorful bracts, which are modified leaves. Its leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

Mussaenda grandibracteata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
