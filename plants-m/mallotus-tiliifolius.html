Mallotus tiliifolius

Mallotus tiliifolius, commonly known as the Indian almond tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and pinnate, with small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its ornamental foliage and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of timber.
