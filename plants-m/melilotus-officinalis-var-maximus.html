Melilotus officinalis var. maximus

Melilotus officinalis var. maximus, commonly known as sweet clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been widely naturalized in North America. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters. Sweet clover is known for its strong, sweet scent and is often used in agriculture as a forage crop.

It also has medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
