Mallotus subjaponicus

Mallotus subjaponicus, commonly known as the Japanese holly or Japanese tallowtree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually glossy and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers followed by orange-red berries that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
