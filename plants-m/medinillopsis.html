Medinillopsis

Medinillopsis, commonly known as the "Mexican orchid" or "flower of the sun," is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

The plants are characterized by their showy, often fragrant flowers, which come in a variety of colors including yellow, orange, and red. Medinillopsis species are epiphytic, meaning they grow on other plants without being parasitic, and they require high humidity and indirect sunlight to thrive.
