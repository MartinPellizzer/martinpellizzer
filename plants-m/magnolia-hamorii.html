Magnolia hamorii

Magnolia hamorii, commonly known as the Hamor's magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrant blossoms.
