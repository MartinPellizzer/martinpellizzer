Monolophus philippianus

Monolophus philippianus, commonly known as the Philippine mountain pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of the Philippines.

It is characterized by its slender, straight trunk and dense, needle-like foliage that forms a conical shape. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist climates and well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
