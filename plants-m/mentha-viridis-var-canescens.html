Mentha viridis var. canescens

Mentha viridis var. canescens, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Mentha viridis var. canescens is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.
