Micromeria × garajonayii

Micromeria × garajonayii, commonly known as the Canary Island cypress, is a hybrid shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in coastal and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, cone-like fruits.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
