Malaxis reineckeana

Malaxis reineckeana, commonly known as the Reinecke's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

This terrestrial orchid typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. It features a slender, erect stem with a single, showy flower that is usually white or pale pink in color. The plant has a unique inflorescence structure, with the flower emerging from a specialized bract.

Its botanical characteristics make it a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts and botanists alike.
