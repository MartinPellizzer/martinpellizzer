Mentha aquatica var. calaminthifolia

Mentha aquatica var. calaminthifolia, commonly known as water mint, is a variant of the water mint species native to moist, temperate regions.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, stream banks, and other aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats. Its leaves are often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for their cooling properties.

The plant's botanical features make it well adapted to waterlogged soils and cool climates.
