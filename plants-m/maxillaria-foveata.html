Maxillaria foveata

Maxillaria foveata, commonly known as the spotted maxillaria, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and pseudobulbs that bear lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with distinctive dark spots on the petals and sepals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Maxillaria foveata is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
