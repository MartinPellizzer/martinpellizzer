Mirbelia oxyclada

Mirbelia oxyclada, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive silvery foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a silky appearance.

Mirbelia oxyclada is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
