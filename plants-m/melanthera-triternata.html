Melanthera triternata

Melanthera triternata, commonly known as the three-parted bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its simple, branching stems and small, delicate leaves that are often submerged in water. The plant produces tiny, greenish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and ditches.

Melanthera triternata is an important part of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
