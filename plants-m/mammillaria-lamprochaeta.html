Mammillaria lamprochaeta

Mammillaria lamprochaeta, commonly known as the silver-spined mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is often cultivated as a decorative succulent.
