Miconia turgida

Miconia turgida, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Brazilian star, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. Miconia turgida is often found in humid, lowland forests and is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticultural research.
