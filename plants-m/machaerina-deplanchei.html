Machaerina deplanchei

Machaerina deplanchei, commonly known as the Desert Poppy, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow or orange petals and is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in coastal dunes and rocky habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing the soil.

Due to its ecological importance and unique appearance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
