Macrostylis cassiopoides

Macrostylis cassiopoides, commonly known as the Cassiopoides grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green hue, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
