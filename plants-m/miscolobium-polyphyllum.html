Miscolobium polyphyllum

Miscolobium polyphyllum, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist conditions. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a contrasting throat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Miscolobium polyphyllum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
