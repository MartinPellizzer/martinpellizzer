Mammillaria tacubayensis

Mammillaria tacubayensis, commonly known as the Tacubaya hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a ribbed stem and spines that are typically white or yellow. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
