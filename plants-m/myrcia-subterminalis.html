Myrcia subterminalis

Myrcia subterminalis, commonly known as the pink myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a glossy surface, while its fruits are small, woody capsules that release seeds when mature.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
