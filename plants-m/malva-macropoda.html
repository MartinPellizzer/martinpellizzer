Malva macropoda

Malva macropoda, commonly known as the large-flowered mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Malva macropoda is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
