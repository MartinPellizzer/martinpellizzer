Malacothamnus fasciculatus subsp. splendidus

Malacothamnus fasciculatus subsp.

splendidus, commonly known as the golden globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its compact, globe-shaped inflorescences that are typically bright yellow, with showy, tubular flowers.

The plant has hairy, silvery-green leaves that are often clustered at the base, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ornamental purposes and ecological habitats.
