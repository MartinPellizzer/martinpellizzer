Myrcia gigantifolia

Myrcia gigantifolia, commonly known as the giant myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible and contain numerous seeds. Myrcia gigantifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities.

It also has traditional uses in folk medicine.
