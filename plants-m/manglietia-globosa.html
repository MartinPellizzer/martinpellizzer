Manglietia globosa

Manglietia globosa, commonly known as the globe magnolia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has thick, leathery leaves that are dark green and glossy. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.

The tree is also notable for its slow growth and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
