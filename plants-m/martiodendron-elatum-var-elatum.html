Martiodendron elatum var. elatum

Martiodendron elatum var. elatum, commonly known as the goldenbush, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, golden-yellow foliage that remains vibrant throughout the growing season. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white and appear in late spring.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
