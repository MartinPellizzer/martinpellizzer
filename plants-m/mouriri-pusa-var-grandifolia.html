Mouriri pusa var. grandifolia

Mouriri pusa var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaf mouriri, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces elliptical, orange-red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
