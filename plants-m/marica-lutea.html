Marica lutea

Marica lutea, commonly known as the yellow marica, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy species in arid environments. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Marica lutea is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
