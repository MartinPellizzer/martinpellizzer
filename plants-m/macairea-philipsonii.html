Macairea philipsonii

Macairea philipsonii, commonly known as the Philippine orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
