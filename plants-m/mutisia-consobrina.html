Mutisia consobrina

Mutisia consobrina, commonly known as the "Cape mountain mutisia," is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Mutisia consobrina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in gardens.
