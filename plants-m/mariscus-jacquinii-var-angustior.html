Mariscus jacquinii var. angustior

Mariscus jacquinii var. angustior, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mariscus, is a rare and delicate plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, pale green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests or along stream banks. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered, highlighting the need for its protection.
