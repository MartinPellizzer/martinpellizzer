Mollinedia fruticulosa

Mollinedia fruticulosa, commonly known as the Chilean firebush, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in coastal and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Mollinedia fruticulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
