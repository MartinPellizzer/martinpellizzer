Moranopteris taenifolia

Moranopteris taenifolia, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a unique reproductive system, producing spores on the underside of its fronds rather than flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Moranopteris taenifolia plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and contributing to forest biodiversity.
