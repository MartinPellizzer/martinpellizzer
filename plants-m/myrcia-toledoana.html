Myrcia toledoana

Myrcia toledoana, commonly known as the Toledo myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. Myrcia toledoana produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not widely consumed by humans.

It plays an important role in the local ecosystem, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
