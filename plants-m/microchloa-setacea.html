Microchloa setacea

Microchloa setacea, commonly known as the hairy microchloa, is a small, perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Microchloa setacea is often found in shaded, humid environments such as forest understories.

Its compact size and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for small garden spaces and container planting.
