Miegia gigantea

Miegia gigantea, commonly known as the giant miegia, is a striking plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that can reach up to two meters in length, providing a lush canopy in its natural habitat. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom during the wet season. Its roots are fibrous and robust, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-rich, moist soils.

Miegia gigantea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
