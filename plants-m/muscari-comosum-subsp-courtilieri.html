Muscari comosum subsp. courtilieri

Muscari comosum subsp.

courtilieri, commonly known as the Persian bluebell, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, making it one of the earliest flowering bulbs in its native habitat. Its bulbs are adapted to well-drained soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

The subspecies is noted for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
