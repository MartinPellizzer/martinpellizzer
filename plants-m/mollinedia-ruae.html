Mollinedia ruae

Mollinedia ruae, commonly known as the river mollinedia, is a rare and specialized plant native to specific riverine habitats in South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are adapted to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils along riverbanks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which are pollinated by insects. Due to habitat destruction, Mollinedia ruae is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
