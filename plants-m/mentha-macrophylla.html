Mentha macrophylla

Mentha macrophylla, commonly known as peppermint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its dark green, slightly wrinkled leaves and strong, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Peppermint is a member of the Lamiaceae family, known for its square stems and opposite leaves.

It is widely cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy, medicine, and flavoring.
