Monochaetum villosum

Monochaetum villosum, commonly known as the woolly spike rush, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance. The plant features wiry, hairy stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. Its flowering spikes are typically borne at the base of the plant, and the flowers are small and inconspicuous.

Monochaetum villosum is often found in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along stream banks.
