Merremia heringeri

Merremia heringeri, commonly known as the Heringer's morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Its rapid growth and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in suitable climates.
