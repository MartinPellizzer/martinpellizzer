Malva trionoides

Malva trionoides, commonly known as the yellow mallow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Malva trionoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
