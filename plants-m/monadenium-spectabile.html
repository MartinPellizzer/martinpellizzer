Monadenium spectabile

Monadenium spectabile, commonly known as the showy monadenium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are arranged in clusters and are pollinated primarily by insects.

Monadenium spectabile is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
