Mammillaria radiosa f. arizonica

Mammillaria radiosa f. arizonica, commonly known as the Arizona hedgehog cactus, is a compact, spineless cactus native to the Sonoran Desert.

It features a globular stem with a dense covering of white, woolly hairs that help reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are typically short-lived but attract pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
