Mespilus coccinea subvar. indentata

Mespilus coccinea subvar. indentata, commonly known as the red medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, ribbed, red-orange fruits that ripen in late autumn. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have serrated margins. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is notable for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
