Melaleuca pachystachya

Melaleuca pachystachya, commonly known as the broad-stalked tea tree, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, fluffy white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy habitats.

Melaleuca pachystachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in indigenous cultures.
