Moraea versicolor

Moraea versicolor, commonly known as the rainbow moraea, is a striking perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its showy, colorful flowers that range from pink to purple and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, often forming clusters at the top of the stems.

Moraea versicolor is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant display.
