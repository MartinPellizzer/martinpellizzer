Mammillaria gasseriana

Mammillaria gasseriana, commonly known as the Gasser's hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a covering of white, woolly spines. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to arid environments. It produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months.

Mammillaria gasseriana is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
