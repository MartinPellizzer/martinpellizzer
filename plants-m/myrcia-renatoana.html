Myrcia renatoana

Myrcia renatoana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white or pink flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruits are small, red, and berry-like, often containing several seeds.

Myrcia renatoana is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
