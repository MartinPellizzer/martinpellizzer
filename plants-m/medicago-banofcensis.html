Medicago banofcensis

Medicago banofcensis, commonly known as Banofe's clover, is a flowering plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, exhibiting a preference for temperate climates. Medicago banofcensis is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria, contributing to soil fertility.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and agricultural research.
