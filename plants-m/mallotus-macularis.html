Mallotus macularis

Mallotus macularis, commonly known as the spotted mallot, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
