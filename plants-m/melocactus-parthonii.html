Melocactus parthonii

Melocactus parthonii, commonly known as the Parthon's melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are typically green or yellowish in color. The plant produces a single, large, fleshy fruit that resembles a melon, which is edible and has a sweet, mild flavor. Melocactus parthonii is adapted to survive in extreme drought conditions, storing water in its stem tissue.

It is also noted for its slow growth rate and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.
