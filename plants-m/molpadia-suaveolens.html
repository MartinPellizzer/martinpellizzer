Molpadia suaveolens

Molpadia suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented molpadia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its pleasant aroma.
