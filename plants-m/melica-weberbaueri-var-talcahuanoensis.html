Melica weberbaueri var. talcahuanoensis

Melica weberbaueri var. talcahuanoensis, commonly known as the Talcahuano meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly Chile.

It typically grows in moist, temperate regions and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in meadows, riverbanks, and other wetland habitats.

Botanically, it is noted for its fine texture and adaptability to varying soil conditions.
