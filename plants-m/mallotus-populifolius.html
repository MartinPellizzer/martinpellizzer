Mallotus populifolius

Mallotus populifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf mallot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release seeds.

Mallotus populifolius is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
