Matrella

Matrella, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that are often covered in a silvery or grayish bloom, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, round fruits resembling small apples. Matrella thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
