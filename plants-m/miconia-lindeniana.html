Miconia lindeniana

Miconia lindeniana, commonly known as the broadleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange to red fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in humid, lowland forests and thrives in areas with high rainfall.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in certain regions.
