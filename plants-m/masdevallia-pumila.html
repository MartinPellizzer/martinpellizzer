Masdevallia pumila

Masdevallia pumila, commonly known as the dwarf masdevallia, is a miniature orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact size, typically growing to less than 5 centimeters in height. The plant features a short, stout rhizome and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its leaves are usually fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to its high-altitude, cool climate.

Masdevallia pumila is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
