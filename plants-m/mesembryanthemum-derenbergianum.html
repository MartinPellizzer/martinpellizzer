Mesembryanthemum derenbergianum

Mesembryanthemum derenbergianum, commonly known as the Cape dandelion, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, often hairy leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems. It blooms during the winter months, producing small, dandelion-like flowers that attract pollinators.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
