Mammillaria bambusiphila

Mammillaria bambusiphila, commonly known as the bamboo milk barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a dense covering of white, woolly bristles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, and its stems are typically ribbed and spiny. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a potted plant.
