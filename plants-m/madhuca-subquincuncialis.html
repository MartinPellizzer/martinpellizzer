Madhuca subquincuncialis

Madhuca subquincuncialis, commonly known as the slender tallow tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by spherical fruits containing a single seed.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
