Mentha crispata var. lacerata

Mentha crispata var. lacerata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf marsh mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in swampy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Mentha crispata var. lacerata is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
