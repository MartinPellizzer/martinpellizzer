Myroxylon punctatum

Myroxylon punctatum, commonly known as the copal tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and can grow up to 30 meters in height. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by woody, resinous fruits. Its bark and sap are valued for their aromatic properties and have been used in traditional medicine.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
