Machilus kwangtungensis var. sanduensis

Machilus kwangtungensis var. sanduensis, commonly known as the Guangdong sanduensis mahonia, is a variant of the Machilus genus native to southern China.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The foliage is typically dark green and glossy, with elliptical leaves that have a slightly serrated margin. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

This plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
