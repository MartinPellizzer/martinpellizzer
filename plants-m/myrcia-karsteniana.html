Myrcia karsteniana

Myrcia karsteniana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant features elliptical, glossy leaves with a fragrant, resinous scent when crushed. Its small, white flowers are clustered in inflorescences and are followed by edible, red, fleshy berries.

Myrcia karsteniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
