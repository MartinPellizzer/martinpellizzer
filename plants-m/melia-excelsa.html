Melia excelsa

Melia excelsa, commonly known as the sweet gum or Philippine mahogany, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and broad, lobed leaves that are often shed seasonally. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by distinctive, spiny fruit capsules containing seeds. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it is valued for its hard, durable wood.

Melia excelsa also has medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
