Mentha × gracilis subsp. veronensis

Mentha × gracilis subsp.

veronensis, commonly known as the Veronese mint, is a hybrid species within the mint family, Lamiaceae. It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and fragrant, aromatic leaves.

The plant typically produces small, tubular flowers that are usually purple or blue in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and mild mint flavor, making it popular in gardens and herbal teas.
