Macphersonia pteridophylla

Macphersonia pteridophylla, commonly known as the Chinese water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are typically divided into small, lance-shaped segments. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and wetlands. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are borne on the underside of the fronds.

Macphersonia pteridophylla plays an important ecological role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to aquatic life.
