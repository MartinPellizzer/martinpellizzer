Myrceugenia sellowiana

Myrceugenia sellowiana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are opposite in arrangement and emit a strong aromatic scent when crushed. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in spring.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, attracting various bird species.
