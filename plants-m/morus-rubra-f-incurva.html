Morus rubra f. incurva

Morus rubra f. incurva, commonly known as the red mulberry, is a cultivar of the mulberry tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red leaves that turn a deep red or maroon color in the fall. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as mulberries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often grown for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

This variety is also valued for its adaptability to different climates and its role in supporting local wildlife.
