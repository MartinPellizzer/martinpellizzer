Myristica vouri

Myristica vouri, commonly known as the Vouri nut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its dense, glossy foliage and fragrant flowers. The plant produces a unique seed with an aromatic, oily kernel that is used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its botanical features include a slender trunk, compound leaves, and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release the seeds when mature.
