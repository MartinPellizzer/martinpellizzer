Medinilla rubrovenia

Medinilla rubrovenia, commonly known as the red-veined medinilla, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves with prominent red veins. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that are often surrounded by velvety, red bracts. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

Medinilla rubrovenia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
