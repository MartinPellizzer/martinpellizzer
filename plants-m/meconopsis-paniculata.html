Meconopsis paniculata

Meconopsis paniculata, commonly known as the Himalayan blue poppy, is a striking alpine plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its vivid blue to purple flowers, which bloom in early summer and are often surrounded by velvety, glandular hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Meconopsis paniculata is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
