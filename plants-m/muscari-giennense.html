Muscari giennense

Muscari giennense, commonly known as the Giennese squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant produces a narrow, grass-like foliage that emerges in early spring. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in dry, rocky soils, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Muscari giennense is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
