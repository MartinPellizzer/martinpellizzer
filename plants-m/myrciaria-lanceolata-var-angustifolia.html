Myrciaria lanceolata var. angustifolia

Myrciaria lanceolata var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf myrtle, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, orange-colored berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal environments with high salinity.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local communities.
