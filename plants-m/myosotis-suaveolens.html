Myosotis suaveolens

Myosotis suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented forget-me-not, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a faintly fragrant scent. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Myosotis suaveolens is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
