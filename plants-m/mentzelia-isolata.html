Mentzelia isolata

Mentzelia isolata, commonly known as the desert bluebells, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky soils. It has adapted to survive in harsh conditions with its deep root system and reduced leaf surface.

Mentzelia isolata plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
