Malva vitifolia

Malva vitifolia, commonly known as the vitifoliate mallows, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Malva vitifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
