Melampodium longipilum

Melampodium longipilum, commonly known as the slenderleaf goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open plains and rocky areas.

Melampodium longipilum is an important plant for pollinators and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
