Mycetia macrocarpa

Mycetia macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited mycetia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Leguminosae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, India, and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important component of soil enrichment in its native habitats.
