Mundulea lucens

Mundulea lucens, commonly known as the shining mundulea, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seeds are encased in hard pods that open to release the seeds when mature.

Mundulea lucens plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is often found in disturbed or degraded habitats.
