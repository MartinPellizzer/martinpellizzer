Milium vernale proles scabrum

Milium vernale proles scabrum, commonly known as the rough-stemmed millet, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into three-angled, dry seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields or along roadsides.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
