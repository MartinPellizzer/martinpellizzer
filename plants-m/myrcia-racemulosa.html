Myrcia racemulosa

Myrcia racemulosa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered an invasive species in some regions.
