Melilotus italicus var. confertus

Melilotus italicus var. confertus, commonly known as sweet clover, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in terminal clusters, and its trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment and as a forage crop. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in agricultural settings for its ecological and economic benefits.

Additionally, it has been historically used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
