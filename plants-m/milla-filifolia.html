Milla filifolia

Milla filifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, gray-green foliage and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces long, flat pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to harsh climates.

Milla filifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide shade in dry landscapes.
