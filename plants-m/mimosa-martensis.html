Mimosa martensis

Mimosa martensis, commonly known as the pink mimosa, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and riverbanks. Its flowers are often clustered in spherical heads, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Mimosa martensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
