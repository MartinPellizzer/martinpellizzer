Mentha sylvatica

Mentha sylvatica, commonly known as wood mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its fragrant, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are often used in herbal teas and have a mild minty aroma.
