Micranthes parvula

Micranthes parvula, commonly known as the smallflower pellionia, is a small herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, and its leaves are ovate with a smooth texture. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Micranthes parvula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
