Mezobromelia hutchisonii

Mezobromelia hutchisonii, commonly known as the Hutchison's bromeliad, is a species of flowering plant in the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central pool to collect rainwater, which serves as a microhabitat for various organisms. Its inflorescence consists of vibrant, colorful flowers that attract pollinators such as birds and insects.

Mezobromelia hutchisonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
