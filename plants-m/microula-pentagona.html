Microula pentagona

Microula pentagona, commonly known as the five-angled sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its small, fleshy, spiny leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. Its unique pentagonal shape of the fruit is a distinguishing feature.
