Matudina

Matudina, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Bignoniaceae and is characterized by its showy, pendulous yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy texture and a smooth, grayish bark. Matudina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
