Montiopsis trifida

Montiopsis trifida, commonly known as the three-lobed montiopsis, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the family Montiaceae and is characterized by its small, succulent leaves and delicate, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often lobed, giving it the common name, and it produces small, inconspicuous fruits.

Montiopsis trifida is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
