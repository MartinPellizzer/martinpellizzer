Musa livingstoniana

Musa livingstoniana, commonly known as the Livingston's banana, is a species of flowering plant in the Musaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and clustered, edible fruits. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are usually yellow or green and are followed by the development of banana-like berries.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has limited use in food production compared to other banana varieties.
