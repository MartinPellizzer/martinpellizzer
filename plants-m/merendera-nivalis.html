Merendera nivalis

Merendera nivalis, commonly known as the snow merendera, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are typically narrow and hairy, adapted to withstand cold temperatures and strong winds.

Merendera nivalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
