Myagrum bursifolium

Myagrum bursifolium, commonly known as the burrleaf myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of northern Australia, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a rough, leathery texture and its clusters of white or pale pink flowers. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several small, hard seeds.

Myagrum bursifolium is often used in traditional Aboriginal medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
