Mistyllus spumosus

Mistyllus spumosus, commonly known as the foamy mistflower, is a unique member of the Ericaceae family, characterized by its distinctive, foamy appearance on its stems.

It is native to the temperate regions of the southern United States, where it thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. The plant features small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of white or pale pink. Its foliage is typically dense and evergreen, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Mistyllus spumosus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
