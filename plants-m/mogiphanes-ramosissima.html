Mogiphanes ramosissima

Mogiphanes ramosissima, commonly known as the African mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees in sub-Saharan Africa.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in opposite pairs along its stems. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries, which are typically red or orange, are a food source for birds and other animals.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can weaken its host tree if left unchecked.
