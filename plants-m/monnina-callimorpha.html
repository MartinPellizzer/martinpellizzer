Monnina callimorpha

Monnina callimorpha, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking blue-violet flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
