Machaeranthera viscosa

Machaeranthera viscosa, commonly known as stickyleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a sticky texture. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and stream banks.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
