Melastoma maximilianum

Melastoma maximilianum, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks.
