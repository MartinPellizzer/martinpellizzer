Momordica trifoliolata

Momordica trifoliolata, commonly known as the three-leaf bitter gourd, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and squash. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets, and its distinctive, spiny fruits. The fruits are typically green when unripe and turn orange when mature, containing numerous seeds.

It is valued for its edible fruits, which are used in traditional cuisines and have medicinal properties.
