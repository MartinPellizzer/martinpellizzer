Mikania bolivarensis

Mikania bolivarensis, commonly known as Bolivian spiderflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine pubescence.

Mikania bolivarensis is often found in humid, tropical environments and can be invasive in certain regions.
