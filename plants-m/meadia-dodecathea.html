Meadia dodecathea

Meadia dodecathea, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its segmented, leaf-like stems that are often brightly colored. It produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically during the holiday season.

Meadia dodecathea prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular indoor plant.
