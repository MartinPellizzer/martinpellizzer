Magnolia fordiana var. hainanensis

Magnolia fordiana var. hainanensis, commonly known as the Hainan magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to southern China.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, dark green upper surface. It thrives in humid, subtropical climates and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
