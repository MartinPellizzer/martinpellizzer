Mentha rudaeana

Mentha rudaeana, commonly known as rough mint, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, giving it a minty scent and making it useful in traditional herbal medicine.

Mentha rudaeana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.
