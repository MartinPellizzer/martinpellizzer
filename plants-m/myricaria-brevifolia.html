Myricaria brevifolia

Myricaria brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved myricacia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in steppe and desert habitats.

Myricaria brevifolia plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small animals and insects.
