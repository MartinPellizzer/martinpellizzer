Miconia barkeri

Miconia barkeri, commonly known as the Brazilian star apple, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. The fruits are edible and have a sweet, juicy pulp with a mild flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of fruit in warmer climates.
