Myrceugenia franciscensis

Myrceugenia franciscensis, commonly known as the Francisco's myrtle, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the temperate rainforests of southern Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. It produces small, edible berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.

Due to habitat loss, this species is considered vulnerable and is often found in conservation areas.
