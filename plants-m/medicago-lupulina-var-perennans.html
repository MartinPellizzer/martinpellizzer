Medicago lupulina var. perennans

Medicago lupulina var. perennans, commonly known as the perennial sainfoin, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a deep root system, allowing it to thrive in dry, well-drained soils. It is often used in agricultural settings for forage and soil nitrogen fixation.

Its varietal form, perennans, distinguishes it from the annual varieties of Medicago lupulina.
