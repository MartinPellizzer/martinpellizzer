Modiolastrum lateritium

Modiolastrum lateritium, commonly known as the rock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its root system is deep and fibrous, enabling it to access water from lower soil layers.

Modiolastrum lateritium plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitats.
