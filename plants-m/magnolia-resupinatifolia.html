Magnolia resupinatifolia

Magnolia resupinatifolia, commonly known as the hanging magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its pendulous, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release its seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
