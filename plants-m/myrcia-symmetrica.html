Myrcia symmetrica

Myrcia symmetrica, commonly known as the symmetrical myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Myrcia symmetrica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, moist garden environments.
