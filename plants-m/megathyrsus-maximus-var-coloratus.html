Megathyrsus maximus var. coloratus

Megathyrsus maximus var. coloratus, commonly known as the red-veined grass or red-veined bamboo, is a striking ornamental grass native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and feathery, light green leaves with distinctive red veins that add a vibrant visual contrast. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is prized for its aesthetic value in landscaping and its ability to add texture and color to gardens.
