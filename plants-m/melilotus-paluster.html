Melilotus paluster

Melilotus paluster, commonly known as lesser melilot, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has slender, branching stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment.
