Malva × adulterina

Malva × adulterina, commonly known as the common mallows, is a hybrid species within the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and gardens.

Its fibrous stems and capsule fruits contribute to its adaptability and spread in various habitats.
