Miconia nianga

Miconia nianga, commonly known as the giant miconia, is a striking tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in diameter, making it one of the largest leaf plants in the world. The plant produces clusters of small, white, waxy flowers that are highly attractive to pollinators like bees and birds. Its rapid growth rate and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments contribute to its invasive potential in certain regions.

Botanically, Miconia nianga belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is known for its unique, showy floral structures.
