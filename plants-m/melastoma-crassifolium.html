Melastoma crassifolium

Melastoma crassifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf melastoma, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that are typically arranged opposite on the stems. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a red or purple calyx. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Melastoma crassifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
