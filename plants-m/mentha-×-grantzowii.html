Mentha × grantzowii

Mentha × grantzowii, commonly known as hybrid mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Mentha × piperita (peppermint) and Mentha × cunninghamii (apple mint). The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and fragrant, often aromatic, foliage. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Mentha × grantzowii is widely cultivated for its aromatic properties and used in culinary, medicinal, and ornamental applications.
