Mentha sylvestris var. pubescens

Mentha sylvestris var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy wood mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or violet, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its characteristic minty scent and medicinal properties.
