Malcolmia erosa

Malcolmia erosa, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils.

The species is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
