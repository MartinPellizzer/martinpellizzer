Myrtus brachyphylla

Myrtus brachyphylla, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and aromatic, with a dense, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by dark, edible berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Myrtus brachyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional herbal remedies.
