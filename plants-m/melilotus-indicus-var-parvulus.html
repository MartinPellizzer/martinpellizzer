Melilotus indicus var. parvulus

Melilotus indicus var. parvulus, commonly known as small-flowered melilot, is a herbaceous annual plant native to southern Asia.

It features slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval to lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, making it ecologically significant in its native habitat.
