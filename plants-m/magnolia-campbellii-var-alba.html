Magnolia campbellii var. alba

Magnolia campbellii var. alba, commonly known as the white magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The tree has smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual appeal.

This variety is a rare and elegant form of the broader Magnolia campbellii species.
