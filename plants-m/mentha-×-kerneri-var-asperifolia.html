Mentha × kerneri var. asperifolia

Mentha × kerneri var. asperifolia, commonly known as the roughleaf mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and strong aromatic scent, typical of the mint genus. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue or purple, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in herbal remedies.
