Mentha × dumetorum var. ulophylla

Mentha × dumetorum var. ulophylla, commonly known as "hairy mint," is a hybrid plant belonging to the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its hairy, often grayish-green leaves and a strong, pungent aroma. This variety is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in moist, shady environments. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is often used in herbal remedies and as a decorative plant in gardens due to its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
