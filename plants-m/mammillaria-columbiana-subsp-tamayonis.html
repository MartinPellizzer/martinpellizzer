Mammillaria columbiana subsp. tamayonis

Mammillaria columbiana subsp.

tamayonis, commonly known as the Tamayo's mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico. It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is covered in white to pale green tubercles.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
