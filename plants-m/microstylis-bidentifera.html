Microstylis bidentifera

Microstylis bidentifera, commonly known as the two-toothed grass, is a small, herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a distinctive two-toothed appearance at the base of the leaf blades. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wet meadows.

Microstylis bidentifera is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
