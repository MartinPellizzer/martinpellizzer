Malaxis verruculosa

Malaxis verruculosa, commonly known as the疣唇兰, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, often on the forest floor or in rocky crevices. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, with a distinctive, warty appearance on the lip of each flower. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil.

Malaxis verruculosa is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
