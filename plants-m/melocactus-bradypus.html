Melocactus bradypus

Melocactus bradypus, commonly known as the slow-growing melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem, which is typically covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces striking yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by bright red fruits. Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.

Melocactus bradypus is adapted to survive in extreme desert conditions with minimal water and high sunlight exposure.
