Myrceugenia pilotantha var. nothorufa

Myrceugenia pilotantha var. nothorufa, commonly known as the red-flowered myrtle, is a variant of the myrtle family plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, clustered red flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its botanical features include aromatic foliage and a compact growth habit.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
