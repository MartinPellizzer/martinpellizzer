Milium muticum

Milium muticum, commonly known as the common millet, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a small annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils and open areas. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in temperate regions across Europe and parts of Asia.

Milium muticum is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is sometimes used as a cover crop or for erosion control.
