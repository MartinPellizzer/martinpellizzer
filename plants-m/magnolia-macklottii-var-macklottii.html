Magnolia macklottii var. macklottii

Magnolia macklottii var. macklottii, commonly known as the Macklott's magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves and its showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The flowers are typically borne on short, hairy pedicels and are clustered in racemes. The tree has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 20 feet.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
