Mentha rosanii

Mentha rosanii, commonly known as the pink mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often a striking pinkish or reddish hue. The plant produces small, pale purple to pink flowers in whorls along the stem, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Mentha rosanii is valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
