Medicago rotata subsp. blancheana

Medicago rotata subsp.

blancheana, commonly known as Blanche's tickvetch, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers and trifoliate leaves.

The subspecies is distinguished by its pale, almost white flower color and its compact growth habit. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

This plant plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.
