Mutisia linearifolia var. hookeri

Mutisia linearifolia var. hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's mutisia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows as a shrub with linear, slightly hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage and floral display.
