Morina nepalensis subsp. nepalensis

Morina nepalensis subsp.

nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal moringa, is a shrub native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its small, white to yellowish flowers and pinnate leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has a compact growth habit with multiple branches and is often found at high altitudes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
