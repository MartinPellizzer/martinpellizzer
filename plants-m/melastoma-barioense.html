Melastoma barioense

Melastoma barioense, commonly known as the Bario Melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Bario region of Sarawak, Malaysia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded undergrowth of tropical forests, preferring well-drained soil. Its leaves are usually opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Melastoma barioense is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
