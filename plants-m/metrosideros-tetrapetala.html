Metrosideros tetrapetala

Metrosideros tetrapetala, commonly known as the Four-petaled Christmas Bush, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in coastal and lowland forest areas. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers that are clustered in dense, showy inflorescences. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a leathery texture, while its bark is smooth and reddish-brown.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
