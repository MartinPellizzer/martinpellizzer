Matucana roseiflora

Matucana roseiflora, commonly known as the rose-flowered matucana, is a species of cactus native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines and clusters of bright pink or red flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in arid gardens and collections.
