Muscari dolioliforme

Muscari dolioliforme, commonly known as the Persian grape hyacinth, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to violet in color, resembling tiny grapes. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge from a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Muscari dolioliforme is valued for its early spring blooms and its ability to naturalize over time.
