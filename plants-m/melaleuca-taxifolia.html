Melaleuca taxifolia

Melaleuca taxifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paperbark, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that appear in dense clusters. The plant produces a fragrant, honey-scented nectar that attracts various pollinators, including bees and birds. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often growing in wetland or swampy areas.

The bark of Melaleuca taxifolia is fibrous and peels off in thin layers, giving it its common name.
