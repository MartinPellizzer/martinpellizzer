Melia azedarach var. cochinchinensis

Melia azedarach var. cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Chinese chinaberry tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and often have a distinctive, slightly serrated appearance. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to orange-red berries containing seeds. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to various regions for its rapid growth and adaptability.

However, it can become invasive in some areas, outcompeting native plant species.
