Meridiana subbipinnata

Meridiana subbipinnata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and palmate leaves that are deeply divided into narrow, linear lobes. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value and adaptability have made it a popular choice among horticulturists.
