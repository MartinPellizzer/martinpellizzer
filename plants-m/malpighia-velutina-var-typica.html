Malpighia velutina var. typica

Malpighia velutina var. typica, commonly known as the velvet passionfruit, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It produces small, hairy, orange to red fruits that are typically harvested when fully mature. The plant has palmate leaves with a velvety texture, which gives it its common name. Its flowers are usually yellow with red sepals and are pollinated by insects.

The fruit is valued for its unique flavor and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
