Malva aegyptia subsp. iljinii

Malva aegyptia subsp.

iljinii, commonly known as the Iljin's mallows, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton.

This subspecies is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
