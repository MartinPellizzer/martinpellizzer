Mesua clemensiorum

Mesua clemensiorum, commonly known as the Indian rose or red sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its ornamental, fragrant white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The tree has simple, alternate leaves and produces small, reddish-brown fruits. Its wood is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and incense.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
