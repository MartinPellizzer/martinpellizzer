Micromeles alnifolia

Micromeles alnifolia, commonly known as the alderleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

Micromeles alnifolia is adapted to well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry environments.
