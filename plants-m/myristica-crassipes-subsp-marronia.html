Myristica crassipes subsp. marronia

Myristica crassipes subsp.

marronia, commonly known as the binturong tree, is a member of the Myristicaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia. It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and pinnate leaves.

The plant produces fragrant, yellowish flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its botanical significance lies in its unique chemical composition, including compounds used in traditional medicine and perfumery.

The subspecies marronia is distinguished by its specific morphological traits and habitat preferences.
