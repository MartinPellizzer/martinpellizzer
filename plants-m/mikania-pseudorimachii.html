Mikania pseudorimachii

Mikania pseudorimachii, commonly known as the creeping Mikania, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to parts of Central and South America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and sprawling habit, often forming dense mats on the ground. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters.

Mikania pseudorimachii is also known for its ability to spread quickly, making it both a useful ground cover and a potential weed in certain environments.
