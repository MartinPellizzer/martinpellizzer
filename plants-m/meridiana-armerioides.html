Meridiana armerioides

Meridiana armerioides, commonly known as the silverleaf armeria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and low-maintenance addition to Mediterranean-style landscapes.
