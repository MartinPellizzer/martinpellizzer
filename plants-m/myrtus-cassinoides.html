Myrtus cassinoides

Myrtus cassinoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Myrtus cassinoides is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
