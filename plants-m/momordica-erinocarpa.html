Momordica erinocarpa

Momordica erinocarpa, commonly known as the horned melon or African horned cucumber, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, green fruit that resembles a horned melon. The plant produces yellow flowers and has a trailing growth habit with palmate leaves. The fruit is edible when ripe, with a thick, leathery rind and a sweet, sometimes tangy interior.

It is often cultivated for its unique appearance and culinary uses.
