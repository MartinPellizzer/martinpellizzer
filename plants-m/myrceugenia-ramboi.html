Myrceugenia ramboi

Myrceugenia ramboi, commonly known as the Rambo's myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to the cloud forests of southern Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

Myrceugenia ramboi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its potential ecological and medicinal properties.
