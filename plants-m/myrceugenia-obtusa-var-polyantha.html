Myrceugenia obtusa var. polyantha

Myrceugenia obtusa var. polyantha, commonly known as the white-flowered myrtle, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, edible berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic foliage.
