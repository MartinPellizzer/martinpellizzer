Minuartia hirsuta subsp. frutescens

Minuartia hirsuta subsp.

frutescens, commonly known as the hairy rock-cress, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions. It features small, lanceolate leaves that are covered in fine, glandular hairs, giving it a hairy appearance.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers with four petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

This subspecies is often found in tundra and mountainous environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.
