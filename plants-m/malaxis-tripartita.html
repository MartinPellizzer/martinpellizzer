Malaxis tripartita

Malaxis tripartita, commonly known as the three-parted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant features a slender, wiry stem with a few leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a spike. Its flowers have three sepals and three petals, with the lateral petals often resembling a lip.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
