Malaxis unifolia f. variegata

Malaxis unifolia f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated hooded orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, hood-like floral structure and striking variegated leaf patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and emit a faint fragrance.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is highly valued in botanical collections and conservation efforts.
