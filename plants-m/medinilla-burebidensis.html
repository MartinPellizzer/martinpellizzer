Medinilla burebidensis

Medinilla burebidensis, commonly known as the Burebidensis Medinilla, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, glossy foliage and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically elliptical, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface.

Medinilla burebidensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to shaded environments.
