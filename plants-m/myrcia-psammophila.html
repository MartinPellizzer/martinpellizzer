Myrcia psammophila

Myrcia psammophila, commonly known as the sandplain myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes and dry forests.

Myrcia psammophila is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
