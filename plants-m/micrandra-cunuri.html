Micrandra cunuri

Micrandra cunuri, commonly known as the Brazilian velvet tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant features a short trunk with a dense crown and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a woody pod containing several seeds, and it is adapted to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local communities.
