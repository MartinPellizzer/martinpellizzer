Melanthera hastata subsp. cubensis

Melanthera hastata subsp.

cubensis, commonly known as Cuban hastate melanthera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Cuba. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, lanceolate leaves and showy yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands or grasslands. It is notable for its distinctive leaf shape and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
