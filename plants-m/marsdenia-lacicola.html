Marsdenia lacicola

Marsdenia lacicola, commonly known as the Philippine star vine, is a climbing plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by follicles containing seeds with silky hairs. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
