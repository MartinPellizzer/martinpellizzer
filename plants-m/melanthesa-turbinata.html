Melanthesa turbinata

Melanthesa turbinata, commonly known as the turbinated melanthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, turbinated (spiral-shaped) flower heads that resemble small cones. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, with a rough texture.
