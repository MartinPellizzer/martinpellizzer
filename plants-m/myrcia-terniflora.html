Myrcia terniflora

Myrcia terniflora, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its fragrant, small white or pink flowers. The plant has elliptical, glossy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Myrcia terniflora thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its essential oils are valued for their aromatic and potential medicinal properties.
