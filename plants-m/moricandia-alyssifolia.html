Moricandia alyssifolia

Moricandia alyssifolia, commonly known as "lamb's-quarters," is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features alternate, ovate leaves with a hairy surface and a distinctive yellowish-green coloration. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by spherical, three-lobed seed pods. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Moricandia alyssifolia is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread easily through wind-dispersed seeds.
