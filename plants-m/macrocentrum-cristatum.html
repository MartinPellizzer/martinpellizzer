Macrocentrum cristatum

Macrocentrum cristatum, commonly known as the Crowned Orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, crown-like inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with intricate patterns. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in botanical gardens.
