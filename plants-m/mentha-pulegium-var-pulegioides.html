Mentha pulegium var. pulegioides

Mentha pulegium var. pulegioides, commonly known as narrow-leaved pennyroyal, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces tiny, pale blue or white flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. It is known for its strong, aromatic scent and is often found in moist, shaded areas.

Due to its medicinal and pesticidal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
