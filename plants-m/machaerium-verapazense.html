Machaerium verapazense

Machaerium verapazense, commonly known as the Verapaz grass, is a perennial shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Machaerium verapazense is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
