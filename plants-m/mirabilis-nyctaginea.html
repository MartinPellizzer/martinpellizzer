Mirabilis nyctaginea

Mirabilis nyctaginea, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that open at night and emit a sweet fragrance. The plant is a succulent, with thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. Its leaves are typically narrow and grayish-green, reducing water loss through transpiration.

The flowers, which range in color from white to pink, attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths and bats.
