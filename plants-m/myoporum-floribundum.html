Myoporum floribundum

Myoporum floribundum, commonly known as the coastal myoporum, is a shrub native to coastal regions of southern Australia.

It features aromatic, evergreen leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a bluish or grayish hue. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for coastal and arid environments.

Myoporum floribundum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize sandy soils.
