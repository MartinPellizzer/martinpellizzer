Mesembryanthemum variifolium

Mesembryanthemum variifolium, commonly known as the variable leaf ice plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, often blue-green leaves that are adapted to store water, making it well-suited for arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, and is often used in rock gardens or as a decorative ground cover.

Its unique foliage and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
