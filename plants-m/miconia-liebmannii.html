Miconia liebmannii

Miconia liebmannii, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a member of the Melastomataceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. Its flowers are typically showy, with five petals and bright colors such as red or pink.

Miconia liebmannii is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some areas.
