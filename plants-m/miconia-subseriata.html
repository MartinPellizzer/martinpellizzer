Miconia subseriata

Miconia subseriata, commonly known as the silverleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery or whitish pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom throughout the year, followed by red, fleshy berries. Miconia subseriata is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable subject in both ecological and horticultural studies.
