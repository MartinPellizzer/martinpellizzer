Myrcia grazielae

Myrcia grazielae, commonly known as the Graceful Myrcia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces small, red, berry-like fruits that are edible but not widely consumed by humans.

Myrcia grazielae is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
