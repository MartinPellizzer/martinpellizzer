Muraltia pappeana

Muraltia pappeana, commonly known as the African coffee tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers followed by bean-like pods containing edible seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Muraltia pappeana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
