Malortiea gracilis

Malortiea gracilis, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet, which bloom in late summer. Its flowering stems are often hairy, and the plant prefers well-drained, rocky soils.

Malortiea gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
