Mimulus luteus var. youngeanus

Mimulus luteus var. youngeanus, commonly known as the Yellow Monkeyflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as stream banks and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
