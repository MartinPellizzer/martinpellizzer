Melia robusta

Melia robusta, commonly known as the Indian chinchona, is a small evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes species known for their medicinal properties. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The tree is valued for its wood, which is durable and used in construction, as well as for its potential medicinal applications.
