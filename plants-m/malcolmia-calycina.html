Malcolmia calycina

Malcolmia calycina, commonly known as the white catchfly, is a flowering plant native to regions of Europe, North Africa, and western Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, hairy, ovate leaves and produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers. Its flowers bloom in the summer months and are pollinated primarily by insects.

The plant is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
