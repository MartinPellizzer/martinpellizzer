Masdevallia hepatica

Masdevallia hepatica, commonly known as the liver orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, often colorful flower structure with a distinctive lip that resembles a liver, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its leaves are usually dark green and strap-like, and it produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple.

Masdevallia hepatica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
