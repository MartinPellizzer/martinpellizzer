Melothria pallidinervia

Melothria pallidinervia, commonly known as the pale-veined passionfruit, is a member of the Passifloraceae family.

It is a climbing vine that produces small, yellow or orange fruits with a distinctive, edible pulp. The plant is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with pale veins, giving it its scientific name.

The flowers are showy, with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments, typical of the passionfruit family.
