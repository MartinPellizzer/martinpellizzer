Myrcia rostrata f. communis

Myrcia rostrata f. communis, commonly known as the common myrcia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and showy red or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, while its fruits are small, fleshy berries that contain numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
