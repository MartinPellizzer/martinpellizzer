Mimosa tetrandra

Mimosa tetrandra, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions and sandy areas.

Mimosa tetrandra is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
