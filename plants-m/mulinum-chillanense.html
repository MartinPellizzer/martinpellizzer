Mulinum chillanense

Mulinum chillanense, commonly known as the Chilean mullein, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It features tall, hairy stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various arid environments.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used by indigenous communities for respiratory ailments.
