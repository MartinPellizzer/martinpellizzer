Mammillaria brongniartii

Mammillaria brongniartii, commonly known as the Brongniart's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines and small, rounded tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its spiny exterior. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
