Myrcia rugosior

Myrcia rugosior, commonly known as the rough myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its bark is rough and fissured, giving it its common name, while its leaves are used in traditional medicine.

The flowers are usually white or pink and attract various pollinators such as bees and birds.
