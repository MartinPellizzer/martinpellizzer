Mespilus salicifolia

Mespilus salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaved medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its willow-like leaves that are elongated and serrated, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by fruiting bodies that develop into fleshy, edible berries. Mespilus salicifolia is typically found in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in woodland or hedgerow environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
