Melocactus acispinosus

Melocactus acispinosus, commonly known as the spiny melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its globular shape and dense covering of spines, which provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its green body. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Melocactus acispinosus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
