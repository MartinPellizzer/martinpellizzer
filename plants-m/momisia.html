Momisia

Momisia, commonly known as the moonflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its striking, large, white or pale yellow flowers that open at night. The plant typically has compound leaves and produces pods that contain seeds. Momisia species are native to various regions in Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

These plants are often valued for their ornamental beauty and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
