Minuartia campestris subsp. squarrosa

Minuartia campestris subsp.

squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrosa sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions. It features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has slender, branched stems covered with small, scale-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil in harsh environments and providing nectar for pollinators.
