Malva lusitanica subsp. pallescens

Malva lusitanica subsp.

pallescens, commonly known as the pale mallows, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus.

The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy pink or purple flowers with five petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
