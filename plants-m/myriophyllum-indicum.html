Myriophyllum indicum

Myriophyllum indicum, commonly known as watermilfoil, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Myriophyllum indicum is often used in ornamental water gardens and has some traditional medicinal uses in various cultures.
