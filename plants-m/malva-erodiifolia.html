Malva erodiifolia

Malva erodiifolia, commonly known as the hedge mallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus, and is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy pink or purple flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and wet meadows.

Malva erodiifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
