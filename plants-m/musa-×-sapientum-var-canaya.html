Musa × sapientum var. canaya

Musa × sapientum var. canaya, commonly known as the Canaya banana, is a variety of the common banana plant.

It is characterized by its large, elongated, and often curved fruit, which typically has a yellowish skin when ripe. The plant is a herbaceous perennial, growing as a clump of pseudostems formed from overlapping leaves. It produces clusters of berries, each containing a single seed, though the fruit is typically seedless in cultivated forms.

This variety is valued for its flavor and is often grown for both commercial and ornamental purposes.
