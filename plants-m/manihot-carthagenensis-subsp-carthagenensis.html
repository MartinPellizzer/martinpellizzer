Manihot carthagenensis subsp. carthagenensis

Manihot carthagenensis subsp.

carthagenensis, commonly known as the Carthage arrowroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa. It features a tuberous root system that is the primary storage organ for starch.

The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with simple, alternate, and usually hairy leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

The plant is valued for its starchy roots, which are used in traditional cuisine and as a source of carbohydrates.
