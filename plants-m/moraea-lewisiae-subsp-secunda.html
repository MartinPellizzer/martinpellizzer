Moraea lewisiae subsp. secunda

Moraea lewisiae subsp.

secunda, commonly known as the Cape daffodil, is a perennial herb native to South Africa. It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange.

The plant typically grows in well-drained soils in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces showy blooms from late winter to early spring.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, early-season flowers.
