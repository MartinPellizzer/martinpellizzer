Mentha barbata

Mentha barbata, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and square, erect stems. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorls along the stem, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Mentha barbata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
