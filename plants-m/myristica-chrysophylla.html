Myristica chrysophylla

Myristica chrysophylla, commonly known as the golden myristica, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen foliage and fragrant flowers. The plant produces a unique seed with a yellowish aril, which is highly valued in traditional medicine and perfumery. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is considered a vulnerable species in its natural range.
