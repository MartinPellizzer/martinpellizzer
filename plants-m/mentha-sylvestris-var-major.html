Mentha sylvestris var. major

Mentha sylvestris var. major, commonly known as wild mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are rich in essential oils, contributing to its strong menthol scent and medicinal properties.

It is often used in traditional herbal remedies and as a culinary herb.
