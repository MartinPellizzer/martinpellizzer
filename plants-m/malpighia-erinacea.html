Malpighia erinacea

Malpighia erinacea, commonly known as the red mamey or red mamey sapote, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that produces small, red, fleshy berries. The plant belongs to the family Malpighiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and clusters of small white flowers. The fruit is edible, with a sweet and slightly tart flavor, and is often used in jams, desserts, and beverages.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
