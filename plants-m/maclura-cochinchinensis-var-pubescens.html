Maclura cochinchinensis var. pubescens

Maclura cochinchinensis var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy yellow wood.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its distinctive yellow bark and yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by orange to red fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
