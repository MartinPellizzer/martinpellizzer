Mentzelia aurea

Mentzelia aurea, commonly known as the golden evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the late afternoon and evening, attracting pollinators such as moths and beetles. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The plant has a fibrous root system and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.
