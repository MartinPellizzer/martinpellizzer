Mallotus longifolius var. pubescens

Mallotus longifolius var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy longleaf mallot, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
