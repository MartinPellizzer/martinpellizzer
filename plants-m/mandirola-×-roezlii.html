Mandirola × roezlii

Mandirola × roezlii, commonly known as the hybrid mandirola, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Mandirola monilifera and Mandirola roezlii.

It is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant features compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
