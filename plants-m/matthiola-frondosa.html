Matthiola frondosa

Matthiola frondosa, commonly known as the fronded stock, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with distinctive four-petaled flowers. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in grayish-blue hairs, and its leaves are elongated and slightly lobed. It produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

Matthiola frondosa is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.
