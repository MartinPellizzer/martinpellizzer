Myrcia rhabdoides

Myrcia rhabdoides, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant red or orange flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Due to its invasive potential in some regions, it requires careful management to prevent ecological disruption.
