Microlepia yakusimensis

Microlepia yakusimensis, commonly known as the Yakushima grass fern, is a rare and endangered species native to the subtropical forests of Yakushima Island, Japan.

It is a small, delicate fern characterized by its slender, arching fronds that typically grow to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features a creeping rhizome and produces spore-bearing sporangia on the underside of its fertile fronds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of evergreen forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is highly sensitive to environmental changes and conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
