Myrcia schottii

Myrcia schottii, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces berries that start green and ripen to red, attracting various wildlife species. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some medicinal uses in traditional practices.

However, it can become invasive in certain environments if not managed properly.
