Meconopsis merakensis var. merakensis

Meconopsis merakensis var. merakensis, commonly known as the Merak meconopsis, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region of Nepal and India.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes, often in mountainous areas with rich, loamy soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
