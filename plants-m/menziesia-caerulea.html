Menziesia caerulea

Menziesia caerulea, commonly known as blue menziesia, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that turn a striking reddish-purple in the fall. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Menziesia caerulea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
