Malus mandshurica subsp. sachalinensis

Malus mandshurica subsp.

sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin apple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Russian Far East and northeastern China. It produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are typically not edible by humans but are occasionally used in traditional medicine.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
