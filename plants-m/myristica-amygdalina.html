Myristica amygdalina

Myristica amygdalina, commonly known as the African nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing a single seed. The seeds, often referred to as "nutmeg," are rich in essential oils and have a strong, spicy aroma.

The tree is valued for its aromatic properties and has traditional uses in medicine and as a spice.
