Mentha longifolia var. mosoniensis

Mentha longifolia var. mosoniensis, commonly known as the Mosonian mint, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and slender, creeping stems. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its essential oils contain menthol and other volatile compounds, making it valuable in both traditional medicine and aromatherapy.
