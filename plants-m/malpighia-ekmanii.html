Malpighia ekmanii

Malpighia ekmanii, commonly known as the Ecuadorian beautyberry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malpighiaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of Ecuador and is characterized by its showy, violet to red bracts that resemble berries. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and its leaves are typically alternate and ovate. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in cloud forests.

The fruit is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor, making it a valued plant in local ecosystems and for culinary use.
