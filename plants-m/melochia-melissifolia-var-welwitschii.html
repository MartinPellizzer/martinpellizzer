Melochia melissifolia var. welwitschii

Melochia melissifolia var. welwitschii, commonly known as the African nettle, is a tropical shrub characterized by its distinctive, net-veined leaves.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is native to regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Angola and Namibia. The plant features small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savanna habitats.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
