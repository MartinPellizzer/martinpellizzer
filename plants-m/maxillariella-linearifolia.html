Maxillariella linearifolia

Maxillariella linearifolia, commonly known as the slender-leaf orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. The plant features linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. Its growth habit is usually compact, with a pseudobulb-like structure that helps store water and nutrients.

Maxillariella linearifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
