Melastoma malabathricum var. normale

Melastoma malabathricum var. normale, commonly known as the common melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by orange-red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
