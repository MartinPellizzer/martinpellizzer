Machaeranthera pinnatifida var. glaberrima

Machaeranthera pinnatifida var. glaberrima, commonly known as the glabrous prairie coneflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and narrow, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities and a valuable species for ecological restoration.
