Myosotis arenaria

Myosotis arenaria, commonly known as the sand gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in harsh, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a basal rosette of foliage.

Myosotis arenaria is a perennial species that is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in mountainous areas.
