Mitrasacme pilosa var. pilosa

Mitrasacme pilosa var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy mitrasacme, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
