Metalepis haughtii

Metalepis haughtii, commonly known as the Mexican grass or velvetgrass, is a perennial grass native to Mexico.

It features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in a fine, silky texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental appeal and ecological value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
