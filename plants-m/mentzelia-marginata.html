Mentzelia marginata

Mentzelia marginata, commonly known as the narrowleaf mentzelia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or pale green margin. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to cream-colored and are arranged in clusters at the tips of the branches.

Mentzelia marginata is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
