Monoporus bipinnatus

Monoporus bipinnatus, commonly known as the two-lobed fern, is a unique plant characterized by its intricate frond structure.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is native to tropical regions of Africa. The plant features bipinnate leaves, meaning the fronds are divided into two pairs of pinnae, giving it a delicate, lacy appearance. Due to its rarity, it is often studied for its potential in botanical research and conservation efforts.
