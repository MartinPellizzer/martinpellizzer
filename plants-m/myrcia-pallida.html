Myrcia pallida

Myrcia pallida, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, white, aromatic flowers. The plant produces small, round, red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Myrcia pallida thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and forest understories.

Its leaves are aromatic and have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
