Megaclinium strobiliferum

Megaclinium strobiliferum, commonly known as the spikegrass, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces distinctive spike-like inflorescences. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Megaclinium strobiliferum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
