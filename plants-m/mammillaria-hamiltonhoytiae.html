Mammillaria hamiltonhoytiae

Mammillaria hamiltonhoytiae, commonly known as the Hamilton Hoyt mammillaria, is a small, spineless cactus native to the Sonoran Desert of Mexico.

It features a globular stem with white to pale green ribs and is covered in dense, woolly white spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise muted appearance. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stem.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
