Matisia lasiocalyx

Matisia lasiocalyx, commonly known as the rough-leaved matisia, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, spiny fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.

The species is valued for its hard wood and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
