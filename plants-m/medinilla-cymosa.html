Medinilla cymosa

Medinilla cymosa, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or velvet leaf, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, waxy flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant produces small, round fruits that are edible and have a citrus-like flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade.

Medinilla cymosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and showy blooms.
