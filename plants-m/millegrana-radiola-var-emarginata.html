Millegrana radiola var. emarginata

Millegrana radiola var. emarginata, commonly known as the margined millegrana, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. The leaves are typically narrow and linear, with a slightly wavy or notched edge, giving it the "emarginata" variety name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
