Moraea clavata

Moraea clavata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved moraea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and striking, funnel-shaped flowers. The flowers typically bloom in shades of pink, purple, and white, often with a distinctive dark spot at the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
