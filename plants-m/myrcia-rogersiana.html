Myrcia rogersiana

Myrcia rogersiana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are often eaten by birds and other wildlife. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
