Monardella villosa var. subserrata

Monardella villosa var. subserrata, commonly known as hairy beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, tubular pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or meadows.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
