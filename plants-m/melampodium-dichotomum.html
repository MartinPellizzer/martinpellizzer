Melampodium dichotomum

Melampodium dichotomum, commonly known as the two-branched sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
