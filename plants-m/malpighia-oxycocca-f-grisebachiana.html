Malpighia oxycocca f. grisebachiana

Malpighia oxycocca f. grisebachiana, commonly known as the pink granadilla, is a flowering plant in the family Malpighiaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its vibrant pink or red fruits. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are rich in vitamin C and have a tart flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy, five-petaled flowers that bloom in clusters.
