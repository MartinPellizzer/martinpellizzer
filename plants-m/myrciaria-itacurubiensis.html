Myrciaria itacurubiensis

Myrciaria itacurubiensis, commonly known as the Itacurubi berry, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small evergreen shrub that produces small, orange-red berries with a sweet, tangy flavor. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and guava. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a pleasant scent when crushed.

The fruit is often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine due to its nutritional and medicinal properties.
