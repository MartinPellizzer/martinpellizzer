Mentha arvensis subsp. lapponica

Mentha arvensis subsp.

lapponica, commonly known as alpine mint, is a perennial herb native to northern regions of Europe, Asia, and North America. It typically grows in moist, rocky, or sandy soils in alpine or subarctic environments.

The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically pink or purple, appearing in whorls along the stem.

It is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional medicine or as a culinary herb.
