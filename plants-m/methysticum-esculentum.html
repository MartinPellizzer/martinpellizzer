Methysticum esculentum

Methysticum esculentum, commonly known as kava, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stem and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its roots contain compounds such as kavalactones, which are responsible for its sedative effects.

Cultivation of kava is often practiced in traditional settings for its cultural and medicinal significance.
