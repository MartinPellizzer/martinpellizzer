Mespilus lutea

Mespilus lutea, commonly known as the yellow medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its oval, dark green leaves and fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The fruit is a pome, typically turning from green to yellowish-brown as it ripens, and is often harvested in late autumn. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit and seasonal changes make it a notable species in botanical gardens and historical landscapes.
