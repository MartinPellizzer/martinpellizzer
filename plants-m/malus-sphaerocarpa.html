Malus sphaerocarpa

Malus sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the Chinese crabapple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, ornamental fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. The tree produces white or pink flowers in early spring, adding aesthetic value to gardens. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
