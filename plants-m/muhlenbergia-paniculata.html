Muhlenbergia paniculata

Muhlenbergia paniculata, commonly known as the panicled muhlenbergia, is a warm-season grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and clusters of small, fluffy flowers that appear in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for ornamental grass gardens.

Muhlenbergia paniculata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
