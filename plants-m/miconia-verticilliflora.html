Miconia verticilliflora

Miconia verticilliflora, commonly known as the whorled miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pink, arranged in whorls along the stems. Its leaves are opposite or whorled, with a velvety texture and often have a distinctive reddish or purple hue on the underside.

Miconia verticilliflora is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
