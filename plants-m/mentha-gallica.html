Mentha gallica

Mentha gallica, commonly known as wild mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the upper stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Mentha gallica is known for its strong, aromatic scent and has been used historically in herbal medicine and culinary applications.
