Myrcia sessilissima

Myrcia sessilissima, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white or pinkish flowers that appear in spring. Its fruits are small, red, fleshy berries that are edible but often have a strong, spicy flavor.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
