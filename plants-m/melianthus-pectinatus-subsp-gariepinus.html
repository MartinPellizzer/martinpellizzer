Melianthus pectinatus subsp. gariepinus

Melianthus pectinatus subsp.

gariepinus, commonly known as the Cape melianthus, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers.

The plant produces distinctive, inflated seed pods that split open to release seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
