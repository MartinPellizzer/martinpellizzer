Microstylis partitiloba

Microstylis partitiloba, commonly known as the splintered stalk sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often forming dense stands in swampy areas. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, with the inflorescence often divided into two distinct parts, giving it its species name "partitiloba.".

The plant is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
