Microstylis pierrei

Microstylis pierrei, commonly known as the dwarf sedge, is a small perennial herb native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. The plant has slender, triangular stems and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its sedge appearance.

Microstylis pierrei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
