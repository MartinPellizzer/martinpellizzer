Mimosa paraguariae var. chacoensis

Mimosa paraguariae var. chacoensis, commonly known as the Chaco mimosa, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly found in the Chaco region of Paraguay and Argentina.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features bipinnate leaves that are delicate and fern-like, and it produces yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical heads. It is adapted to dry and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in such conditions.

The plant is sometimes used in traditional medicine and for ornamental purposes due to its unique foliage and growth habit.
