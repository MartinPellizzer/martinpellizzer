Mammillaria glanduligera

Mammillaria glanduligera, commonly known as the glandular mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, spherical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its compact size and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
