Mentha longifolia var. kashmiriana

Mentha longifolia var. kashmiriana, commonly known as Kashmir mint, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly menthol-like leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in whorled clusters along the stems. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its cooling and digestive effects.
