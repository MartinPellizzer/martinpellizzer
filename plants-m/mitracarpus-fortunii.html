Mitracarpus fortunii

Mitracarpus fortunii, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with a crown-like structure. The plant produces small, hairy fruits called cypsela, which are typically dispersed by wind. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Mitracarpus fortunii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
