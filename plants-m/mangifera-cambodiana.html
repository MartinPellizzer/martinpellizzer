Mangifera cambodiana

Mangifera cambodiana, commonly known as the Cambodian mango, is a tropical fruit-bearing tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its large, fragrant flowers and oval-shaped fruits. The tree can grow up to 15 meters tall, with thick, rough bark and broad, pinnate leaves. Its fruit is typically yellow when ripe, with a sweet and aromatic pulp that is highly valued in local cuisines.

The plant is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its importance in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
