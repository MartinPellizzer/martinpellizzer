Mimulus dentilobus

Mimulus dentilobus, commonly known as the toothed monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red tubular flowers, which are adapted for pollination by hummingbirds. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and canyon walls. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a serrated or toothed margin, giving it its scientific name.

Mimulus dentilobus is a perennial herb that thrives in full sun to partial shade.
