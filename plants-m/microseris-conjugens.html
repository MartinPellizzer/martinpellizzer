Microseris conjugens

Microseris conjugens, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its seeds are enclosed in cylindrical pods and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid regions.
