Mimosa bahamensis

Mimosa bahamensis, commonly known as the Bahamian mimosa, is a shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant has a woody stem and is typically found in coastal areas and sandy soils. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Mimosa bahamensis is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental features.
