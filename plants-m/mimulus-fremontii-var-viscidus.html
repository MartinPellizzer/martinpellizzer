Mimulus fremontii var. viscidus

Mimulus fremontii var. viscidus, commonly known as the sticky monkeyflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that give it a sticky texture, hence its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in open woodlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
