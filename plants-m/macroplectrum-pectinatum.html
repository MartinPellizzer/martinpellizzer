Macroplectrum pectinatum

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive pinnate fronds with a pectinate structure. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in humid tropical forests. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a leathery texture and a serrated margin.
