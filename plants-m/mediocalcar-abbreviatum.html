Mediocalcar abbreviatum

Mediocalcar abbreviatum, commonly known as the dwarf sand verbena, is a small, herbaceous annual plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions and has a short growing season.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in xerophytic plant communities.
