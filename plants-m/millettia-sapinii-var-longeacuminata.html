Millettia sapinii var. longeacuminata

Millettia sapinii var. longeacuminata, commonly known as the long-acuminate millettia, is a tropical climbing plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged with elongated leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemose inflorescences. Its fruits are legumes, containing several seeds that are often used in traditional medicine.

This variant is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
