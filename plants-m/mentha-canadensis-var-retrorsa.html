Mentha canadensis var. retrorsa

Mentha canadensis var. retrorsa, commonly known as Canadian mint or wild mint, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The leaves and flowering tops are often used in herbal teas and for their aromatic, cooling properties.
