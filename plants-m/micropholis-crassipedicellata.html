Micropholis crassipedicellata

Micropholis crassipedicellata, commonly known as the thick-shelled tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and distinctive, pod-like fruits. The plant is well adapted to coastal environments, with a root system that helps it withstand salt and wind. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, producing a sweet, fragrant scent that attracts pollinators.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in dry, sandy soils along shorelines.
