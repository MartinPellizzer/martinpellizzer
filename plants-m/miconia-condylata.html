Miconia condylata

Miconia condylata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Brazilian cherry, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of soft, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Miconia condylata is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive.

Its botanical features make it an important species in tropical ecosystems and a subject of ecological study.
