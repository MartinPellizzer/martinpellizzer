Miconia cundinamarcensis

Miconia cundinamarcensis, commonly known as the Cundinamarca miconia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Melastomataceae.

It is native to the Andean region of Colombia, particularly in the Cundinamarca department. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue bracts that surround its small flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it has been cultivated in botanical gardens and is of interest for its ecological and horticultural significance.
