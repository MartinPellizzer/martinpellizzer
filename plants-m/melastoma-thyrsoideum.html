Melastoma thyrsoideum

Melastoma thyrsoideum, commonly known as the scarlet flame or red flame plant, is a flowering shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Melastoma thyrsoideum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
