Melia elegans

Melia elegans, commonly known as the elegant mahogany, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family, which includes species known for their valuable hardwoods. The plant is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by winged fruits that contain seeds.

Melia elegans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its wood, which is prized for its durability and fine grain.
