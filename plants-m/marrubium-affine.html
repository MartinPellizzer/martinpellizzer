Marrubium affine

Marrubium affine, commonly known as lesser horehound, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features upright, branching stems covered with grayish-white hairs. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

Marrubium affine is known for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
