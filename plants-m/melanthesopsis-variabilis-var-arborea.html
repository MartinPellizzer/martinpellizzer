Melanthesopsis variabilis var. arborea

Melanthesopsis variabilis var. arborea, commonly known as the tree melanthus, is a unique variant of the melanthus genus.

It is characterized by its woody structure, distinguishing it from the herbaceous forms of its species. The plant typically features broad, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by fluffy, seed-bearing pods.

This variant is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in temperate regions.
