Melica persica subsp. jacquemontii

Melica persica subsp.

jacquemontii, commonly known as Persian grass or Jacquemont's melica, is a member of the Poaceae family. It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-white spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters along the stem. Native to regions of Asia, including parts of Turkey and the Caucasus, it thrives in moist, temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
