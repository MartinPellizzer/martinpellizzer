Myrtus virgultosa

Myrtus virgultosa, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant features small, dark green leaves that are often fragrant, and it produces tiny white flowers followed by dark blue berries. Myrtus virgultosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in hedges or as a ground cover.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.
