Mimulus luteus var. micranthus

Mimulus luteus var. micranthus, commonly known as the yellow monkeyflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, yellow-orange flowers that are typically tubular in shape, with five lobes that are often reflexed. The plant has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically hairy and glandular. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and marshy areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
