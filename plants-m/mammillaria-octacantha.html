Mammillaria octacantha

Mammillaria octacantha, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a dense covering of white, woolly spines. The plant typically grows to a height of 5 to 10 centimeters and has a greenish-gray stem with areoles. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in late spring or early summer.

This cactus is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
