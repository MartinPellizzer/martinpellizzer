Muricaria battandieri

Muricaria battandieri, commonly known as the Battandieri's sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, spiky blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, silver-gray leaves that are adapted to tolerate saline and arid conditions. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline, often forming dense colonies.

Muricaria battandieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal dunes.
