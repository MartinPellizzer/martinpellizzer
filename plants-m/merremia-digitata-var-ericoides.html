Merremia digitata var. ericoides

Merremia digitata var. ericoides, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a perennial vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive silver or white underside, which gives it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Its rapid growth habit makes it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
