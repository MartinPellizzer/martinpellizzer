Mentha cazurroi

Mentha cazurroi, commonly known as Corsican mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its fragrant properties and medicinal uses. Mentha cazurroi thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its calming and digestive benefits.
