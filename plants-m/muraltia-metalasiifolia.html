Muraltia metalasiifolia

Muraltia metalasiifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Ceraseira*, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Leguminosae (Fabaceae) family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is known for its ornamental value, featuring clusters of small, fragrant white flowers followed by bright red, edible berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and fruit production.

The leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant has a shallow root system that makes it suitable for container gardening.
