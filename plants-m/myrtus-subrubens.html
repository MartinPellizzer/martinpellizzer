Myrtus subrubens

Myrtus subrubens, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its berries, which turn from red to black as they mature, are edible but often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
