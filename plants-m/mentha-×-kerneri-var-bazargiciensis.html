Mentha × kerneri var. bazargiciensis

Mentha × kerneri var. bazargiciensis, commonly known as the Balkan mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. This variety thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. The plant is widely used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and digestive properties.

Its cultivation is valued both for ornamental purposes and for its essential oils.
