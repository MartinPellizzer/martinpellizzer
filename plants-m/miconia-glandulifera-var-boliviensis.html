Miconia glandulifera var. boliviensis

Miconia glandulifera var. boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian miconia, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest of South America.

It is a large, fast-growing tree that can reach heights of up to 30 meters. The plant is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous tiny seeds.

This species is known for its ecological importance in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
