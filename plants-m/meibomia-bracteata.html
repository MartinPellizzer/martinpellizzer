Meibomia bracteata

Meibomia bracteata, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy texture, while its flowers are zygomorphic with five petals.

Meibomia bracteata is widely cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and adaptability to indoor growing conditions.
