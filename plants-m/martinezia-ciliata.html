Martinezia ciliata

Martinezia ciliata, commonly known as the silky mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, pale pink to white flowers with five petals. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

Martinezia ciliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
