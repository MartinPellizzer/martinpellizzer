Melocactus andinus

Melocactus andinus, commonly known as the Andean melon cactus, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a small, globular cactus with a thick, ribbed stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. Its spines are typically short and yellowish, providing protection from herbivores. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months.

It is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated as a houseplant.
