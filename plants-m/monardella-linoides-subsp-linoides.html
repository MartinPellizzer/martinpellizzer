Monardella linoides subsp. linoides

Monardella linoides subsp.

linoides, commonly known as narrowleaf monardella, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of showy, tubular flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its flowers are primarily pollinated by bees and other insects, and it produces small, dry fruits containing seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
