Malpighia cornistipulata

Malpighia cornistipulata, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant flowers and compact growth habit.

The fruit, which is a small, fleshy drupe, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.
