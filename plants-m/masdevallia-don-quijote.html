Masdevallia don-quijote

Masdevallia don-quijote, commonly known as the Don Quixote orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often vibrant floral structures. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, preferring cool and moist conditions. Its flowers are usually small to medium in size, with intricate patterns and a variety of colors, including shades of pink, purple, and white.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
