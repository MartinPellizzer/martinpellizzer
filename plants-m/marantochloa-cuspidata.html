Marantochloa cuspidata

Marantochloa cuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to the wet tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family, which includes many ornamental and economically important species. The plant features striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with silver or white markings. It produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale yellow flowers, typically blooming during the wet season.

Despite its ornamental value, it is not widely cultivated for commercial purposes.
