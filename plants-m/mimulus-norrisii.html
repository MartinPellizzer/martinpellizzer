Mimulus norrisii

Mimulus norrisii, commonly known as the Norris's monkeyflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows. The plant features showy, bright orange to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

Mimulus norrisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
