Melaleuca coccinea

Melaleuca coccinea, commonly known as the red melaleuca, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers that appear in dense, cylindrical clusters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.

Melaleuca coccinea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract native pollinators.
