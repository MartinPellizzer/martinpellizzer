Mimetes

Mimetes, commonly known as the "ghost flower," is a genus of flowering plants native to South Africa.

These plants are characterized by their unique, often strikingly shaped flowers that resemble other organisms, such as insects or animals. The flowers are typically small and clustered, with vibrant colors that attract pollinators. Mimetes species are often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in Mediterranean climates.

They are valued in horticulture for their ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
