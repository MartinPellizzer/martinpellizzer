Mansonia gagei

Mansonia gagei, commonly known as the African mangosteen, is a tropical tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by round, fleshy fruits resembling the mangosteen. Its botanical features include a woody stem, alternate leaves, and a unique fruit structure that contains several segments.

It is often found in moist, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
