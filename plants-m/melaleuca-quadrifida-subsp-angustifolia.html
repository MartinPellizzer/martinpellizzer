Melaleuca quadrifida subsp. angustifolia

Melaleuca quadrifida subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf bottlebrush, is a shrub native to Australia. It is characterized by its striking red or pink bottlebrush-like flowers that appear in clusters at the ends of its branches.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments. Melaleuca quadrifida subsp.

angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
