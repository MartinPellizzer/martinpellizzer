Myosotis cespitosa var. multiflora

Myosotis cespitosa var. multiflora, commonly known as the clustered forget-me-not, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with dense clusters of small, sky-blue flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its delicate, flowering displays.
