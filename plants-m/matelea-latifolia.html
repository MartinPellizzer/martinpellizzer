Matelea latifolia

Matelea latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf matelea, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family, which includes milkweeds and other flowering plants with showy, often fragrant flowers. The plant is characterized by its large, broad leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its ecological importance and aesthetic value, it is often cultivated for both landscape beauty and supporting local pollinators.
