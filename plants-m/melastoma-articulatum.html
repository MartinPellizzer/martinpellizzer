Melastoma articulatum

Melastoma articulatum, commonly known as the purple mallow or red mallow, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, or purple, which bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Melastoma articulatum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
