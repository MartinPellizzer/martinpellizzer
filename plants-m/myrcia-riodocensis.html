Myrcia riodocensis

Myrcia riodocensis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fragrant foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural use.

The plant also has traditional medicinal uses in some South American cultures.
