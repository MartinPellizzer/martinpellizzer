Moraea crispa

Moraea crispa, commonly known as the spurred bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking, spurred flowers that range in color from white to purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Moraea crispa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
