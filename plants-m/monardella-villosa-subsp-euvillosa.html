Monardella villosa subsp. euvillosa

Monardella villosa subsp.

euvillosa, commonly known as the white gayfeather, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spike-like clusters of white to pale purple flowers.

The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are hairy and often covered in fine, white pubescence. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to well-drained soils. Monardella villosa subsp.

euvillosa is a valuable plant for pollinators, particularly butterflies and bees.
