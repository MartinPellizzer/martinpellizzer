Myrcia melanosticta

Myrcia melanosticta, commonly known as the black-stained myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to black as they mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
