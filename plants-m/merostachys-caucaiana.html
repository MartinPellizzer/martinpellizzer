Merostachys caucaiana

Merostachys caucaiana, commonly known as the Cauca bamboo, is a perennial grass native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, humid climates. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
