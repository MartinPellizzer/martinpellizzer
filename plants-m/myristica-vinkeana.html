Myristica vinkeana

Myristica vinkeana, commonly known as the Kew's nutmeg, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces distinctive fruits that contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its botanical significance lies in its unique chemical composition and historical use in herbal remedies.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered an endangered species requiring conservation efforts.
