Michelia pilifera

Michelia pilifera, commonly known as the downy michelia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Michelia pilifera is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its pleasant fragrance.
