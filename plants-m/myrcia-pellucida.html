Myrcia pellucida

Myrcia pellucida, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white flowers. The plant produces berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
