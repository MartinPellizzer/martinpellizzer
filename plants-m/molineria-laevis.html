Molineria laevis

Molineria laevis, commonly known as the smooth tawny sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It has slender, grass-like leaves that are smooth and hairless, with a light green color. The plant produces small, pale yellow to brownish flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and wet meadows.

Molineria laevis plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and wildlife.
