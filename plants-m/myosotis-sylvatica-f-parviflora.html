Myosotis sylvatica f. parviflora

Myosotis sylvatica f. parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf blue violet, is a compact, low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It features small, delicate flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, with five petals and a yellow center. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in gardens and floral arrangements.
