Miconia sessilifolia

Miconia sessilifolia, commonly known as the sessile-leaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers that are followed by distinctive, round, fleshy fruits. Miconia sessilifolia is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a challenge for land management.
