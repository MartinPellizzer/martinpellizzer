Macodes lanceolata

Macodes lanceolata, commonly known as the white-veined orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, waxy white petals with prominent green veins. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in its natural habitat. Its leaves are elongated and glossy, and it produces fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters.

Macodes lanceolata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
