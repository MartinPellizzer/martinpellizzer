Mendoncia puberula var. macropus

Mendoncia puberula var. macropus, commonly known as the large-fruited mendoncia, is a distinctive member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces large, edible fruits with a thick rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp. Native to the tropical regions of South America, it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and flavor, it is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
