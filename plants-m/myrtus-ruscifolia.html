Myrtus ruscifolia

Myrtus ruscifolia, commonly known as the woolly myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are small, ovate, and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its woolly texture. It produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, typically blooming in spring.

This plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also valued for its aromatic properties.
