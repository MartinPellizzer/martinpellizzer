Myristica crassipes subsp. crassipes

Myristica crassipes subsp.

crassipes, commonly known as the swamp nutmeg, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark brown seeds that are highly valued for their essential oils.

The plant typically grows in wet, swampy habitats and has dark green, leathery leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, while the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release the aromatic seeds.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
