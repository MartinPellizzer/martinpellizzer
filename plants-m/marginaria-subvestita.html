Marginaria subvestita

Marginaria subvestita, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often found in moist, disturbed habitats.

Marginaria subvestita is an annual herb that can spread rapidly, making it a common weed in agricultural and garden settings.
