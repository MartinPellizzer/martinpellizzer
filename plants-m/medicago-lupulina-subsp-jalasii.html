Medicago lupulina subsp. jalasii

Medicago lupulina subsp.

jalasii, commonly known as the Jalas' sainfoin, is a perennial legume native to parts of Central Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, oval-shaped pods that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
