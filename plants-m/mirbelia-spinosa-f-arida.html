Mirbelia spinosa f. arida

Mirbelia spinosa f. arida, commonly known as the spinous mirbelia, is a shrub native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, spiny leaves and yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with low rainfall. It has a woody structure and produces small, hard pods that contain seeds.

This subspecies is part of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities.
