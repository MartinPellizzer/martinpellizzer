Myrciaria maximiliana

Myrciaria maximiliana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá-do-cerrado, is a small evergreen shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red, edible berries that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in savannas and forests.

Its ornamental value and nutritional benefits make it both a horticultural and culinary interest.
