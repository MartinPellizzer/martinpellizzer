Muscaria decipiens

Muscaria decipiens, commonly known as the devil's bolete, is a fungal species belonging to the Boletaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown cap and a pale yellow to cream-colored stem. The fruiting bodies typically appear in late summer and early autumn, growing in association with various tree species. The mushroom contains psychoactive compounds, including ibotenic acid and muscimol, which can induce hallucinogenic effects.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to avoid consuming this species.
