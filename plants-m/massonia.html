Massonia

Massonia, commonly known as the African ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves and small, star-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions. Its leaves often form a dense, low-growing mat, and it produces copious amounts of small, white or pale pink flowers during the warmer months.

Massonia is also known for its ability to produce a powdery coating on its leaves, which helps reduce water loss.
