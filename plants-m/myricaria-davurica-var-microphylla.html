Myricaria davurica var. microphylla

Myricaria davurica var. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It features small, narrow leaves that are densely packed, reducing water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes, which bloom in summer and attract pollinators such as bees. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in sandy or saline soils.

This species is often used for stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion due to its hardy nature.
