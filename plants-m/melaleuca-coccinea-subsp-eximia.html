Melaleuca coccinea subsp. eximia

Melaleuca coccinea subsp.

eximia, commonly known as the red bottlebrush, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive red, bottlebrush-like flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has smooth, aromatic bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including honeyeaters and birds.
