Microlepia aculeata

Microlepia aculeata, commonly known as the spiny sword fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its wiry, spiny rachises and lanceolate, serrated fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its fronds are usually evergreen, and it produces sporangia on the underside of the leaves.

Microlepia aculeata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soil with moderate moisture.
