Mikania amblyolepis

Mikania amblyolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mikania, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain areas.
