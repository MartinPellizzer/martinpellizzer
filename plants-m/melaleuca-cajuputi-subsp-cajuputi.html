Melaleuca cajuputi subsp. cajuputi

Melaleuca cajuputi subsp.

cajuputi, commonly known as white stringy bark, is a tropical rainforest tree native to northern Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, white to grey bark that peels in long, stringy strips.

The plant produces small, bottle-shaped flowers that are typically white or yellow, followed by woody, cone-like fruits. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and riparian habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
