Miltinea

Miltinea, commonly known as the milkweed vine, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Apocynaceae.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a distinguishing feature and can be toxic. Miltinea species are often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, shaded environments.

Its floral structure attracts various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
