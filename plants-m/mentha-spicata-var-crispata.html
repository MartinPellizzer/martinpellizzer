Mentha spicata var. crispata

Mentha spicata var. crispata, commonly known as curled mint, is a variant of spearmint characterized by its distinctive curled leaves.

This plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist, shaded environments. It produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are aromatic and have a strong minty scent, making it popular in culinary and medicinal uses.

Its horticultural value lies in its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
