Myrospermum erythroxylum

Myrospermum erythroxylum, commonly known as the red-flowered myrospermum, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has elliptical, leathery leaves that are usually covered in a fine, powdery coating. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
