Madaria corymbosa var. fragaria

Madaria corymbosa var. fragaria, commonly known as the fragrant corymbosa, is a flowering plant characterized by its clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

It is native to certain regions of Asia and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant has aromatic foliage that emits a subtle, sweet scent, particularly when crushed. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a smooth texture and a mild fragrance.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant aroma.
