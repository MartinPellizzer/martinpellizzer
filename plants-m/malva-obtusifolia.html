Malva obtusifolia

Malva obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved mallow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with rounded edges and vibrant pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist meadows and along riverbanks. Its hairy stems and showy blooms make it a distinctive addition to gardens and natural habitats.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
