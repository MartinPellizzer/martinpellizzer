Myrcia govinha

Myrcia govinha, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and showy, clustered white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Myrcia govinha thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
