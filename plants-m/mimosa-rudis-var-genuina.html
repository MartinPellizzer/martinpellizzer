Mimosa rudis var. genuina

Mimosa rudis var. genuina, commonly known as the coastal mimosa, is a variant of the coastal mimosa native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that open in response to touch. The plant thrives in sandy, coastal soils and is adapted to salt spray and wind exposure. It plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for coastal wildlife.

Its botanical features make it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
