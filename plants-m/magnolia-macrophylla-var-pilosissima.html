Magnolia macrophylla var. pilosissima

Magnolia macrophylla var. pilosissima, commonly known as the Mexican magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 20 meters in its natural habitat. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping.
