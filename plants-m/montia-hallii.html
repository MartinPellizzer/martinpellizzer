Montia hallii

Montia hallii, commonly known as Hall's monkeyflower, is a small annual herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. The plant features slender, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are showy, with bright yellow petals that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Montia hallii is characterized by its unique floral structure and is often found in moist, calcareous soils.
