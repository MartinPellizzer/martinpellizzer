Macrosiphonia guaranitica

Macrosiphonia guaranitica, commonly known as the Guarani orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a slender, leafy stem and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained soil. It is characterized by its unique floral structure and is often found in association with other epiphytic and terrestrial orchids.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a threatened species in its native range.
