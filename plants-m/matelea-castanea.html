Matelea castanea

Matelea castanea, commonly known as the red tamarind tree, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its striking red, showy flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces woody capsules that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent.

Matelea castanea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
