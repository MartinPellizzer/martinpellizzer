Myrtus brasiliana var. lucida

Myrtus brasiliana var. lucida, commonly known as Brazilian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a popular plant in horticulture and herbal practices.
