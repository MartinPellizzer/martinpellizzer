Malvaviscus integrifolius

Malvaviscus integrifolius, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Mexican mallow, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of small, pink or red flowers. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
