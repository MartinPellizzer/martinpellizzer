Miconia leablanchetiana

Miconia leablanchetiana, commonly known as the white miconia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to several meters in height, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are often followed by distinctive, purple-black fruits. Miconia leablanchetiana is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a common sight in humid forests.
