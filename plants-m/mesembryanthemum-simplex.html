Mesembryanthemum simplex

Mesembryanthemum simplex, commonly known as the common ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in spring and summer. It is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardiness and low maintenance requirements.

Mesembryanthemum simplex is also valued for its ability to prevent soil erosion in dry landscapes.
