Morus australis

Morus australis, commonly known as the Australian mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The tree produces edible berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Morus australis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to provide shade.
