Mezoneuron sumatranum

Mezoneuron sumatranum, commonly known as the Sumatran moonseed, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and palmate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, fleshy fruits. Its botanical features include the presence of calcium oxalate crystals in its tissues, which are common in the Menispermaceae family.

Mezoneuron sumatranum is often found in tropical rainforests and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
