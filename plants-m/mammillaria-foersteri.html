Mammillaria foersteri

Mammillaria foersteri, commonly known as the Foerster's mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a covering of white, woolly hairs. The plant typically features a central stem with radial spines and a crown of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
