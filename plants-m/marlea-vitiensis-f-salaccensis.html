Marlea vitiensis f. salaccensis

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, thriving in the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually appearing in shades of white and pale yellow, adding to its ornamental value.
