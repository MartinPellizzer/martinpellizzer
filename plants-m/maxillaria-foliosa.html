Maxillaria foliosa

Maxillaria foliosa, commonly known as the leafy maxillaria, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in two rows along the stem. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-draining soil.

Maxillaria foliosa is appreciated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate for orchid enthusiasts.
