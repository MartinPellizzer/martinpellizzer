Malapoenna geniculata

Malapoenna geniculata, commonly known as the Malapoenna climbing plant, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a woody vine characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its botanical features include a distinctive branching pattern and the ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Malapoenna geniculata is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
