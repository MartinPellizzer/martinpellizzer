Macrococculus

Macrococculus, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its oval, yellowish-green fruits that turn red when ripe. The plant has simple, alternate leaves and produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are rich in vitamin C and have been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

The botanical name *Phyllanthus emblica* is often associated with this species.
