Myristica americana

Myristica americana, commonly known as the American nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, oily seeds that are used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains two seeds, which are the source of the spice nutmeg.

The tree thrives in humid, tropical climates and is valued for its economic and ecological significance.
