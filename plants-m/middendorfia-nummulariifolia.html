Middendorfia nummulariifolia

Middendorfia nummulariifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf mallow, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
