Meconopsis prainiana

Meconopsis prainiana, commonly known as the Himalayan blue poppy, is a striking alpine plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue or violet flowers, which bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a hairy texture, and it has a short, sturdy stem.

Meconopsis prainiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with specific growing conditions.
