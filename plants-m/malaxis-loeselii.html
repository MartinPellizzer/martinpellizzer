Malaxis loeselii

Malaxis loeselii, commonly known as the Loesel's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats and prefers cool, moist conditions. Its roots are fibrous, and it has a short rhizome that supports its growth.

Malaxis loeselii is noted for its small, greenish-yellow to white flowers that bloom in late summer.
