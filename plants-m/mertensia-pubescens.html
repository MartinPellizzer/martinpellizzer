Mertensia pubescens

Mertensia pubescens, commonly known as the hairy bluebells, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features hairy stems and leaves with a slightly rough texture, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Mertensia pubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
