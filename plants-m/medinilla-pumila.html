Medinilla pumila

Medinilla pumila, commonly known as the dwarf medinilla, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green with a slightly waxy texture. Its flowers are typically pink or red, with a distinctive three-lobed calyx and a spurred corolla.

Medinilla pumila thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
