Mespilus calicina

Mespilus calicina, commonly known as the small medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often lobed leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in early spring, followed by fruit that ripens in autumn. The fruit is a pomes, typically covered in a woolly coating that protects it until maturity.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
