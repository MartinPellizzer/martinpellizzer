Muhlenbergia foliosa var. ambigua

Muhlenbergia foliosa var. ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous muhlenbergia, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and fine, blue-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often mistaken for the more well-known muhlenbergia species. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate foliage and airy seed heads, which add visual interest to gardens.
