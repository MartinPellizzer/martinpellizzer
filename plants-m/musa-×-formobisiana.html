Musa × formobisiana

Musa × formobisiana, commonly known as the banana hybrid, is a cultivated variety resulting from crossbreeding between different banana species.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and clusters of fruit that typically mature to a yellow color. The plant is a member of the Musaceae family and is primarily grown for its edible fruit. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its genetic diversity, it is often used in breeding programs to develop disease-resistant banana varieties.
