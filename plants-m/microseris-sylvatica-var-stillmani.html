Microseris sylvatica var. stillmani

Microseris sylvatica var. stillmani, commonly known as the Stillman's woodland salsify, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and forest edges. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, with a stout, ridged stem.

Its flowers are pale yellow to white, and it produces elongated, slender seeds that are dispersed by wind.
