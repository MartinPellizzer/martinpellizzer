Myzorrhiza

Myzorrhiza, commonly known as the mycorrhizal fungus, is a symbiotic association between fungi and plant roots.

This relationship enhances the plant's ability to absorb water and nutrients, particularly phosphorus, from the soil. The fungi form a network of hyphae around and within the root cells, increasing the surface area for absorption. Myzorrhiza is crucial for the health and survival of many plant species in various ecosystems.

This mutualistic relationship has been observed in a wide range of plant families, including trees, shrubs, and grasses.
