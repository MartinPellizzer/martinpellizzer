Musa campestris var. sabahensis

Musa campestris var. sabahensis, commonly known as the Sabah banana, is a variant of the banana plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and produces clusters of yellow, edible fruits. The plant thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil. It is primarily cultivated for its fruit, which is rich in nutrients and has a sweet, creamy texture.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental qualities due to its striking foliage.
