Melicytus ramiflorus subsp. fasciger

Melicytus ramiflorus subsp.

fasciger, commonly known as the silver tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a leathery texture.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
