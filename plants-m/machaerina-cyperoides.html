Machaerina cyperoides

Machaerina cyperoides, commonly known as the New Zealand flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetland or swampy environments, forming dense clumps with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, three-ranked flowers.

Machaerina cyperoides plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
