Melastoma ibaguense

Melastoma ibaguense, commonly known as the Ibaguense Melastoma, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America, particularly Colombia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Melastoma ibaguense is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
