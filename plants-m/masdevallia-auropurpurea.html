Masdevallia auropurpurea

Masdevallia auropurpurea, commonly known as the golden purple masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety petals that range from deep purple to golden yellow, creating a visually stunning display. The plant typically grows as a miniature orchid, with compact growth and often epiphytic or lithophytic habits. Its flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique and colorful blooms.
