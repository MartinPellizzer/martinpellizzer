Melastoma sylvestre

Melastoma sylvestre, commonly known as the wild melastoma, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a white or yellow center. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Melastoma sylvestre is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
