Microthlaspi natolicum var. pentadactylonis

Microthlaspi natolicum var. pentadactylonis, commonly known as the five-fingered rockcress, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are pinnately divided into five to seven pairs of lobes, giving it the common name.
