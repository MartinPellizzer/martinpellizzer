Mimulus coccineus var. wolfii

Mimulus coccineus var. wolfii, commonly known as the scarlet monkeyflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features striking red-orange flowers with a distinctive hooded shape, typical of the Mimulus genus. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often glandular, contributing to its aromatic foliage. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variant is valued in gardens for its vibrant blooms and ecological benefits to pollinators.
