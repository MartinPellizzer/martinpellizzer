Mesosphaerum melissoides

Mesosphaerum melissoides, commonly known as the honeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical heads, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
