Miconia membranicalyx

Miconia membranicalyx, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences with small, white to pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains numerous small seeds.

Miconia membranicalyx is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.
