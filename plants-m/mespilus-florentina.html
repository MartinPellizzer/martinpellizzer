Mespilus florentina

Mespilus florentina, commonly known as the Persian medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region and parts of Turkey.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The fruit is a unique, bumpy, edible pome that ripens in late autumn and is often used in jams and culinary dishes. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional medicine.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.
