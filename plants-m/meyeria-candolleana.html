Meyeria candolleana

Meyeria candolleana, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool climates. The plant features showy, five-petaled flowers that are usually pink or red in color. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough, hairy texture.

Meyeria candolleana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate zones.
