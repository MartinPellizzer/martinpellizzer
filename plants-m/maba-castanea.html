Maba castanea

Maba castanea, commonly known as the African chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing smooth, chestnut-like seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and has traditional uses in local communities.
