Macoubea reticulata

Macoubea reticulata, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compact rosette of velvety, waxy leaves and clusters of showy, tubular flowers. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including purple, blue, and white, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Macoubea reticulata is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ease of care and aesthetic appeal.
