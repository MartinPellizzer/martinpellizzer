Medinilla megacalyx

Medinilla megacalyx, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that resemble bells. The plant produces large, showy bracts that often overshadow the actual small flowers, giving it a striking appearance. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant.

Medinilla megacalyx is also valued for its ornamental use in horticulture and traditional medicine.
