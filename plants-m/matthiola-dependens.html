Matthiola dependens

Matthiola dependens, commonly known as the hanging wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its drooping, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. It typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers due to its compact size and ornamental value.
