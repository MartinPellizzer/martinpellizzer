Myrtus macrochlamys

Myrtus macrochlamys, commonly known as the large-chalmed myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.

The fruit, which is a small, fleshy berry, is edible and has been used in traditional culinary and medicinal practices.
