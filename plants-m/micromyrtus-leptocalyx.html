Micromyrtus leptocalyx

Micromyrtus leptocalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a small evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bright red, brush-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Micromyrtus leptocalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
