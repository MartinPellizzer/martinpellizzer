Miconia axillaris subsp. proctorii

Miconia axillaris subsp.

proctorii, commonly known as the Proctor's miconia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America. It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves with a glossy surface.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical botanical gardens.
