Matthiola incana var. variegata

Matthiola incana var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated stock, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive variegated foliage, featuring white and green patterns on the leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and easy care requirements.

Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
