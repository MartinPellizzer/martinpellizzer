Monolopia lanceolata

Monolopia lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf false nettle, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy on the underside, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. Its stems are often reddish and can become woody with age.
