Miconia obscura

Miconia obscura, commonly known as the dark miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and showy, violet to purple flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically eaten by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. Miconia obscura is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
