Mammillaria subulifera

Mammillaria subulifera, commonly known as the needle spine cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique morphology and adaptability, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and desert gardens.
