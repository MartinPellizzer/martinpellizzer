Medicago laciniata var. brachyantha

Medicago laciniata var. brachyantha, commonly known as the narrow-leafed clover, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features trifoliate leaves with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is characterized by its erect growth habit and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability.

This variety is also used in forage and as a cover crop in agricultural settings.
