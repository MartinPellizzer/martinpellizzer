Melocactus hystrix

Melocactus hystrix, commonly known as the hooked melon cactus, is a species native to southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and spiny, ribbed structure. The plant produces a single, fleshy, orange-red fruit that is edible and resembles a melon. Its spines are typically straight and can be quite long, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Melocactus hystrix is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
