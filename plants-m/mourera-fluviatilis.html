Mourera fluviatilis

Mourera fluviatilis, commonly known as the river mourera, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and wetlands.

Mourera fluviatilis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
