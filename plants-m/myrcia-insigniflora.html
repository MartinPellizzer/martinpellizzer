Myrcia insigniflora

Myrcia insigniflora, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forested areas.

Myrcia insigniflora is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses.
