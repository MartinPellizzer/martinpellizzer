Monactis rhombifolia

Monactis rhombifolia, commonly known as the diamondleaf plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its rhombic-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Monactis rhombifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
