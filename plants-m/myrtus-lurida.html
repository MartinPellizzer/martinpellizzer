Myrtus lurida

Myrtus lurida, commonly known as the pale myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, white to pinkish flowers and leathery, dark green leaves. Myrtus lurida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It also has a fragrant scent, which is released when the leaves are crushed.
