Mentha × submollis

Mentha × submollis, commonly known as marsh mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to wetlands and marshy areas in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, square stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found near water bodies.

Mentha × submollis is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in herbal remedies for its calming and antispasmodic effects.
