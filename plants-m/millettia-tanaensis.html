Millettia tanaensis

Millettia tanaensis, commonly known as the Tana millettia, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its growth habit includes a straight trunk and spreading branches, making it suitable for agroforestry systems.

The tree is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility in agricultural settings.
