Myrcia fascicularis

Myrcia fascicularis, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Myrcia fascicularis is also known for its aromatic leaves and has been used in various cultural and ecological contexts.
