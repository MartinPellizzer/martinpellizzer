Magnolia stellata f. rosea

Magnolia stellata f. rosea, commonly known as the Rosea Magnolia, is a cultivar of the stellata magnolia, characterized by its double pink flowers.

This variety is prized for its showy, fragrant blooms that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. The flowers are typically pink or white, with a rounded, almost spherical shape. It is a deciduous tree or shrub that thrives in USDA hardiness zones 5 through 8.

The plant is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
