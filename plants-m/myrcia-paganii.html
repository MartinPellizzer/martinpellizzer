Myrcia paganii

Myrcia paganii, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various bird species. Myrcia paganii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, posing challenges to local ecosystems.
