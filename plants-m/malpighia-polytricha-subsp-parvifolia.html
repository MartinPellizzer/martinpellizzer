Malpighia polytricha subsp. parvifolia

Malpighia polytricha subsp.

parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved acorn berry, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of bright red, edible berries.

The plant belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is known for its ornamental value due to its vibrant flowers and fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate.

The berries are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
