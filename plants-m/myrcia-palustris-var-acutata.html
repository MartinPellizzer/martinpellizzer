Myrcia palustris var. acutata

Myrcia palustris var. acutata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved swamp myrtle, is a shrub native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which appear in clusters during the spring. Its fruit is a small, dark purple berry that contains several seeds.

This species is adapted to moist, acidic soils and is often found in swampy or marshy habitats.
