Mucuna longipedicellata

Mucuna longipedicellata, commonly known as the long pedicel cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and long, slender pods that contain small, dark seeds. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates. It is often used as a forage crop and in traditional medicine.

Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, adding ornamental value to the landscape.
