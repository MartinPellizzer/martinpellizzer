Maxillaria falcata

Maxillaria falcata, commonly known as the hooked maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces strikingly colorful, fragrant flowers with a distinctive hooked shape. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often a mix of white, yellow, and orange hues.

Maxillaria falcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
