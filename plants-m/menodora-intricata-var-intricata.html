Menodora intricata var. intricata

Menodora intricata var. intricata, commonly known as the intricate menodora, is a species native to certain regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its slender stems and compound leaves with finely serrated margins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds when mature.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Menodoridae and is noted for its complex floral morphology and ecological adaptability.
