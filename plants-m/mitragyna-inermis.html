Mitragyna inermis

Mitragyna inermis, commonly known as kratom, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves with a rough texture. The plant contains alkaloids, particularly mitragynine and 7-hydroxymitragynine, which contribute to its pharmacological properties. Its roots and leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their stimulant and analgesic effects.

The tree thrives in humid climates and is typically found in lowland rainforests.
