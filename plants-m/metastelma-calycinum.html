Metastelma calycinum

Metastelma calycinum, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking orange to yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and grows as a climbing vine with woody stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
