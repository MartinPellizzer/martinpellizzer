Malus baccata var. mandshurica

Malus baccata var. mandshurica, commonly known as the Manchurian crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its oval, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is a small, yellowish-red apple that ripens in late summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy, tolerating cold climates.

The plant is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of edible fruit.
