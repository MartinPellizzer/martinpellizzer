Mimosa discobola

Mimosa discobola, commonly known as the discoid mimosoid, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and showy, yellow flowers. The plant produces small, spherical seed pods that split open when mature, dispersing its seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Mimosa discobola is notable for its rapid growth and ability to form dense colonies in its natural habitat.
