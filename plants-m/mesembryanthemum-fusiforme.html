Mesembryanthemum fusiforme

Mesembryanthemum fusiforme, commonly known as the horned daisy, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and white. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves and stems. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a distinctive addition to Mediterranean and arid-region gardens.
