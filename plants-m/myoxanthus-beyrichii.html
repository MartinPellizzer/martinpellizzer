Myoxanthus beyrichii

Myoxanthus beyrichii, commonly known as the golden mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of specific host trees, primarily in South America.

It has small, leathery leaves that are usually green but can turn yellow or orange under certain conditions. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are often clustered together. Its berries are red or orange and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can affect the health of its host tree if it becomes too abundant.
