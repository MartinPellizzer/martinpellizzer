Meyeria ilienii

Meyeria ilienii, commonly known as the African orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its elegant, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along riverbanks. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil.

Meyeria ilienii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
