Mesosorus

Mesosorus, commonly known as the midrib or central vein, is a botanical feature found in the leaves of many ferns and fern allies.

It serves as the primary structural support for the leaf blade and plays a crucial role in the transport of water and nutrients. The mesosorus is typically more prominent than the other veins and can be easily identified by its thicker, more visible appearance. In some species, it may also contain specialized structures like sori, which are clusters of spore-producing organs.

This feature is essential for the plant's physiological function and reproductive processes.
