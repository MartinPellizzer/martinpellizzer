Muhlenbergia alpestris

Muhlenbergia alpestris, commonly known as the plains muhlenbergia, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green blades that are often adorned with a silver-white underside. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pink to purple in color, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Muhlenbergia alpestris is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
