Mesosetum altum

Mesosetum altum, commonly known as tall setgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like clusters of pale green to brownish flowers.

Mesosetum altum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
