Monardella frutescens

Monardella frutescens, commonly known as narrowleaf monardella, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands and meadows, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. The plant features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, tubular flowers are typically pink or purple and bloom in late summer.

Monardella frutescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and serves as a nectar source for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
