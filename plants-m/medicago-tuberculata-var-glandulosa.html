Medicago tuberculata var. glandulosa

Medicago tuberculata var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular medick, is a low-growing perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features trifoliate leaves with glandular hairs and produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. It is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value also make it a popular choice for landscaping.
