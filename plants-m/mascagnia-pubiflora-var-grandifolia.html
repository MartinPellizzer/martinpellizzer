Mascagnia pubiflora var. grandifolia

Mascagnia pubiflora var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered Mascagnia, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, leathery foliage with a distinctive pubescent texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
