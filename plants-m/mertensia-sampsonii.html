Mertensia sampsonii

Mertensia sampsonii, commonly known as the Sampson's bluebells, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and grow on sturdy, upright stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Mertensia sampsonii is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
