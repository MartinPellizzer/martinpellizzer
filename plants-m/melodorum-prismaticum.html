Melodorum prismaticum

Melodorum prismaticum, commonly known as the rainbow lily, is a striking plant native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, multicolored petals that range from deep reds to soft yellows and blues. The plant has a slender, upright stem that supports its showy flowers, which bloom in clusters during the warm seasons. Its leaves are broad and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal in both gardens and indoor settings.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its unique coloration and adaptability to various soil types.
