Mundulea anceps

Mundulea anceps, commonly known as the red acacia, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorned foliage and small, yellow flowers that bloom throughout the dry season. The plant has adapted to harsh environments with deep root systems that allow it to access groundwater. Its leaves are often shed during droughts, reducing water loss and conserving resources.

Mundulea anceps plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert wildlife.
