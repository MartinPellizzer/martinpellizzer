Maerua aethiopica

Maerua aethiopica, commonly known as the African glorybower, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woody structure and its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant has small, often inconspicuous flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. Its leaves are usually small, thick, and succulent, helping it conserve water.

Maerua aethiopica is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of wood and fuel.
