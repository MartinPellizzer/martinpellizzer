Maackia tashiroi var. taiwaniana

Maackia tashiroi var. taiwaniana, commonly known as the Taiwan maackia, is a deciduous shrub native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters, with slender branches and trifoliate leaves. Its flowers are small, pale yellow, and clustered in racemes, while the fruit is a legume that splits open to release seeds.

This species is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
