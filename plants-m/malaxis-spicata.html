Malaxis spicata

Malaxis spicata, commonly known as the spicate malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are linear and often clustered at the base of the stem.

Malaxis spicata is known for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
