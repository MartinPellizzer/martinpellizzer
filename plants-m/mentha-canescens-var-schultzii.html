Mentha canescens var. schultzii

Mentha canescens var. schultzii, commonly known as the Schultze mint, is a perennial herb native to the southern United States.

It features aromatic, serrated leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for its refreshing scent and potential health benefits.
