Machaeranthera laetevirens

Machaeranthera laetevirens, commonly known as the happy green coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flower heads that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Its botanical features include hairy stems, lance-shaped leaves, and a distinctive conical shape to its flower head.
