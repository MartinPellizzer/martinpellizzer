Miconia lilacina

Miconia lilacina, commonly known as the lilac miconia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, lilac-colored flowers that bloom throughout the year. Miconia lilacina is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some tropical gardens, though it can become invasive in certain areas.
