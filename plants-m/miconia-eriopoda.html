Miconia eriopoda

Miconia eriopoda, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains several seeds.

Miconia eriopoda is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets.
