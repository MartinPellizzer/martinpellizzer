Manihot tomentella

Manihot tomentella, commonly known as the woolly arrowleaf, is a perennial shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
