Matricaria auriculata

Matricaria auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate chamomile, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small yellow buttons. The plant features finely divided, feather-like leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers with a central disc. It is often cultivated for its aromatic properties and used in herbal remedies.

Matricaria auriculata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, ornamental plant.
