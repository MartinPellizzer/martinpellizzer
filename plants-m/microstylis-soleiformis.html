Microstylis soleiformis

Microstylis soleiformis, commonly known as the flat-stemmed microstylis, is a rare and delicate plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, grass-like leaves and slender, often prostrate stems that grow close to the ground. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists.

Due to habitat destruction, Microstylis soleiformis is considered endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
