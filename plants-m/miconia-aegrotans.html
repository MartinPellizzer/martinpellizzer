Miconia aegrotans

Miconia aegrotans, commonly known as the weeping miconia, is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety underside. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds. Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed habitats make it an invasive species in some regions.
