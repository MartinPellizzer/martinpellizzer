Megalastrum substrigosum

Megalastrum substrigosum, commonly known as the rock heath, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky and well-drained soils, often in high-altitude environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are evergreen, leathery, and adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Megalastrum substrigosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
