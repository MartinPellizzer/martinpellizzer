Mohavea confertiflora

Mohavea confertiflora, commonly known as the Mohave desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh desert conditions. The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually small, gray-green, and covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Mohavea confertiflora plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing sandy soils.
