Macphersonia gracilis var. trichocarpa

Macphersonia gracilis var. trichocarpa, commonly known as the silky bamboo, is a variant of the slender bamboo species native to China.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs covering the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically a vibrant green, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its graceful growth habit and unique texture.
