Mammillaria bocensis var. rubida

Mammillaria bocensis var. rubida, commonly known as the red-hair mammillaria, is a compact cactus native to Mexico.

It features a rounded stem with numerous small, ribbed tubercles that are often covered in white or pale blue spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its foliage is characterized by a dense covering of fine, reddish-brown hairs, giving it its common name.

It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.
