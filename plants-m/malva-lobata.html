Malva lobata

Malva lobata, commonly known as the broadleaf mallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, lobed leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers with five petals, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Malva lobata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
