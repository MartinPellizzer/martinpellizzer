Menezesiella ouricanense

Menezesiella ouricanense, commonly known as the Ourican sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall. Its morphology includes a sprawling growth habit with branched stems that often root at the nodes.

Menezesiella ouricanense plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is of interest in ecological and horticultural studies.
