Myrcia zetekiana

Myrcia zetekiana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and fragrant, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are aromatic and have been utilized in various cultural practices.

Myrcia zetekiana thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.
