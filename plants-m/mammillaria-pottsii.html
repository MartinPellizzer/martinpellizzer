Mammillaria pottsii

Mammillaria pottsii, commonly known as the Potts' hedgehog cactus, is a small, spineless cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and dense covering of white, woolly hairs, which help protect it from intense sunlight and moisture loss. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its gray-green body. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
