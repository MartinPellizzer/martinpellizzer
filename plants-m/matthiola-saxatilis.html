Matthiola saxatilis

Matthiola saxatilis, commonly known as the rock stock, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in full sun and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Matthiola saxatilis is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
