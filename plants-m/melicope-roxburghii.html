Melicope roxburghii

Melicope roxburghii, commonly known as the Indian laurustin, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white flowers. The plant produces smooth, dark brown fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional medicine practices.
