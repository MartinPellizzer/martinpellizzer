Moringa amara

Moringa amara, commonly known as the bitter moringa, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Moringaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it drought-resistant. Its leaves, seeds, and flowers are used in traditional medicine and as a food source despite their bitter taste.

The plant's botanical features include a woody stem, trifoliate leaves, and capsule fruits containing oval seeds.
