Malva verticillata subsp. chinensis

Malva verticillata subsp.

chinensis, commonly known as Chinese mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia. It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its fibrous stems and leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.
