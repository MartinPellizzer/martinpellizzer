Mora oleifera

Mora oleifera, commonly known as the Mexican mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The tree produces edible berries that are rich in nutrients and often used in traditional cuisine. Its bark and leaves have been used in herbal medicine for their potential health benefits.

Mora oleifera is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
