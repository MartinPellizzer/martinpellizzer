Momordica officinarum-elaterium

Momordica officinarum-elaterium, commonly known as the bitter apple or wild cucumber, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourds and cucurbits. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that give way to spiky, orange or red fruits. Its fruits contain numerous seeds enclosed in a fleshy, edible pulp, though the fruit is generally considered bitter.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
