Myrcia dictyophleba

Myrcia dictyophleba, commonly known as the zigzag myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are often fragrant and have a leathery texture. It produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

Myrcia dictyophleba is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests, where it thrives in the understory.
