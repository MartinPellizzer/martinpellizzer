Miconia argentimuricata

Miconia argentimuricata, commonly known as silver-leaved miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Miconia argentimuricata is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as rainforests and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
