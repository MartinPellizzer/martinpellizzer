Myrtoleucodendron scabrum

Myrtoleucodendron scabrum, commonly known as the rough-leaved myrtle, is a shrub native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its rough, scaly bark and small, leathery leaves. It produces white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and birds.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
