Mezzettia parviflora var. havilandii

Mezzettia parviflora var. havilandii, commonly known as Haviland's mezzettia, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and native plant gardens.
