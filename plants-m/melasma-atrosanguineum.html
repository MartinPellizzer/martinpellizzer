Melasma atrosanguineum

Melasma atrosanguineum, commonly known as the black bloodroot, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features a stout, reddish rhizome and produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant's leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a smooth, waxy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Botanically, it is notable for its distinctive root system and its role in native ecosystems.
