Memecylon walikalense

Memecylon walikalense, commonly known as the African starfruit or wild starfruit, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by bright orange or red berries. Its fruits are edible, though often tart, and are used in local cuisines.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
