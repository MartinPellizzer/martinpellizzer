Mimusops racemosa

Mimusops racemosa, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite and elliptical in shape. The tree produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by orange or red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and forest margins.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
