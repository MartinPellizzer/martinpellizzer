Myrtus micrantha

Myrtus micrantha, commonly known as dwarf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves and small white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its berries, which are typically red or black, are edible and often used in culinary applications.

Myrtus micrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils with full sun.
