Meliosma lepidota subsp. longipes

Meliosma lepidota subsp.

longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed silverleaf, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its finely textured, silver-gray leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers in early spring, which are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
