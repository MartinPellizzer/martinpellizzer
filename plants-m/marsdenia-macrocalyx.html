Marsdenia macrocalyx

Marsdenia macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered Marsdenia, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a distinctive corona structure. Its fruits are follicles that release seeds with silky hairs, aiding in wind dispersal.

Marsdenia macrocalyx is often found in tropical forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
