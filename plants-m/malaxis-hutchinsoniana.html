Malaxis hutchinsoniana

Malaxis hutchinsoniana, commonly known as the Hutchinson's lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and a tall, slender flowering stem that bears a cluster of small, pale green to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and swampy areas. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces pseudobulbs that store nutrients and water.

The species is notable for its delicate appearance and ecological significance in its native range.
