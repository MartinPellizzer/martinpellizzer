Myristica fusiformis subsp. fusiformis

Myristica fusiformis subsp.

fusiformis, commonly known as the slender nutmeg, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical fruit that contains a single seed.

The plant produces aromatic oils and is closely related to the spice nutmeg. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy surface.

The species is valued for its unique botanical features and potential medicinal properties.
