Myrcia stenocymbia

Myrcia stenocymbia, commonly known as the narrow-cup myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically elliptical and have a glossy surface. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Myrcia stenocymbia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities.
