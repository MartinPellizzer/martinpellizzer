Myrcia vauthiereana

Myrcia vauthiereana, commonly known as the Vauthier's myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its leaves are typically elliptical and glossy, providing a dense canopy in its natural habitat.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
