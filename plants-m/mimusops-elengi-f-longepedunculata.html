Mimusops elengi f. longepedunculata

Mimusops elengi f. longepedunculata, commonly known as the long pedunculate satinwood, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark and elliptical leaves that are usually opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in folk medicine.

The plant thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
