Mormodes pardina var. unicolor

Mormodes pardina var. unicolor, commonly known as the white orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elegant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as rainforests, where it thrives on the forest floor or on tree roots. Its leaves are broad and glossy, providing a lush green backdrop to its delicate blooms.

Due to its rarity and beauty, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
