Mammillaria karwinskiana

Mammillaria karwinskiana, commonly known as the Karwinskiana mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a dense covering of white to pale green spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
