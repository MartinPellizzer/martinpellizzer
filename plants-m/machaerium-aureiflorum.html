Machaerium aureiflorum

Machaerium aureiflorum, commonly known as golden flame grass, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its tall, slender stalks and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in dense clusters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Machaerium aureiflorum is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
