Miscanthus intermedius

Miscanthus intermedius, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fluffy, feathery plumes during the flowering season, which typically occurs in late summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for ornamental gardens and ecological restoration projects.

Miscanthus intermedius is also valued for its ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions.
