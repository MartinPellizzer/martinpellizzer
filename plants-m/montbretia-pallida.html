Montbretia pallida

Montbretia pallida, commonly known as the white montbretia, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the genus Gladiolus and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of trumpet-shaped flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, and the plant thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Montbretia pallida is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
