Muraltia spinosa

Muraltia spinosa, commonly known as the prickly pear or desert date, is a deciduous shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and pinnately compound leaves that are often shed during dry seasons. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by edible, fleshy fruits resembling small dates. Its deep root system allows it to thrive in poor soils and withstand prolonged drought.

Muraltia spinosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local communities.
