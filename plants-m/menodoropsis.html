Menodoropsis

Menodoropsis, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its showy, often violet or purple, composite flowers. The plants typically have hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Menodoropsis species are often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
