Mesembryanthemum tenuiflorum

Mesembryanthemum tenuiflorum, commonly known as the ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves and small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems. It blooms during the cooler months, often after rainfall, and is known for its ability to thrive in sandy, well-drained soils.

The ice plant is also popular as a ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
