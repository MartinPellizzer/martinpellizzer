Maxillaria ramosissima

Maxillaria ramosissima, commonly known as the branched maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with a distinctive red or purple lip. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Maxillaria ramosissima is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
