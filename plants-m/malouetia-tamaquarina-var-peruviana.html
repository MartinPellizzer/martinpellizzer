Malouetia tamaquarina var. peruviana

Malouetia tamaquarina var. peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian malouetia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
