Myrcia munizii

Myrcia munizii, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule containing several seeds, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
