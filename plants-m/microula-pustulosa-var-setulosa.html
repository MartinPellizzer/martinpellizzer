Microula pustulosa var. setulosa

Microula pustulosa var. setulosa, commonly known as the hairy microula, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered inflorescences and pubescent (hairy) leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers are usually small and greenish-white, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain seeds.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
