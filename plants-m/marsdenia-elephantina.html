Marsdenia elephantina

Marsdenia elephantina, commonly known as the elephant's foot, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy, and often branched stems that resemble the legs of an elephant, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically small and narrow, adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Marsdenia elephantina is well-suited for xerophytic gardens and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.
