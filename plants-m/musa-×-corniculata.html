Musa × corniculata

Musa × corniculata, commonly known as the horsehead banana, is a hybrid plant that belongs to the Musaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often curved fruit, which resembles a horse's head, hence its common name. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and is cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit. It typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

The leaves are large, broad, and have a waxy surface, contributing to its tropical appearance.
