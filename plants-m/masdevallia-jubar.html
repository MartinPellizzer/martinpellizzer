Masdevallia jubar

Masdevallia jubar, commonly known as the Jubar Masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean highlands of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, often colorful flowers that bloom during the cooler months. The plant typically has a rhizomatous root system and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This orchid thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires careful attention to light and moisture levels for optimal growth.
