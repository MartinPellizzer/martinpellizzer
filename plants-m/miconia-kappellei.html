Miconia kappellei

Miconia kappellei, commonly known as the Kappelle's miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, cream-colored flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often accompanied by a strong, unpleasant odor. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains numerous small seeds.

Miconia kappellei is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.
