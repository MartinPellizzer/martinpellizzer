Myristica platysperma

Myristica platysperma, commonly known as the broad-seeded nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by bright red, fleshy fruits that enclose the valuable seeds used for nutmeg and mace. Its botanical features include a distinctively shaped seed with a thick, aromatic outer layer.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its commercial spice products.
