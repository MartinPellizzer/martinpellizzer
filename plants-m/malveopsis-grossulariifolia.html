Malveopsis grossulariifolia

Malveopsis grossulariifolia, commonly known as the Chinese rose or Chinese hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its showy, rose-like flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Malveopsis grossulariifolia is part of the Malvaceae family, which includes other plants such as hibiscus and cotton.
