Mecodium atrovirens

Mecodium atrovirens, commonly known as the blackleaf mecodium, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, slightly hairy leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
