Myristica fatua subsp. affinis

Myristica fatua subsp.

affinis, commonly known as the wild nutmeg, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers.

The plant produces fruit that contains a hard, aromatic seed, similar to the nutmeg used in culinary applications. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
