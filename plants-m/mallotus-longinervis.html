Mallotus longinervis

Mallotus longinervis, commonly known as the long-veined mallot, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated, prominent veins. The plant produces small, greenish flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
