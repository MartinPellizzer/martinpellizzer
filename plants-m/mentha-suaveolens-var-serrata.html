Mentha suaveolens var. serrata

Mentha suaveolens var. serrata, commonly known as the serrated mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to regions of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 60 cm in height. The leaves are lance-shaped, serrated along the edges, and have a distinctive minty aroma. It produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late summer.

This variety is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
