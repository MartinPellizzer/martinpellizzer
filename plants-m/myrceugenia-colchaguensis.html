Myrceugenia colchaguensis

Myrceugenia colchaguensis, commonly known as the Chilean myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the temperate rainforests of southern Chile.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has dark green, leathery foliage. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to red as it matures.

Myrceugenia colchaguensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
