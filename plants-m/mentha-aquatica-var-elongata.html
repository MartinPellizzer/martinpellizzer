Mentha aquatica var. elongata

Mentha aquatica var. elongata, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and stream sides.

It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, often with a reddish tinge. The leaves are lance-shaped, slightly hairy, and emit a strong minty aroma when crushed. It produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorls along the stem, typically blooming in summer.

This variety is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.
