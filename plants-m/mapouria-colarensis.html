Mapouria colarensis

Mapouria colarensis, commonly known as the African snowbell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Mapouria colarensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
