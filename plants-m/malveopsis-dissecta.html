Malveopsis dissecta

Malveopsis dissecta, commonly known as the dissected mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its delicate, palmately lobed leaves and showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
