Mormodes leucochila

Mormodes leucochila, commonly known as the white-veined orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, while its flowers have three sepals and three petals, with the latter often being showy and white.

The orchid's blooming period usually occurs during the rainy season, adding to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
