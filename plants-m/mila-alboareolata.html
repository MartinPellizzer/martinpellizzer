Mila alboareolata

Mila alboareolata, commonly known as the white-spotted milkweed, is a tropical plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or pale green pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Mila alboareolata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinators.
