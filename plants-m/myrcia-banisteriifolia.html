Myrcia banisteriifolia

Myrcia banisteriifolia, commonly known as Brazilian pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a variety of soil types.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
