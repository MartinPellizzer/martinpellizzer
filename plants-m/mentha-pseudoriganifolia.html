Mentha pseudoriganifolia

Mentha pseudoriganifolia, commonly known as Indian mint or wild mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue in color. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

Mentha pseudoriganifolia thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
