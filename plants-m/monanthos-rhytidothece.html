Monanthos rhytidothece

Monanthos rhytidothece, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence.

It is native to regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia, and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes make it an aggressive weed in agricultural settings.

Due to its adaptability, it is often considered a problematic invasive species in certain environments.
