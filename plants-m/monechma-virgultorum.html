Monechma virgultorum

Monechma virgultorum, commonly known as the Virginia mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their fragrant leaves and flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas. It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive minty scent when crushed.

The showy white to pale blue flowers appear in clusters at the ends of the stems, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
