Melia bukayun

Melia bukayun, commonly known as the Philippine mahogany or white mahogany, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Mahogany family (Meliaceae) and is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkin-like inflorescences, followed by winged fruits containing seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is often used in furniture and construction.

The plant also has medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects.
