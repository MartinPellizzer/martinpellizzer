Mentha arvensis subsp. sylvatica

Mentha arvensis subsp.

sylvatica, commonly known as woodland mint, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

The plant features opposite, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and have a strong, minty aroma. Its flowers are small, tubular, and clustered in whorls, often blooming in late summer.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
