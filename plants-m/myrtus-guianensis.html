Myrtus guianensis

Myrtus guianensis, commonly known as the Guianese myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, dark berries that contain aromatic seeds.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental and aromatic properties.
