Momordica charantia subsp. macroloba

Momordica charantia subsp.

macroloba, commonly known as the giant bitter melon, is a variant of the bitter gourd native to tropical regions. It is characterized by its large, elongated fruits that can grow up to 30 cm in length.

The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces yellow, fragrant flowers. Its fruit is typically green when unripe and turns orange when mature.

The plant is cultivated for its edible fruit, which is used in various cuisines and traditional medicine.
