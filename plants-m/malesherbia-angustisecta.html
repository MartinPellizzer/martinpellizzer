Malesherbia angustisecta

Malesherbia angustisecta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved malesherbia, is a member of the Malvaceae family.

It is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
