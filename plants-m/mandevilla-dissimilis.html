Mandevilla dissimilis

Mandevilla dissimilis, commonly known as the flame vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters along its stems. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Mandevilla dissimilis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant flowers and vigorous growth habit.
