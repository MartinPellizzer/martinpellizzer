Magnolia Fordiana Var. Hainanensis

Hainan magnolia, scientifically known as Magnolia fordiana var. hainanensis, is a rare and striking tree native to the island of Hainan in China. This flowering plant is celebrated for its large, showy white blooms that appear in early spring, adding a dramatic touch to its natural habitat. It is one of the few magnolia species found exclusively in this region, making it a unique botanical treasure. The tree is often found in moist, shaded forests, where it thrives in the humid climate. Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, the Hainan magnolia is considered a species of conservation concern.
