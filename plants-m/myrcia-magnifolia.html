Myrcia magnifolia

Myrcia magnifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian beauty or red myrtle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, red or orange flowers. The plant produces small, aromatic fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in rainforests.

Myrcia magnifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
