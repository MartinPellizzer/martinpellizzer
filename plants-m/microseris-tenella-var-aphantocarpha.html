Microseris tenella var. aphantocarpha

Microseris tenella var. aphantocarpha, commonly known as the slender globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments.

This variety is distinguished by its reduced or absent seed pods, a key feature in its botanical classification.
