Medinilla caudatifolia

Medinilla caudatifolia, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or long-leaved medinilla, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of fragrant, waxy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed raw or used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Medinilla caudatifolia is valued for its aesthetic appeal and its ability to adapt to various soil types.
