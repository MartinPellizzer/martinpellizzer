Millettia solomonensis

Millettia solomonensis, commonly known as Solomon's millettia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal research.
