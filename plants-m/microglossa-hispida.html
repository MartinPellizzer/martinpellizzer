Microglossa hispida

Microglossa hispida, commonly known as the woolly microglossa, is a small, evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Microglossa hispida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique texture and resilience.
