Miconia araneifera

Miconia araneifera, commonly known as the spider-plant or net-leaf miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that often have a net-like venation pattern, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Miconia araneifera is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in some regions due to its aggressive spread and ecological impact.
