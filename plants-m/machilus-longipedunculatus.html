Machilus longipedunculatus

Machilus longipedunculatus, commonly known as the long-pedunculate chinquapin, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, followed by edible, nut-like fruits.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
