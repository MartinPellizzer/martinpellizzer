Maxillaria paulistana

Maxillaria paulistana, commonly known as the Paulista Maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a contrasting lip. Its leaves are typically elliptical and arranged in two rows along the stem.

Maxillaria paulistana thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
