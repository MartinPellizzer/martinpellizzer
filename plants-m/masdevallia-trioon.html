Masdevallia trioon

Masdevallia trioon, commonly known as the trioon orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically blooms during the cooler months, showcasing intricate, spurred petals that are characteristic of the Masdevallia genus. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
