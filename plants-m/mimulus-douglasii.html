Mimulus douglasii

Mimulus douglasii, commonly known as the yellow monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in woodland areas. It has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Mimulus douglasii is a member of the Physostegia genus and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
