Masdevallia coccinea

Masdevallia coccinea, commonly known as the blood red orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers with intricate, velvety petals and sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually dark green and broad, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

Masdevallia coccinea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
