Mida acuminata

Mida acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed mida, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are often brightly colored, ranging from red to violet, which attracts pollinators like birds and insects.

Mida acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
