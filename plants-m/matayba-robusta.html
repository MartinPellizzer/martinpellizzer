Matayba robusta

Matayba robusta, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Erythroxylum robustum*, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Erythroxylaceae and is characterized by its attractive red flowers and elliptical leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are often clustered in panicles and bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also notable for its medicinal properties, with some parts used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
