Metrosideros polymorpha var. polymorpha

Metrosideros polymorpha var. polymorpha, commonly known as the ʻōhiʻa lehua, is a native Hawaiian tree characterized by its vibrant red or yellow flowers and dark, rough bark.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is well adapted to the tropical and sub-tropical climates of Hawaii. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions, making it a common sight in Hawaiian forests. It plays a significant ecological role, providing habitat and food for native birds and insects.

The ʻōhiʻa lehua is also culturally important to the people of Hawaii, symbolizing strength and resilience.
