Malva moreniana

Malva moreniana, commonly known as the woolly mallow, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry meadows and rocky areas. Its flowers are typically five-petaled and have a distinctive, mallow-like appearance.

Malva moreniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
