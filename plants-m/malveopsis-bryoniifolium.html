Malveopsis bryoniifolium

Malveopsis bryoniifolium, commonly known as the bryoniifolium mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
