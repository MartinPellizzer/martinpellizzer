Monarda fistulosa var. rubra

Monarda fistulosa var. rubra, commonly known as the scarlet bee balm, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its showy, tubular red flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and features heart-shaped, aromatic leaves with a minty scent. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and native plant landscapes.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a valuable addition to both cultivated and natural environments.
