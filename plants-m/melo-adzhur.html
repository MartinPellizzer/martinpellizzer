Melo adzhur

Melo adzhur, commonly known as the African honeyfig, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by round, fuzzy fruits that turn yellow or orange when mature. The fruit contains a single, large, edible seed encased in a fibrous pulp.

It is often cultivated for its fruit, which is valued for its sweet, honey-like flavor.
