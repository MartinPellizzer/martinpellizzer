Moricandia arvensis var. populifolia

Moricandia arvensis var. populifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that resemble those of poplar trees, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Moricandia arvensis var. populifolia is a herbaceous perennial that can grow up to 1 meter in height.
