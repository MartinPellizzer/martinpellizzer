Myrcia hirtellifolia

Myrcia hirtellifolia, commonly known as the hairy myrcia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, hairy pubescence. Its flowers are typically pink or red, with a strong, aromatic fragrance.

Myrcia hirtellifolia is often found in shaded undergrowth and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
