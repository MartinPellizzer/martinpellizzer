Maxillaria echinophyta

Maxillaria echinophyta, commonly known as the spiny maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, and its flowers are often clustered in racemes. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

The species is noted for its resilience and ease of care, making it suitable for both novice and experienced growers.
