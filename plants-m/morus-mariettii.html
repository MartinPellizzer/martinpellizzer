Morus mariettii

Morus mariettii, commonly known as the Japanese mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, fuzzy fruits that are typically consumed by silkworms. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its fibrous bark and edible leaves.

Morus mariettii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to adapt to various soil conditions.
