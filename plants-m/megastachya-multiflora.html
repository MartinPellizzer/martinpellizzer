Megastachya multiflora

Megastachya multiflora, commonly known as the mountain lily, is a flowering plant native to the high elevations of the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Megastachya multiflora is often found in alpine meadows and is a popular choice for rock gardens due to its ornamental value.
