Moehringia markgrafii

Moehringia markgrafii, commonly known as the Alpine snowbell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are small, hairy, and often clustered at the base, while its stems are prostrate or ascending.

Moehringia markgrafii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
