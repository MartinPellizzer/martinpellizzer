Mackaya neesiana

Mackaya neesiana, commonly known as the orchid vine, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pendulous, often colorful blooms that can range from white to pink and yellow. Its leaves are typically ovate or elliptical, with a smooth surface and a waxy texture.

Mackaya neesiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates.
