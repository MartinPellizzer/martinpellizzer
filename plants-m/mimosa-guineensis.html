Mimosa guineensis

Mimosa guineensis, commonly known as the African sensitive plant, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers followed by pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and savannas.

Mimosa guineensis is also valued for its medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal remedies.
