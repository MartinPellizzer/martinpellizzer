Mesembryanthemum capillare

Mesembryanthemum capillare, commonly known as the woolly bush or silver leaf, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fuzzy foliage that gives it a silvery appearance, and its small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its fleshy leaves and stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
