Macrotyloma bieense

Macrotyloma bieense, commonly known as the African burr legume, is a herbaceous plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces yellow flowers that attract pollinators. Its pods are hairy and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
