Mentha × velutina var. hortivaga

Mentha × velutina var. hortivaga, commonly known as the velvet mint, is a hybrid plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and produces small, pale blue or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves used in herbal teas and culinary applications.
