Malva moschata f. subglabra

Malva moschata f. subglabra, commonly known as the smooth musk mallow, is a variant of the musk mallow species characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves.

This perennial plant typically grows in moist, fertile soils and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with five petals and a distinctive central tuft of stamens. The leaves are heart-shaped and often have a soft, velvety texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
