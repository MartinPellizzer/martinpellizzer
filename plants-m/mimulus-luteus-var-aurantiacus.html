Mimulus luteus var. aurantiacus

Mimulus luteus var. aurantiacus, commonly known as the golden monkeyflower, is a variant of the yellow monkeyflower native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red-orange flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and woodland edges. It has heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in a fine, sticky substance.

This variety is valued for its vibrant color and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental appeal.
