Myrcia riedeliana

Myrcia riedeliana, commonly known as the Riedel's myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, black seeds.

Myrcia riedeliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
