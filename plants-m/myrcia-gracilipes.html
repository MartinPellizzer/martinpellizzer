Myrcia gracilipes

Myrcia gracilipes, commonly known as the slender myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Myrcia gracilipes is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
