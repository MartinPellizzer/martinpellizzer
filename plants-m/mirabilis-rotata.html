Mirabilis rotata

Mirabilis rotata, commonly known as the whirligig flower, is a strikingly beautiful flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Mirabilis genus, which includes several species known for their vibrant, showy blooms. The plant produces clusters of four-petaled, tubular flowers that are often brightly colored, ranging from red to orange and yellow. Its unique flower structure allows for efficient pollination by both insects and hummingbirds.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
