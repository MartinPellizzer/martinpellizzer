Malva julii

Malva julii, commonly known as the Sierra Madre mallows, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the plant is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
