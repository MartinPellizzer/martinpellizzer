Medusantha eriophylla var. coriifolia

Medusantha eriophylla var. coriifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf medusantha, is a variant of the medusantha plant native to specific regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, adding to its ornamental value. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

This variant is valued in horticulture for its unique leaf structure and adaptability to various soil conditions.
