Matricaria matricarioides

Matricaria matricarioides, commonly known as the common tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its yellow flower heads composed of many tiny florets. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are finely divided and covered in a soft, hairy texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Matricaria matricarioides is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
