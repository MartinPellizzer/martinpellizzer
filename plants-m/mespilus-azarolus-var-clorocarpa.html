Mespilus azarolus var. clorocarpa

Mespilus azarolus var. clorocarpa, commonly known as the green persimmon, is a variant of the persimmon species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking green foliage and small, edible fruits that ripen in the autumn. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

The flowers are inconspicuous, appearing in clusters, and the fruit is usually pear-shaped with a smooth, waxy skin.
