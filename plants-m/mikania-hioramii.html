Mikania hioramii

Mikania hioramii, commonly known as the Chinese Mikania, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its creeping growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by oval-shaped fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
