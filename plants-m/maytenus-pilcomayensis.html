Maytenus pilcomayensis

Maytenus pilcomayensis, commonly known as the Peruvian mayten, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in humid, tropical forests. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by fleshy, red berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a distinctive, serrated margin.

Maytenus pilcomayensis is valued for its ecological role and has been studied for potential medicinal properties.
