Myrcia dasyblasta var. ovata

Myrcia dasyblasta var. ovata, commonly known as the glossy myrtle, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, tropical environments. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that contain numerous seeds.

The plant is often valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
