Megalastrum pubirhachis

Megalastrum pubirhachis, commonly known as the woolly star, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. Its leaves are usually thick and leathery, adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

The flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer, are pollinated primarily by insects.
