Mentha nepalensis

Mentha nepalensis, commonly known as Nepal mint, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, refreshing menthol scent.

Mentha nepalensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its cooling and anti-inflammatory effects.
