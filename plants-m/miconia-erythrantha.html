Miconia erythrantha

Miconia erythrantha, commonly known as the red-veined miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent red veins, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are followed by round, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Miconia erythrantha is also known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, which can impact local ecosystems.
