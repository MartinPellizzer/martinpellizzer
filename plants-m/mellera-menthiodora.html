Mellera menthiodora

Mellera menthiodora, commonly known as the fragrant mellera, is a tropical evergreen tree native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are aromatic when crushed. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by oval-shaped fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has potential applications in traditional medicine.
