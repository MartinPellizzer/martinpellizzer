Mentha × rotundifolia f. elongata

Mentha × rotundifolia f. elongata, commonly known as the elongated peppermint, is a variant of the peppermint plant characterized by its slightly rounded leaves.

This form exhibits a more compact growth habit compared to the typical peppermint, with leaves that are broader and more rounded in shape. It is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves, which are used in herbal teas and aromatherapy. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
