Medicago gordeievii

Medicago gordeievii, commonly known as the Gordeev's clover, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a deep root system, allowing it to thrive in poor, sandy soils. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
