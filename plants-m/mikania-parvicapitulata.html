Mikania parvicapitulata

Mikania parvicapitulata, commonly known as the small-headed morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, showy flowers. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the early morning, giving it its common name. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.
