Majorana sipylea

Majorana sipylea, commonly known as sweet fennel, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its feathery, aromatic leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, round seeds that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its leaves and seeds contain volatile oils that contribute to its distinctive licorice-like flavor.

Majorana sipylea thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation.
