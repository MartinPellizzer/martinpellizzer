Melastoma pellegrinianum

Melastoma pellegrinianum, commonly known as the Pellegrin's melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, often pink or red bracts that surround its small flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a rough, leathery texture.

Melastoma pellegrinianum is known for its ability to thrive in humid climates and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
