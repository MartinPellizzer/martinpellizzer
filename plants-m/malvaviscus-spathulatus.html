Malvaviscus spathulatus

Malvaviscus spathulatus, commonly known as the broadleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
