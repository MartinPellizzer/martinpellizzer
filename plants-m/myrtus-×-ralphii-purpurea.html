Myrtus × ralphii purpurea

Myrtus × ralphii purpurea, commonly known as purple myrtle, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with a distinctive purple hue on the underside of the leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, which are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental value make it suitable for gardens and as a hedge plant.
