Monarda malloryi

Monarda malloryi, commonly known as Mallory's bee balm, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Mallory's bee balm is often found in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full sunlight.
