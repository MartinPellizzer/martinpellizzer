Melaleuca stenostachya var. pendula

Melaleuca stenostachya var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping melaleuca, is a shrub or small tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches and clusters of small, aromatic leaves that emit a distinctive scent. The plant produces creamy-white to pale yellow flowers, which are typically borne in dense, cylindrical spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate salt spray, making it suitable for coastal landscaping.
