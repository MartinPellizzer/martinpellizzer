Masdevallia infracta subsp. curtipes

Masdevallia infracta subsp.

curtipes, commonly known as the dwarf masdevallia, is a compact orchid species native to the Andean regions of South America. It features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink.

The plant typically grows at high altitudes, thriving in cool, humid environments with dappled sunlight. Its unique floral structure and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for enthusiasts of miniature orchids.

It is often cultivated in terrariums or as a tabletop plant due to its compact size and ease of care.
