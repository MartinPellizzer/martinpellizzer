Microchilus hetaerioides

Microchilus hetaerioides, commonly known as the silverleaf cactus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cactaceae.

It is native to the arid regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in a dense layer of white, waxy hairs that reflect sunlight and reduce water loss. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange and bloom during the warmer months.

Microchilus hetaerioides is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.
