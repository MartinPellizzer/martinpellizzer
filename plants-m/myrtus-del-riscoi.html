Myrtus del-riscoi

Myrtus del-riscoi, commonly known as the Del-Risco myrtle, is a rare shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its berries, which are typically blue or black, are edible and have a slightly tart flavor.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
