Microcos antidesmifolia var. antidesmifolia

Microcos antidesmifolia var. antidesmifolia, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange ray flowers and green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, oval fruits called achenes, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
