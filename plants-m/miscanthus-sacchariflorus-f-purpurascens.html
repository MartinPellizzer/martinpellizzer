Miscanthus sacchariflorus f. purpurascens

Miscanthus sacchariflorus f. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple fountain grass, is a ornamental grass species native to Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that produce feathery, pinkish-purple plumes during the late summer and fall. The plant is characterized by its clump-forming growth habit and its ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade. It is often used in landscaping for its striking color and texture.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is valued for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
