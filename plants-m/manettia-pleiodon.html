Manettia pleiodon

Manettia pleiodon, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false mallow, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a soft, textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year in warm climates. Its stems are often woody and can grow up to several meters in height, making it a notable species in its native habitat.

Manettia pleiodon is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
