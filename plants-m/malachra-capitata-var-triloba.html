Malachra capitata var. triloba

Malachra capitata var. triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or red, with a prominent yellow center. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and thrives in well-drained soils, often growing in sandy or rocky coastal habitats. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
