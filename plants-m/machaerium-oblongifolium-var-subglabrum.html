Machaerium oblongifolium var. subglabrum

Machaerium oblongifolium var. subglabrum, commonly known as the smooth-leaved machaerium, is a perennial legume native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, supporting compound leaves with elongated, ovate leaflets. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers, followed by pod-like fruits containing several elliptical seeds. Its leaves are typically glabrous or nearly so, giving it a smooth appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas.
