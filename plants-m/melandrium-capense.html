Melandrium capense

Melandrium capense, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit, a round, orange-red berry, is edible and has a tangy, slightly sweet flavor.

Melandrium capense is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
