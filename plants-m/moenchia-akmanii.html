Moenchia akmanii

Moenchia akmanii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that typically reaches heights of less than 10 centimeters. The plant has small, narrow leaves arranged in a rosette pattern, and its flowers are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Moenchia akmanii plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
