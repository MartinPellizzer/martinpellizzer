Mentzelia parvifolia var. transiens

Mentzelia parvifolia var. transiens, commonly known as the Transient Blazingstar, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is known for its short blooming period, giving it its common name "Transient.".
