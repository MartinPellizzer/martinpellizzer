Myrtus phillyreifolia

Myrtus phillyreifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its aromatic properties have made it valuable in both horticulture and herbal practices.
