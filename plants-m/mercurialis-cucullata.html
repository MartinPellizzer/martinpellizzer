Mercurialis cucullata

Mercurialis cucullata, commonly known as the hooded mercury or the bellflower mercury, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive hood-like structure. The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and the stems are erect with a reddish tinge.

Mercurialis cucullata is an annual or biennial plant that blooms from late spring to early autumn.
