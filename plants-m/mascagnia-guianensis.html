Mascagnia guianensis

Mascagnia guianensis, commonly known as the Guianese mascagnia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

The plant is a shrub or small tree, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The plant is also noted for its milky sap, which is a common feature in many members of the Apocynaceae family.
