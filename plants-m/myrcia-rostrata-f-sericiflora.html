Myrcia rostrata f. sericiflora

Myrcia rostrata f. sericiflora, commonly known as the silky-leaved myrtle, is a variant of the myrtle plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that have a silky texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a small, round capsule that contains several seeds.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and aromatic qualities.
