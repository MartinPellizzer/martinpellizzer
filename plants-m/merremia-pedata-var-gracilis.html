Merremia pedata var. gracilis

Merremia pedata var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red. The plant thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover.

The seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, orange-red capsule that splits open when mature.
