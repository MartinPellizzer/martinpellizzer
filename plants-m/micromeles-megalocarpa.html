Micromeles megalocarpa

Micromeles megalocarpa, commonly known as the giant fruit tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces massive, woody fruits that can weigh several kilograms when mature. These fruits have a thick, fibrous outer layer and contain numerous seeds enclosed in a fleshy pulp.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often studied for its role in local biodiversity and potential horticultural value.
