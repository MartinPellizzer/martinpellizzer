Malus sieversii f. dragavzevii

Malus sieversii f. dragavzevii, commonly known as the Dragavzevii apple, is a rare botanical variant of the wild apple species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral morphology, including larger, more showy flowers compared to typical Malus species. The plant exhibits unique genetic traits that make it valuable for breeding programs aimed at improving apple varieties. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure for optimal growth.

Its conservation status is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its potential contribution to genetic diversity in cultivated apples.
