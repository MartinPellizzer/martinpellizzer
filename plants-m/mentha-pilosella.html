Mentha pilosella

Mentha pilosella, commonly known as velvet mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is known for its aromatic foliage. It typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.

Mentha pilosella is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported cooling and digestive properties.
