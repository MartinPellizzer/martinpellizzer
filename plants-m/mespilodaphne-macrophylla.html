Mespilodaphne macrophylla

Mespilodaphne macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leafed mespilodaphne, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are often pubescent on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, sweet-scented fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
