Maerua uniflora

Maerua uniflora, commonly known as the one-flowered maerua, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, pale green leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater.

Maerua uniflora plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
