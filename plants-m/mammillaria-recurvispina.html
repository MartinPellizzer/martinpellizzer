Mammillaria recurvispina

Mammillaria recurvispina, commonly known as the recurving spine cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a distinctive rib structure and prominent spines. The plant features white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a dense, woolly covering on its stems.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.
