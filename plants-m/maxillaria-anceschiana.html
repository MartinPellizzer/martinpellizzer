Maxillaria anceschiana

Maxillaria anceschiana, commonly known as the Brazilian night-blooming orchid, is a species native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom at night. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

Maxillaria anceschiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
