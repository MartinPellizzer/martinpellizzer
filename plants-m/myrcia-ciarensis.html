Myrcia ciarensis

Myrcia ciarensis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when mature. Myrcia ciarensis is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical regions.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
