Maxillaria gomeziana

Maxillaria gomeziana, commonly known as the Gomez's orchid, is a species native to Central America, particularly found in countries like Mexico and Guatemala.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and attractive, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces showy, dark violet to purple blooms with yellow markings, often appearing in late spring to early summer. Its pseudobulbs are cylindrical and covered in a thick, leathery epidermis, which helps it survive in its tropical habitat.

Maxillaria gomeziana is appreciated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
