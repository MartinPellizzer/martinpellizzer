Millettia championii

Millettia championii, commonly known as the African silk tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often trifoliate or pinnate, and its showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink. The plant produces flat, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

Millettia championii is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
