Miscanthidium teretifolium

Miscanthidium teretifolium, commonly known as the slender false miscanthus, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often described as having a bamboo-like appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late summer, which are clustered in panicles.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat for various invertebrates and serving as a food source for certain bird species.
