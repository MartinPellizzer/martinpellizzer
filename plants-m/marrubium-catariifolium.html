Marrubium catariifolium

Marrubium catariifolium, commonly known as the Canary Island horehound, is a perennial herb native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas. It has a strong, bitter scent and is part of the mint family, Lamiaceae.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
