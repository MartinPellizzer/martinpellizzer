Magnolia membranacea

Magnolia membranacea, commonly known as the Chinese magnolia or the paper magnolia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its flowers are fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release winged seeds.
