Morus mongolica var. barkamensis

Morus mongolica var. barkamensis, commonly known as the Chinese mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to northern China and adjacent regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically gray-green in color and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as mulberries, which are edible and have a sweet, juicy flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food for silkworms.

This variety is also valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in traditional Chinese horticulture.
