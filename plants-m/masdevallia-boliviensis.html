Masdevallia boliviensis

Masdevallia boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian masdevallia, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is native to the Andean regions of Bolivia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers that display a range of colors, including pink, purple, and white. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments at high altitudes, often in cloud forests. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that help it conserve moisture in its native habitat.

Masdevallia boliviensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
