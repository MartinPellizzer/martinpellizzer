Macrocalyx chrysanthemifolius

Macrocalyx chrysanthemifolius, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and hairy stems that help it retain moisture in dry conditions.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
