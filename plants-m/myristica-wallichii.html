Myristica wallichii

Myristica wallichii, commonly known as the nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and fragrant, woody fruits. The plant is renowned for producing the aromatic seeds used in the spice trade, with the outer covering resembling a dried fruit. Its roots and other parts are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The tree thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
