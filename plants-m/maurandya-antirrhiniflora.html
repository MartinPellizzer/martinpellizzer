Maurandya antirrhiniflora

Maurandya antirrhiniflora, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, orange-yellow flowers that resemble trumpets, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is a deciduous perennial, typically growing as a climbing vine with trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its botanical features include showy inflorescences and a preference for well-drained soil and full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
