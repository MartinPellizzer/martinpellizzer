Myristica furfuracea

Myristica furfuracea, commonly known as the African nutmeg or wild nutmeg, is a tropical evergreen tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody fruits that contain the spice-like seeds used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape. Its seeds are encased in a red, fleshy aril, which is often used in local remedies for its medicinal properties.

The tree is valued for its aromatic oils and has been used in various cultural and therapeutic practices across its native region.
