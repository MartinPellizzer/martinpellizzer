Mespilus × grandiflora

Mespilus × grandiflora, commonly known as the European whitebeam, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its oval, dark green leaves and showy white or pale pink flowers that appear in spring. The fruit is a small, round drupe with a sweet, edible flesh surrounding a hard pit. It is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional landscapes.
