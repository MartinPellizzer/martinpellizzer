Myrtus reticulata

Myrtus reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern. The plant produces small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark blue, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Myrtus reticulata is also valued for its aromatic properties and historical use in traditional medicine.
