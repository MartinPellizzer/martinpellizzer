Manilkara sideroxylon

Manilkara sideroxylon, commonly known as the yellow kempas, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces yellowish-white flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.

The plant also has historical significance in traditional medicine and as a source of natural latex.
