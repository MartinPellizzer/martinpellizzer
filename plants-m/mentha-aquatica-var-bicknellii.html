Mentha aquatica var. bicknellii

Mentha aquatica var. bicknellii, commonly known as Bicknell's water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, typical of the Lamiaceae family. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and wet meadows.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and a subject of study in botany and ecology.
