Melica digitata

Melica digitata, commonly known as the digitate brome, is a grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, elongated leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, temperate environments, often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Melica digitata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
