Moricandia sinaica

Moricandia sinaica, commonly known as the Sinai mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and North Africa.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard plants. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It features small, yellow flowers and elongated, narrow leaves.

Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in local cuisines.
