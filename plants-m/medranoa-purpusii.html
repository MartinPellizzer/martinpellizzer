Medranoa purpusii

Medranoa purpusii, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to the southern United States and Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange-red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has woody stems and broad, ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warmer climates.

Medranoa purpusii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
