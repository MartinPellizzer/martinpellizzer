Maxillaria fragrans

Maxillaria fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in varying environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a compact rosette pattern.

Maxillaria fragrans thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
