Myanthus sanguineus

Myanthus sanguineus, commonly known as the scarlet gourd, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking red to orange-yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical fruits that mature to a deep red color, often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species in horticulture.
