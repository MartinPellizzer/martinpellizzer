Melastoma geniculatum

Melastoma geniculatum, commonly known as the mountain melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are usually pink or red, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
