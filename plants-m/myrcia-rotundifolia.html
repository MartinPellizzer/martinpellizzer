Myrcia rotundifolia

Myrcia rotundifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are elliptical or ovate, with a glossy surface and a distinctive aromatic scent.

Myrcia rotundifolia is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
