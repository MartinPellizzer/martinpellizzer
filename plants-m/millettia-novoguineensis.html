Millettia novoguineensis

Millettia novoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea millettia, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests and secondary growth areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and potential horticultural applications.
