Malpighia glabra var. guatemalensis

Malpighia glabra var. guatemalensis, commonly known as the West Indian cherry, is a shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, bright red berries that are rich in vitamin C and other antioxidants. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits.

The botanical features of this variety include its erect growth habit and the presence of stellate (star-shaped) hairs on its stems and leaves.
