Malpighia ambigua

Malpighia ambigua, commonly known as the wild acerola or Mexican wolfberry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malpighiaceae.

It is native to Central and South America, where it typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its small, bright red berries that are rich in vitamin C and other nutrients. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy coating, while its flowers are usually pink or white.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the edible fruits it produces.
