Mammillaria radiaissima

Mammillaria radiaissima, commonly known as the radiate mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a dense covering of white, radial spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its spiny exterior. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for collectors and desert gardeners.
