Mentha × muelleriana

Mentha × muelleriana, commonly known as the Mexican mint or wild mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with opposite, serrated leaves that emit a strong mint scent when crushed. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or purple, appearing in whorled clusters.

Mentha × muelleriana is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to its refreshing flavor and medicinal properties.
