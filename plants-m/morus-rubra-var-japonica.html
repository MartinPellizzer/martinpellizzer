Morus rubra var. japonica

Morus rubra var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese mulberry, is a variant of the mulberry tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically a dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are edible for both humans and animals. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its leaves, which are used in silk production.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide shade in gardens and landscapes.
