Miconia valenciana

Miconia valenciana, commonly known as the Valencian miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences with bright yellow flowers that attract various pollinators. Its fruits are small, berry-like structures that contain numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in warm climates, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
