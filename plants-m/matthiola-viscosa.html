Matthiola viscosa

Matthiola viscosa, commonly known as sticky stock, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its glandular, sticky leaves and stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Matthiola viscosa is a perennial herb that is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
