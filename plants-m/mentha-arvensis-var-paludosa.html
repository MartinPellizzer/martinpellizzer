Mentha arvensis var. paludosa

Mentha arvensis var. paludosa, commonly known as marsh mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh habitats.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 60 cm in height. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in dense spikes. Its leaves are lance-shaped, aromatic, and have a strong menthol scent.

This variety is closely related to other mint species and is often used in traditional medicine for its cooling and digestive properties.
