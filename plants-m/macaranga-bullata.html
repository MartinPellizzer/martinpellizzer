Macaranga bullata

Macaranga bullata, commonly known as the white-veined mallow, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a fast-growing shrub or small tree that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The leaves are palmately lobed, with a rough, waxy texture and prominent white veins. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in secondary forests and along roadsides.
