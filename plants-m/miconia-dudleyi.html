Miconia dudleyi

Miconia dudleyi, commonly known as the Dudley miconia, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 3 meters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
