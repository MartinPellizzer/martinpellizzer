Mangifera mucronulata

Mangifera mucronulata, commonly known as the Philippine mango, is a tropical tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves and fragrant, yellow-orange fruits. The fruit has a thick, fibrous skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp that is typically consumed fresh. This species is valued for its edible fruit and is often found in lowland forests and cultivated areas.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems and traditional agriculture.
