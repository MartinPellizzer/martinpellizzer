Moricandia moricandioides var. cavanillesiana

Moricandia moricandioides var. cavanillesiana, commonly known as the Cavanilles' leafy poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its striking, papery blue or purple petals and its fern-like, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in Mediterranean climates.
