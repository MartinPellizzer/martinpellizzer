Musa × paradisiaca f. tuba

Musa × paradisiaca f. tuba, commonly known as the banana plant, is a cultivated variety of the banana plant that is prized for its large, edible berries.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its broad, sword-shaped leaves and clusters of fleshy, segmented fruits. The plant is typically propagated through suckers or rhizomes, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Its fruit is rich in potassium and other nutrients, making it a popular food source worldwide.
