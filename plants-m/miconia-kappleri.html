Miconia kappleri

Miconia kappleri, commonly known as the Kappler's miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that are often followed by bright red or orange berries. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

However, it is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its competitive nature and potential ecological impact.
