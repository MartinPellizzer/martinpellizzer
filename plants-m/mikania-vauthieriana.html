Mikania vauthieriana

Mikania vauthieriana, commonly known as the Brazilian Mikania, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture.

Mikania vauthieriana is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain areas.
