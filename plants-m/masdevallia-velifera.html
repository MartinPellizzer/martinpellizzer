Masdevallia velifera

Masdevallia velifera, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a striking orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves and striking, long-lasting flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and requires high air circulation to prevent fungal diseases. Its unique foliage and elegant blooms make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.

Masdevallia velifera is typically grown as a terrestrial or epiphytic plant, depending on the specific conditions it is provided.
