Melothria trilobata var. costaricensis

Melothria trilobata var. costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican sourberry, is a tropical vine native to Central America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a serrated edge and produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that turn red when ripe. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and climbing habit, often found in humid, shaded environments. Its fruits are edible but have a tart, acidic flavor, making them popular in local cuisines.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
