Meriania pulcherrima

Meriania pulcherrima, commonly known as the Christmas bell or angel's trumpets, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant is a shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
