Misanteca mutisii

Misanteca mutisii, commonly known as the Colombian orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, showy flowers that typically feature a combination of white, yellow, and green hues. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Its delicate structure and vibrant blooms make it a popular subject in botanical studies and horticulture.

Due to habitat loss, it is also considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
