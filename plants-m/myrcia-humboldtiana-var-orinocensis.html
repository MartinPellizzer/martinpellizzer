Myrcia humboldtiana var. orinocensis

Myrcia humboldtiana var. orinocensis, commonly known as the Orinoco myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Venezuela and Colombia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
