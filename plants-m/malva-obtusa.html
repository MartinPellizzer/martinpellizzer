Malva obtusa

Malva obtusa, commonly known as the broadleaf mallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and soft, fuzzy surface. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically five-petaled and have a distinctive, slightly notched appearance.

Malva obtusa is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
