Machilus ovatilobus

Machilus ovatilobus, commonly known as the oval-lobed sandarac tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are ovate with rounded lobes and have a smooth, glossy surface.

The tree produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.
