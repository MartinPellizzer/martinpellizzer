Menispermum acuminatum

Menispermum acuminatum, commonly known as Indian snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Menispermaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in umbels. Its fruits are berries that turn red when mature, containing several seeds.

Botanically, it is notable for its medicinal properties and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
