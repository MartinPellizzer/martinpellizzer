Melampodium cinerascens

Melampodium cinerascens, commonly known as the grayleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its grayish-green foliage and produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Melampodium cinerascens is also noted for its potential medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal remedies.
