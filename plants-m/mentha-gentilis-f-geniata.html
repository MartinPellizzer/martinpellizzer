Mentha gentilis f. geniata

Mentha gentilis f. geniata, commonly known as the sweet mint, is a botanical variant of the mint plant characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its square stems and opposite leaves. This subspecies is distinguished by its more intense and sweeter scent compared to other mint varieties. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

The plant is often used in culinary applications and traditional herbal remedies due to its medicinal properties.
