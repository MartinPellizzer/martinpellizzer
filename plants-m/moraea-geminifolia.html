Moraea geminifolia

Moraea geminifolia, commonly known as the twin-leaf moraea, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to southern Africa.

This perennial herb is characterized by its elegant, sword-shaped leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in shades of purple and white. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant produces multiple flower stems, each bearing several blooms, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its botanical beauty and adaptability make it a valued ornamental plant in horticulture.
