Medinilla longidens

Medinilla longidens, commonly known as the long-stemmed Medinilla, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy, red or orange berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Medinilla longidens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
