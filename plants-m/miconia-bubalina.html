Miconia bubalina

Miconia bubalina, commonly known as the pink miconia, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a round, fleshy berry that turns red when mature.

Miconia bubalina is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and continuous flowering.
