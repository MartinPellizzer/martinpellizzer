Myrcia regeliana

Myrcia regeliana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Myrcia regeliana is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
