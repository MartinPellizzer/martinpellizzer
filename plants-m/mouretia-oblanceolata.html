Mouretia oblanceolata

Mouretia oblanceolata, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain flat, edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often grown for its ornamental value.

The leaves and seeds have been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
