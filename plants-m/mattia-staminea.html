Mattia staminea

Mattia staminea, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Mattia staminea is known for its rapid growth and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
