Mentha rudaeana var. ranina

Mentha rudaeana var. ranina, commonly known as the Alpine mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Mentha rudaeana var. ranina is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional herbal medicine.
