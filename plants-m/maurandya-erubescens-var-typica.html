Maurandya erubescens var. typica

Maurandya erubescens var. typica, commonly known as the scarlet mallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is a perennial shrub that grows to about 1-2 meters in height.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking colorful, heat-tolerant plants.
