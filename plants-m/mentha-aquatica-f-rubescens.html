Mentha aquatica f. rubescens

Mentha aquatica f. rubescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed water mint, is a variant of the water mint plant characterized by its distinctive reddish stems.

This plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist or wet environments such as marshes and stream sides. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, pale blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically aromatic and can be used in herbal teas or as a culinary herb.

It is a perennial plant that thrives in partial shade to full sun, depending on the moisture level of its surroundings.
