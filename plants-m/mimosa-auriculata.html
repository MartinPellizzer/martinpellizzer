Mimosa auriculata

Mimosa auriculata, commonly known as the golden mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a hardy species. Its flowers are typically arranged in spherical or cylindrical clusters, and the plant is often used in ornamental gardening.

Mimosa auriculata is also notable for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
