Myrtella bennigseniana

Myrtella bennigseniana, commonly known as the Bennigsen's myrtle, is a rare and ornamental plant belonging to the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
