Matthiola sinuata var. bracteata

Matthiola sinuata var. bracteata, commonly known as the bracted wallflower, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to yellow and pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features slender, branched stems covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is adapted to well-drained soils in full sun.
