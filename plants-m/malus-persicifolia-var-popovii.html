Malus persicifolia var. popovii

Malus persicifolia var. popovii, commonly known as the Popov's apple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its oval, serrated leaves and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, tart apples that are typically not suitable for eating raw but are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a hardy plant in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value lies in its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
