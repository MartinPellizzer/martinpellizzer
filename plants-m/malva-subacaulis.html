Malva subacaulis

Malva subacaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive wavy margin. The plant produces showy, pale pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed habitats.

Malva subacaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
