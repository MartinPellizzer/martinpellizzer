Melilotus strictus

Melilotus strictus, commonly known as narrowleaf melilot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and is valued for its nitrogen-fixing abilities and historical use in traditional medicine.
