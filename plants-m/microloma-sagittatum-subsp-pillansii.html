Microloma sagittatum subsp. pillansii

Microloma sagittatum subsp.

pillansii, commonly known as the Pillans' arrowhead, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features distinctive arrow-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its unique foliage and compact growth make it a desirable ornamental plant in gardens.
