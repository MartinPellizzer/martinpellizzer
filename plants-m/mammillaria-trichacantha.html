Mammillaria trichacantha

Mammillaria trichacantha, commonly known as the hooked mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem covered in white, woolly spines and small, rounded tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
