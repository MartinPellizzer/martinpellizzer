Monstera tablasensis

Monstera tablasensis, commonly known as the Mexican giant shield fern, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant has a climbing habit and produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers. Its foliage is often adorned with natural holes and splits, a feature known as fenestrations.

Monstera tablasensis thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and humidity to flourish.
