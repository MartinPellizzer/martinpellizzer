Maesa pusilliflora

Maesa pusilliflora, commonly known as the dwarf flame tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round, fleshy fruits that are edible and often consumed by local wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
