Miconia laxiflora

Miconia laxiflora, commonly known as the broadleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often attracting pollinators like birds and insects. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that contain numerous seeds.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is considered a problematic species in some regions.
