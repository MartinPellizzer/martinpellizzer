Medicago littoralis var. aegyptiaca

Medicago littoralis var. aegyptiaca, commonly known as the Egyptian goatweed, is a variant of the annual legume Medicago littoralis.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in coastal and saline environments, showing a high tolerance for salt and drought. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is often found in Mediterranean and Middle Eastern regions, where it thrives in disturbed soils and coastal dunes.
