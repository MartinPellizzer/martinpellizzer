Magnolia calophylla

Magnolia calophylla, commonly known as the Mexican magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Magnolia calophylla is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
