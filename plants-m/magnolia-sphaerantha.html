Magnolia sphaerantha

Magnolia sphaerantha, commonly known as the globe magnolia, is a rare and distinctive species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its spherical, flower-like inflorescence that resembles a closed globe, which opens as the flowers mature. The plant produces large, white, fragrant blooms that are highly ornamental and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are broad and glossy, contributing to its visual appeal in both garden and conservation settings.

Due to its limited natural range and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable but endangered species in botanical conservation efforts.
