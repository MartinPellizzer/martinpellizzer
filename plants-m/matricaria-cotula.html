Matricaria cotula

Matricaria cotula, commonly known as field chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers with white centers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as fields and roadsides. It has feathery, aromatic leaves and produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Matricaria cotula is often used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
