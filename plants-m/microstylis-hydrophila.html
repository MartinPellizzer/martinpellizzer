Microstylis hydrophila

Microstylis hydrophila, commonly known as the water microstylis, is a small aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in tropical regions.

It has delicate, thread-like leaves that float on the water surface, aiding in photosynthesis. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically submerged, contributing to its reproductive strategy. Its root system helps stabilize sediment and improve water clarity.

Microstylis hydrophila plays a minor but important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small aquatic life.
