Millettia ferruginea subsp. ferruginea

Millettia ferruginea subsp.

ferruginea, commonly known as the red-stemmed legume, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits.

The plant is noted for its distinctive reddish-brown stems and leaves with a rough, hairy texture. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing lens-shaped seeds.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fuelwood in its native habitats.
