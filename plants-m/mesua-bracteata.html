Mesua bracteata

Mesua bracteata, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
