Melica ciliata var. taurica

Melica ciliata var. taurica, commonly known as the Tauric milletgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as meadows, wetlands, and riverbanks. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves with a distinctive ciliate margin, giving it its scientific name. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing flowers.

This variety is often noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in ecological restoration projects.
