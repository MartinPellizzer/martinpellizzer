Memora lenta

Memora lenta, commonly known as the slow memory plant, is a rare species native to the temperate regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slow growth rate and unique foliage, which features waxy, dark green leaves with intricate veining. The plant produces small, delicate white flowers during the spring and summer months. Its botanical name, Memora lenta, reflects its slow growth and the notion that it "remembers" environmental conditions over time.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for study and conservation.
