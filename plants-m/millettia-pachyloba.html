Millettia pachyloba

Millettia pachyloba, commonly known as the thick-lobed millettia, is a flowering plant species belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. Millettia pachyloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
