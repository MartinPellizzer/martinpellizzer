Mabea montana subsp. biglandulosa

Mabea montana subsp.

biglandulosa, commonly known as the mountain mabea, is a perennial shrub native to the highlands of Mexico. It is characterized by its dense, woody stems and small, elliptical leaves with prominent glands.

The plant produces clusters of white to pale yellow flowers, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
