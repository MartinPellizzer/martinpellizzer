Mammillaria herrerae

Mammillaria herrerae, commonly known as the Herrera's pincushion cactus, is a small, globular cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, white spines and a compact, rounded shape. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 centimeters and features a ribbed stem. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Mammillaria herrerae is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a houseplant.
