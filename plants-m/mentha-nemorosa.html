Mentha nemorosa

Mentha nemorosa, commonly known as wood mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are often aromatic. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically violet or white, arranged in dense spikes.

Mentha nemorosa is known for its refreshing, minty scent and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
