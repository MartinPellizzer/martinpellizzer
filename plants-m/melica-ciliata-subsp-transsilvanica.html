Melica ciliata subsp. transsilvanica

Melica ciliata subsp.

transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with ciliate margins.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils, often in meadows and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
