Mammea glauca

Mammea glauca, commonly known as the white mammea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are typically followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.
