Mimosa deltoides

Mimosa deltoides, commonly known as the velvetleaf mimosa, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, featuring compound leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellow, spherical flowers that appear in clusters, usually in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in disturbed areas and along roadsides.

Mimosa deltoides is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative propagation.
