Maprounea africana var. orientalis

Maprounea africana var. orientalis, commonly known as the African maple or oriental maple, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that resemble those of maple trees, with five to seven lobes. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, spherical fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
