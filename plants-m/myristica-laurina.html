Myristica laurina

Myristica laurina, commonly known as the nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The tree produces small, fleshy fruits that enclose a single seed, which is used to make nutmeg and mace. Its aromatic oils are also used in perfumes and traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its economic and ecological significance in its native habitat.
