Myrica pringlei

Myrica pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's wax myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Myrica pringlei is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
