Malacothamnus densiflorus var. viscidus

Malacothamnus densiflorus var. viscidus, commonly known as the sticky goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is characterized by its dense, yellowish-orange inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has glandular hairs that give it a sticky texture, which is a distinguishing feature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in chaparral and coastal sage scrub habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
