Malva × durii

Malva × durii, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves and showy, pale pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves are adapted to thrive in disturbed soils and sunny environments.

It is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides, where it can become a weed due to its rapid growth and prolific seed production.
