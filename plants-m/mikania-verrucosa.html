Mikania verrucosa

Mikania verrucosa, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the morning, giving it its common name. It is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and forests.

Mikania verrucosa is also known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain regions.
