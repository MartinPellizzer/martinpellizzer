Mentha rotundata

Mentha rotundata, commonly known as round-leaf mint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its round, slightly hairy leaves that are often wrinkled and have a strong, minty aroma. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in summer, typically in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Mentha rotundata is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its aromatic properties.
