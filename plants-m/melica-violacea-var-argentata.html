Melica violacea var. argentata

Melica violacea var. argentata, commonly known as the silver millet grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and dense, silver-gray foliage due to the presence of fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically clustered in panicles. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and providing habitat for small wildlife.
