Melastoma dentatum

Melastoma dentatum, commonly known as the toothed melastoma, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves with serrated edges and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Melastoma dentatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
