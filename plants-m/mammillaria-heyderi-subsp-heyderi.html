Mammillaria heyderi subsp. heyderi

Mammillaria heyderi subsp.

heyderi, commonly known as the Heyder's hedgehog cactus, is a small, spineless cactus native to Mexico. It features a globular or cylindrical stem with numerous areoles, from which both spines and flowers emerge.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, and its flowers are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright light, making it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.

Due to its compact size and unique appearance, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in arid environments.
