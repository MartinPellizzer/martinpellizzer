Myrcia guajavifolia

Myrcia guajavifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or guajavifolia myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in humid, shaded environments.

Myrcia guajavifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
