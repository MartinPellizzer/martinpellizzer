Myrcia piauhiensis

Myrcia piauhiensis, commonly known as the Piauhi myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil, specifically the states of Piauí and Ceará, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom during the wet season. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

Myrcia piauhiensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
