Malvaviscus oaxacanus

Malvaviscus oaxacanus, commonly known as the Mexican mallow or red mallow, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its vibrant red or pink flowers, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
