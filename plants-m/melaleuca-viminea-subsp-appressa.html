Melaleuca viminea subsp. appressa

Melaleuca viminea subsp.

appressa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paperbark, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. Its bark is fibrous and peels in strips, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.
