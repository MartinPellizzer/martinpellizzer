Myrcia prismatica

Myrcia prismatica, commonly known as the purple myrtle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

Myrcia prismatica thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
