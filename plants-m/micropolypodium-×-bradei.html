Micropolypodium × bradei

Micropolypodium × bradei, commonly known as the hybrid rock fern, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Micropolypodium calycina and Micropolypodium scolopendrium.

It is a small, epiphytic fern that thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found growing on tree trunks or rocks. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and covered in fine, scale-like structures. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sorus, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Micropolypodium × bradei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
