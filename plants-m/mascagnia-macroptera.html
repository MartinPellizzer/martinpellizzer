Mascagnia macroptera

Mascagnia macroptera, commonly known as the giant nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its thick, woody stems and large, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by dark blue to black berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their potential antifungal and antibacterial properties.

The plant is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.
