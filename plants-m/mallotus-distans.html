Mallotus distans

Mallotus distans, commonly known as the Chinese holly or broadleaf holly, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant has simple, alternate, and leathery leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by red or orange berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and is often used in horticulture for its attractive foliage and seasonal berries.
