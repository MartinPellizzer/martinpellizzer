Miconia pulverata

Miconia pulverata, commonly known as the powdered miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers that are often covered in fine hairs. Its fruits are follicles that release tiny seeds dispersed by wind.

Miconia pulverata is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
