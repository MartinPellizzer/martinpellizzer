Menabea venenata

Menabea venenata, commonly known as the toxic menabea, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Menabiaceae and is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers. The plant is notable for containing toxic compounds, which can be harmful if ingested. Its leaves are typically broad and glossy, while its stems are woody and somewhat succulent.

Due to its toxicity, it is often cultivated with caution in horticultural settings.
