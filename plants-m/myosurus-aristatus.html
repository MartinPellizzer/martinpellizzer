Myosurus aristatus

Myosurus aristatus, commonly known as the spikeless willow or false willow, is a small, shrub-like plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its slender, often hairless stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in catkins.

Myosurus aristatus is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
