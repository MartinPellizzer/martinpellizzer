Mentha × dumetorum var. marchica

Mentha × dumetorum var. marchica, commonly known as "march mint," is a hybrid plant belonging to the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. This variety is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and is often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a strong, minty scent and is used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported digestive and calming properties.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread readily in suitable habitats.
