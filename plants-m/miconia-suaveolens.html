Miconia suaveolens

Miconia suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented miconia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains numerous seeds.

Miconia suaveolens is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
