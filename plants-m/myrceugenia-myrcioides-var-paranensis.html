Myrceugenia myrcioides var. paranensis

Myrceugenia myrcioides var. paranensis, commonly known as the Paraná myrtle, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of 3–6 meters. The plant features glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. Its small, fragrant white flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by edible, spherical berries.

The Paraná myrtle is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
