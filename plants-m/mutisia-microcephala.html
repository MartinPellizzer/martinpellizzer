Mutisia microcephala

Mutisia microcephala, commonly known as the small-headed mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with narrow, leathery leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with ray florets that give it a daisy-like appearance.

Mutisia microcephala is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
