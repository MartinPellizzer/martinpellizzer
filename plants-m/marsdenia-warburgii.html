Marsdenia warburgii

Marsdenia warburgii, commonly known as the Warburg's Marsdenia, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, and it is known for its fibrous, durable latex.

Marsdenia warburgii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
