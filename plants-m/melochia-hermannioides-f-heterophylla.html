Melochia hermannioides f. heterophylla

Melochia hermannioides f. heterophylla, commonly known as the variable leaf melochia, is a variant of the melochia genus native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, where the leaves exhibit significant variation in shape and size, often displaying a lobed or heart-shaped morphology. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, with smooth, slightly hairy stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are usually alternate, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, clustered in panicle-like inflorescences, and the plant is often cultivated for its unique leaf patterns and adaptability to various growing conditions.
