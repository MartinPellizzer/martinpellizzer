Mina hederifolia

Mina hederifolia, commonly known as the creeping wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows close to the ground, forming a dense mat of foliage. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
