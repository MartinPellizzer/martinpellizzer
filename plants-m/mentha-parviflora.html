Mentha parviflora

Mentha parviflora, commonly known as narrow-leaved mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to their minty fragrance.

Mentha parviflora is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes other well-known mint species.
