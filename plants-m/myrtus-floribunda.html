Myrtus floribunda

Myrtus floribunda, commonly known as the flowering myrtle, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, dark green leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white, fragrant flowers in spring, which are followed by small, leathery berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Myrtus floribunda is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical uses in perfumery and herbal remedies.
