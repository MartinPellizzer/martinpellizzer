Mikania banisteriae var. monagasensis

Mikania banisteriae var. monagasensis, commonly known as the Monagasensis mile-a-minute vine, is a fast-growing, herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. It is known for its aggressive growth habit, often forming dense mats that can smother other vegetation.

Due to its rapid spread, it is considered an invasive species in some regions where it is introduced.
