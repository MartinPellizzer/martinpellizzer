Mesosphaerum crassifolium

Mesosphaerum crassifolium, commonly known as the thick-leaved mesosphaerum, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to full sun exposure.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
