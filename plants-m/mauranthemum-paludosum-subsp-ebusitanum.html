Mauranthemum paludosum subsp. ebusitanum

Mauranthemum paludosum subsp.

ebusitanum, commonly known as the Ebusitan marsh mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Japan. It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. It has a low-growing habit with sprawling stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
