Myoporum st-johnii

Myoporum st-johnii, commonly known as the St. John's myoporum, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to sandy soils and salt-laden environments, making it a hardy coastal species.

Myoporum st-johnii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
