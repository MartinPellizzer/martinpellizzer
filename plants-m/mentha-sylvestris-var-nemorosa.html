Mentha sylvestris var. nemorosa

Mentha sylvestris var. nemorosa, commonly known as wood mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stem, typically blooming from June to August. Wood mint thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its essential oils contain menthol and other compounds that contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.
