Mertensia circinnata

Mertensia circinnata, commonly known as bluebells, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Mertensia circinnata is a perennial herb that plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
