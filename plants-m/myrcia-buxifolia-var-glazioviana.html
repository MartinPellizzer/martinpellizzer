Myrcia buxifolia var. glazioviana

Myrcia buxifolia var. glazioviana, commonly known as the Glaziov's myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are edible for certain bird species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
