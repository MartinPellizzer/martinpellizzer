Mariscus aureus

Mariscus aureus, commonly known as the golden mariscus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens and borders.
