Monechma leucoderme

Monechma leucoderme, commonly known as the white-stemmed monechma, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its root system is fibrous and contributes to soil stabilization in its native environment.

Monechma leucoderme is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in forest ecosystems.
