Malva albens

Malva albens, commonly known as white mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in disturbed areas.

Malva albens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its soothing properties.
