Mentha × kerneri var. haynaldiana

Mentha × kerneri var. haynaldiana, commonly known as haynaldian mint, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly square stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other mint species, and is often used in herbal teas and as a flavoring agent.

This variety is also noted for its hardiness and adaptability to a range of growing conditions.
