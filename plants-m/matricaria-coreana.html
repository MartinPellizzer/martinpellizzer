Matricaria coreana

Matricaria coreana, commonly known as Korean chamomile, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with white centers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are hairy and serrated, while the flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Matricaria coreana is often used in traditional medicine for its calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
