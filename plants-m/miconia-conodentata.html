Miconia conodentata

Miconia conodentata, commonly known as the red miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green with a smooth texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are fleshy berries that turn red when mature, attracting various bird species.

Miconia conodentata is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
