Mentha × schultzii

Mentha × schultzii, commonly known as Schultze mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or blue, typically arranged in whorls along the stems. Mentha × schultzii is valued for its fragrant leaves, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and aromatic gardening.
