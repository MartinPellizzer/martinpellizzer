Myrceugenia bracteosa var. seriatopedunculata

Myrceugenia bracteosa var. seriatopedunculata, commonly known as the variable myrtle, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, which bloom in clusters during the spring. Its fruit is a small, spherical berry that changes color from green to red as it matures.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
