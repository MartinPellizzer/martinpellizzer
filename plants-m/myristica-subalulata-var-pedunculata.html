Myristica subalulata var. pedunculata

Myristica subalulata var. pedunculata, commonly known as the fragrant nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody fruits that contain the spice nutmeg. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by oblong, fleshy fruits that turn red when mature. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

The tree is valued for its nutmeg and mace, which are harvested from its seeds and aril, respectively.
