Melicope magnifolia

Melicope magnifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf melicope, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by orange-red berries. Its bark is thick and rugged, often exuding a strong, aromatic scent.

Melicope magnifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
