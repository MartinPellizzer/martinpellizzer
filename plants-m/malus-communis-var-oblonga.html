Malus communis var. oblonga

Malus communis var. oblonga, commonly known as the oblong apple, is a variant of the domesticated apple.

It is characterized by its elongated, oval-shaped fruits that are typically smaller than standard apples. The plant is a deciduous tree with a rounded crown and produces white flowers in the spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth margin.

This variety is valued for its fruit quality and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
