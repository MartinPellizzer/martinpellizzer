Meconopsis integrifolia subsp. integrifolia

Meconopsis integrifolia subsp.

integrifolia, commonly known as the Welsh poppy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Wales and western England. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in early summer.

The plant features basal leaves that are typically heart-shaped and have smooth, entire margins. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland and streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is cultivated in gardens and protected in the wild.
