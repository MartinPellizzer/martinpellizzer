Mesochlaena asplenioides

Mesochlaena asplenioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically less than 30 cm in length. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and wetlands. Its rhizomes are often submerged, anchoring the plant and facilitating the spread of its spore-producing structures.

Mesochlaena asplenioides plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small invertebrates and contributing to water quality through its natural filtration properties.
