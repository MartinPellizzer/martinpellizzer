Monarda fistulosa var. maheuxii

Monarda fistulosa var. maheuxii, commonly known as the Maheux's bee balm, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces showy, tubular red or pink flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a fragrant, mint-like scent.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in gardens.
