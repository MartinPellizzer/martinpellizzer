Macrozanonia macrocarpa

Macrozanonia macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant cup fern, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can grow up to several meters in length, making it a notable presence in its natural habitat. The plant produces distinctive, cup-shaped spore cases called sporangia, which are clustered in dense groups along the undersides of its fronds. Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in the study of tropical flora.

Macrozanonia macrocarpa thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks in rainforests.
