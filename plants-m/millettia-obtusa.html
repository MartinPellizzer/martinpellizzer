Millettia obtusa

Millettia obtusa, commonly known as the Chinese tallow tree, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its alternate, pinnate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other parts of the world, sometimes becoming an invasive species.

Its botanical features make it an important subject in both ecological and horticultural studies.
