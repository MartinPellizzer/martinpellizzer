Massonia pedunculata

Massonia pedunculata, commonly known as the African ice plant, is a succulent perennial native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and drought tolerance.

Massonia pedunculata is also valued for its ability to prevent soil erosion in rocky or sandy soils.
