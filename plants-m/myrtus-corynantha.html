Myrtus corynantha

Myrtus corynantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a shrub native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves that are typically narrow and dark green. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by elliptical, dark blue to black berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Myrtus corynantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in Indigenous Australian cultures.
