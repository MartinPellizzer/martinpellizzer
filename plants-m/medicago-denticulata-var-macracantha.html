Medicago denticulata var. macracantha

Medicago denticulata var. macracantha, commonly known as the broadleaf sainfoin, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant is characterized by its robust root system and ability to thrive in well-drained soils. It is often used in forage and soil stabilization due to its hardiness and nitrogen-fixing capabilities.

Its botanical structure supports its role in ecological and agricultural systems.
