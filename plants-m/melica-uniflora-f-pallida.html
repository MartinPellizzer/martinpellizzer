Melica uniflora f. pallida

Melica uniflora f. pallida, commonly known as the pale meadowgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial species characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish inflorescences, which are typically composed of a single spikelet, giving it its specific epithet "uniflora.". It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands, often found in temperate regions.

Its pale coloration and unique floral structure make it a distinctive and ecologically important component of its native ecosystems.
