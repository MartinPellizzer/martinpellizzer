Myrcia riparia

Myrcia riparia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it is considered a problematic species in some regions.
