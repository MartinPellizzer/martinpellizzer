Moraea tricuspis

Moraea tricuspis, commonly known as the three-lobed glory, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, sword-shaped leaves and showy, three-lobed flowers. The flowers are typically purple or violet in color and bloom in late summer or early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
