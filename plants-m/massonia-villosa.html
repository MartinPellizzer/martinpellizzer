Massonia villosa

Massonia villosa, commonly known as the woolly massonia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, cylindrical stems covered in soft, white, woolly hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are thick and succulent, adapted for storing water, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Massonia villosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
