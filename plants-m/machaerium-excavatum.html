Machaerium excavatum

Machaerium excavatum, commonly known as the hollow legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet having a distinctive hollow midrib, giving it its common name.

The flowers are usually small and clustered, ranging in color from white to pink, and are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits.
