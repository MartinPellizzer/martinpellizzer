Mammillaria miqueliana

Mammillaria miqueliana, commonly known as the Mexican pincushion cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a ribbed stem and a distinctive crown of white, woolly spines. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 centimeters and features pale green to blue-gray foliage. It produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
