Mascarenhasia arborescens var. anceps

Mascarenhasia arborescens var. anceps, commonly known as the Mascarene tree, is a tropical plant native to the Mascarenhas Islands.

It is characterized by its woody structure and large, leathery leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the local ecosystem and cultural practices of the region.
