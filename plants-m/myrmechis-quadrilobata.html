Myrmechis quadrilobata

Myrmechis quadrilobata, commonly known as the four-lobed mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that grows on the branches of eucalyptus trees in Australia.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are typically red or yellow and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

The plant plays an important ecological role in supporting native wildlife, particularly birds that feed on its berries.
