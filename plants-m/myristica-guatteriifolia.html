Myristica guatteriifolia

Myristica guatteriifolia, commonly known as the Guatteria myristica, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Myristicaceae, which includes several species of aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain aromatic seeds, which are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in the study of tropical flora and its ecological roles.
