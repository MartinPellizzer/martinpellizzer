Malva elata

Malva elata, commonly known as the common mallow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and sunny to partially shaded locations. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
