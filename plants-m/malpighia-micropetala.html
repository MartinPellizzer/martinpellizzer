Malpighia micropetala

Malpighia micropetala, commonly known as the dwarf granadilla, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its clustered, bright red berries that resemble small grapes. The plant produces small, oval leaves with a glossy surface and has a compact growth habit. It thrives in tropical climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

The berries are edible, though they are typically used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
