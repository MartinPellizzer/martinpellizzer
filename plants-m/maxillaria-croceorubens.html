Maxillaria croceorubens

Maxillaria croceorubens, commonly known as the red-orange orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from orange to red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, warm environments. Its inflorescence consists of multiple flowers arranged in a spike, with each bloom featuring a distinctive, recurved lip.

The species is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.

Morphology

Maxillaria croceorubens has leaves that are elliptical in shape, arranged in a basal rosette, with a dark green color and a leathery texture.

The stem is short and thick, with a smooth, pale green bark that grows upright. Its flowers are complex, featuring a yellowish-orange lip and a white to pinkish upper part, blooming during the summer months and pollinated primarily by bees. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, black seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring the plant in moist, well-drained soil.
