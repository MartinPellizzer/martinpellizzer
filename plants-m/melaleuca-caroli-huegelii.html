Melaleuca caroli-huegelii

Melaleuca caroli-huegelii, commonly known as the Caroli's melaleuca, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its slender, erect shrub form with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, bottle-shaped flowers that are typically pale yellow to cream in color, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Melaleuca caroli-huegelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
