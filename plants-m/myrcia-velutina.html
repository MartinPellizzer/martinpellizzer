Myrcia velutina

Myrcia velutina, commonly known as velvet myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Myrcia velutina thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in humid, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species in botanical studies.
