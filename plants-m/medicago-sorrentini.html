Medicago sorrentini

Medicago sorrentini, commonly known as the Sorrentine clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is typically found in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

Medicago sorrentini is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
