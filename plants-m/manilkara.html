Manilkara

Manilkara, commonly known as the sapodilla, is a tropical tree belonging to the Sapotaceae family.

It is native to the Americas, particularly Central and South America, and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces a fruit with a smooth, yellowish skin and a sweet, creamy pulp. The sapodilla tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and has a long lifespan, often living for several decades.

Its wood is highly valued for its durability and fine grain, making it useful in construction and furniture-making.
