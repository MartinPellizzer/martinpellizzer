Miconia rubiginosa var. platyura

Miconia rubiginosa var. platyura, commonly known as the broad-leaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by dark, berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, which can make it an invasive species in certain regions.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of tropical ecosystems, though its spread can have ecological impacts.
