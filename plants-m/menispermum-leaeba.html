Menispermum leaeba

Menispermum leaeba, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowroot, is a perennial climbing plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a toxic, milky sap that contains various alkaloids, which can be harmful if ingested. Its roots are sometimes used in traditional medicine, though caution is advised due to their potent nature.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.
