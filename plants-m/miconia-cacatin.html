Miconia cacatin

Miconia cacatin, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that are followed by distinctive, winged fruits. Miconia cacatin is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a common invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and challenging to manage in natural ecosystems.
