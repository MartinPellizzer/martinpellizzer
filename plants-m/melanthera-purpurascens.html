Melanthera purpurascens

Melanthera purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, purple-to-red inflorescences. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and its flowers are arranged in dense, conical clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Melanthera purpurascens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
