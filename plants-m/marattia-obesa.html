Marattia obesa

Marattia obesa, commonly known as the fiddlehead fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fiddlehead-shaped young fronds that curl tightly before unfurling. The plant has a tall, slender trunk that is covered in a dense mass of roots and rhizomes. Its fronds are bioluminescent, emitting a faint glow in the dark, which is a unique adaptation for attracting nocturnal pollinators.

This rare and unusual plant is often found in humid, shaded environments near streams or in dense forests.
