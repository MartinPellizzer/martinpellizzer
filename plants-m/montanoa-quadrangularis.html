Montanoa quadrangularis

Montanoa quadrangularis, commonly known as the quadrangular montanoa, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems that are often quadrangular in cross-section. The plant produces small, yellowish to orange flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Montanoa quadrangularis is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
