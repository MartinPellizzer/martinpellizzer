Menispermum cotoneaster

Menispermum cotoneaster, commonly known as the Indian mulberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Menispermaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically orange or red in color, though they are not commonly consumed by humans. Its botanical features include a woody stem, palmately lobed leaves, and a distinctive, aromatic scent.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
