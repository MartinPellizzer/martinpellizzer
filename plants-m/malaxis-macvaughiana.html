Malaxis macvaughiana

Malaxis macvaughiana, commonly known as the MacVaugh's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils under shady forest conditions. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it conserve moisture.

Malaxis macvaughiana is a rare and endangered species, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
