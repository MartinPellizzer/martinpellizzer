Mammillaria albicans f. fraileana

Mammillaria albicans f. fraileana, commonly known as the White-Flowered Mammillaria, is a rare and distinctive cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its pale green to white stems, which are covered in small, white tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its pale foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its rarity and specific care requirements, it is a popular choice among experienced cactus enthusiasts.
