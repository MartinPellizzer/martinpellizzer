Matricaria anomala

Matricaria anomala, commonly known as the common tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and is often found in moist, shaded areas. It produces small, dark seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Matricaria anomala is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing benefits.
