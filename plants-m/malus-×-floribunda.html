Malus × floribunda

Malus × floribunda, commonly known as the weeping apple or weeping crabapple, is a deciduous ornamental tree native to China.

It is characterized by its cascading branches that create a distinctive weeping appearance, with small, showy flowers in spring and colorful foliage in autumn. The tree produces small, edible apples that are typically not suitable for consumption but are used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in USDA hardiness zones 4 through 8 and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
