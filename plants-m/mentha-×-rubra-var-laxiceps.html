Mentha × rubra var. laxiceps

Mentha × rubra var. laxiceps, commonly known as red mint, is a hybrid species within the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its reddish-pink flowers and aromatic, slightly minty scent. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb with upright, square stems and opposite leaves. Its foliage is often a deep green with a slight reddish tinge, especially on the underside.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in herbal teas or as a culinary herb.
