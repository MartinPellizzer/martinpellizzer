Mentha gentilis var. gracilis

Mentha gentilis var. gracilis, commonly known as slender mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are narrow and have a mild, refreshing mint scent, making it useful in culinary and aromatic applications.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
