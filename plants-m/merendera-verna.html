Merendera verna

Merendera verna, commonly known as the spring merendera, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Merendera verna is often found in moist, sunny habitats like meadows and woodland edges.
