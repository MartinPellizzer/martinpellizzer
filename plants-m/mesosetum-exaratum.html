Mesosetum exaratum

Mesosetum exaratum, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands and along the edges of marshes, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is well adapted to wetland environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.

Due to its ecological importance, it is often found in conservation and restoration projects.
