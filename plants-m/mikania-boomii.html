Mikania boomii

Mikania boomii, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and slender, hairy stems. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple, opening in the morning and closing at dusk. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

Mikania boomii is often used in traditional medicine and is also popular as an ornamental plant due to its attractive blooms.
