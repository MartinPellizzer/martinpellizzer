Matayba juglandifolia

Matayba juglandifolia, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a large evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces large, woody fruits that contain multiple seeds, known as Brazil nuts. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall and has a dense, rugged bark. It thrives in humid, tropical climates with high rainfall and is an important species for both ecological and economic reasons.

The seeds are rich in nutrients and are highly valued in international markets.
