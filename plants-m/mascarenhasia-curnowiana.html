Mascarenhasia curnowiana

Mascarenhasia curnowiana, commonly known as the Mascarene daisy, is a flowering plant native to the Mascarene Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to salty, well-drained soils. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous fruits.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
