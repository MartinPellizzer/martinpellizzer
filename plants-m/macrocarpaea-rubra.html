Macrocarpaea rubra

Macrocarpaea rubra, commonly known as the red-flowered macrocarpaea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, hairy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant floral display and compact size.
