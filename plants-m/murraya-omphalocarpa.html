Murraya omphalocarpa

Murraya omphalocarpa, commonly known as the sacred fig or Indian banyan, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round, orange-red fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid climates and is often found growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Murraya omphalocarpa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and cultural significance in various regions.
