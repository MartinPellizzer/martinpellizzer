Medicago laciniata subsp. schimperiana

Medicago laciniata subsp.

schimperiana, commonly known as the Schimper's narrow-leaf medick, is a perennial legume native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its finely divided, narrow leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in early summer.

The plant produces small, round pods containing several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind and animals. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, and is often found in dry, rocky environments.

As a member of the Fabaceae family, it plays a role in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility.
