Manihot crassisepala

Manihot crassisepala, commonly known as the broad-sepaled manioc, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its diverse and often milky sap. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins and can grow up to several meters in height. Its flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for its starchy root, though it is less commonly used than other manioc species.
