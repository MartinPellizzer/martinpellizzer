Micranthemum trisetosum

Micranthemum trisetosum, commonly known as narrowleaf water mallow, is a small aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in shallow water, such as ponds, marshes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are tiny and clustered, usually in dense inflorescences, and are typically greenish-white in color.

Micranthemum trisetosum is often used in water gardens and as a decorative plant in aquariums due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in aquatic environments.
