Marshallia trinervia

Marshallia trinervia, commonly known as the prairie coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in prairies and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Marshallia trinervia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
