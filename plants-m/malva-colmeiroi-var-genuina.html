Malva colmeiroi var. genuina

Malva colmeiroi var. genuina, commonly known as the Canary Islands mallows, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pentagonal flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, adapting to the island's unique climate and soil conditions. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
