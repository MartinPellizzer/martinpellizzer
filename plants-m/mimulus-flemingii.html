Mimulus flemingii

Mimulus flemingii, commonly known as the Fleming's monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as streambanks and meadows. The plant produces striking orange-yellow flowers with two lips, characteristic of the genus Mimulus. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Mimulus flemingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a pollinator attractant.
