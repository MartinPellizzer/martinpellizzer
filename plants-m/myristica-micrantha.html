Myristica micrantha

Myristica micrantha, commonly known as the small-fruited nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody structure and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces berries that contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a glossy surface.

The species is valued for its unique chemical compounds, including myristicin and elemicin, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.
