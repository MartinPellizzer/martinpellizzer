Micronychia acuminata

Micronychia acuminata, commonly known as the slender leaf lichen, is a species of lichen that belongs to the family Lecanoraceae.

It is characterized by its crustose growth form, forming a thin, adherent layer on rock surfaces, typically in shaded, moist environments. The thallus is usually pale to dark yellowish in color, with a granular texture and distinct lobes. It contains a variety of secondary metabolites, including usnic acid and atranorin, which contribute to its chemical complexity.

This lichen is often found in temperate regions and is used in ecological studies to monitor air quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.
