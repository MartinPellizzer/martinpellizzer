Melastoma paucidens

Melastoma paucidens, commonly known as the small-flowered melastoma, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, with five petals and prominent stamens. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release seeds.

Melastoma paucidens is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
