Marantochloa congensis

Marantochloa congensis, commonly known as the Congo arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family, which includes a variety of ornamental and edible plants. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikes.

Marantochloa congensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential use in traditional medicine.
