Mertensia brachycalyx

Mertensia brachycalyx, commonly known as the short-calyx bluebells, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of sky-blue, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Mertensia brachycalyx is valued in gardens for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits to pollinators.
