Microchilus stigmatopterus

Microchilus stigmatopterus, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowers are clustered in inflorescences.

Microchilus stigmatopterus is noted for its unique floral structure, which includes a prominently marked lip that aids in pollination by specific insect species.
