Mentha × niliacea

Mentha × niliacea, commonly known as hybrid mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant that belongs to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Mentha longifolia (spearmint) and Mentha aquatica (watermint). The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Mentha × niliacea is often found in wet or moist environments, such as stream banks and marshy areas.

It is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
