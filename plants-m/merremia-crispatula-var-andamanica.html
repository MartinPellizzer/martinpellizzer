Merremia crispatula var. andamanica

Merremia crispatula var. andamanica, commonly known as the Andaman morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the Andaman Islands.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is characterized by its tendrils that aid in climbing and its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release its seeds, contributing to its natural spread.
