Maxillaria scalariformis

Maxillaria scalariformis, commonly known as the scalloped orchid, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces dark green, velvety leaves and clusters of pale yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive scalloped pattern on the petals. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Maxillaria scalariformis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a home setting.
