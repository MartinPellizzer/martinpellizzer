Myrceugenia cucullata var. acutata

Myrceugenia cucullata var. acutata, commonly known as the acute-leaved myrtle, is a shrub native to the temperate rainforests of southern Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves with acute tips, and it produces small, clustered white flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
