Malvaviscus arboreus var. sepium

Malvaviscus arboreus var. sepium, commonly known as the scarlet bower vine, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and dense, trailing habit, often used as a climbing plant in gardens. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom throughout the warmer months. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.

The plant thrives in humid, sunny conditions and is well-suited for tropical and subtropical climates.
