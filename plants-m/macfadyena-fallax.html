Macfadyena fallax

Macfadyena fallax, commonly known as the Crown of Thorns, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features woody stems with thorn-like projections, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

It is well adapted to arid environments, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.
