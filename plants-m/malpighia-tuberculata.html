Malpighia tuberculata

Malpighia tuberculata, commonly known as the granadilla, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that produces small, bright red berries that are rich in vitamin C. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are typically pink or white, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

The fruit is often used in juices, desserts, and as a natural remedy due to its nutritional and medicinal properties.
