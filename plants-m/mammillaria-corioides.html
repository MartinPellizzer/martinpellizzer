Mammillaria corioides

Mammillaria corioides, commonly known as the woolly mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in late spring or early summer.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
