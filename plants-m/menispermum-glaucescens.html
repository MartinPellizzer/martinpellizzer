Menispermum glaucescens

Menispermum glaucescens, commonly known as the blue cohosh, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Menispermaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with three leaflets. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. Its berries are red or orange when mature and contain several seeds.

The plant is often found in moist woodlands and is valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
