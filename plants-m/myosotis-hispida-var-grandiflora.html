Myosotis hispida var. grandiflora

Myosotis hispida var. grandiflora, commonly known as the hairy forget-me-not, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it its species name "hispida.". The plant produces clusters of small, blue or purple flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a similar hairy texture.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.
