Mirabilis albida

Mirabilis albida, commonly known as the white four o'clock, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Nyctaginaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that open in the late afternoon. Mirabilis albida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
