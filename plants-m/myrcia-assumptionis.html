Myrcia assumptionis

Myrcia assumptionis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

The fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that contains a single seed.
