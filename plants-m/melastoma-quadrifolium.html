Melastoma quadrifolium

Melastoma quadrifolium, commonly known as the four-leaf melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a distinctive four-leaf clover-like appearance, which contributes to its common name.

Melastoma quadrifolium is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
