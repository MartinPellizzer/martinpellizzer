Mormodes atropurpurea

Mormodes atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple-flowered orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its striking, violet-pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and a moderate growth habit. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
