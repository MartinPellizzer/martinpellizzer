Mammea malayana

Mammea malayana, commonly known as the Malay apple or wild apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by fleshy, orange-red berries that contain several seeds. Its fruit is edible, though it has a slightly tart flavor, and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
