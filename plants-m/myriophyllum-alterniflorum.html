Myriophyllum alterniflorum

Myriophyllum alterniflorum, commonly known as watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has slender, branching stems that are often covered in small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically found in clusters near the water's surface. It is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as lakes and ponds.

Watermilfoil plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
