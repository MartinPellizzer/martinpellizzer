Myristica tomentella

Myristica tomentella, commonly known as the hairy nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces a unique fruit that contains a dark, aromatic seed known as the nutmeg-like spice. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental and potential medicinal value, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens and conservation programs.
