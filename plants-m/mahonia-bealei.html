Mahonia bealei

Mahonia bealei, commonly known as the Beale Mahonia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features compound leaves that are typically alternate and have a leathery texture with spiny margins. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are followed by blue-black berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
