Myrciaria linearifolia

Myrciaria linearifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a small evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The leaves and fruits are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
