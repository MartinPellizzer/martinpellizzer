Microstylis longispica

Microstylis longispica, commonly known as the long-spined microstylis, is a species of flowering plant in the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that are typically green or pale in color. It is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its delicate structure and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
