Myriophyllum amphibium var. latifolium

Myriophyllum amphibium var. latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has slender, branching stems that are often found in shallow lakes and ponds. The plant produces small, green to reddish flowers that float on the water surface. Its leaves are opposite, broad, and oval-shaped, with a smooth margin.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
