Melampodium divaricatum

Melampodium divaricatum, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and wetlands. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces bright yellow flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
