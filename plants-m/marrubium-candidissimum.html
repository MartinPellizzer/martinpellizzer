Marrubium candidissimum

Marrubium candidissimum, commonly known as white horehound, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features hairy, gray-green leaves and produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in clusters. The plant is characterized by its strong, bitter scent and is often found in rocky or dry soils. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its medicinal properties.

Marrubium candidissimum is used in traditional medicine for its expectorant and anti-inflammatory effects.
