Melastoma glandulosum

Melastoma glandulosum, commonly known as the glossy evergreen shrub, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of pink or white, which bloom during the rainy season. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Melastoma glandulosum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
