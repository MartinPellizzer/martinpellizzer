Misanteca areolata

Misanteca areolata, commonly known as the spotted spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise rugged appearance. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which reduces water loss and protects against intense sunlight.

Misanteca areolata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in low-maintenance conditions.
