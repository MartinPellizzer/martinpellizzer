Medicago bondevii

Medicago bondevii, commonly known as the Bondevi sainfoin, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Medicago bondevii is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in ecological restoration and sustainable agriculture.
