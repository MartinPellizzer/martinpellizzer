Margotia laserpitioides

Margotia laserpitioides, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible seeds.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
