Mentha × grazensis

Mentha × grazensis, commonly known as the German mint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Mentha pulegium and Mentha longifolia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated edge. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in whorled clusters during the summer months. Mentha × grazensis is native to Central Europe and is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.
