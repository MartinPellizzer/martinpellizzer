Mammillaria cornifera var. mutica

Mammillaria cornifera var. mutica, commonly known as the fuzzy nipple cactus, is a compact, globular cactus native to Mexico.

It features a spiny, segmented stem covered in dense, white, woolly hairs that give it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
