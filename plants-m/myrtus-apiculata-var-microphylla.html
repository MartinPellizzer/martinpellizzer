Myrtus apiculata var. microphylla

Myrtus apiculata var. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves that are typically less than 1 centimeter in length. The plant produces tiny, fragrant white flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, black berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental berries make it both a useful and attractive plant in horticulture.
