Miconia karsticola

Miconia karsticola, commonly known as the karst miconia, is a species native to the limestone-rich regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. It produces berries that are often consumed by local wildlife.

Due to its restricted habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
