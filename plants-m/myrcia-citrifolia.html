Myrcia citrifolia

Myrcia citrifolia, commonly known as Surinam cherry, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright red, edible berries that are rich in vitamin C and have a tart, citrus-like flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Myrcia citrifolia is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
