Mentha × dumetorum var. melancholica

Mentha × dumetorum var. melancholica, commonly known as black horehound, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers and hairy, glandular leaves that emit a strong, minty aroma. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cooler, moist environments. It is often found in alpine meadows and is known for its medicinal properties, including potential antispasmodic and digestive benefits.

Its distinct appearance and aromatic qualities make it a notable species within the mint family.
