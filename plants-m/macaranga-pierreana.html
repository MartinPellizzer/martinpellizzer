Macaranga pierreana

Macaranga pierreana, commonly known as the Pierre's makaranga, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often hairy leaves, which can vary in shape from ovate to elliptic. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
