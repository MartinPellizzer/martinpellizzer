Mitostigma niveum

Mitostigma niveum, commonly known as the white-stemmed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces tiny, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in small inflorescences. Its berries are typically red or yellow and contain a single seed.

Mitostigma niveum is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
