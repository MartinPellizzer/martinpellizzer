Malcolmia parviflora var. brachypoda

Malcolmia parviflora var. brachypoda, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 5 cm in length. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to gardens and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
