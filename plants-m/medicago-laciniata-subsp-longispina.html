Medicago laciniata subsp. longispina

Medicago laciniata subsp.

longispina, commonly known as the longspined sicklevetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and trifoliate leaves with elongated, serrated leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its nitrogen-fixing ability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration and as a forage plant.
