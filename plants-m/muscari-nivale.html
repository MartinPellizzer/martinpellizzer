Muscari nivale

Muscari nivale, commonly known as the snow muscari, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. The plant produces dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. It is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Muscari nivale is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
