Murraya paniculata var. exotica

Murraya paniculata var. exotica, commonly known as the Christmas rose or white coral tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in tropical and subtropical regions.
