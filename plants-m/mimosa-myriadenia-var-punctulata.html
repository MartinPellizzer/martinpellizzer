Mimosa myriadenia var. punctulata

Mimosa myriadenia var. punctulata, commonly known as the spotted mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is known for its sensitive response to touch, causing the leaves to fold shut when disturbed. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The varietal name "punctulata" refers to the small, dotted patterns on its leaves, which add to its visual appeal.
