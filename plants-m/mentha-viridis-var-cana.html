Mentha viridis var. cana

Mentha viridis var. cana, commonly known as green mint or variegated mint, is a variant of the common mint species.

It is characterized by its distinctive variegated leaves that display shades of green and white or yellow. This plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments. It produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that appear in the summer months.

Mentha viridis var. cana is often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to its aromatic properties.
