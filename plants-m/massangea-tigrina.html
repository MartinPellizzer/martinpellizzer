Massangea tigrina

Massangea tigrina, commonly known as the tiger vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and distinctive, waxy, yellow flowers with purple veins. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in rainforests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
