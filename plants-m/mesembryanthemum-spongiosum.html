Mesembryanthemum spongiosum

Mesembryanthemum spongiosum, commonly known as the spongeplant or rock rose, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, crinkled leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in the early morning and close by midday. It is often found growing on rocky outcrops and in sandy soils.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
