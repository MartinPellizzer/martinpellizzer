Melodinus silvaticus

Melodinus silvaticus, commonly known as the wild melodine, is a tropical flowering plant belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

It is characterized by its attractive, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant is native to regions in Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are ovate and glossy, and it produces small, elliptical fruits that contain seeds.

Melodinus silvaticus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
