Miscanthus oligostachyus var. intermedius

Miscanthus oligostachyus var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate Chinese silver grass, is a variant of the Miscanthus genus native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and feathery, silver-green plumes that add ornamental value to gardens. This plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wetlands, and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense clumps, making it a useful plant for erosion control.

The species is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and providing habitat for small wildlife.
