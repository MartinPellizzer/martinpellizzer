Myrcia hostmanniana var. robustior

Myrcia hostmanniana var. robustior, commonly known as the robust myrcia, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, tropical environments. The plant features elliptical to ovate leaves with a glossy, dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, and are usually pink or red, attracting pollinators like bees and birds.

The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed, contributing to its ecological role in the region.
