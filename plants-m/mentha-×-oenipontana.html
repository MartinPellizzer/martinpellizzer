Mentha × oenipontana

Mentha × oenipontana, commonly known as the hybrid mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a sterile hybrid resulting from the cross between Mentha spicata (spearmint) and Mentha viridis (ovalleaf mint). The plant is characterized by its fragrant, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Mentha × oenipontana is often used in culinary and aromatic applications due to its aromatic properties.
