Mentha × rotundifolia f. parviflora

Mentha × rotundifolia f. parviflora, commonly known as narrow-leaved mint, is a hybrid plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its round or nearly round leaves, which distinguish it from other mint species. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. It produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and is used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.
