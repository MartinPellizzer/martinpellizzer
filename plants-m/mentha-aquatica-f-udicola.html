Mentha aquatica f. udicola

Mentha aquatica f. udicola, commonly known as the water mint, is a variant of the water mint species native to moist and wet environments.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in wetlands, stream banks, and other aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers are often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.
