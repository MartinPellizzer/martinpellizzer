Megacarpaea megalocarpa

Megacarpaea megalocarpa, commonly known as the giant seed plant, is a unique species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, woody structure and produces massive, heavy fruits that can weigh several kilograms. The plant's botanical features include thick, fibrous bark and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in dense inflorescences.

The fruit, which is a type of follicle, contains a single large seed and plays a crucial role in the plant's reproductive strategy.
