Moehringia linearifolia

Moehringia linearifolia, commonly known as the slenderleaf moehringia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette formation, and it produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.

Moehringia linearifolia is also known for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
