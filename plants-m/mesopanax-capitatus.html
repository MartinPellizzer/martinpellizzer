Mesopanax capitatus

Mesopanax capitatus, commonly known as the clustered sycamore, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its smooth, gray bark and palmately compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by woody, spherical fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional medicine.
