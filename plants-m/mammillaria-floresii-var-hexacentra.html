Mammillaria floresii var. hexacentra

Mammillaria floresii var. hexacentra, commonly known as the Mexican star cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a dense covering of white to pale blue spines. The plant typically produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its spiny exterior. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
