Melica persica f. inaequiglumis

Melica persica f. inaequiglumis, commonly known as Persian millet grass, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is a variant of Melica persica, characterized by its distinctive leaf and inflorescence morphology. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
