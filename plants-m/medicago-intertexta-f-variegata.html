Medicago intertexta f. variegata

Medicago intertexta f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated goat's rue, is a variant of the common goat's rue, characterized by its striking variegated leaf patterns.

This perennial herb belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet displaying a mix of green and white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by elongated pods containing several seeds.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
