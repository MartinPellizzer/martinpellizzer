Mikania stuebelii var. latifolia

Mikania stuebelii var. latifolia, commonly known as giant wisteria, is a vigorous climbing vine native to South America.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 15 cm in length, contributing to its striking appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive if not managed properly.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its fibrous roots and pinnate leaf structure.
