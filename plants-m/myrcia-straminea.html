Myrcia straminea

Myrcia straminea, commonly known as the yellow myrtle, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, small white or yellow flowers and leathery, elliptical leaves. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that contain aromatic seeds.

Myrcia straminea is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
