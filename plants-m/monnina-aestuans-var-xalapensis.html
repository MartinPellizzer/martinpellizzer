Monnina aestuans var. xalapensis

Monnina aestuans var. xalapensis, commonly known as the coastal bluestem, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its succulent, ribbed stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along dunes and shorelines, showing adaptations to salt spray and wind. Its foliage is often bluish-green, and it produces bright pink to reddish flowers in late spring to early summer.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance in stabilizing coastal habitats.
