Mentha procera

Mentha procera, commonly known as tall mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and slender, aromatic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems. Mentha procera is a member of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic oils and medicinal properties.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
