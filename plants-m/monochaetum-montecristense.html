Monochaetum montecristense

Monochaetum montecristense, commonly known as the Montecristo spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the island of Montecristo in the Galápagos Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces long, narrow spikelets that are often adorned with a single bristle at the tip. The plant thrives in coastal and rocky habitats, adapted to the unique climate and soil conditions of its island home. It plays a role in stabilizing the local ecosystem and is of interest to botanists due to its endemic status.

Its morphology reflects evolutionary adaptations to the harsh environmental conditions of the Galápagos.
