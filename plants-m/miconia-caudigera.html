Miconia caudigera

Miconia caudigera, commonly known as the Brazilian star apple, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or red. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces edible, yellow-orange fruits that are often consumed raw or cooked.

It is also known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.
