Menziesia empetriformis

Menziesia empetriformis, commonly known as the creeping menziesia, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded, forested areas. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, with a dense, mat-like growth habit. Its flowers are inconspicuous, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

Menziesia empetriformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
