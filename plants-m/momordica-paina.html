Momordica paina

Momordica paina, commonly known as the African bitter melon, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its tendrils, lobed leaves, and spiny, bitter fruits. The plant typically grows as a vine, climbing supports with its long, trailing stems. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The fruit, which is edible when ripe, is often used in traditional cuisines and has medicinal properties.
