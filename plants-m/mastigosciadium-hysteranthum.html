Mastigosciadium hysteranthum

Mastigosciadium hysteranthum, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the remote highlands of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its unique, translucent leaves that appear almost ethereal in sunlight. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are predominantly white and violet, with a peculiar, almost skeletal structure. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils.
