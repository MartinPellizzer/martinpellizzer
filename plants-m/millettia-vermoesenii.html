Millettia vermoesenii

Millettia vermoesenii, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces pod fruits that contain flattened, winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal. It is valued for its durable, heavy timber, which is used in construction and furniture-making.

The plant also plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing abilities.
