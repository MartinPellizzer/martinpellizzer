Malvaviscus rivularis

Malvaviscus rivularis, commonly known as the river mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams or in humid forests.

Its fibrous stems and mucilaginous seeds have been used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.
