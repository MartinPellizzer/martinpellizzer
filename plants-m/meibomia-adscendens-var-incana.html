Meibomia adscendens var. incana

Meibomia adscendens var. incana, commonly known as the woolly meibomia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
