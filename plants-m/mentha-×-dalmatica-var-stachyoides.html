Mentha × dalmatica var. stachyoides

Mentha × dalmatica var. stachyoides, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and square, erect stems. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, typical of the Lamiaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.
