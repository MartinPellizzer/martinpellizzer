Myrcia ouropretoensis

Myrcia ouropretoensis, commonly known as the Ouro Preto myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil, specifically the state of Minas Gerais, where it grows in humid, tropical forests. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that contain aromatic seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
