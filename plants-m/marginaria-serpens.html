Marginaria serpens

Marginaria serpens, commonly known as the snake's head or snake's head plant, is a unique species belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, serpentine stems that resemble a snake's body, hence its common name. Marginaria serpens is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
