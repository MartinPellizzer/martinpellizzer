Mahonia racemosa

Mahonia racemosa, commonly known as the common mahonia, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with spiny margins and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. Mahonia racemosa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

It thrives in partial to full shade and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
