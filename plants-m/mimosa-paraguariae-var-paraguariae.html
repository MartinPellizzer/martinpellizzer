Mimosa paraguariae var. paraguariae

Mimosa paraguariae var. paraguariae, commonly known as the Paraguayan mimosa, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched, a trait known as thigmonastic response. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in racemes, typical of the genus Mimosa.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid tropical and subtropical regions.
