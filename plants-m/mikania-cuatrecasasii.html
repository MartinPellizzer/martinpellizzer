Mikania cuatrecasasii

Mikania cuatrecasasii, commonly known as the Cuatrecasas' Mikania, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it can become a problematic species in certain ecosystems.
