Myrcia pernambucensis

Myrcia pernambucensis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that contains several seeds.

Myrcia pernambucensis is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental and aromatic properties.
