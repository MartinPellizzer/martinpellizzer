Monstera tuberculata

Monstera tuberculata, commonly known as the Swiss cheese plant, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that develop distinctive holes and notches as they mature. The plant features aerial roots that help it climb towards light, and its stems can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in humid, warm environments with bright, indirect light.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
