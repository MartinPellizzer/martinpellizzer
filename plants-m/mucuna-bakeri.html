Mucuna bakeri

Mucuna bakeri, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and trifoliate leaves, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. The plant produces distinctive, curved pods that contain small, yellowish seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.

Mucuna bakeri also plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
