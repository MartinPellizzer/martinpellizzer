Mollinedia macrantha

Mollinedia macrantha, commonly known as the Natal grass or Natal flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks, thriving in moist to waterlogged soils. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower spikes that bloom in late summer. Its flowers are usually greenish or reddish in color, and it is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance.

Mollinedia macrantha plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
