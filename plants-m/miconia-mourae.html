Miconia mourae

Miconia mourae, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit, which resembles a starfruit, is edible and has a tangy flavor.

Miconia mourae is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
