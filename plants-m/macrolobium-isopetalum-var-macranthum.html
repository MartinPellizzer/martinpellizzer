Macrolobium isopetalum var. macranthum, commonly known as the large-flowered macrolobium, is a striking member of the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range from pink to purple in color. The plant features palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a velvety texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is native to parts of South America.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
