Meniscosta scandens

Meniscosta scandens, commonly known as the climbing meniscus vine, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems that climb and twine around supports, often reaching significant lengths. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a smooth margin.

Meniscosta scandens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
