Maranta composita

Maranta composita, commonly known as the "Beadleaf" or "Painted leaf," is a strikingly ornamental plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its uniquely shaped, glossy leaves that resemble beads or painted patterns, often with contrasting colors on either side. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family, which includes many species known for their ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-light conditions. Maranta composita typically grows as an evergreen perennial, with upright stems and clusters of small, inconspicuous white flowers.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and as a decorative plant in humid environments.
