Myrcia maxonii

Myrcia maxonii, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in some parts of the world, although it can become invasive in certain environments.
