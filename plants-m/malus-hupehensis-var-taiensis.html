Malus hupehensis var. taiensis

Malus hupehensis var. taiensis, commonly known as the Taihsien crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its ornamental flowers, which range from white to pink, and its distinctive, often spiny fruit. The plant has elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The species is valued for its hardiness and ability to adapt to various environmental conditions.
