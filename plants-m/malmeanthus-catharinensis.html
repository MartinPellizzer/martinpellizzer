Malmeanthus catharinensis

Malmeanthus catharinensis, commonly known as the Brazilian ironwood, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small shrub or tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, often with a pale yellow to greenish color.

The species is notable for its hard, dense wood, which is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay.
