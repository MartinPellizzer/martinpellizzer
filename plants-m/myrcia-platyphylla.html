Myrcia platyphylla

Myrcia platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leaf myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Myrcia platyphylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual interest.
