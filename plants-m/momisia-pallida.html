Momisia pallida

Momisia pallida, commonly known as the pale momisia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces small, pale yellow to white flowers. Its botanical features include trifoliate leaves and stipules, which are common in the legume family.

Momisia pallida is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
