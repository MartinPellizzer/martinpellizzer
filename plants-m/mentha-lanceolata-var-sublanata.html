Mentha lanceolata var. sublanata

Mentha lanceolata var. sublanata, commonly known as narrow-leaf mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stem, typically in late summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in herbal teas and culinary applications.
