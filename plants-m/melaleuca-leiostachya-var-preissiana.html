Melaleuca leiostachya var. preissiana

Melaleuca leiostachya var. preissiana, commonly known as the broad-leaved tea tree, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, often silver-green leaves that are aromatic when crushed. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers, which are typical of the Myrtaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world for horticultural purposes.
