Mucuna imbricata

Mucuna imbricata, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant produces elongated, flat pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Mucuna imbricata is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it both a valuable forage crop and a potential weed.

Its seeds contain significant amounts of protein and are sometimes used as a source of livestock feed.
