Masdevallia infracta subsp. obscurans

Masdevallia infracta subsp.

obscurans, commonly known as the dark-stemmed masdevallia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America. This orchid is characterized by its compact growth habit and elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence.

The flowers are typically pale green to yellowish with dark purple or brown markings, and they are arranged in a loose inflorescence. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively rare occurrence, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
