Musa hillii

Musa hillii, commonly known as the dwarf banana plant, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, elongated bananas that are generally not consumed by humans but are used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are broad and can reach up to 2 meters in length, providing a striking visual appearance.

Musa hillii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some medicinal applications in local cultures.
