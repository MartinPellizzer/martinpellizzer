Marsilea hickenii

Marsilea hickenii, commonly known as the Hawaiian water clover, is a small aquatic fern native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It has four small, heart-shaped leaves that form a rosette at the water's surface. The plant produces tiny, yellowish flowers that develop into dark, spherical fruits. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Its unique appearance and habitat make it an important component of Hawaii's freshwater ecosystems.
