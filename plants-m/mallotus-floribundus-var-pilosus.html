Mallotus floribundus var. pilosus

Mallotus floribundus var. pilosus, commonly known as the hairy mallotus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and have a rough texture due to the presence of stiff hairs. This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
