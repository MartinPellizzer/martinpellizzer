Mapouria podocephala

Mapouria podocephala, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in umbels, often blooming in late summer.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
