Mulguraea scoparia

Mulguraea scoparia, commonly known as the desert mulga, is a shrub native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, narrow leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers, which are typically arranged in clusters along the stems. Mulguraea scoparia is adapted to low rainfall and high temperatures, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.

Its structure and growth patterns contribute to soil stabilization and provide habitat for various desert fauna.
