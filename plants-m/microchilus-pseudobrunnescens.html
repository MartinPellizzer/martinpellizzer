Microchilus pseudobrunnescens

Microchilus pseudobrunnescens, commonly known as the pale-brown microchilus, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats, often found in cloud forests. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a pale green to brownish hue.

It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months.
