Maxillaria meleagris

Maxillaria meleagris, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, often epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, often with intricate patterns. Its leaves are typically linear and arranged in a rosette, while its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture from the air.

Maxillaria meleagris is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
