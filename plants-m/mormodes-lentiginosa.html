Mormodes lentiginosa

Mormodes lentiginosa, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spotted leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are ovate with a velvety texture and often bear dark purple or brown spots.

The flowers, which are usually pink or white, bloom in late spring and early summer.
