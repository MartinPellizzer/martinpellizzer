Marsdenia sultanis

Marsdenia sultanis, commonly known as the Sultan's Marsdenia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asclepiadaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems and opposite, ovate leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, with a distinctive corona structure.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
