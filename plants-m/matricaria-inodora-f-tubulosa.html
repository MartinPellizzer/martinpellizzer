Matricaria inodora f. tubulosa

Matricaria inodora f. tubulosa, commonly known as the tubular yarrow, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a robust, upright stem covered in fine, hairy hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flower heads composed of tubular florets surrounded by ray florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
