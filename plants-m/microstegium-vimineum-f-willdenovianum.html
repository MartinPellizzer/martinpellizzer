Microstegium vimineum f. willdenovianum

Microstegium vimineum f. willdenovianum, commonly known as the willdenovian false nettle, is a variant of the common blueweed.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America, characterized by its slender, climbing stems and alternate leaves. The leaves are typically oval to lance-shaped with a serrated margin and a hairy surface. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by triangular, papery fruits.

This form is often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as streambanks and woodland edges.
