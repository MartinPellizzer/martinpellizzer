Machaerium longistipitatum

Machaerium longistipitatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed machaerium, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, which are typically clustered at the tips of the branches. Machaerium longistipitatum is known for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.
