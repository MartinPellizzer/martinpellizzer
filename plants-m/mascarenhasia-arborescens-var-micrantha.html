Mascarenhasia arborescens var. micrantha

Mascarenhasia arborescens var. micrantha, commonly known as the Mascarene tree, is a flowering plant native to the Mascarene Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with a dense, rounded canopy and smooth, grayish bark. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a leathery texture with a glossy surface.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
