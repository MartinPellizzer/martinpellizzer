Matricaria exigua

Matricaria exigua, commonly known as the lesser tansy, is a small annual herb belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in dry meadows and disturbed soils. The plant features slender stems and small, yellow flower heads that resemble daisies. Its leaves are aromatic and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive scent.

Matricaria exigua is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
