Mentha × villosa f. noalhatiana

Mentha × villosa f. noalhatiana, commonly known as the Kashmir mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soil. Its leaves are widely used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and analgesic properties.

It is also valued in culinary applications for its refreshing, mint-like aroma.
