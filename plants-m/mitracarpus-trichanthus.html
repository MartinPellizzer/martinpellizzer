Mitracarpus trichanthus

Mitracarpus trichanthus, commonly known as the silky mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, the Caribbean, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and showy, pale yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and thrives in well-drained soils with partial to full sunlight. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
