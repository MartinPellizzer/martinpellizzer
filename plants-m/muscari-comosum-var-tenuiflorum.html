Muscari comosum var. tenuiflorum

Muscari comosum var. tenuiflorum, commonly known as the slender-flowered grape hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade conditions. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that emerge in early spring.

It is valued for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
