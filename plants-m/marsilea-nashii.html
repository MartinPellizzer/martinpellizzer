Marsilea nashii

Marsilea nashii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water clover, is a small aquatic fern native to parts of Asia.

It features four small, floating leaves that resemble a clover, with each leaflet being narrow and elongated. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and wetlands. Its reproductive structures include spore-producing sporocarps that develop on the underside of its leaves.

Marsilea nashii plays a role in water filtration and provides habitat for microorganisms and small aquatic life.
