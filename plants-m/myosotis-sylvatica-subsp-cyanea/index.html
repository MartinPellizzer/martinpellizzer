Myosotis Sylvatica Subsp. Cyanea

Myosotis sylvatica subsp. cyanea is a strikingly beautiful flowering plant known for its delicate blue-violet petals. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, it thrives in moist, shady environments like woodlands and stream banks. This subspecies is often mistaken for the common forget-me-not due to its similar appearance. Its blooms typically appear in spring and early summer, adding a splash of color to natural landscapes. Despite its modest size, it holds a special place in horticulture and floral symbolism.
