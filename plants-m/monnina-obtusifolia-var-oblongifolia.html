Monnina obtusifolia var. oblongifolia

Monnina obtusifolia var. oblongifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf monnina, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky soils. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, pink to purple flowers are arranged in dense clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is characterized by its oblong, narrow leaves, distinguishing it from other varieties of Monnina.
