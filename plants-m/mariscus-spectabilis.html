Mariscus spectabilis

Mariscus spectabilis, commonly known as the showy mariscus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain its seeds.

Mariscus spectabilis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
