Monoteles paradoxa

Monoteles paradoxa, commonly known as the paradoxical monoteles, is a rare and enigmatic flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a single, strikingly elongated flower that resembles a slender spike. The plant's leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along its stem, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in dense forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and fragile habitat, Monoteles paradoxa is considered a species of conservation concern.
