Mimulus latidens

Mimulus latidens, commonly known as the broadleaf monkeyflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange flowers, which are typically borne in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often broad and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in stream banks and wet meadows.

Mimulus latidens plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
