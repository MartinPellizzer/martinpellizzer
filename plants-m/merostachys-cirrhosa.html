Merostachys cirrhosa

Merostachys cirrhosa, commonly known as the silky grass or silky sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetland or marshy environments, often growing in dense mats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various wildlife species.
