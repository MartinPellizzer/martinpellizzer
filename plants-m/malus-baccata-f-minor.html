Malus baccata f. minor

Malus baccata f. minor, commonly known as the dwarf Chinese crabapple, is a compact cultivar of the crabapple tree.

It is characterized by its small, rounded form and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring. The plant features oval, glossy leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding seasonal interest. It is a hardy shrub or small tree, suitable for ornamental use in gardens.

The fruit, which is edible but tart, is often used in jams and preserves.
