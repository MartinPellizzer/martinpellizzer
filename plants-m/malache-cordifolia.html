Malache cordifolia

Malache cordifolia, commonly known as the heart-shaped leaf plant, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Malache cordifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
