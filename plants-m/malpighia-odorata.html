Malpighia odorata

Malpighia odorata, commonly known as the West Indian cherry or acerola, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It produces small, red, cherry-like fruits that are rich in vitamin C and other antioxidants. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, white flowers with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

The botanical structure of Malpighia odorata includes a woody stem, alternate leaves, and a racemose inflorescence.
