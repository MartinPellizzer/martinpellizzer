Malus × floribunda f. scheideckeri

Malus × floribunda f. scheideckeri, commonly known as the Weigela cherry or Scheidecker's crabapple, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, showy pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub with thorny branches and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its fruit is a small, red or yellow apple-like berry that ripens in late summer.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
