Musa × paradisiaca var. hookeri

Musa × paradisiaca var. hookeri, commonly known as the Manila banana, is a cultivar of the banana plant native to the Philippines.

It is a herbaceous plant with a pseudostem formed by the tightly packed leaves, and it produces large, elongated fruit clusters called hands. The leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a waxy surface, while the flowers are clustered in a central inflorescence. This variety is valued for its sweet, edible fruit and is often cultivated for both commercial and ornamental purposes.

It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.
