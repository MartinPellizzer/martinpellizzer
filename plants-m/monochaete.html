Monochaete

Monochaete, commonly known as the single-stemmed orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its solitary, erect stems that bear a single inflorescence. The flowers are typically showy, with a variety of colors and intricate patterns that attract pollinators. Monochaete species are native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in South America and parts of Africa.

These plants are often epiphytic, growing on trees or rocks in humid environments.
