Melanoseris paniculata

Melanoseris paniculata, commonly known as the panicled melastome, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences of small, five-petaled flowers, often in shades of pink or red. Its flowers are typically borne on long, slender peduncles, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Melanoseris paniculata is adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in both moist and dry conditions.
