Mirabilis ambigua

Mirabilis ambigua, commonly known as the wandering jew, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Mirabilis ambigua is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental flowers and ease of care.
