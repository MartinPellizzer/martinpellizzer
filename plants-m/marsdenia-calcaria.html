Marsdenia calcaria

Marsdenia calcaria, commonly known as the rock fig, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocky surfaces or in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
