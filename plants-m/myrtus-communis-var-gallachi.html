Myrtus communis var. gallachi

Myrtus communis var. gallachi, commonly known as the Spanish myrtle, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features small, dark green, aromatic leaves with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces tiny, white, fragrant flowers in spring, which are followed by small, dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery and traditional medicine.
