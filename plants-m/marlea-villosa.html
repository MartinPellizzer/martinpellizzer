Marlea villosa

Marlea villosa, commonly known as the hairy marigold, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Marlea villosa is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
