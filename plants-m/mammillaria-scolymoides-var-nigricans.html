Mammillaria scolymoides var. nigricans

Mammillaria scolymoides var. nigricans, commonly known as the black-spined mammillaria, is a succulent cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white to grayish woolly hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its spines are typically black or dark brown, giving it its distinctive appearance.

It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
