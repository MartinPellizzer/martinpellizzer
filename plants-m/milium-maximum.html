Milium maximum

Milium maximum, commonly known as the greater millet, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces numerous tiny grains that are often used as a staple food in various parts of the world. Milium maximum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and valuable for agricultural purposes.
