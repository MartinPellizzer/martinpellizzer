Myrcia unana

Myrcia unana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals. Myrcia unana is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils.

It is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
