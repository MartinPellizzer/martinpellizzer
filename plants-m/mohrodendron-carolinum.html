Mohrodendron carolinum

Mohrodendron carolinum, commonly known as the Carolina mohrodendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated margin and produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers in the spring. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in woodland edges. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
