Mikania chagalensis

Mikania chagalensis, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it both a popular ornamental plant and a potential weed. Its flowers bloom in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
