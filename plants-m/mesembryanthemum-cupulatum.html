Mesembryanthemum cupulatum

Mesembryanthemum cupulatum, commonly known as the "Cup-shaped ice plant," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that form a rosette pattern, and it produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Mesembryanthemum cupulatum is also known for its ability to tolerate frost and its long blooming period, typically from spring to early summer.
