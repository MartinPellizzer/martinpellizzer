Macaranga tamiana

Macaranga tamiana, commonly known as the white mangosteen, is a fast-growing shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. Its fruits are fleshy, yellow berries that contain a single seed.

Macaranga tamiana is often found in disturbed habitats and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
