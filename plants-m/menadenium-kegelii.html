Menadenium kegelii

Menadenium kegelii, commonly known as the Kegel's spider plant, is a striking member of the Asparagaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its long, arching stems that bear clusters of bright green, spider-like leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by blue-black berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light.

Menadenium kegelii is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
