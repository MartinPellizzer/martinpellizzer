Margbensonia thevetiifolia

Margbensonia thevetiifolia, commonly known as the Socotra cypress, is a rare and unique plant native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its distinctive, umbrella-shaped crown and spiral arrangement of branches, which give it a prehistoric appearance. The plant has scale-like leaves that are tightly packed along the stems, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists due to its endemic status and ecological significance.
