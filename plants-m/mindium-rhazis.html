Mindium rhazis

Mindium rhazis, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its delicate, silvery-green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its unique appearance makes it a sought-after species among botanists and horticulturists.

Due to its limited habitat, it is considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
