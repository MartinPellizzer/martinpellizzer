Myrcia abbotiana

Myrcia abbotiana, commonly known as the Abbot's myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the humid forests of southeastern Brazil, where it thrives in warm, moist environments. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are glossy and oval in shape, and its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. It produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when mature.

Myrcia abbotiana is often valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
