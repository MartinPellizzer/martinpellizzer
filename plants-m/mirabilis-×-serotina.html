Mirabilis × serotina

Mirabilis × serotina, commonly known as the evening primrose, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Onagraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The seeds are enclosed in a capsule that splits open when mature, dispersing the seeds by wind.
