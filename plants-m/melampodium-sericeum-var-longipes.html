Melampodium sericeum var. longipes

Melampodium sericeum var. longipes, commonly known as the silky everlasting or long-stemmed everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The plant is known for its long-lasting blooms, which remain intact for extended periods, earning it the common name "everlasting.".
