Malacomeles denticulata

Malacomeles denticulata, commonly known as the toothed milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. It is a perennial shrub that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its milky sap is toxic and can cause skin irritation, making it important to handle with care.
