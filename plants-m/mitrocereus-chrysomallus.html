Mitrocereus chrysomallus

Mitrocereus chrysomallus, commonly known as the golden crown cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly spines. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for desert gardens.

Due to its slow growth and specific care requirements, it is considered a challenging but rewarding plant for experienced growers.
