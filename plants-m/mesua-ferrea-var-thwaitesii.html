Mesua ferrea var. thwaitesii

Mesua ferrea var. thwaitesii, commonly known as the red ironwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark reddish-brown timber, which is highly valued for its durability and strength. The tree has compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets and produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and coastal areas.

The species is also noted for its slow growth and long lifespan, making it an important component of tropical ecosystems.
