Macaranga misimae

Macaranga misimae, commonly known as the Malayan paper tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, alternate, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a unique structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its rapid growth rate and ability to thrive in disturbed habitats make it an important pioneer species in forest succession.

The leaves are often used in traditional practices and have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
