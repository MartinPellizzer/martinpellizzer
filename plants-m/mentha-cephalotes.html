Mentha cephalotes

Mentha cephalotes, commonly known as the Cape mint, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in whorled clusters at the ends of the branches. Mentha cephalotes is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
