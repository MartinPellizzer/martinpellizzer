Magnolia purpurea var. denudata

Magnolia purpurea var. denudata, commonly known as the bare-branch magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The flowers are typically pink or white and are borne on bare branches, giving the plant its common name. This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to a range of growing conditions.
