Matucana elongata

Matucana elongata, commonly known as the elongated matucana, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its slender, columnar stem and pinnate leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically appearing during the warmer months.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice for indoor cultivation and botanical collections.
