Mulgedium laevigatum

Mulgedium laevigatum, commonly known as the smooth mulgedium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, usually greenish-white in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system consists of fibrous roots that help anchor the plant and absorb nutrients from the soil.

Mulgedium laevigatum is often found in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
