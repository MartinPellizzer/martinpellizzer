Mertensia cunninghamii

Mertensia cunninghamii, commonly known as the bluebells of the south, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It features attractive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet, blooming in spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silky hair. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Mertensia cunninghamii is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits to native pollinators.
