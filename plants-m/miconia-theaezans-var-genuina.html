Miconia theaezans var. genuina

Miconia theaezans var. genuina, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters in height, with a dense canopy and broad, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by bright orange, star-shaped fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
