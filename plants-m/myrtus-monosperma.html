Myrtus monosperma

Myrtus monosperma, commonly known as the single-seeded myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces a single seed per fruit, which is a distinctive feature of its reproductive biology. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Myrtus monosperma is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
