Maranta polystachya

Maranta polystachya, commonly known as the arrowroot plant, is a tropical species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage that often displays vibrant red, orange, and yellow hues. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are typically borne on slender, leaf-like bracts. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a source of starch.

Maranta polystachya thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
