Muraltia acicularis

Muraltia acicularis, commonly known as the needlepod muraltia, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Muraltia acicularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its unique foliage.
