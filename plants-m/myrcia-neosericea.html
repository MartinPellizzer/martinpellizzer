Myrcia neosericea

Myrcia neosericea, commonly known as the silky myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. Myrcia neosericea is typically found in moist, shaded areas of tropical rainforests.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
