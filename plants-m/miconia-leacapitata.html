Miconia leacapitata

Miconia leacapitata, commonly known as the broadleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Miconia leacapitata is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it a significant plant in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
