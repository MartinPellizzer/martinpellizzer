Myrcia coriacea var. jacquiniana

Myrcia coriacea var. jacquiniana, commonly known as the jacquinian guava, is a shrub or small tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It features elliptical to ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter, sometimes whitish underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange to red berries. Its bark is thick and rugged, often with a reddish or brownish hue.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
