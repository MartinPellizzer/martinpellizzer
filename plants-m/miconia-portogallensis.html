Miconia portogallensis

Miconia portogallensis, commonly known as the Brazilian star apple, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, star-shaped fruits. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions, though it can become invasive in certain areas.
