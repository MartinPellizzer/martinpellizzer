Mirabilis planta

Mirabilis planta, commonly known as the four o'clock plant, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that open in the late afternoon and close by dawn. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with slender, branching stems and opposite leaves. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, purple, and white.

Mirabilis planta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
