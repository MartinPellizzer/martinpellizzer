Mentha mutabilis

Mentha mutabilis, commonly known as the variable mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that can range in color from white to pink, depending on environmental conditions. Its leaves are typically serrated and emit a strong minty fragrance when crushed.

Mentha mutabilis is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and as a source of essential oils.
