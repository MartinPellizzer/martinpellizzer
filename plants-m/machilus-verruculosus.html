Machilus verruculosus

Machilus verruculosus, commonly known as the rough-leafed maha, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its rough, often hairy foliage. The leaves are typically elliptical with a serrated margin and a distinctive aromatic scent. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by oval, dark blue berries.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
