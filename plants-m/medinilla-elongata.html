Medinilla elongata

Medinilla elongata, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or velvetleaf.

It is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including the Philippines and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pink flowers. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Medinilla elongata is also valued for its ability to produce edible fruits, which have a sweet, tangy flavor.
