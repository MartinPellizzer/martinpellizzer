Momordica cochinchinensis

Momordica cochinchinensis, commonly known as bitter melon, is a vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, warty, elongated fruits that turn from green to orange when mature. The plant belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers and pumpkins. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with five petals.

The plant is valued for its edible fruit, which is used in various cuisines and traditional medicine.
