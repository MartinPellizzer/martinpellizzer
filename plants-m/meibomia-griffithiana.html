Meibomia griffithiana

Meibomia griffithiana, commonly known as the Indian orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil efficiently.

Meibomia griffithiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
