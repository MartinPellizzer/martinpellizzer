Mollinedia sphaerantha

Mollinedia sphaerantha, commonly known as the globe bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive spherical inflorescence, which consists of numerous small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually oval and covered in fine hairs, and it produces showy, violet-colored blooms.

Mollinedia sphaerantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and light conditions.
