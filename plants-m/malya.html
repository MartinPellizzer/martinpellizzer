Malya

Malya, commonly known as the "wild fig" or "Indian date," is a tropical plant belonging to the Moraceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible, fig-like fruits. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in regions of Southeast Asia, Africa, and the Indian subcontinent. Its fruit is rich in nutrients and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The Malya plant also plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
