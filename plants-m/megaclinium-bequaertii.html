Megaclinium bequaertii

Megaclinium bequaertii, commonly known as the Bequaert's giant sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and produces panicles of small, white or pale green flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its upright, grass-like appearance.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
