Macrosolen creatophyllus

Macrosolen creatophyllus, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, translucent, balloon-like leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the rainy season, which are often hidden within the foliage. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, with a waxy coating that reduces water loss.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered an endangered species.
