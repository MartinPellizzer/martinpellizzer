Millettia soyauxii

Millettia soyauxii, commonly known as the African ebony, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by legumes containing compressed seeds.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests and riverine habitats.
