Mniochloa strephioides

Mniochloa strephioides, commonly known as the spiral grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver or blue. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. Mniochloa strephioides thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Its unique spiral arrangement of leaves gives it a distinctive appearance and makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
