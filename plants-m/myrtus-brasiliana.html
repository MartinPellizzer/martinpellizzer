Myrtus brasiliana

Myrtus brasiliana, commonly known as Brazilian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, often with a fragrant scent. Its small, white flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by small, red berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
