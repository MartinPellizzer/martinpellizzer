Microcos membranifolia

Microcos membranifolia, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive membranous texture. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the year under proper care. It is a popular ornamental plant due to its compact size and ability to thrive in indoor environments.

Microcos membranifolia prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
