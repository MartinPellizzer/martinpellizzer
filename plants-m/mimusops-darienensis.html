Mimusops darienensis

Mimusops darienensis, commonly known as the Darien gum tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, yellowish fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
