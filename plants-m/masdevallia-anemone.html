Masdevallia anemone

Masdevallia anemone, commonly known as the Anemone Masdevallia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that resemble anemones, with white petals and a prominent, often colorful lip. The plant typically grows as a miniature orchid, with compact foliage and a preference for high humidity and cool temperatures. It thrives in shaded environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil to flourish.

Its unique appearance and rarity make it a prized addition to specialized orchid collections.
