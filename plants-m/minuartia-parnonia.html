Minuartia parnonia

Minuartia parnonia, commonly known as the Pannonian rockcress, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which is characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and often aromatic foliage. The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils, often found in alpine or subalpine regions. Its leaves are linear to oblanceolate, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring.

Minuartia parnonia is adapted to harsh environments and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover in horticultural settings.
