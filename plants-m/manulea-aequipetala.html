Manulea aequipetala

Manulea aequipetala, commonly known as the smooth-leaved manulea, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the cooler months. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a smooth texture, contributing to its common name.

Manulea aequipetala is adapted to harsh, mountainous environments and requires well-drained soil and full sun to thrive.
