Malanea campylocarpa

Malanea campylocarpa, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are glossy and leathery in texture. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by bright red berries in the autumn. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found at high elevations.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
