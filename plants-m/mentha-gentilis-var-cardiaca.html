Mentha gentilis var. cardiaca

Mentha gentilis var. cardiaca, commonly known as heart mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its essential oils contain menthol and other compounds that contribute to its cooling and soothing properties.
