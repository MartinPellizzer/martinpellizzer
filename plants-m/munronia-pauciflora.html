Munronia pauciflora

Munronia pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered munronia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or swampy areas. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in association with other wetland plants.
