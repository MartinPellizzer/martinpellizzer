Melodinus murpe

Melodinus murpe, commonly known as the melodious murple, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often a deep emerald green with a glossy texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom during the rainy season. Its unique floral structure attracts a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Melodinus murpe is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
