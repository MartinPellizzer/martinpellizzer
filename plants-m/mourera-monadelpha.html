Mourera monadelpha

Mourera monadelpha, commonly known as the desert rose, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and waxy leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in spring. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots and the ability to store water in its stems.

Mourera monadelpha is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
