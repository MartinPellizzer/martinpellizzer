Mascagnia nana

Mascagnia nana, commonly known as the dwarf mascagnia, is a low-growing, succulent plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It features small, rounded leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the cooler months, adding a delicate touch of color to its foliage. It is well adapted to full sun and requires minimal water, making it an ideal choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its compact size and resilience, Mascagnia nana is a popular plant among succulent enthusiasts.
