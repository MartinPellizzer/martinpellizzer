Mulgedium lessertianum

Mulgedium lessertianum, commonly known as the silverleaf mulgedium, is a rare and elusive plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its lance-shaped leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting specific pollinators. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and requires a cool, humid environment to flourish.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
