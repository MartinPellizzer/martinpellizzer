Marsdenia hatschbachii

Marsdenia hatschbachii, commonly known as the Hatshbach's Marsdenia, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are follicles that release seeds with tufts of hair for wind dispersal.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
