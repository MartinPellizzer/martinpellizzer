Mentha joffrei

Mentha joffrei, commonly known as the West African spearmint, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or white, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and emit a strong, minty fragrance.

Mentha joffrei is often used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and antimicrobial properties.
