Maxillaria vinacea

Maxillaria vinacea, commonly known as the red-tipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, dark red sepals and petals that contrast with its white or pale yellow lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are fragrant and bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season.

The botanical features of Maxillaria vinacea make it a popular choice for both horticultural collections and botanical research.
