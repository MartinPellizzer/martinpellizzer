Melolobium parviflorum

Melolobium parviflorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It features small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, and its leaves are narrow and often hairy.

The plant is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
