Magnolia kaifui

Magnolia kaifui, commonly known as the Kaimana magnolia, is a species native to the island of Hawaii.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, and it has a slow growth rate, making it a long-lived species.

The plant is adapted to the humid, tropical climate of Hawaii and thrives in well-drained soil.
