Miscanthus rufipilus

Miscanthus rufipilus, commonly known as red feather grass, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and feathery, reddish-pink plumes that appear in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along riverbanks. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

It plays a role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
