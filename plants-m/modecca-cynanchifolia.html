Modecca cynanchifolia

Modecca cynanchifolia, commonly known as the bluebell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Modecca cynanchifolia is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forests or along stream banks.
