Macphersonia gracilis var. lasiostachys

Macphersonia gracilis var. lasiostachys, commonly known as the silky leaved grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a silky texture. The plant produces panicles of small, pale green to white flowers, which are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
