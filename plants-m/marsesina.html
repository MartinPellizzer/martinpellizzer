Marsesina

Marsesina, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Marsesina species are often found in temperate regions, particularly in North America and parts of Europe.

Its foliage is usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
