Magnolia plumieri

Magnolia plumieri, commonly known as the sweetbay magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The tree has smooth, gray bark and elliptical, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its flowers are often used in perfumes and have a subtle, sweet scent.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens for its seasonal display.
