Mariscus kyllingioides

Mariscus kyllingioides, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and small, star-shaped yellow flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand salty sea air and strong coastal winds. Its flowering period typically occurs during the summer months, attracting various pollinators.

Mariscus kyllingioides is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
