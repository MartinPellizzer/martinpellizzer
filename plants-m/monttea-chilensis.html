Monttea chilensis

Monttea chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean monttea, is a flowering plant native to Chile.

It belongs to the family Montiaceae and is characterized by its small, succulent leaves and clusters of tiny, star-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and coastal areas. It is known for its ability to store water in its fleshy leaves, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

The flowers are typically pale blue or white, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
