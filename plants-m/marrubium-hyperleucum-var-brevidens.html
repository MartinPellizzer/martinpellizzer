Marrubium hyperleucum var. brevidens

Marrubium hyperleucum var. brevidens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved horehound, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and gray-green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in rocky or dry habitats.

Marrubium hyperleucum var. brevidens is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
