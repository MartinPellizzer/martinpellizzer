Milium effusum subsp. schmidtianum

Milium effusum subsp.

schmidtianum, commonly known as Schmidt's millet, is a flowering plant species native to Central Asia. It is a small annual grass that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky soils.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white or pale green flowers. Its seeds are often used as a traditional food source in some regions.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in arid environments.
