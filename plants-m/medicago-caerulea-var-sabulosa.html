Medicago caerulea var. sabulosa

Medicago caerulea var. sabulosa, commonly known as the sand clover, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender stems and small, blue to violet-colored flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Medicago caerulea var. sabulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
