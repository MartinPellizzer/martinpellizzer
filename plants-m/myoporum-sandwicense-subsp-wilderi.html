Myoporum sandwicense subsp. wilderi

Myoporum sandwicense subsp.

wilderi, commonly known as the Kona myoporum, is a shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant thrives in coastal and dry forest habitats, often growing in well-drained soils. Its growth habit is typically low and spreading, forming dense mats or shrubs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
