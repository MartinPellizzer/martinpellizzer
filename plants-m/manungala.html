Manungala

Manungala, commonly known as the sacred fig tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, fig-like fruits. The tree can grow quite tall, reaching up to 30 meters in height, and is often found in moist, tropical forests. Its roots system is extensive, helping to stabilize the soil and prevent erosion.

The plant is also valued for its cultural and medicinal significance in many traditional communities.
