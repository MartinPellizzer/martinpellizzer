Mesembryanthemum longistylum

Mesembryanthemum longistylum, commonly known as the long-stalked ice plant, is a succulent perennial native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained sandy soils and full sunlight, making it well-suited for arid environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is also notable for its ability to survive in harsh conditions, showcasing its adaptability to drought and high temperatures.
