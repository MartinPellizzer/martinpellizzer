Menispermum miersii

Menispermum miersii, commonly known as the Miers' ginger, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and palmate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

The plant has been studied for its potential medicinal properties due to the presence of various alkaloids.
