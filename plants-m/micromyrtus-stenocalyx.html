Micromyrtus stenocalyx

Micromyrtus stenocalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalypts and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark is smooth and greyish, and it often grows in coastal or sandy soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses by Indigenous communities.
