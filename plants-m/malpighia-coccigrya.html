Malpighia coccigrya

Malpighia coccigrya, commonly known as the scarlet grana or red mulberry, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red berries that mature in the fall. The plant has alternate, simple leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are small, clustered, and typically white or pink. The berries are edible, though they have a tart flavor, and are sometimes used in local cuisines or as ornamental plants.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
