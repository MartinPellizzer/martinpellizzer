Malva caroliniana

Malva caroliniana, commonly known as the Carolina mallow, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring heart-shaped leaves and showy pink or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other well-known species like cotton and hibiscus. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often found in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks and in woodland edges.
