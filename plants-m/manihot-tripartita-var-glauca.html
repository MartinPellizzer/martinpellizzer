Manihot tripartita var. glauca

Manihot tripartita var. glauca, commonly known as the waxy beggar's ticks, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, waxy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by triangular, three-lobed fruits. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation efforts.
