Myrtus loureiroi

Myrtus loureiroi, commonly known as the Portuguese myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Portugal and Spain.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Myrtus loureiroi thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant foliage and seasonal blooms.
