Muraltia lancifolia

Muraltia lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved muraltia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves, which are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, fleshy fruits. Muraltia lancifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.
