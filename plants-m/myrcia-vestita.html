Myrcia vestita

Myrcia vestita, commonly known as the silky myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. Its small, fragrant white flowers are typically clustered in inflorescences and bloom during the warmer months.

The leaves are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its silky texture and providing some resistance to herbivory.
