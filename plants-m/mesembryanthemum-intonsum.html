Mesembryanthemum intonsum

Mesembryanthemum intonsum, commonly known as the ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the summer months. It is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in coastal and sandy areas.
