Medinilla salicina

Medinilla salicina, commonly known as the silver medinilla, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale pink, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Medinilla salicina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
