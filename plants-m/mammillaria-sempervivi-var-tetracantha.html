Mammillaria sempervivi var. tetracantha

Mammillaria sempervivi var. tetracantha, commonly known as the four-spined mammillaria, is a compact cactus native to Mexico.

It features a globular stem with numerous small, rounded tubercles that bear white or pale blue flowers. The plant has spines that are typically arranged in clusters of four, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a potted plant.
