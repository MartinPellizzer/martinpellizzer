Myrica palustris

Myrica palustris, commonly known as the wax myrtle, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its small, dark blue berries and fragrant, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and perfumes. The plant thrives in wetland habitats, including swamps and marshes, and is highly adaptable to varying soil conditions. Its dense growth habit makes it an effective erosion control plant in moist environments.

Myrica palustris also plays a role in supporting local wildlife, providing food and shelter for various birds and insects.
