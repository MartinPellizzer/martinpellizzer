Mentha × villosa subvar. exserta

Mentha × villosa subvar. exserta, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a hybrid perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and square, upright stems. The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Mentha × villosa subvar. exserta is valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in herbal teas or as a ornamental plant.
