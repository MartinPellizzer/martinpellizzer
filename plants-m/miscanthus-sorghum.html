Miscanthus sorghum

Miscanthus sorghum, commonly known as silvergrass, is a tall, perennial grass native to Asia.

It features slender, silver-gray blades and produces fluffy, seed-bearing spikes during the late summer and early fall. The plant is characterized by its robust root system and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a potential bioenergy crop.

Its growth habit makes it a popular choice for landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
