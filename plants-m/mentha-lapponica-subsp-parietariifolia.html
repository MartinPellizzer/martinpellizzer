Mentha lapponica subsp. parietariifolia

Mentha lapponica subsp.

parietariifolia, commonly known as the wall mint, is a perennial herb native to northern regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often along cliffs or in alpine environments.

The plant features slender, creeping stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are hairy and aromatic. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pale blue to purple, arranged in loose spikes.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
