Medicago medicaginoides

Medicago medicaginoides, commonly known as the sickle vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by slender, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Medicago medicaginoides is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration and soil improvement.
