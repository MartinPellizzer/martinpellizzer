Myrtus prostrata

Myrtus prostrata, commonly known as prostrate myrtle, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, oval, dark green leaves that are aromatic and covered in a fine white bloom. The plant produces tiny, pale pink to white flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Myrtus prostrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
