Mucuna platyphylla

Mucuna platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaf velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to flat, pod-like fruits containing kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing abilities.
