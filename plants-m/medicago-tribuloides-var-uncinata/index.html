Medicago Tribuloides Var. Uncinata

Medicago tribuloides var. uncinata is a unique variant of the medick plant known for its distinctive hooked seed pods. This plant thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is commonly found in arid and semi-arid regions. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in early spring, adding a subtle touch of color to the landscape. The variety's name, uncinata, refers to the hooked tips of its seeds, which aid in seed dispersal. Despite its modest appearance, this plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
