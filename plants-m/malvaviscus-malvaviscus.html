Malvaviscus malvaviscus

Malvaviscus malvaviscus, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Mexican mallow, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus, and is known for its mucilaginous properties in its seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forests.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
