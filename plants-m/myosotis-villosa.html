Myosotis villosa

Myosotis villosa, commonly known as the hairy forget-me-not, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate blue or purple flowers, which bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are hairy and oval-shaped, contributing to its common name.

Myosotis villosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
