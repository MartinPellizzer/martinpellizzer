Masdevallia herzogii

Masdevallia herzogii, commonly known as the Herzog's masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, often brightly colored flowers that feature a unique combination of petals and sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage consists of long, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

Due to its striking appearance and limited natural habitat, it is a prized species among orchid enthusiasts.
