Matisia alata subsp. alata

Matisia alata subsp.

alata, commonly known as the white-leaf matisia, is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America. It is characterized by its alternate, elliptic to ovate leaves that are usually white or pale green on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
