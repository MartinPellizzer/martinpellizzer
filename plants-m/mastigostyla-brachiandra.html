Mastigostyla brachiandra

Mastigostyla brachiandra, commonly known as the flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange inflorescences that resemble flames, which appear in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, narrow leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing as a tree or shrub.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is commonly used in landscaping and horticulture.
