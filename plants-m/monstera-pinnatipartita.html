Monstera pinnatipartita

Monstera pinnatipartita, commonly known as the Swiss cheese plant, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that develop distinctive holes and notches as they mature. The plant has a strong, twisting stem that can grow up to 20 feet in length, making it a popular choice for vertical gardens. Its foliage is evergreen, and it produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by green, spherical fruits.

The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires regular watering to maintain its lush, foliage-filled appearance.
