Mimulus filicaulis

Mimulus filicaulis, commonly known as the golden weed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling the shape of a small cup. The plant has slender, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

Mimulus filicaulis is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an annual or biennial species.
