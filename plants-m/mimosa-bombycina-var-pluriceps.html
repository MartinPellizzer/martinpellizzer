Mimosa bombycina var. pluriceps

Mimosa bombycina var. pluriceps, commonly known as the woolly mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a shrub-like growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and dry regions.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a distinctive species in its native habitat.
