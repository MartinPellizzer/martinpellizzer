Melastoma pileatum

Melastoma pileatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf strobilanthes, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments. Its foliage is covered in fine, short hairs, giving it a soft, tactile appearance.

Melastoma pileatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in tropical climates.
