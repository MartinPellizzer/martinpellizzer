Myristica membranacea

Myristica membranacea, commonly known as the membranous nutmeg, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is closely related to the nutmeg tree, Myristica fragrans. The plant is characterized by its aromatic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and greenish-yellow.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its unique botanical and economic properties.
