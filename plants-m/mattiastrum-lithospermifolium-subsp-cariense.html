Mattiastrum lithospermifolium subsp. cariense

Mattiastrum lithospermifolium subsp.

cariense, commonly known as the Caribean mat grass, is a perennial herb native to the Caribbean region. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, usually appearing in late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology compared to other subspecies of Mattiastrum.
