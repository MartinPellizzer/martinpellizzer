Manihot caerulescens subsp. macrantha

Manihot caerulescens subsp.

macrantha, commonly known as the blue manioc, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its striking blue-green leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers.

The plant produces edible tuberous roots, which are used in traditional food preparation. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland tropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and potential agricultural uses, it is cultivated in some parts of the world.
