Marcus-kochia

Marcus-kochia, commonly known as the silverleaf kochia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the amaranth family (Amaranthaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. Marcus-kochia is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and attractive, feathery appearance.
