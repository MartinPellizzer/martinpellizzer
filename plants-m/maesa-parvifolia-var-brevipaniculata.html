Maesa parvifolia var. brevipaniculata

Maesa parvifolia var. brevipaniculata, commonly known as the small-leaved maesa, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are arranged in short, branched inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

It is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses.
