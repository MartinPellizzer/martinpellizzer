Miconia grisebachii

Miconia grisebachii, commonly known as the purple miconia, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its striking, velvety purple leaves. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
