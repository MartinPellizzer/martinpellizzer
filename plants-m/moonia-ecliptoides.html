Moonia ecliptoides

Moonia ecliptoides, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, white, star-shaped flowers that open at night and close during the day. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its fragrant blooms. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture.
