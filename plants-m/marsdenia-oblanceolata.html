Marsdenia oblanceolata

Marsdenia oblanceolata, commonly known as the broadleaf milkvine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by follicle fruits containing elliptical seeds.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
