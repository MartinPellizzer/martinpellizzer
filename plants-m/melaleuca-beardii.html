Melaleuca beardii

Melaleuca beardii, commonly known as the slender bottlebrush, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, cylindrical leaves and distinctive bottlebrush-like flowers that are typically pink or red. The plant produces small, woody fruits called capsules, which contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

Melaleuca beardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
