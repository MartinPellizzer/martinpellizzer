Malacantha heudelotiana

Malacantha heudelotiana, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features showy, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule-like fruits that contain toxic seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
