Medicago cordata

Medicago cordata, commonly known as narrow-leafed clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils.

Medicago cordata is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
