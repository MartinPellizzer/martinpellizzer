Magnolia biloba

Magnolia biloba, commonly known as the Chinese magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches. Its bark is smooth and grayish, becoming fissured with age.

Magnolia biloba is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
