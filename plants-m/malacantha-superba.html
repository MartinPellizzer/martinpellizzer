Malacantha superba

Malacantha superba, commonly known as the giant flame vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from orange to red, often with a yellow throat. The plant produces long, trailing stems that can reach several meters in length, making it suitable for trellises or arbors. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Malacantha superba is valued in horticulture for its vibrant blooms and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
