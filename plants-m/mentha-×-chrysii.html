Mentha × chrysii

Mentha × chrysii, commonly known as the golden mint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Mentha × piperita and Mentha × gracilis.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which stand out against its dark green, aromatic leaves. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and mild mint-like fragrance.

Mentha × chrysii is a perennial herb that produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
