Medicago erecta

Medicago erecta, commonly known as the upright medick, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom in early summer. Medicago erecta is often found in disturbed soils and is used in some regions as a forage crop for livestock.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil fertility.
