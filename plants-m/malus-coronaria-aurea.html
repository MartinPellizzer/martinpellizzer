Malus coronaria aurea

Malus coronaria aurea, commonly known as the golden apple service tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western regions of North America.

It is characterized by its showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves appear. The plant produces small, tart apples that are often used in jams and preserves. Its foliage turns a vibrant yellow in the fall, adding ornamental value to the landscape.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both aesthetic and edible benefits.
