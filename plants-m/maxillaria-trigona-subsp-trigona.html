Maxillaria trigona subsp. trigona

Maxillaria trigona subsp.

trigona, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is a species native to Central and South America. It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging inflorescence and vibrant, fragrant flowers.

The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are elliptical and arranged in two rows, while the roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
