Monticalia andicola var. andicola

Monticalia andicola var. andicola, commonly known as the Andean mountain orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, thriving in cool, humid environments. Its leaves are often lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers display a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
