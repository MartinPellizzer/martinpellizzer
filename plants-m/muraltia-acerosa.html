Muraltia acerosa

Muraltia acerosa, commonly known as the sour orange or sour plum, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of edible seeds. Muraltia acerosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing food for various wildlife species.
