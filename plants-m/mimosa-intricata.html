Mimosa intricata

Mimosa intricata, commonly known as the golden mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a soft, ornamental appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ability to fix nitrogen through root nodules makes it beneficial for soil enrichment.
