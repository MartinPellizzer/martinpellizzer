Mollugo hirta var. lotoides

Mollugo hirta var. lotoides, commonly known as the hairy mossy spurge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features small, fleshy, green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a moss-like appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by bead-like seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its succulent leaves and unique floral structure make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
