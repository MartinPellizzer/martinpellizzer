Mentha pseudovalis

Mentha pseudovalis, commonly known as the Siberian mint, is a perennial herb native to the colder regions of Asia and Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, minty aroma, while its flowers are small and clustered in whorls.

Mentha pseudovalis is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
