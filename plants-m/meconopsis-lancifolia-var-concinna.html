Meconopsis lancifolia var. concinna

Meconopsis lancifolia var. concinna, commonly known as the narrow-leaved meconopsis, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of alpine flora.
