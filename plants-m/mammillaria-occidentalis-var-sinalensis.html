Mammillaria occidentalis var. sinalensis

Mammillaria occidentalis var. sinalensis, commonly known as the Sinaloa nipple cactus, is a small, spiny cactus native to Mexico.

It typically grows to a height of 5-10 centimeters, with a globular shape and dense, white to pale blue spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. It prefers well-draining soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This variety is known for its compact size and ornamental value in arid landscapes.
