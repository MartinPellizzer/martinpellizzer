Millettia atenensis

Millettia atenensis, commonly known as the yellow millettia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands.

Millettia atenensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
