Malpighia oxycocca

Malpighia oxycocca, commonly known as the acerola cherry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It produces small, bright red fruits that are rich in vitamin C and other antioxidants. The plant belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small white flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

The fruit's high acidity and intense flavor make it a popular ingredient in health foods and beverages.
