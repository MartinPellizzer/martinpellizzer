Myrtillus

Myrtillus, commonly known as the common bilberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the family Ericaceae.

It is native to Europe, Asia, and North America, typically growing in acidic soils in forested areas. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are rich in antioxidants and anthocyanins. Its leaves are elliptical with a leathery texture, and its flowers are bell-shaped, typically pink or white.

Myrtillus plays an important ecological role by providing food for wildlife and contributing to forest biodiversity.
