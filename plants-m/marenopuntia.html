Marenopuntia

Marenopuntia, commonly known as the desert rose, is a genus of cacti native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and showy, often pink or red, flowers that bloom in the evening. The plants are typically low-growing and have a compact, rounded shape. They possess a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing them to survive in dry conditions.

Marenopuntia species are popular among succulent enthusiasts due to their ornamental value and hardy nature.
