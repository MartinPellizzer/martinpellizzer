Mentha arvensis f. angustifolia

Mentha arvensis f. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf mint, is a subspecies of field mint native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and strong, aromatic menthol scent. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is often used in herbal medicine and as a culinary herb due to its refreshing flavor and medicinal properties.
