Menodora africana

Menodora africana, commonly known as African moonseed, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by fleshy, orange berries. Its roots and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
