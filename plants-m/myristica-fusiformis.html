Myristica fusiformis

Myristica fusiformis, commonly known as the slender nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen belonging to the Myristicaceae family.

It is native to the Indo-Australian archipelago, including regions of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its elongated, spindle-shaped fruits that contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its unique botanical and economic properties.
