Maxillaria pudica

Maxillaria pudica, commonly known as the shy orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb that supports its foliage and flowers. The plant produces pendulous, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. Its leaves are elliptical and arranged in two rows along the pseudobulb.

Maxillaria pudica thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
