Monardella villosa var. subglabra

Monardella villosa var. subglabra, commonly known as white beardplant, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as open woodlands and grasslands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
