Mucuna toyoshimae

Mucuna toyoshimae, commonly known as the Japanese velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, hairy pods that contain elongated seeds. The plant produces a dense canopy, which helps in soil stabilization and erosion control. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils.

Mucuna toyoshimae is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.
