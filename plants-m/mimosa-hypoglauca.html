Mimosa hypoglauca

Mimosa hypoglauca, commonly known as the silver mimosa, is a small shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Mimosa hypoglauca is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
