Mentha aquatica subsp. sibirica

Mentha aquatica subsp.

sibirica, commonly known as Siberian water mint, is a perennial herb native to northern regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist or aquatic environments such as marshes, streams, and wetlands.

The plant has slender, square stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, pale blue or purple flowers appear in whorled clusters, and it produces aromatic, menthol-like compounds.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
