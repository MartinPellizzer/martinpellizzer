Mimusops balota

Mimusops balota, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant yellow flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically eaten by birds and mammals, aiding in seed dispersal. Its wood is valued for its durability and fine grain, making it useful in woodworking.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.
