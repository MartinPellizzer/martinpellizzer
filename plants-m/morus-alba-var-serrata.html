Morus alba var. serrata

Morus alba var. serrata, commonly known as the Japanese white mulberry, is a variant of the white mulberry tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its serrated leaf margins and pale green bark. This plant is often cultivated for its leaves, which are used in the production of silk. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

The tree can grow up to 10 meters tall and is valued for its ornamental qualities as well as its ecological role in supporting silkworms.
