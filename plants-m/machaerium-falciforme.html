Machaerium falciforme

Machaerium falciforme, commonly known as the sickleleaf legume, is a tropical shrub native to regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with sickle-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Machaerium falciforme is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
