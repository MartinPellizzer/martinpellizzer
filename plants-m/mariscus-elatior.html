Mariscus elatior

Mariscus elatior, commonly known as the greater marshmallow, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Malvaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in wet, marshy environments. The plant features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its mucilage-rich roots, which have soothing properties.
