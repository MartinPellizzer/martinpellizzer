Myrcia pseudosplendens

Myrcia pseudosplendens, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and glossy, elliptical leaves. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded areas of the rainforest.

Myrcia pseudosplendens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
