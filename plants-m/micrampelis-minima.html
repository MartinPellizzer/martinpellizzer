Micrampelis minima

Micrampelis minima, commonly known as the dwarf climbing bromeliad, is a small, epiphytic plant native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or branches. The plant features a short, creeping stem with small, clustered leaves that form a rosette shape. Its flowers, which are usually yellow or orange, are inconspicuous but attract small insects.

Micrampelis minima is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
