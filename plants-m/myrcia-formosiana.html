Myrcia formosiana

Myrcia formosiana, commonly known as the Taiwan myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Taiwan and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and fragrant, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are glossy and lance-shaped, while the fruit is a small, woody capsule containing aromatic seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
