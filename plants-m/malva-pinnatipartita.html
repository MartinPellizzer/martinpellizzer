Malva pinnatipartita

Malva pinnatipartita, commonly known as the broadleaf mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive serrated margin and a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers with five petals and a central cluster of stamens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Malva pinnatipartita is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
