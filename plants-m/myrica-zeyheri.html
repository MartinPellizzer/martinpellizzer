Myrica zeyheri

Myrica zeyheri, commonly known as the Zeyher's wax myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in coastal areas and is characterized by its dense, glossy dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by waxy, berry-like fruits. Myrica zeyheri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in coastal landscaping.

It has also been traditionally used in local medicine for its purported health benefits.
