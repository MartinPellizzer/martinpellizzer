Maxillaria azulensis

Maxillaria azulensis, commonly known as the Blue Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and floral structures. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, typically arranged in a fan shape around the pseudobulbs.

The flowers are highly fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths.
