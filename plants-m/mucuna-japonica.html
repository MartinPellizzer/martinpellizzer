Mucuna japonica

Mucuna japonica, commonly known as Japanese velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, hairy pods that contain flat, bean-like seeds. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It produces a significant amount of nitrogen through its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Mucuna japonica is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
