Mesynium australe subsp. glandulosum

Mesynium australe subsp.

glandulosum, commonly known as the glandular mesynium, is a perennial herb native to southern Australia. It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy, particularly along the nodes.

The leaves are lanceolate, with a prominent midrib and glandular dots on their surfaces. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring.

It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
