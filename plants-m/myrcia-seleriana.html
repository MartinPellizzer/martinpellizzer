Myrcia seleriana

Myrcia seleriana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, glossy green foliage and showy red or orange flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various birds and animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal and lowland regions.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a problematic species in certain ecosystems.
