Melastoma alpestre

Melastoma alpestre, commonly known as the alpine melastoma, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 3,000 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are small, clustered, and usually pink or red in color. Melastoma alpestre produces berries that are edible for some bird species, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
