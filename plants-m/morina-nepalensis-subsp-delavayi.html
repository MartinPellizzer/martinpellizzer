Morina nepalensis subsp. delavayi

Morina nepalensis subsp.

delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay's moringa, is a shrub native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and China. It typically grows at elevations between 2,000 and 3,500 meters, thriving in temperate and subtropical climates.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like, containing several seeds, and it is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Botanically, it is notable for its aromatic foliage and potential medicinal properties.
