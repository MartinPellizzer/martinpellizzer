Malvastrum trilobatum

Malvastrum trilobatum, commonly known as the three-lobed mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with three distinct lobes. The plant produces showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
