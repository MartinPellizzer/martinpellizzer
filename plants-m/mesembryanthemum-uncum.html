Mesembryanthemum uncum

Mesembryanthemum uncum, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent species characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and leaves that often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is well adapted to hot, dry climates.

Mesembryanthemum uncum is often used in rock gardens and as an ornamental plant due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
