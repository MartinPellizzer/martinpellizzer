Masdevallia constricta

Masdevallia constricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf masdevallia, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, often fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration pattern. The plant typically features long, narrow leaves and produces blooms in shades of purple, pink, and white. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and resilience.
