Macroclinium robustum

Macroclinium robustum, commonly known as the robust morning glory, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces long, twisting stems that can climb up to several meters in height. Its flowers are usually blue or violet in color and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Macroclinium robustum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
