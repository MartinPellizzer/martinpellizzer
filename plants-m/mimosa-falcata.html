Mimosa falcata

Mimosa falcata, commonly known as the sickle mimosa, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its root system is well-developed, allowing it to thrive in moist, sandy soils.

Mimosa falcata is often found in wetlands and along the edges of swamps, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
