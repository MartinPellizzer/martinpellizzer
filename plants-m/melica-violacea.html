Melica violacea

Melica violacea, commonly known as violet grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Melica violacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
