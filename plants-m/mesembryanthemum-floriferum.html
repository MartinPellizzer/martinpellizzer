Mesembryanthemum floriferum

Mesembryanthemum floriferum, commonly known as the flowering ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves and clusters of small, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments. It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.

The flowers are typically short-lived but produce a profuse display, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
