Microcerasus tomentosa f. cinerascens

Microcerasus tomentosa f. cinerascens, commonly known as the cinerascens form of the tomentosa cherry, is a shrub characterized by its finely hairy, grayish bark and small, oval leaves.

It produces clusters of white flowers in spring, which are followed by small, red berries in late summer. The plant is native to China and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

This form is distinguished by its lighter, ash-gray bark compared to the typical variety.
