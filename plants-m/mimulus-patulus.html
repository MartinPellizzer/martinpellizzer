Mimulus patulus

Mimulus patulus, commonly known as the slender monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems and produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with spurred petals. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Mimulus patulus is valued in gardens for its vibrant blooms and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
