Miconia quadricaulis

Miconia quadricaulis, commonly known as the four-stem miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its distinctive four-angled stems. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are usually opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface.

Miconia quadricaulis is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.
