Malva borbonica

Malva borbonica, commonly known as the Bourbon mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and somewhat woody.

Malva borbonica is often grown as an ornamental plant for its beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
