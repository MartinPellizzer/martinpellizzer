Mniopsis selaginoides

Mniopsis selaginoides, commonly known as the rockweed or seaside dandelion, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with fleshy, succulent stems and leaves that are adapted to withstand saline and arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its root system is extensive, helping it anchor in rocky or sandy soils.

Mniopsis selaginoides is often found in maritime environments, showcasing its resilience to coastal stressors.
