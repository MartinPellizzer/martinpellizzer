Myrcia tetraphylla

Myrcia tetraphylla, commonly known as the four-leaf myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, showy white or pink flowers. It typically grows in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Myrcia tetraphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions for its medicinal properties.
