Malva diphylla

Malva diphylla, commonly known as the two-leaved mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-lobed leaves and showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Malva diphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
