Mallotus dispar

Mallotus dispar, commonly known as the roughleaf mallot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, including parts of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its rough, often hairy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
