Myrcia lundiana

Myrcia lundiana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces dark red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and along stream banks.

Myrcia lundiana is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
