Macreightia myristicoides

Macreightia myristicoides, commonly known as the fragrant sandalwood tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, woody stems and leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its bark and wood are highly valued for their fragrant properties, used in traditional medicine and perfumery.
