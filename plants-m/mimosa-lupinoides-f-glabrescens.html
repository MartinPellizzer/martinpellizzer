Mimosa lupinoides f. glabrescens

Mimosa lupinoides f. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth mimosa, is a variant of the mimosa plant characterized by its reduced hair coverage on the stems and leaves.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is native to parts of South America. The plant typically features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its growth habit is usually shrub-like, with a spreading or upright form depending on environmental conditions.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
