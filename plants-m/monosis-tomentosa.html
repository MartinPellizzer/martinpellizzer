Monosis tomentosa

Monosis tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly monosis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along riverbanks. Its leaves are typically broad and oval-shaped, with a dense covering of fine hairs that give it a woolly appearance.

Monosis tomentosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
