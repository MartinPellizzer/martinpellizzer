Malva pulchella var. abyssinica

Malva pulchella var. abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian mallows, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks, where it contributes to local biodiversity.

Its fibrous roots and hairy stems are adapted to retain moisture in its native arid environment.
