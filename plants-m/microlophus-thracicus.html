Microlophus thracicus

Microlophus thracicus, commonly known as the Thrace wall lizard, is a species of gecko native to parts of southeastern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender body, long limbs, and a distinctive coloration that varies with season and environment. The plant-like appearance of its scales and the way it clings to vertical surfaces give it a botanical resemblance to certain climbing plants. While not a plant, it is often observed in habitats where flora and fauna interact closely, such as rocky outcrops and shrublands.

Its ecological role includes insect control and serving as prey for larger predators.
