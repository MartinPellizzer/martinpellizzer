Malvastrum jacens var. palmatifidum

Malvastrum jacens var. palmatifidum, commonly known as the broadleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, palmately divided leaves that can reach up to 15 inches in diameter. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This perennial is often found in disturbed areas and along roadsides due to its adaptability and rapid growth.
