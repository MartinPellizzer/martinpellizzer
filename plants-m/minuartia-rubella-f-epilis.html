Minuartia rubella f. epilis

Minuartia rubella f. epilis, commonly known as the pink sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to rocky and coastal environments.

It features small, clustered leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white, powdery wax, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces delicate, pink or red flowers that bloom in early summer, adding a vibrant touch to its habitat. It is well adapted to harsh conditions, thriving in well-drained, sandy soils.

This variety is particularly noted for its ornamental value and resilience in challenging environments.
