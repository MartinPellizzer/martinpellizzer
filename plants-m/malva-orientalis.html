Malva orientalis

Malva orientalis, commonly known as the Persian mallows, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its fibrous roots and mucilage-rich leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.
