Mussaenda philippica var. aurorae

Mussaenda philippica var. aurorae, commonly known as the golden Mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences that range in color from white to yellow, often with a reddish or orange tinge. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
