Medicago hispida f. inermis

Medicago hispida f. inermis, commonly known as the smooth goatgrass, is a variant of the goatweed species.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The flowers are typically small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

This plant is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in cultivated areas.
