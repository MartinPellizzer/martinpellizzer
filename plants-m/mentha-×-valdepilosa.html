Mentha × valdepilosa

Mentha × valdepilosa, commonly known as the hybrid mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a cross between Mentha × piperita (peppermint) and Mentha × spicata (spearmint), resulting in a plant with a strong, aromatic scent. The leaves are typically heart-shaped, serrated, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small, tubular, and clustered in whorls. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is widely cultivated for its essential oils and used in various culinary and medicinal applications.
