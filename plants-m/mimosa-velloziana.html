Mimosa velloziana

Mimosa velloziana, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fluffy pods. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different environments. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in clusters, and the plant is often used in ornamental gardening.

Mimosa velloziana is also notable for its sensitivity to touch, as its leaves fold shut when disturbed.
