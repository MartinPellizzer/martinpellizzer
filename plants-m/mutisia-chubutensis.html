Mutisia chubutensis

Mutisia chubutensis, commonly known as the Chubut mutisia, is a flowering plant native to the Patagonian region of Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Mutisia chubutensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
