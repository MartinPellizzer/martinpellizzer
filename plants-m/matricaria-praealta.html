Matricaria praealta

Matricaria praealta, commonly known as the tall chamomile, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its yellow flower heads surrounded by white, petal-like structures. The plant typically grows up to 1.5 meters tall, with finely divided, aromatic leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, producing a strong, sweet scent similar to chamomile.

Matricaria praealta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
