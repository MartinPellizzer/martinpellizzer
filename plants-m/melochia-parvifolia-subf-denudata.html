Melochia parvifolia subf. denudata

Melochia parvifolia subf.

denudata, commonly known as the nakedleaf melochia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often glabrous or nearly so, giving it its common name.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage.
