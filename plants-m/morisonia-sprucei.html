Morisonia sprucei

Morisonia sprucei, commonly known as the yellow sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves that are typically arranged opposite along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Morisonia sprucei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in arid gardens.
