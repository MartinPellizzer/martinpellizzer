Minuartia stellata subsp. pseudosaxifraga

Minuartia stellata subsp.

pseudosaxifraga, commonly known as the starry rockcress, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May.

The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid or rocky habitats. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in sunny, well-drained soils.

This subspecies is often found in crevices and on rocky slopes, where it forms dense, ground-covering patches.
