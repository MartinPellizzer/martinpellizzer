Murraya macrophylla

Murraya macrophylla, commonly known as the Chinese hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and showy yellow flowers with red centers. The plant produces orange-red berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by birds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Murraya macrophylla is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
