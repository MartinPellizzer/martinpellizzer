Meum piperatum

Meum piperatum, commonly known as the peppered meadow cranesbill, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. Meum piperatum is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy habitats.

It is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
