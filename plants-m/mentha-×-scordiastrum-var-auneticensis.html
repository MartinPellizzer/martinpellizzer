Mentha × scordiastrum var. auneticensis

Mentha × scordiastrum var. auneticensis, commonly known as the alpine peppermint, is a hybrid plant that belongs to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly menthol-like leaves and is native to the alpine regions of Central Asia. This variety typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale purple or blue, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its botanical features make it a unique and valuable species in alpine ecosystems.
