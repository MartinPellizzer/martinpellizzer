Myrcia tijucensis

Myrcia tijucensis, commonly known as Tijucan myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Myrcia tijucensis is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
