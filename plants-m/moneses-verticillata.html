Moneses verticillata

Moneses verticillata, commonly known as the white fringed gentian, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has a basal rosette of leaves. Its flowers are tubular with fringed petals and are pollinated primarily by flies and beetles.

It is a perennial herb that blooms from July to September.
