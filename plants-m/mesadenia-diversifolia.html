Mesadenia diversifolia

Mesadenia diversifolia, commonly known as the variable-leaf mesadenia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves that vary in color and texture depending on environmental conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom during the wet season. Mesadenia diversifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests or near water bodies.

Its unique foliage and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
