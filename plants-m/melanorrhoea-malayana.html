Melanorrhoea malayana

Melanorrhoea malayana, commonly known as the Malay blackwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its dark, dense wood and evergreen leaves. The plant typically grows in lowland and hill forests, reaching heights of up to 30 meters. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, while its fruit is a small, smooth, yellowish drupe.
