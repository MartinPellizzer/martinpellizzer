Manglilla pellucida

Manglilla pellucida, commonly known as the glassy mangrove, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It belongs to the family Lythraceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and stems that store water. The plant has small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the early morning. Its roots form a complex network that helps stabilize coastal soils and filter out salt.

Manglilla pellucida is an important component of mangrove ecosystems, providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
