Medicula lupulina subsp. jalasii

Medicula lupulina subsp.

jalasii, commonly known as the Cape jasmine, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often giving it a fragrant appearance.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a shrub-like structure with woody stems. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
