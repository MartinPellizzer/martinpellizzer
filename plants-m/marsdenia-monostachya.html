Marsdenia monostachya

Marsdenia monostachya, commonly known as the single-stemmed Marsdenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, often followed by follicles containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
