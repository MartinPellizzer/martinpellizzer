Medinilla andasibeensis

Medinilla andasibeensis, commonly known as the Madagascar velvet flower, is a member of the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of Madagascar, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its striking, velvety purple bracts that enclose its small, white flowers. Its leaves are typically ovate and glossy, with a waxy texture that helps retain moisture.

Medinilla andasibeensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
