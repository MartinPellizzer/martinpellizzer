Mentha viridis var. cordato-ovata

Mentha viridis var. cordato-ovata, commonly known as broad-leaf mint, is a variant of the green mint species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often slightly heart-shaped at the base. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near streams or in woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
