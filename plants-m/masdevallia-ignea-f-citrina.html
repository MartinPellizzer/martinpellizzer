Masdevallia ignea f. citrina

Masdevallia ignea f. citrina, commonly known as the Citrine Fire Orchid, is a hybrid orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, vivid yellow flowers that resemble flames. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces intricate, spurred flowers with a sweet, spicy fragrance.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or terrariums due to its specific climatic requirements.
