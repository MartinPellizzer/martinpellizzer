Myrcia gracilis var. sessiliflora

Myrcia gracilis var. sessiliflora, commonly known as the Brazilian peppercorn, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. These berries contain numerous seeds and are often dispersed by birds.

It is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
