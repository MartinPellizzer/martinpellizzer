Medicago hemicoerulea

Medicago hemicoerulea, commonly known as the narrow-leafed clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. This plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands. It has small, hairy stems and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Medicago hemicoerulea is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant for agricultural and ecological purposes.
