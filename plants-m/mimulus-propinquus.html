Mimulus propinquus

Mimulus propinquus, commonly known as the narrowleaf monkeyflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as streambanks and woodland edges. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually less than 10 centimeters in length. Its showy, orange-yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Mimulus propinquus is often found in California and Oregon, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
