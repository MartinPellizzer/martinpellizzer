Mentha arvensis var. nemoralis

Mentha arvensis var. nemoralis, commonly known as wood mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive minty aroma, while its flowers are small, tubular, and arranged in whorls.

This variety is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported cooling and digestive properties.
