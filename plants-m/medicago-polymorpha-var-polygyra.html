Medicago polymorpha var. polygyra

Medicago polymorpha var. polygyra, commonly known as Turkey rhombus, is a perennial forage legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves with rounded leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in racemes, which are followed by small, hairy pods containing lens-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in pasture and erosion control.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
