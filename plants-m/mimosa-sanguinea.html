Mimosa sanguinea

Mimosa sanguinea, commonly known as the red mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant's rapid growth and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
