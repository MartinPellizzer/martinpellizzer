Marginaria villagranii

Marginaria villagranii, commonly known as the Mexican fireweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments such as rocky soils and open fields. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Marginaria villagranii is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its striking appearance.
