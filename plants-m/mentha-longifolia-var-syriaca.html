Mentha longifolia var. syriaca

Mentha longifolia var. syriaca, commonly known as Syrian mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its fragrant leaves, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
