Mesosphaerum paniculatum

Mesosphaerum paniculatum, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, with a flowering stem that bears a panicle of small yellow flowers. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its delicate appearance.
