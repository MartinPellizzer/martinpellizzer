Myriophyllum scabratum

Myriophyllum scabratum, commonly known as narrow-leaved watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually less than 1 centimeter in length. The plant produces tiny, green flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as lakes and ponds.

Myriophyllum scabratum plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
