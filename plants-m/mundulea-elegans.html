Mundulea elegans

Mundulea elegans, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody pods containing seeds. It is adapted to survive in dry, nutrient-poor soils and is drought-tolerant.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various animals.
