Myrcia caerulescens

Myrcia caerulescens, commonly known as the blue myrtle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

Myrcia caerulescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
