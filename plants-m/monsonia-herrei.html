Monsonia herrei

Monsonia herrei, commonly known as the yellow bell orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble bells. The plant typically grows in rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Monsonia herrei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-draining soil.
