Mentha longifolia var. demissa

Mentha longifolia var. demissa, commonly known as creeping mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit and small, aromatic leaves that emit a minty fragrance. The plant produces tiny, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded areas.

Creeping mint is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal remedies.
