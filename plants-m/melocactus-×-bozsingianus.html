Melocactus × bozsingianus

Melocactus × bozsingianus, commonly known as the Bozsingianus melocactus, is a hybrid cactus species native to northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and dense, spiny areoles. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, where it thrives with minimal water and full sun exposure. Its flowers, which are usually yellow or orange, bloom in the evening and are pollinated by nocturnal insects.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
