Malacothrix californica

Malacothrix californica, commonly known as the California hairgrass, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves that form dense, low-growing clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikes, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in coastal and inland grasslands, demonstrating adaptability to various environmental conditions.

Malacothrix californica plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
