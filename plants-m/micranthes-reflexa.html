Micranthes reflexa

Micranthes reflexa, commonly known as the bentleaf sandwort, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The leaves are narrow, often curled or reflexed at the edges, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as dunes or disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
