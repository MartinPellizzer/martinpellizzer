Melicope laxa

Melicope laxa, commonly known as the roughleaf melicope, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in lowland rainforests. The plant has simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves that are usually dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers are small and fragrant, typically clustered in panicles, and it produces small, woody fruits.

Melicope laxa is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses in its native regions.
