Myrtus nivellei subsp. nivellei

Myrtus nivellei subsp.

nivellei, commonly known as the Corsican myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, aromatic leaves with a silvery underside, and it produces white, five-petaled flowers in spring.

The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

The berries it produces are edible but are typically used in local cuisines or traditional medicine.
