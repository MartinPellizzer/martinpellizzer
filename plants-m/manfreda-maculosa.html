Manfreda maculosa

Manfreda maculosa, commonly known as the spotted manfreda, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, and its striking, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are capable of storing water, allowing it to survive in drought conditions.

The species is also noted for its ability to produce offsets, which helps in its propagation and spread.
