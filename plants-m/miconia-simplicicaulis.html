Miconia simplicicaulis

Miconia simplicicaulis, commonly known as the silky miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Miconia simplicicaulis is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.
