Myristica laurifolia

Myristica laurifolia, commonly known as the nutmeg-laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed, which is often used in traditional medicine. Its aromatic properties make it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
