Medica radiata

Medica radiata, commonly known as the radiate medica, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant is native to certain regions of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional herbal remedies.
