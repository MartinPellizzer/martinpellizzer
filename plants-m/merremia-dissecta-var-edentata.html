Merremia dissecta var. edentata

Merremia dissecta var. edentata, commonly known as the toothless morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves that are typically green with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, blooming in the early morning. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
