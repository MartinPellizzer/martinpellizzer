Medinilla purpurea

Medinilla purpurea, commonly known as the purple velvet plant, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often giving it a velvety appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Medinilla purpurea is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
