Mapania bancana

Mapania bancana, commonly known as the banana tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves that form a pseudostem. The plant produces a fruit known as the banana, which is rich in nutrients and widely consumed globally. Its growth cycle involves the development of a flower inside the pseudostem, which eventually leads to fruiting.

Mapania bancana requires warm, humid climates and well-drained soil to thrive.
