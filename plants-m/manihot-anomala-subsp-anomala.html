Manihot anomala subsp. anomala

Manihot anomala subsp.

anomala, commonly known as arrowleaf or arrowhead plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its milky sap and diverse ecological roles.
