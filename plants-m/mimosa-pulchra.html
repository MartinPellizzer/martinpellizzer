Mimosa pulchra

Mimosa pulchra, commonly known as the pink mimosas, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through its root nodules. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its unique, sensitive leaves fold shut when touched, adding an interesting botanical feature to its appearance.
