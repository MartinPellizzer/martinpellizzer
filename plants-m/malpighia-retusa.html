Malpighia retusa

Malpighia retusa, commonly known as the scarlet gooseberry, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red, fleshy fruits that are often mistaken for berries. The plant produces small, white flowers with five petals, and its leaves are typically ovate with serrated edges. The fruits are edible, though they have a tart flavor and are often used in culinary applications or as ornamental plants.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is valued for both its aesthetic appeal and nutritional content.
