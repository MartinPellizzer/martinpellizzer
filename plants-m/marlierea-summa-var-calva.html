Marlierea summa var. calva

Marlierea summa var. calva, commonly known as the white-flowered marlierea, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a smooth upper surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often with a faintly fragrant aroma. Its bark is smooth and grayish in color, and it is often found growing in moist, shaded environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
