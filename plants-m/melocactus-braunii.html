Melocactus braunii

Melocactus braunii, commonly known as the Braun's melon cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a solitary cactus with a swollen, ribbed stem that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments.

The plant is well adapted to full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
