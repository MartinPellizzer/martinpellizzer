Melaleuca parviflora

Melaleuca parviflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters. The plant is part of the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known species like eucalyptus and tea tree. It is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.

Melaleuca parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
