Mimosa cumana

Mimosa cumana, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often in response to touch or movement. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Mimosa cumana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
