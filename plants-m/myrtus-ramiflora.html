Myrtus ramiflora

Myrtus ramiflora, commonly known as the flowering myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features fragrant, dark green leaves and produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers. The plant is characterized by its branched growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Myrtus ramiflora thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

It is also valued for its aromatic properties and historical use in traditional medicine.
