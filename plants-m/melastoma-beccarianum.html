Melastoma beccarianum

Melastoma beccarianum, commonly known as the Bornean melastoma, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its inflorescence consists of clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or red, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Melastoma beccarianum is often found in shaded, moist environments and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitat.
