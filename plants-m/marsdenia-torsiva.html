Marsdenia torsiva

Marsdenia torsiva, commonly known as the twisted Marsdenia, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, with five petals and prominent stamens. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
