Malvastrum jacens var. genuinum

Malvastrum jacens var. genuinum, commonly known as the common mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features palmate leaves with lobed margins and produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens. It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage and long blooming period.

The species is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
