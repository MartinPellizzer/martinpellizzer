Miconia paraguayensis var. paraguayensis

Miconia paraguayensis var. paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by birds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
