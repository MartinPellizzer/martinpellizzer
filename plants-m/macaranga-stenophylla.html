Macaranga stenophylla

Macaranga stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf macaranga, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

The species is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
