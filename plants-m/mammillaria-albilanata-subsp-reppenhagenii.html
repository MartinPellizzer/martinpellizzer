Mammillaria albilanata subsp. reppenhagenii

Mammillaria albilanata subsp.

reppenhagenii, commonly known as the White Lanata Cactus, is a species native to Mexico. It is characterized by its pale green to white cylindrical stems covered in dense, white spines.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and prefers full sun exposure. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
