Magnolia aequinoctialis

Magnolia aequinoctialis, commonly known as the equinox magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in early spring, often around the time of the equinox. The leaves are broad, ovate, and have a smooth, glossy surface. The tree produces elliptical, red-brown fruits that contain winged seeds.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
