Mammillaria bergenii

Mammillaria bergenii, commonly known as the Bergen's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and dense covering of white to pale yellow spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its compact size and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
