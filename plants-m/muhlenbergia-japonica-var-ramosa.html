Muhlenbergia japonica var. ramosa

Muhlenbergia japonica var. ramosa, commonly known as the Japanese muhlenbergia, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that produce fluffy, silver-gray seed heads, which are visually striking. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adding a soft texture to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate appearance and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
