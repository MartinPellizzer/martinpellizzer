Mandarus divaricata

Mandarus divaricata, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with small, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in Mediterranean climates.
