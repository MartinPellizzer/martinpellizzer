Maillardia occidentalis

Maillardia occidentalis, commonly known as the western marigold, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant features showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Maillardia occidentalis thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant in arid environments.
