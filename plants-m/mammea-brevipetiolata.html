Mammea brevipetiolata

Mammea brevipetiolata, commonly known as the short-stemmed mammea, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by woody, oval-shaped fruits containing several seeds.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the tree is valued for its ornamental appearance and potential ecological benefits.
