Morella kraussiana

Morella kraussiana, commonly known as the Cape waxwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its waxy, leathery leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing one or two seeds. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
