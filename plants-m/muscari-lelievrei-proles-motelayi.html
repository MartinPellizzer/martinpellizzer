Muscari lelievrei proles motelayi

Muscari lelievrei proles motelayi, commonly known as the Motelayi musk mallow, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to deep purple. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and distinctive musky fragrance.

This species is part of the Amaryllidaceae family and is well-suited for rock gardens and shady borders.
