Maerua siamensis

Maerua siamensis, commonly known as the Siam mallow, is a shrub native to tropical Africa and parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
