Mamillopsis

Mamillopsis, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Malvaceae.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The flowers are typically clustered in umbels and are usually yellow or greenish in color. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.
