Myrrhinium lanceolatum

Myrrhinium lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceleaf myrrhplant, is a member of the Araceae family, native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces striking inflorescences composed of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of many aroid species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some tropical gardens and botanical collections.
