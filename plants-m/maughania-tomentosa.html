Maughania tomentosa

Maughania tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly maughania, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a humid, temperate climate.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
