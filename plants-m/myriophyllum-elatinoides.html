Myriophyllum elatinoides

Myriophyllum elatinoides, commonly known as water starwort, is a submerged aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It has slender, branching stems that are often covered in tiny, scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown, and it relies on water for reproduction and growth. Its structure allows it to thrive in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Myriophyllum elatinoides plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
