Mentha hirsuta var. pyramidalis

Mentha hirsuta var. pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal hairy mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Mentha hirsuta var. pyramidalis is often used in herbal medicine and as an ornamental plant in gardens.
