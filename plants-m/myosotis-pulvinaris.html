Myosotis pulvinaris

Myosotis pulvinaris, commonly known as the cushion bluebell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It features a compact, cushion-like growth habit with dense clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or rocky areas.

Myosotis pulvinaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
