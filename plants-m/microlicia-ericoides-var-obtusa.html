Microlicia ericoides var. obtusa

Microlicia ericoides var. obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leaved microlicia, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its compact growth habit. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are typically oval or elliptic in shape, with a dense, dark green coloration. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late spring or early summer.

This species is often found in rocky or well-drained soils, thriving in cool, humid environments.
