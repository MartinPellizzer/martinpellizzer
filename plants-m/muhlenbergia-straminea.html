Muhlenbergia straminea

Muhlenbergia straminea, commonly known as the silver bluestem, is a warm-season grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and tufted flower heads that appear in late summer. This plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Muhlenbergia straminea is also valued for its role in stabilizing soils and supporting local wildlife.
