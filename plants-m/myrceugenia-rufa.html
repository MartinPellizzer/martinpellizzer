Myrceugenia rufa

Myrceugenia rufa, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the temperate regions of southern Brazil, Paraguay, and Argentina. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. It produces small, reddish berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Myrceugenia rufa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
