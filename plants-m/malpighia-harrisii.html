Malpighia harrisii

Malpighia harrisii, commonly known as the Harris's acerola, is a small shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its small, red, edible fruits that resemble cherries. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

The fruit is rich in vitamin C and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
