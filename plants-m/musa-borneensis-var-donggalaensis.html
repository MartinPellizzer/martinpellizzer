Musa borneensis var. donggalaensis

Musa borneensis var. donggalaensis, commonly known as the Donggala banana, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and clustered fruiting stems. The plant produces edible bananas that are typically harvested when they are still green and unripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil and requires regular watering for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for both its agricultural yield and ornamental qualities.
