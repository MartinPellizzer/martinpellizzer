Marsdenia tomentosa

Marsdenia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly Marsdenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are typically opposite and ovate, while its stems are covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence.
