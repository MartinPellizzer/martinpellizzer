Mentha × gillotii

Mentha × gillotii, commonly known as giant mint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Mentha hangelii and Mentha × piperita.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a strong menthol scent. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems, typically in late summer. Mentha × gillotii is native to parts of Europe and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas.

Its aromatic properties make it a popular choice for ornamental and medicinal purposes.
