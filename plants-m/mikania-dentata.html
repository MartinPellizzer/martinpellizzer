Mikania dentata

Mikania dentata, commonly known as the giant horseweed, is a climbing vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in disturbed habitats and is often found in wetlands and along roadsides.

Mikania dentata is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense mats, making it an invasive species in some regions.
