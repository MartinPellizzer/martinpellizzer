Microcoecia repens

Microcoecia repens, commonly known as the creeping microcoecia, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its root system is fibrous and helps it anchor in rocky or sandy soils.

Microcoecia repens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
