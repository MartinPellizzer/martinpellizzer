Magnolia gilbertoi

Magnolia gilbertoi, commonly known as the Gilbert's magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves with a smooth margin. Its bark is typically grayish-brown and becomes fissured with age.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
