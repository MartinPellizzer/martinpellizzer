Mentha canescens var. duffourii

Mentha canescens var. duffourii, commonly known as the Duffour's mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically arranged in terminal clusters. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its aromatic leaves and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for use in traditional herbal remedies.
