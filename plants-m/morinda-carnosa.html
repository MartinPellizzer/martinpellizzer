Morinda carnosa

Morinda carnosa, commonly known as the red moringa or red Indian woad, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Morinda carnosa is valued for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional remedies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
