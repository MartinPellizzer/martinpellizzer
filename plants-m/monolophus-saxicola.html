Monolophus saxicola

Monolophus saxicola, commonly known as the rock lichen, is a symbiotic organism composed of a fungus and algae or cyanobacteria.

It thrives in rocky habitats, particularly in arid and semi-arid regions, where it forms a close relationship with its host substrate. The plant's structure is typically crust-like, adhering tightly to surfaces such as stone or soil. It lacks true roots, stems, and leaves, relying instead on its symbiotic partners for nutrients and photosynthesis.

Monolophus saxicola plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing microhabitats for other organisms in its environment.
