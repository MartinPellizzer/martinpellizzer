Mentha brevispicata

Mentha brevispicata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a serrated edge and emit a strong menthol scent when crushed.

Mentha brevispicata is often used in traditional medicine for its cooling and antiseptic properties.
