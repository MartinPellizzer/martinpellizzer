Mimosa malacocentra

Mimosa malacocentra, commonly known as the golden mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched, a trait known as thigmonastic response. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and ability to tolerate various soil conditions contribute to its adaptability in different environments.
