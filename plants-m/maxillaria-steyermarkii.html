Maxillaria steyermarkii

Maxillaria steyermarkii, commonly known as the Steyermark's maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within forest understories. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white with intricate markings, and they bloom in clusters during the warmer months.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
