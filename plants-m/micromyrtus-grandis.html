Micromyrtus grandis

Micromyrtus grandis, commonly known as the broad-leaved paperbark, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a pale, fibrous layer beneath. Its lance-shaped leaves are glossy and dark green, often with a slight waxy coating.

Micromyrtus grandis typically grows in coastal heathlands and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils.
