Myrcia chapadinhaeana

Myrcia chapadinhaeana, commonly known as the Chapadinha myrtle, is a shrub native to Brazil, particularly in the states of Bahia and Pernambuco.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a small, red drupe that contains several seeds.

Myrcia chapadinhaeana is often found in humid, tropical environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
