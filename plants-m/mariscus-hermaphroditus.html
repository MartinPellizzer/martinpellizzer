It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in moist, calcareous soils along streambanks and wet meadows. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a distinctive, feathery appearance.

Mariscus hermaphroditus is primarily a herbaceous perennial, though it may occasionally exhibit semi-woody growth in certain conditions.
