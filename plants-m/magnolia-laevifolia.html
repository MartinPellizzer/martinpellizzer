Magnolia laevifolia

Magnolia laevifolia, commonly known as the Chinese magnolia or the broad-leaf magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The leaves are broad, ovate, and have a smooth, glossy surface. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
