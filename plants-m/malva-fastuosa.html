Malva fastuosa

Malva fastuosa, commonly known as the showy mallows, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple, with five petals and a prominent central column. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft, velvety texture.

Malva fastuosa is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
