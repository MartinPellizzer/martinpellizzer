Maxillaria liparophylla

Maxillaria liparophylla, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers with a lip that resembles a slipper. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a contrasting yellow or white lip, and it blooms during the warmer months.

Maxillaria liparophylla is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
