Melilotus officinalis var. expansa

Melilotus officinalis var. expansa, commonly known as broadleaf sweet clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with broad, lance-shaped leaflets and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to various regions around the world. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

Broadleaf sweet clover is valued for its medicinal properties and as a forage crop for livestock.
