Mucuna lane-poolei

Mucuna lane-poolei, commonly known as the purple velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, purple flowers. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it both a useful agricultural plant and an invasive species in some areas.
