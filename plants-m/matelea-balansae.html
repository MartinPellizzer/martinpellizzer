Matelea balansae

Matelea balansae, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, lantern-like blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its nocturnal flowering habit, it is also called the night-blooming cereus.
