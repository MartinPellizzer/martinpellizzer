Mapouria cephalantha

Mapouria cephalantha, commonly known as the African orchid or the white orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Mapouria cephalantha is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
