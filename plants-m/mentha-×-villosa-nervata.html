Mentha × villosa-nervata

Mentha × villosa-nervata, commonly known as the woolly-veined mint, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both Mentha villosa and Mentha nervata.

It is a perennial herb with square stems and opposite leaves that are often covered in fine, woolly hairs. The leaves are typically gray-green in color, with prominent veins that give the plant its distinctive appearance. It produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its aromatic properties and potential medicinal uses.
