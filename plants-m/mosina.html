Mosina

Mosina, commonly known as the white wood sorrel, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Violaceae.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, delicate white or pale pink flowers. Native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia, it thrives in moist, shady environments. The plant contains oxalic acid, which can be toxic in large quantities.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and bloom.
