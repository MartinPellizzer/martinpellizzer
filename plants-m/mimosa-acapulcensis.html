Mimosa acapulcensis

Mimosa acapulcensis, commonly known as the Acapulco mimosa, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its small, pod-like fruits contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Mimosa acapulcensis is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
