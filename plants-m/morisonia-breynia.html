Morisonia breynia

Morisonia breynia, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often appearing during the holiday season. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and usually elliptical or ovate in shape.

Morisonia breynia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
