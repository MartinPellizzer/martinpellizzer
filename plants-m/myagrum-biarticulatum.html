Myagrum biarticulatum

Myagrum biarticulatum, commonly known as the two-bent myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a compact, bushy form with glossy, dark green foliage. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink and appear in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
