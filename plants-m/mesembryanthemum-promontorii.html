Mesembryanthemum promontorii

Mesembryanthemum promontorii, commonly known as the Cape Peninsula sandflower, is a succulent plant native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in its arid environment. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
