Magdaris nodiflora

Magdaris nodiflora, commonly known as the clustered flowering plant, is a species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact shrub form and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically features dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
