Monanthes laxiflora f. chlorotica

Monanthes laxiflora f. chlorotica, commonly known as the chlorotic form of the laxiflora lily, is a variant of the Monanthes laxiflora species.

This plant is characterized by its reduced leaf size and a pale, chlorotic coloration, which is a result of genetic variation. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in woodland areas. The flowers are small and white, with a subtle fragrance, and they bloom in early summer.

Its unique appearance makes it a rare and interesting subject for botanical study.
