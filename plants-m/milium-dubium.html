Milium dubium

Milium dubium, commonly known as the false brome, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white or pale green flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense clumps.

Milium dubium is often found in moist, grassy habitats and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
