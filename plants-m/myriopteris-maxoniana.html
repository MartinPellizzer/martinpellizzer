Myriopteris maxoniana

It is characterized by its delicate, intricate fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Myriopteris maxoniana plays a role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat by contributing to soil stability and providing microhabitats for various organisms.
