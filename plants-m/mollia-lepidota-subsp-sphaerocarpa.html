Mollia lepidota subsp. sphaerocarpa

Mollia lepidota subsp.

sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the woolly mollia, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically gray-green and covered in a fine layer of hair.

This subspecies is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun.
