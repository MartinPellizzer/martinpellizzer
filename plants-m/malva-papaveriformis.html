Malva papaveriformis

Malva papaveriformis, commonly known as the poppy mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, showy, pink to purple flowers that resemble those of the poppy, hence its common name. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, brown seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Malva papaveriformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
