Mulinum ulicinum

Mulinum ulicinum, commonly known as field mullein, is a biennial plant belonging to the Scrophulariaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are typically covered in soft, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces tall, erect stems that bear clusters of yellow-orange flowers in late summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used for its soothing effects on respiratory ailments.
