Melinis maitlandii

Melinis maitlandii, commonly known as Maitland's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Melinis maitlandii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone environments.
