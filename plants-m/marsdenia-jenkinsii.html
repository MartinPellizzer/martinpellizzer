Marsdenia jenkinsii

Marsdenia jenkinsii, commonly known as the Jenkins' Marsdenia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asclepiadaceae family.

It is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and produces yellowish-white flowers with a distinctive corona. Its fruits are follicles that contain numerous small, black seeds.

Marsdenia jenkinsii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
