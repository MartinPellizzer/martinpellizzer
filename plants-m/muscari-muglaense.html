Muscari muglaense

Muscari muglaense, commonly known as the Mugla squill, is a bulbous plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.

Muscari muglaense is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
