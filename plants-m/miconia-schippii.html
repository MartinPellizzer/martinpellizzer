Miconia schippii

Miconia schippii, commonly known as the Schippi's miconia, is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences with small, pink to red flowers that attract various pollinators. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous tiny seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

Miconia schippii is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to shade, making it a common species in understory habitats.
