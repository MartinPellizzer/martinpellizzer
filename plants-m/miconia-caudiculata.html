Miconia caudiculata

Miconia caudiculata, commonly known as the pineapple guava, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, woody stem and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits resembling pineapples in appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
