Minthostachys mandoniana

Minthostachys mandoniana, commonly known as the Andean mint, is a perennial herb native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a fragrant, mint-like aroma, and it produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

Due to its unique scent and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
