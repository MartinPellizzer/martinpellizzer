Manihot tripartita var. porrecta

Manihot tripartita var. porrecta, commonly known as the arrowleaf cassava, is a variant of the cassava plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are broad and heart-shaped, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces edible roots that are rich in starch and used in various culinary applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

Its cultivation is significant in regions where it serves as a staple food source.
