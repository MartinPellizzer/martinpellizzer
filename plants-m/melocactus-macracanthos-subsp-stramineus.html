Melocactus macracanthos subsp. stramineus

Melocactus macracanthos subsp.

stramineus, commonly known as the yellow-tufted melocactus, is a slow-growing cactus native to Brazil. It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and prominent, yellowish spines that give it its common name.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for desert gardens and collections.
