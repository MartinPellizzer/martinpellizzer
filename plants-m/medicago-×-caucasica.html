Medicago × caucasica

Medicago × caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian clover, is a perennial legume native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in agricultural settings. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in forage systems.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
