Melissa rotundifolia

Melissa rotundifolia, commonly known as lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, serrated leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation. Its leaves are often used in teas and aromatherapy for their calming properties.

Melissa rotundifolia is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
