Myagrum utriculatum

Myagrum utriculatum, commonly known as the bladder nut or bladderpod, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits that resemble bladders, which are enclosed in a fleshy, red or orange covering. The plant has simple, alternate leaves and produces small white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and sand dunes.

Myagrum utriculatum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique fruit structure.
