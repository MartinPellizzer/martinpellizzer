Machaerium fertile

Machaerium fertile, commonly known as the hairy machaerium, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its growth habit includes both shrub and tree forms, depending on the species and environmental conditions.

Machaerium fertile is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in various soil types.
