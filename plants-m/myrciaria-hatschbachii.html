Myrciaria hatschbachii

Myrciaria hatschbachii, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a small evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both landscaping and horticultural cultivation.
