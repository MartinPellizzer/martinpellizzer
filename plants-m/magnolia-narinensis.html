Magnolia narinensis

Magnolia narinensis, commonly known as the Nariño magnolia, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of Colombia.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 10–15 meters, featuring a broad, rounded crown. The plant is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in early spring, often preceding the foliage. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.
