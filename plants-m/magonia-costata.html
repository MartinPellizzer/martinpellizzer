Magonia costata

Magonia costata, commonly known as the coastal magnolia, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along shorelines, showcasing its adaptability to coastal environments. Its growth habit is usually shrub-like, with multiple stems forming dense thickets.

Magonia costata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
