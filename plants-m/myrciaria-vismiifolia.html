Myrciaria vismiifolia

Myrciaria vismiifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil and is characterized by its dark purple to black berries. The plant has small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface and produces fragrant white flowers. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

The berries are edible and have a tart, slightly sweet flavor, making them popular in local cuisine.
