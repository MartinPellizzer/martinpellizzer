Mimosa brevipetiolata var. pauperoides

Mimosa brevipetiolata var. pauperoides, commonly known as the poorleaf mimosa, is a low-growing shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It features small, delicate leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems, giving it a mat-like appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in spherical clusters, which are followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salinity and wind exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
