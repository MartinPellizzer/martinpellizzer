Mimulus laceratus

Mimulus laceratus, commonly known as the fringed monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as streambanks and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture.

Mimulus laceratus is a perennial species that plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
