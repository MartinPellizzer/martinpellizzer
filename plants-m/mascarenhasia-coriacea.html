Mascarenhasia coriacea

Mascarenhasia coriacea, commonly known as the Mascarene scabious, is a flowering plant native to the Mascarene Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It is characterized by its rough, bark-like stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to coastal and dry environments.
