Mikania tafallana

Mikania tafallana, commonly known as the Andean Mikania, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and slender, hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are arranged in clusters, typical of the daisy family. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

Mikania tafallana is often found in moist, shaded areas and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
