Melica aurantiaca var. montevidensis

Melica aurantiaca var. montevidensis, commonly known as the red panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, often reddish-colored stems. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are typically golden or reddish in color.

This variety is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.
