Mandevilla minor

Mandevilla minor, commonly known as the dwarf mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a vine that produces clusters of trumpet-shaped, fragrant flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green and are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and plenty of sunlight.

Mandevilla minor is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
