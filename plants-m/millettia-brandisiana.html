Millettia brandisiana

Millettia brandisiana, commonly known as the Chinese silk tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain flat, seed-rich seeds used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its growth habit and foliage make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm regions.
