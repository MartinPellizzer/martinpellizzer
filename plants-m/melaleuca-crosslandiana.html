Melaleuca crosslandiana

Melaleuca crosslandiana, commonly known as the Northern Territory bottlebrush, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flowers, which are typically red or pink in color, and its narrow, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

Melaleuca crosslandiana is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
