Myrtus umbellulifera

Myrtus umbellulifera, commonly known as the Mediterranean myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, dark green leaves and small white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

Myrtus umbellulifera is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
