Matthiola dentata

Matthiola dentata, commonly known as the toothed stock, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring slender stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Matthiola dentata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a mild, spicy scent.
