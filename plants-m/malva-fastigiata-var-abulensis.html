Malva fastigiata var. abulensis

Malva fastigiata var. abulensis, commonly known as the Abulensis mallows, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to mauve flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves with a hairy texture, and its stems are typically covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as roadsides and gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive floral display.
