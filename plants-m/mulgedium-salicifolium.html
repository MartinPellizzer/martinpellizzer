Mulgedium salicifolium

Mulgedium salicifolium, commonly known as the willow-leaf mulgedium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and heart-shaped, willow-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, which appear in late spring to early summer. Mulgedium salicifolium thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
