Maxillaria gigantea subsp. condorensis

Maxillaria gigantea subsp.

condorensis, commonly known as the Condore orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America. It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late winter or early spring.

The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually broad and strap-like, and it produces inflorescences with multiple flowers in a raceme.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
