Mesomelaena stygia var. preissii

Mesomelaena stygia var. preissii, commonly known as the Black Nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are toxic to humans and animals. The plant has alternate, simple leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The flowers are small and greenish-white, often clustered in umbels, and bloom in spring.
