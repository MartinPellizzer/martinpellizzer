Mitracarpus fernandesii

Mitracarpus fernandesii, commonly known as the Socotra cypress, is a unique evergreen tree native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its distinctive, scale-like leaves and twisted, gnarled trunk. The plant exhibits a slow growth rate and can live for several centuries, making it a relic of ancient flora. It thrives in arid and rocky environments, adapting to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is protected and considered a vulnerable species.
