Melilotus petitpierrianus

Melilotus petitpierrianus, commonly known as the dwarf melilot, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of around 30 centimeters and has trifoliate leaves composed of three small leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Melilotus petitpierrianus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in traditional medicine.
