Mesosphaerum longiflorum

Mesosphaerum longiflorum, commonly known as the long-flowered mesosphaerum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Mesosphaeraceae and is characterized by its delicate, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it typically grows in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
