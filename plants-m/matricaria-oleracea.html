Matricaria oleracea

Matricaria oleracea, commonly known as garden chamomile, is an annual herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellow flowers with white centers, resembling daisies. The plant is often grown for its aromatic properties and is used in herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its essential oils, derived from the flowers, are valued for their calming and anti-inflammatory effects.
