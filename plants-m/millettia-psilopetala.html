Millettia psilopetala

Millettia psilopetala, commonly known as the slender milkwood tree, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries such as Kenya, Tanzania, and Uganda. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often pinnate and have a feathery appearance. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by flat, pod-like fruits.

The tree is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
