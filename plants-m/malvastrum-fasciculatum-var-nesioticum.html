Malvastrum fasciculatum var. nesioticum

Malvastrum fasciculatum var. nesioticum, commonly known as the hummingbird flower, is a striking perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its nectar-rich blossoms attract hummingbirds and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
