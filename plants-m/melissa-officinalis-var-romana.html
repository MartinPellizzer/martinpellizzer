Melissa officinalis var. romana

Melissa officinalis var. romana, commonly known as lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of western Asia.

It features fragrant, serrated leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, lemony scent, which comes from compounds like citral and limonene. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.

Its leaves are often used in teas and traditional medicine for their calming and soothing properties.
