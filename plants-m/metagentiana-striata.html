Metagentiana striata

Metagentiana striata, commonly known as the striated metagentian, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that display a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
