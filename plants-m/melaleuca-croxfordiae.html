Melaleuca croxfordiae

Melaleuca croxfordiae, commonly known as the Croxford's bottlebrush, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the coastal regions of northern Australia, particularly Queensland and the Northern Territory. The plant is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical, bottlebrush-like flowers that are typically red or pink in color. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal heathlands.

Melaleuca croxfordiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh coastal conditions.
