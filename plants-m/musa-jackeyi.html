Musa jackeyi

Musa jackeyi, commonly known as the Jackey's banana, is a tropical plant belonging to the Musaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and clustered inflorescences. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or red when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in various regions for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
