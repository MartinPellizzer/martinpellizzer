Myriostachya wightiana var. longispicula

Myriostachya wightiana var. longispicula, commonly known as the long-spined panic grass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of South Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear long, slender bracts. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and fibrous, with a bluish-green coloration.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
