Megastachya oxylepis var. capitata

Megastachya oxylepis var. capitata, commonly known as the golden crown beardroot, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking yellow inflorescence, which consists of numerous narrow, tubular flowers arranged in a dense, terminal cluster. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its foliage consists of linear, grass-like leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscapic gardens due to its low water requirements.
