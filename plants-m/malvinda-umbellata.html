Malvinda umbellata

Malvinda umbellata, commonly known as the white mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus, and is characterized by its showy, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb with hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are arranged in umbels, giving it the species name *umbellata*, and it produces small, round seeds enclosed in a fluffy, cotton-like capsule.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.
