Myosotis scorpioides subsp. palustris

Myosotis scorpioides subsp.

palustris, commonly known as marsh forget-me-not, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as marshes, ditches, and stream banks.

The plant features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

The species is often found in wetlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
