Matelea yanomamica

Matelea yanomamica, commonly known as the Yanomami moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, white, nocturnal blossoms that open at night and close by morning. The plant belongs to the Apocynaceae family, which includes milkweed species, and features showy, tubular flowers with prominent stamens. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while the stems are covered in fine hairs.

This plant is culturally significant to the Yanomami people, who use it for medicinal and ritual purposes.
