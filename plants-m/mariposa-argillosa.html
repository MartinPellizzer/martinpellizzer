Mariposa argillosa

Mariposa argillosa, commonly known as the clay larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant prefers moist, clay-rich soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Mariposa argillosa is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buttercups and anemones.
