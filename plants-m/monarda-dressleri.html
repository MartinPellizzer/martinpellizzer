Monarda dressleri

Monarda dressleri, commonly known as Lemon Mint, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces showy, tubular red or orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are fragrant and have a citrus-like scent, making it popular in herbal teas and culinary uses.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun.
