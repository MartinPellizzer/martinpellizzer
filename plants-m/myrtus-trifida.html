Myrtus trifida

Myrtus trifida, commonly known as the three-lobed myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are often fragrant and have a three-lobed shape. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale pink and bloom in spring.

The berries that follow are dark blue to black and are edible, though they are not commonly consumed by humans.
