Menispermum populifolium

Menispermum populifolium, commonly known as the American prickly ash.

It is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America, characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its roots contain alkaloids and have been used in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in woodland environments.
