Myriocarpa densiflora

Myriocarpa densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered myriocarpa, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often consumed by various wildlife species. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with thick leaves and deep roots that help it survive in harsh environments.

Myriocarpa densiflora plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing food for local fauna.
