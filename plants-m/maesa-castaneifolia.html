Maesa castaneifolia

Maesa castaneifolia, commonly known as the Chinese holly or red holly, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny branches and small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits that persist through the winter. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.

Maesa castaneifolia is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
