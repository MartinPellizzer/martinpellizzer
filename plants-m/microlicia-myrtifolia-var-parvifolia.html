Microlicia myrtifolia var. parvifolia

Microlicia myrtifolia var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved microlicia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the Rubiaceae family.
