Maerua scebelensis

Maerua scebelensis, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces edible fig-like fruits that are an important food source for both wildlife and humans. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Maerua scebelensis plays a significant role in stabilizing soils and supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
