Monanthotaxis lucidula

Monanthotaxis lucidula, commonly known as the glossy monanthotaxis, is a rare and striking plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a glossy, smooth texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the wet season. Its unique leaf structure and floral arrangement make it a subject of interest in botanical studies.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is often found in protected areas.
