Myrceugenia myrcioides var. loefgreniana

Myrceugenia myrcioides var. loefgreniana, commonly known as the Loefgren's myrtle, is a species native to the temperate regions of South America, particularly in Chile.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped, glossy dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. It produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers a humid, temperate climate with partial to full sunlight.
