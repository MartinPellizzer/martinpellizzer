Matelea fournieri

Matelea fournieri, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is known for its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in shaded, moist environments. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by moths, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that contain the seeds.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
