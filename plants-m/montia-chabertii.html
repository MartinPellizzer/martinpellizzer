Montia chabertii

Montia chabertii, commonly known as Chabert's commonweed, is a small, herbaceous annual plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant has opposite, elliptic leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters. Its flowering period occurs from spring to early summer, and it relies on insect pollination.

Montia chabertii is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a characteristic species of riparian habitats.
