Mimosa malacocentra var. angustifolia

Mimosa malacocentra var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. It is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils, where it contributes to local biodiversity.

Its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed sites make it both ecologically significant and sometimes invasive.
