Manihot aesculifolia

Manihot aesculifolia, commonly known as the arrowleaf manioc, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with arrowhead-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the spurge family. Its roots are used in traditional medicine and as a source of starch.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its distinctive foliage.
