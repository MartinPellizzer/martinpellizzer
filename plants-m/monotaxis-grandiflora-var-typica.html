Monotaxis grandiflora var. typica

Monotaxis grandiflora var. typica, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter, typically displaying vibrant red or pink hues. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Monotaxis grandiflora var. typica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
