Mesembryanthemum medium

Mesembryanthemum medium, commonly known as the ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to store water in its leaves allows it to survive in arid environments.
