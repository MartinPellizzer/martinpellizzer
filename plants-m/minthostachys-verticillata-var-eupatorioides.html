Minthostachys verticillata var. eupatorioides

Minthostachys verticillata var. eupatorioides, commonly known as the creeping mint or false mint, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It features aromatic, hairy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine for their cooling properties. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its essential oils contain compounds like menthol and carvone, contributing to its distinctive minty aroma.
