Myrcia biptera

Myrcia biptera, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Myrcia biptera is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
