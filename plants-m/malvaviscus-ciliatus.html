Malvaviscus ciliatus

Malvaviscus ciliatus, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Mexican mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. The plant is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its spread.
