Magnolia amazonica

Magnolia amazonica, commonly known as the Amazon magnolia, is a striking tropical tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a smooth surface. Its bark is smooth and gray, often exuding a milky sap when damaged.

Magnolia amazonica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its floral display.
