Myrtillocactus geometrizans var. grandiareolatus

Myrtillocactus geometrizans var. grandiareolatus, commonly known as the giant areola cactus, is a rare and striking species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems covered in large, rounded areolas that are often adorned with spines. The plant produces vibrant purple or blue flowers during the summer months, adding to its ornamental appeal. Its slow growth rate makes it a prized specimen among cactus enthusiasts.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat-specific requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
