Malva

Malva, commonly known as mallows, is a genus of flowering plants in the mallow family, Malvaceae.

These plants are characterized by their showy, often pink or purple flowers and velvety leaves. They are typically herbaceous, with some species being shrubs or even trees. Malva species are native to various regions, including Europe, Asia, and Africa, and many are cultivated for their ornamental value.

They thrive in moist, well-drained soils and are often found in gardens and wild habitats.
