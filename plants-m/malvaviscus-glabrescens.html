Malvaviscus glabrescens

Malvaviscus glabrescens, commonly known as the silky mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial or shrub, often found in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated primarily by insects.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
