Mesosphaerum rubicundum

Mesosphaerum rubicundum, commonly known as the red-tipped globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular, spiny fruit capsules and bright red bracts that surround the flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss.

Mesosphaerum rubicundum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscapes due to its drought tolerance.
