Microstylis lancifolia

Microstylis lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved microstylis, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in spikes, and it produces tiny, three-angled fruits.

Microstylis lancifolia is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting insect life.
