Mikania cuencana

Mikania cuencana, commonly known as the Andean morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
