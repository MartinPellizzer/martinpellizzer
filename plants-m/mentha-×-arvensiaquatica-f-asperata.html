Mentha × arvensiaquatica f. asperata

Mentha × arvensiaquatica f. asperata, commonly known as rough marsh mint, is a hybrid plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and square stems, typical of many mint species. This plant thrives in moist, wetland environments, often found near streams, ditches, and marshes. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically violet or blue in color, arranged in whorled clusters.

The plant is known for its aromatic leaves, which emit a strong mint scent when crushed.
