Montia minor

Montia minor, commonly known as lesser blueweed, is a small, annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, streambanks, and forest edges. The plant has alternate, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy, and it produces small, blue to purple flowers in clusters. Its flowers are typically less than 1 centimeter in diameter and have five petals.

Montia minor is part of the Montiaceae family and is known for its delicate appearance and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
