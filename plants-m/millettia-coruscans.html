Millettia coruscans

Millettia coruscans, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous leguminous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often trifoliate or pinnate, and its showy, yellow, racemose inflorescences that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and it is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Millettia coruscans is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation, making it useful in agroforestry systems.
