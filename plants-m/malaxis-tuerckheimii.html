Malaxis tuerckheimii

Malaxis tuerckheimii, commonly known as the Tuerckheim's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or rocky slopes. Its leaves are linear and arranged in two rows along the stem, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Malaxis tuerckheimii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
