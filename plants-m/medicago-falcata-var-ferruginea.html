Medicago falcata var. ferruginea

Medicago falcata var. ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty medic, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with a distinctive reddish-brown coloration on the underside, and its small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant produces cylindrical, hairy pods that contain several oval seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
