Marsdenia hirta

Marsdenia hirta, commonly known as the hairy Marsdenia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asclepiadaceae family.

It is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are yellowish-green and clustered in umbels, often adorned with a distinctive calyx.

Marsdenia hirta is often used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
