Miconia conoapiculata

Miconia conoapiculata, commonly known as the red miconia, is a tropical shrub native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a reddish underside. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets make it an invasive species in some regions.

The plant's botanical features include its striking foliage and unique fruit structure, which contribute to its ecological impact.
