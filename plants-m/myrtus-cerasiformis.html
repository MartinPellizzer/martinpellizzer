Myrtus cerasiformis

Myrtus cerasiformis, commonly known as the cherry myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, dark green foliage and produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring. The plant is notable for its ornamental value, with showy, cherry-like red berries that appear in late summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

Myrtus cerasiformis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
