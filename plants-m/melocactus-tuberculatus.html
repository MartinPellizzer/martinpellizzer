Melocactus tuberculatus

Melocactus tuberculatus, commonly known as the spiny pear cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem covered in prominent, spiny tubercles. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers during the rainy season, which are typically short-lived. Its dense spines provide protection from herbivores and help reduce water loss.

Melocactus tuberculatus is well adapted to desert conditions, storing water in its thick stem and thriving in full sun.
