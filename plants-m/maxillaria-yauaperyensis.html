Maxillaria yauaperyensis

Maxillaria yauaperyensis, commonly known as the Yauapé orchid, is a species native to Brazil, specifically the state of Pará.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its intricate and often fragrant floral structures. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are usually small to medium in size, with a distinctive coloration that ranges from white to pale pink, often featuring intricate patterns.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
