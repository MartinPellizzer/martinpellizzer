Myrospermum frutescens var. emarginatum

Myrospermum frutescens var. emarginatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed myrospermum, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is a small shrub characterized by its woody stems and narrow, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
