Miconia neoepiphytica

Miconia neoepiphytica, commonly known as the epiphytic miconia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. This plant is often found growing on trees as an epiphyte, meaning it attaches itself to other plants without deriving nutrients from them. Its inflorescence consists of clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom throughout the wet season.

Miconia neoepiphytica is also known for its rapid growth and potential invasiveness in certain ecosystems.
