Miconia pseudoaplostachya

Miconia pseudoaplostachya, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that are typically followed by bright red, fleshy fruits resembling starfruits. Its rapid growth rate and ability to form dense thickets make it an invasive species in some regions.

The botanical features of Miconia pseudoaplostachya make it both ecologically significant and challenging to manage.
