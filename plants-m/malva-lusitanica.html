Malva lusitanica

Malva lusitanica, commonly known as the Portuguese mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and northern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other plants like hibiscus and cotton.

Malva lusitanica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
