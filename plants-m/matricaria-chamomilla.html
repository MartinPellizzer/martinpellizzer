Matricaria chamomilla

Matricaria chamomilla, commonly known as German chamomile, is an annual herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, which bloom in the summer months. The plant contains essential oils, particularly chamazulene, which contribute to its calming properties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Its botanical name reflects its origin, with "Matricaria" derived from the Latin word for "mother's grass," highlighting its historical use in herbal medicine.
