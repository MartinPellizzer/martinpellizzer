Manihot violacea subsp. violacea

Manihot violacea subsp.

violacea, commonly known as the purple arrowleaf, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically purple or reddish in color, with a striking contrast to its green veins.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant foliage.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
