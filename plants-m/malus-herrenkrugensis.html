Malus herrenkrugensis

Malus herrenkrugensis, commonly known as the Herrenkrug apple, is a rare and ancient species of apple native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is characterized by its small, tart fruits with a distinctive red and yellow coloration. The plant is a deciduous tree with simple, alternate leaves and produces flowers in the spring. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in traditional medicine.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
