Muraltia serpylloides

Muraltia serpylloides, commonly known as the sweet pepper tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. These flowers give way to small, red, fleshy berries that are edible and have a sweet, peppery flavor.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and colorful fruit.
