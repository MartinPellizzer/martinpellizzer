Medinilla subsessilis

Medinilla subsessilis, commonly known as the velvet tangerine.

It is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible and have a tangy flavor. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowers.

Medinilla subsessilis is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
