Marsypianthes tubulosa

Marsypianthes tubulosa, commonly known as the tubular lily, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as rainforests and montane regions. Its leaves are broad and glossy, often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral structure.
