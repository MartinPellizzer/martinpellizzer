Miscanthus matsumurae

Miscanthus matsumurae, commonly known as the Japanese silver grass, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and silky, silver-white plumes that appear in the autumn. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and stream banks. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue during the growing season.

Miscanthus matsumurae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and conservation efforts.
