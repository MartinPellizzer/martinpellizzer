Mnemion segetale

Mnemion segetale, commonly known as the field knapweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and wastelands. It has a taproot system and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Mnemion segetale is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
