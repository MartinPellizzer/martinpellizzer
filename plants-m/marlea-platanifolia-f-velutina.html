Marlea platanifolia f. velutina

Marlea platanifolia f. velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf sandthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its unique foliage and adaptability make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
