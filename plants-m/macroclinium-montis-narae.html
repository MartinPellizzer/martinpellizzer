Macroclinium montis-narae

Macroclinium montis-narae, commonly known as the mountain nara orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments with partial shade, often growing on rocky substrates or in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, which helps to reflect excess light and retain moisture.

Due to habitat destruction and limited distribution, Macroclinium montis-narae is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
