Melothria alba

Melothria alba, commonly known as the white melon or white cucumber, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It produces small, pale green to white fruits that are typically eaten raw or cooked. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces yellow flowers. Its botanical name reflects its pale coloration, and it is often cultivated for its edible, mild-tasting fruit.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is part of the Cucurbitaceae family.
