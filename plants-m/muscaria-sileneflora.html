Muscaria sileneflora

Muscaria sileneflora, commonly known as the silene muscari, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

The plant is often found in meadows and along roadsides, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
