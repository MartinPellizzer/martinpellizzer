Malesherbia linearifolia var. paniculata

Malesherbia linearifolia var. paniculata, commonly known as the paniculate malesherbia, is a striking plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its elongated, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with multiple stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
