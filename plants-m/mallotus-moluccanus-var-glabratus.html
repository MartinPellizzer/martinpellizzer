Mallotus moluccanus var. glabratus

Mallotus moluccanus var. glabratus, commonly known as the smooth mallot, is a tropical shrub native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds.

It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
