Medinilla pulogensis

Medinilla pulogensis, commonly known as the velvet tangerine.

This tropical plant is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its glossy, velvety leaves and fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its unique appearance and pleasant fragrance make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
