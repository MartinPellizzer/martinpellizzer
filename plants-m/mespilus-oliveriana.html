Mespilus oliveriana

Mespilus oliveriana, commonly known as the Oliver's whitebead, is a rare and lesser-known species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white, bead-like fruits that mature in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands and streamside areas. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The flowers are usually pinkish-white and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value in botanical gardens.
