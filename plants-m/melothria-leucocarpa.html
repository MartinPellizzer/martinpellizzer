Melothria leucocarpa

Melothria leucocarpa, commonly known as the white-fruited sourberry, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It produces small, green, fleshy fruits that turn yellow or white when mature, often with a sour or acidic taste. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is part of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers and squash. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires support for its climbing growth habit.
