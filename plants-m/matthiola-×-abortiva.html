Matthiola × abortiva

Matthiola × abortiva, commonly known as the common wallflower, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of fragrant, four-petaled flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically produces small, rounded, green pods that contain several seeds. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
