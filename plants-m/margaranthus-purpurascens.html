Margaranthus purpurascens

Margaranthus purpurascens, commonly known as the purple margarita plant, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, cylindrical flower heads that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Margaranthus purpurascens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
