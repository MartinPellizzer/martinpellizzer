Melhania × benjaminii

Melhania × benjaminii, commonly known as the Benjamin melon, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its vigorous vines and large, lobed leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces yellow flowers that transition into orange to red fruits, which are typically harvested when fully mature. Its fruit is edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, making it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

Melhania × benjaminii thrives in warm climates and requires full sun and well-drained soil for optimal growth.
