Myrcia alternifolia

Myrcia alternifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are red, fleshy berries that contain several seeds.

Myrcia alternifolia is often found in moist, tropical environments and is known for its ornamental value.
