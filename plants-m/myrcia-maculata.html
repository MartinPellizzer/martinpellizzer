Myrcia maculata

Myrcia maculata, commonly known as the spotted myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that often have a reddish or purplish tint. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Myrcia maculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
