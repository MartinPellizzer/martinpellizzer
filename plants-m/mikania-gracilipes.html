Mikania gracilipes

Mikania gracilipes, commonly known as the slender Mikania, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Asteraceae family. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open forests.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it can become a problematic weed in some areas.
