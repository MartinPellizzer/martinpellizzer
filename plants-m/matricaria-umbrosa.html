Matricaria umbrosa

Matricaria umbrosa, commonly known as the shadow fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, daisy-like yellow flowers and hairy, gray-green leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded or semi-shaded environments, such as woodlands and rocky slopes. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and ragweed.

Matricaria umbrosa is often used in herbal medicine for its purported calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
