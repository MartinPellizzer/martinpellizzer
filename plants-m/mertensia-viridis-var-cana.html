Mertensia viridis var. cana

Mertensia viridis var. cana, commonly known as the silverleaf bluebells, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are silver-gray in color due to dense pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, sky-blue flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a valuable addition to woodland gardens.

Its foliage and flowers are both ornamental, contributing to its popularity among gardeners.
