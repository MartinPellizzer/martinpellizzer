Melochia oblonga

Melochia oblonga, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red-rooted morning glory, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to red, typically blooming in the morning. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often turning a deep red when mature.

Melochia oblonga is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
