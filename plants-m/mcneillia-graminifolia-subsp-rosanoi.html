Mcneillia graminifolia subsp. rosanoi

Mcneillia graminifolia subsp.

rosanoi, commonly known as the Rosano's grass, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, grass-like flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments at high altitudes.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of alpine flora.
