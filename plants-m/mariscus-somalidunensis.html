Mariscus somalidunensis

Mariscus somalidunensis, commonly known as the Somali mariscus, is a rare flowering plant native to the arid regions of Somalia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is often found in rocky habitats.
