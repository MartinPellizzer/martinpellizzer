Micrococca oligandra

Micrococca oligandra, commonly known as the red-flowered henna.

It is a shrub native to southern Africa, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, tubular red flowers. The plant produces small, round, red berries that contain seeds used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Micrococca oligandra is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
