Marila cespedesiana

Marila cespedesiana, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces trumpet-shaped, violet to blue flowers that bloom in the morning. It has fibrous roots and is adapted to withstand salt spray and sandy soils.

Marila cespedesiana is often found in disturbed coastal habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
