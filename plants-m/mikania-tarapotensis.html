Mikania tarapotensis

Mikania tarapotensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian devil's ivy, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Ecuador.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. Mikania tarapotensis is often found in humid, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a valuable ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in certain regions.
