Medinilla latericia

Medinilla latericia, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or velvet leaf, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Medinilla latericia is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
