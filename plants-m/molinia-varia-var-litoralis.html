Molinia varia var. litoralis

Molinia varia var. litoralis, commonly known as the coastal moorgrass, is a variant of the moorgrass species native to coastal regions.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime environments. This plant is adapted to salt spray and wind exposure, making it a hardy species. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a tufted growth habit.

It plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and preventing erosion in coastal ecosystems.
