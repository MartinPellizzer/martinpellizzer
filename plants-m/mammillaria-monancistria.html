Mammillaria monancistria

Mammillaria monancistria, commonly known as the Mexican night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and flowering habits, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
