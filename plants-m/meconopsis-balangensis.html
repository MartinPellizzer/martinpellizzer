Meconopsis balangensis

Meconopsis balangensis, commonly known as the Himalayan gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments and thrives in cool, moist conditions. Meconopsis balangensis has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
