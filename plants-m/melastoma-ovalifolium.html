Melastoma ovalifolium

Melastoma ovalifolium, commonly known as the oval-leaved melastoma, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a capsule that releases small, dry seeds when mature.

Melastoma ovalifolium is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
