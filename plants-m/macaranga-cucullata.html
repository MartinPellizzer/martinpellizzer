Macaranga cucullata

Macaranga cucullata, commonly known as the broadleaf paper mulberry, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by orange to red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its rapid growth and fibrous bark make it valuable for both ecological and industrial purposes.
