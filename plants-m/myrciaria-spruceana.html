Myrciaria spruceana

Myrciaria spruceana, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are small and white, and it produces fruit from late summer to early winter.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in herbal medicine.
