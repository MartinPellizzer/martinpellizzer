Malus prunifolia f. dulcis

Malus prunifolia f. dulcis, commonly known as the sweetleaf apple, is a botanical variant of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its small, edible fruits that have a sweet, subacid flavor. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a serrated margin, and it produces white or pale pink flowers in spring.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary potential.
