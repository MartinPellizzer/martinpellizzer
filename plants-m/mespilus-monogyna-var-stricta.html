Mespilus monogyna var. stricta

Mespilus monogyna var. stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, which are typically narrow and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by distinctive, fleshy fruits that ripen in autumn. The fruit has a unique, aromatic flavor and is often used in culinary applications.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable addition to gardens and landscapes.
