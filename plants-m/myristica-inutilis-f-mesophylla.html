Myristica inutilis f. mesophylla

Myristica inutilis f. mesophylla, commonly known as the mesophyll form of the false nutmeg, is a variant of the genus Myristica, characterized by its unique leaf morphology and growth habit.

This plant is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and exhibits a distinct mesophyll structure in its leaves, which contributes to its photosynthetic efficiency. It typically grows as an understory plant in humid, shaded environments, adapting to low-light conditions with its broad, leathery foliage. The plant's botanical significance lies in its morphological variation and ecological role within its native habitat.

Its study provides insights into the adaptive strategies of plants in tropical ecosystems.
