Margaranthus lemmonii

Margaranthus lemmonii, commonly known as the Lemmon's margarita, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces showy, white to pale yellow flowers with prominent central disks, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Margaranthus lemmonii is typically found in rocky soils and can thrive in arid environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
