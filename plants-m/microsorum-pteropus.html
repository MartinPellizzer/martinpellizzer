Microsorum pteropus

Microsorum pteropus, commonly known as the Japanese water fern, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like leaves that give it a delicate, lily-like appearance. This plant thrives in slow-moving or still freshwater environments, often found in shaded, humid conditions. Its rhizomes spread along the substrate, anchoring the plant and allowing it to colonize new areas.

Microsorum pteropus is popular in aquariums due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide shelter for aquatic life.
