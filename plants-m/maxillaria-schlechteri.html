Maxillaria schlechteri

Maxillaria schlechteri, commonly known as the Schlechter's maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green. The plant typically blooms in the summer, with flowers that have a distinctive, spurred lip. Its leaves are ovate and arranged in two rows along the stem, and it prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

Maxillaria schlechteri is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
