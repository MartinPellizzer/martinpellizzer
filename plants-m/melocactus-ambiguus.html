Melocactus ambiguus

Melocactus ambiguus, commonly known as the ambiguous melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem covered in dense, white spines that provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically short-lived but attract various pollinators. Its root system is shallow and adapted to absorb moisture from infrequent rains.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in arid climates.
