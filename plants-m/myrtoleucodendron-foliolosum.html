Myrtoleucodendron foliolosum

Myrtoleucodendron foliolosum, commonly known as the leafy myrtle-leaved tree, is a species native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers that are highly fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its bark is smooth and dark brown, with a distinctive, aromatic scent when cut.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
