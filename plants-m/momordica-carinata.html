Momordica carinata

Momordica carinata, commonly known as the horned melon, is a tropical vine native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiky, green, warty fruit that resembles a horned helmet. The plant has palmate leaves and produces yellow flowers that develop into the unique, edible fruit. The fruit's interior contains a sweet, juicy pulp with black seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary use in various cuisines.
