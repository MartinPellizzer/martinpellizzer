Melanospermum rupestre

Melanospermum rupestre, commonly known as the rock melon or desert melon, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a sweet, melon-like flavor. It thrives in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Melanospermum rupestre plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
