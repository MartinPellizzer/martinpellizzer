Myrcia neotovarensis

Myrcia neotovarensis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Myrcia neotovarensis is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
