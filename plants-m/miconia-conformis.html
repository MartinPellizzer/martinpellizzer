Miconia conformis

Miconia conformis, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically velvety in texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Miconia conformis is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its invasive potential, it is often considered a problematic species in certain regions.
