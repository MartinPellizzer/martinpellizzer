Myrtus pulchella

Myrtus pulchella, commonly known as the sweet myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, dark green leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its fragrant foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and hedging.

The berries it produces are typically small and dark, often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
