Miconia fasciculata

Miconia fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that are often arranged in whorls. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers with five petals, which are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Miconia fasciculata is also notable for its potential use in traditional medicine.
