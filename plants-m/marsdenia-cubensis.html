Marsdenia cubensis

Marsdenia cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban Marsdenia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

It is native to Cuba and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with five petals. Its fruits are follicles that contain seeds with tufts of hair, aiding in wind dispersal.

Marsdenia cubensis is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
