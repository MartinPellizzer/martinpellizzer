Melaleuca huegelii subsp. pristicensis

Melaleuca huegelii subsp.

pristicensis, commonly known as the Pristicent Melaleuca, is a shrub or small tree native to northern Australia. It is characterized by its aromatic, often glandular leaves and shows a preference for well-drained soils in coastal or sandy environments.

The plant produces small, bottle-shaped flower clusters that are typically white or pale pink, and it is often found in coastal dunes or heathlands. It is a hardy species that can tolerate salt spray and is adapted to semi-arid conditions.

The subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
