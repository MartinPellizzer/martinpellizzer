Melastoma fothergilla

Melastoma fothergilla, commonly known as the flame azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a yellowish or greenish tint. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a glossy surface. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds.
