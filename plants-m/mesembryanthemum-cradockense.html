Mesembryanthemum cradockense

Mesembryanthemum cradockense, commonly known as the Cradock's ice plant, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, ribbed stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in spring. It is adapted to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular ornamental plant in dry climates.
