Memora diffusa

Memora diffusa, commonly known as the spreading memora, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive if not controlled.
