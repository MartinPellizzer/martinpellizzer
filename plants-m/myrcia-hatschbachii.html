Myrcia hatschbachii

Myrcia hatschbachii, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. Its fruits are small, woody drupes that contain a single seed.

Myrcia hatschbachii is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
