Meconopsis speciosa

Meconopsis speciosa, commonly known as the Himalayan blue poppy, is a striking flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue or violet petals, which are often speckled with contrasting colors. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its flowers bloom in early summer, adding a dramatic visual impact to alpine landscapes.

Meconopsis speciosa is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
