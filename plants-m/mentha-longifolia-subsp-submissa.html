Mentha longifolia subsp. submissa

Mentha longifolia subsp.

submissa, commonly known as narrow-leaf mint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated margin.

The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in whorled clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its use in herbal teas and as a source of essential oils.
