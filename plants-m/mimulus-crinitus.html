Mimulus crinitus

Mimulus crinitus, commonly known as the curly mallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, salmon-pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in moist, rocky habitats. It is a perennial herb that grows up to 3 feet tall, with a branching structure that supports its vibrant blooms.

Mimulus crinitus is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
