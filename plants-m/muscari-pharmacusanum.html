Muscari pharmacusanum

Muscari pharmacusanum, commonly known as the Persian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers in early spring, which are arranged in a loose raceme. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically evergreen throughout the year. Its bulbs are underground storage organs that allow it to survive harsh winter conditions.

Muscari pharmacusanum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
