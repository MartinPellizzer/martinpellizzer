Macrochaetium neesii

Macrochaetium neesii, commonly known as the African grass or tall blady grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle with small, spikelet-like flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Macrochaetium neesii is often used in ornamental gardening due to its upright growth habit and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
