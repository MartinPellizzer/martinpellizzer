Marah horrida

Marah horrida, commonly known as the horrid mallow, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pale yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Marah horrida is known for its hardiness and ability to withstand drought conditions.
