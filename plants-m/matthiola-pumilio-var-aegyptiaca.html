Matthiola pumilio var. aegyptiaca

Matthiola pumilio var. aegyptiaca, commonly known as the Egyptian stock, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbages and mustards. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring and early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy as well.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual beauty and pleasant scent.
