Marlierea verticillaris

Marlierea verticillaris, commonly known as the clustered marlierea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in moist, shaded environments.

Marlierea verticillaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
