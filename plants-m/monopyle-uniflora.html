Monopyle uniflora

Monopyle uniflora, commonly known as the single-flowered monopyle, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and solitary, showy flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
