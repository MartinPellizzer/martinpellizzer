Myrcia bicolor

Myrcia bicolor, commonly known as the pink myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and emit a strong, pleasant fragrance when crushed.

Myrcia bicolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
