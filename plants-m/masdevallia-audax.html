Masdevallia audax

Masdevallia audax, commonly known as the Audax orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in cool, humid environments at high altitudes. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually small, with intricate patterns and a wide range of colors, including red, orange, and yellow.

Masdevallia audax is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique beauty.
