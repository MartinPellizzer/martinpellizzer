Malpighia epedunculata subsp. epedunculata

Malpighia epedunculata subsp.

epedunculata, commonly known as the Brazilian wolfberry, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil. It is characterized by its small, bright red berries that are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves and produces flowers that are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits, which are rich in antioxidants.
