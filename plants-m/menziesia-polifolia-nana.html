Menziesia polifolia nana

Menziesia polifolia nana, commonly known as dwarf fairybells, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, elliptical leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

Menziesia polifolia nana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
