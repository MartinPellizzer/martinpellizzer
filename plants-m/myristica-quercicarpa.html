Myristica quercicarpa

Myristica quercicarpa, commonly known as the red sandalwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myristicaceae and is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and fragrant, reddish-brown heartwood. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red drupes containing a single seed. Its wood is highly valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

The plant also plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
