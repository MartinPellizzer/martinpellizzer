Mentha × bulgarica

Mentha × bulgarica, commonly known as Bulgarian mint, is a hybrid species within the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. This plant is native to the Balkan region and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and calming properties.

Mentha × bulgarica is also cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and fragrance.
