Muscari kerneri

Muscari kerneri, commonly known as the Persian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces dense clusters of small, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that are often evergreen in mild climates. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Muscari kerneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in temperate gardens.
