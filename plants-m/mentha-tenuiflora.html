Mentha tenuiflora

Mentha tenuiflora, commonly known as creeping mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit and aromatic, slightly serrated leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Mentha tenuiflora is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its refreshing scent and mild menthol-like properties.
