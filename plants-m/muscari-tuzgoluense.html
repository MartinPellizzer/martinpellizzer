Muscari tuzgoluense

Muscari tuzgoluense, commonly known as the Tuzgolu musk mallow, is a bulbous perennial plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, spherical clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces dense, blue to violet-colored blooms that appear in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
