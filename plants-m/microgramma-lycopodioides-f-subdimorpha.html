Microgramma lycopodioides f. subdimorpha

Microgramma lycopodioides f. subdimorpha, commonly known as the subdimorphic microgramma, is a fern-like plant belonging to the family Lycopodiales.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant exhibits a dimorphic structure, with distinct differences between the sporophytic and gametophytic phases. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique morphology and reproductive features make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
