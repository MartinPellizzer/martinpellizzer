Mespilus monogyna proles typica

Mespilus monogyna proles typica, commonly known as the common whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white to yellowish flowers that appear in clusters, followed by edible, round berries. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, turning yellow in autumn. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in traditional landscapes.
