Mokof andina

Mokof andina, commonly known as the Andean orchid, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often colorful blooms. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous terrain, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in its arid environment.

Mokof andina is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural significance in Andean communities.
