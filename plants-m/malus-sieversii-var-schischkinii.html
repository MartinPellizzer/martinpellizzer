Malus sieversii var. schischkinii

Malus sieversii var. schischkinii, commonly known as the Schischkin apple, is a wild apple species native to Central Asia.

It is a small tree with distinctive red-brown bark and oval, dark green leaves. The flowers are white and borne in clusters, followed by small, yellowish-green fruits. This variety is valued for its genetic diversity and is often used in apple breeding programs.

Its ornamental qualities make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
