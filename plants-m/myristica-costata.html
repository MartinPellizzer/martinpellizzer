Myristica costata

Myristica costata, commonly known as the Java nut or Costata nut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed. The seeds are encased in a hard, aromatic aril, which is valued for its nutty flavor and used in culinary applications.

Myristica costata is also known for its potential medicinal properties and is cultivated for its unique botanical and economic significance.
