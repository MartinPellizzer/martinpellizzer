Mikania leiolaena

Mikania leiolaena, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail along the ground. Its fibrous roots and stems make it adaptable to various soil types and environments.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
