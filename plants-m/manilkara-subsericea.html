Manilkara subsericea

Manilkara subsericea, commonly known as the red sapote, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and large, glossy, ovate leaves. The tree produces yellowish-green flowers that are followed by round, orange-red fruits with a sweet, creamy pulp. Its latex, known as chicle, has been historically used in the production of chewing gum.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and economic importance in local markets.
