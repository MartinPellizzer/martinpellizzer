Medicago papillosa var. citrina

Medicago papillosa var. citrina, commonly known as yellow goat's rue, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features delicate, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
