Maxillaria cepula

Maxillaria cepula, commonly known as the purple-tinged orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by a short rhizome and a cluster of pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a yellow lip. Its leaves are elliptical and arranged in two rows along the stem.

Maxillaria cepula thrives in humid, well-drained environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.
