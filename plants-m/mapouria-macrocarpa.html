Mapouria macrocarpa

Mapouria macrocarpa, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a large evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, oblong fruits containing several seeds. Its bark is thick and deeply fissured, and the wood is highly valued for its durability and fine grain.

The plant has significant ecological and economic importance in its native range.
