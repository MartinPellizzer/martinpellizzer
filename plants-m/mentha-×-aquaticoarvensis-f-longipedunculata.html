Mentha × aquaticoarvensis f. longipedunculata

Mentha × aquaticoarvensis f. longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-pedunculate water mint, is a hybrid aquatic plant belonging to the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its elongated inflorescences and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating. The plant typically grows in freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a mild minty scent.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
