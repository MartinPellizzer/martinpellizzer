Miliusa montana var. major

Miliusa montana var. major, commonly known as the mountain miliusa, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes plants like the cashew and poison ivy. The plant is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. It produces edible, red, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine.
