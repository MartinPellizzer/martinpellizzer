Munnozia

Munnozia, commonly known as the desert lily, is a genus of flowering plants native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

These plants are characterized by their succulent, often hairy leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. Munnozia species typically thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as deep root systems and water-storing tissues. They are often found in rocky soils and can withstand periods of drought.

The genus includes several species, each with unique floral structures and growth habits.
