Macleania crenulata

Macleania crenulata, commonly known as the silverleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks and in swamps. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a distinctive waxy, silver underside. Its showy, pink to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Macleania crenulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
