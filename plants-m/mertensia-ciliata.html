Mertensia ciliata

Mertensia ciliata, commonly known as the Carolina bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a soft, velvety texture.

Mertensia ciliata is a herbaceous perennial that is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
