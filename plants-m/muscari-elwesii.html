Muscari elwesii

Muscari elwesii, commonly known as the Persian bluebell, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

Muscari elwesii is also valued for its ability to naturalize and spread readily in suitable conditions.
