Mikania haenkeana

Mikania haenkeana, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and trailing habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the early morning. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and can root at the nodes.

Mikania haenkeana is often found in disturbed areas, such as along roadsides and in open fields.
