Melandrium napuligerum

Melandrium napuligerum, commonly known as the Naples catchfly, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its succulent stems and leaves. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
