Maxillaria loefgrenii

Maxillaria loefgrenii, commonly known as the Loefgren's orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant produces pseudobulbs that support its evergreen leaves, which are typically elliptical and leathery. Its flowers are usually fragrant and come in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a distinctive lip.

Maxillaria loefgrenii is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
