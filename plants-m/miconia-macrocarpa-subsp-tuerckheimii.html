Miconia macrocarpa subsp. tuerckheimii

Miconia macrocarpa subsp.

tuerckheimii, commonly known as the Tuerckheim's miconia, is a member of the Melastomataceae family. It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
