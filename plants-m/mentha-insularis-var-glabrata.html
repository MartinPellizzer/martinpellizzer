Mentha insularis var. glabrata

Mentha insularis var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth island mint, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically hairless or very sparsely hairy. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue to purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Mentha insularis var. glabrata is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in herbal teas or as a culinary herb.
