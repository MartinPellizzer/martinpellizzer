Mahernia exappendiculata var. tomentosa

Mahernia exappendiculata var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly mahernia, is a rare and unusual plant native to specific regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, almost fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.
