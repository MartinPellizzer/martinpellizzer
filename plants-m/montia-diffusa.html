Montia diffusa

Montia diffusa, commonly known as the common bladderwort, is a small flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, a feature adapted to its moist, often boggy habitat. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers arranged in clusters at the tips of its stems. It is a member of the Montiaceae family and is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and marshy areas.

Montia diffusa is also known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils, making it a hardy and resilient species.
