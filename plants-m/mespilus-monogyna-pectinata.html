Mespilus monogyna pectinata

Mespilus monogyna pectinata, commonly known as the European whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and small, white flowers that appear in early spring. The tree produces edible berries that are typically harvested in late summer, though they are not as commonly consumed as other fruits. It is often found in woodland areas and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
