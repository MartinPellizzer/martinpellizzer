Manulea alternifolia

Manulea alternifolia, commonly known as the alternate-leaf manulea, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its alternate arrangement of leaves, which gives it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in shrubland or woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in inflorescences that resemble a panicle.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
