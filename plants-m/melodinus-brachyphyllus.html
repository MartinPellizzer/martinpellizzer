Melodinus brachyphyllus

Melodinus brachyphyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved melodine, is a tropical plant belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant is native to regions of Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological role.

The fruit is a capsule that releases small, hairy seeds when mature.
