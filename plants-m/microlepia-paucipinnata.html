Microlepia paucipinnata

Microlepia paucipinnata, commonly known as the few-pinnate water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. The plant has delicate, floating fronds that are divided into fewer pinnae compared to other fern species. Its rhizomes are submerged and help anchor the plant while absorbing nutrients from the water.

Microlepia paucipinnata plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
