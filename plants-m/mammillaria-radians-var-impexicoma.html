Mammillaria radians var. impexicoma

Mammillaria radians var. impexicoma, commonly known as the Mexican star cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical or slightly elongated stem, covered in white to pale yellow tubercles. The plant produces small, vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
