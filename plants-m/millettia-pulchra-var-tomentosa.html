Millettia pulchra var. tomentosa

Millettia pulchra var. tomentosa, commonly known as the silky millettia, is a strikingly ornamental climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lush, dark green, velvety leaves that are covered in a dense, soft pubescence, giving it a tactile and visually appealing texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by distinctive, winged seed pods. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a houseplant.

Due to its rapid growth and climbing habit, it requires support structures and regular pruning to maintain its form.
