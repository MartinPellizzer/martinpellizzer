Mertensia papillosa var. fusiformis

Mertensia papillosa var. fusiformis, commonly known as the broadleaf bluebell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue flowers during the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and valuable component of woodland ecosystems.
