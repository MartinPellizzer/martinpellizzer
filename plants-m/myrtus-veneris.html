Myrtus veneris

Myrtus veneris, commonly known as the sacred myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, resinous leaves and is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and rocky gardens.

Myrtus veneris has a long history of cultural and religious significance, particularly in ancient Greek and Roman traditions.
