Masdevallia paquishae

Masdevallia paquishae, commonly known as the Andean ghost orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the high-altitude cloud forests of Peru.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its intricate, often translucent petals and sepals. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic growth habit. Its flowers are usually small, with a unique coloration that ranges from white to pale pink, and it blooms during the cooler months.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
