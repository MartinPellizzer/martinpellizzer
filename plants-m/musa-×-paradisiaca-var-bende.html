Musa × paradisiaca var. bende

Musa × paradisiaca var. bende, commonly known as the banana plant, is a tropical herbaceous plant belonging to the Musaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length, and its clustered inflorescence that produces both male and female flowers. The plant is cultivated primarily for its edible fruit, which is rich in potassium and fiber. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil and requires regular watering.

The variety 'bende' is particularly noted for its high yield and disease resistance, making it a popular choice for commercial banana farming.
