Monvillea smithiana

Monvillea smithiana, commonly known as the Smith's monvillea, is a rare and striking orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions with well-drained soil. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a dense rosette structure that helps conserve moisture.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable and somewhat elusive species in the orchid world.
