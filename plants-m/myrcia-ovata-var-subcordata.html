Myrcia ovata var. subcordata

Myrcia ovata var. subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf myrcia, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its elliptical to ovate leaves that are often slightly narrowed at the base. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink to red in color, which bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and it is often found in moist, shaded habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses.
