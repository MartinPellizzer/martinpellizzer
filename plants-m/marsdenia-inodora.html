Marsdenia inodora

Marsdenia inodora, commonly known as the sweet-scented Marsdenia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asclepiadaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are often fragrant. It is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India and Indonesia.

Marsdenia inodora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique aromatic properties.
