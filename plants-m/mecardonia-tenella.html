Mecardonia tenella

Mecardonia tenella, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mecardonia, is a small, annual herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters and features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface.

Mecardonia tenella is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a characteristic species of certain dry, rocky habitats.
