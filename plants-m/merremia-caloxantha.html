Merremia caloxantha

Merremia caloxantha, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along forest edges.

Due to its rapid growth and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
