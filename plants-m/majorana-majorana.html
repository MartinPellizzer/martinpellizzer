Majorana majorana

Majorana majorana, commonly known as sweet wormwood, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers have been used in traditional medicine and as a seasoning in various cuisines.
