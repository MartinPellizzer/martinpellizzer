Macrolobium froesii

Macrolobium froesii, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in the humid, tropical environment.

The botanical features of Macrolobium froesii make it a popular subject for both horticultural enthusiasts and botanical research.
