Menodora scabra var. ramosissima

Menodora scabra var. ramosissima, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and alternate leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in umbels, and it produces berries that range from yellow to red as they mature.

This species is often found in moist, disturbed soils in woodland edges and open fields.
