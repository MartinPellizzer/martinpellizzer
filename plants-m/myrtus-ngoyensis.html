Myrtus ngoyensis

Myrtus ngoyensis, commonly known as Ngoy's myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known species like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and leathery, evergreen leaves. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in moist forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
