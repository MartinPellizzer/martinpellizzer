Mimulopsis diffusa

Mimulopsis diffusa, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with a short, fibrous root system adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, with five petals and a prominent yellow throat. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, helping to reduce water loss in harsh environments.

Mimulopsis diffusa is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
