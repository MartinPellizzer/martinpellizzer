Mimulus erosus

Mimulus erosus, commonly known as the yellow monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, spurred flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats, including streambanks and meadows. It has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, oval fruits containing several seeds.

Mimulus erosus is a perennial species that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in regions with well-drained soils.
