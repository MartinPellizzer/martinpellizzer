Macropelma angustifolium

Macropelma angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf macropelma, is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive botanical features.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features a thick, rhizomatous stem with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe, which is a common trait in the Araceae family.

The plant's leaves are usually narrow and elongated, contributing to its common name.
