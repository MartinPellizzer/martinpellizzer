Micambe violacea

Micambe violacea, commonly known as the violet bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue petals that resemble small bells, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cooler temperatures. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Micambe violacea is often cultivated for its beauty and is a popular choice in rock gardens and alpine settings.
