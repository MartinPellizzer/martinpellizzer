Maxillaria insolita

Maxillaria insolita, commonly known as the solitary maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically blooms during the summer months, and its flowers have a unique, elongated shape with intricate patterns on the petals. Maxillaria insolita thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

It is appreciated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
