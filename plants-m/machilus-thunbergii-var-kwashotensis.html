Machilus thunbergii var. kwashotensis

Machilus thunbergii var. kwashotensis, commonly known as the Korean machilus, is a variant of the evergreen tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by berries that are used in traditional medicine.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in forest ecosystems.
