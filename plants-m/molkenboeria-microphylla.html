Molkenboeria microphylla

Molkenboeria microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf milkweed, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its delicate appearance and ecological importance, it is a valued plant in native gardens and conservation efforts.
