Myrtus randiana

Myrtus randiana, commonly known as the Cape myrtle, is a flowering shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and have a distinct aromatic scent when crushed.

Myrtus randiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to coastal and sandy soils.
