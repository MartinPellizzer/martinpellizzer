Masdevallia × rushtonii

Masdevallia × rushtonii, commonly known as the Rushton's masdevallia, is a hybrid orchid species native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, often colorful floral structure. The plant typically produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom during the cooler months. Its foliage consists of narrow, strap-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

This orchid thrives in high humidity and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
