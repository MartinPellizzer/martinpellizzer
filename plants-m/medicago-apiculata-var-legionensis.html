Medicago apiculata var. legionensis

Medicago apiculata var. legionensis, commonly known as the Armenian clover, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Anatolia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid soils and is adapted to temperate climates. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
