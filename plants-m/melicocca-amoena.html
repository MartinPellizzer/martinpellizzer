Melicocca amoena

Melicocca amoena, commonly known as the beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright purple berries that appear in the fall, providing a striking visual contrast against its dark green foliage. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are typically oval in shape and have a slightly hairy texture. It produces fragrant, white flowers in the spring, which are clustered in loose inflorescences.

Melicocca amoena is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to support local wildlife.
