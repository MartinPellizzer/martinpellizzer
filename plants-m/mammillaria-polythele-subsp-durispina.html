Mammillaria polythele subsp. durispina

Mammillaria polythele subsp.

durispina, commonly known as the spiny mammillaria, is a small cactus native to Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, radial spines and prominent areoles.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a striking contrast to its spiny structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
