Mentha capitata

Mentha capitata, commonly known as wild mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in terminal clusters, which give it its scientific name. Mentha capitata is known for its strong, aromatic mint scent and is often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
