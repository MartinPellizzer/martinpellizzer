Masdevallia patchicutzae

Masdevallia patchicutzae, commonly known as the Ecuadorian masdevallia, is a rare and delicate orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration. The plant typically grows as a miniature epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapted to the high humidity and cool temperatures of its environment.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
