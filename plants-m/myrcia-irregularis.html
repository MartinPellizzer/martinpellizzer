Myrcia irregularis

Myrcia irregularis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a distinctive myrtle-like scent when crushed.

The fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that matures in late summer.
