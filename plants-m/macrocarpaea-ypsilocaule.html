Macrocarpaea ypsilocaule

Macrocarpaea ypsilocaule, commonly known as the slender-stemmed macrocarpaea, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically clustered in whorls, producing showy, tubular blooms in shades of red and purple.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
