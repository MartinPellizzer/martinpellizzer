Melica micrantha

Melica micrantha, commonly known as slender woodoat, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside.

Melica micrantha plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments and is often found in association with other wetland plant species.
