Mentzelia parvifolia

Mentzelia parvifolia, commonly known as the smallflower bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats. The plant features slender stems that are often branched and covered in fine, hair-like structures. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pale blue or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Mentzelia parvifolia is known for its ability to thrive in harsh conditions, making it an important component of its native ecosystems.
