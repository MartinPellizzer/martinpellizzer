Malus × gloriosa

Malus × gloriosa, commonly known as the glorious crabapple, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its ornamental flowers, which range in color from white to deep red, and its showy, edible fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 meters, with a rounded, spreading canopy. Its leaves are ovate and have a glossy green surface, turning yellow in the autumn.

The tree is valued for its spring blooms and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
