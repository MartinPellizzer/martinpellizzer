Medusantha plumosa

Medusantha plumosa, commonly known as the feathered mist flower, is a rare and striking plant characterized by its ethereal, feathery inflorescences.

Native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, it thrives in cool, moist environments with partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine, silvery down that reflects light. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that bloom in late spring, adding a soft, cloud-like appearance to its foliage.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, Medusantha plumosa is considered a botanical curiosity and a subject of scientific interest.
