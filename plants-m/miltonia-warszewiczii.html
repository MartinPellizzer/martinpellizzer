Miltonia warszewiczii

Miltonia warszewiczii, commonly known as the Warszewicz's orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in the canopy of tropical forests. Its flowers are borne on terminal inflorescences, often appearing in clusters.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its beauty in horticultural settings.
