Moltkia ciliata

Moltkia ciliata, commonly known as the silky mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its scientific name reflects its silky texture, with "ciliata" referring to the fringed edges of its leaves.

Moltkia ciliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
