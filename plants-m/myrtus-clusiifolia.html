Myrtus clusiifolia

Myrtus clusiifolia, commonly known as the common myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East.

It features narrow, dark green leaves that are often aromatic and have a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, which are followed by dark blue, waxy berries. Myrtus clusiifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has a long history of use in traditional medicine and as a culinary spice.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
