Myrrhina anthemoides

Myrrhina anthemoides, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It is a perennial species that is drought-tolerant and prefers full sun.

Myrrhina anthemoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
