Mesembryanthemum persistens

Mesembryanthemum persistens, commonly known as the persistent ice plant, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it an attractive addition to rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
