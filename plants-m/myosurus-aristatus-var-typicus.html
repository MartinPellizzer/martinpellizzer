Myosurus aristatus var. typicus

Myosurus aristatus var. typicus, commonly known as the common water milfoil, is a perennial aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has finely divided, feather-like leaves that are often submerged, with some leaves emerging above the water surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months.

Myosurus aristatus var. typicus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
