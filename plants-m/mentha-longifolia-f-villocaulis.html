Mentha longifolia f. villocaulis

Mentha longifolia f. villocaulis, commonly known as woolly mint, is a horticultural form of the spearmint species.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive texture and appearance. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with square stems and opposite leaves. It produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorls along the stem.

This variety is valued in gardens for its ornamental qualities and aromatic properties.
