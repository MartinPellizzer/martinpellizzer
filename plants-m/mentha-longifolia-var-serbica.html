Mentha longifolia var. serbica

Mentha longifolia var. serbica, commonly known as Serbian mint, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly minty scent and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Serbian mint is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies.
