Malleastrum gracile

Malleastrum gracile, commonly known as the slender malleastrum, is a small shrub native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant features small, narrow leaves and produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers. It is often found in scrublands and along dry streambeds.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for xerophytic conditions and minimal water requirements.
