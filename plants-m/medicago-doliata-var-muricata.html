Medicago doliata var. muricata

Medicago doliata var. muricata, commonly known as the rough medick, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides.

Medicago doliata var. muricata is also valued for its role in nitrogen fixation and as a forage plant for livestock.
