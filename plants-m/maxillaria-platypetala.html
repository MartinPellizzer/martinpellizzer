Maxillaria platypetala

Maxillaria platypetala, commonly known as the flat-petaled orchid, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its sympodial growth habit and pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a distinctive flat petal. Its leaves are typically elliptical and arranged in two rows along the stem.

Maxillaria platypetala thrives in humid, shaded environments and is appreciated for its ornamental value in horticulture.
