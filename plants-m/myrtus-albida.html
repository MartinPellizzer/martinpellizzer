Myrtus albida

Myrtus albida, commonly known as white myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark blue berries. The plant has aromatic, narrow leaves that are often used in perfumery and traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Myrtus albida is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
