Masoala

Masoala, commonly known as the sacred tree of the Betsileo people in Madagascar, is a unique plant species native to the island's eastern rainforests.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and striking red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in the humid, nutrient-rich soils of the Masoala National Park, where it plays a vital role in the local ecosystem. Its seeds are dispersed by birds, contributing to the biodiversity of the region.

Due to its cultural and ecological significance, conservation efforts are essential to protect this rare and valuable species.
