Mespilus monogyna proles kyrtostyla

Mespilus monogyna proles kyrtostyla, commonly known as the European whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces small, edible berries that ripen in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin.

The bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age, and the tree is often found in woodland habitats.
