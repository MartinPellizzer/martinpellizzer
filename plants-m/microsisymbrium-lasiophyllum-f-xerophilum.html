Microsisymbrium lasiophyllum f. xerophilum

Microsisymbrium lasiophyllum f. xerophilum, commonly known as the desert lichen, is a specialized symbiotic organism composed of a fungal partner and a cyanobacterial component.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, where it plays a crucial role in soil stabilization and nutrient cycling. The plant exhibits a unique structure, forming a crust-like growth that adheres tightly to rocky surfaces. Its ability to survive extreme desiccation is due to the protective properties of its fungal sheath.

This species is an important indicator of ecological conditions in dryland ecosystems.
