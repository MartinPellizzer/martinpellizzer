Moquilea paraensis

Moquilea paraensis, commonly known as the Peruvian quinoa, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces small, round seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients, making it a valuable crop. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soils.

Due to its nutritional value, Moquilea paraensis has gained attention as a potential alternative to traditional grains.
