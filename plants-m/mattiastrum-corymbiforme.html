Mattiastrum corymbiforme

Mattiastrum corymbiforme, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, coral-like clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a woody stem and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Mattiastrum corymbiforme is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
