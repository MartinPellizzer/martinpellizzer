Myrica esculenta var. auriculata

Myrica esculenta var. auriculata, commonly known as the golden wax myrtle, is a variant of the wax myrtle species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its aromatic foliage and adaptability to various soil types make it a popular ornamental plant.

The varietal name 'auriculata' refers to the ear-shaped leaves that distinguish it from other Myrica species.
