Myrceugenia pallida

Myrceugenia pallida, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and oval, leathery leaves. It produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature.

Myrceugenia pallida is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
