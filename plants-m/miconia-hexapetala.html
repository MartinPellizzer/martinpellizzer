Miconia hexapetala

Miconia hexapetala, commonly known as the six-petaled miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pink flowers that typically have six petals, giving it its common name. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its growth habit is often bushy or shrub-like. It is part of the Melastomataceae family, known for its ornamental value and ecological significance in tropical ecosystems.

Miconia hexapetala is often found in moist, shaded areas within rainforests.
