Melaleuca spectabilis

Melaleuca spectabilis, commonly known as the silky tea tree, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant produces showy, pink or white flowers in clusters, which bloom from late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in Indigenous Australian medicine.
