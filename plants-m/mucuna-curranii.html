Mucuna curranii

Mucuna curranii, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein. Mucuna curranii is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a useful and sometimes invasive species in various ecosystems.
