Millettia bequaertii

Millettia bequaertii, commonly known as the Bequaert's Millettia, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its twining stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

Millettia bequaertii is often found in secondary forests and disturbed habitats, where it plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
