Masdevallia ionocharis var. approviata

Masdevallia ionocharis var. approviata, commonly known as the Sapphire Orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the high-altitude cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue-violet petals and a unique, elongated lip that distinguishes it from other Masdevallia species. The plant typically grows as a miniature epiphyte, clinging to tree trunks in its natural habitat. It requires cool, humid conditions and bright, indirect light to thrive.

Due to its rarity and beauty, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
