Malvastrum hillii

Malvastrum hillii, commonly known as the hillii mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Malvastrum hillii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
