Mascagnia conformis

Mascagnia conformis, commonly known as the desert nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing shrub with thick, fleshy stems and leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, which open at night. Its fruit is a fleshy, yellow berry that contains several seeds.

Mascagnia conformis is well-suited to hot, dry climates and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
