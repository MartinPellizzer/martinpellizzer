Myrica burmanni

Myrica burmanni, commonly known as the Burmann's wax myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its often humid and tropical habitat. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in catkins, followed by dark, waxy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. Myrica burmanni thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves and unique appearance make it a notable species in botanical studies and local ecosystems.
