Myrtus mirifolia

Myrtus mirifolia, commonly known as the sacred myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic evergreen leaves and small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are thick and glossy, often used in traditional herbal remedies.

The fruit, a small berry, is edible and contains a single seed.
