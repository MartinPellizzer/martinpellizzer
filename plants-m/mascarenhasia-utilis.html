Mascarenhasia utilis

Mascarenhasia utilis, commonly known as the Mascarene daisy, is a flowering plant native to the Mascarene Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and well-drained soils. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.
