Maxillaria ringens

Maxillaria ringens, commonly known as the showy maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces large, fragrant flowers with a distinctive pinkish-purple color. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern around the stem. Maxillaria ringens typically blooms in the summer, producing showy inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Its botanical classification places it within the Orchidaceae family, making it a popular choice for both horticultural collections and botanical research.
