Magnolia venezuelensis

Magnolia venezuelensis, commonly known as the Venezuelan magnolia, is a striking evergreen tree native to the Andean regions of Venezuela.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy on top. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it often grows in humid, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
