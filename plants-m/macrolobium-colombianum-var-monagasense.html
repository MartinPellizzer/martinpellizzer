Macrolobium colombianum var. monagasense

Macrolobium colombianum var. monagasense, commonly known as the Colombian giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the Orinoco River basin in Venezuela.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter, providing a unique habitat for aquatic life. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and flooded forests. Its flowers are showy, with white petals and a yellow center, blooming during the wet season.

This variety is notable for its role in maintaining ecological balance in its native wetlands.
