Machilus pomifer

Machilus pomifer, commonly known as the fragrant mahogany tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a drupe, typically containing a single seed, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

The tree is valued for its aromatic wood and ornamental appeal in horticulture.
