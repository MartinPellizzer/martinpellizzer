Magnolia viridipetala

Magnolia viridipetala, commonly known as the Emerald Petal Magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The flowers are typically white or pale green with a distinct greenish tinge, giving the plant its common name. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
