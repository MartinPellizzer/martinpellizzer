Miconia dendroides

Miconia dendroides, commonly known as the tree miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a globose, fleshy capsule that contains numerous seeds.

Miconia dendroides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
