Masdevallia xanthina var. pallida

Masdevallia xanthina var. pallida, commonly known as the pale yellow masdevallia, is a hybrid orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, pendant inflorescences and flowers that range from pale yellow to cream in color. The plant typically grows in cool, shaded environments, often at high altitudes, and requires high humidity and well-draining soil. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is prized for its delicate, long-lasting blooms.

This variety is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively manageable care requirements.
