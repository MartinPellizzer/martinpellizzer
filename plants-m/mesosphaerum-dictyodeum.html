Mesosphaerum dictyodeum

Mesosphaerum dictyodeum, commonly known as the spiky coral tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, coral-like inflorescences that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, white or yellowish flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Mesosphaerum dictyodeum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
