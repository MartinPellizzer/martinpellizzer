Morrenia incana

Morrenia incana, commonly known as the silverleaf morrenia, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Morrenia incana is drought-tolerant and has adapted to survive in harsh, sun-exposed conditions.
