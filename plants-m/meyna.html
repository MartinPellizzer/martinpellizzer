Meyna

Meyna, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a sacred plant in Indian culture and holds significant religious and spiritual importance.

It belongs to the Nymphaeaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. The plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom on long, sturdy stalks rising from water. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often float on the surface of still waters.

The meyna is also known for its ability to thrive in muddy waters and is often associated with purity and enlightenment in various cultural traditions.
