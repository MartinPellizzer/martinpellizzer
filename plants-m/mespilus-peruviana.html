Mespilus peruviana

Mespilus peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its oval, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically harvested when they are fully ripe and slightly soft. These berries have a unique flavor, combining sweet and tart notes, and are often used in culinary applications.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
