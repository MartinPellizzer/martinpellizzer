Monadenium chevalieri

Monadenium chevalieri, commonly known as the African silver grass, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It features a compact, bushy growth habit with dense clusters of small, silver-gray leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by seed capsules. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its fleshy leaves and stems.

Monadenium chevalieri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique appearance and hardy nature.
