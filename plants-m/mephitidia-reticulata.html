Mephitidia reticulata

Mephitidia reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It lacks true roots and absorbs nutrients through its leaves, which are adapted for photosynthesis and nutrient uptake. The plant produces small, white or pale green berries that are often consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. Its intricate network of veins gives it its common name, and it plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat.

However, it can be detrimental to the health of its host trees if left unchecked.
