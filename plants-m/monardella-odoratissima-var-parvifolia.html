Monardella odoratissima var. parvifolia

Monardella odoratissima var. parvifolia, commonly known as California fennel or narrowleaf monardella, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a mild anise-like scent, and its flowers are typically pink to purple, arranged in dense terminal clusters. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It is part of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs, and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.

The variety parvifolia is distinguished by its smaller, more slender leaves compared to other varieties of Monardella.
