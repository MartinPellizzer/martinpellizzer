Mecodium szechuanense

Mecodium szechuanense, commonly known as the Sichuan mecodium, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
