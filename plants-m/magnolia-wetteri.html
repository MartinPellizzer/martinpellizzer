Magnolia wetteri

Magnolia wetteri, commonly known as the Wetter's magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Magnolia wetteri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and seasonal display.
