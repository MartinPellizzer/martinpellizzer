Muhlenbergia aspericaulis

Muhlenbergia aspericaulis, commonly known as rough muhlenbergia, is a perennial grass native to the southern United States.

It features slender, upright stems with hairy, rough-textured leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers in late summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Muhlenbergia aspericaulis is often used in native plant gardens for its ecological benefits and low maintenance requirements.
