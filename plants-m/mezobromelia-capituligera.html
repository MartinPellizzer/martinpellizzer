Mezobromelia capituligera

Mezobromelia capituligera, commonly known as the golden star hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a prominent central stamen, which resemble a star. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

Mezobromelia capituligera thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
