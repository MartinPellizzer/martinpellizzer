Miconia heteroporata

Miconia heteroporata, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by distinctive, bright red fruits containing numerous seeds. Miconia heteroporata is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it an important subject in ecological and horticultural studies.
