Mentha vagans

Mentha vagans, commonly known as wandering mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are fragrant and have a minty scent, making it useful in culinary and medicinal applications.

Mentha vagans thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.
