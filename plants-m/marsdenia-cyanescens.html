Marsdenia cyanescens

Marsdenia cyanescens, commonly known as the blue milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family, which includes other milkweeds, and is characterized by its milky sap. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with a distinctive crown of filaments at the base.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and unique floral structure.
