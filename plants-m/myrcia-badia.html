Myrcia badia

Myrcia badia, commonly known as the black myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a strong, aromatic scent.

The flowers are small, white, and clustered, and they bloom in the summer months.
