Mespilus tinctoria

Mespilus tinctoria, commonly known as the red mume, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by small, sweet-smelling fruits resembling cherries. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and prefers a sunny to partially shaded environment. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Mespilus tinctoria is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit, which can be used in culinary and medicinal applications.
