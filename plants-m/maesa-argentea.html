Maesa argentea

Maesa argentea, commonly known as silver maesa, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value. Its leaves are broad and leathery, often with a silvery or grayish underside, contributing to its common name.

Maesa argentea is valued in horticulture for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in partial shade.
