Montanoa revealii

Montanoa revealii, commonly known as the Mexican skyflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the high elevations of central Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its flowers bloom in clusters of bright yellow to orange, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat. Montanoa revealii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or open slopes.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
