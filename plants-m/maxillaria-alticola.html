Maxillaria alticola

Maxillaria alticola, commonly known as the mountain maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, thriving in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its pseudobulbs are short and stout, supporting its evergreen leaves, which are narrow and leathery.

Maxillaria alticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
