Meliosma polyptera

Meliosma polyptera, commonly known as the silverleaf maple, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by winged fruits that disperse its seeds. Its bark is smooth and grayish, becoming fissured with age, and it prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Meliosma polyptera is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
