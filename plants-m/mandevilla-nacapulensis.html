Mandevilla nacapulensis

Mandevilla nacapulensis, commonly known as the Nacapulensis mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its climbing growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces vibrant, colorful blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy texture, while its stems are hairy and tend to climb or trail.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
