Miconia pleiomagdalenensis

Miconia pleiomagdalenensis, commonly known as the Magdalen Islands miconia, is a member of the Melastomataceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are showy, with five petals that are usually pink or white, and it produces small, round berries as fruit.

It is native to the Caribbean, particularly the Magdalen Islands, and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
