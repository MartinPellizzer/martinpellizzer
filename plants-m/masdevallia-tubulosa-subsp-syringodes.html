Masdevallia tubulosa subsp. syringodes

Masdevallia tubulosa subsp.

syringodes, commonly known as the violet-tipped masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America. It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and produces striking, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive, tubular shape.

The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or rocks in montane forests. Its leaves are elongated and leathery, and it thrives in cool, high-altitude conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
