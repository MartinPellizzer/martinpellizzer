Machaeranthera shastensis var. montana

Machaeranthera shastensis var. montana, commonly known as Shasta tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has linear to lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular florets arranged in a disc-like structure.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
