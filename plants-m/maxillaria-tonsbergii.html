Maxillaria tonsbergii

Maxillaria tonsbergii, commonly known as the Tonsberg orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers with a distinctive pink and white coloration. The plant typically blooms during the warmer months, showcasing intricate floral structures with prominent sepals and petals. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil, and it thrives in moist, well-drained environments.

Maxillaria tonsbergii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
