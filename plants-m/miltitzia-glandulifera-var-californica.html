Miltitzia glandulifera var. californica

Miltitzia glandulifera var. californica, commonly known as the California miltitzia, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are often glandular-dotted, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.
