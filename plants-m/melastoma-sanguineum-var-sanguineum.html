Melastoma sanguineum var. sanguineum

Melastoma sanguineum var. sanguineum, commonly known as the red-stemmed melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its attractive, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in the summer months. The plant has small, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are often reddish in color. It is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a versatile plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
