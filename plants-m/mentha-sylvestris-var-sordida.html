Mentha sylvestris var. sordida

Mentha sylvestris var. sordida, commonly known as the bitter wood mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, square stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves contain menthol and other volatile oils, which contribute to its distinctive scent and medicinal properties.
