Mentha ovata

Mentha ovata, commonly known as oval mint, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to temperate regions of Europe, western Asia, and North Africa. The plant is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and strong aromatic scent, which is due to the presence of essential oils. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Mentha ovata is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
