Malva linnaei

Malva linnaei, commonly known as the Linnaean mallow, is a flowering plant species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton, hibiscus, and okra. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Malva linnaei is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its soothing properties.
