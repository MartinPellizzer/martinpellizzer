Mimulus ringens

Mimulus ringens, commonly known as the water primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its bright orange or yellow flowers, which are often clustered in small groups. The plant has broad, waxy leaves that are typically floating on the surface of water or growing in wet soil. It thrives in aquatic or semi-aquatic environments and is often found in ponds, marshes, and along streams.

Mimulus ringens is an annual herb that plays a role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
