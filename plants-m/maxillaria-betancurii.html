Maxillaria betancurii

Maxillaria betancurii, commonly known as the Betancurii orchid, is a species native to South America, particularly Colombia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its pseudobulbs and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple with contrasting yellow markings. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Maxillaria betancurii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
