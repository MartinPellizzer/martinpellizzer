Malveopsis spicata var. normalis

Malveopsis spicata var. normalis, commonly known as the common spicate malveopsis, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, pinkish to purple bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically features palmate leaves with lobed margins and is native to regions of southern Africa. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
