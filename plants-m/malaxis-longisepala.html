Malaxis longisepala

Malaxis longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepaled Malaxis, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Malaxis longisepala is valued for its unique floral structure and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
