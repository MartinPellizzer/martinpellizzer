Miconia attenuata

Miconia attenuata, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowroot or arrowleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by distinctive, triangular fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is considered a problematic species in some regions.
