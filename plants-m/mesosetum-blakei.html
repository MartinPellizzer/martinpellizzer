Mesosetum blakei

Mesosetum blakei, commonly known as Blake's grass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist, nutrient-rich soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Mesosetum blakei is often found in association with other wetland vegetation, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
