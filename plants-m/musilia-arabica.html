Musilia arabica

Musilia arabica, commonly known as the Arabian coffee plant, is a species native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit, known as a coffee cherry, contains the seeds used to make coffee.

Musilia arabica is valued for its unique flavor profile and historical significance in coffee cultivation.
