Melia guineensis

Melia guineensis, commonly known as African snowball, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and other related species. The plant is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a slightly rough texture.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
