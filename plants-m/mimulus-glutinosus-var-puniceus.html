Mimulus glutinosus var. puniceus

Mimulus glutinosus var. puniceus, commonly known as the crimson monkeyflower, is a striking annual plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, due to their nectar-rich structure.

The species is well-suited for moist, sunny habitats and is often found in streambanks and wet meadows.
