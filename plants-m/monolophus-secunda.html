Monolophus secunda

Monolophus secunda, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale purple flowers and silver-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid environments. Its leaves are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Monolophus secunda is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes.
