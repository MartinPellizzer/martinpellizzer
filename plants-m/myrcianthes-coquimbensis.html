Myrcianthes coquimbensis

Myrcianthes coquimbensis, commonly known as the Coquimbo myrcia, is a shrub native to Chile.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to arid conditions. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which have been used in traditional medicine.

The species is of interest for its ecological role and potential economic value.
