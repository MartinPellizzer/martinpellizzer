Myrtus orbicularis

Myrtus orbicularis, commonly known as the broadleaf myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, dark berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Myrtus orbicularis is also valued for its aromatic properties and historical use in perfumery and medicine.
