Monella ventricosa

Monella ventricosa, commonly known as the balloon vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its swollen, balloon-like seed pods that give it its common name. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The fruit is edible and has a mild, sweet flavor, making it a popular choice for ornamental and culinary use.
