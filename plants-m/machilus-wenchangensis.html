Machilus wenchangensis

Machilus wenchangensis, commonly known as the Wenchang privet, is a small evergreen tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by dark blue, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in subtropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
