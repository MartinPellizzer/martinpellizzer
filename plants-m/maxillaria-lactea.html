Maxillaria lactea

Maxillaria lactea, commonly known as the Milky Maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

The orchid produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding its survival in variable conditions.
