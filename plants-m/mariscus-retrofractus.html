Mariscus retrofractus

Mariscus retrofractus, commonly known as the backward fruit plant, is a unique member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its peculiar, elongated fruits that often twist in an unusual direction as they mature. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, well-drained soils. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with five petals.

Due to its rare and unusual growth pattern, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and horticulture.
