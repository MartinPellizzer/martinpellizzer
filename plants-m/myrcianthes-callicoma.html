Myrcianthes callicoma

Myrcianthes callicoma, commonly known as the golden guava, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of about 5 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fruits that are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with a strong aromatic scent.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fragrant foliage and fruit.
