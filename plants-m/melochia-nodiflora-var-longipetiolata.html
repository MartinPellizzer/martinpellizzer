Melochia nodiflora var. longipetiolata

Melochia nodiflora var. longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled melochia, is a tropical shrub characterized by its distinctive morphology.

It features elongated petioles, which are the leaf stalks, giving it a unique appearance compared to other varieties of Melochia. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its flowers are typically greenish-yellow, arranged in clusters. It is native to regions in Africa and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage.
