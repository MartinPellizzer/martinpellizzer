Molinia caerulea f. pallescens

Molinia caerulea f. pallescens, commonly known as the pale moor grass, is a variant of the common moor grass.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in wet, acidic habitats such as bogs and heathlands. The plant features narrow, pale blue-green blades that are shorter and more compact than the typical form. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale blue flowers.

This form is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in moist environments.
