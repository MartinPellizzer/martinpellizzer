Myrtus bracteata

Myrtus bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern Africa, particularly South Africa. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and small, clustered white flowers. It produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when mature.

Myrtus bracteata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
