Malaxis anceps

Malaxis anceps, commonly known as the narrow-leaved malaxis, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Malaxis anceps is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
