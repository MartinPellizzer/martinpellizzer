Monotropa hypopitys var. hypophegea

Monotropa hypopitys var. hypophegea, commonly known as the Indian pipe, is a unique flowering plant characterized by its lack of chlorophyll and reliance on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrients.

It produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments such as forests and woodlands. Its structure consists of a single stem with a cluster of leaves at the base and a terminal inflorescence.

Due to its pale coloration and ghostly appearance, it is often referred to as a "ghost plant.".
