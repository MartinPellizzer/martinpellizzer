Malus prasomila var. lutea-praecox

Malus prasomila var. lutea-praecox, commonly known as the early yellow crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom early in the spring, typically before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, tart apples that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly serrated margin, and the bark is smooth and grayish in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.
