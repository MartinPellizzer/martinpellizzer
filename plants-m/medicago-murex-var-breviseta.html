Medicago murex var. breviseta

Medicago murex var. breviseta, commonly known as the short-stalked sainfoin, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has a deep root system, which allows it to thrive in dry, rocky soils. It is often found in grasslands and along dry streambeds.

This variety is valued for its nitrogen-fixing abilities and is used in ecological restoration and livestock forage.
