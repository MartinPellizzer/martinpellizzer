Melocactus pruinosus var. concinnus

Melocactus pruinosus var. concinnus, commonly known as the velvet melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, globose stem covered in a dense, velvety layer of white to pale green waxy hairs. The plant produces large, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in the evening. Its unique appearance and ability to thrive in harsh desert conditions make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.

The variety var. concinnus is distinguished by its more compact growth and uniformly colored stem compared to other varieties of Melocactus pruinosus.
