Mikania cacerensis

Mikania cacerensis, commonly known as Mexican morning glory, is a vine species native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and trailing habit. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming throughout the growing season. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy and twining.

Mikania cacerensis is often found in disturbed areas, such as along roadsides and in open woodlands.
