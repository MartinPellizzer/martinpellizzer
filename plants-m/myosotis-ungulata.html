Myosotis ungulata

Myosotis ungulata, commonly known as the mountain mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and the flowers are often clustered in small inflorescences.

Myosotis ungulata is known for its resilience to cold and harsh weather conditions.
