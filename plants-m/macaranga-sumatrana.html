Macaranga sumatrana

Macaranga sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran paper tree, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a fast-growing deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in Indonesia, Malaysia, and Thailand. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powder, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits that release tiny seeds.
