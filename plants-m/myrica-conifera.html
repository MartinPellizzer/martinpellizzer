Myrica conifera

Myrica conifera, commonly known as the wax myrtle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, conifer-like leaves that are often coated with a waxy layer, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by dark blue berries, which are an important food source for various birds and wildlife. Myrica conifera thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its aromatic foliage and adaptability make it a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects.
