Mesembryanthemum rogersii

Mesembryanthemum rogersii, commonly known as the Rogers' ice plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Mesembryanthemum rogersii is also noted for its ability to store water in its leaves, allowing it to survive in arid environments.
