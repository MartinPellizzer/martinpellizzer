Malus hamelii

Malus hamelii, commonly known as the Hamel's apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its ornamental value due to its showy flowers and colorful fruits. The plant typically grows as a deciduous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth margin, and its flowers are white to pink, appearing in clusters.

The fruits are small, round apples that are edible but not commonly cultivated for commercial use.
