Macrosolen wrayi

Macrosolen wrayi, commonly known as the giant spiderwort, is a striking member of the Amaryllis family (Amaryllidaceae).

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter, typically in shades of purple and white. Its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that are often used in horticultural displays.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant in warmer regions.
