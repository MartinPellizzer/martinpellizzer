Maranta concinna

Maranta concinna, commonly known as the velvetleaf arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family, which includes a variety of ornamental and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color.

Maranta concinna is primarily cultivated for its ornamental value and its starchy roots, which are used in traditional cuisines.
