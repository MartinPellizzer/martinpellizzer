Maxillaria inaudita

Maxillaria inaudita, commonly known as the Silent Maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces fragrant, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive yellow lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical forests. Its flowers bloom during the warmer months, and it is noted for its unique and subtle fragrance.

Maxillaria inaudita is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and relatively easy care requirements.
