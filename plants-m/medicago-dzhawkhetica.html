Medicago dzhawkhetica

Medicago dzhawkhetica, commonly known as the Caucasian clover, is a perennial legume native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has a deep root system, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils and semi-arid environments. It is often used as a forage crop due to its high nutritional value for livestock.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation, contributing to ecological balance in its native habitat.
