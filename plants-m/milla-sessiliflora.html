Milla sessiliflora

Milla sessiliflora, commonly known as the sessile-flower milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Milla sessiliflora is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for various pollinators.
