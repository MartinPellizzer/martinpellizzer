Myrobalanus sericocarpa

Myrobalanus sericocarpa, commonly known as the silver apple or Indian date, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy on top and hairy underneath. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by distinctive, woody, silver-haired fruits that are edible. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
