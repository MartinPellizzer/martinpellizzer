Monerma repens

Monerma repens, commonly known as the creeping monerma, is a unique plant species with distinctive botanical features.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its prostrate growth habit and fleshy, succulent stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Monerma repens is adapted to arid environments, making it a hardy and resilient species in dry climates.
