Moenchia erecta var. octandra

Moenchia erecta var. octandra, commonly known as the eight-lobed moenchia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
