Mitracarpus laeteviridis

Mitracarpus laeteviridis, commonly known as the green velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, green pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of leguminous seeds.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the legume family, Fabaceae.
