Mahonia confusa var. bournei

Mahonia confusa var. bournei, commonly known as the Bornean mahonia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with a deep green color and a leathery texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by blue-black berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its evergreen foliage and seasonal blooms.
