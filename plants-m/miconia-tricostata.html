Miconia tricostata

Miconia tricostata, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the plant is also known for its rapid growth and potential invasiveness in certain regions.

Miconia tricostata is characterized by its ornamental value and ecological impact in its native and introduced habitats.
