Mesoneuron hallieri

Mesoneuron hallieri, commonly known as the red-tipped pine.

This evergreen conifer is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that are typically reddish at the tips, providing a striking visual contrast against its bluish-green foliage. It produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of the coniferous family. The plant is native to specific regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a unique and valuable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
