Methonica virescens var. plantii

Methonica virescens var. plantii, commonly known as the Emerald Spire, is a striking evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, dark green foliage that forms a tall, columnar shape.

Native to the subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, well-drained soils and partial to full shade. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in spring, which are followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and air-purifying properties.

Its leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory effects.
