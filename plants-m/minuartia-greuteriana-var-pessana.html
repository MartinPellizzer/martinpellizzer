Minuartia greuteriana var. pessana

Minuartia greuteriana var. pessana, commonly known as the Pessana's rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically has basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate, with a dense covering of fine hairs. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
