Megaclinium

Megaclinium, commonly known as the giant beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically green or brown in color. Megaclinium thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
