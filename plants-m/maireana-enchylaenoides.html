Maireana enchylaenoides

Maireana enchylaenoides, commonly known as the Grey Box, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its grey-green, leathery leaves and dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the autumn and winter seasons, which are followed by woody, cylindrical capsules containing seeds. It is adapted to low rainfall and nutrient-poor soils, making it a hardy species suited to harsh environments.

Its ecological significance includes providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
