Melochia frutescens

Melochia frutescens, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red velvetleaf, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically green with a reddish or purplish underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release hairy, wind-dispersed seeds.

Melochia frutescens is often found in disturbed habitats and is considered a weed in agricultural areas.
