Myosotis myriantha

Myosotis myriantha, commonly known as the many-flowered forget-me-not, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It features upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage and produces clusters of small, delicate blue to purple flowers. The plant typically blooms from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Myosotis myriantha thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins and wetlands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in temperate climates.
