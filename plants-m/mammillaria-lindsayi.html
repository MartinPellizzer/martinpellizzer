Mammillaria lindsayi

Mammillaria lindsayi, commonly known as the Lindsay's hedgehog cactus, is a small, globular cactus native to Mexico.

It features a dense covering of white, woolly hairs that help protect it from intense sunlight and dehydration. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
