Mesembryanthemum crassipes

Mesembryanthemum crassipes, commonly known as the broad-leaved ice plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and vibrant, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and yellow. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to drought conditions. Its unique structure allows it to store water efficiently, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Mesembryanthemum crassipes is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
