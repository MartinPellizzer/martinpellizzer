Momordica charantia var. longirostrata

Momordica charantia var. longirostrata, commonly known as the bitter melon or karela, is a vine species native to Southeast Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and spiny fruit, which can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces yellow flowers and has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its fruit contains numerous small, white seeds enclosed in a fibrous pulp.

This plant is often cultivated for its edible fruit, which is used in various culinary traditions and traditional medicine.
