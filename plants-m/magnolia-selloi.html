Magnolia selloi

Magnolia selloi, commonly known as the Sello's magnolia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant produces elliptical, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. Its bark is smooth and grayish in color, and it can grow as a tree or shrub depending on environmental conditions.

The flowers bloom in early spring, making it a notable ornamental plant in horticulture.
