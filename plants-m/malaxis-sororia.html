Malaxis sororia

Malaxis sororia, commonly known as the slender malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring in soil, while its leaves are narrow and grass-like.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is notable for its unique floral structure and ecological role in its native habitat.
