Malabaila porphyrodiscus

Malabaila porphyrodiscus, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with deep red veins. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
