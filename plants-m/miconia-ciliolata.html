Miconia ciliolata

Miconia ciliolata, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by bright red berries. Miconia ciliolata is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural studies.
