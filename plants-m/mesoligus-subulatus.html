Mesoligus subulatus

Mesoligus subulatus, commonly known as the slender starwort, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, needle-like leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. Native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it often grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has a fibrous root system and exhibits a low-growing, mat-forming habit.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive yet often overlooked component of its native ecosystems.
