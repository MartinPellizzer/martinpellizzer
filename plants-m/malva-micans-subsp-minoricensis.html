Malva micans subsp. minoricensis

Malva micans subsp.

minoricensis, commonly known as the Minorca mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Balearic Islands. It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and showy pink to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. It is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms and foliage.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
