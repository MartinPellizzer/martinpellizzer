Maackia australis

Maackia australis, commonly known as the Australian maackia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry forests.

Maackia australis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
