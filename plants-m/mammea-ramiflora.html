Mammea ramiflora

Mammea ramiflora, commonly known as the ramiflorous mammea, is a tropical tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and alternate, simple, elliptic leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

Mammea ramiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
