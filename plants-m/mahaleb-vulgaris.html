Mahaleb vulgaris

Mahaleb vulgaris, commonly known as the cherry laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans and livestock. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a slight aromatic scent.

Mahaleb vulgaris is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
