Mesosphaerum caatingense

Mesosphaerum caatingense, commonly known as the caatinga acacia, is a shrub native to the semi-arid regions of northeastern Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has adapted to arid conditions with deep roots and a thick, waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
