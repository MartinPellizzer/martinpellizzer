Mimosa micropteris var. micropteris

Mimosa micropteris var. micropteris, commonly known as the small-leaf mimosa, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small, deciduous shrub characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and pink to reddish-pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and its flowers are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed sites, making it both a useful pioneer species and a potential invasive in some regions.
