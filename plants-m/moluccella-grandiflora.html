Moluccella grandiflora

Moluccella grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered moluccella, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive purple or red throat. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded woodland areas.

Moluccella grandiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
