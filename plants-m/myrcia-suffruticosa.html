Myrcia suffruticosa

Myrcia suffruticosa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas.

Myrcia suffruticosa has a long history of use in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
