Monochaetum subglabrum

Monochaetum subglabrum, commonly known as the slender spike grass, is a species of grass native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as heathlands and woodland edges. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, pale flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
