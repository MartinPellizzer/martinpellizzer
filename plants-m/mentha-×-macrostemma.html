Mentha × macrostemma

Mentha × macrostemma, commonly known as giant horsemint, is a hybrid mint species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, making it a striking presence in wildflower meadows. The plant produces showy, purple to pink flowers in whorls along the stem, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, minty aroma, often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.

Mentha × macrostemma thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.
