Monardella villosa var. glabella

Monardella villosa var. glabella, commonly known as hairy beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and showy, tubular pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is a valuable nectar source for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
