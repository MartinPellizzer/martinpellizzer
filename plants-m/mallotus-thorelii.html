Mallotus thorelii

Mallotus thorelii, commonly known as the African snowball, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded clusters of small white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

Mallotus thorelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its winter flowering habit.
