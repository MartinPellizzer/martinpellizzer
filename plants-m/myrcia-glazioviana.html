Myrcia glazioviana

Myrcia glazioviana, commonly known as the Glaziov's myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a glossy appearance, while its fruits are small, woody capsules that contain aromatic seeds.

Myrcia glazioviana is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.
