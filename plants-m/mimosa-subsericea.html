Mimosa subsericea

Mimosa subsericea, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and showy, fluffy yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a woody stem with a smooth, pale bark and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
