Mimosa tremula var. tenuis

Mimosa tremula var. tenuis, commonly known as the slender mimosa, is a shrub native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are often described as resembling the fingers of a hand. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or near freshwater sources.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, although it can be invasive in certain regions.
