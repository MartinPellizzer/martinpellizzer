Moraea cooperi

Moraea cooperi, commonly known as the Cooper's moraea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of funnel-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated in gardens around the world.
