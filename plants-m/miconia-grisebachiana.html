Miconia grisebachiana

Miconia grisebachiana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Miconia grisebachiana*, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
