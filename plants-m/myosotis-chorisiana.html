Myosotis chorisiana

Myosotis chorisiana, commonly known as the water forget-me-not, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its small, delicate blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and streambanks, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Myosotis chorisiana is a perennial species that plays an important role in its native ecosystem by providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
