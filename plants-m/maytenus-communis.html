Maytenus communis

Maytenus communis, commonly known as the Brazilian tea tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, dark green upper surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing small, hard seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed areas.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
