Moringa peregrina

Moringa peregrina, commonly known as the edible wild mallow, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its small, green, edible leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid environments. Its leaves are rich in nutrients such as vitamins A and C, calcium, and protein, making it a valuable food source.

Moringa peregrina is also used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
