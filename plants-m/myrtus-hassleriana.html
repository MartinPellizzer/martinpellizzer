Myrtus hassleriana

Myrtus hassleriana, commonly known as the Chilean myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its small, fragrant white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by dark blue berries.

Myrtus hassleriana thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to tolerate salty air and strong winds.
