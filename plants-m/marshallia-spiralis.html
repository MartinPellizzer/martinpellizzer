Marshallia spiralis

Marshallia spiralis, commonly known as the spiralleaf coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking, pale yellow to orange coneflowers with prominent, spiraling bracts that give it its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet and blooms from late summer into early fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soils, making it a suitable choice for native gardens.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
