Malaxis cernua

Malaxis cernua, commonly known as the nodding Malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that hang downward, giving it a delicate appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and forest floors. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces a single, elongated flower spike.

Malaxis cernua is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its subtle beauty and ecological significance in its native habitats.
