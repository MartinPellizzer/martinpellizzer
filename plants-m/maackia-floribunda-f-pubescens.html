Maackia floribunda f. pubescens

Maackia floribunda f. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent maackia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often compound leaves. The plant features small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in racemes. Its leaves are pinnately compound and covered with a fine layer of pubescence, giving it a soft, hairy appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
