Miconia ibarrae

Miconia ibarrae, commonly known as the Ibarra's miconia, is a member of the Melastomataceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Miconia ibarrae is often found in humid, lowland forests and is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
