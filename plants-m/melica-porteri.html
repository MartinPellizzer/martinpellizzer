Melica porteri

Melica porteri, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant has slender, leafy stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy surface.

Melica porteri plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
