Mikania lanuginosa

Mikania lanuginosa, commonly known as the hairy beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, creeping stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. It is native to the Americas and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forests.

Mikania lanuginosa is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain regions.
