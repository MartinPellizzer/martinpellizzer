Malus prunifolia var. dulcissima

Malus prunifolia var. dulcissima, commonly known as the sweetleaf apple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its oval, serrated leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries in late summer. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
