Maxillaria pseudoneglecta

Maxillaria pseudoneglecta, commonly known as the pink-flowered maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, velvety pink petals that often feature intricate patterns. The plant produces fragrant, butterfly-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
