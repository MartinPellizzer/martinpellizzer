Mariscus cornelii-ostenii

Mariscus cornelii-ostenii, commonly known as the Cornelian cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the genus Mariscus within the family Ericaceae, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves appear. Its red, fleshy berries are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.
