Miconia robustissima

Miconia robustissima, commonly known as the giant miconia, is a large shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Miconia robustissima is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, often invading natural habitats.

It is also valued in horticulture for its striking foliage and ornamental appeal.
