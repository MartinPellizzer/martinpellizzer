Maxillaria cozieriana

Maxillaria cozieriana, commonly known as the Cozier's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms in the summer and has a sympodial growth habit. Its flowers are often clustered in racemes and are known for their intricate, intricate structure.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
