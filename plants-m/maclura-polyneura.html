Maclura polyneura

Maclura polyneura, commonly known as the yellow wood or honey locust, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are divided into 7 to 15 pairs of oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, spiny pods containing edible seeds. Its bark is thick and deeply furrowed, often with a distinctive amber hue.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used historically for medicinal purposes.
