Madhuca borneensis

Madhuca borneensis, commonly known as the Borneo wax tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing a single seed. Its latex is used in traditional medicine, and the wood is valued for its durability and fine grain.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various wildlife species.
