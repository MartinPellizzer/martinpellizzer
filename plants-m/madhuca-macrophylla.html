Madhuca macrophylla

Madhuca macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf mahua, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The tree produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the dry season. Its fruit is a capsule containing numerous small, hard seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in various cultures.
