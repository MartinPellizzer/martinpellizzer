Myrceugenia rufescens

Myrceugenia rufescens, commonly known as the red-flowered myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to the temperate rainforests of southern South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, red or pink flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in humid, cool environments and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

Myrceugenia rufescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
