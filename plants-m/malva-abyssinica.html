Malva abyssinica

Malva abyssinica, commonly known as Abyssinian mallows, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia and parts of East Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. The plant is characterized by its broad, palmate leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

The plant has traditional uses in local medicine and is also appreciated for its ornamental value.
