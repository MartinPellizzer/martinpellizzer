Medinilla lanceata

Medinilla lanceata, commonly known as the lanceleaf medinilla, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that are often followed by bright red or orange berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Medinilla lanceata is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
