Malvaviscus acerifolius

Malvaviscus acerifolius, commonly known as the scarlet bower vine, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting hummingbirds and other pollinators. The plant has heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory properties. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its propagation.
