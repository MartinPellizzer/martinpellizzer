Myrciaria rubiginosa

Myrciaria rubiginosa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá-preto, is a species of shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in local cuisine. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a fragrant aroma, while its flowers are small and white or pink.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit and foliage.
