Muraltia exappendiculata

Muraltia exappendiculata, commonly known as the red muraltia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a glossy appearance.

Muraltia exappendiculata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
