Myrtus madagascariensis

Myrtus madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, black berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas of its native habitat.

Myrtus madagascariensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in local ecosystems.
