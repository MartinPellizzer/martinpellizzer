Maieta lanata

Maieta lanata, commonly known as the woolly maieta, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and showy, pale yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and riverbanks. Its hairy leaves help reduce water loss, adapting it to humid climates.

Maieta lanata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
