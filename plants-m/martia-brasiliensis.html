Martia brasiliensis

Martia brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian martia, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often blooming in the spring. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
