Myrcia ellipticifolia

Myrcia ellipticifolia, commonly known as the elliptic-leaved myrtle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its elliptical-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Myrcia ellipticifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
