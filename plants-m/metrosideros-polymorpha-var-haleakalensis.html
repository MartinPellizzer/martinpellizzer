Metrosideros polymorpha var. haleakalensis

Metrosideros polymorpha var. haleakalensis, commonly known as the Haleakalensis Hawaiian Christmas tree, is a variant of the native Hawaiian koa tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive red flowers and is endemic to the island of Maui. The plant thrives in coastal and lowland areas, often found in dry forests and shrublands. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a leathery texture, while its bark is smooth and reddish in color.

This variety plays an important ecological role in Hawaiian ecosystems and is a symbol of the island's natural heritage.
