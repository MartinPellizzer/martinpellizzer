Melastoma verticillatum

Melastoma verticillatum, commonly known as the clustered melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, clustered inflorescences. The flowers are typically pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are dispersed by birds and other animals.
