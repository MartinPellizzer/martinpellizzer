Mentha viridis var. angustifolia

Mentha viridis var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a strong, peppermint-like aroma. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and is used in culinary and medicinal applications.
