Medicago sativa subvar. kandagharokabulica

Medicago sativa subvar. kandagharokabulica, commonly known as Kandahar clover, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, making it a valuable forage crop in dry regions. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and improves soil fertility through nitrogen fixation.

This subspecies is also noted for its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
