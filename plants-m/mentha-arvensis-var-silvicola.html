Mentha arvensis var. silvicola

Mentha arvensis var. silvicola, commonly known as the forest mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a rough, serrated margin. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or violet, appearing in whorled clusters.

The essential oils of this variety contain menthone and other aromatic compounds, contributing to its use in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.
