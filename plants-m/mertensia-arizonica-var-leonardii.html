Mertensia arizonica var. leonardii

Mertensia arizonica var. leonardii, commonly known as Arizona bluebells, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a characteristic species of woodland and rocky habitats.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
