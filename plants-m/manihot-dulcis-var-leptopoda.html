Manihot dulcis var. leptopoda

Manihot dulcis var. leptopoda, commonly known as the arrowleaf batata, is a variant of the sweet potato plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, arrowhead-like leaves and tuberous roots that are typically orange to reddish in color. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its tubers are rich in carbohydrates and can be used in various culinary preparations.

This variety is valued for both its nutritional content and adaptability to different growing conditions.
