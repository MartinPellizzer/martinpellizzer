Myrcia albotomentosa var. rubescens

Myrcia albotomentosa var. rubescens, commonly known as the red-hair myrtle, is a shrub native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its hairy, pale green leaves that turn reddish-brown with age. Its small, fragrant white flowers are followed by edible, orange-red berries.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
