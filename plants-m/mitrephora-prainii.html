Mitrephora prainii

Mitrephora prainii, commonly known as the Bornean mitrephora, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
