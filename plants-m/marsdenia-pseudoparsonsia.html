Marsdenia pseudoparsonsia

Marsdenia pseudoparsonsia, commonly known as the Parson's Marsdenia, is a climbing plant belonging to the Asclepiadaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, with a distinctive corona that surrounds the five stamens. This species is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is often found in forested areas.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
