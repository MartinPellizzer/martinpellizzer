Matthiola revoluta var. tibetana

Matthiola revoluta var. tibetana, commonly known as the Tibetan wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, dry conditions. Its leaves are often hairy and have a slightly aromatic scent, while the flowers come in shades of yellow, orange, and red.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
