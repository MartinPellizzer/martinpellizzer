Machilus platycarpus

Machilus platycarpus, commonly known as the broad-fruited sandarach, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a small, woody drupe, which contains a single seed.

The tree is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its aromatic wood and ornamental qualities.
