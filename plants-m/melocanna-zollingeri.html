Melocanna zollingeri

Melocanna zollingeri, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a striking member of the grass family, Poaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. This plant is characterized by its massive, hollow stems that can grow up to several meters in height. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, waxy texture that helps it thrive in humid climates.

Melocanna zollingeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
