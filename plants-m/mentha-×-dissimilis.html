Mentha × dissimilis

Mentha × dissimilis, commonly known as catnip, is a hybrid plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its square, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions, including North America.

Mentha × dissimilis is known for its aromatic properties and is often used in herbal remedies and as a stimulant for cats.
