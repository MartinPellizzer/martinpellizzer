Marrubium heterocladum var. microdontum

Marrubium heterocladum var. microdontum, commonly known as the small-toothed harts tongue plant, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale pink to white, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
