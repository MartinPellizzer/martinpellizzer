Medusea fructus-pini

Medusea fructus-pini, commonly known as the pine fruit orchid, is a unique epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, forested environments.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble pine cones, which is a distinctive botanical feature. The plant's roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding debris. It typically grows on trees, particularly pines, in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its reproductive structures are specialized for wind pollination, reflecting its evolutionary adaptation to its ecological niche.
