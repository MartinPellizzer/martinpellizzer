Margbensonia disperma

Margbensonia disperma, commonly known as the scattered margbensia, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, sprawling growth habit and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale green, which bloom in late spring. Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for certain ecosystems.

Due to habitat loss, it is currently listed as endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
