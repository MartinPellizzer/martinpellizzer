Mandragora caulescens

Mandragora caulescens, commonly known as the slender mandrake, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive, often twisted root system. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or white in color. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Mandragora caulescens contains tropane alkaloids, which have historically been used in traditional medicine and folk remedies.
