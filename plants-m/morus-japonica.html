Morus japonica

Morus japonica, commonly known as the Japanese mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically lobed and have a smooth texture. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that mature into fluffy, white clusters. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its leaves, which are used in traditional silk production.

Morus japonica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide shade in gardens.
