Manihot hilariana

Manihot hilariana, commonly known as the African arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its roots are edible and have a starchy texture similar to taro and cassava, making it a valuable food source in some regions.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
