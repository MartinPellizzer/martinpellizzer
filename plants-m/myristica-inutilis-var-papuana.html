Myristica inutilis var. papuana

Myristica inutilis var. papuana, commonly known as the Papuan nutmeg, is a member of the Myristicaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Indonesia and Papua.

It is a small evergreen tree that produces distinctive reddish-brown fruits containing aromatic seeds. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The seeds, often referred to as "nutmeg," are valued for their essential oils and medicinal properties.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is typically found in lowland forests.
