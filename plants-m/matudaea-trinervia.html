Matudaea trinervia

Matudaea trinervia, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the Andes, is a striking member of the Araceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a striking violet coloration and produces showy, spadix-like flowers that are often surrounded by a colorful spathe. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and its traditional use in Andean cultures for ceremonial and medicinal purposes.

Matudaea trinervia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
