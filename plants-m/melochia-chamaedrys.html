Melochia chamaedrys

Melochia chamaedrys, commonly known as the creeping melochia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a shrub or vine that typically grows close to the ground, with slender stems that can spread over large areas. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-yellow in color, followed by red or orange berries. Its leaves are usually ovate or elliptical, with a slightly hairy texture.

Melochia chamaedrys is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.
