Mentha gentilis subsp. rubra

Mentha gentilis subsp.

rubra, commonly known as red mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves that are typically reddish or purple in color.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in loose spikes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Red mint is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its menthol-like fragrance and cooling properties.
