Mollugo herniarioioes

Mollugo herniarioioes, commonly known as the common bladderwort, is a small flowering plant belonging to the family Molluginaceae.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It has a creeping growth habit and often forms dense mats in disturbed or open habitats.

Its botanical features include the presence of specialized structures called pseudobulbs, which help store water and nutrients.
