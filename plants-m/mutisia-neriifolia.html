Mutisia neriifolia

Mutisia neriifolia, commonly known as the blackleaf mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina, Chile, and Peru.

The plant features a woody stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits known as cypsela.

Mutisia neriifolia is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
