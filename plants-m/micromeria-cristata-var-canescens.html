Micromeria cristata var. canescens

Micromeria cristata var. canescens, commonly known as the desert sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant has hairy, grayish foliage that helps it retain moisture in arid environments. It blooms from spring to early summer, producing nectar that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats.
