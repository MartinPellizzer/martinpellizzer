Malus sylvestris var. rhodopaea

Malus sylvestris var. rhodopaea, commonly known as the Greek apple or wild apple, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the forests of Greece and the Balkans, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its small, ornamental flowers and fruit, which are typically red or yellow in color. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic value.

This variety is also valued for its historical and ecological significance in its native region.
