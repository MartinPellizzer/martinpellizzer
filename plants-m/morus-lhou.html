Morus lhou

Morus lhou, commonly known as the Chinese mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fuzzy fruit. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, while the bark and roots have also been utilized in herbal remedies.

The tree plays an important role in ecological systems, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
