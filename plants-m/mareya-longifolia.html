Mareya longifolia

Mareya longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved mareya, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a tropical look.

Its ornamental value lies in its elegant foliage and delicate floral display.
