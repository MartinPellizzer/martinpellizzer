Myrcia orthophylla

Myrcia orthophylla, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are aromatic and have been utilized in various cultural practices.

Myrcia orthophylla thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.
