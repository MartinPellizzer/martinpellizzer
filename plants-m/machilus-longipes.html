Machilus longipes

Machilus longipes, commonly known as the long-fruited laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, elongated leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Machilus longipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
