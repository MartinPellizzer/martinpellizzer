Majana canina

Majana canina, commonly known as dog's milk, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, white or yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, milky sap. It is often found in arid or semi-arid environments and has adapted to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
