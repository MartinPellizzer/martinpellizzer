Martiodendron excelsum

Martiodendron excelsum, commonly known as the tall tamarack, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
