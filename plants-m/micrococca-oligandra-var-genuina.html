Micrococca oligandra var. genuina

Micrococca oligandra var. genuina, commonly known as the golden micrococca, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

The leaves are leathery and aromatic, contributing to its resilience in dry conditions.
