Massonia ensifolia

Massonia ensifolia, commonly known as the sword-leaved massonia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Massonia ensifolia is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
