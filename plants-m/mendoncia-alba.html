Mendoncia alba

Mendoncia alba, commonly known as the white mendoncia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate white petals and compact growth habit. The plant typically blooms during the spring and early summer, producing clusters of small, fragrant flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
