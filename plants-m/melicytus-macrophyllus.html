Melicytus macrophyllus

Melicytus macrophyllus, commonly known as the large-leaved melicytus, is a species of flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in coastal and lowland forest environments. The plant is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are woody capsules that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
