Myristica chalmersii

Myristica chalmersii, commonly known as the New Zealand nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, reddish-brown flowers. The tree produces small, egg-shaped fruits that contain a single seed, which is encased in a hard, aromatic shell. The plant is notable for its unique chemical compounds, including myristicin and elemicin, which contribute to its distinctive aroma and medicinal properties.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understorey of temperate rainforests.
