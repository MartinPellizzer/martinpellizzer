Malvastrum belloa

Malvastrum belloa, commonly known as the showy malva, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping in arid regions.
