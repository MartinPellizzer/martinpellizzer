Myrcia neorostrata

Myrcia neorostrata, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are typically pale yellow or white, and it produces small, dark blue or black berries.

Myrcia neorostrata is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional Brazilian medicine.
