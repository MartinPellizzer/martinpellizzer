Mentha longifolia f. prodanii

Mentha longifolia f. prodanii, commonly known as the Serbian mint, is a subspecies of the longleaf mint.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan region, particularly Serbia and parts of Greece. The plant features slender, aromatic stems and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive minty fragrance. Its flowers are small, pale blue to violet, and arranged in dense spikes.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and calming properties.
