Melaleuca leucadendra var. speciosa

Melaleuca leucadendra var. speciosa, commonly known as the White Melaleuca, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late winter to early spring. Its aromatic foliage and showy inflorescences make it a popular ornamental plant.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.
