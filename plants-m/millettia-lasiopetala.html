Millettia lasiopetala

Millettia lasiopetala, commonly known as the hairy petal millettia, is a deciduous tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional medicine.
