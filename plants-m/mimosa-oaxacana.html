Mimosa oaxacana

Mimosa oaxacana, commonly known as the Oaxaca mimosa, is a shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and can thrive in full sun.

Mimosa oaxacana is also known for its sensitive leaves that fold when touched, a trait shared with other mimosa species.
