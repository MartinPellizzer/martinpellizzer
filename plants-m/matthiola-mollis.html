Matthiola mollis

Matthiola mollis, commonly known as the velvet flower or sweet rocket, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of fragrant, four-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a mild, spicy scent reminiscent of mustard.

It is a short-lived perennial that blooms throughout the spring and early summer.
