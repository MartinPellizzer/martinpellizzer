Melaleuca viminea var. major

Melaleuca viminea var. major, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a species native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical, bottlebrush-like inflorescences that are typically red or pink in color. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in coastal or sandy soils. It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height.

The flowers are primarily pollinated by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
