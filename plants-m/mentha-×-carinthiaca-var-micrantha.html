Mentha × carinthiaca var. micrantha

Mentha × carinthiaca var. micrantha, commonly known as narrow-leaved mint, is a hybrid mint species native to the Alps.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and a strong, minty aroma. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained soil. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale purple or blue.

This variety is often used in herbal teas and for its aromatic properties in culinary applications.
