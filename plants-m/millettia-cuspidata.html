Millettia cuspidata

Millettia cuspidata, commonly known as the red sanders, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its fine grain and durability. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.

Due to its ornamental value and timber use, it is also cultivated in horticultural settings.
