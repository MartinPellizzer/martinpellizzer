Miconia cosangensis

Miconia cosangensis, commonly known as the Cosanga miconia, is a species of flowering plant in the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
