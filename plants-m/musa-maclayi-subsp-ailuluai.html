Musa maclayi subsp. ailuluai

Musa maclayi subsp.

ailuluai, commonly known as the Maclay's banana, is a lesser-known subspecies of the banana genus native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and clusters of elongated, fleshy berries that are typically yellow when mature.

The plant is primarily cultivated for its edible fruits, which have a sweet and aromatic flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil and requires regular watering for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
