Melica persica var. flava

Melica persica var. flava, commonly known as Persian millet, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green with a slight bluish tint. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, yellowish flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and used in traditional herbal medicine.
