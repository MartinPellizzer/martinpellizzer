Melaleuca stenostachya subsp. amplior

Melaleuca stenostachya subsp.

amplior, commonly known as the broad-stemmed bottlebrush, is a shrub native to northern Australia. It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and distinctive bottlebrush-like flowers that are typically red or pink.

The plant produces small, woody fruits called capsules, which release seeds when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
