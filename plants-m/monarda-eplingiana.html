Monarda eplingiana

Monarda eplingiana, commonly known as Epling's bee balm, is a member of the Lamiaceae family and is native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Monarda eplingiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
