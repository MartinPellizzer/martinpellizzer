Meriania ciliaris

Meriania ciliaris, commonly known as the silverleaf meriania, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Meriania ciliaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
