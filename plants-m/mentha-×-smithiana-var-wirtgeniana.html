Mentha × smithiana var. wirtgeniana

Mentha × smithiana var. wirtgeniana, commonly known as the Wirtgen's mint, is a hybrid species within the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and square, hairy stems typical of the genus Mentha. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue to purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine due to its menthol-like compounds.
