Mussaenda rufescens

Mussaenda rufescens, commonly known as the red-flowered Mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually pink to red in color, arranged in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
