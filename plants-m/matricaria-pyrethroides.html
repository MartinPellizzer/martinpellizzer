Matricaria pyrethroides

Matricaria pyrethroides, commonly known as the African pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers and linear, aromatic leaves. The plant is notable for containing pyrethroid compounds, which are natural insecticides used in pest control. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

Due to its chemical properties, it has been cultivated for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
