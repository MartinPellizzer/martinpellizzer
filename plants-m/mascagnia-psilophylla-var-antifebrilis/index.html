Mascagnia Psilophylla Var. Antifebrilis

Mascagnia antifebrilis, scientifically known as Mascagnia psilophylla var. antifebrilis, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico. This variant is distinguished by its unique morphological features, including its compact growth habit and pale green to blue-gray foliage. It thrives in harsh, semi-desert environments where it has adapted to extreme temperature fluctuations and limited water availability. Due to its limited distribution and specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species. Understanding and conserving Mascagnia antifebrilis is crucial for preserving biodiversity in its native ecosystems.
