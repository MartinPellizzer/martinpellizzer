Marsdenia engleriana

Marsdenia engleriana, commonly known as the Engler's Marsdenia, is a climbing plant belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant features opposite, elliptic leaves with a waxy surface and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are follicles that release seeds with tufts of hair for wind dispersal.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
