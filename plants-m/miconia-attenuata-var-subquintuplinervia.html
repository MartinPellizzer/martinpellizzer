Miconia attenuata var. subquintuplinervia

Miconia attenuata var. subquintuplinervia, commonly known as the quintuple-veined miconia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its large, palmate leaves with five to seven primary veins. The leaves are typically dark green and glossy, with a waxy surface that reflects light. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries.

It is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some areas.
