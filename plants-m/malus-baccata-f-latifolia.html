Malus baccata f. latifolia

Malus baccata f. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf crabapple, is a cultivar of the crabapple tree characterized by its wide, ovate leaves.

It is native to China and is valued for its ornamental qualities, including its showy pink or white flowers and small, edible fruits. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its foliage provides dense shade, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, thriving in temperate climates.
