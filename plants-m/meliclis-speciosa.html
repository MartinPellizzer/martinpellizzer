Meliclis speciosa

Meliclis speciosa, commonly known as the showy meliclis, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that feature clusters of tubular, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments within forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Meliclis speciosa is valued in horticulture for its unique floral display and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
