Machilus thunbergii var. condorensis

Machilus thunbergii var. condorensis, commonly known as the Condor tree, is a variant of the Machilus genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by elliptical, reddish-brown berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic wood.
