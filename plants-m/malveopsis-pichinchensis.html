Malveopsis pichinchensis

Malveopsis pichinchensis, commonly known as the Pichincha mallows, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as cloud forests.

Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves with a soft, hairy texture.
