Micropyrum albaredae

Micropyrum albaredae, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada lily, is a rare and endemic plant species found exclusively in the Sierra Nevada mountain range of California.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to grow in high-altitude, rocky habitats. Its botanical significance lies in its unique genetic makeup and its role in the local ecosystem.

Conservation efforts are ongoing to protect this endangered species from habitat loss and climate change.
