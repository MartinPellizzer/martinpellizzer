Monarda dispersa

Monarda dispersa, commonly known as wild bergamot, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, lavender-colored flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist woodlands and prairies, reaching heights of 1 to 3 feet. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are square in cross-section.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
