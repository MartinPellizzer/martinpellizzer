Molinia squarrosa

Molinia squarrosa, commonly known as the hairy moor grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Eurasia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is typically found in heathlands and wet meadows.

Molinia squarrosa plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
