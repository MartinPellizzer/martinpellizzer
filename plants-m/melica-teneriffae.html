Melica teneriffae

Melica teneriffae, commonly known as the Canary Islands grass, is a species of grass native to the Canary Islands.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the mild, Mediterranean climate of its native habitat.

Melica teneriffae is often found in open, sunny areas such as coastal slopes and rocky outcrops.
