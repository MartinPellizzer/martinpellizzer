Mentzelia tridentata

Mentzelia tridentata, commonly known as the three-toothed gayfeather, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with three distinct teeth. The plant produces tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of violet to blue-purple tubular flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.
