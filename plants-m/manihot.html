Manihot

Manihot, commonly known as cassava, is a tropical plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to South America but is now widely cultivated in Africa, Asia, and the Caribbean. The plant produces a large, woody root that is the primary part used for food. The roots are rich in carbohydrates and are often processed into flour or starch.

It is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it an important crop in many developing countries.
