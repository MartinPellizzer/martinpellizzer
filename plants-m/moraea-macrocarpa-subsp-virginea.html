Moraea macrocarpa subsp. virginea

Moraea macrocarpa subsp.

virginea, commonly known as the Natal lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features strap-shaped leaves that are typically grass-like in appearance, and it produces striking, showy inflorescences with clustered, tubular flowers.

The flowers are usually pink to purple in color and bloom in late summer to early autumn. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable climates.
