Microstylis ventricosa

Microstylis ventricosa, commonly known as the globular sedge, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a smooth surface.

Microstylis ventricosa is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
