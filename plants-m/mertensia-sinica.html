Mertensia sinica

Mertensia sinica, commonly known as Chinese bluebells, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to violet in color, blooming in early spring. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Mertensia sinica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
