Mariscus restioides var. effusus

Mariscus restioides var. effusus, commonly known as the velvetleaf tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a woody stem with a rough, bark-like texture, and its leaves are typically narrow and opposite. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or rocky habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate saline conditions.
