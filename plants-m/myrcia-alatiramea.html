Myrcia alatiramea

Myrcia alatiramea, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries. Myrcia alatiramea is often found in moist, shaded environments within dense forest understories.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
