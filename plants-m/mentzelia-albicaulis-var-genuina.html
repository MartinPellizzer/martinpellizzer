Mentzelia albicaulis var. genuina

Mentzelia albicaulis var. genuina, commonly known as the whitestem blazingstar, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its pale green to white stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert and grassland habitats.

This variety is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
