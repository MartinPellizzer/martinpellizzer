Mazus pumilus

Mazus pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf mazus, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, succulent leaves that are typically gray-green in color, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is often used in ornamental horticulture.
