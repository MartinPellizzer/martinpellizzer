Matricaria armeniaca

Matricaria armeniaca, commonly known as Armenian chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with white ray florets. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soils. Its leaves are pinnately lobed and have a mild, aromatic scent.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
