Monardella macrantha var. hallii

Monardella macrantha var. hallii, commonly known as Hall's mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features aromatic, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a serrated edge. The plant produces showy, tubular blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used by Indigenous peoples for various purposes.
