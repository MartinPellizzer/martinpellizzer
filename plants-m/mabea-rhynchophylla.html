Mabea rhynchophylla

Mabea rhynchophylla, commonly known as the yellow mangrove, is a tropical plant native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that thrives in saline and brackish water environments. The plant has smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a waxy surface. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a capsule containing numerous seeds.

Mabea rhynchophylla plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for marine and terrestrial organisms.
