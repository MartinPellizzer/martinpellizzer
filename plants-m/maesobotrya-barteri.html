Maesobotrya barteri

Maesobotrya barteri, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a tropical tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are typically alternate and have a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in panicles, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. Its bark is thick and often fissured, with a reddish-brown color.

The tree is valued for its durable, heavy wood, which is used in furniture and construction.
