Macrostelia calyculata

Macrostelia calyculata, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. It has a tuberous root system and heart-shaped leaves.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
