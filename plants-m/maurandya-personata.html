Maurandya personata

Maurandya personata, commonly known as the common maurandya, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple. The plant has opposite, hairy, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
