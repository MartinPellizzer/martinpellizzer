Murucuia perfoliata

Murucuia perfoliata, commonly known as the leafy broom.

This perennial plant is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its distinctive foliage. It features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
