Macroclinium hirtzii

Macroclinium hirtzii, commonly known as the hairy macroclinium, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its leaves are broad and often covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers, which bloom in clusters, are usually a mix of white and yellow, attracting various pollinators.
