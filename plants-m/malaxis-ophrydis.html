Malaxis ophrydis

Malaxis ophrydis, commonly known as the orchid with the "lips" or "false lips," is a terrestrial orchid belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Thailand, and Indonesia. The plant features a slender, wiry stem with a single, lance-shaped leaf and a showy, greenish-yellow inflorescence. Its flowers are characterized by a distinctive lip that resembles a small, fleshy lip, giving it its common name.

Malaxis ophrydis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
