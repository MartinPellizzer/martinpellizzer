Mesembryanthemum antemeridianum

Mesembryanthemum antemeridianum, commonly known as the morning glory of the succulent family, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the early morning. The plant thrives in arid environments and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its flowers typically range in color from pink to purple and are pollinated by insects.

The species is also known for its ability to survive in harsh conditions due to its thick, waxy cuticle.
