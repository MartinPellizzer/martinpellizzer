Myrcia bathisiifolia

Myrcia bathisiifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features glossy, elliptical leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. Its small, fragrant white flowers are followed by edible, red, fleshy berries that attract various wildlife.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
