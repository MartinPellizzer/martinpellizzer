Musa tuberculata

Musa tuberculata, commonly known as the mountain banana, is a species of flowering plant in the Musaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Nepal, and Bhutan. The plant is characterized by its large, broad leaves and produces clusters of yellow or orange fruits that are edible. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and small-scale farms.
