Munroa squarrosa

Munroa squarrosa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed dandelion, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, erect stems with lance-shaped leaves that have a rough, hairy texture. Its bright yellow flowers appear in spring and are followed by elongated, bristly seed heads.

Munroa squarrosa is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.
