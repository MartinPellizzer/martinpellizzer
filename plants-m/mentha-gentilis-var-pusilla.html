Mentha gentilis var. pusilla

Mentha gentilis var. pusilla, commonly known as dwarf mint, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features aromatic, opposite leaves that are typically rounded or ovate, with a dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or shaded borders.

Its compact size and fragrant foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental and culinary purposes.
