Mimosa goldmanii

Mimosa goldmanii, commonly known as the golden mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Mimosa goldmanii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
