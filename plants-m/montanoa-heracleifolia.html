Montanoa heracleifolia

Montanoa heracleifolia, commonly known as the Mexican milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, smooth seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Montanoa heracleifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
