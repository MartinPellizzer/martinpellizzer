Marcetia taxifolia var. decumbens

Marcetia taxifolia var. decumbens, commonly known as the creeping fern, is a variant of the fern species Marcetia taxifolia.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with fronds that lie flat along the ground. The plant features lanceolate, serrated leaves that are typically bright green and covered in a fine, velvety texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forest floors.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to form dense, ground-covering mats.
