Miconia angustidentata

Miconia angustidentata, commonly known as the narrow-toothed miconia, is a member of the Melastomataceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins. It produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that are typically followed by bright red or orange berries.

The species is notable for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain areas.
