Myrtus vahlii

Myrtus vahlii, commonly known as the Arabian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves with a fragrant, resinous scent when crushed. Its small white flowers are followed by dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Myrtus vahlii thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky slopes and coastal areas.
