Myriandra ledifolia

Myriandra ledifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf myriandra, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils in arid or semi-arid regions.

Myriandra ledifolia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
