Myristica venosa

Myristica venosa, commonly known as the nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, woody stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange-red fruits containing aromatic seeds. The seeds, known as nutmegs, are enclosed in a reddish outer layer called mace.

Myristica venosa is valued for its essential oils and is widely cultivated for its economic and medicinal properties.
