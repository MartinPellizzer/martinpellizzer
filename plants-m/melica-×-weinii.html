Melica × weinii

Melica × weinii, commonly known as the hybrid meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species that belongs to the Poaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Melica nuda and Melica uniflora, characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to blue-green flowers, which are often clustered in dense panicles.

Melica × weinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators and soil stabilization.
