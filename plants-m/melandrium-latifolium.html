Melandrium latifolium

Melandrium latifolium, commonly known as the common catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has alternate, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
