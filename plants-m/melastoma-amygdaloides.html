Melastoma amygdaloides

Melastoma amygdaloides, commonly known as the purple false heather, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Melastoma amygdaloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
