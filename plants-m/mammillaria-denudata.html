Mammillaria denudata

Mammillaria denudata, commonly known as the naked mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem that is often covered with spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus collections.
