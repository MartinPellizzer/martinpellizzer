Moquiniella rubra

Moquiniella rubra, commonly known as the red moquiniella, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant produces small, showy pink to red flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Moquiniella rubra thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
