Musa sabuana

Musa sabuana, commonly known as the African banana, is a species of banana plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and produces clusters of elongated, edible fruits. The plant is typically grown in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its fruit is often used in local cuisines and has a flavor similar to plantains.

Musa sabuana plays an important role in both agricultural and ecological systems in its native habitat.
