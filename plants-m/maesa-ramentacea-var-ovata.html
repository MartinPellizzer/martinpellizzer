Maesa ramentacea var. ovata

Maesa ramentacea var. ovata, commonly known as the woolly maesa, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs.

It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries, which are an important food source for various wildlife.
