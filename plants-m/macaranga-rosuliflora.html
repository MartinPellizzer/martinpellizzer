Macaranga rosuliflora

Macaranga rosuliflora, commonly known as the rosette-leaf macaranga, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive rosette arrangement of leaves, which are typically broad and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Macaranga rosuliflora thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
