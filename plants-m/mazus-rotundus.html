Mazus rotundus

Mazus rotundus, commonly known as roundleaf mazus, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features succulent, rounded leaves that are typically bright green and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Mazus rotundus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
