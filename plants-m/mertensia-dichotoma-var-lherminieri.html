Mertensia dichotoma var. lherminieri

Mertensia dichotoma var. lherminieri, commonly known as the Lherminier's bluebells, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces striking blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained, loamy soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
