Mentha × villosa var. similis

Mentha × villosa var. similis, commonly known as woolly mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, pale purple to blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Mentha × villosa var. similis is often used in herbal teas and has mild mint-like aromatics.
