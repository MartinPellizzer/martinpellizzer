Myrceugenia myrtoides var. stricta

Myrceugenia myrtoides var. stricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a species native to southern Brazil.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, reddish-brown fruits that are edible for certain wildlife but not typically consumed by humans. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in Atlantic Forest regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
