Mentha bianoris

Mentha bianoris, commonly known as the white mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Mentha bianoris is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for its aromatic and potentially therapeutic properties.

Its cultivation requires well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
