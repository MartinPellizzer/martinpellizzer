Microloma spinosum subsp. dinteri

Microloma spinosum subsp.

dinteri, commonly known as the Dinter's spiny lily, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its spiny stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture.

The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
