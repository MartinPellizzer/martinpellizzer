Maxillaria disciflora

Maxillaria disciflora, commonly known as the disc-flowered orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a compact, pseudobulbous structure and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink with a distinctive yellow or orange disc in the center. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Maxillaria disciflora is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
