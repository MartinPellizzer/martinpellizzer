Mancoa perennis

Mancoa perennis, commonly known as the everlasting or perpetual grass, is a perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to subtropical climates.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitats.
