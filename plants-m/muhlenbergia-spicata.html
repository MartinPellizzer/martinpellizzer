Muhlenbergia spicata

Muhlenbergia spicata, commonly known as silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and fluffy, silver-tipped panicles that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens and restoration projects. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds ornamental value.

Muhlenbergia spicata is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
