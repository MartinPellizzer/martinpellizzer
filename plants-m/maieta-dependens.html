Maieta dependens

Maieta dependens, commonly known as the hanging bellflower, is a member of the Campanulaceae family.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that hang from the stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are usually blue or violet in color, with five petals and a prominent corolla.
