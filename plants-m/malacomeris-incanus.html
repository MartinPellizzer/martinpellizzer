Malacomeris incanus

Malacomeris incanus, commonly known as the slender leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, narrow leaves and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale purple. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing in the undergrowth of tropical forests. Its roots are specialized for absorbing nutrients from the forest floor, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for successful growth.

Due to habitat loss, Malacomeris incanus is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
