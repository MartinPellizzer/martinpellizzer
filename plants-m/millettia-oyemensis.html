Millettia oyemensis

Millettia oyemensis, commonly known as the Oyemensi Millettia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in racemes, and can vary in color from white to pink.

Millettia oyemensis is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and its potential use in traditional medicine.
