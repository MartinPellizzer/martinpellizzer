Macaranga mellifera

Macaranga mellifera, commonly known as the honey leaf tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a fast-growing shrub or small tree that typically reaches heights of up to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by elliptical, woody fruits.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed habitats.
