Mikania fulva

Mikania fulva, commonly known as the yellow cocklebur, is a climbing vine native to the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green color and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. Its fruits are spiny capsules that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Mikania fulva is often considered an invasive species in certain regions due to its aggressive growth habit.
