Muscari coeleste

Muscari coeleste, commonly known as the blue musk grape, is a bulbous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to deep purple. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that emerge in early spring.

Muscari coeleste is relatively low maintenance and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
