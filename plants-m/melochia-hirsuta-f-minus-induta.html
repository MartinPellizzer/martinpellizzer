Melochia hirsuta f. minus-induta

Melochia hirsuta f. minus-induta, commonly known as the lesser hairy melochia, is a variant of the hairy melochia, characterized by its reduced indumentum or hairy covering compared to the typical form.

It is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia, typically growing in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant features palmate leaves with three to five lobes, and its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in inflorescences. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds.

The species is noted for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
