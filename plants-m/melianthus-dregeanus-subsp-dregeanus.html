Melianthus dregeanus subsp. dregeanus

Melianthus dregeanus subsp.

dregeanus, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its showy, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects.

The plant has thick, succulent stems and dark green, ovate leaves with a waxy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, wind-dispersed seeds.
