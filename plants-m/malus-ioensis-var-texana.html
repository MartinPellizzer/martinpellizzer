Malus ioensis var. texana

Malus ioensis var. texana, commonly known as the Texas crabapple, is a small deciduous tree native to the south-central United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are often used in jams and jellies. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth margin and a slightly serrated edge.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.
