Melaleuca sciotostyla

Melaleuca sciotostyla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

Melaleuca sciotostyla is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate salty air, making it suitable for coastal gardens.
