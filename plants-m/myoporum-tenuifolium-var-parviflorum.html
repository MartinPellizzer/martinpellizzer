Myoporum tenuifolium var. parviflorum

Myoporum tenuifolium var. parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered myoporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white to greyish hair. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in spring and summer. It is adapted to coastal and sandy soils, showing some tolerance to salt spray.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
