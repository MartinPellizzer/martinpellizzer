Mentha multifida

Mentha multifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves that are typically narrow and pointed, often with a rough texture. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Mentha multifida is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in herbal remedies for its cooling and digestive effects.
