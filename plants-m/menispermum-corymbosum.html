Menispermum corymbosum

Menispermum corymbosum, commonly known as Indian snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the Menispermaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, palmately lobed leaf structure and has a climbing habit, often found in moist woodlands. Its fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that contains several seeds.

The plant has been historically used in traditional medicine, though its toxicity requires caution in handling.
