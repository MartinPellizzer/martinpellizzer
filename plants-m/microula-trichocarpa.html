Microula trichocarpa

Microula trichocarpa, commonly known as the hairy microula, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of China, Mongolia, and the Tibetan Plateau. The plant is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Microula trichocarpa is often used in traditional medicine and has potential ecological significance in its native habitats.
