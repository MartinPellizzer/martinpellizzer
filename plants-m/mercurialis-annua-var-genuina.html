Mercurialis annua var. genuina

Mercurialis annua var. genuina, commonly known as the common mercury plant, is a variant of the annual mercury plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae, characterized by its erect, branching stems and alternate leaves that are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are surrounded by modified leaves called cyathia, a feature typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in roadsides, fields, and waste areas.

The species is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal medicine.
