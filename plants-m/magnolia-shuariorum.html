Magnolia shuariorum

Magnolia shuariorum, commonly known as the Shuariorum magnolia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow petals that surround a cluster of yellow stamens. Its leaves are broad and glossy, providing a striking contrast to the floral display.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in warm climates.
