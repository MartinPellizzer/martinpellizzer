Malaxis panamensis

Malaxis panamensis, commonly known as the Panamanian malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom during the dry season. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Malaxis panamensis is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is notable for its unique floral structure and ecological adaptations.
