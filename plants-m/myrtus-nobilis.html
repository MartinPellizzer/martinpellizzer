Myrtus nobilis

Myrtus nobilis, commonly known as sweet myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Myrtus nobilis thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery and traditional medicine.
