Mitracarpus puberulus

Mitracarpus puberulus, commonly known as the hairy mitracarpus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous traits and often compound leaves. The plant features small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are palmately compound, with several leaflets arranged radiating from a central point.

Mitracarpus puberulus is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
