Mimosa spegazzinii

Mimosa spegazzinii, commonly known as the velvet mimosa, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves that give it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellow, ball-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Mimosa spegazzinii is also known for its sensitivity to touch, as its leaves fold shut when disturbed.
