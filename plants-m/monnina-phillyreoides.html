Monnina phillyreoides

Monnina phillyreoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf monnina, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are showy, with five pink to purple petals and a distinctive yellow center, blooming from late spring to early summer.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
