Meconopsis pinnatifolia

Meconopsis pinnatifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved monk's hood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple spurred flowers, which are typically arranged in a raceme. The plant has pinnately divided leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Meconopsis pinnatifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens with cool, humid conditions.
