Malus angustifolia

Malus angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved apple or Chinese white pear, is a species of apple tree native to China.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is typically a small, yellow or green apple with a tart flavor. This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for temperate climates.
