Myrtus communis subsp. lusitanica

Myrtus communis subsp.

lusitanica, commonly known as Portuguese myrtle, is a shrub native to southern Europe and North Africa. It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture.

The plant produces small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded locations.

This subspecies is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its fragrance and hardy nature.
