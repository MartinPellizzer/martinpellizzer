Masdevallia erythrochaete var. gaskelliana

Masdevallia erythrochaete var. gaskelliana, commonly known as the Gaskell's red-tipped masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated flowers that often display a deep red hue at the base, contrasting with its green or purple sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, strap-like leaves that are well adapted to absorbing moisture from the air.

This variety is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
