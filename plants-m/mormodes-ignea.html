Mormodes ignea

Mormodes ignea, commonly known as the flame orchid, is a striking tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange and red flowers, which resemble flames, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are known for their long-lasting color, making it a popular choice for ornamental purposes.

The botanical features of Mormodes ignea include its unique floral structure and adaptability to tropical climates.
