Maranta inscripta

Maranta inscripta, commonly known as the prayer plant, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, ornate foliage that changes color and orientation in response to light and temperature, a phenomenon known as nyctinasty. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with intricate patterns of silver and green, making it a popular ornamental plant. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires indirect sunlight to maintain its vibrant appearance.

Maranta inscripta is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and is relatively easy to care for in indoor environments.
