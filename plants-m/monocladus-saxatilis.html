Monocladus saxatilis

Monocladus saxatilis, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in cyathia, a unique floral structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Monocladus saxatilis is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient plant in harsh landscapes.
