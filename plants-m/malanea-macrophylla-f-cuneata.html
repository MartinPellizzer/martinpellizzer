Malanea macrophylla f. cuneata

Malanea macrophylla f. cuneata, commonly known as the broad-leafed malanea, is a variant of the Malanea macrophylla species, characterized by its distinctive cuneate (wedge-shaped) leaves.

This plant is native to specific regions in South America, particularly in Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical environments. The leaves are large, broad, and often have a leathery texture, which helps the plant conserve moisture in its native habitat. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, adding ornamental value to its appearance.

Due to its unique foliage and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
