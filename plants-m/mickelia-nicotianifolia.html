Mickelia nicotianifolia

Mickelia nicotianifolia, commonly known as the tobacco leaf plant, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that resemble those of tobacco plants, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an annual or short-lived perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are smooth and glossy, with a slightly waxy texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Mickelia nicotianifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage.
