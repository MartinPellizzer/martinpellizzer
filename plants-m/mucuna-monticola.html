Mucuna monticola

Mucuna monticola, commonly known as mountain mucuna, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, curved pods that contain numerous seeds. The plant produces a significant amount of nitrogen through its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria in its root nodules. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to orange, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Mucuna monticola is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop due to its rapid growth and nutritional value.
