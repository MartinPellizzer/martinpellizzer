Mertensia nervosa

Mertensia nervosa, commonly known as Virginia bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from blue to purple, blooming in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

Mertensia nervosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
