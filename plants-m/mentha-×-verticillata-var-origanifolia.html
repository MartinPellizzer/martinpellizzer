Mentha × verticillata var. origanifolia

Mentha × verticillata var. origanifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf water mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, typically from July to August. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams, ponds, and wet meadows.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
