Manihot saxicola

Manihot saxicola, commonly known as the rock manioc, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in cyathia, typical of the spurge family. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their potential anti-inflammatory properties.

Manihot saxicola thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.
