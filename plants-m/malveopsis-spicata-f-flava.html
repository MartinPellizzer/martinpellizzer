Malveopsis spicata f. flava

Malveopsis spicata f. flava, commonly known as the yellow-flowered mallow, is a variant of the species Malveopsis spicata, which belongs to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which contrast with the typical pink or red hues of the species. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is broad and heart-shaped, with a velvety texture.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
