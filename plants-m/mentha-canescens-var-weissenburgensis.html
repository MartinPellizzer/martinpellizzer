Mentha canescens var. weissenburgensis

Mentha canescens var. weissenburgensis, commonly known as the Weissenburg mint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces tiny, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in loose spikes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
