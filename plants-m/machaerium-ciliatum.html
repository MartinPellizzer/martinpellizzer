Machaerium ciliatum

Machaerium ciliatum, commonly known as the silky machaerium, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features slender, often hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with finely toothed margins. Its flowers are typically small and clustered in panicles, often with a pale to yellowish coloration.

Machaerium ciliatum is adapted to well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.
