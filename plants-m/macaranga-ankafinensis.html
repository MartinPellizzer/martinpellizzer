Macaranga ankafinensis

Macaranga ankafinensis, commonly known as the Madagascar paperleaf tree, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
