Mentha requienii var. pilosula

Mentha requienii var. pilosula, commonly known as the silky mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves that give it a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for garden cultivation.

Due to its mild mint flavor, it is often used in culinary and herbal applications.
