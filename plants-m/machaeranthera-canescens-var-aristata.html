Machaeranthera canescens var. aristata

Machaeranthera canescens var. aristata, commonly known as the aristate blazingstar, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy foliage and tall, upright stems that bear clusters of yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, oval fruits called follicles, which contain numerous seeds. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance to pollinators.
