Mascarenhasia arborescens

Mascarenhasia arborescens, commonly known as the Mascarene tree, is a flowering plant native to the Mascarene Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
