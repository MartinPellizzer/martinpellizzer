Machaerina iridifolia

Machaerina iridifolia, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. The plant has woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Machaerina iridifolia is an important species in Australian ecosystems and is also cultivated for its ornamental value.
