Melampyrum × ambiguum

Melampyrum × ambiguum, commonly known as the hybrid bladder campion, is a cross between Melampyrum lathyroides and Melampyrum nodosum.

It is a herbaceous annual plant that typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. The plant features distinctive red-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, often in clusters at the ends of its stems. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and slightly hairy, with a lanceolate shape.

The hybrid is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting pollinators.
