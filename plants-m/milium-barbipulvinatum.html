Milium barbipulvinatum

Milium barbipulvinatum, commonly known as the woolly millet, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces tiny, white to pale green flowers that are clustered in spikelets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or disturbed areas.

Milium barbipulvinatum is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
