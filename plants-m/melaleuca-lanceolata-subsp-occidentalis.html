Melaleuca lanceolata subsp. occidentalis

Melaleuca lanceolata subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the Western bottlebrush, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and distinctive bottlebrush-like flowers that are typically red or pink in color.

The plant produces small, woody fruits called follicles, which contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
