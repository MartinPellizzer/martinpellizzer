Mussaenda citrifolia

Mussaenda citrifolia, commonly known as the golden trumpet bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble trumpets, which are clustered at the ends of its branches. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy underside. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Mussaenda citrifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
