Mariscus schaffnerianus

Mariscus schaffnerianus, commonly known as the Schaffner's rock cress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces small, elongated seed pods.

Mariscus schaffnerianus is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in rock gardens.
