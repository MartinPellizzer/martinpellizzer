Mouriri purpurascens

Mouriri purpurascens, commonly known as the purple mouriri, is a species of flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is a small tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. Its flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, and are usually white or pale pink in color.

The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed, and it plays an important role in the local ecosystem by providing food for various wildlife species.
