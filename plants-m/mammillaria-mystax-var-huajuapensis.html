Mammillaria mystax var. huajuapensis

Mammillaria mystax var. huajuapensis, commonly known as the "Cactus of the Gods," is a species of cactus native to the Sierra Madre mountains in Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is a slow-growing species that requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated as a decorative cactus in collections.
