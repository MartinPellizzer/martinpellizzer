Mollinedia tomentosa

Mollinedia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly mollinedia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape and covered in dense, white tomentum, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Mollinedia tomentosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
