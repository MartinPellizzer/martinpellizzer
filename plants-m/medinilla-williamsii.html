Medinilla williamsii

Medinilla williamsii, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a tropical shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has glossy, dark green leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Medinilla williamsii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is popular in horticultural gardens.
