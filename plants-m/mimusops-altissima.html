Mimusops altissima

Mimusops altissima, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and broad, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and fine grain, making it important in local economies.

The plant also plays a role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
