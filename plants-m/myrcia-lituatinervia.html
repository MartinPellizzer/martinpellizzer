Myrcia lituatinervia

Myrcia lituatinervia, commonly known as the pink myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. Its flowers are typically small, pink or white, and are clustered in dense inflorescences.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
