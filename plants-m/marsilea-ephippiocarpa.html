Marsilea ephippiocarpa

Marsilea ephippiocarpa, commonly known as the water clover, is a small aquatic fern that thrives in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its distinctive whorled arrangement of four to six leaves, which resemble the shape of a clover. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into green, spherical fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in still or slow-moving water, such as ponds and ditches.

Marsilea ephippiocarpa plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various organisms.
