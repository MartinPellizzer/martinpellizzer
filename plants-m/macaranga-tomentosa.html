Macaranga tomentosa

Macaranga tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly macaranga, is a flowering plant species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in tropical rainforests. The plant is characterized by its hairy, tomentose leaves, which give it its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Macaranga tomentosa plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
