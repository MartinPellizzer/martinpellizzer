Monardella elegantula

Monardella elegantula, commonly known as pinkroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic when crushed, contributing to its use in herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands.

Monardella elegantula is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
