Memecylon candolleanum

Memecylon candolleanum, commonly known as the white memecylon, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

The species is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and thickets.
