Maxillaria synsepala

Maxillaria synsepala, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink and white flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns resembling slipper-like structures. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, often in shaded, humid environments. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Maxillaria synsepala is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in many botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
