Monardella australis subsp. jokerstii

Monardella australis subsp.

jokerstii, commonly known as Jokerst's mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and showy, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils in mountainous regions. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
