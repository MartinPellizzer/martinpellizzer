Malva digitata

Malva digitata, commonly known as the five-finger mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves that resemble fingers, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Malva digitata is a perennial shrub that can grow up to 1 meter tall, with a distinctive branching pattern.
