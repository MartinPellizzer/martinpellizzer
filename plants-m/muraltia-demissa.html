Muraltia demissa

Muraltia demissa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or sweet acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets, and it produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is notable for its edible, sweet-tart red berries that ripen in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and ecological landscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically valuable in native habitats.
