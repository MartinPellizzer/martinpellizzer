Malaxis labrosa

Malaxis labrosa, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green, strap-like leaves and its striking, pinkish-white inflorescence that resembles a tresses of hair. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are arranged in a spiral pattern along a single, erect stem, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Malaxis labrosa is a perennial species, often forming colonies through vegetative propagation.
