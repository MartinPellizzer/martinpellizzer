Myristica griffithii

Myristica griffithii, commonly known as the Indian nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant produces distinctive, red, fleshy fruits that enclose a single seed, which is used for its aromatic properties. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Myristica griffithii is valued for its seeds, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
