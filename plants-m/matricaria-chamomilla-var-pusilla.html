Matricaria chamomilla var. pusilla

Matricaria chamomilla var. pusilla, commonly known as dwarf chamomile, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow central disc, typical of the Asteraceae family. The plant produces tiny, aromatic flowers that bloom in late summer, releasing a subtle, calming scent. Its leaves are finely divided and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and use in herbal teas.
