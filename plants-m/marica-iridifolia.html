Marica iridifolia

Marica iridifolia, commonly known as the bluebell of the Andes, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in alpine meadows.

Marica iridifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
