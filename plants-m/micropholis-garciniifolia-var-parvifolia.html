Micropholis garciniifolia var. parvifolia

Micropholis garciniifolia var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved white cedar, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
