Melanthium luridum

Melanthium luridum, commonly known as the yellow false nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf structures and often edible seeds. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and is noted for its ability to thrive in rich, loamy soils.
