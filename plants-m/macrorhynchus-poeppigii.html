Macrorhynchus poeppigii

Macrorhynchus poeppigii, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in high-altitude areas, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
