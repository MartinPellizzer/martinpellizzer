Mirabilis nyctaginea var. pilosa

Mirabilis nyctaginea var. pilosa, commonly known as the woolly four o'clock, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, soft texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming pattern.
