Mansoa verrucifera

Mansoa verrucifera, commonly known as the roughleaf mansoa, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
