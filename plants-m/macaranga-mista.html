Macaranga mista

Macaranga mista, commonly known as the white-veined macaranga, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent white veins. The plant is often found in lowland rainforests and can grow up to 20 meters tall. Its bark is smooth and grayish, while its leaves are usually alternate and have a leathery texture.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds.
