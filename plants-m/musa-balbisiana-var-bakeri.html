Musa balbisiana var. bakeri

Musa balbisiana var. bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's banana, is a variant of the banana plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach lengths of up to three meters. The plant produces clusters of large, elongated berries that are typically yellow when ripe. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and fruit.

This variety is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
