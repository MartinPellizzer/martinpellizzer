Moraea violacea

Moraea violacea, commonly known as the purple moraea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Moraea violacea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
