Mertensia rhamnoides

Mertensia rhamnoides, commonly known as the Carolina bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy underneath. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Mertensia rhamnoides is valued for its ornamental value and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
