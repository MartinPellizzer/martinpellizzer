Melaleuca obliqua

Melaleuca obliqua, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paperbark, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals layers of pale, fibrous tissue. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers in spikes during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often covered with a fine layer of white wax.

Melaleuca obliqua is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and wetland areas.
