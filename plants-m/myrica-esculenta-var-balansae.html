Myrica esculenta var. balansae

Myrica esculenta var. balansae, commonly known as the Balansae wax myrtle, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Malaysia and Indonesia.

It is characterized by its small, waxy leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by dark blue to black berries that are edible. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its use in traditional medicine.
