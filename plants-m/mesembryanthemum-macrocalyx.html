Mesembryanthemum macrocalyx

Mesembryanthemum macrocalyx, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves and showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates. It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, known for its adaptations to dry environments.

The flowers are often used in ornamental gardening due to their striking appearance and resilience.
