Montia erythrophylla

Montia erythrophylla, commonly known as the red-fruited mallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Montiaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and showy, pinkish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions. Its fruits are small, red capsules that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Montia erythrophylla is a short-lived perennial that plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
