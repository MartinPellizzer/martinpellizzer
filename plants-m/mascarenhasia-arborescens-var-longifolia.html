Mascarenhasia arborescens var. longifolia

Mascarenhasia arborescens var. longifolia, commonly known as the Mascarene tree, is a tropical plant native to the Mascarene Islands.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The flowers are typically white or pale pink and are clustered in terminal panicles, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or limestone areas.

It has ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
