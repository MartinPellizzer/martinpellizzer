Masdevallia gnoma

Masdevallia gnoma, commonly known as the dwarf masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with a short, hairy rhizome and a single, narrow leaf. The plant produces a few showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and lavender, typically during the spring and summer months. Its flowers are characterized by their intricate, spurred petals and a distinctive, narrow lip.

Masdevallia gnoma prefers cool, humid environments and is often found growing in shaded, forested areas.
