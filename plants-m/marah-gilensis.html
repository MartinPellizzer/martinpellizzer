Marah gilensis

Marah gilensis, commonly known as the Gilan bitter melon, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia, including regions of Iran and Turkey. The plant produces elongated, bitter fruits that are typically harvested when they are still green. Its leaves are lobed and can be used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in warm, arid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
