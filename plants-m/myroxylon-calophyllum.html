Myroxylon calophyllum

Myroxylon calophyllum, commonly known as the Brazil wood, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its dense, dark red heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and rich color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and capsule fruits that contain flat, winged seeds. Its leaves are alternate and pinnate, with small, elliptical leaflets.

Myroxylon calophyllum plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
