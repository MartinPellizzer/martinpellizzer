Micromyrtus sessilis

Micromyrtus sessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, dark green, narrow leaves, and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for coastal environments.

Its hardy nature and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for landscaping in arid and semi-arid regions.
