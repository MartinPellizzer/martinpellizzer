Melocactus rotula var. validispinus

Melocactus rotula var. validispinus, commonly known as the red-tipped melocactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, ribbed stems that are typically green with red or purple tips. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and collections.
