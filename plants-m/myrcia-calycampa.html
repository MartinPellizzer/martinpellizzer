Myrcia calycampa

Myrcia calycampa, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly found in countries like Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, dark seeds.

Myrcia calycampa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in folk medicine.
