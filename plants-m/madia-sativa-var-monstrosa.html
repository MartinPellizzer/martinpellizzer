Madia sativa var. monstrosa

Madia sativa var. monstrosa, commonly known as the giant madia, is a striking perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can reach heights of up to 10 feet, making it a dramatic addition to gardens. The plant produces large, showy white to pink flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Due to its size and visual impact, it is often cultivated in spacious gardens or as a landscape specimen.
