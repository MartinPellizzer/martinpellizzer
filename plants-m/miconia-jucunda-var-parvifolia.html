Miconia jucunda var. parvifolia

Miconia jucunda var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth surface and a glossy appearance. The plant produces small, round fruits that are often covered in soft, hairy structures.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
