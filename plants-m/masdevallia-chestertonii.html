Masdevallia chestertonii

Masdevallia chestertonii, commonly known as the Chesterton's masdevallia, is a rare and striking orchid native to the Andean highlands of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, often velvety foliage and its showy, long-lasting blooms that come in a variety of hues, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments at high altitudes, often on rock faces or tree trunks. Its flowers are arranged in a spiral pattern, a unique feature among orchids, and it is prized by collectors for its beauty and rarity.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a challenging plant to cultivate outside its native range.
