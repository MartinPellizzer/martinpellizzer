Morella arborea

Morella arborea, commonly known as the wax myrtle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, which are followed by berries that turn from red to black as they mature. Morella arborea is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Its aromatic foliage and berries also make it an important plant in ecological systems, supporting various wildlife species.
