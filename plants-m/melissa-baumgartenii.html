Melissa baumgartenii

Melissa baumgartenii, commonly known as the dwarf lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters and features small, white to pale blue flowers. Its leaves are fragrant and have a mild lemony scent, making it popular in herbal teas and aromatherapy.

Melissa baumgartenii thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.
