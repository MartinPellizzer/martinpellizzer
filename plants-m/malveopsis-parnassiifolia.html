Malveopsis parnassiifolia

Malveopsis parnassiifolia, commonly known as the Parnassian mallow, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed, resembling those of the Parnassius butterfly, hence its common name.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.
