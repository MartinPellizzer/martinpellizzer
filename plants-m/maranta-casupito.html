Maranta casupito

Maranta casupito, commonly known as the velvetleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often colorful foliage. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often grown as an ornamental plant.

Maranta casupito is also valued for its starchy roots, which can be used in culinary applications.
