Myristica morindiifolia

Myristica morindiifolia, commonly known as the Maluku nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody fruits that contain the spice nutmeg. The plant produces small, white flowers and has dark green, leathery leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its economic importance as a source of nutmeg and mace.

The botanical features of this plant make it a significant species in both ecological and commercial contexts.
