Mankyua

Mankyua, commonly known as the "Chinese potato," is a tuberous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its starchy, edible underground stems. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, while its flowers are usually white or pale purple.

Mankyua is valued for its nutritional content and is used in various traditional dishes across its native regions.
