Monarda punctata

Monarda punctata, commonly known as the spotted beebalm, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, square stems and showy, tubular flowers. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and a distinctive red or purple spot on the underside. Its flowers, which bloom in shades of red, pink, or purple, attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Monarda punctata is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
