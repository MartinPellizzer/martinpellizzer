Mendoncia pubescens

Mendoncia pubescens, commonly known as the hairy mendoncia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soils.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
