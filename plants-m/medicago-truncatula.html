Medicago truncatula

Medicago truncatula, commonly known as crimson clover, is a leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and improves soil fertility through nitrogen fixation. It is widely used as a forage crop and in ecological restoration projects.

Its adaptability to various climates makes it a valuable species in agricultural and environmental contexts.
