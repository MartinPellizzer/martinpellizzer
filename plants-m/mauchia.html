Mauchia

Mauchia, commonly known as the silky glume grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The flowers are typically arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in panicles, giving the plant a delicate appearance. Mauchia species are native to various regions in Africa and are often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

These plants are generally herbaceous and have a short lifespan, completing their life cycle within a single growing season.
