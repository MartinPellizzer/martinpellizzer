Mertensia myosotifolia

Mertensia myosotifolia, commonly known as bluebells, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the northeastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Mertensia myosotifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
