Medinilla subcordata

Medinilla subcordata, commonly known as the velvet tangerine.

This evergreen shrub is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. It produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible and have a citrus-like flavor. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
