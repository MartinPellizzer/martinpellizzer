Mikania strobilifera

Mikania strobilifera, commonly known as the golden crown vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences, giving it its common name. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges and often have a hairy texture.

Mikania strobilifera is often found in shaded, humid environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
