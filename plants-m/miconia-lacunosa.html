Miconia lacunosa

Miconia lacunosa, commonly known as the lace-leaf miconia, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves with a distinctive lace-like pattern of veins and lobes. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red berries. Miconia lacunosa is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets make it an invasive species in some regions.
