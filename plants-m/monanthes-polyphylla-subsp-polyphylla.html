Monanthes polyphylla subsp. polyphylla

Monanthes polyphylla subsp.

polyphylla, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with purple.

The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of violet or blue, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
