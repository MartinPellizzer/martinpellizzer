Myosotis uliginosa

Myosotis uliginosa, commonly known as the bog myrtle, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs and swampy areas.

Myosotis uliginosa is also known for its aromatic leaves, which have been used historically in herbal remedies.
