Manihot epruinosa

Manihot epruinosa, commonly known as the powdered soursop or white soursop, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves and clusters of small, white, fleshy fruits that resemble those of the soursop. The plant is known for its high content of calcium oxalate crystals, which contribute to its medicinal properties. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value.

The fruit's unique texture and flavor make it a popular ingredient in traditional remedies and culinary uses.
