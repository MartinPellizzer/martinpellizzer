Myrcia vacciniifolia

Myrcia vacciniifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features elliptical, glossy leaves that are often dark green in color. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually white or pale pink and appear in clusters.

Myrcia vacciniifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
