Muscarella latilabris

Muscarella latilabris, commonly known as the broad-lipped muscari, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its clustered, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. Its leaves are strap-shaped and grass-like, forming a dense rosette at the base.

Muscarella latilabris is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
