Myrtus sessilis

Myrtus sessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves that are often covered in a white, waxy coating. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature.

Myrtus sessilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
