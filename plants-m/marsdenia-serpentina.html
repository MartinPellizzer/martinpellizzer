Marsdenia serpentina

Marsdenia serpentina, commonly known as the serpent vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its long, serpentine stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy green appearance.

Marsdenia serpentina is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments.
