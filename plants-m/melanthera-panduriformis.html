Melanthera panduriformis

Melanthera panduriformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and conical flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and swamps. Its flowers are usually purple to pink in color and bloom in late summer.

Melanthera panduriformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
