Macaranga quadriglandulosa

Macaranga quadriglandulosa, commonly known as the four-gland mallow, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its alternate, simple leaves with four glands on the petiole and leaf blade. The species is also noted for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
