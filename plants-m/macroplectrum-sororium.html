Macroplectrum sororium

Macroplectrum sororium, commonly known as the purple-leafed fern, is a striking tropical plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, feathery fronds that can grow up to three meters in length, with a distinctive purple hue on the underside of its leaves. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistently moist soil to flourish. Its reproductive structures are unique, featuring spore-bearing cones that are often hidden within the foliage.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
