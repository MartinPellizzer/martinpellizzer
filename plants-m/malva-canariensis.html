Malva canariensis

Malva canariensis, commonly known as the Canary mallows, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Malva canariensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
