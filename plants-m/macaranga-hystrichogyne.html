Macaranga hystrichogyne

Macaranga hystrichogyne, commonly known as the woolly macaranga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that release tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is notable for its role in supporting local biodiversity and its use in traditional medicine.
