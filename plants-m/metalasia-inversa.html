Metalasia inversa

Metalasia inversa, commonly known as the upside-down plant, is a unique species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive growth pattern, where the flower heads face downward. The plant has a compact, shrub-like structure with small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated by insects, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.

Metalasia inversa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unusual appearance.
