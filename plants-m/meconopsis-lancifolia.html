Meconopsis lancifolia

Meconopsis lancifolia, commonly known as the lancifoliate monkshood, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Meconopsis, which is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy and often toxic flowers. The plant features palmately lobed leaves and produces striking blue or purple spurred flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
