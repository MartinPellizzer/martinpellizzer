Mentha × verticillata var. diversifolia

Mentha × verticillata var. diversifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves that are often narrower than those of its parent species. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. Its flowers are small, tubular, and clustered in whorls, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often used in herbal medicine and as a decorative plant in water gardens.
