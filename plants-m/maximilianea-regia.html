Maximilianea regia

Maximilianea regia, commonly known as the royal maximilianea, is a striking flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant blossoms that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant flowers.

Maximilianea regia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to well-drained soils.
