Mangifera bompardii

Mangifera bompardii, commonly known as the Bompard mango, is a tropical fruit-bearing tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its large, elongated fruit with a thick, fibrous skin. The tree can grow up to 15 meters tall and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. Its fruit is typically harvested when fully mature, and it is valued for its sweet, aromatic pulp.

The plant is adapted to warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
