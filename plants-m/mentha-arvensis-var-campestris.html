Mentha arvensis var. campestris

Mentha arvensis var. campestris, commonly known as field mint, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist soils along roadsides, meadows, and wastelands. It has a creeping growth habit with rhizomes that allow it to spread rapidly.

Field mint is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in herbal remedies.
