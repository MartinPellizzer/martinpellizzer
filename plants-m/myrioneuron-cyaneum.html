Myrioneuron cyaneum

Myrioneuron cyaneum, commonly known as the bluebell of the genus Myrioneuron, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Myrioneuron cyaneum is valued in horticulture for its vibrant color and ability to thrive in humid, temperate climates.
