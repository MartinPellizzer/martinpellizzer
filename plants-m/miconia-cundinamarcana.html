Miconia cundinamarcana

Miconia cundinamarcana, commonly known as the Amazonian beautyberry, is a tropical shrub native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright purple berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of dense tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
