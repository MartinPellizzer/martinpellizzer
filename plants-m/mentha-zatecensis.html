Mentha zatecensis

Mentha zatecensis, commonly known as Zatec mint, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
