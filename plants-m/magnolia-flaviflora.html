Magnolia flaviflora

Magnolia flaviflora, commonly known as the yellow-flowered magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its large, showy, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves appear. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy on the upper surface. Its bark is smooth and grey, becoming fissured with age.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
