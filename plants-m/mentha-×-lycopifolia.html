Mentha × lycopifolia

Mentha × lycopifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Mentha hortensis and Mentha × piperita.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and strong, menthol-like aroma. This plant is typically found in moist, shady environments and is often used in herbal teas and aromatherapy. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Mentha × lycopifolia is also valued for its medicinal properties, including its ability to soothe digestive issues and reduce stress.
