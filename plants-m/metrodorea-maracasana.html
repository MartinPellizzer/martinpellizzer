Metrodorea maracasana

Metrodorea maracasana, commonly known as the Maracasana orchid, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually brightly colored, with intricate patterns that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticulturists.
