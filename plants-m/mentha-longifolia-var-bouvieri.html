Mentha longifolia var. bouvieri

Mentha longifolia var. bouvieri, commonly known as French mint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive silver-gray underside. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is prized for its aromatic leaves, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
