Melothrianthus smilacifolius

Melothrianthus smilacifolius, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in the morning. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it can become a problematic weed in certain ecosystems.
