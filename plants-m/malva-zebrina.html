Malva zebrina

Malva zebrina, commonly known as the zebra mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, velvety leaves with a striking zebra-like pattern of dark and light green. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Malva zebrina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
