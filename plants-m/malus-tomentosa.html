Malus tomentosa

Malus tomentosa, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish bark and broad, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by edible pears that are sweet and aromatic. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

Malus tomentosa is also valued in horticulture for its adaptability and resistance to certain diseases.
