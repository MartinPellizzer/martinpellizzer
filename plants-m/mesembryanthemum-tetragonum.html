Mesembryanthemum tetragonum

Mesembryanthemum tetragonum, commonly known as the four-cornered ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is widely cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
