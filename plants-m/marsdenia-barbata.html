Marsdenia barbata

Marsdenia barbata, commonly known as the hairy Marsdenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and often have a rough texture.

Marsdenia barbata is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
