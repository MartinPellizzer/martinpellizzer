Monoplegma sphaerospermum

Monoplegma sphaerospermum, commonly known as the globe cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a short, thick, cylindrical stem that is often covered in a layer of white, waxy powder, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. Its most distinctive characteristic is the spherical, segmented fruit that forms after flowering, which contains the plant's seeds.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is a rare and protected species in its native environment.
