Magnolia sieboldii

Magnolia sieboldii, commonly known as the Japanese magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its flowers are borne on short pedicels and have a distinctive, fragrant scent.

Magnolia sieboldii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
