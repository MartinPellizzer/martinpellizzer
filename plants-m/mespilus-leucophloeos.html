Mespilus leucophloeos

Mespilus leucophloeos, commonly known as the white-flanged medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, velvety, white-flanged fruit that ripens in the autumn. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while the flowers are white and appear in clusters. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
