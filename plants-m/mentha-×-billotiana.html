Mentha × billotiana

Mentha × billotiana, commonly known as African mint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Mentha piperita (peppermint) and Mentha arvensis (field mint).

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly minty scent and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring square stems and opposite, ovate leaves. Its flowers are small and tubular, usually purple or pink, and appear in whorls along the stem.

Mentha × billotiana is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb, though it is less commonly cultivated than other mint species.
