Morus serrata

Morus serrata, commonly known as the Japanese mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fuzzy fruits. It is often cultivated for its leaves, which are used in the production of silk, and for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
