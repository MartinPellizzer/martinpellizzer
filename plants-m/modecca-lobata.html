Modecca lobata

Modecca lobata, commonly known as the waxy leaf vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, and often glossy leaves that are typically lobed or deeply divided. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and vertical landscaping.
