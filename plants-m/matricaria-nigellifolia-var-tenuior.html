Matricaria nigellifolia var. tenuior

Matricaria nigellifolia var. tenuior, commonly known as the slender feverfew, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers and finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its foliage is aromatic and contains sesquiterpene lactones, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The varietal form tenuior is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to the typical form of the species.
