Myricaria hoffmeisteri

Myricaria hoffmeisteri, commonly known as the Hoffmeister's tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woody stems and small, scale-like leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for harsh environmental conditions.

Myricaria hoffmeisteri plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
