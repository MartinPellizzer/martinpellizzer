Masdevallia popayanensis

Masdevallia popayanensis, commonly known as the Popayan masdevallia, is a rare and striking orchid native to the high-altitude cloud forests of Colombia.

It is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit and its showy, often colorful flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant typically features long, slender leaves and produces inflorescences with numerous small, intricate flowers. It thrives in cool, humid environments with high light exposure, making it a challenging but rewarding species for experienced orchid growers.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered endangered and requires specific care to cultivate successfully.
