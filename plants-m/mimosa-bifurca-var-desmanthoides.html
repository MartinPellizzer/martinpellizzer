Mimosa bifurca var. desmanthoides

Mimosa bifurca var. desmanthoides, commonly known as the desert mimosa, is a shrub native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that close when touched, a trait known as thigmonastic response. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-adapted to harsh desert environments.

Its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria enhances its ecological role in nutrient cycling.
