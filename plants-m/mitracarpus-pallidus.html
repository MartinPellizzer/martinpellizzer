Mitracarpus pallidus

Mitracarpus pallidus, commonly known as the pale mitracarpus, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive fruit structure. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
