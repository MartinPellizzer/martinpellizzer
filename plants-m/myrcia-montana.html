Myrcia montana

Myrcia montana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Myrcia montana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
