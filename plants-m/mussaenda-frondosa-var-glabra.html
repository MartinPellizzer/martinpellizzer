Mussaenda frondosa var. glabra

Mussaenda frondosa var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth-leaved mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate, and smooth leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, white to cream-colored flowers that are often surrounded by showy, bract-like structures. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
