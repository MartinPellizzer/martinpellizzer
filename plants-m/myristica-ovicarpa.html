Myristica ovicarpa

Myristica ovicarpa, commonly known as the egg-fruit or golden apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its large, yellow-orange, egg-shaped fruits. The plant produces a fragrant, oily resin known as nutmeg, which is highly valued in culinary and medicinal applications. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and glossy, while its flowers are small and white with a strong scent.

The tree thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in lowland tropical forests.
