Mesembryanthemum rigidicaule

Mesembryanthemum rigidicaule, commonly known as the rigid iceplant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are thick and waxy, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Mesembryanthemum rigidicaule is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
