Melocactus antonii

Melocactus antonii, commonly known as the Mexican torch cactus, is a species native to the dry regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem that often develops a reddish or brownish color as it matures. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It has a slow growth rate and is well adapted to arid environments with minimal water requirements.

Its unique appearance and hardiness make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
