Myrtus borbonis

Myrtus borbonis, commonly known as sweet myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are small, red berries that contain seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

Myrtus borbonis is also valued in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory qualities.
