Myriandra rosmarinifolia

Myriandra rosmarinifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Myriandra rosmarinifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in indigenous Australian practices.
