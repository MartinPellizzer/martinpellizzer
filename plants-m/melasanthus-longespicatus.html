Melasanthus longespicatus

Melasanthus longespicatus, commonly known as the long-spined melasanthus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melanthiaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Melasanthus longespicatus is notable for its unique floral structure and its role in supporting local insect populations.
