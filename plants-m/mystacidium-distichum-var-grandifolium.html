Mystacidium distichum var. grandifolium

Mystacidium distichum var. grandifolium, commonly known as the giant leaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to host trees through its haustoria.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 15 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are typically red or orange and contain sticky seeds that adhere to birds for dispersal.

This species is found in tropical and subtropical regions, often in forest canopies where it thrives on a variety of host trees.
