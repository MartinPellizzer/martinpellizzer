Medicago orientalis subsp. graeca

Medicago orientalis subsp.

graeca, commonly known as Greek clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers.

The plant has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun. It is often found in dry grasslands, rocky areas, and along roadsides. Medicago orientalis subsp.

graeca is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a forage plant for livestock.
