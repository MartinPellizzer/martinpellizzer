Mentha × dumetorum var. emiliae

Mentha × dumetorum var. emiliae, commonly known as "Emilia's mint," is a hybrid species within the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodland floors and stream banks. It has a creeping growth habit with rhizomatous stems that allow it to spread vegetatively.

The leaves are usually rounded or ovate, with a strong menthol scent when crushed.
