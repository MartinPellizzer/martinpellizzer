Malva assurgentiflora

Malva assurgentiflora, commonly known as the showy mallows, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Malva assurgentiflora is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
