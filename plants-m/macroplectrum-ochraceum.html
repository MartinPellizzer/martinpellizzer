Macroplectrum ochraceum

Macroplectrum ochraceum, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and is often found in humid, shaded environments. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are typically elongated and grass-like, and it produces clusters of fragrant, showy blooms.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
