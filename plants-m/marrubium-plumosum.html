Marrubium plumosum

Marrubium plumosum, commonly known as the woolly horehound, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its gray-green, woolly leaves and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It has a creeping growth habit with branched stems that can form dense mats.

Marrubium plumosum is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
