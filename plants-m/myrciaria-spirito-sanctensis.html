Myrciaria spirito-sanctensis

Myrciaria spirito-sanctensis, commonly known as the Holy Spirit myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, small white flowers and leathery, elliptical leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rocky soils.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
