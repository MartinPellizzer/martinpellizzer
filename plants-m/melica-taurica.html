Melica taurica

Melica taurica, commonly known as Taurian grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region.

It typically grows in rocky and mountainous areas, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are usually green to reddish in color.

Melica taurica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
