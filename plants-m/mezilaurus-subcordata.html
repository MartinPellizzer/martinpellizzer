Mezilaurus subcordata

Mezilaurus subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed hakea, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family, which is characterized by its diverse floral structures and adaptations to nutrient-poor soils. Its inflorescences are usually cylindrical or spike-like, bearing small, tubular flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Mezilaurus subcordata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
