Mussaenda tomentosa

Mussaenda tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly Mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of showy, yellow-orange bract-like flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape and covered in a dense layer of fine, white to grayish pubescence.

Mussaenda tomentosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
