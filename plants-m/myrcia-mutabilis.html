Myrcia mutabilis

Myrcia mutabilis, commonly known as the variable myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the humid forests of South America, particularly in Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. It typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

Myrcia mutabilis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
