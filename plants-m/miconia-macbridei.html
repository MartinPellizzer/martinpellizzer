Miconia macbridei

Miconia macbridei, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or red miconia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, red bracts that resemble flowers. The plant belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in tropical climates. It produces small white flowers that are followed by bright red fruits, which contain seeds dispersed by birds.

Due to its invasive potential, it is often considered a problematic species in certain regions.
