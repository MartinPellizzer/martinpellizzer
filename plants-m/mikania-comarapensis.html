Mikania comarapensis

Mikania comarapensis, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a vigorous vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread extensively, often climbing over other vegetation. Its fibrous stems and hairy leaves are typical of the Asteraceae family.

Mikania comarapensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
