Mentha longifolia var. austroafghanica

Mentha longifolia var. austroafghanica, commonly known as Afghan mint, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies for its cooling and antiseptic effects.
