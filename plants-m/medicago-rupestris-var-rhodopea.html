Medicago rupestris var. rhodopea

Medicago rupestris var. rhodopea, commonly known as the Rhodope medic.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is native to the Balkan Peninsula. This variant is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
