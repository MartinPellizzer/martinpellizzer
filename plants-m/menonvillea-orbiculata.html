Menonvillea orbiculata

Menonvillea orbiculata, commonly known as the globular cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and dense arrangement of small, spiny stems. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and succulent gardens.
