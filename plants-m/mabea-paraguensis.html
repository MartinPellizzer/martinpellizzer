Mabea paraguensis

Mabea paraguensis, commonly known as the Paraguayan apple or *Araza*, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Brazil, Paraguay, and Argentina.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are similar in appearance to apples but have a tangy, acidic flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.

Mabea paraguensis is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
