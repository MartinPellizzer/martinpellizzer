Mimulus guttatus

Mimulus guttatus, commonly known as the common yellow water-lily, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with five petals and a distinctive spur at the back of each bloom. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as stream banks, wet meadows, and pond edges. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Mimulus guttatus is also known for its ability to hybridize with other species in the Mimulus genus, contributing to its genetic diversity.
