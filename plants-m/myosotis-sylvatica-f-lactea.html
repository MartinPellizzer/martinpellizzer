Myosotis sylvatica f. lactea

Myosotis sylvatica f. lactea, commonly known as the milky forget-me-not, is a variant of the common forget-me-not plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive milky white flowers, which differ from the typical blue or purple hues of the standard species. The plant is native to Europe and thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

The f. lactea form is prized in horticulture for its unique coloration and ornamental value.
