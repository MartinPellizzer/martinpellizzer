Maba hillebrandii

Maba hillebrandii, commonly known as the African maba tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and has a dense canopy that provides shade. Its bark is often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting various wildlife species.

The plant is also valued for its timber, which is used in construction and crafts.
