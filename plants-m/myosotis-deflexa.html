Myosotis deflexa

Myosotis deflexa, commonly known as the bent bluebell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It typically grows in damp, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. Its flowers are small, blue to violet, and bell-shaped, blooming in late winter to early spring.

The leaves are lance-shaped, slightly hairy, and arranged alternately along the stem.
