Morocarpus dichotomus var. ochraceus

Morocarpus dichotomus var. ochraceus, commonly known as the ochraceous morocarpus, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It features a short, branching stem with dense clusters of fleshy, cylindrical leaves that are typically green with a yellowish or orange hue. The leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps the plant conserve water in its harsh environment. It produces small, yellow flowers during the rainy season, adding a subtle ornamental value.

This variety is prized for its compact growth and unique coloration, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
