Mentha boenninghausiana

Mentha boenninghausiana, commonly known as the alpine mint, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and calming properties.

Mentha boenninghausiana is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
