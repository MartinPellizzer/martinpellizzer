Mammillaria mexicensis

Mammillaria mexicensis, commonly known as the Mexican nipple cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and dense covering of white to pale yellow spines. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 centimeters and has a compact, rounded form. It produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
