Myrcia complicata

Myrcia complicata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its small, clustered flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible.

Myrcia complicata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
