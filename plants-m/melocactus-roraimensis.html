Melocactus roraimensis

Melocactus roraimensis, commonly known as the Roraima melon cactus, is a unique cactus species native to the tepuis of Venezuela and Brazil.

It is characterized by its globular shape and a dense covering of white, woolly hairs that protect it from intense sunlight and moisture loss. The plant produces a single, elongated, yellowish-green fruit that is edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are typically pink or red, appearing at the top of the plant during the wet season.

Due to its habitat in remote and ecologically sensitive areas, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
