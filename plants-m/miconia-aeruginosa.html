Miconia aeruginosa

Miconia aeruginosa, commonly known as the silver miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white or pinkish flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting various pollinators. Its fruits are red, fleshy berries that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its dispersal by animals.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
