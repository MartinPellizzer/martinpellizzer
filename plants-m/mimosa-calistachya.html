Mimosa calistachya

Mimosa calistachya, commonly known as the desert mimosa, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features feathery, bipinnate leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers in clusters. It is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.

Mimosa calistachya plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
