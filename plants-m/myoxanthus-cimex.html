Myoxanthus cimex

Myoxanthus cimex, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and produces small, white flowers that are often clustered together. The plant is notable for its aromatic leaves, which emit a pleasant scent when crushed. Myoxanthus cimex thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky outcrops.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
