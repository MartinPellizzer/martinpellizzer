Monochaetum neglectum

Monochaetum neglectum, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the wetlands and coastal areas of eastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and a tall, flowering spike that produces small, greenish-brown flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tidal zones or along the edges of freshwater bodies.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in wetland restoration projects.
