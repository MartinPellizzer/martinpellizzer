Mentha × dumetorum var. dubia

Mentha × dumetorum var. dubia, commonly known as the "dubia mint," is a hybrid species within the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a mild, minty fragrance.
