Madaria polycarpha

Madaria polycarpha, commonly known as the desert milkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Apocynaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and showy flowers. The plant typically grows as a low, sprawling shrub with woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pink to reddish-purple, clustered in dense, raceme-like inflorescences.

Madaria polycarpha is adapted to harsh desert conditions, with deep root systems and a ability to store water efficiently.
