Mentha cyrtodonta

Mentha cyrtodonta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mint, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, minty fragrance.

Mentha cyrtodonta is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
