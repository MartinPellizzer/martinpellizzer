Mastichina

Mastichina, commonly known as the mastich tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rutaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces a resin that has been historically used for medicinal and aromatic purposes. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a leathery texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its historical significance in traditional medicine.
