Megastachya pastoensis

Megastachya pastoensis, commonly known as the Pasto spikegrass, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Ecuador and Colombia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense inflorescences. The plant produces striking, elongated spikelets that are often purple or reddish in color, adding ornamental value to its appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
