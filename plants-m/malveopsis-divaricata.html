Malveopsis divaricata

Malveopsis divaricata, commonly known as the variable mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus, and is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
