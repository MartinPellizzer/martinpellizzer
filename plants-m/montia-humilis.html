Montia humilis

Montia humilis, commonly known as the common watercress, is a low-growing annual herb native to North America.

It typically thrives in moist, often rocky soils near streams, springs, or wet meadows. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along its slender, branching stems. Its flowers are tiny, white to pinkish, and borne in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Montia humilis is often found in temperate regions and is known for its peppery, tangy flavor, making it a valued wild vegetable.
