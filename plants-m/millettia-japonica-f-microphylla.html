Millettia japonica f. microphylla

Millettia japonica f. microphylla, commonly known as the Japanese millettia or small-leaved millettia, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its foliage is dense and often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation, supporting soil fertility in its native habitats.
