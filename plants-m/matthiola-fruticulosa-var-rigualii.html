Matthiola fruticulosa var. rigualii

Matthiola fruticulosa var. rigualii, commonly known as the Sierra Madre stock, is a compact, herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It features small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring. The plant has linear, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in its dry habitat. It is often found growing in rocky soils and is adapted to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging environments.
