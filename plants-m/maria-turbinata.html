Maria turbinata

Maria turbinata, commonly known as the turban lily, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, turban-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture.

Maria turbinata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
