Miconia demissifolia

Miconia demissifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Miconia demissifolia is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

However, it is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its competitive nature and ability to form dense stands.
