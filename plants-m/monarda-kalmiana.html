Monarda kalmiana

Monarda kalmiana, commonly known as Kalm's bergamot, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or lavender in color, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its foliage is fragrant and has been used historically by Indigenous peoples for medicinal purposes.

Monarda kalmiana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.
