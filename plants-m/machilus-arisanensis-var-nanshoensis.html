Machilus arisanensis var. nanshoensis

Machilus arisanensis var. nanshoensis, commonly known as the Nanshoen Chinese laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
