Maranta pavonina

Maranta pavonina, commonly known as the velvetleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family, characterized by its striking, often colorful foliage. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and vibrant patterns of red, pink, and green. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant's rhizomes are also used in traditional medicine and as a source of starch.
