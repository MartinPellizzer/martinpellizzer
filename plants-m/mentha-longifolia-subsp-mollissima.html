Mentha longifolia subsp. mollissima

Mentha longifolia subsp.

mollissima, commonly known as woolly mint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a soft, velvety texture.

The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.
