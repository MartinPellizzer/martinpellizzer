Medinilla triplinervia

Medinilla triplinervia, commonly known as the velvet tangerine, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are typically ovate with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance.

Medinilla triplinervia thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
