Mikania perstipulata

Mikania perstipulata, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and trailing habit. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and forest edges, due to its adaptability.

Its fibrous roots and vigorous growth make it an invasive species in some regions.
