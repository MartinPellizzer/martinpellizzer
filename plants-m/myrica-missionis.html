Myrica missionis

Myrica missionis, commonly known as the Mission myrtle, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by oval, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Myrica missionis is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use by indigenous peoples.
