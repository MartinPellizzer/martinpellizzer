Monnina lehmanniana

Monnina lehmanniana, commonly known as blue false flax, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces delicate blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to dry environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
