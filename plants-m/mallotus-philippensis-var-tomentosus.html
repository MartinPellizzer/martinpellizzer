Mallotus philippensis var. tomentosus

Mallotus philippensis var. tomentosus, commonly known as the hairy mallotus, is a shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, elliptical leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
