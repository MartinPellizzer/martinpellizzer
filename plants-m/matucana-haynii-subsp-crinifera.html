Matucana haynii subsp. crinifera

Matucana haynii subsp.

crinifera, commonly known as the Crinifera Matucana, is a rare and striking bromeliad native to the cloud forests of Ecuador. It is characterized by its long, arching inflorescence that resembles a crown, giving it its common name.

The plant has elongated, narrow leaves that form a rosette, capable of holding water, a feature common in bromeliads. Its flowers are typically bright red or orange, adding to its ornamental value.

It thrives in high humidity and indirect light, making it a unique addition to specialized plant collections.
