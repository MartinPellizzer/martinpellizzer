Mcneillia graminifolia subsp. hungarica

Mcneillia graminifolia subsp.

hungarica, commonly known as the Hungarian grass. It is a perennial grass species native to Central and Eastern Europe.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of grassland ecosystems.
