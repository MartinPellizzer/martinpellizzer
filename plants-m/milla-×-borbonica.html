Milla × borbonica

Milla × borbonica, commonly known as the hybrid milla, is a plant that results from the crossbreeding of two species within the Milla genus.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are broad, leathery, and often have a glossy appearance.

Milla × borbonica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
