Mirandea sylvatica

Mirandea sylvatica, commonly known as the forest mirandea, is a rare and elusive plant species native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in dense woodlands and along stream banks. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, Mirandea sylvatica is considered endangered in many areas.
