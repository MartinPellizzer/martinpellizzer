Mimusops eickii

Mimusops eickii, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a tropical tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and large, glossy, elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions for both its beauty and ecological benefits.
