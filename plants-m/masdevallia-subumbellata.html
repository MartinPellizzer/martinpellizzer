Masdevallia subumbellata

Masdevallia subumbellata, commonly known as the Umbellate Masdevallia, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its compact, hairy pseudobulbs and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant produces intricate inflorescences with numerous small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique and delicate floral display.
