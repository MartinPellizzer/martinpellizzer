Melilotus × haussknechtianus

Melilotus × haussknechtianus, commonly known as the sweet clover, is a hybrid plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is known for its high content of volatile oils, which contribute to its aromatic properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it both useful in agriculture and a potential allergen due to its pollen.
