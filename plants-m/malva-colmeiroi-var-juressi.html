Malva colmeiroi var. juressi

Malva colmeiroi var. juressi, commonly known as the Sierra Madre mallows, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus, and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the stems are hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges, where it thrives in full sun.
