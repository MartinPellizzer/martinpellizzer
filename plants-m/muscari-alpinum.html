Muscari alpinum

Muscari alpinum, commonly known as the alpine musk grape, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet. The plant produces a distinctive musky fragrance, which is particularly noticeable when the flowers are in bloom. Muscari alpinum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and early spring blooming period.
