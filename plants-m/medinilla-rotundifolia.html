Medinilla rotundifolia

Medinilla rotundifolia, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or velvet flower, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces fleshy, orange-red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Medinilla rotundifolia thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
