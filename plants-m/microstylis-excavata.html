Microstylis excavata

Microstylis excavata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments in North America.

It typically grows in dense clumps, reaching heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters at the base of the stems. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form extensive colonies in suitable habitats.

Microstylis excavata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
