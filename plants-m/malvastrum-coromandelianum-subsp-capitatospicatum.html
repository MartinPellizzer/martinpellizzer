Malvastrum coromandelianum subsp. capitatospicatum

Malvastrum coromandelianum subsp.

capitatospicatum, commonly known as the Cape Hottentot, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive, showy inflorescences that consist of numerous small, pink to purple flowers clustered in dense, rounded heads.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with serrated edges, and the stems are often covered in fine, white hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions with a Mediterranean climate.
