Mammillaria rettigiana

Mammillaria rettigiana, commonly known as the Rettig's pincushion cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring a pale green body covered in white or pale blue spines. The plant typically grows to a height of about 5-7 centimeters and has a compact, rounded shape. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or orange, appearing in the spring.

Mammillaria rettigiana is well-suited for rock gardens and requires well-draining soil and full sun to thrive.
