Medusagyne oppositifolia

Medusagyne oppositifolia, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by round, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional uses.
