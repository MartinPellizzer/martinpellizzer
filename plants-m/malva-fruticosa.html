Malva fruticosa

Malva fruticosa, commonly known as the woolly mallow, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
