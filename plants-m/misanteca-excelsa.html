Misanteca excelsa

Misanteca excelsa, commonly known as the noble mountain orchid, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations above 2,000 meters. Its flowers, which can be pink, purple, or white, bloom in late summer and are known for their intricate petal structure.

Due to its habitat specificity, Misanteca excelsa is considered a rare and endangered species.
