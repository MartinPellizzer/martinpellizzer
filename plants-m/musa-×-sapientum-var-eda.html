Musa × sapientum var. eda

Musa × sapientum var. eda, commonly known as the Eda banana, is a cultivated variety of the genus Musa.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender fruit bunches that produce elongated, pale yellow to orange-colored fingers. The plant is a diploid hybrid, resulting from crosses between different Musa species, and is valued for its unique flavor and texture. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

The Eda banana is primarily grown for its edible fruit, which is rich in potassium and dietary fiber.
