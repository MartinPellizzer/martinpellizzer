Melocactus margaritaceus

Melocactus margaritaceus, commonly known as the "pearl cactus," is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, columnar stem and a dense covering of white, waxy spines that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its slow growth rate and ability to store water in its stem make it well adapted to harsh desert environments.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique aesthetic.
