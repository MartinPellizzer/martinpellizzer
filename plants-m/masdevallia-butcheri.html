Masdevallia butcheri

Masdevallia butcheri, commonly known as the Butcher's Masdevallia, is a rare and striking orchid native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often colorful flowers that display a complex arrangement of petals and sepals. The plant typically grows as a miniature epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in its natural habitat. Its botanical features include a short, stout stem and a single, showy inflorescence.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable and endangered species in botanical conservation efforts.
