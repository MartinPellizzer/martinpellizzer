Microcerasus prostrata f. turcomanica

Microcerasus prostrata f. turcomanica, commonly known as the Turcoman cherry, is a low-growing shrub native to Central Asia.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and horticultural collections.
