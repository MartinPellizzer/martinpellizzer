Maxillaria gracilipes

Maxillaria gracilipes, commonly known as the slender-lipped maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive yellow and purple coloration. The plant typically features long, narrow leaves and has a pseudobulb structure typical of many orchid species. Its flowers are borne on lateral spikes and often bloom in late summer or early autumn.

Maxillaria gracilipes is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
