Myrcia asperorum

Myrcia asperorum, commonly known as the rough myrtle, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruits are typically small, red berries that contain several seeds.

Myrcia asperorum is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical regions.
