Musa kamengensis

Musa kamengensis, commonly known as the Assam banana, is a species of banana native to the northeastern region of India.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by large, broad leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of berries, which are typically yellow when ripe and contain a starchy, edible pulp. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its unique genetic makeup, it is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers.
