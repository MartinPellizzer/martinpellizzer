Mammea odorata

Mammea odorata, commonly known as the fragrant mammea, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and distinctive, spiny fruit. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and parks.
