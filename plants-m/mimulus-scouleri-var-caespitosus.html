Mimulus scouleri var. caespitosus

Mimulus scouleri var. caespitosus, commonly known as the tufted monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and bright orange or yellow tubular flowers. The species thrives in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and forest floors. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, hairy structures.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
