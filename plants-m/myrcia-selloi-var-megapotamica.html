Myrcia selloi var. megapotamica

Myrcia selloi var. megapotamica, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are rich in essential oils and have been utilized for their antimicrobial properties.

This species thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.
