Masdevallia pachyura f. pardinella

Masdevallia pachyura f. pardinella, commonly known as the Pachyura Pardinella Masdevallia, is a unique orchid species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, dense growth habit and striking, often velvety foliage. The plant typically produces small, intricate flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in high-altitude environments with high humidity and partial shade, requiring well-draining, acidic soil.

This hybrid form is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and challenging cultivation requirements.
