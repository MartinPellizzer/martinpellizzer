Mesembryanthemum purpureocroceum var. flavocroceum

Mesembryanthemum purpureocroceum var. flavocroceum, commonly known as the Orange Canary, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed leaves that are typically green with a reddish or purplish tinge. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a striking contrast to its foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and low-maintenance gardens.
