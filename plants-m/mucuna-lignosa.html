Mucuna lignosa

Mucuna lignosa, commonly known as the woody cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, woody stems and trifoliate leaves with long, slender leaflets. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that give way to elongated, pod-like fruits containing kidney-shaped seeds. Mucuna lignosa is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, enriching the soil and making it a valuable plant in agroforestry systems.

Its rapid growth and hardy nature make it an important species for erosion control and forage production.
