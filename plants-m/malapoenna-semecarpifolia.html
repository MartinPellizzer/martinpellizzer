Malapoenna semecarpifolia

Malapoenna semecarpifolia, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, star-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, green, fleshy fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
