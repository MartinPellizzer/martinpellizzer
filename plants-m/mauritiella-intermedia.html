Mauritiella intermedia

Mauritiella intermedia, commonly known as the African date palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a tall, slender palm characterized by its long, pinnate leaves and clustered, yellowish-orange fruits. The plant produces edible fruits that are often consumed fresh or processed into various products. Its robust growth habit makes it suitable for ornamental and agricultural purposes.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
