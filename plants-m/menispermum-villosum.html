Menispermum villosum

Menispermum villosum, commonly known as the hairy Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, tubular flowers that resemble a pipe. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, and its stems are often covered in fine, hairy hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The fruit is a red, fleshy berry that contains several seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
