Meiocarpidium

Meiocarpidium, commonly known as the "spore-bearing structure of certain liverworts," is a specialized reproductive organ found in certain members of the Marchantiophyta class.

It is typically located on the thallus and serves as the site where spores are produced through meiosis. The structure is often flask-shaped, with a narrow neck leading to a swollen base containing the spore sac. Meiocarpidium plays a crucial role in the sexual reproduction of these primitive plants.

Its morphology and function are key to understanding the reproductive strategies of early land plants.
