Mitropsidium brownianum

Mitropsidium brownianum, commonly known as the Brownian mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of specific host trees.

It has small, leathery leaves and produces white, fleshy berries that are a source of food for various birds and small mammals. The plant relies on its host for water and some nutrients, while it photosynthesizes to obtain additional energy. It is native to certain regions of South America and is often found in humid, tropical forests.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can have significant impacts on the health of its host trees.
