Mimosa xiquexiquensis

Mimosa xiquexiquensis, commonly known as the Brazilian mimosa, is a shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in sandy soils.

Mimosa xiquexiquensis is also known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas.
