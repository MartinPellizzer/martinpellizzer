Mahernia grossularifolia

Mahernia grossularifolia, commonly known as the mountain hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the highlands of southern Africa.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. Its fruit is a small, red berry that contains several seeds.

Mahernia grossularifolia is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
