Mikania hitchcockii

Mikania hitchcockii, commonly known as Hitchcock's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in the early morning, giving it its common name. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture, while its stems are hairy and twining.

Mikania hitchcockii is often found in moist, open areas such as woodland edges and disturbed soils.
