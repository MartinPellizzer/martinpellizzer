Momordica denticulata

Momordica denticulata, commonly known as the spiny bitter melon, is a tropical vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces elongated, green fruits that turn orange when mature, often featuring a warty surface. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates.

Botanically, it is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
