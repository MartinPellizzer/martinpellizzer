Melochia parvifolia var. normalis

Melochia parvifolia var. normalis, commonly known as the small-leaved melochia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically divided into five to seven lobes, and small, pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
