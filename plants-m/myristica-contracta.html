Myristica contracta

Myristica contracta, commonly known as the Malabar nut or black pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces dark brown, aromatic seeds that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that ripens to a deep red or black color.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for its unique foliage and medicinal properties.
