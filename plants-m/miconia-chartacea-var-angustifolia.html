Miconia chartacea var. angustifolia

Miconia chartacea var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Americas.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, with prominent veins. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and unique fruit.

However, it can be invasive in some regions, posing a threat to native ecosystems.
