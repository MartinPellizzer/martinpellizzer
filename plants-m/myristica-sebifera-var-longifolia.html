Myristica sebifera var. longifolia

Myristica sebifera var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Moluccas (Maluku) Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody fruits that contain the valuable spice nutmeg. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by large, fleshy berries that turn red when mature. Its leaves are elongated and leathery, with a distinctive aromatic scent.

This variety is primarily cultivated for its nutmeg and mace, which are used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
