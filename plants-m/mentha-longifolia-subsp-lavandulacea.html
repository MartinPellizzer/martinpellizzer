Mentha longifolia subsp. lavandulacea

Mentha longifolia subsp.

lavandulacea, commonly known as lavender mint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its fragrance is similar to both mint and lavender, making it popular in culinary and aromatic applications.

It is often used in herbal teas and as a companion plant in gardens.
