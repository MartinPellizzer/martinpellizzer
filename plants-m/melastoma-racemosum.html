Melastoma racemosum

Melastoma racemosum, commonly known as the red-rooted mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers in racemes, which bloom from summer to autumn. Its roots are often reddish in color, giving it its common name, and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, moist conditions.
