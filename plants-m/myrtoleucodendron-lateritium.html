Myrtoleucodendron lateritium

Myrtoleucodendron lateritium, commonly known as the silver-leaved myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

The plant is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from dense pubescence on the leaves. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in inflorescences, typical of the Myrtaceae family.

The plant is often found in rocky or well-drained soils, thriving in dry to semi-arid environments.
