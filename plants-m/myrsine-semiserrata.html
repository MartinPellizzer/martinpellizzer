Myrsine semiserrata

Myrsine semiserrata, commonly known as the serrate myrsine, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrsinaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, including parts of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical in shape, and have serrated margins, giving it its common name.

The flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and the plant produces red, fleshy berries that are often consumed by birds.
