Menziesia tubiflora

Menziesia tubiflora, commonly known as the red stem cinquefoil, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with five leaflets, and its flowers are typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Menziesia tubiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
