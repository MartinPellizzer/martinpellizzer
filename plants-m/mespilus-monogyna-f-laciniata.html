Mespilus monogyna f. laciniata

Mespilus monogyna f. laciniata, commonly known as the bristly medlar, is a botanical variant of the common medlar.

It is characterized by its finely serrated leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits that ripen in late summer. It is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils.

This form is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in horticulture.
