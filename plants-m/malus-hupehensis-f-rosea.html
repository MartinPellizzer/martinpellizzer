Malus hupehensis f. rosea

Malus hupehensis f. rosea, commonly known as the Rosaeflowering crabapple, is a cultivar of the crabapple tree native to China.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring clusters of pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate with a serrated margin.

The fruit, which is a small apple-like pome, is edible but not typically cultivated for consumption.
