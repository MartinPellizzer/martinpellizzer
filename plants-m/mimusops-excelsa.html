Mimusops excelsa

Mimusops excelsa, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical tree native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces a fruit with a thick, leathery rind and a sweet, juicy pulp containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The plant is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture-making.
