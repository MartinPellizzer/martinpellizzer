Moraea viscaria var. bituminosa

Moraea viscaria var. bituminosa, commonly known as the bituminous moraea, is a striking perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of striking, often purple or violet flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its cultivation in horticultural settings.
