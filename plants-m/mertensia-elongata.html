Mertensia elongata

Mertensia elongata, commonly known as the longleaf bluebells, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Mertensia elongata plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ecological and aesthetic contributions.
