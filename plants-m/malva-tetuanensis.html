Malva tetuanensis

Malva tetuanensis, commonly known as the Tetuan mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or scrubland habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
