Miscopetalum cernuum

Miscopetalum cernuum, commonly known as the drooping mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, branching stems that often droop, giving it a graceful appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers clustered in terminal spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a soft, fuzzy texture.

Miscopetalum cernuum thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
