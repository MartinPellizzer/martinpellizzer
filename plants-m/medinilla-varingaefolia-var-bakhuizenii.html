Medinilla varingaefolia var. bakhuizenii

Medinilla varingaefolia var. bakhuizenii, commonly known as the Bornean velvetleaf, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or white, which bloom during the wet season. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for indoor cultivation, though it thrives best in high humidity and indirect light.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
