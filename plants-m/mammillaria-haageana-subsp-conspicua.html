Mammillaria haageana subsp. conspicua

Mammillaria haageana subsp.

conspicua, commonly known as the showy mammillaria, is a small, globular cactus native to Mexico. It is characterized by its pale green to yellowish stems, which are covered in short, white spines.

The plant produces pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and collections.
