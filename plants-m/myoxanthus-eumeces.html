Myoxanthus eumeces

Myoxanthus eumeces, commonly known as the yellow sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that produces small, yellow flowers clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with deep roots and thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its reproductive success.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important component of its native desert ecosystems.
