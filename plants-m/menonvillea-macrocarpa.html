Menonvillea macrocarpa

Menonvillea macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant bromeliad, is a striking plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence that can reach several feet in height. The plant features a rosette of broad, waxy leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and provides a habitat for small organisms. Its flowers are typically pink or red, with a unique structure that attracts pollinators like hummingbirds and bats.

Menonvillea macrocarpa is a slow-growing plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-draining soil.
