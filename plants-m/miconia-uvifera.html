Miconia uvifera

Miconia uvifera, commonly known as the fruit-bearing miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. These berries are edible and have a sweet, slightly tart flavor, making them popular in local cuisines.

Miconia uvifera is also notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of tropical environments.
