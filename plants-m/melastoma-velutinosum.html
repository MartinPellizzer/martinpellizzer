Melastoma velutinosum

Melastoma velutinosum, commonly known as the velvet strobilanthes, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually velvety to the touch, giving it its common name.

Melastoma velutinosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems.
