Malus nivalis

Malus nivalis, commonly known as the snow apple, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the Himalayas and is characterized by its ornamental white flowers and edible, red berries. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin.

The fruit, which ripens in autumn, is used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.
