Mariscus micans

Mariscus micans, commonly known as the silverleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves and showy, pale purple to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes. Its scientific name reflects its distinctive appearance, with "micans" referring to the shiny, waxy coating on its leaves.

Mariscus micans is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
