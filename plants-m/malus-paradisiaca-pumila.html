Malus paradisiaca pumila

Malus paradisiaca pumila, commonly known as the dwarf apple tree, is a small deciduous tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its rounded crown and thorny branches. The tree produces small, sweet-tart apples that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, while its flowers are white with pinkish tinges.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in horticulture.
