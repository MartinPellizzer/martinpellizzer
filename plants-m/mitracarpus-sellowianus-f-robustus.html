Mitracarpus sellowianus f. robustus

Mitracarpus sellowianus f. robustus, commonly known as the Brazilian wild bean, is a shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a rough, hairy texture and produces small, yellow flowers. Its fruit is a legume that contains several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
