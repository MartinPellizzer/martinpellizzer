Macfadyena bracteosa

Macfadyena bracteosa, commonly known as the Crown of Thorns, is a flowering plant native to tropical and southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces striking clusters of yellow or orange flowers. The plant is characterized by its spiny stems and leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and resilience.
