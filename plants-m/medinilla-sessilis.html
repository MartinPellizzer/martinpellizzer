Medinilla sessilis

Medinilla sessilis, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in humid environments.

Medinilla sessilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
