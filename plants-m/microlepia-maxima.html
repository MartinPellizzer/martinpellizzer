Microlepia maxima

Microlepia maxima, commonly known as the giant chain fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pinnate fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its spores are produced in clusters on the underside of the fronds, typical of the fern family.

Microlepia maxima is often found in tropical rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
