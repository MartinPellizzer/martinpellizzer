Micropholis trunciflora

Micropholis trunciflora, commonly known as the dwarf hibiscus, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine white pubescence. The plant produces small, showy flowers with five petals, typically in shades of pink or white. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens where it adds a tropical flair.
