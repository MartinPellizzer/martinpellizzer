Malaxis corymbosa

Malaxis corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered lady's tresses, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its dense, corymbose inflorescence of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant has a short, creeping rhizome and produces aerial roots that help it attach to trees or other substrates. Its leaves are linear and often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
