Mammillaria felipensis

Mammillaria felipensis, commonly known as the Felip's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and spiny, ribbed stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in arid environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in the spring.

It is a popular choice for collectors due to its compact size and attractive appearance.
