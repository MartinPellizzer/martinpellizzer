Magnolia panamensis

Magnolia panamensis, commonly known as the Panama magnolia, is a striking flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pinkish-white petals that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows as a tall, slender tree with smooth, grayish bark and elongated, narrow leaves. Its flowers emit a sweet, fragrant aroma, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
