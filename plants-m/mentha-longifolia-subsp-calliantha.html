Mentha longifolia subsp. calliantha

Mentha longifolia subsp.

calliantha, commonly known as narrow-leaved mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright stems that are often hairy and bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a strong aromatic scent.

The flowers are small, tubular, and typically violet or blue in color, arranged in dense terminal clusters. This subspecies is characterized by its slender leaves and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
