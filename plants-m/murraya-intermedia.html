Murraya intermedia

Murraya intermedia, commonly known as the Chinese tallow tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, simple, ovate leaves with a glossy green upper surface and a pale underside. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries containing toxic seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is adaptable to various soil types.
