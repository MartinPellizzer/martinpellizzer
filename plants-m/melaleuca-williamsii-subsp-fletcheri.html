Melaleuca williamsii subsp. fletcheri

Melaleuca williamsii subsp.

fletcheri, commonly known as the Northern Territory Melaleuca, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to northern Australia. It typically grows in sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

The plant has aromatic, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, appearing in clusters at the ends of branches.

It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and salt spray.
