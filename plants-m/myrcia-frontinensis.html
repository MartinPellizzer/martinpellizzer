Myrcia frontinensis

Myrcia frontinensis, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that belongs to the Myrtaceae family. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions, though it can become invasive in some areas.
