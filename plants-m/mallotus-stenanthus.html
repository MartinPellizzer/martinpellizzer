Mallotus stenanthus

Mallotus stenanthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallot, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
