Merinthopodium

Merinthopodium, commonly known as the "false brome," is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family (Poaceae).

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midvein.

The flowers are wind-pollinated, and the plant is often found in temperate regions across Europe and Asia.
