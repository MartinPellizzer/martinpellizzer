Malaxis venosa

Malaxis venosa, commonly known as the veined malaxis, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

The orchid is noted for its delicate appearance and is occasionally cultivated for ornamental purposes.
