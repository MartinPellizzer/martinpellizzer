Mayaca sellowiana f. longipedicellata

Mayaca sellowiana f. longipedicellata, commonly known as the long-armed mayaca, is a variant of the mayaca genus native to South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on long pedicels. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in ecological and horticultural studies.
