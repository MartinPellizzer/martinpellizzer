Microcybe multiflora subsp. multiflora

Microcybe multiflora subsp.

multiflora, commonly known as the Florida bottlebrush, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of bright red flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal areas and sandy soils.

Its unique floral structure makes it an important species for pollinators and a valued ornamental plant.
