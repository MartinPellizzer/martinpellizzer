Metalasia pulcherrima var. pallescens

Metalasia pulcherrima var. pallescens, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a street tree in urban landscapes.
