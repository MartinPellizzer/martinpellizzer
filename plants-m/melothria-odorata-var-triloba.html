Melothria odorata var. triloba

Melothria odorata var. triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed sourkraut or sour melon, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It produces small, green, three-lobed fruits that are typically harvested when they are still unripe and sour. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its fruits are often used in traditional cuisine, particularly in soups and stews.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to climb structures.
