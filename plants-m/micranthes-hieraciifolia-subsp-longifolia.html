Micranthes hieraciifolia subsp. longifolia

Micranthes hieraciifolia subsp.

longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf micranthes, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its small, clustered flowers and finely divided, fern-like leaves.

Native to the eastern United States, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of native plant communities and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
