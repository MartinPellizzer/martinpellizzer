Mentha × cantalica

Mentha × cantalica, commonly known as the hybrid mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Mentha spicata (spearmint) and Mentha viridis (ovalleaf mint). The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring square stems and opposite, serrated leaves. Its fragrant, small flowers are usually pink or purple, blooming in late summer.

Mentha × cantalica is widely cultivated for its aromatic leaves, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
