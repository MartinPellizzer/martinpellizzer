Morus macroura

Morus macroura, commonly known as the long-stamen mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The tree produces edible, black berries that are rich in nutrients and commonly consumed by both humans and animals. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.

The plant has also been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
