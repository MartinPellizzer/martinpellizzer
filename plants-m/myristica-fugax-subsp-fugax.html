Myristica fugax subsp. fugax

Myristica fugax subsp.

fugax, commonly known as the fragrant nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia. It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic seeds, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody capsules containing the aromatic nutmeg kernel. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a leathery texture with a distinct scent.

The species is notable for its economic importance and ecological role in its native habitat.
