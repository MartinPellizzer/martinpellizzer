Millettia versicolor

Millettia versicolor, commonly known as the variable millettia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including China, India, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or on rocky slopes.

Millettia versicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
