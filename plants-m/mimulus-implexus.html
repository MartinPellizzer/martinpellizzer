Mimulus implexus

Mimulus implexus, commonly known as the twisted monkeyflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces, and its stems are often branched and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Mimulus implexus plays a role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
