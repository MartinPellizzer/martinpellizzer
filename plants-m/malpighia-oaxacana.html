Malpighia oaxacana

Malpighia oaxacana, commonly known as the Oaxacan beautyberry, is a small shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. The plant has simple, alternate leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

The flowers are small, white, and clustered, producing a distinctive fragrance that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
