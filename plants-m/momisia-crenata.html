Momisia crenata

Momisia crenata, commonly known as the serrated momisia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are often yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces pods that contain seeds.

Momisia crenata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and floral display.
