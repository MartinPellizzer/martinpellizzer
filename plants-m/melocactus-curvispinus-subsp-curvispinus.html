Melocactus curvispinus subsp. curvispinus

Melocactus curvispinus subsp.

curvispinus, commonly known as the curved spine melocactus, is a cactus native to Mexico. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem that often develops a reddish or brownish color with age.

The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the summer months. Its spines are curved and often have a reddish tinge, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.
