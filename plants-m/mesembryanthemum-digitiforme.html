Mesembryanthemum digitiforme

Mesembryanthemum digitiforme, commonly known as the fingerleaf iceplant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is widely cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
