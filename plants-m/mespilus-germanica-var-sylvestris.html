Mespilus germanica var. sylvestris

Mespilus germanica var. sylvestris, commonly known as the wild medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its oval, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is a pome, typically turning from green to yellowish-brown as it ripens, and has a distinctive musky aroma. The plant is known for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden environments.
