Miconia tonduzii var. furfuracea

Miconia tonduzii var. furfuracea, commonly known as the hairy miconia, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in dense, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are often overlooked due to the prominent foliage. Its growth habit is shrub-like, with multiple stems arising from the base.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
