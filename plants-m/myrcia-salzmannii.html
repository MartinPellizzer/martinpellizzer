Myrcia salzmannii

Myrcia salzmannii, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Myrcia salzmannii is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
