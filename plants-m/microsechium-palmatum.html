Microsechium palmatum

Microsechium palmatum, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the Andes, is a perennial aquatic plant native to South America.

It features striking, palmately lobed leaves that float on the water's surface, often with a waxy coating that repels water. The plant produces showy, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native wetland habitats.
