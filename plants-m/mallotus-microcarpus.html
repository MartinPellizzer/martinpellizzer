Mallotus microcarpus

Mallotus microcarpus, commonly known as the small-fruited mallot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant features compound leaves with opposite arrangement, and its small, clustered flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow. The fruit is a small, round drupe that matures to a dark purple or black color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
