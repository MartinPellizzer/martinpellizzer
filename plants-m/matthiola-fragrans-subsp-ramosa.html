Matthiola fragrans subsp. ramosa

Matthiola fragrans subsp.

ramosa, commonly known as the fragrant wallflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, brightly colored flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red.

The subspecies ramosa is distinguished by its more branched and dense foliage compared to the typical form. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and rock gardens.

The plant is also valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in ornamental horticulture.
