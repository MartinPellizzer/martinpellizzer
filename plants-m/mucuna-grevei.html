Mucuna grevei

Mucuna grevei, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, hairy, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is also recognized for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities, which enhance soil fertility.

The seeds of Mucuna grevei are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.
