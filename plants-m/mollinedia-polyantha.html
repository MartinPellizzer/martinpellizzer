Mollinedia polyantha

Mollinedia polyantha, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded areas.

Mollinedia polyantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
