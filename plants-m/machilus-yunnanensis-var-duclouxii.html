Machilus yunnanensis var. duclouxii

Machilus yunnanensis var. duclouxii, commonly known as the Yunnan Machilus variant, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to China, particularly in the Yunnan province, where it thrives in humid, subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, aromatic leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by small, dark berries.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and ecological significance.
