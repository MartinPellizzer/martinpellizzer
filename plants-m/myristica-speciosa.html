Myristica speciosa

Myristica speciosa, commonly known as the nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It is part of the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces a unique fruit that contains the aromatic seeds used to make nutmeg and mace. Its botanical features include a smooth, greyish-brown bark and small, clustered inflorescences.

The plant is also valued for its essential oils and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.
