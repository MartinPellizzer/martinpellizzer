Myristica umbrosa

Myristica umbrosa, commonly known as the dark myristica, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The plant produces distinctive fruit capsules that contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its botanical significance lies in its unique chemical composition, which includes compounds like myristicin and elemicin.

Myristica umbrosa is also valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
