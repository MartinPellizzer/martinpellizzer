Malache melanommata

Malache melanommata, commonly known as the black-stemmed malache, is a unique member of the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom during the warmer months. Native to tropical regions of Africa, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
