Melia japonica var. albicans

Melia japonica var. albicans, commonly known as the white-flowered chinaberry tree, is a variant of the chinaberry tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and its distinctive white flowers that bloom in summer. The tree produces small, yellowish fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its showy flowers and rapid growth.

However, it can be invasive in some regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
