Mandevilla contrerasii

Mandevilla contrerasii, commonly known as the Contreras' mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a vigorous climber with thick, fleshy stems and heart-shaped, glossy leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming from spring to fall. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil and full sun for optimal growth.

Mandevilla contrerasii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and attractive foliage.
