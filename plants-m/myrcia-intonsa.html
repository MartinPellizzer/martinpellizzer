Myrcia intonsa

Myrcia intonsa, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Myrcia intonsa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has also been studied for its potential pharmacological properties.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
