Melica weberbaueri var. typica

Melica weberbaueri var. typica, commonly known as Weberbauer's meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that helps it conserve moisture.

Botanically, it is characterized by its unique floral structure and is often found in high-altitude meadows and scree slopes.
