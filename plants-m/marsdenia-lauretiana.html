Marsdenia lauretiana

Marsdenia lauretiana, commonly known as the Christmas star, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen, opposite leaves that are often adorned with white or yellow spots. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a houseplant and in gardens.
