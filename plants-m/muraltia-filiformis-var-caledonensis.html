Muraltia filiformis var. caledonensis

Muraltia filiformis var. caledonensis, commonly known as the Caledonian coral tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Indo-Pacific.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems that resemble coral, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often followed by small, red or orange fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
