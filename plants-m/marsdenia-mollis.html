Marsdenia mollis

Marsdenia mollis, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite and have a velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Marsdenia mollis is often cultivated as a ground cover or in hanging baskets due to its adaptability and aesthetic value.
