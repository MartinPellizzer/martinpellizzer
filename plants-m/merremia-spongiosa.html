Merremia spongiosa

Merremia spongiosa, commonly known as the sponge gourd, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and large, pale green, sponge-like fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically followed by the distinctive, inflated fruit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit.

The botanical features of Merremia spongiosa make it a notable species in the Convolvulaceae family.
