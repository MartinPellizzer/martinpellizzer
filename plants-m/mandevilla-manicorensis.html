Mandevilla manicorensis

Mandevilla manicorensis, commonly known as the Brazilian mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features woody stems and is known for its vigorous growth habit, often reaching several meters in height. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and plenty of sunlight.

Mandevilla manicorensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking floral display.
