Mimulus glandulosus

Mimulus glandulosus, commonly known as the sticky monkeyflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its glandular-stemmed structure, which gives it a sticky texture. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers with four petals, typically arranged in a showy display. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Mimulus glandulosus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and streambanks.
