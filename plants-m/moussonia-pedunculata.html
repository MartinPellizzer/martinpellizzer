Moussonia pedunculata

Moussonia pedunculata, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a waxy texture, and it produces small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of purple, pink, and white.

Moussonia pedunculata is widely cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
