Miconia cuneifolia

Miconia cuneifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or tropical hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to red bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are often overlooked due to the prominent bracts. Miconia cuneifolia is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage and floral display.
