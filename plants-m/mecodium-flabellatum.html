Mecodium flabellatum

Mecodium flabellatum, commonly known as the fanleaf mecodium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, often with a bluish or purplish hue. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xerophytic gardens.

Its unique foliage and compact growth make it an attractive addition to rock gardens and arid landscapes.
