Maxillaria nervosa

Maxillaria nervosa, commonly known as the nervy maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces fragrant, showy flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically features dark green, velvety leaves and has a pseudobulb structure typical of many orchids. Its flowers are usually yellow or green with red or purple markings, and they bloom in late winter to early spring.

Maxillaria nervosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
