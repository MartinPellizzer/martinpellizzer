Malus × purpurea var. eleyi

Malus × purpurea var. eleyi, commonly known as the Eleyi crabapple, is a cultivar of the apple tree, characterized by its ornamental value.

It is a hybrid species within the genus Malus, known for its vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, providing visual interest throughout the growing season.

The fruit, while not typically edible, is small and ornamental, often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
