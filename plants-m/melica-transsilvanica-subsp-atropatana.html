Melica transsilvanica subsp. atropatana

Melica transsilvanica subsp.

atropatana, commonly known as the Transylvanian melic, is a perennial grass species native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.

The plant has slender, erect stems and produces spikelets that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators and soil stability.
