Maranthes polyandra

Maranthes polyandra, commonly known as the rainbow ginger, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful inflorescences that resemble a cluster of tiny flowers, often displaying shades of red, yellow, and orange. The plant features long, strap-like leaves that are glossy and can grow up to several feet in length. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Maranthes polyandra is typically grown as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings due to its sensitivity to direct sunlight.
