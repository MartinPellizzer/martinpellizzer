Myrcia gomidesioides

Myrcia gomidesioides, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it typically grows in humid, tropical environments. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that contain several seeds.

Myrcia gomidesioides is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its aromatic properties.
