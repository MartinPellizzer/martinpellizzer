Microchilus xeranthum

Microchilus xeranthum, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its compact, succulent growth habit and fleshy, waxy leaves that help it conserve water. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to harsh desert environments.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
