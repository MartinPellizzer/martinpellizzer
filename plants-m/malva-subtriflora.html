Malva subtriflora

Malva subtriflora, commonly known as the narrowleaf mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus, and is characterized by its hairy stems and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Malva subtriflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
