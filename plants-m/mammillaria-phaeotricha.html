Mammillaria phaeotricha

Mammillaria phaeotricha, commonly known as the pale-hairy mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and dense covering of pale yellow to white spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a houseplant.
