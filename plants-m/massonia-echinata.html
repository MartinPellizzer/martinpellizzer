Massonia echinata

Massonia echinata, commonly known as the horned massonia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, cylindrical stems that are often branched and covered in small, spiny tubercles. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and it is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Massonia echinata is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
