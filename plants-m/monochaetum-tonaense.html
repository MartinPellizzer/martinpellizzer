Monochaetum tonaense

Monochaetum tonaense, commonly known as the Tonaense spikegrass, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale yellow flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
