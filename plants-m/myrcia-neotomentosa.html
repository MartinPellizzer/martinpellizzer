Myrcia neotomentosa

Myrcia neotomentosa, commonly known as the woolly myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and leathery, glossy leaves. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Myrcia neotomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
