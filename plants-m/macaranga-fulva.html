Macaranga fulva

Macaranga fulva, commonly known as the white sycamore, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by green, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The tree is also notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed habitats.
