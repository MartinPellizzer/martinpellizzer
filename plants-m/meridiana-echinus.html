Meridiana echinus

Meridiana echinus, commonly known as the spiny hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, woody stems and large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, hairy seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it both visually striking and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
