Mussaenda nannanii

Mussaenda nannanii, commonly known as the golden trumpet bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble trumpets, which are highly attractive to pollinators. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Mussaenda nannanii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
