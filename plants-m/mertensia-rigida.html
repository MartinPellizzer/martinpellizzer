Mertensia rigida

Mertensia rigida, commonly known as bluebells, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and often turn reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Mertensia rigida is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
