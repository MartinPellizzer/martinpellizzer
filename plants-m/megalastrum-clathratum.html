Megalastrum clathratum

Megalastrum clathratum, commonly known as the coral tree, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, coral-like inflorescences that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has a woody stem with a fibrous, spongy texture, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy surface. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
