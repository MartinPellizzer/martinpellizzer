Mentzelia multicaulis var. uintahensis

Mentzelia multicaulis var. uintahensis, commonly known as Uintah pricklymint, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

This variant is distinguished by its more robust growth form compared to other Mentzelia species.
