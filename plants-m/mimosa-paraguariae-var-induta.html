Mimosa paraguariae var. induta

Mimosa paraguariae var. induta, commonly known as the velvet mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage and clusters of small, yellow, spherical flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a unique texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for warm climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
