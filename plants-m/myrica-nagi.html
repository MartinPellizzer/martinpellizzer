Myrica nagi

Myrica nagi, commonly known as the Japanese wax tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Myricaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces waxy, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Myrica nagi thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate regions.
