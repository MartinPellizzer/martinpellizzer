Miconia andreana

Miconia andreana, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit tree, is a member of the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a bright yellow, fleshy berry that contains numerous seeds.

Miconia andreana is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other tropical regions for horticultural purposes.
