Mentha hirsuta var. subspicata

Mentha hirsuta var. subspicata, commonly known as the hairy mint or woolly mint, is a variant of the mint plant native to regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and a strong, aromatic scent. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowering stems bear small, tubular flowers that are often clustered at the tips.

The botanical features of this variety make it a distinctive and valuable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
