Millettia oblata subsp. oblata

Millettia oblata subsp.

oblata, commonly known as the Chinese flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring.

The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges and produces long, slender pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in soil enrichment.
