Monarthrocarpus dolabriformis

Monarthrocarpus dolabriformis, commonly known as the sickle-shaped liverwort, is a species of leafy liverwort belonging to the family Porellaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, sickle-shaped thallus that is often flattened and covered with small, rounded lobes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, known as gemmae, are produced in clusters on the upper surface of the thallus.

This liverwort is widely distributed in tropical and subtropical regions and plays a role in soil stabilization and nutrient cycling in its habitat.
