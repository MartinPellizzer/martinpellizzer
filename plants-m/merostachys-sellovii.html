Merostachys sellovii

Merostachys sellovii, commonly known as the Sello's grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated as an ornamental plant.
