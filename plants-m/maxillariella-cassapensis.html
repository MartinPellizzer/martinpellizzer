Maxillariella cassapensis

Maxillariella cassapensis, commonly known as the Cassa's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structure. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a yellow or greenish throat. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Maxillariella cassapensis is appreciated for its ornamental value and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique beauty.
