Melampodium ternatum

Melampodium ternatum, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive purple band around the base.

Melampodium ternatum is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
