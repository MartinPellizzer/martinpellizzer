Malva pseudoborealis

Malva pseudoborealis, commonly known as the Arctic mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy pink or purple flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, rocky soils and is often found in tundra and alpine environments.

Malva pseudoborealis is adapted to cold climates and has a slow growth rate due to its harsh habitat.
