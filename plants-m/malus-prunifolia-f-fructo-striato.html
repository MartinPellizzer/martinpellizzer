Malus prunifolia f. fructo-striato

Malus prunifolia f. fructo-striato, commonly known as the striped-fruited apple, is a botanical variant of the apple tree characterized by its distinctive fruit features.

The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of Central Asia. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals. The most notable botanical trait is the striped pattern on the skin of its fruits, which sets it apart from other apple cultivars.

This variety is primarily valued for its ornamental qualities and unique fruit appearance.
