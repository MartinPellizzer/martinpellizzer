Myosotis laxa subsp. cespitosa

Myosotis laxa subsp.

cespitosa, commonly known as the clustered forget-me-not, is a herbaceous perennial native to New Zealand. It features a low-growing, mat-forming habit with dense clusters of small, blue to violet flowers.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and its stems are usually prostrate or ascending. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest margins and wetlands.

The flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
