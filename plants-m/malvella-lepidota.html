Malvella lepidota

Malvella lepidota, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus, and is known for its soft, hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive appearance and unique foliage.
