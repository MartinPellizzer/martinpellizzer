Monochilon

Monochilon, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and swamps. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Monochilon produces small purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.
