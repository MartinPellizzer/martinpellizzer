Mucuna junghuhniana

Mucuna junghuhniana, commonly known as the jungle velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It produces dense, velvety foliage and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and prominent, hairy tendrils. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are typically small and white or pink, followed by pod fruits containing angular, flat seeds.

Mucuna junghuhniana is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing properties and use in traditional medicine.
