Mammillaria pondii subsp. setispina

Mammillaria pondii subsp.

setispina, commonly known as the Spiny Mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in white, woolly spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.
