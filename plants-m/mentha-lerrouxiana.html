Mentha lerrouxiana

Mentha lerrouxiana, commonly known as the lesser mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and has small, opposite leaves with a distinctive minty aroma. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and are arranged in whorls along the stem.

Mentha lerrouxiana is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
