Metagentiana rhodantha

Metagentiana rhodantha, commonly known as the red-flowered metagentian, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals and yellow central disk, which attract a variety of pollinators. Native to the mountainous regions of South America, this plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture that helps reduce water loss.

The plant produces numerous small seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation across diverse habitats.
