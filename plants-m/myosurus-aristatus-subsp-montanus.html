Myosurus aristatus subsp. montanus

Myosurus aristatus subsp.

montanus, commonly known as the mountain mosquito plant, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
