Myrcia castrensis

Myrcia castrensis, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a capsule containing numerous tiny, black seeds.

Myrcia castrensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in folk medicine.
