Miconia leasylvatica

Miconia leasylvatica, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Miconia leasylvatica*, is a species of flowering plant in the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest biome of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical conditions. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. It produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various bird species.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
