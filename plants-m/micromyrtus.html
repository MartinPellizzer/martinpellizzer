Micromyrtus

Micromyrtus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf myrtle, is a genus of flowering plants in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and aromatic foliage. The plants typically grow as shrubs or small trees, often found in coastal or sandy habitats. Micromyrtus species produce small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink.

These plants are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes used in horticulture.
