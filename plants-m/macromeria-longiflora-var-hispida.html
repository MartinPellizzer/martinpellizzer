Macromeria longiflora var. hispida

Macromeria longiflora var. hispida, commonly known as the hispid macromeria, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving the plant a rough texture. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its resilience to drought conditions.
