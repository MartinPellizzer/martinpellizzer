Medicago rugosa f. incisa

Medicago rugosa f. incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed clover, is a perennial legume characterized by its slender, serrated leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to regions of Europe and Asia. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment and pasture systems.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a useful forage plant in various agricultural settings.
