Mimulopsis excellens

Mimulopsis excellens, commonly known as the golden star flower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its root system is fibrous, allowing it to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.

Mimulopsis excellens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
