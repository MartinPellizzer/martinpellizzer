Melilotus macrorhizus

Melilotus macrorhizus, commonly known as large-rooted sweet clover, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a deep, taproot system that allows it to thrive in well-drained soils. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.

It is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural settings.
