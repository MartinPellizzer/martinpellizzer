Malva ruderalis

Malva ruderalis, commonly known as the common mallow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and abandoned fields. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture. Its flowers are pink or purple, with five petals and a distinctive central column.

Malva ruderalis is known for its adaptability and resilience in various environmental conditions.
