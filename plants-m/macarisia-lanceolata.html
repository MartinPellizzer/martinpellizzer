Macarisia lanceolata

Macarisia lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed daisy, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with white ray florets. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders. Its foliage is often covered in fine, silky hairs, adding texture to its appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
