Malus microcarpa

Malus microcarpa, commonly known as the Chinese white apple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, yellow or red fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it has a rounded, compact growth habit.

Malus microcarpa is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive spring blossoms and autumn foliage.
