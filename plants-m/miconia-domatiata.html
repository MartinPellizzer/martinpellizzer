Miconia domatiata

Miconia domatiata, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, and it is sometimes used in local cuisines.

Miconia domatiata is also known for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
