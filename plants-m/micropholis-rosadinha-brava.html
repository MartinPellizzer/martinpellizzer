Micropholis rosadinha-brava

Micropholis rosadinha-brava, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood.

It is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil. The plant has smooth, dark-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy green surface. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and white or pale pink, appearing in clusters.

The tree is valued for its hard, dense wood, which is used in musical instruments and furniture.
