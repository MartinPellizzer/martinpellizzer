Macfadyena mollis

Macfadyena mollis, commonly known as the sweet shower vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped seed pods that split open to release seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Macfadyena mollis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
