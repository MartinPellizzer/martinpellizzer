Mimusops petiolaris

Mimusops petiolaris, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds. The tree is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in furniture and construction.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland forest areas.
