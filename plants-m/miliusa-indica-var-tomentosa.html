Miliusa indica var. tomentosa

Miliusa indica var. tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy miliusa, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, round fruits resembling small citrus oranges. The leaves are thick and leathery, with a waxy texture, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
