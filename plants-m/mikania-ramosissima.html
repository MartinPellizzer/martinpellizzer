Mikania ramosissima

Mikania ramosissima, commonly known as the Mexican devil's ivy, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture and produces small, yellowish flowers. It is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and forests, due to its adaptability.

Its botanical features make it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in certain regions.
