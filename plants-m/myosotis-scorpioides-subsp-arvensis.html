Myosotis scorpioides subsp. arvensis

Myosotis scorpioides subsp.

arvensis, commonly known as the field forget-me-not, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in fields, meadows, and along roadsides.

The plant features slender, branching stems with opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are usually hairy. Its flowers are blue to purple, with five petals and a distinctive spur, blooming from late spring to early autumn.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
