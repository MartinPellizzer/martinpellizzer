Myosotis cyanea

Myosotis cyanea, commonly known as the blue-eyed Mary, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slight pubescence.

Myosotis cyanea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
