Malva purpurea

Malva purpurea, commonly known as the purple mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features broad, velvety leaves with a deep purple underside and produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus, and is characterized by its hairy stems and capsule fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Malva purpurea is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat skin conditions and respiratory ailments.
