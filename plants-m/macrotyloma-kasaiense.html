Macrotyloma kasaiense

Macrotyloma kasaiense, commonly known as the Kasai cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to Central Africa.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used as a forage crop for livestock.

Its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil fertility in agricultural systems.
