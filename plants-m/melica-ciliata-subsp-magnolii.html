Melica ciliata subsp. magnolii

Melica ciliata subsp.

magnolii, commonly known as the magnolia grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and the Americas. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
