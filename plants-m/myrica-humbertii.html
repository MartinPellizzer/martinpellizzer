Myrica humbertii

Myrica humbertii, commonly known as the Humbert wax myrtle, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Myricaceae family and is characterized by its small, wax-coated leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or yellowish in color. Its fruit is a dark, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.

Myrica humbertii is adapted to grow in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal areas.
