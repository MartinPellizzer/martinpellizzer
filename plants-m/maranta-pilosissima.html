Maranta pilosissima

Maranta pilosissima, commonly known as the velvetleaf arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family, which includes many ornamental and economically important species. The plant is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often used in horticulture for their aesthetic appeal. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or red in color.

The plant is adapted to grow in humid, shaded environments, making it a popular choice for indoor and tropical gardens.
