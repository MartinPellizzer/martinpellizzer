Macleania nervosa

Macleania nervosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, pale pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with multiple stems and dense foliage.

Macleania nervosa is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and along stream banks.
