Mentha arvensis subsp. haplocalyx

Mentha arvensis subsp.

haplocalyx, commonly known as field mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or violet, arranged in loose spikes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in disturbed areas.

This subspecies is known for its aromatic leaves, which contain essential oils used in herbal remedies and flavoring.
