Miconia humilis

Miconia humilis, commonly known as the dwarf miconia, is a small shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically covered in soft, hairy structures. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in warm climates as a decorative plant.
