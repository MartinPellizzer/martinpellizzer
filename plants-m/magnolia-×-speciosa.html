Magnolia × speciosa

Magnolia × speciosa, commonly known as the Chinese magnolia, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Magnolia denudata and Magnolia liliiflora.

It is native to eastern China and is characterized by its large, showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows as a deciduous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and have a smooth, dark green upper surface.

The flowers emit a sweet fragrance and are an important feature in ornamental horticulture.
