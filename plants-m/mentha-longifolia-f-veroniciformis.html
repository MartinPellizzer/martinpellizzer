Mentha longifolia f. veroniciformis

Mentha longifolia f. veroniciformis, commonly known as the Veronica-leaved mint, is a subspecies of the common mint plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rounded leaves that resemble those of the plant Veronica, giving it its common name. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates. It produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves and is used in herbal teas and culinary applications.
