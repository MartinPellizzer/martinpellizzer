Memecylon rivulare

Memecylon rivulare, commonly known as the river memecylon, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in wet, lowland forests. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, round berries that are edible.

Memecylon rivulare plays a role in local ecosystems and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
