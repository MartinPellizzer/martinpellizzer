Melampodium dombeyanum

Melampodium dombeyanum, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It features bright orange to yellow ray flowers that resemble sunflowers, with a distinctive yellow center. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical name reflects its flowering characteristics, with "melampodium" meaning "cup-like flower.".
