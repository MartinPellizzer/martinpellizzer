Medicago sauvagei

Medicago sauvagei, commonly known as the wild medic, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is often found in dry, disturbed soils and can thrive in arid environments. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in poor soil conditions.

Medicago sauvagei is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
