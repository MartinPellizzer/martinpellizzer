Mentha × fraseri

Mentha × fraseri, commonly known as peppermint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Mentha spicata and Mentha viridiflora.

It is characterized by its strong, aromatic scent and is widely cultivated for its essential oils. The plant has square stems, opposite leaves, and produces small, pale purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Mentha × fraseri is a perennial herb native to Europe and has been naturalized in many parts of the world.
