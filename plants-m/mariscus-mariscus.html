Mariscus mariscus

Mariscus mariscus, commonly known as the Mediterranean marshmallow, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or marshy habitats across southern Europe and parts of North Africa. The plant features erect, hairy stems and produces clusters of pink or purple flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fleshy and adapted to store water.

Mariscus mariscus is noted for its ornamental value and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
