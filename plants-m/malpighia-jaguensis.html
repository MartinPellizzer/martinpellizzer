Malpighia jaguensis

Malpighia jaguensis, commonly known as the jaguena or yellow mamey, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its small, fleshy, yellow-orange fruits that are edible. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or red in color. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
