Myrceugenia bracteosa var. australis

Myrceugenia bracteosa var. australis, commonly known as the southern myrtle, is a species native to southern South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in humid, temperate climates. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in inflorescences, while the fruit is a small, woody capsule containing several seeds.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
