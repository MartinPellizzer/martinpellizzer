Medicago lupulina var. glanduligera

Medicago lupulina var. glanduligera, commonly known as the glandular brome, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by slender, cylindrical pods. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its glandular features on the leaves and stems contribute to its unique botanical identity.
