Mangifera macrocarpa

Mangifera macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant mango, is a species of flowering plant in the Anacardiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, elongated fruits that can weigh up to 10 kilograms. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is typically yellow when ripe and has a sweet, aromatic pulp.

The tree is valued for its fruit and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
