Melastoma dodecandrum

Melastoma dodecandrum, commonly known as the 12-flowered melastoma, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.

Melastoma dodecandrum is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
