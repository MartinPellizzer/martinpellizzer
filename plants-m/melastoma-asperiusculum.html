Melastoma asperiusculum

Melastoma asperiusculum, commonly known as the rough-leaved melastoma, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features small, oval leaves with a rough, textured surface, and its flowers are typically red or purple, arranged in clusters. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
