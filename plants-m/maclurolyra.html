Maclurolyra

Maclurolyra, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a deciduous plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from a dense covering of fine, reflective hairs on the leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to thrive in dry, sandy soils.

The silverleaf tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to stabilize desert landscapes.
