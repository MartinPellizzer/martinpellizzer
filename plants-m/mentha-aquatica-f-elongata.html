Mentha aquatica f. elongata

Mentha aquatica f. elongata, commonly known as the long-leaf water mint, is a variant of the water mint species native to moist, often shaded environments.

It is characterized by its elongated leaves and aromatic, slightly minty scent. The plant typically grows in wetlands, stream banks, and other water-adjacent habitats. Its flowering stems bear small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
