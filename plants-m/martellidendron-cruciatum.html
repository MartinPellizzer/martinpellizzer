Martellidendron cruciatum

Martellidendron cruciatum, commonly known as the cross-leaved mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive cross-shaped leaf lobes, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance contribute to its popularity among gardeners.
