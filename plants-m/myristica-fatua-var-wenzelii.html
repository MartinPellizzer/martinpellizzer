Myristica fatua var. wenzelii

Myristica fatua var. wenzelii, commonly known as the Wenzel's nutmeg, is a rare tropical plant belonging to the Myristicaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its aromatic, woody fruit that contains a highly valued seed kernel. The plant produces small, white flowers and has compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets. Its habitat is typically in lowland rainforests, where it thrives in humid, shaded conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and rarity, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
