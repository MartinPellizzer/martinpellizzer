Miconia thysanophylla

Miconia thysanophylla, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a round, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

Miconia thysanophylla is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it an invasive species in some regions.
