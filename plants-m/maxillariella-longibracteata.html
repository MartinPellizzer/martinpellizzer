Maxillariella longibracteata

Maxillariella longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract orchid, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb that supports its growth in the humid, shaded environment. The plant produces slender, green stems with ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, with elongated bracts that give the species its common name.

Maxillariella longibracteata is known for its delicate appearance and is often found growing in clusters on the forest floor.
