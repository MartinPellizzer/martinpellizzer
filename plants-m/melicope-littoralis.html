Melicope littoralis

Melicope littoralis, commonly known as the coastal melicope, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along shorelines, displaying a compact growth habit with glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in saline and coastal environments.

Melicope littoralis plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and supporting local biodiversity.
