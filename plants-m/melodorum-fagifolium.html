Melodorum fagifolium

Melodorum fagifolium, commonly known as the silverleaf mallow, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, silver-veined leaves that give it its common name, and it produces showy, pinkish-white flowers in the spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its botanical characteristics include opposite leaf arrangement and a hairy stem, which help distinguish it from similar species.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
