Mimosa cafesiana

Mimosa cafesiana, commonly known as the coffee mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, milky sap that can be toxic if ingested. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses worldwide.
