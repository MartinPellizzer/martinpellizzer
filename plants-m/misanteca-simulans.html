Misanteca simulans

Misanteca simulans, commonly known as the false mimosa, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in sandy or coastal soils. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can remain viable for several years.

Misanteca simulans is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
