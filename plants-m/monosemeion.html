Monosemeion

Monosemeion, commonly known as the "lunar flower," is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white petals that open only under moonlight, a phenomenon attributed to its unique phototropic response. The plant's leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water in its harsh environment. Its flowers emit a faint, sweet fragrance that is most noticeable during the night.

Due to its elusive nature and limited distribution, the Monosemeion remains a subject of fascination for botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
