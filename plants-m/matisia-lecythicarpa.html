Matisia lecythicarpa

Matisia lecythicarpa, commonly known as the yellow matisia, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used for their essential oils, which have antimicrobial properties.

The tree is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
