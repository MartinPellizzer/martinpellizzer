Myrcia lechleriana

Myrcia lechleriana, commonly known as the lechleriana myrtle, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
