Monotaxis linifolia var. cuneata

Monotaxis linifolia var. cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved monotaxis, is a variant of the monotaxis plant native to specific regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in a dense, grass-like manner. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or pale in color.

This variant is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features and ecological adaptations.
