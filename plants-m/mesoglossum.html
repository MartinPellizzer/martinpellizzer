Mesoglossum

Mesoglossum, commonly known as the middle lip of the orchid flower, is a specialized structure found in the orchid genus.

It is a modified petal that plays a crucial role in pollination by guiding pollinators toward the reproductive organs. The mesoglossum is often adorned with intricate patterns and colors that enhance its visual appeal and functional efficiency. It is typically located between the labellum and the column, serving as a bridge for pollinators.

Botanically, it is a key feature in the orchid's reproductive strategy and floral morphology.
