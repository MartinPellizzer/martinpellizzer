Myrceugenia seriatoramosa

Myrceugenia seriatoramosa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, where it can become invasive.
