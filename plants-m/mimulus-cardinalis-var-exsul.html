Mimulus cardinalis var. exsul

Mimulus cardinalis var. exsul, commonly known as the scarlet monkeyflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant red-orange flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as streambanks and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
