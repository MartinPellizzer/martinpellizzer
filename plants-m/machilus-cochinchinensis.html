Machilus cochinchinensis

Machilus cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Chinese mahogany, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves that are glossy on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by dark purple berries. Its wood is valued for its durability and fine grain, making it useful in furniture and construction.

The plant also has medicinal uses in traditional Chinese medicine.
