Macaranga depressa var. genuina

Macaranga depressa var. genuina, commonly known as the broadleaf paper plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in umbels, and its fruits are three-winged capsules. It is commonly found in moist, shaded areas of lowland forests.
