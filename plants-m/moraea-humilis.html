Moraea humilis

Moraea humilis, commonly known as the dwarf moraea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, delicate flowers. The flowers are typically blue, purple, or white, with a distinctive three-lobed structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
