Meineckia vestita

Meineckia vestita, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, three-lobed seed pods. Meineckia vestita thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
