Muscari comosum

Muscari comosum, commonly known as the grape hyacinth, is a bulbous plant native to southern Europe and the Middle East.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, resembling grapes, hence its common name. The plant produces a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.

Muscari comosum is also valued for its ability to naturalize and form dense stands over time.
