Melocactus flavispinus

Melocactus flavispinus, commonly known as the yellow-spined melon cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and bright yellow spines that give it its common name. The plant produces a single, large, pale yellow or green fruit, which is edible and has a mild, sweet flavor. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sun exposure.

The botanical features of Melocactus flavispinus make it an attractive and resilient plant for xeriscaping and succulent gardens.
