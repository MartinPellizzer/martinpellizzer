Matricaria pectinata

Matricaria pectinata, commonly known as the white yarrow, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features a compact, bushy growth habit with silver-gray, pinnately divided leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is often found in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties.

Matricaria pectinata is also cultivated as a ornamental plant for its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
