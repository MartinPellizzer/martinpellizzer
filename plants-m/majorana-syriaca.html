Majorana syriaca

Majorana syriaca, commonly known as sweet fennel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in umbrella-like clusters. The plant produces aromatic seeds that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its essential oils contain compounds like limonene and anethole, which contribute to its distinctive fragrance.

Majorana syriaca thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for herb gardens.
