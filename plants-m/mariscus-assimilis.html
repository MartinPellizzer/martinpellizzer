Mariscus assimilis

Mariscus assimilis, commonly known as the Mexican spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and bear the showy flowers.

Mariscus assimilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
