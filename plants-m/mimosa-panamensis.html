Mimosa panamensis

Mimosa panamensis, commonly known as the Panama mimosa, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Mimosa panamensis is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets.
