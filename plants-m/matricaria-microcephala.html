Matricaria microcephala

Matricaria microcephala, commonly known as the small-headed chamomile, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features finely divided, gray-green leaves and produces yellow flowers with white ray florets. It is often cultivated for its aromatic properties and used in herbal remedies.

Matricaria microcephala thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, ornamental plant.
