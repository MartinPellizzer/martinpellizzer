Myrcia pulchra

Myrcia pulchra, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Myrcia pulchra thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and dense forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in warm regions.
