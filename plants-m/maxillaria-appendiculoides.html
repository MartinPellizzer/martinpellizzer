Maxillaria appendiculoides

Maxillaria appendiculoides, commonly known as the orchid with the hooked lip, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are ovate and arranged in a fan-like pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The unique, spurred lip of the flower is a distinguishing feature, aiding in pollination by attracting specific insect pollinators.
