Melastoma kahayanense

Melastoma kahayanense, commonly known as the Kahayan melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in Indonesia and Malaysia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its erect or shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces showy, often pink or red flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Melastoma kahayanense is also known for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments, making it a common species in tropical rainforests.
