Malva lucida

Malva lucida, commonly known as the silky mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other familiar species like hibiscus and cotton. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a native, low-maintenance plant.
