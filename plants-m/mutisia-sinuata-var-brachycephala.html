Mutisia sinuata var. brachycephala

Mutisia sinuata var. brachycephala, commonly known as the narrow-headed mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a short, sturdy stem with broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in compact clusters, and bloom during the warmer months.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.
