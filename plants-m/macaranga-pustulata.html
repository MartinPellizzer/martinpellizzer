Macaranga pustulata

Macaranga pustulata, commonly known as the hairy paper tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a distinctive pubescent surface. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various organisms.
