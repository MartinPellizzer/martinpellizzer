Malus longepedunculata

Malus longepedunculata, commonly known as the long pedunculate apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pink flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, edible apples that are typically yellow or red in color. Native to eastern Asia, it thrives in temperate climates and well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional horticulture.
