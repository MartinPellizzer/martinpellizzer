Myosurus aristatus var. apetalus

Myosurus aristatus var. apetalus, commonly known as the lesser marsh woundwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist, often calcareous habitats in Europe.

It typically grows in wet meadows, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and ditches. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often reddish in color, with alternate, lanceolate leaves that are usually hairless. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, and are typically greenish or pale yellow.

This variety is distinguished by the absence of petals, giving it its scientific name "apetalus.".
