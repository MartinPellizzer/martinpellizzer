Medicago turbinata subvar. aculeata

Medicago turbinata subvar. aculeata, commonly known as the aculeate sainfoin, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves with a distinctive, hairy texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, raceme-like clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

This subvariety is often used in forage and soil stabilization due to its hardiness and nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
