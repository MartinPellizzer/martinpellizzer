Melastoma heterobase

Melastoma heterobase, commonly known as the variable mallow, is a tropical shrub belonging to the family Melastomataceae.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is often found in moist, shaded forest understories.
