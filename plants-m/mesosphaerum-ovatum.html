Mesosphaerum ovatum

Mesosphaerum ovatum, commonly known as the oval globemallow, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

Mesosphaerum ovatum is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
