Mimosa filipetiola

Mimosa filipetiola, commonly known as the sensitive plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched, a response triggered by rapid changes in turgor pressure within the leaf cells. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and greenhouses.

Its unique movement and delicate appearance make it a fascinating subject for both botanical study and horticultural display.
