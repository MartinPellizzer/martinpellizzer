Maxillaria curtipes

Maxillaria curtipes, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety pink flowers with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

Maxillaria curtipes is valued for its ornamental beauty and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
