Morus alba subvar. macrophylla

Morus alba subvar. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf mulberry, is a variant of the white mulberry native to China.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 20 cm in length. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called mulberries, which are typically white or pink in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of silk production.

The leaves are also used in traditional medicine and as feed for silkworms.
