Macrorhynchus pterocarpus

Macrorhynchus pterocarpus, commonly known as the ironwood tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree has a straight trunk and a broad, spreading canopy with compound leaves. Its flowers are small and yellowish, and it produces woody pods containing seeds.

The plant is often found in lowland rainforests and is important for both ecological and economic reasons.
