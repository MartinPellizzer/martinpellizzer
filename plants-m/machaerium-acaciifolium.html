Machaerium acaciifolium

Machaerium acaciifolium, commonly known as the acacia-leaf machaerium, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow or white flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. Its seeds are enclosed in small, leathery pods, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Machaerium acaciifolium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
