Myrcia perforata

Myrcia perforata, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, red, fleshy berries. Myrcia perforata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and fragrant blooms.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
