Millettia albiflora

Millettia albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered millettia, is a species of legume native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
