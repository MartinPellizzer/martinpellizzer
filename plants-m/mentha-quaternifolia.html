Mentha quaternifolia

Mentha quaternifolia, commonly known as Japanese mint, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are often rounded or heart-shaped. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Mentha quaternifolia is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
