Melilotus dentatus var. sibiricus

Melilotus dentatus var. sibiricus, commonly known as Siberian sweet clover, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a taproot system and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in meadows and disturbed sites. It is known for its high nectar content, making it an important plant for pollinators.

Additionally, it has been used historically in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
