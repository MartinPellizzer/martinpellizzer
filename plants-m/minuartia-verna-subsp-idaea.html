Minuartia verna subsp. idaea

Minuartia verna subsp.

idaea, commonly known as the spring rockcress, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to alpine and arctic regions. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in early spring.

The plant has succulent, often hairy leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold climates.

This subspecies is often found in mountainous areas across Europe and parts of Asia.
