Mitolepis arbuscula

Mitolepis arbuscula, commonly known as the dwarf dandelion, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Mitolepis arbuscula is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators.
