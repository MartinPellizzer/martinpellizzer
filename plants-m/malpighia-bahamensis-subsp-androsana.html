Malpighia bahamensis subsp. androsana

Malpighia bahamensis subsp.

androsana, commonly known as the Andros beautyberry, is a small shrub native to the Bahamas. It is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The plant produces bright red, berry-like fruits that are edible and often consumed by local wildlife. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and salt spray.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological role and limited distribution.
