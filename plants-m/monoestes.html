Monoestes

Monoestes, commonly known as the "one-flower plant," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Acanthaceae.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which typically features a single, showy flower per stem. These plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Africa and the Americas. They often grow in shaded, moist environments and are valued for their ornamental appeal.

The genus includes several species, many of which are cultivated for their vibrant blooms and ease of care.
