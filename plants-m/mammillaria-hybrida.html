Mammillaria hybrida

Mammillaria hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a dense covering of small, white spines. The plant typically produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and collections.
