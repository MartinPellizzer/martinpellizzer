Melia

Melia, commonly known as the chinaberry tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Meliaceae.

It is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often ovate or lanceolate leaves. The flowers are typically small and yellow, arranged in panicles, and the fruit is a drupe with a smooth, yellowish exterior. Melia species are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

They are valued for their ornamental beauty and have also been used in traditional medicine.
