Myrcia neocambessedeana

Myrcia neocambessedeana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine and are also valued for their essential oils.

The fruit, which is small and berry-like, contains seeds that are dispersed by animals.
