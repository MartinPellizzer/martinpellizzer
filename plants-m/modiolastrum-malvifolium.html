Modiolastrum malvifolium

Modiolastrum malvifolium, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallows, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety down. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in native gardens.
