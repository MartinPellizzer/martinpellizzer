Mentzelia pumila var. humilis

Mentzelia pumila var. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf milkweed, is a low-growing annual plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features slender, branching stems covered in fine, white hairs, and small, pale green to yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has opposite, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other small insects.
