Myrtus virotii

Myrtus virotii, commonly known as the Canary Islands myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to the unique climate of the Canary Islands.

Myrtus virotii is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in local ecosystems.
