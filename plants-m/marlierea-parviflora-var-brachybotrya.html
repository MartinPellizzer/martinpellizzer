Marlierea parviflora var. brachybotrya

Marlierea parviflora var. brachybotrya, commonly known as the dwarf fruiting marlierea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
