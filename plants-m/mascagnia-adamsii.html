Mascagnia adamsii

Mascagnia adamsii, commonly known as the Adams' mescal bean, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of bean-like fruits. Its leaves are narrow and waxy, which helps reduce water loss in dry climates.

Mascagnia adamsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
