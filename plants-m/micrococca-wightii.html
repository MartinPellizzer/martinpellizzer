Micrococca wightii

Micrococca wightii, commonly known as the Indian star flower, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a small shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Micrococca wightii is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental beauty in gardens.
