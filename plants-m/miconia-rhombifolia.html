Miconia rhombifolia

Miconia rhombifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian shield tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains numerous seeds.

Miconia rhombifolia is known for its rapid growth and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.
