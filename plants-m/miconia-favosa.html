Miconia favosa

Miconia favosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine, velvet-like hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are followed by distinctive, spiky, red to purple berries. Miconia favosa is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and potentially problematic in non-native environments.
