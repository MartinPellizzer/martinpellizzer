Monarda commutata

Monarda commutata, commonly known as wild bergamot, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features aromatic, tubular flowers that are typically pink to lavender in color, arranged in showy clusters at the top of its hairy stems. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in moist woodlands and meadows, and it is a valuable nectar source for pollinators.

Monarda commutata is also known for its medicinal properties, with its dried leaves used in herbal remedies.
