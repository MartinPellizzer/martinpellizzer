Mesembryanthemum pellucidum

Mesembryanthemum pellucidum, commonly known as the glassy ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, translucent leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It is a member of the Aizoaceae family, known for its unique adaptations to harsh climates.

Its ability to survive in poor soil conditions makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
