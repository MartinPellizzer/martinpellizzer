Mammillaria collina

Mammillaria collina, commonly known as the Mexican snowball cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is covered in white, woolly spines that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by red, spherical fruits. Mammillaria collina is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique morphology and ease of care.
