Morella pensylvanica

Morella pensylvanica, commonly known as Pennsylvania waxnut, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green leaves and distinctive, waxy, spherical fruits that mature in the fall. The plant belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is adapted to acidic, well-drained soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects, and its seeds are dispersed by birds.

The plant is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
