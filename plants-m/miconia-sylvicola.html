Miconia sylvicola

Miconia sylvicola, commonly known as the forest miconia, is a species of flowering plant in the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in humid lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
