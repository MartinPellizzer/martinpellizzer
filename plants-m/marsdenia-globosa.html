Marsdenia globosa

Marsdenia globosa, commonly known as the globular milkweed, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its soft, succulent stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom in clusters. Its latex sap is milky and can be toxic, containing cardiac glycosides.

Marsdenia globosa is often found in humid, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands.
