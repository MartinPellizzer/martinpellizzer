Mesembryanthemum mutans

Mesembryanthemum mutans, commonly known as the ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves and vibrant, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with a unique ability to excrete salt through its leaves, a process known as halophytism. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The species is also known for its rapid growth and ability to propagate through both seeds and cuttings.
