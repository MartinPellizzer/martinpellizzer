Medicago sativa subsp. vulgaris

Medicago sativa subsp.

vulgaris, commonly known as common alfalfa, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant is known for its deep root system, which helps it thrive in arid conditions. It fixes nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria, making it valuable for soil enrichment.

This species is widely cultivated for forage and as a crop in agriculture.
