Malva nuttalloides

Malva nuttalloides, commonly known as the Nuttall's mallows, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, accompanied by heart-shaped leaves with a soft, fuzzy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
