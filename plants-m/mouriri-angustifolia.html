Mouriri angustifolia

Mouriri angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mouriri, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters and has elliptical, glossy, dark green leaves that are often narrow and pointed. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-yellow fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in undergrowth along forest edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
