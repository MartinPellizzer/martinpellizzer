Moricandia arvensis var. fabariifolia

Moricandia arvensis var. fabariifolia, commonly known as the field moricandia, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is an annual herb characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers with four petals, typical of the Brassicaceae family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its seeds are small and can remain viable in the soil for several years, contributing to its persistent presence in various habitats.
