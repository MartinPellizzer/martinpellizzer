Mangifera quadrifida var. spathulifolia

Mangifera quadrifida var. spathulifolia, commonly known as the spoonleaf mango, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its broad, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that eventually develop into edible mango fruits. Its ornamental value is also notable due to its attractive foliage.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
