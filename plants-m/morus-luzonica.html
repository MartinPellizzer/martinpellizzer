Morus luzonica

Morus luzonica, commonly known as the Luzon mulberry, is a species of mulberry native to the Philippines.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically consumed by local wildlife. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the Moraceae family and is valued for its ecological and potential medicinal properties.
