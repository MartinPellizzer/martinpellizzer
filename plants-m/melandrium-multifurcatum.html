Melandrium multifurcatum

Melandrium multifurcatum, commonly known as the forked catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, branched stems and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has alternate, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Melandrium multifurcatum is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy, clustered flowers.
