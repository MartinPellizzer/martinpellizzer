Miconia paludigena

Miconia paludigena, commonly known as the swamp miconia, is a herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It typically grows in marshy or flooded areas, adapting to waterlogged soils with its fibrous root system. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy layer to reduce water loss. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a distinctive white or pale pink coloration.

Miconia paludigena plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
