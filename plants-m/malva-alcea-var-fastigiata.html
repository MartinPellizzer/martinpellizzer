Malva alcea var. fastigiata

Malva alcea var. fastigiata, commonly known as the upright mallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright, branching stems that are covered in soft, fuzzy hairs. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with serrated edges and a velvety texture. Its showy flowers are typically pink to purple, with five petals and a central tuft of stamens.

This plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often grown for its ornamental value in gardens.
