Mahonia alexandri

Mahonia alexandri, commonly known as the Alexanders' mahonia, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically evergreen and have a leathery texture. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by blue-black berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

Mahonia alexandri is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
