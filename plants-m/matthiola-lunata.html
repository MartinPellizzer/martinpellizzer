Matthiola lunata

Matthiola lunata, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale purple flowers that open at night. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other flowering plants such as cabbages and mustards.

The moonflower is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal blooming habit.
