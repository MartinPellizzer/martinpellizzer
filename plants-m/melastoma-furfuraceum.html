Melastoma furfuraceum

Melastoma furfuraceum, commonly known as the hairy melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountainous regions. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red in color, with showy petals and prominent stamens. Its leaves are ovate, hairy, and often have a rough texture, contributing to its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
