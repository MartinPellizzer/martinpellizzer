Minuartia peploides

Minuartia peploides, commonly known as the white sand saxifrage, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in tundra and mountainous environments. The plant has small, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh climates. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens, adding to its ornamental appeal.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in extreme environments and is adapted to withstand cold temperatures and strong winds.
