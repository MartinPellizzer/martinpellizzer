Metalasia pungens

Metalasia pungens, commonly known as the spiked metalasia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in xeriscapes.
