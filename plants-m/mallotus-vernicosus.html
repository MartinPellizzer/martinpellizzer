Mallotus vernicosus

Mallotus vernicosus, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several flat, lens-shaped seeds. Its botanical features make it notable in both horticulture and ecological studies.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties.
