Mammillaria biglandulosa

Mammillaria biglandulosa, commonly known as the two-lobed milk barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white to pale green tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its compact size and unique appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus collections.
