Mespilus

Mespilus, commonly known as the common medlar, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its oval, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit, which is a pome, is typically harvested in autumn and has a unique texture that becomes softer with age.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
