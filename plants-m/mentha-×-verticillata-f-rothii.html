Mentha × verticillata f. rothii

Mentha × verticillata f. rothii, commonly known as the rothii water mint, is a hybrid perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in whorled clusters, typical of the Mentha genus. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and wet meadows.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers make it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
