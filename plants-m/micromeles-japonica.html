Micromeles japonica

Micromeles japonica, commonly known as Japanese false indigo, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces distinctive seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Micromeles japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
