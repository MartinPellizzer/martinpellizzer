Miconia thomasiana

Miconia thomasiana, commonly known as the Thomasiana miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a problematic species in certain regions.
