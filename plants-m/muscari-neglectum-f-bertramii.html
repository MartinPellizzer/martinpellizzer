Muscari neglectum f. bertramii

Muscari neglectum f. bertramii, commonly known as the Bertram's musk mallow, is a compact, bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that form a basal rosette, and its bulbs are covered in a fibrous, white coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
