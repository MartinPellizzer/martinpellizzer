Mandevilla immaculata

Mandevilla immaculata, commonly known as the white mandevilla, is a flowering vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and trailing habit, making it suitable for trellises or hanging baskets. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a slight fragrance. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Mandevilla immaculata thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
