Melodinus angustifolius

Melodinus angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved melodine, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in regions with low rainfall.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
