Moltkia suffruticosa

Moltkia suffruticosa, commonly known as the silverleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by dark blue berries in the fall. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and native plant gardens.
