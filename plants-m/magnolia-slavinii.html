Magnolia slavinii

Magnolia slavinii, commonly known as the Slavin's magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age.

Magnolia slavinii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
