Microstylis lloensis

Microstylis lloensis, commonly known as the Lloens lowland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or marshy areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and small, three-angled stems.

Microstylis lloensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to high-altitude environments.
