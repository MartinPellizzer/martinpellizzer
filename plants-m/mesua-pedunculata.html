Mesua pedunculata

Mesua pedunculata, commonly known as the Indian rose or red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth texture.

Mesua pedunculata is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
