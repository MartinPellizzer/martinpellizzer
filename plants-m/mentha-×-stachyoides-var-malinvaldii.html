Mentha × stachyoides var. malinvaldii

Mentha × stachyoides var. malinvaldii, commonly known as the Malinvaldii mint, is a hybrid species within the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves and is used in herbal teas and culinary applications.
