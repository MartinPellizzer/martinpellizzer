Miconia cookii

Miconia cookii, commonly known as the Cook Islands miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
