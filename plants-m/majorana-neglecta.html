Majorana neglecta

Majorana neglecta, commonly known as wild marjoram, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and emit a strong, spicy scent when crushed.

Majorana neglecta is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields.
