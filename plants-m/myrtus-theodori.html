Myrtus theodori

Myrtus theodori, commonly known as the Ethiopian myrtle, is a shrub native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are small, berry-like structures that contain aromatic seeds.

Myrtus theodori is valued for its fragrance and has been traditionally used in herbal remedies.
