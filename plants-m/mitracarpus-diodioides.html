Mitracarpus diodioides

Mitracarpus diodioides, commonly known as the yellow false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, fleshy leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a taproot system and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.

The fruit is a capsule that releases tiny seeds when mature, aiding in its dispersal.
