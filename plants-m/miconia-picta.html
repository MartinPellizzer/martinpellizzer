Miconia picta

Miconia picta, commonly known as the painted miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to red and purple. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers that are followed by distinctive, winged fruits. Miconia picta is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

It is also noted for its ornamental value in horticulture due to its striking foliage.
