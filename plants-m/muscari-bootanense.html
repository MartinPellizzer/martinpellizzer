Muscari bootanense

Muscari bootanense, commonly known as the Persian grape hyacinth, is a bulbous plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Turkey and Iran. The plant produces dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, resembling tiny grapes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Muscari bootanense is also valued for its ability to naturalize and return year after year, adding vibrant color to early spring gardens.
