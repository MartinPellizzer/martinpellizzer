Myriocephalus villosissimus

Myriocephalus villosissimus, commonly known as the hairy-headed corymbia, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique texture.

This species is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient addition to native gardens.
