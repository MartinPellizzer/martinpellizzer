Myrosmodes noemiae

Myrosmodes noemiae, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its delicate, yellowish-white inflorescences that resemble a misty cloud.

Native to the moist woodlands of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture, and emit a subtle, aromatic scent when crushed. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, Myrosmodes noemiae is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
