Marsdenia ecorpuscula

Marsdenia ecorpuscula, commonly known as the tiny body vine, is a member of the Asclepiadaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing herb that produces small, fragrant flowers with five lobes, typically in shades of white or pink. The plant has thin, wiry stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptical in shape. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its unique appearance and delicate flowers, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
