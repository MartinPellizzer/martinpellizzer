Miconia eurychinioides

Miconia eurychinioides, commonly known as the red miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. It is also known for its ability to form dense thickets, which can impact local ecosystems.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in certain regions.
