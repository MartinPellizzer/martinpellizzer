Marlimorimia warmingii

Marlimorimia warmingii, commonly known as the Brazilian flame vine, is a striking tropical plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically dark green in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Marlimorimia warmingii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of biodiversity in its native habitat.
