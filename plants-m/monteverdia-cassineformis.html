Monteverdia cassineformis

Monteverdia cassineformis, commonly known as the Mexican sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to southern Mexico.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, light gray bark that peels in thin layers. The tree has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland tropical forests.
