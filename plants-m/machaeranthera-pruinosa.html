Machaeranthera pruinosa

Machaeranthera pruinosa, commonly known as the pruinose blazingstar, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and southern United States.

It features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive, slightly sticky texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Its botanical name reflects its unique floral structure and the waxy coating on its stems and leaves.
