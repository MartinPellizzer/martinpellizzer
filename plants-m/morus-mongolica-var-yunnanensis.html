Morus mongolica var. yunnanensis

Morus mongolica var. yunnanensis, commonly known as the Yunnan mulberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southwestern China.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically pale green on the underside. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called syconia, which are often consumed by local wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to rocky or mountainous terrain.

This species is valued for its ecological role and potential use in traditional medicine.
