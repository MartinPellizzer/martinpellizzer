Musa × paradisiaca f. kilola

Musa × paradisiaca f. kilola, commonly known as the kilola banana, is a cultivar of the banana plant characterized by its unique fruit morphology and genetic composition.

It is a hybrid species within the Musa genus, typically resulting from crosses between different banana varieties. The plant features large, elongated leaves and produces clusters of fruit that are often smaller and more elongated compared to standard bananas. The fruit's peel is generally thinner and has a distinct flavor profile, making it popular in certain regional cuisines.

This variety is also valued for its resilience to certain diseases, contributing to its agricultural significance.
