Mahonia aquifolium f. albovariegata

Mahonia aquifolium f. albovariegata, commonly known as the white-veined mahonia, is a varietal form of the evergreen shrub Mahonia aquifolium.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, featuring glossy dark green leaves with white or yellow variegations along the veins. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by blue-black berries in the fall. It thrives in partial to full shade and is well-suited for woodland gardens.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
