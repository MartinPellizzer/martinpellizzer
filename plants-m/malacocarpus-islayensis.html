Malacocarpus islayensis

Malacocarpus islayensis, commonly known as the Islay mountain heath, is a shrub native to the Scottish Highlands.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as moorlands and rocky outcrops.

Malacocarpus islayensis plays an important ecological role in supporting local insect and bird populations.
