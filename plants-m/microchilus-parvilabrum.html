Microchilus parvilabrum

Microchilus parvilabrum, commonly known as the small-lipped microchilus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, small flowers with a unique labellum that is shorter than the other petals. Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, this species thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid with a short rhizome and a single, erect stem.

Its flowers are often pale green or yellowish, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.
