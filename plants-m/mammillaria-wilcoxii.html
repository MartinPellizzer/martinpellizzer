Mammillaria wilcoxii

Mammillaria wilcoxii, commonly known as the Wilcox mammillaria, is a small cactus native to the Sonoran Desert in Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are often followed by small, woolly fruits. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species.
