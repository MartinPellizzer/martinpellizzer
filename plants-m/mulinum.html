Mulinum

Mulinum, commonly known as white mullein, is a biennial plant belonging to the Scrophulariaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers in the second year of growth, typically from late summer to early autumn. Native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, it thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
