Myrcia opaca

Myrcia opaca, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or myrcia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits resembling cherries. Its leaves are generally elliptical, glossy, and have a fragrant, spicy aroma.

Myrcia opaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
