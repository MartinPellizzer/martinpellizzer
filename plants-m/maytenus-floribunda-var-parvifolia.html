Maytenus floribunda var. parvifolia

Maytenus floribunda var. parvifolia, commonly known as the dwarf mayten, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
