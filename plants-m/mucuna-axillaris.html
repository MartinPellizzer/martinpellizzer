Mucuna axillaris

Mucuna axillaris, commonly known as the velvet bean or cowpea vine, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, showy flowers that range from white to purple. The plant produces elongated pods containing small, edible seeds that are rich in protein. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in traditional agriculture and herbal medicine.

Mucuna axillaris also has a rapid growth rate, making it useful for soil cover and erosion control.
