Melastoma trichotomum

Melastoma trichotomum, commonly known as the three-branched melastoma, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red and pink, often with a contrasting white or yellow center. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Melastoma trichotomum is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
