Mesembryanthemum lingueforme

Mesembryanthemum lingueforme, commonly known as the tongue-shaped ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, triangular leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, and it produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, or yellow. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
