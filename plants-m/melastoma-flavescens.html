Melastoma flavescens

Melastoma flavescens, commonly known as the yellow melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories and has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Melastoma flavescens is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
