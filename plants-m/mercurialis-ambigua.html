Mercurialis ambigua

Mercurialis ambigua, commonly known as the devil's-bit scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spurred flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant grows from a rhizomatous root system and has a creeping habit, often forming dense mats. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and branching.

It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows and along riverbanks.
