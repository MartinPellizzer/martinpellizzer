Microstegium stapfii

Microstegium stapfii, commonly known as the African bristlegrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like in appearance. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikes and are wind-pollinated.

Microstegium stapfii is valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage.
