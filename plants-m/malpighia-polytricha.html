Malpighia polytricha

Malpighia polytricha, commonly known as the red mulberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are rich in vitamin C and are often consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation, while its ecological importance supports local wildlife.
