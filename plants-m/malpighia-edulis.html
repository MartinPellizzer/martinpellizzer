Malpighia edulis

Malpighia edulis, commonly known as the acerola cherry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It produces small, bright red fruits that are rich in vitamin C and other antioxidants. The plant belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small white flowers. The fruit's botanical structure includes a thin, acidic peel and a segmented, juicy pulp with numerous seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of natural supplements.
