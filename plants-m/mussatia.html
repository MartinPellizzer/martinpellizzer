Mussatia

Mussatia, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture, while the fruits are berries that turn from green to red when mature.

Mussatia is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
