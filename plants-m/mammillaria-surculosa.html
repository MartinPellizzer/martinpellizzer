Mammillaria surculosa

Mammillaria surculosa, commonly known as the woolly mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and dense covering of white to pale blue spines. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange and appear at the top of the plant during the spring and summer months.

Mammillaria surculosa is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections due to its compact size and ornamental appearance.
