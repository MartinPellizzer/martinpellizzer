Malpighia hispaniolica

Malpighia hispaniolica, commonly known as the West Indian cherry or the pitahaya, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its small, bright red berries that are rich in vitamin C. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with simple, alternate leaves that are ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and usually pink or white, and it produces fruits that are edible and popular in local cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
