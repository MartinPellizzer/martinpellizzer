Marsdenia erecta

Marsdenia erecta, commonly known as the upright Marsdenia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asclepiadaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Marsdenia erecta is a climbing or trailing plant that can thrive in a variety of soil types, provided it receives adequate sunlight and drainage.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both indoor and outdoor gardens.
