Malvastrum macrostachyum

Malvastrum macrostachyum, commonly known as the showy malva, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with red centers, which bloom in summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine, soft hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping in arid regions.
