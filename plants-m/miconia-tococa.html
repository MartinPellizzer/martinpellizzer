Miconia tococa

Miconia tococa, commonly known as the red miconia, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.
