Mesembryanthemum fastigiatum

Mesembryanthemum fastigiatum, commonly known as the variable carpetweed, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, often blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to store water and reduce transpiration. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it an ideal plant for arid or semi-arid environments.
