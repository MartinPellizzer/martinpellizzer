Moquilea elata

Moquilea elata, commonly known as the desert sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom during the dry season. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh, drought-prone environments through deep root systems and thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, playing a role in local ecosystems.

Moquilea elata is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in regions with similar climates.
