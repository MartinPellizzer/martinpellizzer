Malva fastigiata f. rotundata

Malva fastigiata f. rotundata, commonly known as the rounded mallows, is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its broad, rounded leaves and showy, pinkish-purple flowers.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus, and is native to southern Europe. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer, adding ornamental value to gardens.
