Myristica longifolia

Myristica longifolia, commonly known as the nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a unique fruit that contains the aromatic seeds used to make nutmeg and mace. Its botanical significance lies in its essential oils and medicinal properties.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental features and ecological importance in its native habitat.
