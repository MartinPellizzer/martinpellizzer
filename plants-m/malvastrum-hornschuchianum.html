Malvastrum hornschuchianum

Malvastrum hornschuchianum, commonly known as the horned mallow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in dry, rocky soils. The plant is characterized by its distinctive horn-like seed pods and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It has a sprawling growth habit and is often found in open habitats such as fields and coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
