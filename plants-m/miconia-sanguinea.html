Miconia sanguinea

Miconia sanguinea, commonly known as the blood miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring broad, heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains numerous seeds.

Miconia sanguinea is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
