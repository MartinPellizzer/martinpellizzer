Mimosa emoryana var. chihuahuana

Mimosa emoryana var. chihuahuana, commonly known as the Chihuahua mimosa, is a shrub native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that fold shut when touched, a trait known as thigmonastic response. The plant produces small, yellow, spherical flowers that bloom in spring, adding a bright touch to its habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various wildlife species.
