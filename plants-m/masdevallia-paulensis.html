Masdevallia paulensis

Masdevallia paulensis, commonly known as the Paul's Masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, often colorful flower structures. The plant typically features a pseudobulb with fleshy leaves that help it store water in its native high-altitude environment. Its flowers are usually small to medium-sized, with a distinctive shape and a variety of color patterns.

Masdevallia paulensis is valued by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental appeal and unique botanical features.
