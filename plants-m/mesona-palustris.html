Mesona palustris

Mesona palustris, commonly known as swamp mesona, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in eastern North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

Mesona palustris plays a role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
