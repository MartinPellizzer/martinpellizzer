Malpighiodes leucanthele

Malpighiodes leucanthele, commonly known as the white-flowered Malpighiopsis, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Malpighiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and moths. Its leaves are typically ovate with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
