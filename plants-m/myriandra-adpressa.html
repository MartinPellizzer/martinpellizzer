Myriandra adpressa

Myriandra adpressa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in heathlands and open forests. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that appear in clusters. It is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to coastal and inland environments.

Myriandra adpressa plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for native pollinators.
