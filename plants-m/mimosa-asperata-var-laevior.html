Mimosa asperata var. laevior

Mimosa asperata var. laevior, commonly known as the smooth mimosa, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, fern-like leaves that are typically smooth and slightly hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in response to touch, a trait known as thigmonastic movement. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for dry, arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptive traits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
