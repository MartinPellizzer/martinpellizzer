Mikania viscosa

Mikania viscosa, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a vigorous climbing vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a fibrous stem that can root at the nodes, allowing it to spread rapidly. Its rapid growth makes it an invasive species in many regions, often outcompeting native vegetation.

Mikania viscosa is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and environmental conditions.
