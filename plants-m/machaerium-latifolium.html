Machaerium latifolium

Machaerium latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf machaerium, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features compound leaves with broad, lance-shaped leaflets that are often arranged in an odd-pinnate pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural applications.
