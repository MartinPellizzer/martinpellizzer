Mimulus javanicus

Mimulus javanicus, commonly known as the Java monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its vibrant orange or yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy environments and has heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Mimulus javanicus is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
