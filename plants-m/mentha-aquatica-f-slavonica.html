Mentha aquatica f. slavonica

Mentha aquatica f. slavonica, commonly known as Slavonic water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in whorled clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and wet meadows.

This subspecies is notable for its distinct fragrance and its traditional use in herbal medicine.
