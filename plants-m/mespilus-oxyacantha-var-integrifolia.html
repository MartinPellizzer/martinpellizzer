Mespilus oxyacantha var. integrifolia

Mespilus oxyacantha var. integrifolia, commonly known as the European whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and oval, dark green leaves that are entire, giving it the "integrifolia" designation. The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by edible, yellow berries in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ecological importance and historical use in traditional medicine.
