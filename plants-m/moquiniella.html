Moquiniella

Moquiniella, commonly known as the dwarf pincushion cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny structure. The plant produces small, colorful flowers, typically in shades of yellow or orange, during the warmer months. Moquiniella thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
