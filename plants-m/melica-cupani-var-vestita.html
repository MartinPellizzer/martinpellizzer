Melica cupani var. vestita

Melica cupani var. vestita, commonly known as the velvet grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, soft, and silky foliage that gives it a distinctive, almost velvety texture. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, greenish flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
