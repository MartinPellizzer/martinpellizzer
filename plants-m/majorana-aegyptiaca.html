Majorana aegyptiaca

Majorana aegyptiaca, commonly known as the Egyptian cumin or sweet fennel, is an aromatic herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other well-known herbs like parsley and dill. The plant features feathery, finely divided leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in umbels. Its seeds are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications due to their distinctive flavor and aromatic properties.

Majorana aegyptiaca thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a hardy and versatile plant for gardens and agriculture.
