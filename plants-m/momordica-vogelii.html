Momordica vogelii

Momordica vogelii, commonly known as the African bitter melon, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and pumpkins. The plant produces long, green fruits that are typically harvested when immature, though they can be eaten when mature. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with five petals.

The plant is known for its sharp, bitter taste and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
