Mesosphaerum conspersum

Mesosphaerum conspersum, commonly known as the woolly mesosphaerum, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive, woolly appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
