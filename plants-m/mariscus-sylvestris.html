Mariscus sylvestris

Mariscus sylvestris, commonly known as the wild mariscus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by spherical, fleshy fruits. Mariscus sylvestris is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and pharmacological studies.
