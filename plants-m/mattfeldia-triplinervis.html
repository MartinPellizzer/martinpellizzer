Mattfeldia triplinervis

Mattfeldia triplinervis, commonly known as the three-nerved fern, is a delicate and rare fern species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of soft, silvery hairs. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest undergrowth, where it thrives in cool and humid conditions. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature among ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Mattfeldia triplinervis is considered a botanically interesting but endangered species.
