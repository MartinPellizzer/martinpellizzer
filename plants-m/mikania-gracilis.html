Mikania gracilis

Mikania gracilis, commonly known as slender mikania, is a climbing vine native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically clustered in small inflorescences. It is often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as forest edges and stream banks.

Mikania gracilis is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
