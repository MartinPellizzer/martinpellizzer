Myroxylon pedicellatum

Myroxylon pedicellatum, commonly known as the balata tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The tree is notable for producing a natural latex known as balata, which is used in various industrial applications. Its bark and wood are also valued for their medicinal and commercial properties.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
