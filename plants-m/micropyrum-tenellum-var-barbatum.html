Micropyrum tenellum var. barbatum

Micropyrum tenellum var. barbatum, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, giving it its common name. Its flowers are bell-shaped, usually blue or violet, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
