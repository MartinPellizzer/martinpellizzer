Minuartia Juniperina Subsp. Glandulifera

Minuartia juniperina subsp. glandulifera is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the rocky slopes of central Asia. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is known for its small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. This subspecies is distinguished by its glandular leaves and pale pink to white petals. Due to its limited habitat and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered endangered. Conservation efforts are crucial to preserving this unique and beautiful plant.
