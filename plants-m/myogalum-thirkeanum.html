Myogalum thirkeanum

Myogalum thirkeanum, commonly known as the golden thirkean, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sunlight to flourish.
