Machilus glaucescens var. metzii

Machilus glaucescens var. metzii, commonly known as the Guangxi mahonia, is a shrub native to southern China.

It features glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by dark blue, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and seasonal fruiting.
