Merkusia lyratifolia

Merkusia lyratifolia, commonly known as the lyreleaf tree, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lyre-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Merkusia lyratifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
