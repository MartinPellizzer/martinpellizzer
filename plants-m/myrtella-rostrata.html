Myrtella rostrata

Myrtella rostrata, commonly known as the rostrated myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often fragrant and its small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. It produces small, fleshy berries that are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor.

Myrtella rostrata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
