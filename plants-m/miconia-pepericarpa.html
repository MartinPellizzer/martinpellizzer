Miconia pepericarpa

Miconia pepericarpa, commonly known as the purple miconia, is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, followed by distinctive purple or red berries.

Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine, and it is also cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance.
