Myrtus aubletii

Myrtus aubletii, commonly known as the African myrtle, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Myrtus aubletii is often used in hedges and for its ornamental value due to its fragrant foliage.
