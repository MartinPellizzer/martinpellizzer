Mimosa conferta f. brevipila

Mimosa conferta f. brevipila, commonly known as the dwarf mimosa, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to South America.

It features small, fern-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, spherical yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
