Mahonia × lindsayi

Mahonia × lindsayi, commonly known as the golden Mahonia, is a hybrid evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant's leaves are typically dark green with a leathery texture and often have a glossy appearance. It produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds but not recommended for human consumption.

Mahonia × lindsayi thrives in partial to full shade and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
