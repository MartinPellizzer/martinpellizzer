Melastoma theifolium

Melastoma theifolium, commonly known as the thief's leaf, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, often purple or red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are opposite and usually have a distinctive shape, contributing to its common name.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.
