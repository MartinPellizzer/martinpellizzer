Mammea africana

Mammea africana, commonly known as the African mammea, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Its roots and bark have been traditionally used in folk medicine for their potential therapeutic properties.
