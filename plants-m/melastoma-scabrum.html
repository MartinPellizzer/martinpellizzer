Melastoma scabrum

Melastoma scabrum, commonly known as the rough melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and understoreys. Its leaves are often covered in a rough, scaly texture, giving it its common name.

The flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and a distinctive tubular shape.
