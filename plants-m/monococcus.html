Monococcus

Monococcus, commonly known as the common wheat, is a species of grass that plays a vital role in global agriculture.

It is characterized by its spikelet structure, which consists of a single floret enclosed in two bracts. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into grains, which are the edible part of the plant. Monococcus is an annual plant that thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a key crop for food security and economic stability worldwide.
