Myrcia pseudoapoda

Myrcia pseudoapoda, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a strong, spicy fragrance.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
