Marrubium setaceum

Marrubium setaceum, commonly known as the hairy horehound, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, woolly leaves and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It has a strong, aromatic scent and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Marrubium setaceum is also valued as a source of honey due to its nectar-rich flowers.
