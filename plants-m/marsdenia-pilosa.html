Marsdenia pilosa

Marsdenia pilosa, commonly known as the hairy Marsdenia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asclepiadaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, with a distinctive corona structure.

Marsdenia pilosa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
