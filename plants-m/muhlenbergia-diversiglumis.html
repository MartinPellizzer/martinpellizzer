Muhlenbergia diversiglumis

Muhlenbergia diversiglumis, commonly known as the blue muhlenbergia, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and its distinctive blue-green foliage. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically pink to purplish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Muhlenbergia diversiglumis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in native plant gardens.
