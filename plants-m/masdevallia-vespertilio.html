Masdevallia vespertilio

Masdevallia vespertilio, commonly known as the night moth orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, moth-like appearance, with dark, velvety petals and sepals that resemble the wings of a nocturnal insect. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and requires high air humidity and indirect light to thrive. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a delicate, fragrant aroma.

Masdevallia vespertilio is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and rarity in cultivation.
