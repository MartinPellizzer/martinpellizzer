Manilkara dahomeyensis

Manilkara dahomeyensis, commonly known as the African sapodilla, is a tropical tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, glossy leaves. The tree produces round, yellowish fruits with a sweet, creamy pulp that contains edible seeds. Its latex, which is milky and sticky, has been used traditionally for various purposes, including as a natural adhesive.

The plant is valued not only for its fruit but also for its ecological and economic importance in its native habitat.
