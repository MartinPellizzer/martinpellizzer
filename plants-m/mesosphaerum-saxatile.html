Mesosphaerum saxatile

Mesosphaerum saxatile, commonly known as the rock mesosphaerum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and arid environments.

It typically grows in crevices and on rock surfaces, showcasing adaptations to harsh, dry conditions. The plant features small, clustered leaves and produces yellowish or greenish flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its root system is extensive, helping it anchor firmly in unstable substrates.

Mesosphaerum saxatile plays a role in stabilizing soil in rocky habitats and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
