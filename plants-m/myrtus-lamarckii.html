Myrtus lamarckii

Myrtus lamarckii, commonly known as the common myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, dark green leaves and small, urn-shaped flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically red or black when mature, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

Myrtus lamarckii thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile plant for gardens and landscapes.
