Moriera stenoptera

Moriera stenoptera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved moringa, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are often arranged in an opposite pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a source of nutrients.

The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.
