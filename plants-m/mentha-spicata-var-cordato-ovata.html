Mentha spicata var. cordato-ovata

Mentha spicata var. cordato-ovata, commonly known as round-leaf mint, is a variant of spearmint characterized by its heart-shaped to oval leaves.

It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically growing in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its aromatic leaves contain high levels of menthol, making it useful in culinary and medicinal applications.

The variety is often cultivated for its distinct flavor and ornamental value in gardens.
