Mirabilis aggregata

Mirabilis aggregata, commonly known as the clustered four o'clock, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright red or orange flowers that open in the late afternoon. The plant has hairy, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Mirabilis aggregata is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
