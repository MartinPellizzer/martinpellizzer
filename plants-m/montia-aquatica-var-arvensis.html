Montia aquatica var. arvensis

Montia aquatica var. arvensis, commonly known as water bladderwort, is a aquatic plant that thrives in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its small, floating leaves and specialized structures called bladders, which are used to trap small aquatic organisms. The plant is typically found in shallow, still or slow-moving water bodies. It has a short life cycle and is often considered an indicator species for nutrient-rich waters.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
