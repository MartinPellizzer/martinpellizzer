Meconopsis lancifolia subsp. lepida

Meconopsis lancifolia subsp.

lepida, commonly known as the Alpine meadow gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas. It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which are typically borne on long, slender pedicels.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
