Malus communis var. mitis

Malus communis var. mitis, commonly known as the sweet apple or honey apple, is a variety of the apple tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit. The leaves are typically oval with a smooth margin, and the flowers are white with five petals. The fruit is usually smaller than standard apples and has a sweeter, more delicate flavor.

This variety is also valued for its cold hardiness and ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.
