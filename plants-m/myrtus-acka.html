Myrtus acka

Myrtus acka, commonly known as the common myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but often used in ornamental gardening. Its leaves are fragrant and have been used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic properties.

Myrtus acka thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.
