Malvastrum densiflorum

Malvastrum densiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and somewhat woody at the base.

Malvastrum densiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
