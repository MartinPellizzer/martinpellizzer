Myristica duplopunctata subsp. duplopunctata

Myristica duplopunctata subsp.

duplopunctata, commonly known as the two-spotted nutmeg, is a species of flowering plant in the Myristicaceae family. It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines.

The plant is characterized by its aromatic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are alternate and elliptic, with a smooth texture and a strong scent.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release the aromatic seeds, which are harvested for their medicinal and culinary properties.
