Microlepia pilosella f. cristata

Microlepia pilosella f. cristata, commonly known as the cristate sword fern, is a unique variant of the microlepia pilosella species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flattened, and crisped fronds that give it a decorative appearance. This fern is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its botanical features include small, lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture.

The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking foliage.
