Myrtus mucronata var. opaca

Myrtus mucronata var. opaca, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, dark green leaves that are aromatic and covered in a fine, powdery bloom. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that give way to edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
