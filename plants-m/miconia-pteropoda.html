Miconia pteropoda

Miconia pteropoda, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and velvety to the touch. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Miconia pteropoda is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable plant in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
