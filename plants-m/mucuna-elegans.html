Mucuna elegans

Mucuna elegans, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, velvety, and often hairy pods that contain large, edible seeds. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers, which are followed by the development of its characteristic pods. Mucuna elegans is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in agroecosystems.

Its seeds contain compounds with potential medicinal and agricultural applications.
