Mentha citrata

Mentha citrata, commonly known as lemon mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright green, slightly hairy leaves and a distinctive lemony fragrance. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Lemon mint is often used in culinary applications for its refreshing taste and aromatic properties.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
