Matelea vaupesana

Matelea vaupesana, commonly known as the "Venezuelan moonflower," is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Venezuela and Colombia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy, white flowers that open at night. The plant is a climbing vine with opposite, ovate leaves and produces distinctive, fleshy fruits that contain seeds.

Its nocturnal blooming habit and fragrant flowers make it a unique and attractive species in tropical gardens.
