Melaleuca drummondii

Melaleuca drummondii, commonly known as the Drummond's tea tree, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Western Australia and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces a fragrant, aromatic essential oil from its leaves, which is used in various traditional and commercial applications. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or inland regions.

Melaleuca drummondii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native habitats.
