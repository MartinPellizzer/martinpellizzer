Makokoa

Makokoa, commonly known as the sacred tree of the Māori people, is a species of tree native to New Zealand.

It is part of the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and showy white flowers. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and is often found in coastal and lowland areas. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in traditional Māori carving and construction.

The plant also holds cultural and spiritual significance in Māori mythology and traditions.
