Melittis hispanica

Melittis hispanica, commonly known as Spanish honeyflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and coastal areas. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
