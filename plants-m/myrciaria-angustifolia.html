Myrciaria angustifolia

Myrciaria angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.
