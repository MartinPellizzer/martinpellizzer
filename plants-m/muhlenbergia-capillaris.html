Muhlenbergia capillaris

Muhlenbergia capillaris, commonly known as hairgrass, is a native grass species of the United States.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and produces delicate, feathery seed heads. This perennial plant thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight. It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.

Muhlenbergia capillaris plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects and birds.
