Melicope acuminata

Melicope acuminata, commonly known as the Hawaiian melicope, is a member of the Rutaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Hawaiian Islands. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in inflorescences, while its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

Melicope acuminata plays a significant role in the ecological systems of Hawaii, providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
