Microsorum pappei

Microsorum pappei, commonly known as the Java fern, is a popular aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, with elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments and is often used in aquariums due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal. Its leaves are often adorned with small, rounded spore cases called sporangia, which are used for reproduction.

Microsorum pappei is also valued for its ability to provide shelter for aquatic life and its low maintenance requirements.
