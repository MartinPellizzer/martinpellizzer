Mespilus xalapensis

Mespilus xalapensis, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, orange to reddish berries.

Its fruits are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications, and the plant is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
