Mattiastrum lamprocarpum

Mattiastrum lamprocarpum, commonly known as the silverleaf mat grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
