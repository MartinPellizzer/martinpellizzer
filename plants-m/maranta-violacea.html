Maranta violacea

Maranta violacea, commonly known as the violet arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet and green variegated leaves, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in moist, shaded environments.

Maranta violacea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in humid, indoor conditions.
