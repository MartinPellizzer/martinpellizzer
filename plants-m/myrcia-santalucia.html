Myrcia santalucia

Myrcia santalucia, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Myrcia santalucia is often found in humid, tropical forests and is valued for its ornamental beauty and potential medicinal properties.
