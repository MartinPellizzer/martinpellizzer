Mimosa myriophylla

Mimosa myriophylla, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a soft, delicate appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in spherical clusters, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Mimosa myriophylla is also known for its sensitive leaves that fold when touched, a trait that adds to its unique appeal.
