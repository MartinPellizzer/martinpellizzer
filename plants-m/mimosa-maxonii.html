Mimosa maxonii

Mimosa maxonii, commonly known as the yellow mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Mimosa maxonii is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
