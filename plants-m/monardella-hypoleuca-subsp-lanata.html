Monardella hypoleuca subsp. lanata

Monardella hypoleuca subsp.

lanata, commonly known as woolly monarda, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its hairy, woolly foliage, which gives it its subspecific name "lanata.".

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink to red, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native range.
