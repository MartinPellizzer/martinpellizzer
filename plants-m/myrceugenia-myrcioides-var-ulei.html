Myrceugenia myrcioides var. ulei

Myrceugenia myrcioides var. ulei, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a leathery texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in local traditional medicine.
