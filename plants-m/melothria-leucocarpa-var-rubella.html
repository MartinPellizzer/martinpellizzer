Melothria leucocarpa var. rubella

Melothria leucocarpa var. rubella, commonly known as the red cucumber, is a unique variant of the melon family native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, elongated fruits that range in color from yellow to vibrant red, depending on maturity. The plant has a trailing growth habit, with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow with five petals, and it produces fruits that are edible when fully ripe.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and culinary value.
