Madhuca stipulacea

Madhuca stipulacea, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed, which is used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
