Microstigma incanum

Microstigma incanum, commonly known as the silverleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees.

It has small, scale-like leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are usually red or orange and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Microstigma incanum is native to regions of Africa and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
