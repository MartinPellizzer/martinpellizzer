Mentha × villosa var. cordifolia

Mentha × villosa var. cordifolia, commonly known as heartleaf mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers make it a valuable plant for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
