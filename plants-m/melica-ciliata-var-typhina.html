Melica ciliata var. typhina

Melica ciliata var. typhina, commonly known as the silky grass or velvet grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs that cover the leaves and stems, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like arrangement of small, spikelets.

Melica ciliata var. typhina is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and is also valued in ecological restoration projects.
