Myrospermum frutescens

Myrospermum frutescens, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and small, white to yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in dense, spherical clusters.

Myrospermum frutescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and visual interest.
