Montanoa wercklei

Montanoa wercklei, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces vibrant orange to red composite flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Montanoa wercklei is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
