Mespilodaphne nitida

Mespilodaphne nitida, commonly known as the slender mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Mespilodaphne nitida is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
