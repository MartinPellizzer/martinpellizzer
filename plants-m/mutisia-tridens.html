Mutisia tridens

Mutisia tridens, commonly known as the yellow mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of tubular florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in moist, sunny habitats and has a woody stem with broad, lance-shaped leaves.

Mutisia tridens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
