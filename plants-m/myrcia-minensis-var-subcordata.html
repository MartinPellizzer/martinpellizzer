Myrcia minensis var. subcordata

Myrcia minensis var. subcordata, commonly known as the red-flowered myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, depending on the variety. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal regions.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its ecological importance in the region.
