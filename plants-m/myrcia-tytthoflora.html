Myrcia tytthoflora

Myrcia tytthoflora, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces edible berries that are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Myrcia tytthoflora plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
