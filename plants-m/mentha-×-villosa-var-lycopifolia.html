Mentha × villosa var. lycopifolia

Mentha × villosa var. lycopifolia, commonly known as the woolly mint or horse mint, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the northern regions of the Americas.

It features a sprawling growth habit with hairy, square stems and aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.

Its foliage is widely used in herbal medicine for its cooling and digestive properties.
