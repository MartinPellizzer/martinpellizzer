Microcerasus prostrata f. griffithii

Microcerasus prostrata f. griffithii, commonly known as the Griffith's dwarf stone pine, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features dense, compact foliage with small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries. It is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it suitable for alpine gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its attractive form and seasonal color changes.
