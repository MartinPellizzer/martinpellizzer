Malvastrum palmeri var. involucratum

Malvastrum palmeri var. involucratum, commonly known as the desert mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the spring and summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in desert ecosystems.
