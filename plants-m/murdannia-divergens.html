Murdannia divergens

Murdannia divergens, commonly known as the broadleaf watergrass, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its spreading growth habit and distinctive, broad, lance-shaped leaves that often float on the water's surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, arranged in dense clusters. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the soil in aquatic environments.

Murdannia divergens plays a vital role in maintaining the ecological balance of wetlands by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
