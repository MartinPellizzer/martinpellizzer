Machaerium papilisetosum

Machaerium papilisetosum, commonly known as the hairy spikegrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has hairy, serrated leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites.

Machaerium papilisetosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
