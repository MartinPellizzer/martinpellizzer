Monstera adansonii var. laniata

Monstera adansonii var. laniata, commonly known as the "split-leaf monstera," is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that develop fenestrations or splits as they mature. The plant is a climbing vine that requires support to grow vertically. Its foliage is glossy and dark green, with a unique veining pattern that adds to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers warm, humid environments.
