Matthiola damascena

Matthiola damascena, commonly known as the Damascus stock, is an annual or biennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbages and mustards. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its stems are erect and branching.

Matthiola damascena is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and for its use in perfumery due to its aromatic properties.
