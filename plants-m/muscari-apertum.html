Muscari apertum

Muscari apertum, commonly known as the Persian bluebell, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It features clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge from a bulbous base. It is well-suited for rock gardens and shady borders due to its compact size and ornamental flowers.

Muscari apertum is also valued for its ability to naturalize and spread readily in suitable conditions.
