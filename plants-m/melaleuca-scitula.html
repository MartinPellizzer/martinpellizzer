Melaleuca scitula

Melaleuca scitula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paperbark, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds. It typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, often forming dense thickets.

Melaleuca scitula is adapted to withstand salt spray and is sometimes used in coastal landscaping.
