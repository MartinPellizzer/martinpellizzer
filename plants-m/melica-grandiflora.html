Melica grandiflora

Melica grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flower basket grass, is a species of grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Melica grandiflora plays an important ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
