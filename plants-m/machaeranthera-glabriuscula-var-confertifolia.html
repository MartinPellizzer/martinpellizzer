Machaeranthera glabriuscula var. confertifolia

Machaeranthera glabriuscula var. confertifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf blazingstar, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central and southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. It is often found in prairies, open woodlands, and disturbed sites.

The variety confertifolia is distinguished by its more densely packed flower heads compared to the typical form.
