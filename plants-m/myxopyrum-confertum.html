Myxopyrum confertum

Myxopyrum confertum, commonly known as the common marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It typically grows in moist soils, often found in marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and bear small, spike-like clusters of flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Myxopyrum confertum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
