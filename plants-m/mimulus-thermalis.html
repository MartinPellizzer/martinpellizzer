Mimulus thermalis

Mimulus thermalis, commonly known as the thermal monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny environments such as streambanks and meadows. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Mimulus thermalis is part of the Physostegia genus and is known for its adaptability to various soil types.
