Masdevallia geminiflora

Masdevallia geminiflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered masdevallia, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that thrives in cool, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often colorful flowers that are typically borne in pairs. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to store moisture in its native habitat.

Masdevallia geminiflora is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral structure and ornamental value.
