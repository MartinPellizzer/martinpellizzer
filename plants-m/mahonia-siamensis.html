Mahonia siamensis

Mahonia siamensis, commonly known as the Siam Mahonia, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with a serrated edge and a distinctive yellowish-green color. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in the spring, followed by blue-black berries in the fall. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Mahonia siamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
