Matelea diversifolia var. surgens

Matelea diversifolia var. surgens, commonly known as the climbing matelea, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its twining stems and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often in full sun. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the flowers are usually pale yellow to orange in color.

This variant is distinguished by its more vigorous growth habit compared to other forms of Matelea diversifolia.
