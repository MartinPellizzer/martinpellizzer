Malaxis puberula

Malaxis puberula, commonly known as the pubescent malaxis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of China, India, and Nepal. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, with a distinctive, hairy appearance that gives it its common name.

The species is noted for its delicate structure and is often found in association with specific mycorrhizal fungi.
