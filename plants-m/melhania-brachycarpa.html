Melhania brachycarpa

Melhania brachycarpa, commonly known as the short-fruited melhania, is a member of the Malvaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, warm climates. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its flowers are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. The fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that contains several seeds.

It is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
