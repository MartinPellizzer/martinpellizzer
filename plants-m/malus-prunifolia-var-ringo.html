Malus prunifolia var. ringo

Malus prunifolia var. ringo, commonly known as the Japanese apple tree, is a deciduous species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy white to pink flowers in spring and red or yellow foliage in autumn. The tree produces small, edible apples that are often used in traditional Japanese cuisine. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is also valued for its adaptability and resistance to certain diseases.
