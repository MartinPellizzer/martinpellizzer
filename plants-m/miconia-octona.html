Miconia octona

Miconia octona, commonly known as the broadleaf miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red berries that are edible for some bird species. Miconia octona is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of tropical ecosystems, though its invasive potential requires careful management.
