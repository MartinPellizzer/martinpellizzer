Mentha aepycaulos

Mentha aepycaulos, commonly known as the tall mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue in color. Its leaves are aromatic, with a strong mint scent, and are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Mentha aepycaulos thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for garden cultivation.
