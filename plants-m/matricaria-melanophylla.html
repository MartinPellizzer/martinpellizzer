Matricaria melanophylla

Matricaria melanophylla, commonly known as the dark-leaved chamomile, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flower heads and dark green, velvety leaves. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their mild sedative and anti-inflammatory properties.
