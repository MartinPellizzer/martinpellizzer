Myosotis dichotoma

Myosotis dichotoma, commonly known as the narrow-leaved forget-me-not, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite leaves which are lanceolate and often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Myosotis dichotoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and conservation areas.
