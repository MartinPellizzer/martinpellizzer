Mimosa plumosa

Mimosa plumosa, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery leaves that give it a soft, cloud-like appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its rapid growth and spreading habit, it can become invasive in some regions if not managed properly.
