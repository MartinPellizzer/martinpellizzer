Marsdenia tsaiana

Marsdenia tsaiana, commonly known as the Tsaian Marsdenia, is a flowering plant native to southern China.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and opposite leaves. The plant produces distinctive, showy flowers with a yellow corona and greenish-white petals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
