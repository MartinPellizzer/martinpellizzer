Marianthus villosus

Marianthus villosus, commonly known as the hairy marianthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some horticultural gardens.
