Masdevallia siphonantha

Masdevallia siphonantha, commonly known as the siphonanthus masdevallia, is a unique orchid species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often brightly colored flowers that bloom in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant typically features hairy leaves and a pseudobulb structure, which helps it store moisture in its native high-altitude environment. It thrives in cool, humid conditions with indirect light, making it a popular choice for shade-loving orchid enthusiasts.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in greenhouses or specialized indoor settings.
