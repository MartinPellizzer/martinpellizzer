Mesua macrantha

Mesua macrantha, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Mesua macrantha is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in various regions.
