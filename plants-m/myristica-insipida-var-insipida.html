Myristica insipida var. insipida

Myristica insipida var. insipida, commonly known as the nutmeg myrtle, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark brown bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red berries containing a single seed. Its most notable feature is the aromatic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
