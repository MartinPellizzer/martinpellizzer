Mammillaria cantera

Mammillaria cantera, commonly known as the Cantera nipple cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a short, thick stem and a crown of spines. The plant features white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a dense covering of fine, white hairs.

It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.
