Miscanthus matsumurae var. longiberbis

Miscanthus matsumurae var. longiberbis, commonly known as the long-awned Japanese silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and panicles of pale silver-green flowers that appear in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
