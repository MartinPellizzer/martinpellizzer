Miconia pleiofluminensis

Miconia pleiofluminensis, commonly known as the Brazilian miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Amazon basin.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences with white or pinkish flowers that attract various pollinators. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Miconia pleiofluminensis is known for its rapid growth and is often found in moist, shaded environments within the rainforest.
