Mentha × carnuntiae

Mentha × carnuntiae, commonly known as the Hungarian marsh mint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Mentha longifolia and Mentha aquatica.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly minty scent and is native to Central and Eastern Europe. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as wet meadows and stream banks. It has lance-shaped leaves with a serrated margin and produces small, pale blue or purple flowers.

Mentha × carnuntiae is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in herbal remedies.
