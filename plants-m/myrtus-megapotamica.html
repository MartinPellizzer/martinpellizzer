Myrtus megapotamica

Myrtus megapotamica, commonly known as the Argentine myrtle, is a flowering shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers and narrow, leathery leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

Myrtus megapotamica is also used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial benefits.
