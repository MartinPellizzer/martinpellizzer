Mediolobivia aureiflora var. rubriflora

Mediolobivia aureiflora var. rubriflora, commonly known as the golden-flowered red-veined mediolobivia, is a strikingly colorful plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves that display a vibrant mix of green and red hues. The flowers, which bloom in spring, are golden-yellow with deep red veins, creating a striking visual contrast. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance, it is a popular choice for botanical gardens and specialized horticultural collections.
