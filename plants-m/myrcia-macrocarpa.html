Myrcia macrocarpa

Myrcia macrocarpa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.

Myrcia macrocarpa is also known for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in perfumery.
