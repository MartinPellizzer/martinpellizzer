Mecodium longifolium

Mecodium longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved mecodium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Mecodium longifolium is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, making it a hardy and adaptable species.
