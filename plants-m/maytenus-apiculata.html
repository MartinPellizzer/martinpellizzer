Maytenus apiculata

Maytenus apiculata, commonly known as the Brazilian mayten, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody fruits containing single seeds. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils and partial shade.

Maytenus apiculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
