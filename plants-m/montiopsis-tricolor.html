Montiopsis tricolor

Montiopsis tricolor, commonly known as the three-color montiopsis, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces three distinct color variations on its flowers, typically white, yellow, and red, which give it its common name. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or arid soils.

Montiopsis tricolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
