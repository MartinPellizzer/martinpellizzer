Masdevallia angulata

Masdevallia angulata, commonly known as the angular masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, angular-shaped pseudobulbs and its striking, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and requires high air humidity and moderate temperatures to thrive. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

Masdevallia angulata is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively compact size.
