Myrcia sellowiana

Myrcia sellowiana, commonly known as Brazilian pepper or araçá-do-mato, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy red flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and humid forests.

Myrcia sellowiana is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
