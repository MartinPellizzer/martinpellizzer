Maughaniella

Maughaniella, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that resemble stars. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy, dark green surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
