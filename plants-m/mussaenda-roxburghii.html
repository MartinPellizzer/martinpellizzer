Mussaenda roxburghii

Mussaenda roxburghii, commonly known as the flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, orange-red inflorescences that resemble flames, which attract pollinators like bees and birds. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Mussaenda roxburghii is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
