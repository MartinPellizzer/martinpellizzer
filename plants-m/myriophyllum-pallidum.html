Myriophyllum pallidum

Myriophyllum pallidum, commonly known as narrowleaf watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has feathery, branched leaves that are typically less than 1 centimeter in width. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in freshwater lakes and ponds with moderate to slow water flow.

Myriophyllum pallidum plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for fish and invertebrates.
