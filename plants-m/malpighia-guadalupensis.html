Malpighia guadalupensis

Malpighia guadalupensis, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Malpighia guadalupensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
