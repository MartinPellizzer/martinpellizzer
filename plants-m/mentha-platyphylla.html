Mentha platyphylla

Mentha platyphylla, commonly known as narrow-leaf mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Mentha platyphylla is often used in herbal medicine for its aromatic properties and potential digestive benefits.

Its essential oils are also valued in the fragrance and flavor industries.
