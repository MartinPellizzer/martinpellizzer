Messerschmidia sibirica var. angustior

Messerschmidia sibirica var. angustior, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sibirian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically blue to violet in color. The leaves are narrow and lanceolate, often hairy, and are arranged alternately along the stem. This plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
