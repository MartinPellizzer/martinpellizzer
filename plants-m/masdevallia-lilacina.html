Masdevallia lilacina

Masdevallia lilacina, commonly known as the lilac orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep violet. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in rocky or forested areas. Its pseudobulbs are cylindrical and help store water and nutrients, while its leaves are strap-like and evergreen.

Masdevallia lilacina is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
