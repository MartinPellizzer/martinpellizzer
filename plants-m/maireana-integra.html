Maireana integra

Maireana integra, commonly known as the Grey Box, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its grey-green foliage and rough, fissured bark. The plant has small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters, typically during spring. Maireana integra is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
