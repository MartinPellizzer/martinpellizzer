Myodocarpus fraxinifolius var. francii

Myodocarpus fraxinifolius var. francii, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical Africa.

It has a straight, cylindrical trunk with smooth, grey bark that peels off in thin strips. The leaves are simple, alternate, and pinnately compound, with lanceolate leaflets that are glossy and dark green on top. The flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.
