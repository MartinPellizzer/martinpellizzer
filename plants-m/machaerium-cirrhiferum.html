Machaerium cirrhiferum

Machaerium cirrhiferum, commonly known as the silky machaerium, is a tropical shrub native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features slender, often hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with a distinctive pink or purple hue.

Machaerium cirrhiferum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic foliage.
