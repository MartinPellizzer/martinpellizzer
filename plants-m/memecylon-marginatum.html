Memecylon marginatum

Memecylon marginatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed memecylon, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often accompanied by red or purple calyces. The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
