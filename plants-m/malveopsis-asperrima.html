Malveopsis asperrima

Malveopsis asperrima, commonly known as the rough mallow, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and broad, lobed leaves. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, with five petals and a prominent central stamen.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
