Masdevallia medusa

Masdevallia medusa, commonly known as the Medusa orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful inflorescences that resemble the mythological figure of Medusa, with intricate, curling petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually small to medium in size, with a unique structure that includes a specialized lip or labellum.

Masdevallia medusa requires specific environmental conditions, including high humidity and indirect light, to thrive in cultivation.
