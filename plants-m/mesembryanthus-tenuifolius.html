Mesembryanthus tenuifolius

Mesembryanthus tenuifolius, commonly known as the ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple, typically during the cooler months. Its root system is shallow, enabling it to absorb moisture from light rains.

Mesembryanthus tenuifolius is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and drought tolerance.
