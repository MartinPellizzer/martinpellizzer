Magnolia denudata var. elongata

Magnolia denudata var. elongata, commonly known as the elongated magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The leaves are typically elongated and heart-shaped, providing a striking contrast to the white blossoms. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
