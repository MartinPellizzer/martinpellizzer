Muhlenbergia gracilis var. enervis

Muhlenbergia gracilis var. enervis, commonly known as the slender muhlenbergia, is a grass species native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. This variety is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soils, making it a hardy ornamental grass. It produces small, fluffy seed heads that add visual interest in the fall.

The plant is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and aesthetic appeal.
