Mertensia blepharocalyx

Mertensia blepharocalyx, commonly known as the bellflower mertensia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, often found in woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
