Monnina chlamydantha

Monnina chlamydantha, commonly known as the pink sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in arid regions.
