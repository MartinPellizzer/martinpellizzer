Miconia barbipilis

Miconia barbipilis, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Miconia barbipilis is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed habitats, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and potentially problematic in certain ecosystems.
