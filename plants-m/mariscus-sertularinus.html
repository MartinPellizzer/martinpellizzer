Mariscus sertularinus

Mariscus sertularinus, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or violet flowers and silver-gray, scale-like leaves. The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found in dunes and rocky shores. It is a perennial herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
