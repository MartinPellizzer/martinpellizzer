Minuartia caroliniana

Minuartia caroliniana, commonly known as Carolina rockcress, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with distinct four-petaled flowers. The plant typically has bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are small, linear, and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as woodland edges and disturbed areas.
