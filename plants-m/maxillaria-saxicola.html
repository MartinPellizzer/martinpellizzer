Maxillaria saxicola

Maxillaria saxicola, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces clusters of fragrant, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive hooded lip. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern around the pseudobulbs.

Maxillaria saxicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
