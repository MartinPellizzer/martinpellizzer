Mimosa dimidiata

Mimosa dimidiata, commonly known as the small mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shrubland and grassland habitats, often in coastal areas. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils.

The flowers are followed by pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
