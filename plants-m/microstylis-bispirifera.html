Microstylis bispirifera

Microstylis bispirifera, commonly known as the spiral-stemmed sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and distinctive inflorescence that features a spiral arrangement of spikelets. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are adapted for wind pollination, a common trait among sedges.

Microstylis bispirifera plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates in its native environments.
