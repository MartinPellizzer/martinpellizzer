Malus sylvestris var. eleyi

Malus sylvestris var. eleyi, commonly known as the Eley's apple, is a wild species of apple native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its small, tart fruits and rugged, deciduous growth habit. The plant typically grows in mountainous areas and has small, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It produces white flowers in spring, which are followed by edible, though acidic, apples.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in local horticulture.
