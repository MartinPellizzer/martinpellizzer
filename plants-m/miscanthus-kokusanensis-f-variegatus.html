Miscanthus kokusanensis f. variegatus

Miscanthus kokusanensis f. variegatus, commonly known as the variegated Japanese silver grass, is a striking ornamental grass characterized by its distinctive variegated foliage.

This plant features narrow, blade-like leaves with a striking combination of white, green, and pink hues along their edges. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, producing fluffy, silver-white plumes in the autumn. The variegation is due to genetic mutations that affect chlorophyll distribution, resulting in its unique appearance.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is well-suited for moist, well-drained soils.
