Mentha omissa

Mentha omissa, commonly known as wild mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and has opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or pink, arranged in whorls along the stems.

Mentha omissa is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
