Myrciaria nitida var. dives

Myrciaria nitida var. dives, commonly known as the Brazilian blackcurrant, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves with a smooth, glossy surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The berries are often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to their nutritional and medicinal properties.
