Myrcia hedraiophylla

Myrcia hedraiophylla, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Myrcia hedraiophylla is also valued for its aromatic wood and has been used in traditional medicine.
