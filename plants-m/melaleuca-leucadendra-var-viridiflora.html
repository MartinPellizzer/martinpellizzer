Melaleuca leucadendra var. viridiflora

Melaleuca leucadendra var. viridiflora, commonly known as the greenflower bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking greenish-white flowers that bloom in dense, brush-like clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.
