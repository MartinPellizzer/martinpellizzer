Magnolia crassipes

Magnolia crassipes, commonly known as the swamp magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The tree has smooth, gray bark and elliptical, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its flowers are typically 6 to 8 inches in diameter and have a strong, sweet fragrance.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.
