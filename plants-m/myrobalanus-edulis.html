Myrobalanus edulis

Myrobalanus edulis, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by clustered, orange-red fruits that are edible when fully mature. The fruit has a sweet, astringent flavor and is rich in nutrients, making it valuable in traditional cuisines.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.
