Melastoma plumosum

Melastoma plumosum, commonly known as the silky mallow, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its attractive, palmate leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, dark seeds.

Melastoma plumosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
