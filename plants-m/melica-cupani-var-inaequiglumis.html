Melica cupani var. inaequiglumis

Melica cupani var. inaequiglumis, commonly known as the unequal-glume melica, is a grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and elongated, narrow leaves. The inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with spikelets that have unequal glumes, giving it its specific epithet. It typically grows in moist habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

This variety is often found in regions with temperate climates and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
