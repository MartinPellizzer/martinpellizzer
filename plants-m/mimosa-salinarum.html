Mimosa salinarum

Mimosa salinarum, commonly known as the saltbush mimosa, is a shrub native to arid and coastal regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, fern-like leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. Mimosa salinarum thrives in saline soils and is often found in salt lakes and coastal dunes.

Its ability to tolerate high salinity makes it an important component of ecosystems in harsh, arid climates.
