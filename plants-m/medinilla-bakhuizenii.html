Medinilla bakhuizenii

Medinilla bakhuizenii, commonly known as the velvet tangerine.

This tropical shrub is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its striking, velvety red bracts that resemble a tangerine peel. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves and produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Medinilla bakhuizenii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and long-lasting floral display.
