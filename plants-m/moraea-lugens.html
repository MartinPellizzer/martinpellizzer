Moraea lugens

Moraea lugens, commonly known as the Cape bellflower, is a South African native plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, clustered blooms during the summer months.

Moraea lugens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
