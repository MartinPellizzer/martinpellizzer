Maniltoa plurijuga

Maniltoa plurijuga, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a rough texture.

Maniltoa plurijuga is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
