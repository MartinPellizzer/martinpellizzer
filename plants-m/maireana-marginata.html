Maireana marginata

Maireana marginata, commonly known as the coastal daisy or coastal bluebottle, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub with silver-gray foliage and produces showy blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand salt spray and coastal winds. It plays an important role in stabilizing dune ecosystems and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Maireana marginata is also valued for its ornamental appeal in coastal gardens.
