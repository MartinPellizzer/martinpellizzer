Malacocarpus grossei

Malacocarpus grossei, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to two meters in diameter, providing a unique floating habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant produces showy white flowers that bloom at night, attracting pollinators such as moths. Its roots anchor it in the mud, while its leaves float on the water's surface, contributing to the ecosystem's biodiversity.

Malacocarpus grossei is valued for its ecological role in water purification and as a symbol of the rich botanical diversity of its native regions.
