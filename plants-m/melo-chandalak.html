Melo chandalak

Melo chandalak, commonly known as the Indian date palm, is a tropical plant native to South Asia.

It belongs to the family Arecaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, green fruits that mature into sweet, fibrous dates. Its roots are deep and robust, allowing it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.
