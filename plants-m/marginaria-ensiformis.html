Marginaria ensiformis

Marginaria ensiformis, commonly known as the slender marginaria, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are elongated and narrow, often with a slightly serrated edge.

Marginaria ensiformis produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
