Mimulus depressus

Mimulus depressus, commonly known as the yellow monkeyflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in stream banks and woodland areas.

Mimulus depressus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
