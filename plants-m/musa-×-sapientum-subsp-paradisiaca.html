Musa × sapientum subsp. paradisiaca

Musa × sapientum subsp.

paradisiaca, commonly known as the common banana, is a tropical plant belonging to the Musaceae family. It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and a clustered growth pattern, with the edible fruit developing from the flower.

The plant is cultivated for its fleshy, segmented fruit, which comes in various colors and sizes. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The banana plant is also notable for its rapid growth and the presence of a pseudostem formed by tightly packed leaf sheaths.
