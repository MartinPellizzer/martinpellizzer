Miconia boissieriana

Miconia boissieriana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Miconia boissieriana is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in some regions due to its aggressive spread and ecological impact.
