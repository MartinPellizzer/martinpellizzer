Melicope balloui

Melicope balloui, commonly known as the Hawaiian melicope, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in mesic to wet forest habitats. The plant has opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in axillary or terminal inflorescences.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
