Mygalurus geniculatus

Mygalurus geniculatus, commonly known as the slender tree-dwelling spider, is a species of mygalomorph spider found in various regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender body and long legs, which aid in its arboreal lifestyle. The spider's exoskeleton is typically a combination of brown and gray hues, providing effective camouflage among tree bark and foliage. It is a solitary hunter, relying on its excellent vision and ambush tactics to capture prey.

Mygalurus geniculatus plays a role in controlling insect populations in its habitat, contributing to ecological balance.
