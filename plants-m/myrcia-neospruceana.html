Myrcia neospruceana

Myrcia neospruceana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule containing numerous small, dark seeds.

Myrcia neospruceana is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental and aromatic properties.
