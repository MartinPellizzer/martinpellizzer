Malvaviscus pleurantherus

Malvaviscus pleurantherus, commonly known as the silverleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are ovate and covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in shaded, moist areas.
