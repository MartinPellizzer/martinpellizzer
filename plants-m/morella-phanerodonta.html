Morella phanerodonta

Morella phanerodonta, commonly known as the glossy black gum, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The tree produces woody fruits that contain one or two seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swamps and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
