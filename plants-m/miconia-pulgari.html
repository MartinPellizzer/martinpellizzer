Miconia pulgari

Miconia pulgari, commonly known as the "false hibiscus," is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It is often found in humid, shaded environments within the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its invasive potential, it is considered a problematic species in some regions.
