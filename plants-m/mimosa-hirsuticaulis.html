Mimosa hirsuticaulis

Mimosa hirsuticaulis, commonly known as the hairy mimosa, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 feet, characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or bean family, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.

Its unique foliage and delicate blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
