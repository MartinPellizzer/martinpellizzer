Mimulus cardinalis var. griseus

Mimulus cardinalis var. griseus, commonly known as the gray's monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and bright orange to red tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and woodland areas. It produces showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This variant is distinguished by its pale, fuzzy appearance compared to the more vibrant forms of Mimulus cardinalis.
