Myrmecodendron hindsii

Myrmecodendron hindsii, commonly known as the ant-tree, is a climbing plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense foliage, often forming thickets in tropical rainforests. The plant has a unique relationship with ants, providing shelter and food in exchange for protection from herbivores. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically white or pale yellow, and it produces fleshy, berry-like fruits.

Myrmecodendron hindsii plays an important ecological role in its habitat by supporting ant colonies and contributing to forest biodiversity.
