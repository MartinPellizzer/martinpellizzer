Mirabilis

Mirabilis, commonly known as the four o'clock flower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Onagraceae.

It is characterized by its showy, often colorful flowers that open in the late afternoon and close at night or in the early morning. The plants are native to the Americas and include species such as Mirabilis jalibotis and Mirabilis multiflora. They typically have opposite, simple leaves and produce capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.

The genus is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions, making it popular in gardens and as ornamental plants.
