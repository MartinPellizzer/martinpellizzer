Malouetia amazonica

Malouetia amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian flame tree, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its bark is thick and fissured. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland tropical areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
