Myosotis echinophora

Myosotis echinophora, commonly known as the spiked forget-me-not, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Myosotis echinophora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
