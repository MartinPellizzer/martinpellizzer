Mentha × dumetorum subvar. deseglisei

Mentha × dumetorum subvar. deseglisei, commonly known as the European purple mint, is a hybrid species within the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often purple-tinged on the underside. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters. It is native to southern Europe and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.
