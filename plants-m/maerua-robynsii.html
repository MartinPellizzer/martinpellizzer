Maerua robynsii

Maerua robynsii, commonly known as the red sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, displaying a compact, rounded shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. Its leaves are usually small, oval, and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Maerua robynsii is well adapted to drought conditions and is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
