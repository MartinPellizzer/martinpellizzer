Matthiola livida var. incisa

Matthiola livida var. incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and often have a serrated edge, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is a hardy perennial that is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
