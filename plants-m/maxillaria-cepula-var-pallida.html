Maxillaria cepula var. pallida

Maxillaria cepula var. pallida, commonly known as the pale maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically lanceolate and arranged in two rows along the stem. This variety is noted for its relatively easy care requirements and is popular among orchid enthusiasts.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity and well-draining soil.
