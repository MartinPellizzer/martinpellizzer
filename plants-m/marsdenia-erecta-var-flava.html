Marsdenia erecta var. flava

Marsdenia erecta var. flava, commonly known as the yellow-flowered Marsdenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and showy yellow flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly waxy texture. This variety is distinguished by its vibrant yellow corolla, which contrasts with its green foliage.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
