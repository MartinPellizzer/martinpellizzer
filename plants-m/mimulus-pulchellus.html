Mimulus pulchellus

Mimulus pulchellus, commonly known as the yellow monkeyflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in shaded woodland areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Mimulus pulchellus plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
