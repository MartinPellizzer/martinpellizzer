Madhuca cuneata

Madhuca cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mahua, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in various regions for its wood and fruit.

The species is also notable for its ability to thrive in a range of soil types and climatic conditions.
