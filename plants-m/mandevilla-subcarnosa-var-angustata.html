Mandevilla subcarnosa var. angustata

Mandevilla subcarnosa var. angustata, commonly known as the narrowleaf mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves and clusters of trumpet-shaped, fragrant flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant is a vigorous climber, often used in ornamental horticulture for its showy blooms and ability to thrive in warm climates. It prefers full sun to partial shade and well-draining soil, making it suitable for trellises or as a container plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both aesthetic appeal and easy maintenance.
