Musa × prematura

Musa × prematura, commonly known as the banana plant, is a hybrid species within the Musa genus.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and tall, clustered inflorescences that bear fruit. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and is cultivated for its edible berries, which are rich in nutrients. Botanically, it exhibits a complex genetic structure due to its hybrid origin.

Its growth habit includes a short stem with a pseudostem formed by tightly packed leaf bases.
