Mentha × petrakii

Mentha × petrakii, commonly known as the hybrid mint, is a cross between Mentha longifolia and Mentha rotundifolia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems. Mentha × petrakii is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Its rapid growth and spreading habit make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, low-maintenance plant.
