Myrcia linearifolia

Myrcia linearifolia, commonly known as the slender-leaved myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils, making it valuable in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.

Myrcia linearifolia is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
