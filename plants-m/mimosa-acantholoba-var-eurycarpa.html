Mimosa acantholoba var. eurycarpa

Mimosa acantholoba var. eurycarpa, commonly known as the broad-fruited mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are divided into numerous small leaflets. The plant produces fluffy, yellowish-pink flower heads that bloom in spring. Its fruits are large, spherical pods that split open to release seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
