Macrochordion macracanthum

Macrochordion macracanthum, commonly known as the giant feathergrass, is a tall perennial grass species native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
