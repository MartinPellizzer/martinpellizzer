Morus arbuscula

Morus arbuscula, commonly known as the dwarf mulberry, is a small, deciduous tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, aggregate fruits. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to urban environments. It produces edible berries that are consumed by both humans and wildlife.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
