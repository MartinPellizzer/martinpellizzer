Melochia goldbergii

Melochia goldbergii, commonly known as the golden melochia, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It is native to Central and South America and thrives in warm, humid climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
