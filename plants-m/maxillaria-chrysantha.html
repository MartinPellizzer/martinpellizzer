Maxillaria chrysantha

Maxillaria chrysantha, commonly known as the golden maxillaria, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns of darker hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its pseudobulbs are cylindrical and covered in a fibrous sheath, while its leaves are broad and grass-like.

Maxillaria chrysantha is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
