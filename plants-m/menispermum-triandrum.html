Menispermum triandrum

Menispermum triandrum, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing seeds dispersed by birds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Botanically, it is notable for its unique chemical compounds, some of which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.
