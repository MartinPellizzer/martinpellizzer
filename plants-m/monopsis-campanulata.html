Monopsis campanulata

Monopsis campanulata, commonly known as the bell-shaped monopsis, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family, which includes many bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are characterized by their bell-shaped form and a range of colors from white to violet.

Monopsis campanulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
