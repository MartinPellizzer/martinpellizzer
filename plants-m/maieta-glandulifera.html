Maieta glandulifera

Maieta glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular mayweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant produces glandular trichomes, which are tiny, sticky structures on its leaves and stems. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Maieta glandulifera is often found in meadows, roadsides, and along fences.
