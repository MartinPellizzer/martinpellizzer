Monsonia biflora

Monsonia biflora, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often forming a compact rosette of leaves. Its flowers are borne on long, slender stalks and are known for their vibrant color and durability.

Monsonia biflora is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
