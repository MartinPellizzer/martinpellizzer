Mouriri acutiflora

Mouriri acutiflora, commonly known as the sharp-flowered mouriri, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small tree or shrub that typically grows in humid, lowland forests. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and arranged in clusters, while the fruit is a fleshy, yellow drupe.

Mouriri acutiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
