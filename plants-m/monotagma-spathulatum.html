Monotagma spathulatum

Monotagma spathulatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved spathulate plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant features broad, spatulate-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. Its flowers are small and clustered in capitula, often displaying a range of colors from yellow to orange.

Monotagma spathulatum is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
