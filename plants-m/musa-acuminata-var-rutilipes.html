Musa acuminata var. rutilipes

Musa acuminata var. rutilipes, commonly known as the red-veined banana, is a striking variant of the banana plant characterized by its vibrant red veins on the leaves.

This variety belongs to the Musaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia, where it thrives in tropical climates. The plant produces large, elongated fruits that are typically consumed when fully mature, though they are less common in commercial cultivation compared to other banana varieties. Its ornamental value is significant, making it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.

The unique coloration of its leaves sets it apart from standard banana plants, enhancing its aesthetic appeal.
