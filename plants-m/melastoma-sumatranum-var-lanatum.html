Melastoma sumatranum var. lanatum

Melastoma sumatranum var. lanatum, commonly known as the hairy melastoma, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant has hairy foliage, which gives it its varietal name "lanatum," derived from the Latin word for wool. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
