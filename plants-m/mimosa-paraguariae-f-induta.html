Mimosa paraguariae f. induta

Mimosa paraguariae f. induta, commonly known as the velvet mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its soft, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with a compact form and a long blooming season.
