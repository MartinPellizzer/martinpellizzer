Malva ribifolia

Malva ribifolia, commonly known as the red mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and showy pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.

Malva ribifolia is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
