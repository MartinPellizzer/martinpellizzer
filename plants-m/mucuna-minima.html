Mucuna minima

Mucuna minima, commonly known as the dwarf velvet bean, is a small climbing legume native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, hairy pods that contain several seeds. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. It produces a distinctive, sweet-smelling odor, which attracts various pollinators.

Mucuna minima is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of protein-rich seeds.
