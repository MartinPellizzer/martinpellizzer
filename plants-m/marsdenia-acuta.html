Marsdenia acuta

Marsdenia acuta, commonly known as the sharp-leaved Marsdenia, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in umbels, followed by follicle fruits containing seeds. Marsdenia acuta is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
