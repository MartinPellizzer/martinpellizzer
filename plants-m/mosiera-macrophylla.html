Mosiera macrophylla

Mosiera macrophylla, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter, providing a floating platform for various aquatic life. The plant has a thick, spongy stem that allows it to remain afloat, while its roots anchor it in the mud at the bottom of ponds or lakes. Its flowers are showy, with white petals and a yellow center, blooming in the early morning and closing by midday.

Mosiera macrophylla plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by offering shelter and oxygen to water organisms.
