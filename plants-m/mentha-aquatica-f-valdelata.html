Mentha aquatica f. valdelata

Mentha aquatica f. valdelata, commonly known as the water mint, is a variant of the water mint species native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems. The plant typically grows in moist or aquatic habitats, such as marshes and stream sides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a strong minty scent.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in herbal remedies.
