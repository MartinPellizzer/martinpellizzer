Matricaria discoidea subsp. occidentalis

Matricaria discoidea subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as western chamomile, is a flowering plant native to western North America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers and finely divided, fern-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, white to pale yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of tubular florets.

The species is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
