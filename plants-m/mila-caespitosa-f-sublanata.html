Mila caespitosa f. sublanata

Mila caespitosa f. sublanata, commonly known as the woolly mila, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy, sublanate leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in some regions for garden use.
