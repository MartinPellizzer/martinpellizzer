Malva moschata var. laciniata

Malva moschata var. laciniata, commonly known as the fragrant mallow or velvetleaf mallow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in a fine, velvety down. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. Its fibrous roots and hairy stems are typical of the Malvaceae family.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.
