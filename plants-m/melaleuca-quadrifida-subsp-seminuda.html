Melaleuca quadrifida subsp. seminuda

Melaleuca quadrifida subsp.

seminuda, commonly known as the slender bottlebrush, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of pink or red flowers that bloom in spring.

The leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine, silky hairs. This species is adapted to sandy soils and saline environments, making it a hardy plant in coastal areas.

It plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.
