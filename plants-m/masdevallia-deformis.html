Masdevallia deformis

Masdevallia deformis, commonly known as the twisted masdevallia, is a rare and exquisite orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, often contorted floral structure, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as a miniature epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually a striking combination of purple and white, with intricate patterns that enhance its visual appeal.

Due to its rarity and specific environmental requirements, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
