Malva major

Malva major, commonly known as the greater mallows, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture and prominent veins. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple, with five petals and a fuzzy center. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along riverbanks.

Malva major is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its soothing properties.
