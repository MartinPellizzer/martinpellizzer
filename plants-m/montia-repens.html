Montia repens

Montia repens, commonly known as silverleaf creeper, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tiny white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, making it an effective ground cover in suitable habitats.
