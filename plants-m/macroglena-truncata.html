Macroglena truncata

Macroglena truncata, commonly known as the cutleaf water plantain, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and wetlands. The plant has elongated, ribbon-like leaves that are often notched or truncate at the ends, giving it its scientific name. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, producing abundant seeds that disperse through water.

Macroglena truncata plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and amphibians.
