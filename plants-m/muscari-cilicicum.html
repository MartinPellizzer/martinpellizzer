Muscari cilicicum

Muscari cilicicum, commonly known as the Cyprus grape hyacinth, is a bulbous plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, resembling grapes, hence its common name. The plant produces strap-like, grassy leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, blooming in early spring.

Muscari cilicicum is also valued for its fragrance and is sometimes used in floral arrangements.
