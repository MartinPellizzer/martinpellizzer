Macaranga eglandulosa

Macaranga eglandulosa, commonly known as the smooth macaranga, is a tropical plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is also known for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
