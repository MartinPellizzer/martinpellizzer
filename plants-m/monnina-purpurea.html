Monnina purpurea

Monnina purpurea, commonly known as purple flax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 3 feet tall, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces clusters of small, showy purple to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Onagraceae and is characterized by its distinctive seed capsules that release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.
