Muraltia rubicunda

Muraltia rubicunda, commonly known as the red muraltia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and legume fruits. The plant is notable for its clusters of small, fragrant, pinkish-red flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Muraltia rubicunda is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
