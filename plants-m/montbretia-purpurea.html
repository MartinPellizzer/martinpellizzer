Montbretia purpurea

Montbretia purpurea, commonly known as the orange lily, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the genus Gladiolus and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, showy flowers. The flowers typically bloom in shades of orange, red, and yellow, with a distinctive spurred petal at the base. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical features include lance-shaped leaves and a rhizomatous root system that aids in its propagation.
