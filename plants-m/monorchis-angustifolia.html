Monorchis angustifolia

Monorchis angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, delicate orchid flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale pink. Native to specific regions in Asia, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil, and it often forms symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
