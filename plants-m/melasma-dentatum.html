Melasma dentatum

Melasma dentatum, commonly known as the toothed bladderwort, is a aquatic plant characterized by its distinctive toothed leaves.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. The plant has small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white in color. Its roots are specialized for anchoring in muddy substrates and absorbing nutrients.

Melasma dentatum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small invertebrates.
