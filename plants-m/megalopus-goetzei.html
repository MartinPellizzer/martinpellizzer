Megalopus goetzei

Megalopus goetzei, commonly known as the Goetze's giant caterpillar, is a species of lepidopteran found in tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, robust body covered in dense, hairy setae that provide protection against predators. The larvae feed primarily on the leaves of various shrubs and trees, contributing to the ecological dynamics of their habitat. The plant's role in the lifecycle of this species is significant, as it serves as a primary food source during the early developmental stages.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, Megalopus goetzei is an important subject for studies on tropical biodiversity and insect-plant interactions.
