Massonia pygmaea subsp. pygmaea

Massonia pygmaea subsp.

pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf massonia, is a small, succulent plant native to southern Africa. It features a short, thick stem with dense clusters of small, fleshy leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color.

The plant produces star-shaped, white or pink flowers during the warmer months, which are borne on short, erect pedicels. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact size and ornamental foliage make it a desirable addition to succulent collections.
