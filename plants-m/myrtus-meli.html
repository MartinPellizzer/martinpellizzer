Myrtus meli

Myrtus meli, commonly known as sweet myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Myrtus meli is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
