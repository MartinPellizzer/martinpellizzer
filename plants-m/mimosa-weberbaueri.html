Mimosa weberbaueri

Mimosa weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer mimosa, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, spherical flower heads that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.

Mimosa weberbaueri is noted for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas, making it both ecologically significant and occasionally invasive.
