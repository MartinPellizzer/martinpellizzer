Mirabilis corymbosa

Mirabilis corymbosa, commonly known as the four o'clock flower, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the late afternoon and close at dawn. The plant produces vibrant blooms in shades of red, pink, yellow, and white, often with contrasting color patterns. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.
