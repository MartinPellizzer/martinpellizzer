Meioperis pannosa

Meioperis pannosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf senna, is a tropical plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves, which are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated, bean-like pods. It is native to regions of Africa and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The plant's botanical features make it a subject of interest in both ecological and horticultural studies.
