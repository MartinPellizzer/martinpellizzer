Masdevallia chrysochaete

Masdevallia chrysochaete, commonly known as the golden hair orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, hairy inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or golden in color, with intricate patterns that attract pollinators.

Due to its beauty and rarity, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
