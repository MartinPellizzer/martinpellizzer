Melilotus officinalis subsp. albus

Melilotus officinalis subsp.

albus, commonly known as white melilot, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant produces small, spherical pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. White melilot is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant for soil enrichment.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.
