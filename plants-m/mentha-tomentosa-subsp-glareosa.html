Mentha tomentosa subsp. glareosa

Mentha tomentosa subsp.

glareosa, commonly known as the woolly mint or hairy mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves and square, aromatic stems typical of the mint family.

The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Mentha tomentosa subsp.

glareosa is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
