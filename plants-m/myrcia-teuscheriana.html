Myrcia teuscheriana

Myrcia teuscheriana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule containing numerous tiny, dark seeds.

Myrcia teuscheriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
