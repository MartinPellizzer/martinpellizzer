Myrcia thyrsoidea

Myrcia thyrsoidea, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its foliage is dense and glossy, with leaves that are often used in traditional medicine.

The fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that contains a single seed.
