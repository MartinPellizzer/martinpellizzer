Moehringia argenteria

Moehringia argenteria, commonly known as silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from dense covering of fine, hair-like trichomes. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland floors.

Moehringia argenteria is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
