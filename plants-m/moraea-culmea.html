Moraea culmea

Moraea culmea, commonly known as the Natal lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces racemes of pale yellow to orange flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

Moraea culmea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
