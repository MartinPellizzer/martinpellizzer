Miconia paludosa

Miconia paludosa, commonly known as the swamp miconia, is a tropical plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in swampy, poorly drained soils. The plant has large, broad leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in its humid environment. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually white or pale green, and it produces berries that are edible for certain bird species.

Miconia paludosa plays an important role in the ecological dynamics of its wetland habitat.
