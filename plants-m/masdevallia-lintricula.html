Masdevallia lintricula

Masdevallia lintricula, commonly known as the lace orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, lace-like foliage and delicate, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. It prefers cool temperatures and high humidity, making it a popular choice for greenhouse cultivation.

Its unique appearance and elegant blooms make it a sought-after ornamental plant among orchid enthusiasts.
