Mulgedium macrophyllum

Mulgedium macrophyllum, commonly known as the giant leaf mulgedium, is a striking plant native to the temperate rainforests of the Pacific Northwest.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length, which are typically a deep green with a waxy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers during the late spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is often featured in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
