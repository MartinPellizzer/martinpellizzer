Mespilus tomentosa var. coccinea

Mespilus tomentosa var. coccinea, commonly known as the red-edged whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a pale underside. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, yellow berries in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or on rocky slopes.

The variety is noted for its ornamental value and historical use in hedgerows.
