Macaranga racemosa

Macaranga racemosa, commonly known as the hairy stinging tree, is a tropical plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is known for its potential to cause skin irritation due to the presence of urushiol-like compounds.
