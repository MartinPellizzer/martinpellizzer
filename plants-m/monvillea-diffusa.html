Monvillea diffusa

Monvillea diffusa, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming during the warmer months. Monvillea diffusa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its unique foliage and delicate blooms make it a sought-after ornamental plant in botanical gardens and private collections.
