Mentha longifolia subvar. psilostachya

Mentha longifolia subvar. psilostachya, commonly known as woolly mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for its aromatic and medicinal properties.
