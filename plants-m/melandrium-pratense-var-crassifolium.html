Melandrium pratense var. crassifolium

Melandrium pratense var. crassifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved catchfly, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
