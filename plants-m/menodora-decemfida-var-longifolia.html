Menodora decemfida var. longifolia

Menodora decemfida var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved devil's bit, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the family Menodoridae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported medicinal properties.

The species is adapted to moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
