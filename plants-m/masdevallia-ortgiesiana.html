Masdevallia ortgiesiana

Masdevallia ortgiesiana, commonly known as the Ortgies' masdevallia, is a rare and exquisite orchid native to the high-altitude cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often colorful floral structure. The plant typically grows as a sympodial orchid with pseudobulbs, and its flowers are known for their striking, intricate patterns and vibrant hues. It thrives in cool, humid conditions with high light exposure, making it a challenging but rewarding plant for advanced growers.

Due to its limited natural habitat and threats from deforestation, it is considered endangered and is often cultivated in conservation programs.
