Mutisia coccinea

Mutisia coccinea, commonly known as the red mutisia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy, bright red flowers that are often surrounded by yellow ray florets, making it visually striking. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in open woodland or along stream banks.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
