Myrtus cuspidata

Myrtus cuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are aromatic and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of tiny white flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, dark blue berries. Myrtus cuspidata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its fragrance and hardy nature.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a versatile addition to coastal and Mediterranean climates.
