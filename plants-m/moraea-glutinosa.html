Moraea glutinosa

Moraea glutinosa, commonly known as the sticky moraea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant produces a dense cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a short, fibrous root system. Moraea glutinosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is adapted to well-drained, sandy soils.
