Mentha viridis var. tauscheri

Mentha viridis var. tauscheri, commonly known as the Tauscher's mint, is a variant of the green mint species native to Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, pointed leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers arranged in whorls along the stems, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves and is used in traditional medicine and herbal teas.
