Macropsychanthus schimpffii

Macropsychanthus schimpffii, commonly known as the Schimpff's ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the arid regions of Namibia.

It is characterized by its unique, lantern-like inflorescence that resembles a ghostly lantern, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting specific pollinators such as moths. Its succulent leaves are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
