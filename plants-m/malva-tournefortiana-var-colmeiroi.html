Malva tournefortiana var. colmeiroi

Malva tournefortiana var. colmeiroi, commonly known as the Alborz mallows, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments. Its fibrous roots help prevent soil erosion, and it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
