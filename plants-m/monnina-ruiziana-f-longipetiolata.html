Monnina ruiziana f. longipetiolata

Monnina ruiziana f. longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled monnina, is a variant of the monnina genus native to South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with long petioles. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and vibrant floral display.
