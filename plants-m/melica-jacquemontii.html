Melica jacquemontii

Melica jacquemontii, commonly known as Jacquemont's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit with fine, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
