Molinia caerulea var. subspicata

Molinia caerulea var. subspicata, commonly known as purple moor grass, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that grows in moist, acidic habitats such as bogs and heathlands. The plant features tufted clumps of dark green leaves with a bluish-gray sheen. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late summer, are topped with purple to pinkish flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
