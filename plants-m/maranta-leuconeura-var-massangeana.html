Maranta leuconeura var. massangeana

Maranta leuconeura var. massangeana, commonly known as the prayer plant, is a tropical species native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage that changes with the light cycle, often displaying shades of red, pink, and green. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while the stems are often adorned with red or purple markings. This plant is known for its unique ability to fold its leaves at night, a behavior called nyctinasty.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.
