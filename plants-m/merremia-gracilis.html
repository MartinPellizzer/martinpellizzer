Merremia gracilis

Merremia gracilis, commonly known as the slender morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Merremia gracilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
