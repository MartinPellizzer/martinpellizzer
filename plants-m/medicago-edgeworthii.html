Medicago edgeworthii

Medicago edgeworthii, commonly known as Edgeworth's clover, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has hairy stems and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Medicago edgeworthii is often found in sagebrush steppe and is of interest to botanists due to its ecological and potential medicinal properties.
