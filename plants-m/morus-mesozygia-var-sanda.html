Morus mesozygia var. sanda

Morus mesozygia var. sanda, commonly known as the sanda mulberry, is a variant of the mulberry tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are edible and often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

This variety is valued not only for its fruit but also for its role in providing shade and supporting local wildlife.
