Mammillaria decipiens var. albescens

Mammillaria decipiens var. albescens, commonly known as the White-Furred Pincushion Cactus, is a compact, globular cactus native to Mexico.

It features a pale green to white body with prominent white spines that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant has a dense covering of small, white tubercles that contribute to its unique look. It produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its pale foliage.

This variety is well-suited for rock gardens and requires well-draining soil and minimal water to thrive.
