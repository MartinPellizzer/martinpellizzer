Merremia borneensis

Merremia borneensis, commonly known as the Borneo morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for garden use in suitable regions.
