Myroxylon molle

Myroxylon molle, commonly known as the copal tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, rough bark and can grow up to 30 meters tall. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, fragrant fruits containing numerous seeds. Its sap, known as copal, is a resinous substance used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
