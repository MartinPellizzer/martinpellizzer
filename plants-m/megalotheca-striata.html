Megalotheca striata

Megalotheca striata, commonly known as the striped thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its botanical features include a stout, branching stem and a composite flower head composed of both ray and disk flowers.

Megalotheca striata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
