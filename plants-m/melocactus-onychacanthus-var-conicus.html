Melocactus onychacanthus var. conicus

Melocactus onychacanthus var. conicus, commonly known as the cone-shaped melon cactus, is a distinctive species native to southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stem that forms a dense, spiny structure resembling a cone. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged exterior. It thrives in arid environments and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic conditions.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and desert gardens.
