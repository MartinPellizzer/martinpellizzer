Myosurus minimus var. major

Myosurus minimus var. major, commonly known as the greater mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a fuzzy appearance. The flowers are small, white or pale pink, and arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and is known for its ability to spread rapidly through runners.
