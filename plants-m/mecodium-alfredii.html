Mecodium alfredii

Mecodium alfredii, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its compact rosette of fleshy, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of violet, pink, and white, which bloom from spring to autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Mecodium alfredii is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
