Melia azedarach var. floribunda

Melia azedarach var. floribunda, commonly known as the Persian lilac, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its rapid growth, spreading crown, and distinctive, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture, while the bark is smooth and grayish in color. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to various regions for landscaping purposes.

It also contains toxic compounds, making it important to handle with care.
