Macrosamanea pubiramea

Macrosamanea pubiramea, commonly known as the hairy ramea, is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its thick, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or yellowish flowers that are typically followed by orange to red berries. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential therapeutic properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
