Macadamia youngiana

Macadamia youngiana, commonly known as the silver nut.

This evergreen shrub or small tree is native to Australia and belongs to the Proteaceae family. It is characterized by its lance-shaped, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by hard, woody pods containing edible nuts.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained, acidic soils.
