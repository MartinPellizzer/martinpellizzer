Miconia semijuga

Miconia semijuga, commonly known as the red miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains numerous seeds.

Miconia semijuga is often cultivated for its ornamental value but can be invasive in certain regions.
