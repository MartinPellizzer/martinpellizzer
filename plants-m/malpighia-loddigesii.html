Malpighia loddigesii

Malpighia loddigesii, commonly known as the red mombin, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
