Mogiphanes glauca

Mogiphanes glauca, commonly known as the blue-green mistflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, clustered blue-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

Mogiphanes glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
