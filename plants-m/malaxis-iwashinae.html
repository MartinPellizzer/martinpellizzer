Malaxis iwashinae

Malaxis iwashinae, commonly known as the Iwashina's orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its botanical features include a single, unbranched stem and a single, lance-shaped leaf.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
