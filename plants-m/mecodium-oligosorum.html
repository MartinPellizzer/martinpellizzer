Mecodium oligosorum

Mecodium oligosorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mecodium, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color. It is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments and is often drought-tolerant.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
