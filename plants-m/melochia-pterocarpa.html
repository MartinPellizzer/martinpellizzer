Melochia pterocarpa

Melochia pterocarpa, commonly known as the giant melon or melochia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to red fruits resembling small melons. Its fruit is edible, though the seeds are often consumed as a spice in some cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
