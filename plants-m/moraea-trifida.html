Moraea trifida

Moraea trifida, commonly known as the three-lobed gladiolus, is a member of the Iridaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, sword-shaped leaves and showy, colorful flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant produces tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, supporting its vibrant, tubular flowers. Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.

Moraea trifida prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.
