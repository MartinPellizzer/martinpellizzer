Marina neglecta var. elongata

Marina neglecta var. elongata, commonly known as the elongated sea holly, is a coastal plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found along shorelines and dunes. Its elongated leaves give it a distinctive appearance, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

It is drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance once established.
