Muscari tenuiflorum lusus flavescens

Muscari tenuiflorum lusus flavescens, commonly known as the yellow-flowered grape hyacinth, is a bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its compact, grass-like foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant produces a fragrant, sweet scent, which attracts pollinators such as bees and flies. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in temperate climates.
