Morus multicaulis

Morus multicaulis, commonly known as the many-stemmed mulberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically pubescent underneath. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called mulberries, which are edible and popular in various cuisines. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

Morus multicaulis is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
