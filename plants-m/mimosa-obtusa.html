Mimosa obtusa

Mimosa obtusa, commonly known as the soft mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellow, spherical flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its ability to fix nitrogen through root nodules makes it beneficial for soil enrichment.
