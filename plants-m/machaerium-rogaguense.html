Machaerium rogaguense

Machaerium rogaguense, commonly known as the Brazilian broom, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, clustered leaves that are often trifoliate. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to white, with a distinctive spurred shape, and it produces small, dry legumes as fruits.

Machaerium rogaguense is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and ecological succession.
