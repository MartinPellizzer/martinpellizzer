Munroa argentina

Munroa argentina, commonly known as the Argentine thistle, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. It has spiny leaves and produces showy purple or pink flowers that attract pollinators.

Munroa argentina is often used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
