Marcgravia angustifolia

Marcgravia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mistletoe, is a hemi-parasitic plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round berries that are dispersed by animals. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss in its humid, tropical environment.

Marcgravia angustifolia plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various insect species and contributing to the biodiversity of the canopy layers in rainforests.
