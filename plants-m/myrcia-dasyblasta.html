Myrcia dasyblasta

Myrcia dasyblasta, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and fragrant, small white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a glossy surface, while its fruits are small, woody capsules that contain several seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
