Macaranga ciliata

Macaranga ciliata, commonly known as the veined macaranga, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with distinctive veins. The plant is often found in lowland rainforests and can grow as a shrub or small tree. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically greenish-yellow in color.

Macaranga ciliata is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
