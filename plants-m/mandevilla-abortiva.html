Mandevilla abortiva

Mandevilla abortiva, commonly known as the wild mandevilla, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in summer. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces orange or red berries containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardens and as a houseplant.
