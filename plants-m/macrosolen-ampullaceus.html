Macrosolen ampullaceus

Macrosolen ampullaceus, commonly known as the giant bellflower, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, bell-shaped flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in length. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to its often arid habitat. Its flowering period typically occurs during the warmer months, and it thrives in full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and growth habits, it is a popular choice for botanical gardens and specialized collections.
