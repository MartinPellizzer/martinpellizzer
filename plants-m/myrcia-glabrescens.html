Myrcia glabrescens

Myrcia glabrescens, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a problematic species in certain regions.
