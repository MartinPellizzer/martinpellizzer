Mappa rugosa

Mappa rugosa, commonly known as the rough mallow, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its scientific name reflects its rugged appearance, with "rugosa" meaning rough in Latin.

Mappa rugosa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
