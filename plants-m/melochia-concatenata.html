Melochia concatenata

Melochia concatenata, commonly known as the red-rooted false hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsules containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Melochia concatenata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
