Moraea cistiflora

Moraea cistiflora, commonly known as the Cape bell or the white bell, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped white or pale pink flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy inflorescences that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Moraea cistiflora is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
