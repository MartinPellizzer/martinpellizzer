Monipsis

Monipsis, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in woodland habitats. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture.

Monipsis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
