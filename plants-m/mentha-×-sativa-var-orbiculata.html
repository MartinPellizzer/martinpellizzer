Mentha × sativa var. orbiculata

Mentha × sativa var. orbiculata, commonly known as round-leaf mint, is a hybrid mint species characterized by its rounded, oval-shaped leaves.

It is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer, contributing to its ornamental value. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, making it useful in culinary and medicinal applications.

However, it is less commonly cultivated than other mint varieties due to its specific growing requirements.
