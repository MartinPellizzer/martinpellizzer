Marcus-kochia littorea

Marcus-kochia littorea, commonly known as sea buckthorn, is a shrub native to coastal regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow or orange berries, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. The plant has small, alternate leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. It thrives in saline and sandy soils, making it well adapted to coastal environments.

Marcus-kochia littorea is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing dunes and preventing erosion.
