Mezobromelia brownii

Mezobromelia brownii, commonly known as the Brown's bromeliad, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central tank to collect water. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vibrant, long-lasting flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are typically covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in its humid, tropical habitat.

Mezobromelia brownii is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
