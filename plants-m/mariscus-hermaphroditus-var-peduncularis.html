Mariscus hermaphroditus var. peduncularis

Mariscus hermaphroditus var. peduncularis, commonly known as the pedunculate mariscus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in umbels. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a distinctive, feathery appearance.
