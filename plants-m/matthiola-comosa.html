Matthiola comosa

Matthiola comosa, commonly known as the woolly stock, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Matthiola comosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
