Melica magellanica

Melica magellanica, commonly known as the Magellan grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Melica magellanica is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
