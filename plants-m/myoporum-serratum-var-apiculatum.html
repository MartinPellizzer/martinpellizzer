Myoporum serratum var. apiculatum

Myoporum serratum var. apiculatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf myoporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

The variety apiculatum is characterized by its finely toothed leaves and compact growth habit.
