Moldenkea

Moldenkea, commonly known as the sea holly, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The genus includes several species, many of which are native to coastal areas and thrive in saline or sandy soils.

Moldenkea plants are often cultivated for their ornamental value due to their unique and showy appearance.
