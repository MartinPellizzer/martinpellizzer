Mikania decumbens

Mikania decumbens, commonly known as creeping morningglory, is a vigorous vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively, making it a common weed in disturbed areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and climates.
