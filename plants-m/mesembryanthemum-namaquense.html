Mesembryanthemum namaquense

Mesembryanthemum namaquense, commonly known as the Namibian ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves and small, star-shaped flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with a thick cuticle to reduce water loss. It produces a copious amount of white or pale pink flowers during the summer months.

Its unique morphology and ability to thrive in harsh conditions make it a notable species in xerophytic ecosystems.
