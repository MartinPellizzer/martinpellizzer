Mammillaria avila-camachoi

Mammillaria avila-camachoi, commonly known as the Avila-Camacho mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white to pale green tubercles. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable addition to succulent collections.
