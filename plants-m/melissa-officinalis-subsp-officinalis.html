Melissa officinalis subsp. officinalis

Melissa officinalis subsp.

officinalis, commonly known as lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are often used in herbal teas and culinary applications due to their calming properties.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported soothing and antispasmodic effects.
