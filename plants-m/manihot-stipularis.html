Manihot stipularis

Manihot stipularis, commonly known as the stipular manioc, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, green stems and alternate, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its roots are used in traditional medicine and as a source of starch.

However, the plant contains toxic compounds that require proper processing before consumption.
