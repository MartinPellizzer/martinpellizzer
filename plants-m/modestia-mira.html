Modestia mira

Modestia mira, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a sweet fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its delicate blossoms.

Modestia mira is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and unique blooming pattern.
