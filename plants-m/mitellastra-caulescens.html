Mitellastra caulescens

Mitellastra caulescens, commonly known as the slender mitella, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a slender, upright stem with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
