Mammillaria pseudoscrippsiana

Mammillaria pseudoscrippsiana, commonly known as the Scrippsian mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a covering of white to pale yellow spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth rate, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
