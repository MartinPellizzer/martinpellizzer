Miconia bullosa

Miconia bullosa, commonly known as the bubbleleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that often have a waxy texture and prominent veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Miconia bullosa is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.

However, it is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its aggressive spread and ecological impact.
