Mutisia caldasiana

Mutisia caldasiana, commonly known as the Caldasian mutisia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit. The plant typically features showy yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, providing a textured appearance.

Mutisia caldasiana is often found in moist, shaded environments, growing in forest understories or along stream banks.
