Malaxis gibbosa

Malaxis gibbosa, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, twisted inflorescence and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption in poor soils.

Malaxis gibbosa is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its distinctive appearance.
