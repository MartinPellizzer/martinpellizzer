Moschoxylum pentandrum

Moschoxylum pentandrum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-purple flower heads that resemble small cones. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowering period occurs from late summer to early fall, producing abundant nectar that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and ecological benefits.
