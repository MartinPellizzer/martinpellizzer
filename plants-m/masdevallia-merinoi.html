Masdevallia merinoi

Masdevallia merinoi, commonly known as the Merino's Masdevallia, is a striking orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often bicolor flowers that display a mix of red, orange, and yellow hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are elongated and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its native habitat.

This species is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively compact size.
