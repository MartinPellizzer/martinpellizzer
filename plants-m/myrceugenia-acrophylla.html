Myrceugenia acrophylla

Myrceugenia acrophylla, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly in the temperate regions of Chile and Argentina. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature.

Myrceugenia acrophylla is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and fragrant blossoms.
