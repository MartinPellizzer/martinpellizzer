Miliusa manickamiana

Miliusa manickamiana, commonly known as the Manickamiana satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and smooth, aromatic bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits containing several seeds. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a threatened species in its native range.
