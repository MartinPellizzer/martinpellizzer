Matthiola tenella

Matthiola tenella, commonly known as the winter flowering stock, is an annual or biennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard plants. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming from late autumn to early spring. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it has a strong, pungent odor similar to mustard.

Matthiola tenella is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can also be used in traditional herbal medicine.
