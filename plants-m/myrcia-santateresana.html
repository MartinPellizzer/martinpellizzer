Myrcia santateresana

Myrcia santateresana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest biome in Brazil, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, woody drupes that contain a single seed.

Myrcia santateresana is valued for its ecological role and has potential uses in traditional medicine.
