Myrcia minensis

Myrcia minensis, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to others. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Myrcia minensis is also noted for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
