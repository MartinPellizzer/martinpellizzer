Miconia acreana

Miconia acreana, commonly known as the Brazilian glory flower, is a member of the Melastomataceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to the Amazon rainforest of South America. The plant is characterized by its large, showy, pink to red bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. Its leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Miconia acreana is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, which can impact local ecosystems.
