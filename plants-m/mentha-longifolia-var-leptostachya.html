Mentha longifolia var. leptostachya

Mentha longifolia var. leptostachya, commonly known as slender-stemmed mint, is a variant of the longleaf mint species.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

This variety is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.
