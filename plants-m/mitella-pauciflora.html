Mitella pauciflora

Mitella pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered mitella, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clustered foliage. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically oval or elliptic, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Mitella pauciflora thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
