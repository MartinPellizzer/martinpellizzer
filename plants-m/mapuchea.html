Mapuchea

Mapuchea, commonly known as the "flower of the Andes," is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to the cold, harsh conditions of mountainous environments. Mapuchea is often found in the Andes and surrounding areas, where it thrives in temperate to alpine climates.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular subject in horticulture and botanical studies.
