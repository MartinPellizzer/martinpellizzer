Melampodium anomalum

Melampodium anomalum, commonly known as the zigzag ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive zigzagging stems and clusters of yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Melampodium anomalum is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and ecological value to pollinators.
