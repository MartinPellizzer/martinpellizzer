Mertensia cumingiana

Mertensia cumingiana, commonly known as Cuming's bluebells, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking blue, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Mertensia cumingiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
