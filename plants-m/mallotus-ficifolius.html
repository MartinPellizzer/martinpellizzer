Mallotus ficifolius

Mallotus ficifolius, commonly known as the fig-leaf mallot, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that resemble those of the common fig, hence its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit, which is a small, round drupe, is often used in traditional medicine.

The tree is valued for its ornamental foliage and has ecological significance in its native habitats.
