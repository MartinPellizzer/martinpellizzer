Malache rosea

Malache rosea, commonly known as the rose-like malache, is a striking plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink to purple flowers, which bloom in clusters and are often described as resembling roses. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color, providing a lush backdrop to its showy blossoms. Malache rosea thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Its botanical name reflects its visual resemblance to roses, despite being a member of the Malvaceae family.
