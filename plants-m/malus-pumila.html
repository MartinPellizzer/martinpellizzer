Malus pumila

Malus pumila, commonly known as the Japanese apple or dwarf apple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It produces small, sweet, edible apples that are typically harvested in the fall. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are oval to lance-shaped with a serrated margin. Its flowers are white and appear in clusters, followed by the characteristic red or yellow fruits.

Malus pumila is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and fruit.
