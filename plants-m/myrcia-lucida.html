Myrcia lucida

Myrcia lucida, commonly known as the Brazilian beauty or the golden myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

Myrcia lucida is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some medicinal uses in local cultures.
