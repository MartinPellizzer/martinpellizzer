Melandrium persicum

Melandrium persicum, commonly known as Persian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region and parts of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its succulent leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.
