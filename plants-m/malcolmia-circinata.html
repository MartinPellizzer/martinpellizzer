Malcolmia circinata

Malcolmia circinata, commonly known as the blueweed or silverleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, silver-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Malcolmia circinata is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
