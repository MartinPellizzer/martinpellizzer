Melocactus argenteus

Melocactus argenteus, commonly known as the silver melon or silver barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem covered in a dense layer of white, woolly spines that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces a single, large, yellowish flower at the top of the stem during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing, drought-tolerant cactus that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and rockeries.
