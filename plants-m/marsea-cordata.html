Marsea cordata

Marsea cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf marsea, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy on the upper surface. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance.

Marsea cordata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.
