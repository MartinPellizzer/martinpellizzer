Mentha × dumetorum var. sirmicola

Mentha × dumetorum var. sirmicola, commonly known as the Sirmian hemp mint, is a hybrid plant species within the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves and its ability to produce small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. This variety is native to parts of Europe and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its botanical features include opposite leaves with serrated edges and a strong, minty fragrance.

The plant is notable for its potential medicinal properties and its role in traditional herbal remedies.
