Massonia nodicarpa

Massonia nodicarpa, commonly known as the African ice plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces pink to red flowers in summer, which are borne on short, erect pedicels. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its thick stems for survival.

Massonia nodicarpa is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance.
