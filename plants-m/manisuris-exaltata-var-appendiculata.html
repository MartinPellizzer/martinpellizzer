Manisuris exaltata var. appendiculata

Manisuris exaltata var. appendiculata, commonly known as the hairy leaf manisuris, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, ascending stems and lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a slightly rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.
