Melanosinapis amplexicaulis

Melanosinapis amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping stem orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique growth habit, with its leaves tightly clasping the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with intricate patterns that resemble a mosaic, attracting specific pollinators. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
