Mimusops comorensis

Mimusops comorensis, commonly known as the Comoro tamarind, is a flowering plant native to the Comoros Islands.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The tree produces fragrant, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its natural dispersal.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
