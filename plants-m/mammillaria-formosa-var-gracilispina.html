Mammillaria formosa var. gracilispina

Mammillaria formosa var. gracilispina, commonly known as the slender-spined mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense covering of small, white to pale yellow spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in succulent gardens and as a houseplant.
