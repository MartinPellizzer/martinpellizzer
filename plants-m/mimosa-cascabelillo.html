Mimosa cascabelillo

Mimosa cascabelillo, commonly known as the "trompeta de la muerte" or "death's trumpet," is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces distinctive, inflated seed pods that resemble small trumpets, hence its common name. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Mimosa cascabelillo is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in certain ecosystems.
