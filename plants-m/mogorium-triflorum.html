Mogorium triflorum

Mogorium triflorum, commonly known as the three-flowered mogorium, is a rare flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tri-lobed leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil. Its unique floral structure has made it a subject of interest for botanists studying plant evolution.

Due to habitat loss, Mogorium triflorum is now considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
