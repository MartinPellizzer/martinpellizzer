Mimosa nudiflora

Mimosa nudiflora, commonly known as the naked-flowered sensitive plant, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold when touched, a response triggered by touch-sensitive hairs. The plant produces small, yellow, globular flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique botanical features make it both an ornamental and ecologically valuable plant.
