Myrtus osteomeloides

Myrtus osteomeloides, commonly known as the stone myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Myrtus osteomeloides thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
