Milium wilcoxianum

Milium wilcoxianum, commonly known as the Wilcox's millet, is a flowering plant species native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Milium wilcoxianum is often found in desert regions and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations.
