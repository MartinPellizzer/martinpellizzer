Mentha × schultziana f. angustata

Mentha × schultziana f. angustata, commonly known as narrow-leaf peppermint, is a hybrid mint species characterized by its slender, elongated leaves.

It is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its aromatic leaves contain high levels of menthol, making it valuable for both medicinal and commercial purposes.

This variety is often cultivated for its essential oils and used in aromatherapy and flavoring applications.
