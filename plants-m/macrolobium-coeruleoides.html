Macrolobium coeruleoides

Macrolobium coeruleoides, commonly known as the blue-veined orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, blue-veined flowers that resemble delicate butterflies. The plant features long, slender stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers bloom in clusters, producing a unique and visually appealing display that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its beauty and ecological significance.
