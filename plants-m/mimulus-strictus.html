Mimulus strictus

Mimulus strictus, commonly known as the narrowleaf monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and wet meadows. The plant features showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Mimulus strictus plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
