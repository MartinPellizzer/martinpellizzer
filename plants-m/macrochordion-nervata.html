Macrochordion nervata

Macrochordion nervata, commonly known as the veined marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It features long, slender stems that often grow in dense clusters, and its leaves are narrow with prominent veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically greenish or brownish in color. Macrochordion nervata thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in coastal and freshwater marshes.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
