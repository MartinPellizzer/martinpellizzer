Marrubium laricum

Marrubium laricum, commonly known as the alpine horehound, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant features a basal rosette of grey-green, hairy leaves and produces small, blue or violet flowers in early summer. It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly bitter leaves and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Marrubium laricum is valued for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions and its historical use in treating respiratory ailments.
