Myrciaria delicatula var. conferta

Myrciaria delicatula var. conferta, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, bright red fruits that resemble cherries. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves and produces fragrant white flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

The fruit is rich in antioxidants and is used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
