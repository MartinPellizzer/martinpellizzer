Myrtoleucodendron teretifolium

Myrtoleucodendron teretifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf myrtle-leaf tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
