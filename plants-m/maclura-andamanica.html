Maclura andamanica

Maclura andamanica, commonly known as the Andaman yellow wood, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the Andaman Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It belongs to the family Moraceae, which includes figs and mulberries, and is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and distinctive yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are typically yellow when ripe.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
