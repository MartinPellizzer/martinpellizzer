Maranta leopardina

Maranta leopardina, commonly known as the leopard plant, is a striking tropical species native to South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, patterned leaves that resemble a leopard's spots. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Maranta leopardina requires consistent moisture and indirect light to flourish.
