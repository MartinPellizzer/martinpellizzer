Melochia pyramidata var. flava

Melochia pyramidata var. flava, commonly known as the yellow melochia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree with a distinctive, pyramidal growth habit and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens due to its vibrant foliage and striking floral display.
