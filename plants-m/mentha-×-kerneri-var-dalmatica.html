Mentha × kerneri var. dalmatica

Mentha × kerneri var. dalmatica, commonly known as Dalmatian mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its essential oils, rich in menthol and other compounds, make it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.

It is often used in herbal teas and traditional remedies for its cooling and soothing properties.
