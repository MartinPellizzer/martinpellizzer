Manihot maracasensis

Manihot maracasensis, commonly known as the Maracasense arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is cultivated for its starchy tuberous roots, which are used as a food source in various cuisines.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often grown in warm, humid climates.
