Maianthemum viridiflorum

Maianthemum viridiflorum, commonly known as green-flowered false lily of the valley, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features a dense rosette of glossy, dark green leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during late spring to early summer. It thrives in shaded, moist woodland environments and is often found in rich, well-drained soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important component of forest understories.
