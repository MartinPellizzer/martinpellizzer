Machaerium floribundum var. floribundum

Machaerium floribundum var. floribundum, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its erect, branched stems and dense clusters of yellow to orange flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in whorls around the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The flowers bloom in spring and summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
