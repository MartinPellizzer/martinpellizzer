Mentha × benllochi

Mentha × benllochi, commonly known as the hybrid mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Mentha viridis (apple mint) and Mentha spicata (spearmint), resulting in a plant with a distinctive, aromatic scent. The leaves are typically oval or lance-shaped, with a serrated edge and a smooth, waxy texture. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue or purple, arranged in whorled clusters.

This plant is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses, as well as for its ornamental value in gardens.
