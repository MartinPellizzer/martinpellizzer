Mapinguari desvauxianus

Mapinguari desvauxianus, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its unique appearance, featuring long, slender leaves and a single, striking white flower that blooms briefly during the rainy season. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surfaces of trees without deriving nutrients from them. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is highly sensitive to environmental changes.
