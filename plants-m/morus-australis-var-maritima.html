Morus australis var. maritima

Morus australis var. maritima, commonly known as the coastal mulberry, is a shrub or small tree native to coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy beneath. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in sandy or saline soils and is well adapted to coastal environments.

Due to its hardiness, it is sometimes used in coastal stabilization projects.
