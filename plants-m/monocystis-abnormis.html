Monocystis abnormis

Monocystis abnormis, commonly known as the abnormal cyst-forming alga, is a unicellular protist belonging to the phylum Euglenophyta.

It is characterized by its unique ability to form a single cyst during adverse environmental conditions, which allows it to survive periods of stress. The organism lacks a rigid cell wall, instead having a flexible plasma membrane that facilitates its motility. It contains chloroplasts for photosynthesis and is often found in freshwater environments.

Due to its specialized cyst formation, it plays a role in aquatic ecosystems as both a primary producer and a potential indicator of environmental change.
