Menezesiella salesopolitana

Menezesiella salesopolitana, commonly known as the Chilean sea holly, is a flowering plant native to southern Chile.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or rocky soils. It produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
