Mischocarpus stipitatus

Mischocarpus stipitatus, commonly known as the red-berried holly, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often adorned with red or purple veins. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are an important food source for various birds and mammals. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
