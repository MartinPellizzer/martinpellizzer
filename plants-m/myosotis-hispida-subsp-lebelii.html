Myosotis hispida subsp. lebelii

Myosotis hispida subsp.

lebelii, commonly known as the hairy forget-me-not, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America. It features slender stems and produces small, delicate blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are typically hairy, giving the plant its common name, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. This subspecies is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for gardens with suitable growing conditions.
