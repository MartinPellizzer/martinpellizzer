Medinilla punicea

Medinilla punicea, commonly known as the red medinilla, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the summer months. Its leaves are typically glossy and dark green, with a velvety texture.

Medinilla punicea is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and as a houseplant.
