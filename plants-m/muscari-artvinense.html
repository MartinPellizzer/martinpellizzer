Muscari artvinense

Muscari artvinense, commonly known as the Artvin bluebell, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of northeastern Turkey, particularly in the Artvin province. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Muscari artvinense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
