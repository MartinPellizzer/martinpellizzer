Musa religiosa

Musa religiosa, commonly known as the sacred banana, is a species of flowering plant in the Musaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and clustered, edible fruits. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are usually yellow or green and are followed by elongated, curved fruits.

Musa religiosa is valued not only for its nutritional content but also for its cultural and religious significance in various regions.
