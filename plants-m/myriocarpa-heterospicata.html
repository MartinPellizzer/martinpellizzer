Myriocarpa heterospicata

Myriocarpa heterospicata, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive globular shape and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves and stems that help it conserve water in dry environments. Its seeds are enclosed in a spiny capsule, which aids in dispersal by animals.

Myriocarpa heterospicata plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing food for local wildlife.
