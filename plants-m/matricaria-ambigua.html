Matricaria ambigua

Matricaria ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous chamomile, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with white centers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments and has a sprawling, herbaceous growth habit. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, while its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Matricaria ambigua is often used in traditional medicine for its potential calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
