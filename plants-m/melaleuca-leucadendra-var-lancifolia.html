Melaleuca leucadendra var. lancifolia

Melaleuca leucadendra var. lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paperbark, is a species native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a white or pale inner layer. The plant produces small, fragrant white or cream flowers that bloom in winter, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.
