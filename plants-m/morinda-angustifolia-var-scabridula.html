Morinda angustifolia var. scabridula

Morinda angustifolia var. scabridula, commonly known as roughleaf morinda, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
