Myrcia kegeliana var. angustifolia

Myrcia kegeliana var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf myrtle, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically aromatic and have a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, which bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that contain several seeds.

This species is often found in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in humid, coastal environments.
