Marsdenia affinis

Marsdenia affinis, commonly known as the slender Marsdenia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

It is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, China, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its trailing or climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often pubescent. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a tubular corolla and five lobes, and it produces small, fleshy berries as fruit.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.
