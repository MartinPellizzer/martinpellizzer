Mentha silesiaca

Mentha silesiaca, commonly known as Silesian mint, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Mentha silesiaca is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
