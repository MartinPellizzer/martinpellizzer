Microsemia polygaloides

Microsemia polygaloides, commonly known as the small-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a deep taproot system, allowing it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in grasslands, sagebrush steppe, and rocky soils.

Microsemia polygaloides plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
