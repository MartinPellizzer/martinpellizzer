Magnolia rotundifolia

Magnolia rotundifolia, commonly known as the Japanese magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, broad, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its flowers are typically pink or white, with a subtle fragrance.

The tree is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
