Macroglena gemmata

Macroglena gemmata, commonly known as the gemmata water flea, is a tiny aquatic organism belonging to the phylum Parachnida.

It is characterized by its transparent, elongated body and the presence of a distinctive, shield-like structure called a lorica. This organism is typically found in freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes, where it thrives in slow-moving or still water. Macroglena gemmata reproduces through a process called gemmation, where small buds called gemmae detach from the parent organism to form new individuals.

Its simple morphology and ecological role make it an important indicator species for water quality assessment.
