Miconia clathrantha

Miconia clathrantha, commonly known as the tropical beautyberry, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, clustered inflorescences that produce small, fleshy berries. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Miconia clathrantha is also notable for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, which can impact local ecosystems.
