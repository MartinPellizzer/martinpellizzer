Morinda pedunculata

Morinda pedunculata, commonly known as the red morinda or red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often grown in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
