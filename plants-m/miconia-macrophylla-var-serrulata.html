Miconia macrophylla var. serrulata

Miconia macrophylla var. serrulata, commonly known as the broad-leaf miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The leaves are typically dark green and have serrated edges, giving the plant its scientific name. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by orange-red berries.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage.
