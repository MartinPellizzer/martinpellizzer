Martia petiolata

Martia petiolata, commonly known as the slenderleaf mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features slender, hairy stems with alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The flowers are showy, with five pink to purple petals that surround a prominent central stamen cluster, often appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens or as a border plant.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in a garden setting.
