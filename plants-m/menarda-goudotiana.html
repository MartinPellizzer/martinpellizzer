Menarda goudotiana

Menarda goudotiana, commonly known as the Goudot's menardia, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
