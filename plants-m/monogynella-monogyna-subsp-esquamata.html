Monogynella monogyna subsp. esquamata

Monogynella monogyna subsp.

esquamata, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its narrow, grayish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.
