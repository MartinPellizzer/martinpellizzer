Myrcia extranea

Myrcia extranea, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several small, edible seeds.

Myrcia extranea is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
