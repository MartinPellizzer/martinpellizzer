Miconia tinifolia var. caudata

Miconia tinifolia var. caudata, commonly known as the long-tailed miconia, is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often pubescent on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
