Mimulus × hybridus

Mimulus × hybridus, commonly known as the hybrid monkeyflower, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Mimulus guttatus and Mimulus lomatophyllus. The plant typically features bright orange or yellow flowers that bloom from spring to summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in streambanks and wet meadows.

Mimulus × hybridus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
