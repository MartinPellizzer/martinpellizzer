Mapania imeriensis

Mapania imeriensis, commonly known as the Imereti maple, is a deciduous tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the family Sapindaceae and is characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by orange-red samaras that are dispersed by wind. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
