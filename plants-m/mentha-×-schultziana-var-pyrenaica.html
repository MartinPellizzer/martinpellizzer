Mentha × schultziana var. pyrenaica

Mentha × schultziana var. pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean mint, is a hybrid species native to the Pyrenees region.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its strong minty scent and medicinal properties.

It is often used in traditional herbal remedies and as a flavoring agent in culinary applications.
