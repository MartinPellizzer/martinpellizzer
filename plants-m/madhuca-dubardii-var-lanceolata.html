Madhuca dubardii var. lanceolata

Madhuca dubardii var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lance-leaf mahua, is a deciduous tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its lanceolate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces edible seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
