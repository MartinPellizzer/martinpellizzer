Melianthus trimenianus

Melianthus trimenianus, commonly known as the red milkweed tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has a sturdy, often multi-stemmed structure with thick, waxy leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy species suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a popular choice for both landscaping and conservation efforts.
