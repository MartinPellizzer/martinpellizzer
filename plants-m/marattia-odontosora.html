Marattia odontosora

Marattia odontosora, commonly known as the toothed fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Marattiaceae and is characterized by its large, compound fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive toothed margin that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and montane forests.

The plant's rhizome is thick and woody, supporting its tall, sturdy trunk.
