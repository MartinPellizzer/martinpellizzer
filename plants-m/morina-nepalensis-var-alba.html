Morina nepalensis var. alba

Morina nepalensis var. alba, commonly known as the white moringa, is a variant of the moringa plant native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It is a small, shrub-like plant characterized by its white flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain. Its flowers are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The plant has limited commercial use but is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
