Mallotus auriculatus

Mallotus auriculatus, commonly known as the ear-shaped mallot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its distinctive ear-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental foliage and is occasionally used in traditional medicine.
