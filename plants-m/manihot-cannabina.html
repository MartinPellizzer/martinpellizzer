Manihot cannabina

Manihot cannabina, commonly known as arrowleaf mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features palmately lobed leaves that are typically arrow-shaped, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological restoration projects.
