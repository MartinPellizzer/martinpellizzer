Milium latifolium

Milium latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf millet, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its seeds are edible and have been used as a grain in traditional cuisines.

Milium latifolium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
