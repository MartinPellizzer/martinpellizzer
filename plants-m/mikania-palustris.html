Mikania palustris

Mikania palustris, commonly known as swamp milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands and moist soils. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the summer. It is often found in swampy areas and is a host plant for the monarch butterfly.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in wet conditions make it an important part of wetland ecosystems.
