Millettia piscatoria

Millettia piscatoria, commonly known as the water millettia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along streambanks. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can be dispersed by water, contributing to its distribution in wet habitats.

Millettia piscatoria is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
