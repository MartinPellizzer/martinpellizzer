Melica stricta var. albicaulis

Melica stricta var. albicaulis, commonly known as the white-stemmed brome, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in moist habitats such as wet meadows, marshes, and stream banks. The plant has slender, upright stems that are often covered in a fine white bloom, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

The inflorescence consists of a loose panicle of spikelets, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.
