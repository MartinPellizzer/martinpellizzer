Malache sulphurea

Malache sulphurea, commonly known as the sulfur yellow bell, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and thrives in humid, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in terminal clusters, creating a dramatic visual impact. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its bold, eye-catching blooms.
