Maesa haplobotrys

Maesa haplobotrys, commonly known as the tropical evergreen shrub, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces dark blue to black berries that are edible but often have a mild, acidic flavor. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
