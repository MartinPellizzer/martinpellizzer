Mitrasacme polymorpha var. calycina

Mitrasacme polymorpha var. calycina, commonly known as the calycine mitrasacme, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, clustered inflorescences and small, bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
