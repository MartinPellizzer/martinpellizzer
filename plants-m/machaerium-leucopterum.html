Machaerium leucopterum

Machaerium leucopterum, commonly known as the white-winged machaerium, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces legume pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
