Malva stipulacea

Malva stipulacea, commonly known as the stipulate mallows, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves with stipules, and it produces showy, pink to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Malva stipulacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
