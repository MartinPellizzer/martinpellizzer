Malus baccata f. pendula

Malus baccata f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping crabapple, is a cultivar of the crabapple tree characterized by its distinctive cascading branches.

It features small, fragrant white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by red or yellow fruits in the fall. The plant is native to China and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It is often used in ornamental landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and seasonal interest.

The weeping form adds a dramatic visual element to gardens and parks.
