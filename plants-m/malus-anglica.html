Malus anglica

Malus anglica, commonly known as the English apple or wild apple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, edible apples that are typically sour and have a high acidity. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Malus anglica is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in apple cultivation.
