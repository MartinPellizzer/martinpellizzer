Morus alba var. elongata

Morus alba var. elongata, commonly known as the elongated mulberry, is a variant of the white mulberry native to China.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that can reach up to 20 cm in length, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically white or pale pink in color. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food for silkworms.

This variety is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and its resilience to urban environments.
