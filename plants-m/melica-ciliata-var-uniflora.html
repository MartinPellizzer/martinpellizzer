Melica ciliata var. uniflora

Melica ciliata var. uniflora, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges. The plant features slender, grass-like blades with a distinctive ciliate (fringed) edge along the leaf margins. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet per stem, which is a characteristic feature of the varietal name "uniflora.".

The species is often noted for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators and maintaining soil stability in its native environments.
