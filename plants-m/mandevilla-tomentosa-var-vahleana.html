Mandevilla tomentosa var. vahleana

Mandevilla tomentosa var. vahleana, commonly known as the velvet mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. It is a vigorous climber that thrives in warm, humid climates and requires full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and as a houseplant.
