Melochia argentina var. genuinum

Melochia argentina var. genuinum, commonly known as the Brazilian spinach or velvetleaf, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds. It is often cultivated as a leafy vegetable in some regions due to its edible leaves, which have a mild, spinach-like flavor.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types.
