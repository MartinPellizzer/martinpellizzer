Mussaenda divaricata

Mussaenda divaricata, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered yellow flowers that resemble small umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Mussaenda divaricata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
