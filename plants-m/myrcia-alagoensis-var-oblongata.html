Myrcia alagoensis var. oblongata

Myrcia alagoensis var. oblongata, commonly known as the Alagoas myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in local medicine.
