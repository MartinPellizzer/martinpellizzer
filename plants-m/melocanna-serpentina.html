Melocanna serpentina

Melocanna serpentina, commonly known as the giant bamboo or giant bamboo grass, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically green and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, which contribute to its robust growth.

Melocanna serpentina is native to Southeast Asia and is often found in moist, tropical environments.
