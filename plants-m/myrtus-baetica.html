Myrtus baetica

Myrtus baetica, commonly known as the Iberian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its fragrant, dark green leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or black when mature. Myrtus baetica thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in perfumery and traditional medicine.
