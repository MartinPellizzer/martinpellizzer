Machaerium latifolium var. manaoense

Machaerium latifolium var. manaoense, commonly known as the Manaoense Machaerium, is a tropical shrub native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
