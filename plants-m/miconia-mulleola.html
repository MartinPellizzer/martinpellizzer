Miconia mulleola

Miconia mulleola, commonly known as the small miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It is often found in humid, lowland forests and is adapted to thrive in shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
