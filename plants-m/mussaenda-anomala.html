Mussaenda anomala

Mussaenda anomala, commonly known as the flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, brightly colored inflorescences that resemble clusters of small, lantern-like flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

Mussaenda anomala is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
