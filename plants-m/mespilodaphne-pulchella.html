Mespilodaphne pulchella

The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of pinkish-white flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its berries, which are typically red or orange, contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Mespilodaphne pulchella is native to Australia and is often found in coastal and dry woodland habitats.
