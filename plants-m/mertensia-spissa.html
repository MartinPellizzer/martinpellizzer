Mertensia spissa

Mertensia spissa, commonly known as bluebells, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Mertensia spissa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to naturalize in suitable environments.
