Mespilus monogyna var. armeniaca

Mespilus monogyna var. armeniaca, commonly known as the Caucasian medlar, is a variant of the common medlar native to the Caucasus region.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree with rough, fissured bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers in clusters, followed by edible, fleshy fruits that ripen in late summer. The fruit has a unique texture, often described as a mix of apple and pear, with a strong, aromatic flavor.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
