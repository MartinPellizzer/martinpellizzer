Monstera pertusa f. klotzschiana

Monstera pertusa f. klotzschiana, commonly known as the Variegated Monstera, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a mix of green, white, and sometimes yellow, creating a striking variegation pattern. The plant has a climbing habit and produces aerial roots that help it attach to trees or supports in its natural habitat. Its foliage is not only ornamental but also plays a role in photosynthesis and water retention.

This plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil to flourish.
