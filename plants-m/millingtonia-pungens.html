Millingtonia pungens

Millingtonia pungens, commonly known as the sweet acacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania.

It is a small, deciduous tree or shrub that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a woody pod containing several seeds, and it is often found in dry, sandy soils.

Millingtonia pungens is also valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine.
