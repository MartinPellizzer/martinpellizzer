Machaerium huanucoense

Machaerium huanucoense, commonly known as the Huanuco grass, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical or subtropical climates and is often found in shaded, forested areas. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of yellow or white.

Machaerium huanucoense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
