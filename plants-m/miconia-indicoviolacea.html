Miconia indicoviolacea

Miconia indicoviolacea, commonly known as the purple viola miconia, is a species of flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets make it an invasive species in some regions.
