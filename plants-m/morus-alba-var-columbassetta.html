Morus alba var. columbassetta

Morus alba var. columbassetta, commonly known as the white mulberry, is a variant of the white mulberry tree native to China.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are usually pale green with a slightly hairy texture. The tree produces small, aggregate fruits known as mulberries, which are typically white or pink in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its leaves, which are a vital food source for silkworms.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types.
