Mammillaria atrosanguinea

Mammillaria atrosanguinea, commonly known as the black blood mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white to grayish spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a striking contrast to its dark, almost black coloration. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
