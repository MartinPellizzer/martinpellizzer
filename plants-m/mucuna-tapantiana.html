Mucuna tapantiana

Mucuna tapantiana, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, violet to purple flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain a single, large, flat seed. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it useful in agricultural systems.

However, it can be invasive and is sometimes considered a weed in cultivated areas.
