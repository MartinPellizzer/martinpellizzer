Mazus henryi var. elatior

Mazus henryi var. elatior, commonly known as the greater mazus, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial native to China.

It features small, hairy, ovate to elliptical leaves with a bluish-green hue and a dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Mazus henryi var. elatior is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover due to its ornamental value and hardiness.
