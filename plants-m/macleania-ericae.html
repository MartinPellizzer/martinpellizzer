Macleania ericae

Macleania ericae, commonly known as the Florida heath, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shady, humid environments. Its compact growth habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for gardeners in suitable climates.

Macleania ericae plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small wildlife.
