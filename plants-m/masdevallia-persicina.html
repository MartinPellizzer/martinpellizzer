Masdevallia persicina

Masdevallia persicina, commonly known as the pink masdevallia, is a striking orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink to purple flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows as a miniature epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage consists of narrow, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

This species is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique coloration and relatively compact size.
