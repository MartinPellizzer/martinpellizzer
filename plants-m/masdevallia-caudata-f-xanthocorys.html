Masdevallia caudata f. xanthocorys

Masdevallia caudata f. xanthocorys, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed masdevallia, is a striking orchid species native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, pendulous inflorescence and vibrant, colorful flowers that often display a mix of red, orange, and yellow hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks and branches in its natural habitat. Its foliage consists of narrow, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

This unique hybrid is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and the intricate beauty of its blooms.
