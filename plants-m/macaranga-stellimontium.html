Macaranga stellimontium

Macaranga stellimontium, commonly known as the star-leaved macaranga, is a tropical shrub or small tree belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped leaves, which are usually opposite or whorled and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is native to Southeast Asia and is often found in moist, shaded forest understories.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
