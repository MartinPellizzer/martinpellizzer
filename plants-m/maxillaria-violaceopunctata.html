Maxillaria violaceopunctata

Maxillaria violaceopunctata, commonly known as the violet-punctated maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of violet and white. The flowers often have distinctive purple markings, which contribute to its common name. The plant typically blooms in the summer, and its leaves are broad and grass-like.

It is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
