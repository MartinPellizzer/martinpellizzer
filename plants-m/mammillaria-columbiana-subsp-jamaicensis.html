Mammillaria columbiana subsp. jamaicensis

Mammillaria columbiana subsp.

jamaicensis, commonly known as the Jamaican nipple cactus, is a small, globular cactus native to Jamaica. It features prominent, white, woolly spines and a dense covering of pale blue to white hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

This subspecies is notable for its compact growth and ornamental appearance, often cultivated as a decorative plant.
