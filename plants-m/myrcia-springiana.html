Myrcia springiana

Myrcia springiana, commonly known as the pink myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its attractive pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and often have a slightly aromatic scent.

Myrcia springiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
