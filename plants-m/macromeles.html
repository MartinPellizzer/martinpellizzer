Macromeles

Macromeles, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical Africa.

These plants are characterized by their velvety foliage and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. The leaves are typically rounded and have a thick, waxy texture, which helps them retain moisture in their native environment. Macromeles species are often cultivated as ornamental plants due to their aesthetic appeal and ease of care.

They thrive in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making them popular houseplants.
