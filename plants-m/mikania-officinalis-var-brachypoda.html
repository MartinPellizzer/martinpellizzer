Mikania officinalis var. brachypoda

Mikania officinalis var. brachypoda, commonly known as the short-stemmed chia plant, is a variant of the chia plant native to South America.

It is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family, characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy, branched stems. The plant produces small, oval seeds that are rich in omega-3 fatty acids, making them nutritionally valuable. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its seeds and ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it distinct from other varieties of Mikania.
