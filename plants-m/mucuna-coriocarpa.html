Mucuna coriocarpa

Mucuna coriocarpa, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, hairy pods that contain small, dark seeds. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural systems. Its flowers are typically purple or violet, and it produces a strong, sweet scent that attracts pollinators.

The seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein in some cuisines.
