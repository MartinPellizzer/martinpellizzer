Myrcia neomontana

Myrcia neomontana, commonly known as the mountain myrtle, is a shrub native to the cloud forests of South America, particularly in regions of Ecuador and Peru.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers that bloom during the wet season. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Myrcia neomontana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
