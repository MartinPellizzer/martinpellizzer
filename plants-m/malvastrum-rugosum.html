Malvastrum rugosum

Malvastrum rugosum, commonly known as the rough mallow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Malvastrum rugosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage and floral display.
