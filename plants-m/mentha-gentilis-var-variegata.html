Mentha gentilis var. variegata

Mentha gentilis var. variegata, commonly known as variegated mint, is a hybrid species of the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves, which display shades of green, white, and sometimes yellow. This plant is native to southern Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden borders or container planting.

The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
