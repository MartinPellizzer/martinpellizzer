Morina lehmanniana

Morina lehmanniana, commonly known as the Lehmann's moringa, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a tropical or subtropical climate.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is valued for its potential health benefits.
