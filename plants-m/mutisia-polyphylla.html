Mutisia polyphylla

Mutisia polyphylla, commonly known as the many-leaved mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy yellow flower heads. The plant features a woody stem with alternate, pinnately compound leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture.

Its flowers bloom in spring and are surrounded by pale green to yellow bracts, making it a distinctive and ornamental species in its native habitat.
