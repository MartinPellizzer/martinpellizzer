Mentha × niliaca subsp. timbalii

Mentha × niliaca subsp.

timbalii, commonly known as Timbalai mint, is a hybrid species within the Mentha genus. It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia.

This subspecies is characterized by its aromatic leaves and flowering spikes, which are typically purple or pink in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

It is valued for its essential oils, which are used in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.
