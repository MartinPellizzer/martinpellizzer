Mentha requienii subsp. requienii

Mentha requienii subsp.

requienii, commonly known as the Corsican mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a strong minty scent.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other members of the mint family.
