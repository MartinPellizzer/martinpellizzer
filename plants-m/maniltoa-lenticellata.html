Maniltoa lenticellata

Maniltoa lenticellata, commonly known as the smooth-bark maniltoa, is a member of the Rutaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, light gray bark that lacks lenticels, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a leathery texture with a prominent midrib.

The flowers are small, white, and clustered in inflorescences, often blooming in spring.
