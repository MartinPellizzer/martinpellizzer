Monardella pinetorum

Monardella pinetorum, commonly known as pine mountain beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in open coniferous forests and dry slopes, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, tubular flowers range in color from pink to purple and bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Monardella pinetorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
