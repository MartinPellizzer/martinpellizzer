Mila caespitosa f. kubeana

Mila caespitosa f. kubeana, commonly known as the Kubean tufted milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant features hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive, slightly serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and open habitats.

This variety is notable for its role in supporting local pollinators and its ornamental value in native plant gardens.
