Maianthemum henryi var. szechuanicum

Maianthemum henryi var. szechuanicum, commonly known as the Sichuan false Solomon's seal, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters and features slender, grass-like leaves that are usually arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, white or greenish-white flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in umbels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and subtle floral display.
