Mesembryanthemum frutescens

Mesembryanthemum frutescens, commonly known as the ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, often blue-green leaves that store water. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, pink, and white, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is widely cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
