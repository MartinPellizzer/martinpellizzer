Mundulea menabeensis

Mundulea menabeensis, commonly known as the Madagascar acacia, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds covered in a fibrous coat. It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by fixing nitrogen in the soil.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable to extinction.
