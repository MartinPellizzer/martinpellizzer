Melica magnolii var. gigantea

Melica magnolii var. gigantea, commonly known as the giant magnolia grass, is a striking member of the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces panicles of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. Native to the eastern United States, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for gardens with suitable conditions.
