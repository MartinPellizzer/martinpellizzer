Malva mexicana

Malva mexicana, commonly known as Mexican mallow, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring broad, velvety leaves and showy pink or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and okra, and is characterized by its hirsute (hairy) stems and capsule fruits. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant is often found in moist, sunny environments.

Malva mexicana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
