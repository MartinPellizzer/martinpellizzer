Mentha × durandoana

Mentha × durandoana, commonly known as pineapple mint, is a hybrid mint species resulting from the cross between Mentha × piperita and Mentha × gracilis.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly minty scent and its distinctive, pineapple-like fragrance. The plant produces small, pale pink or white flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems. Its leaves are narrow, serrated, and often have a silvery underside.

Mentha × durandoana is commonly cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and as a source of essential oils.
