Malus domestica var. dasyphylla

Malus domestica var. dasyphylla, commonly known as the rough-leaved apple, is a variant of the domestic apple tree.

It is characterized by its thick, warty bark and its distinctive, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or red in color. It is native to regions of Central Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This variety is well adapted to temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
