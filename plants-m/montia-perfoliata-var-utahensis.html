Montia perfoliata var. utahensis

Montia perfoliata var. utahensis, commonly known as the Utah leafy blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Montiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, leaf-like bracts that enclose the flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and clustered in dense inflorescences.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in cool, humid environments.
