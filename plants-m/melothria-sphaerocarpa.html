Melothria sphaerocarpa

Melothria sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the round-leaf little cucumber, is a small, herbaceous vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It produces small, round, green fruits that are edible and have a mild, cucumber-like flavor. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a food source in some regions.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both gardens and natural ecosystems.
