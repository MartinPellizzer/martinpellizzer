Myrtus mossambicensis

Myrtus mossambicensis, commonly known as the Mozambique myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a fragrant, resinous scent, and small white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Myrtus mossambicensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
