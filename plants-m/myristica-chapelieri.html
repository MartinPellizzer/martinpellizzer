Myristica chapelieri

Myristica chapelieri, commonly known as the Madagascar nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody fruits that contain a highly valued seed kernel. The plant produces small, white flowers and has compound leaves with elongated leaflets. Its seeds are used to produce essential oils and have historical significance in traditional medicine.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to the unique ecological conditions of its native habitat.
