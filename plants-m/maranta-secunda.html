Maranta secunda

Maranta secunda, commonly known as the silver leaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with silver and green patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are often hidden among the leaves. Its rhizomes are used as a starchy root crop, similar to arrowroot.

Maranta secunda thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive leaves.
