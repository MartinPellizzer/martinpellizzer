Momordica casea

Momordica casea, commonly known as bitter melon, is a tropical vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, ridged fruit that turns from green to orange as it matures. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its fruit is widely used in Asian cuisines, though its bitter taste is often mitigated through cooking.

Botanically, it is notable for its complex chemical composition, including various bioactive compounds.
