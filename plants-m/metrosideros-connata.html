Metrosideros connata

Metrosideros connata, commonly known as the Cook Islands flame tree, is a flowering plant native to the Cook Islands and other parts of Polynesia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows up to 10 meters tall, with a dense canopy of glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces striking clusters of red, orange, or yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year, especially during the warmer months. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and it is often used in traditional Hawaiian and Polynesian culture.
