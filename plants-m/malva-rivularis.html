Malva rivularis

Malva rivularis, commonly known as the water mallows, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as stream banks, ditches, and marshes. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance.

Malva rivularis is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
