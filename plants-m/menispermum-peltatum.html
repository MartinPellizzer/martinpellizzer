Menispermum peltatum

Menispermum peltatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the Menispermaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and unique, tubular flowers that resemble a pipe. The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits that contain toxic seeds, making it important to handle with care. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking shade-tolerant plants.
