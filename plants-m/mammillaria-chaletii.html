Mammillaria chaletii

Mammillaria chaletii, commonly known as the Chaletii Hedgehog Cactus, is a small, spineless cactus native to Mexico.

It features a globular or slightly cylindrical stem with a greenish-gray color and is covered in white, woolly hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise muted appearance. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is often sought after by cactus enthusiasts.
