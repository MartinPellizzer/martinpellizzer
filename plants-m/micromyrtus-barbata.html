Micromyrtus barbata

Micromyrtus barbata, commonly known as the barbed myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, urn-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and often have a distinctive barbed or serrated edge, contributing to its common name.

Micromyrtus barbata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
