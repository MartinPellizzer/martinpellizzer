Macropsychanthus rigidus

Macropsychanthus rigidus, commonly known as the rigid ghostflower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, ghost-like appearance, with pale, translucent petals that resemble spectral forms. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom at night, a feature that aids in its pollination by nocturnal insects. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to store water in its harsh, dry environment.

Due to its limited distribution and fragile habitat, Macropsychanthus rigidus is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
