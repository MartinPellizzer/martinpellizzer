Mammillaria subducta

Mammillaria subducta, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and dense, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its compact size and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
