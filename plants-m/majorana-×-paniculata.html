Majorana × paniculata

Majorana × paniculata, commonly known as the hybrid marjoram, is a cross between Majorana hortensis and Majorana paniculata.

It is a flowering plant in the family Lamiaceae, characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are used in culinary applications, while the flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This hybrid is valued for both its ornamental and medicinal properties.
