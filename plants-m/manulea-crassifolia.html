Manulea crassifolia

Manulea crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved manulea, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are adapted to conserve water in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep groundwater sources.

Manulea crassifolia is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is well-suited to harsh, semi-desert conditions.
