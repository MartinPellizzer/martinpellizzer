Masdevallia leonardoi

Masdevallia leonardoi, commonly known as the Leonard's masdevallia, is a rare and exotic orchid native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often colorful flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, thriving in humid, shaded environments with high air circulation. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette, while its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the forest floor.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a delicately endangered species.
