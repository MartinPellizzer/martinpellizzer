Manihot purpureocostata

Manihot purpureocostata, commonly known as the purple-costate arrowleaf, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking purple veins on its heart-shaped leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its roots contain compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
