Myrcia alloiota var. subcordata

Myrcia alloiota var. subcordata, commonly known as the broad-leafed myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and aromatic properties.
