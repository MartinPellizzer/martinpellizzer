Muscaria adscendens

Muscaria adscendens, commonly known as the ascending fly agaric, is a species of fungi belonging to the Amanita genus.

It is characterized by a distinctive white cap that turns yellowish with age, often adorned with a ring around the stem. The gills are typically white and closely spaced, and the stem is usually pale with a bulbous base. This mushroom is found in temperate regions, often growing in association with coniferous trees.

It is important to note that it contains psychoactive compounds and is not edible.
