Meoschium monostachys

Meoschium monostachys, commonly known as the narrow-leaved meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like cluster of small, two-lipped flowers.

Meoschium monostachys is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
