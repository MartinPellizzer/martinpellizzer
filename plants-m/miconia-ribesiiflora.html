Miconia ribesiiflora

Miconia ribesiiflora, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in branched inflorescences. Its fruits are fleshy berries that turn red when mature, containing numerous seeds.

Miconia ribesiiflora is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of tropical environments.
