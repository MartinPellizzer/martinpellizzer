Moraea iriopetala var. vegeta

Moraea iriopetala var. vegeta, commonly known as the Cape bellflower, is a striking perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, often with a contrasting throat. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a sought-after plant in horticulture.
