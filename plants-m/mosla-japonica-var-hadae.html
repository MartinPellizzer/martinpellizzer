Mosla japonica var. hadae

Mosla japonica var. hadae, commonly known as the Korean mosquito plant, is a perennial herb native to Korea and parts of eastern Asia.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its medicinal properties have been traditionally used in herbal remedies for digestive and respiratory ailments.
