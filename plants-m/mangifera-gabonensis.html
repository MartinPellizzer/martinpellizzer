Mangifera gabonensis

Mangifera gabonensis, commonly known as the Gabon mango, is a tropical tree native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its large, compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces edible fruits with a sweet, aromatic pulp that is often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid climates and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.

Due to its unique flavor and nutritional value, it is gaining attention for its potential in agricultural and culinary applications.
