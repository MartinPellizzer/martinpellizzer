Malva richii

Malva richii, commonly known as the rich's mallows, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft texture.

Malva richii is often found in moist soils near streams and wetlands, making it a characteristic wetland species.
