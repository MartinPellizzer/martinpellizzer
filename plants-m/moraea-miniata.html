Moraea miniata

Moraea miniata, commonly known as the Cape bell, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Moraea miniata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its delicate, showy blooms.
