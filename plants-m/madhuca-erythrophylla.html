Madhuca erythrophylla

Madhuca erythrophylla, commonly known as the red-leaf mahua, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves that turn red in autumn. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, starchy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
