Medinilla oligantha

Medinilla oligantha, commonly known as the velvet tangerine, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible and have a citrus-like flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Medinilla oligantha is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and unique foliage.
