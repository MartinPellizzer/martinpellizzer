Matricaria montana

Matricaria montana, commonly known as mountain chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers and linear, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, white to yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its essential oils, derived from the flowers, are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine for their calming properties.

Matricaria montana thrives in well-drained soils and cooler climates, making it a hardy perennial in suitable environments.
