Microcos latistipulata var. lanceolata

Microcos latistipulata var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf microcos, is a variant of the microcos plant native to parts of Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the dry season. Its botanical features include a woody stem with rough, hairy bark and small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
