Melilotus caeruleus var. laxiflorus

Melilotus caeruleus var. laxiflorus, commonly known as blue sweet clover, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in clusters along the stems. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
