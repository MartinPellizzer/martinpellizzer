Minuartia ciliata

Minuartia ciliata, commonly known as the silky rock-cress, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale pink. The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils, often found in alpine or arctic environments. Its leaves are usually covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Minuartia ciliata is adapted to harsh climates and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardiness.
