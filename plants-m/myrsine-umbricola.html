Myrsine umbricola

Myrsine umbricola, commonly known as the dark-leaved myrsine, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Myrsinaceae and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are berry-like, containing several seeds, and it is often found in shaded, moist environments.

Myrsine umbricola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
