Mertensia meyeriana

Mertensia meyeriana, commonly known as Meyer's bluebells, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces clusters of striking, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Mertensia meyeriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
