Malus sorbus

Malus sorbus, commonly known as whitebeam, is a genus of deciduous trees and shrubs native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple leaves and showy, white or yellowish flowers. The fruit is a small, dry drupe that turns red or orange in autumn. Whitebeam species are often found in woodlands and are valued for their ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.

They are also important for supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects.
