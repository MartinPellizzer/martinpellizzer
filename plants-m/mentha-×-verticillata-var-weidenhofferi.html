Mentha × verticillata var. weidenhofferi

Mentha × verticillata var. weidenhofferi, commonly known as the Weidenhoffer's water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for wetland restoration and ecological landscaping.
