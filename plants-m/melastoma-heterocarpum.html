Melastoma heterocarpum

Melastoma heterocarpum, commonly known as the purple mangostin, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking purple flowers and dark, segmented fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are ovate with a glossy surface, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

The fruit, which is edible, has a thick, aromatic rind and a segmented, juicy interior.
