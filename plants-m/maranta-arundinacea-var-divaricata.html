Maranta arundinacea var. divaricata

Maranta arundinacea var. divaricata, commonly known as the arrowroot plant, is a tropical perennial herb native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often variegated with white and green markings. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its rhizomes are the primary part used, containing a starchy substance that is processed into a gluten-free flour.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and its culinary and medicinal applications.
