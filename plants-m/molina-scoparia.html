Molina scoparia

Molina scoparia, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Molina scoparia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
