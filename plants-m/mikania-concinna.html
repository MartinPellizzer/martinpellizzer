Mikania concinna

Mikania concinna, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a vigorous vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail along surfaces. It thrives in a variety of soil types and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its invasive nature in some regions, it is often considered a weed in agricultural and natural settings.
