Myrceugenia ribeireana

Myrceugenia ribeireana, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, edible berries. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically black when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal regions with well-drained soil.

Myrceugenia ribeireana is valued not only for its culinary uses but also for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
