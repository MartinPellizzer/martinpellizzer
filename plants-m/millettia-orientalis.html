Millettia orientalis

Millettia orientalis, commonly known as the oriental millettia, is a deciduous climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically small, greenish-white, and arranged in clusters. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Millettia orientalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage.
