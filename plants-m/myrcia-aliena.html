Myrcia aliena

Myrcia aliena, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Myrcia aliena has been introduced to other regions, where it sometimes becomes an invasive species.
