Muhlenbergia uniflora var. terrae-novae

Muhlenbergia uniflora var. terrae-novae, commonly known as the Nova Scotia muhlenbergia, is a perennial grass native to the coastal regions of Nova Scotia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, silver-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear delicate, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to maritime climates with salt spray.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in coastal dune ecosystems.
