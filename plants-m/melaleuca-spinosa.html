Melaleuca spinosa

Melaleuca spinosa, commonly known as the spinous tea-tree, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and produces small, bottle-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy environments. It is a member of the Myrtaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like eucalyptus and tea-tree.

Melaleuca spinosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
