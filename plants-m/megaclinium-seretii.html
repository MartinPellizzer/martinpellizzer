Megaclinium seretii

Megaclinium seretii, commonly known as the Seretii's grass, is a perennial plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale green to brown florets. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
