Myrciaria vexator

Myrciaria vexator, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá-do-brejo, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and clusters of small, red, edible fruits. The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in wetlands and riverine forests. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces a distinctive, aromatic fragrance.

The fruit is rich in vitamin C and is used both in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.
