Mimosa acroconica

Mimosa acroconica, commonly known as the yellow mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

However, it is considered invasive in some regions due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
