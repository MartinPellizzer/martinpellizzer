Matricaria broussonetii

Matricaria broussonetii, commonly known as the Egyptian chamomile, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flower heads that resemble chamomile. The plant is characterized by its aromatic properties, with a scent reminiscent of chamomile due to the presence of essential oils. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

The flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their calming and anti-inflammatory effects.
