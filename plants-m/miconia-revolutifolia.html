Miconia revolutifolia

Miconia revolutifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a large shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically white or pale pink, and it produces berries that are edible for some animals.

Miconia revolutifolia is often found in moist, shaded areas of lowland forests.
