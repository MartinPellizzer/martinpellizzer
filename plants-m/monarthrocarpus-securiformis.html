Monarthrocarpus securiformis

Monarthrocarpus securiformis, commonly known as the African sand dollar, is a unique succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded clusters of fleshy, green to blue-green leaves that form a compact, ball-like structure. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with thick leaves that store water and reduce transpiration. It produces small, yellowish flowers during the rainy season, which are followed by fruit that splits open to release seeds.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a decorative plant.
