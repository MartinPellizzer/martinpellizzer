Memorialis hispida var. gardneriana

Memorialis hispida var. gardneriana, commonly known as the Gardner's spiny holly, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright growth habit and small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its distinctive appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and landscape design.
