Mediolobivia sarothroides

Mediolobivia sarothroides, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a striking flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale purple, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by succulent enthusiasts.
