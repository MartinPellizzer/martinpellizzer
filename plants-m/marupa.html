Marupa

Marupa, commonly known as the "jellybean plant," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its striking, colorful, bean-like seed pods that range in hues from yellow to orange and red. These pods are not only ornamental but also edible, often consumed raw or cooked.

Marupa thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and edible seeds.
