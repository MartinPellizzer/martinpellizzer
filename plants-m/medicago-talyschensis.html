Medicago talyschensis

Medicago talyschensis, commonly known as the Talyschense clover, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It has small, round, and hairy seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Medicago talyschensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and potential for agricultural use.
