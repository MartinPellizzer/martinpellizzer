Microcerasus glandulosa f. nakaii

Microcerasus glandulosa f. nakaii, commonly known as the Nakai's waxleaf cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in early spring, followed by small, red berries in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental gardens.

Its compact growth habit and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for landscape design.
