Mentha × montana

Mentha × montana, commonly known as marsh mint, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the wetlands of North America.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Mentha × montana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near ponds, streams, and other water bodies.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, making it a valuable plant for both ecological and potential medicinal uses.
