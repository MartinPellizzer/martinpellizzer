Montbretia capensis

Montbretia capensis, commonly known as the yellow oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its bright orange to yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant features a fibrous root system and produces small, reddish-brown tubers underground. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern.

Montbretia capensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soils.
