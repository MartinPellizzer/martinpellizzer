Mimulus primuloides var. minimus

Mimulus primuloides var. minimus, commonly known as the dwarf yellow monkeyflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically reaches a height of only a few inches, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in stream banks and wet meadows.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
