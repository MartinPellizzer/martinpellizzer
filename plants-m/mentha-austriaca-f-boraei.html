Mentha austriaca f. boraei

Mentha austriaca f. boraei, commonly known as the alpine mint, is a cold-hardy perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. This variety thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and forested areas at high elevations. The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.

Its growth habit and botanical features make it well adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments.
