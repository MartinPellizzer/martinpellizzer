Myrcia marmeladensis

Myrcia marmeladensis, commonly known as the orange myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically orange in color, which are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Myrcia marmeladensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
