Malus sieboldii

Malus sieboldii, commonly known as the Japanese flowering crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, edible apples that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Malus sieboldii is also valued for its ornamental value and is often used in landscaping for its seasonal interest.
