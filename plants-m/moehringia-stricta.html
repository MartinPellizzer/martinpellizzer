Moehringia stricta

Moehringia stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf moehringia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white flowers bloom in clusters during the spring and early summer.

Moehringia stricta is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its delicate appearance and ecological importance in supporting local insect populations.
