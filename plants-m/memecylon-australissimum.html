Memecylon australissimum

Memecylon australissimum, commonly known as the Australian memecylon, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and features lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests and understorey habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
