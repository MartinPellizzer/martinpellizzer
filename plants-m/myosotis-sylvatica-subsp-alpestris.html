Myosotis sylvatica subsp. alpestris

Myosotis sylvatica subsp.

alpestris, commonly known as the alpine forget-me-not, is a subspecies native to mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, preferring well-drained conditions. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in open meadows or along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in cooler, high-altitude environments.
