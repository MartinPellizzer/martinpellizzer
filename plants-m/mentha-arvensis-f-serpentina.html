Mentha arvensis f. serpentina

Mentha arvensis f. serpentina, commonly known as creeping mint, is a variant of the field mint species.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit and aromatic, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its essential oils contain menthol and other compounds, making it useful in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.
