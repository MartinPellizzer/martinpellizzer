Mikania ulei

Mikania ulei, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and trailing habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the early morning. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often twining.

Mikania ulei is known for its ability to spread quickly, making it both a valuable forage plant and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
