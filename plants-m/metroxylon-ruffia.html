Metroxylon ruffia

Metroxylon ruffia, commonly known as the Ruffia palm, is a species of palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible, fibrous fruits. Its trunk is valued for its strong, fibrous material used in traditional crafts and construction.

The Ruffia palm plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
