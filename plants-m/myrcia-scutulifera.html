Myrcia scutulifera

Myrcia scutulifera, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, with a pleasant aromatic scent when crushed.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.
