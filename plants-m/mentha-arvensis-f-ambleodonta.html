Mentha arvensis f. ambleodonta

Mentha arvensis f. ambleodonta, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a horticultural form of the field mint.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. This form is typically smaller than the standard Mentha arvensis and has a more compact growth habit. It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive texture and color.

The plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.
