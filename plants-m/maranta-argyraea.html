Maranta argyraea

Maranta argyraea, commonly known as the silver-veined arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage with prominent veins. The plant features upright, tuberous roots that are used in traditional medicine and as a starch source. Its leaves are typically oval or lance-shaped, and it thrives in humid, shady environments.

Maranta argyraea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
