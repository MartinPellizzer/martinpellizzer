Macropsychanthus lauterbachii

Macropsychanthus lauterbachii, commonly known as the Lauterbach's ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, delicate inflorescences that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant produces small, pale blue to white flowers that bloom during the cooler months, typically in spring. Its leaves are succulent, adapted to store water in its native dry environment.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
