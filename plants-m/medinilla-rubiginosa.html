Medinilla rubiginosa

Medinilla rubiginosa, commonly known as the red velvet plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-red flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Medinilla rubiginosa is also valued for its ability to purify indoor air and enhance the aesthetic appeal of indoor spaces.
