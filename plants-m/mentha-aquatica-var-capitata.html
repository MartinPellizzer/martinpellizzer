Mentha aquatica var. capitata

Mentha aquatica var. capitata, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist environments in Europe and Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are often submerged or floating in water. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams.

Mentha aquatica var. capitata is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
