Myriophyllum filiforme

Myriophyllum filiforme, commonly known as narrowleaf watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It has slender, branching stems that are often covered with small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces tiny, floating flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as lakes and ponds.

Myriophyllum filiforme plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
