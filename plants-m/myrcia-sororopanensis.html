Myrcia sororopanensis

Myrcia sororopanensis, commonly known as the Amazonian myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine.

Myrcia sororopanensis is also valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumes and as a natural remedy.
