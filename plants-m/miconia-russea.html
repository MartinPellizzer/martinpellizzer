Miconia russea

Miconia russea, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by distinctive, round, fleshy fruits. Miconia russea is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable plant in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
