Monochoria australasica

Monochoria australasica, commonly known as the Australian arrowhead, is a water-loving plant native to the wetlands of Australia.

It is a perennial herb with slender, branching stems that often grow in shallow aquatic environments. The plant features heart-shaped, arrowhead-like leaves that are typically submerged or floating. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and it produces distinctive, boat-shaped fruits.

Monochoria australasica plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
