Masdevallia plantaginea

Masdevallia plantaginea, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant prefers cool, humid environments and is often grown as a terrestrial or epiphytic orchid. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store moisture in its natural habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is relatively easy to cultivate with proper care.
