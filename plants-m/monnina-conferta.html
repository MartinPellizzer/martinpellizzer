Monnina conferta

Monnina conferta, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance.

Monnina conferta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
