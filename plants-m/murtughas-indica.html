Murtughas indica

Murtughas indica, commonly known as the Indian date palm, is a tall, palm-like tree native to South Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach several meters in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into sweet, juicy dates. It thrives in warm, arid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is not only a food source but also has cultural and economic significance in many regions.
