Malaxis lunifera

Malaxis lunifera, commonly known as the moon orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, moon-like appearance, with pale green to white flowers that resemble a crescent moon. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in humid, tropical environments. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated spike bearing several small, fragrant flowers.

The botanical features of Malaxis lunifera make it a popular subject in botanical gardens and among orchid enthusiasts.
