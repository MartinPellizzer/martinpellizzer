Matelea filipes

Matelea filipes, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a climbing shrub that produces large, showy white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Due to its unique flowering habit and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
