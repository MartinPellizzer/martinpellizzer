Maxillaria inaequisepala

Maxillaria inaequisepala, commonly known as the unequal-sepal maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often fragrant flowers that display a variety of colors, including yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

The unique structure of its sepals and petals makes it a distinctive and sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
