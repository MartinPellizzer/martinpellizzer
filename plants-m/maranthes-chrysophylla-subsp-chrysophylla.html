Maranthes chrysophylla subsp. chrysophylla

Maranthes chrysophylla subsp.

chrysophylla, commonly known as the golden-leaf maranthes, is a species native to southern Africa. It is a perennial herb characterized by its rosette growth habit and striking yellow-green foliage.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its unique leaf coloration.
