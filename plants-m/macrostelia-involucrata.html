Macrostelia involucrata

Macrostelia involucrata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on long, slender stalks. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The species is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive weed in certain areas.
