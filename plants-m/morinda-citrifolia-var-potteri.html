Morinda citrifolia var. potteri

Morinda citrifolia var. potteri, commonly known as the Hawaiian mamane, is a shrub or small tree native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray-green bark and oval-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, orange-red berries. It thrives in coastal and upland habitats, often growing in dry, well-drained soils.

Due to its ecological and cultural significance, it is also valued for its traditional uses in Hawaiian medicine and as a food source.
