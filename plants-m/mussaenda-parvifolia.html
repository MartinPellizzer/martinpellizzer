Mussaenda parvifolia

Mussaenda parvifolia, commonly known as the dwarf flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, orange-red bracts that resemble flowers, though they are actually modified leaves. The plant has simple, opposite, and ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Mussaenda parvifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
