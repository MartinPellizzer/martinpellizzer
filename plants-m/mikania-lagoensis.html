Mikania lagoensis

Mikania lagoensis, commonly known as the Brazilian morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the morning, giving it its common name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Mikania lagoensis is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain areas.
