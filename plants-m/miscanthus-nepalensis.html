Miscanthus nepalensis

Miscanthus nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal silver grass, is a perennial grass native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery plumes that bloom in the late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a silvery-blue hue that adds ornamental value to gardens.

Miscanthus nepalensis is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
