Mediolobivia pectinata

Mediolobivia pectinata, commonly known as the featherleaf orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pale lavender flowers during the spring season. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
