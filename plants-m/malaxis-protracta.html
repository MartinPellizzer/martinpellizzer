Malaxis protracta

Malaxis protracta, commonly known as the long-stemmed Malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and linear, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces a single, elongated spike bearing several pale green to yellowish flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its delicate appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
