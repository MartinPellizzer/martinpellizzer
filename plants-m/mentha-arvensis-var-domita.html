Mentha arvensis var. domita

Mentha arvensis var. domita, commonly known as field mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue in color. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
