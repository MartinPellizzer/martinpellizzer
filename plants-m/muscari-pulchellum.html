Muscari pulchellum

Muscari pulchellum, commonly known as the Persian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features dense clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

Muscari pulchellum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to naturalize over time.
