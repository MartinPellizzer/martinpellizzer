Monocladus amplexicaulis

Monocladus amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping monoclad, is a unique plant species belonging to the family Monocladaceae.

It is characterized by its singular stem, which often wraps around its base, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are alternate and often have a waxy surface, which helps in reducing water loss.

The flowers, though small, are usually borne in clusters and can be either white or pale yellow in color.
