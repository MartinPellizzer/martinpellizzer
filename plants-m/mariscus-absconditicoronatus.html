Mariscus absconditicoronatus

Mariscus absconditicoronatus, commonly known as the hidden-crown mariscus, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and features small, silver-gray leaves covered in fine, reflective hairs. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pale blue and violet, which bloom during the short summer season. Its unique morphology and restricted habitat make it a subject of interest for botanists studying alpine flora.

Due to its elusive nature and limited distribution, it remains poorly understood and is considered a conservation concern.
