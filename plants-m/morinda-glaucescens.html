Morinda glaucescens

Morinda glaucescens, commonly known as the blue morinda, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that range from green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Morinda glaucescens is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
