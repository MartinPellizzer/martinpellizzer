Myrtus coriacea

Myrtus coriacea, commonly known as rough myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves that are rough to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark blue or black berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Myrtus coriacea is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
