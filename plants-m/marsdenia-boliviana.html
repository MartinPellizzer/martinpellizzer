Marsdenia boliviana

Marsdenia boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian Marsdenia, is a climbing shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are follicles that contain seeds with a tuft of hair, aiding in wind dispersal.

Marsdenia boliviana is often found in humid, tropical environments and is valued for its ornamental potential.
