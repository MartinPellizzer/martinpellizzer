Myrcia berberis var. latifolia

Myrcia berberis var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf myrcia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the coastal regions of southeastern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
