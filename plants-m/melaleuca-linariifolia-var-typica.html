Melaleuca linariifolia var. typica

Melaleuca linariifolia var. typica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf paperbark, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, papery bark that peels in strips, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bottle-shaped flowers that are typically red or pink, which bloom in winter and attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a smooth surface and a slight waxy coating.

It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in heathlands and open forests.
