Mesembryanthemum hoerleinianum

Mesembryanthemum hoerleinianum, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or Physalis peruviana, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and spherical, orange-red berries that resemble small tomatoes. The plant thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are succulent, and the plant is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid climates.

The fruit is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor, often used in culinary applications.
