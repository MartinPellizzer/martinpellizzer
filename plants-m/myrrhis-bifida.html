Myrrhis bifida

Myrrhis bifida, commonly known as the splitleaf myrrh, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom in late summer. Myrrhis bifida typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
