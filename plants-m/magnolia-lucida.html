Magnolia lucida

Magnolia lucida, commonly known as the shining magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The tree has smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its flowers are typically 6 to 8 inches in diameter and are composed of nine petals.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
