Mesembryanthemum angulipes

Mesembryanthemum angulipes, commonly known as the angular leaf ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, often blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is widely cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
