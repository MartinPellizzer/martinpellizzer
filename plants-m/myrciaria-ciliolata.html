Myrciaria ciliolata

Myrciaria ciliolata, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit. The plant produces small, black berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and covered in fine hairs.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland areas with well-drained soil.
