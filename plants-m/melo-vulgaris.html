Melo vulgaris

Melo vulgaris, commonly known as the cantaloupe, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a climbing vine that produces large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers. The fruit is characterized by its netted rind and sweet, aromatic flesh. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Melo vulgaris is widely cultivated for its edible fruit, which is rich in vitamins A and C.
