Mangifera timorensis

Mangifera timorensis, commonly known as the Timor mango, is a tropical tree native to the island of Timor in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and fragrant, yellow-orange fruits. The fruit is typically small to medium in size, with a fibrous pulp that is sweet and aromatic. It is adapted to grow in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is valued for its edible fruit and potential use in horticulture and conservation efforts.
