Mimulus glabratus var. michiganensis

Mimulus glabratus var. michiganensis, commonly known as the Michigan monkeyflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the United States.

It is characterized by its bright orange to yellow tubular flowers, which are typically borne in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are generally hairless, giving it a smooth appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other riparian habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
