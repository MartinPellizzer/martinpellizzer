Medicago hispida var. inermis

Medicago hispida var. inermis, commonly known as goat's rue, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, spurred flowers that bloom in shades of purple or pink. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in rocky or disturbed areas. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment.

However, it can become invasive in certain environments if not managed properly.
