Megalastrum martinicense

Megalastrum martinicense, commonly known as the Martinique starfruit, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped fruits. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are typically harvested when they are fully mature. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruits.
