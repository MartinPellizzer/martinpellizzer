Melilotus indicus var. exaltatus

Melilotus indicus var. exaltatus, commonly known as the tall sweet clover, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia and the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, covered with small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense spikes. The plant produces trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it is known for its sweet, honey-like fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
