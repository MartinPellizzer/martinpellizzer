Masdevallia pachygyne

Masdevallia pachygyne, commonly known as the thick-leafed masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dark green, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically during the cooler months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
