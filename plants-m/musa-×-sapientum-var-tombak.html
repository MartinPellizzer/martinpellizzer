Musa × sapientum var. tombak

Musa × sapientum var. tombak, commonly known as the red banana, is a variety of banana characterized by its distinctive reddish-purple peel.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is cultivated for both its ornamental value and edible fruit. The plant is a herbaceous perennial, typically growing as a clump of pseudostems formed from overlapping leaves. Its fruit develops in clusters called hands, with each fruit being a elongated, curved berry.

The variety is prized for its unique coloration and is often used in horticultural displays.
