Mespilus crenulata

Mespilus crenulata, commonly known as the European whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves with wavy edges. The tree produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries in late summer.

It is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ecological importance and ornamental qualities.
