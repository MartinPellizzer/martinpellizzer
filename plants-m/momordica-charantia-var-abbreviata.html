Momordica charantia var. abbreviata

Momordica charantia var. abbreviata, commonly known as the short-stalked bitter melon, is a variant of the bitter gourd native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its shorter, more compact stems compared to the typical bitter melon. The plant produces fruits that are generally smaller and have a less pronounced ridge pattern. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits.

Botanically, it belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, sharing traits with other cucurbits like cucumbers and squash.
