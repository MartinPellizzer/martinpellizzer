Melia angustifolia

Melia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf chinaberry, is a deciduous tree native to southern Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The tree produces small, berry-like fruits that turn from green to yellow or orange as they mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to various regions around the world.

The plant has also been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
