Musa × paradisiaca var. vittata

Musa × paradisiaca var. vittata, commonly known as the spotted banana, is a cultivar of the banana plant characterized by its distinctive variegated leaves.

The leaves exhibit a striking pattern of green and white or yellow markings, which make it a popular ornamental plant. It is a member of the Musaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia. This variety is typically grown for its aesthetic appeal rather than for its fruit, as the fruit is usually not edible.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
