Marsdenia grandiflora

Marsdenia grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered Marsdenia, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive corona. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
