Maxillaria heterophylla var. longifolia

Maxillaria heterophylla var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved maxillaria, is a hybrid orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often with a contrasting lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and ease of cultivation under the right conditions.
