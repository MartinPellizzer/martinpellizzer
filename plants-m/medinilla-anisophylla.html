Medinilla anisophylla

Medinilla anisophylla, commonly known as the velvet tangerine.

This tropical plant is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its striking, waxy, violet-pink flowers that resemble citrus fruits. The leaves are broad, glossy, and often have a velvety texture, contributing to its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Medinilla anisophylla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
