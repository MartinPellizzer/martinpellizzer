Myagrum grandiflorum

Myagrum grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered myrtle, is a tropical shrub native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, fragrant white to pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has smooth, dark green foliage and produces small, edible berries that are used in traditional Aboriginal cuisine. Myagrum grandiflorum thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens in its native region.
