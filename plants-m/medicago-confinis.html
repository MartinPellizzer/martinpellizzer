Medicago confinis

Medicago confinis, commonly known as the narrowleaf medick, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps it thrive in poor soil conditions. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along roadsides.

Medicago confinis is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
