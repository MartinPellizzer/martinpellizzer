Melocactus appropinquatus

Melocactus appropinquatus, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a species of cactus native to the dry regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and a crown of long, spines that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green body. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments.

Melocactus appropinquatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique form and resilience.
