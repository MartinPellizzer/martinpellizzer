Malus orientalis

Malus orientalis, commonly known as the Chinese apple or oriental crabapple, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its small, ornamental fruits and showy flowers. The plant typically grows as a deciduous tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a serrated margin.

The flowers, which appear in early spring, are usually white or pink and are followed by small, tart fruits that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine.
