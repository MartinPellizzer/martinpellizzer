Musa calosperma

Musa calosperma, commonly known as the red river banana, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and clustered inflorescences. The plant produces edible berries that are typically small, red, and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Musa calosperma is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its edible fruit.
