Morina kokanica

Morina kokanica, commonly known as the Kokanica moringa, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and pinnately compound leaves. The plant is typically shrub-like, with a height ranging from 1 to 3 meters. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are sometimes used in local cuisines.
