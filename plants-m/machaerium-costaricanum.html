Machaerium costaricanum

Machaerium costaricanum, commonly known as the Costa Rican machaerium, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in humid, tropical climates. Its flowers are often pink or purple, and it produces small, pod-like fruits.

Machaerium costaricanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological roles in supporting local biodiversity.
