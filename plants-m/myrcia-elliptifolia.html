Myrcia elliptifolia

Myrcia elliptifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink in color. Its fruit is a small, red berry that contains several seeds.

Myrcia elliptifolia is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a variety of habitats including forests and coastal areas.
