Magnolia glauca var. argentea

Magnolia glauca var. argentea, commonly known as the silver magnolia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale green, waxy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, appearing in early spring before the leaves emerge. Its foliage turns a vibrant yellow in the fall, adding ornamental value to the landscape.

This variety is prized for its elegant appearance and adaptability to a variety of soil types.
