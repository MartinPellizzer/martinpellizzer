Masdevallia semiteres

Masdevallia semiteres, commonly known as the narrow-leaf masdevallia, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated flowers that often display vivid colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique and colorful blooms.
