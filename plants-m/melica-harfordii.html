Melica harfordii

Melica harfordii, commonly known as Harford's brome, is a perennial grass species native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces spikelets that are usually tinged with purple or reddish hues. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Melica harfordii plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments and is of interest to botanists and ecologists.
