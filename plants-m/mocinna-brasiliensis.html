Mocinna brasiliensis

Mocinna brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian coffee tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits containing seeds. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, and it is known for its aromatic properties.

The plant is primarily cultivated for its seeds, which are used in the production of coffee.
