Maba forbesii

Maba forbesii, commonly known as the African wild pear, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits. Its fruit is often used in local cuisines and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

Maba forbesii is also valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
