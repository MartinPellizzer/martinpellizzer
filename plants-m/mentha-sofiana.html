Mentha sofiana

Mentha sofiana, commonly known as the Persian mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or violet in color. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a strong, minty fragrance.

Mentha sofiana is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal remedies.
