Mansoa montecillensis

Mansoa montecillensis, commonly known as the Peruvian bluebell, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
