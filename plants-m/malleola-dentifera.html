Malleola dentifera

Malleola dentifera, commonly known as the toothed malleola, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, serrated leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom during the wet season. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Malleola dentifera is often found in humid forest understories where it contributes to the biodiversity of the ecosystem.
