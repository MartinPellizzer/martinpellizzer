Muraltia namaquensis

Muraltia namaquensis, commonly known as the Namaqua bushveld bean, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in shades of pink or white.

The seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods and are an important food source for local wildlife.
