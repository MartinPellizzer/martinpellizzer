Melothria scaberrima

Melothria scaberrima, commonly known as the sour sop or custard apple, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is a small, deciduous shrub that produces oval, hairy fruits with a thick, scabrous rind. The fruit contains a creamy, aromatic pulp that is rich in nutrients and has a unique, sweet-tart flavor. The plant's leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is commonly cultivated for its edible fruit.
