Matricaria nigellifolia

Matricaria nigellifolia, commonly known as blackseed matricaria, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, followed by dark, oily seeds that are rich in essential oils and nutrients. Its leaves are generally narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinctive aromatic scent.

This species is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and as a source of natural oils.
