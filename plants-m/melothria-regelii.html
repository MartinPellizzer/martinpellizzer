Melothria regelii

Melothria regelii, commonly known as the yellow passionfruit, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are rich in vitamin C and have a tart, tangy flavor. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces small, white flowers. Its botanical classification places it in the family Passifloraceae, which includes other passionfruit species.

Melothria regelii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
