Machilus salicinus

Machilus salicinus, commonly known as the Chinese laurustin, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale yellow, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Machilus salicinus is also valued for its aromatic properties and historical use in traditional medicine.
