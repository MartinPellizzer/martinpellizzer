Montia hallii var. variabilis

Montia hallii var. variabilis, commonly known as the variable montia, is a flowering plant species native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Montiaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It is an annual or biennial herb that exhibits considerable variation in leaf shape and flower size, contributing to its common name.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
