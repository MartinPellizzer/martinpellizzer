Muhlenbergia gooddingii

Muhlenbergia gooddingii, commonly known as Goodding's muhlenbergia, is a grass species native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Muhlenbergia gooddingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
