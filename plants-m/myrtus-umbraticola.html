Myrtus umbraticola

Myrtus umbraticola, commonly known as the shade myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in woodland and shaded areas. The plant is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Myrtus umbraticola is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to tolerate partial shade.
