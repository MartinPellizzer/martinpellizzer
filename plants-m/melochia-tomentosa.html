Melochia tomentosa

Melochia tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy melochia, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific, often growing in moist, shady environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and can become invasive in certain areas.
