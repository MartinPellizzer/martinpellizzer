Melica bonariensis

Melica bonariensis, commonly known as the Argentine brome, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and elongated, narrow leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically green or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Melica bonariensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
