Mangifera duperreana

Mangifera duperreana, commonly known as the giant mango, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and fragrant, yellow-orange fruits. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a broad, umbrella-like canopy. Its fruit, which is typically harvested when fully ripe, contains a sweet, fibrous pulp surrounded by a thick, inedible rind.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential economic importance in regions where it is cultivated.
