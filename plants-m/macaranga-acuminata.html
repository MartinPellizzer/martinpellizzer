Macaranga acuminata

Macaranga acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-leaved macaranga, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a fast-growing shrub or small tree that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins and a distinctive acuminate tip. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsules that release tiny seeds.

Macaranga acuminata is often found in disturbed sites and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
