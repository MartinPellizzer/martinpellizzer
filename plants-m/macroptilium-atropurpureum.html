Macroptilium atropurpureum

Macroptilium atropurpureum, commonly known as purple prairie clover, is a perennial legume native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking purple to pink flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens and restoration projects. Its root system helps prevent soil erosion, and it plays a vital role in nitrogen fixation.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
