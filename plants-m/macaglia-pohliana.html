Macaglia pohliana

Macaglia pohliana, commonly known as the Pohliana macaglia, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the study of island flora.
