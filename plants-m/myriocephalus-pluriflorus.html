Myriocephalus pluriflorus

Myriocephalus pluriflorus, commonly known as the many-flowered helmet plant, is a unique species native to the subtropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive helmet-shaped inflorescence, which consists of numerous small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage is broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy texture that helps retain moisture.

The flowers, which bloom in late spring, are usually a mix of white and pale yellow, attracting pollinators like bees and moths.
