Miconia tentaculicapitata

Miconia tentaculicapitata, commonly known as the tentacle-headed miconia, is a member of the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some areas.
