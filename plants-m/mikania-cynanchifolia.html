Mikania cynanchifolia

Mikania cynanchifolia, commonly known as Mexican devil's vine, is a vigorous climbing plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, facilitating its rapid spread. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and forests.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered an invasive species in many regions.
