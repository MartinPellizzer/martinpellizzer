Muhlenbergia umbrosa subsp. attenuata

Muhlenbergia umbrosa subsp.

attenuata, commonly known as the slender muhlenbergia, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

This subspecies typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late summer, which are clustered in panicles.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
