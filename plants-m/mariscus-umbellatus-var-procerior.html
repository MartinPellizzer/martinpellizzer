Mariscus umbellatus var. procerior

Mariscus umbellatus var. procerior, commonly known as the long-stemmed mariscus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique floral structure and limited distribution.
