Miconia abbreviata

Miconia abbreviata, commonly known as the short-stalked miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are typically followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Miconia abbreviata is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable plant in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
