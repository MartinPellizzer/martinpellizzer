Magnolia grandiflora var. exoniensis

Magnolia grandiflora var. exoniensis, commonly known as the Exeter magnolia, is a cultivar prized for its large, showy white flowers and elegant, broad-ovate leaves.

It is a deciduous tree native to the southern United States, particularly Florida and Georgia, and thrives in USDA hardiness zones 7 through 9. The plant features a slow growth rate and a rounded, spreading canopy, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping. Its flowers, which appear in early spring, emit a sweet fragrance and are followed by dark red, fuzzy seed pods.

The leaves turn yellow in the fall, adding seasonal interest to the landscape.
