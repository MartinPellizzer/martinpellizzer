Monardella robinsonii

Monardella robinsonii, commonly known as Robinson's monardella, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
