Memecylon zanzibaricum

Memecylon zanzibaricum, commonly known as the Zanzibar memecylon, is a small evergreen tree native to the coastal regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by oval, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal forests and mangroves.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
