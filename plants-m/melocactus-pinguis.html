Melocactus pinguis

Melocactus pinguis, commonly known as the fat melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stem that can grow up to 1 meter in height, often resembling a melon in shape and texture. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by small, red, edible fruits. Its succulent structure allows it to store water efficiently, making it well-adapted to desert environments.

The species is also prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in arid gardens.
