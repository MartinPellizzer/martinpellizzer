Medinilla pedunculosa

Medinilla pedunculosa, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or chocolate plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to yellow or orange when mature.

Medinilla pedunculosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
