Medusea globosa

Medusea globosa, commonly known as the globe spongeplant, is a unique succulent native to arid regions.

It features a compact, globular shape with thick, fleshy stems that store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, which help minimize water loss.

Medusea globosa is well adapted to full sun and requires minimal watering, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
