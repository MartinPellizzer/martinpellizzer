Mimulus guttatus subsp. haidensis

Mimulus guttatus subsp.

haidensis, commonly known as the Haiden's monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its showy, bright orange or yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The subspecies typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant is pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
