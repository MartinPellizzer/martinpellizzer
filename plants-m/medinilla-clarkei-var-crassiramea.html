Medinilla clarkei var. crassiramea

Medinilla clarkei var. crassiramea, commonly known as the broad-leaf medinilla, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and indoor cultivation.
