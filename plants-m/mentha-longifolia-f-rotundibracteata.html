Mentha longifolia f. rotundibracteata

Mentha longifolia f. rotundibracteata, commonly known as round-bract mint, is a horticultural form of the spearmint plant.

It is characterized by its rounded bracts that enclose the flower clusters, distinguishing it from the typical form of Mentha longifolia. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pale blue in color. It is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves and ornamental value in gardens.
