Melicope fatuhivensis

Melicope fatuhivensis, commonly known as the Tahitian olive, is a small evergreen tree native to the Society Islands in French Polynesia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically green when mature, though they may turn yellow or red depending on the variety. Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
