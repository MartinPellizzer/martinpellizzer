Memora peregrina

Memora peregrina, commonly known as the wandering memory plant, is a rare and elusive species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like leaves and small, pale-blue flowers that bloom only under specific environmental conditions. The plant has a unique ability to retain traces of past environmental conditions in its cellular structure, making it a subject of scientific interest. Its growth is slow and requires precise soil composition and temperature ranges to thrive.

Due to its rarity and ecological sensitivity, it is considered a conservation priority in its native habitat.
