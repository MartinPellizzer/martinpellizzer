Micranthocereus dolichospermaticus

Micranthocereus dolichospermaticus, commonly known as the long-sepal cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its green, ribbed stems and spiny areoles. The plant produces pale yellow flowers that bloom during the summer months. Its adaptations include succulent stems for water storage and nocturnal blooming to avoid daytime heat.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well-suited for rocky and well-drained soils.
