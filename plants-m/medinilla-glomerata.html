Medinilla glomerata

Medinilla glomerata, commonly known as the clustered medinilla, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered inflorescences that produce showy, waxy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has thick, leathery leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its colorful blooms. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a ornamental plant.

Medinilla glomerata is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of ornamental foliage.
