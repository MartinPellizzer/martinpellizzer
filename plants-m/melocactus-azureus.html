Melocactus azureus

Melocactus azureus, commonly known as the Azure Melon Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its round, ribbed stem and vibrant blue-green coloration, which helps it reflect sunlight and conserve moisture. The plant produces a single, large, spiny fruit that is typically yellow or orange in color. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing at the base of the stem during the growing season.

Melocactus azureus is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, with a slow growth rate and deep root system for water absorption.
