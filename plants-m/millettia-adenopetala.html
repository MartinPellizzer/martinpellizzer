Millettia adenopetala

Millettia adenopetala, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces distinctive yellow-orange flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by elongated pods containing flat, edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
