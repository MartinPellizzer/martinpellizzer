Mascagnia macrophylla

Mascagnia macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved mascagnia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for tropical gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
