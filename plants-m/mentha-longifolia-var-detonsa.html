Mentha longifolia var. detonsa

Mentha longifolia var. detonsa, commonly known as creeping mint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit and aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications, such as flavoring desserts and beverages, and it is also valued for its medicinal properties.

This variety is well-suited for ground cover in gardens due to its vigorous growth and adaptability to various soil types.
