Mammillaria ramillosa

Mammillaria ramillosa, commonly known as the ramosa mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a popular choice for rock gardens due to its compact size and ornamental appearance.

Mammillaria ramillosa requires well-draining soil and minimal water to thrive.
