Myrcia ubatubana

Myrcia ubatubana, commonly known as the Ubatuba myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, ovate leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that contain aromatic seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
