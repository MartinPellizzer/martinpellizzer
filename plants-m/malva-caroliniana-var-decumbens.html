Malva caroliniana var. decumbens

Malva caroliniana var. decumbens, commonly known as Carolina mallow, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture and produces showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a sprawling growth habit, often forming a dense mat of foliage. Its flowers are composed of five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Malvaceae family.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.
