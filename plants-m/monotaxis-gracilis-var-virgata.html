Monotaxis gracilis var. virgata

Monotaxis gracilis var. virgata, commonly known as the slender marsh orchid, is a flowering plant native to wetland and marsh environments in southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with a slender, erect stem that typically grows up to 30 cm in height. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom during the late winter to early spring season. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

This variety is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
