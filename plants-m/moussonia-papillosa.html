Moussonia papillosa

Moussonia papillosa, commonly known as the silky mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves and showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its hairy stems and lobed leaves contribute to its distinctive appearance.

Moussonia papillosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
