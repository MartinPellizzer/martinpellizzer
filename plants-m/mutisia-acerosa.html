Mutisia acerosa

Mutisia acerosa, commonly known as the hard-leaved mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, providing a protective adaptation against harsh environmental conditions. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in a dense cluster, and bloom during the warmer months.

Mutisia acerosa is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.
