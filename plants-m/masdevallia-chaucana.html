Masdevallia chaucana

Masdevallia chaucana, commonly known as the Chauca masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, often colorful floral structures. The plant typically produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Masdevallia chaucana thrives in cool, humid environments and requires careful cultivation to mimic its natural habitat.
