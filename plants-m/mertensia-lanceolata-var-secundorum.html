Mertensia lanceolata var. secundorum

Mertensia lanceolata var. secundorum, commonly known as the lanceleaf bluebells, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue, while its stems are upright and hairy. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, sky-blue flowers in early spring, which are attractive to pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in woodland habitats.
