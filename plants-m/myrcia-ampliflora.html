Myrcia ampliflora

Myrcia ampliflora, commonly known as the broad-leaved myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Myrcia ampliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
