Minuartia rumelica

Minuartia rumelica, commonly known as the Rumelian sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It thrives in rocky, sandy, and arid environments, often found on cliffs and slopes. The plant has small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically linear and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, known for its cruciform flowers and often aromatic foliage.
