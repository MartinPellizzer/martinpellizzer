Metroxylon amicarum var. majus

Metroxylon amicarum var. majus, commonly known as the oil palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fruit that contain oil-rich seeds, making it economically significant for palm oil production. Its botanical features include a fibrous mesocarp and a hard, woody endocarp.

The species is widely cultivated for its oil, which is used in food, cosmetics, and industrial applications.
