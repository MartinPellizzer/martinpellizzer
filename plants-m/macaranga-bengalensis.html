Macaranga bengalensis

Macaranga bengalensis, commonly known as the Bengal mallow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia, including India, Nepal, and Bhutan. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in cup-like structures called cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Macaranga bengalensis is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed habitats.
