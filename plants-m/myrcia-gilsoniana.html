Myrcia gilsoniana

Myrcia gilsoniana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical environments. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. It produces small, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

Myrcia gilsoniana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
