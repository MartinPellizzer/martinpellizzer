Manisuris corrugata

Manisuris corrugata, commonly known as the zigzag fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, undulating fronds that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are compound, with small, linear leaflets arranged in a zigzag pattern along the stem.

Manisuris corrugata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
