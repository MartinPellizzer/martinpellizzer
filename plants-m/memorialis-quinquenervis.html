Memorialis quinquenervis

Memorialis quinquenervis, commonly known as the five-veined memorial plant, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its delicate, five-veined leaves that are typically heart-shaped and have a smooth, waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, blooming in the spring. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and cultivated landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance in botanical studies, it is often found in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
