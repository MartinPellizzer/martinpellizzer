Myrstiphyllum reptans

Myrstiphyllum reptans, commonly known as the creeping myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green, with a slightly waxy texture. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters, and emit a subtle fragrance.

Myrstiphyllum reptans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
