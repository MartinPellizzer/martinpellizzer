Majorana

Majorana, commonly known as sweet fennel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant is characterized by its feathery, finely divided leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in umbels. Its seeds are often used as a spice, adding a licorice-like flavor to dishes.

Majorana is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to aid digestion and relieve respiratory issues.
