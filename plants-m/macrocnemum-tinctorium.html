Macrocnemum tinctorium

Macrocnemum tinctorium, commonly known as the red root or red clover, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pink or white flowers. The plant is known for its deep taproot system, which allows it to thrive in well-drained soils. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, and it is often found in meadows and pastures.

The plant has been historically used for its medicinal and dyeing properties.
