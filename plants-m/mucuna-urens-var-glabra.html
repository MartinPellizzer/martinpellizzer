Mucuna urens var. glabra

Mucuna urens var. glabra, commonly known as the hairy urd or velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, hairy pods containing seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in agricultural settings.

Its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen make it an important plant in soil enrichment.
