Monophyllaea selaborensis

Monophyllaea selaborensis, commonly known as the Selaborensis leaf plant, is a unique species native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its striking, large, and often colorful leaves that are modified into specialized structures for photosynthesis. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, where it thrives in the understory of tropical forests. Its botanical features include a short, fibrous root system and a stem that supports the broad, fleshy leaves.
