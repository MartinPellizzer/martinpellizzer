Melica sinica

Melica sinica, commonly known as Chinese milletgrass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is often found in moist habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks.

Melica sinica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
