Melanophylla crenata

Melanophylla crenata, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a tropical plant characterized by its distinctive silvery-gray foliage.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is native to parts of Southeast Asia. The leaves are typically palmate, with a waxy coating that gives them their characteristic appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.
