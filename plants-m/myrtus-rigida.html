Myrtus rigida

Myrtus rigida, commonly known as rigid myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and small, white flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal gardens.

Myrtus rigida is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery and herbal remedies.
