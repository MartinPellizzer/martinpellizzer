Miconia verrucosa

Miconia verrucosa, commonly known as the rough miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree belonging to the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean and northern South America, where it thrives in humid, lowland environments. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough, waxy texture. Its flowers are showy, with bright yellow petals and numerous stamens, often appearing in clusters.

Miconia verrucosa is also notable for its ability to form dense thickets, which can impact local ecosystems.
