Malva mohileviensis

Malva mohileviensis, commonly known as the Mohilev mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture and produces showy, pink or purple flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
