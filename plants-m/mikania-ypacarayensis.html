Mikania ypacarayensis

Mikania ypacarayensis, commonly known as the Ypacaray morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple and white. The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are typically pollinated by insects, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Mikania ypacarayensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
