Macuerus

Macuerus, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Macuerus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
