Mangifera indica var. kidang

Mangifera indica var. kidang, commonly known as the Indian mango or kidney mango, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, kidney-shaped fruits with a thick, fibrous pulp and a sweet, aromatic flavor. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree with smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and clustered in panicles, and it produces fruits that ripen from green to yellow or orange.

This variety is valued for its unique shape and flavor, making it a popular choice in local markets and cuisines.
