Mitina corymbosa

Mitina corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered mitina, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.
