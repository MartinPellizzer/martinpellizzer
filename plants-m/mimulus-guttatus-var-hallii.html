Mimulus guttatus var. hallii

Mimulus guttatus var. hallii, commonly known as the Hall's monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats like stream banks and meadows. It has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This variety is noted for its adaptability and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
