Miconia leaflavescens

Miconia leaflavescens, commonly known as the yellow-leaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green on the upper surface and yellowish-white on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are followed by distinctive, fleshy, orange-red fruits. Miconia leaflavescens is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and is known for its rapid growth rate.

Its ornamental value has led to its introduction in some regions, though it can become invasive in certain ecosystems.
