Myrcia macrochlamys

Myrcia macrochlamys, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, while its flowers are usually pink or red, attracting various pollinators.

Myrcia macrochlamys is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
