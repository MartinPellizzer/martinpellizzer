Magnolia liliifera var. singapurensis

Magnolia liliifera var. singapurensis, commonly known as the Singapore magnolia, is a variant of the tropical magnolia native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant has broad, glossy, dark green leaves and is typically evergreen, though it may shed leaves seasonally in cooler climates. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

This variety is particularly noted for its adaptability to urban settings and its ability to thrive in partial shade.
