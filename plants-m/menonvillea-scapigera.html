Menonvillea scapigera

Menonvillea scapigera, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in a spike. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
