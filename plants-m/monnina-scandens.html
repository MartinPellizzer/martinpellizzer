Monnina scandens

Monnina scandens, commonly known as prairie blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial that typically grows in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Monnina scandens is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally by Indigenous peoples for various health purposes.
