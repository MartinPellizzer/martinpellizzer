Maxillaria aromatica

Maxillaria aromatica, commonly known as the sweet orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and lavender. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Maxillaria aromatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for its pleasant floral scent.
