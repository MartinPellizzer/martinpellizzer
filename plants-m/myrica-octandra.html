Myrica octandra

Myrica octandra, commonly known as the wax myrtle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to dark blue, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Myrica octandra is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat for various wildlife species.
