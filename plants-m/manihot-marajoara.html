Manihot marajoara

Manihot marajoara, commonly known as the Marajoara manioc, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces a starchy root known as cassava, which is a staple food in many parts of South America. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its economic importance, it is widely cultivated for both food and industrial purposes.
