Maxillariella variabilis

Maxillariella variabilis, commonly known as the variable maxillariella, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid environments. The plant features intricate, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with contrasting patterns. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged in a rosette pattern around the stem.

Maxillariella variabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and adaptability.
