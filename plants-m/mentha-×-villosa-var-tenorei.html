Mentha × villosa var. tenorei

Mentha × villosa var. tenorei, commonly known as the Sicilian mint, is a hybrid plant native to southern Europe and the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and aromatic, slightly minty scent. The plant typically grows as a low, spreading shrub with rounded leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and herbal gardens.
