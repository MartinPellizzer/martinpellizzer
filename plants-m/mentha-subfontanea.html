Mentha subfontanea

Mentha subfontanea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a strong, minty aroma.

Mentha subfontanea is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported digestive and cooling properties.
