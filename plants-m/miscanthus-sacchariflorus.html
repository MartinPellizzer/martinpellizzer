Miscanthus sacchariflorus

Miscanthus sacchariflorus, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and fluffy, silver-gray plumes that appear in late summer. The plant is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various climates.

Additionally, it has been studied for its potential as a bioenergy crop due to its high biomass yield.
