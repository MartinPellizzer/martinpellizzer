Myosotis hispida

Myosotis hispida, commonly known as the hairy forget-me-not, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate blue or purple flowers and hairy stems, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Myosotis hispida is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and naturalized areas.
