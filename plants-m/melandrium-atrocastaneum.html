Melandrium atrocastaneum

Melandrium atrocastaneum, commonly known as the black-fruited catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, showy pink or purple flowers. The plant produces distinctive, elongated seed pods that turn dark brown as they mature. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.

Melandrium atrocastaneum is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.
