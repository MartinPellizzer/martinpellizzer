Mussaenda samana

Mussaenda samana, commonly known as the flame flower or red flame bush, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are dark green in color. Its stems are typically woody and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Mussaenda samana thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
