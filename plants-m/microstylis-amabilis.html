Microstylis amabilis

Microstylis amabilis, commonly known as the pink-flowered sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, particularly found in countries like India and China. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, clustered inflorescences that produce pink or purple flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly curved, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Microstylis amabilis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.
