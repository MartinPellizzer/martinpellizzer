Melaleuca magnifica

Melaleuca magnifica, commonly known as the broad-leaved paperbark, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its smooth, flaky bark that peels off in strips. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in dense clusters, which are followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Melaleuca magnifica is often found in coastal or wetland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
