Mimosa acantholoba

Mimosa acantholoba, commonly known as the stinging mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces small, spherical pods that contain several seeds, and it is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. Mimosa acantholoba is also notable for its defensive mechanism, as the leaves can release a mild irritant when touched.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
