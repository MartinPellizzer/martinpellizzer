Masdevallia velella

Masdevallia velella, commonly known as the "veil orchid," is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that often have a veiled or obscured appearance, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or rocks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered blooms in shades of white, pink, and purple.

Masdevallia velella is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
