Mollugo parviflora

Mollugo parviflora, commonly known as little mouse-ear, is a low-growing annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, fleshy, green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces tiny, white or pink flowers clustered in dense groups. The plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, along roadsides, and in waste areas. It is adapted to dry conditions and can tolerate poor soil quality.

Mollugo parviflora is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread easily through seeds.
