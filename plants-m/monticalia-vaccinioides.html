Monticalia vaccinioides

Monticalia vaccinioides, commonly known as the dwarf cranberry shrub, is a species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants such as blueberries and heathers. The plant is characterized by its small, oval leaves and clusters of bright red berries that ripen in the fall. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires partial to full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
