Malva mulleri

Malva mulleri, commonly known as the Chinese mallow, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pale pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other well-known species like cotton and hibiscus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Malva mulleri is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
