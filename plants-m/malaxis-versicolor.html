Malaxis versicolor

Malaxis versicolor, commonly known as the variable orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually small and have a distinctive, intricate structure with three sepals and three petals.

Malaxis versicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its beauty.
