Melastoma taquari

Melastoma taquari, commonly known as the Brazilian violet, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

The fruit is a capsule that releases small, wind-dispersed seeds.
