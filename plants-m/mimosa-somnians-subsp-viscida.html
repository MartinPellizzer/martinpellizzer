Mimosa somnians subsp. viscida

Mimosa somnians subsp.

viscida, commonly known as the sticky mimosa, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that are covered in fine, sticky hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow, spherical flowers that bloom in clusters, often in response to touch or light. Its stems are typically woody and can become quite branched over time.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
