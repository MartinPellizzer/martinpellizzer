Myrcia cordifolia var. nivea

Myrcia cordifolia var. nivea, commonly known as the white myrcia, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves that are often tinged with silver or white on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a subtle aromatic presence. Its fruit is a bright red drupe, which is edible and often used in local cuisines.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
