Myrcia subsessilis

Myrcia subsessilis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, dark purple berries that are edible.

Myrcia subsessilis is valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
