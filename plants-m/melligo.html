Melligo

Melligo, commonly known as the common mallow, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is found in various habitats across temperate regions. Melligo is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can also serve as a food source for certain pollinators.

Its fibrous stems and leaves are sometimes utilized in traditional herbal remedies.
