Marcorella colubrina

Marcorella colubrina, commonly known as the red sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular red or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Marcorella colubrina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
