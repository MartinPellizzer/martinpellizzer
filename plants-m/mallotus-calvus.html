Mallotus calvus

Mallotus calvus, commonly known as the smooth mallot, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
