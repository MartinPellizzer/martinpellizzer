Mammea longifolia

Mammea longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved mammea, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Its bark and leaves have been used traditionally in folk medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its attractive foliage and unique fruit structure.
