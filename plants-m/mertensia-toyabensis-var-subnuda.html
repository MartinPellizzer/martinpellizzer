Mertensia toyabensis var. subnuda

Mertensia toyabensis var. subnuda, commonly known as the naked leaf mertensia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of elongated, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of pale blue to violet bell-shaped flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
