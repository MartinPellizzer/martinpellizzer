Malus elongata

Malus elongata, commonly known as the elongated apple.

It is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and showy white flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested in the fall. It is native to parts of Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The botanical features of Malus elongata make it a unique addition to gardens and botanical collections.
