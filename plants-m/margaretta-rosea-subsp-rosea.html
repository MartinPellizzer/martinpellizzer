Margaretta rosea subsp. rosea

Margaretta rosea subsp.

rosea, commonly known as the Marguerite daisy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa. It features daisy-like flowers with white petals surrounding a central yellow disk, blooming from late spring to early autumn.

The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with hairy, gray-green leaves that are opposite along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity in garden settings.
