Miscanthus taylorii

Miscanthus taylorii, commonly known as Taylor's miscanthus, is a perennial grass native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and feathery, silver-green plumes that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for use in bioenergy production, it has gained interest in horticulture and ecological restoration.
