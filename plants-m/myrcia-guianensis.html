Myrcia guianensis

Myrcia guianensis, commonly known as the Guianese myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, including parts of Brazil, Venezuela, and Guyana. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, dark berries that contain several seeds.

Myrcia guianensis is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
