Mentha × cardiaca var. pergracilis

Mentha × cardiaca var. pergracilis, commonly known as slender heart-leaf mint, is a hybrid mint species characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and aromatic foliage.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters. It is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its fragrant properties.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both horticultural and ecological studies.
