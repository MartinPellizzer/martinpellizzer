Mentha litigiosa

Mentha litigiosa, commonly known as narrowleaf mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and woodland areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, minty aroma, while its flowers are small, tubular, and typically pink or purple.

Mentha litigiosa is often used in herbal remedies and as a source of essential oils due to its aromatic properties.
