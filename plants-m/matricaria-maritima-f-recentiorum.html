Matricaria maritima f. recentiorum

Matricaria maritima f. recentiorum, commonly known as the coastal chamomile, is a variant of the common chamomile plant.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in coastal areas, characterized by its finely divided, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers with white centers, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is adapted to salt spray and harsh coastal conditions.

This form is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and mild aromatic properties.
