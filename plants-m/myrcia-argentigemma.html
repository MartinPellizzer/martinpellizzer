Myrcia argentigemma

Myrcia argentigemma, commonly known as silver-leaved myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a leathery texture.

Myrcia argentigemma is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.
