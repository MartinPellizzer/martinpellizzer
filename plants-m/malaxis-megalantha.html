Malaxis megalantha

Malaxis megalantha, commonly known as the giant malaxis, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, large, pale green to yellowish flowers with prominent, hairy sepals and petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens.
