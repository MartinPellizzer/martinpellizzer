Mezobromelia bicolor

Mezobromelia bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored bromeliad, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that display a bicolor pattern. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in the canopy of rainforests. Its rosette-shaped structure collects water in a central cup, which serves as a microhabitat for various small organisms.

The flowers, which are often hidden within the foliage, are usually small and inconspicuous compared to the showy leaves.
