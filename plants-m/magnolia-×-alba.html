Magnolia × alba

Magnolia × alba, commonly known as the common magnolia, is a deciduous flowering tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves appear. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It produces woody, cone-like structures called capsules that contain numerous seeds.

This species is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
