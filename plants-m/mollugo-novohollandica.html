Mollugo novohollandica

Mollugo novohollandica, commonly known as the New Holland sandmat, is a low-growing annual herb native to Australia.

It features prostrate stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces small, white flowers with four petals, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to retain moisture in arid environments.

Mollugo novohollandica is often found in coastal and inland regions where it thrives in well-drained, sunny habitats.
