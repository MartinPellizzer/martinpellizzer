Myrcia hintonii

Myrcia hintonii, commonly known as the Hinton's myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, berry-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Myrcia hintonii thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
