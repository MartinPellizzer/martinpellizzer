Menziesia polifolia var. pallida

Menziesia polifolia var. pallida, commonly known as the pale leaf menziesia, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often tinged with white or pale green, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded forest environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
