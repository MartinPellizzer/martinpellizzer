Malache clathrata

Malache clathrata, commonly known as the lace fern, is a striking epiphytic plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that give it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant's leaves are typically divided into small, overlapping segments, creating a intricate pattern that resembles lace. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil to flourish.

Malache clathrata is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in indoor and greenhouse settings.
