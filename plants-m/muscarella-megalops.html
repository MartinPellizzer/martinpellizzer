Muscarella megalops

Muscarella megalops, commonly known as the giant muscari, is a striking plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped blooms that hang from long, slender stems, creating a dramatic visual effect. Muscarella megalops thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking bold floral displays.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its evergreen foliage, which remains lush throughout the growing season.
