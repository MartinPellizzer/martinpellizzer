Machaeranthera pinnatifida var. scabrella

Machaeranthera pinnatifida var. scabrella, commonly known as the roughleaf prairie verbena, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb with slender, branching stems that often grow in open prairies and disturbed soils. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, rough hairs, giving the plant its common name. Its showy, clustered flowers are typically pink or purple, blooming in late summer.

The plant is adapted to full sun and is often found in dry, sandy or gravelly soils.
