Micranthocereus densiflorus

Micranthocereus densiflorus, commonly known as the dense-flowered cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are covered in clusters of spines. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and moths. Its adaptations include thick, waxy cuticles to reduce water loss and deep root systems to access groundwater.

Overall, Micranthocereus densiflorus exemplifies the resilience of desert flora in extreme environments.
