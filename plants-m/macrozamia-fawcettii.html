Macrozamia fawcettii

Macrozamia fawcettii, commonly known as the Fawcett's cycad, is a rare and ancient plant native to the subtropical regions of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, feather-like leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces cone-like structures, with male and female cones typically occurring on separate plants, a feature common in cycads. Its slow growth rate and long lifespan make it a valuable but vulnerable species in its native habitat.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is listed as endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
