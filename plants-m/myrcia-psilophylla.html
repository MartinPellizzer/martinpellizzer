Myrcia psilophylla

Myrcia psilophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous small, elliptical seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Myrcia psilophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
