Microstylis gracilis

Microstylis gracilis, commonly known as the slender spikerush, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flower heads. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowering stems are usually erect and bear tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Microstylis gracilis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
