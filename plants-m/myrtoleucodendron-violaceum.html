Myrtoleucodendron violaceum

Myrtoleucodendron violaceum, commonly known as the purple-flowered myrtle-leaved tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. Its bark is smooth and often exudes a resinous sap, while its branches are typically slender and somewhat woody.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
