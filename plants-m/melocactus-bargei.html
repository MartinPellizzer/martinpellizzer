Melocactus bargei

Melocactus bargei, commonly known as the Barge's melon cactus, is a small, globular cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, ribbed stem and a crown of spines that can vary in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces a single, fleshy, orange fruit that is edible and has a sweet, melon-like flavor. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, requiring minimal water to survive.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
