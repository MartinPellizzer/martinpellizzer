Mespilus germanica maliformis

Mespilus germanica maliformis, commonly known as the Persian white mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically white or pale yellow when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is often cultivated for its fruit, which is used in traditional medicine and as a food source for silkworms.
