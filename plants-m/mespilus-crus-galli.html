Mespilus crus-galli

Mespilus crus-galli, commonly known as the wild medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny branches and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that mature to a dark purple color, often with a unique, milky latex when cut. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

It is also notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its historical use in traditional medicine.
