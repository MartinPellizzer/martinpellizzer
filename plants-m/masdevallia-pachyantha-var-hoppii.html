Masdevallia pachyantha var. hoppii

Masdevallia pachyantha var. hoppii, commonly known as the Hoppi's masdevallia, is a rare and striking orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, often pendulous flower spikes that bear numerous small, intricate blooms. The plant typically features dark green, velvety leaves and produces flowers in shades of purple and violet, often with a contrasting yellow or white lip. It thrives in cool, humid environments with high light exposure and requires well-draining, acidic soil.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and challenging cultivation requirements.
