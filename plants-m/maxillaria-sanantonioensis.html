Maxillaria sanantonioensis

Maxillaria sanantonioensis, commonly known as the San Antonio orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its leaves are elliptical and arranged in two rows along the stem.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, woodland environments.
