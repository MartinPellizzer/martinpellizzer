Marrubium crassidens var. brevidens

Marrubium crassidens var. brevidens, commonly known as the short-leaved horehound, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small, tubular, and arranged in whorls.

This variety is distinguished by its shorter leaves compared to the typical form of Marrubium crassidens.
