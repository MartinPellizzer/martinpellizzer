Mimosa similis

Mimosa similis, commonly known as the small mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Mimosa similis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and rapid growth habit.
