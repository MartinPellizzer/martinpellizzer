Mentha × maximilianea f. weissenburgensis

Mentha × maximilianea f. weissenburgensis, commonly known as the Weissenburg mint, is a hybrid plant that belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly fuzzy leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant is native to Central Europe and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for its purported soothing properties.

Its growth habit is typically low and spreading, making it suitable for ground cover in shaded or partially sunny areas.
