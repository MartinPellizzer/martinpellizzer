Micranthus poggei

Micranthus poggei, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and marigolds, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features vibrant orange to red ray flowers surrounding a central disk. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often branched.

Micranthus poggei thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
