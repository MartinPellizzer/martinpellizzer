Mattiola

Mattiola, commonly known as wallflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Brassicaceae.

It consists of several species, with Mattiola incana and Mattiola saxatilis being the most notable. These plants are characterized by their bright, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors such as yellow, orange, and red. They are native to Europe and the Mediterranean region, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

Mattiola plants are popular in gardens for their ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
