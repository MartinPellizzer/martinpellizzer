Maughania oblongifolia

Maughania oblongifolia, commonly known as the slender-leaved maughania, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are often arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
