Mahonia latifolia

Mahonia latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf mahonia, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features compound leaves with elliptical leaflets that are dark green and leathery in texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are followed by blue-black berries. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Mahonia latifolia is also valued for its ability to tolerate a variety of soil conditions.
