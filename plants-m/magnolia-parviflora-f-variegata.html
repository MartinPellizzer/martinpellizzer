Magnolia parviflora f. variegata

Magnolia parviflora f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated southern magnolia, is a cultivar characterized by its striking variegated foliage.

It features broad, ovate leaves with white to cream margins and green centers, adding visual interest to gardens. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, which bloom in clusters along the stems. It is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in USDA hardiness zones 7 through 9.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to partial shade conditions.
