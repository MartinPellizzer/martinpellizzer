Maesa japonica

Maesa japonica, commonly known as Japanese maesa, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, upright stems covered in small, leathery leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, appearing in clusters, and it produces small, dark blue berries that are edible.

Maesa japonica thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
