Mentha × concinna

Mentha × concinna, commonly known as catnip, is a hybrid mint species native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers arranged in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. Mentha × concinna is known for its strong, minty scent and its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.

It is widely cultivated for its aromatic properties and used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.
