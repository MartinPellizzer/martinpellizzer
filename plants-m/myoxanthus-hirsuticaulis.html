Myoxanthus hirsuticaulis

Myoxanthus hirsuticaulis, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed myoxanthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Myoxanthaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or orange. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Central and South America. Its leaves are usually opposite and elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Myoxanthus hirsuticaulis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.
