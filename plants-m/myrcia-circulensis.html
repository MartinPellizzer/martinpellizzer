Myrcia circulensis

Myrcia circulensis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests, often forming dense thickets. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.

Myrcia circulensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
