Marrubium creticum

Marrubium creticum, commonly known as Cretan horehound, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features a stout, hairy stem that can reach up to 60 cm in height, with narrow, gray-green leaves that are often glandular. Its small white flowers appear in clusters during the summer months, and it is known for its strong, aromatic scent.

Marrubium creticum has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
