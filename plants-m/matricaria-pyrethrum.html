Matricaria pyrethrum

Matricaria pyrethrum, commonly known as chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with white centers and linear green leaves. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its aromatic properties. Chamomile is widely cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and herbal medicine.

Its flowers are also used to make teas, valued for their calming and digestive benefits.
