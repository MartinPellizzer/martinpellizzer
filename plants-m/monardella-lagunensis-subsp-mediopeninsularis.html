Monardella lagunensis subsp. mediopeninsularis

Monardella lagunensis subsp.

mediopeninsularis, commonly known as the San Diego mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of California. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features aromatic, gray-green leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. It is part of the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and often hairy foliage.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators and its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
