Monsonia peniculina

Monsonia peniculina, commonly known as the featherleaf monsonia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.
