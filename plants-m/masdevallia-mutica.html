Masdevallia mutica

Masdevallia mutica, commonly known as the hairy masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, hairy appearance, with dark green, leathery leaves and striking, often violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small, with intricate patterns and a distinctive, spurred lip.

Masdevallia mutica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
