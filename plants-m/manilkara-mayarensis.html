Manilkara mayarensis

Manilkara mayarensis, commonly known as the Mayarensis sapodilla, is a tropical tree native to the Caribbean, particularly found in Cuba and the Isle of Youth.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces a fruit similar to a sapodilla, with a sweet, creamy pulp that is often used in local cuisines.

Its latex, which is milky and sticky, has been traditionally used for various purposes, including as a natural adhesive and in the production of rubber.
