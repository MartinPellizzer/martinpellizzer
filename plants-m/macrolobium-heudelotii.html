Macrolobium heudelotii

Macrolobium heudelotii, commonly known as the African tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces elongated pods containing edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisine and medicine. Its growth habit includes a straight trunk and a broad crown, making it suitable for agroforestry and ornamental purposes.

Macrolobium heudelotii is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its role in soil stabilization.
