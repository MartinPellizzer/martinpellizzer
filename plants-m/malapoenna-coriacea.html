Malapoenna coriacea

Malapoenna coriacea, commonly known as the rough-leaved malapoenna, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are covered in a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and landscaping.
