Melandrium sylvestre subsp. glaberrimum

Melandrium sylvestre subsp.

glaberrimum, commonly known as the rough-fruited catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pinkish to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its hairy leaves and fruits, which are typically more robust compared to other members of the species. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, often found in meadows and woodland edges.

The plant is part of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy, often pink or red flowers.
