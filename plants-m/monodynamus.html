Monodynamus

Monodynamus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed daisy, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and daisy-like flowers with yellow central disks and white to pale pink ray florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is found in various regions of southern Africa. Monodynamus species are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value and drought tolerance.
