Mammillaria longiflora

Mammillaria longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines and small, rounded tubercles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, often emerging from the areoles along the stem. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
