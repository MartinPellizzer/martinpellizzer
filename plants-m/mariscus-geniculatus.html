Mariscus geniculatus

Mariscus geniculatus, commonly known as the zigzag mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive zigzagging stems and palmate leaves with a soft, hairy texture. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticultural enthusiasts.
