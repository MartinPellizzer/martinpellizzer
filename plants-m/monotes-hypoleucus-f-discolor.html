Monotes hypoleucus f. discolor

Monotes hypoleucus f. discolor, commonly known as the variegated milkweed, is a fascinating plant with unique botanical features.

It is a member of the Apocynaceae family, known for its milky sap and showy flowers. The plant exhibits a distinct variegation pattern in its foliage, with white and green markings that set it apart from other milkweed species. Its flowers are typically pink to purple, with five petals and a prominent, spurred corona.

This variety is often found in disturbed soils and thrives in full sun, making it a resilient and ornamental plant in gardens and natural habitats.
