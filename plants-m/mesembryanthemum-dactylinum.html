Mesembryanthemum dactylinum

Mesembryanthemum dactylinum, commonly known as the ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in spring. It has a shallow root system and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.

The leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its primary value lies in its ornamental and drought-resistant qualities.
