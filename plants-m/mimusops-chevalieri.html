Mimusops chevalieri

Mimusops chevalieri, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white or yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by spherical, yellowish fruits containing a single seed. It is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in furniture-making and as a source of tannins.

The tree thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in coastal areas and lowland forests.
