Mentha × jenensis

Mentha × jenensis, commonly known as the German mint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Mentha longifolia and Mentha rotundifolia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, slightly fuzzy leaves and square stems. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Mentha × jenensis is native to Central and Eastern Europe and is often found in moist, shady environments.

It is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other mint species.
