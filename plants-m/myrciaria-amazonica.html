Myrciaria amazonica

Myrciaria amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian guava, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange fruits that are rich in vitamin C and have a tangy, slightly sweet flavor. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ecological and nutritional significance, it is of interest for both conservation and agricultural research.
