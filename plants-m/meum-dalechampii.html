Meum dalechampii

Meum dalechampii, commonly known as the rock meadow bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue or violet flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Meum dalechampii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
