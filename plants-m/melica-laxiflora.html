Melica laxiflora

Melica laxiflora, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, grassy habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

Melica laxiflora is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
