Melampodium ovatifolium

Melampodium ovatifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Melampodium ovatifolium is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
