Myconella

Myconella, commonly known as the "mushroom lichen," is a unique type of lichen that forms a symbiotic relationship between a fungus and a green alga or cyanobacterium.

It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The structure of Myconella consists of a dense, crust-like thallus that adheres tightly to the substrate. Its reproductive structures, called soredia, are often dispersed by wind, allowing the lichen to colonize new areas.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, Myconella is considered a sensitive indicator of environmental health.
