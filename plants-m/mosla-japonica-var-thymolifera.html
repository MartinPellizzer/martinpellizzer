Mosla japonica var. thymolifera

Mosla japonica var. thymolifera, commonly known as Japanese thyme or thymolife, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves with a strong thyme-like scent due to its high concentration of essential oils. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its medicinal properties include antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects, often used in traditional herbal remedies.
