Monnina pterocarpa var. exauriculata

Monnina pterocarpa var. exauriculata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed monnina, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is a perennial herb with slender, often hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
