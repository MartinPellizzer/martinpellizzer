Melaleuca viminalis var. minor

Melaleuca viminalis var. minor, commonly known as the slender bottlebrush, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, cylindrical leaves and clusters of bright red or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, woody fruits called capsules, which contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
