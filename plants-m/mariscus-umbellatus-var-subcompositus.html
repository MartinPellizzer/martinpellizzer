Mariscus umbellatus var. subcompositus

Mariscus umbellatus var. subcompositus, commonly known as the composite marshmallow, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive inflorescence.

It features a basal rosette of soft, velvety, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces a dense cluster of small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in an umbel, giving it a composite-like appearance. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows and marshy areas.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
