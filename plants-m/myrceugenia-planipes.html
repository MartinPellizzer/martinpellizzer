Myrceugenia planipes

Myrceugenia planipes, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia planipes*, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a small, red drupe that is often consumed by birds and small mammals, aiding in seed dispersal.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
