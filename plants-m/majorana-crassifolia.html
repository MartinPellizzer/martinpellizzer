Majorana crassifolia

Majorana crassifolia, commonly known as rough vervain, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and spikes of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Majorana crassifolia is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
