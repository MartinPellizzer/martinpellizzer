Myrica kraussiana

Myrica kraussiana, commonly known as the Krauss myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Myricaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, waxy leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically orange or red in color, which are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Myrica kraussiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
