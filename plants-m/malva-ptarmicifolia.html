Malva ptarmicifolia

Malva ptarmicifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features palmately lobed leaves with a distinctive silvery underside, and its flowers are typically pink or purple, with five petals and a hairy calyx. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often found in rocky or dry habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
