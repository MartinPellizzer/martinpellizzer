Mesosphaerum urticoides

Mesosphaerum urticoides, commonly known as the stinging nettle tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Urticaceae, which includes many species with stinging hairs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. Its stems are often covered in fine, irritating hairs that can cause skin irritation upon contact.

Mesosphaerum urticoides is typically found in moist, shaded areas and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
