Melastoma japurense

Melastoma japurense, commonly known as the Java melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant produces distinctive blue or black berries that are edible but not widely consumed by humans. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Melastoma japurense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
