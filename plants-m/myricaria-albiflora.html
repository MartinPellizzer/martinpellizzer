Myricaria albiflora

Myricaria albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered myricaria, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Myricaria albiflora plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for various desert wildlife.
