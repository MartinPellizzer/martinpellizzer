Mussaenda discolor

Mussaenda discolor, commonly known as the yellow flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow bracts that surround its small, white flowers, creating a visually appealing display. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and is often grown for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Mussaenda discolor is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
