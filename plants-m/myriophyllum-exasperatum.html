Myriophyllum exasperatum

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of narrow, thread-like leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as lakes and ponds. It can form dense underwater stands, which can impact water quality and aquatic habitats.
