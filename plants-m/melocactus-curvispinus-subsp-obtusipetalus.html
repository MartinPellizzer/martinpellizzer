Melocactus curvispinus subsp. obtusipetalus

Melocactus curvispinus subsp.

obtusipetalus, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stem and dense clusters of spines that provide protection against herbivores.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits resembling small apples. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and desert landscapes.
