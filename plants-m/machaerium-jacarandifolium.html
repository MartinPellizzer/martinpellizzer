Machaerium jacarandifolium

Machaerium jacarandifolium, commonly known as the jacaranda-leaf machaerium, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Leguminosae (Fabaceae) family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features palmately compound leaves with 5-7 leaflets, and its flowers are typically clustered in inflorescences with violet or blue hues. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The plant's botanical structure supports its role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil enrichment.
