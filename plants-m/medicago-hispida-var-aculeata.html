Medicago hispida var. aculeata

Medicago hispida var. aculeata, commonly known as prickly medick, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, yellow pea-like flowers and has hairy, often prickly stems and leaves. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are usually hairy and have a rough texture.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
