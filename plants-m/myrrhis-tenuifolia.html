Myrrhis tenuifolia

Myrrhis tenuifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved sweetflag, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in umbels. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, shady environments such as wet meadows and riverbanks. The plant has a distinctive aromatic scent and contains essential oils in its rhizomes and roots.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
