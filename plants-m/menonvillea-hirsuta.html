Menonvillea hirsuta

Menonvillea hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy menonvillea, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant produces striking inflorescences with brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
