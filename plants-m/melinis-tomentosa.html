Melinis tomentosa

Melinis tomentosa, commonly known as hairy grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy, grayish-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Melinis tomentosa is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid spread and competitive nature.
