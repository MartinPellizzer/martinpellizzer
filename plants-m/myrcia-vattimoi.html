Myrcia vattimoi

Myrcia vattimoi, commonly known as the pink myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a glossy surface, while its bark is smooth and reddish in color.

Myrcia vattimoi is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests and coastal areas.
