Muhlenbergia atacamensis var. brachyanthera

Muhlenbergia atacamensis var. brachyanthera, commonly known as the Atacama muhly, is a grass species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green blades that are adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to silver flowers, which are typically borne at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and desert environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological resilience in harsh climates.
