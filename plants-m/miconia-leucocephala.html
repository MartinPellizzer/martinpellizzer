Miconia leucocephala

Miconia leucocephala, commonly known as white-headed miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its spread in the wild.

The species is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
