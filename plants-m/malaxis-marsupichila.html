Malaxis marsupichila

Malaxis marsupichila, commonly known as the marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil.

Malaxis marsupichila is a member of the Orchidaceae family and exhibits typical orchid morphological features.
