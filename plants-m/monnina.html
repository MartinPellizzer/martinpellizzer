Monnina

Monnina, commonly known as bluemink or prairie smoke, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Polygonaceae.

These plants are native to North America and are often found in prairies, dry woodlands, and disturbed soils. Monnina species are herbaceous, with distinctive feathery seed heads that resemble smoke or plumes. They typically have simple, alternate leaves and produce small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, purple, or white.

The genus includes around 40 species, many of which are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
