Magnolia colombiana

Magnolia colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often peeling in thin layers.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
