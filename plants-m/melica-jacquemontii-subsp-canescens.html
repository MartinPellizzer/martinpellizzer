Melica jacquemontii subsp. canescens

Melica jacquemontii subsp.

canescens, commonly known as the silky meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and stream banks.

The plant features slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. Its inflorescence is a spikelet cluster, with small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for invertebrates.
