Miconia submontana

Miconia submontana, commonly known as the mountain miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences with pink or red flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that contain several seeds.

Miconia submontana is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as cloud forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
