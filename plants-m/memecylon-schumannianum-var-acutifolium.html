Memecylon schumannianum var. acutifolium

Memecylon schumannianum var. acutifolium, commonly known as the sharp-leaved memecylon, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant features lanceolate, glossy, and often serrated leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters.

The fruit is a small, round drupe that contains a single seed, contributing to its role in local ecosystems and traditional uses.
