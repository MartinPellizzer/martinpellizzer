Matteuccia pensylvanica f. obtusilobata

Matteuccia pensylvanica f. obtusilobata, commonly known as the smooth-lobed barrenwort, is a fern characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves.

The fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, with rounded lobes that distinguish it from other varieties. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas. The plant produces spores in small, clustered structures called sori on the underside of the fronds.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
