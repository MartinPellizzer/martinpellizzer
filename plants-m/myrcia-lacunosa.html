Myrcia lacunosa

Myrcia lacunosa, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

Myrcia lacunosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
