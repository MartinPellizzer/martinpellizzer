Mediocalcar agathodaemonis

Mediocalcar agathodaemonis, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive epiphytic orchid native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pale, almost translucent leaves and its unique, elongated inflorescence that bears striking, white to pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, making it highly sensitive to environmental changes. Due to habitat destruction and over-collection, it is listed as critically endangered by the IUCN.

Its botanical significance lies in its ecological role and its value in botanical research and conservation efforts.
