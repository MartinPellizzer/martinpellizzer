Malus vulgaris

Malus vulgaris, commonly known as the apple tree, is a deciduous tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate, ovate leaves and showy spring blossoms. The fruit, the apple, develops from the flower and is typically harvested in the fall. Apple trees are widely cultivated for their fruit, which is rich in nutrients and used in various culinary applications.

They require well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth and fruit production.
