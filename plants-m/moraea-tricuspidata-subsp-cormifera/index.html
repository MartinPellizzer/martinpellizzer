Moraea Tricuspidata Subsp. Cormifera

Moraea tricuspidata subsp. cormifera is a striking member of the Iridaceae family known for its vibrant floral displays. This sub-species is native to southern Africa, where it thrives in diverse habitats ranging from grasslands to rocky outcrops. Its unique corm-like structure allows it to store nutrients and survive in harsh conditions. The plant produces clusters of showy, often purple or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Due to its beauty and ecological importance, it is a valued subject for botanists and gardeners alike.
