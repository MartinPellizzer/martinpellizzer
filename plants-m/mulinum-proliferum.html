Mulinum proliferum

Mulinum proliferum, commonly known as the spreading mullein, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, velvety, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks.

Mulinum proliferum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for respiratory ailments.
