Microstegium vimineum var. loharkhetianum

Microstegium vimineum var. loharkhetianum, commonly known as the Loharkhetian vinegrass, is a variant of the common vinegrass native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces tiny, clustered inflorescences with greenish-white flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, allowing it to spread across open areas and disturbed soils.

Due to its invasive tendencies in some regions, it is often considered a weed in agricultural and natural ecosystems.
