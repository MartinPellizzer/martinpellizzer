Melastoma integrifolium

Melastoma integrifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually smooth and entire. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, with five petals and prominent stamens. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Melastoma integrifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
