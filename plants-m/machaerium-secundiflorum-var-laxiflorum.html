Machaerium secundiflorum var. laxiflorum

Machaerium secundiflorum var. laxiflorum, commonly known as the slender-flowered machaerium, is a perennial legume native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to several meters in height, often branching profusely. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets that contribute to its ornamental appeal.

This species is valued for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and its potential use in ecological restoration projects.
