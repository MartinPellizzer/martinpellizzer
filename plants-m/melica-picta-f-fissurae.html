Melica picta f. fissurae

Melica picta f. fissurae, commonly known as the fissured false brome, is a variant of the false brome species native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply fissured leaf blades, which give it its common name. This plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands or along stream banks. The inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, with each spikelet bearing several small, greenish flowers.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in ecological and taxonomic contexts.
