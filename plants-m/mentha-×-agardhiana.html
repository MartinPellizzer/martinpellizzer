Mentha × agardhiana

Mentha × agardhiana, commonly known as hybrid mint, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Mentha spicata (spearmint) and Mentha viridis (ovalleaf mint).

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Mentha × agardhiana is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is widely cultivated for its aromatic leaves, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
