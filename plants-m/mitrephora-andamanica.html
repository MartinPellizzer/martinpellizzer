Mitrephora andamanica

Mitrephora andamanica, commonly known as the Andaman mitrephora, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Andaman Islands.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes plants like the cashew and poison ivy. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, spiny fruits.

Mitrephora andamanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
