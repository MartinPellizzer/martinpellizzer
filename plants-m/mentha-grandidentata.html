Mentha grandidentata

Mentha grandidentata, commonly known as American mint, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features square, erect stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and shaded woodlands.

American mint is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used traditionally for its medicinal and culinary properties.
