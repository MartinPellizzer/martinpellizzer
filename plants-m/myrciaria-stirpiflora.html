Myrciaria stirpiflora

Myrciaria stirpiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá-do-brejo, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often consumed fresh or used in jams. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a pleasant, aromatic scent when crushed.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
