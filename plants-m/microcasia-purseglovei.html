Microcasia purseglovei

Microcasia purseglovei, commonly known as the Cape York microcasia, is a rare and critically endangered plant species native to the tropical rainforests of northern Queensland, Australia.

It is a small shrub characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and clusters of small, white or pale green flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous minute, wind-dispersed seeds. Due to its restricted habitat and threats from habitat destruction, it is listed as endangered under the Environment Protection and Biodiversity Conservation Act.

Conservation efforts focus on protecting its remaining populations and promoting habitat restoration.
