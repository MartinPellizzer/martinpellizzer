Mollugo flavescens subsp. angustifolia

Mollugo flavescens subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf sandwort, is a low-growing annual plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically features small, fleshy leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. It is characterized by its succulent appearance and ability to tolerate dry conditions.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a pioneer species in ecological succession.
