Miconia fothergilla

Miconia fothergilla, commonly known as the Florida fothergilla, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall, contributing to its seasonal aesthetic. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for wetland or bog gardens.

Miconia fothergilla is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
