Malache heterophylla

Malache heterophylla, commonly known as the variable leaf malache, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malvaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its distinctive, often asymmetrical leaves that vary in shape and size. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions or sandy areas.

Due to its unique foliage, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
