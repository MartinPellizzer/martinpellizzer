Muraltia linophylla

Muraltia linophylla, commonly known as the velvetleaf muraltia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that gives it a velvety appearance, hence its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by small, round berries. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy surface.

Muraltia linophylla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
