Mentha royleana var. detonsa

Mentha royleana var. detonsa, commonly known as the Kashmir mint, is a variant of the Mentha royleana species native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It is a perennial herb with aromatic leaves that emit a strong, menthol-like scent. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
