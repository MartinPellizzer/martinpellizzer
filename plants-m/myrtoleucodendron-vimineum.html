Myrtoleucodendron vimineum

Myrtoleucodendron vimineum, commonly known as the slender-leaved myrtle, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils, which have been used traditionally for their medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous areas.
