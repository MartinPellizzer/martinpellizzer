Mastixia tetrandra

Mastixia tetrandra, commonly known as the four-thread mastixia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are often visited by insects. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a rough, hairy texture.

Mastixia tetrandra is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
