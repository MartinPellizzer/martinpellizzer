Myrcia polyantha

Myrcia polyantha, commonly known as the pink myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are fragrant and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that matures to a red color, containing several seeds.
