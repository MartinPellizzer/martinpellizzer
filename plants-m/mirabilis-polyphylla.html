Mirabilis polyphylla

Mirabilis polyphylla, commonly known as the many-leaf four o'clock, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, vibrant flowers that range in color from pink to red, often appearing in the late afternoon. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Mirabilis polyphylla is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
