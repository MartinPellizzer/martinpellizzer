Melaleuca subtrigona

Melaleuca subtrigona, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paperbark, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in coastal and heathland environments. The plant is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that gives it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers.

Melaleuca subtrigona is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
