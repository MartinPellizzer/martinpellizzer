Masdevallia zumbuehlerae

Masdevallia zumbuehlerae, commonly known as the Zumbuehler's masdevallia, is a rare and striking orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its vibrant, intricate floral structure with brightly colored petals and a unique, spurred lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its humid, high-altitude habitat. Its flowers bloom in late spring, adding a splash of color to the dense forest understory.

Due to its limited distribution and specific environmental requirements, it is considered a valuable species in botanical research and conservation efforts.
