Melaleuca lutea

Melaleuca lutea, commonly known as the yellow melaleuca, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal or sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal dunes. Its flowers are borne in dense clusters and attract various pollinators, including bees and birds.

Melaleuca lutea is also known for its ability to thrive in saline environments, making it an important species in coastal ecosystems.
