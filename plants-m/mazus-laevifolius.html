Mazus laevifolius

Mazus laevifolius, commonly known as silverleaf mazus, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with silver-gray leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Mazus laevifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
