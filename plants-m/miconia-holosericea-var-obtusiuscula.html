Miconia holosericea var. obtusiuscula

Miconia holosericea var. obtusiuscula, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a species native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers, which are typically surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains numerous small seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed habitats and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
