Monnina insignis

Monnina insignis, commonly known as the pink mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with hairy, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Monnina insignis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
