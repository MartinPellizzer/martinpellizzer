Machilus globosus

Machilus globosus, commonly known as the globe-laurel, is a species of evergreen tree native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants such as cinnamon and bay laurel. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a spherical drupe, which contains a single seed, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appearance and is also used in landscaping due to its adaptability to various soil types.
