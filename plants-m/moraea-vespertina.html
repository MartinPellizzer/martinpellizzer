Moraea vespertina

Moraea vespertina, commonly known as the evening glory, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from deep purple to red, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, cylindrical seed pods that mature in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Moraea vespertina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
