Meconopsis sulphurea

Meconopsis sulphurea, commonly known as the golden meconopsis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the plant is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

Meconopsis sulphurea is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
