Merremia bambusetorum

Merremia bambusetorum, commonly known as the bamboo vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and long, flexible stems. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its fibrous roots and ability to climb make it suitable for use in living fences or as a ornamental plant.

However, it can be invasive in some areas if not managed properly.
