Merostachys capitata var. angustifolia

Merostachys capitata var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bamboo grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
