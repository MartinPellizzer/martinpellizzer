Melocanna blumei

Melocanna blumei, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, robust grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its massive, hollow stems that can reach heights of over 20 meters. The plant has large, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. Its rhizomes are edible and have been utilized in local cuisines.

Melocanna blumei is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
