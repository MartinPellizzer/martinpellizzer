Manganaroa paraensis

Manganaroa paraensis, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that often display a mix of red, orange, and yellow hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and drawing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding environment, and it plays a role in the ecological balance of the rainforest.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
