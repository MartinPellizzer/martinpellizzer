Machaeranthera bigelovii var. mucronata

Machaeranthera bigelovii var. mucronata, commonly known as the blunt-fruited blazingstar, is a flowering plant native to the central and western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with prominent central disk flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
