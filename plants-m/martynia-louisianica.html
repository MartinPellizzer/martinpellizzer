Martynia louisianica

Martynia louisianica, commonly known as the Louisiana fireflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces striking red-orange flowers with a distinctive, fiery appearance. The plant belongs to the family Solanaceae, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its small, glandular leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
