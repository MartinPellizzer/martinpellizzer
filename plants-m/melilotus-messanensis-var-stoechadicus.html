Melilotus messanensis var. stoechadicus

Melilotus messanensis var. stoechadicus, commonly known as the southern melilot, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a taproot system that helps it thrive in well-drained soils.

This variety is often found in dry, sunny habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential in ecological restoration.
