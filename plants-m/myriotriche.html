Myriotriche

Myriotriche, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Africa, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in dense, spherical heads, often resembling a crown. Myriotriche species typically have narrow, grass-like leaves and are often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in horticultural studies.
