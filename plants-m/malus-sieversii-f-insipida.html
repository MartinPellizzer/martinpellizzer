Malus sieversii f. insipida

Malus sieversii f. insipida, commonly known as the insipid form of the wild apple, is a rare botanical variant native to Central Asia.

This subspecies is characterized by its pale, often whitish or yellowish leaves and reduced fruit size compared to typical Malus sieversii. It is considered a genetic ancestor of many cultivated apple varieties and holds significant value in plant breeding programs. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils.

Its unique traits make it an important subject of study in both botanical and agricultural sciences.
