Myrcia recurvata var. grandifolia

Myrcia recurvata var. grandifolia, commonly known as the grandifolia myrtle, is a species native to the rainforests of northeastern South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant features elliptical, glossy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a capsule containing numerous small, dark seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
