Microcephalum sericeum

Microcephalum sericeum, commonly known as the silver-leafed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine silvery down, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
