Melica panicoides

Melica panicoides, commonly known as panicled meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. Its inflorescence consists of a paniculate arrangement of spikelets, which are usually greenish or pale yellow in color.

Melica panicoides plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates in its native ecosystems.
