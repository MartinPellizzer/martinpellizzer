Malanea hirsuta

Malanea hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy malanea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its unique morphology makes it a distinctive member of the Malvaceae family.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.
