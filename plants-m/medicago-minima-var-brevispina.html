Medicago minima var. brevispina

Medicago minima var. brevispina, commonly known as the dwarf sainfoin, is a low-growing leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers in umbel-like clusters. The plant is characterized by its short, stout stem and is often found in dry, rocky soils. It has a deep root system that helps it thrive in arid conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
