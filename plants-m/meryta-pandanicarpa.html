Meryta pandanicarpa

Meryta pandanicarpa, commonly known as the pandan fruit tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Araliaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces fleshy, orange-colored fruits that are edible and have a distinct aromatic flavor. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and fruit yield.

The leaves are also used in traditional cooking and crafts in various regions.
