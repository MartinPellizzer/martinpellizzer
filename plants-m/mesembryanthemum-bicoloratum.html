Mesembryanthemum bicoloratum

Mesembryanthemum bicoloratum, commonly known as the ice plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range from white to pink. The plant is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems. It blooms during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer.

Mesembryanthemum bicoloratum is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
