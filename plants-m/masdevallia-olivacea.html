Masdevallia olivacea

Masdevallia olivacea, commonly known as the Olive Orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, green, and yellow, often with a distinctive yellow lip. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Masdevallia olivacea is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
