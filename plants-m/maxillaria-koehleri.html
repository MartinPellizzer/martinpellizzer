Maxillaria koehleri

Maxillaria koehleri, commonly known as the Koehler's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizome and pseudobulbs that support its flowering structure. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. Its leaves are usually linear and arranged in a rosette pattern around the pseudobulbs.

Maxillaria koehleri thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
