Monardella exilis

Monardella exilis, commonly known as slender monardella, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Monardella exilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
