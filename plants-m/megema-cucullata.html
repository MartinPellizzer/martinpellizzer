Megema cucullata

Megema cucullata, commonly known as the hooded bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that are often blue or purple in color. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, showy blooms during the spring and early summer.

Megema cucullata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climate conditions.
