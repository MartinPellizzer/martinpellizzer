Malveopsis stenopetala

Malveopsis stenopetala, commonly known as the narrow-petaled mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Malveopsis stenopetala is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
