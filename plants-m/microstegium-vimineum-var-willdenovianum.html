Microstegium vimineum var. willdenovianum

Microstegium vimineum var. willdenovianum, commonly known as the slender false indigo, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems that often form dense mats on the ground. The plant produces small, pale blue to white flowers in late summer, which are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

This species is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and is considered an invasive plant in some regions due to its rapid growth and ability to outcompete native vegetation.
