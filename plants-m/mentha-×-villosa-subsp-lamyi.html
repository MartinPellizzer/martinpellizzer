Mentha × villosa subsp. lamyi

Mentha × villosa subsp.

lamyi, commonly known as catnip, is a hybrid plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae). It is characterized by its hairy, square stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions. Mentha × villosa subsp.

lamyi is known for its aromatic properties and is often used in herbal remedies and as a companion plant in gardens.
