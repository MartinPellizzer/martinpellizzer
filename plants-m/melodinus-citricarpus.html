Melodinus citricarpus

Melodinus citricarpus, commonly known as the sour orange tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its fragrant, compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically sour and have a high acidity, hence its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is cultivated in various parts of the world.
