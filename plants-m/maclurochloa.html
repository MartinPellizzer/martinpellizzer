Maclurochloa

Maclurochloa, commonly known as the narrowleaf paspalum, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and grassy areas. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Maclurochloa is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for native wildlife.
