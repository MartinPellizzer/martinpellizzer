Margaritopsis domatiicola

Margaritopsis domatiicola, commonly known as the rock cactus, is a unique succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and its ability to store water in its fleshy stems. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate touch to its rugged appearance. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, which help minimize water loss in its harsh environment.

Margaritopsis domatiicola is well adapted to rocky soils and extreme temperatures, making it a resilient species in its natural habitat.
