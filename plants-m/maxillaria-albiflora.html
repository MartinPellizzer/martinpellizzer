Maxillaria albiflora

Maxillaria albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces elegant, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive greenish throat. The plant typically blooms during the summer months, and its flowers are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths. The leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in humid, well-drained environments and is relatively easy to care for in a tropical or subtropical climate.
