Miconia leucantha

Miconia leucantha, commonly known as white miconia, is a species of flowering plant in the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. It produces berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature.

Miconia leucantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
