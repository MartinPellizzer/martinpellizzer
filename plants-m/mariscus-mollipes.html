Mariscus mollipes

Mariscus mollipes, commonly known as the slender marsh plant, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, swampy environments and is often found in association with other wetland flora. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, contributing to its overall herbaceous appearance.

Mariscus mollipes plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various insects and small aquatic organisms.
