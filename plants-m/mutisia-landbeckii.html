Mutisia landbeckii

Mutisia landbeckii, commonly known as the Andean mutisia, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a sturdy, branching stem with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the warmer months.

Mutisia landbeckii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
