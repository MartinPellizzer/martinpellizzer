Morus brunoniana

Morus brunoniana, commonly known as the Chinese mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and rapid growth rate. The tree produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically eaten by birds and other animals. It is often cultivated for its fibrous bark, which is used in papermaking and textile production.

Morus brunoniana is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
