Malpighia glandulifera

Malpighia glandulifera, commonly known as the rough mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are edible. Its botanical characteristics include glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
