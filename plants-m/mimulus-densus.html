Mimulus densus

Mimulus densus, commonly known as the dense monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of bright orange or yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as streambanks and forest floors. It has heart-shaped leaves and is part of the Physostegia genus within the Plantaginaceae family.

Mimulus densus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits to pollinators.
