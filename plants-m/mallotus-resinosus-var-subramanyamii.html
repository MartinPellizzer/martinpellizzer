Mallotus resinosus var. subramanyamii

Mallotus resinosus var. subramanyamii, commonly known as the Indian rubber tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and alternate, simple, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous small, flat seeds. It is valued for its latex, which has been traditionally used in the production of natural rubber.

This variant is distinguished by its unique morphological traits and ecological adaptations to its native habitat.
