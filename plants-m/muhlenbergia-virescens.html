Muhlenbergia virescens

Muhlenbergia virescens, commonly known as green muhlenbergia, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a suitable choice for native gardens and erosion control.

Muhlenbergia virescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting local wildlife and providing habitat for pollinators.
