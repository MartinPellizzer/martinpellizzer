Melissa alpina

Melissa alpina, commonly known as alpine lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are fragrant and often used in herbal teas for their calming properties.

Melissa alpina thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to alpine gardens.
