Monvillea calliantha

Monvillea calliantha, commonly known as the golden bell orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, well-drained environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base of the plant.

Monvillea calliantha is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in shaded gardens or as a potted plant.
