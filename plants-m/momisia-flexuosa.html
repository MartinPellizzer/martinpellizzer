Momisia flexuosa

Momisia flexuosa, commonly known as the flexible momisia, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, adapting to the harsh conditions of its native habitat. Its structure features a sprawling or climbing growth habit, with stems that can twist and bend, giving it its common name.

The flowers are usually pale blue or white, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
