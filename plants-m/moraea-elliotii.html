Moraea elliotii

Moraea elliotii, commonly known as Elliot's moraea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces inflorescences with multiple spurred flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
