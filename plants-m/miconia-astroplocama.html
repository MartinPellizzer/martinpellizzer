Miconia astroplocama

Miconia astroplocama, commonly known as the golden miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its fruits are red, fleshy berries that contain numerous seeds.

Miconia astroplocama is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
