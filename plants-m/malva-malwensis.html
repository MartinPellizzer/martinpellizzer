Malva malwensis

Malva malwensis, commonly known as the African marshmallow, is a perennial herb native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus plants. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found near water sources.

Its soft, hairy stems and mucilaginous flowers make it a distinctive and adaptable species.
