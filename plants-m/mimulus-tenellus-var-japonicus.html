Mimulus tenellus var. japonicus

Mimulus tenellus var. japonicus, commonly known as the Japanese monkey flower, is a delicate annual herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features slender stems and small, bright yellow flowers that resemble miniature monkey faces, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or along streambanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in early spring.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its charming appearance.
