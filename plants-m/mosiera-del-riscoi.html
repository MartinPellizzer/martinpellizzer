Mosiera del-riscoi

Mosiera del-riscoi, commonly known as the Sierra Madre dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Mosiera del-riscoi is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, exhibiting drought resistance through its deep root system.
