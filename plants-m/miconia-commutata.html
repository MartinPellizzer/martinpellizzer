Miconia commutata

Miconia commutata, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by round, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. Miconia commutata is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and potentially problematic in non-native environments.
