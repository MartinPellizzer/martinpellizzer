Malva aspera

Malva aspera, commonly known as rough hollyhock, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and okra, and is characterized by its soft, fuzzy stems and broad, lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Malva aspera is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
