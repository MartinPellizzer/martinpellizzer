Myosotis eliae

Myosotis eliae, commonly known as the Elean bluebell, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in wet, boggy areas and is often found in coastal regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Myosotis eliae is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
