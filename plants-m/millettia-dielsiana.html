Millettia dielsiana

Millettia dielsiana, commonly known as the Diels' Millettia, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often dispersed by animals, contributing to its ecological role in forest ecosystems.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
