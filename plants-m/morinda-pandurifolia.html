Morinda pandurifolia

Morinda pandurifolia, commonly known as the Chinese red root or red sandalwood, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its red or reddish-brown roots, which are highly valued in traditional medicine. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Morinda pandurifolia is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and ornamental value.
