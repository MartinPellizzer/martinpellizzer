Miconia neocrotonifolia

Miconia neocrotonifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit or false miconia, is a member of the Melastomataceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. Its flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, and typically have five petals.

The fruit is a persistent, fleshy capsule that can be reddish or yellowish in color.
