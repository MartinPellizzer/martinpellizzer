Moringa rivae subsp. rivae

Moringa rivae subsp.

rivae, commonly known as the African horseradish tree, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa. It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are often used for propagation. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species adapted to harsh conditions. The leaves and seeds of Moringa rivae subsp.

rivae are valued for their nutritional and medicinal properties.
