Mimulus michiganensis

Mimulus michiganensis, commonly known as the Michigan monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the United States, particularly in the Great Lakes region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist environments. The plant features showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

Mimulus michiganensis plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ecological and ornamental qualities.
