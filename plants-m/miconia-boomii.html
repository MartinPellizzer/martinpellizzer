Miconia boomii

Miconia boomii, commonly known as the giant miconia, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in diameter, making it one of the largest leaf plants in the world. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting various pollinators. Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in shaded environments have made it a popular ornamental species.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in some regions due to its aggressive spread and ecological impact.
