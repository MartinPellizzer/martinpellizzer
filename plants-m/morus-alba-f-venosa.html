Morus alba f. venosa

Morus alba f. venosa, commonly known as the variegated mulberry, is a cultivar of the white mulberry tree.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves that display a mix of white, green, and pink hues. This unique foliage makes it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes. The plant is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
