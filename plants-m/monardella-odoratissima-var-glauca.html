Monardella odoratissima var. glauca

Monardella odoratissima var. glauca, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, lavender-blue flower clusters that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like butterflies and bees. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-blue bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits in native gardens.
