Mentha × locyana

Mentha × locyana, commonly known as the hybrid mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a cross between Mentha × piperita (peppermint) and Mentha × cunninghamii (wild mint), resulting in a plant with a strong, aromatic scent. The leaves are typically narrow and pointed, with a rough texture, and the plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or white. Mentha × locyana is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its high menthol content.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
