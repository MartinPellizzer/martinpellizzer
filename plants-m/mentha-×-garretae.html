Mentha × garretae

Mentha × garretae, commonly known as Garrett's mint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Mentha spicata (spearmint) and Mentha viridiflora (green mint).

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly minty scent and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb with square stems and opposite leaves. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually purple or pink in color, appearing in clusters during the summer months.

Mentha × garretae is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses due to its pleasant fragrance and potential health benefits.
