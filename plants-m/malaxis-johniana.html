Malaxis johniana

Malaxis johniana, commonly known as the John's Malaxis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments within forests. The plant features a slender, rhizomatous stem with narrow, linear leaves. Its flowers are clustered in a spike and are characterized by their pale green to yellowish coloration.

Malaxis johniana is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental qualities.
