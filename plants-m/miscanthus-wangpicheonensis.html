Miscanthus wangpicheonensis

Miscanthus wangpicheonensis, commonly known as the Wangpicheon Miscanthus, is a species of grass native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and feather-like leaves that give it a graceful appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as riverbanks and wet meadows. It produces small, clustered panicles of flowers that bloom in the late summer to early autumn.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for use in ecological restoration, it has gained attention in horticulture and conservation efforts.
