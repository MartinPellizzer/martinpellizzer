Myrtus oonophylla

Myrtus oonophylla, commonly known as the coastal myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark blue to black berries. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, with a strong aromatic scent when crushed.

Myrtus oonophylla is well adapted to salt spray and can thrive in harsh coastal environments.
