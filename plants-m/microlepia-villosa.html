Microlepia villosa

Microlepia villosa, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia and parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Microlepia villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
