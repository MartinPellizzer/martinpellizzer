Malaxis lindeniana

Malaxis lindeniana, commonly known as the Lindenberg's Malaxis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. Native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant has linear leaves and produces flowers that are typically arranged in a loose spike.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicate and less commonly cultivated species.
