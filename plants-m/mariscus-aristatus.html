Mariscus aristatus

Mariscus aristatus, commonly known as the spiked mariscus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring finely divided leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers. Its roots are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

Mariscus aristatus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens and herbaceous borders.
