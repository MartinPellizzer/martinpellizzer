Maxillaria ochroglossa

Maxillaria ochroglossa, commonly known as the golden lip orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, producing multiple pseudobulbs. The plant features strap-shaped leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive golden lip. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to orange, with a contrasting darker lip.

Maxillaria ochroglossa thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
