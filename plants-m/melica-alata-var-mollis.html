Melica alata var. mollis

Melica alata var. mollis, commonly known as the silky meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hair-like leaves that give it a distinctive, silky appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, marshes, and riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, greenish-brown flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators and soil stabilization.
