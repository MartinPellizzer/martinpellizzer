Medinilla parva

Medinilla parva, commonly known as the miniature medinilla, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems. The plant produces fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling a cluster of small bells. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Medinilla parva is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
