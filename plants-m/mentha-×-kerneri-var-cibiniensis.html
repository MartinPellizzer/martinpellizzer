Mentha × kerneri var. cibiniensis

Mentha × kerneri var. cibiniensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mint, is a hybrid mint species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and clusters of small, pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is valued for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.

Its hybrid origin contributes to its adaptability and distinct scent compared to other mint species.
