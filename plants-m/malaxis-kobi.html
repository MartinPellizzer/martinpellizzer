Malaxis kobi

Malaxis kobi, commonly known as the Kobi orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil.

Malaxis kobi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
