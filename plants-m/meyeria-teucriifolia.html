Meyeria teucriifolia

Meyeria teucriifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf daphne, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which features a silvery-white underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers during the winter months. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Meyeria teucriifolia prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
