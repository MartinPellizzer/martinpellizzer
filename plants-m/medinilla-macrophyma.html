Medinilla macrophyma

Medinilla macrophyma, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or velvet plant, is a tropical shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that give it its common name, and its striking, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces fleshy, orange-red berries that are edible and often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Medinilla macrophyma is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
