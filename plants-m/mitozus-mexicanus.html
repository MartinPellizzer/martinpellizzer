Mitozus mexicanus

Mitozus mexicanus, commonly known as the Mexican mitozus, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is considered an important species for studying plant resilience in semi-arid ecosystems.
