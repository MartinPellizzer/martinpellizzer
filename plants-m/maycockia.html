Maycockia

Maycockia, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth texture, and the flowers emit a sweet fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators.

Maycockia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is prized for its unique nocturnal blooming habit.
