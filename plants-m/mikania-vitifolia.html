Mikania vitifolia

Mikania vitifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf vine, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and climbing habit. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a silvery underside, giving it its common name. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Mikania vitifolia is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and forest edges, and can be invasive in certain regions.
