Microlicia myrtifolia var. genuina

Microlicia myrtifolia var. genuina, commonly known as the red-flowered microlicia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in warm, humid climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally grown in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
