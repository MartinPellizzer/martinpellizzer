Malva olbia

Malva olbia, commonly known as the Armenian mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other well-known species like hibiscus and cotton. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Malva olbia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
