Monsonia arabica

Monsonia arabica, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a compact, evergreen shrub with a dense rosette of foliage. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers indirect sunlight.

Monsonia arabica is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
