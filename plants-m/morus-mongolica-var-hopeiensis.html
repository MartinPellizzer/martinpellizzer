Morus mongolica var. hopeiensis

Morus mongolica var. hopeiensis, commonly known as the Hopei mulberry, is a variant of the mulberry tree native to northern China.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically reddish when young and turn dark green as they mature. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are edible and often used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.

This variety is also valued for its role in supporting silkworm cultivation.
