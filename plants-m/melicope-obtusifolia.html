Melicope obtusifolia

Melicope obtusifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf melicope, is a tropical tree native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often elliptical leaves that are usually leathery in texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits containing aromatic seeds.

Melicope obtusifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
