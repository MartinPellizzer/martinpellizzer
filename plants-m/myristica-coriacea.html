Myristica coriacea

Myristica coriacea, commonly known as the nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and produces distinctive, woody fruits that contain the aromatic seeds used for nutmeg and mace. The plant belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is valued for its essential oils and spices. Its bark and leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its economic value.
