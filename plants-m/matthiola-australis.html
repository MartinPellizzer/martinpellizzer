Matthiola australis

Matthiola australis, commonly known as the Australian stock, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and usually white or pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Matthiola australis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine by Indigenous Australian communities.
