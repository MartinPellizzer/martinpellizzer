Malachra palmata

Malachra palmata, commonly known as the slender malachra, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its slender, unbranched stem. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish flowers, followed by red, fleshy fruits that contain a single seed. Its leaves are long and pinnate, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
