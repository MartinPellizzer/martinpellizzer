Mentha longifolia f. reticulatinervosa

Mentha longifolia f. reticulatinervosa, commonly known as the reticulate-veined mint, is a variant of the narrow-leaved mint.

It is characterized by its distinct, finely veined leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands or along stream banks. It produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic leaves and is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.
