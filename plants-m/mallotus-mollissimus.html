Mallotus mollissimus

Mallotus mollissimus, commonly known as velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, sour fruits that are used in traditional cuisine. Its leaves are often used in local medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for both its ornamental and utilitarian properties.
