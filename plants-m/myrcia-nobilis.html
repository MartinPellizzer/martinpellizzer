Myrcia nobilis

Myrcia nobilis, commonly known as the Brazilian beauty or Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a strong, spicy scent when crushed.

The flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the plant produces small, edible berries that are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
