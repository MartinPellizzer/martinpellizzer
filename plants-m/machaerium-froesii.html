Machaerium froesii

Machaerium froesii, commonly known as the Brazilian grass or Brazilian panicgrass, is a member of the Fabaceae family.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in South America. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, clustered flowers that are often pink or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture.

Machaerium froesii is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun.
