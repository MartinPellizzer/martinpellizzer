Mammillaria lactescens

Mammillaria lactescens, commonly known as the Milky Mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It prefers well-draining soil and full sun exposure, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for collectors and succulent enthusiasts.
