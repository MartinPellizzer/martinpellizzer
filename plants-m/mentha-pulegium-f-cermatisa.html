Mentha pulegium f. cermatisa

Mentha pulegium f. cermatisa, commonly known as narrow-leaved pennyroyal, is a variant of the pennyroyal plant characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs known for their essential oils. This subspecies is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its small, white to pale blue flowers appear in clusters and bloom during the summer months.

The plant is noted for its strong, minty fragrance and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
