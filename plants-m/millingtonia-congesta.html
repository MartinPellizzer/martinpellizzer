Millingtonia congesta

Millingtonia congesta, commonly known as the satinwood or yellow bell, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and clusters of fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped, leathery leaves that are glossy on top and lighter in color underneath. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
