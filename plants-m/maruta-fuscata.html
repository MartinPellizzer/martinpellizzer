Maruta fuscata

Maruta fuscata, commonly known as the dark marula, is a small to medium-sized tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spherical heads, followed by woody, spherical pods containing edible seeds.

It is adapted to dry, seasonal climates and plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
