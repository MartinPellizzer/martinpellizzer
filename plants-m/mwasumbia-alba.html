Mwasumbia alba

Mwasumbia alba, commonly known as the white milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Mwasumbia alba is known for its milky sap, which is a characteristic feature of the milkweed genus.
