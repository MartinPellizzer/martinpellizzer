Maxillaria farinifera

Maxillaria farinifera, commonly known as the powdered maxillaria, is a tropical orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of pink and white, often with a distinctive yellow throat. It thrives in humid, well-drained conditions and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Maxillaria farinifera is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
