Melaleuca

Melaleuca, commonly known as the bottlebrush, is a genus of flowering plants in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

These plants are native to Australia and are characterized by their distinctive cylindrical flowers that resemble brushes. The leaves are typically narrow and linear, often with a silver or grayish underside. Melaleuca species are known for their aromatic oils and are often used in traditional medicine.

They are also valued for their ability to thrive in coastal and sandy soils.
