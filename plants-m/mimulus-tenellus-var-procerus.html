Mimulus tenellus var. procerus

Mimulus tenellus var. procerus, commonly known as the slender yellow monkeyflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features elongated, slender stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. Its flowers are showy, with bright yellow petals that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
