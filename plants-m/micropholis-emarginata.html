Micropholis emarginata

Micropholis emarginata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed micropholis, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows to a height of about 10 meters and has slender, branching stems with smooth, greyish bark. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a pointed tip and a wavy margin. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by small, round berries.

This plant is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
