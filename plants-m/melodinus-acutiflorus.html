Melodinus acutiflorus

Melodinus acutiflorus, commonly known as the sharpflower melodine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that are typically bright red or orange, designed to attract pollinators such as birds and bats. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by animals. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture.

Melodinus acutiflorus thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
