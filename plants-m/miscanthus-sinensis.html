Miscanthus sinensis

Miscanthus sinensis, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and feathery, silver-tipped plumes that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late summer, which are followed by the characteristic fluffy seeds. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various conditions, it is widely cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
