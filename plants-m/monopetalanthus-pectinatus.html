Monopetalanthus pectinatus

Monopetalanthus pectinatus, commonly known as the yellow dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features a basal rosette of broad, waxy leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green color. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in spring, producing small seeds that are easily dispersed by the wind.

It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
