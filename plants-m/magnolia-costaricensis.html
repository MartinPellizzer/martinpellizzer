Magnolia costaricensis

Magnolia costaricensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it typically grows in moist, well-drained soils.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
