Mendoncia zarucchii

Mendoncia zarucchii, commonly known as the golden spire orchid, is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of golden-yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has a complex root system that helps it thrive in nutrient-poor soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its unique appearance.

Due to its limited habitat and threats from habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species.
