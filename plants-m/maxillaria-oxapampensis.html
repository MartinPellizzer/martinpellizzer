Maxillaria oxapampensis

Maxillaria oxapampensis, commonly known as the Andean orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at elevations ranging from 2,000 to 4,000 meters. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, often with intricate patterns. Its pseudobulbs are cylindrical and serve as storage organs for water and nutrients.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
