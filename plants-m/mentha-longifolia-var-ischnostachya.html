Mentha longifolia var. ischnostachya

Mentha longifolia var. ischnostachya, commonly known as narrow-leaved mint, is a variant of the mint family (Lamiaceae) characterized by its slender, elongated leaves.

This plant typically grows in temperate regions and prefers well-drained soil and partial shade. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue or violet in color. The leaves are aromatic and have a minty scent, making it a popular choice for culinary and medicinal uses.

It is often used in herbal teas and traditional remedies due to its refreshing aroma and potential health benefits.
