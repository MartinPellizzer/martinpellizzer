Marsdenia gymnemoides

Marsdenia gymnemoides, commonly known as the climbing milkweed vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by follicles containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical environments, often growing in moist, shaded areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional medicine practices.
