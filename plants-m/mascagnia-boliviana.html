Mascagnia boliviana

Mascagnia boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian mescal bean, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Bolivia and Peru.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

The plant has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties, though its toxic alkaloids require caution in use.
