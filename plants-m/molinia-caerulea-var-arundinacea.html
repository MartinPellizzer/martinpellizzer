Molinia caerulea var. arundinacea

Molinia caerulea var. arundinacea, commonly known as purple moor grass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive purple to blue-green foliage, which becomes more pronounced in the autumn. The plant produces inflorescences of spikelets that are often tinged with purple, adding ornamental value to landscapes. It thrives in wet, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, heaths, and other moist environments.

This variant is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
