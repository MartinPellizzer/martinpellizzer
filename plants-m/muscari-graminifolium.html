Muscari graminifolium

Muscari graminifolium, commonly known as the grass-leaved grape hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The plant typically blooms in early spring, adding a delicate touch of color to gardens and natural habitats. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a hardy choice for temperate climates.

Muscari graminifolium is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and ease of cultivation.
