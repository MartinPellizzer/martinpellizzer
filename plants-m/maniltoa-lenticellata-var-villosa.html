Maniltoa lenticellata var. villosa

Maniltoa lenticellata var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy maniltoa, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy texture on its stems and leaves, which gives it its varietal name "villosa.". The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its leaves are elongated and have a slightly waxy appearance, contributing to its unique botanical profile.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
