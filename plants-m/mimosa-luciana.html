Mimosa luciana

Mimosa luciana, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It produces small, edible pods that contain seeds, and it is often used in ornamental gardening.

The plant's ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment.
