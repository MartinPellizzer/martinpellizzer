Maxillaria sibundoyensis

Maxillaria sibundoyensis, commonly known as the Sibundoy orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in its mountainous habitat. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to orange, with intricate patterns that attract pollinators such as bees and moths.

Maxillaria sibundoyensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
