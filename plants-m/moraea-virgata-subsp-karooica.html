Moraea virgata subsp. karooica

Moraea virgata subsp.

karooica, commonly known as the Karoo lily, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy blooms during the summer months.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and protected areas.
