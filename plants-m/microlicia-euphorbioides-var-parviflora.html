Microlicia euphorbioides var. parviflora

Microlicia euphorbioides var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered microlicia, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish-green flowers, which are typical of the genus Microlicia. It is native to tropical regions of South America and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
