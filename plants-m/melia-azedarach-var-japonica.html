Melia azedarach var. japonica

Melia azedarach var. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese chinaberry tree, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves that are alternate and pinnately arranged, with oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by orange or red berries that contain toxic seeds. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in various parts of the world.

The tree is also noted for its ability to thrive in a range of soil types and climates.
