Mikania arrojadoi

Mikania arrojadoi, commonly known as the Brazilian wisteria, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and rapid growth rate. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often resembling those of the wisteria genus. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture.

Mikania arrojadoi is often found in moist, disturbed habitats and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
