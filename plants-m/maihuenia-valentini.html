Maihuenia valentini

Maihuenia valentini, commonly known as the Andean ice plant, is a succulent plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its fleshy, bluish-green leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid and cold environments. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers during the summer months, adding a delicate floral display to its rocky habitat. Maihuenia valentini is adapted to extreme conditions, with a slow growth rate and a long lifespan.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
