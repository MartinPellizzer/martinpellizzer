Melaleuca exuvia

Melaleuca exuvia, commonly known as the silver bottlebrush, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves and striking red or pink bottlebrush-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. Its aromatic foliage and showy inflorescences make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Melaleuca exuvia is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and sandy environments.
