Momordica angulata

Momordica angulata, commonly known as the horned melon or African horned melon, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiky, greenish-yellow fruit with a thick, leathery rind. The fruit contains a sweet, edible pulp surrounding black, seed-like structures. The plant belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers and pumpkins.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its unique and flavorful fruit.
