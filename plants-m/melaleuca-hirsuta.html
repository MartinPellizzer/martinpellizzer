Melaleuca hirsuta

Melaleuca hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy bottlebrush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical, red or pink flower spikes that resemble bottle brushes. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in coastal and sandy environments.

Melaleuca hirsuta is also known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
