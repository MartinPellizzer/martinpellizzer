Melaleuca mimosoides

Melaleuca mimosoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paperbark, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, flaky bark that peels off in thin layers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in clusters, which are typically followed by fluffy, cotton-like seed heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subtropical regions.

Melaleuca mimosoides is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
