Mesadenella atroviridis

Mesadenella atroviridis, commonly known as the black-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green leaves and striking, violet-blue flowers with prominent black veins. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Mesadenella atroviridis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
