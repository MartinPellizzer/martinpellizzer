Melastoma macrocarpum

Melastoma macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited melastoma, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by distinctive, fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
