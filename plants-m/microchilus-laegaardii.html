Microchilus laegaardii

Microchilus laegaardii, commonly known as the Cape orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to South Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate patterns on the petals. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the arid environment.

Microchilus laegaardii is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
