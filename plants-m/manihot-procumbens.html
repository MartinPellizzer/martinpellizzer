Manihot procumbens

Manihot procumbens, commonly known as arrowleaf groundcherry, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that grows in low, sprawling clusters with heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into edible, pear-shaped fruits. Its botanical features include hairy stems and leaves, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its nutritional value and use in traditional medicine.
