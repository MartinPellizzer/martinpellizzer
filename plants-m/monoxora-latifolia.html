Monoxora latifolia

Monoxora latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf monoxora, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branching stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by egg-shaped, pale green fruits. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a slightly rough texture.

Monoxora latifolia is typically found in grassy areas and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.
