Momordica fasciculata

Momordica fasciculata, commonly known as the creeping cucumber, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and pumpkins. The plant produces small, green, cucumber-like fruits that are typically harvested when immature. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are trailing and often root at the nodes.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
