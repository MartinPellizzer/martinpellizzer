Mimosa rondoniana

Mimosa rondoniana, commonly known as the Rondonia mimosa, is a shrub native to the Amazon rainforest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its root system has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, contributing to soil enrichment.

Due to its ecological importance and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
