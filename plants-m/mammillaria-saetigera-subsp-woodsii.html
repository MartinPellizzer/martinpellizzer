Mammillaria saetigera subsp. woodsii

Mammillaria saetigera subsp.

woodsii, commonly known as the Wood's hedgehog cactus, is a small, spineless cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico. It features a globular or cylindrical stem with prominent areoles and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant has a dense covering of fine, white hairs, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or indoor cultivation.

Due to its compact size and unique appearance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
