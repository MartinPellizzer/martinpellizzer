Myrtillocactus chende

Myrtillocactus chende, commonly known as the blue cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive blue-gray coloration due to its dense covering of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, violet flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its foliage. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to survive in dry conditions.

Myrtillocactus chende is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh environments.
