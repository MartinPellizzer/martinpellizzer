Mitrephora multiflora

Mitrephora multiflora, commonly known as the many-flowered mitrephora, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually odd-pinnate with lanceolate leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in terminal clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Mitrephora multiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
