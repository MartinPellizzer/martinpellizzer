Maerua angolensis subsp. angolensis

Maerua angolensis subsp.

angolensis, commonly known as the Angola thornbush, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained sandy or rocky soils.

The plant has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that releases tiny seeds when mature.

This species is adapted to withstand drought and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
