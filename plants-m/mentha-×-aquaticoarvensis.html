Mentha × aquaticoarvensis

Mentha × aquaticoarvensis, commonly known as water mint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Mentha aquatica and Mentha rotundifolia.

It is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and a strong, minty aroma. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as stream banks, wet meadows, and pond edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a serrated margin and are often used in herbal remedies.

Mentha × aquaticoarvensis is a perennial herb that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
