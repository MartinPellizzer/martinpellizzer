Manihot tripartita var. seminuda

Manihot tripartita var. seminuda, commonly known as the Mexican arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces edible tubers, which are rich in starch and have a slightly sweet flavor. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and can grow up to several feet in height. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The tubers are often used as a starchy food source and can be prepared similarly to potatoes.
