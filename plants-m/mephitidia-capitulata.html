Mephitidia capitulata

Mephitidia capitulata, commonly known as the purple globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, green, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native landscaping.
