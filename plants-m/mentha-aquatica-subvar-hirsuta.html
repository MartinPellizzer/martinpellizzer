Mentha aquatica subvar. hirsuta

Mentha aquatica subvar. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy water mint, is a perennial herb native to wetlands and marshy areas in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and a strong, aromatic scent. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense colonies. Its flowers are small, tubular, and arranged in whorls along the stem, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
