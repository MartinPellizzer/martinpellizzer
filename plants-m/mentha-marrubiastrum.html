Mentha marrubiastrum

Mentha marrubiastrum, commonly known as narrow-leaf mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue, arranged in loose spikes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Mentha marrubiastrum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and antiseptic properties.
