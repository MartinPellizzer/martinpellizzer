Mertensia ferruginea

Mertensia ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive reddish-brown underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Mertensia ferruginea is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
