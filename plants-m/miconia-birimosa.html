Miconia birimosa

Miconia birimosa, commonly known as the Brazilian glorybower, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter. Miconia birimosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and floral display.
