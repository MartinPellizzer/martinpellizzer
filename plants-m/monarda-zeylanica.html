Monarda zeylanica

Monarda zeylanica, commonly known as Indian bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their mild sedative and antispasmodic properties.

Monarda zeylanica thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.
