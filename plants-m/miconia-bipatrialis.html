Miconia bipatrialis

Miconia bipatrialis, commonly known as the two-country miconia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
