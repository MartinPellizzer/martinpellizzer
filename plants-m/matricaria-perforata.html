Matricaria perforata

Matricaria perforata, commonly known as the perforate chamomile, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small yellow flowers with white centers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Matricaria perforata is often used in traditional medicine for its mild sedative and anti-inflammatory properties.
