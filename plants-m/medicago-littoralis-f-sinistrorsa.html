Medicago littoralis f. sinistrorsa

Medicago littoralis f. sinistrorsa, commonly known as the coastal medic.

This plant is a variant of the coastal medick, characterized by its distinctive left-handed twisting of the flower stems, a trait known as sinistrorsa. It typically grows in coastal areas, where it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and oval-shaped pods, which are typical of the Fabaceae family.

It is often found in maritime environments and is adapted to withstand salt spray and wind.
