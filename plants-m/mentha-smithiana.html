Mentha smithiana

Mentha smithiana, commonly known as the American peppermint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, cool menthol scent, while its flowers are small and pale blue or purple.

Mentha smithiana is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in herbal remedies.
