Maerua becquetii

Maerua becquetii, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and alternate, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife.
