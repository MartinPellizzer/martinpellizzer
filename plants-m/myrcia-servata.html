Myrcia servata

Myrcia servata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals. Myrcia servata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.

However, it can become invasive in some regions if not managed properly.
