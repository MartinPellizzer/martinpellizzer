Mespilus phaenopyrum

Mespilus phaenopyrum, commonly known as the Persian white mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The tree produces edible berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.

The plant has historical significance in traditional medicine and horticulture.
