Mesembryanthemum rhizophorum

Mesembryanthemum rhizophorum, commonly known as the root mesem, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, which is characterized by its fleshy leaves and flowers adapted to arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is known for its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems and roots. Its flowers, which are usually yellow or orange, bloom in the early morning and close by midday.

Mesembryanthemum rhizophorum is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
