Meconopsis × musgravei

Meconopsis × musgravei, commonly known as the Himalayan poppy, is a hybrid species native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are palmately lobed, often with a velvety texture.

Meconopsis × musgravei is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
