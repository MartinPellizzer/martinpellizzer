Mimosa calodendron var. leprosa

Mimosa calodendron var. leprosa, commonly known as the white mimosa, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are pinnately compound, giving it a lacy appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to shed its leaves in response to touch, a trait known as thigmonasty.
