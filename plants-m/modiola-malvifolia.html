Modiola malvifolia

Modiola malvifolia, commonly known as the white mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. Its showy white flowers, which bloom in summer, are often clustered in loose inflorescences.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.
