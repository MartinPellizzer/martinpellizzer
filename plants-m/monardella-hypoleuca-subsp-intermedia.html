Monardella hypoleuca subsp. intermedia

Monardella hypoleuca subsp.

intermedia, commonly known as the pink mintsweet, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a perennial herb with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its aromatic foliage and vibrant flowers make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
