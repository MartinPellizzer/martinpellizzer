Maianthemum bifolium f. bipartitum

Maianthemum bifolium f. bipartitum, commonly known as the parti-flowered False Lily-of-the-Valley, is a rare and distinctive cultivar of the common False Lily-of-the-Valley.

It is characterized by its unique leaf structure, featuring two distinct lobes that give it a bipartite appearance. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural collections.
