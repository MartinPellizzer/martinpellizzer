Malaxis slamatensis

Malaxis slamatensis, commonly known as the Malagasy orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and specialized growth habits. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are usually small and greenish-white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Due to its restricted habitat and limited distribution, Malaxis slamatensis is considered a rare and endangered species.
