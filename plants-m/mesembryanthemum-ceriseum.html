Mesembryanthemum ceriseum

Mesembryanthemum ceriseum, commonly known as the ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments. Its unique ability to store water in its leaves helps it survive in harsh conditions.

Mesembryanthemum ceriseum is often used in rock gardens and as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
