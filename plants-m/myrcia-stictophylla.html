Myrcia stictophylla

Myrcia stictophylla, commonly known as the scarlet myrtle, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are leathery and glossy, with a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

The fruit is a small, red, berry-like structure that matures in late summer and is often consumed by various bird species.
