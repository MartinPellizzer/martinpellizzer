Malus baccata f. aureomarginata

Malus baccata f. aureomarginata, commonly known as the golden-margin crabapple, is a compact, ornamental tree or shrub native to China.

It features small, showy white flowers in spring, followed by bright red, edible fruits in autumn. The leaves are typically oval with a smooth texture and a distinctive golden edge, which adds visual interest throughout the growing season. This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a versatile choice for smaller spaces.
