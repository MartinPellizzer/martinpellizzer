Myrtus communis var. gibertii

Myrtus communis var. gibertii, commonly known as the Gibraltar myrtle, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features small, dark green, oval-shaped leaves with a fragrant, slightly waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white, fragrant flowers in spring, which are followed by small, dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in Mediterranean cultures.
