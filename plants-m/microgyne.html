Microgyne

Microgyne, commonly known as the small honeycomb plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Meliaceae.

It is characterized by its unique, small, and often clustered inflorescences that resemble a honeycomb structure. The leaves are typically alternate, simple, and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin. Microgyne species are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

They are often found in forest understories and are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
