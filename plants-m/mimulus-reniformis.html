Mimulus reniformis

Mimulus reniformis, commonly known as the yellow waterleaf, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in stream banks and wet meadows. Its scientific name reflects its distinctive leaf shape, with "reniformis" meaning kidney-shaped in Latin.

Mimulus reniformis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
