Myrcia ventuarensis

Myrcia ventuarensis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains several seeds.

Myrcia ventuarensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
