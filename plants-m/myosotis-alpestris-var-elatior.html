Myosotis alpestris var. elatior

Myosotis alpestris var. elatior, commonly known as the Alpine Forget-me-not, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, sky-blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a white or pale blue center. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, adapted to withstand harsh mountain conditions.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of remembrance and enduring love in some cultures.
