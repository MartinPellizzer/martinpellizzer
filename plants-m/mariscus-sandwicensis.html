Mariscus sandwicensis

Mariscus sandwicensis, commonly known as the sandplain marigold, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and is typically found in dry, sandy soils on coastal dunes. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsules as its fruit. It is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, including high salinity and strong winds.

Due to habitat loss, it is listed as endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
