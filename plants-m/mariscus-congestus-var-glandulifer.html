Mariscus congestus var. glandulifer

Mariscus congestus var. glandulifer, commonly known as the glandular mariscus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in glandular structures. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, followed by small, fleshy fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
