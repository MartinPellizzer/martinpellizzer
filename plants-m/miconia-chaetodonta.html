Miconia chaetodonta

Miconia chaetodonta, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of short, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered white or yellowish flowers that are often followed by distinctive, spiny fruits. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its invasive potential in some regions, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural and natural ecosystems.
