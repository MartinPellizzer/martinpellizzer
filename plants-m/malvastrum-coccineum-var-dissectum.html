Malvastrum coccineum var. dissectum

Malvastrum coccineum var. dissectum, commonly known as the red velvetleaf or crimson mallow, is a flowering plant in the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are deeply lobed and have a velvety texture, giving the plant its common name. It is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in moist, sunny environments.

This plant is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.
