Madhuca latifolia

Madhuca latifolia, commonly known as the greater wax tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The tree is well-known for its production of edible wax, which is used in traditional crafts and as a food source for certain animals. Its fruits are small, round, and contain a single seed, making it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and role in local ecosystems.
