Mimosa pseudopaniculata

Mimosa pseudopaniculata, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in panicle-like clusters, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its unique foliage, it is often used in landscape design to add texture and visual interest.
