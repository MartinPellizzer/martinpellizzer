Malus communis var. acerba

Malus communis var. acerba, commonly known as the crabapple tree, is a species of apple tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, ornamental fruits and showy flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 meters, with a rounded or irregular crown. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a serrated margin.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.
