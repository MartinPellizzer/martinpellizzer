Musa fehi

Musa fehi, commonly known as the Tongan banana, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and clustered fruiting structures. The plant produces edible bananas that are typically smaller and more elongated compared to common commercial varieties. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Musa fehi is an important crop for local agriculture and traditional diets in its native regions.
