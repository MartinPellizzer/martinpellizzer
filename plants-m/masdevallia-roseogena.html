Masdevallia roseogena

Masdevallia roseogena, commonly known as the Pink-veined Masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that display a striking combination of pink and white hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that help it conserve moisture in its high-altitude environment.

Masdevallia roseogena is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively compact growth habit.
