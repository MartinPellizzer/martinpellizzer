Maundia triglochinoides

Maundia triglochinoides, commonly known as the African marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist to waterlogged soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or floating, and it produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in spikes. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Maundia triglochinoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
