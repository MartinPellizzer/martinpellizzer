Miconia discolorata

Miconia discolorata, commonly known as the white miconia, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a white, waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds make it an invasive species in some regions.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though caution is needed due to its ecological impact.
