Malpighia paniculata

Malpighia paniculata, commonly known as the granadilla, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, fleshy, red berries that resemble grapes. The plant belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is known for its ornamental value due to its vibrant flowers. The fruit is edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and is often used in juices and desserts.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
