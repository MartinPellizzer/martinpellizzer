Morus vernonii

Morus vernonii, commonly known as the Vernon mulberry, is a species of mulberry native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food for silkworms.

The leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
