Maxillaria lentiginosa

Maxillaria lentiginosa, commonly known as the chocolate orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces fragrant, dark brown to purple flowers that are often covered in a velvety texture. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a compact rosette.

Maxillaria lentiginosa is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
