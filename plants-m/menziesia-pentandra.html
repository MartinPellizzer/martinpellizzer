Menziesia pentandra

Menziesia pentandra, commonly known as the red spruce, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces white to pinkish flowers in clusters, which are visited by various pollinators. Its berries are dark blue and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Menziesia pentandra thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
