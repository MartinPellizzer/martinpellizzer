Miconia inamoena

Miconia inamoena, commonly known as the red miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed and have a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Miconia inamoena is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain environments.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some regions, though care must be taken to prevent its spread.
