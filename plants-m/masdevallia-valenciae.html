Masdevallia valenciae

Masdevallia valenciae, commonly known as the Valencia orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and its striking, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing intricate flowers with a mix of sepals and petals that form a showy display. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in cool, humid environments with partial shade.

Masdevallia valenciae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
