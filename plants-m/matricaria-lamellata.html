Matricaria lamellata

Matricaria lamellata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved chamomile, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features finely divided, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers with a central disc. It is often used in traditional medicine for its calming and anti-inflammatory properties.

Matricaria lamellata thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
