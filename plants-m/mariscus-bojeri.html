Mariscus bojeri

Mariscus bojeri, commonly known as the Bojer's mariscus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent shrub that typically grows in rocky or arid environments. The plant features small, fleshy leaves and produces clusters of pale yellow or greenish-white flowers. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions with its water-storing stems and reduced leaf surface.

Mariscus bojeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in xerophytic gardens.
