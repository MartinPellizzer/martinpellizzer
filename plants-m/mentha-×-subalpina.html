Mentha × subalpina

Mentha × subalpina, commonly known as alpine mint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Mentha arvensis and Mentha suaveolens.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly minty leaves and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers cooler climates. Mentha × subalpina is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 60 cm.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
