Mulinum dipterygia

Mulinum dipterygia, commonly known as the broad-leafed mullein, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features tall, hairy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters at the top of the stem, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

The species is adapted to rocky and dry habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.
