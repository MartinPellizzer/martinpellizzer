Mentha longifolia var. campiensis

Mentha longifolia var. campiensis, commonly known as Brazilian mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features square, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive, aromatic fragrance. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in whorled clusters, typical of the Lamiaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other mint species.
