Monardella anemonoides

Monardella anemonoides, commonly known as the clustered monardella, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants, and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from white to pinkish-purple.

Monardella anemonoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
