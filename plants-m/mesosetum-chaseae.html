Mesosetum chaseae

Mesosetum chaseae, commonly known as the Chase's mesosetum, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family. Mesosetum chaseae thrives in wetland habitats, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its ecological importance lies in providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
