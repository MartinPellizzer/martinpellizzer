Mina cordata

Mina cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf or heartleaf vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically bright green and have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Mina cordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
