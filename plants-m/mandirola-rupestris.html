Mandirola rupestris

Mandirola rupestris, commonly known as the rock mandirola, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a succulent shrub that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant features small, fleshy leaves that are adapted to store water, a characteristic common in arid environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Mandirola rupestris is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
