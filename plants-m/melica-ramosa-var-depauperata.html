Melica ramosa var. depauperata

Melica ramosa var. depauperata, commonly known as the slender wood grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture.

This variety is notable for its reduced floral structure compared to other Melica species, contributing to its unique botanical appearance.
