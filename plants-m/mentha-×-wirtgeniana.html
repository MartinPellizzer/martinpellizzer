Mentha × wirtgeniana

Mentha × wirtgeniana, commonly known as the hybrid mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Mentha spicata (spearmint) and Mentha longifolia (wild mint). The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and fragrant, small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Mentha × wirtgeniana is widely used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its aromatic essential oils.
