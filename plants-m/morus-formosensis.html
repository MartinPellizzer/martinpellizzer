Morus formosensis

Morus formosensis, commonly known as the Taiwan mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and smooth, grey bark. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
