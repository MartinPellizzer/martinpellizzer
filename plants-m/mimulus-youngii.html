Mimulus youngii

Mimulus youngii, commonly known as the Sierra primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in mountainous regions. It blooms in the spring and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and forest edges.

Mimulus youngii is a perennial species that plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
