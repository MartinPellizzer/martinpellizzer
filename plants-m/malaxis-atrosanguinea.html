Malaxis atrosanguinea

Malaxis atrosanguinea, commonly known as the black blood orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the wet tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, dark red to black-colored flowers that resemble blood, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or along riverbanks. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to its striking appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
