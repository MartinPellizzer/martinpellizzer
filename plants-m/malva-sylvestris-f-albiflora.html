Malva sylvestris f. albiflora

Malva sylvestris f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered mallow, is a flowering plant in the Malvaceae family.

It is a cultivated form of the common mallow, characterized by its pale to white petals instead of the typical pink or purple hues. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with soft, fuzzy textures and produces showy, five-petaled flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, often found in gardens and naturalized areas.

This ornamental plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
