Minuartia meyeri var. cypricola

Minuartia meyeri var. cypricola, commonly known as the pink sandstone saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and arid environments.

It is characterized by its mat-forming growth habit and small, fleshy, often pink or reddish leaves. The plant produces delicate white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and harsh weather conditions.

This variety is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in rock gardens.
