Melaleuca glaucocalyx

Melaleuca glaucocalyx, commonly known as the silver-leafed melaleuca, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, bottle-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.

Melaleuca glaucocalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
