Microtatorchis multiflora

Microtatorchis multiflora, commonly known as the multiblade fern, is a delicate, herbaceous plant native to the humid tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features a compact rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, which bloom during the wet season. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the rich, organic soil in its natural habitat.

Microtatorchis multiflora is often cultivated as a ornamental plant for its elegant appearance and air-purifying qualities.
