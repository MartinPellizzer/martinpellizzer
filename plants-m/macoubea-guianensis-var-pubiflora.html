Macoubea guianensis var. pubiflora

Macoubea guianensis var. pubiflora, commonly known as the Guianese macoubea, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Guyana and Suriname.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface, depending on the variety.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its vibrant floral display.
