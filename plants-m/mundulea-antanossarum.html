Mundulea antanossarum

Mundulea antanossarum, commonly known as the African sand thorn, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small leaflets and produces pods containing seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it important for soil fertility in degraded ecosystems.
