Mikania angulata

Mikania angulata, commonly known as the angular Mikania, is a climbing vine native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in heads, typical of the daisy family. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open fields.

Mikania angulata is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative means.
