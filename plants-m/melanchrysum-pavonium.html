Melanchrysum pavonium

Melanchrysum pavonium, commonly known as the sweet violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant belongs to the family Violaceae and is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies. Its flowers are rich in essential oils and have been used to treat respiratory conditions and skin ailments.

Melanchrysum pavonium thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental and therapeutic plants.
