Melocactus griseus

Melocactus griseus, commonly known as the gray melon cactus, is a species native to the dry regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem covered in dense, white spines that provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces striking yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by small, round fruits. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments.

Melocactus griseus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
