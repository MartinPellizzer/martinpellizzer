Macrocnemum tetrandrum

Macrocnemum tetrandrum, commonly known as the four-stamen grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to various regions of Asia, including China, India, and Southeast Asia. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinct sheath at the base.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets, each containing four stamens, which is a key identifying feature of the species.
