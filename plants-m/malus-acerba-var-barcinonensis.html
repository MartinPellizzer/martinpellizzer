Malus acerba var. barcinonensis

Malus acerba var. barcinonensis, commonly known as the Barcino cherry, is a rare and ornamental species native to Spain.

It is characterized by its small, sweet, and edible fruits that ripen in late summer. The plant produces white to pink flowers in early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a botanically significant and potentially endangered species.
