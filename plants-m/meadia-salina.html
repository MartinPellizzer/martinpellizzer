Meadia salina

Meadia salina, commonly known as salt marsh meadia, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the United States.

It thrives in brackish or saline wetlands, showcasing its adaptability to high-salt environments. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, helping it retain moisture in fluctuating tidal conditions.

Meadia salina plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal soils and supporting local biodiversity.
