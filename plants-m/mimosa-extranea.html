Mimosa extranea

Mimosa extranea, commonly known as the Brazilian sensitive plant, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Mimosa genus within the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in spherical or cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its sensitive leaf response, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
