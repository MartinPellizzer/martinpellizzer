Microchaeta integrifolia

Microchaeta integrifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf microchaeta, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with entire margins. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

Microchaeta integrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
