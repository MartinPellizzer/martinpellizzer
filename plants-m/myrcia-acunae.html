Myrcia acunae

Myrcia acunae, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of white or pinkish flowers. It produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red or yellow when ripe.

Myrcia acunae is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
