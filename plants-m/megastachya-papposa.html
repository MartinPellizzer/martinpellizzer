Megastachya papposa

Megastachya papposa, commonly known as the golden cupplant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, forming clustered inflorescences. Its seeds are surrounded by a pappus, a feathery structure that aids in wind dispersal.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
