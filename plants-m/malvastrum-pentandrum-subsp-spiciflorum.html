Malvastrum pentandrum subsp. spiciflorum

Malvastrum pentandrum subsp.

spiciflorum, commonly known as the spiciflorum mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and hairy leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape.

The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
