Matucana calliantha

Matucana calliantha, commonly known as the golden torch cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that are often covered in a dense layer of golden to yellowish spines. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a vibrant touch to its desert habitat. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
