Musa hirta

Musa hirta, commonly known as the hairy banana, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are covered in fine, hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of flowers followed by elongated, curved fruits that are typically green when unripe. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Musa hirta thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
