Myrcia megaphylla

Myrcia megaphylla, commonly known as the broad-leaf myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered, and typically have a fragrant scent, attracting various pollinators.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, dark seeds.
