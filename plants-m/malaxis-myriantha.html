Malaxis myriantha

Malaxis myriantha, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, pale green to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient uptake.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
