Muscari atropatanum

Muscari atropatanum, commonly known as the Persian grape hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It produces dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from deep purple to violet. The plant typically blooms in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to gardens and natural landscapes. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from the base of the plant.

Muscari atropatanum is well-suited for rock gardens and can thrive in partial shade to full sun conditions.
