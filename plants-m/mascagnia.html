Mascagnia

Mascagnia, commonly known as the nightshade or desert nightshade, is a genus of flowering plants in the Solanaceae family.

Mascagnia species are typically shrubs or small trees with thick, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water. They produce showy, often yellow or orange flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths.
