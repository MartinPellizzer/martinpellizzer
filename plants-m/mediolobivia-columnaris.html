Mediolobivia columnaris

Mediolobivia columnaris, commonly known as the slender columnar cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that grow in a vertical, columnar form, often reaching several meters in height. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss and provide protection from herbivores. It produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in the spring.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
