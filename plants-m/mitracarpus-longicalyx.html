Mitracarpus longicalyx

Mitracarpus longicalyx, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in hard, woody capsules, which contribute to its common name.

Mitracarpus longicalyx is valued for its durability and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
