Mentha × niliaca var. notarisii

Mentha × niliaca var. notarisii, commonly known as the Hungarian mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and stream banks. Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported digestive and calming properties.

The species is valued for its strong mint scent and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
