Microseris laciniata subsp. leptosepala

Microseris laciniata subsp.

leptosepala, commonly known as the narrow-sepaled biscuitroot, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture.

The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are arranged in a cluster at the end of each stem. Its roots are thick and fleshy, adapted for storing water in arid environments.

It is typically found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.
