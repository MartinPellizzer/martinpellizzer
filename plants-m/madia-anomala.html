Madia anomala

Madia anomala, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky soils. The plant features basal rosettes of gray-green, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and the plant is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Madia anomala is also valued for its medicinal properties and has been used by indigenous peoples for various traditional purposes.
