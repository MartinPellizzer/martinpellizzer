Morella domingana

Morella domingana, commonly known as the Dominican wax myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myricaceae.

It is native to the Caribbean, particularly the Dominican Republic, and is characterized by its small, wax-coated leaves. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that contain a single seed.

Morella domingana is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and swampy areas.
