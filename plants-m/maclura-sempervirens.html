Maclura sempervirens

Maclura sempervirens, commonly known as the yellow wood or honey locust, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and compound leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by distinctive, orange to red berries. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its autumn foliage and unique fruit, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
