Malpighia rotundifolia

Malpighia rotundifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian wolfberry or pitaya, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It produces small, bright red berries that are rich in vitamin C and antioxidants. The plant has a dense, thorny foliage and prefers well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and it is often cultivated for both ornamental and edible purposes.

The fruit is typically harvested when fully ripe, and the plant is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
