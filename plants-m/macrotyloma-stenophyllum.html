Macrotyloma stenophyllum

Macrotyloma stenophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. Its seeds are often used in traditional agriculture for their nutritional value and adaptability to poor soils.

The species is valued for its resilience and contribution to sustainable farming practices.
