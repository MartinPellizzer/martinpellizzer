Mitellopsis pentandra

Mitellopsis pentandra, commonly known as the five-angled mitella, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often lobed, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Mitellopsis pentandra is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
