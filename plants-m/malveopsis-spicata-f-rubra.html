Malveopsis spicata f. rubra

Malveopsis spicata f. rubra, commonly known as the red-stemmed mallows, is a variant of the species Malveopsis spicata, characterized by its distinctive reddish stems.

This plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton, and is native to certain regions of Africa. It typically grows as an herbaceous annual or perennial, depending on the climate, and features showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The leaves are usually palmately lobed, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
