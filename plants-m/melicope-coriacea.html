Melicope coriacea

Melicope coriacea, commonly known as the rough melicope, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in coastal or lowland forest habitats. The plant has rough, bark-like stems and dark green, leathery leaves. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits.

Melicope coriacea is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some island cultures.
