Malaxis breviscapa

Malaxis breviscapa, commonly known as the short-capped Malaxis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories. The plant features a slender, erect stem with few leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in a raceme. Its floral structure is characterized by narrow, hood-like sepals and petals, typical of the Orchidaceae family.

Malaxis breviscapa is noted for its delicate appearance and is often found in lowland and mountainous habitats.
