Molina trimera

Molina trimera, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate and pubescent, while its fruits are fleshy berries that contain several seeds.

Molina trimera is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
