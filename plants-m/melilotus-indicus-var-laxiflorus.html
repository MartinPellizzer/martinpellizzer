Melilotus indicus var. laxiflorus

Melilotus indicus var. laxiflorus, commonly known as the slender-flowered melilot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
