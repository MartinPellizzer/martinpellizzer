Matthiola maroccana var. puberula

Matthiola maroccana var. puberula, commonly known as the Moroccan wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, brightly colored flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically covered in a fine layer of hair, giving the plant a fuzzy appearance. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
