Microchilus campanulatus

Microchilus campanulatus, commonly known as the bellflower of the genus Microchilus, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family, which includes various species of bellflowers. The plant is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
