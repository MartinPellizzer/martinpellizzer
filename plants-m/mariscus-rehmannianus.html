Mariscus rehmannianus

Mariscus rehmannianus, commonly known as the Rehmann's marigold, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in sunny environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers range in color from yellow to orange.

Mariscus rehmannianus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
