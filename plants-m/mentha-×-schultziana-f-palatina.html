Mentha × schultziana f. palatina

Mentha × schultziana f. palatina, commonly known as the Palatinate peppermint, is a hybrid mint species originating from Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly menthol-like scent and its dark green, serrated leaves. The plant typically grows as a sprawling perennial with square stems and clusters of small, pale purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of essential oils.
