Muraltia dispersa

Muraltia dispersa, commonly known as the spreading muraltia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white. Its fruit is a legume that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

Muraltia dispersa is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
