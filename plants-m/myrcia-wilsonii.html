Myrcia wilsonii

Myrcia wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains several seeds.

Myrcia wilsonii is often found in shaded understory environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
