Mattiastrum kurdistanicum

Mattiastrum kurdistanicum, commonly known as the Kurdish milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of northern Iraq and Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a cluster of small flowers. The plant features hairy, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is adapted to grow in rocky and arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant properties.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
