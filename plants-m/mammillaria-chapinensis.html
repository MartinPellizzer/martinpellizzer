Mammillaria chapinensis

Mammillaria chapinensis, commonly known as the Chapin's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and dense covering of spines, which provide protection against herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Mammillaria chapinensis is a popular choice for succulent collections due to its compact size and ornamental appearance.
