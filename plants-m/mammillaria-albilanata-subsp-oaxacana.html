Mammillaria albilanata subsp. oaxacana

Mammillaria albilanata subsp.

oaxacana, commonly known as the Oaxaca nipple cactus, is a small, globular cactus native to Mexico. It features a pale green to white body with prominent, rounded tubercles that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
