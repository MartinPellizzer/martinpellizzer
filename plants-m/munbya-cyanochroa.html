Munbya cyanochroa

Munbya cyanochroa, commonly known as the blue-veined gum, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves that are typically a glossy green on the upper surface. The tree produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits. Its foliage provides habitat for various native wildlife, and it is often found in dry sclerophyll forests and woodlands.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses by Indigenous communities.
