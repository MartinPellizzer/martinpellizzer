Melloa fraxinifolia

Melloa fraxinifolia, commonly known as the white mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in the summer, which are followed by fluffy, seed-bearing pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Melloa fraxinifolia is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for respiratory ailments.
