Melocactus mazelianus subsp. andinus

Melocactus mazelianus subsp.

andinus, commonly known as the Andean melocactus, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America. It is characterized by its distinctive, ribbed stem and spiny, clustered areoles.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations ranging from 3,000 to 5,000 meters. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing during the warmer months.

This subspecies is adapted to cold and arid conditions, making it a resilient and unique member of the Cactaceae family.
