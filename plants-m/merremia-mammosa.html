Merremia mammosa

Merremia mammosa, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, swollen, bottle-shaped fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses.
