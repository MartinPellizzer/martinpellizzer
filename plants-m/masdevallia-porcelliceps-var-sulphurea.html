Masdevallia porcelliceps var. sulphurea

Masdevallia porcelliceps var. sulphurea, commonly known as the sulphur-tipped masdevallia, is a striking orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns resembling porcelain. The plant typically grows as a miniature orchid, with slender stems and compact foliage. Its unique coloration and delicate appearance make it a prized specimen among orchid enthusiasts.

It thrives in cool, humid environments with high light exposure and well-draining soil.
