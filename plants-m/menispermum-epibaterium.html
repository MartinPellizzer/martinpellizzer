Menispermum epibaterium

Menispermum epibaterium, commonly known as the climbing aristolochia, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, fragrant flowers that resemble female reproductive structures. The plant produces yellowish-green fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
