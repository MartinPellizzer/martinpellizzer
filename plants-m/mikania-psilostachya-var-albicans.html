Mikania psilostachya var. albicans

Mikania psilostachya var. albicans, commonly known as the white-flowered mile-a-minute weed, is a climbing vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its rapid growth rate and long, slender stems that can spread quickly through vegetative propagation. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the summer months. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are covered in fine, hairy hairs.

This species is often considered an invasive plant in regions outside its native range due to its aggressive growth habit.
