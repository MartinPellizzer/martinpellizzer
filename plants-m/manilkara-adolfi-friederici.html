Manilkara adolfi-friederici

Manilkara adolfi-friederici, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and produces clusters of woody pods containing the edible Brazil nuts. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall and has a dense, buttressed trunk. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, often arranged in panicles, and it relies on specific pollinators for reproduction.

The plant plays a vital ecological role in the rainforest, supporting diverse wildlife and contributing to the ecosystem's biodiversity.
