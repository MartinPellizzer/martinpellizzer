Mimulus linearis var. lutea

Mimulus linearis var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow monkeyflower, is a perennial herb native to western North America.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking yellow-orange flowers with two lips, typical of the monkeyflower genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in stream banks and woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
