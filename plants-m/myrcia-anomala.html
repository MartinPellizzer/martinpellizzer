Myrcia anomala

Myrcia anomala, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slightly waxy texture. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences and bloom during the summer months.

The fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when mature and contains several seeds.
