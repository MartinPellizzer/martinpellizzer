Macrotyloma biflorum var. appressepuberulum

Macrotyloma biflorum var. appressepuberulum, commonly known as the small-flowered cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are used as a food source.

It is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.
