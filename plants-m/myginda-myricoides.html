Myginda myricoides

Myginda myricoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf myrtle, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Myricaceae family, which includes species commonly referred to as myrtles. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in wet, acidic soils and is often found in swamps or along stream banks.

The plant has been used historically for its aromatic properties and in traditional medicine.
