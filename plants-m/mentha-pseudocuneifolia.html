Mentha pseudocuneifolia

Mentha pseudocuneifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a serrated margin and emits a strong, minty fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Mentha pseudocuneifolia is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
