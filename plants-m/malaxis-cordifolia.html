Malaxis cordifolia

Malaxis cordifolia, commonly known as the heart-leaved malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, erect stems with elliptical, heart-shaped leaves that are often dark green in color. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in forest understories.

Malaxis cordifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
