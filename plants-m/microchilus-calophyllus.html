Microchilus calophyllus

Microchilus calophyllus, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, often covered in a waxy coating to retain moisture.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
