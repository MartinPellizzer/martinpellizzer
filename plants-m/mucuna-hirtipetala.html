Mucuna hirtipetala

Mucuna hirtipetala, commonly known as the hairy velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features compound leaves with three leaflets, and its flowers are typically pale green to yellowish in color. The plant produces small, spherical pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Mucuna hirtipetala is notable for its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural systems.

Its hairy stems and leaves contribute to its distinctive appearance and ecological role.
