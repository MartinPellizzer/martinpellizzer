Menziesia ciliicalyx var. purpurea

Menziesia ciliicalyx var. purpurea, commonly known as the purpleleaf menziesia, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are typically purple-tinged on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in late spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its botanical features include hairy stems and elliptical, serrated leaves.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
