Marsdenia rubicunda

Marsdenia rubicunda, commonly known as the red-flowered Marsdenia, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to red in color, which bloom during the summer months. Its fruits are follicles that contain seeds covered in a sticky, fleshy coating.

Marsdenia rubicunda is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
