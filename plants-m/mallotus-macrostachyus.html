Mallotus macrostachyus

Mallotus macrostachyus, commonly known as the giant hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter, with five broad, waxy petals. The plant has compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
