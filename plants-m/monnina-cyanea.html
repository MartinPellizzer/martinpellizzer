Monnina cyanea

Monnina cyanea, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking blue-violet flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Monnina cyanea is valued in gardens for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators.
