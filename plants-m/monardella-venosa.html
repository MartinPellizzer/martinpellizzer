Monardella venosa

Monardella venosa, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its aromatic foliage.

Monardella venosa is also a popular choice for native gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
