Miconia myrtiformis

Miconia myrtiformis, commonly known as the tropical beautyberry, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright blue berries that are edible for some bird species but toxic to humans. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Miconia myrtiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
