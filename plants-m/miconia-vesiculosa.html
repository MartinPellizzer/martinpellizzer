Miconia vesiculosa

Miconia vesiculosa, commonly known as the blister miconia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are typically borne on long, drooping inflorescences. Miconia vesiculosa is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments, often found in lowland rainforests.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in some areas due to its rapid growth and ability to outcompete native vegetation.
