Mucuna neocaledonica

Mucuna neocaledonica, commonly known as the Calypso bean, is a climbing legume native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, showy flowers that range from white to purple. The plant produces elongated, fibrous pods containing several elliptical seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Mucuna neocaledonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
