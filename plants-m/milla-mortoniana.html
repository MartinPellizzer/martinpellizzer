Milla mortoniana

Milla mortoniana, commonly known as the Morton's milla, is a rare and lesser-known plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests. Its botanical features include a short, creeping stem and a unique arrangement of leaves that aid in photosynthesis.

Due to its limited habitat and endangered status, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
