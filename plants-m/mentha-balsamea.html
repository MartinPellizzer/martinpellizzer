Mentha balsamea

Mentha balsamea, commonly known as wild mint, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a fragrant, minty aroma. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Mentha balsamea is often used in herbal remedies and is valued for its aromatic properties.
