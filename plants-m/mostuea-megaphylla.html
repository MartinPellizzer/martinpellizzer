Mostuea megaphylla

Mostuea megaphylla, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible tubers that are similar in texture to yams. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and potential agricultural value.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil for optimal growth.
