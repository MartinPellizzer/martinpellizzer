Myriostachya longispicula

Myriostachya longispicula, commonly known as the long-spined panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, spike-like inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its flowering period occurs during the dry season, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its adaptability, it is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is considered an important component of local ecosystems.
