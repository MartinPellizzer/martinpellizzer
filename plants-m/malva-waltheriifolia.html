Malva waltheriifolia

Malva waltheriifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf mallow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its fibrous roots and mucilaginous leaves make it a valuable plant in traditional medicine and ecological restoration.
