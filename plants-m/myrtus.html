Myrtus

Myrtus, commonly known as myrtle, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia, with several species commonly used in horticulture. The plant is characterized by its aromatic evergreen leaves and small, fragrant white or pink flowers. Myrtus species often produce edible berries, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant's essential oils, derived from its leaves and flowers, are valued for their fragrance and therapeutic properties.
