Mitracarpus brachystigma

Mitracarpus brachystigma, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mitracarpus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its small, daisy-like flowers are usually yellow or orange, and it produces distinctive, ribbed fruits known as schizocarps.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
