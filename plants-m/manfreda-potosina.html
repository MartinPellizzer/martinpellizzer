Manfreda potosina

Manfreda potosina, commonly known as the Potosi manfreda, is a striking member of the Asparagaceae family native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting to its harsh desert environment. The plant produces tall, spire-like inflorescences with vibrant orange to red flowers, which attract pollinators like bees and birds. Its succulent nature makes it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and arid landscapes.
