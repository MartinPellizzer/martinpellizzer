Mimulus minusculus

Mimulus minusculus, commonly known as the small monkeyflower, is a delicate annual plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Mimulus minusculus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
