Mapania amplivaginata

Mapania amplivaginata, commonly known as the broad-vessel mapania, is a rare and specialized plant species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves that often exhibit a unique venation pattern, which aids in efficient water transport. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, and its fruits are capsule-like structures containing multiple seeds. Due to its limited geographic range and specific habitat requirements, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.

Botanically, it is of interest for its morphological adaptations to high humidity and nutrient-poor soils.
