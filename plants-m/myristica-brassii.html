Myristica brassii

Myristica brassii, commonly known as the African nutmeg, is a member of the Myristicaceae family and is native to tropical Africa.

It is a small evergreen tree that produces small, fragrant flowers and oval-shaped fruits. The fruit contains a single seed, which is encased in a hard, aromatic outer layer. The plant is valued for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
