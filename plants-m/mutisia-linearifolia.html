Mutisia linearifolia

Mutisia linearifolia, commonly known as the linear-leaved mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina, Chile, and Peru.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is known for its narrow, linear leaves that give it its common name. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Mutisia linearifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in arid environments.
