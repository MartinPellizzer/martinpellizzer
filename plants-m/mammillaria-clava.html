Mammillaria clava

Mammillaria clava, commonly known as the clustered milk barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a dense covering of white to pale green tubercles. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 centimeters and features short, spiny ribs. It produces yellow flowers in late spring or early summer.

Mammillaria clava is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
