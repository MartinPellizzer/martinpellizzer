Monotoca lineata var. linifolia

Monotoca lineata var. linifolia, commonly known as the slender leaf tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in the spring. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in poor soil conditions and withstand drought. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils along stream banks and in desert washes.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various desert wildlife species.
