Myrtus oaxacana

Myrtus oaxacana, commonly known as Oaxacan myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the highlands of Oaxaca, Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, lance-shaped leaves and small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Myrtus oaxacana is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in local medicine.
