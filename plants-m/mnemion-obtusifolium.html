Mnemion obtusifolium

Mnemion obtusifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf knapweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal rosettes of broad, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, which are surrounded by pale green bracts. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Mnemion obtusifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
