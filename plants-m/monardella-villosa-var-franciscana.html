Monardella villosa var. franciscana

Monardella villosa var. franciscana, commonly known as Franciscan monardella, is a perennial herb native to the coastal regions of California.

It features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a fuzzy texture. The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, often found in coastal scrub habitats.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance to local pollinators.
