Mesembryanthemum longipapillatum

Mesembryanthemum longipapillatum, commonly known as the long-papillate ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves covered in fine, hair-like papillae, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to store water in its thick leaves allows it to survive in dry conditions with minimal care.
