Machaerium surinamense

Machaerium surinamense, commonly known as the Surinam grass, is a tropical perennial grass native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its leguminous plants, and it typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant has slender, erect stems and small, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in spicate inflorescences, and it produces legume pods that contain several seeds.

Machaerium surinamense is sometimes used in landscaping due to its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid climates.
