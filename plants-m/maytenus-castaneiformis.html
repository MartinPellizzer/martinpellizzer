Maytenus castaneiformis

Maytenus castaneiformis, commonly known as the chestnut-fig tree, is a member of the family Celastraceae and is native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape with a leathery texture. The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
