Melicope irifica

Melicope irifica, commonly known as the irifa melicope, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to Madagascar.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in tropical moist forests. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, with four petals, and it produces small, round fruits that are yellow when mature.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
