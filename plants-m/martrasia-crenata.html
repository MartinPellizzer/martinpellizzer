Martrasia crenata

Martrasia crenata, commonly known as the crested martrasia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wavy-edged leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil in full sun. Its ornamental value comes from its unique foliage and delicate blooms, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Martrasia crenata is relatively easy to care for, requiring minimal water and regular pruning to maintain its shape.
