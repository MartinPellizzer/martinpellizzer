Myoseris purpurea

Myoseris purpurea, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its showy purple flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Myoseris purpurea is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant color and ease of cultivation.
