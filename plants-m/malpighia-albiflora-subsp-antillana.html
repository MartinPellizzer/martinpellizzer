Malpighia albiflora subsp. antillana

Malpighia albiflora subsp.

antillana, commonly known as the white-flowered acerola, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America. It produces small, bright red berries that are rich in vitamin C and other antioxidants.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

The flowers are white and clustered, adding to its ornamental value.
