Mentha × alandica

Mentha × alandica, commonly known as Aland mint, is a hybrid mint species native to Finland.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated edge. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers arranged in whorled clusters, typical of the Lamiaceae family. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near streams or in wet meadows.

Mentha × alandica is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
