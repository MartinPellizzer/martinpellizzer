Mitrephora maingayi

Mitrephora maingayi, commonly known as the yellow mitrephora, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a drupe, often containing a single seed surrounded by a fleshy, aromatic pulp.
