Mesosetum molle

Mesosetum molle, commonly known as slender bluestem, is a warm-season grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Mesosetum molle is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
