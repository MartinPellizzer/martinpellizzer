Myroxylon ellipticum var. panamense

Myroxylon ellipticum var. panamense, commonly known as the Panama bulletwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic qualities. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain flat, winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.
