Manilkara napali

Manilkara napali, commonly known as Napali champa, is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces a fruit with a thick, inedible outer layer and a sweet, edible pulp inside. Its latex, similar to that of the sapodilla, has been used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
