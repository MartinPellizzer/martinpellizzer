Melaleuca pilosa

Melaleuca pilosa, commonly known as the woolly bottlebrush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and bright red or pink flower spikes that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea tree species. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a soft, woolly appearance.

Melaleuca pilosa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
