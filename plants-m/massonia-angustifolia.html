Massonia angustifolia

Massonia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf massonia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent species characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, white or pink flowers during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Massonia angustifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
