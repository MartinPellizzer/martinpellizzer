Monarda brevis

Monarda brevis, commonly known as narrowleaf horsemint, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are tubular and clustered in whorls, usually appearing in shades of purple or pink.

The leaves and flowers are often used in herbal teas due to their mild minty aroma.
