Morinda oligocephala

Morinda oligocephala, commonly known as the red morinda, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy, red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas or disturbed soils.

The berries are a source of antioxidants and have been used for their purported health benefits in various cultures.
