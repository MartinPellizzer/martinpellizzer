Miconia punctata

Miconia punctata, commonly known as the spotted miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in small, raised spots, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Miconia punctata is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

It is also noted for its potential invasiveness in some regions outside its native range.
