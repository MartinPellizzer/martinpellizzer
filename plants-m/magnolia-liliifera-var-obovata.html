Magnolia liliifera var. obovata

Magnolia liliifera var. obovata, commonly known as the Persian magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are broad, ovate, and often have a waxy texture, providing a striking visual contrast to the flowers. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of climates.
