Miconia idiogena

Miconia idiogena, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Miconia* tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a large shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pink flowers that are often pollinated by insects. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains numerous seeds.

Miconia idiogena is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some areas.
