Melochia venosa var. typica

Melochia venosa var. typica, commonly known as the velvetleaf or melochia, is a tropical plant characterized by its distinctive velvety, pubescent leaves.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous annual or perennial, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a waxy surface and often have a bluish or purplish hue.

The flowers are small, clustered, and usually yellow or greenish, producing capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.
