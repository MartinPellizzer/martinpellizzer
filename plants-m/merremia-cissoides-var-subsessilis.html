Merremia cissoides var. subsessilis

Merremia cissoides var. subsessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved morning glory, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale yellow to orange in color. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found climbing trees or shrubs in its natural habitat. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, bean-like seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
