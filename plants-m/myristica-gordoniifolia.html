Myristica gordoniifolia

Myristica gordoniifolia, commonly known as the Malayan nutmeg tree, is a member of the Myristicaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen canopy, which consists of large, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are followed by distinctive three-lobed fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its seeds are valued for their essential oils, which are used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and montane rainforests.
