Mandevilla angustissima

Mandevilla angustissima, commonly known as the narrowleaf mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces showy blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and slightly pointed, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Mandevilla angustissima is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and as a houseplant due to its ornamental value.
