Malapoenna glaberrima

Malapoenna glaberrima, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has a rugged, gnarled trunk and thick, leathery leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

Malapoenna glaberrima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to provide shade in landscaping.
