Melastoma martiusianum

Melastoma martiusianum, commonly known as the red-flowered melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, often red or pink bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, with opposite, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green. It produces small, white or yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by berries that contain several seeds.

Melastoma martiusianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
