Musa liukiuensis

Musa liukiuensis, commonly known as the Ryukyu banana, is a rare and endemic plant species native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces edible, banana-like fruits that are typically smaller and more elongated compared to common bananas. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.

Its botanical features make it an important subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
