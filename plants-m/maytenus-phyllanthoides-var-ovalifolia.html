Maytenus phyllanthoides var. ovalifolia

Maytenus phyllanthoides var. ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf mayten, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical or ovate in shape, with a glossy, dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
