Martia opifera

Martia opifera, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, warm environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Martia opifera is also known for its role in traditional medicine and as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
