Mammillaria canescens

Mammillaria canescens, commonly known as the woolly mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, white woolly covering that covers the stems and flowers, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in early spring.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens due to its compact size and ornamental value.
