Mimosa cordistipula var. pubescens

Mimosa cordistipula var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy mimosa, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves that are pinnately arranged and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellow, spherical flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained sandy or loamy soils.

It is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
