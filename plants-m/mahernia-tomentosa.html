Mahernia tomentosa

Mahernia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly mahernia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, white tomentose (fuzzy) covering on its stems and leaves, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in dry, rocky environments and is often found in coastal regions.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
