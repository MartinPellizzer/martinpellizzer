Melaleuca pauperiflora subsp. pauperiflora

Melaleuca pauperiflora subsp.

pauperiflora, commonly known as the slender bottlebrush, is a shrub native to the northern regions of Queensland, Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, cylindrical stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The leaves are typically narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and sandy environments.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and its ability to withstand salt spray and wind.
