Masdevallia allenii

Masdevallia allenii, commonly known as the Allen's masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, often colorful flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant typically features long, narrow leaves and produces pendulous inflorescences with showy, asymmetrical blooms. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique and delicate appearance.
