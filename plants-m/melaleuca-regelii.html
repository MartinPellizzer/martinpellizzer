Melaleuca regelii

Melaleuca regelii, commonly known as the Regel's paperbark, is a small evergreen shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a pale, fibrous inner layer. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a glossy upper surface and a pale underside.

Melaleuca regelii is often found in coastal heathlands and is adapted to well-drained, sandy soils.
