Micranthella rosea

Micranthella rosea, commonly known as the roseleaf micranthus, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features delicate, rose-like flowers with pink to purple petals that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are densely arranged along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
