Miconia wilsonii

Miconia wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, showy, violet-blue bracts that resemble petals. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and prominent veins. It produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by berry-like fruits.

Miconia wilsonii is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and along stream banks.
