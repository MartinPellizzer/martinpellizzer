Micambe ornithopodioides

Micambe ornithopodioides, commonly known as the African orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, yellow, and green. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Micambe ornithopodioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
