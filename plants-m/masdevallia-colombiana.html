Masdevallia colombiana

Masdevallia colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and its intricate, often colorful flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in cloud forests at high altitudes. Its leaves are usually elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces a single, showy inflorescence with multiple flowers.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
