Manihot purpurea

Manihot purpurea, commonly known as the purple arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking purple foliage and edible tubers that are rich in starch. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The tubers can be processed into flour and are used in various culinary applications.
