Mespilus insegnae

Mespilus insegnae, commonly known as the Sierra Madre persimmon, is a rare and endemic species native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible but not widely cultivated. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated margin.
