Malesherbia bracteata var. campanulata

Malesherbia bracteata var. campanulata, commonly known as the bellflower malesherbia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shrubland or open woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and branched.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
