Micrococca oligandra var. pubescens

Micrococca oligandra var. pubescens, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are opposite, elliptical, and covered in fine pubescence, giving the plant its varietal name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for warm, arid climates.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
