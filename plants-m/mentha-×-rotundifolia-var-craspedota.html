Mentha × rotundifolia var. craspedota

Mentha × rotundifolia var. craspedota, commonly known as round-leaf mint, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its rounded, broad leaves that are often slightly toothed or lobed. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorled clusters. It is native to Europe and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves are used in culinary and medicinal applications, similar to other mint species.
