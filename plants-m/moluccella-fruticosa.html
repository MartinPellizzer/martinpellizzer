Moluccella fruticosa

Moluccella fruticosa, commonly known as the shrub mallow, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, woody stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are ovate and slightly hairy, with a rough texture.

Moluccella fruticosa is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
