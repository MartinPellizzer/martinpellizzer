Melastoma bicolor

Melastoma bicolor, commonly known as the red-and-white melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. Its flowers are distinctive, with two contrasting colors—red on the outside and white on the inside—arranged in clusters.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.
