Mussaenda rehderiana

Mussaenda rehderiana, commonly known as the Chinese flame bush, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble flames, which are clustered at the ends of its branches. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive, maple-like appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Mussaenda rehderiana prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
