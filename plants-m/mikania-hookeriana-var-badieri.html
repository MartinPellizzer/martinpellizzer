Mikania hookeriana var. badieri

Mikania hookeriana var. badieri, commonly known as the creeping mikania, is a perennial vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems that can grow along the ground or climb supports. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a distinctive pubescent surface.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks.
