Macrorhynchus laciniatus

Macrorhynchus laciniatus, commonly known as the fringed gentian, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers with distinctive fringed petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a serrated margin, while its stems are often branched and hairy.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
