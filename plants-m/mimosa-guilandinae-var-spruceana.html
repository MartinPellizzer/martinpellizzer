Mimosa guilandinae var. spruceana

Mimosa guilandinae var. spruceana, commonly known as the Spruce's mimosa, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and showy, yellow, spherical flower heads. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen make it an important pioneer species in ecological succession.
