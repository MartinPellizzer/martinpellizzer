Monotropastrum pubescens

Monotropastrum pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It possesses small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are often clustered together. Its berries are typically red or orange, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is native to parts of Africa, the Middle East, and southern Europe, where it thrives in woodland environments.
