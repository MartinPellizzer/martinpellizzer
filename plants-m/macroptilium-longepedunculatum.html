Macroptilium longepedunculatum

Macroptilium longepedunculatum, commonly known as the long pedunculate burr medic, is a perennial legume native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and trifoliate leaves with oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, elongated clusters, which are followed by hairy, spiny pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment and forage in agricultural settings.
