Mammillaria diguetii

Mammillaria diguetii, commonly known as the Desert Cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
