Monotropa uniflora var. tripetala

Monotropa uniflora var. tripetala, commonly known as the tripetal ghostplant, is a member of the Aristolochiaceae family.

It is a hemiparasitic plant that relies on the roots of other plants for nutrients, while still performing photosynthesis. The plant is characterized by its white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate and typically have three lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often found in shaded, moist forest understories across eastern North America.
