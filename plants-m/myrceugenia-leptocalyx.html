Myrceugenia leptocalyx

Myrceugenia leptocalyx, commonly known as the slender-leaved myrtle, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters.

It is often found in humid, tropical forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
