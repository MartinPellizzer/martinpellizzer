Malaxis termensis var. termensis

Malaxis termensis var. termensis, commonly known as the Termens orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Africa, particularly South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or rocky outcrops. Its rhizomes are fibrous, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous minute seeds.

This species is notable for its unique floral structure and is often found in specific microhabitats within its native range.
