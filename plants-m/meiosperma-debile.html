Meiosperma debile

Meiosperma debile, commonly known as the slender burroweed, is a low-growing annual plant native to the western United States.

It has small, pale green leaves and produces fluffy, white to tan seeds that are adapted for wind dispersal. The plant typically thrives in sandy or disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its life cycle is short, with rapid growth and seed production to ensure survival in harsh conditions.

Botanically, it is characterized by its simple, branching structure and its ability to tolerate drought.
