Mundia glauca

Mundia glauca, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Mundia glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
