Myristica insularis

Myristica insularis, commonly known as the island nutmeg, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody fruits that contain both nutmeg and mace, two highly valued spice products. The plant has compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that are pollinated by insects. Its growth is slow, and it can reach heights of up to 15 meters in its native habitat.

The tree is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its fragrant foliage and unique fruiting structure.
