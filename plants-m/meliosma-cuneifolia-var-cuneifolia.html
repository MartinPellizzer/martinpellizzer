Meliosma cuneifolia var. cuneifolia

Meliosma cuneifolia var. cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf meliosma, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring slender, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, dark green upper surface and a lighter, hairy underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in late spring, which are clustered in cymes. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their astringent properties.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native habitats.
