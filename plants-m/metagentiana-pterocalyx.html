Metagentiana pterocalyx

Metagentiana pterocalyx, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, flame-colored flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and it produces small, woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
