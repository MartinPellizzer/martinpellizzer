Myristica lasiocarpa

Myristica lasiocarpa, commonly known as the hairy nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The plant produces a unique fruit with a thick, spiky rind that encases a single seed. The seed contains a valuable oil used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its aromatic properties.
