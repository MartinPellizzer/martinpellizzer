Mikania glomerata

Mikania glomerata, commonly known as "butterfly vine," is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and trailing habit. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to cover trellises or fences.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
