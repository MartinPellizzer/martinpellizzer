Mertensia bakeri

Mertensia bakeri, commonly known as Baker's bluebells, is a flowering plant native to the Appalachian Mountains of the United States.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of pale blue, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a smooth, waxy texture.

Mertensia bakeri is a perennial herb that plays an important role in its native ecosystem.
