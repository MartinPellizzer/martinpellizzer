Monarda punctata var. lasiodonta

Monarda punctata var. lasiodonta, commonly known as the spotted beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, pinkish-purple tubular flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and butterflies. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, and its stems are square and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in native gardens.
