Malpighia samanensis

Malpighia samanensis, commonly known as the Saman tree or angel's trumpets, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant is a small to medium-sized tree with a dense canopy and produces edible berries that are rich in vitamin C. Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
