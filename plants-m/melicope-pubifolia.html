Melicope pubifolia

Melicope pubifolia, commonly known as the hairy melicope, is a small evergreen tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its smooth, often hairy bark and opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal forests.

Melicope pubifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some island cultures.
