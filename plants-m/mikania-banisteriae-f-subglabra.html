Mikania banisteriae f. subglabra

Mikania banisteriae f. subglabra, commonly known as the smoothleaf Mikania, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves that are smooth and glabrous on both surfaces. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it can become a problematic weed in certain ecosystems.
