Macroplectrum gladiifolium

Macroplectrum gladiifolium, commonly known as the swordleaf spurge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as swamps and wetlands. The plant is characterized by its elongated, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in cup-like structures called cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The plant contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans if ingested.
