Mimosa tanalarum

Mimosa tanalarum, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant produces fluffy, white seed pods that disperse its seeds by wind, aiding in its spread along sandy shores. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dune ecosystems.

Mimosa tanalarum is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to stabilize coastal soils.
