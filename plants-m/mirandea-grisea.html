Mirandea grisea

Mirandea grisea, commonly known as the gray mirandea, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grayish stems and small, delicate leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or pale yellow, which bloom during the short growing season in its alpine habitat. Mirandea grisea is adapted to cold, nutrient-poor soils and thrives in areas with high UV exposure.

Due to its limited distribution and fragile ecosystem, it is considered a vulnerable species facing threats from climate change and habitat loss.
