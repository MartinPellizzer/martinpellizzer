Medicago × komarovii var. cretacea

Medicago × komarovii var. cretacea, commonly known as the Cretan clover, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

Its adaptability to harsh environments makes it a valuable species in ecological restoration projects.
