Momordica microphylla

Momordica microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaf bitter gourd, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and pumpkins. The plant produces small, bitter green fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with five petals.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
