Machaerium tolimense

Machaerium tolimense, commonly known as the Tolima machaerium, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume pods and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being elliptical in shape, and it produces small, yellowish-white flowers.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
