Microlepia ciliata

Microlepia ciliata, commonly known as the hairy maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The fronds are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, with a slender, arching appearance. The sori, which are clusters of sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds and are covered by a protective indusium.

This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
