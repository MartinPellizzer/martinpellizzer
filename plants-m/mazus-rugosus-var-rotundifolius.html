Mazus rugosus var. rotundifolius

Mazus rugosus var. rotundifolius, commonly known as the round-leaved mazus, is a low-growing perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features small, ovate to round leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly succulent texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and ground covers.
