Misodendrum quadriflorum

Misodendrum quadriflorum, commonly known as the four-flowered misodendrum, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive four-flowered inflorescence, which gives it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite and often have a slightly serrated edge.

The flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a pale green or white color.
