Monotaxis gracilis

Monotaxis gracilis, commonly known as the slender glassworts, is a halophytic plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It thrives in saline environments such as salt marshes and sandy shores, demonstrating remarkable adaptability to high salt concentrations. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that store water and reduce water loss, a key adaptation to its arid habitat. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences, and it reproduces primarily through seeds.

Monotaxis gracilis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various small invertebrates.
