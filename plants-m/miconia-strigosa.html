Miconia strigosa

Miconia strigosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are densely covered with short, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Miconia strigosa is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive in certain regions.

Its botanical features make it an important subject in studies of tropical flora and ecological dynamics.
