Manilkara jaimiqui subsp. emarginata

Manilkara jaimiqui subsp.

emarginata, commonly known as the Margined Sapote, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America. It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and yellowish flowers.

The tree produces a spherical fruit with a thick, inedible outer pulp and a sweet, edible seed inside. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.

The subspecies is distinguished by its emarginate (notched) leaf base, which sets it apart from other Manilkara species.
