Madhuca sandakanensis

Madhuca sandakanensis, commonly known as the Philippine mahogany or sandakan mahogany, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant yellow flowers. The tree produces hard, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. It typically grows in lowland rainforests and is often found in secondary forests.

Due to habitat loss and overexploitation, it is considered a threatened species.
