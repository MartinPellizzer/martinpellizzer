Mimusops elengi var. parvifolia

Mimusops elengi var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often clustered at the ends of branches. The plant produces small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange or red drupes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of timber.
