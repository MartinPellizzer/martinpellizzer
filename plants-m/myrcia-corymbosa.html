Myrcia corymbosa

Myrcia corymbosa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Myrcia corymbosa is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
