Myrcia innovans

Myrcia innovans, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and glossy, dark green leaves. It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments.

Myrcia innovans is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
