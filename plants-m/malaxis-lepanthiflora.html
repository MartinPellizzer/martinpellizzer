Malaxis lepanthiflora

Malaxis lepanthiflora, commonly known as the showy malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption in poor soil conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
