Myristica trobogarii

Myristica trobogarii, commonly known as the Borneo nutmeg, is a member of the Myristicaceae family and is native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It is a small evergreen tree that produces distinctive red fruits containing aromatic seeds. The plant is closely related to the spice nutmeg (Myristica fragrans) and shares similar botanical characteristics. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are unisexual and arranged in panicles.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and montane forests.
