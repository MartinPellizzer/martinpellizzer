Molina juncea

Molina juncea, commonly known as the common flax, is an annual herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Linaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences. Its fibrous stalks are valued for their strong, flexible fibers used in textile production.

Molina juncea thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for both its industrial and ornamental uses.
