Malvastrum phyllanthos

Malvastrum phyllanthos, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false mallow, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Malvastrum phyllanthos is known for its adaptability and can be invasive in certain regions.
