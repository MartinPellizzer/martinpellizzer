Maranta amabilis

Maranta amabilis, commonly known as the velvetleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage that often displays shades of red, purple, and green. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family, which includes other ornamental plants known for their vibrant leaves. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense clumps.

Maranta amabilis is often cultivated as a decorative plant for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
