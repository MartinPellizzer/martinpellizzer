Miconia dunstervillei

Miconia dunstervillei, commonly known as the Guiana miconia, is a striking tropical shrub or small tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter, often with a velvety texture and dark green coloration. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that are highly fragrant, attracting a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains numerous small seeds, facilitating its dispersal by birds and other animals.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
