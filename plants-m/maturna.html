Maturna

Maturna, commonly known as the African pear, is a small deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, green fruits that turn yellow when ripe. Maturna is valued for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for wildlife in its native region.
