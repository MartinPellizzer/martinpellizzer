Merostachys speciosa

Merostachys speciosa, commonly known as the showy grass, is a striking ornamental grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features tall, slender culms that can reach up to 6 feet in height, adorned with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often a deep green color. The plant produces fluffy, silver-white panicles in late summer, adding visual interest to gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Merostachys speciosa is valued for its architectural form and seasonal display, making it a popular choice for landscape design.
