Malapoenna japonica

Malapoenna japonica, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Malapoenna japonica is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
