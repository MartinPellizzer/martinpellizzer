Mentha glauca var. parvifolia

Mentha glauca var. parvifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved blue mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a bluish-green color. The plant produces pale purple to white flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

This variety is valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
