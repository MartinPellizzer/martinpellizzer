Miconia lechleri

Miconia lechleri, commonly known as the rubber tree or copaiba, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a large shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its dark green, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. Its latex sap is highly valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally by indigenous peoples.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
