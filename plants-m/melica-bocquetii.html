Melica bocquetii

Melica bocquetii, commonly known as Bocquet's brome, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climates. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its resilience to extreme temperatures.

Botanically, it is classified within the Poaceae family, contributing to the diversity of grass species in its native habitat.
