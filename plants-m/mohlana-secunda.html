Mohlana secunda

Mohlana secunda, commonly known as the red-stemmed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features slender, upright stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Mohlana secunda is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it a hardy species in its native habitat.
