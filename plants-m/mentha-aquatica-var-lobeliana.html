Mentha aquatica var. lobeliana

Mentha aquatica var. lobeliana, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves that are often submerged or floating on water surfaces. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in spikes during the summer months. It thrives in moist or aquatic environments, such as ponds, marshes, and stream banks.

Its leaves are used in herbal teas and have been historically valued for their medicinal properties.
