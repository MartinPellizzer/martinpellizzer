Menadena

Menadena, commonly known as the red-veined sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves with striking red veins and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often grown as an ornamental due to its vibrant foliage and is also used in traditional medicine. Menadena thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical name, Menadena, is derived from the Greek words "men" (meaning "moon") and "adena" (meaning "to bind"), possibly referring to its flowering cycle.
