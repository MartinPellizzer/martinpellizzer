Mentha glomerata var. aurantiaca

Mentha glomerata var. aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange catmint, is a cultivar of the catmint plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its bright orange to reddish purple flower clusters, which appear in late summer. The leaves are typically gray-green and have a strong, minty fragrance. This variety is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a versatile addition to many landscapes.
