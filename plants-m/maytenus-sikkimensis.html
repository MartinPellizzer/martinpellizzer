Maytenus sikkimensis

Maytenus sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim mayten, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayan regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by fleshy, orange-red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments at moderate altitudes and is often found in forest understories.

Maytenus sikkimensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
