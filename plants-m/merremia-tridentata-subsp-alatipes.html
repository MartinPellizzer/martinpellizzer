Merremia tridentata subsp. alatipes

Merremia tridentata subsp.

alatipes, commonly known as the winged morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical and subtropical regions. It features heart-shaped leaves with three prominent lobes and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange.

The plant is characterized by its distinctive winged veins on the leaves, which give it its subspecific name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found climbing over shrubs or trees.

The flowers are typically pollinated by insects, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and ornamental value.
