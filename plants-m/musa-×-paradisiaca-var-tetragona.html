Musa × paradisiaca var. tetragona

Musa × paradisiaca var. tetragona, commonly known as the burried plantain, is a tropical plant belonging to the Musaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and produces clusters of bananas that are typically smaller than those of common banana varieties. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its edible fruit, which has a sweet and slightly starchy flavor.

The variety is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
