Malus cerasifera macrocarpa

Malus cerasifera macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited crabapple, is a deciduous tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy white or pink flowers in spring. The tree produces large, edible fruits that are often used in jams and beverages. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and turn yellow in autumn, adding to its seasonal appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its aesthetic and culinary contributions.
