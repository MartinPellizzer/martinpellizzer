Medinilla crassifolia var. diaphana

Medinilla crassifolia var. diaphana, commonly known as the silver medinilla, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for indoor cultivation or as a container plant.

The varietal name 'diaphana' refers to its translucent or glassy appearance of the leaves under certain lighting conditions.
