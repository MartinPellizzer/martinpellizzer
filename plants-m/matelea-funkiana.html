Matelea funkiana

Matelea funkiana, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant is known for its fragrant blooms and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem.

The flowers close during the day, making it a nocturnal bloom, which is a notable botanical feature.
