Mallotus nepalensis

Mallotus nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal mallot, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal, Bhutan, and northern India.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
