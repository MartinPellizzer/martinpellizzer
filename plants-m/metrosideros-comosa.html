Metrosideros comosa

Metrosideros comosa, commonly known as the Christmas Bush, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its small, urn-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange, typically blooming during the summer months. The plant has slender, drooping branches and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden settings.

The species is also valued for its role in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for native wildlife.
