Macaranga lugubris

Macaranga lugubris, commonly known as the sad leaf tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and are also valued for their rapid growth and ability to provide shade.
