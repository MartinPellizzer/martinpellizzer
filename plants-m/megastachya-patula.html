Megastachya patula

Megastachya patula, commonly known as the narrowleaf false dragonhead, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
