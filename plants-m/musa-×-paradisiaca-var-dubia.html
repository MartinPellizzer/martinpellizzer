Musa × paradisiaca var. dubia

Musa × paradisiaca var. dubia, commonly known as the wild banana, is a less cultivated variant of the banana plant.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and clusters of small, green, inedible berries. This variety is native to Southeast Asia and is often found in tropical rainforests. The plant is known for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage.

It is also of interest to botanists for its genetic diversity and potential for breeding programs.
