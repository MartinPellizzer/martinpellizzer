Matisia exalata

Matisia exalata, commonly known as the yellow matisia, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lecythidaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, orange-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, which attract various pollinators. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.

The plant is also valued for its hard, durable wood and has been used in traditional medicine.
