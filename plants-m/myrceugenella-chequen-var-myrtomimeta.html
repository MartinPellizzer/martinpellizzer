Myrceugenella chequen var. myrtomimeta

Myrceugenella chequen var. myrtomimeta, commonly known as the mimetic myrtle, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white to pinkish flowers. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.
