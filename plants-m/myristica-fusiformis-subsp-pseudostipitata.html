Myristica fusiformis subsp. pseudostipitata

Myristica fusiformis subsp.

pseudostipitata, commonly known as the slender nutmeg. It is a species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Maluku Islands.

The plant is characterized by its elongated, spindle-shaped fruits that contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy surface.

The subspecies is distinguished by its shorter stipules compared to the typical form of Myristica fusiformis.
