Malcolmia biloba

Malcolmia biloba, commonly known as the Egyptian mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves and grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
