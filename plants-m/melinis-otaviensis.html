Melinis otaviensis

Melinis otaviensis, commonly known as the Otavi grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry conditions.

Melinis otaviensis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-maintenance settings.
