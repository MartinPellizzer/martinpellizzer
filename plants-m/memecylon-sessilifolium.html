Memecylon sessilifolium

Memecylon sessilifolium, commonly known as the sessile-leaf memecylon, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, compact foliage with small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually sessile, meaning they lack a petiole. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by round, fleshy berries that are often red or yellow when mature.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal in shaded garden settings.
