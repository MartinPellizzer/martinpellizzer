Madia exigua subsp. macrocephala

Madia exigua subsp.

macrocephala, commonly known as the giant madia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
