Momordica garriepensis

Momordica garriepensis, commonly known as the African bitter gourd, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourds and cucurbits. The plant produces elongated, warty fruits that are typically green when mature, though they may turn yellow or orange. Its leaves are heart-shaped with lobed margins, and it has tendrils that aid in climbing.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a vegetable in local cuisines.
