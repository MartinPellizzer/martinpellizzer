Musa banksii

Musa banksii, commonly known as the giant banana plant, is a large herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its towering, pseudostem structure composed of tightly packed leaf sheaths. The plant produces large, fleshy berries that are typically green when mature, though they can turn yellow or red depending on the variety. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
