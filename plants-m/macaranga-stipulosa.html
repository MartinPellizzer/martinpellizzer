Macaranga stipulosa

Macaranga stipulosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Indian rubber plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by oval, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Macaranga stipulosa is also known for its fast growth rate and ability to colonize disturbed areas.
