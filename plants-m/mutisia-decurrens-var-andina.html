Mutisia decurrens var. andina

Mutisia decurrens var. andina, commonly known as the Andean mutisia, is a perennial shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, thriving in cool and temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses among local communities.
