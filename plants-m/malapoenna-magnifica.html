Malapoenna magnifica

Malapoenna magnifica, commonly known as the flame tree, is a striking tropical tree native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual impact during the flowering season. The plant has compound leaves composed of multiple leaflets, which are typically alternate and ovate in shape. Its bark is smooth and often has a reddish hue, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Malapoenna magnifica is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a street tree due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in warm climates.
