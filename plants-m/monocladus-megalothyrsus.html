Monocladus megalothyrsus

Monocladus megalothyrsus, commonly known as the giant thornplant, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stem and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces elongated, spiny fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky substance. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
