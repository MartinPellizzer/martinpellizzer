Mammillaria elongata f. subcrocea

Mammillaria elongata f. subcrocea, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed hedgehog cactus, is a unique cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white, woolly spines. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing at the tips of the stems during the spring and summer months.

This variety is prized for its compact size and distinctive appearance, making it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
