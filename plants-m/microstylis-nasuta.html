Microstylis nasuta

Microstylis nasuta, commonly known as the narrow-styled grass, is a small perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in environments with high humidity and abundant moisture. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Microstylis nasuta is often used in ecological studies due to its role in wetland ecosystems and its sensitivity to environmental changes.
