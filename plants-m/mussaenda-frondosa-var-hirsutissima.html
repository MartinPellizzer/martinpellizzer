Mussaenda frondosa var. hirsutissima

Mussaenda frondosa var. hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow bracts that surround small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant has palmately lobed, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for tropical gardens and landscaping.
