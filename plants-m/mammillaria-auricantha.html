Mammillaria auricantha

Mammillaria auricantha, commonly known as the golden crown cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a distinctive yellowish-green body covered in white to pale green spines. The plant features short, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are reduced to tubercles, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus collections.
