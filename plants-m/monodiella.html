Monodiella

Monodiella, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plants typically produce vibrant blooms in shades of purple, pink, and white, often with contrasting veins. Monodiella species are popular as houseplants due to their ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.

They thrive in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.
