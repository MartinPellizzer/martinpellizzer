Microcos globulifera

Microcos globulifera, commonly known as the globe flower, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive spherical seed pods, which give the plant its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a rough texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring.

The botanical features of Microcos globulifera make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
