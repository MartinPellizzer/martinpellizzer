Mammillaria conopsea

Mammillaria conopsea, commonly known as the Mexican moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular plant with a thick, fleshy stem that is often covered in white, waxy tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.

Mammillaria conopsea is popular as a houseplant due to its compact size and attractive appearance.
