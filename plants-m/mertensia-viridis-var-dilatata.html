Mertensia viridis var. dilatata

Mertensia viridis var. dilatata, commonly known as the broadleaf bluebells, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptic in shape, with a bluish-green coloration on both surfaces. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue to violet, arranged in clusters at the base of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the genus Mertensia.
