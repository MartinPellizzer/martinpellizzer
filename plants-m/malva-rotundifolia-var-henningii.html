Malva rotundifolia var. henningii

Malva rotundifolia var. henningii, commonly known as Henning's mallow, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rounded, velvety leaves and showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers are composed of five petals and are surrounded by five sepals, which are often mistaken for additional petals.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
