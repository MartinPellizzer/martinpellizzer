Mesua glabra

Mesua glabra, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and striking white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Mesua glabra is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
