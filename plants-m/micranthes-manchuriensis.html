Micranthes manchuriensis

Micranthes manchuriensis, commonly known as the Korean mountain sandwort, is a perennial herb native to northeastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and mountainous regions. The plant features small, clustered flowers with four petals, often in shades of white or pale pink. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, arranged in a basal rosette.

Micranthes manchuriensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
