Myrciaria maschalantha

Myrciaria maschalantha, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by the edible fruits. Its leaves are elliptical, with a glossy surface and serrated edges.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and culinary value.
