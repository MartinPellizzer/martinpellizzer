Mentha suaveolens var. bullata

Mentha suaveolens var. bullata, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and slender, aromatic stems. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its essential oils contain menthol and other compounds, making it valuable in both traditional medicine and aromatherapy.
