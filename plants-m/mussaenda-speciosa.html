Mussaenda speciosa

Mussaenda speciosa, commonly known as the flame bush or golden flame, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of bright yellow bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces small, white, five-petaled flowers that are often overlooked beneath the showy bracts. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage and unique floral display.

Mussaenda speciosa is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
