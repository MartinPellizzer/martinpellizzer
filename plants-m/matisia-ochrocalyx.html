Matisia ochrocalyx

Matisia ochrocalyx, commonly known as the yellow-veined matisia, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves with prominent yellow veins, which give it its common name. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. Its bark is thick and often has a rough, fissured texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
