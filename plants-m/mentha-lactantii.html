Mentha lactantii

Mentha lactantii, commonly known as the milky mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Mentha lactantii is known for its aromatic, slightly minty scent and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

It is not commonly cultivated for commercial use but is of interest to botanists and herbalists.
