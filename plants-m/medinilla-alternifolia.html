Medinilla alternifolia

Medinilla alternifolia, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or velvetleaf, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red bracts that surround its small, white flowers. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy texture.

Medinilla alternifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
