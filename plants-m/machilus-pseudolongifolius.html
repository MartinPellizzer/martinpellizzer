Machilus pseudolongifolius

Machilus pseudolongifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf machilus, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate, and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, spherical berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation projects.
