Malva setigera

Malva setigera, commonly known as the narrowleaf mallows, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and clusters of pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other plants like cotton and hibiscus.

Malva setigera is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
