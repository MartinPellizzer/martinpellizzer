Microstachys fruticulosa

Microstachys fruticulosa, commonly known as the fragrant shrub, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces tiny, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in dense inflorescences. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in shaded forest understories.

The leaves are simple, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
