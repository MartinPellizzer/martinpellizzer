Marsdenia obovata

Marsdenia obovata, commonly known as the broad-ovate Marsdenia, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The leaves are typically ovate to broadly ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by follicles containing elliptical seeds.

This plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
