Mappa denticulata

Mappa denticulata, commonly known as the toothed map plant, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with distinctive toothed edges, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in a circular pattern, and are visited by various pollinators.

Mappa denticulata is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
