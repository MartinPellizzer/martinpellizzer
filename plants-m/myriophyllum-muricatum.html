Myriophyllum muricatum

Myriophyllum muricatum, commonly known as the spiked watermilfoil, is an aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown, which develop into tiny, dark seeds. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes.

Myriophyllum muricatum is often found in shallow waters with soft, muddy substrates.
