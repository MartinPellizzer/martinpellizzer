Macroptilium panduratum

Macroptilium panduratum, commonly known as the purple crown wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive purple flowers and feathery foliage. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
