Mahonia diversifolia

Mahonia diversifolia, commonly known as the Evergreen barberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are alternate and have spiny tips, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by blue-black berries that are edible for birds. Mahonia diversifolia thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Its hardy nature makes it suitable for a variety of soil types and climates.
