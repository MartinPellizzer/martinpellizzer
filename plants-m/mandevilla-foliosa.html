Mandevilla foliosa

Mandevilla foliosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves and clusters of trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant. It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is known for its vigorous growth habit.

Mandevilla foliosa is typically grown as a climbing or trailing plant, making it suitable for trellises, hanging baskets, or as a ground cover.
