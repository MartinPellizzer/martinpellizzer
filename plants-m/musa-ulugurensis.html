Musa ulugurensis

Musa ulugurensis, commonly known as the Uluguru banana, is a species native to the Uluguru Mountains in Tanzania.

It is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Musaceae family, characterized by its large, broad leaves and clustered inflorescences. The plant produces edible berries that are typically consumed fresh or used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
