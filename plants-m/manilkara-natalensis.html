Manilkara natalensis

Manilkara natalensis, commonly known as Natal sapote, is a tropical evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces a fleshy, orange-colored fruit with a sweet, custard-like pulp that is edible. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is used in both culinary and traditional medicinal applications.
