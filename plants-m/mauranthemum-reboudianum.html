Mauranthemum reboudianum

Mauranthemum reboudianum, commonly known as the Reboundian starflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small stars. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom in the spring and early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged in a basal rosette, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Mauranthemum reboudianum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
