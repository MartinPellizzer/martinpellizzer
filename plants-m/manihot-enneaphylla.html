Manihot enneaphylla

Manihot enneaphylla, commonly known as the arrowleaf tallowtree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Its roots are known for their high starch content, making it a valuable resource in some regions for food and industrial purposes.
