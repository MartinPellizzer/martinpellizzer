Myrcia tafelbergica

Myrcia tafelbergica, commonly known as the Tafelberg myrtle, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, white to pinkish flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Myrcia tafelbergica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
