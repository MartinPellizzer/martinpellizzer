Malaxis verticillata var. pubescens

Malaxis verticillata var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
