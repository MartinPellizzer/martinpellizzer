Mimosa montana

Mimosa montana, commonly known as the mountain mimosa, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched, a trait known as thigmonastic response. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, grayish seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Mimosa montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
