Myosotis bryonoma

Myosotis bryonoma, commonly known as the New Zealand forget-me-not, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It features delicate blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring, typically on slender, upright stems. The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins and streamside habitats.

Myosotis bryonoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native insect populations.
