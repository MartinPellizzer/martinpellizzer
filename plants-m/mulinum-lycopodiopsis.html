Mulinum lycopodiopsis

Mulinum lycopodiopsis, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, moss-like appearance with tiny, scale-like leaves arranged in whorls around the stem. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. It reproduces via spores rather than seeds, and its life cycle includes both a gametophyte and sporophyte stage.

Mulinum lycopodiopsis is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms.
