Malcolmia incrassata

Malcolmia incrassata, commonly known as the woolly blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, sky-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal areas and dry habitats due to its drought-resistant properties.

The flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
