Mahonia × savilliana

Mahonia × savilliana, commonly known as the golden smilacina, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its holly-like leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a yellow or orange margin. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by blue-black berries. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in partial shade.

Mahonia × savilliana is also noted for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
