Myristica hypargyraea var. gillespieana

Myristica hypargyraea var. gillespieana, commonly known as the golden nutmeg tree, is a member of the Myristicaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant is notable for its aromatic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, fertile soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
