Myrianthus

Myrianthus, commonly known as the flame tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, South America, and the Caribbean. The plant is characterized by its showy, often red or orange, clustered flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are typically palmately compound, with several leaflets radiating from a central point.

Myrianthus species are valued for their ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
