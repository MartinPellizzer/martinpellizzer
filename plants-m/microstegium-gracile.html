Microstegium gracile

Microstegium gracile, commonly known as slender wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purplish tinge on the underside. Its flowers are white to pale yellow and arranged in clusters, blooming from late spring to early summer.

Microstegium gracile is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments and is valued for its ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
