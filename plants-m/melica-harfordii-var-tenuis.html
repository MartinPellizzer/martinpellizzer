Melica harfordii var. tenuis

Melica harfordii var. tenuis, commonly known as narrowleaf basketgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in wet environments.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
