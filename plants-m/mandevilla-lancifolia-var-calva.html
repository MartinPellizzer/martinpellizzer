Mandevilla lancifolia var. calva

Mandevilla lancifolia var. calva, commonly known as the white mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant is a vigorous climber, often used in ornamental landscaping due to its showy blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its rapid growth and ability to produce abundant flowers make it a popular choice for trellises and garden structures.
