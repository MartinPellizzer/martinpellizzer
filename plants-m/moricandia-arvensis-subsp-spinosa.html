Moricandia arvensis subsp. spinosa

Moricandia arvensis subsp.

spinosa, commonly known as prickly malva, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and parts of Europe. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems covered in stiff, spinose hairs.

The plant produces showy pink to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a hairy texture.

It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.
