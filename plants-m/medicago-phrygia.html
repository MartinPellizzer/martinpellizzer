Medicago phrygia

Medicago phrygia, commonly known as Turkish clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters. Its root system is well-developed, allowing it to thrive in dry, sandy soils.

Medicago phrygia is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable for soil enrichment.
