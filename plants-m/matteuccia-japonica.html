Matteuccia japonica

Matteuccia japonica, commonly known as Japanese barrenwort, is a perennial fern native to East Asia.

It features distinctive, bipinnate leaves that are bright green and often have a slightly glossy appearance. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sorus, which are located on the underside of the fronds. Matteuccia japonica thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in cool, humid climates.
