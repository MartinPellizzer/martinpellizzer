Myrtus tenuifolia

Myrtus tenuifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, leathery leaves that are often fragrant and have a distinctive silver-gray hue. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers in spring, which are followed by dark blue, edible berries. It is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

Myrtus tenuifolia is often used in Mediterranean gardens for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
