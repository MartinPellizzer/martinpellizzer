Microseris alpestris

Microseris alpestris, commonly known as the alpine sow-thistle, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and finely divided leaves. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by fluffy seed heads that disperse easily.

Microseris alpestris is often used in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
