Mortonia effusa

Mortonia effusa, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features broad, waxy leaves that are typically silver or gray-green in color, providing a striking visual contrast. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a globose berry that ripens to a deep blue or black, often containing several seeds.

Mortonia effusa is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun.
