Mezierea

Mezierea, commonly known as the common thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, oval-shaped leaves and pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia and thrives in well-drained soils. Mezierea is widely used in herbal medicine and culinary applications due to its essential oils and antioxidant properties.

It is also valued in traditional landscaping for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
