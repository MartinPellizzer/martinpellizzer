Myrtus raran

Myrtus raran, commonly known as the fragrant myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, dark green leaves that are aromatic when crushed, releasing a subtle, sweet scent. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible berries that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Myrtus raran thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value and aromatic properties make it a popular choice for gardens and traditional herbal remedies.
