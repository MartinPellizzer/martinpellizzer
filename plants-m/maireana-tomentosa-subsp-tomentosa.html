Maireana tomentosa subsp. tomentosa

Maireana tomentosa subsp.

tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly maireana, is a shrub native to Australia. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, where it is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are usually small, narrow, and covered in a fine, white pubescence.

It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle ornamental value to the landscape.
