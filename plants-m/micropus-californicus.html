Micropus californicus

Micropus californicus, commonly known as the California milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open areas such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and the stems are erect and often branched.

It plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for various pollinators, including butterflies and bees.
