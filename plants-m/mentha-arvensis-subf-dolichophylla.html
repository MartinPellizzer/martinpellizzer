Mentha arvensis subf. dolichophylla

Mentha arvensis subf.

dolichophylla, commonly known as the long-leaf field mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and aromatic, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or violet, arranged in loose clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other members of the Mentha genus.
