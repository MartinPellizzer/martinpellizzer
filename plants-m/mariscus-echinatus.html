Mariscus echinatus

Mariscus echinatus, commonly known as the spiny mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features hairy, ribbed stems and heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves. Its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences and are usually pink or purple in color.

Mariscus echinatus is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
