Malva peruviana

Malva peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian mallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. The plant is characterized by its large, velvety leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Malva peruviana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
