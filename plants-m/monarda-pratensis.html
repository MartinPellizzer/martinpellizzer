Monarda pratensis

Monarda pratensis, commonly known as narrowleaf horsemint, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, tubular flowers are typically pink to purple and bloom in whorls along the stems.

Monarda pratensis is valued for its medicinal properties and serves as a habitat for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
