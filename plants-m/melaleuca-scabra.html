Melaleuca scabra

Melaleuca scabra, commonly known as the rough bottlebrush, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, ribbed, and often rough-textured leaves. The plant produces cylindrical, bottlebrush-like flowers that are typically red or pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in coastal and sandy environments.

Melaleuca scabra is also known for its aromatic oils, which have been used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.
