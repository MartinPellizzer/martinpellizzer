Mouriri pendulifolia

Mouriri pendulifolia, commonly known as the hanging mouriri, is a species of flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is a small tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its pendulous inflorescences, which bear small, white to yellowish flowers. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

Mouriri pendulifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
