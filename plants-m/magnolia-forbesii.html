Magnolia forbesii

Magnolia forbesii, commonly known as the Forbes magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves appear. The tree has simple, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall, and its bark is smooth with a reddish-brown color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in botanical studies.
