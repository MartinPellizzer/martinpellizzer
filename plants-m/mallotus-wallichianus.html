Mallotus wallichianus

Mallotus wallichianus, commonly known as the Indian chestnut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, spiny fruits containing edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.

The tree is adaptable to various soil types but thrives best in well-drained, fertile soils.
