Malanea sarmentosa f. sparsipilosa

Malanea sarmentosa f. sparsipilosa, commonly known as the slender mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered with fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that contain seeds coated in a sticky substance to aid in seed dispersal by birds.

This species is often found in tropical and subtropical regions where it forms symbiotic relationships with various tree species.
