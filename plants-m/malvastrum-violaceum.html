Malvastrum violaceum

Malvastrum violaceum, commonly known as the purple mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its velvety, purple-pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped, soft green leaves with a fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
