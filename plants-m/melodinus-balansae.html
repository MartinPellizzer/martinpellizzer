Melodinus balansae

Melodinus balansae, commonly known as the Madagascar star vine, is a climbing plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family, which includes various toxic and ornamental plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a follicle that splits open to release seeds covered in a sticky, fleshy coating.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
