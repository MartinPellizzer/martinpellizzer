Melocactus rufispinus f. elongatus

Melocactus rufispinus f. elongatus, commonly known as the red-spined elongated melocactus, is a unique variant of the melocactus genus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical stem that can grow up to several feet in height, making it distinct from the more compact forms of its species. The plant features dense, red or reddish-brown spines that provide protection against herbivores and intense sunlight. Its areoles, which are specialized structures for housing spines and flowers, are prominent and well-developed.

This variety thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, adapting well to desert environments.
