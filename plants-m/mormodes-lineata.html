Mormodes lineata

Mormodes lineata, commonly known as the lineated orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family, characterized by its elegant, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and often have a distinct veining pattern, while its flowers are usually white or pale pink with a subtle fragrance.

Mormodes lineata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
