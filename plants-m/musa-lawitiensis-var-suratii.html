Musa lawitiensis var. suratii

Musa lawitiensis var. suratii, commonly known as the Surat banana, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and clustered fruit bunches. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for cultivation, it is sometimes grown in botanical gardens and tropical regions.
