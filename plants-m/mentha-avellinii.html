Mentha avellinii

Mentha avellinii, commonly known as apple mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces small, pale purple to pink flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade.

Its aromatic leaves are used in culinary and medicinal applications, similar to other mint species.
