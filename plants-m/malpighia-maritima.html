Malpighia maritima

Malpighia maritima, commonly known as the sea berry or acerola cherry, is a small, tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It produces small, red, edible fruits that are rich in vitamin C and antioxidants. The plant thrives in coastal areas and sandy soils, often found in maritime climates. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a smooth texture.

The flowers are typically white or pink, and the plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand salt spray.
