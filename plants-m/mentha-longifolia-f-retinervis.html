Mentha longifolia f. retinervis

Mentha longifolia f. retinervis, commonly known as the narrow-veined spearmint, is a variant of the spearmint species characterized by its distinctive leaf venation.

This plant is native to regions of Europe and parts of Asia, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and serrated, with a strong, refreshing mint aroma. The flowers are small and tubular, typically appearing in clusters, and are pollinated by various insects.

It is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves used in culinary and medicinal applications.
