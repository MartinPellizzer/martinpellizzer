Medinilla loranthoides

Medinilla loranthoides, commonly known as the mistletoe medinilla, is a hemiparasitic plant belonging to the family Loranthaceae.

It grows as a leafy, branched shrub that attaches itself to the branches of its host tree, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its specialized haustoria that penetrate the host's vascular system. Its leaves are usually opposite and ovate, with a leathery texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits.

Medinilla loranthoides plays a role in ecological interactions, providing food for birds and insects while also influencing the health of its host tree.
