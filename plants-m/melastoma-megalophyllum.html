Melastoma megalophyllum

Melastoma megalophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaved melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
