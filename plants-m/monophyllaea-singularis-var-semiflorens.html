Monophyllaea singularis var. semiflorens

Monophyllaea singularis var. semiflorens, commonly known as the semi-flowering monophyllaea, is a rare and unique species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, large, and velvety leaves that are a deep emerald green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, pale pink flowers that bloom sparingly, typically during the late afternoon. Its growth habit is slow, with a compact, bushy form that makes it an attractive specimen for botanical gardens.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
