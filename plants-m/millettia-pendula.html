Millettia pendula

Millettia pendula, commonly known as the hanging millettia, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features compound leaves with long, slender stipules and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters. The plant bears pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. Millettia pendula thrives in humid environments and is often found in forests or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in suitable climates.
