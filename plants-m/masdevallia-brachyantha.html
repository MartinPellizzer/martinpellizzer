Masdevallia brachyantha

Masdevallia brachyantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, terrestrial growth habit and striking, often colorful flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant typically features narrow, strap-like leaves and produces pendulous inflorescences with showy, spurred petals. It thrives in cool, humid environments with partial shade, making it a popular choice for greenhouse cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value and specific growing requirements, it is often sought after by orchid enthusiasts.
