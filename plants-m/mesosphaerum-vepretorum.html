Mesosphaerum vepretorum

Mesosphaerum vepretorum, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a humid, warm climate to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic and practical properties.
