Masdevallia psittacina

Masdevallia psittacina, commonly known as the parrot orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, brightly colored flowers that resemble a parrot's beak, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. Its inflorescence consists of a single, erect spike bearing several showy, bilaterally symmetric flowers.

Masdevallia psittacina is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant colors and intricate floral structure.
