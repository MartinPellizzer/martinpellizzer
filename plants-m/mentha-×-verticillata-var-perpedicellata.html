Mentha × verticillata var. perpedicellata

Mentha × verticillata var. perpedicellata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy and arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. It thrives in moist, aquatic or semi-aquatic environments such as wet meadows, stream banks, and marshes.

Its aromatic leaves contain menthol and other essential oils, making it a valued plant in traditional medicine and herbal remedies.
