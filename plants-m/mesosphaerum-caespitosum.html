Mesosphaerum caespitosum

Mesosphaerum caespitosum, commonly known as the tufted mesosphaerum, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant features small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish-white in color, and it produces small, spherical fruits.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
