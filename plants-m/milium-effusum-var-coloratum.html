Milium effusum var. coloratum

Milium effusum var. coloratum, commonly known as the colored millet, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces delicate, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its compact growth habit and vibrant floral display make it a popular choice for horticultural collections.
