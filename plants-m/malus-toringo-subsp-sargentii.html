Malus toringo subsp. sargentii

Malus toringo subsp.

sargentii, commonly known as the Sargent crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China. It features slender, arching branches and produces small, fragrant flowers in early spring.

The plant has oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding ornamental value. Its fruit is a small, yellowish apple that ripens in late summer.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to grow in a variety of garden settings.
