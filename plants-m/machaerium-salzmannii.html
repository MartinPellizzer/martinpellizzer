Machaerium salzmannii

Machaerium salzmannii, commonly known as the Salzmann's machaerium, is a tropical shrub native to regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its seeds are encased in pods and can remain dormant for extended periods before germinating.

Machaerium salzmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
