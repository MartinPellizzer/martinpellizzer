Mariscus glomeratus

Mariscus glomeratus, commonly known as the clustered mossy tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in coastal and saline environments. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are typically clustered at the base of the stems. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often green or yellowish in color.

Mariscus glomeratus is well adapted to drought and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient plant in harsh environments.
