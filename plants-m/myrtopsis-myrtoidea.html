Myrtopsis myrtoidea

Myrtopsis myrtoidea, commonly known as the slender myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known plants like eucalyptus and myrtle. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and aromatic, often with a faintly fragrant scent. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in spring.

The shrub is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.
