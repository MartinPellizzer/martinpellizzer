Mimulus luteus var. depauperatus

Mimulus luteus var. depauperatus, commonly known as the pale yellow monkeyflower, is a variant of the yellow monkeyflower native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its reduced floral structures, including smaller petals and a more subdued yellow color compared to the typical form. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and in open woodlands. It features trailing stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
