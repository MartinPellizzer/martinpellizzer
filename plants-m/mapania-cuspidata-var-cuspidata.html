Mapania cuspidata var. cuspidata

Mapania cuspidata var. cuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mapania, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in spring and are pollinated by insects.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
