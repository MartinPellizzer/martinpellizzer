Madia gracilis subsp. collina

Madia gracilis subsp.

collina, commonly known as the Sierra dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to western North America. It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats such as meadows and slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
