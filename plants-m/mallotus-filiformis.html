Mallotus filiformis

Mallotus filiformis, commonly known as the slender mallot, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by distinctive, woody capsules that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
