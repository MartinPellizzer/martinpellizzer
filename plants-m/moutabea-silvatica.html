Moutabea silvatica

Moutabea silvatica, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a member of the Burseraceae family and is native to tropical Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a dense canopy. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by round, woody fruits containing seeds. Its heartwood is highly valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

The plant is also important in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
