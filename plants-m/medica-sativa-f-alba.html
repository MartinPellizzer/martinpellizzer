Medica sativa f. alba

Medica sativa f. alba, commonly known as the white variety of hemp, is a botanical variant of Cannabis sativa.

It is characterized by its light-colored leaves and flowers, which distinguish it from the more commonly known green varieties. This plant is primarily cultivated for its fibers and has a lower concentration of tetrahydrocannabinol (THC) compared to other Cannabis species. The botanical features of Medica sativa f. alba include its serrated leaves and dioecious flowering structure.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
