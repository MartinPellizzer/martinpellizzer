Maranta pubescens

Maranta pubescens, commonly known as the velvetleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves that often display vibrant red or purple hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread easily, making it both a desirable ornamental plant and a potential weed in certain environments.

Maranta pubescens thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for shaded garden areas or indoor cultivation.
