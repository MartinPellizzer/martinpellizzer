Marama picta

Marama picta, commonly known as the painted marama, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, showy yellow flowers. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds.

Marama picta is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
