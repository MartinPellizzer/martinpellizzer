Mimosa floribunda var. willdenowii

Mimosa floribunda var. willdenowii, commonly known as the willdenow's mimosa, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that open in response to touch, a phenomenon known as thigmonastic movement. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique foliage and flowering habit.

The species is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its natural habitat.
