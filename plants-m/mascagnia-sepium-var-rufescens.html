Mascagnia sepium var. rufescens

Mascagnia sepium var. rufescens, commonly known as the red-sepium mescal bean, is a variant of the mescal bean plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is a climbing shrub that produces small, yellowish flowers and red, fleshy fruits. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are often pubescent. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

The fruit contains seeds that are used in traditional practices and have historical significance in indigenous cultures.
