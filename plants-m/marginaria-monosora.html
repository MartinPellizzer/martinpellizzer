Marginaria monosora

Marginaria monosora, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically features succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in dry environments. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor itself in sandy or rocky soils.

Marginaria monosora plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native ecosystem.
