Malaxis lyonnetii

Malaxis lyonnetii, commonly known as the Lyonnet's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces a single spike of small, clustered flowers.

Malaxis lyonnetii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
