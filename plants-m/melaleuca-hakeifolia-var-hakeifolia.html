Melaleuca hakeifolia var. hakeifolia

Melaleuca hakeifolia var. hakeifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paperbark, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and showy, bottlebrush-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces woody capsules as fruit, which contain numerous seeds. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often growing in heathlands or open forests.

Melaleuca hakeifolia var. hakeifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage and flowering habit.
