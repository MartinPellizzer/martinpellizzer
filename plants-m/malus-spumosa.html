Malus spumosa

Malus spumosa, commonly known as the foamy apple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its white, foamy flowers that appear in early spring, giving it its common name. The plant has ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, edible apples in the fall. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a decorative plant.

Malus spumosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
