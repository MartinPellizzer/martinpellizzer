Mutisia sanguinea

Mutisia sanguinea, commonly known as the bloodflower, is a striking plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its dense, velvety foliage. The plant produces showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a smooth surface and a dark green color.

Mutisia sanguinea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
