Mammillaria coniflora

Mammillaria coniflora, commonly known as the Mexican night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique blooming habit and delicate appearance, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
