Miscanthus tanakae

Miscanthus tanakae, commonly known as Japanese silver grass, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and fluffy, silver-tan plumes that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and stream banks. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside.

Miscanthus tanakae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and as a ornamental plant in gardens.
