Moussonia pendula

Moussonia pendula, commonly known as the hanging moussonia, is a striking epiphytic plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its long, pendant stems that can reach several meters in length, supporting clusters of small, fleshy, waxy leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale green flowers that are often fragrant, attracting various pollinators. Its unique growth habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for hanging baskets or as a decorative plant in greenhouses.

Moussonia pendula thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires regular watering to maintain its lush, cascading form.
