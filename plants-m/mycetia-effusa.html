Mycetia effusa

Mycetia effusa, commonly known as the African senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant features pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins and produces yellow flowers in racemose inflorescences. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, and it is adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional African medicine.
