Mammillaria multidigitata

Mammillaria multidigitata, commonly known as the many-fingered milk barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.
