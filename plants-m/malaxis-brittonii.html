Malaxis brittonii

Malaxis brittonii, commonly known as the Britton's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks. The plant features a slender, upright stem with a single, lance-shaped leaf. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in a loose inflorescence, often with a faint fragrance.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its unique appearance and ecological role in its habitat.
