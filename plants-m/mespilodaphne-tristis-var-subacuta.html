Mespilodaphne tristis var. subacuta

Mespilodaphne tristis var. subacuta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mespilodaphne, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with smooth, pale grey bark that exudes a fragrant resin when cut. The leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a distinctive narrow, acuminate tip, contributing to its botanical uniqueness. The flowers are small, fragrant, and white, typically appearing in clusters, while the fruit is a small, round drupe with a smooth, yellowish skin.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
