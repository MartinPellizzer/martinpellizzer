Matthiola ramuliflora

Matthiola ramuliflora, commonly known as the clustered wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, brightly colored flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are upright and often branched.

Matthiola ramuliflora is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
