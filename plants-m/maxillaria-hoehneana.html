Maxillaria hoehneana

Maxillaria hoehneana, commonly known as the Hoehne's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy flowers. The plant typically produces fragrant, violet-colored blooms with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Maxillaria hoehneana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
