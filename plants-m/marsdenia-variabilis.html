Marsdenia variabilis

Marsdenia variabilis, commonly known as the variable Marsdenia, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink in color. Its fruit is a follicle that contains several seeds, and it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
