Modecca coccinea

Modecca coccinea, commonly known as the scarlet beauty, is a striking flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers, which are clustered in dense inflorescences, making it a visually appealing species. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, providing a contrasting texture to its showy blooms.

Due to its ornamental value, Modecca coccinea is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
