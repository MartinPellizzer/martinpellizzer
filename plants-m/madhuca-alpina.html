Madhuca alpina

Madhuca alpina, commonly known as the mountain tallow tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by spherical fruits containing sticky, amber-colored seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
