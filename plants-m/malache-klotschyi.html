Malache klotschyi

Malache klotschyi, commonly known as the Klotschyi mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces white, fleshy berries that are an important food source for native birds. It is native to Australia and is often found in dry forest and woodland habitats.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can have a significant impact on the health of its host trees.
