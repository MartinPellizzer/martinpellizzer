Menziesia ciliicalyx var. bicolor

Menziesia ciliicalyx var. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored willow-leaf azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which features a striking bicolor pattern on its leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a finely toothed margin, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is adapted to acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade.
