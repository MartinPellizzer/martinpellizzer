Myristica sarcantha

Myristica sarcantha, commonly known as the fragrant nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody structure and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by distinctive, three-lobed fruits that enclose the valuable nutmeg seeds. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, making it an important species in traditional medicine and perfumery.

Myristica sarcantha is also noted for its ecological significance in supporting local biodiversity.
