Mesosphaerum selaginifolium

Mesosphaerum selaginifolium, commonly known as the rock cress, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or coastal environments. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, with four petals. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, adapting to its harsh habitat.

Mesosphaerum selaginifolium is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
