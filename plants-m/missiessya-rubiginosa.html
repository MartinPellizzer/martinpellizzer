Missiessya rubiginosa

Missiessya rubiginosa, commonly known as the redstem dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, white bracts that surround small, red berries, which are an important food source for birds. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a reddish stem, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

The redstem dogwood is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
