Maxillaria alberti

Maxillaria alberti, commonly known as the Albert's orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy flowers. The plant typically produces intricate, fragrant blooms with a mix of purple, pink, and white hues. Its pseudobulbs are cylindrical, and it prefers warm, humid environments with good air circulation.

Maxillaria alberti is often cultivated for its ornamental value in both greenhouse and tropical gardens.
