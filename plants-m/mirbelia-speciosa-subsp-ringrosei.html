Mirbelia speciosa subsp. ringrosei

Mirbelia speciosa subsp.

ringrosei, commonly known as the ringrose mirbelia, is a shrub native to the southwest of Western Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive, ring-like arrangement of flowers around the stem, which gives it its common name.

The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is adapted to grow in sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions with a Mediterranean climate.
