Malus prunifolia var. grandiflora

Malus prunifolia var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces showy, white or pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, red or yellow fruits. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This variety is also valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators.
