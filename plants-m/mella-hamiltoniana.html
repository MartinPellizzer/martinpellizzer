Mella hamiltoniana

Mella hamiltoniana, commonly known as the Hamilton's melia, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Mahogany family (Meliaceae) and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by winged fruits that contain a single seed. It is valued for its durable, aromatic wood, which is used in traditional crafts and furniture.

Additionally, it plays an important role in the ecological systems of its native habitats.
