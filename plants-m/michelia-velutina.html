Michelia velutina

Michelia velutina, commonly known as the velvet michelia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that are typically covered in a fine, velvety hair, giving it its common name. Michelia velutina is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile choice for temperate climates.
