Mentha × rotundifolia f. exstans

Mentha × rotundifolia f. exstans, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a hybrid species within the Mentha genus, characterized by its rounded, fuzzy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and shaded woodlands.

Its aromatic leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent in culinary applications.
