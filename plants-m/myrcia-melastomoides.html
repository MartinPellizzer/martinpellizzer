Myrcia melastomoides

Myrcia melastomoides, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often consumed by birds and other wildlife.
