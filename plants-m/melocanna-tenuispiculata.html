Melocanna tenuispiculata

Melocanna tenuispiculata, commonly known as the slender spike bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a smooth, waxy surface.

This bamboo is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
