Masdevallia paranaensis

Masdevallia paranaensis, commonly known as the Parana orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of small, brightly colored blossoms in shades of pink, red, and white. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Masdevallia paranaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
