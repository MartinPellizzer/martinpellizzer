Masdevallia sarcophylla

Masdevallia sarcophylla, commonly known as the "Coffin Leaf Masdevallia," is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, dark green, leathery leaves that resemble a coffin, giving it its common name. The plant produces striking, colorful flower spikes with intricate, often fragrant blooms. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires high light exposure but is sensitive to direct sunlight.

Masdevallia sarcophylla is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and relatively manageable care requirements.
