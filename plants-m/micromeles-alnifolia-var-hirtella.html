Micromeles alnifolia var. hirtella

Micromeles alnifolia var. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy alderleaf, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are densely covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that releases tiny seeds when mature.

This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and along stream banks.
