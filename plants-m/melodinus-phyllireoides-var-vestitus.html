Melodinus phyllireoides var. vestitus

Melodinus phyllireoides var. vestitus, commonly known as the velvet-leaved melodinus, is a tropical climbing plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a deep green color with a slightly pubescent surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are often white or pale yellow, followed by fruit that resembles a small, elongated capsule.

This plant is often grown for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of tropical and subtropical climates.
