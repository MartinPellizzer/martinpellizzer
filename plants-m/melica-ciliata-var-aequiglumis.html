Melica ciliata var. aequiglumis

Melica ciliata var. aequiglumis, commonly known as the smooth-leafed brome, is a grass species characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, marshes, and stream banks. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft texture.

This variety is notable for its ecological adaptability and role in supporting local wildlife.
