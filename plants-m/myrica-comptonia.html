Myrica comptonia

Myrica comptonia, commonly known as Compton's wax myrtle, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark, waxy berries. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and swamps.

Myrica comptonia plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
