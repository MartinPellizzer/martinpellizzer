Mussaenda landia var. holosericea

Mussaenda landia var. holosericea, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red bracts that surround small, yellow flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are typically short-lived but attract a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
