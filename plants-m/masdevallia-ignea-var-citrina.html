Masdevallia ignea var. citrina

Masdevallia ignea var. citrina, commonly known as the Citrine Flame Orchid, is a striking orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange floral displays, which are particularly vivid in the sunlit environments where it thrives. The plant features intricate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a compact rosette. It prefers cool, high-altitude habitats with high humidity and well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its unique appearance and limited natural range, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
