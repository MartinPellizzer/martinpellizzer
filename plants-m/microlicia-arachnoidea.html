Microlicia arachnoidea

Microlicia arachnoidea, commonly known as the spider-fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and unique, spiny fruit that resembles a spider's web. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing before the leaves.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
