Mentha diemenica var. serpyllifolia

Mentha diemenica var. serpyllifolia, commonly known as the Tasmanian mint, is a variant of the native Australian mint species.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and distinctive flowering spikes that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, with a strong minty scent when crushed.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in herbal remedies.
