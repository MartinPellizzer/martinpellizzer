Monolena pilosiuscula

Monolena pilosiuscula, commonly known as the hairy white daisy, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a composite head. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Monolena pilosiuscula is primarily ornamental but can also serve as a food source for certain insect species.
