Machaerium pungens

Machaerium pungens, commonly known as the spiny machaerium, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, often pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its seeds are enclosed in pods, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Machaerium pungens is also noted for its use in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
