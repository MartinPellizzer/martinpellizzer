Manilkara schweinfurthii var. chevalieri

Manilkara schweinfurthii var. chevalieri, commonly known as the African manioc or bitter manioc, is a tropical tree native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and smooth, often reddish bark. The plant produces yellowish flowers followed by edible, starchy fruits that are used in local cuisines. Its latex sap is toxic and has been historically used for rubber production.

The species is valued for its ecological role and traditional uses in various regions.
