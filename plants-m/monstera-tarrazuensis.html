Monstera tarrazuensis

Monstera tarrazuensis, commonly known as the Tarrazu monstera, is a tropical plant native to the cloud forests of Costa Rica.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves that resemble those of the more widely known Monstera deliciosa. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually a deep green with a waxy texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by edible, spiky fruits.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it has become a popular houseplant among enthusiasts.
