Meridiana tesselata

Meridiana tesselata, commonly known as the tessellated meridiana, is a unique succulent plant native to specific arid regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, geometric leaf patterns that resemble a mosaic, giving it its common name. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. Its flowers are typically small and may appear in clusters, often in shades of yellow or white.

Meridiana tesselata is well-suited for rock gardens or xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and striking appearance.
