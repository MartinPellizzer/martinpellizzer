Millettia galliflagrans

Millettia galliflagrans, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces distinctive, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It is valued for its hard, dense wood, which is used in traditional craftsmanship and furniture.

Due to overharvesting and habitat loss, it is now considered an endangered species.
