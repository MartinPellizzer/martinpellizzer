Mammillaria subtetragona

Mammillaria subtetragona, commonly known as the four-angled mammilla, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth rate, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
