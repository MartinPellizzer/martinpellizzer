Mapouria umbrosa

Mapouria umbrosa, commonly known as the shade-loving maple, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and produces small, fragrant flowers in spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a leathery texture.

Mapouria umbrosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities.
