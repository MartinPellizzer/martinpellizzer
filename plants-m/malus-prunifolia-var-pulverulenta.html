Malus prunifolia var. pulverulenta

Malus prunifolia var. pulverulenta, commonly known as the powdered crabapple, is a unique variant of the crabapple tree native to China.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that appear in early spring, followed by small, yellowish fruits. The leaves are oval-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, turning red in the fall. This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low-maintenance.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for various garden settings.
