Marrubium vulgare var. apulum

Marrubium vulgare var. apulum, commonly known as Apulian horehound, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features gray-green, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive scent. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
