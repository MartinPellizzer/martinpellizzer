Myrcia glauca

Myrcia glauca, commonly known as the silky myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Myrcia glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
