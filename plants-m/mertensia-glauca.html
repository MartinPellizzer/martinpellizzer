Mertensia glauca

Mertensia glauca, commonly known as bluebells, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often hairy to the touch. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or shaded areas.

Mertensia glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
