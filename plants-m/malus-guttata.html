Malus guttata

Malus guttata, commonly known as the spotted apple tree, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves with prominent white or yellow spots. The tree produces small, yellow-green fruits that are typically not edible for humans but are used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are white and appear in clusters, adding ornamental value to the tree.

The plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits in gardens and landscapes.
