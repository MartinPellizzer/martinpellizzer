Machaerium floribundum

Machaerium floribundum, commonly known as the clustered machaerium, is a tropical shrub native to regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pink flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, fertile soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
