Miconia flammea var. major

Miconia flammea var. major, commonly known as the flame miconia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a large shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its broad, palmate leaves that are often bright green with a reddish or purple underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that are followed by large, showy red or orange berries.

These berries are an important food source for various bird species, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
