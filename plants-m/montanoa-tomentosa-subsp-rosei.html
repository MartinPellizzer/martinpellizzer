Montanoa tomentosa subsp. rosei

Montanoa tomentosa subsp.

rosei, commonly known as the roseleaf montanoa, is a shrub native to the highlands of Mexico. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a soft, fuzzy appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
