Modecca pinnatisecta

Modecca pinnatisecta, commonly known as the broadleaf starling tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pinnatisected leaves that give it a distinctive, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, woody fruits containing seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
