Muscari tauricum

Muscari tauricum, commonly known as the Tauric grape hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It produces clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.

Muscari tauricum is relatively low maintenance and resistant to pests and diseases.
