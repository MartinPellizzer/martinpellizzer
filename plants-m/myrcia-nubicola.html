Myrcia nubicola

Myrcia nubicola, commonly known as the mountain myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in the Andes. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Myrcia nubicola is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in local cultures.
