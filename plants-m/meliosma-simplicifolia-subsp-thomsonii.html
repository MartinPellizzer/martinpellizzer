Meliosma simplicifolia subsp. thomsonii

Meliosma simplicifolia subsp.

thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's meliosma, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical to ovate in shape.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its foliage is often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland or swampy habitats.
