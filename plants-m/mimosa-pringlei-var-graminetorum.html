Mimosa pringlei var. graminetorum

Mimosa pringlei var. graminetorum, commonly known as the desert mimosa, is a shrub native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit and trifoliate leaves that fold shut when touched, a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces small, yellow, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low precipitation.

Its ecological importance includes providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in desert environments.
