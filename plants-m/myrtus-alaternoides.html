Myrtus alaternoides

Myrtus alaternoides, commonly known as the common myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, reddish-black berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for coastal and rocky gardens.

Myrtus alaternoides is also valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in perfumery and culinary applications.
