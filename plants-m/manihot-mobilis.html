Manihot mobilis

Manihot mobilis, commonly known as the creeping arrowleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
