Malus eleyi

Malus eleyi, commonly known as the Eleyi apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the highlands of Ethiopia and is characterized by its small, yellowish fruits that ripen in the autumn. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, while its flowers are white or pink and appear in clusters.

The Eleyi apple is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential in horticultural breeding programs.
