Mabea piriri var. obovata

Mabea piriri var. obovata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia uniflora*, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces bright red, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and commonly used in jams and desserts. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
