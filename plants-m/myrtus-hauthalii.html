Myrtus hauthalii

Myrtus hauthalii, commonly known as the Hauthal myrtle, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, dark green, leathery leaves and produces clusters of pale pink to white flowers in the spring. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant foliage, which is often used in traditional medicine and perfumery. Its fruit, a small, dark blue berry, is edible but not commonly consumed.

Myrtus hauthalii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
