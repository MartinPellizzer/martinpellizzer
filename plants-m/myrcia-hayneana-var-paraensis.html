Myrcia hayneana var. paraensis

Myrcia hayneana var. paraensis, commonly known as the Paraense myrtle, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of up to 6 meters. The plant is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are woody capsules containing several seeds, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded environments.

It plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
