Mangostana

Mangostana, commonly known as the mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Garciniaceae and is characterized by its thick, spiky rind and segmented, fragrant fruit. The fruit's pericarp contains a rich array of bioactive compounds, including xanthones, which are believed to have antioxidant properties. The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its leaves are dark green and glossy, contributing to its ornamental value in addition to its culinary and medicinal uses.
