Medicago vardanis

Medicago vardanis, commonly known as the Armenian clover, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soils.

Medicago vardanis is also valued for its potential in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
