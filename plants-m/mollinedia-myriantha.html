Mollinedia myriantha

Mollinedia myriantha, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a velvety appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, and it produces distinctive, three-lobed seeds.

Mollinedia myriantha is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in wet conditions.
