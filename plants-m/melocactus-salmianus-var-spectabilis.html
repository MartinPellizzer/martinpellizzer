Melocactus salmianus var. spectabilis

Melocactus salmianus var. spectabilis, commonly known as the Spectacular Melocactus, is a rare and striking cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and vibrant, spiky areoles that bear white or pale yellow spines. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a prized specimen among cactus enthusiasts.

Due to its endangered status, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
