Melica cretica f. laxiflora

Melica cretica f. laxiflora, commonly known as the loose-flowered crested grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged, giving it its common name. It typically blooms with small, inconspicuous flowers that are often green or pale blue in color.

This plant is native to regions of Europe and western Asia and is often found in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands.
