Malpighia wendtii

Malpighia wendtii, commonly known as the wendt's granadilla, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red, fleshy fruits that resemble small grapes. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine pubescence, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The fruit is edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and is often used in culinary and traditional medicine applications.
