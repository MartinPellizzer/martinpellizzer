Morysia diversifolia

Morysia diversifolia, commonly known as the diverse-leaved morysia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that vary in color from deep green to silvery-white, often with intricate veining patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting a variety of pollinators. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Morysia diversifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden environments.
