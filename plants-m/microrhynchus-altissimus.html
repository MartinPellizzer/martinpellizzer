Microrhynchus altissimus

Microrhynchus altissimus, commonly known as the giant burrweed, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant produces distinctive, spiny fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become a weed in agricultural areas.
