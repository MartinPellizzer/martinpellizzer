Mentha cyprica

Mentha cyprica, commonly known as Cyprus mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pale blue or violet flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and basil. Mentha cyprica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and digestive properties.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens and landscapes.
