Mimulus aurantiacus subsp. australis

Mimulus aurantiacus subsp.

australis, commonly known as the yellow monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its bright yellow, spurred flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and in coastal areas. It has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in coastal heathlands and open woodlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
