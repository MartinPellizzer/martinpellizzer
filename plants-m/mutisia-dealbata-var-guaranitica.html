Mutisia dealbata var. guaranitica

Mutisia dealbata var. guaranitica, commonly known as the Guarani sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil and Paraguay.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, showy yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as savannas and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in a fine white pubescence.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its striking floral display.
