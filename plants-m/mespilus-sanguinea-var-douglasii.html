Mespilus sanguinea var. douglasii

Mespilus sanguinea var. douglasii, commonly known as the Douglas's whitebeam, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape, with a rough texture. It produces small, yellowish fruits that are edible but not as widely consumed as other species in the genus.

This variety is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
