Mammillaria tecta

Mammillaria tecta, commonly known as the Mexican hat cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and dense, white to pale green spines. The plant produces small, showy yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Mammillaria tecta is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its compact size and unique appearance.
