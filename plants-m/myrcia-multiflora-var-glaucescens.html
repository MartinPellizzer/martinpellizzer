Myrcia multiflora var. glaucescens

Myrcia multiflora var. glaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous myrcia, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in a whitish, waxy coating, giving them a bluish-gray appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale pink, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that is edible and contains a single seed.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
