Mallotus esquirolii

Mallotus esquirolii, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, featuring a rough, fissured bark. The plant produces clusters of yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The tree is valued for its ornamental flowers and is often cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic appeal.
