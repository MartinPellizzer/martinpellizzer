Mimosa viscosa var. inermis

Mimosa viscosa var. inermis, commonly known as the soft mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and small, yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has a trailing growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

The species is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
