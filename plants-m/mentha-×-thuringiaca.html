Mentha × thuringiaca

Mentha × thuringiaca, commonly known as German mint, is a hybrid mint species originating from Central Europe.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

German mint is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for its aromatic and purportedly soothing properties.
