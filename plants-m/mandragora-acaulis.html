Mandragora acaulis

Mandragora acaulis, commonly known as the barren mandrake, is a rare and striking plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, leafless stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by violet-black berries. Due to its rarity and historical associations with folklore and medicine, it is highly valued in botanical and ecological studies.

Its survival is threatened by habitat loss and over-collection, making conservation efforts crucial for its preservation.
