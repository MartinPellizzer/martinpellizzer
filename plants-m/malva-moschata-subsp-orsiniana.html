Malva moschata subsp. orsiniana

Malva moschata subsp.

orsiniana, commonly known as the Egyptian mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its large, showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often found in moist, calcareous soils. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its soothing properties.
