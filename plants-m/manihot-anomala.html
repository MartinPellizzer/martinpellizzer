Manihot anomala

Manihot anomala, commonly known as the arrowleaf mallow, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its roots contain compounds that have been used traditionally in herbal medicine.

Botanically, it belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other plants like cotton and hibiscus.
