Mitracarpus eritrichoides

Mitracarpus eritrichoides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of small, sky-blue flowers resembling mist. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.

The blue mistflower is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
