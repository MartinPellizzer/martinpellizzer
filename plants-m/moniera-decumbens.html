Moniera decumbens

Moniera decumbens, commonly known as the creeping moniera, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with stems that lie close to the ground and often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Moniera decumbens is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
