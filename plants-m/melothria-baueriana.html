Melothria baueriana

Melothria baueriana, commonly known as the dwarf cucurbit, is a small, herbaceous vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers, squashes, and gourds. The plant produces small, round, green fruits that are typically less than 2 centimeters in diameter. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with five petals.

Melothria baueriana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and compact growth habit.
