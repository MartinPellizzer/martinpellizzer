Mutisia vicia

Mutisia vicia, commonly known as the yellow mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a shrub-like structure with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Mutisia vicia is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
