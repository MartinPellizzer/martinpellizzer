Mentha fontana var. conferta

Mentha fontana var. conferta, commonly known as marsh mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and riparian habitats.

It features aromatic, slightly hairy leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found near streams, ponds, and marshes.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used for digestive and respiratory remedies.
