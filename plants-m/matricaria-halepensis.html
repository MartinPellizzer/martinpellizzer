Matricaria halepensis

Matricaria halepensis, commonly known as the Caucasian chamomile, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with white centers, which bloom throughout the summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny areas and has a creeping growth habit with hairy stems. Its leaves are finely divided and covered in a soft, downy layer.

The essential oils extracted from its flowers are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine for their calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
