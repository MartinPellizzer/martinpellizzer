Madronella viminea

Madronella viminea, commonly known as the slender madrone, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its small, dark berries are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

The shrub's dense foliage and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
