Matisia hirta

Matisia hirta, commonly known as the hairy matisia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in humid lowland forests. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and clustered, often yellow or greenish-yellow in color.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.
