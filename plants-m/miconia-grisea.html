Miconia grisea

Miconia grisea, commonly known as the gray miconia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to several meters in height, characterized by its large, palmate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that are typically followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a distinctive appearance.

Miconia grisea is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
