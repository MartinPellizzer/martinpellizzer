Madaroglossa angustifolia

Madaroglossa angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf madaroglossa, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a woody stem that becomes more pronounced with age.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.
