Miconia fulva var. aubletiana

Miconia fulva var. aubletiana, commonly known as the golden miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that are followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
