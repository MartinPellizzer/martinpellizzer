Macroplectrum

Macroplectrum, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a genus of aquatic plants native to tropical regions of South America.

These plants are characterized by their large, broad leaves that float on the surface of still or slow-moving waters. The leaves are typically thick and waxy, which helps them stay buoyant and resist decomposition. Macroplectrum species also produce showy, fragrant flowers that bloom above the water, attracting pollinators such as beetles.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter for aquatic life and contributing to water filtration.
