Matthiola chorassanica

Matthiola chorassanica, commonly known as the Persian candytuft, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. It is often found in rocky or arid environments due to its drought-resistant nature.

Matthiola chorassanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
