Messerschmidia valga

Messerschmidia valga, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It typically grows in moist meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. Its leaves are narrow and lanceolate, often with a slightly hairy texture.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
