Matricaria lamellata var. turcomanica

Matricaria lamellata var. turcomanica, commonly known as the Turkestan chamomile, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their mild sedative and anti-inflammatory properties.

The essential oil extracted from its flowers contains compounds like bisabolol and chamazulene, which contribute to its therapeutic uses.
