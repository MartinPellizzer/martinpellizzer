Morus trilobata

Morus trilobata, commonly known as the three-lobed mulberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes other mulberry species. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which have three distinct leaflets with serrated edges. It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn black when mature.

Morus trilobata is often cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value.
