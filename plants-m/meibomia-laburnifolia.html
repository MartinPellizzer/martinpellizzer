Meibomia laburnifolia

Meibomia laburnifolia, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that resemble small flames, blooming in clusters along the branches. The plant has a dense, broad canopy with compound leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its bark is smooth and greyish, often exfoliating to reveal a lighter inner layer.

Meibomia laburnifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its vibrant floral display.
