Magnolia denudata var. pyramidalis

Magnolia denudata var. pyramidalis, commonly known as the Chinese pagoda tree, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its upright, pyramidal shape and its showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree has simple, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding to its seasonal appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional Chinese gardens.
