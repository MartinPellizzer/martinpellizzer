Merremia nymphaeifolia

Merremia nymphaeifolia, commonly known as the waterleaf vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating on water surfaces, giving it a distinctive aquatic appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically borne in clusters, and it has a fibrous, woody stem that can grow quite long. Its leaves are usually broad and have a smooth, waxy texture, which helps them retain moisture in humid environments.

Merremia nymphaeifolia is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
