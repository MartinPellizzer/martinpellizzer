Maxillaria leucaimata

Maxillaria leucaimata, commonly known as the white-lipped orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its sympodial growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with prominent yellow lip petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Maxillaria leucaimata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
