Melocactus ferreophilus

Melocactus ferreophilus, commonly known as the iron-loving melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, ribbed, cylindrical stem that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and rockeries.
