Montanoa ternifolia

Montanoa ternifolia, commonly known as the three-leaved montanoa, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America and northern South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Montanoa ternifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
