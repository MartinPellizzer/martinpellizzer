Myrcia canapuensis

Myrcia canapuensis, commonly known as Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a fragrant scent when crushed.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
