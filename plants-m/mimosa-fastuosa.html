Mimosa fastuosa

Mimosa fastuosa, commonly known as the sensitive plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that fold shut when touched, a response triggered by changes in touch or light. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and indoor cultivation.

Its rapid growth and unique movement make it an intriguing subject for botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
