Myrcia multiflora f. ovalifolia

Myrcia multiflora f. ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaved myrcia, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It features glossy, elliptical leaves that are often arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that ripens in the late summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
