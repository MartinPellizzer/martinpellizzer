Mexotis

Mexotis, commonly known as the Mexican lily, is a genus of flowering plants native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, often fragrant flowers. The plants typically have strap-like leaves and produce clusters of bell-shaped blooms in shades of white, yellow, or orange. Mexotis species are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens and as houseplants.

They prefer well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
