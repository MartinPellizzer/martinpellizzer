Microphyton suffocatum

Microphyton suffocatum, commonly known as the suffocating microphyton, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its small, filamentous structure.

It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant's leaves are microscopic, often less than a millimeter in length, and are arranged in dense, tangled clusters. Its reproductive structures are concealed within the dense foliage, making it challenging to identify without specialized equipment.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
