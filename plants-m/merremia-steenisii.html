Merremia steenisii

Merremia steenisii, commonly known as the African moon vine, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
