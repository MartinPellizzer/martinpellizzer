Millettia utilis

Millettia utilis, commonly known as the useful millettia, is a climbing leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Millettia utilis is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.

Its adaptability to various soil types contributes to its ecological and economic importance.
