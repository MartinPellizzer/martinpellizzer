Mentha gentilis var. heleogeton

Mentha gentilis var. heleogeton, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, typical of the mint family. It thrives in moist environments such as stream banks and wet meadows.

Its essential oils, rich in menthol and other terpenes, contribute to its distinctive cooling and medicinal properties.
