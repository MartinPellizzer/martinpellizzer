Muhlenbergia appressa

Muhlenbergia appressa, commonly known as the narrowleaf muhlenbergia, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often described as having a bluish-green color. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer to early fall.

Muhlenbergia appressa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in wetland ecosystems.
