Musa campestris var. miriensis

Musa campestris var. miriensis, commonly known as the Miriensis banana, is a variety of the banana plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and clusters of yellow, edible fruit. The plant is typically cultivated for its sweet, starchy fruit, which has a flavor similar to plantains. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its striking foliage.
