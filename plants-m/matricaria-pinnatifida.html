Matricaria pinnatifida

Matricaria pinnatifida, commonly known as the German chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with white centers. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers. It is often cultivated for its aromatic properties and is used in herbal medicine.

Matricaria pinnatifida thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners and herbalists alike.
