Melandrium rubrum var. serpentinicola

Melandrium rubrum var. serpentinicola, commonly known as the serpentinite rockstar, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky, calcareous soils.

It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in nutrient-poor, well-drained environments, often found on serpentinite outcrops. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in a fine pubescence.

The species is adapted to withstand harsh conditions, making it a resilient and specialized flora of alpine and rocky habitats.
