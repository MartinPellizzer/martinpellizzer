Maxillaria frechettei

Maxillaria frechettei, commonly known as the Frechette's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowering structures. Its flowers are usually pink or purple with a distinctive white lip, and it blooms during the warmer months.

Maxillaria frechettei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
