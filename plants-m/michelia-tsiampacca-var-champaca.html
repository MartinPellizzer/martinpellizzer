Michelia tsiampacca var. champaca

Michelia tsiampacca var. champaca, commonly known as champaca, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, and it often has a strong, sweet scent.

Champaca is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is also used in traditional medicine.
