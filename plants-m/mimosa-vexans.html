Mimosa vexans

Mimosa vexans, commonly known as the sensitive plant, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Mimosa vexans is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique responsiveness to touch.
