Myrtus javanica

Myrtus javanica, commonly known as Java myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule containing numerous small, dark seeds.

Myrtus javanica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a mild, aromatic scent reminiscent of myrrh.
