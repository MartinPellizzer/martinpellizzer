Morgania aspera

Morgania aspera, commonly known as the rough morgania, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and can reach heights of up to 2 feet. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough, textured surface, giving it its common name. Its small, white to pinkish flowers bloom in late spring, adding ornamental value to the landscape.

Morgania aspera is often found in shaded areas and is considered a valuable component of native plant communities.
