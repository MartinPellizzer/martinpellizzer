Marattia papuana

Marattia papuana, commonly known as the Papuan tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the Pacific Islands, particularly Papua New Guinea.

It belongs to the family Marattiaceae and is characterized by its large, compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive trunk that is covered with remnants of old fronds, giving it a unique, ancient appearance. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in coastal or lowland rainforests.

Marattia papuana plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various species.
