Moenchia mantica subsp. caerulea

Moenchia mantica subsp.

caerulea, commonly known as blue moenchia, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Amaranthaceae family. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers.

The leaves are typically lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, giving them a textured appearance. This subspecies is native to parts of Europe and is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
