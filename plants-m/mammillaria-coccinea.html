Mammillaria coccinea

Mammillaria coccinea, commonly known as the red mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white to pale blue tubercles. The plant produces vibrant red flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections.
