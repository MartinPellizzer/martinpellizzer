Mentha longifolia var. hapalophylla

Mentha longifolia var. hapalophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mint, is a variety of the pennyroyal plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks. It has a strong, aromatic scent and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.

However, it is important to note that this species can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.
