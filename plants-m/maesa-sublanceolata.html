Maesa sublanceolata

Maesa sublanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maesa, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
