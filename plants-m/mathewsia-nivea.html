Mathewsia nivea

Mathewsia nivea, commonly known as the white-flowered rock lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in rock gardens.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
