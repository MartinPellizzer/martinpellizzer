Machaerium angustifolium f. glaucum

Machaerium angustifolium f. glaucum, commonly known as the glaucous machaerium, is a variant of the slender leaved machaerium native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy, bluish-white coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow to greenish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Its unique foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners interested in exotic plant species.
