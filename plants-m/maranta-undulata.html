Maranta undulata

Maranta undulata, commonly known as the zigzag plant, is a tropical flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its striking, waxy, undulating leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb, often forming dense clumps with broad, colorful foliage. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually white or pale pink, which appear in clusters.

Maranta undulata is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
