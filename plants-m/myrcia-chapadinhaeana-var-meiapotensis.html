Myrcia chapadinhaeana var. meiapotensis

Myrcia chapadinhaeana var. meiapotensis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle or meiapotense myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a fragrant scent. It produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, red berries.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
