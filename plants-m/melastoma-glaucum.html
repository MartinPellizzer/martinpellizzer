Melastoma glaucum

Melastoma glaucum, commonly known as the silverleaf melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, silver-green leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and shrublands. It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is known for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
