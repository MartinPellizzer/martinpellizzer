Mesosetum discolor

Mesosetum discolor, commonly known as the variable grass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver or gray. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are typically clustered at the ends of the stems. Mesosetum discolor is adapted to wetland environments and thrives in moist soils, making it a common sight in marshes and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
