Matthiola reticulata

Matthiola reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined stock, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and a compact, upright growth habit. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and borders.

Matthiola reticulata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
