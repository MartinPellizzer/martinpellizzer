Melastoma molkenboerii

Melastoma molkenboerii, commonly known as the African beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Following flowering, it develops dark purple to black berries that are edible for birds and small mammals.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting wildlife.
