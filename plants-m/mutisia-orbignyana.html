Mutisia orbignyana

Mutisia orbignyana, commonly known as the white mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in countries like Argentina, Bolivia, and Peru.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, white to pale yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and has a woody stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Mutisia orbignyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
