Myosotis intermedia subsp. lebelii

Myosotis intermedia subsp.

lebelii, commonly known as the Lebel's forget-me-not, is a herbaceous perennial native to New Zealand. It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, sky-blue flowers with five petals.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This subspecies thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
