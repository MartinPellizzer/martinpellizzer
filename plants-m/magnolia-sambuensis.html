Magnolia sambuensis

Magnolia sambuensis, commonly known as the Sambu magnolia, is a species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a distinct fragrance. The plant produces elliptical, dark green leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, making it a significant component of the forest canopy in its natural habitat.
