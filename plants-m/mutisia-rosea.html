Mutisia rosea

Mutisia rosea, commonly known as the white tree of the Andes, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, showy white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces a distinctive, fragrant aroma. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
