Myrcia apodocarpa

Myrcia apodocarpa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces reddish-brown berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

Myrcia apodocarpa is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other parts of the world, sometimes becoming invasive.
