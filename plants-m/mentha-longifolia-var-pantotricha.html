Mentha longifolia var. pantotricha

Mentha longifolia var. pantotricha, commonly known as woolly mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and aromatic, slightly minty scent. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for its purported calming and digestive properties.
