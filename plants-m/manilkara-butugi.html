Manilkara butugi

Manilkara butugi, commonly known as the Butugi or Butug tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen canopy and smooth, dark bark. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible berries that are used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a glossy surface, while its fruits contain a single seed surrounded by a fleshy pulp.

The plant is valued for its ornamental features and medicinal properties.
