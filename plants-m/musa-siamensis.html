Musa siamensis

Musa siamensis, commonly known as the Thai banana, is a species of flowering plant in the family Musaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand, and is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and clustered fruit. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Musa siamensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as for its fruit.
