Milium coerulescens var. songaricum

Milium coerulescens var. songaricum, commonly known as the blue oat grass, is a flowering plant native to central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and panicles of small, blue-green flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that contribute to its ecological role in stabilizing soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
