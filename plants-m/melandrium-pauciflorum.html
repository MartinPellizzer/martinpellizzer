Melandrium pauciflorum

Melandrium pauciflorum, commonly known as the lesser catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and typically grows in dry, disturbed soils such as roadsides and meadows. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Melandrium pauciflorum is also noted for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
