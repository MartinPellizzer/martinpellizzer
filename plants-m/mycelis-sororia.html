Mycelis sororia

Mycelis sororia, commonly known as the common mycelial fungus, is a unique plant-like organism that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It lacks true roots, stems, and leaves, instead relying on a network of thread-like hyphae to absorb nutrients from the soil. Its reproductive structures, known as sporocarps, produce and release spores into the air. The plant plays a vital role in decomposing organic matter and recycling nutrients in ecosystems.

Due to its subterranean nature, it is often overlooked despite its ecological significance.
