Miconia rimachii

Miconia rimachii, commonly known as the Chilean miconia, is a species of flowering plant in the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to the Andean region of South America, particularly Chile and Argentina. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it has been introduced to other regions, though it can become invasive in some areas.
