Miconia altomacaensis

Miconia altomacaensis, commonly known as the highland miconia, is a species of flowering plant in the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly in the Andean foothills of Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
