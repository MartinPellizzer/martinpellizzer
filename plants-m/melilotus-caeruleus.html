Melilotus caeruleus

Melilotus caeruleus, commonly known as blue melilot, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight.

Blue melilot is often used in traditional herbal medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
