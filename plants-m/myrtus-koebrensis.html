Myrtus koebrensis

Myrtus koebrensis, commonly known as the common myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features fragrant, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. The plant produces small, white flowers that give way to dark blue berries, which are edible but typically used in culinary applications. Myrtus koebrensis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its aromatic properties and historical significance in various cultures further highlight its botanical and cultural importance.
