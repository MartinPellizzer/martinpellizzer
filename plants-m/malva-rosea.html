Malva rosea

Malva rosea, commonly known as the rose mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its large, showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

Malva rosea is often grown as an ornamental plant for its beauty and ability to attract pollinators.
