Misanteca tikalana

Misanteca tikalana, commonly known as the sacred forest tree, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and broad, compound leaves that provide shade beneath its canopy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators. Its roots are known for their medicinal properties, used by indigenous communities for centuries.

The tree plays a vital role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat.
