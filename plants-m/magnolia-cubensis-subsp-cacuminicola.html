Magnolia cubensis subsp. cacuminicola

Magnolia cubensis subsp.

cacuminicola, commonly known as the Cuban magnolia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Cuba. It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The leaves are broad, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a dark green upper surface. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and parks in warm climates.
