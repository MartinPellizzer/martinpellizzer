Malaxis nigrescens

Malaxis nigrescens, commonly known as the black orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers, which are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has a slender, creeping rhizome. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense clusters near the ground.

The species is noted for its striking appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
