Mespilus laciniata

Mespilus laciniata, commonly known as the European whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval or elliptic in shape. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to red or yellow as it matures.

The bark is smooth and gray, with shallow fissures, and the tree is known for its longevity and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
