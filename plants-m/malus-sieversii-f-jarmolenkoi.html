Malus sieversii f. jarmolenkoi

Malus sieversii f. jarmolenkoi, commonly known as the Jarmolenko crabapple, is a botanical variant of the wild apple species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, often fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically dark green and oval-shaped, providing a striking contrast to the showy blossoms. This form is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

It also plays a role in the genetic diversity of cultivated apples, contributing to breeding programs aimed at improving fruit traits.
