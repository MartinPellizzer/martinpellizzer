Malcolmia runcinata

Malcolmia runcinata, commonly known as the white mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, woolly leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a common sight in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and medicinal purposes.
