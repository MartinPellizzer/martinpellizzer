Monardella montana

Monardella montana, commonly known as mountain monardella, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture. Its flowers are typically pink to purple, arranged in whorls along the stems, and bloom in late summer.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and meadows at higher elevations.
