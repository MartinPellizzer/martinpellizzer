Machilus gamblei

Machilus gamblei, commonly known as the Chinese laurel, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are typically followed by dark blue berries. Machilus gamblei thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
