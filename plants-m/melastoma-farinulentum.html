Melastoma farinulentum

Melastoma farinulentum, commonly known as the sticky melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color, with a distinctive, sticky texture on the petals. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine, glandular hairs.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
