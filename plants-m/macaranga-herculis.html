Macaranga herculis

Macaranga herculis, commonly known as the giant macaranga, is a fast-growing deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats, making it a common species in secondary forests and along roadsides. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds.

The tree is also notable for its role in supporting local biodiversity, particularly in tropical ecosystems.
