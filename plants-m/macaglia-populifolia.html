Macaglia populifolia

Macaglia populifolia, commonly known as the poplar-leaved macaglia, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that resemble those of poplar trees, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.
