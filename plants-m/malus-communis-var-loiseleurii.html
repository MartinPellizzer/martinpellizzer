Malus communis var. loiseleurii

Malus communis var. loiseleurii, commonly known as the Loiseleurii apple, is a cultivar of the apple tree known for its ornamental value.

It features a compact, rounded growth habit with dense, dark green foliage that turns vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. The tree produces small, sweet, and tart apples that are often used for cooking or cider-making. It is valued in gardens for its year-round interest and ability to thrive in a variety of climates.

This variety is also noted for its disease resistance and adaptability to different soil types.
