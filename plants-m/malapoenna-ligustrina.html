Malapoenna ligustrina

Malapoenna ligustrina, commonly known as the African privet, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the summer months. These flowers are followed by small, black berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
