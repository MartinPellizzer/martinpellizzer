Menispermum rimosum

Menispermum rimosum, commonly known as the creeping menispermum, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its roots contain various alkaloids, which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
