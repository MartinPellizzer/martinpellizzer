Momordica multiflora var. albopilosa

Momordica multiflora var. albopilosa, commonly known as the white-haired bitter melon, is a variant of the bitter melon plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pale green, ribbed fruits that are typically smaller than the standard bitter melon. The leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture, and the plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. This variety is valued for its edible fruits, which have a slightly bitter taste and are used in various traditional cuisines.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
