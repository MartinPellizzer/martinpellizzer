Mariscus circumclusus

Mariscus circumclusus, commonly known as the clustered mariscus, is a rare and intriguing plant species characterized by its unique growth pattern.

It is a perennial herb that typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or stream banks. The plant features small, clustered leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish-white in color, and bloom in late spring.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Mariscus circumclusus is considered a botanically significant but endangered species.
