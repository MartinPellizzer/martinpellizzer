Maireana lobiflora

Maireana lobiflora, commonly known as the river red gum, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along riverbanks and in dry coastal areas. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in arid conditions.

Maireana lobiflora plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
