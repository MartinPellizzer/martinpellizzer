Mapouria capillacea

Mapouria capillacea, commonly known as the silky mapouria, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and pleasant scent.
