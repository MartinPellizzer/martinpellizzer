Mespilus viridis

Mespilus viridis, commonly known as the green medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its oval, dark green leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically harvested when they are still firm and unripe. These fruits have a distinctive, aromatic flavor and are often used in culinary applications.

The green medlar is also valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
