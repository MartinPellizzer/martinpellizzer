Mentha nemophila

Mentha nemophila, commonly known as the forest mint, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features upright stems covered in fine, hairy hairs and produces small, pale purple to white flowers. Its leaves are oval-shaped, serrated, and emit a strong, minty aroma when crushed.

Mentha nemophila is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
