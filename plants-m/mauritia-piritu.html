Mauritia piritu

Mauritia piritu, commonly known as the Brazilian palm, is a tall, slender palm tree native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It has a smooth, gray trunk and can grow up to 20 meters in height. The plant produces large, feather-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern at the top of the trunk. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and it produces edible fruits that are consumed by various wildlife species.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for numerous animals.
