Mesembryanthemum musculinum

Mesembryanthemum musculinum, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Mesembryanthemum musculinum is also known for its ability to store water in its leaves, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.
