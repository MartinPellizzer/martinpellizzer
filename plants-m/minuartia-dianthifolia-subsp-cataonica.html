Minuartia dianthifolia subsp. cataonica

Minuartia dianthifolia subsp.

cataonica, commonly known as the Cataonic rock-cress, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to mountainous regions of the Mediterranean. It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and stipitate glands.

The subspecies is distinguished by its pale pink to white flowers and hairy, lanceolate leaves. It typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments.
