Mediocalcar congestum

Mediocalcar congestum, commonly known as the clustered sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub with small, narrow leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its blooming period typically occurs in the spring and early summer.

Mediocalcar congestum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native pollinators.
