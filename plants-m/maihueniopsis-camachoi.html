Maihueniopsis camachoi

Maihueniopsis camachoi, commonly known as the Andean sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments and thrives at high elevations. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads.
