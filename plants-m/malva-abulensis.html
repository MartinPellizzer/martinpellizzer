Malva abulensis

Malva abulensis, commonly known as the Abulensis mallows, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
