Marlierea paniculata

Marlierea paniculata, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with multiple small leaflets and produces woody pods that contain seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display.

Marlierea paniculata thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is adaptable to various soil types.
