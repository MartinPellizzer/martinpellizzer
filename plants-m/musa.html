Musa

Musa, commonly known as the banana plant, is a tropical herbaceous plant belonging to the Musaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and striking inflorescence that bears clusters of bananas. The plant's pseudostem is composed of tightly packed leaf sheaths, giving it a tree-like appearance. Musa species are primarily cultivated for their edible fruit, which varies in size, color, and flavor across different varieties.

These plants thrive in warm, humid climates and require well-drained soil for optimal growth.
