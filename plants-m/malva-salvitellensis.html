Malva salvitellensis

Malva salvitellensis, commonly known as the common mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cotton and hibiscus. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture and produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in meadows, along streambanks, and in disturbed habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
