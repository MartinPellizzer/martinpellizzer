Machaerium trifoliolatum

Machaerium trifoliolatum, commonly known as the three-leaved machaerium, is a tropical perennial plant native to regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically features trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being ovate to lanceolate in shape. It produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Machaerium trifoliolatum is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
