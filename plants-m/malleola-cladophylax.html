Malleola cladophylax

Malleola cladophylax, commonly known as the golden mistplant, is a unique species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, fibrous root system that helps it anchor in nutrient-poor soil. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to yellow, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically broad and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in humid environments.

Malleola cladophylax is also known for its slow growth rate and ability to thrive in shaded conditions.
