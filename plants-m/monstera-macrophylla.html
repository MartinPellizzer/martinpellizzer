Monstera macrophylla

Monstera macrophylla, commonly known as the giant monstera, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 feet in diameter. The plant has a climbing habit and produces aerial roots that help it attach to support structures. Its leaves are typically a deep green with distinctive splits and holes as they mature.

Monstera macrophylla thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.
