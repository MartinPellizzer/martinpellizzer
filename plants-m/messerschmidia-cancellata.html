Messerschmidia cancellata

Messerschmidia cancellata, commonly known as the spotted bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant has hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive, wavy margin. Its flowers are typically arranged in a loose cluster at the top of the plant.

Messerschmidia cancellata is adapted to cold, rocky environments and is often found in alpine meadows.
