Mentha suaveolens var. lepteilema

Mentha suaveolens var. lepteilema, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spearmint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a distinct minty fragrance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue to purple in color, arranged in loose spikes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and flavoring.
