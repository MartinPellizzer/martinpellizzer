Mertensia gracilis

Mertensia gracilis, commonly known as slender bluebells, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Mertensia gracilis is valued in gardens for its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
