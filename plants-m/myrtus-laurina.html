Myrtus laurina

Myrtus laurina, commonly known as sweet myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Myrtus laurina is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
