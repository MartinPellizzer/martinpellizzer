Memora moringifolia

Memora moringifolia, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, featuring simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by fleshy, orange-red berries containing several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in warm, humid climates.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers.
