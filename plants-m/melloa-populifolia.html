Melloa populifolia

Melloa populifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf melloa, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which bloom during the rainy season. Its growth habit is shrub-like, with multiple stems that can reach heights of up to two meters.

The botanical features of Melloa populifolia make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
