Mastrucium insulare

Mastrucium insulare, commonly known as the island starflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or near freshwater habitats. Its leaves are typically ovate with a smooth texture, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Mastrucium insulare is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
