Miconia nigricans

Miconia nigricans, commonly known as black miconia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a large shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its broad, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a berry that turns from green to black as it matures, containing numerous small seeds.

Miconia nigricans is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some areas.
