Masdevallia amanda

Masdevallia amanda, commonly known as Amanda's masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, often colorful flower structures with showy, spurred petals. The plant typically grows as a miniature epiphyte, clinging to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually dark green and strap-like, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Masdevallia amanda is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique morphology and delicate beauty.
