Machilus breviflorus

Machilus breviflorus, commonly known as the short-flowered woodbine, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are simple, elliptical, and have a glossy surface, while its flowers are usually white or pale yellow.

The fruit is a small, dark berry that develops from the flower and is an important food source for various birds and mammals.
