Microcorys capitata

Microcorys capitata, commonly known as the clustered microcorys, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, clustered inflorescences and hairy stems. The plant produces white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
