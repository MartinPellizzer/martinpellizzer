Mahonia repens var. rotundifolia

Mahonia repens var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf mahonia, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It features glossy, elliptic to round leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by blue-black berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded garden environments.
