Mentha × rotundifolia var. webberi

Mentha × rotundifolia var. webberi, commonly known as Webber's round-leaf mint, is a hybrid mint species characterized by its distinctive rounded leaves.

It is native to the eastern United States and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer, adding ornamental value to its habitat. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, making it useful in herbal remedies and culinary applications.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.
