Mucuna sanjappae

Mucuna sanjappae, commonly known as the Sanjappae velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, hairy pods that contain elongated, kidney-shaped seeds. The plant produces a profuse amount of yellow flowers, which are often followed by the development of its characteristic fruiting bodies. It is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial in soil enrichment.

The seeds of Mucuna sanjappae are sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.
