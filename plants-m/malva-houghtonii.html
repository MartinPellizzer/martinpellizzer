Malva houghtonii

Malva houghtonii, commonly known as Houghton's mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its hairy, oval-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
