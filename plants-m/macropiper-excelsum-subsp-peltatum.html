Macropiper excelsum subsp. peltatum

Macropiper excelsum subsp.

peltatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved pepperbush, is a member of the Piperaceae family. It is characterized by its large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and have a distinct veining pattern.

The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its fruits are capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is native to the wet tropical regions of South America, particularly in the Amazon basin.
