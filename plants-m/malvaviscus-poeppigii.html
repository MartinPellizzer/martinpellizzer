Malvaviscus poeppigii

Malvaviscus poeppigii, commonly known as the Mexican mallow or yellow mallow, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers and woolly, hairy leaves. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in shaded or moist environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
