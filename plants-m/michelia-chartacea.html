Michelia chartacea

Michelia chartacea, commonly known as the paper mulberry, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to reddish as it matures.

Michelia chartacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in various cultures.
