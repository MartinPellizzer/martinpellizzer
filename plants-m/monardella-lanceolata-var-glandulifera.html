Monardella lanceolata var. glandulifera

Monardella lanceolata var. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular lemon mint, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often glandular-dotted, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in dry, rocky habitats.

Its aromatic foliage and nectar-rich flowers make it a valuable plant for pollinators.
