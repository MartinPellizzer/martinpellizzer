Miscanthus polydactylos

Miscanthus polydactylos, commonly known as the many-fingered miscanthus, is a perennial grass native to southern China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and feathery plumes that give it a graceful appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as marshes and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Miscanthus polydactylos is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
