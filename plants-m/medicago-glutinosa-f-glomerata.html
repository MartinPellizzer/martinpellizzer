Medicago glutinosa f. glomerata

Medicago glutinosa f. glomerata, commonly known as sticky medick, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, sticky leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. It is a valuable forage plant for livestock due to its high nutritional value and palatability.

Additionally, it plays a role in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility through its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.
