Mallotus amentiformis

Mallotus amentiformis, commonly known as the hairy antler tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnate and often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits that contain several seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
