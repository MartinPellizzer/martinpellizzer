Mimulus cupreus

Mimulus cupreus, commonly known as the copper monkeyflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features striking, bright orange to red tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Mimulus cupreus is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
