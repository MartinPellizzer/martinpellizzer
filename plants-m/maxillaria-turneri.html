Maxillaria turneri

Maxillaria turneri, commonly known as the Turner's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in forest understories. The plant features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive, fragrant scent.

The orchid's botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitats.
