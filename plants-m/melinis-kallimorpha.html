Melinis kallimorpha

Melinis kallimorpha, commonly known as the purpletop millet, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and purple-tinted flower heads. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often used in ornamental gardening due to their striking color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Melinis kallimorpha is also known for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in disturbed areas.
