Miconia weddellii

Miconia weddellii, commonly known as the Weddell miconia, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are typically dispersed by animals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a problematic species in certain regions.
