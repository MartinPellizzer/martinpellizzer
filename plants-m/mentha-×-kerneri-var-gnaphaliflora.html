Mentha × kerneri var. gnaphaliflora

Mentha × kerneri var. gnaphaliflora, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring erect, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of purple or pink, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils, giving it a minty scent and making it a popular choice for ornamental and culinary purposes.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of garden environments.
