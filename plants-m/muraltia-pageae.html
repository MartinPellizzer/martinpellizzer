Muraltia pageae

Muraltia pageae, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Brazilian wild cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round, red fruits that resemble cherries, which are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.
