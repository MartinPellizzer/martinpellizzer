Micromyrtus racemosa

Micromyrtus racemosa, commonly known as the coastal bottlebrush, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that are typically red or pink in color. The plant has small, narrow leaves and is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils. It plays an important role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a notable species in its native range.
