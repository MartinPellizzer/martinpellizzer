Martianthus stachydifolius

Martianthus stachydifolius, commonly known as the silverleaf stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, powdery hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
