Mimusops balata var. sieberi

Mimusops balata var. sieberi, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that turn from green to yellow or red when mature. Its bark is thick and corky, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its timber.
