Mutisia bipontini

Mutisia bipontini, commonly known as the Andean mutisia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and shrublands, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers display a mix of yellow and white petals.

Mutisia bipontini is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
