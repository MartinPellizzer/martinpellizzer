Myoporum serratum var. subserratum

Myoporum serratum var. subserratum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf myoporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It features narrow, serrated leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers clustered in terminal spikes, which bloom in spring. It is often found in coastal and lowland areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and erosion control.
