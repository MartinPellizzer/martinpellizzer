Maytenus phyllanthoides

Maytenus phyllanthoides, commonly known as the Mexican mullein, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the family Phyllanthaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in axillary or terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Maytenus phyllanthoides is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
