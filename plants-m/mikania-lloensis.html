Mikania lloensis

Mikania lloensis, commonly known as the Peruvian morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and heliotropic leaves that follow the sun. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

Mikania lloensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
