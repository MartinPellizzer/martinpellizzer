Melia parviflora

Melia parviflora, commonly known as the smallflower chinaberry, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces yellow-orange berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans and livestock. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Melia parviflora is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
