Memecylon magnifoliatum

Memecylon magnifoliatum, commonly known as the broad-leaf memecylon, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are glossy and often have a distinct midrib. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
