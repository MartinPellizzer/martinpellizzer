Mimulus inodorus

Mimulus inodorus, commonly known as the pale mimic primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a rough texture and a slightly hairy surface.

Mimulus inodorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
