Mapania longifolia

Mapania longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf mapania, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or wetland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
