Moquilea acioa

Moquilea acioa, commonly known as the Andean quinoa, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces tiny, edible seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients, making it a valuable crop for both traditional and modern agriculture. Moquilea acioa thrives in cool, high-altitude environments with well-drained soil.

Its cultivation is closely tied to the cultural practices of indigenous communities in the Andean region.
