Morinda cinnamomea

Morinda cinnamomea, commonly known as the Indian madder or red sanders, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
