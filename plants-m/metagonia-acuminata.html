Metagonia acuminata

Metagonia acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed metagonia, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to certain regions of South America, particularly in Brazil and Argentina. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid, tropical environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
