Monolophus bracteatus

Monolophus bracteatus, commonly known as the bracted monolophus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Central America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bracts. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The showy yellow flowers appear in clusters and are followed by small, dry fruits known as achenes.
