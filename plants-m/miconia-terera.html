Miconia terera

Miconia terera, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or red miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bright red flowers that are followed by distinctive, round, orange-red fruits. Miconia terera is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a common invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it an important subject in ecological studies due to its impact on local flora and fauna.
