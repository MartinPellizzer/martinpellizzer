Minuartia villarsii var. grignensis

Minuartia villarsii var. grignensis, commonly known as the Grignan rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Pyrenees region of France.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its mat-forming growth habit, with small, fleshy leaves and bright yellow flowers. The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. It is adapted to harsh climates and is an important component of mountainous flora.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
