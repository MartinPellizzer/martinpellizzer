Marantochloa monophylla var. holostachya

Marantochloa monophylla var. holostachya, commonly known as the red-veined swamp taro, is a tropical plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves with deep red veins against a dark green background. The plant thrives in waterlogged or moist soils and is often found in marshy areas and along riverbanks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense stands.

While it is primarily cultivated for ornamental purposes, it also plays a role in traditional medicine and local ecosystems.
