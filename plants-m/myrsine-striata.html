Myrsine striata

Myrsine striata, commonly known as the red-flowered myrsine, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrsinaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves with a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a distinctive red calyx. Its fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that contains several seeds.

Myrsine striata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
