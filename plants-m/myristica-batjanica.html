Myristica batjanica

Myristica batjanica, commonly known as the Malabar nut or Batjan's nut, is a species of evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody structure and large, leathery leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers and spherical fruits that contain a single seed surrounded by a rich, aromatic aril. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.

Myristica batjanica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
