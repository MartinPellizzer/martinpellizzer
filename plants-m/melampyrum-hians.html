Melampyrum hians

Melampyrum hians, commonly known as the common touch-me-not, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, woodlands, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Melampyrum hians is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities through symbiotic relationships with root nodules.
