Massonia versicolor

Massonia versicolor, commonly known as the rainbow ice plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that form a dense rosette. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique coloration and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
