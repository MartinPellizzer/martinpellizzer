Melampodium diffusum var. lanceolatum

Melampodium diffusum var. lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as roadsides and prairies. It has a creeping growth habit and produces achenes that are dispersed by wind.

Its botanical features make it a hardy, drought-tolerant species suitable for xeriscaping.
