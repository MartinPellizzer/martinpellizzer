Metamyriactis pycnophylla

Metamyriactis pycnophylla, commonly known as the dense-leaved mistplant, is a unique species characterized by its dense, overlapping foliage that gives it a compact appearance.

It is native to the humid, shaded understories of tropical rainforests in Southeast Asia. The plant features small, elliptical leaves with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture in its moist habitat. Its flowering occurs in clusters, producing tiny, pale green flowers that are often overlooked.

Despite its modest appearance, it plays an important role in supporting local insect populations and maintaining forest biodiversity.
