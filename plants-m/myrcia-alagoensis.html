Myrcia alagoensis

Myrcia alagoensis, commonly known as the Alagoas myrtle, is a shrub native to the northeastern region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that release seeds when mature.

The plant is often found in humid, tropical environments and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
