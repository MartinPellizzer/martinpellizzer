Metrosideros polymorpha f. strigosa

Metrosideros polymorpha f. strigosa, commonly known as the silver-leafed ʻōhiʻa, is a variant of the ʻōhiʻa tree native to Hawaii.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on the leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is known for its adaptability to various soil types. It produces small, red flowers that are an important nectar source for native birds.

This variant is often found in coastal and lowland areas, contributing to the unique biodiversity of Hawaiian ecosystems.
