Macroscepis equatorialis

Macroscepis equatorialis, commonly known as the equatorial orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and sword-shaped, and it thrives in warm, moist climates with high humidity.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens.
