Muraltia candollei

Muraltia candollei, commonly known as the yellow muraltia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant flowers and adaptability to various soil conditions.

Muraltia candollei is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
