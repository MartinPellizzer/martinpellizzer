Muscarella delicatula

Muscarella delicatula, commonly known as the slender muscaria, is a species of bracket fungus that belongs to the family Tramellaceae.

It is characterized by its small, thin, and often brightly colored caps that can range from yellow to orange or red. The stem is typically short and club-shaped, and the fungus is known for its delicate and fragile structure. It grows on decaying wood in forested areas, particularly in temperate regions.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to mycologists and nature enthusiasts.
