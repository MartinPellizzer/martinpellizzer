Moquiniastrum pulchrum

Moquiniastrum pulchrum, commonly known as the pretty moquinia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like appearance. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often pink or purple in color, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
