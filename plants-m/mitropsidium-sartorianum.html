Mitropsidium sartorianum

Mitropsidium sartorianum, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in spines and produce clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant has a succulent structure, allowing it to store water in its thick, fleshy stems. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
