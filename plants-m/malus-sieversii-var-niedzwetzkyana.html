Malus sieversii var. niedzwetzkyana

Malus sieversii var. niedzwetzkyana, commonly known as the Niedzwetzkyana apple, is a rare and ancient species of apple native to the Tian Shan Mountains in Central Asia.

It is considered a wild ancestor of many modern apple varieties and possesses a unique genetic makeup that contributes to its hardiness and adaptability. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, featuring simple, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. Its fruit is generally small, tart, and not typically consumed fresh but is valued for its genetic diversity in breeding programs.

This variety plays a significant role in the study of apple evolution and conservation efforts.
