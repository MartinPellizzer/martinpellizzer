Millettia heterocarpa

Millettia heterocarpa, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive pod fruits. The plant is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
