Marlierea tomentosa

Marlierea tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly marlierea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with a soft, velvety texture.

Marlierea tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
