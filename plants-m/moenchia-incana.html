Moenchia incana

Moenchia incana, commonly known as the hoary moenchia, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage due to dense pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
