Myrcia serrana

Myrcia serrana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruits are reddish berries that contain several seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Myrcia serrana is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
