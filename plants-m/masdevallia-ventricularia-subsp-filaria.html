Masdevallia ventricularia subsp. filaria

Masdevallia ventricularia subsp.

filaria, commonly known as the Filamentous Masdevallia, is a rare and delicate orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador. It is characterized by its slender, filament-like leaves and small, intricate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink.

The plant thrives in cool, humid environments with high air circulation, often growing epiphytically on tree trunks or branches. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts.

Due to habitat destruction, it is considered endangered and requires careful conservation efforts.
