Monardella linoides var. anemonoides

Monardella linoides var. anemonoides, commonly known as the mountain monardella, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, showy white to pale pink flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, typically found in open meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of native plant communities and a popular choice for native gardens.
