Mucuna velutina

Mucuna velutina, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with small, heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces distinctive, pod-like fruits that contain several elliptical, flat seeds. Mucuna velutina is known for its rapid growth and ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it useful in agricultural settings.

It also contains compounds with potential medicinal and toxic properties.
