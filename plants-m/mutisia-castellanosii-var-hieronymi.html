Mutisia castellanosii var. hieronymi

Mutisia castellanosii var. hieronymi, commonly known as the Castellanos' mutisia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a tall, branching stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy yellow flowers appear in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variant is distinguished by its unique floral morphology and habitat preferences, making it a notable species in botanical studies.
