Mespilus monogyna proles intermedia

Mespilus monogyna proles intermedia, commonly known as the European medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, ribbed, and often rough-textured bark. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, round, and typically brown fruits that ripen in autumn. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall.

The species is known for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
