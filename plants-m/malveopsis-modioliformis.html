Malveopsis modioliformis

Malveopsis modioliformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallow, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are typically pale pink to white, with five petals and a central yellow stamen.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
