Melolobium macrocalyx var. brachycarpum

Melolobium macrocalyx var. brachycarpum, commonly known as the small-fruit melolobium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its erect growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in terminal umbels, which bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that matures in late summer, containing several seeds.

The species is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland grassland habitats.
