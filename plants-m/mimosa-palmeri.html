Mimosa palmeri

Mimosa palmeri, commonly known as the desert mimosa, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its soft, feathery leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in sandy or rocky soils. Mimosa palmeri is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, which helps improve soil fertility.

Its unique growth form and ecological role make it an important species in desert ecosystems.
