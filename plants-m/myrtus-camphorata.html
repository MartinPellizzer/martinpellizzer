Myrtus camphorata

Myrtus camphorata, commonly known as the common myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, dark green leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, berry-like fruits. Its essential oils, particularly containing cineol, are valued for their medicinal and aromatic properties.

Myrtus camphorata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
