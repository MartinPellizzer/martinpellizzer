Maba quartzitarum

Maba quartzitarum, commonly known as the diamond plant, is a unique species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, translucent leaves that resemble quartz crystals, giving it its common name. The plant exhibits a distinct growth pattern, often forming dense clusters with a compact, bushy appearance. Its botanical features include small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by fruit that contains tiny, seed-like structures.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, Maba quartzitarum is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
