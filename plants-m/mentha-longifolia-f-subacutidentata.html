Mentha longifolia f. subacutidentata

Mentha longifolia f. subacutidentata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mint, is a variant of the longleaf mint species.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves with a pointed tip, which distinguish it from other mint varieties. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue or purple in color.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic leaves and is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.
