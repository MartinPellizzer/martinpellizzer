Mentha longifolia var. barthiana

Mentha longifolia var. barthiana, commonly known as Barth's mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves that are often wrinkled and have a strong menthol scent. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for its cooling and digestive properties.
