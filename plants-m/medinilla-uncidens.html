Medinilla uncidens

Medinilla uncidens, commonly known as the velvet tangerine, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Medinilla uncidens is also valued for its ability to produce edible fruits, which are small and tart, similar in flavor to tangerines.
