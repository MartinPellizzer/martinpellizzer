Meliosma lepidota subsp. dumicola

Meliosma lepidota subsp.

dumicola, commonly known as the hairy meliosma, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are hairy on both surfaces, giving it a distinctive texture.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate partial shade.
