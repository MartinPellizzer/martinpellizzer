Molina tomentosa

Molina tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly molina, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, cool environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
