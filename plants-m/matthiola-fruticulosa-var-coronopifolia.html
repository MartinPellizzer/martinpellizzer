Matthiola fruticulosa var. coronopifolia

Matthiola fruticulosa var. coronopifolia, commonly known as the crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 30 cm tall, featuring dense, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The flowers are typically pale blue to purple in color, with five petals and a distinctive crown-like appearance. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
