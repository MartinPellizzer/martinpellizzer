Mimosa eurycarpoides

Mimosa eurycarpoides, commonly known as the broad-fruited mimosa, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnately compound leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that are followed by distinctive, inflated pods containing several seeds. Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and habitat restoration.

The plant is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
