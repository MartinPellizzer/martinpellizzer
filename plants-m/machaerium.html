Machaerium

Machaerium, commonly known as the "broom grass," is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It consists of over 100 species, predominantly found in tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. These plants are characterized by their compound leaves and small, often showy flowers that can be white, yellow, or purple. Many species of Machaerium are used for forage, erosion control, or ornamental purposes due to their hardy nature and adaptability.

Some species also have medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
