Melicytus ramiflorus var. samoensis

Melicytus ramiflorus var. samoensis, commonly known as the Samoan gloryflower, is a shrub or small tree native to the islands of Samoa and nearby regions in the Pacific.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, pale yellow to cream-colored flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal and lowland forests.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems and traditional practices.
