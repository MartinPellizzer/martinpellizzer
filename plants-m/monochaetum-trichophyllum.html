Monochaetum trichophyllum

Monochaetum trichophyllum, commonly known as the silky feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its fine, silky, and densely tufted foliage that gives it a soft, feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in coastal and wetland areas, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowering stems produce small, spike-like inflorescences that are often overlooked due to the plant's ornamental foliage.

Monochaetum trichophyllum is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in horticultural settings for its unique texture and visual interest.
