Matudaea trinervia var. hirsuta

Matudaea trinervia var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy matudaea, is a tropical plant characterized by its hairy foliage and robust growth habit.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, commonly known as the palm family, and is native to Central and South America. The plant features a slender, unbranched stem with large, pinnately compound leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. Its inflorescences are typically clustered at the base of the plant, producing small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale yellow.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates for its unique appearance.
