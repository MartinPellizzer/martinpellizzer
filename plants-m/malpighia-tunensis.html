Malpighia tunensis

Malpighia tunensis, commonly known as the Trinidadian starfruit, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean.

It produces small, bright orange, star-shaped fruits that are edible and have a tangy flavor. The plant belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

The fruit's unique shape and color make it a popular ornamental and culinary plant in its native region.
