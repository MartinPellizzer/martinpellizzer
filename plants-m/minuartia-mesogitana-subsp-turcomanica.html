Minuartia mesogitana subsp. turcomanica

Minuartia mesogitana subsp.

turcomanica, commonly known as the Turcomanian rock-cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae. It is a low-growing, perennial herb that typically forms dense mats on rocky or gravelly soils.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, often covered in fine hairs, and are adapted to arid environments.
