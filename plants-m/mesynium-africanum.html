Mesynium africanum

Mesynium africanum, commonly known as the African mesynium, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and woody stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Mesynium africanum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
