Melaleuca brachyandra

Melaleuca brachyandra, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paperbark, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to summer. It is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

Melaleuca brachyandra is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
