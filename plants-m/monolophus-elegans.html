Monolophus elegans

Monolophus elegans, commonly known as the elegant lichen, is a foliose lichen that exhibits a distinctive botanical structure.

It typically grows on bark, soil, or rock surfaces and forms a dense, leaf-like thallus. The plant is characterized by its complex arrangement of photobionts and fungal hyphae, which work together to absorb nutrients and water. Its morphology includes a central layer of fungal tissue surrounded by a layer of algal cells.

Monolophus elegans is often found in moist, shaded environments and plays a vital role in ecological succession and nutrient cycling.
