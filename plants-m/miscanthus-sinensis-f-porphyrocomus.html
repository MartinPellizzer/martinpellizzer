Miscanthus sinensis f. porphyrocomus

Miscanthus sinensis f. porphyrocomus, commonly known as the purple moor grass, is a compact, ornamental grass with distinctive reddish-purple foliage.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that turn a vibrant red-purple in the autumn, adding visual interest to garden landscapes. The plant produces feathery, silver-white plumes in the late summer to early autumn, enhancing its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its seasonal color changes and low maintenance requirements.
