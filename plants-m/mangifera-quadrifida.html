Mangifera quadrifida

Mangifera quadrifida, commonly known as the four-lobed mango, is a species of flowering plant in the Anacardiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its distinctive four-lobed leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The fruit is typically small, with a rough, spiny rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp. It is less commonly cultivated than other mango varieties but is valued for its unique flavor and ornamental qualities.

The plant thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
