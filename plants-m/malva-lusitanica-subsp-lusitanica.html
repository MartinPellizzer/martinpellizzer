Malva lusitanica subsp. lusitanica

Malva lusitanica subsp.

lusitanica, commonly known as the Portuguese mallows, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers with five petals, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice in horticulture.
