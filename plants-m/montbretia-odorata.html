Montbretia odorata

Montbretia odorata, commonly known as the sweet cycad, is a species native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Zamiaceae family and is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces distinctive cones, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants. It is known for its slow growth and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
