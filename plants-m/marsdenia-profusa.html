Marsdenia profusa

Marsdenia profusa, commonly known as the clustered milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and India.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family, which is known for its milkweed species. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.

The latex sap of Marsdenia profusa contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
