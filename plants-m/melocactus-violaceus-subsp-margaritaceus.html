Melocactus violaceus subsp. margaritaceus

Melocactus violaceus subsp.

margaritaceus, commonly known as the violet melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America. It is characterized by its cylindrical stem that is typically violet or purple in color, with a distinctive rib structure.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the rainy season, which are followed by fleshy, red fruits containing edible seeds. It has a slow growth rate and is well adapted to harsh desert conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in cactus gardens.
