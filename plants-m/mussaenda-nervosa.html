Mussaenda nervosa

Mussaenda nervosa, commonly known as the velvet flower or white flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble flames, with bright yellow bracts surrounded by small white flowers. The plant has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
