Monardella viminea

Monardella viminea, commonly known as slender beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and tubular, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Monardella viminea is an important nectar source for native bees and other pollinators.
