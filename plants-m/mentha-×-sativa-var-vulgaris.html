Mentha × sativa var. vulgaris

Mentha × sativa var. vulgaris, commonly known as peppermint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Mentha spicata and Mentha aquatica.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly fuzzy leaves and its strong, cool menthol scent. The plant is a perennial herb that thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight. It has square stems and opposite leaves, and it is widely cultivated for its essential oils used in medicine, food, and aromatherapy.

Peppermint is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes and seeds.
