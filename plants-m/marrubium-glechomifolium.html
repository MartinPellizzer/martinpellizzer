Marrubium glechomifolium

Marrubium glechomifolium, commonly known as creeping mouse-ear, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a low-growing, mat-forming habit with small, hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy plant adapted to dry and sunny environments.
