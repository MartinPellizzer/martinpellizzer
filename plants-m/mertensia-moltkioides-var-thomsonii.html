Mertensia moltkioides var. thomsonii

Mertensia moltkioides var. thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's bluebells, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features striking, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
