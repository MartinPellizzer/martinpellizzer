Musa × paradisiaca var. odorata

Musa × paradisiaca var. odorata, commonly known as the fragrant banana, is a cultivar of the banana plant known for its distinctive aromatic qualities.

This variety produces large, elongated leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The flowers are clustered in a large inflorescence, and the fruit develops in clusters of elongated, curved bananas. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and is cultivated for both its fruit and its fragrant properties.

It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.
