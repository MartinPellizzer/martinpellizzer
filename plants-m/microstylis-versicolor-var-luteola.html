Microstylis versicolor var. luteola

Microstylis versicolor var. luteola, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features slender, upright stems that are often tinged with yellow or orange, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in the late summer and early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full sunlight.
