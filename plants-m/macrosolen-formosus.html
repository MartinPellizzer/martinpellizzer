Macrosolen formosus

Macrosolen formosus, commonly known as the red-tipped photinia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its vibrant red bracts that surround small white flowers. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped with a serrated margin. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated as an ornamental shrub.
