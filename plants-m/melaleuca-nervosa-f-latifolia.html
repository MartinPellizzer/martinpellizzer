Melaleuca nervosa f. latifolia

Melaleuca nervosa f. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf paperbark tree, is a variant of the narrow-leaved paperbark tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of greyish-white bark. The plant produces small, bottle-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, which appear in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions.
