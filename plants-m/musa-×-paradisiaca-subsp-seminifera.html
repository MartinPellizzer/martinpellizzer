Musa × paradisiaca subsp. seminifera

Musa × paradisiaca subsp.

seminifera, commonly known as the banana plant, is a member of the Musaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia. It is a herbaceous plant that can grow up to 6 meters tall, with large, broad leaves that are typically a deep green color.

The plant produces clusters of flowers, which are followed by the familiar elongated fruits known as bananas. It thrives in tropical climates and requires warm temperatures, high humidity, and well-draining soil.

This subspecies is notable for its ability to produce both edible fruit and ornamental value.
