Mercurialis annua var. monoica

Mercurialis annua var. monoica, commonly known as the one-seed mercuryplant, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces a single, elongated seed capsule that matures in late summer or early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
