Mephitidia tubiflora

Mephitidia tubiflora, commonly known as the swamp bladderwort, is a unique aquatic plant found in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures that trap air and help it float on water surfaces. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged or just at the water's edge. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding water and mud.

Mephitidia tubiflora plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small aquatic life.
