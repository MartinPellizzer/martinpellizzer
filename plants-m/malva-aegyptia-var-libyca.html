Malva aegyptia var. libyca

Malva aegyptia var. libyca, commonly known as the Libyan mallows, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers with five petals, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber highlights its cultural and practical significance.
