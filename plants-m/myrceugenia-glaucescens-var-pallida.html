Myrceugenia glaucescens var. pallida

Myrceugenia glaucescens var. pallida, commonly known as the pale myrtle, is a species native to South America, particularly in Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its glossy, pale green to white leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, dark purple berries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

The leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine and have aromatic properties.
