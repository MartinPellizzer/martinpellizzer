Mesospinidium roseum

Mesospinidium roseum, commonly known as the rose-colored spiny moss, is a unique species of liverwort characterized by its delicate, moss-like appearance.

It features small, greenish-yellow thallus with intricate, spiny structures called gemmae that aid in asexual reproduction. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or on damp rocks. Its name derives from the rose-like hue of its reproductive structures, which are typically reddish or pinkish in color.

Mesospinidium roseum plays a vital role in nutrient cycling and soil stabilization in its natural habitat.
