Mammillaria cirrhifera var. longiseta

Mammillaria cirrhifera var. longiseta, commonly known as the long-spined hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, red fruits. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its slow growth rate and unique morphology make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
