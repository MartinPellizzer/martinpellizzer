Melastomastrum afzelii var. dialonkeanum

Melastomastrum afzelii var. dialonkeanum, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and riverine areas. It has opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
