Miconia argyroneura

Miconia argyroneura, commonly known as silverleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery or whitish bloom, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by berry-like fruits that are edible but have a mild flavor. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a problematic species in certain regions.
