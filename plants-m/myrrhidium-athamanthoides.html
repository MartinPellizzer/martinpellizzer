Myrrhidium athamanthoides

Myrrhidium athamanthoides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed myrrh, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Myrrhidium athamanthoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
