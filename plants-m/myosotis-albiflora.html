Myosotis albiflora

Myosotis albiflora, commonly known as the white forget-me-not, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its delicate, five-petaled white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streambanks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Myosotis albiflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
