Mokof punctata

Mokof punctata, commonly known as the spotted mokof, is a unique plant native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy leaves that are often adorned with distinctive spots or streaks. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in shades of white or pale yellow.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, Mokof punctata is considered a rare and sought-after species in botanical collections.
