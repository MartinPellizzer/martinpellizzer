Mammillaria dioica var. armillata

Mammillaria dioica var. armillata, commonly known as the Mexican pincushion cactus, is a compact, globular cactus native to Mexico.

It features a dense covering of white to pale pink spines and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a slow growth rate and is well-suited for rock gardens or as a container plant. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, requiring minimal water to prevent root rot.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
