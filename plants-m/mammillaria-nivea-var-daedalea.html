Mammillaria nivea var. daedalea

Mammillaria nivea var. daedalea, commonly known as the White Ghost Cactus, is a compact, globular cactus with a dense covering of white, woolly spines.

It originates from the arid regions of Mexico, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant features a pale green stem that becomes more prominent as it matures. Its flowers are typically pale yellow or greenish-yellow, appearing in late spring.

It is a slow-growing species that is well-suited for rock gardens or as a container plant.
