Melilotus ornithopodioides

Melilotus ornithopodioides, commonly known as the birdfoot melilot, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces pods that contain several small, rounded seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and herbal applications.
