Myrcia follii

Myrcia follii, commonly known as Brazilian pepper tree, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. Its small, white or pinkish flowers are followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Myrcia follii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual characteristics.
