Mecodium fimbriatum

Mecodium fimbriatum, commonly known as the fringed mecodium, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or inland areas with a Mediterranean climate. The plant features delicate, fimbriated (fringed) leaves that give it its common name. Its flowers are small and usually white or pale blue, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Mecodium fimbriatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and low maintenance requirements.
