Madhuca longifolia var. latifolia

Madhuca longifolia var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved torch tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical, dark green leaves that are often covered in a whitish or silvery pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year, followed by edible, sweet, and sticky drupes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local communities.
