Malus theifera

Malus theifera, commonly known as the common crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, ornamental flowers and fruit. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters, with a rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, turning yellow in the fall.

The fruit, a small apple-like pome, is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
