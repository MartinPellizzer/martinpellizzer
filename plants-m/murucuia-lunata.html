Murucuia lunata

Murucuia lunata, commonly known as the moonflower vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night and close by dawn. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces fragrant blooms that attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in warm climates.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
