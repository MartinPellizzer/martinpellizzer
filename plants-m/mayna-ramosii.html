Mayna ramosii

Mayna ramosii, commonly known as the Mexican flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a distinctive trunk with a rough, fissured bark. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

Mayna ramosii thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
