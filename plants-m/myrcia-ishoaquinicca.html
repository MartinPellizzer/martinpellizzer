Myrcia ishoaquinicca

Myrcia ishoaquinicca, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a small, round berry that contains several seeds.

Myrcia ishoaquinicca is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
