Myrcia cujabensis var. longifolia

Myrcia cujabensis var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved myrcia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the rainforests of northern South America, particularly in regions of Brazil and Venezuela. The plant is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but often have a strong, spicy flavor.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.
