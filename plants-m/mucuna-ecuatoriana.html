Mucuna ecuatoriana

Mucuna ecuatoriana, commonly known as the Ecuadorian velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, hairy pods that contain small, dark seeds. The plant produces a rich source of protein and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
