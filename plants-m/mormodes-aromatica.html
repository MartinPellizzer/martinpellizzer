Mormodes aromatica

Mormodes aromatica, commonly known as the aromatic orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has a slender, erect stem with a few leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique fragrance.
