Microstylis muscifera var. stelostachya

Microstylis muscifera var. stelostachya, commonly known as the narrow-leaf marsh sedge, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences with inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in temperate regions.

Its botanical structure supports its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
