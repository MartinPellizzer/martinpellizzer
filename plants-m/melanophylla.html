Melanophylla

Melanophylla, commonly known as the blackleaf plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Malvaceae.

It is characterized by its dark green to almost black foliage, which gives it its common name. The leaves are typically large, ovate, and have a velvety texture. Melanophylla species are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

They produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are often followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.
