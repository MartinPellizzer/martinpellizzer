Menispermum palmatum

Menispermum palmatum, commonly known as Indian snakeroot, is a woody perennial vine native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its palmately compound leaves that resemble a hand. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its roots and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported medicinal properties.
