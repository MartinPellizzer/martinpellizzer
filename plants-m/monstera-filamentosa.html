Monstera filamentosa

Monstera filamentosa, commonly known as the "Filamentous Monster Vine," is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that develop fenestrations, or holes, as the plant matures. The leaves are typically a deep green with a velvety texture, and the plant produces small, pale green flowers followed by orange berries. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-draining soil and indirect sunlight.

Monstera filamentosa is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ability to climb or trail.
