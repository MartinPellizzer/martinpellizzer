Mikania oreopola

Mikania oreopola, commonly known as the mountain Mikania, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and yellow, often clustered in spherical inflorescences.

Mikania oreopola is adapted to cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests and alpine meadows.
