Meniscium × thwaitesii

Meniscium × thwaitesii, commonly known as the hybrid meadow grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it typically thrives in moist, open habitats.

Meniscium × thwaitesii is often found in meadows, damp woodlands, and along stream banks.
