Mattia graeca

Mattia graeca, commonly known as the Sicilian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically blue or purple in color. The leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
