Meliclis

Meliclis, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often overlooked due to its prominent foliage. Meliclis is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a challenge for agricultural management.
