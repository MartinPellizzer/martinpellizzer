Miconia carpishana

Miconia carpishana, commonly known as the red miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains numerous seeds.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is considered a problematic species in many regions.
