Morysia

Morysia, commonly known as the silverleaf morysia, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. Morysia thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in xerophytic plant adaptations.
