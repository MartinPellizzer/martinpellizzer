Myrtus striatula

Myrtus striatula, commonly known as the striated myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often marked with distinct white or pale stripes. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, which are followed by dark blue berries. Myrtus striatula thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

It is also valued for its aromatic properties and historical use in traditional medicine.
