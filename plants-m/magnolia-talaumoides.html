Magnolia talaumoides

Magnolia talaumoides, commonly known as the Mexican magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Magnoliaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its bark is smooth and grayish, becoming fissured with age.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its floral display.
