Melilotus laevis

Melilotus laevis, commonly known as silky melilot, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces pods containing several small, rounded seeds, and it is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. Silky melilot is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
