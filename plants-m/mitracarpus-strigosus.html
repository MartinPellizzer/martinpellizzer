Mitracarpus strigosus

Mitracarpus strigosus, commonly known as the hairy mitracarpus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of pink to red, and it produces distinctive, spiny seed pods.

Mitracarpus strigosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and bloom.
