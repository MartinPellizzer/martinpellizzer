Mapania cuspidata var. angustifolia

Mapania cuspidata var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mapania, is a variant of the mapania plant characterized by its slender, elongated leaves.

It is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, tropical environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, adding to its ornamental appeal. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a smooth, glossy surface, contributing to its visual distinctiveness.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
