Medinilla robusticaulis

Medinilla robusticaulis, commonly known as the robust stalked medinilla, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has thick, sturdy stems and broad, leathery leaves that help it thrive in humid, warm climates. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to tolerate partial shade.

Medinilla robusticaulis is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
