Meconopsis chelidoniifolia

Meconopsis chelidoniifolia, commonly known as the Welsh poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Wales and western England.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive heart-shaped base. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Meconopsis chelidoniifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is considered a rare and delicate species.
