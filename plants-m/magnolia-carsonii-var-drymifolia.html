Magnolia carsonii var. drymifolia

Magnolia carsonii var. drymifolia, commonly known as the dryland magnolia, is a rare and highly ornamental plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and large, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping. Its slow growth rate and compact form make it suitable for small gardens or rockeries.

Botanically, it is noted for its unique floral structure and resilience to drought conditions.
