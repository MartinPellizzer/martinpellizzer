Mentha × villosa f. burckhardtiana

Mentha × villosa f. burckhardtiana, commonly known as Burckhardt's hairy mint, is a hybrid species within the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and aromatic, often fragrant foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodland areas or along stream banks. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
