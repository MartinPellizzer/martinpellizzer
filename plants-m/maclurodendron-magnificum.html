Maclurodendron magnificum

Maclurodendron magnificum, commonly known as the magnificent maclurodendron, is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, often displaying vivid variegation of green, white, and gold. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and purple, which bloom in late spring. Its roots are known for their medicinal properties, containing compounds used in traditional herbal remedies.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is currently listed as an endangered species.
