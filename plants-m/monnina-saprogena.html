Monnina saprogena

Monnina saprogena, commonly known as the swamp milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, often saturated soils and is often found in marshy or swampy environments.

Monnina saprogena plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and provides habitat for various insect species.
