Microchilus trifasciatus

Microchilus trifasciatus, commonly known as the three-banded fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are arranged in three distinct bands along the fronds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
