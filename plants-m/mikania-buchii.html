Mikania buchii

Mikania buchii, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a vigorous vine native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively through runners and seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in disturbed areas.

Due to its invasive nature, it can outcompete native vegetation in certain regions.
