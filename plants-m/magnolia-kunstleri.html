Magnolia kunstleri

Magnolia kunstleri, commonly known as the Kunstler's magnolia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its bark is typically grayish-brown and becomes fissured with age.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in warm climates for its striking floral display.
