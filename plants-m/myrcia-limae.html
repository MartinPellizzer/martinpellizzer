Myrcia limae

Myrcia limae, commonly known as Brazilian peppertree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is known for its aromatic properties.

Myrcia limae is also recognized for its role in local ecosystems and its potential economic value.
