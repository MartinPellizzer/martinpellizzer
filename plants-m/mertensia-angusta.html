Mertensia angusta

Mertensia angusta, commonly known as narrowleaf bluebells, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features striking, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, downy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Mertensia angusta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
