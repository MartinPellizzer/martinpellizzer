Magnolia fordiana

Magnolia fordiana, commonly known as the Chinese magnolia or the white magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves appear. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a slight roughness as it ages.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual appeal.
