Melpomene tsatchelae

Melpomene tsatchelae, commonly known as the Red Violet of Madagascar, is a strikingly beautiful flowering plant native to the island nation of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant purple to red flowers, which bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest undergrowth where it thrives in moist, well-drained soil. Its leaves are ovate and covered in a fine pubescence, while its stems are slender and somewhat woody.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
