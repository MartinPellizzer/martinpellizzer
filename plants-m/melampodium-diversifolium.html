Melampodium diversifolium

Melampodium diversifolium, commonly known as the diverse-leaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and prairies. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Melampodium diversifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
