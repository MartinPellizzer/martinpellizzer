Malus butigniana

Malus butigniana, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces small, sweet, edible fruits that are similar to pears but often used in traditional Chinese medicine. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
