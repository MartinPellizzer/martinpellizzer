Malpighia uniflora

Malpighia uniflora, commonly known as the Mexican hibiscus or white coral tree, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its flowers are showy, with five white petals and a yellow center, and are borne singly or in small clusters.

The fruit is a small, bright red berry that contains several seeds, making it a notable ornamental and edible plant.
