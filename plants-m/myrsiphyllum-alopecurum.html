Myrsiphyllum alopecurum

Myrsiphyllum alopecurum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf myrsine, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Myrsinaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Myrsiphyllum alopecurum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage.
