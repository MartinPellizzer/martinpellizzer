Michelia champaca var. blumei

Michelia champaca var. blumei, commonly known as the golden champa or blume's champaca, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its aromatic flowers in horticultural settings.
