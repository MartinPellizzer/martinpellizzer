Mimosa monilifera

Mimosa monilifera, commonly known as the weeping mimosa, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its feathery, soft green foliage and delicate, fern-like leaves that are compound and pinnate. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its rapid growth and spreading habit, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
