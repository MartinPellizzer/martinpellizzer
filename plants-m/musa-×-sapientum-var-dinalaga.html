Musa × sapientum var. dinalaga

Musa × sapientum var. dinalaga, commonly known as the Dinalaga banana, is a cultivar of the genus Musa native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elongated, and yellowish-pink fruits, which are typically harvested when they are still green. The plant is a herbaceous perennial with broad, lance-shaped leaves that form a pseudostem. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and is often used in both culinary and ornamental contexts.
