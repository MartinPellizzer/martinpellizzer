Megaclinium arnoldianum

Megaclinium arnoldianum, commonly known as the Arnold's giant grass, is a tall perennial grass native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its robust, erect stems and large, narrow leaves that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, which are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous grasslands.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in temperate gardens.
