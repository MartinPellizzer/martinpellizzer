Megalopanax rex

Megalopanax rex, commonly known as the royal giant aralia, is a striking member of the Araliaceae family native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, making it one of the largest leafy plants in the world. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by bright red berries, which are an important food source for various wildlife. Its unique morphology and impressive size make it a standout in botanical gardens and a subject of scientific interest.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of moisture and nutrient-rich soil to flourish.
