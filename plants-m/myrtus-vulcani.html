Myrtus vulcani

Myrtus vulcani, commonly known as the Sicilian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, dark green leaves and small white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and rocky gardens.

Myrtus vulcani is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
