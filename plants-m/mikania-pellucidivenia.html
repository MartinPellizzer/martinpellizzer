Mikania pellucidivenia

Mikania pellucidivenia, commonly known as the glabrous milkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces fluffy, white seeds with silky pappi that aid in wind dispersal. Its leaves are typically opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Mikania pellucidivenia is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open fields.
