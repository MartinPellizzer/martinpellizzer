Myrcia dilucida

Myrcia dilucida, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Myrcia dilucida thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its aromatic properties and ornamental value make it a popular plant in horticulture.
