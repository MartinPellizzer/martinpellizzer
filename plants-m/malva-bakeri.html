Malva bakeri

Malva bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant produces showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are broad and lobed, often with a velvety texture.

Malva bakeri is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
