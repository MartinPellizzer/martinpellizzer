Momordica schliebenii

Momordica schliebenii, commonly known as the Schlieben's bitter melon, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and pumpkins. The plant produces distinctive, spiny fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, while the flowers are yellow with five petals.

The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
