Mentha longibracteata

Mentha longibracteata, commonly known as the long bract mint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Mentha longibracteata is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential digestive and calming properties.
