Mentha viridis var. crispa

Mentha viridis var. crispa, commonly known as the curled mint or woolly mint, is a variant of the green mint plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curled leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Mentha viridis var. crispa is valued for its aromatic leaves and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
