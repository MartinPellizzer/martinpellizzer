Mentha longifolia var. oxeilema

Mentha longifolia var. oxeilema, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and fragrant, pale purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a strong, minty aroma due to the presence of essential oils in its leaves and stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and herbal cultivation.

This variety is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
