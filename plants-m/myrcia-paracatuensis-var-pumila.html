Myrcia paracatuensis var. pumila

Myrcia paracatuensis var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf myrcia, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring small, elliptical leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or reddish in color, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
