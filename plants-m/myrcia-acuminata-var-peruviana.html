Myrcia acuminata var. peruviana

Myrcia acuminata var. peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian myrtle, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Peru and Ecuador.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but not widely consumed by humans. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
