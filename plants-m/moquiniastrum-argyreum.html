Moquiniastrum argyreum

Moquiniastrum argyreum, commonly known as the silverleaf watermilfoil, is a aquatic plant native to North America.

It has finely divided, feather-like leaves that are typically covered in a silvery or whitish coating, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. Its small, green flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences and bloom in late summer.

Moquiniastrum argyreum plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
