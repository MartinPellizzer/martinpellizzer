Melanthium blandum

Melanthium blandum, commonly known as the slender milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are slender and often reddish in color.

Melanthium blandum is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for various pollinators.
