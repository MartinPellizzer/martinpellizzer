Mimosa subvestita

Mimosa subvestita, commonly known as the velvet mimosa, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different environments. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, velvety texture, which gives it its common name.

Mimosa subvestita is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its aesthetic qualities.
