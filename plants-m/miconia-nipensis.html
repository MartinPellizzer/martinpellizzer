Miconia nipensis

Miconia nipensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian miconia, is a member of the Melastomataceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to the Hawaiian Islands, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences with white to pinkish flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are fleshy berries that contain numerous seeds, facilitating its spread.

Miconia nipensis is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various habitats, making it an important species in Hawaiian ecology.
