Melica patagonica

Melica patagonica, commonly known as Patagonian grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet at the tip of each stem, which is a distinctive feature of the genus Melica.

Melica patagonica is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
