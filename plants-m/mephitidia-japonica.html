Mephitidia japonica

Mephitidia japonica, commonly known as the Japanese stinkhorn, is a unique fungal plant that produces a strong, foul odor to attract flies for spore dispersal.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as decaying wood or soil. The fruiting body of this plant is characterized by a fleshy, bulbous base and a stalk topped with a spore-producing cap. Its reproductive strategy relies heavily on insect pollination, which is unusual for fungi.

The plant plays an important ecological role in decomposing organic matter and recycling nutrients in forest ecosystems.
