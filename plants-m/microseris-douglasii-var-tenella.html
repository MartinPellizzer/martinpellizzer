Microseris douglasii var. tenella

Microseris douglasii var. tenella, commonly known as the narrowleaf biscuitroot, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, spherical heads. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its fibrous roots are used by native peoples for food and medicinal purposes.
