Mephitidia capitata var. ferruginea

Mephitidia capitata var. ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty-headed mephit, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown foliage.

It is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a waxy texture, and its flowers are small and clustered, often with a subtle fragrance. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
