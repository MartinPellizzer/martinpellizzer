Modecca macrophylla

Modecca macrophylla, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, fiery orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces distinctive, woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The botanical features of Modecca macrophylla make it a striking addition to gardens and landscapes.
