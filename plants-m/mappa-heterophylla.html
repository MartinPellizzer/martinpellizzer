Mappa heterophylla

Mappa heterophylla, commonly known as the variable leaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its variable leaf shapes, which can range from ovate to heart-shaped, depending on environmental conditions. The plant produces showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Mappa heterophylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to different climates.
