Microsaccus griffithii

Microsaccus griffithii, commonly known as the Kashmir mountain bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, preferring well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Microsaccus griffithii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
