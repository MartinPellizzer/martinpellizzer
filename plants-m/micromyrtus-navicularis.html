Micromyrtus navicularis

Micromyrtus navicularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf melaleuca, is a small evergreen shrub native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often in areas with high salinity and wind exposure. Its small, lance-shaped leaves are glossy and have a distinct, spicy scent when crushed.

The flowers are usually white or pink, arranged in clusters, and attract various pollinators such as bees and birds.
