Mimulus alsinoides

Mimulus alsinoides, commonly known as the yellow monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that resemble small monkey faces, which are typically about 1 to 2 inches in diameter. The plant grows from a basal rosette of leaves and has slender, branching stems that bear the showy inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, streambanks, and wetlands.

Mimulus alsinoides is a perennial species that blooms from spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
