Malaxis lagotis

Malaxis lagotis, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant features a slender, upright stem with a single, lance-shaped leaf. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in a spike, often with a faint fragrance.

Malaxis lagotis is known for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
