Malea pilosa

Malea pilosa, commonly known as the hairy malea, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
