Myrtus corticosa

Myrtus corticosa, commonly known as the rough myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, dark green leaves that are covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. Its flowers are typically pale pink or white, and it produces small, spherical berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Myrtus corticosa is often used in traditional medicine and has a pleasant, aromatic scent.
