Mentha longifolia var. galatae

Mentha longifolia var. galatae, commonly known as Galician mint, is a variety of the mint family (Lamiaceae) native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies and culinary applications due to their refreshing scent.

This variant is distinguished by its compact growth habit and unique floral morphology compared to other mint species.
