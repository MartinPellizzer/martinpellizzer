Marianthemum spathulatum

Marianthemum spathulatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its unique nocturnal blooming habit adds an enchanting element to the landscape.
