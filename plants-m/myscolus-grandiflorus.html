Myscolus grandiflorus

Myscolus grandiflorus, commonly known as the grandiflorous mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that thrives on a variety of host trees.

It possesses specialized structures called haustoria that allow it to extract water and nutrients from its host. The plant produces small, white or pale green berries that are often consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. Its leaves are typically broad and opposite, with a waxy surface that reduces water loss.

While it can be invasive in some regions, it plays a role in supporting local wildlife by providing food and shelter.
