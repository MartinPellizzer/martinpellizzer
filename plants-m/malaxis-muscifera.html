Malaxis muscifera

Malaxis muscifera, commonly known as the mossy orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its root system is adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil, and it forms symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi.

The species is notable for its unique appearance and ecological significance in its native habitat.
