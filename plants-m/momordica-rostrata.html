Momordica rostrata

Momordica rostrata, commonly known as the horned cucumber, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, horn-shaped fruit that resembles a miniature horn. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers and has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit, which has a slightly bitter taste.

The fruit is used in various cuisines and is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
