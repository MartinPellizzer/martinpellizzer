Mimulus purpureus

Mimulus purpureus, commonly known as the purple monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to reddish-pink flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils along streambanks and forest edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem.

Mimulus purpureus is often found in disturbed habitats and is considered an indicator species for wetland environments.
