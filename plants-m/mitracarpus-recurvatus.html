Mitracarpus recurvatus

Mitracarpus recurvatus, commonly known as the spiral senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release seeds.

Mitracarpus recurvatus is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
