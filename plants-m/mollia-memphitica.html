Mollia memphitica

Mollia memphitica, commonly known as the Egyptian water lily, is a flowering plant native to the Nile River region.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has a unique ability to thrive in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it in the mud, while its leaves form a floating platform.

Mollia memphitica plays an important ecological role by providing habitat for aquatic life and contributing to water filtration.
