Montbretia concolor

Montbretia concolor, commonly known as the yellow oxalis, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features showy, orange-yellow flowers with a distinctive red center, often arranged in clusters atop sturdy stems. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a delicate, fern-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Montbretia concolor is known for its long blooming period, typically from late spring to early summer.
