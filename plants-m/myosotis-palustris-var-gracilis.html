Myosotis palustris var. gracilis

Myosotis palustris var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender marsh violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland environments in North America.

It features slender stems and small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, acidic soils. It is often found in bogs, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
