Mespilus oxyacantha var. obovata

Mespilus oxyacantha var. obovata, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate to obovate leaves, and white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, yellow or orange berries in the autumn, which are edible but often tart.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in hedgerows and gardens.
