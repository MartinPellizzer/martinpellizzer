Mikania microcephala

Mikania microcephala, commonly known as the small-headed morning glory, is a vigorous climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a rough texture and produces small, showy flowers that range from white to pale purple. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it both ornamental and invasive in some regions. Its flowers typically bloom in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
