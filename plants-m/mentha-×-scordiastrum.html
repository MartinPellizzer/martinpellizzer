Mentha × scordiastrum

Mentha × scordiastrum, commonly known as catnip, is a hybrid plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. Mentha × scordiastrum is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its aromatic properties and historical use in herbal remedies.
