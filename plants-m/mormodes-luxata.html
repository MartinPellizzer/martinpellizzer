Mormodes luxata

Mormodes luxata, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a striking tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and a distinctive star-shaped lip, which are key botanical features. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often displaying a mix of yellow and green hues.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections worldwide.
