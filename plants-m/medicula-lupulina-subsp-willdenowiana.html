Medicula lupulina subsp. willdenowiana

Medicula lupulina subsp.

willdenowiana, commonly known as the willdenow's golden saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers.

The plant is typically found in rocky or gravelly habitats, often growing in full sun. Its flowers bloom in spring and are pollinated by insects such as bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
