Meridiana elliptica

Meridiana elliptica, commonly known as the Madagascar violets, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are elliptical in shape, hence the species name, and often display a waxy, glossy texture.

The flowers come in a variety of colors, including purple, pink, and white, and are known for their unique, spurred shape.
