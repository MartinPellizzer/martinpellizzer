Melocanna longispiculata

Melocanna longispiculata, commonly known as the long-spined bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, clump-forming grass that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant features long, slender culms with prominent, sharp spines along the nodes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and structural characteristics in horticulture.
