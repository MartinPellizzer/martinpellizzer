Myosotis macrosiphon

Myosotis macrosiphon, commonly known as the giant water violet, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and stream banks, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often floating on the water's surface.

The species is notable for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
