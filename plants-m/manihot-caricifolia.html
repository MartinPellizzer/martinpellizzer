Manihot caricifolia

Manihot caricifolia, commonly known as the African arrowroot or swamp arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that grows in wet, marshy environments. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its roots are edible and have a starchy texture similar to yams.

It is often cultivated for its nutritional value and medicinal properties.
