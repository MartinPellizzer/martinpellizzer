Malvastrum plumosum var. atacamense

Malvastrum plumosum var. atacamense, commonly known as the Atacama mallows, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a compact growth habit and is adapted to survive in extreme drought conditions. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environments.
