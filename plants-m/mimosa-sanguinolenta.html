Mimosa sanguinolenta

Mimosa sanguinolenta, commonly known as the blood mimosa, is a strikingly colorful shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright red and orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a visually appealing display. The plant produces small, fern-like leaves that are sensitive to touch, folding shut when disturbed, a trait known as thigmonasty. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Additionally, its unique coloration and adaptive features make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
