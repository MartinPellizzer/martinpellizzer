Magnolia rosea

Magnolia rosea, commonly known as the rose magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The flowers are typically pink to white in color, with a velvety texture and a distinctive rose-like appearance. The tree has smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves that turn yellow in the fall.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
