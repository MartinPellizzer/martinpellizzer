Monstera pinnatifida

Monstera pinnatifida, commonly known as the Swiss cheese plant, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that develop fenestrations, or holes, as they mature. The plant produces small, spadix-shaped inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of the Araceae family. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and climb vertically, often reaching several meters in height.

The leaves are typically a deep green with a waxy texture, making it a popular ornamental plant in indoor gardening.
