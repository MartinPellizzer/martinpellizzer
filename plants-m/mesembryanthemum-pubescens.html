Mesembryanthemum pubescens

Mesembryanthemum pubescens, commonly known as the woolly ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, ribbed stems and leaves covered in fine, white hairs that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to store water in its leaves makes it drought-tolerant and suitable for arid environments.
