Myrcia nervata

Myrcia nervata, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and red or purple berries. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves with prominent veins, and it often grows in moist, shaded environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties.

Myrcia nervata plays an important role in local ecosystems, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
