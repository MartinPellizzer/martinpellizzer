Mentha × nicholsoniana

Mentha × nicholsoniana, commonly known as the hybrid mint, is a cross between Mentha viridiflora and Mentha arvensis.

It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its aromatic leaves and flowering spikes. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

The essential oils produced by the plant are widely used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.
