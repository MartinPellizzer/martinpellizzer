Myanthus deltoideus

Myanthus deltoideus, commonly known as the delta mallow, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
