Meyerocactus

Meyerocactus, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a type of cactus that belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its tall, columnar stems. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, apple-like fruits. Meyerocactus is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to produce edible fruits.
