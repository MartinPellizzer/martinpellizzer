Matthiola longipetala subsp. bicornis

Matthiola longipetala subsp.

bicornis, commonly known as the two-horned stock, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, four-petaled flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple.

The subspecies is distinguished by the presence of two prominent, elongated lobes on the petals, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
