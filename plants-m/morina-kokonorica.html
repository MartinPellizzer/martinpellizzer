Morina kokonorica

Morina kokonorica, commonly known as the Tibetan moringa, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is adapted to cold and arid conditions, showcasing resilience to harsh environmental factors. It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of alpine flora.
