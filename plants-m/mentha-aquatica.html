Mentha aquatica

Mentha aquatica, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist environments such as stream banks, wet meadows, and marshy areas. The plant has square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are often aromatic. Its flowers are small, pale blue to violet, and arranged in whorls along the stems.

Mentha aquatica is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in herbal teas and traditional remedies.
