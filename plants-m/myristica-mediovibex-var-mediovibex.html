Myristica mediovibex var. mediovibex

Myristica mediovibex var. mediovibex, commonly known as the Malabar nut or wild nutmeg, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of India and Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark brown seeds that resemble nutmeg. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by yellowish fruits containing the aromatic seeds. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a distinct fragrance.

The tree is valued for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
