Mezilaurus triunca

Mezilaurus triunca, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and broad, palmate leaves. The plant produces striking orange-red flowers in clusters during the dry season, which are followed by distinctive fruit pods. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
