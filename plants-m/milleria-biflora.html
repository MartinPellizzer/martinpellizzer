Milleria biflora

Milleria biflora, commonly known as the yellow milleria, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to gardens. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Milleria biflora is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
