Myrtus berteroana

Myrtus berteroana, commonly known as the Bertero's myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Andes mountains of South America, specifically in countries like Peru and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Myrtus berteroana is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
