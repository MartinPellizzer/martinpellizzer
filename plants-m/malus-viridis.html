Malus viridis

Malus viridis, commonly known as the green apple tree, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to parts of eastern Asia, including China and Korea. The plant is characterized by its small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are ovate and have a smooth texture, while the fruit is a small, sour apple that ripens in late summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both its flowers and fruit.
