Mucuna gracilipes

Mucuna gracilipes, commonly known as the slender velvetbean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, slender pods containing small, dark seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Mucuna gracilipes is notable for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it valuable in agricultural systems.
