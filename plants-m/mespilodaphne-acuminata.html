Mespilodaphne acuminata

Mespilodaphne acuminata, commonly known as the Indian date palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, sweet fruits similar to dates. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is often cultivated in gardens and for commercial purposes.
