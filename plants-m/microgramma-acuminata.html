Microgramma acuminata

Microgramma acuminata, commonly known as the slender maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Blechnaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and have a finely toothed margin. Native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, it thrives in shaded, moist environments. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit and produces small, spore-bearing structures called soruses on the underside of its fronds.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and greenhouse cultivation.
