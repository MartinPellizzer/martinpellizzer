Miconia grossidentata

Miconia grossidentata, commonly known as the broad-leaf miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers with five petals, often arranged in clusters. It is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain regions, making it a concern for ecological management.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous small, winged seeds.
