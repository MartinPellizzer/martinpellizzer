Miconia leapallida

Miconia leapallida, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a dense layer of soft, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are often followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Miconia leapallida is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a common invasive species in certain regions.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a challenge for land managers.
