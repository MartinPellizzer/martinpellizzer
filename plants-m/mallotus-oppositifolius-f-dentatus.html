Mallotus oppositifolius f. dentatus

Mallotus oppositifolius f. dentatus, commonly known as the serrated mallotus, is a variant of the mallotus plant characterized by its distinctive serrated leaf margins.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture with prominent veins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage.
