Myrrhinium sarcopetalum

Myrrhinium sarcopetalum, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive scent. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils, preferring cool, shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while the flowers are borne in clusters at the base of the plant.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
