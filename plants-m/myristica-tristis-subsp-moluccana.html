Myristica tristis subsp. moluccana

Myristica tristis subsp.

moluccana, commonly known as the Moluccan nutmeg, is a small evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia. It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is closely related to the nutmeg tree, Myristica fragrans.

The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that give way to red, fleshy berries. The seeds, or nuts, are enclosed in a thick, aromatic aril and are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

This subspecies is valued for its unique botanical features and economic importance in the region.
