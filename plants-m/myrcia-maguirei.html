Myrcia maguirei

Myrcia maguirei, commonly known as the Maguire's myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, often with a waxy coating, while its flowers are usually white or pale pink.

Myrcia maguirei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.
