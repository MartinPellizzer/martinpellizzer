Muhlenbergia dubia

Muhlenbergia dubia, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silvery-blue foliage and delicate, fluffy seed heads that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. It is often found in wetlands, prairies, and along stream banks.

Muhlenbergia dubia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
