Myrcia myriantha

Myrcia myriantha, commonly known as the Brazilian beauty or myrtle myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Myrcia myriantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
