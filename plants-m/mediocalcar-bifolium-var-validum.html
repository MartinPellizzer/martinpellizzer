Mediocalcar bifolium var. validum

Mediocalcar bifolium var. validum, commonly known as the two-leaved sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It features a compact rosette of fleshy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are typically oval or spatulate in shape, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

This variant is distinguished by its robust growth and adaptability to sandy soils.
