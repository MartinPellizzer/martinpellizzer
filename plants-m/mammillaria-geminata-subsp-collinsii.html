Mammillaria geminata subsp. collinsii

Mammillaria geminata subsp.

collinsii, commonly known as the Collins' mammillaria, is a small cactus native to Mexico. It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and smooth, green to blue-green stems.

The plant produces white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It has a dense covering of white radial spines and is well adapted to arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
