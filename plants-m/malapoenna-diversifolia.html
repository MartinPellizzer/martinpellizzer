Malapoenna diversifolia

Malapoenna diversifolia, commonly known as the Malapoenna bush, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which consist of multiple leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
