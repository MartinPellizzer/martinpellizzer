Macrotropis bancana

Macrotropis bancana, commonly known as the Bankan tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous structure and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, often used in traditional cuisines. Its leaves are compound, typically consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland areas with a warm climate.
