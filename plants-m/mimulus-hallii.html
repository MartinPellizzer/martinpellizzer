Mimulus hallii

Mimulus hallii, commonly known as the Hall's monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streams and in meadows. Mimulus hallii has heart-shaped leaves and is part of the Phrymaceae family.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
