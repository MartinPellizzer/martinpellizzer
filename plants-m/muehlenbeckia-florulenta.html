Muehlenbeckia florulenta

Muehlenbeckia florulenta, commonly known as the silverleaf creeper, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a trailing perennial that produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers. The plant has slender, flexible stems that can root at the nodes, allowing it to spread rapidly. Its foliage is characterized by silvery-gray leaves with a fuzzy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and ability to cover ground effectively.
