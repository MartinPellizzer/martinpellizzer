Miconia sulbahiensis

Miconia sulbahiensis, commonly known as the Brazilian star-miconia, is a species of flowering plant in the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves and showy, clustered pink or red flowers. It produces small, dark blue berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
