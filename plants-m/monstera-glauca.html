Monstera glauca

Monstera glauca, commonly known as the Variegated Monstera, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that feature a distinctive variegation of light green and white patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers followed by black, berry-like fruits. Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Monstera glauca thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
