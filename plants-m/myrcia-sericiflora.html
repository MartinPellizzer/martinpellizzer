Myrcia sericiflora

Myrcia sericiflora, commonly known as the silky myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are glossy and lance-shaped, while its flowers are clustered in inflorescences.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
