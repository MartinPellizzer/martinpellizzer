Microcos grandiflora

Microcos grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered microcos, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture and produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The flowers are borne on long, slender stalks and bloom in the summer months.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions.
