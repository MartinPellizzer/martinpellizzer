Meyeria hispida

Meyeria hispida, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Meyeria hispida is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
