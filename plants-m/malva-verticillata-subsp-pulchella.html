Malva verticillata subsp. pulchella

Malva verticillata subsp.

pulchella, commonly known as the purple mallow, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, riverbanks, and woodland edges. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like cotton and hibiscus.

The subspecies pulchella is distinguished by its more vibrant flower color and slightly different leaf morphology compared to the main species.
