Mentha × intermedia

Mentha × intermedia, commonly known as peppermint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between watermint and spearmint.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of 30–90 cm. The plant has square, hollow stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a serrated margin. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Peppermint is known for its strong, aromatic scent and is widely cultivated for its essential oils and medicinal properties.
