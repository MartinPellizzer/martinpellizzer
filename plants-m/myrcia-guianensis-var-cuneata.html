Myrcia guianensis var. cuneata

Myrcia guianensis var. cuneata, commonly known as the Guianan myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil, Colombia, and Venezuela. The plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
