Miscanthus matsudae var. glabrescens

Miscanthus matsudae var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth Japanese silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and fluffy, silver-gray plumes that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive silver sheen on the upper surface.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes due to its elegant appearance and seasonal interest.
