Manihot guyanensis

Manihot guyanensis, commonly known as the Guyana arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces edible tubers underground, similar to cassava. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves and is often grown for its starchy roots. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is adaptable to various climates.

The tubers are used in cooking and have a mild, slightly sweet flavor.
