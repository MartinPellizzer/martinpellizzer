Monarda punctata subsp. stanfieldii

Monarda punctata subsp.

stanfieldii, commonly known as the spotted beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs.

The plant is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular purple or pink flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and meadows.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
