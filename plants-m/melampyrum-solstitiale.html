Melampyrum solstitiale

Melampyrum solstitiale, commonly known as the summer pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Oxyales order and belongs to the genus Melampyrum within the family Oxyaceae. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands, roadsides, and woodland edges.

Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
