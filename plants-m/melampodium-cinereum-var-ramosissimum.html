Melampodium cinereum var. ramosissimum

Melampodium cinereum var. ramosissimum, commonly known as the silverleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of yellow to orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, whitish hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
