Myrciaria leucophloea

Myrciaria leucophloea, commonly known as white-flowered myrcia, is a species of shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its white or pale yellow flowers and leathery, elliptical leaves. It typically grows in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Myrciaria leucophloea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
