Macropsychanthus grandistipulus

Macropsychanthus grandistipulus, commonly known as the grand-stippled psychanthus, is a unique member of the Psychantheaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, large, and velvety leaves that are adorned with prominent, elongated stipules. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is a sought-after specimen in botanical gardens and specialized collections.
