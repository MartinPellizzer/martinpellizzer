Malva decumbens

Malva decumbens, commonly known as the prostrate mallows, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Americas.

It features sprawling stems that often lie flat on the ground, with heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy pink or purple flowers with five petals, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its attractive foliage and flowers, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
