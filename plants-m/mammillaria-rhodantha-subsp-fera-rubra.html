Mammillaria rhodantha subsp. fera-rubra

Mammillaria rhodantha subsp.

fera-rubra, commonly known as the red-tipped mammillaria, is a succulent cactus native to Mexico. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in white to grayish spines.

The flowers are usually pink or red, adding a striking contrast to the greenish-gray body of the plant. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright sunlight to flourish.

This subspecies is prized for its compact size and ornamental value in rock gardens and as a potted plant.
