Mutisia heliantha

Mutisia heliantha, commonly known as the golden mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and dry forest habitats. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, showy blooms.
