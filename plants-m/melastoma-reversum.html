Melastoma reversum

Melastoma reversum, commonly known as the reverse melastoma, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its distinctive, often showy flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.
