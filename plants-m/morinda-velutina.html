Morinda velutina

Morinda velutina, commonly known as velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a drupe, typically containing one or two seeds, and it has a distinctive reddish-brown color when mature.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a velvety texture, which gives the plant its common name.
