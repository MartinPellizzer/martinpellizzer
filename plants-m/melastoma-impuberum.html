Melastoma impuberum

Melastoma impuberum, commonly known as the white-flowered melastoma, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a distinctive red calyx. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.
