Myriophyllum isoetophilum

Myriophyllum isoetophilum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often submerged, though they may emerge near the water's surface. It is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes.

Myriophyllum isoetophilum plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
