Maxillaria poicilothece

Maxillaria poicilothece, commonly known as the violet lady's slipper orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its pseudobulbs and linear leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive violet coloration and intricate patterns. Its blooming period typically occurs during the warmer months, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Maxillaria poicilothece is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
