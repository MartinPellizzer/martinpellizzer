Mentha foroiulensis

Mentha foroiulensis, commonly known as the Romanian mint, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a serrated edge and emit a strong menthol scent when crushed.

Mentha foroiulensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
