Murbeckia

Murbeckia, commonly known as the "blue-eyed grass," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Xanthorrhoeaceae.

These plants are native to southern Africa and are characterized by their small, delicate flowers that often have a blue or purple hue. Murbeckia species typically grow in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. They produce showy inflorescences that resemble grass-like structures, which is why they are sometimes referred to as "blue-eyed grass.".

The genus includes several species, some of which are cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens.
