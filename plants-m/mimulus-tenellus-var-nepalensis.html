Mimulus tenellus var. nepalensis

Mimulus tenellus var. nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and India.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, rocky habitats at high altitudes. The plant features bright orange to yellow flowers with five petals, which are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in its native environment.
