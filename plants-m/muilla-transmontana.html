Muilla transmontana

Muilla transmontana, commonly known as the mountain muilla, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, fibrous stems. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from white to pale yellow.

Muilla transmontana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens for its resilience and aesthetic qualities.
