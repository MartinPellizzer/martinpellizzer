Mormodes elegans

Mormodes elegans, commonly known as the elegant orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually broad and strap-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
