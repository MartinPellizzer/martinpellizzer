Masdevallia picea

Masdevallia picea, commonly known as the Black Orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green to almost black foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a velvety texture. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires high light intensity but protection from direct sunlight.

Masdevallia picea is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in specialized botanical gardens.
