Marrubium orientale

Marrubium orientale, commonly known as oriental horehound, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 60 cm tall, featuring gray-green, hairy leaves and small blue or purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its strong, bitter scent and contains compounds such as marrubium, which has been used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and herbal remedies.
