Mentha × heleonastes

Mentha × heleonastes, commonly known as the hybrid mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a cross between Mentha longifolia (spearmint) and Mentha × piperita (peppermint), resulting in a plant with a strong, aromatic scent. The leaves are typically oval-shaped, serrated, and have a glossy green appearance. It produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or pink, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This plant is often cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy, perfumes, and medicinal applications.
