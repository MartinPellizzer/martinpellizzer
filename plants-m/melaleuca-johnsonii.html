Melaleuca johnsonii

Melaleuca johnsonii, commonly known as Johnson's melaleuca, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub native to the northern regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often in heathlands and woodland areas. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or white, and it produces woody capsules that contain numerous small seeds.

Melaleuca johnsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
