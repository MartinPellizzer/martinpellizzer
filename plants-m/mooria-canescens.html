Mooria canescens

Mooria canescens, commonly known as the silver leaf tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, inconspicuous white flowers. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

Mooria canescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
