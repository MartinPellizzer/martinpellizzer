Maughania paniculata

Maughania paniculata, commonly known as the tropical panicled grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
