Monanthos integer

Monanthos integer, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
