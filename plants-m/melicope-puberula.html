Melicope puberula

Melicope puberula, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by spherical, yellowish fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal and lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
