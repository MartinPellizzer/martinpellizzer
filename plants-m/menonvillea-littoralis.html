Menonvillea littoralis

Menonvillea littoralis, commonly known as the coastal menonvillea, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and is typically found in sandy or rocky coastal habitats. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds.

Menonvillea littoralis plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
