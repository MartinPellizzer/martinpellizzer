Massonia sanguinea

Massonia sanguinea, commonly known as the red-stemmed massonia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that are often tinged with a reddish hue. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink, typically during the cooler months. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to store water in arid environments.

Massonia sanguinea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
