Myrtus fasciculata

Myrtus fasciculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, dark green leaves that are arranged in dense clusters. The plant produces small, white flowers that are highly fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a small, dark blue berry that contains several seeds.

Myrtus fasciculata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and is also valued for its aromatic properties.
