Mentha arvensis f. ocymoides

Mentha arvensis f. ocymoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf field mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic and often medicinal properties. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. It is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

This variety is noted for its strong mint aroma and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
