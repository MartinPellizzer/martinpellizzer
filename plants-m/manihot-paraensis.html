Manihot paraensis

Manihot paraensis, commonly known as the Amazonian arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and woody stems. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its roots are tuberous and are used as a starchy food source in some indigenous communities.

Due to its adaptability and nutritional value, it holds potential for agricultural use in tropical regions.
