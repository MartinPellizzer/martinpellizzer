Mimosa lenis

Mimosa lenis, commonly known as the soft mimosa, is a species of flowering plant in the Fabaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and pinkish-white flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Mimosa lenis has a shallow root system and is adapted to thrive in humid climates.

Its flowers bloom in clusters and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
