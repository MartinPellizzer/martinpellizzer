Murraya paniculata var. zollingeri

Murraya paniculata var. zollingeri, commonly known as the Zollinger's flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible but often used as ornamental elements in gardens. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in coastal environments.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
