Mulgedium tataricum

Mulgedium tataricum, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the cold regions of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy, aiding in moisture retention in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine or tundra habitats.

Mulgedium tataricum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
