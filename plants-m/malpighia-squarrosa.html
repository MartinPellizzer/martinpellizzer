Malpighia squarrosa

Malpighia squarrosa, commonly known as the red mombin or pitahaya, is a shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Malpighia squarrosa is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.
