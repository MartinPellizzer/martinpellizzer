Myrceugenia miersiana

Myrceugenia miersiana, commonly known as the Miers' myrtle, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Atlantic Forest of eastern Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which is characterized by aromatic leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically has narrow, leathery leaves and produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are small, woody berries that contain seeds, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Myrceugenia miersiana plays an important role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
