Medicago rigidula subsp. zoharyi

Medicago rigidula subsp.

zoharyi, commonly known as the Zohary's narrowleaf medick, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
