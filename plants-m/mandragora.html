Mandragora

Mandragora, commonly known as the devil's apple, is a flowering plant belonging to the nightshade family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often twisted root that resembles a human figure. The plant produces bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or white, and its leaves are broad and ovate. Mandragora contains various alkaloids, some of which have been used historically in medicine and magic.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often associated with folklore and has been regarded as both a powerful and dangerous plant.
