Melanthera deltoidea

Melanthera deltoidea, commonly known as the deltoid ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces purple to pinkish flower heads in late summer.

Melanthera deltoidea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
