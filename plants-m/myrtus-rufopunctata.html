Myrtus rufopunctata

Myrtus rufopunctata, commonly known as the red-spotted myrtle, is a species of flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and glossy, dark green leaves with distinctive red or purple spots. Myrtus rufopunctata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
