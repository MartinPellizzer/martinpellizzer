Microlepia brasiliensis

Microlepia brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian microlepia, is a fern species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are arranged in a distinctive pattern on the underside of its leaves.

Microlepia brasiliensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its elegant foliage.
