Myrtillocactus grandiareolatus

Myrtillocactus grandiareolatus, commonly known as the giant areola cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and distinctive, large areolas that are often covered with dense clusters of spines. The plant produces vibrant purple or blue flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for desert gardens.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
