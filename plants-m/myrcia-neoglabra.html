Myrcia neoglabra

Myrcia neoglabra, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule containing several seeds.

Myrcia neoglabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
