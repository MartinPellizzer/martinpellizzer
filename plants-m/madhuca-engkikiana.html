Madhuca engkikiana

Madhuca engkikiana, commonly known as the Engkik tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and large, elliptical leaves. The tree produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its roots are known for their ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

The Engkik tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in lowland rainforests.
