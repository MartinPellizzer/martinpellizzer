Maughania lacei

Maughania lacei, commonly known as the lacey maughania, is a unique plant species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage that gives it a distinctive and ornamental appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are often covered in fine, hair-like structures that help it retain moisture in humid environments.

While not widely cultivated, it is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ecological role in its native ecosystems.
