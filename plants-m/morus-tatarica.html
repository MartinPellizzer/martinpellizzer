Morus tatarica

Morus tatarica, commonly known as the Tatarian mulberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes other mulberry species and figs. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a food source for silkworms.

The bark is smooth and gray, with a distinctive flaky texture.
