Mormodes calceolata

Mormodes calceolata, commonly known as the star-shaped orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped flowers, which are typically white or pale pink with a contrasting purple or brown center. The plant has a compact growth habit, with slender, hairy stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires specific conditions to bloom, making it a challenging species to cultivate.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Mormodes calceolata is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
