Melastoma arborescens

Melastoma arborescens, commonly known as the red-rooted everlet, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub characterized by its evergreen leaves and clusters of showy, pink or red flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are often eaten by birds and small mammals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Melastoma arborescens has a compact growth habit and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
