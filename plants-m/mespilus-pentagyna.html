Mespilus pentagyna

Mespilus pentagyna, commonly known as the five-angled medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive five-angled fruit, which is typically covered in a white, waxy bloom. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are pollinated by insects.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
