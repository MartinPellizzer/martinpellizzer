Mesona rubra

Mesona rubra, commonly known as red-flowered mesona, is a flowering plant native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its vibrant red flowers, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Mesona rubra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
