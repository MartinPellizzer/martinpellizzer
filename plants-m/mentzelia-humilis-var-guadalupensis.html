Mentzelia humilis var. guadalupensis

Mentzelia humilis var. guadalupensis, commonly known as the Guadalupe milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has narrow, hairy leaves that are often tinged with reddish hues. The plant produces small, pale yellow to greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system to access moisture.

This variety is part of the Loasaceae family and is known for its ecological importance to native pollinators.
