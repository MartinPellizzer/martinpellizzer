Melastoma fallax

Melastoma fallax, commonly known as the purple-flowered melastoma, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range from pink to purple, often with a reddish hue. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Melastoma fallax is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and gardens.
