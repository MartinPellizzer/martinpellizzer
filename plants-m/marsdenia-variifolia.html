Marsdenia variifolia

Marsdenia variifolia, commonly known as the variable milkvine, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptic leaves that vary in shape and size along the stem. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a fleshy, yellowish berry that contains several seeds.

Marsdenia variifolia is often found in tropical and subtropical forests, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments.
