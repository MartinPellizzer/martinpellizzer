Melochia hirsuta var. glabrescens

Melochia hirsuta var. glabrescens, commonly known as the velvetleaf or hairy mallow, is a variant of the melochia genus native to tropical regions.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, hairy texture. The plant produces showy, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to its appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it can become invasive in certain environments.
