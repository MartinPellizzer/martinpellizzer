Myrtus × ralphii

Myrtus × ralphii, commonly known as the hybrid myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen foliage and small, white flowers. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Myrtus × ralphii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional medicine.
