Myrovernix longifolius

Myrovernix longifolius, commonly known as the longleaf myrovernix, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its roots are fibrous and capable of absorbing nutrients from shallow soil layers.

It thrives in moist, well-drained environments and is often found in forest understories.
