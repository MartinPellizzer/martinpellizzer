Mespilus oxyacantha f. mediterranea

Mespilus oxyacantha f. mediterranea, commonly known as the Mediterranean medlar, is a rare and ancient cultivar of the medlar tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny fruit that ripens in late autumn. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It has a compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, dark purple leaves.

Due to its historical significance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of old-world flora.
