Myriocarpa purpurascens

Myriocarpa purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-fruited desert bean, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, compound leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn a striking purple color as they mature. Myriocarpa purpurascens is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.
