Manihot microcarpa

Manihot microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited cassava, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and has a fibrous root system. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy texture.

While not widely cultivated for food, it is of interest for its potential ecological and medicinal properties.
