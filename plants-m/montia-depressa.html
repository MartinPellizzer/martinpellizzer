Montia depressa

Montia depressa, commonly known as the creeping montia, is a low-growing annual plant native to North America.

It typically thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and forest floors. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pale blue to white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Montia depressa is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in wetland habitats.
