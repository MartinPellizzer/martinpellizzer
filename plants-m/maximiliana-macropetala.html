Maximiliana macropetala

Maximiliana macropetala, commonly known as the large-petaled bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a white throat. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, white pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Maximiliana macropetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
