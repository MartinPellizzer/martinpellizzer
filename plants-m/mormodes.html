Mormodes

Mormodes, commonly known as the "garden nightshade," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the Solanaceae family.

These plants are characterized by their showy, often trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and red. The leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture. Mormodes species are native to various regions in Africa, the Americas, and Southeast Asia.

They are often cultivated for their ornamental value and can also be used in traditional medicine.
