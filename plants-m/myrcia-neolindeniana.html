Myrcia neolindeniana

Myrcia neolindeniana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in humid, shaded environments.

Myrcia neolindeniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
