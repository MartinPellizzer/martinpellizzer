Macreightia albens

Macreightia albens, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silvery, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is adapted to arid environments, showcasing succulent features that help it conserve water.
