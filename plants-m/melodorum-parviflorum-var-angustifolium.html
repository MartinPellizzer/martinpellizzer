Melodorum parviflorum var. angustifolium

Melodorum parviflorum var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved melodyflower, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale pink, during the spring and early summer. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.
