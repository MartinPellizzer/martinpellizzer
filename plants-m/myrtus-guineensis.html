Myrtus guineensis

Myrtus guineensis, commonly known as African myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Africa.

It features fragrant, dark green leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and have a slightly tart flavor. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

Myrtus guineensis is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
