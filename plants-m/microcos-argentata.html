Microcos argentata

Microcos argentata, commonly known as silverleaf microcos, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant has small, rounded leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
