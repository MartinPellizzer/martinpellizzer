Medinilla samoensis

Medinilla samoensis, commonly known as the Samoan medinilla, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its attractive, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant features glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland forest areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some horticultural settings, though it remains relatively rare in the wild.
