Muhlenbergia cualensis

Muhlenbergia cualensis, commonly known as the Cueli muhlenbergia, is a species of grass native to South America, specifically found in Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and loose, feathery panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into bristly, awned seeds.

Muhlenbergia cualensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
