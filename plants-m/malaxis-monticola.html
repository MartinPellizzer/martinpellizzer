Malaxis monticola

Malaxis monticola, commonly known as the mountain malaxis, is a species of orchid native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, terrestrial growth habit and produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a sympodial growth pattern, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are typically elliptical and arranged in a rosette, and it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Malaxis monticola is valued for its unique floral structure and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
