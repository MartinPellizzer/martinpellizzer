Mentha cordata

Mentha cordata, commonly known as wild mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with a serrated margin. The leaves are typically aromatic and emit a strong mint scent when crushed. It produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters during the summer months.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.
