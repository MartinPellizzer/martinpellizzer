Malus × moerlandsii

Malus × moerlandsii, commonly known as the Moerlandsii apple, is a hybrid species of apple tree originating from the Netherlands.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy white blossoms in spring and vibrant red foliage in autumn. The tree produces small, tart apples that are not typically consumed fresh but are valued for their use in jams and preserves. It is a deciduous plant that thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated in gardens and public spaces for its seasonal visual interest.
