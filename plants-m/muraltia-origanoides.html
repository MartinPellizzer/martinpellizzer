Muraltia origanoides

Muraltia origanoides, commonly known as the sweet acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, round, red or orange fruits that are edible for some animals but toxic to others. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky areas.

Muraltia origanoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
