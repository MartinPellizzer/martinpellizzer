Molinia caerulea var. lightfootii

Molinia caerulea var. lightfootii, commonly known as Lightfoot's moor grass, is a variant of the moor grass species native to parts of Europe and North America.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blue-green blades. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic soils, often found in bogs, fens, and other moist habitats. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spike of small, pale blue to purple flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland environments.
