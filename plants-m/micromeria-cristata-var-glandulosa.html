Micromeria cristata var. glandulosa

Micromeria cristata var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular fringed sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that produce a strong, pungent scent. The plant features showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its unique morphology and chemical composition make it an important species in ecological and botanical studies.
