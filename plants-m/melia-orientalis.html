Melia orientalis

Melia orientalis, commonly known as the Chinese sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by woody, spherical fruits containing flat, winged seeds. Melia orientalis is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

Its growth habit and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitats.
