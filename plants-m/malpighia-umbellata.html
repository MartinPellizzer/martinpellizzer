Malpighia umbellata

Malpighia umbellata, commonly known as the red mallow, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring glossy, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, red, fleshy fruits that resemble berries, which are edible and often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and a prominent central stamen, contributing to its ornamental value.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for both its ornamental beauty and fruit production.
