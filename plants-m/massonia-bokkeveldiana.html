Massonia bokkeveldiana

Massonia bokkeveldiana, commonly known as the Bokkeveld massonia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, which is characterized by its fleshy leaves and adaptations to arid environments. The plant features a short, thick stem with dense clusters of narrow, fleshy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically during the summer months.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
