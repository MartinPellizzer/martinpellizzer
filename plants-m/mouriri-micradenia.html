Mouriri micradenia

Mouriri micradenia, commonly known as the Brazilian wax tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes other notable plants like eucalyptus and clove. The tree is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in tropical climates.

The plant is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
