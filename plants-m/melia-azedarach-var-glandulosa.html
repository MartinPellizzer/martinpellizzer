Melia azedarach var. glandulosa

Melia azedarach var. glandulosa, commonly known as the Chinese catalpa, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces long, cylindrical pods that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.

The variety glandulosa is distinguished by its glandular structures on the leaves and stems, which contribute to its unique botanical features.
