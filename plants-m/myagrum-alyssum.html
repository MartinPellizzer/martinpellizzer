Myagrum alyssum

Myagrum alyssum, commonly known as the white sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and small, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. The plant produces fragrant white flowers in clusters, which bloom during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Myagrum alyssum is also valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional crafts and as a source of essential oils.
