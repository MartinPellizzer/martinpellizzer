Muscari dilutum

Muscari dilutum, commonly known as the Persian grape hyacinth, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It produces clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Muscari dilutum is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to other hyacinth species.
