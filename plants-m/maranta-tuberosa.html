Maranta tuberosa

Maranta tuberosa, commonly known as the arrowroot plant, is a tropical species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its upright, tuberous rhizomes that are rich in starch and used as a food source. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often variegated with silver or white markings. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible roots.

The leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
