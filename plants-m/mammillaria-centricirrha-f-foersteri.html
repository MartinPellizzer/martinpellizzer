Mammillaria centricirrha f. foersteri

Mammillaria centricirrha f. foersteri, commonly known as the Foerster's mammillaria, is a small cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white to pale green tubercles. The plant produces white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
