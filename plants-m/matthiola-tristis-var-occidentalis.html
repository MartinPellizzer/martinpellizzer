Matthiola tristis var. occidentalis

Matthiola tristis var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other members like cabbage and mustard. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring and early summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can thrive in well-drained soil with full sun.

The species is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
