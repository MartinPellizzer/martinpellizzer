Mandevilla campanulata

Mandevilla campanulata, commonly known as the bell mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves and can grow as a trailing vine or shrub. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Mandevilla campanulata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as a houseplant.
