Myosotis inflexa

Myosotis inflexa, commonly known as the bent willow herb, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its slender, willow-like stems and small, delicate blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Myosotis inflexa is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
