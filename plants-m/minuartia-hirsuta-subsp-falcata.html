Minuartia hirsuta subsp. falcata

Minuartia hirsuta subsp.

falcata, commonly known as the falcate sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae. It is characterized by its hairy, succulent stems and small, often blue or purple flowers.

This subspecies is typically found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in arid or semi-arid environments. The leaves are usually narrow and linear, and the plant is well adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and is of interest to botanists studying alpine and arid flora.
