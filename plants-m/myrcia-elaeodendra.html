Myrcia elaeodendra

Myrcia elaeodendra, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a variety of soil types.

Due to its ornamental value, it has been introduced to other parts of the world, sometimes becoming an invasive species.
