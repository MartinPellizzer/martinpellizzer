Malacothrix saxatilis var. commutata

Malacothrix saxatilis var. commutata, commonly known as the rock malacothrix, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and arid environments.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescence and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in crevices and on rocky slopes, adapting to harsh, well-drained soils. This variety is notable for its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions, making it a hardy species in Mediterranean and similar climates.
