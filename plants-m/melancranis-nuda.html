Melancranis nuda

Melancranis nuda, commonly known as the naked melancranis, is a unique plant species characterized by its lack of typical foliar structures.

It belongs to the family Melancranidaceae and is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia. The plant exhibits a succulent stem with minimal leaf coverage, which helps it conserve water in arid environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of yellow and green.

Despite its simple appearance, it plays a significant role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
