Marsdenia carterae

Marsdenia carterae, commonly known as Carter's Marsdenia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean and is characterized by its climbing habit and opposite, elliptic leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by follicles containing seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in secondary forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
