Malacantha alnifolia

Malacantha alnifolia, commonly known as the alderleaf mallow, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces showy, pink to purple flowers in late spring. Its botanical features include a woody stem, alternate leaf arrangement, and capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Malacantha alnifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
