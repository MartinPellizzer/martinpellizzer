Micrechites tubulosus

Micrechites tubulosus, commonly known as the slender spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, tubular leaves and inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a cup-like structure typical of the spurge family. The plant produces a milky latex when the stems or leaves are damaged, which is a common feature among Euphorbiaceae species. It is native to parts of Africa and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions.

Micrechites tubulosus is often found in disturbed soils and can be a pioneer species in certain ecosystems.
