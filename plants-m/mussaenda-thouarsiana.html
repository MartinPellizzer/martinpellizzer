Mussaenda thouarsiana

Mussaenda thouarsiana, commonly known as the flame flower or red hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Mussaenda thouarsiana is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
