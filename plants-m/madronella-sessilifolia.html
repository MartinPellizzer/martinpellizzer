Madronella sessilifolia

Madronella sessilifolia, commonly known as the sessile-leaf madronella, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in moist, acidic soils within forested areas. The plant features simple, elliptical leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems. Its small, white flowers bloom in clusters and are followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some wildlife.

The plant plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food and habitat for various animal species.
