Monochoria vaginalis var. africana

Monochoria vaginalis var. africana, commonly known as African water spinach, is a aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and long, slender stems that often float on the water surface. The plant thrives in shallow water and wetlands, exhibiting rapid growth under favorable conditions. Its leaves are edible and used in various cuisines, particularly in African and Southeast Asian countries.

It plays a role in water filtration and provides habitat for small aquatic organisms.
