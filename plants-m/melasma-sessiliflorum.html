Melasma sessiliflorum

Melasma sessiliflorum, commonly known as the sessile-flower melasma, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, small, sessile flowers that are often clustered together. The plant typically produces showy, brightly colored blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually simple, lance-shaped, and have a smooth texture.

Melasma sessiliflorum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
