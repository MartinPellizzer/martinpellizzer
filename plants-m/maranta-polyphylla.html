Maranta polyphylla

Maranta polyphylla, commonly known as the "prayer plant," is a striking tropical species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often variegated leaves that fold upward at night, a behavior known as nyctinasty. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is prized for its ornamental value in both indoor and outdoor gardens. Its leaves can vary in shape and color, with some cultivars displaying deep red, green, and pink hues.

Maranta polyphylla thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires indirect light to flourish.
