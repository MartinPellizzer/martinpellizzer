Miconia alfredi

Miconia alfredi, commonly known as the Alfred's miconia, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically dispersed by birds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
