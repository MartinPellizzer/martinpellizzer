Minuartia cherlerioides

Minuartia cherlerioides, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear cress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine and arctic regions.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its small, rosette-forming leaves and clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to cold climates and thrives in rocky or gravelly soils. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in rock gardens and alpine plant collections.
