Mormolyca chacoensis

Mormolyca chacoensis, commonly known as the Chacoan grass, is a perennial herb native to the dry regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its ecological importance and unique adaptations, it is often found in grassland ecosystems of the Chaco region.
