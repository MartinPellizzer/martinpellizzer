Melica gmelinii

Melica gmelinii, commonly known as the slender brome, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, typically with two to three florets per spikelet. Melica gmelinii thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
