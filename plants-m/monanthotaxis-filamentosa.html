Monanthotaxis filamentosa

Monanthotaxis filamentosa, commonly known as the threadleaf starling tree, is a rare and unique species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
