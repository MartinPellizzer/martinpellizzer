Morinda pandurifolia var. oblonga

Morinda pandurifolia var. oblonga, commonly known as the narrow-leaved morinda, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
