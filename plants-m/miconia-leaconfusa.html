Miconia leaconfusa

Miconia leaconfusa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Miconia leaconfusa*, is a species of flowering plant in the family Melastomataceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil and is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom throughout the year. It is often found in moist, shaded areas and can grow up to several meters in height.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
