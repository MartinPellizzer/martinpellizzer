Marsdenia crocea

Marsdenia crocea, commonly known as the red-flowered Marsdenia, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces striking red, spurred flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fibrous roots and stems have been used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.
