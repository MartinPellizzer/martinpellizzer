Myosurus aristatus var. brachypodus

Myosurus aristatus var. brachypodus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved woodruff, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has slender, erect stems covered in fine, silky hairs, and its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
