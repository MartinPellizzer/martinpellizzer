Monnina sanmarcosana

Monnina sanmarcosana, commonly known as the San Marcos morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its succulent, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from violet to red, typically blooming in the early morning. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Monnina sanmarcosana thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.
