Micromeria microphylla

Micromeria microphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved micromeria, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and compact growth habit, typically reaching heights of less than 1 meter. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats.

Botanically, it is classified within the family Brassicaceae, which includes other flowering plants with similar floral structures.
