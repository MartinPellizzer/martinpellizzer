Mespilus cordifolia

Mespilus cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf mespilus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, heart-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, round berries that ripen in late summer. These berries are typically consumed fresh or used in jams and preserves.

Mespilus cordifolia is often found in woodland areas and thrives in well-drained soils.
