Meconopsis horridula var. abnormis

Meconopsis horridula var. abnormis, commonly known as the Himalayan glory flower, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has a robust root system and thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This variant is notable for its unique floral structure and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in specialized gardens.
