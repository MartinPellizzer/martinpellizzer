Milla vittata

Milla vittata, commonly known as the spotted milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, often pink or red flowers. The plant has distinctive, oval-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and prominent veins. Its latex sap is milky and can be toxic to some animals.

Milla vittata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.
