Mentha sylvestris var. longifolia

Mentha sylvestris var. longifolia, commonly known as woodland mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, minty aroma, while its flowers are small, tubular, and usually purple or blue.

This variety is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb, though it is less commonly cultivated than other mint species.
