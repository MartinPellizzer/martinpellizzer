Monarda fistulosa var. mollis

Monarda fistulosa var. mollis, commonly known as the soft-stemmed bee balm, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces clusters of pink to red flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are aromatic and slightly hairy, with a minty scent when crushed.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
