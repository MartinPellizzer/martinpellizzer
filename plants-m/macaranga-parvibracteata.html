Macaranga parvibracteata

Macaranga parvibracteata, commonly known as the small-bract makara, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is notable for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
