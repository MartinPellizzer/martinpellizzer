Minthostachys rubra

Minthostachys rubra, commonly known as the red mint or red-stemmed mint, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple hues. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or lavender in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in herbal medicine.
