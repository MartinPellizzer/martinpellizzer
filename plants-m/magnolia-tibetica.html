Magnolia tibetica

Magnolia tibetica, commonly known as the Tibetan magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves appear. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the autumn. It prefers cool, moist climates and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

The bark is smooth and grayish, with a distinctive fragrance when crushed.
