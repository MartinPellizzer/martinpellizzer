Mussaenda pullei

Mussaenda pullei, commonly known as the flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its bright orange or red bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often surrounded by the showy bracts. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are simple, opposite, and have a velvety texture, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.
