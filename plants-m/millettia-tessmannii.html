Millettia tessmannii

Millettia tessmannii, commonly known as the African tamarind tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnate and often have a distinctive reddish or yellowish coloration. The tree produces yellow flowers followed by long, flat pods containing edible seeds. It is valued for its hard wood and is often used in traditional medicine.

The plant also plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
