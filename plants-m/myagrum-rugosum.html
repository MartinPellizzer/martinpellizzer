Myagrum rugosum

Myagrum rugosum, commonly known as the rough myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub characterized by its rough, fissured bark and evergreen leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Myagrum rugosum is often found in coastal and sandy soils, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
