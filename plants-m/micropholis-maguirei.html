Micropholis maguirei

Micropholis maguirei, commonly known as the Maguire's micropholis, is a rare shrub native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and produces tiny, clustered white flowers. The plant is typically found in coastal areas and thrives in sandy soils. It has a low growth habit and is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its unique appearance.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
