Myrceugenia euosma var. nana

Myrceugenia euosma var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a compact shrub native to the temperate rainforests of southern Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden settings.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in horticultural collections.
