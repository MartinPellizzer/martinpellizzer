Mallotus claoxyloides var. cordatus

Mallotus claoxyloides var. cordatus, commonly known as the heart-shaped mallot, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are a distinguishing feature. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
