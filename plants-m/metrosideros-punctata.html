Metrosideros punctata

Metrosideros punctata, commonly known as the New Zealand Christmas tree, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or orange flowers that appear in clusters, often around December, hence its common name. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a leathery texture, while the bark is smooth and reddish-brown. It thrives in coastal and moist forest environments, and its flowers attract a variety of pollinators.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
