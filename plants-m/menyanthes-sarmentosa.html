Menyanthes sarmentosa

Menyanthes sarmentosa, commonly known as the stinking bladderwort, is a flowering plant native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Menyanthaceae family and is characterized by its small, floating leaves and distinctive, urn-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and wetlands. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink and bloom in early summer.

The plant is known for its ability to produce a strong, unpleasant odor, which is thought to deter herbivores and attract specific pollinators.
