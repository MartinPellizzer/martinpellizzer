Micranthella clinopodifolia

Micranthella clinopodifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a fuzzy texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in late summer to early autumn.

It is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
