Myriophyllum verticillatum subsp. pinnatifidum

Myriophyllum verticillatum subsp.

pinnatifidum, commonly known as water starwort, is a submerged aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It features whorled leaves that are finely divided, giving it a feathery appearance.

The plant typically grows in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments such as lakes and ponds. Its small, green flowers are borne in spike-like clusters and are often submerged or only partially emergent.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
