Malus sachalinensis

Malus sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin apple, is a deciduous tree native to the Russian Far East and northeastern China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value due to its showy white flowers and vibrant red foliage in autumn. The plant produces small, edible apples that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
