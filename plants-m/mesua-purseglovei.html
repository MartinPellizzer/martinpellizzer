Mesua purseglovei

Mesua purseglovei, commonly known as the Pemba flame tree, is a tropical tree native to the island of Pemba in the Zanzibar archipelago.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing before the leaves in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a fragrant, resinous scent.

Mesua purseglovei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks in tropical regions.
