Mentha × piperita var. heuffelii

Mentha × piperita var. heuffelii, commonly known as the German peppermint, is a hybrid mint species characterized by its aromatic, slightly serrated leaves and strong menthol scent.

It is a perennial herb native to Europe and is often cultivated for its essential oils used in aromatherapy and herbal medicine. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with square stems and opposite leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its flowering spikes produce small, pale blue to purple flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
