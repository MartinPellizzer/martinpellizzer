Meninia

Meninia, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage, which results from a dense covering of fine, waxy hairs. The leaves are typically alternate and ovate, with a leathery texture. Meninia species are native to southern Africa and are often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

They produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.
