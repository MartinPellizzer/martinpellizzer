Malva fragrans

Malva fragrans, commonly known as the sweet violet, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa, and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, fragrant purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves and flowers are often used in herbal remedies due to their soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.
