Microtropis grandifolia var. grandifolia

Microtropis grandifolia var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered microtropis, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite, and ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its foliage and unique floral display.
