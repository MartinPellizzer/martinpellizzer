Mentha gentilis var. ciliata

Mentha gentilis var. ciliata, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving them a woolly texture. The plant produces small, pale purple to blue flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Mentha gentilis var. ciliata is valued for its aromatic leaves and is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.
