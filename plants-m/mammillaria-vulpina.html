Mammillaria vulpina

Mammillaria vulpina, commonly known as the fox tail cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem covered in white, woolly spines and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a small, compact cactus that often forms dense clumps due to its branching habit.

Mammillaria vulpina is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
