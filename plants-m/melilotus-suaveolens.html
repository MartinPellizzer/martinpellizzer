Melilotus suaveolens

Melilotus suaveolens, commonly known as sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is known for its sweet, aromatic scent and is often used in agricultural settings as a forage crop.

It also has historical uses in traditional medicine and as a source of biofuel due to its high biomass yield.
