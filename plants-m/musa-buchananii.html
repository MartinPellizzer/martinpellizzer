Musa buchananii

Musa buchananii, commonly known as the African banana, is a flowering plant native to central and western Africa.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and tall, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces edible berries, which are typically consumed raw or used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is cultivated in various regions for both aesthetic and nutritional purposes.
