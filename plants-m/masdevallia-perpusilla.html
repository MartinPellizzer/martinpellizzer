Masdevallia perpusilla

Masdevallia perpusilla, commonly known as the miniature masdevallia, is a small orchid native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its compact size and intricate, often colorful flower spikes that bloom in shades of pink, red, and purple. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often epiphytic on trees or rocks. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, delicate flowers that attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and unique appearance.
