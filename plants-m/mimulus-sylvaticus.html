Mimulus sylvaticus

Mimulus sylvaticus, commonly known as the wood's yellow, is a flowering plant native to the forests of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb with erect, branched stems that bear numerous yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Mimulus sylvaticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting insect populations.
