Malus crataegifolia

Malus crataegifolia, commonly known as the European hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, white or pinkish flowers, and small, red berries that ripen in the autumn. The plant is part of the Rosaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and adaptable species.

The leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and the plant is often used in hedgerows and as a landscape plant.
