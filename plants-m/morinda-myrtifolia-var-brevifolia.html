Morinda myrtifolia var. brevifolia

Morinda myrtifolia var. brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved morinda, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers, which are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

This species is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
