Manilkara zapota

Manilkara zapota, commonly known as the cherimoya or custard apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The fruit is distinctive, with a rough, spiny exterior and a creamy, sweet flesh containing numerous black seeds. The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and economic importance, particularly for its edible fruit and latex.

Its growth is typically in warm, humid climates, and it thrives in well-drained soils.
