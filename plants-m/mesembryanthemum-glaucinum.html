Mesembryanthemum glaucinum

Mesembryanthemum glaucinum, commonly known as the silverleaf mesem, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, grey-green leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a versatile and attractive addition to arid landscapes.
