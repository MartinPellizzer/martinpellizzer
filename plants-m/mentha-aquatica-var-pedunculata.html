Mentha aquatica var. pedunculata

Mentha aquatica var. pedunculata, commonly known as water mint, is a variant of the water mint plant native to moist environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in wet soils near streams, ponds, and marshes, and it often spreads through rhizomes. Its aromatic leaves and flowers are used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for their cooling and digestive properties.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
