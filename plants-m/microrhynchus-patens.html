Microrhynchus patens

Microrhynchus patens, commonly known as the small-beaked flax, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Linaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal spikes. It is often found in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks and in open woodlands.
