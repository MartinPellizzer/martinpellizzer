Marsdenia dregea

Marsdenia dregea, commonly known as the African milk vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits. Its latex sap is milky and can be toxic, containing cardiac glycosides.

Marsdenia dregea is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
