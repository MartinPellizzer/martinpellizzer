Melica bauhini

Melica bauhini, commonly known as the slender brome, is a grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, which contain both male and female flowers. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Melica bauhini is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
