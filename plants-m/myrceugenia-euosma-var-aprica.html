Myrceugenia euosma var. aprica

Myrceugenia euosma var. aprica, commonly known as the yellow myrtle, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by edible, berry-like fruits that are yellow when ripe. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and moist forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
