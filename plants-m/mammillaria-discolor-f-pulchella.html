Mammillaria discolor f. pulchella

Mammillaria discolor f. pulchella, commonly known as the White-Flowered Bunny Ears, is a compact cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its pale green to white tubercles and small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a rounded, globular shape with a dense arrangement of spines. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated as a decorative plant.
