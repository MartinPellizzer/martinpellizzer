Mikania pannosa

Mikania pannosa, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. It is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Mikania pannosa is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
