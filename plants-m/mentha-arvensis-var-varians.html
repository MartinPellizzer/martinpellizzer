Mentha arvensis var. varians

Mentha arvensis var. varians, commonly known as field mint or wild mint, is a variant of the broad-leaf mint species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and thrives in temperate climates. It is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in wetlands.

Its essential oils contain menthol and other compounds, making it valuable in both traditional and modern herbal applications.
