Musa × sapientum var. dubia

Musa × sapientum var. dubia, commonly known as the wild banana, is a less commonly cultivated variant of the banana plant.

It is characterized by its robust, elongated leaves and clusters of small, green, inedible fruits. This variety is native to Southeast Asia and is often found in tropical rainforests. The plant exhibits vigorous growth and can reach heights of several meters.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for genetic research and plant breeding programs.
