Mitracarpus schizangius var. angustifolius

Mitracarpus schizangius var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mitracarpus, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with woody stems and a compact form.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to coastal and rocky environments.
