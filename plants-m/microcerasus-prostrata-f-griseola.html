Microcerasus prostrata f. griseola

Microcerasus prostrata f. griseola, commonly known as the silver-leaved stone pine, is a variant of the prostrate stone pine characterized by its distinctive grayish-blue foliage.

It is a shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of China, where it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by edible berries that are a food source for various wildlife. Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and landscape design.

The varietal form is notable for its unique coloration and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
