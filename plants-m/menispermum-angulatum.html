Menispermum angulatum

Menispermum angulatum, commonly known as the angular menispermum, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by yellow, fleshy berries that are edible for some wildlife. Its roots contain alkaloids and have been used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.
