Marquesia acuminata

Marquesia acuminata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Erythroxylum acuminatum*, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Erythroxylaceae and is characterized by its small, red flowers and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticulture.
