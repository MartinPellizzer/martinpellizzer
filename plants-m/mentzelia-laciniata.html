Mentzelia laciniata

Mentzelia laciniata, commonly known as the fringed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and its lance-shaped, deeply lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in desert regions. It is a perennial species that produces showy blooms during the summer months.

Mentzelia laciniata is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used by indigenous peoples for various therapeutic purposes.
