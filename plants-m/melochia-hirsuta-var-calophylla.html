Melochia hirsuta var. calophylla

Melochia hirsuta var. calophylla, commonly known as the hairy melochia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features palmate leaves with a hairy texture, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by fleshy, red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a notable species in botanical gardens and ecological studies.
