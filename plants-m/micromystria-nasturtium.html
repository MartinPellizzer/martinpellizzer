Micromystria nasturtium

Micromystria nasturtium, commonly known as the common watercress, is a aquatic plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It thrives in freshwater environments such as streams, ponds, and ditches, often growing in shallow waters with muddy or sandy substrates. The plant has slender, branching stems that are typically less than 30 cm in length, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin. Small, white or yellowish flowers with four petals appear in clusters, and the plant produces small, round, green or yellow fruits.

It is known for its peppery taste and has been used historically in herbal medicine and as a culinary herb.
