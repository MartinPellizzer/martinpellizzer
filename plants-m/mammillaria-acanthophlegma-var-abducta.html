Mammillaria acanthophlegma var. abducta

Mammillaria acanthophlegma var. abducta, commonly known as the Mexican hedgehog cactus, is a small, spineless cactus native to Mexico.

It features a globular or cylindrical stem covered in white, woolly hairs that help reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for succulent collections.
