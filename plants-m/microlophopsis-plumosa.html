Microlophopsis plumosa

Microlophopsis plumosa, commonly known as the feather-leaf fern, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Microlophopsis plumosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
