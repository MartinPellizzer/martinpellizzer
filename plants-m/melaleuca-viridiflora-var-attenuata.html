Melaleuca viridiflora var. attenuata

Melaleuca viridiflora var. attenuata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom, giving them a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in spring. It is adapted to coastal and sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
