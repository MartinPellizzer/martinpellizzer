Malacocarpus catamarcensis

Malacocarpus catamarcensis, commonly known as the Catamarca cactus, is a rare and unique plant native to the arid regions of Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant has a compact growth habit, typically forming small, rounded clumps. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stems to survive in dry conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
