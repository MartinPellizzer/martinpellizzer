Microtropis polycarpa

Microtropis polycarpa, commonly known as the many-fruited microtropis, is a tropical climbing plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish in color. Its fruits are follicles that develop in clusters, giving the plant its common name.

Microtropis polycarpa is native to Southeast Asia and is often found in humid, lowland forest environments.
