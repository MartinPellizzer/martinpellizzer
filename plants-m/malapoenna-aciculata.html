Malapoenna aciculata

Malapoenna aciculata, commonly known as the needleleaf malapoenna, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that help reduce water loss in arid conditions. Its small, white or yellowish flowers appear in clusters and are pollinated by insects.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
