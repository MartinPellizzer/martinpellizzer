Mentha × piperita var. pimentum

Mentha × piperita var. pimentum, commonly known as Mexican mint or pimenta, is a hybrid mint species characterized by its aromatic, slightly spicy scent.

It is native to Central America and has been introduced to various regions, including parts of the United States. The plant typically grows as an upright perennial with square stems and opposite leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, tubular, and clustered in whorls, producing nectar that attracts pollinators.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and use in herbal teas and culinary applications.
