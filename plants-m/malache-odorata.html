Malache odorata

Malache odorata, commonly known as the fragrant wood or sweet wood, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, woody stems that emit a pleasant scent when crushed. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are small and clustered, often producing a sweet fragrance that attracts pollinators.
