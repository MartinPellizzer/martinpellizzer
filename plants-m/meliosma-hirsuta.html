Meliosma hirsuta

Meliosma hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy meliosma, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and related trees. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically greenish-yellow and appear in clusters, while the fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Meliosma hirsuta thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
