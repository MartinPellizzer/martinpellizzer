Menispermum myosuroides

Menispermum myosuroides, commonly known as the false mandrake, is a climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Menispermaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but should be consumed with caution. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and medicinal studies.
