Moquilea anneae

Moquilea anneae, commonly known as the Anneae's moquilea, is a unique plant species native to the arid regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its compact, succulent-like structure with thick, waxy leaves that help retain moisture in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the dry season, which are adapted for pollination by nocturnal insects. Its root system is shallow but extensive, allowing it to efficiently absorb water from the soil.

Due to habitat loss, Moquilea anneae is considered a vulnerable species and is of interest to botanists studying desert flora.
