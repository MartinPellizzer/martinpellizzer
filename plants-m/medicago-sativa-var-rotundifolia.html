Medicago sativa var. rotundifolia

Medicago sativa var. rotundifolia, commonly known as roundleaf alfalfa, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its rounded, trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a deep root system, allowing it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. It is valued for its high nutritional value and is often used as forage for livestock.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.
