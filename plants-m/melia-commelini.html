Melia commelini

Melia commelini, commonly known as the Indian chincherinchee, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes species known for their aromatic wood and medicinal properties. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
