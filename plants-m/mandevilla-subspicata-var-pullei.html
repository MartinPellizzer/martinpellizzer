Mandevilla subspicata var. pullei

Mandevilla subspicata var. pullei, commonly known as the flame vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from orange to red, often with a yellow throat. The plant produces showy blooms throughout the growing season, typically from spring to fall. Its foliage consists of large, heart-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy.

Mandevilla subspicata var. pullei thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and as a houseplant.
