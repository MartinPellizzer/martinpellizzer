Mertensia umbratilis

Mertensia umbratilis, commonly known as the shade mertensia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Mertensia umbratilis is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
