Mentha trachypriononta

Mentha trachypriononta, commonly known as the rough-stemmed mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and flowering spikes that produce small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture, and it emits a strong minty fragrance.

Mentha trachypriononta is often used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and cooling properties.
