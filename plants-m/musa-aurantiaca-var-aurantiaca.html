Musa aurantiaca var. aurantiaca

Musa aurantiaca var. aurantiaca, commonly known as the red banana, is a tropical plant belonging to the Musaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking red-purple pseudostem and elongated, fleshy, orange-colored berries that are typically harvested when fully mature. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained, fertile soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are large, broad, and typically a deep green, providing a lush appearance.

The fruit is not only ornamental but also edible, though it is less commonly consumed compared to other banana varieties.
