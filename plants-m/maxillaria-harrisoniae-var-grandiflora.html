Maxillaria harrisoniae var. grandiflora

Maxillaria harrisoniae var. grandiflora, commonly known as the Harrison's Maxillaria, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces large, velvety purple or violet blooms with yellow markings, which appear during the warm season. Its pseudobulbs are cylindrical, and it thrives in bright, indirect light with high humidity.

This variety is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
