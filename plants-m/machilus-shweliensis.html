Machilus shweliensis

Machilus shweliensis, commonly known as the Shweli machil, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland forests and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

Its botanical features include simple, alternate leaves with a glossy surface and small, clustered fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.
