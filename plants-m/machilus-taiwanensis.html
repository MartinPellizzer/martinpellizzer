Machilus taiwanensis

Machilus taiwanensis, commonly known as Taiwan laurel, is a small evergreen tree native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries, which are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in subtropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
