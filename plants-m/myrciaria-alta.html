Myrciaria alta

Myrciaria alta, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a distinctive, slightly tart flavor. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and emit a fragrant aroma when crushed.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
