Mapouria berizokae

Mapouria berizokae, commonly known as the Malagasy orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and vibrant coloration. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded forest environments, where it relies on specific symbiotic relationships with fungi for nutrient absorption. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
