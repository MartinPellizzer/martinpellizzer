Myodocarpus fraxinifolius var. lobatus

Myodocarpus fraxinifolius var. lobatus, commonly known as the broadleaf myodocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with lobed margins, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its hard, durable wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
