Melaleuca sphaerodendra

Melaleuca sphaerodendra, commonly known as the broad-leaved paperbark, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its smooth, flaky bark that peels away in strips, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are broad and oval, with a glossy green surface and a white underside.

Melaleuca sphaerodendra produces small white or pink flowers in clusters, which are followed by cylindrical, woody fruit capsules.
