Margaritaria obovata

Margaritaria obovata, commonly known as the diamondleaf or oblongleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in the tropics and subtropics.

The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are typically clustered together, followed by berries that are toxic to many animals. Margaritaria obovata is also known for its ability to survive in shaded environments, which contributes to its success as a parasite.
