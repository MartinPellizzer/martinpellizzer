Myrcia maraana

Myrcia maraana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to Brazil and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Myrcia maraana is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a variety of soil types.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and sometimes invasive in non-native environments.
