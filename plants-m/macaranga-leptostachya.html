Macaranga leptostachya

Macaranga leptostachya, commonly known as the narrow-stamen paper mulberry, is a fast-growing tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by woody, spherical fruits. Its fibrous bark and leaves have been traditionally used for making paper and textiles.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats, making it an important pioneer species in forest succession.
