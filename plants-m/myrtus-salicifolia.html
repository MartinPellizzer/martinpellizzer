Myrtus salicifolia

Myrtus salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaf myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pale pink to white in color. Its aromatic leaves and berries are used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

Myrtus salicifolia thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.
