Melinis tenuissima

Melinis tenuissima, commonly known as slender grass or narrowleaf grass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically forms dense mats of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often less than 1 centimeter wide. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open areas such as roadsides and prairies.

Its fibrous root system helps prevent soil erosion, making it an important plant in ecological restoration projects.
