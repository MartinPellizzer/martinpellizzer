Miconia superposita

Miconia superposita, commonly known as the layered miconia, is a species of flowering plant in the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, broad leaves and showy, clustered inflorescences that produce small, white to pink flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it has been introduced to other tropical regions, though it can become invasive in some areas.
