Mitostax pallida

Mitostax pallida, commonly known as the pale mitostax, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, whitish bloom. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the cooler months. It is adapted to survive in low-water environments, with a deep root system that allows it to access groundwater.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
