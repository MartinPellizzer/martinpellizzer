Macrolobium obtusum

Macrolobium obtusum, commonly known as the broad-leafed tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow-orange pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Macrolobium obtusum is valued for both its ornamental appearance and its ecological benefits.
