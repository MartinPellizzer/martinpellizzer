Maxillaria acutifolia

Maxillaria acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, acuminate leaves and produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized epiphyte, often found in humid tropical forests. Its flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, making it a sought-after plant for orchid enthusiasts.

The species is noted for its ease of cultivation and adaptability to various growing conditions.
