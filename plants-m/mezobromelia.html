Mezobromelia

Mezobromelia, commonly known as the bromeliad, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Bromeliaceae.

These plants are native to the Americas, particularly Central and South America, and are known for their striking, often colorful foliage. Mezobromelia species typically have rosettes of leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and serves as a microhabitat for various organisms. They are epiphytic, meaning they grow on other plants without being parasitic, and they can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
