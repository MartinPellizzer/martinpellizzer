Melilotoides kafirniganica

Melilotoides kafirniganica, commonly known as the Afghan melilot, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the legume family. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and less-studied species in botanical research.
