Melastoma impetiolare

Melastoma impetiolare, commonly known as the velvetleaf melastoma, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the spring and summer. Its fruits are capsules that release tiny seeds when mature, aiding in its propagation.

Melastoma impetiolare is often found in moist, shaded forest environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
