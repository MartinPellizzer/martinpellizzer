Melocactus pachycephalus

Melocactus pachycephalus, commonly known as the thick-headed melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, thick stem and a single, rounded, ribbed head that resembles a melon. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, with a deep root system and a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Melocactus pachycephalus is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
