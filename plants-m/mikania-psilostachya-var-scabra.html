Mikania psilostachya var. scabra

Mikania psilostachya var. scabra, commonly known as roughleaf mile-a-minute, is a vigorous climbing vine native to the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture on both sides, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively, often becoming invasive in non-native regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and weed management.
