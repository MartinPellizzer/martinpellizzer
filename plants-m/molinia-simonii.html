Molinia simonii

Molinia simonii, commonly known as the purple moor grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in moist, acidic soils, often found in peat bogs and heathlands. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding a subtle purple hue to the landscape. Its rhizomatous growth allows it to spread and form extensive colonies, contributing to soil stabilization in its habitat.

Molinia simonii plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
