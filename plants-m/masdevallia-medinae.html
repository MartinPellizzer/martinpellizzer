Masdevallia medinae

Masdevallia medinae, commonly known as the Medina's masdevallia, is a rare and striking orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its intricate, often brightly colored flowers with complex petal structures. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, with slender stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its blooming period occurs during the cooler months, and it thrives in high humidity and partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
