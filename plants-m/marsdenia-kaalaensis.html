Marsdenia kaalaensis

Marsdenia kaalaensis, commonly known as the Kaala mountain vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, yellowish-green fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
