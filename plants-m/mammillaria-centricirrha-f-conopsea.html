Mammillaria centricirrha f. conopsea

Mammillaria centricirrha f. conopsea, commonly known as the Conopsea mammillaria, is a rare and distinctive cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that are often covered in a layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise rugged appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its rarity and unique features, it is often sought after by cactus enthusiasts and botanists alike.
