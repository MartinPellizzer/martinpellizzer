Musa errans

Musa errans, commonly known as the wild banana, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, elongated leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that eventually develop into elongated, finger-like fruits. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, Musa errans is cultivated in various tropical regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
