Mucuna macrophylla

Mucuna macrophylla, commonly known as the giant cowpea, is a tropical legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and showy, crimson flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, slender pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both its seeds and ornamental value.

Mucuna macrophylla is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
