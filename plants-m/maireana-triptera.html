Maireana triptera

Maireana triptera, commonly known as the triptera ghostgum, is a small, deciduous tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, three-lobed seed pods. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and resistance to browsing.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in native landscapes.
