Melicope pteleifolia

Melicope pteleifolia, commonly known as Hawaiian sandarac, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and dry forests. The plant has simple, opposite, and elliptical leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually yellow or white and appear in clusters.

The fruit is a small, rough, woody drupe that contains a single seed.
