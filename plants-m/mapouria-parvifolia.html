Mapouria parvifolia

Mapouria parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved mapouria, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical in shape, with a smooth margin and a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, round fruits that contain a single seed.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
