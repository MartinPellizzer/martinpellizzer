Monarda fistulosa var. brevis

Monarda fistulosa var. brevis, commonly known as the narrowleaf wild bergamot, is a perennial herb native to the eastern and central United States.

It features showy, tubular purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped, aromatic leaves that are often used in herbal teas. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
