Masdevallia mopsus

Masdevallia mopsus, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a tropical orchid native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety petals and sepals that often display intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowering season occurs during the warmer months, producing clusters of fragrant, showy blooms.

Due to its unique appearance and rarity, Masdevallia mopsus is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
