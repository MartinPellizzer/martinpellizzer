Myrtus lindleyana

Myrtus lindleyana, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Myrtus lindleyana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
