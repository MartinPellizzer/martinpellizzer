Mimosa brachyloba

Mimosa brachyloba, commonly known as the dwarf mimosa, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are composed of numerous small, pinnate leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
