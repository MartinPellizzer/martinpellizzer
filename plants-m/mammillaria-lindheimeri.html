Mammillaria lindheimeri

Mammillaria lindheimeri, commonly known as the Lindheimer's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense clusters of white to pale green spines. The plant produces showy yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Mammillaria lindheimeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a desert garden setting.
