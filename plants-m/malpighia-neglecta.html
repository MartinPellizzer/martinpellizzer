Malpighia neglecta

Malpighia neglecta, commonly known as the small physalis or the little strawberry bush, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive orange-red calyces that enclose its small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are usually yellow or pink, and the fruit develops in a fleshy, lantern-like structure.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
