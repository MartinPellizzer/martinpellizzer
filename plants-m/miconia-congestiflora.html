Miconia congestiflora

Miconia congestiflora, commonly known as the tropical beauty leaf, is a species native to the Americas, particularly found in tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers, which are followed by fruit that turns red when mature.

Miconia congestiflora is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments, making it a common sight in tropical forests.
