Mendoncella marginata

Mendoncella marginata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
