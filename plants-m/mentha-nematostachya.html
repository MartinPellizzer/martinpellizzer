Mentha nematostachya

Mentha nematostachya, commonly known as narrow-leaved mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a strong, minty fragrance.

Mentha nematostachya is often used in traditional medicine for its purported cooling and digestive properties.
