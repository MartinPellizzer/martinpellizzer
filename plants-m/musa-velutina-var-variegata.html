Musa velutina var. variegata

Musa velutina var. variegata, commonly known as the velvet banana, is a variant of the banana plant characterized by its unique foliage and fruiting habits.

This plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and warm temperatures. Its leaves are broad, dark green, and often have a velvety texture, giving it its common name. The fruit, which is typically smaller than standard bananas, has a thick, fibrous peel and a sweet, starchy flavor.

It is primarily cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and is also used in some local cuisines.
