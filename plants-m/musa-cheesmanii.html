Musa cheesmanii

Musa cheesmanii, commonly known as the Cheesman banana, is a rare and ornamental species native to the highlands of New Guinea.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces striking, clustered inflorescences with both male and female flowers, typical of the Musa genus. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
